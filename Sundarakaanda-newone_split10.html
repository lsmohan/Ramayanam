Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 10
In this Sarga Hanumān approaches the magnificent bedstead and the mighty Rāvaṇa sleeping on it. He sees many beautiful women who have dozed off under the power of intoxication, hugging the musical instruments they were playing.
He then finds Manḍōdaree, an extraordinarily beautiful woman, on a bed that is distinctly set aside. Based on her youthful looks and extraordinary charm, he mistakes her to be Seetā and jumps with joy.
5.10.1
तत्र दिव्योपमं मुख्यम्
स्फाटिकं रत्नभूषितम् ।
अवेक्षमाणो हनुमान्
ददर्श शयनासनम् ।
दान्तकाञ्चनचित्राङ्गैः
वैडूर्यैश्च वरासनैः ।
महार्हास्तरणोपेतैः
उपपन्नं महाधनैः ॥
Looking around, Hanumān saw a prominent bedstead which resembled like one in heaven, encrusted with crystals and gems, its legs and other parts inlaid with designs of ivory, gold and lazulite, covered with bedspreads of the best class, along with posh and best couches.
5.10.3
तस्य चैकतमे देशे
सोऽग्य्रमालाविभूषितम् ।
ददर्श पाण्डुरं छत्रम्
ताराधिपतिसन्निभम् ॥
Then, over there, he saw a white umbrella, bright like the lord of stars decorated with superb garlands.
5.10.4
जातरूपपरिक्षिप्तम्
चित्रभानुसमप्रभम् ।
अशोकमालाविततम्
ददर्श परमासनम् ।
वालव्यजनहस्ताभिः
वीज्यमानं समन्ततः ।
गन्धैश्च विविधैर्जुष्टम्
वरधूपेन धूपितम् ।
परमास्तरणास्तीर्णम्
आविकाजिनसंवृतम् ।
दामभिर्वरमाल्यानाम्
समन्तादुपशोभितम् ॥
The superb bedstead he saw was dazzling, like the sun. It was decorated with garlands of Aṡōka blooms. Women, on every side of it, were weaving fly-whisks. It was fragrant, treated with the vapors of the best of incense. It was covered with sheep-skin and best of the best bedspreads. It was draped with festoons of rare flowers.
5.10.7
तस्मिन् जीमूतसंकाशम्
प्रदीप्तोत्तमकुण्डलम् ।
लोहिताक्षं महाबाहुम्
महारजतवाससम् ।
लोहितेनानुलिप्ताङ्गम्
चन्दनेन सुगन्धिना ।
सन्ध्यारक्तमिवाकाशे
तोयदं सतटिद्गणम् ।
वृतमाभरणैर्दिव्यैः
सुरूपं कामरूपिणम् ।
सवृक्षवनगुल्माढ्यम्
प्रसुप्तमिव मन्दरम् ।
क्रीडित्वोपरतं रात्रौ
वराभरणभूषितम् ।
प्रियं राक्षसकन्यानाम्
राक्षसानां सुखावहम् ।
पीत्वाऽप्युपरतं चापि
ददर्श स महाकपिः ।
भास्वरे शयने वीरम्
प्रसुप्तं राक्षसाधिपम् ॥
The great Vānara saw the heroic lord of the Rākshasas, the darling of the Rākshasa women and the unfailing joy of Rākshasas’, lying fast asleep on that splendid bed, with his eyes turned blood-red after night-long sport and potations.
He of mighty arms was immense like a cloud. Wearing dazzling and exquisite ear-pendants, (his body) smeared with fragrant red-sandal paste, he resembled a great rain-cloud in the scarlet evening sky with lightning playing on it.
He of attractive body, who could take any form at will, covered in splendid ornaments, looked as if he were the Mandara mountain with its wealth of groves and thickets that lay asleep there.
5.10.12
निःश्वसन्तं यथा नागम्
रावणं वानरर्षभः ।
आसाद्य परमोद्विग्नः
सोऽपासर्पत्सुभीतवत् ॥
Getting close to Rāvaṇa whose breath was like the hiss of a serpent, he was taken aback and recoiled like one who had had a terrible fright.
Imagine a small monkey, the form in which Hanumān was at this moment, to enjoy the cute reaction of Hanumān described in these two Ṡlōkas.
5.10.13
अथाऽऽरोहणमासाद्य
वेदिकाऽन्तरमाश्रितः ।
सुप्तं राक्षसशार्दूलम्
प्रेक्षते स्म महाकपिः ॥
Then that great Vānara took to a stairway and climbed on to an elevated platform and looked at the sleeping tiger among Rākshasas.
5.10.14
शुशुभे राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
स्वपतः शयनोत्तमम् ।
गन्धहस्तिनि संविष्टे
यथा प्रस्रवणं महत् ॥
With the King of Rākshasas asleep on it, that superb bedstead looked like a great spring with an elephant in rut lying in it.
5.10.15
काञ्चनाङ्गदनध्दै च
ददर्श स महात्मनः ।
विक्षिप्तौ राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
भुजाविन्द्रध्वजोपमौ ॥
Then he saw the stretched arms of the mighty lord of Rākshasas adorned with golden armlets resembling the flagstaff of Indra.
5.10.16
ऐरावतविषाणाग्रैः
आपीडनकृतव्रणौ ।
वज्रोल्लिखितपीनांसौ
विष्णुचक्रपरिक्षतौ ॥
They were marked with scars from the thrust of the sharp tips of Airāvata’s tusks. Those stout shoulders were rubbed by Vajra. They withstood the strike by the discus of Vishṇu.
5.10.17
पीनौ समसुजातांसौ
संगतौ बलसंयुतौ ।
सुलक्षणनखाङ्गुष्ठौ
स्वङ्गुलीतललक्षितौ ॥
Those arms were stout, strong, symmetrical, well formed and were a perfect fit to his body, with finely molded nails, thumbs, fingers and palms.
5.10.18
संहतौ परिघाकारौ
वृत्तौ करिकरोपमौ ।
विक्षिप्तौ शयने शुभ्रे
पञ्चशीर्षाविवोरगौ ॥
Their joints were strong. They looked like iron clubs. They were round like the trunk of an elephant. Strewn on that bed, they shone like serpents with five heads.
5.10.19
शशक्षतजकल्पेन
सुशीतेन सुगन्धिना ।
चन्दनेन परार्ध्येन
स्वनुलिप्तौ स्वलङ्कृतौ ॥
Those well decorated arms that shone with the brilliance of the fresh blood of a hare were cooled and became fragrant with the smearing of priceless sandal paste.
5.10.20
उत्तमस्त्रीविमृदितौ
गन्धोत्तमनिषेवितौ ।
यक्षपन्नगगन्धर्व
देवदानवराविणौ ॥
They were massaged well by the best of women. They were smeared with the best of perfumes. They were the ones who made Yakshas, Pannagas, Gandharvas, Dēvas and Asuras wail in distress.
5.10.21
ददर्श स कपिस्तत्र
बाहू शयनसंस्थितौ ।
मन्दरस्यांतरे सुप्तौ
महाही रुषिताविव ॥
To the eyes of that Vānara, those arms that lay stretched on the bed looked like angry serpents sleeping in the folds of Mandara mountain.
5.10.22
ताभ्यां स परिपूर्णाभ्याम्
भुजाभ्यां राक्षसेश्वरः ।
शुशुभेऽचलसङ्काशः
शृङ्गाभ्यामिव मन्दरः ॥
Those two arms of the lord of Rākshasas who was like a mountain, shone like two peaks of Mandara mountain.
5.10.23
चूतपुन्नागसुरभिः
वकुलोत्तमसंयुतः ।
मृष्टान्नरससंयुक्तः
पानगन्धपुरस्कृतः ।
तस्य राक्षससिंहस्य
निश्चक्राम महामुखात् ।
शयानस्य विनिःश्वासः
पूरयन्निव तद् गृहम् ॥
The breath that came from the huge mouth of that lion among Rākshasas as he lay asleep seemed to fill the entire chamber with the intense smell of the liquor (he consumed) and the flavors of sumptuous food (he ate), making one recall the fragrance of Cūta, Punnāga, Surabhi and Vakula flowers.
5.10.25
मुक्तामणिविचित्रेण
काञ्चनेन विराजितम् ।
मकुटेनापवृत्तेन
कुण्डलोज्वलिताननम् ॥
He was shining with the golden crown studded with many a pearl and gem, which was slightly displaced. His face was lit up by the dazzle of his ear-pendants.
5.10.26
रक्तचन्दनदिग्धेन
तथा हारेण शोभिना ।
पीनायतविशालेन
वक्षसाऽभिविराजितम् ॥
He was splendid, with his long and wide chest that was smeared with red-sandal paste and adorned with necklaces.
5.10.27
पाण्डरेणापविद्धेन
क्षौमेण क्षतजेक्षणम् ।
महार्हेण सुसंवीतम्
पीतेनोत्तमवाससा ॥
He eyes were blood-red. The white silk (upper) garment on him slipped to a side. The superb yellow cloth, which only the most opulent could afford, was wrapped around him tight.
5.10.28
माषराशिप्रतीकाशम्
निश्श्वसन्तं भुजङ्गवत् ।
गाङ्गे महति तोयान्ते
प्रसुप्तमिव कुञ्जरम् ॥
He was like a mound of black gram. His breath was like the hiss of a serpent. He was like an elephant sleeping in the wide waters of river Gaṅgā.
5.10.29
चतुर्भिः काञ्चनैर्दीपैः
दीप्यमानचतुर्दिशम् ।
प्रकाशीकृतसर्वाङ्गम्
मेघं विद्युद्गणैरिव ॥
With four golden lamps shining on him from four sides, his entire body was illuminated like a cloud by the flashes of lightning.
5.10.30
पादमूलगताश्चापि
ददर्श सुमहात्मनः ।
पत्नीः स प्रियभार्यस्य
तस्य रक्षःपतेर्गृहे ॥
He saw, in that house, the loving wives of the mighty lord of Rākshasas lying at his feet.
5.10.31
शशिप्रकाशवदनाः
चारुकुण्डलभूषिताः ।
अम्लानमाल्याभरणा
ददर्श हरियूथपः ॥
The Vānara chief saw (women) with moon-bright faces wearing beautiful ear-pendants and adorned with flower garlands that never seemed to wilt.
5.10.32
नृत्तवादित्रकुशला
राक्षसेन्द्रभुजाङ्कगाः ।
वराभरणधारिण्यो
निषण्णा ददृशे हरिः ॥
The Vānara saw (women) who were skilled in dance and in playing musical instruments who threw themselves into the arms and lap of that lord of Rākshasas, wearing exquisite ornaments.
5.10.33
वज्रवैडूर्यगर्भाणि
श्रवणान्तेषु योषिताम् ।
ददर्श तापनीयानि
कुण्डलान्यङ्गदानि च ॥
He saw women with ear-pendants hanging from the ends of their ears and armlets made of gold purified in fire and studded with lazulite and diamonds.
5.10.34
तासां चन्द्रोपमैर्वक्त्रैः
शुभैर्ललितकुण्डलैः ।
विरराज विमानं तत्
नभस्तारागणैरिव ॥
That Vimāna, with the moon-bright faces of those women and their beautiful, fine ear-pendants, shone like the night sky lit by stars.
5.10.35
मदव्यायामखिन्नास्ता
राक्षसेन्द्रस्य योषितः ।
तेषु तेष्ववकाशेषु
प्रसुप्तास्तनुमध्यमाः ॥
Those women of the lord of Rākshasas, of slender waists, slept wherever they found room, overcome by the spell of passion and exhausted by playing love sports.
5.10.36
अङ्गहारैस्तथैवान्या
कोमलैर्नृत्तशालिनी ।
विन्यस्तशुभसर्वाङ्गी
प्रसुप्ता वरवर्णिनी ॥
One woman of great complexion who was skilled in dance slept with all of her delicate limbs in exquisite dance poses.
5.10.37
काचिद्वीणां परिष्वज्य
प्रसुप्ता सम्प्रकाशते ।
महानदीप्रकीर्णेव
नलिनी पोतमाश्रिता ॥
Another woman, sleeping hugging a Veeṇa looked like an uprooted lotus plant clinging to a float, carried off by a mighty stream.
5.10.38
अन्या कक्षगतेनैव
मड्डुकेनासितेक्षणा ।
प्रसुप्ता भामिनी भाति
बालपुत्रेव वत्सला ॥
Another, a black-eyed beauty, who slept holding the Maḍḍuka drum under her arm looked like a loving mother with her infant child.
5.10.39
पटहं चारुसर्वाङ्गी
पीड्य शेते शुभस्तनी ।
चिरस्य रमणं लब्ध्वा
परिष्वज्येव भामिनी ॥
Another, of beautiful figure and lovely breasts, slept hugging tightly the Paṭaha instrument as if it were her lover with whom she was united after a long time.
5.10.40
काचिद्वंशं परिष्वज्य
सुप्ता कमललोचना ।
रहः प्रियतमं गृह्य
सकामेव च कामिनी ॥
Another, of lotus eyes, slept hugging a flute, as if she fell asleep, caught in deep passion with her dearest lover in a secret place.
5.10.41
विपञ्चीं परिगृह्यान्या
नियता नृत्तशालिनी ।
निद्रावशमनुप्राप्ता
सह कान्तेव भामिनी ॥
Another, a highly skilled dancer, took a seven stringed Veeṇa and fell asleep hugging it, as she would with a lover.
5.10.42
अन्या कनकसङ्काशैः
मृदुपीनैर्मनोरमैः ।
मृदङ्गं परिपीड्याङ्गैः
प्रसुप्ता मत्तलोचना ॥
Another, of golden complexion and soft, plump and lovely limbs, was sleeping, tightly hugging a Mṛdaṅga, her eyelids closed under the weight of intoxication.
5.10.43
भुजपार्श्वान्तरस्थेन
कक्षगेन कृशोदरी ।
पणवेव सहानिन्द्या
सुप्ता मदकृतश्रमा ॥
Another, a flawless one with a slender waist, exhausted with intoxication, held the instrument Paṇava under her arms to her side and fell asleep.
5.10.44
डिण्डिमं परिगृह्यान्या
तथैवासक्तडिण्डिमा ।
प्रसुप्ता तरुणं वत्सम्
उपगूह्येव भामिनी ॥
Another woman hugged the instrument Ḍiṇḍima that was beside her like she would her young son, and fell asleep.
5.10.45
काचिदाडम्बरं नारी
भुजसंयोगपीडितम् ।
कृत्वा कमलपत्राक्षी
प्रसुप्ता मदमोहिता ॥
Another woman of lotus eyes fell asleep overpowered by intoxication, holding the instrument Āḍambara tightly in her arms.
5.10.46
कलशीमपविध्यान्या
प्रसुप्ता भाति भामिनी ।
वसन्ते पुष्पशबला
मालेव परिमार्जिता ॥
Another woman who kicked a pitcher as she slept looked like a multi-colored garland that was sprinkled with water in spring (to keep it fresh).
5.10.47
पाणिभ्यां च कुचौ काचित्
सुवर्णकलशोपमौ ।
उपगूह्याबला सुप्ता
निद्राबलपराजिता ॥
Another slender one was overpowered by sleep and slept holding her breasts, that were like pots made of gold, with her hands.
5.10.48
अन्या कमलपत्राक्षी
पूर्णेन्दुसदृशानना ।
अन्यामालिङ्ग्य सुश्रोणीम्
प्रसुप्ता मदविह्वला ॥
Another one, of lotus petal-like eyes and face lovely as the full moon, slept, fuddled with drink, hugging another one of wide hips.
5.10.49
आतोद्यानि विचित्राणि
परिष्वज्य वरस्त्रियः ।
निपीड्य च कुचैस्सुप्ता
कामिन्यः कामुकानिव ॥
Thus the beautiful, impassioned women slept, with the various instruments which they had been playing pressed to their breasts as if they were their lovers.
5.10.50
तासामेकान्तविन्यस्ते
शयानां शयने शुभे ।
ददर्श रूपसम्पन्नाम्
अपरां स कपिः स्त्रियम् ॥
Then the Vānara saw a woman, of extraordinary beauty, sleeping on a beautiful bedstead that was set distinctly away from all others.
5.10.51
मुक्तामणिसमायुक्तैः
भूषणैः सुविभूषिताम् ।
विभूषयन्तीमिव तत्
स्वश्रिया भवनोत्तमम् ॥
Her own innate radiance combined with the dazzle of the ornaments studded with pearls and gems with which she was well decorated, seemed to brighten up that extraordinary apartment.
5.10.52
गौरीं कनकवर्णाभाम्
इष्टामन्तः पुरेश्वरीम् ।
कपिर्मन्दोदरीं तत्र
शयानां चारुरूपिणीम् ॥
That woman, a charming beauty of bright golden complexion, whom that Vānara saw there sleeping was Manḍōdaree, (Rāvaṇa’s) beloved, and empress of the inner apartments.
5.10.53
स तां दृष्ट्वा महाबाहुः
भूषितां मारुतात्मजः ।
तर्कयामास सीतेति
रूपयौवनसम्पदा ।
हर्षेण महता युक्तो
ननन्द हरियूथपः ॥
The son of Vāyu, of mighty arm, seeing how well she was adorned and how charming and youthful she looked, concluded to himself that she must be Seetā. The Vānara chief was filled with joy, overtaken by immense happiness.
5.10.54
आस्फोटयामास चुचुम्ब पुच्छं
ननन्द चिक्रीड जगौ जगाम ।
स्तम्भानरोहन्निपपात भूमौ
निदर्शयन् स्वां प्रकृतिं कपीनाम् ॥
Then he became delirious. He kissed his tail. He patted himself on his shoulders. He indulged in antics and sang. He moved about back and forth. He climbed up the pillars and jumped down true to his monkey nature.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे दशमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the tenth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
