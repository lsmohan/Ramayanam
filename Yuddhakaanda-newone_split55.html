Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 55
In this Sarga, hearing that Vajradaṃshṭra was slain by Aṅgada, Rāvaṇa sends Akampana to fight with the Vānaras. Akampana goes with a huge army, ignoring the bad omens he sees. A fierce battle ensues between Vānaras and Rākshasas.
6.55.1
वज्रदंष्ट्रं हतं श्रुत्वा
वालिपुत्रेण रावणः ।
बलाध्यक्षमुवाचेदम्
कृताञ्जलिमवस्थितम् ॥
Hearing that Vajradaṃshṭra was slain by the son of Vāli, Rāvaṇa told the commander of the army, who stood before him with folded hands:
6.55.2
शीघ्रं निर्यान्तु दुर्धर्षाः
राक्षसा भीमविक्रमाः ।
अकम्पनं पुरस्कृत्य
सर्वशस्त्रप्रकोविदम् ॥
Let the invincible Rākshasas of formidable prowess start immediately under the command of Akampana, who is a master of all Ṡastras.
6.55.3
एष शास्ता च गोप्ता च
नेता च युधि सम्मतः ।
भूतिकामश्च मे नित्यम्
नित्यं च समरप्रियः ॥
He is known as a great commander, protector and leader in a battle. He is ever devoted to my interest and always enjoys a fight.
6.55.4
एष जेष्यति काकुत्स्थौ
सुग्रीवं च महाबलम् ।
वानरांश्चापरान् घोरान्
हनिष्यति परन्तपः ॥
He will vanquish the scions of the Kākutsthas and Sugreeva of immense strength. He, scorcher of the foe, will slaughter all the other formidable Vānaras also.
6.55.5
परिगृह्य स तामाज्ञाम्
रावणस्य महाबलः ।
बलं सम्प्रेरयामास
तदा लघुपराक्रमः ॥
Following those orders of Rāvaṇa, he, of immense strength and swift prowess, spurred the army into action.
6.55.6
ततो नानाप्रहरणा
भीमाक्षा भीमदर्शनाः ।
निष्पेतू रक्षसां मुख्या
बलाध्यक्षप्रचोदिताः ॥
Many notable Rākshasas with intimidating eyes and a frightful appearance, armed with a variety of weapons, started, prodded by the commander of the forces.
6.55.7
रथमास्थाय विपुलम्
तप्तकाञ्चनभूषणम् ।
मेघाभो मेघवर्णश्च
मेघस्वनमहास्वनः ।
राक्षसैः संवृतो घोरैः
तदा निर्यात्यकम्पनः ॥
Then started Akampana himself, huge and black as a cloud and with a voice like thunder, surrounded by formidable Rākshasas, on a grand chariot treated with gold purified in fire.
6.55.8
न हि कम्पयितुं शक्यः
सुरैरपि महामृधे ।
अकम्पनस्ततस्तेषाम्
आदित्य इव तेजसा ॥
Powerful like the sun, Akampana could not be shaken in a fight even by the Dēvas.
6.55.9
तस्य निर्धावमानस्य
संरब्धस्य युयत्सया ।
अकस्माद्दैन्यमागच्छत्
हयानां रथवाहिनाम् ॥
As he dashed forth in haste, eager to attack, the horses that drew his chariot, all of a sudden, became dispirited.
6.55.10
व्यस्फुरन्नयनं चास्य
सव्यं युद्धाभिनन्दिनः ।
विवर्णो मुखवर्णश्च
गद्गदश्चाभवत् स्वनः ॥
(And equally suddenly), he, who was so keen on fighting, found that his left eye quivered, his face turned pale and his voice developed a quaver in it.
6.55.11
अभवत् सुदिने चापि
दुर्दिनं रूक्षमारुतम् ।
ऊचुः खगा मृगाः सर्वे
वाचः क्रूरा भयावहाः ॥
That fine day suddenly turned out to be a nasty one with a fierce gale. All the birds and beasts uttered terrible and eerie cries.
6.55.12
स सिंहोपचितस्कन्धः
शार्दूलसमविक्रमः ।
तानुत्पातानचिन्त्यैव
निर्जगाम रणाजिरम् ॥
But he, whose shoulders were as imposing as those of a lion, and whose stride recalled that of a tiger, marched on to the battle field, ignoring those evil portents.
6.55.13
तदा निर्गच्छतस्तस्य
रक्षसः सह राक्षसैः ।
बभूव सुमहान्नादः
क्षोभयन्निव सागरम् ॥
As the Rākshasa marched on along with his Rākshasas, there arose a tremendous din that seemed to put the sea in a turmoil.
6.55.14
तेन शब्देन वित्रस्ता
वानराणां महाचमूः ।
द्रुमशैलप्रहरणा
योद्धुं समवतिष्ठत ॥
The great army of Vānaras, alarmed by that din, took their stand, ready to fight with trees and rocks.
6.55.15
तेषां युद्धं महारौद्रम्
सञ्जज्ञे हरिरक्षसाम् ।
रामरावणयोरर्थे
समभित्यक्तजीविनाम् ॥
There ensued a fierce battle between Vānaras and Rākshasas, who were prepared to throw away their lives in the cause of Rāma and Rāvaṇa.
6.55.16
सर्वे ह्यति बलाः शूराः
सर्वे पर्वतसन्निभाः ।
हरयो राक्षसाश्चैव
परस्परजिघांसवः ॥
The Vānaras and the Rākshasas, all of them exceedingly strong, brave and mountainous in size, were bent upon slaughtering each other.
6.55.17
तेषां विनर्दतां शब्दः
संयुगेऽतितरस्विनाम् ।
सुश्रुवे सुमहान् क्रोधात्
अन्योन्यमभिगर्जताम् ॥
Then, there was heard a huge din on the battle field from the roar of those powerful warriors as they shouted at each other in wrath.
6.55.18
रजश्चारुणवर्णाभम्
सुभीममभवद्भृशम् ।
उद्धतं हरिरक्षोभिः
संरुरोध दिशो दश ॥
Then there arose tawny-colored dust kicked up by the heels of the Vānaras and the Rākshasas eerily blotting out the ten directions.
6.55.19
अन्योन्यं रजसा तेन
कौशेयोद्धूतपाण्डुना ।
संवृतानि च भूतानि
ददृशुर्न रणाजिरे ॥
The dust, which rose up like a white silk cloth, covered all the creatures; and no one could see the others on the battlefield.
6.55.20
न ध्वजा न पताका वा
चर्म वा तुरगोऽपि वा ।
आयुधं स्यन्दनं वाऽपि
ददृशे तेन रेणुना ॥
Because of that dust, neither banners nor flags, neither coat of mail nor horse, neither weapon nor chariot, could be seen.
6.55.21
शब्दश्च सुमहांस्तेषाम्
नर्दतामभिधावताम् ।
श्रूयते तुमुले युद्धे
न रूपाणि चकाशिरे ॥
No one was visible; only the mighty din of their shouts as they attacked each other could be heard, in that tumultuous battle.
6.55.22
हरीनेव सुसङ्क्रुद्धा
हरयो जघ्नुराहवे ।
राक्षसाश्चापि रक्षांसि
निजघ्नुस्तिमिरे तदा ॥
Then, in their indiscriminate wrath, and in that battle fought in darkness the Vānaras killed Vānaras, and the Rākshasas, their own Rākshasas.
6.55.23
परांश्चैव विनिघ्नन्तः
स्वांश्च वानरराक्षसाः ।
रुधिरार्द्रां तदा चक्रुः
महीं पङ्कानुलेपनाम् ॥
Killing foe and friend alike, the Vānaras and Rākshasas drenched the ground with blood and smeared it with mire.
6.55.24
ततस्तु रुधिरौघेण
सिक्तं व्यपगतं रजः ।
शरीरशवसङ्कीर्णा
बभूव च वसुन्धरा ॥
The dust settled with the ground drenched in blood, and the ground was packed with corpses and (severed) bodies.
6.55.25
द्रुमशक्तिगदाप्रासैः
शिलापरिघतोमरैः ।
हरयो राक्षसाश्चैव
जघ्नुरन्योन्यमोजसा ॥
The Vānaras and Rākshasas struck each other with trees, rocks and Prāsas, Parighas, Tōmaras, javelins, maces, with all their power.
6.55.26
बाहुभिः परिघाकारैः
युद्ध्यन्तः पर्वतोपमाः ।
हरयो भीमकर्माणः
राक्षसान् जघ्नुराहवे ॥
The Vānaras, huge as mountains, with arms strong as Parighas, capable of fearsome feats, slew the Rākshasas in that battle.
6.55.27
राक्षसास्त्वपि सङ्क्रुद्धाः
प्रासतोमरपाणयः ।
कपीन्निजघ्निरे तत्र
शस्त्रैः परमदारुणैः ॥
The Rākshasas too, wielding extremely terrible Ṡastras like Prāsas and Tōmaras slaughtered the Vānaras in rage.
6.55.28
अकम्पनः सुसङ्क्रुद्धो
राक्षसानां चमूपतिः ।
संहर्षयति तान् सर्वान्
राक्षसान् भीमविक्रमान् ॥
Akampana, the commander of the Rākshasa army, showing his wrath (at the Vānaras), cheered up all those Rākshasas of terrific prowess.
6.55.29
हरयस्त्वपि रक्षांसि
महाद्रुममहाश्मभिः ।
विदारयन्त्यभिक्रम्य
शस्त्राण्याच्छिद्य वीर्यतः ॥
The Vānaras pounced on the Rākshasas, snatched their weapons, and showing their valor, tore them apart with huge trees and huge rocks.
6.55.30
एतस्मिन्नन्तरे वीरा
हरयः कुमुदो नलः ।
मैन्दश्च द्विविदः क्रुद्धाः
चक्रुर्वेगमनुत्तमम् ॥
Meanwhile, the Vānara Veeras Kumuda, Nala, Mainda and Dwivida, revved up their assault like never seen before.
6.55.31
ते तु वृक्षैर्महावेगा
राक्षसानां चमूमुखे ।
कदनं सुमहच्चक्रुः
लीलया हरियूथपाः ।
ममन्थू राक्षसान् सर्वे
वानरा गणशो भृशम् ॥
Armed with trees, the great captains of the Vānara forces wrought havoc on the enemy, as if it was child’s play. All the Vānaras pounded the Rākshasas mercilessly, taking quite a few of them on at a time.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे पञ्चपञ्चाशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty second Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
