Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 72
In this Sarga, hearing that Atikāya is also slain, just as the strong Rākshasas like Dhūmrāksha, Akampana, Prahasta and Kumbhakarṇa already were, Rāvaṇa feels miserable and comes to terms with the fact that Rāma and Lakshmaṇa are extraordinary, as they could also free themselves from the bonds of Indrajit's arrows. He is unable to think of anyone else who can destroy Rāma and his army.
He instructs the Rākshasas to keep guard everywhere in the city, and a vigil on the movements of the Vānaras.
6.72.1
अतिकायं हतं श्रुत्वा
लक्ष्मणेन महौजसा ।
उद्वेगमगमद्राजा
वचनं चेदमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing that Atikāya was slain by Lakshmaṇa of extraordinary energy, the king was shaken and said these words:
6.72.2
धूभ्राक्षः परमामर्षी
धन्वी शस्त्रभृतां वरः ।
अकम्पनः प्रहस्तश्च
कुम्भकर्णस्तथैव च ।
एते महाबला वीरा
राक्षसा युद्धकाङ्क्षिणः ।
जेतारः परसैन्यानाम्
परैर्नित्यापराजिताः ।
निहतास्ते महावीर्या
रामेणाक्लिष्टकर्मणा ॥
Dhūmrāksha, foremost of bowmen, whose wrath was formidable, and Akampana, Prahasta and Kumbhakarṇa were destroyed, along with their armies, by Rāma, who does things with ease.
These Rākshasas of immense strength, ever thirsting for fight, were valiant, and only knew winning over the enemy forces, and defeated their enemies as a matter of routine.
6.72.4
राक्षसाः सुमहाकाया
नानाशस्त्रविशारदाः ।
अन्ये च बहवः शूरा
महात्मानो निपातिताः ॥
Many other heroic Rākshasas, Mahātmas of huge bodies, savants of all kinds of Ṡastras, were also struck down.
6.72.5
प्रख्यातबलवीर्येण
पुत्रेणेन्द्रजिता मम ।
यौ हि तौ भ्रातरौ वीरौ
बद्धौ दत्तवरैः शरैः ।
यन्न शक्यं सुरैः सर्वैः
असुरैर्वा महाबलैः ।
मोक्तुं तद्बन्धनं घोरम्
यक्षगन्धर्वकिन्नरैः ।
तन्न जाने प्रभावैर्वा
मायया मोहनेन वा ।
शरबन्धाद्विमुक्तौ तौ
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
The two brothers were bound by my son Indrajit, who is renowned for his strength and valor, with arrows that had been endowed with special powers by the boon of the Dēvas, from the dreaded bonds of which no Sura or Asura, however strong they might be, and no Yaksha, Gandharva or Pannaga, could free oneself.
I do not know what power or magic or trickery those two brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa have, to free themselves from the bonds of those arrows.
6.72.8
ये योधा निर्गताः शूरा
राक्षसा मम शासनात् ।
ते सर्वे निहता युद्धे
वानरैः सुमहाबलैः ॥
Every one of the warriors that went to fight by my command has been killed in the battle by Vānaras, surely of extraordinary strength.
6.72.9
तं न पश्याम्यहं युद्धे
योऽद्य रामं सलक्ष्मणम् ।
नाशयेत् सबलं वीरम्
ससुग्रीवविभीषणम् ॥
I cannot find anyone today who can destroy Rāma the Veera, along with Lakshmaṇa, Sugreeva, Vibheeshaṇa and the army.
6.72.10
अहो नु बलवान् रामो
महदस्त्रबलं च वै ।
यस्य विक्रममासाद्य
राक्षसा निधनं गताः ॥
Ah! What tremendous strength and mastery of great Astras does Rāma have, unable to face whose prowess, the Rākshasas have met their end.
6.72.11
तद्भयाद्धि पुरी लङ्का
पिहितद्वारतोरणा ।
अप्रमत्तैश्च सर्वत्र
गुप्तै रक्ष्या पुरी त्वियम् ॥
It is for the fear of him all the doors and gateways of this city of Laṅkā are kept locked. And, let this city be protected everywhere by the troops in high alert.
6.72.12
अशोकवनिकायां च
यत्र सीताऽभिरक्ष्यते ।
निष्क्रामो वा प्रवेशो वा
ज्ञातव्यः सर्वदैव नः ॥
Seetā also should be guarded in the Aṡōka Vana and we must know who is going out or coming in at any time.
6.72.13
यत्र यत्र भवेद् गुल्मः
तत्रतत्र पुनःपुनः ।
सर्वतश्चापि तिष्ठध्वम्
स्वैःस्वैः परिवृता बलैः ॥
And the troops everywhere must be reinforced by you, as you take take position everywhere supported by your respective forces.
6.72.14
द्रष्टव्यं च पदं तेषाम्
वानराणां निशाचराः ।
प्रदोषे वाऽर्धरात्रे वा
प्रत्यूषे वापि सर्वतः ॥
Vigil must be kept on the movements of the Vānaras, O Night-rovers, even in the evenings, middle of the night and early hours of the day!
6.72.15
नावज्ञा तत्र कर्तव्या
वानरेषु कदाचन ।
द्विषतां बलमुद्युक्तम्
आपतत् किं स्थितं सदा ॥
Never underestimate the Vānaras. Beware whether the enemy forces are stationary or getting ready for an attack or already on the move to attack.
6.72.16
ततस्ते राक्षसाः सर्वे
श्रुत्वा लङ्काधिपस्य तत् ।
वचनं सर्वमातिष्ठन्
यथावत्तु महाबलाः ॥
Hearing those words of the lord of Laṅkā, all the Rākshasas of immense strength, took positions, as were told.
6.72.17
स तान् सर्वान् समादिश्य
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ।
मन्युशल्यं वहन् दीनः
प्रविवेश स्वमालयम् ॥
Having instructed them thus, Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, dejected and tormented by agony, retired to his own residence.
6.72.18
ततः स सन्दीपितकोपवह्निः
निशाचराणामधिपो महाबलः ।
तदेव पुत्रव्यसनं विचिन्तयन्
मुहुर्मुहुश्चैव तदा व्यनिश्वसत् ॥
With the fire of anger raging in him, the Lord of Night-rovers, of immense strength, heaved deep sighs again and again, his thoughts occupied by the fate met by his sons.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्विसप्ततितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventy second Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
