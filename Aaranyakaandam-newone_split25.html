Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 25
In this Sarga, the fearsome battle starts between Khara’s army and Rāma. Khara’s army rains showers of weapons, arrows, boulders and trees. Rāma breaks them into pieces and cuts off the heads, arms, thighs of the army. Rākshasas by the thousands fall dead. The surviving Rākshasas run to Dūshaṇa seeking refuge. Dūshaṇa consoles them and rallies them again against Rāma.
3.25.1
अवष्टब्धधनुं रामं
क्रुद्धं च रिपुघातिनम् ।
ददर्शाऽश्रममागम्य
खरस्सह पुरस्सरैः ॥
Khara came to the Āṡrama. He and his followers who were walking ahead with him saw Rāma in ire, ready to kill the enemy, with his bow held upright.
3.25.2
तं दृष्ट्वा सशरं चापम्
उद्यम्य खरनिस्स्वनम् ।
रामस्याभिमुखं सूतं
चोद्यतामित्यचोदयत् ॥
On seeing Rāma, Khara lifted his bow with the arrow (mounted on it), and rumbling harshly, he told his charioteer to drive up to Rāma.
3.25.3
स खरस्याज्ञया सूतः
तुरगान् समचोदयत् ।
यत्र रामो महाबाहुः
एको धून्वन्स्थितो धनुः ॥
Following the order of Khara, the charioteer goaded the horses on to where Rāma of the mighty arm stood twanging his bow.
3.25.4
तं तु निष्पतितं दृष्ट्वा
सर्वे ते रजनीचराः ।
नर्दमाना महानादं
सचिवाः पर्यवारयन् ॥
Seeing him ready for the fray, all of his night-roving ministers rallied around him and let off loud war-whoops.
3.25.5
स तेषां यातुधानानां
मध्ये रथगतः खरः ।
बभूव मध्ये ताराणां
लोहिताङ्ग इवोदितः ॥
Khara, seated in his chariot among the Rākshasas, looked like the planet Mars risen among the constellations.
3.25.6
ततश्शरसहस्रेण
राममप्रतिमौजसम् ।
अर्दयित्वा महानादं
ननाद समरे खरः ॥
Irking Rāma of unequalled strength with thousands of arrows, Khara let off a mighty yell, in that fight.
3.25.7
ततस्तं भीमधन्वानं
क्रुद्धाः सर्वे निशाचराः ।
रामं नानाविधैः शस्स्रैः
अभ्यवर्षन्त दुर्जयम् ॥
And all those Night-rovers in their rage rained down upon the invincible Rāma of the terrible bow, a shower of weapons of every kind.
3.25.8
मुद्गरैः पट्टसैश्शूलैः
प्रासैः खङ्गै परश्वथैः ।
राक्षसास्समरे रामं
निजघ्नू रोषतत्पराः ॥
In all their fury, the Rākshasas fought by hitting Rāma with Mudgaras, Paṭṭiṡas, spears, Prasas and Paraṡus.
3.25.9
ते वलाहकसङ्काशा
महानादा महौजसः ।
अभ्यधावन्त काकुत्स्थं
रथैर्वाजिभिरेव च ।
गजैः पर्वतकूटाभै
रामं युद्धे जिघांसवः ॥
Those Rākshasas of immense strength, gigantic like clouds, mounted on chariots, horses and elephants that were as big as mountain crests, dashed towards Rāma to slay him, making loud noises.
3.25.10
ते रामे शरवर्षाणि
व्यसृजन् रक्षसां गणाः ।
शैलेन्द्रमिव धाराभिः
वर्षमाणा वलाहकाः ॥
The hordes of Rākshasas rained showers of arrows on Rāma, like clouds battering a mountain with torrential rains.
3.25.11
स तैः परिवृतो घोरैः
राघवो रक्षसां गणैः ।
तिथिष्विव महादेवो
वृतः पारिषदाङ्गणैः ॥
Surrounded by those terrible Rākshasa hordes from all directions, Rāghava looked liked the Mahā Dēva surrounded by the Gaṇas, his followers.
3.25.12
तानि मुक्तानि शस्त्राणि
यातुधानैस्सराघवः ।
प्रतिजग्राह विशिखैः
नद्योघानिव सागरः ॥
Rāghava took in all those weapons hurled by the Rākshasas, with his (torrents of) arrows, as the ocean would take in the inflow of rivers.
3.25.13
स तैः प्रहरणैर्घोरैः
भिन्नगात्रो न विव्यथे ।
रामः प्रदीप्तैर्बहुभिः
वज्रैरिव महाचलः ॥
Though his body was pierced by those horrid weapons, Rāma was no more distraught by them than a great mountain struck by many a shining thunderbolt would.
3.25.14
स विद्धः क्षतजादिग्धः
सर्वगात्रेषु राघवः ।
बभूव रामः सन्ध्याभ्रैः
दिवाकर इवावृतः ॥
Hit in every part of the body and bleeding, Rāghava looked like the sun covered by the evening clouds.
3.25.15
विषेदुर्देवगन्धर्वाः
सिद्धाश्च परमर्षयः ।
एकं सहस्रैर्बहुभिः
तदा दृष्ट्वा समावृतम् ॥
Seeing one man thus hemmed in by many thousands, Dēvas, Gandharvas, Siddhas and the great Ṛshis felt sad.
3.25.16
ततो रामस्सुसङ्कृद्धो
मण्डलीकृतकार्मुकः ।
ससर्ज निशितान्बाणान्
शतशोऽथ सहस्रश ॥
Then, Rāma, extremely enraged, bent his bow almost to a semi-circle and shot hundreds and thousands of sharp arrows.
3.25.17
दुरावारान्दुर्विषहान्
कालदण्डोपमान् रणे ।
मुमोच लीलया रामः
कङ्कपत्रानजिह्मगान् ॥
Rāma, in that fight, shot arrows that were winged with feathers of the falcon and comparable to the rod of Death, effortlessly. They flew straight and could not be stopped and were extremely unbearable.
3.25.18
ते शराश्शत्रुसैन्येषु
मुक्ता रामेण लीलया ।
आददू रक्षसां प्राणान्
पाशाः कालकृता इव ॥
Those arrows, playfully let loose by Rāma among the army of the enemy, took the lives of the Rākshasas like the noose of Death would.
3.25.19
भित्त्वा राक्षसदेहांस्तान्
ते शरा रुधिराप्लुताः ।
अन्तरिक्षगता रेजुः
दीप्ताग्निसमतेजसः ॥
Splitting the bodies of the Rākshasas, those arrows, bathed in blood, flew into the air with the glow of the blaze of a fire.
3.25.20
असङ्ख्येयास्तु रामस्य
सायकाश्चापमण्डलात् ।
विनिष्पेतुरतीवोग्रा
रक्षःप्राणापहारिणः ॥
Innumerable were the extremely terrible arrows that emerged from the Rāma’s bow that was bent into a semi-circle, taking the lives of the Rākshasas.
3.25.21
तैर्धनूंषि ध्वजाग्राणि
वर्माणि च शिरांसि च ।
बाहून्सहस्ताभरणान्
ऊरून्करिकरोपमान् ।
चिच्छेद रामस्समरे
शतशोऽथ सहस्रशः ॥
With those arrows, Rāma, in that fight, cut off, by the hundreds and thousands, bows, the tops of pennants, armor, heads, arms decorated with ornaments, and thighs that resembled the trunk of an elephant.
3.25.23
हयान्काञ्चनसन्नाहान्
रथयुक्तान्ससारथीन् ।
गजांश्च सगजारोहान्
सहयान्सादिनस्तथा ।
पदातीन्समरे हत्वा
ह्यनयद्यमसादनम् ॥
He sent away to the abode of Death horses with gold trappings yoked to chariots along with the charioteers, elephants and the elephant-riders, horses and the horse-riders and also the foot-soldiers.
3.25.24
ततो नालीकनाराचैः
तीक्ष्णाग्रैश्च विकर्णिभिः ।
भीममार्तस्वरं चक्रुः
भिध्यमाना निशाचराः ॥
Shot by pipe-shaft arrows, iron-shaft arrows, and twisted-head arrows, the Night-rovers let deafening wails in agony.
3.25.25
तत्सैन्यं निशितैर्बाणैः
अर्दितं मर्मभेदिभिः ।
रामेण न सुखं लेभे
शुष्कं वनमिवाग्निना ॥
That army, harrowed by Rāma’s sharp arrows that pierced their vitals, could find no respite, like a dry patch of woods that caught fire would not.
3.25.26
केचिद्भीमबलाश्शूराः
शूलान्खङ्गान्परश्वधान् ।
रामस्याभिमुखं गत्वा
चिक्षिपुः परमायुधान् ॥
Only a few mighty strong warriors went towards Rāma and threw the best of their weapons, the spears, the swords and the Paraṡus.
3.25.27
तेषाम्  बाणैर्महाबाहुः
शस्त्राण्यावार्य राघवः ।
जहार समरे प्राणान्
चिच्छेद च शिरोधरान् ॥
Rāghava of the mighty arm warded off their weapons with his arrows, in that fight, and slew them by severing their necks.
3.25.28
ते छिन्नशिरसः पेतुः
छिन्नवर्मशरासनाः ।
सुपर्णवातविक्षिप्ता
जगत्यां पादपा यथा ॥
They tumbled to the ground, with their heads, armors and bows cut off, like trees uprooted by the gush of wind from the wings of Suparṇa.
Suparṇa is another name for Garuḍa.
3.25.29
अवशिष्टाश्च ये तत्र
विषण्णाश्च निशाचराः ।
खरमेवाभ्यधावन्त
शरणार्थं शरार्दिताः ॥
The Night-rovers that were left there ran in despair to Khara seeking refuge, harrowed by the arrows.
3.25.30
तान्सर्वान्पुनरादाय
समाश्वास्य च दूषणः ।
अभ्यधावत काकुत्स्थं
क्रुद्धो रुद्रमिवान्तकः ॥
Dūshaṇa consoled them and rallying them again, dashed towards Kākutstha in great anger like the God of Death towards Rudra.
3.25.31
निवृत्तास्तु पुनस्सर्वे
दूषणाश्रयनिर्भयाः ।
राममेवाभ्यधावन्त
सालतालशिलायुधाः ॥
All of them, finding leadership in Dūshaṇa turned back and dashed towards Rāma armed with pine trees, palm trees and boulders.
3.25.32
शूलमुद्गरहस्ताश्च
चापहस्ता महाबलाः ।
सृजन्तश्शरवर्षाणि
शस्त्रवर्षाणि संयुगे ।
द्रुमवर्षाणि मुञ्चन्तः
शिलावर्षाणि राक्षसाः ॥
The Rākshasas, wielding spears, Mudgaras and bows fought, raining showers of arrows, Ṡastras, trees and boulders.
3.25.34
तद्बभूवाद्भुतं युद्धं
तुमुलं रोमहर्षणम् ।
रामस्य च महाघोरं
पुनस्तेषां च रक्षसाम् ॥
And again, the amazing, hair-rising and tumultuous fight between Rāma and Rākshasas became terribly gruesome.
3.25.35
ते समन्तादतिक्रुद्धा
राघवं पुनरभ्ययुः ।
तैश्च सर्वा दिशो दृष्ट्वा
प्रदिशश्च समावृताः ।
राक्षसैरुद्यतप्रासैः
शरवर्षाभिवर्षिभिः ।
स कृत्वा भैरवं नादम्
अस्त्रं परमभास्वरम् ।
संयोजयत गान्धर्वं
राक्षसेषु महाबलः ॥
They attacked Rāghava from all sides, in all fury. Seeing the Rākshasas with raised Prāsas hem from all the four sides and in between, raining down showers of arrows, he of immense strength let off a terrifying shout and cast the blazing Gandharva Astra on those Rākshasas.
3.25.37
ततश्शरसहस्राणि
निर्ययुश्चापमण्डलात् ।
सर्वा दश दिशो बाणैः
आवार्यन्त समागतैः ॥
Then a flood of thousands of arrows emerged from the bow that was bent to a semi-circle. Then all the ten directions were covered with those arrows.
This is the first time we get to witness Rāma’s larger than life fury, strength and his extra-ordinary weapons.
3.25.38
नाददानं शरान्घोरान्
नमुञ्चन्तं शिलीमुखान् ।
विकर्षमाणं पश्यन्ति
राक्षसास्ते शरार्दिताः ॥
The Rākshasas, harried by the arrows, could not see him taking those deadly arrows or drawing the string or shooting them.
3.25.39
शरान्धकारमाकाशम्
आवृणोत्सदिवाकरम् ।
बभूवावस्थितो रामः
प्रवमन्निव ताञ्छरान् ॥
The darkness caused by the arrows blotted out the sky and the sun. It seemed as if Rāma was spewing out the arrows.
3.25.40
युगपत्पतमानैश्च
युगपच्च हतैर्भृशम् ।
युगपत्पतितैश्चैव
विकीर्णा वसुधा भवत् ॥
The earth was entirely filled at once with those that were falling, those that had already fell and those that were long dead.
3.25.41
निहताः पतिताः क्षीणाः
छिन्ना भिन्ना विदारिताः ।
तत्र तत्र स्म दृश्यन्ते
राक्षसास्ते सहस्रशः ॥
The Rākshasas in their thousands, were seen all over the place hit, fallen, losing life, torn apart and broken apart.
3.25.42
सोष्णीषैरुत्तमाङ्गैश्च
साङ्गदैर्बाहुभिस्तथा ।
ऊरुभिर्जानुभिश्छिन्नैः
नानारूपैविभूषणैः ।
हयैश्च द्विपमुख्यैश्च
रथैर्भिन्नैरनेकशः ।
चामरैर्व्यजनैश्छत्रैः
ध्वजैर्नानाविधैरपि ।
रामस्य बाणाभिहतैः
विचित्रैश्शूलपट्टिसैः ।
खङ्गै खण्डीकृतैः प्रासैः
विकीर्णैश्च परश्वथैः ।
चूर्णिताभिश्शिलाभिश्च
शरैश्चित्रैरनेकशः ।
विच्छिन्नैस्समरे भूमिः
विकीर्णाऽभूद्भयङ्करा ॥
The battle ground was a terrible sight, strewn thick with heads with turbans on, arms with their armlets, and hacked thighs and knees adorned with various ornaments, with horses, excellent elephants and broken chariots; with many kinds of fly-whisks and fans, umbrellas, pennants, assorted spears and Paṭṭiṡas and swords and Prāsas broken into pieces, and Paraṡus scattered all around, and boulders smashed into powder and arrows broken out of shape, hit by Rāma’s arrows.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे पञ्चविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty fifth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
