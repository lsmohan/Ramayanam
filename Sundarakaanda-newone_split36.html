Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 36
In this Sarga, Hanumān gives the ring with the name of Rāma inscribed on it to Seetā. Seeing it, she feels as if it were Rāma himself who has come to her. Then she affectionately praises Hanumān for crossing the hundred Yōjanas-wide ocean and penetrating into the city of Laṅkā all by himself, without the fear of Rāvaṇa.
She inquires once again about the wellbeing of Rāma and hopes that Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva would soon come, and Bharata would send his armies, to free Lanka of Rākshasas.
Hanumān assures her that Rāma would come very soon, destroy the Rākshasas and regain her. He tells that Rāma is equally submerged in grief over her loss, that he does not even sleep well, thinking of her all the time. He assures her that Rāma is making every effort and would lose no time in taking action once he hears of her whereabouts from him.
Seeing the ring and hearing those reassuring words, her face shows the calmness of the early post-monsoon nights well-lit by the moon, but with a few clouds still lingering.
5.36.1
भूय एव महातेजा
हनूमान्पवनात्मजः ।
अब्रवीत्प्रश्रितं वाक्यम्
सीताप्रत्ययकारणात् ॥
Once again, the immensely powerful Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, said these words, in all humbleness, to instill confidence in Seetā.
5.36.2
वानरोऽहं महाभागे
दूतो रामस्य धीमतः ।
रामनामाङ्कितं चेदम्
पश्य देव्यङ्गुलीयकम् ॥
O blessed one! I, a Vānara, is (truly) the envoy of the sagacious Rāma! Look at this ring, O queen, that is imprinted with the name of Rāma!
5.36.3
प्रत्ययार्थं तवाऽनीतम्
तेन दत्तं महात्मना ।
समाश्वसिहि भद्रं ते
क्षीणदुःखफला ह्यसि ॥
I brought this, given to me by that Mahātma, to convince you. Be comforted, and may all bode well for you! Soon your sorrow will come to an end.
5.36.4
गृहीत्वा प्रेक्षमाणा सा
भर्तुः करविभूषणम् ।
भर्तारमिव सम्प्राप्ता
जानकी मुदिताऽभवत् ॥
Taking it and looking at it, which used to adorn the hand of her husband, Jānaki felt very happy, as if it were her husband himself who had arrived.
5.36.5
चारु तद्वदनं तस्याः
ताम्रशुक्लायतेक्षणम् ।
अशोभत विशालाक्ष्या
राहुमुक्त इवोडुराट् ॥
Her beautiful face with wide, white copper-tinged eyes glowed like the king of the night relieved from the grip of Rāhu.
King of the night refers to the moon.
5.36.6
ततस्सा ह्रीमती बाला
भर्तृसन्देशहर्षिता ।
परितुष्टा प्रियं कृत्वा
प्रशशंस महाकपिम् ॥
Then, she of childlike disposition, blushed, feeling happy on receiving that message from her husband. Immensely pleased, she then praised the great Vānara affectionately.
5.36.7
विक्रान्तस्त्वं समर्थस्त्वम्
प्राज्ञस्त्वं वानरोत्तम ।
येनेदं राक्षसपदम्
त्वयैकेन प्रधर्षितम् ॥
You are truly powerful! You are indeed capable! O best of Vānaras, you are very talented! For, you are able to penetrate into this place of Rākshasas all by yourself.
5.36.8
शतयोजनविस्तीर्णः
सागरो मकरालयः ।
विक्रमश्लाघनीयेन
क्रमता गोष्पदीकृतः ॥
With your praise-worthy valor you reduced the hundred Yōjanas wide ocean infested with crocodiles into (the size of) a footprint of a cow.
5.36.9
न हि त्वां प्राकृतं मन्ये
वानरं वानरर्षभ ।
यस्य ते नास्ति सन्त्रासो
रावणान्नापि सम्भ्रमः ॥
I do not think that you are an ordinary Vānara, O bull among Vānaras, for you have no fear and are not perturbed, even regarding someone like Rāvaṇa!
5.36.10
अर्हसे च कपिश्रेष्ठ
मया समभिभाषितुम् ।
यद्यसि प्रेषितस्तेन
रामेण विदितात्मना ॥
Since Rāma, who knows well what he wants, has sent you, you surely must be the right one, O best of Vānaras, to engage in a conversation with me.
5.36.11
प्रेषयिष्यति दुर्धर्षो
रामो न ह्यपरीक्षितम् ।
पराक्रममविज्ञाय
मत्सकाशं विशेषतः ॥
Rāma, the formidable one, would not have sent you without checking who you are and without knowing about your valor, especially to someone like me.
5.36.12
दिष्ट्या च कुशली रामो
धर्मात्मा सत्यसङ्गरः ।
लक्ष्मणश्च महातेजाः
सुमित्रानन्दवर्धनः ॥
By the grace of Providence, Rāma, the Dharmātma, who is ever true to his word, is keeping well and also the immensely powerful Lakshmaṇa, who augurs the happiness of Sumitrā!
5.36.13
कुशली यदि काकुत्स्थः
किं नु सागरमेखलाम् ।
महीं दहति कोपेन
युगान्ताग्निरिवोत्थितः ॥
But then, why has Rāma, who is all well, not set the entire earth girdled by the oceans on fire with his rage, like the fire that rages at the conclusion of the Yuga?
5.36.14
अथवा शक्तिमन्तौ तौ
सुराणामपि निग्रहे ।
ममैव तु न दुःखानाम्
अस्ति मन्ये विपर्ययः ॥
Or, they (the brothers) might of course be powerful enough even to hold off the Dēvas; but it must be that my sorrows are not destined to end, I think.
5.36.15
कच्चिन्न व्यथितो रामः
कच्चिन्न परितप्यते ।
उत्तराणि च कार्याणि
कुरुते पुरुषोत्तमः ॥
I hope Rāma did not give himself up to suffering. I hope he did not cave in to complete misery. I hope that the foremost of the spirited beings is on the mission that he needs to pursue immediately.
5.36.16
कच्चिन्न दीनस्सम्भ्रान्तः
कार्येषु न च मुह्यति ।
कच्चित्पुरुषकार्याणि
कुरुते नृपतेस्सुतः ॥
I trust that the son of the king is not so depressed and dazed as to be remiss in action, or lacking in will or initiative.
5.36.17
द्विविधं त्रिविधोपायम्
उपायमपि सेवते ।
विजिगीषुस्सुहृत्कच्चित्
मित्रेषु च परन्तपः ॥
I hope Rāma is actively employing the two Upāyas and the three Upāyas! I hope the scorcher of the foe is on the offensive against his adversaries and maintaining good relationship with his allies!
The Upāyas are Sāma (conciliation), Dāna (offering), Bhēda (fissure) and Daṇḍa (militancy).
5.36.18
कच्चिन्मित्राणि लभते
मित्रैश्चाप्यभिगम्यते ।
कच्चित्कल्याणमित्त्रश्च
मित्त्त्रैश्चापि पुरस्कृतः ॥
I hope he is gaining new allies and more are seeking alliance with him! And I hope his allies mean well for him and hold him in good regard!
5.36.19
कच्चिदाशास्ति देवानाम्
प्रसादं पार्थिवात्मजः ।
कच्चित्पुरुषकारं च
दैवं च प्रतिपद्यते ॥
I hope the prince seeks the blessings of the Dēvas! I hope he puts his trust in his own efforts and in the will of Dēvas!
5.36.20
कच्चिन्न विगतस्नेहः
प्रवासान्मयि राघवः ।
कच्चिन्मां व्यसनादस्मात्
मोक्षयिष्यति वानर ॥
I hope Rāghava has not ceased to love me because I am far away. Will he ever redeem me from these travails?
5.36.21
सुखानामुचितो नित्यम्
असुखानामनौचितः ।
दुःखमुत्तरमासाद्य
कच्चिद्रामो न सीदति ॥
I hope Rāma, who deserves all comforts and is not used to any discomforts, is not sinking under the immense suffering!
5.36.22
कौसल्यायास्तथा कच्चित्
सुमित्रायास्तथैव च ।
अभीक्ष्णं श्रूयते कच्चित्
कुशलं भरतस्य च ॥
I hope he is constantly getting news about the the welfare of Kausalya, Sumitrā and Bharata!
5.36.23
मन्निमित्तेन मानार्हः
कच्चिच्छोकेन राघवः ।
कच्चिन्नान्यमना रामः
कच्चिन्मां तारयिष्यति ॥
I hope Rāma is holding up his dignity well in the face of the grief over my loss! I hope Rāma is not distracted with anything else and will save me (from this predicament)!
5.36.24
कच्चिदक्षौहिणीं भीमाम्
भरतो भ्रातृवत्सलः ।
ध्वजिनीं मन्त्रिभिर्गुप्ताम्
प्रेषयिष्यति मत्कृते ॥
I hope Bharata who loves his brother will send the formidable Akshouhiṇis of armies that are well managed by the ministers, for my sake!
5.36.25
वानराधिपतिश्शीमान्
सुग्रीवः कच्चिदेष्यति ।
मत्कृते हरिभिर्वीरैः
वृतो दन्तनखायुधैः ॥
I hope Sugreeva, the blessed Lord of Vānaras, will come for my sake, along with the valiant Vānaras equipped with fangs and nails for weapons!
5.36.26
कच्चिच्छ लक्ष्मणश्शूरः
सुमित्रानन्दवर्धनः ।
अस्त्रविच्छरजालेन
राक्षसान्विधमिष्यति ॥
I hope the valiant Lakshmaṇa, who augurs the joy of Sumitrā and is a master of all Astras destroys the Rākshasas with a host of arrows!
5.36.27
रौद्रेण कच्चिदस्त्रेण
ज्वलता निहतं रणे ।
द्रक्ष्याम्यल्पेन कालेन
रावणं ससुहृज्जनम् ॥
I hope to see before long Rāvaṇa and his ilk killed in a battle by fierce, flaming arrows.
5.36.28
कच्चिन्न तद्धेमसमानवर्णं
तस्याननं पद्मसमानगन्धि ।
मया विना शुष्यति शोकदीनम्
जलक्षये पद्ममिवातपेन ॥
I hope his face, that glows like gold and smells sweet like the lotus, is not wilting away in grief over my loss like a lotus under the scorching sun when the water dwindles!
5.36.29
धर्मापदेशात्त्यजतश्च राज्यं
मां चाप्यरण्यं नयतः पदातिम् ।
नासीद्व्यथा यस्य न भीर्न शोकः
कच्चित्स धैर्यं हृदये करोति ॥
Without worry, fear or sorrow, he gave up the kingdom and even made me go on foot to the forest for the sake of Dharma. I hope his heart continues to brim with the same grit.
5.36.30
न चास्य माता न पिता च नान्यः
स्नेहाद्विशिष्टोऽस्ति मया समो वा ।
तावत्त्वहं दूत जिजीविषेयं
यावत्प्रवृत्तिं शृणुयां प्रियस्य ॥
Neither his mother, nor his father, nor anyone else is dearer than, or as dear as, I am to him. O envoy! I shall hold on to this life so long as I continue to hear the wellbeing of my beloved!
5.36.31
इतीव देवी वचनं महार्थं
तं वानरेन्द्रं मधुरार्थमुक्त्वा ।
श्रोतुं पुनस्तस्य वचोऽभिरामं
रामार्थयुक्तं विरराम रामा ॥
Speaking these words that betrayed all poignancy and sweetness, to that lord of Vānaras, the queen paused, yearning to hear again, pleasant words about Rāma.
5.36.32
सीताया वचनं श्रुत्वा
मारुतिर्भीमविक्रमः ।
शिरस्यञ्जलिमाधाय
वाक्यमुत्तरमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing the words of Seetā, the son of Vāyu of terrific prowess responded with these words, with his joined palms placed above his head:
5.36.33
न त्वामिहस्थां जानीते
रामः कमललोचने ।
तेन त्वां नानयत्याशु
शचीमिव पुरन्दरः ॥
O you of lotus eyes! Rāma does not know that you are here. That is why he has not taken you away immediately, like Indra did Ṡaci.
5.36.34
मम श्रुत्वैव तु वचः
क्षिप्रमेष्यति राघवः ।
चमूं प्रकर्षन्महतीम्
हर्यृक्षगणसङ्कुलाम् ॥
The moment he hears the word from me, Rāghava will come immediately pulling together a mighty army brimming with Vānaras and Ṛkshas.
5.36.35
विष्टम्भयित्वा बाणौघैः
अक्षोभ्यं वरुणालयम् ।
करिष्यति पुरीं लङ्काम्
काकुत्स्थः शान्तराक्षसाम् ॥
Immobilizing the imperturbable abode of Varuṇa with a flood of arrows, Kākutstha will destroy the entire Rākshasa population of Laṅkā.
Abode of Varuṇa refers to the ocean.
5.36.36
तत्र यद्यन्तरा मृत्युः
यदि देवास्सहासुराः ।
स्थास्यन्ति पथि रामस्य
स तानपि वधिष्यति ॥
Rāma will kill even Dēvas and Asuras and even the lord of Death, if they come in his way.
5.36.37
तवादर्शनजेनार्ये
शोकेन स परिप्लुतः ।
न शर्म लभते रामः
सिंहार्दित इव द्विपः ॥
Deluged by the grief of not seeing you, O noble one, Rāma can find no more solace than an elephant beset by a lion.
5.36.38
मलयेन च विन्ध्येन
मेरुणा मन्दरेण च ।
दर्दुरेण च ते देवि
शपे मूलफलेन च ।
यथा सुनयनं वल्गु
बिम्बोष्ठं चारु दर्शनम् ।
मुखं द्रक्ष्यसि रामस्य
पूर्णचन्द्रमिवोदितम् ॥
This I swear to you, O Dēvi, by the Malaya and the Vindhya, and by the Mēru, the Mandara and the Dardura, and by the roots and fruits (on which I feed) that you will see Rāma’s handsome face with its lovely eyes, Bimba fruit-like lips, charming looks, bright like the rising full moon.
5.36.40
क्षिप्रं द्रक्ष्यसि वैदेहि
रामं प्रस्रवणे गिरौ ।
शतक्रतुमिवासीनम्
नागराजस्य मूर्धनि ॥
Soon you will see, O Vaidēhee, Rāma seated on the Prasravaṇa mountain, like Indra mounted on the king of elephants.
5.36.41
न मांसं राघवो भुङ्क्ते
न चाऽपि मधु सेवते ।
वन्यं सुविहितं नित्यम्
भक्तमश्नाति पञ्चमम् ॥
Rāghava eats no meat and drinks no mead; in the fifth watch of the day he just takes a well-prepared meal of forest produce.
5.36.42
नैव दंशान्न मशकान्
न कीटान्न सरीसृपान् ।
राघवोऽपनयेद्गात्रात्
वद्गतेनान्तरात्मना ॥
With his mind wholly absorbed in thoughts of you, he does not drive away from his body even the gnats and the mosquitoes, the insects and the creeping and crawling creatures.
5.36.43
नित्यं ध्यानपरो रामो
नित्यं शोकपरायणः ।
नान्यच्चिन्तयते किञ्चित्
स तु कामवशं गतः ॥
Rāma is in melancholy always. He is taken over by grief all the time. He does not think even a little of anything else, given to raging desire (for you).
5.36.44
अनिद्रस्सततं रामः
सुप्तोऽपि च नरोत्तमः ।
सीतेति मधुरां वाणीम्
व्याहरन्प्रतिबुध्यते ॥
Rāma never sleeps. Even if he falls asleep, that best of men wakes up calling ‘Seetā’ in a sweet voice.
5.36.45
दृष्ट्वा फलं वा पुष्पं वा
यद्वाऽन्यत्सुमनोहरम् ।
बहुशो हा प्रियेत्येवम्
श्वसंस्त्वामभिभाषते ॥
When he sees a fruit or flower or any other object that steals one’s heart, he sighs and speaks about you repeatedly.
5.36.46
स देवि नित्यं परितप्यमानः
त्वामेव सीतेत्यभिभाषमाणः ।
धृतव्रतो राजसुतो महात्मा
तवैव लाभाय कृतप्रयत्नः ॥
O Dēvi! He is perpetually tormented, speaking only about you saying ‘O Seetā!’. The prince, a Mahātma, with resolve, makes every effort to regain you.
5.36.47
सा रामसङ्कीर्तनवीतशोका
रामस्य शोकेन समानशोका ।
शरन्मुखे साम्बुदशेषचन्द्रा
निशेव वैदेहसुता बभूव ॥
The daughter of Vidēha kingdom, who was grieving equally as Rāma was gave up her sorrow and shone like the night well-lit by the moon in the early days of post monsoon season albeit with a few clouds still lingering.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे षट्त्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty sixth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
