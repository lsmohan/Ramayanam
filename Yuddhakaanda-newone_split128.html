Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 128
In this final Sarga of Yuddha Kāṇḍa Bharata officially hands over the kingdom back to Rāma, asserting how important it is for Ayōdhyā to be governed by one as capable as Rāma for it not to fall apart. He wishes him that he should reign over the kingdom as long as the planets and earth last. With it becoming clear that Bharata is giving the kingdom back wholeheartedly, Rāma accepts it.
Then attendants give a bath to Rāma, Bharata, Lakshmaṇa, Sugreeva, Vibheeshaṇa and disentangle the matted locks of Rāma. Then Rāma proceeds to Ayōdhyā in a splendid chariot, with Sugreeva, Hanumān, Seetā and the women of Sugreeva following him.
Meanwhile, the ministers in Ayōdhyā, urging the Purōhita to make arrangements for the coronation, proceed to meet Rāma. With Bharata holding the reins of the chariot, Ṡatrughna, the parasol, Lakshmaṇa and Vibheeshaṇa, fly-whisks, Rāma proceeds to Ayōdhyā, shining like Indra. Sugreeva follows on an elephant and the Vānaras, assuming human form, also follow on elephants.
Rāma reaches Ayōdhyā and is received by the people of Ayōdhyā, and feels immensely happy to see Ayōdhyā teeming with happy and prosperous folks. The people of Ayōdhyā are amazed hearing the stories of the accomplishments of Vānaras. Rāma reaches the palace that served as the residence of his father and all the Ikshwākus before him.
Sugreeva orders the Vānaras to fly with all speed and bring water from the four oceans and from hundreds of rivers, for the coronation. Vasishṭha, assisted by the Brāhmaṇas, seats Rāma and Seetā on a diamond-studded chair. Vasishṭha, Vāmadēva, Jābāli, Kaṡyapa, Katyāyana, Suyajña, Gautama and Vijaya, give them the ceremonial bath of coronation. Vāyu then presents him with a garland of hundred golden lotuses and another chain of pearls on behalf of Indra. Gandharvas sing and Apsaras dance.
Rāma then honors the Brāhmaṇas by giving them horses, cows, bulls, gold coins, ornaments and expensive clothes. Rāghava then presents a gold chain studded with gems to Sugreeva, a pair of lovely armlets to Aṅgada, and an unexcelled pearl necklace to Seetā, and a pair of spotless clothes to Hanumān. Seetā then gives the necklace that was given to her to Hanumān, in honor of his extra-ordinary power, skill, courage, ability, humility and intelligence. All the Vānaras are presented with gifts that they covet.
All the Vānaras take leave and reach Kishkindhā, and Vibheeshaṇa returns to Laṅkā.
Rāma then proposes that Lakshmaṇa should get installed as the crown-prince and bear the weight of the kingdom along with him. Lakshmaṇa refuses the offer in spite of all attempts to persuade him. Rāma then installs Bharata as the crown-prince.
Rāma rules the kingdom for ten thousand years, and over the course of that time, performs many Yajñas, giving oblations to Dēvas and honorariums to Brāhmaṇas.
People live happily and with all prosperity. Everyone adheres to Dharma, without harrying each other. Everyone live free from the fear of wild animals, disease and thieves, during the reign of Rāma. People of all the four Varṇas lead happy and contented lives, engaged in their respective activities.
6.128.1
शिरस्यञ्जलिमाधाय
कैकेय्यानन्दवर्धनः ।
बभाषे भरतो ज्येष्ठम्
रामं सत्यपराक्रमम् ॥
Raising his joined palms to his head, Bharata, the delight of Kaikēyee said to Rāma, his elder brother of true prowess:
6.128.2
पूजिता मामिका माता
दत्तं राज्यमिदं मम ।
तद्ददामि पुनस्तुभ्यम्
यथा त्वमददा मम ॥
My mother was honored by your giving this kingdom to me. I am giving it back to you, just as you have given it to me.
6.128.3
धुरमेकाकिना न्यस्ताम्
वृषभेण बलीयसा ।
किशोरवद्गुरुं भारम्
न वोढुमहमुत्सहे ॥
I do not like to venture to pull this heavy weight, any more than a calf would, to pull the weight that only a strong bull can.
6.128.4
वारिवेगेन महता
भिन्नः सेतुरिव क्षरन् ।
दुर्बन्धनमिदं मन्ये
राज्यच्छिद्रमसन्वृतम् ॥
I am convinced that this kingdom cannot be held together, With its vulnerabilities left uncovered, any more than a dam that is leaking through its cracks caused by a huge flood of water, can be.
6.128.5
गतिं खर इवाश्वस्य
हन्सस्येव च वायसः ।
नान्वेतुमुत्सहे वीर
तव मार्गमरिन्दम ॥
I do not aspire to tread in your footsteps, O Veera, O subduer of the foe, any more than a donkey can the horse's, or a crow can the swan's.
6.128.6
यथा च रोपितो वृक्षो
जातश्चान्तर्निवेशने ।
महानपि दुरारोहो
महास्कन्धः प्रशाखवान् ।
शीर्येत पुष्पितो भूत्वा
न फलानि प्रदर्शयेन् ।
तस्य नानुभवेदर्थम्
यस्य हेतोः स रोपितः ।
एषोपमा महाबाहो
त्वमर्थं वेत्तुमर्हसि ।
यद्यस्मान्मनुजेन्द्र त्वं
भर्ताभृत्यान्न शाधि हि ॥
O lord of peoples! If you do not take the reigns and lead us, your dependents, it would be like a tree planted in the backyard which grew to be so huge that cannot even be climbed, with a huge trunk and a multitude of branches, but not yielding any fruit even after flowering, essentially failing to serve the purpose for which it was planted. O you of mighty arm! I hope you understand (what I mean) by this metaphor!
6.128.9
जगदद्याभिषिक्तम्
त्वामनुपश्यतु राघव ।
प्रतपन्तमिवादित्यं
मध्याह्ने दीप्ततेजसम् ॥
May the world, O Rāghava, see you crowned to-day blazing with the fiery splendor of the midday sun.
6.128.10
तूर्यसङ्घातनिर्घोषैः
काञ्चीनूपुरनिस्वनैः ।
मधुरैर्गीतशब्दैश्च
प्रतिबुध्यस्व शेष्व च ॥
May you be woken up with the blowing of trumpets and the tinkling of girdles and anklets, and lulled into sleep with the most melodious songs and sounds.
6.128.11
यावदावर्तते चक्रम्
यावती च वसुन्धरा ।
तावत्त्वमिह लोकस्य
स्वामित्वमभिवर्तय ॥
You shall rule over this world as long as the planets move in their orbits and the earth lasts!
6.128.12
भरतस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
रामः परपुरञ्जयः ।
तथेति प्रतिजग्राह
निषसादासने शुभे ॥
Hearing those words of Bharata, Rāma, conqueror of enemy cities, said, 'so shall it be!', and accepting (the kingdom), took his seat on the auspicious chair.
6.128.13
ततः शत्रुघ्नवचनात्
निपुणाः श्मश्रुवर्धनाः ।
सुखहस्ताः सुशीघ्राश्च
राघवं पर्यवारयन् ॥
Then, at Ṡatrughna’s instance, skilled hair-dressers with gentle and swift hands attended Rāghava.
6.128.14
पूर्वं तु भरते स्नाते
लक्ष्मणे च महाबले ।
सुग्रीवे वानरेन्द्रे च
राक्षसेन्द्रे विभीषणे ।
विशोधितजटः स्नातः
चित्रमाल्यानुलेपनः ।
महार्हवसनोपेतः
तस्थौ तत्र श्रिया ज्वलन् ॥
After Bharata had taken bath, followed by Lakshmaṇa of immense strength, Sugreeva, the lord of Vānaras, Vibheeshaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, he had his matted locks disentangled and had a bath.
Then, as he stood there, adorned with a variety of garlands, smeared with a variety of unguents and clothed in the most expensive raiment, he dazzled with all the opulence.
6.128.16
प्रतिकर्म च रामस्य
कारयामास वीर्यवान् ।
लक्ष्मणस्य च लक्ष्मीवान्
इक्ष्वाकुकुलवर्धनः ॥
The valiant one (Bharata) made arrangements for the decoration of Rāma and (Ṡatrughna), the promoter of Ikshwāku dynasty, did the same for Lakshmaṇa.
6.128.17
प्रतिकर्म च सीतायाः
सर्वा दशरथस्त्रियः ।
आत्मनैव तदा चक्रुः
मनस्विन्यो मनोहरम् ॥
All the good-natured women of Daṡaratha personally attended on decorating Seetā exquisitely.
6.128.18
ततो वानरपत्नीनाम्
सर्वासामेव शोभनम् ।
चकार यत्नात्कौसल्या
प्रहृष्टा पुत्रवत्सला ॥
Thereupon, the rejoicing Kousalyā, with love for her son overflowing, decorated all the wives of the Vānaras with a great deal of care.
6.128.19
ततः शत्रुघ्नवचनात्
सुमन्त्रो नाम सारथिः ।
योजयित्वाभिचक्राम
रथं सर्वाङ्गशोभनम् ॥
Then, at the bidding of Ṡatrughna, Sumantra, the charioteer, yoked the horses to a chariot whose every part was exquisitely wrought, and brought it over there.
6.128.20
अर्कमण्डलसङ्काशम्
दिव्यं दृष्ट्वा रथं स्थितम् ।
आरुरोह महाबाहू
रामः परपुरंजयः ॥
Seeing that splendid chariot, which was glowing like the sun, made ready, Rāma, conquerer of enemy cities, of mighty arm, got into it.
6.128.21
सुग्रीवो हनुमांश्चैव
महेन्द्रसदृशद्युती ।
स्नातौ दिव्यनिभैर्वस्त्रैः
जग्मतुः शुभकुण्डलौ ॥
Sugreeva and Hanumān glowing like Mahēndra, followed (him), having taken bath, putting on clothes that resembled those of Dēvas and wearing lovely ear-pendants.
6.128.22
सर्वाभरणजुष्टाश्च
ययुस्ताः शुभकुण्डलाः ।
सुग्रीवपत्न्याः सीता च
द्रष्टुं नगरमुत्सुकाः ॥
And the wives of Sugreeva, adorned with every kind of ornament and lovely ear-pendants, went along with Seetā, eager to see the city.
6.128.23
अयोध्यायान् तु सचिवा
राज्ञो दशरथस्य च ।
पुरोहितं पुरस्कृत्य
मन्त्रयामासुरर्थवत् ॥
In Ayōdhyā, the ministers of king Daṡaratha, led by the Purōhita, made all the plans diligently.
6.128.24
अशोको विजयश्चैव
सिद्धार्थश्च समाहिताः ।
मन्त्रयन्रामवृद्ध्यर्थम्
ऋद्ध्यर्थं नगरस्य च ॥
Aṡōka, Vijaya and Siddhārtha, diligently wrought strategies to advance the prosperity of Rāma and the wealth of the city.
6.128.25
सर्वमेवाभिषेकार्थम्
जयार्हस्य महात्मनः ।
कर्तुमर्हथ रामस्य
यद्यन्मङ्गलपूर्वकम् ।
इति ते मन्त्रिणः सर्वे
सन्दिश्य तु पुरोहितम् ।
नगरान्निर्ययुस्तूर्णन्
रामदर्शनबुद्धयः ॥
All the ministers, instructing the Purōhita, 'May you arrange all that is needed for the auspicious coronation of Mahātma Rāma, who deserves to reign successfully (for a long time)' and left the city expediently, all set in their minds to meet Rāma.
6.128.27
हरियुक्तं सहस्राक्षो
रथमिन्द्र इवानघः ।
प्रययौ रथमास्थाय
रामो नगरमुत्तमम् ॥
Rāma, the sinless one, seated in the chariot like Indra, the thousand-eyed, on his chariot yoked with green-colored horses, set out towards the unexcelled city.
6.128.28
जग्राह भरतो रश्मीन्
शत्रुघ्नश्छत्रमाददे ।
लक्ष्मणो व्यजनन् तस्य
मूर्ध्नि सम्पर्यवीजयत् ॥
Bharata took up the reins. Ṡatrughna held the parasol above Rāma's head. Lakshmaṇa fanned him with a fly-whisk.
6.128.29
श्वेतं च वालव्यजनम्
जगृहे परितः स्थितः ।
अपरं चन्द्रसङ्काशम्
राक्षसेन्द्रो विभीषणः ॥
Vibheeshaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, who stood nearby, held another white fly-whisk that shone like the moon.
6.128.30
ऋषिसङ्घैर्तदाकाशे
देवैश्च समरुद्गणैः ।
स्तूयमानस्य रामस्य
शुश्रुवे मधुरध्वनिः ॥
From the sky were heard the melodious sounds of the hosts of Ṛshis, Dēvas and the hosts of Maruts chanting the praises of Rāma.
6.128.31
ततः शत्रुञ्जयं नाम
कुञ्जरं पर्वतोपमम् ।
आरुरोह महातेजाः
सुग्रीवो प्लवगर्षभः ॥
Thereupon, Sugreeva, a bull among Vānaras, of extraordinary power, got on to an elephant called Ṡatruñjaya that was huge like a mountain.
6.128.32
नवनागसहस्राणि
ययुरास्थाय वानराः ।
मानुषं विग्रहं कृत्वा
सर्वाभरणभूषिताः ॥
Assuming human forms and adorned with every kind of ornament, the Vānaras followed, riding on nine thousand elephants.
6.128.33
शङ्खशब्दप्रणादैश्च
दुन्दुभीनां च निस्वनैः ।
प्रययौ पुरुषव्याघ्रस्तां
पुरीं हर्म्यमालिनीम् ॥
The tiger among men, accompanied by the blaring sounds of the conches and the din of kettle-drums, went forth to the city that was lined with lofty mansions.
6.128.34
ददृशुस्ते समायान्तम्
राघवं सपुरःसरम् ।
विराजमानन् वपुषा
रथेनातिरथं तदा ॥
Then they (the people of Ayōdhyā) beheld Rāghava, a super-charioter, his magnificent body glowing, come riding on the chariot with his retinue.
6.128.35
ते वर्धयित्वा काकुत्स्थम्
रामेण प्रतिनन्दिताः ।
अनुजग्मुर्महात्मानम्
भ्रातृभिः परिवारितम् ॥
They hailed Rāma, the descendant of Kakutstha, and were greeted by him in return. They proceeded behind that Mahātma who was surrounded by his brothers.
6.128.36
अमात्यैर्ब्राह्मणैश्चैव
तथा प्रकृतिभिर्वृतः ।
श्रिया विरुरुचे रामो
नक्षत्रैरिव चन्द्रमाः ॥
Surrounded by ministers, Brāhmaṇas, and officials of various departments, Rāma shone with the (royal) opulence, like the moon among the stars.
6.128.37
स पुरोगामिभिस्तूर्यैः
तालस्वस्तिकपाणिभिः ।
प्रव्याहरद्भिर्मुदितैः
मङ्गलानि वृतो ययौ ॥
He went on, with some marching in front blowing trumpets, some playing cymbals and Swastikas with their hands, and some pronouncing auspicious invocations with gaiety, surrounding him.
6.128.38
अक्षतं जातरूपं च
गावः कन्याः सहद्विजाः ।
नरा मोदकहस्ताश्च
रामस्य पुरतो ययुः ॥
Men with sanctified rice, gold, and balls of sweets in their hands, along with cows, virgins and Brāhmaṇas walked in front of Rāma.
6.128.39
सख्यं च रामः सुग्रीवे
प्रभावं चानिलात्मजे ।
वानराणां च तत्कर्म
ह्याचचक्षेऽथ मन्त्रिणाम् ॥
Rāma told his ministers about the friendship with Sugreeva, the power of the son of Vāyu, and the great feats that the Vānaras accomplished.
6.128.40
श्रुत्वा च विस्मयं जग्मुः
अयोध्यापुरवासिनः ।
वानराणां च तत्कर्म
राक्षसानां च तद्बलम् ॥
The denizens of the city of Ayōdhyā were amazed on hearing about the accomplishments of Vānaras and about the mighty Rākshasa forces.
6.128.41
विभीषणस्य संयोगम्
अचचक्षेऽथ मन्त्रिणाम् ।
द्युतिमानेतदाख्याय
रामो वानरसन्वृतः ।
हृष्टपुष्टजनाकीर्णाम्
अयोध्यां प्रविवेश ह ॥
Then, Rāma spoke to his ministers about his meeting with Vibheeshaṇa. After recounting it, the resplendent Rāma, along with the Vānaras, entered Ayōdhyā that was teeming with happy and prosperous folk.
6.128.42
ततो ह्यभ्युच्छ्रयन्पौराः
पताकास्ते गृहे गृहे ।
ऐक्ष्वाकाध्युषितं रम्यम्
आससाद पितुर्गृहम् ॥
The citizens hoisted flags over every house. And he reached the lovely residence of his father which served as the abode of the successive Ikshwākus.
6.128.43
पितुर्भवनमासाद्य
प्रविश्य च महात्मनः ।
कौसल्यां च सुमित्रां च
कैकेयीमभिवाद्य च ॥
Reaching and entering the residence of his father, an extraordinary soul, he made his prostrations to Kousalyā, Sumitrā and Kaikēyee.
6.128.44
अथाब्रवीद्राजपुत्रो
भरतं धर्मिणां वरम् ।
अर्थोपहितया वाचा
मधुरं रघुनन्दनः ॥
Then the prince, the delight of Raghus, in a gentle voice, said these words that were appropriate for that moment, to Bharata, the foremost among the adherents of Dharma.
6.128.45
तच्च मद्भवनन् श्रेष्ठम्
साशोकवनिकं महत् ।
मुक्तावैदूर्यसङ्कीर्णम्
सुग्रीवस्य निवेदय ॥
Please show my exquisite residence that is studded with pearls and lazulite everywhere, including the Aṡōka pleasance, to Sugreeva.
6.128.46
तस्य तद्वचनन् श्रुत्वा
भरतः सत्यविक्रमः ।
हस्ते गृहीत्वा सुग्रीवं
प्रविवेश तमालयम् ॥
On hearing those words of Rama, Bharata of true prowess took Sugreeva by the hand and entered that abode.
6.128.47
ततस्तैलप्रदीपांश्च
पर्यङ्कास्तरणानि च ।
गृहीत्वा विविशुः क्षिप्रं
शत्रुघ्नेन प्रचोदिताः ॥
Retainers ordered by Ṡatrughna came in all haste, bringing oil-lamps, couches, mats and spreads.
6.128.48
उवाच च महातेजाः
सुग्रीवं राघवानुजः ।
अभिषेकाय रामस्य
दूतानाज्ञापय प्रभो ॥
Bharata, the younger brother of Rāghava, of extra-ordinary power, said to Sugreeva, "O lord! Please instruct your retainers to make arrangements for Rama's coronation!"
6.128.49
सौवर्णान्वानरेन्द्राणाम्
चतुर्णां चतुरो घटान् ।
ददौ क्षिप्रन् स सुग्रीवः
सर्वरत्नविभूषितान् ॥
Sugreeva, without losing time, passed on four golden pots studded with diamonds to four Vānara chiefs, (instructing them):
6.128.50
यथा प्रत्यूषसमये
चतुर्णां सागराम्भसाम् ।
पूर्णैर्घटैः प्रतीक्षध्वम्
तथा कुरुत वानराः ॥
O Vānaras! Do whatever is needed to see that these pots are filled with the waters of the four oceans by dawn tomorrow!
6.128.51
एवमुक्ता महात्मानो
वानरा वारणोपमाः ।
उत्पेतुर्गगनं शीघ्रम्
गरुडा इव शीघ्रगाः ॥
Thus instructed by that Mahātma, the Vānaras, huge like elephants, and rivaling the speed of Garuḍa, sprang into the sky in all haste.
6.128.52
जाम्बवांश्च हनूमांश्च
वेगदर्शी च वानरः ।
ऋषभश्चैव कलशान्
जलपूर्णानथानयन् ।
नदीशतानां पञ्चानाम्
जले कुम्भैरुपाहरन् ॥
Jāmbavān, Hanumān, Ṛshabha, and another Vānara called Vēgadarsi brought pots filled with water from five hundred rivers.
6.128.54
पूर्वात्समुद्रात्कलशम्
जलपूर्णमथानयत् ।
सुषेणः सत्त्वसम्पन्नः
सर्वरत्नविभूषितम् ॥
Sushēṇa, the powerful one, brought water from the eastern sea, filled in a diamond-studded pot.
6.128.55
ऋषभो दक्षिणात्तूर्णम्
समुद्राज्जलमानयत् ।
रक्तचन्दनशाखाभिः
सन्वृतं काञ्चनं घटम् ॥
Ṛshabha, with all expediency, brought water from the southern sea, in a golden pot covered with red sandal twigs.
6.128.56
गवयः पश्चिमात्तोयम्
आजहार महार्णवात् ।
रत्नकुम्भेन महता
शीतं मारुतविक्रमः ॥
Gavaya, whose speed rivaled that of Vāyu, brought the cool waters of the great ocean on the west, in a large diamond-studded pot.
6.128.57
उत्तराच्च जलं शीघ्रम्
गरुडानिलविक्रमः ।
आजहार स धर्मात्मा
नलः सर्वगुणान्वितः ॥
Nala, the Dharmātma, who was endowed with every desirable virtue and whose speed rivaled that of Garuḍa and Vāyu, swiftly brought the water from the ocean on the north.
6.128.58
ततस्तैर्वानरश्रेष्ठैः
आनीतं प्रेक्ष्य तज्जलम् ।
अभिषेकाय रामस्य
शत्रुघ्नः सचिवैः सह ।
पुरोहिताय श्रेष्ठाय
सुहृद्भ्यश्च न्यवेदयत् ॥
Then, Ṡatrughna, along with the ministers, presented that water brought by the Vānara chiefs for the coronation of Rāma to the eminent Purōhita and his companions.
6.128.59
ततः स प्रयतो वृद्धो
वसिष्ठो ब्राह्मणैः सह ।
रामन् रत्नमये पीठे
ससीतं संन्यवेशयत् ॥
Then the veteran Vasishṭha, assisted by the Brāhmaṇas seated Rāma and Seetā expediently on a diamond-studded chair.
6.128.60
वसिष्ठो वामदेवश्च
जाबालिरथ काश्यपः ।
कात्यायनः सुयज्ञश्च
गौतमो विजयस्तथा ।
अभ्यषिञ्चन्नरव्याघ्रं
प्रसन्नेन सुगन्धिना ।
सलिलेन सहस्राक्षम्
वसवो वासवं यथा ॥
Vasishṭha and Vāmadēva, Jābāli and Kaṡyapa, Katyāyana and Suyajña, Gautama and Vijaya, gave the ceremonial bath (of coronation) to that tiger among men with pure and fragrant water, as the Vasus did to Vāsava.
6.128.62
ऋत्विग्भिर्ब्राह्मणैः पूर्वम्
कन्याभिर्मन्त्रिभिस्तथा ।
योधैश्चैवाभ्यषिञ्चंस्ते
सम्प्रहृष्टाः सनैगमैः ।
सर्वौषधिरसैश्चापि
दैवतैर्नभसि स्थितैः ।
चतुर्हिर्लोकपालैश्च
सर्वैर्देवैश्च सङ्गतैः ॥
They then had the ceremonial bath performed, with the extracts of every kind of herb, by Ṛtviks and Brāhmaṇas first, and then by maidens, ministers, warriors, business leaders, and by the four guardians of the quarters and the Dēvas who stood in the sky, and by the assemblage of all Dēvas who descended down.
6.128.64
ब्रह्मणा निर्मितं पूर्वम्
किरीटं रत्नशोभितम् ।
अभिषिक्तः पुरा येन
मनुस्तं दीप्ततेजसम् ।
तस्यान्ववाये राजानः
क्रमाद्येनाभिषेचिताः ।
सभायां हेमक्लुप्तायां
शोभितायां महाधनैः ।
रत्नैर्नानाविधैश्चैव
चित्रितायां सुशोभनैः ।
नानारत्नमये पीठे
कल्पयित्वा यथाविधि ।
किरीटेन ततः पश्चात्
वसिष्ठेन महात्मना ।
ऋत्विग्भिर्भूषणैश्चैव
समयोक्ष्यत राघवः ॥
Mahātma Vasishṭha then seated Rāghava, in accordance with the custom, on a chair that was studded with a variety of gems in an assembly hall that had extremely rich furnishings, works of gold and was lovely with designs of a variety of precious gems.
Then Rāghava was decorated by the Ṛtviks with many ornaments and with the crown that was fashioned by Brahma himself, which was lovely with encrusted jewels and was glowing with splendor, with which Manu himself was consecrated once upon a time, and all the successive kings since then.
6.128.68
छत्रं तस्य च जग्राह
शत्रुघ्नः पाण्डुरन् शुभम् ।
श्वेतन् च वालव्यजनन्
सुग्रीवो वानरेश्वरः ।
अपरन् चन्द्रसङ्काशम्
राक्षसेन्द्रो विभीषणः ॥
Ṡatrughna held the lovely white parasol over his head. Sugreeva, lord of the Vānaras, fanned him with a white fly-whisk, while Vibheeshaṇa, lord of the Rākshasas, with another that was bright like the moon.
6.128.70
मालां ज्वलन्तीं वपुषा
काञ्चनीं शतपुष्कराम् ।
राघवाय ददौ वायुः
वासवेन प्रचोदितः ॥
Vāyu, at the instance of Vāsava, gave Rāghava a shining garland of gold with a hundred golden lotuses.
6.128.71
सर्वरत्नसमायुक्तम्
मणिभिश्च विभूषितम् ।
मुक्ताहारं नरेन्द्राय
ददौ शक्रप्रचोदितः ॥
And, at the instance of Ṡakra, he also gave another chain of pearls that was decorated with jewels and was embellished with every kind of precious stone.
6.128.72
प्रजगुर्देवगन्धर्वा
ननृतुश्चाप्सरोगणाः ।
अभिषेके तदर्हस्य
तदा रामस्य धीमतः ॥
The Dēvas and Gandharvas sang and the Apsaras danced as the sagacious Rāma, deservingly, was crowned.
6.128.73
भूमिः सस्यवती चैव
फलवन्तश्च पादपाः ।
गन्धवन्ति च पुष्पाणि
बभूवू राघवोत्सवे ॥
On that festive occasion of Rāghava's coronation, the earth yielded plenty, the trees were loaded with fruit, and the flowers gave forth fragrance.
6.128.74
सहस्रशतमश्वानाम्
धेनूनां च गवां तथा ।
ददौ शतवृषान् पूर्वम्
द्विजेभ्यो मनुजर्षभः ॥
That bull among men, to begin with, gave away a hundred thousand horses and an equal number of cows that had borne calves recently, as well as a hundred bulls, to the Brāhmaṇas.
6.128.75
त्रिंशत्कोटीर्हिरण्यस्य
ब्राह्मणेभ्यो ददौ पुनः ।
नानाभरणवस्त्राणि
महार्हाणि च राघवः ॥
And on top of it Rāghava gifted to the Brāhmaṇas thirty crore gold coins, and many ornaments and expensive clothes.
6.128.76
अर्करश्मिप्रतीकाशाम्
काञ्चनीं मणिविग्रहाम् ।
सुग्रीवाय स्रजन् दिव्याम्
प्रायच्छन्मनुजर्षभः ॥
The bull among men then presented Sugreeva a splendid gold garland that was studded with gem stones all over it and glistened like the rays of the sun.
6.128.77
वैदूर्यमयचित्रे च
मणिरत्नविभूषिते ।
वालिपुत्राय धृतिमान्
अङ्गदायाङ्गदे ददौ ॥
And he affectionately bestowed on Vāli's son Aṅgada a pair of armlets that were lovely with lazulite and ornate with diamonds and other gems.
6.128.78
मणिप्रवरजुष्टं तं
मुक्ताहारमनुत्तमम् ।
सीतायै प्रददौ रामः
चन्द्ररश्मिसमप्रभम् ॥
And to Seetā, Rāma gave an unexcelled pearl necklace that was set with the best of gems and glowed with the radiance of the moon.
6.128.79
अरजे वाससी दिव्ये
शुभान्याभरणानि च ।
अवेक्षमाणा वैदेही
प्रददौ वायुसूनवे ॥
And as Seetā watched, he gave to the son of Vāyu a pair of splendid spotless clothes and many lovely ornaments.
6.128.80
अवमुच्यात्मनः कण्ठात्
हारं जनकनन्दिनी ।
अवैक्षत हरीन्सर्वान्
भर्तारं च मुहुर्मुहुः ॥
Taking off the garland from her neck, the daughter of Janaka looked at all the Vānaras and then at her husband repeatedly.
6.128.81
तामिङ्गितज्ञः सम्प्रेक्ष्य
बभाषे जनकात्मजाम् ॥
Guessing the intent of the daughter of Janaka, he said, turning to her:
6.128.81
प्रदेहि सुभगे हारम्
यस्य तुष्टासि भामिनि ।
तेजो धृतिर्यशो दाक्ष्यं
सामर्थ्यं विनयो नयः ।
पौरुषन् विक्रमो बुद्धिः
यस्मिन्नेतानि सर्वदा ॥
My dear lovely and blessed lady! Give the necklace to whomever you are pleased with and in whom power, courage, skill, capability, humility, shrewdness, manliness, prowess and intelligence are ever present.
6.128.83
ददौ सा वायुपुत्राय
तं हारमसितेक्षणा ।
हनूमान्स्तेन हारेण
शुशुभे वानरर्षभः ।
चन्द्रांशुचयगौरेण
श्वेताभ्रेण यथाचलः ॥
Then she of black eyes gave the necklace to the son of Vāyu. Wearing that necklace that was white like the rays of the moon, Hanumān, the bull among Vānaras, shone like a mountain with a white cloud on it.
6.128.84
सर्वे वानरवृद्धाश्च
ये चान्ये वानरोत्तमाः ।
वासोभिर्भूषणैश्चैव
यथार्हं प्रतिपूजिताः ॥
All the elderly Vānaras and the other prominent Vānaras were honored suitably with clothes and ornaments.
6.128.85
ततो द्विविद मैन्दाभ्यां
नीलाय च परन्तपः ।
सर्वान्कामगुणान्वीक्ष्य
प्रददौ वसुधाधिपः ॥
Then the lord of the earth, the tormentor of his foes, turned to Neela, Mainda and Dwivida and gave them all that one would covet.
6.128.86
विभीषणोऽथ सुग्रीवो
हनुमान् जाम्बवांस्तथा ।
सर्ववानरवृद्धाश्च
रामेणाक्लिष्टकर्मणा ।
यथार्हं पूजिताः सर्वे
कामै रत्नैश्च पुष्कलैः ।
प्रहृष्टमनसः सर्वे
जग्मुरेव यथागतम् ॥
Vibheeshaṇa, Sugreeva, Hanumān, Jāmbavān and all the elderly Vānaras, appropriately honored by Rāma, who did everything with ease, with many desirable objects and plenty of gem stones left, just as they had come, delighted.
6.128.88
नत्वा सर्वे महात्मानः
ततस्ते वानरर्षभाः ।
विसृष्टाः पार्थिवेन्द्रेण
किष्किन्धां समुपागमन् ॥
Then all those Mahātmas, the bulls among Vānaras, bowed to that lord of the earth, and taking leave of him, reached Kishkindhā.
6.128.89
सुग्रीवो वानरश्रेष्ठो
दृष्ट्वा रामाभिषेचनम् ।
पूजितश्चैव रामेण
किष्किन्धां प्राविशत्पुरीम् ॥
Sugreeva, the foremost of the Vānaras, after thus witnessing the coronation of Rāma and duly honored by Rāma, was back in the city of Kishkindhā.
6.128.90
विभीषणोऽपि धर्मात्मा
सह तैर्नैरृतर्षभैः ।
लब्ध्वा कुलधनं राजा
लङ्कां प्रायान् महायशाः ॥
King Vibheeshaṇa, a Dharmātma of great renown, having acquired his ancestral property, returned to Laṅkā, along with all the bulls among Rākshasas.
6.128.91
स राज्यमखिलं शासन्
निहतारिर्महायशाः ।
राघवः परमोदारः
शशास परया मुदा ॥
Having vanquished all his enemies, and ruling the entire kingdom (unopposed), Rāghava of great renown and supreme generosity enjoyed it all with great felicity.
6.128.92
उवाच लक्ष्मणं रामो
धर्मज्ञं धर्मवत्सलः ॥
Ever loving to adhere to Dharma, Rāma said to Lakshmaṇa who was well-versed in Dharma:
6.128.93
आतिष्ठ धर्मज्ञ मया सहेमां
गां पूर्वराजाध्युषितां बलेन ।
तुल्यं मया त्वं पितृभिर्धृता या
तां यौवराज्ये धुरमुद्वहस्व ॥
O knower of Dharma! Rule this earth along with me, which had been reigned over by the kings in the past with their might! Installed as the crown-prince, please carry, equally along with me, the weight of this kingdom, just as it had been carried by our forefathers!
6.128.94
सर्वात्मना पर्यनुनीयमानो
यदा न सौमित्रिरुपैति योगम् ।
नियुज्यमानोऽपि च यावराज्ये
ततोऽभ्यषिञ्चद्भरतं महात्मा ॥
Failing to gain the consent of Sowmitri to his appointment as the crown-prince, in spite of every attempt at persuading him, he, a Mahātma, consecrated Bharata (as the crown prince).
6.128.95
पौण्डरीकाश्वमेधाभ्यान्
वाजपेयेन चासकृत् ।
अन्यैश्च विविधैर्यज्ञैः
अयजत्पार्थिवर्षभः ॥
The bull among the kings gave oblations to the Dēvas by performing the Pouṇḍareeka, Aṡwamēdha, Vājapēya and many other Yajñas, many a time.
6.128.96
राज्यन् दशसहस्राणि
प्राप्य वर्षाणि राघवः ।
शताश्वमेधानाजह्रे
सदश्वान्भूरिदक्षिणान् ॥
Reigning over the kingdom for ten thousand years, Rāghava performed one hundred Aṡwamēdha Yajñas with excellent horses, giving away large quantities of money as honorariums (to Brāhmaṇas).
6.128.97
आजानुलम्बिबाहुः स
महावक्षाः प्रतापवान् ।
लक्ष्मणानुचरो रामः
शशास पृथिवीमिमाम् ॥
He, of mighty chest and scorching valor, whose arms reached down to his knees, ruled this earth assisted by Lakshmaṇa.
6.128.98
राघवश्चापि धर्मात्मा
प्राप्य राज्यमनुत्तमम् ।
ईजे बहुविधैर्यज्ञैः
ससुतभ्रातृबान्धवः ॥
Having obtained an unexcelled kingdom, Rāghava, the Dharmātma, gave oblations to the Dēvas, by performing many kinds of Yajñas, along with his sons, brothers and relatives.
6.128.99
न पर्यदेवन्विधवा
न च व्यालकृतं भयम् ।
न व्याधिजं भयं चासीत्
रामे राज्यं प्रशासति ॥
While Rāma ruled the kingdom, there were no wailing widows, no danger from wild animals, and no fear born of diseases.
6.128.100
निर्दस्युरभवल्लोको
नानर्थः कश्चिदस्पृशत् ।
न च स्म वृद्धा बालानां
प्रेतकार्याणि कुर्वते ॥
The world was free from thieves. No one was touched by any calamity. No elders had to perform last rites for the younger ones.
6.128.101
सर्वं मुदितमेवासीत्
सर्वो धर्मपरोअभवत् ।
राममेवानुपश्यन्तो
नाभ्यहिन्सन्परस्परम् ॥
Everyone was happy. Everyone adhered to Dharma. With their eyes ever on Rāma, they harried not each other.
6.128.102
आसन्वर्षसहस्राणि
तथा पुत्रसहस्रिणः ।
निरामया विशोकाश्च
रामे राज्यं प्रशासति ॥
People lived for thousands of years unafflicted by disease or grief and bearing thousands of children while Rāma ruled the kingdom.
6.128.103
रामो रामो राम इति
प्रजानामभवन् कथाः ।
रामभूतं जगादभूत्
रामे राज्यं प्रशासति ॥
Of Rāma and Rāma alone the people talked all the time and the world lost itself in Rāma, while Rāma ruled the kingdom.
6.128.104
नित्यपुष्पा नित्यफलाः
तरवस्तत्र निर्वृणाः ।
कालवर्षी च पर्जन्यः
सुखस्पर्शश्च मारुतः ॥
The trees were in flower and yielded fruit perennially. The clouds rained on time and the wind's touch was ever pleasant.
6.128.105
ब्राह्मणाः क्षत्रिया वैश्याः
शूद्रा लोभविवर्जिताः ।
स्वकर्मसु प्रवर्तन्ते
तुष्ठाः स्वैरेव कर्मभिः ॥
Free from greed, Brāhmaṇas, Kshatriyas, Vaiṡyas and Ṡūdras were busy engaged in their respective activities, happy and contented with what they did.
6.128.106
आसन् प्रजा धर्मपरा
रामे शासति नानृताः ।
सर्वे लक्षणसम्पन्नाः
सर्वे धर्मपरायणाः ।
दशवर्षसहस्राणि
रामो राज्यमकारयत् ॥
People followed Dharma and never resorted to untruth, while Rāma ruled. Everyone was endowed with good features. Everyone considered Dharma as the ultimate. Rāma thus ruled the kingdom for ten thousand years.
6.128.107
धर्म्यं यशस्यमायुष्यं
राज्ञां च विजायावहम् ।
आदिकाव्यमिदं चार्षं
पुरा वाल्मीकिना कृतम् ।
पठेद्यः शृणुयाल्लोके
नरः पापात्प्रमुच्यते ॥
6.128.109
पुत्रकामश्च पुत्रान्वै
धनकामो धनानि च ।
लभते मनुजो लोके
श्रुत्वा रामाभिषेचनम् ।
महीं विजयते राजा
रिपूंश्चाप्यधितिष्ठति ॥
6.128.110
राघवेण यथा माता
सुमित्रा लक्ष्मणेन च ।
भरतेन च कैकेयी
जिवपुत्रास्तथा स्त्रियः ।
भविष्यन्ति सदानन्दाः
पुत्रपौत्रसमन्विताः ॥
6.128.112
श्रुत्वा रामायणमिदं
दीर्घमायुश्च विन्दति ।
रामस्य विजयं चैव
सर्वमक्लिष्ठकर्मणः ॥
6.128.113
शृणोति य इदं काव्यं
पुरा वाल्मीकिना कृतम् ।
श्रद्दधानो जितक्रोधो
दुर्गाण्यतितरत्यसौ ॥
6.128.114
समागम्य प्रवासान्ते
रमन्ते सह बान्धवैः ।
शृण्वन्ति य इदं काव्यं
पुरा वाल्मीकिना कृतम् ॥
6.128.115
ते प्रार्थितान् वरान् सर्वान्
प्राप्नुवन्तीह राघवात् ।
श्रवणेन सुराः सर्वे
प्रीयन्ते संप्रशृण्वताम् ॥
6.128.116
विनायकाश्च शाम्यन्ति
गृहे तिष्ठन्ति यस्य वै ।
विजयेत महीं राजा
प्रवासि स्वस्तिमान् भवेत् ॥
6.128.117
स्त्रियो रजस्वलाः श्रुत्वा
पुत्रान् सूयुरनुत्तमान् ।
पूजयंश्च पठंश्चनम्
इतिहासं पुरातनम् ।
सर्वपापैः प्रमुच्येत
दीर्घमायुरवाप्नुयात् ॥
6.128.118
प्रणम्य शिरसा नित्यं
श्रोतव्यं क्षत्रियैर्द्विजात् ।
ऐश्वर्यं पुत्रलाभश्च
भविष्यति न संशयः ।
रामायणमिदं कृत्स्नं
शृण्वतः पठतः सदा ॥
6.128.120
प्रीयते सततं रामः
सहि विष्णुः सनातनः ।
आदिदेवो महाबाहुः
हरिर्नारायणः प्रभुः ।
साक्षाद्रामो रघुश्रेष्ठः
शेषो लक्ष्मण उच्यते ॥
6.128.121
एवमेतत्पुरावृत्तम्
आख्यानं भद्रमस्तु वः ।
प्रव्याहरत विस्रब्धम्
बलं विष्णोः प्रवर्धताम् ॥
6.128.122
देवाश्च सर्वे तुष्यन्ति
ग्रहणाच्छ्रवणात्तथा ।
रामायणस्य श्रवणे
तुष्यन्ति पितरः सदा ॥
6.128.123
भक्त्या रामस्य ये चेमां
संहितामृषिणा कृताम् ।
ये लिखन्तीह च नराः
तेषां वासस्त्रिविष्टपे ॥
6.128.124
कुटुम्बवृद्धिं धनधान्यवृद्धिम्
स्त्रियश्च मुख्याह् सुखमुत्तमं च ।
श्रुत्वा शुभं काव्यमिदं महार्थम्
प्राप्नोति सर्वां भुवि चार्थसिद्धिम् ॥
6.128.125
आयुष्यमारोग्यकरं यशस्यम्
सौभ्रातृकं बुद्धिकरं शुभं च ।
श्रोतव्यमेतन्नियमेन सद्भिः
आख्यानमोजस्करमृद्धिकामैः ॥
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे अष्टाविंशत्युत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and twenty eighth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
