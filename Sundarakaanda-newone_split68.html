Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 68
In this Sarga, Hanuman relates to Rāma the conversation between Seetā and him, and how he consoled her with reassuring words.
5.68.1
अथाहमुत्तरं देव्या
पुनरुक्तस्ससम्भ्रमम् ।
तव स्नेहान्नरव्याघ्र
सौहार्दादनुमान्य वै ॥
O tiger among men! The lady while giving me leave, hastened to say these words again, betraying her love and affection for you:
5.68.2
एवं बहुविधं वाच्यो
रामो दाशरथिस्त्वया ।
यथा मामाप्नुयाच्छीघ्रम्
हत्वा रावणमाहवे ॥
You should say this to Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha in as many ways as possible, so that he regains me quickly, killing Rāvaṇa in battle.
5.68.3
यदि वा मन्यसे वीर
वसैकाहमरिन्दम ।
कस्मिंश्चित्संवृते देशे
विक्रान्तश्श्वो गमिष्यसि ॥
If you could give a thought to it,  O Veera! O subduer of foe,  you might as well take rest here for one more day  in some concealed location and leave tomorrow. 
5.68.4
मम चाप्यल्पभाग्यायाः
सान्निध्यात्तव वीर्यवन् ।
अस्य शोकविपाकस्य
मुहूर्तं स्याद्विमोक्षणम् ॥
I, who is only so much fortunate,  will find relief from this haunting grief, because of your presence, O valiant one, at least for a little while!
5.68.5
गते हि त्वयि विक्रान्ते
पुनरागमनाय वै ।
प्राणानामपि सन्देहो
मम स्यान्नात्र संशयः ॥
Once you, of great prowess, leave, even though it is only to come back again, my survival will be in doubt, and there is no doubt about it.
5.68.6
तवादर्शनजश्शोको
भूयो मां परितापयेत् ।
दुःखाद्धुःखपराभूताम्
दुर्गतां दुःखभागिनीम् ॥
Once you are out of sight, the grief will haunt me again. The ill-fated me, whose lot is filled with nothing but sorrows, is being thrown from one sorrow to another.
5.68.7
अयं च वीर सन्देहः
तिष्ठतीव ममाग्रतः ।
सुमहांस्त्वत्सहायेषु
हर्यृक्षेषु हरीश्वर ॥
And there is this big nagging question that  stands staring in front of me, O Lord of Vānaras,  about your aides, the Vānaras and Ṛkshas:
5.68.8
कथं नु खलु दुष्पारम्
तरिष्यन्ति महोदधिम् ।
तानि हर्यृक्षसैन्यानि
तौ वा नरवरात्मजौ ॥
How will that army of Vānaras and Ṛkshas,  or for that matter, those  two sons of the king of men,  cross the great ocean that  is so difficult to cross! 
5.68.9
त्रयाणामेव भूतानाम्
सागरस्यास्य लङ्घने ।
शक्तिस्स्याद्वैनतेयस्य
तव वा मारुतस्य वा ॥
Only three beings among all the creatures  are capable of jumping over the ocean,  Vainatēya, you and Vāyu! 
5.68.10
तदस्मिन्कार्यनिर्योगे
वीरैवं दुरतिक्रमे ।
किं पश्यसि समाधानम्
ब्रूहि कार्यविदां वरः ॥
That being the challenge ahead  that is quite difficult to surmount,  O Veera, O best among taskmasters, tell me what you see as a solution?  
5.68.11
काममस्य त्वमेवैकः
कार्यस्य परिसाधने ।
पर्याप्तः परवीरघ्न
यशस्यस्ते बलोदयः ॥
Certainly, you are capable of  killing every warrior among the enemies and  accomplishing the mission all by yourself,  and it would bring you quite a fame! 
5.68.12
बलैस्समग्रैर्यदि माम्
हत्वा रावणमाहवे ।
विजयी स्वां पुरीं रामो
नयेत्तत्स्याद्यशस्करम् ॥
But Rāma would be held in good esteem, if he is victorious in killing Rāvaṇa and all his forces in the battle and takes me to his home city! 
5.68.13
यथाऽहं तस्य वीरस्य
वनादुपधिना हृता ।
रक्षसा तद्भयादेव
तथा नार्हति राघवः ॥
There is no need for Rāghava to fear the Rākshasa and take me away (in stealth) like the Rākshasa did by tricking the Veera in the Vana.
5.68.14
बलैस्तु सङ्कुलां कृत्वा
लङ्कां परबलार्दनः ।
मां नयेद्यदि काकुत्स्थः
तत्तस्य सदृशं भवेत् ॥
It would be befitting of Kākutstha,  who pulverizes his enemies,  if he takes me away, throwing  Laṅkā into turmoil with his power.
5.68.15
तद्यथा तस्य विक्रान्तम्
अनुरूपं महात्मनः ।
भवेदाहवशूरस्य
तथा त्वमुपपादय ॥
Hence, please arrange it so,  that that Mahātma, a dare devil in battles,  would get to display all the prowess  that is the like of his! 
5.68.16
तदर्थोपहितं वाक्यम्
प्रश्रितं हेतुसंहितम् ।
निशम्याहं तत श्शेषम्
वाक्यमुत्तरमब्रुवम् ॥
Hearing those purposeful and well-reasoned words  that were spoken in all sincerity,  I responded, saying what was left to be said: 
5.68.17
देवि हर्यृक्षसैन्यानाम्
ईश्वरः प्लवतां वरः ।
सुग्रीवस्सत्त्वसम्पन्नः
तवार्थे कृतनिश्चयः ॥
O Dēvi! Sugreeva, the best of Vānaras  and the Lord of Vānara and Ṛksha armies,  endowed with immense power,  is completely committed to your cause. 
5.68.18
तस्य विक्रमसम्पन्नाः
सत्त्ववन्तो महाबलाः ।
मनस्सङ्कल्पसम्पाता
निदेशे हरयः स्थिताः ॥
The Vānaras of terrific prowess,  great power and immense strength,  who can move at the speed of their thought,  are standing ready, at his beck and call. 
5.68.19
येषां नोपरि नाधस्तात्
न तिर्यक्सज्जते गतिः ।
न च कर्मसु सीदन्ति
महत्स्वमिततेजसः ॥
Nothing can obstruct their movement  upward, downward or sideways.  Endowed with limitless power,  they do not cave in even under  the most demanding of tasks. 
5.68.20
असकृत्तैर्महाभागैः
वानरैर्बलदर्पितैः ।
प्रदक्षिणीकृता भूमिः
वायुमार्गानुसारिभिः ॥
The earth was encircled, many a time, by those blessed Vānaras who are haughty with their strength and who follow the tracks of the wind.
5.68.21
मद्विशिष्टाश्च तुल्याश्च
सन्ति तत्र वनौकसः ।
मत्तः प्रत्यवरः कश्चित्
नास्ति सुग्रीवसन्निधौ ॥
The Vana dwellers that are there  are either better than me  or at least as good as me.  There is no one in Sugreeva’s company  who is inferior to me. 
5.68.22
अहं तावदिह प्राप्तः
किं पुनस्ते महाबलाः ।
न हि प्रकृष्टाः प्रेत्यन्ते
प्रेष्यन्ते हीतरे जनाः ॥
If I myself could come here,  what to say of those mighty strong ones?  The exceptional ones will never be deployed (first),  but only the ordinary ones, is it not? 
5.68.23
तदलं परितापेन
देवि मन्युर्व्यपैतु ते ।
एकोत्पातेन ते लङ्काम्
एष्यन्ति हरियूथपाः ॥
Hence, O Dēvi, enough of worry; give up your grief!  The warrior chiefs of Vānaras will  arrive in Laṅkā in a single hop. 
5.68.24
मम पृष्ठगतौ तौ च
चन्द्रसूर्याविवोदितौ ।
त्वत्सकाशं महाभागे
नृसिंहावागमिष्यतः ॥
Those two lions among men of immense strength  will come here to your presence, seated on my back,  like the rising sun and moon. 
5.68.25
अरिघ्नं सिंहसङ्काशम्
क्षिप्रं द्रक्ष्यसि राघवम् ।
लक्ष्मणं च धनुष्पाणिम्
लङ्काद्वारमुपस्थितम् ॥
You will soon see lion-like Rāghava who decimates his enemies,  and Lakshmaṇa with bow in his hand,  arriving at the gates of Laṅkā! 
5.68.26
नखदंष्ट्रायुधान् वीरान्
सिंहशार्दूलविक्रमान् ।
वानरान्वारणोन्द्राभान्
क्षिप्रं द्रक्षसि सङ्गतान् ॥
You will very soon see  the company of Vānara Veeras  who are terrific, like tigers and lions  and majestic like elephants,  having nails and fangs for weapons. 
5.68.27
शैलाम्बुदनिकाशानाम्
लङ्कामलयसानुषु ।
नर्दतां कपिमुख्यानाम्
अचिराच्छ्रोष्यसि स्वनम् ॥
Before it is too long, you will hear the sound of the roar of chiefs of Vānaras  who resemble mountains and clouds themselves,  on the flanks of Laṅkā’s Malaya mountain.
5.68.28
निवृत्तवनवासं च
त्वया सार्धमरिन्दमम् ।
अभिषिक्तमयोध्यायाम्
क्षिप्रं द्रक्ष्यसि राघवम् ॥
You will very soon see Rāghava, the subduer of foe, completing his stay in the Vana and getting consecrated in Ayōdhyā along with you.
5.68.29
ततो मया वाग्भिरदीनभाषिणा
शिवाभिरिष्टाभिरभिप्रसादिता ।
जगाम शान्तिं मम मैथिलात्मजा
तवापि शोकेन तदाभिपीडिता ॥
The daughter of Mithilā’s King, who was tormented badly by the grief of (separation from) you, found solace, calmed by my cheering, pleasing and well-boding words.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये । ।
चतुर्विंशत्सहस्रिकायां संहितायां
सुन्दरकाण्डे अष्टषष्टितमस्सर्गः ॥
Thus concludes Sundara Kāṇḍa  consisting of sixty eight Sargas,  in the glorious Rāmāyaṇa,  the first ever poem of humankind,  of twenty four thousand Ṡlōkas  composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki. 


Meaning, notes and commentary by: Krishna Sharma
