Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 29
In this Sarga, Rāma taunts Khara for harrowing the Ṛshis of Daṇḍaka Araṇya. He tells Khara that anyone who commits evil deeds will pay for them when the time is ripe. He tells Khara that death is certain for him at his hands.
Khara becomes furious. He sneers at Rāma saying that only a lowly Kshatriya like Rāma would indulge in self-praise like he did. Then he hurls his flaming club at Rāma and Rāma shatters it into pieces with his arrows.
3.29.1
खरं तु विरथं रामो
गदापाणिमवस्थितम् ।
मृदुपूर्वं महातेजाः
परुषं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Rāma of immense strength, rebuked Khara, who no more had a chariot and stood with his club in the hand, in strong words, though in a gentle manner.
3.29.2
गजाश्वरथसम्बाधे
बले महति तिष्ठता ।
कृतं सुदारुणं कर्म
सर्वलोकजुगुप्सितम् ॥
Commanding a great force teeming with elephants, chariots and horses, you committed a dastardly act that is repulsive to the entire world.
3.29.3
उद्वेजनीयो भूतानां
नृशंसः पापकर्मकृत् ।
त्रयाणामपि लोकानाम्
ईश्वरोऽपि न तिष्ठति ॥
A cruel person, even if he were to be the lord of all the three worlds, cannot go on forever doing evil deeds and terrorizing all beings.
3.29.4
कर्म लोकविरुद्धं तु
कुर्वाणं क्षणदाचर ।
तीक्ष्णं सर्वजनो हन्ति
सर्पं दुष्टमिवागतम् ॥
O Rākshasa! Anyone who is cruel and against the world will be killed by everyone, just as a vicious serpent that sways into their midst would be.
3.29.5
लोभात्पापानि कुर्वाणः
कामाद्वा यो न बुध्यते ।
भ्रष्टः पश्यति तस्यान्तं
ब्राह्मणी करकादिव ॥
Those who commit sinful deeds driven by greed and lust and never realize what they do will surely face the results at the end, like a red lizard that swallows hailstones.
3.29.6
वसतो दण्डकारण्ये
तापसान्धर्मचारिणः ।
किन्नु हत्वा महाभागान्
फलं प्राप्स्यसि राक्षस ॥
O Rākshasa! What is that that you gain by killing the blessed, Dharma-adhering Tapasvis who live in Daṇḍaka Araṇya?
3.29.7
न चिरं पापकर्माणः
क्रूरा लोकजुगुप्सिताः ।
ऐश्वर्यं प्राप्य तिष्ठन्ति
शीर्णमूला इव द्रुमाः ॥
The stature of those who are cruel and commit sinful deeds and are abhorred by the world will not last long, like a tree whose roots are rotten.
3.29.8
अवश्यं लभते जन्तुः
फलं पापस्य कर्मणः ।
घोरं पर्यागते काले
द्रुमाः पुष्पमिवार्तवम् ॥
All beings are bound to pay dearly for their sinful deeds when the time ripens, as surely as trees bloom when the season arrives.
3.29.9
नचिरात्प्राप्यते लोके
पापानां कर्मणां फलम् ।
सविषाणामिवान्नानां
भुक्तानां क्षणदाचर ॥
The consequences of sinful deeds, O Rākshasa, shall be experienced soon enough in this world like for those eating poisoned food!
3.29.10
पापमाचरतां घोरं
लोकस्याप्रियमिच्छताम् ।
अहमासादितो राजा
प्राणान्हन्तुं निशाचर ॥
I, being a king, was commissioned to take the lives of the Night-rovers who are bent on harassing the world and are guilty of sinful deeds!
3.29.11
अद्य हि त्वां मया मुक्ताः
शराः काञ्चनभूषणाः ।
विदार्यातिपतिष्यन्ति
वल्मीकमिव पन्नगाः ॥
The arrows, adorned with gold, shot by me today shall come out after rending you, like serpents out of anthill.
3.29.12
ये त्वया दण्डकारण्ये
भक्षिता धर्मचारिणः ।
तानद्य निहतस्सङ्ख्ये
ससैन्योऽनुगमिष्यसि ॥
Slain today in this fight, you, along with your army, shall meet the same fate as that of the Dharma-adhering (Tapasvis) whom you devoured in the Daṇḍaka Araṇya!
3.29.13
अद्य त्वां निहतं बाणैः
पश्यन्तु परमर्षयः ।
निरयस्थं विमानस्था
ये त्वया हिंसिताः पुरा ॥
Today, the Maharshis whom you had tormented earlier shall see you in hell, killed by the arrows, from their Vimānas!
3.29.14
प्रहर त्वं यथाकामं
कुरु यत्नं कुलाधम ।
अद्य ते पातयिष्यामि
शिरस्तालफलं यथा ॥
Strike me, you may, as hard as you can, O base-born, but I shall bring your head tumbling down like the fruit of palm!
3.29.15
एवमुक्तस्तु रामेण
खरस्संरक्तलोचनः ।
प्रत्युवाच ततो रामं
प्रहसन्क्रोधमूर्छितः ॥
Thus taunted by Rāma, Khara, with his eyes turned blood red, responded laughing, losing his mind in rage:
3.29.16
प्राकृतान्राक्षसान्हत्वा
युद्धे दशरथात्मज ।
आत्मना कथमात्मानम्
अप्रशस्यं प्रशंससि ॥
O son of Daṡaratha! You praise yourself for killing ordinary Rākshasas in fight, which, in fact, is nothing worth speaking of!
3.29.17
विक्रान्ता बलवन्तो वा
ये भवन्ति नरर्षभाः ।
कथयन्ति न ते किञ्चित्
तेजसा स्वेन गर्विताः ॥
The true bulls among men who are strong and brave do not boast even the least and do not succumb to arrogance because of their prowess.
3.29.18
प्राकृतास्त्वकृतात्मानो
लोके क्षत्रियपांसनाः ।
निरर्थकं विकत्थन्ते
यथा राम विकत्थसे ॥
O Rāma, you are boasting about yourself  like a lowly, immature and ordinary Kshatriya, in vain!
3.29.19
कुलं व्यपदिशन्वीरः
समरे कोऽभिधास्यति ।
मृत्युकाले हि सम्प्राप्ते
स्वयमप्रस्तवे स्तवम् ॥
Would a Veera ever indulge in the mention of his lineage and in self-praise at the inopportune moment of imminent death in battle?
3.29.20
सर्वथैव लघुत्वं ते
कत्थनेन विदर्शितम् ।
सुवर्णप्रतिरूपेण
तप्तेनेव कुशाग्निना ॥
Your boasts only expose your lowliness in every which way. It is like the flame of Kuṡa grass fooling itself to be molten gold.
3.29.21
न तु मामिह तिष्ठन्तं
पश्यसि त्वं गदाधरम् ।
धराधरमिवाकम्प्यं
पर्वतं धातुभिश्चितम् ॥
Do you not see me standing here wielding the club, firm like a mountain with its layers of expanses of (heavy) minerals?
3.29.22
पर्याप्तोऽहं गदापाणिः
हन्तुं प्राणान्रणे तव ।
त्रयाणामपि लोकानां
पाशहस्त इवान्तकः ॥
I, armed with the club in my hand, am good enough to take your life in this fight, like Yama, with noose in his hand is, to take the lives of everyone in the three worlds.
3.29.23
कामं बह्वपि वक्तव्यं
त्वयि वक्ष्यामि न त्वहम् ।
अस्तं गच्छेद्धि सविता
युद्धविघ्नस्ततो भवेत् ॥
There is a lot to say about you. But I shall not venture into it now as the sun might set (by the time I finish). Then the fight will be disrupted.
3.29.24
चतुर्दश सहस्राणि
राक्षसानां हतानि ते ।
त्वद्विनाशात्करोम्येषां
तेषामश्रुप्रमार्जनम् ॥
I shall destroy you and wipe off the tears shed for the fourteen thousand Rākshasas you killed.
3.29.25
इत्युक्त्वा परमक्रुद्धः
तां गदां परमाङ्गदः ।
खरश्चिक्षेप रामाय
प्रदीप्तामशनिं यथा ॥
Having said this, the extremely enraged Khara of imposing limbs hurled at Rāma the club that dazzled like a thunderbolt.
3.29.26
खरबाहुप्रयुक्ता सा
प्रदीप्ता महती गदा ।
भस्मवृक्षांश्च गुल्मांश्च
कृत्वागात्तत्समीपतः ॥
That mighty flaming club hurled by Khara left him, reducing the trees and bushes on its path into ashes.
3.29.27
तामापतन्तीं ज्वलितां
मृत्युपाशोपमां गदाम् ।
अन्तरिक्षगतां रामः
चिच्छेद बहुधा शरैः ॥
Rāma, with many arrows, shattered into pieces that flaming club, which was fast approaching like the noose of death, while it was still in flight.
3.29.28
सा विकीर्णा शरैर्भग्ना
पपात धरणीतले ।
गदा मन्त्रौषधबलैः
व्यालीव विनिपातिता ॥
Broken by the arrows and shattered into pieces that club fell to the ground like a female serpent downed by the power of spells and herbs.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे एकोनत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty ninth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
