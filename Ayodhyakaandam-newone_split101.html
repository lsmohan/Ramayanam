Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 101
In this Sarga, Rāma asks why Bharata came to the Vana wearing robes of bark and matted locks. Bharata tells him that Daṡaratha died in grief over him, having done what he could not, at the behest of Kaikēyee.
Bharata then pleads with Rāma, on his own behalf and on the behalf of all the ministers and the subjects of the kingdom, to return to the kingdom and crown himself as the king.
Rāma appreciates Bharata for his strength of character and says that he should not reproach his mother. He asserts that the elders enjoy the liberty to demand anything from their capable children, wives and Ṡishyas.
He says that Daṡaratha, in his best judgment, has apportioned the rule of the kingdom to Bharata and life in Daṇḍaka forest to him, and as such, both of them are obliged to follow that command.
Rāma further asserts that he would consider that whatever is enjoined on him by his father, a revered man, would do utmost good to him.
2.101.1
तं तु रामस्समाश्वास्य
भ्रातरं गुरुवत्सलम् ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
प्रष्टुं समुपचक्रमे ॥
Rāma, receiving Bharata who was affectionate to elders, began to ask him, along with Lakshmaṇa:
2.101.2
किमेतदिच्छेयमहं
श्रोतुं प्रव्याहृतं त्वया ।
यस्मात्त्वमागतो देशम्
इमं चीरजटाजिनः ॥
I would like to hear you tell me why you have come to this area wearing robes of bark, matted locks and deerskin!
2.101.3
किं निमित्तमिमं देशं
कृष्णाजिनजटाधरः ।
हित्वा राज्यं प्रविष्टस्त्वं
तत्सर्वं वक्तुमर्हसि ॥
Why did you, apparently clad in the skin of black deer and wearing matted locks, enter this region, leaving the kingdom? Please tell me all about it!
2.101.4
इत्युक्तः कैकयीपुत्रः
काकुत्स्थेन महात्मना ।
प्रगृह्य बलवद्भूयः
प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Thus asked by Kākutstha, the Mahātma, the son of Kaikēyee, holding him tightly again, said these words, with joined palms:
2.101.5
आर्यं तातः परित्यज्य
कृत्वा कर्म सुदुष्करम् ।
गतस्स्वर्गं महाबाहुः
पुत्रशोकाभिपीडितः ॥
Our father, he of the mighty arm, after doing the most difficult of tasks of abjuring you, the noble one, left to Heaven, tormented by the grief for his son.
2.101.6
स्त्रिया नियुक्तः कैकेय्या
मम मात्रा परन्तप ।
चकार सुमहत्पापम्
इदमात्मयशोहरम् ॥
O scorcher of the foe! Constrained to it by my mother Kaikēyee, a woman, he committed a great sin that ruined his reputation.
2.101.7
सा राज्यफलमप्राप्य
विधवा शोककर्शिता ।
पतिष्यति महाघोरे
निरये जननी मम ॥
That mother of mine, widowed, worn out by grief and having failed to get the fruit of the Kingdom, will fall into a dreadful hell.
2.101.8
तस्य मे दासभूतस्य
प्रसादं कर्तुमर्हसि ।
अभिषिञ्चस्व चाधैव
राज्येन मघवानिव ॥
Crown yourself like Indra, this very day as the ruler of this kingdom. You must do me, your servant, this kindness.
2.101.9
इमाः प्रकृतयस्सर्वा
विधवा मातरश्च याः ।
त्वत्सकाशमनुप्राप्ता
प्रसादं कर्तुमर्हसि ॥
All these subjects and our widowed mothers have come to you; bless them with your grace.
2.101.10
तदानुपूर्व्या युक्तं च
युक्तं चात्मनि मानद ।
राज्यं प्राप्नुहि धर्मेण
सकामान्सुहृदः कुरु ॥
O you you upholds everyone's dignity! Take this kingdom, in accordance with Dharma, that has rightly come to you hereditarily, and thus fulfill the wish of all your well-wishers.
2.101.11
भवत्वविधवा भूमिः
समग्रा पतिना त्वया ।
शशिना विमलेनेव
शारदी रजनी यथा ॥
May this vast earth be saved by your lordship from becoming a widow, like a post-monsoon night, by the clear moon.
2.101.12
एभिश्च सचिवैस्सार्धं
शिरसा याचितो मया ।
भ्रातुश्शिष्यस्य दासस्य
प्रसादं कर्तुमर्हसि ॥
I beseech you bowing my head along with these ministers. May you shower your kindness on this brother, Ṡishya and servant (of yours).
2.101.13
तदिदं शाश्वतं पित्र्यं
सर्वं प्रकृतिमण्डलम् ।
पूजितं पुरुषव्याघ्र
नातिक्रमितुमर्हसि ॥
O tiger among men! You should not overlook (the wish of) this well regarded wide spectrum of subjects who belong to our father and generations prior.
2.101.14
एवमुक्त्वा महाबाहुः
सबाष्पः कैकयीसुतः ।
रामस्य शिरसा पादौ
जग्राह विधिवत्पुनः ॥
Having said this, the son of Kaikēyee, he of the mighty arm, again bowed his head tearfully at Rāma's feet in a custom honored manner.
2.101.15
तं मत्तमिव मातङ्गं
निःश्वसन्तं पुनः पुनः ।
भ्रातरं भरतं रामः
परिष्वज्येदमब्रवीत् ॥
Then Rāma took his brother Bharata, who was sighing repeatedly like an elephant in rut, into his embrace and said:
2.101.16
कुलीनस्सत्त्वसम्पन्नः
तेजस्वी चरितव्रतः ।
राज्यहेतोः कथं पापम्
आचरेत्त्वद्विधो जनः ॥
The likes of you, who is born into a noble family, who is endowed with great strength, who is supremely radiant and is of proven character, would never indulge in vice for the sake of kingdom.
2.101.17
न दोषं त्वयि पश्यामि
सूक्ष्ममप्यरिसूदन ।
न चापि जननीं बाल्यात्
त्वं विगर्हितुमर्हसि ॥
O annihilator of foes! I do not see even an iota of fault in you! Your mother should not be reproached by you either, out of immaturity!
2.101.18
कामकारो महाप्राज्ञ
गुरूणां सर्वदाऽनघ ।
उपपन्नेषु दारेषु
पुत्रेषु च विधीयते ॥
O sagacious and blameless one, Elders are at liberty to demand whatever they wish from capable wives and children!
2.101.19
वयमस्य यथा लोके
सङ्ख्यातास्सौम्य साधुभिः ।
भार्याः पुत्राश्च शिष्याश्च
त्वमनुज्ञातुमर्हसि ॥
O gentle one! You should understand that we are those  (capable) wives, sons and Ṡishyas of him,  of whom it was said so by the great seers of the world!
2.101.20
वने वा चीरवसनं
सौम्य कृष्णाजिनाम्बरम् ।
राज्ये वाऽपि महाराजो
मां वासयितुमीश्वरः ॥
The King has as much authority to make me live in the forest, clad in tree bark and the skin of the black buck, as in the Kingdom.
2.101.21
यावत्पितरि धर्मज्ञे
गौरवं लोकसत्कृतम् ।
तावद्धर्मभृतां श्रेष्ठ
जनन्यामपि गौरवम् ॥
O you, the first one to uphold Dharma! A mother also deserves the same respect that the world expects should be shown to a father who knows Dharma.
2.101.22
एताभ्यां धर्मशीलाभ्यां
वनं गच्छेति राघव ।
मातापितृभ्यामुक्तोऽहं
कथमन्यत्समाचरे ॥
When I was told, 'go to Vana' by my Dharma adherent father and mother, how could I, O Rāghava, do otherwise?
It must be noted, as Rāma makes it clear here, that neither Kaikēyee, nor Daṡaratha crossed the line of Dharma in what they have done.
Kaikēyee was legitimate in demanding the boons. Daṡaratha had no choice but to oblige, to conform to Dharma. It is the Artha, the purpose, that was put at risk by Kaikēyee.
Kaikēyee, provoked by Manthara, felt scared of the imagined dominance of her co-wife, Kousalyā, over her. And she thought that she could prevent that fate by using the boons.
But she did not understand that it is not that simple. She did not know that she was throwing the entire kingdom into chaos and her own life into terrible disgrace and that her own son would abhor her.
2.101.23
त्वया राज्यमयोध्यायां
प्राप्तव्यं लोकसत्कृतम् ।
वस्तव्यं दण्डकारण्ये
मया वल्कलवाससा ॥
You must rule the kingdom living in Ayōdhyā, well commended by people. I must live in the Daṇḍaka forest, wearing robes of bark.
2.101.24
एवं कृत्वा महाराजो
विभागं लोकसन्निधौ ।
व्यादिश्य च महातेजा
दिवं दशरथो गतः ॥
Having effected this division in the presence of everyone, and issued orders accordingly, the supremely radiant great king Daṡaratha has gone to Heaven.
2.101.25
स च प्रमाणं धर्मात्मा
राजा लोकगुरुस्तव ।
पित्रा दत्तं यथाभागम्
उपभोक्तुं त्वमर्हसि ॥
And he, the King, a Dharmātma, a man of stature in the world, should be sufficient authority for you. You should enjoy the share allotted to you by our father.
2.101.26
चतुर्दश समास्सौम्य
दण्डकारण्यमाश्रितः ।
उपभोक्ष्येत्वहं दत्तं
भागं पित्रा महात्मना ॥
O gentle one! As for me, I shall enjoy life in the Daṇḍaka forest for fourteen years, the share given to me by our father, the Mahātma.
2.101.27
यदब्रवीन्मां नरलोकसत्कृतः
पिता महात्मा विबुधाधिपोपमः ।
तदेव मन्ये परमात्मनो हितं
न सर्वलोकेश्वर भावमप्ययम् ॥
Whatever my father, the Mahātma, who was honored by the world of men and who was comparable to the Lord of the Dēvas, enjoined on me, is what I regard as of utmost good to me over even the lordship of all the worlds!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकोत्तरशततमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and first Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
