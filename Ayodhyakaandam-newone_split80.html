Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 80
This Sarga describes the massive and quick construction of roads and camp houses for Bharata and his army that stretch along the banks of river Gaṅgā.
2.80.1
अथ भूमिप्रदेशज्ञाः
सूत्रकर्मविशारदाः ।
स्वकर्माभिरताश्शूराः
खनका यन्त्रकास्तथा ।
कर्मान्तिकाः स्थपतयः
पुरुष यन्त्रकोविदाः ।
तथा वार्धकयश्चैव
मार्गिणो वृक्षतक्षकाः ।
कूपकारास्सुधाकाराः
वंशकर्मकृतस्तथा ।
समर्था ये च द्रष्टारः
पुरतस्ते प्रतस्थिरे ॥
Then land surveyors, measurement experts, strong and committed workers, excavators, machine operators, skilled workers, architects, engineers, carpenters, road layers, tree cutters, well drillers, masons, bamboo craftsmen and competent supervisors headed out in advance.
Again, this Sarga shows how timeless Rāmāyaṇa really is. If one reads this Sarga in isolation without the context, one would easily mistake it to be the description of a massive construction project of contemporary times that is done on a war-footing.
Also, this Sarga is suggestive of the massive opulence, power and capabilities of the kingdom that Rāma left behind without a second thought.
2.80.4
स तु हर्षात्तमुद्देशम्
जनौघो विपुलः प्रयान् ।
अशोभत महावेगः
समुद्र इव पर्वणि ॥
That great multitude of people proceeding joyfully on that task shined like a sea that burst into a swell on a full moon day.
2.80.5
ते स्ववारं समास्थाय
वर्त्मकर्मणि कोविदाः ।
करणैर्विविधोपेतैः
पुरस्तात्सम्प्रतस्थिरे ॥
The experts in laying the roads went ahead along with their teams, taking a variety of tools with them.
2.80.6
लतावल्ली श्च गुल्मांश्च
स्थाणूनश्मन एव च ।
जनायांचक्रिरे मार्गम्
छिन्दन्तो विविधान्द्रुमान् ॥
They removed creepers, bushes and stones, smashed boulders and cut many kinds of trees and laid the road.
2.80.7
अवृक्षेषु च देवेषु
केचिद्वृक्षानरोपयन् ।
केचित्कुठारैष्टङ्कैश्च
दात्रैश्चिन्दन्क्वचित्क्वचित् ॥
Some of them planted trees where there were none. Others, with axes, hatchets and saws cut (where there were many).
2.80.8
अपरे वीरणस्तम्भान्
बलिनो बलवत्तराः ।
विधमन्ति स्म दुर्गाणि
स्थलानि च तत स्ततः ॥
And other men who were strong pulled strong rooted reeds. Others leveled the mounds that were found here and there.
2.80.9
अपरेऽपूरयन्कूपान्
पांसुभिश्श्वभ्रमायतम् ।
निम्नभागान्स्ततः केचित्
समान्श्चक्रु स्समन्ततः ॥
Others filled up deep and wide pits with earth. Yet others leveled the depressed spots.
2.80.10
बबन्धुर्बन्धनीयांश्च
क्षोद्यान्सञ्चुक्षुदुस्तदा ।
बिभिदुर्भेदनीयांश्च
तांस्तान्देशान्नरा स्तदा ॥
They bridged wherever it could be bridged, crushed wherever it could be crushed and blasted whatever needed to be blasted.
2.80.11
अचिरेणैव कालेन
परिवाहान्बहूदकान् ।
चक्रुर्बहुविधाकारान्
सागरप्रतिमान्बहून् ॥
In no time they created reservoirs of different shapes that resembled oceans with plentiful water.
2.80.12
निर्जलेषु च देशेषु
खानयामासुरुत्तमान् ।
उदपानान्बहुविधान्
वेदिकापरिमण्डितान् ॥
In places where there was no water, they dug majestic wells and set up a variety of benches around them.
2.80.13
ससुधाकुट्टिमतलः
प्रपुष्पितमहीरुहः ।
मत्तोद्घुष्ट द्विजगणः
पताकाभिरलङ्कृतः ।
चन्दनोदकसंसिक्तो
नानाकुसुमभूषितः ।
बह्वशोभत सेनायाः
पन्थास्सुरपथोपमः ॥
The roads thus made for the army shone like the roads of Heaven, paved smooth and plastered with lime, lined with flowering trees that attracted flocks of intoxicated, noisy birds, adorned with flags and buntings, sprinkled with sandal flavored water, and decorated with a great variety of flowers.
2.80.15
आज्ञाप्याथ यथाऽज्ञप्ति
युक्तास्तेऽधिकृता नराः ।
रमणीयेषु देशेषु
बहुस्वादुफलेषु च ।
यो निवेशस्त्वभिप्रेतो
भरतस्य महात्मनः ।
भूयस्तं शोभयामासुः
भूषाभिर्भूषणोपमम् ॥
The men who were in-charge, committing themselves to their tasks, passing down appropriate instructions, built resplendent camp houses for Mahātma Bharata according to specifications in exotic places filled with trees bearing fruits of great taste, with decorations that glistened like jewels.
2.80.17
नक्षत्रेषु प्रशस्तेषु
मुहूर्तेषु च तद्विदः ।
निवेशान् स्थापयामासुः
भरतस्य महात्मनः ॥
The men who were conversant with the conjunctions of stars with moon, commenced the camps of Mahatma Bharata at auspicious moments.
2.80.18
बहुपांसुचयाश्चापि
परिखापरिवारिताः ।
तत्रेन्द्रकीलप्रतिमाः
प्रतोलीवरशोभिताः ।
प्रासादमालावितताः
सौधप्राकारसंवृताः ।
पताकाशोभितास्सर्वे
सुनिर्मितमहापथाः ।
विसर्पद्भिरिवाऽकाशे
विटङ्काग्रविमानकैः ।
समुच्छ्रितैर्निवेशास्ते
बभुश्शक्रपुरोपमाः ॥
Those camps shined like the city of Indra, enclosed by moats, protected by sand heaps, looking like the mountains of Indrakeela, with the best of roads and well laid highways, with rows of towering buildings and palaces enclosed in compound walls, decorated with flags and pennants, and residences seeming to float in the sky with the their turrets peaking with Vimānas.
2.80.21
जाह्नवीं तु समासाद्य
विविधद्रुमकाननाम् ।
शीतलामलपानीयाम्
महामीनसमाकुलाम् ॥
(The camps and roads) reached up to river Jahnavi (Gaṅgā) that boasted of woods of a variety of trees, abounding in large fish and clear and cool waters.
2.80.22
सचन्द्रतारागणमण्डितं यथा
नभः क्षपायाममलं विराजते ।
नरेन्द्रमार्गस्स तथा व्यराजत
क्रमेण रम्यः शुभशिल्पिनिर्मितः ॥
Just as the sky shines in the night illuminated gradually by moon and stars, that royal pathway shined resplendently, as it was built gradually by the best of architects.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे अशीतितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eightieth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
