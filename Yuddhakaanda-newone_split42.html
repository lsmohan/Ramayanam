Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 42
In this Sarga, the Rākshasas report to Rāvaṇa that Laṅkā is under attack. He climbs on to the terrace of his palace and sees for himself the sea of Vānaras that surround Laṅkā.
Meanwhile, the sight of Laṅkā makes Rāma think of the suffering of Seetā. He becomes overwhelmed with grief and orders the Vānara army to charge forth.
Prepared to lay down their lives in the cause of Rāma, they attack Laṅkā with boulders and trees. They fill up the moats with earth, rocks, grass and logs of wood, and dash towards the ramparts, smash them and get inside. The various captains of the Vānara forces seize the four entrances as planned, hailing victory to Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva.
The outraged Rāvaṇa then unleashes his forces, who jump into the fray like the surge of an ocean, with the cries of ‘Victory to the King’. They create a mighty din of blaring conches, which is mixed with the trumpeting of elephants, neighing of horses and the rumble of chariots.
Then ensues a battle between Rākshasas and Vānaras creating a mire of flesh and blood.
6.42.1
ततस्ते राक्षसास्तत्र
गत्वा रावणमन्दिरम् ।
न्यवेदयन् पुरीं रुद्धाम्
रामेण सह वानरैः ॥
The Rākshasas then went to Rāvaṇa’s mansion and told him that the city was besieged by Rāma and the Vānaras.
6.42.2
रुद्धां तु नगरीं श्रुत्वा
जातक्रोधो निशाचरः ।
विधानं द्विगुणं कृत्वा
प्रासादं सोऽध्यरोहत ॥
On hearing that the city was seized the Night-rover became outraged. Doubling the defenses, he climbed on to the top of his palace.
6.42.3
स ददर्शावृतां लङ्काम्
सशैलवनकाननाम् ।
असङ्ख्येयैर्हरिगणैः
सर्वतो युद्धकाङ्क्षिभिः ॥
He saw Laṅkā along with its hills, woods and forests surrounded on all sides by numberless companies of Vānaras lusting for battle.
6.42.4
स दृष्ट्वा वानरैः सर्वाम्
वसुधां कवलीकृताम् ।
कथं क्षपयितव्याः स्युः
इति चिन्तापरोऽभवत् ॥
Seeing the whole earth gobbled up by the Vānaras as it were, he was plunged in thought, wondering how they could be destroyed.
6.42.5
स चिन्तयित्वा सुचिरम्
धैर्यमालम्ब्य रावणः ।
राघवं हरियूथांश्च
ददर्शायतलोचनः ॥
Having thought for a while and summoning courage, Rāvaṇa gazed at Rāghava and the Vānara companies, wide-eyed in wonder.
6.42.6
राघवः सह सैन्येन
मुदितो नाम पुप्लुवे ।
लङ्कां ददर्श गुप्तां वै
सर्वतो राक्षसैर्वृताम् ॥
(At the same time), Rāghava cheerfully dashed forward along with the army, and beheld Laṅkā surrounded and protected on all sides by the Rākshasas.
6.42.7
दृष्ट्वा दाशरथिर्लङ्काम्
चित्रध्वजपताकिनीम् ।
जगाम सहसा सीताम्
दूयमानेन चेतसा ॥
On seeing Laṅkā with its beautiful flags and buntings, the thoughts of the son of Daṡaratha turned at once to Seetā, his heart wrenched with grief.
6.42.8
अत्र सा मृगशावाक्षी
मत्कृते जनकात्मजा ।
पीड्यते शोकसन्तप्ता
कृशा स्थण्डिलशायिनी ॥
Here, longing for me, suffers the fawn-eyed daughter of Janaka, tormented, worn out, burnt by grief and lying down on the bare ground.
6.42.9
पीड्यमानां स धर्मात्मा
वैदेहीमनुचिन्तयन् ।
क्षिप्रमाज्ञापयामास
वानरान् द्विषतां वधे ॥
The sufferings of Vaidēhee thus overwhelming his mind and tormenting him, that Dharmātma ordered the Vānaras forthwith to proceed towards the destruction of the enemy.
6.42.10
एवमुक्ते तु वचने
रामेणाक्लिष्टकर्मणा ।
सङ्घर्षमाणाः प्लवगाः
सिंहनादैरनादयन् ॥
When Rāma, who did things with ease, commanded thus, the Vānaras vied with each other to let off battle-cries!
6.42.11
शिखरैर्विकिरामैनाम्
लङ्कां मुष्टिभिरेव वा ।
इति स्म दधिरे सर्वे
मनांसि हरियूथपाः ॥
The Vānara captains made a resolve in their minds that they would smash Laṅkā with boulders or even with their bare fists alone.
6.42.12
उद्यम्य गिरिशृङ्गाणि
शिखराणि महान्ति च ।
तरूंश्चोत्पाट्य विविधान्
तिष्ठन्ति हरियूथपाः ॥
The captains of the Vānara army stood, holding up mountain crests and huge peaks and pulling out all kinds of trees.
6.42.13
प्रेक्षतो राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
तान्यनीकानि भागशः ।
राघवप्रियकामार्थम्
लङ्कामारुरुहुस्तदा ॥
Before the very eyes of the lord of the Rākshasas, each of those contingents climbed up Laṅkā, to fulfill the dear wish of Rāghava.
6.42.14
ते ताम्रवक्त्रा हेमाभा
रामार्थे त्यक्तजीविताः ।
लङ्कामेवाभ्यवर्तन्त
सालतालशिलायुधाः ॥
Those Vānaras with their red faces and golden complexions, focused completely on the assault on Laṅkā armed with palm and pine trees and boulders, prepared to lay down their lives for Rāma’s sake.
6.42.15
ते द्रुमैः पर्वताग्रैश्च
मुष्टिभिश्च प्लवङ्गमाः ।
प्राकाराग्राण्यसंख्यानि
ममन्थुस्तोरणानि च ॥
With tree-trunks, boulders and their doubled fists they smashed numberless tops of ramparts and gateways.
6.42.16
परिखाः पूरयन्ति स्म
प्रसन्नसलिलायुताः ।
पांसुभिः पर्वताग्रैश्च
तृणैः काष्ठैश्च वानराः ॥
The Vānaras filled the moats of clear water with earth, rocks, grass and logs of wood.
6.42.17
ततः सहस्रयूथाश्च
कोटीयूथाश्च वानराः ।
कोटीशतयुताश्चान्ये
लङ्कामारुरुहुस्तदा ॥
Then the Vānara warriors, in their countless thousands and crores and others in their hundreds of crores climbed up into Laṅkā.
6.42.18
काञ्चनानि प्रमर्दन्तः
तोरणानि प्लवङ्गमाः ।
कैलासशिखराभाणि
गोपुराणि प्रमथ्य च ॥
The Vānaras smashed golden archways and bulldozed towers that resembled the peaks of Kailāsa mountain.
6.42.19
आप्लवन्तः प्लवन्तश्च
गर्जन्तश्च प्लवङ्गमाः ।
लङ्कां तामभिधावन्ति
महावारणसन्निभाः ॥
The Vānaras, resembling great tuskers, dashed towards Laṅkā, roaring and jumping up and down.
6.42.20
जयत्यतिबलो रामो
लक्ष्मणश्च महाबलः ।
राजा जयति सुग्रीवो
राघवेणाभिपालितः ।
इत्येवं घोषयन्तश्च
गर्जन्तश्च प्लवङ्गमाः ।
अभ्यधावन्त लङ्कायाः
प्राकारं कामरूपिणः ॥
The Vānaras who could take any form at will dashed towards the ramparts of Laṅkā roaring and hailing, ‘Victory to the exceedingly strong Rāma, and immensely strong Lakshmaṇa and to the king Sugreeva who is under the care and command of Rāghava!’
6.42.22
वीरबाहुः सुबाहुश्च
नलश्च वनगोचरः ।
निपीड्योपनिविष्टास्ते
प्राकारं हरियूथपाः ।
एतस्मिन्नन्तरे चक्रुः
स्कन्धावारनिवेशनम् ॥
Meanwhile, Veerabāhu, Subāhu and Nala, the Vana-dwellers, captains of the Vānaras, battered their way through the outer rampart and got a foothold inside.
6.42.23
पूर्वद्वारं तु कुमुदः
कोटीभिर्दशभिर्वृतः ।
आवृत्य बलवांस्तस्थौ
हरिभिर्जितकाशिभिः ॥
Kumuda, the strong one, seized the eastern gate with ten crore Vānaras diplaying victorious glee.
6.42.24
साहाय्यार्थं तु तस्यैव
निविष्टः प्रघसो हरिः ।
पनसश्च महाबाहुः
वानरैर्बहुभिर्वृतः ॥
To support him, the Vānara Praghasa and Panasa of the mighty arm were stationed nearby, each supported in turn by a multitude of Vānaras.
6.42.25
दक्षिणं द्वारमागम्य
वीरः शतवलिः कपिः ।
आवृत्य बलवांस्तस्थौ
विंशत्या कोटिभिर्वृतः ॥
The valiant and strong Vānara, Ṡatabali, went to the southern gate and seized it, supported by twenty crore Vānaras.
6.42.26
सुषेणः पश्चिमद्वारम्
गतस्तारापिता हरिः ।
आवृत्य बलवांस्तस्थौ
षष्टिकोटिभिरावृतः ॥
The strong Vānara Sushēṇa, father of Tāra seized and stood by the western entrance supported by sixty crores (of Vānaras).
6.42.27
उत्तरं द्वारमासाद्य
रामः सौमित्रिणा सह ।
आवृत्य बलवांस्तस्थौ
सुग्रीवश्च हरीश्वरः ॥
The powerful Rāma, along with Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva, the lord of the Vānaras, reached the northern entrance, and besieged it.
6.42.28
गोलाङ्गूलो महाकायो
गवाक्षो भीमदर्शनः ।
वृतः कोट्या महावीर्यः
तस्थौ रामस्य पार्श्वतः ॥
Gavāksha, the Gōlāṅgūlas of humongous body, terrible in appearance and extraordinary in valor stood by the side of Rāma with a crore of his followers.
6.42.29
ऋक्षाणां भीमवेगानाम्
धूम्रः शत्रुनिबर्हणः ।
वृतः कोट्या महावीर्यः
तस्थो रामस्य पार्श्वतः ॥
Dhūmra, destroyer of the foe, supported by a crore of Ṛkshas of frightful speed, also stood by the side of Rāma.
6.42.30
सन्नद्धस्तु महावीर्यः
गदापाणिर्विभीषणः ।
वृतो यत्तैस्तु सचिवैः
तस्थौ तत्र महाबलः ॥
And Vibheeshaṇa of immense strength and extreme valor, armed with a mace, stood there ready (for the fray), along with his ministers who were equally ready.
6.42.31
गजो गवाक्षो गवयः
शरभो गन्धमादनः ।
समन्तात् परिधावन्तो
ररक्षुर्हरिवाहिनीम् ॥
Gaja, Gavāksha, Gavaya, Ṡarabha and Gandhamādana patrolled the Vānara army from every side.
6.42.32
ततः कोपपरीतात्मा
रावणो राक्षसेश्वरः ।
निर्याणं सर्वसैन्यानाम्
द्रुतमाज्ञापयत्तदा ॥
Thereupon Rāvaṇa, the lord of the Rākshasas, beside himself with fury, ordered the instant marching out of all his armies.
6.42.33
एतच्छुत्वा ततो वाक्यम्
रावणस्य मुखोद्गतम् ।
सहसा भीमनिर्घोषम्
उद्घुष्टं रजनीचरैः ॥
The moment they heard those words come out of the mouth of Rāvaṇa the Night-rovers raised thunderous shouts.
6.42.34
ततः प्रचोदिता भेर्यः
चन्द्रपाण्डरपुष्कराः ।
हेमकोणाहता भीमा
राक्षसानां समन्ततः ॥
And then, drums with moon-white faces, beaten by the Rākshasas with golden drumsticks, crashed on the ear everywhere.
6.42.35
विनेदुश्च महाघोषाः
शङ्खाः शतसहस्रशः ।
राक्षसानां सुघोराणाम्
मुखमारुतपूरिताः ॥
Hundreds of thousands of conches, blown by frightful Rākshasas, blared, creating a mighty din.
6.42.36
ते बभुः शुभनीलाङ्गाः
सशङ्खाः रजनीचराः ।
विद्युन्मण्डलसन्नद्धाः
सबलाका इवाम्बुदाः ॥
With those (white) conches against their bright black bodies, the Night-rovers shone like clouds highlighted by lightning and cranes flying across.
6.42.37
निष्पतन्ति ततः सैन्या
हृष्टा रावणचोदिताः ।
समये पूर्यमाणस्य
वेगा इव महोदधेः ॥
Those armies, excited by the call of Rāvaṇa, surged out, like the magnificent ocean that swiftly inundates (the worlds) at the end of Time.
6.42.38
ततो वानरसैन्येन
मुक्तो नादः समन्ततः ।
मलयः पूरितो येन
स सानुप्रस्थकन्दरः ॥
Thereupon there arose a great din everywhere from the Vānara army, which filled the Malaya mountain and all its peaks, hollows and caves.
6.42.39
शङ्खदुन्दुभिसङ्घुष्टः
सिंहनादस्तरस्विनाम् ।
पृथिवीं चान्तरिक्षं च
सागरं चैव नादयन् ।
गजानां बृंहितैः सार्धम्
हयानां हेषितैरपि ।
रथानां नेमिघोषैश्च
रक्षसां पदनिःस्वनैः ॥
The battle shouts of those powerful (warriors), mixed with the sounds of conch and kettle-drum, along with the trumpeting of elephants, neighing of horses, the rumbling of chariot wheels, and the stamping (of the ground) by the Rākshasas, reverberated over the entire earth, sky and ocean.
6.42.41
एतस्मिन्नन्तरे घोरः
सङ्ग्रामः समवर्तत ।
रक्षसां वानराणां च
यथा देवासुरे पुरा ॥
With that, there ensued a terrible fight between the Rākshasas and the Vānaras, like the one between the Suras and the Asuras once upon a time.
6.42.42
ते गदाभिः प्रदीप्ताभिः
शक्तिशूलपरश्वधैः ।
निजघ्नुर्वानरान् घोराः
कथयन्तः स्वविक्रमान् ॥
Those terrible Rākshasas, boasting their valor, attacked the Vānaras with shining maces, javelins, spears and hatchets.
6.42.43
राजा जयति सुग्रीव
इति शब्दो महानभूत् ।
तथा वृक्षैर्महाकायाः
पर्वताग्रैश्च वानराः ।
निजघ्नुस्तानि रक्षांसि
नखैर्दन्तैश्च वेगिताः ॥
The Vānaras of humongous bodies swiftly attacked the Rākshasas with boulders and trees, while the air reverberated with the cries of ‘Victory to the king Sugreeva!’
6.42.45
राजन् जय जयेत्युक्त्वा
स्वस्वनामकथान्ततः ।
राक्षसास्त्वपरे भीमाः
प्राकारस्था महीगतान् ।
वानरान् भिन्दिपालैश्च
शूलैश्चैव व्यदारयन् ॥
Then other fierce Rākshasas, proclaiming their own names and hailing, ‘Victory to the King!’ from the ramparts, hacked the Vānaras who were on the ground, with Bhindipālas and spears.
6.42.46
वानराश्चापि सङ्क्रुद्धाः
प्राकारस्थान् महीगताः ।
राक्षसान् पातयामासुः
समाप्लुत्य प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
The Vānaras who were on the ground also became enraged and flew up and threw down the Rākshasas who were on the ramparts.
6.42.47
स सम्प्रहारस्तुमुलो
मांसशोणितकर्दमः ।
रक्षसां वानराणां च
सम्बभूवाद्भुतोपमः ॥
That tumultuous fight between Rākshasas and Vānaras, which created a mire of flesh and blood was a spectacle to behold and marvel.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्विचत्वारिंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty second Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
