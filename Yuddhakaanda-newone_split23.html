Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 23
In this Sarga, Rāma describes the bad and terrifying omens he sees all around, which portend that a war is certain and so is the imminent loss of life both among Rākshasas and Vānaras.
Rāma, Lakshmaṇa, Sugreeva, Vibheeshaṇa and all the Vānaras march forward to attack Laṅkā from all sides.
6.23.1
निमित्तानि निमित्तज्ञो
दृष्ट्वा लक्ष्मणपूर्वजः ।
सौमित्रिं सम्परिष्वज्य
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Lakshmaṇa’s elder brother, knowledgeable about (various kinds of) omens, saw the omens (that then developed) and said these words to Sowmitri, as he embraced him:
6.23.2
परिगृह्योदकं शीतम्
वनानि फलवन्ति च ।
बलौघं संविभज्येमम्
व्यूह्य तिष्ठेम लक्ष्मण ॥
Let us find woods that offer cool water and fruits to station the huge army, divided into small battalions.
6.23.3
लोकक्षयकरं भीमम्
भयं पश्याम्युपस्थितम् ।
निबर्हणं प्रवीराणाम्
ऋक्षवानररक्षसाम् ॥
I feel that a terrible disaster is imminent, which will wipe out a large population and destroy the bear, Vānara and Rākshasa warriors.
Rāma, based on the omens, does not see the possibility of Rāvaṇa returning Seetā and hence sees that war is imminent.
6.23.4
वाताश्च कलुषा वान्ति
कम्पते च वसुन्धरा ।
पर्वताग्राणि वेपन्ते
पतन्ति च महीरुहाः ॥
The wind is blowing, carrying dust. The ground is in tremor, the mountain crests are shaking and the trees are tumbling down.
6.23.5
मेघाः क्रव्यादसङ्काशाः
परुषाः परुषस्वनाः ।
क्रूराः क्रूरं प्रवर्षन्ति
मिश्रं शोणितबिन्दुभिः ॥
Clouds look like Rākshasas, pugnacious with a grating rumble. They look savage, with their showers vicious mixed with drops of blood.
6.23.6
रक्तचन्दनसङ्काशा
सन्ध्या परमदारुणा ।
ज्वलतः प्रपतत्येतत्
आदित्यादग्निमण्डलम् ॥
The evening sky is extremely terrifying glowing like a paste of red sandal. The flaming sun is spewing balls of fire.
6.23.7
दीना दीनस्वराः क्रूराः
सर्वतो मृगपाक्षिणः ।
प्रत्यादित्यं विनर्दन्ति
जनयन्तो महद्भयम् ॥
Glum animals and birds everywhere bark and bray eerily at the sun, in despondent and terrifying tones, rousing a great fear.
6.23.8
रजन्यामप्रकाशस्तु
सन्तापयति चन्द्रमाः ।
कृष्णरक्तांशुपर्यन्तो
लोकक्षय इवोदितः ॥
The moon casts gloom everywhere, hardly illuminating the night. With red and black spots and rays, he seems to rise like he would when the world comes to an end.
6.23.9
ह्रस्वो रूक्षोऽप्रशस्तश्च
परिवेषः सुलोहितः ।
आदित्ये विमले नीलम्
लक्ष्म लक्ष्मण दृश्यते ॥
The halo around the sun has become small and insignificant; extremely reddish, it looks savage. O Lakshmaṇa! There seems to appear a black spot on the bright disc of the sun!
6.23.10
रजसा महता चापि
नक्षत्राणि हतानि च ।
युगान्तमिव लोकानाम्
पश्य शंसन्ति लक्ष्मण ॥
And, yonder, Lakshmaṇa, the stars obscured by the pall of thick dust seem to presage the end of the Yuga!
6.23.11
काकाः श्येनास्तथा गृध्रा
नीचैः परिपतन्ति च ।
शिवाश्चाप्यशिवान्नादान्
नदन्ति सुमहाभयान् ॥
Crows, eagles and vultures hover at lower altitudes. Jackals wail terribly with their lugubrious cries.
6.23.12
शैलैः शूलैश्च खड्गैश्च
विसृष्टैः कपिराक्षसैः ।
भविष्यत्यावृता भूमिः
मांसशोणितकर्दमा ॥
The earth will be covered under the rocks, spears and swords hurled by the Vānaras and the Rākshasas and under the mire of flesh and gore.
6.23.13
क्षिप्रमद्यैव दुर्धर्षाम्
पुरीं रावणपालिताम् ।
अभियाम जवेनैव
सर्वतो हरिभिर्वृताः ॥
Let us invade from all sides with no further delay the impregnable city ruled by Rāvaṇa, taking the Vānaras with us.
6.23.14
इत्येवमुक्त्वा धर्मात्मा
धन्वी सङ्ग्रामधर्षणः ।
प्रतस्थे पुरतो रामो
लङ्कामभिमुखो विभुः ॥
Saying this, lord Rāma, the Dharmātma, who could stand up to any enemy in a battle, set out for Laṅkā, armed with the bow.
6.23.15
सविभीषणसुग्रीवाः
सर्वे ते वानरर्षभाः ।
प्रतस्थिरे विनर्दन्तो
निश्चिता द्विषतां वधे ॥
All the bulls among Vānaras, along with Sugreeva and Vibheeshaṇa followed, letting off strident shouts, resolved upon the slaughter of the foe.
6.23.16
राघवस्य प्रियार्थं तु
धृतानां वीर्यशालिनाम् ।
हरीणां कर्मचेष्टाभिः
तुतोष रघुनन्दनः ॥
The scion of the Raghus was vastly pleased as he saw the brave and valiant Vānaras exhibiting their feats of valor and skill to win his approbation.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे त्रयोविंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty third Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
