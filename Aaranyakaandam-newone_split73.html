Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 73
In this Sarga, Kabandha describes to Rāma the path that leads to the abode of Sugreeva, starting from the stretch that is abundant with various kinds of flowering trees, which leads to the lake Paṃpā of cool, clear and pure water, and then to the Mataṅga Āṡrama area where anchorite Ṡabari lives, and on to Ṛshyamūka mountain and then finally to the cave where Sugreeva lives along with four other Vānaras.
3.73.1
निदर्शयित्वा रामाय
सीतायाः प्रतिपादने ।
वाक्यमन्वर्थमर्थज्ञः
कबन्धः पुनरब्रवीत् ॥
Having told Rāma how to search for Seetā, Kabandha continued with apt and necessary information:
3.73.2
एष राम शिवः पन्था
यत्रैते पुष्पिता द्रुमाः ।
प्रतीचीं दिशमाश्रित्य
प्रकाशन्ते मनोरमाः ।
जम्बूप्रियालपनस
प्लक्षन्यग्रोधसिन्धुकाः ।
अश्वत्थाः कर्णिकाराश्च
चूताश्चान्ये च पादपाः ।
धन्वना नागवृक्षाश्च
तिलका नक्तमालकाः ।
नीलाशोकाः कदम्बाश्च
करवीराश्च पुष्पिताः ।
अग्निमुख्या अशोकाश्च
सुरक्ताः पारिभद्रकाः ॥
O Rāma! Take this nice west-bound path that enchants the heart, gleams with trees in flower, such as Jambū, Priyāla, Panasa, Plakshanya, Grōdha, Sindhuka, Aṡvattha, Karṇikāra, Cūtā, and others like Dhanvana, Nāgavṛksha, Tilakā, Naktamālaka, Nīlāṡōka, Kadamba, Karavīra, Agnimukhya, Aṡōka, Suraktāḥ and Pāribhadraka.
3.73.5
तानारुह्याथवा भूमौ
पातयित्वा च तान्बलात् ।
फलान्यमृतकल्पानि
भक्षयन्तौ गमिष्यथः ॥
Go on your way, eating the Amṛta-like fruits, getting to them by either climbing the trees or by forcing them to fall down.
3.73.6
तदतिक्रम्य काकुत्थ्स
वनं पुष्पितपादपम् ।
नन्दनप्रतिमं चान्यत्
कुरवो ह्युत्तरा इव ॥
After you pass through that Vana with trees in flower, you will find another one, which resembles Nandana and the Uttara-kuru region.
3.73.7
सर्वकामफला यत्र
पादपास्तु मधुरस्रवाः ।
सर्वे च ऋतवस्तत्र
वने चैत्ररथे यथा ॥
Giving fruits perennially that are juicy and sweet, that Vana has all the seasons at all times, like the Caitraratha.
3.73.8
फलभारानतास्तत्र
महाविटपधारिणः ।
शोभन्ते सर्वतस्तत्र
मेघपर्वतसन्निभाः ॥
Trees with huge boughs that are bowed down by the weight of their fruit, looking like clouds and mountains, adorn that area.
3.73.9
तानारुह्याथवा भूमौ
पातयित्वा यथासुखम् ।
फलान्यमृतकल्पानि
लक्ष्मणस्ते प्रदास्यति ॥
Lakshmaṇa can bring you those Amṛta-like fruit, either by climbing the trees or by shaking them down, whichever is easy.
3.73.10
चङ्क्रमन्तौ वरान्देशान्
शैलाच्छैलं वनाद्वनम् ।
ततः पुष्करिणीं वीरौ
पम्पां नाम गमिष्यथः ॥
Going from mountain to mountain and Vana to Vana, through that excellent region, O Veeras, you will reach the lake named Paṃpā.
3.73.11
अशर्करामविभ्रंशाम्
समतीर्थामशैवलाम् ।
राम सञ्जातवालूकाम्
कमलोत्पलशालिनीम् ॥
Its beaches are shallow uniformly, with no pebbles, no slippery spots and no mass. Sandy mounds rise up (here and there around it), and it is adorned with lotuses and black lilies.
3.73.12
तत्र हंसाः प्लवाः क्रौञ्चाः
कुरराश्चैव राघव ।
वल्गुस्वना विकूजन्ति
पम्पासलिलगोचराः ॥
O Rāghava! In the waters of Paṃpā can be seen swans, Plavas, Krouncas and Kuraras warbling sweetly.
3.73.13
नोद्विजन्ते नरान्दृष्ट्वा
वधस्याकोविदाश्शुभाः ।
घृतपिण्डोपमान् स्थूलान्
तान्द्विजान्भक्षयिष्यथः ॥
Those wonderful creatures are not afraid of people, as they know nothing of being killed. They are like solid balls of butter. You can eat those birds.
3.73.14
रोहितान्वक्रतुण्डांश्च
नडमीनांश्च राघव ।
पम्पायामिषुभिर्मत्स्यान्
तत्र राम वरान्हतान् ।
निस्त्वक्पक्षानयस्तप्तान्
अकृशानेककण्टकान् ।
तव भक्त्या समायुक्तो
लक्ष्मणस्सम्प्रदास्यति ॥
Lakshmaṇa with his shafts can catch the best and sumptuous fish from Paṃpā, the Rohitas, the Vakratundas and the Nadaminas, remove their bones, scales and fins, strip away the speckled skin, broil them on iron skewers, and serve them to you with all devotion.
3.73.16
भृशंते खादतो मत्स्यान्
पम्पायाः पुष्पसञ्चये ।
पद्मगन्धि शिवं वारि
सुखशीतमनामयम् ।
उद्धृत्य सतताक्लिष्टम्
रौप्यस्फाटिकसन्निभम् ।
असौ पुष्करपर्णेन
लक्ष्मणः पाययिष्यति ॥
As you thoroughly enjoy eating the fish, Lakshmaṇa can bring the best, healthy, pure and moderately cold water carrying the smell of lotuses and clear like crystal and silver, from the midst of the flowers in Paṃpā and serve you in a (cupped) lotus leaf, for a drink.
Wow! What a luxury! Would a drink served in a five-star hotel compare with this experience?
3.73.18
स्थूलान्गिरिगुहाशय्यान्
वराहान्वनचारिणः ।
अपां लोभादुपावृत्तान्
वृषभानिव नर्दतः ।
रूपान्वितांश्च पम्पायां
द्रक्ष्यसि त्वं नरोत्तम ॥
O best of men! You will see wild boars that are eye-catching, stout and bellowing like bulls, that rest in the mountain caves, coming to Paṃpā to drink water.
3.73.20
सायाह्ने विचरन्राम
विटपीन्माल्यधारिणः ।
शीतोदकं च पम्पाया
दृष्ट्वा शोकं विहास्यसि ॥
O Rāma! Strolling in the evenings, you will forget your grief watching the cool water of Paṃpā and the trees with garlands of flowers.
3.73.21
सुमनोभिश्चितांस्तत्र
तिलकान्नक्तमालकान् ।
उत्पलानि च फुल्लानि
पङ्कजानि च राघव ॥
O Rāghava! There you will see Tilaka and Naktamālaka trees in flower, spread all over, and lotuses and blue lilies in bloom.
3.73.22
न तानि कश्चिन्माल्यानि
तत्रारोपयिता नरः ।
न च वै म्लानतां यन्ति
न च शीर्यन्ति राघव ॥
O Rāghava! Those garlands of flowers are not plucked by anyone to wear; nor do they wilt, nor fall off.
3.73.23
मतङ्गशिष्यास्तत्राऽसन्
ऋषयस्सुसमाहिताः ।
तेषां भाराभितप्तानाम्
वन्यमाहरतां गुरोः ।
ये प्रपेतुर्महीं तूर्णम्
शरीरात्स्वेदबिन्दवः ।
तानि जातानि माल्यानि
मुनीनां तपसा तदा ।
स्वेदबिन्दुसमुत्थानि
न विनश्यन्ति राघव ॥
The Ṡishyas of Mataṅga, who are very focused Ṛshis, used to live there. As they toiled to collect the produce of the Vana for their Guru, drops of sweat that fell down on the ground from their bodies turned immediately into those garlands because of the power of their Tapa. O Rāghava, they never perish, as they came out of their sweat.
3.73.25
तेषां गतानामद्यापि
दृश्यते परिचारिणी ।
श्रमणी शबरी नाम
काकुत्स्थ चिरजीविनी ॥
O Kākutstha, while all of them have passed away, an anchorite by name Ṡabari who served them, who is blessed with long life, can be seen even today.
3.73.26
त्वां तु धर्मे स्थिता नित्यम्
सर्वभूतनमस्कृतम् ।
दृष्ट्वा देवोपमं राम
स्वर्गलोकं गमिष्यति ॥
O Rāma! She, a constant adherent of Dharma, will attain Heaven after seeing you, who are regarded by all the people as a Dēva.
3.73.27
ततस्तद्राम पम्पायाः
तीरमासाद्य पश्चिमम् ।
आश्रमस्थानमतुलम्
गुह्यं काकुत्स्थ पश्यसि ॥
O Rāma, descendant of Kakutstha! When you reach the western shore of Paṃpā, you will see (her) unique and hidden Āṡrama.
3.73.28
न तत्राक्रमितुं नागाः
शक्नुवन्ति तमाश्रमम् ।
विविधास्तत्र वै नागा
वने तस्मिंश्च पर्वते ।
ऋषेस्तत्र मतङ्गस्य
विधानात्तच्च काननम् ॥
Elephants cannot intrude into that Āṡrama, though there are many kinds on that mountain and in the Vana, because that forest obeys the rules of Mataṅga.
3.73.30
तस्मिन्नन्दनसङ्काशे
देवारण्योपमे वने ।
नानाविहगसङ्कीर्णे
रंस्यसे राम निर्वृतः ॥
O Rāma! You will forget all cares and enjoy in that woods, an equal of Nandana and the other celestial woods, crowded with birds of every kind.
3.73.31
ऋष्यमूकश्च पम्पायाः
पुरस्तात्पुष्पितद्रुमः ।
सुदुःखारोहणो नाम
शिशुनागाभिरक्षितः ।
उदारो ब्रह्मणा चैव
पूर्वकाले विनिर्मितः ॥
Ṛshyamūka mountain, full of trees in bloom, protected by baby elephants, difficult to climb, is ahead of Paṃpā. It is majestic, built by Brahma long time ago.
3.73.32
शयानः पुरुषो राम
तस्य शैलस्य मूर्धनि ।
यत्स्वप्ने लभते वित्तम्
तत्प्रबुद्धोऽधिगच्छति ॥
O Rāma! Anyone sleeping on the crest of the mountain, will attain, when he wakes up, all the treasures that he sees in his dream.
As you read this to your children (or grandchildren), do not be surprised if they ask you to take them immediately to Ṛshyamūka! They also want to see the baby elephants, in Mataṅga Āṡrama, on their way!
3.73.33
नत्वेनं विषमाचारः
पापकर्माऽधिरोहति ।
यस्तु तं विषमाचारः
पापकर्माऽधिरोहति ।
तत्रैव प्रहऱन्त्येनम्
सुप्तमादाय राक्षसाः ॥
No sinner, who harbors bad ways of life, (dare) climb up that mountain. If a sinner who harbors bad ways of life climbs up, he will be beaten up by Rākshasas when he sleeps.
Hearing this, your children will make a resolve in their mind that they will never harbor a bad way of life, so that they can one day go to Ṛshyamūka and dream about a treasure and get it when they wake up.
Do not spoil their dreams by saying Ṛshyamūka is fictional. When they grow up they will realize that it is symbolic of good opportunities that lie ahead of them.
3.73.35
तत्रापि शिशुनागानाम्
आक्रन्धश्श्रूयते महान् ।
क्रीडतां राम पम्पायाम्
मतङ्गारण्यवासिनाम् ॥
O Rāma! The loud trumpets of the baby elephants that live in the Mataṅga Araṇya can be heard even from there, as they play in Paṃpā.
3.73.36
सिक्ता रुधिरधाराभिः
संहृत्य परमद्विपाः ।
प्रचरन्ति पृथक्कीर्णा
मेघवर्णास्तरस्विनः ॥
Giant and mighty strong tuskers, dark as the clouds, with blood flowing from fighting each other, roam all over the place, all by themselves.
3.73.37
ते तत्र पीत्वा पानीयम्
विमलं शीतमव्ययम् ।
निर्वृतास्संविगाहन्ते
वनानि वनगोचराः ॥
Having contently drunk the pure, cool and abundant water, these wild creatures return to the depths of the Vana.
3.73.38
ऋक्षांश्च द्वीपिनश्चैव
नीलकोमलकप्रभान् ।
रुरूनपेतापजयान्
दृष्ट्वा शोकं जयिष्यसि ॥
Seeing the bears and the leopards of silky black sheen, and Rurus that elude pursuit, you will forget your grief.
3.73.39
राम तस्य तु शैलस्य
महती शोभते गुहा ।
शिलापिधाना काकुत्स्थ
दुःखं चास्याः प्रवेशनम् ॥
O Rāma, descendant of Kakutstha! That mountain is adorned with a great cave, which is covered by a boulder making it difficult to enter.
3.73.40
तस्या गुहायाः प्राग्द्वारे
महान्शीतोदको ह्रदः ।
फलमूलान्वितो रम्यो
नानामृगसमावृतः ॥
There is a big pool of cool water at its eastern entrance. The place abounds in fruits and roots and is delightful with many kinds of beasts gathering there.
3.73.41
तस्यां वसति सुग्रीवः
चतुर्भिस्सह वानरैः ।
कदाचिच्छिखरे तस्य
पर्वतस्यावतिष्ठते ॥
There lives Sugreeva along with four Vānaras, going, at times, to the top of the mountain.
3.73.42
कबन्धस्त्वनुशास्यैवम्
तावुभौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
स्रग्वी भास्करवर्णाभः
खे व्यरोचत वीर्यवान् ॥
Kabandha of great valor, adorned with a garland, shining in the sky with the brightness of sun, thus concluded his advice to both Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
3.73.43
तं तु खस्थं महाभागम्
कबन्धं रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
प्रस्थितौ त्वं व्रजस्वेति
वाक्यमूचतुरन्तिके ॥
And at the end of it, they, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, set out on their journey, told Kabandha, the blessed one, who was in the sky, ‘Farewell to you’.
3.73.44
गम्यतां कार्यसिद्ध्यर्थम्
इति तावब्रवीत्स च ।
सुप्रीतौ तावनुज्ञाप्य
कबन्धः प्रस्थितस्तदा ॥
‘May you proceed and be successful in your endeavor”, said Kabandha, a man of great discipline, to both of them, and left, taking proper leave of them.
3.73.45
स तत्कबन्धः प्रतिपद्य रूपं
वृतश्श्रिया भास्करतुल्यदेहः ।
निदर्शयन्राममवेक्ष्य खस्थः
सख्यं कुरुष्वेति तदाभ्युवाच ॥
Kabandha with his new body that shone like the sun, and with a halo of glory about him, looked at Rāma from his place in the sky and told him, ‘make friends with Sugreeva’, pointing in that direction.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे त्रिसप्ततितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventy third Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
