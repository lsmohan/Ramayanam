Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 49
In this Sarga, Aṅgada cheers up the Vānaras who became tired and felt dejected, asserting that there is no other formula for success than to get over the feeling of hopelessness and to deploy their skills. He reminds them of how annoyed and ruthless Sugreeva could be if they failed in their search. Gandhamādana agrees with Aṅgada. All the Vānaras search the mountain up and down again. After resting for a while, they start the search again in the southerly direction.
4.49.1
अथाङ्गदस्तदा सर्वान्
वानरानिदमब्रवीत् ।
परिश्रान्तो महाप्राज्ञः
समाश्वास्य शनैर्वचः ॥
Then the great sagacious Aṅgada, tired as he was, spoke gently, cheering up all those Vānaras:
4.49.2
वनानि गिरयो नद्यो
दुर्गाणि गहनानि च ।
दर्यो गिरिगुहाश्चैव
विचिता नः समन्ततः ।
तत्र तत्र सहास्माभिः
जानकी न च दृश्यते ।
तथा रक्षोपहर्ता च
सीतायाश्चैव दुष्कृती ॥
We have, together, searched everywhere, in the Vanas, in the hills, by the rivers, in the inaccessible and impenetrable thickets, in the hollows and in the mountain caves, but have not found Jānaki or the evil Rākshasa who had carried off Seetā.
4.49.4
कालश्च नो महान्यातः
सुग्रीवश्चोग्रशासनः ।
तस्माद्भवन्तस्सहिता
विचिन्वन्तु समन्ततः ॥
A great deal of time has passed. Sugreeva is very serious about what he has commanded us to do. So, all of you, joining together, should search everywhere.
4.49.5
विहाय तन्द्रीं शोकं च
निद्रां चैव समुत्थिताम् ।
विचिनुध्वं यथा सीताम्
पश्यामो जनकात्मजाम् ॥
Give up this weariness, sadness and drowsiness that is taking over you. Let us search and find Seetā, Janaka’s daughter.
4.49.6
अनिर्वेदं च दाक्ष्यं च
मनसश्चापराजयः ।
कार्यसिद्धिकराण्याहुः
तस्मादेतद्ब्रवीम्यहम् ॥
Not caving in to hopelessness, not letting the mind accept defeat and being skillful in execution are said to be the secrets of success and that is why I say this.
4.49.7
अद्यापीदं वनं दुर्गम्
विचिन्वन्तु वनौकसः ।
खेदं त्यक्त्वा पुनस्सर्वैः
वनमेतद्विचीयताम् ॥
All of you, Vana dwellers, give up your gloom and search again this tangled wilderness today.
4.49.8
अवश्यं कुर्वतां तस्य
दृश्यते कर्मणः फलम् ।
परं निर्वेदमागम्य
न हि नो मीलनं क्षमम् ॥
Those who work will certainly realize the fruits of their labor. On the other hand, if we let ourselves succumb to hopelessness, we will find it difficult to even close our eyes.
4.49.9
सुग्रीवः क्रोधनो राजा
तीक्ष्णदण्डश्च वानराः ।
भेतव्यं तस्य सततम्
रामस्य च महात्मनः ॥
Sugreeva can easily get irate and he is ruthless in punishing. We should always be wary of him and the Mahātma Rāma.
4.49.10
हितार्थमेतदुक्तं वः
क्रियतां यदि रोचते ।
उच्यतां हि क्षमं यत्तत्
सर्वेषामेव वानराः ॥
O Vānaras! I say this for your own good; do accordingly, if it appeals to you or tell me whatever else is right for us to do!
4.49.11
अङ्गदस्य वचश्श्रुत्वा
वचनं गन्धमादनः ।
उवाचाऽव्यक्तया वाचा
पिपासाश्रमखिन्नया ॥
Hearing those words of Aṅgada, Gandhamādana responded in a voice that became indistinct as he suffered from thirst and weariness:
4.49.12
सदृशं खलु वो वाक्यम्
अङ्गदो यदुवाच ह ।
हितं चैवानुकूलं च
क्रियतामस्य भाषितम् ॥
Whatever Aṅgada said should certainly be acceptable to you. His advice, practical and valuable, should be followed.
4.49.13
पुनर्मार्गामहे शैलान्
कन्दरांश्च शिलस्तथा ।
काननानि च शून्यानि
गिरिप्रस्रवणानि च ॥
Let us search again among the hills, the hollows, the boulders, the forests, the mountains, the streams and the desolate places.
4.49.14
यथोद्दिष्टानि सर्वाणि
सुग्रीवेण महात्मना ।
विचिन्वन्तु वनं सर्वे
गिरिदुर्गाणि सर्वशः ॥
All of you should join together and search the Vana and the thickets on the mountains as have been instructed by Mahātma Sugreeva.
4.49.15
ततस्समुत्थाय पुनः
वानरास्ते महाबलाः ।
विन्ध्यकाननसङ्कीर्णाम्
विचेरुर्दक्षिणां दिशम् ॥
Thereupon, those mighty strong Vānaras got up and searched again in the southerly direction, where lay the thick wilderness of the Vindhyas.
4.49.16
ते शारदाभ्रप्रतिमम्
श्रीमद्रजतपर्वतम् ।
शृङ्गवन्तं दरीवन्तम्
अधिरुह्य च वानराः ।
तत्र लोध्रवनं रम्यम्
सप्तपर्णवनानि च ।
विचिन्वंतो हरिवराः
सीतादर्शनकाङ्क्षिणः ॥
Ascending that gorgeous Silver Mountain which was majestic like a post-monsoon cloud with its many peaks and hollows, the Vānaras, eager to find Seetā, searched the beautiful Vanas of Lōdhra and Saptaparṇa on it.
4.49.18
तस्याग्रमधिरूढास्ते
श्रान्ता विपुलविक्रमाः ।
न पश्यन्ति स्म वैदेहीम्
रामस्य महिषीं प्रियाम् ॥
They, of as great prowess as they were, became tired by the time they climbed to the top of the mountain. They could not find Vaidēhee, the beloved queen of Rāma.
4.49.19
ते तु दृष्टिगतं दृष्ट्वा
तं शैलं बहुकन्दरम् ।
अवारोहन्त हरयो
वीक्षमाणास्समन्ततः ॥
After looking through every place on that mountain of many hollows that caught their sight, the Vānaras climbed down, looking everywhere.
4.49.20
अवरुह्य ततो भूमिम्
श्रान्ता विगतचेतसः ।
स्थित्वा मुहूर्तं तत्राथ
वृक्षमूलमुपाश्रिताः ॥
As they climbed down to the ground, they became tired and listless. After resting there for a Muhūrta, they assembled at the base of the trees.
4.49.21
ते मुहूर्तं समाश्वस्ताः
किञ्चिद्भग्नपरिश्रमाः ।
पुनरेवोद्यताः कृत्स्नाम्
मार्गितुं दक्षिणां दिशम् ॥
Having rested for a Muhūrta and shed some of their fatigue, they started again to search the entire South.
4.49.22
हनुमत्प्रमुखास्ते तु
प्रस्थिताः प्लवगर्षभाः ।
विन्ध्यमेवादितः कृत्वा
विचेरुस्ते समन्ततः ॥
Hanumān and the other great bulls among Vānaras went on with their search everywhere, with the Vindhyas as their starting point.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे एकोनपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty ninth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
