Baala Kaanda - Sarga 65
In this Sarga, the story of Viṡwāmitra concludes as he attains Brahmatva, which is attested to by all the Dēvas, Brahma and Vasishṭha.
1.65.1
अथ हैमवतीं राम
दिशं त्यक्त्वा महामुनिः ।
पूर्वां दिशमनुप्राप्य
तपस्तेपे सुदारुणम् ॥
O Rāma! Then the Mahā Muni left the Himalayas in the north and went in the easterly direction and performed intense Tapa.
1.65.2
मौनं वर्षसहस्रस्य
कृत्वा व्रतमनुत्तमम् ।
चकाराप्रतिमं राम
तपः परमदुष्करम् ॥
O Rāma! For one thousand years, he practiced absolute silence in an exceptional manner and did very severe and unparalleled Tapa.
1.65.3
पूर्णे वर्षसहस्रे तु
काष्ठभूतं महामुनिम् ।
विघ्नैर्बहुभिराधूतम्
क्रोधो नान्तरमाविशत् ।
स कृत्वा निश्चयं राम
तप आतिष्ठदव्ययम् ॥
By the end of a thousand years, that Mahā Muni became a mere skeleton. None of the impediments on the way could make him frustrated or angry. He performed the Tapa with unwavering determination.
1.65.4
तस्य वर्षसहस्रस्य
व्रते पूर्णे महाव्रतः ।
भोक्तुमारब्धवानन्नम्
तस्मिन् काले रघूत्तम ।
इन्द्रो द्विजातिर्भूत्वा तम्
सिद्धमन्नमयाचत ॥
O best among the Raghu dynasty! After that highly determined Muni thus adhered to that strict discipline for one thousand years and was about to take a morsel of food, Indra came in the form of a Brāhmaṇa and begged him for the food that he was about to eat.
1.65.6
तस्मै दत्त्वा तदा सिद्धम्
सर्वं विप्राय निश्चितः ।
निःशेषितेऽन्ने भगवान्
अभुक्त्वैव महातपाः ।
न किञ्चिदवदद्विप्रम्
मौनव्रतमुपस्थितः ।
अथ वर्षसहस्रं वै
नोच्छ्वसन् मुनिपुङ्गवः ॥
That great Tapasvi, Bhagawān, the one who was observing strict silence at that time, gave the food that he was about to eat to that Brāhmaṇa without saying a word and watched him eat all of it without leaving out even a single particle. Then that great Muni held his breath for another one thousand years.
1.65.8
तस्यानुच्छ्वसमानस्य
मूर्ध्नि धूमो व्यजायत ।
त्रैलोक्यं येन सम्भ्रान्तम्
आदीपितमिवाभवत् ॥
As he thus held his breath for long, smoke emerged from the high point of his head. The heat was felt by all the three worlds and they became terrified.
Thus far, you have seen many a time in Bāla Kāṇḍa, Indra trying to disrupt Yajñas and Tapa. He disrupts Yajñas because he does not want to be de-seated. He disrupts Tapa because it concentrates a humongous amount of energy that could harm the world, as indicated in these Ṡlōkas.
1.65.9
ततो देवास्सगन्धर्वाः
पन्नगासुरराक्षसाः ।
मोहितास्तेजसा तस्य
तपसा मन्दरश्मयः ।
कश्मलोपहताः सर्वे
पितामहमथाब्रुवन् ॥
Then the Dēvas, along with Gandharvas, Pannagas, Asuras and Rākshasas, with attention completely grabbed by his radiance, with powers utterly paled by his great Tapa, and with minds greatly agitated, went to the Progenitor of all and said:
1.65.10
बहुभिः कारणैर्देव
विश्वामित्रो महामुनिः ।
लोभितः क्रोधितश्चैव
तपसा चाभिवर्धते ॥
O Dēva! For various reasons, the great Muni is able to advance with his Tapa in spite of the many enticements and annoyances thrown his way.
1.65.11
न ह्यस्य वृजिनं किञ्चित्
दृश्यते सूक्ष्ममप्यथ ।
न दीयते यदि त्वस्य
मनसा यदभीप्सितम् ।
विनाशयति त्रैलोक्यम्
तपसा सचराचरम् ॥
There is not even an iota of weakness or fault left in him anymore. If there is any more delay in granting whatever he wishes, all the three worlds with their movable and immovable parts could be shattered by his Tapa.
1.65.13
व्याकुलाश्च दिशः सर्वा
न च किञ्चित्प्रकाशते ।
सागराः क्षुभिताः सर्वे
विशीर्यन्ते च पर्वताः ॥
There is disquiet in every direction. Nothing has retained its original glow. All the oceans are getting agitated. And all the mountains are getting shattered.
Just as we discovered in modern times that there is enormous power captured in each single atom, the Ṛshis in ancient times had discovered that there is enormous power deposited in every one of us and that can be accentuated and released through intense Tapa.
This Ṡlōka gives a sense of the magnitude of that power. On the Richter scale (used for expressing the energy released by the movements of tectonic plates that causes earthquakes and tsunamis), it would almost be 10, the highest possible.
1.65.14
प्रकम्पते च पृथिवी
वायुर्वाति भृशाकुलः ।
ब्रह्मन्न प्रतिजानीमो
नास्तिको जायते जनः ॥
The earth is shaking entirely. The wind is whirling without direction. People are losing faith in Providence. We simply do not know what to do.
1.65.15
सम्मूढमिव त्रैलोक्यम्
सम्प्रक्षुभितमानसम् ।
भास्करो निष्प्रभश्चैव
महर्षेस्तस्य तेजसा ॥
The world is getting petrified by total disorientation. Even the sun seems to be paled by his radiance.
1.65.16
बुद्धिं न कुरुते यावन्
नाशे देव महामुनिः ।
तावत्प्रसाद्यो भगवान्
अग्निरूपो महाद्युतिः ।
कालाग्निना यथा पूर्वम्
त्रैलोक्यं दह्यतेऽखिलम् ।
देवराज्यं चिकीर्षेत
दीयतामस्य यन्मतम् ॥
O Dēva! This Mahā Muni, Bhagawān who shines like fire with magnificent radiance must be placated before he makes up his mind to destroy everything, just as the Fire of Time had burnt out everything once up on a time. It is better to give him whatever he wants, even if it is the reign over the Heaven.
1.65.18
ततः सुरगणाः सर्वे
पितामहपुरोगमाः ।
विश्वामित्रं महात्मानम्
वाक्यं मधुरमब्रुवन् ॥
Then all the Dēvas led by the Progenitor (Brahma) said these sweet words to the Mahātma Viṡwāmitra:
1.65.19
ब्रह्मर्षे स्वागतं तेऽस्तु
तपसा स्म सुतोषिताः ।
ब्राह्मण्यं तपसोग्रेण
प्राप्तवानसि कौशिक ॥
We are very pleased with your Tapa. O son of Kuṡika! You have attained Brahmatva with your intense Tapa! O Brahmarshi! Welcome to you!
1.65.20
दीर्घमायुश्च ते ब्रह्मन्
ददामि समरुद्गणः ।
स्वस्ति प्राप्नुहि भद्रं ते
गच्छ सौम्य यथासुखम् ॥
I, along with all the Maruts (Dēvas), also grant you a very long life. May all bode well for you. O dear! Please go and live happily forever!
1.65.21
पितामहवचः श्रुत्वा
सर्वेषां च दिवौकसाम् ।
कृत्वा प्रणामं मुदितो
व्याजहार महामुनिः ॥
Hearing those words of the Progenitor and all the Dēvas, the Mahā Muni was pleased and paid respects to them all and said:
1.65.22
ब्राह्मण्यं यदि मे प्राप्तम्
दीर्घमायुस्तथैव च ।
ओङ्कारश्च वषट्कारो
वेदाश्च वरयन्तु माम् ॥
If I have acquired Brahmatva and was granted long life, may I also acquire Aum, Vashaṭ and Vēdas!
Aum, Vashaṭ and Vēdas are the prerogative of Brāhmaṇas, just as Valor, law and order, etc., are prerogatives of a Kshatriya.
1.65.23
क्षत्रवेदविदां श्रेष्ठो
ब्रह्मवेदविदामपि ।
ब्रह्मपुत्रो वसिष्ठो माम्
एवं वदतु देवताः ।
यद्ययं परमः कामः
कृतो यान्तु सुरर्षभाः ॥
O bulls among Dēvas! May Vasishṭha, the son of Brahma, the best among those who know all of Kshatriya and Brāhmaṇa Vidyas, attest that I have attained Brahmatva! May this wish of mine be fulfilled before you leave!
1.65.24
ततः प्रसादितो देवैः
वसिष्ठो जपतां वरः ।
सख्यं चकार ब्रह्मर्षिः
एवमस्त्विति चाब्रवीत् ॥
Then Vasishṭha, the best among the performers of Japa upon the request of the Dēvas, forged friendship with him and also attested that he had attained Brahmatva!
1.65.25
ब्रह्मर्षिस्त्वं न सन्देहः
सर्वं सम्पत्स्यते तव ।
इत्युक्त्वा देवताश्चापि
सर्वा जग्मुर्यथाऽऽगतम् ॥
The Dēvas also said: "You have indeed become a Brahmarshi, without doubt, and you will gain everything" and departed.
1.65.26
विश्वामित्रोऽपि धर्मात्मा
लब्ध्वा ब्राह्मण्यम् उत्तमम् ।
पूजयामास ब्रह्मर्षिम्
वसिष्ठम् जपताम् वरम् ॥
Viṡwāmitra, the Dharmātma, having gained the distinguished Brahmatva paid respects to the Brahmarshi Vasishṭha, prominent among those who perform Japa.
1.65.27
कृत कामो महीम् सर्वाम्
चचार तपसि स्थितः ।
एवं त्वनेन ब्राह्मण्यम्
प्राप्तं राम महात्मना ॥
O Rāma! Thus, that Mahātma gained Brahmatva. After his wish was thus fulfilled, he traveled across the entire earth continuing his Tapa, as before.
Ṡatānanda, the son of Ahalya has been telling the story of Viṡwāmitra to Rāma, thus far. He concludes the story in these Ṡlōkas.
1.65.28
एष राम मुनिश्रेष्ठ
एष विग्रहवांस्तपः ।
एष धर्मपरो नित्यम्
वीर्यस्यैष परायणम् ॥
O Rāma! This is that prominent Muni. He is the embodiment of Tapa. His dedication to Dharma is perpetual. His fortitude is ultimate.
1.65.29
एवमुक्त्वा महातेजा
विरराम द्विजोत्तमः ।
शतानन्दवचः श्रुत्वा
रामलक्ष्मणसन्निधौ ।
जनकः प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यम्
उवाच कुशिकात्मजम् ॥
After that supremely radiant and prominent Brāhmaṇa Ṡatānanda thus concluded narrating the story to Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, Janaka said to the son of Kuṡika with folded hands:
1.65.31
धन्योऽस्म्यनुगृहीतोऽस्मि
यस्य मे मुनिपुङ्गव ।
यज्ञं काकुत्स्थसहितः
प्राप्तवानसि धार्मिक ॥
O Dhārmika! O foremost among Munis! I feel blessed and fortunate to have you here to witness the Yajña along with the princes of Kākutstha lineage.
1.65.32
पालितोऽहं त्वया ब्रह्मन्
दर्शनेन महामुने ।
गुणा बहुविधाः प्राप्ताः
तव सन्दर्शनान्मया ॥
O Mahā Muni! O Brahman! My soul is purified by your presence. My being is enriched in a multitude of ways by your graceful presence.
1.65.33
विस्तरेण च ते ब्रह्मन्
कीर्त्यमानं महत्तपः ।
श्रुतं मया महातेजो
रामेण च महात्मना ॥
O Brahman! The story of your laudable great Tapa is now heard by me and Mahātma Rāma.
1.65.34
सदस्यैः प्राप्य च सदः
श्रुतास्ते बहवो गुणाः ।
अप्रमेयं तपस्तुभ्यं
अप्रमेयं च ते बलम् ।
अप्रमेया गुणाश्चैव
नित्यं ते कुशिकात्मज ॥
O son of Kuṡika! Everyone that had come here to the Yajña have now heard of your many great qualities. Your Tapa is beyond comprehension. Your strength and perseverance is beyond anyone's grasp. Your capabilities at any time are beyond measure.
1.65.35
तृप्तिराश्चर्यभूतानाम्
कथानां नास्ति मे विभो ।
कर्मकालो मुनिश्रेष्ठ
लम्बते रविमण्डलम् ॥
I certainly cannot have enough of hearing your amazing stories. But the sun is now setting. And it is time for ablutions.
1.65.36
श्वःप्रभाते महातेजो
द्रष्टुमर्हसि मां पुनः ।
स्वागतं तपतां श्रेष्ठ
मामनुज्ञातुमर्हसि ॥
O supremely radiant! So I shall visit you again after day-break tomorrow. Welcome again to you, O pre-eminent Tapasvi! May I be permitted to take leave of you for now!
1.65.37
एवमुक्तो मुनिवरः
प्रशस्य पुरुषर्षभम् ।
विससर्जाशु जनकम्
प्रीतं प्रीतमनास्तदा ॥
The pleased, foremost of Munis lauded the pleased Janaka, the best among men and saw him off.
1.65.38
एवमुक्त्वा मुनिश्रेष्ठम्
वैदेहो मिथिलाधिपः ।
प्रदक्षिणं चकाराशु
सोपाध्यायः सबान्धवः ॥
The ruler of the kingdom of Vidēha and the city of Mithilā, along with Purōhitas and all the people related to him circumambulated the foremost among the Munis.
1.65.39
विश्वामित्रोऽपि धर्मात्मा
सरामः सहलक्ष्मणः ।
स्ववाटमभिचक्राम
पूज्यमानो महर्षिभिः ॥
Viṡwāmitra, the Dharmātma, along with Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, respected by all the Maharishis, then went to rest at his accommodation.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे पञ्चषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty fifth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
