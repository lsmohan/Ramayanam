Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 4
In this Sarga, Seetā cries seeing Virādha carry off Rāma and Lakshmaṇa on his shoulders. Then they make all haste to kill him. They cut off his arms. He falls down. And they hack him with their swords and pound him against the ground. Since Virādha cannot be killed by weapons, Rāma tells Lakshmaṇa to dig a pit to bury him.
Virādha at that moment remembers that he was originally a Gandharva and had got the form of Rākshasa because of a curse and that he would be relieved from that curse by Rāma. He asks Rāma to bury him. Lakshmaṇa digs a pit and buries him.
3.4.1
ह्रियमाणौ तु काकुत्स्थौ
दृष्ट्वा सीता रघूत्तमौ ।
उच्चैस्स्वरेण चुक्रोश
प्रगृह्य सुमहाभुजौ ॥
When she saw those descendants of Kakutstha of mighty arm, the best of the scions of the Raghus, being thus carried off, Seetā screamed at the top of her voice.
3.4.2
एष दाशरथी रामः
सत्यवान् शीलवान् शुचिः ।
रक्षसा रौद्ररूपेण
ह्रियते सहलक्ष्मणः ॥
This son of Daṡaratha, Rāma, who is truthful, virtuous and pure, is being carried off along with Lakshmaṇa by this dreadful Rākshasa!
3.4.3
मामृक्षा भक्षयिष्यन्ति
शार्दूलद्वीपिनस्तथा ।
मां हरोत्सृज काकुत्स्थौ
नमस्ते राक्षसोत्तम ॥
Bears will devour me, or tigers and cheetahs. O best of Rākshasas, I bow to you, take me and leave the Kākutsthās!
3.4.4
तस्यास्तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
वैदेह्या रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
वेगं प्रचक्रतुर्वीरौ
वधे तस्य दुरात्मनः ॥
When they heard those words of Vaidēhee, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa made all haste to kill the wicked Virādha.
3.4.5
तस्य रौद्रस्य सौमित्रिः
सव्यं बाहुं बभञ्ज ह ।
रामस्तु दक्षिणं बाहुं
तरसा तस्य रक्षसः ॥
Sowmitri broke the left arm of that dreadful one and Rāma the right arm, with all force.
3.4.6
स भग्नबाहुस्सविग्नो
पपाताशु विमूर्छितः ।
धरण्यां मेघसङ्काशो
वज्रभिन्न इवाचलः ॥
With his arms broken and perturbed, he who looked like a cloud, fainted and tumbled down instantly like a mountain cleft broken by a thunderbolt.
3.4.7
मुष्टिभिर्जानुभिः पद्भिः
सूदयन्तौ तु राक्षसम् ।
उद्यम्योद्यम्य चाप्येनं
स्थण्डिले निष्पिपेषतुः ॥
Pummeling the Rākshasa with fist and knee and foot, they lifted him up and threw him down on the ground again and again.
3.4.8
स विद्धो बहुभिर्बाणैः
खङ्गाभ्यां च परिक्षतः ।
निष्पिष्टो बहुधा भूमौ
न ममार स राक्षसः ॥
But though hit by many an arrow and hacked and hewed by their swords, and pounded against the ground, the Rākshasa would not die.
3.4.9
तं प्रेक्ष्य रामः सुभृशम्
अवध्यमचलोपमम् ।
भयेष्वभयदश्श्रीमान्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Realizing that the mountain like (Virādha) cannot be killed thus, Rāma, the gifted one, the one who gives comfort to the fear-stricken, said these words (to Lakshmaṇa):
3.4.10
तपसा पुरुषव्याघ्र
राक्षसोऽयं न शक्यते ।
शस्त्रेण युधि निर्जेतुं
राक्षसं निखनावहे ॥
O tiger among men! Because of his Tapa, this Rākshasa cannot be subdued by weapons. Let us bury this Rākshasa.
3.4.11
कुञ्जरस्येव रौद्रस्य
राक्षसस्यास्य लक्ष्मण ।
वनेऽस्मिन् सुमहच्छ्वभ्रं
खन्यतां रौद्रवर्चसः ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! May a huge pit be dug in this Vana, for this dreadful, elephant-like Rākshasa of awful bearing!
3.4.12
इत्युक्त्वा लक्ष्मणं रामः
प्रदरः खन्यतामिति ।
तस्थौ विराधमाक्रम्य
कण्ठे पादेन वीर्यवान् ॥
The valiant Rāma, thus saying ‘may a pit be dug!’ to Lakshmaṇa, stood with his leg firmly planted on Virādha’s neck.
3.4.13
तच्छ्रुत्वा राघवेणोक्तं
राक्षसः प्रश्रितं वचः ।
इदं प्रोवाच काकुत्थ्सं
विराधः पुरुषर्षभम् ॥
On hearing what Rāghava said, Virādha, the Rākshasa said these humble words to Kākutstha, the bull among men:
3.4.14
हतोऽस्मि पुरुषव्याघ्र
शक्रतुल्यबलेन वै ।
मया तु पूर्वं त्वं मोहान्
न ज्ञातः पुरुषर्षभ ॥
I lie dead, because of your strength, that is comparable to that of Ṡakra! I did not realize right in the beginning that it is you, because of my ignorance, O bull among men!
3.4.15
कौसल्यासुप्रजा राम
तातस्त्वं विदितो मया ।
वैदेही च महाभागा
लक्ष्मणश्च महायशाः ॥
Now, my dear, I realize that you are Rāma, Kousalyā’s worthy son, and that these are the blessed Vaidēhee and Lakshmaṇa of great renown.
3.4.16
अभिशापादहं घोरां
प्रविष्टो राक्षसीं तनुम् ।
तुम्बुरुर्नाम गन्धर्वः
शप्तो वैश्रवणेन ह ॥
I am a Gandharva by name Tumburu. I was cursed by Vaiṡravaṇa. Because of that curse, I got this horrid body of a Rākshasa!
3.4.17
प्रसाद्यमानश्च मया
सोऽब्रवीन्मां महायशाः ।
यदा दाशरथी रामः
त्वां वधिष्यति संयुगे ।
तदा प्रकृतिमापन्नो
भवान्स्वर्गं गमिष्यति ॥
He, of great renown, upon my beseeching, said: ‘When Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, slays you in battle, you shall recover your original form and come back to Heaven’.
3.4.18
अनुपस्थीयमानो मां
स क्रुद्धो व्याजहार ह ।
इति वैश्रवणो राजा
रम्भासक्तमुवाच ह ॥
Vaiṡravaṇa, the lord, spoke thus when he became angry with me for indulging myself with Rambha and failed to serve him.
3.4.19
तव प्रसादान्मुक्तोऽहम्
इहशापात्सुदारुणात् ।
भुवनं स्वं गमिष्यामि
स्वस्ति वोऽस्तु परन्तप ॥
By your grace I stand freed from this terrible curse. I shall go back to my world and may all bode well for you, O scorcher of foes!
3.4.20
इतो वसति धर्मात्मा
शरभङ्गः प्रतापवान् ।
अध्यर्धयोजने तात
महर्षिस्सूर्यसन्निभः ॥
My dear! Maharshi Ṡarabhaṅga, a Dharmātma, a man of prowess, who is like Sun (in his radiance), lives one and half Yōjanas from here.
3.4.21
तं क्षिप्रमधिगच्छ त्वं
स ते श्रेयोऽभिधास्यति ।
अवटे चापि मां राम
प्रक्षिप्य कुशली व्रज ॥
Go to him immediately and he will speak in your best interest. Bury me in a pit and go, and may all bode well for you!
3.4.22
रक्षसां गतसत्त्वानां
एष धर्मस्सनातनः ।
अवटे ये निधीयन्ते
तेषां लोकाः सनातनाः ॥
It is a time honored custom for Rākshasas who lose their lives. Those (Rākshasas) who are buried in a pit attain eternal worlds.
3.4.23
ततः खनित्रमादाय
लक्ष्मणश्श्वभ्रमुत्तमम् ।
अखनत्पार्श्वतस्तस्य
विराधस्य महात्मनः ॥
Then Lakshmaṇa took a crowbar and dug a huge pit next to that Mahātma Virādha.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे चतुर्थस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fourth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
