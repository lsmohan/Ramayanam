Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 68
In this short Sarga, Vasishṭha makes it loud and clear that the kingdom was given to Bharata and hence the next order of business was nothing other than bringing him to Ayōdhyā.
He asks the messengers to start immediately and instructs them not to say anything about Rāma's exile or Daṡaratha's death. He tells them to convey merely that he is urgently needed in Ayōdhyā.
Then the messengers proceed on their long journey and finally reach the city of Girivraja.
2.68.1
तेषां हि वचनं श्रुत्वा
वसिष्ठः प्रत्युवाच ह ।
मित्रामात्यगणान्सर्वान्
ब्राह्मणांस्तानिदं वचः ॥
On hearing those words, Vasishṭha responded to the King's many confidants, to the retinue of ministers and to all of the Brāhmaṇas, saying:
2.68.2
यदसौ मातुलकुले
दत्तराज्यं परं सुखी ।
भरतो वसति भ्रात्रा
शत्रुघ्नेन समन्वितः ।
तच्छीघ्रं जवना दूता
गच्छन्तु त्वरितैर्हयैः ।
आनेतुं भ्रातरौ वीरौ
किं समीक्षामहे वयम् ॥
There is not much to deliberate here. The kingdom has already been given to Bharata. He is now at his maternal uncle's house along with Ṡatrughna. May messengers go immediately on swift horses and bring the two valiant brothers here!
In the previous Sarga the ministers and Brāhmaṇas asked to install someone from the Ikshwāku dynasty as the king, without explicitly mentioning a name.
They did not specify whether it should be Bharata (as per the demand of Kaikēyee, which the king was bound to) or Rāma, whom everyone loved to have as the king.
In this Ṡlōka Vasishṭha sticks to Dharma and essentially puts an end to their hopes of having Rāma installed as the king.
2.68.4
गच्छन्त्विति ततस्सर्वे
वसिष्ठं वाक्यमब्रुवन् ।
तेषां तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
वसिष्ठो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Everyone responded to him saying, "May they (the messengers) leave (as instructed)!" Hearing that response, Vasishṭha added immediately:
2.68.5
एहि सिद्धार्थ विजय
जयन्ताशोक नन्दन ।
श्रूयतामिति कर्तव्यम्
सर्वानेव ब्रवीमि वः ॥
O Siddhārtha, Jayanta, Vijaya, Asoka and Nandana! Come here and listen carefully as to what must be done now.
2.68.6
पुरं राजगृहं गत्वा
शीघ्रं शीघ्रजवै र्हयैः ।
त्यक्तशोकैरिदं वाच्यः
शासनाद्भरतो मम ॥
Leave your grief here. Tell Bharata the exact words that I instruct you, after reaching the city of Rājagṛha on swift horses:
2.68.7
पुरोहितस्त्वां कुशलम्
प्राह सर्वे च मन्त्रिणः ।
त्वरमाणश्च निर्याहि
कृत्यमात्ययिकं त्वया ॥
Purōhita and all the ministers enquire about your wellbeing. May you start immediately! We need you for something that is extremely important!
2.68.8
मा चास्मै प्रोषितं रामम्
मा चास्मै पितरं मृतम् ।
भवन्तश्शंसिषुर्गत्वा
राघवाणामिमं क्षयम् ॥
After arriving there, you should tell him neither about Rāma's exile, nor about the death of his father, nor the calamity that had befallen the clan of Raghu.
2.68.9
कौशेयानि च वस्त्राणि
भूषणानि वराणि च ।
क्षिप्रमादाय राज्ञश्च
भरतस्य च गच्छत ॥
Please quickly take exquisite ornaments and silk garments for Bharata and the king (as gifts), and leave!
2.68.10
दत्तपथ्यशना दूता
जग्मुस्स्वं स्वं निवेशनम् ।
केकयांस्ते गमिष्यन्तो
हयानारुह्य संमतान् ॥
Those messengers who were bound for Kēkaya kingdom were then given allowances and rations for travel. Mounting the best of horses, they then left to their respective residences.
2.68.11
ततः प्रास्थानिकं कृत्वा
कार्यशेषमनन्तरम् ।
वसिष्ठेनाभ्यनुज्ञाता
दूतास्संत्वरिता ययुः ॥
Then, having completed the rest of the arrangements for the journey and taking leave of Vasishṭha, those messengers started in haste.
2.68.12
न्यन्तेनापरतालस्य
प्रलम्बस्योत्तरं प्रति ।
निषेवमाणा स्ते जग्मुः
नदीं मध्येन मालिनीम् ॥
They went around the farthest reach of the Aparatāla mountain, and along the river Mālini to the northern side of the Pralamba mountain.
2.68.13
ते हस्तिनापुरे गङ्गाम्
तीर्त्वा प्रत्यङ्मुखा ययुः ।
पाञ्चालदेशमासाद्य
मध्येन कुरुजाङ्गलम् ।
सरांसि च सुपूर्णानि
नदीश्च विमलोदकाः ।
निरीक्षमाणास्ते जग्मुः
दूताः कार्यवशाद्द्रुतम् ॥
Then they crossed river Gaṅgā at Hastināpura, and going west through the heart of the Kurujāṅgala country, they reached the area of Pāñcāla and went on in all haste because of the urgency of their mission, seeing many lakes full of water and streams of clear waters, on the way.
2.68.15
ते प्रसन्नोदकां दिव्याम्
नानाविहगसेविताम् ।
उपातिजग्मुर्वेगेन
शरदण्डां जनाकुलाम् ॥
They quickly crossed the magnificent river Ṡaradaṇḍa, of pleasing waters, that was frequented by birds of many kinds and was thronged with people.
2.68.16
निकूलवृक्षमासाद्य
दिव्यं सत्योपयाचनम् ।
अभिगम्याभिवाद्यं तम्
कुलिङ्गां प्राविशन्पुरीम् ॥
Reaching a sacred tree on the bank of the river that was worthy of reverence, and the one that had earned the distinction of 'The Granter of wishes', and circumambulating it, they proceeded to enter the city of Kuliṅga.
2.68.17
आभिकालं ततः प्राप्य
ते बोधिभवनाच्च्युताम् ।
पितृपैतामहीं पुण्याम्
तेरुरिक्षुमतीं नदीम् ॥
Then, reaching the village of Abhikāla, they crossed the sacred river Ikshumati that flowed from the mountain Bōdibhavana and had been under their rule for generations.
2.68.18
अवेक्ष्याञ्जलिपानांश्च
ब्राह्मणान्वेदपारगान् ।
ययुर्मध्येन बाह्लीकान्
सुदामानं च पर्वतम् ॥
They saw the Brāhmaṇas who were masters of Vēdas, who lived on nothing but water that they could take in their palms joined. Then they passed through the middle of the Bāhlika region and reached the mountain Sudāma.
2.68.19
विष्णोः पदं प्रेक्षमाणा
विपाशांचापि शाल्मलीम् ।
नदीर्वापी स्तटाकानि
पल्वलानि सरांसि च ।
पश्यन्तो विविधांश्चापि
सिंहाव्याघ्रमृग द्विपान् ।
ययुः पथाऽतिमहता
शासनं भर्तुरीप्सवः ॥
They saw the sacred spot with the imprint of Vishnu's foot, the river Vipāṡa and the tree Ṡālmali, as well as many streams, pools, tanks, ponds and lakes, and many a lion, tiger, deer and elephant, as they went on that extraordinarily long journey, eager to fulfill their master's injunctions.
The mention of 'long journey' here is worthy of note.
Among the many that were not invited to the failed consecration of Rāma were the father-in-law of Rāma (Janaka) himself and the father-in-law of Daṡaratha (King of Kēkaya).
The reason is that both of them were too far away from Ayōdhyā to reach in time for the consecration, which was organized in a hurry, when Daṡaratha started seeing bad omens, which he interpreted as omens of death for himself, unsuspecting of the calamity that would befall.
2.68.21
ते श्रान्तवाहना दूता
विकृष्णेन पथा ततः ।
गिरिव्रजं पुरवरम्
शीघ्रमासेदुरञ्जसा ॥
Just as their mounts were getting tired by the long journey, those messengers promptly and safely reached the prominent city of Girivraja.
2.68.22
भर्तुः प्रियार्थं कुलरक्षणार्थं
भर्तुश्च वंशस्य परिग्रहार्थम् ।
अहेडमाना स्त्वरया स्म दूता
रात्र्यान्तु ते तत्पुरमेव याताः ॥
For the pleasure of their master, to safeguard the welfare of the dynasty and to save the dignity of the lineage of their master, the messengers reached that city by night, without relaxing and with the best speed they could muster.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे अष्टषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty eighth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
