Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 36
In this Sarga, Lakshmaṇa accepts the explanation of Tārā and softens. Sugreeva, feeling comfortable, responds to Lakshmaṇa. He explains how much he is indebted to Rāma for the favor done by him. He praises Rāma’s strength and says that Rāma could take the life of Rāvaṇa by himself, and his own role is merely to assist him. Then he asks for forgiveness for anything he has done overstepping his boundary.
The pleased Lakshmaṇa praises Sugreeva for his gratitude and adherence to Dharma. He says Rāma would feel well supported with Sugreeva helping him. He urges Sugreeva to start immediately along with him to go to Rāma and console him. He asks for the forgiveness of Sugreeva for the harsh words he spoke earlier.
4.36.1
इत्युक्तस्तारया वाक्यम्
प्रश्रितं धर्मसंहितम् ।
मृदुस्वभावस्सौमित्रिः
प्रतिजग्राह तद्वचः ॥
Sowmitri’s softened heart accepted the respectful explanation of Tārā that was very much in line with Dharma.
4.36.2
तस्मिन्प्रतिगृहीते तु
वाक्ये हरिगणेश्वरः ।
लक्ष्मणात्सुमहत्त्रासम्
वस्त्रं क्लिन्नमिवात्यजत् ॥
With that explanation accepted, the lord of hordes of Vānaras cast aside the fear caused by Lakshmaṇa’s rage, as one would clothes that became wet.
4.36.3
ततः कण्ठगतं माल्यम्
चित्रं बहुगुणं महत् ।
चिच्छेद विमदश्चासीत्
सुग्रीवो वानरेश्वरः ॥
Then Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras, pulled to pieces the lovely, exquisite, garland of various hues, casting off the insobriety.
4.36.4
स लक्ष्मणं भीमबलम्
सर्ववानरसत्तमः ।
अब्रवीत्प्रश्रितं वाक्यम्
सुग्रीवस्सम्प्रहर्षयन् ॥
And Sugreeva, the best among all Vānaras, said these words with all deference to Lakshmaṇa of terrific strength, making him happy:
4.36.5
प्रणष्टा श्रीश्च कीर्तिश्च
कपिराज्यं च शाश्वतम् ।
रामप्रसादात्सौमित्रे
पुनश्चाप्तमिदं मया ॥
O Sowmitri! I regained this wealth, stature and the eternal kingdom of Vānaras because of Rāma’s grace!
4.36.6
कश्शक्तस्तस्य देवस्य
ख्यातस्य स्वेन कर्मणा ।
तादृशम् प्रतिकुर्वीत
अंशेवापि नृपात्मज ॥
O prince! Who will be able to repay even a portion of the favor he, the lord, widely reputed for his extraordinary deeds, did?
4.36.7
सीतां प्राप्स्यति धर्मात्मा
वधिष्यति च रावणम् ।
सहायमात्रेण मया
राघवस्स्वेन तेजसा ॥
Rāghava, the Dharmātma, employing his own power will kill Rāvaṇa and regain Seetā! I will be nothing but of mere assistance.
4.36.8
सहायकृत्यं किं तस्य
येन सप्त महाद्रुमाः ।
शैलश्च वसुधा चैव
बाणेनैकेन दारिताः ॥
What kind of assistance can be given to one who tore apart seven Sala trees, a mountain and the earth itself with a single arrow?
4.36.9
धनुर्विष्फारयाणस्य
यस्य शब्देन लक्ष्मण ।
सशैला कम्पिता भूमिः
सहायैः किं नु तस्य वै ॥
Why would he need any assistance, O Lakshmaṇa, the twanging of whose bow shook the earth along with its mountains ?
4.36.10
अनुयात्रां नरेन्द्रस्य
करिष्येऽहं नरर्षभ ।
गच्छतो रावणं हन्तुम्
वैरिणं सपुरस्सरम् ॥
O bull among men! All I can do is to just follow, as the lord of men goes ahead with his retainers to kill the enemy, Rāvaṇa.
4.36.11
यदि किञ्चिदतिक्रान्तम्
विश्वासात्प्रणयेन वा ।
प्रेष्यस्य क्षमितव्यं मे
न कश्चिन्नापराध्यति ॥
If I, your humble servant, have overstepped my boundary because of friendship or because of confidence, may I be forgiven; to err is human!
4.36.12
इति तस्य ब्रुवाणस्य
सुग्रीवस्य महात्मनः ।
अभवल्लक्ष्मणः प्रीतः
प्रेम्णा चेदमुवाच ह ॥
With Mahātma Sugreeva saying this, Lakshmaṇa became very pleased and he said this with affection:
4.36.13
सर्वथा हि मम भ्राता
सनाथो वानरेश्वर ।
त्वया नाथेन सुग्रीव
प्रश्रितेन विशेषतः ॥
O Sugreeva! O Lord of Vānaras! With you being there to protect him, especially in such a humble manner, my brother has all the protection he could wish for.
4.36.14
यस्ते प्रभावस्सुग्रीव
यच्च ते शौचमार्जवम् ।
अर्हस्त्वं कपिराज्यस्य
श्रियं भोक्तुमनुत्तमाम् ॥
O Sugreeva! With your prowess, purity and straightforwardness, you surely deserve to enjoy the unequaled luxury of the Vānara kingdom!
4.36.15
सहायेन तु सुग्रीव
त्वया रामः प्रतापवान् ।
वधिष्यति रणे शत्रून्
अचिरान्नात्र संशयः ॥
Rāma, the valiant one, O Sugreeva, will kill the enemy in a fight with your help, not before it is too long, without a doubt.
4.36.16
धर्मज्ञस्य कृतज्ञस्य
सङ्ग्रामेष्वनिवर्तिनः ।
उपपन्नं च युक्तं च
सुग्रीव तव भाषितम् ॥
O Sugreeva! The words you spoke are meaningful and befit a grateful and Dharma-knowing person like you, who never turned your back in a fight!
4.36.17
दोषज्ञस्सति सामर्थ्ये
कोऽन्यो भाषितुमर्हति ।
वर्जयित्वा मम ज्येष्ठम्
त्वां च वानरसत्तम ॥
No other person, except you and my brother, being as capable as you are, would ever confess to their own faults.
4.36.18
सदृशश्चासि रामस्य
विक्रमेण बलेन च ।
सहायो दैवतैर्दत्तः
चिराय हरिपुङ्गव ॥
You, O bull among Vānaras, an equal of Rāma in prowess and strength, are a god-sent help, after a long time.
4.36.19
किं तु शीघ्रमितो वीर
निष्क्रम त्वं मया सह ।
सान्त्वयस्व वयस्यं च
भार्याहरणकर्शितम् ॥
You should, O Veera, start from here immediately, to console your friend who is burnt out by the grief of the abduction of his wife.
4.36.20
यच्च शोकाभिभूतस्य
श्रुत्वा रामस्य भाषितम् ।
मया त्वं परुषाण्युक्तः
तत् क्षमस्व सखे मम ॥
Please forgive, my friend, my language that you found harsh, which resulted from my hearing the words Rāma uttered under the stress of grief.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे षट्त्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty sixth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
