Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 28
In this Sarga Rāma describes the rainy season to Lakshmaṇa, as many of those images he sees reminds him of his own fate and the fate of Seetā, along with the good fortunes of Sugreeva, and the sober state of Bharata.
He reflects that he has no choice but to patiently wait till he receives the grace of rivers and also Sugreeva.
4.28.1
स तदा वालिनं हत्वा
सुग्रीवमभिषिच्य च ।
वसन्माल्यवतः पृष्ठे
रामो लक्ष्मणमब्रवीत् ॥
After slaying Vāli and crowning Sugreeva, Rāma said these words to Lakshmaṇa, while he lived on the terraces of Mālyavata:
4.28.2
अयं स कालस्सम्प्राप्तः
समयोऽद्य जलागमः ।
सम्पश्य त्वं नभो मेघैः
संवृतं गिरिसन्निभैः ॥
The season for the downpour has arrived; look at the sky covered with mountainous clouds.
4.28.3
नवमासधृतं गर्भम्
भास्करस्य गभस्तिभिः ।
पीत्वा रसं समुद्राणाम्
द्यौः प्रसूते रसायनम् ॥
Having been pregnant for nine months drinking the waters of the oceans with the rays of the sun, the sky delivers life-giving water now.
The water cycle, from oceans to clouds to mountains to rivers and back to oceans, seems to be well known even in the days of Rāma!
And what a wonderful and poetic image it is wrapped in here!
4.28.4
शक्यमम्बरमारुह्य
मेघसोपानपङ्क्तिभिः ।
कुटजार्जुनमालाभिः
अलङ्कर्तुं दिवाकरम् ॥
It looks as if one could climb up to the sky using the rows of clouds as steps and decorate the sun with garlands of Kuṭaja and Arjuna flowers.
4.28.5
सन्ध्यारागोत्थितैस्ताम्रैः
अन्तेष्वधिक पाण्डरैः ।
स्निग्धैरभ्रपटच्छेदैः
बद्धव्रणमिवाम्बरम् ॥
Covered, as the sun sets, with the smooth reddish clouds with shining white edges the sky looks as if its (bleeding) wounds are covered with pieces of cloth.
4.28.6
मन्दमारुत निश्वासम्
सन्ध्याचन्दनरञ्जितम् ।
आपाण्डुजलदं भाति
कामातुरमिवाम्बरम् ॥
The sky covered in white clouds looks as if it is caught in the pangs of passion with the gentle breeze for its sighs and evening colors for sandal paste.
4.28.7
एषा घर्मपरिक्लिष्टा
नववारिपरिप्लुता ।
सीतेव शोकसन्तप्ता
मही बाष्पं विमुञ्चति ॥
Just as Seetā sheds tears scorched by grief, the earth seem to be flooded with fresh waters after being parched by the summer heat (for long).
4.28.8
मेघोदरविनिर्मुक्ताः
कर्पूर दल शीतलाः ।
शक्यमञ्जलिभिः पातुम्
वाताः केतकिगन्धिनः ॥
It seems we can fill our cupped-palms with the breeze pouring out of the wombs of clouds that is cool like the leaves of minty-camphor and perfumed with the fragrance of Kētaka plants, and drink it.
4.28.9
एष फुल्लार्जुनश्शैलः
केतकैरधिवासितः ।
सुग्रीव इव शान्तारिः
धाराभिरभिषिच्यते ॥
This mountain, with the Arjuna trees in flower and the over-powering scent of the Kētaka, is bathed by the torrential showers like Sugreeva was (bathed in showers of consecration) after his enemy had been quelled.
4.28.10
मेघकृष्णाजिनधराः
धारायज्ञोपवीतिनः ।
मारुतापूरितगुहाः
प्राधीता इव पर्वताः ॥
The hills seem to be ready to study Vedas, adorned with clouds for the skin of black antelope and the torrents of showers for the sacred thread, filling their lungs of caves with rumbling breeze.
4.28.11
कशाभिरिव हैमीभिः
विद्युद्भिरिवताडितम् ।
अन्तस्तनितनिर्घोषं
सवेदनमिवाम्बरम् ॥
The sky seems to be in agony struck with the golden whips of lightning crying out loud with the rumblings of the thunder.
4.28.12
नीलमेघाश्रिता विद्युत्
स्फुरन्ती प्रतिभाति मे ।
स्फुरन्ती रावणस्याङ्के
वैदेहीव तपस्विनी ॥
The lightning flashing against the black clouds reminds me of the suffering Vaidēhee writhing in Rāvaṇa’s lap.
4.28.13
इमास्ता मन्मथवताम्
हिताः प्रतिहता दिशः ।
अनुलिप्ता इव घनैः
नष्टग्रहनिशाकराः ॥
The four quarters, darlings of the impassioned, are indistinguishable from each other, covered completely by the clouds, and with the whereabouts of planets and the moon lost.
4.28.14
क्वचिद्बाष्पाभिसंरुद्धान्
वर्षागमसमुत्सुकान् ।
कुटजान्पश्य सौमित्रे
पुष्पितान्गिरिसानुषु ।
मम शोकाभिभूतस्य
कामसन्दीपनान् स्थितान् ॥
O Sowmitri! Look at the Kuṭaja trees in flower on the mountain side covered in mist rejoicing the onset of rains! They make me long for love, even as I am tormented by grief!
4.28.15
रजः प्रशान्तं सहिमोऽद्य वायुः
निदाघदोषप्रसराः प्रशान्ताः ।
स्थिता हि यात्रा वसुधाधिपानां
प्रवासिनो यान्ति नरास्स्वदेशान् ॥
The dust settled, the wind is misty, the discomforts of summer have been allayed, the campaigns of the rulers of the earth are suspended and those that are abroad return to their homes.
4.28.16
सम्प्रस्थिता मानसवासलुब्धाः
प्रियान्विताः सम्प्रति चक्रवाकाः ।
अभीक्ष्णवर्षोदकविक्षतेषु
यानानि मार्गेषु न सम्पतन्ति ॥
Those (swans) that yearn to live in lake Mānasa have started their journey. Cakravākas have joined their love mates. Vehicles are not able to make their way through the pathways battered by splashes of rain.
Ṡlōkas 16 to 50, which are in long meter, are widely agreed to be unauthentic. Though they carry some lyrical and poetic beauty, they lack the crispness and the degree of poetic beauty that is characteristic of Vālmeeki’s pen. They are considered to be of recent origin.
4.28.17
क्वचित्प्रकाशं क्वचिदप्रकाशं
नभः प्रकीर्णाम्बुधरं विभाति ।
क्वचित्क्वचित्पर्वतसन्निरुद्धं
रूपं यथा शान्तमहार्णवस्य ॥
Dense with clouds, that render it dark at some places and leaving it bright at the others, the sky is spectacular like a placid sea blocked by mountains here and there.
4.28.18
व्यामिश्रितं सर्जकदम्बपुष्पैः
नवं जलं पर्वतधातुताम्रम् ।
मयूरकेकाभिरनुप्रयातम्
शैलापगाश्शीघ्रतरं वहन्ति ॥
The mountain rapids swiftly carry the fresh waters red with every mineral along with the Sarja and Kadamba flowers, followed in rhythm by the cawing of peacocks.
4.28.19
रसाकुलं षट्पदसन्निकाशं
प्रभुज्यते जम्बुफलं प्रकामम् ।
अनेकवर्णं पवनावधूतं
भूमौ पतत्याम्रफलं विपक्वम् ॥
The Jambū fruit full of juice that is as black as the bumble bee, is eaten with avidity; the overripe mango of various hues falls to the ground, shaken by the wind.
4.28.20
विद्युत्पताकास्सबलाकमालाः
शैलेन्द्रकूटाकृतिसन्निकाशाः ।
गर्जन्ति मेघाः समुदीर्णनादाः
मत्ता गजेन्द्रा इव संयुगस्थाः ॥
With lightning for banners and rows of cranes for garlands, clouds as huge as mountain-crests roar explosively like tuskers in rut on the battlefield.
4.28.21
वर्षोदकाप्यायितशाद्वलानि
प्रवृत्तनृत्तोत्सवबर्हिणानि ।
वनानि निर्वृष्टवलाहकानि
पश्यापराह्णेष्वधिकं विभान्ति ॥
Look at these Vanas splendidly shining in the afternoons where the clouds emptied their water, satiating the swards and sending the peacocks in a riot of dance.
4.28.22
समुद्वहन्तस्सलिलातिभारं
बलाकिनो वारिधरा नदन्तः ।
महत्सु शृङ्गेषु महीधराणां
विश्रम्य विश्रम्य पुनः प्रयान्ति ॥
The rumbling clouds, on which the cranes attend, carry the heavy loads of water, resting from time to time on the huge crests of the mountain.
4.28.23
मेघाभिकामा परिसम्पतन्ती
सम्मोदिता भाति बलाकपङ्क्तिः ।
वातावधूता वरपौण्डरीकी
लम्बेव माला रचिताऽम्बरस्य ॥
The joyous cranes, impassioned at the (sight of) the clouds fly everywhere in rows. They look splendid as if they are garlands of supremely white lotuses hanging form the sky swayed by the breeze.
4.28.24
बालेन्द्रगोपान्तरचित्रितेन
विभाति भूमिर्नवशाद्वलेन ।
गात्रानुपृक्तेन शुकप्रभेण
नारीव लाक्षोक्षितकम्बलेन ॥
The earth, with its green swards streaked with young ladybirds, is gorgeous like a woman clad in parrot-green colored shawl adorned with dots of red lac.
4.28.25
निद्रा शनैः केशवमभ्युपैति
द्रुतं नदी सागरमभ्युपैति ।
हृष्टा बलाका घनमभ्युपैति
कान्ता सकामा प्रियमभ्युपैति ॥
Sleep, slowly, takes over Kēsava (Lord Vishṇu). River, in haste, joins the ocean. Cranes, joyously, join clouds. Woman, yearning, joins her beloved.
Lord Vishṇu takes a nap in the milky ocean for four months, from the Ēkādaṡi (eleventh day) of the month of Āshaḍha to the Ēkādaṡi (eleventh day) of the month of Kārteeka.
4.28.26
जाता वनान्ताश्शिखिसम्प्रनृत्ता
जाताः कदम्बाः सकदम्बशाखाः ।
जाता वृषा गोषु समानकामा
जाता मही सस्यवनाभिरामा ॥
The interiors of the Vanas are filled with peacocks. The Kadamba trees have their branches laden with flowers. The bulls are as impassioned as the cows. The earth is beautiful with its abundance of crops.
4.28.27
वहन्ति वर्षन्ति नदन्ति भान्ति
ध्यायन्ति नृत्यन्ति समाश्वसन्ति ।
नद्यो घना मत्तगजा वनान्ताः
प्रियाविहीनाश्शिखिनः प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
The rivers flow, the clouds rain, the elephants in rut trumpet, the forest interiors are bright, lovers separated from each other are absorbed in thoughts of each other, the peacocks dance and the monkeys stay calm.
4.28.28
प्रहर्षिताः केतकपुष्पगन्धम्
आघ्राय हृष्टा वननिर्झरेषु ।
प्रपातशब्दाकुलिता गजेन्द्राः
सार्धं मयूरैस्समदा नदन्ति ॥
Huge tuskers in rut, inhaling with delight the scent of Kētaka flowers, and excited by the noise of waterfalls in the forest, trumpet in tune with the peacocks’ cries.
4.28.29
धारानिपातैरभिहन्यमानाः
कदम्बशाखासु विलम्बमानाः ।
क्षणार्जितं पुष्परसावगाढं
शनैर्मदं षट्चरणास्त्यजन्ति ॥
The bees hanging on to the boughs of Kadamba trees are troubled by the water rushing along them. The exaltation that came upon them instantaneously when they tasted the sweetness of the flowers, fades away slowly.
4.28.30
अङ्गारचूर्णोत्करसन्निकाशैः
फलैस्सुपर्याप्तरसैस्समृद्धैः ।
जम्बूद्रुमाणां प्रविभान्ति शाखाः
निलीयमाना इव षट्पदौघैः ॥
The boughs of the Jambū trees, with charcoal-black succulent fruit everywhere, look as if swarms of bumble-bees are sucking on them.
4.28.31
तटित्पताकाभिरलङ्कृतानाम्
उदीर्णगम्भीरमहारवाणाम् ।
विभान्ति रूपाणि बलाहकानां
रणोत्सुकानामिव वारणानाम् ॥
The clouds, adorned with banners of lightning and rumbling with majestic thunder look like elephants trumpeting ready for the fight in war.
4.28.32
मार्गानुगश्शैलवनानुसारी
सम्प्रस्थितो मेघरवं निशम्य ।
युद्धाभिकामः प्रतिनादशङ्की
मत्तो गजेन्द्रः प्रतिसन्निवृत्तः ॥
The tusker in rut that is eager for a fight turns back in its tracks on the woody mountains, nervously mistaking the rumble of the cloud for the trumpet of an enemy tusker.
4.28.33
क्वचित्प्रगीता इव षट्पदौघैः
क्वचित्प्रनृत्ता इव नीलकण्ठैः ।
क्वचित्प्रमत्ता इव वारणेन्द्रैः
विभान्त्यनेकाश्रयिणो वनान्ताः ॥
The interiors of the Vanas that harbor life of various kinds, seem to be singing at places with their swarms of bees, dancing at places with their groups of peacocks, drunk at others with their great tuskers.
4.28.34
कदम्बसर्जार्जुनकन्दलाढ्या
वनान्तभूमिर्नववारिपूर्णा ।
मयूरमत्ताभिरुतप्रनृत्तैः
आपानभूमिप्रतिमा विभाति ॥
The interior of the forest, rich with its wealth of Kadamba, Arjuna, Sarja and Kandala flowers, and waters full of their nectar, resembles a tavern, with the peacocks cawing and dancing like mad.
4.28.35
मुक्तासकाशं सलिलं पतद्वै
सुनिर्मलं पत्रपुटेषु लग्नम् ।
हृष्टा विवर्णच्छदना विहङ्गाः
सुरेन्द्रदत्तं तृषिताः पिबन्ति ॥
The thirsty birds with colorful plumage joyously drink the drops of water fallen on the leaves, shining like pearls given to them by the King of Dēvas.
4.28.36
षट्पादतन्त्रीमधुराभिधानं
प्लवङ्गमोदीरितकण्ठतालम् ।
आविष्कृतं मेघमृदङ्गनादैः
वनेषु सङ्गीतमिव प्रवृत्तम् ॥
It looks as if there is a concert on in the Vana, with the bees providing the dulcet music of strings, the frogs keeping rhythm with their croaking, and the clouds playing the Mṛdaṅga.
4.28.37
क्वचित्प्रनृत्तै: क्वचिदुन्नदद्भि:
क्वचिच्च वृक्षाग्रनिषण्णकायैः ।
व्यालम्बबर्हाभरणैर्मयूरैः
वनेषु सङ्गीतमिव प्रवृत्तम् ॥
The peacocks with their hanging plumage for ornaments, seem to have their own concert, some dancing here and some cawing there and others perched on tree-tops (as audience).
4.28.38
स्वनैर्घनानां प्लवगाः प्रबुद्धाः
विहाय निद्रां चिरसन्निरुद्धाम् ।
अनेकरूपाकृतिवर्णनादाः
नवाम्बुधाराभिहता नदन्ति ॥
Frogs of many shapes, sizes, colors and voices, are croaking in various tones, awakened from their long hibernation by the noise of the thunder and the splash of fresh rains.
4.28.39
नद्यस्समुद्वाहितचक्रवाकाः
तटानि शीर्णान्यपवाहयित्वा ।
दृप्ता नवप्राभृतपूर्णभोगाः
द्रुतं स्वभर्तारमुपोपयान्ति ॥
The prideful rivers, full and content with the fresh gift of waters, carry the Cakravākas along in their currents, as they hasten to meet their lord, (the ocean).
4.28.40
नीलेषु नीला: प्रविभान्ति सक्ता:
मेघेषु मेघा नववारिपूर्णा: ।
दवाग्निदग्धेषु दवाग्निदग्धाः
शैलेषु शैला इव बद्धमूलाः ॥
Black rainclouds seem to stick to other black clouds like rocks charred by the forest fire lying next to other charred rocks.
4.28.41
प्रहृष्टसन्नादितबर्हिणानि
सशक्रगोपाकुलशाद्वलानि ।
चरन्ति नीपार्जुनवासितानि
गजास्सुरम्याणि वनान्तराणि ॥
Elephants roam in the lovely interiors of the Vanas, with the intoxicated peacocks cawing and the green swards alive with the ladybird, while the perfume of Neepa and Arjuna fills the air.
4.28.42
नवाम्बुधाराहतकेसराणि
द्रुतं परित्यज्य सरोरुहाणि ।
कदम्बपुष्पाणि सकेसराणि
नवानि हृष्टा भ्रमराः पिबन्ति ॥
Leaving aside quickly the lotuses whose filaments have been threshed by the pouring rain, the bees exultingly drink the nectar of the fresh Kadamba flowers that have filaments.
4.28.43
मत्ता गजेन्द्रा मुदिता गवेन्द्रा
वनेषु विश्रान्ततरा मृगेन्द्रा ।
रम्या नगेन्द्रा निभृता नरेन्द्राः
प्रक्रीडितो वारिधरैस्सुरेन्द्रः ॥
The great tuskers are in rut. The bulls, lords of the herd, are joyous. The lords of beasts (lions) in the Vana are charging forth. The mountains are lovely. The kings of men stay put. The King of the Dēvas play with the clouds.
4.28.44
मेघास्समुद्धूतसमुद्रनादाः
महाजलौघैर्गगनावलम्बाः ।
नदीस्तटाकानि सरांसि वापीः
महीं च कृत्स्नामपवाहयन्ति ॥
The roaring clouds put the oceans to shame. Hanging down from the sky, they flood the streams, ponds, lakes and step-wells, and deluge the entire earth.
4.28.45
वर्षप्रवेगा विपुलाः पतन्ति
प्रवान्ति वातास्समुदीर्णघोषाः ।
प्रणष्टकूलाः प्रवहन्ति शीघ्रं
नद्योजलैर्विप्रतिपन्नमार्गाः ॥
The downpour of rains is everywhere. The breezes have turned into gales. With their banks battered, rivers carry waters out of their courses.
4.28.46
नरैर्नरेन्द्रा इव पर्वतेन्द्राः
सुरेन्द्रदत्तै: पवनोपनीतैः ।
घनाम्बुकुम्भैरभिषिच्यमाना
रूपं श्रियं स्वामिव दर्शयन्ति ॥
The mountains display their glory getting a bath with the waters, sent in pots of clouds by the Lord of Dēvas and carried by the wind, like kings do when they get a bath of coronation from their subjects.
4.28.47
घनोपगूढं गगनं न तारा
न भास्करो दर्शनमभ्युपैति ।
नवैर्जलौघैर्धरणी वितृप्ता
तमो विलिप्ता न दिशः प्रकाशाः ॥
Covered by the clouds, the sky reveals neither the stars nor the sun. With the torrents of fresh water, the earth is satiated. The cardinals painted by darkness are indistinguishable from one another.
4.28.48
महान्ति कूटानि महीधराणां
धाराभिधौतान्यधिकं विभान्ति ।
महाप्रमाणैर्विपुलैः प्रपातैः
मुक्ताकलापैरिव लम्बमानैः ॥
The magnanimous crests of the mountains shine splendidly, washed clean by the torrents of rain and with the tall and giant waterfalls (adorning them) like long garlands of pearl-clusters.
4.28.49
शैलोपलप्रस्खलमानवेगाः
शैलोत्तमानां विपुलाः प्रपाताः ।
गुहासु सन्नादितबर्हिणासु
हारा विकीर्यन्त इवावभान्ति ॥
The huge mountain cascades bouncing off the mountain boulders and entering the caves that are reverberating with the cawing of peacocks look like pearl garlands strewn around.
4.28.50
शीघ्रप्रवेगा विपुलाः प्रपाताः
निर्धौतशृङ्गोपतला गिरीणाम् ।
मुक्ताकलापप्रतिमाः पतन्तो
महागुहोत्सङ्गतलैर्ध्रियन्ते ॥
The huge caverns are enjoy wearing the huge rapids and cascades that, having washed clean the mountain crests, fall on them like garlands of pearls.
4.28.51
सुरतामर्दविच्छिन्नाः
स्वर्गस्त्रीहारमौक्तिकाः ।
पतन्ति चातुला दिक्षु
तोयधारास्समन्ततः ॥
The cascades roll down everywhere, in all directions, as if they are bunches of pearls from the garlands broken during the love-play of celestial women.
4.28.52
निलीयमानैर्विहगैः
निमीलद्भिश्च पङ्कजैः ।
विकसन्त्या च मालत्या
गतोऽस्तं ज्ञायते रविः ॥
That the sun is setting is known only from the birds settling down in their nests, lotuses closing on themselves and the Mālati vines blooming.
4.28.53
वृत्ता यात्रा नरेन्द्राणाम्
सेना प्रतिनिवर्तते ।
वैराणि चैव मार्गाश्च
सलिलेन समीकृताः ॥
The expeditions of the kings became disrupted along the way. The water leveled their hostilities just as it did the pathways.
4.28.54
मासि प्रोष्ठपदे ब्रह्म
ब्राह्मणानां विवक्षताम् ।
अयमध्यायसमयः
सामगानामुपस्थितः ॥
This is Bhadrapada month, the time for the Brāhmaṇas, students of Sāma Vēda, to start their recitations.
4.28.55
निवृत्तकर्मायतनो
नूनं सञ्चितसञ्चयः ।
आषाढीमभ्युपगतो
भरतः कोसलाधिपः ॥
Bharata, the Lord of Kōsala, would certainly have readied himself for (the season that starts after) Āshaḍha, finishing household activities and securing the necessary provisions.
4.28.56
नूनमापूर्यमाणायाः
सरव्या वर्धते रयः ।
मां समीक्ष्य समायान्तम्
अयोध्याया इव स्वनः ॥
The river Sarayu, swelling with waters, must be rushing with the roar and din (that could only be expected) of Ayōdhyā upon seeing me come (back).
4.28.57
इमास्स्फीतगुणा वर्षाः
सुग्रीवस्सुखमश्नुते ।
विजितारिः सदारश्च
राज्ये महति च स्थितः ॥
Sugreeva, with his foe defeated, wife back and established in the mighty kingdom, should be very happy with these bountiful showers.
4.28.58
अहं तु हृतदारश्च
राज्याच्य महतश्च्युतः ।
नदीकूलमिव क्लिन्नम्
अवसीदामि लक्ष्मण ॥
But I, O Lakshmaṇa, with my wife abducted, fallen away from a great kingdom, am sinking away like a soaked river bank.
4.28.59
शोकश्च मम विस्तीर्णो
वर्षाश्च भृशदुर्गमाः ।
रावणश्च महान् शत्रुः
अपारं प्रतिभाति मे ॥
My grief that finds no end, these rains that are heavy, and Rāvaṇa who is a mighty foe, seem to me to be insurmountable!
4.28.60
अयात्रां चैव दृष्ट्वेमाम्
मार्गांश्च भृशदुर्गमान् ।
प्रणते चैव सुग्रीवे
न मया किञ्चिदीरितम् ॥
Seeing these pathways quite impassable for making any expedition, I had to but keep quiet, even though Sugreeva is very much at my beck and call.
4.28.61
अपि चातिपरिक्लिष्टम्
चिराद्दारैस्समागतम् ।
आत्मकार्यगरीयस्त्वात्
वक्तुं नेच्छामि वानरम् ॥
I did not feel like pressing upon that Vānara, as he was just reunited with his wife after a long period of hardships, and also as my task is not simple by any means.
4.28.62
स्वयमेव हि विश्रम्य
ज्ञात्वा कालमुपागतम् ।
उपकारं च सुग्रीवो
वेत्स्यते नात्र संशयः ॥
After getting enough rest, Sugreeva himself will know when it is time to pay back the help he received, without a doubt.
4.28.63
तस्मात्कालप्रतीक्षोऽहम्
स्थितोऽस्मि शुभलक्षण ।
सुग्रीवस्य नदीनां च
प्रसादमनुपालयन् ॥
Hence, O you of auspicious marks, I shall (patiently) wait till it is time for the grace of Sugreeva as well as for the grace of rivers.
4.28.64
उपकारेण वीरस्तु
प्रतीकारेण युज्यते ।
अकृतज्ञोऽप्रतिकृतो
हन्ति सत्त्ववतां मनः ॥
A Veera always pays back the favor he has received; those who do not remember or reciprocate will certainly hurt the feelings of good men.
These three Ṡlōkas are same as Ṡlōkas 45, 46 and 47 of Sarga 27.
4.28.65
अथैवमुक्तः प्रणिधाय लक्ष्मणः
कृताञ्जलिस्तत्प्रतिपूज्य भाषितम् ।
उवाच रामं स्वभिरामदर्शनं
प्रदर्शयन्दर्शनमात्मनश्शुभम् ॥
Lakshmaṇa, thus told, joining his palms in reverence, accepted, with due regard, what had been said. Showing his good feelings in his eyes, he said to Rāma of charming looks:
4.28.66
यथोक्तमेतत्तव सर्वमीप्सितं
नरेन्द्र कर्ता नचिराद्धरीश्वरः ।
शरत्प्रतीक्षः क्षमतामिमं भवान्
जलप्रपातं रिपुनिग्रहे धृतः ॥
O lord of men! The Lord of Vānaras will, not too long from now, do all that you wish and said. May you hold yourself patiently till these torrential rains come to an end. Please wait for the arrival of post-monsoon season, fully resolved to destroy the foe.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे अष्टाविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty eighth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
