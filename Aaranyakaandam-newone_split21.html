Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 21
In this Sarga, Khara wonders why Ṡūrpaṇakhā came back, again crying, when he has sent the fourteen Rākshasas to kill Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, and asks her about the same.
Ṡūrpaṇakhā tells Khara how all the fourteen Rākshasas were killed by Rāma and about the strength of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa. She dismisses Khara’s strength and declares that he is no equal to Rāma. She challenges him to kill Rāma in fight, if he is truly up to the reputation he has earned thus far. Or else, she says, he should flee away from Daṇḍaka Araṇya, which is now virtually claimed by Rāma.
3.21.1
स पुनः पतितां दृष्ट्वा
क्रोधाच्छूर्पणखां खरः ।
उवाच व्यक्तया वाचा
तामनर्थार्थमागताम् ॥
Seeing Ṡūrpaṇakhā come again and throw herself down before him, Khara asked her, whose coming boded no good, in an angry but clear voice:
3.21.2
मया त्विदानीं शूरास्ते
राक्षसा रुधिराशनाः ।
त्वत्प्रियार्थं विनिर्दिष्टाः
किमर्थं रुद्यते पुनः ॥
I have just now sent those valiant and blood-thirsty Rākshasas to do your pleasure. Why do you cry again?
3.21.3
भक्ताश्चैवानुरक्ताश्च
हिताश्च मम नित्यशः ।
हन्यमाना न हन्यन्ते
न न कुर्युर्वचो मम ॥
They are loyal to me, love me, and always keep my best interest in mind. They would never disobey my word. They can never be killed, regardless of how much one tries.
3.21.4
किमेतच्छ्रोतुमिच्छामि
कारणं यत्कृते पुनः ।
हा नाथेति विनर्दन्ती
सर्पवल्लुठसि क्षितौ ॥
I would like to hear the reason for you to wail again, ‘Ha, O Lord’ writhing on the ground like a snake!
3.21.5
अनाथवद्विलपसि
नाथे तु मयि संस्थिते ।
उत्तिष्ठोत्तिष्ठ माभैषीः
वैक्लव्यं त्यज्यतामिह ॥
While I am there to protect you, why do you cry as if there is none for you? Get up, do not be scared, get over the befuddlement now!
3.21.6
इत्येवमुक्ता दुर्धर्षा
खरेण परिसान्त्विता ।
विमृज्य नयने सास्रे
खरं भ्रातरमब्रवीत् ॥
Comforted by those words of Khara, she, the formidable one, wiped her tears and told her brother Khara:
3.21.7
अस्मीदानीमहं प्राप्ता
हृतश्रवणनासिका ।
शोणितौघपरिक्लिन्ना
त्वया च परिसन्त्विता ॥
True enough, I came here with my nose and ears cut and my body drenched with blood and received succor from you.
3.21.8
प्रेषिताश्च त्वया वीर
राक्षसास्ते चतुर्दश ।
निहन्तुं राघवं क्रोधान्
मत्प्रियार्थं सलक्षणम् ॥
And fourteen Rākshasas were sent by you, O Veera, in rage, to kill Rāghava along with Lakshmaṇa, to please me.
3.21.9
ते तु रामेण सामर्षाः
शूलपट्टिसपाणयः ।
समरे निहतास्सर्वे
सायकैर्मर्मभेदिभिः ॥
But those truculent Rākshasas armed with spears and tridents, were all killed by Rāma in the fight by the arrows that tore their vitals.
3.21.10
तान्दृष्ट्वा पतितान्भूमौ
क्षणेनैव महाबलान् ।
रामस्य च महत्कर्म
महांस्त्रासोऽभवन्मम ॥
Seeing those mighty strong ones fall on the ground instantly and the mighty deed of Rāma, I was struck by a great terror.
3.21.11
अहमस्मि समुद्विग्ना
विषण्णा च निशाचर ।
शरणं त्वां पुनः प्राप्ता
सर्वतोभयदर्शिनी ॥
O Night-rover! I am agitated and gloomy. I see danger from everywhere. I need your assurance again.
3.21.12
विषादनक्राध्युषिते
परित्रासोर्मिमालिनि ।
किं मां न त्रायसे मग्नां
विपुले शोकसागरे ॥
Why do you not save me? I am submerged in an ocean of grief infested with the crocodiles of gloom and billowing with waves of terror!
3.21.13
एते च निहता भूमौ
रामेण निशितैः शरैः ।
येऽपि मे पदवीं प्राप्ता
राक्षसाः पिशिताशनाः ॥
The flesh eating Rākshasas who followed me lie on the ground killed by the sharp arrows of Rāma.
3.21.14
मयि ते यद्यनुक्रोशो
यदि रक्षस्सु तेषु च ।
रामेण यदि ते शक्तिः
तेजो वास्ति निशाचर ।
दण्डकारण्यनिलयं
जहि राक्षसकण्टकम् ॥
If you have any pity for me and for those Rākshasas, and if you have the strength and power to stand up to Rāma, O Night-rover, destroy this thorn in the side of Rākshasas, who has come to live in the Daṇḍaka Araṇya.
3.21.15
यदि रामं ममामित्रं
न त्वमद्यवधिष्यसि ।
तवैव चाग्रतः प्राणां
त्यक्षामि निरपत्रपा ॥
If you do not kill that enemy of mine right today, I shall give up my life, right in front of you in disgrace.
3.21.16
बुद्ध्याहमनुपश्यामि
न त्वं रामस्य संयुगे ।
स्थातुं प्रतिमुखे शक्तः
सबलश्च महात्मनः ॥
But I can see well enough, in my mind, that you cannot stand against Mahātma Rāma in a fight, even with the support of your army.
3.21.17
शूरमानी न शूरस्त्वं
मिथ्यारोपितविक्रमः ।
मानुषौ यो न शक्नोषि
हन्तुं तौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
You boast of your valor, but you have none, with a falsely earned reputation for prowess, for you cannot slay even two mere mortals, those Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
3.21.18
रामेण यदि ते शक्तिः
तेजो वास्ति निशाचर ।
दण्डकारण्यनिलयं
जहि तं कुलपांसन ॥
O Night-rover! You bring disgrace to the race. If you have the strength and the power to face Rāma, you must kill him, who has taken Daṇḍaka Araṇya for his residence.
3.21.19
निस्सत्वस्याल्प वीर्यस्य
वासस्ते कीदृशस्त्विह ।
अपयाहि जनस्थानात्
त्वरितस्सहबान्धवः ॥
How absurd that you live here with no count-worthy strength or valor? Go away immediately from Janasthāna, along with your kin!
3.21.20
रामतेजोभिभूतो हि
त्वं क्षिप्रं विनशिष्यसि ।
स हि तेजस्समायुक्तो
रामो दशरथात्मजः ।
भ्राता चास्य महावीर्यो
येन चास्मि विरूपिता ॥
You will surely be destroyed, negated by the power of Rāma; for Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, has immense power and his brother, who disfigured me, is also of great valor.
3.21.22
एवं विलप्य बहुशो
राक्षसी प्रथितोदरी ।
कराभ्यामुदरं हत्वा
रुरोद भृशदुःखिता ॥
Thus wept the Rākshasi with monstrous belly, saying many a thing. She, extremely tormented, cried loudly beating her belly with her hands.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे एकविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty first Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
