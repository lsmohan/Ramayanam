Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 39
In this Sarga, seeing Hanumān ready to take leave, Seetā, her voice choked with tears, tells him that everything depends on him alone, as to how he keeps his spirits high and how he propels Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, who are sunken in grief, into action. Hanumān assures her, reminding her of the extraordinary prowess of Rāma and the extraordinary strength of Lakshmaṇa.
Seetā then implores Hanumān to stay for one more day giving her company and keeping the grief away from her. She asks a question that has been nagging her all the while, as to how the Vānara and Ṛksha army and, for that matter, the two brothers, would be able to cross the ocean. She then answers her own question saying that Hanumān is capable enough to manage everything. She tells him that she would like to see Rāma come to Laṅkā and destroy it himself with his arrows and take her back with him, displaying his prowess.
Hanumān reassures her, describing in many a word how capable Rāma and Lakshmaṇa and all the Vānaras are, and how everyone among the Vānaras is better than him, or at least as good as him, and how they encircled the earth many times. He tells her that the brothers will come to Laṅkā seated on his back and that the other Vānaras were capable of reaching Laṅkā in one single hop.
He assures her that it is only a matter of time before she sees Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Vānaras at the gates of Laṅkā, kill the Rākshasas and take her back and implores her to manage herself and bide the time till then.
5.39.1
मणिं दत्त्वा ततः सीता
हनूमन्तमथाब्रवीत् ॥
Seetā gave the ornament to Hanumān and said:
5.39.1
अभिज्ञानमभिज्ञातम्
एतद्रामस्य तत्त्वतः ।
मणिं तु दृष्ट्वा रामो वै
त्रयाणां संस्मरिष्यति ।
वीरो जनन्या मम च
राज्ञो दशरथस्य च ॥
Rāma knows this token very well. Rāma, the Veera, will be reminded of three people, my mother, myself and King Daṡaratha when he sees this jewel.
5.39.3
स भूयस्त्वं समुत्साहे
चोदितो हरिसत्तम ।
अस्मिन्कार्यसमारम्भे
प्रचिन्तय यदुत्तरम् ॥
And again, it is up to you, O eminent Vānara, as to how you would keep your spirits high enough and think of what is to be done next to get things moving.
5.39.4
त्वमस्मिन्कार्यनिर्योगे
प्रमाणं हरिसत्तम ।
हनुमन्यत्नमास्थाय
दुःखक्षयकरो भव ।
तस्य चिन्तयतो यत्नो
दुःखक्षयकरो भवेत् ॥
You shall decide, O eminent Vānara, how all this should be managed! You should be the one, O Hanumān, who would bring an end to this sorrow with your unceasing efforts. Try to think of what can be done to annihilate this grief.
5.39.5
स तथेति प्रतिज्ञाय
मारुतिर्भीमविक्रमः ।
शिरसा वन्द्य वैदेहीम्
गमनायोपचक्रमे ॥
Promising, ‘so shall it be!’, the son of Vāyu, of terrific prowess, saluted Vaidēhee bowing his head and got ready to leave.
5.39.6
ज्ञात्वा सम्प्रस्थितं देवी
वानरं मारुतात्मजम् ।
बाष्पगद्गदया वाचा
मैथिली वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing the Vānara, the son of Vāyu, ready to depart, lady Maithili said these words with her voice choked with tears:
5.39.7
कुशलं हनुमन्ब्रूयाः
सहितौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
सुग्रीवं च सहामात्यम्
वृद्धान् सर्वांश्च वानरान् ।
ब्रूयास्त्वं वानरश्रेष्ठ
कुशलं धर्मसंहितम् ॥
Please inquire, on my behalf, O Hanumān, about the wellbeing of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa who are always together. Likewise, O best of Vānaras, inquire in a way that custom agrees, about the wellbeing of Sugreeva, his ministers, and all the elderly Vānaras.
5.39.9
यथा स च महाबाहुः
मां तारयति राघवः ।
अस्माद्धुःखाम्बुसंरोधात्
त्वं समाधातुमर्हसि ॥
You should so plead my cause with Rāghava that he of the mighty arm takes me to the other end of this ocean of grief (immediately).
5.39.10
जीवन्तीं मां यथा रामः
सम्भावयति कीर्तिमान् ।
तत्तथा हनुमन्वाच्यम्
वाचा धर्ममवाप्नुहि ॥
Please say whatever needs to be said to Rāma of great renown to make him caress me while I am still alive. You will gain all the goodness of performing Dharma, by doing so.
5.39.11
नित्यमुत्साहयुक्ताश्च
वाचश्रुत्वा त्वयेरिताः ।
वर्धिष्यते दाशरथेः
पौरुषं मदवाप्तये ॥
The more often the son of Daṡaratha hears motivating words spoken by you, the stronger his zeal will become in regaining me.
5.39.12
मत्सन्देशयुता वाचः
त्वत्तश्श्रुत्वा च राघवः ।
पराक्रमविधिं वीरो
विधिवत्संविधास्यति ॥
Hearing those words from you that carry my message to him, Rāghava, the Veera, will deploy his power and take proper action.
5.39.13
सीताया वचनं श्रुत्वा
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ।
शिरस्यञ्जलिमाधाय
वाक्यमुत्तरमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing the words of Seetā, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, replied with these words, with his palms joined in reverence placed on his head:
5.39.14
क्षिप्रमेष्यति काकुत्स्थो
हार्यृक्षप्रवरैर्वृतः ।
यस्ते युधि विजित्यारीन्
शोकं व्यपनयिष्यति ॥
Very soon Kākutstha will come, accompanied by the chiefs of Vānaras and Ṛkshas; vanquishing his enemies in battle, he will wipe away your grief.
5.39.15
न हि पश्यामि मर्त्येषु
नासुरेषु सुरेषु वा ।
यस्तस्य क्षिपतो बाणान्
स्थातुमुत्सहतेऽग्रतः ॥
I cannot imagine anyone, be it a human or an Asura or a Sura who can stand against him when he shoots arrows with great speed.
5.39.16
अप्यर्कमपि पर्जन्यम्
अपि वैवस्वतं यमम् ।
स हि सोढुं रणे शक्तः
तव हेतोर्विशेषतः ॥
He can stand up even to the God of Rain or to Sun or to Sun’s son Yama, especially if it is for the sake of you.
5.39.17
स हि सागरपर्यन्ताम्
महीं शासितुमर्हति ।
त्वन्निमित्तो हि रामस्य
जयो जनकनन्दिनि ॥
He is capable of commanding the earth bordered by the oceans; all that he wins over is for your sake alone, O Delight of Janaka!
5.39.18
ततस्तं प्रस्थितं सीता
वीक्षमाणा पुनः पुनः ।
भर्तृस्नेहान्वितं वाक्यम्
सौहार्दादनुमानयत् ॥
Seetā looked wistfully at him again and again as he was ready to leave, and received with affection and regard the words he spoke that betrayed his affection for her husband.
5.39.19
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
सम्यक्सत्यं सुभाषितम् ।
जानकी बहुमेनेऽथ
वचनं चेदमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those words, well-spoken by him, every one of which was nothing but a reflection of the truth, Jānaki appreciated him immensely. She went on to add:
5.39.20
यदि वा मन्यसे वीर
वसैकाहमरिन्दम ।
कस्मिंश्चित्संवृते देशे
विश्रान्तःश्वो गमिष्यसि ॥
If you could give a thought to it, O Veera! O subduer of foe, you might as well take rest here for one more day in a some concealed location and leave tomorrow.
5.39.21
मम चेदल्पभाग्याया
सान्निध्यात्तव वानर ।
अस्य शोकस्य महतो
मुहूर्तं मोक्षणं भवेत् ॥
I, who is only so much fortunate, will find relief from this immense grief because of your presence, O Vānara, at least for a little while!
5.39.22
गते हि हरिशार्दूल
पुनरागमनाय तु ।
प्राणानामपि सन्देहो
ममस्यान्नात्र संशयः ॥
O tiger among Vānaras, once you leave, even though it is only to come back again, my survival will be in doubt, and there is no doubt about it.
5.39.23
तवादर्शनजः शोको
भूयो मां परितापयेत् ।
दुःखाद्धुःखपरामृष्टाम्
दीपयन्निव वानर ॥
The grief of not seeing you will torment me again like a fire, O Vānara, throwing me from sadness into greater sadness!
5.39.24
अयं च वीर सन्देहः
तिष्ठतीव ममाग्रतः ।
सुमहांस्त्वत्सहायेषु
हर्यृक्षेषु हरीश्वरः ॥
And there is this big nagging question that stands staring in front of me, O Lord of Vānaras, about your aids, the Vānaras and Ṛkshas:
5.39.25
कथं नु खलु दुष्पारम्
तरिष्यन्ति महोदधिम् ।
तानि हर्यृक्षसैन्यानि
तौ वा नरवरात्मजौ ॥
How will that army of Vānaras and Ṛkshas, or for that matter, those two sons of the king of men, cross the great ocean that is so difficult to cross!
5.39.26
त्रयाणामेव भूतानाम्
सागरस्यास्य लङ्घने ।
शक्तिस्स्याद्वैनतेयस्य
तव वा मारुतस्य वा ॥
Only three beings among all the creatures are capable of jumping over the ocean, Vainatēya, you and Vāyu!
5.39.27
तदस्मिन्कार्यनिर्योगे
वीरैवं दुरतिक्रमे ।
किं पश्यसि समाधानम्
त्वं हि कार्यविदां वरः ॥
While that being the challenge ahead that is quite difficult to surmount, O Veera, what do you see as a solution? Anyway, you are the best among taskmasters!
5.39.28
काममस्य त्वमेवैकः
कार्यस्य परिसाधने ।
पर्याप्तः परवीरघ्न
यशस्यस्ते फलोदयः ॥
Certainly, you are capable of killing every warrior among the enemies and accomplishing the mission all by yourself, and it would bring you quite a fame!
5.39.29
बलैस्समग्रैर्यदि माम्
रावणं जित्य संयुगे ।
विजयी स्वपुरं यायात्
तत्तस्य सदृशं भवेत् ॥
But it would be befitting of him if he defeats Rāvaṇa and all his forces in the battle and returns to his home city along with me!
5.39.30
शरैस्तु सङ्कुलां कृत्वा
लङ्कां परबलार्दनः ।
मां नयेद्यदि काकुत्स्थः
तत्तस्य सदृशं भवेत् ॥
It would be befitting of Kākutstha, who pulverizes his enemies, if he takes me away, throwing Laṅkā into turmoil with his arrows!
5.39.31
तद्यथा तस्य विक्रान्तम्
अनुरूपं महात्मनः ।
भवेदाहवशूरस्य
तथा त्वमुपपादय ॥
Hence, please arrange it so, that that Mahātma, a dare devil in battles, would get to display all the prowess that is the like of his!
5.39.32
तदर्थोपहितं वाक्यम्
प्रश्रितं हेतुसंहितम् ।
निशम्य हनुमान्शेषम्
वाक्यमुत्तरमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those purposeful and well-reasoned words that were spoken in all sincerity, Hanumān replied, saying what he had left to say:
5.39.33
देवि हर्यृक्षसैन्यानाम्
ईश्वरः प्लवतां वरः ।
सुग्रीवस्सत्त्वसम्पन्नः
तवार्थे कृतनिश्चयः ॥
O Dēvi! Sugreeva, the best of Vānaras and the lord of Vānara and Ṛksha armies, endowed with immense power, is completely committed to your cause.
5.39.34
स वानरसहस्राणाम्
कोटीभिरभिसंवृतः ।
क्षिप्रमेष्यति वैदेहि
राक्षसानां निबर्हणः ॥
He, along with thousands of Vānaras, and surrounded by crores of them, will come here very soon to kill the Rākshasas!
5.39.35
तस्य विक्रमसम्पन्नाः
सत्त्ववन्तो महाबलाः ।
मनस्सङ्कल्पसम्पाता
निदेशे हरयः स्थिताः ॥
The Vānaras of terrific prowess, great power and immense strength, who can move at the speed of their thought, are standing ready, at his beck and call.
5.39.36
येषां नोपरि नाधस्तात्
न तिर्यक्सज्जते गतिः ।
न च कर्मसु सीदन्ति
महत्स्वमिततेजसः ॥
Nothing can obstruct their movement upward, downward or sideways. Endowed with limitless power, they do not cave in even under the most demanding of tasks.
5.39.37
असकृत्तैर्महोत्साहैः
ससागरधराधरा ।
प्रदक्षिणीकृता भूमिः
वायुमार्गानुसारिभिः ॥
With great zeal, they circled the earth and its oceans and mountains many a time, following the path of the wind.
5.39.38
मद्विशिष्टाश्च तुल्याश्च
सन्ति तत्र वनौकसः ।
मत्तः प्रत्यवरः कश्चित्
नास्ति सुग्रीवसन्निधौ ॥
The Vana dwellers that are there are either better than me or at least as good as me. There is no one in Sugreeva’s company who is inferior to me.
5.39.39
अहं तावदिह प्राप्तः
किं पुनस्ते महाबलाः ।
न हि प्रकृष्टाः प्रेष्यन्ते
प्रेष्यन्ते हीतरे जनाः ॥
If I myself could come here, what to say of those mighty strong ones? The exceptional ones will never be deployed (first), but only the ordinary ones, is it not?
5.39.40
तदलं परितापेन
देवि शोको व्यपैतु ते ।
एकोत्पातेन ते लङ्काम्
एष्यन्ति हरियूथपाः ॥
Hence, O Dēvi, enough of worry; give up your grief! The warrior chiefs of Vānaras will arrive in Laṅkā in a single hop.
5.39.41
मम पृष्ठगतौ तौ च
चन्द्रसूर्याविवोदितौ ।
त्वत्सकाशं महासत्त्वौ
नृसिंहावागमिष्यतः ॥
Those two lions among men of immense strength will come here to your presence, seated on my back, like the rising sun and moon.
5.39.42
ततो वीरौ नरवरौ
सहितौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
आगम्य नगरीं लङ्काम्
सायकैर्विधमिष्यतः ॥
Coming here together, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, the two Veeras, the best among men, will destroy Laṅkā with their arrows.
5.39.43
सगणं रावणं हत्त्वा
राघवो रघुनन्दनः ।
त्वामादाय वरारोहे
स्वपुरीं प्रतियास्यति ॥
Killing Rāvaṇa and his stooges, Rāghava, the delight of Raghu dynasty, will return to his city, taking you with him, O you of excellent Kaṭee!
5.39.44
तदाश्वसिहि भद्रं ते
भव त्वं कालकाङ्क्षिणी ।
नचिराद्द्रक्ष्यसे रामम्
प्रज्वलन्तमिवानलम् ॥
Be comforted! May all go well for you! Begin counting the days down and before long, you will see Rāma, blazing like fire.
5.39.45
निहते राक्षसेन्द्रेऽस्मिन्
सपुत्रामात्यबान्धवे ।
त्वं समेष्वसि रामेण
शशाङ्केनेव रोहिणी ॥
With the lord of Rākshasas killed along with his children, ministers and relations, you will join Rāma, like Rōhiṇee does the moon.
5.39.46
क्षिप्रं त्वं देवि शोकस्य
पारं यास्यसि मैथिलि ।
रावणं चैव रामेण
निहतं द्रक्ष्यसेऽचिरात् ॥
You will soon reach the other end of this (ocean of) grief, O Dēvi! Before long, O Maithili, you will see Rāvaṇa killed by Rāma!
5.39.47
एवमाश्वास्य वैदेहीम्
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ।
गमनाय मतिं कृत्वा
वैदेहीं पुनरब्रवीत् ॥
Comforting Vaidēhee thus, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, all set in his mind to leave, said once again to Vaidēhee:
5.39.48
तमरिघ्नं कृतात्मानम्
क्षिप्रं द्रक्ष्यसि राघवम् ।
लक्ष्मणं च धनुष्पाणिम्
लङ्काद्वारमुपागतम् ॥
You will soon see Rāghava, resolved to decimate his enemies, and Lakshmaṇa, with bow in his hand, arriving at the gates of Laṅkā!
5.39.49
नखदंष्ट्रायुधान्वीरान्
सिंहशार्दूलविक्रमान् ।
वानरान्वारणेन्द्राभान्
क्षिप्रं द्रक्ष्यसि सङ्गतान् ॥
You will very soon see the company of Vānara Veeras who are terrific like tigers and lions and majestic like elephants, having nails and fangs for weapons.
5.39.50
शैलाम्बुदनिकाशानाम्
लङ्कामलयसानुषु ।
नर्दतां कपिमुख्यानाम्
आर्ये यूधान्यनेकशः ॥
O noble one! You will see chiefs of Vānaras and warriors in limitless numbers roaring on the flanks of Laṅkā’s Malaya mountain, resembling mountains and clouds themselves.
5.39.51
स तु मर्मणि घोरेण
ताडितो मन्मथेषुणा ।
न शर्म लभते रामः
सिंहार्धित इव द्विपः ॥
Rāma, hit at his vitals by the terrible darts of Manmatha, knows no more peace than an elephant oppressed by a lion.
5.39.52
मा रुदो देवि शोकेन
माभूत्ते मनसोऽप्रियम् ।
शचीव पत्या शक्रेण
भर्त्रा नाथवती ह्यसि ॥
O Dēvi, do not cry with grief! May there be no disquiet in your heart, for you do have a protector in your husband, as Ṡaci does in Ṡakra.
5.39.53
रामाद्विशिष्टः कोऽन्योऽस्ति
कश्चित्सौमित्रिणा समः ।
अग्निमारुतकल्पौ तौ
भ्रातरौ तव संश्रयौ ॥
There is no one who can excel Rāma. There is no one who is a match for Lakshmaṇa. You have (none other than) those two brothers, who are comparable to fire and wind for your protection!
5.39.54
नास्मिंश्चिरं वत्स्यसि देवि देशे
रक्षोगणैरध्युषितेऽतिरौद्रे ।
न ते चिरादामगमनं प्रियस्य
क्षमस्व मत्सङ्गमकालमात्रम् ॥
You will not be here for long in this place that is inhabited by hosts of extremely cruel Rākshasas! Before long your beloved will come. Please bear with this, but only till I meet him.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे एकोनचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty ninth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
