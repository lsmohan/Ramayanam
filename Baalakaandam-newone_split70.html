Baala Kaanda - Sarga 70
This Sarga narrates the beginning of the ceremonial 'engagement'of Rāma and Seetā. Janaka invites his brother Kuṡadhwaja to be part of the Yajña and the marriage of Seetā.
Vasishṭha narrates the lineage of Rāma to king Janaka at the honorable request of Daṡaratha.
1.70.1
ततः प्रभाते जनकः
कृतकर्मा महर्षिभिः ।
उवाच वाक्यं वाक्यज्ञः
शतानन्दं पुरोहितम् ॥
After having all the morning activities completed by the Maharshis, Janaka, the prudent, said to Ṡatānanda, the Purōhita:
1.70.2
भ्राता मम महातेजा
यवीयानतिधार्मिकः ।
कुशध्वज इति ख्यातः
पुरीमध्यवसच्छुभाम् ।
वार्याफलकपर्यन्ताम्
पिबन्निक्षुमतीं नदीम् ।
साङ्काश्यां पुण्यसङ्काशाम्
विमानमिव पुष्पकम् ॥
My younger brother, the supremely radiant and ultimate Dhārmika, Kuṡadhwaja lives in the auspicious city of Sānkāṡya that stretches into the waters bordered by iron posts, which is like a Pushpaka Vimāna, enjoying the waters of the river Ikshumati.
1.70.4
तमहं द्रष्टुमिच्छामि
यज्ञगोप्ता स मे मतः ।
प्रीतिं सोऽपि महातेजा
इमां भोक्ता मया सह ॥
I would like to see him. He wanted to be part of managing my Yajña. He, the supremely radiant would also enjoy all of this along with me.
1.70.5
एवमुक्ते तु वचने
शतानन्दस्य सन्निधौ ।
आगताः केचिद् अव्यग्रा
जनकस्तान् समादिशत् ।
शासनात्तु नरेन्द्रस्य
प्रययुः शीघ्रवाजिभिः ।
समानेतुं नरव्याघ्रम्
विष्णुमिन्द्राज्ञया यथा ॥
Just as this was being told to Ṡatānanda, there came a few sensible and mature envoys. Janaka commanded them, and by that order of the king they left immediately on very fast horses, akin to rushing to reach Vishṇu upon the orders of Indra, to bring that tiger among men (Kuṡadhwaja).
Kuṡadhwaja is the brother of Janaka, just as Vishṇu is the brother of Indra.
1.70.7
साङ्काश्यां ते समागम्य
ददृशुश्च कुशध्वजम् ।
न्यवेदयन् यथावृत्तम्
जनकस्य च चिन्तितम् ॥
They reached Sānkāṡya, visited Kuṡadhwaja, and conveyed the message verbatim, just as it was in the mind of Janaka.
The skill of envoys is to convey the message exactly, in word and spirit.
1.70.8
तद्वृत्तं नृपतिः श्रुत्वा
दूतश्रेष्ठैर्महाजवैः ।
आज्ञयाथ नरेन्द्रस्य
आजगाम कुशध्वजः ॥
On hearing those orders of Janaka from the fast and best of envoys, Kuṡadhwaja came immediately (to Mithilā).
1.70.9
स ददर्श महात्मानम्
जनकं धर्मवत्सलम् ।
सोऽभिवाद्य शतानन्दम्
राजानं चापि धार्मिकम् ।
राजार्हं परमं दिव्यम्
आसनं चाध्यरोहत ॥
He then visited Mahātma Janaka, who was always interested in Dharma. He paid respects to Ṡatānanda and to the Dhārmika king and took seat on an impressive chair befitting a king.
1.70.10
उपविष्टाबुभौ तौ तु
भ्रातरावतितेजसौ ।
प्रेषयामासतुर्वीरौ
मन्त्रिश्रेष्ठं सुदामनम् ॥
After taking their respective seats, the supremely radiant and brave brothers, directed the best of ministers Sudāmana:
1.70.11
गच्छ मन्त्रिपते शीघ्रम्
चक्ष्वाकममितप्रभम् ।
आत्मजैः सह दुर्द्धर्षम्
आनयस्व समन्त्रिणम् ॥
O chief of ministers! May you go immediately and bring here the invincible and supremely radiant king of Ikshwāku lineage (Daṡaratha) along with his two sons and ministers!
1.70.12
औपकार्य्यं स गत्वा तु
रघूणां कुलवर्धनम् ।
ददर्श शिरसाचैनं
अभिवाद्येदमब्रवीत् ॥
He then went to the guest house and visited the advancer of Raghu dynasty, bowed respectfully and said:
1.70.13
अयोध्याधिपते वीर
वैदेहो मिथिलाधिपः ।
स त्वां द्रष्टुं व्यवसितः
सोपाध्यायपुरोहितम् ॥
O Veera! O lord of Ayōdhyā! The king of the country of Vidēha and city of Mithilā would like to see you along with your Gurus and Purōhitas.
1.70.14
मन्त्रिश्रेष्ठवचः श्रुत्वा
राजा सर्षिगणस्तदा ।
सबन्धुरगमत्तत्र
जनको यत्र वर्तते ॥
Hearing those words of the best of ministers, that king along with Ṛshis and relatives went to the place of Janaka.
1.70.15
स राजा मन्त्रिसहितः
सोपाध्यायः सबान्धवः ।
वाक्यं वाक्यविदां श्रेष्ठो
वैदेहमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
That king, best among the most eloquent, accompanied by ministers, relatives and Gurus said to the king of Vidēha:
1.70.16
विदितं ते महाराज
इक्ष्वाकुकुलदैवतम् ।
वक्ता सर्वेषु कृत्येषु
वसिष्ठो भगवानृषिः ॥
O great king! You would very well know that the Bhagawān, the Ṛshi Vasishṭha is god for everyone in the lineage of Ikshwāku and the spokesperson at all our major events.
1.70.17
विश्वामित्राभ्यनुज्ञातः
सह सर्वैर्महर्षिभिः ।
एष वक्ष्यति धर्मात्मा
वसिष्ठो मे यथाक्रमम् ॥
With the permission of Viṡwāmitra and all the great Ṛshis, this Dharmātma Vasishṭha would now speak about me, in the proper manner!
1.70.18
एवमुक्त्वा नरश्रेष्ठे
राज्ञां मध्ये महात्मनाम् ।
तूष्णीम्भूते दशरथे
वसिष्ठो भगवानृषिः ।
उवाच वाक्यं वाक्यज्ञो
वैदेहं सपुरोधसम् ॥
Thus concluded Daṡaratha, the foremost among men in the midst of all of the kings. The Bhagawān, Ṛshi, Vasishṭha, who knew the right words to put forth, then spoke to the king of Vidēha, who was accompanied by his Purōhitas:
1.70.20
अव्यक्तप्रभवो ब्रह्मा
शाश्वतो नित्य अव्ययः ।
तस्मान्मरीचिः सञ्जज्ञे
मरीचेः काश्यपः सुतः ॥
The eternal, permanent and imperishable Brahma emerged from the unmanifest. From him was born Mareeci. From Mareeci, Kāṡyapa.

1.70.21
विवस्वान् काश्यपाज्जज्ञे
मनुर्वैवस्वतः स्मृतः ।
मनुः प्रजापतिः पूर्वम्
इक्ष्वाकुस्तु मनोः सुतः ॥
From Kāṡyapa was born Vivaswan (sun). Manu is known to be the son of Vivaswan. Manu was a Prajāpati, a progenitor. Ikshwāku was the son of Manu.
1.70.22
तमिक्ष्वाकुमयोध्यायाम्
राजानं विद्धि पूर्वकम् ।
इक्ष्वाकोस्तु सुतः श्रीमान्
कुक्षिरित्येव विश्रुतः ॥
Ikshwāku was the first king of Ayōdhyā. The richly endowed son of Ikshwāku was well known by the name Kukshi.
1.70.23
कुक्षेरथात्मजः श्रीमान्
विकुक्षिरुदपद्यत ।
विकुक्षेस्तु महातेजा
बाणः पुत्रः प्रतापवान् ॥
The well-endowed Vikukshi was born as the son of Kukshi. Vikukshi's son was the supremely radiant and valiant Bāṇa.
1.70.24
बाणस्य तु महातेजा
अनरण्यः प्रतापवान् ।
अनरण्यात्पृथुर्जज्ञे
त्रिशङ्कुस्तु पृथोः सुतः ॥
Bāṇa’s son was the supremely radiant and valiant Anaraṇya. From Anaraṇya was born Pṛthu. Pṛthu's son was Triṡaṅku.
1.70.25
त्रिशङ्कोरभवत् पुत्रो
दुन्धुमारो महायशाः ।
युवनाश्वसुतस्त्वासीत्
मान्धाता पृथिवीपतिः ॥
The well renowned Dundhumāra (also known as Yuvanāṡwa) came from Triṡaṅku. The son of Yuvanāṡwa was the king Māndhāta.
1.70.26
मान्धातुस्तु सुतः श्रीमान्
सुसन्धिरुदपद्यत ।
सुसन्धेरपि पुत्रौ द्वौ
ध्रुवसन्धिः प्रसेनजित् ॥
Māndhāta’s son was the well-endowed Susandhi. Susandhi had two sons, Dhruvasandhi and Prasēnajit.
1.70.27
यशस्वी ध्रुवसन्धेस्तु
भरतो नाम नामतः ।
भरतात्तु महातेजा
असितो नाम जातवान् ॥
Dhruvasandhi had the renowned son by name Bharata. Bharata had a supremely radiant son by name Asita.
1.70.28
यस्यैते प्रतिराजान
उदपद्यन्त शत्रवः ।
हैहयास्तालजङ्घाश्च
शूराश्च शशिबिन्दवः ॥
The Haihayas, Tālajaṅghas, Ṡūras, Ṡaṡibindus opposed Asita and became his enemies.
1.70.29
तांस्तु स प्रतियुद्ध्यन् वै
युद्धे राजा प्रवासितः ।
हिमवन्तमुपागम्य
भृगुप्रस्रवणेऽवसत् ।
असितोऽल्पबलो राजा
मन्त्रिभिः सहितस्तदा ॥
He was forced out of the kingdom, while resisting and fighting them. With his remaining forces and with his ministers, he went to the Himalayas and lived in the area of Bhṛguprasravaṇa.
1.70.30
द्वे चास्य भार्ये गर्भिण्यौ
बभूवतुरिति श्रुतम् ।
एका गर्भविनाशाय
सपत्न्यै सगरं ददौ ॥
We hear that he had two wives, both of whom were pregnant and one of them poisoned the other to kill the fetus.
1.70.31
ततः शैलवरं रम्यम्
बभूवाभिरतो मुनिः ।
भार्गवश्च्यवनो नाम
हिमवन्तमुपाश्रितः ॥
A Muni by name Cyavana from the lineage of Bṛghu was also staying on that great and beautiful mountain.
1.70.32
तत्रैका तु महाभागा
भार्गवं देववर्चसम् ।
ववन्दे पद्मपत्राक्षी
काङ्क्षन्तीं सुतमात्मनः ॥
Then that blessed one, with eyes like lotus petals, wishing to have a son, went and bowed to Cyavana, who was lustrous like a Dēva.
1.70.33
तमृषिं साऽभ्युपागम्य
कालिन्दी चाभ्यवादयत् ।
स तामभ्यवदद्विप्रः
वरेप्सुं पुत्रजन्मनि ॥
Kālindi approached that Ṛshi and prostrated. That Brāhmaṇa then said to her, who was wishing for a boon to have a son:
1.70.34
तव कुक्षौ महाभागे
सुपुत्रः सुमहाबलः ।
महावीर्यो महातेजा
अचिरात् सञ्जनिष्यति ।
गरेण सहितः श्रीमान्
मा शुचः कमलेक्षणे ॥
O blessed one! You are now carrying a mighty son. That supremely radiant, well-endowed and valiant son will be born soon, along with the poison. O lotus eyed one! Do not worry!
1.70.36
च्यवनं तु नमस्कृत्य
राजपुत्री पतिव्रता ।
पतिशोकातुरा तस्मात्
पुत्रं देवी व्यजायत ॥
That princess and devout wife, grieving at the fate of her husband, bowed to Cyavana and thereafter gave birth to a son.
1.70.37
सपत्न्या तु गरस्तस्यै
दत्तो गर्भजिघांसया ।
सह तेन गरेणैव
जातः स सगरोऽभवत् ॥
As he was born along with the poison that was given by his step mother to destroy the fetus, he came to be known as Sagara.
Sagara = Sa + Gara = along with Gara, poison.
1.70.38
सगरस्यासमञ्जस्तु
असमञ्जात्तथांशुमान् ।
दिलीपोंऽशुमतः पुत्रो
दिलीपस्य भगीरथः ॥
From Sagara, Asamañja, from Asamañja, Aṃṡumān, from Aṃṡumān, Dileepa were born. Dileepa’s son was Bhageeratha.
1.70.39
भगीरथात्ककुत्स्थश्च
ककुत्स्थस्य रघुस्सुतः ।
रघोस्तु पुत्रस्तेजस्वी
प्रवृद्धः पुरुषादकः ।
कल्माषपादो ह्यभवत्
तस्माज्जातश्च शङ्खणः ॥
From Bhageeratha, Kakutstha was born. Kakutstha’s son was Raghu. Raghu's son was the radiant Pravṛddha, who became Purushādaka and Kalmāshapāda. From him was born, Ṡaṅkhaṇa.
Pravṛddha, due to a curse from Vasishṭha lived eating the flesh of humans. Hence he was known as Purushādaka (Cannibal). He then tried to curse Vasishṭha back, but was stopped by his wife. Then he dropped the water on his own feet and hence he came to be known also as Kalmāshapāda (person with blemished feet).
1.70.40
सुदर्शनः शङ्खणस्य
अग्निवर्णः सुदर्शनात् ।
शीघ्रगस्त्वग्निवर्णस्य
शीघ्रगस्य मरुः सुतः ।
मरोः प्रशुश्रुकस्त्वासीत्
अम्बरीषः प्रशुश्रुकात् ॥
From Ṡaṅkhaṇa was born Sudarṡana. Agnivarṇa was born of Sudarṡana. Agnivarṇa’s son was Ṡeeghraga and Ṡeeghraga had Maru as his son. Maru had Praṡuṡṛuka as his son and from Praṡuṡṛuka came Ambareesha.
1.70.42
अम्बरीषस्य पुत्रोऽभूत्
नहुषः पृथिवीपतिः ।
नहुषस्य ययातिश्च
नाभागस्तु ययातिजः ॥
King Nahusha happened to be the son of Ambareesha. Nahusha’s son was Yayāti. Nābhāga was born to Yayāti.
1.70.43
नाभागस्य बभूवाज
अजाद्दशरथोऽभवत् ।
अस्माद्दशरथाज्जातौ
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
From Nābhāga was born Aja. From Aja was born Daṡaratha. From this Daṡaratha were born Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
1.70.44
आदिवंशविशुद्धानाम्
राज्ञां परमधर्मिणाम् ।
इक्ष्वाकुकुलजातानाम्
वीराणां सत्यवादिनाम् ।
रामलक्ष्मणयोरर्थे
त्वत्सुते वरये नृप ।
सदृशाभ्यां नरश्रेष्ठ
सदृशे दातुमर्हसि ॥
O king! O best among men! I would like to choose your daughters as wives for this Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, who belong to a clean and auspicious dynasty, who are born into the lineage of Ikshwāku, who are known to be ultimate observers of Dharma, upholders of truth and Veeras! May you consider giving those suitable girls to these most suitable boys!
From those days of Rāmāyaṇa till now, this Ṡlōka, or its equivalent embellished version in the local tongue, is enacted every year in every village of India, before reenacting the wedding of Seetā and Rāma.
One of the most celebrated of human emotions, described in this Ṡlōka, has since been touching every sensitive and sensible heart like a fresh breeze.
We are all thus indebted to the first among poets, Vālmeeki, one of our beloved ancestors, for giving us such a memorable Ṡlōka. Things like this made India a destination for people from all over the world for millennia. (Just as USA is today)
The Rāmāyaṇa has etched these cherishable sensitivities and sensibilities in a medium that, to use the words from Bhagavad Gita, cannot be burned by fire, cannot be flooded by water and cannot be blown away by the winds.
We must all congratulate ourselves for preserving that medium by merely becoming the readers.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे सप्ततितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventieth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
