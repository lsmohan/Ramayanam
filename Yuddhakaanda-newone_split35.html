Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 35
Rāvaṇa rebukes his ministers for doing nothing even after knowing about Rāma crossing the ocean and his army camping on its southern shore.
Then Mālyavān, his mother’s father and elderly statesman, best of his ministers, advises Rāvaṇa that he should give Seetā back and negotiate peace with such a formidable enemy as Rāma.
He puts forth at least four reasons why he should do so: one, that Dharma eventually wins over Adharma; two, that all the Ṛshis whom he harassed wish for the success of Rāma; three, that he has immunity from death from Dēvas, Dānavas and Yakshas, but not from humans, Vānaras and Ṛkshas; and four, that evil omens are seen everywhere in Laṅkā.
6.35.1
तेन शङ्खविमिश्रेण
भेरीशब्देन राघवः ।
उपयाति महाबाहू
रामः परपुरञ्जयः ॥
The din of beating drums mixed with the blaring conches announced the arrival of the scion of the Raghus, conqueror of the citadel of the foe.
6.35.2
तं निनादं निशम्याथ
रावणो राक्षसेश्वरः ।
मुहूर्तं ध्यानमास्थाय
सचिवानभ्युदैक्षत ॥
Hearing those sounds, Rāvaṇa, the Lord of Rākshasas, thought for a while and looked at his ministers.
6.35.3
अथ तान् सचिवांस्तत्र
सर्वानाभाष्य रावणः ।
सभां सन्नादयन् सर्वाम्
इत्युवाच महाबलः ।
जगत्सन्तापनः क्रूरो
गर्हयन् राक्षसेश्वरः ॥
Rāvaṇa of immense strength, Lord of Rākshasas, a cruel one, harrier of the world, greeted everyone and roared reprovingly, the entire assembly hall reverberating.
6.35.4
तरणं सागरस्यापि
विक्रमं बलसञ्चयम् ।
यदुक्तवन्तो रामस्य
भवन्तस्तन्मया श्रुतम् ॥
I have heard all that you have said about Rāma’s crossing the ocean, his prowess, and the army he has gathered about him.
6.35.5
भवतश्चाप्यहं वेद्मि
युद्धे सत्यपराक्रमान् ।
तूष्णीकानीक्षतोऽन्योन्यम्
विदित्वा रामविक्रमम् ॥
And I can see that you, who used to display indefeasible prowess on the battlefield, keep quiet and look at each other, after learning about Rāma’s prowess.
6.35.6
ततस्तु सुमहाप्राज्ञो
माल्यवान् नाम राक्षसः ।
रावणस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
मातुः पैतामहोऽब्रवीत् ॥
Having heard the words of Rāvaṇa, Mālyavān, his mother’s father, a highly sagacious Rākshasa, said:
6.35.7
विद्यास्वभिविनीतो यो
राजा राजन्नयानुगः ।
स शास्ति चिरमैश्वर्यम्
अरींश्च कुरुते वशे ॥
A King who is well instructed in various subjects and follows the principles of statecraft, O King, keeps his enemies under control and commands sovereign power for long years.
6.35.8
सन्दधानो हि कालेन
विगृह्णंश्चारिभिः सह ।
स्वपक्षवर्धनं कुर्वन्
महदैश्वर्यमश्नुते ॥
He confronts the enemies as well as makes peace with them based on the situation, and thereby strengthens his position and commands great power.
6.35.9
हीयमानेन कर्तव्यो
राज्ञा सन्धिः समेन च ।
न शत्रुमवमन्येत
ज्यायान् कुर्वीत विग्रहम् ॥
A king whose power has diminished, or is about the same as that of the enemy is better off calling for peace. Never should an enemy be insulted. Confrontation makes sense only when one clearly enjoys superior power.
6.35.10
तन्मह्यं रोचते सन्धिः
सह रामेण रावण ।
यदर्थमभियुक्ताःस्म
सीता तस्मै प्रदीयताम् ॥
Therefore I am in favor of peace with Rāma, O Rāvaṇa; let us give back Seetā, for the sake of whom we are being invaded.
6.35.11
यस्य देवर्षयः सर्वे
गन्धर्वाश्च जयैषिणः ।
विरोधं मा गमस्तेन
सन्धिस्ते तेन रोचताम् ॥
Do not make an enemy out of the one whose victory all Dēvas, Ṛshis and Gandharvas seek. Please consider making peace with him.
6.35.12
असृजद्भगवान् पक्षौ
द्वावेव हि पितामहः ।
सुराणामसुराणां च
धर्माधर्मौ तदाश्रयौ ॥
The Grandsire of the worlds, the Bhagawān, created only two kinds, Suras and Asuras. Dharma and Adharma flourish, practiced by them.
6.35.13
धर्मो हि श्रूयते पक्षो
ह्यमराणां महात्मनाम् ।
अधर्मो रक्षसां पक्षो
ह्यसुराणां च रावण ॥
It is heard, in general, that Dharma can be found with the Amaras (Suras), the Mahātmas and Adharma with Rākshasas, the Asuras.
6.35.14
धर्मो वै ग्रसतेऽधर्मम्
ततः कृतमभूद्युगम् ।
अधर्मो ग्रसते धर्मम्
ततस्तिष्यः प्रवर्तते ॥
When Dharma overwhelms Adharma, it will be the age of Kṛta. When Adharma overwhelms Dharma, it is the age of Tishya (Kali).
6.35.15
तत्त्वया चरता लोकान्
धर्मो विनिहतो महान् ।
अधर्मः प्रगृहीतश्च
तेनास्मद्बलिनः परे ॥
You adopted Adharma and assaulted the all important Dharma, wherever you went. That is why the enemies (who adhere to Dharma) are stronger than us.
6.35.16
स प्रमादाद्विवृद्धस्तेऽ -
धर्मोऽभिग्रसते हि नः ।
विवर्धयति पक्षं च
सुराणां सुरभावनः ॥
That Adharma which you strengthened by your heedlessness devours us now; it works in favor of the Suras and strengthens their side.
6.35.17
विषयेषु प्रसक्तेन
यत्किञ्चित्कारिणा त्वया ।
ऋषीणामग्निकल्पानाम्
उद्वेगो जनितो महान् ॥
Indulged in the pursuit of pleasures, you have behaved recklessly doing whatever you pleased greatly disquieting the Ṛshis who are (brilliant, pure and powerful) like fire.
6.35.18
तेषां प्रभावो दुर्धर्षः
प्रदीप्त इव पावकः ।
तपसा भावितात्मानो
धर्मस्यानुग्रहे रताः ॥
None can stand against their (yogic) power that blazes like fire. With their minds purified by Tapa they are devoted to the strengthening of Dharma.
6.35.19
मुख्यैर्यज्ञैर्यजन्त्येते
नित्यं तैस्तैर्द्विजातयः ।
जुह्वत्यग्नींश्च विधिवत्
वेदांश्चोच्चैरधीयते ॥
Those Brāhmaṇas perform powerful Yajñas all the time. They offer oblations into fire in a customary manner chanting Vedas in full-throated voices.
6.35.20
अभिभूय च रक्षांसि
ब्रह्मघोषानुदैरयन् ।
दिशोऽपि विद्रुताः सर्वाः
स्तनयित्नुरिवोष्णगे ॥
The roar of their chants of Vēdas, defying the (threats of) Rākshasas, makes all the Rākshasas flee in every direction like the clouds do in summer.
6.35.21
ऋषीणामग्निकल्पानाम्
अग्निहोत्रसमुत्थितः ।
आदत्ते रक्षसां तेजो
धूमो व्याप्य दिशो दश ॥
The smoke that arises from the ritual fires by the Ṛshis who are like fire themselves, envelops the ten directions dissipating the power of the Rākshasas.
6.35.22
तेषु तेषु च देशेषु
पुण्येष्वेव दृढव्रतैः ।
चर्यमाणं तपस्तीव्रम्
सन्तापयति राक्षसान् ॥
The severe Tapa performed by them of firm vows and strict discipline, in various places, is bound to scorch the Rākshasas.
6.35.23
देवदानवयक्षेभ्यो
गृहीतश्च वरस्त्वया ।
मानुषा वानरा ऋक्षा
गोलाङ्गूला महाबलाः ।
बलवन्त इहागम्य
गर्जन्ति दृढविक्रमाः ॥
You secured, as a boon, immunity from death at the hands of the Dēvas, Dānavas and Yakshas. But it is humans, Vānaras, Ṛkshas and Gōlāṅgūlas of great strength and unshakable prowess that have come here, roaring.
6.35.25
उत्पातान् विविधान् दृष्ट्वा
घोरान् बहुविधांस्तथा ।
विनाशमनुपश्यामि
सर्वेषां रक्षसामहम् ॥
On seeing the various kinds of omens and other terrible things happening, I foresee the destruction of all the Rākshasas as imminent.
6.35.26
खराभिस्तनिता घोरा
मेघाः प्रतिभयङ्कराः ।
शोणितेनाभिवर्षन्ति
लङ्कामुष्णेन सर्वतः ॥
Horrid clouds rumbling harshly and drenching Laṅkā with showers of hot blood all over the place is extremely terrifying.
6.35.27
रुदतां वाहनानां च
प्रपतन्त्यस्रबिन्दवः ।
ध्वजा ध्वस्ता विवर्णाश्च
न प्रभान्ति यथा पुरा ॥
The mounts (horses and elephants) cry, shedding tears. The flags are dusty and faded and do not shine as they used to.
6.35.28
व्याला गोमायवो गृध्रा
वाश्यन्ति च सुभैरवम् ।
प्रविश्य लङ्कामनिशम्
समवायांश्च कुर्वते ॥
Jackals, beasts of prey and vultures make extremely terrifying sounds; they constantly barge into Laṅkā and throng in large numbers.
6.35.29
कालिकाः पाण्डरैर्दन्तैः
प्रहसन्त्यग्रतः स्थिताः ।
स्त्रियः स्वप्नेषु मुष्णन्त्यो
गृहाणि प्रतिभाष्य च ॥
Women of black complexion and white teeth are seen in dreams, stealing items from households, standing in front and laughing, and talking back (with no modesty or apprehension).
6.35.30
गृहाणां बलिकर्माणि
श्वानः पर्युपभुञ्जते ।
खरा गोषु प्रजायन्ते
मूषिका नकुलेषु च ॥
The offerings made in the households to propitiate the spirits are eaten away by dogs. Donkeys are born to cows and mice to ichneumons.
6.35.31
मार्जारा द्वीपिभिः सार्धम्
सूकराः शुनकैः सह ।
किन्नरा राक्षसैश्चापि
समीयुर्मानुषैः सह ॥
Cats pair with tigers, pigs with dogs, Kinnaras with Rākshasas and humans.
6.35.32
पाण्डुरा रक्तपादाश्च
विहङ्गाः कालचोदिताः ।
राक्षसानां विनाशाय
कपोता विचरन्ति च ॥
As the unusual times would have it, white doves with red feet hover (everywhere) for the destruction of Rākshasas.
6.35.33
वीचीकूचीति वाश्यन्त्यः
शारिका वेश्मसु स्थिताः ।
पतन्ति ग्रथिताश्चापि
निर्जिताः कलहैषिणः ॥
Parrots that are household pets fall down in droves chased by bellicose birds, letting off shrilling noises.
6.35.34
पाक्षिणश्च मृगाः सर्वे
प्रत्यादित्यं रुदन्ति च ।
करालो विकटो मुण्डः
परुषः कृष्णपिङ्गलः ।
कालो गृहाणि सर्वेषाम्
काले कालेऽन्ववेक्षते ॥
All the birds and beasts turn to the sun and wail. Death, ugly, harsh and frightening, with his clean-shaved head and smoky-red complexion casts his eye at every house, time and again.
6.35.35
एतान्यन्यानि दुष्टानि
निमित्तान्युत्पतन्ति च ।
ज्ञात्वा प्रधार्य कार्याणि
क्रियतामायतिक्षमम् ॥
Keeping in mind these and other baleful portents that are seen everywhere, you should think carefully and do what is better for the future.
6.35.36
इदं वचस्तत्र निगद्य माल्यवान्
परीक्ष्य रक्षोधिपतेर्मनः पुनः ।
अनुत्तमेषूत्तमपौरुषो बली
बभूव तूष्णीं समवेक्ष्य रावणम् ॥
Having spoken thus, but again, sensing what is on the mind of the Lord of Rākshasas, the strong Mālyavān, who was the foremost among his unexcelled colleagues, stayed silent.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे पञ्चत्रिंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty fifth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
