Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 38
In this Sarga, Sugreeva, along with Lakshmaṇa goes to Rāma on the royal palanquin, surrounded by hundreds of Vānaras.
Sugreeva bows and touches the feet of Rāma with his head. The pleased Rāma embraces him and offers a seat and praises him for adhering to Dharma and for caring about friendship. He then asks Sugreeva to plan for the expedition along with his ministers.
Then Sugreeva reports to Rāma that preparations are already underway and that hundreds of Vānaras sent all over the earth had brought crores and crores of Vānaras from across the earth along with them, and that they will kill Rāvaṇa and his kith and kin in battle and bring back Seetā.
Rāma is pleased to see that the efforts are already under way.
4.38.1
प्रतिगृह्य च तत्सर्वम्
उपायनमुपाहृतम् ।
वानरान्सान्त्वयित्वा च
सर्वानेव व्यसर्जयत् ॥
After receiving all those gifts that were brought and thanking the Vānaras, he dismissed them all.
4.38.2
विसर्जयित्वा स हरीन्
शूरांस्तान्कृतकर्मणः ।
मेने कृतार्थमात्मानम्
राघवं च महाबलम् ॥
Having dismissed those valiant Vānaras who had done the work entrusted to them, he felt that the mighty Rāghava and himself were well served of their causes.
4.38.3
स लक्ष्मणो भीमबलम्
सर्ववानरसत्तमम् ।
अब्रवीत्प्रश्रितं वाक्यम्
सुग्रीवं सम्प्रहर्षयन् ।
किष्किन्धाया विनिष्क्राम
यदि ते सौम्य रोचते ॥
Then Lakshmaṇa, feeling very happy, spoke these words deferentially to Sugreeva of fierce strength, the best among all Vānaras: ‘O gentle one! If you may so please, let us (now) start from Kishkindhā!’
4.38.4
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
लक्ष्मणस्य सुभाषितम् ।
सुग्रीवः परमप्रीतो
वाक्यमेतदुवाच ह ।
एवं भवतु गच्छामः 
स्थेयं त्वच्छासने मया ॥
Feeling very happy to hear those well spoken words of Lakshmaṇa, Sugreeva said, ‘Very well, let us go, I shall abide by your command.’
4.38.6
तमेवमुक्त्वा सुग्रीवो
लक्ष्मणं शुभलक्षणम् ।
विसर्जयामास तदा
ताराद्याश्चैव योषितः ॥
Having thus answered Lakshmaṇa of the auspicious marks, Sugreeva dismissed Tāra and the other women.
4.38.7
एहीत्युच्चैर्हरिवरान्
सुग्रीवस्समुदाहरत् ।
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
हरयश्शीघ्रमाययुः ।
बद्धाञ्जलिपुटास्सर्वे
ये स्युः स्त्रीदर्शनक्षमाः ॥
Sugreeva then called aloud to the great Vānaras, ‘Come here!’ Hearing those words, all those Vānaras who were permitted to see the women (of the king), entered, with their palms joined in reverence.
4.38.8
तानुवाच ततः प्राप्तान्
राजाऽर्कसदृशप्रभः ।
उपस्थापयत क्षिप्रम्
शिबिकां मम वानराः ॥
And that king, dazzling like the sun told those who came there, ‘Get my palanquin ready, quick ’.
4.38.9
श्रुत्वा तु वचनं तस्य
हरयश्शीघ्रविक्रमाः ।
समुपस्थापयामासुः
शिबिकां प्रियदर्शनाम् ॥
No sooner did those fast-faced Vānaras hear those words than they made a palanquin ready there, that looked beautiful.
4.38.10
तामुपस्थापितां दृष्ट्वा
शिबिकां वानराधिपः ।
लक्ष्मणाऽरुह्यतां शीघ्रम्
इति सौमित्रिमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing that palanquin which was thus made ready, the king of Vānaras said to Sowmitri: ‘Get in quick, Lakshmaṇa!’
4.38.11
इत्युक्त्वा काञ्चनं यानम्
सुग्रीवस्सूर्यसन्निभम् ।
बहुभिर्हरिभिर्युक्तम्
आरुरोह सलक्ष्मणः ॥
Saying thus, Sugreeva, along with Lakshmaṇa got into that golden vehicle which shone like the sun and was carried by multiples of Vānaras.
4.38.12
पाण्डुरेणातपत्रेण
ध्रियमाणेन मूर्धनि ।
शुक्लैश्च वालव्यजनैः
धूयमानैस्समन्ततः ।
शङ्खभेरीनिनादैश्च
वन्दिभिश्चाभिनन्दितः ।
निर्ययौ प्राप्य सुग्रीवो
राज्यश्रियमनुत्तमाम् ॥
With the (royal) white umbrella raised over his head, and the fly-whisks playing about him, and hearing his praises sung by the Vānaras, combined with the blaring of conches and blasting of kettledrums, Sugreeva went forth, having won a kingdom of unparalleled opulence.
4.38.14
स वानरशतैस्तीक्ष्णैः
बहुभिश्शस्त्रपाणिभिः ।
परिकीर्णो ययौ तत्र
यत्र रामो व्यवस्थितः ॥
With many hundreds of fierce Vānaras armed with weapons following him, he went to the place where Rāma dwelt.
4.38.15
स तं देशमनुप्राप्य
श्रेष्ठं रामनिषेवितम् ।
अवातरन्महातेजाः
शिबिकायास्सलक्ष्मणः ॥
On reaching that great place where Rāma dwelled, he of immense strength got off the palanquin along with Lakshmaṇa.
4.38.16
आसाद्य च ततो रामम्
कृताञ्जलिपुटोऽभवत् ।
कृताञ्जलौ स्थिते तस्मिन्
वानराश्चाभवंस्तथा ॥
Upon approaching Rāma, he joined his palms in reverence. And as he joined his palms and stood there, so did the other Vānaras.
4.38.17
तटाकमिव तं दृष्ट्वा
रामः कुड्मलपङ्कजम् ।
वानराणां महत्सैन्यम्
सुग्रीवे प्रीतिमानभूत् ॥
On seeing that great army of Vānaras that looked like a great lake full of lotus buds, Rāma was greatly pleased with Sugreeva.
4.38.18
पादयोः पतितं मूर्ध्ना
तमुत्थाप्य हरीश्वरम् ।
प्रेम्णा च बहुमानाच्च
राघवः परिषस्वजे ॥
Rāghava raised the Lord of Vānaras as he bowed down and touched with his head, the feet of Rāma. Then he embraced him with affection and utmost regard.
4.38.19
परिष्वज्य च धर्मात्मा
निषीदेति ततोऽब्रवीत् ।
निषण्णं तं ततो दृष्ट्वा
क्षितौ रामोऽब्रवीत्ततः ॥
The Dharmātma who embraced him then said, ‘Please be seated’. After he sat down on the floor, Rāma said:
4.38.20
धर्ममर्थं च कामं च
यस्तु काले निषेवते ।
विभज्य सततं वीरः
स राजा हरिसत्तम ॥
O best of Vānaras! He who constantly balances his time to attend, as needed, to Dharma, Artha and Kāma, is a true king.
4.38.21
हित्वा धर्मं तथाऽर्थं च
कामं यस्तु निषेवते ।
स वृक्षाग्रे यथा सुप्तः
पतितः प्रतिबुध्यते ॥
He who neglects Dharma and Artha and pursues Kāma alone, is like one who dozes off on the top of a tree, only to realize after falling down.
4.38.22
अमित्राणां वधे युक्तो
मित्राणां सङ्ग्रहे रतः ।
त्रिवर्गफलभोक्ता च
राजा धर्मेण युज्यते ॥
Only the king who is constantly engaged in killing the enemies and gaining friends can adhere to Dharma and enjoy all the three (Dharma, Artha and Kāma).
4.38.23
उद्योगसमयस्त्वेष
प्राप्तश्शत्रु निषूदन  ।
सञ्चिन्त्यतां हि पिङ्गेश
हरिभिस्सह मन्त्रिभिः ॥
O destroyer of enemies! The time for expeditions has come. May you, O Lord of Vānaras, make plans in consultation with the Vānara advisors!
4.38.24
एवमुक्तस्तु सुग्रीवो
रामं वचनमब्रवीत् ।
प्रणष्टा श्रीश्च कीर्तिश्च
कपिराज्यं च शाश्वतम् ।
त्वत्प्रसादान्महाबाहो
पुनःप्राप्तमिदं मया ॥
Having been urged thus, Sugreeva said these words to Rāma: ‘It is by your grace that the lost riches, stature and the eternal kingdom of Vānaras were regained by me.’
4.38.26
तव देव प्रसादाच्च
भ्रातुश्च जयतां वर ।
कृतं न प्रतिकुर्याद्यः
पुरुषाणां स दूषकः ॥
O lord! O foremost among victorious! One who does not repay for the kind favor done by you and your brother should be the most abominable among men!
4.38.27
एते वानरमूख्याश्च
शतशश्शत्रुसूदन ।
प्राप्ताश्चादाय बलिनः
पृथिव्यां सर्ववानरान् ॥
Here are hundreds of prominent Vānaras of great strength who have come back bringing with them every Vānara to be found anywhere on earth, O slayer of the foe.
4.38.28
ऋक्षाश्चावानराश्शूरा
गोलाङ्गूलाश्च राघव ।
कान्तारवनदुर्गाणाम्
अभिज्ञा घोरदर्शनाः ।
देवगन्धर्वपुत्राश्च
वानराः कामरूपिणः ।
स्वैस्स्वैः परिवृतास्सैन्यैः
वर्तन्ते पथि राघव ॥
Bears, Vānaras and Gōlāṅgūlas of great valor and frightful appearance who are familiar with forests and tangled wilderness, and Vānaras who are the sons of Dēvas and Gandharvas that can take any form at will are on their way, O Rāghava, followed by their troops.
4.38.30
शतैश्शतसहस्रैश्च
कोटिभिश्च प्लवङ्गमाः ।
आयुतैश्चावृता वीर
शङकुभिश्च परंतप ।
अर्बुदैरर्बुदशतैः
मध्यैश्चान्त्यैश्च वानराः ।
समुद्रैश्च परार्धैश्च
हरयो हरियूथपाः ।
आगमिष्यन्ति ते राजन्
महेन्द्रसमविक्रमाः ।
मेघपर्वतसङ्काशा
मेरुविन्ध्य कृतालयाः ॥
O King! O Veera! O Scorcher of the foe! Vānaras and their captains, who are comparable to Mahēndra in their valor, that are like clouds and mountains, who made the Mēru and Vindhya mountains their abode are on their way in their hundreds, hundreds of thousands, crores, Āyutas, Ṡankus, Arbudas, hundreds of Arbudas, Madhyas, Antyas, Samudras and Parārdhas.
Āyuta = 10,000
Laksha = 1,00,000
Crore = 1,00,00,000
One Ṡanku = One Laksha Crores
One Arbuda = One Thousand Ṡankus
One Madhya = Ten Arbuda
One Antya = Ten Madhya
One Samudra = Twenty Antya
One Parārdha = Thirty Samudras
4.38.33
ते त्वामभिगमिष्यन्ति
राक्षसं ये सबान्धवम् ।
निहत्य रावणं युद्धे 
ह्यानयिष्यन्ति मैथिलीम् ॥
The Vānaras who can kill Rāvaṇa the Rākshasa along with his kith and kin in battle and bring back Maithili are coming to join you.
4.38.34
ततस्तमुद्योगमवेक्ष्य वीर्यवान्
हरिप्रवीरस्य निदेशवर्तिनः ।
बभूव हर्षाद्वसुधाधिपात्मजः
प्रबुद्धनीलोत्पलतुल्यदर्शनः ॥
On seeing that concerted effort by the great Veera among Vānaras who was clearly amenable to his will, the prince of great valor felt very happy, his eyes looking like black lilies in full blossom.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे अष्टत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty eighth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
