Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 34
In this Sarga, upon seeing Lakshmaṇa, the scared Sugreeva springs up from his seat and his womenfolk do the same. Lakshmaṇa expresses his rage to Sugreeva. He accuses him of dalliance, ungratefulness and false promises. He threatens to kill him if he does not fulfill his promise.
4.34.1
तमप्रतिहतं क्रुद्धम्
प्रविष्टं पुरुषर्षभम् ।
सुग्रीवो लक्ष्मणं दृष्ट्वा
बभूव व्यथितेन्द्रियः ॥
Seeing that enraged bull among men come in with none daring to obstruct him, Sugreeva’s senses fell in a turmoil.
4.34.2
क्रुद्धं निश्श्वसमानं तम्
प्रदीप्तमिव तेजसा ।
भ्रातुर्व्यसनसन्तप्तम्
दृष्ट्वा दशरथात्मजम् ।
उत्पपात हरिश्रेष्ठो
हित्वा सौवर्णमासनम् ।
महान्महेन्द्रस्य यथा
स्वलङ्कृत इव ध्वजः ॥
On seeing Daṡaratha’s son breathe heavily in wrath, blazing like a fire with his power and burning with grief at his brother’s suffering, that chief of Vānaras sprang from his golden seat like the great and fully-decorated flagstaff of Mahēndra.
4.34.4
उत्पतन्तमनूत्पेतू
रुमाप्रभृतयः स्त्रियः ।
सुग्रीवं गगने पूर्णं
चन्द्रं तारागणा इव ॥
Ruma and the other women also got up in step with Sugreeva as stars follow the full moon in the sky.
4.34.5
संरक्तनयनः श्रीमान्
संचचार  कृताञ्जलिः ।
बभूवावस्थितस्तत्र
कल्पवृक्षो महानिव ॥
The blessed Sugreeva, his eyes bloodshot moved closer and stood there with his palms joined in supplication, like the great Kalpa Vṛksha.
Kalpa Vṛksha is the celestial wish-granting tree.
4.34.6
रुमाद्वितीयं सुग्रीवम्
नारीमध्यगतं स्थितम् ।
अब्रवील्लक्ष्मणः क्रुद्धः
सतारं शशिनं यथा ॥
And the infuriated Lakshmaṇa addressed Sugreeva, who was surrounded by Ruma and other women like the moon by the stars:
4.34.7
सत्त्वाभिजनसम्पन्नः
सानुक्रोशो जितेन्द्रियः ।
कृतज्ञस्सत्यवादी च
राजा लोके महीयते ॥
Only a king who is powerful, grateful, and compassionate, with mastery over his senses, with a rich retinue of loving people, is held in high regard in this world.
4.34.8
यस्तु राजा स्थितोऽधर्मे
मित्राणामुपकारिणाम् ।
मिथ्या प्रतिज्ञां कुरुते
को नृशंसतरस्ततः ॥
Who can be more terrible than a king who is divorced to Dharma and who makes false promises to friends who have helped him?
4.34.9
शतमश्वानृते हन्ति
सहस्रं तु गवानृते ।
आत्मानं स्वजनं हन्ति
पुरुषः पुरुषानृते ॥
Telling a lie in the matter of a horse is as sinful as killing a hundred horses. Telling a lie in the matter of a cow is as sinful as killing a thousand cows. Telling a lie in the matter of a man is as sinful as killing all of one’s kin including oneself.
4.34.10
पूर्वं कृतार्थो मित्राणाम्
न तत्प्रतिकरोति यः ।
कृतघ्नस्सर्वभूतानाम्
स वध्यः प्लवगेश्वर ॥
The ingrate who does not return the favor after having had his object fulfilled, deserves death at the hands of every creature, O Lord of the Vānaras!
4.34.11
गीतोऽयं ब्रह्मणा श्लोकः
सर्वलोकनमस्कृतः ।
दृष्ट्वा कृतघ्नं क्रुद्धेन
तन्निबोध प्लवङ्गम ॥
O Vānara, You shall know this Ṡlōka that was uttered by Brahma who is saluted by all the worlds, when he saw a an ungrateful person:
4.34.12
गोघ्नेचैव सुरापे च
चोरे भग्नव्रते तथा ।
निष्कृतिर्विहिता सद्भिः
कृतघ्ने नास्ति निष्कृतिः ॥
There is expiation even for a thief, a drunkard or one who breaks his vow or kills a cow, but none for the person who is ungrateful.
4.34.13
अनार्यस्त्वं कृतघ्नश्च
मिथ्यावादी च वानर ।
पूर्वं कृतार्थो रामस्य
न तत्प्रतिकरोषि यत् ॥
You are ignoble, ungrateful and a liar, O Vānara, for, you are not repaying Rāma, because of whom your object was accomplished.
4.34.14
ननु नाम कृतार्थेन
त्वया रामस्य वानर ।
सीताया मार्गणे यत्नः
कर्तव्यः कृतमिच्छता ॥
Should you not now, having accomplished your object because of Rāma, busy yourself with the search for Seetā?
4.34.15
स त्वं ग्राम्येषु भोगेषु
सक्तो मिथ्याप्रतिश्रवः ।
न त्वां रामो विजानीते
सर्पं मण्डूकराविणम् ॥
You of false promises, are indulged in sporting in the company of women. Rāma did not know that you are a serpent imitating the croak of a frog.
4.34.16
महाभागेन रामेण
पापः करुणवेदिना ।
हरीणां प्रापितो राज्यम्
त्वं दुरात्मा महात्मना ॥
You, an evil sinner, attained the kingdom of Vānaras, because of the kindness of the blessed Mahātma Rāma.
4.34.17
कृतं चेन्नाभिजानीषे
राघवस्य महात्मनः ।
सद्यस्त्वं निशितैर्भावाः
हतो द्रक्ष्यसि वालिनम् ॥
If you cannot appreciate what Mahātma Rāma has done for you, you shall, killed by sharp arrows, see Vāli.
4.34.18
न च सङ्कुचितः पन्था
येन वाली हतो गतः ।
समये तिष्ठ सुग्रीव
मा वालिपथमन्वगाः ॥
The road that the slain Vāli took, is not yet closed; stick to your compact Sugreeva, do not follow the path of Vāli!
4.34.19
न नूनमिक्ष्वाकुवरस्य कार्मुका
शरांश्च तान् वज्र सन्निभान् ।
ततस्सुखं नाम निषेवसे सुखी
न रामकार्यं मनसाऽप्यवेक्षसे ॥
Evidently you have not known the arrows that fly from the bow of the foremost of the Ikshwākus that strike like a thunderbolt, or else you would not be relaxed like this, absorbed in pleasures, your mind completely turned away from Rāma’s work.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे चतुस्त्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty fourth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
