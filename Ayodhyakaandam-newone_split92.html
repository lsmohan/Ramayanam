Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 92
In this Sarga, Bharata thanks Bharadwāja for the hospitality and asks him regarding the whereabouts of Rāma. Bharadwāja tells him that Rāma would be living on Citrakūṭa mountain, which is three and half Yōjanas to the south from there.
As Bharata and entourage are set to leave, his mothers approach Bharadwāja and pay obeisance to him. Bharadwāja asks Bharata to introduce his mothers to him. Bharata introduces Kousalyā and Sumitrā. And then, pointing to Kaikēyee, he says that she is his mother, and is responsible for the exile of Rāma, death of Daṡaratha and the distress that he experiences.
Bharadwāja tells him not to find fault with her. He says that the exile of Rāma would result in the betterment of Dēvas, Dānavas and Ṛshis in future.
Bharata, his retinue, the women and the army move on in the southerly direction, crossing mountains and rivers.
2.92.1
ततस्तां रजनीमुष्य
भरतस्सपरिच्छदः ।
कृतातिथ्यो भरद्वाजं
कामादभिजगाम ह ॥
After receiving hospitality and spending the night there, Bharata, accompanied by his retinue, approached Bharadwāja, to find out (the whereabouts of Rāma).
2.92.2
तं ऋषिः पुरुषव्याघ्रं
प्राञ्जलिं प्रेक्ष्य चाऽगतम् ।
हुताग्निहोत्रो भरतं
भरद्वाजोऽभ्यभाषत ॥
To that Bharata, the tiger among men, who came and stood by him with joined palms, Bharadwāja, the Ṛshi, who had just completed the fire rituals, said:
2.92.3
कच्चिदत्र सुखा रात्रिः
तवास्मद्विषये गता ।
समग्रस्ते जनः कच्चित्
आतिथ्ये शंस मेऽनघ ॥
Did you pass the night comfortably in our place? Are your people completely pleased with the hospitality? Tell me, O blameless one!
2.92.4
तमुवाचाञ्जलिं कृत्वा
भरतोऽभिप्रणम्य च ।
आश्रमादभिनिष्क्रान्तम्
ऋषिमुत्तमतेजसम् ॥
Bharata, paying obeisance to that Ṛshi of supreme radiance as he came out of the Āṡrama, answered with palms joined:
2.92.5
सुखोषितोऽस्मि भगवन्
समग्रबलवाहनः ।
तर्पितस्सर्वकामैश्च
सामात्यो बलवत्त्वया ॥
I, along with my ministers, army and the mounts had a comfortable stay and are satisfied having everything that could be wished for, lavishly pressed upon us by you.
2.92.6
अपेतक्लमसन्तापाः
सुभिक्षास्सुप्रतिश्रयाः ।
अपि प्रेष्यानुपादाय
सर्वे स्म सुसुखोषिताः ॥
All of us, including the servants, have recovered from the fatigue and stress, having been fed well, housed well and rested well.
2.92.7
आमन्त्रयेऽहं भगवन्
कामं त्वामृषिसत्तमः ।
समीपं प्रस्थितं भ्रातुः
मैत्रेणेक्षस्व चक्षुषा ॥
I beg to take leave of you, Bhagawan! Please cast your kindly eyes upon me, o eminent Ṛshi, as I set out eagerly to meet my brother.
2.92.8
आश्रमं तस्य धर्मज्ञ
धार्मिकस्य महात्मनः ।
आचक्ष्व कतमो मार्गः
कियानिति च शंस मे ॥
Please tell me, O Dharmajña, where that Dhārmika, the Mahātma, lives! Tell me how to go there and how far it is.
2.92.9
इति पृष्टस्तु भरतं
भ्रातृदर्शनलालसम् ।
प्रत्युवाच महातेजा
भरद्वाजो महातपाः ॥
Asked thus by Bharata, in his eagerness to see his brother, the supremely radiant Bharadwāja, of great Tapa, responded:
2.92.10
भरतार्धतृतीयेषु
योजनेष्वजने वने ।
चित्रकूटो गिरिस्तत्र
रम्यनिर्झरकाननः ॥
Three and a half Yōjanas from here, in the uninhabited Vana, O Bharata, lies Citrakūṭa mountain, of beautiful streams and woods.
2.92.11
उत्तरं पार्श्वमासाद्य
तस्य मन्दाकिनी नदी ।
पुष्पितद्रुमसञ्छन्ना
रम्यपुष्पितकानना ॥
To the northern side of it runs river Mandākini that is covered by flowering trees and edged with delightful woods in bloom.
2.92.12
अनन्तरं तत्सरितः
चित्रकूटश्च पर्वतः ।
तयोः पर्णकुटी तात
तत्र तौ वसतो ध्रुवम् ॥
Citrakūṭa mountain lies next to it. My dear child, they must be living, I am sure, in a hut covered by leaves, right there.
2.92.13
दक्षिणेनैव मार्गेण
सव्यदक्षिणमेव वा ।
गजवाजिरथाकीर्णां
वाहिनीं वाहिनीपते ।
वाहयस्व महाभाग
ततो द्रक्ष्यसि राघवम् ॥
Make your army, O leader of armies, which teems with elephants, horses and chariots, march in the southern, rather, south-easterly direction. O supremely blessed one, you will see Rāghava there.
2.92.14
प्रयाणमिति तच्छ्रुत्वा
राजराजस्य योषितः ।
हित्वा यानानि यानार्हाः
ब्राह्मणं पर्यवारयन् ॥
Hearing that they were to start, instead of getting on to their carriages, the consorts of (Daṡaratha) the King of Kings, gathered around that savant of the Vedas, leaving their carriages.
2.92.15
वेपमाना कृशा दीना
सह देव्या सुमित्रया ।
कौसल्या तत्र जग्राह
कराभ्यां चरणौ मुनेः ॥
Kousalyā, trembling, thin and pitiful, along with Sumitrā, clasped the feet of the Muni with her hands.
2.92.16
असमृद्धेन कामेन
सर्वलोकस्य गर्हिता ।
कैकेयी तस्य जग्राह
चरणौ सव्यपत्रपा ॥
Then, Kaikēyee, reproached by the entire world, her cherished wish unfulfilled, clasped his feet, overcome with shame.
2.92.17
तं प्रदक्षिणमागम्य
भगवन्तं महामुनिम् ।
अदूरार्भरतस्यैव
तस्थौ दीनमनास्तदा ॥
Circumambulating that Bhagawān, the great Muni, she stood by Bharata, feeling downcast.
2.92.18
ततः पप्रच्छ भरतं
भरद्वाजो दृढव्रतः ।
विशेषं ज्ञातुमिच्छामि
मात्रूणां तव राघव ॥
Bharadwāja, of steadfast vows, asked Bharata: 'I would like to know about your mothers, O Rāghava!'
2.92.19
एवमुक्तस्तु भरतो
भरद्वाजेन धीमता ।
उवाच प्राञ्जलिर्भूत्वा
वाक्यं वचनकोविदः ॥
Asked thus by the sagacious Bharadwāja, Bharata, who knew the right words, said these words, with palms joined in reverence:
2.92.20
यामिमां भगवन् दीनां
शोकानशनकर्शिताम् ।
पितुर्हि महिषीं देवीं
देवतामिव पश्यसि ।
एषा तं पुरषव्याघ्रं
सिंहविक्रान्तगामिनम् ।
कौसल्या सुषुवे रामं
धातारमदितिर्यथा ॥
O Bhagawan, this despondent lady, who was worn out by grief and fasting, who looks like a Dēvata, is Kousalyā, the chief queen of my father. She gave birth to Rāma, the tiger among men, of lion's stride, as Aditi bore Dhāta.
2.92.22
अस्या वामभुजं श्लिष्टा
यैषा तिष्ठति दुर्मनाः ।
कर्णिकारस्य शाखेव
शीर्णपुष्पा वनान्तरे ।
एतस्यास्तु सुतौ देव्याः
कुमारौ देववर्णिनौ ।
उभौ लक्ष्मणशत्रुघ्नौ
वीरौ सत्यपराक्रमौ ॥
The one who is highly distressed and standing leaning on her left shoulder, wilting like the branch with shriveled flowers of the Karṇikāra tree in the Vana, is the lady whose sons are the duo Lakshmaṇa and Ṡatrughna, Veeras of true valor, comparable to Dēvas.
2.92.24
यस्याः कृते नरव्याघ्रौ
जीवनाशमितो गतौ ।
राजपुत्रविहीनश्च
स्वर्गं दशरथो गतः ।
क्रोधनामकृतप्रज्ञां
दृप्तां सुभगमानिनीम् ।
ऐश्वर्यकामां कैकेयीम्
अनार्यामार्यरूपिणीम् ।
ममैतां मातरं विद्धि
नृशंसां पापनिश्चयाम् ।
यतोमूलं हि पश्यामि
व्यसनं महादात्मनः ॥
And know that this cruel, ill-tempered, arrogant and immature woman is my mother Kaikēyee, of evil resolve, whose noble bearing belies her ignoble nature, who craves for riches and thinks herself to be the most beautiful, because of whom the tigers among men left with their lives destroyed, the king Daṡaratha went to Heaven losing his sons, and I am caught in this great distress.
2.92.27
इत्युक्त्वा नरशार्दूलो
बाष्पगद्गदया गिरा ।
स निशस्वास ताम्राक्षो
नागः कृद्ध इव श्वसन् ॥
Having said this in a voice choked with tears, that tiger among men, his eyes red, heaved a great sigh, hissing like an enraged serpent.
2.92.28
भरद्वाजो महर्षिस्तं
ब्रुवन्तं भरतं तथा ।
प्रत्युवाच महाबुद्धिः
इदं वचनमर्थवत् ॥
When Bharata spoke thus, Bharadwāja, the supremely wise Maharshi uttered these words of significance:
2.92.29
न दोषेणावगन्तव्या
कैकेयी भरत त्वया ।
रामप्रव्राजनं ह्येतत्
सुखोदर्कं भविष्यति ॥
You should not, O Bharata, find fault with Kaikēyee; for this exiling of Rāma will result in a happy future.
2.92.30
देवानां दानवानां च
ऋषीणां भावितात्मनाम् ।
हितमेव भविष्यद्धि
रामप्रव्राजनादिह ॥
This exiling of Rāma will only do good to Dēvas, Dānavas, and Ṛshis who have realized their true Self.
2.92.31
अभिवाद्य तु संसिद्धः
कृत्वा चैनं प्रदक्षिणम् ।
आमन्त्र्य भरतस्सैन्यं
युज्यतामित्यचोदयत् ॥
Bharata took leave of him, after paying obeisance and circumambulating him. Becoming ready, he prompted the army also to get ready for the journey.
2.92.32
ततो वाजिरथान्युक्तान्
दिव्यान्हेमपरिष्कृतान् ।
अध्यारोहत्प्रयाणार्थी
बहून्बहुविधो जनः ॥
Then all the different people got on the splendid gold-treated chariots yoked with horses.
2.92.33
गजकन्या गजाश्चैव
हेमकक्ष्याः पताकिनः ।
जीमूता इव घर्मान्ते
सघोषास्सम्प्रतस्थिरे ॥
Cow and bull elephants, with golden girths, carrying banners, moved rumbling like clouds in the monsoon season.
2.92.34
विविधान्यपि यानानि
महन्ति च लघूनि च ।
प्रययुस्सुमहार्हाणि
पादै रेव पदातयः ॥
A variety of luxurious carriages, big and small, set out, with foot soldiers keeping pace with them.
2.92.35
अथ यानप्रवेकैस्तु
कौसल्याप्रमुखाः स्त्रियः ।
रामदर्शनकाङ्क्षिण्यः
प्रययुर्मुदितास्तदा ॥
Then, in exquisite carriages, started Kousalyā and other ladies, happy and eager to see Rāma.
2.92.36
चन्द्रार्कतरुणाभासां
निर्युक्तां शिबिकां शुभाम् ।
आस्थाय प्रययौ श्रीमान्
भरतस्सपरिच्छदः ॥
Getting on a splendid palanquin that was bright as the rising moon and the morning sun, which had been made ready for him, Bharata, of rich bearing, started along with his retinue.
2.92.37
सा प्रयाता महासेना
गजवाजिरथाकुला ।
दक्षिणां दिशमावृत्य
महामेघ इवोत्थितः ।
वनानि तु व्यतिक्रम्य
जुष्टानि मृगपक्षिभिः ।
गङ्गायाः परवेलायां
गिरिष्वपि नदीषु च ॥
That great army with its herds of elephants, horses and chariots, seeming to obscure the entire south like a rising cloud, traversed the woods full of birds and beasts, and journeyed crossing the mountains and rivers on the other side of Gaṅgā.
2.92.39
सा सम्प्रहृष्टद्विपवाजियोधा
वित्रासयन्ती मृगपक्षिसङ्घान् ।
महद्वनं तत्प्रतिगाहमाना
रराज सेना भरतस्य तत्र ॥
That army of Bharata, with its elephants, horses and foot-soldiers in high spirits, shone in all its splendor, as it entered the great Vana, frightening the flocks of birds and beasts.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे द्विनवतितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninety second Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
