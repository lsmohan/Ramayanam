Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 26
In this Sarga, Seetā broods over her predicament. She wonders whether she has done terrible things in her past lives, or whether her Puṇya is completely depleted, for her to be in such a predicament.
Even then, she defiantly tells the Rākshasis that they can eat her if they so wish, but she would not yield to their badgering. She warns them that the bad days for Laṅkā are not too far away, when Rāma’s arrows would burn the city down, turning it into a cremation ground. She tells them that, in not a distant future, she hopes to see all the Rākshasas dead and Rākshasis in every house wailing just as she does now.
Yet, she wonders why Rāma has not come so far to her rescue and conjures up various reasons. She wonders whether Rāma knows where she is. She fears that Rāma might have been killed by Rāvaṇa by ruse. She wonders whether Rāma has taken to the life of a Vana-dweller giving up arms. She feels it is better for her to give up her life, having fallen into the hands of the wicked Rāvaṇa.
5.26.1
प्रसक्ताश्रुमुखीत्वेवम्
ब्रुवन्ती जनकात्मजा ।
अधोगतमुखी बाला
विलप्तुमुपचक्रमे ॥
Thinking to herself thus and with her face drenched in tears and hanging down, that young dame began to cry.
5.26.2
उन्मत्तेव प्रमत्तेव
भ्रान्तचित्तेव शोचती ।
उपावृत्ता किशोरीव
विवेष्टन्ती महीतले ॥
She wailed like one who was possessed, had lost her mind and was given to delusions. She rolled on the ground like a filly that was let to relieve itself of its fatigue.
5.26.3
राघवस्य प्रमत्तस्य
रक्षसा कामरूपिणा ।
रावणेन प्रमथ्याहम्
आनीता क्रोशती बलात् ॥
I have been forced by Rāvaṇa, the Rākshasa, who can take any form at will, and have been brought here forcibly as I cried, while Rāghava was distracted.
5.26.4
राक्षसीवशमापन्ना
भर्त्स्यमाना सुदारुणम् ।
चिन्तयन्ती सुदुःखार्ता
नाहं जीवितुमुत्सहे ॥
Fallen into the hands of these Rākshasis, harassed extremely terribly and crying in extreme misery, I bear no penchant for life.
5.26.5
न हि मे जीवितेनार्थो
नैवार्थेर्न च भूषणैः ।
वसन्त्या राक्षसीमध्ये
विना रामं महारथम् ॥
Living in the midst of these Rākshasis, and away from Rāma, the great charioter, neither this life, nor riches, nor ornaments have any meaning.
5.26.6
अश्मसारमिदं नूनम्
अथवाप्यजरामरम् ।
हृदयं मम येनेदम्
न दुःखेनावशीर्यते ॥
Surely this heart of mine is made of iron, or it must be immune from aging and death, to remain unshattered by such grief.
5.26.7
धिङ्मामनार्यामसतीम्
याऽहं तेना विना कृता ।
मुहूर्तमपि रक्षामि
जीवितं पापजीविता ॥
Dhik! I must be ignoble! I am sinful! I am a vile creature! (Or else, how is it that) I am able to cling to this wretched life even for an hour, after being separated from him!
5.26.8
का च मे जीविते श्रद्धा
सुखे वा तं प्रियं विना ।
भर्तारं सागरान्ताया
वसुधायाः प्रियंवदम् ॥
What do I have to do with this life or comforts without him, the lord of the ocean-girdled earth, who always speaks pleasantly!
5.26.9
भिद्यतां भक्ष्यतां वापि
शरीरं विसृजाम्यहम् ।
न चाप्यहं चिरं दुःखम्
सहेयं प्रियवर्जिता ॥
I shall cast away this body! Let it be torn into pieces and be eaten! I cannot, deprived of my beloved, bear this grief any longer!
5.26.10
चरणेनापि सव्येन
न स्पृशेयं निशाचरम् ।
रावणं किं पुनरहम्
कामयेयं विगर्हितम् ॥
I would not touch Rāvaṇa, the nocturnal, even with my left foot, what then, to speak of loving the despicable wretch!
5.26.11
प्रत्याख्यातं न जानाति
नात्मानं नात्मनः कुलम् ।
यो नृशंसस्वभावेन
मां प्रार्थयितुमिच्छति ॥
He is being hard-hearted in his wooing of me; neither does he realize that I reject him, nor is he worried about (the destruction of) his clan.
5.26.12
छिन्ना भिन्ना विभक्ता वा
दीप्तेवाग्नौ प्रदीपिता ।
रावणं नोपतिष्ठेयम्
किं प्रलापेन वश्चिरम् ॥
You may cut me, pound me, tear me to pieces, or burn me in glowing fire, but I will never give myself up to Rāvaṇa. What is the use of your prolonged blustering?
5.26.13
ख्यातः प्राज्ञः कृतज्ञश्च
सानुक्रोशश्च राघवः ।
सद्वृत्तो निरनुक्रोशः
शङ्के मद्भाग्यसङ्क्षयात् ॥
Rāghava is known for his sagacity, deep sense of gratitude, kindness to all and for his blameless life; I guess it must be that my good days are behind me that he shows no pity for me.
5.26.14
राक्षसानां सहस्राणि
जनस्थाने चतुर्दश ।
येनैकेन निरस्तानि
स मां किं नाभिपद्यते ॥
Otherwise, why does he, who destroyed fourteen thousand Rākshasas in Janasthāna singlehandedly, not come to my rescue?
5.26.15
निरुद्धा रावणेनाहम्
अल्पवीर्येण रक्षसा ।
समर्थः खलु मे भर्ता
रावणं हन्तुमाहवे ॥
I am trapped by Rāvaṇa, a Rākshasa of trifling valor; my husband is very much capable of killing this Rāvaṇa in a fight.
5.26.16
विराधो दण्डकारण्ये
येन राक्षसपुङ्गवः ।
रणे रामेण निहतः
स मां किं नाभिपद्यते ॥
Why does Rāma, who killed Virādha, a bull among Rākshasas, in fight in Daṇḍaka Araṇya, not come to my rescue?
5.26.17
कामं मध्ये समुद्रस्य
लङ्केयं दुष्प्रधर्षणा ।
न तु राघवबाणानाम्
गतिरोधो भविष्यति ॥
This Laṅkā, situated in the middle-of an ocean, may be invincible; but there is nothing that can stop the onslaught of Rāghava’s arrows!
5.26.18
किन्नु तत्कारणं येन
रामो दृढपराक्रमः ।
रक्षसापहृतां भार्याम्
इष्टां नाभ्यवपद्यते ॥
What then could be the reason for Rāma, of well-established prowess, not rescuing his beloved wife who has been abducted by a Rākshasa?
5.26.19
इहस्थां मां न जानीते
शङ्के लक्ष्मणपूर्वजः ।
जानन्नपि हि तेजस्वी
धर्षणं मर्षयिष्यति ॥
I guess the elder brother of Lakshmaṇa does not know that I am here; if he did, would that man of power put up with this insult (even for a moment)?
5.26.20
हृतेति योऽधिगत्वा माम्
राघवाय निवेदयेत् ।
गृध्रराजोऽपि स रणे
रावणेन निपातितः ॥
Even the king of eagles, who could have met and told Rāghava that I had been carried off, was killed by Rāvaṇa in the fight.
5.26.21
कृतं कर्म महत्तेन
मां तथाऽभ्यवपद्यता ।
तिष्ठता रावणद्वन्द्वे
वृद्धेनापि जटायुषा ॥
Jaṭāyu, indeed did a great deed, in coming to my rescue and standing against Rāvaṇa in the duel, aged as he was.
5.26.22
यदि मामिह जानीयात्
वर्तमानां स राघवः ।
अद्य बाणैरभिक्रुद्धः
कुर्याल्लोकमराक्षसम् ॥
If only Rāghava knows that I am here, he would rid this world of all Rākshasas with his arrows this very day, in his rage.
5.26.23
विधमेच्च पुरीं लङ्काम्
शोषयेच्च महोदधिम् ।
रावणस्य च नीचस्य
कीर्तिं नाम च नाशयेत् ॥
He would annihilate the city of Laṅkā, dry up the ocean and shred the name and fame of the base Rāvaṇa.
5.26.24
ततो निहतनाथानाम्
राक्षसीनां गृहे गृहे ।
यथाहमेवं रुदती
तथा भूयो न संशयः ॥
With their husbands dead, Rākshasis in every house would cry endlessly, just as I do now, without a doubt.
5.26.25
अन्विष्याराक्षसां लङ्काम्
कुर्याद्रामस्सलक्ष्मणः ।
न हि ताभ्यां रिपुर्दृष्टो
मुहूर्तमपि जीवति ॥
Rāma, along with Lakshmaṇa, will comb Laṅkā and free it of Rākshasas. No foe who falls in their line of sight can survive even for a Muhūrta.
5.26.26
चिताधूमाकुलपथा
गृध्रमण्डलसङ्कुला ।
अचिरेण तु लङ्केयम्
श्मशानसदृशी भवेत् ॥
Before it is too long, this Laṅkā will turn into cremation grounds covered in smoke from funeral pyres and crowded with flocks of vultures.
5.26.27
अचिरेणैव कालेन
प्राप्स्याम्येव मनोरथम् ।
दुष्प्रस्थानोऽयामाख्याति
सर्वेषां वो विपर्ययम् ॥
My wish will come true before it is too long. The evil path you trek presages dire consequences for all of you.
5.26.28
यादृशानीह दृश्यन्ते
लङ्कायामशुभानि वै ।
अचिरेणैव कालेन
भविष्यति हतप्रभा ॥
The kind of bad omens seen in Laṅkā evidently portend that its splendor will be undone before it is too long.
5.26.29
नूनं लङ्का हते पापे
रावणे राक्षसाधमे ।
शोषं यास्यति दुर्धर्षा
प्रमदा विधवा यथा ॥
Surely, with Rāvaṇa, the vile and sinful Rākshasa, killed, the invincible Laṅkā will dry up like a widowed woman.
5.26.30
पुण्योत्सवसमुत्था च
नष्टभर्त्री सराक्षसी ।
भविष्यति पुरी लङ्का
नष्टभर्त्री यथाङ्गना ॥
Like a woman who has lost her husband, the city of Laṅkā, with its lord lost, and only Rākshasis remaining, will be devoid of all festivities.
5.26.31
नूनं राक्षसकन्यानाम्
रुदन्तीनां गृहे गृहे ।
श्रोष्यामि नचिरादेव
दुःखार्तानामिह ध्वनिम् ॥
I shall surely hear the reverberations of misery here before it is too long, with the Rākshasa women in every household wailing.
5.26.32
सान्धकारो हतद्योता
हतराक्षसपुङ्गवा ।
भविष्यति पुरी लङ्का
निर्दग्धा रामसायकैः ॥
Burned down to ashes by the arrows of Rāma, Laṅkā will lose all its glitter with darkness enveloping it and all the bulls among Rākshasas killed.
5.26.33
यदि नाम स शूरो माम्
रामो रक्तान्तलोचनः ।
जानीयाद्वर्तमानां हि
रावणस्य निवेशने ॥
How I wish that the heroic Rāma, the corners of whose eyes are blood-red, comes to know that I am here, bound in the house of Rāvaṇa ...!
5.26.34
अनेन तु नृशंसेन
रावणेनाधमेन मे ।
समयो यस्तु निर्दिष्टः
तस्य कालोऽयमागतः ।
स च मे विहितो मृत्युः
अस्मिन् दुष्टे न वर्तते ॥
The time limit set by this cruel and base Rāvaṇa is fast approaching; why is it that the death that is impending upon me, is not on him!
5.26.35
अकार्यं ये न जानन्ति
नैरृताः पापकारिणः ।
अधर्मात्तु महोत्पातो
भविष्यति हि सांप्रतम् ।
नैते धर्मं विजानन्ति
राक्षसाः पिशिताशनाः ॥
These Rākshasas of sinful deeds do not know what should be and should not be done. A great calamity is imminent now because they are hurting Dharma. These Rākshasas, who feed on raw flesh, do not have any sense of Dharma.
5.26.37
ध्रुवं मां प्रातराशार्थे
राक्षसः कल्पयिष्यति ।
साऽहं कथं करिष्यामि
तं विना प्रियदर्शनम् ।
रामं रक्तान्तनयनम्
अपश्यन्ती सुदुःखिता ॥
Certainly the Rākshasa is going to have me for breakfast. But what can I do (about it), without Rāma, of pleasing looks (by my side)! I am overwhelmed by grief, not seeing him, the corners of whose eyes are blood-red!
5.26.38
यदि कश्चित् प्रदाता मे
विषस्याद्य भवेदिह ।
क्षिप्रं वैवस्वतं देवम्
पश्येयं पतिना विना ॥
If there is anyone here who can kindly give me some poison, I, bereft of my husband, would rather go and visit the lord of Death, without any delay.
5.26.39
नाजानाज्जीवतीं रामः
स मां लक्ष्मणपूर्वजः ।
जानन्तौ तौ न कुर्याताम्
नोर्य्वां हि मम मार्गणम् ॥
Rāma, the elder brother of Lakshmaṇa, do not know that I am still alive; for if they do, they would not stop searching for me all over the earth!
5.26.40
नूनं ममैव शोकेन
स वीरो लक्ष्मणाग्रजः ।
देवलोकमितो यातः
त्यक्त्वा देहं महीतले ॥
Surely, that Veera, the older brother of Lakshmaṇa, grieving for me, must also have left to the divine worlds from here, discarding the mortal body on the earth.
5.26.41
धन्या देवास्सगन्धर्वाः
सिद्धाश्च परमर्षयः ।
मम पश्यन्ति ये नाथम्
रामं राजीवलोचनम् ॥
Blessed indeed would be those Dēvas, Gandharvas and Maharshis who see my lord Rāma of lotus eyes.
5.26.42
अथवा न हि तस्यार्थो
धर्मकामस्य धीमतः ।
मया रामस्य राजर्षेः
भार्यया परमात्मनः ॥
Or it could be that that sagacious Rājarshi, who contemplates on higher objects, does not find any use of me, his wife, engrossed only in Dharma!
5.26.43
दृश्यमाने भवेत्प्रीतिः
सौहृदं नास्त्यपश्यतः ।
नाशयन्ति कृतघ्नास्तु
न रामो नाशयिष्यति ॥
One likes what one sees and loses connection with what one does not. Those who forget the favors they received let the relationship die; but never Rāma.
5.26.44
किं नु मे न गुणाः केचित्
किं वा भाग्यक्षयो मम ।
याहं सीदामि रामेण
हीना मुख्येन भामिनी ॥
Or is it that I am defective? Or is it that my good days are behind me? Otherwise, why would I, a lovely woman, perish like this without my chief, Rāma?
5.26.45
श्रेयो मे जीवितान्मर्तुम्
विहीनाया महात्मनः ।
रामादक्लिष्टचारित्रात्
शूराच्छत्रुनिबर्हणात् ॥
I am better dead than alive, bereft of Rāma, a Mahātma of blemish-less character and a Ṡūra, who decimates his foe!
5.26.46
अथवा न्यस्तशत्रौ तौ
वने मूलफलाशिनौ ।
भ्रातरौ हि नरश्रेष्ठौ
संवृत्तौ वनगोचरौ ॥
Or did the two brothers, the best among men, give up arms, and become used to the life in the Vana, subsisting on roots and fruits?
5.26.47
अथवा राक्षसेन्द्रेण
रावणेन दुरात्मना ।
छद्मना घातितौ शूरौ
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
Or, are the heroic brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa killed by a ruse by the evil-minded Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas?
5.26.48
साऽहमेवङ्गते काले
मर्तुमिच्छामि सर्वथा ।
न च मे विहितो मृत्युः
अस्मिन् दुःखेऽपि वर्तते ॥
In any case, the situation having turned out to be what it is, I only wish that I die. But I am not blessed with death, even in this miserable situation.
5.26.49
धन्याः खलु महात्मानो
मुनयस्त्यक्तकिल्बिषाः ।
जितात्मनो महाभागा
येषां न स्तः प्रियाप्रिये ॥
Fortunate are the highly blessed Munis, Mahātmas who know no sin and exercise control over their minds, for, they hold nothing dear or detestable.
5.26.50
प्रियान्न संभवेद्दुःखम्
अप्रियादधिकं भयम् ।
ताभ्यां हि ये वियुज्यन्ते
नमस्तेषां महात्मनाम् ॥
Salutations to those Mahātmas who detach themselves from what they like, the loss of which creates misery, as well as from what they do not like, the excess of which creates fear!
5.26.51
साऽहं त्यक्ता प्रियेणैव
रामेण विदितात्मना ।
प्राणांस्त्यक्ष्यामि पापस्य
रावणस्य गता वशम् ॥
Abjured by my beloved Rāma, who knows very well what he does, and fallen into the hands of the sinful Rāvaṇa, I wish only to give up my life.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे षडिवंश स्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty sixth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
