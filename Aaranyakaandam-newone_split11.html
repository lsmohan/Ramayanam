Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 11
In this long Sarga, Rāma reaches the settlement of Āṡramas in the Daṇḍaka Araṇya. He lives in the Āṡramas of Munis for ten years. Then he returns to the Āṡrama of Suteekshṇa.
After living there for a while, Rāma, one day, approaches Suteekshṇa and tells him that he would like to visit Maharshi Agastya, about whom he had heard from many people. Rāma asks him for directions to the Āṡrama of Agastya. Suteekshṇa is pleased with Rāma’s interest in visiting Agastya and gives him directions to Agastya’s place.
Rāma proceeds to Agastya’s Āṡrama along with Lakshmaṇa and Seetā. On his way he spends a night at the Āṡrama of the brother of Agastya. Next morning, he proceeds to Agastya’s place. On the way, he describes the greatness of Agastya to Lakshmaṇa, as one who relieved the southern region of the flesh eating Rākshasas. He recalls the story of how Agastya put an end to the Rākshasa brothers Vātāpi and Ilvala, who were slaughtering Brāhmaṇas by the thousands.
Rāma reaches Agastya Āṡrama and asks Lakshmaṇa to go first and inform Agastya of their arrival.
3.11.1
अग्रतः प्रययौ रामः
सीता मध्ये सुमध्यमा ।
पृष्ठतस्तु धनुष्पाणिः
लक्ष्मणोऽनुजगाम ह ॥
Rāma led the way, and Lakshmaṇa followed behind holding a bow, with Seetā, of slender waist, in the middle.
Some people think that Rāma always walked in front and Lakshmaṇa always behind. Some others think that it is the other way around.
Both are correct. Sometimes Rāma led the way and at other times it is Lakshmaṇa who walked ahead.
Compare this Ṡlōka with Ṡlōkas 2.52.95-96.
3.11.2
तौ पश्यमानौ विविधान्
शैलप्रस्थान्वनानि च ।
नदीश्च विविधा रम्या
जग्मतुस्सीतया सह ।
सारसांश्चक्रवाकांश्च
नदीपुलिनचारिणः ।
सरांसि च सपद्मानि
युक्तानि जलजैः खगैः ।
यूथबद्धांश्च पृषतान्
मदोन्मत्तान् विषाणिनः ।
महिषांश्च वराहांश्च
नागांश्च द्रुमवैरिणः ॥
The two of them proceeded along with Seetā seeing a variety of mountain sides, Vanas and many a pleasant stream, Sārasa and Cakravāka birds on the beaches of the rivers and lotus ponds with water fowl, herds of spotted deer, horned buffaloes full of lust and boars and tuskers, the nemesis of trees.
3.11.5
ते गत्वा दूरमध्वानं
लम्बमाने दिवाकरे ।
ददृशुस्सहिता रम्यं
तटाकं योजनायतम् ।
पद्मपुष्करसम्बाधं
गजयूथैरलङ्कृतम् ।
सारसैर्हंसकादम्बैः
सङ्कुलं जलचारिभिः ॥
Together, they travelled a long distance, and by the sunset, they reached a beautiful Yōjana-wide lake full of water fowl like Hamsa and Kādamba.
3.11.7
प्रसन्नसलिले रम्ये
तस्मिन्सरसि शुश्रुवे ।
गीतवादित्रनिर्घोषो
न तु कश्चन दृश्यते ॥
Sounds of song and music were heard from that pleasant lake with its transparent water although there was no one to be seen.
3.11.8
ततः कौतूहलाद्रामो
लक्ष्मणश्च महाबलः ।
मुनिं धर्मभृतं नाम
प्रष्टुं समुपचक्रमे ॥
Then, Rāma and mighty strong Lakshmaṇa became curious and asked a Muni by name Dharmabhṛta about it.
3.11.9
इदमत्यद्भुतं श्रुत्वा
सर्वेषां नो महामुने ।
कौतूहलं महज्जातं
किमिदं साधु कथ्यताम् ॥
O Mahā Muni, we have all become very curious listening to this wonderful sound. Can you please tell us about it in detail?
3.11.10
तेनैवमुक्तो धर्मात्मा
राघवेण मुनिस्तदा ।
प्रभवं सरसः कृत्स्नम्
आख्यातुमुपचक्रमे ॥
Then, the Muni, asked thus by Rāghava, started to tell everything about the lake, including how it came into existence.
3.11.11
इदं पञ्चाप्सरो नाम
तटाकं सार्वकालिकम् ।
निर्मितं तपसा राम
मुनिना माण्डकर्णिना ॥
This lake which is full all through the year, is known as Pañcāpsara. It was built by Muni Māṇḍakarṇi through his powers of Tapa.
3.11.12
स हि तेपे तपस्तीव्रं
माण्डकर्णिर्महामुनिः ।
दशवर्षसहस्राणि
वायुभक्षो जलाश्रयः ॥
He, the Mahā Muni Māṇḍakarṇi, did intense Tapa for ten thousand years living on air and water.
3.11.13
ततः प्रव्यथितास्सर्वे
देवास्साग्निपुरोगमाः ।
अब्रुवन् वचनं सर्वे
परस्परसमागताः ।
अस्माकं कस्यचित् स्थानम्
एष प्रार्थयते मुनिः ।
इति संविग्नमनसः
सर्वे ते त्रिदिवौकसः ॥
Then, all the Dēvas were worried. Led by Agni, they got together and thought: ‘This Muni clearly wants to usurp one of us’. The Dēvas felt extremely disturbed.
3.11.15
तत्र कर्तुं तपोविघ्नं
देवैस्सर्वैर्नियोजिताः ।
प्रधानाप्सरसः पञ्च
विद्युच्चलितवर्चसः ॥
Then, to derail the Tapa, five of the foremost Apsarasas, who were dazzling like flashes of lightening, were deployed by the Dēvas.
3.11.16
अप्सरोभिस्ततस्ताभिः
मुनिर्दृष्टपरावरः ।
नीतो मदनवश्यत्वं
सुराणां कार्यसिद्धये ॥
Those Apsaras, who knew the ebb and flow (of this world), made that Muni succumb to lust, to serve the purpose of Dēvas.
The Apsaras are conscious that they are not doing the right thing. Yet they are realistic (know the ebb and flow) enough to obey the Dēvas.
And Dēvas also have no other option but to guard against people who they think are attempting to debase them from their positions.
3.11.17
ताश्चैवाप्सरसः पञ्च
मुनेः पत्नीत्वमागताः ।
तटाके निर्मितं तासाम्
अस्मिन्नन्तर्हितं गृहम् ॥
Then those five Apsaras became the wives of the Muni. A house was built for them, submerged and invisible in this lake.
3.11.18
तथैवाप्सरसः पञ्च
निवसन्त्यो यथासुखम् ।
रमयन्ति तपोयोगान्
मुनिं यौवनमास्थितम् ॥
Then the five Apsaras lived happily entertaining the Muni, who remained young, using the powers of his Tapa.
3.11.19
तासां सङ्क्रीडमानानां
एष वादित्रनिस्स्वनः ।
श्रूयते भूषणोन्मिश्रो
गीतशब्दो मनोहरः ॥
What we hear is a mix of the sound of the musical instruments, the tinkling of jewelry and the the dulcet sounds of the songs they sing, as they distract themselves!
3.11.20
आश्चर्यमिति तस्यैतत्
वचनं भावितात्मनः ।
राघवः प्रतिजग्राह
सह भ्रात्रा महायशाः ॥
Learning this from that pure soul, Rāghava and his renowned brother said, ‘This is amazing!’
3.11.21
एवं कथयमानस्य
ददर्शाश्रममण्डलम् ।
कुशचीरपरिक्षिप्तं
ब्राह्म्या लक्ष्म्या समावृतम् ॥
As he said this, he saw before him, the settlement of Āṡramas (decorously) covered with Kuṡa grass and bast wear with a halo of Brāhmic luster.
3.11.22
प्रविश्य सह वैदेह्या
लक्ष्मणेन च राघवः ।
उवास मुनिभिस्सर्वैः
पूज्यमानो महायशाः ॥
Entering (the settlement of Āṡramas) with Vaidēhee and Lakshmaṇa, the renowned Rāghava lived there, treated with all regard by the Munis.
3.11.23
तदा तस्मिन्सकाकुत्स्थः
श्रीमत्याश्रममण्डले ।
उषित्वा तु सुखं तत्र
पूज्यमानो महर्षिभिः ।
जगाम चाश्रमांस्तेषां
पर्यायेण तपस्विनाम् ।
येषामुषितवान् पूर्वं
सकाशे स महास्त्रवित् ॥
Then Kākutstha, the knower of great Astras, lived in that settlement of Āṡramas in all comfort, regarded well by all Maharshis. He took turns going to the Āṡramas of the Tapasvis near whom he had lived earlier.
3.11.25
क्वचित्परिदशान्मासान्
एकं संवत्सरं क्वचित् ।
क्वचिच्छ चतुरो मासान्
पञ्च षट्चापरान्क्वचित् ।
अपरत्राधिकं मासात्
अप्यर्धमधिकं क्वचित् ।
त्रीन्मासानष्टमासांश्च
राघवो न्यवसत्सुखम् ॥
In some of them Rāghava stayed for about ten months, in others for a year, in yet others for four months, in some others five or six months or for longer periods, while elsewhere he spent a month and a half, or three or eight months.
3.11.27
तथा संवसतस्तस्य
मुनीनामाश्रमेषु वै ।
रमतश्चानुकूल्येन
ययुस्संवत्सरा दश ॥
Ten years passed by comfortably, as he lived and enjoyed in those Āṡramas of Munis.
3.11.28
परिवृत्य च धर्मज्ञो
राघवस्सह सीतया ।
सुतीक्ष्णस्याश्रमं श्रीमान्
पुनरेवाजगाम ह ॥
Having thus made the round of the Āṡramas, Rāghava, the blessed one, the Dharmajña, went back again to Suteekshṇa’s Āṡrama.
3.11.29
स तमाश्रममासाद्य
मुनिभिः प्रतिपूजितः ।
तत्रापि न्यवसद्रामः
कञ्चित्कालमरिन्दमः ॥
Upon reaching the Āṡrama, Rāma, the subduer of foes, was welcomed by the Munis. He lived there also for some time.
3.11.30
तदाश्रमस्थो विनयात्
कदाचित्तं महामुनिम् ।
उपासीनस्सकाकुत्स्थः
सुतीक्ष्णमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Once, when he was in that Āṡrama, Kākutstha, sat by the Mahā Muni Suteekshṇa and said these words to him, in all deference.
3.11.31
अस्मिन्नरण्ये भगवान्
अगस्त्यो मुनिसत्तमः ।
वसतीति मया नित्यं
कथाः कथयतां श्रुतम् ॥
I always hear people speak of Bhagawān Agastya, the eminent Muni, who lives here in this Araṇya.
3.11.32
न तु जानामि तं देशं
वनस्यास्य महत्तया ।
कुत्राऽश्रमपदं पुण्यं
महर्षेस्तस्य धीमतः ॥
But this Vana is vast and I do not know the exact place (where he lives). Where is the holy Āṡrama of that sagacious Maharshi?
3.11.33
प्रसादात्तत्र भवतः
सानुजस्सह सीतया ।
अगस्त्यमभिगच्छेयम्
अभिवादयितुं मुनिम् ॥
With your gracious blessings, I would like to go with my brother and Seetā to offer my obeisance to the Muni Agastya.
3.11.34
मनोरथो महानेष
हृदि मे परिवर्तते ।
यद्यहं तं मुनिवरं
शुश्रूषेयमपि स्वयम् ॥
This intense desire to personally serve that foremost of the Munis suffuses my mind.
3.11.35
इति रामस्य स मुनिः
श्रुत्वा धर्मात्मनो वचः ।
सुतीक्ष्णः प्रत्युवाचेदं
प्रीतो दशरथात्मजम् ॥
Hearing these words of Rāma the Dharmātma, Muni Suteekshṇa was very pleased. He responded to that son of Daṡaratha with these words:
3.11.36
अहमप्येतदेव त्वां
वक्तुकामस्सलक्ष्मणम् ।
अगस्त्यमभिगच्छेति
सीतया सह राघव ॥
I too wanted to tell you and Lakshmaṇa the same. Please visit Agastya, O Rāghava, along with Seetā!
3.11.37
दिष्ट्या त्विदानीमर्थेऽस्मिन्
स्वयमेव ब्रवीषि माम् ।
अहमाख्यामि ते वत्स
यत्रागस्त्यो महामुनिः ॥
By the grace of heavens, you have yourself talked about this. I will tell you, my dear, where Agastya the Mahā Muni is.
3.11.38
योजनान्याश्रमात्तात
याहि चत्वारि वै ततः ।
दक्षिणेन महान् श्रीमान्
अगस्त्यभ्रातुराश्रमः ।
स्थलप्राये वनोद्देशे
पिप्पलीवनशोभिते ।
बहुपुष्पफले रम्ये
नानाशकुनिनादिते ॥
Go southwards for four Yōjanas from this Āṡrama. There you will see a beautiful Āṡrama of the brother of Agastya, located in the wooded uplands, adorned by groves of Pippala trees, abounding in fruits and flowers of every kind, delightful to the eye and resounding with the notes of many birds.
3.11.40
पद्मिन्यो विविधास्तत्र
प्रसन्नसलिलाश्शिवाः ।
हंसकारण्डवाकीर्णाः
चक्रवाकोपशोभिताः ।
तत्रैकां रजनीं व्युष्य
प्रभाते राम गम्यताम् ॥
There are many ponds with clear holy waters with their appeal enhanced by the flocks of Haṃsa, Kāraṇḍa, Cakravāka birds that inhabit it. Stay there for the night and start the next morning.
3.11.41
दक्षिणां दिशमास्थाय
वनषण्डस्य पार्श्वतः ।
तत्रागस्त्याश्रमपदं
गत्वा योजनमन्तरा ॥
Proceed in the southerly direction by the side of the thick woods and after going a distance of one Yōjana, (you will find) Agastya’s Āṡrama there.
3.11.42
रमणीये वनोद्धेशे
बहुपादपसंवृते ।
रंस्यते तत्र वैदेही
लक्ष्मणश्च सह त्वया ।
स हि रम्यो वनोद्देशो
बहुपादपसङ्कुलः ॥
That area of the Vana is beautiful, surrounded by many trees. Vaidēhee and Lakshmaṇa would enjoy it, along with you.
An area of Vana filled with many trees would naturally be enchanting, wouldn’t it?
3.11.44
यदि बुद्धिः कृता द्रष्टुं
अगस्त्यं तं महामुनिम् ।
अद्यैव गमने बुद्धिं
रोचयस्व महायशः ॥
O renowned one, if your mind is set on seeing that Mahā Muni Agastya, you might as well try to go today!
3.11.45
इति रामो मुनेश्श्रुत्वा
सह भ्रात्राभिवाद्य च ।
प्रतस्थेऽगस्त्यमुद्धिश्य
सानुजस्सीतया सह ॥
Having heard these words of Muni, Rāma, along with his brother, paid obeisance (to him). He started on his way to (see) Agastya along with his brother and Seetā.
3.11.46
पश्यन्वनानि रम्याणि
पर्वतांश्चाभ्रसन्निभान् ।
सरांसि सरितश्चैव
पथि मार्गवशानुगाः ।
सुतीक्ष्णेनोपदिष्टेन
गत्वा तेन पथा सुखम् ।
इदं परमसंहृष्टो
वाक्यं लक्ष्मणमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing the beautiful woods and mountains that were (huge) like clouds and the lakes and streams that flowed along the way, Rāma proceeded, enjoying the route indicated by Suteekshṇa.
Extremely delighted, he said to Lakshmaṇa:
3.11.48
एतदेवाश्रमपदं
नूनं तस्य महात्मनः ।
अगस्त्यस्य मुनेर्भ्रातुः
दृश्यते पुण्यकर्मणः ।
यथा हि मे वनस्यास्य
ज्ञाताः पथि सहस्रशः ।
सन्नताः फलभारेण
पुष्पभारेण च द्रुमाः ॥
The presence of these trees, thousands of them  drooping with the weight of fruits and flowers indicate without a doubt, that these Āṡrama grounds  are of  that Mahātma, the Muni of holy deeds,  the brother of Muni Agastya. 
3.11.50
पिप्पलीनां च पक्वानां
वनादस्मादुपागतः ।
गन्धोऽयं पवनोक्षिप्तः
सहसा कटुकोदयः ॥
From that wood comes, all of a sudden, the acrid smell of ripe Pippala carried by the wind.
3.11.51
तत्र तत्र च दृश्यन्ते
सङ्क्षिप्ताः काष्ठसञ्चयाः ।
लूनाश्च पथि दृश्यन्ते
दर्भा वैडूर्यवर्चसः ॥
We see, here and there, on our way, bundles of firewood gathered and fresh-clipped Darbha grass that shines like lazulite.
3.11.52
एतच्च वनमध्यस्थं
कृष्णाभ्रशिखरोपमम् ।
पावकस्याश्रमस्थस्य
धूमाग्रं सम्प्रदृश्यते ॥
And there we see, in the heart of the forest, rising like the crest of a black cloud, the smoke from the (sacred) fires of the Āṡrama.
3.11.53
विविक्तेषु च तीर्थेषु
कृतस्नाना द्विजातयः ।
पुष्पोपहारं कुर्वन्ति
कुसुमैस्स्वयमार्जितै ॥
The Brāhmaṇas, having finished their bath in the holy shallow waters, are offering flower oblations with flowers they themselves gathered.
3.11.54
तत्सुतीक्ष्णस्य वचनं
यथा सौम्य मया श्रुतम् ।
अगस्त्यस्याश्रमो भ्रातुः
नूनमेष भविष्यति ।
निगृह्य तपसा मृत्युं
लोकानां हितकाम्यया ।
यस्य भ्रात्रा कृतेयं दिक्
शरण्या पुण्यकर्मणा ॥
O gentle one, this must be the Āṡrama  of the brother of Agastya  who contained (the impending) death with Tapa,  and made his abode in this (southern) region  to further the welfare of the people, which fits the description I heard from Suteekshṇa. 
3.11.56
इहैकदा किल क्रूरो
वातापिरपि चेल्वलः ।
भ्रातरौ सहितावास्तां
ब्राह्मणघ्नौ महासुरौ ॥
Once upon a time, it seems it was here, that the two Brāhmaṇa-killing cruel Rākshasas, the brothers Vātāpi and Ilvala, used to live.
3.11.57
धारयन् ब्राह्मणं रूपं
इल्वलस्संस्कृतं वदन् ।
आमन्त्रयति विप्रान् स्म
श्राद्धमुद्दिश्य निर्घृणः ॥
The pitiless Ilvala used to approach Brāhmaṇas, himself disguised as one. Speaking in a refined language, he used to invite them to partake in the Ṡrāddha feast.
3.11.58
भ्रातरं संस्कृतं कृत्वा
ततस्तं मेषरूपिणम् ।
तान् द्विजान्भोजयामास
श्राद्धदृष्टेन कर्मणा ॥
Then he used to cook his brother, who had taken the form of a sheep, and make the Brāhmaṇas eat, as required by the Ṡrāddha ritual.
There is a legend which says that Brāhmaṇas used to eat meat once upon a time, until this episode happened, at the end of which Agastya barred Brāhmaṇas from eating meat.
3.11.59
ततो भुक्तवतां तेषां
विप्राणामिल्वलोऽब्रवीत् ।
वातापे निष्क्रमस्वेति
स्वरेण महता वदन् ॥
Once those Brāhmaṇas completed eating, Ilvala used to shout loudly: "Hey Vātāpi! Come out!"
3.11.60
ततो भ्रातुर्वचश्श्रुत्वा
वातापिर्मेषवन्नदन् ।
भित्त्वा भित्त्वा शरीराणि
ब्राह्मणानां विनिष्पतत् ॥
On hearing his brother call, Vātāpi, bleating like a sheep, would tear apart the bodies of the Brāhmaṇas and come out.
3.11.61
ब्राह्माणानां सहस्राणि
ताभ्यामेवं परंतप ।
विनाशितानि संहत्य
नित्यशः पिशिताशया ॥
Thousands of Brāhmaṇas, O scorcher of foes, were slaughtered in this manner by both of them together, impelled by their urge for flesh.
3.11.62
अगस्त्येन तदा देवैः
प्रार्थितेन महर्षिणा ।
अनुभूय किल श्राद्धे
भक्षितस्समहासुरः ॥
Then, Agastya, urged by Dēvas and Maharshis, had (the good meal of) Ṡrāddha, eating that monstrous Rākshasa, and digesting him.
3.11.63
ततस्सम्पन्नमित्युक्त्वा
दत्त्वा हस्तोदकं ततः ।
भ्रातरं निष्क्रमस्वेति
चेल्वलस्सोऽभ्यभाषत ॥
Then, after giving water to wash hands, and after asking ‘Are you satisfied?’, Ilvala called to his brother, ‘Come out!’
The custom of formally asking a Brāhmaṇa after offering the Ṡrāddha meal, ‘Are you satisfied?’ continues even to this day.
3.11.64
स तं तथा भाषमाणं
भ्रातरं विप्रघातिनम् ।
अब्रवीत्प्रहसन्धीमान्
अगस्त्यो मुनिसत्तमः ॥
To that brother, the killer of Brāhmaṇas, who called thus, Agastya, the sagacious and eminent Muni said, laughing:
3.11.65
कुतो निष्क्रमितुं शक्तिः
मया जीर्णस्य राक्षसः ।
भ्रातुस्ते मेषरूपस्य
गतस्य यमसादनम् ॥
Where is the ability for him to come out? The Rākshasa has been digested by me. Your brother, who took the form of a goat has gone to the abode of Yama!
3.11.66
अथ तस्य वचश्श्रुत्वा
भ्रातुर्निधनसंश्रयम् ।
प्रधर्षयितुमारेभे
मुनिं क्रोधान्निशाचरः ।
सोऽभिद्रवन्मुनिश्रेष्ठं
मुनिना दीप्ततेजसा ।
चक्षुषानलकल्पेन
निर्दग्धो निधनं गतः ॥
On hearing those words that announced the death of his brother, the enraged Night-rover rushed upon that foremost of Munis to kill him.
Then he was burnt to death by the the fire-ball like eyes of that Muni, of effulgent radiance.
3.11.68
तस्यायमाश्रमो भ्रातुः
तटाकवनशोभितः ।
विप्रानुकम्पया येन
कर्मेदं दुष्करं कृतम् ॥
This Āṡrama, beautiful with the woods and lakes, belongs to the brother of that Muni, who carried out such a difficult task out of concern for Brāhmaṇas.
3.11.69
एवं कथयमानस्य
तस्य सौमित्रिणा सह ।
रामस्यास्तं गतस्सूर्यः
सन्ध्याकालोऽभ्यवर्ततः ॥
As Rāma was telling this to Sowmitri, the sun went down and evening set in.
3.11.70
उपास्य पश्चिमां सन्ध्यां
सह भ्रात्रा यथाविधि ।
प्रविवेशाऽश्रमपदं
तमृषिं सोऽभ्यवादयत् ॥
He, along with his brother, offered Vandanas to Sandhya in a customary manner and entered the grounds of the Āṡrama, greeting the Ṛshi with all deference.
3.11.71
सम्यक् प्रतिगृहीतश्च
मुनिना तेन राघवः ।
न्यवसत्तां निशामेकां
प्राश्य मूलफलानि च ॥
Well received by that Muni, Rāghava dined on roots and fruits and stayed there for that one night
3.11.72
तस्यां रात्य्रां व्यतीतायां
विमले सूर्यमण्डले ।
भ्रातरं तमगस्त्यस्य
ह्यामन्त्रयत राघवः ॥
After the night passed and a clear sun broke out, Rāghava took leave of that brother of Agastya, (saying):
3.11.73
अभिवादये त्वां भगवन्
सुखमध्युषितो निशाम् ।
आमन्त्रये त्वां गच्छामि
गुरुं ते द्रष्टुमग्रजम् ॥
O Bhagawan! My Vandanas to you. I spent the night comfortably. I would like to take leave of you and go to your revered elder brother.
3.11.74
गम्यतामिति तेनोक्तो
जगाम रघुनन्दनः ।
यथोद्दिष्टेन मार्गेण
वनं तच्चावलोकयन् ।
नीवारान्पनसांस्तालां
तिमिशान्वन्जुलान्धवान् ।
चिरिबिल्वान्मधूकांश्च
बिल्वानपि च तिन्दुकान् ॥
The delight of Raghus, told by him to proceed, took the route that had been described to him, seeing the Vana along the way, with wild rice, jack fruit, palm, Timiṡa, Vaṅjula, Dhava, Ciribilva, Madhūka, Bilva and Tinduka trees.
3.11.76
पुष्पितान्पुष्पिताग्राभिः
लताभिरनुवेष्टितान् ।
ददर्श रामश्शतशः
तत्र कान्तारपादपान् ।
हस्तिहस्तैर्विमृदितान्
वानरैरुपशोभितान् ।
मत्तैश्शकुनिसङ्घैश्च
शतशश्च प्रणादितान् ॥
Rāma saw hundreds of forest trees in full flower, covered with flowering vines, which were rubbed against by elephants, dotted (on their branches) with monkeys, and that echoed with the cooing of hundreds of flocks of birds full of passion.
3.11.78
ततोऽब्रवीत्समीपस्थं
रामो राजीवलोचनः ।
पृष्ठतोऽनुगतं वीरं
लक्ष्मणं लक्ष्मिवर्धनम् ॥
Then, Rāma of lotus eyes said to Lakshmaṇa, the Veera, the augmenter of opulence, who was following him closely behind:
3.11.79
स्निग्धपत्रा यथा वृक्षा
यथा क्षान्ता मृगद्विजाः ।
आश्रमो नातिदूरस्थो
महर्षेर्भावितात्मनः ॥
The Āṡrama of the Maharshi who has realized the Self should not be far away, if the tenderness of the leaves of trees and the calmness of the birds and beasts is any indication.
3.11.80
अगस्त्य इति विख्यातो
लोके स्वेनैव कर्मणा ।
आश्रमो दृश्यते तस्य
परिश्रान्तश्रमापहः ।
प्राज्यधूमाकुलवनः
चीरमालापरिष्कृतः ।
प्रशान्तमृगयूथश्च
नानाशकुनिनादितः ॥
We (now) see the Āṡrama, which eases the fatigue of visitors, covered by smoke (from fire rituals) decorated with bast festoons, full of quiet animals and filled with the sounds of many kinds of birds, of Agastya who has become famous in this world by his (illustrious) deeds.
3.11.82
तस्येदमाश्रमपदं
प्रभावाद्यस्य राक्षसैः ।
दिगियं दक्षिणा त्रासात्
दृश्यते नोपभुज्यते ॥
These are the grounds of his Āṡrama because of whose prowess Rākshasas do not dare to cast a glance at this region in the south and enjoy it.
3.11.83
यदा प्रभृति चाक्रान्ता
दिगियं पुण्यकर्मणा ।
तदा प्रभृति निर्वैराः
प्रशान्ता रजनीचराः ॥
Since the time this region was taken over by him, of holy deeds, the Night-rovers gave up their hostility and became quiet.
3.11.84
नाम्ना चेयं भगवतो
दक्षिणा दिक्प्रदक्षिणा ।
प्रथिता त्रिषु लोकेषु
दुर्धर्षा क्रूरकर्मभिः ॥
This southern region of great potential, no more accessible to those of cruel deeds, is celebrated in the three worlds in his, the Bhagawān’s, name.
3.11.85
मार्गं निरोद्धुं निरतो
भास्करस्याचलोत्तमः ।
निदेशं पालयन्यस्य
विन्द्यशैलो न वर्धते ।
अयं दीर्घायुषस्तस्य
लोके विश्रुतकर्मणः ।
अगस्त्यस्याश्रमः श्रीमान्
विनीतजनसेवितः ॥
This splendid Āṡrama, which is populated by many a docile person belongs to that long-living Agastya of well regarded deeds,
by obeying whose command, Vindhya, the best of mountains, stopped growing, even though it always wanted to grow high enough to block the path of the sun.
3.11.87
एष लोकार्चितस्साधुः
हिते नित्यरतस्सताम् ।
अस्मानभिगतानेष
श्रेयसा योजयिष्यति ॥
This good man, who is honored by everyone and who is always interested in doing good for good people, will guide us in the right path, if we go there.
3.11.88
आराधयिष्याम्यत्राहं
अगस्त्यं तं महामुनिम् ।
शेषं च वनवासस्य
सौम्य वत्स्याम्यहं प्रभो ॥
My dear! O capable one! O gentle one! I will spend time attending on this Mahā Muni, Agastya, for the rest of my stay in the Vana.
3.11.89
अत्र देवास्सगन्धर्वाः
सिद्धाश्च परमर्षयः ।
अगस्त्यं नियताहारं
सततं पर्युपासते ॥
Here, Dēvas, Gandharvas, Siddhas and Maharshis always serve Agastya, who keeps to a limited and precise diet.
3.11.90
नात्र जीवेन्मृषावादी
क्रूरो वा यदि वा शठः ।
नृशंसः कामवृत्तो वा
मुनिरेष तथाविधः ॥
No one who tells lies or is cruel or wicked or is a cheat or given to whims and fancies lives here. Such is the nature of this Muni.
3.11.91
अत्र देवाश्च यक्षाश्च
नागाश्च पतगैस्सह ।
वसन्ति नियताहारा
धर्ममाराधयिष्णवः ॥
Dēvas, Yakshas, Nāgas, along with birds live here, eating sparingly and with high regard to Dharma.
3.11.92
अत्र सिद्धा महात्मानो
विमानैस्सूर्यसन्निभैः ।
त्यक्तदेहा नवैर्देहैः
स्वर्याताः परमर्षयः ॥
From here many Mahātmas, who were great Ṛshis, with their Tapa becoming fruitful, gave up their earthly bodies, acquired new (ethereal) bodies and went to Heaven on Vimānas that were (dazzling) like the sun.
3.11.93
यक्षत्वममरत्वं च
राज्यानि विविधानि च ।
अत्र देवाः प्रयच्छन्ति
भूतैराराधिताश्शुभैः ॥
Dēvas, worshipped here by many people who are pure, confer on them Yakshahood, divinity and a variety of lordships.
3.11.94
आगताः स्माऽश्रमपदम्
सौमित्रे प्रविशाग्रतः ।
निवेदयेह मां प्राप्तम्
ऋषये सीतया सह ॥
O Sowmitri! We have arrived at the grounds of the Āṡrama. Go in first, and inform the Ṛshi that I am here along with Seetā.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे एकादशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eleventh Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
