Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 58
In this Sarga, Daṡaratha asks Sumantra how Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Seetā were living in the Vana and what they conveyed to him. Sumantra conveys the messages from all three.
2.58.1
प्रत्याश्वस्तो यदा राजा
मोहात्प्रत्यागतं पुनः ।
अथाऽजुहाव तं सूतम्
रामवृत्तान्तकारणात् ॥
The King, after recovering from his swoon and feeling a little better, sent for the charioteer, wishing to hear news of Rāma.
2.58.2
अथ सूतो महाराजम्
कृताञ्जलिरुपस्थितः ।
राममेवानुशोचन्तम्
दुःखशोकसमन्वितम् ।
वृद्धं परमसन्तप्तम्
नवग्रहमिव द्विपम् ।
विनिश्वसन्तं ध्यायन्तम्
स्वस्थमिव कुङञरम् ॥
And the charioteer with folded hands waited upon that aged supreme King, who was heaving great sighs thinking of nothing but Rāma, brooding like a tusker that is ill, overwhelmed by sadness and grief and distraught like a newly trapped elephant.
2.58.4
राजा तु रजसा सूतम्
ध्वस्ताङ्गं समुपस्थितम् ।
अश्रुपूर्णमुखं दीनम्
उवाच परमार्तवत् ॥
The king, in all anxiety, asked the charioteer who stood nearby with his body covered in dust and face filled with tears, and looking helpless and despondent:
2.58.5
क्वनु वत्स्यति धर्मात्मा
वृक्षमूलमुपाश्रितः ।
सोऽत्यन्तसुखितस्सूत
किमशिष्यति राघवः ॥
O charioteer, where is the Dharmātma (Rāma) living? At the foot of which tree did he make his abode? What could Rāghava, who was used to the best of comforts, eat?
2.58.6
दुःखस्यानुचितो दुःखम्
सुमन्त्र शयनोचितः ।
भूमिपालात्मजो भूमौ
शेते कथमनाथवत् ॥
How can the son of the ruler of the earth, who knows no hardships, face the hardship? How could he, who is used to sleeping on a bed, lie on the bare ground like a forlorn creature?
2.58.7
यं यान्तमनुयान्ति स्म
पदातिरथकुञ्जराः ।
स वत्स्यति कथं रामो
विजनं वन माश्रितः ॥
How could Rāma, who is followed always by infantry, chariotry and elephantry, wherever he goes, live in a Vana that has no humans around?
2.58.8
व्यालैर्मृगैराचरितम्
कृष्णसर्पनिषेवितम् ।
कथं कुमारौ वैदेह्या
सार्धं वन मुपस्थितौ ॥
How could the two princes and the princess of Vidēha live in the Vana that is infested by black serpents and in which savage beasts roam?
2.58.9
सुकुमार्या तपस्विन्या
सुमन्त्र सह सीतया ।
राजपुत्रौ कथं पादै
रवरुह्य रथाद्गतौ ॥
How could the sons of a royal family along with the delicate and pitiable Seetā go on foot, getting off the chariot?
2.58.10
सिद्धार्थः खलु सूत त्वम्
येन दृष्टौ ममाऽत्मजौ ।
वनान्तं प्रविशन्तौ तौ
अश्विनाविवमन्दरम् ॥
You are indeed blessed, O charioteer, to have had the fortune of seeing my sons enter the Vana, like Aṡwini Dēvatas alighting on Mandara mountain!
2.58.11
किमुवाच वचो रामः
किमुवाच च लक्ष्मणः ।
सुमन्त्र वनमासाद्य
किमुवाच च मैथिली ॥
On reaching the Vana, O Sumantra, what did Rāma say, what did Lakshmaṇa say and what did the princess of Mithilā say?
2.58.12
आसितं शयितं भुक्तम्
सूत रामस्य कीर्तय ।
जीविष्यामहमेतेन
ययातिरिव साधुषु ॥
Tell me, O charioteer, how Rāma sat, slept and ate. I shall also, doing the same way, live just as Yayāti did in the company of Sādhus.
The story of Yayāti is alluded to in Rāmāyaṇa a few times. The full story of Yayāti is available in Ādi Parva of Mahābhārata. Yayāti indulges in sensual pleasures on borrowed youth and then gets disgusted about it. Then he performs intense Tapa and attains Heaven.
There, Yayāti boasts with Indra, that he was the greatest among the Tapasvis. As a consequence he falls down from the Heaven. Indra grants him a boon to fall among Sādhus. There they bequeath their entire Puṇya and thus Yayāti regains Heaven. Similarly, Daṡaratha hopes to change his life style to match that of Rāma.
2.58.13
इति सूतो नरेन्द्रेण
बोधितस्सज्जमानया ।
उवाच वाचा राजानम्
स बाष्पपरिबद्धया ॥
Thus asked by the lord of peoples, the charioteer replied in a faltering voice, choked by tears, to that king:
2.58.14
अब्रवीन्मां महाराज
धर्ममेवानुपालयन् ।
अञ्जलिं राघवः कृत्वा
शिरसाऽभिप्रणम्य च ॥
O supreme king! Rāghava said to me, in complete adherence to Dharma, with palms joined in reverence and bowing his head in salutations to you:
2.58.15
सूत मद्वचनात्तस्य
तातस्य विदितात्मनः ।
शिरसा वन्दनीयस्य
वन्द्यौ पादौ महात्मनः ॥
O Charioteer! I would like to say that the feet of my sagacious father, the Mahātma, which deserve to be touched by my head are receiving my salutations!
2.58.16
सर्वमन्तःपुरं वाच्यम्
सूत मद्वचनात्त्वया ।
आरोग्यमविशेषेण
यथार्हं चाभिवादनम् ॥
Please convey salutations, on my behalf, to everyone in the inner quarters, without exception and any distinction, in an appropriate manner, and my enquiries about their health and wellbeing!
2.58.17
माता च मम कौसल्या
कुशलं चाभिवादनम् ।
अप्रमादं च वक्तव्या
ब्रूयाश्चैनामिदं वचः ॥
Tell my mother Kousalyā that we are doing well and that we offer our obeisance. Tell her that she should be alert. Please also add:
2.58.18
धर्मनित्या यथाकालम्
अग्न्यगारपरा भव ।
देवि देवस्य पादौ च
देववत्परिपालय ॥
O queen! Constantly adhering to Dharma, you may zealously see that all fire rituals are performed in a timely manner. And you may devote yourself to the feet of the king, as if he is your god!
2.58.19
अभिमानं च मानं च
त्यक्त्वा वर्तस्य मातृषु ।
अनु राजानमार्यां च
कैकेयीमम्ब कारय ॥
Please regard all mothers without any pretense of pride and haughtiness! Please see that revered Kaikēyee would oblige and be in good terms with the king.
2.58.20
कुमारे भरते वृत्तिः
वर्तितव्या च राजवत् ।
अर्थज्येष्ठा हि राजानो
राजधर्ममनुस्मर ॥
Regarding prince Bharata, you shall treat him as if he were the king. Keep in mind the royal customs and that kings have the first claim on the treasury.
2.58.21
भरतः कुशलं वाच्यो
वाच्यो मद्वचनेन च ।
सर्वास्वेव यथान्यायम्
वृत्तिं वर्तस्व मातृषु ॥
On my behalf, please inquire about the wellbeing of Bharata and tell him that it behooves on him to treat all the mothers in an appropriate manner.
2.58.22
वक्तव्यश्च महाबाहुः
इक्ष्वाकुकुलनन्दनः ।
पितरं यौवराज्यस्थो
राज्यस्थमनुपालय ॥
Be sure to tell him, of the mighty arm, the delight of the Ikshwākus, that he shall hold the office of crown prince in accordance with the wishes of father, who is still the King.
2.58.23
अतिक्रान्तवया राजा
मास्मैनं व्यवरोरुधः ।
कुमार राज्ये जीव त्वम्
तस्यैवाज्ञाप्रवर्तनात् ॥
The king is advanced in age. May you not restrain him in any manner. May his wish and commands be carried out by you from the office of crown prince.
2.58.24
अब्रवीच्चापि मां भूयो
भृशमश्रूणि वर्तयन् ।
मातेव मम माता ते
द्रष्टव्या पुत्रगर्धिनी ॥
And he said, repeatedly, shedding a flood of tears, "Please look after my mother who loves her son so dearly, as if she were your own mother".
2.58.25
इत्येवं मां महराज
ब्रुवन्नेव महायशाः ।
रामो राजीवताम्राक्षो
भृशमश्रूण्यवर्तयत् ॥
Saying this to me, O supreme King, the lotus eyed Rāma of great repute shed a flood of tears.
2.58.26
लक्ष्मणस्तु सुसङ्कृद्धो
निश्श्वसन्वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
But Lakshmaṇa, full of anger, heaving violent sighs, said these words:
These Ṡlōkas succinctly represents the legitimate reaction, not only of Lakshmaṇa, but also of everyone in Ayōdhyā.
2.58.26
केनायमपराधेन
राजपुत्रो विवासितः ।
राज्ञा तु खलु कैकेय्या
लघुत्वाश्रित्य शासनम् ।
कृतं कार्यमकार्यं वा
वयं येनाभिपीडिताः ॥
What was the offense, for which the son of the king had to be exiled ? Whatever the king did, appropriate or not, obeying Kaikēyee's petty dictates, has brought us this hardship.
2.58.28
यदि प्रव्राजितो रामो
लोभकारणकारितम् ।
वरदाननिमित्तं वा
सर्वथा दुष्कृतं कृतम् ॥
Whether it is for the reason of honoring a boon that was given, or for some other selfish reason, sending Rāma in exile is a reprehensible act, in any sense.
2.58.29
इदं तावद्यथाकामम्
ईश्वरस्य कृते कृतम् ।
रामस्य तु परित्यागे
न हेतु मुपलक्षये ॥
I do not see any reason for Rāma to be abandoned except that it has been done as per the whims and fancies of the people at the helm.
2.58.30
असमीक्ष्य समारब्धम्
विरुद्धं बुध्दिलाघवात् ।
जनयिष्यति सङ्क्रोशम्
राघवस्य विवासनम् ॥
The exiling of Rāma, which has been done in a flippant manner without proper thought, negating everything that is plausible, can generate nothing but sorrow.
2.58.31
अहं तावन्महाराजे
पितृत्वं नोपलक्षये ।
भ्राता भर्ता च बन्धुश्च
पिता च मम राघवः ॥
Hence, I cannot see a father in that supreme king. Rāghava alone is my brother, master, relative and father.
2.58.32
सर्वर्लोकप्रियं त्यक्त्वा
सर्वलोकहिते रतम् ।
सर्वलोकोऽनुरज्येत
कथं त्वाऽनेनकर्मणा ॥
How can you hope to be liked by this world, by doing such an act, of abandoning someone who is the darling of all worlds and who is devoted to the good of all?
2.58.33
सर्वप्रजाभिरामं हि
रामं प्रव्राज्य धार्मिकम् ।
सर्वलोकं विरुध्येमम्
कथं राजा भविष्यसि ॥
How can you continue as a king, having antagonized the entire world by sending Rāma, the Dhārmika, the darling of all worlds, into exile?
2.58.34
जानकी तु महाराज
निश्श्वसन्ती मनस्विनी ।
भूतोपहतचित्तेव
विष्ठिता विस्मृता स्मिता ॥
As for the sensitive daughter of Janaka, O supreme king, she stood oblivious, like one possessed, heaving great sighs.
2.58.35
अदृष्टपूर्वव्यसना
राज्यपुत्री यशस्विनी ।
तेन दुःखेन रुदती
नैव मां किञ्चिदब्रवीत् ॥
Having never experienced such a hardship, that well regarded daughter of a king wept in sadness; and did not say a word to me.
2.58.36
उद्वीक्षमाणा भर्तारम्
मुखेन परिशुष्यता ।
मुमोच सहसा बाष्पम्
मां प्रयान्तमुदीक्ष्य सा ॥
She looked up at her master, her face parched by grief. Tears rolled off her face as she followed me with her eyes when I departed.
2.58.37
तथैव रामोऽश्रुमुखः कृताञ्जलिः
स्थितोऽभवल्लक्ष्मणबाहुपालितः ।
तथैव सीता रुदती तपस्विनी
निरीक्षते राजरथं तथैव माम् ॥
Rāma, his face full of tears, stood there with joined palms, waited upon by the solicitous Lakshmaṇa. Similarly, the sensitive Seetā, weeping, transfixed her eyes on the royal chariot and me.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे अष्टपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty eighth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
