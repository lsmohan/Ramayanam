Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 33
In this Sarga, Saramā, one among the Rākshasis who guards Seetā, who is considerate and affectionate to Seetā, comforts her, telling that Rāma was not killed and that Rāvaṇa had played a trick on her, showing a fake head and fake bow. Saramā assures her that her sorrows are about to end and all the riches and comforts will be hers again. She tells her that Rāma had crossed the ocean and was camping on the southern shore along with the army, and that she had actually seen Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
While she is comforting Seetā thus, both of them hear the clatter of Rāvaṇa’s army getting ready for the battle and filling the royal thoroughfares. Saramā assures Seetā that Rāma will kill those Rākshasa troops including Rāvaṇa, and that he will recover her. She assures her that, united, both she and Rāma will find the happiness they deserve.
6.33.1
सीतां तु मोहितां दृष्ट्वा
सरमा नाम राक्षसी ।
आससादाथ वैदेहीम्
प्रियां प्रणयिनी सखी ॥
Seeing that Seetā was confounded (with the false news), her much loved and affectionate friend, a Rākshasi by name Saramā, approached Vaidēhee whom she held dear.
6.33.2
मोहितां राक्षसेन्द्रेण
सीतां परमदुःखिताम् ।
आश्वासयामास तदा
सरमा मृदुभाषिणी ॥
Saramā of gentle speech comforted Seetā who became extremely sad by the trick played by the Lord of Rākshasas.
6.33.3
सा हि तत्र कृता मित्रम्
सीतया रक्ष्यमाणया ।
रक्षन्ती रावणादिष्टा
सानुक्रोशा दृढव्रता ॥
Seetā, who was guarded by her, found a friend in her, a staunch but compassionate woman who guarded her as ordered by Rāvaṇa.
6.33.4
सा ददर्श ततः सीताम्
सरमा नष्टचेतनाम् ।
उपावृत्योत्थितां ध्वस्ताम्
वडवामिव पांसुषु ॥
Saramā saw that Seetā had lost her senses and was like a mare covered in dust having rolled on the ground.
6.33.5
तां समाश्वासयामास
सखी स्नेहेन सुव्रता ॥
Being a good friend, and one of good discipline, she comforted her with all affection:
6.33.5
समाश्वसिहि वैदेहि
मा भूत्ते मनसो व्यथा ।
उक्ता यद्रावणेन त्वम्
प्रत्युक्तं च स्वयं त्वया ।
सखी स्नेहेन तद्भिरु
मया सर्वं प्रतिश्रुतम् ॥
Cheer up Vaidēhee! Let your mind not give itself away to worries! As a friend and out of love for you, O timid one, I heard everything that Rāvaṇa said to you and the response he got directly from you.
6.33.7
लीनया गगने शून्ये
भयमुत्सृज्य रावणात् ।
तव हेतोर्विशालाक्षि
न हि मे जीवितं प्रियम् ॥
I hid myself in the empty sky, disregarding all my fears of Rāvaṇa. My life is no more dearer to me than serving your cause.
6.33.8
स सम्भ्रान्तश्च निष्क्रान्तो
यत्कृते राक्षसाधिपः ।
तच्च मे विदितं सर्वम्
अभिनिष्क्रम्य मैथिलि ॥
I went out and found out all about why the Lord of Rākshasas left in such a haste, O Maithili!
6.33.9
न शक्यं सौप्तिकं कर्तुम्
रामस्य विदितात्मनः ।
वधश्च पुरुषव्याघ्रे
तस्मिन्नैवोपपद्यते ॥
It is impossible to kill Rāma, the vigilant tiger among men, in his sleep; It is simply absurd.
6.33.10
न त्वेव वानरा हन्तुम्
शक्याः पादपयोधिनः ।
सुरा देवर्षभेणेव
रामेण हि सुरक्षिताः ॥
Nor is it easy to kill the Vānaras, who fight with trees for weapons, while they are under the protection of Rāma, like Suras under the protection of (Indra,) the bull among Dēvas.
6.33.11
दीर्घवृत्तभुजः श्रीमान्
महोरस्कः प्रतापवान् ।
धन्वी संहननोपेतो
धर्मात्मा भुवि विश्रुतः ॥
He, the blessed bowman, with long and stout arms, broad chest and chiseled limbs, famed as a Dharmātma in the world, is a true man of prowess.
6.33.12
विक्रान्तो रक्षिता नित्यम्
आत्मनश्च परस्य च ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
कुलीनो नयशास्त्रवित् ॥
Valiant, highborn, expert in statecraft, he, along with his brother Lakshmaṇa, not only protects himself but also others.
6.33.13
हन्ता परबलौघानाम्
अचिन्त्यबलपौरुषः ।
न हतो राघवः श्रीमान्
सीते शत्रुनिबर्हणः ॥
O Seetā! The blessed Rāghava, the slayer of foe, of unimaginable strength and virility, who destroys hordes of enemy forces, is not killed.
6.33.14
अयुक्तबुद्धिकृत्येन
सर्वभूतविरोधिना ।
इयं प्रयुक्ता रौद्रेण
माया मायाविना त्वयि ॥
A trick was played on you by him, who is brutal and practices trickery. He thinks and does everything that is inappropriate and is hostile with just about every creature.
6.33.15
शोकस्ते विगतः सर्वः
कल्याणं त्वामुपस्थितम् ।
ध्रुवं त्वां भजते लक्ष्मीः
प्रियं ते भवति शृणु ॥
Your sorrows have come to an end and everything good will happen to you. Listen, riches and comforts will be yours again, and it will all be just the way you like.
6.33.16
उत्तीर्य सागरं रामः
सह वानरसेनया ।
सन्निविष्टः समुद्रस्य
तीरमासाद्य दक्षिणम् ॥
Having crossed the sea along with the Vānara army Rama reached the southern shore and has camped.
6.33.17
दृष्टो मे परिपूर्णार्थः
काकुत्स्थः सहलक्ष्मणः ।
सहितैः सागरान्तस्थैः
बलैस्तिष्ठति रक्षितः ॥
I saw Kākutstha along with Lakshmaṇa, his objective all but fulfilled. He is protected by the army that, coming together, stood near the ocean.
6.33.18
अनेन प्रेषिता ये च
राक्षसा लघुविक्रमाः ।
राघवस्तीर्ण इत्येव
प्रवृत्तिस्तैरिहाहृता ॥
It is the swift Rākshasas that he sent that brought him the news that Rāghava has crossed over the ocean.
6.33.19
स तां श्रुत्वा विशालाक्षि
प्रवृत्तिं राक्षसाधिपः ।
एष मन्त्रयते सर्वैः
सचिवैः सह रावणः ॥
It is on hearing that news, O you of wide eyes, that Rāvaṇa, the Lord of Rākshasas, conferred with all his ministers.
6.33.20
इति ब्रुवाणा सरमा
राक्षसी सीतया सह ।
सर्वोद्योगेन सैन्यानाम्
शब्दं शुश्राव भैरवम् ॥
After saying these words, Saramā heard, along with Seetā, the frightful din made by the feverish preparations of the army.
6.33.21
दण्डनिर्घातवादिन्याः
श्रुत्वा भेर्या महास्वनम् ।
उवाच सरमा सीताम्
इदं मधुरभाषिणी ॥
Hearing the great clatter of the drums that emerged as they were beaten by sticks, the sweet-spoken Saramā said this to Seetā:
6.33.22
सन्नाहजननी ह्येषा
भैरवा भीरु भेरिका ।
भेरीनादं च गम्भीरम्
शृणु तोयदनिस्वनम् ॥
O timid one! Listen to the frightful sound of the drums that is like the deep rumbling of the rainclouds, which is certainly meant for enthusing (the army) to get ready.
6.33.23
कल्प्यन्ते मत्तमातङ्गा
युज्यन्ते रथवाजिनः ।
हृष्यन्ते तुरागारूढाः
प्रासहस्ताः सहस्रशः ॥
Elephants in rut are being readied, horses are being yoked to the chariots, and thousands, with Prāsas in their hands, rejoice mounting the horses.
6.33.24
तत्र तत्र च सन्नद्धाः
सम्पतन्ति पदातयः ।
आपूर्यन्ते राजमार्गाः
सैन्यैरद्भुतदर्शनैः ।
वेगवद्भिर्नदद्भिश्च
तोयौघैरिव सागरः ॥
Foot soldiers are gathering in clusters. The thoroughfares are filling with swift, noisy and marvelous-looking troops, looking like an ocean with advancing billows of water.
6.33.25
शस्त्राणां च प्रसन्नानाम्
चर्मणां वर्मणां तथा ।
रथवाजिगजानां च
भूषितानां च रक्षसाम् ।
प्रभां विसृजतां पश्य
नानावर्णां समुत्थिताम् ।
वनं निर्दहतो घर्मे
यथा रूपं विभावसोः ॥
Look at the fine weapons, armors, shields, the decorations of chariots, horses and elephants and the ornaments of Rākshasas illuminating the environment with a multitude of colors like forest fires do in summer.
6.33.27
घण्टानां शृणु निर्घोषम्
रथानां शृणु निस्वनम् ।
हयानां हेषमाणानाम्
शृणु तूर्यध्वनिं यथा ।
उद्यतायुधहस्तानाम्
राक्षसेन्द्रानुयायिनाम् ।
सम्भ्रमो रक्षसामेष
तुमुलो रोमहर्षणः ॥
Listen to the ringing of bells, the rumble of chariots and the neighing of horses that sound like the blare of Tūrya, and the tumultuous and hair-raising commotion of the Rākshasas as they follow the Lord of Rākshasas wielding arms in their raised hands.
6.33.29
श्रीस्त्वां भजति शोकघ्नी
रक्षसां भयमागतम् ।
रामः कमलपत्राक्षो
दैत्यानामिव वासवः ।
विनिर्जित्य जितक्रोधः
त्वामचिन्त्यपराक्रमः ।
रावणं समरे हत्वा
भर्ता त्वाऽधिगमिष्यति ॥
Good luck that wipes out grief will be yours and a great fear will come upon the Rākshasas. Rāma, your husband of the lotus eyes and unimaginable prowess, a master of his temper, will kill Rāvaṇa in the battle like Vāsava did Daityas, and will recover you.
6.33.31
विक्रमिष्यति रक्षस्सु
भर्ता ते सहलक्ष्मणः ।
यथा शत्रुषु शत्रुघ्नो
विष्णुना सह वासवः ॥
Your husband, along with Lakshmaṇa will attack and penetrate into Rākshasa forces like Vishṇu, the destroyer of foe, along with Vāsava, did into the enemy forces.
6.33.32
आगतस्य हि रामस्य
क्षिप्रमङ्कगतां सतीम् ।
अहं द्रक्ष्यामि सिद्धार्थाम्
त्वां शत्रौ विनिपातिते ॥
I shall soon see Rāma coming and destroying the foe, and you, a chaste wife, seated on his lap, with your desire fulfilled.
6.33.33
अश्रूण्यानन्दजानि त्वम्
वर्तयिष्यसि शोभने ।
समागम्य परिष्वज्य
तस्योरसि महोरसः ॥
United with him of the mighty chest and embracing him, you will, O lovely one, shed tears of joy on his breast.
6.33.34
अचिरान्मोक्ष्यते सीते
देवि ते जघनं गताम् ।
धृतामेकां बहून् मासान्
वेणीं रामो महावलः ॥
Before long, Rāma of immense strength, O Seetā, will unbind your tresses that drop down to your hips worn in a single plait for these many months.
6.33.35
तस्य दृष्ट्वा मुखं देवि
पूर्णचन्द्रमिवोदितम् ।
मोक्ष्यसे शोकजं वारि
निर्मोकमिव पन्नगी ॥
Seeing his face that is like the full moon risen, you will cast away the tears of grief, O Dēvi, as a female serpent does its slough.
6.33.36
रावणं समरे हत्वा
न चिरादेव मैथिलि ।
त्वया समग्रः प्रियया
सुखार्हो लप्स्यते सुखम् ॥
Slaying Rāvaṇa in battle, and united with you, his beloved, before long O Maithili, he who deserves all happiness, will finally find it.
6.33.37
समागता त्वं वीर्येण
मोदिष्यसि महात्मना ।
सुवर्षेण समायुक्ता
यथा सस्येन मेदिनी ॥
You will rejoice, united with that heroic Mahātma, like the earth with standing crops does on receiving copious showers.
6.33.38
गिरिवरमभितोऽनुवर्तमानो
हय इव मण्डलमाशु य करोति ।
तमिह शरणमभ्युपेहि देवि
विदसकरं प्रभवो ह्ययं प्रजानाम् ॥
Seek refuge, O Dēvi, in the lord who causes days (and nights), who is the source of all creatures and who goes around (Mēru), the best of the mountains like a horse going in circles.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे त्रयस्त्रिंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty third Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
