Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 18
After entering his father's inner apartment, in this Sarga, Rāma sees him miserable and unable to speak a word. He fails to fathom the reason and asks queen Kaikēyee for the reason.
Kaikēyee tells him in no uncertain terms, what the king could not. She tells him about the two boons and she asks him to leave to the forest immediately, so that the arrangements made for his consecration could be used for Bharata's consecration and he would make his father a keeper of his word.
2.18.1
स ददर्शासने रामो
निषण्णं पितरं शुभे ।
कैकेयीसहितं दीनम्
मुखेन परिशुष्यता ॥
Rāma saw his father seated on an splendid couch along with Kaikēyee, looking miserable, his face parched with grief.
2.18.2
स पितुश्चरणौ पूर्वम्
अभिवाद्य विनीतवत् ।
ततो ववन्दे चरणौ
कैकेय्या स्सुसमाहितः ॥
With utmost humility and composure, he offered respects to his father's feet and then to the feet of Kaikēyee.
2.18.3
रामेत्युक्त्वा तु वचनम्
बाष्पपर्याकुलेक्षणः ।
शशाक नृपतिर्दीनो
नेक्षितुं नाभिभाषितुम् ॥
'O Rāma!' said he, with tears in his eyes. He could say no more, and was not able to even glance at him, overtaken by grief!
2.18.4
तदपूर्वं नरपतेः
दृष्ट्वा रूपं भयावहम् ।
रामोऽपि भयमापन्नः
पदा स्पृष्ट्वेव पन्नगम् ॥
Having never seen him in such a terrible shape, Rāma was seized with fear, like one who had stepped on a serpent.
2.18.5
इन्द्रियैरप्रहृष्टैस्तम्
शोकसन्तापकर्शितम् ।
निश्श्वसन्तं महाराजम्
व्यथिताकुलचेतसम् ।
ऊर्मिमालिनमक्षोभ्यम्
क्षुभ्यन्तमिव सागरम् ।
उपप्लुतमिवादित्यम्
उक्तानृतमृषिं यथा ॥
There is no sign of joy in any of his senses of that king of kings. He was wilting under the fire of grief and sighing deeply. His mind was perturbed by immense grief. Unshakable ocean that he was, he then convulsed like a ocean with a series of tides. He was like the sun eclipsed and like a Ṛshi who had uttered a lie.
2.18.7
अचिन्त्यकल्पं हि पितुः
तं शोकमुपधारयन् ।
बभूव संरब्धतर
स्समुद्र इव पर्वणि ॥
Thinking of that unfathomable grief of his father, he became agitated like the ocean on a full moon day.
2.18.8
चिन्तयामास च तदा
रामः पितृहिते रतः ।
किं स्विदद्यैव नृपतिः
न मां प्रत्यभिनन्दति ॥
Rāma, who wished all well for his father, was at his wit's end, in spite of being as discerning as he was, as to why the king did not greet him with delight as usual!
2.18.9
अन्यदा मां पिता दृष्ट्वा
कुपितोऽपि प्रसीदति ।
तस्य मामद्य संप्रेक्ष्य
किमायासः प्रवर्तते ॥
He used to regain his good cheer even if he was angry, on seeing me. Why is he feeling so sad seeing me today?
2.18.10
स दीन इव शोकार्तो
विषण्णवदनद्युतिः ।
कैकेयीमभिवाद्यैव
रामो वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Racked by grief, and feeling miserable, Rāma asked Kaikēyee with a sad, lack-luster face as he made his prostrations to her:
2.18.11
कच्चिन्मया नापराद्धम्
अज्ञानाद्येन मे पिता ।
कुपितस्तन्ममाचक्ष्व
त्वं चैवैनं प्रसादय ॥
Have I unwittingly offended my father? Please tell me why he is angry with me! You alone can make him become graceful towards me!
2.18.12
अप्रसन्नमनाः किन्नु
सदा मां प्रति वत्सलः ।
विवर्णवदनो दीनो
न हि मामभिभाषते ॥
He is always affectionate towards me. Why is he unhappy? Why is he not speaking? Why is he so depressed? Why is his face so pale?
2.18.13
शरीरो मानसो वापि
कच्चिदेनं न बाधते ।
सन्तापोवाऽभितापो वा
दुर्लभं हि सदा सुखम् ॥
Is he suffering from bodily pain or mental anguish? For it is not given to anyone to be all right all the time!
2.18.14
कच्चिन्न किञ्चिद्भरते
कुमारे प्रियदर्शने ।
शत्रुघ्ने वा महासत्त्वे
मात्रूणां वा ममाशुभम् ॥
I hope no misfortune has befallen on the pleasing Bharata or on the mighty strong Ṡatrughna or on any of my mothers!
2.18.15
अतोषयन्महाराजम्
अकुर्वन्वा पितुर्वचः ।
मुहूर्तमपि नेच्छेयम्
जीवितुं कुपिते नृपे ॥
If I were to make the lord of kings unhappy, or were to not fulfill the word of my father, or even to gain the displeasure of the king, I would not wish to live even for a moment!
2.18.16
यतोमूलं नरः पश्येत्
प्रादुर्भावमिहात्मनः ।
कथं तस्मिन्नवर्तेत
प्रत्यक्षे सति दैवते ॥
How else can any son be with one's father who is the very source of one's own existence and who is like a god right in front of the eyes?
2.18.17
कच्चित्ते परुषं किञ्चित्
अभिमानात्पिता मम ।
उक्तो भवत्या कोपेन
यत्रास्य लुलितं मनः ॥
I hope you have not spoken harshly to my father from the self-indulgence his love gives you, or in anger, wounding his feelings?
2.18.18
एतदाचक्ष्व मे देवि
तत्त्वेन परिपृच्छतः ।
किं निमित्तमपूर्वोयम्
विकारो मनुजाधिपे ॥
I ask you, O queen, tell me everything as it is! What is the reason for the ruler of the people to be disoriented like this, as never before?
2.18.19
एवमुक्ता तु कैकेयी
राघवेण महात्मना ।
उवाचेदं सुनिर्लज्जा
धृष्टमात्महितं वचः ॥
To that question of Rāghava, the Mahatma, Kaikēyee had the face to say these selfish words, without the slightest shame:
2.18.20
न राजा कुपितो राम
व्यसनं नास्य किञ्चन ।
किञ्चिन्मनोगतंत्वस्य
त्वद्भयान्नाभिभाषते ॥
O Rāma! Neither the king is angry nor is he suffering from any pain! He is just scared to say what is on his mind to you!
2.18.21
प्रियं त्वामप्रियं वक्तुम्
वाणी नास्योपवर्तते ।
तदवश्यं त्वया कार्यम्
यदनेनाश्रुतं मम ॥
His tongue is not cooperating to spell out words that are unkind to a person whom he loves. But you must do without fail what he has promised to me.
2.18.22
एष मह्यं वरं दत्त्वा
पुरा मामभिपूज्य च ।
स पश्चात्तप्यते राजा
यथाऽन्यः प्राकृतस्तथा ॥
This king, out of great regard, had given me a boon in the past. But now he repents for it like any other common creature.
2.18.23
अतिसृज्य ददानीति
वरं मम विशांपतिः ।
स निरर्धं गतजले
सेतुं बन्धितुमिच्छति ॥
Having promised me the boon, the king now futilely attempts to build a dam after the water has drained off.
2.18.24
धर्ममूलमिदं राम
विदितं च सतामपि ।
तत्सत्यं न त्यजेद्राजा
कुपितस्त्वत्कृते यथा ॥
Keeping one's word is the cornerstone of Dharma as any sane minded person can tell. I hope this angry king would not renege on his word because of you.
2.18.25
यदि तद्वक्ष्यते राजा
शुभं वा यदि वाऽशुभम् ।
करिष्यसि ततस्सर्वम्
आख्यास्यामि पुनस्त्वहम् ॥
If you are ready to do whatever the king would say, regardless of it being good or bad for you, I shall tell you everything in detail.
2.18.26
यदि त्वभिहितं राज्ञा
त्वयि तन्न विपत्स्यते ।
ततोऽहमभिधास्यामि
न ह्येष त्वयि वक्ष्यति ॥
If what the king would say will not go in vain, I shall tell it to you, for he would not be able to!
2.18.27
एतत्तु वचनं श्रुत्वा
कैकेय्या समुदाहृतम् ।
उवाच व्यथितो रामः
तां देवीं नृपसन्निधौ ॥
Hearing those well laid out words of Kaikēyee, the troubled Rāma responded to that lady, in the presence of the king, saying:
2.18.28
अहो धिङ्नार्हसे देवि
वक्तुं मामीदृशं वचः ।
अहं हि वचनाद्राज्ञः
पतेयमपि पावके ।
भक्ष्ययेयं विषं तीक्ष्णम्
मज्जेयमपि चार्णवे ।
नियुक्तो गुरुणा पित्रा
नृपेण च हितेन च ॥
What a pity, O Queen, that you speak like this! I will even jump into fire to honor the word of the king! I will consume deadly poison or drown in the ocean, if I were commanded to do so by the king, who is my respected father and well wisher!
2.18.30
तद्ब्रूहि वचनं देवि
राज्ञो यदभिकाङ्क्षितम् ।
करिष्ये प्रतिजाने च
रामो द्विर्नाभिभाषते ॥
Hence, O queen, please tell me what the king wants! I promise to fulfill it! Rāma does not speak in two voices!
2.18.31
तमार्जवसमायुक्तम्
अनार्या सत्यवादिनम् ।
उवाच रामं कैकेयी
वचनं भृशदारुणम् ॥
The ignoble Kaikēyee then spoke these utterly ruthless words to Rāma, who was honest to the core and unwavering adherent of truth:
2.18.32
पुरा दैवासुरे युद्धे
पित्रा ते मम राघव ।
रक्षितेन वरौ दत्तौ
सशल्येन महारणे ॥
Once upon a time, O Rāghava, in the dreadful battle between Dēvas and Asuras, your father, who was badly hit by arrows was protected by me and he then gave me two boons!
2.18.33
तत्र मे याचितो राजा
भरतस्याभिषेचनम् ।
गमनं दण्डकारण्ये
तव चाद्यैव राघव ॥
Hence, O Rāghava, I asked the king for the consecration of Bharata and your exile to the forest of Daṇḍaka right away!
2.18.34
यदि सत्यप्रतिज्ञं त्वम्
पितरं कर्तुमिच्छसि ।
आत्मानं च नरश्रेष्ठ
मम वाक्यमिदं शृणु ॥
If you want to make yourself and your father the keepers of your words, O best among men, listen to my words:
2.18.35
सन्निदेशे पितुस्तिष्ठ
यथा तेन प्रतिश्रुतम् ।
त्वयाऽरण्यं प्रवेष्टव्यम्
नव वर्षाणि पञ्च च ॥
Abide by your father's command! In accordance with his promise, go to the forest for nine and five years!
2.18.36
भरतस्त्वभिषिच्येत
यदेतदभिषेचनम् ।
त्वदर्थे विहितं राज्ञा
तेन सर्वेण राघव ॥
May everything that is arranged by the king for your consecration be used for the consecration of Bharata!
2.18.37
सप्त सप्त च वर्षाणि
दण्डकारण्यमाश्रितः ।
अभिषेकमिमं त्यत्त्वा
जटाजिनधरो वस ॥
Forsaking this consecration, may you live in Daṇḍaka forest for seven and seven years with hair unkempt and clad in deer-skin!
2.18.38
भरतः कोसलपुरे
प्रशास्तु वसुधामिमाम् ।
नानारत्न समाकीर्णाम्
सवाजिरथकुञ्जराम् ॥
May Bharata rule this earth that is splendid with all varieties of gems and full of horses, chariots and elephants, from this city of the kingdom of Kōsala!
2.18.39
एतेन त्वां नरेन्द्रोऽयम्
कारुण्येन समाप्लुतः ।
शोकसंक्लिष्ट वदनो
न शक्नोति निरीक्षितुम् ॥
That is the reason why this lord of peoples is feeling pity for you and his face is filled with grief and he is unable to look at you straight!
2.18.40
एतत्कुरु नरेन्द्रस्य
वचनं रघुनन्दन ।
सत्येन महता राम
तारयस्व नरेश्वरम् ॥
Carry out the King's promise, O scion of the Raghu dynasty, and help the lord of men, O Rāma, to redeem himself by such exemplary adherence to truth!
2.18.41
इतीव तस्यां परुषं वदन्त्यां
न चैव रामः प्रविवेश शोकम् ।
प्रविव्यथे चापि महानुभावो
राजा तु पुत्रव्यसनाभितप्तः ॥
Such terrible words uttered by her could not make Rāma succumb to any sort of grief, but the noble king suffered a lot, agonized by the misfortune that had befallen on his son.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे अष्टादशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighteenth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
