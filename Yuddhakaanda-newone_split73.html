Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 73
In this Sarga, Indrajit consoles Rāvaṇa who is filled with grief over the death of his sons and brothers. He offers to go to the battle himself, and slay Rāma along with Lakshmaṇa and destroy the Vānara army.
He accordingly goes to the battle accompanied by formidable Rākshasa forces. Indrajit infuses the power of Brahma's Astra into all the weapons. Indrajit and the Rākshasas fight the battle with a zeal for victory, hacking the Vānaras with the weapons powered by Brahma's Astra. The Vānaras try to put forth a concerted counterattack, but in vain.
Indrajit then strikes every Vānara chief, like Hanumān, Sugreeva, Aṅgada and Dwivida with multiples of arrows, and pulverizes the entire Vānara army with a flood of arrows, throwing them into a turmoil. He then makes himself invisible with magical powers and stations himself in the sky, from where he continues to send the downpour of arrows, spears and other weapons. He then sends a torrent of arrows on to Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
Rāma tells Lakshmaṇa that they should yield to the power of the Brahma's Astra. Rāma and Lakshmaṇa fall senseless. Securing that victory, Indrajit heads back to Laṅkā.
6.73.1
ततो हतान् राक्षसपुङ्गवांस्तान्
देवान्तकादित्रिशिरोतिकायान् ।
रक्षोगणास्तत्र हतावशिष्टाः
ते रावणाय त्वरितं शशंसुः ॥
The hordes of Rākshasas who were left alive went quickly to Rāvaṇa and reported the death of Dēvāntaka, Triṡira, Atikāya, the bulls among the Rākshasas.
6.73.2
ततो हतांस्तान् सहसा निशम्य
राजा मुमोहाश्रुपरिप्लुताक्षः ।
पुत्रक्षयं भ्रातृवधं च घोरम्
विचिन्त्य राजा विपुलं प्रदध्यौ ॥
As soon as he heard they were all slain, the king fainted, his eyes brimming with tears. Thinking of the terrible loss of his sons and the death of his brothers, the king pondered over it for long.
6.73.3
ततस्तु राजानमुदीक्ष्य दीनम्
शोकार्णवे सम्परिपुप्लुवानम् ।
रथर्षभो राक्षसराजसूनुः
तमिन्द्रजिद्वाक्यमिदं बभाषे ॥
Then Indrajit, a bull among charioters and the son of the Rākshasa King, addressing the king who was woebegone and drowned completely in a ocean of grief, said these words:
6.73.4
न तात मोहं प्रतिगन्तुमर्हसि
यत्रेन्द्रजिज्जीवति नैरृतेश ।
नेन्द्रारिबाणाभिहतो हि कश्चित्
प्राणान् समर्थः समरे ऽभिपातुम् ॥
O king of Rākshasas! You do not have to feel so miserable as long as Indrajit is alive, for no one can escape with his life struck by the arrows of this foe of Indra.
6.73.5
पश्याद्य रामं सह लक्ष्मणेन
मद्बाणनिर्भिन्नविकीर्णदेहम् ।
गतायुषं भूमितले शयानम्
शितैः शरैराचितसर्वगात्रम् ॥
This very day you shall see Rāma, along with Lakshmaṇa, lie dead on the ground, with their bodies torn to pieces by my arrows, and sharp arrows sticking on every limb of theirs.
6.73.6
इमां प्रतिज्ञां शृणु शक्रशत्रोः
सुनिश्चितां पौरुषदैवयुक्ताम् ।
अद्यैव रामं सह लक्ष्मणेन
सन्तर्पयिष्यामि शरैरमोघैः ।
अद्येन्द्रवैवस्वतविष्णुरुद्र
साध्याश्च वैश्वानरचन्द्रसूर्याः ।
द्रक्ष्यन्ति मे विक्रममप्रमेयं
विष्णोरिवोग्रं बलियज्ञवाटे ॥
Hear me, the foe of Indra, take this vow in all earnestness:
'I swear by all the Dēvas and by my own valor that I shall drown Rāma and Lakshmaṇa with a surfeit of arrows that never go in vain.
Indra, Vaivaswata, Vishṇu, Rudra, the Sādhyas, Vaiṡwānara, Candra and Surya shall see my measureless prowess recalling the fierce prowess of Vishṇu on the Yajña grounds of Bali.'
6.73.8
स एवमुक्त्वा त्रिदशेन्द्रशत्रुः
आपृच्छ्य राजानमदीनसत्त्वः ।
समारुरोहानिलतुल्यवेगम्
रथं खरश्रेष्ठसमाधियुक्तम् ॥
Having said this, the foe of Indra of undamped energy, took leave of the king and got into his chariot that could fly as fast as the wind, with the best of the mules yoked to it.
6.73.9
तमास्थाय महातेजा
रथं हरिरथोपमम् ।
जगाम सहसा तत्र
यत्र युद्धमरिन्दमः ॥
Getting into that chariot which was comparable to the chariot of the sun, that subduer of foe quickly proceeded to the battlefield.
6.73.10
तं प्रस्थितं महात्मानम्
अनुजग्मुर्महाबलाः ।
संहर्षमाणा बहवो
धनुःप्रवरपाणयः ॥
As that Mahātma proceeded thus, many (Rākshasas) of immense strength followed him with renewed enthusiasm, holding the best of bows in their hands.
6.73.11
गजस्कन्धगताः केचित्
केचित् प्रवरवाजिभिः ।
व्याघ्रवृश्चिकमार्जार
खरोष्ट्रैश्च भुजंगमैः ।
वराहैः श्वापदैः सिंहैः
जम्बुकैः पर्वतोपमैः ।
काकहंस मयूरैश्च
राक्षसा भीम विक्रमाः ।
प्रासमुद्गरनिस्त्रिंश
परश्वधगदाधराः ।
भुशुण्डिमुद्गरायष्टि
शतघ्नीपरिघायुधाः ॥
Some followed riding on elephants, some on the best of the horses, some on tigers, deer, cats, donkeys, camel, serpents, boars, lions, foxes that were huge like mountains, and others on crows, swans and peacocks.
The Rākshasas of terrific prowess armed themselves with Prāsas, Mudgaras, swords, battle-axes, maces, Bhujangas, Yashṭis, Ṡataghnis and Parighas.
6.73.14
स शङ्खनिनदैः पूर्णैः
भेरीणां चापि निस्वनैः ।
जगाम त्रिदशेन्द्रारिः
स्तूयमानो निशाचरैः ॥
Accompanied by the reverberating sounds of blare of conches and blast of kettledrums, the foe of Indra proceeded, with the Night-rovers singing his praises.
6.73.15
स शङ्खशशिवर्णेन
छत्रेण रिपुसूदनः ।
रराज प्रतिपूर्णेन
नभश्चान्द्रमसा यथा ॥
With an umbrella that was white like the conch and the moon, that destroyer of the foe shone like the full moon in the sky.
6.73.16
अवीज्यत ततो वीरो
हैमैर्हेमविभूषितैः ।
चारुचामरमुख्यैश्च
मुख्यः सर्वधनुष्मताम् ॥
The valiant one, foremost of bowmen, was fanned by the best of fly-whisks that were beautiful with golden handles and decorated with gold ornaments.
6.73.17
स तु दृष्ट्वा विनिर्यान्तम्
बलेन महतावृतम् ।
राक्षसाधिपतिः श्रीमान्
रावणः पुत्रमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing his son ready to leave supported by a great army, Rāvaṇa, the blessed lord of Rākshasas, said to him:
6.73.18
त्वमप्रतिरथः पुत्र
त्वया वै वासवो जितः ।
किंपुनर्मानुषं धृष्यम्
निहनिष्यसि राघवम् ॥
There is no charioter, my son, who can stand against you. Even Vāsava was vanquished by you, what to speak of a human, however invincible he is! You will certainly destroy Rāghava.
6.73.19
ततस्त्विन्द्रजिता लङ्का
सूर्यप्रतिमतेजसा ।
रराजाप्रतिवीर्येण
द्यौरिवार्केण भास्वता ॥
At that moment, Laṅkā shone with the sun-like splendor of Indrajit who had unchallenged valor, as the sky does with the splendid sun.
6.73.20
स सम्प्राप्य महातेजा
युद्धभूमिमरिन्दमः ।
स्थापयामास रक्षांसि
रथं प्रति समन्ततः ॥
Reaching the battlefield, that subduer of the foe of extraordinary power, stationed the Rākshasas all around his chariot.
6.73.21
रथेनाश्वयुजा वीरः
शीघ्रं गत्वा निकुम्भिलाम् ।
स संप्राप्य महातेजा
यज्ञभूमिमरिंदमः ॥
Then that Veera, the subduer of the foe, of extraordinary power, went to Nikumbhila, the Yajña grounds, on his chariot that was yoked with horses.
This block of ten Ṡlōkas, which describes the black magic performed by Indrajit and his disappearance into the sky seems to be an insert from a different palm-leaf version. The flow of the narration is smoother without this episode.
6.73.22
ततस्तु हुतभोक्तारम्
हुतभुक्सदृशप्रभः ।
जुहुवे राक्षसश्रेष्ठः
विधिवन्मत्रसत्तमैः ॥
Then that best of the Rākshasas, who shone with the splendor of fire offered oblations to the fire, the recipient of oblations, in a customary manner, chanting of highly significant Mantras.
6.73.23
स हविर्लाजसंस्कारैः
माल्यगन्धपुरस्कृतैः ।
जुहुवे पावकं तत्र
राक्षसेन्द्रः प्रतापवान् ॥
The fiery chief of the Rākshasas performed Yajña, offering oblations mixed with puffed rice into the fire, the fire pit decorated with flowers and treated with perfumes.
6.73.24
शस्त्राणि शरपत्राणि
समिधोऽथ विभीतकाः ।
लोहितानि च वासांसि
स्रुवं कार्ष्णायसं तथा ॥
He used red-colored clothes, twigs of Vibheetaka, ladles made of iron and weapons in place of Ṡarapatras.
Ṡarapatras, Vibheetaka twigs and ladles made of iron are used in black magic rituals.
6.73.25
स तत्राग्निं समास्तीर्य
शरपत्रैः सतोमरैः ।
छागस्य कृष्णवर्णस्य
गलं जग्राह जीवतः ॥
Placing Ṡarapatras and Tōmaras around the fire, he offered the head of a live black sheep (as an oblation).
6.73.26
सकृदेव समिद्धस्य
विधूमस्य महार्चिषः ।
बभूवुस्तानि लिङ्गानि
विजयं यान्यदर्शयन् ॥
Then there arose suddenly bright smokeless tongues of flame from that great fire, serving as an omen presaging victory.
6.73.27
प्रदक्षिणावर्तशिखः
तप्तकाञ्चनसन्निभः ।
हविस्तत् प्रतिजग्राह
पावकः स्वयमुत्थितः ॥
The fire that dazzled like molten gold rose up and received that oblation with its tongues of flame that whirled in clockwise direction.
6.73.28
सोऽस्त्रमाहारयामास
ब्राह्ममस्त्रविशारदः ।
धनुश्चात्मरथं चैव
सर्वं तत्राभ्यमन्त्रयत् ॥
He, a savant of Astras, then invoked the power of Brahma's Astra and and chanted Mantras, infusing it on to his bow, his chariot and everything else.
6.73.29
तस्मिन्नाहूयमानेऽस्त्रे
हूयमाने च पावके ।
सार्कग्रहेन्दुनक्षत्रम्
वितत्रास नभःस्थलम् ॥
As that Astra was invoked and as oblations were offered to the fire, the entire sky, along with the sun, the moon, the planets and the stars, trembled.
6.73.30
स पावकं पावकदीप्ततेजा
हुत्वा महेन्द्रप्रतिमप्रभावः ।
सचापबाणासिरथाश्वसूतः
खेऽन्तर्दधेत्मानमचिन्त्यवीर्यः ॥
After the oblations were thus offered into the fire, he, who was blazing like the glow of the fire with valor that was difficult even to conceive and was powerful like Mahēndra, disappeared into the sky, along with his bow, arrows, sword, chariot, horses and charioteer.
6.73.31
ततो हयरथाकीर्णम्
पताकाध्वजशोभितम् ।
निर्ययौ राक्षसबलम्
नर्दमानं युयुत्सया ॥
The Rākshasa army then marched, eager for the fray, letting off strident shouts, with its multitudes of horses and chariots adorned with banners and buntings.
6.73.32
ते शरैर्बहुभिश्चित्रैः
तीक्ष्णवेगैरलङ्कृतैः ।
तोमरैरङ्कुशैश्चापि
वानरान् जघ्नुराहवे ॥
They slew the Vānaras with many sharp, assorted and bedecked arrows of great speed as well as with Tōmaras and goads.
6.73.33
रावणिस्तु ततः क्रुद्धः
तान्निरीक्ष्य निशाचरान् ।
हृष्टा भवन्तो युद्ध्यन्तु
वानराणां जिघांसया ॥
Rāvaṇa's son, in wrath, looked at the Night-rovers (and shouted), 'Fight with all fervor, with zeal to kill the Vānaras!'
6.73.34
ततस्ते राक्षसाः सर्वे
नर्दन्तो जयकाङ्क्षिणः ।
अभ्यवर्षंस्ततो घोरम्
वानरान् शरवृष्टिभिः ॥
Then, all the Rākshasas, shouting, with a zeal for victory, showered a rain of dread arrows on the Vānaras.
6.73.35
स तु नालीकनाराचैः
गदाभिर्मुसलैरपि ।
रक्षोभिः संवृतः सङ्ख्ये
वानरान् विचकर्त ह ॥
With the Rākshasas around him supporting, he hacked the Vānaras with Nāleeka and Nārāca arrows, and also with mace and pestle, in that battle.
6.73.36
ते वध्यमानाः समरे
वानराः पादपायुधाः ।
अभ्यद्रवन्त सहिता
रावणिं रणकर्कशम् ॥
Thus being slaughtered by him in the fight, the Vānaras, grabbing trees for weapons, mounted a concerted attack on the son of Rāvaṇa, a ruthless one in battle.
6.73.37
इन्द्रजित्तु ततः क्रुद्धो
महातेजा महाबलः ।
वानराणां शरीराणि
व्यधमद्रावणात्मजः ॥
Then, the incensed Indrajit, son of Rāvaṇa, of immense strength and extraordinary power, hacked the bodies of the Vānaras.
6.73.38
शरेणैकेन च हरीन्
नव पञ्च च सप्त च ।
चिच्छेद समरे क्रुद्धो
राक्षसान् सम्प्रहर्षयन् ॥
In his rage, he cut down the Vānaras five to seven to nine of them at a time with a single arrow in that battle, generating cheer among the Rākshasas.
6.73.39
स शरैः सूर्यसङ्काशैः
शातकुम्भविभूषितैः ।
वानरान् समरे वीरः
प्रममाथ सुदुर्जयः ॥
He, the valiant and invincible one, pounded the Vānaras with his arrows that were decked with gold and dazzling like the sun.
6.73.40
ते भिन्नगात्राः समरे
वानराः शरपीडिताः ।
पेतुर्मथितसङ्कल्पाः
सुरैरिव महासुराः ॥
With their bodies mangled by the arrows, their limbs broken in that battle, the Vānaras fell, like the great Asuras whose will was battered by the Suras.
6.73.41
तं तपन्तमिवादित्यम्
घोरैर्बाणगभस्तिभिः ।
अभ्यधावन्त सङ्क्रुद्धाः
संयुगे वानरर्षभाः ॥
As he made advances towards them burning them with his terrible arrows like the sun does with his rays, the bulls among Vānaras were enraged and dashed towards him.
6.73.42
ततस्तु वानराः सर्वे
भिन्नदेहा विचेतसः ।
व्यथिता विद्रवन्ति स्म
रुधिरेण समुक्षिताः ॥
But then, their bodies wounded badly and covered with blood, and their senses lost, all the Vānaras fled in pain.
6.73.43
रामस्यार्थे पराक्रम्य
वानरास्त्यक्तजीविताः ।
नर्दन्तस्तेऽनिवृत्तास्तु
समरे सशिलायुधाः ॥
But ready to give up their lives for the cause of Rāma, the Vānaras, without turning their backs, let off great shouts in that battle arming themselves with rocks.
6.73.44
ते द्रुमैः पर्वताग्रैश्च
शिलाभिश्च प्लवङ्गमाः ।
अभ्यवर्षन्त समरे
रावणिं समवस्थिताः ॥
Making a determined stand, the Vānaras rained trees, mountain crests and rocks on the son of Rāvaṇa, in that battle.
6.73.45
तद्द्रुमाणां शिलानां च
वर्षं प्राणहरं महत् ।
व्यपोहत महातेजा
रावणिः समितिञ्जयः ॥
The extraordinarily powerful son of Rāvaṇa, who was ever victorious in battle, warded off that murderous shower of rocks and trees (with ease).
6.73.46
ततः पावकसङ्काशैः
शरैराशीविषोपमैः ।
वानराणामनीकानि
बिभेद समरे प्रभुः ॥
And the all powerful one tore the Vānara forces in that battle with arrows that burned like fire and bit like venomous serpents.
6.73.47
अष्टादशशरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
स विद्ध्वा गन्धमादनम् ।
विव्याध नवभिश्चैव
नलं दूरादवस्थितम् ॥
He struck Gandhamādana with eighteen sharp arrows, and assailed Nala who was farther away, with nine.
6.73.48
सप्तभिस्तु महा वीर्यो
मैन्दं मर्मविदारणैः ।
पञ्चभिर्विशिखैश्चैव
गजं विव्याध संयुगे ।
जाम्बवन्तं तु दशभिः
नीलं त्रिंशद्भिरेव च ॥
And he of extraordinary valor struck Mainda with seven that tore his vitals apart, and Gaja with five arrows in that battle, and then Jāmbavān with ten and Neela with thirty.
6.73.49
सुग्रीवमृषभं चैव
सोऽङ्गदं द्विविदं तथा ।
घोरैर्दत्तवरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
निष्प्राणानकरोत्तदा ॥
With sharp, dread arrows that were infused with the power from boons, he struck Sugreeva, Aṅgada and Dwivida, almost taking the life out of them.
6.73.50
अन्यानपि तदा मुख्यान्
वानरान् बहुभिः शरैः ।
अर्दयामास सङ्क्रुद्धः
कालाग्निरिव मूर्च्छितः ॥
Beside himself with rage, he pounded many other great Vānara chiefs with his arrows, like the Fire of Dissolution would.
6.73.51
स शरैः सूर्यसङ्काशैः
सुमुक्तैः शीघ्रगामिभिः ।
वानराणामनीकानि
निर्ममन्थ महारणे ॥
He pulverized the armies of the Vānaras in that fierce battle, with arrows of sun-like luster that were well shot and flew fast.
6.73.52
आकुलां वानरीं सेनाम्
शरजालेन मोहिताम् ।
हृष्टः स परया प्रीत्या
ददर्श क्षतजोक्षिताम् ॥
With supreme delight he saw the Vānara army thrown into a turmoil, drenched with blood and numbed into insensibility by that torrential pour of arrows.
6.73.53
पुनरेव महातेजा
राक्षसेन्द्रात्मजो बली ।
संसृज्य बाणवर्षं च
शस्त्रवर्षं च दारुणम् ।
ममर्द वानरानीकम्
परितस्त्विन्द्रजित्बली ॥
Again, Indrajit of immense power, the strong son of the Rākshasa King, pounded the Vānara forces with a downpour of arrows and other terrible weapons.
6.73.55
स्वसैन्यमुत्सृज्य समेत्य तूर्णम्
महारणे वानरवाहिनीषु ।
अदृश्यमानः शरजालमुग्रं
ववर्ष नीलाम्बुधरो यथाऽम्बु ॥
Then making himself invisible, he left his army and quickly went into the midst of the Vānara forces, and rained a fierce torrent of arrows like a black cloud discharging waters.
6.73.56
ते शक्रजिद्बाणविशीर्णदेहा
मायाहता विस्वरमुन्नदन्तः ।
रणे निपेतुर्हरयोऽद्रिकल्पा
यथेन्द्रवज्राभिहता नगेन्द्राः ॥
Their bodies mauled by the arrows of the vanquisher of Ṡakra, uttering discordant cries and struck by his magical powers, the mountainous Vānaras fell in that battle, like the kings of the mountains struck by the Vajra of Indra.
6.73.57
ते केवलं सन्ददृशुः शिताग्रान्
बाणान् रणे वानरवाहिनीषु ।
मायानिगूढं तु सुरेन्द्रशत्रुम्
न चात्र तं राक्षसमप्यपश्यन् ॥
They could only see the sharp arrows fall on the Vānara forces in that battle; they could not see the Rākshasa, the foe of the lord of the Suras, who hid himself with his magical powers.
6.73.58
ततः स रक्षोधिपतिर्महात्मा
सर्वा दिशो बाणशतैः शिताग्रैः ।
प्रच्छादयामास रविप्रकाशैः
विदारयामास च वानरेन्द्रान् ॥
Then that Mahātma, the lord of Rākshasas, filled all the cardinals with hundreds of sharp-tipped arrows that shone like the sun, tearing apart the Vānara chiefs.
6.73.59
स शूलनिस्त्रिंशपरश्वधानि
व्याविध्य दीप्तानलसप्रभाणि ।
सविस्फुलिङ्गोज्ज्वलपावकानि
ववर्ष तीर्व्रं प्लवगेन्द्रसैन्ये ॥
Then he rained on the Vānara army a torrent of spears, swords and hatchets that dazzled like raging fire, and fell like blazing fire, emitting sparks.
6.73.60
ततो ज्वलनसङ्काशैः
शरैर्वानरयूथपाः ।
ताडिताः शक्रजिद्बाणैः
प्रफुल्ला इव किंशुकाः ॥
Struck by the arrows of the conqueror of Ṡakra that burned them like fire, the Vānara captains looked like Kiṃṡuka trees in flower, (their bodies drenched in blood).
6.73.61
तेऽन्योन्यमभिसर्पन्तो
निनदन्तश्च विस्वरम् ।
राक्षसेन्द्रास्त्रनिर्भिन्ना
निपेतुर्वानरर्षभाः ॥
They went to each other (for comfort) and wailed in discordant notes. Hacked by the Astras of the Rākshasa chief, the bulls among Vānaras tumbled to the ground.
6.73.62
उदीक्षमाणा गगनम्
केचिन्नेत्रेषु ताडिताः ।
शरैर्विविशुरन्योन्यम्
पेतुश्च धरणीतले ॥
Some, looking up at the sky were hit in the eye by the arrows. They tried to shield themselves beneath each other and lay low on the ground.
6.73.63
हनुमन्तं च सुग्रीवम्
अङ्गदं गन्धमादनम् ।
जाम्बवन्तं सुषेणं च
वेगदर्शिनमेव च ।
मैन्दं च द्विविदं नीलम्
गवाक्षं गजगोमुखौ ।
केसरिं हरिलोमानम्
विद्युदृंष्ट्रं च वानरम् ।
सूर्याननं ज्योतिमुखम्
तथा दधिमुखं हरिम् ।
पावकाक्षं नलं चैव
कुमुदं चैव वानरम् ।
प्रासैः शूलैः शितैर्बाणैः
इन्द्रजिन्मन्त्रसंहितैः ।
विव्याध हरिशार्दूलान्
सर्वांस्तान् राक्षसोत्तमः ॥
That foremost of Rākshasas hit all the Vānaras including Hanumān, Sugreeva, Aṅgada, Gandhamādana, Jāmbavān, Sushēṇa, Vēgadarsi, Mainda, Dwivida, Neela, Gavāksha, Gavaya, Kēsari, Harilōma, and Vidyuddaṃshṭra, Suryānana, Jyōtirmukha, Dadhimukha, Pāvakāksha, Nala and Kumuda with Prāsas, spears, and sharp arrows that were invoked with the powers of Astras.
6.73.67
स वै गदाभिर्हरियूथमुख्यान्
निर्भिद्य बाणैस्तपनीयपुङ्खैः ।
ववर्ष रामं शरवृष्टिजालैः
सलक्ष्मणं भास्कररश्मिकल्पैः ॥
After hacking the chiefs of the Vānara battalions with maces and arrows with gold rings, he rained a downpour of arrows that fell like the rays of the sun (without a gap) on Rāma and also on Lakshmaṇa.
6.73.68
स बाणवर्षैरभिवर्ष्यमाणो
धारानिपातानिव तानचिन्त्य ।
समीक्षमाणः परमाद्भुतश्री
रामस्तदा लक्ष्मणमित्युवाच ॥
Drenched in the downpour of those arrows, but caring about them no more seriously than one would a torrential rain, Rāma of extremely marvelous charm, said this to Lakshmaṇa:
6.73.69
असौ पुनर्लक्ष्मण राक्षसेन्द्रो
ब्रह्मास्त्रमाश्रित्य सुरेन्द्रशत्रुः ।
निपातयित्वा हरिसैन्यमस्मान्
शितैः शरैरर्दयति प्रसक्तम् ॥
This Rākshasa chief, a foe of the lord of Suras, is striking down our Vānara forces and battering them incessantly with sharp arrows relying on the power of Brahma's Astra.
6.73.70
स्वयम्भुवा दत्तवरो महात्मा
खमास्थितोऽन्तर्हितभीमकायः ।
कथं नु शक्यो युधि नष्टदेहो
निहन्तुमद्येन्द्रजिदुद्यतास्त्रः ॥
This Mahātma has stationed himself in the sky and made his humongous body invisible with the power of the boons granted by the Self-born. How can he, Indrajit, be slain in the battle, as he makes himself bodiless, yet wielding every weapon?
6.73.71
मन्ये स्वयम्भूर्भगवानचिन्त्यो
यस्यैतदस्त्रं प्रभवश्च योऽस्य ।
बाणावपातांस्त्वमिहाद्य धीमन्
मया सहाव्यग्रमनाः सहस्व ॥
I think it is impossible to imagine the (greatness of the) Bhagawān, the Self-born, to whom this Astra belongs and from whom its power emerges. Therefore, O sagacious one, you have to put up with this downpour of arrows along with me, keeping your composure.
6.73.72
प्रच्छादयत्येष हि राक्षसेन्द्रः
सर्वादिशः सायकवृष्टिजालैः ।
एतच्च सर्वं पतिताग्र्यशूरम्
न भ्राजते वानरराजसैन्यम् ॥
This foremost of Rākshasas has filled all the cardinals with the downpour of his arrows. The mood of the army of the Vānara king is subdued, with all its warriors fallen.
6.73.73
आवां तु दृष्ट्वा पतितौ विसञ्ज्ञौ
निवृत्तयुद्धौ गतरोषहर्षो ।
ध्रुवं प्रवेक्ष्यत्यमरारिवासम्
असौ समादाय रणाग्रलक्ष्मीम् ॥
Seeing us retreat from the fight and fall senseless with no sign of joy or wrath, he will certainly retreat to the abode of the foe of Dēvas, securing his first victory in the battle.
6.73.74
ततस्तु ताविन्द्रजिदस्त्रजालैः
बभूवतुस्तत्र तथा विशस्तौ ।
स चापि तौ तत्र विदर्शयित्वा
ननाद हर्षाद्युधि राक्षसेन्द्रः ॥
Both of them then fell, struck down by the torrent of the Astras of Indrajit. Seeing them in that state, the foremost of the Rākshasas let off joyous shouts in that battle.
6.73.75
स तत्तदा वानरसैन्यमेवम्
रामं च सङ्ख्ये सह लक्ष्मणेन ।
विषादयित्वा सहसा विवेश
पुरीं दशग्रीवभुजाभिगुप्ताम् ॥
Having thus brought sorrow in that battle to the Vānara army as well as to Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, he quickly went back to the city protected by the strong arm of the Ten-necked.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे त्रिसप्ततितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventy third Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
