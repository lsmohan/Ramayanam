Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 21
In this Sarga, Kousalyā and Lakshmaṇa try to dissuade Rāma from his decision to go to the Vana to fulfill his father's promise to Kaikēyee.
Lakshmaṇa speaks in extreme language saying that he would kill anyone that comes in the way of Rāma's consecration. He even suggests that his father should be imprisoned or killed if he resists the crowning of Rāma. Kousalyā argues that Dharma demands that he should stay back and serve the mother.
Rāma explains why he should follow through his father's promise in accordance with Dharma. He urges Lakshmaṇa and Kousalyā to understand his point of view. He seeks the permission and blessings from his mother to leave to the Vana.
The many Sargas hitherto, starting from Sarga 7, were filled with high degree of emotion. In this Sarga, reason and discernment takes the front stage, in the words of Rāma, as he defends his decision to fulfill the words of his father.
In the many Sargas to come, we will see the challenges faced by a leader in defending his tough decision.
2.21.1
तथा तु विलपन्तीं ताम्
कौसल्यां राममातरम् ।
उवाच लक्ष्मणो दीनः
तत्कालसदृशं वचः ॥
To that crying Kousalyā, the mother of Rāma, Lakshmaṇa, feeling sad himself, told these apt words:
2.21.2
न रोचते ममाप्येतत्
आर्ये यद्राघवो वनम् ।
त्यक्त्वा राज्यश्रियं गच्छेत्
स्त्रिया वाक्यवशं गतः ॥
O venerable one! I too do not like the thought of Rāghava going to Vana and giving up the luxury of the kingdom based on the words of a woman!
Every charismatic hero, virtuous or otherwise, enjoys the support of constituencies of people that are enchanted by him. Those people make his support base.
They live and die for their hero. Their focus is on the Artha (advancement or gain) of their hero, even if it means keeping aside the principles of Dharma.
In this story of Rāmāyaṇa, Lakshmaṇa serves as a symbol of that support base.
The drama between a leader and his support base, with all its nuances is illustrated throughout Rāmāyaṇa through the interaction between the characters of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
A leader is more than a hero. He does not let his support base set the direction. Rather he carries them with him, in the direction that he feels is right.
Keeping in mind that Lakshmaṇa is a symbol of that support base helps in interpreting many events in the Rāmāyaṇa aptly.

2.21.3
विपरीतश्च वृद्धश्च
विषयैश्च प्रधर्षितः ।
नृपः किमिव न ब्रूयात्
चोद्यमानस्समन्मथः ॥
What would an aged and perverted king not say when incited, out of indulgence and under the entrancement of lust?
2.21.4
नास्यापराधं पश्यामि
नापि दोषं तथाविधम् ।
येन निर्वास्यते राष्ट्रात्
वनवासाय राघवः ॥
I see neither a fault in Rāghava, nor an offense committed by him that warrants his banishment from the kingdom to live in the Vana!
2.21.5
न तं पश्याम्यहं लोके
परोक्षमपि यो नरः ।
स्वमित्रोऽपि निरस्तोऽपि
योऽस्य दोषमुदाहरेत् ॥
I see no one in this world who find fault with him even in private, even among those who earned his enmity or displeasure!
2.21.6
देवकल्पमृजुं दान्तम्
रिपूणामपि वत्सलम् ।
अवेक्षमाणः को धर्मम्
त्यजेत्पुत्रमकारणात् ॥
How can anyone in his good sense of Dharma abandon his upright, calm and collected son, who is comparable to Dēvas and who is kind even to his enemies?
Dharma is so nuanced that anyone can claim it to be on their side of the argument.
2.21.7
तदिदं वचनं राज्ञः
पुनर्बाल्यमुपेयुषः ।
पुत्रः को हृदये कुर्यात्
राजवृत्तमनुस्मरन् ॥
The king seemed to have slipped into his childhood again! Which son, who is so conversant with statecraft, would take the words of such king to heart?
2.21.8
यावदेव न जानाति
कश्चिदर्थमिमं नरः ।
तावदेव मया सार्धम्
आत्मस्थं कुरु शासनम् ॥
Before anyone knows about this plan, may you take hold of the power, and you have my full support!
This is one of the possibilities what Daṡaratha wished, in his desperation. See Ṡlōka  2.12.86b-87a.
2.21.9
मया पार्श्वे सधनुषा
तव गुप्तस्य राघव ।
क स्समर्थोऽधिकं कर्तुम्
कृतान्तस्येव तिष्ठतः ॥
No one can dare commit excesses against you, O Rāghava, when you stand up like Yama, while I protect you standing on your side, with a bow in my hand!
2.21.10
निर्मनुष्यामिमां कृत्स्नाम्
अयोध्यां मनुजर्षभ ।
करिष्यामि शरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
यदि स्थास्यति विप्रिये ॥
O bull among men! I can decimate the entire Ayōdhyā with my sharp arrows with no one left alive, in case it turns hostile to you!
2.21.11
भरतस्याथ पक्ष्यो वा
यो वाऽस्य हितमिच्छति ।
सर्वानेतान्वधिष्यामि
मृदुर्हि परिभूयते ॥
I shall slay everyone who sides or sympathizes with Bharata. For, the soft hearted would be trampled upon!
2.21.12
प्रोत्साहितोऽयं कैकेय्या
स दुष्टो यदि नः पिता ।
अमित्रभूतो निस्सङ्गम्
वध्यतां बध्यतामपि ॥
If our father, instigated by Kaikēyee, were to turn evil and against you, he should be imprisoned or even killed, dispassionately!
2.21.13
गुरोरप्यवलिप्तस्य
कार्याकार्यमजानतः ।
उत्पथं प्रतिपन्नस्य
कार्यं भवति शासनम् ॥
Even elders must be restrained if they succumb to egotism, or lose the sense of right and wrong and tread the wrong path!
2.21.14
बलमेष किमाश्रित्य
हेतुं वापुरुषर्षभ ।
दातुमिच्छति कैकेय्यै
राज्यं स्थितमिदं तव ॥
What reason or power can he can rely on, O bull among men, to give away this kingdom that belongs to you, to Kaikēyee!
2.21.15
त्वया चैव मया चैव
कृत्वा वैरमनुत्तमम् ।
काऽस्य शक्तिश्श्रियं दातुम्
भरतायारिशासन ॥
O harrower of foes! What strength does he have, to incur the unparalleled enmity of yours and mine, to bestow this plentitude upon Bharata?
2.21.16
अनुरक्तोऽस्मि भावेन
भ्रातरं देवि तत्त्वतः ।
सत्येन धनुषा चैव
दत्तेनेष्टेन ते शपे ॥
I love my brother from the bottom of my heart! O Queen! I swear this on truth, on my bow, on all my generosity and on my Yajñas!
2.21.17
दीप्तमग्निमरण्यं वा
यदि रामः प्रवेक्ष्यति ।
प्रविष्टं तत्र मां देवि
त्वं पूर्वमवधारय ॥
Be assured, O Queen, that I will be there ahead of Rāma, whether he goes to Vana or jumps into blazing fire!
2.21.18
हरामि वीर्याद्दुःखं ते
तम स्सूर्य इवोदितः ।
देवी पश्यतु मे वीर्यम्
राघवश्चैव पश्यतु ॥
I will dispel your misery, just as the rising sun would dispel darkness! May you witness my strength and so may Rāghava!
2.21.19
एतत्तु वचनं श्रुत्वा
लक्ष्मणस्य महात्मनः ।
उवाच रामं कौसल्या
रुदन्ती शोकलालसा ॥
Hearing those words of Mahātma Lakshmaṇa, Kousalyā spoke to Rāma, still crying in misery:
Why is Lakshmaṇa being referred to as Mahatma here, especially when he is speaking in such extreme language (said so even by Rāma, refer to Ṡlōka 43 below)?
The answer lies in the definition of the word Mahātma. In modern times, we know this word as the title of M.K. Gandhi. We understand it as a title to be used for exceptional personalities.
But during the times of Rāmāyaṇa, the word Mahatma referred to any person whose concerns of life were beyond the mundane and petty.
Lakshmaṇa here, is concerned with matters of change of regime of a great kingdom, which he feels is unjust!
2.21.20
भ्रातुस्ते वदतः पुत्र
लक्ष्मणस्य श्रुतं त्वया ।
यदत्रानन्तरं कार्यम्
कुरुष्व यदि रोचते ॥
My son! You have heard what your brother has to say. If it appeals to you, please proceed with whatever is to be done next!
2.21.21
न चाधर्म्यं वच श्शृत्वा
सपत्न्या मम भाषितम् ।
विहाय शोकसन्तप्ताम्
गन्तुमर्हसि मामितः ॥
It does not behoove on you to go away, heeding the unjust words of my co-wife, leaving me burning with grief!
2.21.22
धर्मज्ञ यदि धर्मिष्ठो
धर्मं चरितुमिच्छसि ।
शुश्रूष मामिहस्थस्त्वम्
चर धर्ममनुत्तमम् ॥
You are the knower of Dharma! If you like to adhere to Dharma and follow it, you should stay back and serve me, for there is no better Dharma than this!
2.21.23
शुश्रूषुर्जननीं पुत्र
स्वगृहे नियतो वसन् ।
परेण तपसा युक्तः
काश्यपस्त्रिदिवं गतः ॥
My son! Kāṡyapa, staying at home, serving his mother diligently and doing great Tapa, ascended to Heaven!
2.21.24
यथैव राजा पूज्यस्ते
गौरवेण तथाऽस्म्यहम् ।
त्वां नाहमनुजानामि
न गन्तव्यमितो वनम् ॥
As much as the king deserves your respects and regards, so do I! You do not have my permission and hence you must not go to Vana!
Rāmāyaṇa is a story of complex situations and choices, just as the other great epic of India, Mahābhārata, is!
Every character in these epics feels that Dharma is on his or her side, and argues for it.
But what matters, is whether they drag Dharma into the service of their Artha (gain) or Kāma (pleasure), or whether they put Dharma above their self-serving Artha and Kāma.
This point is discussed explicitly in Ṡlōkas 56 and 57 below.
2.21.25
त्वद्वियोगान्न मे कार्यम्
जीवितेन सुखेन वा ।
त्वया सह मम श्रेयः
तृणानामपि भक्षणम् ॥
I shall have no use for life or comforts at the cost of separation from you. Even to eat grass with you would be felicity for me.
2.21.26
यदि त्वं यास्यसि वनम्
त्यक्त्वा मां शोकलालसाम् ।
अहं प्रायमिहासिष्ये
न हि शक्ष्यामि जीवितुम् ॥
If you go to Vana leaving me, the grief stricken one alone, I cannot continue to live any longer! I shall starve myself to death!
2.21.27
ततस्त्वं प्राप्स्यसे पुत्र
निरयं लोकविश्रुतम् ।
ब्रह्महत्यामिवाधर्मात्
समुद्र स्सरितां पतिः ॥
And that will bring you a retribution that all the world will hear of; as the ocean, the lord of streams, suffered for killing a Brāhmaṇa, unjustly!
2.21.28
विलपन्तीं तथा दीनाम्
कौसल्यां जननीं ततः ।
उवाच रामो धर्मात्मा
वचनं धर्मसंहितम् ॥
Then, Rāma the Dharmātma, said these words of Dharma to his mother Kousalyā, who was crying in desperation:
2.21.29
नास्ति शक्तिः पितुर्वाक्यम्
समतिक्रमितुं मम ।
प्रसादये त्वां शिरसा
गन्तुमिच्छाम्यहं वनम् ॥
I am incapable of disobeying my father's words! I have made up my mind to go to the Vana! I bow my head and beg for your concession!
A leader has to use all the tools at hand to make his case, while being, of course, genuine and authentic.
In Ṡlōka 29, Rāma expresses his inability and appeals to emotion.
In Ṡlōka 30, he quotes examples from the past (referred to as 'case law' in legal parlance).
In Ṡlōka 31, he emphasizes, with another example, that even dire consequences should not sway one away from obliging Dharma.
In Ṡlōka 32, he argues that one should follow Dharma regardless of its apparent absurdity.
In Ṡlōkas 34 and 35, he articulates that his decision is not out of bounds; rather, it is within the established pattern of Dharma.
2.21.30
ऋषिणा च पितुर्वाक्यम्
कुर्वता व्रतचारिणा ।
गौर्हता जानता धर्मम्
कण्डुनाऽपि विपश्चिता ॥
Ṛshi Kaṇḍu, who knows Dharma very well, killed a cow, upon the word of his father, even though he is a scholar and man of strict observances!
2.21.31
अस्माकं च कुले पूर्वम्
सगरस्याज्ञया पितुः ।
खनद्भिस्सागरैर्भूमिम्
अवाप्तस्सुमहान्वधः ॥
In our own family, the sons of Sagara met with terrible death, digging through the earth at the command of their father!
2.21.32
जामदग्न्येन रामेण
रेणुका जननी स्वयम् ।
कृत्ता परशुनाऽरण्ये
पितुर्वचनकारिणा ॥
Rāma, the son of Jamadagni, axed his own mother Rēṇukā in the forest at the bidding of his father.
2.21.33
एतैरन्यैश्च बहुभिः
देवि देवसमैः कृतम् ।
पितुर्वचनमक्लीबम्
करिष्यामि पितुर्हितम् ॥
These and many other men who are comparable to Dēvas, ensured that the words of their fathers did not go in vain ! O Queen! I shall also do whatever pleases my father.
2.21.34
न खल्वेतन्मयैकेन
क्रियते पितृशासनम् ।
एतैरपि कृतं देवि
ये मया तव कीर्तिताः ॥
Surely I am not the only one to carry out a father's command; these others, whom I have named, my lady, also have done it!
2.21.35
नाहं धर्ममपूर्वं ते
प्रतिकूलं प्रवर्तये ।
पूर्वैरयमभिप्रेतो
गतो मार्गोऽनुगम्यते ॥
I am not following an unknown Dharma, against your wish. I am only following what has been accepted and followed by people in the past.
2.21.36
तदेतत्तु मया कार्यम्
क्रियते भुवि नान्यथा ।
पितुर्हि वचनं कुर्वन्
न कश्चिन्नाम हीयते ॥
Hence, in this matter, which I am called upon to do on this earth, I would not be able to do anything differently! One cannot go wrong by fulfilling the words of his father!
2.21.37
तामेवमुक्त्वा जननीम्
लक्ष्मणं पुनरब्रवीत् ।
वाक्यं वाक्यविदां श्रेष्ठः
श्रेष्ठस्सर्वधनुष्मताम् ॥
Having said this to his mother, he, the foremost of the masters of speech and the best among bowmen, spoke again to Lakshmaṇa:
2.21.38
तव लक्ष्मण जानामि
मयि स्नेहमनुत्तमम् ।
विक्रमं चैव सत्त्वं च
तेजश्च सुदुरासदम् ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! I know about your unequalled affection for me! I also know about your true strength and valor, and that no one can stand in the proximity of your radiance!
2.21.39
मम मातुर्महद्दुःखम्
अतुलं शुभलक्षण ।
अभिप्रायमविज्ञाय
सत्यस्य च शमस्य च ॥
O man of auspicious qualities! My mother is grieving immeasurably, because she does not know the deeper significance of following the truth and being restrained!
2.21.40
धर्मो हि परमो लोके
धर्मे सत्यं प्रतिष्ठितम् ।
धर्मसंश्रितमेतच्च
पितुर्वचनमुत्तमम् ॥
Dharma is the ultimate goal in this world. Even truth finds its basis in Dharma. The laudable decision of my father is fully in accordance with Dharma.
Rāma emphasizes that he is fully convinced with the decision of his father, of obliging the boons asked for by Kaikēyee.
2.21.41
संश्रुत्य च पितुर्वाक्यम्
मातुर्वा ब्राह्मणस्य वा ।
न कर्तव्यं वृथा वीर
धर्ममाश्रित्य तिष्ठता ॥
O Veera! Those who adhere to Dharma should not renege on their word given to father or mother or Brāhmaṇa.
Mother, father and Brāhmaṇa have done what they can for you, even before you know how to ask.
One must, therefore, keep one's word to them at all cost, without looking for a benefit in the future, but in appreciation and gratitude towards what they have done already in the past.
2.21.42
सोऽहं न शक्ष्यामि पितुः
नियोगमतिवर्तितुम् ।
पितुर्हिवचनाद्वीर
कैकेय्याऽहं प्रचोदितः ॥
O Veera! Hence, I am not capable of disobeying the command of our father! Kaikēyee asked me to go to the forest only because of the word given by our father!
2.21.43
तदेतां विसृजानार्याम्
क्षत्रधर्माश्रितां मतिम् ।
धर्ममाश्रय मा तैक्ष्ण्यम्
मद्बुद्धिरनुगम्यताम् ॥
Hence leave the ignoble thought that is born out of Kshatra Dharma! Follow the ultimate Dharma! Give up your extremism! I hope you understand my thought process!
Kshatra Dharma is to fight for your right, without caving in.
Dharma is to do what is right in the larger context.
2.21.44
तमेवमुक्त्वा सौहार्दात्
भ्रातरं लक्ष्मणाग्रजः ।
उवाच भूयः कौसल्याम्
प्राञ्जलिश्शिरसानतः ॥
Having said this to his brother in all affection, the elder brother of Lakshmaṇa addressed Kousalyā again with bowed head and joined palms:
2.21.45
अनुमन्यस्व मां देवि
गमिष्यन्तमितो वनम् ।
शापिताऽसि मम प्राणैः
कुरु स्वस्त्ययनानि मे ॥
O lady! Please allow me to leave to the Vana from here! I adjure you by my life, you must not say no. Please give me your blessings!
2.21.46
तीर्णप्रतिज्ञश्च वनात्
पुनरेष्याम्यहं पुरीम् ।
ययातिरिव राजर्षिः
पुरा हित्वा पुनर्दिवम् ॥
After fulfilling my promise, I will return from the Vana to the city, just as Rājarshi Yayāti, once upon a time, returned to Heaven after losing it once!
2.21.47
शोकस्सन्धार्यतां मातः
हृदये साधु मा शुचः ।
वनवासादिहैष्यामि
पुनः कृत्वा पितुर्वचः ॥
Mother! May the grief in your heart be quieted down in full! Do not cry! I will fulfill my father's words and come back here from the Vana!
2.21.48
त्वया मया च वैदेह्या
लक्ष्मणेन सुमित्रया ।
पितुर्नियोगे स्थातव्यम्
एष धर्मस्सनातनः ॥
You, me, the princess of Vidēha, Lakshmaṇa and Sumitrā must abide by the command of the father. This is a time honored Dharma!
2.21.49
अम्ब संहृत्य सम्भारान्
दुःखं हृदि निगृह्य च ।
वनवासकृता बुद्धिः
मम धर्म्याऽनु वर्त्यताम् ॥
O mother! Please keep aside the preparations for the crowning. Please contain the grief in your heart. May you accept this righteous resolve of mine to go to Vana!
2.21.50
एतद्वचस्तस्य निशम्य माता
सुधर्म्यमव्यग्रमविक्लबं च ।
मृतेव संज्ञां प्रतिलभ्य देवी
समीक्ष्य रामं पुनरित्युवाच ॥
Hearing those righteous, serene and well considered words, the queen felt as if she had risen from the dead and taking a good look at Rāma, said:
2.21.51
यथैव ते पुत्र! पिता तथाऽहं
गुरु स्स्वधर्मेण सुहृत्तया च ।
न त्वाऽनुजानामि न मां विहाय
सुदुःखितामर्हसि गन्तुमेवम् ॥
My dear son! I am also your Guru like your father, both by affection and by Dharma! I will not give my permission. You must not leave, abandoning me in complete grief!
2.21.52
किं जीवितेनेह विना त्वया मे
लोकेन वा किं स्वधयाऽमृतेन ।
श्रेयो मुहूर्तं तव सन्निधानं
ममेह कृत्स्नादपि जीवलोकात् ॥
What good is life here or life beyond with Swadha or Amṛta, without you? A single moment here with you is more desirable for me than eternity with all the beings of all Lōkas!
Swadha refers to the offerings made to the ancestors on the anniversary of their ascendance to the afterworld.
2.21.53
नरैरिवोल्काभिरपोह्यमानो
महागजोऽध्वानमनुप्रविष्टः ।
भूयः प्रजज्वाल विलापमेवं
निशम्य रामः करुणं जनन्याः ॥
Hearing the piteous wails of his mother, Rāma was greatly distressed, like a mighty elephant on its path blocked by torch bearing men.
2.21.54
न मातरं चैव विसंज्ञकल्पा
मार्तं च सौमित्रिमभिप्रतप्तम् ।
धर्मे स्थितो धर्म्यमुवाच वाक्यं
यथा स एवार्हति तत्र वक्तुम् ॥
To that mother, who was almost unconscious and to that son of Sumitrā, who was sad and distressed, he, who was firmly established in Dharma, said the words that were in conformance with Dharma, as he alone, and no one else could, in such a moment.
2.21.55
अहं हि ते लक्ष्मण! नित्यमेव
जानामि भक्तिं च पराक्रमं च ।
मम त्वभिप्रायमसन्निरीक्ष्य
मात्रा सहाभ्यर्दसि मां सुदुःखम् ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! I am always aware of your strength and devotion to me! You, along with my mother, are causing great distress to me, by missing to see my point of view!
2.21.56
धर्मार्थकामाः खलु तात लोके
समीक्षिता धर्मफलोदयेषु ।
ते तत्र सर्वे स्युरसंशयं मे
भार्येव वश्याऽभिमता सुपुत्रा ॥
My dear! I have no doubt that Dharma, Artha and Kāma are seen in the advancement of Dharma only, like a loving and devoted wife and good children (are seen in the context of advancement of husband)!
2.21.57
यस्मिंस्तु सर्वे स्युरसन्निविष्टा
धर्मो यत स्स्यात्तदुपक्रमेत ।
द्वेष्यो भवत्यर्थपरो हि लोके
कामात्मता खल्वपि न प्रशस्ता ॥
When faced with a situation where all (three) of them do not seem to come together right away, one must begin with whatever leads to Dharma in the end.
Those who focus on Artha as the end goal will find themselves becoming abominable in this world! Similarly one who keeps Kāma as the end goal, gains no respect.
2.21.58
गुरुश्च राजा च पिता च वृद्धः
क्रोधात्प्रहर्षाद्यदि वाऽपि कामात् ।
यद्व्यादिशेत्कार्यमवेक्ष्य धर्मं
कस्तन्न कुर्यादनृशंसवृत्तिः ॥
Be it out of joy or indulgence or rage, how can anyone, who can see the Dharma, not obey the directive of a revered and aged father, who is also the king, unless he is a wretched soul!
2.21.59
स वै न शक्नोमि पितुः प्रतिज्ञा-
मिमामकर्तुं सकलां यथावत् ।
स ह्यावयोस्तात! गुरुर्नियोगे
देव्याश्च भर्ता स गति स्सधर्मः ॥
I cannot but fulfill my father's promise in letter and spirit, for he is our elder and hence and is very well entitled to the privilege of commanding us, my dear! And as for the queen, he is her provider, her refuge and her Dharma.
2.21.60
तस्मिन्पुनर्जीवति धर्मराजे
विशेषतस्स्वे पथि वर्तमाने ।
देवी मया सार्धमितोऽपगच्छेत्
कथं स्विदन्या विधवेव नारी ॥
While he, the lord of Dharma, is alive keeping well to his path of duty, how can the queen go with me, like a woman who has lost her husband?
2.21.61
सा माऽनुमन्यस्व वनं व्रजन्तं
कुरुष्व न स्स्वस्त्ययनानि देवि! ।
यथा समाप्ते पुनराव्रजेयं
यथा हि सत्येन पुनर्ययातिः ॥
Hence, grant me permission for my departure to the Vana. O lady! Bless me to return at the end, just as Yayāti returned (to Heaven) by the strength of adherence to truth!
2.21.62
यशो ह्यहं केवलराज्यकारणा
न्न पृष्ठतः कर्तुमलं महोदयम् ।
अदीर्घकाले न तु देवि जीविते
वृणेऽवरामद्य महीमधर्मतः ॥
I cannot put my renown, which is of more significance, behind, for the sake of gaining the kingdom. O lady! Life is too short to seek this land, transgressing Dharma!
Like you, the reader, I also can not help thinking: why do we not have a leader like this today, who puts the reputation of his character above everything else? Asked differently, why don't leaders like this rise up in public life, in contemporary times?
In contemporary times, it is easy to get by with not just a few blemishes on one's character, but with an entire swath of them.
In the days of yore or even in close-knit communities such as in villages today, people live and die for the reputation of good character.
Today that place is occupied by achievements, accomplishments and accumulation of wealth.
If one accumulates enough wealth and shares a bit of it with others, everyone is willing to turn a blind eye to the source of that wealth.
2.21.63
प्रसादयन्नरवृषभ स्समातरं
पराक्रमाज्जिगमिषुरेव दण्डकान् ।
अथानुजं भृशमनुशास्य दर्शनं
चकार तां हृदि जननीं प्रदक्षिणम् ॥
That hero, his mind set on leaving for Daṇḍaka, did his best to impress upon his younger brother the purport of following Dharma. That bull among men placated his mother, and circumambulated her in all reverence.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty first Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
