Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 27
In this Sarga, seeing Khara ready to fight with Rāma, Triṡira pleads with Khara to give him a chance to fight with Rāma. Khara agrees.
The fight between Rāma and Triṡira is fierce like that between a lion and an elephant. At the end, Rāma slays the horses, strikes down the flag, kills the charioteer and finally cuts off the three heads of Triṡira with three sharp and swift arrows.
The few remaining attendants of Khara that were left flee away from Rāma, like deer from a lion. The outraged Khara pulls them back and rushes towards Rāma.
3.27.1
खरं तु रामाभिमुखम्
प्रयान्तं वाहिनीपतिः ।
राक्षसस्त्रिशिरा नाम
सन्निपत्येदमब्रवीत् ॥
To that Khara, who was thus advancing against Rāma, Triṡira, the leader of the army, made salutations and said these words:
3.27.2
मां नियोजय विक्रान्त
सन्निवर्तस्व साहसात् ।
पश्य रामं महाबाहुं
संयुगे विनिपातितम् ॥
Assign me to that task, O ye of valor! Hold back your impatience. See Rāma of mighty arm struck down in the fight!
3.27.3
प्रतिजानामि ते सत्यम्
आयुधं चाहमालभे ।
यथा रामं वधिष्यामि
वधार्हं सर्वरक्षसाम् ॥
I solemnly swear, with my hand on the hilt, that I will kill Rāma who deserves to be killed by every Rākshasa.
3.27.4
अहं वास्य रणे मृत्युः
एष वा समरे मम ।
विनिवृत्य रणोत्साहान्
मुहूर्तं प्राश्निको भव ॥
Hold back from your rush to fight just for a moment and check whether I will be his death or he will be mine.
3.27.5
प्रहृष्टो वा हते रामे
जनस्थानं प्रयास्यसि ।
मयि वा निहते रामं
संयुगायोपयास्यसि ॥
Either Rāma dies and you go to Janasthāna rejoicing or I die and you go to Rāma to fight.
3.27.6
खरस्त्रिशिरसा तेन
मृत्युलोभात्प्रसादितः ।
गच्छ युध्येत्यनुज्ञातो
राघवाभिमुखो ययौ ॥
Inviting his own death, Trisirasa thus pleaded with Khara and got the word, ‘Go, fight!’ from him and went towards Rāma.
3.27.7
त्रिशिराश्च रथेनैव
वाजियुक्तेन भास्वता ।
अभ्यद्रवद्रणे रामं
त्रिशृङ्ग इव पर्वतः ॥
Triṡira, (gigantic) like a triple-crested mountain, ran towards Rāma in his shining horse-drawn chariot.
Triṡira means one with three heads.
3.27.8
शरधारासमूहान्स
महामेघ इवोत्सृजन् ।
व्यसृजत्सदृशं नादं
जलार्द्रस्य तु दुन्दुभेः ॥
He rained showers of arrows, like a cloud. And He shouted loudly, like a wet kettle-drum.
3.27.9
आगच्छन्तं त्रिशिरसं
राक्षसं प्रेक्ष्य राघवः ।
धनुषा प्रतिजग्राह
विधुन्वन्सायकान् शितान् ॥
Rāghava, seeing Triṡira the Rākshasa advance, responded by grasping the bow and letting off sharp arrows.
3.27.10
स सम्प्रहारस्तुमुलो
रामत्रिशिरसोर्महान् ।
बभूवातीव बलिनोः
सिंहकुञ्जरयोरिव ॥
That intense and great fight between Rāma and Triṡira was like the fight between a lion an elephant of exceeding strength.
3.27.11
ततस्त्रिशिरसा बाणैः
ललाटे ताडितस्त्रिभिः ।
अमर्षी कुपितो रामः
संरब्धमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Hit by Triṡira with three arrows on the forehead, the enraged Rāma, said these words in anger and scorn:
3.27.12
अहो विक्रमशूरस्य
राक्षसस्येदृशं बलम् ।
पुष्पैरिव शरैर्यस्य
ललाटेऽस्मिन्परिक्षतः ।
ममापि प्रतिगृह्णीष्व
शरांश्चापगुणच्युतान्  ॥
Wow! Look at the strength of the valiant and brave Rākshasa! I was struck on my forehead by his arrows, as if by flowers! Now check my arrows too, shot from the bowstring!
3.27.13
एवमुक्त्वा तु संरब्धः
शरानाशीविषोपमान् ।
त्रिशिरोवक्षसि क्रुद्धो
निजघान चतुर्दश ॥
Saying thus, he planted fourteen arrows, venomous like vipers, on the chest of Triṡira immediately.
3.27.14
चतुर्भिस्तुरगानस्य
शरैः सन्नतपर्वभिः ।
न्यपातयत तेजस्वी
चतुरस्तस्य वाजिनः ॥
The mighty warrior then struck down his four fleet horses with four arrows that were furrowed at the nodes of their shafts.
3.27.15
अष्टभिस्सायकैस्सूतं
रथोपस्थान्न्यपातयत् ।
रामश्चिच्छेद बाणेन
ध्वजं चास्य समुच्छ्रितम् ॥
Rāma threw down the charioteer from the chariot with eight arrows and with one other arrow, he cut off his lofty flag.
3.27.16
ततो हतरथात्तस्मात्
उत्पतन्तं निशाचरम् ।
बिभेद रामस्तं बाणैः
हृदये सोऽभवज्जडः ॥
And as the Night-rover jumped out of his disabled chariot, Rāma shot arrows into his heart rendering him lifeless.
3.27.17
सायकैश्चाप्रमेयात्मा
सामर्षस्तस्य रक्षसः ।
शिरांस्यपातयद्रामो
वेगवद्भिस्त्रिभिश्शितैः ॥
The enraged Rāma, of faculties beyond measure, shot down the three heads of that Rākshasa with three swift and sharp arrows.
3.27.18
स भूमौ रुधिरोद्गारी
रामबाणाभिपीडितः ।
न्यपतत्पतितैः पूर्वं
स्वशिरोभिर्निशाचरः ॥
And along with his heads that had already fallen, the Rākshasa himself, mangled by Rāma’s arrows, fell down on the ground, with blood gushing out.
3.27.19
हतशेषास्ततो भग्ना
राक्षसाः खरसंश्रया ।
द्रवन्ति स्म न तिष्ठन्ति
व्याघ्रत्रस्ता मृगा इव ॥
Then the Rākshasas, who attend upon Khara, that were left alive but hurt, fled without stopping there for a moment, like deer from a tiger.
3.27.20
तान्खरो द्रवतो दृष्ट्वा
निवर्त्य रुषितस्स्वयम् ।
राममेवाभिदुद्राव
राहुश्चन्द्रमसं यथा ॥
Seeing them flee, Khara was outraged. He then dragged them back and rushed towards Rāma like Rāhu towards the moon.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे सप्तविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty seventh Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
