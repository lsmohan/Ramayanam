Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 83
In this Sarga, hearing the terrible din that arose from the clash of Vānaras and Rākshasas at the western gate, Rāma sends additional forces led by Jāmbavān to the assistance of Hanumān. Hanumān, who is already returning from the fray, meets them on the way and they all come back to Rāghava and report to him the slaying, supposedly, of Seetā. Rāma falls in a swoon hearing the news.
Lakshmaṇa, distraught with grief, gives vent to his frustration over Rāma giving up his kingdom in the first place, and courting Dharma in preference, which resulted in all that ensued, including the abduction of Seetā, and her (supposed) slaying.
6.83.1
राघवश्चापि विपुलम्
तं राक्षसवनौकसाम् ।
श्रुत्वा सङ्ग्रामनिर्घोषम्
जाम्बवन्तमुवाच ह ॥
Meanwhile, on hearing the huge din that arose from the the fight between those Rākshasas and Vana dwellers Rāghava remarked to Jāmbavān:
6.83.2
सौम्य नूनं हनुमता
क्रियते कर्म दुष्करम् ।
श्रूयते हि यथा भीमः
सुमहानायुधस्वनः ॥
Hanumān is certainly trying to accomplish something that is quite impossible! Listen to the frightful and huge din raised by the clash of weapons, my dear!
6.83.3
तद्गच्छ कुरु साहाय्यम्
स्वबलेनाभिसंवृतः ।
क्षिप्रमृक्षपते तस्य
कपिश्रेष्ठस्य युध्यतः ॥
Hence, go quickly with your forces, O King of Ṛkshas, to assist the great Vānara engaged in the fight!
6.83.4
ऋक्षाराजस्तथोक्तस्तु
स्वेनानीकेन संवृतः ।
आगच्छत् पश्चिमं द्वारम्
हनुमान् यत्र वानरः ॥
Saying 'surely!', the King of Ṛkshas headed, with his forces, towards the western gate where Hanumān, the Vānara, was fighting.
6.83.5
अथायान्तं हनूमन्तम्
ददर्शर्क्षपतिः पथि ।
वानरैः कृतसङ्ग्रामैः
श्वसद्भिरभिसंवृतम् ॥
Then the King of Ṛkshas saw on the way Hanumān returning with Vānaras around him, having finished the fight, but heaving great sighs.
6.83.6
दृष्ट्वा पथि हनूमांश्च
तदृक्षबलमुद्यतम् ।
नीलमेघनिभं भीमम्
सन्निवार्य न्यवर्तत ॥
Hanumān stopped and turned back the bear force that he saw on the way, which was marching forth looking like a formidable black cloud.
6.83.7
स तेन हरिसैन्येन
सन्निकर्षं महायशाः ।
शीघ्रमागम्य रामाय
दुःखितो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
And he of great renown, along with the Vānara army, reached the presence of Rāma quickly and said these words, in grief:
6.83.8
समरे युद्ध्यमानानाम्
अस्माकं प्रेक्षतां पुरः ।
जघान रुदतीं सीताम्
इन्द्रजिद्रावणात्मजः ॥
As we fought the battle, Indrajit, the son of Rāvaṇa, killed Seetā who was crying, right in front of our eyes.
6.83.9
उद्भ्रान्तचित्तस्तां दृष्ट्वा
विषण्णोऽहमरिन्दम ।
तदहं भवतो वृत्तम्
विज्ञापयितुमागतः ॥
O subduer of foe! Seeing her (thus killed), and with my mind in a complete shock, I came here, quite woebegone, to appraise you of this development.
6.83.10
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राघवः शोकमूर्च्छितः ।
निपपात तदा भूमौ
छिन्नमूल इव द्रुमः ॥
Hearing those words of his, Rāghava, in grief, fell down in a swoon on the ground, like a tree cut at the base.
6.83.11
तं भूमौ देवसङ्काशम्
पतितं प्रेक्ष्य राघवम् ।
अभिपेतुः समुत्पत्य
सर्वतः कपिसत्तमाः ॥
All the eminent Vānaras from every side sprang up and gathered around Rāghava, seeing him, an equal of Dēvas, fall on the ground.
6.83.12
असिञ्चन् सलिलैश्चैनम्
पद्मोत्पलसुगन्धिभिः ।
प्रदहन्तमनासाद्यम्
सहसाऽग्निमिवोच्छिखम् ॥
They sprinkled water that bore the fragrance of lotus and lily all over him whose body was unapproachable like a fire that had burst suddenly into a blaze.
6.83.13
तं लक्ष्मणोऽथ बाहुभ्याम्
परिष्वज्य सुदुःखितः ।
उवाच राममस्वस्थम्
वाक्यं हेत्वर्थसंयुतम् ॥
Sobbing uncontrollably, Lakshmaṇa took him into his embrace with his hands. Then he said these words to the distressed Rāma, that were reasonable and made sense:
Lakshmaṇa, since the beginning, was unhappy about the decision of Rāma to give up the kingdom. Now that the whole endeavor of finding Seetā has come to naught, with her supposedly killed, he finds his stance vindicated. He feels, at least for the moment, that it is wealth that drives everything, and adherence to Dharma does not get anyone anywhere. He says the same in so many words here, finding an occasion to vent the steam that has been cooking up inside him for long.
6.83.14
शुभे वर्त्मनि तिष्ठन्तम्
त्वामार्य विजितेन्द्रियम् ।
अनर्थेभ्यो न शक्नोति
त्रातुं धर्मो निरर्थकः ॥
The Dharma (which you adhered to) went for naught; it is not able to save you, the noble one, who mastered all the senses and stayed on the path of righteousness, from these adversities.
6.83.15
भूतानां स्थावराणां च
जङ्गमानां च दर्शनम् ।
यथाऽस्ति न तथा धर्मः
तेन नास्तीति मे मतिः ॥
Dharma is not real and cannot be seen like the animate and inanimate objects can be, and hence I do not think it exists.
6.83.16
यथैव स्थावरं व्यक्तम्
जङ्गमं च तथाविधम् ।
नायमर्थस्तथा युक्तः
त्वद्विधो न विपद्यते ॥
This (Dharma) is not tangible like the inanimate or animate; for, if only so, one like you should not suffer from adversities.
6.83.17
यद्यधर्मो भवेद्भूतो
रावणो नरकं व्रजेत् ।
भवांश्च धर्मयुक्तो वै
नैवं व्यसनमाप्नुयात् ॥
If Adharma exists for real, Rāvaṇa should be in hell, and you, who adhere to Dharma, should not be afflicted by calamities.
6.83.18
तस्य च व्यसनाभावाद्
व्यसनं च गते त्वयि ।
धर्मो भवत्यधर्मश्च
परस्परविरोधिनौ ॥
With no adversities affecting him, and all adversities striking you, Dharma seems to become Adharma, though each one of them is supposedly the diametrically opposite of the other.
6.83.19
धर्मेणोपलभेद्धर्मम्
अधर्मं चाप्यधर्मतः ।
यद्यधर्मेण युज्येयुः
एष्वधर्मः प्रतिष्ठितः ।
न धर्मेण वियुज्येरन्
अधर्मरुचयो जनाः ।
धर्मेण चरतां धर्मम्
चैषां धर्मफलं भवेत् ॥
Does one following Dharma get the fruits of Dharma? Does one following Adharma get the fruits of Adharma?
If only the ones sticking to Adharma gets the fruits of Adharma, and those with even an inclination for Adharma does not get the fruits of Dharma, people who genuinely follow Dharma would beget the fruits of Dharma.
6.83.21
यस्मादर्था विवर्धन्ते
येष्वधर्मः प्रतिष्ठितः ।
क्लिश्यन्ते धर्मशीलाश्च
तस्मादेतौ निरर्थकौ ॥
But seeing those addicted to Adharma flourish, and those who follow Dharma undergo trials and tribulations, it is futile even to talk about these two.
6.83.22
वध्यन्ते पुण्यकर्माणो
यद्यधर्मेण राघव ।
अधर्मेण हतो धर्मः
स हतः किं करिष्यति ॥
If people of good deeds are killed by Adharma it amounts to Dharma being killed by Adharma. What can one do, O Rāghava, when Dharma itself is killed?
6.83.23
अथवा विहितेनायम्
हन्यते हन्ति वा परम् ।
विधिरालिप्यते तेन
न स पापेन कर्मणा ॥
Or, if it is because of Providence that one kills the other or gets killed, the fruits of that sinful deed belongs to Providence and not to the one who committed the act.
6.83.24
अदृष्टप्रतिकारेण
स्वव्यक्तेनासता सता ।
कथं शक्यं परं प्राप्तुम्
धर्मेणारिविकर्शन ॥
How can (it be said that) Dharma leads to the ultimate good, O harrier of the foe, if its rewards are so intangible and are not evident?
6.83.25
यदि सत् स्यात् सतां मुख्य
नासत् स्यात्तव किञ्चन ।
त्वया यदीदृशं प्राप्तम्
तस्मात् सन्नोपपद्यते ॥
If it (Dharma) did in fact exist, O foremost of good men, no evil should have happened to you. Because this has befallen you, it must be said that it does not exist.
6.83.26
अथवा दुर्बलः क्लीबो
बलं धर्मोऽनुवर्तते ।
दुर्बलो हृतमर्यादो
न सेव्य इति मे मतिः ॥
Or else, if Dharma is weak, cannot stand for itself, and submits itself to the might, I do not think one should rely on it which is so fragile and yields itself so easily.
6.83.27
बलस्य यदि चेद्धर्मो
गुणभूतः पराक्रमे ।
धर्ममुत्सृज्य वर्तस्व
यथा धर्मे तथा बले ॥
If Dharma is just supplementary to strength in matters that demand prowess, you might as well give up Dharma and rely on strength instead of on Dharma.
6.83.28
अथ चेत् सत्यवचनम्
धर्मः किल परन्तप ।
अनृतस्त्वय्यकरुणः
किं न बद्धस्त्वया पिता ॥
If Dharma demands one to honor one's word, O tormentor of the foe, why did you not hold responsible our father, for being so merciless and for not keeping his word in your case (of not giving you the kingdom he promised)!
6.83.29
यदि धर्मो भवेद्भूतो
अधर्मो वा परन्तप ।
न स्म हत्वा मुनिं वज्री
कुर्यादिज्यां शतक्रतुः ॥
If there is such a thing called Dharma or Adharma, O tormentor of the foe, the wielder of Vajra would not have slain the Muni, or, having done it, would not have performed a Yajña in expiation.
Does anyone know the story being referred to here?
6.83.30
अधर्मसंश्रितो धर्मो
विनाशयति राघव ।
सर्वमेतद्यथाकामम्
काकुत्स्थ कुरुते नरः ॥
When Dharma is so polluted with Adharma, it only leads to ruination, O Rāghava! That is why, O Kākutstha, people do whatever they feel like, all the time!
6.83.31
मम चेदं मतं तात
धर्मोऽयमिति राघव ।
धर्ममूलं त्वया छिन्नम्
राज्यमुत्सृजता तदा ॥
This is what I think is (the ultimate) Dharma! The very basis of Dharma was severed off when you gave up the kingdom!
6.83.32
अर्थेभ्यो हि प्रवृद्धेभ्यः
संवृत्तेभ्यस्ततस्ततः ।
क्रियाः सर्वाः प्रवर्तन्ते
पर्वतेभ्य इवापगाः ॥
It is from wealth, gathered from every source and accumulated, that all purposeful action flows, like rivers from mountains.
6.83.33
अर्थेन हि विमुक्तस्य
पुरुषस्याल्पतेजसः ।
विच्छिद्यन्ते क्रियाः सर्वा
ग्रीष्मे कुसरितो यथा ॥
All endeavors of a man who is stripped of his wealth, and possesses little power fail like trickles drying up in summer.
6.83.34
सोऽयमर्थं परित्यज्य
सुखकामः सुखैधितः ।
पापमारभते कर्तुम्
ततो दोषः प्रवर्तते ॥
The man who is raised in luxuries but gives up his wealth would only resort to sinful deeds and wrongful behavior to satisfy his craving for pleasures.
6.83.35
यस्यार्थास्तस्य मित्राणि
यस्यार्थास्तस्य बान्धवाः ।
यस्यार्थाः स पुमान् लोके
यस्यार्थाः स च पण्डितः ॥
Where there is wealth there are friends; where there is wealth there are relatives. One who has wealth is considered manly; one who has wealth is considered wise, in this world!
6.83.36
यस्यार्थाः स च विक्रान्तो
यस्यार्थाः स च बुद्धिमान् ।
यस्यार्थाः स महाभागो
यस्यार्थाः स महागुणः ॥
A man of wealth is a man of prowess. A man of wealth is a man of intelligence. A man of wealth is a man of great fortunes. A man of wealth is a man of great virtues.
6.83.37
अर्थस्यैते परित्यागे
दोषाः प्रव्याहृता मया ।
राज्यमुत्सृजता वीर
येन बुद्धिस्त्वया कृता ॥
I do not know what you thought, O Veera, when you gave up the kingdom, but this is what I have to say about the ills of giving up wealth.
6.83.38
यस्यार्था धर्मकामार्थाः
तस्य सर्वं प्रदक्षिणम् ।
अधनेनार्थकामेन
नार्थः शक्यो विचिन्वता ॥
Dharma, Artha and Kāma are always at the service of one who has wealth. One who has no wealth but wants Artha and Kāma, may struggle, but would never, gain wealth.
6.83.39
हर्षः कामश्च दर्पश्च
धर्मः क्रोधः शमो दमः ।
अर्थादेतानि सर्वाणि
प्रवर्तन्ते नराधिप ॥
Everything, from happiness to pleasures, to pride, to adherence to Dharma, to one's frown being feared, to the virtues of control over thought and action are possible only by having wealth, O King of men!
6.83.40
येषां नश्यत्ययं लोकः
चरतां धर्मचारिणाम् ।
तेऽर्थास्त्वयि न दृश्यन्ते
दुर्दिनेषु यथा ग्रहाः ॥
Life in this world is as good as nonexistent without all these in place, as it happens to one who follows Dharma but whose wealth disappears like heavenly bodies on a cloudy day.
6.83.41
त्वयि प्रव्रजिते वीर
गुरोश्च वचने स्थिते ।
रक्षसाऽपहृता भार्या
प्राणैः प्रियतरा तव ॥
O Veera, you went into exile in deference to the word of your father and your wife, who is dearer to you than life, was abducted by the Rākshasa!
6.83.42
तदद्य विपुलं वीर
दुःखमिन्द्रजिता कृतम् ।
कर्मणा व्यपनेष्यामि
तस्मादुत्तिष्ठ राघव ॥
(In any case), I shall wipe out this extreme grief, O Veera, caused by the act of Indrajit! Please get up, O Rāghava!
6.83.43
उत्तिष्ठ नरशार्दूल
दीर्घबाहो दृढव्रत ।
किमात्मानं महात्मानम्
कृतात्मानं न बुध्यसे ॥
Please get up, O tiger among men, O you of long arm, of steadfast vows! Why do you not realize your greatness, and how carefully you cultivated it?
6.83.44
अयमनघ तवोदितः प्रियार्थं
जनकसुतानिधनं निरीक्ष्य रुष्टः ।
सहयगजरथां सराक्षसेन्द्रां
भृशमिषुभिर्विनिपातयामि लङ्काम् ॥
I am extremely outraged, O sinless one, seeing the daughter of Janaka slain; I shall please you by completely destroying Laṅkā with its horses, elephants and chariots, along with the King of Rākshasas, with my arrows.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे त्र्यशीतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighty third Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
