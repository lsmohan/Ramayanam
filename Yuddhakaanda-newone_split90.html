Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 90
In this Sarga, Indrajit, whose chariot was broken, manages to sneak back into the city and come back on another chariot to continue the fight. He slays hundreds and thousands of Vānaras with his arrows. The Vānaras seek refuge in Lakshmaṇa.
Lakshmaṇa with his spectacular skill of archery shoots down the Rākshasas and takes aim at Indrajit. Those mighty warriors then get within close quarters of each other and launch a fierce attack with floods of arrows. Lakshmaṇa cuts off the head of the charioteer of Indrajit.
Meanwhile Indrajit also shoots arrows at Vibheeshaṇa and Vibheeshaṇa, in rage, slays his horses. Lakshmaṇa foils Indrajit’s further attempts at striking Vibheeshaṇa.
Indrajit then shoots Yama's Astra. Lakshmaṇa strikes it down with Kubēra's Astra. Lakshmaṇa then shoots Varuṇa's Astra and Indrajit strikes it down with Rudra's Astra. Indrajit then shoots Agni's Astra and Lakshmaṇa renders it futile with Surya's Astra. Indrajit then shoots Asura's Astra and Lakshmaṇa strikes it down with Mahēṡwara's Astra.
Then Lakshmaṇa shoots the powerful Indra's Astra, swearing on the adherence of Rāma to Dharma and Truth. Indra's Astra severs the head of Indrajit from his body. The Rākshasas flee seeing Indrajit fallen dead and the Vānaras jump with joy. Dēvas, Gandharvas and other celestial beings shower flowers and praise Lakshmaṇa.
6.90.1
स हताश्वो महातेजा
भूमौ तिष्ठन्निशाचरः ।
इन्द्रजित् परमक्रुद्धः
सम्प्रजज्वाल तेजसा ॥
With the horses slain, Indrajit took a stand on the ground. and he, of splendid radiance, blazing with power, became extremely furious.
6.90.2
तौ धन्विनौ जिघांसन्ताौ
अन्योन्यमिषुभिर्भृशम् ।
विजयेनाभिनिष्क्रान्तौ
वने गजवृषाविव ॥
The two bowmen, each intent on shooting arrows endlessly till he killed the other, fought like tuskers in the forest, bent on victory.
6.90.3
निबर्हयन्तश्चान्योन्यम्
ते राक्षसवनौकसः ।
भर्तारं न जहुर्युद्धे
सम्पतन्तस्ततस्ततः ॥
The Rākshasas and the Vana dwellers, attacked and destroyed each other everywhere, albeit not swaying away from their leaders.
6.90.4
ततस्तान् राक्षसान् सर्वान्
हर्षयन् रावणात्मजः ।
स्तुवानो हर्षमाणश्च
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Rāvaṇa’s son raised the spirits of all the Rākshasas, saying these words in happiness, and in praise of them:
6.90.5
तमसा बहुलेनेमाः
संसक्ताः सर्वतो दिशः ।
नेह विज्ञायते स्वो वा
परो वा राक्षसोत्तमाः ॥
O eminent Vānaras! With all the quarters enveloped in extreme darkness, it is impossible to distinguish our people from theirs.
6.90.6
धृष्टं भवन्तो युध्यन्तु
हरीणां मोहनाय वै ।
अहं तु रथमास्थाय
आगमिष्यामि संयुगे ॥
Go on fighting audaciously, confounding the Vānaras. Meanwhile, I shall be back, riding in a chariot.
6.90.7
तथा भवन्तः कुर्वन्तु
यथेमे काननौकसः ।
न युद्ध्येयुर्दुरात्मानः
प्रविष्टे नगरं मयि ॥
You should so manage that the wicked Vānaras do not attack after I go to the city.
6.90.8
इत्युक्त्वा रावणसुतो
वञ्चयित्वा वनौकसः ।
प्रविवेश पुरीं लङ्काम्
रथहेतोरमित्रहा ॥
Saying this and eluding the vigilant Vānaras, Rāvaṇa’s son, slayer of the foe, entered the city to get a chariot.
6.90.9
स रथं भूषयित्वाथ
रुचिरं हेमभूषितम् ।
प्रासासिशतसम्पूर्णम्
युक्तं परमवाजिभिः ।
अधिष्ठितं हयज्ञेन
सूतेनाप्तोपदेशिना ।
आरुरोह महातेजा
रावणिः समितिञ्जयः ॥
Rāvaṇa's son, of extraordinary power, a winner of battles, got into a chariot that was beautifully decorated with works of gold, loaded with hundreds of Prāsas and swords, and yoked with excellent horses, with the charioteer who knew the horses well enough to command them, ready in the driver's seat.
6.90.11
स राक्षसगणैर्मुख्यैः
वृतो मन्दोदरीसुतः ।
निर्ययौ नगराद्वीरः
कृतान्तबलचोदितः ॥
The valiant son of Manḍōdaree, supported by the best of Rākshasas forces, marched forth from the city, compelled by the will of Providence.
6.90.12
सोऽभिनिष्क्रम्य नगरात्
इन्द्रजित् परवीरहा ।
आभ्ययाज्जवनैरश्वैः
लक्ष्मणं सविभीषणम् ॥
Emerging from the city (on the chariot) with the fleet horses, Indrajit, destroyer of enemy warriors, went directly to accost Lakshmaṇa and Vibheeshaṇa.
6.90.13
ततो रथस्तमालोक्य
सौमित्री रावणात्मजम् ।
वानराश्च महावीर्या
राक्षसश्च विभीषणः ।
विस्मयं परमं जग्मुः
लाघवात्तस्य धीमतः ॥
When they saw him seated in a chariot (again), Sowmitri, the valiant Vānaras and Vibheeshaṇa, the Rākshasa, were all surprised at the incredible swiftness of that sagacious one.
6.90.14
रावणिश्चापि सङ्क्रुद्धो
रणे वानरयूथपान् ।
पातयामास बाणौघैः
शतशोऽथ सहस्रशः ॥
The irate son of Rāvaṇa struck down the Vānara chiefs in their hundreds and thousands, with his floods of arrows.
6.90.15
स मण्डलीकृतधनू
रावणिः समितिञ्जयः ।
हरीनभ्यहनत् क्रुद्धः
परं लाघवमास्थितः ॥
Bending his bow into a semicircle, and displaying extraordinary deftness, Rāvaṇa's son, winner of battles, mowed down the Vānaras in his wrath.
6.90.16
ते वध्यमाना हरयो
नाराचैर्भीमविक्रमाः ।
सौमित्रिं शरणं प्राप्ताः
प्रजापतिमिव प्रजाः ॥
Even the Vānaras with fierce prowess, being thus decimated by the Nārācās, sought the protection of Sowmitri, like all creatures seeking protection from the Great Progenitor.
6.90.17
ततः समरकोपेन
ज्वलितो रघुनन्दनः ।
चिच्छेद कार्मुकं तस्य
दर्शयन् पाणिलाघवम् ॥
Then the delight of Raghus, blazing with fury in that fight broke his bow, displaying the deftness of his hands.
6.90.18
सोऽन्यत् कार्मुकमादाय
सज्यं चक्रे त्वरन्निव ।
तदप्यस्य त्रिभिर्बाणैः
लक्ष्मणो निरकृन्तत ॥
He took another bow quickly and strung it. Lakshmaṇa broke that one also with three arrows.
6.90.19
अथैनं छिन्नधन्वानम्
आशीविषविषोपमैः ।
विव्याधोरसि सौमित्री
रावणिं पञ्चभिः शरैः ॥
And then, with five more arrows, which were as poisonous as poisonous snakes, Sowmitri struck the son of Rāvaṇa on the chest.
6.90.20
ते तस्य कायं निर्भिद्य
महाकार्मुकनिस्सृताः ।
निपेतुर्धरणीं बाणा
रक्ता इव महोरगाः ॥
Those arrows, shot from the great bow, pierced through his body and fell on the ground, looking like huge, scarlet snakes.
6.90.21
स भिन्नवर्मा रुधिरम्
वमन् वक्त्रेण रावणिः ।
जग्राह कार्मुकश्रेष्ठम्
दृढज्यं बलवत्तरम् ॥
Bleeding from his mouth and his armor broken, the son of Rāvaṇa took another extraordinarily strong bow, best of its kind, fitted with a sturdy bowstring.
6.90.22
स लक्ष्मणं समुद्दिश्य
परं लाघवमास्थितः ।
ववर्ष शरवर्षाणि
वर्षाणीव पुरन्दरः ॥
Then, with extreme dexterity, he shot a torrent of arrows aimed at Lakshmaṇa, like Purandara sending a torrent of downpour.
6.90.23
मुक्तमिन्द्रजिता तत्तु
शरवर्षमरिन्दमः ।
अवारयदसम्भ्रान्तो
लक्ष्मणः सुदुरासदम् ॥
Lakshmaṇa, subduer of the foe, coolly warded off that spate of arrows shot by Indrajit, which were extremely difficult for anyone else to stand against.
6.90.24
दर्शयामास च तदा
रावणिं रघुनन्दनः ।
असम्भ्रान्तो महातेजाः
तदद्भुतमिवाभवत् ॥
The delight of Raghus, of extraordinary power, hardly perturbed, thus put up a show that was nothing short of a marvel, for the the son of Rāvaṇa.
6.90.25
ततस्तान् राक्षसान् सर्वान्
त्रिभिरेकैकमाहवे ।
अविध्यत् परमक्रुद्धः
शीघ्रास्त्रं सम्प्रदर्शयन् ।
राक्षसेन्द्रसुतं चापि
बाणौघैः समताडयत् ॥
He hit all the Rākshasas, in extreme wrath, with three arrows apiece, making a show of his command over Astras. And at the same time he assailed the son of the Rākshasa King with a flood of arrows.
6.90.27
सोऽतिविद्धो बलवता
शत्रुणा शत्रुघातिना ।
असक्तं प्रेषयामास
लक्ष्मणाय बहून् शरान् ॥
Extremely harried by the strong Lakshmaṇa, his foe, a destroyer of all his enemies, he (Indrajit) shot an uninterrupted stream of arrows at him.
6.90.28
तानप्राप्तान् शितैर्बाणैः
चिच्छेद परवीरहा ।
सारथेरस्य च रणे
रथिनो रघुसत्तमः ।
शिरो जहार धर्मात्मा
भल्लेनानतपर्वणा ॥
The eminent descendant of Raghus, destroyer of enemy warriors, cut them down with sharp arrows, before they could reach him.
Then that Dharmātma, a great charioter, cut off the head of the charioteer with a Bhalla arrow with nudged joints.
6.90.29
असूतास्ते हयास्तत्र
रथमूहुरविक्लवाः ।
मण्डलान्यभिधावन्तः
तदद्भुतमिवाभवत् ॥
But the horses, unperturbed and to the amazement of everyone, had maneuvered the chariot by themselves in a circular pattern, with no charioteer to drive them.
6.90.30
अमर्षवशमापन्नः
सौमित्रिर्द्दढविक्रमः ।
प्रत्यविद्ध्यद्धयांस्तस्य
शरैर्वित्रासयन् रणे ॥
Not so pleased with it, Sowmitri of unshakable prowess, in that fight, struck the horses with arrows to shake their dauntless spirit.
6.90.31
अमृष्यमाणस्तत्कर्म
रावणस्य सुतो बली ।
विव्याध दशभिर्बाणैः
सौमित्रिं तममर्षणम् ॥
Finding it hard to accept that affront, the strong son of Rāvaṇa struck the indignant Sowmitri with ten arrows.
6.90.32
ते तस्य वज्रप्रतिमाः
शराः सर्पविषोपमाः ।
विलयं जग्मुराहत्य
कवचं काञ्चनप्रभम् ॥
But those arrows that fell like the thunderbolt and bit like venomous serpents, were destroyed, striking the armor that shone like gold.
6.90.33
अभेद्यकवचं मत्वा
लक्ष्मणं रावणात्मजः ।
ललाटे लक्ष्मणं बाणैः
सुपुङ्खैस्त्रिभिरिन्द्रजित् ।
अविध्यत् परमक्रुद्धः
शीघ्रास्त्रं च प्रदर्शयन् ॥
Realizing that the armor of Lakshmaṇa was impenetrable, and extremely enraged at that, Indrajit, the son of Rāvaṇa, shot three arrows, which had perfect rings around them, at the forehead of Lakshmaṇa, showing his deftness over Astras.
6.90.35
तैः पृषत्कैर्ललाटस्थैः
शुशुभे रघुनन्दनः ।
रणाग्रे समरश्लाघी
त्रिशृङ्ग इव पर्वतः ॥
With those arrows sticking from his forehead, the delight of Raghus, who dearly loved a fight, shone on the battlefield like a mountain with three crests.
6.90.36
स तथाह्यर्दितो बाणैः
राक्षसेन महामृधे ।
तमाशु प्रतिविव्याध
लक्ष्मणः पञ्चभिः शरैः ।
विकृष्येन्द्रजितो युद्धे
वदने शुभकुण्डले ॥
Thus wounded by the arrows of the Rākshasa in that fierce battle, Lakshmaṇa, drawing his bow to the full, hit Indrajit with five arrows on the face, which was adorned with lovely ear-pendants.
6.90.37
लक्ष्मणेन्द्रजितौ वीरौ
महाबलशरासनौ ।
अन्योन्यं जघ्नतुर्बाणैः
विशिखैर्भीमविक्रमौ ॥
Lakshmaṇa and Indrajit, both valiant, fierce in their prowess, possessing extraordinary strength and wielding bows, struck each other with sharp-tipped arrows.
6.90.38
ततः शोणितदिग्धाङ्गौ
लक्ष्मणेन्द्रजितावुभौ ।
रणे तौ रेजतुर्वीरौ
पुष्पिताविव किंशुकौ ॥
With all their limbs smeared in blood, the two warriors, Lakshmaṇa and Indrajit, shone on the battlefield like Kiṃṡuka trees in flower.
6.90.39
तौ परस्परमभ्येत्य
सर्वगात्रेषु धन्विनौ ।
घोरैर्विव्यधतुर्बाणैः
कृतभावावुभौ जये ॥
Their minds firmly made up on winning the fight, and getting within close quarters of each other, the two bowmen, struck every limb of each other with terrible arrows.
6.90.40
ततः समरकोपेन
संयुक्तो रावणात्मजः ।
विभीषणं त्रिभिर्बाणैः
विव्याध वदने शुभे ॥
Rāvaṇa’s son, filled with the fury of battle, struck Vibheeshaṇa on his seemly face with three arrows.
6.90.41
अयोमुखैस्त्रिर्भिर्विद्ध्वा
राक्षसेन्द्रं विभीषणम् ।
एकैकेनाभिविव्याध
तान् सर्वान् हरियूथपान् ॥
Having struck Vibheeshaṇa, the best of Rākshasas, with three iron-tipped arrows, he assailed each one of the Vānara chiefs with a single arrow each.
6.90.42
तस्मै दृढतरं क्रुद्धो
जघान गदयाहयान् ।
विभीषणो महातेजा
रावणेः स दुरात्मनः ॥
Extremely outraged at what he did, the extraordinarily powerful Vibheeshaṇa struck with his mace and killed the horses of the wicked son of Rāvaṇa.
6.90.43
स हताश्वादवप्लुत्य
रथान्निहतसारथेः ।
अथशक्तिं महातेजाः
पितृव्याय मुमोच ह ॥
Getting off the chariot, whose horses as well as charioteer had been slain, he then hurled a Ṡakti at his uncle.
6.90.44
तामापतन्तीं सम्प्रेक्ष्य
सुमित्रानन्दवर्धनः ।
चिच्छेद निशितैर्बाणैः
दशधा साऽपतद्भुवि ॥
Seeing it coming fast, Sumitrā's perpetual joy struck it with ten sharp arrows and it fell on the ground, broken into ten pieces.
6.90.45
तस्मै दृढधनुः क्रुद्धो
हताश्वाय विभीषणः ।
वज्रस्पर्शसमान् पञ्च
ससर्जोरसि मार्गणान् ॥
Vibheeshaṇa in his anger struck him, whose horses had been slain, with five arrows from his sturdy bow, which hit his chest with the impact of Vajra.
6.90.46
ते तस्य कायं निर्भिद्य
रुक्मपुङ्खा निमित्तगाः ।
बभूवुर्लोहितादिग्धा
रक्ता इव महोरगाः ॥
Those arrows with golden rings, which flew straight to the target, pierced through his body. Bedaubed with blood, they looked like huge red serpents.
6.90.47
स पितृव्याय सङ्क्रुद्ध
इन्द्रजिच्छरमाददे ।
उत्तमं रक्षसां मध्ये
यमदत्तं महाबलः ॥
Thereupon Indrajit of extraordinary strength, angry with his uncle and standing amidst Rākshasas, took out a great arrow given by Yama.
6.90.48
तं समीक्ष्य महातेजा
महेषुं तेन संहितम् ।
लक्ष्मणोऽप्याददे बाणम्
अन्यं भीमपराक्रमः ।
कुबेरेण स्वयं स्वप्ने
स्वस्मै दत्तं महात्मना ।
दुर्जयं दुर्विषह्यं च
सेन्द्रैरपि सुरासुरैः ॥
Seeing that mighty arrow mounted on the bow, Lakshmaṇa of extraordinary power and fierce prowess also took another arrow of unimaginable impact that was given to him by Kubēra in a dream, which even the Suras and Asuras along with Indra could not stand against, much less overpower, it.
6.90.50
तयोस्ते धनुषी श्रेष्ठे
बाहुभिः परिघोपमैः ।
विकृष्यमाणे बलवत्
क्रौञ्चाविव चुकूजतुः ॥
Their great bows warbled like a pair of curlews, drawn with all their force by their arms that looked like Parighas.
6.90.51
ताभ्यां तौ धनुषि श्रेष्ठे
संहितौ सायकोत्तमौ ।
विकृष्यमाणौ वीराभ्याम्
भृशं जज्वलतुः श्रिया ॥
The excellent arrows shone with great splendor as they were mounted on their bows and were drawn to the full by those warriors.
6.90.52
तौ भासयन्तावाकाशम्
धनुर्भ्यां विशिखौ च्युतौ ।
मुखेन मुखमाहत्य
सन्निपेततुरोजसा ॥
Shot from the bows, the sharp arrows lit up the sky and struck each other head-on, colliding with all their force.
6.90.53
सन्निपातस्तयोरासीत्
शरयोर्घोररूपयोः ।
सधूमविस्फुलिङ्गश्च
तज्जोऽग्निर्दारुणोऽभवत् ॥
The collision between the two frightful-looking arrows created sparks, flames and smoke that were terrifying.
6.90.54
तौ महाग्रहसङ्काशौ
अन्योन्यं सन्निपत्य च ।
सङ्ग्रामे शतधा यान्तौ
मेदिन्यां विनिपेततुः ॥
Crashing into each other like giant planets, they broke into a hundred fragments and fell down on the ground.
6.90.55
शरौ प्रतिहतौ दृष्ट्वा
तावुभौ रणमूर्धनि ।
व्रीडितौ जातरोषौ च
लक्ष्मणेन्द्रजितौ तदा ॥
Seeing their arrows struck down, both Lakshmaṇa and Indrajit felt ashamed and became angry.
6.90.56
स संरब्धस्तु सौमित्रिः
अस्त्रं वारुणमाददे ।
रौद्रं महेन्द्रजिद्युद्धे
व्यसृजद्युधि निष्ठितः ।
तेन तद्विहतंत्वस्त्रम्
वारुणं परमाद्भुतम् ॥
Reacting instantly, Sowmitri took Varuṇa's Astra (and shot it). Indrajit, who was also fighting with zest then shot Rudras's Astra, which struck the amazing and extraordinary Varuṇa's Astra down.
6.90.58
ततः क्रुद्धो महातेजा
इन्द्रजित् समितिञ्जयः ।
आग्नेयं सन्दधे दीप्तम्
स लोकं सङ्क्षिपन्निव ।
सौरेणास्त्रेण तद्वीरो
लक्ष्मणः प्रत्यवारयत् ॥
Then Indrajit of tremendous power, a winner of battles, launched the blazing Astra of Agni, as if he were to destroy the world in his rage. The valiant Lakshmaṇa thwarted it with the Astra of Surya.
6.90.59
अस्त्रं निवारितं दृष्ट्वा
रावणिः क्रोधमूर्च्छितः ।
आसुरं शत्रुनाशाय
घोरमस्त्रं समाददे ।
तस्माच्चापाद्विनिष्पेतुः
भास्वराः कूटमुद्गराः ।
शूलानि च भुशुण्ड्यश्च
गदाः खड्गाः परश्वधाः ॥
Seeing that Astra rendered futile, the son of Rāvaṇa, beside himself with rage, launched the dreaded Astra of Asuras, to destroy the enemy, which, with its power, caused a multitude of shining hammers, Mudgaras, spears, Bhuṡundis, maces, swords and battle-axes, to emerge from the bow.
6.90.61
तद्दृष्ट्वा लक्ष्मणः सङ्ख्ये
घोरमस्त्रमथासुरम् ।
अवार्यं सर्वभूतानाम्
सर्वशत्रविदारणम् ।
माहेश्वरेण द्युतिमान्
तदस्त्रं प्रत्यवारयत् ॥
Seeing that terrible Astra of Asuras coming, which could tear apart all the other Ṡastras, and which no creature could hope to stop, Lakshmaṇa of great splendor countered it with the Astra of Mahēṡwara.
6.90.63
तयोः सुतुमुलं युद्धम्
सम्बभूवाद्भुतोपमम् ।
गगनस्थानि भूतानि
लक्ष्मणं पर्यवारयन् ॥
And the creatures of the sky hovered where Lakshmaṇa was, to watch the marvel of their tumultuous fight.
6.90.64
भैरवाभिरुते भीमे
युद्धे वानररक्षसाम् ।
भूतैर्बहुभिराकाशम्
विस्मितैरावृतं बभौ ॥
The sky shone, crowded with the creatures that watched with amazement the fierce fight between Vānaras and Rākshasas that raised a frightful din.
6.90.65
ऋषयः पितरो देवा
गन्धर्वा गरुडोरगाः ।
शतक्रतुं पुरस्कृत्य
ररक्षुर्लक्ष्मणं रणे ॥
The Ṛshis, the Dēvas, the Pitṛ Dēvatas, the Gandharvas, the Garuḍas and the Uragas, led by Indra, protected Lakshmaṇa in that fight.
6.90.66
अथान्यं मार्गणश्रेष्ठम्
सन्दधे राघवानुजः ।
हुताशनसमस्पर्शम्
रावणात्मजदारणम् ।
सुपत्रमनुवृत्ताङ्गम्
सुपर्वाणं सुसंस्थितम् ।
सुवर्णविकृतं वीरः
शरीरान्तकरं शरम् ।
दुरावारं दुर्विषह्यम्
राक्षसानां भयावहम् ।
आशीविषविषप्रख्यम्
देवसङ्घैः समर्चितम् ।
येन शक्रो महातेजा
दानवानजयत् प्रभुः ।
पुरा दैवासुरे युद्धे
वीर्यवान् हरिवाहनः ॥
Then the younger brother of Rāghava launched another powerful arrow, whose touch was like that of fire, which would tear apart the son of Rāvaṇa, which was fitted with splendid feathers and was adorned with gold, whose shaft was well-proportioned and smooth-jointed, which could not be stopped and which no one could withstand, which put fear into Rākshasas, which was deadly like the venom of a serpent, which was admired by the companies of Dēvas, which was used once upon a time by the valiant Lord Ṡakra of extraordinary power to defeat the Dānavas in the battle between Dēvas and Asuras.
6.90.70
तदैन्द्रमस्त्रं सौमित्रिः
संयुगेष्वपराजितम् ।
शरश्रेष्ठं धनुः श्रेष्ठे
विकर्षन्निदमब्रवीत् ।
लक्ष्मीवाँल्लक्ष्मणो वाक्यम्
अर्थसाधकमात्मनः ॥
Mounting that Astra of Indra, which was the best of the arrows and which never knew defeat in a fight, on the best of the bows and drawing it to the full, Lakshmaṇa, the son of Sumitrā, of opulent bearing, said these words that would help accomplish his objective:
6.90.71
धर्मात्मा सत्यसन्धश्च
रामो दाशरथिर्यदि ।
पौरुषे चाप्रतिद्वन्द्वः
शरैनं जहि रावणिम् ॥
If Rāma, Daṡaratha’s son, is in truth a Dharmātma who avows truth, and has no equal in his manliness, may this arrow slay this son of Rāvaṇa!
6.90.72
इत्युक्त्वा बाणमाकर्णम्
विकृष्य तमजिह्मगम् ।
लक्ष्मणः समरे वीरः
ससर्जेन्द्रजितं प्रति ।
ऐन्द्रास्त्रेण समायोज्य
लक्ष्मणः परवीरहा ॥
Saying this and pulling the arrow up to his ear, the valiant Lakshmaṇa, destroyer of enemy warriors, shot it, infusing it with the power of Indra's Astra, at Indrajit.
6.90.74
तच्छिरः सशिरस्त्राणम्
श्रीमज्ज्वलितकुण्डलम् ।
प्रमथ्येन्द्रजितः कायात्
पातयामास भूतले ॥
It severed the head of Indrajit with its helmet and its lovely gleaming ear-pendants and threw it down on the ground.
6.90.75
तद्राक्षसतनूजस्य
छिन्नस्कन्धं शिरो महत् ।
तपनीयनिभं भूमौ
ददृशे रुधिरोक्षितम् ॥
That huge head of the Rākshasa’s son, severed from the body and drenched with blood, shone on the ground like gold purified in fire.
6.90.76
हतः स निपपाताथ
धरण्यां रावणात्मजः ।
कवची सशिरस्त्राणो
विप्रविद्ध शरासनः ॥
Rāvaṇa’s son, thus slain tumbled down to the ground, along with his armor and helmet, his bow thrown afar.
6.90.77
चुक्रुशुस्ते ततः सर्वे
वानराः सविभीषणाः ।
हृष्यन्तो निहते तस्मिन्
देवा वृत्रवधे यथा ॥
Then all the Vānaras, along with Vibheeshaṇa shouted in joy, rejoicing at his death, like the Dēvas did when Vṛtra was slain.
6.90.78
अथान्तरिक्षे देवानाम्
ऋषीणां च महात्मनाम् ।
जज्ञेऽथ जयसन्नादो
गन्धर्वाप्सरसामपि ॥
And in the sky was heard applause from the Mahātmas like Dēvas and Ṛshis and also Gandharvas and Apsarasas.
6.90.79
पतितं तमभिज्ञाय
राक्षसी सा महाचमूः ।
वध्यमाना दिशो भेजे
हरिभिर्जितकाशिभिः ॥
Realizing that he was struck down, the Rākshasa army flew in every direction as the Vānaras, who were basking in the glory of victory, beat them up.
6.90.80
वानरैर्वध्यमानास्ते
शस्त्राण्युत्सृज्य राक्षसाः ।
लङ्कामभिमुखाः सस्त्रुः
नष्टसञ्ज्ञाः प्रधाविताः ॥
Beaten up by the Vānaras, the Rākshasas lost their senses and fled towards Laṅkā, abandoning their weapons.
6.90.81
दुद्रुवुर्बहुधा भीता
राक्षसाः शतशो दिशः ।
त्यक्त्वा प्रहरणान् सर्वे
पट्टिशासिपरश्वधान् ॥
Hundreds of them, throwing down all their weapons, Paṭṭiṡas, swords and battle-axes, fled in terror in every direction.
6.90.82
केचिल्लङ्कां परित्रस्ताः
प्रविष्टा वानरार्दिताः ।
समुद्रे पतिताः केचित्
केचित् पर्वतमाश्रिताः ॥
Oppressed by the Vānaras, some entered Laṅkā in extreme fear, some fell in the ocean, and others escaped to the mountains.
6.90.83
हतमिन्द्रजितं दृष्ट्वा
शयानं समरक्षितौ ।
राक्षसानां सहस्रेषु
न कश्चित् प्रत्यदृश्यत ॥
None of the thousands of Rākshasas who saw Indrajit slain and lying on the battle field, was to be seen anywhere, anymore.
6.90.84
यथाऽस्तं गत आदित्ये
नावतिष्ठन्ति रश्मयः ।
तथा तस्मिन्निपतिते
राक्षसास्ते गता दिशः ॥
Just as the sun’s rays vanish when the sun sets, the Rākshasas vanished in every direction with him falling down.
6.90.85
शान्तरश्मिरिवादित्यो
निर्वाण इव पावकः ।
स बभूव महाबाहुः
व्यपास्तगतजीवितः ॥
His limbs flagging with life gone out of them, Indrajit of mighty arm lay dead, looking like fire that had been put off and like the sun whose rays had lost their intensity.
6.90.86
प्रशान्तपीडाबहुलो
नष्टारिष्टः प्रहर्षवान् ।
बभूव लोकः पतिते
राक्षसेन्द्रसुते तदा ॥
Relieved of its suffering and freed from its troubles the world became joyful, with the son of the Rākshasa King fallen.
6.90.87
हर्षं च शक्रो भगवान्
सह सर्वैः सुरर्षभैः ।
जगाम निहते तस्मिन्
राक्षसे पापकर्मणि ॥
Bhagawān Ṡakra along with all the bulls among Suras felt very happy, with that Rākshasa of sinful deeds slain.
6.90.88
आकाशे चापि देवानाम्
शुश्रुवे दुन्दुभिस्वनः ।
नृत्यद्भिरप्सरोभिश्च
गन्धर्वैश्च महात्मभिः ॥
The booming of kettle drums of the Dēvas and the sounds of dancing Apsarasas and the Gandharvas were heard from the sky.
6.90.89
ववर्षुः पुष्पवर्षाणि
तदद्भुतमभूत्तदा ।
प्रशशंसुर्हते तस्मिन्
राक्षसे क्रूरकर्मणि ॥
Then they showered flowers, which made an amazing spectacle to behold. They also applauded the slaying of that Rākshasa who committed cruel deeds.
6.90.90
शुद्धा आपो नभश्चैव
जहृषुर्देवदानवाः ।
आजग्मुः पतिते तस्मिन्
सर्वलोकभयावहे ॥
The waters and the sky became clear. Dēvas and Dānavas were happy and descended down, with him, who terrified the worlds, fallen.
6.90.91
ऊचुश्च सहितास्तुष्टाः
देवगन्धर्वदानवाः ।
विज्वराः शान्तकलुषाः
ब्राह्मणा विचरन्त्विति ॥
Coming together and happy, the Dēvas, Dānavas and Gandharvas said, "May all the Brāhmaṇas go about their life relieved from their suffering and freed from their worries."
6.90.92
ततोऽभ्यनन्दन् संहृष्टाः
समरे हरियूथपाः ।
तमप्रतिबलं दृष्ट्वा
हतं नैर्ऋतपुङ्गवम् ॥
And the Vānara chiefs, delighted seeing that bull among the Rākshasa, of matchless strength, fallen in battle, congratulated Lakshmaṇa.
6.90.93
विभीषणो हनूमांश्च
जाम्बवांश्चर्क्षयूथपः ।
विजयेनाभिनन्दन्तः
तुष्टुवुश्चापि लक्ष्मणम् ॥
Vibheeshaṇa, Hanumān and Jāmbavān, the leader of the Ṛkshas, congratulated Lakshmaṇa on his victory and applauded him.
6.90.94
क्ष्वेडन्तश्च प्लवन्तश्च
गर्जन्तश्च प्लवङ्गमाः ।
लब्धलक्षा रघुसुतम्
परिवार्योपतस्थिरे ॥
The Vānaras who accomplished their mission, gathered around Lakshmaṇa, the descendant of Raghu, shouting, jumping and roaring.
6.90.95
लाङ्गूलानि प्रविध्यन्तः
स्फोटयन्तश्च वानराः ।
लक्ष्मणो जयतीत्येवम्
वाक्यं विश्रावयंस्तदा ॥
Lashing their tails on the ground and slapping their shoulders, they were heard shouting, ‘Victory to Lakshmaṇa!’
6.90.96
अन्योन्यं च समाश्लिष्य
कपयो हृष्टमानसाः ।
चक्रुरुच्चावचगुणा
राघवाश्रयसत्कथाः ॥
Their hearts filled with joy, the Vānaras hugged each other and related stories of the great virtues of Rāghava to each other.
6.90.97
तदसुकरमथाभिवीक्ष्य हृष्टाः
प्रियसुहृदो युधि लक्ष्मणस्य कर्म ।
परममुपलभन् मनःप्रहर्षम्
विनिहतमिन्द्ररिपुं निशम्य देवाः ॥
All the well-wishers of Lakshmaṇa were very happy seeing him accomplish such a difficult task. The hearts of Dēvas were filled with extreme joy hearing that that foe of Indra was slain.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे नवतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninetieth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
