Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 16
In this Sarga, Vāli assures Tārā that there is nothing to fear on account of Rāma since he is well aware of Dharma. Vāli also assures her that he is going to only subdue the pride of Sugreeva but not kill him.
Then Vāli goes to fight with Sugreeva. A terrible fight ensues between them. They pound each other with fists, hands, feet and trees. After a while, Vāli gains upper hand in the fight while Sugreeva's strength weakens. Rāma notices that Sugreeva has lost his strength and is looking for help.
Rāma then mounts his arrow on the bow and shoots it. With the force of that arrow, Vāli falls down on the ground, like the flagstaff of Indra.
4.16.1
तामेवं ब्रुवतीं ताराम्
ताराधिपनिभाननाम् ।
वाली निर्भर्त्सयामास
वचनं चेदमब्रवीत् ॥
When Tārā, whose face was brighter than the lord of stars, spoke thus, Vāli reprimanded her and said:
4.16.2
गर्जतोऽस्य च सुसंरब्धं
भ्रातुः शत्रोर्विशेषतः ।
मर्षयिष्यामि केनापि
कारणेन वरानने ॥
O you of lovely face! No matter what the reason is, how can I put up with this pompous roar of a brother, who is an enemy to boot!
4.16.3
अधर्षितानां शूराणाम्
समरेष्वनिवर्तिनाम् ।
धर्षणामर्षणं भीरु
मरणादतिरिच्यते ॥
O timid one! It is worse than death for Veeras who are too powerful to be assailed and who never turned their backs on the battlefield, to submit to threats!
4.16.4
सोढुं न च समर्थोऽहम्
युद्धकामस्य संयुगे ।
सुग्रीवस्य च सम्रम्भम्
हीनग्रीवस्य गर्जतः ॥
I cannot tolerate this exuberance, this squeaky roar and this itch for a fight, of Sugreeva!
4.16.5
न च कार्यो विषादस्ते
राघवं प्रति मत्कृते ।
धर्मज्ञश्च कृतज्ञश्च
कथं पापं करिष्यति ॥
You should not be worried because of Rāghava, with regard to me; How can he, who knows Dharma and possesses a great sense of gratitude, do anything sinful?
4.16.6
निवर्तस्व सह स्त्रीभिः
कथं भूयोऽनुगच्छसि ।
सौहृदं दर्शितं तारे
मयि भक्तिः कृता त्वया ॥
Go back along with all the women; why do you still follow me? O Tāra, you have shown your love and your devotion to me.
4.16.7
प्रतियोत्स्याम्यहं गत्वा
सुग्रीवं जहि सम्भ्रमम् ।
दर्पमात्रं विनेष्यामि
न च प्राणैर्विमोक्ष्यते ॥
I shall go and fight Sugreeva. But be easy in your mind. I shall merely subdue his pride, not deprive him of his life.
4.16.8
अहं ह्याजिस्थितस्यास्य
करिष्यामि यथेप्सितम् ।
वृक्षैर्मुष्टिप्रहारैश्च
पीडितः प्रतियास्यति ।
न मे गर्वितमायस्तम्
सहिष्यति दुरात्मवान् ॥
I shall merely do what he, looking for a fight, wants. He will go back pounded by trees and the blows of fists. The rogue cannot stand up to my vigor and pride.
4.16.9
कृतं तारे सहायत्वम्
सौहृदं दर्शितं मयि ।
शापिताऽपि मम प्राणैः
निवर्तस्व जनेन च ।
अहं जित्वा निवर्तिष्ये
तमहं भ्रातरं रणे ॥
O Tāra! I appreciate your help and the concern you have shown me. Swear on my life that you will go back with all others. I shall return vanquishing him, my brother, in the fight.
4.16.11
तं तु तारा परिष्वज्य
वालिनं प्रियवादिनी ।
चकार रुदती मन्दम्
दक्षिणा सा प्रदक्षिणम् ॥
Tārā, who always wished well, embraced Vāli. Crying in a low tone, she, the capable one, circumambulated him.
4.16.12
ततः स्वस्त्ययनं कृत्वा
मन्त्रविद्विजयैषिणी ।
अन्तःपुरं सह स्त्रीभिः
प्रविष्टा शोकमोहिता ॥
Praying for his success, she invoked blessings on him uttering the appropriate mantras. She went back to the inner quarters, along with other women, dazed with grief.
4.16.13
प्रविष्टायां तु तारायाम्
सह स्त्रीभिस्स्वमालयम् ।
नगरान्निर्ययौ क्रुद्धो
महासर्प इव श्वसन् ॥
After Tārā left to her residence along with the other women, he emerged from the city in wrath, hissing like a great serpent.
4.16.14
स निश्श्वस्य महातेजा
वाली परमरोषणः ।
सर्वतश्चारयन् दृष्टिम्
शत्रुदर्शनकाङ्क्षया ॥
Heavily breathing, the powerful and extremely enraged Vāli looked all around to find out where his enemy was.
4.16.15
स ददर्श ततश्श्रीमान्
सुग्रीवं हेमपिङ्गलम् ।
सुसंवीतमवष्टब्धम्
दीप्यमानमिवानलम् ॥
He, the blessed one, then saw Sugreeva, who looked diffident with his cloth tightened and was blazing like fire in his red-gold complexion.
4.16.16
स तं दृष्ट्वा महावीर्यम्
सुग्रीवं पर्यवस्थितम् ।
गाढं परिदधे वासो
वाली परमरोषणः ॥
On seeing Sugreeva of immense valor standing ready for the fray, the irascible Vāli drew his cloth tight about his loins.
4.16.17
स वाली गाढसंवीतो
मुष्टिमुद्यम्य वीर्यवान् ।
सुग्रीवमेवाभिमुखो
ययौ योद्धुं कृतक्षणः ॥
The valiant Vāli, tightening the cloth around him, advanced towards Sugreeva with clenched fist eager to fight, now that an opportunity had presented itself.
4.16.18
श्लिष्टं मुष्टिं समुद्यम्य
संरब्धतरमागतः ।
सुग्रीवोऽपि समुद्दिश्य
वालिनं हेममालिनम् ॥
Sugreeva too, raising his clenched fist, rushed towards Vāli who was wearing a golden chain.
4.16.19
तं वाली क्रोधताम्राक्षः
सुग्रीवं रणपण्डितम् ।
आपतन्तं महावेगम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Vāli, his eyes copper-red with anger, said to Sugreeva, the veteran warrior, as he bore down upon him in all speed:
4.16.20
एष मुष्टिर्मयाबद्धो
गाढः सुनियताङ्गुलिः ।
मया वेगविमुक्तस्ते
प्राणानादाय यास्यति ॥
This tightly clenched fist of mine, with all the fingers tightly bound together, let off by me with all swiftness, is going to walk off with your life.
4.16.21
एवमुक्तस्तु सुग्रीवः
क्रुद्धो वालिनमब्रवीत् ।
तवचैष हरन्प्राणान्
मुष्टिः पततु मूर्धनि ॥
Sugreeva, thus jeered, became enraged and said to Vāli: ‘It is on your head that my fist shall descend, taking your life.’
4.16.22
ताडितस्तेन सङ्कृद्धः
समभिक्रम्य वेगतः ।
अभवच्छोणितोद्गारी
सापीड इव पर्वतः ॥
Hit by him who advanced swiftly, he became enraged with blood spurting from him like a cascade from a mountain.
4.16.23
सुग्रीवेण तु निःशंकम्
सालमुत्पाट्य तेजसा ।
गात्रेष्वभिहतो वाली
वज्रेणेव महागिरिः ॥
As for Sugreeva, he pulled out a Sāla tree by his sheer strength and delivered an unmistakable blow to the limbs of Vāli, like Vajra striking a mountain.
4.16.24
स तु वाली प्रचलितः
सालताडनविह्वलः ।
गुरुभारसमाक्रान्तो
नौ सार्थ इव सागरे ॥
And Vāli, stunned by the blows from the Sāla tree, reeled like an overloaded vessel on the high seas carrying merchants and their merchandise.
4.16.25
तौ भीमबलविक्रान्तौ
सुपर्णसमवेगिनौ ।
प्रयुद्धौ घोरवपुषौ
चन्द्रसूर्याविवाम्बरे ।
परस्परममित्रघ्नौ
छिद्रान्वेषणतत्परौ ॥
The two of them, slayers of the foe, possessed of terrifying bodies, strength and prowess, looking for every weakness in each other, commanding the speed of Suparṇa, fought as if they were the sun and the moon fighting in the sky.
4.16.26
ततोऽवर्धत वाली तु
बलवीर्यसमन्वितः ।
सूर्यपुत्रो महावीर्यः
सुग्रीवः परिहीयते ॥
Gradually, Vāli improved in strength and prowess while the son of the sun, Sugreeva, of immense strength, weakened.
4.16.27
वालिना भग्नदर्पस्तु
सुग्रीवो मन्दविक्रमः ।
वालिनं प्रति सामर्षो
दर्शयामास लाघवम् ॥
His pride vanquished by Vāli, his speed slowed down, Sugreeva, in his rage against Vāli, summoned his fighting skills (again).
4.16.28
वृक्षैः सशाखैः शिखरैः
वज्रकोटिनिभैर्नखैः ।
मुष्टिभिर्जानुभिः पद्भिः
बाहुभिश्च पुनः पुनः ।
तयोर्युद्धमभूद्घोरम्
वृत्रवासवयोरिव ॥
A terrible fight ensued between the two, like the fight between Vṛtra and Vāsava as they attacked each other repeatedly, using trees with boughs and foliage, nails that were sharp-edged like the Vajra, fists, knees, feet and hands.
4.16.30
तौ शोणिताक्तौ युध्येताम्
वानरौ वनचारिणौ ।
मेघाविव महाशब्दैः
तर्जमानौ परस्परम् ॥
Drenched in blood, the Vānaras, creatures of the wild, fought on, threatening each other and making loud sounds like clouds.
4.16.31
हीयमानमथोऽपश्यत्
सुग्रीवं वानरेश्वरम् ।
वीक्षमाणं दिशश्चैव
राघवस्स मुहुर्मुहुः ॥
Rāghava saw Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras, growing weaker and looking repeatedly in every direction.
4.16.32
ततो रामो महातेजा
आर्तं दृष्ट्वा हरीश्वरम् ।
शरं च वीक्षते वीरो
वालिनो वधकांक्षया ॥
On seeing the Lord of Vānaras feeling helpless, Rāma, the immensely strong Veera, set his sight on his arrow, intending to kill Vāli.
4.16.33
ततो धनुषि सन्धाय
शरमाशीविषोपमम् ।
पूरयामास तच्चापम्
कालचक्रमिवान्तकः ॥
Then, stringing the bow and mounting that arrow which was like a venomous viper, he drew the string like Yama wielding Kālacakra.
4.16.34
तस्य ज्यातलघोषेण
त्रस्ताः पत्ररथेश्वराः ।
प्रदुद्रुवुर्मृगाश्चैव
युगान्त इव मोहिताः ॥
The reverberating sound of the bow-string made the great birds and beasts flee, as they thought that the universe was coming to an end.
4.16.35
मुक्तस्तु वज्रनिर्घोषः
प्रदीप्ताशनिसन्निभः ।
राघवेण महाबाणो
वालिवक्षसि पातितः ॥
The arrow, discharged by Rāghava, fell on the breast of Vāli with the din of the thunderbolt and blazing like lightning.
4.16.36
ततस्तेन महातेजा
वीर्ययुक्तः कपीश्वरः ।
वेगेनाभिहतो वाली
निपपात महीतले ।
इन्द्रध्वज इवोद्धूतः
पौर्णमास्यां महीतले ।
आश्वयुक्समये मासि
गतसत्त्वो विचेतनः ॥
Hit by the arrow with a terrific force, Vāli, the Lord of Vānaras of immense strength and valor, fell down on the ground, his energy depleted and his senses lost, like the flagstaff of Indra brought down on the full moon day of the month of Āṡvayuja.
4.16.38
नरोत्तमः कालयुगान्तकोपमं
शरोत्तमं काञ्चनरूप्यभासितम् ।
ससर्ज दीप्तं तममित्रमर्दनं
सधूममग्निं मुखतो यथा हरः ॥
That best among men, who resembled Yama at the time of dissolution of the universe, released that best of the dazzling arrows that crushes the enemies, which was decked with gold and silver, like Hara blowing fire and smoke from His mouth.
4.16.39
अथोक्षितश्शोणिततोयविस्रवैः
प्रपुष्पिताशोक इवानिलोद्धतः ।
विचेतनो वासवसूनुराहवे
प्रभ्रंशितेन्द्रध्वजवत्क्षितिं गतः ॥
Drenched with blood and sweat, the son of Vāsava fell senseless on the battlefield, like an Aṡōka tree in full flower brought down by the wind and like Indra’s flagstaff thrown down.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे षोडशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixteenth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
