Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 66
In this Sarga, on seeing the fierce Kumbhakarṇa, the Vānaras and their chiefs flee. Aṅgada tells them that it is only a huge scarecrow set up by the Rākshasas and goads them to destroy it. They get together and hurl trees and rocks at him, which fall to the ground, smashed and broken, on touching him.
Kumbhakarṇa pulverizes the Vānara army and lets out a thunderous roar. The Vānaras flee again and hide wherever they can find shelter, in the trenches, in the caves, up on the mountains and underneath the water. With quite an effort, Aṅgada then brings them back again to fight, appealing to their valor, reason and allaying their fears.
6.66.1
स लङ्घयित्वा प्राकारम्
गिरिकूटोपमो महान् ।
निर्ययौ नगरात्तूर्णम्
कुम्भकर्णो महाबलः ॥
Kumbhakarṇa of immense strength, lofty like a mountain peak, came out of the city swiftly, jumping over the ramparts.
6.66.2
स ननाद महानादम्
समुद्रमभिनादयन् ।
जनयन्निव निर्घातान्
विधमन्निव पर्वतान् ॥
He uttered a mighty shout that reverberated in the ocean, seemed to out do thunderbolts and blow away the mountains.
6.66.3
तमवध्यं मधवता
यमेन वरुणेन वा ।
प्रेक्ष्य भीमाक्षमायान्तम्
वानरा विप्रदुद्रुवुः ॥
The Vānaras fled on seeing him of the frightful eyes come, who was immune from death at the hands of even Indra, Yama and Varuṇa.
6.66.4
तांस्तु विप्रद्रुतान् दृष्ट्वा
राजपुत्रोऽङ्गदोऽब्रवीत् ।
नलं नीलं गवाक्षं च
कुमुदं च महाबलम् ॥
Seeing them flee, Aṅgada, the prince, addressed Nala, Neela, Gavāksha and Kumuda, of immense strength:
6.66.5
आत्मानस्तानि विस्मृत्य
वीर्याण्यभिजनानि च ।
क्व गच्छत भयत्रस्ताः
प्राकृता हरयो यथा ॥
Where are you going, frightened like some ordinary Vānaras, forgetting your valor, your noble ancestry, and all?
6.66.6
साधु सौम्या निवर्तध्वम्
किं प्राणान् परिरक्षथ ।
नालं युद्धाय वै रक्षो
महतीयं बिभीषिका ॥
For heaven’s sake come back, my friends, for what purpose are you trying to save your lives? This Rākshasa is not capable of fighting, for it is a just a huge scarecrow.
6.66.7
महतीमुत्थितामेनाम्
राक्षसानां बिभीषिकाम् ।
विक्रमाद्विधमिष्यामो
निवर्तध्वं प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
Come back, O Vānaras! We will, with your prowess, destroy this huge scarecrow set up by the Rākshasas!
6.66.8
कृच्छ्रेण तु समाश्वस्य
सङ्गम्य च ततस्ततः ।
वृक्षान् गृहीत्वा हरयः
सम्प्रतस्थू रणाजिरम् ॥
Calming themselves with great effort, the Vānaras gathered in knots here and there, grabbed trees, and headed to the battlefield.
6.66.9
ते निवृत्य तु संरब्धाः
कुम्भकर्णं वनौकसः ।
निजघ्नुः परमक्रुद्धाः
समदा इव कुञ्जराः ॥
Returning thus with all urgency, the Vana dwellers struck Kumbhakarṇa with all the fury of elephants in rut.
6.66.10
प्रांशुभिर्गिरिशृङ्गैश्च
शिलाभिश्च महाबलः ।
पादपैः पुष्पिताग्रैश्च
हन्यमानो न कम्पते ॥
Though struck by tall peaks, rocks and trees in flower, he of immense strength was not shaken even a bit.
6.66.11
तस्य गात्रेषु पतिता
भिद्यन्ते शतशः शिलाः ।
पादपाः पुष्पिताग्राश्च
भग्नाः पेतुर्महीतले ॥
Striking his body, the rocks, shivered into hundreds of pieces, and the trees in flower, broken, fell on the ground.
6.66.12
सोऽपि सैन्यानि सङ्क्रुद्धो
वानराणां महौजसाम् ।
ममन्थ परमायत्तो
वनान्यग्निरिवोत्थितः ॥
Outraged and putting forth all his strength, he pounded the army of those extremely powerful Vānaras as a raging fire consumes the forest.
6.66.13
लोहितार्द्रास्तु बहवः
शेरते वानरर्षभाः ।
निरस्ताः पतिता भूमौ
ताम्रपुष्पा इव द्रुमाः ॥
Many bulls among Vānaras, shoved (by him) fell on the ground, their bodies wet with blood, like trees that bore red flowers.
6.66.14
लङ्घयन्तः प्रधावन्तो
वानरा नावलोकयन् ।
केचित्समुद्रे पतिताः
केचिद्गगनमाश्रिताः ॥
The Vānaras, leaped and ran without looking back, some falling into the sea and some flying into the sky.
6.66.15
वध्यमानास्तु ते वीरा
राक्षसेन च लीलया ।
सागरं येन ते तीर्णाः
पथा तेनव दुद्रुवुः ॥
Attacked by the Rākshasa for whom it was child's play, those Veeras fled along the same track by which they had crossed the ocean.
6.66.16
ते स्थलानि तथा निम्नम्
विषण्णवदना भयात् ।
ऋक्षा वृक्षान् समारूढाः
केचित् पर्वतमाश्रिताः ॥
Some ducked in low lying trenches their faces miserable with fear, and some got up on the mountains and the Ṛkshas climbed up the trees.
6.66.17
ममज्जुरर्णवे केचिद्
गुहाः केचित्समाश्रिताः ।
निपेतुः केचिदपरे
केचिन्नैवावतस्थिरे ।
केचिद्भूमौ निपतिताः
केचित्सुप्ता मृता इव ॥
Some hid under the ocean's water, and some took refuge in caves. Some fled and some could not even stand steady. Some fell on the ground and some pretended to be dead.
6.66.18
तान् समीक्ष्याङ्गदो भग्नान्
वानरानिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Looking at those Vānaras whose morale was broken, Aṅgada said these words:
6.66.19
अवतिष्ठत युध्यामो
निवर्तध्वं प्लवङ्गमाः ।
भग्नानां वो न पश्यामि
परिगम्य महीमिमाम् ।
स्थानं सर्वे निवर्तध्वम्
किं प्राणान् परिरक्षथ ॥
Come back, O Vānaras! Take a stand, and let us fight! I do not see you, defeated as you are, finding a shelter no matter where you go on this earth. For what purpose are you trying to save your lives, please turn back!
6.66.20
निरायुधानां द्रवताम्
असङ्गगतिपौरुषाः ।
दारा ह्यपहसिष्यन्ति
स वै घातः सुजीवताम् ॥
O you, whose valor and prowess knows no check! Your wives will sneer at you if you flee, giving up your arms! That is nothing but death for one who has thus far led an honorable life!
Lord Krishna tells the same to Arjuna, when he was upset by the prospect of killing his own kith and kin:
Bhagavadgeeta 2.34:
अकीर्तिं चापि भूतानि
कथयिष्यंति तेऽव्ययाम् ।
संभावितस्य चाकीर्तिः
मरणादतिरिच्यते ॥
People will recall your infamous deeds forever. For the honorable, disrepute is worse than death.
6.66.21
कुलेषु जाताः सर्वे स्म
विस्तीर्णेषु महत्सु च ।
क्व गच्छथ भयत्रस्ता
प्राकृता हरयो यथा ।
अनार्याः खलु यद्भीताः
त्यक्त्वा वीर्यं प्रधावत ॥
You are all born into noble, extensive and reputed families. Where will you go (and hide), scared like ordinary Vānaras? By giving up your (natural) valor and running out of fear, you become ignoble!
6.66.23
विकत्थनानि वो यानि
तदा वै जनसंसदि ।
तानि वः क्व नु यातानि
सोदग्राणि महान्ति च ॥
Where did all those outsized boasts that you so fervently made in the midst of gatherings of people go?
6.66.24
भीरुप्रवादाः श्रूयन्ते
यस्तु जीविति धिक्कृतः ।
मार्गः सत्पुरुषैर्जुष्टः
सेव्यतां त्यज्यतां भयम् ॥
Cowards who hang on to life even after being shamed, will hear only scorn and contempt. Please get over your fear and take the path taken by honorable men.
6.66.25
शयामहे वा निहताः
पृथिव्यामल्पजीविताः ।
प्राप्नुयामो ब्रह्मलोकम्
दुष्प्रापं च कुयोधिभिः ॥
We will fall on the ground, slain, if we are bestowed with only a short life; but we will ascend to the world of Brahma, which no coward can ever hope to.
6.66.26
अवाप्नुयामः कीर्तिं वा
निहत्वा शत्रुमाहवे ।
निहता वीरलोकस्य
भोक्ष्यामो वसु वानराः ॥
O Vānaras! If we slay the foe, we will enjoy fame in this world! Or if we are slain, we will enjoy the privileges reserved for the Veeras in the afterworld.
Again, Krishna tells the same to Arjuna, summarizing his advice:
Bhagavadgeeta 2.37:
हतो वा प्राप्स्यसि स्वर्गम्
जित्वा वा भोक्ष्यसे महीम्
If slain, you will attain heaven. If victorious, you will enjoy the world.
6.66.27
न कुम्भकर्णः काकुत्स्थम्
दृष्ट्वा जीवन् गमिष्यति ।
दीप्यमानमिवासाद्य
पतङ्गो ज्वलनं यथा ॥
Kumbhakarṇa is not going to escape alive once he happens to come in the presence of Kākutstha, any more than the moth that comes near a blazing fire.
6.66.28
पलायनेन चोद्दिष्टाः
प्राणान् रक्षामहे वयम् ।
एकेन बहवो भग्ना
यशो नाशं गमिष्यति ॥
If we run away from what we are supposed to do, we might save our lives, but with so many of us defeated by just one, our reputation will be completely ruined.
6.66.29
एवं ब्रुवाणं तं शूरम्
अङ्गदं कनकाङ्गदम् ।
द्रवमाणास्ततो वाक्यम्
ऊचुः शूरविगर्हितम् ॥
When the heroic Aṅgada of the golden shoulder-rings rebuked them thus, they, who had taken to their heels, answered him in words that any man of prowess would sneer at:
6.66.30
कृतं नः कदनं घोरम्
कुम्भकर्णेन रक्षसा ।
न स्थानकालो गच्छामो
दयितं जीवितं हि नः ॥
We have fought a fierce battle with the Rākshasa Kumbhakarṇa. This is not a time to make a stand; our lives are dear to us and we must go.
6.66.31
एतावदुक्त्वा वचनम्
सर्वे ते भेजिरे दिशः ।
भीमं मीमाक्षमायान्तम्
दृष्ट्वा वानरयूथपाः ॥
Saying this the Vānara chiefs scattered in all directions finding that the formidable foe with frightful eyes was bearing down upon them.
6.66.32
द्रवमाणास्तु ते वीरा
अङ्गदेन वलीमुखाः ।
सान्त्वैश्चैवानुमानैश्च
ततः सर्वे निवर्तिताः ॥
But finally, all those fleeing, but nonetheless valiant, Vānaras were brought back through pleading and reasoning by Aṅgada.
6.66.33
प्रहर्षमुपनीताश्च
वालिपुत्रेण धीमता ।
आज्ञाप्रतीक्षास्तस्थुश्च
सर्वे वानरयूथपाः ॥
With cheer brought back to them by the sagacious son of Vāli, all the Vānara chiefs stood ready, waiting for orders (from him).
6.66.34
ऋषभशरभमैन्दधूम्रनीलाः
कुमुदसुषेणगवाक्षरम्भताराः ।
द्विविदपनसवायुपुत्रमुख्याः
त्वरिततराभिमुखं रणं प्रयाताः ॥
Then chiefs like Ṛshabha, Ṡarabha, Mainda, Dhūmra, Neela, Kumuda, Sushēṇa, Gavāksha, Rambha, Tāra, Dwivida, Panasa and the son of Vāyu, proceeded towards the scene of battle, in all the haste.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे षट्षष्टितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty sixth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
