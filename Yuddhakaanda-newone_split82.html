Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 82
In this Sarga, Hanumān appeals to all the Vānara chiefs to fight back. He restores their spirits and leads the fight. After successfully putting a check on the Rākshasa forces led by Indrajit, Hanumān asks the Vānaras not to attempt to make any further advances, as Seetā, for whose sake the war is fought, is no more. He suggests that they should go to Rāma and Sugreeva to report the matter and do what they decide as the appropriate course of action. Indrajit then goes to the shrine of Nikumbhila and offers oblations of flesh and blood into fire, for the prosperity of the Rākshasas.
6.82.1
श्रुत्वा तु भीमनिर्ह्रादम्
शक्राशनिसमस्वनम् ।
वीक्षमाणा दिशः सर्वा
दुद्रुवुर्वानरर्षभाः ॥
Hearing that frightful roar that resembled the sound of the thunderbolt of Ṡakra, all the bulls among Vānaras looked in every direction and fled.
6.82.2
तानुवाच ततःसर्वान्
हनुमान् मारुतात्मजः ।
विषण्णवदनान् दीनान्
त्रस्तान्विद्रवतः पृथक् ॥
Then, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu said to each one of them who was fleeing terrified, sad and with woebegone faces:
6.82.3
कस्माद्विषण्णवदना
विद्रवध्वं प्लवङ्गमाः ।
त्यक्तयुद्धसमुत्साहाः
शूरत्वं क्व नु वो गतम् ॥
Why do you flee, O Vānaras, with your faces so woebegone, giving up all enthusiasm to fight? Where has all your heroism gone?
6.82.4
पृष्ठतोऽनुव्रजध्वं माम्
अग्रतो यान्तमाहवे ।
शूरैरभिजनोपेतैः
अयुक्तं हि निवर्तितुम् ॥
Let me lead the fight and you follow me. It does not behoove a highborn warrior to turn his back (in the battlefield).
6.82.5
एवमुक्ताः सुसंक्रुद्धाः
वायुपुत्रेण वानराः ।
शैलशृङ्गान् द्रुमांश्चैव
जगृहुर्हृष्टमानसाः ॥
Thus admonished by the son of Vāyu and their fury rekindled, they armed themselves with mountain crests and trees, their hearts filled with high spirits.
6.82.6
अभिपेतुश्च गर्जन्तो
राक्षसान् वानरर्षभाः ।
परिवार्य हनूमन्तम्
अन्वयुश्च महाहवे ॥
Uttering war cries, the bulls among Vānaras, dashed towards the Rākshasas, gathering around Hanumān and following his lead in that great battle.
6.82.7
स तैर्वानरमुख्यैश्च
हनुमान् सर्वतो वृतः ।
हुताशन इवार्चिष्मान्
अदहच्छत्रुवाहिनीम् ॥
With the Vānara chiefs supporting him on all sides, he, like a fire with stretched tongues, burnt down the enemy forces.
6.82.8
स राक्षसानां कदनम्
चकार सुमहाकपिः ।
वृतो वानरसैन्येन
कालान्तकयमोपमः ॥
With the Vānara army about him, the great Vānara wreaked havoc among the Rākshasas as if he were Yama who puts an end to Time.
6.82.9
स तु कोपेन चाविष्टः
शोकेन च महाकपिः ।
हनुमान् रावणिरथे
महतीं पातयच्छिलाम् ॥
Overwhelmed by wrath and grief, Hanumān threw a huge rock on the chariot of Rāvaṇa’s son.
6.82.10
तामापतन्तीं दृष्ट्वैव
रथः सारथिना तदा ।
विधेयाश्वसमायुक्तः
सुदूरमपवाहितः ॥
The moment he saw it coming, the charioteer diverted his chariot, to which were yoked well trained horses, away to a long distance.
6.82.11
तमिन्द्रजितमप्राप्य
रथस्थं सहसारथिम् ।
विवेशधरणीं भित्त्वा
सा शिला व्यर्थमुद्यता ॥
Instead of striking Indrajit and the charioteer who were in the chariot, that rock shattered the earth and got buried in it, and became wasted.
6.82.12
पातितायां शिलायां तु
रक्षसां व्यथिता चमूः ।
निपतन्त्या च शिलया
राक्षसा मथिता भृशम् ॥
However, that rock, as it fell, caused great suffering among the Rākshasas and where it fell, the Rākshasas were squashed to pulp.
6.82.13
तमभ्यधावन् शतशो
नदन्तः काननौकसः ।
ते द्रुमांश्च महावीर्या
गिरिशृङ्गाणि चोद्यताः ।
क्षिपन्तीन्द्रजितं सङ्ख्ये
वानरा भीमविक्रमाः ॥
Hundreds of Vana dwellers of extraordinary valor dashed towards him (Indrajit), holding trees and mountain crests and shouting. And those Vānaras of terrific prowess hurled them at Indrajit in that fight.
6.82.14
वृक्षशैलमहावर्षम्
विसृजन्तः प्लवङ्गमाः ।
शत्रूणां कदनं चक्रुः
नेदुश्च विविधैः स्वरैः ॥
The Vānaras then assailed the foe with a great downpour of trees and rocks, uttering battle cries of every kind.
6.82.15
वानरैस्तैर्महावीर्यैः
घोररूपा निशाचराः ।
वीर्यादभिहता वृक्षैः
व्यचेष्टन्त रणक्षितौ ॥
The Night-rovers of terrifying forms, attacked by Vānaras of extraordinary valor and hit with the trees with force, fell down and lay writhing on the ground.
6.82.16
स्वसैन्यमभिवीक्ष्याथ
वानरार्दितमिन्द्रजित् ।
प्रगृहीतायुधः क्रुद्धः
परानभिमुखो ययौ ॥
Seeing his army pressed hard by the Vānaras, Indrajit was in rage and attacked the enemy, arming himself with weapons.
6.82.17
स शरौघानवसृजन्
स्वसैन्येनाभिसंवृतः ।
जघान कपिशार्दूलान्
स बहून् दृढविक्रमः ॥
With his forces supporting him all around, he of unswerving prowess slew many bulls among Vānaras by raining volleys of arrows.
6.82.18
शूलैरशनिभिः खड्गैः
पट्टिशैः कूटमुद्गरैः ।
ते चाप्यनुचरास्तस्य
वानरान् जघ्नुराहवे ॥
His followers also slew many Vānaras in that battle with spears, Vajras, swords, Paṭṭiṡas, hammers and Mudgaras.
6.82.19
सस्कन्धविटपैः सालैः
शिलाभिश्च महाबलः ।
हनुमान् कदनं चक्रे
रक्षसां भीमकर्मणाम् ॥
(In response) Hanumān of immense strength inflicted terrible suffering on the Rākshasas of frightful deeds, with rocks and burly Sāla trees with many boughs.
6.82.20
स निवार्य परानीकम्
अब्रवीत्तान् वनौकसः ।
हनुमान् सन्निवर्तध्वम्
न नः साध्यमिदं बलम् ।
त्यक्त्वा प्राणान् विवेष्टन्तो
रामप्रियचिकीर्षवः ।
यन्निमित्तं हि युद्ध्यामो
हता सा जनकात्मजा ।
इममर्थं हि विज्ञाप्य
रामं सुग्रीवमेव च ।
तौ यत् प्रतिविधास्येते
तत् करिष्यामहे वयम् ॥
Having thus repelled the enemy forces, Hanumān said to the Vana dwellers:
Let us go back; there is no point in fighting with this force. Janaka’s daughter, for whose sake we have been fighting, prepared to lay our lives to please Rāma, has been slain.
Let us go and apprise Rāma and Sugreeva of this development and then we will do what they decide as the appropriate response.
6.82.23
इत्युक्त्वा वानरश्रेष्ठो
वारयन् सर्ववानरान् ।
शनैः शनैरसंत्रस्तः
सबलः सन्न्यवर्तत ॥
Saying this, that eminent Vānara stopped all the Vānaras from making any further advances and slowly but steadily retreated along with his army.
6.82.24
ततः प्रेक्ष्य हनूमन्तम्
व्रजन्तं यत्र राघवः ।
स होतुकामो दुष्टात्मा
गतश्चैत्यनिकुम्भिलाम् ॥
When he found that Hanumān was going where Rāghava was, the evil minded one (Indrajit) (took the opportunity) to go to the shrine of Nikumbhila to perform offerings.
The reason for Indrajit to go to Nikumbhila and make offerings will be evident later in this Sarga and in the following Sargas.
6.82.25
निकुम्भिलामधिष्ठाय
पावकं जुहवेन्द्रजित् ।
यज्ञभूम्यां तु विधिवत्
पावकस्तेन रक्षसा ।
हूयमानः प्रजज्वाल
मांसशोणितभुक्तदा ॥
Sitting in Nikumbhila, Indrajit made offerings in the fire at the Yajña grounds in a customary manner. The fire blazed, consuming the flesh and blood offered by that Rākshasa.
6.82.27
सोऽर्चिःपिनद्धो ददृशे
होमशोणिततर्पितः ।
सन्ध्यागत इवादित्यः
सुतीव्रोऽग्निः समुत्थितः ॥
Fed well with the offerings of blood, the fire razed fiercely, with its tongues of flame twisted around each other, looking like the sun in the evening sky.
6.82.28
अथेन्द्रजिद्राक्षसभूतये तु
जुहाव हव्यं विधिना विधानवित् ।
दृष्ट्वा व्यतिष्ठन्त च राक्षसास्ते
महासमूहेषु नयानयज्ञाः ॥
Indrajit, who was well-versed in the procedures, made oblations in a customary manner, for the prosperity of the Rākshasas.
And the Rākshasas, who knew what should and what should not be done (according to the rules), stood by the side watching, in large numbers.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्व्यशीतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighty second Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
