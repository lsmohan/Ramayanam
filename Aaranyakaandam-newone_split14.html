Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 14
In this Sarga, Rāma, as he heads to Pancavaṭi, sees a gigantic bird Jaṭāyu and asks who he is. Jaṭāyu says that he is a friend of his father Daṡaratha and speaks to Rāma affectionately.
Jaṭāyu explains his lineage starting all the way from Kardama Prajāpati and takes the occasion to explain how all beings came to be created from Kardama.
Jaṭāyu offers that he can be a companion to Rāma in the Vana and guard Seetā when Rāma and Lakshmaṇa go out. Rāma feels very happy, thanks Jaṭāyu. He proceeds to Pancavaṭi along with Lakshmaṇa, Seetā and Jaṭāyu.
3.14.1
अथ पञ्चवटीं गच्छन्
अन्तरा रघुनन्दनः ।
आससाद महाकायं
गृध्रं भीमपराक्रमम् ॥
On his way to Pancavaṭi, (Rāma) the delight of Raghus came across a gigantic eagle of formidable prowess.
3.14.2
तं दृष्ट्वा तौ महाभागौ
वटस्थं रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
मेनाते राक्षसं पक्षिं
ब्रुवाणौ को भवानिति ॥
Seeing him on a banyan tree, the blessed Rāma and Lakshmaṇa thought that bird was a Rākshasa. They asked, ‘Who are you?’.
3.14.3
स तौ मधुरया वाचा
सौम्यया प्रीणयन्निव ।
उवाच वत्स मां विद्धि
वयस्यं पितुरात्मनः ॥
Then, he, in a sweet and gentle voice evidently intent on pleasing them, said: ‘My child! Know that I am a friend of your father’.
3.14.4
स तं पितृसखं बुद्ध्वा
पूजयामास राघवः ।
स तस्य कुलमव्यग्रं
अथ पप्रच्छ नाम च ॥
Knowing him to be his father’s friend, Rāghava paid respects to him. Then he enquired about his name and lineage.
The modern urban mind, touchy about any conversation on lineages and castes, may go off tangent here and wonder: ‘why do people ask others about their lineage or caste’?
Well, asking about lineage or caste in the olden times (and even today in villages) is equivalent to asking, ‘where do you work?’ in modern times.
We understand people either by knowing them personally or by learning about their background.
Knowing lineage was a way of knowing people in olden times. Knowing employment (or vocation) is a way of knowing people in modern times.
3.14.5
रामस्य वचनं श्रुत्वा
सर्वभूतसमुद्भवम् ।
आचचक्षे द्विजस्तस्मै
कुलमात्मानमेव च ॥
In response to the words of Rāma, the bird told him about its lineage and also about the creation of all beings.
3.14.6
पूर्वकाले महाबाहो
ये प्रजापतयोऽभवन् ।
तान्मे निगदतस्सर्वान्
आदितश्शृणु राघव ॥
Hear me tell, O Rāghava of the mighty arm, let me start recounting from the beginning, and give the details of Prajāpatis that were there a long time ago.
3.14.7
कर्दमः प्रथमस्तेषां
विक्रीतस्तदनन्तरः ।
शेषश्च संश्रयश्चैव
बहुपुत्रश्च वीर्यवान् ।
स्थाणुर्मरीचिरत्रिश्च
क्रतुश्चैव महाबलः ।
पुलस्त्यश्चाङ्गिराश्चैव
प्रचेताः पूलहस्तथा ।
दक्षो विवस्वानपरोऽ-
रिष्टनेमिश्च राघव ।
काश्यपश्च महातेजाः
तेषामासीच्च पश्चिमः ॥
Kardama was the first among them. Then came Vikreeta, Ṡēsha, Saṃṡraya, the valiant Bahuputra, Sthāṇu, Atri, Mareeci, Kratu of great strength, Pulastya, Aṅgeerasa, Pracēta, Pulaha, and Daksha. After that came Vivaswan. The supremely radiant Kāṡyapa, who was also known as Arishṭanēmi was the last of them.
3.14.10
प्रजापतेस्तु दक्षस्य
बभूवुरिति विश्रुतम् ।
षष्टिर्दुहितरो राम
यशस्विन्यो महायशः ॥
O renowned Rāma! It is well known that Prajāpati Daksha had sixty daughters of great fame.
3.14.11
काश्यपः प्रतिजग्राह
तासामष्टौ सुमध्यमाः ।
अदितिं च दितिं चैव
दनुमप्यथ कालिकाम् ।
ताम्रां क्रोधवशां चैव
मनुं चाप्यनलामपि ॥
Kāṡyapa married eight of those women of lovely waists, Aditi, Diti, Danu, Kālika, Tāmra, Krōdhvaṡa, Manu and Anala.
3.14.12
तास्तु कन्यास्ततः प्रीतः
काश्यपः पुनरब्रवीत् ।
पुत्रांस्त्रैलोक्यभर्तॄन्वै
जनयिष्यथ मत्समान् ॥
The pleased Kāṡyapa, said to those young women: ‘Bear me sons who are equal to me, that can rule all the three worlds.’
3.14.13
अदितिस्तन्मना राम
दितिश्च मनुजर्षभ ।
कालिका च महाबाहो
शेषास्त्वमनसोऽभवन् ॥
O Rāma of the might arm, O bull among men! Aditi, Diti and Kālika were enthusiastic about it. while the rest were not as much.
3.14.14
अदित्यां जज्ञिरे देवाः
त्रयस्त्रिंशदरिंदम ।
आदित्या वसवो रुद्रा
ह्यश्विनौ च परन्तप ॥
O subduer of the foe! Thirty three Dēvas known as the Ādityas, the Vasus, the Rudras and the Aṡwins were born to Aditi.
Twelve Ādityas, eight Vasus, eleven Rudras, and two Aṡwins make up a total of thirty three Dēvas.
3.14.15
दितिस्त्वजनयत्पुत्रान्
दैत्यांस्तात यशस्विनः ।
तेषामियं वसुमती
पुराऽसीत्सवनार्णवा ॥
My child! Diti gave birth to the famed Daityas. This earth with its vegetation and oceans belonged to them, in the beginning.
3.14.16
दनुस्त्वजनयत्पुत्रं
अश्वग्रीवमरिन्दम ।
नरकं कालकंचैव
कालिकापि व्यजायत ॥
O subduer of foes! Danu gave birth to Hayagreeva. Kālika gave birth to Naraka and Kālaka.
3.14.17
क्रौञ्चीं भासीं तथा श्येनीं
धृतराष्ट्रीं तथा शुकीम् ।
ताम्रापि सुषुवे कन्याः
पञ्चैता लोकविश्रुताः ॥
Tāmra gave birth to five daughters, famed in the world as Krouncee, Bhāsee, Ṡyēnee, Dhṛtarāshṭree and Ṡukee.
3.14.18
उलूकाञ्जनयत्क्रौञ्ची
भासी भासान्व्यजायत ।
श्येनी श्येनांश्च गृध्रांश्च
व्यजायत सुतेजसः ।
धृतराष्ट्रीतु हंसांश्च
कलहंसांश्च सर्वशः ॥
Krouncee gave birth to owls, Bhāsee to vultures; Ṡyēnee brought forth the powerful hawks and eagles. Dhṛtarāshṭree gave birth to all the swans and Kalahaṃsas.
3.14.20
चक्रवाकांश्च भद्रं ते
विजज्ञे सापि भामिनी ।
शुकी नतां विजज्ञे तु
नताया विनता सुता ।
दश क्रोधवशा राम
विजज्ञे ह्यात्मसम्भवाः ।
मृगीं च मृगमन्दां च
हरीं भद्रमदामपि ।
मातङ्गीमपि शार्दूलीं
श्वेतां च सुरभिं तथा ।
सर्वलक्षणसम्पन्नां
सुरसां कद्रुकामपि ॥
O Rāma, may all bode well for you! That lovely woman also gave birth to Cakravākas. Ṡukee brought forth Nata, and Nata’s daughter was Vinata. Krōdhvaṡa gave birth to ten daughters, Mṛgee, Mṛgamanda, Hari, Bhadramada, Mātaṅgi, Ṡardūli, Ṡwēta, Surabhi, Surasa endowed with perfectly shaped body, and Kadruka.
3.14.23
अपत्यं तु मृगास्सर्वे
मृग्या नरवरोत्तम ।
ऋक्षाश्च मृगमन्दायाः
सृमराश्चमरा स्तथा ॥
O best of the best among men! All deer are the offspring of Mṛgee, and the bears, Sṛmaras and Camaras, are born of Mṛgamanda.
The progress of mankind never stops. We learn more and more every day about everything, including about how we came into being. Just in the last couple of centuries alone, with the theory of evolution backed by the science of genome, we came to know a lot more about how we evolved over time.
Before we had all this information, we depended on imagination and legends to answer the question of how we evolved. This Sarga describes one such.
What is striking about the description of genealogy in this Sarga is that it establishes that all beings, from Dēvas to humans to elephants to horses to snakes to birds to trees, are biological cousins, just as modern science says.
The kinship among all species has been a key aspect of Indian Thought from the beginning. It helped the Indian Civilization feel oneness with all beings, including mammals, birds and trees.
Whereas, it must be noted that Abrahamic Theosophy went in the direction of saying that man is special and is created in the image of god, and the rest of the beings are created for his consumption and pleasure. Thus the Abrahamic Theosophy fails to give theosophical support for humans to feel kinship with other beings.
This idea of kinship among beings and the evolutionary progress among the ten Avatāras of Vishṇu made it easier for Indians to digest and embrace the theory of evolution. And the lack of kinship among beings made it difficult for the Abrahamic religions to reconcile with the theory of evolution, and hence the vehement and continued opposition to it.
3.14.24
हर्याश्च हरयोऽपत्यं
वानराश्च तरस्स्विनः ।
ततस्त्विरावतीं नाम
जज्ञे भद्रमदा सुताम् ॥
The lions are the offspring of Hari; likewise the swift monkeys. And then, Bhadramada gave birth to a daughter by name Airāvati.
3.14.25
तस्यास्स्वैरावतः पुत्रो
लोकनाथो महागजः ।
मातङ्ग्या स्त्वथ मातङ्गा
अपत्यं मनुजर्षभ ॥
O bull among men! Her son was Airavata, the great tusker, lord of the world. The elephants are the children of Mātaṅgi,
3.14.26
गोलाङ्गूलांश्च शार्दूली
व्याघ्रांश्चाजनयत्सुतान् ।
दिशागजांश्च काकुत्स्थ
श्वेताप्यजनयत्सुतान् ॥
O Kākutstha! Ṡardūli gave birth to Gōlāṅgūlas and tigers. Ṡwēta gave birth to the elephants of the cardinals as her sons.
3.14.27
ततो दुहितरौ राम
सुरभिर्द्वेव्यजायत ।
रोहिणीं नाम भद्रं ते
गन्धर्वीं च यशस्स्विनीम् ॥
O Rāma, may all bode well for you! Then Surabhi gave birth to Rōhiṇee and the renowned Gandharvee, as her daughters.
3.14.28
रोहिण्यजनयद्गावै
गन्धर्वी वाजिनस्सुतान् ।
सुरसाऽजनयन्नागान्
राम कद्रूस्तु पन्नगान् ॥
Rōhiṇee gave birth to cows, and Gandharvee to horses. Surasa gave birth to the Nāgas and Kadru to the Pannagas.
Nāgas are many-hooded serpents and Pannagas are ordinary snakes.
3.14.29
मनुर्मनुष्यान् जनयत्
राम पुत्रान् यशस्विनः ।
ब्राह्मणान् क्षत्रियान् वैश्यान्
शूद्रांश्च मनुजर्षभ ।
सर्वान् पुण्यफलान् वृक्षान्
अनलापि व्यजायत ॥
O Rāma, O bull among men! Manu created the renowned humans as her children, the Brāhmaṇas, Kshatriyas, Vaiṡyas and Sūdras. Anala brought forth all trees that bear wholesome fruit.
Reading this Sarga, one cannot help but realize that there are many legends of how the many species came into being.
Even considering just the human species, the legend here says that they are born of Manu. Bhagavad Geeta says that the four Varṇas were created by Bhagawān Krishna. Purusha Sooktam says that the four classes of humans emerged from various body parts of the Purusha.
When taken literally, these legends may seem to contradict each other, but when taken figuratively, one can see how all of them say the same thing, that there are four classes among humans.
Contrary to what people of today tend to believe, it should be noted that there is no mention of one of these four being higher or lower to the other.
3.14.30
विनता च शुकी पौत्री
कद्रूः च सुरसा स्वसा ।
कद्रूर्नागं सहस्रास्यं
विजज्ञे धरणीधरम् ।
द्वौ पुत्रौ विनतायास्तु
गरुडोऽरुण एव च ॥
As far as Vinata the grand daughter of Ṡuki and Kadru, the sister of Surasa are concerned, Kadru gave birth to the Nāga of thousand hoods who bears the earth on his head and Vinata had two sons, Garuḍa and Aruṇa.
3.14.32
तस्माज्जातोऽहमरुणात्
सम्पातिस्तु ममाग्रजः ।
जटायुरिति मां विद्धि
श्येनीपुत्रमरिन्दम ॥
O subduer of the foe! From that Aruṇa, I was born; and Saṃpāti is my elder brother. Know that I am Jaṭāyu, the son of Ṡyēnee.
3.14.33
सोऽहं वाससहायस्ते
भविष्यामि यदीच्छसि ।
इदं दुर्गं हि कान्तारं
मृगराक्षस सेवितम् ।
सीतां च तात रक्षिष्ये
त्वयि याते सलक्ष्मणे ॥
I shall be your neighbor and companion, if you please. This is a dense wilderness infested by beasts and Rākshasas. My child, I will look after Seetā, when you and Lakshmaṇa go out.
3.14.34
जटायुषं तं प्रतिपूज्य राघवो
मुदा परिष्वज्य च संनतोऽभवत् ।
पितुर्हि शुश्राव सखित्वमात्मवान्
जटायुषा सङ्कथितं पुनः पुनः ॥
Rāghava reciprocated Jaṭāyu with all honor and with all joy, embraced him and offered his Vandanas. He sincerely listened to Jaṭāyu, as he repeatedly mentioned his friendship with his father.
3.14.35
स तत्र सीतां परिदाय मैथिलीं
सहैव तेनातिबलेन पक्षिणा ।
जगाम तां पञ्चवटीं सलक्ष्मणो
रिपून्दिधक्षञ्छलभानिवानलः ॥
Taking Maithili with him and joined by that mighty strong bird, he, along with Lakshmaṇa, went on to Pancavaṭi, to burn the foes like fire would moths.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे चतुर्दशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fourteenth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
