Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 13
In this Sarga, Mahāpārṡwa suggests to Rāvaṇa that he should enjoy Seetā by force and that he should have nothing more to fear than he already had. Then Rāvaṇa reveals that he forced himself upon Puñjikasthalā once upon a time, and Lord Brahma who came to know about it cursed that his head will shiver into a hundred pieces if he ever forces himself upon another woman. Then he reassures himself and everyone else saying that he can burn Rāma with his arrows and subdue the enemy forces with the help of his own forces.
6.13.1
रावणं क्रुद्धमाज्ञाय
महापार्श्वो महाबलः ।
मुहूर्तमनुसञ्चिन्त्य
प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing that Rāvaṇa was very angry, the immensely strong Mahāpārṡwa thought for a while, and then said, with his palms joined in reverence:
6.13.2
यः खल्वपि वनं प्राप्य
मृगव्यालसमाकुलम् ।
न विबेन्मधु सम्प्राप्तम्
स नरो बालिशो भवेत् ॥
One who manages to enter a forest that is infested with wild deer and beasts, but fails to drink the honey he finds, is none but a fool.
6.13.3
ईश्वरस्येश्वरः कोऽस्ति
तव शत्रुनिबर्हण ।
रमस्व सह वैदेह्या
शत्रूनाक्रम्य मूर्धसु ॥
You are the lord and there is no one to lord over you, O scorcher of the foe! Stomp on the heads of the enemies, and enjoy yourself with Vaidēhee.
6.13.4
बलात् कुक्कुटवृत्तेन
वर्तस्व सुमहाबल ।
आक्रम्याक्रम्य सीतां वै
तथा भुङ्क्ष्व रमस्व च ॥
O you of immense strength! Force yourself on Seetā like a cock does (itself on a hen) and enjoy her and sport with her.
6.13.5
लब्धकामस्य ते पश्चात्
आगमिष्यति किं भयम् ।
प्राप्तमप्राप्तकालं वा
सर्वं प्रतिविधास्यते ॥
What more do you have to fear by quenching your passion? Whatever (danger) is already present or whatever might arise in future, can be dealt with very easily.
6.13.6
कुम्भकर्णः सहास्माभिः
इन्द्रजिच्च महाबलः ।
प्रतिषेधयितुं शक्तौ
सवज्रमपि वज्रिणम् ॥
Kumbhakarṇa and the mighty strong Indrajit, along with us, can stand against even Indra, the wielder of the thunderbolt.
6.13.7
उपप्रदानं सान्त्वं वा
भेदं वा कुशलैः कृतम् ।
समतिक्रम्य दण्डेन
सिद्धिमर्थेषु रोचय ॥
I would rather gain the object by force than using the other (three) methods of buying out, negotiating with and inducing divisions (in the enemy camp), favored by some who happen to be good at them.
6.13.8
इह प्राप्तान् वयं सर्वान्
शत्रूंस्तव महाबल ।
वशे शस्त्रप्रतापेन
करिष्यामो न संशयः ॥
O you of immense strength! We will, for sure, subdue all of your enemies, should they come here, with the power of the Ṡastras.
6.13.9
एवमुक्तस्तदा राजा
महापार्श्वेन रावणः ।
तस्य सम्पूजयन् वाक्यम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Rāvaṇa, the king, thus told by Mahāpārṡwa, appreciated his advice and said these words:
6.13.10
महापार्श्व निबोध त्वम्
रहस्यं किञ्चिदात्मनः ।
चिरवृत्तं तदाख्यास्ये
यदवाप्तं मया पुरा ॥
O Mahāpārṡwa! You should know a secret concerning me. Let me tell you what happened a long time ago.
6.13.11
पितामहस्य भवनम्
गच्छन्तीं पुञ्जिकस्थलाम् ।
चञ्चूर्यमाणामद्राक्षम्
आकाशेऽग्निशिखामिव ॥
One day I saw Puñjikasthalā, who shone like a tongue of flame, furtively gliding through the sky, bound for the Grandsire’s abode.
6.13.12
सा प्रसह्य मया भुक्ता
कृता विवसना ततः ।
स्वयम्भूभवनं प्राप्ता
लोलिता नलिनी यथा ॥
I disrobed and enjoyed her forcefully. Then, like a crumpled lotus vine, she reached the abode of the Self-born.
6.13.13
तस्य तच्च तदा मन्ये
ज्ञातमासीन्महात्मनः ।
अथ सङ्कुपितो वेधा
मामिदं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
That Mahātma then apparently came to know about what had happened. Then he, Lord Brahma, became angry and said these words to me:
6.13.14
अद्यप्रभृति यामन्याम्
बलान्नारीं गमिष्यसि ।
तदा ते शतधा मूर्धा
फलिष्यति न संशयः ॥
Hereafter, if you violate any other woman by force, your head shall shiver into a hundred pieces. And let there be no misgivings about it.
6.13.15
इत्यहं तस्य शापस्य
भीतः प्रसभमेव ताम् ।
नारोपये बलात् सीताम्
वैदेहीं शयने शुभे ॥
Being afraid of His curse, I am hesitant to force Seetā, the princess of Vidēha, to get on to my wonderful bed.
6.13.16
सागरस्येव मे वेगो
मारुतस्येव मे गतिः ।
नैतद्दाशरथिर्वेद
ह्यासादयति तेन माम् ॥
My force is like that of the ocean and my speed like that of the wind. The son of Daṡaratha does not know this, and hence he dares to come near me.
6.13.17
यस्तु सिंहमिवासीनम्
सुप्तं गिरिगुहाशये ।
क्रुद्धं मृत्युमिवासीनम्
प्रबोधयितुमिच्छति ॥
He is trying to provoke one who is like a lion asleep in its mountain lair and like Death waiting angrily.
6.13.18
न मत्तो निर्गतान् बाणान्
द्विजिह्वानिव पन्नगान् ।
रामः पश्यति सङ्ग्रामे
तेन मामभिगच्छति ॥
Rāma rushes towards me only because he has never seen the arrows that come flying from my hands, like serpents with forked tongues.
6.13.19
क्षिप्रं वज्रोपमैर्बाणैः
शतधा कार्मुकच्युतैः ।
राममादीपयिष्यामि
उल्काभिरिव कुञ्जरम् ॥
In no time, with thunderbolt-like arrows shot from my bow in their hundreds, I shall burn Rāma, like meteors does an elephant.
6.13.20
तच्चास्य बलमादास्ये
बलेन महता वृतः ।
उदयन् सविता काले
नक्षत्राणामिव प्रभाम् ॥
Supported by a huge army, I shall take on his forces like the rising sun takes away the brightness of the stars.
6.13.21
न वासवेनापि सहस्रचक्षुषा
युधाऽस्मि शक्यो वरुणेन वा पुनः ।
मया त्वियं बाहुबलेन निर्जिता
पुरी पुरा वैश्रवणेन पालिता ॥
Not even Vāsava of thousand eyes can vanquish me in battle, and neither can Varuṇa.
With the sheer strength of my doughty arm, I wrenched this city from Vaiṡravaṇa who once ruled it.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे त्रयोदशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirteenth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
