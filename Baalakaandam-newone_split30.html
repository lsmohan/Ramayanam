Baala Kaanda - Sarga 30
In this Sarga, with the protection provided by Rāma, Viṡwāmitra successfully completes his Yajña. Rāma hurls away Māreecha into the ocean and kills all the other Rākshasas that are fouling the Yajñas. This is Rāma's first success in restoring the order that was disturbed by Rāvaṇa and his goons.
1.30.1
अथ तौ देशकालज्ञौ
राजपुत्रावरिन्दमौ ।
देशे काले च वाक्यज्ञौ
अब्रूतां कौशिकं वचः ॥
Then, the duo of princes, the subjugators of detractors, who knew what to say when and where, asked that descendant of Kuṡika:
1.30.2
भगवन् श्रोतुमिच्छावो
यस्मिन् काले निशाचरौ ।
संरक्षणीयौ तौ ब्रह्मन्
नातिवर्तेत तत्क्षणम् ॥
O Bhagawan! We would like to know when the protection from Rākshasas is needed. We want to be conscious of the timing.
1.30.3
एवं ब्रुवाणौ काकुत्स्थौ
त्वरमाणौ युयुत्सया ।
सर्वे ते मुनयः प्रीताः
प्रशशंसुर्नृपात्मजौ ॥
The Munis felt very happy hearing the words of the princes of Kākutstha lineage displaying their eagerness to fight, and applauded them and said:
1.30.4
अद्यप्रभृति षड्रात्रम्
रक्षतं राघवौ युवाम् ।
दीक्षां गतो ह्येष मुनिः
मौनित्वं च गमिष्यति ॥
O princes of Raghu dynasty! Starting from now, we need your protection for six nights. The Muni (Viṡwāmitra) is in Deeksha. He will be silent and will not speak with anyone.
1.30.5
तौ च तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राजपुत्रौ यशस्विनौ ।
अनिद्रौ षडहोरात्रम्
तपोवनमरक्षताम् ॥
The duo of those renowned princes, followed that request and protected the Yajña for six days and nights, without sleeping.
1.30.6
उपासाञ्चक्रतुर्वीरौ
यत्तौ परमधन्विनौ ।
ररक्षतुर्मुनिवरम्
विश्वामित्रमरिन्दमौ ॥
Bearing the ultimate of bows, those two Veeras, subjugators of enemies, stayed nearby in full alert and protected the eminent Muni, Viṡwāmitra.
1.30.7
अथ काले गते तस्मिन्
षष्ठेऽहनि समागते ।
सौमित्रिमब्रवीद्रामो
यत्तो भव समाहितः ॥
As time passed, and as the sixth day approached Rāma said to Lakshmaṇa: "Be on high alert and ready!"
This is just how warriors and even players of a sport encourage each other on the field. The equivalent modern expression would be "Be ready, buddy!"
1.30.8
रामस्यैवं ब्रुवाणस्य
त्वरितस्य युयुत्सया ।
प्रजज्वाल ततो वेदिः
सोपाध्यायपुरोहिता ॥
Rāma, saying thus, became ready for an imminent fight. And at the same time the altar with Priests and Purōhita glowed brilliantly with fire.
Vālmeeki would have been an excellent screenplay writer and cinematographer, had he lived today.
Read these three Ṡlōkas carefully.
In the first one, you can see the camera moving slowly from the ferocious face of Rāma to the bright and red fire on the altar.
In the second Ṡlōka the camera zooms out to include nearby objects like Darbha and the ladles and then finally to include Viṡwāmitra.
In the third Ṡlōka, the camera follows the fire upwards into the sky, from where the sound is heard.
This moment of raising fire on the altar is very significant. It represents the grand finale of everything that went thus far in the story of Rāmāyaṇa. The Avatāra of Rāma is to restore those sacred fires of Yajñas that are symbolic of the connection between Humans and Gods.
1.30.9
सदर्भचमसस्रुक्का
ससमित्कुसुमोच्चया ।
विश्वामित्रेण सहिता
वेदिर्जज्वाल सर्त्विजा ॥
The altar glowed in that fire with all the Darbhas, Camasas, Srukkus around it, decorated with Samidhas and arrangements of flowers and with the radiant Viṡwāmitra.
Darbha is a type of grass used in Vēdic ceremonies. Camasas are bowls. Srukkus are ladles. Samidhas are pieces of firewood used in Yajñas.
1.30.10
मन्त्रवच्च यथान्यायम्
यज्ञोऽसौ सम्प्रवर्तते ।
आकाशे च महान् शब्दः
प्रादुरासीद्भयानकः ॥
As the Yajña proceeded with all the Mantras as it should, a sudden, loud, thunderous and frightening sound was heard above in the sky.
1.30.11
आवार्य गगनं मेघो
यथा प्रावृषि निर्गतः ।
तथा मायां विकुर्वाणौ
राक्षसावभ्यधावताम् ॥
Two Rākshasas appeared swiftly covering the sky like dark clouds in the monsoon months.
1.30.12
मारीचश्च सुबाहुश्च
तयोरनुचराश्च ये ।
आगम्य भीमसङ्काशा
रुधिरौघमवासृजन् ॥
The fearsome Māreeca and Subāhu, along with their henchmen came out and created a downpour of blood.
1.30.13
सा तेन रुधिरौघेण
वेदिर्जज्वाल मण्डिता ।
सहसाऽभिद्रुतो रामः
तानपश्यत्ततो दिवि ॥
The tongues of fire from the altar rose with the shower of blood. Rāma rushed there and saw them in the sky.
1.30.14
तावापतन्तौ सहसा
दृष्ट्वा राजीवलोचनः ।
लक्ष्मणं त्वभिसम्प्रेक्ष्य
रामो वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
The wide, lotus eyed Rāma, saw them coming down fast and said to Lakshmaṇa:
1.30.15
पश्य लक्ष्मण दुर्वृत्तान्
राक्षसान् पिशिताशनान् ।
मानवास्त्रसमाधूतान्
अनिलेन यथा घनान् ॥
I am going to drive these ill-behaving, man-eating Rākshasas away just as a wild breeze drives away the clouds, with this Mānavāstra.
1.30.16
मानवं परमोदारं
अस्त्रं परमभास्वरम् ।
चिक्षेप परमक्रुद्धो
मारीचोरसि राघवः ॥
The prince of Raghu dynasty, then, hurled the bright, glowing and powerful Mānavāstra, aimed at the chest of Māreeca.
1.30.17
स तेन परमास्त्रेण
मानवेन समाहतः ।
सम्पूर्णं योजनशतम्
क्षिप्तः सागरसम्प्लवे ॥
That extremely powerful Mānavāstra struck him so hard that he was thrown away at a distance of a hundred Yōjanas, into the surging high seas.
1.30.18
विचेतनं विघूर्णन्तम्
शीतेषु बलताडितम् ।
निरस्तं दृश्य मारीचम्
रामो लक्ष्मणमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing Māreeca, who had become unconscious, thrown away reeling at a distance, by that cold Astra, Rāma said to Lakshmaṇa:
'Cold Astra' denotes an Astra that does not create fire.
1.30.19
पश्य लक्ष्मण शीतेषुम्
मानवं धर्मसंहितम् ।
मोहयित्वा नयत्येनम्
न च प्राणैर्व्ययुज्यत ॥
Look, this cold Astra, the Mānavāstra, which has a rightful behavior made him only unconscious and threw him away, but did not take his life.
1.30.20
इमानपि वधिष्यामि
निर्घृणान् दुष्टचारिणः ।
राक्षसान् पापकर्मस्थान्
यज्ञघ्नान् रुधिराशनान् ॥
I will now kill the rest of these ill-behaved, cruel-hearted, blood-thirsty, Yajña-blocking Rākshasas!
A real Veera will not get carried away with his success, and would not continue the killing spree indiscriminately.
He would be clear in his mind about who and why he has to kill. Rāma is very conscious of why he has to kill these Rākshasas, or, in other words, why they deserve to be killed.
1.30.21
सङ्गृह्यास्त्रं ततो रामो
दिव्यमाग्नेयमद्भुतम् ।
सुबाहूरसि चिक्षेप
स विद्धः प्रापतद्भुवि ॥
Then Rāma reached to the divine, mesmerizing Agnēyāstra and hurled it at the chest of Subāhu, making him fall down dead.
1.30.22
शेषान् वायव्यमादाय
निजघान महायशाः ।
राघवः परमोदारो
मुनीनां मुदमावहन् ॥
Then, that highly admired and magnanimous prince of Raghu dynasty killed the rest of the Rākshasas with Vāyavyāstra making the Munis extremely happy.
1.30.23
स हत्वा राक्षसान् सर्वान्
यज्ञघ्नान् रघुनन्दनः ।
ऋषिभिः पूजितस्तत्र
यथेन्द्रो विजये पुरा ॥
The prince of Raghu dynasty was then hailed and revered by all the Munis, just as Indra was, upon his own success, in the past.
1.30.24
अथ यज्ञे समाप्ते तु
विश्वामित्रो महामुनिः ।
निरीतिका दिशो दृष्ट्वा
काकुत्स्थमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Contented as the Yajña was completed successfully, and seeing the world relieved in every direction, the great Muni Viṡwāmitra said to the prince of Kākutstha lineage :
1.30.25
कृतार्थोऽस्मि महाबाहो
कृतं गुरुवचस्त्वया ।
सिद्धाश्रममिदं सत्यम्
कृतं राम महायशः ॥
O Rāma of mighty arm and great renown! You, indeed, fulfilled the promise of your father. My purpose is served and this Siddhāṡrama stands here true to its name!
This Ṡlōka is a simple summary and essence of all that happened.
The skill of summing up a momentous and arduous endeavor in clear and simple words, is something that comes naturally to the gifted and through effort, to others.
Also, notice the humility of Viṡwāmitra in giving the entire credit to Daṡaratha, Rāma and to Siddhāṡrama.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे त्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirtieth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
