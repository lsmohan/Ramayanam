Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 24
In this Sarga the Vānara army gets ready and lets off war hoofs, which are reciprocated by the Rākshasas in Laṅkā. Rāma thinks of Seetā and her pitiable condition, while he also admires the beauty of Laṅkā. Then Rāma establishes divisions of the army and juxtaposes them into a formation, with himself and Lakshmaṇa leading a division at the head of that formation.
Once the formation of the army is complete, Rāma orders the release of Ṡuka. Ṡuka goes back to Rāvaṇa, who inquires about his clipped wings. Ṡuka conveys in brief what had happened to him at the hands of the Vānaras. He makes it clear to Rāvaṇa that the only two options he has, is to give Seetā back to Rāma or to be prepared for the battle. The enraged Rāvaṇa says that he will not surrender Seetā under any circumstances. He roars, saying that even Dēvas, Gandharvas and Dānavas combined cannot stand against him in fight, and that even the entire world cannot threaten him enough to make him surrender Seetā.
6.24.1
सा वीरसमिती राज्ञा
विरराज व्यवस्थिता ।
शशिना शुभनक्षत्रा
पौर्णमासीव शारदी ॥
The army of heroic warriors in formation with the king (in its midst), shone like the bright stars around the moon in the full moon night of post-monsoon season.
6.24.2
प्रचचाल च वेगेन
त्रस्ता चैव वसुन्धरा ।
पीड्यमाना बलौघेन
तेन सागरवर्चसा ॥
The earth trembled as if it were scared from the stampede of the massive army that seemed like a surge of the ocean.
Doesn’t this remind one of the stampede in the movie Lion King?
6.24.3
ततः शुश्रुवुराक्रुष्टम्
लङ्कायां काननोकसः ।
भेरीमृदङ्गसङ्घुष्टम्
तुमुलं रोमहर्षणम् ॥
Then the creatures of the wilds heard the tumultuous and hair-raising din of kettle drums and Mṛdaṅgas coming from Laṅkā.
6.24.4
बभूवुस्तेन घोषेण
संहृष्टा हरियूथपाः ।
अमृष्यमाणास्तं घोषम्
विनेदुर्घोषवत्तरम् ॥
That tumult only served to buoy up the spirits of the Vānara chiefs and provoke them to outshout the foe.
6.24.5
राक्षसास्तु प्लवङ्गानाम्
शुश्रुवुश्चापि गर्जितम् ।
नर्दतामिव दृप्तानाम्
मेघानामम्बरे स्वनम् ॥
The Rākshasas too heard the roar of Vānaras as they, in their pride, created a din like the thunderous clouds in the sky.
6.24.6
दृष्ट्वा दाशरथिर्लङ्काम्
चित्रध्वजपताकिनीम् ।
जगाम मनसा सीताम्
दूयमानेन चेतसा ॥
Seeing Laṅkā with its picturesque flags and buntings, the thoughts of Daṡaratha’s son went to Seetā, his heart taken over by grief:
6.24.7
अत्र सा मृगशावाक्षी
रावणेनोपरुद्ध्यते ।
अभिभूता ग्रहेणेव
लोहिताङ्गेन रोहिणी ॥
Seetā, of the fawn eyes, is kept in confinement there by Rāvaṇa, like the star Rōhiṇee beset by planet Mars.
6.24.8
दीर्घमुष्णं च निश्वस्य
समुद्वीक्ष्य च लक्ष्मणम् ।
उवाच वचनं वीरः
तत्कालहितमात्मनः ॥
Heaving a deep and hot sigh, and looking at Lakshmaṇa, the Veera spoke these words, relevant to him under the current situation.
6.24.9
आलिखन्तीमिवाकाशम्
उत्थितां पश्य लक्ष्मण ।
मनसेव कृतां लङ्काम्
नगाग्रे विश्वकर्मणा ॥
Look at (this city of) Laṅkā, seeming to be Vishwakarma’s imagination taken shape instantly, rising from the top of the mountain to touch the sky!
6.24.10
विमानैर्बहुभिर्लङ्का
सङ्कीर्णा भुवि राजते ।
विष्णोः पदमिवाकाशम्
छादितं पाण्डरैर्घनैः ॥
Crowded with many towers, Laṅkā shines on the earth, like the foot of Vishṇu in the sky covered by white clouds.
6.24.11
पुष्पितैः शोभिता लङ्का
वनैश्चैत्ररथोपमैः ।
नानापतङ्गसङ्घुष्टैः
फलपुष्पोपगैः शुभैः ॥
Laṅkā is splendid with fruit and flower and reverberating with the cooing of a variety of birds, its woods in flower recalling Caitraratha.
6.24.12
पश्य मत्तविहङ्गानि
प्रलीनभ्रमराणि च ।
कोकिलाकुलषण्डानि
दोधवीति शिवोऽनिलः ॥
Look at the amorous birds and the black bees that are quiet (drunken heavily), and the pleasant breeze that turns the branches into swings for the throngs of Kōkilas!
6.24.13
इति दाशरथी रामो
लक्ष्मणं समभाषत ।
बलं च तद्वै विभजन्
शास्त्रदृष्टेन कर्मणा ॥
After reflecting thus with Lakshmaṇa, Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, established divisions of the forces following the principles laid out in the Ṡastras.
6.24.14
शशास कपिसेनाया
बलमादाय वीर्यवान् ।
अङ्गदः सह नीलेन
तिष्ठेदुरसि दुर्जयः ॥
He then ordered, ‘Let the invincible and heroic Aṅgada, taking his division of the forces, stay at the chest region of the formation, along with Neela!’
6.24.15
तिष्ठेद्वानरवाहिन्या
वानरौघसमावृतः ।
आश्रित्य दक्षिणं पार्श्वम्
ऋषभो वानरर्षभः ॥
Let Ṛshabha, a bull among Vānaras, guard the right flank of the army with a large contingent of Vānaras.
6.24.16
गन्धहस्तीव दुर्धर्षः
तरस्वी गन्धमादनः ।
तिष्ठेद्वानरवाहिन्याः
सव्यं पार्श्वं समाश्रितः ॥
Let the invincible Gandhamādana, as formidable as a tusker in rut, guard the left flank of the Vānara force.
6.24.17
मूर्ध्नि स्थास्याम्यहं यत्तोः
लक्ष्मणेन समन्वितः ।
जाम्बवांश्च सुषेणश्च
वेगदर्शी च वानरः ।
ऋक्षमुख्या महात्मानः
कुक्षिं रक्षन्तु ते त्रयः ॥
I will, in all preparedness, stay at the head of the formation along with Lakshmaṇa.
And let the three, Jāmbavān, Sushēṇa and Vēgadarsi the Vānara, along with the mighty bear chiefs, guard the belly region of the formation.
6.24.18
जघनं कपिसेनायाः
कपिराजोऽभिरक्षतु ।
पश्चार्धमिव लोकस्य
प्रचेतास्तेजसा वृतः ॥
Let the King of the Vānaras with his immense power protect the hip region of the formation, just as Pracēta (Varuṇa) does the western half of the World.
6.24.19
सुविभक्तमहाव्यूहा
महावानररक्षिता ।
अनीकिनी सा विभभौ
यथा द्यौः साभ्रसम्प्लवा ॥
The army, thus disposed and impregnably guarded by the greatest of the Vānaras, shone like the sky filled with sailing clouds.
6.24.20
प्रगृह्य गिरिशृङ्गाणि
महतश्च महीरुहान् ।
आसेदुर्वानरा लङ्काम्
विमर्दयिषवो रणे ॥
Arming themselves with mountain crests and huge trees, the Vānaras reached Laṅkā eager to pummel it.
6.24.21
शिखरैर्विकिरामैनाम्
लङ्कां मुष्टिभिरेव वा ।
इति स्म दधिरे सर्वे
मनांसि हरिसत्तमाः ॥
All those eminent Vānaras made up their minds: ‘We will smash this Laṅkā with these mountain crests or with our fists.’
6.24.22
ततो रामो महातेजाः
सुग्रीवमिदमब्रवीत् ।
सुविभक्तानि सैन्यानि
शुक एष विमुच्यताम् ॥
Then Rāma of immense strength said these words to Sugreeva: ‘The army formation is complete; let this Ṡuka be released!’
6.24.23
रामस्य वचनं श्रुत्वा
वानरेन्द्रो महाबलः ।
मोचयामास तं दूतम्
शुकं रामस्य शासनात् ॥
Hearing the words of Rāma, the Lord of Vānaras, of immense strength, released the messenger Ṡuka following the orders of Rāma.
6.24.24
मोचितो रामवाक्येन
वानरैश्चाभिपीडितः ।
शुकः परमसन्त्रस्तो
रक्षोधिपमुपागमत् ॥
Ṡuka, in abject terror from the harassment by Vānaras, and set free by the order of Rāma, went to the Lord of Rākshasas.
6.24.25
रावणः प्रहसन्नेव
शुकं वाक्यमभाषत ॥
Rāvaṇa, with a smile (in an attempt to cheer him up), said these words to Ṡuka:
6.24.26
किमिमौ ते सितौ पक्षौ
लूनपक्षश्च दृश्यसे ।
कच्चिन्नानेकचित्तानाम्
तेषां त्वं वशमागतः ॥
Looks like your wings are clipped, and feathers are plucked, aren’t they? Hope you have not fallen into the hands of some crazy folks!
6.24.27
ततः स भयसंविग्नः
तथा राज्ञाऽभिचोदितः ।
वचनं प्रत्युवाचेदम्
राक्षसाधिपमुत्तमम् ॥
Still convulsing with fear, but prodded by the king, he responded to that best of the lords of the Rākshasas:
6.24.28
सागरस्योत्तरे तीरे
ब्रुवंस्ते वचनं तथा ।
यथासन्देशमक्लिष्टम्
सान्त्वयं श्लक्ष्णया गिरा ॥
I conveyed your words, just as I have been commanded, in simple words and in a friendly and gentle voice, on the northern shore of the ocean.
6.24.29
क्रुद्धैस्तैरहमुत्प्लुत्य
दृष्टमात्रैः प्लवङ्गमेः ।
गृहीतोऽस्म्यपि चारब्धो
हन्तुं लोप्तुं च मुष्टिभिः ॥
At the very sight of me, they became angry, and jumped on me and grabbed me, and began pounding me with their fists and plucking my feathers.
6.24.30
नैव सम्भाषितुं शक्याः
सम्प्रश्नोऽत्र न लभ्यते ।
प्रकृत्या कोपनास्तीक्ष्णा
वानरा राक्षसाधिप ॥
The Vānaras are irascible and aggressive in nature, O Lord of Rākshasas; it was simply impossible to make a conversation (with them), nor was there an opportunity to ask (them) anything.
6.24.31
स च हन्ता विराधस्य
कबन्धस्य खरस्य च ।
सुग्रीवसहितो रामः
सीतायाः पदमागतः ॥
Rāma, the slayer of Virādha, Kabandha and Khara, has come with Sugreeva, looking for Seetā.
6.24.32
स कृत्वा सागरे सेतुम्
तीर्त्वा च लवणोदधिम् ।
एष रक्षांसि निर्धूय
धन्वी तिष्ठति राघवः ॥
Rāghava has built a bridge on the ocean and crossed that body of salt water. And here he stands armed with the bow, in utter contempt of Rākshasas.
6.24.33
ऋक्षवानरसङ्घानाम्
अनीकानि सहस्रशः ।
गिरिमेघनिकाशानाम्
छादयन्ति वसुन्धराम् ॥
Thousands of companies of Ṛkshas and Vānaras, huge as hills or clouds, blot out the earth from view.
6.24.34
राक्षसानां बलौघस्य
वानरेन्द्रबलस्य च ।
नैतयोर्विद्यते सन्धिः
देवदानवयोरिव ॥
There can possibly be no accord between the Rākshasa forces and Vānara forces, any more than there could have been between the Dēvas and the Dānavas.
6.24.35
पुरा प्राकारमायान्ति
क्षिप्रमेकतरं कुरु ।
सीतां वाऽस्मै प्रयच्छाशु
सुयुद्धं वा प्रदीयताम् ॥
Decide quickly one way or the other before they are at the ramparts; either surrender Seetā to him or (be ready to) give a good fight.
6.24.36
शुकस्य वचनं श्रुत्वा
रावणो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ।
रोषसंरक्तनयनो
निर्दहन्निव चक्षुषा ॥
Hearing those words of Ṡuka, Rāvaṇa responded with these words, his eyes turning deep red with rage as if he would burn him with them.
6.24.37
यदि मां प्रति युद्ध्येरन्
देवगन्धर्वदानवाः ।
नैव सीतां प्रदास्यामि
सर्वलोकभयादपि ॥
I will not give up Seetā, even if all the Dēvas, Dānavas and Gandharvas join in an attack against me, nor even if all the worlds come together to threaten me.
6.24.38
कदा नामाभिधावन्ति
राघवं मामकाः शराः ।
वसन्ते पुष्पितं मत्ता
भ्रमरा इव पादपम् ॥
Ah! how soon will my arrows chase Rāghava like drunken bees chasing the trees in flower in spring!
6.24.39
कदा तूणीशयैर्दीप्तैः
गणशः कार्मुकच्युतैः ।
शरैरादीपयाम्येनम्
उल्काभिरिव कुञ्जरम् ॥
When will the volley upon volley of flaming arrows that have long lain in their quivers burn him, like firebrands do the elephant!
6.24.40
तच्चास्य बलमादास्ये
बलेन महता वृतः ।
ज्योतिषामिव सर्वेषाम्
प्रभामुद्यन् दिवाकरः ॥
I, with my huge army, will strip him of his vaunted strength, like rising sun does to the light of all stars.
6.24.41
सागरस्येव मे वेगो
मारुतस्येव मे गतिः ।
न हि दाशरथिर्वेद
तेन मां योद्धुमिच्छति ॥
Raghava does not know that I have the strength of the ocean and the speed of the wind, and that is why he thinks of fighting me.
6.24.42
न मे तूणीशयान् बाणान्
सविषानिव पन्नगान् ।
रामः पश्यति सङ्ग्रामे
तेन मां योद्धुमिच्छति ।
न जानाति पुरा वीर्यम्
मम युद्धे स राघवः ॥
Rāma does not know about the arrows that lie in my quiver like venomous serpents; and that is why he thinks of fighting me. Rāghava has had no taste of my prowess in battle till now.
6.24.43
मम चापमयीं वीणाम्
शरकोणैः प्रवादिताम् ।
ज्याशब्दतुमुलां घोराम्
आर्तभीतमहास्वनाम् ।
नाराचतलसन्नादाम्
तां ममाहितवाहिनीम् ।
अवगाह्य महारङ्गम्
वादयिष्याम्यहं रणे ॥
Entering the stage called the enemy forces in battle, I shall play on my bow like on a Veeṇa, twanging it with finger picks of arrows, creating tumultuous sounds from the string and a rhythm from the release of Nārāca arrows, with the eerie and loud cries of the distressed and the frightened serving as the reverberation.
6.24.45
न वासवेनापि सहस्रचक्षुषा
यथाऽस्मि शक्यो वरुणेन वा स्वयम् ।
यमेन वा धर्षयितुं शराग्निना
महाहवे वैश्रवणेन वा पुनः ॥
Neither Vāsava of thousand eyes nor Varuṇa himself, nor Yama and not even Vaiṡravaṇa has enough fire power of arrows to stand against me in a fierce battle.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे चतुर्विंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty fourth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
