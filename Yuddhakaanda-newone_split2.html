Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 2
In this Sarga, Sugreeva urges Rāma not to worry how they would cross the ocean. He assures Rāma that all the Vānaras are ready to even die for his sake. He asks Rāma to use his sharp intellect and focus on a finding a way to cross the ocean. He suggests that a bridge should be built over the ocean. He says that he sees good omens and somehow the Vānaras will cross the ocean. He asks Rāma to give up the grief and show his fiery side.
6.2.1
तं तु शोकपरिद्यूनम्
रामं दशरथात्मजम् ।
उवाच वचनं श्रीमान्
सुग्रीवः शोकनाशनम् ॥
To that Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, who was gravely worried about it, the blessed Sugreeva said these words to dispel the worry:
6.2.2
किं त्वं सन्तप्यसे वीर
यथाऽन्यः प्राकृतस्तथा ।
मैवं भूस्त्यज सन्तापम्
कृतघ्न इव सौहृदम् ॥
Why are you so worried, O Veera, like any other ordinary person? Don’t be so consumed by it, let go off your worry, like an ingrate would, a friendship!
6.2.3
सन्तापस्य च ते स्थानम्
न हि पश्यामि राघव ।
प्रवृत्तावुपलब्धायाम्
ज्ञाते च निलये रिपोः ॥
There is no place for your worry, O Rāghava, now that we have news (of Seetā) and know where the enemy lives.
6.2.4
मतिमाञ्छास्त्रवित् प्राज्ञः
पण्डितश्चासि राघव ।
त्यजेमां प्राकृतां बुद्धिम्
कृतात्मेवार्थदूषणीम् ॥
You are intelligent, a master of Astras a savant of worldly affairs and a man of great discernment. Let go off these pedestrian thoughts, like any resolute man would, thoughts that ruin the purpose.
6.2.5
समुद्रं लङ्घयित्वा तु
महानक्रसमाकुलम् ।
लङ्कामारोहयिष्यामः
हनिष्यामश्च ते रिपुम् ॥
Crossing the ocean that is infested with the giant crocodiles we will ascend to Laṅkā and also kill your enemy.
6.2.6
निरुत्साहस्य दीनस्य
शोकपर्याकुलात्मनः ।
सर्वार्थान्यवसीदन्ति
व्यसनं चाधिगच्छति ॥
All objects of a person who is dejected, who lacks enthusiasm, and whose mind is overwhelmed by grief, will be ruined; and he will fall only into (greater) troubles.
6.2.7
इमे शूराः समर्थाश्च
सर्वे नो हरियूथपाः ।
त्वत्प्रियार्थं कृतोत्साहाः
प्रवेष्टुमपि पावकम् ।
एषां हर्षेण जानामि
तर्कश्चास्मिन् दृढो मम ॥
All the valiant and capable chiefs of our Vānara army are ready to plunge even into fire for your sake; I know this from their enthusiasm and from everything I can reason out.
6.2.8
विक्रमेण समानेष्ये
सीतां हत्वा यथा रिपुम् ।
रावणं पापकर्माणम्
तथा त्वं कर्तुमर्हसि ॥
It is for you to do now whatever it takes to kill the enemy, Rāvaṇa of evil deeds, and regain Seetā with your prowess.
6.2.9
सेतुरत्र यथा बद्ध्येत्
यथा पश्येम तां पुरीम् ।
तस्य राक्षसराजस्य
तथा त्वं कुरु राघव ॥
O Rāghava! Please do what it takes to build a bridge and reach Laṅkā, the city of the king of Rākshasas.
6.2.10
दृष्ट्वा तां तु पुरीं लङ्काम्
त्रिकूटशिखरे स्थिताम् ।
हतं च रावणं युद्धे
दर्शनादवधारय ॥
Take it from me that the moment we see that city of Laṅkā situated on the top of the Trikūṭa peak, Rāvaṇa is as good as slain.
6.2.11
अबद्ध्वा सागरे सेतुम्
घोरे तु वरुणालये ।
लङ्का न मर्दितुं शक्या
सेन्द्रैरपि सुरासुरैः ॥
Without building a bridge over the ocean, the abode of Varuṇa, it would be impossible to batter down Laṅkā even for the Suras and Asuras even with the help of Indra.
6.2.12
सेतुर्बद्धः समुद्रे च
यावल्लङ्कासमीपतः ।
सर्वं तीर्णं च मे सैन्यम्
जितमित्युपधारय ।
इमे हि समरे वीरा
हरयः कामरूपिणः ॥
The moment we build the bridge and all my army crosses the ocean and arrives at Laṅkā, victory is ours, I assure you. These Vānaras, who can take any form at will, are heroic fighters.
6.2.14
तदलं विक्लवा बुद्धी
राजन् सर्वार्थनाशिनी ।
पुरुषस्य हि लोकेऽस्मिन्
शोकः शौर्यापकर्षणः ॥
Therefore, O King, enough of the disquiet that would only ruin all prospects. Sadness only diminishes the bravery of a man.
6.2.15
यत्तु कार्यं मनुष्येण
शौण्डीर्यमवलम्बता ।
तदलंकरणायैव
कर्तुर्भवति सत्वरम् ॥
You should now summon the courage that is demanded of any man. It is an adornment for anyone who wants to get things done.
6.2.16
अस्मिन् काले महाप्राज्ञ
सत्त्वमातिष्ठ तेजसा ।
शूराणां हि मनुष्याणाम्
त्वद्विधानां महात्मनाम् ।
विनष्टे वा प्रनष्टे वा
शोकः सर्वार्थनाशनः ॥
It is time for you now, O Sagacious one, to display your power and energy.
Grief, no matter whether it is over something that is lost or destroyed, would only lead to the ruin of everything that is at stake, for Mahātmas and valiant men like you.
6.2.17
त्वं तु बुद्धिमतां श्रेष्ठः
सर्वशास्त्रार्थकोविदः ।
मद्विधैः सचिवैः सार्धम्
अरिं जेतुमिहार्हसि ॥
You are the smartest of all. You are a savant of all Ṡastras. With counsellors like me assisting you, you are bound to vanquish the enemy.
6.2.18
न हि पश्याम्यहं कं चित्
त्रिषु लोकेषु राघव ।
गृहीतधनुषो यस्ते
तिष्ठेदभिमुखो रणे ॥
I can’t think of anyone, O Rāghava, in any of the three worlds, who can face you in battle when you are armed with the bow.
6.2.19
वानरेषु समासक्तम्
न ते कार्यं विपत्स्यते ।
अचिराद्द्रक्ष्यसे सीताम्
तीर्त्वा सागरमक्षयम् ॥
Your work, entrusted to the Vānaras, will not suffer. Crossing the sea that never depletes, you shall see Seetā before long!
6.2.20
तदलं शोकमालम्ब्य
क्रोधमालम्ब भूपते ।
निश्चेष्टाः क्षत्रिया मन्दाः
सर्वे चण्डस्य बिभ्यति ॥
Therefore enough of grieving; summon all your wrath, O lord of the earth! Inactivity can easily make Kshatriyas dull. Everyone fears a ruthless man.
6.2.21
लङ्घनार्थं च घोरस्य
समुद्रस्य नदीपतेः ।
सहास्माभिरिहोपेतः
सूक्ष्मबुद्धिर्विचारय ॥
Please, O you of incisive intellect, think of how you, along with all of us, can cross the formidable ocean, the lord of rivers.
6.2.22
लञ्घिते तत्र तैः सैन्यैः
जितमित्येव निश्चिसु ।
सर्वं तीर्णं च मे सैन्यम्
जितमित्यवधार्यताम् ॥
With all my armies crossing the ocean, take it for granted that the victory is certain.
6.2.23
इमे हि हरयः शूराः
समरे कामरूपिणः ।
तानरीन् विधमिष्यन्ति
शिलापादपवृष्टिभिः ॥
These Vānaras are valiant and can take any form at will in the battle. They will destroy the foe with torrents of boulders and trees.
6.2.24
कथञ्चित्परिपश्यामि
लङ्घितं वरुणालयम् ।
हतमित्येव तं मन्ये
युद्धे शत्रुनिबर्हण ॥
I can clearly see that somehow we will cross the abode of Varuṇa. Consider him slain in the battle, O you who delight in the fray!
6.2.25
किमुक्त्वा बहुधा चापि
सर्वथा विजयी भवान् ।
निमित्तानि च पश्यामि
मनो मे सम्प्रहृष्यति ॥
There is no use saying the same thing in multiple ways! You will win, by all means. I see good omens and my mind is filled with joy.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्वितीयस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the second Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
