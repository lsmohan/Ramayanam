Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 30
In this Sarga, Hanumān, after watching the Rākshasis threaten Seetā and hearing Seetā and Trijaṭa speak, feels very happy that she is found, in search of whom thousands of Vānaras has gone everywhere.
He feels that the first order of his duty is to comfort her, who might otherwise take her own life before the day-break. Then he wonders how he could make a conversation with her without scaring her, for she might mistake him for Rāvaṇa in disguise. He is worried that her screams might draw the attention of the Rākshasis, who would call upon the Rākshasas, which would result in a fight, in which he might get captured or killed.
He realizes that if he does not speak to her, she might give up her life, finding no hope of rescue, and if he does speak to her, he may get caught, or killed and thus leaving no one who can reach her who is lodged in a secure place in the middle of an ocean. He sees that the cause of Rāma would be hurt either way.
After thinking over thus for a while, he decides to quietly sing the praises of Rāma, remaining on the tree branch, which would create trust in her.
5.30.1
हनुमानपि विक्रान्तः
सर्वं शुश्राव तत्त्वतः ।
सीतायास्त्रिजटायाश्च
राक्षसीनां च तर्जनम् ॥
Hanumān of great prowess heard every thing that Seetā said, Trijaṭa said and also the threats from the Rākshasis.
5.30.2
अवेक्षमाणस्तां देवीम्
देवतामिव नन्दने ।
ततो बहुविधां चिन्ताम्
चिन्तयामास वानरः ॥
Hanumān felt that she was like a goddess in the Nandana Vana. Then the Vānara thought over many things.
5.30.3
यां कपीनां सहस्राणि
सुबहून्ययुतानि च ।
दिक्षु सर्वासु मार्गन्ते
सेयमासादिता मया ॥
(Finally,) I am able to see Seetā in search of whom tens of thousands of Vānaras went in every direction.
5.30.4
चारेण तु सुयुक्तेन
शत्रोः शक्तिव्यपेक्षया ।
गूढेन चरता तावत्
अवेक्षितमिदं मया ॥
Being a spy, I have been very careful, keeping the strength of the enemy in mind. I am able to see all of this because I could manage to move around in secrecy.
5.30.5
राक्षसानां विशेषश्च
पुरी चेयमवेक्षिता ।
राक्षसाधिपतेरस्य
प्रभावो रावणस्य च ॥
I have seen this city and Rākshasas of every kind, and also, the majesty of Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas.
5.30.6
युक्तं तस्याप्रमेयस्य
सर्वसत्त्वदयावतः ।
समाश्वासयितुं भार्याम्
पतिदर्शनकाङ्क्षिणीम् ॥
It behooves me to console the wife of that man who is beyond every measure and who is kind to one and all creatures. She is badly longing to see her husband.
5.30.7
अहमाश्वासयाम्येनाम्
पूर्णचन्द्रनिभाननाम् ।
अदृष्टदुःखां दुःखार्ताम्
दुःखस्यान्तमगच्छतीम् ॥
I shall certainly console this lady whose face shines with the brightness of the full moon. She, who is a stranger to hardships, is now distressed with grief and does not see an end to her sorrow.
5.30.8
यद्यप्यहमिमां देवीम्
शोकोपहतचेतनाम् ।
अनाश्वास्य गमिष्यामि
दोषवद्गमनं भवेत् ॥
If I go without consoling this lady whose heart is stressed out by grief, my trip would be in vain.
5.30.9
गते हि मयि तत्रेयम्
राजपुत्री यशस्विनी ।
परित्राणमविन्दन्ती
जानकी जीवितं त्यजेत् ॥
For when I am gone, Jānaki, this princess of great renown, might take her own life, finding none that might save her.
5.30.10
मया च स महाबाहुः
पूर्णचन्द्रनिभाननः ।
समाश्यासयितुं न्याय्यः
सीतादर्शनलालसः ॥
He of mighty arm with face bright like the full moon, who is eager to see Seetā, also deserves to be comforted by me.
5.30.11
निशाचरीणां प्रत्यक्षम्
अनर्हं चाभिभाषितम् ।
कथन्नु खलु कर्तव्यम्
इदं कृच्छ्रगतो ह्यहम् ॥
It is not possible to talk (to her) in the presence of the Rākshasis. What shall I do? I am caught in quite a difficult situation!
5.30.12
अनेन रात्रिशेषेण
यदि नाश्वास्यते मया ।
सर्वथा नास्ति सन्देहः
परित्यक्ष्यति जीवितम् ॥
If not comforted before the night ends, she would, without a doubt, give up her life by one means or the other.
5.30.13
रामश्च यदि पृच्छेन्माम्
किं मां सीताब्रवीद्वचः ।
किमहं तं प्रति ब्रूयाम्
असंभाष्य सुमध्यमाम् ॥
What would I say to Rāma, if he asks, 'What message did Seetā have for me?', if I do not converse with this lady of beautiful waist?
5.30.14
सीतासन्देशरहितम्
मामितस्त्वरयाऽगतम् ।
निर्दहेदपि काकुत्स्य
क्रुद्धस्तीव्रेण चक्षुषा ॥
If I hurry back from here without taking a message from Seetā, Kākutstha will burn me away with the intensity of anger in his eyes.
5.30.15
यदि चोद्योजयिष्यामि
भर्तारं रामकारणात् ।
व्यर्थमागमनं तस्य
ससैन्यस्य भविष्यति ॥
(If Seetā gives up her life) my imploring the master (Sugreeva) for the sake of Rāma and his coming here along with his army would be for naught.
5.30.16
अन्तरं त्वहमासाद्य
राक्षसीनामिह स्थितः ।
शनैराश्वासयिष्यामि
सन्तापबहुलामिमाम् ॥
I shall wait here and seek an opportune time when these Rākshasis are not as vigilant and gently console her who is extremely troubled.
5.30.17
अहं त्वतितनुश्चैव
वानरश्च विशेषतः ।
वाचं चोदाहरिष्यामि
मानुषीमिह संस्कृताम् ॥
I am very small in size, and a Vānara at that. However, I can speak here the refined language of humans.
5.30.18
यदि वाचं प्रदास्यामि
द्विजातिरिव संस्कृताम् ।
रावणं मन्यमाना माम्
सीता भीता भविष्यति ।
वानरस्य विशेषेण
कथं स्यादभिभाषणम् ॥
But when I speak the refined language like a Brāhmaṇa, Seetā might mistake me for Rāvaṇa (in disguise) and get scared, thinking, 'how would it be possible for a Vānara to speak like this?'
5.30.19
अवश्यमेव वक्तव्यम्
मानुषं वाक्यमर्थवत् ।
मया सान्त्वयितुं शक्या
नान्यथेयमनिन्दिता ॥
There is no other way except speaking in the language of humans in a meaningful manner. How else can I console this blemish-less one?
5.30.20
सेयमालोक्य मे रूपम्
जानकी भाषितं तथा ।
रक्षोभिस्त्रासिता पूर्वम्
भूयस्त्रासं गमिष्यति ॥
But then, Jānaki, who is already frightened by the Rākshasas, might get scared again seeing my form and my speech.
5.30.21
ततो जातपरित्रासा
शब्दं कुर्यान्मनस्विनी ।
जानाना मां विशालाक्षी
रावणं कामरूपिणम् ॥
The wide-eyed one, taking me for Rāvaṇa, the one who can take any form at will, will get scared and scream.
5.30.22
सीतया च कृते शब्दे
सहसा राक्षसीगणाः ।
नानाप्रहरणो घोरः
समेयादन्तकोपमः ॥
On hearing her cries, the hordes of cruel Rākshasis who resemble Yama will storm here armed with many weapons.
5.30.23
ततो मां सम्परिक्षिप्य
सर्वतो विकृताननाः ।
वधे च ग्रहणे चैव
कुर्युर्यत्नं यथाबलम् ॥
Then they, with their ugly faces, will surround me from all sides and will try to capture me or kill me using all their strength.
5.30.24
गृह्य शाखाः प्रशाखाश्च
स्कन्धांश्चोत्तमशाखिनाम् ।
दृष्ट्वा विपरिधावन्तम्
भवेयुर्भयशङ्किताः ॥
Then, seeing me elude them by jumping from branch to bough and bough to trunk, they will become suspicious and scared.
5.30.25
मम रूपं च सम्प्रेक्ष्य
वने विचरतो महत् ।
राक्षस्यो भयवित्रस्ता
भवेयुर्विकृताननाः ॥
Seeing me jump around in the Vana with my huge body the Rākshasis, with their ugly faces, will panic and be terrified.
5.30.26
ततः कुर्युस्समाह्वानम्
राक्षस्यो रक्षसामपि ।
राक्षसेन्द्रनियुक्तानाम्
राक्षसेन्द्रनिवेशने ॥
Then they will call the Rākshasas who were deployed by the lord of Rākshasas to protect his palace.
5.30.27
ते शूलशक्तिनिस्त्रिंश
विविधायुधपाणयः ।
आपतेयुर्विमर्देऽस्मिन्
वेगेनोद्वेगकारणात् ॥
Responding to the commotion, they will join the melee in a hurry armed with many weapons like spear, Ṡakti and sword.
5.30.28
संरुद्धस्तैस्तु परितो
विधमन् रक्षसां बलम् ।
शक्नुयां न तु संप्राप्तुम्
परं पारं महोदधेः ॥
Once they beset me from every side, it will be difficult for me to chase them away and make to the other side of the great ocean.
5.30.29
मां वा गृह्णीयुराप्लुत्य
बहवाश्शीघ्रकारिणः ।
स्यादियं चागृहीतार्था
मम च ग्रहणं भवेत् ॥
Some of them may be fast enough to jump upon and capture me. Then, on the one hand I will be caught and on the other, she may not even know about it.
5.30.30
हिंसाभिरुचयो हिंस्युः
इमां वा जनकात्मजाम् ।
विपन्नं स्यात्ततः कार्यम्
रामसुग्रीवयोरिदम् ॥
Drawn to cruelty, they may even kill this daughter of Janaka, foiling the very purpose of the enterprise of Rāma and Sugreeva.
5.30.31
उद्देशे नष्टमार्गेऽस्मिन्
राक्षसैः परिवारिते ।
सागरेण परिक्षिप्ते
गुप्ते वसति जानकी ॥
Jānaki is kept in secrecy in an area that is closed to all roads guarded by Rākshasas and surrounded by the ocean.
5.30.32
विशस्ते वा गृहीते वा
रक्षोभिर्मयि संयुगे ।
नान्यं पश्यामि रामस्य
सहायं कार्यसाधने ॥
Should the Rākshasas kill me or capture me in the altercation, there is no one that I can think of who could help in achieving the purpose of Rāma.
5.30.33
विमृशंश्च न पश्यामि
यो हते मयि वानरः ।
शतयोजनविस्तीर्णम्
लङ्घयेत महोदधिम् ॥
No matter how hard I think, I cannot think of a Vānara who can jump over the mighty ocean that is one hundred Yōjanas wide, once I am done with.
5.30.34
कामं हन्तुं समर्थोऽस्मि
सहस्राण्यपि रक्षसाम् ।
न तु शक्ष्यामि सम्प्राप्तुम्
परं पारं महोदधेः ॥
I am quite capable of killing even thousands of Rākshasas; but then, I would be (so exhausted) that I might not be able to reach the other side of the mighty ocean.
5.30.35
असत्यानि च युद्धानि
संशयो मे न रोचते ।
कश्च निस्संशयं कार्यम्
कुर्यात्प्राज्ञः ससंशयम् ॥
I do not like a fight, for the outcome is uncertain; why would anyone in his right mind turn a situation from certain to uncertain?
5.30.36
प्राणत्यागश्च वैदेह्या
भवेदनभिभाषणे ।
एष दोषो महान्हि स्यात्
मम सीताभिभाषणे ॥
If I do not speak, it would certainly result in Vaidēhee giving up her life. But if I do speak to Seetā, it would result in this terrible situation (of me being caught or killed by the Rākshasas; and either way, it would hurt the cause of Rāma).
5.30.37
भूताश्चार्था विनश्यन्ति
देशकालविरोधिताः ।
विक्लबं दूतमासाद्य
तमः सूर्योदये यथा ॥
Objectives which are as good as gained might be ruined by a foolish envoy who goes against the time and place, as surely as darkness is ruined when the sun comes up.
5.30.38
अर्थानर्थान्तरे बुद्धिः
निश्चितापि न शोभते ।
घातयन्ति हि कार्याणि
दूताः पण्डितमानिनः ॥
Even a mind that is steady falters saddled between pros and cons. The envoy who flatters oneself as wise would only ruin the matters at hand.
5.30.39
न विनश्येत्कथं कार्यम्
वैक्लब्यं न कथं भवेत् ।
लङ्घनं च समुद्रस्य
कथं नु न वृथा भवेत् ।
कथं नु खलु वाक्यं मे
शृणुयान्नोद्विजेत वा ॥
How am I to make sure that the objective is not ruined, judgement does not falter, crossing the ocean does not go in vain and she does not panic on hearing my words?
5.30.40
इति सञ्चिन्त्य हनुमान्
चकार मतिमान्मतिम् ॥
Thinking it over thus, the sagacious Hanumān made up his mind on what to do.
5.30.41
राममक्लिष्टकर्माणम्
सुबन्धुमनुकीर्तयन् ।
नैनामुद्वेजयिष्यामि
तद्बन्धुगतमानसाम् ॥
I would not scare her if I sing praises of her dearest relative Rāma, who accomplishes everything with ease, since her mind is already filled with the thoughts of that companion.
5.30.42
इक्ष्वाकूणां वरिष्ठस्य
रामस्य विदितात्मनः ।
शुभानि धर्मयुक्तानि
वचनानि समर्पयन् ।
श्रावयिष्यामि सर्वाणि
मधुरां प्रब्रुवन् गिरम् ।
श्रद्धास्यति यथाहीयम्
तथा सर्वं समादधे ॥
I shall speak every sweet word about Rāma, the best among the Ikshwākus who is reputed for his personality, that is necessary to create trust in her.
5.30.44
इति स बहुविधं महानुभावो
जगतिपतेः प्रमदामवेक्षमाणः ।
मधुरमवितथं जगाद वाक्यम्
द्रुमविटपान्तरमास्थितो हनूमान् ॥
And Hanumān, the great soul, spoke these sweet and flawless words from his place on the tree-branch looking at the lady of the lord of the world.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे त्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirtieth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
