Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 49
In this Sarga, Hanumān is struck by awe seeing the power, charm, majesty and opulence of the king of Rākshasas. He feels that that king could have become the ruler of the world of Dēvas itself, if only he had not been given to the path of Adharma.
5.49.1
ततस्स कर्मणा तस्य
विस्मितो भीमविक्रमः ।
हनुमान्रोषताम्राक्षो
रक्षोधिपमवैक्षत ॥
Hanumān of formidable prowess, looked, his eyes turning copper-red with anger, at the King of the Rākshasas, astonished at his exploits.
5.49.2
भ्राजमानं महार्हेण
काञ्चनेन विराजता ।
मुक्ताजालावृतेनाथ
मकुटेन महाद्युतिम् ॥
(He saw him) shining with extraordinary brilliance wearing a priceless, dazzling golden crown that was wrapped in a lace-work of pearls.
5.49.3
वज्रसंयोगसंयुक्तैः
महार्हमणिविग्रहैः ।
हैमैराभरणैश्चित्रैः
मनसेव प्रकल्पितैः ॥
The variety of gold ornaments on him that were studded with clusters of diamonds and priceless jewels were so perfect, that they seemed to be imagination that had taken shape by itself.
5.49.4
महार्हक्षौमसंवीतम्
रक्तचन्दनरूषितम् ।
स्वानुलिप्तं विचित्राभिः
विविधाभिश्च भक्तिभिः ॥
He was clad in expensive silk garments, and his body was smeared with red sandal paste on which were traced various interesting patterns.
5.49.5
विचित्रैर्दर्शनीयैश्च
रक्ताक्षैर्भीमदर्शनैः ।
दीप्ततीक्ष्णमहादंष्ट्रैः
प्रलम्बदशनच्छदैः ।
शिरोभिर्दशभिर्वीरम्
भ्राजमानं महौजसम् ।
नानाव्यालसमाकीर्णैः
शिखरैरिव मन्दरम् ॥
That Veera of immense power shone with his ten heads, their blood-red eyes frightening, big sharp teeth gleaming, and their lips pouting, fascinating the beholder like Mandara, with its peaks crowded with various beasts, does.
5.49.7
नीलाञ्जनचयप्रख्यम्
हारेणोरसि राजता ।
पूर्णचन्द्राभवक्त्रेण
सबलाकमिवाम्बुदम् ॥
With his face bright like the moon, his body resembling a mound of black collyrium, his chest shining with gem strings, he looked like a (black) raincloud with (white) cranes flying against it.
5.49.8
बाहुभिर्बद्धकेयूरैः
चन्दनोत्तमरूषितैः ।
भ्राजमानाङ्गदैः पीनैः
पञ्चशीर्षैरिवोरगैः ॥
His stout arms were dazzling like five-hooded serpents, smeared with rare sandal and with Aṅgadas and shoulder-rings tied to them.
5.49.9
महतिस्फाटिके चित्रे
रत्नसंयोगसंस्कृते ।
उत्तमास्तरणास्तीर्णे
सूपविष्टं वरासने ॥
He was comfortably seated on a big, splendid and attractive throne made of crystal and studded with diamonds that was covered with superb spreads.
5.49.10
अलङ्कृताभिरत्यर्थम्
प्रमदाभिः समन्ततः ।
वालव्यजनहस्ताभिः
आरात्समुपसेवितम् ॥
Close to him on all sides were women waiting upon him with richly decorated whisks in their hands.
5.49.11
दुर्धरेण प्रहस्तेन
महापार्श्वेन रक्षसा ।
मन्त्रिभिर्मन्त्रतत्त्वज्ञैः
निकुम्भेन च मन्त्रिणा ।
सुखोपविष्टं रक्षोभिः
चतुर्भिर्बलदर्पितैः ।
कृत्स्नं परिवृतं लोकम्
चतुर्भिरिव सागरैः ॥
With the four Rākshasas, Durdhara, Prahasta, Mahāpārṡwa and Nikumbha, ministers who were savants of strategy, puffing with pride because of their strength, sitting happily around him, he looked like the Earth surrounded by the four oceans.
5.49.13
मन्त्रिभिर्मन्त्रतत्त्वज्ञैः
अन्यैश्च शुभबुद्धिभिः ।
अन्वास्यमानं रक्षोभिः
सुरैरिव सुरेश्वरम् ॥
Attended upon closely by ministers, strategists and other Rākshasas endowed with intellect and wisdom, he was like the lord of the Suras waited upon by Suras.
5.49.14
अपश्यद्राक्षसपतिम्
हनुमानतितेजसम् ।
विष्ठितं मेरुशिखरे
सतोयमिव तोयदम् ॥
Hanumān saw the Lord of Rākshasas whose splendor was limitless and who looked like a raincloud on the peak of Mēru mountain that had not yet discharged its waters, thus seated.
5.49.15
स तैस्सम्पीड्यमानोऽपि
रक्षोभिर्भीमविक्रमैः ।
विस्मयं परमं गत्वा
रक्षोधिपमवैक्षत ॥
Even though he was being tormented by the Rākshasas of terrible prowess, he could not help being extremely amazed, looking at that lord of the Rākshasas.
5.49.16
भ्राजमानं ततो दृष्ट्वा
हनुमान्राक्षसेश्वरम् ।
मनसा चिन्तयामास
तेजसा तस्य मोहितः ॥
Seeing that dazzling lord of Rākshasas and stupefied by his radiance, Hanumān thought to himself:
5.49.17
अहो रूपमहो धैर्यम्
अहो सत्त्वमहो द्युतिः ।
अहो राक्षसराजस्य
सर्वलक्षणयुक्तता ॥
Ah, what charm, what mettle, what power and what brilliance! How well every quality that there is, is blended in this king of Rākshasas!
5.49.18
यद्यधर्मो न बलवान्
स्यादयं राक्षसेश्वरः ।
स्यादयं सुरलोकस्य
सशक्रस्यापि रक्षिता ॥
If only this lord of Rākshasas had not caved in under the powerful lure of Adharma, he might as well have become the ruler of the world of Suras and Ṡakra.
5.49.19
अस्य क्रूरैर्नृशंसैश्च
कर्मभिर्लोककुत्सितैः ।
सर्वे बिभ्यति खल्वस्मात्
लोकास्सामरदानवाः ।
अयं ह्युत्सहते क्रुद्धः
कर्तुमेकार्णवं जगत् ॥
His cruel and unkind acts are despised by the (entire) world. All the worlds including those of Amaras and Dānavas are scared of him. Roused to wrath, he would even be ready to reduce the world into one single ocean.
5.49.20
इति चिन्तां बहुविधाम्
अकरोन्मतिमान् हरिः ।
दृष्ट्वा राक्षसराजस्य
प्रभावममितौजसः ॥
Thus many a thought crossed the mind of the Vānara when he saw the power of the king of Rākshasas and his limitless strength.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे एकोनपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty ninth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
