Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 92
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa's ministers report to him the news of the death of Indrajit, his son. He cries piteously, oppressed by the grief for his son. His grief turning into rage, he thinks of killing the real Seetā, unlike his son, who killed a phantom Seetā to deceive the Vānaras. His well-wishers seek to stop him, but in vain.
Rāvaṇa goes to the Aṡōka pleasance and Seetā cries, seeing him come with a sword in hand. She fears that she might be killed. She wonders whether Rāma and Lakshmaṇa have already been killed. Meanwhile, Supārṡwa, a minister with sound ethics, counsels Rāvaṇa, saying that it does not befit him, the younger brother of Vaiṡravaṇa, who has studied Vedas, to kill a woman. Rāvaṇa, heeding that counsel, goes back.
6.92.1
ततः पौलस्त्यसचिवाः
श्रुत्वा चेन्द्रजितं हतम् ।
आचचक्षुरभिज्ञाय
दशग्रीवाय सत्वरा ॥
Then, hearing about the death of Indrajit and getting the news confirmed, the ministers of the Ten-necked descendant of Paulastya reported the matter to him expeditiously:
6.92.2
युद्धे हतो महाराज
लक्ष्मणेन तवात्मजः ।
विभीषणसहायेन
मिषतां नो महाद्युतिः ॥
O great king! Your son of extraordinary splendor was slain in battle by Lakshmaṇa, aided by Vibheeshaṇa, while we all watched.
6.92.3
शूरः शूरेण सङ्गम्य
संयुगेष्वपराजितः ।
लक्ष्मणेन हतः शूरः
पुत्रस्तु विबुधेन्द्रजित् ।
गतः स परमान् लोकान्
शरैः सन्ताप्य लक्ष्मणम् ॥
Your son, a hero, who vanquished Indra, who never knew defeat in battles, has met another hero, Lakshmaṇa, who is worthy of him, in the fight and was killed by him. Oppressed by the arrows of Lakshmaṇa, he went to higher worlds.
6.92.4
स तं प्रतिभयं श्रुत्वा
वधं पुत्रस्य दारुणम् ।
घोरमिन्द्रजितः सङ्ख्ये
कश्मलं चाविशन्महत् ॥
On hearing the terrifying news of the terrible and ghastly death of his son Indrajit in the battle, Rāvaṇa fell into a dead faint.
6.92.5
उपलभ्य चिरात् सञ्ज्ञाम्
राजा राक्षसपुङ्गवः ।
पुत्रशोकार्दितो दीनो
विललापाकुलेन्द्रियः ॥
Regaining senses after a long while, the king, a bull among Rākshasas, cried pitifully, oppressed by the grief for his son and with all his senses in turmoil:
6.92.6
हा राक्षसचमूमुख्य
मम वत्स महाबल ।
जित्वेन्द्रं कथमद्य त्वम्
लक्ष्मणस्य वशं गतः ॥
Alas! O my child of immense strength, foremost among the Rākshasa warriors, how could you, who had vanquished Indra, succumb to Lakshmaṇa today?
6.92.7
ननु त्वमिषुभिः क्रुद्धो
भिन्द्याः कालान्तकावपि ।
मन्दरस्यापि शृङ्गाणि
किं पुनर्लक्ष्मणं युधि ॥
Roused to wrath, you could surely cleave in twain even Time, the destroyer, and Death itself, with your arrows, and even the peaks of Mandara; how much more easily Lakshmaṇa, in a fight!
6.92.8
अद्य वैवस्वतो राजा
भूयो बहुमतो मम ।
येनाद्य त्वं महाबाहो
संयुक्तः कालधर्मणा ॥
Today, the great Yama, son of Vivaswan, gains additional reverence from me, for, he could make you submit to the laws of Time, O you of mighty arm!
6.92.9
एष पन्थाः सुयोधानाम्
सर्वामरगणेष्वपि ।
यः कृते हन्यते भर्तुः
स पुमान् स्वर्गमृच्छति ॥
This is the path taken by all the great warriors even among the hosts of Dēvas. One who is slain in the service of his master ascends only to Heaven.
6.92.10
अद्य देवगणाः सर्वे
लोकपालास्तथर्षयः ।
हतमिन्द्रजितं श्रुत्वा
सुखं स्वप्स्यन्ति निर्भयाः ॥
All the companies of the Dēvas, the Guardians of the worlds and the Ṛshis, will sleep happily tonight without fear, hearing that Indrajit is slain.
6.92.11
अद्य लोकास्त्रयः कृत्स्ना
पृथिवी च सकानना ।
एकेनेन्द्रजिता हीना
शून्येव प्रतिभाति मे ॥
The three worlds in their entirety and the earth with all its forests seem totally empty to me, lacking just one soul, Indrajit.
6.92.12
अद्य नैर्ऋतकन्यानाम्
श्रोष्याम्यन्तःपुरे रवम् ।
करेणुसङ्घस्य यथा
निनादं गिरिगह्वरे ॥
I will hear in the inner apartments the wail of the Rākshasa maids today, like the trumpeting of grieving she-elephants in a mountain-cave.
6.92.13
यौवराज्यं च लङ्कां च
रक्षांसि च परन्तप ।
मातरं मां च भार्यां च
क्व गतोऽसि विहाय नः ॥
Where have you gone, O scorcher of the foe, leaving Laṅkā, your office of Crown Prince, the Rākshasas, your mother, me and your wife?
6.92.14
मम नाम त्वया वीर
गतस्य यमसादनम् ।
प्रेतकार्याणि कार्याणि
विपरीते हि वर्तसे ॥
You should have been the one to perform funeral rites to me when I go to the abode of Yama; but you have turned it topsy-turvy.
6.92.15
स त्वं जीवति सुग्रीवे
लक्ष्मणे च सराघवे ।
मम शल्यमनुद्धृत्य
क्व गतोऽसि विहाय नः ॥
Where have you gone, abandoning us, before extricating the thorns on my side - Sugreeva, Lakshmaṇa and Rāghava - who are still live?
6.92.16
एवमादिविलापार्तम्
रावणं राक्षसाधिपम् ।
आविवेश महान् कोपः
पुत्रव्यसनसम्भवः ॥
A great rage, born out of the tragedy that befell his son, took over Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, as he lamented thus bitterly.
6.92.17
प्रकृत्या कोपनं ह्येनम्
पुत्रस्य पुनराधयः ।
दीप्तं सन्दीपयामासुः
घर्मेऽर्कमिव रश्मयः ॥
The agony caused by the loss of his son further inflamed his naturally irascible temper, as summer does the rays of the sun.
6.92.18
ललाटे भ्रुकुटीभिश्च
सङ्गताभिर्व्यारोचत ।
युगान्ते सह नक्रैस्तु
महोर्मिभिरिवोदधिः ॥
With his eyebrows knotted on the forehead, he looked like the ocean agitated at the end of Time, crocodiles infesting it and billows rising high.
6.92.19
कोपाद्विजृम्भमाणस्य
वक्त्राद्व्यक्तमभिज्वलन् ।
उत्पपात सधूमाग्निः
वृत्रस्य वदनादिव ॥
In that wrath he let off a big yawn. From his face that clearly seemed to be in a blaze, arose fire and smoke, like from the mouth of Vṛtra.
6.92.20
स पुत्रवधसन्तप्तः
शूरः क्रोधवशं गतः ।
समीक्ष्य रावणो बुद्ध्या
वैदेह्या रोचयद्वधम् ॥
Scorched by grief for his son and overpowered by rage, the heroic Rāvaṇa, in the best of his judgment, felt like killing Vaidēhee.
This Ṡlōka is in the wrong place. It is repeated later in the right place. See 6.92.34.
6.92.21
तस्य प्रकृत्या रक्ते च
रक्ते क्रोधाग्निनाऽपि च ।
रावणस्य महाघोरे
दीप्ते नेत्रे बभूवतुः ॥
Rāvaṇa's naturally red eyes became even more reddish with his burning anger, blazing extremely balefully.
6.92.22
घोरं प्रकृत्या रूपं तत्
तस्य क्रोधाग्निमूर्च्छितम् ।
बभूव रूपं क्रुद्धस्य
रुद्रस्येव दुरासदम् ॥
His naturally terrifying presence became even more unapproachable with a blazing rage overpowering him, making him look like Rudra in His wrath.
6.92.23
तस्य क्रुद्धस्य नेत्राभ्याम्
प्रापतन्नश्रुबिन्दवः ।
दीपाभ्यामिव दीप्ताभ्याम्
सार्चिषः स्नेहबिन्दवः ॥
From his eyes blazing with wrath, dropped tears that looked like burning drops of oil dropping from blazing torches.
6.92.24
दन्तान् विदशतस्तस्य
श्रूयते दशनस्वनः ।
यन्त्रस्याकृष्यमानस्य
महतो दानवैरिव ॥
The sound from his teeth as he ground them resembled the rumble of the huge machine drawn by Dānavas.
6.92.25
कालाग्निरिव सङ्क्रुद्धो
यां यां दिशमवैक्षत ।
तस्यां तस्यां भयत्रस्ता
राक्षसाः संविलिल्यिरे ॥
In whichever direction, he, blazing like the fire of Destruction of the worlds, looked, the Rākshasas who were in that direction, terrified, avoided falling in the line of his sight.
6.92.26
तमन्तकमिव क्रुद्धम्
चराचरचिखादिषुम् ।
वीक्षमाणं दिशः सर्वा
राक्षसा नोपचक्रमुः ॥
No Rākshasa dared approach him, as he looked about in every direction, looking like the enraged Yama, ready to devour everything that was mobile and immobile.
6.92.27
ततः परमसङ्क्रुद्धो
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ।
अब्रवीद्रक्षसां मध्ये
संस्तम्भयिषुराहवे ॥
Then the extremely enraged Rāvaṇa, Ruler of the Rākshasas, spoke in the midst of those Rākshasas asserting his intention to continue the stand off.
6.92.28
मया वर्षसहस्राणि
चरित्वा दुश्चरं तपः ।
तेषु तेष्ववकाशेषु
स्वयम्भूः परितोषितः ।
तस्यैव तपसो व्युष्ट्या
प्रसादाच्च स्वयम्भुवः ।
नासुरेभ्यो न देवेभ्यो
भयं मम कदाचन ॥
I performed fierce Tapa for thousands of years, with which the Self-born was pleased, many a time. As a result of that Tapa and because of the blessings of the Self-born, I do not have any fear ever either from the Asuras or from the Dēvas.
6.92.30
कवचं ब्रह्मदत्तं मे
यदादित्यसमप्रभम् ।
देवासुरविमर्देषु
न भिन्नं वज्रशक्तिभिः ॥
The armor that was given to me by Brahma, which shines like the sun, could not be cloven even by Vajra and Ṡakti in the battle between Dēvas and Asuras.
6.92.31
तेन मामद्य संयुक्तम्
रथस्थमिह संयुगे ।
प्रतीयात् कोऽद्य मामाजौ
साक्षादपि पुरन्दरः ॥
With me wearing it and riding in the chariot, who can stand against me in the fight today, even if it is Purandara himself?
6.92.32
यत्तदाऽभिप्रसन्नेन
सशरं कार्मुकं महत् ।
देवासुरविमर्देषु
मम दत्तं स्वयम्भुवा ।
अद्य तूर्यशतैर्भीमम्
धनुरुत्थाप्यतां मम ।
रामलक्ष्मणयोरेव
वधाय परमाहवे ॥
Let the great bow that was given to me by the extremely pleased Self-born at the time of the battle between Dēvas and Asuras be taken out today in the midst of hundreds of trumpets blaring, to slay Rāma and Lakshmaṇa in the ultimate of the battles.
6.92.34
स पुत्रवधसन्तप्तः
शूरः क्रोधवशं गतः ।
समीक्ष्य रावणो बुद्ध्या
सीतां हन्तुं व्यवस्यत ॥
Scorched by grief for his son and overpowered by rage, the heroic Rāvaṇa, in the best of his judgment, decided to kill Seetā.
6.92.35
प्रत्यवेक्ष्य तु ताम्राक्षः
सुघोरो घोरदर्शनः ।
दीनो दीनस्वरान् सर्वान्
तानुवाच निशाचरान् ॥
Looking terrifying with his reddened eyes, he, the formidable one, looked at all the woebegone Night-rovers, and, in his misery, said:
6.92.36
मायया मम वत्सेन
वञ्चनार्थं वनौकसाम् ।
किञ्चिदेव हतं तत्र
सीतेयमिति दर्शितम् ॥
Something was killed over there and through magic it was shown as Seetā by my dear son in order to deceive the Vana-dwellers.
6.92.37
तदिदं तथ्यमेवाहम्
करिष्ये प्रियमात्मनः ।
वैदेहीं नाशयिष्यामि
क्षत्रबन्धुमनुव्रताम् ॥
I will make that, which gives me pleasure, happen in reality. I shall annihilate Vaidēhee who clings to the worthless Kshatriya.
6.92.38
इत्येवमुक्त्वा सचिवान्
खड्गमाशु परामृशत् ।
उत्प्लुत्य गुणसम्पन्नम्
विमलाम्बरवर्चसम् ।
निष्पपात स वेगेन
सभायाः सचिवैर्वृतः ॥
Saying this to the ministers, he put his hand on the excellent sword that shone like clear blue sky. And springing up, he left the assembly quickly, with the ministers about him.
6.92.39
रावणः पुत्रशोकेन
भृशमाकुलचेतनः ।
सङ्क्रुद्धः खड्गमादाय
सहसा यत्र मैथिली ॥
His heart in a turmoil with grief for his son, Rāvaṇa took the sword in rage and proceeded quickly to where Maithili was.
6.92.40
व्रजन्तं राक्षसं प्रेक्ष्य
सिंहनादं विचुक्रुशुः ।
ऊचुश्चान्योन्यमाश्लिष्य
सङ्क्रुद्धं प्रेक्ष्य राक्षसाः ॥
On seeing him going there, the Rākshasas let off roaring shouts. And seeing how wrathful he was, they hugged and told each other:
6.92.41
अद्यैनं तावुभौ दृष्ट्वा
भ्रातरौ प्रव्यथिष्यतः ।
लोकपाला हि चत्वारः
क्रुद्धेनानेन निर्जिताः ।
बहवः शत्रवश्चापि
संयुगेषु निपातिताः ॥
Those two brothers shall writhe in agony on seeing him. Were not the four guardians of the Worlds vanquished by him in his wrath? Were not countless foes also struck down by him in battle after battle?
6.92.43
त्रिषु लोकेषु रत्नानि
भुङ्क्ते चाहृत्य रावणः ।
विक्रमे च बले चैव
नास्त्यस्य सदृशो भुवि ॥
He gathers the best of everything from everywhere in the three worlds and enjoys them. There is no equal for Rāvaṇa on the earth either in prowess or in strength.
6.92.44
तेषां सञ्जल्पमानानाम्
अशोकवनिकां गताम् ।
अभिदुद्राव वैदेहीम्
रावणः क्रोधमूर्च्छितः ॥
As they were chatting thus, Rāvaṇa, overpowered with rage, went to Seetā who was in the Aṡōka pleasance.
6.92.45
वार्यमाणः सुसङ्क्रुद्धः
सुहृद्भिर्हितबुद्धिभिः ।
अभ्यधावत सङ्क्रुद्धः
खे ग्रहो रोहिणीमिव ॥
Though his friends who wished him well sought to prevent him, he rushed upon her like an evil planet on the star Rōhiṇee in the sky.
6.92.46
मैथिली रक्ष्यमाणा तु
राक्षसीभिरनिन्दिता ।
ददर्श राक्षसं क्रुद्धम्
निस्त्रिंशवरधारिणम् ॥
Maithili, who was being guarded by Rākshasis and who was never found to be at fault, saw that enraged Rākshasa, wielding that striking sword.
6.92.47
तं निशाम्य सनिस्त्रिंशम्
व्यथिता जनकात्मजा ।
निवार्यमाणं बहुशः
सुहृद्भिरनिवर्तिनम् ।
सीता दुःखसमाविष्टा
विलपन्तीदमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing him come wielding the sword and not turning back in spite of many attempts by his friends, Seetā, the daughter of Janaka, said this, overwhelmed by grief and crying:
6.92.48
यथाऽयं मामभिक्रुद्धः
समभिद्रवति स्वयम् ।
वधिष्यति सनाथां माम्
अनाथामिव दुर्मतिः ॥
It seems that this wicked one is going to kill me from the way he himself is rushing upon me in rage, as if I, who have a protector, do not have one.
6.92.49
बहुशश्चोदयामास
भर्तारं मामनुव्रताम् ।
भार्या भव रमस्वेति
प्रत्याख्यातो ध्रुवं मया ।
सोऽयं ममानुपस्थाने
व्यक्तं नैराश्यमागतः ।
क्रोधमोहसमाविष्टो
निहन्तुं मां समुद्यतः ॥
He tried multiple times to persuade me, who am devoted to my husband, saying, ‘become my wife!’, and was spurned by me firmly. Hence he, finding me unyielding, has clearly given up all hopes. Gripped by resentment and infatuation, he seems to be ready to kill me.
6.92.51
अथवा तौ नरव्याघ्रौ
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
मन्निमित्तमनार्येण
समरेऽद्य निपातितौ ।
अहो धिङ्मन्निमित्तोऽयम्
विनाशो राजपुत्रयोः ॥
Or perhaps the brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, tigers among men, have been struck down by this ignoble creature in battle on my account. Dhik on me, that I should be the cause of the death of the princes!
6.92.53
अथवा पुत्रशोकेन
अहत्वा रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
विधमिष्यति मां रौद्रो
राक्षसः पापनिश्चयः ॥
Or else, unable to slay Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, this Rākshasa, bent on committing evil acts, might kill me, in his rage and grief for his son.
6.92.54
हनूमतस्तु तद्वाक्यम्
न कृतं क्षुद्रया मया ।
यद्यहं तस्य पृष्ठेन
तदायासमनिर्जिता ।
नाद्यैवमनुशोचेयम्
भर्तुरङ्कगता सती ॥
That advise of Hanumān was not followed by me, a silly fool. If only I had gone, carried on his back, even though not won (by Rāma), I would have been sitting on my husband's lap, not to have to lament like this.
6.92.55
मन्ये तु हृदयं तस्याः
कौसल्यायाः फलिष्यति ।
एकपुत्रा यदा पुत्रम्
विनष्टं श्रोष्यते युधि ॥
Kousalyā's heart, I am worried, will burst when she, who has only one son, hears that her son died in the battle.
6.92.56
सा हि जन्म च बाल्यं च
यौवनं च महात्मनः ।
धर्मकार्याणि रूपं च
रुदन्ती संस्मरिष्यति ॥
She will surely cry recalling the birth, childhood, youth, his appearance and the many righteous deeds of that Mahātma.
6.92.57
निराशा निहते पुत्रे
दत्त्वा श्राद्धमचेतना ।
अग्निमावेक्ष्यते नूनम्
आपो वाऽपि प्रवेक्ष्यति ॥
All her hopes (for life) dashed with her son slain, she, with her mind completely numbed, would perform the final rites for him and jump into fire or drown herself in water.
6.92.58
धिगस्तु कुब्जामसतीम्
मन्थरां पापनिश्चयाम् ।
यन्निमित्तमिदं दुःखम्
कौसल्या प्रतिपत्स्यते ॥
Dhik on the wicked humpback Manthara and her sinful resolve, because of which this great tragedy befell Kousalyā!
6.92.59
इत्येवं मैथिलीं दृष्ट्वा
विलपन्तीं तपस्विनीम् ।
रोहिणीमिव चन्द्रेण
विना ग्रहवशं गताम् ।
एतस्मिन्नन्तरे तस्य
अमात्यो शीलवान् शुचिः ।
सुपार्श्वो नाम मेधावी
रावणं राक्षसेश्वरम् ।
निवार्यमाणं सचिवै
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Meanwhile, on seeing Maithili suffer and lament thus like Rōhiṇee beset by an evil planet in the absence of the moon, a sagacious minister with sound ethics and good conscience, Supārṡwa by name, addressed Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, whom the ministers were trying to hold back, saying these words:
6.92.62
कथं नाम दशग्रीव
साक्षाद्वैश्रवणानुज ।
हन्तुमिच्छसि वैदेहीम्
क्रोधाद्धर्ममपास्य च ॥
How can you, O Ten-necked, being the younger brother of Vaiṡravaṇa himself, think of killing Vaidēhee in your rage, leaving Dharma by the wayside!
6.92.63
वेदविद्याव्रतस्नातः
स्वकर्मनिरतः सदा ।
स्त्रियाः कस्माद्वधं वीर
मन्यसे राक्षसेश्वर ॥
Having worked hard to qualify yourself to study all branches of Vedas, and having always been zealous in performing every activity that is prescribed for you, how can you, O Veera, O Lord of the Rākshasas, bring yourself to kill a woman!
6.92.64
मैथिलीं रूपसम्पन्नाम्
प्रत्यवेक्षस्व पार्थिव ।
तस्मिन्नेव सहास्माभिः
आहवे क्रोधमुत्सृज ॥
Be patient, O King, with the extraordinarily beautiful Maithili! Vent out your anger, along with us, only on him, in the battle!
6.92.65
अभ्युत्थानं त्वमद्यैव
कृष्णपक्षचतुर्दशीम् ।
कृत्वा निर्याह्यमावास्याम्
विजयाय बलैर्वृतः ॥
Make preparations for a fresh offensive right today, the fourteenth day of the dark fortnight, and march forth tomorrow, the new moon day, with your forces, to garner victory.
6.92.66
शूरो धीमान् रथी खड्गी
रथप्रवरमास्थितः ।
हत्वा दशरथिं रामम्
भवान् प्राप्स्यति मैथिलीम् ॥
The hero, the sagacious one, the able charioter, and the great wielder of sword that you are, you shall go riding in your splendid chariot and kill Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha; and then, Maithili will be yours.
6.92.67
स तद्दुरात्मा सुहृदा निवेदितं
वचः सुधर्म्यं प्रतिगृह्य रावणः ।
गृहं जगामाथ ततश्च वीर्यवान्
पुनः सभां च प्रययौ सुहृद्वृतः ॥
Heeding that virtuous counsel tendered by his well-wisher, the evil-minded Rāvaṇa went home. And from there the valiant one went to the council hall again, along with his friends.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्विनवतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninety second Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
