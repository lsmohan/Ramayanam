Baala Kaanda - Sarga 8
The backdrop of Kōsala kingdom and the city of Ayōdhyā were described in the previous Sargas.
This Sarga focuses on a problem faced by the King Daṡaratha and the action he plans to take to address it.
1.8.1
तस्य त्वेवम्प्रभावस्य
धर्मज्ञस्य महात्मनः ।
सुतार्थं तप्यमानस्य
नासीद्वंशकरस्सुतः ॥
That radiant Mahātma and expert in Dharma (Daṡaratha) however, was bereft of offspring to continue the dynasty.
1.8.2
चिन्तयानस्य तस्यैवम्
बुद्धिरासीन्महात्मनः ।
सुतार्थी वाजिमेधेन
किमर्थं न यजाम्यहम् ॥
He was worried about it and it occurred to him: "Why not I perform the Aṡwamēdha Yāga, to be blessed with offspring!"
1.8.3
स निश्चितां मतिं कृत्वा
यष्टव्यमिति बुद्धिमान् ।
मन्त्रिभिस्सह धर्मात्मा
सर्वैरेव कृतात्मभिः ।
ततोऽब्रवीदिदं राजा
सुमन्त्रं मन्त्रिसत्तमम् ।
शीघ्रमानय मे सर्वान्
गुरूंस्तान् सपुरोहितान् ॥
The king, the Dharmātma and the prudent, having made up his mind to perform the Yāga in conjunction with the noble minded ministers, called upon the chief minister Sumantra and requested him to arrange for the presence of all his Gurus along with all the priests.
1.8.5
ततस्सुमन्त्रस्त्वरितम्
गत्वा त्वरितविक्रमः ।
समानयत् स तान् सर्वान्
समस्तान् वेदपारगान् ।
सुयज्ञं वामदेवं च
जाबालिमथ काश्यपम् ।
पुरोहितं वसिष्ठं च
ये चान्ये द्विजसत्तमाः ॥
The expedient Sumantra, immediately arranged for the presence of all the Gurus, who were well versed in Vēdas: Suyajña, Vāmadēva, Jābāli, Kāṡyapa and other great Brāhmaṇas including Vasishṭha, the Purōhita.
1.8.7
तान् पूजयित्वा धर्मात्मा
राजा दशरथस्तदा ।
इदं धर्मार्थसहितम्
वाक्यं श्लक्ष्णमथाब्रवीत् ॥
Receiving them with all honour, King Daṡaratha, the Dharmātma said the following words, that were in pursuit of Dharma and Artha:
Every act we perform can be dissected as serving one or more of the four purposes, Dharma, Artha, Kāma or Mōksha.
This Ṡlōka says that having offspring serves the purposes of Dharma and Artha.
1.8.8
मम लालप्यमानस्य
पुत्रार्थं नास्ति वै सुखम् ।
तदर्थं हयमेधेन
यक्ष्यामीति मतिर्मम ॥
I am not happy. My heart is yearning for offspring. Hence I am thinking of performing the Aṡwamēdha Yāga.
1.8.9
तदहं यष्टुमिच्छामि
शास्त्रदृष्टेन कर्मणा ।
कथं प्राप्स्याम्यहं कामम्
बुद्धिरत्रविचार्यताम् ॥
I would like to perform it in strict conformance with the Ṡāstras. Please help me with the best of your ideas on how I can go about it.
1.8.10
ततस्साध्विति तद्वाक्यम्
ब्राह्मणाः प्रत्यपूजयन् ।
वसिष्ठप्रमुखास्सर्वे
पार्थिवस्य मुखाच्च्युतम् ॥
Vasishṭha and other Brāhmaṇas, praising what had fallen from the king’s lips responded saying that it is, indeed, a good idea.
1.8.11
ऊचुश्च परमप्रीताः
सर्वे दशरथं वचः ॥
In full appreciation of Daṡaratha's idea, they said,
1.8.11
सम्भारास्सम्भ्रियन्तां ते
तुरङ्गश्च विमुच्यताम् ।
सर्वथा प्राप्स्यसे पुत्रान्
अभिप्रेतांश्च पार्थिव ।
यस्य ते धार्मिकी बुद्धिः
इयं पुत्रार्थमागता ॥
Let all the arrangements be made and let the horse be released! You will, indeed, be blessed with lovable sons. Your desire to have offspring is very much natural, appropriate and in accordance with Dharma.
1.8.13
ततः प्रीतोऽभवद्राजा
श्रुत्वैतद्द्विजभाषितम् ।
अमात्यांश्चाब्रवीद्राजा
हर्षपर्याकुलेक्षणः ॥
The king was happy hearing the words of Brāhmaṇas and with eyes full of joyful tears, said to the ministers:
1.8.14
सम्भारास्सम्भ्रियन्तां मे
गुरूणां वचनादिह ।
समर्थाधिष्ठितश्चाश्वः
सोपाध्यायो विमुच्यताम् ॥
Let all the preparations be made according to the instructions of our Gurus. Let the horse be released guarded by able warriors and followed by teachers of Ṡāstras.
1.8.15
सरय्वाश्चोत्तरे तीरे
यज्ञभूमिर्विधीयताम् ।
शान्तयश्चाभिवर्धन्ताम्
यथाकल्पं यथाविधि ॥
Let the ground be prepared on the northern banks of river Sarayu for performing the Yāga. Let invocations be made for peace as per the traditions and Ṡāstras.
1.8.16
शक्यः प्राप्तुमयं यज्ञः
सर्वेणापि महीक्षिता ।
नापराधो भवेत्कष्टो
यद्यस्मिन् क्रतुसत्तमे ॥
This Yāga, one among the best, is very demanding and difficult to perform through to the end, without committing any errors. Otherwise, every other king would have performed it.
Yajñas are performed to create certain specific spiritual energies. Any errors or faults in the process will earn the criticism of the learned on the one hand, and give scope for the wrong energies (Brahma Rākshasas) to be generated, on the other hand. Hence, only the very capable, the very meticulous take on the task of performing an Yajña.
Think of the process of synthesizing a chemical product. Even a simple error in the process can result in wrong or potentially dangerous product (molecule). Or think of making a spaceship. A single error in its making is all that it takes to result in disaster. The same is true with Yajñas. If something goes wrong, the consequences can be highly adverse.
1.8.17
छिद्रं हि मृगयन्तेऽत्र
विद्वांसो ब्रह्मराक्षसाः ।
निहतस्य च यज्ञस्य
सद्यः कर्ता विनश्यति ॥
The learned as well as the Brahma Rākshasas will only be eager to find any violations. A failed Yāga will also take down the performer, for certain.
1.8.18
तद्यथा विधिपूर्वं मे
क्रतुरेष समाप्यते ।
तथा विधानं क्रियताम्
समर्थाः करणेष्विह ॥
Hence, you, the able individuals, please plan on ensuring that the Yāga is performed to its full completion strictly in accordance with the Ṡāstras.
1.8.19
तथेति चाब्रुवन् सर्वे
मन्त्रिणः प्रत्यपूजयन् ।
पार्थिवेन्द्रस्य तद्वाक्यम्
यथाज्ञप्तं निशम्य ते ॥
The ministers acknowledged the king's words in full deference and said that they would do as commanded.
1.8.20
तथा द्विजास्ते धर्मज्ञा
वर्द्धयन्तो नृपोत्तमम् ।
अनुज्ञातास्ततस्सर्वे
पुनर्जग्मुर्यथागतम् ॥
Brāhmaṇas, the savants of Dharma wished the king well, took proper leave and left.
1.8.21
विसर्जयित्वा तान् विप्रान्
सचिवानिदमब्रवीत् ।
ऋत्विग्भिरुपदिष्टोऽयम्
यथावत् क्रतुराप्यताम् ॥
After seeing off the Brāhmaṇas, Daṡaratha turned to the ministers and said: Please perform the Yāga as per the instructions of the Ṛtviks.
1.8.22
इत्युक्त्वा नृपशार्दूलः
सचिवान् समुपस्थितान् ।
विसर्जयित्वा स्वं वेश्म
प्रविवेश महाद्युतिः ॥
Saying thus, the radiant tiger among men (Daṡaratha) dismissed the assembly of ministers and returned home.
1.8.23
ततस्स गत्वा ताः पत्नीः
नरेन्द्रो हृदयप्रियाः ।
उवाच दीक्षां विशत
यक्ष्येऽहं सुतकारणात् ॥
Upon reaching home, he told his dear wives about the plan to perform a Yajña that would bless them with offspring and asked them to take Deeksha.
Deeksha is a formal act of commencement and commitment to a certain ritual with all the accompanying practices and routine that may last from a few hours to a few months to a few years to a life time.
1.8.24
तासां तेनातिकान्तेन
वचनेन सुवर्चसाम् ।
मुखपद्मान्यशोभन्त
पद्मानीव हिमात्यये ॥
Upon hearing those cheerful words, their faces shone like lotuses at the end of winter.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे अष्टमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
