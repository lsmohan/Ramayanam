Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 59
In this Sarga, Sumantra describes to King Daṡaratha, the gloomy and lifeless state of everything that he has seen, as he returned from the Vana without Rāma in the chariot, starting from the Vana, to the city and to the palace.
Daṡaratha feels extremely distressed. He regrets not consulting anyone including his ministers, before letting Rāma go to Vana. He says that if it is in his power, he would immediately order Rāma to come back. He pleads pitifully with Sumantra to take him where Rāma is.
2.59.1
ममत्वश्वा निवृत्तस्य
न प्रावर्तन्त वर्त्मनि ।
उष्णमश्रु प्रमुञ्चन्तो
रामे सम्प्रस्थिते वनम् ॥
When I wished to return, on Rāma's leaving for the Vana, the horses, shedding warm tears, would not move.
2.59.2
उभाभ्यां राजपुत्राभ्याम्
अथ कृत्वाहमञ्जलिम् ।
प्रस्थितो रथमास्थाय
तद्दुःखमपि धारयन् ॥
After bidding farewell with joined palms to the two princes, I got onto the chariot and managed to leave, holding my grief.
2.59.3
गुहेन सार्धं तत्रैव
स्थितोऽस्मि दिवसान्बहून् ।
आशया यदि मां रामः
पुनश्शब्दापयेदिति ॥
I tarried for many days with Guha in the hope that I might hear from Rāma again.
2.59.4
विषये ते महाराज
रामव्यसनकर्शिताः ।
अपि वृक्षाः परिम्लानाः
सपुष्पाङ्कुरकोरकाः ॥
In your country, O supreme King, even the trees that are full of flowers, shoots and buds, are withering, drained of their vitals by the thought of Rāma's woes.
2.59.5
उपतप्तोदका नद्यः
पल्वलानि सरांसि च ।
परिशुष्कपलाशानि
वनान्युपवनानि च ॥
The water in the rivers, ponds and lakes is boiling. The leaves in the forests and parks have dried up.
2.59.6
न च सर्पन्ति सत्त्वानि
व्यासा न प्रचरन्ति च ।
रामशोकाभिभूतं तत्
निष्कूजमभवद्वनम् ॥
No creature shows any sign of life. Wild beasts stopped roaming about. Overwhelmed by grief for Rāma, the Vana lies still.
2.59.7
लीनपुष्करपत्राश्च
नरेन्द्र कलुषोदकाः ।
सन्तप्तपद्माः पद्मिन्यो
लीनमीनविहङ्गमाः ॥
O lord of peoples! The waters of lotus pools have become turbid. The lotuses in them are parched. Their petals are not to be seen. And the fish and birds are nowhere in sight.
2.59.8
जलजानि च पुष्पाणि
माल्यानि स्थलजानि च ।
नाद्य भान्त्यल्पगन्धीनि
फलानि च यथापुरम् ॥
The flowers in the water, and the festoons on land and also the fruits, have lost their wonted luster and are left with but little of their former fragrance.
2.59.9
अत्रोद्यानानि शून्यानि
प्रलीनविहगानि च ।
न चाभिरामा नारामान्
पश्यामि मनुजर्षभ ॥
O bull among men! Here in the city, I find the parks empty, the birds sunk from sight, the gardens anything but a pleasure to the eye!
2.59.10
प्रविशन्तमयोध्यां माम्
न कश्चिदभिनन्दति ।
नरा राममपश्यन्तो
निश्श्वसन्ति मुहुर्मुहुः ॥
No one greeted me when I entered Ayōdhyā; People were heaving sighs repeatedly, not seeing Rāma.
2.59.11
देव राजरथं दृष्ट्वा
विना राममिहागतम् ।
दुःखादश्रुमुखस्सर्वो
राजमार्गगतो जनः ॥
My lord! Seeing the royal chariot returning without Rāma the faces of everyone on the royal pathway were bedewed with tears, out of grief.
2.59.12
हर्म्यैर्विमानैः प्रासादैः
अवेक्ष्यरथमागतम् ।
हाहाकारकृतानार्यो
रामादर्शनकर्शिताः ॥
On seeing me return (without Rāma) the women, from their mansions, high towers and palaces, wailed, "Alas, alas!', pining for the sight of Rāma's face.
This Ṡlōka is the same as 2.57.18
2.59.13
आयतैर्विमलैर्नेत्रैः
अश्रुवेगपरिप्लुतैः ।
अन्योन्यमभिवीक्षन्तेऽ -
व्यक्तमार्ततराः स्त्रियः ॥
With their wide and lustrous eyes blurred by swift-flowing tears, the sorely distressed women looked at each other, feeling at a loss.
This Ṡlōka is the same as 2.57.19
2.59.14
नामित्राणां न मित्राणाम्
उदासीनजनस्य च ।
अहमार्ततया किञ्चित्
अशेषमुपलक्षये ॥
I could find no difference in their agony, be they friends, foes or unconcerned.
2.59.15
अप्रहृष्टमनुष्या च
दीननागतुरङ्गमा ।
आर्तस्वरपरिम्लाना
विनिश्श्वसितनिस्स्वना ।
निरानन्दा महाराज
रामप्रव्राजनातुरा ।
कौसल्या पुत्रहीनेव
अयोध्या प्रतिभाति मा ॥
Ayōdhyā, with its joyless people, and its dispirited elephants and horses, exhausted by cries of distress and long sighs, lost all its glee, agonized by the exile of Rāma. To me, Ayōdhyā seems to mirror, in its gloom, Kousalyā, who is now without her son.
The last sentence of Ṡlōka 16 is poignant. The entire city of Ayōdhyā grieves like a mother who has lost her own son.
Just as the entire community of Mathura considered that Krishna belonged to everyone of them, the entire city of Ayōdhyā feels that Rāma is her own son.
History, now and then, produces such darlings, who earn the affection and love of an entire community, city and even country.
Rāmāyaṇa is a song to celebrate all such blessed souls, and show life as seen from their trajectory of life.
2.59.17
सूतस्य वचनं श्रुत्वा
वाचा परमदीनया ।
बाष्पोपहतया राजा
तं सूतमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those words of the charioteer, the king, convulsing in sobs and feeling despondent, said these words to the charioteer:
2.59.18
कैकेय्या विनियुक्तेन
पापाभिजनभावया ।
मया न मन्त्रकुशलैः
वृद्धैस्सह समर्थितम् ॥
After being incited by Kaikēyee, who harbored evil thoughts and was surrounded by evil people, I have not thought it through along with strategists and wise counsel.
2.59.19
न सुहृद्भिर्नचामात्यैः
मन्त्रयित्वा न नैगमैः ।
मयायमर्थस्सम्मोहात्
स्त्रीहेतोस्सहसा कृतः ॥
Without discussing the matter with friends, ministers or the chief citizens, I foolishly and in undue haste decided upon this course for a woman's sake.
2.59.20
भवितव्यतया नूनम्
इदं वा व्यसनं महत् ।
कुलस्यास्य विनाशाय
प्राप्तं सूत यदृच्छया ॥
Perhaps this great calamity is nothing but imminent, a work of Providence, for the undoing of this family.
2.59.21
सूत यद्यस्ति ते किञ्चित्
मया तु सुकृतं कृतम् ।
त्वं प्रापयाऽऽशु मां रामम्
प्राणास्सन्त्वरयन्तिमाम् ॥
Charioteer, if there is anything worthy that I have done for you ever, please take me to Rāma immediately. My fast-ebbing life gives me no rest!
2.59.22
यद्यद्यापि ममैवाज्ञा
निवर्तयतु राघवम् ।
न शक्ष्यामि विना रामम्
मुहूर्तमपि जीवितुम् ॥
If I can have my way even now, may Rāghava be brought back! I cannot afford to live even for a moment without Rāma.
2.59.23
अथवाऽपि महाबाहुः
गतो दूरं भविष्यति ।
मामेव रथमारोप्य
शीघ्रं रामाय दर्शय ॥
Or, if he of the mighty arm might have gone a long way by this time, take me on the chariot and show to Rāma.
2.59.24
वृत्तदंष्ट्रो महेष्वासः
क्वासौ लक्ष्मणपूर्वजः ।
यदि जीवामि साध्वेनम्
पश्येयं सीतया सह ॥
Where is that elder brother of Lakshmaṇa, who holds the great bow, and whose teeth are beautifully rounded? If I can have a good look at him and Seetā, I will be able to hold on to this life.
2.59.25
लोहिताक्षं महाबाहुम्
आमुक्तमणिकुण्डलम् ।
रामं यदि न पश्येयम्
गमिष्यामि यमक्षयम् ॥
If I cannot see him of the red-tinged eyes and the great arms, wearing the ear-pendants of pearl and gem, I cannot but head to the abode of Yama.
2.59.26
अतो नु किं दुःखतरम्
सोऽहमिक्ष्वाकुनन्दनम् ।
इमामवस्थामापन्नो
नेह पश्यामि राघवम् ॥
What else could be of greater grief, than not being able to Rāghava, the delight of Ikshwāku dynasty, right here!
2.59.27
हा राम रामानुज हा
हा वैदेहि तपस्विनि ।
न मां जानीथ दुःखेन
म्रियमाणमनाथवत् ॥
Alas, Rāma, alas, Rāma's younger brother, alas, the princess of Vidēha the poor sufferer, you do not know that I am dying of grief like a forlorn creature !
2.59.28
स तेन राजा दुःखेन
भृशमर्पितचेतनः ।
अवगाढस्सुदुष्पारम्
शोकसागरमब्रवीत् ॥
And the King, with his heart sunk in an abyss of grief, bogged down in an impassable ocean of misery, said:
2.59.29
रामशोकमहाभोगः
सीताविरहपारगः ।
श्वसितोर्मि महावर्तो
बाष्पफेनजालाविलः ।
बाहुविक्षेपमीनौघो
विक्रन्दित महास्वनः ।
प्रकीर्णकेशशैवालः
कैकेयीबडबामुखः ।
ममाश्रुवेगप्रभवः
कुब्जावाक्यमहाग्रहः ।
वरवेलो नृशंसाया
रामप्रव्राजनायतः ।
यस्मिन्बत निमग्नोऽहम्
कौसल्ये राघवं विना ।
दुस्तरो जीवता देवि
मयाऽयं शोकसागरः ॥
O Kousalyā, good lady! Alas! There is no crossing for me, in this life, of the ocean of sorrow in which I lie submerged. The only luxury that there is in it, is the grief for Rāma. It is as wide as my yearning to see Seetā. My sighs are its tides and swirls. My tears are its clashing waters that bear froth. The flinging of my arms is its shoal of fish. My cries are its roar. It is filled with my flowing tears. My disheveled hair is the moss that is spread on it. Kaikēyee is the volcano burning underneath it. The words of the humpback are its crocodiles. Its shores are marked by the boons granted to a cruel woman. It is as wide as the exile of Rāma.
For the first time, Vālmeeki makes it explicit that Daṡaratha knows about the hand of Manthara behind this entire chain of events.
2.59.33
अशोभनं योऽहमिहाद्य राघवं
दिदृक्षमाणो न लभे सलक्ष्मणम् ।
इतीव राजा विलपन्महायशाः
पपात तूर्णं शयने समूर्छितः ॥
Nothing could be worse than my inability at this time to have the sight of Rāghava and Lakshmaṇa. Lamenting thus, that King of great renown fell down on his bed in a swoon instantly.
2.59.34
इति विलपति पार्थिवे प्रणष्टे
करुणतरं द्विगुणं च रामहेतोः ।
वचनमनुनिशम्य तस्य देवी
भयमगमत्पुनरेव राममाता ॥
And as the King, thus wailing fell into a faint, in a doubly pitiful state on account of Rāma, his queen, the mother of Rāma, shuddered with fear again, hearing his words.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकोनषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty ninth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
