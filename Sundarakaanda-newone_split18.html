Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 18
In this Sarga, Hanumān watches Rāvaṇa as he comes to the Aṡōka Vana surrounded by many of his beautiful wives and women, all of whom are still under the hangover of drink and sleep. Hanumān is taken aback by Rāvaṇa’s dazzling radiance and watches him, hidden behind the leaves.
5.18.1
तथा विप्रेक्षमाणस्य
वनं पुष्पितपादपम् ।
विचिन्वतश्च वैदेहीम्
किंचिच्छेषा निशाऽभवत् ॥
The night all but passed, while he, on his mission of search for Vaidēhee, watched that Vana full of trees in flower.
5.18.2
षडङ्गवेदविदुषाम्
क्रतुप्रवरयाजिनाम् ।
शुश्राव ब्रह्मघोषान् स
विरात्रे ब्रह्मरक्षसाम् ॥
He then heard, as the night receded, the reverberating chants of Vedas by the Brāhmaṇas among Rākshasas who were well-versed in the Vedas including the six ancillary texts, and seasoned in performing the most challenging of the Yajñas.
5.18.3
अथ मङ्गलवादित्र
शब्दैः श्रुतिमनोहरैः ।
प्राबुध्यत महाबाहुः
दशग्रीवो महाबलः ॥
Then the Ten-necked (Rāvaṇa), of mighty arm and immense strength, woke up to the auspicious sounds of the musical instruments that were a delight to the ears and heart.
5.18.4
विबुध्यतु यथाकालम्
राक्षसेन्द्रः प्रतापवान् ।
स्रस्तमाल्याम्बरधरो
वैदेहीमन्वचिन्तयत् ॥
The powerful lord of Rākshasas woke up at the regular hour and (instantly) thought of Vaidēhee, his loosened raiment and garland still sagging down.
5.18.5
भृशं नियुक्तस्तस्यां च
मदनेन मदोत्कटः ।
न स तं राक्षसः कामम्
शशाकात्मनि गूहितुम् ॥
His mind forcibly dragged towards her and his body gripped by raging passion, that Rākshasa was unable to contain his lust.
5.18.6
स सर्वाभरणैर्युक्तो
बिभ्रच्छ्रियमनुत्तमाम् ।
तां नगैर्बहुभिर्जुष्टाम्
सर्वपुष्पफलोपगैः ।
वृतां पुष्करिणीभिश्च
नानापुष्पोपशोभिताम् ।
सदा मदैश्च विहगैः
विचित्रां परमाद्भुताम् ।
ईहामृगैश्च विविधैः
जुष्टां दृष्टिमनोहरैः ।
वीथीः संप्रेक्षमाणश्च
मणिकाञ्चनतोरणाः ।
नानामृगगणाकीर्णाम्
फलैः प्रपतितैर्वृताम् ।
अशोकवनिकामेव
प्राविशत्संततद्रुमाम् ॥
Adorned with every ornament and exuding extraordinary opulence, he then went to the Aṡōka Vana, which was full of thickets of trees that gave every kind of flower and fruit, surrounded by ponds and splendid with flowers everywhere, marvelous with its variety of amorous birds, filled with pets that were a delight to eyes and hearts and hordes of many other kinds of animals, covered with fruits that had fallen everywhere, watching the streets on the way, their arches made of gold and encrusted with gems.
5.18.10
अङ्गनाशतमात्रं तु
तं व्रजन्तमनुव्रजत् ।
महेन्द्रमिव पौलस्त्यम्
देवगन्धर्वयोषितः ॥
Like the women of Dēvas and Gandharvas follow Mahēndra, a hundred damsels followed (Rāvaṇa), the descendant of Pulastya.
5.18.11
दीपिकाः काञ्चनीः काश्चित्
जगृहुस्तत्र योषितः ।
वालव्यजनहस्ताश्च
तालवृन्तानि चापराः ॥
Some of those women carried golden torches, some had fly-whisks in their hands and others, palm-leaf fans.
5.18.12
काञ्चनैरपि भृङ्गारैः
जह्रुः सलिलमग्रतः ।
मण्डलाग्रान् बृसीश्चापि
गृह्यान्याः पृष्ठतो ययुः ॥
Others walked in front carrying water in gold pitchers, while others walked behind carrying swords and small mats.
5.18.13
काचिद्रत्नमयीं स्थालीम्
पूर्णां पानस्य भामिनी ।
दक्षिणा दक्षिणेनैव
तदा जग्राह पाणिना ॥
One lovely woman carried a pitcher studded with gems that was filled with a drink in her right hand, walking on his right side.
5.18.14
राजहंसप्रतीकाशम्
छत्रं पूर्णशशिप्रभम् ।
सौवर्णदण्डमपरा
गृहीत्वा पृष्ठतो ययौ ॥
Another walked behind him holding an umbrella with a gold handle that looked like the royal swan and was bright as the full moon.
5.18.15
निद्रामदपरीताक्ष्यो
रावणस्योत्तमाः स्त्रियः ।
अनुजग्मुः पतिं वीरम्
घनं विद्युल्लता इव ॥
Their eyes still heavy with intoxication, the foremost among the women of Rāvaṇa followed their valiant husband like flashes of lightning following a cloud.
5.18.16
व्याविद्धहारकेयूराः
समामृदितवर्णकाः ।
समागलितकेशान्ताः
सस्वेदवदनास्तथा ।
घूर्णन्त्यो मदशेषेण
निद्रया च शुभाननाः ।
स्वेदक्लिष्टाङ्गकुसुमाः
सुमाल्याकुलमूर्धजाः ।
प्रयान्तं नैऋतपतिम्
नार्यो मदिरलोचनाः ।
बहुमानाच्च कामाच्च
प्रिया भार्यास्तमन्वयुः ॥
Rāvaṇa’s beloved queens, the women of intoxicating eyes, followed, with love and regard, their beloved, the lord of Rākshasas.
They were still unsteady from the lingering effect of drink and sleep, with their necklaces and armlets slipping, creams on their bodies rubbed off, hair hanging loose and garlands entangled in the tresses, faces covered with perspiration and flowers spoiled by the perspiration.
5.18.19
स च कामपराधीनः
पतिस्तासां महाबलः ।
सीतासक्तमना मन्दो
मन्दाञ्चितगतिर्बभौ ॥
Their husband, of immense strength, completely taken over by lust, his mind blunted and focused on nothing but Seetā, walked slowly, with a stylish gait.
5.18.20
ततः काञ्चीनिनादं च
नूपुराणां च निस्वनम् ।
शुश्राव परमस्त्रीणाम्
स कपिर्मारुतात्मजः ॥
Then the ape, the son of Vāyu, heard the jingling of girdles and the tinkling of anklets of those lovely women.
5.18.21
तं चाप्रतिमकर्माणम्
अचिन्त्यबलपौरुषम् ।
द्वारदेशमनुप्राप्तम्
ददर्श हनुमान् कपिः ॥
Then Hanumān, the Vānara also saw him (Rāvaṇa) whose strength and pride were beyond imagination and whose accomplishments were beyond comparison, come near the area of the entrance.
5.18.22
दीपिकाभिरनेकाभिः
समन्तादवभासितम् ।
गन्धतैलावसिक्ताभिः
ध्रियमाणाभिरग्रतः ॥
His features were brightly lit on every side by the light of the many lamps of fragrant oil that were carried in front of him.
5.18.23
कामदर्पमदैर्युक्तम्
जिह्मताम्रायतेक्षणम् ।
समक्षमिव कन्दर्पम्
अपविद्धशरासनम् ॥
Full of lust, pride and arrogance and with his curvy eyes turned red, he looked like Manmatha himself albeit with the bow cast aside.
5.18.24
मथितामृतफेनाभम्
अरजोवस्त्रमुत्तमम् ।
सलीलमनुकर्षन्तम्
विमुक्तं सक्तमङ्गदे ॥
He was pulling up, in a stylish manner, his exquisite and spotlessly clean upper garment that was as white as the froth of churned Amṛt, as it slipped off and stuck to his armlet.
Is this where Indian film makers get such ideas from?
5.18.25
तं पत्रविटपे लीनः
पत्रपुष्पघनावृतः ।
समीपमिव संक्रान्तम्
निध्यातुमुपचक्रमे ॥
Hiding on the leafy branch and covered with leaves and flowers, he felt that Rāvaṇa was coming closer, and positioned himself to watch him closely.
5.18.26
अवेक्षमाणस्तु ततो
ददर्श कपिकुञ्जरः ।
रूपयौवनसम्पन्ना
रावणस्य वरस्त्रियः ॥
Scanning around, that elephant among Vānaras saw the women of Rāvaṇa who were endowed with great beauty and youth.
5.18.27
ताभिः परिवृतो राजा
सुरूपाभिर्महायशाः ।
तन्मृगद्विजसङ्घुष्टम्
प्रविष्टः प्रमदावनम् ॥
Surrounded by those women, that charming king of great fame then entered that pleasure garden that was reverberating with the sounds of birds and beasts.
5.18.28
क्षीबो विचित्राभरणः
शङ्कुकर्णो महाबलः ।
तेन विश्रवसः पुत्रः
स दृष्टो राक्षसाधिपः ।
वृतः परमनारीभिः
ताराभिरिव चन्द्रमाः ।
तं ददर्श महातेजाः
तेजोवन्तं महाकपिः ॥
The great Vānara of supreme power saw the mighty strong and drunken lord of Rākshasas, the immensely powerful son of Viṡravasu adorned with a variety of ornaments and ears looking like conches as he was surrounded by those excellent women, like moon by the stars.
5.18.30
रावणोऽयं महाबाहुः
इति सञ्चिन्त्य वानरः ।
अवप्लुतो महातेजा
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ॥
Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, a Vānara of immense power, thought that he, of mighty arm, must be Rāvaṇa, and jumped down.
5.18.31
स तथाप्युग्रतेजाः सन्
निर्धूतस्तस्य तेजसा ।
पत्रगुह्यान्तरे सक्तो
हनुमान् संवृतोऽभवत् ॥
Even though he himself was of fierce radiance, he was taken aback by his (Rāvaṇa’s) radiance, and remained hidden covered by the leaves.
5.18.32
स तामसितकेशान्तां
सुश्रोणीं संहतस्तनीम् ।
दिदृक्षुरसितापाङ्गाम्
उपावर्तत रावणः ॥
Rāvaṇa came closer, wanting to get a sight of that woman of beautiful Kaṭee and black tresses whose breasts were closely pressing against each other and the ends of whose eye lashes were pitch black.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे अष्टादशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighteenth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
