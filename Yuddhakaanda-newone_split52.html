Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 52
In this Sarga, a tumultuous, gargantuan and fierce fight ensues between Vānaras and Rākshasas led by Dhūmrāksha. Vānaras pound Rākshasas with trees and rocks. Rākshasas tear apart Vānaras with arrows and spears. Seeing Rākshasas flee the battle, Dhūmrāksha comes down heavily on the Vānara force. Seeing the Vānara army struggle under the assault of Dhūmrāksha, Hanumān smashes his chariot with a huge rock and him with a huge tree. Dhūmrāksha collapses and the Rākshasas, fleeing, retreat into Laṅka.
6.52.1
धूम्राक्षं प्रेक्ष्य निर्यान्तम्
राक्षसं भीमविक्रमम् ।
विनेदुर्वानराः सर्वे
प्रहृष्टा युद्धकाङ्क्षिणः ॥
The Vānaras, in their eagerness to fight and letting off war whoops were only happy to see Dhūmrāksha, the Rākshasa of terrific prowess, arrive.
6.52.2
तेषां सुतुमुलं युद्धम्
सञ्जज्ञे हरिरक्षसाम् ।
अन्योन्यं पादपैर्घोरम्
निघ्नतां शूलमुद्गरैः ॥
A tumultuous fight ensued between the Vānaras and the Rākshasas as they assailed each other fiercely with trees, and with spears and Mudgaras.
6.52.3
घोरैश्च परिघैश्चित्रैः
त्रिशूलैश्चापि संहतैः ।
राक्षसैर्वानरा घोरैः
विनिकृत्ताः समन्ततः ।
वानरै राक्षसाश्चापि
द्रुमैर्भूमौ समीकृताः ॥
The Vānaras were torn apart on all sides by fierce Rākshasa bands with deadly iron bars and tridents of various kinds. And the Vānaras leveled the Rākshasas to the ground with trees.
6.52.4
राक्षसाश्चापि सङ्क्रुद्धा
वानरान्निशितैः शरैः ।
विव्यधुर्घोरसङ्काशैः
कङ्कपत्रैरजिह्मगैः ॥
The enraged Rākshasas rampaged the Vānaras with sharp and fierce arrows, that flew straight to the target propelled by the feathers of Kaṅka bird.
6.52.5
ते गदाभिश्च भीमाभिः
पट्टिशैः कूटमुद्गरैः ।
विदार्यमाणा रक्षोभिः
वानरास्ते महाबलाः ।
अमर्षाज्जनितोद्धर्षाः
चक्रुः कर्माण्यभीतवत् ॥
When their bodies were torn by the fierce maces, Paṭṭiṡas and Kūta-Mudgaras of the Rākshasas, the Vānaras of immense strength, with their wrath firing up their enthusiasm, went about their business as if they knew no fear.
6.52.7
शरनिर्भिन्नगात्रास्ते
शूलनिर्भिन्नदेहिनः ।
जगृहुस्ते द्रुमांस्तत्र
शिलाश्च हरियूथपाः ।
ते भीमवेगा हरयो
नर्दमानास्ततस्ततः ।
ममन्थू राक्षसान् भीमान्
नामानि च बभाषिरे ॥
With their limbs torn by arrows and bodies pierced by spears, the Vānara warriors of terrific speed everywhere snatched trees and rocks and shouting stridently proclaiming their names, they pounded the fierce Rākshasas.
6.52.9
तद्वभूवाद्भुतं घोरम्
युद्धं वानररक्षसाम् ।
शिलाभिर्विविधाभिश्च
बहुभिश्चैव पादपैः ॥
There ensued an awe-inspiring fight between Rākshasas and Vānaras who fought with a multitude of trees and every kind of rock.
6.52.10
राक्षसा मथिताः केचित्
वानरैर्जितकाशिभिः ।
ववमू रुधिरं केचित्
मुखै रुधिरभोजनाः ।
पार्श्वेषु दारिताः केचित्
केचिद्राशीकृता द्रुमैः ।
शिलाभिश्चूर्णिताः केचित्
केचिद्दन्तैर्विदारिताः ॥
Holding their breath tight, the Vānaras pounded the Rākshasas. Some of them, who fed on blood, vomited gore. Some had their flanks gashed, some were reduced to a shapeless mass battered by trees, some were pulverized by rocks, and some were torn by teeth.
6.52.12
ध्वजैर्विमथितैर्भग्नैः
खरैश्च विनिपातितैः ।
रथैर्विध्वंसितैश्चापि
पतितै रजनीचरैः ।
गजेन्द्रैः पर्वताकारैः
पर्वताग्रैर्वनौकसाम् ।
मथितैर्वाजिभिः कीर्णम्
सारोहैर्वसुधातलम् ॥
The grounds were littered with chariots that were destroyed, flagstaffs that were smashed and broken, donkeys that were struck down, Night-rovers that were felled, mighty elephants that resembled mountains, and horses that were squashed by the Vānaras with mountain crests.
6.52.14
वानरैर्भीमविक्रान्तैः
आप्लुत्याप्लुत्य वेगितैः ।
राक्षसाः करजैस्तीक्ष्णैः
मुखेषु विनिकर्तिताः ।
विवर्णवदना भूयो
विप्रकीर्णशिरोरुहाः ।
मूढाः शोणितगन्धेन
निपेतुर्धरणीतले ॥
Vānaras fierce in their attack jumped on Rākshasas again and again and with sharp nails clawed their faces. The Rākshasas fell on the ground fainting under the overwhelming smell of blood, their faces turning pale and their hair disheveled.
6.52.16
अन्ये परमसङ्क्रुद्धा
राक्षसा भीमनिस्वनाः ।
तलैरेवाभिधावन्ति
वज्रस्पर्शसमैर्हरीन् ॥
Some Rākshasas rushed upon the Vānaras in a frenzy of fury with frightful yells, attacking them with their open palms, which fell with the force of the thunderbolt.
6.52.17
वानरैरापतन्तस्ते
वेगिता वेगवत्तरैः ।
मुष्टिभिश्चरणैर्दन्तैः
पादपैश्चावपोथिताः ॥
As they swooped down on them swiftly, the Vānaras, who were even more swift, smashed them with their clenched fists, their feet, their nails and trees.
6.52.18
वानरैर्हन्यमानास्ते
राक्षसा विप्रदुद्रुवुः ।
सैन्यं तु विद्रुतं दृष्ट्वा
धूम्राक्षो राक्षसर्षभः ।
क्रोधेन कदनं चक्रे
वानराणां युयुत्सताम् ॥
Beaten up by the Vānaras, the Rākshasas fled. Seeing his army fleeing, Dhūmrāksha, a bull among Rākshasas, in his rage, caused great havoc on the Vānaras who were intent on fighting.
6.52.19
प्रासैः प्रमथिताः केचित्
वानराः शोणितस्रवाः ।
मुद्गरैराहताः केचित्
पतिता धरणीतले ॥
Some Vānaras were pounded with Prāsas and blood poured out of them. Some fell on the ground struck with Mudgaras.
6.52.20
परिघैर्मथिताः केचित्
भिन्दिपालैर्विदारिताः ।
पट्टिशैराहताः केचित्
विह्वलन्तो गतासवः ॥
Some were beaten up with Parighas, some were torn up by Bhindipālas and some fell dead whirling, hit by Paṭṭiṡas.
6.52.21
केचिद्विनिहताः शूलै
रुधिरार्द्रा वनौकसः ।
केचिद्विद्राविता नष्टाः
सबलै राक्षसैर्युधि ॥
Some Vana-dwellers, struck by spears were drenched with blood. Some were lost without a trace, chased by the strong Rākshasas.
6.52.22
विभिन्नहृदयाः केचित्
एकपार्श्वेन दारिताः ।
विदारिता स्त्रिशूलैश्च
केचिदान्त्रैर्विनिःसृताः ॥
Some were cleft to the heart, others had one flank sheared down. Some ripped up by tridents, were disemboweled.
6.52.23
तत्सुभीमं महायुद्धम्
हरिराक्षससङ्कुलम् ।
प्रबभौ शब्दबहुलम्
शिलापादपसङ्कुलम् ॥
That gargantuan, tumultuous and fierce fight between Vānaras and the Rākshasas, with trees and rocks thrown everywhere, raised a terrific din.
6.52.24
धनुर्ज्यातन्त्रिमधुरम्
हिक्कातालसमन्वितम् ।
मन्दस्तनितसङ्गीतम्
युद्धगान्धर्वमाबभौ ॥
With the twang of bowstrings sounding like the melody of string instruments, the hiccups keeping rhythm, and the dying-down voices seeming to sing a song, the scene of the war was like a music concert.
6.52.25
धूम्राक्षस्तु धनुष्पाणिः
वानरान् रणमूर्धनि ।
हसन् विद्रावयामास
दिशस्तु शरवृष्टिभिः ॥
Dhūmrāksha, standing with the bow in his hand on the forefront of the battle field, scattered the Vānaras in all directions with a downpour of arrows, laughing.
6.52.26
धूम्राक्षेणार्दितं सैन्यम्
व्यथितं दृश्य मारुतिः ।
अभ्यवर्तत सङ्क्रुद्धः
प्रगृह्य विपुलां शिलाम् ॥
Māruti, outraged on seeing the army downcast battered by Dhūmrāksha, pounced upon him, snatching a huge rock.
6.52.27
क्रोधाद्द्विगुणताम्राक्षः
पितृतुल्यपराक्रमः ।
शिलां तां पातयामास
धूम्राक्षस्य रथं प्रति ॥
The natural redness of his eyes doubled by anger, he who equaled his father in prowess, hurled that rock at the chariot of Dhūmrāksha.
6.52.28
आपतन्तीं शिलां दृष्ट्वा
गदामुद्यम्य सम्भ्रमात् ।
रथादाप्लुत्य वेगेन
वसुधायां व्यतिष्ठत ॥
Seeing the rock flying towards him, he grabbed the mace in a hurry and jumped down from the chariot, and took his stand on the ground.
6.52.29
सा प्रमथ्य रथं तस्य
निपपात शिला भुवि ।
सचक्रकूबरं साश्वम्
सध्वजं सशरासनम् ॥
Smashing the chariot including its wheels, yoke, horses, flagstaff and bows, the rock fell to the ground.
6.52.30
स भङ्क्त्वा तु रथं तस्य
हनुमान् मारुतात्मजः ।
रक्षसां कदनं चक्रे
सस्कन्धविटपैर्द्रुमैः ॥
Having thus smashed the chariot, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, worked havoc among the Rākshasas with whole trees, with their trunks and boughs.
6.52.31
विभिन्नशिरसो भूत्वा
राक्षसाः शोणितोक्षिताः ।
द्रुमैः प्रव्यथिताश्चान्ये
निपेतुर्धरणीतले ॥
The Rākshasas, some with their heads smashed and their bodies covered with blood, others in agony from the blows of the trees, fell down on the ground.
6.52.32
विद्राव्य रासक्षं सैन्यम्
हनुमान् मारुतात्मजः ।
गिरेः शिखरमादाय
धूम्राक्षमभिदुद्रुवे ॥
Having put to flight the Rākshasa army, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, snatched a mountain-crest and rushed upon Dhūmrāksha.
6.52.33
तमापतन्तं धूम्राक्षो
गदामुद्यम्य वीर्यवान् ।
विनर्दमानः सहसा
हनुमन्तमभिद्रवत् ॥
Dhūmrāksha, the valiant one, with uplifted mace dashed immediately, roaring, towards Hanumān who was thus rushing towards him.
6.52.34
ततः क्रुद्धस्तु वेगेन
गदां तां बहुकण्टकाम् ।
पातयामास धूम्राक्षो
मस्तके तु हनूमतः ॥
Dhūmrāksha, in all his rage and with all his force, lodged the many-spiked mace on Hanumān’s head.
6.52.35
ताडितः स तया तत्र
गदया भीमरूपया ।
स कपिर्मारुतबलः
तं प्रहारमचिन्तयन् ।
धूम्राक्षस्य शिरोमध्ये
गिरिशृङ्गमपातयत् ॥
Completely unmindful of the blow from that frightful mace the Vānara, who was as strong as the wind, lodged the mountain-crest right on the center Dhūmrāksha’s head.
6.52.37
स विह्वलितसर्वाङ्गो
गिरिशृङ्गेण ताडितः ।
पपात सहसा भूमौ
विकीर्ण इव पर्वतः ॥
Struck down by that mountain crest, he collapsed instantly, all his limbs rocking, on the ground like a mountain that had been blown apart.
6.52.38
धूम्राक्षं निहतं दृष्ट्वा
हतशेषा निशाचराः ।
त्रस्ताः प्रविविशुर्लङ्काम्
वध्यमानाः प्लवङ्गमैः ॥
On seeing Dhūmrāksha slain, the Rākshasas who were still alive retreated to Laṅkā terrified, chased to death by the Vānaras.
6.52.39
स तु पवनसुतो निहत्य शत्रून्
क्षतजवहाः सरितश्च सन्निकीर्य ।
रिपुवधजनितश्रमो महात्मा
मुदमगमत्कपिभिः सुपूज्यमानः ॥
Having slain the foe and causing rivers of blood to flow, the mighty son of Vāyu, who was exhausted expending his energies in killing the enemy, found elation in the plaudits from the Vānaras.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्विपञ्चाशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty second Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
