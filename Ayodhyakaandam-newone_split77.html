Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 77
In this Sarga, Bharata, for the well being of his father who had attained an ethereal body, honors Brāhmaṇas with copious amounts of money, food, clothing and other things.
Bharata and Ṡatrughna go to the funeral pyre on the thirteenth day, and seeing no traces of the body left except for a few bones, cry bitterly.
Vasishṭha and Sumantra console them and the officials hasten them to complete the remaining ceremonies.
2.77.1
ततो दशाहेऽतिगते
कृतशौचो नृपात्मजः ।
द्वादशेऽहनि सम्प्राप्ते
श्राद्धकर्माण्यकारयत् ॥
After the passing of ten days the prince ceremonially purified himself, and performed Ṡrāddha ceremonies on the twelfth day.
2.77.2
ब्राह्मणेभ्यो ददौ रत्नम्
धनमन्नं च पुष्कलम् ।
वासांसि च महार्हाणि
रत्नानि विविधानि च ॥
(On that occasion) he honored Brāhmaṇas with copious amounts of food grains and money, expensive clothings, precious stones, and a variety of exquisite gems.
2.77.3
बास्तिकं बहु शुक्लं च
गाश्चापि शतशस्तथा ।
दासीदासं च यानं च
वेश्मानि सुमहान्ति च ।
ब्राह्मणेभ्यो ददौ पुत्रो
राज्ञस्तस्यौर्ध्वदैहिकम् ॥
The son gave to Brāhmaṇas herds of white goats, hundreds of cows, servants and servant-maids, vehicles and big houses on this occasion of the king assuming an ethereal body of the upper worlds.
2.77.4
ततः प्रभातसमये
दिवसेऽथ त्रयोदशे ।
विललाप महाबाहुः
भरतश्शोकमूर्छितः ।
शब्दापिहितकण्ठस्तु
शोधनार्थमुपागतः ।
चितामूले पितुर्वाक्यम्
इदमाह सुदुःखितः ॥
At dawn on the thirteenth day, When he arrived at the funeral pyre of his father to examine the remnants, Bharata of mighty arm wept aloud, overwhelmed by grief and said these words with extreme sadness:
2.77.6
तात यस्मिन्निसृष्टोऽहम्
त्वया भ्रातरि राघवे ।
तस्मिन्वनं प्रव्रजिते
शून्ये त्यक्तोऽस्म्यहं त्वया ॥
O father, you had put me in the hands of Rāghava. With him leaving for the Vana, you have now left me in a vacuum.
2.77.7
यस्या गतिरनाथायाः
पुत्रः प्रवाजितो वनम् ।
तामम्बां तात कौसल्याम्
त्यक्त्वा त्वं क्व गतो नृप ॥
O father! O King! Where have you gone, abandoning mother Kousalyā, who is now like an orphan, with her son, her sole hope, having been exiled to the forest?
2.77.8
दृष्ट्वा भस्मारुणं तच्च
दग्धास्थि स्थानमण्डलम् ।
पितुश्शरीरनिर्वाणम्
निष्टनन्विषसाद सः ॥
Seeing that funeral place red with hot ash and with no traces of his father's body left except for the remaining burnt bones, he cried at the height of his voice.
2.77.9
स तु दृष्ट्वा रुदन् दीनः
पपात धरणीतले ।
उत्थाप्यमानश्शक्रस्य
यन्त्रध्वज इव च्युतः ॥
Seeing that, he cried in his extreme dejection and fell down on the ground like Indra's flagstaff whose fasteners have been removed.
2.77.10
अभिपेतुस्ततस्सर्वे
तस्यामात्याश्शुचिव्रतम् ।
अन्तकाले निपतितम्
ययातिमृषयो यथा ॥
Then all his ministers ran up to him who kept to his vows of purity, like the sages to Yayāti did, as he fell when his time (in Heaven) was up.
2.77.11
शत्रुघ्न श्चापि भरतम्
दृष्ट्वा शोकम् परिप्लुतः ।
विसंज्ञो न्यपतद्भूमौ
भूमिपालमनुस्मरन् ॥
Ṡatrughna also, seeing Bharata, was overcome with grief thinking of the King and fell on the ground senseless.
2.77.12
उन्मत्त इव निश्चेता
विललाप सुदुःखितः ।
स्मृत्वा पितुर्गुणाङ्गानि
तानि तानि तथा तथा ॥
Like a lunatic who is out of his mind, he cried with extreme grief recalling the many virtues of his father that were well known.
2.77.13
मन्थराप्रभवस्तीव्रः
कैकेयीग्राहसङ्कुलः ।
वरदानमयोऽक्षोभ्योऽ -
मञ्जयच्छोकसागरः ॥
This grave and unshakable ocean of grief, started by Manthara and agitated by the crocodile that is Kaikēyee, submerged everyone in the form of irrevocable boons.
2.77.14
सुकुमारं च बालं च
सततं लालितं त्वया ।
क्व तात भरतं हित्वा
विलपन्तं गतो भवान् ॥
Where have you gone, O father, leaving the young Bharata weeping, whom you have brought up so fondly?
2.77.15
ननु भोज्येषु पानेषु
वस्त्रेष्वाभरणेषु च ।
प्रवारयसि नस्सर्वान्
तन्नः कोऽन्यः करिष्यति ॥
Did you not delight in pressing upon us food and drink, clothes and ornaments, who would do that for us now?
2.77.16
अवदारणकाले तु
पृथिवी नावदीर्यते ।
या विहीना त्वया राज्ञा
धर्मज्ञेन महात्मना ॥
Why did the earth, which is bereaved of you, the King, the Mahatma and the knower of Dharma, not shatter into pieces, as it should have?
2.77.17
पितरि स्वर्गमापन्ने
रामे चारण्यमाश्रिते ।
किं मे जीवितसामर्थ्यम्
प्रवेक्ष्यामि हुताशनम् ॥
With father gone to Heaven and Rāma taken to the forest, how can I possibly go on living? I shall consign myself to the fire.
2.77.18
हीनो भ्रात्रा च पित्रा च
शून्यामिक्ष्वाकुपालिताम् ।
अयोध्यां न प्रवेक्ष्यामि
प्रवेक्ष्यामि तपोवनम् ॥
With no brothers and father, I shall not enter the empty Ayōdhyā, the City that Ikshwākus ruled. Rather, I shall go to a Vana that is suitable for Tapa.
2.77.19
तयोर्विलपितं श्रुत्वा
व्यसनं चान्ववेक्ष्य तत् ।
भृशमार्ततरा भूयः
सर्वएवानुगामिनः ॥
Hearing their cries and seeing their distress, everyone who followed them felt very sad again.
2.77.20
ततो विषण्णौ श्रान्तौ च
शत्रुघ्नभरतावुभौ ।
धरण्यां संव्यवेष्टेताम्
भग्नशृङ्गाविवर्षभौ ॥
Both Ṡatrughna and Bharata, dejected and weary, rolled on the ground like bulls whose horns were broken.
2.77.21
ततः प्रकृतिमान्वैद्यः
पितुरेषां पुरोहितः ।
वसिष्ठो भरतं वाक्यम्
उत्थाप्य तमुवाच ह ॥
Thereupon, Vasishṭha of character and knowledge par excellence, the Purōhita of their father, raised up Bharata and said to him:
2.77.22
त्रयोदशोऽयं दिवसः
पितुर्वृत्तस्य ते विभो ।
सावशेषास्थिनिचये
किमिह त्वं विलम्भसे ॥
My lord, it is the thirteenth day after the death of your father. Why are you delaying like this here, when you should be collecting the remaining bones?
2.77.23
त्रीणि द्वन्द्वानि भूतेषु
प्रवृत्तान्यविशेषतः ।
तेषु चापरिहार्येषु
नैवं भवितुमर्हसि ॥
The three duos prevail over all creatures without exception; these being irremediable, you must not grieve thus!
The three duos are: Hunger and thirst, pain and pleasure and old age and death.
2.77.24
सुमन्त्रश्चापि शत्रुघ्नम्
उत्थाप्याभिप्रसाद्य च ।
श्रावयामास तत्त्वज्ञः
सर्वभूतभवाभवम् ॥
Sumantra, too, raised up Ṡatrughna and comforted him speaking on how all beings emerge and disappear, being the philosopher that he was.
2.77.25
उत्थितौ च नरव्याघ्रौ
प्रकाशेते यशस्विनौ ।
वर्षातपपरिक्लिनौ
पृथगिन्द्रध्वजाविव ॥
The two tigers among men, of great renown, who rose up to their feet, looked like the flagstaffs of Indra beaten by sun and rain.
2.77.26
अश्रूणि परिमृद्नन्तौ
रक्ताक्षौ दीनभाषिणौ ।
अमात्यास्त्वरयन्ति स्म
तनयौ चापराः क्रियाः ॥
The officials hastened the sons, who were wiping the tears from their bloodshot eyes and were speaking thus pitifully, to get on with the rest of the ceremonies.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे सप्तसप्ततितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventy seventh Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
