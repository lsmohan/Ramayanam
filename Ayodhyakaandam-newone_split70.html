Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 70
In this Sarga, the messengers reach Bharata. They give him the gifts they brought with them. Bharata, in turn, honors them with many gifts. Bharata passes on the gifts he received from the messengers to his grandfather and uncle. His grandfather and uncle give him gifts in turn. But he is in no mood to enjoy them.
He immediately, along with Ṡatrughna, starts on his journey to Ayōdhyā.
2.70.1
भरते ब्रुवति स्वप्नम्
दूतास्ते क्लान्तवाहनाः ।
प्रविश्यासह्यपरिखम्
रम्यं राजगृहं पुरम् ॥
While Bharata was relating his dream, the messengers on their exhausted mounts entered the impregnable and magnificent city of Rājagṛha.
2.70.2
समागम्य तु राज्ञा च
राजपुत्रेण चार्चिताः ।
राज्ञः पादौ गृहीत्वा तु
तमूचुर्भरतं वचः ॥
They waited on the King and his son. Received by them with all courtesy, they bowed at the feet of the King. Then they said to Bharata:
2.70.3
पुरोहितस्त्वां कुशलम्
प्राह सर्वे च मन्त्रिणः ।
त्वरमाणश्च निर्याहि
कृत्यमात्ययिकं त्वया ॥
The Purōhita and all the ministers wish you well. Please start in all haste, business that brooks no delay awaits you.
2.70.4
इमानि च महार्हाणि
वस्त्राण्याभरणानि च ।
प्रतिगृह्य विशालाक्ष
मातुलस्य च दापय ॥
Take these priceless garments and ornaments, O wide-eyed one, and give them to your uncle.
2.70.5
अत्र विशंतिकोट्यस्तु
नृपतेर्मातुलस्य ते ।
दशकोट्यस्तु सम्पूर्णाः
तथैव च नृपात्मज ॥
O prince! Of these, twenty crores worth are intended for the King, and ten crores worth for your uncle.
2.70.6
प्रतिगृह्य तु तत्सर्वम्
स्वनुरक्त स्सुहृज्जने ।
दूतानुवाच भरतः
कामैस्सम्प्रतिपूज्य तान् ॥
Bharata, who loved his near and dear, accepted it all. Bharata, likewise pleased the messengers with all they could wish for, and asked them:
2.70.7
कच्चित्सुकुशली राजा
पिता दशरथो मम ।
कच्चिच्चारोगता रामे
लक्ष्मणे च महात्मनि ॥
Is the King Daṡaratha, my father, well? Are Rāma and the Mahātma Lakshmaṇa in good health?
2.70.8
आर्या च धर्मनिरता
धर्मज्ञा धर्मदर्शिनी ।
अरोगा चापि कौसल्या
माता रामस्य धीमतः ॥
Is the mother of the sagacious Rāma, the noble Kousalyā, who knows Dharma, sees Dharma and is devoted to Dharma, in the enjoyment of good health?
2.70.9
कच्चित्सुमित्रा धर्मज्ञा
जननी लक्ष्मणस्य या ।
शत्रुघ्नस्य च वीरस्य
साऽरोगा चापि मध्यमा ॥
Is Sumitrā, who is well-versed in Dharma, the mother of Lakshmaṇa and the heroic Ṡatrughna, the middle one (of the mothers), in good health?
2.70.10
आत्मकामा सदा चण्डी
क्रोधना प्राज्ञमानिनी ।
अरोगा चापि मे माता
कैकेयी किमुवाच ह ॥
And is my self-absorbed mother Kaikēyee, the difficult one who gets easily angered and and full of conceit, well? What did she say?
Vālmeeki waited this long, for the right opportunity, to complete the caricature of Kaikēyee, by describing her through the words of her own son.
2.70.11
एवमुक्तास्तु ते दूताः
भरतेन महात्मना ।
ऊचुस्सप्रश्रयं वाक्यम्
इदं तं भरतं तदा ॥
Thus asked by Mahātma Bharata, the messengers responded to him deferentially:
2.70.12
कुशलास्ते नरव्याघ्र
येषां कुशलमिच्छसि ।
श्रीश्च त्वां वृणुते पद्मा
युज्यतां चापि ते रथः ॥
All of them, O tiger among men, are well, of whom you wish well. The Goddess of Fortune with lotus in her hand seeks you. Let your chariot be yoked!
2.70.13
भरतश्चापि तान् दूतान्
एवमुक्तोऽभ्यभाषत ।
आपृच्चेऽहं महाराजम्
दूतास्सन्त्वरयन्ति माम् ॥
Thus told, Bharata responded to them: "I will let the King know that the messengers are hastening me".
2.70.14
एवमुक्त्वा तु तान् दूतान्
भरतः पार्थिवात्मजः ।
दूतै स्सञ्चोदितो वाक्यम्
मातामहमुवाच ह ॥
The prince Bharata, thus urged by those messengers, said to his grandfather:
2.70.15
राजन् पितुर्गमिष्यामि
सकाशं दूतचोदितः ।
पुनरप्यहमेष्यामि
यदा मे त्वं स्मरिष्यसि ॥
I must go to my father, O King, being urged by the messengers to do so. But I shall come again, whenever you think of me!
2.70.16
भरतेनैवमुक्तस्तु
नृपो मातामहस्तदा ।
तमुवाच शुभं वाक्यम्
शिरस्याघ्राय राघवम् ॥
Thus told by Bharata, the King, his grandfather, smelled his head and spoke these pleasing words to that scion of the Raghu dynasty:
2.70.17
गच्छ तातानुजाने त्वाम्
कैकेयीसुप्रजास्त्वया ।
मातरं कुशलं ब्रूयाः
पितरं च परन्तप ॥
My child, Kaikēyee has a good son in you. Please go, O scorcher of foes, you have my permission! Give my greetings and good wishes to your mother as well as to your father!
2.70.18
पुरोहितं च कुशलम्
ये चान्ये द्विजसत्तमाः ।
तौ च तात महेष्वासौ
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
Convey greetings also to the Purōhita and to all the other great Brāhmaṇas, as well as to the duo of brothers, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, the mighty archers!
2.70.19
तस्मै हस्त्युत्तमांश्चित्रान्
कम्बलानजिनानि च ।
अभिसत्कृत्य कैकेयो
भरताय धनं ददौ ॥
Then the King of Kēkaya honored him with well-bred elephants, beautiful carpets and deer skins and gave him plenty of money.
2.70.20
रुक्मनिष्कसहस्रे द्वे
षोडशाश्वशतानि च ।
सत्कृत्य कैकयीपुत्रम्
केकयो धनमादिशत् ॥
The King of Kēkaya ordered two thousand gold pieces and sixteen hundred horses to be given to the son of Kaikēyee, with all honor.
2.70.21
तथाऽमात्यानभिप्रेतान्
विश्वास्यांश्च गुणान्वितान् ।
ददावश्वपतिः क्षिप्रम्
भरतायानुयायिनः ॥
And Aṡwapati deputed forthwith his favorite officials, who were men of virtue and trustworthy, to accompany Bharata.
2.70.22
ऐरावतानैन्द्रशिरान्
नागान्वै प्रियदर्शनान् ।
खरान् श्रीघ्रान्सुसंयुक्तान्
मातुलोऽस्मै धनं ददौ ॥
His uncle bestowed upon him as presents elephants of pleasing appearance from the lineage of Airāvata born on Indraṡira mountains, and fleet mules broken to the saddle.
2.70.23
अन्तःपुरेऽति संवृद्धान्
व्याघ्रवीर्यबलान्वितान् ।
दंष्ट्राऽऽयुधान्महाकायान्
शुनश्चोपायनं ददौ ॥
He also gifted him several huge canines that bore the strength and power of tigers, which had been bred and raised with great care in the inner quarters.
2.70.24
स दत्तं केकयेन्द्रेण
धनं तन्नाभ्यनन्दत ।
भरतः कैकयीपुत्रो
गमनत्वरया तदा ॥
However, Bharata, the son of Kaikēyee, Could not enjoy any of those gifts given by the King of Kēkaya, as he was in all anxiety to leave.
2.70.25
बभूव ह्यस्य हृदये
चिन्ता सुमहती तदा ।
त्वरया चापि दूतानाम्
स्वप्नस्यापि च दर्शनात् ॥
His mind, indeed, was preoccupied with great anxiety, born out of the omens in his dreams and the urgency bid on him by the messengers.
2.70.26
स स्ववेश्माभ्यतिक्रम्य
नरनागाश्वसंवृतम् ।
प्रपेदे सुमहच्छ्रीमान्
राजमार्गमनुत्तमम् ॥
Then, after leaving his residence that was full of people, elephants and horses, that man of great fortunes came on to the splendid and unparalleled royal highway.
2.70.27
अभ्यतीत्य ततोऽपश्यत्
अन्तःपुरमुदारधीः ।
ततस्तद्भरतश्श्रीमान्
आविवेशानिवारितः ॥
The noble minded Bharata, after crossing it, headed to the inner quarters and entered it unchecked.
2.70.28
स मातामहमापृच्छ्य
मातुलं च युधाजितम् ।
रथमारुह्य भरतः
शत्रुघ्नसहितो ययौ ॥
After taking leave from his grandfather as well as from his uncle Yudhājit, he boarded the chariot and left, along with Ṡatrughna.
2.70.29
रथान्मण्डल चक्रांश्च
योजयित्वा परश्शतम् ।
उष्ट्र गोऽश्वबलैर्भृत्या
भरतं यान्तमन्वयुः ॥
And his staff, yoking camels, oxen, horses and mules, to more than a hundred chariots that were studded with a variety of gems followed Bharata.
2.70.30
बलेन गुप्तो भरतो महात्मा
सहार्यकस्याऽत्मसमैरमात्यैः ।
आदाय शत्रुघ्नमपेतशत्रु-
र्गृहाद्ययौ सिद्ध इवेन्द्रलोकात् ॥
Mahatma Bharata, whom no enemy dared to confront, started from the house of his grandfather like an accomplished person from the world of Indra, taking Ṡatrughna along with him, guarded by the army, and accompanied by the ministers who were comparable to him!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे सप्ततितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventieth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
