Baala Kaanda - Sarga 32
In this Sarga, Viṡwāmitra starts telling the story of Kuṡa, his sons and the hundred daughters of one of those sons, who were turned into hunchbacks by the God of Wind. This Sarga leaves us in a mood of helplessness that befalls all of a sudden.
1.32.1
ब्रह्मयोनिर्महानासीत्
कुशो नाम महातपाः ।
अक्लिष्टव्रतधर्मज्ञः
सज्जनप्रतिपूजकः ॥
Once upon a time, there was a king by name Kuṡa. He had done great Tapa. He was the offspring of Brahma. He was very obedient and respectful to the deserving. He was a savant of Dharma. He had performed demanding Vratas with ease.
1.32.2
स महात्मा कुलीनायाम्
युक्तायां सुगुणोल्बणान् ।
वैदर्भ्यां जनयामास
सदृशांश्चतुरः सुतान् ।
कुशाम्बं कुशनाभं च
अधूर्तरजसं वसुम् ॥
That Mahātma, with the lovable princess of Vidarbha who came from a great lineage, had four sons Kuṡāmba, Kuṡanābha, Adhūrtarajasa and Vasu who were cultured and virtuous just as he was.
1.32.3
दीप्तियुक्तान् महोत्साहान्
क्षत्रधर्मचिकीर्षया ।
तानुवाच कुशः पुत्रान्
धर्मिष्ठान् सत्यवादिनः ।
क्रियतां पालनं पुत्रा
धर्मं प्राप्स्यथ पुष्कलम् ॥
Kuṡa, being zealous of providing good governance, said to those enthusiastic, energetic, ethical and righteous sons: Please govern well to bring order and promote righteousness!
1.32.5
ऋषेस्तु वचनं श्रुत्वा
चत्वारो लोकसम्मताः ।
निवेशं चक्रिरे सर्वे
पुराणां नृवरास्तदा ॥
In accordance with those words of that Ṛshi Those four sons, the best among men, who were regarded highly by all the peoples, built four cities.
There are three types of kings:
1. Those who understand nothing but the 'power' aspect of being a king. 2. Those who provide the governance, just as needed. 3. Those who envision the future needs and prepare the kingdom for it.
The word Ṛshi is used for kings of the third type. The generic meaning of the word Ṛshi is: Visionary and Architect. Anyone who can envision the future at a broad level and can craft a system to meet its needs can be called a Ṛshi.
1.32.6
कुशाम्बस्तु महातेजाः
कौशाम्बीमकरोत्पुरीम् ।
कुशनाभस्तु धर्मात्मा
पुरं चक्रे महोदयम् ।
आधूर्तरजसो राम
धर्मारण्यं महीपतिः ।
चक्रे पुरवरं राजा
वसुश्चक्रे गिरिव्रजम् ॥
The dynamic Kuṡāmba built a city called Kouṡāmbi. The Dharmātma Kuṡanābha built a city called Mahōdaya. The able ruler Adhūrtarajasa built the city of Dharmāraṇya. The king Vasu built the city of Girivraja.
1.32.8
एषा वसुमती राम
वसोस्तस्य महात्मनः ।
एते शैलवराः पञ्च
प्रकाशन्ते समन्ततः ॥
O Rāma, This place belongs to that Mahātma Vasu. It is surrounded by five lofty mountains.
1.32.9
सुमागधी नदी रम्या
मगधान् विश्रुताऽययौ ।
पञ्चानां शैलमुख्यानाम्
मध्ये मालेव शोभते ॥
This famous and lovely river, Sumāgadhi, meanders through these five prominent mountains like a garland and reaches the land of Magadha.
1.32.10
सैषा हि मागधी राम
वसोस्तस्य महात्मनः ।
पूर्वाभिचरिता राम
सुक्षेत्रा सस्यमालिनी ॥
O Rāma! The stretch of this river that flows from east to west with rich and green fields adorning its path belongs to that Vasu.
1.32.11
कुशनाभस्तु राजर्षिः
कन्याशतमनुत्तमम् ।
जनयामास धर्मात्मा
घृताच्यां रघुनन्दन ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! The Rājarshi, the Dharmātma Kuṡanābha had one hundred unequalled daughters through Ghṛtāci.
A King worth being called a Ṛshi is referred to as Rājarshi.
Ghṛtāci is another Apsara like Rambha and Ūrvasi.
1.32.12
तास्तु यौवनशालिन्यो
रूपवत्यः स्वलङ्कृताः ।
उद्यानभूमिमागम्य
प्रावृषीव शतह्रदाः ।
गायन्त्यो नृत्यमानाश्च
वादयन्त्यश्च सर्वशः ।
आमोदं परमं जग्मुः
वराभरणभूषिताः ॥
Those beautiful and charming young girls, dazzlingly decorated with every fashionable ornament, one day went into a lovely garden, shining like fine lines of lightning against the drizzles of monsoon, singing, dancing, bantering and playing, enjoying themselves in merriment beyond words.
Ponder for a moment, to visualize this scene. You cannot get enough of a glimpse of those beautiful girls, as they keep moving among the trees and plants and groves of the garden, just as we cannot get enough of the view of lightning in the sky filled with clouds. With descriptions like this, Vālmeeki celebrates the pinnacle of human experiences and touches the gentle, subtle and otherwise unreachable parts of our hearts.
1.32.14
अथ ताश्चारुसर्वाङ्ग्यो
रूपेणाप्रतिमा भुवि ।
उद्यानभूमिमागम्य
तारा इव घनान्तरे ॥
Those beautiful girls, their every curve exquisitely chiseled, unequaled in the entire earth were brilliant in that garden like stars on a cloudy night.
1.32.15
ताः सर्वगुणसम्पन्ना
रूपयौवनसंयुताः ।
दृष्ट्वा सर्वात्मको वायुः
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing those beautiful, young and lovely girls, the God of Wind, who permeates everything, said:
The scene was so enchanting. The God of Wind was already touching their every curve. Yet, he could not have enough of it.
He was making a tickling proposal to all of them by promising eternal youth.
1.32.16
अहं वः कामये सर्वा
भार्या मम भविष्यथ ।
मानुषस्त्यज्यतां भावो
दीर्घमायुरवाप्स्यथ ॥
I would like to have all of you as my wives. May you all rise beyond the confines of mortality and live for a very long time with me!
1.32.17
चलं हि यौवनं नित्यम्
मानुषेषु विशेषतः ।
अक्षयं यौवनं प्राप्ता
अमर्यश्च भविष्यथ ॥
Youth can only be seen in passing and is fleeting in humans. Enjoy endless youth and become immortal with me!
1.32.18
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
वायोरक्लिष्टकर्मणः ।
अपहास्य ततो वाक्यम्
कन्याशतमथाब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those wooing words of the God of Wind, those one hundred girls laughed and said:
1.32.19
अन्तश्चरसि भूतानाम्
सर्वेषां त्वं सुरोत्तम ।
प्रभावज्ञाः स्म ते सर्वाः
किमस्मानवमन्यसे ॥
We know about your free spirit that pervades every being that exists. We know your wonderful abilities. But why do you insult us?
The insult here is that the God of Wind took it for granted that they could be wooed with enticements.
1.32.20
कुशनाभसुताः सर्वाः
समर्थास्त्वां सुरोत्तम ।
स्थानाच्च्यावयितुं देवम्
रक्षामस्तु तपो वयम् ॥
O foremost of the Dēvas! All of us are the daughters of Kuṡanābha. With our powers of Tapa, we can easily depose you from your exalted position and privilege. But we do not feel like wasting it on you.
1.32.21
माभूत्स कालो दुर्मेधः
पितरं सत्यवादिनम् ।
नावमन्यस्व धर्मेण
स्वयं वरमुपास्महे ॥
O pinhead! Let that time never come, when we would insult our great, truthful father by going our own way to find a groom!
1.32.22
पिता हि प्रभुरस्माकम्
दैवतं परमं हि नः ।
यस्य नो दास्यति पिता
स नो भर्ता भविष्यति ॥
Our father is our lord and he is the ultimate god for us. Whoever he gives us to in matrimony will be our husband.
1.32.23
तासां तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
वायुः परमकोपनः ।
प्रविश्य सर्वगात्राणि
बभञ्ज भगवान् प्रभुः ॥
Hearing those words, the God of Wind felt enraged and entered each limb and organ of all of them, making them incapacitated.
1.32.24
ताः कन्याः वायुना भग्ना
विविशुर्नृपतेर्गृहम् ।
प्रापतन् भुवि सम्भ्रान्ताः
सलज्जाः साश्रुलोचनाः ॥
Thus incapacitated, the girls returned to the king's palace and fell on the floor with shame, disorientation and eyes filled with tears.
1.32.25
स च ता दयिता दीनाः
कन्याः परमशोभनाः ।
दृष्ट्वा भग्नास्तदा राजा
सम्भ्रान्त इदमब्रवीत् ॥
The king, troubled and perturbed on seeing his beautiful and cheerful girls fall like that, incapacitated, lost, bent and bowed, said:
1.32.26
किमिदं कथ्यतां पुत्र्यः
को धर्ममवमन्यते ।
कुब्जाः केन कृताः सर्वा
वेष्टन्त्यो नाभिभाषथ ।
एवं राजा विनिश्वस्य
समाधिं सन्दधे ततः ॥
What happened? Who is offending Dharma? Who made you hunchbacks like this? Why are you rolling on the ground like this without saying a word? Asking them thus, taking a deep breath, the king waited to hear from them.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे द्वात्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty second Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
