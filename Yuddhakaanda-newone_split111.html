Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 111
In this Sarga, Manḍōdaree comes to the battlefield and wails over her slain husband. She laments pitiably, recalling everything, from Rāvaṇa underestimating the power of Rāma when he killed Khara and his fourteen thousand Rākshasas, to his not heeding the advice of Māreeca, to his committing the despicable act of abducting Seetā through deception and force, to his ignoring the destruction wrought by Hanumān singlehandedly, to his not heeding the advice of Vibheeshaṇa, and even her own.
She laments how her husband, who once subjugated all the three worlds and was invincible even to the Dēvas, now lies in the battlefield covered in dust, his fortunes turned against him. She laments how Rāvaṇa, driven by fate, became blind to the beauty of his own women and sought Seetā, a chaste woman devoted to her husband and how the curses of all those women he harassed caused his death and the destruction of all the Rākshasas. She laments her fate of having to spend the last part of her life without a protector and without the comforts and luxuries that she had been used to.
Rāma asks Vibheeshaṇa to send away the women folk and perform the final rites for Rāvaṇa. Vibheeshaṇa is reluctant to perform the rites for someone who had harassed the worlds, committed evil acts and coveted other men's wives. Rāma convinces him that, in spite of all those said vices, Rāvaṇa was a warrior of high order, who had never lost a single battle till then, and hence deserved a customary funeral. In deference to those words of Rāma, Vibheeshaṇa performs the final rites for Rāvaṇa, his slain brother.
6.111.1
तासां विलपमानानाम्
तथा राक्षसयोषिताम् ।
ज्येष्ठा पत्नी प्रिया दीना
भर्तारं समुदैक्षत ॥
While the Rākshasa women were thus wailing, the eldest and dearest wife among them all, looked long at her lord, woebegone.
6.111.2
दशग्रीवं हतं दृष्ट्वा
रामेणाचिन्त्यकर्मणा ।
पतिं मण्डोदरी तत्र
कृपणा पर्यदेवयत् ॥
Seeing her husband, the Ten-necked, slain by Rāma of unimaginable exploits, Manḍōdaree wailed pitifully.
6.111.3
ननु नाम महाबाहो
तव वैश्रवणानुज ।
क्रुद्धस्य प्रमुखे स्थातुम्
त्रस्यत्यपि पुरन्दरः ॥
Was not even Purandara afraid of standing against you when you are Angry, O younger brother of Vaiṡravaṇa, of mighty arm!
6.111.4
ऋषयश्च महान्तोऽपि
गन्धर्वाश्च यशस्विनः ।
ननु नाम तवोद्वेगात्
चारणाश्च दिशो गताः ॥
Did not the Ṛshis, the great souls, Gandharvas, the greatly reputed ones, and Cāraṇas, flee in all directions, scared of you!
6.111.5
स त्वं मानुषमात्रेण
रामेण युधि निर्जितः ।
न व्यपत्रपसे राजन्
किमिदं राक्षसर्षभ ॥
How is it, O King, O bull among the Rākshasas, that you are not ashamed of having been defeated in battle by Rāma, a mere human?
6.111.6
कथं त्रैलोक्यमाक्रम्य
श्रिया वीर्येण चान्वितम् ।
अविषह्यं जघान त्वाम्
मानुषो वनगोचरः ॥
How could a human, one roaming in the Vanas, kill you, who are endowed with valor, wealth, and who had dominated all the three worlds and against whom anyone could hardly stand!
6.111.7
मानुषाणामविषये
चरतः कामरूपिणः ।
विनाशस्तव रामेण
संयुगे नोपपद्यते ॥
It does not stand to reason that you, who stay in a place that no man can reach and who can take any form at will, should have fallen to Rāma in battle.
6.111.8
न चैतत्कर्म रामस्य
श्रद्दधामि चमूमुखे ।
सर्वतः समुपेतस्य
तव तेनाभिमर्षणम् ॥
I cannot believe this exploit of Rāma in the battlefront, of his attacking you, who are equipped with every weapon.
6.111.9
यदैव च जनस्थाने
राक्षसैर्बहुभिर्वृतः ।
खरस्तव हतो भ्राता
तदेवासौ न मानुषः ॥
The moment your brother Khara, who had the support of many Rākshasas, was killed in Janasthāna, it was clear that he (Rāma) was not a mere human.
6.111.10
यदैव नगरीं लङ्काम्
दुष्प्रवेशां सुरैरपि ।
प्रविष्टो हनुमान् वीर्यात्
तदैव व्यथिता वयम् ॥
We were quite distressed when Hanumān, relying only on his own valor, was able to enter into the city of Laṅkā, which even Suras could not penetrate.
6.111.11
यदैव वानरैर्घोरैः
बद्धः सेतुर्महार्णवे ।
तदैव हृदयेनाहम्
शङ्के रामममानुषम् ॥
I, in my heart, suspected that Rāma is not a mere human, right when the formidable Vānaras built a bridge over the huge ocean.
6.111.12
अथवा रामरूपेण
कृतान्तः स्वयमागतः ।
मायां तव विनाशाय
विधायाप्रतितर्किताम् ॥
Perhaps Yama the destroyer himself came in the guise of Rāma to destroy you, deploying a trick of magic that can hardly be imagined.
6.111.13
अथवा वासवेन त्वम्
धर्षितोऽसि महाबल ।
वासवस्य तु का शक्तिः
त्वां द्रष्टुमपि संयुगे ॥
Or is it that you were confronted by Vāsava, O you of extraordinary strength! But does Vāsava have such power, as to even lift his eyes to look at you in the battlefield?
6.111.14
व्यक्तमेष महायोगी
परमात्मा सनातनः ।
अनादिमध्यनिधनो
महतः परमो महान् ।
तमसः परमो धाता
शङ्खचक्रगदाधरः ।
श्रीवत्सवक्षा नित्यश्रीः
अजय्यः शाश्वतो ध्रुवः ॥
Without a doubt he is the Mahāyōgi, the Paramātman, the Sanātana, one without beginning, middle or end, who is vaster than the Mahat and beyond darkness. He is the one who supports the Universe. He is the one who holds the Ṡankha, Cakra and Gada in his hands and bears the mark of Sreevatsa on His chest. He is the one who is ever blissful, invincible, eternal and immovable.
6.111.16
मानुषं वपुरास्थाय
विष्णुः सत्यपराक्रमः ।
सर्वैः परिवृतो देवैः
वानरत्वमुपागतैः ।
सर्वलोकेश्वरः श्रीमान्
लोकानां हितकाम्यया ।
सराक्षसपरीवारम्
हतवांस्त्वां महाद्युतिः ॥
Vishṇu, the Lord of all the worlds, of true valor and extraordinary splendor, has come in the guise of a human, along with the Dēvas who have taken birth as Vānaras and slew you and all the Rākshasas, to the relief of all the worlds.
6.111.18
इन्द्रियाणि पुरा जित्वा
जितं त्रिभुवनं त्वया ।
स्मरद्भिरिव तद्वैरम्
इन्द्रियैरेव निर्जितः ॥
Once upon a time, by conquering the senses, you had conquered all the three worlds. It seems as if the senses have kept the grudge and have taken revenge by conquering you now.
6.111.19
क्रियतामविरोधश्च
राघवेणेति यन्मया ।
उच्यमानो न गृह्णासि
तस्येयं व्युष्टिरागता ॥
You would not heed my words when I pleaded with you not to make an enemy of Rāghava, and here it is, the consequence.
6.111.20
अकस्माच्चाभिकामोऽसि
सीतां राक्षसपुङ्गव ।
ऐश्वर्यस्य विनाशाय
देहस्य स्वजनस्य च ॥
All of a sudden, for no good reason, you, O bull among Rākshasas, became infatuated with Seetā, to bring destruction upon yourself, your people and to all that you possess.
6.111.21
अरुन्धत्या विशिष्टां ताम्
रोहिण्याश्चापि दुर्मते ।
सीतां धर्षयता मान्याम्
त्वया ह्यसदृशं कृतम् ॥
O evil-minded one! You have done something that should have never be done, humiliating Seetā who surpasses even Arundhati and Rōhiṇee (in her devotion to her husband) and is worthy of great regard.
6.111.22
वसुधायाश्च वसुधाम्
श्रियः श्रीं भर्तृवत्सलाम् ।
सीतां सर्वानवद्याङ्गीम्
अरण्ये विजने शुभाम् ।
आनयित्वा तु तां दीनाम्
छद्मनाऽऽत्मस्वदूषण ।
अप्राप्य चैव तं कामम्
मैथिलीसङ्कमे कृतम् ।
पतिव्रतायास्तपसा
नूनं दग्धोऽसि मे प्रभो ॥
Seetā, the fine lady, whose every limb is perfect, who loves her husband dearly, is Earth to the Earth herself (in forbearance) and Lakshmee to Lakshmee herself (in opulence).
My lord, having tricked her and abducted her from the forest when there was no one else, you lie here burnt by the power of a chaste wife's suffering, bringing destruction on all of your kith and kin, without your interest in that princess of Mithilā even being realized.
6.111.24
तदैव यन्न दग्धस्त्वम्
धर्षयंस्तनुमध्यमाम् ।
देवा बिभ्यति ते सर्वे
सेन्द्राः साग्निपुरोगमाः ॥
The reason you were not burnt away right when you forcefully abducted her, is that all the Dēvas along with Indra, who are represented by Agni, are afraid of you.
6.111.25
अवश्यमेव लभते
फलं पापस्य कर्मणः ।
घोरं पर्यागते काले
कर्ता नास्त्यत्र संशयः ॥
One who commits sinful deeds will certainly reap their terrible fruits when the time comes, without a question.
6.111.26
शुभकृच्छुभमाप्नोति
पापकृत् पापमश्नुते ।
विभीषणः सुखं प्राप्तः
त्वं प्राप्तः पापमीदृशम् ॥
Good things come to one who does good deeds. And terrible things, to one who does sinful deeds. Happiness happened to be the lot of Vibheeshaṇa and wretchedness like this, yours.
6.111.27
सन्त्यन्याः प्रमदास्तुभ्यम्
रूपेणाभ्यधिकास्ततः ।
अनङ्गवशमापन्नः
त्वं तु मोहान्न बुध्यसे ॥
There are many other women there, who are even more beautiful, who want you, which you did not realize being delusional, caught in the toils of passion.
6.111.28
न कुलेन न रूपेण
न दाक्षिण्येन मैथिली ।
मयाऽधिका वा तुल्या वा
त्वं तु मोहान्न बुध्यसे ॥
Neither by descent nor by beauty nor by abilities is Seetā my superior or even my equal, but, in your delusion, you could not see it.
6.111.29
सर्वदा सर्वभूतानाम्
नास्ति मृत्युरलक्षणः ।
तव तावदयं मृत्युः
मैथिलीकृतलक्षणः ।
सीतानिमित्तजो मृत्युः
त्वया दूरादुपाहृतः ॥
There is one reason or the other for the death of all creatures, all the time. Similarly, in your case, Maithili proved to be the reason for your death.
You brought about your death yourself from a far away place, which only seems to have been caused by Seetā.
6.111.31
मैथिली सह रामेण
विशोका विहरिष्यति ।
अल्पपुण्या त्वहं घोरे
पतिता शोकसागरे ॥
Maithili will enjoy happily the company of Rāma while I, whose Puṇya is meager, find myself plunged in the dreadful ocean of grief.
6.111.32
कैलासे मन्दरे मेरौ
तथा चैत्ररथे वने ।
देवोद्यानेषु सर्वेषु
विहृत्य सहिता त्वया ।
विमानेनानुरूपेण
या याम्यतुलया श्रिया ।
पश्यन्ती विविधान् देशान्
तांस्तांश्चित्रस्रगम्बरा ।
भ्रंशिता कामभोगेभ्यः
साऽस्मि वीर वधात्तव ।
सैवान्येवास्मि संवृत्ता
धिग् राज्ञां चञ्चलाः श्रियः ॥
I enjoyed, in your company, visiting Kailāsa, Mandara and Mēru, the woods of Caitraratha and the pleasances of the Dēvas.
I enjoyed, wearing a variety of dresses and garlands, and going in air cars that are worthy of unequaled opulence, visiting many places everywhere.
O Veera, with your death, I am bereft of all those enjoyments and luxuries. I have now become someone else; Dhik, how unreliable are these riches!
6.111.35
हा राजन् सुकुमारं ते
सुभ्रु सुत्वक् समुन्नसम् ।
कान्तिश्रीद्युतिभिस्तुल्यम्
इन्दुपद्मदिवाकरैः ।
किरीटकूटोज्ज्वलितम्
ताम्रास्यं दीप्तकुण्डलम् ।
मदव्याकुललोलाक्षम्
भूत्वा यत्पानभूमिषु ।
विविधस्रग्धरं चारु
वल्गुस्मितकथं शुभम् ।
तदेवाद्य तवेदं हि
वक्त्रं न भ्राजते प्रभो ।
रामसायकनिर्भिन्नम्
सिक्तं रुधिरविस्रवैः ।
विशीर्णमेदोमस्तिष्कम्
रूक्षं स्यन्दनरेणुभिः ॥
O my king, O my lord! Ah! your face, which used to be lovely, delicate and charming with lovely eyebrows, high nose, glowing skin, lovely smile, ripe-red lips and winsome words, rival the moon in brilliance, the lotus in splendor and the sun in radiance, shine with lofty crown, dazzling earrings, and a variety of garlands, with eyes flitting in turbid intoxication when you reveled in the drinking grounds, has now lost all that shine, struck down by Rāma’s arrows, turning red with the drenching blood, its marrow and skull shattered and caked with dust from the chariot wheels.
6.111.39
हा पश्चिमा मे सम्प्राप्ता
दशा वैधव्यदायिनी ।
या मयाऽऽसीन्न सम्बुद्धा
कदाचिदपि मन्दया ॥
Ah! I, the dull-witted, never thought of the possibility of widowhood befalling me in my later years.
6.111.40
पिता दानवराजो मे
भर्ता मे राक्षसेश्वरः ।
पुत्रो मे शक्रनिर्जेता
इत्येवं गर्विता भृशम् ॥
I was quite prideful that my father is the king of Dānavas, my husband is the lord of Rākshasas, and my son is the vanquisher of Ṡakra.
6.111.41
दृप्तारिमर्दनाः शूराः
प्रख्यातबलपौरुषाः ।
अकुतश्चिद्भया नाथा
ममेत्यासीन्मतिर्द्दढा ॥
I was extremely confident in my mind that my protectors, well known for their strength and self-esteem, were heroes who knew no fear from any quarter and were capable of quelling every prideful enemy.
6.111.42
तेषामेवंप्रभावानाम्
युष्माकं राक्षसर्षभाः ।
कथं भयमसम्बुद्धम्
मानुषादिदमागतम् ॥
How, then, O bulls among Rākshasas, did threat, wholly unsuspected, come to all of you, who are so powerful!
6.111.43
स्निग्धेन्द्रनीलनीलं तु
प्रांशुशैलोपमं महत् ।
केयूराङ्गदवैडूर्य
मुक्ताहारस्रगुज्ज्वलम् ।
कान्तं विहारेष्वधिकम्
दीप्तं सङ्ग्रामभूमिषु ।
भात्याभरणभाभिः
यद्विद्युद्भिरिव तोयदः ।
तदेवाद्य शरीरं ते
तीक्ष्णैर्नैकैः शरैश्चितम् ।
पुनर्दुर्लभसंस्पर्शम्
परिष्वक्तुं न शक्यते ।
श्वाविधः शलकैर्यद्वत्
बाणैर्लग्नैर्निरन्तरम् ।
स्वर्पितैर्मर्मसु भृशम्
सञ्छिन्नस्नायुबन्धनम् ।
क्षितौ निपतितं राजन्
श्यामं रुधिरसच्छवि ।
वज्रप्रहाराभिहतो
विकीर्ण इव पर्वतः ॥
Your body, that used to shine like sapphire, be commanding like a lofty hill, brilliant with bracelets, armlets, chains of emerald and pearl, and garlands of flowers, exceedingly winsome in pastimes and blazing fiercely on the battlefields, and shine, with the dazzle of its ornaments, like a raincloud lit up by lightning,
is pierced today by many a sharp arrow, difficult to touch, impossible to embrace, so covered without gap, like the porcupine with quills, by the arrows which have pierced the vital parts and torn the guts and ligatures.
It lies on the ground, like a mountain shattered to pieces, struck down by the Vajra, turning dark and assuming the color of blood.
6.111.48
हा स्वप्नः सत्यमेवेदम्
त्वं रामेण कथं हतः ।
त्वं मृत्योरपि मृत्युः स्याः
कथं मृत्युवशं गतः ॥
Is this real or am I dreaming? How could you be slain by Rāma? How could you, who are the death to Death itself, succumb to death?
6.111.49
त्रैलोक्यवसुभोक्तारम्
त्रैलोक्योद्वेगदं महत् ।
जेतारं लोकपालानाम्
क्षेप्तारं शङ्करस्य च ॥
You enjoyed the wealth of all the three worlds. You kept all the three worlds on their toes. You vanquished the Guardians of the Worlds. You shook even Ṡaṅkara himself.
This block of nine Ṡlōkas constitute a single long sentence, with multiple clauses. For the sake of convenience, I translated each clause into a (short) sentence of its own.
6.111.50
दृप्तानां निग्रहीतारम्
आविष्कृतपराक्रमम् ।
लोकक्षोभयितारं च
नादैर्भूतविराविणम् ॥
You put a check on all the arrogant souls. You made everyone take note of your supreme prowess. You sent the worlds into a turmoil. You made the creatures wail at the very sound of your voice.
6.111.51
ओजसा दृप्तवाक्यानाम्
वक्तारं रिपुसन्निधौ ।
स्वयूथभृत्यवर्गाणाम्
गोप्तारं भीमकर्मणाम् ॥
You spoke with all the arrogance that your power could afford you, when you faced your enemies. You managed your own bands of staff and armies of formidable deeds.
6.111.52
हन्तारं दानवेन्द्राणाम्
यक्षाणां च सहस्रशः ।
निवातकवचानां च
सङ्ग्रहीतारमाहवे ॥
You destroyed the Dānava chiefs and Yakshas in their thousands and subdued the Nivātakavacas in the battle.
6.111.53
नैकयज्ञविलोप्तारम्
त्रातारं स्वजनस्य च ।
धर्मव्यवस्थाभेत्तारम्
मायास्रष्टारमाहवे ॥
You ruined innumerable Yajñas. You protected all your people. You pilloried the entire system of Dharma. You wrought many a magical illusion in battles.
6.111.54
देवासुरनृकन्यानाम्
आहर्तारं ततस्ततः ।
शत्रुस्त्रीशोकदातारम्
नेतारं स्वजनस्य च ॥
You carried off the best of the girls of the Dēvas, Asuras and humans from everywhere. You gave grief to the women folk of the enemies. You were a leader of your people.
6.111.55
लङ्काद्वीपस्य गोप्तारम्
कर्तारं भीमकर्मणाम् ।
अस्माकं कामभोगानाम्
दातारं रथिनां वरम् ॥
You guarded the island of Laṅkā. You accomplished many a formidable exploit. You provided us with all comforts and luxuries. You are the best among the charioters.
6.111.56
एवंप्रभावं भर्तारम्
दृष्ट्वा रामेण पातितम् ।
स्थिराऽस्मि या देहमिमम्
धारयामि हतप्रिया ॥
I must have been made of a rock, to hang on to this life with my beloved dead, and seeing a husband who has accomplished so much, struck down by Rāma.
6.111.57
शयनेषु महार्हेषु
शयित्वा राक्षसेश्वर ।
इह कस्मात् प्रसुप्तोऽसि
धरण्यां रेणुगुण्ठितः ॥
Having lain on the most expensive beds, O lord of the Rākshasas,how come you lie here on the bare earth, covered in dust?
6.111.58
यदा ते तनयः शस्तो
लक्ष्मणेनेन्द्रजिद्युधि ।
तदात्वभिहता तीव्रम्
अद्य त्वस्मिन्निपातिता ॥
It was a severe blow to me, when your son Indrajit was killed by Lakshmaṇa in the battle; but now, I am struck down completely.
6.111.59
साहं बन्धुजनैर्हीना
हीना नाथेन च त्वया ।
विहीना कामभोगैश्च
शोचिष्ये शाश्वतीः समाः ॥
And on top of it, I must suffer for endless years, without my kith and kin, without you, my protector, and without comforts and luxuries.
6.111.60
प्रपन्नो दीर्घमध्वानम्
राजन्नद्यासि दुर्गमम् ।
नय मामपि दुःखार्ताम्
न जीविष्ये त्वया विना ॥
You have set out today on a long and strenuous journey, o King. Take me, the grief-stricken one too, with you; I cannot live without you.
6.111.61
कस्मात्त्वं मां विहायेह
कृपणां गन्तुमिच्छसि ।
दीनां विलपितीं मन्दाम्
किंवा मां नाभिभाषसे ॥
How could you wish to go away leaving me here forlorn? Why do you not respond to me as I lament pitifully?
6.111.62
दृष्ट्वा न खल्वसि क्रुद्धो
मामिहानवकुण्ठिताम् ।
निर्गतां नगरद्वारात्
पद्भ्यामेवागतां प्रभो ॥
I hope you are not angry with me, my Lord, for coming out of the walls of the city, (in public) on foot without putting on a veil!
6.111.63
पश्येष्टदार दारांस्ते
भ्रष्टलज्जावकुण्ठितान् ।
बहिर्निष्पतितान् सर्वान्
कथं दृष्ट्वा न कुप्यसि ॥
Look at your women, O fondest of husbands, who came casting aside their veils losing all sense of modesty! How is it that you are not angry at seeing them all come out in public?
6.111.64
अयं क्रीडासहायस्ते
नाथ लालप्यते जनः ।
न चैनमाश्वासयसि
किं वा न बहुमन्यसे ॥
These souls, your playmates, weep, O lord; why do you not comfort them? Do you not care for them any more?
6.111.65
यास्त्वया विधवा राजन्
कृता नैकाः कुलस्त्रियः ।
पतिव्रता धर्मपरा
गुरुशुश्रूषणे रताः ।
ताभिः शोकाभितप्ताभिः
शप्तः परवशं गतः ।
त्वया विप्रकृताभिर्यत्
तदा शप्तं तदागतम् ॥
Innumerable family women who followed Dharma, served their elders, and were devoted to their husbands, were turned into widows by you.
It is the curses of those women who burned with grief that made you fall to the foe. It is the curse of those women, whom you had separated (from their loved ones) that came to haunt you.
6.111.67
प्रवादः सत्य एवायम्
त्वां प्रति प्रायशो नृप ।
पतिव्रतानां नाकस्मात्
पतन्त्यश्रूणि भूतले ॥
The saying that the tears of devoted wives do not fall on the ground in vain, has proved only too true in your case, O King!
6.111.68
कथं च नाम ते राजन्
लोकानाक्रम्य तेजसा ।
नारीचौर्यमिदं क्षुद्रम्
कृतं शौण्डीर्यमानिना ॥
How was such a despicable act as stealing women done by you, being the hero that you consider yourself to be and having subjugated all the the worlds with your might, O king!
6.111.69
अपनीयाश्रमाद्रामम्
यन्मृगच्छद्मना त्वया ।
आनीता रामपत्नी सा
तत्ते कातर्यलक्षणम् ॥
That you carried off the wife of Rāma by taking him away from the Āṡrama with the ruse of (the charming) deer shows your cowardice.
6.111.70
कातर्यं च न ते युद्धे
कदाचित् संस्मराम्यहम् ।
तत्तुभाग्यविपर्यासात्
नूनं ते पक्वलक्षणम् ॥
But I cannot at all remember you showing cowardice in any battle. It must just be a sign of fortunes finally and completely turning against you.
6.111.71
अतीतानागतार्थज्ञो
वर्तमानविचक्षणः ।
मैथिलीमाहृतां दृष्ट्वा
ध्यात्वा निश्वस्य चायतम् ।
सत्यवाक् स महाभागो
देवरो मे यदब्रवीत् ।
सोऽयं राक्षसमुख्यानाम्
विनाशः पर्युपस्थितः ।
कामक्रोधसमुत्थेन
व्यसनेन प्रसङ्गिना ॥
On seeing the princess of Mithilā carried off (by you), my brother-in-law, who has a great sense of the past, present and the future, thought for a long while and sighed deeply.
The imminent destruction of all the great Rākshasas that the blessed one who is wedded to truth warned you about has come to pass now, all because of your obsession with women, propelled by anger and lust.
6.111.73
निर्वृत्तस्त्वत्कृतेऽनर्थः
सोऽयं मूलहरो महान् ।
त्वया कृतमिदं सर्वम्
अनाथं रक्षसां कुलम् ॥
This great catastrophe that struck at the root has happened solely because of you. The entire race of Rākshasas is made protector-less by you.
6.111.74
न हि त्वं शोचितव्यो मे
प्रख्यातबलपौरुषः ।
स्त्रीस्वभावात्तु मे बुद्धिः
कारुण्ये परिवर्तते ॥
May be I should not be mourning you, for you are renowned for your strength and valor. But I cannot help feeling miserable, being a woman.
6.111.75
सुकृतं दुष्कृतं च त्वम्
गृहीत्वा स्वां गतिं गतः ।
आत्मानमनुशोचामि
त्वद्वियोगेन दुःखिता ॥
You have gone your way, taking with you whatever you did, the good and the bad. I am worried about myself now, feeling miserable, separated from you.
6.111.76
सुहृदां हितकामानाम्
न श्रुतं वचनं त्वया ।
भ्रातऽणां चापि कार्त्स्न्येन
हितमुक्तं दशानन ॥
You would not listen to the advice of friends and well-wishers, O Ten-faced; nor would you heed at all the good advice of your brothers.
6.111.77
हेत्वर्थयुक्तं विधिवत्
श्रेयस्करमदारुणम् ।
विभीषणेनाभिहितम्
न कृतं हेतुमत्त्वया ॥
You did not care to follow the well-reasoned and meaningful advice of Vibheeshaṇa that was in accordance with the Ṡāstras, laid out in a cogent manner without at all being harsh on you; it would have only helped your well being.
6.111.78
मारीचकुम्भकर्णाभ्याम्
वाक्यं मम पितुस्तदा ।
न श्रुतं वीर्यमत्तेन
तस्येदं फलमीदृशम् ॥
Likewise, you, intoxicated with your valor, would not heed the words of Māreeca, Kumbhakarṇa and even my father; and here, you pay for it.
6.111.79
नीलजीमूतसङ्काश
पीताम्बर शुभाङ्गद ।
स्वगात्राणि विनिक्षिप्य
किं शेषे रुधिराप्लुतः ।
प्रसुप्त इव शोकार्ताम्
किं मां न प्रतिभाषसे ॥
How can you, magnificent like the black raincloud, clad in yellow garment and adorned with shoulder rings be content to lie, all your limbs flung about and drowned in blood ?
Why do you not answer me, who is oppressed by grief, as though you are asleep?
6.111.81
महावीर्यस्य दक्षस्य
संयुगेष्वपलायिनः ।
यातुधानस्य दौहित्र
किं च मां नाभ्युदीक्षसे ॥
Why do you, the son of the daughter of the capable Rākshasa (Mālyavanta) of extraordinary valor who never turned his back in a battle, not look at me?
6.111.82
उत्तिष्ठोत्तिष्ठ किं शेषे
प्राप्ते परिभवे नवे ।
अद्य वै निर्भयां लङ्काम्
प्रविष्टाः सूर्यरश्मयः ॥
Get up, please get up! How can you lie still when a disgrace that was never known in the past, threatens? Today the sun’s rays have entered Laṅkā unafraid.
6.111.83
येन सूदयसे शत्रून्
समरे सूर्यवर्चसा ।
वज्रो वज्रधरस्येव
सोऽयं ते सततार्चितः ।
रणे शत्रुप्रहरणो
हमजालपरिष्कृतः ।
परिघो व्यवकीर्णस्ते
बाणैश्छिन्नः सहस्रधा ॥
Your Parigha, which shines like the sun with ornate works of gold wrought on it, which was treasured like the Vajra of the wielder of Vajra, which strikes down the enemies in the battlefield, which you used to destroy your enemies in battles, lies shivered by arrows into thousands of pieces, scattered everywhere.
6.111.85
प्रियामिवोपगुह्य त्वम
शेषे समरमेदिनीम् ।
अप्रियामिव कस्माच्च
मां नेच्छस्यभिभाषितुम् ॥
You lie embracing the battlefield as if it were your beloved; but why do you not like to speak to me, as if you hate me?
6.111.86
धिगस्तु हृदयं यस्या
ममेदं न सहस्रधा ।
त्वयि पञ्चत्वमापन्ने
फलते शोकपीडितम् ॥
Dhik on my heart, it does not shatter into thousands of pieces tormented by grief, even with you lying dead here!
6.111.87
इत्येवं विलपन्ती सा
बाष्पपर्याकुलेक्षणा ।
स्नेहोपस्कन्नहृदया
देवी मोहमुपागमत् ॥
Lamenting thus, the lady fell into a swoon, her eyes turbid with tears and her love for him wrenching her heart.
6.111.88
कश्मलाभिहता सन्ना
बभौ सा रावणोरसि ।
सन्ध्याऽनुरक्ते जलदे
दीप्ता विद्युदिवोज्ज्वला ॥
Struck with sorrow, she lay exhausted upon Rāvaṇa’s chest shining like a streak of lightning against a raincloud that had turned red in the evening sky.
6.111.89
तथागतां समुत्थाप्य
सपत्न्यस्तां भृशातुराः ।
पर्यवस्थापयामासू
रुदन्त्यो रुदतीं भृशम् ॥
Quite concerned seeing her in that condition, her co-wives, who were themselves crying, lifted her up who was crying even more miserably, and tried to console her.
6.111.90
न ते सुविदिता देवि
लोकानां स्थितिरध्रुवा ।
दशाविभागपर्याये
राज्ञां चञ्चलया श्रिया ॥
Do you not know very well, my lady, that this world would not stay the same forever, and that each passing phase turns the fortune of kings over?
6.111.91
इत्येवमुच्यमाना सा
सशब्दं प्ररुरोद ह ।
स्नापयन्ती तदास्रेण
स्तनौ वक्त्रं सुनिर्मलम् ॥
Thus being exhorted, she wailed aloud bathing her clear face and her breasts with tears.
6.111.92
एतस्मिन्नन्तरे रामो
विभीषणमुवाच ह ।
संस्कारः क्रियतां भ्रातुः
स्त्रियश्चैता निवर्तय ॥
Meanwhile, Rāma told Vibheeshaṇa, ‘Please send away the women and perform the last rites for your brother’.
6.111.93
तमुवाच ततो धीमान्
विभीषण इदं वचः ।
विमृश्य बुद्ध्या धर्मज्ञो
धर्मार्थसहितं हितम् ॥
The sagacious Vibheeshaṇa, a Dharmajña, after careful consideration, responded to him with these meaningful words that align with Dharma:
6.111.94
त्यक्तधर्मव्रतं क्रूरम्
नृशंसमनृतं तथा ।
नाहमर्होऽस्मि संस्कर्तुम्
परदाराभिमर्शिनम् ॥
I cannot (with good conscience) perform the last rites for one who was so cruel and wicked, and was a liar, who abjured adherence to Dharma, and who laid his hands on other peoples’ wives.
6.111.95
भ्रातृरूपो हि मे शत्रुः
एषसर्वाहिते रतः ।
रावणो नार्हते पूजाम्
पूज्योऽपि गुरुगौरवात् ॥
He, who indulged in harming everyone, is, to me, an enemy in the guise of a brother. Rāvaṇa does not deserve any honors, even though he is entitled to respect as an elder.
6.111.96
नृशंस इति मां कामम्
वक्ष्यन्ति मनुजा भुवि ।
श्रुत्वा तस्यागुणान् सर्वे
वक्ष्यन्ति सुकृतं पुनः ॥
Quite likely, men in this world are going to denounce me as a heartless wretch; but they would say that I have done right, after they hear about all his vices.
6.111.97
तच्छ्रुत्वा परमप्रीतो
रामो धर्मभृतां वरः ।
विभीषणमुवाचेदम्
वाक्यज्ञं वाक्यकोविदः ॥
Pleased with what he heard, Rāma, foremost of those who patronize Dharma, an expert in the choice of words, said these words to Vibheeshaṇa who knew what each word meant:
6.111.98
तवापि मे प्रियं कार्यम्
त्वत्प्रभावाच्च मे जितम् ।
अवश्यं तु क्षमं वाच्यो
मया त्वं राक्षसेश्वर ॥
I too ought to do something that pleases you, for it is because of your help that I could win. I have something that I must tell you, O lord of Rākshasas!
6.111.99
अधर्मानृतसंयुक्तः
कामं त्वेष निशाचरः ।
तेजस्वी बलवान् शूरो
संयुगेषु च नित्यशः ॥
This Night-rover has very well been guilty of lying and abjuring Dharma, but he was also strong and powerful and always emerged a hero in battles.
6.111.100
शतक्रतुमुखैर्वीरैः
श्रूयते न पराजितः ।
महात्मा बलसम्पन्नो
रावणो लोकरावणः ॥
Rāvaṇa, who made the worlds wail, was also known to be a Mahātma endowed with great strength and to have never been defeated by Veeras like Ṡatakratu.
6.111.101
मरणान्तानि वैराणि
निर्वृत्तं नः प्रयोजनम् ।
क्रियतामस्य संस्कारो
ममाप्येष यथा तव ॥
All enmities end with death, and our purpose is served. The last rites shall be done for him. He is the me to me as he is to you.
6.111.102
त्वत्सकाशाद्दशग्रीवः
संस्कारं विधिपूर्वकम् ।
प्राप्तुमर्हति धर्मज्ञ
त्वं यशोभाग्भविष्यसि ॥
O Dharmajña! The Ten-necked deserves final rites in a customary manner from you; and doing so would only add to your name.
6.111.103
राघवस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
त्वरमाणो विभीषणः ।
संस्कारयितुमारेभे
भ्रातरं रावणं हतम् ॥
Hearing those words of Rāghava, Vibheeshaṇa hastened to arrange the funeral rites for Rāvaṇa, his slain brother.
6.111.104
स प्रविश्य पुरीं लङ्काम्
राक्षसेन्द्रो विभीषणः ।
रावणस्याग्निहोत्रम्
तु निर्यासयति सत्वरम् ॥
Vibheeshaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, went to the city of Laṅkā and ordered the ritual fire from Rāvaṇa's house to be brought.
6.111.105
शकटान् दारुरूपाणि
अग्नीन् वै याजकांस्तथा ।
तथा चन्दनकाष्ठानि
काष्ठानि विविधानि च ।
अगरूणि सुगन्धीनि
गन्धांश्च सुरभींस्तथा ।
मणिमुक्तप्रवालानि
निर्यापयति राक्षसः ॥
The Rākshasa also ordered carts, utensils made of wood, embers, priests, billets of sandal and a variety of other woods, Agaru, fragrances and scents, diamonds, pearls and corals to be brought.
6.111.107
आजगाम मुहूर्तेन
राक्षसैः परिवारितः ।
ततो माल्यवता सार्धम्
क्रियामेव चकार सः ॥
In a quick while, with Rākshasas about him, he came back and performed the rites assisted by Mālyavanta.
6.111.108
सौवर्णीं शिबिकां दिव्याम्
आरोप्य क्षौमवासनम् ।
रावणं राक्षसाधीशम्
अश्रुपूर्णमुखा द्विजाः ।
तूर्यघोषैश्च विविधैः
स्तुवद्भिश्चाभिनन्दितम् ।
पताकाभिश्च चित्राभिः
सुमनोभिश्च चित्रिताम् ।
उत्क्षिप्य शिबिकाम् तां तु
विभीषण पुरोगमाः ।
दक्षिणाभिमुखाः सर्वे
गृह्य काष्ठानि भेजिरे ॥
The Brāhmaṇas, their faces covered in tears, placed the lord of Rākshasas who was covered with silk garments, on a splendid golden bier, as trumpets blared and chants went up in his praise.
Lifting up that bier, which was painted with multiple colors and was decked with flowers and flags, all of them took a southbound path, with Vibheeshaṇa walking in the front, taking the billets with them.
6.111.111
अग्नयो दीप्यमानास्ते
तदाध्वर्युसमीरिताः ।
शरणाभिगताः सर्वे
पुरस्तात्तस्य ते ययुः ॥
The priests blew the fire and it raged with huge flames. And the live fires that were contained in their holders till then raged and blew towards Rāvaṇa.
6.111.112
अन्तःपुराणि सर्वाणि
रुदमानानि सत्वरम् ।
पृष्ठतोऽनुययुस्तानि
प्लवमानानि सर्वतः ॥
Women from all the inner-quarters followed right behind quickly, wailing, and leaping (to keep the pace with men).
This Ṡlōka seems to be in a wrong place. It fits better between Ṡlōka 107 and 108 above.
6.111.113
रावणं प्रयते देशे
स्थाप्य ते भुशदुःखिताः ।
चितां चन्दनकाष्ठैश्च
पद्मकोशीरसंवृताम् ।
ब्राह्म्या संवर्तयामासुः
राङ्कवास्तरणावृताम् ॥
Stricken with extreme grief, they laid down Rāvaṇa's body on a sanctified ground, and arranged, as prescribed in the Vēdas, a funeral pyre with the logs of sandal wood and sprinkled it with the fragrances of Padmaka and Uṡeera and covered it with a woolen spread.
These Ṡlōkas seems to be an alternative version of what was described from Ṡlōkas 108 through 111 above.
6.111.114
वर्तते वेदविहितो
राज्ञो वै पश्चिमः क्रतुः ।
प्रचक्रू राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
पितृमेधमनुत्तमम् ।
वेदिं च दक्षिणप्राच्याम्
यथास्थानं च पावकम् ॥
The last rites for the king proceeded as prescribed in the Vēdas, as part of which they also performed a unique and special rite called Pitṛmēdha. A Vēdi was made towards the southwest and fire was kindled in it, in a customary manner.
6.111.116
पृषदाज्येन सम्पूर्णम्
स्रुवं स्कन्धे प्रचिक्षिपुः ।
पादयोः शकटं प्रादुः
ऊर्वोश्चोलूखलं तदा ॥
The ladle filled with ghee mixed with curds was kept on the shoulder (of Rāvaṇa), the cart was kept by the feet and the mortar on the thighs.
6.111.117
दारुपात्राणि सर्वाणि
अरणिं चोत्तरारणिम् ।
दत्त्वा तु मुसलं चान्यम्
यथा स्थानं विचक्रमुः ॥
Leaving all the wooden vessels, the Araṇi, the Uttara Araṇi, the pestles and everything else in the right places, they went around (the pyre).
6.111.118
शास्त्रदृष्टेन विधिना
महर्षिविहितेन च ।
तत्र मेध्यं पशुं हत्वा
राक्षसेन्द्रस्य राक्षसाः ।
परिस्तरणिकां राज्ञो
घृताक्तां समवेशयन् ॥
The Rākshasas, following the custom laid down by the Maharshis and was seen in the Ṡāstras, killed an animal (sheep) that met the prescribed specifications, and placed its omentum dipped in ghee on the face of the king, the lord of Rākshasas.
6.111.119
गन्धैर्माल्यैरलङ्कृत्य
रावणं दीनमानसाः ।
विभीषणसहायास्ते
वस्त्रैश्च विविधैरपि ।
लाजैश्चावकिरन्ति स्म
बाष्पपूर्णमुखास्तदा ॥
With woebegone, tear-covered faces, taking the help of Vibheeshaṇa, they decorated Rāvaṇa with garlands and a variety of garments, and then sprinkled perfumes and puffed grain.
6.111.121
स ददौ पावकं तस्य
विधियुक्तं विभीषणः ।
स्नात्वा चैवार्द्रवस्त्रेण
तिलान् दर्भविमिश्रितान् ।
उदकेन च संमिश्रान्
प्रदाय विधिपूर्वकम् ।
प्रदाय चोदकं तस्मै
मूर्ध्ना चैनं नमस्य च ॥
Then Vibheeshaṇa set fire to the pyre, and taking bath and with dripping clothes, offered sesame seeds mixed with Darbha grass, and then water on the head, and bowed, all as laid out by custom.
6.111.123
ताः स्त्रियोऽनुनयामास
सान्त्वयित्वा पुनःपुनः ।
गम्यतामिति ताः सर्वा
विविशुर्नगरं ततः ॥
Comforted again and again and cajoled to return, all the women went back to the city from there.
6.111.124
प्रविष्टासु पुरीं स्त्रीषु
राक्षसेन्द्रो विभीषणः ।
रामपार्श्वमुपागम्य
तदाऽतिष्ठद्विनीतवत् ॥
Once the women returned to the city, Vibheeshaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, came and stood by Rāma’s side with all deference.
6.111.125
रामोऽपि सह सैन्येन
ससुग्रीवः सलक्ष्मणः ।
हर्षं लेभे रिपुं हत्वा
वृत्रं वज्रधरो यथा ॥
Having slain the enemy like the wielder of Vajra did Vṛtra, Rāma rejoiced, along with Lakshmaṇa, Sugreeva and the army.
6.111.126
ततो विमुक्त्वा सशरं शरासनम्
महेन्द्रदत्तं कवचं च तन्महत् ।
विमुच्य रोषं रिपुनिग्रहात्ततो
रामः सुसौम्यत्वमुपागतोऽरिहा ॥
Then, having laid aside the bow and the arrows and that great armor sent by Mahēndra, and also renouncing the wrath he harbored to subdue the enemy, Rāma, the destroyer of the foe, assumed a gentle mien.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे एकादशोत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and eleventh Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
