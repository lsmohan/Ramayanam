Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 23
In this Sarga, as Khara’s army starts, many a bad omen portending disaster, are seen. But Khara does not care for them, in his folly. He excites the army with his roar. The army marches on. Maharshis and Dēvas gather to witness the battle, hailing victory to Rāma.
3.23.1
तस्मिन्याते बले घोरम्
अशिवं शोणितोदकम् ।
अभ्यवर्षन्महामेघः
तुमुलो गर्दभारुणः ॥
As that grisly army set forth, tumultuous, massive and donkey-colored clouds showered ominous rain that resembled blood.
3.23.2
निपेतुस्तुरगास्तस्य
रथयुक्ता महाजवाः ।
समे पुष्पचिते देशे
राजमार्गे यदृच्छया ॥
The mighty fast horses which were yoked to the chariot, stumbled unexpectedly even on the highway that was even and strewn with flowers.
3.23.3
श्यामं रुधिरपर्यन्तं
बभूव परिवेषणम् ।
अलातचक्रप्रतिमं
परिगृह्य दिवाकरम् ॥
The sun was surrounded by a dark corona with a red edge, resembling a circle seen when a fire-brand is whirled.
3.23.4
ततो ध्वजमुपागम्य
हेमदण्डं समुच्छ्रितम् ।
समाक्रम्य महाकायः
तस्थौ गृध्रस्सुदारुणः ॥
Then a frightful vulture with an immense body came hovering and perched itself on the flag at the top of the tall golden staff.
3.23.5
जनस्थानसमीपे तु
समागम्य खरस्वनाः ।
विस्वरान्विविधांश्चक्रुः
मांसादा मृगपक्षिणः ॥
Various kinds of birds and beasts that love feeding on flesh gathered around Janasthāna, uttering discordant cries in their grating voices.
3.23.6
व्याजह्रुश्च प्रदीप्तायां
दिशि वै भैरवस्वनम् ।
अशिवं यातुधानानां
शिवा घोरा महास्वनाः ॥
Jackals with loud and grating voices howled hideously facing the quarter where (the sun) was blazing, presaging disaster to the Rākshasas.
3.23.7
प्रभिन्नगिरिसङ्काशाः
तोयशोणितधारिणः ।
आकाशं तदनाकाशं
चक्रुर्भीमा वलाहकाः ॥
Frightful clouds swollen with blood-red water, that resembled mountains that were blasted, covered the sky without an opening.
3.23.8
बभूव तिमिरं घोरम्
उद्धतं रोमहर्षणम् ।
दिशो वा विदिशो वापि
न च व्यक्तं चकाशिरे ॥
An immense darkness, so terrifying that it made one’s hair stand on end descended, making it impossible to see the quarters or the intermediate points.
3.23.9
क्षतजार्द्रसवर्णाभा
सन्ध्याकालं विना बभौ ।
खरस्याभिमुखा नेदुः
तदा घोरमृगाः खगाः ।
कङ्कगोमायुगृध्राश्च
चुक्रुशुर्भयशंसिनः ॥
A scarlet twilight, ugly like a raw wound, descended in an untimely manner. Fierce beasts and birds howled and cried at Khara. Eagles, jackals and vultures uttered lugubrious cries, portending calamity.
3.23.10
नित्याशुभकरा युद्धे
शिवा घोरनिदर्शनाः ।
नेदुर्बलस्याभिमुखं
ज्वालोद्गारिभिराननैः ॥
Repulsive vixens with flaming mouths, which always bode disaster in battle, wailed facing that army.
3.23.11
कबन्धः परिघाभासो
दृश्यते भास्करान्तिके ।
जग्राह सूर्यं स्वर्भानुः
अपर्वणि महाग्रहः ।
प्रवाति मारुतश्शीघ्रं
निष्प्रभोऽभूद्दिवाकरः ॥
A headless torso that looked like an iron club was seen in the vicinity of the sun. The vindictive Rāhu eclipsed the sun though it was not the day of new moon. The wind blew a violent gale and the sun became lackluster.
3.23.13
उत्पेतुश्च विना रात्रिं
ताराः खद्योतसप्रभाः ।
संलीनमीनविहगा
नलिन्यश्शुष्कपङ्कजाः ।
तस्मिन् क्षणे बभूवुश्च
विना पुष्पफलैर्द्रुमाः ॥
Stars twinkling like fireflies emerged even though it was not night. The lotuses in the lotus ponds dried up and the fish and aquatic birds therein were subdued. Suddenly the trees were denuded of fruit and flower.
3.23.14
उद्धूतश्च विना वातं
रेणुर्जलधरारुणः ।
वीचीकूचीति वाश्यन्त्यो
बभूवुस्तत्र शारिकाः ॥
A storm of dust resembling a red-gray cloud arose without any wind blowing. Ṡārika birds tweeted confusedly.
3.23.15
उल्काश्चापि सनिर्घाता
निपेतुर्घोरदर्शनाः ।
प्रचचाल मही सर्वा
सशैलवनकानना ॥
Meteors along with lightning fell portending disaster. The earth with its mountains, woods and forests, shook.
3.23.16
खरस्य च रथस्थस्य
नर्दमानस्य धीमतः ।
प्राकम्पत भुजस्सव्यः
स्वरश्चास्यावसज्जत ॥
As the capable Khara seated in his chariot let off a war-whoop, he felt a tremor in his left arm, and his voice quavered.
3.23.17
सास्रा सम्पद्यते दृष्टिः
पश्यमानस्य सर्वतः ।
ललाटे च रुजा जाता
न च मोहान्न्यवर्तत ॥
His vision was blurred with tears, as he looked all around. His forehead throbbed with pain. But in his folly he would not turn back.
3.23.18
तान्समीक्ष्य महोत्पातान्
उत्थितान्रोमहर्षणान् ।
अब्रवीद्राक्षसान्सर्वान्
प्रहसन्सखरस्तदा ॥
When he saw those hair-raising ominous portents, Khara said to all the Rākshasas with a laugh:
3.23.19
महोत्पातानिमान्सर्वान्
उत्थितान्घोरदर्शनान् ।
न चिन्तयाम्यहं वीर्यात्
बलवान्दुर्बलानिव ॥
I have immense strength. Like someone who is strong would not care about a weakling, I do not care about these portents, ominous though they might be.
3.23.20
तारा अपि शरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
पातयामि नभस्स्थलात् ।
मृत्युं मरणधर्मेण
सङ्क्रुद्धो योजयाम्यहम् ॥
With my sharp arrows I could shoot down even the stars from the sky. Provoked to wrath, I could inflict death on Death itself.
3.23.21
राघवं तं बलोत्सिक्तं
भ्रातरं चास्य लक्ष्मणम् ।
अहत्वा सायकैस्तीक्ष्णैः
नोपावर्तितुमुत्सहे ॥
I do not like returning without killing Rāghava who is haughty with his strength, and his brother Lakshmaṇa, with my sharp arrows.
3.23.22
सकामा भगिनी मेऽस्तु
पीत्वा तु रुधिरं तयोः ।
यन्निमित्तस्तु रामस्य
लक्ष्मणस्य विपर्ययः ॥
Let my sister, with whom Rāma and Lakshmaṇa misbehaved, have her wish fulfilled drinking the blood of those two.
3.23.23
न क्वचित्प्राप्तपूर्वो मे
संयुगेषु पराजयः ।
युष्माकमेतत्प्रत्यक्षं
नानृतं कथयाम्यहम् ॥
I have never known defeat in battle thus far. You have seen this with your own eyes, and you know that I am not lying.
3.23.24
देवराजमपि क्रुद्धो
मत्तैरावतयायिनम् ।
वज्रहस्तं रणे हन्यां
किं पुनस्तौ कुमानुषौ ॥
In my wrath, I could slay even the king of Dēvas himself, who comes mounted on Airāvata in rut and armed with Vajra; then what to speak of these despicable humans?
3.23.25
सा तस्य गर्जितं श्रुत्वा
राक्षसस्य महाचमूः ।
प्रहर्षमतुलं लेभे
मृत्युपाशावपाशिता ॥
That large army took extreme joy in hearing that roar of the Rākshasa, as the grip of the noose of death tightened on it.
3.23.26
समीयुश्च महात्मानो
युद्धदर्शनकाङ्क्षिणः ।
ऋषयो देवगन्दर्वाः
सिद्धाश्च सहचारणैः ॥
To witness that great battle, came together the Mahātmas, the Ṛshis, Dēvas, Gandharvas, Siddhas and Cāraṇas.
3.23.27
समेत्य चोचुस्सहिताः
तेऽन्योन्यं पुण्यकर्मणः ।
स्वस्ति गोब्राह्मणेभ्योऽस्तु
लोकानां ये च संमताः ॥
They, who have done many deeds of Puṇya, joining with each other, said: ‘May all be well with the cows and the Brāhmaṇas, and with all beings that are regarded by the world!’.
We hear the invocation, "May the cows and Brāhmaṇas be well, may all be well!" many a time, at the completion of any Pūja or Hōma, in temples and elsewhere.
Why cows and Brāhmaṇas? There are three reasons.
Both cows and Brāhmaṇas (spiritual scientists) are soft in nature and defenseless, but doggedly do their job every single day without fail. And both of them do not know how to bargain a good remuneration for their work. Their survival depends on the mercy of the society.
The invocation "May the cows and Brāhmaṇas be well!" really means, "May the society be mature and prosperous enough to be thankful and take care of the sections who do not know how to fight for themselves!"
This is the first reason.
The work done by cows is so mundane that it is easy to ignore its value. The work done by Brāhmaṇas is so abstract that it is easy to overlook its value.
The invocation "May the cows and Brāhmaṇas be well!" really means, "May the society be evolved enough to see the value of the mundane and the value of the abstract, along with everything in between!"
This is the second reason.
The second part of this Ṡlōka says ‘and all those who are regarded by the world’. Why are cows and Brāhmaṇas given as examples of those who are regarded by the society?
Cows are regarded for giving the materials for the survival of body and Brāhmaṇas for giving the guidance for the survival of the spirit.
Cows are instrumental for the society in harvesting the bounty that the earth can give and Brāhmaṇas in receiving the powers and blessings that the heavens can bestow.
Earth represents the hardware of life and heavens represent the software of life.
This is the third reason.
3.23.28
जयतां राघवस्संख्ये
पौलस्त्यान् रजनीचरान् ।
चक्रहस्तो यथा युद्धे
सर्वानसुरपुङ्गवान् ॥
May victory be to Rāghava in the fight over the Night-rovers, the descendants of Pulastya, just as it was for the wielder of Cakra over all the prominent Asuras!
Wielder of Cakra (discus) is Vishṇu.
3.23.29
एतच्चान्यच्च बहुशो
ब्रुवाणाः परमर्षयः ।
जातकौतूहलास्तत्र
विमानस्थाश्च देवताः ।
ददृशुर्वाहिनीं तेषां
राक्षसानां गतायुषाम् ॥
The great Ṛshis who were saying this and talking about many other things, and the greatly excited Dēvas seated in their Vimānas watched that army of Rākshasas whose life was already forfeit.
3.23.31
रथेन तु खरो वेगात्
उग्रसैन्यो विनिस्सृतः ।
तं दृष्ट्वा राक्षसं भूयो
राक्षसाश्च विनिस्सृताः ॥
Khara, the (commander of) the fierce army zoomed fast on his chariot. Seeing him, the Rākshasa (zoom thus), the Rākshasas followed suit.
3.23.32
श्येनगामी पृथुग्रीवो
यज्ञशत्रुर्विहङ्गमः ।
दुर्जयः करवीराक्षः
परुषः कालकार्मुकः ।
मेघमाली महामाली
सर्पास्यो रुधिराशनः ।
द्वादशैते महावीर्याः
प्रतस्थुरभितः खरम् ॥
Ṡyēnagāmi, Pṛthugreeva, Yajñaṡatru, Vihaṅgama, Durjaya, Karaveerāksha, Parusha, Kālakārmuka, Mēghamāli, Mahāmāli, Sarpāsya and Rudhirāṡana, these twelve, endowed with great strength, started (standing guard) around Khara.
3.23.34
महाकपालिस्स्थूलाक्षः
प्रमाथी त्रिशिरास्तथा ।
चत्वार एते सेनान्यो
दूषणं पृष्ठतो ययुः ॥
And these four, Mahākapāli, Sthūlāksha, Pramāthi and Triṡira, started along with Dūshaṇa.
3.23.35
सा भीमवेगा समराभिकामा
महाबला राक्षसवीरसेना ।
तौ राजपुत्रौ सहसाभ्युपेता
माला ग्रहाणामिव चन्द्रसूर्यौ ॥
That mighty army of Rākshasa warriors of terrific speed, impatient for battle, swiftly came up to the princes, like planets lining up to the sun and the moon.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे त्रयोविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty third Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
