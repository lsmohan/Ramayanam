Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 1
This Sarga describes Rāma's personality, Daṡaratha's decision to crown him as the king and assembling everyone to make that announcement.
2.1.1
गच्छता मातुलकुलम्
भरतेन तदाऽनघः ।
शत्रुघ्नो नित्यशत्रुघ्नो
नीतः प्रीतिपुरस्कृतः ॥
When Bharata had left for the home of his maternal uncle, he took along with him, out of affection, Ṡatrughna, the unafflicted, the destroyer of foes.
There are four parts in this Sarga. First part (Ṡlōkas 1- 30) describes the excellent qualities of Rāma. This is like his resume.
The second part (Ṡlōkas 31-39) describes consideration for the well deserving promotion for him to be the future king.
The third part (Ṡlōkas 40-41) describes taking the decision to give the promotion after discussing with the inner circles.
The final and fourth part (Ṡlōkas 31-41) describes the preparation for the announcement.
I have seen this four part sequence repeat in corporate life many a time. The more I read Ramayana, the more I appreciate its timelessness.
Every successful executive ensures that these steps are done diligently in this specific order.
2.1.2
स तत्र न्यवसद्भ्रात्रा
सह सत्कारसत्कृतः ।
मातुलेनाश्वपतिना
पुत्रस्नेहेन लालितः ॥
There he lived with his brother, entertained with all hospitality, and cherished by his uncle Aṡwapati with paternal affection.
2.1.3
तत्रापि निवसन्तौ तौ
तर्प्यमाणौ च कामतः ।
भ्रातरौ स्मरतां वीरौ
वृद्धं दशरथं नृपम् ॥
However, even though they are happy and liked to be there, their minds are always occupied with thoughts of their valiant brothers and the aged king Daṡaratha.
2.1.4
राजाऽपि तौ महातेजाः
सस्मार प्रोषितौ सुतौ ।
उभौ भरतशत्रुघ्नौ
महेन्द्रवरुणोपमौ ॥
And that supremely radiant king also has been thinking, again and again, about the sons Bharata and Ṡatrughna, who are away, and comparable with Mahēndra and Varuṇa.
2.1.5
सर्व एव तु तस्येष्टाः
चत्वारः पुरुषर्षभाः ।
स्वशरीराद्विनिर्वृत्ताः
चत्वार इव बाहवः ॥
He loved all the four of them, best among the men, as if they were four arms extending from his own body.
2.1.6
तेषामपि महातेजा
रामो रतिकरःपितुः ।
स्वयम्भूरिव भूतानाम्
बभूव गुणवत्तरः ॥
Even among them, Rāma of blazing power and surpassing virtues was most endearing to the father, like the Self-born (Brahma) was to all beings.
2.1.7
स हि देवैरुदीर्णस्य
रावणस्य वधार्थिभिः ।
अर्थितो मानुषे लोके
जज्ञे विष्णुस्सनातनः ॥
Was not he, after all, the eternal Vishnu himself, who has taken a birth into this world to slay the conceited Rāvaṇa, upon the request of the Dēvas?
2.1.8
कौसल्या शुशुभे तेन
पुत्रेणामिततेजसा ।
यथा वरेण देवानाम्
अदितिर्वज्रपाणिना ॥
Even Kousalyā shined splendidly because of the boundless radiance of her son, as Aditi did because of Indra, the best among the Dēvas, with Vajra adorning his hand.
Vajra is the weapon of Indra.
2.1.9
स हि रूपोपपन्नश्च
वीर्यवाननसूयकः ।
भूमावनुपमस्सूनुः
गुणैर्दशरथोपमः ॥
He was extremely handsome, full of prowess, free from envy and comparable to Daṡaratha in virtues. He was, verily, an incomparable son on this earth.
2.1.10
स तु नित्यं प्रशान्तात्मा
मृदुपूर्वं च भाषते ।
उच्यमानोऽपि परुषम्
नोत्तरं प्रतिपद्यते ॥
He was always calm and collected, and spoke gently. He wouldn't get provoked even if spoken to harshly.
2.1.11
कथञ्चिदुपकारेण
कृतेनैकेन तुष्यति ।
न स्मरत्यपकाराणाम्
शतमप्यात्मवत्तया ॥
Fully assured of himself, he remembers even the smallest favor done to him and would easily forgive even hundreds of offenses against him.
2.1.12
शीलवृद्धैर्ज्ञानवृद्धैः
वयोवृद्धैश्च सज्जनैः ।
कथयन्नास्त वै नित्यम्
अस्त्रयोग्यान्तरेष्वपि ॥
Always, even in between his practice of weapons, he used to have discourses with good people, who are ripened with age, wisdom or character.
2.1.13
बुद्धिमान्मधुराभाषी
पूर्वभाषी प्रियंवदः ।
वीर्यवान्न च वीर्येण
महता स्वेन विस्मितः ॥
He was intelligent and spoke pleasantly. He always led the conversations with right words. He was valiant, but was never obsessed with his great valor.
2.1.14
न चानृतकथो विद्वान्
वृद्धानां प्रतिपूजकः ।
अनुरक्तः प्रजाभिश्च
प्रजाश्चाप्यनुरञ्जते ॥
He spoke nothing but truth. He was learned, but at the same time, he was enthusiastic about respecting elders. He was loved by his people and he loved his people.
2.1.15
सानुक्रोशो जितक्रोधो
ब्राह्मणप्रतिपूजकः ।
दीनानुकम्पी धर्मज्ञो
नित्यं प्रग्रहवांश्चुचिः ॥
He was very empathetic. He was in full control of his temperament. He enthusiastically honored Brāhmaṇas. He was kind to those in distress. He was a savant of Dharma. He was thoughtful and pure.
2.1.16
कुलोचितमतिः क्षात्रम्
धर्मं स्वं बहुमन्यते ।
मन्यते परया कीर्त्या
महत्स्वर्गफलं ततः ॥
His thoughts were befitting of his race. He has high regard to his Kshātra Dharma. He considers that it will bring him great reputation in this world and reward in the Heaven hereafter.
2.1.17
नाश्रेयसि रतो विद्वान्
नविरुद्धकथारुचिः ।
उत्तरोत्तरयुक्तौ च
वक्ता वाचस्पतिर्यथा ॥
He doesn't delve into frivolous pursuits. He doesn't like conspiracy theories. He was learned and eloquent like Bṛhaspati in debates.
2.1.18
अरोगस्तरुणो वाग्मी
वपुष्मान्देशकालवित् ।
लोके पुरुषसारज्ञः
साधुरेको विनिर्मितः ॥
He was free from disease, young, eloquent and handsome. He has a great sense of place, time and people. He was created in this world, as one of a kind of gentleman.
2.1.19
स तु श्रेष्ठैर्गुणैर्युक्तः
प्रजानां पार्थिवात्मजः ।
बहिश्चर इव प्राणो
बभूव गुणतः प्रियः ॥
With such excellent qualities, that prince endeared himself to the people and was like their own life moving around outside.
2.1.20
सम्यग्विद्याव्रतस्नातो
यथावत्साङ्गवेदवित् ।
इष्वस्त्रे च पितुश्श्रेष्ठो
बभूव भरताग्रजः ॥
He graduated in high colors in the studies of all the Vēdas including all their branches. That elder brother of Bharata surpassed his father in the skill of archery.
2.1.21
कल्याणाभिजनस्साधुः
अदीनः सत्यवागृजुः ।
वृद्धैरभिविनीतश्च
द्विजैर्धर्मार्थदर्शिभिः ॥
He was a perfect gentleman of noble descent. He was straightforward, honest and uncowed in spirit. He was properly trained by mature Brāhmaṇas who were well versed in Dharma and Artha.
2.1.22
धर्मकामार्थतत्त्वज्ञः
स्मृतिमान्प्रतिभानवान् ।
लौकिके समयाचारे
कृतकल्पो विशारदः ॥
He was a savant of the essence of Dharma, Artha and Kāma. He was talented and had great memory. He was skilled and knowledgeable in matters of worldly etiquette and manners.
Rāmāyaṇa's focus is mainly on the first three of the Purushārthas, i.e., Dharma, Artha and Kāma, contrary to the belief of some people that it is about Mōksha. There is hardly any discussion in Rāmāyaṇa about the nature after life or universe or Brahman, as is the case with Mahābhārata in general and Bhagavadgeeta in particular.
2.1.23
निभृतस्संवृताकारो
गुप्तमन्त्रस्सहायवान् ।
अमोघक्रोधहर्षश्च
त्यागसंयमकालवित् ॥
All of this was covered in his humility. He kept his own counsel. Neither his anger nor pleasure was never in vain. He knew when to yield and when to hold.
2.1.24
दृढभक्तिस्स्थिरप्रज्ञो
नासद्ग्राही न दुर्वचाः ।
निस्तन्द्रिरप्रमत्तश्च
स्वदोषपरदोषवित् ॥
He was firm in his devotion. His intellect was unwavering. He would neither take misinformation, nor speak one. He was never lazy and always vigilant. He was aware of his own shortcomings and those of others.
2.1.25
शास्त्रज्ञश्च कृतज्ञश्च
पुरुषान्तरकोविदः ।
यः प्रग्रहानुग्रहयोः
यथान्यायं विचक्षणः ॥
He knew Ṡāstras. He knew how to be grateful. He had a deep understanding of the variations among people. He was very discreet in granting favors and delivering justice, in a fair and equitable manner.
2.1.26
सत्सङ्ग्रहप्रग्रहणे
स्थानविन्निग्रहस्य च ।
आयकर्मण्युपायज्ञः
सन्दृष्टव्ययकर्मवित् ॥
He knew whom to support and whom to suppress. He knew the Upāyas of earning and and responsibilities in spending.
Upāya has the connotation of technique and strategy.
2.1.27
श्रैष्ठ्यं शास्त्रसमूहेषु
प्राप्तो व्यामिश्रकेषु च ।
अर्थधमौ च सङ्गृह्य
सुखतन्त्रो न चालसः ॥
He was proficient in many Ṡāstras and also in the interdisciplinary subjects. He knows how to pursue Artha and Dharma that would ultimately lead to happiness. He was never lethargic.
Note that Artha and Dharma are not taken to be the ends in themselves. Rather they are enablers of happiness.
2.1.28
वैहारिकाणां शिल्पानाम्
विज्ञाताऽर्थविभागवित् ।
आरोहे विनये चैव
युक्तो वारणवाजिनाम् ॥
He knew how to use money for arts and entertainment. He knew how to subdue and mount horses and elephants.
2.1.29
धनुर्वेदविदां श्रेष्ठो
लोकेऽतिरथसम्मतः ।
अभियाता प्रहर्ता च
सेनानयविशारदः ॥
He was best among the archers in the world. He was hailed even by Atirathas. He knew well how to run armies straight into the enemy camps and annihilate them.
Ratha means Chariot. This word is used as part of the ranking of warriors. The ranking starts from foot soldier, at the bottom, on to horse mounted soldier, to elephant mounted warrior to chariot mounted warrior.
And among the chariot mounted, there are Atirathas (Super) Mahārathas (Great) and Artharathas ('half-as-good').
Rāma was hailed even by the Atirathas.
2.1.30
अप्रधृष्यश्च सङ्ग्रामे
क्रुध्दैरपि सुरासुरैः ।
अनसूयो जितक्रोधो
न दृप्तो न च मत्सरी ।
न चावमन्ता भूतानाम्
न च कालवशानुगः ॥
Even the most resolute Dēvas and Rākshasas could not contain him in fight. He was free from envy, anger, hubris and resentment. He never slighted or disparaged any being. Circumstances never cowed him.
2.1.31
एवं श्रेष्ठगुणैर्युक्तः
प्रजानां पार्थिवात्मजः ।
सम्मतस्त्रिषु लोकेषु
वसुधायाः क्षमागुणैः ।
बुद्ध्या बृहस्पतेस्तुल्यो
वीर्येणापि शचीपतेः ॥
He was held in high esteem by people of all three worlds. He was like the Earth in forbearance, Bṛihaspati in wisdom and Indra in valor. Such excellent qualities made him the most suitable prince for the people.
2.1.33
तथा सर्वप्रजाकान्तैः
प्रीतिसंजननैः पितुः ।
गुणैर्विरुरुचे रामो
दीप्तस्सूर्य इवांशुभिः ॥
With such qualities that are a source of delight, he became endearing to all people and to his father, and was shining like the resplendent sun.
2.1.34
तमेवं व्रतसम्पन्नम्
अप्रधृष्यपराक्रमम् ।
लोक पालोपमं नाथम्
अकामयत मेदिनी ॥
He was comparable to the Guardians of the worlds, with such avowed virtues and indomitable prowess that the lands were awaiting him to be the lord.
2.1.35
एतैस्तु बहुभिर्युक्तम्
गुणैरनुपमैस्सुतम् ।
दृष्ट्वा दशरथो राजा
चक्रे चिन्तां परन्तपः ॥
The aged king Daṡaratha, the harrier of the foes, who had a long life, seeing his son endowed with such innumerable and incomparable qualities, thought:
2.1.36
अथ राज्ञो बभूवैवम्
वृद्धस्य चिरजीविनः ।
प्रीतिरेषा कथं रामो
राजा स्यान्मयि जीवति ॥
How would I have the pleasure of seeing Rāma become the king while I was alive!
2.1.37
एषा ह्यस्य परा प्रीतिः
हृदि संपरिवर्तते ॥
This thought, born out of great affection, occupied his mind constantly:
2.1.37
कदा नाम सुतं द्रक्ष्यामि
अभिषिक्तमहं प्रियम् ॥
How do I get to see my beloved son crowned as the king!
2.1.38
वृद्धिकामो हि लोकस्य
सर्वभूतानुकम्पनः ।
मत्तः प्रियतरो लोके
पर्जन्य इव वृष्टिमान् ॥
Like a cloud that only knows how to shower, his only urge is to help the world prosper! With his ability to empathize with everyone, he is even more likable to people than me!
2.1.39
यमशक्रसमो वीर्ये
बृहस्पतिसमो मतौ ।
महीधरसमो धृत्याम्
मत्तश्च गुणवत्तरः ॥
With valor comparable to that of Yama and Ṡakra, intellect comparable to that of Bṛhaspati and firmness comparable to that of mountains he surpasses me in every aspect!
2.1.40
महीमहमिमां कृत्स्नाम्
अधितिष्ठन्तमात्मजम् ।
अनेन वयसा दृष्ट्वा
यथास्वर्गमवाप्नुयाम् ॥
At my age, seeing the son ruling the entire earth is nothing short of attaining the heaven itself.
2.1.41
इत्येतैर्विविधैस्तैस्तैः
अन्यपार्थिवदुर्लभैः ।
शिष्टैरपरिमेयैश्च
लोके लोकोत्तरैर्गुणैः ।
तं समीक्ष्य महाराजो
युक्तं समुदितैश्शुभैः ।
निश्चित्य सचिवैस्सार्धम्
युवराजममन्यत ॥
Finding united in him all those excellent qualities that are hard to be found among kings, as well as many other uncommon virtues that are too numerous to count, the King deliberated with his ministers and decided on proclaiming him as the heir apparent to the throne.
2.1.43
दिव्यन्तरिक्षे भूमौ च
घोरमुत्पातजं भयम् ।
सञ्चचक्षेऽथ मेधावी
शरीरे चात्मनो जराम् ॥
Thereafter, seeing bad omens on the earth and in the skies, That thoughtful Daṡaratha talked about his aging body.
2.1.44
पूर्णचन्द्राननस्याथ
शोकापनुदमात्मनः ।
लोके रामस्य बुबुधे
सम्प्रियत्वं महात्मनः ॥
He felt that the darling of the worlds with the countenance of a full moon would relieve him of all his worries.
2.1.45
आत्मनश्च प्रजानां च
श्रेयसे च प्रियेण च ।
प्राप्तकालेन धर्मात्मा
भक्त्या त्वरितवान् नृपः ॥
That king, who was a Dharmātma, for the sake of his own comfort and well being and for that of all the people wanted to act quickly, in a timely manner.
2.1.46
नानानगरवास्तव्यान्
पुथग्जानपदानपि ।
समानिनाय मेदिन्याः
प्रधानान्पृथिवीपतीन् ॥
He summoned all the important kings and all the important personalities from the cities and the rural areas.
2.1.47
न तु केकयराजानम्
जनकं वा नराधिपः ।
त्वरया चानयामास
पश्चात्तौ श्रोष्यतः प्रियम् ॥
For want of time, he did not invite king Janaka or the king of Kēkaya assuming that they would only be happy to learn about the news later.
Note that king Janaka is the father-in-law of Rāma and king of Kēkaya is the father-in-law of Daṡaratha.
2.1.48
तान्वेश्मनानाभरणैः
यथाऽर्हं प्रतिपूजितान् ।
ददर्शालङ्कृतो राजा
प्रजापतिरिव प्रजाः ॥
When they were welcomed with due honors, properly lodged and presented with jewels, the king, clad in robes of state, received them, as a progenitor would meet his progeny.
2.1.49
अथोपविष्टे नृपतौ
तस्मिन्परबलार्दने ।
ततः प्रविविशुश्शेषा
राजानो लोकसम्मताः ॥
When he, the harrier of foes, had taken his seat, all the remaining kings acclaimed by people, entered.
2.1.50
अथ राजवितीर्णेषु
विविधेष्वासनेषु च ।
राजानमेवाभिमुखाः
निषेदुर्नियता नृपाः ॥
Then, the kings occupied the various seats assigned to them, facing the king, in an orderly manner.
2.1.51
सलब्धमानैर्विनयान्वितैर्नृपैः
पुरालयैर्जानपदैश्च मानवैः ।
उपोपविष्टैर्नृपतिर्वृतो बभौ
सहस्रचक्षुर्भगवानिवामरैः ॥
Surrounded by the obedient and respecting kings, chiefs from cities and rural areas, who were well received with honors, he was shining like the thousand-eyed lord Indra surrounded by the Dēvas.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे प्रथमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the first Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
