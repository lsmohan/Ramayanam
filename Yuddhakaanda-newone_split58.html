Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 58
In this Sarga, Vānaras and Rākshasas led by Prahasta clash. They fight fiercely, each smashing and tearing the other apart. The Vānara Dwivida slays the Rākshasa Narāntaka, the Vānara Durmukha slays the Rākshasa Samunnata, Jāmbavān slays the Rākshasa Mahānāda, and Tāra slays the Rākshasa Kumbhahanu. With the bodies of Vānaras and Rākshasas strewn around like fallen mountains, the battleground becomes a ghastly sight.
Then Neela rushes towards Prahasta and Prahasta shoots a downpour of arrows on him, which he can do nothing about  but endure in silence. Neela then snatches the bow from Prahasta’s hand and Prahasta attacks Neela with a pestle. Neela smashes the head of Prahasta with a boulder.
With their leader dead, the Rākshasa army becomes dispirited. and unable to continue with the fight anymore, retreats to Laṅkā. With praise showered on him for his accomplishment, Neela feels flattered.
6.58.1
ततः प्रहस्तं निर्यान्तम्
दृष्ट्वा भीमपराक्रमम् ।
उवाच सस्मितं रामो
विभीषणमरिन्दमः ॥
Seeing Prahasta of terrible prowess come out, Rāma, subduer of the foe, said to Vibheeshaṇa with a smile:
6.58.2
क एष सुमहाकायो
बलेन महता वृतः ।
आचक्ष्व मे महाबाहो
वीर्यवन्तं निशाचरम् ॥
Who is he with a huge body, accompanied by a great force? Tell me of this valiant Night-rover O you of the mighty arm!
6.58.3
राघवस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
प्रत्युवाच विभीषणः ॥
Hearing those words of Rāghava, Vibheeshaṇa replied:
6.58.3
एष सेनापतिस्तस्य
प्रहस्तो नाम राक्षसः ।
लङ्कायां राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
त्रिभागबलसंवृतः ।
वीर्यवानस्त्रविच्छूरः
प्रख्यातश्च पराक्रमे ॥
He is the army chief, a Rākshasa by name Prahasta. He is accompanied by three quarters of the total force of Laṅkā of the king of Rākshasas. He is valiant, heroic in temper, an expert in the use of weapons, and famed for his prowess.
6.58.5
ततः प्रहस्तं निर्यान्तम्
भीमं भीमपराक्रमम् ।
गर्जन्तं सुमहाकायम्
राक्षसैरभिसंवृतम् ।
ददर्श महती सेना
वानराणां बलीयसाम् ।
अतिसञ्जातरोषाणाम्
प्रहस्तमभिगर्जताम् ॥
On seeing the fierce Prahasta of formidable prowess and huge body march forth followed by his Rākshasas, letting off war-whoops, the huge and strong army of Vānaras became furious and shouted back at Prahasta.
6.58.7
खड्गशक्त्यृष्टिबाणाश्च
शूलानि मुसलानि च ।
गदाश्च परिघाः प्रासा
विविधाश्च परश्वधाः ।
धनूंषि च विचित्राणि
राक्षसानां जयैषिणाम् ।
प्रगृहीतान्यशोभन्त
वानरानभिधावताम् ॥
The swords, javelins, broadswords, arrows, spears, pestles, maces, Parighas, Prāsas, variety of axes and splendid bows wielded by the Rākshasas dazzled the eye as they, seeking victory, dashed to attack the Vānaras.
6.58.9
जगृहुः पादपांश्चापि
पुष्पितान् वानरर्षभाः ।
शिलाश्च विपुला दीर्घा
योद्धुकामाः प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
The bulls among Vānara chiefs snatched trees in flower and boulders that were long and big, eager to jump and fight.
6.58.10
तेषामन्योन्यमासाद्य
सङ्ग्रामः सुमहानभूत् ।
बहूनामश्मवृष्टिं च
शरवृष्टिं च वर्षताम् ॥
As they clashed with each other, there ensued a great battle and a downpour of boulders and arrows.
6.58.11
बहवो राक्षसा युद्धे
बहून् वानरयूथपान् ।
वानरा राक्षसांश्चापि
निजघ्नुर्बहवो बहून् ॥
In that battle, the innumerable Rākshasas slew innumerable Vānara warriors and the innumerable Vānaras slew innumerable Rākshasas.
6.58.12
शूलैः प्रमथिताः केचित्
केचिच्च परमायुधैः ।
परिघैराहताः केचित्
केचिच्छिन्नाः परश्वधैः ॥
Some were mangled by spears and some by the ultimate weapon. Some were struck down by Parighas and others were smashed by battle-axes.
6.58.13
निरुच्छ्वासाः कृताः केचित्
पतिता धरणीतले ।
विभिन्नहृदयाः केचित्
इषुसन्धानसन्दिताः ॥
Some, beaten out of breath, fell down on the ground. Others had their hearts rent, shot by the arrows.
6.58.14
केचिद्द्विधाकृताः खड्गैः
स्फुरन्तः पतिता भुवि ।
वानरा राक्षसैः शूलैः
पार्श्वतश्चावदारिताः ॥
Some cut into two by the sword, fell on the ground, writhing. The bodies of some Vānaras were torn sideways by the Rākshasas with spears.
6.58.15
वानरैश्चापि सङ्क्रुद्धै
राक्षसौघाः समन्ततः ।
पादपैर्गिरिशृङ्गैश्च
सम्पिष्टा वसुधातले ॥
And the furious Vānaras squashed the hosts of Rākshasas against the ground, with trees and mountain crests.
6.58.16
वज्रस्पर्शतलैर्हस्तैः
मुष्टिभिश्च हता भृशम् ।
वमन् शोणितमास्येभ्यो
विदीर्णवदनेक्षणाः ॥
Hit with the open palm that fell like a thunderbolt and pummeled with the fists (by the Vānaras), the Rākshasas vomited blood, while their faces and eyes were torn off.
6.58.17
आर्तस्वनं च स्वनताम्
सिंहनादं च नर्दताम् ।
बभूव तुमुलः शब्दो
हरीणां रक्षसां युधि ॥
With some roaring like lions and others wailing in distress there arose a cacophony of sounds of Vānaras and Rākshasas on that battlefield.
6.58.18
वानरा राक्षसाः क्रुद्धा
वीरमार्गमनुव्रताः ।
विवृत्तनयनाः क्रूराः
चक्रुः कर्माण्यभीतवत् ॥
The dreaded Vānaras and Rākshasas fought fearlessly, rolling their eyes in fury, with the true spirit of warriors.
6.58.19
नरान्तकः कुम्भहनुः
महानादः समुन्नतः ।
एते प्रहस्तसचिवाः
सर्वे जघ्नुर्वनौकसः ॥
Narāntaka, Kumbhahanu, Mahānāda and Samunnata, all of them lieutenants of Prahasta, slaughtered the Vana dwellers.
6.58.20
तेषामापततां शीघ्रम्
निघ्नतां चापि वानरान् ।
द्विविदो गिरिशृङ्गेण
जघानैकं नरान्तकम् ॥
As they rushed upon and slew the Vānaras, Dwivida slew one of them, Narāntaka, with a mountain crest.
6.58.21
दुर्मुखः पुनरुत्थाय
कपिः स विपुलद्रुमम् ।
राक्षसं क्षिप्रहस्तस्तु
समुन्नतमपोथयत् ॥
The Vānara Durmukha, who had a swift hand, snatched a big tree and pounded Samunnata with it.
6.58.22
जाम्बवांस्तु सुसङ्क्रुद्धः
प्रगृह्य महतीं शिलाम् ।
पातयामास तेजस्वी
महानादस्य वक्षसि ॥
The powerful Jāmbavān snatched a huge rock in fury and knocked Mahānāda on the chest with it.
6.58.23
अथ कुम्भहनुस्तत्र
तारेणासाद्य वीर्यवान् ।
वृक्षेणाभिहतो मूर्ध्नि
प्राणान् सन्त्याजयद्रणे ॥
Hit on the head with a tree hurled by Tāra, the valiant Kumbhahanu was relieved of his life in that battle.
6.58.24
अमृष्यमाणस्तत्कर्म
प्रहस्तो रथमास्थितः ।
चकार कदनं घोरम्
धनुष्पाणिर्वनौकसाम् ॥
Provoked to wrath by such feats, the chariot bound Prahasta, bow in hand, wreaked havoc on the Vānaras.
6.58.25
आवर्त इव सञ्जज्ञे
उभयोः सेनयोस्तदा ।
क्षुभितस्याप्रमेयस्य
सागरस्येव निस्वनः ॥
The din that arose from those two armies resembled that of the measureless ocean in turmoil at the time of Dissolution.
6.58.26
महता हि शरौघेण
प्रहस्तो युद्धकोविदः ।
अर्दयामास सङ्क्रुद्धो
वानरान् परमाहवे ॥
The enraged Prahasta, a seasoned warrior, harried the Vānaras in that tremendous battle with a huge flood of arrows.
6.58.27
वानराणां शरीरैश्च
राक्षसानां च मेदिनी ।
बभूव निचिता घोरा
पतितैरिव पर्वतैः ॥
With the bodies of Vānaras and Rākshasas strewn around like fallen mountains, the (battle) ground became a ghastly sight.
6.58.28
सा मही रुधिरौघेण
प्रच्छन्ना सम्प्रकाशते ।
सञ्छन्ना माधवे मासि
पलाशैरिव पुष्पितैः ॥
That battlefield, covered with the flood of gore, looked like the earth in spring months covered by Palāṡa flowers.
6.58.29
हतवीरौघवप्रां तु
भग्नायुधमहाद्रुमाम् ।
शोणितौघमहातोयाम्
यमसागरगमिनीम् ।
यकृत्प्लीहमहापङ्काम्
विनिकीर्णान्त्रशैवलाम् ।
भिन्नकायशिरोमीनाम्
अङ्गावयवशाद्वलाम् ।
गृध्रहंसगणाकीर्णाम्
कङ्कसारससेविताम् ।
मेदःफेनसमाकीर्णाम्
आर्तस्तनितनिस्वनाम् ।
तां कापुरुषदुस्ताराम्
युद्धभूमिमयीं नदीम् ।
नदीमिव घनापाये
हंससारससेविताम् ।
राक्षसाः कपिमुख्याश्च
तेरुस्तां दुस्तरां नदीम् ।
यथा पद्मरजोध्वस्ताम्
नलिनीं गजयूथपाः ॥
The Vānara chiefs and the Rākshasas waded through the battleground that looked like a river with blood for its immense body of water that seemed to be covered in lotus pollen, as easily as the lords of elephants do in a lotus pond.
It was not something that men of lesser mettle could hope to ford, having the bodies of fallen warriors for its banks, (smashed) livers and viscera for its mire, entrails strewn around and dismantled limbs for moss, broken heads and bodies for fish and broken weapons for its huge trees and fat for froth.
Vultures and eagles enjoyed hovering over it like swans and Sārasa birds do over rivers in the post-monsoon season.
It seemed to be headed for the ocean of death with the roar of the distraught cries of those in pain.
6.58.34
ततः सृजन्तं बाणौघान्
प्रहस्तं स्यन्दने स्थितम् ।
ददर्श तरसा नीलो
विनिघ्नन्तं प्लवङ्गमान् ॥
Then Neela saw Prahasta, seated in his chariot, shooting out floods of arrows and decimating the Vānaras with his might.
6.58.35
उद्धूत इव वायुः खे
महदभ्रबलं बलात् ।
समीक्ष्याभिद्रुतं युद्धे
प्रहस्तो वाहिनीपतिः ।
रथेनादित्यवर्णेन
नीलमेवाभिदुद्रुवे ॥
Prahasta, the Commander-in-Chief, seeing Neela rush towards him to fight with all his strength, like a strong gale in the sky chasing a huge bank of clouds, dashed straight against that Neela, in his sun-bright chariot in a counterattack.
6.58.36
स धनुर्धन्विनां श्रेष्ठो
विकृष्य परमाहवे ।
नीलाय व्यसृजद्बाणान्
प्रहस्तो वाहिनीपतिः ॥
Drawing his bow to the full, that foremost of bowmen shot his arrows at Neela in that fierce fight.
6.58.37
ते प्राप्य विशिखा नीलम्
विनिर्भिद्य समाहिताः ।
महीं जग्मुर्महावेगा
रुषिता इव पन्नगाः ॥
The fast-flying arrows fell simultaneously on Neela, pierced through his body like angry serpents and then plunged into the earth.
6.58.38
नीलः शरैरभिहतो
निशितैर्ज्वलनोपमैः ।
स तं परमदुर्धर्षम्
आपतन्तं महाकपिः ।
प्रहस्तं ताडयामास
वृक्षमुत्पाट्य वीर्यवान् ॥
On being struck by such sharp arrows that burnt him like fire, the great and valiant Vānara pulled out a tree and dealt a blow to the formidable Prahasta as he came on.
6.58.40
स तेनाभिहतः क्रुद्धो
नदन् राक्षसपुङ्गवः ।
ववर्ष शरवर्षाणि
प्लवङ्गानां चमूपतौ ॥
That bull among Rākshasas, incensed by the attack, roared, and shot a volley of arrows on the commander of the Vānara forces.
6.58.41
तस्य बाणगणान् घोरान्
राक्षसस्य महाबलः ।
अपारयन् वारयितुम्
प्रत्यगृह्णान्निमीलितः ॥
Unable to ward off the frightful arrows of the Rākshasa, he of immense strength simply shut his eyes and let them fall on him.
6.58.42
यथैव गोवृषो वर्षम्
शारदं शीघ्रमागतम् ।
एवमेव प्रहस्तस्य
शरवर्षं दुरासदम् ।
निमीलिताक्षः सहसा
नीलः सेहे सुदारुणम् ॥
With closed eyes, Neela took in that irresistible and dreadful torrent of arrows of Prahasta like a bull caught in a sporadic post-monsoon shower.
6.58.43
रोषितः शरवर्षेण
सालेन महता महान् ।
प्रजघान हयान्नीलः
प्रहस्तस्य मनोजवान् ॥
Enraged by that spate of arrows, Neela, the mighty Vānara, slew Prahasta’s horses which were swift as thought, with a huge Sāla tree.
6.58.44
ततः स चापमुद्गृह्य
प्रहस्तस्य महाबलः ।
बभञ्ज तरसा नीलो
ननाद च पुनःपुनः ॥
Springing up and snatching the bow from Prahasta’s hand, Neela, with his tremendous strength broke it, uttering strident shouts.
6.58.45
विधनुस्तु कृतस्तेन
प्रहस्तो वाहिनीपतिः ।
प्रगृह्य मुसलं घोरम्
स्यन्दनादवपुप्लुवे ॥
Deprived of his bow, Prahasta, the Commander-in-Chief, jumped down from his chariot, arming himself with a dread pestle.
6.58.46
तावुभौ वाहिनीमुख्यौ
जातवैरौ तरस्विनौ ।
स्थितौ क्षतजदिग्धाङ्गौ
प्रभिन्नाविव कुञ्जरौ ॥
The two powerful army chiefs, between whom arose (irreconcilable) enmity, faced each other like elephants in rut, their bodies drenched with blood oozing from the wounds.
6.58.47
उल्लिखन्तौ सुतीक्ष्णाभिः
दंष्ट्राभिरितरेतरम् ।
सिंहशार्दूलसदृशौ
सिंहशार्दूलचेष्टितौ ॥
They were like a lion and a tiger both in appearance and in their manner of fighting, tearing apart each other with their huge fangs.
6.58.48
विक्रान्तविजयौ वीरौ
समरेष्वनिवर्तिनौ ।
काङ्क्षमाणौ यशः प्राप्तुम्
वृत्रवासवयोः समौ ॥
Those valiant ones, who were ever victorious in every fray, and who never ran away from a fight, were resolved on winning renown equal to that of Vṛtra and Vāsava.
6.58.49
आजघान तदा नीलम्
ललाटे मुसलेन सः ।
प्रहस्तः परमायत्तः
तस्य सुस्राव शोणितम् ॥
Prahasta dealt a blow on Neela’s forehead with his pestle putting all his force behind it, when he (Neela) started bleeding heavily.
6.58.50
ततः शोणितदिग्धाङ्गः
प्रगृह्य सुमहातरुम् ।
प्रहस्तस्योरसि क्रुद्धो
विससर्ज महाकपिः ॥
The great Vānara, his body drenched in blood, pulled up a huge tree and hurled it at Prahasta’s breast in fury.
6.58.51
तमचिन्त्य प्रहारं स
प्रगृह्य मुसलं महत् ।
अभिदुद्राव बलिनम्
बलान्नीलं प्लवङ्गमम् ॥
Unmindful of the blow, he grabbed a huge pestle and rushed upon Neela, the strong Vānara with all his force.
6.58.52
तमुग्रवेगं संरब्धम्
आपतन्तं महाकपिः ।
ततः सम्प्रेक्ष्य जग्राह
महावेगो महाशिलाम् ॥
Seeing him come suddenly thus in great speed, the great Vānara of great speed grabbed a huge boulder.
6.58.53
तस्य युद्धाभिकामस्य
मृधे मुसलयोधिनः ।
प्रहस्तस्य शिलां नीलो
मूर्ध्नि तूर्णमपातयत् ॥
In that fight, Neela then lodged that boulder immediately on the head of Prahasta who was fighting zestfully armed with the pestle.
6.58.54
सा तेन कपिमुख्येन
विमुक्ता महती शिला ।
बिभेद बहुधा घोरा
प्रहस्तस्य शिरस्तदा ॥
That huge, dreadful boulder, which was thus hurled by the chief of the Vānaras shivered the head of Prahasta into many pieces.
6.58.55
स गतासुर्गतश्रीको
गतसत्त्वो गतेन्द्रियः ।
पपात सहसा भूमौ
छिन्नमूल इव द्रुमः ॥
With his life, luster, strength and senses lost, he tumbled down on the earth immediately like a tree axed at the root.
6.58.56
प्रभिन्नशिरसस्तस्य
बहु सुस्राव शोणितम् ।
शरीरादपि सुस्राव
गिरेः प्रस्रवणं यथा ॥
Great amounts of blood flowed from his shattered head and also from his body like a cascade from a mountain.
6.58.57
हते प्रहस्ते नीलेन
तदकम्प्यं महद्बलम् ।
रक्षसामप्रहृष्टानाम्
लङ्कामभिजगाम ह ॥
With Prahasta thus killed by Neela, the unshakable huge army of Rākshasas lost heart and headed back to Laṅkā.
6.58.58
न शेकुः समरे स्थातुम्
निहते वाहिनीपतौ ।
सेतुबन्धं समासाद्य
विकीर्णं सलिलं यथा ॥
With the leader of the army slain, they could not remain in the battlefield any more than water can (in its place) when the dam containing it is broken.
6.58.59
हते तस्मिंश्चमूमुख्ये
राक्षसास्ते निरुद्यमाः ।
रक्षःपतिगृहं गत्वा
ध्यानमूकत्वमास्थिताः ।
प्राप्ताः शोकार्णवं तीव्रम्
निस्सञ्ज्ञा इव तेऽभवन् ॥
With the chief of the army slain, those Rākshasas lost their will (to fight), went to the residence of the lord of Rākshasas, and stood in meditative silence.
Plunged in an agonizing ocean of grief, they looked as if they had become senseless.
6.58.61
ततस्तु नीलो विजयी महाबलः
प्रशस्यमानः स्वकृतेन कर्मणा ।
समेत्य रामेण सलक्ष्मणेन च
प्रहृष्टरूपस्तु बभूव यूथपः ॥
Neela of immense strength, the victorious chief, then joined Rāma and Lakshmaṇa. He felt quite flattered receiving accolades for his accomplishment.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे अष्टपञ्चाशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty eighth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
