Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 37
In this Sarga Māreeca makes it clear to Rāvaṇa that he is completely misinformed of Rāma’s strength as well as character. He tells Rāvaṇa that Rāma was not exiled by his father and he himself abjured the kingdom and luxuries to make his father a man of his word.
Māreeca warns that getting on the wrong side of Rāma is nothing short of courting death. He advices Rāvaṇa to take counsel from his ministers and take a decision after weighing all the pros and cons, and taking an honest count of his own strengths and weaknesses and that of Rāma.
3.37.1
तच्छृत्वा राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
वाक्यं वाक्यविशारदः ।
प्रत्युवाच महाप्राज्ञो
मारीचो राक्षसेश्वरम् ॥
Hearing those words of the lord of Rākshasas, the mighty strong Māreeca, who was good with his choice of words responded to that ruler of Rākshasas saying:
3.37.2
सुलभाः पुरुषा राजन्
सततं प्रियवादिनः ।
अप्रियस्य तु पथ्यस्य
वक्ता श्रोता च दुर्लभः ॥
It is easy to find, O king, people who always talk sweetly! But when it comes to bitter truths that are not so pleasing to the ears, it is hard to find one who would venture to say it and one who would be willing to listen.
3.37.3
न नूनं बुध्यसे रामं
महावीर्यं गुणोन्नतम् ।
अयुक्तचारश्चपलो
महेन्द्रवरुणोपमम् ॥
Certainly, you do not seem to know Rāma. You are imagining things, lacking good intelligence. He is of extraordinary valor and lofty character and is comparable to the great Indra and Varuṇa.
3.37.4
अपि स्वस्ति भवेत्तात
सर्वेषां भुवि रक्षसाम् ।
अपि रामो सुसङ्क्रुद्धः
कुर्याल्लोकमराक्षसम् ॥
My dear, I hope everything bodes well for all the Rākshasas of the earth! I hope Rāma does not get enraged to the extent of eradicating all Rākshasas of the world!
3.37.5
अपि ते जीवितान्ताय
नोत्पन्ना जनकात्मजा ।
अपि सीतानिमित्तं च
न भवेद्व्यसनं मम ॥
I hope the daughter of Janaka is not born to bring an end to your life. And I hope I will not have to fall into trouble because of Seetā.
3.37.6
अपि त्वामीश्वरं प्राप्य
कामवृत्तं निरङ्कुशम् ।
न विनश्येत्पुरी लङ्का
त्वया सह सराक्षसा ॥
I hope, having got you, a wayward who knows no restraint, as its king, the city of Laṅkā with all the Rākshasas, does not perish along with you!
3.37.7
त्वद्विधः कामवृत्तो हि
दुश्शीलः पापमन्त्रितः ।
आत्मानं स्वजनं राष्ट्रं
स राजा हन्ति दुर्मतिः ॥
A king like you, a philanderer, a wicked one, a man of bad character and evil thoughts, certainly destroys himself, his people and his realm.
3.37.8
न च पित्रा परित्यक्तो
नामर्यादः कथञ्चन ।
न लुब्धो न च दुश्शीलो
न च क्षत्रियपांसनः ।
न च धर्मगुणैर्हीनः
कौसल्यानन्दवर्धनः ।
न तीक्ष्णो न च भूतानां
सर्वेषामहिते रतः ॥
Neither has he been abandoned by his father, nor does he lack restraint. Neither is he greedy, nor is he of bad character. Neither is he a wretched Kshatriya, nor does he lack Dharma or virtues. The delight of Kousalyā is neither abrasive nor takes any delight in harming all beings.
3.37.10
वञ्चितं पितरं दृष्ट्वा
कैकेय्या सत्यवादिनम् ।
करिष्यामीति धर्मात्मा
तात प्रव्रजितो वनम् ॥
My dear! Seeing his truth-abiding father duped by Kaikēyee, the Dharmātma said he would make the word come true, and left for the Vana.
Most succinctly put!
3.37.11
कैकेय्याः प्रियकामार्थं
पितुर्दशरथस्य च ।
हित्वा राज्यं च भोगांश्च
प्रविष्टो दण्डकावनम् ॥
To fulfill the dear wish of Kaikēyee and that of his father Daṡaratha, he abjured the kingdom and the luxuries and came to the Vana of Daṇḍaka.
3.37.12
न रामः कर्कशस्तात
नाविद्वान्नाजितेन्द्रियः ।
अनृतं दुश्श्रुतं चैव
नैव त्वं वक्तुमर्हसि ॥
Neither is Rāma a savage, nor is he unlettered, nor is he not a master of his senses. You should not utter lies and spread slander.
3.37.13
रामो विग्रहवान् धर्मः
साधुस्सत्यपराक्रमः ।
राजा सर्वस्य लोकस्य
देवानां मघवानिव ॥
Rāma is an embodiment of Dharma. He is a good man and a man of true valor. He is the king of all worlds like Indra is of all the Dēvas.
3.37.14
कथं त्वं तस्य वैदेहीं
रक्षितां स्वेन तेजसा ।
इच्छसि प्रसभं हर्तुं
प्रभामिव विवस्वतः ॥
How can you think of snatching Vaidēhee while she is being protected by his power? It is like snatching sunlight away from sun!
3.37.15
शरार्चिषमनाधृष्यं
चापखङ्गेन्धनं रणे ।
रामाग्निं सहसा दीप्तं
न प्रवेष्टुं त्वमर्हसि ॥
You should not hurry to throw yourself into the invincible blazing fire that is Rāma, which has arrows for tongues of flame and bow and sword for fuel!
3.37.16
धनुर्व्यादितदीप्तास्यं
शरार्चिषममर्षणम् ।
चापबाणधरं तीक्ष्णं
शत्रुसैन्यप्रहारिणम् ।
राज्यं सुखं च सन्त्यज्य
जीवितं चेष्टमात्मनः ।
नात्यासादयितुं तात
रामान्तकमिहार्हसि ॥
Throwing away your kingdom, comforts, life and all else that you hold dear, you should not rush headlong towards death in the guise of Rāma, who has a bow that is like a wide open flaming mouth throwing out arrows like sparks, and who decimates enemy forces in his rage and wrath, wielding that bow and arrows.
3.37.18
अप्रमेयं हि तत्तेजो
यस्य सा जनकात्मजा ।
न त्वं समर्थस्तां हर्तुं
रामचापाश्रयं वने ॥
Measureless is the strength of him who has that daughter of Janaka for his own. You are not capable of carrying her off who is in the Vana under the protection of Rāma’s bow.
3.37.19
तस्य सा नरसिंहस्य
सिंहोरस्कस्य भामिनी ।
प्राणेभ्योऽपि प्रियतरा
भार्या नित्यमनुव्रता ॥
That lady is an ever devoted wife of that lion among men, who has a leonine chest. She is dearer to him than his own life.
3.37.20
न सा धर्षयितुं शक्या
मैथिल्योजस्विनः प्रिया ।
दीप्तस्येव हुताशस्य
शिखा सीता सुमध्यमा ॥
That princess of Mithilā, of slender waist, the darling of the supremely radiant (Rāma), can not be held any more than the flames of a blazing fire can be.
3.37.21
किमुद्यममिमं व्यर्थं
कृत्वा ते राक्षसाधिप ।
दृष्टश्चेत्वं रणे तेन
तदन्तं तव जीवितम् ॥
What is the point of this futile effort, O king of Rākshasas! It would be the end of your life when he sets his eyes upon you in battle.
3.37.22
जीवितं च सुखं चैव
राज्यं चैव सुदुर्लभम् ।
यदीच्छसि चिरं भोक्तुं
मा कृथा रामविप्रियम् ॥
If you like to enjoy for a while this life, comforts and kingdom, which are not easy to obtain, do not get on the wrong side of Rāma.
3.37.23
स सर्वैस्सचिवैस्सार्धम्
विभीषणपुरोगमैः ।
मन्त्रयित्वा तु धर्मिष्ठैः
कृत्वा निश्चयमात्मनः ।
दोषाणां च गुणानां च
सम्प्रधार्य बलाबलम् ।
आत्मनश्च बलं ज्ञात्वा
राघवस्य च तत्वतः ।
हिताहितं विनिश्चित्य
क्षमं त्वं कर्तुमर्हसि ॥
Hence you should take a firm decision after getting counsel from Vibheeshaṇa and other ministers who are Dharmātmas and weighing the pros and cons. You should take an honest count of your strengths and weaknesses and those of Rāghava. You should figure out which is good and which is not, and act accordingly.
3.37.25
अहं तु मन्ये तव न क्षमं रणे
समागमं कोसलराजसूनुना ।
इदं हि भूयः शृणु वाक्यमुत्तमं 
क्षमं च युक्तं च निशाचरेश्वर ॥
As for me, I do not think it is wise for you to face the prince of Kōsala kingdom in a fight. O lord of Night-rovers, I will tell you one more thing that is relevant, apt and better. Please listen again to these words:
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे सप्तत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty seventh Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
