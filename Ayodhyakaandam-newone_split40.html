Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 40
In this Sarga, after paying obeisance to Daṡaratha, Kousalyā and Sumitrā, Seetā, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa get on the chariot and leave for the Vana. Daṡaratha arranges for enough fine clothes and ornaments for Seetā, to last for the duration of her stay in Vana. He places many armaments, shields, gloves and vests for the brothers, in the chariot.
As Rāma departs, the entire city cries. Rāma, unable to see them all crying, and unwilling to prolong their misery, urges the charioteer Sumantra to drive the chariot fast. The king's people turn back after following Rāma for a distance.
2.40.1
अथ रामश्च सीता च
लक्ष्मणश्च कृताञ्जलिः ।
उपसङ्गृह्य राजानम्
चक्रुर्दीनाः प्रदक्षिणम् ॥
Then Rāma, Seetā and Lakshmaṇa paid respects to the King by touching his feet. With heavy hearts, they circumambulated him with palms joined in reverence.
2.40.2
तं चापि समनुज्ञाप्य
धर्मज्ञस्सीतया सह ।
राघवश्शोकसम्मूढो
जननीमभ्यवादयत् ॥
Rāma, the knower of Dharma, after duly obtaining his father's permission (to leave), with his mind stupefied by grief, paid obeisance to his mother, accompanied by Seetā.
2.40.3
अन्वक्षं लक्ष्मणो भ्रातुः
कौसल्यामभ्यवादयत् ।
अथ मातु स्सुमित्राया
जग्राह चरणौ पुनः ॥
Following his brother, Lakshmaṇa also paid obeisance to Kousalyā, and then he clasped the feet of his mother Sumitrā.
2.40.4
तं वन्दमानं रुदती
माता सौमित्रिमब्रवीत् ।
हितकामा महाबाहुम्
मूर्ध्न्युपाघ्राय लक्ष्मणम् ॥
Mother Sumitrā, wishing her son Lakshmaṇa of mighty arm all well, smelled his head as he prostrated, and said, crying:
2.40.5
सृष्टस्त्वं वनवासाय
स्वनुरक्तस्सुहृज्जने ।
रामे प्रमादं मा कार्षीः
पुत्र भ्रातरि गच्छति ॥
My dear son! You are extremely committed to the well being of the people you love. You seem to have born destined to live in Vana. Never be inattentive towards your brother Rāma who is on his way to Vana.
The first sentence here defines the essence of Lakshmaṇa's character quite succinctly. Those specific words coming from his own mother, makes them that much more authentic.
People of such character are becoming rarer these days. Even the few who are still there, are not respected or valued by the current society and the value system.
The system tells everyone to take care of themselves first and then the others, optionally.
2.40.6
व्यसनी वा समृद्धो वा
गतिरेष तवानघ ।
एष लोके सतां धर्मो
यज्ज्येष्ठवशगो भवेत् ॥
O sinless one! He is your ultimate recourse regardless of whether he is facing hardships or enjoying riches. That is the Dharma for all good people in this world, to be obedient to the brother, who is older.
2.40.7
इदं हि वृत्तमुचितम्
कुलस्यास्य सनातनम् ।
दानं दीक्षा च यज्ञेषु
तनुत्यागो मृधेषु च ॥
Performing charity, conducting Yajñas, being bound by one's custom, and giving up one's life in a good fight are considered to be appropriate and characteristic of your lineage.
2.40.8
लक्ष्मणं त्वेवमुक्त्वा सा
संसिद्धं प्रियराघवम् ।
सुमित्रा गच्छ गच्छेति
पुनः पुनरुवाच तम् ॥
After saying thus, Sumitrā repeated to Lakshmaṇa, who was ready to leave with his beloved brother Rāghava: 'May you go now!'
2.40.9
रामं दशरथं विध्दि
मां विध्दि जनकात्मजाम् ।
अयोध्यामटवीं विध्दि
गच्छ तात यथासुखम् ॥
Look upon Rāma as Daṡaratha himself, on the daughter of Janaka as me, and on the forest as Ayōdhyā. Go, my child, and may you be happy!
2.40.10
ततः सुमन्त्रः काकुत्स्थम्
प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यमब्रवीत् ।
विनीतो विनयज्ञश्च
मातलिर्वासवं यथा ॥
Then Sumantra, who was well versed with the demands of courtesy, said to the prince of Kākutstha lineage with palms joined in all deference, as Mātali might say to Vāsava:
Vāsava is another name for Indra. Mātali is Indra's charioteer.
2.40.11
रथमारोह भद्रं ते
राजपुत्र महायशः ।
क्षिप्रं त्वां प्रापयिष्यामि
यत्र मां राम वक्ष्यसि ॥
O Prince of great renown! Please mount the chariot! May all bode well for you! I shall drop you swiftly, O Rāma, wherever you tell me to!
2.40.12
चतुर्दश हि वर्षाणि
वस्तव्यानि वने त्वया ।
तान्युपक्रमितव्यानि
यानि देव्यासि चोदितः ॥
You now have to begin the fourteen years that the queen has demanded of you, to live in the Vana!
2.40.13
तं रथं सूर्यसङ्काशम्
सीता हृष्टेन चेतसा ।
आरुरोह वरारोहा
कृत्वालङ्कारमात्मनः ॥
Seetā of exquisite hips decorated herself and with cheerful mind mounted the chariot, which was gleaming like the sun.
2.40.14
अथो ज्वलनसङ्काशम्
चामीकरविभूषितम् ।
तमारुरुहतुस्तूर्णम्
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
Then both the brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa quickly mounted the chariot which was decorated fully with gold, and was shining like a blazing fire.
2.40.15
वनवासं हि संख्याय
वासांस्याभरणानि च ।
भर्तारमनुगच्छन्त्यै
सीतायै श्वशुरो ददौ ॥
The father-in-law then gave Seetā, who was leaving with her husband fine clothes and ornaments, enough for the number of years that she would live in the Vana.
2.40.16
तथैवायुधजालानि
भ्रातृभ्यां कवचानि च ।
रथोपस्थे प्रतिन्यस्य
सचर्म कठिनं च तत् ॥
He then placed, in the middle of the chariot, many armaments, shields, sturdy hand gloves and protective vests for the brothers.
2.40.17
सीतातृतीयानारूढान्
दृष्ट्वा दृष्टमचोदयत् ।
सुमन्त्रस्सम्मतानश्वान्
वायुवेगसमान्जवे ॥
Seeing that they, three in all with Seetā, had got in, Sumantra spurred those splendid horses whose speed rivals that of the wind.
2.40.18
प्रतियाते महारण्यम्
चिररात्राय राघवे ।
बभूव नगरे मूर्छा
बलमूर्छा जनस्य च ॥
When Rāghava started thus on his long sojourn to live in the Vana, the entire city was stupefied and all the people lost their vitality and strength.
Vālmeeki  is very visual as we noted a few times earlier.
Herein he gives a graphic description of the scene of Rāma's departure from the city, capturing the mood of the entire city.
2.40.19
तत्समाकुलसम्भ्रान्तम्
मत्तसङ्कुपितद्विपम् ।
हयशिञ्जितनिर्घोषम्
पुरमासीन्महास्वनम् ॥
There arose a great uproar from the din of people in a state of violent agitation, from the raging elephants in rut and and from the jingling of horses' trappings.
2.40.20
तत स्सबालवृद्धा सा
पुरी परमपीडिता ।
राममेवाभिदुद्राव
घर्मार्ता सलिलं यथा ॥
The entire city including the children and the aged, tormented by grief, ran after Rāma, as those afflicted by the oppressive heat of summer would run for water.
2.40.21
पार्श्वतः पृष्ठतश्चैव
लम्बमानास्तदुन्मुखाः ।
बाष्पपूर्णमुखास्सर्वे
तमूचुर्भृशनिस्वनाः ॥
Hanging on to the sides and the rear of the chariot, with their eyes full of tears and their gaze fixed on him, all of them wailed aloud saying:
2.40.22
संयच्छ वाजिनां रश्मीन्
सूत याहि शनैश्शनैः ।
मुखं द्रक्ष्याम रामस्य
दुर्दर्शं नो भविष्यति ॥
O charioteer! Hold the reins of the horses and go slow so that we may get a good glance of the face of Rāma, for soon we will not be able to see him.
2.40.23
आयसं हृदयं नूनम्
राममातुरसंशयम् ।
यद्देवगर्भप्रतिमे
वनं याति न भिद्यते ॥
Rāma's mother must have a heart of iron, for sure! Or else, how did it not break into pieces, seeing a son, who is like a youthful god, go to Vana!
2.40.24
कृतकृत्या हि वैदेही
छायेवानुगता पतिम् ।
न जहाति रता धर्मे
मेरुमर्कप्रभा यथा ॥
The princess of Vidēha, fully devoted to Dharma, is following her husband like a shadow and not leaving him, like the sunlight that never leaves the Mēru mountain. She is, indeed, fortunate!
2.40.25
अहो लक्ष्मण सिद्धार्थ
स्सततं प्रियवादिनम् ।
भ्रातरं देवसङ्काशम्
यस्त्वं परिचरिष्यसि ॥
Ah Lakshmaṇa! You are indeed fortunate that you will be able to serve your brother who is like a god and speaks pleasantly at all times!
2.40.26
महत्येषा हि ते सिध्दिः
एष चाभ्युदयो महान् ।
एष स्वर्गस्य मार्गश्च
यदेनमनुगच्छसि ॥
It must indeed be greatly fulfilling for you that you are able to follow (him) like this. This must be considered as the greatest of fortunes and a doorway to Heaven.
2.40.27
एवं वदन्तस्ते सोढुम्
न शेकुर्बाष्पमागतम् ।
नरास्तमनुगच्छन्तः
प्रियमिक्ष्वाकुनन्दनम् ॥
Saying thus, the people who were following their beloved Rāma, the delight of Ikshwāku dynasty, could not hold back the tears flowing from their eyes.
2.40.28
अथ राजा वृत स्त्रीभिः
दीनाभिर्दीनचेतनः ।
निर्जगाम प्रियं पुत्रम्
द्रक्ष्यामीति ब्रुवन् गृहात् ॥
Then the king, feeling miserable and surrounded by women who were equally miserable, came out of his house saying, "let me take a look at my beloved son!"
2.40.29
शुश्रुवे चाग्रतः स्त्रीणाम्
रुदन्तीनां महास्वनः ।
यथा नादः करेणूनाम्
बद्धे महति कुञ्जरे ॥
He heard the loud wailing of women ahead of him, which resembled the trumpeting of female elephants when their great male is taken captive.
2.40.30
पिता हि राजा काकुत्स्थः
श्रीमान् सन्नस्तदाऽभवत् ।
परिपूर्णः शशी काले
ग्रहेणोपप्लुतो यथा ॥
And that father, the blessed king of Kākutstha dynasty, was sunk in gloom like the full moon at the time of eclipse.
2.40.31
स च श्रीमानचिन्त्यात्मा
रामो दशरथात्मजः ।
सूतं सञ्चोदयामास
त्वरितं वाह्यतामिति ॥
That blessed soul of unimaginable aplomb then urged the charioteer to drive faster.
2.40.32
रामो याहीति सूतं तम्
तिष्ठेति स जनस्तदा ।
उभयं नाशकत्सूतः
कर्तुमध्वनि चोदितः ॥
With Rāma urging him to move forward, and the people crying out to him to hold back, the charioteer found himself unable to do both things at the same time.
2.40.33
निर्गच्छति महाबाहौ
रामे पौरजनाश्रुभिः ।
पतितैरभ्यवहितम्
प्रशशाम महीरजः ॥
As Rāma of mighty arm kept moving on, the dust that rose from the earth was subsided with the tears of the citizens.
2.40.34
रुदिताश्रुपरिद्यूनम्
हाहाकृतमचेतनम् ।
प्रयाणे राघवस्यासीत्
पुरं परमपीडितम् ॥
The entire city, at that time of Rāma's departure, was stupefied and extremely distressed. It was drenched by tears completely. And its heart cried out uncontrollably.
2.40.35
सुस्राव नयनैः स्त्रीणाम्
अस्रमायाससम्भवम् ।
मीनसङ्क्षोभचलितै
स्सलिलं पङ्कजैरिव ॥
Tears rolled down from the eyes of women, like water drops from lotuses that were shaken by the fish.
2.40.36
दृष्ट्वा तु नृपति श्श्रीमान्
एकचित्तगतं पुरम् ।
निपपातैव दुःखेन
हतमूल इव द्रुमः ॥
Seeing the entire city grieving in unison, the blessed king fell on the floor overcome by grief, like a tree severed at its root.
2.40.37
ततो हलहलाशब्दो
जज्ञे रामस्य पृष्ठतः ।
नराणां प्रेक्ष्य राजानम्
सीदन्तं भृशदुःखितम् ॥
Then a tumultuous uproar from the people arose in the trail that was left by Rāma, when people saw the king becoming enfeebled out of overwhelming grief.
2.40.38
हा रामेति जनाः केचित्
राममातेति चापरे ।
अन्तःपुरं समृद्धं च
क्रोशन्तः पर्यदेवयन् ॥
"O dear Rāma!" cried out some, "O dear mother of Rāma!" cried others, making everyone in the inner quarters also cry.
2.40.39
अन्वीक्षमाणो रामस्तु
विषण्णं भ्रान्तचेतसम् ।
राजानं मातरं चैव
ददर्शानुगतौ पथि ॥
Rāma turned his head and saw the extremely distressed and completely disoriented king and mother following him down the street.
2.40.40
स बद्ध इव पाशेन
किशोरो मातरं यथा ।
धर्मपाशेन सङ्क्षिप्तः
प्रकाशं नाभ्युदैक्षत ॥
Like a foal that is tied down cannot behold its mother, he could not take a good look at his parents bound by the harness of Dharma.
2.40.41
पदातिनौ च यानार्हौ
अदुःखार्हौ सुखोचितौ ।
दृष्ट्वा सञ्चोदयामास
शीघ्रं याहीति सारथिम् ॥
Unable to behold those who were used only to riding on a chariot come on foot, and unable to see those who were used only to happiness and joy become completely distressed, he urged the charioteer to drive away fast.
2.40.42
न हि तत्पुरुषव्याघ्रो
दुःखदं दर्शनं पितुः ।
मातुश्च सहितुं शक्तः
तोत्रार्दित इव द्विपः ॥
That tiger among men could not bear that distressing sight of his father and mother, as it hurt like a goad would an elephant.
2.40.43
प्रत्यगारमिवायान्ती
वत्सला वत्सकारणात् ।
बद्धवत्सा यथा धेनू
राममाताभ्यधावत ॥
Like a loving cow returning to its pen runs to its calf that is tethered, the mother of Rāma ran towards him.
2.40.44
तथा रुदन्तीं कौसल्याम्
रथं तमनुधावतीम् ।
क्रोशन्तीं राम रामेति
हा सीते लक्ष्मणेति च ।
रामलक्ष्मणसीतार्थम्
स्रवन्तीं वारि नेत्रजम् ।
असकृत्प्रैक्षत तदा
नृत्यन्तीमिव मातरम् ॥
Then he repeatedly glanced at his mother, who was crying and running after the chariot, wailing "Hey Rāma, Rāma! Hey Seetā and Lakshmaṇa!", shedding tears for Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Seetā with her gait out of step, as if she was dancing.
2.40.46
तिष्ठेति राजा चुक्रोश
याहि याहीति राघवः ।
सुमन्त्रस्य बभूवात्मा
चक्रयोरिव चान्तरा ॥
With the king shouting 'stop!' and Rāghava shouting 'go, go!', Sumantra felt as if he was caught between two wheels.
2.40.47
नाश्रौषमिति राजानम्
उपालब्धोऽपि वक्ष्यसि ।
चिरं दुःखस्य पापिष्ठम्
इति रामस्तमब्रवीत् ॥
"You can tell the king that you could not hear him, if you were to be reproached. But prolonging this grief is a sin in itself!" said Rāma to him.
2.40.48
रामस्य च वचः कुर्वन्
अनुज्ञाप्य च तं जनम् ।
व्रजतोऽपि हयान् शीघ्रम्
चोदयामास सारथिः ॥
Following the words of Rāma, and convincing the people that were out there, the charioteer flogged the horses that were running, to make them run even faster.
2.40.49
न्यवर्तत जनो राज्ञो
रामं कृत्वा प्रदक्षिणम् ।
मनसाप्यश्रुवेगैश्च
न न्यवर्तत मानुषम् ॥
The king's people finally turned back after circumambulating Rāma, but not their minds nor the flow of their tears.
2.40.50
यमिच्छेत्पुनरायान्तम्
नैनं दूरमनुव्रजेत् ।
इत्यमात्या महाराजम्
ऊचुर्दशरथं वचः ॥
"Whomever we wish to see returning soon, should not be followed too far!" said the ministers to the great king Daṡaratha.
2.40.51
तेषां वचः सर्वगुणोपपन्नं
प्रस्विन्नगात्रः प्रविषण्णरूपः ।
निशम्य राजा कृपणः सभार्यो
व्यवस्थितस्तं सुतमीक्षमाणः ॥
Listening to their wise counsel, the woebegone King, his body bathed in sweat, his semblance giving shape to grief itself, stopped where he was with his wives, looking at the son (who was departing).
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे चत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fortieth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
