Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 51
In this Sarga, Hanumān delivers the message of Sugreeva to Rāvaṇa, warning him that he cannot expect to hold on to his life, having committed such a grave offense against Rāma, and tells him that it is in his interest to immediately return Seetā. He warns Rāvaṇa in strong words, saying that he is holding a five-hooded serpent and a fire in his hand in the form of Seetā that will burn Laṅkā, destroying all his kith and kin and everything that there is. Hearing those words of Hanumān and extremely enraged, Rāvaṇa orders him to be killed.
5.51.1
तं समीक्ष्य महासत्त्वम्
सत्त्ववान्हरिसत्तमः ।
वाक्यमर्थवदव्यग्रः
तमुवाच दशाननम् ॥
Looking steadily at that mighty strong Ten-faced, the powerful and eminent Vānara said these poignant words with no hesitation whatsoever:
5.51.2
अहं सुग्रीवसंदेशात्
इह प्राप्तस्तवालयम् ।
राक्षसेन्द्र हरीशस्त्वाम्
भ्राता कुशलमब्रवीत् ॥
I came here to your abode by the orders of Sugreeva. O Lord of Rākshasas! The Lord of Vānaras, a brother to you, inquires your wellbeing.
5.51.3
भ्रातुश्शृणु समादेशम्
सुग्रीवस्य महात्मनः ।
धर्मार्थोपहितं वाक्यम्
इह चामुत्र च क्षमम् ॥
Listen to this message from your brother, the Mahātma Sugreeva, that conforms to Dharma and serves Artha meant for your welfare here and hereafter.
5.51.4
राजा दशरथो नाम
रथकुञ्जरवाजिमान् ।
पितेव बन्धुर्लोकस्य
सुरेश्वरसमद्युतिः ॥
There was a famous king by name Daṡaratha, who possessed a great army consisting of chariot and elephant forces and cavalry.
He was like a father in his solicitude towards all creatures and equal in splendor to the Lord of the Dēvas.
5.51.5
ज्येष्ठस्तस्य महाबाहुः
पुत्रः प्रियकरः प्रभुः ।
पितुर्निदेशान्निष्क्रान्तः
प्रविष्टो दण्डकावनम् ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
सीतया चापि भार्यया ।
रामो नाम महातेजा
धर्म्यं पन्थानमास्थितः ॥
His eldest and lordly son of mighty arm who (always) does what pleases (everyone), departed (the kingdom) at his father’s bidding and entered the Daṇḍaka Vana along with his brother Lakshmaṇa and wife Seetā. He is of immense strength and his name is Rāma, who always adheres to the path of Dharma.
5.51.7
तस्य भार्या वने नष्टा
सीता पतिमनुव्रता ।
वैदेहस्य सुता राज्ञो
जनकस्य महात्मनः ॥
His wife Seetā, daughter of Mahātma Janaka, the mighty King of Vidēha, who followed her husband, has been lost in the Vana.
5.51.8
स मार्गमाणस्तां देवीम्
राजपुत्रः सहानुजः ।
ऋश्यमूकमनुप्राप्तः
सुग्रीवेण समागतः ॥
While searching for that lady the prince, along with his younger brother, came to Ṛshyamūka and met Sugreeva.
5.51.9
तस्य तेन प्रतिज्ञातम्
सीतायाः परिमार्गणम् ।
सुग्रीवस्यापि रामेण
हरिराज्यं निवेदितम् ॥
Sugreeva promised to search for Seetā. (In turn), Rāma also pledged the (retrieval of) the Vānara kingdom to Sugreeva.
5.51.10
ततस्तेन मृधे हत्वा
राजपुत्रेण वालिनम् ।
सुग्रीवः स्थापितो राज्ये
हर्यृक्षाणां गणेश्वरः ॥
With Vāli killed by the prince in fight, Sugreeva was established in the kingdom as the lord of the hordes of Vānaras and Ṛkshas.
5.51.11
त्वया विज्ञातपूर्वश्च
वाली वानरपुङ्गवः ।
रामेण निहतस्सङ्ख्ये
शरेणैकेन वानरः ॥
Vāli, a bull among Vānaras, who was well known to you since early on, was killed by Rāma in the battle with the strike of a single arrow.
5.51.12
स सीतामार्गणे व्यग्रः
सुग्रीवः सत्यसङ्गरः ।
हरीन् सम्प्रेषयामास
दिशस्सर्वा हरीश्वरः ॥
Being true to his word, Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras, sent Vānaras in all directions, being keen on the search for Seetā.
5.51.13
तां हरीणां सहस्राणि
शतानि नियुतानि च ।
दिक्षु सर्वासु मार्गन्ते
ह्यधश्चोपरि चाम्बरे ॥
Thousands, hundreds and lakhs of apes are looking for her in every direction, on the earth below and in the sky above.
5.51.14
वैनतेयसमाः केचित्
केचित्तत्रानिलोपमाः ।
असङ्गगतयश्शीघ्रा
हरिवीरा महाबलाः ॥
Some of them are equals of Vainatēya and some are comparable to the wind. There is nothing that can stop those swift and mighty strong Vānara Veeras.
5.51.15
अहं तु हनुमान्नाम
मारुतस्यौरसस्सुतः ।
सीतायास्तु कृते तूर्णम्
शतयोजनमायतम् ।
समुद्रं लङ्घयित्वैव
तां दिदृक्षुरिहागतः ।
भ्रमता च मया दृष्टा
गृहे ते जनकात्मजा ॥
As for me, my name is Hanumān and I am born from the seed of Vāyu. For the sake of Seetā, I quickly hopped over the hundred Yōjana wide ocean, and came here very much to see her. And as I roamed about, I saw the daughter of Janaka in your house.
5.51.17
तद्भवान् दृष्टधर्मार्थः
तपःकृतपरिग्रहः ।
परदारान् महाप्राज्ञ
नोपरोद्धुं त्वमर्हसि ॥
You know all about Dharma and Artha and you have done Tapa, gaining its fruits. One, as extremely wise as you, should not restrain another man’s wife by force.
5.51.18
न हि धर्मविरुद्धेषु
बह्वपायेषु कर्मसु ।
मूलघातिषु सज्जन्ते
बुद्धिमन्तो भवद्विधाः ॥
Astute ones like you would not engage in deeds that are contrary to Dharma and associated with many perils that are bound to strike at your very root.
5.51.19
कश्च लक्ष्मणमुक्तानाम्
रामकोपानुवर्तिनाम् ।
शराणामग्रतः स्थातुम्
शक्तो देवासुरेष्वपि ॥
Who is there even among the Dēvas or the Asuras who can stand in the path of the arrows shot by Lakshmaṇa, that follow the tracks of Rāma’s wrath?
5.51.20
न चापि त्रिषु लोकेषु
राजन्विद्येत कश्चन ।
राघवस्य व्यलीकं यः
कृत्वा सुखमवाप्नुयात् ॥
There is no one in the three worlds, O King, who committed an offense against Rāghava and found peace.
5.51.21
तत् त्रिकालहितं वाक्यम्
धर्म्यमर्थानुबन्धि च ।
मन्यस्व नरदेवाय
जानकी प्रतिदीयताम् ॥
I hope you appreciate these words, which bode well for your past, present and future and which are grounded in Dharma and serve Artha! May Jānaki be returned to that Dēva among men!
5.51.22
दृष्टा हीयं मया देवी
लब्धं यदिह दुर्लभम् ।
उत्तरं कर्म यच्छेषम्
निमित्तं तत्र राघवः ॥
As impossible as (I thought) it could be, I have seen the lady here. Whatever is left to be done now, is up to Rāghava.
5.51.23
लक्षितेयं मया सीता
तथा शोकपरायणा ।
गृह्य यां नाभिजानासि
पञ्चास्यामिव पन्नगीम् ॥
I have seen how extremely sad Seetā is. You do not realize that you have captured a five-hooded female serpent.
5.51.24
नेयं जरयितुं शक्या
सासुरैरमरैरपि ।
विषसंसृष्टमत्यर्थम्
भुक्तमन्नमिवौजसा ॥
No one is powerful enough to digest her, be it Amaras with help from Asuras, any more than one can, any food mixed with copious amounts of poison.
5.51.25
तपस्सन्तापलब्धस्ते
योऽयं धर्मपरिग्रहः ।
न स नाशयितुं न्याय्य
आत्मप्राणपरिग्रहः ॥
It is not proper for you to ruin this long lease of life you have gained by putting yourself through harsh Tapa and through the deeds of Dharma.
5.51.26
अवध्यतां तपोभिर्याम्
भवान् समनुपश्यति ।
आत्मनः सासुरैर्देवैः
हेतुस्तत्राप्ययं महान् ॥
Perhaps you think of the immunity from death that you earned through your Tapa from the hands of the Dēvas and Asuras but that left you with a bigger reason (to worry).
This Ṡlōka refers to Rāvaṇa asking Lord Ṡiva for the boon of immunity from death in the hands of the Dēvas and Asuras and, in his arrogance, not in the hands of humans and Vānaras. Hence, it is a reason for him to worry, now that his death in the hands of Rāma and Sugreeva are imminent.
5.51.27
सुग्रीवो न हि देवोऽयम्
नासुरो न च राक्षसः ।
न दानवो न गन्धर्वो
न यक्षो न च पन्नगः ।
तस्मात्प्राणपरित्राणम्
कथं राजन्करिष्यसि ॥
This Sugreeva is neither a Dēva, nor an Asura, nor a Rākshasa, nor a Dānava, nor a Gandharva, nor a Yaksha, nor a Pannaga; how would you save yourself from him, O king?
5.51.28
न तु धर्मोपसंहारम्
अधर्मफलसंहितम् ।
तदेव फलमन्वेति
धर्मश्चाधर्मनाशनः ॥
The fruits of Dharma are never enjoyed in conjunction with the fruits of Adharma. It yields only what it is bound to. But Dharma does not annul Adharma.
5.51.29
प्राप्तं धर्मफलं तावत्
भवता नात्र संशयः ।
फलमस्याप्यधर्मस्य
क्षिप्रमेव प्रपत्स्यसे ॥
No doubt that you have enjoyed the fruits of Dharma thus far; but very soon, you will reap the results of your Adharma.
5.51.30
जनस्थानवधं बुद्ध्वा
बुद्ध्वा वालिवधं तथा ।
रामसुग्रीवसख्यं च
बुध्यस्व हितमात्मनः ॥
Keeping in mind the slayings in Janasthāna and keeping in mind the slaying of Vāli, and also the alliance between Rāma and Sugreeva, think about what is good for yourself.
5.51.31
कामं खल्वहमप्येकः
सवाजिरथकुञ्जराम् ।
लङ्कां नाशयितुं शक्तः
तस्यैष तु न निश्चयः ॥
I can, all by myself, destroy Laṅkā along with its horses, chariots and elephants. But that is not what he (Rāma) has in mind.
5.51.32
रामेण हि प्रतिज्ञातम्
हर्यृक्षगणसन्निधौ ।
उत्सादनममित्राणाम्
सीता यैस्तु प्रधर्षिता ॥
Rāma took a vow, in the presence of the hosts of Vānaras and Ṛkshas, that he would exterminate his enemies by whom Seetā was wronged.
5.51.33
अपकुर्वन् हि रामस्य
साक्षादपि पुरन्दरः ।
न सुखं प्राप्नुयादन्यः
किं पुनस्त्वद्विधो जनः ॥
Having caused harm to Rāma, no one, even if it is none other than Indra, can expect to find peace; how then could any other, like you?
5.51.34
यां सीतेत्यभिजानासि
येयं तिष्ठति ते वशे ।
कालरात्रीति तां विद्धि
सर्वलङ्काविनाशिनीम् ॥
Know that she, whom you think as Seetā and is under your control, is none other than the Night that brings Time to an end, ready to destroy the entire Laṅkā.
5.51.35
तदलं कालपाशेन
सीताविग्रहरूपिणा ।
स्वयं स्कन्धावसक्तेन
क्षेममात्मनि चिन्त्यताम् ॥
Enough of your tying your neck by yourself with the noose of Death that only happened to appear in the form of Seetā; you should worry about your own wellbeing!
5.51.36
सीतायास्तेजसा दग्धाम्
रामकोपप्रपीडिताम् ।
दह्यमानामिमां पश्य
पुरीं साट्टप्रतोलिकाम् ॥
You shall see this city burning with all its streets and towers, ignited by the flame called Seetā and worsened by the rage of Rāma.
5.51.37
स्वानि मित्राणि मन्त्रींश्च
ज्ञातीन् भ्रात्रून् सुतान् हितान् ।
भोगान्दारांश्च लङ्कां च
मा विनाशमुपानय ॥
Do not bring destruction upon your own friends, ministers, kinsmen, brothers, sons, well-wishers, luxuries, wives and Laṅkā itself.
5.51.38
सत्यं राक्षसराजेन्द्र
शृणुष्व वचनं मम ।
रामदासस्य दूतस्य
वानरस्य विशेषतः ॥
Give heed, O emperor of Rākshasas, to these words that are nothing but the truth, of this servant and messenger of Rāma, especially a Vānara.
5.51.39
सर्वान् लोकान् सुसंहृत्य
सभूतान् सचराचरान् ।
पुनरेव तथा स्रष्टुम्
शक्तो रामो महायशाः ॥
Rāma of mighty renown can exterminate all the worlds and all the beings, mobile and immobile, therein, completely, and recreate them all again.
5.51.40
देवासुरनरेन्द्रेषु
यक्षरक्षोगणेषु च ।
विद्याधरेषु सर्वेषु
गन्धर्वेषूरगेषु च ।
सिद्धेषु किन्नरेन्द्रेषु
पतत्रिषु च सर्वतः ।
सर्वभूतेषु सर्वत्र
सर्वकालेषु नास्ति सः ।
यो रामं प्रतियुध्येत
विष्णुतुल्यपराक्रमम् ॥
There is no one among Dēvas, Asuras, the leaders of men, hosts of Yakshas, Rākshasas, all Vidyādharas, Gandharvas, Uragas, Siddhas, Kinnaras, birds from every direction, all creatures in all places, at any time who could stand against and fight with Rāma who is comparable to Vishṇu in prowess.
5.51.43
सर्वलोकेश्वरस्यैवम्
कृत्वा विप्रियमुत्तमम् ।
रामस्य राजसिंहस्य
दुर्लभं तव जीवितम् ॥
With you committing such a grave offense against Rāma, lion among kings and the lord of all the worlds, your life is at extreme risk.
5.51.44
देवाश्च दैत्याश्च निशाचरेन्द्र
गन्धर्वविद्याधरनागयक्षाः ।
रामस्य लोकत्रयनायकस्य
स्थातुं न शक्तास्समरेषु सर्वे ॥
O Lord of Night-rovers! All the Dēvas, Daityas, Gandharvas, Vidyādharas, Nāgas, Yakshas, together, cannot stand against Rāma, the master of the three worlds, in fight.
5.51.45
ब्रह्मा स्वयम्भूश्चतुराननो वा
रुद्रस्त्रिणेत्रस्त्रिपुरान्तको वा ।
इन्द्रो महेन्द्रस्सुरनायको वा
त्रातुं न शक्ता युधि रामवध्यम् ॥
Neither Brahma the Four-Faced, Self-Created, nor Rudra the Triple-eyed who destroyed the three cities, nor Indra the great Lord of the Suras, can save anyone who deserves death at the hands of Rāma in a fight.
5.51.46
स सौष्ठवोपेतमदीनवादिनः
कपेर्निशम्याप्रतिमोऽप्रियं वचः ।
दशाननः कोपविवृत्तलोचनः
समादिशत्तस्य वधं महाकपेः ॥
Having heard those strong words of the peerless Vānara, who spoke with no fear, the words that were so unpleasant, the Ten-faced rolled his eyes in anger and ordered the great Vānara to be killed.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे एकपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty first Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
