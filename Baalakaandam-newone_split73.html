Baala Kaanda - Sarga 73
So far in the Bāla Kāṇḍa, we have seen exotic and esoteric stories that are beyond the scale of humans, connecting the heaven with the earth and the earth with the heaven, such as the birth of Kārtikēya, descent of Gaṅgā, churning of the milky ocean, the escapade of Indra and Ahalya, the unparalleled story of Viṡwāmitra and his creation of an alternate heaven, and so on.
In this Sarga, Vālmeeki is changing the stage. He brings us from those esoteric worlds, literally, down to the earth and to the human plane. From this Sarga on, for the majority of time, he will take us through a whirlwind of human emotions, issues, choices, endeavors, follies and triumphs.
Vālmeeki is going to start that transition with a wedding, a highly celebrated and cherished event in human life across places and times, of the four sons of Daṡaratha, but not without the jarring note of the arrival of Yudhājit, the maternal uncle of Bharata, that hints at the trouble that would ensue in the future.
This wedding is not an ordinary wedding. It is the most earthly, human wedding that every one of us can relate to. It defined the meaning of wedding and marriage for an entire civilization. It is reenacted in every village and town of India from time immemorial, year after year.
Read this Sarga to witness that wedding of Seetā with Rāma and to become part of that celebration.
1.73.1
यस्मिंस्तु दिवसे राजा
चक्रे गोदानमुत्तमम् ।
तस्मिं स्तु दिवसे शूरो
युधाजित्समुपेयिवान् ॥
The same day on which the king (Daṡaratha) made the exceptional donation of the cows on behalf of his sons, the valiant Yudhājit arrived.
1.73.2
पुत्रः केकयराजस्य
साक्षाद्भरतमातुलः ।
दृष्ट्वा पृष्ट्वा च कुशलम्
राजानमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
(Yudhājit), son of the king of Kēkaya and the maternal uncle of Bharata, met the king (Daṡaratha) and enquired about his well-being and said:
1.73.3
केकयाधिपती राजा
स्नेहात् कुशलमब्रवीत् ।
येषां कुशलकामोऽसि
तेषां सम्प्रत्यनामयम् ॥
The king of Kēkaya affectionately enquires about your well-being. Everyone whose welfare you seek, is doing well and keeping healthy.
1.73.4
स्वस्रीयं मम राजेन्द्र
द्रष्टुकामो महीपतिः ।
तदर्थमुपयातोऽहं
अयोध्यां रघुनन्दन ॥
O lord of kings! O descendant of Raghu! The ruler of the land (the king of Kēkaya) wanted to see my nephew. Hence I went to Ayōdhyā.
1.73.5
श्रुत्वा त्वहमयोध्यायाम्
विवाहार्थं तवात्मजान् ।
मिथिलामुपयातांस्तु
त्वया सह महीपते ।
त्वरयाभ्युपयातोऽहम्
द्रष्टुकाम स्स्वसुस्सुतम् ॥
O King! Having heard in Ayōdhyā that you had left for Mithilā along with your sons for performing their wedding, I came here immediately, to see my sister's son.
The seeds of ensuing trouble, the involvement of Kēkaya kings in the affairs of Ayōdhyā are hinted at here.
1.73.6
अथ राजा दशरथः
प्रियातिथिमुपस्थितम् ।
दृष्ट्वा परमसत्कारैः
पूजार्हं समपूजयत् ॥
Then king Daṡaratha, seeing that honorable guest who thus arrived, treated him with the best of hospitalities.
1.73.7
ततस्तामुषितो रात्रिम्
सह पुत्रैर्महात्मभिः ।
प्रभाते पुनरुत्थाय
कृत्वा कर्माणि कर्मवित् ।
ऋषींस्तदा पुरस्कृत्य
यज्ञवाटमुपागमत् ॥
Having spent that night with his sons, the Mahātmas, that responsible king woke up early in the morning and completed the customary rituals. Then he reached the hall of Yajña, with the Ṛshis leading the way.
1.73.9
युक्ते मुहूर्ते विजये
सर्वाभरणभूषितैः ।
भ्रातृभिस्सहितो रामः
कृतकौतुकमंगलः ।
वसिष्ठं पुरतः कृत्वा
महर्षीनपरानपि ।
पितुस्समीपमाश्रित्य
तस्थौ भ्रातृभिरावृतः ॥
In the Vijaya Muhūrta, Rāma along with his brothers, adorned with all the auspicious and festive ornaments came and stood in front his father, surrounded by his brothers and led by Vasishṭha and other Ṛshis.
Muhūrta is a point in time in general, an auspicious one in particular.
1.73.11
वसिष्ठो भगवानेत्य
वैदेहमिदमब्रवीत् ।
राजा दशरथो राजन्
कृतकौतुकमङ्गलैः ।
पुत्रैर्नरवर श्रेष्ठ
दातारमभिकाङ्क्षते ॥
Bhagawān Vasishṭha then went to the king of Vidēha and said: O king! O best among men! King Daṡaratha, along with his sons, having all the auspicious wedding preparations done, is waiting for you, the giver of brides!
1.73.12
दातृप्रतिग्रहीतृभ्याम्
सर्वार्थाः प्रभवन्ति हि ।
स्वधर्मं प्रतिपद्यस्व
कृत्वा वैवाह्यमुत्तमम् ॥
Every activity in this world is a result of a giver and a recipient meeting each other. May you do your righteous part by performing the exceptional ceremony of wedding!
1.73.13
इत्युक्तः परमोदारो
वसिष्ठेन महात्मना ।
प्रत्युवाच महातेजा
वाक्यं परमधर्मवित् ॥
In response to those words of Mahātma Vasishṭha, Janaka, the most charitable and the supremely radiant, who was fully aware of Dharma, said:
1.73.14
कस्स्थितः प्रतिहारो मे
कस्याज्ञा सम्प्रतीक्ष्यते ।
स्वगृहे को विचारोऽस्ति
यथा राज्यमिदं तव ॥
No door keeper of mine bars your way! No one’s permission is needed for you to enter! Why hesitate to enter your own home, when this entire kingdom is yours!
It is amazing how well this Ṡlōka, written eons ago, echoes the sentiment and expression, down to the words of choice, of Indian hospitality.
1.73.15
कृतकौतुकसर्वस्वा
वेदिमूलमुपागताः ।
मम कन्या मुनिश्रेष्ठ
दीप्ता वह्नेरिवार्चिषः ॥
O best among Munis! My daughters, having performed all the auspicious rites in preparation for the wedding, are ready at the foot of the Vēdi, shining like the luster of the flames of fire!
Vēdi refers to the Hōma Kuṇḍa and also the area around it, based on the context. The term Vēdika is an alternate word for Vēdi.
1.73.16
सज्जोऽहं त्वत्प्रतीक्षोऽस्मि
वेद्यामस्यां प्रतिष्ठितः ।
अविघ्नं कुरुतां राजा
किमर्थमवलम्बते ॥
I wait for you, ready, at the Vēdi. May the king (Daṡaratha) proceed without any hindrance. There is nothing else that might induce any delay!
1.73.17
तद्वाक्यं जनकेनोक्तम्
श्रुत्वा दशरथस्तदा ।
प्रवेशयामास सुतान्
सर्वानृषिगणानपि ॥
With those words of Janaka, Daṡaratha led his sons and hosts of Ṛshis (to the Vēdi).
1.73.18
ततो राजा विदेहानाम्
वसिष्ठमिदमब्रवीत् ।
कारयस्व ऋषे सर्वं
ऋषिभिः सह धार्मिक ।
रामस्य लोकरामस्य
क्रियां वैवाहिकीं विभो ॥
Then the king of Vidēha spoke to Vasishṭha: "O Dhārmika! O Ṛshi! O competent one! Please perform along with all the Ṛshis, the wedding ceremony of Rāma, the darling of all the worlds!"
1.73.20
तथेत्युक्त्वा तु जनकम्
वसिष्ठो भगवानृषिः ।
विश्वामित्रं पुरस्कृत्य
शतानन्दं च धार्मिकम् ।
प्रपामध्ये तु विधिवत्
वेदिं कृत्वा महातपाः ।
अलञ्चकार तां वेदिम्
गन्धपुष्पै स्समन्ततः ।
सुवर्णपालिकाभिश्च
छिद्रकुम्भैश्च साङ्कुरैः ।
अङ्कुराढ्यैश्शरावैश्च
धूपपात्रै स्सधूपकैः ।
शङ्खपात्रै स्स्रुवैस्स्रुग्भिः
पात्रैरर्घ्याभिपूरितैः ।
लाजपूर्णैश्च पात्रीभिः
अक्षतैरभिसंस्कृतैः ॥
Vasishṭha, the Bhagawān and Ṛshi said to Janaka: 'Be it so!' That Mahā Tapasvi, with Viṡwāmitra and the Dhārmika Ṡatānanda leading him, reached the canopy. They prepared the Vēdi in the centre of the canopy as laid out in the Ṡāstras, by decorating it with flowers, sandal wood, golden plates, perforated pots with sprouts, earthen bowls with sprouts, vessels with burning incense of exquisite fragrance, bowls in the shape of shells, the ladles called Srukku and Sruva used for fire rituals, water filled decorated pots, vessels full of puffed grains, and filled with cleansed and sanctified Akshata.
Nowadays, we use the word 'sprouts' to refer to the ready-to-eat health food items, the soaked beans from which sprouts emerge.
But in this Ṡlōka, sprouts refers to the tiny baby plants, (shoots) that are still rooted in the soil in a pot or shallow bowl. Akshata means rice coated lightly with turmeric.
These Ṡlōkas give the items that are traditionally used for decorating the wedding stage. Everyone who is part of preparing a wedding stage (household members as well as professional decorators) would benefit from knowing this list of auspicious items.
1.73.24
दर्भैस्समैस्समास्तीर्य
विधिवन्मन्त्रपूर्वकम् ।
अग्निमादाय वेद्यां तु
विधिमन्त्रपुरस्कृतम् ।
जुहावाग्नौ महातेजा
वसिष्ठो भगवानृषिः ॥
Vasishṭha, the supremely radiant Bhagawān, laid out Darbha grass strands, cut to equal size on the Vēdi, and kindled the fire in the Vēdi as laid out in the Ṡāstras and then performed the Hōma, chanting the appropriate Mantras.
1.73.25
ततस्सीतां समानीय
सर्वाभरणभूषिताम् ।
समक्षमग्ने स्संस्थाप्य
राघवाभिमुखे तदा ।
अब्रवीज्जनको राजा
कौसल्यानन्दवर्धनम् ॥
King Janaka then led Seetā, who was in splendid attire, adorned with assorted ornaments, to the Vēdi and had her stand facing the prince of Raghu dynasty, witnessed by Agni (the God of Fire), and spoke to Rāma, who fills the heart of Kousalyā with joy:
1.73.27
इयं सीता मम सुता
सहधर्मचरी तव ।
प्रतीच्छ चैनां भद्रं ते
पाणिं गृह्णीष्व पाणिना ॥
"This is my daughter Seetā. Please accept her as your partner in life in discharging all the righteous responsibilities, by taking her hand into your hand! May all bode well for you!"
This Ṡlōka, one of the most celebrated Ṡlōkas in the Rāmāyaṇa carries all the emotions of a father and all the people whose hearts are filled with sweet memories of the bride’s childhood and the solemnity of the responsibility laid in the hands of the bridegroom and the best of wishes that goes with it!
1.73.28
पतिव्रता महाभागा
छायेवानुगता सदा ।
इत्युक्त्वा प्राक्षिपद्राजा
मन्त्रपूतं जलं तदा ॥
"This blessed one will be your faithful, devoted and loyal wife. She will always follow you like a shadow."
Speaking thus, the king then poured water sanctified with Mantras.
1.73.29
साधु साध्विति देवाना
मृषीणां वदतां तदा ।
देवदुन्दुभिर्निर्घोषः
पुष्पवर्षो महानभूत् ॥
"Excellent! Wonderful!" applauded the Dēvas and Ṛshis! The celestial kettledrums resounded with gusto. Flowers were showered from the skies!
1.73.30
एवं दत्त्वा तदा सीताम्
मन्त्रोदकपुरस्कृताम् ।
अब्रवीज्जनको राजा
हर्षेणाभिपरिप्लुतः ॥
Then king Janaka, after thus giving away Seetā in matrimony to Rāma by pouring water sanctified by Mantras, was overwhelmed with joy and said:
1.73.31
लक्ष्मणागच्छ भद्रं ते
ऊर्मिलामुद्यतां मया ।
प्रतीच्छ पाणिं गृह्णीष्व
माभूत्कालस्य पर्ययः ॥
O Lakshmaṇa, please come! May all bode well for you! Please accept my daughter Ūrmilā, whom I wish to give you, by holding her hand in yours! May there not be any delay!
1.73.32
तमेवमुक्त्वा जनको
भरतं चाभ्यभाषत ।
गृहाण पाणिं माण्डव्याः
पाणिना रघुनन्दन ॥
Then Janaka, addressing Bharata said: "O descendant of Raghu dynasty! please take the hand of Māndavi in your hand!"
1.73.33
शत्रुघ्नं चापि धर्मात्मा
अब्रवीज्जनकेश्वरः ॥
King Janaka, the Dharmātma, then said to Ṡatrughna:
1.73.33
श्रुतकीर्त्या महाबाहो
पाणिं गृह्णीष्व पाणिना ।
सर्वे भवन्तस्सौम्याश्च
सर्वे सुचरितव्रताः ।
पत्नीभिस्सन्तु काकुत्स्था
माभूत्कालस्य पर्ययः ॥
O you of mighty arm! Please take the hand of Ṡrutakeerti in your hand!
O princes of Kākutsthā lineage! All of you are gentle and consistently of good character! May you also now have the company of your wives! May there not be any delay!
1.73.35
जनकस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
पाणीन् पाणिभिरास्पृशन् ।
चत्वारस्ते चतसृणाम्
वसिष्ठस्य मते स्थिताः ॥
Hearing those words of Janaka, those four, in accordance with the wish of Vasishṭha, held the hands of the four sisters with their hands.
1.73.36
अग्निं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य
वेदिं राजानमेव च ।
ऋषींश्चैव महात्मानः
सभार्या रघुसत्तमाः ।
यथोक्तेन तदा चक्रुः
विवाहं विधिपूर्वकम् ॥
Those jewels of Raghu dynasty, the Mahātmas along with their wives, circumambulated the sacred fire, the Vēdi, the king and the Ṛshis. Thus they married, just as were instructed, in a customary manner.
1.73.37
काकुत्स्थैश्च गृहीतेषु
ललितेषु च पाणिषु ।
पुष्पवृष्टिर्महत्यासीत्
अन्तरिक्षात्सुभास्वरा ॥
Just as those princes of Kākutsthā lineage were taking the hands of those slender girls, the bright skies presented a splendid shower of flowers.
1.73.38
दिव्यदुन्दुभिनिर्घोषैः
गीतवादित्रनिस्वनैः ।
ननृतुश्चाप्सरस्सङ्घा
गन्धर्वाश्च जगुः कलम् ।
विवाहे रघुमुख्यानाम्
तदद्भुतमदृश्यत ॥
In that wedding ceremony of those prominent princes of Raghu dynasty, the celestial kettledrums resounded with gusto, Apsaras danced to the tunes of songs and instrumental music and Gandharvas sang with incredible melody! It was marvelous to behold!
1.73.40
ईदृशे वर्तमाने तु
तूर्योद्घुष्टनिनादिते ।
त्रिरग्निं ते परिक्रम्य
ऊहुर्भार्यां महौजसः ॥
As the air was filled with the jubilant sounds of dancing and singing like this, those splendid looking brothers circumambulated the fire three times with their wives and were united in marriage.
1.73.41
अथोपकार्यां जग्मुस्ते
सभार्या रघुनन्दनाः ।
राजाऽप्यनुययौ पश्यन्
सर्षिसंघ स्सबान्धवः ॥
Then the descendants of Raghu dynasty, along with their wives went to their guest quarters. The King followed them, accompanied by the hosts of Ṛshis and relatives, never taking his eyes off them.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे त्रिसप्ततितमस्सर्ग:॥
Thus concludes the seventy third Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
