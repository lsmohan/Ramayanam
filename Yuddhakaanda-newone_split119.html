Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 119
In this Sarga, Mahēṡwara, on behalf of all the Dēvas, wishes Rāma well, and advises him to go back to Ayōdhyā, to bring joy to everyone, to rule the kingdom for long, and to ascend to Heaven in the end.
Then, Daṡaratha, who also came from Heaven in his Vimāna, overwhelmed with joy on seeing Rāma, takes him onto his lap, and expresses his delight over his slaying of the foe, completing fourteen years in exile, and being set to go back to Ayōdhyā to bring joy to Kousalyā and others. Then Rāma pleads with Daṡaratha to forgive Kaikēyee and Bharata by revoking what he said in the past to Kaikēyee, 'I abjure you along with your son!' Daṡaratha agrees gladly.
Daṡaratha then appreciates Lakshmaṇa for serving Rāma, and asks him to continue to do the same, and thus serve Dharma and gain great reputation for himself. He then asks Seetā not to be resentful about Rāma abjuring her, as he did so only to force her purification from the touch of evil Rāvaṇa. He praises her devotion to her husband and commends the near-impossible act she performed, which would bring her renown that far surpasses the renown of all other women.
Daṡaratha then leaves for the world of Indra.
6.119.1
एतच्छ्रुत्वा शुभं वाक्यम्
राघवेण सुभाषितम् ।
इदं शुभतरं वाक्यम्
व्याजहार महेश्वरः ॥
Having heard those heartening and well spoken words of Rāghava, Mahēṡwara said these even more heartening words:
6.119.2
पुष्कराक्ष महाबाहो
महावक्षः परन्तप ।
दिष्ट्या कृतमिदं कर्म
त्वया शस्त्रभृतां वर ॥
O tormentor of the foe, of mighty arm, lotus-like eyes, and broad chest! By the grace of Providence, this much has been accomplished by you, the foremost of bowmen!
6.119.3
दिष्ट्या सर्वस्य लोकस्य
प्रवृद्धं दारुणं तमः ।
अपावृत्तं त्वया सङ्ख्ये
राम रावणजं भयम् ॥
The terrible darkness called the fear of Rāvaṇa, that enveloped all the worlds, has been removed by you in the battle.
6.119.4
आश्वास्य भरतं दीनम्
कौसल्यां च यशस्विनीम् ।
कैकेयीं च सुमित्रां च
दृष्ट्वा लक्ष्मणमातरम् ।
प्राप्य राज्यमयोध्यायाम्
नन्दयित्वा सुहृज्जनम् ।
इक्ष्वाकूणां कुले वंशम्
स्थापयित्वा महाबल ।
इष्ट्वा तुरगमेधेन
प्राप्य चानुत्तमं यशः ।
ब्राह्मणेभ्यो धनं दत्त्वा
त्रिदिवं गन्तुमर्हसि ॥
Going back to the kingdom of Ayōdhyā, you should comfort the miserable Bharata and Kousalyā of great renown and visit Kaikēyee and Sumitrā, Lakshmaṇa’s mother, and bring joy to your friends and well-wishers.
Ensuring the continuity of the line of Ikshwāku by raising progeny, O you of extraordinary strength, should garner unequaled fame by performing the Aṡwamēdha Yajña, and distributing the largess among the Brāhmaṇas, you should ascend to Heaven.
6.119.7
एष राजा विमानस्थः
पिता दशरथस्तव ।
काकुत्स्थ मानुषे लोके
गुरुस्तव महायशाः ॥
O Kākutstha, here is your father Daṡaratha of great renown, seated in his Vimāna, who was your revered elder while he existed in the world of humans.
6.119.8
इन्द्रलोकं गतः श्रीमान्
त्वया पुत्रेण तारितः ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
त्वमेनमभिवादय ॥
The blessed one went to the world of Indra, redeemed by you, his son. Make your prostrations to him along with your brother Lakshmaṇa.
6.119.9
महादेववचः श्रुत्वा
काकुत्स्थः सहलक्ष्मणः ।
विमानशिखरस्थस्य
प्रणाममकरोत् पितुः ॥
Hearing those words of Mahēṡwara, Kākutstha, along with Lakshmaṇa, made prostrations to his father who was seated at the head of the Vimāna.
6.119.10
दीप्यमानं स्वया लक्ष्म्या
विरजोअम्बरधारिणम् ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
ददर्श पितरं प्रभुः ॥
He, the lord, along with his brother Lakshmaṇa, beheld his father clad in spotless raiment and glowing in his innate glory.
6.119.11
हर्षेण महताऽऽविष्टो
विमानस्थो महीपतिः ।
प्राणैः प्रियतरं दृष्ट्वा
पुत्रं दशरथस्तदा ॥
On seeing his son who was more than life to him, Daṡaratha, the lord of the earth, seated in the Vimāna, was overwhelmed with joy.
6.119.12
आरोप्याङ्के महाबाहुः
वरासनगतः प्रभुः ।
बाहुभ्यां सम्परिष्वज्य
ततो वाक्यं समाददे ॥
That lord of mighty arm, who was seated on a splendid chair had him sit on his lap, took him into his embrace and said these words.
6.119.13
न मे स्वर्गो बहुमतः
सम्मानश्च सुरर्षभैः ।
त्वया राम विहीनस्य
सत्यं प्रतिशृणोमि ते ॥
Let me tell you the truth: without you by me, I prize neither Heaven, nor the regard I receive from the bulls among Dēvas.
6.119.14
अद्य त्वां निहतामित्रम्
दृष्ट्वा सम्पूर्णमानसम् ।
निस्तीर्णवनवासं च
प्रीतिरासीत् परा मम ॥
I am delighted beyond measure today seeing you all-accomplished, having slain the foe and having completed the term of exile.
6.119.15
कैकेय्या यानि चोक्तानि
वाक्यानि वदतां वर ।
तव प्रव्राजनार्थानि
स्थितानि हृदये मम ॥
The words uttered by Kaikēyee, demanding your exile, still rankle in my heart, O best of orators!
6.119.16
त्वां तु दृष्ट्वा कुशलिनम्
परिष्वज्य सलक्ष्मणम् ।
अद्य दुःखाद्विमुक्तोऽस्मि
नीहारादिव भास्करः ॥
Having seen you well and happy today, and embraced you and Lakshmaṇa, I am freed from grief, like the sun from the mist.
6.119.17
तारितोऽहं त्वया पुत्र
सुपुत्रेण महात्मना ।
अष्टावक्रेण धर्मात्मा
कहोलो ब्राह्मणो यथा ॥
You, my good son and a mighty soul, have redeemed me, my child, as Ashṭāvakra did the Brāhmaṇa called Kahōla.
6.119.18
इदानीं तु विजानामि
यथा सौम्य सुरेश्वरैः ।
वधार्थं रावणस्येदम्
विहितं पुरुषोत्तम ॥
Now I understand, my dear, how all this was fashioned by the chiefs of the Dēvas for the annihilation of Rāvaṇa, O best of the spirited ones!
6.119.19
सिद्धार्था खलु कौसल्या
या त्वां राम गृहं गतम् ।
वनान्निवृत्तं संहृष्टा
द्रक्ष्यते शत्रुसूदनम् ॥
Kousalyā will indeed be the blessed one, O Rāma, rejoicing on seeing you return home from the Vana having destroyed the foe!
6.119.20
सिद्धार्थाः खलु ते राम
नरा ये त्वां पुरीं गतम् ।
राज्ये चैवाभिषिक्तं
द्रक्ष्यन्ते वसुधाधिपम् ॥
Those men will indeed be the blessed ones, who see you return to the city and crowned as the lord of the earth!
6.119.21
अनुरक्तेन बलिना
शुचिना धर्मचारिणा ।
इच्छामि त्वामहं द्रष्टुम्
भरतेन समागतम् ॥
I cannot wait to see you reunited with Bharata, a follower of Dharma, who is strong, pure-hearted and has love for you.
6.119.22
चतुर्दश समाः सौम्य
वने निर्यापितास्त्वया ।
वसता सीतया सार्धम्
लक्ष्मणेन च धीमता ॥
You, my dear, have completed fourteen years in the forest, along with Seetā and the sagacious Lakshmaṇa.
6.119.23
निवृत्तवनवासोऽसि
प्रतिज्ञा सफला कृता ।
रावणं च रणे हत्वा
देवास्ते परितोषिताः ॥
You have completed your stay in the Vana. You fulfilled your vow by slaying Rāvaṇa in the battle. Dēvas are also made happy by you.
6.119.24
कृतं कर्म यशः श्लाध्यम्
प्राप्तं ते शत्रुसूदन ।
भ्रातृभिः सह राज्यस्थो
दीर्घमायुरवाप्नुहि ॥
You have achieved a task worthy of praise. Great reputation is yours, O destroyer of the foe! Established in the kingdom with your brothers, may you live long!
6.119.25
इति ब्रुवाणं राजानम्
रामः प्राञ्जलिरब्रवीत् ॥
To that king who spoke thus, Rāma said, with his palms joined in reverence:
6.119.25
कुरु प्रसादं धर्मज्ञ
कैकेय्या भरतस्य च ।
सपुत्रां त्वां त्यजामीति
यदुक्ता कैकयी त्वया ।
स शापः केकयीं घोरः
सपुत्रां न स्पृशेत् प्रभो ॥
May you, O Dharmajña, be pleased to shower your grace on Kaikēyee and Bharata! May the dreaded curse you pronounced, O lord, on Kaikēyee, saying, 'I abjure you along with your son', not affect Kaikēyee or her son!
Rāma is lauded as Dharmātma, Mahātma, etc. for a reason.
He seeks forgiveness even for Kaikēyee, who is the very reason for him to go through such terrible trials and tribulations. Such is the character of great men like Rāma, who can discern the intent behind actions. Kaikēyee wrecked his life, but the reason is her error in judgment, and hence deserves forgiveness. Rāvaṇa wrecked his life too, but the reason is wickedness, and hence deserves death.
Rāma's greatness also lies in recalling the curse laid by Daṡaratha, at this specific moment, without losing himself in the emotion of the moment, which is filled with the jubilation of slaying Rāvaṇa, being reunited with Seetā, and seeing his father again.
6.119.27
स तथेति महाराजो
राममुक्त्वा कृताञ्जलिम् ।
लक्ष्मणं च परिष्वज्य
पुनर्वाक्यमुवाच ह ॥
Saying 'may it be so!' to Rāma, who beseeched him with palms joined in reverence, the great king continued, saying these words to Lakshmaṇa, taking him into his embrace,
6.119.28
रामं शुश्रूषता भक्त्या
वैदेह्या सह सीतया ।
कृता मम महाप्रीतिः
प्राप्तं धर्मफलं च ते ॥
By serving Rāma with all the devotion along with Seetā, the princess of Vidēha, you have garnered the fruits of Dharma and given me immense pleasure.
6.119.29
धर्मं प्राप्स्यसि धर्मज्ञ
यशश्च विपुलं भुवि ।
रामे प्रसन्ने स्वर्गं च
महिमानं तथैव च ॥
With Rāma pleased with you, O Dharmajña, you achieve Dharma, earn supreme glory, garner widespread fame in this world and attain Heaven in the after life.
6.119.30
रामं शुश्रूष भद्रं ते
सुमित्रानन्दवर्धन ।
रामः सर्वस्य लोकस्य
शुभेष्वभिरतः सदा ॥
O enhancer of the joy of Sumitrā! Continue serving Rāma, and may all bode well for you; for he is always invested in the good of the entire world.
6.119.31
एते सेन्द्रास्त्रयो लोकाः
सिद्धाश्च परमर्षयः ।
अभिगम्य महात्मानम्
अर्चन्ति पुरुषोत्तमम् ॥
Here come the Siddhas, Supreme Ṛshis and all the three worlds along with Indra to pay homage to the Mahātma, the foremost of the spirited beings!
6.119.32
एतत्तदुक्तमव्यक्तम्
अक्षरं ब्रह्मनिर्मितम् ।
देवानां हृदयं सौम्य
गुह्यं रामः परन्तपः ॥
They have proclaimed, my child, Rāma, scorcher of the foe, as none other than the great mystery, the Brahman, the Imperishable and Unmanifested Reality and the essence of all the Dēvas.
The word unmanifested does not exist in English dictionary. I use it, as a made up word, as an equivalent of Avyakta.
This Ṡlōka could have been misplaced at the time of modern day compilation. It fits better at the end of Āditya Hṛdayam (between Ṡlōkas 6.105.24 and 6.105.25) with a minor change from रामः परन्तपः to राम परन्तप.
6.119.33
अवाप्तं धर्मचरणम्
यशश्च विपुलं त्वया ।
रामं शुश्रूषता भक्त्या
वैदेह्या सह सीतया ॥
By serving Rāma with all the devotion, along with Seetā, the princess of Vidēha, you have done your part in following Dharma and you also gained wide renown for it.
6.119.34
इत्युक्त्वा लक्ष्मणं राजा
स्नुषां बद्धाञ्जलिं स्थिताम् ।
पुत्रीत्याभाष्य मधुरं
शनैरेनामुवाच ह ॥
Having said this to Lakshmaṇa, the king spoke these words slowly to his daughter-in-law who stood with her palms joined in reverence, addressing her as 'My child!' in a doting voice:
6.119.35
कर्तव्यो न तु वैदेहि
मन्युस्त्यागमिमं प्रति ।
रामेणेदं विशुद्ध्यर्थम्
कृतं वै त्वद्धितैषिणा ॥
May you not, O Vaidēhee, be resentful about his abjuring you; Rāma did so only to help you become purified (of the touch of the evil one)!
6.119.36
सुदुष्करमिदं पुत्रि
तव चारित्रलक्षणम् ।
कृतं यत्तेऽन्यनारीणां
यशो ह्यभिभविष्यति ।
MEANINGFUL
What you have done, which is so difficult, ।
my child, makes history, and would
eclipse the renown of all other women! ॥
6.119.38
न त्वं कामं समाधेया
भर्तृशुश्रूषणं प्रति ।
अवश्यं तु मया वाच्यम्
एष ते दैवतं परम् ॥
You need no instruction regarding how to serve your husband, but it behooves me to say that he is the god, ultimate, for you.
6.119.39
इति प्रतिसमादिश्य
पुत्रौ सीतां च राघवः ।
इन्द्रलोकं विमानेन
ययौ दशरथो नृपः ॥
Having thus advised his sons and Seetā, Daṡaratha, the king of Raghu's lineage, went back to the world of Indra in his Vimāna.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे एकोनविंशत्युत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and nineteenth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
