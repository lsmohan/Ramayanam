Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 68
In this Sarga, the Rākshasas inform Rāvaṇa that Kumbhakarṇa has been slain. They inform him that his humongous body fell blocking the entrances before it finally got buried in the bed of the ocean.
Rāvaṇa falls down in a faint on hearing the news. Dēvāntaka, Narāntaka, Triṡira and Atikāya cry at the loss of their uncle. Mahōdara and Mahāpārṡwa become overwhelmed with grief on hearing about the loss of their brother.
Rāvaṇa, after regaining senses, laments immensely, the death of his younger brother. He gets disillusioned about life and feels terrible at the prospect of Dēvas jeering him. He deeply resents snubbing off the good advice given by Vibheeshaṇa.
6.68.1
कुम्भकर्णं हतं दृष्ट्वा
राघवेण महात्मना ।
राक्षसा राक्षसेन्द्राय
रावणाय न्यवेदयन् ॥
Seeing Kumbhakarṇa slain by Rāghava, a Mahātma, the Rākshasas informed Rāvaṇa, the king of Rākshasas, of it.
6.68.2
राजन् स कालसङ्काशः
संयुक्तः कालकर्मणा ।
विद्राव्य वानरीं सेनाम्
भक्षयित्वा च वानरान् ॥
O King, he, who was like Time (that puts an end to everything) himself, who made the Vānara army flee and who devoured Vānaras, had now come under the siege of Time.
6.68.3
प्रतपित्वा मुहूर्तं च
प्रशान्तो राम तेजसा ।
कायेनार्धप्रविष्टेन
समुद्रं भीमदर्शनम् ॥
He, who showed prowess for quite a while, was calmed by the power of Rāma. His body, lying half- buried in the sea makes a horrible sight to behold.
6.68.4
निकृत्तनासाकर्णेन
विक्षरद्रुधिरेण च ।
रुद्ध्वा द्वारं शरीरेण
लङ्कायाः पर्वतोपमः ॥
With nose and ears cut off, and blood flowing, his mountainous body fell blocking the entrance of Laṅkā.
6.68.5
कुम्भकर्णस्तव भ्राता
काकुत्स्थशरपीडितः ।
लगण्डभूतो विकृतो
दावदग्ध इव द्रुमः ॥
Kumbhakarṇa, your brother, struck by the arrows of Kākutstha, turned into a shapeless mass like a tree burnt by a forest fire.
6.68.6
श्रुत्वा विनिहतं सङ्ख्ये
कुम्भकर्णं महाबलम् ।
रावणः शोकसन्तप्तो
मुमोह च पपात च ॥
Hearing that Kumbhakarṇa of immense strength had been slain in the battle, Rāvaṇa was overwhelmed with grief and fell down in a faint.
6.68.7
पितृव्यं निहतं दृष्ट्वा
देवान्तकनरान्तकौ ।
त्रिशिराश्चातिकायश्च
रुरुदुः शोकपीडिताः ॥
On learning that their uncle was slain, Dēvāntaka, Narāntaka, Triṡira and Atikāya cried, oppressed by grief.
6.68.8
भ्रातरं निहतं दृष्ट्वा
रामेणाक्लिष्टकर्मणा ।
महोदरमहापार्श्वौ
शोकाक्रान्तौ बभूवतुः ॥
On learning that their brother was slain by Rāma, who did things with ease, Mahōdara and Mahāpārṡwa were overwhelmed with grief.
6.68.9
ततः कृच्छ्रात् समासाद्य
सञ्ज्ञां राक्षसपुङ्गवः ।
कुम्भकर्णवधाद्दीनो
विललापाकुलेन्द्रियः ॥
Regaining his senses with great difficulty and feeling forlorn from the death of Kumbhakarṇa, that bull among Vānaras lamented, with his senses in turmoil:
6.68.10
हा वीर रिपुदर्पघ्न
कुम्भकर्ण महाबल ।
त्वं मां विहाय वै दैवात्
यातोऽसि यमसादनम् ॥
Alas, O Kumbhakarṇa! O you of mighty strength! O heroic one and queller of the pride of the foe! You, abandoning me, have gone to the abode of Yama, willed by Providence!
6.68.11
मम शल्यमनुद्धत्य
बान्धवानां महाबल ।
शत्रुसैन्यं प्रताप्यैकः
क्व मां सन्त्यज्य गच्छसि ॥
O you of immense strength! Having tormented the enemy forces single handedly, and before you could remove the thorn in my side and in that of all our kin, where did you go, abandoning me!
6.68.12
इदानीं खल्वहं नास्मि
यस्य मे दक्षिणो भुजः ।
पतितो यं समाश्रित्य
न बिभेमि सुरासुरात् ॥
I am now as good as dead, with my right hand fallen, relying on which I feared neither the Suras nor the Asuras!
6.68.13
कथमेवंविधो वीरो
देवदानवदर्पहा ।
कालाग्नि प्रतिमो ह्यद्य
राघवेण रणे हतः ॥
How could a Veera like you, who humbled the pride of Dēvas and Dānavas, who is no less fierce than the Conflagration of Time (that burns up the worlds), get slain today in the battle by Rāghava!
6.68.14
यस्य ते वज्रनिष्पेषो
न कुर्याद्व्यसनं सदा ।
स कथं रामबाणार्तः
प्रसुप्तोऽसि महीतले ॥
How could you, whom even a blow of Vajra could not hurt, suffer from the arrows of Rāma, and lie down on the bare earth in the sleep that knows no waking?
6.68.15
एते देवगणाः सार्धम्
ऋषिभिर्गगने स्थिताः ।
निहतं त्वां रणे दृष्ट्वा
निनदन्ति प्रहर्षिताः ॥
The Ṛshis, along with hordes of Dēvas, shout with joy standing in the sky, seeing you slain in the fight.
6.68.16
ध्रुवमद्यैव संहृष्टा
लब्धलक्षाः प्लवङ्गमाः ।
आरोक्ष्यन्तीह दुर्गाणि
लङ्काद्वाराणि सर्वशः ॥
Having accomplished their goal and rejoicing, the Vānaras must surely be climbing all over the defenses and entrances of Laṅkā.
6.68.17
राज्येन नास्ति मे कार्यम्
किं करिष्यामि सीतया ।
कुम्भकर्णविहीनस्य
जीविते नास्ति मे रतिः ॥
What business do I have with the Kingdom, what will I do with Seetā, and what interest does life hold for me, with my having lost Kumbhakarṇa!
6.68.18
यद्यहं भ्रातृहन्तारम्
न हन्मि युधि राघवम् ।
ननु मे मरणं श्रेयो
न चेदं व्यर्थजीवितम् ॥
If I do not slay in fight Rāghava, the slayer of my brother, it is better for me to die than to lead a life that would be so futile!
6.68.19
अद्यैव तं गमिष्यामि
देशं यत्रानुजो मम ।
नहि भ्रातऽन् समुत्सृज्य
क्षणं जीवितुमुत्सहे ॥
I shall go now itself to the place where my younger brother is. I do not like to live, even for a moment, without my brother.
6.68.20
देवा हि मां हसिष्यन्ति
दृष्ट्वा पूर्वापकारिणम् ।
कथमिन्द्रं जयिष्यामि
कुम्भकर्ण हते त्वयि ॥
The Dēvas will jeer at me, who has harmed them in the past. How can I vanquish Indra, O Kumbhakarṇa, now that you have been slain?
6.68.21
तदिदं मामनुप्राप्तम्
विभीषणवचः शुभम् ।
यदज्ञानान्मया तस्य
न गृहीतं महात्मनः ॥
Here I reap the fruits of not heeding the good advice of Vibheeshaṇa, the Mahātma, in my folly.
6.68.22
विभीषणवचो यावत्
कुम्भकर्णप्रहस्तयोः ।
विनाशोऽयं समुत्पन्नो
मां व्रीडयति दारुणः ॥
The destruction of Prahasta and Kumbhakarṇa, which happened just as warned by Vibheeshaṇa, makes me feel terribly ashamed.
6.68.23
तस्यायं कर्मणः प्राप्तो
विपाको मम शोकदः ।
यन्मया धार्मिकः श्रीमान्
स निरस्तो विभीषणः ॥
This misery is the fruit I reap from my sin of snubbing off Vibheeshaṇa, a Dhārmika who stands tall.
6.68.24
इति बहुविधमाकुलान्तरात्मा
कृपणमतीव विलप्य कुम्भकर्णम् ।
न्यपतदथ दशाननो भृशार्तः
तमनुजमिन्द्ररिपुं हतं विदित्वा ॥
The deepest layers of his heart thus set in turmoil in many ways upon his learning that his younger brother, the foe of Indra, was slain, the Ten-faced fell down, extremely oppressed.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे अष्टषष्टितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty eighth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
