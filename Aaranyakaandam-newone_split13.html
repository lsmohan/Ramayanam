Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 13
In this Sarga, Agastya expresses his happiness over the visit of Rāma. He commends Seetā for following her husband with all devotion, even though he lost his riches and position.
Rāma then asks Agastya to suggest him a place where he can build a residence for himself. Carefully considering that request, Agastya advises him to go to a beautiful place famously known as Pancavaṭi, near Gōdāvaree, which was only at a distance of two Yōjanas from there.
Agastya also tells Rāma that he became aware of the details of Daṡaratha ordering him to go to Vana, through his powers of Tapa. He says that Daṡaratha is fortunate to have a son like Rāma.
Rāma takes leave of Agastya and proceeds to Pancavaṭi following the directions given by Agastya.
3.13.1
राम प्रीतोऽस्मि भद्रं ते
परितुष्टोऽस्मि लक्ष्मण ।
अभिवादयितुं यन्मां
प्राप्तौ स्थ स्सह सीतया ॥
I am happy, O Rāma, may all good attend you, and I am extremely pleased with you, O Lakshmaṇa, that both of you came with Seetā to offer your obeisance to me.
3.13.2
अध्वश्रमेण वां खेदो
बाधते प्रचुरश्रमः ।
व्यक्तमुत्कण्ठते चापि
मैथिली जनकात्मजा ॥
You are both weary with the fatigue of the way; and it is evident that Maithili, the daughter of Janaka, also wants to take rest.
3.13.3
एषा हि सुकुमारी च
दुःखैश्च न विमानिता ।
प्राज्यदोषं वनं प्राप्ता
भर्तृस्नेहप्रचोदिता ॥
She is delicate and never has suffered hardships. She came to the Vana which abounds in perils, prompted by her love for her husband.
3.13.4
यथैषा रमते राम
इह सीता तथा कुरु ।
दुष्करं कृतवत्येषा
वने त्वामनुगच्छती ॥
She took upon a hard task in following you to the Vana; you should see to it, Rāma, that Seetā finds pleasure in living here.
3.13.5
एषा हि प्रकृतिः स्त्रीणां
आसृष्टे रघुनन्दन ।
समस्थमनुरज्यान्ति
विषमस्थं त्यजन्ति च ॥
The nature of women, from the beginning, has been such that they give their love to the man whom prosperity attends, but abandon one whom adversity besieges.
3.13.6
शतह्रदानां लोलत्वं
शस्त्राणां तीक्ष्णतां तथा ।
गरूडानिलयोश्शैघ्र्यं
अनुगच्छन्ति योषितः ॥
Women follow very closely, lightning in fickleness, weapons in sharpness and Garuḍa and wind in impetuousness.
3.13.7
इयं तु भवतो भार्या
दोषैरेतैर्विवर्जिता ।
श्लाघ्या च व्यपदेश्या च
यथा देवी ह्यरुन्धती ॥
But this wife of yours is totally free from such flaws; she is worthy of all praise and fit to be held up as an exemplar, like the divine Arundhati herself.
3.13.8
अलङ्कृतोऽयं देशश्च
यत्र सौमित्रिणा सह ।
वैदेह्या चानया राम
वत्स्यसि त्वमरिन्दम ॥
O Rāma, subduer of foes! This region feels embellished by the presence of you along with Sowmitri and Seetā.
3.13.9
एवमुक्तस्समुनिना
राघवस्संयताञ्जलिः ।
उवाच प्रश्रितं वाक्यं
ऋषिं दीप्तमिवानलम् ॥
Told thus by the Muni, Rāghava, with his palms joined in reverence, said these words in all deference to that Ṛshi who was radiant like fire.
3.13.10
धन्योऽस्म्यनुगृहीतोऽस्मि
यस्य मे मुनिपुङ्गवः ।
गुणैस्सभ्रातृभार्यस्य
वरदः परितुष्यति ॥
I am indeed fortunate and blessed that the eminent Muni, the bestower of boons, is pleased with the virtues of my brother, my wife and me.
3.13.11
किन्तु व्यादिश मे देशं
सोदकं बहुकाननम् ।
यत्राश्रमपदं कृत्वा
वसेयं निरतस्सुखम् ॥
But please tell me of a place, well-wooded and with plenty of water, where I can build an Āṡrama and live comfortably.
3.13.12
ततोऽब्रवीन्मुनिश्रेष्ठः
श्रुत्वा रामस्य तद्वचः ।
ध्यात्वा मुहूर्तं धर्मात्मा
धीरो धीरतरं वचः ॥
Then, hearing those words of Rāma and contemplating for a moment, that best among Munis, the Dharmātma, the considerate one, said these well-considered words:
3.13.13
इतो द्वियोजने तात
बहुमूलफलोदकः ।
देशो बहुमृगश्श्रीमान्
पञ्चवट्यभिविश्रुतः ॥
My dear child! There is a splendid place known well as Pancavaṭi at a distance of two Yōjanas from here, with plenty of roots, fruits and water and deer in abundance.
3.13.14
तत्र गत्वाश्रमपदं
कृत्वा सौमित्रिणा सह ।
रंस्यसे त्वं पितुर्वाक्यं
यथोक्तमनुपालयन् ॥
Going there and building an Āṡrama along with Sowmitri, you can enjoy, while scrupulously following your father’s behest.
3.13.15
कालोऽयं गतभूयिष्ठो
यः काल स्तव राघव ।
समयो यो नरेन्द्रेण
कृतो दशरथेन ते ।
तीर्णप्रतिज्ञः काकुत्स्थ
सुखं राज्ये निवत्स्यसि ॥
O Rāghava! A good portion of the time stipulated by the King Daṡaratha has already passed. O Kākutstha! You would fulfill the oath and shall live happily in the kingdom!
3.13.16
धन्यस्ते जनको राम
स राजा रघुनन्दन ।
यस्त्वया ज्येष्ठपुत्रेण
ययातिरिव तारितः ॥
O Rāma, O delight of Raghus! Your father, the king, is indeed fortunate like Yayāti, to be redeemed by you, his eldest son!
3.13.17
विदितो ह्येष वृत्तान्तो
मम सर्वस्तवानघ ।
तपसश्च प्रभावेण
स्नेहाद्दशरथस्य च ॥
O blameless one! I learned about all these happenings because of my affection for Daṡaratha, using my powers of Tapa!
3.13.18
हृदयस्थश्च ते छन्दो
विज्ञातस्तपसा मया ।
इहावासं प्रतिज्ञाय
मया सह तपोवने ।
अतश्च त्वामहं ब्रूमि
गच्छ पञ्चवटीमिति ॥
I know what you are thinking deep in your heart, through my powers of Tapa. Though I would be glad if you live here along with me in this Vana of Tapasvis, I would rather advice you to go to Pancavaṭi.
3.13.20
स हि रम्यो वनोद्देशो
मैथिली तत्र रंस्यते ।
स देशश्श्लाघनीयश्च
नातिदूरे च राघव ॥
That region of Vana is indeed beautiful. Maithili will certainly enjoy it there. And that place worthy of mention is not too far.
3.13.21
गोदावर्यास्समीपे च
मैथिली तत्र रंस्यते ।
प्राज्यमूलफलश्चैव
नानाद्विजगणायुतः ।
विविक्तश्च महाबाहो
पुण्योरम्यस्तथैव च ॥
O you of mighty arm! Maithili would enjoy that place which is near Gōdāvaree. It is beautiful and holy with plenty of roots, fruits and flocks of many kinds of birds and without much movement of people.
3.13.22
भवानपि सदारश्च
शक्तश्च परिरक्षणे ।
अपि चात्र वसन्राम
तापसान्पालयिष्यसि ॥
You will have your wife with you, but you are fully capable of protecting her. And living there, O Rāma, you can also protect the Tapasvis.
3.13.23
एतदालक्ष्यते वीर
मधूकानां महद्वनम् ।
उत्तरेणास्य गन्तव्यं
न्यग्रोधमभिगच्छता ॥
O Veera! The great Vana with the Madhūka trees can be seen from here. Go to the north of it towards the Banyan tree.
3.13.24
ततः स्थलमुपारुह्य
पर्वतस्याविदूरतः ।
ख्यातः पञ्चवटीत्येव
नित्यपुष्पितकाननः ॥
From there, go up the high grounds and the forest famously known as Pancavaṭi which is perennially in flower, is not far from that mountain.
3.13.25
अगस्त्येनैवमुक्तस्तु
रामस्सौमित्रिणा सह ।
सत्कृत्यामन्त्रयामास
तमृषिं सत्यवादिनम् ॥
Thus advised by Agastya, Rāma along with Sowmitri, paid his respects to him and took leave of the Ṛshi who always advocated the truth.
3.13.26
तौ तु तेनाभ्यनुज्ञातौ
कृतपादाभिवन्दनौ ।
तदाश्रमात्पञ्चवटीं
जग्मतुस्सीतया सह ॥
Then, taking his permission and prostrating at his feet, both (the brothers) left for Pancavaṭi along with Seetā.
3.13.27
गृहीतचापौ तु नराधिपात्मजौ
विषक्ततूणौ समरेष्वकातरौ ।
यथोपदिष्टेन पथा महर्षिणा
प्रजग्मतुः पञ्चवटीं समाहितौ ॥
Taking the bows in hand and tying the quivers (to their shoulders) those princes, fully alert and with no fear of battle, went to Pancavaṭi following the directions given by the Maharshi.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे त्रयोदशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirteenth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
