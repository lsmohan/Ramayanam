Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 15
In this Sarga, Vāli comes out of the inner quarters annoyed by the roar of Sugreeva. Tārā tells him that she feels anxious and suspicious about Sugreeva returning to fight with such arrogance and zest. She expresses her apprehension that he might have got some additional help.
She also tells him that she heard from Aṅgada that Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, the princes of Ayōdhyā have come to help Sugreeva. She advises Vāli to install Sugreeva, his younger brother, and the best of his kin, as heir-apparent and seek the friendship of Rāma. Vāli, driven by his fate does not listen to her.
4.15.1
अथ तस्य निनादं तम्
सुग्रीवस्य महात्मनः ।
शुश्रावान्तः पुर गतो
वाली भ्रातुरमर्षणः ॥
Vāli, who was in the inner quarters, was annoyed on hearing that strident shout of his brother, Sugreeva, the Mahātma.
4.15.2
श्रुत्वा तु तस्य निनदम्
सर्वभूतप्रकम्पनम् ।
मदश्चैकपदे नष्टः
क्रोधश्चापतितो महान् ॥
That roar, which made every creature tremble, dissipated his inebriation in an instant and aroused a great wrath.
4.15.3
स तु रोषपरीताङ्गो
वाली स कनकप्रभः ।
उपरक्त इवादित्यः
सद्यो निष्प्रभतां गतः ॥
Vāli of golden sheen instantly lost his luster, like the sun when eclipsed, with his rage taking over every single limb of his.
4.15.4
वाली दंष्ट्राकरालस्तु
क्रोधाद्दीप्ताग्निसन्निभः ।
भात्युत्पतितपद्मस्तु
समृणाळ इव ह्रदः ॥
Vāli’s protruding fierce teeth, as he was ablaze in raze, were like red lotuses that arose, along with their stacks, from a pond.
4.15.5
शब्दं दुर्मर्षणं श्रुत्वा
निष्पपात ततो हरिः ।
वेगेन चरणन्यासैः
दारयन्निव मेदिनीम् ॥
On hearing that intolerable roar, the Vānara walked out fast, as if he would tear the earth apart with his feet.
4.15.6
तं तु तारा परिष्वज्य
स्नेहाद्दर्शितसौहृदा ।
उवाच त्रस्तसम्भ्रान्ता
हितोदर्कमिदं वचः ॥
Tārā embraced him out of love for him, and showing all her affection, spoke these words that would bode well for him, in a flutter of anxiety.
4.15.7
साधु क्रोधमिमं वीर
नदीवेगमिवागतम् ।
शयनादुत्थितः काल्यम्
त्यज भुक्तामिव स्रजम् ॥
Let go of this anger completely, O Veera, that has come upon you like rapids on a stream, just as one, rising from bed in the morning, would throw away the flowers of yester-night!
4.15.8
सहसा तव निष्क्रामो
मम तावन्न रोचते ।
श्रूयतां चाभिधास्यामि
यन्निमित्तं निवार्यसे ॥
I do not like you rushing like this immediately. Please listen, I will tell you why I am trying to stop you.
4.15.9
पूर्वमापतितः क्रोधात्
स त्वामाह्वयते युधि ।
निष्पत्य च निरस्तस्ते
हन्यमानो दिशो गतः ॥
He came here once before, and challenged you to a fight. You went out and beat him. Losing the fight, he fled in every direction.
4.15.10
त्वया तस्य निरस्तस्य
पीडितस्य विशेषतः ।
इहैत्य पुनराह्वानम्
शङ्कां जनयतीव मे ॥
His coming back after being thus repulsed and completely crushed, and challenging you again, fills me with anxious doubts.
4.15.11
दर्पश्च व्यवसायश्च
यादृशस्तस्य नर्दतः ।
निनादश्चापि संरम्भो
नैतदल्पं हि कारणम् ॥
The arrogance, the zest, and the urgency evident in the sound of his roar, cannot be for a trivial reason.
4.15.12
नासहायमहं मन्ये
सुग्रीवं तमिहागतम् ।
अवष्टब्धसहायश्च
यमाश्रित्यैष गर्जति ॥
I do not think Sugreeva has come here unaccompanied. The confidence in his roar indicates that he has got the help of someone extraordinary.
4.15.13
प्रकृत्या निपुणश्चैव
बुद्धिमांश्चैव वानरः ।
अपरीक्षितवीर्येण
सुग्रीवस्सहनैष्यति ॥
He, the Vānara, is very skilled and shrewd by nature. He would not make friends with anyone without testing one’s valor.
4.15.14
पूर्वमेव मया वीर
श्रुतं कथयतो वचः ।
अङ्गदस्य कुमारस्य
वक्ष्याम्यद्य हितं वचः ॥
O Veera! I heard something being said earlier. Let me relay to you those words of our son Aṅgada, which are in your interest.
4.15.15
अङ्गदस्तु कुमारोऽयम्
वनान्तमुपनिर्गतः ।
प्रवृत्तिस्तेन कथिता
चारैरासीन्निवेदिता ॥
Our son Aṅgada brought news when he came back from the deep woods, which was told to him by informers.
4.15.16
अयोध्याधिपतेः पुत्रौ
शूरौ समरदुर्जयौ ।
इक्ष्वाकूणां कुले जातौ
प्रथितौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
सुग्रीवप्रियकामार्थम्
प्राप्तौ तत्र दुरासदौ ॥
Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, sons of the Lord of Ayōdhyā, born into the lineage of Ikshwāku, who are heroes invincible in fight and difficult to stand against, have come to fulfill the wish of Sugreeva.
4.15.17
स ते भ्रातुर्हि विख्यातः
सहायो रणकर्मणि ।
रामः परबलामर्दी
युगान्ताग्निरिवोत्थितः ॥
Rāma, who is famously known for his exploits in battles and for decimating enemy forces as if he were the fire that brings an end to the Yuga, is now an ally of Sugreeva.
4.15.18
निवासवृक्षः साधूनाम्
आपन्नानां परा गतिः ।
आर्तानां संश्रयश्चैव
यशसश्चैकभाजनम् ॥
He is the tree under which good people find shelter. He is the recourse for people in hardship. He is the refuge for those who are distressed. He alone is the place where renown can be found.
4.15.19
ज्ञानविज्ञानसम्पन्नः
निदेशे निरतः पितुः ।
धातूनामिव शैलेन्द्रो
गुणानामाकरो महान् ॥
He is rich in his awareness and understanding. He always abides by the will of his father. Just as a mountain is a repository of minerals, he is a great mine of virtues.
4.15.20
तत्क्षमं न विरोधस्ते
सह तेन महात्मना ।
दुर्जयेनाप्रमेयेन
रामेण रणकर्मसु ॥
It is not good for you to make an enemy of Rāma, a Mahātma, who is immeasurably invincible in every kind of combat in a battle.
4.15.21
शूर वक्ष्यामि ते किञ्चित्
न चेच्छाम्यभ्यसूयितुम् ।
श्रूयतां क्रियतां चैव
तव वक्ष्यामि यद्धितम् ॥
O Ṡūra! Let me give my humble opinion. I don’t like you becoming angry with me! I hope you hear what I say that is for your own good and do accordingly.
4.15.22
यौवराज्येन सुग्रीवम्
तूर्णं साध्वभिषेचय ।
विग्रहं मा कृथा वीर
भ्रात्रा राजन्यवीयसा ॥
Install Sugreeva without delay as heir-apparent. O Veera! You must not be at odds with your younger brother!
4.15.23
अहं हि ते क्षमं मन्ये
तेन रामेण सौहृदम् ।
सुग्रीवेण च सम्प्रीतिम्
वैरमुत्सृज्य दूरतः ॥
I think it would be right and proper to win Rāma’s friendship and Sugreeva’s affection, renouncing all feelings of enmity.
4.15.24
लालनीयो हि ते भ्राता
यवीयानेष वानरः ।
तत्र वा सन्निहस्थो वा
सर्वथा बन्धुरेव ते ॥
That Vānara, your brother, is after all, younger to you and hence deserves to be doted upon. Whether he is here or elsewhere, he is your kin in every way.
4.15.25
न हि तेन समं बन्धुम्
भुवि पश्यामि कञ्चन ।
दानमानादिसत्कारैः
कुरुष्व प्रत्यनन्तरम् ।
वैरमेतत्समुत्सृज्य
तव पार्श्वे स तिष्ठतु ॥
I cannot think of anyone else on this earth who is as close a kin to you as he is. Promote him to a place that is next only to yourself giving gifts, honor and regard accordingly. May he stand by your side, giving up all this enmity!
4.15.27
सुग्रीवो विपुलग्रीवः
तवबन्धुस्सदा मतः ।
भ्रातृस्सौहृदमालम्ब
नान्या गतिरिहास्ति ते ॥
Sugreeva of wide neck is always considered to be your kin. Show brotherly affection towards him. There is no other alternative for you.
4.15.28
यदि ते मत्प्रियं कार्यम्
यदि चावैषि मां हिताम् ।
याच्यमानः प्रियत्वेन
साधु वाक्यं कुरुष्व मे ॥
If you would do my pleasure and believe that I only wish good for you, you must follow my advice sincerely as I implore you out of love.
4.15.29
प्रसीद पथ्यं शृणु जल्पितं हि मे
न रोष मेवानुविधातुमर्हसि ।
क्षमो हि ते कोसलराजसूनुना
न विग्रहश्शक्रसमानतेजसा ॥
Be kind and listen to my humble and well-meaning advice. You shall not be led by your anger. It is not good to stand against the son of the King of Kōsala, whose prowess equals that of Ṡakra.
4.15.30
तदा हि तारा हितमेव वाक्यं
तं वालिनं पथ्यमिदं बभाषे ।
न रोचते तद्वचनं हि तस्य
कालाभिपन्नस्य विनाशकाले ॥
Thus spoke Tārā to Vali, words that were good for him and in his best interest. But those words were not liked by him, as he was driven by fate and the time for his end was near.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे पञ्चदशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifteenth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
