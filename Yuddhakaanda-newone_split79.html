Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 79
In this Sarga, the Rākshasas led by Makarāksha attack the Vānaras. Unable to challenge their assault, the Vānaras flee. Then Rāma puts a check to the Rākshasas by showering a volley of arrows. On seeing the Rākshasas thus restrained, Makarāksha becomes furious and challenges Rāma to a duel, bragging about his strength.
A fierce fight ensues between Rāma and Makarāksha. Rāma breaks the bow and chariot of Makarāksha and strikes down the horses and charioteer. Makarāksha hurls a powerful spear, which he had obtained from Rudra, at Rāma. Rāma shatters it to pieces with his arrows. Finally, Rāma strikes down Makarāksha with the Astra of Agni.
6.79.1
निर्गतं मकाराक्षं ते
दृष्ट्वा वानरयूथपाः ।
आप्लुत्य सहसा सर्वे
योद्धुकामा व्यवस्थिताः ॥
As soon as they saw Makarāksha set out, the Vānara chiefs sprang up and became ready for the fight.
6.79.2
ततः प्रवृत्तं सुमहत्
तद्युद्धं रोमहर्षणम् ।
निशाचरैः प्लवङ्गानाम्
देवानां दानवैरिव ॥
Then there ensued a great, hair-rising fight between the Night-rovers and the Vānaras, like the one between Dēvas and Dānavas.
6.79.3
वृक्षशूलनिपातैश्च
शिलापरिघपातनैः ।
अन्योन्यं मर्दयन्ति स्म
तदा कपिनिशाचराः ॥
Hurling trees and spears, throwing rocks and Parighas, the Vānaras and Night-rovers then battered each other.
6.79.4
शक्तिखड्गगदाकुन्तैः
तोमरैश्च निशाचराः ।
पट्टिशैर्भिन्दिपालैश्च
बाणपातैः समन्ततः ।
पाशमुद्गरदण्डैश्च
निर्घातैश्चापरे तदा ।
कदनं कपिवीराणाम्
चक्रुस्ते रजनीचराः ॥
The Night-rovers fought with the Vānara warriors everywhere striking them down with Ṡaktis, swords, maces, Kuntas, Tōmaras, Paṭṭiṡas, Bhindipālas, arrows, nooses, Mudgaras, rods and other weapons.
6.79.6
बाणौघैरर्दिताश्चापि
खरपुत्रेण वानराः ।
सम्भ्रान्तमनसः सर्वे
दुद्रुवुर्भयपीडिताः ॥
Harried by the volley of arrows of the son of Khara, all the Vānaras were bewildered and fled, oppressed by fear.
6.79.7
तान् दृष्ट्वा राक्षसाः सर्वे
द्रवमाणान् वलीमुखान् ।
नेदुस्ते सिंहवद्धृष्टा
राक्षसा जितकाशिनः ॥
Seeing the Vānaras flee, the Rākshasas roared like lions in delight, basking in the glory of victory.
6.79.8
विद्रवत्सु तदा तेषु
वानरेषु समन्ततः ।
रामस्तान् वारयामास
शरवर्षेण राक्षसान् ॥
Seeing that the Vānaras were fleeing everywhere, Rāma, with a shower of arrows, put a check on the Rākshasas.
6.79.9
वारितान् राक्षसान् दृष्ट्वा
मकराक्षो निशाचरः ।
क्रोधानलसमाविष्टो
वचनं चेदमब्रवीत् ॥
On seeing the Rākshasas held at bay, the Night-rover Makarāksha, gripped by a fiery rage, said these words:
6.79.10
तिष्ठ राम मया सार्धम्
द्वन्द्वयुद्धं ददामि ते ।
त्याजयिष्यामि ते प्राणान्
धनुर्मुक्तैः शितैः शरैः ॥
Stay right there and engage with me in a duel! I shall extricate the life force out of you with the sharp arrows that are let loose from my bow.
6.79.11
यत्तदा दण्डकारण्ये
पितरं हतवान् मम ।
मदग्रतः स्वकर्मस्थम्
दृष्ट्वा रोषोऽभिवर्धते ॥
My outrage swells as I see the one who killed my father in Daṇḍaka Araṇya stand right in front of me to gain his own ends.
6.79.12
दह्यन्ते भृशमङ्गानि
दुरात्मन् मम राघव ।
यन्मयाऽसि न दृष्टस्त्वम्
तस्मिन् काले महावने ॥
The fact that I did not set my eyes on you at that time in that great Vana, burns every limb of mine, O evil one, O Rāghava!
6.79.13
दिष्ट्याऽसि दर्शनं राम
मम त्वं प्राप्तवानिह ।
काङ्क्षितोऽसि क्षुधार्तस्य
सिंहस्येवेतरो मृगः ॥
By the grace of Providence, O Rāma, I get to see you here, whom I have been seeking like a starving lion seeking deer!
6.79.14
अद्य मद्बाणवेगेन
प्रेतराङ्विषयं गतः ।
ये त्वया निहता वीराः
सह तैश्च समेष्यसि ॥
Dispatched by the force of my arrows, you shall go to the abode of the king of the dead, and join the heroes you slew.
6.79.15
बहुनाऽत्र किमुक्तेन
शृणु राम वचो मम ।
पश्यन्तु सकला लोकाः
त्वां मां चैव रणाजिरे ॥
There is no need to say it in so many ways; listen to my words, O Rāma: 'may all the worlds see (the spectacle of) you and me engaged in a duel.'
6.79.16
अस्त्रैर्वा गदया वापि
बाहुभ्यां वा रणाजिरे ।
अभ्यस्तं येन वा राम
वर्ततां तेन वा मृधम् ॥
Let the fight be with Astras or mace or with bare hands, whatever you have practiced, O Rāma!
6.79.17
मकराक्षवचः क्षुत्वा
रामो दशरथात्मजः ।
अब्रवीत् प्रहसन् वाक्यम्
उत्तरोत्तरवादिनम् ॥
Hearing those words of Makarāksha, who went on to blabber without an end, Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha said these words, laughing:
6.79.18
कत्थसे किं वृथा रक्षो
बहून्यसदृशानि तु ।
न रणे शक्यते जेतुम्
विना युद्धेन वाग्बलात् ॥
Why do you waste your breath bragging so much about yourself, O Rākshasa! You cannot win a battle without fighting, with the mere force of your words.
6.79.19
चतुर्दशसहस्राणि
रक्षसां त्वत्पिता च यः ।
त्रिशिरा दूषणश्चैव
दण्डके निहता मया ॥
Fourteen thousand Rākshasas including your father, and also Triṡira and Dūshaṇa were slain by me in Daṇḍaka.
6.79.20
स्वाशितास्तव मांसेन
गृध्रगोमायुवायसाः ।
भविष्यन्त्यद्य वै पाप
तीक्ष्णतुण्डनखाङ्कुराः ॥
Vultures, jackals and crows, with sharp beaks and nails should have a nice meal out of your flesh today, you sinful one!
6.79.21
राघवेणैवमुक्तस्तु
खरपुत्रो निशाचरः ।
बाणौघानमुचत्तस्मै
राघवाय रणाजिरे ॥
On this retort by Rāghava, the son of Khara, the Night-rover, released a volley of arrows on Rāghava, in that battlefield.
6.79.22
तान् शरान् शरवर्षेण
रामश्चिच्छेद नैकधा ।
निपेतुर्भुवि ते छिन्ना
रुक्मपुङ्खाः सहस्रशः ॥
Rāma cut those arrows into pieces with his own shower of arrows. And they, with gold rings, fell on the ground, shivered into thousands of bits.
6.79.23
तद्युद्धमभवत्तत्र
समेत्यान्योन्यमोजसा ।
रक्षसः खरपुत्रस्य
सूनोर्दशरथस्य च ॥
Thus ensued a great fight between the Rākshasa, the son of Khara, and the son of Daṡaratha, as they collided with each other with all their power.
6.79.24
जीमूतयोरिवाकाशे
शब्दो ज्यातलयोस्तदा ।
धनुर्मुक्तः स्वनोत्कृष्टः
श्रूयते च रणाजिरे ॥
In that battlefield, the sound of the arrows as they left the bow, mixed with the sound of the bowstring and the thud of the striking palms, filled the sky, like thunder.
6.79.25
देवदानवगन्धर्वाः
किन्नराश्च महोरगाः ।
अन्तरिक्षगताः सर्वे
द्रष्टुकामास्तदद्भुतम् ॥
All the Dēvas, Dānavas, Gandharvas, Kinnaras and the great Uragas crowded in the sky to see that amazing spectacle.
6.79.26
विद्धमन्योन्यगात्रेषु
द्विगुणं वर्धते परम् ।
कृतप्रतिकृतान्योन्यम्
कुरुतां तौ रणाजिरे ॥
Their strength doubled as they battered each other's body and as they responded to each other's challenge in that battlefield.
6.79.27
राममुक्तांस्तु बाणौघान्
राक्षसस्त्वच्छिनद्रणे ।
रक्षोमुक्तांस्तु रामो वै
नैकधा प्राच्छिनच्छरैः ॥
The volley of arrows shot by Rāma were smashed by the Rākshasa and those released by the Rākshasa were similarly shattered into many, many tiny bits, by the arrows of Rāma, in that battle.
6.79.28
बाणौघैर्वितताः सर्वा
दिशश्च प्रदिशस्तथा ।
सञ्छन्ना वसुधा चैव
समन्तान्न प्रकाशते ॥
The four quarters and the intermediate directions were all obscured by that deluge of arrows, and the earth everywhere was covered by them, becoming scarcely visible.
6.79.29
ततः क्रुद्धो महाबाहुः
धनुश्चिच्छेद रक्षसः ।
अष्टाभिरथ नाराचैः
सूतं विव्याध राघवः ॥
Rāghava of mighty arm, infuriated, broke down the bow of the Rākshasa and then with eight Nārāca arrows struck down his charioteer.
6.79.30
भित्त्वा रथं शरै रामो
हत्वा अश्वानपातयत् ।
विरथो वसुधास्थः स
मकराक्षो निशाचरः ॥
Rāma, with his arrows, broke his chariot and struck down and killed the horses. Losing his chariot, Makarāksha, the Night-rover, stood on the bare ground.
6.79.31
तत्तिष्ठद्वसुधां रक्षः
शूलं जग्राह पाणिना ।
त्रासनं सर्वभूतानाम्
युगान्ताग्निसमप्रभम् ॥
That Rākshasa, standing there holding a spear in his hand, blazed like the Fire of Dissolution, terrifying all the creatures.
6.79.32
दुरवापं महच्छूलम्
रुद्रवत्तं भयंकरम् ।
जाज्वल्यमानमाकाशे
संहारास्त्रमिवापरम् ।
यं दृष्ट्वा देवताः सर्वा
भयार्ता विद्रुता दिशः ॥
That ferocious and great spear, that shone like the Astra of Death in the sky, extremely difficult to obtain, at the very sight of which even all the Dēvas fled in terror in every direction in the past, was given by Rudra.
6.79.33
विभ्राम्य तु महच्छूलम्
प्रज्वलन्तं निशाचरः ।
स क्रोधात्प्राहिणोत्तस्मै
राघवाय महात्मने ॥
The Night-rover whirled that great blazing spear and hurled it at Rāghava in fury.
6.79.34
तमापतन्तं ज्वलितम्
खरपुत्रकराच्च्युतम् ।
बाणैस्तु त्रिभिराकाशे
शूलं चिच्छेद राघवः ॥
Rāghava broke off that blazing spear with three arrows while it was still in flight in midair, having left the hand of Khara's son.
6.79.35
स च्छिन्नो नैकाधा शूलो
दिव्यहाटकमण्डितः ।
व्यशीर्यत महोल्केव
रामबाणार्दितो भुवि ॥
That spear, shining in its splendid gold cover, shattered into many pieces struck by the arrows of Rāma, fell on the ground like a meteor.
6.79.36
तच्छूलं निहतं दृष्ट्वा
रामेणाक्लिष्टकर्मणा ।
साधु साध्विति भूतानि
व्याहरन्ति नभोगता ॥
On seeing that spear destroyed by Rāma, who does everything with ease, all creatures that had assembled in the sky applauded, saying, ‘Astounding!’
6.79.37
तं दृष्ट्वा निहतं शूलम्
मकराक्षो निशाचरः ।
मुष्टिमुद्यम्य काकुत्स्थम्
तिष्ठ तिष्ठेति चाब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing that spear destroyed, Makarāksha, the Night-rover, raised his doubled fist and rushed upon Kākutstha, saying, 'stay right there, (here I come)!'
6.79.38
स तं दृष्ट्वा पतन्तं वै
प्रहस्य रघुनन्दनः ।
पावकास्त्रं ततो रामः
सन्दधे तु शरासने ॥
Seeing him thus dash towards him, the delight of Raghus laughed and mounted the Astra of Agni on his bow.
6.79.39
तेनास्त्रेण हतं रक्षः
काकुत्स्थेन तदा रणे ।
सञ्छिन्नहृदयं तत्र
पपात च ममार च ॥
Struck down by Kākutstha with that Astra in the battle, the Rākshasa, with his heart shattered into pieces, fell down dead.
6.79.40
दृष्ट्वा ते राक्षसाः सर्वे
मकराक्षस्य पातनम् ।
लङ्कामेवाभ्यधावन्त
रामबाणभयार्दिताः ॥
Seeing Makarāksha thus felled down, all the Rākshasas fled to Laṅkā, terrified of Rāma’s arrows.
6.79.41
दशरथनृपपुत्रबाणवेगै
रजनिचरं निहतं खरात्मजं तम् ।
प्रददृशुरथ देवताः प्रहृष्टा
गिरिमिव वज्रहतं यथा विकीर्णम् ॥
The Dēvas were in extreme joy, seeing that Night-rover, the son of Khara, shattered into pieces, struck by the force of the arrows of King Daṡaratha's son, like a mountain struck by Vajra.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे एकोनाशीतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventy ninth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
