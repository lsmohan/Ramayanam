Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 17
In this Sarga, Vibheeshaṇa comes to Rāma seeking refuge in him. Seeing him appear in the sky with weapons and four followers, Sugreeva and others suspect that he has come to attack them, and become ready to fight with him.
Vibheeshaṇa approaches them and introduces himself. He says that he is upset with the conduct of his brother and empathizes with the fate of the miserable Seetā, and that he failed in convincing his brother to give Seetā back to Rāma, and that his brother ill-treated him, and thus he came seeking refuge in Rāma.
Sugreeva informs Rāma about the arrival of Vibheeshaṇa and expresses his misgivings about him, saying that he could be a Rākshasa in disguise trying to infiltrate into the enemy forces only to strike at an opportune time. He suggests that Vibheeshaṇa be killed.
Then Rāma asks all the Vānara chiefs to give their opinions regarding what should be done. Many of the Vānara chiefs speak up, essentially agreeing with Sugreeva, that the Rākshasa cannot be trusted. They suggest that they should send a spy to find out more about him, and take action based on what they learn.
Then Hanumān speaks out, disagreeing with them. He says that it is not practical to send a spy and find out more. He says that he rather sees Vibheeshaṇa as genuine, with a genuine reason to seek refuge in Rāma seeing the slaying of Vāli and Sugreeva’s coronation.
6.17.1
इत्युक्त्वा परुषं वाक्यम्
रावणं रावणानुजः ।
आजगाम महूर्तेन
यत्र रामः सलक्ष्मणः ॥
Having spoken bluntly thus to Rāvaṇa, the younger brother of Rāvaṇa went, within the same hour, to the place where Rāma and Lakshmaṇa were.
6.17.2
तं मेरुशिखराकारम्
दीप्तामिव शतह्रदाम् ।
गगनस्थं महीस्थास्ते
ददृशुर्वानराधिपाः ॥
The Vānara chiefs on the ground saw him appear in the sky, resembling a peak of Mēru mountain and blazing like a streak of lightning.
6.17.3
ते चाप्यनुचरास्तस्य
चत्वारो भीमविक्रमाः ।
तेऽपि वर्मायुधोपेता
भूषणोत्तम भूषिताः ॥
With him also were four of his followers of formidable prowess. They were also armed with weapons and shields and adorned with the best of ornaments.
6.17.4
स च मेघाचलप्रख्यो
वज्रायुधसमप्रभः ।
वरायुधधरो वीरो
दिव्याभरणभूषितः ॥
He resembled a cloud and a mountain with his radiance blazing like that of Vajra, the weapon (of Indra), He was wielding the best of weapons and was adorned with splendid ornaments.
6.17.5
तमात्मपञ्चमं दृष्ट्वा
सुग्रीवो वानराधिपः ।
वानरैः सह दुर्द्धर्षः
चिन्तयामास बुद्धिमान् ॥
On seeing him, who was accompanied by four others, Sugreeva, the invincible and sagacious king of the Vānaras, thought over the matter, along with the other Vānaras.
6.17.6
चिन्तयित्वा मुहूर्तं तु
वानरांस्तानुवाच ह ।
हनुमत्प्रमुखान् सर्वान्
इदं वचनमुत्तमम् ॥
After thinking for a while, he said these well considered words to all the Vānara chiefs like Hanumān:
6.17.7
एष सर्वायुधोपेतः
चतुर्भिः सह राक्षसैः ।
राक्षसोऽभ्येति पश्यध्वम्
अस्मान् हन्तुं न संशयः ॥
This Rākshasa, armed with all kinds of weapons and supported by four other Rākshasas is coming to kill us, without a doubt.
6.17.8
सुग्रीवस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
सर्वे ते वानरोत्तमाः ।
सालानुद्यम्य शैलांश्च
इदं वचनमब्रुवन् ॥
On hearing those words of Sugreeva, all of those prominent Vānaras, holding up Sāla trees and boulders, said these words:
6.17.9
शीघ्रं व्यादिश नो राजन्
वधायैषां दुरात्मनाम् ।
निपतन्तु हताश्चैते
धरण्यामल्पतेजसः ॥
Command us quick, O king, we will kill these evil souls! These of trifle strength shall fall on the ground, dead.
6.17.10
तेषां सम्भाषमाणानाम्
अन्योन्यं स विभीषणः ।
उत्तरं तीरमासाद्य
खस्थ एव व्यतिष्ठत ॥
As they were talking among themselves, Vibheeshaṇa reached the northern shore and remained hovering in the sky.
6.17.11
उवाच च महाप्राज्ञः
स्वरेण महता महान् ।
सुग्रीवं तांश्च सम्प्रेक्ष्य
सर्वान् वानरयूथपान् ॥
Then the sagacious and mighty (Vibheeshaṇa) saw Sugreeva and the other Vānara chiefs and announced in a clear and loud voice:
6.17.12
रावणो नाम दुर्वृत्तो
राक्षसो राक्षसेश्वरः ।
तस्याहमनुजो भ्राता
विभीषण इति श्रुतः ॥
Rāvaṇa, a Rākshasa given to evil ways, is the King of the Rākshasas. I am his younger brother known as Vibheeshaṇa.
6.17.13
तेन सीता जनस्थानात्
हृता हत्वा जटायुषम् ।
रुद्धा च विवशा दीना
राक्षसीभिः सुरक्षिता ॥
He killed Jaṭāyu and carried off Seetā from Janasthāna. Kept in confinement and closely guarded by Rākshasis, she is helpless and miserable.
6.17.14
तमहं हेतुभिर्वाक्यैः
विविधैश्च न्यदर्शयम् ।
साधु निर्यात्यतां सीता
रामायेति पुनःपुनः ॥
I advised him repeatedly to give Seetā back to Rāma, citing many a good reason to do so.
6.17.15
स च न प्रतिजग्राह
रावणः कालचोदितः ।
उच्यमानं हितं वाक्यम्
विपरीत इवौषधम् ॥
Rāvaṇa, goaded by destiny, did not appreciate the words that I said in his best interest, like a pervert denying medicine.
6.17.16
सोऽहं परुषितस्तेन
दासवच्चावमानितः ।
त्यक्त्वा पुत्रांश्च दारांश्च
राघवं शरणं गतः ॥
I have been insulted by him with harsh words, as if I am his slave. I came to seek refuge in Rāghava, giving up my wife and children.
6.17.17
सर्वलोकशरण्याय
राघवाय महात्मने ।
निवेदयत मां क्षिप्रम्
विभीषणमुपस्थितम् ॥
Quickly inform Rāghava the Mahātma, the refuge of all the worlds, that Vibheeshaṇa has come to him.
6.17.18
एतत्तु वचनं श्रुत्वा
सुग्रीवो लघुविक्रमः ।
लक्ष्मणस्याग्रतो रामम्
संरब्धमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
On hearing those words, Sugreeva of quick strides, went up to Rāma and told him in great agitation in the presence of Lakshmaṇa:
6.17.19
रावणस्यानुजो भ्राता
विभीषण इति श्रुतः ।
चतुर्भिः सह रक्षोभिः
भवन्तं शरणं गतः ॥
Rāvaṇa’s younger brother, known as Vibheeshaṇa along with four other Rākshasas, seeks refuge in you.
6.17.20
मन्त्रे व्यूहे नये चारे
युक्तो भवितुमर्हसि ।
वानराणां च भद्रन्ते
परेषां च परन्तप ॥
O Scorcher of the foe! It behooves you to bring your attention to planning, strategy, diplomacy and spies! May all bode well for you regarding both the Vānaras and the enemies.
6.17.21
अन्तर्धानगता ह्येते
राक्षसाः कामरूपिणः ।
शूराश्च निकृतिज्ञाश्च
तेषां जातु न विश्वसेत् ॥
These Rākshasas, who can take any form at will, can remain unseen. They are valiant and crafty. They can never be trusted.
6.17.22
प्रणिधी राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
रावणस्य भवेदयम् ।
अनुप्रविश्य सोऽस्मासु
भेदं कुर्यान्न संशयः ॥
This must be the spy of Rāvaṇa, the Rākshasa King. Joining us, he would, without a doubt, create dissension amongst us.
6.17.23
अथवा स्वयमेवैष
छिद्रमासाद्य बुद्धिमान् ।
अनुप्रविश्य विश्वस्ते
कदाचित् प्रहरेदपि ॥
Or, he himself might be clever enough to join us, gain your confidence, find the weak spots, and strike at any time.
6.17.24
मित्राटवीबलं चैव
मौलं भृत्यबलं तथा ।
सर्वमेतद्बलं ग्राह्यम्
वर्जयित्वा द्विषद्बलम् ॥
One should utilize only the standing army, hired mercenaries, the forces of one’s allies and those of the forest-belt around one’s kingdom, but never take help from the forces of one’s enemies.
6.17.25
प्रकृत्या राक्षसोह्येष
भ्राताऽमित्रस्य ते प्रभो ।
आगतश्च रिपोः साक्षात्
कथमस्मिन् हि विश्वसेत् ॥
He is a Rākshasa by his very nature. And he is the brother of your foe. He has come straight from the enemy camp. How can we repose any trust in him?
6.17.26
रावणेन प्रणिहितम्
तमवेहि विभीषणम् ।
तस्याहं निग्रहं मन्ये
क्षमं क्षमवतां वर ॥
Know that Rāvaṇa himself sent Vibheeshaṇa. I think the right thing to do is to restrain him, O foremost among the forgivers!
6.17.27
राक्षसो जिह्मया बुद्ध्या
सन्दिष्टोऽयमिहागतः ।
प्रहर्तुं मायया च्छन्नो
विश्वस्ते त्वयि राघव ॥
O Rāghava! This Rākshasa who has come here was sent with nefarious motives, to strike you secretly using trickery after gaining your confidence.
6.17.28
प्रविष्टः शत्रुसैन्यं हि
प्राज्ञः शत्रुरतर्कितः ।
निहन्यादन्तरं लब्ध्वा
उलूक इव वायसान् ॥
Cleverly entering the enemy force without raising any suspicion, he will kill when the opportunity presents itself, as the owl in the story did among the crows.
6.17.29
वध्यतामेष दण्डेन
तीव्रेण सचिवैः सह ।
रावणस्य नृशंसस्य
भ्राता ह्येष विभीषणः ॥
This Vibheeshaṇa is the brother of the cruel Rāvaṇa. He along with his advisors should be killed with the most severe punishment.
6.17.30
एवमुक्त्वा तु तं रामम्
संरब्धो वाहिनीपतिः ।
वाक्यज्ञो वाक्यकुशलम्
ततो मौनमुपागमत् ॥
The eloquent, albeit agitated Commander-in-Chief of army said this to Rāma, who himself was skilled at words, and then stayed silent.
6.17.31
सुग्रीवस्य तु तद्वाक्यम्
श्रुत्वा रामो महायशाः ।
समीपस्थानुवाचेदम्
हनुमत्प्रमुखान् हरीन् ॥
On hearing those words of Sugreeva, Rāma of great repute, said this to the Vānara chiefs, like Hanumān, who were near him:
6.17.32
यदुक्तं कपिराजेन
रावणावरजं प्रति ।
वाक्यं हेतुमदर्थ्यं च
भवद्भिरपि तच्छ्रुतम् ॥
You have heard the words spoken by the king of the Vānaras, which are quite reasonable, regarding Rāvaṇa’s younger brother.
6.17.33
सुहृदामर्थकृच्छ्रेषु
युक्तं बुद्धिमता सदा ।
समर्थेनोपसन्देष्टुम्
शाश्वतीं भूतिमिच्छता ॥
It behooves on those who are intelligent, capable and zealous about the abiding welfare (of everyone), to give advice to friends who are in a tough situation.
6.17.34
इत्येवं परिपृष्टास्ते
स्वं स्वं मतमतन्द्रिताः ।
सोपचारं तदा रामम्
ऊचुर्हितचिकीर्षवः ॥
Thus requested, they, who were only eager to please Rāma, expressed their respective opinions with zeal and with all due respects.
6.17.35
अज्ञातं नास्ति ते किञ्चित्
त्रिषु लोकेषु राघव ।
आत्मानं सूचयन् जानन्
पृच्छस्यस्मान् सुहृत्तया ॥
O Rāghava! There is nothing in the three worlds that is not known to you. You truly know and feel the Self. You ask us only out of your good nature.
6.17.36
त्वं हि सत्यव्रतः शूरो
धार्मिको दृढविक्रमः ।
परीक्ष्यकारी स्मृतिमान्
निसृष्टात्मा सुहृत्सु च ॥
You are a Dhārmika with rock-like prowess. You are valiant and steadfast in your vows. Your memory is strong and you check everything carefully. Your heart goes for your friends.
6.17.37
तस्मादेकैकशस्तावत्
ब्रुवन्तु सचिवास्तव ।
हेतुतो मतिसम्पन्नाः
समर्थाश्च पुनस्तथा ॥
Therefore let us, your counsellors, highly intelligent and capable, speak out our opinions one by one, with reasons.
6.17.38
इत्युक्ते राघवायाथ
मतिमानङ्गदोऽग्रतः ।
विभीषणपरीक्षार्थम्
उवाच वचनं हरिः ॥
Then the sagacious Vānara Aṅgada spoke first, suggesting that Vibheeshaṇa should be put to the test.
6.17.39
शत्रोः सकाशात् सम्प्राप्तः
सर्वथा शङ्क्य एव हि ।
विश्वासयोग्यः सहसा
न कर्तव्यो विभीषणः ॥
He has come from the enemy camp and hence must be suspected. Vibheeshaṇa cannot be taken into confidence right away.
6.17.40
छादयित्वाऽऽत्मभावं हि
चरन्ति शठबुद्धयः ।
प्रहरन्ति च रन्ध्रेषु
सोऽनर्थः सुमहान् भवेत् ॥
The crooked ones, anyway, go around hiding their true intentions. They strike when opportunity presents itself. And that would only be a disaster (for us).
6.17.41
अर्थानर्थौ विनिश्चित्य
व्यवसायं भजेत ह ।
गुणतः सङ्ग्रहं कुर्यात्
दोषतस्तु विवर्जयेत् ॥
A path must be pursued only after proper deliberation of pros and cons. Whatever has merits should be pursued and whatever has issues should be abandoned.
6.17.42
यदि दोषो महांस्तस्मिन्
त्यज्यतामविशङ्कितम् ।
गुणान् वपि बहून् ज्ञात्वा
सङ्ग्रहः क्रियतां नृप ॥
If there are any major concerns about him, he should be rejected without a doubt; on the other hand, if we come to know of a good number of positive qualities in him, he should be accepted, O King!
6.17.43
शरभस्त्वथ निश्चित्य
साद्ध्यं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Then Ṡarabha said something more practical:
6.17.43
क्षिप्रमस्मिन्नरव्याघ्र
चारः प्रतिविधीयताम् ।
प्रणिधाय हि चारेण
यथावत् सूक्ष्मबुद्धिना ।
परीक्ष्य च ततः कार्यो
यथान्याय्यं परिग्रहः ॥
O tiger among men! May a spy be appointed in this matter. An appropriate course of action can be taken up after intelligence is gathered through a shrewd spy and after examining it in a proper manner.
6.17.45
जाम्बवांस्त्वथ सम्प्रेक्ष्य
शास्त्रबुद्ध्या विचक्षणः ।
वाक्यं विज्ञापयामास
गुणवद्दोषवर्जितम् ॥
Then the astute Jāmbavān, looking at the matter from the perspective of the science of polity, submitted his opinion, which bore flawless wisdom.
6.17.46
बद्धवैराच्च पापाच्च
राक्षसेन्द्राद्विभीषणः ।
अदेशकाले सम्प्राप्तः
सर्वथा शङ्क्यतामयम् ॥
Vibheeshaṇa comes from the sinful King of the Rākshasas, our arch enemy, at a wrong time and place. He should be suspected in every which way.
6.17.47
ततो मैन्दस्तु सम्प्रेक्ष्य
नयापनयकोविदः ।
वाक्यं वचनसम्पन्नो
बभाषे हेतुमत्तरम् ॥
Thereupon the articulate Mainda, a savant of good and bad polity, thought over it and said these extremely reasonable words:
6.17.48
अनुजो नाम तस्यैष
रावणस्य विभीषणः ।
पृच्छ्यतां मधुरेणायम्
शनैर्नरवरेश्वर ॥
Vibheeshaṇa is none other than the younger brother of Rāvaṇa. O best of the lords of men, let him be interrogated, gently and slowly.
6.17.49
भावमस्य तु विज्ञाय
तत्त्वतस्त्वं करिष्यसि ।
यदि दुष्टो न दुष्टो वा
बुद्धिपूर्वं नरर्षभ ॥
O bull among men! you can take the proper course of action after knowing exactly what he is up to, and after assessing whether he is evil or not.
6.17.50
अथ संस्कारसम्पन्नो
हनूमान् सचिवोत्तमः ।
उवाच वचनं श्लक्ष्णम्
अर्थवन्मधुरं लघु ॥
Then Hanumān, the best of counsellors, who had rich training, said a few, but gentle, pleasant and meaningful words:
6.17.51
न भवन्तं मतिश्रेष्ठम्
समर्थं वदतां वरम् ।
अतिशाययितुं शक्तो
बृहस्पतिरपि ब्रुवन् ॥
You excel in intellect. You are the best among those who are skilled with words. Even Bṛihaspati cannot hope to excel you in speech.
6.17.52
न वादान्नापि सङ्घर्षान्
नाधिक्यान्न च कामतः ।
वक्ष्यामि वचनं राजन्
यथार्थं राम गौरवात् ॥
O Rāma! O King! However, I venture to speak what I genuinely feel only out of regard for you; but not to contradict (anyone), nor to profess something, nor to show my superiority, nor because I am just itching to speak.
6.17.53
अर्थानर्थनिमित्तं हि
यदुक्तं सचिवैस्तव ।
तत्र दोषं प्रपश्यामि
क्रिया न ह्युपपद्यते ॥
I see a flaw in the suggestion made by your counselors to evaluate the pros and cons; it is simply impractical to carry out.
6.17.54
ऋते नियोगात् सामर्थ्यम्
अवबोद्धुं न शक्यते ।
सहसा विनियोगो हि
दोषवान् प्रति भाति मा ॥
(His) capabilities cannot be ascertained without entrusting (him) with some responsibility. But it does not seem right to entrust (him) with a responsibility right away.
6.17.55
चारप्रणिहितं युक्तम्
यदुक्तं सचिवैस्तव ।
अर्थस्यासम्भवात्तत्र
कारणं नोपपद्यते ॥
The suggestion of your counselors, that the right course of action is to send spies, is not a workable strategy because the circumstances do not permit it.
6.17.56
अदेशकाले सम्प्राप्त
इत्ययं यद्विभीषणः ।
विविक्षा तत्र मेऽस्तीयम्
तां निबोध यथामति ॥
Regarding the observation that this Vibheeshaṇa has come here at a wrong time and place, I have something to say, please bear with me and listen:
6.17.57
एष देशश्च कालश्च
भवतीह यथातथा ।
पुरुषात्पुरुषं प्राप्य
तथा दोषगुणावपि ॥
The circumstances here being what they are, he left one person to join the other after judging the virtues and vices of each.
6.17.58
दौरात्म्यं रावणे दृष्ट्वा
विक्रमं च तथा त्वयि ।
युक्तमागमनं तस्य
सदृशं तस्य बुद्धितः ॥
Considering the evil ways of Rāvaṇa and similarly the prowess of yours, it only seems right for him to come here, following what his mind says.
6.17.59
अज्ञातरूपैः पुरुषैः
स राजन् पृच्छ्यतामिति ।
यदुक्तमत्र मे प्रेक्षा
काचिदस्ति समीक्षिता ॥
Upon delving on the suggestion of sending a man in disguise to question him, I have something to say:
6.17.60
पृच्छ्यमानो विशङ्केत
सहसा बुद्धिमान् वचः ।
तत्र मित्रं प्रदुष्येत
मिथ्या पृष्टं सुखागतम् ॥
Any intelligent man, thus questioned, would at once become suspicious. And we might lose an ally, whom we could acquire with no effort, by this kind of untrusting questioning.
6.17.61
अशक्यः सहसा राजन्
भावो वेत्तुं परस्य वै ।
अन्तःस्वभावैर्गीतैस्तैः
नैपुण्यं पश्यतां भृशम् ॥
It is not easy, O King, to find the intentions of others immediately. It is quite difficult for anyone to decipher the true intentions from their eloquent boastings.
6.17.62
नत्वस्य ब्रुवतो जातु
लक्ष्यते दुष्टभावता ।
प्रसन्नं वदनं चापि
तस्मान्मे नास्ति संशयः ॥
No ill intentions are ever evident in his speech. His face is also quite pleasant. Hence I have no doubt (about his genuineness).
6.17.63
अशङ्कितमतिः स्वस्थो
न शठः परिसर्पति ।
न चास्य दुष्टा वाक्चापि
तस्मान्नास्तीह संशयः ॥
Anyone with wrong intentions will neither be at ease nor be devoid of apprehensions in his mind, while approaching (others).
6.17.64
आकारश्छाद्यमानोऽपि
न शक्यो विनिगूहितुम् ।
बलाद्धि विवृणोत्येव
भावमन्तर्गतं नृणाम् ॥
Whatever is deep inside of men comes to the fore by itself with force. It cannot be suppressed, no matter how hard one tries to hide it.
6.17.65
देशकालोपपन्नं च
कार्यं कार्यविदां वर ।
सफलं कुरुते क्षिप्रम्
प्रयोगेणाभिसंहितम् ॥
You are the best among those who know the significance of taking an action! One’s success lies in taking action in a quick and timely manner as suited for the situation.
6.17.66
उद्योगं तव सम्प्रेक्ष्य
मिथ्यावृत्तं च रावणम् ।
वालिनश्च वधं श्रुत्वा
सुग्रीवं चाभिषेचितम् ।
राज्यं प्रार्थयमानश्च
बुद्धिपूर्वमिहागतः ।
एतावत्तु पुरस्कृत्य
विद्यते तस्य सङ्ग्रहः ॥
Having seen your determined efforts, and the dishonest conduct of Rāvaṇa, and having heard of the slaying of Vāli and the coronation of Sugreeva, he has come here on purpose, seeking the kingdom for himself. Considering this, he should be accepted.
6.17.68
यथाशक्ति मयोक्तं तु
राक्षसस्यार्जवं प्रति ।
त्वं प्रमाणं तु शेषस्य
श्रुत्वा बुद्धिमतां वर ॥
I have spoken, as much as I could, about the honest intentions of the Rākshasa. The rest, after hearing (all of us) out, is left to your judgement, and you are the best among all intelligent men!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे सप्तदशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventeenth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
