Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 2
In this Sarga, the Vānaras run helter-skelter, afraid of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, suspecting that they are sent by Vāli. Finally, the ministers of Sugreeva gather around him. Hanumān chides Sugreeva for behaving like a monkey, true to his nature, and losing his mind even before all the information is known.
Sugreeva retorts saying that Vāli is shrewd in conducting his affairs and could deploy any number of ways to strike an enemy. Sugreeva asks Hanumān to go to Rāma and Lakshmaṇa in the guise of a common man and find out about them and their intentions behind entering the Vana. The magnanimous Hanumān, then, goes to the place where Rāma and Lakshmaṇa are.
4.2.1
तौ तु दृष्ट्वा महात्मानौ
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
वरायुधधरौ वीरौ
सुग्रीवश्शङ्कितोऽभवत् ॥
Sugreeva was worried seeing the brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa the Mahātmas, the Veeras, with exquisite weapons on them.
4.2.2
उद्विग्नहृदयस्सर्वा
दिशस्समवलोकयन् ।
न व्यतिष्ठत कस्मिंश्चित्
देशे वानरपुङ्गवः ॥
That bull among Vānaras, feeling agitated and looking around in all directions, was unable to stay calm and remain in one place.
4.2.3
नैव चक्रे मनः स्थातुम्
वीक्षमाणो महाबलौ ।
कपेः परमभीतस्य
चित्तं व्यवससाद ह ॥
As he looked at those men of immense strength, he could not hold his mind steady. His mind, gripped by extreme fear, became very weak.
4.2.4
चिन्तयित्वा स धर्मात्मा
विमृश्य गुरुलाघवम् ।
सुग्रीवः परमोद्विग्नः
सर्वैरनुचरै स्सह ॥
Sugreeva, the Dharmātma, fully aware of his own strengths and weaknesses, felt extremely agitated, as did his followers.
4.2.5
ततस्स सचिवेभ्यस्तु
सुग्रीवः प्लवगाधिपः ।
शशंस परमोद्विग्नः
पश्यंस्तौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
Greatly agitated upon seeing Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, Sugreeva, King of Vānaras, said to his ministers:
4.2.6
एतौ वनमिदं दुर्गम्
वालिप्रणिहितौ ध्रुवम् ।
छद्मना चीरवसनौ
प्रचरन्ताविहागतौ ॥
For sure, these two, who are going about in this dense Vana, must have come here sent by Vāli, in this deceptive guise, wearing garments of tree bark.
4.2.7
ततस्सुग्रीवसचिवा
दृष्ट्वा परमधन्विनौ ।
जग्मुर्गिरितटात्तस्मात्
अन्यछ्चिखरमुत्तमम् ॥
Then the ministers of Sugreeva, after a look at the two great bowmen, fled from that side of the hill to another high peak.
4.2.8
ते क्षिप्रमधिगम्याथ
यूथपा यूथपर्षभम् ।
हरयो वानरश्रेष्ठम्
परिवार्योपतस्थिरे ॥
Then leaders of Vānara groups quickly assembled and stood around that great Vānara, a bull among leaders.
4.2.9
एकमेकायनगताः
प्लवमाना गिरेर्गिरिम् ।
प्रकम्पयन्तो वेगेन
गिरीणां शिखराण्यपि ॥
Then, in unison, they flew from hill to hill, making the crests of mountains shake under the impact of their swift movements.
4.2.10
ततश्शाखामृगास्सर्वे
प्लवमाना महाबलाः ।
बभञ्जुश्च नगांस्तत्र
पुष्पितान्दुर्गसंश्रितान् ॥
And those jumping animals of branches broke down the flowering trees on the hills. with their immense strength.
4.2.11
आप्लवन्तो हरिवराः
सर्वतस्तं महागिरिम् ।
मृगमार्जारशार्दूलान्
त्रासयन्तो ययुस्तदा ॥
Then, those great monkeys fled, jumping around all over that great mountain, scaring the deer, cats and tigers on their way.
Monkeys are referred to as animals of branches, as they live on branches.
4.2.12
ततस्सुग्रीवसचिवाः
पर्वतेन्द्रं समाश्रिताः ।
सङ्गम्य कपिमुख्येन
सर्वे प्राञ्जलय स्थिताः ॥
Then all the ministers of Sugreeva who resided on that king of mountains, joined the chief of Vānaras and waited upon him (Sugreeva) with palms joined.
4.2.13
ततस्तं भयसंविग्न
वालिकिल्बिषशङ्कितम् ।
उवाच हनुमान्वाक्यम्
सुग्रीवं वाक्यकोविदः ॥
And Hanumān, who was good with the choice of words, said these words to Sugreeva who was cowed by fear, worried about the wicked designs of Vāli.
4.2.14
सम्भ्रमस्त्यज्यतामेष
सर्वैर्वालिकृते महान् ।
मलयोऽयं गिरिवरो
भयं नेहास्ति वालिनः ॥
All of you, please set aside this undue panic because of Vāli, for, this is Malaya mountain and there is no fear of Vāli here.
4.2.15
यस्मादुद्विग्नचेतास्त्वम्
विद्रुतो हरिपुङ्गव ।
तं क्रूरदर्शनं क्रूरम्
नेह पश्यामि वालिनम् ॥
O bull among Vānaras! I do not see (signs of) the cruel and dreadful looking Vāli here, thinking of whom you are getting agitated and fleeing.
4.2.16
यस्मात्तव भयं सौम्य
पूर्वजात्पापकर्मणः ।
स नेह वाली दुष्टात्मा
न ते पश्याम्यहं भयम् ॥
My dear! I do not see here a reason for the fear of Vāli, your sinful older brother, because of whom you are scared.
4.2.17
अहो शाखामृगत्वं ते
व्यक्तमेव प्लवङ्गम ।
लघुचित्ततयाऽऽत्मानम्
न स्थापयसि यो मतौ ॥
O Vānara, what a display of your monkey mind and shallow nature, which does not let your mind remain steady!
4.2.18
बुद्धिविज्ञानसम्पन्न
इङ्गितैस्सर्वमाचर ।
न ह्यबुद्धिं गतो राजा
सर्वभूतानि शास्ति हि ॥
You should do everything thoughtfully, with complete awareness and reading the signals carefully. Not being thoughtful is not a way for a king to command all beings.
4.2.19
सुग्रीवस्तु शुभं वाक्यम्
श्रुत्वा सर्वं हनूमतः ।
ततश्शुभतरं वाक्यम्
हनूमन्तमुवाच ह ॥
Hearing all the good sense that Hanumān put forth, Sugreeva responded to Hanumān with words that made even more sense:
4.2.20
दीर्घबाहू विशालाक्षौ
शरचापासिधारिणौ ।
कस्य न स्याद्भयं दृष्ट्वा
ह्येतौ सुरसुतोपमौ ॥
Who will not be scared on seeing the men of long arms and wide eyes that resemble the sons of Dēvas, armed with bow, arrow and sword?
4.2.21
वालिप्रणिहितावेतौ
शङ्केऽहं पुरुषोत्तमौ ।
राजानो बहुमित्राश्च
विश्वासो नात्र हि क्षमः ॥
I fear that these two, the foremost of spirited beings, are sent by Vāli. Kings have many friends. One cannot be complacent in matters like this.
4.2.22
अरयश्च मनुष्येण
विज्ञेयाश्छद्मचारिणः ।
विश्वस्तानामविश्वस्ताः
छिद्रेषु प्रहरन्त्यपि ॥
One must be able to detect enemies in disguise. Those who are distrustful will certainly hit those who trust, whenever an opportunity presents itself.
4.2.23
कृत्येषु वाली मेधावी
राजानो बहुदर्शनाः ।
भवन्ति परहन्तारः
ते ज्ञेयाः प्राकृतैर्नरैः ।
तौ त्वया प्राकृतेनैव
गत्वा ज्ञेयौ प्लवङ्गम ।
इङ्गितानां प्रकारैश्च
रूपव्याभाषणेन च ॥
Vāli is shrewd in the management of affairs. Kings have many strategies to strike anyone who is against them. Their plans should be discovered through common men. O Vānara! Go to them pretending to be a common man and assess (who they are) based on their appearance, talk, manners and gestures.
4.2.25
लक्षयस्व तयोर्भावम्
प्रहृष्टमनसौ यदि ।
विश्वासयन्प्रशंसाभिः
इङ्गितैश्च पुनः पुनः ।
ममैवाभिमुखं स्थित्वा
पृच्छ त्वं हरिपुङ्गव ।
प्रयोजनं प्रवेशस्य
वनस्यास्य धनुर्धरौ ॥
Check out their intentions. If they seem to be well-disposed, gain their trust by praising them and pleasing them by your gestures, repeatedly. O bull among Vānaras! Then, standing in such a way that I can see your face, ask them why they entered this Vana armed with bows.
4.2.27
शुद्धात्मानौ यदि त्वेतौ
जानीहि त्वं प्लवङ्गम ।
व्याभाषितैर्वा विजेया
स्याद्दुष्टाऽदुष्टता तयोः ॥
O Vānara! Make sure that their intentions are pure. Asses their good or bad intentions by talking to them.
4.2.28
इत्येवं कपिराजेन
सन्दिष्टो मारुतात्मजः ।
चकार गमने बुद्धिम्
यत्र तौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
Thus directed by the King of Vānaras, the son of Vāyu prepared to go and meet Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
4.2.29
तथेति सम्पूज्य वचस्तु तस्य
तत्कपेस्सुभीमस्य दुरासदस्य च ।
महानुभावो हनुमान्ययौ तदा
स यत्र रामोऽतिबलश्च लक्ष्मणः ॥
Hanumān, the magnanimous one, receiving those instructions given by that inviolable Vānara who was gripped by fear, went to the place where the mighty strong Rāma and Lakshmaṇa were.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे द्वितीयस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the second Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
