Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 7
In this Sarga, Rāma reaches Suteekshṇa’s Āṡrama. Suteekshṇa tells him that he was waiting to see him without going to Heaven, even though Indra came down to take him there.
He offers to give his privileges for the higher worlds to Rāma, just as Ṡarabhaṅga did, but Rāma politely refuses and asks Suteekshṇa to show a place where he could to stay.
Suteekshṇa tells him to stay in his Āṡrama which was full of Ṛshis and where fruits and roots were abundant. He tells that the only inconvenience at that Āṡrama would be that wild beasts invading the place now and then, though they did not harm anyone.
Rāma tells him that he may be provoked to kill the beasts, which may not be to the liking of Suteekshṇa and hence it is better for him to move on to another place.
3.7.1
रामस्तु सहितो भ्रात्रा
सीतया च परन्तपः ।
सुतीक्ष्णस्याश्रमपदं
जगाम सह तैर्द्विजैः ॥
Then Rāma, harrier of the foe, accompanied by his brother and Seetā, went to Suteekshṇa’s Āṡrama along with those Brāhmaṇas.
3.7.2
स गत्वा दीर्घमध्वानं
नदीस्तीर्त्वा बहूदकाः ।
ददर्श विमलं शैलं
महामेघमिवोन्नतम् ॥
Having gone a long way, crossing rivers in spate, he saw a clear mountain that was lofty like a huge cloud.
3.7.3
ततस्तमिक्ष्वाकुवरौ
सन्ततं विविधैर्द्रुमैः ।
काननं तौ विविशतुः
सीतया सह राघवौ ॥
The foremost of the descendants of Ikshwāku and Raghu, along with Seetā, entered the forest filled with many kinds of trees.
3.7.4
प्रविष्टस्तु वनं रम्यमं
बहुपुष्पफलद्रुमम् ।
ददर्शाश्रममेकान्ते
चीरमालापरिष्कृतम् ॥
Entering that beautiful Vana crowded with trees loaded with fruit and flower, Rāma saw a sequestered Āṡrama decorated with drapes made of bast.
3.7.5
तत्र तापसमासीनं
मलपङ्कजधारिणम् ।
रामस्सुतीक्ष्णं विधिवत्
तपोवृद्धमभाषत ॥
Rāma addressed with due deference, the Tapasvi Suteekshṇa who was seated there, rich with Tapa and besmeared with lotus-like marks:
3.7.6
रामोऽहमस्मि भगवन्
भवन्तं द्रष्टुमागतः ।
तन्माभिवद धर्मज्ञ
महर्षे सत्यविक्रम ॥
O Dharmajña, O blessed one! O Maharshi of true convictions! I am Rāma, I have come here to see you; be pleased to speak to me.
3.7.7
स निरीक्ष्य ततो धीरो
रामं धर्मभृतां वरम् ।
समाश्लिष्य च बाहुभ्याम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Thereupon the intrepid one looked at Rāma, who was foremost among the upholders of Dharma. Embracing him with both arms, he said:
3.7.8
स्वागतं ते रघुश्रेष्ठ
राम सत्यभृतां वर ।
आश्रमोऽयं त्वयाऽक्रान्तः
सनाथ इव साम्प्रतम् ॥
Welcome to you, O Rāma, O best of Raghus, O foremost among the upholders of Truth! This hermitage has now found a protector with you stepping here.
3.7.9
प्रतीक्षमाणस्त्वामेव
नारोहेऽहं महायशः ।
देवलोकमितो वीर
देहं त्यक्त्वा महीतले ।
चित्रकूटमुपादाय
राज्यभ्रष्टोऽसि मे श्रुतः ॥
I am waiting for you, O Veera, o man of great renown, without going to the world of Dēvas and leaving this body on the earth, after hearing that you have came to Citrakūṭa upon being displaced from your kingdom.
3.7.10
इहोपयातः काकुत्थ्स
देवराजश्शतक्रतुः ।
उपागम्य च मे देवो
महादेवस्सुरेश्वरः ।
सर्वान् लोकाञ्जितानाह
मम पुण्येन कर्मणा ॥
O Kākutstha! Ṡatakratu (Indra), the King of Dēvas came here. And he, the Mahā Dēva, the lord of the Dēvas, told me that I had won all the worlds by my deeds of Puṇya.
3.7.12
तेषु देवर्षिजुष्टेषु
जितेषु तपसा मया ।
मत्प्रसादात्सभार्यस्त्वं
विहरस्व सलक्ष्मणः ॥
Please be my guest, along with your wife, to visit those worlds, which were won by me through Tapa and in which celestial Ṛshis live.
3.7.13
तमुग्रतपसं दीप्तं
महर्षिं सत्यवादिनम् ।
प्रत्युवाचात्मवान्रामो
ब्रह्माणमिव वासवः ॥
The sensible Rāma, replying as Indra might to Brahma, told that great Maharshi, who is truthful and resplendent with the intense Tapa he did:
3.7.14
अहमेवाहरिष्यामि
स्वयं लोकान्महामुने ।
आवासं त्वहमिच्छामि
प्रदिष्टमिह कानने ॥
O great Muni! I shall attain all those worlds with my own efforts! For now, I just want a place to live in this Vana, that you might show.
Rāma here gives the same reply that he gave to Ṡarabhaṅga earlier (Ṡlōka 3.5.32).
3.7.15
भवान्सर्वत्र कुशलः
सर्वभूतहिते रतः ।
आख्यातश्शरभङ्गेण
गौतमेन महात्मना ॥
Mahatma Ṡarabhaṅga, descendant of Gautama, spoke of you as one who knows everything and who keeps the welfare of every creature at heart.
3.7.16
एवमुक्तस्तु रामेण
महर्षिर्लोकविश्रुतः ।
अब्रवीन्मधुरं वाक्यं
हर्षेण महतायुतः ॥
Asked thus by Rāma, the world renowned Maharshi was delighted beyond measure and said these sweet words:
3.7.17
अयमेवाश्रमो राम
गुणवान्रम्यतामिह ।
ऋषिसङ्घानुचरितः
सदा मूलफलैर्युतः ॥
This Āṡrama, inhabited by hosts of Ṛshis, and perennially abundant with fruits and roots, might itself be suitable in every way; you may as well enjoy it here!
3.7.18
इममाश्रममागम्य
मृगसङ्घा महीयसः  ।
अहत्वा प्रतिगच्छन्ति
लोभयित्वाऽकुतोभयाः ।
नान्यद्धोषं भवेदत्र
मृगेभ्योऽन्यत्र विद्धि वै ॥
But droves of great beasts do invade this Āṡrama, and move about fearlessly; they cause great distraction, coming and going as they please. There is no other problem here other than those beasts.
3.7.19
तच्छृत्वा वचनं तस्य
महर्षेर्लक्ष्मणाग्रजः ।
उवाच वचनं धीरो
विगृह्य सशरं धनुः ॥
Hearing those words of the Maharshi, the intrepid elder brother of Lakshmaṇa, grabbed the bow along with the arrows, and said these words:
3.7.20
तानहं सुमहाभाग
मृगसङ्घान्समागतान् ।
हन्यां निशितधारेण
शरेणाशनिवर्चसा ॥
O highly blessed one! I shall kill those invading droves of beasts with sharp edged arrows that are nudged at the joints!
3.7.21
भवांस्तत्राभिषज्येत
किंस्यात्कृच्छ्रतरं ततः ।
एतस्मिन्नाश्रमे वासं
चिरं तु न समर्थये ॥
But you might be sensitive to those beasts, in which case nothing could be more painful (to you)! Hence I do not think I will be able to live in this Āṡrama for long.
3.7.22
तमेवमुक्त्वोपरमं
रामस्सन्ध्यामुपागमत् ।
 अन्वास्य पश्चिमां सन्ध्यां
तत्र वासमकल्पयत् ।
सुतीक्ष्णस्याऽश्रमे रम्ये
सीतया लक्ष्मणेन च ॥
Rāma concluded the conversation thus and proceeded to perform his evening oblations. Finishing them, he rested at Suteekshṇa’s delightful Āṡrama along with Seetā and Lakshmaṇa (for the night).
3.7.24
ततश्शुभं तापसभोज्यमन्नं
स्वयं सुतीक्ष्णः पुरुषर्षभाभ्याम् ।
ताभ्यां सुसत्कृत्य ददौ महात्मा
सन्द्यानिवृत्तौ रजनीं मवेक्ष्य ॥
Noting that the evening had passed and night had arrived, Suteekshṇa the Mahātma personally honored those bulls among men, giving them the best food apt for Tapasvis.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे सप्तमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventh Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
