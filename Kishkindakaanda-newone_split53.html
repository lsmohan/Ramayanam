Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 53
In this Sarga, the Vānaras fall in despair, realizing that the time limit set by Sugreeva has been exceeded and they would meet with no less than death as punishment for not finding Seetā and accomplishing the mission.
Aṅgada then says, that as sincere and capable as they all are, they have to accept the fact that they have failed in the mission and the time given to them is over. He says that Sugreeva is ruthless, is his former sworn enemy and he would not be easy on anyone for not carrying out the mission. He says that the ruthless Sugreeva would surely punish them with death, if for nothing else, but just to please Rāghava.
Aṅgada proposes that it is better for all of them to fast unto death, than being punished by Sugreeva and being subjected to humiliation. Tāra suggests that they should go back into the cave and live there, where there is no fear of anyone.
Everyone, keeping their full confidence in Aṅgada, ask him about the best course of action.
4.53.1
ततस्ते ददृशुर्घोरम्
सागरं वरुणालयम् ।
अपारमभिगर्जन्तम्
घोरैरूर्मिभिराकुलम् ॥
They beheld the dreaded shoreless ocean, the abode of Varuṇa, that thundered and surged with its frightful billowing tides.
4.53.2
मयस्य मायाविहितम्
गिरिदुर्गं विचिन्वताम् ।
तेषां मासो व्यतिक्रान्तो
यो राज्ञा समयः कृतः ॥
The time limit of one month given to them by the king had passed while they were searching the impenetrable mountain cave fashioned by the magic of Maya.
4.53.3
विन्ध्यस्य तु गिरेः पादे
सम्प्रपुष्पितपादपे ।
उपविश्य महात्मानः 
चिन्तामापेदिरे तदा ॥
Sitting at the foothills of the Vindhyas that was crowded with flowering trees, those Mahātmas thought about it seriously.
4.53.4
ततः पुष्पातिभाराग्रान्
लताशतसमावृतान् ।
द्रुमान्वासन्तिकान्दृष्ट्वा
बभूवुर्भयशङ्किताः ॥
Seeing the tops of the trees covered with flowers and hundreds of creepers, and taking them to be signs of the arrival of spring, they were quite worried.
4.53.5
ते वसन्तमनुप्राप्तम्
प्रतिवेद्य परस्परम् ।
नष्टसन्देशकालार्था
निपेतुर्धरणीतले ॥
Telling each other that spring had arrived and that the time limit had exceeded, they threw themselves on the ground (in despair).
4.53.6
ततस्तान्कपिवृद्धांश्च
शिष्टांश्चैव वनौकसः ।
वाचा मधुरयाऽभाष्य
यथावदनुमान्य च ।
स तु सिंहवृषस्कन्धः
पीनायतभुजः कपिः ।
युवराजो महाप्राज्ञः
अङ्गदो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Then Aṅgada, the sagacious Crown Prince of long and stout arms and of shoulders like those of a lion and a bull addressed the elder Vānaras and other honorable Vana dwellers with pleasing words after paying due respects.
4.53.8
शासनात्कपिराजस्य
वयं सर्वे विनिर्गताः ।
मासः पूर्णो बिलस्थानाम्
हरयः किं न बुद्ध्यते ॥
O Vānaras! Do you not realize that a whole month has passed while we were in the cave, after we started on the mission set by the king of Vānaras?
4.53.9
वयमाश्वयुजे मासि
कालसङ्ख्याव्यवस्थिताः ।
प्रस्थितास्सोऽपि चातीतः
किमतः कार्यमुत्तरम् ॥
We have started in the month of Aswayuja according to the calendar. And the time has passed. What are we to do now?
4.53.10
भवन्तः प्रत्ययं प्राप्ताः
नीतिमार्गविशारदाः ।
हितेष्वभिरता भर्तुः
निसृष्टास्सर्वकर्मसु ॥
You are fully vested in the wellbeing of the lord. You have earned his trust. You are experts in strategy. You carry out everything successfully.
4.53.11
कर्मस्वप्रतिमास्सर्वे
दिक्षु विश्रुतपौरुषाः ।
मां पुरस्कृत्य निर्याताः
पिङ्गाक्षप्रतिचोदिताः ॥
You have no equal in executing any task. You are famous for your prowess in every direction of the world. You have set out, sent by the tawny-eyed, with me as your leader.
4.53.12
इदानीमकृतार्थानाम्
मर्तव्यं नात्र संशयः ।
हरिराजस्य सन्देशम्
अकृत्वा कस्सुखी भवेत् ॥
Having failed in our mission, we can do nothing but give up our lives. Who can hope to be happy having failed in carrying out the order of the king of Vānaras?
4.53.13
अस्मिन्नतीते काले तु
सुग्रीवेण कृते स्वयम् ।
प्रायोपवेशनं युक्तम्
सर्वेषां च वनौकसाम् ॥
With the time limit set by Sugreeva exceeded, it is better for all of us Vana dwellers to fast unto death!
4.53.14
तीक्ष्णः प्रकृत्या सुग्रीवः
स्वमिभावे व्यवस्थितः ।
न क्षमिष्यति नस्सर्वान्
अपराधकृतो गतान् ॥
Sugreeva is ruthless by nature. Moreover, he is established as the sovereign lord now.
He will not forgive any one of us if we return after committing a transgression (against his command).
4.53.15
अप्रवृत्तौ च सीतायाः
पापमेव करिष्यति ।
तस्मात् क्षममिहाद्यैव
गन्तुं प्रायोपवेशनम् ।
त्यक्त्वा पुत्रांश्च दारांश्च
धनानि च गृहाणि च ॥
Hearing no information about Seetā, he would only do the worst conceivable. Hence, it is better to fast unto death here and now, giving up our wives, children, houses and riches.
4.53.16
ध्रुवं नो हिंसते राजा
सर्वान्प्रतिगतानितः ।
वधेनाप्रतिरूपेण
श्रेयान्मृत्युरिहैव नः ॥
The king will certainly torture all of us who return from here to death like never known anywhere. To die here is a better option for us.
4.53.17
न चाहं यौवराज्येन
सुग्रीवेणाभिषेचितः ।
नरेन्द्रेणाभिषिक्तोऽस्मि
रामेणाक्लिष्टकर्मणा ॥
I was not consecrated by Sugreeva as the crown prince. It was Rāma, the lord of people, who accomplishes things effortlessly, by whom I was consecrated.
4.53.18
स पूर्वं बद्धवैरो माम्
राजा दृष्ट्वा व्यतिक्रमम् ।
घातयिष्यति दण्डेन
तीक्ष्णेन कृतनिश्चयः ॥
He was my sworn enemy once and is the king now. Seeing his orders gone unfulfilled, he will certainly be determined to give the worst punishment and kill me.
4.53.19
किं मे सुहृद्भिर्व्यसनं
पश्यद्भिर्जीवितान्तरे ।
इहैव प्रायमासिष्ये
पुण्ये सागररोधसि ॥
What is the point in having my friends witness me being tortured in the prime of my life? I shall fast unto death right here on the auspicious shore of the ocean.
4.53.20
एतच्छ्रुत्वा कुमारेण
युवराजेन भाषितम् ।
सर्वे ते वानरश्रेष्ठाः
करुणं वाक्यमब्रुवन् ॥
Hearing those words of the young crown prince, all the prominent apes empathized with him saying:
4.53.21
तीक्ष्णः प्रकृत्या सुग्रीवः
प्रियासक्तश्च राघवः ।
समीक्ष्याकृत कार्यांस्तु
तस्मिंश्च समये गते ।
अदृष्टायां च वैदेह्याम्
 दृष्ट्वा चैव समागतान् ।
राघवप्रियकामाय
घातयिष्यत्यसंशयम् ॥
Sugreeva is ruthless by nature and Rāghava badly wants his beloved. When Sugreeva sees us returning without finding Seetā, and the time lapsing without the mission accomplished, he will have us killed, without a doubt, from the desire to please Rāghava.
4.53.23
न क्षमं चापराद्धानाम्
गमनं स्वामिपार्श्वतः ।
प्रधानबूताश्च वयम्
सुग्रीवस्य समागताः ॥
We, the best among those who Sugreeva has, have come here together. It is impossible for us to present ourselves before our master, after failing to carry out the orders.
4.53.24
इहैव सीतामन्विष्य
प्रवृत्तिमुपलभ्य वा ।
नो चेद्गच्छाम तं वीरम्
गमिष्यामो यमक्षयम् ॥
We either go to that Veera after searching and getting the news of Seetā, or go to the abode of Yama.
4.53.25
प्लवङ्गमानां तु भयार्दितानां
श्रुत्वा वचस्तार इदं बभाषे ।
अलं विषादेन बिलं प्रविश्य
वसाम सर्वे यदि रोचते वः ॥
Hearing those words of the frightened Vānaras, Tāra said, ‘Enough of feeling helpless; we can go into this cave and live there, if you like’
4.53.26
इदं हि मायाविहितं सुदुर्गमं
प्रभूतवृक्षोदकभोज्यपेयम् ।
इहास्ति नो नैव भयं पुरन्दरात्
न राघवाद्वानरराजतोऽपि वा ॥
This magical creation is impenetrable, and is abundant in trees, water, edibles and drinks. There is no fear there even from Purandara, much less from Rāghava or the King of the apes.
4.53.27
श्रुत्वाऽङ्गदस्यापि वचोऽनुकूलम्
ऊचुश्च सर्वे हरयः प्रतीताः ।
यथा न हन्येम तथा विधानम्
असक्तमद्यैव विधीयतां नः ॥
Hearing the words of Aṅgada and keeping full confidence in him, all the Vānaras spoke in agreement, saying: ‘Tell us at once what should be done to avoid being punished with death’.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे त्रिपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty third Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
