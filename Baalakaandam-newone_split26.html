Baala Kaanda - Sarga 26
This Sarga is for children. Make sure you read it to them. This Sarga helps them develop admiration for Rāma, the mighty hero, because of the physical prowess he displays in killing Tāṭaka.
That admiration will continue into their adult lives, when their focus will shift from the mighty Rāma to the judicious, disciplined, thoughtful and resolute Rāma.
1.26.1
मुनेर्वचनमक्लीबम्
श्रुत्वा नरवरात्मजः ।
राघवः प्राञ्जलिर्भूत्वा
प्रत्युवाच दृढव्रतः ॥
Hearing that resolute words of the Muni, the prince of Raghu dynasty, who observed his own moral compass, said to him:
1.26.2
पितुर्वचननिर्देशात्
पितुर्वचनगौरवात् ।
वचनं कौशिकस्येति
कर्तव्यमविशङ्कया ॥
I have full respect for my father's words. And my father sent me with you to follow your instructions. Hence I shall do whatever you ask me to do.
People working in the corporate world or government positions know the importance of chain of command and the rules of deputation very well.
When someone is deputed to another department, he or she would have to serve two masters. The new temporary master and the old permanent master to whom he will return to.
A judicious person would always make sure, that whatever he was asked to do is legitimate from three perspectives:
1) The temporary master's perspective. 2) The permanent master's perspective and 3) Own ethical and moral perspective.
Rāma is discussing all the three perspectives here.
Rāmāyaṇa is about Dharma, Artha and Kāma. The discussion here in these Ṡlōkas, is about Dharma, legitimacy.
1.26.3
अनुशिष्टोऽस्म्ययोध्यायाम्
गुरुमध्ये महात्मना ।
पित्रा दशरथेनाहम्
नावज्ञेयं च तद्वचः ॥
In the midst of many great elders in Ayōdhyā, my father Daṡaratha instructed me to follow your instructions. How can I say “no!” to his command?
1.26.4
सोऽहं पितुर्वचः श्रुत्वा
शासनाद्ब्रह्मवादिनः ।
करिष्यामि न सन्देहः
ताटकावधमुत्तमम् ॥
Hence, in accordance with the words of my father and in accordance with the instructions from you, the knower and speaker of great truths, I shall, without a doubt, kill Tāṭaka, which is the right thing to be done!
1.26.5
गोब्राह्मणहितार्थाय
देशस्यास्य सुखाय च ।
तव चैवाप्रमेयस्य
वचनं कर्त्तुमुद्यतः ॥
For the sake of Brāhmaṇas and Cows, and for the sake of the well-being of these lands I am ready to obey your command which is beyond normal considerations.
See the comment on Brāhmaṇas and Cows in the previous Sarga.
1.26.6
एवमुक्त्वा धनुर्मध्ये
बध्वा मुष्टिमरिन्दमः ।
ज्याघोषमकरोत्तीव्रम्
दिशः शब्देन नादयन् ॥
Saying thus, that subjugator of enemies, held the bow in the middle with his fist and made twanging sounds by plucking the bow string that reverberated in all ten directions.
The ten directions (east, north, west, south, northeast, northwest, southeast, southwest, up and down) is an expression equivalent to the expression of 360o in the modern times.
I cannot help but visualize Rāma's hand with the glove (Ṡlōka 1.22.9), fiercely gripping the bow in slow motion and close up, as if the bow will be crushed with the strength of his fist.
1.26.7
तेन शब्देन वित्रस्ताः
ताटकावनवासिनः ।
ताटका च सुसङ्क्रुद्धा
तेन शब्देन मोहिता ॥
All the animals living in that Tāṭaka forest were terrified. Tāṭaka herself had to take note of the sound and became enraged.
1.26.8
तं शब्दमभिनिध्याय
राक्षसी क्रोधमूर्च्छिता ।
श्रुत्वा चाभ्यद्रवद्वेगात्
यतः शब्दो विनिस्सृतः ॥
She figured out quickly the direction from which the sound emerged and dashed in that direction, enraged.
1.26.9
तां दृष्ट्वा राघवः क्रुद्धाम्
विकृतां विकृताननाम् ।
प्रमाणेनातिवृद्धां च
लक्ष्मणं सोऽभ्यभाषत ॥
Seeing that enraged and crooked face On the huge and horrid body, Rāma said to Lakshmaṇa:
1.26.10
पश्य लक्ष्मण यक्षिण्या
भैरवं दारुणं वपुः ।
भिद्येरन् दर्शनादस्या
भीरूणां हृदयानि च ॥
Look at the dreadful and horrid body of this Yakshiṇi! Her very appearance can pound the hearts of the weak-minded into pieces!
1.26.11
एनां पश्य दुराधर्षाम्
मायाबलसमन्विताम् ।
विनिवृत्तां करोम्यद्य
हृतकर्णाग्रनासिकाम् ॥
I will cut off her ears and nose and make this female, who is invincible with her magical powers, flee.
1.26.12
न ह्येनामुत्सहे हन्तुम्
स्त्रीस्वभावेन रक्षिताम् ।
वीर्यं चास्या गतिं चापि
हनिष्यामीति मे मतिः ॥
I do not feel like killing her because she is, after all, a woman. But I will deplete her energy and speed.
1.26.13
एवं ब्रुवाणे रामे तु
ताटका क्रोधमूर्च्छिता ।
उद्यम्य बाहू गर्जन्ती
राममेवाभ्यधावत ॥
As Rāma was saying those words, the enraged Tāṭaka dashed on him with her hands lifted in the air.
1.26.14
विश्वामित्रस्तु ब्रह्मर्षिः
हुङ्कारेणाभिभर्त्स्य ताम् ।
स्वस्ति राघवयोरस्तु
जयं चैवाभ्यभाषत ॥
The Brahmarshi Viṡwāmitra, intimidating her with a snort, hailed "victory and safety" to the princes of Raghu dynasty.
1.26.15
उद्धून्वाना रजो घोरम्
ताटका राघवावुभौ ।
रजोमोहेन महता
मुहूर्तं सा व्यमोहयत् ॥
She then raised a storm of dust that covered the duo and confused them for a moment.
Raising dust to cover and confuse the enemy seems to be an eternal trick. In any case, it can be seen being used in Bollywood as well as Hollywood movies.
1.26.16
ततो मायां समास्थाय
शिलावर्षेण राघवौ ।
अवाकिरत्सुमहता
ततश्चुक्रोध राघवः ॥
With her magical powers, she bombarded them with a hurl of stones and rocks, irritating the prince of Raghu dynasty.
1.26.17
शिलावर्षं महत्तस्याः
शरवर्षेण राघवः ।
प्रतिहत्योपधावन्त्याः
करौ चिच्छेद पत्रिभिः ॥
He countered that rain of stones and rocks with a deluge of arrows and then cut her hands off.
1.26.18
ततश्छिन्नभुजां श्रान्तां
अभ्याशे परिगर्जतीम् ।
सौमित्रिरकरोत् क्रोधात्
हृतकर्णाग्रनासिकाम् ॥
The son of Sumitrā cut her nose and ears as she was lying nearby shouting and yelling in exhaustion, with her hands cut off.
1.26.19
कामरूपधरा सद्यः
कृत्वा रूपाण्यनेकशः ।
अन्तर्द्धानं गता यक्षी
मोहयन्तीव मायया ।
अश्मवर्षं विमुञ्चन्ती
भैरवं विचचार ह ॥
With her magical powers, she assumed many forms appearing at many places at the same time and bombarding them with stones and rocks from all directions.
1.26.20
ततस्तावश्मवर्षेण
कीर्यमाणौ समन्ततः ।
दृष्ट्वा गाधिसुतः श्रीमान्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing the duo engulfed in that shower of rocks and stones, the resplendent son of Gādhi (Viṡwāmitra) said:
1.26.21
अलं ते घृणया वीर
पापैषा दुष्टचारिणी ।
यज्ञविघ्नकरी यक्षी
पुरा वर्द्धति मायया ॥
You are being considerate to this Yakshi. She is wretched, evil and can spoil Yajñas if left alone. She has the magical powers to regain her strength again and again.
1.26.22
वध्यतां तावदेवैषा
पुरा सन्ध्या प्रवर्तते ।
रक्षांसि सन्ध्याकालेषु
दुर्द्धर्षाणि भवन्ति वै ॥
Kill her before it is time for the sun to set. Rākshasas become even more invincible at the time of sunset.
1.26.23
इत्युक्तस्तु तदा यक्षीं
अश्मवृष्ट्याभिवर्षतीम् ।
दर्शयन् शब्दवेधित्वम्
तां रुरोध स सायकैः ॥
Thus advised, he stopped the Yakshi’s shower of stones with arrows, showing his skill in aiming by the ear.
1.26.24
सा रुद्धा शरजालेन
मायाबलसमन्विता ।
अभिदुद्राव काकुत्स्थम्
लक्ष्मणं च विनेदुषी ॥
Her shower of rocks and stones restrained thus by the shower of arrows, she dashed towards Rāma and Lakshmaṇa with a roar, using her magical powers.
1.26.25
तामापतन्तीं वेगेन
विक्रान्तामशनीमिव ।
शरेणोरसि विव्याध
सा पपात ममार च ॥
As she approached them with lightning speed and fell on them with all her power, Rāma shot an arrow into her chest. She then fell and died.
1.26.26
तां हतां भीमसङ्काशाम्
दृष्ट्वा सुरपतिस्तदा ।
साधु साध्विति काकुत्स्थम्
सुराश्च समपूजयन् ॥
Seeing that horrible being fallen, All the Dēvas and their lord Indra applauded the prince of Kākutstha dynasty saying “Well done, Well done!” and paid due approbations.
1.26.27
उवाच परमप्रीतः
सहस्राक्षः पुरन्दरः ।
सुराश्च सर्वे संहृष्टा
विश्वामित्रमथाब्रुवन् ॥
All the Dēvas and their lord, the Thousand-eyed Indra, felt extremely happy and said in a jubilant voice to Viṡwāmitra:
1.26.28
मुने कौशिक भद्रं ते
सेन्द्रास्सर्वे मरुद्गणाः ।
तोषिताः कर्मणानेन
स्नेहं दर्शय राघवे ॥
All the Dēvas along with Indra are very happy. May all bode well for you! May Rāma be showered with your affection!
1.26.29
प्रजापतेर्भृशाश्वस्य
पुत्रान् सत्यपराक्रमान् ।
तपोबलभृतो ब्रह्मन्
राघवाय निवेदय ॥
O Brahman, may you pass on the the truly mighty Astras that are created to Bhṛṡāṡva, and that were reinforced by the energy of Tapa to the prince of Raghu dynasty!
1.26.30
पात्रभूतश्च ते ब्रह्मन्
तवानुगमने धृतः ।
कर्तव्यं च महत्कार्यम्
सुराणां राजसूनुना ॥
He, who has been loyally following you is well qualified to receive the Astras. This prince is set to carry on a great task for the Dēvas.
1.26.31
एवमुक्त्वा सुराः सर्वे
जग्मुर्हृष्टा यथागतम् ।
विश्वामित्रं पुरस्कृत्य
ततः सन्ध्या प्रवर्तते ॥
Speaking thus, the Dēvas departed to their respective places after paying due respects to Viṡwāmitra, as the sun was setting.
1.26.32
ततो मुनिवरः प्रीतः
ताटकावधतोषितः ।
मूर्ध्नि राममुपाघ्राय
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Then, the eminent and pleased Muni, feeling happy about the elimination of Tāṭaka, said to Rāma, smelling his head:
"Smelling the head" is an expression that indicates an elderly person leaning over a younger person as a gesture of affection. It is similar to 'kissing the forehead' in modern times.
1.26.33
इहाद्य रजनीं राम
वसाम शुभदर्शन ।
श्वःप्रभाते गमिष्यामः
तदाश्रमपदं मम ॥
My dear Rāma! We shall stay here for the night and will start to my Āṡrama tomorrow morning.
1.26.34
विश्वामित्रवचः श्रुत्वा
हृष्टो दशरथात्मजः ।
उवास रजनीं तत्र
ताटकाया वने सुखम् ॥
Hearing those words of Viṡwāmitra, the son of Daṡaratha felt very happy. He spent the night in that Tāṭaka Vana in comfort.
This is Rāma’s first major accomplishment. A Yakshiṇi who troubled an entire area was eliminated. What a feeling! Vālmeeki says Rāma spent the night in ‘comfort’. It must certainly have been much more than that.
1.26.35
मुक्तशापं वनं तच्च
तस्मिन्नेव तदाऽहनि ।
रमणीयं विबभ्राज
यथा चैत्ररथं वनम् ॥
The Vana also, relieved from the curse, shone forth luxuriously like the Caitraratha Vana, right on that very day.
Caitraratha is the luxurious heavenly garden of Kubēra. This expression is used in a song by Vēṭūri Sundara Rāma Mūrty, penned for a Telugu movie.
1.26.36
निहत्य तां यक्षसुतां स रामः
प्रशस्यमानः सुरसिद्धसङ्घैः ।
उवास तस्मिन् मुनिना सहैव
प्रभातवेलां प्रतिबोध्यमानः ॥
Having killed that daughter of Yaksha, and having been lauded by all the Dēvas, that Rāma, along with the Muni stayed in the Vana for the night, till woken up by the Muni in the morning.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे षड्विंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty sixth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
