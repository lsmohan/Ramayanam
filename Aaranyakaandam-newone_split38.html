Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 38
In this Sarga Māreeca continues to dissuade Rāvaṇa. He tells Rāvaṇa how Viṡwāmitra requested Daṡaratha to send Rāma for the protection of the Yajña and how he was flung into the ocean a hundred Yōjanas away by the arrows of Rāma.
Māreeca, without mincing words, warns Rāvaṇa of the dire consequences of earning Rāma’s displeasure. Māreeca tells that the entire Laṅkā would burn to ashes and all the Rākshasas would be slaughtered and he will see himself in the abode of Death, if he were to abduct Seetā. Māreeca suggests to Rāvaṇa that he should rather keep himself to the wives he already has.
3.38.1
कदाचिदप्यहं वीर्यात्
पर्यटन्पृथिवीमिमाम् ।
बलं नागसहस्रस्य
धारयन्पर्वतोपमः ।
नीलजीमूतसङ्काशः
तप्तकाञ्चनकुण्डलः ।
भयं लोकस्य जनयन्
किरीटी परिघायुधः ।
व्यचरं दण्डकारण्ये
ऋषिमांसानि भक्षयन् ॥
Once upon a time, I used to roam around this earth with all my might, brandishing my strength equal to that of a thousand elephants, with a mountainous body that rivals a black cloud, wearing a crown and ear pendants of gold purified in fire, terrifying the world wielding a Parigha for a weapon. I used to go to Daṇḍaka Araṇya and eat the flesh of the Ṛshis.
3.38.3
विश्वामित्रोऽथ धर्मात्मा
मद्वित्रस्तो महामुनिः ।
स्वयं गत्वा दशरथं
नरेन्द्रमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Then, Viṡwāmitra, the great Muni and Dharmātma, fearing me, went to the king Daṡaratha in person and said these words:
3.38.4
अद्य रक्षतु मां रामः
पर्वकाले समाहितः ।
मारीचान्मे भयं घोरं
समुत्पन्नं नरेश्वर ॥
O lord of people! I am gripped with terrible fear of Māreeca! Please do allow Rāma come and offer protection to me with complete focus, at the time of Yajña!
3.38.5
इत्येवमुक्तो धर्मात्मा
राजा दशरथस्तदा ।
प्रत्युवाच महाभागं
विश्वामित्रं महामुनिम् ॥
Thus requested, king Daṡaratha then responded to the great blessed Muni Viṡwāmitra:
3.38.6
बालो द्वादशवर्षोऽयम्
अकृतास्त्रश्च राघवः ।
कामं तु मम यत्सैन्यं
मया सह गमिष्यति ॥
Rāghava is a child and is barely twelve years old. He is not even trained in archery. But I shall certainly come along with my army.
3.38.7
बलेन चतुरङ्गेण
स्वयमेत्य निशाचरान् ।
वधिष्यामि मुनिश्रेष्ठ
शत्रूंस्ते मनसेप्सितान् ॥
O distinguished Muni! I would like to come in person, with all four divisions of the army to slay your enemies, the Night-rovers!
3.38.8
इत्येवमुक्तस्समुनी
राजानमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Thus told, that Muni responded to the king:
3.38.9
रामान्नान्यद्बलं लोके
पर्याप्तं तस्य रक्षसः ।
देवतानामपि भवान्
समरेष्वभिपालकः ।
आसीत्तव कृतं कर्म
त्रिलोके विदितं नृप ॥
‘No strength other than Rāma’s is a match for that of that Rākshasa!’ You did provide protection even in the war waged by the Dēvas! And what you did then is well known in all the three worlds!
3.38.10
काममस्तु महत्सैन्यं
तिष्ठत्विह परन्तप ।
बालोऽप्येष महातेजाः
समर्थस्तस्य निग्रहे ।
गमिष्ये राममादाय
स्वस्ति तेऽस्तु परन्तप ॥
O scorcher of foes! Yes, Surely it is a huge army! But let it stay here. Even though he is young, he is of immense strength and is capable of restraining him (Māreeca). I shall go, taking Rāma with me, may all bode well for you!
Māreeca presents here an abridged version of the story, obviously. He spares the detail of Daṡaratha’s disinclination to send Rāma and the persuasion to do so by Vasishṭha.
3.38.12
एवमुक्त्वा तु स मुनि
तमादाय नृपात्मजम् ।
जगाम परमप्रीतो
विश्वामित्रस्स्वमाश्रमम् ॥
Saying thus, Viṡwāmitra, the Muni, returned to his Āṡrama happily, taking the prince with him.
3.38.13
तं तदा दण्डकारण्ये
यज्ञमुद्दिश्य दीक्षितम् ।
बभूवोपस्थितो रामः
चित्रं विष्फारयन्धनुः ॥
Then, as he took the vows of Yajña in the Daṇḍaka Araṇya, Rāma stood by him, twanging the bow.
3.38.14
अजातव्यञ्जनश्रीमान्
पद्मपत्रनिभेक्षणः ।
एकवस्त्रधरो धन्वी
शिखी कनकमालया ।
शोभयन् दण्डकारण्यं
दीप्तेन स्वेन तेजसा ।
अदृश्यत ततो रामो
बालचन्द्र इवोदितः ॥
With the signs of manhood not yet visible on him, Rāma looked like the rising young moon.
The blessed one, holding the bow, with eyes like lotus petals, adorned with gold necklace, hair coiled on the head, clad in a single piece of cloth, made that Daṇḍaka Araṇya glow with his innate radiance.
3.38.16
ततोऽहं मेघसङ्काशः
तप्तकाञ्चनकुण्डलः ।
बली दत्तवरोदर्पात्
आजगाम तदाश्रमम् ॥
Then, I went to that Āṡrama, wearing ear-pendants of gold purified by fire, looking like a cloud and haughty with my strength and the boons that I had obtained.
3.38.17
तेन दृष्टः प्रविष्टोऽहं
सहसैवोद्यतायुधः ।
मां तु दृष्ट्वा धनुस्सज्यम्
असम्भ्रान्तश्चकार सः ॥
He saw me as soon as I entered holding up my weapon. Unperturbed upon seeing me, he simply went on to string his bow.
3.38.18
अवजानन्नहं मोहात्
बालोऽयमिति राघवम् ।
विश्वामित्रस्य तां वेदिम्
अभ्यधावं कृतत्वरः ॥
Taking Rāghava to be a mere lad and disregarding him in my folly, I hurried and dashed towards the Vēdi of Viṡwāmitra.
3.38.19
तेन मुक्तस्ततो बाणः
शितश्शत्रुनिबर्हणः ।
तेनाहं त्वाहतः क्षिप्तः
समुद्रे शतयोजने ॥
Thereupon, struck by the sharp arrow that he, the slayer of enemies, released, I was flung afar into the sea which was a hundred Yōjanas away.
3.38.20
नेच्छता तात मां हन्तुं
तदा वीरेण रक्षितः ।
रामस्य शरवेगेन
निरस्तोऽहमचेतनः ॥
My dear! I was thus spared, because he, the Veera, did not want to kill me. I was only tossed senseless, by the speed of Rāma’s arrow.
3.38.21
पातितोऽहं तदा तेन
गम्भीरे सागराम्भसि ।
प्राप्य संज्ञां चिरात्तात
लङ्कां प्रतिगतः पुरीम् ॥
My dear! I was thus thrown into the depths of the ocean by him. Regaining senses after a long time, I then reached the city of Laṅkā.
3.38.22
एवमस्मि तदा मुक्तः
सहायास्तु निपातिताः ।
अकृतास्त्रेण बालेन
रामेणाक्लिष्टकर्मणा ॥
I was thus spared by Rāma, who accomplishes things with utmost ease. My accomplices, however, were killed by that lad who was barely trained in archery.
3.38.23
तन्मया वार्यमाणस्त्वं
यदि रामेण विग्रहम् ।
करिष्यस्यापदं घोरां
क्षिप्रं प्राप्स्यसि रावण ॥
Hence, if you were to antagonize him disregarding my warning, O Rāvaṇa, you will find yourself in great trouble in no time.
3.38.24
क्रीडारतिविधिज्ञानां
समाजोत्सवशालिनाम् ।
रक्षसां चैव सन्तापम्
अनर्थं चाहरिष्यसि ॥
You will only bring suffering and ruin to the Rākshasas, who otherwise revel in parties and functions, and enjoy sports and the art of love making!
3.38.25
हर्म्यप्रासादसम्बाधां
नानारत्नविभूषिताम् ।
द्रक्ष्यसि त्वं पुरीं लङ्कां
विनष्टां मैथिलीकृते ॥
You will see Laṅkā that is full of palaces and multi-storied buildings and is adorned with gems of every kind, destroyed on Maithili’s account.
3.38.26
अकुर्वन्तोऽपि पापानि
शुचयः पापसंश्रयात् ।
परपापैर्विनश्यन्ति
मत्स्या नागह्रदे यथा ॥
Even those who are pure and have committed no sin, will get destroyed for the sins of others, like fish in a pool that is inhabited by snakes.
3.38.27
दिव्यचन्दनदिग्धाङ्गान्
दिव्याभरणभूषितान् ।
द्रक्ष्यस्यभिहतान्भूमौ
तव दोषात्तु राक्षसान् ॥
You will see the Rākshasas, whose limbs are smeared with exquisite sandal paste and are adorned with splendid jewels, lying slaughtered on the ground because of your mistake.
3.38.28
हृतदारान् सदारांश्च
दश विद्रवतो दिशः ।
हतशेषानशरणान्
द्रक्ष्यसि त्वं निशाचरान् ॥
You will see the remaining Night-rovers fleeing, along with their wives or with their wives snatched away (from them), in all the ten directions, finding no refuge.
3.38.29
शरजालपरिक्षिप्ताम्
अग्निज्वालासमावृताम् ।
प्रदग्धभवनां लङ्कां
द्रक्ष्यसि त्वं न संशयः ॥
You will, without a doubt, see Laṅkā drowned in a torrent of arrows, engulfed in flames and its buildings burnt down (to the ground).
Māreeca lays out the consequences without mincing words.
3.38.30
परदाराभिमर्शात्तु
नान्यत्पापतरं महत् ।
प्रमदानां सहस्राणि 
तव राजन्परिग्रहः ॥
There is no sin more a heinous than coveting another man’s wife. Have you not already taken thousands of women for your wives?
3.38.31
भव स्वदारनिरतः
स्वकुलं रक्ष राक्षस ।
मानमृद्धिं च राज्यं च
जीवितं चेष्टमात्मनः ॥
Keep your interest confined to your wives. Save your kith and kin, O Rākshasa, and also your repute, wealth, kingdom, and the life that you love dearly.
3.38.32
कलत्राणि च सौम्यानि
मित्रवर्गं तथैव च ।
यदीच्छसि चिरं भोक्तं
मा कृथा रामविप्रियम् ॥
If you wish to enjoy your delicate wives and also your friends for long, do not earn the displeasure of Rāma.
3.38.33
निवार्यमाणस्सुहृदा मया भृशं
प्रसह्य सीतां यदि धर्षयिष्यसि ।
गमिष्यसि क्षीणबलस्सबान्धवो
यमक्षयं रामशरात्तजीवितः ॥
In spite of being earnestly dissuaded by me, your good friend, if you were to abduct Seetā forcibly, your army will be decimated and you will go to the abode of Death along with your kith and kin, your life taken by Rāma’s arrows!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे अष्टत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty eighth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
