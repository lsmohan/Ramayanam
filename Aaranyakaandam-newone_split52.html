Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 52
In this Sarga, Seetā cries for Jaṭāyu who has been done to death. She cries pitifully saying, ‘Save me, O Rāma, O Lakshmaṇa!’. She clings to the trees hugging them tightly, but Rāvaṇa snatches her away. Lord Brahma, however, considers the job (destruction of Rāvaṇa) to be all but done. The Ṛshis feel sad and happy at once.
It is a scene of high contrast, with the delicate and beautiful Seetā seen next to Rāvaṇa, who is like a black cloud. As she is carried off with speed, the flowers on her head and ornaments on her body fall on the ground, scattered. The beasts, birds, ponds, mountains, sun and the Dēvatas of the Vana cry for her.
3.52.1
सा तु ताराधिपमुखी
रावणेन समीक्ष्यतम् ।
गृध्रराजं विनिहतम्
विललाप सुदुःखिता ॥
She, of face resembling the lord of stars, wailed aloud bitterly seeing the lord of eagles thus been done to death by Rāvaṇa:
Moon is the lord of stars.
3.52.2
निमित्तं लक्षणज्ञानम्
शकुनिस्वरदर्शनम् ।
अवश्यं सुखदुःखेषु
नराणां प्रतिदृश्यते ॥
People certainly get an inkling of the onset of good or evil from the sounds of the birds and changes in their own body.
Changes in the body, such as a twitching eye is said to portend the onset of good or evil. A twitch in the right eye is considered a good omen and twitch in the left eye is considered a bad omen, for men, and vice versa for women.
3.52.3
नूनं राम न जानासि
महद्व्यसनमात्मनः ।
धावन्ति नूनं काकुत्स्थम्
मदर्थं मृगपक्षिणः ॥
O Rāma! You certainly do not know the great calamity that has befallen me. The birds and beasts are certainly rushing to Kākutstha for the sake of (conveying the news about) me!
3.52.4
अयं हि पापचारेण
मां त्रातुमभिसङ्गतः ।
शेते विनिहतो भूमौ
ममाभाग्याद्विहंगमः ॥
Alas, it is my misfortune that the bird who came to my rescue, is done to death by this evil one, and is lying on the ground!
3.52.5
त्राहि मामद्य काकुत्स्थ
लक्ष्मणेति वराङ्गना ।
सुसन्त्रस्ता समाक्रन्दत्
शृण्वतां तु यथान्तिके ॥
‘Save me, save me now, O Kākutstha, and you Lakshmaṇa!’ cried that best of the women in her terror, as if they were within earshot.
3.52.6
तां क्लिष्टमाल्याभरणाम्
विलपन्तीमनाथवत् ।
अभ्यधावत वैदेहीम्
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ॥
Rāvaṇa, the lord of the Rākshasas, dashed to her as she wept like one forlorn, her garlands and ornaments all crumpled.
3.52.7
तां लतामिव वेष्टन्तीम्
आलिङ्गन्तीं महाद्रुमान् ।
मुञ्च मुञ्चेति बहुशः
प्रवदन्राक्षसाधिपः ।
क्रोशन्तीं राम रामेति
रामेण रहितां वने ।
जीवितान्ताय केशेषु
जग्राहान्तकसन्निभः ॥
Bereft of Rāma, crying ‘O Rāma, O Rāma’, she clung to the giant trees like a vine. And the lord of the Rākshasas, who looked like the lord of Death, bringing death upon himself, caught her by the hair saying, ‘leave it, leave it!’.
3.52.9
प्रधर्षितायां सीतायाम्
बभूव सचराचरम् ।
जगत्सर्वममर्यादम्
तमसाऽऽन्धेन संवृतम् ।
न वाति मारुतस्तत्र
निष्प्रभोऽभूद्दिवाकरः ॥
With Seetā thus violated, the world was enveloped in darkness. All its creatures, stationary and moving, found the norms that govern them come to null. No wind blew there and the sun lost his luster.
3.52.10
दृष्ट्वा सीतां परामृष्टाम्
दीनां दिव्येन चक्षुषा ।
कृतं कार्यमिति श्रीमान्
व्याजहार पितामहः ॥
The magnanimous grandsire (Brahma) noting through his divine eye the pitiful Seetā thus being touched (by Rāvaṇa) said, ‘it is all but done!’
3.52.11
प्रहृष्टा व्यथिताश्चासन्
सर्वे ते परमर्षयः ।
दृष्ट्वा सीतां परामृष्टाम्
दण्डकारण्यवासिनः ।
रावणस्य विनाशं च
प्राप्तं बुद्ध्वा यदृच्छया ॥
All the great Ṛshis who lived in Daṇḍaka Araṇya, felt sad and happy at once, sad for Seetā being thus touched, and happy knowing that the destruction of Rāvaṇa was imminent (at last) by the turn of destiny.
3.52.13
स तु तां राम रामेति
रुदन्तीं लक्ष्मणेति च ।
जगामादाय चाकाशम्
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ॥
Rāvaṇa, the lord of the Rākshasas, rose up into the sky, carrying her off, as she wailed ‘O Rāma, O Rāma’ and ‘O Lakshmaṇa!’
3.52.14
तप्ताभरणजुष्टाङ्गी
पीतकौशेयवासिनी ।
रराजराजपुत्री तु
विद्युत्सौदामिनी यथा ॥
The princess, with her body covered in jewels of burnished gold and garments of yellow silk, shone like a lightning flash from the Sudāma mountain.
Vālmeeki does not fail to note the visuals of the scene even at the time of such a sad event, and narrates them beautifully.
3.52.15
उद्धूतेन च वस्त्रेण
तस्याः पीतेन रावणः ।
अधिकं परिबभ्राज
गिरिर्दीप्त इवाग्निना ॥
With her yellow garment fluttering (around him) in the wind, Rāvaṇa looked like a mountain lit up by fire.
3.52.16
तस्याः परमकल्याण्याः
ताम्राणि सुरभीणि च ।
पद्मपत्राणि वैदेह्या
अभ्यकीर्यन्त रावणम् ॥
The red and fragrant lotus petals worn by Vaidēhee, the home of all auspiciousness, fell scattered all over Rāvaṇa.
3.52.17
तस्याः कौशेयमुद्धूतम्
आकाशे कनकप्रभम् ।
बभौ चादित्यरागेण
ताम्रमभ्रमिवातपे ॥
Her silk garment of golden sheen, lit up by the scarlet of the evening sun and fluttering in the wind, shone like a reddened cloud.
3.52.18
तस्यास्तत्सुनसं वक्त्रम्
आकाशे रावणाङ्कगम् ।
न रराज विना रामम्
विनालमिव पङ्कजम् ॥
But being on the lap of Rāvaṇa in the sky, and bereft of Rāma, her face with its shapely nose did not shine, like a lotus without its stalk would not.
3.52.19
बभूव जलदं नीलम्
भित्त्वा चन्द्र इवोदितः ।
सुललाटं सुकेशान्तम्
पद्मगर्भाभमव्रणम् ।
शुक्लैस्सुविमलैर्दन्तै
प्रभावद्भिरलङ्कृतम् ।
तस्यास्तद्विमलं वक्त्रम्
आकाशे रावणाङ्कगम् ।
रुदितं व्यपमृष्टास्रम्
चन्द्रवत्प्रियदर्शनम् ।
सुनासं चारुताम्रोष्ठम्
आकाशे हाटकप्रभम् ॥
Her face exhausted by crying, tears wiped, nevertheless with lovely forehead, beautiful red lips, handsome nose, tresses that were jet-black to the end, flawless like the lotus that had just bloomed, and adorned with flashing white teeth, looked, as she was being carried off by Rāvaṇa on his lap in the sky, like the moon that had burst through a rift in the clouds.
3.52.22
राक्षसेन समाधूतम्
तस्यास्तद्वदनं शुभम् ।
शुशुभे न विना रामम्
दिवा चन्द्र इवोदितः ॥
Her beautiful face, trembling as she was carried off by the Rākshasa and deprived of Rāma’s company, was grim like the moon rising during the day.
3.52.23
सा हेमवर्णा नीलाङ्गम्
मैथिली राक्षसाधिपम् ।
शुशुभे काञ्चनी काञ्ची
नीलं गजमिवाश्रिता ॥
Maithili of golden complexion looked like a golden girdle on a black elephant against the black body of the lord of Rākshasas.
3.52.24
सा पद्मगौरी हेमाभा
रावणं जनकात्मजा ।
विद्युद्घनमिवाविश्य
शुशुभे तप्तभूषणा ॥
Janaka’s daughter of golden complexion, with burnished ornaments, resembling a yellow lotus, set against Rāvaṇa, looked like a lightning flash against a cloud.
3.52.25
तस्या भूषणघोषेण
वैदेह्या राक्षसाधिपः ।
बभौ सचपलो नीलः
सघोष इव तोयदः ॥
The tinkling of the ornaments of Vaidēhee made the lord of Rākshasas look like a black cloud that was rumbling with lightning.
3.52.26
उत्तमाङ्गाच्च्युता तस्याः
पुष्पवृष्टिस्समन्ततः ।
सीताया ह्रिममाणायाः
पपात धरणीतले ॥
As Seetā was being carried off, the flowers on her head fell all around on the earth.
3.52.27
सा तु रावणवेगेन
पुष्पवृष्टिः समन्ततः ।
समाधूता दशग्रीवम्
पुनरेवाभ्यवर्तत ॥
But those flowers that fell around followed the Ten-necked Rāvaṇa again, as they were swept into the air as he zoomed fast.
3.52.28
अभ्यवर्तत पुष्पाणाम्
धारा वैश्रवणानुजम् ।
नक्षत्रमाला विमला
मेरुं नगमिवोन्नतम् ॥
The stream of flowers followed the younger brother of Vaiṡravaṇa, like rows of impeccable stars lining up to the towering Mēru mountain.
3.52.29
चरणान्नूपुरं भ्रष्टम्
वैदेह्या रत्नभूषितम् ।
विद्युन्मण्डलसङ्काशम्
पपात मधुरस्वनम् ॥
The gem studded anklet of Vaidēhee, which looked like the arc of lightning, slipped from her foot and fell down, making a pleasing sound.
3.52.30
तरुप्रवालरक्ता सा
नीलाङ्गं राक्षसेश्वरम् ।
प्राशोभयत वैदेही
गजं कक्ष्येव काञ्चनी ॥
Vaidēhee with her complexion like the reddish gold of tender shoots on trees, lit up the black body of the king of Rākshasas like a golden girth does an elephant.
3.52.31
तां महोल्कामिवाकाशे
दीप्यमानां स्वतेजसा ।
जहाराऽऽकाशमाविश्य
सीतां वैश्रवणानुजः ॥
Rising into the sky, the younger brother of Vaiṡravaṇa thus abducted her, who with her inherent luster shone like a comet in the sky.
3.52.32
तस्यास्तान्यग्निवर्णानि
भूषणानि महीतले ।
सघोषाण्यवकीर्यन्त
क्षीणास्तारा इवाम्बरात् ॥
Her ornaments that shone like the various shades of fire fell tinkling to the ground, scattered like stars that fell from the sky.
3.52.33
तस्यास्स्तनान्तराद्भ्रष्टो
हारस्ताराधिपद्युतिः ।
वैदेह्या निपतन्भाति
गङ्गेव गगनाच्च्युता ॥
The necklace with the soothing shine of the lord of stars, that fell slipping between her breasts looked like Gaṅgā falling from the sky.
3.52.34
उत्पातवाताभिहता
नानाद्विजगणायुताः ।
माभैरिति विधूताग्रा
व्याजह्रुरिव पादपाः ॥
Swayed by the gush of the wind, the trees with flocks of birds on them shook their tops as if to tell (her), ‘do not be scared!’
3.52.35
नलिन्यो ध्वस्तकमलाः
त्रस्तमीनजलेचराः ।
सखीमिव गतोच्छ्वासाम्
अन्वशोचन्त मैथिलीम् ॥
The lotus ponds, their flowers wilted, their fish and other aquatic creatures frightened, grieved for Maithili, as if she were their dear friend who had stopped breathing.
3.52.36
समन्तादभिसम्पत्य
सिंह व्याघ्र मृग गजाः ।
अन्वधावंस्तदा रोषात्
सीतां छायानुगामिनः ॥
Irate lions, tigers, deer and elephants from everywhere ran jostling together following her shadow.
3.52.37
जलप्रपातास्रमुखाः
शृङ्गैरुच्छ्रितबाहुभिः ।
सीतायां ह्रियमाणायाम्
विक्रोशन्तीव पर्वताः ॥
The mountains seemed to cry out with their cascades for tears and the crests for uplifted arms, as Seetā was being carried off.
3.52.38
ह्रियमाणां तु वैदेहीम्
दृष्ट्वा दीनो दिवाकरः ।
प्रतिध्वस्तप्रभश्श्रीमान्
आसीत्पाण्डरमण्डलः ॥
The effulgent sun felt miserable seeing Vaidēhee being carried off; his luster dimmed and his orb became pale.
3.52.39
नास्ति धर्मः कुतस्सत्यम्
नार्जवं नानृशंसता ।
यत्र रामस्य वैदेहीम्
भार्यां हरति रावणः ।
इति सर्वाणि भूतानि
गणशः पर्यदेवयन् ॥
‘There is neither Dharma, nor honesty, nor kindness and what to speak of Truth, when Rāvaṇa could abduct Vaidēhee, the wife of Rāma!’ lamented all creatures in unison.
3.52.40
वित्रस्तका दीनमुखा
रुरुदुर्मृगपोतकाः ।
उद्वीक्ष्योद्वीक्ष्य नयनैः
अस्रपाताविलेक्षणाः ॥
The fawns, terrified and woebegone, wept, looking up repeatedly with their eyes turbid with tears.
3.52.41
सुप्रवेपितगात्राश्च
बभूवुर्वनदेवताः ।
विक्रोशन्तीं दृढं सीताम्
दृष्ट्वा दुःखं तथा गताम् ॥
Seeing Seetā thus taken over by grief and crying aloud bitterly, the Dēvatas of the Vana trembled form head to toe.
3.52.42
तां तु लक्ष्मण रामेति
क्रोशन्तीं मधुरस्वरम् ।
अवेक्षमाणां बहुशो
वैदेहीं धरणीतलम् ।
स तामाकुलकेशान्ताम्
विप्रमृष्टविशेषकाम् ।
जहारात्म विनाशाय
दशग्रीवो मनस्स्विनीम् ॥
The Ten-necked, inviting his own destruction, carried off Vaidēhee, the prideful one, as she, with her hair disheveled, and her Tilaka out of shape, cried ‘O Lakshmaṇa, O Rāma!’ in her sweet voice, looking at the ground repeatedly.
3.52.44
ततस्तु सा चारुदती शुचिस्मिता
विनाकृता बन्धुजनेन मैथिली ।
अपश्यती राघवलक्ष्मणावुभौ
विवर्णवक्त्राभयभारपीडिता ॥
Maithili’s face that usually sported a pure white smile with lovely teeth became pale, as she was weighed down by fear, being separated from all her kith and kin and with Rāghava and Lakshmaṇa being out of sight.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे द्विपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty second in Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
