Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 34
In this Sarga, upon hearing the harsh words of Ṡūrpaṇakhā, Rāvaṇa asks her about the details of Rāma. Ṡūrpaṇakhā tells Rāvaṇa everything about Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Seetā. She makes a special mention of the beauty of Seetā and tells that she would make a good wife for Rāvaṇa. She says that she tried to snatch Seetā and bring him as a wife for Rāvaṇa, and that is the reason for her disfiguration by Lakshmaṇa. She encourages Rāvaṇa to kill Rāma and Lakshmaṇa and abduct Seetā by force and make her his wife.
3.34.1
ततश्शूर्पणखां दृष्ट्वा
ब्रुवन्तीं परुषं वचः ।
अमात्यमध्ये सङ्क्रुद्धः
परिपप्रच्छ रावणः ॥
Seeing Ṡūrpaṇakhā speak such harsh words while he was among the midst of ministers, Rāvaṇa became angry and asked:
3.34.2
कश्च रामः कथंवीर्यः
किंरूपः किंपराक्रमः ।
किमर्थं दण्डकारण्यं
प्रविष्टस्सुदुरासदम् ॥
Who is Rāma? How does he look? What is his strength? What is his strike like? Why did he enter the impenetrable Daṇḍaka Araṇya?
3.34.3
आयुधं किञ्च रामस्य
निहता येन राक्षसाः ।
खरश्च निहतस्संख्ये
दूषणस्त्रिशिरास्तथा ॥
What is the weapon Rāma used in the fight, with which he killed the Rākshasas, including Khara, Dūshaṇa and Triṡira?
3.34.4
इत्युक्ता राक्षसेन्द्रेण
राक्षसी क्रोधमूर्छिता ।
ततो रामं यथातत्वम्
आख्यातुमुपचक्रमे ॥
Hearing those words of the lord of Rākshasas, The Rākshasi, who was consumed by rage, started to tell faithfully in detail about Rāma.
3.34.5
दीर्घबाहुर्विशालाक्षः
चीरकृष्णाजिनाम्बरः ।
कन्दर्पसमरूपश्च
रामो दशरथात्मजः ॥
Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, is very handsome like Manmatha with long arms and broad chest. He wears robes of bast and the skin of the black antelope.
3.34.6
शक्रचापनिभं चापं
विकृष्य कनकाङ्गदम् ।
दीप्तान् क्षिपति नाराचान्
सर्पानिव महाविषान् ॥
He shoots dazzling Nārācās that fall like venomous vipers, by drawing the string of his bow, which with its gold bands looks like the bow of Ṡakra.
3.34.7
नाददानं शरान्घोरान्
नमुञ्चन्तं शिलीमुखान् ।
न कार्मुकं विकर्षन्तं
रामं पश्यामि संयुगे ॥
I could not see, in that battle, when Rāma took out the dreadful arrows, when he pulled the string of the bow and when he released those sharps barbs.
3.34.8
हन्यमानं तु तत्सैन्यं
पश्यामि शरवृष्टिभिः ।
इन्द्रेणेवोत्तमं सस्यम्
आहतन्त्वश्मवृष्टिभिः ॥
I could only see the army killed by that torrent of arrows, like crops hit by Indra with a storm of hail.
3.34.9
रक्षसां भीमरूपाणां
सहस्राणि चतुर्दश ।
निहतानि शरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
तेनैकेन पदातिना ।
अर्धाधिकमुहूर्तेन
खरश्च सहदूषणः ।
ऋषीणामभयं दत्तं
कृतक्षेमाश्च दण्डकाः ॥
Fourteen thousand fearsome looking Rākshasas along with Khara and Dūshaṇa were killed in a matter of one and half Muhūrta by the sharp arrows of just one person who stood on the ground without a mount, giving assurance to the Ṛshis from fear and making Daṇḍaka safe.
3.34.11
एका कथंचिन्मुक्ताऽहं
परिभूय महात्मना ।
स्त्रीवधं शङ्कमानेन
रामेण विदितात्मना ॥
I am the only one to escape somehow, being dismissed in disgrace by that Mahatma Rāma, the conscientious one, because he was not willing to kill a woman.
3.34.12
भ्राता चास्य महातेजा
गुणवांस्तुल्यविक्रमः ।
अनुरक्तश्च भक्तश्च
लक्ष्मणो नाम वीर्यवान् ॥
His loving and devoted brother, Lakshmaṇa by name, of great qualities, is also a mighty strong Veera, an equal to him in prowess.
3.34.13
अमर्षी दुर्जयो जेता
विक्रान्तो बुद्धिमान्बली ।
रामस्य दक्षिणो बाहुः
नित्यं प्राणो बहिश्चरः ॥
He is of great prowess, invincible, smart, strong and victorious against all comers. He is the right hand of Rāma. He is like the life-energy of Rāma, that happens to be ranging outside the body.
3.34.14
रामस्य तु विशालाक्षी
पूर्णेन्दुसदृशानना ।
धर्मपत्नी प्रिया भर्तुः
नित्यं प्रियहिते रता ॥
Rāma’s lawful wife, with wide eyes and a face like the full moon, the love of her husband, is ever devoted to his wellbeing.
3.34.15
सा सुकेशी सुनासोरुः
सुरूपा च यशस्विनी ।
देवतेव वनस्यास्य
राजते श्रीरिवापरा ॥
That woman of renown has lovely figure, lovely hair, shapely nose and great thighs. She shines like the Dēvata of the Vana and like another Lakshmee.
3.34.16
तप्तकाञ्चनवर्णाभा
रक्ततुङ्गनखी शुभा ।
सीता नाम वरारोहा
वैदेही तनुमध्यमा ॥
Her name is Seetā and she is the princess of Vidēha. She has the complexion of gold purified in fire, and high nails of deep crimson color. Her Kati is exquisite and her waist is slender.
3.34.17
नैव देवी न गन्धर्वी
न यक्षी न च किन्नरी ।
नैवंरूपा मया नारी
दृष्टपूर्वा महीतले ॥
I have never seen a woman of such beauty, on the surface of the earth and even among Dēvas, Gandharvas, Yakshas and Kinnaras.
3.34.18
यस्य सीता भवेद्भार्या
यं च हृष्टा परिष्वजेत् ।
अतिजीवेत्स सर्वेषु
लोकेष्वपि पुरन्दरात् ॥
He who has Seetā for wife and whom she embraces with joy would have the best life in all the worlds, better than even that of Purandara.
3.34.19
सा सुशीला वपुश्श्लाघ्या
रूपेणाप्रतिमा भुवि ।
तवानुरूपा भार्या स्यात्
त्वं च तस्यास्तथा पतिः ॥
She is virtuous, excels by the beauty of her body, and has no equal in beauty on the earth. She would be the perfect wife for you, and similarly, you her husband.
3.34.20
तां तु विस्तीर्णजघनां
पीनोत्तुङ्गपयोधराम्  ।
भार्यार्थे च तवानेतुम्
उद्यताहं वराननाम् ।
विरूपितास्मि क्रूरेण
लक्ष्मणेन महाभुज ॥
O you of the mighty arm! When I tried to carry off that woman of lovely face, large hips and high and full breasts, so that she could become your wife, I was disfigured by the cruel Lakshmaṇa!
3.34.21
तां तु दृष्ट्वाद्य वैदेहीं
पूर्णचन्द्रनिभाननाम् ।
मन्मथस्य शराणां वै
त्वं विधेयो भविष्यसि ॥
If you see Vaidēhee, whose face is radiant like the full moon, you will succumb right away to the darts of Manmatha.
3.34.22
यदि तस्यामभिप्रायो
भार्यार्थे तव जायते ।
शीघ्रमुद्ध्रियतां पादो
जयार्थमिह दक्षिणः ॥
If you feel like taking her as your wife, put forth your right foot without delay on the way to victory.
3.34.23
कुरु प्रियं तथा तेषां
रक्षसां राक्षसेश्वर ।
वधात्तस्य नृशंसस्य
रामस्याश्रमवासिनः ॥
O lord of Rākshasas! Kill that cruel Rāma who lives in the Āṡrama, and propitiate those Rākshasas!
3.34.24
तं शरैर्निशितैर्हत्वा
लक्ष्मणं च महारथम् ।
हतनाथां सुखं सीतां
यथावदुपभोक्ष्यसि ॥
Killing him with sharp arrows, and also Lakshmaṇa, the great charioter, you can enjoy Seetā, who no more would have a husband, happily as you wish!
3.34.25
रोचते यदि ते वाक्यं
ममैतद्राक्षसेश्वर ।
क्रियतां निर्विशङ्केन
वचनं मम रावण ॥
O Rāvaṇa! O lord of Rākshasas! If you like these words of mine, you shall follow my words with no further hesitation!
3.34.26
विज्ञायेहात्मशक्तिं च
ह्रियतामबला बलात् ।
सीता सर्वानवद्याङ्गी
भार्यार्थे राक्षसेश्वर ॥
Now, O lord of Rākshasas, you shall, with your superior strength, forcefully abduct Seetā of flawless limbs and make her your wife.
3.34.27
निशम्य रामेण शरैरजिह्मगैः
हतान् जनस्थानगतान्निशाचरान् ।
खरं च बुद्ध्वा निहतं च दूषणं
त्वमत्र कृत्यं प्रतिपत्तुमर्हसि ॥
Now that you have heard of the killing of the Rākshasas residing in Janasthāna by Rāma with arrows that fly straight, and know about the slaying of Khara and Dūshaṇa, you shall decide what is to be done!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे चतुस्त्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty fourth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
