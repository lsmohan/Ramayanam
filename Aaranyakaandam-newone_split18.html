Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 18
In this Sarga, Rāma facetiously tells Ṡūrpaṇakhā that as he was already married, she would have to deal with a co-wife, if she marries him. He tells her to approach Lakshmaṇa who does not have a wife. She immediately approaches Lakshmaṇa and asks him to take her for his wife, saying that she would be a perfect match for him.
Lakshmaṇa also, in jest, tells her that he is an attendant of Rāma and if she marries him, she would also become a servant along with him. Hence he advices her to go back to Rāma, and again, in jest, suggests that Rāma would easily give up Seetā for her.
Not getting the sarcasm in Lakshmaṇa’s words, Ṡūrpaṇakhā goes back to Rāma and tells him that she would eat away Seetā, so that she does not have to deal with a co-wife. She plunges on Seetā. Rāma stops her and asks Lakshmaṇa to disfigure her. Lakshmaṇa lops of her nose and ears. She flees away screaming to her brother Khara and tells him about Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and all that happened to her.
3.18.1
ततश्शूर्पणखां रामः
कामपाशावपाशिताम् ।
स्वच्छया श्लक्ष्णया वाचा
स्मितपूर्वमथाब्रवीत् ॥
Rāma smiled at Ṡūrpaṇakhā who was experiencing the pangs of passion and spoke in gentle voice:
3.18.2
कृतदारोऽस्मि भवति
भार्येयं दयिता मम ।
त्वद्विधानां तु नारीणां
सुदुःखा ससपत्नता ॥
Madam! I already have a wife. This woman is my lovely wife. It would be sad for a woman like you to have a co-wife.
3.18.3
अनुजस्त्वेष मे भ्राता
शीलवान्प्रियदर्शनः ।
श्रीमानकृतदारश्च
लक्ष्मणो नाम वीर्यवान् ॥
But here is this younger brother of mine, known as Lakshmaṇa, who is virtuous, good-looking, richly endowed and strong, who does not have a wife.
3.18.4
अपूर्वी भार्यया चार्थी
तरुणः प्रियदर्शनः ।
अनुरूपश्च ते भर्ता
रूपस्यास्य भविष्यति ॥
He does not have a wife and needs one. He is youthful and charming. He will be a good match as a husband, for you.
3.18.5
एनं भज विशालाक्षि
भर्तारं भ्रातरं मम ।
असपत्ना वरारोहे
मेरुमर्कप्रभा यथा ॥
O woman of lovely Kaṭee! O lady of wide eyes! Take him, my brother, for your husband, like the sun-shine does the Mēru mountain. You will not have to worry about a co-wife.
3.18.6
इति रामेण सा प्रोक्ता
राक्षसी काममोहिता ।
विसृज्य रामं सहसा
ततो लक्ष्मणमब्रवीत् ॥
Told thus by Rāma, she, the Rākshasi, gripped by the pangs of passion, left Rāma alone and turned immediately to Lakshmaṇa saying:
3.18.7
अस्य रूपस्य ते युक्ता
भार्याहं वरवर्णिनी ।
मया सह सुखं सर्वान्
दण्डकान्विचरिष्यसि ॥
I, with the best complexion, am the perfect match for you as a wife. Enjoy roaming the entire Daṇḍaka along with me!
3.18.8
एवमुक्तस्तु सौमित्री
राक्षस्या वाक्यकोविदः ।
ततश्शूर्पणखीं स्मित्वा
लक्ष्मणो युक्तमब्रवीत् ॥
Asked thus by the Rākshasi, Lakshmaṇa, the son of Sumitrā, who was (also) adept at words, smiled at Ṡūrpaṇakhā and responded aptly:
3.18.9
कथं दासस्य मे दासी
भार्या भवितुमिच्छसि ।
सोऽहमार्येण परवान्
भ्रात्रा कमलवर्णिनि ॥
O woman of lotus shine! I am an attendant of my brother. Why would you want to become my wife and reduce yourself to be subservient to another servant?
3.18.10
समृद्धार्थस्य सिद्धार्था
मुदितामलवर्णिनी ।
आर्यस्य त्वं विशालाक्षि
भार्या भव यवीयसी ॥
O woman of wide eyes and spotless complexion! Become the younger wife of my revered brother, who has all that the heart can desire. You will attain your fondest hopes!
3.18.11
एनां विरूपामसतीं
करालां निर्णतोदरीम् ।
भार्यां वृद्धां परित्यज्य
त्वामेवैष भजिष्यति ॥
He would give up this old, bad, ugly, frightening and flat-stomached one. He will certainly go with you.
Lakshmaṇa, obviously is imitating Ṡūrpaṇakhā’s description of Seetā in jest.
3.18.12
को हि रूपमिदं श्रेष्ठं
संत्यज्य वरवर्णिनि ।
मानुषीषु वरारोहे
कुर्याद्भावं विचक्षणः ॥
Who among men, O you of beautiful Kaṭee, will give up one (like you) who has the best figure and great complexion, for the sake of a mortal woman?
3.18.13
इति सा लक्ष्मणेनोक्ता
कराला निर्णतोदरी ।
मन्यते तद्वचस्तथ्यं
परिहासाविचक्षणा ॥
Utterly incapable of seeing the sarcasm in the words of Lakshmaṇa, she, the frightful one with a drooping belly, thought that he said it sincerely.
3.18.14
सा रामं पर्णशालायां
उपविष्टं परन्तपम् ।
सीतया सह दुर्दर्षम्
अब्रवीत्काममोहिता ॥
And she, taken over by passion, said to the invincible Rāma, scorcher of the foe, as he sat in the cottage with Seetā:
3.18.15
एनां विरूपामसतीं
करालां निर्णतोदरीम् ।
वृद्धां भार्यामवष्टभ्य
मां न त्वं बहुमन्यसे ॥
You disregard me completely, stuck on this old wife of yours, who is ugly, frightful and flat-bellied.
3.18.16
अद्येमां भक्षयिष्यामि
पश्यतस्तव मानुषीम् ।
त्वया सह चरिष्यामि
निस्सपत्ना यथासुखम् ॥
I will eat away this mortal woman now right before your eyes. Then I can roam with you happily without the worry of a co-wife.
3.18.17
इत्युक्त्वा मृगशाबाक्षीं
अलातसदृशेक्षणा ।
अभ्यधावत्सुसङ्कृद्धा
महोल्कां रोहिणीमिव ॥
Saying this, she with eyes (fearsome) like live coal dashed in a rage onto (Seetā) of fawn eyes, like a giant shooting star onto the star Rōhiṇee.
3.18.18
तां मृत्युपाशप्रतिमां
आपतन्तीं महाबलः ।
निगृह्य रामः कुपित
स्ततो लक्ष्मणमब्रवीत् ॥
The mighty strong Rāma, enraged, stopping her who was falling like the noose of Death, said to Lakshmaṇa:
3.18.19
क्रूरैरनार्यै स्सौमित्रे
परिहासः कथञ्चन ।
न कार्यः पश्यवैदेहीं
कथञ्चित्सौम्य जीवतीम् ॥
O Sowmitri! O gentle one! One should never jest with the wicked and the ignoble! Look at Vaidēhee, she barely breathes!
3.18.20
इमां विरूपामसतीम्
अतिमत्तां महोदरीम् ।
राक्षसीं पुरुषव्याघ्र
विरूपयितुमर्हसि ॥
O tiger among men! Disfigure this ugly, wicked, pot-bellied and demented Rākshasi!
Disfiguring a harmful woman, instead of killing, seems to be the norm of the day. In Bāla Kāṇḍa also, Rāma wanted to let go of Tāṭaka with disfigurement, by cutting her nose and ears (1.26.11) instead of killing her because she was a woman and such disfigurement seems to deplete the energy (1.26.11-12).
3.18.21
इत्युक्तो लक्ष्मणस्तस्याः
क्रुद्धो रामस्य पार्श्वतः ।
उद्धृत्य खङ्गं चिच्छेद
कर्णनासं महाबलः ॥
Thus told, Lakshmaṇa of immense strength whipped out the knife that was beside Rāma and cut off her nose and ears, in wrath.
3.18.22
निकृत्तकर्णनासा तु
विस्वरं सा विनद्य च ।
यथागतं प्रदुद्राव
घोरा शूर्पणखा वनम् ॥
With her nose and ears lopped off, the horrid Ṡūrpaṇakhā fled back screaming into the Vana whence she had come.
3.18.23
सा विरूपा महाघोरा
राक्षसी शोणितोक्षिता ।
ननाद विविधान्नादान्
यथा प्रावृषि तोयदः ॥
The ugly and dreadful Rākshasi, with blood dripping, made all sorts of sounds like the clouds of monsoon season.
3.18.24
सा विक्षरन्ती रुधिरं
बहुधा घोरदर्शना ।
प्रगृह्य बाहू गर्जन्ती
प्रविवेश महावनम् ॥
With blood streaming from her in torrents, the woman, who was a frightful sight, entered the great Vana wringing her hands and bellowing.
3.18.25
ततस्तु सा राक्षससङ्घसंवृतं
खरं जनस्थानगतं विरूपिता ।
उपेत्य तं भ्रातरमुग्रदर्शनं
पपात भूमौ गगनाद्यथाऽशनिः ॥
Arriving before Khara, her fierce-looking brother, who was in Janasthāna surrounded by the hordes of Rākshasas, she fell down on the ground like a thunderbolt from the sky.
3.18.26
ततस्सभार्यं भयमोहमूर्छिता
सलक्ष्मणं राघवमागतं वनम् ।
विरूपणं चात्मनि शोणितोक्षिता
शशंस सर्वं भगिनी खरस्य सा ॥
And that sister of Khara, who was drenched in blood and almost fainting with fear and bewilderment, told him all about Rāghava who had come to the Vana along with Lakshmaṇa and wife, and about her own disfigurement.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे अष्टादशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighteenth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
