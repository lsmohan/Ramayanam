Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 21
In this Sarga, Seetā tells Rāvaṇa that she is the devoted wife of Rāma, and she cannot be separated from Rāma any more than sunlight can be separated from the sun. She makes it clear that he cannot hope for her to be his wife. She advices him to be contented with his wives.
She tells him to follow the advice and path of good men and advises him that the best thing for him to do is to take her back to Rāma and seek friendly terms with Rāma. She assures him that Rāma would forgive him, as Rāma is known for his kindness to the people who take refuge in him.
Or else, she warns, that he cannot escape the arrows of Rāma, no matter where he hides and that all his Rākshasas and his city will be destroyed by the arrows of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
5.21.1
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
सीता रौद्रस्य रक्षसः ।
आर्ता दीनस्वरा दीनम्
प्रत्युवाच शनैर्वचः ॥
On hearing those words of the fierce Rākshasa, Seetā, feeling distressed and miserable, answered slowly in a doleful voice:
5.21.2
दुःखार्ता रुदती सीता
वेपमाना तपस्विनी ।
चिन्तयन्ती वरारोहा
पतिमेव पतिव्रता ।
तृणमन्तरतः कृत्वा
प्रत्युवाच शुचिस्मिता ॥
The suffering, crying and trembling Seetā, of pure smile and beautiful Kaṭee, a devoted wife, overwhelmed by grief, answered him, placing a straw between herself and him, thinking only of her husband:
5.21.3
निवर्तय मनो मत्तः
स्वजने क्रियतां मनः ।
न मां प्रार्थयितुं युक्तम्
सुसिद्धिमिव पापकृत् ॥
Give up your hankering for me. Direct your mind towards your own women. You cannot hope for me any more than a sinner can aspire for the ultimate bliss.
5.21.4
अकार्यं न मया कार्यम्
एकपत्न्या विगर्हितम् ।
कुलं सम्प्राप्तया पुण्यम्
कुले महति जातया ॥
What should not be done and would be condemned by the world, will never be done by a wife devoted to a single man, especially by one born into a great family and married into another pious one.
5.21.5
एवमुक्त्वा तु वैदेही
रावणं तं यशस्विनी ।
राक्षसं पृष्ठतः कृत्वा
भूयो वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
After saying these words, Vaidēhee of great renown, turned her back to Rāvaṇa, the Rākshasa, and said these words again :
5.21.6
नाहमौपयिकी भार्या
परभार्या सती तव ।
साधु धर्ममवेक्षस्व
साधु साधुव्रतं चर ॥
I, being the devoted wife of another man, will not make a good wife for you. Think carefully and deeply about Dharma. Do what virtuous men do.
5.21.7
यथा तव तथान्येषाम्
दारा रक्ष्या निशाचर ।
आत्मानमुपमां कृत्वा
स्वेषु दारेषु रम्यताम् ॥
O Rākshasa! O Night-rover! Just like yours, everyone else’s wives deserve to be protected. Think how it would be for you (if your wives are carried off); be contented enjoying your own wives.
5.21.8
अतुष्टं स्वेषु दारेषु
चपलं चलितेन्द्रियम् ।
नयन्ति निकृतिप्रज्ञम्
परदाराः पराभवम् ॥
The man who is not satisfied with his own wives, whose mind becomes fickle, senses go awry, and whose faculties become impaired will land only in disgrace by (seeking) other’s wives.
5.21.9
इह सन्तो न वा सन्ति
सतो वा नानुवर्तसे ।
तथाहि विपरीता ते
बुद्धिराचारवर्जिता ॥
Are there no good men here? Or are you not listening to whoever is there? It seems so, given the way your mind has become perverted, distanced from all conventions.
5.21.10
वचो मिथ्याप्रणीतात्मा
पथ्यमुक्तं विचक्षणैः ।
राक्षसानामभावाय
त्वं वा न प्रतिपद्यसे ॥
Or, are you, with your mind given to hallucinations, unable to appreciate the wholesome advice given by the wise, only to see the Rākshasas annihilated?
5.21.11
अकृतात्मानमासाद्य
राजानमनये रतम् ।
समृद्धानि विनश्यन्ति
राष्ट्राणि नगराणि च ॥
Countries and cities that are otherwise plentiful can get totally ruined under the governance of kings who lose control of their minds and indulge in distractions.
5.21.12
तथेयं त्वां समासाद्य
लङ्का रत्नौघसङ्कुला ।
अपराधात्तवैकस्य
नचिराद्विनशिष्यति ॥
Similarly, having got you for a king, this Laṅkā, abundant in every kind of riches, can get ruined in no time because of the indiscretion of just one person, you!
5.21.13
स्वकृतैर्हन्यमानस्य
रावणादीर्घदर्शिनः ।
अभिनन्दन्ति भूतानि
विनाशे पापकर्मणः ॥
All creatures will rejoice, O Rāvaṇa, when they see someone who commits sinful acts perish because of his own faults and myopic vision.
5.21.14
एवं त्वां पापकर्माणम्
वक्ष्यन्ति निकृता जनाः ।
दिष्ट्यैतद्व्यसनं प्राप्तो
रौद्र इत्येव हर्षिताः ॥
The people who have suffered at your hands and from your sinful acts, would (one day) rejoice and exclaim: “It is by good fortune that this cruel one met his ruin!”
5.21.15
शक्या लोभयितुं नाहम्
चश्वर्येण धनेन वा ।
अनन्या राघवेणाहम्
भास्करेण प्रभा यथा ॥
It is impossible to tempt me with the offer of riches or lordship. I am as inalienable from Rāghava as sunlight is from the sun.
5.21.16
उपधाय भुजं तस्य
लोकनाथस्य सत्कृतम् ।
कथं नामोपधास्यामि
भुजमन्यस्य कस्य चित् ॥
Having rested on the well-respected shoulder of the lord of the worlds, how can I rest on the shoulder of anyone else?
5.21.17
अहमौपयिकी भार्या
तस्यैव वसुधापतेः ।
व्रतस्नातस्य विप्रस्य
विद्येव विदितात्मनः ॥
Only he, that lord of the earth, deserves me as his wife, just as only a Brāhmaṇa who has had the ceremonial bath after completing his studies and who has realized the Self, deserves the ultimate knowledge.
5.21.18
साधु रावण रामेण
मां समानय दुःखिताम् ।
वने वाशितया सार्धम्
करेण्वेव गजाधिपम् ॥
O Rāvaṇa! Unite me, who is mired in sadness, with Rāma, which is the right thing to do, like uniting a female elephant with the lordly elephant in the Vana.
5.21.19
मित्रमौपयिकं कर्तुम्
रामः स्थानं परीप्सता ।
वधं चानिच्छता घोरम्
त्वयासौ पुरुषर्षभः ॥
You should seek the friendship of Rāma, a bull among men, if you want to remain in your position and want to avoid a dreadful death.
5.21.20
विदितः स हि धर्मज्ञः
शरणागतवत्सलः ।
तेन मैत्री भवतु ते
यदि जीवितुमिच्छसि ॥
That Dharmajña is broad-minded and is kind and loving towards anyone seeking refuge in him. Seek good terms with him if you want to hang on to your life!
5.21.21
प्रसादयस्व त्वं चैनम्
शरणागतवत्सलम् ।
मां चास्मै नियतो भूत्वा
निर्यातयितुमर्हसि ॥
Plead for his grace, who is kind and loving towards anyone seeking respite in him. Think calmly and take me back to him.
5.21.22
एवं हि ते भवेत्स्वस्ति
सम्प्रदाय रघूत्तमे ।
अन्यथा त्वं हि कुर्वाणो
वधं प्राप्स्यसि रावण ॥
This is the only way for you to remain safe and prosper, by giving me back to that foremost of the Raghus. O Rāvaṇa, anything short of that which you may do, would only make you meet with your death!
This intrepid roar of the lioness Seetā must be familiar to the readers, from what she said to Rāvaṇa, when he tried to carry her off in the first place (See Araṇya Kāṇḍa, Sargas 47, 53 and 56).
5.21.23
वर्जयेद्वज्रमुत्सृष्टम्
वर्जयेदन्तकश्चिरम् ।
त्वद्विधं तु न सङ्कृद्धो
लोकनाथः स राघवः ॥
One like you may be left alone by Vajra, and even by lord Yama for a long time, but not by Rāghava, the lord of the worlds, whose wrath has been roused.
5.21.24
रामस्य धनुषः शब्दम्
श्रोष्यसि त्वं महास्वनम् ।
शतक्रतुविसृष्टस्य
निर्घोषमशनेरिव ॥
You are bound to hear the terrifying sound of the bowstring of Rāma, that falls on the ears like that of a thunderbolt hurled by Ṡatakratu (Indra).
5.21.25
इह शीघ्रं सुपर्वाणो
ज्वलितास्या इवोरगाः ।
इषवो निपतिष्यन्ति
रामलक्ष्मणलक्षणाः ॥
Soon enough there will fall here straight-jointed arrows bearing the names of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa like serpents with blazing mouths.
5.21.26
रक्षांसि परिनिघ्नन्तः
पुर्यामस्यां समन्ततः ।
असंपातं करिष्यन्ति
पतन्तः कङ्कवाससः ॥
Sped by the feathers of Kaṅka bird, those arrows will rain down without an inch of space left between them, slaughtering the Rākshasas and covering every part of this city.
5.21.27
राक्षसेन्द्रमहासर्पान्
स रामगरुडो महान् ।
उद्धरिष्यति वेगेन
वैनतेय इवोरगान् ॥
Just as the son of Vinata pounces on the snakes and carries them off, so will that great Garuḍa, Rāma grab the mighty serpents, the Rākshasa Chiefs.
5.21.28
अपनेष्यति मां भर्ता
त्वत्तः शीघ्रमरिन्दमः ।
असुरेभ्यः श्रियं दीप्ताम्
विष्णुस्त्रिभिरिव क्रमैः ॥
My husband, the subduer of enemies, will take me away from you as easily as Vishnu did the glowing riches of the Asuras, just with his three strides.
5.21.29
जनस्थाने हतस्थाने
निहते रक्षसां बले ।
अशक्तेन त्वया रक्षः
कृतमेतदसाधु वै ॥
When the army of Rākshasas was destroyed, when Janasthāna became a land of the dead, O Rākshasa, you could not do anything but resort to this despicable act.
5.21.30
आश्रमं तु तयोः शून्यम्
प्रविश्य नरसिंहयोः ।
गोचरं गतयोर्भ्रात्रोः
अपनीता त्वयाधम ॥
O base creature! You entered the Āṡrama when it was free of the brothers, the lions among men who happened to go out, and carried me off.
5.21.31
न हि गन्धमुपाघ्राय
रामलक्ष्मणयोस्त्वया ।
शक्यं संदर्शने स्थातुम्
शुना शार्दूलयोरिव ॥
If only you had smelt the presence of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa around, you would not have dared to risk being seen by them, no more than a dog would risk being seen by a pair of tigers.
5.21.32
तस्य ते विग्रहे ताभ्याम्
युगग्रहणमस्थिरम् ।
वृत्रस्येवेन्द्रबाहुभ्याम्
बाहोरेकस्य विग्रहे ॥
You can hardly imagine standing off against those two, any more than the one-handed Vritra could ward off that Indra who fought with both hands.
5.21.33
क्षिप्रं तव स नाथो मे
रामः सौमित्रिणा सह ।
तोयमल्पमिवादित्यः
प्राणानादास्यते शरैः ॥
Very soon my Lord Rāma and Sowmitri will snuff out your life with their arrows, like the sun does, a shallow pool of water.
5.21.34
गिरिं कुबेरस्य गतोऽथ वालयं
सभां गतो वा वरुणस्य राज्ञः ।
असंशयं दाशरथेर्न मोक्ष्यसे
महाद्रुमः कालहतोऽशनेरिव ॥
Your time has come to an end. You cannot escape from Daṡaratha even if you hide on the mountain of Kubēra or in his house, or in the court of Varuṇa, any more than a giant tree can, from lightning.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे एकविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty first Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
