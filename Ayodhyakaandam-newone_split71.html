Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 71
In this Sarga, Bharata travels from his grandfather's place to Ayōdhyā, in all haste for seven days, crossing many rivers, traversing many forests and seeing many villages and cities along the way.
After the long journey, he reaches Ayōdhyā and witnesses a city that is far different from what he knew it as. Every place is desolate. Every house is unkempt. Even pleasure gardens are empty. No one can be seen coming into the city or going out of the city.
He finally enters the palace of the king with his head hanging and feeling miserable, distressed and joyless.
2.71.1
स प्राङ्मुखो राजगृहात्
अभिनिर्याय राघवः ।
ततस्सुदामां द्युतिमान्
सन्तीर्यावेक्ष्य तां नदीम् ।
ह्लादिनीं दूरपारां च
प्रत्यक्स्रोतस्तरङ्गिणीम् ।
शतद्रूमतरच्छ्रीमान्
नदीमिक्ष्वाकुनन्दनः ॥
Going east from Rājagṛha, that radiant scion of Raghu dynasty came to river Sudāma and crossed it. The gracious son of the Ikshwākus then crossed river Hlādini which lay wide between its banks, and the Ṡatadru flowing westwards, with crested waves.
In Ṡlōkas 1 through 17, it appears that Vālmeeki is taking his own sweet time to describe boring geography and places, at a time when Bharata is about to learn of the unspeakable calamity that has befallen Ayōdhyā and his life.
No, that is not the case. Vālmeeki is actually using a literary technique here. He is indicating the speed with which Bharata has to cross many hard to traverse places, at a moment when his mind is at the utmost unease.
To get a sense of this, think of the oft repeated scene in modern movies. The hero rushes his wounded father or brother or lover or friend to a hospital on a bike. Along the path, he is shown going through city roads, side roads, country roads, crowded markets, railway line crossings, crowds of buffalos blocking the road, etc. The scene lasts for almost two minutes, making it gripping.
When you are in a hurry, every obstacle feels more projected. Every mile seems to be packed with more things than usual. You notice things that you normally miss. That is the phenomenon that Vālmeeki is describing here.
2.71.3
ऐलाधाने नदीं तीर्त्वा
प्राप्य चापरपर्पटान् ।
शिलामकुर्वतीं तीर्त्वा
आग्नेयं शल्यकर्तनम् ।
सत्यसन्धश्शुचिश्श्रीमान्
प्रेक्षमाण श्शिलावहाम् ।
अत्ययात्स महाशैलान्
वनं चैत्ररथं प्रति ॥
Crossing the river at Ēlādhāna he reached the country of Aparaparpaṭa. Seeing the river Ṡilāvaha, which ran swiftly sweeping down boulders, that man of pure heart, rich bearing and who was very true to his word, went on, passing the city of Ṡalyakartana, that lay to the southeast. And crossing great mountains he went towards Caitraratha forest.
2.71.5
सरस्वतीं च गङ्गां च
युग्मेन प्रतिपद्यच ।
उत्तरान्वीरमत्स्यानाम्
भारुण्डं प्राविशद्वनम् ॥
Reaching the confluence of rivers Saraswati and Gaṅgā, he went on to the forest of Bhāruṇḍa to the north of the country of Veeramatsya.
2.71.6
वेगिनीं च कुलिङ्गाख्याम्
ह्लादिनीं पर्वताऽऽवृताम् ।
यमुनां प्राप्य सन्तीर्णः
बलमाश्वासयत्तदा ॥
Crossing the pleasing and swift flowing river Kuliṅga that was surrounded by hills, he reached river Yamuna, crossed it and allowed his army to rest.
2.71.7
शीतीकृत्य तु गात्राणि
क्लान्तानाश्वास्य वाजिनः ।
तत्र स्नात्वा च पीत्वा च
प्रायादादाय चोदकम् ॥
Helping his tired horses relax by splashing water on them and giving them enough rest, he bathed, quenched his thirst and, taking water with him, went on.
2.71.8
राजपुत्रो महारण्यम्
अनभीक्ष्णोपसेवितम् ।
भद्रो भद्रेण यानेन
मारुतः खमिवात्ययात् ॥
That able prince with his able fleet, breezed through many less travelled forests like wind sweeping across the sky.
2.71.9
भागीरथीं दुष्प्रतराम्
अंशुधाने महानदीम् ।
उपायाद्राघवस्तूर्णम्
प्राग्वटे विश्रुते पुरे ॥
Rāghava quickly reached the great river Bhāgeerathi that was very difficult to cross, at the well known city of Prāgvaṭa in the country of Aṃṡudhāna.
2.71.10
स गङ्गां प्राग्वटे तीर्त्वा
समायात्कुटिकोष्ठिकाम् ।
सबलस्तां स तीर्त्वाऽथ
समायाद्धर्मवर्धनम् ॥
Crossing Gaṅgā at Prāgvaṭa, he reached the stream of Kuṭikōshṭhika. Crossing it with his army, he reached the region of Dharmavardhana.
2.71.11
तोरणं दक्षिणार्धेन
जम्बूप्रस्थमुपागमत् ।
वरूथं च ययौ रम्यम्
ग्रामं दशरथात्मजः ॥
Then he reached the village of Jambūprastha, that was to the south of the area of Tōraṇam. The son of Daṡaratha then reached the beautiful village of Varūtha.
2.71.12
तत्र रम्ये वने वासम्
कृत्वाऽसौ प्राङ्मुखो ययौ ।
उद्यानमुज्जिहानायाः
प्रियका यत्र पादपाः ॥
Halting for a time by the pleasant woods there, he went east towards the garden Ujjihāna that abounded in Priyaka trees.
2.71.13
सालांस्तु प्रियकान्प्राप्य
शीघ्रानास्थाय वाजिनः ।
अनुज्ञाप्याथ भरतः
वाहिनीं त्वरितो ययौ ॥
After reaching the groves of Sāla and Priyaka trees, he took the fastest of the horses and proceeded quickly, ordering his forces to follow him.
2.71.14
वासं कृत्वा सर्वतीर्थे
तीर्त्वा चोत्तानिकां नदीम् ।
अन्या नदीश्च विविधाः
पार्वतीयैस्तुरङ्गमैः ।
हस्तिपृष्ठकमासाद्य
कुटिकामत्यवर्तत ।
ततार च नरव्याघ्रो
लौहित्ये स कपीवतीम् ॥
Halting at a place called Sarvatheertha, He crossed river Uttānika and many others on his mountain-bred horses. He then crossed the stream Kuṭika mounted on an elephant. Then that tiger among men crossed the river Kapeevati at Louhitya.
2.71.16
एकसाले स्थाणुमतीम्
विनते गोमतीं नदीम् ।
कलिङ्गनगरे चापि
प्राप्य सालवनं तदा ।
भरतः क्षिप्रमागच्छत्
सुपरिश्रान्तवाहनः ॥
Bharata then crossed river Sthāṇumati at Ēkasāla and river Gōmati at Vinata. He then reached the city of Kaliṅga and the woods of Sāla trees thereby. With his exhausted horses, he proceeded quickly.
2.71.17
वनं च समतीत्याशु
शर्वर्यामरुणोदये ।
अयोध्यां मनुना राज्ञा
निर्मितां सन्ददर्श ह ॥
Crossing the woods swiftly at night, he beheld, in the early dawn, Ayōdhyā that was built by King Manu.
2.71.18
तां पुरीं पुरुषव्याघ्रः
सप्तरात्रोषितः पथि ।
अयोध्यामग्रतो दृष्ट्वा
सारथिं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing the city of Ayōdhyā in front of him, that tiger among men, who had been on the road for seven days, said to the charioteer:
2.71.19
एषा नातिप्रतीता मे
पुण्योद्याना यशस्विनी ।
अयोध्या दृश्यते दूरात्
सारथे पाण्डुमृत्तिका ।
यज्वभिर्गुणसम्पन्नैः
ब्राह्मणैर्वेदपारगैः ।
भूयिष्ठमृद्धैराकीर्णा
राजर्षिपरिपालिता ॥
O charioteer! This city of Ayōdhyā, from this distance, does not appear to be that renowned city of whitish clay, full of pleasant gardens, virtuous performers of Yajñas, Brāhmaṇas who are masters of Vedas, people who are well-to-do and ruled by great Rājarshis!
2.71.21
अयोध्यायां पुरा शब्दः
श्रूयते तुमुलो महान् ।
समन्तान्नरनारीणाम्
तमद्य न श्रुणोम्यहम् ॥
Ayōdhyā used to be a place of great buzz with vibrant men and women everywhere! I do not hear any of it today!
2.71.22
उद्यानानि हि सायाह्ने
क्रीडित्वोपरतैर्नरैः ।
समन्तात्परिधावद्भिः
प्रकाशन्ते ममान्यथा ॥
The pleasure gardens used to look different, with men running everywhere after having sported, and rested there in the evenings.
2.71.23
तान्यद्यानुरुदन्तीव
परित्यक्तानि कामिभिः ।
अरण्यभूतेव पुरी
सारथे प्रतिभाति मे ॥
The pleasure gardens seemed to be crying, deserted by the romantic lovers. The city, O charioteer, looks like a forest to me!
2.71.24
न ह्यत्र यानैर्दृश्यन्ते
न गजैर्न च वाजिभिः ।
निर्यान्तो वाऽभियान्तो वा
नरमुख्या यथापुरम् ॥
I do not see the prominent men on their vehicles, elephants, or horses going out or coming in like they used to in the past.
2.71.25
उद्यानानि पुरा भान्ति
मत्तप्रमुदितानि च ।
जनानां रतिसंयोगे -
ष्वत्यन्तगुणवन्ति च ॥
The pleasure gardens, the best places for lovers for their love-trysts used to look lusty and filled with joy!
2.71.26
तान्येतान्यद्य पश्यामि
निरानन्दानि सर्वशः ।
स्रस्तपर्णैरनुपथम्
विक्रोशद्भिरिव द्रुमैः ॥
All of those seem cheerless today everywhere, with the trees that line their pathways seeming to cry out, with their leaves fallen.
2.71.27
नाद्यापि श्रूयते शब्दः
मत्तानां मृगपक्षिणाम् ।
संरक्तां मधुरां वाणीम्
कलं व्याहरतां बहु ॥
Now, even the sweet amorous cooing of intoxicated birds and beasts cannot be heard!
2.71.28
चन्दनागरुसम्पृक्तो
धूपसम्मूर्छितोऽतुलः ।
प्रवाति पवनश्श्रीमान्
किन्नुनाद्य यथापुरम् ॥
Why is there no rich breeze like before, charged with the scent of sandal and Agaru and the smoke of incense?
2.71.29
भेरीमृदङ्गवीणानाम्
कोणसङ्घट्टितः पुनः ।
किमद्य शब्दो विरतः
सदाऽदीनगतिः पुरा ॥
Why are the lively sounds of big drums, Mṛdaṅgas and Veeṇas that were heard incessantly in the past, silenced?
2.71.30
अनिष्टानि च पापानि
पश्यामि विविधानि च ।
निमित्तान्यमनोज्ञानि
तेन सीदति मे मनः ॥
I see so many unpleasant and evil sights and disheartening portents, that my heart is sinking.
2.71.31
सर्वथा कुशलं सूत
दुर्लभं मम बन्दुषु ।
तथाह्यसति सम्मोहे
हृदयं सीदतीव मे ॥
Nowhere, O charioteer, could I find my people to be doing well. My heart is sinking, though I cannot find the reason for it!
2.71.32
विषण्णश्श्रान्तहृदयः
त्रस्तस्सुलुलितेन्द्रियः ।
भरतः प्रविवेशाशु
पुरीमिक्ष्वाकुपालिताम् ॥
Despondent, weary at heart, scared, and his senses drooping, Bharata quickly entered the city ruled by the Ikshwākus.
2.71.33
द्वारेण वैजयन्तेन
प्राविशच्छ्रान्तवाहनः ।
द्वास्स्थैरुत्थाय विजयम्
पृष्टस्तै स्सहितो ययौ ॥
He entered with his tired mounts by the Vaijayanta Gate. People at the gate rose and greeted him with cries of ' Victory!' and followed him.
2.71.34
स त्वनेकाग्रहृदयो
द्वास्स्थं प्रत्यर्च्य तं जनम् ।
सूतमश्वपतेः क्लान्तम्
अब्रवीत्तत्र राघवः ॥
Greeting the people at the gate with courtesy, Rāghava, with his heart pulled in many directions, said to the exhausted favorite charioteer of Aṡwapati:
2.71.35
किमहं त्वरयाऽनीतः
कारणेन विनानघ ।
अशुभाशङ्कि हृदयम्
शीलं च पततीव मे ॥
Could I have been brought urgently without a cause, O blameless one? My heart suspects that something is wrong. I am not my normal self today.
2.71.36
श्रुता नो यादृशाः पूर्वम्
नृपतीनां विनाशने ।
आकारांस्तानहं सर्वान्
इह पश्यामि सारथे ॥
All that we hear as signs of the passing away of Kings, O charioteer, I see here.
2.71.37
सम्मार्जनविहीनानि
परुषाण्युपलक्षये ।
असंयत कवाटानि
श्रीविहीनानि सर्वशः ।
बलिकर्मविहीनानि
धूपसम्मोदनेन च ।
अनाशितकुटुम्बानि
प्रभाहीनजनानि च ।
अलक्ष्मीकानि पश्यामि
कुटुम्बिभवनान्यहम् ॥
I see residential buildings unkempt and dirty, with their windows left ajar. None of the usual splendor can be seen anywhere. There are no normal daily rites with the smoke of incense rising. I see families going without food, people lacking luster of any sort and the complete absence of the sense of wellbeing.
2.71.39
अपेतमाल्यशोभानि
ह्यसम्मृष्टाजिराणि च ।
देवागाराणि शून्यानि
न चाभान्ति यथापुरम् ॥
The temples are not adorned with bright garlands. Their courtyards are left unmaintained. They are desolate and lack the usual luster.
2.71.40
देवतार्चाः प्रविद्धाश्च
यज्ञगोष्ठ्यस्तथाविधाः ।
माल्यापणेषु राजन्ते
नाद्य पण्यानि वा तथा ॥
Worship in the temples seems to have been interrupted, as well as the assemblies for Yajñas. Nothing is being offered for sale now, in the shops where flowers used to be sold.
2.71.41
दृश्यन्ते वणिजोऽप्यद्य
न यथापूर्वमत्रवै ।
ध्यानसंविग्नहृदयाः
नष्टव्यापारयन्त्रिताः ॥
The traders too are not their usual selves, their activities suspended for lack of business and their minds deeply disturbed.
2.71.42
देवायतनचैत्येषु
दीनाः पक्षिगणास्तथा ।
मलिनं चाश्रुपूर्णाक्षम्
दीनं ध्यानपरं कृशम् ।
सस्त्रीपुंसं च पश्यामि
जनमुत्कण्ठितं पुरे ॥
The flocks of birds in the temples and on shrines in the squares look woebegone. I see all people in the city, men and women alike, their eyes full of tears, dejected, lost in thoughts, full of anxiety. and their bodies withered.
2.71.44
इत्येवमुक्त्वा भरतः
सूतं तं दीनमानसः ।
तान्यनिष्टान्ययोध्यायाम्
प्रेक्ष्य राजगृहं ययौ ॥
Speaking thus to the charioteer, Bharata, depressed by the sight of those evil portents in Ayōdhyā, proceeded to the king's palace.
2.71.45
तां शून्यशृङ्गाटकवेश्मरथ्यां
रजोऽरुणद्वारकवाटयन्त्राम् ।
दृष्ट्वा पुरीमिन्द्रपुरप्रकाशां
दुःखेन सम्पूर्णतरो बभूव ॥
Seeing the desolate squares, pathways and houses, and bolts on the doors and windows turned tawny with dust, in that city that used to shine like the city of Indra, his cup of misery became full.
2.71.46
बहूनि पश्यन्मनसोऽप्रियाणि
यान्यन्यदा नास्य पुरे बभूवुः ।
अवाक्छिरा दीनमना नहृष्टः
पितुर्महात्मा प्रविवेश वेश्म ॥
Seeing many unpleasant things which he had never seen before in that city, that Mahatma entered his father's house, his head hanging down, feeling distressed and joyless.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकसप्ततितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventy first Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
