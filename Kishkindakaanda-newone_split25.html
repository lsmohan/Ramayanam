Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 25
In this Sarga, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa console Sugreeva, Tārā and Aṅgada. Rāma reflects on the fate that is in no one's control and exhorts everyone to do what should be done. Lakshmaṇa gives specific directions to Sugreeva as to what should be done. Tāra, minister of Sugreeva gets a palanquin, which was splendid like a chariot. Sugreeva and Aṅgada place the body of Vāli on the palanquin.
Everyone, including the Vānara women, go in a procession wailing. The funeral pyre is prepared on a sand-flat surrounded by the water of a stream. Tārā takes the head of her dead husband onto her lap and cries bitterly saying that Yama has come in the form of Rāma and has taken him away making her and all other wives of Vāli, widows.
Aṅgada lights the funeral pyre. Everyone cries. All of them go to the water and give water libations to Vāli. After the funeral rites are done Sugreeva reaches out to Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
4.25.1
स सुग्रीवं च तारां च
साङ्गदं सहलक्ष्मणः ।
समानशोकः काकुत्स्थः
सान्त्वयन्निदमब्रवीत् ॥
Kākutstha, along with Lakshmaṇa, consoled Sugreeva, Tārā and Aṅgada, while grieving as much as they did:
4.25.2
न शोकपरितापेन
श्रेयसा युज्यते मृतः ।
यदत्रानन्तरं कार्यम्
तत्समाधातुमर्हथ ॥
Wailing and anguishing would be of no use to the dead. You should go about what is to be done next.
4.25.3
लोकवृत्तमनुष्ठेयम्
कृतं वो बाष्पमोक्षणम् ।
न कालादुत्तरं किञ्चित्
कर्म शक्यमुपासितुम् ॥
Whatever is customary should be done. You have shed your tears; there is not much that can be done after one’s passing.
4.25.4
नियतिः कारणं लोके
नियतिः कर्मसाधनम् ।
नियतिस्सर्वभूतानाम्
नियोगेष्विह कारणम् ॥
It is the cosmic order that makes everything happen in this world. It is ordained by the cosmic order that things are accomplished. It is the cosmic order that prompts all creatures to do its bidding.
It may be surprising to see Rāma holding the cosmic order, nature of things and time, but not himself responsible for killing Vāli.
This is because Rāma truly holds the view that everyone is ultimately driven by destiny. He says the same even in the case of Kaikēyee; while everyone blames her for the disaster, Rāma holds that it is Providence that is responsible for her to behave the way she behaved (Ṡlōka 2.22.16).
4.25.5
न कर्ता कस्यचित्कश्चित्
नियोगे नापिचेश्वरः ।
स्वभावे वर्तते लोकः
तस्य कालः परायणम् ॥
No one can make anyone do anything; not even Eeswara. The world moves according to its nature which is ultimately decided by Time.
4.25.6
न कालः कालमत्येति
न कालः परिहीयते ।
स्वभावं च समासाद्य
न कश्चिदतिवर्तते ॥
Time is never going to overstep itself. And no one can overstep Time. No one can go against their own nature that is built into them.
4.25.7
न कालस्यास्ति बन्धुत्वम्
न हेतुर्न पराक्रमः ।
न मित्रज्ञातिसम्बन्धः
कारणं नात्मनो वशः ॥
Time has no reason, no relations, no friends, no kith and kin. No one can control it or win over it.
4.25.8
किं तु कालपरीणामो
द्रष्टव्यः साधु पश्यता ।
धर्मश्चार्थश्च कामश्च
कालक्रमसमाहिताः ॥
Yet those who can discern things should be able to see how time evolves to bring Dharma, Artha and Kāma to their culmination.
4.25.9
इतस्स्वां प्रकृतिं वाली
गतःप्राप्तः क्रियाफलम् ।
धर्मार्थकाम संयोगैः
पवित्रं प्लवगेश्वरः ॥
Vāli attained his original state, henceforth. The Lord of Vānaras reaped the irrefutable fruit of the seeds of Dharma, Artha and Kama that he sowed.
4.25.10
स्वधर्मस्य च संयोगात्
जितस्तेन महात्मना ।
स्वर्गः परिगृहीतश्च
प्राणानपरिरक्षता ॥
Vāli, the Mahātma, by sticking to his own nature than to his mortal life won the Heaven and reached it.
4.25.11
एषा वै नियतिश्श्रेष्ठा
यां गतो हरियूथपः ।
तदलं परितापेन
प्राप्तकालमुपास्यताम् ॥
The appointed end, which the leader of the Vānara warriors has attained, is the highest possible; so cease mourning for him, and see to what must be done now.
4.25.12
वचनान्ते तु रामस्य
लक्ष्मणः परवीरहा ।
अवदत्प्रश्रितं वाक्यम्
सुग्रीवं गतचेतसम् ॥
After Rāma ended speaking, Lakshmaṇa, destroyer of enemy warriors said these words, in a humble manner, to the disconcerted Sugreeva.
4.25.13
कुरु त्वमस्य सुग्रीव
प्रेतकार्यमनन्तरम् ।
ताराङ्गदाभ्यां सहितो
वालिनो दहनं प्रति ॥
O Sugreeva! Please prepare, along with Tāra and Aṅgada, for the cremation of Vāli, and perform the final rites!
Tāra, as opposed to Tārā the wife of Vāli, is the male Vānara, a minister of Sugreeva.
4.25.14
समाज्ञापय काष्ठानि
शुष्कानि च बहूनि च ।
चन्दनादीनि दिव्यानि
वालिसंस्कारकारणात् ॥
Give orders for the collection of ample quantities of dry firewood, including the splendid sandal wood, for the cremation of Vāli.
4.25.15
समाश्वासय चैनं त्वम्
अङ्गदं दीनचेतसम् ।
मा भूर्बालिशबुद्धिस्त्वम्
त्वदधीनमिदं पुरम् ॥
Please console the despondent Aṅgada! Do not let the (disturbed) child in you take over, for the city is now in your hands.
4.25.16
अङ्गदस्त्वानयेन्माल्यम्
वस्त्राणि विविधानि च ।
घृतं तैलमथो गन्धान्
यच्चात्र समनन्तरम् ॥
Let Aṅgada collect and bring garlands, flowers, clothes of different kinds, oil, ghee, scents and such other things as would be needed.
4.25.17
त्वं तार शिबिकां शीघ्रम्
आदायागच्छ सम्भ्रमात् ।
त्वरा गुणवती युक्ता
ह्यस्मिन्काले विशेषतः ॥
You, O Tāra, go in all haste and bring a palanquin quick; expediency is of essence, especially in a situation like this.
4.25.18
सज्जीभवन्तु प्लवगाः
शिबिकावाहनोचिताः ।
समर्था बलिनश्चैव
निर्हरिष्यन्ति वालिनम् ॥
Let strong and able Vānaras who can carry the palanquin be ready to take Vāli!
4.25.19
एवमुक्त्वा तु सुग्रीवम्
सुमित्रानन्दवर्धनः ।
तस्थौ भ्रातृसमीपस्थो
लक्ष्मणः परवीरहा ॥
Having thus advised Sugreeva, Lakshmaṇa, augmenter of Sumitrā’s happiness and destroyer of enemy warriors, stood by his brother’s side.
4.25.20
लक्ष्मणस्य वचश्श्रुत्वा
तारस्सम्भ्रान्तमानसः ।
प्रविवेश गुहां शीघ्रम्
शिबिकासक्तमानसः ॥
Hearing those words of Lakshmaṇa, Tāra went in all haste to the cave to fetch the palanquin.
4.25.21
आदाय शिबिकां तारः
स तु पर्यापतत्पुनः ।
वानरैरुह्यमानां ताम्
शूरैरुद्वहनोचितैः ॥
Tāra took that palanquin and returned quickly, with it being carried by mighty strong Vānaras who were skilled at carrying a palanquin.
4.25.22
दिव्यां भद्रासनयुताम्
शिबिकां स्यन्दनोपमाम् ।
पक्षिकर्मभिराचित्राम्
द्रुमकर्मविभूषिताम् ।
आचितां चित्रपत्तीभि
स्सुनिविष्टां समन्ततः ।
विमानमिव सिद्धानाम्
जालवातायनान्विताम् ।
सुनियुक्तां विशालां च
सुकृतां विश्वकर्मणा ।
दारुपर्वतकोपेताम्
चारुकर्मपरिष्कृताम् ।
वराभरणहारैश्च
चित्रमाल्योपशोभिताम् ।
गुहागहनसञ्छन्नाम्
रक्तचन्दनभूषिताम् ।
पुष्पौघैस्समभिच्छन्नाम्
पद्ममालाभिरेव च ।
तरुणादित्यवर्णाभिः
भ्राजमानाभिरावृताम् ॥
It was splendid like a chariot with a comfortable seat, amazing carvings in the likeness of birds and resplendent woodwork of figurines of weight bearers.
It was like an air car that carries Siddhas well constructed by master craftsmen with vent-holes and casements and awnings plastered with red sandal paste.
It had beautiful carvings of trees and mountains. It was winsome with exquisite ornaments, chains and wonderful garlands.
It was dazzling like the morning sun, covered in bunches of flowers and garlands of lotuses.
4.25.27
ईदृशीं शिबिकां दृष्ट्वा
रामो लक्ष्मणमब्रवीत् ।
क्षिप्रं विनीयतां वाली
प्रेतकार्यं विधीयताम् ॥
On seeing that palanquin, Rāma said to Lakshmaṇa, ‘Vāli should be quickly taken and the funeral rites conducted.’
4.25.28
ततो वालिनमुद्यम्य
सुग्रीवश्शिबिकां तदा ।
आरोपयत विक्रोशन्
अङ्गदेन सहैव तु ॥
Then Sugreeva, along with Aṅgada, wailing aloud, raised Vāli and put him in the palanquin.
4.25.29
आरोप्य शिबिकां चैव
वालिनं गतजीवितम् ।
अलङ्कारैश्च विविधैः
माल्यैर्वस्त्रैश्च भूषितम् ।
आज्ञापयत्तदा राजा
सुग्रीव प्लवगेश्वरः ॥
Raising and placing the dead body of Vāli that was richly decorated with garlands and garments on the palanquin, Sugreeva, the king and the Lord of Vānaras, gave orders:
4.25.30
और्ध्वदैहिकमार्यस्य
क्रियतामनुरूपतः ।
विश्राणयन्तो रत्नानि
विविधानि बहून्यपि ।
अग्रतः प्लवगा यान्तु
शिबिका समनन्तरम् ॥
Let the funeral rites be performed for the noble one in a fitting manner. Let the Vānaras go in front distributing ample quantities of gems of various kinds.
4.25.32
राज्ञामृद्दिविशेषा हि
दृश्यन्ते भुवि यादृशाः ।
तादृशैरिह कुर्वन्तु
वानरा भर्तृसत्क्रियाम् ॥
Let the Vānaras perform the rites in a manner that meets the expectations of the world for extraordinarily opulent kings.
4.25.33
तादृशं वालिनः क्षिप्रम्
प्राकुर्वन्नौर्ध्वदैहिकम् ।
अङ्गदं परिरभ्याऽशु
तारप्रभृतयस्तदा ।
क्रोशन्तः प्रययुस्सर्वे
वानरा हतबान्धवाः ॥
The final rites for Vāli were done right away in that manner. Tāra, and all other Vānaras, surrounding Aṅgada, went (in a procession) wailing, having lost a loved relation.
4.25.34
ततः प्रणिहिताः सर्वा
वानर्योस्य वशानुगाः ।
चुक्रुशु र्वीर वीरेति
भूयः क्रोशन्ति ताः प्रियम् ॥
Then the Vānara women, who had submitted themselves to Vāli, followed crying ‘O Veera, O Veera!’ for their beloved, repeatedly.
4.25.35
ताराप्रभृतयस्सर्वा
वानर्यो हरियूथपाः ।
अनुजग्मुर्हि भर्तारम्
क्रोशन्त्यः करुणस्वनाः ॥
Tārā and all the other Vānara women, who had lost their loved relation, followed their husband wailing pitifully.
4.25.36
तासां रुदितशब्देन
वानरीणां वनान्तरे ।
वनानि गिरयश्चैव
विक्रोशन्तीव सर्वतः ॥
With the sound of the wailing of the Vānara women in the Vana (echoing), it felt as if the Vanas and the mountains in every direction were wailing.
4.25.37
पुलिने गिरिनद्यास्तु
विविक्ते जलसंवृते ।
चितां चक्रुस्सुबहवो
वानराश्शोककर्शिताः ॥
In a secluded spot surrounded by water, on the sand-flat of a mountain stream, many Vānaras, grief-stricken, built the funeral pyre.
4.25.38
अवरोप्य ततस्स्कन्धात्
शिबिकां वाहनोचिताः ।
तस्थुरेकान्तमाश्रित्य
सर्वे शोकसमन्विताः ॥
The pall-bearers took the palanquin off their shoulders and laid it down. They stood in a huddle at a distance, all of them wrapped in gloom.
4.25.39
ततस्तारा पतिं दृष्ट्वा
शिबिकातलशायिनम् ।
आरोप्याङ्के शिरस्तस्य
विललाप सुदुःखिता ॥
Tārā looked at her husband lying on the floor of the palanquin, and took his head on her lap and cried bitterly:
4.25.40
हा वानर महाराज
हा नाथ मम वत्सल ।
हा महार्ह महाबाहो
हा मम प्रिय पश्य माम् ।
जनं न पश्यसीमं त्वं
कस्माच्छोकाभिपीडितम् ॥
Alas! O great King of the Vānaras! O my lord who loved me dearly! O supremely worthy one of mighty arm! O my beloved, see my plight! Don’t you see these people of yours here who are distraught with grief for you?
4.25.42
प्रहृष्टमिव ते वक्त्रं
गतासोरपि मानद ।
अस्तार्कसमवर्णं च
लक्ष्यते जीवतो यथा ॥
There is still a smile on your face even though you have lost your life, O Mānada! You look as if you are still alive shining like the setting sun.
4.25.43
एष त्वां रामरूपेण
कालः कर्षति वानर ।
येन स्म विधवास्सर्वाः
कृता एकेषुणा वने ॥
Yama has come in the form of Rāma to take you away. With a single arrow of his, shot during the fight, all of us have become widows.
4.25.44
इमास्तास्तव राजेन्द्र
वानर्योऽप्लवगास्तव ।
पादैर्विकृष्ट मध्वानम्
आगताः किं न बुध्यसे ।
तवेष्टा ननु चैवेमा
भार्याश्चन्द्रनिभाननाः ॥
O lord of kings! All these Vānara women have all walked, all the way here, without hopping as usual, don’t you see? Are these wives of yours, of moon-bright faces not dear to you at all?
4.25.45
इदानीं नेक्षसे कस्मात् 
सुग्रीवं प्लवगेश्वर ।
एते हि सचिवा राजन्
तारप्रभृतयस्तव ।
पुरवासी जनश्चायम्
परिवार्यासतेऽनघ ॥
O Lord of Vānaras! Why are you not looking at Sugreeva; O King, and at these ministers like Tāra and others; O sinless one, and at these people, these city dwellers, sitting around (you)?
4.25.47
विसर्जयैतान् प्लवगान्
यथापुरमरिन्दम ।
ततः क्रीडामहे सर्वा
वनेषु मदनोत्कटाः ॥
O subduer of foe! Why do you not dismiss all these Vānaras like you used to do, so that we can all go and play in the Vanas, under the mounting tide of passion!
4.25.48
एवं विलपतीं ताराम्
पतिशोकपरिप्लुताम् ।
उत्थापयन्ति स्म तदा
वानर्यश्शोककर्शिताः ॥
Then the grief stricken Vānara women raised Tārā up, who was thus crying, inundated by the grief for her husband.
4.25.49
सुग्रीवेण ततस्सार्धम्
अङ्गदः पितरं रुदन् ।
चितामारोपयामास
शोकेनाभिप्लुतेन्द्रियः ॥
Thereupon Aṅgada, along with Sugreeva, with his senses overtaken by grief, lifted his father on to the pyre, crying.
4.25.50
ततोऽग्निं विधिवद्दत्त्वा
सोऽपसव्यं चकार ह ।
पितरं दीर्घमध्वानम्
प्रस्थितं व्याकुलेन्द्रियः ॥
Then setting fire to it as the rules ordained, he, his senses in a whirl, circumambulated his father who had set out on the longest journey, in the anti-clock-wise direction.
4.25.51
संस्कृत्य वालिनं ते तु
विधिपूर्वं प्लवङ्गमाः ।
आजग्मुरुदकं कर्तुम्
नदीं शीतजलां शिवाम् ॥
Having thus completed the final rites for Vāli in a customary manner, the Vānaras went to a river whose water was cold and pure, to offer water libations.
4.25.52
ततस्ते सहितास्तत्र
अङ्गदं स्थाप्य चाग्रतः ।
सुग्रीवतारासहिताः
सिषिचुर्वानरा जलम् ॥
With Aṅgada leading them, the Vānaras, including Sugreeva and Tārā, gave water libations.
4.25.53
सुग्रीवेणेव दीनेन
दीनो भूत्वा महाबलः ।
समानशोकः काकुत्स्थः
प्रेतकार्याण्यकारयत् ॥
The mighty strong Kākutstha, sharing Sugreeva’s sorrow, and feeling miserable in sympathy with his misery, had the funeral rites thus done for Vāli.
4.25.54
ततस्तु तं वालिनमग्र्यपौरुषं
प्रकाशमिक्ष्वाकुवरेषुणा हतम् ।
प्रदीप्य दीप्ताग्निसमौजसं तदा
सलक्ष्मणं राममुपेयिवान्हरिः ॥
And having cremated Vāli who was powerful like a blazing fire, known for his consummate pride and valor, killed by a the arrow of the best among Ikshwākus, the Vānara (Sugreeva) reached out to Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे पञ्चविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty fifth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
