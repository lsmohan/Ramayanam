Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 13
In this Sarga, the pleadings of Daṡaratha continue. He curses Kaikēyee for her cruelty to Rāma. He bemoans his own misfortune for having to be in this predicament. He tries to invoke the good side of Kaikēyee by praising her. But all goes in vain, as Kaikēyee demands him to fulfill the boons.
The night passes as he bemoans, laments, curses and pleads. He does not feel like waking up in the morning.
2.13.1
अतदर्हं महाराजम्
शयानमतथोचितम् ।
ययातिमिव पुण्यान्ते
देवलोकात्परिच्युतम् ।
अनर्थरूपा सिद्धार्था
ह्यभीता भयदर्शिनी ।
पुनराकारयामास
तमेव वरमङ्गना ॥
Kaikēyee was bent upon achieving her ends. She continued to press upon the king for granting of the same boons. She was looking like the embodiment of ill-fortune. She was not at all scared, rather, was looking scary. The King, who did not deserve to be in such a predicament, was lying on the floor, which is woefully unfit for his highness. He looked like king Yayāti who fell from the Heaven after exhausting the balance of Puṇya.
2.13.3
त्वं कत्थसे महाराज
सत्यवादी दृढव्रतः ।
मम चेमं वरं कस्मात्
विधारयितुमिच्छसि ॥
O! Mighty king, you boast of being the adherent of truth and of being steadfast in your vows! How is it that you now want to renege on granting this boon?
2.13.4
एवमुक्तस्तु कैकेय्या
राजा दशरथस्तदा ।
प्रत्युवाच ततः क्रुद्धो
मुहूर्तं विह्वलन्निव ॥
Having been challenged by Kaikēyee in that manner, king Daṡaratha lost himself for a moment and then spoke angrily:
2.13.5
मृते मयि गते रामे
वनं मनुजपुङ्गवे ।
हन्तानार्ये ममामित्रे
सकामा सुखिनी भव ॥
Wretchedness! may you be happy, O heinous woman, O my foe! With me dead and Rāma, the best among men, gone to the Vana!
2.13.6
स्वर्गेऽपि खलु रामस्य
कुशलं दैवतैरहम् ।
प्रत्यादेशादभिहितम्
धारयिष्ये कथं बत ॥
When I go to Heaven, even if I try to pretend that all is well with Rāma, the Dēvas will not believe it. What a pity!
2.13.7
कैकेय्याः प्रियकामेन
रामः प्रव्राजितो मया ।
यदि सत्यं ब्रवीम्येतत्
तदसत्यं भविष्यति ॥
Nor will it be believed if I tell the truth that Rāma was banished by me for the sake of pleasing Kaikēyee!
2.13.8
अपुत्रेण मया पुत्रः
श्रमेण महता महान् ।
रामो लब्धो महाबाहु
स्सकथं त्यज्यते मया ॥
I had no children. Then, after arduous efforts, I was blessed with the great Rāma of mighty arm as a son! How can I just let him go?
2.13.9
शूरश्च कृतविद्यश्च
जितक्रोधो क्षमापरः ।
कथं कमलपत्राक्षो
मया रामो विवास्यते ॥
He is brave, skilled with many Vidyas, has forbearance and is in complete control of his temper! How can I banish that lotus-eyed Rāma!
2.13.10
कथमिन्दीवरश्यामम्
दीर्घबाहुं महाबलम् ।
अभिराममहं रामम्
प्रेषयिष्यामि दण्डकान् ॥
How can I send the charming Rāma, of long arms and mighty strength and of sheen like that of dark lily, to Daṇḍaka Vana?
2.13.11
सुखानामुचितस्यैव
दुःखैरनुचितस्य च ।
दुःखं नामानुपश्येयम्
कथं रामस्य धीमतः ॥
Rāma is worthy of being happy, not miserable. How can I see Rāma of fine faculties undergo suffering?
2.13.12
यदि दुःखमकृत्वाऽद्य
मम संक्रमणं भवेत् ।
अदुःखार्हस्य रामस्य
तत स्सुखमवाप्नुयाम् ॥
I would rather be happy if I die now, without having to inflict grief upon Rāma, who by no means deserve to suffer.
2.13.13
नृशंसे पापसङ्कल्पे
रामं सत्यपराक्रमम् ।
किं विप्रियेण कैकेयि
प्रियं योजयसे मम ।
अकीर्तिरतुला लोके
ध्रुवः परिभवश्च मे ॥
O cruel one! O woman of wicked resolve! O Kaikēyee! Why are you set against my dear Rāma, a man of fair prowess? Infamy and disgrace in this world are certainly in store for me!
2.13.14
तथा विलपतस्तस्य
परिभ्रमितचेतसः ।
अस्तमभ्यगमत्सूर्यो
रजनी चाभ्यवर्तत ॥
As he was crying like this with dazed mind, the sun had set into the mountains and the night had descended.
This was the night before coronation. At exactly the same hour, the scene in Rāma's palace was very different. He was initiated to the fast by Vasishṭha. He prayed to Lord Vishnu and was about to take rest on the mat of Darbha grass along with Seetā.
2.13.15
सा त्रियामा तथार्त्तस्य
चन्द्रमण्डलमण्डिता ।
राज्ञो विलपमानस्य
न व्यभासत शर्वरी ॥
The course of night, lit with the brilliant moon, did not feel bright to that despondent and crying King.
2.13.16
तथैवोष्णं विनिश्वस्य
वृद्धो दशरथो नृपः ।
विललापार्तवद्युखम्
गगनासक्तलोचनः ॥
That aged king Daṡaratha, heaving hot sighs cried miserably like a sick man, staring into the night sky!
How easy it would have been for the king to dismiss Kaikēyee as a mad woman gripped by baseless fears, and deny the preposterous boons!
But that is not the standard the kings of Ikshwāku dynasty held themselves up to! That is what makes Rāmāyaṇa the unique story it is, worth remembering even after many millennia!
2.13.17
न प्रभातं त्वयेच्छामि
निशे नक्षत्रभूषणे ।
क्रियतां मे दया भद्रे
मयाऽयं रचितोऽञ्जलिः ॥
O night, adorned with the many shining stars! I hope not you give way to dawn! Please show mercy on me, O auspicious one! I bring together my hands in supplication to you!
2.13.18
अथवा गम्यतां शीघ्रम्
नाहमिच्छामि निर्घृणाम् ।
नृशंसां कैकयीं द्रष्टुम्
यत्कृते व्यसनं महत् ॥
Or may be you should pass quickly! For, I would not like to continue to see the face of this cruel and heartless Kaikēyee, because of whom a great peril has befallen!
2.13.19
एवमुक्त्वा ततो राजा
कैकेयीं संयताञ्जलिः ।
प्रसादयामास पुनः
कैकेयीं चेदमब्रवीत् ॥
Saying thus, the king urged Kaikēyee again for mercy with his palms joined in supplication. He spoke to Kaikēyee again:
2.13.20
साधु वृत्तस्य दीनस्य
त्वद्गतस्य गतायुषः ।
प्रसादः क्रियतां देवि
भद्रे राज्ञो विशेषतः ॥
O Queen! O auspicious one! May you be kind to this despondent who is at your mercy! It is none other than the King, who is at the fag end of life, having had a smooth and good life thus far, that is beseeching you!
2.13.21
शून्ये न खलु सुश्रोणि
मयेदं समुदाहृतम् ।
कुरु साधु प्रसादं मे
बाले सहृदया ह्यसि ॥
O exquisite-hipped lady! Hope I am not speaking into vacuum! O youthful one! May you show the best of your kindness! You are indeed good at your heart!
2.13.22
प्रसीद देवि रामो मे
त्वद्दत्तं राज्यमव्ययम् ।
लभतामसितापाङ्गे
यशः परमवाप्नु हि ॥
O queen! Show mercy! O beauty of dark eyelashes! May you have the glory of giving this eternal kingdom to Rāma!
2.13.23
मम रामस्य लोकस्य
गुरूणां भरतस्य च ।
प्रियमेतद्गुरुश्रोणि
कुरु चारुमुखेक्षणे ॥
O lady of ample hips and charming eyes and beautiful face! May you do this to please everyone including me, Rāma, Bharata and Gurus!
2.13.24
विशुद्धभावस्य सुदुष्टभावा
ताम्रेक्षणस्याश्रुकलस्य राज्ञः ।
श्रुत्वा विचित्रं करुणं विलापं
भर्तुर्नृशंसा न चकार वाक्यम् ॥
That cruel lady heard the King who was crying and pleading in many ways with reddened eyes filled with tears; but that ill-natured woman would not heed the words of the pure-natured man.
2.13.25
ततस्स राजा पुनरेव मूर्छितः
प्रियामतुष्टां प्रतिकूलभाषिणीम् ।
समीक्ष्य पुत्रस्य विवासनं प्रति
क्षितौ विसंज्ञो निपपात दुःखितः ॥
Seeing his dear wife not pleased, and hearing her speak in nothing but adverse terms regarding not sending his son into exile, the King lost his consciousness and fell in a fit on the floor again.
2.13.26
इतीव राज्ञो व्यथितस्य सा निशा
जगाम घोरं श्वसतो मनस्विनः ।
विबोध्यमानः प्रतिबोधनं तदा
निवारयामास स राजसत्तमः ॥
The night passed while the sensible king was thus lamenting and sighing terribly. That eminent king dissuaded (his staff) from waking him up in the morning.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे त्रयोदशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirteenth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
