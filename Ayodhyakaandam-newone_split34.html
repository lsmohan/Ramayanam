Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 34
In this Sarga,  Rāma seeks permission from his father for his departure to Vana along with Seetā and Lakshmaṇa.
Daṡaratha, finding no way out of the predicament, tells Rāma that Rāma should rather take the kingdom from him by force, by placing him under restraint. But Rāma conveys his resolve to go to the Vana. Then, prodded by Kaikēyee once again, the king gives permission to Rāma and wishes him all well in Vana.
And then, he finally asks Rāma to stay at least for the night and leave the morning after. Rāma says that it is not worth enjoying one more night of luxuries as they would not be there from the next day onwards, anyway.
In the presence of every one, he then asserts that he is abjuring of all his claims to the kingdom or any part thereof, and all of it can be conferred on Bharata.
2.34.1
ततः कमलपत्राक्षः
श्यामो निरुपमो महान् ।
उवाच राम स्तं सूतम्
पितुराख्याहि मामिति ॥
Then the magnanimous and peerless Rāma, of lovely dark complexion and eyes beautiful like lotus petals, told the charioteer, 'Tell my father that I have come.'
2.34.2
स रामप्रेषितः क्षिप्रम्
सन्तापकलुषेन्द्रियः ।
प्रविश्य नृपतिं सूतो
निश्वसन्तं ददर्श ह ॥
Thus prompted by Rāma, the charioteer, with his senses faltering under grief, went swiftly and saw the sighing King.
2.34.3
उपरक्तमिवादित्यम्
भस्मच्छन्नमिवानलम् ।
तटाकमिव निस्तोयम्
अपश्यज्जगतीपतिम् ॥
The lords of the world, at that time, looked like the sun that was eclipsed, like bright fire covered by ash, and like a lake bereft of water.
2.34.4
आलोक्य तु महाप्राज्ञः
परमाकुलचेतसम् ।
राममेवानुशोचन्तम्
सूतः प्राञ्जलिरासदत् ॥
Then the wise charioteer, with palms joined, approached the king whose mind was highly disturbed and who was completely preoccupied with thoughts of Rāma.
2.34.5
तं वर्धयित्वा राजानम्
सूतः पूर्वं जयाशिषा ।
भयविक्लबया वाचा
मन्दया श्लक्ष्णमब्रवीत् ॥
After greeting the king invoking victory upon him, the charioteer heSeetāntly and apprehensively spoke in a soft and gentle voice:
2.34.6
अयं स पुरुषव्याघ्रो
द्वारि तिष्ठति ते सुतः ।
ब्राह्मणेभ्यो धनं दत्वा
सर्वञ्चैवोपजीविनाम् ॥
your son, the tiger among men, having distributed his wealth to Brāhmaṇas and servants, is waiting at the entrance.
2.34.7
स त्वा पश्यतु भद्रं ते
रामस्सत्यपराक्रमः ।
सर्वान् सुहृद आपृच्छ्य
त्वामिदानीं दिदृक्षते ॥
May all bode well for you! May Rāma, of true valor, have your audience now. He has consulted all his well wishers and would like to see you.
2.34.8
गमिष्यति महारण्यम्
तं पश्य जगतीपते ।
वृतं राजगुणै स्सर्वैः
आदित्यमिव रश्मिभिः ॥
He is set to leave for the great forest. O lord of the worlds! May you behold him, from whom princely virtues emanate like rays from the sun!
2.34.9
स सत्यवादी धर्मात्मा
गाम्भीर्यात्सागरोपमः ।
आकाश इव निष्पङ्को
नरेन्द्रः प्रत्युवाच तम् ॥
That lord of peoples, the Dharmātma who swore by truth, who was deep like an ocean and unsoiled like the sky, responded to him saying:
2.34.10
सुमंन्त्रानय मे दारान्
ये केचिदिह मामकाः ।
दारैः परिवृतस्सर्वैः
द्रष्टुमिच्छामि धार्मिकम् ॥
O Sumantra! Bring every single one of my wives here. I would like to see the Dhārmika (Rāma) in the company of all my wives.
2.34.11
सोऽन्तःपुरमतीत्यैव
स्त्रियस्ता वाक्यमब्रवीत् ।
आर्याह्वयति वो राजा
गम्यतां तत्र मा चिरम् ॥
And he quickly went to the women's apartments, and said to the ladies, 'O noble ladies, the King calls you. May you present yourself there without delay.'
2.34.12
एवमुक्ताः स्त्रिय स्सर्वाः
सुमन्त्रेण नृपाज्ञया ।
प्रचक्रमु स्तद्भवनम्
भर्तुराज्ञाय शासनम् ॥
Hearing the order of the king thus conveyed by Sumantra, all of those ladies went to the king's palace in accordance with their husband's summons.
2.34.13
अर्धसप्तशतास्तास्तु
प्रमदास्ताम्रलोचनाः ।
कौसल्यां परिवार्याथ
शनैर्जग्मुर्धृतव्रताः ॥
Three hundred and fifty of those copper-eyed devout women went slowly, following Kousalyā.
2.34.14
आगतेषु च दारेषु
समवेक्ष्य महीपतिः ।
उवाच राजा तं सूतम्
सुमन्त्राऽनय मे सुतम् ॥
Seeing the arrival of his wives, the king, the ruler of the land, then told the charioteer Sumantra to bring Rāma.
2.34.15
स सूतो राममादाय
लक्ष्मणं मैथिलीं तदा ।
जगामाभिमुखस्तूर्णम्
सकाशं जगतीपतेः ॥
The charioteer immediately brought Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Seetā to the presence of the ruler of the world.
2.34.16
स राजा पुत्रमायान्तम्
दृष्ट्वा दूरात्कृताञ्जलिम् ।
उत्पपातासनात्तूर्णम्
आर्त स्त्रीजनसंवृतः ॥
The king, surrounded by the women, rose from his seat with grief when he saw his son from a distance, approaching him with joined palms.
2.34.17
सोऽभिदुद्राव वेगेन
रामं दृष्ट्वा विशाम्पतिः ।
तमसंप्राप्य दुःखार्तः
पपात भुवि मूर्छितः ॥
Seeing Rāma, the ruler of the people sprang toward him eagerly, only to fall down in a faint, overwhelmed with grief.
2.34.18
तं रामोऽभ्यपतत् क्षिप्रम्
लक्ष्मणश्च महारथः ।
विसंज्ञमिव दुःखेन
सशोकं नृपतिं तदा ॥
Rāma and the mighty warrior Lakshmaṇa dashed immediately to the king who lay senseless, crying in grief!
2.34.19
स्त्रीसहस्रनिनादश्च
संजज्ञे राजवेश्मनि ।
हा हा रामेति सहसा
भूषणध्वनिमूर्छितः ॥
Thereupon rose a great din, a thousand women crying, ' Alas, Rāma, alas!' mingled with the noise of their jingling ornaments.
2.34.20
तं परिष्वज्य बाहुभ्याम्
तावुभौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
पर्यंङ्के सीतया सार्धम्
रुदन्तः समवेशयन् ॥
Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, crying, gathered him in their arms and placed him on the couch, assisted by Seetā.
2.34.21
अथ रामो मुहूर्तेन
लब्धसंज्ञं महीपतिम् ।
उवाच प्राञ्जलिर्भूत्वा
शोकार्णवपरिप्लुतम् ॥
Then Rāma, with joined palms, said to the king who regained his senses after a while and was drowned in an ocean of grief:
2.34.22
आपृच्छे त्वां महाराज
सर्वेषामीश्वरोऽसि नः ।
प्रस्थितं दण्डकारण्यम्
पश्य त्वं कुशलेन माम् ॥
O great king! You are the lord of us all! I seek your permission! May your graceful glances shower on me as I head to Daṇḍaka Vana !
2.34.23
लक्ष्मणं चानुजानीहि
सीता चान्वेति मां वनम् ।
कारणैर्बहुभि स्तथ्यैः
वार्यमाणौ न चेच्छतः ॥
Please give permission to Lakshmaṇa also. Seetā is also following me to Vana. For many a cogent reason, they could not be dissuaded.
2.34.24
अनुजानीहि सर्वान्नः
शोकमुत्सृज्य मानद ।
लक्ष्मणं मां च सीतां च
प्रजापतिरिव प्रजाः ॥
Grant your permission to Lakshmaṇa, Seetā and all of us, like Brahma to all the people. May you overcome your grief, O upholder of the self respect of all!
2.34.25
प्रतीक्षमाणमव्यग्रम्
अनुज्ञां जगतीपतेः ।
उवाच राजा सम्प्रेक्ष्य
वनवासाय राघवम् ॥
Seeing that Rāghava, who thus sought permission from the lord of lands in all honesty regarding his departure to Vana, the king said:
2.34.26
अहं राघव कैकेय्या
वरदानेन मोहितः ।
अयोध्यायास्त्वमेवाद्य
भव राजा निगृह्य माम् ॥
I have been taken advantage of by Kaikēyee, O Rāghava, through the boon I granted her. Place me under restraint and make yourself the King of Ayōdhyā right away!
2.34.27
एवमुक्तो नृपतिना
रामो धर्मभृतां वरः ।
प्रत्युवाचाञ्जलिं कृत्वा
पितरं वाक्यकोविदः ॥
Told thus by the king, the eloquent Rāma, the foremost among the keepers of Dharma, responded to his father, with joined palms:
2.34.28
भवान्वर्ष सहस्राय
पृथिव्या नृपते पतिः ।
अहं त्वरण्येवत्स्यामि
न मे कार्यं त्वयाऽनृतम् ॥
May you be the ruler of the lands, for thousands of years, O lord of the peoples! As for me, forest is the place to dwell. May you not dishonor your word for my sake!
2.34.29
नव पञ्च च वर्षाणि
वनवासे विहृत्य ते ।
पुनःपादौ ग्रहीष्यामि
प्रतिज्ञान्ते नराधिप ॥
I shall spend nine and five years dwelling in the Vana, and then, after fulfilling the pledge, O lord of the people, I shall return to lie at your feet.
2.34.30
रुदन्नार्तः प्रियं पुत्रम्
सत्यपाशेन संयतः ।
कैकेय्या चोद्यमानस्तु
मिथो राजा तमब्रवीत् ॥
Crying pitifully, bound by honor to his word, and prodded by Kaikēyee in covert gestures, the king said to his beloved son:
2.34.31
श्रेयसे वृद्धये तात
पुनरागमनाय च ।
गच्छस्वारिष्टमव्यग्रः
पन्थानमकुतोभयम् ॥
Go, my child! May your path be free of all dangers, troubles and threats! May you return to enjoy prosperity and abundance!
2.34.32
न हि सत्यात्मनस्तात
धर्माभिमनस स्तव ।
विनिवर्तयितुं बुद्धिः
शक्यते रघुनन्दन ॥
My dear child! You hold truth in your heart and your mind is focused on Dharma! And your decision, O delight of Raghu dynasty, seems next to impossible to change!
2.34.33
अद्य त्विदानीं रजनीम्
पुत्र मा गच्छ सर्वथा ।
एकाहदर्शनेनापि
साधु तावच्चराम्यहम् ॥
But by no means, shall you leave right away, at this hour of the night! I shall feel better at least by that much, by seeing you here for one more day!
2.34.34
मातरं मां च सम्पश्यन्
वसेमामद्य शर्वरीम् ।
तर्पित स्सर्वकामैस्त्वम्
श्वः काले साधयिष्यसि ॥
Stay here tonight with me and your mother, and let me entertain you with all that you like. You can leave in the morning.
2.34.35
दुष्करं क्रियते पुत्र
सर्वथा राघव त्वया ।
मत्प्रियार्थं प्रियांस्त्यक्त्वा
यद्यासि विजनं वनम् ॥
My son! O Rāghava! What you are doing is by no means easy; going to the uninhabited Vana, leaving everyone you love, just to please me!
2.34.36
न चैतन्मे प्रियं पुत्र
शपे सत्येन राघव ।
छन्नया छलितस्त्वस्मि
स्त्रिया छन्नाग्निकल्पया ॥
My son! None of this is to my liking at all! O Rāghava! I swear by all that is true! I was hoodwinked by the secret agenda of a woman, who was like a concealed fire.
2.34.37
वञ्चना या तु लब्धा मे
तां त्वं निस्तर्तुमिच्छसि ।
अनया वृत्तसादिन्या
कैकेय्याऽभिप्रचोदितः ॥
And, you want to save me from this disgrace, urged by Kaikēyee who is simply exploiting the good nature of people!
2.34.38
न चैतदाश्चर्यतमम्
यत्तज्येष्ठस्सुतो मम ।
अपानृतकथं पुत्र
पितरं कर्तुमिच्छसि ॥
My son! It is no big surprise that you, the eldest of my sons, like to make your father's word not become untrue!
2.34.39
अथ रामस्तथा श्रुत्वा
पितुरार्तस्य भाषितम् ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
दीनो वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Listening to such grief stricken words of his father, Rāma, along with brother Lakshmaṇa, spoke these heart-rending words:
2.34.40
प्राप्स्यामि यानद्य गुणान्
को मे श्वस्तान् प्रदास्यति ।
अपक्रमणमेवातः
सर्वकामैरहं वृणे ॥
I may get everything that I want for one more day. But who is going to give them to me from tomorrow onwards? Hence I would seek, of all, only my departure.
2.34.41
इयं सराष्ट्रा सजना
धनधान्यसमाकुला ।
मया विसृष्टा वसुधा
भरताय प्रदीयताम् ॥
Let this land that I completely relinquish with all its provinces and peoples, replete with wealth and grains, be bestowed on Bharata.
2.34.42
वनवासकृता बुद्धिः
न च मेऽद्य चलिष्यति ।
यस्तुष्टेन वरो दत्तः
कैकेय्यै वरद त्वया ।
दीयतां निखिलेनैव
सत्यस्त्वं भव पार्थिव ॥
My decision to go to the Vana cannot be shaken now. O giver of largesse! May the boon that was given to Kaikēyee out of gratification be fulfilled in full! O King, may you be counted on for keeping your word!
Recall that Daṡaratha gave the boons to Kaikēyee in full gratitude when she saved his life in the war fought by Dēvas and Asuras.
2.34.43
अहं निदेशं भवतो
यथोक्तमनुपालयन् ।
चतुर्दश समा वत्स्ये
वने वनचरैस्सह ॥
I shall follow your order to the hilt and live for fourteen years in the Vana in the company of the other dwellers of the Vana.
2.34.44
मा विमर्शो वसुमती
भरताय प्रदीयताम् ।
न हि मे काङ्क्षितं राज्यम्
सुखमात्मनि वा प्रियम् ।
यथानिदेशं कर्तुं वै
तवैव रघुनन्दन ॥
No further deliberations are needed. Let the country be given to Bharata. I wish to enjoy neither the kingdom nor the comforts. O delight of Raghu dynasty, I would just like to comply with your word.
2.34.46
अपगच्छतु ते दुःखम्
माभूर्बाष्पपरिप्लुतः ।
न हि क्षुभ्यति दुर्धर्षः
समुद्रः सरितां पतिः ॥
May you be relieved of this grief! May you not shed tears! The lord of rivers, the inviolable ocean, never allows himself to be overborne.
2.34.47
नैवाहं राज्यमिच्छामि
न सुखं न च मेदिनीम् ।
नैव सर्वानिमान् कामान्
न स्वर्गं नैव जीवितम् ॥
I seek neither the kingdom, nor comforts, nor the princess of Mithilā. Nor any of these luxuries, nor heaven, nor life.
2.34.48
त्वामहं सत्यमिच्छामि
नानृतं पुरुषर्षभ ।
प्रत्यक्षं तव सत्येन
सुकृतेन च ते शपे ॥
Rather, O Bull among men, I would like to see you as a man of your word, not otherwise. I swear in your presence, on everything that is true and every good deed of life.
2.34.49
न च शक्यं मया तात
स्थातुं क्षणमपि प्रभो ।
स शोकं धारय स्वेमम्
न हि मेऽस्ति विपर्ययः ॥
I cannot stay here a moment longer, father; do not make yourself miserable on this account. There is no going back for me.
2.34.50
अर्थितो ह्यस्मि कैकेय्या
वनं गच्छेति राघव ।
मया चोक्तं व्रजामीति
तत्सत्यमनुपालये ॥
O Rāghava! I have been asked by Kaikēyee to go to the Vana, and I have said I would, and I shall keep my word.
2.34.51
मा चोत्कण्ठां कृथा देव
वने रंस्यामहे वयम् ।
प्रशान्तहरिणाकीर्णे
नानाशकुनिनादिते ॥
You must not entertain any anxiety on my account, my lord! We shall enjoy in the Vana where gentle deer abound and the air is filled with the songs of birds.
In Sarga 28, Rāma says to Seetā that Vana is a terrible place to live. But here, he says, it is a wonderful place to live. Why the contradiction?
This is the contradiction of life itself. It could look terrible or delightful, based on your vantage point.
When everything goes well, the world looks beautiful. When things go wrong the world looks terrible.
When one is concerned about the delicate Seetā, Vana looks like a dreaded place. When one is resolute to go and live there, it rather appears to be a beautiful place.
2.34.52
पिता हि दैवतं तात
देवतानामपि स्मृतम् ।
तस्माद्दैवतमित्येव
करिष्यामि पितुर्वचः ॥
Scriptures say that even Dēvas regard father as a deity. Hence I shall do my father's will, looking upon it as the command of my deity.
2.34.53
चतुर्दशसु वर्षेषु
गतेषु नरसत्तम ।
पुनर्द्रक्ष्यसि मां प्राप्तम्
सन्तापोऽयं विमुच्यताम् ॥
O best among men! After four and ten years, you will certainly see me return. Hence, please be relieved of this worry!
2.34.54
येन संस्तम्भनीयोऽयम्
सर्वो बाष्पगलो जनः ।
स त्वं पुरुषशार्दूल
किमर्थं विक्रियां गतः ॥
O tiger among men! You should be the one to console all these people whose voices are wet and choking. It does not behoove you to become dejected like this.
2.34.55
पुरं च राष्ट्रं च मही च केवला
मया निसृष्टा भरताय दीयताम् ।
अहं निदेशं भवतोऽनुपालयन्
वनं गमिष्यामि चिराय सेवितुम् ॥
May this city, kingdom and this entire earth, abjured by me, be conferred on Bharata. In following your order, I shall leave to the Vana, to dwell for a long time.
This is the first time Rāma is giving a sort of public speech, after coming to know about the two boons by Kaikēyee.
He is asserting himself, in no uncertain terms, that he has decided to leave for the Vana, making no claim whatsoever to anything that is part of the kingdom, thus bringing a final closure to the entire issue.
The oath, in its solemnity, is similar to the oath taken by Bheeshma in the epic Mahābhārata when he abjured his claim to his father's kingdom and any part thereof.
2.34.56
मया निसृष्टां भरतो महीमिमां
सशैलषण्डां सपुरां सकाननाम् ।
शिवां सुसीमामनुशास्तु केवलं
त्वया यदुक्तं नृपते तथास्तु तत् ॥
Fully abjured by me, let this land, with its mountain ranges, cities and forests and well defined boundaries, be ruled by Bharata. May everything be done according to your word!
2.34.57
न मे तथा पार्थिव धीयते मनो
महत्सु कामेषु न चात्मनःप्रिये ।
यथा निदेशे तव शिष्टसम्मते
व्यपैतु दुःखं तव मत्कृतेऽनघ ॥
O faultless one! My mind is not as much set on Comforts, luxuries and on personal indulgences as it is set on obeying your command, which is acceptable to the noblest of men. May you not grieve on account of me.
2.34.58
तदद्य नैवानघ राज्यमव्ययं
न सर्वकामान्न सुखं न मैथिलीम् ।
न जीवितं त्वामनृतेन योजयन्
वृणीय सत्यं व्रतमस्तु ते तथा ॥
Hence, O faultless one, I would not seek anything, be it the imperishable kingdom, or all the desirable luxuries and comforts, or the princess of Mithilā, or life itself, that would lend you to be untruthful. May your commitment to your word prove to be absolutely truthful!
2.34.59
फलानि मूलानि च भक्षयन्वने
गिरींश्च पश्यन् सरितस्सरांसि च ।
वनं प्रविश्यैव विचित्रपादपम्
सुखी भविष्यामि तवास्तु निर्वृतिः ॥
Entering the forest that is full of wonderful trees, I shall live there happily Enjoying the roots and fruits, and the sight of the many mountains, lakes and rivers; so may you set your mind at rest.
2.34.60
एवं स राजा व्यसनाभिपन्नः
शोकेन दुःखेन च ताम्यमानः ।
आलिङ्ग्य पुत्रं सुविनष्टसंज्ञो
मोहं गतो नैव चिचेष्ट किंञ्चित् ॥
The King, overwhelmed by the disaster, with his body and mind tormented by grief, fell into a dead faint and lay motionless as he embraced his son.
2.34.61
देव्यस्तत स्संरुरुदुस्समेता
स्तां वर्जयित्वा नरदेवपत्नीम् ।
रुदन् सुमन्त्रोऽऽपि जगाम मूर्छां
हाहाकृतं तत्र बभूव सर्वम् ॥
And all the wives of the lord of peoples wept aloud, save that one wife alone. Sumantra also cried and fainted. The place was filled with cries everywhere.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे चतुस्त्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty fourth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
