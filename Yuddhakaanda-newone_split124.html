Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 124
In this Sarga, Rāma pays a visit to Bharadwāja on his way back to Ayōdhyā. Bharadwāja tells him that everything is well in Ayōdhyā and his mothers are doing well, and Bharata is eagerly waiting for him.
Bharadwāja also tells Rāma how agonized he was seeing him leave for the Vana abjuring everything, the comforts, the luxuries and the kingdom, and is now happy to see him come back with friends, having destroyed his enemies.
Bharadwāja tells Rāma that he knew, with the power of his Tapa, all that had happened, from the ups and downs in Janasthāna, to the abduction of Seetā, the friendship with Sugreeva, Hanumān finding Seetā, to Rāvaṇa getting killed and the Dēvas giving him boons.
Then Bharadwāja tells him that he too would like to grant him a boon of his asking. Rāma asks that he make all the trees in their path, as they go to Ayōdhyā, yield fruit, even though it is not the season for them. Bharadwāja grants the boon and all the Vānaras are happy eating them.
6.124.1
पूर्णे चतुर्दशे वर्षे
पञ्चम्यां लक्ष्मणाग्रजः ।
भरद्वाजाश्रमं प्राप्य
ववन्दे नियतो मुनिम् ।
सोऽपृच्छदभिवाद्यैनम्
भरद्वाजं तपोधनम् ॥
With the fourteen years completed (in exile), the elder brother of Lakshmaṇa arrived at the Āṡrama of Bharadwāja on the fifth day of the bright fortnight and bowed to that Muni in all deference. After paying his obeisance, he asked Bharadwāja who was rich with his Tapa:
6.124.2
शृणोषि कच्चिद्भगवन्
सुभिक्षानामयं पुरे ।
कच्चित्स युक्तो भरतो
जीवन्त्यपि च मातरः ॥
I hope, O Bhagawan, you are hearing that the city is plentiful and free from disease, and Bharata is engaged in ensuring the same, and the mothers are alive and well!
6.124.3
एवमुक्तस्तु रामेण
भरद्वाजो महामुनिः ।
प्रत्युवाच रघुश्रेष्ठम्
स्मितपूर्वं प्रहृष्टवत् ॥
Thus asked by Rāma, Bharadwāja, the great Muni replied to that best of Raghus, delighted and with a smile:
6.124.4
पङ्कदिग्धस्तु भरतो
जटिलस्त्वां प्रतीक्षते ।
पादुके ते पुरस्कृत्य
सर्वं च कुशलं गृहे ॥
Bharata, with unkempt body and disheveled hair, is waiting for you keeping your sandals in front of him; and yes, all is well at home.
There is nothing better than having a physical object, that serves as a symbol for one's mind, speaking about one's values, what one stands for, what one wants and does not want in life, what one strives for and so on. The sandals of Rāma that he kept in front of him for fourteen years served as one such symbol for Bharata, showing who he was at the core. This is important for one who is thrown against his will into a situation that he never wanted.
In modern times, we see Gandhi finding such a symbol in his hand-spun loincloth and the watch that hung around his waist, conveying everything about him.
6.124.5
त्वां पुरा चीरवसनम्
प्रविशन्तं महावनम् ।
स्त्रीतृतीयं च्युतं राज्यात्
धर्मकामं च केवलम् ।
पदातिं त्यक्तसर्वस्वम्
पितृनिर्देशकारिणम् ।
सर्वभोगैः परित्यक्तम्
स्वर्गच्युतमिवामरम् ।
दृष्ट्वा तु करुणा पूर्वम्
ममासीत् समितिञ्जय ।
कैकेयीवचने युक्तम्
वन्यमूलफलाशिनम् ॥
At that time, when you, as a trio that included even a woman, set out into the formidable forest on foot clad in clothes made of bark, falling from the kingdom like a Dēva falling from Heaven, abjuring everything and completely giving up every kind of luxury and comfort with only the roots and fruits of the forest for your food, to abide by the command of your father and follow the word of Kaikēyee truthfully, with your mind set on nothing but Dharma, I was in agony, O victor of battles!
6.124.8
साम्प्रतं सुसमृद्धार्थम्
समित्रगणबान्धवम् ।
समीक्ष्य विजितारिं त्वाम्
मम प्रीतिरनुत्तमा ॥
But now, seeing you in the company of of abundance of friends and relatives with all your objectives accomplished and your enemies vanquished, I feel pleased like never before.
Every man, no matter how grown up he is, needs a father or a father figure, who feels happy about his accomplishments. The joy of your father over your accomplishments gives a whole different meaning to those accomplishments and boosts the sense of self-worth like nothing else can.
6.124.9
सर्वं च सुखदुःखं ते
विदितं मम राघव ।
यत्त्वया विपुलं प्राप्तम्
जनस्थाननिवासिना ॥
I know about all the ups and downs, that you have gone through, O Rāghava, and what happened to you while you lived at Janasthāna, in detail.
6.124.10
ब्राह्मणार्थे नियुक्तस्य
रक्षतः सर्वतापसान् ।
रावणेन हृता भार्या
बभूवेयमनिन्दिता ॥
While you were engaged in the cause of Brāhmaṇas, and in protecting all the Tapasvis, your blameless wife was abducted by Rāvaṇa.
6.124.11
मारीचदर्शनं चैव
सीतोन्मथनमेव च ।
कबन्धदर्शनं चैव
पम्पाभिगमनं तथा ।
सुग्रीवेण च ते सख्यम्
यथा वाली हतस्त्वया ।
मार्गणं चैव वैदेह्याः
कर्म वातात्मजस्य च ।
विदितायां च वैदेह्याम्
नलसेतुर्यथा कृतः ।
यथा वा दीपिता लङ्का
प्रहृष्टैर्हरियूथपैः ।
सपुत्रबान्धवामात्यः
सबलः सहवाहनः ।
यथा विनिहतः सङ्ख्ये
रावणो बलदर्पितः ।
यथा च निहते तस्मिन्
रावणो देवकण्टके ।
समागमश्च त्रिदशैः
यथा दत्तश्च ते वरः ।
सर्वं ममैतद्विदितम्
तपसा धर्मवत्सल ।
संपतन्ति च मे शिष्याः
प्रवृत्ताख्याः पुरीमितः ॥
O you, who are affectionate to Dharma! With the power of my Tapa I came to know everything, from (Rāvaṇa) going to Māreeca and then carrying off Seetā, to you encountering Kabandha and going to Paṃpā, to making friendship with Sugreeva, to Vāli being killed by you, to the search for Vaidēhee, to the feat done by the son of Vāyu, to his finding Vaidēhee, to the building of the bridge by Nala, to how Laṅkā was set in flames by the exulting Vānara chiefs, to how Rāvaṇa, who was too haughty because of his strength, was destroyed in the battle, along with his sons, relatives, ministers, armies and mounts, to how with Rāvaṇa, a thorn on the side for Dēvas, slain, the Dēvas came down and gave boons to you. Also, anyway, my Ṡishyas who carry all kinds of news, frequent the city from here.
6.124.17
अहमप्यद्य ते दद्मि
वरं शस्त्रभृतां वर ।
अर्ध्यं प्रतिगृहाणेदम्
अयोध्यां श्वो गमिष्यसि ॥
I will also give you a boon, O foremost of bowmen! Please enjoy my hospitality today and go to Ayōdhyā tomorrow.
6.124.18
तस्य तच्छिरसा वाक्यम्
प्रतिगृह्य नृपात्मजः ।
बाढमित्येव संहृष्टो
श्रीमान् वरमयाचत ॥
Accepting those words with bowed head, the prince said in delight, 'Sure, I will!', and then, the blessed one asked the boon:
6.124.19
अकाले फलिनो वृक्षाः
सर्वे चापि मधुस्रवाः ।
फलान्यमृतकल्पानि
बहूनि विविधानि च ।
भवन्तु मार्गे भगवन्
अयोध्यां प्रति गच्छतः ॥
May all the trees yield fruit and ooze honey even though it is not the season. May we find all varieties of nectar-sweet fruits, aplenty, O Bhagawan, on our way back to Ayōdhyā!
6.124.21
तथेति च प्रतिज्ञाते
वचनात् समनन्तरम् ।
अभवन् पादपास्तत्र
स्वर्गपादपसन्निभाः ॥
As soon as he uttered the words, 'May it be so!', the trees over there became like the ones in Heaven.
6.124.22
निष्फलाः फलिनश्चासन्
विपुष्पाः पुष्पशालिनः ।
शुष्काः समग्रपत्रास्ते
नगाश्चैव मधुस्रवाः ।
सर्वतो योजनास्तिस्रो
गच्छतामभवंस्तदा ॥
Throughout the distance of the three Yōjanas of their journey, the trees that had no fruit, bore fruit, that had no flower, were covered in flower and that were dry, were covered with leaves, and all of them oozed honey.
6.124.23
ततः प्रहृष्टाः प्लवगर्षभास्ते
बहूनि दिव्यानि फलानि चैव ।
कामादुपाश्नन्ति सहस्रशस्ते
मुदाऽन्विताः स्वर्गजितो मुदेव ॥
Then the extremely pleased bulls among Vānaras, in their thousands, enjoyed those divinely delicious fruits, as many as they wished, rejoicing as if they had won Heaven itself!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे चतुर्विंशत्युत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and twenty fourth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
