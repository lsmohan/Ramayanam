Baala Kaanda - Sarga 42
This Sarga presents the beginning of one of the most celebrated legends of India, that of the descent of river Gaṅgā to the earth. In this Sarga, it is shown that it took more than three generations of able kings before someone capable of bringing Gaṅgā to the earth was born.
1.42.1
कालधर्मं गते राम
सगरे प्रकृतीजनाः ।
राजानं रोचयामासुः
अंशुमन्तं सुधार्मिकम् ॥
O Rāma! After Sagara passed away, all the officers and people of that kingdom chose Aṃṡumān, the Dhārmika, as their king.
1.42.2
स राजा सुमहानसीत्
अंशुमान् रघुनन्दन ।
तस्य पुत्रो महानासीत्
दिलीप इति विश्रुतः ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Aṃṡumān became an illustrious ruler. He had a son by name Dileepa, a great soul respected by all.
1.42.3
तस्मिन् राज्यं समावेश्य
दिलीपे रघुनन्दन ।
हिमवच्छिखरे पुण्ये
तपस्तेपे सुदारुणम् ॥
He established Dileepa in charge of the kingdom and went to the auspicious Himalayas and performed arduous Tapa.
The flexibility of the people in those days amazes me.
On the one hand they would be ruling a kingdom, bearing the brunt of all responsibilities and enjoying the glory. And on the other hand, they just give it all up and go inwards to do Tapa for years.
Their idea of life was certainly not that of seeking opportunities or making wins or proving themselves, as it is now.
Peace is inside and excitement is outside. People of those times were constantly in touch with the peace inside, and not consumed by the excitement outside.
I wish we were still holding on to that higher and bigger picture of life, instead of measuring ourselves with bank balances and level in the power echelons.
1.42.4
द्वात्रिंशच्च सहस्राणि
वर्षाणि सुमहायशाः ।
तपोवनं गतो राम
स्वर्गं लेभे तपोधनः ॥
That great Aṃṡumān was in the Tapōvana for thirty two thousand years and with that accrued Tapa he ascended to Heaven.
1.42.5
दिलीपस्तु महातेजाः
श्रुत्वा पैतामहं वधम् ।
दुःखोपहतया बुद्ध्या
निश्चयं नाध्यगच्छत ॥
The supremely radiant Dileepa also felt very sad about the fate of his grand uncles. But he could not think of anything that he could do.
1.42.6
कथं गङ्गावतरणम्
कथं तेषां जलक्रिया ।
तारयेयं कथं चैनान्
इति चिन्तापरोऽभवत् ॥
He kept thinking: How am I going to bring the Gaṅgā? How am I going to offer the water oblations? How can I help them go to Heaven?
1.42.7
तस्य चिन्तयतो नित्यम्
धर्मेण विदितात्मनः ।
पुत्रो भगीरथो नाम
जज्ञे परमधार्मिकः ॥
As he was constantly, dutifully, thinking of it, a son by name Bhageeratha was born to him, who also was a great Dhārmika.
The word Dhārmika appears in the Rāmāyaṇa perhaps a fourth or third as many times as the number of Ṡlōkas as there are (with some exaggeration). The entire ethos of the philosophy of India revolved around Dharma in those days.
Dharma, simply said, is shouldering the right responsibilities and discharging them in the righteous manner, regardless of the challenges and demands involved.
1.42.8
दिलीपस्तु महातेजा
यज्ञैर्बहुभिरिष्टवान् ।
त्रिंशद्वर्षसहस्राणि
राजा राज्यमकारयत् ॥
The supremely radiant Dileepa performed many Yajñas. He ruled the kingdom for thirty thousand years.
1.42.9
अगत्वा निश्चयं राजा
तेषामुद्धरणं प्रति ।
व्याधिना नरशार्दूल
कालधर्ममुपेयिवान् ॥
O tiger among men! Dileepa could not find a way to relieve their predicament! He passed away worrying about it.
1.42.10
इन्द्रलोकं गतो राजा
स्वार्जितेनैव कर्मणा ।
राज्ये भगीरथं पुत्रं
अभिषिच्य नरर्षभः ॥
He crowned his son Bhageeratha as the new king and by virtue of the good Karma he had accumulated, ascended to the abode of Indra.
1.42.11
भगीरथस्तु राजर्षिः
धार्मिको रघुनन्दन ।
अनपत्यो महातेजाः
प्रजाकामः स चाप्रजः ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Bhageeratha was a great Rājarshi and a great Dhārmika. He wanted to have children, but had none.
1.42.12
मन्त्रिष्वाधाय तद्राज्यम्
गङ्गावतरणे रतः ।
स तपो दीर्घमातिष्ठत्
गोकर्णे रघुनन्दन ।
ऊर्ध्वबाहुः पञ्चतपा
मासाहारो जितेन्द्रियः ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! He set himself on the endeavor of bringing Gaṅgā to the earth, left the kingdom in charge of the ministers and went to do a long Tapa at the auspicious Gōkarṇa, standing in the middle of five fires with hands stretched to the sky, eating only once in a month and having all his senses under complete control.
The five fires are: four fires on four sides, the fifth one being the sun.
1.42.13
तस्य वर्षसहस्राणि
घोरे तपसि तिष्ठतः ।
अतीतानि महाबाहो
तस्य राज्ञो महात्मनः ।
सुप्रीतो भगवान् ब्रह्मा
प्रजानां पतिरीश्वरः ॥
O Rāma of mighty arm! Thousands of years passed while that Mahātma king was doing arduous Tapa. Bhagawān Brahma, the lord of all peoples, was pleased.
1.42.15
ततस्सुरगुणैस्सार्द्धम्
उपागम्य पितामहः ।
भगीरथं महात्मानम्
तप्यमानमथाब्रवीत् ॥
The Progenitor, along with many Dēvas appeared in front of Bhageeratha the Mahātma, and said:
1.42.16
भगीरथ महाभाग
प्रीतस्तेऽहं जनेश्वर ।
तपसा च सुतप्तेन
वरं वरय सुव्रत ॥
O King! O Bhageeratha! O blessed one! I am pleased with the Tapa you have performed. Please ask for a boon that you would like to be granted!
1.42.17
तमुवाच महातेजाः
सर्वलोकपितामहम् ।
भगीरथो महाभागः
कृताञ्जलिरुपस्थितः ॥
Approaching him with folded hands, that blessed, supremely radiant, Bhageeratha asked the progenitor of all worlds:
1.42.18
यदि मे भगवन् प्रीतो
यद्यस्ति तपसः फलम् ।
सगरस्यात्मजास्सर्वे
मत्तस्सलिलमाप्नुयुः ।
गङ्गायास्सलिलक्लिन्ने
भस्मन्येषां महात्मनाम् ।
स्वर्गं गच्छेयुरत्यन्तम्
सर्वे मे प्रपितामहाः ॥
If you are pleased with me, if my Tapa deserves any reward, may all the sons of Sagara get the water oblations from me! With their ashes doused by the waters of Gaṅgā, may all my great grand-uncles ascend to Heaven and live there forever.
1.42.20
देया च सन्ततिर्देव
नावसीदेत्कुलं च नः ।
इक्ष्वाकूणां कुले देव
एष मेऽस्तु वरः परः ॥
And also please bless me with children, so that the dynasty of Ikshwāku continues without interruption.
1.42.21
उक्तवाक्यं तु राजानम्
सर्वलोकपितामहः ।
प्रत्युवाच शुभां वाणीम्
मधुरां मधुराक्षराम् ॥
To the king, who asked thus, the progenitor of all worlds replied in the most pleasing voice, with pleasing words.
1.42.22
मनोरथो महानेष
भगीरथ महारथ ।
एवं भवतु भद्रं ते
इक्ष्वाकुकुलवर्द्धन ॥
O Bhageeratha, the great charioteer! O forebear of Ikshwāku dynasty! The wishes you sought are great, indeed! May you be thus granted! May all bode well for you!
1.42.23
इयं हैमवती गङ्गा
ज्येष्ठा हिमवतस्सुता ।
तां वै धारयितुं शक्तो
हरस्तत्र नियुज्यताम् ॥
However, only lord Ṡiva is capable of taking the impact of Gaṅgā, the elder daughter of Himalayas and hence plead for his help.
1.42.24
गङ्गायाः पतनं राजन्
पृथिवी न सहिष्यति ।
तां वै धारयितुं वीर
नान्यं पश्यामि शूलिनः ॥
O King! Earth cannot withstand the impact of the fall of massive Gaṅgā. No one other than Ṡiva, the holder of Ṡūla, can I see as capable of withstanding the impact.
Throughout the Rāmāyaṇa you see events and activities of a mega scale. With that comes mega concerns and mega answers, like that of having to commission Ṡiva, the primordial power.
Rāmāyaṇa takes us on a ride that nicely swings between the infinitesimal nuances of daily life and the infinite factors larger than life.
1.42.25
तमेवमुक्त्वा राजानम्
गङ्गां चाभाष्य लोककृत् ।
जगाम त्रिदिवं देवः
सह देवैर्मरुद्गणैः ॥
Speaking thus to the King and after apprising Gaṅgā, the creator of the worlds returned to Heaven along with all the Dēvas and Maruts.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे द्विचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty second Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
