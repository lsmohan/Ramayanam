Baala Kaanda - Sarga 68
In this Sarga, the envoys of Janaka reach Ayōdhyā and convey king Janaka’s message to king Daṡaratha, about his desire to give Seetā in marriage to Rāma. Daṡaratha is exhilarated at the news and consults with his Gurus and ministers about the proposal from Janaka. All of them respond in positive and the decision to go to Mithilā is made.
1.68.1
जनकेन समादिष्टा
दूतास्ते क्लान्तवाहनाः ।
त्रिरात्रमुषिता मार्गे
तेऽयोध्यां प्राविशन् पुरीम् ॥
The envoys, thus ordered by Janaka, reached Ayōdhyā after travelling three days resting only at night, their horses completely exhausted.
1.68.2
राज्ञो भवनमासाद्य
द्वारस्थानिदमब्रुवन् ।
शीघ्रं निवेद्यतां राज्ञे
दूतान्नो जनकस्य च ॥
After reaching the king's palace, they said to the guards at the gate: "May you go immediately and tell the king that the envoys of Janaka are here!"
1.68.3
इत्युक्ता द्वारपालास्ते
राघवायन्यवेदयन् ।
ते राजवचनाद्दूता
राजवेश्म प्रवेशिताः ।
ददृशुर्देवसङ्काशम्
वृद्धं दशरथं नृपम् ॥
The gate guards immediately relayed the news to the king of Raghu dynasty (Daṡaratha). At the word of the king, the envoys entered the king’s palace and saw the aged and venerable king Daṡaratha, who was like a Dēva.
1.68.4
बद्धाञ्जलिपुटाः सर्वे
दूता विगतसाध्वसाः ।
राजानं प्रयता वाक्यम्
अब्रुवन् मधुराक्षरम् ॥
With folded hands, relieved of fear, all the envoys told these sweet words to the king, with all modesty:
1.68.5
मैथिलो जनको राजा
साग्निहोत्रपुरस्कृतम् ।
कुशलं चाव्ययं चैव
सोपाध्यायपुरोहितम् ।
मुहुर्मुहुर्मधुरया
स्नेहसंयुक्तया गिरा ।
जनकस्त्वां महाराजा
पृच्छते सपुरःसरम् ॥
O great king! Janaka, the king of Mithilā, in the most friendly and dulcet words possible, would like to enquire about your wellbeing and prosperity, surrounded by the Purōhitas, Gurus and always led by contingents of staff and army, and known for the performance of pristine fire rituals.
1.68.7
पृष्ट्वा कुशलमव्यग्रम्
वैदेहो मिथिलाधिपः ।
कौशिकानुमते वाक्यम्
भवन्तमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
The king of the city of Mithilā and state of Vidēha, after enquiring about your well-being, with all honesty and due respect and with the assent of the son of Kuṡika (Viṡwāmitra) would like to say:
1.68.8
पूर्वं प्रतिज्ञा विदिता
वीर्यशुल्का ममात्मजा ।
राजानश्च कृतामर्षा
निर्वीर्या विमुखीकृताः ॥
I had made it well known that my daughter can be claimed only by a valiant hero. Kings who resented and felt bitter about it, were defeated and turned away.
1.68.9
सेयं मम सुता राजन्
विश्वामित्रपुरस्सरैः ।
यदृच्छयाऽऽगतैर्वीरैः
निर्जिता तव पुत्रकैः ॥
O king! Your heroic son has now claimed my daughter having arrived here by chance, led by Viṡwāmitra.
1.68.10
तच्च राजन् धनुर्दिव्यम्
मध्ये भग्नं महात्मना ।
रामेण हि महाराज
महत्यां जनसंसदि ॥
O king among great kings! That great bow was broken in the middle by Mahātma Rāma in front of a gathering of many great people.
1.68.11
अस्मै देया मया सीता
वीर्यशुल्का महात्मने ।
प्रतिज्ञां कर्तुमिच्छामि
तदनुज्ञातुमर्हसि ॥
My daughter, a valiant man’s prize, should hence be given to this Mahātma (Rāma). So would I like to vow and pronounce with your kind permission.
1.68.12
सोपाध्यायो महाराज
पुरोहितपुरस्सरः ।
शीघ्रमागच्छ भद्रं ते
द्रष्टुमर्हसि राघवौ ॥
O great king! May you come here without any delay along with your Purōhitas and Gurus to see the duo of princes of Raghu dynasty!
1.68.13
प्रीतिं च मम राजेन्द्र
निर्वर्तयितुमर्हसि ।
पुत्रयोरुभयोरेव
प्रीतिं त्वमपि लप्स्यसे ॥
O lord of kings! May you oblige and make me happy! May you also have the joy of seeing both your sons!
1.68.14
एवं विदेहाधिपतिः
मधुरं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ।
विश्वामित्राभ्यनुज्ञातः
शतानन्दमते स्थितः ।
इत्युक्त्वा विरता दूता
राजगौरव शङ्किताः ॥
"These are the friendly words of the king of Vidēha, spoken with full support of Ṡatānanda and with full permission of Viṡwāmitra" concluded the envoys and waited for the response of the venerable king.
1.68.16
दूतवाक्यं तु तच्छ्रुत्वा
राजा परमहर्षितः ।
वसिष्ठं वामदेवं च
मन्त्रिणोऽन्यांश्च सोऽब्रवीत् ॥
The king was extremely joyous hearing those words of the envoys. Then, addressing Vasishṭha, Vāmadēva, and other ministers, he said:
1.68.17
गुप्तः कुशिकपुत्रेण
कौसल्यानन्दवर्द्धनः ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
विदेहेषु वसत्यसौ ॥
Under the able guardianship of the son of Kuṡika (Viṡwāmitra), the joy of Kousalyā (Rāma) along with brother Lakshmaṇa, is now in the country of Vidēha.
Overwhelmed by joy, Daṡaratha could not say how proud he was of Rāma. Hence he refers to him as the son of Kousalyā, prince of Kākutstha lineage and prince of Raghu dynasty, but not as 'my son'!
1.68.18
दृष्टवीर्यस्तु काकुत्स्थो
जनकेन महात्मना ।
सम्प्रदानं सुतायास्तु
राघवे कर्त्तुमिच्छति ॥
Mahātma Janaka, having witnessed the valor of the prince of Kākutstha lineage, would like to give her daughter's hand in marriage to the prince of Raghu dynasty.
1.68.19
यदि वो रोचते वृत्तम्
जनकस्य महात्मनः ।
पुरीं गच्छामहे शीघ्रम्
माभूत्कालस्य पर्ययः ॥
If all of you are positive about the proposal and background of Mahātma Janaka we shall go to his city immediately, without losing any time!
A king would not act on his own, without proper consultation of the Gurus and top officials even in matters concerning his son's marriage.
In the current days of atomic families and individualistic mores, we have lost the sense of involving others appropriately in matters of our concern and participating appropriately in matters of others' concern.
The instinctual makeup of human species is, obviously, to live collectively. But we are increasingly becoming detached from each other in matters that matter most.
We are increasingly getting isolated from each other. We are surrounded by formalities. We are buffered by gadgets. We are wearing the armor of individualism. We are being cooked within. We are becoming stressed. There is no one to hear the murmurs of our hearts. There is no heart close enough to rhyme with ours.
Such is the life we have been building of late, fast and furious, in the name of progress.
1.68.20
मन्त्रिणो बाढमित्याहुः
सह सर्वैर्महर्षिभिः ।
सुप्रीतश्चाब्रवीद्राजा
श्वो यात्रेति स मन्त्रिणः ॥
The ministers along with all the Maharshis said "Yes!" The king felt happy and said to the ministers to get ready for the journey on the next day.
1.68.21
मन्त्रिणस्तां नरेन्द्रस्य
रात्रिं परमसत्कृताः ।
ऊषुस्ते मुदिताः सर्वे
गुणैः सर्वैस्समन्विताः ॥
The respectable and virtuous envoys were honored and stayed back for the night.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे अष्टषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty eighth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
