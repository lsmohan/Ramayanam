Baala Kaanda - Sarga 12
In this Sarga Daṡaratha gets the blessings of the Ṛshis to start the Yajña, and orders the ministers to make the necessary preparations.
This Sarga has about the same content as Sarga 8. In fact, some of the Ṡlōkas are exactly the same as those of Sarga 8. The duplication can be attributed to the variations in multiple hand-written versions, before the print versions that came to be known as Eastern Edition and Southern Edition emerged. Please refer to the commentary in Sarga 4, for more details about the two editions.
The flow would be more logical and smooth if Sarga 8 is removed from its place and merged with this Sarga 12.
1.12.1
ततः काले बहुतिथे
कस्मिंश्चित्सुमनोहरे ।
वसन्ते समनुप्राप्ते
राज्ञो यष्टुं मनोऽभवत् ॥
Then, after a few moons, arrived a beautiful spring. The king felt like starting the Yajña.
1.12.2
ततः प्रसाद्य शिरसा
तं विप्रं देववर्णिनम् ।
यज्ञाय वरयामास
सन्तानार्थं कुलस्य वै ॥
After paying obeisance and getting the blessings of the Brāhmaṇa, who could be compared to Dēvas, he chose him to be the conductor of the Yajña that would give him offspring to continue his dynasty after him.
1.12.3
तथेति च स राजानम्
उवाच च सुसत्कृतः ।
सम्भाराः सम्भ्रियन्तां ते
तुरगश्च विमुच्यताम् ॥
The well-honored Brāhmaṇa said: "Let all the arrangements be made and let the horse be released!"
1.12.4
ततो राजाब्रवीद्वाक्यम्
सुमन्त्रं मन्त्रिसत्तमम् ॥
Then the king said to that prominent minister Sumantra:
1.12.4
सुमन्त्रावाहय क्षिप्रम्
ऋत्विजो ब्रह्मवादिनः ।
सुयज्ञं वामदेवं च
जाबालिमथ काश्यपम् ।
पुरोहितं वसिष्ठं च
ये चाप्यन्ये द्विजातयः ॥
O Sumantra! please arrange for the presence of all the Ṛtviks, who delve into the secrets of the universe, like Suyajña, Vāmadēva, Jābāli, Kāṡyapa, and Vasishṭha, the Purōhita and other eminent Brāhmaṇas.
1.12.6
ततः सुमन्त्रस्त्वरितम्
गत्वा त्वरितविक्रमः ।
समानयत्स तान् सर्वान्
समर्थान् वेदपारगान् ॥
The expedient Sumantra, immediately arranged for the presence of all of them, who were well versed in Vēdas.
1.12.7
तान् पूजयित्वा धर्मात्मा
राजा दशरथस्तदा ।
धर्मार्थसहितं युक्तम्
श्लक्ष्णं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
King Daṡaratha, the Dharmātma paid respects to all the invitees and said the following words, in pursuance of Dharma and Artha.
1.12.8
मम लालप्यमानस्य
पुत्रार्थं नास्ति वै सुखम् ।
तदर्थं हयमेधेन
यक्ष्यामीति मतिर्मम ॥
I am not happy. My heart is yearning for offspring. Hence I am thinking of performing Aṡwamēdha Yāga.
1.12.9
तदहं यष्टुमिच्छामि
शास्त्रदृष्टेन कर्मणा ।
ऋषिपुत्रप्रभावेन
कामान् प्राप्स्यामि चाप्यहम् ॥
I would like to perform the Yajña in strict accordance with the Ṡāstras. And I am hoping to get my desire fulfilled with the able guidance of the son of the Ṛshi (Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga).
1.12.10
ततः साध्विति तद्वाक्यम्
ब्राह्मणाः प्रत्यपूजयन् ।
वसिष्ठप्रमुखाः सर्वे
पार्थिवस्य मुखाच्च्युतम् ॥
Vasishṭha and other Brāhmaṇas responded saying that it is, indeed, a good idea.
1.12.11
ऋश्यशृङ्गपुरोगाश्च
प्रत्यूचुर्नृपतिं तदा ॥
Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga and all the others, then, said to the king:
1.12.11
सम्भाराः सम्भ्रियन्तां ते
तुरगश्च विमुच्यताम् ।
सर्वथा प्राप्स्यसे पुत्रान्
चतुरोऽमितविक्रमान् ।
यस्य ते धार्मिकी बुद्धिः
इयं पुत्रार्थमागता ॥
Let all the arrangements be made and let the horse be released! Your desire to have offspring is very much natural, appropriate and in accordance with Dharma. You will, indeed, be blessed with four exceptionally chivalrous sons.
1.12.13
ततः प्रीतोऽभवद्राजा
श्रुत्वा तु द्विजभाषितम् ।
अमात्यांश्चाब्रवीद्राजा
हर्षेणेदं शुभाक्षरम् ॥
The king was happy hearing the words of Brāhmaṇas and said these auspicious words to the ministers:
1.12.14
गुरूणां वचनाच्छीघ्रम्
सम्भाराः सम्भ्रियन्तु मे ।
समर्थाधिष्ठितश्चाश्वः
सोपाध्यायो विमुच्यताम् ॥
Let all the preparations be made according to the instructions of our Gurus. Let the horse be released guarded by able warriors and followed by teachers of Ṡāstras.
1.12.15
सरय्वाश्चोत्तरे तीरे
यज्ञभूमिर्विधीयताम् ।
शान्तयश्चाभिवर्द्धन्ताम्
यथाकल्पं यथाविधि ॥
Let the ground be prepared on the northern banks of river Sarayu for performing the Yāga. Let invocations be made for peace as per the traditions and Ṡāstras.
1.12.16
शक्यः कर्तुमयं यज्ञः
सर्वेणापि महीक्षिता ।
नापराधो भवेत्कष्टो
यद्यस्मिन् क्रतुसत्तमे ॥
This Yāga, one among the best, is very demanding and difficult to perform through to the end, without mistakes. If it were easy, every other king would have performed it.
1.12.17
छिद्रं हि मृगयन्तेऽत्र
विद्वांसो ब्रह्मराक्षसाः ।
विघ्नितस्य हि यज्ञस्य
सद्यः कर्ता विनश्यति ॥
The learned as well as the Brahma Rākshasas will only be eager to find violations in the performance. A failed Yāga will also take down the performer, for certain.
1.12.18
तद्यथा विधिपूर्वं मे
क्रतुरेष समाप्यते ।
तथा विधानं क्रियताम्
समर्थाः करणेष्विह ॥
Hence, you, the able souls, please plan on ensuring that the Yāga is performed to the end strictly in accordance with the rules and procedures laid out in the Ṡāstras.
1.12.19
तथेति च ततः सर्वे
मन्त्रिणः प्रत्यपूजयन् ।
पार्थिवेन्द्रस्य तद्वाक्यम्
 यथाऽऽज्ञप्तमकुर्वत ॥
The ministers appreciated the words of the king and did accordingly, as had been directed.
1.12.20
ततो द्विजास्ते धर्मज्ञं
अस्तुवन् पार्थिवर्षभम् ।
अनुज्ञातास्ततः सर्वे
पुनर्जग्मुर्यथाऽऽगतम् ॥
Brāhmaṇas, then, praised the king, the savant of Dharma, and took proper leave and left.
1.12.21
गतेष्वथ द्विजाग्र्येषु
मन्त्रिणस्तान्नराधिपः ।
विसर्ज्जयित्वा स्वं वेश्म
प्रविवेश महाद्युतिः ॥
After the best of those Brāhmaṇas left, the radiant king dismissed the assembly of ministers and went to his residence.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे द्वादशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twelfth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
