Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 10
In this Sarga, Vibheeshaṇa gives his counsel to Rāvaṇa, when there is no one in his presence except his ministers. He says that ever since Seetā arrived in Laṅkā, everyone had been noticing bad omens. He advices that it is prudent for Rāvaṇa to give Seetā back to Rāma. Rāvaṇa, annoyed with Vibheeshaṇa’s counsel, dismisses him.
6.10.1
ततः प्रत्युषसि प्राप्ते
प्राप्तधर्मार्थनिश्चयः ।
राक्षसाधिपतेर्वेश्म
भीमकर्मा विभीषणः ॥
After the break of dawn the next day, Vibheeshaṇa, of fierce deeds, went to the residence of the Lord of Rākshasas, clear in his mind about what begets Artha, and what would put (Rāvaṇa) on the course of Dharma.
6.10.2
शैलाग्रचयसङ्काशम्
शैलशृङ्गमिवोन्नतम् ।
सुविभक्तमहाकक्ष्यम्
महाजनपरिग्रहम् ॥
(That palatial residence of Rāvaṇa) was lofty like the peak of a mountain. Its (towers) resembled mountain peaks. Its apartments were well laid out. Many notables lived there.
6.10.3
मतिमद्भिर्महामात्रैः
अनुरक्तैरधिष्ठितम् ।
राक्षसैश्चाप्तपर्याप्तैः
सर्वतः परिरक्षितम् ॥
It was looked after by sagacious and dedicated ministers, and well-guarded on all sides by the most trusted and capable Rākshasas.
6.10.4
मत्तमातङ्गनिश्वासैः
व्याकुलीकृतमारुतम् ।
शङ्खघोषमहाघोषम्
तूर्यनादानुनादितम् ॥
The air (in that residence) was stirred by the deep breathing of elephants in rut. (The residence) resounded with the blare of conches and reverberated with the blowing of the Tūryas.
6.10.5
प्रमदाजनसम्बाधम्
प्रजल्पितमहापथम् ।
तप्तकाञ्चननिर्यूहम्
भूषणोत्तमभूषितम् ॥
Women folk could be seen there everywhere. The major pathways were lively with the chatter of people. Its turrets were covered with gold purified in fire. It was decorated with the best of ornaments.
6.10.6
गन्धर्वाणामिवावासम्
आलयं मरुतामिव ।
रत्नसञ्चयसम्बाधम्
भवनं भोगिनामिव ॥
It resembled the residences of Gandharvas and abodes of Maruts. With abundance of precious gems, it was like the palace of serpents.
6.10.7
तं महाभ्रमिवादित्यः
तेजोविस्तृतरश्मिमान् ।
अग्रजस्यालयं वीरः
प्रविवेश महाद्युतिः ॥
That Veera of supreme brilliance entered the residence of his elder brother, like the sun, radiating with his rays in every direction, entering a cloud.
6.10.8
पुण्यान् पुण्याहघोषांश्च
वेदविद्भिरुदाहृतान् ।
शुश्राव सुमहातेजा
भ्रातुर्विजयसंश्रितान् ॥
He, the supremely powerful one, then heard the chants by masters of Vēdas, invoking blessings on his brother for victory.
6.10.9
पूजितान् दधिपात्रैश्च
सर्पिर्भिः सुमनोक्षतैः ।
मन्त्रवेदविदो विप्रान्
ददर्श सुमहाबलः ॥
He, of immense strength, saw Brāhmaṇas well-versed in the Mantras and Brāhmaṇa portions of the Vēdas, being honored with the gift of vessels filled with curds, flowers and unbroken rice.
6.10.10
स पूज्यमानो रक्षोभिः
दीप्यमानम् स्वतेजसा ।
आसनस्थं महावाहुः
ववन्दे धनदानुजम् ॥
Received by the Rākshasas with due honor he of mighty arm made salutations to his brother, the younger brother of Kubēra, who was seated, glowing with his innate radiance.
6.10.11
स राजदृष्टिसम्पन्नम्
आसनं हेमभूषितम् ।
जगाम समुदाचारम्
प्रयुज्याचारकोविदः ॥
He occupied the seat adorned with gold, indicated by the King’s glance, observing all the customary courtesies, being conversant with customs.
6.10.12
स रावणं महात्मानम्
विजने मन्त्रिसन्निधौ ।
उवाच हितमत्यर्थम्
वचनं हेतुनिश्चितम् ॥
And then he tendered his counsel that was well-reasoned and wholesome to Rāvaṇa, the Mahātma, with no one else present except the ministers.
6.10.13
प्रसाद्य भ्रातरं ज्येष्ठम्
सान्त्वेनोपस्थितक्रमः ।
देशकालार्थसंवादी
दृष्टलोकपरावरः ॥
After pleading with his brother to accommodate him, he, who had seen the ups and downs of the world, expounded on the situation and the object in a systematic manner:
6.10.14
यदाप्रभृति वैदेही
सम्प्राप्तेमां पुरीं तव ।
तदाप्रभृति दृश्यन्ते
निमित्तान्यशुभानि नः ॥
Ever since Vaidēhee arrived at this city of yours, we have been seeing omens that augured ill for us.
6.10.15
सस्फुलिङ्गः सधूमार्चिः
सधूमकलुषोदयः ।
मन्त्रसन्धुक्षितोऽप्यग्निः
न सम्यगभिवर्धते ॥
Even though the offerings made into them are accompanied by the chanting of Mantras, the flames of the ritual fires do not rise steadily; they are smoky and spluttering and for that matter, they are engulfed in smoke right from the bottom.
6.10.16
अग्निष्ठेष्वग्निशालासु
तथा ब्रह्मस्थलीषु च ।
सरीसृपाणि दृश्यन्ते
हव्येषु च पिपीलिकाः ॥
Reptiles are invading the kitchens, the fire chambers and the places where the Vēdas are taught, and ants are being found in the sacred offerings.
6.10.17
गवां पयांसि स्कन्नानि
विमदा वीरकुञ्जराः ।
दीनमश्वाः प्रहेषन्ते
न च ग्रासाभिनन्दिनः ॥
The milk from cows is precipitating into curds. The doughty elephants are getting dispirited. The horses are neighing listlessly and are not enjoying eating grass.
6.10.18
खरोष्ट्राश्वतरा राजन्
भिन्नरोमाः स्रवन्ति नः ।
न स्वभावेऽवतिष्ठन्ते
विधानैरपि चिन्तिताः ॥
Donkeys, camels and mules, O King, are losing their hair and are shedding tears. They are not able to be their normal selves even with systematic medicinal treatment.
6.10.19
वायसाः सङ्घशः क्रूरा
व्याहरन्ति समन्ततः ।
समवेताश्च दृश्यन्ते
विमानाग्रेषु सङ्घशः ॥
Dread crows are flocking together and hovering everywhere. They can be seen, in flocks, on the top of the towers.
6.10.20
गृध्राश्च परिलीयन्ते
पुरीमुपरिपिण्डिताः ।
उपपन्नाश्च सन्ध्ये द्वे
व्याहरन्त्यशिवं शिवाः ॥
Vultures wheel over the city in large numbers and come down on the houses. Jackals are howling inauspiciously both at dawn and dusk.
6.10.21
क्रव्यादानां मृगाणां च
पुरीद्वारेषु सङ्घशः ।
श्रूयन्ते विपुला घोषाः
सविस्फूर्जितनिःस्वनाः ॥
Carnivores and other wild beasts are gathering in mobs at the city gates and the din of their thunderous barking and roaring is being heard endlessly.
6.10.22
तदेवं प्रस्तुते कार्ये
प्रायश्चित्तमिदं क्षमम् ।
रोचये वीर वैदेही
राघवाय प्रदीयताम् ॥
Such being the state of things, I think, O Veera, we should offer expiation by way of giving Vaidēhee back to Rāghava.
6.10.23
इदं च यदि वा मोहात्
लोभाद्वा व्याहृतं मया ।
तत्रापि च महाराज
न दोषं कर्तुमर्हसि ॥
In any case, please do not take it wrong, O great king, even if I have said something out of confusion or greed (for life).
6.10.24
अयं च दोषः सर्वस्य
जनस्यास्योपलक्ष्यते ।
रक्षसां राक्षसीनां च
पुरस्यान्तःपुरस्य च ॥
For, these bad omens are seen by all people, the Rākshasas and the Rākshasis, in the city and in the inner quarters.
6.10.25
श्रावणे चास्य मन्त्रस्य
निवृत्ताः सर्वमन्त्रिणः ।
अवश्यं च मया वाच्यम्
यद्दृष्टमपि वा श्रुतम् ।
सम्प्रधार्य यथान्यायम्
तद्भवान् कर्तुमर्हति ॥
All your ministers refrain from saying this, but it is my responsibility to say whatever I saw or heard. You shall consider this and do what you think is appropriate.
6.10.26
इति स्म मन्त्रिणां मध्ये
भ्राता भ्रातरमूचिवान् ।
रावणं राक्षसश्रेष्ठम्
पथ्यमेतद्विभीषणः ॥
So said Vibheeshaṇa, in the midst of the ministers, giving wholesome advice to his brother Rāvaṇa, foremost of the Rākshasas.
6.10.27
हितं महार्थं मृदु हेतुसंहितं
व्यतीतकालायतिसम्प्रतिक्षमम् ।
निशम्य तद्वाक्यमुपस्थितज्वरः
प्रसङ्गवानुत्तरमेतदब्रवीत् ॥
Annoyed on hearing those words that were so pregnant with meaning, so supported by cogent-reasoning and were so much in full consideration of the past, present and future, and spoken so gently, he responded saying these words, in his eagerness for an altercation:
6.10.28
भयं न पश्यामि कुतश्चिदप्यहं
न राघवः प्राप्स्यति जातु मैथिलीम् ।
सुरैः सहेन्द्रैरपि सङ्गतः कथं
ममाग्रतः स्थास्यति लक्ष्मणाग्रजः ॥
I do not see a cause of worry from anywhere. Rāghava shall never have Maithili. Lakshmaṇa’s elder brother cannot dare confront me, even with the support of Indra and all the Dēvas.
6.10.29
इतीदमुक्त्वा सुरसैन्यनाशनो
महाबलः संयति चण्डविक्रमः ।
दशाननो भ्रातरमाप्तवादिनं
विसर्जयामास तदा विभीषणम् ॥
Saying these words, the Ten-faced of immense strength and fierce prowess in battle, who had destroyed the forces of Dēvas, dismissed his brother Vibheeshaṇa who spoke in his best interest.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे दशमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the tenth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
