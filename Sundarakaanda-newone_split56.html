Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 56
In this Sarga, Hanumān consoles Seetā and takes leave of her while she praises him for what he had accomplished and urges him to do what he can so that Rāma destroys Laṅkā and takes her back in a manner that befits his valor, name and fame.
Then, getting ready to leap over the ocean, he climbs the mountain Arishṭa and increases his body size. The mountain, under the pressure of his weight sinks into the ground with the trees on it falling off and its peaks shaking violently. Hanumān then springs into the sky, eager to see Rāma.
5.56.1
ततस्तु शिंशुपामूले
जानकीं पर्युपस्थिताम् ।
अभिवाद्याब्रवीद्दिष्ट्या
पश्यामि त्वामिहाक्षताम् ॥
Then making his prostrations to Jānaki who sat under the Ṡiṃṡupa tree, he said: ‘By the grace of Providence I find you unharmed!’
5.56.2
ततस्तं प्रस्थितं सीता
वीक्षमाणा पुनः पुनः ।
भर्तृस्नेहान्वितं वाक्यम्
हनुमन्तमभाषत ॥
Then looking at him again and again as he was set to leave, Seetā said to Hanumān these words that showed her love for her husband:
5.56.3
काममस्य त्वमेवैकः
कार्यस्य परिसाधने ।
पर्याप्तः परवीरघ्न
यशस्यस्ते बलोदयः ।
MEANINGG
O destroyer of foe! You are the only one ।
who could accomplish a mission like this!
You deserve wide renown for the grit you have shown! ॥
5.56.5
शरैस्तु सङ्कुलां कृत्वा
लङ्कां परबलार्दनः ।
मां नयेद्यदि काकुत्स्थः
तत्तस्य सदृशं भवेत् ॥
It would be befitting of Kākutstha, if he, the destroyer of enemy forces, takes me back, after putting Laṅkā in turmoil with his arrows.
5.56.6
तद्यथा तस्य विक्रान्तम्
अनुरूपं महात्मनः ।
भवत्याहवशूरस्य
तथा त्वमुपपादय ॥
You must so contrive it that the expedition would be worthy of the prowess of that Ṡūra of battles.
5.56.7
तदर्थोपहितं वाक्यम्
प्रश्रितं हेतुसंहितम् ।
निशम्य हनुमांस्तस्या
वाक्यमुत्तरमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those reasonable words of hers that carried a deep meaning, Hanumān responded saying:
5.56.8
क्षिप्रमेष्यति काकुत्स्थः
हर्यृक्षप्रवरैर्वृतः ।
यस्ते युधि विजित्यारीन्
शोकं व्यपनयिष्यति ॥
Kākutstha, who will defeat the enemies in the battle and put an end to your grief, will come here soon along with Vānaras and Ṛkshas.
5.56.9
एवमाश्वास्य वैदेहीम्
हनुमान् मारुतात्मजः ।
गमनाय मतिं कृत्वा
वैदेहीमभ्यवादयत् ॥
Thus consoling Vaidēhee and set to take leave, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, made prostrations to her.
5.56.10
ततस्स कपिशार्दूलः
स्वामिसन्दर्शनोत्सुकः ।
आरुरोह गिरिश्रेष्ठम्
अरिष्टमरिमर्दनः ॥
Eager to meet his master, the Vānara, smasher of the foe, climbed on to the great mountain, Arishṭa.
5.56.11
तुङ्गपद्मकजुष्टाभिः
नीलाभिर्वनराजिभिः ।
सोत्तरीयमिवाम्भोदैः
शृंगान्तरविलम्बिभिः ॥
Dark woods covered it and elephants with red patches on their bodies dotted it. It was shining, with clouds hanging between its crests looking like an upper garment.
5.56.12
बोध्यमानमिव प्रीत्या
दिवाकरकरैश्शुभैः ।
उन्मिषन्तमिवोद्धूतैः
लोचनैरिव धातुभिः ॥
Its bright shining patches of ores looked like eyes opened wide looking up, upon it being woken up affectionately by the sun with his lovely rays.
5.56.13
तोयौघनिस्स्वनैर्मन्द्रैः
प्राधीतमिव पर्वतम् ।
प्रगीतमिव विस्पष्टैः
नानाप्रस्रवणस्वनैः ॥
The rumble of the masses of water of the mountain sounded like the chant of Vēdas, while the gentle sounds of its streams resembled musical notes.
5.56.14
देवदारुभिरत्युच्चैः
ऊर्ध्वबाहुमिव स्थितम् ।
प्रपातजलनिर्घोषैः
प्राक्रुष्टमिव सर्वतः ॥
With its tall Dēvadāru trees, it seemed as if it stood stretching its arms upwards. With the din of its cascades, it seemed as if it was roaring, from every direction.
5.56.15
वेपमानमिव श्यामैः
कम्पमानैश्शरद्वनैः ।
वेणुभिर्मारुतोद्धूतैः
कूजन्तमिव कीचकैः ॥
The swaying dark woods made that mountain look as if it was shivering in that post-monsoon season. With the wind blowing through the hallow bamboos the mountain seemed as if it was whistling.
5.56.16
निश्श्वसन्तमिवामर्षात्
घोरैराशीविषोत्तमैः ।
नीहारकृतगम्भीरैः
ध्यायन्तमिव गह्वरैः ॥
With its huge, dreadful and poisonous snakes hissing, the mountain appeared as if it was sighing. With its deep caverns covered in snow, the mountain looked as if it was meditating.
5.56.17
मेघपादनिभैः पादैः
प्रक्रान्तमिव सर्वतः ।
जृंम्भमाणमिवाऽकाशे
शिखरैरभ्रमालिभिः ॥
With its foothills resembling low hanging clouds, it looked as if the mountain was trying to tread in every direction. With rings of clouds hovering on its peaks it looked as if it was yawning.
5.56.18
कूटैश्च बहुधा कीर्णैः
शोभितं बहुकन्दरैः ।
सालतालाश्वकर्णैश्च
वंशैश्च बहुभिर्वृतम् ॥
It was lovely with its many caves and many crests. It was covered with many Sala, palm, Aṡwakarṇa and bamboo trees.
5.56.19
लतावितानैर्विततैः
पुष्पवद्भिरलङ्कृतम् ।
नानामृगगणाकीर्णम्
धातुनिष्यन्दभूषितम् ॥
It was decorated everywhere with creepers in full flower. It teemed with many herds of deer and was adorned by streaks oozing from its ores.
5.56.20
बहुप्रस्रवणोपेतम्
शिलासञ्चयसङ्कटम् ।
महर्षियक्षगन्धर्व
किन्नरोरगसेवितम् ॥
It had many streams flowing through its clusters of boulders nestling with each other. It was frequented by Maharshis, Yakshas, Gandharvas, Kinnaras and Uragas.
5.56.21
लतापादपसङ्घातम्
सिंहाध्युषितकन्दरम् ।
व्याघ्रसङ्घसमाकीर्णम्
स्वादुमूलफलद्रुमम् ॥
It was thick with creepers and trees and its caves were filled with lions. It was filled with hordes of tigers and and vegetation yielding delicious roots and fruits.
5.56.22
तमारुरोह हनुमान्
पर्वतं पवनात्मजः ।
रामदर्शनशीघ्रेण
प्रहर्षेणाभिचोदितः ॥
Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, climbed that mountain, excited at the prospect of seeing Rāma soon.
5.56.23
तेन पादतलाक्रान्ता
रम्येषु गिरिसानुषु ।
सघोषास्समशीर्यन्त
शिलाश्चूर्णीकृतास्ततः ॥
Under the pressure of his feet, the boulders on the lovely flanks of the mountain became shattered with explosive noises and were reduced to dust.
5.56.24
स तमारुह्य शैलेन्द्रम्
व्यवर्धत महाकपिः ।
दक्षिणादुत्तरं पारम्
प्रार्थयन् लवणाम्भसः ॥
Getting up on the great mountain, the mighty Vānara grew in size on the southern shore, getting ready to go to the northern shore of those salty waters.
5.56.25
अधिरुह्य ततो वीरः
पर्वतं पवनात्मजः ।
ददर्श सागरं भीमम्
मीनोरगनिषेवितम् ॥
Climbing on to that mountain, the son of Vāyu, the Veera, took a good look at the daunting ocean brimming with fish and snakes.
5.56.26
स मारुत इवाऽकाशम्
मारुतस्यात्मसम्भवः ।
प्रपेदे हरिशार्दूलो
दक्षिणादुत्तरां दिशम् ॥
That son of Vāyu, a tiger among Vānaras, then took to the skies, like the wind blowing from the south to the north.
5.56.27
स तदा पीडितस्तेन
कपिना पर्वतोत्तमः ।
ररास सह तैर्भूतैः
प्रविशन्वसुधातलम् ।
कम्पमानैश्च शिखरैः
पतद्भिरपि च द्रुमैः ॥
Pressed by the Vānara, the great mountain and the creatures on it groaned. With its peaks shaking and its trees falling off, the mountain sank into the ground.
5.56.28
तस्योरुवेगोन्मथिताः
पादपाः पुष्पशालिनः ।
निपेतुर्भूतले रुग्णाः
शक्रायुधहता इव ॥
Shaken by the speed of his thighs the trees in flower fell on the ground, smashed as if they were struck down by Ṡakra’s weapon.
5.56.29
कन्दरान्तरसंस्थानाम्
पीडितानां महौजसाम् ।
सिंहानां निनदो भीमो
नभो भिन्दन् स शुश्रुवे ॥
The dreadful roar of lions that became crushed in the interiors of the caves reverberated, rending the sky.
5.56.30
त्रस्तव्यावृत्तवसना
व्याकुलीकृतभूषणाः ।
विद्याधर्यः समुत्पेतुः
सहसा धरणीधरात् ॥
Vidyādhara women quickly sprang from that mountain, scared, with their clothes in disorder and their ornaments jumbled up.
5.56.31
अतिप्रमाणा बलिनो
दीप्तजिह्वा महाविषाः ।
निपीडितशिरोग्रीवा
व्यचेष्टन्त महाहयः ॥
Big, strong and virulently poisonous snakes, huge in size and with flaming tongues, wriggled, their heads and necks badly battered.
5.56.32
किन्नरोरगगन्धर्व
यक्षविद्याधरास्तदा ।
पीडितं तं नगवरम्
त्यक्त्वा गगनमास्थिताः ॥
Then, Kinnaras, Uragas, Gandharvas, Yakshas and Vidyādharas rose into the sky, abandoning that great mountain which was thus pressed.
5.56.33
स च भूमिधरः श्रीमान्
बलिना तेन पीडितः ।
सवृक्षशिखरोदग्रः
प्रविवेश रसातलम् ॥
That rich mountain, with its trees and peaks that stood tall, sank into Rasātala, oppressed by the pressure of that strong one.
5.56.34
दशयोजनविस्तारः
त्रिंशद्योजनमुच्छ्रितः ।
धरण्यां समतां यातः
स बभूव धराधरः ॥
That ten-Yōjana wide and thirty-Yōjana tall mountain became level with the ground.
5.56.35
स लिलङ्घयिषुर्भीमम्
सलीलं लवणार्णवम् ।
कल्लोलास्फालवेलान्तम्
उत्पपात नभो हरिः ॥
The Vānara then sprang into the sky in a graceful manner, to leap over that daunting mass of salty water whose billows crashed violently on the shore.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे षट्पञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty sixth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
