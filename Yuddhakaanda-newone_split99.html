Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 99
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa deploys a great Astra on the Vānaras, which kills many of them, while the others flee. Advancing, Rāvaṇa comes within striking distance of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa. Lakshmaṇa shoots arrows on him first. Striking down Lakshmaṇa's arrows, he goes past him, and faces Rāma directly.
The two giants, Rāma and Rāvaṇa clash with each other, face to face. Rāvaṇa sends a downpour of arrows on Rāma and Rāma cuts them down with Bhalla arrows. They send a barrage of arrows, whose tips are in a variety of shapes, on each other. Rāma shoots Rudra's Astra. Rāvaṇa strikes it down and shoots Asura's Astra. Rāma discharges Agni's Astra, and strikes it down.
6.99.1
महोदरमहापार्शो
हतौ दृष्ट्वा तु राक्षसौ ।
तस्मिंश्च निहते वीरे
विरूपाक्षे महाबले ।
आविवेश महान् क्रोधो
रावणं तं महामृधे ।
सूतं सञ्चोदयामास
वाक्यं चेदमुवाच ह ॥
A great rage took over Rāvaṇa in that great battle, as he saw the Rākshasas Mahōdara and Mahāpārṡwa slain, and also Virūpāksha struck down meanwhile. Then he prodded his charioteer into action, saying these words:
6.99.3
निहतानाममात्यानाम्
रुद्धस्य नगरस्य च ।
दुःखमेषोऽपनेष्यामि
हत्वा तौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
I shall remove this misery, born out of the death of the ministers and from the capture of the city, by slaying those two, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
6.99.4
रामवृक्षं रणे हन्मि
सीतापुष्पफलप्रदम् ।
प्रशाखा यस्य सुग्रीवो
जाम्बवान् कुमुदो नलः ।
द्विविदश्चैव मैन्दश्च
अङ्गदो गन्धमादनः ।
हनूमांश्च सुषेणश्च
सर्वे च हरियूथपाः ॥
I shall destroy in the battle, the tree called Rāma, which has Seetā for fruit and flower, and Sugreeva, Jāmbavān, Kumuda, Nala, Dwivida, Mainda, Aṅgada, Gandhamādana, Hanumān, Sushēṇa and all the other Vānara commanders for the boughs.
6.99.6
स दिशो दश धोषेण
रथस्यातिरथो महान् ।
नादयन् प्रययौ तूर्णम्
राघवं चाभ्यधावत ॥
That mighty super-charioter started immediately in his chariot, the din from its rumble filling all the ten cardinals, and dashed towards Rāghava, shouting.
6.99.7
पूरिता तेन शब्देन
सनदीगिरिकानना ।
सञ्चचाल मही सर्वा
सवराहमृगद्विपा ॥
Filled with that noise, the earth, along with its rivers, mountains and forests, and with its boars, deer and elephants, shook.
6.99.8
तामसं स महाघोरम्
चकारास्त्रं सुदारुणम् ।
निर्ददाह कपीन् सर्वान्
ते प्रपेतुः समन्ततः ॥
He shot the most dreaded and extremely terrible Astra of darkness, that burnt away all the Vānaras, who tumbled down everywhere.
6.99.9
उत्पपात रजो घोरम्
तैर्भग्नैः सम्प्रधावितैः ।
न हि तत्सहितुं शेकुः
ब्रह्मणा निर्मितं स्वयम् ॥
There arose an eerie cloud of dust as they took to their heels, broken. They could, after all, not withstand it, which was made by none other than Brahma himself.
6.99.10
तान्यनीकान्यनेकानि
रावणस्य शरोत्तमैः ।
दृष्ट्वा भग्नानि शतशो
राघवः पर्यवस्थितः ॥
Then Rāghava himself took a stand, seeing the many hundreds of contingents of the forces shattered by the powerful arrows of Rāvaṇa.
6.99.11
ततो राक्षसशार्दूलो
विद्राव्य हरिवाहिनीम् ।
स ददर्श ततो रामम्
तिष्ठन्तमपराजितम् ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
विष्णुना वासवं यथा ।
आलिखन्तमिवाकाशम्
अवष्टभ्य महद्धनुः ।
पद्मपत्रविशालाक्षम्
दीर्घबाहुमरिन्दमम् ॥
After chasing away the Vānara forces, that tiger among Rākshasas saw Rāma, a subduer of foe who never knew defeat, whose eyes were wide like lotus petals and whose arms were long, making a stand along with his brother Lakshmaṇa, looking like Vishṇu with Vāsava by his side, leaning on his great bow, which seemed so tall as if it was touching the sky.
6.99.13
ततो रामो महातेजाः
सौमित्रिसहितो बली ।
वानरांश्च रणे भग्नान्
आपतन्तं च रावणम् ।
समीक्ष्य राघवो हृष्टो
मध्ये जग्राह कार्मुकम् ॥
The mighty strong Rāma, along with Sowmitri, had seen Vānaras battered and Rāvaṇa advancing in that fight. Rāghava, only happy (to fight with him directly), grabbed his bow, holding it in the middle.
6.99.15
विस्फारयितुमारेभे
ततः स धनुरुत्तमम् ।
महावेगं महानादम्
निर्भिन्दन्निव मेदिनीम् ॥
And he began twanging that superb, thunderous and extremely powerful bow as if he would rend the earth with it.
6.99.16
रावणस्य च बाणौघै
रामविस्फारितेन च ।
शब्देन राक्षसास्ते च
पेतुश्च शतशस्तदा ॥
The Rākshasas tumbled down in hundreds, from the (terrible) sound of arrows shot by Rāvaṇa and of the twanging of the bow by Rāma.
6.99.17
तयोः शरपथं प्राप्तो
रावणो राजपुत्रयोः ।
स बभौ च यथा राहुः
समीपे शशिसूर्ययोः ॥
Rāvaṇa, now within striking distance of the arrows of the princes, looked like Rāhu in the proximity of the moon and the sun.
6.99.18
तमिच्छन् प्रथमं योद्धुम्
लक्ष्मणो निशितैः शरैः ।
मुमोच धनुरायम्य
शरानग्निशिखोपमान् ॥
Wanting to be the first to fight with his sharp arrows, Lakshmaṇa bent his bow to the full and shot arrows that burnt like tongues of flame.
6.99.19
तान्मुक्तमात्रानाकाशे
लक्ष्मणेन धनुष्मता ।
बाणान् बाणैर्महातेजा
रावणः प्रत्यवारयत् ॥
No sooner did Lakshmaṇa, the great archer, release those arrows into the air than Rāvaṇa intercepted them with arrows of his own.
6.99.20
एकमेकेन बाणेन
त्रिभिस्त्रीन् दशभिर्दश ।
लक्ष्मणस्य प्रचिच्छेद
दर्शयन् पाणिलाघवम् ॥
One arrow with one, three with three, and ten with ten, he cut down Lakshmaṇa's arrows, displaying the deftness of his hand.
6.99.21
अभ्यतिक्रम्य सौमित्रिम्
रावणः समितिञ्जयः ।
आससाद ततो रामम्
स्थितं शैलमिवाचलम् ॥
Going past Sowmitri, Rāvaṇa, winner of many battles, approached Rāma who stood firm like a mountain.
6.99.22
स राघवम् समासाद्य
क्रोधसंरक्तलोचनः ।
व्यसृजच्छरवर्षाणि
रावणो राक्षसेश्वरः ॥
Approaching Rāghava, Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, his eyes blood-red with anger, sent a downpour of arrows.
6.99.23
शरधारास्ततो रामो
रावणस्य धनुश्च्युताः ।
दृष्ट्वैवापततः शीघ्रम्
भल्लान् जग्राह सत्वरम् ॥
Seeing that torrent of arrows come out from Rāvaṇa's bow and fly towards him, Rāma pulled out Bhalla arrows of his own, quickly.
6.99.24
तान् शरौघांस्ततो भल्लैः
तीक्ष्णैश्चिच्छेद राघवः ।
दीप्यमानान् महाघोरान्
क्रुद्धानाशीविषानिव ॥
Rāghava cut those volleys of extremely terrible and blazing arrows that were like angry venomous serpents, into pieces, with the sharp Bhalla arrows.
6.99.25
राघवो रावणं तूर्णम्
रावणो राघवं तदा ।
अन्योन्यं विविधैस्तीक्ष्णैः
शरैरभिववर्षतुः ॥
Then, they sent on each other, Rāghava on Rāvaṇa and Rāvaṇa on Rāghava, a downpour of a variety of sharp arrows, in quick succession.
6.99.26
चेरतुश्च चिरं चित्रम्
मण्डलं सव्यदक्षिणम् ।
बाणवेगान् समुत्क्षिप्तौ
अन्योन्यमपराजितौ ॥
Making a variety of circular movements veering to the left and right, they tried to stay out of the reach of each other's arrows, both remaining undefeated.
6.99.27
तयोर्भूतानि वित्रेसुः
युगपत् सम्प्रयुध्यतोः ।
रौद्रयोः सायकमुचोः
यमान्तकनिकाशयोः ॥
All creatures were terrified as those who shot arrows in extreme wrath and who resembled Yama and Death, were locked in direct combat.
6.99.28
सततं विविधैर्बाणैः
बभूव गगनं तदा ।
घनैरिवातपापाये
विद्युन्मालासमाकुलैः ॥
With those varieties of arrows falling incessantly, the sky looked as it does when the summer draws to a close, covered with clouds and flashes of lightning.
6.99.29
गवाक्षितमिवाकाशम्
बभूव शरवृष्टिभिः ।
महावेगैः सुतीक्ष्णाग्रैः
गृध्रपत्रैः सुवाजितैः ॥
With that downpour of fast and sharp-tipped arrows that were sped by eagle feathers, the sky looked as if it was behind a grid of windows.
6.99.30
शरान्धकारमाकाशम्
चक्रतुः प्रथमं तदा ।
गतेऽस्तं तपने चापि
महामेघाविवोत्थितौ ॥
First they rendered the sky dark with their arrows. Then, even after the sun had set, they kept it obscured like huge, rising clouds do.
6.99.31
बभूव तुमुलं युद्धम्
अन्योन्यवधकाङ्क्षिणोः ।
अनासाद्यमचिन्त्यं च
वृत्रवासवयोरिव ॥
The fight between them, both bent on slaying the other, was like that between Vṛtra and Vāsava which was beyond what anyone could imagine and what anyone could get anywhere near.
6.99.32
उभौ हि परमेष्वासौ
उभौ शस्त्रविशारदौ ।
उभावस्त्रविदां मुख्यौ
उभौ युद्धे विचेरतुः ॥
Both were great bowmen, both were experts in the use of Ṡastras, both were prominent among those who know Astras, and they both clashed in that fight.
6.99.33
उभौ हि येन व्रजतः
तेन तेन शरोर्मयः ।
ऊर्मयो वायुना विद्धा
जग्मुः सागरयोरिव ॥
Wherever they moved in their maneuvers, there followed the billows of arrows, like the billows of the ocean following the wind.
6.99.34
ततः संसक्तहस्तस्तु
रावणो लोकरावणः ।
नाराचमालां रामस्य
ललाटे प्रत्यमुञ्चत ॥
Rāvaṇa, who made the world wail, whose hands were never taken off the bow, shot a string of Nārācās on Rāma's forehead.
6.99.35
रौद्रचापप्रयुक्तां ताम्
नीलोत्पलदलप्रभाम् ।
शिरसा धारयन् रामो
न व्यथां प्रत्यपद्यत ॥
Rāma, pained in the least, considered them, which were shot from that fierce bow and shone like the petals of blue lily, to be ornaments on his head.
6.99.36
अथ मन्त्रानभिजपन्
रौद्रमस्त्रमुदीरयन् ।
शरान् भूयः समादाय
रामः क्रोधसमन्वितः ।
मुमोच च महातेजाः
चापमायम्य वीर्यवान् ॥
The valiant Rāma, of extraordinary power, took out some arrows again, chanted the Mantras of Rudra's Astra, bent the bow to the full and shot them, in his wrath.
6.99.37
ते महामेघसङ्काशे
कवचे पतिताः शराः ।
अवध्ये राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
न व्यथां जनयंस्तदा ॥
Those arrows, falling on the unbreakable armor of the lord of Rākshasas, which looked like a huge cloud, could hardly cause him any pain.
6.99.38
पुनरेवाथ तं रामो
रथस्थं राक्षसाधिपम् ।
ललाटे परमास्त्रेण
सर्वास्त्रकुशलोऽभिनत् ॥
Again, Rāma, an expert in all Astras, struck the king of the Rākshasas seated in his chariot on his forehead with a very powerful Astra.
6.99.39
ते भित्त्वा बाणरूपाणि
पञ्चशीर्षा इवोरगाः ।
श्वसन्तो विविशुर्भूमिम्
रावणप्रतिकूलिताः ॥
Foiled by Rāvaṇa, they split his arrows and entered the earth hissing like five-headed serpents.
6.99.40
निहत्य राघवस्यास्त्रम्
रावणः क्रोधमूर्च्छितः ।
आसुरं सुमहाघोरम्
अन्यदस्त्रं चकार सः ॥
Striking down Rāghava's Astra, Rāvaṇa, in extreme rage, released another extremely powerful and terrible Astra, of Asuras.
6.99.41
सिंहव्याघ्रमुखाश्चान्यान्
कङ्ककाकमुखानपि ।
गृध्रश्येनमुखांश्चापि
शृगालवदनांस्तथा ।
ईहामृगमुखांश्चान्यान्
व्यादितास्यान् भयानकान् ।
पञ्चास्यान् लेलिहानांश्च
ससर्ज निशितान् शरान् ॥
He shot a barrage of sharp arrows, which had tips shaped like the faces of lions and tigers, some like the faces of Kaṅkas and crows, some like the faces of vultures and eagles, some like the faces of jackals, some like the faces of wolves and some with dreadful mouths that were wide agape, and others with five faces, like those of serpents.
I am sure readers would not miss to note that the tips of arrows of Rāvaṇa are shaped like animal faces, while the tips of arrows of Rāma are shaped like celestial objects.
6.99.43
शरान् खरमुखांश्चान्यान्
वराहमुखसंस्थितान् ।
श्वानकुक्कुटवक्त्रांश्च
मकराशीविषाननान् ।
एतानन्यांश्च मायावी
ससर्ज निशितान् शरान् ।
रामं प्रति महातेजाः
क्रुद्धः सर्प इव श्वसन् ॥
He of extraordinary power who knew black magic shot more sharp arrows which had tips shaped like the faces of mules, some like the faces of boars, some like the faces of dogs and chickens, some like the venomous faces of crocodiles, and many others, aimed at Rāma, hissing like an angry serpent.
6.99.45
आसुरेण समाविष्टः
सोऽस्त्रेण रघुपुंगवः ।
ससर्जास्त्रं महोत्साहः
पावकं पावकोपमः ॥
Caught in the grip of the Astra of Asuras, but still blazing like fire with his extraordinary enthusiasm, the bull among Raghus discharged Agni's Astra.
6.99.46
अग्निदीप्तमुखान् बाणान्
तथा सूर्यमुखानपि ।
चन्द्रार्धचन्द्रवक्त्रांश्च
धूमकेतुमुखानपि ।
ग्रहनक्षत्रवक्त्रांश्च
महोल्कामुखसंस्थितान् ।
विद्युज्जिह्वोपमांश्चान्यान्
ससर्ज निशितान् शरान् ॥
He shot sharp arrows which had tips shaped like the flames of fire, some like the sun, some like the moon, some like the half moon, some like comets, some like planets and stars, some like great meteors and some like the flashes of lightning.
6.99.48
ते रावणशरा घोरा
राघवास्त्रसमाहताः ।
विलयं जग्मुराकाशे
जग्मुश्चैव सहस्रशः ॥
Rāvaṇa’s horrid arrows, struck by Rāma’s arrows in mid air, were rendered useless, broken into thousands of pieces.
6.99.49
तदस्त्रं निहतं दृष्ट्वा
रामेणाक्लिष्टकर्मणा ।
हृष्टा नेदुस्ततः सर्वे
कपयः कामरूपिणः ।
सुग्रीवप्रमुखा वीराः
संपरिक्षिप्य राघवम् ॥
Seeing that Astra struck down by Rāma who did things with ease, the valiant Vānaras who could take any form at will, like Sugreeva and others, gathered around Rāghava and shouted in joy.
6.99.51
ततस्तदस्त्रं विनिहत्य राघवः
प्रसह्य तद्रावणबाहुनिस्सृतम् ।
मुदाऽन्वितो दाशरथिर्महाहवे
विनेदुरुच्चैर्मुदिताः कपीश्वराः ॥
Having struck down that Astra that left the hands of Rāvaṇa using all the force in that great battle, the son of Daṡaratha was very pleased with himself. The Vānara chiefs also shouted in joy.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे एकोनशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninety ninth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
