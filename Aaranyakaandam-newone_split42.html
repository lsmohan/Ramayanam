Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 42
Māreeca goes along with Rāvaṇa after showing all the reluctance he can, resigned to the fate of being killed by Rāma. Rāvaṇa feels very happy and both of them head towards Daṇḍaka.
After reaching Daṇḍaka, prodded again by Rāvaṇa, Māreeca takes the form of an enchanting deer, whose limbs, body, face, sides and tail are an eyeful, with the shine of every gem and hue of every mineral.
The deer then darts by the woods around Rāma’s Āṡrama hoping to get noticed by Seetā. Seetā comes out to pluck flowers after some time and becomes wonderstruck seeing the deer which seems to have been made of every gem on earth.
3.42.1
एवमुक्त्वा तु वचनम्
मारीचो रावणं ततः ।
गच्छावेत्यब्रवीद्दीनो
भयाद्रात्रिंचरप्रभोः ॥
After saying those words to Rāvaṇa, Māreeca, scared of the lord of the Night-rovers, said in extreme dejection: ‘Let us go!’
3.42.2
दृष्टश्चाहं पुनस्तेन
शरचापासिधारिणा ।
मद्वधोद्यतशस्त्रेऽण
विनष्टं जीवितं च मे ॥
I am seen, again, by that wielder of arrows, bow and sword. My life is coming to an end with him lifting the weapon to kill me.
3.42.3
न हि रामं पराक्रम्य
जीवन्प्रतिनिवर्तते ।
वर्तते प्रतिरूपोऽसौ
यमदण्डहतस्य ते ॥
No one will return alive, after attacking Rāma. What you see moving in front of you is just my image; I am killed by the rod of Yama already.
3.42.4
किं नु शक्यं मया कर्तुम्
एवं त्वयि दुरात्मनि ।
एष गच्छाम्यहं तात
स्वस्ति तेऽस्तु निशाचर ॥
What can I do with you who are so possessed by evil? So let me move along, my dear. May all bode well for you, O Night-rover!
3.42.5
प्रहृष्टस्त्वभवत्तेन
वचनेन स रावणः ।
परिष्वज्य सुसंश्लिष्टम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Rāvaṇa, feeling very happy to hear those words, took him into his tight embrace and said these words:
3.42.6
एतच्छौण्डीर्ययुक्तं ते
मच्छन्दवशवर्तिनः ।
इदानीमसि मारीचः
पूर्वमन्यो निशाचरः ॥
Now, you are the true Māreeca who complies with my intentions, and displaying your true valor; though you behaved like a stranger for a moment, O Night-rover!
3.42.7
आरुह्यतामयं शीघ्रम्
रथो रत्नविभूषितः ।
मया सह तथा युक्तः
पिशाचवदनैः खरैः ॥
Get on to the gem-studded chariot that is yoked with ghoul-faced mules, along with me!
3.42.8
प्रलोभयित्वा वैदेहीम्
यथेष्टं गन्तुमर्हसि ।
तां शून्ये प्रसभं सीताम्
आनयिष्यामि मैथिलीम् ॥
After luring Vaidēhee, you can go where you wish. I will forcefully carry away Maithili once the place is desolate.
3.42.9
ततो रावणमारीचौ
विमानमिव तं रथम् ।
आरुह्य ययतुश्शीघ्रम्
तस्मादाश्रममण्डलात् ॥
Then, the duo of Rāvaṇa and Māreeca departed from that Āṡrama area quickly getting on the chariot which flew like an aerial car.
3.42.10
तथैव तत्र पश्यन्तौ
पत्तनानि वनानि च ।
गिरींश्च सरितस्सर्वा
राष्ट्राणि नगराणि च ॥
On the way they saw many a town, Vana, mountain, stream, province and city.
3.42.11
समेत्य दण्डकारण्यम्
राघवस्याश्रमं ततः ।
ददर्श सह मारीचो
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ॥
Reaching Daṇḍaka Araṇya along with Māreeca, Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas saw the Āṡrama of Rāma.
3.42.12
अवतीर्य रथात्तस्मात्
ततः काञ्चनभूषणात् ।
हस्ते गृहीत्वा मारीचम्
रावणो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Getting off the chariot that was decorated with gold Rāvaṇa took Māreeca by hand and said these words:
3.42.13
एतत्तदाश्रमपदम्
दृश्यते कदलीवृतम् ।
क्रियतां तत्सखे शीघ्रम्
यदर्थं वयमागताः ॥
The Āṡrama grounds, nestled amidst plantain groves can be seen now. My friend, you shall quickly do what we have come here for.
3.42.14
स रावणवचश्श्रुत्वा
मारीचो राक्षसस्तदा ।
मृगो भूत्वाऽश्रमद्वारि
रामस्य विचचार ह ॥
Hearing those words of Rāvaṇa, Māreeca the Rākshasa took the form of a deer and started to move about in front of the entrance of Rāma’s Āṡrama.
3.42.15
स तु तद्रूपमास्थाय
महदद्भुतदर्शनम् ।
मणिप्रवरशृङ्गाग्रः
सितासितमुखाकृतिः ॥
He adopted a magnificent and marvelous appearance with exquisite gems on the tips of the horns and luminous black and white spots on the face.
3.42.16
रक्तपद्मोत्पलमुख
इन्द्रनीलोत्पलश्रवाः ।
किंचिदभ्युन्नतग्रीव
इंद्रनीलदलाधरः ॥
Its face was shining, one side like red lotus and the other like dark lily. Its ears were like the petals of the blue lily. Its neck was slightly stretched and uplifted. And its lower lip was shining like sapphire.
3.42.17
कुन्देन्दुवज्रसङ्काशम्
उदरं चास्य भास्वरम् ।
मधूकनिभपार्श्वश्च
पद्मकिञ्जल्कसन्निभः ।
वैडूर्यसङ्काशखुरः
तनुजङ्घस्सुसंहतः ॥
Its belly was shining with the sheen of Kunda flower, moon and diamond. Its flanks rivaled the Madhūka flower. The rest of its body resembled the filaments of the lotus in hue. Its hooves were like lazulite. Its waist was tenuous and shapely.
3.42.18
इन्द्रायुधसवर्णेन
पुच्छेनोर्ध्वं विराजता ।
मनोहरस्स्निग्धवर्णो
रत्नैर्नानाविधैर्वृतः ॥
Its tail was lit up with the shine of Indra’s weapon. It was captivating with its silky sheen all over. Its body was speckled with spots that resembled every kind of gem.
3.42.19
क्षणेन राक्षसो जातो
मृगः परमशोभनः ।
वनं प्रज्वलयन्रम्यम्
रामाश्रमपदं च तत् ॥
The Rākshasa turned himself into an extremely beautiful deer instantly lighting up the Vana and Rāma’s Āṡrama grounds.
3.42.20
मनोहरं दर्शनीयम्
रूपं कृत्वा स राक्षसः ।
प्रलोभनार्थं वैदेह्या
नानाधातुविचित्रितम् ।
विचरन्गच्छते तस्मात्
शाद्वलानि समन्ततः ॥
Taking an enticing and captivating form with assorted hues of all minerals and gems to lure Vaidēhee, he went from there, darting all over the green swards.
3.42.22
रूप्यैर्बिन्दुशतैश्चित्रो
भूत्वा स प्रियदर्शनः ।
विटपीनां किसलयान्
भङ्त्क्वाऽदन्विचचार ह ॥
Taking a very attractive form, with hundreds of striking silvery spots, he moved about, browsing the new shoots on the trees.
3.42.23
कदलीगृहकं गत्वा
कर्णिकारानितस्ततः ।
समाश्रयन्मन्दगतिः
सीतासन्दर्शनं तथा ॥
Going to the banana groves and sauntering along the Karṇikāra trees, he moved slowly where Seetā might see him.
3.42.24
राजीवचित्रपृष्ठस्स
विरराज महामृगः ।
रामाश्रमपदाभ्याशे
विचचार यथासुखम् ॥
That majestic beast, its back shining with the lovely tints of lotus, loitered merrily near Rāma’s Āṡrama grounds.
3.42.25
पुनर्गत्वा निवृत्तश्च
विचचार मृगोत्तमः ।
गत्वा मुहूर्तं त्वरया
पुनः प्रतिनिवर्तते ॥
That best of the beasts loitered, going back and forth, again and again. He would go off only for a moment and then return in a great hurry again.
3.42.26
विक्रीडंश्च क्वचिद्भूमौ
पुनरेव निषीदति ।
आश्रमद्वारमागम्य
मृगयूथानि गच्छति ॥
It would play (for a while) and then squat on the ground somewhere. Going up to the entrance of the Āṡrama, it would join the herd of other deer.
3.42.27
मृगयूथैरनुगतः
पुनरेव निवर्तते ।
सीतादर्शनमाकांक्षन्
राक्षसो मृगतां गतः ।
परिभ्रमति चित्राणि
मण्डलानि विनिष्पतन् ॥
Masquerading as a deer, the Rākshasa, wishing to get Seetā’s attention, would follow the herd of other deer and then return. He would take a sudden leap and twirl around in amazing patterns.
3.42.28
समुद्वीक्ष्य च ते सर्वे
मृगा ह्यन्ये वनेचराः ।
उपागम्य समाघ्राय
विद्रवन्ति दिशो दश ॥
All other beasts that roamed the Vana became curious, came near, sniffed and then fled away in all the ten directions.
3.42.29
राक्षसस्सोऽपि तान्वन्यान्
मृगान्मृगवधे रतः ।
प्रच्छादनार्थं भावस्य
न भक्षयति संस्पृशन् ॥
And the Rākshasa, who would have taken delight in killing those beasts of the Vana, would touch but not devour them, to hide his true nature.
3.42.30
तस्मिन्नेव ततः काले
वैदेही शुभलोचना ।
कुसुमावचयव्यग्रा
पादपानभ्यवर्तत ॥
Right at that moment Vaidēhee of lovely eyes came out to pluck flowers from the trees.
3.42.31
कर्णिकारानशोकांश्च
चूतांश्च मदिरेक्षणा ।
कुसुमान्यवचिन्वन्ती
चचार रुचिरानना ॥
She of enchanting looks and charming face went around seeking Karṇikāra, Aṡōka and mango trees, plucking flowers.
3.42.32
अनर्हाऽरण्यवासस्य
सा तं रत्नमयं मृगम् ।
मुक्तामणि विचित्राङ्गम्
ददर्श परमाङ्गना ॥
That woman of extreme feminine delicacy, for whom the forest was no fit place of residence, saw the deer whose limbs seemed to be brilliantly wrought with jewels, pearls and gems.
3.42.33
सा तं रुचिरदन्तोष्ठी
रूप्यधातुतनूरुहम् ।
विस्मयोत्फुल्लनयना
सस्नेहं समुदैक्षत ॥
She of beautiful teeth and lips, her eyes widened in amazement, glanced at the deer whose hair had the hues of silver and every other mineral.
3.42.34
स च तां रामदयिताम्
पश्यन्मायामयो मृगः ।
विचचार पुनश्चित्रम्
दीपयन्निव तद्वनम् ॥
And that magic deer, on seeing Rāma’s wife, darted around seductively again, lighting up that Vana.
3.42.35
अदृष्टपूर्वं तं दृष्ट्वा
नानारत्नमयं मृगम् ।
विस्मयं परमं सीता
जगाम जनकात्मजा ॥
And Seetā, Janaka’s daughter, was wonderstruck on seeing the deer that seemed to be a mass of gems of every kind, the like of which she had never seen before.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे द्विचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः.॥
Thus concludes the forty second Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
