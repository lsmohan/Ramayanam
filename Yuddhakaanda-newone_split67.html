Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 67
In this Sarga, Kumbhakarṇa rampages through the Vānara Army, playing havoc and devouring them. Dwivida, meanwhile, wreaks damage to the Rākshasa forces killing them and their mounts. Hanumān showers rocks and trees on Kumbhakarṇa, which he smashes with ease. Kumbhakarṇa strikes Hanumān with a spear, severely wounding him. Then five Vānara chiefs Ṛshabha, Ṡarabha, Neela, Gavāksha and Gandhamādana, rush to attack Kumbhakarṇa. Kumbhakarṇa knocks all of them down to the ground. Seeing them fall, Vānaras in thousands rush to Kumbhakarṇa and climb on him, whom he simply shakes off and continues to chase and devour the Vānaras.
Then Aṅgada rushes to fight with Kumbhakarṇa and delivers a blow under which Kumbhakarṇa reels and faints. Regaining his senses, Kumbhakarṇa strikes Aṅgada, who then falls unconscious. Kumbhakarṇa then attacks Sugreeva. After a fierce duel between them, Sugreeva falls unconscious, hit with a mountain crest by Kumbhakarṇa. Kumbhakarṇa then carries him off to Laṅkā. Hanumān debates within himself whether he should intervene and free Sugreeva. But then he suspects that Sugreeva, being the king, may not appreciate being saved by someone else. He leaves it to Sugreeva to do what he would after regaining consciousness. Indeed, regaining consciousness, and tearing apart Kumbhakarṇa’s nose and ears, he bounces like a ball and springs into the sky and goes to confer with Rāma.
Kumbhakarṇa returns to the battlefront. With his nose and ears cut off, intoxicated with the smell of blood, he devours Vānaras and Rākshasas indiscriminately. Then Lakshmaṇa takes on Kumbhakarṇa, but his arrows have no affect on him. Ignoring him, Kumbhakarṇa goes past him and rushes towards Rāma.
Then ensues a terrible fight between Rāma and Kumbhakarṇa. Kumbhakarṇa reels under the impact of Rāma's arrows. Meanwhile, Lakshmaṇa suggests that the Vānaras climb on the Rākshasa so that he will no more be able to move with ease under that weight. Vānaras follow Lakshmaṇa’s suggestion. Kumbhakarṇa shakes them off easily. Seeing that, Rāma realizes that Kumbhakarṇa is too dangerous to be given any more time. Seeing that normal arrows are of no effect on him, Rāma shoots an arrow powered with Vāyu's Astra, cutting off one of his arms. Not minding that his arm is cut off, Kumbhakarṇa takes a tree with his other hand and attacks Rāma with it. With an arrow powered by Indra's Astra, Rāma severs his second arm. Then Rāma cuts off both of his legs with two more arrows. Kumbhakarṇa, reduced to head and torso, still attempts to roll on to devour Rāma. Rāma separates the head from the torso with an arrow powered by Indra's Astra. Both his head and torso, as they fall off, knock down many Vānaras.
With Kumbhakarṇa slain, the Vānaras and Dēvas rejoice, and the Rākshasas lament.
6.67.1
ते निवृत्ता महाकायाः
श्रुत्वाऽङ्गदवचस्तदा ।
नैष्ठिकीं बुद्धिमासाद्य
सर्वे सङ्ग्रामकाङ्क्षिणः ॥
Then, on hearing the words of Aṅgada, all of them, of huge bodies, turned back firming up their minds, resolved to fight.
6.67.2
समुदीरितवीर्याश्च
समारोपितविक्रमाः ।
पर्यवस्थापिता वाक्यैः
अङ्गदेन वलीमुखाः ।
प्रयाताश्च गता हर्षम्
मरणे कृतनिश्चयाः ।
चक्रुः सुतुमुलं युद्धम्
वानरास्त्यक्तजीविताः ॥
With their valor reinforced, their prowess reinstated and set on a firm footing by Aṅgada, the Vānaras, regaining their cheer, went on, resolved to fight till death.
The battle that the Vānaras fought without caring for their own lives turned out to be extremely tumultuous.
6.67.4
अथ वृक्षान् महाकायाः
सानूनि सुमहान्ति च ।
वानरास्तूर्णमुद्यम्य
कुम्भकर्णमभिद्रुताः ॥
Then the Vānaras of huge bodies attacked Kumbhakarṇa without wasting any time, holding up huge trees and mountain crests in their hands.
6.67.5
कुम्भकर्णः सुसङ्क्रुद्धो
गदामुद्यम्य वीर्यवान् ।
धर्षयन् स महाकायः
समन्ताद्व्यक्षिपद्रिपून् ॥
Then the valiant Kumbhakarṇa of gigantic body became outraged, and with his mace, he terrorized and tossed the enemies around in all directions.
6.67.6
शतानि सप्त चाष्टौ च
सहस्राणि च वानराः ।
प्रकीर्णाः शेरते भूमौ
कुम्भकर्णेन ताडिताः ॥
Seven to eight hundred Vānaras, and even thousands of them, struck simultaneously and strewn around by Kumbhakarṇa, lay on the ground.
6.67.7
षोडशाष्टौ च दश च
विंशत्त्रिंशत्तथैव च ।
परिक्षिप्य च बाहुभ्याम्
खादन्स परिधावति ।
भक्षयन् भृशसङ्क्रुद्धो
गरुडः पन्नगानिव ॥
He rampaged over the battlefield, grabbing sixteen, eight, ten, twenty or thirty Vānaras at a time with his hands and throwing them into his mouth, as Garuḍa gobbles up snakes.
6.67.8
कृच्छ्रेण च समाश्वस्ताः
सङ्गम्य च ततस्ततः ।
वृक्षाद्रिहस्ता हरयः
तस्थुः सङ्ग्राममूर्धनि ॥
Calming themselves with great effort, the Vānaras gathered in knots here and there, with trees and rocks in their hands, trying to make a stand on the battlefront.
6.67.9
ततः पर्वतमुत्पाट्य
द्विविदः प्लवगर्षभः ।
दुद्राव गिरिशृङ्गाभम्
विलम्ब इव तोयदः ॥
Then, Dwivida, a bull among Vānaras, wrenched a hill and pursued the mountainous foe, looking like a cloud that hung low.
6.67.10
तं समुत्पत्य चिक्षेप
कुम्भकर्णस्य वानरः ।
तमप्राप्य महाकायम्
तस्य सैन्येऽपतत्तदा ॥
He jumped into the air and hurled it at Kumbhakarṇa. Failing to reach him of huge body, it fell on his army.
6.67.11
ममर्दाश्वान् गजांश्चापि
रथांश्चैव नगोत्तमः ।
तानि चान्यानि रक्षांसि
पुनश्चान्यद्गिरेः शिरेः ॥
And that huge rock crushed his horses, elephants and chariots, turning them into pulp. And another mountain crest did the same to other Rākshasas.
6.67.12
तच्छैलशृङ्गाभिहतम्
हताश्वं हतसारथि ।
रक्षसां रुधिरक्लिन्नम्
बभूवायोधनं महत् ॥
With the horses and the charioteers killed struck by that mountain crest, the battlefront of the Rākshasas was drenched in blood.
6.67.13
रथिनो वानरेन्द्राणाम्
शरैः कालान्तकोपमैः ।
शिरांसि नदतां जह्रुः
सहसा भीमनिस्वनाः ॥
The chariot warriors (of the Rākshasa army) who were letting off frightful shouts immediately cut off the heads of the chief Vānaras who were shouting battle-cries, with arrows that fell like Death at the time of the dissolution of the worlds.
6.67.14
वानराश्च महात्मानः
समुत्पाट्य महाद्रुमान् ।
रथानश्वान् गजानुष्ट्रान्
राक्षसानभ्यसूदयन् ॥
And the mighty Vānaras also, wrenching huge trees, destroyed the chariots, horses, elephants, camels and the Rākshasas.
6.67.15
हनुमान् शैलशृङ्गाणि
वृक्षांश्च विविधान् बहून् ।
ववर्ष कुम्भकर्णस्य
शिरस्यम्बरमास्थितः ॥
Hanumān, taking his stand in the sky rained down on Kumbhakarṇa’s head showers of mountain crests and volley of trees of every kind.
6.67.16
तानि पर्वतशृङ्गाणि
शूलेन स बिभेद ह ।
बभञ्ज वृक्षवर्षं च
कुम्भकर्णो महाबलः ॥
Kumbhakarṇa of immense strength shivered the rocks into pieces and broke the trees that rained on him, with his spear.
6.67.17
ततो हरीणां तदनीकमुग्रम्
दुद्राव शूलं निशितं प्रगृह्य ।
तस्थौ स तस्यापततः पुरस्तात्
महीधराग्रं हनुमान् प्रगृह्य ॥
Then he took his sharp spear and chased the fierce army of Vānaras. Just as he began his attack, Hanumān grabbed a mountain peak and blocked him, standing right in front of him.
6.67.18
स कुम्भकर्णं कुपितो जघान
वेगेन शैलोत्तमभीमकायम् ।
स चुक्षुभे तेन तदाऽभिभूतो
मेदार्द्रगात्रो रुधिरावसिक्तः ॥
In his rage, he struck, with all speed, Kumbhakarṇa of frightful body that was huge like a mountain. He was shaken with the impact of that blow, his body drenched with his own gore and fat.
6.67.19
स शूलमाविध्य तडित्प्रकाशं
गिरिर्यथा प्रज्वलिताग्रशृङ्गम् ।
बाह्वन्तरे मारुतिमाजघान
गुहोऽचलं क्रौञ्चमिवोग्रशक्त्या ॥
Then, whirling aloft his spear that shone like lightning and resembled a mountain with a blazing peak, he struck Māruti with it on the chest, as Guha had struck the Krounca mountain with his fierce javelin.
6.67.20
स शूलनिर्भिन्नमहाभूजान्तरः
प्रविह्वलः शोणितमुद्वमन् मुखात् ।
ननाद भीमं हनुमान् महाहवे
युगान्तमेघस्तनितस्वनोपमम् ॥
With his wide chest shattered by the spear, Hanumān was shaken, vomited blood, and let off a terrible yell in that great battle, that was like the thunder of the clouds of the Deluge.
6.67.21
ततो विनेदुः सहसा प्रहृष्टा
रक्षोगणास्तं व्यथितं समीक्ष्य ।
प्लवङ्गमास्तु व्यथिता भयार्ताः
प्रदुद्रुवुः संयति कुम्भकर्णात् ॥
The hordes Rākshasas instantly let off joyous shouts on seeing him thus wounded, while the Vānaras, harassed and gripped by fear, ran away from Kumbhakarṇa, in that battle.
6.67.22
ततस्तु नीलो बलवान्
पर्यवस्थापयन् बलम् ।
प्रविचिक्षेप शैलाग्रम्
कुम्भकर्णाय धीमते ॥
Then the strong Neela held the army together, hurling a mountain crest at the sagacious Kumbhakarṇa.
6.67.23
तमापतन्तं सम्प्रेक्ष्य
मुष्टिनाऽभिजघान ह ।
मुष्टिप्रहाराभिहतम्
तच्छैलाग्रं व्यशीर्यत ।
सविस्फुलिङ्गं सज्वालम्
निपपात महीतले ॥
As soon as he saw it about to fall on him, he struck it with his fist. Struck by the impact of his fist, that mountain peak shivered into pieces and fell on the ground emitting sparks and bursting into flames.
6.67.25
ऋषभः शरभो नीलो
गवाक्षो गन्धमादनः ।
पञ्च वानरशार्दूलाः
कुम्भकर्णमुपाद्रवन् ॥
Then Ṛshabha, Ṡarabha, Neela, Gavāksha and Gandhamādana, the five tigers among Vānaras, rushed to attack Kumbhakarṇa.
6.67.26
शैलैर्वृक्षैस्तलैः पादैः
मुष्टिभिश्च महाबलाः ।
कुम्भकर्णं महाकायम्
सर्वतोऽभिप्रदुद्रुवुः ॥
With rocks and trees, and with their own palms, feet and fists, they, of immense strength, attacked Kumbhakarṇa of gigantic body from all sides.
6.67.27
स्पर्शानिव प्रहारांस्तान्
वेदयानो न विव्यथे ।
ऋषभं तु महावेगम्
बाहुभ्यां परिषस्वजे ॥
Their impact was no more than the touch of a feather for him and hurt him in the least. Then he caught Ṛshabha of terrific speed with his hands and crushed him in his embrace.
6.67.28
कुम्भकर्णभुजाभ्यां तु
पीडितो वानरर्षभः ।
निपपातर्षभो भीमः
प्रमुखागतशोणितः ॥
Ṛshabha, a bull among Vānaras, fierce as he was, fell on the ground, crushed by the arms of Kumbhakarṇa, vomiting blood.
6.67.29
मुष्टिना शरभं हत्वा
जानुना नीलमाहवे ।
आजघान गवाक्षं तु
तलेनेन्द्ररिपुस्तदा ।
पादेनाभ्यहनत् क्रुद्धः
तरसा गन्धमादनम् ॥
The foe of Indra then, in rage, struck down Ṡarabha with his fist, Neela with his knees and Gavāksha with a blow from his open palm, while he kicked Gandhamādana with great force.
6.67.30
दत्तप्रहारव्यथिता
मुमुहुः शोणितोक्षिताः ।
निपेतुस्ते तु मेदिन्याम्
निकृत्ता इव किंशुकाः ॥
Suffering from those blows and feeling dizzy, they fell down on the ground drenched in blood looking like Kiṃṡuka trees that had been cut down.
6.67.31
तेषु वानरमुख्येषु
पतितेषु महात्मसु ।
वानराणां सहस्राणि
कुम्भकर्णं प्रदुद्रुवुः ॥
When those mighty chiefs of Vānaras fell down thus, thousands of Vānaras dashed towards Kumbhakarṇa.
6.67.32
तं शैलमिव शैलाभाः
सर्वे ते प्लवगर्षभाः ।
समारुह्य समुत्पत्य
ददंशुश्च महाबलाः ॥
All those bulls among Vānaras of immense strength, who were huge like mountains, climbed over him like one does a mountain, jumped on him and bit him.
6.67.33
तं नखैर्दशनैश्चापि
मुष्टिभिर्जानुभिस्तथा ।
कुम्भकर्णं महाकायम्
ते जघ्नुः प्लवगर्षभाः ॥
Those bulls among Vānaras struck Kumbhakarṇa of gigantic body, with their fists and knees and tore him with their nails and teeth.
6.67.34
स वानरसहस्रैस्तैः
विचितः पर्वतोपमः ।
रराज राक्षसव्याघ्रो
गिरिरात्मरुहैरिव ॥
With those thousands of Vānaras crowding on him, who was like a mountain, that tiger among Rākshasas shone like a mountain with trees growing on it.
6.67.35
बाहुभ्यां वानरान् सर्वान्
प्रगृह्य सुमहाबलः ।
भक्षयामास सङ्क्रुद्धो
गरुडः पन्नगानिव ॥
He of extremely great strength, grabbed all those Vānaras with his hands and, in rage, devoured them as Garuḍa gobbles up snakes.
6.67.36
प्रक्षिप्ताः कुम्भकर्णेन
वक्त्रे पातालसन्निभे ।
नासापुटाभ्यां निर्जग्मुः
कर्णाभ्यां चैव वानराः ॥
Kumbhakarṇa then threw them into his mouth, which was deep like the nether world. The Vānaras then came out of his ears and nostrils.
6.67.37
भक्षयन् भृशसङ्क्रुद्धो
हरीन् पर्वतसन्निभः ।
बभञ्ज वानरान् सर्वान्
सङ्क्रुद्धो राक्षसोत्तमः ॥
He, who looked like a mountain, devoured the Vānaras in rage. That eminent and outraged Rākshasa tore all the Vānaras into pieces.
6.67.38
मांसशोणितसङ्क्लेदाम्
भूमिं कुर्वन् स राक्षसः ।
चचार हरिसैन्येषु
कालाग्निरिव मूर्च्छितः ॥
Raging like the fire of Dissolution, he stormed through the Vānara army turning the ground miry with flesh and blood.
6.67.39
वज्रहस्तो यथा शक्रः
पाशहस्त इवान्तकः ।
शूलहस्तो बभौ सङ्ख्ये
कुम्भकर्णो महाबलः ॥
Armed with spear on that battlefield, Kumbhakarṇa of immense strength looked like Ṡakra armed with the Vajra and Yama armed with the noose of death.
6.67.40
यथा शुष्काण्यरण्यानि
ग्रीष्मे दहति पावकः ।
तथा वानरसैन्यानि
कुम्भकर्णो विनिर्दहत् ॥
Like fires burn up dry forests in summer, Kumbhakarṇa burnt up the Vānara forces.
6.67.41
ततस्ते वध्यमानास्तु
हतयूथा विनायकाः ।
वानरा भयसंविग्ना
विनेदुर्विकृतैः स्वरैः ॥
As whole companies were destroyed thus, the Vānaras, finding themselves leaderless, wailed aloud in eerie voices, consumed by fright.
6.67.42
अनेकशो वध्यमानाः
कुम्भकर्णेन वानराः ।
राघवं शरणं जग्मुः
व्यथिताः खिन्नचेतसः ॥
Tormented by Kumbhakarṇa in many ways, suffering and hearts broken, the Vānaras went to Rāghava seeking refuge.
6.67.43
प्रभग्नान् वानरान् दृष्ट्वा
वज्रहस्तसुतात्मजः ।
अभ्यधावत वेगेन
कुम्भकर्णं महाहवे ॥
Meanwhile, seeing the Vānaras completely beaten, the son of the son of Indra, (Aṅgada) went in all swiftness to attack Kumbhakarṇa in that great battle.
6.67.44
शैलशृङ्गं महद्गृह्य
विनदन् स मुहुर्मुहुः ।
त्रासयन् राक्षसान् सर्वान्
कुम्भकर्णपदानुगान् ।
चिक्षेप शैलशिखरम्
कुम्भकर्णस्य मूर्धनि ॥
Snatching a huge mountain peak, he let off shout after shout, terrifying all the Rākshasas that followed Kumbhakarṇa, and hurled that mountain peak at his head.
6.67.46
स तेनाभिहतो मूर्ध्नि
शैलेनेन्द्ररिपुस्तदा ।
कुम्भकर्णः प्रजज्वाल
कोपेन महता तदा ।
सोऽभ्यधावत वेगेन
वालिपुत्रममर्षणम् ॥
Hit hard by that mountain crest on the head, Kumbhakarṇa, the foe of Indra, blazing with extreme outrage, dashed onto the son of Vāli, who was also indignant.
6.67.47
कुम्भकर्णो महानादः
त्रासयन् सर्ववानरान् ।
शूलं ससर्ज वै रोषात्
अङ्गदे स महावलः ॥
Frightening the Vānaras with a terrific shout, Kumbhakarṇa of immense strength hurled his spear at Aṅgada, in all fury.
6.67.48
तमापतन्तं बुद्ध्वा तु
युद्धमार्गविशारदः ।
लाघवान्मोचयामास
बलवान् वानरर्षभः ॥
Seeing it coming, the strong bull among Vānaras, a savant of all techniques of fight, escaped from it skillfully.
6.67.49
उत्पत्य चैनं सहसा
तलेनोरस्यताडयत् ।
स तेनाभिहतः कोपात्
प्रमुमोहाचलोपमः ॥
Then he swiftly leaped into the air and landed a blow on his chest with his open palm. Under that blow delivered in rage, he, who was like a mountain, fainted.
6.67.50
स लब्धसञ्ज्ञोऽतिबलो
मुष्टिं संगृह्य राक्षसः ।
अपहासेन चिक्षेप
विसञ्ज्ञः स पपात ह ॥
Regaining his senses, that extremely powerful Rākshasa doubled his fist and lodged it on him with a mocking laugh, making him fall unconscious.
6.67.51
तस्मिन् प्लवगशार्दूले
विसञ्ज्ञे पतिते भुवि ।
तच्छूलं समुपादाय
सुग्रीवमभिदुद्रुवे ॥
Once that tiger among Vānaras fell on the ground unconscious, he (Kumbhakarṇa) took his spear and dashed towards Sugreeva.
6.67.52
तमापतन्तं सम्प्रेक्ष्य
कुम्भकर्णं महाबलम् ।
उत्पपात तदा वीरः
सुग्रीवो वानराधिपः ॥
On seeing Kumbhakarṇa of immense strength coming on, the valiant Sugreeva, king of the Vānaras, sprang up.
6.67.53
स पर्वताग्रमुत्क्षिप्य
समाविध्य महाबलः ।
अभिदुद्राव वेगेन
कुम्भकर्णं महाबलम् ॥
He of immense strength snatched a mountain crest and holding it firmly ran with great speed towards Kumbhakarṇa of immense strength.
6.67.54
तमापतन्तं सम्प्रेक्ष्य
कुम्भकर्णः प्लवङ्गमम् ।
तस्थौ विवृतसर्वाङ्गो
वानरेन्द्रस्य संमुखः ॥
Seeing the Vānara coming, Kumbhakarṇa stood in front of the king of Vānaras baring all his limbs defiantly.
6.67.55
कपिशोणितदिग्धाङ्गम्
भक्षयन्तं प्लवङ्गमान् ।
कुम्भकर्णं स्थितं दृष्ट्वा
सुग्रीवो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing Kumbhakarṇa, all of whose limbs were smeared with blood, standing devouring the Vānaras, Sugreeva said these words:
6.67.56
पातिताश्च त्वया वीराः
कृतं कर्म सुदुष्करम् ।
भक्षितानि च सैन्यानि
प्राप्तं ते परमं यशः ॥
You have brought down many a heroic warrior, and done extraordinary feats; you have devoured our armies and signal glory has become yours.
6.67.57
त्यज तद्वानरानीकम्
प्राकृतैः किं करिष्यसि ।
सह स्वैकं निपातं मे
पर्वतस्यास्य राक्षस ॥
Leave that Vānara army alone, why busy yourself with ordinary folks? See if you can withstand a single assault with this rock that I shall heave at you, O Rākshasa!
6.67.58
तद्वाक्यं हरिराजस्य
सत्त्वधैर्यसमन्वितम् ।
श्रुत्वा राक्षसशार्दूलः
कुम्भकर्णोऽब्रवीद्वचः ॥
On hearing those words of the King of the Vānaras that betrayed grit and courage, Kumbhakarṇa, a tiger among Rākshasas, said these words:
6.67.59
प्रजापतेस्तु पौत्रस्त्वम्
तथैवर्क्षरजस्सुतः ।
धृतिपौरुषसम्पन्नः
कस्माद्गर्जसि वानर ॥
Sure, you are the grandson of the Progenitor and the son of Ṛksharajas and endowed with grit and valor; but why do you shout, O Vānara!
6.67.60
स कुम्भकर्णस्य वचो निशम्य
व्याविध्य शैलं सहसा मुमोच ।
तेनाजघानोरसि कुम्भकर्णं
शैलेन वज्राशनिसन्निभेन ॥
On hearing those words of Kumbhakarṇa he held the rock tight and hurled at him quickly. He hit Kumbhakarṇa on the chest with that rock, which was powerful like Vajra and thunderbolt.
6.67.61
तच्छैलशृङ्गं सहसा विशीर्णं
भुजान्तरे तस्य तदा विशाले ।
ततो विषेदुः सहसा प्लवङ्गा
रक्षोगणाश्चापि मुदा विनेदुः ॥
That mountain crest shivered to bits on his spacious chest. Then the Vānaras were dismayed, but the Rākshasas shouted with joy.
6.67.62
स शैलशृङ्गाभिहतश्चुकोप
ननाद रोषाच्च विवृत्य वक्त्रम् ।
व्याविध्य शूलं च तडित्प्रकाशम्
चिक्षेप हर्यृक्षपतेर्वधाय ॥
Incensed at being struck by the mountain crest, he opened his mouth wide and yelled, showing his annoyance. Firmly gripping his spear that shone like lightning, he threw it to kill the ruler of the Vānaras and the Ṛkshas.
6.67.63
तत्कुम्भकर्णस्य भुजप्रणुन्नम्
शूलं शितं काञ्चनदामजुष्टम् ।
क्षिप्रं समुत्पत्य निगृह्य दोर्भ्यां
बभञ्ज वेगेन सुतोऽनिलस्य ॥
The son of Vāyu swiftly sprang up and caught with both his hands the sharp spear that was adorned with chains of gold and was thrown by Kumbhakarṇa, and broke it into pieces.
Eastern edition has it that the spear was broken by Sugreeva himself, which is more appropriate, for a third person would not interject himself when two great warriors are engaged in a duel. One like Hanumān would certainly abstain from intervening, as is aptly portrayed in Ṡlōkas 74 through 83 below.
6.67.64
कृतं भारसहस्रस्य
शूलं कालायसं महत् ।
बभञ्ज जानुन्यारोप्य
तदा हृष्टः प्लवंगमः ॥
The Vānara pressed that iron spear, which weighed a thousand units, against his knee and broke it and rejoiced.
6.67.65
शूलं भग्नं हनुमता
दृष्ट्वा वानरवाहिनी ।
हृष्टा ननाद बहुशः
सर्वतश्चापि दुद्रुवे ॥
Seeing the spear thus broken by Hanumān, the Vānara army came running from every direction and shouted repeatedly with joy.
6.67.66
सिंहनादं च ते चक्रुः
प्रहृष्टा वनगोचराः ।
मारुतिं पूजयाञ्चक्रुः
दृष्ट्वा शूलं तथागतम् ॥
The Vana dwellers, in their joy, roared like lions. They sang praises of Māruti, seeing the spear broken thus.
6.67.67
स तत्तदा भग्नमवेक्ष्य शूलं
चुकोप रक्षोधिपतिर्महात्मा ।
उत्पाट्य लङ्कामलयात् स शृङ्गं
जघान सुग्रीवमुपेत्य तेन ॥
Seeing the spear thus broken, the outraged leader of Rākshasas, a Mahātma, snatched a peak from the Malaya mountain of Laṅkā, went to Sugreeva, and struck him with it.
6.67.68
स शैलशृङ्गाभिहतो विसञ्ज्ञः
पपात भूमौ युधि वानरेन्द्रः ।
तं प्रेक्ष्य भूमौ पतितं विसञ्ज्ञम्
नेदुः प्रहृष्टास्त्वथ यातुधानाः ॥
Struck by that mountain crest, the Lord of Vānaras fell on the ground unconscious. Seeing him thus fall on the ground unconscious, the Rākshasas shouted with joy.
6.67.69
तमभ्युपेत्याद्भुतघोरवीर्यम्
स कुम्भकर्णो युधि वानरेन्द्रम् ।
जहार सुग्रीवमभिप्रगृह्य
यथाऽनिलो मेघमतिप्रचण्डः ॥
In that fight, Kumbhakarṇa then went to the Lord of Vānaras of amazing and frightful valor, lifted him up and carried him off, like a fierce gale does a cloud.
6.67.70
स तं महामेघनिकाशरूपम्
उत्पाट्य गच्छन् युधि कुम्भकर्णः ।
रराज मेरुप्रतिमानरूपो
मेरुर्यथाऽभ्युच्छ्रितघोरशृङ्गः ॥
As he thus lifted and carried him off who shone like a huge cloud, Kumbhakarṇa, who was like Mēru, shone like Mēru with its tallest and most forbidding peak.
6.67.71
ततस्तमादाय जगाम वीरः
संस्तूयमानो युधि राक्षसेन्द्रैः ।
शृण्वन्निनादं त्रिदिवालयानाम्
प्लवङ्गराजग्रहविस्मितानाम् ॥
Extolled by the great Rākshasas on the battlefield, the heroic one took him away, hearing on his way the clamor of the dwellers of Heaven who were taken aback by the capture of the King of the Vānaras.
6.67.72
ततस्तमादाय तदा स मेने
हरीन्द्रमिन्द्रोपममिन्द्रवीर्यः ।
अस्मिन् हते सर्वमिदं हतं स्यात्
सराघवं सैन्यमितीन्द्रशत्रुः ॥
Taking the Lord of Vānaras, a peer of Indra, with him, the foe of Indra, of valor equal to that of Indra, thought that by knocking him down, the entire Vānara army including Rāghava was knocked down.
6.67.73
विद्रुतां वाहिनीं दृष्ट्वा
वानराणां ततस्ततः ।
कुम्भकर्णेन सुग्रीवम्
गृहीतं चापि वानरम् ।
हनुमांश्चिन्तयामास
मतिमान् मारुतात्मजः ॥
Seeing the Vānara army scatter in all directions, and Sugreeva carried off by Kumbhakarṇa, Hanumān, the sagacious son of Vāyu, thought:
6.67.75
एवं गृहीते सुग्रीवे
किं कर्तव्यं मया भवेत् ।
यद्वि न्याय्यं मया कर्तुम्
तत् करिष्यामि सर्वथा ।
भूत्वा पर्वतसङ्काशो
नाशयिष्यामि राक्षसम् ॥
With Sugreeva being captured thus, what am I expected to do? Whatever is justifiably expected of me, I will do by all means. I will assume a form of mountainous size and will destroy this Rākshasa.
6.67.76
मया हते संयति कुम्भकर्णे
महाबले मुष्टिविशीर्णदेहे ।
विमोचिते वानरपार्थिवे च
भवन्तु हृष्टाः प्लवगाः समस्ताः ॥
With Kumbhakarṇa of immense strength slain by me in the fight, with his body pulverized by the blows from my fist, and with the king of Vānaras freed, all the Vānaras shall rejoice.
6.67.77
अथवा स्वयमप्येष
मोक्षं प्राप्स्यति पार्थिवः ।
गृहीतोऽयं यदि भवेत्
त्रिदशैः सासुरोरगैः ॥
But the King is quite capable of freeing himself even if he was caught by Dēvas, Asuras and Uragas combined.
6.67.78
मन्ये न तावदात्मानम्
बुद्ध्यते वानराधिपः ।
शैलप्रहाराभिहतः
कुम्भकर्णेन संयुगे ॥
I think the ruler of the Vānaras does not yet know what had happened, having lost consciousness from the blow by that rock hurled by Kumbhakarṇa in the fight.
6.67.79
अयं मुहूर्तात् सुग्रीवो
लब्धसञ्ज्ञो महाहवे ।
आत्मनो वानराणां च
यत् पथ्यं तत् करिष्यति ॥
Sugreeva should recover his senses in a while, and do whatever is good for himself and for the Vānaras.
6.67.80
मया तु मोक्षितस्यास्य
सुग्रीवस्य महात्मनः ।
अप्रीतिश्च भवेत् कष्टा
कीर्तिनाशश्च शाश्वतः ॥
Sugreeva, a Mahātma, will be greatly displeased if freed by me, and his reputation will suffer permanent damage.
6.67.81
तस्मान्मुहूर्तं काङ्क्षिष्ये
विक्रमं पार्थिवस्य तु ।
भिन्नं च वानरानीकम्
तावदाश्वासयाम्यहम् ॥
So I shall wait for a while for the King to exert his prowess, and meanwhile apply myself to rallying the scattered Vānara forces.
6.67.82
इत्येवं चिन्तयित्वाथ
हनुमान् मारुतात्मजः ।
भूयः संस्तम्भयामास
वानराणां महाचमूम् ॥
Having thus resolved, Hanumān, the son of Māruta, restored order in the great army of Vānaras again.
6.67.83
स कुम्भकर्णोऽथ विवेश लङ्काम्
स्फुरन्तमादाय महाहरिं तम् ।
विमानचर्यागृहगोपुरस्थैः
पुष्पाग्र्यवर्षैरवकीर्यमाणः ॥
Kumbhakarṇa entered Laṅkā carrying the great Vānara, who was barely moving. He was showered with splendid flowers by those who were on the lofty mansions, sentry posts along the pathways and on the towers at the entrance.
6.67.84
लाजगन्धोदवर्षैस्तु
सिच्यमानः शनैःशनैः ।
राजमार्गस्य शीतत्वात्
संज्ञां प्राप महाबलः ॥
Sugreeva of immense strength regained consciousness slowly, as the showers of puffed rice and sandal water fell on him, and as the cool breeze of the royal pathway touched him.
6.67.85
ततः स सञ्ज्ञामुपलभ्य कृच्छ्रात्
बलीयसस्तस्य भुजान्तरस्थः ।
अवेक्षमाणः पुरराजमार्गं
विचिन्तयामास मुहुर्महात्मा ॥
Regaining his senses with difficulty, the mighty Sugreeva, caught between the hands of that strong one, reflected, observing the royal pathway of the city:
6.67.86
एवं गृहीतेन कथं नु नाम
शक्यं मया सम्प्रतिकर्तुमद्य ।
तथा करिष्यामि यथा हरीणां
भविष्यतीष्टं च हितं च कार्यम् ॥
What can I possibly do now, caught like this? It behooves me to do what the Vānaras would like and what is good for them.
6.67.87
ततः कराग्रैः सहसा समेत्य
राजा हरीणाममरेन्द्रशत्रुम् ।
खरैश्च कर्णौ दशनैश्च नासां
ददंश पादैर्विददार पार्श्वौ ॥
Then, the king of Vānaras quickly tore apart the ears of the foe of the lord of Dēvas with his sharp nails, nose with his teeth and the sides with his feet.
6.67.88
स कुम्भकर्णो हृतकर्णनासो
विदारितस्तेन रदैर्नखैश्च ।
रोषाभिभूतः क्षतजार्द्रगात्रः
सुग्रीवमाविध्य पिपेष भूमौ ॥
His ears and nose gone, torn by teeth and nails, his body wet with blood flowing from them, Kumbhakarṇa, feeling humiliated, hurled Sugreeva on the ground and squashed him.
6.67.89
स भूतले भीमबलाभिपिष्टः
सुरारिभिस्तैरभिहन्यमानः ।
जगाम खं कन्दुकवज्जवेन
पुनश्च रामेण समाजगाम ॥
Squashed thus on the ground by him of formidable strength and attacked by the foes of the Dēvas, he bounced like a ball, took to the skies and went back to confer with Rāma.
6.67.90
कर्णनासाविहीनस्तु
कुम्भकर्णो महाबलः ।
रराज शोणितैः सिक्तो
गिरिः प्रस्रवणैरिव ॥
With no ears and nose, and drenched in blood, Kumbhakarṇa of immense strength shone like a mountain with cascades pouring from it.
6.67.91
शोणितार्द्रो महाकायो
राक्षसो भीमविक्रमः ।
युद्धायाभिमुखो भूयो
मनश्चके महाबलः ॥
The Rākshasa of mountainous body, fierce prowess and immense strength, his body drenched in blood, turned his mind towards the fight again.
6.67.92
अमर्षाच्छोणितोद्गरी
शुशुभे रावणानुजः ।
नीलाञ्जनचयप्रख्यः
ससन्ध्य इव तोयदः ॥
Rāvaṇa’s younger brother, black as a mound of collyrium, spewing blood in his rage, shone like a cloud in the evening sky.
6.67.93
गते तु तस्मिन् सुरराजशत्रुः
क्रोधात् प्रदुद्राव रणाय भूयः ।
अनायुधोऽस्मीति विचिन्त्य रौद्रो
घोरं तदा मुद्गरमाससाद ॥
With him (Sugreeva) escaping thus, the foe of the king of the Dēvas rushed back to the fray in wrath. Realizing that he had no weapon, he grabbed a formidable Mudgara.
6.67.94
ततः स पुर्याः सहसा महौजा
निष्क्रम्य तद्वानरसैन्यमुग्रम् ।
बभक्ष रक्षो युधि कुम्भकर्णः
प्रजा युगान्ताग्निरिव प्रवृद्धः ॥
Quickly emerging from the city, Kumbhakarṇa, the Rākshasa of immense power, devoured the fierce Vānara army on the battlefield, as the blazing conflagration consumes all creatures when the Yuga comes to an end.
6.67.95
बुभुक्षितः शोणितमांसगृध्नुः
प्रविश्य तद्वानरसैन्यमुग्रम् ।
चखाद रक्षांसि हरीन् पिशाचान्
ऋक्षांश्च मोहाद्युधि कुम्भकर्णः ।
यथैव मृत्युर्हरते युगान्ते
स भक्षयामास हरींश्च मुख्यान् ॥
Being hungry and salivating for blood and flesh, Kumbhakarṇa entered those formidable Vānara forces and gobbled up in his muddled state, Rākshasas, Vānaras, Piṡācas and Ṛkshas indiscriminately. He devoured the Vānara chiefs, like Death when the Yuga comes to an end.
6.67.97
एकं द्वे त्रीन् बहून् क्रुद्धो
वानरान् सह राक्षसैः ।
समादायैकहस्तेन
प्रचिक्षेप त्वरन् मुखे ॥
Picking up with one hand the Vānaras as well as Rākshasas, in ones, twos, threes and multiples, he threw them into his mouth quickly.
6.67.98
सम्प्रस्रवंस्तदा मेदः
शोणितं च महाबलः ।
वध्यमानो नगेन्द्राग्रैः
भक्षयामास वानरान् ॥
With fat and blood dribbling from his mouth, he, of immense strength, went on devouring the Vānaras, while being assailed with mountain crests.
6.67.99
ते भक्ष्यमाणा हरयो
रामं जग्मुस्तदा गतिम् ।
कुम्भकर्णो भृशं क्रुद्धः
कपीन् खादन् प्रधावति ॥
As they were being eaten up thus, the Vānaras sought refuge with Rāma, while Kumbhakarṇa in his rage pursued and devoured them.
6.67.100
शतानि सप्त चाष्टौ च
विंशत्त्रिंशत्तथैव च ।
सम्परिष्वज्य बाहुभ्याम्
खादन् विपरिधावति ॥
He chased and took a hug-full of them, seven, eight, twenty or thirty or a hundred at a time, and devoured them.
6.67.101
तस्मिन् काले सुमित्रायाः
पुत्रः परबलार्दनः ।
चकार लक्ष्मणः क्रुद्धो
युद्धं परपुरञ्जयः ॥
Then Lakshmaṇa, Sumitrā's son, harrier of enemy forces and conquerer of enemy cities, entered the fray in anger.
These Ṡlōkas, which describe Kumbhakarṇa's encounter with Lakshmaṇa, and his appreciation of Lakshmaṇa's valor are considered unauthentic.
6.67.102
स कुम्भकर्णस्य शरान्
शरीरे सप्त वीर्यवान् ।
निचखानाददे चान्यान्
विससर्ज च लक्ष्मणः ॥
Lakshmaṇa, the valiant one, lodged seven arrows deep in Kumbhakarṇa's body. Then he grabbed a few more and shot them too.
6.67.103
पीड्यमानस्तदस्त्रम् तु
विशेषं तत्स राक्षसः ।
ततश्चुकोप बलवान्
सुमित्रानंन्दवर्धनः ॥
The Rākshasa was hurt by those arrows, but they were of no consequence. Then the strong one, Sumitrā's perpetual joy, became angry.
6.67.104
अथास्य कवचं शुभ्रम्
जाम्बूनदमयं शुभम् ।
प्रच्छादयामास शरैः
संध्याभ्रमिव मारुतः ॥
He covered the well-wrought and splendid armor made with gold purified in fire (of Kumbhakarṇa) with arrows, as the wind covers the sky with evening clouds.
6.67.105
नीलाञ्जनचयः प्रख्यः
शरैः काञ्चनभूषणैः ।
आपीड्यमानः शुशुभे
मेघैः सूर्य इवांशुमान् ॥
He, who was black as antimony, covered with arrows decorated with gold, shone like a cloud with the rays of the sun gleaming all around it.
6.67.106
ततः स राक्षसो भीमः
सुमित्रानन्दवर्धनम् ।
सावज्ञमेव प्रोवाच
वाक्यं मेघौघनिःस्वनः ॥
Then the Rākshasa tried to patronize Sumitrā's perpetual joy with these words, his voice sounding like thunder from a bank of clouds:
6.67.107
अन्तकस्याप्यकष्टेन
युधिजेतारमाहवे ।
युध्यतामामभीतेन
ख्यापिता वीरता त्वया ॥
By fighting fearlessly with me, who has won over even Yama with ease, you have established your valor.
6.67.108
प्रगृहीतायुधस्येह
मृत्योरिव महामृधे ।
तिष्टलन्नप्रग्रतः पूज्यः
किमु युद्धप्रदायकः ॥
One must be well applauded even for standing in front of me, who stood armed here in this great battle like Death himself. What to speak then, of one who actually gives me a fight?
6.67.109
ऐरावतं समारूढो
वृतः सर्वामरैः प्रभुः ।
नैव शक्रोऽपि समरे
स्थितपूर्वः कदाचन ॥
Even Lord Ṡakra, riding the elephant Airāvata, and followed by all the Dēvas could not make a stand against me in battle, at any time so far.
6.67.110
अद्य त्वयाहं सौमित्रे
बलेनापि पराक्रमैः ।
तोषितो गन्तुमिच्छामि
त्वामनुज्ञाप्य राघवम् ॥
You have made me happy, O son of Sumitrā, with your strength and prowess; but I would like to go to (fight) Rāghava, with your permission.
6.67.111
यत्तु वीर्यबलोत्साहैः
तोषितोऽहं रणे त्वया ।
राममेवैकमिच्छामि
हन्रुं यस्मिन् हते हतम् ॥
I am pleased by the valor, strength and spirit with which you fought; but I would like to slay Rāma alone, for, once he is slain, everyone else is slain.
6.67.112
रामे मयात्र निहते
येऽन्ये स्थास्यन्ति संयुगे ।
तानहं योधयिष्यामि
स्वबलेन प्रमाथिना ॥
Once Rāma is slain by me there, I will have my forces that wreak havoc, fight whoever else still makes a stand in the battle.
6.67.113
इत्युक्तवाक्यं तद्रक्षः
प्रोवाच स्तुतिसंहितम् ।
मृथे घोरतरं वाक्यं
सौमित्रिः प्रहसन्निव ॥
When the Rākshasa said those words in the middle of the battle mixing a bit of praise in them, Sowmitri smiled and gave an unmistakably dire warning:
6.67.114
यस्त्वं शक्रादिभिर्वीरैः
असह्यः प्राप्य पौरुषम् ।
तत्सत्यं नान्यथा वीर
दृष्टस्तेऽद्य पराक्रमः ।
एष दाशरथी रामः
तिष्ठत्यद्रिरिवाचलः ॥
It is true that you have the prowess that Ṡakra and the likes could not have withstood. It, of course, cannot be otherwise, as that prowess, O Veera, is clearly on show today. But the one who is standing (there) firm like a mountain, is Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha!
6.67.115
इति श्रुत्वा ह्यनादृत्य
लक्ष्मणं स निशाचरः ।
अतिक्रम्य च सौमित्रिम्
कुम्भकर्णो महाबलः ।
राममेवाभिदुद्राव
कम्पयन्निव मेदिनीम् ॥
Hearing those words and ignoring Lakshmaṇa, Kumbhakarṇa, the Night-rover of immense strength, went past Sowmitri, dashing towards Rāma, shaking the earth (underneath his feet).
6.67.117
अथ दाशरथी रामो
रौद्रमस्त्रं प्रयोजयन् ।
कुम्भकर्णस्य हृदये
ससर्ज निशितान् शरान् ॥
And Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, invoking Rudra's Astra, shot sharp arrows aiming at the heart of Kumbhakarṇa.
6.67.118
तस्य रामेण विद्धस्य
सहसाऽभिप्रधावतः ।
अङ्गारमिश्राः क्रुद्धस्य
मुखान्निश्चेरुरर्चिषः ॥
Struck by Rāma, he immediately dashed towards him, spewing fire and sparks from his mouth in rage.
6.67.119
रामास्त्रविद्धो घोरं वै
नदन् राक्षसपुङ्गवः ।
अभ्यधावत सङ्क्रुद्धो
हरीन् विद्रावयन् रणे ॥
Struck by that dreadful Astra of Rāma, the bull among Rākshasas was outraged and let off a terrible yell and rushed upon the Vānaras, driving them helter-skelter.
6.67.120
तस्योरसि निमग्नाश्च
शरा बर्हिणवाससः ।
हस्ताच्चापि परिभ्रष्टा
पपातोर्व्यां महागदा ।
आयुधानि च सर्वाणि
विप्रकीर्यन्त भूतले ॥
The arrows with peacock feathers got lodged deeply in his chest. He lost his grip on the mace in his hand and it fell down on the earth. So did the other weapons, which were strewn all over the ground.
6.67.121
स निरायुधमात्मानम्
यदा मेने महाबलः ।
मुष्टिभ्यां चरणाभ्यां च
चकार कदनं महत् ॥
As soon as he realized that he was left without weapons, he fought vigorously with his fists and feet.
6.67.122
स बाणैरतिविद्धाङ्गः
क्षतजेन समुक्षितः ।
रुधिरं परिसुस्राव
गिरिः प्रस्रवणं यथा ॥
Struck badly by the arrows, his body was drenched in blood. The blood poured out (from him) like cascades from a mountain.
6.67.123
स तीर्वेण च कोपेन
रुधिरेण च मूर्च्छितः ।
वानरान् राक्षसानृक्षान्
खादन् विपरिधावति ॥
Dizzy with extreme anger and loss of blood, he ran about aimlessly, eating up Vānaras, Rākshasas and Ṛkshas.
6.67.124
अथ शृङ्गं समाविध्य
भीमं भीमपराक्रमः ।
चिक्षेप राममुद्दिश्य
बलवानन्तकोपमः ॥
Then he of great strength, ferocious and of formidable prowess, looking like Yama himself, grabbed a mountain peak and hurled it, aiming at Rāma.
These four Ṡlōkas seem to be out of place. The flow would be better if they are not present. This could be due to compilers retaining Ṡlōkas from multiple palm-leaf versions.
6.67.125
अप्राप्तमन्तरा रामः
सप्तभिस्तमजिह्मगैः ।
चिच्छेद गिरिशृंङ्गं तम्
पुन्ःसंधायकार्मुकम् ॥
But before it could reach him, Rāma strung his bow again and with seven arrows, cut that mountain peak into pieces.
6.67.126
ततस्तु रामो धर्मात्मा
तस्य शृंङ्गं महत्तदा ।
शरैः काञ्चनचित्राङ्गैः
चिच्छेद भरताग्रजः ॥
Then Rāma, a Dharmātma and elder brother of Bharata, cut that peak to pieces with arrows adorned with gold.
6.67.127
तन्मेरुशिखराकारम्
द्योतमानमिव श्रिया ।
द्वे शते वानरेन्द्राणाम्
पतमानमपातयत् ॥
That peak, which resembled a splendidly shining peak of Mēru Mountain, knocked down two hundred Vānaras as it fell.
6.67.128
तस्मिन् काले स धर्मात्मा
लक्ष्मणो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ।
कुम्भकर्णवधे युक्तो
योगान् परिमृशन् बहून् ॥
Meanwhile, Lakshmaṇa, a Dharmātma, who, focused on slaying Kumbhakarṇa, was thinking of various Upāyas, said these words:
6.67.129
नैवायं वानरान् राजन्
न विजानाति राक्षसान् ।
मत्तः शोणितगन्धेन
स्वान् परांश्चैव खादति ॥
O king! He does not know who is a Vānara and who is a Rākshasa; intoxicated with the smell of blood he eats up his own folk as well as the foe.
6.67.130
साध्वेनमधिरोहन्तु
सर्वतो वानरर्षभाः ।
यूथपाश्च यथा मुख्याः
तिष्ठन्त्वस्य समन्ततः ॥
Let all the bulls among Vānaras climb upon him from all sides, and let the captains and other prominent Vānaras of the divisions take position all around him.
6.67.131
अप्ययं दुर्मतिः काले
गुरुभारप्रपीडितः ।
प्रचरन् राक्षसो भूमौ
नान्यान् हन्यात् प्लवङ्गमान् ॥
Then, this evil one, oppressed by the weight (of the Vānaras), could only crawl on the ground, but not kill the other Vānaras.
6.67.132
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राजपुत्रस्य धीमतः ।
ते समारुरुहुर्हृष्टाः
कुम्भकर्णं प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
Hearing those words of the sagacious prince, the Vānaras climbed on Kumbhakarṇa with excitement.
6.67.133
कुम्भकर्णस्तु सङ्क्रुद्धः
समारूढः प्लवङ्गमैः ।
व्यधूनयत्तान् वेगेन
दुष्टहस्तीव हस्तिपान् ॥
Kumbhakarṇa, irritated, shook off those Vānaras who climbed upon him, as a rogue elephant tosses aside its keepers.
6.67.134
तान् दृष्ट्वा निर्धुतान् रामो
दुष्टोऽयमिति राक्षसः ।
सुमुत्पपात वेगेन
धनुरुत्तममाददे ॥
Seeing them thus being shaken off, Rāma, thinking to himself, ‘this Rākshasa is dangerous’, picked up his great bow and bore down upon him in a hurry.
6.67.135
क्रोधताम्रेक्षणो वीरो
निर्दहन्निव चक्षुषा ।
राघवो राक्षसं रोषात्
अभिदुद्राव वेगितः ।
यूथपान् हर्षयन् सर्वान्
कुम्भकर्णभयार्दितान् ॥
With eyes that were blood-red with anger, that alone seemed to burn everyone, the heroic Rāghava pursued the Rākshasa swiftly, while all the Vānara chiefs, who were oppressed by the fear of Kumbhakarṇa, rejoiced.
6.67.137
स चापमादाय भुजङ्गकल्पं
दृढज्यमुग्रं तपनीयचित्रम् ।
हरीन् समाश्वास्य समुत्पपात
रामो निबद्धोत्तमतूणबाणः ॥
Taking the bow that was tied with a taut string, and was decorated with gold purified in fire, and looked like a serpent, and binding on his shoulder a splendid quiver full of arrows, Rāma proceeded swiftly comforting the Vānaras.
6.67.138
स वानरगणैस्तैस्तु
वृतः परमदुर्जयः ।
लक्ष्मणानुचरो रामः
सम्प्रतस्थे महाबलः ॥
Rāma of immense strength, whom it was impossible to defeat, went, attended by hordes of Vānaras and followed by Lakshmaṇa of immense strength.
6.67.139
स ददर्श महात्मानम्
किरीटिनमरिन्दमम् ।
शोणिताप्लुतसर्वाङ्गम्
कुम्भकर्णं महाबलम् ॥
And then he saw Kumbhakarṇa, a Mahātma of immense strength, and a subduer of the foe, wearing a crown and every limb of his drenched in blood.
6.67.140
सर्वान् समभिधावन्तम्
यथा रुष्टं दिशागजम् ।
मार्गमाणं हरीन् क्रुद्धम्
राक्षसैः परिवारितम् ॥
As he attacked everybody, he looked like an enraged elephant of the cardinals. While the Rākshasas stuck close to him, he sought out the Vānaras in his rage.
6.67.141
विन्ध्यमन्दरसङ्काशम्
काञ्चनाङ्गदभूषणम् ।
स्रवन्तं रुधिरं वक्त्रात्
वर्षामेघमिवोत्थितम् ॥
He, who was huge as the Vindhya and Mandara, was adorned with gold armlets. Blood poured from his mouth like showers from the raincloud high above.
6.67.142
जिह्वया परिलिह्यन्तम्
शोणितं शोणितेक्षणम् ।
मृद्नन्तं वानरानीकम्
कालान्तकयमोपमम् ॥
His eyes were bloodshot. He was licking up the blood with his tongue. He was pounding the Vānara army like Yama at the end of Time.
6.67.143
तं दृष्ट्वा राक्षसश्रेष्ठम्
प्रदीप्तानलवर्चसम् ।
विस्फारयामास तदा
कार्मुकं पुरुषर्षभः ॥
Seeing that prominent Rākshasa, who shone like blazing fire, that bull among men twanged his bow.
6.67.144
स तस्य चापनिर्घोषात्
कुपितो राक्षसर्षभः ।
अमृष्यमाणस्तं घोषम्
भिदुद्राव राघवम् ॥
Enraged by that twanging, which he could not stand, that bull among Rākshasa rushed upon Rāghava.
6.67.145
ततस्तु वातोद्धतमेघकल्पं
भुजङ्गराजोत्तमभोगबाहुम् ।
तमापतन्तं धरणीधराभम्
उवाच रामो युधि कुम्भकर्णम् ॥
Then Rāma said to Kumbhakarṇa who looked like a mountain and like a cloud blown by the wind, whose arms looked like the body of the king of serpents, and who was thus rushing upon him in the battle:
6.67.146
आगच्छ रक्षोधिप मा विषादम्
अवस्थितोऽहं प्रगृहीतचापः ।
अवेहि मां राक्षसवंशनाशनम्
यस्त्वं मुहूर्ताद्भविता विचेताः ॥
Come along, setting aside your grief. Here I am, O leader of Rākshasas, ready with bow in hand. Know that I am the destroyer of the Rākshasa clan, who will render you senseless in just an hour.
6.67.147
रामोऽयमिति विज्ञाय
जहास विकृतस्वनम् ।
अभ्यधावत सङ्क्रुद्धो
हरीन् विद्रावयन् रणे ॥
Knowing that it was Rāma, he uttered a ghastly laugh and chased the Vānaras in wrath, who were fleeing from the battle.
6.67.148
दारयन्निव सर्वेषाम्
हृदयानि वनौकसाम् ।
प्रहस्य विकृतं भीमम्
स मेघस्तनितोपमम् ।
कुम्भकर्णो महातेजा
राघवं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeming to tear apart the hearts of all the Vana dwellers with his horrid and hideous laugh that was as loud as the thunder of rain clouds, Kumbhakarṇa of immense power said these words to Rāghava:
6.67.149
नाहं विराधो विज्ञेयो
न कबन्धः खरो न च ।
न वाली न च मारीचः
कुम्भकर्णोऽहमागतः ॥
I am neither Virādha nor Khara, neither Kabandha, nor Vāli, nor Māreeca. I am Kumbhakarṇa who has come here.
6.67.150
पश्य मे मुद्गरं घोरम्
सर्वकालायसं महत् ।
अनेन निर्जिता देवा
दानवाश्च पुरा मया ॥
Take a good look at this huge and dreadful Mudgara made completely of iron. The Dēvas and Dānavas were vanquished by me in the past with this.
6.67.151
विकर्णनास इति माम्
नावज्ञातुं त्वमर्हसि ।
स्वल्पाऽपि हि न मे पीडा
कर्णनासाविनाशनात् ॥
You shall not underestimate me because I do not have nose and ears. I do not suffer in the least from the loss of my ears and nose.
6.67.152
दर्शयेक्ष्वाकुशार्दूल
वीर्यं गात्रेषु मेऽनघ ।
ततस्त्वां भक्षयिष्यामि
दृष्टपौरुषविक्रमम् ॥
Show your valor, attack my body, O sinless tiger among Ikshwākus! I shall devour you after witnessing your grit and prowess.
6.67.153
स कुम्भकर्णस्य वचो निशम्य
रामः सुपुङ्खान् विससर्ज बाणान् ।
तैराहतो वज्रसमप्रपातै
न चुक्षुभे व्यथते सुरारिः ॥
Hearing those words of Kumbhakarṇa, Rāma shot arrows that had rings around them. But the foe of the Suras did not even flutter, hit by those arrows that fell like Vajra.
6.67.154
यैः सायकैः सालवरा निकृत्ता
वाली हतो वानरपुङ्गवश्च ।
ते कुम्भकर्णस्य तदा शरीरे
वज्रोपमा न व्यथयाम्प्रचक्रुः ॥
Those arrows, which had cut off the tough Sāla trees, and had slain Vāli, the bull among Vānaras, seemed to inflict no pain whatsoever on the body of Kumbhakarṇa which was tough as tough as a diamond.
6.67.155
स वारिधारा इव सायकांस्तान्
पिबन् शरीरेण महेन्द्रशत्रुः ।
जघान रामस्य शरप्रवेगं
व्याविध्य तं मुद्गरमुग्रवेगम् ॥
He seemed to drink with his body the volley of Rāma's arrows that fell like a torrent of rain dampening their extreme speed by hitting them with his Mudgara, which he flung equally fast.
6.67.156
ततस्तु रक्षः क्षतजानुलिप्तं
वित्रासनं देवमहाचमूनाम् ।
विव्याध तं मुद्गरमुग्रवेगं
विद्रावयामास चमूं हरीणाम् ॥
Then the Rākshasa flung the Mudgara, which was smeared with blood and had terrorized the great armies of the Dēvas, with great speed, driving away the Vānara forces.
6.67.157
वायव्यमादाय ततो महास्त्रं
रामः प्रचिक्षेप निशाचराय ।
समुद्गरं तेन जघान बाहुं
स कृत्तबाहुस्तुमुलं ननाद ॥
Then, Rāma took the great Vāyu's Astra and hurled it at the Night-rover, cutting down the arm that wielded the Mudgara. With his arm severed, he let off a dreadful howl.
6.67.158
स तस्य बाहुर्गिरिशृङ्गकल्पः
समुद्गरो राघवबाणकृत्तः ।
पपात तस्मिन् हरिराजसैन्ये
जघान तां वानरवाहनीं च ॥
That arm, huge as a mountain peak, cut down by Rāghava’s arrow, fell, along with the club, on the army of the Vānara king, killing many in that Vānara army.
6.67.159
ते वानरा भग्नहतावशेषाः
पर्यन्तमाश्रित्य तदा विषण्णाः ।
प्रवेपिताङ्गं ददृशुः सुघोरं
नरेन्द्ररक्षोधिपसन्निपातम् ॥
The Vānaras who were saved from being killed or maimed, retreated to a safe distance in fright. Every limb of theirs shivering in fright, they witnessed the terrible encounter between the king of men and the leader of the Rākshasas.
6.67.160
स कुम्भकर्णोऽस्त्रनिकृत्तबाहुः
महान्निकृत्ताग्र इवाचलेन्द्रः ।
उत्पाटयामास करेण वृक्षं
ततोऽभिदुद्राव रणे नरेन्द्रम् ॥
His arm cut off by the Astra, Kumbhakarṇa, looked like a mountain whose top had been lopped off. Pulling out by the root a tree with his other hand, he rushed to fight the King of men.
6.67.161
स तस्य बाहुं सहसालवृक्षं
समुद्यतं पन्नगभोगकल्पम् ।
ऐन्द्रास्त्रयुक्तेन जघान रामो
बाणेन जाम्बूनदचित्रितेन ॥
Invoking the power of Indra's Astra on an arrow decked with gold, Rāma cut off his arm that held the Sāla tree up like a serpent does its hood.
6.67.162
स कुम्भकर्णस्य भुजो निकृत्तः
पपात भूमौ गिरिसन्निकाशः ।
विवेष्टमानोऽभिजघान वृक्षान्
शैलान् शिला वानरराक्षसांश्च ॥
The severed arm of Kumbhakarṇa, which was as big as a mountain, fell down on the ground writhing, striking trees, hills, rocks, and Vānaras as well as Rākshasas.
6.67.163
तं छिन्नबाहुं समवेक्ष्य रामः
समापतन्तं सहसा नदन्तम् ।
द्वावर्धचन्द्रौ निशितौ प्रगृह्य
चिच्छेद पादौ युधि राक्षसस्य ॥
Seeing the Rākshasa immediately come roaring to attack him, even with his arms cut off, Rāma took two sharp arrows whose tips were shaped like a crescent and severed his two legs in that combat.
6.67.164
तौ तस्य पादौ प्रदिशो दिशश्च
गिरीन् गुहाश्चैव महार्णवं च ।
लङ्कां च सेनां कपिराक्षसानां
विनादयन्तौ विनिपेततुश्च ॥
The sound of those legs, as they cracked and fell down reverberated in all the four directions and in between, and among the mountains, caves and oceans, as well as in Laṅkā and among the masses of Vānara and the Rākshasa armies.
6.67.165
निकृत्तबाहुर्विनिकृत्तपादो
विदार्य वक्त्रं वडवामुखाभम् ।
दुद्राव रामं सहसाऽभिगर्जन्
राहुर्यथा चन्द्रमिवान्तरिक्षे ॥
Even with his hands cut off and legs severed, he rolled fast towards Rāma roaring, with his wide-opened mouth that looked like the conflagration in the bowels of the ocean as Rāhu hastening towards the moon in the sky.
6.67.166
अपूरयत्तस्य मुखं शिताग्रै
रामः शरैर्हेमपिनद्धपुङ्खैः ।
स पूर्णवक्त्रो न शशाक वक्तुं
चुकूज कृच्छ्रेण मुमोह चापि ॥
Rāma filled his (Kumbhakarṇa’s) mouth with sharp edged arrows with gold rings around them. His mouth thus filled, he could not speak, but only make indistinct cries with difficulty, before he completely fainted.
6.67.167
अथाददे सूर्यमरीचिकल्पं
स ब्रह्मदण्डान्तककालकल्पम् ।
अरिष्टमैन्द्रं निशितं सुपुङ्खं
रामः शरं मारुततुल्यवेगम् ॥
Then Rāma took out an arrow which dazzled like the rays of the sun and was comparable to the Daṇḍa of Brahma, and to the Death and to the all-destroying Time. That sharp arrow, swift as the wind, was fortified with rings and was powered by Indra's Astra, spelling doom for the enemies.
6.67.168
तं वज्रजाम्बूनदचारुपुङ्खं
प्रदीप्तसूर्यज्वलनप्रकाशम् ।
महेन्द्रवज्राशनितुल्यवेगं
रामः प्रचिक्षेप निशाचराय ॥
Rāma then shot that arrow, which was dazzling like the sun and blazing like fire and was swift like Vajra and thunderbolt of Mahēndra and had rings of gold and gems studded on it, aimed at that Night-rover.
6.67.169
स सायको राघवबाहुचोदितो
दिशः स्वभासा दश सम्प्रकाशयन् ।
विधूमवैश्वानरदीप्तदर्शनो
जगाम शक्राशनिभीमविक्रमः ॥
That arrow, sped by the power of Rāghava’s arms, lighting up the ten directions with its splendor, and dazzling like fire that is free of smoke, flew with the terrific thrust of the thunderbolt of Ṡakra.
6.67.170
स तन्महापर्वतकूटसन्निभं
विवृत्तदंष्ट्रं चलचारुकुण्डलम् ।
चकर्त रक्षोधिपतेः शिरस्तथा
यथैव वृत्रस्य पुरा पुरन्दरः ॥
And it cut off the head of the lord of the Rākshasas, which resembled a great mountain peak. With its twisted fangs and dangling, lovely ear-pendants it resembled the head of Vṛtra that was cut off by Indra in the far past.
6.67.171
कुम्भकर्णशिरो भाति
कुण्डलालङ्कृतं महत् ।
आदित्येऽभ्युदिते रात्रौ
मध्यस्थ इव चन्द्रमाः ॥
The huge head of Kumbhakarṇa, adorned with ear pendants, shone like the moon in the night sky seen in the midst of the dual stars of Punarvasu.
6.67.172
तद्रामबाणाभिहतं पपात
रक्षश्शिरःपर्वतसन्निकाशम् ।
बभञ्ज चर्यागृहगोपुराणि
प्राकारमुच्चं तमपातयच्च ॥
That head of the Rākshasa, huge like a mountain, fell down, destroying the sentry posts, towers as well as the ramparts themselves.
6.67.173
न्यपतत् कुम्भकर्णोऽथ
स्वकायेन निपातयन् ।
प्लवङ्गमानां कोट्यश्च
परितः सम्प्रधावताम् ॥
Kumbhakarṇa, with his body that tumbled down, knocked down crores of Vānaras who were running away to a distance.
6.67.174
तच्चाति कायं हि महत्प्रकाशं
रक्षस्तदा तोयनिधौ पपात ।
ग्राहान् परान् मीनवरान् भुजङ्गान्
ममर्द भूमिं च तदा विवेश ॥
The body of the Rākshasa, huge and imposing, fell in the ocean squashing the huge alligators, great fish and snakes, and the seabed before it embedded itself into it.
6.67.175
तस्मिन् हते ब्राह्मणदेवशत्रौ
महाबले संयति कुम्भकर्णे ।
चचाल भूर्भूमिधराश्च सर्वे
हर्षाच्च देवास्तुमुलं प्रणेदुः ॥
With Kumbhakarṇa of immense strength, an enemy of Brāhmaṇas and Dēvas slain in the battle, the earth and the mountains rocked; the delighted Dēvas created a huge din out of joy.
6.67.176
ततस्तु देवर्षिमहर्षिपन्नगाः
सुराश्च भूतानि सुपर्णगुह्यकाः ।
सयक्षगन्धर्वगणा नभोगताः
प्रहर्षिता रामपराक्रमेण ॥
The Dēvarshis, Maharshis, the Pannagas, the Dēvas, the spirits, the birds, the Guhyakas, the Yakshas and the hordes of Gandharvas, crowded the sky, rejoicing at Rāma’s prowess.
6.67.177
ततस्तु ते तस्य वधेन भूरिणा
मनस्विनो नैर्ऋतराजबान्धवाः ।
विनेदुरुच्चैर्व्यथिता रघूत्तमम्
हरिं समीक्ष्यैव यथा मतङ्गजाः ॥
With his death that was hard to believe, the kith and kin of the king of the Night-rovers were taken aback and lamented loudly in anguish looking at that best of the Raghus, like elephants do upon sighting a lion.
6.67.178
स देवलोकस्य तमो निहत्य
सूर्यो यथा राहुमुखाद्विमुक्तः ।
तथा व्यभासीद्धरि सैन्यमध्ये
निहत्य रामो युधि कुम्भकर्णम् ॥
Having slain Kumbhakarṇa in the battle, Rāma shone in the midst of the Vānara army like the sun released from the clutches of Rāhu shines and removes the darkness from the sky.
6.67.179
प्रहर्षमीयुर्बहवस्तु वानराः
प्रबुद्धपद्मप्रतिमैरिवाननैः ।
अपूजयन् राघवमिष्टभागिनम्
हते रिपौ भीमबले दुरासदे ॥
The innumerable Vānaras then rejoiced with their faces blooming like full-blown lotuses. They applauded Rāghava, who achieved his objective by killing the enemy of terrific strength against whom it was impossible to make a stand.
6.67.180
स कुम्भकर्णं सुरसङ्घमर्दनम्
महत्सु युद्धेषु कदाचिनाजितम् ।
ननन्द हत्वा भरताग्रजो रणे
महासुरं वृत्रमिवामराधिपः ॥
Having slain in battle Kumbhakarṇa, who had pounded companies of Dēvas and was never vanquished even in great battles, the elder brother of Bharata rejoiced, like the Lord of Dēvas did having slain Vṛtra.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे सप्तषष्टितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty seventh Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
