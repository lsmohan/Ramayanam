Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 59
In this Sarga, Jāmbavān and other Vānaras, happy to hear the news of Seetā from Saṃpāti, ask him for more details. Saṃpāti then tells them that his son Supārṡwa, while hovering on the Mahēndra mountain searching for prey, happened to see Rāvaṇa carrying off Seetā.
He tells them that though he is old and has no wings, he can still help them by using his words and intellect. He tells them to resolve to go after Rāvaṇa without any delay.
4.59.1
ततस्तदमृतास्वादम्
गृध्रराजेन भाषितम् ।
निशम्य मुदिता हृष्टाः
ते वचः प्लवगर्षभाः ॥
Hearing those words spoken by the king of eagles, which were as sweet as Amṛt, those bulls among Vānaras felt happy, and rejoiced.
4.59.2
जाम्बवान् वानरश्रेष्ठः
सह सर्वैः प्लवङ्गमैः ।
भूतलात्सहसोत्थाय
गृध्रराजानमब्रवीत् ॥
Jāmbavān, foremost of the Vānaras, springing to his feet along with the rest of the Vānaras, asked the king of eagles:
4.59.3
क्व सीता केन वा दृष्टा
को वा हरति मैथिलीम् ।
तदाख्यातु भवात्सर्वम्
गतिर्भव वनौकसाम् ॥
Where is Seetā? By whom was she seen? Who is he who abducted Maithili? Please tell us all about it. You must save us, the Vana dwellers.
4.59.4
को दाशरथि बाणानाम्
वज्रवेगनिपातिनाम् ।
स्वयं लक्ष्मणमुक्तानाम्
न चिन्तयति विक्रमम् ॥
Who is he who is oblivious of the arrows of the son of Daṡaratha that strike with the speed of Vajra and to the power of the arrows shot by Lakshmaṇa himself?
4.59.5
स हरीन्प्रतिसंयुक्तान्
सीताश्रुतिसमाहितान् ।
पुनराश्वसयन्प्रीतः
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
And he, pleased to see the Vānaras retract from fasting unto death and their eagerness to hear about Seetā, consoled them again and said these words:
4.59.6
श्रूयतामिह वैदेह्या
यथा मे हरणं श्रुतम् ।
येन चापि ममाऽऽख्यातम्
यत्र चाऽऽयतलोचना ॥
Listen and I will tell you about the abduction of Seetā of wide eyes as I heard it and from whom I heard.
4.59.7
अहमस्मिन्गिरौ दुर्गे
बहुयोजनमायते ।
चिरान्निपतितो वृद्धः
क्षीणप्राणपराक्रमः ॥
I have been lying here for long on this impenetrable mountain that is many Yōjanas long, being old and having lost my power and energy.
4.59.8
तं मामेवं गतं पुत्रः
सुपार्श्वोनाम नामतः ।
आहारेण यथाकालम्
बिभर्ति पततां वरः ॥
My son, prominent among birds, known by name as Supārṡwa, has been taking care of me, who has been in this sorry plight, by bringing food regularly.
4.59.9
तीक्ष्णकामास्तु गन्धर्वाः
तीक्ष्णकोपा भुजङ्गमाः ।
मृगाणां तु भयं तीक्ष्णम्
ततस्तीक्ष्णक्षुधा वयम् ॥
Gandharvas are extremely lascivious, snakes are exceedingly irascible, deer are extremely timorous and we are even more extremely ravenous.
4.59.10
स कदाचित्क्षुधार्तस्य
ममाहाराभिकाङ्क्षिणः ।
गतसूर्येऽहनि प्राप्तो
मम पुत्रो ह्यनामिषः ॥
One day, my son came without food late in the day after the sun had set while I was hungry and eagerly waiting for food.
4.59.11
स मायाहारसंरोधात्
पीडितः प्रीतिवर्धनः ।
अनुमान्य यथातत्त्वम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
I, suffering from lack of food, was harsh with him, who actually is a source of joy. Then he respectfully told me what exactly had happened.
4.59.12
अहं तात यथाकालम्
आमिषार्थी खमाप्लुतः ।
महेन्द्रस्य गिरेर्द्वारम्
आवृत्य सुसमाश्रितः  ॥
O father! I flew into the sky at the usual time in quest of meat and was hovering over with full attention over the Mahēndra mountain range.
4.59.13
तत्र सत्त्वसहस्राणाम्
सागरान्तरचारिणाम् ।
पन्थानमेकोऽध्यवसम्
सन्निरोद्धुमवाङ्मुखः ॥
I hovered there looking down to singlehandedly block the thousands of creatures that were moving about in the ocean.
4.59.14
तत्र कश्चिन्मया दृष्टः
सूर्योदयसमप्रभाम् ।
स्त्रियमादाय गच्छन्वै
भिन्नाञ्जनचयप्रभः ॥
Then I saw someone who was like a piece broken off from a mound of collyrium taking with him a woman of the luster of the morning sun.
4.59.15
सोऽहमभ्यवहारार्थम्
तौ दृष्ट्वा कृतनिश्चयः ।
तेन साम्ना विनीतेन
पन्थानमभियाचितः ॥
Upon seeing them, I resolved to to have them for food. But then he begged me to let him go, humbly and persuasively.
4.59.16
न हि सामोपपन्नानाम्
प्रहर्ता विद्यते क्वचित् ।
नीचेष्वपि जनः कश्चित्
किमङ्ग बत मद्विधः ॥
Nobody, not even the lowest of the low, strikes at those who seek to conciliate, how then could someone like me?
4.59.17
स यातस्तेजसा व्योम
सङ्क्षिपन्निव वेगतः ।
अथाऽहं खेचरैर्भूतैः
अभिगम्य सभाजितः ॥
Then he flew away with all force, and that speed made the sky look like a tiny stretch. Then all the creatures of the sky came to me and praised me.
4.59.18
दिष्ट्या जीवति सीतेति
ह्यब्रुवन्मां महर्षयः ।
कथञ्चित्सकलत्रोऽसौ
गतस्ते स्वस्त्यसंशयम् ॥
And the Maharshis said, ‘It is by good fortune that Seetā is alive. He somehow managed to escape with her who should have been protected. Do not worry, it will all be well for you.’
4.59.19
एव मुक्तस्ततोऽहं तैः
सिद्धैः परमशोभनैः ।
स च मे रावणो राजा
रक्षसां प्रतिवेदितः ।
हरन्दाशरथेर्भार्याम्
रामस्य जनकात्मजाम् ।
भ्रष्टाभरणकौशेयाम्
शोकवेगपराजिताम् ।
रामलक्ष्मणयोर्नाम
क्रोशन्तीं मुक्तमूर्धजाम् ॥
Then those Siddhas of supreme luster told me that he was Rāvaṇa, the king of Rākshasas, and he was carrying off the daughter of Janaka and wife of Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, as she, with her ornaments and silken garments all disordered and her hair disheveled, was crying, saying the names of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, beaten by torrents of grief.
4.59.21
एष कालात्ययस्तात
इति वाक्यविदां वरः ।
एतमर्थं समग्रं मे
सुपार्श्वः प्रत्यवेदयत् ॥
Supārṡwa, the best of communicators, concluded saying, ‘That is why I am late, father’, conveying to me all that had happened.
4.59.22
बुद्ध्यता च मया वीर्यम्
रावणस्य दुरात्मनः ।
पुत्रस्सन्तर्जितो वाग्भिः
न त्राता मैथिली कथम् ॥
I found fault with my son for not saving Maithili, as I know how evil minded that powerful Rāvaṇa is.
4.59.23
तस्या विलपितं श्रुत्वा
तौ च सीता वियोजितौ ।
न मे दशरथस्नेहात्
पुत्रेणोत्पादितं प्रियम् ॥
My son failed to do what would please me in spite of hearing Seetā cry, separated from them, and in spite of knowing about my friendship with Daṡaratha.
4.59.24
तच्छृत्वाऽपि हि मे बुद्धिः
नासीत्काचित्पराक्रमे ।
अपक्षो हि कथं पक्षी
कर्म किञ्चित्समारभेत् ॥
Even after hearing that, I could not think of doing anything daring. What can a bird without wings even think of doing?
4.59.25
यत्तु शक्यं मया कर्तुम्
वाग्बुद्धिगुणवर्तिना ।
श्रूयतां तत्प्रवक्ष्यामि
भवतां पौरुषाश्रयम् ॥
But I will do what I can by counseling you, using my wit and wisdom. Listen and I will tell you how it all depends on how you deploy your valor.
4.59.26
वाङ्मतिभ्यां हि सर्वेषाम्
करिष्यामि प्रियं हि वः ।
यद्धि दाशरथेः कार्यम्
मम तन्नात्र संशयः ॥
I will do what would please you just with my words and intellect. The object of the son of Daṡaratha is my object, and there is no doubt about it.
4.59.27
तद्भवन्तो मतिश्रेष्ठा
बलवन्तो मनस्विनः ।
प्रहिताः कपिराजेन
देवैरपि दुरासदाः ॥
You excel with your intellect and you are strong and focused. You, invincible even to the Dēvas, are sent by the king of Vānaras.
4.59.28
रामलक्ष्मणबाणाश्च
निशिताः कङ्कपत्रिणः ।
त्रयाणामपि लोकानाम्
पर्याप्तास्त्राणनिग्रहे ॥
And the sharp arrows of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, sped with the feathers of Kaṅka bird, are quite capable of saving as well as destroying the three worlds.
4.59.29
कामं खलु दशग्रीवः
तेजोबलसमन्वितः ।
भवतां तु समर्थानाम्
न किञ्चिदपि दुष्करम् ॥
No doubt that the Ten-necked is endowed with power and strength; but nothing is hard for you who are so capable.
4.59.30
तदलं कालसङ्गेन
क्रियतां बुद्धिनिश्चयः ।
न हि कर्मसु सज्जन्ते
बुद्धिमन्तो भवद्विधाः ॥
There is no time to lose. Make a resolve in your minds. Smart people like you would not dawdle in their tasks, would they?
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे एकोनषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty ninth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
