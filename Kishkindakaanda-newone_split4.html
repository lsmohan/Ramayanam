Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 4
In this Sarga, Hanumān asks Rāma and Lakshmaṇa who they are and why they have come to the impenetrable, dense Vana. Lakshmaṇa tells Hanumān about Daṡaratha, about Rāma and the loss of kingdom and their stay in the Vana. He tells Hanumān that both Seetā, Rāma’s wife, and he himself, followed Rāma to the Vana and that Seetā was abducted by a Rākshasa when she was alone, and that Danu, son of Diti had told them about Sugreeva, who could help them find Seetā. Lakshmaṇa tells Hanumān, with tears welling in his eyes, that Rāma, as great and capable as he was, would now have to take refuge in Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras.
With the exchange between them, Hanumān feels that the time has come for Sugreeva to regain his kingdom and Lakshmaṇa feels that Rāma’s object is as good as achieved. Hanumān then flies to Sugreeva, carrying Rāma and Lakshmaṇa on his back.
4.4.1
ततः प्रहृष्टौ हनुमान्
कृत्यवानिति तद्वचः ।
श्रुत्वा मधुरसम्भाषम्
सुग्रीवं मनसा गतः ॥
On hearing those words that fell sweetly on the ear, Hanumān, whose purpose seemed to have been served, felt delighted. His thoughts went back to Sugreeva:
4.4.2
भव्यो राज्यागमस्तस्य
सुग्रीवस्य महात्मनः ।
यदयं कृत्यवान्प्राप्तः
कृत्यं चैतदुपागतम् ॥
It seems that Mahātma Sugreeva is going to get his kingdom back; for, he (Rāma) seems to have come with a purpose for which the time is ripe.
4.4.3
ततः परमसंहृष्टो
हनुमान् प्लवगर्षभः ।
प्रत्युवाच ततो वाक्यम्
रामं वाक्यविशारदः ॥
And Hanumān, the bull among Vānaras, who was skilled in speech, felt extremely happy and responded to Rāma:
4.4.4
किमर्थं त्वं वनं घोरम्
पम्पाकाननमण्डितम् ।
आगतस्सानुजो दुर्गम्
नानाव्यालमृगायुतम् ॥
What brought you, along with your younger brother, to this dreadful and impenetrable Vana which is overrun with wild beasts and other animals, except for the beautiful patch of woods that surround Paṃpā?
There must have been some corruption in the text of this Ṡlōka, because Hanumān does not yet know that Lakshmaṇa is the brother of Rāma.
4.4.5
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
लक्ष्मणो रामचोदितः ।
आचचक्षे महात्मानम्
रामं दशरथात्मजम् ॥
Hearing those words of his, Lakshmaṇa, prodded by Rāma, told him all about Mahātma Rāma, son of Daṡaratha:
4.4.6
राजा दशरथो नाम
द्युतिमान्धर्मवत्सलः ।
चातुर्वर्ण्यं स्वधर्मेण
नित्यमेवाभिपालयन् ॥
There was a brilliant king by name Daṡaratha who guarded Dharma as if it was his own child. He diligently discharged his duty of protecting all the four Varṇas.
4.4.7
न द्वेष्टा विद्यते तस्य
न च स द्वेष्टि कञ्चन ।
स च सर्वेषु भूतेषु
पितामह इवापरः ।
अग्निष्टोमादिभिर्यज्ञैः
इष्टवानाप्तदक्षिणैः ॥
There is no one who did not like him and there is none that he did not like. He was like the grandsire for all beings. He performed many Yajñas including Agnishṭōma, giving honorariums generously.
4.4.8
तस्यायं पूर्वजः पुत्रो
रामो नाम जनैः श्रुतः ।
शरण्यस्सर्वभूतानाम्
पितुर्निर्देशपारगः ॥
This is his eldest son, famed among men as Rāma. He is the refuge of all beings and is faithful in executing his father’s commands to the hilt.
4.4.9
वीरो दशरथस्यायं
पुत्राणां गुणवत्तमः ।
राजलक्षणसम्पन्नः
सम्युक्तो राजसम्पदा ।
राज्याद्भ्रष्टो वने वस्तुम्
मया सार्धमिहागतः ॥
This Veera excels among the sons of Daṡaratha by virtue of his great qualities. He of every kingly virtue, was enjoying all the royal luxuries, when he was exiled from the kingdom and came to live in this Vana, accompanied by me.
4.4.11
भार्यया च महातेजाः
सीतयाऽनुगतो वशी ।
दिनक्षये महातेजाः
प्रभयेव दिवाकरः ॥
And he, the supremely radiant one, a master of his senses, was followed by his wife Seetā, like the sun while setting, by the sunlight.
4.4.12
अहमस्यावरो भ्राता
गुणैर्दास्यमुपागतः ।
कृतज्ञस्य बहुज्ञस्य
लक्ष्मणो नाम नामतः ॥
I am his younger brother, Lakshmaṇa by name. His great qualities, his knowledge of many things and his sense of gratitude, made me his humble servant.
4.4.13
सुखार्हस्य महार्हस्य
सर्वभूतहितात्मनः ।
ऐश्वर्येण च हीनस्य
वनवासाश्रितस्य च ।
रक्षसाऽपहृता भार्या
रहिते कामरूपिणा ।
तच्च न ज्ञायते रक्षः
पत्नी येनास्य सा हृता ॥
He, who deserves all luxuries and all honor, having lost all that he had, has taken to living in the Vana. His wife was abducted when she was alone by a Rākshasa, who could take any form at will. We do not know who that Rākshasa is, by whom she was abducted.
4.4.15
दनुर्नाम दितेः पुत्रः
शापाद्राक्षसतां गतः ।
आख्यातस्तेन सुग्रीवः
समर्थो वानराधिपः ॥
The son of Diti, Danu by name, who was cursed with the form of a Rākshasa has said that Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras, is very capable.
4.4.16
स ज्ञास्यति महावीर्यः
तव भार्यापहारिणम् ।
एवमुक्त्वा दनुस्स्वर्गम्
भ्राजमानो गतस्सुखम् ॥
Saying, ‘he of immense strength will be able to find out the abductor of your wife’, Danu ascended to Heaven, happy and shining bright.
4.4.17
एतत्ते सर्वमाख्यातम्
यथातथ्येन पृच्छतः ।
अहं चैव हि रामश्च
सुग्रीवं शरणं गतौ ॥
I have told everything as it is, to answer your question. Rāma and I seek refuge in Sugreeva.
4.4.18
एष दत्त्वा च वित्तानि
प्राप्य चानुत्तमं यशः ।
लोकनाथः पुरा भूत्वा
सुग्रीवं नाथमिच्छति ॥
Once upon a time, he was a lord of the world, giving away money and earning unequalled fame. Now, he would like to take refuge in Sugreeva.
4.4.19
पिता यस्य पुरा ह्यासीत्
शरण्यो धर्मवत्सलः ।
तस्य पुत्रश्शरण्यश्च
सुग्रीवं शरणं गतः ॥
Once upon a time, everyone took refuge in him, and in his father, who protected Dharma. Now he seeks refuge in Sugreeva.
4.4.20
सर्वलोकस्य धर्मात्मा
शरण्यश्शरणं पुरा ।
गुरुर्मे राघवस्सोऽयम्
सुग्रीवं शरणं गतः ॥
Rāghava, the Dharmātma, who is an elder to me, was, once upon a time, the refuge of everyone. Now he seeks refuge in Sugreeva.
4.4.21
यस्य प्रसादे सततम्
प्रसीदेयुरिमाः प्रजाः ।
स रामो वानरेन्द्रस्य
प्रसादमभिकाङ्क्षते ॥
Rāma, in whose grace all people found comfort, now seeks the grace of the Lord of Vānaras.
4.4.22
येन सर्वगुणोपेताः
पृथिव्यां सर्वपार्थिवाः ।
मानितास्सततं राज्ञा
सदा दशरथेन वै ।
तस्यायं पूर्वजः पुत्रः
त्रिषु लोकेषु विश्रुतः ।
सुग्रीवं वानरेन्द्रं तु
रामश्शरणमागतः ॥
Rāma, one renowned in all the three worlds and the eldest son of Daṡaratha who had dealt with all the kings of the earth of every great virtue with utmost rapport, seeks the refuge of Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras.
4.4.24
शोकाभिभूते रामे तु
शोकार्ते शरणं गते ।
कर्तुमर्हति सुग्रीवः
प्रसादं हरियूधपः ॥
Sugreeva, the leader of the Vānaras, should bestow his grace on Rāma who was struck by grief and takes refuge in him in his helplessness.
4.4.25
एवं ब्रुवाणं सौमित्रिम्
करुणं साश्रुलोचनम् ।
हनुमान्प्रत्युवाचेदम्
वाक्यं वाक्यविशारदः ॥
When Sowmitri said these words with tears welling in his eyes, Hanumān, a master of words, responded with these words:
4.4.26
ईदृशा बुद्धिसम्पन्ना
जितक्रोधा जितेन्द्रियाः ।
द्रष्टव्या वानरेन्द्रेण
दिष्ट्या दर्शनमागताः ॥
It is only by good fortune that one would meet people like you, who are intelligent and have mastered their senses and temper. You should be seen by the Lord of Vānaras.
4.4.27
स हि राज्यात्परिभ्रष्टः
कृतवैरश्च वालिना ।
हृतदारो वने त्रस्तो
भ्रात्रा विनिकृतो भृशम् ॥
He also was driven out of his kingdom, by Vāli, his brother, who became his foe. Insulted, and with his wife abducted, he lives in the Vana in fear.
4.4.28
करिष्यति स साहाय्यम्
युवयोर्भास्करात्मजः ।
सुग्रीवस्सह चास्माभिः
सीतायाः परिमार्गणे ॥
Sugreeva, the son of Bhāskara, along with us, can help you search for Seetā.
Bhāskara mean Sun.
4.4.29
इत्येवमुक्त्वा हनुमान्
श्लक्ष्णं मधुरया गिरा ।
बभाषे सोऽभिगच्छेम
सुग्रीवमिति राघवम् ॥
Speaking thus in soft and sweet words, he said to Rāghava, ‘Well, we will now proceed to Sugreeva’.
4.4.30
एवं ब्रुवाणं धर्मात्मा
हनुमन्तं स लक्ष्मणः ।
प्रतिपूज्य यथान्यायम्
इदं प्रोवाच राघवम् ॥
Lakshmaṇa, the Dharmātma, duly honoring Hanumān when he had thus spoken, turned to Rāghava and said these words:
4.4.31
कपिः कथयते हृष्टो
यथाऽयं मारुतात्मजः ।
कृत्यवांत्सोऽपि सम्प्राप्तः
कृतकृत्योऽसि राघव ॥
This Vānara, the son of Vāyu, is evidently happy, based on what he says. He also has a purpose for coming here. Your object is as good as achieved.
4.4.32
प्रसन्नमुखवर्णश्च
व्यक्तं हृष्टश्च भाषते ।
नानृतं वक्ष्यते वीरो
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ॥
Clearly, Hanumān the Veera, the son of Vāyu cannot be disingenuous in his words, based on the joy evident in his talk and on the pleasantness in his face.
4.4.33
ततस्स तु महाप्राज्ञो
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ।
जगामाऽऽदाय तौ वीरौ
हरिराजाय राघवौ ॥
Then the sagacious Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, took the Rāghavas, the Veeras, to the Lord of Vānaras.
4.4.34
भिक्षुरूपं परित्यज्य
वानरं रूपमास्थितः ।
पृष्ठमारोप्य तौ वीरौ
जगाम कपिकुञ्जरः ॥
(Then Hanumān), the elephant among Vānaras, abjured his guise of a mendicant, took his original Vānara form and proceeded, carrying the Veeras on his back.
4.4.35
स तु विपुलयशाः कपिप्रवीरः
पवनसुतः कृतकृत्यवत्प्रहृष्टः ।
गिरिवरमुरुविक्रमः प्रयातः
स शुभमतिस्सह रामलक्ष्मणाभ्याम् ॥
That Vānara, the son of Vāyu, who was good at heart and a great Veera of far-flung renown, rejoiced at having achieved his purpose, and went swiftly to the great mountain taking Rāma and Lakshmaṇa with him.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे चतुर्थस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fourth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
