Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 81
In this Sarga, Indrajit uses his powers of magic to create a phantom Seetā, and plans to kill her before the eyes of the Vānaras. The Vānara army led by Hanumān advances towards the Rākshasa army led by him, when Hanumān sees the grieving and unkempt Seetā seated in Indrajit's chariot.
Hanumān, in agony over seeing her fallen into the hands of Indrajit, rebukes him in strong words for what he has done and warns him of fatal consequences if he harms her. Indrajit, with no care for the warning of Hanumān goes ahead and slays Seetā with his sword. He lets off mighty shouts of joy, hearing which the Vānaras flee.
6.81.1
विज्ञाय तु मनस्तस्य
राघवस्य महात्मनः ।
सन्निवृत्याहवात्तस्मात्
संविवेश पुरं ततः ॥
Realizing what Mahātma Rāghava was thinking, he (Indrajit) turned back from that battlefield and went to the city (of Laṅkā).
6.81.2
सोऽनुस्मृत्य वधं तेषाम्
राक्षसानां तरस्विनाम् ।
क्रोधताम्रेक्षणः शूरो
निर्जगामाथ रावणिः ॥
Then, he, the great warrior, set out (again), his eyes turned red in anger as he recalled the destruction of powerful Rākshasas.
6.81.3
स पश्चिमेन द्वारेण
निर्ययौ राक्षसैर्वृतः ।
इन्द्रजित्तु महावीर्यः
पौलस्त्यो देवकण्टकः ॥
Then, with many Rākshasas around him, Indrajit, the great warrior, a descendant of Paulastya and a thorn on the side of Dēvas, reemerged from the western gateway.
6.81.4
इन्द्रजित्तु ततो दृष्ट्वा
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
रणायाभ्युद्यतौ वीरौ
मायां प्रादुष्करोत्तदा ॥
Then, seeing the valiant brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa ready and avid for the fight, Indrajit deployed his powers of magic.
6.81.5
इन्द्रजित्तु रथे स्थाप्य
सीतां मायामयीं ततः ।
बलेन महताऽऽवृत्य
तस्या वधमरोचयत् ॥
Then Indrajit planned to place a phantom Seetā in the chariot and slay her, himself well protected by a huge Rākshasa force.
6.81.6
मोहनार्थं तु सर्वेषाम्
बुद्धिं कृत्वा सुदुर्मतिः ।
हन्तुं सीतां व्यवसितो
वानराभिमुखो ययौ ॥
Set to kill (phantom) Seetā and hoping to confound everyone that evil-minded one advanced towards the Vānaras.
6.81.7
तं दृष्ट्वा त्वभिनिर्यान्तम्
नगर्याः काननौकसः ।
उत्पेतुरभिसङ्क्रुद्धाः
शिलाहस्ता युयुत्सवः ॥
On seeing him emerge from the city, the dwellers of the Vana sprang up in anger, with boulders in their hands and ready to put up a fight.
6.81.8
हनुमान् पुरतस्तेषाम्
जगाम कपिकुञ्जरः ।
प्रगृह्य सुमहच्छृङ्गम्
पर्वतस्य दुरासदम् ॥
Hanumān, the foremost of the Vānaras, led them, snatching a huge mountain crest which none would dare face.
6.81.9
स ददर्श हतानन्दाम्
सीतामिन्द्रजितो रथे ।
एकवेणीधरां दीनाम्
उपवासकृशाननाम् ।
परिक्लिष्टैकवसनाम्
अमृजां राघवप्रियाम् ।
रजोमलाभ्यामालिप्तैः
सर्वगात्रैर्वरस्त्रियम् ॥
He saw in the chariot of Indrajit, Seetā, the best of women, Rāghava's beloved, woebegone, completely devoid of cheer, her hair braided in a single plait, her face emaciated because of fasting, clad in a single piece of crumpled cloth, and unkempt with every limb of hers smeared with dirt and dust.
6.81.11
तां निरीक्ष्य मुहूर्तं तु
मैथिलीत्यध्यवस्य तु ।
बभूवाचिरदृष्टा हि
तेन सा जनकात्मजा ॥
He looked at her for a while and was certain that it was Maithili; for, it was not too long ago that he had seen her, the daughter of Janaka.
6.81.12
तां दीनां मलदिग्धाङ्गीम्
रथस्थां दृश्य मैथिलीम् ।
बाष्पपर्याकुलमुखो
हनुमान् व्यथितोऽभवत् ॥
On seeing Maithili woebegone, all her limbs smeared with dirt, in that chariot. Hanumān was in agony and his face was covered by tears.
6.81.13
अब्रवीत्तां तु शोकार्ताम्
निरानन्दां तपस्विनीम् ।
सीतां रथस्थितां दृष्ट्वा
राक्षसेन्द्रसुताश्रिताम् ।
किं समर्थितमस्येति
चिन्तयन् स महाकपिः ।
सह तैर्वानरश्रेष्ठैः
अभ्यधावत रावणिम् ॥
Seeing her in that chariot, suffering, devoid of any cheer and oppressed by grief, and fallen into the hands of the son of the Rākshasa King, that great Vānara was worried, and thought aloud, "What is he going to do now!", and rushed towards Rāvaṇa's son along with the other Vānara chiefs.
6.81.15
तद्वानरबलं दृष्ट्वा
रावणिः क्रोधमूर्च्छितः ।
कृत्वा विकोशं निस्त्रिंशम्
मूर्ध्नि सीतामकर्षयत् ॥
On seeing the Vānara forces, Rāvaṇa’s son, filled with rage, drew his sword from its scabbard, and caught Seetā by the hair.
6.81.16
तां स्त्रियं पश्यतां तेषाम्
ताडयामास रावणिः ।
क्रोशन्तीं राम रामेति
मायया योजितां रथे ॥
The son of Rāvaṇa hit Seetā, a ploy created by magical powers, as she cried, 'Rāma! O Rāma!, as they watched.
6.81.17
गृहीतमूर्धजां दृष्ट्वा
हनुमान् दैन्यमागतः ।
शोकजं वारि नैत्राभ्याम्
असृजन्मारुतात्मजः ॥
Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, felt very sad watching her being caught by the hair, tears of agony rolling down from his eyes.
6.81.18
तां दृष्ट्वा चारुसर्वाङ्गीम्
रामस्य महिषीं प्रियाम् ।
अब्रवीत् परुषं वाक्यम्
क्रोधाद्रक्षोधिपात्मजम् ॥
Seeing the beloved queen of Rāma, whose every limb was lovely (even in that condition), he said these strong words to the son of the Rākshasa King, in anger:
6.81.19
दुरात्मन्नात्मनाशाय
केशपक्षे परामृशः ।
ब्रह्मर्षीणां कुले जातो
राक्षसीं योनिमाश्रितः ॥
It is for your own destruction that you touched her by the hair, O you, who are born to a Rākshasa woman, though a descendant of Brahmarshis!
6.81.20
धिक् त्वां पापसमाचारम्
यस्य ते मतिरीदृशी ।
नृशंसानार्य दुर्वृत्त
क्षुद्र पापपराक्रम ।
अनार्यस्येदृशं कर्म
घृणा ते नास्ति निर्घृण ॥
Dhik! What kind of a person are you, to do a hideous thing like this, O cruel, ignoble, vile, sordid one, who uses the prowess only for sinful acts! Who but the ignoble would do such a thing? Do you not have even an iota of pity, O ruthless wretch?
6.81.21
च्युता गृहाच्च राज्याच्च
रामहस्ताच्च मैथिली ।
किं तवैषाऽपराद्धा हि
यदेनां हन्तुमिच्छसि ॥
What offense did Maithili, who lost the protection of home, kingdom and the arms of Rāma, commit against you, that you wish to kill her?
6.81.22
सीतां च हत्वा न चिरम्
जीविष्यसि कथञ्चन ।
वधार्ह कर्मणाऽनेन
मम हस्तगतो ह्यसि ॥
There is no way you can hope to live any longer if you kill Seetā! You deserve to be killed and this act of yours puts your life squarely in my hands!
6.81.23
ये च स्त्रीघातिनां लोका
लोकवध्येषु कुत्सिताः ।
इह जिवितमुत्सृज्य
प्रेत्य तान् प्रतिपत्स्यसे ॥
O you, who deserve death at the hands of all the world! You shall die and go to that world, most execrated of all, to which go those who kill women!
6.81.24
इति ब्रुवाणो हनुमान्
सायुधैर्हरिभिर्वृतः ।
अभ्यधावत सङ्क्रुद्धो
राक्षसेन्द्रसुतं प्रति ॥
Saying this, Hanumān, along with the other armed Vānaras, rushed upon the son of the Rākshasa King in wrath.
6.81.25
आपतन्तं महावीर्यम्
तदनीकं वनौकसाम् ।
रक्षसां भीमवेगानाम्
अनीकं तु न्यवारयत् ॥
As that army of Vana dwellers of extraordinary valor came attacking them, the Rākshasa army of formidable force put up a stiff line of defense.
6.81.26
स तां बाणसहस्रेण
विक्षोभ्य हरिवाहिनीम् ।
हरिश्रेष्ठं हनूमन्तम्
इन्द्रजित् प्रत्युवाच ह ॥
Harrowing the Vānara army with thousands of arrows, Indrajit responded to Hanumān, the eminent Vānara, with these words:
6.81.27
सुग्रीवस्त्वं च रामश्च
यन्निमित्तमिहागताः ।
तां हनिष्यामि वैदेहीम्
अद्यैव तव पश्यतः ॥
I shall kill Vaidēhee, for whose sake Sugreeva, you and Rāma have come here, right now and while you watch!
6.81.28
इमां हत्वा ततो रामम्
लक्ष्मणं त्वां च वानर ।
सुग्रीवं च वधिष्यामि
तं चानार्यं विभीषणम् ॥
After killing her, O Vānara, I shall slay Rāma, Lakshmaṇa, you, Sugreeva and the ignoble Vibheeshaṇa!
6.81.29
न हन्तव्याः स्त्रियश्चेति
यद्ब्रवीषि प्लवङ्गम ।
पीडाकरममित्राणाम्
यत् स्यात् कर्तव्यमेव तत् ॥
As to your contention that women should not be slain, O Vānara, one must do whatever one can to cause grief and misery to the foe.
6.81.30
तमेवमुक्त्वा रुदतीम्
सीतां मायामयीं तदा ।
शितधारेण खड्गेन
निजघानेन्द्रजित् स्वयम् ॥
Having said this, Indrajit struck with his sharp sword the phantom Seetā as she wept.
6.81.31
यज्ञोपवीतमार्गेण
भिन्ना तेन तपस्विनी ।
सा पृथिव्यां पृथुश्रोणी
पपात प्रियदर्शना ।
तामिन्द्रजित्स्वयं हत्वा
हनुमन्तमुवाच ह ॥
Cut athwart, in the pattern that one wears the sacred thread, and cloven, the suffering woman with wide Kaṭee and pleasing looks, fell down on the ground. Having himself killed her thus, Indrajit said to Hanumān:
6.81.33
मया रामस्य पश्येमाम्
प्रियां शस्त्र निषूदिताम् ।
एषा विशस्ता वैदेही
विफलो वः परिश्रमः ॥
Look at this beloved of Rāma, struck by my weapon! Vaidēhee is now dead and all your efforts are a mere waste.
6.81.34
ततः खड्गेन महता
हत्वा तामिन्द्रजित् स्वयम् ।
हृष्टः स रथमास्थाय
विननाद महास्वनम् ॥
Having slaughtered her himself with his huge sword, Indrajit, in high spirits, let off strident shouts, seated in his chariot.
6.81.35
वानराः शुश्रुवुः शब्दम्
अदूरे प्रत्यवस्थिताः ।
व्यादितास्यस्य नदतः
तद्दुर्गं संश्रितस्य च ॥
The Vānaras, who were not far away, heard those shouts made by him with his mouth gaping wide from the midst of forces that stood protecting him.
6.81.36
तथा तु सीतां विनिहत्य दुर्मतिः
प्रहृष्टचेताः स बभूव रावणिः ।
तं हृष्टरूपं समुदीक्ष्य वानरा
विषण्णरूपाः सहसाभिदुद्रुवुः ॥
Having slain Seetā, the evil-minded son of Rāvaṇa was filled with extreme joy in his heart. The Vānaras, seeing him in such joy, became sad and fled immediately.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे एकाशीतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighty first Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
