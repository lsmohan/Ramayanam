Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 59
In this Sarga, Rāma reproaches Lakshmaṇa for coming to him leaving Seetā alone. Lakshmaṇa explains that he did not come on his own, but was abused and driven away by Seetā.
But the grieving Rāma does not accept the explanation. He expresses his unhappiness with Lakshmaṇa for not following his instructions and coming away, reacting to the angry words of Seetā.
3.59.1
अथाश्रमादुपावृत्तम्
अन्तरा रघुनन्दनः ।
परिपप्रच्छ सौमित्रिम्
रामो दुःखार्दितं पुनः ॥
On the way back, Rāma, the delight of Raghus, again asked Sowmitri who had come away from the Āṡrama, and was downcast with distress:
3.59.2
तमुवाचकिमर्थं त्वम्
आगतोऽपास्य मैथिलीम् ।
यदा सा तव विश्वासात्
वने विरहिता मया ॥
Why did you come away leaving Maithili, when I had left her in the Vana, keeping my confidence in you?
3.59.3
दृष्ट्वैवाभ्यागतं त्वां मे
मैथिलीं त्यज्य लक्ष्मण ।
शङ्कमानं महत्पापम्
यत्सत्यं व्यथितं मनः ॥
To be honest, O Lakshmaṇa, as soon as I saw you coming leaving Maithili alone, I was very troubled and feared a great disaster.
3.59.4
स्फुरते नयनं सव्यम्
बाहुश्च हृदयं च मे ।
दृष्ट्वा लक्ष्मण दूरे त्वाम्
सीताविरहितं पथि ॥
On seeing you from a distance, O Lakshmaṇa, coming unaccompanied by Seetā, my left eye and my left arm started twitching and my heart started quivering.
3.59.5
एवमुक्तस्तु सौमित्रिः
लक्ष्मणश्शुभलक्षणः ।
भूयो दुःखसमाविष्टो
दुःखितं राममब्रवीत् ॥
Thus spoken to, Lakshmaṇa, son of Sumitrā of auspicious marks, was again overwhelmed with grief, and said to the grieving Rāma:
3.59.6
न स्वयं कामकारेण
तां  त्यक्त्वाऽहमिहागत: ।
प्रचोदितस्तयैवोग्रैः
त्वत्सकाशमिहागतः ॥
I did not come here on my own, abandoning her. I came here looking for you, driven by her scathing words.
3.59.7
आर्येणेव पराक्रुष्टम्
हा सीते लक्ष्मणेति च ।
परित्राहीति यद्वाक्यम्
मैथिल्यास्तच्छ्रुतिं गतम् ॥
The cry of ‘Ha Seetā, Ha Lakshmaṇa, save me!’ in your voice was heard by Maithili.
3.59.8
सा तमार्तस्वरं श्रुत्वा
तव स्नेहेन मैथिली ।
गच्छ गच्छेति मामाह
रुदन्ती भयविह्वला ॥
Upon hearing that cry of desperation, Maithili became terrified and cried, telling me ‘go, go’, out of love for you.
3.59.9
प्रचोद्यमानेन मया
गच्छेति बहुशस्तया ।
प्रत्युक्ता मैथिली वाक्यम्
इदं त्वत्प्रत्ययान्वितम् ॥
Even though she told me multiple times to go, I, with full confidence in you, told Maithili these words in response:
3.59.10
न तत्पश्याम्यहं रक्षो
यदस्य भयमावहेत् ।
निर्वृता भव नास्त्येतत्
केनाप्येवमुदाहृतम् ॥
I can think of no Rākshasa who can create fear in him. Please relax and ignore that cry. Someone else must have uttered it.
3.59.11
विगर्हितं च नीचं च
कथमार्योऽभिधास्यति ।
त्राहीति वचनं सीते
यस्त्रायेत्त्रिदशानपि ॥
O Seetā! How could the noble one utter such base and despicable words, when he is capable of saving the Dēvatas themselves?
3.59.12
किं निमित्तं तु केनापि
भ्रातुरालम्ब्य मे स्वरम् ।
राक्षसेनेरितं वाक्यम्
त्राहि त्राहीति शोभने ॥
O charming one! These words ‘Save me, save me’ were uttered for some reason, by some Rākshasa, taking the voice of my brother.
3.59.13
विस्वरं व्याहृतं वाक्यम्
लक्ष्मण त्राहि मामिति ।
न भवत्या व्यथा कार्या
कुनारीजनसेविता ॥
The words, ‘Lakshmaṇa, save me!’ are uttered in a strange tone. You should not distress yourself like a lowly woman is apt to do.
3.59.14
अलं वैक्लब्यमालम्ब्य
स्वस्था भव निरुत्सुका ।
न सोऽस्ति त्रिषु लोकेषु
पुमान्वै राघवं रणे ।
जातो वा जायमानो वा
संयुगे यः पराजयेत् ।
न जय्यो राघवो युद्धे
देवैश्शक्रपुरोगमैः ॥
Enough of agitating yourself; please calm down and be at ease. There is no man, already born or to be born, who can defeat Rāghava in an armed encounter. He cannot be defeated in a battle even by the Dēvas with Ṡakra at their head.
3.59.16
एवमुक्ता तु वैदेही
परिमोहितचेतना ।
उवाचाश्रूणि मुञ्चन्ती
दारुणं मामिदं वचः ॥
When told so, Vaidēhee, with her mind gone berserk, said these terrible words to me with tears rolling down:
3.59.17
भावो मयि तवात्यर्थम्
पाप एव निवेशितः ।
विनष्टे भ्रातरि प्राप्तुम्
न च त्वं मामवाप्स्यसि ॥
You are harboring the extremely sinful thought of having me when your brother dies. You will never get me!
3.59.18
सङ्केताद्भरतेन त्वम्
रामं समनुगच्छसि ।
क्रोशन्तं हि यथात्यर्थम्
नैवमभ्यवपद्यसे ॥
You followed Rāma prodded by Bharata, and that is why you are not going to Rāma who is crying bitterly.
3.59.19
रिपुः प्रच्छन्नचारी त्वम्
मदर्थमनुगच्छसि ।
राघवस्यान्तरप्रेप्सुः
तथैनं नाभिपद्यसे ॥
You are an enemy, moving about in disguise. You are interested in me and are looking for an opportunity free from the presence of Rāghava; and that is why you are not going to him.
3.59.20
एवमुक्तो हि वैदेह्या
संरब्धो रक्तलोचनः ।
क्रोधात्प्रस्फुरमाणोष्ठ
आश्रमादभिनिर्गतः ॥
Thus told by Vaidēhee, I started from the Āṡrama hastily, with my eyes turning red and my lips quivering from anger.
3.59.21
एवं ब्रुवाणं सौमित्रिम्
रामस्सन्तापमोहितः ।
अब्रवीद्दुष्कृतं सौम्य
तां विना यत्त्वमागतः ॥
To that Sowmitri who spoke thus, Rāma, dazed with grief, said: ‘My dear, you have done a terrible thing by coming here without her!’
3.59.22
जानन्नपि समर्थं माम्
रक्षसां विनिवारणे ।
अनेन क्रोधवाक्येन
मैथिल्या निस्सृतो भवान् ॥
Even though you know that I am capable of warding off Rākshasas, you came away because of the words Maithili spoke in anger!
3.59.23
न हि ते परितुष्यामि
त्यक्त्वा यद्यासि मैथिलीम् ।
क्रुद्धायाः परुषं श्रुत्वा
तां विहाय त्वमागतः ॥
I am not at all happy about you coming away abandoning Maithili; You heard some angry and harsh words and you just came away leaving her (alone)!
3.59.24
सर्वथा त्वविनीतं ते
सीतया यत्प्रचोदितः ।
क्रोधस्य वशमापन्नो
नाकरोश्शासनं मम ॥
It, by all means, is your failure that you, provoked by Seetā, caved in to anger and ignored my instructions.
Lakshmaṇa is very capable. But he needs Rāma to guide him all the time. Without the guidance of Rāma, Lakshmaṇa is liable to make mistakes.
You will see a sharp contrast in Hanuman, later in Sundara Kāṇḍa, who makes several decisions on his own, with no one to guide or advise him.
3.59.25
असौ हि राक्षसश्शेते
शरेणाभिहतो मया ।
मृगरूपेण येनाहम्
आश्रमादपवाहितः ॥
This Rākshasa, by whom, in the form of a deer, I was drawn away from the Āṡrama, lies dead, shot by my arrows!
3.59.26
विकृष्य चापं परिधाय सायकं
सलीलबाणेन च ताडितो मया ।
मार्गीं तनुं त्यज्य स विक्लबस्वरो
बभूव केयूरधरस्सराक्षसः ॥
I drew (the string of) the bow and shot the arrow, hitting him with the arrow with ease. He gave up the deer form and regained the form of Rākshasa with shoulder rings, crying in distress.
3.59.27
शराहतेनैव तदार्तया गिरा
स्वरं समाऽऽलम्ब्य सुदूरसुश्रवम् ।
उदाहृतं तद्वचनं सुदारुणं
त्वमागतो येन विहाय मैथिलीम् ॥
As soon as he was struck by the arrow, he adopted a pitiful voice that could be heard from a distance, saying those terrible words which made you come, leaving Maithili.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे एकोनषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty ninth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
