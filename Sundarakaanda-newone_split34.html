Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 34
In this Sarga, Hanumān, who heard Seetā describing her story, tells her that he has come as the envoy of Rāma, and that Rāma and Lakshmaṇa inquire her wellbeing. Seetā’s heart swells with joy hearing the wellbeing of the brothers and she feels good that her waiting for a long while without taking her own life has proved to be worth it. She feels very affectionate towards Hanumān and Hanumān then comes close to her.
But as he moves close to her, she doubts if he is actually Rāvaṇa in disguise. She regrets being candid with him. But then, she also wonders whether she is dreaming. She wishes she saw Rāma and Lakshmaṇa in her dream. She also wonders whether she is hallucinating and creating images in her own mind. Finally, she concludes that the Vānara must be Rāvaṇa and stays quiet without answering him.
Hanumān, sensing the doubt n her mind, sings praises of Rāma again and tells her that Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva inquire her wellbeing. He assures her that, not before too long, she would see them coming along with crores of Vānara and kill Rāvaṇa.
He pleads with her to trust him.
5.34.1
तस्यास्तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
हनुमान्हरिपुङ्गवः ।
दुःखाद्दुःखाभिभूतायाः
सान्त्वमुत्तरमब्रवीत् ॥
On hearing those words of hers, who was beset with one misery after the other, Hanumān, the bull among Vānaras spoke these comforting words:
5.34.2
अहं रामस्य सन्देशात्
देवि दूतस्तवागतः ।
वैदेहि कुशली रामः
त्वां च कौशलमब्रवीत् ॥
O Dēvi, O Vaidēhee! I have come here to you as an envoy with a message from Rāma. He is doing well and wishes you well.
5.34.3
यो ब्रह्ममस्त्रं वेदांश्च
वेद वेदविदांवरः ।
स त्वां दाशरथी रामः
देवि कौशलमब्रवीत् ॥
Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, who won the Brahmāstra and knows the Vēdas and who excels among those who have mastered Vēdas inquires your wellbeing.
5.34.4
लक्ष्मणश्च महातेजा
भर्तुस्तेऽनुचरः प्रियः ।
कृतवान्शोकसन्तप्तः
शिरसा तेऽभिवादनम् ॥
And Lakshmaṇa of immense strength, beloved brother and follower of your husband, salutes you bowing his head, his mind scorched by grief.
5.34.5
सा तयोः कुशलं देवी
निशम्य नरसिंहयोः ।
प्रीतिसंहृष्टसर्वाङ्गी
हनुमन्तमथाब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing the wellbeing of both of those lions among men, every limb of that lady swelled with joy and happiness. Then she spoke to Hanumān:
5.34.6
कल्याणी बत गाथेयम्
लौकिकी प्रतिभाति मा ।
एति जीवन्तमानन्दो
नरं वर्षशतादपि ॥
The nice adage that ‘it might take over a hundred years, but one who manages to hang on to life will certainly find joy’ seems to be true, to me.
5.34.7
तया समागते तस्मिन्
प्रीतिरुत्पादिताऽद्भुता ।
परस्परेण चालापम्
विश्वस्तौ तौ प्रचक्रतुः ॥
A measureless affection then engendered in her towards him who came there (looking for her). Then they confided in and talked to each other.
This Ṡlōka seems to be one of those misplaced Ṡlōkas. The flow will be smooth if we ignore this one Ṡlōka here. This Ṡlōka says they confided in each other. But, as is evident from the following Ṡlōkas, there is more conversation to happen, before Seetā trusts Hanumān to be who he says he is.
5.34.8
तस्यास्तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
हनुमान्हरियूथपः ।
सीतायाश्शोकदीनायाः
समीपमुपचक्रमे ॥
Hearing those words of hers, who was in grief and in a pitiful state, Hanumān, chief among Vānara warriors, moved closer to her.
5.34.9
यथा यथा समीपं स
हनुमानुपसर्पति ।
तथा तथा रावणं सा
तं सीता परिशङ्कते ॥
The closer Hanumān moved towards her, the more Seetā felt that he could actually be Rāvaṇa (in disguise).
5.34.10
अहो धिग्दुष्कृतमिदम्
कथितं हि यदस्य मे ।
रूपान्तरमुपागम्य
स एवायं हि रावणः ॥
Oh my! Dhik! It is a terrible mistake that I have been so candid with him! This must be Rāvaṇa in disguise!
5.34.11
तामशोकस्य शाखां सा
विमुक्त्वा शोककर्शिता ।
तस्यामेवानवद्याङ्गी
धरण्यां समुपाविशत् ॥
And she of flawless limbs let go her hold of the branch of the Aṡōka tree and slumped down on the ground in distress.
5.34.12
हनुमानपि दुःखार्ताम्
तां दृष्ट्वा भयमोहिताम् ।
अवन्दत महाबाहुः
ततस्तां जनकात्मजाम् ।
सा चैनं भयवित्रस्ता
भूयो नैवाभ्युदैक्षत ॥
Then, Hanumān of mighty arm, on his part, bowed to the daughter of Janaka, who was overwhelmed with fear and distressed with grief. But she, trembling with fear, would not even look at him again.
5.34.13
तं दृष्ट्वा वन्दमानं तु
सीता शशिनिभानना ।
अब्रवीद्धीर्घमुच्छ्वस्य
वानरं मधुरस्वरा ॥
Then taking a look at the Vānara who was bowing, and heaving a long sigh, Seetā of face pleasant like the moon uttered these words in her sweet voice:
5.34.14
मायां प्रविष्टो मायावी
यदि त्वं रावणस्स्वयम् ।
उत्पादयसि मे भूयः
सन्तापं तन्न शोभनम् ॥
If you are indeed Rāvaṇa himself appearing in front of me in disguise, of which you are capable, you are only aggravating my grief again. It is not fair!
5.34.15
स्वं परित्यज्य रूपं यः
परिव्राजकरूपध्रुत् ।
जनस्थाने मया दृष्टः
त्वं स एवासि रावणः ॥
You are indeed the same Rāvaṇa who gave up his original form and was seen by me in Janasthāna in the form of a wandering mendicant.
5.34.16
उपवासकृशां दीनाम्
कामरूप निशाचर ।
सन्तापयसि मां भूयः
सन्तप्तां तन्न शोभनम् ॥
O Night-rover, who is in disguise! You are tormenting me again, while I already feel tormented and miserable and am emaciated, fasting. This is so unfair!
5.34.17
अथवा नैतदेवं हि
यन्मया परिशङ्कितम् ।
मनसो हि मम प्रीतिः
उत्पन्ना तव दर्शनात् ॥
Perhaps, all this could be just my own imagination and suspicion, for the sight of you engenders pleasure in my mind.
5.34.18
यदि रामस्य दूतस्त्वम्
आगतो भद्रमस्तु ते ।
पृच्छामि त्वां हरिश्रेष्ठ
प्रिया रामकथा हि मे ॥
If you indeed have come here as the messenger of Rāma, may all bode well for you! I would like to ask you, O best of Vānaras, about Rāma, for I love to hear of him.
5.34.19
गुणान्रामस्य कथय
प्रियस्य मम वानर ।
चित्तं हरसि मे सौम्य
नदीकूलं यथा रयः ॥
O Vānara! Please tell me about the qualities of my beloved Rāma! My dear, you are chipping my heart away like rapids does a bank.
5.34.20
अहो स्वप्नस्य सुखता
याहमेवं चिराहृता ।
प्रेषितं नाम पश्यामि
राघवेण वनौकसम् ॥
Ha, what a comforting dream! I see a Vana-dweller sent by Rāghava after a very long time since I have been abducted!
5.34.21
स्वप्नेऽपि यद्यहं वीरम्
राघवं सहलक्ष्मणम् ।
पश्येयं नावसीदेयम्
स्वप्नोऽपि मम मत्सरी ॥
I can hold myself if I could see Rāghava, the Veera, along with Lakshmaṇa, at least in a dream. But even the dream is cross with me!
5.34.22
नाहं स्वप्नमिमं मन्ये
स्वप्ने दृष्ट्वा हि वानरम् ।
न शक्योऽभ्युदयः प्राप्तुम्
प्राप्तश्चाभ्युदयो मम ॥
I do not think this is a dream, for seeing a monkey in a dream should not bode well, but I do feel better.
5.34.23
किन्नु स्याचित्तमोहोऽयम्
भवेद्वातगतिस्त्वियम् ।
उन्मादजो विकारो वा
स्यादियं मृगतृष्णिका ॥
Or, is it that my mind is confused, or the chemicals in my body are in turmoil? Or, is it that I am becoming crazy or perverted? Or, is it a mirage?
5.34.24
अथवा नायमुन्मादो
मोहोऽप्युन्मादलक्षणः ।
सम्बुध्ये चाहमात्मानम्
इयं चापि वनौकसम् ॥
Or may be I am neither confused nor crazy, nor anything such, for I am able to clearly identify myself and this Vānara.
5.34.25
इत्येवं बहुधा सीता
सम्प्रधार्य बलाबलम् ।
रक्षसां कामरूपत्वात्
मेने तं राक्षसाधिपम् ॥
Thinking thus about all the possibilities, Seetā finally concluded that he must be the lord of Rākshasas, because the Rākshasas can assume any form at will.
5.34.26
एतां बुद्धिं तदा कृत्वा
सीता सा तनुमध्यमा ।
न प्रतिव्याजहाराथ
वानरं जनकात्मजा ॥
Having thus decided, Seetā, daughter of Janaka, of slender waist, abstained from answering the Vānara.
5.34.27
सीतायाश्चिन्तितं बुद्ध्वा
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ।
श्रोत्रानुकूलैर्वचनैः
तदा तां संप्रहर्षयत् ॥
Sensing what was going through Seetā’s mind, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, made her happy with words that were pleasing to her ears:
5.34.28
आदित्य इव तेजस्वी
लोककान्तश्शशी यथा ।
राजा सर्वस्य लोकस्य
देवो वैश्रवणो यथा ।
विक्रमेणोपपन्नश्च
यथा विष्णुर्महायशाः ॥
He radiates splendor like the sun. He charms the worlds like the moon. He is the king of all the worlds like the Dēva Vaiṡravaṇa. He is of great renown and he is endowed with prowess like that of Vishṇu.
5.34.30
सत्यवादी मधुरवाक्
देवो वाचस्पतिर्यथा ।
रूपवान्सुभगः श्रीमान्
कन्दर्प इव मूर्तिमान् ॥
He is a man of his word. His speech is sweet like that of the Dēva Bṛihaspati. He is attractive, handsome and has a rich bearing. He looks just like Manmatha.
5.34.31
स्थानक्रोधः प्रहर्ता च
श्रेष्ठो लोके महारथः ।
बाहुच्छायामवष्टब्धो
यस्य लोको महात्मनः ॥
He frowns on what deserves a frown and hits what needs to be hit. He is the best, and he can (singlehandedly) beat an army of ten thousand from his chariot. It is under the able protection of his arm that the entire world reposes safely.
5.34.32
अपकृष्याश्रमपदान्
मृगरूपेण राघवम् ।
शून्ये येनापनीतासि
तस्य द्रक्ष्यसि यत्फलम् ॥
You will see now what happens to him, who has abducted you when no one was around, after luring away Rāghava from the Āṡrama, deploying the phantom deer.
5.34.33
न चिराद्रावणं संख्ये
यो वधिष्यति वीर्यवान् ।
रोषप्रमुक्तैरिषुभिः
ज्वलद्भिरिव पावकैः ।
तेनाहं प्रेषितो दूतः
त्वत्सकाशमिहागतः ।
त्वद्वियोगेन दुःखार्तः
स त्वां कौशलमब्रवीत् ॥
It would not be too long from now before that valiant one who sent me as his messenger to your presence here will be killing him with the arrows that blaze like tongues of fire, shot by him in his rage. He is distraught with grief because of separation from you and inquires your wellbeing.
5.34.35
लक्ष्मणश्च महातेजाः
सुमित्रानन्दवर्धनः ।
अभिवाद्य महाबाहुः
स त्वां कौशलमब्रवीत् ॥
Lakshmaṇa of immense strength and mighty arm, the delight of Sumitrā, also salutes you and inquires your wellbeing.
5.34.36
रामस्य च सखा देवि
सुग्रीवो नाम वानरः ।
राजा वानरमुख्यानाम्
स त्वां कौशलमब्रवीत् ॥
O Dēvi! And the Vānara, Sugreeva by name, Rāma’s friend and the king of Vānara chiefs, also inquires your wellbeing.
5.34.37
नित्यं स्मरति रामस्त्वाम्
ससुग्रीवस्सलक्ष्मणः ।
दिष्ट्या जीवसि वैदेहि
राक्षसीवशमागता ॥
Rāma, along with Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva thinks of your every day. Thank Providence, O Vaidēhee, that you are alive; falling into the hands of Rākshasis!
5.34.38
नचिराद्द्रक्ष्यसे रामम्
लक्ष्मणं च महाबलम् ।
मध्ये वानरकोटीनाम्
सुग्रीवं चामितौजसम् ॥
It would not be too long before you see Rāma, Lakshmaṇa of immense strength and Sugreeva of immeasurable strength along with crores of Vānaras!
5.34.39
अहं सुग्रीवसचिवो
हनुमान्नाम वानरः ।
प्रविष्टो नगरीं लङ्काम्
लङ्घयित्वा महोदधिम् ॥
I am the minister of Sugreeva. I am a Vānara, and my name is Hanumān. I entered this city of Laṅkā after leaping over the great ocean.
5.34.40
कृत्वा मूर्ध्नि पदन्यासम्
रावणस्य दुरात्मनः ।
त्वां द्रष्टुमुपयातोऽहम्
समाश्रित्य पराक्रमम् ॥
Setting my foot on the head of the evil minded Rāvaṇa, I came here to see you, relying on my own prowess alone.
5.34.41
नाहमस्मि तथा देवि
यथा मामवगच्छसि ।
विशङ्का त्यज्यतामेषा
श्रद्धत्स्व वदतो मम ॥
My Dēvi! I am not who you worry I am. Please give up your apprehensions and trust what I say.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे चतुस्त्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty fourth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
