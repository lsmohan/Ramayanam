Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 103
In this Sarga the battle between Rāvaṇa and Rāma continues, with each drenching the other with arrows. Rāma comes down heavily on Rāvaṇa, taunting him for his cowardice in abducting Seetā in his absence in a shameless manner. As the fight progresses, Rāma's valor, strength, enthusiasm doubles,while Rāvaṇa loses his mettle. Seeing him in that flight, the charioteer drives away the chariot from the battle field.
6.103.1
स तेन तु तथा क्रोधात्
काकुत्स्थेनार्दितो रणे ।
रावणः समरश्लाघी
महाक्रोधमुपागमत् ॥
Oppressed thus by the furious Kākutstha, Rāvaṇa, who had a reputation as a good fighter, was greatly incensed.
6.103.2
स दीप्तनयनोऽमर्षात्
चापमुद्यम्य वीर्यवान् ।
अभ्यर्दयत् सुसङ्क्रुद्धो
राघवं परमाहवे ॥
With blazing eyes, and taken over by anger, the valiant one lifted his bow in that great fight and oppressed Rāghava in extreme rage.
6.103.3
बाणधारासहस्रैस्तु
स तोयद इवाम्बरात् ।
राघवं रावणो बाणैः
तटाकमिव पूरयन् ॥
With a thousand fusillades of shafts, Rāvaṇa filled up Rāghava like a cloud in the sky filling up a pond.
6.103.4
पूरितः शरजालेन
धनुर्मुक्तेन संयुगे ।
महागिरिरिवाकम्प्यः
काकुत्स्थो न प्रकम्पते ॥
Though every part of his body was thus covered with the arrows shot from the enemy’s bow in that battle, Kākutstha was shaken in the least and stood like an unshakable, mighty mountain.
6.103.5
स शरैः शरजालानि
वारयन् समरे स्थितः ।
गभस्तीनिव सूर्यस्य
प्रतिजग्राह वीर्यवान् ॥
The valiant one stood his ground in that battle, warding off those torrents of arrows with his own, taking them no more seriously than one does the rays of the sun.
6.103.6
ततः शरसहस्राणि
क्षिप्रहस्तो निशाचरः ।
निजघानोरसि क्रुद्धो
राघवस्य महात्मनः ॥
The quickhanded Night-rover struck Mahātma Rāghava on the chest with thousands of arrows.
6.103.7
स शोणित समादिग्धः
समरे लक्ष्मणाग्रजः ।
दृष्टः फुल्ल इवारण्ये
सुमहान् किंशुकद्रुमः ॥
Bedaubed with blood, Lakshmaṇa’s elder brother looked, on that battlefield, like a huge Kiṃṡuka tree in flower in a forest.
6.103.8
शराभिघातसंरब्धः
सोऽपि जग्राह सायकान् ।
काकुत्स्थः सुमहातेजा
युगान्तादित्यतेजसः ॥
Unable to brook that assault of arrows, the extraordinarily powerful Kākutstha also grabbed arrows that shone like the sun at the end of the Yuga.
6.103.9
ततोऽयोन्यं सुसंरब्धौ
उभौ तौ रामरावणौ ।
शरान्धकारे समरे
नोपालक्षयतां तदा ॥
Rāma and Rāvaṇa, both of whom were engaged zealously in that fight, could not see each other as it became all dark under the cover of the arrows.
6.103.10
ततः क्रोधसमाविष्टो
रामो दशरथात्मजः ।
उवाच रावणं वीरः
प्रहस्य परुषं वचः ॥
Then the valiant Rāma, son of Daṡaratha, burning with rage, said these cutting words to Rāvaṇa, laughing at him:
6.103.11
मम भार्या जनस्थानात्
अज्ञानाद्राक्षसाधम ।
हृता ते विवशा यस्मात्
तस्मात्त्वं नासि वीर्यवान् ॥
O vile scum of a Rākshasa, you, who have abducted my wife from Janasthāna when she was helpless, are no man of valor.
6.103.12
मया विरहितां दीनाम्
वर्तमानां महावने ।
वैदेहीं प्रसभं हृत्वा
शूरोऽहमिति मन्यसे ॥
Having carried off the miserable Vaidēhee, who was alone in that great forest without me beside her, by brute force, you flatter yourself that you are a hero.
6.103.13
स्त्रीषु शूर विनाथासु
परदाराभिमर्शक ।
कृत्वा कापुरुषं कर्म
शूरोऽहमिति मन्यसे ॥
You are a purloiner of other men’s wives. Having done this cowardly act, you think you have done something heroic. All your heroism is only against helpless women.
6.103.14
भिन्नमर्याद निर्लज्ज
चारित्रेष्वनवस्थित ।
दर्पान्मृत्युमुपादाय
शूरोऽहमिति मन्यसे ॥
You violate all decency! You are shameless! You do not stand by any principles! In your haughtiness, you have brought death upon yourself and imagine yourself a hero!
6.103.15
शूरेण धनदभ्रात्रा
बलैः समुदितेन च ।
श्लाघनीयं महत्कर्म
यशस्यं च कृतं त्वया ॥
Ah! What a great, laudable deed, which will surely make you famous, have you done, being the great warrior and commander of all those armies, and the brother of Kubēra that you are!
6.103.16
उत्सेकेनाभिपन्नस्य
गर्हितस्याहितस्य च ।
कर्मणः प्राप्नुहीदानीम्
तस्याद्य सुमहत् फलम् ॥
You shall, this very day, reap the wonderful fruits of your despicable and detrimental act that you committed in your conceit.
6.103.17
शूरोऽहमिति चात्मानम्
अवगच्छसि दुर्मते ।
नैव लज्जाऽस्ति ते सीताम्
चोरवद् व्यपकर्षतः ॥
You think yourself to be a great hero, you wicked creature; you feel no shame at all, having carried off Seetā, like a thief.
6.103.18
यदि मत्सन्निधौ सीता
धर्षितास्यात्त्वया बलात् ।
भ्रातरं तु खरं पश्येः
तदा मत्सायकैर्हतः ॥
If you had tried to violate Seetā in my presence, you would have met your (dead) brother Khara, slain by my arrows.
6.103.19
दिष्ट्याऽसि मम मन्दात्मन्
चक्षुर्विषयमागतः ।
अद्य त्वां सायकैस्तीक्ष्णैः
नयामि यमसादनम् ॥
By the good grace of Providence you, a pinhead, have fallen in the range of my eyesight; I will send you to the abode of Yama this very day with my sharp arrows.
6.103.20
अद्य ते मच्छरैश्छिन्नम्
शिरो ज्वलितकुण्डलम् ।
क्रव्यादा व्यपकर्षन्तु
विकीर्णं रणपांसुषु ॥
This very day, severed by my arrows, and thrown into the dusty battlefield, your head, with its bright ear-pendents, shall be dragged hither and thither by the carrion eaters!
6.103.21
निपत्योरसि गृध्रास्ते
क्षितौ क्षिप्तस्य रावण ।
पिबन्तु रुधिरं तर्षात्
शरशल्यान्तरोत्थितम् ॥
The vultures shall descend on your chest as you lie on the ground, flung down, O Rāvaṇa, and slake their thirst by drinking your blood that wells up from the arrow wounds.
6.103.22
अद्य मद्बाणभिन्नस्य
गतासोः पतितस्य ते ।
कर्षन्त्वन्त्राणि पतगा
गरुत्मन्त इवोरगान् ॥
As you lie dead today, mangled by my arrows, the birds shall pull out your entrails, like eagles tearing apart the serpents.
6.103.23
इत्येवं संवदन् वीरो
रामः शत्रुनिबर्हणः ।
राक्षसेन्द्रं समीपस्थम्
शरवर्षैरवाकिरत् ॥
Taunting him thus, the valiant Rāma, a destroyer of the foe, sprayed a shower of arrows on the king of Rākshasas.
6.103.24
बभूव द्विगुणं वीर्यम्
बलं हर्षश्च संयुगे ।
रामस्यास्त्रबलं चैव
शत्रोर्निधनकाङ्क्षिणः ॥
Rāma's valor, strength, enthusiasm, and the reserves of his Astras seemed to have doubled, as he was thus resolved on annihilating the enemy.
6.103.25
प्रादुर्बभूवुरस्त्राणि
सर्वाणि विदितात्मनः ।
प्रहर्षाच्च महातेजाः
शीघ्रहस्ततरोऽभवत् ॥
Then every single Astra became evident to him, who was absolutely self-aware. The extraordinarily powerful one found himself in high spirits and he became even more adept with his hands.
6.103.26
शुभान्येतानि चिह्नानि
विज्ञायात्मगतानि सः ।
भूय एवार्दयद्रामो
रावणं राक्षसान्तकृत् ॥
Noticing and bolstered by these positive indications, he, the annihilator of Rākshasas, intensified his assault on Rāvaṇa.
6.103.27
हरीणां चाश्मनिकरैः
शरवर्षैश्च राघवात् ।
हन्यमानो दशग्रीवो
विघूर्णहृदयोऽभवत् ॥
The Ten-necked reeled, battered by the fusillades of boulders hurled by the Vānaras and barrage of arrows shot by Rāghava.
6.103.28
यदा च शस्त्रं नारेभे
न चकर्ष शसनम् ।
नास्य प्रत्यकरोद्वीर्यम्
विक्लेवेनान्तरात्मना ॥
His heart in a turmoil, he could neither wield a weapon nor draw his bow, nor respond to his (Rāma's) valor in kind.
6.103.29
क्षिप्ताश्चापि शरास्तेन
शस्त्राणि विविधानि च ।
न रणार्थाय वर्तन्ते
मृत्युकालेऽभिवर्तत ॥
The arrows and the many kinds of weapons hurled by him failed him in that battle, with death hovering over him.
6.103.30
सूतस्तु रथनेताऽस्य
तदवस्थं समीक्ष्य तम् ।
शनैर्युद्धादसम्भ्रान्तो
रथं तस्यापवाहयत् ॥
Noticing his plight, the charioteer slowly and circumspectly drove his chariot away from the battlefield.
These two Ṡlōkas describe the same, but slightly differently. The first one says the charioteer drove away the chariot 'slowly' and the second one says 'quickly'. The only possible explanation for this contradiction is that the second one, which is in a long meter, must have been compiled from an alternate version of Rāmāyaṇa that came into vogue over time.
6.103.31
रथं च तस्याथ जवेन सारथिः
निवार्य भीमं जलदस्वनं तदा ।
जगाम भीत्या समरान्महीपतिम्
निरस्तवीर्यं पतितं समीक्ष्य ॥
Noticing the king lose his mettle and fall, the charioteer was scared and retreated from the battlefield, quickly driving away the chariot that rumbled like a cloud.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे त्र्युत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and third Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
