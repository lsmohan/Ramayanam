Baala Kaanda - Sarga 41
In this Sarga, Sagara sends his grandson Aṃṡumān in search of his sons and the horse. Aṃṡumān learns about the death of his uncles and gets advice from Garuḍa that only the waters of the divine Gaṅgā can send them to Heaven.
He returns to his grandfather with the horse and tells him about the fate of his uncles. Sagara grieves the death of his sons, completes the Yajña but would not find a way to bring Gaṅgā to the earth during his life time.
1.41.1
पुत्रांश्चिरगतान् ज्ञात्वा
सगरो रघुनन्दन ।
नप्तारमब्रवीद्राजा
दीप्यमानं स्वतेजसा ॥
O prince of Raghu Dynasty! Realizing that his children were gone for too long, Sagara told his grandson, who was radiant with natural brilliance:
1.41.2
शूरश्च कृतविद्यश्च
पूर्वैस्तुल्योऽसि तेजसा ।
पितॄणां गतिमन्विच्छ
येन चाश्वोऽपवाहितः ॥
You are valiant, well-read and well-trained to be comparable with any of your ancestors. Please go and find the whereabouts of your uncles and the abductor of the horse.
1.41.3
अन्तर्भौमानि सत्त्वानि
वीर्यवन्ति महान्ति च ।
तेषां त्वं प्रतिघातार्थम्
सासिं गृह्णीष्व कार्मुकम् ॥
The beings underneath the earth are strong and mammoth. Please take with you the Astras and bow that are good for putting them in check.
1.41.4
अभिवाद्याभिवाद्यांस्त्वम्
हत्वा विघ्नकरानपि ।
सिद्धार्थस्सन्निवर्तस्व
मम यज्ञस्य पारगः ॥
Pay respects on your way to those who deserve it. Kill those that come in your way. Come back with your mission accomplished, to take this Yajña to its final completion.
What a crisp and well-articulated commandment!
1.41.5
एवमुक्तोंऽशुमान् सम्यक्
सगरेण महात्मना ।
धनुरादाय खङ्गं च
जगाम लघुविक्रमः ॥
Told thus by the Mahātma Sagara in no uncertain terms, Aṃṡumān took the bow and sword and left immediately.
1.41.6
स खातं पितृभिर्मार्गं
अन्तर्भौमं महात्मभिः ।
प्रापद्यत नरश्रेष्ठः
तेन राज्ञाऽभिचोदितः ॥
O best among men! He, then, prodded by the command of the king, followed along the path that was dug in the ground by his great uncles.
1.41.7
दैत्यदानवरक्षोभिः
पिशाचपतगोरगैः ।
पूज्यमानं महातेजा
दिशागजमपश्यत ॥
On his way, he saw the great, big and radiant elephant that was praised and revered by Daityas, Dānavas, Rākshasas, Piṡācas, Birds and Serpents.
1.41.8
स तं प्रदक्षिणं कृत्वा
पृष्ट्वा चैव निरामयम् ।
पितॄन् स परिपप्रच्छ
वाजिहर्तारमेव च ॥
He paid respects to it by circumambulating and sought its blessings for his good health and well being and then inquired about his uncles and about the abductor of the horse.
1.41.9
दिशागजस्तु तच्छ्रुत्वा
प्रत्याहांशुमतो वचः ।
आसमञ्ज कृतार्थस्त्वम्
सहाश्वः शीघ्रमेष्यसि ॥
That majestic elephant heard him and said: “O son of Asamañja! You shall certainly accomplish your mission! You will get back with the horse very soon!”
1.41.10
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
सर्वानेव दिशागजान् ।
यथाक्रमं यथान्यायम्
प्रष्टुं समुपचक्रमे ॥
After listening to that great elephant, he proceeded to ask all the other great elephants in all the other directions one by one, in an appropriate manner.
1.41.11
तैश्च सर्वैर्दिशापालैः
वाक्यज्ञैर्वाक्यकोविदैः ।
पूजितः सहयश्चैव
गन्तासीत्यभिचोदितः ॥
All the elephants in the other directions that were good at making sensible conversations, also received him with respect and consideration and assured him that he would return along with the horse.
1.41.12
तेषां तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
जगाम लघुविक्रमः ।
भस्मराशीकृता यत्र
पितरस्तस्य सागराः ॥
Taking directions from them, he finally reached the place where his uncles, the sons of Sagara, had been burnt to ash.
1.41.13
स दुःखवशमापन्नः
स्त्वसमञ्जसुतस्तदा ।
चुक्रोश परमार्तस्तु
वधात्तेषां सुदुःखितः ॥
Then the son of Asamañja cried pitifully seeing all his uncles killed.
1.41.14
यज्ञीयं च हयं तत्र
चरन्तमविदूरतः ।
ददर्श पुरुषव्याघ्रो
दुःखशोकसमन्वितः ॥
That tiger among men, crying inconsolably, saw the horse of the Yajña nearby.
1.41.15
स तेषां राजपुत्राणाम्
कर्तुकामो जलक्रियाम् ।
सलिलार्थी महातेजा
न चापश्यज्जलाशयम् ॥
The supremely radiant Aṃṡumān wanted to offer water oblations to the dead princes, but could not find any water around there.
1.41.16
विसार्य निपुणां दृष्टिम्
ततोऽपश्यत् खगाधिपम् ।
पितॄणां मातुलं राम
सुपर्णमनिलोपमम् ॥
O Rāma, he then stretched his acute vision and saw the uncle of his uncles, the lord of birds, with majestic wings and speed comparable to the wind.
The bird referred to here is Garuḍa. He is the uncle of his uncles, because their mother Sumati is the sister of Garuḍa. (See 1.38.14)
1.41.17
स चैवमब्रवीद्वाक्यम्
वैनतेयो महाबलः ।
मा शुचः पुरुषव्याघ्र
वधोऽयं लोकसम्मतः ॥
The mighty strong son of Vinata (Garuḍa) said to him: O tiger among men! Do not grieve. The whole world would appreciate their annihilation.
1.41.18
कपिलेनाप्रमेयेन
दग्धा हीमे महाबलाः ।
सलिलं नार्हसि प्राज्ञ
दातुमेषां हि लौकिकम् ॥
These mighty strong were burnt down by the great Kapila. The earthly waters are not to be given to them.
1.41.19
गङ्गा हिमवतो ज्येष्ठा
दुहिता पुरुषर्षभ ।
तस्यां कुरु महाबाहो
पितॄणां तु जलक्रियाम् ॥
O bull among men! O you of mighty arm! Gaṅgā is the eldest daughter of Himavān. Use her water to offer water oblations to your uncles!
1.41.20
भस्मराशीकृतानेतान्
प्लावयेल्लोकपावनी ।
तया क्लिन्नमिदं भस्म
गङ्गया लोककान्तया ।
षष्टिं पुत्रसहस्राणि
स्वर्गलोकं नयिष्यति ॥
Gaṅgā, the sanctifier of all the worlds, will sanctify them. When Gaṅgā, the beloved of all worlds, touches these ashes, all the six thousand princes will reach the Heaven.
1.41.21
गच्छ चाश्वं महाभाग
तं गृह्य पुरुषर्षभ ।
यज्ञं पैतामहं वीर
संवर्तयितुमर्हसि ॥
O bull among men! O blessed one! May you return along with the horse and help complete the Yajña of your grandfather.
1.41.22
सुपर्णवचनं श्रुत्वा
सोंऽशुमानतिवीर्यवान् ।
त्वरितं हयमादाय
पुनरायान्महायशाः ॥
Hearing those words of Suparṇa (Garuḍa) the valiant and well regarded Aṃṡumān took the horse immediately and returned.
1.41.23
ततो राजानमासाद्य
दीक्षितं रघुनन्दन ।
न्यवेदयद्यथावृत्तम्
सुपर्णवचनं तथा ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty, then he went to the king, who was in Deeksha. He recounted the entire sequence of happenings including the guidance of Garuḍa.
1.41.24
तच्छ्रुत्वा घोरसङ्काशम्
वाक्यमंशुमतो नृपः ।
यज्ञं निर्वर्तयामास
यथाकल्पं यथाविधि ॥
The king heard the dreadful news from Aṃṡumān. He then completed the Yajña in accordance with the protocol and guidelines.
1.41.25
स्वपुरं चागमच्छ्रीमान्
इष्टयज्ञो महीपतिः ।
गङ्गायाश्चागमे राजा
निश्चयं नाध्यगच्छत ॥
The king returned to his city after completing the Yajña. But he could not find a way of getting Gaṅgā.
1.41.26
अकृत्वा निश्चयं राजा
कालेन महता महान् ।
त्रिंशद्वर्षसहस्राणि
राज्यं कृत्वा दिवं गतः ॥
That great king could not complete his mission even after a long time. He ruled the kingdom for thirty thousand years and ascended to Heaven.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे एकचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty first Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
