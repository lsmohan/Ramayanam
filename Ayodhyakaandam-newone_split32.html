Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 32
In this Sarga, Rāma, along with Seetā and Lakshmaṇa distributes his wealth and possessions to Brāhmaṇas, friends, servants, paupers, beggars, students, old people and children that are in need.
Among the recipients are Suyajña, the son of Vasishṭha and a poor Brāhmaṇa by name Trijaṭa.
2.32.1
ततश्शासन माज्ञाय
भ्रातुश्शुभतरं प्रियम् ।
गत्वा स प्रविवेशाशु
सुयज्ञस्य निवेशनम् ॥
Then, on that command of the brother, which was like music to his ears and boding all auspiciousness in it, he went to the residence of Suyajña.
2.32.2
तं विप्रमग्न्यगारस्थम्
वन्दित्वा लक्ष्मणोऽब्रवीत् ॥
Lakshmaṇa paid obeisance to the Brāhmaṇa, who was seated in the fire-chamber, and said,
2.32.2
सखेऽभ्यागच्छ पश्य त्वम्
वेश्म दुष्करकारिणः ॥
Come, my friend, to the house of the one who is doing what is so difficult for anyone to do!
2.32.3
ततस्सन्ध्यामुपास्याशु
गत्वा सौमित्रिणा सह ।
जुष्टं तत्प्राविशल्लक्ष्म्या
रम्यं रामनिवेशनम् ॥
After completing his salutations to the Sandhya, he followed the son of Sumitrā to the beautiful and opulent palace of Rāma.
2.32.4
तमागतं वेदविदम्
प्राञ्जलिस्सीतया सह ।
सुयज्ञमभिचक्राम
राघवोऽग्निमिवार्चितम् ॥
Rāma, along with Seetā, circumambulated Suyajña, the savant of Vedas, who arrived thus, with the reverence of circumambulating Agni after performing a Hōma.
2.32.5
जातरूपमयैर्मुख्यैः
अङ्गदैः कुण्डलैः शुभैः ।
सहेमसूत्रैर्मणिभिः
केयूरैर्वलयैरपि ।
अन्यैश्च रत्नैर्बहुभिः
काकुत्स्थः प्रत्यपूजयत् ।
सुयज्ञं स तदोवाच
रामस्सीता प्रचोदितः ॥
The prince of Kākutstha lineage then honored Suyajña by giving valuable armlets made of pure gold, lovely ear-pendants, gems strung on gold chains, bracelets for the upper arm and the wrist, and many other precious stones. Prompted by Seetā, he then told Suyajña:
2.32.7
हारं च हेमसूत्रं च
भार्यायै सौम्य हारय ।
रशनां चाधुना सीता
दातुमिच्छति ते सखे ॥
My dear friend! O gentle one! Seetā would like to give you this necklace, gold chain and gold girdle. Please take them for your wife!
2.32.8
अङ्गदानि विचित्राणि
केयूराणि शुभानि च ।
प्रयच्छति सखे तुभ्यम्
भार्यायै गच्छती वनम् ॥
Seetā is now set to go to Vana. My dear friend, on this occasion, she would like to give your wife exquisite and elegant armlets and bracelets!
2.32.9
पर्यङ्कमग्य्रास्तरणम्
नानारत्नविभूषितम् ।
तमपीच्छति वैदेही
प्रतिष्ठापयितुं त्वयि ॥
She would also like to have installed in your place, this exquisite bed with fine bedspread, which is encrusted with a variety of precious stones.
2.32.10
नागश्शत्रुञ्जयो नाम
मातुलोऽयं ददौ मम ।
तं ते गजसहस्रेण
ददामि द्विजसत्तम ॥
O eminent Brāhmaṇa! This elephant named Ṡatruñjaya was given to me by my maternal uncle. I give it to you along with thousand more elephants!
2.32.11
इत्युक्तस्स हि रामेण
सुयज्ञः प्रतिगृहृतत् ।
रामलक्ष्मणसीतानाम्
प्रयुयोजाशिषश्शुभाः ॥
Spoken to thus by Rāma, and accepting what was given, Suyajña gave auspicious blessings to Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Seetā.
2.32.12
अथ भ्रातरमव्यग्रम्
प्रियं रामः प्रियंवदः ।
सौमित्रिं तमुवाचेदम्
ब्रह्मेव त्रिदशेश्वरम् ॥
Then Rāma, who always spoke endearingly, said to his beloved brother, son of Sumitrā, in a serene manner, like Brahma speaking to Indra:
2.32.13
अगस्त्यं कौशिकं चैव
तावुभौ ब्राह्मणोत्तमौ ।
अर्चयाहूय सौमित्रे
रत्नैस्सस्यमिवाम्बुभिः ॥
O son of Sumitrā! Please invite Agastya and Kouṡika and honor them with a shower of precious stones like standing crops showered with water.
2.32.14
तर्पयस्व महाबाहो
गोसहस्रैश्च मानद ।
सुवर्णै रजतैश्चैव
मणिभिश्च महाधनैः ॥
O you of mighty arm! O respectful one! Gratify them with thousands of cows, gold, silver and precious gems!
2.32.15
कौसल्यां च य आशीर्भिः
भक्तः पर्युपतिष्ठति ।
आचार्यस्तैत्तिरीयाणाम्
अभिरूपश्च वेदवित् ।
तस्य यानं च दासीश्च
सौमित्रे सम्प्रदापय ।
कौशेयानि च वस्त्राणि
यावत्तुष्यति स द्विजः ॥
Arrange for as many vehicles, clothes, attendants as it takes to make that willing Brāhmaṇa, the scholar of Taittireeya branch of Vēdas, who devotedly attends on Kousalyā giving her blessings fully satisfied.
2.32.17
सूतश्चित्ररथश्चार्य
सचिवस्सुचिरोषितः ।
तोषयैनं महार्हैश्च
रत्नैर्वस्त्रैर्धनैस्तथा ।
पशुकाभिश्च सर्वाभिः
गवां दशशतेन च ॥
Counselor of our noble father and charioteer Citraratha has been in our service for many years. Make him happy with the best of jewels, clothes, wealth, a thousand cows and other cattle.
2.32.18
ये चेमे कठकालापा
बहवो दण्डमाणवाः ।
नित्यस्वाध्यायशीलत्वात्
नान्यत्कुर्वन्ति किञ्चन ।
अलसाः स्वादुकामाश्च
महतां चापि सम्मताः ।
तेषामशीतियानानि
रत्नपूर्णानि दापय ।
शालिवाहसहस्रं च
द्वे शते भद्रकां स्तथा ।
व्यञ्जनार्थं च सौमित्रे
गोसहस्रमुपाकुरु ॥
There are many celibate pupils holding ascetic wands, who study Kaṭha and Kālāpā portions of Vēdas. They spend their entire time devoted to the studies and are too tired to do anything else. They are much esteemed by the wise and they love tasty foods. Give them eighty cartloads of precious stones. Give them paddy carried by a thousand pack bullocks, two hundred bulls to plough the land and a thousand cows for dairy products.
2.32.21
मेखलीनां महासङ्घः
कौसल्यां समुपस्थितः ।
तेषां सहस्रं सौमित्रे
प्रत्येकं सम्प्रदापय ॥
There are a large number of students, wearing girdles of grass, waiting on Kousalyā's pleasure. Give them a thousand cows each, O son of Sumitrā!
2.32.22
अम्बा यथा च सा नन्देत्
कौसल्या मम दक्षिणाम् ।
तथा द्विजातीस्तान्सर्वान्
लक्ष्मणार्चय सर्वशः ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! Honor all those Brāhmaṇas in such a manner that Kousalyā feels proud of my charity.
2.32.23
तत स्सपुरुषव्याघ्रः
तद्धनं लक्ष्मणः स्वयम् ।
यथोक्तं ब्राह्मणेन्द्राणाम्
अददाद्धनदो यथा ॥
Then Lakshmaṇa, tiger among men, personally distributed the entire wealth, as if he was none other than Kubēra, to all those eminent Brāhmaṇas, just as he has been told.
2.32.24
अथाऽब्रवीद्बाष्पगलान्
तिष्ठतश्चोपजीविनः ।
सम्प्रदायबहुद्रव्यम्
एकैकस्योपजीवनम् ॥
Then, he bestowed abundant wealth on each one of the servants that stood there, and said to them, as his throat became wet:
I am not able to find a good equivalent in English for Upajeevinaḥ . I chose the word servant as the last resort. The word 'servant' evokes the notion of being 'servile'. The word Upajeevinaḥ, on the other hand, means people whose livelihood is dependent on serving the needs of a given person, be it housekeeping, running errands or doing chores.
2.32.25
लक्ष्मणस्य च यद्वेश्म
गृहं च यदिदं मम ।
अशून्यं कार्यमेकैकम्
यावदागमनं मम ॥
These houses of Lakshmaṇa and mine, I hope, will not be deserted, by the time I come back from the Vana.
What an emotion packed Ṡlōka?
On the one hand it is about bidding good bye to the servants. On the other hand, it is about leaving the house. Plus, it is about envisioning the image of an empty and abandoned house! Add to that the uncertainty of not knowing how things will shape up by the time he comes back.
No wonder his throat became wet (choked)!
2.32.26
इत्युक्त्वा दुःखितं सर्वम्
जनं तमुपजीविनम् ।
उवाचेदं धनाध्यक्षम्
धनमानीयतामिति ॥
Having spoken thus to the servants who were overwhelmed by sadness, he told the treasurer to fetch the money.
2.32.27
ततोऽस्य धनमाजह्रुः
सर्वमेवोपजीविनः ।
स राशिस्सुमहांस्तत्र
दर्शनीयो ह्यदृश्यत ॥
Then, Rāma's servants brought all of Rāma's money, which stood as a huge mound, making it quite a sight to behold.
2.32.28
ततस्सपुरुषव्याघ्रः
तद्धनं सहलक्ष्मणः ।
द्विजेभ्यो बालवृद्धेभ्यः
कृपणेभ्योऽह्यदापयत् ॥
Then that tiger among men, along with Lakshmaṇa, had that money given off to Brāhmaṇas, old people, children and all those in need.
2.32.29
तत्रासीत्पिङ्गलो गार्ग्यः
त्रिजटो नाम वै द्विजः ।
क्षतवृत्तिर्वने नित्यम्
फालकुद्दाललाङ्गली ॥
Then, there was a Brāhmaṇa called Trijaṭa, of tawny complexion, from the lineage of Garga, who lived by digging the soil in the forest with pick axe, crowbar and plough.
2.32.30
तं वृद्धं तरुणी भार्या
बालानादाय दारकान् ।
अब्रवीद्ब्राह्मणं वाक्यम्
दारिद्र्येणाभिपीडिता ॥
His young wife, stricken by poverty, came to that old man along with her young children and said:
2.32.31
अपास्य फालं कुद्दालम्
कुरुष्व वचनं मम ।
रामं दर्शय धर्मज्ञम्
यदि किञ्चिदवाप्स्यसि ॥
Listen to me. Leave that pick axe and crowbar. Pay a visit to Rāma, who knows Dharma, and you may get something.
2.32.32
स भार्यावचनं श्रुत्वा
शाटीमाच्छाद्य दुश्छदाम् ।
स प्रातिष्ठत पन्थानम्
यत्र रामनिवेशनम् ॥
Heeding the words of his wife, he headed in the direction of Rāma's palace covering himself with a piece of cloth that could hardly cover him.
2.32.33
भृग्वङ्गिरसमं दीप्त्या
त्रिजटं जनसंसदि ।
आपञ्चमायाः कक्ष्यायाः
नैनं कश्चिदवारयत् ॥
No one stopped Trijaṭa, who stood out among the crowd by his radiance that rivaled that of Bhṛgu and Aṅgeerasa, till he reached the fifth courtyard.
2.32.34
स राजपुत्रमासाद्य
त्रिजटो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ।
निर्धनो बहुपुत्रोऽस्मि
राजपुत्र महायशः ।
उञ्छवृत्तिर्वने नित्यम्
प्रत्यवेक्षस्व मामिति ॥
Reaching that prince, Trijaṭa said: O prince of repute! I have no money. I have many children. I live by digging the soil in the forest. Please bear a kind eye to me.
2.32.36
तमुवाच ततो रामः
परिहास समन्वितम् ।
गवां सहस्रमप्येकम्
न नु विश्राणितं मया ।
परिक्षिपसि दण्डेन
यावत्तावदवाप्स्यसि ॥
Rāma, then said in jest, to him: I have not dispensed even a thousand cows yet. Hence, you will get as many cows as would lineup to reach the throw of your staff.
Rāma can be seen being playful and poking fun at times, especially with strangers. We will see this one more time with Ṡoorpaṇakha, later in Araṇya Kāṇḍa.
2.32.37
स शाटीं त्वरितः कट्याम्
सम्भ्रान्तः परिवेष्ट्य ताम् ।
आविद्ध्य दण्डं चिक्षेप
सर्वप्राणेन वेगितः ॥
Feeling extremely delighted, he immediately tied that cloth around his waist and whirled the staff around and sent it flying with all the strength of his life.
2.32.38
स तीर्त्वा सरयूपारम्
दण्डस्तस्य कराच्च्युतः ।
गोव्रजे बहुसाहस्रे
पपातोक्षणसन्निधौ ॥
The staff that left his hand then reached the banks of river Sarayu and fell near a stud-bull in the cow-pen with its many thousand cows.
2.32.39
तं परिष्वज्य धर्मात्मा
आतस्मात्सरयूतटात् ।
आनयामास ता गोपै
स्त्रिजटायाश्रमं प्रति ॥
Then the Dharmātma embraced him and had the herdsmen send all the cows extending to the banks of Sarayu to the Āṡrama of Trijaṭa.
2.32.40
उवाच स ततो रामः
तं गार्ग्यमभिसान्त्वयन् ।
मन्युर्न खलु कर्तव्यः
परिहासो ह्ययं मम ॥
Then Rāma pleaded with that man of Garga lineage not to get angry for his talking in jest.
2.32.41
इदं हि तेजस्तव यद्दुरत्ययं
तदेव जिज्ञासितुमिच्छता मया ।
इमं भवानर्थमभि प्रचोदितो
वृणीष्व किं चेदपरं व्यवस्यति ॥
I wanted to witness this formidable strength of yours. That was the reason behind my request. Please ask if you need anything else.
If you like to practice Sanskrit, try using the last line of this Ṡlōka. It says:
Let me know if you need anything else.
vṛṇeeshva = ask
kiṃ cēdaparaṃ = if anything else
vyavasyati  = is needed.
2.32.42
ब्रवीमि सत्येन नतेऽस्ति यन्त्रणा
धनं हि यद्यन्मम विप्रकारणात् ।
भवत्सु सम्यक्प्रतिपादनेन त
न्मयार्जितं प्रीतियशस्करं भवेत् ॥
Take my word. There need be no limit on what you ask. Whatever I have, is intended to be given to Brāhmaṇas. Whatever I have earned, will bring me pleasure and renown only if it is well bestowed on the likes of you!
2.32.43
तत स्सभार्य स्त्रिजटो महामुनि-
र्गवामनीकं प्रतिगृह्य मोदितः ।
यशोबलप्रीतिसुखोपबृंहणी-
स्तदाशिषः प्रत्यवदन्महात्मनः ॥
And the Mahā Muni Trijaṭa, along with his wife, was delighted to receive the huge herd of cows. Then, he blessed the Mahātma wishing him fame, strength, pleasure and happiness.
2.32.44
स चापि रामः प्रतिपूर्णमानसो
महद्धनं धर्मबलैरुपार्जितम् ।
नियोजयामास सुहृज्जनेऽचिरा-
द्यथार्हसम्मानवचः प्रचोदितः ॥
Rāma, well-content, then bestowed the great wealth that he earned by his own might in the righteous manner, on his long time friends in order to show his appreciation.
2.32.45
द्विज स्सुहृद्भृत्यजनोऽथवा तदा
दरिद्रभिक्षाचरणश्च योऽभवत् ।
न तत्र कश्चिन्न बभूव तर्पितो
यथार्हसम्मानन दान सम्भ्रमैः ॥
Then, no Brāhmaṇa, friend, servant, pauper or beggar was left who was not fully satisfied with the gifts given to them with all due regard.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे द्वात्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the Thirty second Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
