Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 3
In this Sarga, Hanumān approaches Rāma and Lakshmaṇa. He praises them generously and asks who they are. He tells them that Sugreeva would like to be friends with them. Rāma, feeling very happy to see the envoy of Sugreeva whom he is seeking, tells Lakshmaṇa how pleasing he finds the eloquence of Hanumān to be. Rāma asks Lakshmaṇa to address Hanumān appropriately. Lakshmaṇa tells Hanumān that they will be happy to be friends with Sugreeva. Hanumān feels happy about it.
4.3.1
वचो विज्ञाय हनुमान्
सुग्रीवस्य महात्मनः ।
पर्वतादृश्यमूकात्तु
पुप्लुवे यत्र राघवौ ॥
Appreciating the point Sugreeva the Mahātma made, Hanumān flew from the Ṛshyamūka mountain to the place where the Rāghavas were to be found.
4.3.2
कपिरूपं परित्यज्य
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ।
भिक्षुरूपं ततो भेजे
शठबुद्धितया कपिः ॥
Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, then gave up his Vānara form and took the form of a mendicant as he was still unsure (of the Rāghavas).
4.3.3
ततस्स हनुमान्वाचा
श्लक्ष्णया सुमनोज्ञया ।
विनीतवदुपागम्य
राघवौ प्रणिपत्य च ।
आबभाषे तदा वीरौ
यथावत्प्रशशंस च ॥
Then Hanumān approached the Rāghavas with all humility. He saluted them and greeted them in a gentle and heart-warming voice. He also praised the Veeras in an appropriate manner.
4.3.4
सम्पूज्य विधिवद्वीरौ
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ।
उवाच कामतो वाक्यम्
मृदु सत्यपराक्रमौ ॥
Paying obedience to those Veeras of true valor in a customary manner, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, spoke sincerely in a soft voice to them:
4.3.5
राजर्षिदेवप्रतिमौ
तापसौ संशितव्रतौ ।
देशं कथमिमं प्राप्तौ
भवन्तौ वरवर्णिनौ ॥
You of brilliant complexion, look like Rājarshis and Tapasvis comparable to Dēvas. You seem to have done many Vratas. What brings you to this region?
4.3.6
त्रासयन्तौ मृगगणान्
अन्यांश्च वनचारिणः ।
पम्पातीररुहान्वृक्षान्
वीक्षमाणौ समन्ततः ॥
As you go looking around the trees that grow on the shores of Paṃpā, you scare away the herds of beasts and other animals that roam in the Vana.
4.3.7
इमां नदीं शुभजलाम्
शोभयन्तौ तपस्विनौ ।
धैर्यवन्तौ सुवर्णाभौ
कौ युवां चीरवाससौ ।
निःश्वसन्तौ वरभुजौ
पीडयन्ताविमाः प्रजाः ॥
The charm of this river of pious waters is enhanced by you of golden sheen. The creatures that live here are intimidated by you, the brave ones of imposing shoulders. Who are you, clad in tree-bark, heaving deep sighs?
4.3.9
सिंहविप्रेक्षितौ वीरौ
महाबलपराक्रमौ ।
शक्रचापनिभे चापे
गृहीत्वा शत्रुसूदनौ ॥
You look like Veeras with leonine looks, with extraordinary strength and prowess. With a bow that rivals that of Indra, you seem to be annihilators of your foes.
4.3.10
श्रीमन्तौ रूपसम्पन्नौ
वृषभश्रेष्ठविक्रमौ ।
हस्तिहस्तोपमभुजौ
द्युतिमन्तौ नरर्षभौ ॥
You look like bulls among men, radiant, rich, handsome, with arms like the trunks of elephants and the gait of a great bull.
4.3.11
प्रभया पर्वतेन्द्रोऽयम्
युवयोरवभासितः ।
राज्यार्हावमरप्रख्यौ
कथं देशमिहागतौ ॥
This great mountain is lit up by your brilliance. How did you, comparable to Dēvas and deserve to rule a kingdom, happen to come to this place?
4.3.12
पद्मपत्रेक्षणौ वीरौ
जटामण्डलधारिणौ ।
अन्योन्यसदृशौ वीरौ
देवलोकादिवागतौ ॥
You Veeras, rivaling each other, eyes resembling lotus petals and wearing matted locks look as if you have descended from the world of Dēvas.
4.3.13
यदृच्छयेव सम्प्राप्तौ
चन्द्रसूर्यौ वसुन्धराम् ।
विशालवक्षसौ वीरौ
मानुषौ देवरूपिणौ ।
सिंहस्कन्धौ महोत्साहौ
समदाविव गोवृषौ ॥
You look like the Sun and Moon that accidentally descended on the earth. You Veeras, though humans, bear the semblance of Dēvas, with your broad chests. With your lion’s shoulders and your tremendous energy, you remind one of impassioned stud-bulls.
4.3.14
आयताश्च सुवृत्ताश्च
बाहवः परिघोपमाः ।
सर्वभूषणभूषार्हाः
किमर्थं न विभूषिताः ॥
Why are (your) arms, long and stout like iron clubs, that deserve to be adorned with every ornament, not adorned at all?
4.3.15
उभौ योग्यावहं मन्ये
रक्षितुं पृथिवीमिमाम् ।
ससागरवनां कृत्स्नाम्
विन्ध्यमेरुविभूषिताम् ॥
The two of you seem to be perfectly capable of protecting this entire earth with all its oceans and Vanas and the Vindhya and the Mēru mountains that adorn it.
4.3.16
इमे च धनुषी चित्रे
श्लक्ष्णे चित्रानुलेपने ।
प्रकाशेते यथेन्द्रस्य
वज्रे हेमविभूषिते ॥
These amazing and smooth bows, so nicely painted and inlaid with gold, shine like Indra’s (weapon) Vajra.
4.3.17
सम्पूर्णाश्च शितैर्बाणैः
तूणाश्च शुभदर्शनाः ।
जीवितान्तकरैर्घोरैः
ज्वलद्भिरिव पन्नगैः ॥
These fine quivers look amazing, full of sharp and dreadful arrows that gleam like fiery serpents, ready to end lives.
4.3.18
महाप्रमाणौ विपुलौ
तप्त हाटक भूषणौ | ।
खड्गावेतौ विराजेते
निर्मुक्त भुजगाविव ॥
These swords, large and broad, decked with gold purified in fire, dazzle like serpents that have just shed their slough.
4.3.19
एवं मां परिभाषन्तम्
कस्माद्वै नाभिभाषथः ।
सुग्रीवो नाम धर्मात्मा
कश्चिद्वानरयूथपः ।
वीरो विनिकृतो भ्रात्रा
जगद्भ्रमति दुःखितः ॥
Why are you not responding while I say so much? There is a Veera and Dharmātma, a bull among Vānaras, by name Sugreeva. Dealt with unfairly by his brother, he wanders over the earth grief-stricken.
4.3.21
प्राप्तोऽहं प्रेषितस्तेन
सुग्रीवेण महात्मना ।
राज्ञा वानरमुख्यानाम्
हनुमान्नाम वानरः ॥
I, a Vānara, known as Hanumān, have come here, sent by that Mahātma Sugreeva, the King who is acknowledged by all the great Vānara-chiefs.
4.3.22
युवाभ्यां सह धर्मात्मा
सुग्रीवस्सख्यमिच्छति ।
तस्य मां सचिवं वित्तम्
वानरं पवनात्मजम् ।
भिक्षुरूपप्रतिच्छन्नम्
सुग्रीवप्रियकाम्यया ।
ऋष्यमूकादिह प्राप्तम्
कामगं कामरूपिणम् ॥
Sugreeva, the Dharmātma would like to make friends with you. May it be known that I, a Vānara, son of Vāyu is his minister. I, who can take any form and go anywhere at will, came from Ṛshyamūka mountain, in this guise of a mendicant, since Sugreeva so wished.
4.3.24
एवमुक्त्वा तु हनुमान्
तौ वीरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
वाक्यज्ञौ वाक्यकुशलः
पुनर्नोवाच किञ्चन ॥
Speaking thus to the Veeras Rāma and Lakshmaṇa who could understand the implications of what was said, Hanumān, skilled in speech, remained silent.
4.3.25
एतच्छ्रुत्वा वचन्तस्य
रामो लक्ष्मणमब्रवीत् ।
प्रहृष्टवदनश्श्रीमान्
भ्रातरं पार्श्वतः स्थितम् ॥
Having heard what he said, the gracious Rāma, with happiness evident in his face, told his brother Lakshmaṇa, who was by his side:
4.3.26
सचिवोऽयं कपीन्द्रस्य
सुग्रीवस्य महात्मनः ।
तमेव काङ्क्षमाणस्य
ममान्तिकमिहागतः ॥
This is the minister of Mahātma Sugreeva, the King of the Vānaras, whom I seek earnestly. (It is good that) he himself has come here.
4.3.27
तमभ्यभाष सौमित्रे
सुग्रीवसचिवं कपिम् ।
वाक्यज्ञं मधुरैर्वाक्यैः
स्नेहयुक्तमरिन्दम ॥
O Sowmitri! O subduer of foes! Talk, in a friendly manner and with the sweetest words, to this Vānara, the minister of Sugreeva, who seem to be sharp in discerning the purport.
4.3.28
नानृग्वेदविनीतस्य
नाऽऽयजुर्वेदधारिणः ।
नाऽसामवेदविदुषः
शक्यमेवं विभाषितुम् ॥
It would not be possible to speak like this for someone who is not well-trained in the Ṛg Vēda, who does not have the Yajuh Vēda by-heart, and who is not proficient in reciting the Sāma Vēda.
4.3.29
नूनं व्याकरणं कृत्स्नम्
अनेन बहुधा श्रुतम् ।
बहु व्याहरताऽनेन
न किञ्चिदपशब्दितम् ॥
Without a doubt he has heard the science of Grammar expounded repeatedly; he spoke so long and so much without a single grammatical slip.
4.3.30
न मुखे नेत्रयोर्वापि
ललाटे च भ्रुवोस्तथा ।
अन्येष्वपि च गात्रेषु
दोषस्संविदितः क्वचित् ॥
Neither in the face, nor in the eyes, nor on the forehead, nor on the eyebrows nor in any other part of the body could be seen any flaw (while he spoke).
4.3.31
अविस्तरमसन्दिग्धम्
अविलम्बितमद्रुतम् ।
उरःस्थं कण्ठगं वाक्यम्
वर्तते मध्यमे स्वरे ॥
His talk, neither verbose nor ambiguous, neither too slow nor too hurried, came from his chest through the throat with ease, pitched in medium tone.
4.3.32
संस्कारक्रमसम्पन्नाम्
अद्रुतामविलम्बिताम् ।
उच्चारयति कल्याणीम्
वाचं हृदयहारिणीम् ॥
His pronunciation is well-cultivated and amazing. It is awesome and paced well, captivating the heart.
4.3.33
अनया चित्रया वाचा
त्रिस्थानव्यञ्जनस्थया ।
कस्य नाराध्यते चित्तम्
उद्यतासेररेरपि ॥
Who will not be moved, even if he be a foe who lifted a sword, by such amazing speech that emerged from all the three places?
The three places are chest, throat and head.
4.3.34
एवं विधो यस्य दूतो
न भवेत्पार्थिवस्य तु ।
सिद्ध्यन्ति हि कथं तस्य
कार्याणां गतयोऽनघ ॥
O sinless one! Could ever the affairs of a King come to fruition, if he does not have an envoy like this?
4.3.35
एवं गुणगणैर्युक्ता
यस्य स्युः कार्यसाधकाः ।
तस्य सिध्यन्ति सर्वाऽर्था
दूतवाक्यप्रचोदिताः ॥
Whoever has such great taskmasters of great qualities as their envoys, can have all their goals accomplished, driven by their diplomatic skills.
4.3.36
एवमुक्तस्तु सौमित्रिः
सुग्रीवसचिवं कपिम् ।
अभ्यभाषत वाक्यज्ञो
वाक्यज्ञं पवनात्मजम् ॥
Thus bidden, Sowmitri who had command over his words, addressed that Vānara, son of the Vāyu, who was good at discerning the purport, saying:
4.3.37
विदितौ नौ गुणा विद्वन्
सुग्रीवस्य महात्मनः ।
तमेव चाऽऽवां मार्गावः
सुग्रीवं प्लवगेश्वरम् ॥
O wise one! We are well-informed of the esteemed qualities of Sugreeva, and it is him, the lord of the Vānaras, whom we have come seeking.
4.3.38
यथा ब्रवीषि हनुमान्
सुग्रीववचनादिह ।
तत्तथा हि करिष्यावो
वचनात्तव सत्तम ॥
O Hanumān, O good man! We shall do exactly what you have conveyed to us as the word of Sugreeva!
4.3.39
तत्तस्य वाक्यं निपुणं निशम्य
प्रहृष्टरूपः पवनात्मजः कपि: ।
मनस्समाधाय जयोपपत्तौ
सख्यं तदा कर्तुमियेष ताभ्याम् ॥
The Vānara, the son of Vāyu, was delighted to hear those purposive words. With his mind keen on succeeding in his mission, he wanted to build an alliance between them.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे तृतीयस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the third Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
