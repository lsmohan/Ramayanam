Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 123
In this Sarga, the Vimāna Pushpaka takes Rāma, along with Seetā, Lakshmaṇa, Vānaras and Rākshasas from Laṅkā to Ayōdhyā.
On the way, Rāma shows each and every place and describes it to Seetā, the Trikūṭa mountain on which Laṅkā was built, the various spots of the battle field where each and every one of the Rākshasa chiefs, such as Rāvaṇa, Kumbhakarṇa, Indrajit and Prahasta were slain.
He then shows her the beach where they spent the night after crossing the ocean, then the bridge itself, then the Maināka mountain in the middle of the ocean, and the place where the Vānaras camped while constructing the bridge and the shore on the other side of the ocean where Mahādēva showered grace on him.
Then he shows Kishkindhā and Seetā tells him that she would like to go to Ayōdhyā along with the women of the Vānaras. Rāma tells Sugreeva and Sugreeva tells Tārā the same and all the Vānara women get into the Vimāna.
Then Rāma shows her the mountain Ṛshyamūka where he met Sugreeva, the river Paṃpā where he cried in misery for her, the places where he met Ṡabari, where he slew Kabandha and where Jaṭāyu was killed by Rāvaṇa, the Janasthāna where Dūshaṇa and Triṡira were killed by him and the Parṇaṡāla where Rāvaṇa abducted her.
Then he shows her the Āṡramas of Agastya, Suteekshṇa and Ṡarabhaṅga, the places where he slew Virādha, where she visited Anasūyā, and Citrakūṭa where Bharata came to persuade him, the Āṡrama of Bharadwāja, the sacred river Gaṅgā, and Ṡṛṅgibērapura where Guha met him.
And finally, the Vimāna reaches Ayōdhyā which Rāma points out to her. The Vānaras and Rākshasas spring up again and again with joy to behold that city.
6.123.1
अनुज्ञातं तु रामेण
तद्विमानमनुत्तमम् ।
हंसयुक्तं महानादम्
उत्पपात विहायसम् ॥
That unexcelled Vimāna, yoked with swans, rose up into the sky as commanded by Rāma, making a great noise.
6.123.2
पातयित्वा ततश्चक्षुः
सर्वतो रघुनन्दनः ।
अब्रवीन्मैथिलीं सीताम्
रामः शशिनिभाननाम् ॥
Looking around on all sides, Rāma, the delight of Raghus said to Seetā, the princess of Mithilā, whose face shone like the moon:
6.123.3
कैलासशिखराकारे
त्रिकूटशिखरे स्थिताम् ।
लङ्कामीक्षस्व वैदेहि
निर्मितां विश्वकर्मणा ॥
Look, O Vaidēhee, at Laṅkā built by Viṡwakarma on a peak of the Trikūṭa mountain, which resembles the peak of Kailāsa.
6.123.4
एतदायोधनं पश्य
मांसशोणितकर्दमम् ।
हरीणां राक्षसानां च
सीते विशसनं महत् ॥
Look, O Seetā, at this great battlefield that is mired with flesh and blood, where the Vānaras and Rākshasas lost their lives.
6.123.5
अत्र दत्तवरः शेते
प्रमाथी राक्षसेश्वरः ।
तव हेतोर्विशालाक्षि
रावणो निहतो मया ॥
This is where the lord of Rākshasas, the great oppressor, who had been granted a boon (by Brahma) lay dead.
It was for your sake, O you of wide eyes, that Rāvaṇa was slain by me.
6.123.6
कुम्भकर्णोऽत्र निहतः
प्रहस्तश्च निशाचरः ।
धूम्राक्षश्चात्र निहतो
वानरेण हनूमता ॥
This is where Kumbhakarṇa was slain, (this is where) Prahasta, the Night-rover was slain and this is where Dhūmrāksha was slain by the Vānara Hanumān.
6.123.7
विद्युन्माली हतश्चात्र
सुषेणेन महात्मना ।
लक्ष्मणेनेन्द्रजिच्चात्र
रावणिर्निहतो रणे ॥
Vidyunmāli was slain by Mahātma Sushēṇa here. And this is where Indrajit, the son of Rāvaṇa was slain by Lakshmaṇa in the fight.
6.123.8
अङ्गदेनात्र निहतो
विकटो नाम राक्षसः ।
विरूपाक्षश्च दुष्प्रेक्ष्यो
महापार्श्वमहोदरौ ।
अकम्पनश्च निहतो
बलिनोअन्ये च राक्षसाः ।
त्रिशिराश्चातिकायश्च
देवान्तकनरान्तकौ ॥
The Rākshasa called Vikaṭa was slain by Aṅgada here. Virūpāksha whom none even dared to look at, Mahāpārṡwa, Mahōdara, Triṡira, Atikāya, Dēvāntaka, Narāntaka and many other strong Rākshasas were slain here.
6.123.10
युद्धोन्मत्तश्च मत्तश्च
राक्षसप्रवरावुभौ ।
निकुम्भश्चैव कुम्भश्च
कुम्भकर्णात्मजौ बली ।
वज्रदंष्ट्रश्च दंष्ट्रश्च
बहवो राक्षसा हताः ।
मकराक्षक्ष्च दुर्धर्षो
मया युधि निपातितः ॥
Many Rākshasas such as Yuddhōnmatta, Matta, both prominent Rākshasas, Nikumbha, Kumbha, both sons of Kumbhakarṇa, the strong Vajradaṃshṭra and Daṃshṭra were killed. The unassailable Makarāksha was struck down by me in the battle.
6.123.12
अकम्पनश्च निहतः
शोणिताक्षश्च वीर्यवान् ।
यूपाक्षश्च प्रजङ्घश्च
निहतौ तु महाहवे ॥
Akampana was slain and so were Ṡōṇitāksha, the valiant one, Yūpāksha and Prajaṅgha, in the great battle.
6.123.13
विद्युज्जिह्वोऽत्र निहतो
राक्षसो भीमदर्शनः ।
यज्ञशत्रुश्च निहतः
सुप्तघ्नश्च महाबलः ।
सूर्यशत्रुश्च निहतो
ब्रह्मशत्रुस्तथापरः ॥
The fierce looking Rākshasa Vidyujjihva was slain here. Yajñaṡatru was slain, and so was the extraordinarily strong Suptaghna. Sūryaṡatru was slain, and so were Brahmaṡatru and one other.
6.123.14
अत्र मण्डोदरी नाम
भार्या तं पर्यदेवयत् ।
सपत्नीनां सहस्रेण
साग्रेण परिवारिता ॥
This is where his wife named Manḍōdaree, bewailed him, surrounded by a thousand of her co-wives.
6.123.15
एतत्तु दृश्यते तीर्थम्
समुद्रस्य वरानने ।
यत्र सागरमुत्तीर्य
तां रात्रिमुषिता वयम् ॥
This beach of the ocean that can seen here, O you of exquisite face, is where we spent the night after crossing the ocean.
6.123.16
एष सेतुर्मया बद्धः
सागरे सलिलार्णवे ।
तव हेतोर्विशालाक्षि
नलसेतुः सुदुष्करः ॥
This is the bridge, quite an impossible one to build, that I got built by Nala over the salty waters of the ocean for your sake, O you of wide-eyes!
6.123.17
पश्य सागरमक्षोभ्यम्
वैदेहि वरुणालयम् ।
अपारमिव गर्जन्तम्
शङ्खशुक्तिनिषेवितम् ॥
Look, O Vaidēhee, at the roaring and imperturbable ocean, the abode of Varuṇa, that seems to be boundless abounding in conch shells and pearls.
6.123.18
हिरण्यनाभं शैलेन्द्रम्
काञ्चनं पश्य मैथिलि ।
विश्रमार्थं हनुमतो
भित्त्वा सागरमुत्थितम् ॥
Look, O Maithili, at Hiraṇyanābha (Maināka), the king of mountains, which rose up cleaving the waters so that Hanumān might rest on it.
6.123.19
एतत् कुक्षौ समुद्रस्य
स्कन्धावारनिवेशनम् ।
अत्र पूर्वं महादेवः
प्रसादमकरोत्प्रभुः ॥
And this is where, in the middle of the sea, the army had camped (on the bridge). And this is where Mahādēva, the Lord, showered his grace on me.
6.123.20
एतत्तु दृश्यते तीर्थम्
सागरस्य महात्मनः ।
सेतुबन्ध इति ख्यातम्
त्रैलोक्येनाभिपूजितम् ।
एतत् पवित्रं परमम्
महापातकनाशनम् ।
अत्र राक्षसराजोऽयम्
आजगाम विभीषणः ॥
The shore of the mighty ocean that is seen here is famed as Sētubandha, a supremely sacred spot that wipes out even the greatest of sins, and is worshiped by all the three worlds. This is where Vibheeshaṇa, the king of Rākshasas came (and met me).
6.123.22
एषा सा दृश्यते सीते
किष्किन्धा चित्रकानना ।
सुग्रीवस्य पुरी रम्या
यत्र वाली मया हतः ॥
What is seen here, O Seetā, is Kishkindhā of fascinating fauna and flora, the lovely city of Sugreeva, where Vāli was killed by me.
6.123.23
अथ दृष्ट्वा पुरीं सीता
किष्किन्धां वालिपालिताम् ।
अब्रवीत् प्रश्रितं वाक्यम्
रामं प्रणयसाध्वसा ॥
On seeing that city of Kishkindhā which was ruled by Vāli, Seetā said these words to Rāma, with all deference, but hastened by affection:
6.123.24
सुग्रीवप्रियभार्याभिः
ताराप्रमुखतो नृप ।
अन्येषां वानरेन्द्राणाम्
स्त्रीभिः परिवृता ह्यहम् ।
गन्तुमिच्छे सहायोध्याम्
राजधानीं त्वया सह ॥
I wish to go to Ayōdhyā, your capital city, O King, accompanied by Tāra and other beloved wives of Sugreeva and the wives of the other great Vānara chiefs.
Seetā is the only woman aboard Pushpaka, among all men; it is no wonder that she sought the company of other women, especially those of Rāma's friends, who were instrumental in recovering her.
It would not have been that nice if Vānaras themselves asked for their wives to be picked up on the way. It would not have been nice either if Rāma suggested it on his own to the Vānaras.
It is a sweet request at the perfect time, by Seetā, the first one after she was united back with him.
6.123.26
एवमुक्तोऽथ वैदेह्या
राघवः प्रत्युवाच ताम् ।
एवमस्त्विति किष्किन्धाम्
प्राप्य संस्थाप्य राघवः ।
विमानं प्रेक्ष्य सुग्रीवम्
वाक्यमेतदुवाच ह ॥
Thus asked by Vaidēhee, Rāghava replied, 'So shall it be!' Stopping the Vimāna by Kishkindhā, Rāghava looked at Sugreeva and said these words:
6.123.27
ब्रूहि वानरशार्दूल
सर्वान् वानरपुङ्गवान् ।
स्र्रीभिः परिवृताः सर्वे
ह्ययोध्यां यान्तु सीतया ॥
O tiger among Vānaras! Please tell all the bulls among Vānaras that all their women also should come to Ayōdhyā accompanying Seetā!
6.123.28
तथा त्वमपि सर्वाभिः
स्त्रीभिः सह महाबल ।
अभित्वरस्व सुग्रीव
गच्छामः प्लवगेश्वर ॥
O Sugreeva of extraordinary strength! O Lord of Vānaras! You too, please, hasten up along with all your women! We shall go (to Ayōdhyā) along with them.
6.123.29
एवमुक्तस्तु सुग्रीवो
रामेणामिततेजसा ।
वानराधिपतिः श्रीमान्
तैश्च सर्वैः समावृतः ।
प्रविश्यान्तःपुरं शीघ्रम्
तारामुद्वीक्ष्य सोऽब्रवीत् ॥
Thus invited by Rāma of infinite splendor, the blessed Sugreeva, Lord of Vānaras, went to the inner quarters with all the Vānaras accompanying him, and addressing Tārā, said:
6.123.31
प्रिये त्वं सह नारीभिः
वानराणां महात्मनाम् ।
राघवेणाभ्यनुज्ञाता
मैथिलीप्रियकाम्यया ॥
My darling! Rāghava wants to please Seetā and hence gave the permission for you and the women of the mighty Vānaras (to go to Ayōdhyā along with us).
It would have been nicer if the narration included Tārā and other women expressing their joy over seeing their husbands come safe and victorious. May be it was there to begin with, but got lost over the course of time.
6.123.32
त्वर त्वमभिगच्छामो
गृह्य वानरयोषितः ।
अयोध्यां दर्शयिष्यामः
सर्वा दशरथस्त्रियः ॥
Please hasten up, taking the Vānara women along with you! We will visit Ayōdhyā and all the women of Daṡaratha!
6.123.33
सुग्रीवस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
तारा सर्वाङ्गशोभना ।
आहूय चाब्रवीत् सर्वा
वानराणां तु योषितः ॥
Hearing those words of Sugreeva, Tārā, whose every limb was lovely, summoned all the women of the Vānaras and said to them:
6.123.34
सुग्रीवेणाभ्यनुज्ञाता
गन्तुं सर्वैश्च वानरैः ।
मम चापि प्रियं कार्यम्
अयोध्यादर्शनेन च ।
प्रवेशं चापि रामस्य
पौरजानपदैः सह ।
विभूतिं चैव सर्वासाम्
स्त्रीणां दशरथस्य च ॥
All the Vānaras have been given permission by Sugreeva to go. And I too would only be pleased to visit Ayōdhyā, and witness Rāma's re-entry welcomed by the city-dwellers and country-dwellers, and the opulence of all the women of Daṡaratha.
6.123.36
तारया चाभ्यनुज्ञाताः
सर्वा वानरयोषितः ।
नेपथ्यं विधिपूर्वं तु
कृत्वा चापि प्रदक्षिणम् ।
अध्यारोहन् विमानं तत्
सीतादर्शनकाङ्क्षया ॥
Thus getting the word of Tārā, all the Vānara women, having their toilet completed in a customary manner, circumambulated the Vimāna in reverence and got onto it, eager to see Seetā.
6.123.37
ताभिः सहोत्थितं शीघ्रम्
विमानं प्रेक्ष्य राघवः ।
ऋश्यमूकसमीपे तु
वैदेहीं पुनरब्रवीत् ॥
When the Vimāna rose again into the air with all of them on board, and neared Ṛshyamūka, Rāma pointed it out to Seetā, saying:
6.123.38
दृश्यतेऽसौ महान् सीते
सविद्युदिव तोयदः ।
ऋश्यमूको गिरिश्रेष्ठः
काञ्चनैर्धातुभिर्वृतः ॥
Here can be seen the great Ṛshyamūka, one of the best mountains, shining with its veins of gold ore, like a cloud with lightning playing on it.
6.123.39
अत्राहं वानरेन्द्रेण
सुग्रीवेण समागतः ।
समयश्च कृतः सीते
वधार्थं वालिनो मया ॥
This is where I met Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras, and entered into the compact, O Seetā, that Vāli shall be killed by me.
Isn't it amazing that Rāma changed the regimes in two places, in Kishkindhā and Laṅkā single handedly, while he was still in exile?
6.123.40
एषा सा दृश्यते पम्पा
नलिनी चित्रकानना ।
त्वया विहीनो यत्राहम्
विललाप सुदुःखितः ।
अस्यास्तीरे मया दृष्टा
शबरी धर्मचारिणी ॥
The Paṃpā with its lotuses and a variety of woods by it is seen here. This is where I cried in misery, bereft of you. I visited, on its banks, Ṡabari, a strict adherent of Dharma.
Rāma, finally, gets an opportunity to let Seetā know that he too grieved and wept, separated from her.
6.123.42
अत्र योजनबाहुश्च
कबन्धो निहतो मया ।
दृश्यते च जनस्थाने
सीते श्रीमान् वनस्पतिः ।
जटायुश्च महातेजाः
तव हेतोर्विलासिनि ।
रावणेन हतो यत्र
पक्षिणां प्रवरो बली ॥
This is where Kabandha of Yōjana-long arms was killed by me. This magnificent tree that can be seen here in the Janasthāna is where Jaṭāyu of extraordinary power, the strong and foremost of the birds was killed by Rāvaṇa for your sake, O fashionable lady!
6.123.44
खरश्च निहतो यत्र
दूषणश्च निपातितः ।
त्रिशिराश्च महावीर्यो
मया बाणैरजिह्मगैः ।
एतत्तदाश्रमपदम्
अस्माकं वरवर्णिनि ॥
These are our Āṡrama grounds, O you of exquisite complexion, where Dūshaṇa was struck down and and also Triṡira of extraordinary valor, by my straight-flying arrows.
6.123.45
पर्णशाला तथा चित्रा
दृश्यते शुभदर्शने ।
यत्र त्वं राक्षसेन्द्रेण
रावणेन हृता बलात् ॥
The colorful Parṇaṡāla can be seen here, O you of pleasing looks, where you were abducted forcefully by Rāvaṇa, the king of Rākshasas.
6.123.46
एषा गोदावरी रम्या
प्रसन्नसलिला शुभा ।
अगस्त्यस्याश्रमो ह्येष
दृश्यते कदलीवृतः ॥
This is the beautiful Gōdāvaree, of clear, pleasing and auspicious waters. This is the Āṡrama of Agastya that is covered in banana groves that can be seen here.
6.123.47
दीप्तश्चैवाश्रमो ह्येष
सुतीक्ष्णस्य महात्मनः ।
दृश्यते चैव वैदेहि
शरभङ्गाश्रमो महान् ।
उपयातः सहस्राक्षो
यत्र शक्रः पुरन्दरः ॥
This bright Āṡrama is that of Mahātma Suteekshṇa. The great Āṡrama of Ṡarabhaṅga can be seen here, to which which the Thousand-Eyed Ṡakra, the Destroyer-of-the-Citadel paid a visit.
6.123.49
अस्मिन् देशे महाकायो
विराधो निहतो मया ।
अत्र सीते त्वया दृष्टा
तापसी धर्मचारिणी ॥
This is the area where Virādha of humongous body was slain by me. This is where you visited (Anasūyā), the Tāpasi, a strict adherent of Dharma.
6.123.50
असौ सुतनु शैलेन्द्रः
चित्रकूटः प्रकाशते ।
अत्र मां केकयीपुत्रः
प्रसादयितुमागतः ॥
O you of perfect figure! This is the shining Citrakūṭa, a king among mountains, where Bharata came to persuade me.
6.123.51
एषा सा यमुना रम्या
दृश्यते चित्रकानना ।
भरद्वाजाश्रमः श्रीमान्
दृश्यते चैष मैथिलि ॥
This is the lovely Yamuna that can be seen here with its beautiful woods. This one that is seen here is the magnificent Āṡrama of Bharadwāja, O Maithili!
6.123.52
इयं च दृश्यते गङ्गा
पुण्या त्रिपथगा नदी ।
नानाद्विजगणाकीर्णा
सम्प्रपुष्पितकानना ॥
Here can be seen the sacred Gaṅgā that flows in all the three worlds, crowded with many flocks of birds and with woods (on its banks) in full bloom.
In the next two Sargas, we see Rāma stopping at the Āṡrama of Bharadwāja and sending Hanumān from there to Ayōdhyā to convey the news about his arrival to Bharata.
These Ṡlōkas, on the other hand, say that Pushpaka flew all the way to Ayōdhyā. These two contradicting narrations must be from two different manuscripts, both of which the modern day compilers apparently retained instead of choosing one over the other.
6.123.53
शृङ्गिबेरपुरं चैतद्
गुहो यत्र समागतः ।
एषा सा दृश्यते सीते
सरयूर्यूपमालिनी ।
नानातरुशताकीर्णा
सम्प्रपुष्पितकानना ॥
This is the Ṡṛṅgibērapura where Guha met me. And there can be seen Sarayu, O Seetā, with its garland of sacrificial stakes, its hundreds of trees of every kind, and its woods in full flower.
6.123.54
एषा सा दृश्यते सीते
राजधानी पितुर्मम ।
अयोध्यां कुरु वैदेहि
प्रणामं पुनरागता ॥
And here can be seen, O Seetā, the capital city of my father. Please pay your obeisance to Ayōdhyā, having returned to it.
6.123.55
ततस्ते वानराः सर्वे
राक्षसाः सविभीषणः ।
उत्पत्योत्पत्य संहृष्टाः
तां पुरीं ददृशुस्तदा ॥
Then all the Vānaras and Rākshasas including Vibheeshaṇa sprang up again and again with joy, to behold that city.
6.123.56
ततस्तु तां पाम्डुरहर्म्यमालिनीं
विशालकक्ष्यां गजवाजिसङ्कुलाम् ।
पुरीमश्यन् प्लवगाः सराक्षसाः
पुरीं महेन्द्रस्य यथाऽमरावतीम् ॥
The Vānaras, along with the Rākshasas, saw that city with its wide pathways, its rows of lofty white mansions and its crowds of horses and elephants, which resembled Amarāvati, the city of Mahēndra.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे त्रयोविंशत्युत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and twenty third Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
