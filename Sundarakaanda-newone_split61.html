Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 61
In this Sarga, the Vānaras take flight to go back to Rāma and Sugreeva, eager to give the good news and resolute on helping Rāma take revenge. On their way they come across the Madhuvana that was maintained and protected by Sugreeva, and was guarded by his maternal uncle Dadhimukha.
The Vānaras seek the permission of Aṅgada to drink the liquor in that Vana, which he grants after consulting senior Vānaras like Jāmbavān. Intoxicated after consuming the liquor, they have all the fun and frolic in the world pushing each other, jumping up and down and so on.
Seeing them destroy everything, Dadhimukha tries to stop them in every which way possible, yelling, hitting and reasoning with them. But intoxicated, they turn against him, drag him about and beat him almost to death. They empty the Vana completely.
5.61.1
ततो जाम्बवतो वाक्यम्
अगृह्णन्त वनौकसः ।
अङ्गदप्रमुखा वीरा
हनुमांश्च महाकपिः ॥
The Veeras like Aṅgada and the great Vānara Hanumān and all the Vana dwellers accepted the advice of Jāmbavān.
5.61.2
प्रीतिमन्तस्ततः सर्वे
वायुपुत्रपरस्पराः ।
महेन्द्राद्रिं परित्यज्य
पुप्लुवुः प्लवगर्षभाः ॥
Feeling elated, led by the son of Vāyu, those bulls among Vānaras flew, from the Mandara Mountain.
5.61.3
मेरुमन्दरसङ्काशा
मत्ता इव महागजाः ।
छादयन्त इवाकाशम्
महाकाया महाबलाः ॥
They, of immense strength and giant bodies, who resembled the Mēru and Mandara mountains and elephants in rut, seemed to cloud the entire sky.
5.61.4
सभाज्यमानं भूतैस्तम्
आत्मवन्तं महाबलम् ।
हनूमन्तं महावेगम्
वहन्त इव दृष्टिभिः ॥
(With their eyes fixed on him), it seemed as if they carried Hanumān of great composure, speed and immense strength, whom all the creatures honored, with their eyesight.
5.61.5
राघवे चार्थनिर्वृत्तिं
कर्तुं च परमं यशः ।
समाधाय समृद्धार्थाः
सर्वेसिद्धिभिरुन्नता ॥
Set on accomplishing the mission of Rāma and looking for no less than great acclaim, they went, having succeeded in their task and their efforts having come to fruition.
5.61.6
प्रियाख्यानोन्मुखाः सर्वे
सर्वे युद्धाभिनन्दिनः ।
सर्वे रामप्रतीकारे
निश्चितार्था मनस्स्विनः ॥
Everyone was eager to convey the good news. Everyone was ready for a chance to fight. Everyone, in his mind, in all sincerity, was resolute on helping Rāma take revenge.
5.61.7
प्लवमानाः खमाप्लुत्य
ततस्ते काननौकसः ।
नन्दनोपमयासेदुः
वनं द्रुमलतायुतम् ॥
Taking a leap into the sky and flying, the Vānaras came to a Nandana-like Vana that was full of trees and creepers.
5.61.8
यत्तन्मधुवनं नाम
सुग्रीवस्याभिरक्षितम् ।
अधृष्यं सर्वभूतानाम्
सर्वभूतमनोहरम् ॥
Well-protected by Sugreeva, captivating the hearts of everyone and inaccessible to all creatures, it was known as Madhuvana.
5.61.9
यद्रक्षति महावीर्यः
सदा दधिमुखः कपिः ।
मातुलः कपिमुख्यस्य
सुग्रीवस्य महात्मनः ॥
It was guarded by Dadhimukha, a Vānara of great valor. He was the maternal uncle of the chief of Vānaras, Sugreeva, the Mahātma.
5.61.10
ते तद्वनमुपागम्य
बभूवुः परमोत्कटाः ।
वानरा वानरेन्द्रस्य
मनः कान्ततमं महत् ॥
On reaching that great Vana, which the Lord of the Vānaras loved as the apple of his eye, the Vānaras were in a frenzy of excitement.
5.61.11
ततस्ते वानरा हृष्टा
दृष्ट्वा मधुवनं महत् ।
कुमारमभ्ययाचन्त
मधूनि मधुपिङ्गलाः ॥
Excited on seeing that great Madhuvana, the Vānaras of honey complexion begged the young prince (Aṅgada) to let them have the liquor.
5.61.12
ततः कुमारस्तान् वृद्धान्
जाम्बवत्प्रमुखान् कपीन् ।
अनुमान्य ददौ तेषाम्
विसर्गं मधुभक्षणे ॥
After convincing the elderly Vānaras like Jāmbavān, the young prince let them have the liquor.
5.61.13
ततश्चानुमतास्सर्वे
सम्प्रहृष्टा वनौकसः ।
मुदिताः प्रेरिताश्चापि
प्रनृत्यन्तोऽभवंस्तदा ॥
Thus permitted, all the Vana dwellers became extremely happy. Happy and egged on by each other, they started dancing and capering.
5.61.14
गायन्ति केचित्प्रणमन्ति केचित्
नृत्यन्ति केचित्प्रहसन्ति केचित् ।
पतन्ति केचिद्विचरन्ति केचित्
प्लवन्ति केचित्प्रलपन्ति केचित् ॥
Some sang, some bowed, some danced, and others burst out laughing. Some fell on the ground, some walked up and down, some jumped and some talked gibberish.
5.61.15
परस्परं केचिदुपाश्रयन्ते
परस्परं केचिदुपाक्रमन्ते ।
परस्परं केचिदुपब्रुवन्ते
परस्परं केचिदुपारमन्ते ॥
Some leaned on each other for support, some just fell on each other. Some talked with each other and some played with each other.
5.61.16
द्रुमाद्द्रुमं केचिदभिद्रवन्ते
क्षितौ नगाग्रान्निपतन्ति केचित् ।
महीतलात्केचिदुदीर्णवेगा
महाद्रुमाग्राण्यभिसम्पतन्ति ॥
Some ran from one tree to the other. Some fell from the top of the trees on to the ground. Some jumped from the ground to the top of huge trees with great speed.
5.61.17
गायन्तमन्यः प्रहसन्नुपैति
हसन्तमन्यः प्ररुदन्नुपैति ।
रुदन्तमन्यः प्रणुदन्नुपैति
नुदन्तमन्यः प्रणदन्नुपैति ॥
One, laughing, went to another who was singing. One, crying, went to another who was laughing. One, pushing, went to another who was crying. One, shouting, went to another who was pushing.
5.61.18
समाकुलं तत्कपिसैन्यमासीत्
मधुप्रसानोत्कटसत्त्वचेष्टम् ।
न चात्र कश्चिन्न बभूव मत्तो
न चात्र कश्चिन्न बभूव तृप्तः ॥
That army of Vānaras raised a cacophony, losing all sense of control in their frenzy of drinking the liquor. There was no one who was not intoxicated. There was no one who had enough of it.
5.61.19
ततो वनं तत्परिभक्ष्यमाणं
द्रुमांश्च विध्वंसितपत्रपुष्पान् ।
समीक्ष्य कोपाद्धधिवक्रनामा
निवारयामास कपिः कपींस्तान् ॥
Seeing everything in the Vana eaten up, its trees denuded of their leaves and flowers, the enraged Vānara Dadhimukha tried to stop those Vānaras.
5.61.20
स तैः प्रवृद्धैः परिभर्त्स्यमानो
वनस्य गोप्ता हरिवीरवृद्धः ।
चकार भूयो मतिमुग्रतेजा
वनस्य रक्षां प्रति वानरेभ्यः ॥
With them going out of control despite his threats, the veteran Vānara Veera of fierce valor, who was in-charge of the Vana, decided to save the Vana from those Vānaras.
5.61.21
उवाच कांश्चित्परुषाणि धृष्टम्
असक्तमन्यांश्च तलैर्जघान ।
समेत्य कैश्चित्कलहं चकार
तथैव साम्नोपजगाम कांश्चित् ॥
He rebuked some in a stern tone. Being bold and having no hesitation, he smacked some hard with his palm. He went to some and picked up a quarrel. He went to some and tried to reason out.
5.61.22
स तैर्मदादप्रतिवार्य वेगैः
बलाच्च तेन प्रतिवार्यमाणैः ।
प्रधर्षितस्त्यक्तभयैस्समेत्य
प्रकृष्यते चाप्यनवेक्ष्य दोषम् ॥
He tried to stop them forcibly, but, intoxicated, there was no stopping them. Giving up every sense of fear, they got together and turned against him. They even dragged him about, losing all sense of impropriety.
5.61.23
नखैस्तुदन्तो दशनैर्दशन्तः
तलैश्च पादैश्च समापयन्तः ।
मदात्कपिं तं कपयस्समग्रा
महावनं निर्विषयं च चक्रुः ॥
Mauling him with their claws, biting him with their teeth, and pounding him with their hands and feet, they left him almost dead. Intoxicated, all those Vānaras emptied that Vana completely.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे एकषष्टितम स्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty first Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
