Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 19
In this Sarga, Tārā comes to know that Vāli was killed by Rāma. Coming out of the city, she sees Vānaras fleeing as if they were chased by the arrows of Rāma.
They suggest to her to go back and fortify the city and install Aṅgada on the throne, so that all of them could rally behind him, before the Vānaras, who were mistreated earlier by them, take over the fort.
Tārā tells them that she does not care about her son or kingdom when her lord, husband, is dead. She goes past them and past Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, to Vāli who lay on the ground, and cries.
Sugreeva also feels terrible seeing her cry and Aṅgada who had just come.
4.19.1
स वानरमहाराजः
शयानश्शरपीडितः ।
प्रत्युक्तो हेतुमद्वाक्यैः
नोत्तरं प्रत्यपद्यत ॥
The great king of the Vānaras, who lay on the ground wounded by the arrow had nothing to say by way of reply, when he was thus answered cogently.
4.19.2
अश्मभिः प्रविभिन्नाङ्गः
पादपैराहतो भृशम् ।
रामबाणेन चाक्रान्तो
जीवितान्ते मुमोह सः ॥
His limbs broken by stones and severely battered by trees, he, transfixed by Rāma’s arrow, fell into a swoon, at death’s door.
4.19.3
तं भार्या बाणमोक्षेण
रामदत्तेन संयुगे ।
हतं प्लवगशार्दूलम्
तारा शुश्राव वालिनम् ॥
Tārā, his wife, heard that Vāli, a tiger among Vānaras, had been struck down by an arrow shot by Rāma in battle.
4.19.4
सा सपुत्राप्रियं श्रुत्वा
वधं भर्तुस्सुदारुणम् ।
निष्पपात भृशं तस्मात्
उद्विग्ना गिरिकंदरात् ॥
Hearing the shocking news of the terrible slaying of her husband, she came out with her son from the mountain cave in great fear.
4.19.5
ये त्वङ्गदपरीवारा
वानरा भीमविक्रमाः ।
ते सकार्मुकमालोक्य
रामं त्रस्ताः प्रदुद्रुवुः ॥
The retainers of Aṅgada, Vānaras of fierce valor, had fled when they saw Rāma armed with the bow.
4.19.6
सा ददर्श ततस्त्रस्तान्
हरीनापततो भृशम् ।
यूथादिव परिभ्रष्टान्
मृगान्निहतयूथपान् ॥
And she saw the frightened Vānaras as they came running and scurrying, like a flock that gets scattered when its lead is struck down.
4.19.7
तानुवाच समासाद्य
दुःखितान्दुखिता सती ।
रामवित्रासितान्सर्वान्
अनुबद्धानिवेषुभिः ॥
She in her grief, said to all those Vānaras who were also crying in grief, and were scared of Rāma, fleeing as if they were being chased by his arrows:
4.19.8
वानराः राजसिंहस्य
यस्य यूयं पुरस्सराः ।
तं विहाय सुसन्त्रस्ताः
कस्माद्द्रवथ दुर्गताः ॥
O Vānaras, why are you, who marched in the vanguard of that lion among kings, running away in such a bad shape and in great panic, abandoning him?
4.19.9
राज्यहेतोस्स चेद्भ्राता
भ्रात्रा रौद्रेण पातितः ।
रामेण प्रसृतैर्दूरात्
मार्गणैर्दूरपातिभिः ॥
Has he, a brother, for the sake of a kingdom, been struck down by his cruel brother, with the (help of) arrows of Rāma that can hit a target from a distance?
4.19.10
कपिपत्न्या वचश्श्रुत्वा
कपयः कामरूपिणः ।
प्राप्तकालमविक्लिष्टम्
ऊचुर्वचनमङ्गनाम् ॥
The Vānaras, who could take any form at will, answered that woman, the wife of the Vānara, giving her a clear message that was apt for the situation:
4.19.11
जीवपुत्रे निवर्तस्व
पुत्रं रक्षस्व चाङ्गदम् ।
अन्तको रामरूपेण
हत्वा नयति वालिनम् ॥
O lady! You have a son Aṅgada, well and alive! Take care of him! Yama has come in the form of Rāma, killed Vāli and is taking him away.
4.19.12
क्षिप्तान् वृक्षान्समाविध्य
विपुलाश्च शिलास्तथा ।
वाली वज्रसमैर्बाणै
रामेण विनिपातितः ॥
Vāli was struck down by arrows that fell like thunderbolts shattering even the trees and the boulders he hurled.
Vāli did not fight directly with Rāma. Vāli did not throw trees or boulders at Rāma. This is an imagination of the Vānaras. This Ṡlōka is an indication of human nature, of how people are susceptible to imagine things of their liking.
4.19.13
अभिभूतमिदं सर्वम्
विद्रुतं वानरं बलम् ।
अस्मिन् प्लवगशार्दूले
हते शक्रसमप्रभे ॥
With the tiger among Vānaras, equal in splendor to Ṡakra, killed, this entire Vānara force is done to naught and is fleeing.
4.19.14
रक्ष्यतां नगरद्वारम्
अङ्गदश्चाभिषिच्यताम् ।
पदस्थं वालिनः पुत्रम्
भजिष्यन्ति प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
The gateways of the city should be fortified. Aṅgada should be consecrated on the throne. Vānaras will rally in support of the son of Vāli installed in the seat.
4.19.15
अथवाऽरुचितं स्थानम्
इह ते रुचिरानने ।
आविशन्ति हि दुर्गाणि
क्षिप्रमद्यैव वानराः ॥
This, certainly, is not the right place for you to be in, O you of beautiful face! These Vānaras, in no time, are going to occupy the fortresses.
4.19.16
अभार्याश्च सभार्याश्च
सन्त्यत्र वनचारिणः ।
लुब्धेभ्यो विप्रलब्धेयः
तेभ्यो नस्तुमुलं भयम् ॥
There are many Vana-dwellers here, single as well as married. Some are greedy and some have been mistreated by us. There is great danger to us from them.
4.19.17
अल्पान्तरगतानां तु
श्रुत्वा वचनमङ्गना ।
आत्मनः प्रतिरूपं सा
बभाषे चारुहासिनी ॥
After proceeding for a short distance, that woman with a winning smile spoke in words that truly reflected her personality:
4.19.18
पुत्रेण मम किं कार्यम्
राज्येनच किमात्मना ।
कपिसिंहे महाभागे
तस्मिन्भर्तरि नश्यति ॥
What will I do with my son or kingdom, when the supremely blessed lion among Vānaras, my husband, has been destroyed!
4.19.19
पादमूलं गमिष्यामि
तस्यैवाहं महात्मनः ।
योऽसौ रामप्रयुक्तेन
शरेण विनिपातितः ॥
I shall throw myself at the feet of that Mahātma who was struck down by the arrow shot by Rāma.
4.19.20
एवमुक्त्वा प्रदुद्राव
रुदन्ती शोककर्शिता ।
शिरश्चोरश्च बाहुभ्याम्
दुःखेन समभिध्नती ॥
Having said this, she, distraught with grief, ran, weeping and beating her head and breast with both her hands.
4.19.21
सा व्रजन्ती ददर्शाथ
पतिं निपतितं भुवि ।
हन्तारं दानवेन्द्राणाम्
समरेष्वनिवर्तिनम् ॥
Arriving there, she saw, fallen on the ground, her husband, slayer of mighty lords of Dānavas who had never turned his back on the battlefield,
4.19.22
क्षेप्तारं पर्वतेन्द्राणाम्
वज्राणामिव वासवम् ।
महावातसमाविष्टम्
महामेघौघनिस्स्वनम् ॥
who hurls mighty mountains just as Vāsava does the Vajra, whose speed is like that of a gale, whose shout rivals the thunder of massive clouds,
4.19.23
शक्रतुल्यपराक्रान्तं
वृष्ट्वेवोपरतं घनम् ।
नर्दन्तं नर्दतां भीमम्
शूरं शूरेण पातितम् ।
शार्दूलेनामिषस्यार्थे
मृगराजं यथाहतम् ॥
whose prowess equals that of Ṡakra, who is like a cloud that has dislodged its water, whose roar terrifies even those who roar, a Ṡūra struck down by a Ṡūra (Rāma), like the king of deer killed by a tiger for its flesh
4.19.24
अर्चितं सर्वलोकस्य
सपताकं सवेदिकम् ।
नागहेतोस्सुपर्णेन
चैत्यमुन्मथितं यथा ॥
and who was like a shrine along with its pennants and Vēdis, ravaged by an eagle for the sake of a snake (that hid itself under it).
4.19.25
अवष्टभ्य च तिष्ठन्तं
ददर्श धनुरुत्तम् ।
रामं रामानुजं चैव
भर्तुश्चैव तथानुजम् ॥
She saw Rāma standing leaning on his sturdy bow, and also his younger brother as well as the younger brother of her husband.
4.19.26
तानतीत्य समासाद्य
भर्तारं निहतं रणे ।
समीक्ष्य व्यथिता भूमौ
सम्भ्रान्ता निपपात ह ॥
Passing them by and coming near her husband and seeing him fatally wounded in the fight, she fell on the ground in shock and agony.
4.19.27
सुप्तेव पुनरुत्थाय
आर्यपुत्रेति वादिनी ।
रुरोद सा पतिं दृष्ट्वा
संवीतं मृत्युदामभिः ॥
Then, getting up, as though from sleep, she looked at her husband bound fast by the cords of death and cried ‘O son of noble lineage!’,
4.19.28
तामवेक्ष्य तु सुग्रीवः
क्रोशन्तीं कुररीमिव ।
विषादमगमत्कष्टम्
दृष्ट्वा चाङ्गदमागतम् ॥
Sugreeva, on seeing her who wept like a female osprey, and Aṅgada who had just come, felt terribly pained.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे एकोनविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the nineteenth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
