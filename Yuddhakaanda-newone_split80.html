Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 80
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa becomes furious hearing that Makarāksha is slain. He instructs Indrajit to head to the battlefield. Indrajit, after offering oblations to Agni and propitiating Dēvas and Dānavas, heads to the battlefield, following the command of his father.
Using his magical powers, Indrajit ascends to the sky along with his chariot and creates smoke that obfuscates the sky. He then shoots arrows, staying invisible. Unable to locate where Indrajit is, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa shoot arrows into the sky in every direction, and then in the direction in which they see arrows coming from.
Indrajit continues to shoot arrows at Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and the Vānaras. Many Vānaras fall dead. Lakshmaṇa suggests to Rāma that he himself would deploy Brahma's Astra and destroy the entire Rākshasa race. Rāma tells that the entire race of Rākshasas should not be destroyed because of the transgression of just one of them. Rāma then sets himself ready to put an end to Indrajit, with his powerful Astras.
6.80.1
मकराक्षं हतं श्रुत्वा
रावणः समितिञ्जयः ।
क्रोधेन महताऽऽविष्टो
दन्तान् कटकटापयन् ॥
Hearing that Makarāksha was slain, Rāvaṇa, who had won many battles, gnashed his teeth, becoming extremely furious.
6.80.2
कुपितश्च तदा तत्र
किं कार्यमिति चिन्तयन् ।
आदिदेशाथ सङ्क्रुद्धो
रणायेन्द्रजितं सुतम् ॥
He, the enraged one, pondered over what to do next. He then ordered his son Indrajit to head to the battlefield.
6.80.3
जहि वीर महावीर्यौ
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
अदृश्यो दृश्यमानो वा
सर्वथा त्वं बलाधिकः ॥
Go and slay those those two brothers, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, of extraordinary valor, either being visible to them or hiding yourself. You possess superior strength, in every which way.
6.80.4
त्वमप्रतिमकर्माणम्
इन्द्रं जयसि संयुगे ।
किं पुनर्मानुषौ दृष्ट्वा
न वधिष्यसि संयुगे ॥
You vanquished in fight even Indra, who is unequaled in what he can do; why would you not be able to slay a mere human in fight, when you set your eyes on him?
6.80.5
तथोक्तो राक्षसेन्द्रेण
प्रतिगृह्य पितुर्वचः ।
यज्ञभूमौ स विधिवत्
पावकं जुहवेन्द्रजित् ॥
Thus told by his father, the Lord of Rākshasas, Indrajit, obeying his orders, offered oblations to Agni on the Yajña grounds in a customary manner.
6.80.6
जुह्वतश्चापि तत्राग्निम्
रक्तोष्णीषधराः स्त्रियः ।
आजग्मुस्तत्र सम्भ्रान्ता
राक्षस्यो यत्र रावणिः ॥
As the son of Rāvaṇa offered the oblations, Rākshasa women hastened there, wearing turbans of red cloth.
6.80.7
शस्त्राणि शरपत्राणि
समिधोऽथ विभीतकाः ।
लोहितानि च वासांसि
स्रुवं कार्ष्णायसं तथा ॥
Weapons in place of Ṡarapatras, twigs of Vibheetaka, red-colored clothes and ladles made of iron were used.
Ṡarapatras, Vibheetaka twigs and ladles made of iron are used in black magic rituals.
These Ṡlōkas are the same as Ṡlōkas 6.73.19 through 6.73.22.
6.80.8
सर्वतोऽग्निं समास्तीर्य
शरपत्रैः सतोमरैः ।
छागस्य कृष्णवर्णस्य
गलं जग्राह जीवितः ॥
Ṡarapatras and Tōmaras were placed around the fire and the head of a live black sheep was offered (as an oblation).
6.80.9
सकृदेव समिद्धस्य
विधूमस्य महार्चिषः ।
बभूवुस्तानि लिङ्गानि
विजयं दर्शयन्ति च ॥
Then there arose suddenly bright smokeless tongues of flame from that great fire, serving as an omen presaging victory.
6.80.10
प्रदक्षिणावर्तशिखः
तप्तकाञ्चनसन्निभः ।
हविस्तत् प्रतिजग्राह
पावकः स्वयमुत्थितः ॥
The fire that dazzled like molten gold rose up and received that oblation with its tongues of flame that whirled in clockwise direction.
6.80.11
हुत्वाऽग्निं तर्पयित्वाथ
देवदानवराक्षसान् ।
आरुरोह रथश्रेष्ठम्
अन्तर्धानगतं शुभम् ॥
Having thus offered oblations and propitiated the Dēvas, the Dānavas and the Rākshasas, he got into a splendid chariot which could stay invisible (to the foe).
6.80.12
स वाजिभिश्चतुर्भिश्च
बाणैश्च निशितैर्युतः ।
आरोपितमहाचापः
शुशुभे स्यन्दनोत्तमः ॥
That splendid chariot dazzled, drawn by four horses and loaded with great bows along with sharp arrows.
6.80.13
जाज्वल्यमानो वपुषा
तपनीयपरिच्छदः ।
मृगैश्चन्द्रार्धचन्द्रैश्च
स रथः समलङ्कृतः ॥
Treated with gold everywhere, and with animal figurines, full moon and half moon designs splendidly carved on it, it was dazzling in appearance.
6.80.14
जाम्बूनदमहाकम्बुः
दीप्तपावकसन्निभः ।
बभूवेन्द्रजितः केतुः
वैडूर्यसमलङ्कृतः ॥
Indrajit’s flagstaff blazed like fire, adorned with lazulite and with a huge ring of gold around it.
6.80.15
तेन चादित्यकल्पेन
ब्रह्मास्त्रेण च पालितः ।
स बभूव दुराधर्षो
रावणिः सुमहाबलः ॥
Protected by it (the chariot) and by the power of Brahma's Astra, he, the son of Rāvaṇa, of immense strength, was impregnable against any assault.
6.80.16
सोऽभिनिर्याय नगरात्
इन्द्रजित् समितिञ्जयः ।
हुत्वाऽग्निं राक्षसैर्मन्त्रैः
अन्तर्धानगतोऽब्रवीत् ॥
Having marched forth from the city and having offered oblations to Agni chanting Mantras specially used by Rākshasas, and set to make himself invisible, he said:
6.80.17
अद्य हत्वा रणे यौ तौ
मिथ्या प्रव्राजितौ वने ।
जयं पित्रे प्रदास्यामि
रावणाय रणार्जितम् ।
अद्य निर्वानरामुर्वीम्
हत्वा रामं सलक्ष्मणम् ।
करिष्ये परमप्रीतिम्
I shall slay this very day those two whose exile in the Vana is a mere pretext, and give the gift of victory to my father Rāvaṇa. Slaying Rāma and Lakshmaṇa and ridding the earth of Vānaras, I shall make him extremely happy.
6.80.18
इत्युक्त्वाऽन्तरधीयत
Saying this, he vanished from sight.
6.80.19
आपपाताथ सङ्क्रुद्धो
दशग्रीवेण चोदितः ।
तीक्ष्णकार्मुकनाराचैः
तीक्ष्णस्त्विन्द्ररिपू रणे ॥
The cruel foe of Indra, armed with bow and sharp arrows, angrily rushed to the scene of battle under the prompting of the Ten-necked.
6.80.20
स ददर्श महावीर्यो
नागौ त्रिशिरसाविव ।
सृजन्ताविषुजालानि
वीरौ वानरमध्यगौ ॥
He beheld the warriors (Rāma and Lakshmaṇa) of extraordinary valor who looked like three-headed serpents, discharging volleys of arrows from the midst of Vānaras.
The beautiful imagery used back in Bāla Kāṇḍa (1.22.7) comes again here. With quiver raised above one shoulder and the bow raised above the other, and their heads in the middle, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa look like three-headed serpents.
6.80.21
इमौ ताविति सञ्चिन्त्य
सज्यं कृत्वा च कार्मुकम् ।
सन्ततानेषुधाराभिः
पर्जन्य इव वृष्टिमान् ॥
Concluding that they were the duo he was looking for, he strung his bow and drenched them in a shower of arrows, like a cloud does with a torrent of rain.
6.80.22
स तु वैहायसं प्राप्य
सरथो रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
अचक्षुर्विषये तिष्ठन्
विव्याध निशितैः शिऱैः ॥
Ascending into the sky in his chariot, and making himself invisible, he struck Rāma and Lakshmaṇa with sharp arrows.
6.80.23
तौ तस्य शरवेगेन
परीतौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
धनुषी सशरे कृत्वा
दिव्यमस्त्रं प्रचक्रतुः ॥
Feeling the impact of the force of those arrows, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa mounted their arrows on the bow and infused them with the power of celestial Astras.
6.80.24
प्रच्छादयन्तौ गगनम्
शरजालैर्महाबलौ ।
तमस्त्रैः सूर्यसङ्काशैः
नैव सस्पृशतुः शरैः ॥
Then those two of immense strength, blotted the entire sky with their volleys of arrows that blazed like the sun. But he (Indrajit) was not even so much as touched by those arrows.
6.80.25
स हि धूमान्धकारं च
चक्रे प्रच्छादयन्नभः ।
दिशश्चान्तर्दधे श्रीमान्
नीहारतमसावृताः ॥
He, the powerful one, then created dark smoke (with his magical powers) that looked like a dense fog, obfuscating the sky and making the cardinals disappear.
6.80.26
नैव ज्यातलनिर्घोषो
न च नेमिखुरस्वनः ।
शश्रुवे चरतस्तस्य
न च रूपं प्रकाशते ॥
Neither the thud of bowstring hitting the palm nor the clatter of wheels and horse-hooves could be heard, nor could he himself be seen.
6.80.27
घनान्धकारे तिमिरे
शरवर्षमिवाद्भुतम् ।
स ववर्ष महाबाहुः
नाराचशरवृष्टिभिः ॥
He of mighty arm, in that intense darkness, sent a stunning torrent of Nārāca arrows.
6.80.28
स रामं सूर्यसङ्काशैः
शरैर्दत्तवरो भृशम् ।
विव्याध समरे क्रुद्धः
सर्वगात्रेषु रावणिः ॥
In his great wrath, the son of Rāvaṇa, who had obtained many a boon, struck every limb of Rāma with arrows that burned like the sun.
6.80.29
तौ हन्यमानौ नाराचैः
धाराभिरिव पर्वतौ ।
हेमपुङ्खान्नरव्याघ्रौ
तिग्मान् मुमुचतुः शरान् ॥
Those two tigers among men, assailed by arrows that fell on them like showers on a mountain, then shot sharp arrows with gold rings.
6.80.30
अन्तरिक्षे समासाद्य
रावणिं कङ्कपत्रिणः ।
निकृत्य पतगा भूमौ
पेतुस्ते शोणितोक्षिताः ॥
Those arrows, sped by the feathers of Kaṅka bird, reached Rāvaṇa's son in the sky, gored him, and fell down on the ground, daubed with blood.
6.80.31
अतिमात्रं शरौघेण
पीड्यमानौ नरोत्तमौ ।
तानिषून् पततो भल्लेः
अनेकैर्विचकृन्ततुः ॥
As the volleys of arrows (of Indrajit) began to oppress them extremely, they shot Bhalla arrows at them and broke them off in midair into many bits.
6.80.32
यतो हि ददृशाते तौ
शरान्निपततः शितान् ।
ततस्तु तौ दाशरथी
ससृजातेऽस्त्रमुत्तमम् ॥
The sons of Daṡaratha shot the best of Astras in the direction in which they saw those sharp arrows fall from.
6.80.33
रावणिस्तु दिशः सर्वा
रथेनातिरथोऽपतत् ।
विव्याध तौ दाशरथी
लध्वस्त्रो निशितैः शरैः ॥
The son of Rāvaṇa, the super-charioter who was adept at Astras, crisscrossed in every direction in his chariot, continued the assault with his sharp arrows on the two sons of Daṡaratha.
6.80.34
तेनातिविद्धौ तौ वीरौ
रुक्मपुङ्खैः सुसंहितैः ।
बभूवतुर्दाशरथी
पुष्पिताविव किंशुकौ ॥
Struck by those arrows with golden rings that were shot with extraordinary nimbleness the sons of Daṡaratha looked like Kiṃṡuka trees in full bloom.
6.80.35
नास्य वेग गतिं कश्चित्
न च रूपं धनुः शरान् ।
न चान्यद्विदितं किञ्चित्
सूर्यस्येवाभ्रसम्प्लवे ॥
No one could tell where he was, how fast he was moving, where his bow and arrows were, or anything else about him, any more than one can tell where the sun is when masses of clouds blot the sky.
6.80.36
तेन विद्धाश्च हरयो
निहताश्च गतासवः ।
बभूवुः शतशस्तत्र
पतिता धरणीतले ॥
Hundreds of Vānaras, struck by his arrows fell dead on the ground.
6.80.37
लक्ष्मणस्तु सुसङ्क्रुद्धो
भ्रातरं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ।
ब्राह्ममस्त्रं प्रयोक्ष्यामि
वधार्थं सर्वरक्षसाम् ॥
With that, Lakshmaṇa became outraged and said to his brother, 'I shall deploy Brahma's Astra and destroy all the Rākshasas!'
6.80.38
तमुवाच ततो रामो
लक्ष्मणं शुभलक्षणम् ॥
Then Rāma said to Lakshmaṇa who bore all auspicious marks on his person:
6.80.38
नैकस्य हेतो रक्षांसि
पृथिव्यां हन्तु मर्हसि ।
अयुध्यमानं प्रच्छन्नम्
प्राञ्जलिं शरणागतम् ।
पलायन्तं प्रमत्तं वा
न त्वं हन्तुमिहार्हसि ॥
You should not slay all the Rākshasas on the earth because of the transgression of one.
You should not slay one who does not put up a fight, or one who hides oneself or flees or is not on one's own guard, or surrenders oneself with palms joined.
6.80.40
अस्यैव तु वधे यत्नम्
करिष्यावो महाबल ।
आदेक्ष्यावो महावेगान्
अस्त्रानाशीविषोपमान् ॥
We will put every effort to slay him and him alone, O you of mighty strength! Let us deploy extremely powerful Astras that bite like venomous serpents.
6.80.41
तमेनं मायिनं क्षुद्रम्
अन्तर्हितरथं बलात् ।
राक्षसं निहनिष्यन्ति
दृष्ट्वा वानरयूथपाः ॥
The Vānara chiefs themselves are strong enough to kill this wile trickster who is hiding in the sky along with his chariot, if only they could see him.
6.80.42
यद्यैष भूमिं विशते दिवं वा
रसातलं वापि नभस्स्थलं वा ।
एवं निगूढोऽपि ममास्त्रदग्धः
पतिष्यते भूमितले गतासुः ॥
No matter whether he takes to the the earth or to the heavens, to the nether world or to the skies, or hides himself like this, he will fall on the ground dead burnt by my Astras.
6.80.43
इत्येवमुक्त्वा वचनं महात्मा
रघुप्रवीरः प्लवगर्षभैर्वृतः ।
वधाय रौद्रस्य नृशंसकर्मणः
तदा महात्मा त्वरितं निरीक्षते ॥
Saying this, that Mahātma, a great warrior among Raghus, standing in the midst of the bulls among Vānaras, was all set to kill the terrifying Rākshasa of cruel deeds, as soon as possible.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे अशीतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eightieth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
