Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 11
In this Sarga, Hanumān continues the search. He searches through the banquet hall where he finds various liquor jars, meats and pitchers and cups strewn around, and the air filled with various smells.
Not finding Jānaki and realizing that he has been taking a close look at the women of another man, he fears that he might have transgressed Dharma. But he reconciles that, after all, a lost woman cannot be searched for among deer, but only among other women. He is convinced that he looked at the women only with that pure intention and that his mind did not yield to any improper feelings towards them.
5.11.1
अवधूय च तां बुद्धिम्
बभूवावस्थितस्तदा ।
जगाम चापरां चिन्ताम्
सीतां प्रति महाकपिः ॥
Then the great Vānara steadied himself. Abandoning that (mistaken) notion, he thought about Seetā from a different perspective.
5.11.2
न रामेण वियुक्ता सा
स्वप्तुमर्हति भामिनी ।
न भोक्तुं नाप्यलंकर्तुम्
न पानमुपसेवितुम् ।
नान्यं नरमुपस्थातुम्
सुराणामपि चेश्वरम् ।
न हि रामसमः कश्चित्
विद्यते त्रिदशेष्वपि ।
अन्येयमिति निश्चित्य
पानभूमौ चचार सः ॥
She would not possibly be able to sleep, eat, decorate herself or indulge in a drink, separated from Rāma.
She would not even get close to anyone else, even if it was the lord of Suras. For, there was no one comparable to Rāma even among the Dēvas.
Concluding that she must be some other woman, he went on exploring the drinking hall.
5.11.4
क्रीडितेनापराः क्लान्ता
गीतेन च तथाऽपराः ।
नृत्तेन चापराः क्लान्ताः
पानविप्रहतास्तथा ॥
Some were exhausted sporting, others singing and yet others, having danced. Their senses were knocked off by the drinks.
5.11.5
मुरजेषु मृदङ्गेषु
पीठिकासु च संस्थिताः ।
तथाऽऽस्तरणमुख्येषु
संविष्टाश्चापराः स्त्रियः ॥
Other women were lying on Murajas, Mṛdaṅgas, benches and exquisite carpets.
5.11.6
अङ्गनानां सहस्रेण
भूषितेन विभूषणैः ।
रूपसल्लापशीलेन
युक्तगीतार्थभाषिणा ।
देशकालाभियुक्तेन
युक्तवाक्याभिधायिना ।
रताभिरतसंसुप्तम्
ददर्श हरियूथपः ॥
The Vānara chief saw thousands of women adorned with ornaments, who excelled in beauty, in their skill of making conversation, in delving on the nuanced meanings of songs and in their acute sense of time and place, who slept, exhausted in love sports.
5.11.8
तासां मध्ये महाबाहुः
शुशुभे राक्षसेश्वरः ।
गोष्ठे महति मुख्यानाम्
गवां मध्ये यथा वृषः ॥
The lord of Rākshasas of mighty arm shone in their midst like a bull among cows in a huge cowshed.
5.11.9
स राक्षसेन्द्रः शुशुभे
ताभिः परिवृतस्स्वयम् ।
करेणुभिर्यथाऽरण्ये
परिकीर्णो महाद्विपः ॥
Surrounded by those women, the lord of Rākshasas shone like a great tusker surrounded by she-elephants in a forest.
5.11.10
सर्वकामैरुपेतां च
पानभूमिं महात्मनः ।
ददर्श हरिशार्दूलः
तस्य रक्षःपतेर्गृहे ॥
The tiger among Vānaras saw, in that house of the mighty Rāvaṇa, the drinking hall furnished with every kind of luxury.
5.11.11
मृगाणां महिषाणां च
वराहाणां च भागशः ।
तत्र न्यस्तानि मांसानि
पानभूमौ ददर्श सः ॥
He saw meats of deer, buffalo and boar arranged neatly and separately in that banquet hall.
5.11.12
रौक्मेषु च विशालेषु
भाजनेष्वर्धभक्षितान् ।
ददर्श हरिशार्दूलो
मयूरान् कुक्कुटांस्तथा ॥
The tiger among Vānaras saw leftover meats of peacock and chicken in wide golden platters.
5.11.13
वराहवार्ध्राणसकान्
दधिसौवर्चलायुतान् ।
शल्यान् मृगमयूरांश्च
हनुमानन्ववैक्षत ॥
Hanumān also noticed the meats of boar, lamb, porcupine, deer and peacock seasoned with curds and Suvarcala salt.
5.11.14
क्रकरान्विविधान् सिद्धान्
चकोरानर्धभक्षितान् ।
महिषानेकशल्यांश्च
छागांश्च कृतनिष्ठितान् ।
लेह्यानुच्चावचान्पेयान्
भोज्यानि विविधानि च ॥
He saw many leftover meats of Krakara bird, Cakōra bird, wild buffalo, Ēkaṡalya fish and sheep well prepared and kept ready; and many other kinds of foods that were a joy to lick and chew.
5.11.16
तथाऽऽम्ललवणोत्तंसैः
विविधैरागषाडबैः ।
हारनूपुरकेयूरैः
अपविद्धैर्महाधनैः ।
पानभाजनविक्षिप्तैः
फलैश्च विविधैरपि ।
कृतपुष्पोपहारा भूः
अधिकं पुष्यति श्रियम् ॥
The area was full of opulence, evident
from the various foods, some with a tang of salt, others with a touch of sour taste and yet others a mix of all six tastes,
from the priceless necklaces, anklets and armlets scattered everywhere,
from the profusion of pieces of fruit thrown in the drinking cups, and from the floors decorated with flowers.
5.11.18
तत्र तत्र च विन्यस्तैः
सुश्लिष्टैश्शयनासनैः ।
पानभूमिर्विना वह्निम्
प्रदीप्तेवोपलक्ष्यते ॥
That drinking hall, with beds and couches neatly arranged everywhere seemed to dazzle without the need for a fire.
5.11.19
बहुप्रकारैर्विविधैः
वरसंस्कारसंस्कृतैः ।
मांसैः कुशलसम्पृक्तैः
पानभूमिगतैः पृथक् ॥
That hall (was glowing) with many kinds of meats treated with the choicest of seasonings, each arranged separately and artistically.
5.11.20
दिव्याः प्रसन्ना विविधाः
सुराः कृतसुरा अपि ।
शर्कराऽसवमाध्वीक
पुष्पासवफलासवाः ।
वासचूर्णैश्च विविधैः
मृष्टास्तैस्तैः पृथक् पृथक् ॥
That area shone with many clear, divine drinks, including artificial ones, some made from sugar, some from molasses, some from honey and others from flowers and fruits, each one specially treated with appropriate aromatic spices.
5.11.21
सन्तता शुशुभे भूमिः
माल्यैश्च बहुसंस्थितैः ।
हिरण्मयैश्च विविधैः
भाजनैः स्फाटिकैरपि ।
जाम्बूनदमयैश्चान्यैः
करकैरभिसंवृता ॥
That area shone with many garlands decorating the floor, many gold and crystal pitchers and many other small golden cups everywhere.
5.11.23
राजतेषु च कुम्भेषु
जाम्बूनदमयेषु च ।
पानश्रेष्ठं तदा भूरि
कपिस्तत्र ददर्श ह ॥
The Vānara saw plentiful liquor of the best class filled in jars of gold and silver.
5.11.24
सोऽपश्यच्छातकुम्भानि
शीधोर्मणिमयानि च ।
राजतानि च पूर्णानि
भाजनानि महाकपिः ॥
The great Vānara saw wine jars made of gold and encrusted with gems, and also jars made of silver, filled to the brim.
5.11.25
क्वचिदल्पावशेषाणि
क्वचित्पीतानि सर्वशः ।
क्वचिन्नैव प्रपीतानि
पानानि स ददर्श ह ॥
He saw at some places, jars with a little bit (of liquor) left in them, at others, jars that were completely drained and at yet others, jars left completely untouched.
5.11.26
क्वचिद् भक्ष्यांश्च विविधान्
क्वचित्पानानि भागशः ।
क्वचिदन्नावशेषाणि
पश्यन्वै विचचार ह ॥
As he went around, he saw various foods at some places, various drinks at other places and leftovers at yet others.
5.11.27
क्वचित्प्रभिन्नैः करकैः
क्वचिदालोलितैर्घटैः ।
क्वचित्संपृक्तमाल्यानि
जलानि च फलानि च ॥
At some places, he saw broken cups strewn over, at others, overturned pots and at yet others, flowers, fruits and water all mixed up.
5.11.28
शयनान्यत्र नारीणाम्
शुभ्राणि बहुधा पुनः ।
परस्परं समाश्लिष्य
काश्चित्सुप्ता वराङ्गनाः ॥
The various beds of the women there were clean and beautiful on which those best of women slept hugging each other.
5.11.29
काश्चिच्च वस्त्रमन्यस्याः
स्वपन्त्याः परिधाय च ।
आहृत्य चाबलाः सुप्ता
निद्राबलपराजिताः ॥
Vanquished by the power of sleep, some of the women slept covering themselves with the clothes they pulled off others.
5.11.30
तासामुच्छ्वासवातेन
वस्त्रं माल्यं च गात्रजम् ।
नात्यर्थं स्पन्दते चित्रम्
प्राप्य मन्दमिवानिलम् ॥
The clothes and garlands on their bodies fluttered elegantly under the breath of those women, as if they were swayed by a gentle breeze.
5.11.31
चन्दनस्य च शीतस्य
शीधोर्मधुरसस्य च ।
विविधस्य च माल्यस्य
धूपस्य विविधस्य च ।
बहुधा मारुतस्तत्र
गन्धं विविधमुद्वहन् ॥
The breeze carried various scents with it, from the fragrance of cool sandal to the smells of the sweet liquor, to the fragrance of various garlands, to that from vapors of many kinds of incense.
5.11.32
स्नानानां चन्दनानां च
धूपानां चैव मूर्छितः ।
प्रववौ सुरभिर्गन्धो
विमाने पुष्पके तदा ॥
The wind wafting through that Vimāna, Pushpaka, filled it with the splendid scents of sandal used for bathing and of vapors of incense.
5.11.33
श्यामावदातास्तत्रान्याः
काश्चित्कृष्णा वराङ्गनाः ।
काश्चित् काञ्चनवर्णाङ्ग्यः
प्रमदा राक्षसालये ॥
Some of the women in that abode of Rākshasas were of brown complexion, some were lovely in their darker shade and others had limbs shining like gold.
5.11.34
तासां निद्रावशत्वाच्च
मदनेन विमूर्छितम् ।
पद्मिनीनां प्रसुप्तानाम्
रूपमासीद्यथैव हि ॥
Caving in to the power of sleep and exhausted by playing love sports, they looked like lotuses with petals closed.
5.11.35
एवं सर्वमशेषेण
रावणान्तः पुरं कपिः ।
ददर्श सुमहातेजाः
न ददर्श च जानकीम् ॥
Thus the supremely powerful Vānara checked every part of the inner quarters of Rāvaṇa, but could not find Jānaki.
5.11.36
निरीक्षमाणश्च तदा
ताः स्त्रियः स महाकपिः ।
जगाम महतीं चिन्ताम्
धर्मसाध्वसशङ्कितः ॥
Suddenly it occurred to the great Vānara that he might have transgressed Dharma by looking at those women up close. He felt extremely sad about it:
5.11.37
परदारावरोधस्य
प्रसुप्तस्य निरीक्षणम् ।
इदं खलु ममात्यर्थम्
धर्मलोपं करिष्यति ॥
Looking at another man’s wives like this, while they are sleeping, would certainly count as blatant transgression of Dharma.
5.11.38
न हि मे परदाराणाम्
दृष्टिर्विषयवर्तिनी ।
अयं चात्र मया दृष्टः
परदारापरिग्रहः ॥
True, my mind never indulges in the matters of another man’s wives; but, nevertheless, I happened to see the wives of another man here.
5.11.39
तस्य प्रादुरभूच्चिन्ता
पुनरन्या मनस्विनः ।
निश्चितैकान्तचित्तस्य
कार्यनिश्चयदर्शिनी ॥
But Hanumān was earnest and knew how to focus single pointedly on any object of pursuit. A different thought occurred to him, helping him decide what was to be done next.
5.11.40
कामं दृष्टा मया सर्वा
विश्वस्ता रावणस्त्रियः ।
न हि मे मनसः किञ्चित्
वैकृत्यमुपपद्यते ॥
True, all these unsuspecting women of Rāvaṇa were seen by me, but it did not engender any improper feelings in my mind.
5.11.41
मनो हि हेतुः सर्वेषाम्
इन्द्रियाणां प्रवर्तने ।
शुभाशुभास्ववस्थासु
तच्च मे सुव्यवस्थितम् ॥
It is the (vagaries of) mind that lead the senses to indulge in good as well as bad acts; my mind, (fortunately), is pretty stable.
5.11.42
नान्यत्र हि मया शक्या
वैदेही परिमार्गितुम् ।
स्त्रियो हि स्त्रीषु दृश्यन्ते
सदा सम्परिमार्गणे ॥
I cannot possibly search for Vaidēhee elsewhere; for, at anytime, women can be searched for among women only!
5.11.43
यस्य सत्त्वस्य या योनिः
तस्यां तत्परिमार्ग्यते ।
न शक्या प्रमदा नष्टा
मृगीषु परिमार्गितुम् ॥
(For that matter), any creature can be searched for among its kind only; A woman who is lost cannot be searched for among deer!
5.11.44
तदिदं मार्गितं तावत्
शुद्धेन मनसा मया ।
रावणान्तःपुरं सर्वम्
दृश्यते न तु जानकी ॥
It is with pure and clear intention that I searched all over the inner quarters of Rāvaṇa; but there is no sight of Jānaki.
5.11.45
देवगन्धर्वकन्याश्च
नागकन्याश्च वीर्यवान् ।
अवेक्षमाणो हनुमान्
नैवापश्यत जानकीम् ॥
Thus the valiant Hanumān looked among women (born) of Dēvas, Gandharvas and Nāgas, but could not find Jānaki.
5.11.46
तामपश्यन्कपिस्तत्र
पश्यञ्श्चान्या वरस्त्रियः ।
अपक्रम्य तदा वीरः
प्रध्यातुमुपचक्रमे ॥
The valiant Vānara, not finding her there, and seeing only other beautiful women, moved aside and started to ponder.
5.11.47
स भूयस्तु परं श्रीमान्
मारुतिर्यत्नमास्थितः ।
आपानभूमिमुत्सृज्य
तद्विचेतुं प्रचक्रमे ॥
The blessed son of Vāyu left that drinking hall and renewed his search elsewhere with unfailing zest.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे एकादशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eleventh Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
