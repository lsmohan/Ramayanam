Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 65
In this Sarga, Hanumān tells Rāma what he has seen of Seetā, how she was confined in the inner quarters of Rāvaṇa and how she was being threatened by the Rākshasis. Hanumān gives Rāma the Cūḍāmaṇi that Seetā gave him and tells the story of the crow she told him. He also conveys Seetā’s message that she cannot live any more than a month, confined by the Rākshasas.
5.65.1
ततः प्रस्रवणं शैलम्
ते गत्वा चित्रकाननम् ।
प्रणम्य शिरसा रामम्
लक्ष्मणं च महाबलम् ।
युवराजं पुरस्कृत्य
सुग्रीवमभिवाद्य च ।
प्रवृत्तिमथ सीतायाः
प्रवक्तुमुपचक्रमुः ॥
Reaching the Prasravaṇa mountain that was full of a variety of trees, saluting Rāma and the mighty strong Lakshmaṇa with their heads bowed and saluting Sugreeva also, the Vānaras, led by the crown prince, began to relate the news about Seetā.
5.65.3
रावणान्तः पुरे रोधम्
राक्षसीभिश्च तर्जनम् ।
रामे समनुरागं च
यश्चायं समयः कृतः ।
एतदाख्यान्ति ते सर्वे
हरयो रामसन्निधौ ॥
In the presence of Rāma, all the Vānaras narrated about Seetā being confined in Rāvaṇa’s inner quarters, the threatening by the Rākshasis, the deep love (of Seetā) for Rāma and the time limit (set by Rāvaṇa).
5.65.4
वैदेहीमक्षतां श्रुत्वा
रामस्तूत्तरमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing that Vaidēhee was safe and unhurt, Rāma asked:
5.65.5
क्व सीता वर्तते देवी
कथं च मयि वर्तते ।
एतन्मे सर्वमाख्यात
वैदेहीं प्रति वानराः ॥
O Vānaras! Where is Seetā, my queen? How does she hold me in her thoughts? Please tell me everything in detail about Vaidēhee!
5.65.6
रामस्य गदितं श्रुत्वा
हरयो रामसन्निधौ ।
चोदयन्ति हनूमन्तम्
सीतावृत्तान्तकोविदम् ॥
Hearing what Rāma said, the Vānaras urged Hanumān who knew well how Seetā was doing, (to speak) in the presence of Rāma.
5.65.7
श्रुत्वा तु वचनं तेषाम्
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ।
प्रणम्य शिरसा देव्यै
सीतायै तां दिशं प्रति ।
उवाच वाक्यं वाक्यज्ञः
सीताया दर्शनं यथा ॥
Hearing their words, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, turned in the direction in which the lady Seetā was and prostrated to her, with his head bowed. Then he, who was skilled with words, narrated how Seetā was found:
5.65.8
समुद्रं लङ्घयित्वाहम्
शतयोजनमायतम् ।
अगच्छं जानकीं सीताम्
मार्गमाणो दिदृक्षया ॥
Eager to find Seetā, the daughter of Janaka, I went searching, crossing the ocean that was one hundred Yōjanas wide.
5.65.9
तत्र लङ्केति नगरी
रावणस्य दुरात्मनः ।
दक्षिणस्य समुद्रस्य
तीरे वसति दक्षिणे ॥
There, on that southern shore of the southern sea, is situated Laṅkā, the city of evil Rāvaṇa.
5.65.10
तत्र दृष्टा मया सीता
रावणान्तः पुरे सती ।
सन्न्यस्य त्वयि जीवन्ती
रामा राम मनोरथम् ॥
There, I have seen, O Rāma, the chaste and lovely lady Seetā, in the inner quarters of Rāvaṇa, hanging on to life, pinning all her hopes on you.
5.65.11
दृष्टा मे राक्षसीमध्ये
तर्ज्यमाना मुहुर्मुहुः ।
राक्षसीभिर्विरूपाभी
रक्षिता प्रमदावने ॥
I saw her in the women’s pleasance in the midst of Rākshasis, who were surrounding her, guarding her and threatening her repeatedly.
5.65.12
दुःख मासाद्यते देवी
तथाऽदुःखोचिता सती ।
रावणान्तः पुरे रुद्धा
राक्षसीभिस्सुरक्षिता ।
एकवेणीधरा दीना
त्वयि चिन्तापरायणा ।
अधःशय्या विवर्णाङ्गी
पद्मिनीव हिमागमे ।
रावणाद्विनिवृत्तार्था
मर्तव्यकृतनिश्चया ।
देवी कथञ्चित्काकुत्स्थ
त्वन्मना मार्गिता मया ॥
That lady, who deserves no suffering, is grieving confined in the inner quarters of Rāvaṇa, carefully guarded by the Rākshasis.
Dejected, her hair braided in a single plait, thinking only about you, lying down on the bare ground, with every limb of hers colorless, O Kākutstha, she is wilting like lotus wine on the onset of winter.
Somehow, O Veera, I could locate her, who is all but determined to give up her life, turning away from the advances of Rāvaṇa.
5.65.15
इक्ष्वाकुवंशविख्यातिम्
शनैः कीर्तयतानघ ।
सा मया नरशार्दूल
विश्वासमुपपादिता ॥
O sinless one, O tiger among men! I could, slowly and carefully, gain her trust, by singing praises of the lineage of Ikshwākus.
5.65.16
ततस्सम्भाषिता देवी
सर्वमर्थं च दर्शिता ।
रामसुग्रीवसख्यं च
श्रुत्वा प्रीतिमुपागता ।
नियतस्समुदाचारो
भक्तिश्चास्यास्तथा त्वयि ॥
Then, knowing about the entire mission, and the alliance between Rāma and Sugreeva, the Dēvi, who is fully devoted to you and observes best practices, was happy.
5.65.18
एवं मया महाभागा
दृष्टा जनकनन्दिनी ।
उग्रेण तपसा युक्ता
त्वद्भक्त्या पुरुषर्षभ ॥
That is how I found the blessed lady, the delight of Janaka, who is given to rigorous Tapa, and who is completely devoted to you, O bull among men!
5.65.19
अभिज्ञानं च मे दत्तम्
यथा वृत्तं तवान्तिके ।
चित्रकूटे महाप्राज्ञ
वायसं प्रति राघव ॥
As a token of confirmation (that I met her) she told me the incident involving a crow that happened in your presence at Citrakūṭa, O sagacious Rāghava!
5.65.20
विज्ञाप्यश्च नरव्याघ्रो
रामो वायुसुत त्वया ।
अखिलेनेह यद्धृष्टम्
इति मामाह जानकी ॥
“Rāma, the tiger among men should be told everything as has been seen here, O son of Vāyu!”, said Jānaki to me.
5.65.21
अयं चास्मै प्रदातव्यो
यत्नात्सुपरिरक्षितः ।
ब्रुवता वचनान्येवम्
सुग्रीवस्योपशृण्वतः ॥
“Please tell all of this while Sugreeva is listening. And also, give this (jewel), which I have managed to keep safely.”
5.65.22
एष चूडामणिश्श्रीमान्
मया सुपरिरक्षितः ।
मनश्शिलायास्तिलको
गण्डपार्श्वे निवेशितः ।
त्वया प्रणष्ठे तिलके
तं किल स्मर्तुमर्हसि ॥
“I saved this splendid Cūḍāmaṇi carefully. You should remind him how once he put the Tilaka of Manaṡṡila on my cheeks, while it was the Tilaka on my forehead that was erased!”
5.65.23
एष निर्यातितश्श्रीमान्
मया ते वारि सम्भवः ।
एतं दृष्ट्वा प्रहृष्यामि
व्यसने त्वामिवानघ ॥
“I send to you this splendid one, which is born out of water, seeing which is like seeing you, O sinless one! It cheers me up when I feel distressed.”
5.65.24
जीवितं धारयिष्यामि
मासं दशरथात्मज ।
ऊर्ध्वं मासान्न जीवेयम्
रक्षसां वशमागता ॥
O son of Daṡaratha! I can hold on to this life, at most, for a month! Being under the control of the Rākshasas, I will not live for any more than a month.
5.65.25
इति मामब्रवीत्सीता
कृशाङ्गी धर्मचारिणी ।
रावणान्तः पुरे रुद्धा
मृगीवोत्फुल्ललोचना ॥
This is what Seetā, an adherent of Dharma, who was confined in the inner quarters of Rāvaṇa, all her limbs worn out except her eyes that were wide like doe’s, said.
5.65.26
एतदेव मयाख्यातम्
सर्वं राघव यद्यथा ।
सर्वथा सागरजले
संतारः प्रविधीयताम् ॥
I have told everything, O Rāghava, as it happened. Arrangements shall be made now to cross the ocean!
5.65.27
तौ जाताश्वासौ राजपुत्रौ विदित्वा
तच्चाभिज्ञानं राघवाय प्रदाय ।
देव्या चाख्यातं सर्वमेवानुपूर्व्यात्
वाचा सम्पूर्णं वायुपुत्त्र श्शशंस ॥
Noting that the princes were gaining trust (in what he said), the son of Vāyu went on to give Rāghava the token and relayed to him in due order all that the lady had told him.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे पञ्चषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty fifth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
