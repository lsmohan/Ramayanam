Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 96
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa plows through the Vānara army, wreaking havoc and then reaches the place where Rāma is. Meanwhile, Sugreeva, seeing the Vānaras flee, puts Sushēṇa in charge of the Vānara army, and jumps into the fray himself. He harrows the Rākshasas, sending a downpour of boulders, seeing which Virūpāksha mounts a counterattack on him. A fierce duel ensues between Sugreeva and Virūpāksha, which ends with Sugreeva knocking down Virūpāksha. On seeing Virūpāksha dead, both the armies go into a frenzy and fight like two oceans colliding with each other.
6.96.1
तथा तैः कृत्तगात्रैस्तु
दशग्रीवेण मार्गणैः ।
बभूव वसुधा तत्र
प्रकीर्णा हरिभिस्तदा ॥
Then the earth was littered with the Vānaras whose bodies were torn by the arrows of the Ten-necked.
6.96.2
रावणस्याप्रसह्यं तम्
शरसम्पातमेकतः ।
न शेकुः सहितुं दीप्तम्
पतङ्गा ज्वलनं यथा ॥
They could not bear, any more than moths can bear the flame, the torrent of unbearable arrows discharged by Rāvaṇa singlehandedly.
6.96.3
तेऽर्दिता निशितैर्बाणैः
क्रोशन्तो विप्रदुद्रुवुः ।
पावकार्चिस्समाविष्टा
दह्यमाना यथा गजाः ॥
Oppressed by the sharp arrows, they fled, wailing like elephants burning, engulfed in the flames of wildfires.
6.96.4
प्लवङ्गानामनीकानि
महाभ्राणीव मारुतः ।
स ययौ समरे तस्मिन्
विधमन् रावणः शरैः ॥
Rāvaṇa plowed through the Vānara forces in that fight blowing them away like the wind does huge clouds.
6.96.5
कदनं तरसा कृत्वा
राक्षसेन्द्रो वनौकसाम् ।
आससाद ततो युद्धे
राघवं त्वरितस्तदा ॥
Having, with all his might, wreaked havoc among the Vānaras in that fight, the king of the Rākshasas arrived where Rāghava was, with all speed.
6.96.6
सुग्रीवस्तान् कपीन् दृष्ट्वा
भग्नान् विद्रवतो रणे ।
गुल्मे सुषेणं निक्षिप्य
चक्रे युद्धेऽद्भुतं मनः ॥
Sugreeva, seeing the Vānaras broken and fleeing, put Sushēṇa in charge of the army divisions and resolved on putting up a marvelous fight.
6.96.7
आत्मनः सदृशं वीरः
स तं निक्षिप्य वानरम् ।
सुग्रीवोऽभिमुखः शत्रुम्
प्रतस्थे पादपायुधः ॥
Leaving in his place that Vānara, who was his equal, Sugreeva, armed with a tree, proceeded towards the foe.
6.96.8
पार्श्वतः पृष्टतश्चास्य
सर्वे वानर यूथपाः ।
अनुजह्रुर्महाशैलान्
विविधांश्च वनस्पतीन् ॥
All the army chiefs followed on his sides and behind him, armed with huge boulders and different kinds of trees.
6.96.9
ननर्द युधि सुग्रीवः
स्वरेण महता महान् ।
सोथयन् विविधांश्चान्यान्
ममनथोत्तमराक्षसान् ॥
Sugreeva fought, letting off mighty shouts with his mighty voice, crushing the pick of the Rākshasas and pounding the others, of every kind.
6.96.10
ममन्थ च महाकायो
राक्षसान् वानरेश्वरः ।
युगान्तसमये वायुः
प्रवृद्धानगमानिव ॥
The Lord of Vānaras of huge body battered the Rākshasas, like the wind at the end of the Yuga does the fully grown trees.
6.96.11
राक्षसानामनीकेषु
शैलवर्षं ववर्ष ह ।
अश्मवर्षं यथा मेघः
पक्षिसङ्घेषु कानने ॥
He showered a huge downpour of rocks on the Rākshasa forces, like clouds sending a hail storm on flocks of birds in the forest.
6.96.12
कपिराजविमुक्तैस्तैः
शैलवर्षैस्तु राक्षसाः ।
विकीर्णशिरसः पेतुः
निकृत्ता इव पर्वताः ॥
Their heads broken by that shower of rocks sent down by the king of Vānaras, the Rākshasas fell down like mountains that were cut off (by Vajra).
6.96.13
अथ सङ्क्षीयमाणेषु
राक्षसेषु समन्ततः ।
सुग्रीवेण प्रभग्नेषु
पतत्सु निनदत्सु च ।
विरूपाक्षः स्वकं नाम
धन्वी विश्राव्य राक्षसः ।
रथादाप्लुत्य दुर्धर्षो
गजस्कन्धमुपारुहत् ॥
As the Rākshasas were thus being decimated, falling all around broken and wailing, the invincible Virūpāksha, armed with a bow and loudly vaunting himself, sprang down from his chariot and got up on the back of an elephant.
6.96.15
स तं द्विपदमारुह्य
विरूपाक्षो महाबलः ।
ननर्द भीमनिर्ह्रादम्
वानरानभ्यधावत ॥
Riding on that elephant, Virūpāksha of extraordinary strength rushed towards the Vānaras letting off a frightful shout.
6.96.16
सुग्रीवे स शरान् घोरान्
विससर्ज चमूमुखे ।
स्थापयामास चोद्विग्नान्
राक्षसान् सम्प्रहर्षयन् ॥
He then shot dreadful arrows at Sugreeva in that battlefront, steadying the agitated Rākshasas and bringing cheer to them.
6.96.17
सोऽतिविद्धः शितैर्बाणैः
कपीन्द्रस्तेन रक्षसा ।
चुक्रोश च महाक्रोधो
वधे चास्य मनो दधे ॥
Severely battered by the sharp arrows of the Rākshasa, the Lord of Vānaras became extremely enraged and set his mind completely on slaying him.
6.96.18
ततः पादपमुद्धृत्य
शूरः सम्प्रधनो हरिः ।
अभिपत्य जघानास्य
प्रमुखे तु महागजम् ॥
Then the heroic Vānara, a great fighter, uprooted a tree and getting close to him, struck the huge tusker on the face with it.
6.96.19
स तु प्रहाराभिहतः
सुग्रीवेण महागजः ।
अपासर्पद्धनुर्मात्रम्
निषसाद ननाद च ॥
Thus struck by Sugreeva, that mighty elephant slid back a bow’s length and subsiding on its haunches, trumpeted in pain.
6.96.20
गजात्तु मथितात्तूर्णम्
अपक्रम्य स वीर्यवान् ।
राक्षसोऽभिमुखः शत्रुम्
प्रत्युद्गम्य ततः कपिम् ।
आर्षभं चर्म खड्गं च
प्रगृह्य लघुविक्रमः ।
भर्त्सयन्निव सुग्रीवम्
आससाद व्यवस्थितम् ॥
The valiant Rākshasa quickly moved away from that elephant that was battered. Then, with a sword and a buckler made of bull's hide, he, whose maneuvers were swift, went right up to the Vānara Sugreeva who stood his ground resolutely, as if he were to intimidate him.
6.96.22
स हि तस्याभिसङ्क्रुद्धः
प्रगृह्य विपुलां शिलाम् ।
विरूपाक्षाय चिक्षेप
सुग्रीवो जलदोपमाम् ॥
Enraged at him, Sugreeva snatched a boulder that was huge like a cloud and hurled it at Virūpāksha.
6.96.23
स तां शिलामापतन्तीम्
दृष्ट्वा राक्षसपुङ्गवः ।
अपक्रम्य सुविक्रान्तः
खड्गेन प्राहरत्तदा ॥
Seeing that boulder fly towards him, that bull among Rākshasas moved aside in a swift maneuver and hit him with his sword.
6.96.24
तेन खड्गप्रहारेण
रक्षसा बलिना हतः ।
मुहूर्तमभवद्वीरो
विसञ्ज्ञ इव वानरः ॥
Struck by that blow from the sword of that powerful Rākshasa, the valiant Vānara was in shock for a while.
6.96.25
स तदा सहसोत्पत्य
राक्षसस्य महाहवे ।
मुष्टिं संवर्त्य वेगेन
पातयामास वक्षसि ॥
But then he sprang up immediately, doubled his fist and delivered a blow on the chest of that Rākshasa with great momentum, in that fierce battle.
6.96.26
मुष्टिप्रहाराभिहतो
विरूपाक्षो निशाचरः ।
तेन खड्गेन सङ्क्रुद्धः
सुग्रीवस्य चमूमुखे ।
कवचं पातयामास
पद्भ्यामभिहतोऽपतत् ॥
Struck with that blow of the fist, Virūpāksha, the Night-rover became enraged. He then, with his sword, tore apart the armor of Sugreeva. Thus struck, he fell, his feet losing balance.
6.96.27
स समुत्थाय पतितः
कपिस्तस्य व्यसर्जयत् ।
तलप्रहारमशनेः
समानं भीमनिस्वनम् ॥
The Vānara who fell down thus, got up again and delivered a blow with his open palm that fell like a thunderbolt and with the frightening din of thunder.
6.96.28
तलप्रहारं तद्रक्षः
सुग्रीवेण समुद्यतम् ।
नैपुण्यान्मोचयित्वैनम्
मुष्टिनोरस्यताडयत् ॥
The Rākshasa skillfully escaped the force of that blow from the raised fist of Sugreeva and struck him (back) on the chest with his fist.
6.96.29
ततस्तु सङ्क्रुद्धतरः
सुग्रीवो वानरेश्वरः ।
मोक्षितं चात्मनो दृष्ट्वा
प्रहारं तेन रक्षसा ॥
Then Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras, became even more outraged, seeing the the blow delivered by him dodged by the Rākshasa.
6.96.30
स ददर्शान्तरं तस्य
विरूपाक्षस्य वानरः ।
ततो न्यपातयत् क्रोधात्
शङ्खदेशे महत्तलम् ॥
He quickly saw another opportunity, and in that rage, delivered a mighty slap on the eyebrows.
6.96.31
महेन्द्राशनिकल्पेन
तलेनाभिहतः क्षितौ ।
पपात रुधिरक्लिन्नः
शोणितं च समुद्गिरन् ।
स्रोतोभ्यस्तु विरूपाक्षो
जलं प्रस्रवणादिव ॥
Struck by that blow of the palm, which fell like the thunderbolt of Mahēndra, Virūpāksha fell down on the ground drenched in blood and also with blood pouring out through all the orifices like water from a cascade.
6.96.33
विवृत्तनयनं क्रोधात्
सफेनं रुधिराप्लुतम् ।
ददृशुस्ते विरूपाक्षम्
विरूपाक्षतरं कृतम् ॥
Folks found Virūpāksha, whose eyes were swirling because he was in such a rage and whose body was drenched in blood and its froth, to be even more cross-eyed than he already was.
6.96.34
स्फुरन्तं परिवर्तन्तम्
पार्श्वेन रुधिरोक्षितम् ।
करुणं च विनर्दन्तम्
ददृशुः कपयो रिपुम् ॥
The Vānaras found their foe writhing and rolling on his sides, drenched in blood and crying piteously.
6.96.35
तथा तु तौ संयति सम्प्रयुक्तौ
तरस्विनौ वानरराक्षसानाम् ।
बलार्णवौ सस्वनतुश्च भीमौ
महार्णवौ द्वाविव भिन्नवेलौ ॥
Then the two armies of the powerful Vānaras and the Rākshasas, vast like oceans, collided in that fight like two great oceans bursting through their bounds and clashing, creating a frightful din.
6.96.36
विनाशितं प्रेक्ष्य विरूपनेत्रं
महाबलं तं हरिपार्थिवेन ।
बलं समस्तं कपिराक्षसानाम
उन्मत्तगङ्गाप्रतिमं बभूव ॥
Seeing that crooked-eyed one of immense strength destroyed by the king of the Vānaras, the entire forces of the Vānaras and the Rākshasas went into a frenzy like the river Gaṅgā (as it escaped from the locks of lord Ṡiva).
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे षण्णवतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninety sixth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
