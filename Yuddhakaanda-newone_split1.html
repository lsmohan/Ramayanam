Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 1
In this first Sarga of Yuddha Kāṇḍa, Rāma praises Hanumān for accomplishing the mission and finding Seetā. He expresses his gratitude by giving Hanumān an embrace with all his heart and soul. He then begins to ponder about the next step, as to how the Vānaras can cross the ocean.
6.1.1
श्रुत्वा हनुमतो वाक्यम्
यथावदभिभाषितम् ।
रामः प्रीतिसमायुक्तो
वाक्यमुत्तरमब्रवीत् ॥
On hearing those words of Hanumān, which related everything as it was, Rāma was pleased and responded saying:
6.1.2
कृतं हनुमता कार्यम्
सुमहद्भुवि दुर्लभम् ।
मनसाऽपि यदन्येन
न शक्यं धरणीतले ॥
Hanumān has accomplished a great mission, which is next to impossible in this world; others, from anywhere on the face of the earth, cannot even imagine doing something like this.
6.1.3
न हि तं परिपश्यामि
यस्तरेत महार्णवम् ।
अन्यत्र गरुडाद्वायोः
अन्यत्र च हनूमतः ॥
I cannot conceive of any one crossing the great ocean, except Garuḍa and the God of Wind and of course, other than Hanumān.
6.1.4
देवदानवयक्षाणाम्
गन्धर्वोरगरक्षसाम् ।
अप्रधृष्यां पुरीं लङ्काम्
रावणेन सुरक्षिताम् ।
प्रविष्टः सत्त्वमाश्रित्य
जीवन् को नाम निष्क्रमेत् ॥
Who that entered, relying on nothing but one’s own physical strength, the city of Laṅkā that is well protected by Rāvaṇa and is impenetrable even to Dēvas, Dānavas, Yakshas, Gandharvas, Uragas and the Rākshasas, could hope to return alive?
6.1.5
को विशेत् सुदुराधर्षाम्
राक्षसैश्च सुरक्षिताम् ।
यो वीर्यबलसम्पन्नः
न समः स्याद्धनूमतः ॥
And who, however strong and valiant he might be, could even penetrate into that formidable city, which is so jealously guarded by the Rākshasas, unless he is an equal to Hanumān?
6.1.6
भृत्यकार्यं हनुमता
सुग्रीवस्य कृतं महत् ।
एवं विधाय स्वबलम्
सदृशं विक्रमस्य च ॥
Deploying his own strength, and equally his prowess, Hanumān accomplished the great mission that his master Sugreeva had set him on.
6.1.7
यो हि भृत्यो नियुक्तस्सन्
भर्त्रा कर्मणि दुष्करे ।
कुर्यात्तदनुरागेण
तमाहुः पुरुषोत्तमम् ॥
One who, assigned to a task that is next to impossible to accomplish, by his master, does it with zeal and interest, is said to be the best among the spirited beings.
More or less, the same metric is used for annual performance reviews of the employees, in these modern times also. We might, now a days, call them something like: 1) exceeds expectations, 2) meets expectations and 3) does not meet expectations.
6.1.8
यो नियिक्तो परं कार्यम्
न कुर्यान्नृपतेः प्रियम् ।
भृत्यो युक्तः समर्थश्च
तमाहुर्मध्यमं नरम् ॥
One, who is capable and intelligent, that does not stretch himself to do even a bit more than what was asked for, and please the king, is said to be a mediocre man.
6.1.9
नियुक्तो नृपतेः कार्यम्
न कुर्याद्यः समाहितः ।
भृत्यो युक्तः समर्थश्च
तमाहुः पुरुषाधमम् ॥
One who is capable and intelligent, but does not pursue the assigned task with full attention, is said to be the worst of the men.
6.1.10
तन्नियोगे नियुक्तेन
कृतं कृत्यं हनूमता ।
न चात्मा लघुतां नीतः
सुग्रीवश्चापि तोषितः ॥
Hanumān has completed the task that he was assigned to, without falling short in any way, making Sugreeva also very pleased.
6.1.11
अहं च रघुवंशश्च
लक्ष्मणश्च महाबलः ।
वैदेह्या दर्शनेनाद्य
धर्मतः परिरक्षिताः ॥
Discharging his responsibility and finding Seetā, he saved me, the race of Raghus and Lakshmaṇa of the immense strength.
6.1.12
इदं तु मम दीनस्य
मनो भूयः प्रकर्षति ।
यदिहास्य प्रियाख्यातुः
न कुर्मि सदृशं प्रियम् ॥
I only feel nagged by this sadness in my mind that I could give nothing to him in return that is the like of the pleasing news he gave me.
6.1.13
एष सर्वस्वभूतस्तु
परिष्वङ्गो हनूमतः ।
मया कालमिमं प्राप्य
दत्तस्तस्य महात्मनः ॥
At this time, all I can do is to give my embrace, with all my heart and soul, to Hanumān, the Mahātma.
6.1.14
इत्युक्त्वा प्रीतिहृष्टाङ्गो
रामस्तं परिषस्वजे ।
हनूमन्तं महात्मानम्
कृतकार्यमुपागतम् ॥
Saying this and with every limb of his filled with affection, Rāma clasped to his bosom Hanumān, the Mahātma who returned accomplishing his mission.
6.1.15
ध्यात्वा पुनरुवाचेदम्
वचनं रघुसत्तमः ।
हरीणामीश्वरस्यापि
सुग्रीवस्योपशृण्वतः ॥
Thinking for a while, the best of the Raghus said these words again, while Sugreeva, the sovereign of the Vānaras was listening:
6.1.16
सर्वथा सुकृतं तावत्
सीतायाः परिमार्गणम् ।
सागरं तु समासाद्य
पुनर्नष्टं मनो मम ॥
It is great that the search for Seetā was done well. But, I am at a loss on what to do when we get to the ocean.
6.1.17
कथं नाम समुद्रस्य
दुष्पारस्य महाम्भसः ।
हरयो दक्षिणं पारम्
गमिष्यन्ति समाहिताः ॥
How can all the Vānaras, as a body, reach the southern side of the ocean, of wide waters, that is very difficult to cross?
6.1.18
यद्यप्येष तु वृत्तान्तो
वैदेह्या गदितो मम ।
समुद्रपारगमने
हरीणां किमिवोत्तरम् ॥
True, the news of Vaidēhee are conveyed to me, but what is the answer for the question of how the Vānaras would cross the sea?
6.1.19
इत्युक्त्वा शोकसम्भ्रान्तो
रामः शत्रुनिबर्हणः ।
हनुमन्तं महाबाहुः
ततो ध्यानमुपागमत् ॥
Saying this to Hanumān, Rāma of mighty arm, the destroyer of the enemies, who had been troubled by grief, began to ponder.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे प्रथमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the first Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
