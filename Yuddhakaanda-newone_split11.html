Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 11
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa, all set to discuss a war strategy, goes to the council hall with his entourage. He summons all the Rākshasas of Laṅkā to assemble in that hall immediately. His messengers go around the city and proclaim the order of the king. All of them assemble in the council hall and take seats appropriate for their position and relative importance. Observing perfect order and silence, they fix their eyes on the face of their lord.
6.11.1
स बभूव कृशो राजा
मैथिलीकाममोहितः ।
असम्मानाच्च सुहृदाम्
पापः पापेन कर्मणा ॥
The King, a sinner, was worn out from his sinful pursuit of love of Maithili, and from finding no respect for it from the right-thinking people.
6.11.2
अतीतसमये काले
तस्मिन् वै युधि रावणः ।
अमात्यैश्च सुहृद्धिश्च
प्राप्तकालममन्यत ॥
With time running out and war seeming imminent, Rāvaṇa set his mind, as was appropriate at that time, on conferring with his ministers and well-wishers.
6.11.3
स हेमजालविततम्
मणिविद्रुमभूषितम् ।
उपगम्य विनीताश्वम्
आरुरोह महारथम् ॥
He went to his great chariot that was decorated with gems and coral and covered with golden lacework, and got onto it.
6.11.4
तमास्थाय रथश्रेष्ठम्
महामेघसमस्वनम् ।
प्रययौ राक्षसश्रेष्ठो
दशग्रीवः सभां प्रति ॥
Getting onto that best of chariots that rumbled like a huge thundercloud, the Ten-necked, best of the Rākshasas, went towards the council hall.
6.11.5
असिचर्मधरा योधाः
सर्वायुधधरास्तथा ।
राक्षसा राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
पुरतः सम्प्रतस्थिरे ॥
Warriors armed with swords and wearing shields, and Rākshasas wielding various kinds of weapons marched ahead of him.
6.11.6
नानाविकृतवेषाश्च
नानाभूषणभूषिताः ।
पार्श्वतः पृष्ठतश्चैनम्
परिवार्य ययुस्ततः ॥
Others, dressed in ugly attires and adorning a variety of gem stones, followed him, marching by the side and in the rear.
6.11.7
रथैश्चातिरथाः शीघ्रम्
मत्तैश्च वरवारणैः ।
अनूत्पेतुर्दशग्रीवम्
आक्रीडद्भिश्च वाजिभिः ॥
Super-charioters in their chariots and others riding on splendid tuskers in rut and on prancing horses, quickly followed, close behind the Ten-necked.
6.11.8
गदापरिघहस्ताश्च
शक्तितोमरपाणयः ।
परश्वधधराश्चान्ये
तथाऽन्ये शूलपाणयः ॥
So did others armed with clubs, Parighas, Ṡaktis, Tōmaras, hatchets and spears.
6.11.9
ततस्तूर्यसहस्राणाम्
सञ्जज्ञे निस्वनो महान् ।
तुमुलः शङ्खशब्दश्च
सभां गच्छति रावणे ॥
Accompanied by the din of a thousand Tūryas and the clamor of conch-shells Rāvaṇa went to the council hall.
6.11.10
स नेमिघोषेण महान्
सहसाऽभिविनादयन् ।
राजमार्गं श्रिया जुष्टम्
प्रतिपेदे महारथः ॥
The mighty chariot soon entered the opulent Royal way, with the rumble of its wheels creating a great din.
6.11.11
विमलं चातपत्राणम्
प्रगृहीतमशोभत ।
पाण्डरं राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
पूर्णस्ताराधिपो यथा ॥
The spotless white umbrella held over the head of the Lord of Rākshasas shone like the full moon, the lord of stars.
6.11.12
हेममञ्जरिगर्भे च
शुद्धस्फटिकविग्रहे ।
चामरव्यजने तस्य
रेजतुः सव्यदक्षिणे ॥
Fly-whisks with bristles made from the tail of Camari, handles made of clear crystals and hubs adorned with golden laces, shone to his left and right.
6.11.13
ते कृताञ्जलयः सर्वे
रथस्थं पृथिवीस्थिताः ।
राक्षसा राक्षसश्रेष्ठम्
शिरोभिस्तं ववन्दिरे ॥
All the Rākshasas, standing on the ground, heads bowed and their palms joined in reverence, saluted that foremost of the Rākshasas who was seated on the chariot.
6.11.14
राक्षसैः स्तूयमानः सन्
जयाशीर्भिररिन्दमः ।
आससाद महातेजाः
सभां सुविहितां शुभाम् ॥
With the Rākshasas hailing him and wishing him victory, that supremely powerful subduer of foe reached the splendidly wrought council hall.
6.11.15
सुवर्णरजतस्थूणाम्
विशुद्धस्फटिकान्तराम् ।
विराजमानो वपुषा
रुक्मपट्टोत्तरच्छदाम् ।
तां पिशाचशतैः षड्भिः
अभिगुप्तां सदा शुभाम् ।
प्रविवेश महातेजाः
सुकृतां विश्वकर्मणा ॥
It was designed by Viṡwakarma with care with its pillars made of gold and silver, its central areas wrought in clear crystal and its upholstery woven with threads of gold.
He of immense power, his body glowing, entered (that council hall) that was guarded by six hundred Piṡācas.
6.11.17
तस्यां तु वैडूर्यमयम्
प्रियकाजिनसंवृतम् ।
महत् सोपाश्रयं भेजे
रावणः परमासनम् ॥
Rāvaṇa seated himself on the majestic chair in that hall, which was studded with lazulite, covered with the skin of the Priyaka deer, and furnished with lavish cushions.
6.11.18
ततः शशासेश्वरवद्
दूतान् लघुपराक्रमान् ।
समानयत मे क्षिप्रम्
इहैतान् राक्षसानिति ।
कृत्यमस्ति महज्जातम्
समर्थ्यमिह नो महत् ॥
And then he issued the royal command to swift messengers: “Bring the Rākshasas here immediately; There lies a humongous task that demands all of our capabilities”.
6.11.19
राक्षसास्तद्वचः श्रुत्वा
लङ्कायां परिचक्रमुः ।
अनुगेहमवस्थाय
विहारशयनेषु च ।
उद्यानेषु च रक्षांसि
चोदयन्तो ह्यभीतवत् ॥
Hearing those words, those Rākshasas made a round of Laṅkā, entering every house and fearlessly summoning all the Rākshasas, regardless of whether they were at play or in bed or in the pleasure-gardens.
6.11.21
ते रथान् रुचिरानेके
दृप्तानेके दृढान् हयान् ।
नागानन्येऽधिरुरुहुः
जग्मुश्चैके पदातयः ॥
And they all came, some riding in beautiful chariots, some on proud and sturdy horses, others on elephants, and the rest on foot.
6.11.22
सा पुरी परमाकीर्णा
रथकुञ्जरवाजिभिः ।
सम्पतद्भिर्विरुरुचे
गरुत्मद्भिरिवाम्बरम् ॥
The city, with those crowds of chariots, tuskers, and horses converging, looked like the sky crowded with birds.
6.11.23
ते वाहनान्यवस्थाप्य
यानानि विविधानि च ।
सभां पद्भिः प्रविविशुः
सिंहा गिरिगुहामिव ॥
Parking their various mounts and vehicles outside, they entered the council hall on foot like lions entering a mountain cave.
6.11.24
राज्ञः पादौ गृहीत्वा तु
राज्ञा ते प्रतिपूजिताः ।
पीठेष्वन्ये बृसीष्वन्ये
भूमौ केचिदुपाविशन् ॥
After touching the King’s feet, and suitably welcomed by him, they sat down, some on chairs, some on mats and some on the bare floor.
6.11.25
ते समेत्य सभायां वै
राक्षसा राजशासनात् ।
यथार्हमुपतस्थुस्ते
रावणं राक्षसाधिपम् ॥
Thus assembled in the council hall following the summons of the king, the Rākshasas sat around Rāvaṇa, the Lord of Rākshasas in due order.
6.11.26
मन्त्रिणश्च यथा मुख्या
निश्चयार्थेषु पण्डिताः ।
अमात्याश्च गुणोपेताः
सर्वज्ञा बुद्धिदर्शनाः ।
समेयुस्तत्र शतशः
शूराश्च बहवस्तदा ।
सभायां हेमवार्णायाम्
सर्वार्थस्य सुखाय वै ॥
The counselors who were experts in making decisions on matters of import, the well-qualified ministers who knew everything that was to be known and could envision what future the held, and the heroic warriors in their many hundreds, lined up in that council hall according to their position and importance, so that all matters of order could proceed smoothly.
6.11.28
ततो महात्मा विपुलं सुयुग्यं
वरं रथं हेमविचित्रिताङ्गम् ।
शुभं समास्थाय ययौ यशस्वी
विभीषणः संसदमग्रजस्य ॥
Then, Vibheeshaṇa, a Mahātma of great renown riding on a big and excellent chariot that was yoked with splendid horses and whose parts were all made of gold, arrived at the council hall of his elder brother.
6.11.29
स पूर्वजायावरजः शशंस
नामाथ पश्चाच्चरणौ ववन्दे ।
शुकः प्रहस्तश्च तथैव तेभ्यो
ददौ यथार्हं पृथगासनानि ॥
Then the younger brother prostrated at the feet of his older brother announcing himself by his name. Ṡuka and Prahasta also did the same. And they were shown seats appropriate for them.
6.11.30
सुवर्णनानामणिभूषणानां
सुवाससां संसदि राक्षसानाम् ।
तेषां परार्ध्यागरुचन्दनानां
स्रजश्च गन्धाः प्रववुः समन्तात् ॥
The Rākshasas were decked in gold and a variety of ornaments made of gems. The fragrance of the Agaru and sandal and of the garlands worn by the Rākshasas wafted all over the council hall.
6.11.31
न चुक्रुशुर्नानृतमाह कश्चित्
सभासदो नापि जजल्पुरुच्छैः ।
संसिद्धार्थाः सर्व एवोग्रवीर्या
भर्तुः सर्वे ददृशुश्चाननं ते ॥
No one in that council hall uttered a lie; no one shouted and no one could even be heard chitchatting / whispering. All of them, of immense valor, were content with all their objects long fulfilled. Their eyes were fixed on the face their lord.
6.11.32
सरावणः शस्त्रभृतां मनस्विनां
महाबलानां समितौ मनस्वी ।
तस्यां सभायां प्रभया चकाशे
मध्ये वसूनामिव वज्रहस्तः ॥
That Rāvaṇa of immense self esteem, in the midst of the assembly of those confident, mighty strong wielders of weapons, shone like the Wielder of the Thunderbolt amidst the Vasus.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे एकादशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eleventh Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
