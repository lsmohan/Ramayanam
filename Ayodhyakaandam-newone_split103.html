Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 103
In this Sarga, Rāma collapses hearing the news of his father passing away, from Bharata. The brothers and Seetā gather around him and sprinkle water on him to help him regain his senses.
Rāma bemoans the loss of his father, fondly recalling his father's affection for him. Urged by everyone, he then proceeds to river Mandākini to offer water libations to his father.
Returning from the river to the cottage, he cries bitterly and loudly. His brothers and Seetā also cry along with him. Those wails echoed from the mountain and were heard by Bharata's army.
The army guesses that the brothers must have met each other and must be crying over the demise of their father. They dash, without any delay, in every vehicle and mount and on foot to see Rāma. The animals of the wild get frightened with the movement of that large army and run in all directions.
Everyone sees Rāma and Rāma embraces all of them and receives each one of them fittingly.
2.103.1
तां श्रुत्वा करुणां वाचं
पितुर्मरणसंहिताम् ।
राघवो भरतेनोक्तां
बभूव गतचेतनः ॥
On hearing those moving words spoken by Bharata along with the news of his father's death, Rāghava fell into a swoon.
2.103.2
तं तु वज्रमिवोत्सृष्टम्
आहवे दानवारिणा ।
वाग्वज्रंभरते नोक्तम्
अमनोज्ञं परन्तपः ।
प्रगृह्य रामो बाहू वै
पुष्पिताग्रो यथा द्रुमः ।
वने परशुना कृत्तः
तथा भुवि पपात ह ॥
Those terrible words spoken by Bharata hit Rāma like a verbal thunderbolt, resembling the thunderbolt hurled by the foe of Dānavas (Indra) in a battle.
The scorcher of the foe then fell down on the ground, wringing his hands, like a tree in bloom that is axed in a forest.
2.103.4
तथा निपतितं रामं
जगत्यां जगतीपतिम् ।
कूलघातपरिश्रान्तं
प्रसुप्तमिव कुञ्जरम् ।
भ्रातरस्ते महेष्वासं
सर्वतश्शोक कर्शितम् ।
रुदन्तस्सह वैदेह्या
सिषिचुस्सलिलेन वै ॥
The brothers and the princess of Vidēha, gathered around that great bowman who was worn out by grief, on all sides and sprinkled water on Rāma, the lord of the earth, as he lay on the ground, fallen like a dozing elephant that became exhausted by hitting the river bank.
2.103.6
स तु संज्ञां पुनर्लब्ध्वा
नेत्राभ्यामस्रमुत्सृजन् ।
उपाक्रामत काकुत्स्थ
कृपणं बहु भाषितुम् ॥
Regaining his senses, tears rolling down from his eyes, Kākutstha lamented for long, heartbroken, recalling many things.
2.103.7
स रामस्स्वर्गतं श्रुत्वा
पितरं पृथिवीपतिम् ।
उवाच भरतं वाक्यं
धर्मात्मा धर्मसंहितम् ॥
Rāma, the Dharmātma, hearing the news that his father, the lord of the earth, ascended to Heaven, said these words, replete with Dharma, to Bharata:
2.103.8
किं करिष्याम्ययोध्यायां
ताते दिष्टां गतिं गते ।
कस्तां राजवराध्दीनाम्
अयोध्यां पालयिष्यति ॥
What will I do in Ayōdhyā, with father set on a difference course by destiny? Who can govern the Ayōdhyā that has now lost the ablest of the rulers?
2.103.9
किं नु तस्य मया कार्यं
दुर्जातेन महात्मनः ।
यो मृतो मम शोकेन
मया चापि न संस्कृतः ॥
I am of wretched birth and served no purpose for him; for he died from the grief for me and without having the last rites done by me!
2.103.10
अहो भरत सिद्धार्थो
येन राजा त्वयाऽनघ ।
शत्रुघ्नेन च सर्वेषु
प्रेतकृत्येषु सत्कृतः ॥
Bharata, O blameless one, you are fortunate and your life is fulfilled; for you, along with Ṡatrughna, were able to do all the final honors for the King!
2.103.11
निष्प्रधानामनेकाग्रां
नरेन्द्रेण विनाकृताम् ।
निवृत्तवनवासोऽपि
नायोध्यां गन्तुमुत्सहे ॥
Even after completing my exile in Vana, I would not want be eager to go to Ayōdhyā which is direction-less, having lost its chief, that lord of the people.
2.103.12
समाप्तवनवासं माम्
अयोध्यायां परन्तप ।
कोऽनु शासिष्यति पुनः
ताते लोकान्तरं गते ॥
O scorcher of the foe! Who in Ayōdhyā will guide me, with father having gone to a different world, at the end of my exile in Vana!
2.103.13
पुरा प्रेक्ष्य सुवृत्तं मां
पितायान्याह सान्त्वयन् ।
वाक्यानि तानि श्रोष्यामि
कुतश्श्रोत्रसुखान्यहम् ॥
Father used to compliment me, pleased by my conduct. From whom can I hear those words, that are so nice and pleasing to the ears?
2.103.14
एवमुक्त्वा स भरतं
भार्यामभ्येत्य राघवः ।
उवाच शोकसन्तप्तः
पूर्णचन्द्रनिभाननाम् ॥
After saying these words to Bharata, Rāghava turned to his wife, whose face has the glow of moon, and said these words, scorched by grief:
2.103.15
सीते मृतस्ते श्वशुरः
पित्रा हीनोऽसि लक्ष्मण ।
भरतो दुःखमाचष्टे
स्वर्गतं पृथिवीपतिम् ॥
O Seetā, your father-in-law is dead! O Lakshmaṇa, you have become fatherless! Lo, Bharata gives us this sad news of the lord of the earth ascending to Heaven!
2.103.16
ततो बहुगुणं तेषां
बाष्पो नेत्रेष्वजायत ।
तथा ब्रुवति काकुत्स्थे
कुमाराणां यशस्विनाम् ॥
As Kākutstha was saying thus tears rolled down heavily from the eyes of those princes of renown.
2.103.17
ततस्ते भ्रातरस्सर्वे
भृशमाश्वास्य राघवम् ।
अब्रुवन् जगतीभर्तुः
क्रियतामुदकं पितुः ॥
Then all the brothers consoled Rāghava repeatedly and urged him to offer water libations to their father, the lord of the earth.
2.103.18
सा सीता श्वशुरं श्रुत्वा
स्वर्गलोकगतं नृपम् ।
नेत्राभ्यामश्रुपूर्णाभ्याम्
अशकन्नेक्षितुं पतिम् ॥
Seetā, hearing that the king, her father-in-law, had ascended to Heaven, eyes filled with tears, found it difficult to fix her gaze on her husband!
2.103.19
सान्त्वयित्वा तु तां रामो
रुदन्तीं जनकात्मजाम् ।
उवाच लक्ष्मणं तत्र
दुःखितो दुःखितं वचः ॥
Rāma, consoling her, the crying daughter of Janaka, said these words to the weeping Lakshmaṇa, himself weeping:
2.103.20
आनयेङ्गुदिपिण्याकं
चीरमाहर चोत्तरम् ।
जलक्रियार्थं तातस्य
गमिष्यामि महात्मनः ॥
Fetch me a ball of Iṅgudi nuts and also my upper and nether garments of tree bark. I shall go to perform the water rites for father, the Mahātma.
2.103.21
सीता पुरस्ताद्व्रजतु
त्वमेनामभितो व्रज ।
अहं पश्चाद्गमिष्यामि
गतिर्ह्येषा सुदारुणा ॥
Let Seetā go in front, followed by you. I shall come behind you. What a wretched tootle is this!
2.103.22
ततो नित्यानुगस्तेषां
विदितात्मा महामतिः ।
मृदुर्दान्तश्च शान्तश्च
रामे च दृढभक्तिमान् ।
सुमन्त्रस्तैर्नृपसुतैः
सार्धमाश्वास्य राघवम् ।
अवातारयदालम्ब्य
नदीं मन्दाकिनीं शिवाम् ॥
Then the astute, discerning and composed Sumantra who was gentle inside out, who followed them always, and who was staunch in his devotion to Rāma, consoled Rāghava along with the princes, and gave a supporting hand as he climbed down (the mountain) to the holy Mandākini.
2.103.24
ते सुतीर्थां ततः कृच्छ्रात्
उपागम्य यशस्विनः ।
नदीं मन्दाकिनीं रम्यां
सदा पुष्पितकाननाम् ।
शीघ्रश्रोतसमासाद्य
तीर्थं शिवमकर्दमम् ।
सिषिचुस्तूदकं राज्ञे
तातैतत्ते भवत्विति ॥
Reaching with difficulty the delightful Mandākini, with its purifying waters and its woods perennially in flower, they, of good renown, went to the clean bathing shore where the river ran swift and there was no mire, and offered libations, saying, 'May these offerings be yours, O father, O king!'
2.103.26
प्रगृह्य च महीपालो
जलपूरितमञ्जलिम् ।
दिशं याम्यामभिमुखो
रुदन्वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
The ruler of the earth, holding water in his cupped hands, and facing the direction of Yama (south), said as he wept:
2.103.27
एतत्ते राजशार्दूल
विमलं तोयमक्षयम् ।
पितृलोकगतस्याद्य
मद्दत्तमुपतिष्ठतु ॥
O tiger among Kings, may this pure water offered by me abide everlastingly with you, who have gone to the world of the Pitṛ Dēvatas!
2.103.28
ततो मन्दाकिनीतीरात्
प्रत्युत्तीर्य स राघवः ।
पितुश्चकार तेजस्वी
निवापं भ्रातृभिस्सह ॥
Climbing on to the bank from the river Mandākini, the radiant Rāghava, along with his brothers, offered oblations of food to his father.
2.103.29
ऐङ्गुदं बदरीमिश्रं
पिण्याकं दर्भसंस्तरे ।
न्यस्य रामस्सदुःखार्तो
रुदन्वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Placing the ball of crushed Iṅgudi seed mixed with Badari fruits on Darbha grass, Rāma said, as he wept in deep distress:
2.103.30
इदं भुङ्क्ष्वमहाराज
प्रीतो यदशना वयम् ।
यदन्नः पुरुषो भवति
तदन्नास्तस्य देवताः ॥
Eat with pleasure, O great King, this, which is what we eat. For, that which is food for a man is food for the Dēvas!
2.103.31
ततस्तेनैव मार्गेण
प्रत्युत्तीर्य नदीतटात् ।
आरुरोह नरव्याघ्रो
रम्यसानुं महीधरम् ॥
Then that tiger among men tracked back his path from the river climbing up the mountain along the enchanting cliffs.
2.103.32
ततः पर्णकुटीद्वारम्
आसाद्य जगतीपतिः ।
परिजग्राह बाहुभ्याम्
उभौ भरतलक्ष्मणौ ॥
Then that lord of the world, reaching the entrance of the thatched cottage, took Bharata and Lakshmaṇa into his embrace.
2.103.33
तेषां तु रुदतां शब्दात्
प्रतिश्रुत्कोऽभवद्गिरौ ।
भ्रातॄणां सह वैदेह्या
सिंहानामिव नर्दताम् ॥
The wailing of the brothers and the princess of Vidēha echoed from the mountain like the roaring of lions.
2.103.34
महाबलानां रुदतां
कुर्वतामुदकं पितुः ।
विज्ञाय तुमुलं शब्दं
त्रस्ता भरत सैनिकाः ॥
The army of Bharata got scared, hearing those reverberating sounds of the cries of those mighty strong, who had completed the water libations to their father.
2.103.35
अब्रुवंश्चापि रामेण
भरत स्संङ्गतो ध्रुवम् ।
तेषामेव महाञ्छब्दः
शोचतां पितरं मृतम् ॥
Then they said (to each other): 'Bharata must have met with Rāma! That loud sound must be from them crying over the deceased father!'
2.103.36
अथ वासान्परित्यज्य
तं सर्वेऽभिमुखास्स्वनम् ।
अप्येकमनसो जग्मुः
यथास्थानं प्रधाविताः ॥
Then, in one accord, leaving their camps, all of them dashed, albeit in an orderly manner, towards the place where the noise came from.
2.103.37
हयैरन्ये गजैरन्ये
रथैरन्ये स्वलङ्कृतैः ।
सुकुमारा स्तथैवान्ये
पद्भिरेव नरा ययुः ॥
Then they went; some on horses, some on elephants, and some men on foot and the delicate ones (women) on well decorated chariots.
2.103.38
अचिरप्रोषितं रामं
चिरविप्रोषितं यथा ।
द्रष्टुकामो जनस्सर्वो
जगाम सहसाऽऽश्रमम् ॥
All of them made all haste for the Āṡrama, eager to see Rāma, as if he had been long away, though he had left them only a short while before.
2.103.39
भ्रातऽणां त्वरिता स्तत्र
द्रष्टुकामास्समागमम् ।
ययुर्बहुविधैर्यानैः
खुरनेमिस्वनाकुलैः ॥
Eager to witness the meeting of the brothers, they hastened there, on every kind of vehicle, the hoofs and rims of the wheels causing a din.
2.103.40
सा भूमिर्बहुभिर्यानैः
खुरनेमिसमाहता ।
मुमोच तुमुलं शब्दं
द्यौरिवाभ्रसमागमे ॥
And the earth there, pounded by the hooves and rims of the wheels, made a great din like the sky does when the clouds emerge.
2.103.41
तेन वित्रासिता नागाः
करेणुपरिवारिताः ।
आवासयन्तो गन्धेन
जग्मुरन्यद्वनं ततः ॥
Frightened by that clamor, tuskers followed by their cows, left the place for another Vana, diffusing the odor of the ichor, along the way!
2.103.42
वराह वृकसङ्घाश्च
महिषास्सर्पावानराः ।
व्याघ्र गोकर्णगवया
वित्रेसुः पृषतै स्सह ॥
Hordes of boars, wolf packs, buffaloes, serpents, monkeys, tigers, Gōkarṇas and Gavayas were terrified, as were the stags.
2.103.43
रथाङ्गसाह्वा नत्यूहा
हंसाः कारण्डवाः प्लवाः ।
तथा पुंस्कोकिलाः क्रौञ्चा
विसंज्ञा भेजिरे दिशः ॥
Cakravākas, water fowl, swans, Kāraṇḍavas, cranes, and likewise Kōkilas and Krouncas scattered in all directions, losing their orientation.
2.103.44
तेन शब्देन वित्रस्तैः
आकाशं पक्षिभिर्वृतम् ।
मनुष्यैरावृता भूमिः
उभयं प्रबभौ तदा ॥
Both the sky and the earth glowed splendidly, one filled with birds that were frightened by the din, and the other covered with men.
2.103.45
ततस्तं पुरषव्याघ्रं
यशस्विनमरिन्दमम् ।
आसीनं स्थण्डिले रामं
ददर्श सहसा जनः ॥
In no time, those people saw him, that tiger among men, Rāma, the renowned and blameless one, who was seated on the ground.
2.103.46
विगर्हमाणः कैकेयीं
मन्थरासहितामपि ।
अभिगम्य जनो रामं
बाष्पपूर्णमुखोऽभवत् ॥
Reviling Kaikēyee and Manthara, those people crowded around Rāma, their faces covered with tears.
2.103.47
तान्नरान्बाष्पपूर्णाक्षान्
समीक्ष्याथ सुदुःखितान् ।
पर्यष्वजत धर्मज्ञः
पितृवन्मातृवच्च सः ॥
Seeing those people, grieving bitterly and eyes filled with tears, he, who knew Dharma, embraced them as he would his father and mother.
2.103.48
स तत्र कांश्चित्परिषस्वजे नरा-
न्नरास्तु केचित्तु तमभ्यवादयन् ।
चकार सर्वान्सवयस्यबान्धवान्
यथार्ह मासाद्य तदा नृपात्मजः ॥
He embraced some. Some prostrated themselves before him. The son of the king met all his friends and relations, and welcomed each one fittingly.
2.103.49
स तत्र तेषां रुदतां महात्मनां
भुवं च खं चानुनिनादयन्स्वनः ।
गुहा गिरीणां च दिशश्च सन्ततं
मृदङ्गघोषप्रतिमः प्रशुश्रुवे ॥
The swelling sounds of those Mahātmas weeping, filled the earth and sky, reverberating through the mountain-caves and in every direction, resembling the nonstop throbbing rhythms of Mṛdaṅga.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे त्य्रुत्तरशततमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and third Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
