Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 48
In this Sarga, Seetā cries pitiably on seeing Rāma and Lakshmaṇa lying in the battlefield, slain. She wonders how the prophesy of all those wise men who are well-versed in reading body marks could go in vain. She is puzzled at her fate, as she knows of no birth marks on her that portend widowhood for her. She thinks of her mother-in-law who eagerly awaits the return of her son along with Lakshmaṇa and Seetā.
Trijaṭa consoles Seetā, asserting that the brothers are still alive. She says that the army would not be as composed as it was, if their chief is slain. She points to the army, which is neither confounded nor agitated, but is carefully protecting the brothers. She asks Seetā to carefully note the signs of life on the faces of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa. Seetā wishes that Trijata’s words be true. Trijaṭa takes Seetā back in the Pushpaka to the Aṡōka pleasance.
6.48.1
भर्तारं निहतं दृष्ट्वा
लक्ष्मणं च महावलम् ।
विललाप भृशं सीता
करुणं शोककर्शिता ॥
On seeing both her husband and Lakshmaṇa of immense strength slain, Seetā wept pitiably, worn out by grief.
6.48.2
ऊचुर्लक्षणिका ये माम्
पुत्रिण्यविधवेति च ।
तेऽद्य सर्वे हते रामे
ज्ञानिनोऽनृतवादिनः ॥
The experts in reading bodily marks who foretold that I would have children and would know no widowhood are proven wrong today by the death of Rāma.
6.48.3
यज्वनो महिषीं ये माम्
ऊचुः पत्नीं च सत्रिणः ।
तेऽद्य सर्वे हते रामे
ज्ञानिनोऽनृतवादिनः ॥
Those prophets who predicted that I would be the consort of a crowned King and the wife of one who performed many Yajñas too are proven wrong today by the death of Rāma.
6.48.4
ऊचुः संश्रवणे ये माम्
द्विजाः कार्तान्तिकाः शुभाम् ।
तेऽद्य सर्वे हते रामे
ज्ञानिनोऽनृतवादिनः ॥
The Brāhmaṇas who knew the intentions of heavens whom I heard extolling me as one gifted with everything auspicious are proven wrong today by the death of Rāma.
6.48.5
वीरपार्थिवपत्नी त्वम्
ये धन्येति च मां विदुः ।
तेऽद्य सर्वे हते रामे
ज्ञानिनोऽनृतवादिनः ॥
Those sagacious men who assumed me to be a fortunate woman and consort of a valiant king are proven wrong today by the death of Rāma.
6.48.6
इमानि खलु पद्मानि
पादयोर्यैः कुल स्त्रियः ।
आधिराज्येऽभिषिच्यन्ते
नरेन्द्रैः पतिभिः सह ॥
The women of high birth, on whose feet are found these lotus shaped birth marks, are supposed to be consecrated along with their husbands as rulers of a kingdom.
6.48.7
वैधव्यं यान्ति यैर्नार्योऽ -
लक्षणैर्भाग्यदुर्लभाः ।
नात्मनस्तानि पश्यामि
पश्यन्ती हतलक्षणा ॥
All these birth marks went in vain, with me; However, I check, but do not find any inauspicious marks on me that are known to bring the misfortune of widowhood to women.
6.48.8
सत्यनामानि पद्मानि
स्त्रीणामुक्तानि लक्षणैः ।
तान्यद्य निहते रामे
वितथानि भवन्ति मे ॥
The lotus shaped birth marks on a woman are said to bring good fortune without fail, but they went in vain with me, with Rāma slain today.
6.48.9
केशाः सूक्ष्माः समा नीला
भ्रुवौ चासङ्गते मम ।
वृत्ते चारोमशे जङ्घे
दन्ताश्चाविरला मम ॥
My hair is silky, uniformly long and black, my eyebrows do not touch each other, my shanks are rounded and hairless, and there are no gaps between my teeth.
6.48.10
शङ्खे नेत्रे करौ पादौ
गुल्फावूरू च मे चितौ ।
अनुवृत्तनखाः स्निग्धाः
समाश्चाङ्गुलयो मम ॥
My temples, eyes, hands, feet, ankles and thighs are symmetrical, my nails are shiny and perfectly curved, and my fingers and toes are in right proportions.
6.48.11
स्तनौ चाविरलौ पीनौ
मामकौ मग्नचूचुकौ ।
मग्ना चोत्सेधिनी नाभिः
पार्श्वोरस्कं च मे चितम् ॥
My breasts are plump and press against each other, my nipples do not protrude, my navel slopes deep from my wide stomach and my chest and its sides are well-formed.
6.48.12
मम वर्णो मणिनिभो
मृदून्यङ्गरुहाणि च ।
प्रतिष्ठितां द्वादशभिः
मामूचुः शुभलक्षणाम् ॥
My complexion is like that of a polished gem, and the hair on my body is soft. With my stride well balanced on the twelve parts (ten toes and two heels) I have been said to have all auspicious features.
6.48.13
समग्रयवमच्छिद्रम्
पाणिपादं च वर्णवत् ।
मन्दस्मितेत्येव च माम्
कन्यालक्षणिका विदुः ॥
Experts in the study of birth marks of young girls did acknowledge that my hands and feet are full of glow and bear birth marks in the shape of full-grown barley, and that there are no gaps between my fingers and toes and that I sport a pleasant and gentle smile.
6.48.14
आधिराज्येऽभिषेको मे
ब्राह्मणैः पतिना सह ।
कृतान्तकुशलैरुक्तम्
तत्सर्वं वितथीकृतम् ॥
Whatever the experts in deciphering the intentions of the heavens has told - that I would be consecrated in a kingdom along with my husband - has come to naught.
6.48.15
शोधयित्वा जनस्थानम्
प्रवृत्तिमुपलभ्य च ।
तीर्त्वा सागरमक्षोभ्यम्
भ्रातरौ गोष्पदे हतौ ॥
Having combed Janasthāna, having obtained news of me and having crossed the inviolable sea, the brothers have lost their lives in what is as trifle as a cow’s-hoof-puddle.
6.48.16
ननु वारुणमाग्नेयम्
चन्द्रं वायव्यमेव च ।
अस्त्रं ब्रह्मशिरश्चैव
राघवौ प्रत्यपद्यताम् ॥
Did not the scions of Raghus master the celestial Astras presided over by Varuṇa and Agni, Indra and Vāyu and also the Astra called Brahmaṡira?
6.48.17
अदृश्यमानेन रणे
मायया वासवोपमौ ।
मम नाथावनाथाया
निहतौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, who were comparable to Indra, have been slain by the use of trickery and by making oneself invisible in the battlefield. My protectors leave me now unprotected.
6.48.18
न हि दृष्टिपथं प्राप्य
राघवस्य रणे रिपुः ।
जीवन् प्रतिनिवर्तेत
यद्यपि स्यान्मनोजवः ॥
For no enemy, even if he were as swift as the mind, could escape alive once fallen into the line of sight of Rāghava in the battlefield.
6.48.19
न कालस्यातिभारोऽस्ति
कृतान्तश्च सुदुर्जयः ।
यत्र रामः सह भ्रात्रा
शेते युधि निपातितः ॥
Death is invincible and nothing seems to be too heavy for Time to bear, from the fact that Rāma, along with his brother, lies slain in battle.
6.48.20
न शोचामि तथा रामम्
लक्ष्मणं च महाबलम् ।
नात्मानं जननीं वापि
यथा श्वश्रूं तपस्विनीम् ॥
I do not grieve for myself, for Rāma, for Lakshmaṇa of immense strength, or for my own mother so much as I do for my long-suffering mother-in-law.
6.48.21
साऽनुचिन्तयते नित्यम्
समाप्तव्रतमागतम् ।
कदा द्रक्ष्यामि सीतां च
लक्ष्मणं च सराघवम् ॥
She constantly thinks, ‘Ah, when will I see Rāghava return with Seetā and Lakshmaṇa, his vow fulfilled?”
6.48.22
परिदेवयमानां ताम्
राक्षसी त्रिजटाऽब्रवीत् ॥
To her, who was thus crying, the Rākshasi Trijaṭa said:
6.48.22
मा विषादं कृथा देवि
भर्ताऽयं तव जीवति ।
कारणानि च वक्ष्यामि
महान्ति सदृशानि च ।
यथेमौ जीवतो देवि
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
Do not grieve, my lady, your husband is alive. And I shall give you very good reasons why I think these two brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa are well alive.
6.48.24
नहि कोपपरीतानि
हर्षपर्युत्सुकानि च ।
भवन्ति युधि योधानाम्
मुखानि निहते पतौ ॥
The faces of warriors would not be filled with rage as much as they would not be with joy when their lord is slain in battle.
6.48.25
इदं विमानं वैदेहि
पुष्पकं नाम नामतः ।
दिव्यं त्वां धारयेन्नैवम्
यद्येतौ गतजीवितौ ॥
This divine aerial car which is known to fame as the Pushpaka would not carry you, O Vaidēhee, if these two had lost their lives.
6.48.26
हतवीरप्रधाना हि
हतौत्साहा निरुद्यमा ।
सेना भ्रमति सङ्ख्येषु
हतकर्णेव नौर्जले ॥
An army loses its morale and initiative if its chief warrior is slain. It will spin like a ship that has lost its rudder in the middle of the water.
6.48.27
इयं पुनरसम्भ्रान्ता
निरुद्विग्ना तपस्विनी ।
सेना रक्षति काकुत्स्थौ
मया प्रीत्या निवेदितौ ॥
You are worried (unnecessarily)! This army is neither confounded, nor agitated; it is engaged in the protection of Kākutsthas, which I want to stress to you, to cheer you up!
6.48.28
सा त्वं भव सुविस्रब्धा
अनुमानैः सुखोदयैः ।
अहतौ पश्य काकुत्स्थौ
स्नेहादेतद् ब्रवीमि ते ॥
You shall keep faith, because of all these positive indications. Take it that the Kākutsthas are not slain. Let me say this with all my love (for you)!
6.48.29
अनृतं नोक्तपूर्वं मे
न च वक्ष्ये कदाचन ।
चारित्रसुखशीलत्वात्
प्रविष्टाऽसि मनो मम ॥
Never a lie came out of my mouth nor will it ever hereafter. Your chaste and pristine character has lodged you in my affections.
6.48.30
नेमौ शक्यौ रणे जेतुम्
सेन्द्रैरपि सुरासुरैः ।
तादृशं दर्शनं दृष्ट्वा
मया चावेदितं तव ॥
These cannot be vanquished in battle even by all the Dēvas and the Asuras coming together headed by Indra. It is with this firm belief, that I speak to you thus.
6.48.31
इदं च सुमहच्चिह्नम्
शनैः पश्यस्व मैथिलि ।
निस्सञ्ज्ञावप्युभावेतौ
नैव लक्ष्मीर्वियुज्यते ॥
Look at this unmistakable and telling evidence carefully, O Maithili! Both of them lie unconscious, but the glow of life has not departed from them!
6.48.32
प्रायेण गतसत्त्वानाम्
पुरुषाणां गतायुषाम् ।
दृश्यमानेषु वक्त्रेषु
परं भवति वैकृतम् ॥
A certain deformation is often seen on the faces of men bereft of life force at the end of their life span.
6.48.33
त्यज शोकं च मोहं च
दुःखं च जनकात्मजे ।
रामलक्ष्मणयोरर्थे
नाद्य शक्यमजीवितुम् ॥
Give up the grief, the delusions and the sobs for the sake of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, O daughter of Janaka. It is impossible that they should not live.
6.48.34
श्रुत्वा तु वचनं तस्याः
सीता सुरसुतोपमा ।
कृताञ्जलिरुवाचेदम्
एवमस्त्विति मैथिली ॥
On hearing those words of hers, Seetā, the princess of Mithilā, comparable to celestial dames, said, ‘May it be so!’, with her palms joined in a praying gesture.
6.48.35
विमानं पुष्पकं तत्तु
सन्निवर्त्य मनोजवम् ।
दीना त्रिजटया सीता
लङ्कामेव प्रवेशिता ॥
The aerial car Pushpaka, which could fly at the speed of the mind, was then taken back and the doleful Seetā was lodged back in Laṅkā, by Trijaṭa.
6.48.36
ततस्त्रिजटया सार्धम्
पुष्पकादवरुह्य सा ।
अशोकवनिकामेव
राक्षसीभिः प्रवेशिता ॥
She got off the Pushpaka along with Trijaṭa, and was taken by the Rākshasis back to the Aṡōka pleasance.
6.48.37
प्रविश्य सीता बहुवृक्षषण्डां
तां राक्षसेन्द्रस्य विहारभूमिम् ।
सम्प्रेक्ष्य सञ्चिन्त्य च राजपुत्रौ
परं विषादं समुपाजगाम ॥
Returning to the pleasure gardens of the lord of Rākshasas that were filled with many kinds of groves, she pondered long on the plight of the princes and remained overpowered by grief.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे अष्टचत्वारिंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty eighth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
