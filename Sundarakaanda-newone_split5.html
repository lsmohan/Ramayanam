Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 5
In this Sarga, Hanumān sees many Rākshasas and their women enjoying the night brightly lit by the moon. But, not finding Seetā, he feels numb, struck by grief.
5.5.1
ततः स मध्यंगतमंशुमन्तं
ज्योत्स्नावितानं महदुद्वमन्तम् ।
ददर्श धीमान् दिवि भानुमन्तं
गोष्ठे वृषं मत्तमिव भ्रमन्तम् ॥
The sagacious one saw the moon of cool rays ascending to the mid-heavens, casting a huge canopy of moonlight, like an intoxicated bull restlessly pacing the cowshed.
This Sarga does not seem to be authentic, with all its Ṡlōkas set in a longer meter and very much untypical of the simplistic, non-ornamental style of Vālmeeki.
5.5.2
लोकस्य पापानि विनाशयन्तं
महोदधिं चापि समेधयन्तम् ।
भूतानि सर्वाणि विराजयन्तं
ददर्श शीतांशुमथाभियान्तम् ॥
He saw the moon of cool rays sailing (in the sky) relieving the world of its stresses and strains, making the great oceans swell and brightening the moods of all creatures.
5.5.3
या भाति लक्ष्मीर्भुवि मन्दरस्था
तथा प्रदोषेषु च सागरस्था ।
तथैव तोयेषु च पुष्करस्था
रराज सा चारुनिशाकरस्था ॥
The beautiful moon, the lord of the night, was shining with a splendor of Mandara mountain, of the vast ocean and of lotuses in water.
5.5.4
हंसो यथा राजतपञ्जरस्थः
सिंहो यथा मन्दरकन्दरस्थः ।
वीरो यथा गर्वितकुञ्जरस्थः
चन्द्रोऽपि बभ्राज तथाम्बरस्थः ॥
The moon, shining in the sky, was like a swan in a silver cage, like a lion in a cave of Mandara mountain and like a heroic warrior on the back of a proud tusker.
5.5.5
स्थितः ककुद्मानिव तीक्ष्णशृङ्गो
महाचलः श्वेत इवोच्चशृङ्गः ।
हस्तीव जाम्बूनदबद्धशृङ्गो
रराज चन्द्रः परिपूर्णशृङ्गः ॥
The full-orbed moon shone like a bull with mighty hump and sharp horns, like a great white mountain with a lofty peak, and like an elephant with its tusks covered with gold.
5.5.6
विनष्टशीताम्बुतुषारपङ्कः
महाग्रहग्राहविनष्टपङ्कः ।
प्रकाशलक्ष्म्याश्रयनिर्मलाङ्क:
रराज चन्द्रो भगवान् शशाङ्कः ॥
With the lord of the planets (sun) clearing the mist from the cool drizzle and dusting off his hare-shaped spot, the spot of the brightly lit lordly moon itself stood as a symbol of splendor.
5.5.7
शिलातलं प्राप्य यथा मृगेन्द्रो
महारणं प्राप्य यथा गजेन्द्रः ।
राज्यं समासाद्य यथा नरेन्द्रः
तथाप्रकाशो विरराज चन्द्रः ॥
Like a lion on the top of a boulder, like the lord of tuskers on a battlefield, and like the leader of people who is established in a kingdom, shone the moon with his brilliance.
5.5.8
प्रकाशचन्द्रोदयनष्टदोषः
प्रवृत्तरक्षः पिशिताशदोषः ।
रामाभिरामेरितचित्तदोषः
स्वर्गप्रकाशो भगवान् प्रदोषः ॥
The young night got over the problem of darkness with the raise of the moon, but got caught in another one, of Rākshasas getting ready to devour flesh. However, with the lovers letting go off their problem of misgivings, it became bright again as Heaven.
5.5.9
तन्त्रीस्वनाः कर्णसुखाः प्रवृत्ताः
स्वपन्ति नार्यः पतिभिः सुवृत्ताः ।
नक्तंचराश्चापि तथा प्रवृत्ता:
विहर्तुमत्यद्भुतरौद्रवृत्ताः ॥
The music of string instruments, pleasing to the ears, began. Chaste women slept with their husbands. Even Rākshasas of unimaginable ferocity, were out at play.
5.5.10
मत्तप्रमत्तानि समाकुलानि
रथाश्वभद्रासनसङ्कुलानि ।
वीरश्श्रिया चापि समाकुलानि
ददर्श धीमान् स कपिः कुलानि ॥
The sagacious Vānara saw innumerable houses full of drunken men heedless of their surroundings, with the attendant profusion of chariots, horses, elephants of Bhadra breed, couches and opulence of every other kind.
5.5.11
परस्परं चाधिकमाक्षिपन्ति
भुजांश्च पीनानधिकं क्षिपन्ति ।
मत्तप्रलापानधिकं क्षिपन्ति
मत्तानि चान्योन्यमधिक्षिपन्ति ॥
Flinging their stout arms, (the Rākshasas) made fun of each other and indulged in drunk talk vilifying each other.
5.5.12
रक्षांसि वक्षांसि च विक्षिपन्ति
गात्राणि कान्तासु च विक्षिपन्ति ।
रूपाणि चित्राणि च विक्षिपन्ति
दृढानि चापानि च विक्षिपन्ति ॥
Some threw out their chests, some threw themselves on women, some assumed a variety of shapes and forms and others flung their sturdy bows.
5.5.13
ददर्श कान्ताश्च समालभन्त्यः
तथा परास्तत्र पुनः स्वपन्त्यः ।
सुरूपवक्त्राश्च तथा हसन्त्यः
क्रुद्धाः पराश्चापि विनिःश्वसन्त्यः ॥
He saw women, some sleeping, some beautifying themselves with creams and lotions, some with comely faces laughing, and yet others angry, heaving deep sighs.
5.5.14
महागजैश्चापि तथा नदद्भि:
सुपूजितैश्चापि तथा सुसद्भिः ।
रराज वीरैश्च विनिःश्वसद्भिः
ह्रदो भुजङ्गैरिव निःश्वसद्भिः ॥
(The city) was like a pool full of hissing snakes, with its great tuskers trumpeting to honor its men of high stature and its warriors heaving long sighs.
5.5.15
बुद्धिप्रधानान् रुचिराभिधानान्
संश्रद्धधानान् जगतः प्रधानान् ।
नानाविधानान् रुचिराभिधानान्
ददर्श तस्यां पुरि यातुधानान् ॥
He saw, in that city, world-class Rākshasas, some of powerful intellect, some of winsome speech, some of resolute focus and others bearing charming names and indulging in various preoccupations.
5.5.16
ननन्द दृष्ट्वा स च तान् सुरूपान्
नानागुणानात्मगुणानुरूपान् ।
विद्योतमानान्स तदानुरूपान्
ददर्श कांश्चिच्च पुनर्विरूपान् ॥
He felt happy seeing some to be just like him, handsome, bright and endowed with many virtues. But he also saw others of ugly forms with their nature matching their form.
5.5.17
ततो वरार्हाः सुविशुद्धभावाः
तेषां स्त्रियस्तत्र महानुभावाः ।
प्रियेषु पानेषु च सक्तभावा
ददर्श तारा इव सुप्रभावाः ॥
He then saw their women who were brilliant like stars, endowed with virtues that only the best would deserve, who were very capable and absorbed in drinks and in their lovers.
5.5.18
श्रिया ज्वलन्तीस्त्रपयोपगूढा
निशीथकाले रमणोपगूढाः ।
ददर्श काश्चित्प्रमदोपगूढा
यथा विहङ्गाः कुसुमोपगूढाः ॥
He saw, in the depths of the night, some radiating beauty, some extremely shy, some locked in the embraces of their lovers and others drowned in joy like birds covered in flowers.
5.5.19
अन्याः पुनर्हर्म्यतलोपविष्टाः
तत्र प्रियाङ्केषु सुखोपविष्टाः ।
भर्तुः प्रिया धर्मपरा निविष्टा
ददर्श धीमान् मदनाभिविष्टाः ॥
The sagacious one saw others of righteous ways seated in the laps of their loving husbands on the terraces of lofty mansions, caught in the throes of passion.
5.5.20
अप्रावृताः काञ्चनराजिवर्णाः
काश्चित्परार्थ्यास्तपनीयवर्णाः ।
पुनश्च काश्चिच्छशलक्ष्मवर्णाः
कान्तप्रहीणा रुचिराङ्गवर्णाः ॥
He saw some who were naked, some with complexion of streaks of gold, some with that of molten gold of the rarest kind, some with that like the spots on the moon, and others with exquisite figure and complexion along with others who were separated from their lovers.
5.5.21
ततः प्रियान् प्राप्य मनोभिरामान्
सुप्रीतियुक्ताः सुमनोभिरामाः  ।
गृहेषु हृष्टाः परमाभिरामाः
हरिप्रवीरः स ददर्श रामाः ॥
The best of the Veeras among the Vānaras saw some united with their lovers who filled their hearts with joy, some who were full of love and attractive like flowers, and others who were of great allure happy in their houses.
5.5.22
चन्द्रप्रकाशाश्च हि वक्त्रमालाः
वक्राक्षिपक्ष्माश्च सुनेत्रमालाः ।
विभूषणानां च ददर्श मालाः
शतह्रदानामिव चारुमालाः ॥
He saw an endless row of moon bright faces, an endless row of lovely eyes with curved eyelashes and an endless row of ornaments that seemed like an endless row of lightning flashes.
5.5.23
न त्वेव सीतां परमाभिजातां
पथि स्थिते राजकुले प्रजाताम् ।
लतां प्रपुल्लामिव साधु जातां
ददर्श तन्वीं मनसाभिजाताम् ॥
But nowhere could he find Seetā of extraordinary beauty who adhered to righteous ways, born and raised in a royal household and who was like a vine in flower, and slender like one chiseled by the mind.
5.5.24
सनातने वर्त्मनि सन्निविष्टां
रामेक्षणां तां मदनाभिविष्टाम् ।
भर्तुर्मनः श्रीमदनुप्रविष्टां
स्त्रीभ्यो वराभ्यश्च सदा विशिष्टाम् ॥
(He could not find) her, one of a kind and best among all women, who adhered to the time-honored way of life who captivated the rich heart of her husband, whose eyes were filled with the image of Rāma, and who was ravaged by the pangs of love.
5.5.25
उष्णार्दितां सानुसृतास्रकण्ठीम्
पुरा वरार्होत्तमनिष्ककण्ठीम् ।
सुजातपक्ष्मामभिरक्तकण्ठीम्
वनेऽप्रनृत्तामिव नीलकण्ठीम् ॥
(He could not find) her who was suffering from the separation (from her man), whose throat was choked with incessant tears, who in days past had worn rare and lovely necklaces with medallions, whose voice was musical and whose eyelashes were beautifully chiseled and who resembled a peahen in the woods that dances no more.
5.5.26
अव्यक्तरेखामिव चन्द्ररेखां
पांसुप्रदिग्धामिव हेमरेखाम् ।
क्षतप्ररूढामिव बाणरेखां
वायुप्रभिन्नामिव मेघरेखाम् ॥
(He could not find) her who was like a faint outline of the crescent moon, a streak of gold coated with dust, a scar left from the strike of an arrow, and a rag of cloud torn off by the wind.
5.5.27
सीतामपश्यन् मनुजेश्वरस्य
रामस्य पत्नीं वदतां वरस्य ।
बभूव दुःखाभिहतश्चिरस्य
प्लवङ्गमो मन्द इवाचिरस्य ॥
Not finding Seetā, the wife of Rāma, lord of people and best among those skilled with words, the Vānara fell numb for a while, struck by grief.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे पञ्चमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
