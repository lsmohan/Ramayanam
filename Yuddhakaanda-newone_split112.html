Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 112
In this Sarga, Rāma asks Lakshmaṇa to consecrate Vibheeshaṇa as the king of Laṅkā. Vānaras get water from the ocean for the consecration. Lakshmaṇa consecrates Vibheeshaṇa in a customary manner with Rākshasas and Vānaras watching.
Rāma then tells Hanumān to go to Seetā and convey to her about their wellbeing and the death of Rāvaṇa and and bring back her message.
6.112.1
ते रावणवधं दृष्ट्वा
देवगन्धर्वदानवाः ।
जग्मुः स्वैस्वैर्विमानैस्ते
कथयन्तः शुभाः कथाः ॥
Having witnessed the slaying of Rāvaṇa, the Dēvas, Gandharvas and Dānavas rode away in their respective aerial cars, talking of all the remarkable aspects (of the battle).
6.112.2
रावणस्य वधं घोरम्
राघवस्य पराक्रमम् ।
सुयुद्धं वानराणां च
सुग्रीवस्य च मन्त्रितम् ।
अनुरागं च वीर्यं च
सौमित्रेर्लक्ष्मणस्य च ।
पतिव्रतात्वं सीताया
हनूमति पराक्रमम् ।
कथयन्तो महाभागा
जग्मुर्हृष्टा यथागतम् ॥
Those greatly blessed ones went away happily, just as they came, reminiscing the many things, the terrible death of Rāvaṇa, the prowess of Rāghava, the splendid fight of Vānaras, the counsel of Sugreeva, the love and valor of Lakshmaṇa, the son of Sumitrā, the fidelity of Seetā and the prowess of Hanumān.
6.112.4
राघवस्तु रथं दिव्यम्
इन्द्रदत्तं शिखिप्रभम् ।
अनुज्ञाय महाबाहुः
मातलिं प्रत्यपूजयत् ॥
Meanwhile, after duly honoring him, Rāghava let Mātali depart with the fire-bright chariot sent by Indra.
6.112.5
राघवेणाभ्यनुज्ञातो
मातलिः शक्रसारथिः ।
दिव्यं तं रथमास्थाय
दिवमेवारुरोह सः ॥
With due permission from Rāghava, Mātali, the charioteer of Ṡakra got on to that splendid chariot and ascended to Heaven.
6.112.6
तस्मिंस्तु दिवमारूढे
सुरसारथिसत्तमे ।
राघवः परमप्रीतः
सुग्रीवं परिषस्वजे ॥
After that best of charioteers of the Suras thus left for Heaven, Rāghava, in his overflowing joy, embraced Sugreeva.
6.112.7
परिष्वज्य च सुग्रीवम्
लक्ष्मणेनाभिवादितः ।
पूज्यमानो हरिश्रेष्ठैः
आजगाम बलालयम् ॥
Taking Sugreeva into his embrace, receiving salutations from Lakshmaṇa and honors from the Vānara chiefs, he went to the army camp.
6.112.8
अब्रवीच्च तदा रामः
समीपपरिवर्तिनम् ।
सौमित्रिं सत्यसम्पन्नम्
लक्ष्मणं दीप्ततेजसम् ॥
Then Rāma said to Lakshmaṇa, the son of Sumitrā, of blazing power, of true valor, who was next to him:
6.112.9
विभीषणमिमं सौम्य
लङ्कायामभिषेचय ।
अनुरक्तं च भक्तं च
मम चैवोपकारिणम् ॥
Consecrate Vibheeshaṇa, who has been so deeply attached to me, so loyal and so helpful, as the king of Laṅkā, my dear!
6.112.10
एष मे परमः कामो
यदीमं रावणानुजम् ।
लङ्कायां सौम्य पश्येयम्
अभिषिक्तं विबीषणम् ॥
This is all I want, my dear, to see Vibheeshaṇa, the younger brother of Rāvaṇa, consecrated in Laṅkā!
6.112.11
एवमुक्तस्तु सौमित्री
राघवेण महात्मना ।
तथेत्युक्त्वा तु संहृष्टः
सौवर्णं घटमाददे ॥
Thus told by Mahātma Rāghava, and saying 'So shall it be!' with all joy, he procured a pot made of gold.
6.112.12
तं घटं वानरेन्द्राणाम्
हस्ते दत्त्वा मनोजवान् ।
आदिदेश महासत्त्वान्
समुद्रसलिलानये ॥
Placing that pot in the hands of Vānaras who could fly at the speed of thought, he instructed those mighty strong ones to bring water from the ocean.
6.112.13
इति शीघ्रं ततो गत्वा
वानरास्ते महाबलाः ।
आगतास्तज्जलं गृह्य
समुद्राद्वानरोत्तमाः ॥
Then those eminent Vānaras of immense strength went in all haste and returned with the water.
6.112.14
ततस्त्वेकं घटं गृह्य
संस्थाप्य परमासने ।
घटेन तेन सौमित्रिः
अभ्यषिञ्चद्विभीषणम् ।
लङ्कायां रक्षसां मध्ये
राजानं रामशासनात् ।
विधिना मन्त्रदृष्टेन
सुहृद्गणसमावृतम् ॥
Then Sowmitri took a pot, made Vibheeshaṇa sit on a splendid seat, and pouring a potful of water over him, consecrated him King of Laṅkā as ordered by Rāma chanting Mantras in a customary manner, in the midst the assembled Rākshasas and all the hosts of well-wishers.
6.112.16
अभ्यषिञ्चंस्तदा सर्वे
राक्षसा वानरास्तथा ।
प्रहर्षमतुलं गत्वा
तुष्टुवू राममेव ह ॥
All the Rākshasas and Vānaras also consecrated him. In their unparalleled joy, they then sang praises of Rāma.
6.112.17
तस्यामात्या जहृषिरे
भक्ता ये चास्य राक्षसाः ।
दृष्ट्वाऽभिषिक्तं लङ्कायाम्
राक्षसेन्द्रं विभीषणम् ।
राघवः परमां प्रीतिं
जगाम सहलक्ष्मणः ॥
All his ministers and all the Rākshasas loyal to him were happy on seeing Vibheeshaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, consecrated. Rāghava also, along with Lakshmaṇa felt extremely happy.
6.112.19
स तद्राज्यं महत् प्राप्य
रामदत्तं विभीषणः ।
प्रकृतीः सान्त्वयित्वा च
ततो राममुपागमत् ॥
Having thus received the great kingdom given to him by Rāma, Vibheeshaṇa greeted all his subjects and came back to Rāma.
6.112.20
दध्यक्षतान् मोदकांश्च
लाजाः सुमनसस्तदा ।
आजह्रुरथ संहृष्टाः
पौरास्तस्मै निशाचराः ॥
Then the subjects, in their joy, brought him offerings of curds, sanctified rice, Mōdakas, puffed grain and flowers.
6.112.21
स तान् गृहीत्वा दुर्धर्षो
राघवाय न्यवेदयत् ।
माङ्गल्यं मङ्गलं सर्वम्
लक्ष्मणाय च वीर्यवान् ॥
The valiant one accepted them and gave all those most auspicious offerings to Rāghava and Lakshmaṇa.
6.112.22
कृतकार्यं समृद्धार्थम्
दृष्ट्वा रामो विभीषणम् ।
प्रतिजग्राह तत् सर्वम्
तस्यैव प्रियकाम्यया ॥
Rāma accepted those offerings from Vibheeshaṇa who had completed a task of humongous import, as he wanted to please him.
Rāma, being in exile, should not seek or accept any fine or luxurious objects that are common to city life. Here he takes the offerings given to him by Vibheeshaṇa as an exception.
6.112.23
ततः शैलोपमं वीरम्
प्राञ्जलिं पार्श्वतः स्थितम् ।
अब्रवीद्राघवो वाक्यम्
हनुमन्तं प्लवङ्गमम् ॥
Then Rāghava said these words to the valiant and mountainous Vānara Hanumān, who stood on one side with his palms joined in reverence:
6.112.24
अनुज्ञाप्य महाराजम्
इमं सौम्य विभीषणम् ।
प्रविश्य नगरीं लङ्काम्
कौशलं ब्रूहि मैथिलीम् ॥
My dear, please go to the city of Laṅkā with the due permission of this supreme king Vibheeshaṇa and convey to Maithili about our wellbeing.
6.112.25
वैदेह्यै मां कुशलिनम्
सुग्रीवं च सलक्ष्मणम् ।
आचक्ष्व वदतां श्रेष्ठ
रावणं च हतम् ॥
O you, the best among messengers, convey my wellbeing, and that of Sugreeva and Lakshmaṇa, and the death of Rāvaṇa, to Vaidēhee!
6.112.26
प्रियमेतदुदाहृत्य
मैथिल्यास्त्वं हरीश्वर ।
प्रतिगृह्य च सन्देशम्
उपावर्तितुमर्हसि ॥
Conveying these glad tidings to Maithili, O Lord of Vānaras, come back with a message from her.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्वादशोत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and twelfth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
