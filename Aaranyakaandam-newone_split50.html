Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 50
In this Sarga, Jaṭāyu, who had been dozing on the tree, wakes up hearing the cries of Seetā. He introduces himself to Rāvaṇa and chastises him for trying to carry off Seetā, in utter disregard of what is expected of a king. He warns Rāvaṇa that the noose of death is tightening around his neck. He then tells Rāvaṇa, that he cannot escape safely while he is alive. He offers Rāvaṇa a treat of a fight.
3.50.1
तं शब्दमवसुप्तस्तु
जटायुरथ शुश्रुवे ।
निरीक्ष्य रावणं क्षिप्रम्
वैदेहीं च ददर्श सः ॥
Jaṭāyu, who had been dozing, heard those words. Looking around, he immediately saw Rāvaṇa and Vaidēhee.
3.50.2
ततः पर्वतकूटाभः
तीक्ष्णतुण्डः खगोत्तमः ।
वनस्पतिगतश्श्रीमान्
व्याजहार शुभां गिरम् ॥
Thereupon, that blessed sharp-beaked great bird, huge as a mountain crest, who was perched on a tree spoke these meaningful words:
3.50.3
दशग्रीव स्थितो धर्मे
पुराणे सत्यसंश्रयः ।
जटायुर्नाम नाम्नाहम्
गृध्रराजो महाबलः ॥
O you of ten necks! I, of immense strength, am the king of eagles and my name is Jaṭāyu. I cleave to truth and am faithful to the age-old Dharma.
3.50.4
राजा सर्वस्य लोकस्य
महेन्द्रवरुणोपमः ।
लोकानां च हिते युक्तो
रामो दशरथात्मजः ॥
And Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha who is devoted to the welfare of everyone, is the king of all the worlds, like the great Indra and Varuṇa.
3.50.5
तस्यैषा लोकनाथस्य
धर्मपत्नी यशस्विनी ।
सीता नाम वरारोहा
यां त्वं हर्तुमिहेच्छसि ॥
This renowned woman of excellent Kaṭee is wedded to that lord of the worlds in Dharma. Her name is Seetā, whom you wish to carry off.
3.50.6
कथं राजास्थितो धर्मे
परदारान्परामृशेत् ।
रक्षणीया विशेषेण
राजदारा महाबल ॥
How could a king who adheres to Dharma touch the wife of another man? O you of great strength, the wife of a king, especially, must be protected!
3.50.7
निवर्तय मतिं नीचाम्
परदाराभिमर्शनात् ।
न तत्समाचरेद्धीरो
यत्परोऽस्य विगर्हयेत् ।
यथात्मनस्तथान्येषाम्
दारा रक्ष्या विमर्शनात् ॥
Turn away your mind from the base desire to touch another man’s wife. No one in his right mind would do anything that earns others’ rebuke. One must protect others’ wives also, as one would one’s own, from being molested.
3.50.8
अर्थं वा यदि वा कामम्
शिष्टाश्शास्त्रेष्वनागतम् ।
व्यवस्यन्त्यनु राजानम्
धर्मं पौलस्त्यनन्दन ॥
O son of Pulastya! People depend on the king to determine what is Dharma, what is Artha and what is Kāma, when it is not clear from the Ṡastras!
3.50.9
राजा धर्मस्य कामस्य
द्रव्याणां चोत्तमो निधिः ।
धर्मश्शुभं वा पापं वा
राजमूलं प्रवर्तते ॥
The king is the ultimate keeper of Dharma, Kama and Artha. Righteousness, goodness as well as evil stem from the king alone.
3.50.10
पापस्वभावश्चपलः
कथं त्वं रक्षसां वर ।
ऐश्वर्यमभिसम्प्राप्तो
विमानमिव दुष्कृतिः ॥
How is it that, O worst of Rākshasas, you attained the lordship, being as fickle and sinful-minded that you are, like one of evil deeds getting a place in the Vimāna (bound for Heaven)!
3.50.11
कामं स्वभावो यो यस्य
न शक्यः परिमार्जितुम् ।
न हि दुष्टात्मनामार्यम्
आवसत्यालये चिरम् ॥
It is truly impossible to erase in full what is in one’s natural bent; nobility would not last long in the house of crooks.
3.50.12
विषये वा पुरे वा ते
यदा रामो महाबलः ।
नापराध्यति धर्मात्मा
कथं तस्यापराध्यसि ॥
The mighty strong Rāma, the Dharmātma committed no offense against your country or city; how could you, then, transgress in matters of his!
3.50.13
यदि शूर्पणखाहेतोः
जनस्थानगतः खरः ।
अतिवृत्तो हतः पूर्वम्
रामेणाक्लिष्टकर्मणा ।
अत्र ब्रूहि यथातत्त्वम्
को रामस्य व्यतिक्रमः ।
यस्य त्वं लोकनाथस्य
भार्यां हृत्वा गमिष्यसि ॥
Tell me, in truth, what is the fault of Rāma, the lord of the worlds who does everything with ease, whose wife you are carrying off, when it was Khara of Janasthāna who crossed the limits for the sake of Ṡūrpaṇakhā, and got killed?
3.50.15
क्षिप्रं विसृज वैदेहीम्
मा त्वा घोरेण चक्षुषा ।
दहेद्दहनभूतेन
वृत्रमिन्द्राशनिर्यथा ॥
Let go of Vaidēhee immediately before her eyes turn into terrible balls of fire and burn you away, like the Vajra of Indra that burnt Vṛtra!
3.50.16
सर्पमाशीविषं बद्ध्वा
वस्त्रान्ते नावबुध्यसे ।
ग्रीवायां प्रतिसक्तं च
कालपाशं न पश्यसि ॥
You do not know that you have tied a serpent with venom in its fangs to the end of your robe. You do not see the noose of death that is tightening around your neck.
3.50.17
स भारस्सौम्य भर्तव्यो
यो नरं नावसादयेत् ।
तदन्नमपि भोक्तव्यम्
जीर्यते यदनामयम् ॥
My dear! That weight alone is to be carried by which a man is not brought down, and that food alone is to be eaten by which a man would not be troubled while digesting. 
3.50.18
यत्कृत्वा न भवेद्धर्मो
न कीर्तिर्न यशो भुवि ।
शरीरस्य भवेत्खेदः
कस्तत्कर्म समाचरेत् ॥
Who on the earth would take upon a deed that causes pain to the body, but does not bring one any renown, fame or Dharma!
3.50.19
षष्टिर्वर्षसहस्राणि
मम जातस्य रावण ।
पितृपैतामहं राज्यम्
यथावदनुतिष्ठतः ॥
It has been sixty thousand years since I was born, O Rāvaṇa, and am governing the kingdom inherited from ancestors.
3.50.20
वृद्धोऽहं त्वं युवा धन्वी
सशरः कवची रथी ।
तथाप्यादाय वैदेहीम्
कुशली न गमिष्यसि ॥
I am old and you are young armed with bow and arrow and encased in a coat of mail, riding on a chariot. Even then, (I warn you), you cannot have a safe escape, carrying off Vaidēhee!
3.50.21
न शक्तस्त्वं बलाद्धर्तुम्
वैदेहीं मम पश्यतः ।
हेतुभिर्न्यायसंयुक्तैः
धृवां वेदश्रुतीमिव ॥
You cannot, under my watch, carry off Vaidēhee by force any more than the firmly rooted words of the Vēdas can be travestied by specious arguments with the help of polemics.
3.50.22
युद्ध्यस्व यदि शूरोऽसि
मुहूर्तं तिष्ठ रावण ।
शयिष्यसे हतो भूमौ
यथापूर्वं खरस्तथा ॥
If you are truly valiant, O Rāvaṇa, hold on for a second and fight with me! You will fall dead on the ground like Khara did earlier.
3.50.23
असकृत्संयुगे येन
निहता दैत्यदानवाः ।
नचिराच्चीरवासास्त्वाम्
रामो युधि वधिष्यति ॥
Not before too long, you will be killed in fight by Rāma who is clad in tree bark and who had slain Daityas and Dānavas many a time in battles.
3.50.24
किं नु शक्यं मया कर्तुम्
गतौदूरं नृपात्मजौ ।
क्षिप्रं त्वं नश्यसे नीच
तयोर्भीतो न संशयः ॥
What can I do, with the princes gone far away of whom you are so scared! But, you, O base creature, will perish soon without doubt!
3.50.25
न हि मे जीवमानस्य
नयिष्यसि शुभामिमाम् ।
सीतां कमलपत्राक्षीम्
रामस्य महिषीं प्रियाम् ॥
You cannot take Seetā of lotus-petal eyes the auspicious and dear wife of Rāma while I am alive.
3.50.26
अवश्यं तु मया कार्यम्
प्रियं तस्य महात्मनः ।
जीवितेनापि रामस्य
तथा दशरथस्य च ॥
I shall do everything to please Mahātma Rāma and Daṡaratha, even if it means giving up my life.
3.50.27
तिष्ठ तिष्ठ दशग्रीव
मुहूर्तं पश्य रावण ।
युद्धातिथ्यं प्रदास्यामि
यथाप्राणं निशाचर ।
वृन्तादिव फलं त्वां तु
पातयेयं रथोत्तमात् ॥
Hold on for a second, O you of ten necks! Look, O Rāvaṇa, I will treat you with a fight to the best of my abilities. I will throw you off from your chariot O Night-rover, like I can pluck a fruit from its stalk!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे पञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fiftieth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
