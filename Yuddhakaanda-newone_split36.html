Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 36
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa gets angry with Mālyavān for his advice, and accuses him of shifting loyalties to the enemy camp. He boasts again that he will kill Rāma and all the Vānaras in a few days and none would go back alive. He pooh-poohs the power of Rāma who has built a bridge across the ocean and is ready for launching the attack.
He then deploys various Rākshasas in charge of defending the gates of the city and retires to his own residence.
6.36.1
तत्तु माल्यवतो वाक्यम्
हितमुक्तं दशाननः ।
न मर्षयति दुष्टात्मा
कालस्य वशमागतः ॥
But the evil minded Ten-faced could not tolerate the well-intentioned advice given by Mālyavān, driven by destiny.
6.36.2
स बद्ध्वा भ्रुकुटिं वक्त्रे
क्रोधस्य वशमागतः ।
अमर्षात् परिवृत्ताक्षो
माल्यवन्तमथाब्रवीत् ॥
Overtaken by anger, knitting his eyebrows together, and rolling his eyes in rage, he said to Mālyavān:
6.36.3
हितबुद्ध्या यदहितम्
वचः परुषमुच्यते ।
परपक्षं प्रविश्यैव
नैतच्छ्रोत्रं गतं मम ॥
It is not for me to take your advice that is so harsh and against my well being, which you portray as well-intentioned, seemingly shifting your loyalties to the enemy camp.
6.36.4
मानुषं कृपणं रामम्
एकं शाखामृगाश्रयम् ।
समर्थं मन्यसे केन
त्यक्तं पित्रा वनालयम् ॥
Why do you think Rāma, a mere lonely hopeless human, who, abandoned by his father, had taken to the forests and taken refuge with the beasts of the branches, is so capable?
6.36.5
रक्षसामीश्वरं मां च
देवतानां भयङ्करम् ।
हीनं मां मन्यसे केन
ह्यहीनं सर्वविक्रमैः ॥
And what makes you think so low of me, the overlord of the Rākshasas and terror of Dēvas, who lacks nothing and has only prevailed in every endeavor?
6.36.6
वीरद्वेषेण वा शङ्के
पक्षपातेन वा रिपोः ।
त्वयाऽहं परुषाण्युक्तः
परप्रोत्साहनेन वा ॥
Your harsh words to me, I suspect, must be because of your resentment against heroism in others, or your partiality towards the enemies, or because of instigation by the enemies.
6.36.7
प्रभवन्तं पदस्थं हि
परुषं कोऽभिधास्यति ।
पण्डितः शास्त्रतत्त्वज्ञो
विना प्रोत्साहनाद्रिपोः ॥
For, which wise man, who has truly understood the Ṡastras, will speak harshly to someone who is capable and well-established in power, unless he has been instigated by the foe?
6.36.8
आनीय च वनात् सीताम्
पद्महीनामिव श्रियम् ।
किमर्थं प्रतिदास्यामि
राघवस्य भयादहम् ॥
Having brought Seetā, who is like Lakshmee herself except for the lotus, from the forest, why would I give her back? Being scared of Rāghava!?
6.36.9
वृतं वानरकोटीभिः
ससुग्रीवं सलक्ष्मणम् ।
पश्य कैश्चिदहोभिस्त्वम्
राघवं निहतं मया ॥
See Rāghava, along with Sugreeva, Lakshmaṇa and the crores of Vānaras slain by me in a matter of a few days!
6.36.10
द्वन्द्वे यस्य न तिष्ठन्ति
दैवतान्यपि संयुगे ।
स कस्माद्रावणो युद्धे
भयमाहारयिष्यति ॥
How can Rāvaṇa be scared of anyone in battle, when no one, not even Dēvas, can stand up to him in a duel in battle?
6.36.11
द्विधा भज्येयमप्येवम्
न नमेयं तु कस्यचित् ।
एष मे सहजो दोषः
स्वभावो दुरतिक्रमः ॥
I might break into two, but I shall never bend before anybody. Call it a fault, but it was born along with me. Hardly can anyone defeat one’s own nature.
6.36.12
यदि तावत् समुद्रे तु
सेतुर्बद्धो यदृच्छया ।
रामेण विस्मयः कोत्र
येन ते भयमागतम् ॥
It may be that Rāma has somehow managed to build a bridge over the ocean; what is so great about it, and why should one be scared of it?
6.36.13
स तु तीर्त्वार्णवं रामः
सह वानरसेनया ।
प्रतिजानामि ते सत्यम्
न जीवन् प्रतियास्यति ॥
Rāma, who has come, crossing the ocean along with the Vānara army, will not return alive, mark my word.
6.36.14
एवं ब्रुवाणं संरब्धम्
रुष्टं विज्ञाय रावणम् ।
व्रीडितो माल्यवान् वाक्यम्
नोत्तरं प्रत्यपद्यत ॥
When Rāvaṇa thus blurted out in response, Mālyavān realized that he was angry. Feeling humiliated, he made no effort to reply.
6.36.15
जयाशिषा च राजानम्
वर्धयित्वा यथोचितम् ।
माल्यवानभ्यनुज्ञातो
जगाम स्वं निवेशनम् ॥
Blessing the king for victory and prosperity in a suitable manner, and duly dismissed, Mālyavān went to his own residence.
6.36.16
रावणस्तु सहामात्यो
मन्त्रयित्वा विमृश्य च ।
लङ्कायामतुलां गुप्तिम्
कारयामास राक्षसः ॥
Then Rāvaṇa, the Rākshasa, considering everything and in deliberation with his ministers gave orders for the defense of Laṅkā.
6.36.17
स व्यादिदेश पूर्वस्याम्
प्रहस्तं द्वारि राक्षसम् ।
दक्षिणस्यां महावीर्यौ
महापार्श्वमहोदरौ ।
पश्चिमायामथो द्वारि
पुत्रमिन्द्रजितं तथा ।
व्यादिदोश महामायम्
बहुभी राक्षसैर्वृतम् ॥
He ordered the Rākshasa Prahasta to defend the eastern gate, Mahāpārṡwa and Mahōdara of immense valor to defend the southern gate, and his son Indrajit, a master of trickery, supported by a large contingent of Rākshasas, to defend the western gate.
6.36.19
उत्तरस्यां पुरद्वारि
व्यादिश्य शुकसारणौ ।
स्वयं चात्र भविष्यामि
मन्त्रिणस्तानुवाच ह ॥
Ordering Ṡuka and Sāraṇa to defend the northern gate, he told those ministers, ‘I myself will also be there.’
6.36.20
राक्षसं तु विरूपाक्षम्
महावीर्यपराक्रमम् ।
मध्यमेऽस्थापयद् गुल्मे
बहुभिः सह राक्षसैः ॥
And then he placed Virūpāksha of great valor and prowess along with many Rākshasas to defend the central area.
6.36.21
एवं विधानं लङ्कायाः
कृत्वा राक्षसपुङ्गवः ।
कृतकृत्यमिवात्मानम्
मन्यते कालचोदितः ॥
Making those arrangements, that bull among Rākshasas thought that he was done with what needed to be done, driven by his destiny.
6.36.22
विसर्जयामास ततः स मन्त्रिणो
विधानमाज्ञाप्य पुरस्य पुष्कलम् ।
जयाशिषा मन्त्रिगणेन पूजितो
विवेश चान्तःपुरमृद्धिमन्महत् ॥
Thus making ample arrangements for (the defense of) the city, receiving homage and wishes for victory from the ministers and dismissing them, he retired to his opulent home.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे षट्त्रिंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty sixth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
