Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 79
In this Sarga, the kingmakers go to Bharata and ask him to get crowned. Bharata says that it is in the tradition of their lineage for the eldest of the sons to become the king. And hence he would go to the forest to fetch Rāma to be the king. He orders the entire army to be prepared to go to the Vana with him. He orders workmen to build a road through the impassable forest.
Everyone feels happy and they praise Bharata and wish him well.
2.79.1
ततः प्रभातसमये
दिवसेऽथ चतुर्दशे ।
समेत्य राजकर्तारो
भरतं वाक्यमब्रुवन् ॥
At dawn on the fourteenth day, the kingmakers assembled and said to Bharata:
2.79.2
गतो दशरथस्स्वर्गम्
यो नो गुरुतरो गुरुः ।
रामं प्रव्राज्य वै ज्येष्ठम्
लक्ष्मणं च महाबलम् ॥
Daṡaratha, the supreme of our guides, has gone to Heaven, having exiled Rāma, the eldest, and Lakshmaṇa, the mighty strong.
2.79.3
त्वमद्य भव नो राजा
राजपुत्र महायशः ।
सङ्गत्या नापराध्नोति
राज्यमेतदनायकम् ॥
O prince of great renown, you must become our king now. This realm, though without a King, has not succumbed to strife or hostility, fortunately!
2.79.4
अभिषेचनिकं सर्वम्
इदमादाय राघव ।
प्रतीक्षते त्वां स्वजनः
श्रेणयश्च नृपात्मज ॥
Having procured all the materials for the crowning, your people and the representatives of the guilds wait upon you, O Prince!
2.79.5
राज्यं गृहाण भरत
पितृपैतामहं ध्रुवम् ।
अभिषेचय चात्मानम्
पाहि चास्मान्नरर्षभ ॥
Take the kingdom, O Bharata, that has come down as an indefeasible legacy that came from father to son. Crown yourself and protect us, O bull among men.'
2.79.6
अभिषेचनिकं भाण्डम्
कृत्वा सर्वं प्रदक्षिणम् ।
भरतस्तं जनं सर्वम्
प्रत्युवाच धृतव्रतः ॥
Circumambulating the materials assembled for the crowning, Bharata, steadfast in his mind, responded to all those people by saying:
2.79.7
ज्येष्ठस्य राजता नित्यम्
उचिता हि कुलस्य नः ।
नैवं भवन्तो मां वक्तुम्
अर्हन्ति कुशला जनाः ॥
In our family, crowning the eldest is considered to be the right thing to do. You, who knew this well, must not ask this of me.
2.79.8
रामः पूर्वो हि नो भ्राता
भविष्यति महीपतिः ।
अहं त्वरण्ये वत्स्यामि
वर्षाणि नव पञ्च च ॥
Rāma, our eldest brother, will be the lord of this land. And as for myself, I shall live in the forest for nine years and five.
2.79.9
युज्यतां महती सेना
चतुरङ्गमहाबला ।
आनयिष्याम्यहं ज्येष्ठम्
भ्रातरं राघवं वनात् ॥
Let a great army be made ready with all the four mighty divisions. I shall fetch our eldest brother Rāghava from the forest.
2.79.10
अभिषेचनिकं चैव
सर्वमेतदुपस्कृतम् ।
पुरस्कृत्य गमिष्यामि
रामहेतोर्वनं प्रति ॥
Keeping in front of me all these materials that are well prepared for the crowning, I shall go to the Vana for fetching Rāma.
2.79.11
तत्रैवं तं नरव्याघ्रम्
अभिषिच्य पुरस्कृतम् ।
आनेष्यामि तु वै रामम्
हव्यवाहमिवाध्वरात् ॥
Then, having crowned Rāma, the tiger among men, there itself, I shall fetch him, keeping him in front of me like the sacred fire from an Yajña.
2.79.12
न सकामां करिष्यामि
स्वामिमां मातृगन्धिनीम् ।
वने वत्स्याम्यहं दुर्गे
रामो राजा भविष्यति ॥
I shall not let this (woman) who is a mother to me only in name, have her wish. I shall live in the impenetrable forest and Rāma will become the King.
2.79.13
क्रियतां शिल्पिभिः पन्थाः
समानि विषमाणि च ।
रक्षिणश्चानुसम्यान्तु
पथि दुर्गविचारकाः ॥
Let skilled workmen be set to work, leveling the ground where it is uneven and laying the roads. Let them be helped by a protecting force that can navigate through the impenetrable areas.
2.79.14
एवं सम्भाषमाणं तम्
रामहेतोर्नृपात्मजम् ।
प्रत्युवाच जनस्सर्वः
श्रीमद्वाक्यमनुत्तमम् ॥
When the King's son spoke thus regarding Rāma, all the people responded to him in the best and splendid the words, saying:
2.79.15
एवं ते भाषमाणस्य
पद्मा श्रीरुपतिष्ठतात् ।
यस्त्वं ज्येष्ठे नृपसुते
पृथिवीं दातुमिच्छसि ॥
May the Goddess of Plenty, whose hand is adorned by a lotus, ever abide with you, for saying thus, and wishing to give the earth to the eldest son of the king.
2.79.16
अनुत्तमं तद्वचनं नृपात्मज-
प्रभाषितं संश्रवणे निश्यम्य च ।
प्रहर्षजास्तं प्रति बाष्पबिन्दवो
निपेतुरार्यानननेत्रसम्भवाः ॥
Hearing those unparalleled words uttered clear and loud by the King's son, tears of joy emerged from the eyes and rolled down the faces of the nobles.
2.79.17
ऊचुस्ते वचनमिदं निशम्य हृष्टा
स्सामात्यास्सपरिषदो वियातशोकाः ।
पन्थानं नरवर भक्तिमान् जनश्च
व्यादिष्टस्तव वचनाच्च शिल्पिवर्गः ॥
And they, along with ministers and the assembly, answered joyously, all their sorrow vanished: 'By your orders, O best of men, people loyal to you and many skilled workers have been asked to attend to the roads'.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकोनाशीतितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventy ninth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
