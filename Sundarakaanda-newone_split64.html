Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 64
In this Sarga, Dadhimukha returns to Madhuvana and apologizes to Aṅgada for trying to restrain him and the other Vānaras. Aṅgada and the Vānaras then fly to Kishkindhā. Meanwhile, Sugreeva tries to console Rāma, assuring him that the Vānaras must have found Seetā, and they would not have otherwise dared to enter Madhuvana, much less pillage it. As he was thus consoling, the Vānaras, led by Aṅgada and Hanumān, land there. Hanumān informs Rāma that he has seen Seetā. Hearing those Amṛt like words, Rāma becomes overwhelmed with joy, and looks at Hanumān with great regard.
5.64.1
सुग्रीवेणैवमुक्तस्तु
हृष्टो दधिमुखः कपिः ।
राघवं लक्ष्मणं चैव
सुग्रीवं चाभ्यवादयत् ॥
Thus told by Sugreeva, Dadhimukha felt happy and made his obeisance to Rāghava, Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva.
5.64.2
स प्रणम्य च सुग्रीवम्
राघवौ च महाबलौ ।
वानरैः सहितः शूरैः
दिवमेवोत्पपात ह ॥
Making prostrations to Sugreeva and to the mighty strong scions of Raghus, he leaped into the sky along with the valiant Vānaras.
5.64.3
स यथैवाऽगतः पूर्वं
तथैव त्वरितं गतः ।
निपत्य गगनाद्भूमौ
तद्वनं प्रविवेश ह ॥
He then went back as fast as he came. Descending from the sky on to the ground, he entered the Vana.
5.64.4
स प्रविष्टो मधुवनम्
ददर्श हरियूथपान् ।
विमदानुत्थितान्सर्वान्
वेपमानान् मदात्यये ॥
Reaching Madhuvana, he saw all the Vānara warriors getting over their hangover, but still feeling unsteady.
5.64.5
स तानुपागमद्वीरो
बद्ध्वा करपुटाञ्जलिम् ।
उवाच वचनं श्लक्ष्णम्
इदं हृष्टवदङ्गदम् ॥
Reaching them, the Veera, with his palms joined in reverence, said these words to Aṅgada in a soft and happy tone:
5.64.6
सौम्य रोषो न कर्तव्यो
यदेतत्परिवारितम् ।
अज्ञानाद्रक्षिभिः क्रोधात्
भवन्तः प्रतिषेधिताः ॥
O gentle one, please do not be angry for having been obstructed. These guards resisted you out of ignorance and anger.
5.64.7
युवराजस्त्वमीशश्च
वनस्यास्य महाबल ।
मौर्ख्यात्पूर्वं कृतो दोषः
तं भवान् क्षन्तुमर्हति ॥
You are the heir-apparent and have every right over this Vana, O strong one. They were fools to offend you earlier, be pleased to forgive them.
5.64.8
आख्यातं हि मया गत्वा
पितृव्यस्य तवानघ ।
इहोपयातं सर्वेषाम्
एतेषां वनचारिणाम् ॥
O faultless one! I went and told your uncle about all the Vana-rovers arriving here.
5.64.9
स त्वदागमनं श्रुत्वा
सहैभिर्हरियूथपैः ।
प्रहृष्टो न तु रुष्टोऽसौ
वनं श्रुत्वा प्रधर्षितम् ॥
Hearing about your arrival along with all the Vānara warriors and about the transgression into the Vana, he was rather delighted than becoming angry!
5.64.10
प्रहृष्टो मां पितृव्यस्ते
सुग्रीवो वानरेश्वरः ।
शीघ्रं प्रेषय सर्वांस्तान्
इति होवाच पार्थिवः ॥
Sugreeva, your uncle, the King and Lord of Vānaras, became happy and said, ‘bring all of them here at once!’
5.64.11
श्रुत्वा दधिमुखस्येदम्
वचनं श्लक्ष्णमङ्गदः ।
अब्रवीत्तान् हरिश्रेष्ठो
वाक्यं वाक्यविशारदः ॥
Hearing these gently spoken words of Dadhimukha, Aṅgada, the eminent Vānara, skilled in speech, said these words to them (the Vānaras):
5.64.12
शङ्के श्रुतोऽयं वृत्तान्तो
रामेण हरियूथपाः ।
तत्क्षमं नेह नः स्थातुम्
कृते कार्ये परन्तपाः ॥
I guess, O Vānara Warriors, O tormentors of enemies, that Rāma has come to know about everything. It is no more right for us to waste time here having accomplished the mission.
5.64.13
पीत्वा मधु यथाकामम्
विश्रान्ता वनचारिणः ।
किं शेषं गमनं तत्र
सुग्रीवो यत्र मे गुरुः ॥
Well, now that all the Vana-rovers have enjoyed the liquor as they pleased and have rested, what remains to be done is to go to Sugreeva, my revered elder.
5.64.14
सर्वे यथा मां वक्ष्यन्ति
समेत्य हरियूथपाः ।
तथास्मि कर्ता कर्तव्ये
भवद्भिः परवानहम् ॥
I shall do whatever all of you, the Vānara warrior chiefs, together, decide. I am at your command regarding what shall be done.
5.64.15
नाज्ञापयितुमीशोऽहम्
युवराजोऽस्मि यद्यपि ।
अयुक्तं कृतकर्माणो
यूयं धर्षयितुं मया ॥
I am not here to lord over you, even though I am the crown prince. It is not proper for me to challenge those who have accomplished their mission.
5.64.16
ब्रुवतश्चाङ्गदस्यैवम्
श्रुत्वा वचनमव्ययम् ।
प्रहृष्टमनसो वाक्यम्
इदमूचुर्वनौकसः ॥
Hearing these words of eternal virtue spoken by Aṅgada, the Vana-dwellers said these words, their hearts in delight:
5.64.17
एवं वक्ष्यति को राजन्
प्रभुस्सन्वानरर्षभ ।
ऐश्वर्यमदमत्तो हि
सर्वोऽहमिति मन्यते ॥
O bull among Vānaras! O King! Who would ever speak thus, being a lord! Everyone, intoxicated with power, thinks, ‘It is all about me!’
5.64.18
तव चेदं सुसदृशम्
वाक्यं नान्यस्य कस्यचित् ।
सन्नतिर्हि तवाख्याति
भविष्यच्छुभयोग्यताम् ॥
To say something like this is possible only for you, and for no one else. Your modesty forebodes your readiness for the great future that awaits you.
5.64.19
सर्वे वयमपि प्राप्ताः
तत्र गन्तुं कृतक्षणाः ।
स यत्र हरिवीराणाम्
सुग्रीवः पतिरव्ययः ॥
Now, it is the right time for all of us who have arrived here, to go to Sugreeva, the imperishable Lord of the valiant Vānaras.
5.64.20
त्वया ह्यनुक्तैर्हरिभिः
नैव शक्यं पदात्पदम् ।
क्वचिद्गन्तुं हरिश्रेष्ठ
ब्रूमः सत्यमिदं तु ते ॥
O best among Vānaras, let us tell you the truth: Without your explicit orders, the Vānaras will not move even a single step.
5.64.21
एवं तु वदतां तेषाम्
अङ्गदः प्रत्युवाच ह ।
बाढं गच्छाम इत्युक्त्वा
खमुत्पेतुर्महाबलः ॥
To those who were speaking thus, Aṅgada responded, ‘Certainly, let us go!’ Saying thus, he leaped towards the sky.
5.64.22
उत्पतन्तमनूत्पेतुः
सर्वे ते हरियूथपाः ।
कृत्वाकाशं निराकाशम्
यन्त्रोत्क्षिप्ता इवाचलाः ॥
All those Vānara warriors leapt along with him, covering the sky leaving hardly any gap, looking as if a mountain was lifted and held up by a machine.
What kind of machines did they used to have in those days!
5.64.23
तेऽम्बरं सहसोत्पत्य
वेगवन्तः प्लवङ्गमाः ।
विनदन्तो महानादम्
घना वातेरिता यथा ॥
Those swift Vānaras leapt into the sky quickly, making roaring sounds like clouds driven by the wind.
5.64.24
अङ्गदे ह्यननुप्राप्ते
सुग्रीवो वानराधिपः ।
उवाच शोकोपहतम्
रामं कमललोचनम् ॥
While Aṅgada was yet to arrive, Sugreeva, the king of Vānaras said to Rāma of lotus eyes who was oppressed by grief:
5.64.25
समाश्वसिहि भद्रं ते
दृष्टा देवी न संशयः ।
नागन्तुमिह शक्यं तैः
अतीते समये हि नः ॥
Please be at ease, may all bode well for you! Seetā was found, without a doubt! Otherwise, they would dare not come here after the set time limit has expired!
5.64.26
न मत्सकाशमागच्छेत्
कृत्ये हि विनिपातिते ।
युवराजो महाबाहुः
प्लवतां प्रवरोऽङ्गदः ॥
Aṅgada, the crown prince of mighty arm, the best among the Vānaras, would not come into my presence if the mission had been ruined.
5.64.27
यद्यप्यकृतकृत्यानाम्
ईदृशस्स्यादुपक्रमः ।
भवेत्स दीनवदनो
भ्रान्तविप्लुतमानसः ॥
Aṅgada would have had a sad face and his heart would have been broken and disoriented, if they went on doing something like this without having accomplished the mission.
5.64.28
पितृपैतामहं चैतत्
पूर्वकैरभिरक्षितम् ।
न मे मधुवनं हन्यात्
अहृष्टः प्लवगेश्वरः ।
कौसल्यासुप्रजा राम
समाश्वसिहि सुव्रत ॥
If that Lord of Vānaras (Aṅgada) was not overwhelmed with joy, he would have not pillaged the Madhuvana that came down from fathers and grandfathers and was guarded well (through the ages) by all the ancestors.
O Rāma of great discipline, O the best son of Kousalyā, please be at ease!
5.64.29
दृष्टा देवी न सन्देहो
न चान्येन हनूमता ।
न ह्यन्यः कर्मणो हेतुः
साधनेऽस्य हनूमतः ॥
Seetā is found, without a doubt! It must be by none other than Hanumān, for no one else could have accomplished such a task.
5.64.30
हनूमति हि सिद्धिश्च
मतिश्च मतिसत्तमः ।
व्यवसायश्च वीर्यं च
सूर्ये तेज इव ध्रुवम् ॥
O astute one! Dedication, intelligence, enterprise, valor are native to Hanumān like radiance is to the sun.
5.64.31
जाम्बवान्यत्र नेता स्यात्
अङ्गदश्च बलेश्वरः ।
हनुमांश्चाप्यधिष्ठाता
न तस्य गतिरन्यथा ।
मा भूश्चिन्तासमायुक्तः
सम्प्रत्यमितविक्रमः ॥
With Jāmbavān leading, Aṅgada heading the army, Hanumān presiding over the effort, the outcome cannot be anything different. Please do not get worried now, O you of limitless prowess!
5.64.33
ततः किलकिलाशब्दम्
शुश्रावासन्नमम्बरे ।
हनुमत्कर्मदृप्तानाम्
नर्दतां काननौकसाम् ।
किष्किन्धामुपयातानाम्
सिद्धिं कथयतामिव ॥
Then a chirping sound was heard in the sky, indicating the success of the roaring forest-dwellers headed towards Kishkindhā, proud of the accomplishment of Hanumān.
5.64.34
ततश्श्रुत्वा निनादं तम्
कपीनां कपिसत्तमः ।
आयताञ्चितलाङ्गूलः
सोऽभवद्धृष्टमानसः ॥
Hearing the sounds of those Vānaras, the tail of the eminent Vānara (Sugreeva) became erect and his heart became joyful.
5.64.35
आजग्मुस्तेऽपि हरयो
रामदर्शनकांक्षिणः ।
अङ्गदं पुरतः कृत्वा
हनूमन्तं च वानरम् ॥
Then those Vānaras, who were eager to see Rāma, came down, led by Aṅgada and Hanumān.
5.64.36
तेऽङ्गदप्रमुखा वीराः
प्रहृष्ठाश्च मुदान्विताः ।
निपेतुर्हरिराजस्य
समीपे राघवस्य च ॥
Then those Veeras such as Aṅgada landed, happy and delighted, near the king of Vānaras and Rāghava.
5.64.37
हनुमांश्च महाबाहुः
प्रणम्य शिरसा ततः ।
नियतामक्षतां देवीम्
राघवाय न्यवेदयत् ॥
Then, Hanumān of mighty arm, saluting with his head bowed, informed Rāghava that the Dēvi was able to sustain her routine and was unharmed.
5.64.38
दृष्टा देवीति हनुमत्
वदनादमृतोपमम् ।
आकर्ण्य वचनं रामो
हर्षमाप सलक्ष्मणः ॥
Hearing the Amṛt like words ‘Seetā is found!’ coming out of the mouth of Hanumān, he, along with Lakshmaṇa, felt extremely happy.
5.64.39
निश्चितार्थं ततस्तस्मिन्
सुग्रीवं पवनात्मजे ।
लक्ष्मणः प्रीतिमान् प्रीतम्
बहुमानादवैक्षत ॥
Lakshmaṇa looked with affectionate regard at Sugreeva, who was pleased that the trust he had in the son of Vāyu was vindicated.
5.64.40
प्रीत्या च रममाणोऽथ
राघवः परवीरहा ।
बहुमानेन महता
हनुमन्तमवैक्षत ॥
Extremely pleased, Rāghava, destroyer of enemy warriors, beheld Hanumān with great regard.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे चतुःषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty fourth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
