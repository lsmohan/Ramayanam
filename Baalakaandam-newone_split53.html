Baala Kaanda - Sarga 53
This Sarga starts with a great feast given by Vasishṭha to Viṡwāmitra and his army, courtesy Ṡabala. But soon after the feast, things take a different turn, as Viṡwāmitra asks (almost demands) Vasishṭha to give him Ṡabala.
This Sarga gives the taste of Vālmeeki's pen, for the first time, in describing the behavior of a person gripped by emotions.
Viṡwāmitra first stakes it as his right to have Ṡabala. Then he tries to buy it out by offering increasingly ridiculous amounts of compensation, almost with the compulsion of a gambler.
I have been in situations where I have witnessed two senior executives fighting with each other like children, unable to yield from their positions, with repercussions that lasted far longer than anyone would have expected. It is ugly and it is difficult to be there. Something of that sort, only in a much bigger scale, is happening here.
Again, as is characteristic of Vālmeeki, he leaves us in suspense, at the end of this Sarga of an impassioned and difficult conversation between two gargantuan personalities.
1.53.1
एवमुक्ता वसिष्ठेन
शबला शत्रुसूदन ।
विदधे कामधुक्कामान्
यस्य यस्य यथेप्सितम् ।
इक्षून् मधूंस्तथा लाजान्
मैरेयांश्च वरासवान् ।
पानानि च महार्हाणि
भक्ष्यांश्चोच्चावचांस्तथा ।
उष्णाढ्यस्यौदनस्यात्र
राशयः पर्वतोपमाः ।
मृष्टान्नानि च सूपाश्च
दधिकुल्यास्तथैव च ।
नानास्वादुरसानां च
षाडवानां तथैव च ।
भाजनानि सुपूर्णानि
गौडानि च सहस्रशः ॥
O annihilator of enemies (Rāma)! Upon that request from Vasishṭha, Ṡabala made various items ready according to the wishes and preferences of each person, including sugar, honey, popcorn, wines and other exquisite drinks filled in pitchers of various shapes and sizes, a great variety of dishes of food, mountains of hot steaming rice and other great varieties of grain, items cooked with various dals, thousands of vessels full of tasty items of all the six flavors and items made with jaggery and there was an abundant flow of yogurt.
1.53.5
सर्वमासीत्सुसन्तुष्टम्
हृष्टपुष्टजनायुतम् ।
विश्वामित्रबलं राम
वसिष्ठेनाभितर्पितम् ॥
O Rāma! Thus served and satisfied by Vasishṭha, the entire army of Viṡwāmitra became an ocean of fulfilled, happy and contented souls.
1.53.6
विश्वामित्रोऽपि राजर्षिः
हृष्टः पुष्टस्तदाऽभवत् ।
सान्तःपुरवरो राजा
सब्राह्मणपुरोहितः ॥
The Rājarshi Viṡwāmitra also, along with his close confidants and Brāhmaṇas and Purōhitas was very satisfied and happy.
1.53.7
सामात्यो मन्त्रिसहितः
सभृत्यः पूजितस्तदा ।
युक्तः परमहर्षेण
वसिष्ठमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
That properly respected, contented and happy, Viṡwāmitra, along with his ministers and staff, said to Vasishṭha:
1.53.8
पूजितोऽहं त्वया ब्रह्मन्
पूजार्हेण सुसत्कृतः ।
श्रूयतामभिधास्यामि
वाक्यं वाक्यविशारद ॥
O Brahman! O honorable and prudent one! I have been treated well and honored by you. I would like to say something to you.
1.53.9
गवां शतसहस्रेण
दीयतां शबला मम ।
रत्नं हि भगवन्नेतत्
रत्नहारी च पार्थिवः ।
तस्मान्मे शबलां देहि
ममैषा धर्मतो द्विज ॥
O Brāhmaṇa! O Bhagawan! This Ṡabala is a jewel. Jewels of the land belong to the king. Hence give me this Ṡabala as per the provision. I will give you one hundred thousand cows in exchange.
If you happen to buy a thousand acres of land, and later discover that there are diamonds and gold naturally occurring way underneath the surface, does it belong to you?
The laws of various lands at various times varied on this question.
Traditional law in India holds that anything that is excavated from underneath the ground belongs to the nation/state/king.
Viṡwāmitra is clearly subverting that provision in making this demand.
1.53.10
एवमुक्तस्तु भगवान्
वसिष्ठो मुनिसत्तमः ।
विश्वामित्रेण धर्मात्मा
प्रत्युवाच महीपतिम् ॥
The Dharmātma, the best among Munis, the Bhagawan Vasishṭha responded to Viṡwāmitra's words by saying:
1.53.11
नाहं शतसहस्रेण
नापि कोटिशतैर्गवाम् ।
राजन् दास्यामि शबलाम्
राशिभी रजतस्य वा ॥
I cannot give away Ṡabala in exchange for a hundred thousand cows. Not even for a hundred crores of them. Nor for mountains of silver.
1.53.12
न परित्यागमर्हेयम्
मत्सकाशादरिन्दम ।
शाश्वती शबला मह्यम्
कीर्तिरात्मवतो यथा ॥
O tormentor of foes! Ṡabala cannot be separated from me. She will be with me forever, like the reputation of a conscientious person.
1.53.13
अस्यां हव्यं च कव्यं च
प्राणयात्रा तथैव च ।
आयत्तमग्निहोत्रं च
बलिर्होमस्तथैव च ॥
Offerings to gods (Havya), offerings to Pitṛ Dēvatas (Kavya), offerings to Agni, offerings to all beings, the needs of Hōma and the provisions for my very own livelihood are dependent on this cow.
1.53.14
स्वाहाकारवषट्कारौ
विद्याश्च विविधास्तथा ।
आयत्तमत्र राजर्षे
सर्वमेतन्न संशयः ॥
O Rājarshi! For that matter, the entire body of ritual works including Swāha and Vashaṭ are dependent on this cow.
1.53.15
सर्वस्वमेतत्सत्येन
मम तुष्टिकरी सदा ।
कारणैर्बहुभी राजन्
न दास्ये शबलां तव ॥
O King! This cow is everything to me. She always keeps me contented and at peace. For these and many more reasons I would not be able to give away Ṡabala to you.
1.53.16
वसिष्ठेनैवमुक्तस्तु
विश्वामित्रोऽब्रवीत्ततः ।
संरब्धतरमत्यर्थम्
वाक्यं वाक्यविशारदः ॥
Viṡwāmitra, without having to pause for words, immediately responded to that Vasishṭha, saying:
1.53.17
हैरण्यकक्ष्याग्रैवेयान्
सुवर्णाङ्कुशभूषितान् ।
ददामि कुञ्जराणां ते
सहस्राणि चतुर्दश ॥
I will give you fourteen thousand elephants decked with golden chains around their waists and necks along with golden goads.
1.53.18
हैरण्यानां रथानां ते
श्वेताश्वानां चतुर्युजाम् ।
ददामि ते शतान्यष्टौ
किङ्किणीकविभूषितान् ॥
I will give you eight hundred golden chariots adorned with jingle bells, each with four white horses.
1.53.19
हयानां देशजातानाम्
कुलजानां महौजसाम् ।
सहस्रमेकं दश च
ददामि तव सुव्रत ॥
O Suvrata! I will give you eleven thousand Of the strongest, fastest and best breed of horses born in the country.
1.53.20
नानावर्णविभक्तानाम्
वयःस्थानां तथैव च ।
ददाम्येकां गवां कोटिम्
शबला दीयतां मम ॥
I will give one crore young cows in a variety of colors. May Ṡabala be given to me.
1.53.21
यावदिच्छसि रत्नं वा
हिरण्यं वा द्विजोत्तम ।
तावद्ददामि तत्सर्वम्
शबला दीयतां मम ॥
O best among Brāhmaṇas! I will give you gold, precious stones and whatever else you would like. May Ṡabala be given to me.
1.53.22
एवमुक्तस्तु भगवान्
विश्वामित्रेण धीमता ।
न दास्यामीति शबलाम्
प्राह राजन् कथञ्चन ॥
Hearing those words of astute Viṡwāmitra, Bhagawan Vasishṭha said: O king! No matter what, I will not give Ṡabala.
1.53.23
एतदेव हि मे रत्नम्
एतदेव हि मे धनम् ।
एतदेव हि सर्वस्वम्
एतदेव हि जीवितम् ॥
This is my jewel. This is my riches. This is my life. This is everything to me.
1.53.24
दर्शश्च पूर्णमासश्च
यज्ञाश्चैवाप्तदक्षिणाः ।
एतदेव हि मे राजन्
विविधाश्च क्रियास्तथा ॥
O King! This, for me, is Darṡa Yajña, Poorṇa Māsa Yajña and all Yajñas involving honorarium. For that matter, this is the basis for every activity.
Darṡa refers to the fire ritual done on a new moon (Amāvāsya) day. Poorṇa Māsa Yajña refers to the fire ritual done on a full moon (Pourṇami) day.
1.53.25
अदोमूलाः क्रियाः सर्वा
मम राजन्न संशयः ।
बहुना किं प्रलापेन
न दास्ये कामदोहिनीम् ॥
O King! This is the ultimate basis for all of my activities. There is no use of this extensive conversation. I will not give away this Ṡabala that gives me everything.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे त्रिपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty third Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
