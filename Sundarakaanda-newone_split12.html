Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 12
In this Sarga, not finding Seetā, Hanumān thinks, for a moment, that Seetā could be dead, killed by the Rākshasas as she resisted them in order to protect her chastity and honor. He feels dejected at the thought of going back and facing the Vānaras, only to confess that he could not find Seetā.
However, he shakes off that thought immediately and casts away the gloom that had descended on him. He resolves to search every inch of every area under the rule of Rāvaṇa. He sees many beautiful and lovely women as well as many ugly women, but not Seetā. He feels very sad and falls into deep thinking.
5.12.1
स तस्य मध्ये भवनस्य मारुतिः
लतागृहांश्चित्रगृहान्निशागृहान् ।
जगाम सीतां प्रति दर्शनोत्सुको
न चैव तां पश्यति चारुदर्शनाम् ॥
In that mansion, the son of Vāyu passed through arbors, art-galleries and bedrooms, searching earnestly for Seetā, but nowhere could he find her of the enchanting looks.
5.12.2
स चिन्तयामास ततो महाकपिः 
प्रियामपश्यन्रघुनन्दनस्य ताम् ॥
Not finding her, the beloved of the scion of Raghus, that great Vānara then reflected:
5.12.2
ध्रुवं हि सीता म्रियते यथा न मे
विचिन्वतो दर्शनमेति मैथिली ।
सा राक्षसानां प्रवरेण जानकी 
स्वशीलसंरक्ष्णतत्परा सती ।
अनेन नूनं प्रति दुष्टकर्मणा 
हता भवेदार्यपथे परे स्थिता ॥
I could not find Maithili even after searching (everywhere). For sure, she must be dead.
Jānaki, the faithful wife, resolved to keep her virtue unsullied at all cost, firm in her adherence to the noble code of honor, must have been done to death by this monstrously wicked Chief of Rākshasas.
5.12.4
विरूपरूपा विकृता विवर्चसो 
महानना दीर्घविरूपदर्शनाः ।
समीक्ष्य सा राक्षसराजयोषितो 
भयाद्विनष्टा जनकेश्वरात्मजा ॥
The daughter of Lord Janaka should have died of sheer fright from the sight of the women of the Rākshasa king, who look like zombies with deformed and ugly bodies, huge faces and abnormally wide and crooked eyes.
5.12.5
सीतामदृष्ट्वा ह्यनवाप्य पौरुषं
विहृत्य कालं सह वानरैश्चिरम् ।
न मेऽस्ति सुग्रीवसमीपगा गतिः
सुतीक्ष्णदण्डो बलवांश्च वानरः ॥
Having wasted time in idle diversions along with the other Vānaras, instead of finding Seetā and proving my prowess, I dare not go anywhere near that strong Vānara Sugreeva, who (is known for) inflicting draconian punishment.
5.12.6
दृष्टमन्तःपुरं सर्वम्
दृष्टा रावणयोषितः ।
न सीता दृश्यते साध्वी
वृथा जातो मम श्रमः ॥
I have checked the inner apartments thoroughly and seen all the women of Rāvaṇa; but Seetā, the good natured woman, is nowhere to be seen; all my efforts are wasted.
5.12.7
किं नु मां वानराः सर्वे
गतं वक्ष्यन्ति सङ्गताः ।
गत्वा तत्र त्वया वीर
किं कृतं तद्वदस्व नः ॥
What can I say of what the assembled Vānaras will ask, when I return? “Tell us, O Veera! What have you accomplished, having gone there?”
5.12.8
अदृष्ट्वा किं प्रवक्ष्यामि
तामहं जनकात्मजाम् ।
ध्रुवं प्रायमुपैष्यन्ति
कालस्य व्यतिवर्तने ॥
Not having seen her, the daughter of Janaka, what can I tell them? They will certainly go on a fast unto death with the set time limit having expired.
5.12.9
किं वा वक्ष्यति वृद्धश्च
जाम्बवानाङ्गदश्च सः ।
गतं पारं समुद्रस्य
वानराश्च समागताः ॥
Upon my reaching the other side of the ocean, what will Jāmbavān of ripe age say, and what will Aṅgada and the assembled Vānaras say?
5.12.10
अनिर्वेदः श्रियो मूलम्
अनिर्वेदः परं सुखम् ।
अनिर्वेदो हि सततम्
सर्वार्थेषु प्रवर्तकः ।
करोति सफलं जन्तोः
कर्म यत्तत्करोति सः ॥
Keeping the gloomy spirits at bay is essential for prosperity and critical for happiness. It is needed to carry out any affair, at any time. It will bring success to any endeavor that a man takes on.
5.12.11
तस्मादनिर्वेदकृतम्
यत्नं चेष्टेऽहमुत्तमम् ।
अदृष्टांश्च विचेष्यामि
देशान्रावणपालितान् ॥
Hence, I shall cast aside the gloom and put forth my best effort. I shall search all the places that are governed by Rāvaṇa, which I have not seen so far.
5.12.12
आपानशाला विचिताः
तथा पुष्पगृहाणि च ।
चित्रशालाश्च विचिता
भूयः क्रीडागृहाणि च ।
निष्कुटान्तररथ्याश्च
विमानानि च सर्वशः ॥
I have searched the drinking saloons, the arbors, the art galleries, the recreation rooms, the walkways in pleasure gardens and all the towers.
5.12.14
इति सञ्चिन्त्य भूयोऽपि
विचेतुमुपचक्रमे ।
भूमीगृहांश्चैत्यगृहान्
गृहातिगृहकानपि ॥
Thinking thus, he began searching once again the basement cells, the outhouses and palatial houses.
5.12.15
उत्पतन्निष्पतंश्चापि
तिष्ठन्गच्छन् पुनः पुनः ।
अपावृण्वंश्च द्वाराणि
कवाटान्यवघाटयन् ।
प्रविशन्निष्पतंश्चापि
प्रपतन्नुत्पतन्नपि ।
सर्वमप्यवकाशं स
विचचार महाकपिः ॥
Jumping up and coming down, stopping and proceeding again and again, opening the entrances by flinging the doors, going in and coming out, getting all the way down and climbing up, the great Vānara went around every place.
5.12.17
चतुरङ्गुलमात्रोऽपि
नावकाशः स विद्यते ।
रावणान्तःपुरे तस्मिन्
यं कपिर्न जगाम सः ॥
There was not even a space of four inches in the inner apartments of Rāvaṇa that the Vānara did not cover.
5.12.18
प्राकारान्तररथ्याश्च
वेदिकाश्चैत्यसंश्रयाः ।
दीर्घिकाः पुष्करिण्यश्च
सर्वं तेनावलोकितम् ॥
Every place, from the streets inside the ramparts, to the raised platforms around the trees at cross roads, to the stepped wells, to the lotus pools was checked by him.
5.12.19
राक्षस्यो विविधाकारा
विरूपा विकृतास्तथा ।
दृष्टा हनुमता तत्र
न तु सा जनकात्मजा ॥
Hanumān saw Rākshasa women of every shape and size, ugly and deformed, but could not find her, the daughter of Janaka.
5.12.20
रूपेणाप्रतिमा लोके
वरा विद्याधरस्त्रियः ।
दृष्टा हनुमता तत्र
न तु राघवनन्दिनी ॥
The best of the Vidyādhara women, who were unequalled in their beauty were seen by Hanumān, but not the one who was the delight of Rāghava.
5.12.21
नागकन्या वरारोहाः
पूर्णचन्द्र निभाननाः ।
दृष्टा हनुमता तत्र
न तु सीता सुमध्यमा ॥
Young Nāga dames of beautiful hips and moon-bright faces were seen by Hanumān there, but not Seetā of slender waist.
5.12.22
प्रमथ्य राक्षसेन्द्रेण
नागकन्या बलाद्धृताः ।
दृष्टा हनुमता तत्र
न सा जनकनन्दिनी ॥
Young Nāga dames, who were carried off by the Lord of Rākshasas by force were seen by Hanumān there, but not the one who was the delight of Janaka.
5.12.23
सोऽपश्यंस्तां महाबाहुः
पश्यंश्चान्या वरस्त्रियः ।
विषसाद मुहुर्धीमान्
हनुमान् मारुतात्मजः ॥
Hanumān of mighty arms, the sagacious son of Vāyu, felt very depressed again and again, not finding her among the many other lovely women he saw.
5.12.24
उद्योगं वानरेन्द्राणाम्
प्लवनं सागरस्य च ।
व्यर्थं वीक्ष्यानिलसुतः
चिन्तां पुनरुपागमत् ॥
Seeing the efforts of the chiefs of Vānaras and (his own) crossing of the ocean go waste, the son of Vāyu felt very sad again.
5.12.25
अवतीर्य विमानाच्च
हनुमान् मारुतात्मजः ।
चिन्तामुपजगामाथ
शोकोपहतचेतनः ॥
Getting down from the Vimāna, his heart stricken with grief, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, then fell into deep thinking.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे द्वादशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twelfth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
