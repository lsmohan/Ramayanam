Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 44
In this Sarga, Jambumāli attacks Hanumān and a fight ensues between them, with Jambumāli striking Hanumān with arrows and Hanumān hurling boulders, trees and Parigha at him. Hit by a Parigha, Jambumāli gets killed and his body and ornaments get reduced to a powder, with no trace of his head, chariot or horses left. Rāvaṇa, finding it difficult to accept the death of Jambumāli and the Kiṅkaras before him, orders the sons of his ministers to fight Hanumān.
5.44.1
सन्दिष्टो राक्षसेन्द्रेण
प्रहस्तस्य सुतो बली ।
जम्बुमाली महादंष्ट्रो
निर्जगाम धनुर्धरः ।
रक्तमाल्याम्बरधरः
स्रग्वी रुचिरकुण्डलः ।
महान्विवृत्तनयनः
चण्डस्समरदुर्जयः ।
धनुश्शक्रधनुः प्रख्यम्
महद्रुचिरसायकम् ।
विष्फारयाणो वेगेन
वज्राशनिसमस्वनम् ॥
Ordered by the Rākshasa King, the strong Jambumāli, son of Prahasta, of huge fangs and big bulging eyes, clad in reddish garment, wearing red flowers, garlands and bright ear-pendants, armed with a bow comparable to that of Ṡakra and to Vajra and thunderbolt, and with the bright arrows that went along with it, marched out, blazing and invincible in battle.
5.44.4
तस्य विष्फारघोषेण
धनुषो महता दिशः ।
प्रदिशश्च नभश्चैव
सहसा समपूर्यत ॥
The loud sound from the twanging of the string of that bow instantly reverberated in all directions and in between and in the sky.
5.44.5
रथेन खरयुक्तेन
तमागतमुदीक्ष्य सः ।
हनुमान्वेगसम्पन्नो
जहर्ष च ननाद च ॥
Seeing him, who came on a chariot drawn by mules, Hanumān, of terrific speed rejoiced and gave a loud shout.
5.44.6
तं तोरणविटङ्कस्थम्
हनुमन्तं महाकपिम् ।
जम्बुमाली महाबाहुः
विव्याध निशितैश्शरैः ॥
Jambumāli of the mighty arm then struck Hanumān, the great Vānara, who sat on the top of the archway, with sharp arrows.
5.44.7
अर्धचन्द्रेण वदने
शिरस्येकेन कर्णिना ।
बाह्वोर्विव्याध नाराचैः
दशभिस्तं कपीश्वरम् ॥
He struck the lordly Vānara on the face with a half-moon-shaped arrow, on the head with a ear-shaped arrow and on the shoulders with ten Nārāca arrows.
5.44.8
तस्य तच्छुशुभे ताम्रम्
शरेणाभिहतं मुखम् ।
शरदीवाम्बुजं फुल्लम्
विद्धं भास्कररश्मिना ॥
His red face shone, struck by the arrow, like a lotus opening up, hit by the rays of the post-monsoon sun.
5.44.9
तत्तस्य रक्तं रक्तेन
रञ्जितं शुशुभे मुखम् ।
यथाकाशे महापद्मम्
सिक्तं चन्दनबिन्दुभिः ॥
His face, which was naturally red, became even more reddish with the blood, shining like a giant lotus positioned in the sky, sprinkled with drops of red sandal.
5.44.10
चुकोप बाणाभिहतो
राक्षसस्य महाकपिः ।
ततः पार्श्वेऽतिविपुलाम्
ददर्श महतीं शिलाम् ॥
The great Vānara, hit by the arrows, became angry with the Rākshasa, when his eyes fell on a big hefty boulder lying beside him.
5.44.11
तरसा तां समुत्पाट्य
चिक्षेप बलवद्भली ।
तां शरैर्दशभिः क्रुद्धः
ताडयामास राक्षसः ॥
The strong one, with his power, lifted that boulder and threw it with all his force. The Rākshasa, reacting angrily, hit it with ten arrows.
5.44.12
विपन्नं कर्म तद्दृष्ट्वा
हनुमांश्चण्डविक्रमः ।
सालं विपुलमुत्पाट्य
भ्रामयामास वीर्यवान् ॥
Seeing that his effort had failed, the valiant Hanumān of raving prowess, uprooted a huge Sāla tree and gave it a whirl.
5.44.13
भ्रामयन्तं कपिं दृष्ट्वा
सालवृक्षं महाबलम् ।
चिक्षेप सुबहून्बाणान्
जम्बुमाली महाबलः ॥
Seeing the mighty strong Vānara whirl the Sāla tree, Jambumāli of immense strength shot many arrows.
5.44.14
सालं चतुर्भिश्चिच्छेद
वानरं पञ्चभिर्भुजे ।
उरस्येकेन बाणेन
दशभिस्तु स्तनान्तरे ॥
He broke down the Sāla tree with four arrows and hit the Vānara with five arrows on his arms, with one arrow on his chest and with ten between his breast plates.
5.44.15
स शरैः पूरिततनुः
क्रोधेन महता वृतः ।
तमेव परिघं गृह्य
भ्रामयामास वेगितः ॥
With his body covered by arrows, and taken over by a great outrage, he took the same Parigha and swiftly whirled it.
5.44.16
अतिवेगोऽतिवेगेन
भ्रामयित्वा बलोत्कटः ।
परिघं पातयामास
जम्बुमालेर्महोरसि ॥
And he of immense speed and overweening strength whirled the Parigha with great speed and hurled it on the mighty chest of Jambumāli.
5.44.17
तस्य चैव शिरो नास्ति
न बाहू न च जानुनी ।
न धनुर्न रथो नाश्वाः
तत्रादृश्यन्त नेषवः ॥
His head was no more. Neither his arms, nor his knees, nor the bow or the chariot or the horses nor his arrows could be seen there anymore.
5.44.18
स हतस्सहसा तेन
जम्बुमाली महाबलः ।
पपात निहतो भूमौ
चूर्णिताङ्गविभूषणः ॥
The mighty strong Jambumāli, killed, fell down immediately on the ground with his limbs and ornaments reduced to a powder.
5.44.19
जम्बुमालिं च निहतम्
किङ्करांश्च महाबलान् ।
चुक्रोध रावणश्शुत्वा
कोपसंरक्तलोचनः ॥
Hearing the killing of Jambumāli and the mighty strong Kiṅkaras Rāvaṇa became outraged, his eyes turning red in anger.
5.44.20
स रोषसंवर्तितताम्रलोचनः
प्रहस्तपुत्त्रे निहते महाबले ।
अमात्यपुत्त्रानतिवीर्यविक्रमान्
समादिदेशाशु निशाचरेश्वरः ॥
With the mighty strong son of Prahasta killed, the lord of the Night-rovers, rolling his eyes in anger and resentment, ordered out the sons of his ministers of extreme valor and prowess (to fight Hanumān).
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे चतुश्चत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty fourth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
