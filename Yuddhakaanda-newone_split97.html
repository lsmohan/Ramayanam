Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 97
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa, enraged by the death of Virūpāksha, sends Mahōdara to fight the battle. Mahōdara wreaks havoc on the Vānara army, which flees and seeks protection from Sugreeva. Sugreeva then fights with Mahōdara. A terrible duel ensues between them, at the end of which, Sugreeva, snatching a sword that is lying on the ground, cuts off the head of Mahōdara.
6.97.1
हन्यमाने बले तूर्णम्
अन्योन्यं ते महामृधे ।
सरसीव महाघर्मे
सोपक्षीणे बभूवतुः ॥
As the armies went on slaughtering each other in that fierce battle, they became smaller and smaller, like lakes in a scorching summer.
6.97.2
स्वबलस्य तु घातेन
विरूपाक्षवधेन च ।
बभूव द्विगुणं क्रुद्धो
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ॥
With his forces decimated, and with Virūpāksha slain, Rāvaṇa, the king of Rākshasas, became doubly frustrated.
6.97.3
प्रक्षीणं तु बलं दृष्ट्वा
वध्यमानं वलीमुखैः ।
बभूवास्य व्यथा युद्धे
दृष्ट्वा दैवविपर्ययम् ।
उवाच च समीपस्थम्
महोदरमरिन्दमम् ॥
Seeing his army shrunk a great deal slaughtered by the Vānaras, and realizing that fate had turned against him, he was in deep agony.
He said to Mahōdara, a subduer of foe, who was by his side:
6.97.4
अस्मिन् काले महाबाहो
जयाशा त्वयि मे स्थिता ।
जहि शत्रुचमूं वीर
दर्शयाद्य पराक्रमम् ।
भर्तृपिण्डस्य कालोऽयम्
निर्देष्टुं साधु युध्यताम् ॥
At this moment, O you of mighty arm, my hopes of victory lie in you. Show your prowess, O Veera and slay the enemy forces! This is the time to payback your debt to the master whose salt you have eaten. Put up a good fight!
6.97.6
एवमुक्तस्तथेत्युक्त्वा
राक्षसेन्द्रो महोदरः ।
प्रविवेशारिसेनां ताम्
पतङ्ग इव पावकम् ॥
Thus told by the lord of the Rākshasas, Mahōdara said, 'Yes, so shall I do!' and entered the enemy forces like a moth flying into the fire.
6.97.7
ततः स कदनं चक्रे
वानराणां महाबलः ।
भर्तृवाक्येन तेजस्वी
स्वेन वीर्येण चोदितः ॥
That powerful one of immense strength wreaked havoc among the Vānaras, prompted by the command of the master and by his own valor.
6.97.8
वानराश्च महासत्त्वाः
प्रगृह्य विपुलाः शिलाः ।
प्रविश्यारिबलं भीमम्
जघ्नुस्ते रजनीचरान् ॥
The Vānaras of extraordinary mettle snatched huge boulders, penetrated the formidable enemy forces and slew the Night-rovers.
6.97.9
महोदरः सुसङ्क्रुद्धः
शरैः काञ्चनभूषणैः ।
चिच्छेद पाणिपादोरून्
वानराणां महाहवे ॥
Mahōdara, in extreme wrath, cut off the feet, hands and thighs of the Vānaras in that fierce battle, with his arrows that were adorned with gold.
6.97.10
ततस्ते वानराः सर्वे
राक्षसैरर्दिता भृशम् ।
दिशो दश द्रुताः केचित्
केचित् सुग्रीवमाश्रिताः ॥
And then, all the Vānaras were severely assailed by the Rākshasas. Some fled in all the ten directions and others sought protection of Sugreeva.
6.97.11
प्रभग्नां समरे दृष्ट्वा
वानराणां महाचमूम् ।
अभिदुद्राव सुग्रीवो
महोदरमनन्तरम् ॥
Seeing that great army of Vānaras completely beaten up in the battle, Sugreeva rushed towards Mahōdara, who was not far away.
6.97.12
प्रगृह्य विपुलां घोराम्
महीधरसमां शिलाम् ।
चिक्षेप च महातेजाः
तद्वधाय हरीस्वरः ॥
The Lord of Vānaras of extraordinary power snatched a formidable boulder that was huge like a mountain, and hurled at him to kill him.
6.97.13
तामापतन्तीं सहसा
शिलां दृष्ट्वा महोदरः ।
असम्भ्रान्तस्ततो बाणैः
निर्बिभेद दुरासदाम् ॥
Seeing that formidable boulder fly at him all of a sudden, the unperturbed Mahōdara shattered it with his arrows.
6.97.14
रक्षसा तेन बाणौघैः
निकृत्ता सा सहस्रधा ।
निपपात शिला भूमौ
गृध्रचक्रमिवाकुलम् ॥
Shivered by that Rākshasa into a thousand bits, the boulder fell on the ground like a flight of vultures tumbling down in panic.
6.97.15
तां तु भिन्नां शिलां दृष्ट्वा
सुग्रीवः क्रोधमूर्च्छितः ।
सालमुत्पाट्य चिक्षेप
तं स चिच्छेद नैकधा ।
शरैश्च विददारैनम्
शूरः परबलार्दनः ॥
Seeing that boulder shattered thus, Sugreeva was beside himself with rage, and plucked a Sāla tree and hurled it. He (Mahōdara) cut it into multiple pieces. The heroic one, oppressor enemy forces, tore him (Sugreeva) apart with his arrows.
6.97.16
स ददर्श ततः क्रुद्धः
परिघं पतितं भुवि ।
आविध्य तु स तं दीप्तम्
परिघं तस्य दर्शयन् ।
परिघाग्रेण वेगेन
जघानास्य हयोत्तमान् ॥
At that moment, the enraged one (Sugreeva) saw a Parigha lying on the ground. Striking the ground with that dazzling Parigha and threatening him with it, he, in all swiftness, struck down the splendid horses with its knob.
6.97.18
तस्माद्धतहयाद्वीरः
सोऽवप्लुत्य महारथात् ।
गदां जग्राह सङ्क्रुद्धो
राक्षसोऽथ महोदरः ॥
Jumping down from the great chariot whose horses had been slain, the valiant Rākshasa Mahōdara grabbed a mace in rage.
6.97.19
गदापरिघहस्तौ तौ
युधि वीरौ समीयतुः ।
नर्दन्तौ गौवृषप्रख्यौ
घनाविव सविद्युतौ ॥
The two Veeras clashed like two bulls, with mace and Parigha in their hands respectively, roaring like thunderous clouds accompanied by lightning.
6.97.20
ततः क्रुद्धो गदां तस्मै
चिक्षेप रजनीचरः ।
ज्वलन्तीं भास्कराभासाम्
सुग्रीवाय महोदरः ॥
Then the Night-rover Mahōdara hurled the mace, which was blazing and dazzling like the sun, at Sugreeva.
6.97.21
गदां तां सुमहाघोराम्
आपतन्तीं महाबलः ।
सुग्रीवो रोषताम्राक्षः
समुद्यम्य महाहवे ।
आजघान गदां तस्य
परिघेण हरीश्वरः ।
पपात स गदोद्भिन्नः
परिघस्तस्य भूतले ॥
As that extremely dreadful mace flew towards him, Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras, eyes red with anger, lifted up the Parigha and struck the mace with it in that fierce battle. The mace fell on the ground, broken by that Parigha.
6.97.23
ततो जग्राह तेजस्वी
सुग्रीवो वसुधातलात् ।
आयसं मुसलं घोरम्
सर्वतो हेमभूषितम् ॥
Then that powerful Sugreeva grabbed a formidable pestle made of iron and decked with gold all over that was on the ground.
6.97.24
स तमुद्यम्य चिक्षेप
सोऽप्यस्य प्राक्षिपद्गदाम् ।
भिन्नावन्योन्यमासाद्य
पेततुस्तौ महीतले ॥
He lifted it and hurled it (at Mahōdara). He (Mahōdara) too, hurled the mace at him. They collided with each other and fell down on the ground, shattered.
6.97.25
ततो भग्नप्रहरणौ
मुष्टिभ्यां तौ समीयतुः ।
तेजोबलसमाविष्टौ
दीप्ताविव हुताशनौ ॥
With their weapons thus struck down, they, who were endowed with power and strength, then attacked each other with their fists, both blazing like fire.
6.97.26
जघ्नतुस्तौ तदाऽन्योन्यम्
नेदतुश्च पुनःपुनः ।
तलैश्चान्योन्यमाहत्य
पेततुश्च महीतले ॥
They assailed each other, shouting again and again. Beating each other with open palms (for a while), both of them fell on the ground.
6.97.27
उत्पेततुस्तदा तूर्णम्
जघ्नतुश्च परस्परम् ।
भुजैश्चिक्षिपतुर्वीरौ
अन्योन्यमपराजितौ ।
जग्मतुस्तौ श्रमं वीरौ
बाहुयुद्धे परन्तपौ ॥
Springing up again quickly, they assailed each other. The Veeras, undefeated, flung each other away, holding the shoulders. The Veeras, scorchers of the foe, felt exhausted as they wrestled each other.
6.97.28
जहार च तदा खड्गम्
अदूरपरिवर्तिनम् ।
राक्षसश्चर्मणा सार्धम्
महावेगो महोदरः ॥
The Rākshasa Mahōdara, with extraordinary swiftness, grabbed a sword and shield that were lying around not too far from them.
6.97.29
तथैव च महाखड्गम्
चर्मणा पतितं सह ।
जग्राह वानरश्रेष्ठः
सुग्रीवो वेगवत्तरः ॥
Likewise, Sugreeva, the best of the Vānaras, who was even more swift, grabbed another great sword and shield that lay on the ground.
6.97.30
ततो रोषपरीताङ्गौ
नर्दन्तावभ्यधावताम् ।
उद्यतासी रणे हृष्टौ
युद्धे शस्त्रविशारदौ ॥
Then with rage fuming from every limb and holding up the swords in high spirits, the two, who were savants in the use of weapons in the battle, attacked each other, bellowing.
6.97.31
दक्षिणं मण्डलं चोभौ
सुतूर्णं सम्परीयतुः ।
अन्योन्यमभिसङ्क्रुद्धौ
जये प्रणिहितावुभौ ॥
In extreme rage with each other, they both went in circles swiftly (trying to catch each other like in a wrestling ring), and focused on nothing but winning.
6.97.32
स तु शूरो महावेगो
वीर्यश्लाघी महोदरः ।
महाचर्मणि तं खड्गम्
पातयामास दुर्मतिः ॥
Then the heroic and evil-minded Mahōdara, who boasted of his own valor all the time, struck with the sword, with terrific speed, the great shield (of Sugreeva).
6.97.33
लग्नमुत्कर्षतः खड्गम्
खड्गेन कपिकुञ्जरः ।
जहार सशिरस्त्राणम्
कुण्डलोपगतं शिरः ॥
While he tried to retrieve his sword from the shield in which it became stuck, the elephant among Vānaras cut off with his sword his head which was protected by a helmet and decorated with ear-pendants.
6.97.34
निकृत्तशिरसस्तस्य
पतितस्य महीतले ।
तद्बलं राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
दृष्ट्वा तत्र न दृश्यते ॥
The army of the lord of Rākshasas, which saw him fall on the ground with his head cut off, was not to be seen there any more.
6.97.35
हत्वा तं वानरैः सार्धम्
ननाद मुदितो हरिः ।
चुक्रोध च दशग्रीवो
बभौ हृष्टश्च राघवः ॥
Having slain him, The Vānara let off a delighted cry along with all the Vānaras; the Ten-necked became angry and Rāghava was happy.
6.97.36
विषण्णवदनाः सर्वे
राक्षसा दीनचेतसः ।
विद्रवन्ति ततः सर्वे
भयवित्रस्तचेतसः ॥
The hearts of all the Rākshasas sank and their faces looked miserable. All of them fled, their hearts gripped with fear.
6.97.37
महोदरं तं विनिपात्य भूमौ
महागिरेः कीर्णमिवैकदेशम् ।
सूर्यात्मजस्तत्र रराज लक्ष्म्या
सूर्यः स्वतेजोभिरिवाप्रधृष्यः ॥
Tossing Mahōdara on the ground thus, like strewing a chunk of a mountain around, the invincible son of the sun shone splendidly, like the sun does with his native effulgence.
6.97.38
अथ विजयमवाप्य वानरेन्द्रः
समरमुखे सुरयक्षसिद्धसङ्घैः ।
अवनितलगतैश्च भूतसङ्घैः
हरुषसमाकुलितैः निरीक्ष्यमाणः ॥
The king of the Vānaras, who thus scored a victory on the battlefield, was held in awe by the hosts of Dēvas, Yakshas and Siddhas and by the multitudes of creatures on the ground who were overwhelmed with joy.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे सप्तनवतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninety seventh Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
