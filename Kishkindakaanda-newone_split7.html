Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 7
In this Sarga Sugreeva tells Rāma that he knows nothing about the Rākshasa, but will do everything required to fetch Maithili and assist Rāma in killing Rāvaṇa and his hordes.
Sugreeva pleads in many words with Rāma to give up his grief and regain his self-confidence. He tells Rāma that the despondency he shows is unbecoming of him. Sugreeva shows himself as an example of someone who has met with the similar fate, with his wife abducted and he himself driven out of the kingdom, but able to hold himself up without losing self-confidence.
Rāma feels better hearing those words and thanks Sugreeva for consoling him. Rāma also tells Sugreeva that he would do whatever Sugreeva asks him to do. Sugreeva also feels better and considers his object is as good as achieved.
4.7.1
एवमुक्तस्तु सुग्रीवो
रामेणार्तेन वानरः ।
अब्रवीत्प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यम्
सबाष्पं बाष्पगद्गदः ॥
When Rāma asked thus in agony, Sugreeva the Vānara, in tears, said these words with joined palms and in tear-choked voice:
4.7.2
न जाने निलयं तस्य
सर्वथा पापरक्षसः ।
सामर्थ्यं विक्रमं वापि
दौष्कुलेयस्य वा कुलम् ॥
I know nothing of that evil Rākshasa or his wretched lineage or his prowess or his abilities or where he lives.
4.7.3
सत्यं ते प्रतिजानामि
त्यज शोकमरिन्दम ।
करिष्यामि तथा यत्नम्
यथा प्राप्स्यसि मैथिलीम् ॥
But, O subduer of foes, I promise that I will do everything required to fetch Maithili. Please abdicate your grief!
4.7.4
रावणं सगणं हत्वा
परितोष्यात्मपौरुषम् ।
तथाऽस्मि कर्ता न चिरात्
यथा प्रीतो भविष्यसि ॥
I shall do everything needed, in all urgency, for you to kill Rāvaṇa along with his hordes and be happy, with your self-pride restored.
4.7.5
अलं वैक्लब्यमालम्ब्य
धैर्यमात्मगतं स्मर ।
त्वद्विधानां न सदृशम्
ईदृशं बुद्धिलाघवम्. ॥
No more harboring of despondency! Think of the courage that is innate to you. Such infirmity of will is unbecoming to the likes of you.
4.7.6
मयाऽपि व्यसनं प्राप्तम्
भार्याविरहजं महत् ।
नाहमेवं हि शोचामि
न च धैर्यं परित्यजे ॥
I too have suffered intensely, bereaved of my wife; but I do not grieve, nor do I lose my self-confidence.
Sugreeva is in the same situation as Rāma, only for longer. Hence he is the right person to advise Rāma. He understands what Rāma is going through like nobody else can.
4.7.7
नाहं तामनुशोचामि
प्राकृतो वानरोऽपि सन् ।
महात्मा च विनीतश्च
किं पुनर्धृतिमान्भवान् ॥
Even being the ordinary Vānara that I am, I did not succumb to worry; what to say of you, a learned and stoic Mahātma!
4.7.8
बाष्पमापतितं धैर्यात्
निग्रहीतुं त्वमर्हसि ।
मर्यादां सत्त्वयुक्तानाम्
धृतिं नोत्स्रष्टुमर्हसि ॥
You should regain your composure and hold the tears from flowing. Rely on your steadfastness and let the dignity of a capable one like you, not slip off.
4.7.9
व्यसने वाऽर्थकृच्छ्रे वा
भये वा जीवितान्तके ।
विमृशन्वै स्वया बुद्ध्या
धृतिमान्नावसीदति ॥
Even at the time of hardships or loss of riches or fear of losing life, one who is steady at heart would not let oneself sink low, but ponder over things carefully!
4.7.10
बालिशस्तु नरो नित्यम्
वैक्लब्यं योऽनुवर्तते ।
स मज्जत्यवशश्शोके
भाराक्रान्तेव नौर्जले ॥
One has to be stupid to let depression takeover forever. Such a person would easily sink in grief like a heavily loaded boat in water.
4.7.11
एषोऽञ्जलिर्मया बद्धः
प्रणयात्त्वां प्रसादये ।
पौरुषं श्रय शोकस्य
नान्तरं दातुमर्हसि ॥
I beg you with joined palms, it is my love that makes me beseech you not to give way to grief, but to place your trust in valor.
4.7.12
ये शोकमनुवर्तन्ते
न तेषां विद्यते सुखम् ।
तेजश्च क्षीयते तेषाम्
न त्वं शोचितुमर्हसि ॥
Those who cling to grief will know no happiness; their strength will decay; hence you should not succumb to worry.
4.7.13
शोकेनाभिप्रपन्नस्य
जीविते चापि संशयः ।
स शोकं त्यज राजेन्द्र
धैर्यमाश्रय केवलम् ॥
Once taken over by grief, one becomes disillusioned with life. Hence, O lord of kings, give up grief and rely on your self-confidence.
4.7.14
हितं वयस्यभावेन
ब्रूमि नोपदिशामि ते ।
वयस्यतां पूजयन्मे
न त्वं शोचितुमर्हसि ॥
I am not preaching to you, but only saying what is good for you, like a friend. You must give up grieving respecting my friendly insistence.
4.7.15
मधुरं सान्त्वितस्तेन
सुग्रीवेण स राघवः ।
मुखमश्रुपरिक्लिन्नम्
वस्त्रान्तेन प्रमार्जयत् ॥
Thus consoled by Sugreeva in sweet words, Rāghava, with the hem of his garment, wiped his face that was wet all over with tears.
4.7.16
प्रकृतिस्थस्तु काकुत्स्थः
सुग्रीववचनात्प्रभुः ।
सम्परिष्वज्य सुग्रीवम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Kākutstha, the lord, regained normalcy with those words of Sugreeva. He embraced Sugreeva and said these words:
4.7.17
कर्तव्यं यद्वयस्येन
स्निग्धेन च हितेन च ।
अनुरूपं च युक्तं च
कृतं सुग्रीव तत्त्वया ॥
You have done, Sugreeva, whatever is expected of a loving and well-wishing friend, which is timely and apt as well.
4.7.18
एष च प्रकृतिस्थोऽहम्
अनुनीतस्त्वया सखे ।
दुर्लभो हीदृशो बन्धुः
अरस्मिन्काले विशेषतः ॥
I feel normal again, my friend, consoled by you. Rare indeed is a well-wisher like you, especially in these times.
Interesting, ‘especially in these times’ is not just a phrase of modern times. It was in use even in the days of Rāma!
4.7.19
किं तु यत्नस्त्वया कार्यो
मैथिल्याः परिमार्गणे ।
राक्षसस्य च रौद्रस्य
रावणस्य दुरात्मनः ॥
But you shall try searching for Maithili and Rāvaṇa, that fierce and evil Rākshasa.
4.7.20
मया च यदनुष्ठेयम्
विस्रब्धेन तदुच्यताम् ।
वर्षास्विव च सुक्षेत्रे
सर्वं सम्पद्यते तव ॥
You should also tell me, with complete trust, what I should do (for you). All your efforts will bear fruit, like seeds sown in a fertile soil in rainy season.
4.7.21
मया च यदिदं वाक्यम्
अभिमानात्समीरितम् ।
तत्त्वया हरिशार्दूल
तत्त्वमित्युपधार्यताम् ॥
These words that I say out of love for you, should be considered, O tiger among Vānaras, as spoken with utmost sincerity.
4.7.22
अनृतं नोक्तपूर्वं मे
न च वक्ष्ये कदाचन ।
एतत्ते प्रतिजानामि
सत्येनैव शपाम्यहमं ॥
I have never said what I do not mean, and never shall. I promise this to you, swearing on truth.
4.7.23
ततः प्रहृष्टस्सुग्रीवो
वानरैस्सचिवैस्सह ।
राघवस्य वचश्श्रुत्वा
प्रतिज्ञातं विशेषतः ॥
Hearing those words of Rāghava, reinforced as they were by his solemn pledge, Sugreeva and his ministers were vastly pleased.
4.7.24
एवमेकान्तसम्पृक्तौ
ततस्तौ नरवानरौ ।
उभावन्योन्यसदृशम्
सुखं दुःखमभाषताम् ॥
Then they, the human and the Vānara, having much in common with each other, spent time in private, discussing their fortunes and misfortunes.
4.7.25
महानुभावस्य वचो निशम्य
हरिर्नृपाणामृषभस्य तस्य ।
कृतं स मेने हरिवीरमुख्यः
तदा स्वकार्यं हृदयेन विद्वान् ॥
Hearing the words of that magnanimous bull among kings, the Vānara, foremost of the Vānara warriors, knew in his heart of hearts that his purpose was as good as achieved.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे सप्तमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventh Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
