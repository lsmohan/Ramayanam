Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 9
In this Sarga, Sugreeva begins to tell his story of how he used to love and serve his brother Vāli obediently after Vāli became the king following their father's demise, how his brother had a rift with an Asura called Māyāvi over a woman, how Māyāvi challenged Vāli to a fight, but fled into a cave when chased by Vali and Sugreeva, how Vāli entered the cave to kill the Asura, asking Sugreeva to wait at the mouth of the cave, how Vāli did not come out of the cave even after a year passed, how there was not even a sound from him, how blood with froth came out of the cave, how Sugreeva returned to Kishkindhā after giving water ablutions to his brother, how the ministers consecrated him on to the throne, how Vāli returned killing the Asura, how he was outraged seeing Sugreeva consecrated, how he put the ministers in chains and would not relent even when Sugreeva prostrated to Vāli, touching his feet with his crown.
4.9.1
वाली नाम मम भ्राता
ज्येष्ठश्शत्रुनिषूदनः ।
पितुर्बहुमतो नित्यम्
ममापि च तथा पुरा ॥
My brother, by name Vāli, is an annihilator of the foe. He was very dear to my father, always. And in those days, I used to like him too.
4.9.2
पितर्युपरतेऽस्माकम्
ज्येष्ठोऽयमिति मन्त्रिभिः ।
कपीनामीश्वरो राज्ये
कृतः परमसम्मतः ॥
After the death of our father, the ministers established him, the elder one, who enjoyed the esteem of all in the kingdom, as the Lord of Vānaras.
4.9.3
राज्यं प्रशासतस्तस्य
पितृपैतामहं महत् ।
अहं सर्वेषु कालेषु
प्रणतः प्रेष्यवत् स्थितः ॥
While he ruled the great kingdom that had been handed down from father to son, I served him obediently all the time.
4.9.4
मायावी नाम तेजस्वी
पूर्वजो दुन्दुभेः सुतः ।
तेन तस्य महद्वैरम्
स्त्रीकृतं विश्श्रुतं पुरा ॥
Māyāvi, the eldest son of Dundhubhi, had a great enmity with him, caused by the quarrel over a woman, that was well known.
4.9.5
स तु सुप्तजने रात्रौ
किष्किन्धाद्वारमागतः ।
नर्दति स्म सुसंरब्धो
वालिनं चाह्वयद्रणे ॥
He once came to the gates of Kishkindhā at the dead of night when all were asleep, raising a mighty roar and challenging Vāli to fight with him.
4.9.6
प्रसुप्तस्तु मम भ्राता
नर्दतो भैरवस्वनम् ।
श्रुत्वा न ममृषे वाली
निष्पपात जवात्तदा ॥
My brother Vāli, who was sound asleep, became very impatient hearing his dreadful yell, and came out swiftly.
4.9.7
स तु वै निस्सृतः क्रोधात्
तं हन्तुमसुरोत्तमम् ।
वार्यमाणस्तत स्त्रीभिः
मया च प्रणतात्मना ॥
And in his wrath, he went forth to kill that best among the Asuras, even though all the women tried to dissuade him and I too did the same, in all obedience.
4.9.8
स तु निर्धूय ताः सर्वा
निर्जगाम महाबलः ।
ततोऽहमपि सौहार्दात्
निस्सृतो वालिना सह ॥
But, he, of immense strength, ignored all of them and went ahead. Then, out of love, I also went out along with Vāli.
4.9.9
स तु मे भ्रातरं दृष्ट्वा
मां च दूरादवस्थितम् ।
असुरो जातसन्त्रासः
प्रदुद्राव ततो भृशम् ॥
He, the Asura, on seeing my brother and me from afar, fled away in great terror.
4.9.10
तस्मिन् द्रवति सन्त्रस्ते
ह्यावां द्रुततरं गतौ ।
प्रकाशश्च कृतो मार्गः
चन्द्रेणोद्गच्छता तदा ॥
As he fled in terror, we both rushed to chase him, while the path was lit by the rising moon.
4.9.11
स तृणैरावृतं दुर्गम्
धरण्या विवरं महत् ।
प्रविवेशासुरो वेगात्
आवामासाद्य विष्ठितौ ॥
Then the Asura entered a cave, a huge one on this earth, covered with grass and impassable. Moving with all swiftness, we found ourselves at the mouth of the cave.
4.9.12
तं प्रविष्टं रिपुं दृष्ट्वा
बिलं रोषवशं गतः ।
मामुवाच तदा वाली
वचनं क्षुभितेन्द्रियः ॥
Seeing the enemy enter the cave, Vāli was in a rage. All his senses in turmoil, he said these words to me:
4.9.13
इह त्वं तिष्ठ सुग्रीव
बिलद्वारि समाहितः ।
यावत्तत्र प्रविश्याहम्
निहन्मि सहसा रिपुम् ॥
O Sugreeva! Wait here vigilantly, at the entrance of the cave, while I go in and kill the enemy, in no time.
4.9.14
मया त्वेतद्वचश्श्रुत्वा
याचितस्स परन्तपः ।
शापयित्वा तु मां पद्भ्याम्
प्रविवेश बिलं महत् ॥
I begged him hard to allow me to follow him, but he, scorcher of the foe, forbade me, and sealing his refusal by making me take an oath on his feet, entered that huge cave.
4.9.15
तस्य प्रविष्टस्य बिलम्
साग्रस्संवत्सरो गतः ।
स्थितस्य च बिलद्वारि
स कालो व्यत्यवर्तत ॥
Then, over a year passed by for him who entered the cave, and the same time passed for me while waiting at the entrance.
4.9.16
अहं तु नष्टं ज्ञात्वा तम्
स्नेहादागतसम्भ्रमः ।
भ्रातरं न च पश्यामि
पापाशङ्कि च मे मनः ॥
With my brother not to be seen, the sense of loss taking over me and my mind suspecting the worst, my affection (for him) made me agitated.
4.9.17
अथ दीर्घस्य कालस्य
बिलात्तस्माद्विनिस्सृतम् ।
सफेनं रुधिरं रक्तम्
अहं दृष्ट्वा सुदुःखितः ॥
When, after a long time, I found blood with froth issuing from the cave, I was grief-stricken.
4.9.18
नर्दतामसुराणां च
ध्वनिर्मे श्रोत्रमागतः ।
न रतस्य च सङ्ग्रामे
क्रोशतोऽपि स्वनो गुरोः ॥
I heard the noise made by the shouting Asuras, but not the sound of war-whoops of my elder brother involved in fight.
4.9.19
अहं त्ववगतो बुद्ध्या
चिह्नैस्तैर्भ्रातरं हतम् ।
पिधाय च बिलद्वारम्
शिलया गिरिमात्रया ।
शोकार्तश्चोदकं कृत्वा
किष्किन्धामागतस्सखे ॥
My friend! From the signs, I concluded that my brother was slain. I blocked the entrance to the cave with a boulder as big as a hill, and returned to Kishkindhā, distraught with grief, after offering water libations.
4.9.20
गूहमानस्य मे तत्त्वम्
यत्नतो मन्त्रिभिश्श्रुतम् ।
ततोऽहं तैस्समागम्य
सम्मतैरभिषेचितः ॥
Though I tried my best to keep it secret, the ministers came to know (that Vāli was dead). Then all of them coming together, consecrated me on the throne.
4.9.21
राज्यं प्रशासतस्तस्य
न्यायतो मम राघव ।
आजगाम रिपुं हत्वा
वाली तमसुरोत्तमम् ॥
As I was ruling the realm justly, Vāli returned, having slain his foe, the great Asura.
4.9.22
हत्वा शत्रुं स मे भ्राता
प्रविवेश पुरं तदा ।
मानयंस्तं महात्मानम्
यथावच्चाभ्यवादयम् ।
उक्ताश्च नाशिषस्तेन
सन्तुष्टेनान्तरात्मना ॥
I honored that Mahātma, my brother, and offered my salutations in a customary manner after he thus entered the city after slaying the enemy. He was neither pleased with it in his heart nor did he utter a single word of blessing.
4.9.24
अभिषिक्तं तु मां दृष्ट्वा
वाली संरक्तलोचनः ।
मदीयान्मन्त्रिणो बद्ध्वा
परुषं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Rather, seeing me consecrated, his eyes turned red-blood as he was outraged. He put my ministers in chains and spoke harshly.
4.9.25
निग्रहेऽपि समर्थस्य
तं पापं प्रति राघव ।
न प्रावर्तत मे बुद्धिः
भ्रातुर्गौरवयन्त्रिता ॥
O Rāghava! Though I was fully capable of restraining him from that aggression, my mind did not proceed on those lines, refrained by the respect for an elder brother.
4.9.26
नत्वा पादावहं तस्य
मकुटेनास्पृशं प्रभो ।
अपि वाली मम क्रोधात्
न प्रसादं चकार सः ॥
O Lord! I then prostrated and touched his feet with my crown. Even then, being angry with me, he, Vāli, would not bestow his grace on me.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे नवमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
