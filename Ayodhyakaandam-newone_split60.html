Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 60
In this Sarga, Kousalyā, unable to accept the reality that hit upon her, that Rāma would no more be with her, cries like a one possessed.
Sumantra tries his best to console her by saying that Rāma, Seetā and Lakshmaṇa are now accustomed to the life of Vana, enjoying its beauty. He takes pains to explain how Seetā, a delicate woman, is also enjoying the Vana, and is as normal as she had ever been, wearing all her ornaments, as usual.
But all of that is in vain and Kousalyā continues to cry for Rāma.
2.60.1
ततो भूतोपसृष्टेव
वेपमाना पुनः पुनः ।
धरण्यां गतसत्त्वेव
कौसल्या सूतमब्रवीत् ॥
A trembling Kousalyā, lying on the ground with her vitals completed depleted, looking as if she was completely possessed, said to the charioteer:
2.60.2
नय मां यत्र काकुत्स्थः
सीता यत्र च लक्ष्मणः ।
तान्विना क्षणमप्यत्र
जीवितुं नोत्सहेह्यहम् ॥
Take me where the scion of Kākutstha lineage is, and also Seetā and Lakshmaṇa are. I cannot live here without them even for a second.
2.60.3
निवर्तय रथं शीघ्रम्
दण्डकान्नय मामपि ।
अथ तान्नानुगच्छामि
गमिष्यामि यमक्षयम् ॥
Turn back the chariot quick, and take me too, to the Daṇḍaka. If I cannot follow them, I shall go to the abode of Yama!
2.60.4
बाष्पवेगोपहतया
स वाचा सज्जमानया ।
इदमाश्वासयन्देवीम्
सूतः प्राञ्जलिरब्रवीत् ॥
Consoling her in a voice smothered by sobs, the charioteer said to the lady with folded hands:
2.60.5
त्यज शोकं च मोहं च
सम्भ्रमं दुःखजं तथा ।
व्यवधूय च सन्तापम्
वने वत्स्यति राघवः ॥
Please give up the grief, bewilderment and the indulgence born out of sorrow. Rāghava is living in the forest, brushing aside any sort of worry.
2.60.6
लक्ष्मणश्चापि रामस्य
पादौ परिचरन्वने ।
आराधयति धर्मज्ञः
परलोकं जितेन्द्रियः ॥
Lakshmaṇa who knows Dharma, also, with his senses fully tamed, is working his way towards a good afterlife by serving the feet of Rāma.
2.60.7
विजनेऽपि वने सीता
वासं प्राप्य गृहेष्विव ।
विस्रम्भं लभतेऽभीता
रामे सन्न्यस्तमानसा ॥
Seetā, setting her mind fully on Rāma, is living in that desolate forest fearlessly and with confidence as if she is at home.
2.60.8
नास्या दैन्यं कृतं किञ्चित्
सुसूक्ष्ममपि लक्ष्यते ।
उचितेव प्रवासानाम्
वैदेही प्रतिभाति मा ॥
Even the least trace of dejection cannot be seen in her. She seems to me to be made for staying away from home.
2.60.9
नगरोपवनं गत्वा
यथा स्म रमते पुरा ।
तथैव रमते सीता
निर्जनेषु वनेष्वपि ॥
She finds as much pleasure in the Vana, even though no humans are to be found around, as she used to delight in the gardens of the city earlier.
2.60.10
बालेव रमते सीताऽ -
बालचन्द्रनिभानना ।
रामा रामे ह्यधीनात्मा
विजनेऽपि वने सती ॥
That beautiful Seetā, her heart filled with the thoughts of Rāma, her face glowing like a full moon, enjoys like a child, even though she is in the middle of a desolate forest.
2.60.11
तद्गतं हृदयं ह्यस्याः
तदधीनं च जीवितम् ।
अयोध्यापि भवेऽत्तस्या
रामहीना तदा वनम् ॥
Her heart has gone out to him and and her life depends on him. Even Ayōdhyā, without Rāma, would be like a Vana for her.
2.60.12
परि पृच्छति वैदेही
ग्रामांश्च नगराणि च ।
गतिं दृष्ट्वा नदीनां च
पादपान्विविधानपि ॥
The princess of Vidēha, enquires about the villages, cities, about the running rivers and about the variety of trees.
2.60.13
रामं हि लक्ष्मणं वापि
पृष्ट्वा जानाति जानकी ।
अयोध्या क्रोशमात्रे तु
विहारमिव संश्रिता ॥
The daughter of Janaka asks and learns from Rāma or even Lakshmaṇa, as if she is in a garden, only a Krōṡa away from Ayōdhyā.
2.60.14
इदमेव स्मराम्यस्याः
सहसैवोपजल्पितम् ।
कैकेयी संश्रितं वाक्यम्
नेदानीं प्रतिभाति मा ॥
All I can recall about her is only this. She mentioned something about Kaikēyee in haste, but I am not able to recollect it.
2.60.15
ध्वंसयित्वा तु तद्वाक्यम्
प्रमादात्पर्युपत्स्थितम् ।
ह्लादनं वचनं सूतो
देव्या मधुरमब्रवीत् ॥
Quickly dodging his slip of tongue, the charioteer continued with words that would be more appealing and pleasing to the queen.
2.60.16
अध्वना वातवेगेन
सम्भ्रमेणाऽऽतपेन च ।
न विगच्छति वैदेह्याः
चन्द्रांशु सदृशी प्रभा ॥
Neither the journey, nor the gush of the winds, nor the commotion all around, seems to reduce her radiance that rivals the rays of the moon.
2.60.17
सदृशं शतपत्रस्य
पूर्णचन्द्रोपम प्रभम् ।
वदनं तद्वदान्याया
वैदेह्या न विकम्पते ॥
There is no sign of agitation on the face of that generous-speaking princess of Vidēha, which resembles a lotus and glows like the full moon.
2.60.18
अलक्तरसरक्ताभौ
अलक्तरसवर्जितौ ।
अद्यापि चरणौ तस्याः
पद्मकोशसमप्रभौ ॥
Her feet, which glow with the shine of lac, without even wearing it, continue to be as bright as the lotus bud even now.
2.60.19
नूपुरोद्घुष्टहेलेव
खेलं गच्छति भामिनी ।
इदानीमपि वैदेही
तद्रागान्न्यस्त भूषणा ॥
That pretty woman walks playfully in tune with the music of her anklets. Even now, the princess of Vidēha continues to love to wear her ornaments.
2.60.20
गजं वा वीक्ष्य सिंहं वा
व्याघ्रं वा वनमाश्रिता ।
नाऽहारयति सन्त्रासम्
बाहू रामस्य संश्रिता ॥
Neither elephant, nor lion, nor tiger is cause for fear to her, living in the Vana, with the protecting arm of Rāma about her.
2.60.21
न शोच्यास्ते न चात्मनः
शोच्यो नापि जनाधिपः ।
इदं हि चरितं लोके
प्रतिष्ठास्यति शाश्वतम् ॥
There is no reason to be concerned about them. Neither we, nor the king have reason to worry. For this story shall remain etched in the annuls of history forever.
2.60.22
विधूय शोकं परिहृष्टमानसा
महर्षियाते पथि सुव्यवत्स्थिताः ।
वनेरता वन्यफलाशनाः पितुः
शुभां प्रतिज्ञां परिपालयन्ति ते ॥
Shaking off grief and with cheerful hearts they have set themselves firmly on the path that Maharshis treaded. Enjoying the Vana, and relishing its fruits, they are fulfilling their father's apposite pledge.
2.60.23
तथापि सूतेन सुयुक्तवादिना
निवार्यमाणा सुतशोककर्शिता ।
न चैव देवी विरराम कूजितात्
प्रियेति पुत्रेति च राघवेति च ॥
Though the charioteer thus tried to help her with his appropriate words, the lady could not cease sobbing: "Alas, O my dear, O my son, O Rāghava!"
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे षष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixtieth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
