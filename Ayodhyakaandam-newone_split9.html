Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 9
In this Sarga, convinced by Manthara that she and her son Bharata would have a miserable life should Rāma become the king, Kaikēyee asks Manthara for a way out.
Manthara reminds Kaikēyee of the two boons given to her by her husband during the war between Dēvas and Asuras. She tells her to claim those two boons, by asking that Rāma be sent to the Vana and Bharata be consecrated as the crown-prince.
Manthara explains that once Rāma is out of the picture, Bharata will be able to establish himself. Manthara also tells the modus operandi to Kaikēyee; to remove her ornaments, to lie down on the bare floor in the anger chamber, etc. and to persist till her husband agrees.
Kaikēyee feels grateful and exhilarated with the idea and praises Manthara. She goes to the extreme saying that either she would die or Rāma would be exiled to the Vana.
2.9.1
एवमुक्ता तु कैकेयी
क्रोधेन ज्वलितानना ।
दीर्घमुष्णं विनिश्वस्य
मन्थरामिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing this, Kaikēyee, with her face burning in rage, said to Manthara heaving a long and burning sigh:
2.9.2
अद्य राममितः क्षिप्रम्
वनं प्रस्थापयाम्यहम् ।
यौवराज्ये च भरतम्
क्षिप्रमेवाभिषेचये ॥
This very day I shall send Rāma away to the forest, and I shall consecrate Bharata as the crown-prince.
2.9.3
इदं त्विदानीं सम्पश्य
केनोपायेन मन्थरे ।
भरतः प्राप्नुयाद्राज्यम्
न तु रामः कथञ्चन ॥
Think about an Upāya, Manthara, by which Bharata gains the kingdom, and not Rāma, by any means!
2.9.4
एवमुक्ता तया देव्या
मन्थरा पापदर्शिनी ।
रामार्थमुपहिंसन्ती
कैकेयीमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Having thus been asked by her mistress, the evil minded Manthara said these words that would ruin Rāma's prospects:
2.9.5
हन्तेदानीं प्रवक्ष्यामि
कैकेयि श्रूयतां च मे ।
यथा ते भरतो राज्यम्
पुत्रः प्राप्स्यति केवलम् ॥
Ah, good! Now listen, Kaikēyee, I will tell you how your son Bharata alone gets the kingdom!
2.9.6
किं न स्मरसि कैकेयि
स्मरन्ती वा निगूहसे ।
यदुच्यमानमात्मार्थम्
मत्तस्त्वं श्रोतुमिच्छसि ॥
Well, don't you remember, Kaikēyee? Or you remember, but choose not to say! Or, you just like to hear me say what might serve your purpose?
2.9.7
मयोच्यमानं यदि ते
श्रोतुं छन्दो विलासिनि ।
श्रूयतामभिधास्यामि
श्रुत्वा चापि विमृश्यताम् ॥
Dear playful! If it pleases you to hear me say, listen, I will tell you. And after listening, think through it seriously!
2.9.8
श्रुत्वैवं वचनं तस्या
मन्थरायास्तु कैकेयी ।
किञ्चिदुत्थाय शयनात्
स्वास्तीर्णादिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those words of Manthara, Kaikēyee rose half-way from her well spread bed and said:
2.9.9
कथय त्वं ममोपायम्
केनोपायेन मन्थरे ।
भरतः प्राप्नुयाद्राज्यम्
न तु रामः कथञ्चन ॥
O Manthara! Tell me the Upāya, by which Bharata will get the kingdom, and Rāma, by no means!
2.9.10
एवमुक्ता तया देव्या
मन्थरा पापदर्शिनी ।
रामार्थमुपहिंसन्ती
कुब्जा वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Having thus been asked by her mistress, Manthara, the humpback, with her evil vision, said these words that would ruin Rāma's prospects:
2.9.11
तव दैवासुरे युद्धे
सह राजर्षिभिः पतिः ।
अगच्छत्त्वामुपादाय
देवराजस्य साह्यकृत् ।
दिशमास्थाय वै देवि
दक्षिणां दण्डकान्प्रति ।
वैजयन्तमिति ख्यातम्
पुरं यत्र तिमिध्वजः ॥
O Mistress, during the time of war between Dēvas and Asuras, your husband went to the assistance of the king of the Dēvas along with other Rājarshis, and took you along with him.
He set off towards the southern region of Dandakāraṇya to a renowned city called Vaijayanta where Timidhvaja lived.
2.9.13
स शम्बर इति ख्यातः
शतमायो महासुरः ।
ददौ शक्रस्य सङ्ग्रामम्
देवसङ्घैरनिर्जितः ॥
That mighty Asura, who was a master of every kind of magic, was well known as Ṡambara. He could not be vanquished by the many hosts of Dēvas and he fought a tough battle with Ṡakra.
2.9.14
तस्मिन्महति सङ्ग्रामे
पुरुषान्क्षतविक्षतान् ।
रात्रौ प्रसुप्तान्घ्नन्ति स्म
तरसाऽऽसाद्य राक्षसाः ॥
In that great battle, the Rākshasas used to descend in the darkness of the night and kill the sleeping and wounded men by brute force.
2.9.15
तत्राकरोन्महद्युद्धम्
राजा दशरथस्तदा ।
असुरैश्च महाबाहुः
शस्त्रैश्च शकलीकृतः ॥
Then, King Daṡaratha of mighty arm had a fierce fight there. His body was torn to pieces by the Ṡastras of the Asuras.
2.9.16
अपवाह्य त्वया देवि
सङ्ग्रामान्नष्टचेतनः ।
तत्रापि विक्षतश्शस्त्रैः
पतिस्ते रक्षितस्त्वया ॥
My mistress! Then he fell unconscious and you carried him away from the battle field. Even there, he was saved by you when attacked with Ṡastras.
2.9.17
तुष्टेन तेन दत्तौ ते
द्वौ वरौ शुभदर्शने ।
स त्वयोक्तः पतिर्देवि
यदेच्छेयं तदा वरौ ।
गृह्णीयामिति तत्तेन
तथेत्युक्तं महात्मना ॥
Then, O endearing one, you were given two boons by him. O mistress, you then said to your husband, that you would receive those boons when you want them. And that Mahātma said 'so be it!'
2.9.18
अनभिज्ञाम्ह्यहं देवि
त्वयैव कथिता पुरा ।
कथैषा तव तु स्नेहात्
मनसा धार्यते मया ।
रामाभिषेकसम्भारान्
निगृह्य विनिवर्तय ॥
O mistress, I was indeed unaware of this! It was you who told me about this long time ago! I retained in my mind what you mentioned out of friendship. Insist, and stop the preparations for Rāma's consecration!
2.9.20
तौ वरौ याच भर्तारम्
भरतस्याभिषेचनम् ।
प्रव्राजनं च रामस्य
त्वं वर्षाणि चतुर्दश ॥
Claim those two boons from your husband: Coronation of Bharata and banishment of Rāma for fourteen years!
2.9.21
चतुर्दश हि वर्षाणि
रामे प्रव्राजिते वनम् ।
प्रजाभावगतस्नेहः
स्थिरः पुत्रो भविष्यति ॥
When Rāma is in exile in the Vanas for fourteen years, your son will be firmly established by winning the hearts of people by his affection.
2.9.22
क्रोधागारं प्रविश्याऽद्य
क्रुद्धेवाश्वपतेस्सुते ।
शेष्वाऽनन्तर्हितायां त्वम्
भूमौ मलिनवासिनी ॥
Go to the anger chamber, O daughter of Aṡwapati, and lie down on the bare floor wearing soiled garments as though you were in a rage!
2.9.23
मास्मैनं प्रत्युदीक्षेथा
मा चैनमभिभाषथाः ।
रुदन्ती चापि तं दृष्ट्वा
जगत्यां शोकलालसा ॥
Do not look at him! Do not talk to him! continue to weep in full grief, lying on the floor, when you see him!
2.9.24
दयिता त्वं सदा भर्तुः
अत्र मे नास्ति संशयः ।
त्वत्कृते स महाराजो
विशेदपि हुताशनम् ॥
That you are your husband's beloved wife is beyond any doubt. For your sake, the emperor would even jump into burning fire.
2.9.25
न त्वां क्रोधयितुं शक्तो
न क्रृद्धां प्रत्युदीक्षितुम् ।
तव प्रियार्थं राजा हि
प्राणानपि परित्यजेत् ॥
He simply cannot bring himself to make you angry or see you being angry. The king would not even heSeetāte to give up his life to please you.
2.9.26
न ह्यतिक्रमितुं शक्तः
तव वाक्यं महीपतिः ।
मन्दस्वभावे बुध्यस्व
सौभाग्यबलमात्मनः ॥
The king is not capable of transgressing your words. You diffident, know the power of your conjugal relationship!
2.9.27
मणिमुक्तं सुवर्णानि
रत्नानि विविधानि च ।
दद्याद्दशरथो राजा
मास्म तेषु मनः कृथाः ॥
King Daṡaratha might offer you gems and pearls, gold and precious stones of many kinds; you must not be tempted by them.
2.9.28
यौ तौ दैवासुरे युद्धे
वरौ दशरथोऽददात् ।
तौ स्मारय महाभागे
सोऽर्थो न त्वामतिक्रमेत् ॥
O blessed one! Remind Daṡaratha of the two boons he gave during the war between Dēvas and Asuras. Do not get sidetracked from your goal!
2.9.29
यदातु ते वरं दद्यात्
स्वयमुत्थाप्य राघवः ।
व्यवस्थाप्य महाराजम्
त्वमिमं वृणुया वरम् ॥
Only after that descendant of Raghu agrees to grant those boons and personally lifts you up, and only after that emperor settles down, may you ask for these boons:
2.9.30
रामं प्रव्राजयारण्ये
नव वर्षाणि पञ्च च ।
भरतः क्रियतां राजा
पृथिव्याः पार्थिवर्षभः ॥
O majestic one among the rulers of lands! May Rāma be sent in exile for nine and five years! May Bharata be made the king of this land!
2.9.31
चतुर्दश हि वर्षाणि
रामे प्रव्राजिते वनम् ।
रूढश्च कृतमूलश्च
शेषं स्थास्यति ते सुतः ॥
In the fourteen years that Rāma is exiled to the Vanas, your son will grow strong and develop roots, so that he can stay firmly forever.
2.9.32
रामप्रव्राजनं चैव
देवि याचस्व तं वरम् ।
एवं सिद्ध्यन्ति पुत्रस्य
सर्वार्थास्तव भामिनि ॥
Only by asking him the boon for Rāma's exile, O queen, O lovely woman, will all interests of your son be secured.
2.9.33
एवं प्रव्राजितश्चैव
रामोऽरामो भविष्यति ।
भरतश्च हतामित्रः
तव राजा भविष्यति ॥
By being in exile, Rāma will lose all his charisma. Bharata will become the king, thus getting rid of his rival!
2.9.34
येन कालेन रामश्च
वनात्प्रत्यागमिष्यति ।
तेन कालेन पुत्रस्ते
कृतमूलो भविष्यति ।
सुगृहीतमनुष्यश्च
सुहृद्भिस्सार्धमात्मवान् ॥
By the time Rāma returns from the forest, your wise son will develop roots by attracting friends and getting people on his side.
2.9.35
प्राप्तकालं नु मन्येऽहम्
राजानं वीतसाध्वसा ।
रामाभिषेकसङ्कल्पात्
निगृह्य विनिवर्तय ॥
I think the king is at the fag-end of his life. Insist, without any heSeetātion, to change his intention of consecrating Rāma.
2.9.36
अनर्थमर्थरूपेण
ग्राहिता सा ततस्तया ।
हृष्टा प्रतीता कैकेयी
मन्थरामिदमब्रवीत् ।
सा हि वाक्येन कुब्जायाः
किशोरीवोत्पथं गता ।
कैकेयी विस्मयं प्राप्ता
परं परमदर्शना ॥
The otherwise mature Kaikēyee, completely mesmerized by those words of the humpback, treaded the wrong path, like a lass.
Having mistaken the detrimental as beneficial, the convinced Kaikēyee, then, said to Manthara in delight:
2.9.38
कुब्जे त्वां नाभिजानामि
श्रेष्ठां श्रेष्ठाभिधायिनीम् ।
पृथिव्यामसि कुब्जानाम्
उत्तमा बुद्धिनिश्चये ॥
I never realized, O humpback, that you are so good at deliberating and counseling. You are the best among humpbacks in this world!
2.9.39
त्वमेव तु ममाऽर्थेषु
नित्ययुक्ता हितैषिणी ।
नाहं समवबुध्येयम्
कुब्जे राज्ञश्चिकीर्षितम् ॥
You alone have constantly been guarding my interests, and seeking my welfare. I wouldn't have otherwise known the intentions of the King, O humpback!
2.9.40
सन्ति दुस्संस्थिताः कुब्जा
वक्राः परमदारुणाः ।
त्वं पद्ममिव वातेन
सन्नता प्रियदर्शना ॥
There are many deformed humpbacks who are absolutely ugly and crooked. But, you are winsome like a lotus swayed by the breeze!
2.9.41
उरस्तेऽभिनिविष्टं वै
यावत्स्कन्धात् समुन्नतं ।
अधस्ताच्चोदरं शातम्
सुनाभमिव लज्जितम् ॥
Your tight breasts rise as high as your shoulder, while the belly below, with its beautiful navel, seems to have shrunk as though it had been shamed.
2.9.42
परिपूर्णं तु जघनम्
सुपीनौ च पयोधरौ ।
विमलेन्दुसमं वक्त्रम्
अहोराजसि मन्थरे ॥
But your hip is ample and your breasts are rounded and full. And your face is like the bright moon. O Manthara, my goodness, you are simply gorgeous!
2.9.43
जघनं तव निर्घुष्टम्
रशनादामशोभितम् ।
जङ्घे भृशमुपन्यस्ते
पादौ चाप्यायतावुभौ ॥
Adorned with the girdle, your hip rings musically. Your shanks are nicely curved and your feet are long.
2.9.44
त्वमायताभ्यां सक्थिभ्याम्
मन्थरे क्षौमवासिनी ।
अग्रतो मम गच्छन्ती
राजहंसीव राजसे ॥
With your long thighs and clad in silks, O Manthara, you looking splendid walking in front of me like a royal swan!
2.9.45
आसन्याश्शम्बरे मायाः
सहस्रमसुराधिपे ।
सर्वास्त्वयि निविष्टास्ता
भूयश्चान्यास्सहस्रशः ॥
Ṡambara, the Asura had a thousand tricks with him. All of them, and thousands more are lodged in you.
2.9.46
तवेदं स्थगु यद्दीर्घम्
रथघोणमिवायतम् ।
मतयः क्षत्रविद्याश्च
मायाश्चात्र वसन्ति ते ॥
That long hump of yours, which is high like the tower of a chariot, is indeed, a storehouse of ideas, political skills and tricks.
2.9.47
अत्र ते प्रतिमोक्ष्यामि
मालां कुब्जे हिरण्मयीम् ।
अभिषिक्ते च भरते
राघवे च वनं गते ॥
I must decorate it with a golden chain, O humpback, once Bharata is consecrated and Rāghava goes to Vana!
2.9.48
जात्येन च सुवर्णेन
सुनिष्टप्तेन मन्थरे ।
लब्धार्था च प्रतीता च
लेपयिष्यामि ते स्थगु ॥
After achieving the goal and delighted about it, O Manthara, I shall decorate your hump with well treated and high quality gold !
2.9.49
मुखे च तिलकं चित्रम्
जातरूपमयं शुभम् ।
कारयिष्यामि ते कुब्जे
शुभान्याभरणानि च ॥
I shall get made, O humpback, excellent jewelry and exquisite Tilaka of fine quality gold, to adorn your face.
Tilaka is an adornment on the forehead.
2.9.50
परिधाय शुभे वस्त्रे
देवतेव चरिष्यसि ।
चन्द्रमाह्वयमानेन
मुखेनाप्रतिमानना ।
गमिष्यसि गतिं मुख्याम्
गर्वयन्ती द्विषज्जनम् ॥
Donning fine clothes, you shall go about like a goddess. With a peerless face that challenges the moon, you shall become the cynosure, humbling your rivals.
2.9.52
तवापि कुब्जाः कुब्जायाः
सर्वाभरणभूषिताः ।
पादौ परिचरिष्यन्ति
यथैव त्वं सदा मम ॥
And other humpbacks, decked with every kind of jewel, shall wait on you, the humpback, just as you constantly wait on me.
2.9.53
इति प्रशस्यमाना सा
कैकेयीमिदमब्रवीत् ।
शयानां शयने शुभ्रे
वेद्यामग्निशिखामिव ॥
Thus praised, she responded to Kaikēyee, who was lying on the couch resembling the fire in a Vēdi:
2.9.54
गतोदके सेतुबन्धो
न कल्याणि विधीयते ।
उत्तिष्ठ कुरु कल्याणम्
राजानमनुदर्शय ॥
There is no use building a dam after the waters run off! O Kalyāṇi! Get up, do the good thing, and lead the king your way!
2.9.55
तथा प्रोत्साहिता देवी
गत्वा मन्थरया सह ।
क्रोधागारं विशालाक्षी
सौभाग्यमदगर्विता ।
अनेकशतसाहस्रम्
मुक्ताहारं वराङ्गना ।
अवमुच्य वरार्हाणि
शुभान्याभरणानि च ।
ततो हेमोपमा तत्र
कुब्जावाक्यवशं गता ।
संविश्य भूमौ कैकेयी
मन्थरामिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Thus worked upon by her, the Queen, in her overweening pride as the King's favorite, went to the anger chamber along with Manthara.
And there, the lovely wide-eyed woman removed her chain of pearls, worth many hundred thousands, and other costly and beautiful jewels.
Succumbed to the words of the humpback, Kaikēyee of golden hue lay down on the bare floor, and said these following words to Manthara:
2.9.58
इह वा मां मृतां कुब्जे
नृपायावेदयिष्यसि ।
वनं तु राघवे प्राप्ते
भरतः प्राप्स्यति क्षितिम् ॥
O humpback! Tell the king that either I shall die right here or Rāghava shall go to Vana and Bharata gets the kingdom!
2.9.59
न सुवर्णेन मे ह्यर्थो
न रत्नैर्न च भूषणैः ।
एष मे जीवितस्यान्तो
रामो यद्यभिषिच्यते ॥
Neither gold, nor diamonds, nor ornaments are of interest to me. If Rāma is consecrated, it would spell the end of life for me.
2.9.60
अथो पुनस्तां महिषीं महीक्षितो
वचोभिरत्यर्थमहापराक्रमैः ।
उवाच कुब्जा भरतस्य मातरं
हितं वचो राममुपेत्य चाहितम् ॥
​The humpback went on urging the King's favored Queen, the mother of Bharata, with forceful and compelling voice, a course that was good for her and baneful for Rāma.
2.9.61
प्रपत्स्यते राज्यमिदं हि राघवो
यदि ध्रुवं त्वं ससुता च तप्स्यसे ।
अतो हि कल्याणि! यतस्व तत्तथा
यथा सुतस्ते भरतोऽभिषेक्ष्यते ॥
If Rāghava secures the kingdom, it is certain that you and your son will suffer. Therefore, O Kalyāṇi, you must try your best for the consecration of Bharata.
2.9.62
तथाऽतिविद्धा महिषी तु कुब्जया
समाहता वागिषुभिर्मुहुर्मुहुः ।
निधायहस्तौ हृदयेऽतिविस्मिता
शशंस कुब्जां कुपिता पुनः पुनः ॥
Repeatedly prodded thus by the verbal darts of the humpback, and burning with grief and rage, the amazed and incredulous Queen, told the humpback keeping her hand on her heart:
2.9.63
यमस्य वा मां विषयं गतामितो
निशाम्य कुब्जे! प्रतिवेदयिष्यसि ।
वनं गते वा सुचिराय राघवे
समृद्धकामो भरतो भविष्यति ॥
Either Rāghava would be gone to the Vana for long and Bharata's wish would be completely fulfilled, or, O humpback, you would be announcing my departure to the place of Yama.
Yama is the God of Death.
2.9.64
अहं हि नैवास्तरणानि न स्रजो
न चन्दनं नाञ्जनपानभोजनम् ।
न किञ्चिदिच्छामि न चेह जीवितं
न चेदितो गच्छति राघवो वनम् ॥
If Rāghava does not go to the forest, I shall have no desire for anything; these beautiful carpets and garlands, these sandal pastes and unguents, and these foods and drinks, and even for this life.
2.9.65
अथैतदुक्त्वा वचनं सुदारुणं
निधाय सर्वाभरणानि भामिनी ।
असंवृतामास्तरणेन मेदिनीं
तदाऽधिशिश्ये पतितेव किन्नरी ॥
Speaking those horrible words, that enchanting lady threw away all her ornaments and lay down on the bare floor that was not even covered by a carpet, like a Kinnara woman!
2.9.66
उदीर्णसंरम्भतमोवृतानना
तथाऽवमुक्तोत्तममाल्यभूषणा ।
नरेन्द्रपत्नी विमना बभूव सा
तमोवृता द्यौरिव मग्नतारका ॥
With her face overshadowed by her mounting anger, her valuable ornaments and flowers flung all about her, that unhappy wife of the of the ruler of people, looked like the sky muffled in darkness when the stars have sunk out of sight.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे नवमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
