Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 32
In this Sarga, Ṡūrpaṇakhā goes to Lanka, where her brother and the king of Rākshasas, Rāvaṇa lives. Rāvaṇa’s lofty, imposing, magnificent, but domineering, cruel and terrorizing personality, including his complete disregard for Dharma, is described here.
3.32.1
ततश्शूर्पणखा दृष्ट्वा
सहस्राणि चतुर्दश ।
हतान्येकेन रामेण
रक्षसां भीमकर्मणाम् ।
दूषणं च खरं चैव
हतं त्रिशिरसा सह ।
दृष्ट्वा पुनर्महानादं
ननाद जलदो यथा ॥
Ṡūrpaṇakhā, having seen fourteen thousand Rākshasas capable of ghastly deeds, including Khara, Dūshaṇa and Triṡira, killed singlehandedly by Rāma, let off a mighty yell again sounding like a a thunderous cloud.
3.32.3
सा दृष्ट्वा कर्म रामस्य
कृतमन्यैस्सुदुष्करम् ।
जगाम परमोद्विग्ना
लङ्कां रावणपालिताम् ॥
She was gripped by fear seeing what Rāma did, which would be simply impossible for anyone else to do, and hastened to Laṅkā that was ruled by Rāvaṇa.
3.32.4
सा ददर्श विमानाग्रे
रावणं दीप्ततेजसम् ।
उपोपविष्टं सचिवैः
मरुद्भिरिव वासवम् ॥
She saw Rāvaṇa of fiery radiance seated along with his ministers, in the top floor of the palace, like Vāsava attended by the Maruts.
Vāsava is another name for Indra. Maruts are Dēvas.
3.32.5
आसीनं सूर्यसङ्काशे
काञ्चने परमासने ।
रुक्मवेदिगतं प्राज्यं
ज्वलन्तमिव पावकम् ॥
He was seated on his high throne of gold that shone like the sun. He was like the blazing fire emerging from a golden Vēdi, boosted by ample ghee.
3.32.6
देवगन्धर्वभूतानाम्
ऋषीणां च महात्मनाम् ।
अजेयं समरे शूरं
व्यात्ताननमिवान्तकम् ॥
The valiant one, invincible in any fight, was like Death with wide opened jaws (ready to gobble) Dēvas, Gandharvas, and all beings, including Ṛshis who were Mahātmas.
3.32.7
देवासुरविमर्देषु
वज्राशनिकृतव्रणम् ।
ऐरावतविषाणग्रैः
उत्कृष्टकिणवक्षसम् ॥
He had scars wrought by the thunderbolt in the battle between Dēvas and Asuras, and scratches on the chest scraped by Airāvata with the sharp edges of its tusks.
3.32.8
विंशद्भुजं दशग्रीवं
दर्शनीयपरिच्छदम् ।
विशालवक्षसं वीरं
राजलक्षण शोभितम् ॥
That valiant one, with twenty arms, ten heads, and imposingly wide chest, bearing all the marks of royalty, was surrounded paraphernalia that were any eyeful.
3.32.9
स्निग्धवैडूर्यसङ्काशं
तप्तकाञ्चनकुण्डलम् ।
सुभुजं शुक्लदशनं
महास्यं पर्वतोपमम् ॥
He had a complexion that resembled the translucent sheen of lazulite, ear-pendants of gold purified in fire, splendid shoulders, clean-white teeth, wide mouth and was imposing like a mountain.
3.32.10
विष्णुचक्रनिपातैश्च
शतशो देवसंयुगे ।
अन्यैश्शस्त्रप्रहारैश्च
महायुद्धेषु ताडितम् ॥
He was struck hundreds of times by the Cakra of Vishṇu in the war with Dēvas and hit by other weapons in other great battles.
3.32.11
अहताङ्गं समस्तैश्च
देवप्रहरणैस्तथा ।
अक्षोभ्याणां समुद्राणां
क्षोभणं क्षिप्रकारिणम् ॥
Every limb of his was hit by the strikes of the Dēvas. He was capable of throwing even the imperturbable seas into a violent turmoil.
3.32.12
क्षेप्तारं पर्वतेन्द्राणां
सुराणां च प्रमर्दनम् ।
उच्छेत्तारं च धर्माणां
परदाराभिमर्शनम् ॥
He could playfully toss away giant mountains and thrash Dēvas. With utter disregard to Dharma, he laid hands on other men’s wives.
3.32.13
सर्वदिव्यास्त्रयोक्तारं
यज्ञविघ्नकरं सदा ।
पुरीं भोगवतीं प्राप्य
पराजित्य च वासुकिम् ।
तक्षकस्य प्रियां भार्यां
पराजित्य जहार यः ॥
He had every celestial Astra at his disposal. He habitually ruins Yajñas. He captured the city of Bhōgavati by defeating Vāsuki and he abducted the dear wife of Takshaka.
3.32.14
कैलासपर्वतं गत्वा
विजित्य नरवाहनम् ।
विमानं पुष्पकं तस्य
कामगं वै जहार यः ॥
He went to Kailāsa mountain and defeating the Dēva who rode on a man (Kubēra), wrested the Vimāna named Pushpaka that could travel at one’s will.
3.32.15
वनं चैत्ररथं दिव्यं
नलिनीं नन्दनं वनम् ।
विनाशयति यः क्रोधात्
देवोद्यानानि वीर्यवान् ॥
That valiant one, in his anger, destroyed Caitraratha, Nandana, lotus pond and many celestial gardens.
3.32.16
चन्द्रसूर्यौ  महाभागौ
उत्तिष्ठन्तौ परन्तपौ  ।
निवारयति बाहुभ्यां
यश्शैलशिखरोपमः ॥
He, the scorcher of foe, lofty as a mountain crest, could obstruct with his hands (the movements of) the blessed sun and moon as they rose.
3.32.17
दशवर्षसहस्राणि
तपस्तप्वा महावने ।
पुरा स्वयंभुवे धीरः
शिरांस्युपजहार यः ॥
He did Tapa for ten thousand years in the great Vana, and offered his own heads to the Self-born.
3.32.18
देवदानवगन्धर्व
पिशाचपतगोरगैः ।
अभयं यस्य सङ्ग्रामे
मृत्युतो मानुषादृते ॥
He was, in any fight, immune to death from all beings, Dēvas, Dānavas, Gandharvas, Piṡācas, Patagas Uragas, except humans.
3.32.19
मन्त्रैरभिष्टुतं पुण्यम्
अध्वरेषु द्विजातिभिः ।
हविर्धानेषु यस्सोमम्
उपहन्ति महाबलः ॥
With his immense strength, he would destroy the pious and Mantra-infused Sōma extracted and kept safe by the Brāhmaṇas in the vessels of Havi at the time of Yajñas.
3.32.20
आप्तयज्ञहरं क्रूरं
ब्रह्मघ्नं दुष्टचारिणम् ।
कर्कशं निरनुक्रोशं
प्रजानामहिते रतम् ॥
He was cruel, harsh and compassionless. He destroyed Yajñas that were almost complete, killed Brāhmaṇas, committed terrible deeds, and indulged in harming people, all the time.
3.32.21
रावणं सर्वभूतानां
सर्वलोकभयावहम् ।
राक्षसी भ्रातरं शूरं
सा ददर्श महाबलम् ॥
She, the Rākshasi, saw that mighty strong brother of hers, who made all beings cry and terrorized the entire world.
3.32.22
तं दिव्यवस्त्राभरणं
दिव्यमाल्योपशोभितम् ।
आसने सूपविष्टं च
कालकालमिवोद्यतम् ॥
He wore splendid ornaments and clothing, shone with splendid garlands, seated fittingly on his chair, and looked liked Death to Death itself.
3.32.23
राक्षसेन्द्रं महाभागं
पौलस्त्यकुलनन्दनम् ।
रावणं शत्रुहन्तारं
मन्त्रिभिः परिवारितम् ।
अभिगम्याब्रवीद्वाक्यं
राक्षसी भयविह्वला ॥
The Rākshasi, who was terrified, reached that opulent lord of Rākshasas, delight of the Paulastya lineage, destroyer of enemies and spoke to him in the presence of ministers who were around him.
3.32.25
तमब्रवीद्धीप्तविशाललोचनं
प्रदर्शयित्वा भयमोहमूर्छिता ।
सुदारुणं वाक्यमभीतचारिणी
महात्मना शूर्पणखा विरूपिता ॥
Ṡūrpaṇakhā, who normally moved without fear, was now lost with fear and bewilderment, disfigured by the great man.
She showed her (pathetic) condition to the one with wide and fiery eyes and spoke these ruthless words:
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे द्वात्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty second Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
