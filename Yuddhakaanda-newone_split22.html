Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 22
In this Sarga, Rāma threatens the ocean that he will dry it up. He mounts the Brahmāstra on his bow and draws the string, when the ocean agitates violently and the whole world shakes in fear.
Then the ocean appears in the form of a person in front of Rāma and says that he has to obey the laws of nature and hence cannot freeze or dry himself up, putting the lives of all organisms that live in him at peril. However, he promises to help Rāghava by offering him a shallow area, wherein a bridge can be built by Nala, son of Viṡwakarma.
Rāma then says to the ocean that the arrow he mounted on his bow cannot go in vain and has to be lodged on some one. The ocean requests Rāma to lodge the arrow in an area known as Drumakulya, which is being menaced by Dasyus. Rāma lodges his arrow in that area and all the pits dug by Dasyus become scorched. A geyser erupts where the arrows strikes the ground. Rāma blesses that region to flourish with peace and prosperity, abundant with vegetation.
Then Nala builds the bridge over the one hundred Yojana wide ocean in five days, with the Vānaras bringing the boulders, trees and vines needed, and throwing them in the water.
The Vānaras cross the ocean over the bridge, while Rāma and Lakshmaṇa fly over it riding on the backs of Hanumān and Aṅgada respectively. Everyone reaches the other side of the ocean.
6.22.1
अथोवाच रघुश्रेष्ठः
सागरं दारुणं वचः ॥
Then that best of the Raghus addressed the ocean in these tough words:
6.22.1
अद्य त्वां शोषयिष्यामि
सपातालं महार्णव ।
शरनिर्दग्धतोयस्य
परिशुष्कस्य सागर ।
मया शोषितसत्त्वस्य
पांसुरुत्पद्यते महान् ॥
I shall dry up your waters right down to the nether world, O ocean! A great cloud of dust shall rise, when my arrows have burnt up your waters and you have been completely dried up and all creatures in you have been killed!
6.22.3
मत्कार्मुकविसृष्टेन
शरवर्षेण सागर ।
पारं तेऽद्य गमिष्यन्ति
पद्भिरेव प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
By the deluge of arrows discharged from my bow, this very day the Vānaras shall cross over to the other shore by foot.
6.22.4
विचिन्वन्नाभिजानासि
पौरुषं वापि विक्रमम् ।
दानवालय सन्तापम्
मत्तो नाम गमिष्यसि ॥
O abode of the Dānavas! You have not got the wits to gauge my resolve or prowess, or foresee the suffering that is coming to you in my name.
6.22.5
ब्राह्मेणस्त्रेण संयोज्य
ब्रह्मदण्डनिभं शरम् ।
संयोज्य धनुषि श्रेष्ठे
विचकर्ष महाबलः ॥
Then, he of immense strength mounted an arrow that shone like the Daṇḍa of Brahma, invoking in it the power of the Brahmāstra, on his great bow, and drew the string.
6.22.6
तस्मिन् विकृष्टे सहसा
राघवेण शरासने ।
रोदसी सम्पफालेव
पर्वताश्च चकम्पिरे ॥
Mountains rocked and earth and sky seemed to explode, the moment Rāghava drew the string of the bow.
6.22.7
तमश्च लोकमावव्रे
दिशश्च न चकाशिरे ।
परिचुक्षुभिरे चाशु
सरांसि सरितस्तथा ॥
The world was enveloped in darkness and the four quarters lost their light, and lakes and rivers were thrown into a turmoil.
6.22.8
तिर्यक् च सह नक्षत्रैः
सङ्गतौ चन्द्रभास्करौ ।
भास्करांशुभिरादीप्तम्
तमसा च समावृतम् ।
प्रचकाशे तदाऽऽकाशम्
उल्काशतविदीपितम् ।
अन्तरिक्षाच्च निर्घाता
निर्जग्मुरतुलस्वनाः ॥
The sun, the moon and the stars were thrown together in a row. The sky, obscured by darkness, was but feebly lit up by the sun’s rays, and by the hundreds of meteors burning all over it. Deafening peals of thunder broke out from the upper layers of the sky.
6.22.10
पुस्फुरुश्च घना दिव्या
दिवि मारुतपङ्क्तयः ।
बभञ्ज च तदा वृक्षान्
जलदानुद्वहन्नपि ।
अरुजंश्चैव शेलाग्रान्
शिखराणि प्रभञ्जनः ॥
The sky was filled with splendid clouds. Gale after gale, starting in the higher layers of the sky, carried off the clouds, shattered the mountain peaks and snapped the trees.
6.22.11
दिविस्पृशो महामेघाः
सङ्गताः समहास्वनाः ।
मुमुचुर्वैद्युतानग्नीम्
ते महाशनयस्तदा ॥
Huge clouds that reached up to heaven collected, making a mighty din and discharging the fires of lightning, thunderbolts falling from them.
6.22.12
यानि भूतानि दृश्यानि
चुक्रुशुश्चाशनेः समम् ।
अदृश्यानि च भूतानि
ममुचुर्भैरवस्वनम् ॥
The creatures wailed, their cries as deafening as thunder. The spirits uttered weird, appalling cries.
6.22.13
शिश्यिरे चापि भूतानि
सन्त्रस्तान्युद्विजन्ति च ।
सम्प्रविव्यथिरे चापि
न च पस्पन्दिरे भयात् ॥
The creatures lay motionless, agitated and terrified. Traumatized, they lay senseless from abject fear.
6.22.14
सह भूतैः सतोयोर्मिः
सनागः सह राक्षसः ।
सहसाऽभूत्ततो वेगात्
भीमवेगो महोदधिः ।
योजनं व्यतिचक्राम
वेलामन्यत्र सम्प्लवात् ॥
The great ocean, with all the creatures in it, including Nāgas and Rākshasas, became violently agitated and swelled and with the cresting waves of water violated the shoreline by as much as a Yōjana.
6.22.16
तं तथा समतिक्रान्तम्
नातिचक्राम राघवः ।
समुद्धतममित्रघ्नो
रामो नदनदीपतिम् ॥
Rāma, the destroyer of adversaries, refrained from attacking the ocean further as that lord of east and west flowing rivers (submitted himself with the gesture of) crossing the shoreline.
6.22.17
ततो मध्यात् समुद्रस्य
सागरः स्वयमुत्थितः ।
उदयं हि महाशैलान्
मेरोरिव दिवाकरः ॥
Then from the middle of the ocean rose the lord of the ocean, in person, like the morning sun rising over the great mountain Mēru.
6.22.18
पन्नगैः सह दीप्तास्यैः
समुद्रः प्रत्यदृश्यत ।
स्निग्धवैडूर्यसङ्काशो
जाम्बूनदविभूषितः ॥
He, the ocean, appeared accompanied by serpents with blazing mouths. Covered with gold ornaments, he shone like polished lazulite.
6.22.19
रक्तमाल्याम्बरधरः
पद्मपत्रनिभेक्षणः ।
सर्वपुष्पमयीं दिव्याम्
शिरसा धारयन् स्रजम् ॥
He was clad in red and adorned with red garlands. His eyes were (wide and bright) like lotus petals. He wore wreaths made of every kind of splendid flower on his head.
6.22.20
जातरूपमयैश्चैव
तपनीयविभूषितैः ।
आत्मजानां च रत्नानाम्
भूषितो भूषणोत्तमैः ।
धातुभिर्मण्डितः शैलो
विविधैर्हिमवानिव ॥
He was splendid, covered all over with gold purified in fire. Adorned with the best of ornaments made of gems that he himself produced in profusion, he looked like the Himālaya mountain dazzling with mineral patches.
6.22.21
एकावलीमध्यगतम्
तरलं पाण्डरप्रभम् ।
विपुलेनोरसा बिभ्रत्
कौस्तुभस्य सहोदरम् ॥
He wore, on his broad chest, a gem as a pendant suspended in the middle of a single stranded string that was born along with the (celebrated) Koustubha gem.
6.22.22
आघूर्णिततरङ्गौघः
कालिकानिलसङ्कुलः ।
उद्वर्तितमहाग्राहः
सम्भ्रान्तोरगराक्षसः ।
गङगासिन्धुप्रधानाभिः
अपगाभिःसमावृतः ।
देवतानां सरूपाभिः
नानारूपाभिरीश्वरः ।
सागरः समुपक्रम्य
पूर्वमामन्त्र्य वीर्यवान् ।
अब्रवीत् प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यम्
राघवं शरपाणिनम् ॥
Amidst the tumult of the raging rows of billows driven by the high winds that brought the clouds, giant crocodiles floating on the waters with their bellies up, and the Uragas and the Rākshasas fallen into a state of dizziness, accompanied by the great rivers like Gaṅga and Sindhu who had taken the form of various divinities, their valiant lord presented himself before Rāghava who was adorned with a bow in his hand. After paying due respects with his palms joined in reverence, he said these words:
6.22.25
पृथिवी वायुराकाशम्
आपो ज्योतिश्च राघव ।
स्वभावे सौम्य तिष्ठन्ति
शाश्वतं मार्गमाश्रिताः ॥
My dear! (The elements), earth, air, space, water and fire, stay true to their nature, obeying the eternal laws.
6.22.26
तत्स्वभावो ममाप्येष
यदगाधोऽहमप्लवः ।
विकारस्तु भवेद्गाध
एतत्ते प्रवदाम्यहम् ॥
Similarly, I would like to emphasize, plumbless depth and impassability are part of my innate nature. To shrink in depth would mean violating the true nature of my being.
6.22.27
न कामान्न च लोभाद्वा
न भयात् पार्थिवात्मज ।
ग्राहनक्राकुलजलम्
स्तम्भयेयं कथञ्चन ॥
There is no way that I would freeze these waters that are teeming with fish, great and small, O prince, either for fun or for gain or because of fear.
6.22.28
विधास्ये येन गन्तासि
विषहिष्ये ह्यहं तथा ।
ग्राहा न प्रहरिष्यन्ति
यावत् सेना तरिष्यति ।
हरीणां तरणे राम
करिष्यामि यथा स्थलम् ॥
But I shall do all that I can, O Rāma; I shall create room for the Vānaras to cross. and I shall see to it that the crocodiles do not attack, till the army crosses.
6.22.30
तमब्रवीत्तदा रामः
उद्यतो हि नदीपते ।
अमोधोऽयं महाबाणः
कस्मिन् देशे निपात्यताम् ॥
Thereupon Rāma told him, ‘This mighty arrow is already mounted on the bow, where shall I lodge this, O lord of the streams!'
6.22.31
रामस्य वचनं श्रुत्वा
तं च दृष्ट्वा महाशरम् ।
महोदधिर्महातेजा
राघवं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing what Rāma said and looking at that mighty arrow, the immensely powerful lord of ocean said these words to Rāghava:
6.22.32
उत्तरेणावकाशोऽस्ति
कश्चित् पुण्यतमो मम ।
द्रुमकुल्य इति ख्यतो
लोके ख्यातो यथा भवान् ॥
There is this most sacred region, north of me, well known in this world as Drumakulya, just as well known as you are.
6.22.33
उग्रदर्शनकर्माणो
बहवस्तत्र दस्यवः ।
आभीरप्रमुखाः पापाः
पिबन्ति सलिलं मम ॥
There are multitudes of evil, fierce looking and terribly menacing Dasyus like Abheera that suck my water away, in that region.
6.22.34
तैस्तु संस्पर्शनं प्राप्तैः
न सहे पापकर्मभिः ।
अमोघः क्रियतां राम
तत्र तेषु शरोत्तमः ॥
I cannot stand even the touch of those evildoers. Let your great arrow find its unerring target in them, O Rāma.
6.22.35
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
सागरस्य स राघवः ।
मुमोच तं शरं दीप्तम्
वीरः सागरदर्शनात् ॥
On hearing what the ocean said, the heroic Rāghava shot the blazing arrow at the spot indicated by the ocean.
6.22.36
तेन तं मरुकान्तारम्
पृथिव्यां खलु विश्रुतम् ।
निपातितः शरो यत्र
दीप्ताशनिसमप्रभः ॥
The place where his arrow fell blazing like fierce lightning had long been known to all the world as the Impassable Desert.
6.22.37
ननाद च तदा तत्र
वसुधा शल्यपीडिता ।
तस्माद्व्रणमुखात्तोयम्
उत्पपात रसातलात् ॥
The earth, where the arrow struck it, groaned with pain, and where it rent it, spurted up a column of water from Rasātala.
6.22.38
स बभूव तदा कूपो
व्रण इत्यभिविश्रुतः ।
सततं चोत्थितं तोयम्
समुद्रस्येव दृश्यते ॥
That well of water has come to be known as ‘Vraṇa,’ (The Wound) from which spurts water perpetually, making it look like a ocean.
6.22.39
अवदारणशब्दश्च
दारुणः समपद्यत ।
तस्मात्तद्बाणपातेन
आपः कुक्षिष्वशोषयत् ॥
There arose a terrible rending sound from it and water (polluted by the Dasyus) from all the (existing) deep pits was dried up with the impact of the arrow.
6.22.40
विख्यातं त्रिषु लोकेषु
मरुकान्तरामेव तत् ।
शोषयित्वा ततः कुक्षिम्
रामो दशरथात्मजः ।
वरं तस्मै ददौ विद्वान्
मरवेऽमरविक्रमः ॥
After drying up all the pits in that area known in all the three worlds as the Impassable Desert, Rāma, the wise son of Daṡaratha, an equal to Dēvas in prowess, granted a boon to that desert.
6.22.41
पशव्यश्चाल्परोगश्च
फलमूलरसायुतः ।
बहुस्नेहो बहुक्षीरः
सुगन्धिर्विविधौषधः ।
एवमेतैर्गुणैर्युक्तो
बहुभिः सततं मरुः ।
रामस्य वरदानाच्च
शिवः पन्था बभूव ह ॥
Thanks to that boon, that desert became a place for life to flourish bearing almost no trace of disease, with fruits, roots, honey, ghee, milk, pleasant smells, rich herbage and other similar marks of peace and prosperity growing in abundance.
6.22.43
तस्मिन् दग्धे तदा कुक्षौ
समुद्रः सरितां पतिः ।
राघवं सर्वशास्त्रज्ञम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
When all those pits dried up thus, the ocean, the lord of streams, said these words to Rāghava, a savant of all Ṡāstras:
6.22.44
अयं सौम्य नलो नाम
तनुजो विश्वकर्मणः ।
पित्रा दत्तवरः श्रीमान्
प्रतिमो विश्वकर्मणा ॥
This Nala, my dear, is the son of Viṡwakarma. With the boon given by his father, this blessed one is as capable as Viṡwakarma himself.
6.22.45
एष सेतुं महोत्साहः
करोतु मयि वानरः ।
तमहं धारयिष्यामि
तथा ह्येष यथा पिता ॥
Let this Vānara, who is gifted with energy and zest like his father, build a bridge on me and I will provide the support it needs.
6.22.46
एवमुक्त्वोदधिर्नष्टः
समुत्थाय नलस्तदा ।
अब्रवीद्वानरश्रेष्ठो
वाक्यं रामं महाबलम् ॥
The ocean disappeared, saying thus. Then Nala, the best of Vānaras got up and said these words to Rāma of immense strength:
6.22.47
अहं सेतुं करिष्यामि
विस्तीर्णे वरुणालये ।
पितुः सामर्थ्यमास्थाय
तत्त्वमाह महोदधिः ॥
I will, using the abilities bestowed by my father, as has been mentioned by the great ocean, build a bridge over this wide abode of Varuṇa.
6.22.48
दण्ड एव वरो लोके
पुरुषस्येति मे मतिः ।
धिक् क्षमामकृतज्ञेषु
सान्त्वं दानमथापि वा ॥
I am convinced that force is the only thing that is effective on people, in this world. Patience, pleading and being charitable are wholly wasted on ingrates.
6.22.49
अयं हि सागरो भीमः
सेतुकर्मदिदृक्षया ।
ददौ दण्डभयाद्गाधम्
राघवाय महोदधिः ॥
It is only the fear of the rod that made this fierce and mighty ocean interested in seeing the bridge built and make itself shallow for the sake of Rāghava.
6.22.50
मम मातुर्वरो दत्तो
मन्दरे विश्वकर्मणा ।
मया तु सदृशः पुत्रः
तव देवि भविष्यति ॥
My mother was given the boon on the Mandara mountain by Viṡwakarma, 'O Dēvi, you will have a son who would be just like me!'
6.22.51
औरसस्तस्य पुत्रोऽहम्
सदृशो विश्वकर्मणा ।
स्मारितोऽस्म्यहमेतेन
तत्त्वमाह महोदधिः ।
न चाप्यहमनुक्तो वै
प्रब्रूयामात्मनो गुणान् ॥
Born of his seed, I am as capable as Viṡwakarma himself. I am reminded of this by the great ocean, and what he said is true. I am not the one to trumpet my own capabilities unless I am asked.
6.22.53
समर्थश्चाप्यहं सेतुम्
कर्तुं वै वरुणालये ।
तस्मादयैव बध्नन्तु
सेतुं वानरपुङ्गवाः ॥
I am surely capable of building a bridge over this ocean. Let the bulls of Vānaras (begin the work of) building the bridge right away.
6.22.54
ततोऽतिसृष्टा रामेण
सर्वतो हरियूथपाः ।
अभ्युपेतुर्महारण्यम्
हृष्टाः शतसहस्रशः ॥
Then, sent by Rāma, the Vānara warriors in their hundreds and thousands, flew joyfully to the forest from all sides.
6.22.55
ते नगान्नगसङ्काशाः
शाखामृगगणर्षभाः ।
बभञ्जुर्वानरास्तत्र
प्रचकर्षुश्च सागरम् ॥
Those bulls among the hordes of beasts of the branch, each resembling a mountain, cut down the trees and dragged them to the ocean.
6.22.56
ते सालैश्चाश्वकर्णैश्च
धवैर्वंशैश्च वानराः ।
कुटजैरर्जुनैस्तालैः
तिलकैस्तिनिशैरपि ।
बिल्वैश्च सप्तपर्णैश्च
कर्णिकारैश्च पुष्पितैः ।
चूतैश्चाशोकवृक्षैश्च
सागरं समपूरयन् ॥
Those Vānaras filled the ocean with Sāla, Aṡwakarṇa, Dhava, bamboo, Kuṭaja, Arjuna, palm, Tilaka, Tiniṡa, Bilva, Saptaparṇa, and Karṇikāra in bloom, and Cūta and Aṡōka trees.
6.22.58
समूलांश्च विमूलांश्च
पादपान् हरिसत्तमाः ।
इन्द्रकेतूनिवोद्यम्य
प्रजह्रुर्हरयस्तरून् ॥
Those eminent Vānaras lifted and carried the trees like one would the flagstaff of Indra, some of the trees along with the roots, and others broken at the base.
6.22.59
तालान् दाडिमगुल्मांश्च
नारिकेलविभीतकान् ।
करीरान् बकुलान्निम्बान्
समाजह्रुः समन्ततः ॥
They brought stunted palms, pomegranate bushes, coconut palms, Vibheetaka, Kareera, Bakula and neem trees from everywhere.
6.22.60
हस्तिमात्रान् महाकायाः
पाषाणांश्च महाबलाः ।
पर्वतांश्च समुत्पाट्य
यन्त्रैः परिवहन्ति च ॥
They of huge bodies and immense strength lifted boulders of the size of elephants and whole hills, and carried them on machines.
6.22.61
प्रक्षिप्यमाणैरचलैः
सहसा जलमुद्धतम् ।
समुत्पतितमाकाशम्
उपासर्पत्ततस्ततः ॥
As the rocks were being thrown into it, the water rose into the sky immediately and fell down back all over the place.
6.22.62
समुद्रं क्षोभयामासुः
वानराश्च समन्ततः ।
सूत्राण्यन्ये प्रगृह्णन्ति
ह्यायतं शतयोजनम् ॥
The Vānaras thus created turbulence in the water everywhere. Others held strings stretched all through the hundred Yōjanas.
6.22.63
नलश्चक्रे महासेतुम्
मध्ये नदनदीपतेः ।
स तथा क्रियते सेतुः
वानरैर्घोरकर्मभिः ॥
Thus Nala and the Vānaras of formidable deeds built a humongous bridge across the lord of the east and west flowing rivers.
6.22.64
दण्डानन्ये प्रगृह्णन्ति
विचिन्वन्ति तथा परे ।
वानराः शतशस्तत्र
रामस्याज्ञापुरस्सराः ।
मेघाभैः पर्वताग्रैश्च
तृणैः काष्ठैर्बबन्धिरे ॥
Some gathered the logs and some carried them. The Vānaras, as huge as clouds and mountain peaks, in their hundreds, followed the orders of Rāma and built the bridge with vines and logs.
6.22.65
पुष्पिताग्रैश्च तरुभिः
सेतुं बध्नन्ति वानराः ।
पाषाणांश्च गिरिप्रख्यान्
गिरीणां शिखराणि च ।
दृश्यन्ते परिधावन्तो
गृह्यवारणसन्निभाः ॥
The Vānaras used trees, whose tops were covered with flowers, to build the bridge. Huge like elephants, they could be seen hurrying, carrying mountain peaks and boulders that were as huge as mountains.
6.22.67
शिलानां क्षिप्यमाणानाम्
शैलानां च निपात्यताम् ।
बभूव तुमुलः शब्दः
तदा तस्मिन् महोदधौ ॥
As the rocks were thrown and boulders were lodged, there arose a mighty din in that great ocean.
6.22.68
कृतानि प्रथमेनाह्ना
योजनानि चतुर्दश ।
प्रहृष्टैर्गजसङ्काशैः
त्वरमाणैः प्लवङ्गमैः ॥
The buoyant Vānaras, as huge as elephants made haste to complete a stretch of fourteen Yōjanas of the bridge on the first day.
6.22.69
द्वितीयेन तथा चाह्ना
योजनानि तु विंशतिः ।
कृतानि प्लवगैस्तूर्णम्
भीमकायैर्महाबलैः ॥
The Vānaras of terrific bodies and immense strength quickly completed another twenty Yōjanas on the second day.
6.22.70
अह्ना तृतीयेन तथा
योजनानि कृतानि तु ।
त्वरमाणैर्महाकायैः
एकविंशतिरेव च ॥
And on the third day, they of humungous bodies made all haste and completed another twenty one Yōjanas.
6.22.71
चतुर्थेन तथा चाह्ना
द्वाविंशतिरथापि च ।
योजनानि महावेगैः
कृतानि त्वरितैस्तु तैः ॥
On the fourth day, they hastened to swiftly complete another twenty two Yōjanas.
6.22.72
पञ्चमेन तथा चाह्ना
प्लवगैः क्षिप्रकारिभिः ।
योजनानि त्रयोविंशत्
सुवेलमधिकृत्य वै ॥
On the fifth day, the rest of the twenty three Yōjanas were completed to reach the other shore, by the Vānaras who worked in all haste.
6.22.73
स वानरवरः श्रीमान्
विश्वकर्मात्मजो बली ।
बबन्ध सागरे सेतुम्
यथा चास्य पिता तथा ॥
That strong, blessed and best of the Vānaras, the son of Viṡwakarma, who was as skilled as his father, thus built the bridge across the ocean.
6.22.74
स नलेन कृतः सेतुः
सागरे मकरालये ।
शुशुभे सुभगः श्रीमान्
स्वातीपथ इवाम्बरे ॥
That splendid bridge built by Nala shone bright on the sea, the abode of crocodiles, like the track of the constellation of Swāti in the sky.
6.22.75
ततो देवाः सगन्धर्वाः
सिद्धाश्च परमर्षयः ।
आगम्य गगने तस्थुः
द्रष्टुकामास्तदद्भुतम् ॥
The Dēvas, Gandharvas, Siddhas and the great Ṛshis came and hovered in the sky, eager to see that marvel.
6.22.76
दशयोजनविस्तीर्णम्
शतयोजनमायतम् ।
ददृशुर्देवगन्धर्वा
नलसेतुं सुदुष्करम् ॥
Dēvas and Gandharvas beheld that bridge, ten Yōjanas wide and a hundred Yōjanas long, a near-impossible achievement of Nala.
6.22.77
आप्लवन्तः प्लवन्तश्च
गर्जन्तश्च प्लवङ्गमाः ।
तदचिन्त्यमसह्यं च
अद्भुतं रोमहर्षणम् ।
ददृशुः सर्वभूतानि
सागरे सेतुबन्धनम् ॥
The Vānaras shouted and jumped all over it, while all the creatures beheld the building of the bridge across the ocean that was simply marvelous and and gave goosebumps to the beholder.
6.22.78
तानि कोटिसहस्राणि
वानराणां महौजसाम् ।
बध्नन्तः सागरे सेतुम्
जग्मुः पारं महोदधेः ॥
Thus the thousand crores of Vānaras of immense power built the bridge over the ocean and crossed to the other shore of that great body of water.
6.22.79
विशालः सुकृतः श्रीमान्
सुभूमिः सुसमाहितः ।
अशोभत महासेतुः
सीमन्त इव सागरे ॥
Wide, well-made, pleasing to the eye, level and smooth, the great bridge shone on the ocean like the line of the parting of the hair on a woman’s head.
6.22.80
ततः पारे समुद्रस्य
गदापाणिर्विभीषणः ।
परेषामभियानार्थम्
अतिष्ठत् सचिवैः सह ॥
Vibheeshaṇa, adorned with mace in his hand, stood along with his counsellors on the shore of the ocean, ready for the attack on the enemy.
6.22.81
सुग्रीवस्तु ततः प्राह
रामं सत्यपराक्रमम् ॥
Then Sugreeva said to Rāma of true valor:
6.22.82
हनुमन्तं त्वमारोह
अङ्गदं चापि लक्ष्मणः ।
अयं हि विपुलो वीर
सागरो मकारालयः ।
वैहायसौ युवामेतौ
वानरौ तारयिष्यतः ॥
You may ride on Hanumān’s back and let Lakshmaṇa ride on Aṅgada’s. The way over the sea, the home of crocodiles, is a long one, O Veera. Flying, these two will carry both of you across.
6.22.83
अग्रतस्तस्य सैन्यस्य
श्रीमान् रामः सलक्ष्मणः ।
जगाम धन्वी धर्मात्मा
सुग्रीवेण समन्वितः ॥
The blessed Rāma, armed with bow in his hand, proceeded along with Lakshmaṇa, leading the army from the front, with Sugreeva accompanying them.
6.22.84
अन्ये मध्येन गच्छन्ति
पार्श्वतोऽन्ये प्लवङ्गमाः ।
सलिले प्रपतन्त्यन्ये
मार्गमन्ये न लेभिरे ।
केचिद्वैहायसगताः
सुपर्णा इव पुप्लुवुः ॥
Some kept to the middle of the bridge while some walked on the sides. Some others dived into the water finding no room (on the bridge). Some took to the skies, flying like Garuḍa.
6.22.86
घोषेण महता तस्य
सिन्धोर्घोषं समुच्छ्रितम् ।
भीममन्तर्दधे भीमा
तरन्ती हरिवाहिनी ॥
The great din raised by the formidable Vānara army, as it crossed over, drowned even the roaring noise of the ocean.
6.22.87
वानराणां हि सा तीर्णा
वाहिनी नलसेतुना ।
तीरे निविविशे राज्ञा
बहुमूलफलोदके ॥
The Vānara army, having thus crossed over by the bridge built by Nala, camped on the shore that was abundant with roots, fruits and water as commanded by the king.
6.22.88
तमद्भुतं राघवकर्म दुष्करं
समीक्ष्य देवाः सह सिद्धचारणैः ।
उपेत्य रामं सहसा महर्षिभिः
तमभ्यषिञ्चन् सुशुभैर्जलैः पृथक् ॥
Seeing that all but impossible and marvelous exploit of Rāghava, the Dēvas, along with the Siddhas and Cāraṇas approached Rāma and, along with Maharshis, sprinkled auspicious waters, each one individually, on Rāma.
6.22.89
जयस्व शत्रून् नरदेव मेदिनीं
ससागरां पालय शाश्वतीः समाः ।
इतीव रामं नरदेवसत्कृतं
शुभैर्वचोभिर्विविधैरपूजयन् ॥
They showered on Rāma, the one venerated by all kings, many an auspicious blessing, saying, ‘O lord of men! Victory to you over the enemies! Rule the earth up to the ocean’s edge for countless years!’
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्वाविंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty second Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
