Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 52
In this Sarga, Rāma tells Sumantra to go back to Ayōdhyā. Sumantra feels sad for the fate of Rāma, and feels disillusioned about being good natured. Rāma tells him to take care of Daṡaratha and conveys to him the messages to be carried to Daṡaratha, Kousalyā, and Bharata. Sumantra pleads with Rāma to allow him to come with him to the Vana. But Rāma convinces him to go back, saying that, that is the only way Kaikēyee would trust that Rāma has really left for the Vana.
Then Rāma turns to Guha and asks him to get the sap of a banyan tree. He matts his locks and helps Lakshmaṇa also matt his locks. Then he bids farewell to Guha and gets into the boat arranged for him, along with Seetā and Lakshmaṇa.
Seetā prays to Gaṅgā as they cross the river. The three of them, then reach the other side of the river and enter the Vana. Rāma tells Lakshmaṇa to be ever vigilant. He asks Lakshmaṇa to lead the way, while he protects everyone from behind, with Seetā walking in the middle.
2.52.1
प्रभातायां तु शर्वर्याम्
पृथुवक्षा महायशाः ।
उवाच रामः सौमित्रिम्
लक्ष्मणं शुभलक्षणम् ॥
When the night passed and day dawned, Rāma, of imposingly broad chest and of great renown spoke to the well chiseled Lakshmaṇa, the son of Sumitrā:
2.52.2
भास्करोदयकालोऽयम्
गता भगवती निशा ।
असौ सुकृष्णो विहगः
कोकिलस्तात कूजति ।
बर्हिणानां च निर्घोषः
श्रूयते नदतां वने ।
तराम जाह्नवीं सौम्य
शीघ्रगां सागरङ्गमाम् ॥
My dear! The sun is rising, the blessed night has passed, the jet black cuckoo is warbling, and the notes of the peacocks calling in the Vana are vibrant. Let us cross the swift Jāhnavi that hastens to the sea!
Jāhnavi is another name for Gaṅgā.
2.52.4
विज्ञाय रामस्य वचः
सौमित्रिर्मित्रनन्दनः ।
गुहमामन्त्र्य सूतं च
सोऽतिष्ठद्भ्रातुरग्रतः ॥
Sensing Rāma's intent, the son of Sumitrā called upon Guha and the charioteer, and stood waiting in front of his brother.
2.52.5
स तु रामस्य वचनम्
निशम्य प्रतिगृह्य च ।
स्थपतिस्तूर्णमाहूय
सचिवानिदमब्रवीत् ॥
The hunter chief heard Rāma's words and acted upon them. He immediately called his ministers and said to them:
2.52.6
अस्य वाहनसंयुक्ताम्
कर्णग्राहवतीं शुभाम् ।
सुप्रतारां दृढां तीर्थे
शीघ्रं नावमुपाहर ॥
Go, and bring quickly to the launching area, a comely, sturdy and smooth riding boat along with rowers and helmsman.
2.52.7
तं निशम्य समादेशम्
गुहामात्यगणो महान् ।
उपोह्य रुचिरां नावम्
गुहाय प्रत्यवेदयत् ॥
Hearing Guha's orders, his large retinue of officials brought a splendid boat and showed it to him.
2.52.8
ततः स प्राञ्जलिर्भूत्वा
गुहो राघवमब्रवीत् ॥
And Guha with folded hands informed Rāghava,
2.52.8
उपस्थितेयं नौर्देव
भूयः किं करवाणि ते ।
तवामरसुतप्रख्य
तर्तुं सागरगां नदीम् ।
नौरियं पुरुषव्याघ्र
तां त्वमारोह सुव्रत ॥
The boat is ready, my lord, what else can I do for you? O tiger among men, equal to the son of Dēvas! This boat is ready for you to cross the ocean-bound river! O man of great discipline, please get into it!
2.52.10
अथोवाच महातेजा
रामो गुहमिदं वचः ।
कृतकामोऽस्मि भवता
शीघ्रमारोप्यतामिति ॥
And the glorious Rāma said to Guha, 'You have brought me just what I needed. Let everything be loaded on board!'
2.52.11
ततः कलापान् सन्नह्य
खड्गौ बध्वा च धन्विनौ ।
जग्मतुर्येन तौ गङ्गाम्
सीतया सह राघवौ ॥
Then, fixing their quivers, girding their swords, and holding bows in their hands, the princes of the Raghu dynasty, along with Seetā headed towards Gaṅgā.
2.52.12
राममेव तु धर्मज्ञम्
उपगम्य विनीतवत् ।
किमहं करवाणीति
सूतः प्राञ्जलिरब्रवीत् ॥
The charioteer, then humbly approached Rāma, who knew Dharma, and asked with palms joined in reverence, 'What shall I do?'
2.52.13
ततोऽब्रवीद्दाशरथिः सुमन्त्रं
स्पृशन् करेणोत्तमदक्षिणेन ।
सुमन्त्र! शीघ्रं पुनरेव याहि
राज्ञः सकाशे भव चाप्रमत्तः ॥
Touching Sumantra with his right hand, the son of Daṡaratha said, 'Sumantra, go back quickly and attend upon the King in full alert'.
2.52.14
निवर्तस्वेत्युवाचैव
ह्येतावद्धि कृतं मम ।
रथं विहाय पद्भ्यां तु
गमिष्यामि महावनम् ॥
I am glad that you have done all that you did! Please go back with the chariot. And I will proceed on foot from here on to the Vana.
2.52.15
आत्मानं त्वभ्यनुज्ञातम्
अवेक्ष्यार्तः स सारथिः ।
सुमन्त्रः पुरुषव्याघ्रम्
चक्ष्वाकमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Finding himself discharged, the charioteer Sumantra felt sad and said to that tiger among men, the scion of the Ikshwākus:
2.52.16
नातिक्रान्तमिदं लोके
पुरुषेणेह केनचित् ।
तव सभ्रातृभार्यस्य
वासः प्राकृतवद्वने ॥
You are going to live in the forest with your wife and brother like any common man. No one has ever done something like this.
2.52.17
न मन्ये ब्रह्मचर्येऽस्ति
स्वधीते वा फलोदयः ।
मार्दवार्जवयोर्वापि
त्वां चेद्व्यसनमागतम् ॥
If hardships like this could befall even on you, I can only think that observing continence, studying Vedas, maintaining a gentle disposition and even being honest, are no more than futile.
If Vālmeeki was writing a Dharma Ṡastra, he would not have had the liberty to express such a dispirited notion, of the futility of gentle disposition and honesty, etc., through a high-minded character like Sumantra.
But Ramayana is a Kavya and Vālmeeki is a poet. It allows him to portray his characters as down-to-earth humans with every mood and emotion that they undergo. The characters can express what they feel, reflecting not only their wisdom and values, but also the emotion of the moment.
2.52.18
सह राघव वैदेह्या
भ्रात्रा चैव वने वसन् ।
त्वं गतिं प्राप्स्यसे वीर
त्रीन् लोकांस्तु जयन्निव ॥
O Rāma! O Veera! You will certainly be rewarded with the same good times ahead as you would attain by conquering all the three worlds, for living in the Vana along with the princess of Vidēha and your brother.
2.52.19
वयं खलु हता राम
ये त्वयाप्युपवञ्चिताः ।
कैकेय्या वशमेष्यामः
पापाया दुःखभागिनः ॥
As for us, we are certainly hurt, having also received lesser of your grace. We are destined for endless suffering, falling into the hands of the sinful Kaikēyee.
Sumantra is lovingly accusing Rāma for abandoning them by going to Vana!
2.52.20
इति ब्रुवन्नात्मसमम्
सुमन्त्रः सारथिस्तदा ।
दृष्ट्वा दूरगतं रामम्
दुःखार्तो रुरुदे चिरम् ॥
The charioteer Sumantra, saying this and seeing Rāma, whom he cherished like his own life, go far away, wept for long, overwhelmed by grief.
2.52.21
ततस्तु विगते बाष्पे
सूतं स्पृष्टोदकं शुचिम् ।
रामस्तु मधुरं वाक्यम्
पुनः पुनरुवाच तम् ॥
When he stopped shedding tears and purified himself by touching water, Rāma spoke to him in all kindness, assuring him again and again:
2.52.22
इक्ष्वाकूणां त्वया तुल्यम्
सुहृदं नोपलक्षये ।
यथा दशरथो राजा
मां न शोचेत्तथा कुरु ॥
I know no one who is an equal to you in the love you bear to the Ikshwākus; and you must see to it that King Daṡaratha does not grieve on account of me.
2.52.23
शोकोपहतचेताश्च
वृद्धश्च जगतीपतिः ।
कामभावावसन्नश्च
तस्मादेतद्ब्रवीमि ते ॥
I say this to you because the lord of the lands is now old, distressed with grief, and is under the burden of passion.
2.52.24
यद्यदाज्ञापयेत्किञ्चित्
स महात्मा महीपतिः ।
कैकेय्याः प्रियकामार्थम्
कार्यं तदविकाङ्क्षया ॥
Whatever the King, the Mahātma orders to be done for the sake of pleasing Kaikēyee should be done with the least hesitation.
2.52.25
एतदर्थं हि राज्यानि
प्रशासति नरेश्वराः ।
यदेषां सर्वकृत्येषु
मनो न प्रतिहन्यते ॥
After all, kings take upon the burden of the ruling their kingdoms only with the expectation that their will never gets negated.
This Ṡlōka speaks about a fundamental principle of hierarchical systems.
Translated into the lingo of modern corporate world, it says: "Bosses take upon the brunt of management expecting in return no insubordination from the staff".
This is an implicit bargain in the hierarchical system: Boss: "I will take care of you and in return I expect you to follow my will." Staff: "I will listen to you and in return I expect you to take care of me."
This same implicit bargain exists between a husband and wife in conventional marriages and between a parent and child in conventional families. Marriages and families fail only when one party understands the bargain and the other does not.
In these two Ṡlōkas, Rāma is indirectly explaining how he is keeping his end of this implicit bargain, by going to the Vana.
2.52.26
यद्यथा स महाराजो
नालीकमधिगच्छति ।
न च ताम्यति दुःखेन
सुमन्त्र कुरु तत्तथा ॥
O Sumantra! Do everything to make sure that the king's words do not become untrue and he does not feel the need to anguish over anything.
2.52.27
अदृष्टदुःखं राजानम्
वृद्धमार्यं जितेन्द्रियम् ।
ब्रूयास्त्वमभिवाद्यैव
मम हेतोरिदं वचः ॥
Offering salutations on my behalf, please tell these words to the noble and aged king, who has known no sorrow and has mastery over his senses:
2.52.28
नैवाहमनुशोचामि
लक्ष्मणो न च मैथिली ।
अयोध्यायाश्च्युताश्चेति
वने वत्स्यामहेति च ॥
Neither I, nor the princess of Mithilā, nor Lakshmaṇa, grieve for having to leave Ayōdhyā or to live in the Vana.
2.52.29
चतुर्दशसु वर्षेषु
निवृत्तेषु पुनः पुनः ।
लक्ष्मणं मां च सीतां च
द्रक्ष्यसे क्षिप्रमागतान् ॥
We will be back as soon as fourteen years are complete, and you shall be pleased to see Lakshmaṇa, Seetā and me.
2.52.30
एवमुक्त्वा तु राजानम्
मातरं च सुमन्त्र मे ।
अन्याश्च देवीस्सहिताः
कैकेयीं च पुनः पुनः ।
आरोग्यं ब्रूहि कौसल्याम्
अथ पादाभिवन्दनम् ।
सीताया मम चाऽऽर्यस्य
वचनाल्लक्ष्मणस्य च ॥
O Sumantra, having said this to the King, stress upon my mother, and all the other ladies including Kaikēyee, that Seetā, the noble Lakshmaṇa and I make our prostrations to them and enquire about their well-being.
2.52.32
ब्रूयाश्च हि महाराजम्
भरतं क्षिप्रमानय ।
आगतश्चापि भरतः
स्थाप्यो नृपमते पदे ॥
Please tell the great king to send for Bharata immediately and to install him in a position that he, the king, would like, as soon as Bharata arrives.
2.52.33
भरतं च परिष्वज्य
यौवराज्येऽभिषिच्य च ।
अस्मत्सन्तापजं दुःखम्
न त्वामभिभविष्यति ॥
And once Bharata is installed as the crown prince and is taken into your embrace, the grief on my account will subside automatically.
This Ṡlōka speaks about one of the basic aspects of human psychology.
When you take away a toy from a child, the child will be overwhelmed with grief. But once you give another toy in its place, even though it may not be the exact equivalent, the child will feel pacified and will get used to it, forgetting the grief of not having the original toy.
Politicians and managers exploit this basic human nature all the time. They take away something and then give something else to soothe the mood of the people, though for devious reasons.
You can also use this technique, when genuine reasons demand, to make your life and the life of others better.
2.52.34
भरतश्चापि वक्तव्यो
यथा राजनि वर्तसे ।
तथा मातृषु वर्तेथाः
सर्वास्वेवाविशेषतः ॥
It would be appropriate to implore with Bharata that he should treat all mothers just as he treats the king, with no difference whatsoever.
2.52.35
यथा च तव कैकेयी
सुमित्रा च विशेषतः ।
तथैव देवी कौसल्या
मम माता विशेषतः ॥
You should look upon my mother, the lady Kousalyā particularly, exactly as you do upon Kaikēyee and Sumitrā.
2.52.36
तातस्य प्रियकामेन
यौवराज्यमपेक्षता ।
लोकयोरुभयोः शक्यम्
त्वया यत्सुखमेधितुम् ॥
By discharging the duties of the crown prince to the desire and liking of the father, you would attain the best in this world as well as hereafter.
2.52.37
निवर्त्यमानो रामेण
सुमन्त्रः शोककर्शितः ।
तत्सर्वं वचनं श्रुत्वा
स्नेहात्काकुत्स्थमब्रवीत् ॥
Thus asked by Rāma to go back, Sumantra felt enervated with grief. Hearing it all to the end, he spoke to the descendant of Kākutstha, spurred by affection:
2.52.38
यदहं नोपचारेण
ब्रूयां स्नेहादविक्लबः ।
भक्तिमानिति तत्तावत्
वाक्यं त्वं क्षन्तुमर्हसि ॥
If whatever I say out of affection and without hesitation, seem to be of questionable obedience, I hope you will forgive me for that, keeping my unwavering devotion to you in mind!
It is hard to find such a simple, non-ornamental, direct and beautiful language like you find in this conversation between Sumantra and Rāma, elsewhere!
2.52.39
कथं हि त्वद्विहीनोऽहम्
प्रतियास्यामि तां पुरीम् ।
तव तावद्वियोगेन
पुत्रशोकाकुलामिव ॥
How can I go back without you to that city which, bereaved of you, would be distraught with grief as for a child one has lost !
2.52.40
सराममपि तावन्मे
रथं दृष्ट्वा तदा जनः ।
विना रामं रथं दृष्ट्वा
विदीर्येतापि सा पुरी ॥
They grieved so much seeing my chariot with Rāma in it, earlier. They will be no less than shattered to see the chariot without Rāma in it, now.
What heart-wrenching poetry in these two Ṡlōkas !
2.52.41
दैन्यं हि नगरी गच्छेत्
दृष्ट्वा शून्यमिमं रथम् ।
सूतावशेषं स्वं सैन्यम्
हतवीरमिवाऽहवे ॥
The city will be plunged in despair, seeing the chariot empty but for the driver, like an army that sees a chariot without the commander, who dies in the battle.
2.52.42
दूरेऽपि निवसन्तं त्वाम्
मानसेनाग्रतः स्थितम् ।
चिन्तयन्तोऽद्य नूनं त्वाम्
निराहाराः कृताः प्रजाः ॥
Though you would be living far away, you will be right in the forefront of their thoughts, and they will not be able to even touch food for sure, thinking of you.
2.52.43
दृष्टं तद्धि त्वया राम
यादृशं त्वत्प्रवासने ।
प्रजानां सङ्कुलं वृत्तम्
त्वच्छोकक्लान्तचेतसाम् ॥
You yourself saw, O Rāma, the noise and tumult among the crowds, and their exhaustion by grief at the time of your departure!
2.52.44
आर्तनादो हि यः पौरैः
मुक्तस्त्वद्विप्रवासने ।
सरथं मां निशाम्यैव
कुर्युः शतगुणं ततः ॥
Those distressed cries of the citizens at the time of your departure will only rise hundredfold upon seeing me back alone with the chariot.
2.52.45
अहं किं चापि वक्ष्यामि
देवीं तव सुतो मया ।
नीतोऽसौ मातुलकुलम्
सन्तापं मा कृथा इति ।
असत्यमपि नैवाहम्
ब्रूयां वचनमीदृशम् ।
कथमप्रियमेवाहम्
ब्रूयां सत्यमिदं वचः ॥
And then what shall I tell the lady, your mother? Could I say, 'I left your son at his maternal uncle's house, and there is nothing to worry!'
I cannot say such a thing, which is untrue. But how can I tell the truth either, when it is so unpleasant?
Leaving a child to be raised in the maternal uncle's house is a custom that continues from the days of Rāmāyaṇa to even this day in India!
2.52.47
मम तावन्नियोगस्थाः
त्वद्बन्धुजनवाहिनः ।
कथं रथं त्वया हीनम्
प्रवक्ष्यन्ति हयोत्तमाः ॥
How will these well-bred horses that obeyed me in carrying you and those who are dear to you thus far, draw the chariot that is emptied of you?
2.52.48
तन्न शक्ष्याम्यहं गन्तुम्
अयोध्यां त्वदृतेऽनघ ।
वनवासानुयानाय
मामनुज्ञातुमर्हसि ॥
Therefore, I cannot go to Ayōdhyā without you, O sinless one! You must allow me to go with you to live in the Vana.
2.52.49
यदि मे याचमानस्य
त्यागमेव करिष्यसि ।
सरथोऽग्निं प्रवेक्ष्यामि
त्यक्तमात्र इह त्वया ॥
If you forsake me in spite of my beseeching, I shall consign myself to the fire along with the chariot the moment you leave me.
2.52.50
भविष्यन्ति वने यानि
तपोविघ्नकराणि ते ।
रथेन प्रतिबाधिष्ये
तानि सत्त्वानि राघव ॥
In the Vana, O Rāghava, I will drive away any wild animals and creatures that might obstruct your Tapa, with the chariot!
2.52.51
त्वत्कृते न मयाऽवाप्तम्
रथचर्याकृतं सुखम् ।
आशंसे त्वत्कृते नाहम्
वनवासकृतं सुखम् ॥
By your grace I had the joy of driving the chariot. I hope likewise, by your grace, to taste the joys of life in Vana.
2.52.52
प्रसीदेच्छामि तेऽरण्ये
भवितुं प्रत्यनन्तरः ।
प्रीत्याऽभिहितमिच्छामि
भव मे प्रत्यनन्तरः ॥
Take pity on me, I wish to be by your side in the forest. Let me hear you say with pleasure, 'Be with me!'
2.52.53
इमे चापि हया वीर
यदि ते वनवासिनः ।
परिचर्यां करिष्यन्ति
प्राप्स्यन्ति परमां गतिम् ॥
And if these horses too, O Veera, serve you while you live in Vana, will attain the best of the best in the worlds beyond.
2.52.54
तव शुश्रूषणं मूर्ध्ना
करिष्यामि वने वसन् ।
अयोध्यां देवलोकं वा
सर्वथा प्रजहाम्यहम् ॥
Living in the forest, I shall serve you with my head bowed all the way down. For that, I will relinquish Ayōdhyā in every way and even the world of Dēvas, for that matter.
2.52.55
न हि शक्या प्रवेष्टुं सा
मयाऽयोध्या त्वया विना ।
राजधानी महेन्द्रस्य
यथा दुष्कृतकर्मणा ॥
It would be no easier for me to enter Ayōdhyā without you than it is for someone who committed evil to enter the capital city of the great Indra.
2.52.56
वनवासे क्षयं प्राप्ते
ममैष हि मनोरथः ।
यदनेन रथेनैव
त्वां वहेयं पुरीं पुनः ॥
The dream that I cherish is to take you to the city again, in this very chariot, at the end of life in Vana!
2.52.57
चतुर्दश हि वर्षाणि
सहितस्य त्वया वने ।
क्षणभूतानि यास्यन्ति
शतसङ्ख्यान्यतोऽन्यथा ॥
Living with you in the Vana fourteen years shall pass in an instant; or else they will feel like a hundred such.
2.52.58
भृत्यवत्सल तिष्ठन्तम्
भर्तृपुत्रगते पथि ।
भक्तं भृत्यं स्थितं स्थित्याम्
त्वं न मां हातुमर्हसि ॥
I am a loyal dependent and follower of the son of my master. Firm in my devotion, I do everything that is expected of me. Hence, you, known for your affection to your staff, must not abandon me.
2.52.59
एवं बहुविधं दीनम्
याचमानं पुनः पुनः ।
रामो भृत्यानुकम्पी तु
सुमन्त्रमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
When he thus helplessly pleaded again and again in as many ways as possible, Rāma, who was full of compassion for his staff, responded with  these words to Sumantra:
2.52.60
जानामि परमां भक्तिम्
मयि ते भर्तृवत्सल ।
शृणु चापि यदर्थं त्वाम्
प्रेषयामि पुरीमितः ॥
I know the immense devotion you have for me! I know that you have the utmost affection for your master! But listen, I will tell you why I am sending you back to the city from here!
2.52.61
नगरीं त्वां गतं दृष्ट्वा
जननी मे यवीयसी ।
कैकेयी प्रत्ययं गच्छेत्
इति रामो वनं गतः ॥
Seeing you back in the city, the youngest of my mothers, Kaikēyee, will feel assured that Rāma has, for sure, left for the Vana.
2.52.62
परितुष्टा हि सा देवी
वनवासं गते मयि ।
राजानं नातिशङ्केत
मिथ्यावादीति धार्मिकम् ॥
When assured that I have gone to live in the forest, the lady, vastly pleased, will no longer have to suspect the King, who is a Dhārmika, to be of empty words.
People who read Ādi Ṡaṅkarācārya's works are familiar with the phrase Mithyāvādi (मिथ्यावादि) in a different way.
The generic meaning of this word, however, is very simple. It means 'a person of empty words'.
2.52.63
एष मे प्रथमः कल्पो
यदम्बा मे यवीयसी ।
भरतारक्षितं स्फीतम्
पुत्रराज्यमवाप्नुयात् ॥
My foremost goal is to ensure that the youngest of my mothers sees the vast kingdom under the protection of her son Bharata.
2.52.64
मम प्रियार्थं राज्ञश्च
सरथस्त्वं पुरीं व्रज ।
सन्दिष्टश्चासि यानर्थान्
तांस्तान् ब्रूयास्तथा तथा ॥
You must go to the city with the chariot to please me and the King. Please convey verbatim, all the messages that are conveyed to you.
2.52.65
इत्युक्त्वा वचनं सूतम्
सान्त्वयित्वा पुनः पुनः ।
गुहं वचनमक्लीबो
रामो हेतुमदब्रवीत् ॥
Having thus answered the charioteer and pacifying him with repeated exhortations, Rāma, firm in his resolve, spoke these reasonable words to Guha:
2.52.66
नेदानीं गुह योग्योऽयम्
वासो मे सजने वने ।
आवश्यं ह्याश्रमे वासः
कर्तव्यस्तद्गतो विधिः ॥
It would not be proper for me to live in this Vana, which is inhabited by people. I must live in an Āṡrama and follow the rules governing that life.
2.52.67
सोऽहं गृहीत्वा नियमम्
तपस्वि जनभूषणम् ।
हितकामः पितुर्भूयः
सीताया लक्ष्मणस्य च ।
जटाः कृत्वा गमिष्यामि
न्यग्रोधक्षीरमानय ॥
Hence, in doing what is good for the father, and also to Seetā and to Lakshmaṇa I must accept the discipline that is considered as the glory of people who do Tapa. I shall proceed further only with matted locks. Please fetch me the sap of banyan tree!
2.52.68
तत् क्षीरं राजपुत्राय
गुहः क्षिप्रमुपाहरत् ।
लक्ष्मणस्यात्मनश्चैव
रामस्तेनाकरोज्जटाः ।
दीर्घबाहुर्नरव्याघ्रो
जटिलत्वमधारयत् ॥
And Guha immediately brought the sap to that prince. Rāma matted his locks as well as Lakshmaṇa's with the sap. Thus that tiger among men, of long arm, came to wear matted hair.
2.52.70
तौ तदा चीरवसनौ
जटामण्डलधारिणौ ।
आशोभेतामृषिसमौ
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
Thus the duo of brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, clad in robes of bark and with matted hair coiled on their heads, glowed like Ṛshis.
2.52.71
ततो वैखानसं मार्गम्
आस्थितः सह लक्ष्मणः ।
व्रतमादिष्टवान् रामः
सखायं गुहमब्रवीत् ॥
Then, launched into the way of life laid out by Vikhanas, along with Lakshmaṇa, he adopted the required discipline. Then he said to his friend Guha:
The procedures laid by Vikhanas are followed even today in many a temple, including in Tirupati.
2.52.72
अप्रमत्तो बले कोशे
दुर्गे जनपदे तथा ।
भवेथा गुह राज्यं हि
दुरारक्षतमं मतम् ॥
Be ever vigilant with regard to the army, the treasury, the citadel and the realm. For, the protection of a State is regarded as an immensely difficult task.
2.52.73
ततस्तं समनुज्ञाय
गुहमिक्ष्वाकुनन्दनः ।
जगाम तूर्णमव्यग्रः
सभार्यः सह लक्ष्मणः ॥
Then, the delight of Ikshwāku dynasty bid farewell to Guha and started his journey along with his wife and Lakshmaṇa immediately, with no disquiet whatsoever.
2.52.74
स तु दृष्ट्वा नदीतीरे
नावमिक्ष्वाकुनन्दनः ।
तितीर्षुः शीघ्रगां गङ्गाम्
इदं लक्ष्मणमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing the boat at the landing-place, the delight of Ikshwāku dynasty, wishing to cross the swift-flowing Gaṅgā, said to Lakshmaṇa;
2.52.75
आरोह त्वं नरव्याघ्र
स्थितां नावमिमां शनैः ।
सीतां चारोपयान्वक्षम्
परिगृह्य मनस्विनीम् ॥
O tiger among men! Please get slowly on to this boat which is ready. Hold the sensitive Seetā and assist her on board.
2.52.76
स भ्रातुः शासनं श्रुत्वा
सर्वमप्रतिकूलयन् ।
आरोप्य मैथिलीं पूर्वम्
आरुरोहाऽऽत्मवां स्ततः ॥
The sensible Lakshmaṇa, hearing the instructions of his brother and following them without any deviation, first put Seetā on board and then got in himself.
2.52.77
अथारुरोह तेजस्वी
स्वयं लक्ष्मणपूर्वजः ।
ततो निषादाधिपतिः
गुहो ज्ञातीनचोदयत् ॥
Then, the radiant elder brother of Lakshmaṇa, also got in and asked the kinsmen of Guha, the hunter chieftain, to start.
2.52.78
राघवोऽपि महातेजा
नावमारुह्य तां ततः ।
ब्रह्मवत् क्षत्रवच्चैव
जजाप हितमात्मनः ॥
Getting on board that boat, the supremely radiant Rāghava recited a Mantra meant for both Brāhmaṇas and Kshatriyas, which would bode well for him.
2.52.79
आचम्य च यथाशास्त्रम्
नदीं तां सह सीतया ।
प्राणमत्प्रीतिसंहृष्टो
लक्ष्मणश्चामितप्रभः ॥
Having taken the ceremonial sip of water in a customary manner, he felt exhilarated to offer salutations to the river along with Seetā. And so did Lakshmaṇa, of incomparable luster.
2.52.80
अनुज्ञाय सुमन्त्रं च
सबलं चैव तं गुहम् ।
आस्थाय नावं रामस्तु
चोदयामास नाविकान् ॥
Bidding farewell to Sumantra and to Guha and his army, Rāma took his seat in the boat and waved to the boatmen to proceed.
2.52.81
ततस्तैश्चोदिता सा नौः
कर्णधारसमाहिता ।
शुभस्फ्यवेगाभिहता
शीघ्रं सलिलमत्यगात् ॥
The boat, well steered by the helmsman and by the vigorous work on the paddles, swiftly crossed the waters.
2.52.82
मध्यं तु समनुप्राप्य
भागीरथ्यास्त्वनिन्दिता ।
वैदेही प्राञ्जलिर्भूत्वा
तां नदीमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
When they were midstream of the Bhāgeerathi, The irreproachable princess of Vidēha, prayed to the river with joined palms, saying:
2.52.83
पुत्रो दशरथस्यायम्
महाराजस्य धीमतः ।
निदेशं पारयित्वेमम्
गङ्गे त्वदभिरक्षितः ।
चतुर्दश हि वर्षाणि
समग्राण्युष्य कानने ।
भ्रात्रा सह मया चैव
पुनः प्रत्यागमिष्यति ।
ततस्त्वां देवि सुभगे
क्षेमेण पुनरागता ।
यक्ष्ये प्रमुदिता गङ्गे
सर्वकामसमृद्धिनी ॥
O blessed and beautiful Goddess Gaṅgā! When this son of the great King, the perspicacious Daṡaratha, returns with his brother and me, after carrying out the command by your grace and blessings by living for a full fourteen years in the forest, I, having come back safe and sound, and with all my desires fulfilled, shall worship you, in all happiness!
2.52.86
त्वं हि त्रिपथगा देवि
ब्रह्मलोकं समीक्षसे ।
भार्या चोदधिराजस्य
लोकेऽस्मिन् सम्प्रदृश्यसे ।
सा त्वां देवि नमस्यामि
प्रशंसामि च शोभने ॥
My salutations to you, O goddess! You flow in all the three worlds, simultaneously witnessing the world of Brahma and being the queen of the lord of the ocean in this world! My praise to you, O auspicious and splendid one!
2.52.87
प्राप्तराज्ये नरव्याघ्रे
शिवेन पुनरागते ।
गवां शतसहस्राणि
वस्त्राण्यन्नं च पेशलम् ।
ब्राह्मणेभ्यः प्रदास्यामि
तव प्रियचिकीर्षया ॥
When this tiger among men returns, and safely regains his kingdom, I shall please you by presenting hundreds of thousands of cows, garments and the most delicious foods to Brāhmaṇas.
2.52.89
सुराघटसहस्रेण
मांसभूतौदनेन च ।
यक्ष्ये त्वां प्रयता देवि
पुरीं पुनरुपागता ॥
Upon my return to the city, I shall worship you with all diligence, offering a thousand pots of liquor, as well as various dishes of meat, O Goddess!
2.52.90
यानि त्वत्तीरवासीनि
दैवतानि च सन्ति हि ।
तानि सर्वाणि यक्ष्यामि
तीर्थान्यायतनानि च ॥
And I shall worship all the deities that have shrines on your banks and also all sacred spots and temples.
2.52.91
पुनरेव महाबाहुः
मया भ्रात्रा च सङ्गतः ।
अयोध्यां वनवासात्तु
प्रविशत्वनघोऽनघे ॥
May the sinless one of mighty arm (Rāma) return to Ayōdhyā with me and his brother, from his life in the forest, O sinless one!
2.52.92
तथा सम्भाषमाणा सा
सीता गङ्गामनिन्दिता ।
दक्षिणा दक्षिणं तीरम्
क्षिप्रमेवाभ्युपागमत् ॥
In no time, while speaking like this to Gaṅgā, the irreproachable and able Seetā reached the banks on the southern side.
2.52.93
तीरं तु समनुप्राप्य
नावं हित्वा नरर्षभः ।
प्रातिष्ठत सह भ्रात्रा
वैदेह्या च परन्तपः ॥
Reaching the bank and getting off the boat, that bull among men, harrier of the foe (Rāma), proceeded forward along with his brother and the princess of Vidēha.
2.52.94
अथाब्रवीन्महाबाहुः
सुमित्रानन्दवर्धनम् ।
भव संरक्षणार्थाय
सजने विजनेऽपि वा ॥
Then that man of mighty arm (Rāma) said to the perpetual joy of Sumitrā (Lakshmaṇa) may you be on full vigilance (from now on), regardless of whether there are people around or not.
2.52.95
अवश्यं रक्षणं कार्यम्
अदृष्टे विजने वने ।
अग्रतो गच्छ सौमित्रे
सीता त्वामनुगच्छतु ॥
We must be on full alert in this Vana which has no people and which we have never seen before. Please walk in front! Seetā shall follow you!
2.52.96
पृष्ठतोऽहं गमिष्यामि
त्वां च सीतां च पालयन् ।
अन्योन्यस्येह नो रक्षा
कर्तव्या पुरुषर्षभ ।
न हि तावदतिक्रान्ता
सुकरा काचन क्रिया ॥
I will follow from behind protecting you and Seetā. O bull among men! We must protect each other here, for, it will not be easy to set things right again, once they go out of hand.
2.52.97
अद्य दुःखं तु वैदेही
वनवासस्य वेत्स्यति ।
प्रणष्टजनसम्बाधम्
क्षेत्रारामविवर्जितम् ।
विषमं च प्रपातं च
वनमद्य प्रवेक्ष्यति ॥
Today, the princess of Vidēha will learn about the hardships of living in Vana. For, she will be entering interiors of Vana with no press of people and no fields and gardens, all uneven and full of pits.
2.52.99
श्रुत्वा रामस्य वचनम्
प्रतस्थे लक्ष्मणोऽग्रतः ।
अनन्तरं च सीताया
राघवो रघुनन्दनः ॥
Hearing those words of Rāma, Lakshmaṇa walked in the front and Rāghava, the delight of Raghu dynasty, after Seetā.
2.52.100
गतं तु गङ्गापरपारमाशु
रामं सुमन्त्रः प्रततं निरीक्ष्य ।
अध्वप्रकर्षाद्विनिवृत्तदृष्टि
र्मुमोच बाष्पं व्यथित स्तपस्वी ॥
Having kept his gaze fixed on Rāma till he reached the other side of Gaṅgā, the miserable Sumantra ceased looking when he was too far away to be seen; and then shed tears of grief.
2.52.101
स लोकपालप्रतिमप्रभाववां
स्तीर्त्वा महात्मा वरदो महानदीम् ।
ततः समृद्धान् शुभसस्यमालिनः
क्रमेण वत्सान् मुदितानुपागमत् ॥
Having crossed the great river, that Mahātma, who fulfills everyone's wishes and who was comparable to the Guardians of the worlds, proceeded steadily and reached the Vatsa country which was a happy and prosperous region sporting a garland of standing crops.
2.52.102
तौ तत्र हत्वा चतुरो महामृगान्
वराहमृश्यं पृषतं महारुरुम् ।
आदाय मेध्यं त्वरितं बुभुक्षितौ
वासाय काले ययतुर्वनस्पतिम् ॥
Then those two hungry brothers killed four great beasts, including a boar, a white-footed antelope, a spotted antelope and a great deer. They took the fresh meat and quickly reached the foot of a tree, to spend the night there.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे द्विपञ्चाश स्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the second Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
