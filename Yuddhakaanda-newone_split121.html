Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 121
In this Sarga, Vibheeshaṇa pleads with Rāma to stay back for some time and enjoy his hospitality. Rāma politely excuses himself and tells that he want to start his journey back to Ayōdhyā, the path to which is treacherous, as soon as possible so that he can see his brother Bharata who was practicing extreme austerities on account of him.
Vibheeshaṇa tells Rāma that the Vimāna called Pushpaka can take him to Ayōdhyā in just a matter of a day and arranges for it. Rāma and Lakshmaṇa are amazed, seeing that one-of-a-kind Vimāna.
6.121.1
तां रात्रिमुषितं रामम्
सुखोदितमरिन्दमम् ।
अब्रवीत् प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यम्
जयं पृष्ट्वा विभीषणः ॥
When Rāma woke up, after a comfortable, full night's sleep, Vibheeshaṇa hailed him over his victory, and said, with his palms joined in reverence:
6.121.2
स्नानानि चाङ्गरागाणि
वस्त्राण्याभरणानि च ।
चन्दनानि च दिव्यानि
माल्यानि विविधानि च ।
अलङ्कारविदश्चैता
नार्यः पद्मनिभेक्षणाः ।
उपस्थितास्त्वां विधिवत्
स्नापयिष्यन्ति राघव ॥
Everything is ready for your bath - the unguents, the clothes, the jewels, the divine sandal paste and garlands of every kind. These lotus-eyed women are here to give you a perfect bath in a traditional manner.
6.121.4
एवमुक्तस्तु काकुत्स्थः
प्रत्युवाच विभीषणम् ॥
Thus told, Kākutstha responded to Vibheeshaṇa:
6.121.4
हरीन् सुग्रीवमुख्यांस्त्वम्
स्नानेनाभिनिमन्त्रय ॥
Please arrange for the bath of Sugreeva and all the Vānaras.
6.121.5
स तु ताम्यति धर्मात्मा
मम हेतोः सुखोचितः ।
सुकुमारो महाबाहुः
कुमारः सत्यसंश्रयः ॥
The young man, Dharmātma of mighty arm, a delicate one who deserves all comforts and luxuries, is suffering on my account, cleaving to his word.
6.121.6
तं विना केकयीपुत्रम्
भरतं धर्मचारिणम् ।
न मे स्नानं बहुमतम्
वस्त्राण्याभरणानि च ॥
Without Bharata, the son of Kaikēyee, who strictly adheres to Dharma, I find no pleasure in taking bath or in putting on garments and wearing ornaments.
Rāma has completed the fourteen years of exile in Vana. He no more needs to be clad in clothes made of bark. He is free to enjoy all comforts and luxuries of life that he desires. But his mind goes to Bharata who abjured the same on his account.
6.121.7
एतत् पश्य यथा क्षिप्रं
प्रतिगच्छाम तां पुरीम् ।
अयोध्यां गच्छतो ह्येष
पन्थाः परमदुर्गमः ॥
Please see how we can go back to that city as soon as possible; for the path to Ayōdhyā is quite treacherous for one to traverse.
6.121.8
एवमुक्तस्तु काकुत्स्थम्
प्रत्युवाच विभीषणः ॥
Thus asked by Kākutstha, Vibheeshaṇa replied:
6.121.8
अह्ना त्वां प्रापयिष्यामि
तां पुरीं पार्थिवात्मज ॥
O Prince, I shall arrange for you to go to that city in just a day!
6.121.9
पुष्पकं नाम भद्रं ते
विमानं सूर्यसन्निभम् ।
मम भ्रातुः कुबेरस्य
रावणेनाहृतं बलात् ।
हृतं निर्जित्य सङ्ग्रामे
कामगं दिव्यमुत्तमम् ।
त्वदर्थे पालितं चेदम्
तिष्ठत्यतुलविक्रम ॥
This Vimāna called Pushpaka that shines like the sun was taken from my brother Kubēra by Rāvaṇa by force, after vanquishing him in the battle.
This excellent and splendid one that takes one wherever one wishes is ready and at your service; may all bode well for you, O you of unparalleled prowess!
6.121.11
तदिदं मेघसङ्काशम्
विमानमिह तिष्ठति ।
येन यास्यसि यानेन
त्वमयोध्यां गतज्वरः ॥
This Vimāna which is imposing like a cloud is ready here, traveling by which you can reach Ayōdhyā, free of all worries.
6.121.12
अहं ते यद्यनुग्राह्यो
यदि स्मरसि मे गुणान् ।
वस तावदिह प्राज्ञ
यद्यस्ति मयि सौहृदम् ॥
If I am worthy of your grace, if you recall any merit in me and if you consider me as your good friend, please stay here for some time, O sagacious one!
6.121.13
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
वैदेह्या चापि भार्यया ।
अर्चितः सर्वकामैस्त्वम्
ततो राम गमिष्यसि ॥
Please have all your fancies served, along with those of your brother Lakshmaṇa's and wife Vaidēhee's, before you go from here.
6.121.14
प्रीतियुक्तस्य विहितां
ससैन्यः ससुहृद्गणः ।
सत्क्रियां राम मे तावत्
गृहाण त्वं मयोद्यताम् ॥
Please enjoy, along with your army and hosts of your friends, the hospitality that is arranged for you with love and in a customary manner.
6.121.15
प्रणयाद्बहुमानाच्च
सौहृदेन च राघव ।
प्रसादयामि प्रेष्योऽहम्
न खल्वाज्ञापयामि ते ॥
I insist out of affection and regard, with the intimacy our friendship affords. I, by no means, am commanding you; I am your servant.
6.121.16
एवमुक्तस्ततो रामः
प्रत्युवाच विभीषणम् ।
रक्षसां वानराणां च
सर्वेषां चोपशृण्वताम् ॥
Then Rāma, thus requested, responded to Vibheeshaṇa, with all the Rākshasas, Vānaras also listening:
6.121.17
पूजितोऽस्मि त्वया वीर
साचिव्येन परन्तप ।
सर्वात्मना च चेष्टाभिः
सौहृदेन परेण च ॥
O valiant one! O scorcher of the foe! I have already been honored by you in every manner, with your counsel, practical help and great friendship.
6.121.18
न खल्वेतन्न कुर्यां ते
वचनं राक्षसेश्वर ।
तं तु मे भ्रातरं द्रष्टुम्
भरतं त्वरते मनः ॥
It is not that I do not want to honor your request, O lord of Rākshasas! It is just that my mind is eager to see him, Bharata, my brother.
6.121.19
मां निवर्तयितुं योऽसौ
चित्रकूटमुपागतः ।
शिरसा याचतो यस्य
वचनं न कृतं मया ।
कौसल्यां च सुमित्रां च
कैकेयीं च यशस्विनीम् ।
गुहं च सुहृदं चैव
पौरान् जनपदै सह ॥
He came all the way to Citrakūṭa, along with Kousalyā, Sumitrā, the renowned Kaikēyee, Guha, friends, city-dwellers and country-dwellers, to persuade me to return, begging with his bowed head; but I could not honor his request.
6.121.21
अनुजानीहि मां सौम्य
पूजितोऽस्मि विभीषण ।
मन्युर्न खलु कर्तव्यः
सखे त्वां चानुमानये ॥
My dear, please grant me permission. I have been well honored, O Vibheeshaṇa! I hope you will not be angry with me, I plead with you, my friend!
6.121.22
उपस्थापय मे शीघ्रम्
विमानं राक्षसेश्वर ।
कृतकार्यस्य मे वासः
कथं स्यादिह संमतः ॥
Please get me on the Vimāna as soon as possible, O lord of Rākshasas. How is my stay here justified after I am done with my work?
6.121.23
एवमुक्तस्तु रामेण
राक्षसेन्द्रो विभीषणः ।
विमानं सूर्यसम्काशम्
आजुहाव त्वरान्वितः ॥
Thus told by Rāma, Vibheeshaṇa, the king of Rākshasas, ordered the Vimāna which shone like the sun, with all urgency.
This block of six Ṡlōkas constitute a single long sentence, with multiple clauses. For the sake of convenience, I have translated each clause into a (short) sentence of its own.
6.121.24
ततः काञ्चनचित्राङ्गम्
वैडूर्यमयवेदिकम् ।
कूटागारैः परिक्षिप्तम्
सर्वतो रजतप्रभम् ॥
Its every part was lovely and made of gold. It had raised seats studded with lazulite. It had many pavilions and shone with the elegance of silver everywhere.
6.121.25
पाण्डुराभिः पताकाभिः
ध्वजैश्च समलङ्कृतम् ।
काञ्चनं काञ्चनैर्हर्म्यैः
हेमपद्मविभूषितैः ॥
It was well decorated with flag posts and white flags. Made of gold, it had many high rise golden structures which themselves were decorated with golden lotuses.
6.121.26
प्रकीर्णं किङ्किणीजालैः
मुक्तामणिगवाक्षकम् ।
घण्टाजालैः परिक्षिप्तम्
सर्वतो मधुरस्वनम् ॥
There were pellet bells all over it. Its windows were decorated with pearls and gems. With bells hung around everywhere, it emanated melodious sounds.
6.121.27
तं मेरुशिखराकारम्
निर्मितं विश्वकर्मणा ।
बृहद्भिर्भूषितं हर्म्यैः
मुक्तारजतशोभितैः ॥
Built by Viṡwakarma himself, it was shaped like the peak of Mēru, and had huge ornate structures, dazzling with the sheen of pearl and silver.
6.121.28
तलैः स्फाटिकचित्राङ्गैः
वैडूर्यैश्च वरासनैः ।
महार्हास्तरणोपेतैः
उपपन्नं महाधनैः ॥
The floors had a variety of crystal works, and the couches that of lazulite, all covered with extremely expensive, nay, priceless spreads and carpets.
6.121.29
उपस्थितमनाधृष्यम्
तद्विमानं मनोजवम् ।
निवेदयित्वा रामाय
तस्थौ तत्र विभीषणः ॥
Vibheeshaṇa conveyed to Rāma that the Vimāna which no power could stand against and which flew with the speed of thought had arrived, and was waiting for him.
6.121.30
तत्पुष्पकम् कामगमं विमानम्
उपस्थितम् भूधरसंनिकाशम् ।
दृष्ट्वा तदा विस्मयमाजगाम
रामः ससौमित्रिरुदारस्त्त्वः ॥
Rāma and the extremely powerful Sowmitri were amazed on seeing that Vimāna called Pushpaka, which flew wherever one wished, and was majestic like a mountain, that had arrived.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे एकविंशत्युत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and twenty first Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
