Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 20
Everyone at the King's palace is unhappy after Rāma leaves. The many wives of King cry out aloud in grief, making the king feel even more miserable.
Meanwhile Rāma, after reaching the palace of Kousalyā, enters the inner quarters and meets his mother. She takes him into her arms.
Rāma then reveals to her that he has to go to the Daṇḍaka Vana and Bharata would be consecrated in his place, as per the boons Kaikēyee received from Daṡaratha.
Kousalyā laments and cries aloud in grief over the misfortune.
2.20.1
तस्मिंस्तु पुरुषव्याघ्रे
निष्क्रामति कृताञ्जलौ ।
आर्तशब्दो महान् जज्ञे
स्त्रीणामन्तःपुरे तदा ॥
After the tiger among men with palms joined in salutation had left, the women of the inner quarters started crying pitifully saying:
In the previous Sarga, the camera followed Rāma as he left the King's palace. But how was it at the King's palace after Rāma left? Vālmeeki resumes from where he left off.
2.20.2
कृत्येष्वचोदितः पित्रा
सर्वस्यान्तःपुरस्य च ।
गतिर्यश्शरणं चापि
स रामोऽद्य प्रवत्स्यति ॥
Without even his father having to ask him, Rāma became the protector and care-taker of the inner quarters! He is now going away in exile!
These Ṡlōkas indicate what women look for in an able man.
2.20.3
कौसल्यायां यथा युक्तो
जनन्यां वर्तते सदा ।
तथैव वर्ततेऽस्मासु
जन्मप्रभृति राघवः ॥
Since the time of his birth, Rāghava has been respecting us the same way as he has been respecting his own mother Kousalyā!
2.20.4
न क्रुध्यत्यभिशप्तोऽपि
क्रोधनीयानि वर्जयन् ।
क्रुद्धान्प्रसादयन्सर्वान्
स इतोऽद्य प्रवत्स्यति ॥
He does not get annoyed even when irritated. He avoids doing anything that would annoy others. He knows how to put people in good humor when they are mad. Such Rāma is going in exile today!
2.20.5
अबुद्धिर्बत नो राजा
जीवलोकं चरत्ययम् ।
यो गतिं सर्वभूतानाम्
परित्यजति राघवम् ॥
By being stupid enough to abandon Rāghava, unto whom everyone looks up, our king is killing all living beings!
2.20.6
इति सर्वा महिष्यस्ता
विवत्सा इव धेनवः ।
पतिमाचुक्रुशुश्चैव
सस्वरं चापि चुक्रुशुः ॥
Saying thus, all the wives of the king cried aloud like cows that lost their calves, while blaming the king summarily!
This Ṡlōka indicates that the king had more wives than the three. The comparison with 'the cows that lost calves' indicates that Rāma had earned the motherly affection of each one of them.
2.20.7
स हि चान्तःपुरे घोरम्
आर्तशब्दं महीपतिः ।
पुत्रशोकाभिसन्तप्तः
श्रुत्वा व्यालीयताऽसने ॥
Hearing those horrible cries in the inner quarters, the ruler of the lands sank into his couch burning with grief on account of his son's fate.
2.20.8
रामस्तु भृशमायस्तो
निश्श्वसन्निव कुञ्जरः ।
जगाम सहितो भ्रात्रा
मातुरन्तःपुरं वशी ॥
Highly troubled but self-controlled, Rāma entered the inner quarters along with his brother, sighing like an elephant!
The camera now moves on to the face of Rāma who is about to enter the inner quarters of Kousalyā's palace.
2.20.9
सोऽपश्यत्पुरुषं तत्र
वृद्धं परमपूजितम् ।
उपविष्टं गृहद्वारि
तिष्ठतश्चापरान्बहून् ॥
He saw a highly respected old man and several other people at the entrance.
2.20.10
दृष्ट्वैव तु तदा रामम्
ते सर्वे सहसोत्थिताः ।
जयेन जयतां श्रेष्ठम्
वर्धयन्ति स्म राघवम् ॥
As soon as they saw Rāma, they got up and greeted victory to Rāghava, the foremost among the victorious!
2.20.11
प्रविश्य प्रथमां कक्ष्याम्
द्वितीयायां ददर्श सः ।
ब्राह्मणान्वेदसम्पन्नान्
वृद्धान्राज्ञाऽभिसत्कृतान् ॥
He entered the front courtyard and at the entrance of the second courtyard he saw elderly Brāhmaṇas, who were Vēdic scholars that were well honored by the king!
2.20.12
प्रणम्य रामस्तान्विप्रान्
तृतीयायां ददर्श सः ।
स्त्रियो वृद्धास्तथा बाला
द्वाररक्षणतत्पराः ॥
Having paid obeisance to those Brāhmaṇas, Rāma saw elderly women and young women vigilantly guarding the door at the third courtyard.
2.20.13
वर्धयित्वा प्रहृष्टास्ताः
प्रविश्य च गृहं स्त्रियः ।
न्यवेदयन्त त्वरिता
राममातुः प्रियं तदा ॥
Those women felt very happy and greeted victory to him. They went inside in a hurry and conveyed the happy news (of his arrival) to Rāma's mother.
2.20.14
कौसल्यापि तदा देवी
रात्रिं स्थित्वा समाहिता ।
प्रभाते त्वकरोत्पूजाम्
विष्णोः पुत्रहितैषिणी ॥
Having spent all night in anticipation, Kousalyā performed Pūja to Vishnu in the morning for the wellbeing of her son.
2.20.15
सा क्षौमवसना हृष्टा
नित्यं व्रतपरायणा ।
अग्निं जुहोति स्म तदा
मन्त्रवत्कृतमङ्गला ॥
She who followed strict observances every single day, was performing fire ritual at that time chanting Mantras, having purified herself ritually and clad in white silks.
Not only men, women also performed Hōmas during the times of Rāmāyaṇa!
2.20.16
प्रविश्य च तदा रामो
मातुरन्तःपुरं शुभम् ।
ददर्श मातरं तत्र
हावयन्तीं हुताशनम् ॥
Then Rāma entered that auspicious inner apartment and saw his mother offering Havis in Agni.
2.20.17
देवकार्यनिमित्तं च
तत्रापश्यत्समुद्यतम् ।
दध्यक्षतं घृतं चैव
मोदकान्हविषस्तथा ।
लाजान्माल्यानि शुक्लानि
पायसं कृसरं तथा ।
समिधः पूर्णकुम्भांश्च
ददर्श रघुनन्दनः ॥
The scion of Raghu dynasty saw many things ready for performing ritual offerings to Dēvas, such as curds, Akshatas, Ghee, sweet balls, Havis, puffed grain, garlands of white flowers, rice pudding, Kṛisara, Samidhas and pots filled with consecrated water.
Kṛisara is rice cooked along with legumes (usually Moong Dal).
2.20.19
तां शुक्लक्षौमसंवीताम्
व्रतयोगेन कर्शिताम् ।
तर्पयन्तीं ददर्शाद्भिः
देवतां देववर्णिनीम् ॥
He saw his mother, who had become thin due to observances, clad in silky whites and gleaming with a divine luster, offering water ritually to the Dēvas.
2.20.20
सा चिरस्यात्मजं दृष्ट्वा
मातृनन्दनमागतम् ।
अभिचक्राम संहृष्टा
किशोरं बडबा यथा ॥
Seeing the son, the delight of the mother, who came after a long time, she went up to him overjoyed, like a mare to her foal.
2.20.21
स मातरमभिक्रान्ताम्
उपसंगृह्य राघवः ।
परिष्वक्तश्च बाहुभ्याम्
उपाघ्रातश्च मूर्धनि ॥
Rāma touched her feet as she approached. She took him into her arms and gave him a hug and smelled his head.
'Smelling the head' is similar to 'Kissing on the forehad'.
2.20.22
तमुवाच दुराधर्षम्
राघवं सुतमात्मनः ।
कौसल्या पुत्रवात्सल्यात्
इदं प्रियहितं वचः ॥
To that invincible Rāghava, her son, Kousalyā said these pleasing words with motherly affection:
2.20.23
वृद्धानां धर्मशीलानाम्
राजर्षीणां महात्मनाम् ।
प्राप्नुह्यायुश्च कीर्तिं च
धर्मं चोपहितं कुले ॥
May you enjoy long life and renown that every Mahatma, every elderly person, every adherent of Dharma and every Rājarshi enjoys! May you be bounded by the Dharma that you inherited from your ancestors!
2.20.24
सत्यप्रतिज्ञं पितरम्
राजानं पश्य राघव ।
अद्यैव हि त्वां धर्मात्मा
यौवराज्येऽभिषेक्ष्यति ॥
O Rāghava! Be assured that the king, your father would abide by his word and that Dharmātma would consecrate you as crown prince this very day!
What an irony! The king had to abide by a totally different word that he gave!
2.20.25
दत्तमानसमालभ्य
भोजनेन निमन्त्रितः ।
मातरं राघवः किञ्चित्
प्रसार्याञ्जलिमब्रवीत् ॥
Then Kousalyā arranged for him to have food. As he was reaching for the chair to sit on, he raised his joined palms and said to her:
2.20.26
स स्वभावविनीतश्च
गौरवाच्च तदा नतः ।
प्रस्थितो दण्डकारण्यम्
आप्रष्टुमुपचक्रमे ॥
Modest by nature, and impelled even more by respect, Rāma started the conversation to take leave of her to go to Daṇḍaka forest:
2.20.27
देवि नूनं न जानीषे
महद्भयमुपस्थितम् ।
इदं तव च दुःखाय
वैदेह्या लक्ष्मणस्य च ॥
Mother! You seem certainly not to be aware of the great tragedy that has befallen, that would bring misery to you, the princess of Vidēha (Seetā) and Lakshmaṇa!
2.20.28
गमिष्ये दण्डकारण्यम्
किमनेनासनेन मे ।
विष्टरासनयोग्यो हि
कालोऽयं मामुपस्थितः ॥
Why this chair for me, when the time has come for me to go to Daṇḍaka forest and sit on Darbha grass!
2.20.29
चतुर्दश हि वर्षाणि
वत्स्यामि विजने वने ।
मधुमूलफलैर्जीवन्
हित्वा मुनिवदामिषम् ॥
I am to live in the uninhabited Vana, like a Muni for fourteen years giving up all meats and subsisting on honey, roots and fruits!
Rāma, being a Kshatriya, eats meat.
2.20.30
भरताय महाराजो
यौवराज्यं प्रयच्छति ।
मां पुनर्दण्डकारण्ये
विवासयति तापसम् ॥
The lord king will confer the office of crown prince to Bharata while sending me in exile to the Daṇḍaka forest to live like a Muni.
2.20.31
स षट्चाष्टौ च वर्षाणि
वत्स्यामि विजने वने ।
आसेवमानो वन्यानि
फलमूलैश्च वर्तयन् ॥
For six and eight years, I must stay in the uninhabited Vana, living on forest products like fruits and roots!
2.20.32
सा निकृत्तेव सालस्य
यष्टिः परशुना वने ।
पपात सहसा देवी
देवतेव दिवश्च्युता ॥
Immediately (upon hearing that) she fell down like a branch of Sāla tree cut by an axe in a Vana and like a Dēvata who had slipped from Heaven!
2.20.33
तामदुःखोचितां दृष्ट्वा
पतितां कदलीमिव ।
रामस्तूत्थापयामास
मातरं गतचेतसम् ॥
Rāma lifted her up, the one who did not deserve such misery, seeing her fall unconscious like a plantain tree on the ground!
2.20.34
उपावृत्त्योत्थितां दीनाम्
बडबामिव वाहिताम् ।
पांसुकुण्ठितसर्वाङ्गीम्
विममर्श च पाणिना ॥
She looked terrible, like a mare that had drawn heavy weights. All of her body was covered with dust as she rolled on the grounds. Rāma caressed her with his hands.
2.20.35
सा राघवमुपासीनम्
असुखार्ता सुखोचिता ।
उवाच पुरुषव्याघ्रम्
उपशृण्वति लक्ष्मणे ॥
She, who deserved all happiness, distraught with grief now, spoke to Rāghava, that tiger among men who sat by her side, while Lakshmaṇa was listening:
2.20.36
यदि पुत्र न जायेथा
मम शोकाय राघव ।
न स्म दुःखमतो भूयः
पश्येयमहमप्रजाः ॥
My son! I wish you were not born to me to cause this grief! The grief of not having children would have been no worse than this!
2.20.37
एक एव हि वन्ध्याया
श्शोको भवति मानसः ।
अप्रजाऽस्मीति सन्तापो
न ह्यन्यः पुत्र विद्यते ॥
My son! A barren woman has only one kind of grief, that of not having children, but she is saved from everything else!
2.20.38
न दृष्टपूर्वं कल्याणम्
सुखं वा पतिपौरुषे ।
अपि पुत्रेऽपि पश्येयम्
इति रामाऽस्थितं मया ॥
O Rāma! I did not experience any comfort or luxury during the regime of my husband. I have been waiting to enjoy them at least during the reign of my son!
Recall that Daṡaratha confesses earlier (Sloka 2.12.68b-70a) that he was not able to treat Kousalyā in a fair manner, for the fear of displeasing Kaikēyee!
2.20.39
सा बहून्यमनोज्ञानि
वाक्यानि हृदयच्छिदाम् ।
अहं श्रोष्ये सपत्नीनाम्
अवराणां वरा सती ॥
I am the best and foremost of all wives! Yet, I have to bear with many heartbreaking and unkind words from my co-wives, who are inferior to me!
Indeed, having all the riches and being the first wife of the king are not enough for a good life! A woman, more importantly and certainly, needs appreciation and respect from among her peers!
2.20.40
अतो दुःखतरं किं नु
प्रमदानां भविष्यति ।
मम शोको विलापश्च
यादृशोऽयमनन्तकः ॥
What else could be worse for a woman than this endless grief and sorrow that has befallen me!
2.20.41
त्वयि सन्निहितेऽप्येवम्
अहमासं निराकृता ।
किं पुनः प्रोषिते तात
ध्रुवं मरणमेव मे ॥
My child! I am being treated like this, right when you are here! What to say of my fate, if you would be in exile! Nothing but death seems to be certain for me.
2.20.42
अत्यन्तनिगृहीतास्मि
भर्तुर्नित्यमतन्त्रिता ।
परिवारेण कैकेय्या
स्समा वाप्यथवाऽवरा ॥
I lacked freedom of any sort and was always constrained by my husband! I was made to feel equal to the attendants of Kaikēyee, or even inferior!
Manthara very well knows the innuendos being shot from the palace of Kaikēyee towards Kousalyā, perhaps, behind the back of Kaikēyee.
Hence she is concerned that all of it might come back at her from Kousalyā's palace, if Rāma becomes the crown prince.
That explains why Manthara took such an extreme step!
2.20.43
योऽहि मां सेवते कश्चित्
अथवाप्यनुवर्तते ।
कैकेय्याः पुत्रमन्वीक्ष्य
स जनो नाभिभाषते ॥
Whoever is serving and obliging me now, would not even talk to me hereafter, for the fear of the son of Kaikēyee!
2.20.44
नित्यक्रोधतया तस्याः
कथं नु खरवादितत् ।
कैकेय्या वदनं द्रष्टुम्
पुत्र शक्ष्यामि दुर्गता ॥
My son! How can I, having fallen into such miserable state, face up to that Kaikēyee who is always angry and speaks harshly!
2.20.45
दश सप्त च वर्षाणि
जातस्य तव राघव ।
असितानि प्रकाङ्क्षन्त्या
मया दुःखपरिक्षयम् ॥
I have spent ten and seven years since your birth, waiting for the end of this misery!
Some commentators interpret the 'birth' here as 'second birth', i.e., the ceremony of Upanayanam.
2.20.46
तदक्षयं महद्दुःखम्
नोत्सहे सहितुं चिरम् ।
विप्रकारं सपत्नीनाम्
एवं जीर्णाऽपि राघव ॥
I cannot take this endless misery and the insults from my co-wives, well into my old age!
2.20.47
अपश्यन्ती तव मुखम्
परिपूर्णशशिप्रभम् ।
कृपणा वर्तयिष्यामि
कथं कृपणजीविकाम् ॥
How can I lead this life of despondency without seeing your brilliant face that shines like the full moon!
2.20.48
उपवासैश्च योगैश्च
बहुभिश्च परिश्रमैः ।
दुःखं संवर्धितो मोघम्
त्वं हि दुर्गतया मया ॥
You have been raised in vain by me, the unfortunate, with great difficulty, undertaking fasts and many other devotional observances.
2.20.49
स्थिरं तु हृदयं मन्ये
ममेदं यन्न दीर्यते ।
प्रावृषीव महानद्या
स्पृष्टं कूलं नवाम्भसा ॥
My heart must indeed be hard, that it is not crumbling like the bank of a mighty river that swells with fresh waters in the rainy season!
2.20.50
ममैव नूनं मरणं न विद्यते
न चावकाऽशोस्ति यमक्षयेऽमम ।
यदन्तकोऽद्यैव न मां जिहीर्षति
प्रसह्य सिंहो रुदतीं मृगीमिव ॥
Neither does death seem to impinge upon me nor does there seem to be room for me in the world of Yama! Otherwise, why would he, who puts an end to everything, not take me away forcibly, like a lion takes away a crying deer?
2.20.51
स्थिरं हि नूनं हृदयं ममायसं
न भिद्यते यद्भुवि नावदीर्यते ।
अनेन दुःखेन च देहमर्पितं
ध्रुवं ह्यकाले मरणं न विद्यते ॥
My heart, surely, is made of hard steel! Otherwise, why is it not breaking apart into pieces and falling to the ground? My body seems to be destined to endure this grief. I do not see being relieved from it by untimely death!
2.20.52
इदं हि दुःखं यदनर्थकानि मे
व्रतानि दानानि च सम्यमाश्च हि ।
तपश्च तप्तं यदपत्यकारणा-
त्सुनिष्फलं बीजमिवोप्तमूषरे ॥
Alas, my charitable activities and my self-discipline and my Vratas have yielded nothing but this grief! All the Tapa in which I burned myself for the sake of getting offspring is completely wasted, like seed sown in a barren land.
2.20.53
यदि ह्यकाले मरणं स्वयेच्छया
लभेत कश्चिद्गुरुदुःखकर्शितः ।
गताहमद्यैव परेतसंसदं
विना त्वया धेनुरिवात्मजेन वै ॥
If only it were possible for one to will one's own untimely death, to be relieved of this unbearable grief, I should have left for the afterworld right away like a cow that is separated from its calf!
2.20.54
अथापि किं जीवितमद्य मे वृथा
त्वया विना चन्द्रनिभाननप्रभ ।
अनुव्रजिष्यामि वनं त्वयैव गौ-
स्सुदुर्बला वत्समिवानुकाङ्क्षया ॥
Why this futile life for me now, without your bright face that shines like the moon? I shall follow you to the Vana like an emaciated cow goes after her calf out of love!
2.20.55
भृशमसुखममर्षिता तदा
बहु विललाप समीक्ष्य राघवम् ।
व्यसनमुपनिशम्य सा महत्
सुतमिव बद्धमवेक्ष्य किन्नरी ॥
Not finding anything that could give her comfort or relief, she cried terribly looking at Rāma, like a Kinnara woman, who cries seeing her son taken captive!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे विंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twentieth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
