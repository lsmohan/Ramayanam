Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 20
In this Sarga, Tārā grieves over her husband's death. She feels sad that he had to reap the fruits of his misdeeds, in taking the wife of Sugreeva and in driving him away from the kingdom. She feels sad that Vāli,  in his folly, did not heed her advice. She blames the fate for taking his life away and agrees that Rāma is justified in delivering the punishment for his misdeeds and keeping the word given to Sugreeva.
She praises his kingly stature and handsome looks, saying that it is certain that he would raise a stir in the hearts of the Apsaras in Heaven. She beseeches him to bless the young, handsome and pampered Aṅgada. She asks Aṅgada to take a good look at his father for one last time.
She tells Sugreeva that he can have the kingdom and Rumā back, now that his brother and enemy is dead. She begs Vāli to forgive her of anything she might have done to displease him unwittingly.
She resolves herself to fast until death right on the grounds where Vāli lay.
4.20.1
रामचापविसृष्टेन
शरेणान्तकरेण तम् ।
दृष्ट्वा विनिहतं भूमौ
तारा ताराधिपानना ।
सा समासाद्य भर्तारम्
पर्यष्वजत भामिनी ॥
Tārā whose face resembled the lord of stars saw him fallen on the ground struck by the arrow shot from Rāma’s bow that but takes one’s life. That lady approached her husband and embraced him.
Lord of stars refers to the moon.
4.20.2
इषुणाऽभिहतं दृष्ट्वा
वालिनं कुञ्जरोपमम् ।
वानरं पर्वतेन्द्राभम्
शोकसन्तप्तमानसा ।
तारा तरुमिवोन्मूलम्
पर्यदेवयदातुरा ॥
Seeing that Vānara, Vāli, who was (giant) like an elephant, and (splendid) like the lord of mountains, struck down by the arrow and (lying) like an uprooted tree, Tārā, her heart burned by grief, wept bitterly.
4.20.4
रणे दारुण विक्रान्त
प्रवीर प्लवतां वर ।
किं दीनामपुरोभागाम्
अद्य त्वं नाभिभाषसे ॥
O mighty warrior, foremost of the Vānaras, of formidable prowess in war! How is it that you do not speak to me that stands before you miserable, having never spoken anything against you!
4.20.5
उत्तिष्ठ हरिशार्दूल
भजस्व शयनोत्तमम् ।
नैवंविधाश्शेरते हि
भूमौ नृपतिसत्तमाः ॥
Get up, O tiger among Vānaras, on to a magnificent bed; great kings like you do not lie down on the bare ground.
4.20.6
अतीव खलु ते कान्ता
वसुधा वसुधाधिप ।
गतासुरपि यां गात्रैः
मां विहाय निषेवसे ॥
Indeed, isn’t the earth your foremost beloved, O lord of the earth, for you entreat her taking her into your full embrace, abandoning me, even as you are losing your life!
4.20.7
व्यक्तमन्या त्वया वीर
धर्मतः सम्प्रवर्तिता ।
किष्किन्धेव पुरी रम्या
स्वर्गमार्गे विनिर्मिता ॥
Clearly, O Veera, you, who abide by Dharma, have built, in the direction of Heaven, a beautiful city like Kishkindhā!
4.20.8
यान्यस्माभिस्त्वया सार्धम्
वनेषु मधुगन्धिषु ।
विहृतानि त्वया काले
तेषामुपरमः कृतः ॥
All those times of pleasure-wanderings along with you in the Vanas filled with intoxicating scents have been wrought to come to an end.
4.20.9
निरानन्दा निराशाऽहम्
निमग्ना शोकसागरे ।
त्वयि पञ्चत्वमापन्ने
महायूथपयूथपे ॥
With you, the lord of leaders of the hosts (of Vānaras), dead, I am plunged in an ocean of grief, devoid of hope or joy.
4.20.10
हृदयं सुस्थिरं मह्यम्
दृष्ट्वा विनिहतं पतिम् ।
यन्न शोकाभिसन्तप्तम्
स्फुटतेऽद्य सहस्रधा ॥
My heart, though burnt by grief seeing the fallen husband, does not shatter into thousands of pieces, surviving adamantly for my sake.
4.20.11
सुग्रीवस्य त्वया भार्या
हृता स च विवासितः ।
यत्तु त्तस्य त्वया व्युष्टिः
प्राप्तेयं प्लवगाधिप ॥
You took to yourself Sugreeva’s wife and drove him out (of the kingdom); you have now reaped the fruit, O Lord of Vānaras!
4.20.12
निश्श्रेयसपरा मोहात्
त्वया चाहं विगर्हिता ।
यैषाऽब्रुवं हितं वाक्यम्
वानरेन्द्र हितैषिणी ॥
I, who was your well-wisher, ever interested in your wellbeing, speaking words of good advice, was completely neglected, in your folly.
4.20.13
रूपयौवनदृप्तानाम्
दक्षिणानां च मानद ।
नूनमप्सरसामार्य
चित्तानि प्रमथिष्यसि ॥
O Mānada! O noble one! You will assuredly raise a tempest in the hearts of the Apsaras who are proud of their youthful beauty and skills of love-making!
4.20.14
कालो निस्संशयो नूनम्
जीवितान्तकरस्तव ।
बलाद्येनावपन्नोऽसि
सुग्रीवस्यावशो वशम् ॥
Without a doubt, it is the time, which puts an end to life, that has forcefully made you yield to the dominance of Sugreeva!
4.20.15
वैधव्यं शोकसन्तापम्
कृपणं कृपणा सती ।
अदुःखोपचिता पूर्वम्
वर्तयिष्याम्यनाथवत् ॥
Having never known sorrow, I shall now, like any defenseless creature, be plunged in misery, widowed and oppressed by grief.
4.20.16
लालितश्चाङ्गदो वीरः
सुकुमारस्सुखोचितः ।
वर्त्स्यते कामवस्थां मे
पितृव्ये क्रोधमूर्छिते ॥
What will be the fate of Aṅgada, our delicate and pampered son, a Veera who deserves nothing but comforts? How will he survive the angry moods of his uncle?
4.20.17
कुरुष्व पितरं पुत्र
सुदृष्टं धर्मवत्सलम् ।
दुर्लभं दर्शनं वत्स
तव तस्य भविष्यति ॥
Take a good long look, son, at your father, who loved Dharma. My dear! It will be impossible to see him later.
4.20.18
समाश्वासय पुत्रं त्वम्
सन्देशं सन्दिशस्व मे ।
मूर्ध्नि चैनं समाघ्राय
प्रवासं प्रस्थितो ह्यसि ॥
Smell the crown of your son’s head and console him! Please give me your last message for you are on your final journey.
4.20.19
रामेण हि महत्कर्म
कृतं त्वामभिनिघ्नता ।
आनृण्यं च गतं तस्य
सुग्रीवस्य प्रतिश्रवे ॥
In striking you, Rāma did a mighty deed. The promise made to Sugreeva was kept and the obligation was fulfilled.
4.20.20
सकामो भव सुग्रीव
रुमां त्वं प्रतिपत्स्यसे ।
भुङ्क्ष्व राज्यमनुद्विग्नः
शस्तो भ्राता रिपुस्तव ॥
Sugreeva! Have your wishes fulfilled! Have Rumā come back to you! Enjoy the kingdom without worry! Your brother and enemy is killed.
4.20.21
किं मामेवं विलपतीम्
प्रीयं त्वं नाभिभाषसे ।
इमाः पश्य वरा बह्वीः
भार्यास्ते वानरेश्वर ॥
Why do you not speak to me, your beloved, who is crying like this? Look at these countless lovely wives of yours, O Lord of Vānaras!
4.20.22
तस्या विलपितं श्रुत्वा
वानर्यस्सर्वतश्च ताः ।
परिगृह्याङ्गदं दीनम्
दुःखार्ता परिचुक्रुशुः ॥
Hearing her lament thus, the female Vānaras who had gathered there wailed in their grief hugging the woebegone Aṅgada.
4.20.23
किमङ्गदं साङ्गदवीरबाहो
विहाय यातोऽपि चिरप्रवासम् ।
न युक्तमेवं गुणसन्निकृष्टं
विहाय पुत्रं प्रिय चारुवेषम् ॥
How could you, O you of the doughty arm, adorned with armlets, depart on this endless journey abandoning Aṅgada? It is not proper for you to desert your handsome son who embodies virtues just as you do.
4.20.24
किमप्रियं ते प्रियचारुवेष
मया कृतं नाथ सुतेन वा ते ।
सहाङ्गदां मां प्रविहाय वीर
यत्प्रस्थितो दीर्घ मितः प्रवासम् ॥
O my lord of pleasing and handsome looks! What did I or your son do to displease you, that you should go on this endless journey, leaving me behind along with Aṅgada?
4.20.25
यद्यप्रियं किञ्चिदसम्प्रधार्य
कृतं मया स्यात्तव दीर्घबाहो ।
क्षमस्व मे तद्धरिवंशनाथ
व्रजामि मूर्ध्ना तव वीर पादौ ॥
O Veera! O Lord of Vānaras! Please forgive me, if I have done anything to displease you, however unwittingly. I reach to your feet with my head bowed!
4.20.26
तथा तु तारा करुणं रुदन्ती
भर्तुस्समीपे सह वानरीभिः ।
व्यवस्यत प्रायमुपोपवेष्टुम्
अनिन्द्यवर्णा भुवि यत्र वाली ॥
Tārā of faultless sheen, thus wailing pitifully along with all the other Vānara women, resolved herself to fast to death on the grounds where Vāli lay.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे विंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twentieth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
