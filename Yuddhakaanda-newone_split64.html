Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 64
In this Sarga, Mahōdara rebukes Kumbhakarṇa for his proposal that he would go alone to fight the battle. He questions how Kumbhakarṇa, all by himself, can fight Rāma, who singlehandedly slew multitudes of extremely strong Rākshasas in Janasthāna.
Then he turns to Rāvaṇa, and offers him a plan, by which Seetā would come to him of her own accord. The plan is to proclaim widely that five Rākshasas including himself and Kumbhakarṇa will set out to slay Rāma; if they succeed in slaying Rāma nothing more would need to be done, and if not, a rumor should be spread that Rāma is slain, hearing which Seetā, having no one else to take care of her, would give herself to Rāvaṇa, for she, who is used to comforts, cannot continue to let herself be worn out with grief for too long.
6.64.1
तदुक्तमतिकायस्य
बलिनो बाहुशालिनः ।
कुम्भकर्णस्य वचनम्
श्रुत्वोवाच महोदरः ॥
Hearing the words that Kumbhakarṇa of huge body, great arms and strength said, Mahōdara said:
6.64.2
कुम्भकर्ण कुले जातो
धृष्टः प्राकृतदर्शनः ।
अवलिप्तो न शक्नोषि
कृत्यं सर्वत्र वेदितुम् ॥
Though highborn, you, Kumbhakarṇa, are impulsive like an ordinary man. You are so conceited that you are not able to realize what should be done and where.
6.64.3
न हि राजा न जानीते
कुम्भकर्ण नयानयौ ।
त्वं तु कैशोरकाद्धृष्टः
केवलं वक्तुमिच्छसि ॥
Does not the King know what is sound strategy and what is not? You are being childishly presumptuous and so talk for the sake of talking!
6.64.4
स्थानं वृद्धिं च हानिं च
देशकालविभागवित् ।
आत्मनश्च परेषां च
बुध्यते राक्षसर्षभः ॥
This bull among the Rākshasas knows very well about the situation, the advantages and disadvantages of his own and that of his foe, at all times and places.
6.64.5
यत्त्वशक्यं बलवता
कर्तुं प्राकृतबुद्धिना ।
अनुपासितवृद्धेन
कः कुर्यात्तादृशं बुधः ॥
Which sensible man will try what even an ordinary man, who did not even have the benefit of learning from elders, would not expend his energy on?
6.64.6
यांस्तु धर्मार्थकामांस्त्वम्
ब्रवीषि पृथगाश्रयान् ।
अनुबोद्धुं स्वभावेन
नहि लक्षणमस्ति तान् ॥
You say that Dharma, Artha and Kāma are distinct from each other, but there, of course, is no firm criterion to clearly distinguish one from the other.
6.64.7
कर्म चैव हि सर्वेषाम्
कारणानां प्रयोजकम् ।
श्रेयः पापीयसां चात्र
फलं भवति कर्मणाम् ॥
Action is the sole means of attaining all ends. And even evil deeds yield beneficial results (at times).
6.64.8
निश्श्रेयसफलावेव
धर्मार्थावितरावपि ।
अधर्मानर्थयोः प्राप्तिः
फलं च प्रत्यवायिकम् ॥
Whether it is for Dharma, Artha or something else, it is all done expecting some good results. What is not in line with Dharma and Artha may also yield results to the opposite effect (at times).
6.64.9
ऐहलौकिकपारत्रम्
कर्म पुम्भिर्निषेव्यते ।
कर्माण्यपि तु कल्याणि
लभते काममास्थितः ॥
Men perform deeds by which they reap benefits in this world and the next. Even by performing deeds purely out of Kāma one might gain beneficial results.
6.64.10
तत्र क्लृप्तमिदं राज्ञा
हृदि कार्यं मतं च नः ।
शत्रौ हि साहसं यत्तत्
किमिवात्रापनीयते ॥
Keeping this in mind, the king resolved upon a particular course of action and we also approved it. How does display of bravery against the enemy change any of this?
6.64.11
एकस्यैवाभियाने तु
हेतुर्यः प्राहृतस्त्वया ।
तत्राप्यनुपपन्नं ते
वक्ष्यामि यदसाधु च ॥
You give reasons for going alone by yourself to fight the enemy; but let me tell you why it will not work and what is wrong with it.
6.64.12
येन पूर्वं जनस्थाने
बहवोऽतिबला हताः ।
राक्षसा राघवं तं त्वम्
कथमेको जयिष्यसि ॥
Multitudes of extremely strong Rākshasas were slain in the past in Janasthāna by Rāghava. How can you expect to vanquish him all by yourself?
6.64.13
ये पुरा निर्जितास्तेन
जनस्थाने महौजसः ।
राक्षसांस्तान् पुरे सर्वान्
भीतानद्यापि पश्यसि ॥
You can see in the city all those Rākshasas of tremendous power who were vanquished in Janasthāna in the past still frightful (of him) even today.
6.64.14
तं सिंहमिव सङ्क्रुद्धम्
रामं दशरथात्मजम् ।
सर्पं सुप्तमिवाबुध्य
प्रबोधयितुमिच्छसि ॥
Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, is extremely outraged like a lion. You think of rousing him, least realizing that he is like a sleeping serpent.
6.64.15
ज्वलन्तं तेजसा नित्यम्
क्रोधेन च दुरासदम् ।
कस्तं मृत्युमिवासह्यम्
आसादयितुमर्हति ॥
Who would go anywhere near him who ever blazes with power and who, when roused to wrath, can hardly be approached and is as dreaded as death itself?
6.64.16
संशयस्थमिदं सर्वम्
शत्रोः प्रतिसमासने ।
एकस्य गमनं तत्र
नहि मे रोचते भृशम् ॥
With everything about facing the foe is thus in doubt, I do not at all like the idea of just one person going there alone.
6.64.17
हीनार्थः सुसमृद्धार्थम्
को रिपुं प्राकृतं यथा ।
निश्चित्य जीवितत्यागे
वशमानेतुमिच्छति ॥
Who, with one's resources thinned, would attempt to subdue an enemy of ample resources as one might an ordinary enemy, unless one wants to give up one's life as well?
6.64.18
यस्य नास्ति मनुष्येषु
सदृशो राक्षसोत्तम ।
कथमाशंससे योद्धुम्
तुल्येनेन्द्रविवस्वतोः ॥
How can you, O, foremost of Rākshasas, think of fighting one who is an equal of Indra and Surya and who has no peer among humans?
6.64.19
एवमुक्त्वा तु संरब्धः
कुम्भकर्णं महोदरः ।
उवाच रक्षसां मध्ये
रावणं लोकरावणम् ॥
Having spoken thus to Kumbhakarṇa, Mahōdara, in the midst of Rākshasas, turned to Rāvaṇa who made the worlds wail, and said in a haste:
6.64.20
लब्ध्वा पुरस्ताद्वैदेहीम्
किमर्थं त्वं विलम्बसे ।
यदीच्छसि तदा सीता
वशगा ते भविष्यति ॥
Having got Vaidēhee right in front of you, what are you waiting for? If only you wish so, Seetā will submit herself to you.
6.64.21
दृष्टः कश्चिदुपायो मे
सीतोपस्थानकारकः ।
रुचिरश्चेत् स्वया बुद्ध्या
राक्षसेश्वर तं शृणु ॥
I see a Upāya by which Seetā can be made to come to you (of her own accord). Please listen, O lord of Rākshasas, and I hope it catches your imagination.
6.64.22
अहं द्विजिह्वः संह्रादी
कुम्भकर्णो वितर्दनः ।
पञ्च रामवधायैते
निर्यान्तीत्यवघोषय ॥
Have it proclaimed that the five of us, myself, Dwijihva, Samhrādi, Kumbhakarṇa and Vitardana, are setting out to slay Rāma.
6.64.23
ततो गत्वा वयं युद्धम्
दास्यामस्तस्य यत्नतः ।
जेष्यामो यदि ते शत्रून्
नोपायैः कृत्यमस्ति नः ॥
We shall go forth and give him a fight putting in our utmost effort. If we are victorious over the enemy, there will, of course, be no need to employ any Upāya.
6.64.24
अथ जीवति नः शत्रुः
वयं च कृतसंयुगाः ।
ततस्तदभिपत्स्यामो
मनसा यत् समीक्षितम् ॥
If our enemy manages to be alive, even after the fight, then we shall adopt the contemplated course.
6.64.25
वयं युद्धादिहैष्यामो
रुधिरेण समुक्षिताः ।
विदार्यं स्वतनुं बाणै
रामनामाङ्कितैः शितैः ॥
We shall return from the battlefield drenched in blood, our bodies torn by the arrows that bear Rāma’s name.
6.64.26
भक्षितो राघवोऽस्माभिः
लक्ष्मणश्चेति वादिनः ।
तव पादौ ग्रहीष्यामः
त्वं नः कामं प्रपूरय ॥
Saying that we had gobbled up Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, we shall come and fall at your feet, and you will reward us with all that we desire.
6.64.27
ततोऽवघोषय पुरे
गजस्कन्धेन पार्थिव ।
हतो रामः सह भ्रात्रा
ससैन्य इति सर्वतः ॥
And you can then have it proclaimed in the city by a herald on elephant back, O King, that Rāma and his brother Lakshmaṇa have been slain along with all their forces.
6.64.28
प्रीतो नाम ततो भूत्वा
भृत्यानां त्वमरिन्दम ।
भोगांश्च परिवारांश्च
कामांश्च वसु दापय ॥
O subduer of foe! And you, pretending to be pleased, will bestow your staff with luxuries, servants and all that they desire.
6.64.29
ततो माल्यानि वासांसि
वीराणामनुलेपनम् ।
पेयं च बहु योधेभ्यः
स्वयं च मुदितः पिब ॥
And present the Veeras with garlands, clothes, unguents and everything that the warriors deserve; and you too shall drink copiously showing how happy you are.
6.64.30
ततोऽस्मिन् बहुलीभूते
कौलीने सर्वतो गते ।
भक्षितः ससुहृद्रामो
राक्षसैरिति विश्रुते ।
प्रविश्याश्वास्य चापि त्वम्
सीतां रहसि सान्त्वय ।
धनधान्यैश्च कामैश्च
रत्नैश्चैनां प्रलोभय ॥
Once the rumor spreads everywhere and it is widely believed that Rāma and his cohorts have been devoured by the Rākshasas, go to Seetā in privacy, console her and plead with her, tempting her with money, grain, gems and whatever she might covet.
6.64.32
अनयोपधया राजन्
भयशोकानुबन्धया ।
अकामा त्वद्वशं सीता
नष्टनाथा गमिष्यति ॥
With this trick, O King, which will induce fear and grief in her, Seetā, having no one to take care of her, would yield to you, even if she does not like to do so.
6.64.33
रमणीयं हि भर्तारम्
विनष्टमवगम्य सा ।
नैराश्यात् स्त्रीलघुत्वाच्च
त्वद्वशं प्रतिपत्स्यते ॥
Even though her husband is handsome, realizing that he is no more, she, out of despair and as a helpless woman, will submit herself to you.
6.64.34
सा पुरा सुखसंवृद्धा
सुखार्हा दुःखकर्शिता ।
त्वय्यधीनं सुखं ज्ञात्वा
सर्वथोपगमिष्यति ॥
She grew up with luxuries and is used to them. Worn out by grief (for so long), and realizing that her happiness lies in you, she will, by all means, seek you out.
6.64.35
एतत् सुनीतं मम दर्शनेन
रामं हि दृष्ट्वैव भवेदनर्थः ।
इहैव ते सेत्स्यति मोत्सुको भूः
महानयुद्धेन सुखस्य लाभः ॥
I have thought it through (carefully) and decided on this course. The mere sight of Rāma will be your undoing, so do not fret over it; you can gain the best of happiness right here, without even having to fight.
6.64.36
अनष्टसैन्यो ह्यनवाप्तसंशयो
रिपूनयुद्धेन जयन् जनाधिप ।
यशश्च पुण्यं च महन्महीपतिः
श्रियं च कीर्तिं च चिरं समश्नुते ॥
O lord of peoples! The ruler of a land who wins over the enemy without fighting, without losing his army, and without a doubt, will gain Puṇya and fame and long lasting reputation and prosperity!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे चतुष्षष्ठितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty fourth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
