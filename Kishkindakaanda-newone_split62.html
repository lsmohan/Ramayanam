Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 62
In this Sarga, Saṃpāti narrates to Aṅgada what the Maharshi Niṡākara told him - that he foresees Rāvaṇa abducting Seetā, Vānaras coming as the messengers of Rāma and that Saṃpāti should tell them about Seetā.
4.62.1
एवमुक्त्वा मुनिश्रेष्ठम्
अरुदं भृश दुःखितः ।
अथ ध्यात्वा मुहूर्तं तु
भगवानिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Saying this to that eminent Muni, I wept feeling extremely sad. Then the Bhagawān thought for a while and said these words:
4.62.2
पक्षौ च ते प्रपक्षौ च
पुनरन्यौभविष्यतः ।
चक्षुषी चैव प्राणाश्च
विक्रमश्च बलं च ते ॥
You will get new wings and feathers again. You will also recover your eyesight, vigor and strength.
4.62.3
पुराणे सुमहत्कार्यम्
भविष्यं हि मया श्रुतं ।
दृष्टं मे तपसा चैव
श्रुत्वा च विदितं मम ॥
Some time back I heard that something of great significance will unfold in the future. I saw the same with my power of Tapa too.
4.62.4
राजा दशरथो नाम
कश्चिदिक्ष्वाकुवर्धनः  ।
तस्य पुत्रो महातेजा
रामोनाम भविष्यति ॥
There will be a king by name Daṡaratha, who would advance the Ikshwāku dynasty. He will have a son of great power by name Rāma.
4.62.5
अरण्यं च सह भ्रात्रा
लक्ष्मणेन गमिष्यति ।
अस्मिन्नर्थे नियुक्तस्सन्
पित्रा सत्यपराक्रमः ॥
He of infallible prowess, at the command of his father, will go to reside in the forest along with his brother Lakshmaṇa.
4.62.6
नैऋतो रावणो नाम
तस्य भार्यां हरिष्यति ।
राक्षसेन्द्रो जनस्थाने
अवध्यस्सुरदानवैः ॥
A Rākshasa by name Rāvaṇa will abduct his wife in Janasthāna. He would be the king of Rākshasas, unassailable even by the Dēvas or Dānavas.
4.62.7
सा च कामैः प्रलोभ्यन्ती
भक्ष्यैःभोज्यैश्च मैथिली ।
नभोक्ष्यति महाभागा
दुःखमग्ना यशस्विनी ॥
She, the princess of Mithilā, would be enticed by many a pleasure and food. But that blessed one of great renown, submerged in grief, would not enjoy any of them.
4.62.8
परमान्नंच वैदेह्या
ज्ञात्वा दास्यति वासवः  ।
यदन्नममृतप्रख्यम्
सुराणामपिदुर्लभम् ॥
Coming to know of this, Vāsava will send her ultimate delicacies, the equals of nectar, which is beyond the reach even of the Dēvas.
4.62.9
तदन्नं मैथिलीप्राप्य
विज्ञायेन्द्रादिदंत्विति ।
अग्रमुदृत्य रामाय
भूतले निर्वपिष्यति ॥
Knowing that it had come from Indra, she will set aside the first morsel on the ground dedicating to Rāma:
4.62.10
यदि जीवति मे भर्ता
लक्ष्मणो वाऽपि देवरः ।
देवत्वं गच्छ्तोर्वापि
तयोरन्नमिदंत्विति ॥
This food must go to my husband and my brother-in-law Lakshmaṇa regardless of whether they are alive or have ascended to Heaven.
4.62.11
एष्यन्ति प्रेषितास्तत्र
रामदूताः प्लवंगमाः ।
आख्येया राम महिषी
त्वया तेभ्यो विहङ्गम ॥
Vānaras, dispatched as messengers of Rāma, will arrive here. O bird! You should tell them about the queen of Rāma.
4.62.12
सर्वथा तु न गन्तव्यम्
ईदृशः क्व गमिष्यसि ।
देशकालौ प्रतीक्षस्व
पक्षौ त्वं प्रतिपत्स्यसे ॥
You cannot go in this state anywhere, and you shall not. Wait for the right time, and you will regain your wings.
4.62.13
उत्सहेयमहं कर्तुम्
अद्यैव त्वां सपक्षकम् ।
इहस्थस्त्वं तु लोकानाम्
हितं कार्यं करिष्यसि ॥
I could get your wings back right away, but you would do good to the worlds by staying right here.
4.62.14
त्वयाऽपि खलु तत्कार्यम्
तयोश्चनृपपुत्रयोः ।
ब्राह्मणानां गुरुणां च
मुनीनां वासवस्य च ॥
For, you too shall be part of carrying out the object of the two princes, Brāhmaṇas, revered Munis and Vāsava.
4.62.15
इच्छाम्यहमपिद्रष्टुम्
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
नेच्छेचिरं धारयितुम्
प्राणांस्त्यक्ष्ये कलेवरम् ।
महर्षि तु तदब्रवीदेवम्
दृष्टतत्वार्थदर्शनः ॥
‘I too like to see the brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, but I do not wish to hold on to life any longer, I want to cast away this body’ said that Maharshi, who had realized the nature and purpose (of the ultimate).
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे द्विषष्टितम स्सर्ग:॥
Thus concludes the sixty second Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
