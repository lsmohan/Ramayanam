Baala Kaanda - Sarga 38
Viṡwāmitra, after having narrated the story of the birth of Kārtikēya in the previous Sarga, continues to tell another story, that of the descent of Gaṅgā, which will be the subject of the next seven Sargas, including this one.
1.38.1
तां कथां कौशिको रामे
निवेद्य कुशिकात्मजः ।
पुनरेवापरं वाक्यम्
काकुत्स्थमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
The descendant of Kuṡa, Viṡwāmitra, having told that story to Rāma, said again to him:
1.38.2
अयोध्याधिपतिः शूरः
पूर्वमासीन्नराधिपः ।
सगरो नाम धर्मात्मा
प्रजाकामः स चाप्रजः ॥
Once upon a time, there was a valiant king by name Sagara who was a Dharmātma, who ruled the kingdom of Ayōdhyā. He had no children and was seeking one.
Sagara is an ancestor of Rāma, who also ruled the same city of Ayōdhyā.
1.38.3
वैदर्भदुहिता राम
केशिनी नाम नामतः ।
ज्येष्ठा सगरपत्नी सा
धर्मिष्ठा सत्यवादिनी ॥
His first wife was Kēṡini, daughter of the king of Vidarbha. She was an adherent of Dharma avowed to truth.
1.38.4
अरिष्टनेमिदुहिता
रूपेणाप्रतिमा भुवि ।
द्वितीया सगरस्यासीत्
पत्नी सुमतिसञ्ज्ञिता ॥
His second wife was an exceptionally beautiful lady. Her name was Sumati and she was the daughter of Arishṭanēmi.
1.38.5
ताभ्यां सह तथा राजा
पत्नीभ्यां तप्तवांस्तपः ।
हिमवन्तं समासाद्य
भृगुप्रस्रवणे गिरौ ॥
The king, along with his wives, went to Bhṛguprasravaṇa mountain, which was a part of the mountain range of Himalayas, and did Tapa.
1.38.6
अथ वर्षशते पूर्णे
तपसाऽऽराधितो मुनिः ।
सगराय वरं प्रादात्
भृगुः सत्यवतां वरः ॥
After one hundred years of Tapa by Sagara, the pleased Bhṛgu Maharshi gave him a boon.
1.38.7
अपत्यलाभः सुमहान्
भविष्यति तवानघ ।
कीर्तिं चाप्रतिमां लोके
प्राप्स्यसे पुरुषर्षभ ॥
O unafflicted one! You will indeed be blessed with great children! You will gain unequaled fame and name in the world!
1.38.8
एका जनयिता तात
पुत्रं वंशकरं तव ।
षष्टिं पुत्रसहस्राणि
अपरा जनयिष्यति ॥
My dear! One of your wives will give birth to a son who will make your lineage notable. Your other wife will give birth to sixty thousand children.
1.38.9
भाषमाणं महात्मानम्
राजपुत्र्यौ प्रसाद्य तम् ।
ऊचतुः परमप्रीते
कृताञ्जलिपुटे तदा ॥
The two princesses (wives of Sagara) felt very happy and profoundly thanked that great Mahātma, for his benevolent words. Having gained his grace, they spoke to him:
1.38.10
एकः कस्यास्सुतो ब्रह्मन्
का बहून् जनयिष्यति ।
श्रोतुमिच्छावहे ब्रह्मन्
सत्यमस्तु वचस्तव ॥
O Brahman! Your words would indeed become true! Can you tell us which one of us will get one child and which one will get sixty thousand?
1.38.11
तयोस्तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
भृगुः परमधार्मिकः ।
उवाच परमां वाणीम्
स्वच्छन्दोऽत्र विधीयताम् ॥
That supreme Dhārmika, Bhṛgu, answered them: "You may choose however you wish"!
1.38.12
एको वंशकरो वाऽस्तु
बहवो वा महाबलाः ।
कीर्तिमन्तो महोत्साहाः
का वा कं वरमिच्छति ॥
Who would like to have a son that will make the lineage notable? Who would like to have many sons that are exceptionally strong, exceptionally motivated and will be known in this world for long? It is your choice!
People can make themselves notable. Or they can make their lineage or country or community notable with their presence. A subtle but important difference as posed in the question in this Ṡlōka.
1.38.13
मुनेस्तु वचनं श्रुत्वा
केशिनी रघुनन्दन ।
पुत्रं वंशकरं राम
जग्राह नृपसन्निधौ ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Hearing those words of the Muni, Kēṡini chose, witnessed by the king, the son that would make the lineage notable.
1.38.14
षष्टिं पुत्रसहस्राणि
सुपर्णभगिनी तदा ।
महोत्साहान् कीर्तिमतो
जग्राह सुमतिः सुतान् ॥
The sister of Garuḍa (Sumati) chose the sixty thousand sons that would be exceptionally motivated and known for long.
1.38.15
प्रदक्षिणमृषिं कृत्वा
शिरसाभिप्रणम्य च ।
जगाम स्वपुरं राजा
सभार्यो रघुनन्दन ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! The king then circumambulated the Ṛshi and prostrated in front of him and took leave of him. He went back to his city along with his wives.
1.38.16
अथ काले गते तस्मिन्
ज्येष्ठा पुत्रं व्यजायत ।
असमञ्ज इति ख्यातम्
केशिनी सगरात्मजम् ॥
After a while, the elder son of Sagara, known as Asamañja was born to Kēṡini.
1.38.17
सुमतिस्तु नरव्याघ्र
गर्भतुम्बं व्यजायत ।
षष्टिः पुत्राः सहस्राणि
तुम्बभेदाद्विनिःसृताः ॥
O tiger among men! Sumati delivered an object that looked like a gourd. When it broke apart, sixty thousand sons came out of it.
1.38.18
घृतपूर्णेषु कुम्भेषु
धात्र्यस्तान् समवर्द्धयन् ।
कालेन महता सर्वे
यौवनं प्रतिपेदिरे ॥
They were incubated in pots of clarified butter and were raised by nannies. As time passed by, they all grew to be young adults.
1.38.19
अथ दीर्घेण कालेन
रूपयौवनशालिनः ।
षष्टिः पुत्रसहस्राणि
सगरस्याभवंस्तदा ॥
After a long time, those sixty thousand sons of Sagara became handsome and youthful.
1.38.20
स च ज्येष्ठो नरश्रेष्ठः
सगरस्यात्मसम्भवः ।
बालान् गृहीत्वा तु जले
सरय्वा रघुनन्दन ।
प्रक्षिप्य प्रहसन्नित्यम्
मज्जतस्तान् समीक्ष्य वै ॥
O best among men! O prince of Raghu dynasty! The elder son of Sagara (Asamañja) used to throw children in the waters of river Sarayu and derive sadistic pleasure watching them drown.
1.38.21
एवं पापसमाचारः
सज्जनप्रतिबाधकः ।
पौराणामहिते युक्तः
पुत्रो निर्वासितः पुरात् ॥
He used to do commit many evil acts and trouble the simple and good people. The king then expelled his son from the city to save the people from his bullying.
Rāma belongs to the same lineage as Sagara. That dynasty held the well-being of their subjects above everything else, including the well-being of their own kith and kin.
Sagara expelled his son, but with a reason, that can be easily justified. Whereas Rāma, as we know, had to expel Seetā, in deference of the standards held by his subjects, which many people find difficult to justify.
1.38.22
तस्य पुत्रोंऽशुमान्नाम
असमञ्जस्य वीर्यवान् ।
सम्मतः सर्वलोकस्य
सर्वस्यापि प्रियंवदः ॥
The valiant son of Asamañja, known as Amṡuman, on the other hand, was gentle with every one and was liked by all worlds.
1.38.23
ततः कालेन महता
मतिः समभिजायत ।
सगरस्य नरश्रेष्ठ
यजेयमिति निश्चिता ॥
O best among men! After a long time, Sagara thought of performing a Yajña.
Just as normal people celebrate various occasions and perform various types of rituals, kings of those days used to perform Yajñas.
1.38.24
स कृत्वा निश्चयं राम
सोपाध्यायगणस्तदा ।
यज्ञकर्मणि वेदज्ञो
यष्टुं समुपचक्रमे ॥
Having decided to perform the Yajña, he started the necessary and prescribed activities for the Yajña under the guidance of his Purōhitas who were learned in Vēdas.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे अष्टात्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty eighth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
