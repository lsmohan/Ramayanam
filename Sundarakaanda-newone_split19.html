Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 19
In this Sarga, Seetā trembles like a banana plant in a gale, on seeing Rāvaṇa. She is at her worst, worn out by the grief from the separation from Rāma and feeling helpless like a ship caught in high-winds in the middle of an ocean. Rāvaṇa, inviting his own death, tries to entice her.
5.19.1
तस्मिन्नेव ततः काले
राजपुत्री त्वनिन्दिता ।
रूपयौवनसम्पन्नम्
भूषणोत्तमभूषितम् ।
ततो दृष्ट्वैव वैदेही
रावणं राक्षसाधिपम् ।
प्रावेपत वरारोहा
प्रवाते कदली यथा ॥
At about the same time, Vaidēhee, the blemish-less princess, saw Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas who was handsome and youthful and adorned with the best of ornaments. Instantly, she of beautiful Kaṭee trembled like a banana plant in a gale.
5.19.3
आच्छाद्योदरमूरुभ्याम्
बाहुभ्यां च पयोधरौ ।
उपविष्टा विशालाक्षी
रुदन्ती वरवर्णिनी ॥
Covering her stomach with her drawn-up legs and her breasts with her arms, she of lovely complexion and wide eyes sat weeping.
5.19.4
दशग्रीवस्तु वैदेहीम्
रक्षितां राक्षसीगणैः ।
ददर्श सीतां दुःखार्ताम्
नावं सन्नामिवार्णवे ॥
The Ten-necked saw Seetā, the princess of Vidēha, well-guarded by the hordes of Rākshasis, distressed and feeling helpless like a ship sinking in a great ocean.
5.19.5
असंवृतायामासीनाम्
धरण्यां संशितव्रताम् ।
छिन्नां प्रपतितां भूमौ
शाखामिव वनस्पतेः ॥
She, ardently sticking to her discipline and sitting on the bare ground, was like a severed branch of a great forest tree fallen on the ground.
5.19.6
मलमण्डनचित्राङ्गीम्
मण्डनार्हाममण्डिताम् ।
मृणाली पङ्कदिग्धेव
विभाति न विभाति च ॥
Though she deserved to adorn herself dazzlingly, she lacked all luster, having only dirt for ornament. She was like a lotus stalk besmirched with mire, attractive and unattractive at the same time.
5.19.7
समीपं राजसिंहस्य
रामस्य विदितात्मनः ।
सङ्कल्पहयसंयुक्सैः
यान्तीमिव मनोरथैः ॥
She looked as if she was on her way to be in the company of Rāma, that lion among kings who had the utmost clarity about himself, with her mind for a chariot and wishes for steeds.
5.19.8
शुष्यन्तीं रुदतीमेकाम्
ध्यानशोकपरायणाम् ।
दुःखस्यान्तमपश्यन्तीम्
रामां राममनुव्रताम् ॥
That beautiful lady who was faithful to Rāma, could not find an end to her sorrows, being all by herself with nothing else but grief and brooding for company.
5.19.9
वेष्टमानां तथाऽविष्टाम्
पन्नगेन्द्रवधूमिव ।
धूप्यमानां ग्रहेणेव
रोहिणीं धूमकेतुना ॥
She was like a young female snake writhing, constrained by a spell, and like the star Rōhiṇee caught in the smoke of the tail of a comet.
5.19.10
वृत्तशीलकुले जाताम्
आचारवति धार्मिके ।
पुनःसंस्कारमापन्नाम्
जातामिव च दुष्कुले ॥
Though born into an exemplary and virtuous family that adhered to Dharma and scrupulous about the norms of nobility, she looked like one born into an ignoble family who just happened to have been raised (in noble ways).
5.19.11
अभूतेनापवादेन
कीर्तिं निपतितामिव ।
आम्नायानामयोगेन
विद्यां प्रशिथिलामिव ॥
She was like good reputation tarnished by baseless slander and like a skill that had become blunted for lack of practice.
5.19.12
सन्नामिव महाकीर्तिम्
श्रद्धामिव विमानिताम् ।
प्रज्ञामिव परिक्षीणाम्
आशां प्रतिहतामिव ॥
She was like great renown that had faded, faith that was ridiculed, talent all but lost and hopes that were dashed.
5.19.13
आयतीमिव विध्वस्ताम्
आज्ञां प्रतिहतामिव ।
दीप्तामिव दिशं काले
पूजामपहृतामिव ॥
She was like command disobeyed, prospect that had been ruined, conflagration of the four quarters at the time of the dissolution of the worlds, and like Pūja snatched away (by an intruder to whom it was not intended).
5.19.14
पद्मिनीमिव विध्वस्ताम्
हतशूरां चमूमिव ।
प्रभामिव तमोध्वस्ताम्
उपक्षीणामिवापगाम् ॥
She was like a lotus pond that had been ruined, an army with its prize warriors killed, light engulfed by darkness and a river that had all but dried up.
5.19.15
वेदीमिव परामृष्टाम्
शान्तामग्निशिखामिव ।
पौर्णमासीमिव निशाम्
राहुग्रस्तेन्दुमण्डलाम् ॥
She was like a Vēdi defiled, flame subdued and like the night of the full-moon-day with the moon eclipsed by Rāhu.
5.19.16
उत्कृष्टपर्णकमलाम्
वित्रासितविहङ्गमाम् ।
हस्तिहस्तपरामृष्टाम्
आकुलां पद्मिनीमिव ॥
She was like a lotus pond that had been ruined by an elephant with its trunk, with its lotuses and leaves uprooted and the birds on it frightened and driven off.
5.19.17
पतिशोकातुरां शुष्काम्
नदीं विस्रावितामिव ।
परया मृजया हीनाम्
कृष्णपक्षनिशामिव ॥
Overwhelmed with the grief of (not having the company of) her husband, she was like a river that had drained. Lacking good toilet, she was like a night of the dark fortnight.
5.19.18
सुकुमारीं सुजाताङ्गीम्
रत्नगर्भगृहोचिताम् ।
तप्यमानामिवोष्णेन
मृणालीमचिरोद्धृताम् ॥
With well-chiseled limbs and delicate body, she who deserved to live in gem studded inner apartments, was like a lotus stalk that was just extricated and (left to) wilt under the heat (of the sun).
5.19.19
गृहीतामालितां स्तम्भे
यूथपेन विनाकृताम् ।
निःश्वसन्तीं सुदुःखार्ताम्
गजराजवधूमिव ॥
She heaved great sighs in her deep distress, like the mate of a royal tusker separated from the leader of the herd and chained to a post.
5.19.20
एकया दीर्घया वेण्या
शोभमानामयत्नतः ।
नीलया नीरदापाये
वनराज्या महीमिव ॥
She was beautiful without having to make any special effort, just with her long single plait, like the earth in the post-monsoon season shining with dark rows of trees.
5.19.21
उपवासेन शोकेन
ध्यानेन च भयेन च ।
परिक्षीणां कृशां दीनाम्
अल्पाहारां तपोधनाम् ॥
Fasting, brooding, taken over by grief and fear, completely worn-out, emaciated and dejected, she subsisted on minimal food, amassing nothing but Tapa.
5.19.22
आयाचमानां दुःखार्ताम्
प्राञ्जलिं देवतामिव ।
भावेन रघुमुख्यस्य
दशग्रीवपराभवम् ॥
Overwhelmed by sorrow, she seemed to join palms in reverence, imploring Dēvas that the Ten-necked should suffer defeat at the hands of the Chief of the Raghus.
5.19.23
समीक्षमाणां रुदतीमनिन्दितां
सुपक्ष्मताम्रायतशुक्ललोचनाम् ।
अनुव्रतां राममतीव मैथिलीं
प्रलोभयामास वधाय रावणः ॥
The blameless Maithili cried, looking around with her wide eyes that were white with a tinge of red protected by lovely eye lids. Rāvaṇa, inviting (his own) death, tried to entice her whose mind was firmly set on Rāma.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे एकोनविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the nineteenth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
