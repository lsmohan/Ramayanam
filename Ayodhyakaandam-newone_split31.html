Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 31
In this Sarga, Lakshmaṇa, listening to the conversation between Rāma and Seetā, says that he would also like to go to Vana along with Rāma. Rāma says that he should rather stay in Ayōdhyā to take care of Kousalyā and Sumitrā.
Lakshmaṇa argues that Bharata would take good care of Kousalyā and Sumitrā, because of his regard for Rāma. He further argues that Kousalyā also, being a very capable lady, could take care of herself and Sumitrā. He succeeds in convincing Rāma and Rāma agrees that Lakshmaṇa  could come to the Vana with him.
2.31.1
एवं शृत्वा तु संवादम्
लक्ष्मणः पूर्वमागतः ।
बाष्पपर्याकुलमुखः
शोकं सोढुमशक्नुवन् ।
स भ्रातुश्चरणौ गाढम्
निपीड्य रघुनन्दनः ।
सीतामुवाचातियशाम्
राघवं च महाव्रतम् ॥
Having listened to this conversation, Lakshmaṇa who was already there, was unable to contain his grief. With his eyes filled with tears, he tightly hugged the feet of Rāma, the delight of Raghu dynasty. Then he said to Seetā, the highly renowned, and to Rāghava, the highly disciplined:
2.31.3
यदि गन्तुं कृताबुद्धिः
वनं मृगगजायुतम् ।
अहं त्वानुगमिष्यामि
वनमग्रे धनुर्धरः ॥
If you have made up your mind to leave to the Vana, where deer and elephants abound, I shall also go ahead of you, holding the bow.
2.31.4
मया समेतोऽरण्यानि
बहूनि विचरिष्यसि ।
पक्षिभिर्मृगयूथैश्च
संघुष्टानि समन्ततः ॥
May you see me by your side as you roam through the many forests echoing with the cries of birds and sounds of multitude of animals.
2.31.5
न देवलोकाक्रमणम्
नामरत्वमहं वृणे ।
ऐश्वर्यं वापि लोकानाम्
कामये न त्वया विना ॥
I would neither seek the dominance over the world of Dēvas, nor seek the lordship of all the worlds nor like to attain immortality, in preference to being in your company.
2.31.6
एवं ब्रुवाणस्सौमित्रिः
वनवासाय निश्चितः ।
रामेण बहुभिस्सान्त्वैः
निषिध्दः पुनरब्रवीत् ॥
Rāma tried to dissuade the son of Sumitrā who spoke thus, firmly intent on going to Vana. But then Lakshmaṇa said again:
2.31.7
अनुज्ञातश्च भवता
पूर्वमेव यदस्म्यहम् ।
किमिदानीं पुनरिदम्
क्रियते मन्निवारणम् ॥
You have already given me permission. Why are you stopping me again, now?
2.31.8
यदर्थं प्रतिषेधो मे
क्रियते गन्तुमिच्छतः ।
एतदिच्छामि विज्ञातुम्
संशयो हि ममानघ ॥
O faultless one! I would like to know why, then, are you trying to stop me from going! Am I missing anything?
2.31.9
ततोऽब्रवीन्महातेजा
रामो लक्ष्मणमग्रतः ।
स्थितं प्राग्गामिनं वीरम्
याचमानं कृताञ्जलिम् ॥
Then that supremely radiant Rāma spoke to Lakshmaṇa, who stood in front of him, beseeching him thus with joined palms and ready to leave ahead of him:
2.31.10
स्निग्धो धर्मरतो वीरः
सततं सत्पथे स्थितः ।
प्रियः प्राणसमो वश्यो
भ्राता चापि सखा च मे ॥
You are very affectionate. You are interested in Dharma. You are a Veera. You always tread the right path. You are dear to me like my own life. You are always obedient to me. You are not only a brother but also a friend to me.
2.31.11
मयाऽद्य सह सौमित्रे
त्वयि गच्छति तद्वनम् ।
को भरिष्यति कौसल्याम्
सुमित्रां वा यशस्विनीम् ॥
If you go along with me today to that Vana, O son of Sumitrā, who will take care of Kousalyā and Sumitrā, who have had a reputable life thus far!
2.31.12
अभिवर्षति कामैर्यः
पर्जन्यः पृथिवीमिव ।
स कामपाशपर्यस्तो
महातेजा महीपतिः ॥
The supremely radiant ruler of the world, who is capable of giving everything, like the clouds to the earth, is now in the grip of passion (towards Kaikēyee).
The king spent more time with Kaikēyee, his younger wife, unable to treat Kousalyā and Sumitrā as well as he should have.
2.31.13
सा हि राज्यमिदं प्राप्य
नृपस्याश्वपते स्सुता ।
दुःखितानां सपत्नीनाम्
न करिष्यति शोभनम् ॥
The daughter of the king Aṡwapati (Kaikēyee), after gaining the reins of the kingdom, cannot be expected to do good for her despondent co-wives!
2.31.14
न स्मरिष्यति कौसल्याम्
सुमित्रां च सुदुःखिताम् ।
भरतो राज्यमासाद्य
कैकेय्यां पर्यवस्थितः ॥
Bharata, having attained the kingdom and being with Kaikēyee, may not even think of the grieving Kousalyā and Sumitrā.
2.31.15
तामार्यां स्वयमेवेह
राजानुग्रहणेन वा ।
सौमित्रे भर कौसल्याम्
उक्तमर्थमिमं चर ॥
O son of Sumitrā! By yourself, or with the pleasure of the king, may you take care of revered Kousalyā! May you commit to what I am saying!
2.31.16
एवं मम च ते भक्तिः
भविष्यति सुदर्शिता ।
धर्मज्ञ गुरुपूजायाम्
धर्मश्चाप्यतुलो महान् ॥
Lakshmaṇa! O knower of Dharma! It would be a hallmark of your devotion to me if you can do this! Further, serving elders would be incomparable and great Dharma!
2.31.17
एवं कुरुष्व सौमित्रे
मत्कृते रघुनन्दन ।
अस्माभिर्विप्रहीणाया
मातुर्नो न भवेत्सुखम् ॥
Please do so, O son of Sumitrā! O delight of Raghu dynasty! Bereft of us, mother will know no happiness.
2.31.18
एवमुक्तस्तु रामेण
लक्ष्मणश्श्लक्ष्णया गिरा ।
प्रत्युवाच तदा रामम्
वाक्यज्ञो वाक्यकोविदम् ॥
Hearing those words of Rāma, who possessed excellent communication skills, Lakshmaṇa, who was equally eloquent, replied in a gentle manner:
2.31.19
तवैव तेजसा वीर
भरतः पूजयिष्यति ।
कौसल्यां च सुमित्रां च
प्रयतो नात्र संशयः ॥
O Veera! Because of regard to your radiance, Bharata will extend every honor to Kousalyā and Sumitrā with due humility. There shall be no doubt about that.
2.31.20
कौसल्या बिभृयादार्या
सहस्रमपि मद्विधान् ।
यस्यास्सहस्रं ग्रामाणाम्
सम्प्राप्त मुपजीवनम् ॥
There are a thousand villages that thrive because of her grace! Such noble Kousalyā can, in fact, Support a thousand people like me!
2.31.21
तदात्मभरणे चैव
मम मातुस्तथैव च ।
पर्याप्ता मद्विधानां च
भरणाय यशस्विनी ॥
Hence, that well regarded lady is easily capable of taking care of herself as well as my mother and many people like me.
2.31.22
कुरुष्व मामनुचरम्
वैधर्म्यं नेह विद्यते ।
कृतार्थोऽहं भविष्यामि
तव चार्थः प्रकल्पते ॥
There is nothing against Dharma that I see here. Let me be your follower. I feel myself fulfilled. And your object will also be attained.
2.31.23
धनुरादाय सशरम्
खनित्रपिटकाधरः ।
अग्रतस्ते गमिष्यामि
पन्थानमनुदर्शयन् ॥
With arrows and bow, and with a crowbar and a basket, I shall go ahead of you showing the path.
2.31.24
आहरिष्यामि ते नित्यम्
मूलानि च फलानि च ।
वन्यानि यानि चान्यानि
स्वाहाराणि तपस्विनाम् ॥
I shall gather and give you every day, roots, fruits and other products of the Vana worthy of being offered to Tapasvis.
2.31.25
भवांस्तु सह वैदेह्या
गिरिसानुषु रंस्यते ।
अहं सर्वं करिष्यामि
जाग्रतस्स्वपतश्च ते ॥
While you enjoy the slopes of mountains along with Seetā, I shall do everything, when you are awake as well as when you are asleep.
2.31.26
रामस्त्वनेन वाक्येन
सुप्रीतः प्रत्युवाच तम् ॥
Rāma was pleased to hear this response and said:
2.31.26
व्रजापृच्छस्व सौमित्रे
सर्वमेव सुहृज्जनम् ।
ये च राज्ञो ददौ दिव्ये
महात्मा वरुण स्स्वयम् ।
जनकस्य महायज्ञे
धनुषी रौद्रदर्शने ।
अभेद्यकवचे दिव्ये
तूणी चाक्षयसायकौ ।
आदित्यविमलौ चोभौ
खड्गौ हेमपरिष्कृतौ ।
सत्कृत्य निहितं सर्वम्
एतदाचार्यसद्मनि ।
सर्वमायुधमादाय
क्षिप्रमाव्रज लक्ष्मण ॥
O son of Sumitrā, then, please go and take leave of all your friends!
O! Lakshmaṇa, Mahātma Varuṇa had given to Janaka, on the occasion of the Mahā Yajña, two fearsome divine bows, two divine impenetrable armors, two quivers with inexhaustible arrows and two swords that shine as bright as the sun with hilts inlaid with gold.
They are kept in the house of the Guru (Vasishṭha) and are maintained duly. Please go and bring them swiftly!
2.31.30
स सुहृज्जनमामन्त्र्य
वनवासाय निश्चितः ।
इक्ष्वाकुगुरुमागम्य
जग्राहायुधमुत्तमम् ॥
Having decided to live in the Vana, he took leave of his friends and went to the Guru of Ikshwāku dynasty and collected the exquisite weapons from him.
2.31.31
तद्धिव्यं राजशार्दूल
सत्कृतं माल्यभूषितम् ।
रामाय दर्शयामास
सौमित्रिस्सर्वमायुधम् ॥
The son of Sumitrā, tiger among princely men, presented the well treated weapons, which were decorated with flower garlands, to Rāma.
2.31.32
तमुवाचात्मवान् रामः
प्रीत्या लक्ष्मणमागतम् ।
काले त्वमागत सौम्य
काङ्क्षिते मम लक्ष्मण ॥
To that Lakshmaṇa, who returned thus, the spirited Rāma said lovingly: "O gentle one! You have come in time, just the way I wished!"
2.31.33
अहं प्रदातुमिच्छामि
यदिदं मामकं धनम् ।
ब्राह्मणेभ्यस्तपस्विभ्यः
त्वया सह परन्तप ॥
O tormentor of foes! I, along with you, would like to give away all of my riches to Brāhmaṇas, who are rich with Tapa!
2.31.34
वसन्तीह दृढं भक्त्या
गुरुषु द्विजसत्तमाः ।
तेषामपि च मे भूयः
सर्वेषाञ्चोपजीविनाम् ॥
I would like to give to the Brāhmaṇas living here who are unwaveringly devoted to their Gurus and also to everyone who is dependent on me for their livelihood.
2.31.35
वसिष्ठपुत्रं तु सुयज्ञमार्यं
त्वमानयाशु प्रवरं द्विजानाम् ।
अभिप्रयास्यामि वनं समस्ता-
नभ्यर्च्य शिष्टानपरान् द्विजातीन् ॥
Please go and bring swiftly the revered Suyajña, son of Vasishṭha and the foremost among Brāhmaṇas. I will leave to Vana after paying respects to all the foremost Brāhmaṇas of exemplary conduct.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty first Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
