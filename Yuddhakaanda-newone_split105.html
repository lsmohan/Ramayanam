Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 105
In this short Sarga, Agastya, the great Ṛshi comes to Rāma and tells him reveals to him the age-old secret of the victorious, the recitation of the hymn, Āditya Hṛdayam. Rāma receives it from Agastya and recites it turning towards the sun. He experiences supreme felicity and goes on to fight Rāvaṇa, resolved to slay him.
6.105.1
ततो युद्धपरिश्रान्तं
समरे चिन्तया स्थितम् ।
रावणं चाग्रतो दृष्ट्वा
युद्धाय समुपस्थितम् ।
दैवतैश्च समागम्य
द्रष्टुमभ्यागतो रणम् ।
उपागम्याब्रवीद्रामम्
अगस्त्यो भगवानृषिः ॥
Then the Ṛshi, Bhagawān Agastya who had come along with the Dēvas to witness the battle saw Rāma standing in the battlefield somewhat exhausted and concerned on seeing Rāvaṇa stand in front of him ready for the fight again. The Ṛshi approached Rāma and said:
6.105.3
राम राम महाबाहो
शृणु गुह्यं सनातनम् ।
येन सर्वानरीन्वत्स
समरे विजयिष्यसि ॥
O Rāma! O Rāma of mighty arm! Listen to this age-old secret with which, my dear, you can vanquish all your enemies.
6.105.4
आदित्यहृदयं पुण्यम्
सर्वशत्रुविनाशनम् ।
जयावहं जपेन्नित्यम्
अक्षय्यं परमं शिवम् ।
सर्वमङ्गलमाङ्गल्यम्
सर्वपापप्रणाशनम् ।
चिन्ताशोकप्रशमनम्
आयुर्वर्धनमुत्तमम् ॥
This sacred hymn called Āditya Hṛdayam (Brahman, the quintessence of the Sun) which destroys all the enemies and ensures victory, should be chanted every day. This absolutely holy hymn confers benefits that would never get exhausted. It is the most powerful of all hymns that bestow all that is good. This singular hymn washes off all sins, provides relief from every kind of worry and grief, and bestows one with a long life.
6.105.6
रश्मिमन्तं समुद्यन्तम्
देवासुरनमस्कृतम् ।
पूजयस्व विवस्वन्तम्
भास्करं भुवनेश्वरम् ॥
Do Pūja to Vivaswan (the sun) the lord of the worlds who receives salutations from all the Dēvas and Asuras, and who rises, spreading his rays and lighting up every thing.
6.105.7
सर्वदेवात्मको ह्येष
तेजस्वी रश्मिभावनः ।
एष देवासुरगणान्
लोकान् पाति गभिस्तिभिः ॥
He, the powerful one, who protects all with his rays, is the quintessence of all the Dēvas. With his rays, he protects all the worlds and the companies of Dēvas and Asuras.
6.105.8
एष ब्रह्मा च विष्णुश्च
शिवः स्कन्दः प्रजापतिः ।
महेन्द्रो धनदः कालो
यमः सोमो ह्यपां पतिः ॥
He himself is Brahma, Vishṇu and Ṡiva, Skanda, Prajāpati, Mahēndra and Kubēra. He himself is Time, Yama, the moon, and Varuṇa, the lord of all waters.
6.105.9
पितरो वसवः साध्या
ह्यश्विनौ मरुतो मनुः ।
वायुर्वह्निः प्रजाः प्राण
ऋतुकर्ता प्रभाकरः ॥
He himself is the ancestors in the higher worlds, Sādhyas, Aṡwins, Maruts, Manu, Vāyu, Agni, the creatures, the maker of seasons and the one who illuminates (everything).
6.105.10
आदित्यः सविता सूर्यः
खगः पूषा गभस्तिमान् ।
सुवर्णसदृशो भानुः
हिरण्यरेता दिवाकरः ॥
He is the son of Aditi. He brings forth all the creatures into being. He incites everyone into action. He moves across the sky. He nourishes all life. It is his innate nature to radiate. He shines with golden radiance. His seed is golden and he creates the daytime.
6.105.11
हरिदश्वः सहस्रार्चिः
सप्तसप्तिर्मरीचिमान् ।
तिमिरोन्मथनः शम्भुः
त्वष्टा मार्ताण्ड अंशुमान् ॥
His horses are green and his rays are innumerable. His horse is called Sapti. He emits rays and dispels the darkness. He is the well from which happiness springs. He destroys everything by scorching. He brings the world back to life again after the Dissolution, with his rays.
6.105.12
हिरण्यगर्भः शिशिरः
तपनो भास्करो रविः ।
अग्निगर्भोऽदितेः पुत्रः
शङ्खः शिशिरनाशनः ॥
The rich and diverse world comes from his womb. He provides solace to everyone who is battered by the vicissitudes of life. He is the source of heat and brightness. He is the object of everyone's praise. There is fire at his core. He is the son of Aditi. He mellows down naturally at the end of the day. He destroys the snow and fog.
6.105.13
व्योमनाथस्तमोभेदी
ऋग्यजुस्सामपारगः ।
घनवृष्टिरपां मित्रो
विन्ध्यवीथीप्लवङ्गमः ॥
He is the king of the sky. He pierces through the darkness. He alone can fathom the absolute depths of the Vēdas Ṛk, Yajuh and Sāma. He is the cause of downpours and a friend of waters. He hops freely on a course in the sky.
6.105.14
आतपी मण्डली मृत्युः
पिङ्गलः सर्वतापनः ।
कविर्विश्वो महातेजा
रक्तः सर्वभवोद्भवः ॥
He scorches. He has a halo. He is Death itself. He is bright red (at dawn and dusk). He scorches everything. He is the poet (who writes the song of life). He is the universe itself. He is extraordinarily powerful. He loves all and is the source of the cycle of all life.
6.105.15
नक्षत्रग्रहताराणाम्
अधिपो विश्वभावनः ।
तेजसामपि तेजस्वी
द्वादशात्मन्नमोऽस्तु ते ॥
He is the lord of all stars, planets and constellations. He fashions the universe and is the luminary among all luminaries. Salutations to you, who manifests in twelve forms!
6.105.16
नमः पूर्वाय गिरये
पश्चिमायाद्रये नमः ।
ज्योतिर्गणानां पतये
दिनाधिपतये नमः ॥
Salutations to the Eastern mountain! Salutations to the Western mountain! Salutations to you, the lord of the host of luminaries! Salutations to you, the lord of the day!
6.105.17
जयाय जयभद्राय
हर्यश्वाय नमो नमः ।
नमो नमः सहस्रांशो
आदित्याय नमो नमः ॥
Salutations upon salutations to you, who bestows victory, success and security and who rides on green horses! Salutations upon salutations to you of innumerable rays! Salutations upon salutations to you, O son of Aditi!
6.105.18
नम उग्राय वीराय
सारङ्गाय नमो नमः ।
नमः पद्मप्रबोधाय
मार्ताण्डाय नमो नमः ॥
Salutations to you, who is ruthless with the foes, and who prompts the creatures into action! Salutations upon salutations to you of immense speed! Salutations to you, who causes the lotuses to bloom! Salutations upon salutations to you of immense speed! Salutations to you, who breathes life again into the world, after Dissolution!
6.105.19
ब्रह्मेशानाच्युतेशाय
सूर्यायादित्यवर्चसे ।
भास्वते सर्वभक्षाय
रौद्राय वपुषे नमः ॥
Salutations to you, Surya, who is none other than Brahma, Eeṡwara and Acyuta in a physical form, who glows with the splendor of being the son of Aditi, who dazzles the eyes, who devours all life, and who has a fierce body!
6.105.20
तमोघ्नाय हिमध्नाय
शत्रुघ्नायामितात्मने ।
कृतघ्नघ्नाय देवाय
ज्योतिषां पतये नमः ॥
Salutations to you, who dispels darkness, who destroys the snows and the foes, who is limitlessly vast, who destroys the ingrate, who is lustrous and is the lord of luminaries!
6.105.21
तप्तचामीकराभाय
वह्नये विश्वकर्मणे ।
नमस्तमोभिनिघ्नाय
रुचये लोकसाक्षिणे ॥
Salutations to you, of the luster of molten gold, the source of heat, the artificer of the worlds, the embodiment of luster, a witness to all the worlds, and who destroys darkness.
6.105.22
नाशयत्येष वै भूतम्
तदेव सृजति प्रभुः ।
पायत्येष तपत्येष
वर्षत्येष गभस्तिभिः ॥
He is the lord who destroys the world and also the one who recreates it. His rays dry up and scorch everything and and he also drenches everything with showers.
6.105.23
एष सुप्तेषु जागर्ति
भूतेषु परिनिष्ठितः ।
एष एवाग्निहोत्रं च
फलं चैवाग्निहोत्रिणाम् ॥
Residing deep in everybody, he keeps a vigil even when they are asleep. He is the fire-rite as well as the fruit thereof.
6.105.24
वेदाश्च क्रतवश्चैव
क्रतूनां फलमेव च ।
यानि कृत्यानि लोकेषु
सर्व एष रविः प्रभुः ॥
He is the Vēdas, he is the Yajñas and he is the fruit from the Yajñas. He is Ravi who oversees every activity that happens in the world.
6.105.25
एनमापत्सु कृच्छ्रेषु
कान्तारेषु भयेषु च ।
कीर्तयन् पुरुषः कश्चित्
नावसीदति राघव ॥
Whoever extols him, O Rāghava, in times of peril, hardship, fear, or when caught in the wilderness, will not fall apart.
6.105.26
पूजयस्वैनमेकाग्रो
देवदेवं जगत्पतिम् ।
एतत्रिगुणितं जप्त्वा
युद्धेषु विजयिष्यसि ।
अस्मिन् क्षणे महाबाहो
रावणं त्वं वधिष्यसि ॥
Do Pūja to him, the Dēva of the Dēvas, the lord of the worlds, with complete devotion. By reciting this three times, you will emerge victorious in battles. The moment for you to slay Rāvaṇa has arrived, O you of mighty arm!
6.105.27
एवमुक्त्वा तदाऽगस्त्यो
जगाम च यथागतम् ।
एतच्छुत्वा महातेजा
नष्टशोकोऽभवत्तदा ॥
Having said this, Agastya went away, just as he came. Hearing this, the extraordinarily powerful one was relieved of his worries.
6.105.28
धारयामास सुप्रीतो
राघवः प्रयतात्मवान् ।
आदित्यं प्रेक्ष्य जप्त्वा तु
परं हर्षमवाप्तवान् ॥
Rāghava, steadying his mind, meditated on it, greatly pleased. Turning towards the sun and reciting it, he experienced supreme felicity.
6.105.29
त्रिराचम्य शुचिर्भूत्वा
धनुरादाय वीर्यवान् ।
रावणं प्रेक्ष्य हृष्टात्मा
युद्धाय समुपागमत् ।
सर्वयत्नेन महता
वधे तस्य धृतोऽभवत् ॥
Taking water three times and purifying himself, the valiant one took up his bow. He took a good look at Rāvaṇa, and went forth, in high spirits, to fight him, resolved upon slaying him by exerting himself to the full.
6.105.31
अथ रविरवदन्निरीक्ष्य रामं
मुदितमनाः परमं प्रहृष्यमाणः ।
निशिचरपतिसङ्क्षयं विदित्वा
सुरगणमध्यगतो वचस्त्वरेति ॥
And then Ravi, Son-God, from the midst of the hosts of Dēvas, his heart in joy and in great elation, being sure that the destruction of the lord of Night-rovers was imminent, looked at Rāma and said to him, 'Make haste, no more holding back!’
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे पञ्चोत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and fifth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
