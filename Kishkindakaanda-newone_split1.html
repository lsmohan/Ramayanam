Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 1
In this Sarga, the gorgeous spring, with all the trees in bloom, and with the amorous birds calling each other and many a deer playing with its mate, proves to be a curse to Rāma who is missing his beloved, with whom he had enjoyed many such springs earlier. Rāma feels dejected and does not see himself continuing to live on without his beloved, and guesses that Seetā must be feeling the same. Lakshmaṇa strokes him saying that he should not yield to the passions raised in him by the spring, but should focus on the task ahead and tells that there is nothing impossible for one who is motivated.
Meanwhile Sugreeva and other Vānaras see the imposing Rāma and Lakshmaṇa walking towards them and get scared. Sugreeva stands still, motionless. Other Vānaras retreat to their Āṡrama, where they gather usually.
4.1.1
स तां पुष्करिणीं गत्वा
पद्मोत्पलझषाकुलाम् ।
रामस्सौमित्रि सहितो
विललापाकुलेन्द्रियः ॥
Rāma, after thus reaching the lake that was full of lotuses and lilies and teeming with fish, along with Sowmitri, felt greatly agitated, and lamented.
4.1.2
तत्र दृष्ट्वैव तां हर्षात्
इन्द्रियाणि चकम्पिरे ।
स कामवशमापन्नः
सौमित्रिमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
His senses were invigorated with delight, at the very sight of that lake. He felt an intense sense of longing (for Seetā) and said these words to Sowmitri:
4.1.3
सौमित्रे शोभते पम्पा
वैढूर्यविमलोदका ।
फुल्लपद्मोत्पलवती
शोभिता विविधैर्द्रुमैः ॥
O Sowmitri! See the splendid Paṃpā with its water clear as lazulite! It is resplendent with lotuses and lilies in bloom, and with trees of many kinds.
4.1.4
सौमित्रे पश्य पम्पायाः
काननं शुभदर्शनम् ।
यत्र राजन्ति शैलाभा
द्रुमास्सशिखरा इव ॥
O Sowmitri! Look at the woods of the Paṃpā, which are a feast to the eyes, with trees rising high like mountains with lofty crests.
4.1.5
मां तु शोकाभिसन्तप्तम्
माधयः पीडयन्निवै ।
भरतस्य च दुःखेन
वैदेह्या हरणेन च ॥
Scorched as I am with the grief of Bharata’s distress and the abduction of Seetā, the stimulated senses make me struggle.
4.1.6
शोकार्तस्यापि मे पम्पा
शोभते चित्रकानना ।
व्यवकीर्णा बहुविधैः
पुष्पैश्शीतोदका शिवा ॥
But, though distraught with grief, I am enchanted at the sight of the Paṃpā with its variegated woods, its cool and pure waters, strewn with an assortment of flowers.
4.1.7
नलिनैरपि सञ्छन्ना
ह्यत्यर्थंशुभदर्शना ।
सर्पव्यालानुचरिता
मृगद्विजसमाकुला ॥
Covered with a mass of lotuses, it is extremely captivating. Snakes and beasts move about here; deer and birds are abundant.
4.1.8
अधिकं प्रविभात्येतत्
नीलपीतं तु शाद्वलम् ।
द्रुमाणां विविधैः पुष्पैः
परिस्तोमैरिवार्पितम् ॥
The bluish and yellowish grass looks extremely beautiful, with a variety of flowers from the trees (fallen on it), as if it is covered by a carpet.
4.1.9
पुष्पभारसमृद्धानि
शिखराणि समन्ततः ।
लताभिः पुष्पिताग्राभिः
उपगूढानि सर्वशः ॥
The tree tops everywhere are loaded with flowers and are held in tight embrace by the climbing vines which themselves have burst into bloom.
4.1.10
सुखानिलोऽयं सौमित्रे
कालः प्रचुरमन्मथः ।
गन्धवान्सुरभिर्मासो
जातपुष्पफलद्रुमः ॥
O Sowmitri, this pleasant breeze engenders passion. This season is prideful with fragrance (in the air) and the trees putting forth flowers and fruits.
4.1.11
पश्यरूपाणि सौमित्रे
वनानां पुष्पशालिनाम् ।
सृजतां पुष्पवर्षाणि
तोयं तोयमुचामिव ॥
O Sowmitri! See these woods in full bloom letting down a shower of flowers like clouds letting down rain.
4.1.12
प्रस्तरेषु च रम्येषु
विविधाः काननद्रुमाः ।
वायुवेगप्रचलिताः
पुष्पैरवकिरन्ति गाम् ॥
The trees of the forest, shaken by the violent winds, scatter flowers on the ground all over the beautiful mountainsides.
4.1.13
पतितैः पतमानैश्च
पादपस्थैश्च मारुतः ।
कुसुमैः पश्य सौमित्रे
क्रीडन्निव समन्ततः ॥
O Sowmitri! See how the wind everywhere is playing with the flowers that have fallen, that are falling and those that are on the trees.
4.1.14
विक्षिपन्विविधाश्शाखा
नगानां कुसुमोत्कचाः ।
मारुतश्चलितस्थानैः
षट्पदैरनुगीयते ॥
The wind shakes the tree-branches loaded with flowers. The bees, dislodged, hum in rhythm with it.
4.1.15
मत्तकोकिलसन्नादैः
नर्तयन्निव पादपान् ।
शैलकन्दरनिष्क्रान्तः
प्रगीत इव चानिलः ॥
The wind blowing from the mountain caves seems to sing in tune with the amorous Kōkilas and seem to make the trees dance to the tune.
4.1.16
तेन विक्षिपताऽत्यर्थम्
पवनेन समन्ततः ।
अमी संसक्तशाखाग्रा
ग्रथिता इव पादपाः ॥
These trees seem to be tied with each other, their branches enmeshed, swaying from the wind on every side.
4.1.17
स एष सुखसंस्पर्शो
वाति चन्दनशीतलः ।
गन्धमभ्यावहन्पुण्यम्
श्रमापनयनोऽनिलः ॥
But the same wind, with its cool and comforting touch like that of sandal, and carrying the best of fragrances, comforts the fatigued.
4.1.18
अमी पवनविक्षिप्ता
विनदन्तीव पादपाः ।
षट्पदैरनुकूजन्तो
वनेषु मधुगन्धिषु ॥
The trees in the honey-scented woods, swayed by the wind, seem to enjoy as the bees hum along with them.
4.1.19
गिरिप्रस्थेषु रम्येषु
पुष्पवद्भिर्मनोरमैः ।
संसक्तशिखराश्शैला
विराजन्ते महाद्रुमैः ॥
The mountains are resplendent with high crests, their beautiful flanks full of giant trees in bloom, ravishing the heart.
4.1.20
पुष्पसञ्छन्नशिखरा
मारुतोत्क्षेपचञ्चलाः ।
अमी मधुकरोत्तंसाः
प्रगीता इव पादपाः ॥
The trees, their tops covered by flowers, with hovering bees for ornaments and swaying with the wind, look as if they are ready for a performance.
4.1.21
सुपुष्पितांस्तु पश्यैतान्
कर्णिकारांत्समन्ततः ।
हाटकप्रतिसञ्छन्नान्
नरान्पीताम्बरानिव ॥
Look at these Karṇikāras all around, full of flowers. They are like men in yellow garments, covered in gold.
4.1.22
अयं वसन्तस्सौमित्रे
नानाविहगनादितः ।
सीतया विप्रहीणस्य
शोकसन्दीपनो मम ॥
This spring with its choir of many birds, O Sowmitri, enhances my grief of separation from Seetā.
4.1.23
मां हि शोकसमाक्रान्तम्
सन्तापयति मन्मथः ।
हृष्टं प्रवदमानश्च
ममाह्वयति कोकिलः ॥
Manmatha burns my body, plunged in grief as I am. The Kōkila challenges me with its happy notes.
Manmatha is God of Passion.
4.1.24
एष दात्यूहको हृष्टो
रम्ये मां वननिर्झरे ।
प्रणदन्मन्मथाविष्टम्
शोचयिष्यति लक्ष्मण ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! The Dātyūhaka bird, crowing from the sylvan brook, fills me with sadness, suffering as I do in the overpowering grip of passion.
4.1.25
श्रुत्वैतस्य पुरा शब्दम्
आश्रमस्था मम प्रिया ।
मामाहूय प्रमुदिता
परमं प्रत्यनन्दत ॥
Earlier, whenever my beloved, sitting in the Āṡrama, heard this cry, used to feel very happy and call out to me in joy.
4.1.26
एवं विचित्राः पतगा
नानारावविराविणः ।
वृक्षगुल्मलताः पश्य
सम्पतन्ति ततस्ततः ॥
Look, how the amazing birds uttering various cries fly from everywhere to the trees, shrubs and creepers!
4.1.27
विमिश्रा विहगाः पुम्भिः
आत्मव्यूहाभिनन्दिताः ।
भृङ्गराजप्रमुदिताः
सौमित्रे मधुरस्वना: ॥
O Sowmitri! The female birds cooing sweet notes, delighted with the hum of the best of the bees, unite with their males, applauded by the flocks of their kind!
4.1.28
अस्याः कूले प्रमुदिताः
सन्घशः शकुनास्त्विह ।
दात्यूहरतविक्रन्दैः
पुंस्कोकिलरुतैरपि ।
स्वनन्ति पादपाश्चेमे
मामनङ्गप्रदीपकाः ॥
O Sowmitri! Many flocks of birds regale here, on these shores (of Paṃpā). The amorous cries of Dātyūhaka bird and also the coos of the male Kōkilas from the trees kindle the urge for love in me.
4.1.29
अशोकस्तबकाङ्गारः
षट्पदस्वननिस्वनः ।
मां हि पल्लवताम्रार्चिः
वसन्ताग्निः प्रधक्ष्यति ॥
The fire of spring, with the tender shoots for tongues, the bunches of (red) flowers of Aṡōka for sparkles, and the hum of bees for crackling noise, is going to burn me, for certain.
4.1.30
न हि तां सूक्ष्मपक्ष्माक्षीम्
सुकेशीं मृदुभाषिणीम् ।
अपश्यतो मे सौमित्रे
जीवितेऽस्ति प्रयोजनम् ॥
O Sowmitri! There is no purpose to this life, if I do not see her, of delicate eyelids, lovely tresses and soft speech.
4.1.31
अयं हि दयितस्तस्याः
कालो रुचिरकाननः ।
कोकिलाकुलसीमान्तो
दयिताया ममानघ ॥
O sinless one! This season, filled with the coos of Kōkila from beginning to end, and when the woods are lovely, is the favorite of my beloved.
4.1.32
मन्मथाऽयाससम्भूतो
वसन्तगुणवर्धितः ।
अयं मां धक्ष्यति क्षिप्रम्
शोकाग्निर्नचिरादिव ॥
The fire of grief sparked by passion and fanned into flame by the delights of spring, will consume me before long.
4.1.33
अपश्यत स्तां दयिताम्
पश्यतो रुचिरद्रुमान् ।
ममायमात्मप्रभवो
भूयस्त्वमुपयास्यति ॥
The passion engendered in me grows even stronger, with the lovely trees in sight and Seetā not.
4.1.34
अदृश्यमाना वैदेही
शोकं वर्धयते मम ।
दृश्यमानो वसन्तश्च
स्वेदसंसर्गदूषकः ॥
Vaidēhee, not to be seen, whips up my grief, as does the spring seen in front of my eyes which keeps away all traces of sweat (being cool).
4.1.35
मामद्य मृगशाबाक्षी
चिन्ताशोकबलात्कृतम् ।
सन्तापयति सौमित्रे
क्रूरश्चैत्रवनानिलः ॥
O Sowmitri! Now, this cruel breeze of the month of Caitra tortures me, who is already overpowered by grief with the thoughts of the fawn-eyed one.
4.1.36
अमी मयूराश्शोभन्ते
प्रनृत्यन्तस्ततस्ततः ।
स्वैः पक्षैः पवनोद्धूतैः
गवाक्षैः स्फाटिकैरिव ॥
These peacocks dancing here and there, are splendid with their feathers ruffled by the wind, looking like crystal casements.
4.1.37
शिखिनीभिः परिवृताः
ते एते मदमूर्छिताः ।
मन्मथाभिपरीतस्य
मम मन्मथवर्धनाः ॥
Surrounded by the peahens and drunk with love, they intensify my longing for love, submerged by the tides of passion as I am.
4.1.38
पश्य लक्ष्मण नृत्यन्तम्
मयूरमुपनृत्यति ।
शिखिनी मन्मथार्तैषा
भर्तारं गिरिसानुनि ॥
Look at that peahen, Lakshmaṇa, who, mad with love, joins in step with her dancing husband, the peacock, on the hill-side.
4.1.39
तामेव मनसा रामां
मयूरोऽप्युपधावति ।
वितत्य रुचिरौ पक्षौ
रुतैरुपहसन्निव ॥
And the peacock, full of love and with his lovely feathers spread out, follows her running, with cries that seem to make fun of her.
4.1.40
मयूरस्य वने नूनम्
रक्षसा न हृता प्रिया ।
तस्मान्नृत्यति रम्येषु
वनेषु सह कान्तया ॥
Assuredly no Rākshasa has carried off the beloved of the peacock from the forest, and so he can dance with his beloved in the beautiful woods!
4.1.41
मम त्वयं विना वासः
पुष्पमासे सुदुस्सहः ।
पश्य लक्ष्मण संरागम्
तिर्यग्योनिगतेष्वपि ।
यदेषा शिखिनी कामात्
भर्तारमभिवर्तते ॥
It is very difficult for me to live without (her) in this month of bloom. O Lakshmaṇa! Look how impassioned even the simpler forms of life are; even the peahen importunes her mate, impelled by desire.
4.1.43
मामप्येवं विशालाक्षी
जानकी जातसम्भ्रमा ।
मदनेनाभिवर्तेत
यदि नाऽपहृता भवेत् ॥
Jānaki of the wide eyes would have come to me likewise, all aflutter with her longing for love, if only she had not been carried off.
4.1.44
पश्य लक्ष्मण पुष्पाणि
निष्फलानि भवन्ति मे ।
पुष्पभारसमृद्धानाम्
वनानां शिशिरात्यये ॥
See Lakshmaṇa, how all these flowers that bloomed after the passing of winter, filling the woods heavily, are futile for me.
4.1.45
रुचिराण्यपि पुष्पाणि
पादपानामतिश्रिया ।
निष्पलानि महीं यान्ति
समं मधुकरोत्करैः ॥
The flowers, lovely on the majestic trees, are wasted falling on the ground along with the bees.
4.1.46
वदन्ति कामं शकुना
मुदितास्सङ्घशः कलम् ।
आह्वयन्त इवान्योन्यम्
कामोन्मादकरा मम ॥
The birds, carefree, singing sweetly in chorus, seem to call to each other, maddening me with the longing for love.
4.1.47
वसन्तो यदि तत्रापि
यत्र मे वसति प्रिया ।
नूनं परवशा सीता
सापि शोचत्यहं यथा ॥
If it is now spring there where Seetā, my beloved, is held captive, she too must be pining as I do.
4.1.48
नूनं न तु वसन्तस्तं
देशं स्पृशति यत्र सा ।
कथं ह्यसितपद्माक्षी
वर्तयेत्सा मया विना ॥
Spring would not touch the region where she is, for certain. How could she of black eyes live without me?
4.1.49
अथवा वर्तते तत्र
वसन्तो यत्र मे प्रिया ।
किं करिष्यति सुश्रोणी
सा तु निर्भर्त्सिता परैः ॥
Even if there is spring where my beloved is, what can she of the lovely Kaṭee, intimidated by the enemy, do?
4.1.50
श्यामा पद्मपलाशाक्षी
मृदुभाषा च मे प्रिया ।
नूनं वसन्तमासाद्य
परित्यक्ष्यति जीवितम् ॥
My beloved, her speech so gentle and her eyes like lotus petals, will without a doubt give up her life with the onset of the (irresistible) spring.
4.1.51
दृढं हि हृदये बुद्धिः
मम सम्परिवर्तते ।
नालं वर्तयितुं सीता
साध्वी मद्विरहं गता ॥
I know, I feel it in my bones, that Seetā, the gentle one, will not be able to live bereaved of me.
4.1.52
मयि भावो हि वैदेह्याः
तत्त्वतो विनिवेशितः ।
ममापि भावस्सीतायाम्
सर्वथा विनिवेशितः ॥
For the thoughts of Vaidēhee are deeply seated in me, and the thoughts of mine in her as well, in every which way.
4.1.53
एष पुष्पवहो वायुः
सुखस्पर्शो हिमावहः ।
तां विचिन्तयतः कान्ताम्
पावकप्रतिमो मम ॥
This breeze carrying flowers, spreading coolness everywhere and comfortable to the touch, is like fire to me as I am (left) searching for my beloved.
4.1.54
सदा सुखमहं मन्ये
यं पुरा सह सीतया ।
मारुतस्स विना सीताम्
शोकसंजननो मम ॥
The breeze which I used to feel to be always pleasant in the days when I used to be with Seetā, engenders only grief in me, now that she is not here.
4.1.55
तां विनाथ विहङ्गोऽसौ
पक्षी प्रणदितस्तदा ।
वायसः पादपगतः
प्रहृष्टमभिकूजति ॥
This sky bound bird, the crow, which then croaked harshly, is now perched on the tree cawing gladly, when she is not here.
The glad caw of the crow is a good omen.
4.1.56
एष वै तत्र वैदेह्या
विहगः प्रतिहारकः ।
पक्षी मां तु विशालाक्ष्याः
समीपमुपनेष्यति ॥
This bird alone, which oversaw the abduction of Seetā from the sky, has to now take me to the lady of wide eyes.
4.1.57
शृणु लक्ष्मण सन्नादम्
वने मदविवर्धनम् ।
पुष्पिताग्रेषु वृक्षेषु
द्विजानामुपकूजताम् ॥
Listen, O Lakshmaṇa, to the chorus of bird-cries from the woods that stimulates passion, raised by the birds warbling from the tree-tops covered with flowers.
4.1.58
विक्षिप्तां पवनेनैताम्
असौ तिलकमञ्जरीम् ।
षट्पदस्सहसाऽभ्येति
मदोद्धूतामिव प्रियाम् ॥
This bee hastens to this bunch of Tilaka flowers, that are swaying in the breeze, as if it were his beloved reeling drunk.
4.1.59
कामिनामयमत्यन्तम्
अशोकश्शोकवर्धनः ।
स्तबकैः पवनोत्क्षिप्तैः
तर्जयन्निव मां स्थितः ॥
This Aṡōka, which intensifies the pining of lovers, seems to mind me with its bunches of flowers swayed by the wind.
4.1.60
अमी लक्ष्मण दृश्यन्ते
चूताः कुसुमशालिनः ।
विभ्रमोत्सिक्तमनसः
साङ्गरागा नरा इव ॥
These mango trees in bloom look, O Lakshmaṇa, like men fired by thoughts of love, their bodies covered with fragrant unguents.
4.1.61
सौमित्रे पश्य पम्पायाः
चित्रासु वनराजिषु ।
किन्नरा नरशार्दूल
विचरन्ति ततस्ततः ॥
Look, O Sowmitri, O tiger among men, how the Kinnaras move about here and there in these lovely rows of woods of Paṃpā!
4.1.62
इमानि शुभगन्धीनि
पश्य लक्ष्मण सर्वशः ।
नलिनानि प्रकाशन्ते
जले तरुणसूर्यवत् ॥
Look at those sweet-scented lotuses, O Lakshmaṇa, that shine like the morning sun, in the water everywhere.
4.1.63
एषा प्रसन्नसलिला
पद्मनीलोत्पलायुता ।
हंसकारण्डवाकीर्णा
पम्पा सौगन्धिकान्विता ॥
How beautiful this Paṃpā is, with its clear water, its lotus and lily, its teeming swans and Kāraṇḍavas and its Saugandhika flowers!
4.1.64
जले तरुणसूर्याभैः
षट्पदाहतकेसरैः ।
पङ्कजैश्शोभते पम्पा
समन्तादभिसंवृता ॥
The water of Paṃpā is splendid, covered with lotuses shining like the morning sun and their filaments weighed down by the bees.
4.1.65
चक्रवाकयुता नित्यम्
चित्रप्रस्तवनान्तरा ।
मातङ्गमृगयूथैश्च
शोभते सलिलार्थिभिः ॥
It is enchanting, with the ever present Cakravākas, variety of woodland glades, and the hosts of elephants and deer that come to it to slake their thirst.
4.1.66
पवनाहतवेगाभिः
ऊर्मिभिर्विमलेऽम्भसि ।
पङ्कजानि विराजन्ते
ताड्यमानानि लक्ष्मण ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! The lotuses that are swayed by the ripples caused by the wind, look splendid against the clear water!
4.1.67
पद्मपत्रविशालाक्षीम्
सततं पङ्कजप्रियाम् ।
अपश्यतो मे वैदेहीम्
जीवितं नाभिरोचते ॥
Life itself loses its flavor for me, as I do not see Vaidēhee who always loved lotuses and whose eyes are wide like lotus petals!
4.1.68
अहो कामस्य वामत्वम्
यो गतामपि दुर्लभाम् ।
स्मारयिष्यति कल्याणीम्
कल्याणतरवादिनीम् ॥
Ah! how perverse is love, that it keeps reminding me of the auspicious one whose speech is a delight, though she is far away, and hard to attain!
4.1.69
शक्यो धारयितुं कामो
भवेदद्यागतो मया ।
यदि भूयो वसन्तो माम्
न हन्यात्पुष्पितद्रुमः ॥
I would have been able to manage this onslaught of passion if only the onset of spring with its flowering trees did not torment me repeatedly.
4.1.70
यानि स्म रमणीयानि
तया सह भवन्ति मे ।
तान्येवारमणीयानि
जायन्ते मे तया विना ॥
Whatever looked beautiful to me in her company, does not look beautiful any more, without her.
4.1.71
पद्मकोशपलाशानि
दृष्टुं दृष्टिर्हि मन्यते ।
सीताया नेत्रकोशाभ्याम्
सदृशानीति लक्ष्मण ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! My eyes are drawn to the petals of the opening lotus buds, for they are like the Seetā’s eyelids.
4.1.72
पद्मकेसरसंसृष्टो
वृक्षान्तरविनिस्सृतः ।
निश्श्वास इव सीताया
वाति वायुर्मनोहरः ॥
The breeze that has brushed against the lotus filaments and meandered through the trees, steals my heart like Seetā’s breath.
4.1.73
सौमित्रे पश्य पम्पाया
दक्षिणे गिरिसानुनि ।
पुष्पितां कर्णिकारस्य
यष्टिं परमशोभनाम् ॥
O Sowmitri! Look at the lovely branches of the Karṇikāras in bloom on the sides of the hill to the south of the Paṃpā!
4.1.74
अधिकं शैलराजोऽयम्
दातुभिस्सु विभूषितः ।
विचित्रं सृजते रेणुम्
वायुवेग विघट्टितम् ॥
This lord of mountains, ornate with mineral ores, spreads colorful mineral dust blown away by the strong winds.
4.1.75
गिरिप्रस्थास्तु सौमित्रे
सर्वतस्सम्प्रपुष्पितैः ।
निष्पत्रै स्सर्वतो रम्यैः
प्रदीप्ता इव किंशुकैः ॥
The sides of the mountain, O Sowmitri, glow bright, as if they are on fire, with the beautiful Kiṃṡuka trees loaded with flowers and not a leaf to be seen!
4.1.76
पम्पातीररुहाश्चेमे
संसक्ता मधुगन्धिनः ।
मालतीमल्लिकाषण्डाः
करवीराश्च पुष्पिताः ॥
The Mālati, Mallika and Lotus Vines, and the Karaveera trees that grow on the shores of the Paṃpā, and wet with its waters are gloriously in flower, effusing the fragrance of honey.
4.1.77
केतक्य स्सिन्दुवाराश्च
वासन्त्यश्च सुपुष्पिताः ।
माधव्यो गन्धपूर्णाश्च
कुन्दगुल्माश्च सर्वशः ॥
The Kētaka, Sindhuvāra and Vāsanti vines are full of fragrance. Mādhavi vines and Kunda shrubs are laden flowers everywhere.
4.1.78
चिरिबिल्वा मधूकाश्च
वञ्जुला वकुला स्तथा ।
चम्पकास्तिलकाश्चैव
नागवृक्षास्सुपुष्पिताः ।
नीपाश्च वरणाश्चैव
खर्जूराश्च सुपुष्पिता ॥
Ciribilva, Madhūka, Vañjula, Vakula, Campaka, Tilaka, Nāga trees, Neepa, Varaṇa and Kharjūras are in full bloom.
4.1.79
पद्मकाश्चैव शोभन्ते
नीलाशोकाश्च पुष्पिताः ।
लोध्राश्च गिरिपृष्ठेषु
सिंहकेसरपिञ्जराः ॥
Padmakāṡa, Neelāṡōka in flower, are enchanting. So are the Lōdhra trees on hill tops tawny like the lion’s mane.
4.1.80
अङ्कोलाश्च कुरण्टाश्च
चूर्णकाः पारिभद्रकाः ।
चूताः पाटलयश्चैव
कोविदाराश्च पुष्पिताः ।
मुचुकुन्दार्जुनाश्चैव
दृश्यन्ते गिरिसानुषु ।
केतकोद्दालकाश्चैव
शिरीषाः शिंशुपा धवाः ॥
Aṅkōla, Kuraṇṭa, Cūrṇaka, Pāribhadraka Cūta, Pāṭala, Kōvidāra, Mucukunda, Arjuna, Dṛṡyanta, Kētaka, Uddālaka, Ṡireesha, Ṡiṃṡupa and Dhava trees can be seen in full bloom on the mountain sides.
4.1.82
शाल्मल्यः किंशुकाश्चैव
रक्ताः कुरवकास्तथा ।
त्रिनिशा नक्तमालाश्च
चन्दनास्स्यन्दनास्तथा ।
हिन्तालास्तिलकाश्चैव
नागवृक्षाः च पुष्पिताः | ।
पुष्पितान्पुष्पिताग्राभिः
लताभिः परिवेष्टितान् ।
द्रुमान्पश्येह सौमित्रे
पम्पाया रुचिरान्बहून् ॥
Ṡālmalya, Kiṃṡuka, Red Kuravaka, Triniṡā, Naktamāla, Candana, Syandana, Hintāla, Tilaka and Nāga trees are in bloom, with their tops entwined with creepers that are in flower. O Sowmitri! See these abundant beautiful trees, by Paṃpā.
4.1.85
वातविक्षिप्तविटपान्
यथाऽसन्नान्द्रुमानिमान् ।
लतास्समनुवर्तन्ते
मत्ता इव वरस्त्रियः ॥
As the trees come together with their branches blown into each other by the wind the creepers follow them like highly intoxicated women.
4.1.86
पादपात्पादपं गच्छन्
शैलाच्छैलं वनाद्वनम् ।
वाति नैकरसास्वादः
सम्मोदित इवानिलः ॥
And the wind, as if it likes to enjoy a wide variety of tastes, blows from tree to tree, from hill to hill and from wood to wood.
4.1.87
केचित्पर्याप्तकुसुमाः
पादपा मधुगन्धिनः ।
केचिन्मुकुलसंवीताः
श्यामवर्णा इवाऽबभुः ॥
Some trees are splendid, fully covered with flowers and smelling like honey; others, still in bud, splendidly dark.
4.1.88
इदं मृष्टमिदं स्वादु
प्रफुल्लमिदमित्यपि ।
रागयुक्तो मधुकरः
कुसुमेष्वेव लीयते ॥
The bee, impassioned, merges into the flowers, thinking, ‘this is succulent, this is tasty and this is fully blossomed.’
4.1.89
निलीय पुनरुत्पत्य
सहसाऽन्यत्र गच्छति ।
मधुलुब्धो मधुकरः
पम्पातीरद्रुमेष्वसौ ॥
This bee, greedy for honey, enters into a flower, comes out, and goes onto another immediately, among the trees on the banks of Paṃpā.
4.1.90
इयं कुसुमसङ्घातैः
उपस्तीर्णा सुखाकृता ।
स्वयं निपतितैर्भूमिः
शयनप्रस्तरैरिव ॥
The earth here, hidden under masses of flowers that have fallen of themselves, is soft and comfortable like a bed covered with bedspreads.
4.1.91
विविधा विविधैः पुष्पैः
तैरेव नगसानुषु ।
विस्तीर्णाः पीतरक्ताभाः
सौमित्रे प्रस्तराः कृताः ॥
O Sowmitri! With the many flowers spread over them, the many flats on the mountain sides have turned yellow and red.
4.1.92
हिमान्ते पश्य सौमित्रे
वृक्षाणां पुष्पसम्भवम् ।
पुष्पमासे हि तरवः
सङ्घर्षादिव पुष्पिताः ॥
O Sowmitri! See how flowers have begun to appear now that winter has receded and spring arrived; the trees seem to vie with each other in putting forth blooms.
4.1.93
आह्वयन्त इवान्योन्यम्
नगाष्षट्पदनादिताः ।
कुसुमोत्तंसविटपाः
शोभन्ते बहु लक्ष्मण ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! The trees are extremely beautiful, with their boughs wearing coronets of flowers, and seeming to call out to each other with the hum of the bees among them.
4.1.94
एष कारण्डवः पक्षी
विगाह्य सलिलं शुभम् ।
रमते कान्तया सार्धम्
काममुद्दीपयन्मम ॥
The sight of this Kāraṇḍava bird splashing in the pleasant water and playing with its mate inflames my desire.
4.1.95
मन्दाकिन्यास्तु यदिदम्
रूपमेवं मनोहरम् ।
स्थाने जगति विख्याता
गुणास्तस्या मनोरमाः ॥
Only Mandākini has such captivating charm in its form. Its beauty and enchanting qualities are aptly famous all over the world.
4.1.96
यदि दृश्येत सा साध्वी
यदि चेह वसेमहि ।
स्पृहयेयं न शक्राय
नायोध्यायै रघूत्तम ॥
O best among the Raghus! If only Seetā is found and if only we could live here, I would neither seek the position of Ṡakra nor think of Ayōdhyā.
4.1.97
नह्येवं रमणीयेषु
शाद्वलेषु तया सह ।
रमतो मे भवेच्चिन्ता
न स्पृहाऽन्येषु वा भवेत् ॥
For if I could play with her on such lovely swards as these, no cares could plague me, nor desire for aught else.
4.1.98
अमी हि विविधैः पुष्पैः
तरवो रुचिरच्छदाः ।
काननेऽस्मिन्विना कान्ताम्
चित्रमुत्पादयन्ति मे ॥
But these trees with their pleasant foliage and variegated flowers make me gloomy now that my lady-love is not with me.
4.1.99
पश्य शीतजलां चेमाम्
सौमित्रे पुष्करायुताम् ।
चक्रवाकानुचरिताम्
कारण्डव निषेविताम् ॥
Look at this lake, Sowmitri, with its cool waters covered with lotuses, frequented by Cakravākas and abounding in Kāraṇḍavas.
4.1.100
प्लवैः क्रौञ्चैश्च सम्पूर्णाम्
महामृगनिषेविताम् ।
अधिकं शोभते पम्पा
विकूजद्भिर्विहङ्गमैः ॥
Abounding in Plava and Krounca birds, frequented by many a deer, Paṃpā is extremely splendid, with the cooing of many birds.
4.1.101
दीपयन्तीव मे कामम्
विविधा मुदिता द्विजाः ।
श्यामां चन्द्रमुखीं स्मृत्वा
प्रियां पद्मनिभेक्षणाम् ॥
The many happy birds intensify my longing, by bringing to mind my beloved, the lovely one in the prime of youth with her moon-bright face and lotus eyes.
4.1.102
पश्य सानुषु चित्रेषु
मृगीभिस्सहितान्मृगान् ।
मां पुनर्मृगशाबाक्ष्या
वैदेह्या विरहीकृतम् ।
व्यथयन्तीव मे चित्तम्
सञ्चरन्त स्ततस्ततः ॥
Look at the stags that roam with their mates here and there on the beautiful hillside, making me, who is separated from Vaidēhee of the fawn eyes, feel terrible.
4.1.103
अस्मिन्सानुनि रम्ये हि
मत्तद्विजगणायुते ।
पश्येयं यदि तां कान्ताम्
तत स्स्वस्ति भवेन्मम ॥
How nice it would be if I can be with my beloved on these hillsides which are beautiful with flocks of amorous birds.
4.1.104
जीवेयं खलु सौमित्रे
मया सह सुमध्यमा ।
सेवते यदि वैदेही
पम्पायाः पवनं सुखम् ॥
I will be able to live, if Vaidēhee of lovely waist could enjoy the pleasant breeze of Paṃpā along with me.
4.1.105
पद्मसौगन्धिकवहम्
शिवं शोकविनाशनम् ।
धन्या लक्ष्मण सेवन्ते
पम्पोप वनमारुतम् ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! Those who enjoy the pleasant breeze of the woods of Paṃpā that carries the fragrance of Lotus and Saugandhika and banishes grief, are truly fortunate.
4.1.106
श्यामा पद्मपलाशाक्षी
प्रिया विरहिता मया ।
कथं धारयति प्राणान्
विवशा जनकात्मजा ॥
How can my beloved of lotus-petal eyes, the daughter of Janaka, who is in the prime of her youth manage to live separated from me, and having lost control on her own life?
4.1.107
किन्नु वक्ष्यामि राजानम्
धर्मज्ञं सत्यवादिनम् ।
सीताया जनकं पृष्टः
कुशलं जनसंसदि ॥
What can I tell Seetā’s father, King Janaka, a Dharmajña and a man of his word, when asked about her welfare, in the presence of everyone?
4.1.108
या मामनुगता मन्दम्
पित्रा प्रव्राजितं वनम् ।
सीता सत्पथमास्थाय
क्व नु सा वर्तते प्रिया ॥
Where is Seetā, my beloved, who, walking in the ways of the good, followed me, when I, being unlucky, was exiled by my father to the Vana?
4.1.109
तया विहीनः कृपणः
कथं लक्ष्मण धारये ।
या मामनुगता राज्यात्
भ्रष्टं विगतचेतसम् ॥
How can I manage, Lakshmaṇa, miserable from the loss of her who had followed me faithfully when I had lost the kingdom and was beside myself with grief?
4.1.110
तच्चार्वञ्चितपद्माक्षम्
सुगन्धि शुभमव्रणम् ।
अपश्यतो मुखं तस्याः
सीदतीव मतिर्मम ॥
My heart sinks, because I cannot see her face, with its beautiful lotus eyes, its fragrance and flawless beauty.
4.1.111
स्मितहास्यान्तरयुतम्
गुणवन्मधुरं हितम् ।
वैदेह्या वाक्यमतुलम्
कदा श्रोष्यामि लक्ष्मण ॥
When shall I hear, O Lakshmaṇa, Vaidēhee’s sweet, well-wishing and incomparable speech that is informed by good sense and punctuated only by smiles and fun.
4.1.112
प्राप्य दुखं वने श्यामा
मां मन्मथविकर्शितम् ।
नष्टदुःखेव हृष्टेव
साध्वी साध्वभ्यभाषत ॥
That good and young lady used to speak nicely when I longed for love, as if she was very cheerful and had got over the grief of having to come to Vana.
4.1.113
किं नु वक्ष्यामि कौसल्याम्
अयोध्यायां नृपात्मज ।
क्व सा स्नुषेति पृच्छन्तीं
कथं चापि तु मनस्विनीम् ॥
O prince! When I am back in Ayōdhyā, and Kausalya, who dotes upon her, asks me, ‘Where is my daughter-in-law, and how is she?’, what am I to tell her?
4.1.114
गच्छ लक्ष्मण पश्य त्वम्
भरतं भ्रातृवत्सलम् ।
न ह्यहं जीवितुं शक्तः
तामृते जनकात्मजाम् ॥
Lakshmaṇa, you go and see (and be with) Bharata who loves his brothers. For, I cannot live without Janaka’s daughter.
4.1.115
इति रामं महात्मानम्
विलपन्तमनाथवत् ।
उवाच लक्ष्मणो भ्राता
वचनं युक्तमव्ययम् ॥
When Rāma, the Mahātma, thus lamented like one forlorn, his brother Lakshmaṇa addressed him in this direct and appropriate language:
4.1.116
संस्तम्भ राम भद्रं ते
मा शुचः पुरुषोत्तम ।
नेदृशानां मतिर्मन्दा
भवत्यकलुषात्मनाम् ॥
O Rāma! O foremost of spirited beings! May all bode well for you! Steady yourself, you must not grieve! Wisdom shall not betray the likes of you, whose hearts are serene!
4.1.117
त्यज वियोगजं दुःखम्
स्मृत्वा स्नेहं प्रिये जने ।
अतिस्नेहपरिष्वङ्गात्
वर्तिरार्द्रापि दह्यते ॥
Think of your affection for the loved ones, and set aside the grief caused by separation; dipped in a great deal of oil, even the wick that is wet will light up.
4.1.118
यदि गच्छति पातालम्
ततोऽभ्यधिकमेव वा ।
सर्वथा रावणस्तावत्
न भविष्यति राघव ॥
O Rāghava! Rāvaṇa has no chance of survival, even if he goes to the nether world or further below.
4.1.119
प्रवृत्तिर्लभ्यतां तावत्
तस्य पापस्य रक्षसः ।
ततो हास्यति वा सीताम्
निधनं वा गमिष्यति ॥
Let us first get news of the sinful Rākshasa; the moment we do, he cannot but give up Seetā, or face death.
4.1.120
यदि यात्यदितेर्गर्भम्
रावणस्सह सीतया ।
तत्राप्येनं हनिष्यामि
न चेद्दास्यति मैथिलीम् ॥
Even if he were to get into Diti’s womb, taking Seetā with him, I will slay him, should he not return Maithili.
4.1.121
स्वास्थ्यं भद्रं भजस्वार्य
त्यज्यतां कृपणा मतिः ।
अर्थो हि नष्टकार्यार्थैः
नायत्नेनाधिगम्यते ॥
O noble one! Regain the composure and it will bode well (for you). Relinquish the thoughts of despondency. Those who suffered losses cannot accomplish their goal without redoubling the effort.
4.1.122
उत्साहो बलवानार्य
नास्त्युत्साहात्परं बलम् ।
सोत्साहस्यास्ति लोकेऽस्मिन्
न किञ्चिदपि दुर्लभम् ॥
O noble one! Motivation is strength. There is no strength better than motivation. For one who is motivated, nothing is impossible in this world.
4.1.123
उत्साहवन्तः पुरुषा
नावसीदन्ति कर्मसु ।
उत्साहमात्रमाश्रित्य
प्रतिलप्स्याम जानकीम् ॥
A motivated person will not wilt under the weight of the tasks. With motivation alone, we can recover Jānaki.
4.1.124
त्यज्यतां कामवृत्तत्वं
शोकं संन्यस्य पृष्ठतः ।
महात्मानं कृतात्मानम्
आत्मानं नावबुध्यसे ॥
Passion for love should be set aside. Grief should be left behind. You do not realize that you are a Mahātma, with complete control of yourself.
4.1.125
एवं सम्बोधितस्तेन
शोकोपहतचेतनः ।
त्यज्य शोकञ्च मोहञ्च
रामो धैर्यमुपागमत् ॥
Thus addressed by him, Rāma, whose mind had been overtaken by grief, got rid of the grief and confusion, and recovered his courage.
4.1.126
सोऽभ्यतिक्रामदव्यग्रः
तामचिन्त्यपराक्रमः ।
रामः पम्पां सुरुचिराम्
रम्यां पारिप्लवद्रुमाम् ॥
He, of unimaginable prowess, with the disturbance in his mind settled, then passed Paṃpā of beautiful trees that swayed with the breeze.
4.1.127
निरीक्षमाणस्सहसा महात्मा
सर्वं वनं निर्झरकन्दराश्च ।
उद्विग्नचेतास्सह लक्ष्मणेन
विचार्य दुःखोपहतः प्रतस्थे ॥
The Mahātma proceeded, discussing with Lakshmaṇa, looking everywhere in the forest, in the caves and in the vicinity of waterfalls, hit by grief and mind disturbed.
4.1.128
तं मत्तमातङ्गविलासगामी
गच्छन्तमव्यग्रमना महात्मा ।
स लक्ष्मणो राघवमप्रमत्तो
ररक्ष धर्मेण बलेन चैव ॥
And Lakshmaṇa, the Mahātma, whose stylish gait was like that of the elephant in rut, watched over Rāghava responsibly and vigilantly with all his strength and a steady mind.
4.1.129
तावृष्यमूकस्य समीपचारी
चरन्ददर्शाद्भुतदर्शनीयौ ।
शाखामृगाणामधिपस्तरस्वी
वितत्रसे नैव चिचेष्ट किञ्चित् ॥
(Meanwhile) the mighty lord of the tree-top dwellers (Sugreeva) who moved around in the vicinity of Ṛshyamūka mountain, saw the imposing duo and became scared. He stood still, completely motionless.
4.1.130
स तौ महात्मा गजमन्दगामी
शाखामृगस्तत्र चरञ्चरन्तौ ।
दृष्ट्वा विषादं परमं जगाम
चिन्तापरीतो भयभारमग्नः ॥
He, the Mahātma, the tree-top dweller, of leisurely gait of the elephant, was greatly worried upon seeing the duo and sank into absolute sadness, under the weight of fear.
4.1.131
तमाश्रमं पुण्यसुखं शरण्यं
सदैव शाखामृगसेवितान्तम् ।
त्रस्ताश्च दृष्ट्वा हरयोऽभिजग्मुः
महौजसौ राघवलक्ष्मणौ तौ ॥
And all the Vānaras, scared at the sight of the mighty powerful Rāghava and Lakshmaṇa, betook themselves to the Āṡrama, their sanctuary, which was comforting and holy and in whose interior Vānaras gather.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे प्रथमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the first Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
