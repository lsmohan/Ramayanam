Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 57
In this Sarga, dispirited on hearing the death of Akampana, Rāvaṇa broods for a while. He goes around Laṅkā and inspects the army, and realizes that the city is under siege and there is no other option except continuing the fight. He asks Prahasta to proceed against the Vānara army, and at the same time, also for his opinion.
Prahasta reminds him that the matter had been debated already and he had disagreed with his advise of returning Seetā. But he assures Rāvaṇa that he is ready to give up his life in the service of his master. Thus responding, Prahasta prepares himself and his army for the war, making oblations into the fire and getting the blessings of Brāhmaṇas. He sees bad omens as he starts the march, but continues ahead, discounting them.
6.57.1
अकम्पनवधं श्रुत्वा
क्रुद्धो वै राक्षसेश्वरः ।
किञ्चिद्दीनमुखश्चापि
सचिवांस्तानुदैक्षत ॥
On hearing about the death of Akampana, the lord of the Rākshasas was furious and looked at his ministers with his face somewhat downcast.
6.57.2
स तु ध्यात्वा मुहूर्तं तु
मन्त्रिभिः संविचार्य च ।
ततस्तु रावणः पूर्व -
दिवसे राक्षसाधिपः ।
पुरीं परिययौ लङ्काम्
सर्वान् गुल्मानवेक्षितुम् ॥
Having brooded for a while and discussing with his ministers, Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas went around the city of Laṅkā to inspect the army formations in those morning hours.
6.57.3
तां राक्षसगणैर्गुप्ताम्
गुल्मैर्बहुभिरावृताम् ।
ददर्श नगरीं लङ्काम्
पताकाध्वजमालिनीम् ॥
He saw the city with its rows of flags and buntings well guarded by companies of Rākshasas and filled with contingents of armies.
6.57.4
रुद्धां तु नगरीं दृष्ट्वा
रावणो राक्षसेश्वरः ।
उवाचात्महितम् काले
प्रहस्तं युद्धकोविदम् ॥
Finding the city under siege, Rāvaṇa, the lord of the Rākshasas, said to Prahasta, the seasoned warrior, of what would serve his purpose best at that time:
6.57.5
पुरस्योपनिविष्टस्य
सहसा पीडितस्य वा ।
नान्यं युद्धात् प्रपश्यामि
मोक्षं युद्धविशारद ॥
I can see no way of freeing the city, attacked and harried by the enemy, except by fighting, O expert warrior.
6.57.6
अहं वा कुम्भकर्णो वा
त्वं वा सेनापतिर्मम ।
इन्द्रजिद्वा निकुम्भो वा
वहेयुर्भारमीदृशम् ॥
Only myself, Kumbhakarṇa, you, my Commander-in-Chief, Indrajit and Nikumbha, are capable of undertaking this burden.
6.57.7
स त्वं बलमतः शीघ्रम्
आदाय परिगृह्य च ।
विजयायाभिनिर्याहि
यत्र सर्वे वनौकसः ॥
Therefore, quickly take the forces, and with them under your command, march forth for victory to where all the Vana-dwellers are.
6.57.8
निर्याणादेव ते नूनम्
चपला हरिवाहिनी ।
नर्दतां राक्षसेन्द्राणाम्
श्रुत्वा नादं द्रविष्यति ॥
The fickle Vānara forces will flee, for sure, the moment you start the march, just by hearing the roar of the great Rākshasas.
6.57.9
चपला ह्यविनीताश्च
चलचित्ताश्च वानराः ।
न सहिष्यन्ति ते नादम्
सिंहनादमिव द्विपाः ॥
The Vānaras are fickle, undisciplined and their minds lose focus easily. They cannot take the roar of the Rākshasas any more than elephants, the lion’s roar.
6.57.10
विद्रुते च बले तस्मिन्
रामः सौमित्रिणा सह ।
अवशस्ते निरालम्बः
प्रहस्त वशमेष्यति ॥
With them fleeing, and feeling helpless without their support, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa will fall into your hands, O Prahasta.
6.57.11
आपत् संशयिता श्रेयो
न तु निस्संशयीकृता ।
प्रतिलोमानुलोमं वा
यद्वा नो मन्यसे हितम् ॥
Uncertain danger is certainly better than a certain one. What do you think is in our favor and what is not? What is the best course for us?
6.57.12
रावणेनैवमुक्तस्तु
प्रहस्तो वाहिनीपतिः ।
राक्षसेन्द्रमुवाचेदम्
असुरेन्द्रमिवोशना ॥
Thus asked by Rāvaṇa, Prahasta, the Commander-in-Chief, said these words to the lord of Rākshasas, like Uṡanas would, to the lord of the Asuras.
6.57.13
राजन् मन्त्रितपूर्वं नः
कुशलैः सह मन्त्रिभिः ।
विवादश्चापि नो वृत्तः
समवेक्ष्य परस्परम् ॥
This matter was already debated by us, O King, in consultation with your able ministers, and it resulted in us disagreeing with each other.
6.57.14
प्रदानेन तु सीतायाः
श्रेयो व्यवसितं मया ।
अप्रदाने पुनर्युद्धम्
दृष्टमेतत्तथैव नः ॥
My contention was that it is better to give Seetā back. And we clearly knew that a standoff would be inevitable if she was not given back.
6.57.15
सोऽहं दानैश्च मानैश्च
सततं पूजितस्त्वया ।
सान्त्वैश्च विविधैः काले
किं न कुर्यां प्रियं तव ॥
Having been provided and honored by you all the while, and having heard kind words from you at various times, why would I not do what pleases you?
6.57.16
न हि मे जीवितं रक्ष्यम्
पुत्रदारधनानि वा ।
त्वं पश्य मां जुहूषन्तम्
त्वदर्थं जीवितं युधि ॥
I should not try to hang on to my life or to my children, or wife or wealth. You shall see me offering my life as an oblation in the fire of battle for your sake.
6.57.17
एवमुक्त्वा तु भर्तारम्
रावणं वाहिनीपतिः ।
उवाचेदं बलाध्यक्षान्
प्रहस्तः पुरतः स्थितान् ॥
Having thus answered his master Rāvaṇa, Prahasta, the Commander-in-Chief, told the adjutants who were present:
6.57.18
समानयत मे शीघ्रम्
राक्षसानां महद्बलम् ।
मद्बाणशतवेगेन
हतानां च रणाजिरे ।
अद्य तृप्यन्तु मांसादाः
पक्षिणः काननौकसाम् ॥
Assemble quickly, a huge Rākshasa force for me. Carrion-eating birds shall have a feast today on the battlefield off the carcasses of Vānaras slain by the hundreds of arrows I shall shoot with terrific force.
6.57.20
इत्युक्तास्ते प्रहस्तेन
बलाध्यक्षाः कृतत्वराः ।
बलमुद्योजयामासुः
तस्मिन् राक्षसमन्दिरे ॥
Thus told by Prahasta, those adjunct commanders made plans for assembling the forces right from that palace of the Rākshasa.
6.57.21
सा बभूव मुहूर्तेन
भीमौर्नानाविधायुधैः ।
लङ्का राक्षसवीरैस्तैः
गजैरिव समाकुला ॥
Within an hour, Laṅkā was brimming with valiant, dreaded and elephantine Rākshasas armed with every kind of weapon.
6.57.22
हुताशनं तर्पयताम्
ब्राह्मणांश्च नमस्यताम् ।
आज्यगन्धप्रतिवहः
सुरभिर्मारुतो ववौ ॥
The air was filled with the fragrance of the ghee oblations as oblations were offered into the fire and obeisance was made to Brāhmaṇas.
6.57.23
स्रजश्च विविधाकारा
जगृहुस्त्वभिमन्त्रिताः ।
सङ्ग्रामसज्जाः संहृष्टा
धारयन् राक्षसास्तदा ॥
The Rākshasas, ready for battle, took garlands of various shapes that were imbued with mystical powers, and put them on themselves with joy.
6.57.24
सधनुष्काः कवचिनो
वेगादाप्लुत्य राक्षसाः ।
रावणं प्रेक्ष्य राजानम्
प्रहस्तं पर्यवारयन् ॥
Armed with bow and clad in armor, the Rākshasas sprang up immediately, and after bowing to the king Rāvaṇa, they gathered around Prahasta.
6.57.25
अथामन्त्र्य च राजानम्
भेरीमाहत्य भैरवाम् ।
आरुरोह रथं दिव्यम्
प्रहस्तः सज्जकल्पितम् ॥
After conferring with the king and having the war drum beaten, Prahasta got into the splendid chariot that was made ready.
6.57.26
हयैर्महाजवैर्युक्तम्
सम्यक् सूतसुसंयतम् ।
महाजलदनिर्घोषम्
साक्षाच्चन्द्रार्कभास्वरम् ॥
It was yoked with swift horses and was driven by a capable charioteer. It rumbled like a huge cloud and gleamed with the glow of the sun and the moon.
6.57.27
उरगध्वजदुर्द्धर्षम्
सुवरूथं स्वपस्करम् ।
सुवर्णजालसंयुक्तम्
प्रहसन्तमिव श्रिया ॥
With a serpent for an emblem on its intimidating flag post, with its stout fenders and excellent wheels, and adorned with a meshwork of gold, it seemed to be cheerfully opulent.
6.57.28
ततस्तं रथमास्थाय
रावणार्पितशासनः ।
लङ्काया निर्ययौ तूर्णम्
बलेन महता वृतः ॥
Having received the orders from Rāvaṇa, he got into the chariot and went forth from Laṅkā quickly, followed by a great army.
6.57.29
ततो दुन्दुभिनिर्घोषः
पर्जन्यनिनदोपमः ।
वादित्राणां च निनदः
पूरयन्निव मेदिनीम् ।
शुश्रुवे शङ्खशब्दश्च
प्रयाते वाहिनीपतौ ॥
As the Commander-in-Chief marched on, there was heard the booming of kettle drums that resembled the rumble of clouds and the sounds of various instruments and the blare of the conches that seemed to fill the earth.
6.57.30
निनदन्तः स्वरान् घोरान्
राक्षसा जग्मुरग्रतः ।
भीमरूपा महाकायाः
प्रहस्तस्य पुरस्सराः ॥
Letting off terrible shouts, Rākshasas with huge bodies and frightful appearance, marched ahead of Prahasta, leading the van.
6.57.31
नरान्तकः कुम्भहनुः
महानादः समुन्नतः ।
प्रहस्तसचिवा ह्येते
निर्ययुः परिवार्य तम् ॥
Prahasta’s advisers, Narāntaka, Kumbhahanu, Mahānāda and Samunnata, marched along with him.
6.57.32
व्यूढेनैव सुघोरेण
पूर्वद्वारात् स निर्ययौ ।
गजयूथनिकाशेन
बलेन महता वृतः ॥
He marched forth through the eastern entrance with a huge force surrounding him in an intimidating formation, looking like a great herd of elephants.
6.57.33
सागरप्रतिमौघेन
वृतस्तेन बलेन सः ।
प्रहस्तो निर्ययौ क्रुद्धः
कालान्तकयमोपमः ॥
With that army that was vast like an ocean following him, Prahasta, in his wrath, marched forth, like Yama set to destroy the worlds.
6.57.34
तस्य निर्याणघोषेण
राक्षसानां च नर्दताम् ।
लङ्कायां सर्वभूतानि
विनेदुर्विकृतैः स्वरैः ॥
On hearing the din of his march and the shouts of the Rākshasas, all creatures in Laṅkā wailed in jarring notes.
6.57.35
व्यभ्रमाकाशमाविश्य
मांसशोणितभोजनाः ।
मण्डलान्यपसव्यानि
खगाश्चक्रू रथं प्रति ॥
Birds that feed on flesh and blood made circles in the clear sky in an anticlockwise direction over the chariot.
6.57.36
वमन्त्यः पावकज्वालाः
शिवा घोरं ववाशिरे ।
अन्तरिक्षात् पपातोल्का
वायुश्च परुषो ववौ ॥
Jackals howled hideously, spewing blazing fire. A meteor fell from the sky, and the wind blew with a dreaded touch.
6.57.37
अन्योन्यमभिसंरब्धा
ग्रहाश्च न चकाशिरे ।
मेघाश्च खरनिर्घोषा
रथस्योपरि रक्षसः ।
ववृषू रुधिरं चास्य
सिषिचुश्च पुरस्सरान् ॥
At odds with each other, the planets lost their luster. Rumbling raucously, clouds showered blood on the chariot of the Rākshasa drenching the Rākshasas that led the van.
6.57.39
केतुमूर्धनि गृध्रोऽस्य
निलीनो दक्षिणामुखः ।
तुदन्नुभयतः पार्श्वम्
समग्रामहरत् प्रभाम् ॥
A vulture sat on the top of his flagstaff facing south, pressing its feathers on both sides, robbing his wonted cheerfulness.
6.57.40
सारथेर्बहुशश्चास्य
सङ्ग्राममवगाहतः ।
प्रतोदो न्यपतद्धस्तात्
सूतस्य हयसादिनः ॥
The whip slipped multiple times from the hand of the charioteer who came from a lineage of charioteers, as he drove into the battlefield whipping the horses.
6.57.41
निर्याणश्रीश्च याऽस्यासीत्
भास्वरा च सुदुर्लभा ।
सा ननाश मुहूर्तेन
समे च स्खलिता हयाः ॥
The glory and pomp that is associated with the march for a war, had been lost in less than an hour with the horses stumbling on level ground.
6.57.42
प्रहस्तं त्वभिनिर्यान्तम्
प्रख्यातबलपौरुषम् ।
युधि नानाप्रहरणा
कपिसेनाऽभ्यवर्तत ॥
Having thus marched into battle, Prahasta, who was celebrated for his strength and high self esteem, met with the Vānara army armed with a variety of weapons.
6.57.43
अथ घोषः सुतुमुलो
हरीणां समजायत ।
वृक्षानारुजतां चैव
गुर्वीरागृह्णतां शिलाः ॥
Then, there arose a tumultuous din as the Vānaras uprooted trees and snatched heavy rocks.
6.57.44
नदतां राक्षसानां च
वानराणां च गर्जताम् ।
उभे प्रमुदिते सैन्ये
रक्षोगणवनौकसाम् ॥
With the Rākshasas shouting and the Vānaras roaring, the armies of both the Rākshasas and the Vana-dwellers were in high spirits.
6.57.45
वेगितानां समर्थानाम्
अन्योन्यवधकाङ्क्षिणाम् ।
परस्परं चाह्वयताम्
निनादः श्रूयते महान् ॥
A great din was heard as those fast and able warriors challenged each other bent on slaying each other.
6.57.46
ततः प्रहस्तः कपिराजवाहिनीम्
अभिप्रतस्थे विजयाय दुर्मतिः ।
विवृद्धवेगां च विवेश तां चमूं
यथा मुमूर्षुः शलभो विभावसुम् ॥
The foolish Prahasta advanced against the army of the Vānara King hoping to vanquish it; he gained additional speed as he penetrated that army as the moth courting death does when entering the fire.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे सप्तपञ्चाशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty seventh Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
