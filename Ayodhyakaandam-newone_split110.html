Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 110
In this Sarga, Vasishṭha urges Rāma one last time to get crowned and not break the age old tradition of his long lineage, starting from Ikshwāku all the way down to Daṡaratha, of the eldest becoming the king.
2.110.1
क्रुद्धमाज्ञाय रामं तु
वसिष्ठः प्रत्युवाच ह ।
जाबालिरपि जानीते
लोकस्यास्य गतागतिम् ।
निवर्तयितुकामस्तु
त्वामेतद्वाक्यमुक्तवान् ।
इमां लोकसमुत्पत्तिं
लोकनाथ निबोध मे ॥
Sensing that Rāma was irate, Vasishṭha responded: Jābāli is aware of the ways of this world. He said what he said only in the hope of dissuading you from your resolve. Let me put in perspective for you, the emergence of this world, O lord of the worlds!
2.110.3
सर्वं सलिलमेवासीत्
पृथिवी यत्र निर्मिता ।
ततस्समभवद्ब्रह्मा
स्वयम्भूर्दैवतैस्सह ॥
Once upon a time there was nothing but water, from which was built this earth. There emerged Brahma on his own along with Dēvatas.
2.110.4
स वराहस्ततो भूत्वा
प्रोज्जहार वसुन्धराम् ।
असृजच्च जगत्सर्वं
सह पुत्रैः कृतात्मभिः ॥
Then he took the form of a boar and lifted the earth (out of water). Then he created this world along with his sons, who were thus charged to.
2.110.5
आकाशप्रभवो ब्रह्मा
शाश्वतो नित्य अव्ययः ।
तस्मान्मरीचिस्सञ्जज्ञे
मरीचेः कश्यपस्सुतः ॥
The eternal, permanent and imperishable Brahma emerged from nothingness. From him was born Mareeci. Mareeci's son is Kāṡyapa.

2.110.6
विवस्वान्काश्यपाज्जज्ञे
मनुर्वैवस्वतस्सुतः ।
मनुः प्रजापतिः पूर्वम्
इक्ष्वाकुस्तु मनोस्सुतः ॥
From Kāṡyapa was born Vivaswa. Manu was the son of Vivaswa. Manu was a Prajāpati, a progenitor. Ikshwāku was the son of Manu.
2.110.7
यस्येयं प्रथमं दत्ता
समृद्धा मनुना मही ।
तमिक्ष्वाकुमयोध्यायां
राजानं विद्धि पूर्वकम् ॥
Know that that Ikshwāku is the first king of Ayōdhyā, to whom the abundant and prosperous earth was given first by Manu.
2.110.8
इक्ष्वाकोस्तु सुत श्रीमान्
कुक्षिरेवेति विश्रुतः ।
कुक्षेरथात्मजो वीरो
विकुक्षिरुदपद्यत ॥
The richly endowed son of Ikshwāku was well known by the name Kukshi. The well-endowed Vikukshi was born as the son of Kukshi.
2.110.9
विकुक्षेस्तु महातेजा
बाणः पुत्र प्रतापवान् ।
बाणस्य तु महाबाहुः
अनरण्यो महायशाः ॥
Vikukshi's son was the supremely radiant and valiant Bāṇa. Bāṇa's son was the renowned and mighty-armed Anaraṇya.
2.110.10
नानावृष्टिर्बभूवास्मिन्
नदुर्भिक्षं सतां वरे ।
अनरण्ये महाराजे
तस्करो नापि कश्चन ॥
Under the great rule of Anaraṇya, the foremost among good men, there was never a drought or famine and there was no single thief for the count.
2.110.11
अनरण्यान्महाबाहुः
पृथु राजा बभूव ह ।
तस्मात्पृथोमहाराजः
त्रिशङ्कुरुदपद्यत ।
स सत्यवचनाध्वीरः
सशरीरो दिवं गतः ॥
From Anaraṇya was born the king Pṛthu of mighty arm. From that Pṛthu was born the great king Triṡaṅku. That valiant one, by virtue of his truthfulness ascended to Heaven with mortal body.
2.110.12
त्रिशङ्कोरभवत्सूनुः
दुन्धुमारो महायशाः ।
दुन्धुमारान्महातेजा
युवनाश्वो व्यजायत ॥
The supremely renowned Dundhumāra was born as the son of Triṡaṅku. From Dundhumāra was born the supremely radiant Yuvanāṡwa.
2.110.13
युवनाश्वसुतश्श्रीमान्
मान्धाता समपद्यत ।
मान्धातुस्तु महातेजाः
सुसन्धिरुदपद्यत ।
सुसन्धेरपि पुत्रौ द्वौ
ध्रुवसन्धिः प्रसेनजित् ।
यशस्वी ध्रुवसन्धेस्तु
भरतो रिपुसूदनः ॥
Richly endowed Māndhāta was born as the son of Yuvanāṡwa. From Māndhāta was born the supremely radiant Susandhi. Susandhi had two sons, Dhruvasandhi and Prasēnajit. The renowned Bharata, the slayer of enemies, was born to Dhruvasandhi.
2.110.15
भरतात्तु महाबाहोः
असितो नाम जायत ।
यस्यैते प्रतिराजान
उदपद्यन्त शत्रवः ।
हैहयास्तालजङ्घाश्च
शूराश्च शशिबिन्दवः ॥
From Bharata was born the son known as Asita of mighty arm. His rivals, the Haihayas, the Tālajaṅghas, the Ṡūras and the Ṡaṡibindus became his enemies.
2.110.17
तांस्तु सर्वान्प्रतिव्यूह्य
युद्धे राजा प्रवासितः ।
स च शैलवरे रम्ये
बभूवाभिरतो मुनिः ॥
The King had to flee the country, after encountering all of them in battle. Then he became a Muni relishing the beauty of the best of mountains.
2.110.18
द्वे चास्य भार्ये गर्भिण्यौ
बभूवतुरिति श्रुतिः ।
एका गर्भविनाशाय
सपत्न्यै गरलं ददौ ॥
It is said that he had two wives, who became pregnant. One of them poisoned her co-wife so that she would have a miscarriage.
2.110.19
भार्गवश्च्यवनो नाम
हिमवन्तमुपाश्रितः ।
तमृषिं समुपागम्य
कालिन्दी त्वभ्यवादयत् ।
स तामभ्यवदद्विप्रो
वरेप्सुं पुत्रजन्मनि ॥
Cyavana from the lineage of Bhṛgu was living by that (mountain) Himavān. Kālindi approached that Ṛshi and prostrated. That Brāhmaṇa then said to her, who was wishing for a boon to have a son:
2.110.20
पुत्रस्ते भविता देवि
महात्मा लोकविश्रुतः ।
धार्मिकश्च सुशीलश्च
वंशकर्ताऽरिसूदनः ॥
O queen, you will certainly have a son, who will be a Mahātma and Dhārmika and he will be well known in the world. He will be a man of character and a slayer of enemies. He will enhance the name of the lineage.
2.110.21
कृत्वा प्रदक्षिणं हृष्टा
मुनिंतमनुमान्य च ।
पद्मपत्रसमानाक्षं
पद्मगर्भसमप्रभम् ।
ततस्सा गृहमागम्य
देवी पुत्रं व्यजायत ॥
Delighted, she circumambulated and took leave of the Muni and went home. The queen then gave birth to a son whose eyes rivaled lotus petals, and whose radiance rivaled that of Brahma.
2.110.23
सपत्न्या तु गरस्तस्यै
दत्तो गर्भजिघांसया ।
गरेण सह तेनैव
जातस्स सगरोऽभवत् ॥
As he was born along with the poison that was given by his step mother to destroy the fetus, he came to be known as Sagara.
Sagara = Sa + Gara = along with Gara, poison.
2.110.24
स राजा सगरो नाम
यस्समुद्रमखानयत् ।
इष्ट्वा पर्वणि वेगेन
त्रासयन्तमिमाः प्रजाः ॥
He is the king Sagara who was known for performing a Yajña and dredging the ocean that made people panic with the tempo of its swell on full moon days.
2.110.25
असमञ्जस्तु पुत्रोऽभूत्
सगरस्येति न श्श्रुतम् ।
जीवन्नेव स पित्रा तु
निरस्तः पापकर्मकृत् ॥
Then we hear that Asamañja happened to be the son of Sagara. He, of sinful deeds, was abandoned by his father while he was still alive.
2.110.26
अंशुमानिति पुत्रोऽभूत्
असमञ्जस्य वीर्यवान् ।
दिलीपोंशुमतः पुत्रो
दिलीपस्य भगीरथः ॥
A son known as Aṃṡumān, of great prowess, was born to Asamañja. Dileepa was the son of Aṃṡumān, and Bhageeratha was the son of Dileepa.
2.110.27
भगीरथात्ककुत्स्थस्तु
काकुत्स्था येन विश्रुताः ।
ककुत्स्थस्य च पुत्रोऽभूत्
रघुर्येन च राघवाः ॥
Bhageeratha's son was Kakutstha because of whom (his descendants) came to be well known as Kākutsthās. Raghu happened to be the son of Kakutstha because of whom (his descendants) came to be known as Rāghavas.
2.110.28
रघोस्तु पुत्रस्तेजस्वी
प्रवृद्धः पुरुषादकः ।
कल्माषपादस्सौदास
इत्येवं प्रथितो भुवि ॥
The radiant son of Raghu grew up to be famously known in this world as Purushādaka, Kalmāshapāda and Sowdāsa.
2.110.29
कल्माषपादपुत्रोऽभूत्
शङ्खणस्त्विति विश्रुतः ।
यस्तु तद्वीर्यमासाद्य
सहसैन्यो व्यनीनशत् ॥
Kalmāshapāda had a son known well as Ṡaṅkhaṇa, who, pitted against his (Kalmāshapāda's) might, was destroyed along with his army.
2.110.30
शङ्खणस्य च पुत्रोऽभूत्
शूर श्रीमान्सुदर्शनः ।
सुदर्शनस्याग्निवर्णः
अग्निवर्णस्य शीघ्रगः ॥
Ṡaṅkhaṇa's son was the valiant and gracious Sudarṡana. Sudarṡana's son was Agnivarṇa; and Agnivarṇa's son, Ṡeeghraga.
2.110.31
शीघ्रगस्य मरुः पुत्रो
मरोः पुत्रः प्रशुश्रुवः ।
प्रशुश्रुवस्य पुत्रोभूत्
अम्बरीषो महाद्युतिः ॥
Ṡeeghraga's son was Maru, and Maru's son Praṡuṡruva. Praṡuṡruva's son was the resplendent Ambareesha.
2.110.32
अम्बरीषस्य पुत्रोभूत्
नहुषः सत्यविक्रमः ।
नहुषस्य च नाभागः
पुत्रः परमधार्मिकः ॥
Ambareesha's son was Nahusha, a fierce man of his word. Nahusha's son was Nābhāga, an absolute Dhārmika.
For some reason, Yayāti, who was mentioned as the son of Nahusha and the father of Nābhāga in 1.70.42 is missing here.
2.110.33
अजश्च सुव्रतश्चैव
नाभागस्य सुतावुभौ ।
अजस्यैव च धर्मात्मा
राजा दशरथस्सुतः ॥
Nābhāga had two sons, Aja and Suvrata. And Aja's son was the Dharmātma King Daṡaratha.
2.110.34
तस्य ज्येष्ठोऽसि दायादो
राम इत्यभिविश्रुतः ।
तद्गृहाण स्वकं राज्यम्
अवेक्षस्व जनं नृप ॥
You, known to fame as Rāma, his eldest son, are entitled to the inheritance. Hence, accept your very own kingdom and please look after all the people, O King!
2.110.35
इक्ष्वाकूणां हि सर्वेषां
राजा भवति पूर्वजः ।
पूर्वजे नापरः पुत्रो
ज्येष्ठो राज्येऽभिषिच्यते ॥
Only the first born became the king, O king, among all the Ikshwākus! Others would not, when the eldest is alive. Only the eldest gets crowned.
2.110.36
स राघवाणां कुलधर्ममात्मनः
सनातनं नाद्य विहन्तुमर्हसि ।
प्रभूतरत्नामनुशाधि मेदिनीं
प्रभूतराष्ट्रां पितृवन्महायशः ॥
You shall not break now that age old family tradition of the Rāghavas. Rule this kingdom of immense wealth and territory, O renowned one, like your father!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे दशोत्तरशततमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and tenth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
