Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 86
In this Sarga, Vibheeshaṇa advises Lakshmaṇa that he should first try to tear apart the Rākshasa forces, which would force Indrajit to come out. Lakshmaṇa, accordingly, leads the Vānara forces to assault the Rākshasa forces. There ensues a tumultuous battle between the two forces.
On hearing that the morale of his army is weakened, Indrajit comes out without completing the rite. Meanwhile, Hanumān, hurling huge trees, wreaks havoc on the Rākshasa forces. Then the Rākshasas attack Hanumān from all sides, striking him with every kind of weapon. Hanumān puts up a stiff fight with them. Indrajit then takes on Hanumān, showering arrows and other weapons.
Meanwhile Vibheeshaṇa alerts Lakshmaṇa, saying that Indrajit is aiming to kill Hanumān and Lakshmaṇa should intervene and kill Indrajit.
6.86.1
अथ तस्यामवस्थायाम्
लक्ष्मणं रावणानुजः ।
परेषामहितं वाक्यम्
अर्थसाधकमब्रवीत् ॥
The situation being what it was, Rāvaṇa’s younger brother said these words to Lakshmaṇa that would help in accomplishing their mission and in thwarting the interests of the enemy.
6.86.2
यदेतद्राक्षसानीकम्
मेघश्यामं विलोक्यते ।
एतदायोध्यतां शीघ्रम्
कपिभिः पादपायुधैः ॥
Let the Vānaras, armed with trees, engage in a fight without delay with that huge Rākshasa force which looks like a black cloud, from here.
6.86.3
अस्यानीकस्य महतो
भेदने यत लक्ष्मण ।
राक्षसेन्द्रसुतोऽप्यत्र
भिन्ने दृश्यो भविष्यति ॥
Try, O Lakshmaṇa, to tear apart this mighty force, with the splitting of which the son of the Rākshasa King can be seen (coming out).
6.86.4
स त्वमिन्द्राशनिप्रख्यैः
शरैरवकिरन् परान् ।
अभिद्रवाशु यावद्वै
नैतत् कर्म समाप्यते ॥
Charge ahead before the rite is completed spraying your arrows, that are powerful like the thunderbolt of Indra, on the enemy.
6.86.5
जहि वीर दुरात्मानम्
मायापरमधार्मिकम् ।
रावणिं क्रूरकर्माणम्
सर्वलोकभयावहम् ॥
And kill, O Veera, this evil-minded one, the son of Rāvaṇa, who has no sense of Dharma, who terrifies all the worlds with his cruel deeds, relying on his magical powers.
6.86.6
विभीषणवचः श्रुत्वा
लक्ष्मणः शुभलक्षणः ।
ववर्ष शरवर्षाणि
राक्षसेन्द्रसुतं प्रति ॥
Hearing those words of Vibheeshaṇa, Lakshmaṇa of auspicious marks, sent a downpour of arrows on that son of the Rākshasa King.
6.86.7
ऋक्षाः शाखामृगाश्चापि
द्रुमाद्रिनखयोधिनः ।
अभ्यधावन्त सहिताः
तदनीकमवस्थितम् ॥
The Ṛkshas and the Vānaras together, wielding trees, rocks and their own claws for weapons, dashed towards that army which was ready to meet them.
6.86.8
राक्षसाश्च शितैर्बाणैः
असिभिः शक्तितोमरैः ।
अभ्यवर्तन्त समरे
कपिसैन्यजिघांसवः ॥
The Rākshasas too, armed with sharp arrows, swords, Ṡaktis and Tōmaras, confronted them in that fight, bent on destroying that Vānara army.
6.86.9
स सम्प्रहारस्तुमुलः
सञ्जज्ञे कपिराक्षसाम् ।
शब्देन महता लङ्काम्
नादयन् वै समन्ततः ॥
Then there ensued a tumultuous battle between Vānaras and Rākshasas, the mighty din of which reverberated all over Laṅkā.
6.86.10
शस्त्रैश्च बहुधाकारैः
शितैर्बाणैश्च पादपैः ।
उद्यतैर्गिरिशृङ्गैश्च
घोरैराकाशमावृतम् ॥
The sky was covered with formidable weapons of various shapes, with sharp arrows, and with trees and mountain crests that were held up.
6.86.11
ते राक्षसा वानरेषु
विकृताननबाहवः ।
निवेशयन्तः शस्त्राणि
चक्रुस्ते सुमहद्भयम् ॥
The Rākshasas of twisted faces and arms created terror in the Vānaras, hurling weapons at them.
6.86.12
तथैव सकलैर्वृक्षैः
गिरिशृङ्गैश्च वानराः ।
अभिजघ्नुर्निजघ्नुश्च
समरे राक्षसर्षभान् ॥
Similarly, the Vānaras struck the bulls among Rākshasas in that fight with all kinds of trees and mountain crests and killed them.
6.86.13
ऋक्षवानरमुख्यैश्च
महाकायैर्महाबलैः ।
रक्षसां वध्यमानानाम्
महद्भयमजायत ॥
The Rākshasas were terrified as they fought with the bear and Vānara chiefs of huge bodies and immense strength.
6.86.14
स्वमनीकं विषण्णं तु
श्रुत्वा शत्रुभिरार्दितम् ।
उदतिष्ठत दुर्धर्षः
तत्कर्मण्यननुष्ठिते ॥
On hearing that his army was downcast, harrowed by the enemy, the unassailable one had to get up without completing the rite.
6.86.15
वृक्षान्धकारान्निर्गत्य
जातक्रोधः स रावणिः ।
आरुरोह रथं सज्जम्
पूर्वयुक्तं सुसंयुतम् ॥
Then the outraged son of Rāvaṇa emerged from the darkness of the groves and got into the chariot that was ready, yoked and fitted with everything.
6.86.16
स भीमकार्मुकधरः
कालमेघसमप्रभः ।
रक्तास्यनयनः क्रुद्धो
बभौ मृत्युरिवान्तकः ॥
Armed with a terrifying bow and his eyes and face red with anger, he, who shone like a black cloud, resembled Death itself that puts an end to all.
6.86.17
दृष्ट्वैव तु रथस्थं तम्
पर्यवर्तत तद्बलम् ।
रक्षसां भीमवेगानाम्
लक्ष्मणेन युयुत्सताम् ॥
As soon as they saw him on the chariot, the Rākshasa army of terrific speed turned back again to fight Lakshmaṇa.
6.86.18
तस्मिन् काले तु हनुमान्
अद्यम्य सुदुरासदम् ।
धरणीधरसङ्काशो
महावृक्षमरिन्दमः ।
स राक्षसानां तत्सैन्यम्
कालाग्निरिव निर्दहन् ।
चकार बहुभिर्वृक्षैः
निस्सञ्ज्ञं युधि वानरः ॥
At about the same time, Hanumān, subduer of enemies, who was huge like a mountain, lifted up a huge tree that none could hope to resist, and destroyed that army as if he were the fire that consumes the worlds at the end of Time. Striking with many more trees, that Vānara rendered the foe fall senseless in that fight.
6.86.20
विध्वंसयन्तं तरसा
दृष्ट्वैव पवनात्मजम् ।
राक्षसानां सहस्राणि
हनुमन्तमवाकिरन् ॥
When they saw the son of Vāyu wreaking havoc on their forces, thousands of Rākshasas attacked him hurling their weapons at him.
6.86.21
शितशूलधराः शूलैः
असिभिश्चासिपाणयः ।
शक्तिभिः शक्तिहस्ताश्च
पट्टिशैः पट्टिशायुधाः ।
परिधैश्च गदाभिश्च
कुन्तैश्च शुभदर्शनैः ।
शतशश्च शतघ्नीभिः
आयसैरपि मुद्गरैः ।
घौरैः परश्वधैश्चैव
भिन्दिपालैश्च राक्षसाः ।
मुष्टिभिर्वज्रकल्पैश्च
तलैरशनिसन्निभैः ।
अभिजघ्नुः समासाद्य
समन्तात् पर्वतोपमम् ।
तेषामपि च सङ्क्रुद्धः
चकार कदनं महत् ॥
From every side, the Rākshasas attacked Hanumān, who was huge like a mountain.
Some wielding sharp spears struck him with spears. Those wielding swords struck him with swords. Those wielding Ṡaktis struck him with Ṡaktis. Others wielding Paṭṭiṡas struck him with Paṭṭiṡas.
Yet others struck him with Parighas, maces, Bhindipālas, splendid-looking Kuntas, hundreds of Ṡataghnis, Mudgaras made with iron and terrible battle-axes.
Yet others struck him with their fists that fell with the impact of Vajra and with their palms, that felt like thunderbolts.
And he too, in wrath, put up a fierce fight with them.
6.86.25
स ददर्श कपिश्रेष्ठम्
अचलोपममिन्द्रजित् ।
सूदमानमसंत्रस्तम्
अमित्रान् पवनात्मजम् ॥
Indrajit then beheld the son of Vāyu, the foremost of Vānaras, who was unshakable like a mountain, and was slaughtering the enemy forces with no fear whatsoever.
6.86.26
स सारथिमुवाचेदम्
याहि यत्रैष वानरः ।
क्षयमेष हि नः कुर्यात्
राक्षसानामुपेक्षितः ॥
Then he said to the charioteer, 'Go where that Vānara is; He will destroy all of us, the Rākshasas, if we let him alone'.
6.86.27
इत्युक्तः सारथिस्तेन
ययौ यत्र स मारुतिः ।
वहन् परमदुर्धर्षम्
स्थितमिन्द्रजितं रथे ॥
Thus ordered, the charioteer drove the indomitable Indrajit, seated in his chariot, to the place where Māruti was.
6.86.28
सोऽभ्युपेत्य शरान् खड्गान्
पट्टिशांश्च परश्वधान् ।
अभ्यवर्षत दुर्द्धर्षः
कपिमूर्ध्नि स राक्षसः ॥
Having arrived there, the invincible Rākshasa rained arrows, swords, Paṭṭiṡas and battle-axes upon the head of the Vānara.
6.86.29
तानि शस्त्राणि घोराणि
प्रतिगृह्य स मारुतिः ।
रोषेण महताऽऽविष्टो
वाक्यं चेदमुवाच ह ॥
Taking those dread weapons in his stride, Māruti, in a towering rage, said these words:
6.86.30
युद्ध्यस्व यदि शूरोऽसि
रावणात्मज दुर्मते ।
वायुपुत्रं समासाद्य
जीवन्न प्रतियास्यसि ॥
Come and fight with me directly if you are a warrior worth the name, O wicked son of Rāvaṇa! Having come to the son of Vāyu, you will not go back alive.
6.86.31
बाहुभ्यां प्रतियुध्यस्व
यदि मे द्वन्द्वमाहवे ।
वेगं सहस्व दुर्बुद्धे
ततस्त्वं रक्षसां वरः ॥
If you are game for a duel, come and fight me with your bare hands. If you can withstand my onslaught, then you can indeed be counted as the best of the Rākshasas!
6.86.32
हनुमन्तं जिघांसन्तम्
समुद्यतशरासनम् ।
रावणात्मजमाचष्टे
लक्ष्मणाय विभीषणः ॥
As Rāvaṇa’s son was raising his bow to slay Hanumān, Vibheeshaṇa pointed him out to Lakshmaṇa, saying:
6.86.33
यः स वासवनिर्जेता
रावणस्यात्मसम्भवः ।
स एष रथमास्थाय
हनुमन्तं जिघांसति ॥
Seated in his chariot, this son of Rāvaṇa who vanquished Indra, is aiming to slay Hanumān.
6.86.34
तमप्रतिमसंस्थानैः
शरैः शत्रुविदारणैः ।
जीवितान्तकरैर्घोरैः
सौमित्रे रावणिं जहि ॥
O Sowmitri! Kill that son of Rāvaṇa with your fierce arrows that are unequaled in shape and size, which tear apart the vitals of the enemy and take out their lives!
6.86.35
इत्येवमुक्तस्तु तदा महात्मा
विभीषणेनारिविभीषणेन ।
ददर्श तं पर्वतसन्निकाशं
रथस्थितं भीमबलं नदन्तम् ॥
Thus told by Vibheeshaṇa, the terror of his foes, the mighty Lakshmaṇa beheld him (Indrajit) of formidable strength, who looked like a mountain and was roaring from his chariot.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे षडशीतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighty sixth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
