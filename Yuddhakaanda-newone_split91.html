Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 91
In this Sarga, Lakshmaṇa, Hanumān, Indrajit, Jāmbavān and the chiefs of the Vānara army go to Rāma and tell him that Indrajit had been slain by Lakshmaṇa. Rāma is extremely happy and applauds Lakshmaṇa, saying that with the slaying of Indrajit, who is like Rāvaṇa's right hand, the battle is all but won, and that he would slay Rāvaṇa once he comes out with all his forces.
He instructs Sushēṇa to treat Lakshmaṇa’s, Vibheeshaṇa’s and all others’ wounds. Sushēṇa heals everyone with powerful herbs. Everyone is happy, relieved of their wounds and pains.
6.91.1
रुधिरक्लिन्नगात्रस्तु
लक्ष्मणः शुभलक्षणः ।
बभूव हृष्टस्तं हत्वा
शक्रजेतारमाहवे ॥
Even as his body was drenched in blood, Lakshmaṇa of auspicious marks rejoiced, having slain that vanquisher of Indra.
6.91.2
ततः स जाम्बवन्तं च
हनुमन्तं च वीर्यवान् ।
संनिपत्य महातेजाः
तांश्च सर्वान् वनौकसः ।
आजगाम ततःशीघ्रम्
यत्र सुग्रीवराघवौ ।
विभीषणमवष्टभ्य
हनूमन्तं च लक्ष्मणः ॥
Then, offering his salutations to Jāmbavān, Hanumān, and all the Vānaras, the valiant Lakshmaṇa of extraordinary power, flanked by Hanumān and Vibheeshaṇa, went quickly to where Rāghava was.
6.91.4
ततो राममभिक्रम्य
सौमित्रिरभिवाद्य च ।
तस्थौ भ्रातृसमीपस्थः
शक्रस्येन्द्रानुजो यथा ॥
Reaching Rāma, Lakshmaṇa saluted him and stood by his brother, looking like Indra's younger brother (Upēndra) standing next to Indra.
6.91.5
निष्टनन्निव चागम्य
राघवाय महात्मने ।
आचचक्षे तदा वीरो
घोरमिन्द्रजितो वधम् ॥
Then that Veera reported, in a mellowed voice, to Mahātma Rāghava about the slaying of Indrajit in that fierce fight.
6.91.6
रावणेस्तु शिरश्छिन्नम्
लक्ष्मणेन महात्मना ।
न्यवेदयत रामाय
तदा हृष्टो विभीषणः ॥
Vibheeshaṇa also, with great joy, reported to Rāma about the severing of the head of Rāvaṇa's son by Mahātma Lakshmaṇa.
6.91.7
श्रुत्वैव तु महावीर्यो
लक्ष्मणेनेन्द्रजिद्वधम् ।
प्रहर्षमतुलं लेभे
रामो वाक्यमुवाच ह ॥
On hearing of the slaying of Indrajit by Lakshmaṇa, Rāma of extraordinary valor, said these words, feeling extremely happy:
6.91.8
साधु लक्ष्मण तुष्टोऽस्मि
कर्मणा सुकृतं कृतम् ।
रावणेर्हि विनाशेन
जितमित्युपधारय ॥
Well done Lakshmaṇa! I am very happy with the mission that has been so well accomplished! Consider that we have all but won, with the destruction of Rāvaṇa's son!
6.91.9
स तं शिरस्युपाघ्राय
लक्ष्मणं कीर्तिवर्धनम् ।
लज्जमानं बलात् स्नेहात्
अङ्कमारोप्य वीर्यवान् ।
उपवेश्य तमुत्सङ्गे
परिष्वज्यावपीडितम् ।
भ्रातरं लक्ष्मणं स्निग्धम्
पुनःपुनरुदैक्षत ॥
Smelling the head of Lakshmaṇa, who always enhanced his reputation, that valiant one, insisting with affection, placed him, who felt shy, on his lap.
Taking his brother Lakshmaṇa into his tight embrace, he looked at him repeatedly showering all the affection.
6.91.11
शल्यसम्पीडितं शस्तम्
निश्वसन्तं तु लक्ष्मणम् ।
रामस्तु दुःखसन्तप्तः
तं तु निःश्वास पीडितः ।
मूर्ध्नि चैनमुपाघ्राय
भूयः संस्पृश्य च त्वरन् ।
उवाच लक्ष्मणं वाक्यम्
आश्वस्य पुरुषर्षभः ॥
Rāma felt sad about Lakshmaṇa, who was badly wounded by the foe's weapons and was breathing heavily.
Sighing deeply, smelling Lakshmaṇa on the head, and touching him with anxious solicitude, the bull among men said these words to him:
6.91.13
कृतं परमकल्याणम्
कर्म दुष्करकर्मणा ।
अद्य मन्ये हते पुत्रे
रावणं निहतं युधि ।
अद्याहं विजयी शत्रौ
हते तस्मिन् दुरात्मनि ॥
With his son slain today, I think, it is as good as Rāvaṇa himself being slain in the battle.
You did the best that can be hoped for, accomplishing a task that is so extremely difficult. I feel victorious today with this evil-minded foe slain.
6.91.14
रावणस्य नृशंसस्य
दिष्ट्या वीर त्वया रणे ।
छिन्नो हि दक्षिणो बाहुः
स हि तस्य व्यापश्रयः ॥
By the grace of Providence, O Veera, you have cut off the right hand of the cruel Rāvaṇa, on whom he relied heavily.
6.91.15
विभीषणहनूमद्भ्याम्
कृतं कर्म महद्रणे ।
अहोरात्रैस्त्रिभिर्वीरः
कथञ्चिद्विनिपातितः ।
निरमित्रः कृतोऽस्म्यद्य
निर्यास्यति हि रावणः ।
बलव्यूहेन महता
श्रुत्वा पुत्रं निपातितम् ॥
Vibheeshaṇa and Hanumān have done doughty deeds in this great battle. Fighting for three days and three nights, you somehow managed to slay that Veera. It is as good as I am freed of all enemies. Hearing that his son has been slain, Rāvaṇa is bound to come out with his huge army.
6.91.17
तं पुत्रवधसन्तप्तम्
निर्यान्तं राक्षसाधिपम् ।
बलेनावृत्य महता
निहनिष्यामि दुर्जयम् ॥
I will slay him, the invincible one who burns with grief over his slain son, as he comes out with the huge army supporting him.
6.91.18
त्वया लक्ष्मण नाथेन
सीता च पृथिवी च मे ।
न दुष्प्रापा हते त्वद्य
शक्रजेतरि चाहवे ॥
With the vanquisher of Ṡakra slain in the battle by you, my protector, O Lakshmaṇa, neither Seetā, nor even the earth, are beyond me to obtain.
6.91.19
स तं भ्रातरमाश्वास्य
परिष्वज्य च राघवः ।
रामः सुषेणं मुदितः
समाभाष्येदमब्रवीत् ॥
Having thus appreciated his brother and embraced him, Rāma, the scion of the Raghus, who was happy, summoned Sushēṇa and said:
6.91.20
सशल्योऽयं महाप्राज्ञ
सौमित्रिर्मित्रवत्सलः ।
यथा भवति सुस्वस्थः
तथा त्वं समुपाचर ॥
Sowmitri, who is affectionate to his friends, is suffering from the darts (all over his body). O extraordinarily sagacious one, please treat him as is appropriate to make him completely normal again.
6.91.21
विशल्यः क्रियतां क्षिप्रम्
सौमित्रिः सविभीषणः ।
ऋक्षवानरसैन्यानाम्
शूराणां द्रुमयोधिनाम् ।
ये चाप्यन्येऽत्र युध्यन्ति
सशल्या व्रणिनस्तथा ।
तेऽपि सर्वे प्रयत्नेन
क्रियन्तां सुखिनस्त्वया ॥
Sowmitri, Vibheeshaṇa, the warriors of the bear and Vānara armies who fought with trees, and whoever else fought and have the darts and wounds on them, should regain their complete health, with all your best efforts.
6.91.23
एवमुक्तस्तु रामेण
महात्मा हरियूथपः ।
लक्ष्मणाय ददौ नस्तः
सुषेणः परमौषधिम् ॥
Thus instructed by Rāma, Mahātma Sushēṇa, a chief of Vānara army, administered a powerful medicine to Lakshmaṇa, through the nose.
6.91.24
स तस्या गन्धमाघ्राय
विशल्यः समपद्यत ।
तथा निर्वेदनश्चैव
संरूढव्रण एव च ॥
With the inhalation of its aroma, all the pricks fell off, all the wounds became healed and he was completely relieved of his pain.
6.91.25
विभीषणमुखानां च
सुहृदां राघवाज्ञया ।
सर्ववानरमुख्यानाम्
चिकित्सां स तदाऽकरोत् ॥
Then he treated all the allies like Vibheeshaṇa, and all the chiefs of Vānaras, as had been instructed by Rāghava.
6.91.26
ततः प्रकृतिमापन्नो
हृतशल्यो गतक्लमः ।
सौमित्रिर्मुदितस्तत्र
क्षणेन विगतज्वरः ॥
Sowmitri felt happy with the pricks removed, relieved from all the discomfort instantly, fatigue gone and feeling normal again.
6.91.27
तथैव रामः प्लवगाधिपस्तदा
विभीषणश्चर्क्षपतिश्च जाम्बवान् ।
अवेक्ष्य सौमित्रिमरोगमुत्थितं
मुदा ससैन्याः सुचिरं जहर्षिरे ॥
Similarly, Rāma, (Sugreeva,) the Lord of Vānaras, Vibheeshaṇa, Jāmbavān, the lord of Ṛkshas, and all their forces, rejoiced for a long while, seeing Sowmitri spring up completely healed of his afflictions.
6.91.28
अपूजयत् कर्म स लक्ष्मणस्य
सुदुष्करं दाशरथिर्महात्मा ।
बभूव हृष्टो युधि वानरेन्द्रो
निशम्य तं शक्रजितं निपातितं ॥
(Rāma,) the mighty son of Daṡaratha praised that formidable achievement of Lakshmaṇa. (Sugreeva,) the Lord of Vānaras was very happy hearing that the vanquisher of Ṡakra was struck down in that battle.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे एकनवतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninety first Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
