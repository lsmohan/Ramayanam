Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 54
In this Sarga, Rāma, along with Lakshmaṇa and Seetā reach the Āṡrama of Bharadwāja at Prayāga, the place where river Yamuna joins river Gaṅgā . Rāma introduces himself, Lakshmaṇa and Seetā to Bharadwāja. Bharadwāja offers him to stay in his Āṡrama. Rāma excuses himself saying that he is wary of the city and country folk coming there to visit him. Then he requests Bharadwāja to suggest a secluded and suitable place for him to stay. Bharadwāja suggests that he could build an Āṡrama on Citrakūṭa, a beautiful mountain comparable to Gandhamādana. The trio stay at Bharadwāja's Āṡrama for the night.
2.54.1
ते तु तस्मिन्महावृक्षे
उषित्वा रजनीं शिवाम् ।
विमलेऽभ्युदिते सूर्ये
तस्माद्देशात्प्रतस्थिरे ॥
Having spent a quiet night under that great tree, they started from that place when the crisp and clear sun arose.
2.54.2
यत्र भागीरथीं गङ्गाम्
यमुनाभिप्रवर्तते ।
जग्मुस्तं देशमुद्दिश्य
विगाह्य सुमहद्वनम् ॥
Entering the great forest, they set out for the place where river Yamuna joins Gaṅgā, the Bhāgeerathi.
2.54.3
ते भूमिभागान्विविधान्
देशांश्चापि मनोरमान् ।
अदृष्टपूर्वान् पश्यन्तः
तत्र तत्र यशश्विनः ॥
Those souls of great repute, on their way, saw different kinds of landscapes and enchanting regions, that they had never seen before.
2.54.4
यथा क्षेमेण गच्छन् स
पश्यंश्च विविधान् द्रुमान् ।
निवृत्तमात्रे दिवसे
रामः सौमित्रिमब्रवीत् ॥
After treading carefully looking at various kinds of trees till the day drew to a close, Rāma spoke to the son of Sumitrā:
2.54.5
प्रयागमभितः पश्य
सौमित्रे धूममुन्नतम् ।
अग्नेर्भगवतः केतुम्
मन्ये सन्निहितो मुनिः ॥
O son of Sumitrā! See the high rising smoke, the banner of the revered Agni, all around Prayāga. I think that the Muni must be nearby!
2.54.6
नूनं प्राप्ताः स्म सम्भेदम्
गङ्गायमुनयोर्वयम् ।
तथा हि श्रूयते शब्दो
वारिणो वारिघट्टितः ॥
We have certainly arrived at the confluence of the Gaṅgā and the Yamuna, for it is the sound of clashing waters that we hear.
2.54.7
दारूणि परिभिन्नानि
वनजैरुपजीविभिः ।
भरद्वाजाश्रमे चैते
दृश्यन्ते विविधा द्रुमाः ॥
We can see logs of wood cut down by woodmen who make a living by sylvan produce, and the many different trees of Bharadwāja's Āṡrama.
2.54.8
धन्विनौ तौ सुखं गत्वा
लम्बमाने दिवाकरे ।
गङ्गायमुनयोस्सन्धौ
प्रापतुर्निलयं मुनेः ॥
The two bowmen, happily charging forward till the sun was poised for setting, reached the residence of the Muni at the confluence of the Gaṅgā and the Yamuna.
2.54.9
रामस्त्वाश्रममासाद्य
त्रासयन्मृगपक्षिणः ।
गत्वा मुहूर्तमध्वानम्
भरद्वाजमुपागमत् ॥
Reaching the precincts of the Āṡrama, Rāma, whose appearance frightened the deer and birds, approached Bharadwāja.
2.54.10
ततस्त्वाश्रममासाद्य
मुनेर्दर्शनकाङ्क्षिणौ ।
सीतयानुगतौ वीरौ
दूरादेवावतस्थतुः ॥
Upon reaching the Āṡrama, those two Veeras, along with Seetā, waited at a distance to see the Muni.
2.54.11
स प्रविश्य महात्मानम्
ऋषिं शिष्यगणैर्वृतम् ।
संशितव्रतमेकाग्रम्
तपसा लब्धचक्षुषम् ।
हुताग्निहोत्रं दृष्ट्वैव
महाभागं कृताञ्जलिः ।
रामः सौमित्रिणा सार्धम्
सीतया चाभ्यवादयत् ॥
As soon as they saw the blessed Mahātma Ṛshi, who was given to rigorous discipline with great focus, who had performed many fire rituals and attained an inner vision by doing Tapa, and surrounded by many of his Ṡishyas, Rāma with joined palms paid obeisance to him along with the son of Sumitrā and Seetā.
2.54.13
न्यवेदयत चात्मानम्
तस्मै लक्ष्मणपूर्वजः ॥
And the elder brother of Lakshmaṇa introduced himself to him:
2.54.13
पुत्रौ दशरथस्यावाम्
भगवन् रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
भार्या ममेयं वैदेही
कल्याणी जनकात्मजा ।
मां चानुयाता विजनम्
तपोवनमनिन्दिता ॥
O Bhagawan! We are Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, the sons of Daṡaratha. This blessed and beautiful princess of Vidēha, the daughter of Janaka, is my wife. She, the irreproachable one, has followed me to the secluded Tapōvana.
2.54.15
पित्रा प्रव्राज्यमानं माम्
सौमित्रिरनुज प्रियः ।
अयमन्वगमद्भ्राता
वनमेव दृढव्रतः ॥
When my father exiled me, my loving younger brother, son of Sumitrā, who is quite resolved, followed me to the Vana.
2.54.16
पित्रा नियुक्ता भगवन्
प्रवेक्ष्यामस्तपोवनम् ।
धर्ममेव चरिष्याम
स्तत्र मूलफलाशनाः ॥
As ordered by my father, we shall enter the Tapōvana and live in accordance with Dharma, subsisting on roots and fruits.
2.54.17
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राजपुत्रस्य धीमतः ।
उपानयत धर्मात्मा
गामर्घ्यमुदकं ततः ॥
Hearing those words of the perspicacious prince, that Dharmātma made him the ceremonial offering of a cow and of water as a token of hospitality.
2.54.18
नानाविधानन्नरसान्
वन्यमूलफलाश्रयान् ।
तेभ्यो ददौ तप्ततपा
वासं चैवाभ्यकल्पयत् ॥
He, who had performed Tapa, served them with a variety of delicious foods made of fruits and roots grown in the forest and also arranged for their stay.
2.54.19
मृगपक्षिभिरासीनो
मुनिभिश्च समन्ततः ।
राममागतमभ्यर्च्य
स्वागतेनाहतं मुनिः ॥
The Ṛshi, seated the deer and birds, as well as many Munis about him, welcomed Rāma who had thus arrived, with all honor.
What a beautiful sight - a Muni surrounded by deer and birds!
2.54.20
प्रतिगृह्य च तामर्चाम्
उपविष्टं स राघवम् ।
भरद्वाजोऽब्रवीद्वाक्यम्
धर्मयुक्तमिदं तदा ॥
And when Rāghava sat down accepting the hospitality, Bharadwāja said these words that were in conformance with Dharma:
2.54.21
चिरस्य खलु काकुत्स्थ
पश्यामि त्वामिहागतम् ।
श्रुतं तव मया चेदम्
विवासनमकारणम् ॥
It is a long time indeed since I saw you here last, O prince of Kākutstha lineage! I have heard of your being exiled without just cause.
In these crisp words of Bharadwāja, Vālmeeki summarizes the whole episode of Rāma's exile from a neutral and unemotional perspective of an outsider:
"Rāma was exiled without a just cause. Period."
2.54.22
अवकाशो विविक्तोऽयम्
महानद्योस्समागमे ।
पुण्यश्च रमणीयश्च
वसत्विह भवान् सुखम् ॥
This place, at the confluence of two great rivers, is sacred, beautiful and free of human habitation. You can stay here happily.
2.54.23
एवमुक्तस्तु वचनम्
भरद्वाजेन राघवः ।
प्रत्युवाच शुभं वाक्यम्
रामः सर्वहिते रतः ॥
Hearing those words of Bharadwāja, Rāghava, who bore everyone's welfare in mind, replied suitably, saying these well-meaning words:
2.54.24
भगवन्नित आसन्नः
पौरजानपदो जनः ।
सुदर्शमिह मां प्रेक्ष्य
मन्येऽहमिममाश्रमम् ।
आगमिष्यति वैदेहीम्
मां चापि प्रेक्षको जनः ।
अनेन कारणेनाहम्
इह वासं न रोचये ॥
O Bhagawan! I am afraid that the citizens and country-folk who live nearby, knowing that they could easily find me here, might constantly come to this Āṡrama to see me and the princess of Vidēha. Hence, I would not quite like to live here.
2.54.26
एकान्ते पश्यभगवन्
आश्रमस्थानमुत्तमम् ।
रमेत यत्र वैदेही
सुखार्हा जनकात्मजा ॥
Think of some secluded place, O Bhagawan, which would be an excellent site for an Āṡrama and in which the princess of Vidēha, who is used to comfort, will be happy.
2.54.27
एतछ्रुत्वा शुभं वाक्यम्
भरद्वाजो महामुनिः ।
राघवस्य ततो वाक्यम्
अर्थग्राहकमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those well spoken words, Bharadwāja, the eminent Muni, told Rāghava these words that would meet his needs:
One Krōṡa equals two miles.
2.54.28
दशक्रोश इतस्तात
गिरिर्यत्रनिवत्स्यसि ।
महर्षिसेवितः पुण्यः
सर्वतः सुखदर्शनः ।
गोलाङ्गूलानुचरितो
वानरर्क्षनिषेवितः ।
चित्रकूट इति ख्यातो
गन्धमादनसन्निभः ॥
There is a mountain by name Citrakūṭa that is comparable to Gandhamādana at a distance of ten Krōṡas from here, which would be good for you to live in. It is an auspicious place frequented by Maharshis, offering a pleasant view in every direction. It is inhabited by Gōlāṅgūlas, Vānaras and bears.
Gōlāṅgūlas and Vānaras were first mentioned in Bāla Kāṇḍa, Sarga 17.
They were created by Dēvas upon the injunction of Brahma, to aid Vishnu, who would appear in the form of Rāma.
2.54.30
यावता चित्रकूटस्य
नरशृङ्गान्यवेक्षते ।
कल्याणानि समाधत्ते
न पापे कुरुते मनः ॥
The sight of the peaks of Citrakūṭa, will evoke only positive thoughts in one's mind, and never an evil one.
2.54.31
ऋषयस्तत्र बहवो
विहृत्य शरदां शतम् ।
तपसा दिवमारूढाः
कपालशिरसा सह ॥
Many Ṛshis lived there for hundreds of years, did Tapa till their heads whithered into mere skulls and then ascended to Heaven.
2.54.32
प्रविविक्तमहं मन्ये
तं वासं भवतस्सुखम् ।
इह वा वनवासाय
वस राम मया सह ॥
I think you will be happy living there, a place free of human habitation. But you are also welcome to live with me here.
2.54.33
स रामं सर्वकामैस्तम्
भरद्वाजः प्रियातिथिम् ।
सभार्यं सह च भ्रात्रा
प्रतिजग्राह धर्मवित् ॥
Bharadwāja, well versed in Dharma, entertained Rāma, the welcome guest, and his wife and brother with all that they could wish for.
2.54.34
तस्य प्रयागे रामस्य
तं महर्षिमुपेयुषः ।
प्रपन्ना रजनी पुण्याः
चित्राः कथयतः कथाः ॥
The night descended as Rāma and the Maharshi went on conversing on various topics at that Prayāga.
2.54.35
सीता तृतीयः काकुत्स्थः
परिश्रान्तः सुखोचितः ।
भरद्वाजाश्रमे रम्ये
तां रात्रिमवसत्सुखम् ॥
The prince of Kākutstha lineage, who was used to comfort, was very tired. He, along with Lakshmaṇa and Seetā spent the night comfortably in Bharadwāja's pleasant Āṡrama.
2.54.36
प्रभातायां रजन्यां तु
भरद्वाजमुपागमत् ।
उवाच नरशार्दूलो
मुनिं ज्वलिततेजसम् ॥
After the night gave way to dawn, that tiger among men went to Bharadwāja of fiery radiance, and said:
2.54.37
शर्वरीं भगवन्नद्य
सत्यशील तवाश्रमे ।
उषिताः स्मेह वसतिम्
अनुजानातु नो भवान् ॥
O Bhagawan, devotee of truth! We have spent the night comfortably in your Āṡrama. Please permit us to depart to the place where we shall live.
2.54.38
रात्र्यां तु तस्यां व्युष्टायाम्
भरद्वाजोऽब्रवीदिदम् ।
मधुमूलफलोपेतम्
चित्रकूटं व्रजेति ह ॥
After the night thus passed, Bharadwāja told him, "You may proceed to Citrakūṭa which abounds in roots, fruits and honey".
2.54.39
वासमौपयिकं मन्ये
तव राम महाबल ।
नानानगगणोपेतः
किन्नरोरगसेवितः ।
मयूरनादाभिरुतो
गजराजनिषेवितः ।
गम्यतां भवता शैल
श्चित्रकूटः स विश्रुतः ।
पुण्यश्च रमणीयश्च
बहुमूलफलायुतः ॥
Surrounded by many foothills, frequented by Kinnaras and Uragas, resounding on all sides with the cawing of peacocks, and abounding in royal tuskers, it is a place that will, I think, suit a man of great strength like you. Go to the famed mountain Citrakūṭa, which is holy, pleasant and has plenty of roots and fruits.
2.54.41
तत्र कुञ्जरयूथानि
मृगयूथानि चाभितः ।
विचरन्ति वनान्तेस्मिन्
तानि द्रक्ष्यसि राघव ॥
There, deep in the Vana, O Rāghava, you will see herds of elephants and deer moving about!
2.54.42
सरित्प्रस्रवणप्रस्थान्
दरीकन्धरनिर्झरान् ।
चरतः सीतया सार्धम्
नन्दिष्यति मनस्तव ॥
And as you, along with Seetā, go about looking at the mountainsides with their rivers and rivulets, the hollows among the rocks, the caves and the waterfalls, your heart will be filled with joy!
2.54.43
प्रहृष्टको यष्टिककोकिलस्वनै
र्विनादितं तं वसुधाधरं शिवम् ।
मृगैश्च मत्तैर्बहुभिश्च कुञ्जरैः
सुरम्यमासाद्य समावसाश्रमम् ॥
That blessed mountain echoes with the melodious notes of delighted lapwings and cuckoos, and is beautiful with amorous deer and many tuskers. May you reach there and live in your own Āṡrama.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे चतुःपञ्चाश स्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty fourth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
