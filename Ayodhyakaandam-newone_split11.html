Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 11
In this Sarga, the infatuated Daṡaratha, reasserts that he would oblige Kaikēyee with anything that she asks for, and swears in the name of Rāma, whom he loves more than anything in this world, including himself and his other children.
Thus assured, and after calling upon the gods and all beings to witness, Kaikēyee divulges her intent, to have Bharata crowned and to have Rāma sent away to the Vanas.
2.11.1
तं मन्मथशरैर्विद्धम्
कामवेगवशानुगम् ।
उवाच पृथिवीपालम्
कैकेयी दारुणं वचः ॥
To him, the ruler of the earth, who was struck by the dart of love, and swept off his feet by the tides of passion, Kaikēyee spoke these terrible words.
In modern times, even passionate love is often seen as indulgence, and demeaned to little more than lust.
But in classical times, passionate love is lifted into divine realms and is celebrated as an expression of the deepest attraction between the polarities of the universe, of Prakṛti and Purusha.
Love between male and female and love between mother and child are celebrated in the classical times by the Indic civilization, over and over, in a highly glorious manner, a parallel to which is hard to be found in the classical literature of any other civilization.
You may recall the extensive commentary we had on this subject in Bāla Kāṇḍa, Sarga 2, in the context of Vālmeeki seeing one of two love-making birds shot by a hunter, an incident that moved the great poet and set the tone for this great poem.
2.11.2
नास्मि विप्रकृतादेव
केन चिन्नावमानिता ।
अभिप्रायस्तु मे कश्चित्
तमिच्छामि त्वया कृतम् ॥
My lord! I have been neither harmed nor insulted by anybody. But there is something I want you to do for me.
2.11.3
प्रतिज्ञां प्रतिजानीष्व
यदि त्वं कर्तुमिच्छसि ।
अथ तद्व्याहरिष्यामि
यदभिप्रार्थितं मया ॥
If you are willing to fulfill it, give me your word. Then I shall lay out what I want.
2.11.4
तामुवाच महातेजाः
कैकेयीमीषदुत्स्मितः ।
कामी हस्तेन संगृह्य
मूर्धजेषु शुचिस्मिताम् ॥
That supremely radiant King smiling at that Kaikēyee who had a bright smile, and playing with her hair with infatuation, said:
2.11.5
आवलिप्ते न जानासि
त्वत्तः प्रियतरो मम ।
मनुजो मनुजव्याघ्रात्
रामादन्यो न विद्यते ॥
O proud lady, don't you know that there is no one in this world except Rāma, that tiger among men, who is dearer to me than yourself?
2.11.6
तेनाजय्येन मुख्येन
राघवेण महात्मना ।
शपे ते जीवनार्हेण
ब्रूहि यन्मनसेच्छासि ॥
I give you my word, swearing on that invincible and prominent Mahātma Rāghava, the prop of my life. Let me know what you have on your mind!
2.11.7
यं मुहूर्तमपश्यंस्तु
न जीवेयमहं ध्रुवम् ।
तेन रामेण कैकेयि
शपे ते वचनक्रियाम् ॥
O Kaikēyee! I swear to do your bidding, by the name of that Rāma without seeing whom, I cannot live, for sure, even for a moment.
2.11.8
आत्मना वात्मजैश्चान्यैः
वृणेयं मनुजर्षभम् ।
तेन रामेण कैकेयि
शपे ते वचनक्रियाम् ॥
O Kaikēyee! I swear to do your bidding, by the name of that Rāma, the bull among men, whom I seek even over myself and my other children!
2.11.9
भद्रे हृदयमप्येतत्
अनुमृश्योद्धरस्व मे ।
एतत्समीक्ष्य कैकेयि
ब्रूहि यत्साधु मन्यसे ॥
My dear, touch my heart and lift it up! Consider everything that there is, O Kaikēyee, and tell me what you think is right!
2.11.10
बलमात्मनि जानन्ती
न मां शङ्कितुमर्हसि ।
करिष्यामि तव प्रीतिम्
सुकृतेनापि ते शपे ॥
You shall not doubt me, as you very well know the power you have over me! I swear on all the good deeds that I have done, that I will do whatever pleases you!
2.11.11
सा तदर्थमना देवी
तमभिप्रायमागतम् ।
निर्माध्यस्थ्याच्च हर्षाच्च
बभाषे दुर्वचं वचः ॥
The lady, intent on her purpose, told what was on her mind, the abominable words, exultantly and in no uncertain terms.
2.11.12
तेन वाक्येन संहृष्टा
तमभिप्रायमागतम् ।
व्याजहार महाघोरम्
अभ्यागतमिवान्तकम् ॥
Delighted by his words, she spoke what was on her mind, which was like death descending from nowhere, all of a sudden.
2.11.13
यथा क्रमेण शपसि
वरं मम ददासि च ।
तच्छृण्वन्तु त्रयस्त्रिंशत्
वेगास्साग्निपुरोगमाः ॥
May all the thirty three gods, led by Agni bear witness to your oath to give the boon to me!
2.11.14
चन्द्रादित्यौ नभश्चैव
ग्रहा रात्र्यहनी दिशः ।
जगच्च पृथिवी चेयम्
सगन्धर्वा सराक्षसा ।
निशाचराणि भूतानि
गृहेषु गृहदेवता ।
यानि चान्यानि भूतानि
जानीयुर्भाषितं तव ॥
May the moon and sun, the sky, the planets, the night and day, the cardinals of space and the world, this earth along with all the Gandharvas the Rākshasas, the spirits that rage in the night, the deities of every house and the rest of all beings too, bear witness to your words!
2.11.16
सत्यसन्धो महातेजाः
धर्मज्ञः सुसमाहितः ।
वरं मम ददात्येष
तन्मे श्रृण्वन्तु दैवताः ॥
This supremely radiant savant of Dharma, the adherent of truth, in all his sanity and integrity is giving me these boons! May all the gods bear witness to this!
2.11.17
इति देवी महेष्वासम्
परिगृह्याभिशस्य च ।
ततः परमुवाचेदम्
वरदं काममोहितम् ॥
Thus flattering and pinning down that great archer, the willing and infatuated man, she said:
2.11.18
स्मर राजन् पुरावृत्तम्
तस्मिन् दैवासुरे रणे ।
तत्र चाच्यावयच्छत्रुः
तव जीवितमन्तरा ॥
Recall, O King, the war that occurred between the Dēvas and the Asuras long ago, in which you were relieved of everything but your life.
Since Daṡaratha unequivocally promised to Kaikēyee that he would fulfill whatever was on her mind, she did not have to invoke the past, the war between Dēvas and Asuras or the boons here, strictly speaking.
But she does it anyway, being the foolish (gullible) enough to follow the script given by Manthara, verbatim.
2.11.19
तत्र चापि मया देव
यत्त्वं समभिरक्षितः ।
जाग्रत्या यतमानायाः
ततो मे प्राददा वरौ ॥
There, my Lord, you were rescued by me. In appreciation of my being alert in saving you, you granted me two boons.
2.11.20
तौ तु दत्तौ वरौ देव
निक्षेपौ मृगयाम्यहम् ।
तथैव पृथिवीपाल
सकाशे सत्यसङ्गर ॥
My Lord! O protector of the earth! O adherent of truth! I am now seeking those two boons that are safe with you.
2.11.21
तत्प्रतिश्रुत्य धर्मेण
न चेद्दास्यसि मे वरम् ।
अद्यैव हि प्रहास्यामि
जीवितं त्वद्विमानिता ॥
If I am slighted by you by not being granted those boons that were promised, in accordance with Dharma, I shall give up my life right away.
2.11.22
वाङ्मात्रेण तदा राजा
कैकेय्या स्ववशे कृतः ।
प्रचस्कन्द विनाशाय
पाशं मृग इवात्मनः ॥
Bound by those words of Kaikēyee, the king entered, like a deer, into a fatal trap laid by her.
2.11.23
ततः परमुवाचेदम्
वरदं काममोहितम् ॥
Then she said to that willing and infatuated man:
2.11.23
वरौ यौ मे त्वया देव
तदा दत्तौ महीपते ।
तौ तावदहमद्यैव
वक्ष्यामि शृणु मे वचः ॥
My Lord! O ruler of the earth! Listen now, I would now like to spell out those two boons given to me by you!
2.11.24
अभिषेकसमारम्भो
राघवस्योपकल्पितः ।
अनेनैवाभिषेकेण
भरतो मेऽभिषिच्यताम् ॥
Arrangements for Rāma's consecration have been duly made! May those very arrangements be used to consecrate Bharata!
2.11.25
यो द्वितीयो वरो देव
दत्तः प्रीतेन मे त्वया ।
तदा दैवासुरे युद्धे
तस्य कालोऽयमागतः ॥
And now the time has also come for the second boon you, pleased with me during the war between the Dēvas and the Asuras, gave.
2.11.26
नव पञ्च च वर्षाणि
दण्डकारण्यमाश्रितः ।
चीराजिनजटाधारी
रामो भवतु तापसः ॥
May Rāma be bound to the Daṇḍaka Vana for nine years and five, wearing deer skin and sporting matted locks, living like a Muni!
2.11.27
भरतो भजतामद्य
यौवराज्यमकंटकम् ।
एष मे परमः कामो
दत्तमेव वरं वृणे ।
अद्य चैव हि पश्येयम्
प्रयान्तं राघवं वनम् ॥
May Bharata become the Crown Prince without any rivals. This is my ultimate desire. I am only asking for a boon that has already been granted. I would like to see Rāma leaving for the Vana right away.
2.11.29
स राजराजः भव सत्यसङ्गरः
कुलं च शीलं च हि रक्ष जन्म च ।
परत्रवासे हि वदन्त्यनुत्तमं
तपोधनास्सत्यवचो हितं नृणाम् ॥
Being the king of kings that you are, may you adhere to your word!
May you uphold the repute of your character and that of your lineage!
The people enriched with Tapa assert that adherence to truth is second to none, in leading one to a better after life!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकादशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eleventh Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
