Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 109
In this Sarga, Vibheeshaṇa laments his brother's death. Rāma tells him that it is not right to mourn a hero who fought like a true warrior and died in a fight. Rāma orders the final rites to be done for Rāvaṇa.
6.109.1
भ्रातरं निहतं दृष्ट्वा
शयानं निर्जितमं रणे ।
शोकवेगपरीतात्मा
विललाप विभीषणः ॥
On seeing his brother, defeated, struck down and lying in the battlefield, Vibheeshaṇa lamented, quickly overtaken by grief.
6.109.2
वीर विक्रान्त विख्यात
प्रवीण नयकोविद ।
महार्हशयनोपेत
किं शेषे निहतो भुवि ।
विक्षिप्य दीर्घौ निश्चेष्टौ
भुजावङ्गदभूषितौ ।
मुकुटेनापवृत्तेन
भास्कराकारवर्चसा ॥
O you of valor, prowess, fame, who were extremely capable and an expert in strategy! How could you, who possesses so many expensive beds, lie down on the bare ground, slain, with your long arms adorned with shoulder-rings flung about motionless, and your crown of sun-like splendor tilted!
6.109.4
तदिदं वीर सम्प्राप्तम्
मया पूर्वं समीरितम् ।
काममोहपरीतस्य
यत्ते न रुचितं वचः ॥
This, which I had warned a long while ago has come to pass, O valiant one; but you did not like it, being completely taken over by lust and delusion!
6.109.5
यन्न दर्पात् प्रहस्तो वा
नेन्द्रजिन्नापरे जनाः ।
न कुम्भकर्णोऽतिरथो
नातिकायो नरान्तकः ।
न स्वयं त्वममन्येथाः
तस्योदर्कोऽयमागतः ॥
You have reaped the fruit of what, neither Prahasta, nor Indrajit, nor Kumbhakarṇa, nor Atiratha, nor Atikāya, nor Narāntaka nor the others, nor even you yourself, heeded, out of arrogance!
6.109.6
गतः सेतुः सुनीतानाम्
गतो धर्मस्य विग्रहः ।
गतः सत्त्वस्य सङ्क्षेपः
प्रस्तावानां गतिर्गता ।
आदित्यः पतितो भूमौ
मग्नस्तमसि चन्द्रमाः ।
चित्रभानुः प्रशान्तार्चिः
व्यवसायो निरुद्यमः ।
अस्मिन्निपतिते भूमौ
वीरे शस्त्रभृतां वरे ॥
With this valiant one, the foremost of all the wielders of weapons fallen on the ground, the bulwark of polity has disappeared, the incarnation of Dharma has departed, power concentrated at one place fizzled, the object of all praise has vanished, the sun has fallen on the ground, the moon has plunged into darkness, the fire's blazing flames has quieted down, and enthusiasm lost its force.
These Ṡlōkas, some in long meter, are considered unauthentic. They contradict the Ṡlōkas towards the end of the upcoming Sarga 111, where Vibheeshaṇa is reluctant to perform the final rites to his evil brother.
6.109.9
किं शेषमिव लोकस्य
गतसत्त्वस्य संप्रति ।
रणे राक्षसशार्दूले
प्रसुप्त इव पांसुषु ॥
With the tiger among Rākshasas lying in the dust of the battlefield, as though he is asleep, what is there left in the world, with all its strength lost?
6.109.10
धृतिप्रवालः प्रसहाग्र्यपुष्पः
तपोबलः शौर्यनिबद्धमूलः ।
रणे महान् राक्षसराजवृक्षः
सम्मर्दितो राघवमारुतेन ॥
This mighty tree called Rākshasa King, which had courage for its tender shoots, fortitude for its splendid blooms, whose strength lay in Tapa and at whose base was the unfailing heroic valor, has been uprooted by the tempest that is Rāma.
6.109.11
तेजोविषाणः कुलवंशवंशः
कोपप्रसादापरगात्रहस्तः ।
इक्ष्वाकुसिंहावगृहीतदेहः
सुप्तः क्षितौ रावणगन्धहस्ती ॥
The elephant in rut, called Rāvaṇa, which had power for its tusks, high pedigree for its back, anger for its rear parts and graciousness for its trunk, lies on the ground, its body overthrown by the lion Rāma.
6.109.12
पराक्रमोत्साहविजृम्भितार्चिः
निश्वासधूमः स्वबलप्रतापः ।
प्रतापवान् संयति राक्षसाग्निः
निर्वापितो रामपयोधरेण ॥
This fire, called Rākshasa, which had prowess and zest for its soaring tongues of flame, deep sighs for its smoke, innate strength for its scorching power, which showed all its scorching power in the fight is now put out by the raincloud called Rāma.
6.109.13
सिंहर्क्षलाङ्गूलककुद्विषाणः
पराभिजिद्गन्धनगन्धहस्ती ।
रक्षोवृषश्चापलकर्णचक्षुः
क्षितीश्वरव्याघ्रहतोऽवसन्नः ॥
The bull that was this Rākshasa, which had Rākshasas for tail, hump and horns, and lasciviousness for its eyes and ears, which was impetuous like an elephant in rut, and vanquished its enemies, is now destroyed, slain by that tiger, the king of men.
6.109.14
वदन्तं हेतुमद्वाक्यम्
परिमृष्टार्थनिश्चयम् ।
रामः शोकसमाविष्टम्
इत्युवाच विभीषणम् ॥
Then Rāma said this to Vibheeshaṇa who was thus taken over by grief, saying words that made sense and reflected the true reality:
6.109.15
नायं विनष्टो निश्चेष्टः
समरे चण्डविक्रमः ।
अत्युन्नतमहोत्साहः
पतितोऽयमशङ्कितः ॥
He did not fall for lack of effort. He showed his fiery prowess in the fight. He fell, without knowing any fear, and showing extraordinary enthusiasm, of the highest order.
6.109.16
नैवं विनष्टाः शोच्यन्ते
क्षत्रधर्ममवस्थिताः ।
वृद्धिमाशंसमाना ये
निपतन्ति रणाजिरे ॥
Those who fall like this in the battlefield fighting justly like a true hero, seeking better things in life, should not be mourned over.
6.109.17
येन सेन्द्रास्त्रयो लोकाः
त्रासिता युधि धीमता ।
तस्मिन् कालसमायुक्ते
न कालः परिशोचितुम् ॥
When the sagacious one, who terrorized all the three worlds including Indra, has met his end, it is no occasion for lamenting.
6.109.18
नैकान्तविजयो युद्धे
भूतपूर्वः कदाचन ।
परैर्वा हन्यते वीरः
परान्वा हन्ति संयुगे ॥
Victory is never guaranteed for anyone in a fight; a valiant man either slays the enemy or is slain by him.
6.109.19
इयं हि पूर्वैः सन्दिष्टा
गतिः क्षत्रियसम्मता ।
क्षत्रियो निहतः सङ्ख्ये
न शोच्य इति निश्चय ॥
It has been laid down clearly by the ancestors as the appropriate code for Kshatriyas - that a Kshatriya who has been slain in a battle should not be mourned.
6.109.20
तदेवं निश्चयं दृष्ट्वा
तत्त्वमास्थाय विज्वरः ।
यदिहानन्तरं कार्यम्
कल्प्यं तदनुचिन्तय ॥
Thinking of this code, taking its spirit to heart, and disregarding the pain, please think of what is to be done next, given the current situation.
6.109.21
तमुक्तवाक्यं विक्रान्तम्
राजपुत्रं विभीषणः ।
उवाच शोकसन्तप्तो
भ्रातुर्हितमनन्तरम् ॥
To that prince of great prowess, who spoke thus, Vibheeshaṇa, heavy with grief, said what would be appropriate to be done to his brother next:
6.109.22
योऽयं विमर्देषु न भग्नपूर्वः
सुरैः समस्तैरपि वासवेन ।
भवन्तमासाद्य रणे विभग्नो
वेलामिवासाद्य यथा समुद्रः ॥
He never knew defeat in battles, even with all the Suras and with Vāsava; but was defeated in this battle confronting you, like a sea that fails, confronting its shore.
These Ṡlōkas in long meter are considered unauthentic. They contradict the Ṡlōkas towards the end of the upcoming Sarga 111, where Vibheeshaṇa is reluctant to perform the final rites for his evil brother.
6.109.23
अनेन दत्तानि वनीपकेषु
भुक्ताश्च भोगा निभृताश्च भृत्याः ।
धनानि मित्रेषु समर्पितानि
वैराण्यमित्रेषु निपातितानि ॥
He enjoyed luxuries, paid his staff well and was generous to beggars. He shared wealth with his friends and wrecked the foe in his wrath.
6.109.24
एषोहिताग्निश्च महातपाश्च
वेदान्तगः कर्मसु चाग्र्यशूरः ।
एतस्य यत्प्रेतगतस्य कृत्यं
तत्कर्तुमिच्छामि तव प्रसादात् ॥
He performed fire-rites and did great Tapa. He fathomed the depths of the Vēdas and was at the forefront of performing the rites enjoined by the Vēdas. I would like to do what is appropriate for him, a departed soul, with your gracious permission.
6.109.25
स तस्य वाक्यैः करुणैर्महात्मा
सम्बोधितः साधु विभीषणेन ।
आज्ञापयामास नरेन्द्रसूनुः
स्वर्गीयमाधानमदीनसत्त्वः ॥
Thus addressed earnestly in a pitiful voice by Vibheeshaṇa, the prince of extraordinary power ordered the performance of rites that help one fare well in Heaven:
6.109.26
मरणान्तानि वैराणि
निर्वृत्तं नः प्रयोजनम् ।
क्रियतामस्य संस्कारो
ममाप्येष यथा तव ॥
All enmities end with death, and our purpose is served. The last rites shall be done for him. He is the same to me as he is to you.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे नवोत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and ninth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
