Baala Kaanda - Sarga 55
The fight that erupted between Viṡwāmitra's army and the forces created by Ṡabala in the previous Sarga concludes in this Sarga when the army of Viṡwāmitra gets completely eradicated. Viṡwāmitra goes off to the Himalayas, does Tapa, pleases Ṡiva who grants all the Astras in the world to him. He then goes back to Vasishṭha’s Āṡrama and destroys it. Vasishṭha gets outraged and lifts his Daṇḍa.
1.55.1
ततस्तानाकुलान् दृष्ट्वा
विश्वामित्रास्त्रमोहितान् ।
वसिष्ठश्चोदयामास
कामधुक्सृज योगतः ॥
Seeing those forces thus disturbed and confused by the Astras of Viṡwāmitra, Vasishṭha exhorted the wish fulfilling cow to create more forces using Yōgic powers.
1.55.2
तस्या हुम्भारवाज्जाताः
काम्भोजा रविसन्निभाः ।
ऊधसस्त्वथ सञ्जाताः
पप्लवाः शस्त्रपाणयः ।
योनिदेशाच्च यवनाः
शकृद्देशाच्छकास्तथा ।
रोमकूपेषु च म्लेच्छा
हारीतास्सकिरातकाः ॥
From her bellowing came out Kāmbhōjas with the power of the sun. From her udder came out Paplavas holding deadly weapons. From her vagina came out Yavanas. Form the rectum came out Ṡakas. From the pores of her skin came out Mlēcchas, Hāreetas and Kirātas.
1.55.4
तैस्तैर्निषूदितं सर्वम्
विश्वामित्रस्य तत्क्षणात् ।
सपदातिगजं साश्वम्
सरथं रघुनन्दन ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! All the four forces of the army of Viṡwāmitra, the infantry, cavalry, elephantry and the chariotry were completely destroyed by them.
1.55.5
दृष्ट्वा निषूदितं सैन्यम्
वसिष्ठेन महात्मना ।
विश्वामित्रसुतानां तु
शतं नानाविधायुधम् ।
अभ्यधावत्सुसंक्रुद्धम्
वसिष्ठं जपतां वरम् ।
हुङ्कारेणैव तान् सर्वान्
ददाह भगवानृषिः ॥
Seeing the entire army thus destroyed by Mahātma Vasishṭha a hundred of the sons of Viṡwāmitra became outraged and dashed to attack him, the foremost of Munis, with all types of weapons. All of them were scorched instantaneously by a snort from Vasishṭha.
1.55.7
ते साश्वरथपादाता
वसिष्ठेन महात्मना ।
भस्मीकृता मुहूर्तेन
विश्वामित्रसुतास्तदा ॥
All of them were turned into a heap of ashes along with their chariots and horses in a matter of moments, by the Mahātma Vasishṭha.
1.55.8
दृष्ट्वा विनाशितान् पुत्रान्
बलं च सुमहायशाः ।
सव्रीडश्चिन्तयाऽऽविष्टो
विश्वामित्रोऽभवत्तदा ॥
That renowned Viṡwāmitra, seeing his sons and army destroyed thus, felt humiliated and sad.
1.55.9
समुद्र इव निर्वेगो
भग्नदंष्ट्र इवोरगः ।
उपरक्त इवादित्यः
सद्यो निष्प्रभतां गतः ॥
Like an ocean whose fury is spent, like a snake whose fangs are broken, and like the sun who is eclipsed, He became pale and dull.
1.55.10
हतपुत्रबलो दीनो
लूनपक्ष इव द्विजः ।
हतदर्प्पो हतोत्साहो
निर्वेदं समपद्यत ॥
With his sons and army killed, with his ego completely shattered, with his spirits completely subdued, like a bird whose wings were clipped, he felt sad, depressed and helpless.
1.55.11
स पुत्रमेकं राज्याय
पालयेति नियुज्य च ।
पृथिवीं क्षत्रधर्म्मेण
वनमेवान्वपद्यत ॥
He asked one of his sons to take care of the kingdom as is expected of a Kshatriya and went away to the Vanas.
1.55.12
स गत्वा हिमवत्पार्श्वम्
किन्नरोरगसेवितम् ।
महादेवप्रसादार्थम्
तपस्तेपे महातपाः ॥
He went to the mountainsides of the Himalayas where Kinnaras and Uragas dwell and started on a great Tapa to please Mahā Dēva.
Mahā Dēva means 'all powerful', Ṡiva.
1.55.13
केनचित्त्वथ कालेन
देवेशो वृषभध्वजः ।
दर्शयामास वरदो
विश्वामित्रं महाबलम् ॥
In due course, the lord of Dēvas (Ṡiva) with a bull on his pennant, appeared to the mighty Viṡwāmitra to grant him wishes.
1.55.14
किमर्थं तप्यसे राजन्
ब्रूहि यत्ते विवक्षितम् ।
वरदोऽस्मि वरो यस्ते
काङ्क्षितः सोऽभिधीयताम् ॥
O King! Why are you performing Tapa? What are you seeking? Let me know your wish and I shall grant it!
1.55.15
एवमुक्तस्तु देवेन
विश्वामित्रो महातपाः ।
प्रणिपत्य महादेवम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Viṡwāmitra who performed great Tapa, prostrated to Mahā Dēva and said:
1.55.16
यदि तुष्टो महादेव
धनुर्वेदो ममानघ ।
साङ्गोपाङ्गोपनिषदः
सरहस्यः प्रदीयताम् ॥
O unafflicted one! Pleased if you are, grant me the Dhanurvēda in its full, with all its parts and subparts, and with all its nuances!
Dhanurvēda is the science of Archery that includes the different types of arrows with both physical and magical powers.
1.55.17
यानि देवेषु चास्त्राणि
दानवेषु महर्षिषु ।
गन्धर्वयक्षरक्षस्सु
प्रतिभान्तु ममानघ ॥
O unafflicted one! May all Astras that are with Dēvas, Dānavas, Maharshis, Gandharvas, Rākshasas and Yakshas be known to me!
1.55.18
तव प्रसादाद्भवतु
देवदेव ममेप्सितम् ।
एवमस्त्विति देवेशो
वाक्यमुक्त्वा गतस्तदा ॥
O lord of Dēvas! May my wishes be fulfilled by your generosity! "May that be so!" said the lord of Dēvas and left.
1.55.19
प्राप्य चास्त्राणि राजर्षिः
विश्वामित्रो महाबलः ।
दर्पेण महतायुक्तो
दर्पपूर्णोऽभवत्तदा ॥
The mighty strong Rājarshi Viṡwāmitra, who was already proud, became filled with arrogance on gaining all the Astras.
1.55.20
विवर्धमानो वीर्येण
समुद्र इव पर्वणि ।
हतमेव तदा मेने
वसिष्ठमृषिसत्तमम् ॥
With his spirits and strength risen like the ocean on the full moon day, he felt sure of killing Vasishṭha, the foremost of Munis.
1.55.21
ततो गत्वाऽऽश्रमपदम्
मुमोचास्त्राणि पार्थिवः ।
यैस्तत्तपोवनं सर्वम्
निर्दग्धं चास्त्रतेजसा ॥
Then that king went to the Āṡrama and released his weapons and set it on fire.
1.55.22
उदीर्यमाणमस्त्रं तत्
विश्वामित्रस्य धीमतः ।
दृष्ट्वा विप्रद्रुता भीता
मुनयः शतशो दिशः ॥
Seeing those torrent of Astras of that persistent Viṡwāmitra, hundreds of Munis fled in every direction.
1.55.23
वसिष्ठस्य च ये शिष्याः
तथैव मृगपक्षिणः ।
विद्रवन्ति भयाद्भीता
नानादिग्भ्यः सहस्रशः ॥
Thousands of Vasishṭha’s Ṡishyas and the animals and birds of the Āṡrama fled in every direction with fear.
1.55.24
वसिष्ठस्याश्रमपदम्
शून्यमासीन्महात्मनः ।
मुहूर्तमिव निःशब्दम्
आसीदिरिणसन्निभम् ॥
With everyone and everything having fled, the Āṡrama of that Mahātma Vasishṭha bore the dreaded emptiness and silence of a funeral ground for a moment.
1.55.25
वदतो वै वसिष्ठस्य
माभैरिति मुहुर्मुहुः ।
नाशयाम्यद्य गाधेयम्
नीहारमिव भास्करः ॥
Vasishṭha kept telling them all the while: Do not panic! I will destroy Viṡwāmitra, just as the sun would destroy fog.
1.55.26
एवमुक्त्वा महातेजा
वसिष्ठो जपतां वरः ।
विश्वामित्रं तदा वाक्यम्
सरोषमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Then the supremely radiant Vasishṭha, foremost among Munis turned to Viṡwāmitra and said with rage:
1.55.27
आश्रमं चिरसंवृद्धम्
यद्विनाशितवानसि ।
दुराचारोऽसि तन्मूढ
तस्मात्त्वं न भविष्यसि ॥
You Idiot! You have destroyed the Āṡrama that has been nurtured and developed over a long time. You are not going to live any more, having committed this heinous act.
1.55.28
इत्युक्त्वा परमक्रुद्धो
दण्डमुद्यम्य सत्वरः ।
विधूममिव कालाग्निम्
यमदण्डमिवापरम् ॥
Saying thus, he immediately lifted his Daṇḍa with rage, which looked like the Daṇḍa of Yama and like the smokeless Kālāgni.
The generic meaning of Daṇḍa is 'staff'. The Daṇḍa held by Ṛshis is in the shape of 'T'. Kālāgni is a figurative reference to 'time' that burns away everything in the end.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे पञ्चपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty fifth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
