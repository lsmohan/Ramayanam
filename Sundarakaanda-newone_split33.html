Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 33
In this Sarga, Hanumān gets down from the tree and asks Seetā who she is. He pretends as if he does not know who she is. He tries to guess who she is and gradually concludes that she must be Seetā and asks whether she could confirm.
Seetā, feeling happy to hear Hanumān sing praises of Rāma, tells him that she is indeed Seetā and tells how she happened to be where she is and about the time limit of two months set on her life by Rāvaṇa.
5.33.1
सोऽवतीर्य द्रुमात्तस्मात्
विद्रुमप्रतिमाननः ।
विनीतवेषः कृपणः
प्रणिपत्योपसृत्य च ।
तामब्रवीन्महातेजा
हनूमान्मारुतात्मजः ।
शिरस्यञ्जलिमाधाय
सीतां मधुरया गिरा ॥
Hanumān, the sun of Vāyu of supreme radiance, his face glowing coral-red, got down from the tree. He approached her with great sympathy and made humble salutations to her holding his joined palms above his head, and spoke these words in a pleasant voice:
5.33.3
का नु पद्मपलाशाक्षि
क्लिष्टकौशेयवासिनि ।
द्रुमस्य शाखामालम्ब्य
तिष्ठसि त्वमनिन्दिते ॥
O you of lotus-petal eyes in crumpled silk garment leaning on the tree branch! Who are you, O blemish-less one?
5.33.4
किमर्थम् तव नेत्राभ्याम्
वारि स्रवति शोकजम् ।
पुण्डरीकपलाशाभ्याम्
विप्रकीर्णमिवोदकम् ॥
Why are tears of misery flowing from your lotus-petal eyes like water shooting from a spring?
5.33.5
सुराणामसुराणां वा
नागगन्धर्वरक्षसाम् ।
यक्षाणां किन्नराणां वा
का त्वं भवसि शोभने ॥
O beautiful one! Who are you? Do you belong to Suras, Asuras, Nāgas, Gandharvas, Rākshasas, Yakshas or Kinnaras?
5.33.6
का त्वं भवसि रुद्राणाम्
मरुतां वा वरानने ।
वसूनां हि वरारोहे
देवता प्रतिभासि मे ॥
O you of beautiful face! Could you be from Rudras, Maruts or Vasus? You look like one among the Dēvas to me, O you of beautiful Kaṭee!
5.33.7
कि नु चन्द्रमसा हीना
पतिता विबुधालयात् ।
रोहिणी ज्योतिषां श्रेष्ठा
श्रेष्ठसर्वगुणान्विता ॥
Are you that Rōhiṇee, the foremost of all stars endowed with every good quality who had been separated from the moon and had fallen from the heavens?
5.33.8
कोपाद्वा यदि वा मोहात्
भर्तारमसितेक्षणे ।
वसिष्ठं कोपयित्वा त्वम्
नासि कल्याण्यरुन्धती ॥
O you of beautiful black eyes! You are not Arundhati, the Kalyāṇi, are you? You did not offend your husband Vasishṭha out of anger or silliness, did you!
5.33.9
को नु पुत्रः पिता भ्राता
भर्ता वा ते सुमध्यमे ।
अस्माल्लोकादमुं लोकम्
गतं त्वमनुशोचसि ॥
O you of lovely waist! Are you crying about your father or brother or husband who has departed from this world to the other?
5.33.10
रोदनादतिनिश्श्वासात्
भूमिसंस्पर्शनादपि ।
न त्वां देवीमहं मन्ये
राज्ञः संज्ञावधारणात् ॥
I would not think that you are a Dēvata, because you are crying, sighing heavily and standing on the ground (not floating above) and because of the marks of royalty on you!
5.33.11
व्यञ्जनानि च ते यानि
लक्षणानि च लक्षये ।
महिषी भूमिपालस्य
राजकन्या च मे मता ॥
I think, based on your appearance and the marks on you that are apparent, that you are the daughter of a king and wife of a ruler of the earth.
5.33.12
रावणेन जनस्थानात्
बलादपदहृता यदि ।
सीता त्वमसि भद्रं ते
तन्ममाचक्ष्व पृच्छतः ॥
I want to ask you whether you are Seetā who was carried off by Rāvaṇa by force from Janasthāna! Please tell me; may all bode well for you!
5.33.13
यथा हि तव वै दैन्यम्
रूपं चाप्यतिमानुषम् ।
तपसा चान्वितो वेषः
त्वं राममहिषी ध्रुवम् ॥
Based on your pitiful semblance, your extraordinary beauty and your ascetic appearance you must surely be the queen of Rāma!
5.33.14
सा तस्य वचनं श्रुत्वा
रामकीर्तनहर्षिता ।
उवाच वाक्यं वैदेही
हनुमन्तं द्रुमाश्रितम् ॥
On hearing his words of praise for Rāma, Vaidēhee was extremely happy and said these words to Hanumān who was by the tree.
5.33.15
पृथिव्यां राजसिंहानाम्
मुख्यस्य विदितात्मनः ।
स्नुषा दशरथस्याहम्
शत्रुसैन्यप्रतापिनः ॥
Yes, I am the daughter-in-law of Daṡaratha, who was prominent among the lions among kings, who tormented the enemy forces and whose personality is well known.
5.33.16
दुहिता जनकस्याहम्
वैदेहस्य महात्मनः ।
सीतेति नाम नाम्नाऽहम्
भार्या रामस्य धीमतः ॥
I am the daughter of Mahātma Janaka, the king of Vidēha. My name is Seetā and I am the wife of sagacious Rāma.
5.33.17
समा द्वादश तत्राहम्
राघवस्य निवेशने ।
भुञ्जाना मानुषान्भोगान्
सर्वकामसमृद्धिनी ॥
For twelve years, living in the house of Rāghava, I enjoyed every luxury a human can conceive of, knowing no bounds to what I can wish for.
5.33.18
तत्र त्रयोदशे वर्षे
राज्येनेक्ष्वाकुनन्दनम् ।
अभिषेचयितुं राजा
सोपाध्यायः प्रचक्रमे ॥
Then, in the thirteenth year, the king, along with the preceptors, was all set to consecrate the scion of the Ikshwākus, (as the crown prince) in the kingdom.
5.33.19
तस्मिन्सम्भ्रियमाणे तु
राघवस्याभिषेचने ।
कैकयी नाम भर्तारम्
देवी वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
While arrangements were being made for the consecration, one of the queens, Kaikēyee by name, said these words to her husband:
5.33.20
न पिबेयं न खादेयम्
प्रत्यहं मम भोजनम् ।
एष मे जीवितस्यान्तो
रामो यद्यभिषिच्यते ॥
I shall neither drink nor have a daily meal if Rāma is consecrated; that day would be the last day of my life.
5.33.21
यत्तदुक्तं त्वया वाक्यम्
प्रीत्या नृपतिसत्तम ।
तच्छेन्न वितथं कार्यम्
वनं गच्छतु राघवः ॥
O great lord of people! Pleased with me, you gave me your word. Rāghava should go to Vana, if that word is not to become an empty promise!
5.33.22
स राजा सत्यवाग्देव्या
वरदानमनुस्मरन् ।
मुमोह वचनं श्रुत्वा
कैकेय्याः क्रूरमप्रियम् ॥
That king, who was true to his word, recalled the boon he had given and swooned on hearing those cruel and heart-wrenching words.
5.33.23
ततस्तु स्थविरो राजा
सत्ये धर्मे व्यवस्थितः ।
ज्येष्ठं यशस्विनं पुत्रम्
रुदन्राज्यमयाचत ॥
Then the aged king, adhering to Truth and Dharma, cried and requested his renowned elder son to give the kingdom back to him (so that it can be passed on to Bharata).
5.33.24
स पितुर्वचनं श्रीमान्
अभिषेकात्परं प्रियम् ।
मनसा पूर्वमासाद्य
वाचा प्रतिगृहीतवान् ॥
The blessed one, keeping in mind his father’s word ahead of the kingdom, gave his explicit consent.
5.33.25
दद्यान्न प्रतिगृह्णीयात्
न ब्रूयात्किञ्चिदप्रियम् ।
अपि जीवितहेतोर्वा
रामस्सत्यपराक्रमः ॥
Rāma, a man of true valor, only gives, but never takes; and he would never utter an unpleasant word even if it was to save his own life.
5.33.26
स विहायोत्तरीयाणि
महार्हाणि महायशाः ।
विसृज्य मनसा राज्यम्
जनन्यै मां समादिशत् ॥
Then he of great renown left behind his luxurious upper garments, removed the thought of the kingdom from his mind and directed me to the care of his mother.
5.33.27
साहं तस्याग्रतस्तूर्णम्
प्रस्थिता वनचारिणी ।
न हि मे तेन हीनाया
वासस्स्वर्गेऽपि रोचते ॥
However, right then and there I readied myself to walk in the Vana along with him; for I would not be interested in staying without him even in heaven.
5.33.28
प्रागेव तु महाभागः
सॏमित्रिर्मित्रनन्दनः ।
पूर्वजस्यानुयात्रार्थे
द्रुमचीरैरलङ्कृतः ॥
The blessed Sowmitri, a delight of his friends, was already prepared to follow his older brother, clad in tree bark.
5.33.29
ते वयं भर्तुरादेशम्
बहुमान्य दृढव्रताः ।
प्रविष्टास्स्म पुरादृष्टम्
वनं गम्भीरदर्शनम् ॥
Then with firm resolve and in complete deference to the instructions of the lord, we entered the deep woods which we had never seen before.
5.33.30
वसतो दण्डकारण्ये
तस्याहममितौजसः ।
रक्षसाऽपहृता भार्या
रावणेन दुरात्मना ॥
Then, I, the wife of that man of immense power, was abducted by Rāvaṇa, the evil minded Rākshasa, while staying in the Daṇḍaka Araṇya.
5.33.31
द्वौ मासौ तेन मे कालो
जीवितानुग्रहः कृतः ।
ऊर्ध्वं द्वाभ्यां तु मासाभ्याम्
ततस्त्यक्ष्यामि जीवितम् ॥
Just two more months of lease on my life have been given to me by him; once those two months pass, I shall take my own life.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे त्रयस्त्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty third Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
