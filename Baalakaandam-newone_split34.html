Baala Kaanda - Sarga 34
The stories that Viṡwāmitra told in the previous Sargas were actually about his own lineage, as we will learn in this Sarga.
1.34.1
कृतोद्वाहे गते तस्मिन्
ब्रह्मदत्ते च राघव ।
अपुत्रः पुत्रलाभाय
पौत्रीमिष्टिमकल्पयत् ॥
O Rāma, after Brahmadatta left, (married to all his one hundred daughters), Kuṡanābha performed a Yajña that would give him sons, since he did not have any.
The Yajña done by Kuṡanābha is the same as that done by Daṡaratha to get offspring, referred to as Putrakāmeshṭi.
1.34.2
इष्ट्यां च वर्तमानायाम्
कुशनाभं महीपतिम् ।
उवाच परमोदारः
कुशो ब्रह्मसुतस्तदा ॥
As the Yajña was being performed, the kind and considerate Kuṡa, the son of Brahma and the father of Kuṡanābha, said to Kuṡanābha :
1.34.3
पुत्र ते सदृशः पुत्रो
भविष्यति सुधार्मिकः ।
गाधिं प्राप्स्यसि तेन त्वम्
कीर्तिं लोके च शाश्वतीम् ॥
Dear son! You will have a son by name Gādhi. He will be a great Dhārmika like you. He will make your name and fame last forever in this world.
1.34.4
एवमुक्त्वा कुशो राम
कुशनाभं महीपतिम् ।
जगामाकाशमाविश्य
ब्रह्मलोकं सनातनम् ॥
After saying these words to Kuṡanābha, Kuṡa disappeared into the skies and on to the timeless Brahma Lōka.
1.34.5
कस्यचित्त्वथ कालस्य
कुशनाभस्य धीमतः ।
जज्ञे परमधर्मिष्ठो
गाधिरित्येव नामतः ॥
After some time, the eminent Kuṡanābha had a son by name Gādhi, who was a great Dhārmika.
1.34.6
स पिता मम काकुत्स्थ
गाधिः परमधार्मिकः ।
कुशवंशप्रसूतोऽस्मि
कौशिको रघुनन्दन ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! That great Dhārmika, Gādhi was my father. Since I come from that lineage of Kuṡa, I am known as Kouṡika.
1.34.7
पूर्वजा भगिनी चापि
मम राघव सुव्रता ।
नाम्ना सत्यवती नाम
ऋचीके प्रतिपादिता ॥
O Rāma, I also have an elder sister by name Satyavati, a highly principled woman, who is married to Ṛceeka.
1.34.8
सशरीरा गता स्वर्गम्
भर्तारमनुवर्तिनी ।
कौशिकी परमोदारा
प्रवृत्ता च महानदी ॥
She went, Along with her husband, to Heaven with her mortal body and became the river called Kouṡiki.
1.34.9
दिव्या पुण्योदका रम्या
हिमवन्तमुपाश्रिता ।
लोकस्य हितकामार्थम्
प्रवृत्ता भगिनी मम ॥
My sister, with her divine and auspicious waters, flows gorgeously from the Himalayas benefiting all the people on her path.
The time before Rāma was that magical time when gods, human beings and nature could interchange to each other easily.
1.34.10
ततोऽहं हिमवत्पार्श्वे
वसामि निरतः सुखम् ।
भगिन्यां स्नेहसंयुक्तः
कौशिक्यां रघुनन्दन ॥
Since I love my sister, I enjoy spending as much time as possible near the Himalayas.
1.34.11
सा तु सत्यवती पुण्या
सत्ये धर्मे प्रतिष्ठिता ।
पतिव्रता महाभागा
कौशिकी सरितां वरा ॥
That blessed, chaste and clean Satyavati, who is firmly established in Truth and Righteousness, became one of the best among rivers.
1.34.12
अहं हि नियमाद्राम
हित्वा तां समुपागतः ।
सिद्धाश्रममनुप्राप्य
सिद्धोऽस्मि तव तेजसा ॥
O Rāma, for the sake of following the strict regimen required for Tapa, I had to leave her and stay at Siddhāṡrama, where I gained Siddhi, due to your brilliance.
1.34.13
एषा राम ममोत्पत्तिः
स्वस्य वंशस्य कीर्तिता ।
देशस्य च महाबाहो
यन्मां त्वं परिपृच्छसि ॥
O Rāma! That is the story of my ancestry and lineage and the story of this land, that you asked me about.
1.34.14
गतोऽर्द्धरात्रः काकुत्स्थ
कथाः कथयतो मम ।
निद्रामभ्येहि भद्रं ते
मा भूद्विघ्नोऽध्वनीह नः ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! I have been telling these stories for too long. Now it is past mid-night. Please go to bed. May all bode well for you. Hope we do not have any more difficulties in our journey!
These Ṡlōkas give us a taste of the poetry of Vālmeeki. Expect to get drowned in it when we get to Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa.
1.34.15
निष्पन्दास्तरवः सर्वे
निलीना मृगपक्षिणः ।
नैशेन तमसा व्याप्ता
दिशश्च रघुनन्दन ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! The trees are still and silent. Animals and birds are quiet. The darkness of night has spread in all directions.
1.34.16
शनैर्वियुज्यते सन्ध्या
नभो नेत्रैरिवावृतम् ।
नक्षत्रतारागहनम्
ज्योतिर्भिरवभासते ॥
The sun has set gently and quietly long ago. Like thousands of bright eyes watching over us, bright and twinkling stars have filled the sky.
1.34.17
उत्तिष्ठति च शीतांशुः
शशी लोकतमोनुदः ।
ह्लादयन् प्राणिनां लोके
मनांसि प्रभया विभो ॥
O dear lord! The moon is rising spreading his cool light everywhere. The soft and gentle touch of the that light is soothes the hearts of all beings.
1.34.18
नैशानि सर्वभूतानि
प्रचरन्ति ततस्ततः ।
यक्षराक्षससङ्घाश्च
रौद्राश्च पिशिताशनाः ॥
All the nocturnals have come out, moving here and there. The fearsome, nocturnal, man-eating Yakshas and Rākshasas are also out on prowl.
1.34.19
एवमुक्त्वा महातेजा
विरराम महामुनिः ।
साधु साध्विति तं सर्वे
ऋषयो ह्यभ्यपूजयन् ॥
The supremely radiant Muni, saying thus, rested for the night. All the Munis paid respects to him, with deep appreciation of all that he said.
1.34.20
कुशिकानामयं वंशो
महान् धर्मपरः सदा ।
ब्रह्मोपमा महात्मानः
कुशवंश्या नरोत्तमाः ॥
They said: "The highly respected lineage of Kuṡa held Dharma in high esteem. Every one in that lineage was a noble Mahātma comparable to Brahma Dēva himself.
1.34.21
विशेषेण भवानेव
विश्वामित्रो महायशाः ।
कौशिकी च सरिच्छ्रेष्ठा
कुलोद्योतकरी तव ॥
O reputed one! You stand foremost among all of them. Kouṡiki, the best among rivers, makes your lineage glow.
1.34.22
इति तैर्मुनिशार्दूलैः
प्रशस्तः कुशिकात्मजः ।
निद्रामुपागमच्छ्रीमान्
अस्तङ्गत इवांशुमान् ॥
The radiant descendant of Kuṡa (Viṡwāmitra), thus praised by the tigers among Munis rested for the night, like the radiant sun that rests in the evening.
1.34.23
रामोऽपि सहसौमित्रिः
किञ्चिदागतविस्मयः ।
प्रशस्य मुनिशार्दूलम्
निद्रां समुपसेवते ॥
Rāma, along with Lakshmaṇa, also was fascinated (with the story of Viṡwāmitra) and praised that tiger among Munis, and went to sleep.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे चतुस्त्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty fourth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
