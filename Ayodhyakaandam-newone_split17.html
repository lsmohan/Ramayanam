Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 17
Rāma's procession from his palace to the king's palace, which started in the previous Sarga, concludes in this short Sarga, when he enters the private apartment of the king.
2.17.1
स रामो रथमास्थाय
सम्प्रहृष्टसुहृज्जनः ।
पताकाध्वजसम्पन्नम्
महार्हागरुधूपितम् ।
अपश्यन्नगरं श्रीमान्
नानाजनसमाकुलम् ॥
The glorious Rāma, aboard the chariot, surrounded by his extremely delighted friends, beheld the city decorated with banners and pennants, filled with fragrance of precious Agaru incense and crowded with people of every description.
2.17.2
स गृहैरभ्रसङ्काशैः
पाण्डुरैरुपशोभितम् ।
राजमार्गं ययौ रामो
मध्येनागरुधूपितम् ॥
Rāma proceeded through the royal highway that was full of fragrance of Agaru incense and gleaming with white houses on either side resembling lofty clouds.
2.17.3
चन्दनानां च मुख्यानाम्
अगरूणां च सञ्चयैः ।
उत्तमानां च गन्धानाम्
क्षौमकोशाम्बरस्य च ।
अविद्धाभिश्च मुक्ताभिः
उत्तमैस्स्फाटिकैरपि ।
शोभमानमसंबाधैः
तं राजपथमुत्तमम् ।
संवृतं विविधैःपुष्पैः
भक्ष्यैरुच्चावचैरपि ॥
The royal highway was splendid and spacious. It was plentiful with exquisite sandal and Agaru, many more fragrances of high quality, heaps of white silken cloth, articles made of undrilled pearls and crystals, flowers of every kind and food articles of every variety.
2.17.6
ददर्श तं राजपथम्
दिवि देवपथं यथा ।
दध्यक्षतहविर्लाजैः
धूपैरगरुचन्दनैः ।
नानामाल्योपगंधैश्च
सदाऽभ्यर्चितचत्वरम् ॥
Rāma beheld that royal highway which was like a divine pathway. Along the way, he saw many places where Pūjas were performed, evident from the presence of curd, puffed grain, Akshatas, Havis, sandal and Agaru incense and fragrant garlands of every kind.
Havis is an offering to gods such as Ghee, usually offered in Agni.
2.17.7
अशीर्वादान्बहून् शृण्वन्
सुहृद्भिस्समुदीरितान् ।
यथार्हं चापि सम्पूज्य
सर्वानेव नरान्ययौ ॥
He proceeded paying due respects to all the people along the way. He heard blessings showered on him in many a manner, by his well wishers:
2.17.8
पितामहैराचरितम्
तथैव प्रपितामहैः ।
अद्योपादाय तं मार्गम्
अभिषिक्तोऽनुपालय ॥
May you be consecrated today and provide governance following the same path that was followed by your grandfathers and great-grandfathers!
2.17.9
यथास्म पोषिताः पित्रा
यथा सर्वैः पितामहैः ।
ततस्सुखतरं रामे
वत्स्यामस्सति राजनि ॥
We would certainly have even better times under the rule of Rāma than we were blessed with under the regime of his father and all his ancestors!
2.17.10
अलमद्य हि भुक्तेन
परमार्थैरलं च नः ।
यदि पश्याम निर्यान्तम्
रामं राज्ये प्रतिष्ठितम् ॥
Seeing Rāma's procession conclude in his installation as crown prince in the kingdom is more than food and fulfillment of life for us today!
2.17.11
अतो हि नः प्रियतरम्
नान्यत्किञ्चिद्भविष्यति ।
यथाऽभिषेको रामस्य
राज्येनामिततेजसः ॥
Nothing better can happen to us than having the supremely radiant Rāma consecrated!
2.17.12
एताश्चान्याश्च सुहृदाम्
उदासीनः कथाश्शुभाः ।
आत्मसम्पूजनीश्शृण्वन्
ययौ रामो महापथम् ॥
Rāma quietly continued on that great highway taking everything in his stride, as he listened to such words of his well wishers and many other good words in his praise.
2.17.13
न हि तस्मान्मनः कश्चित्
चक्षुषी वा नरोत्तमात् ।
नर श्शक्नोत्यपाक्रष्टुम्
अतिक्रान्तेऽपि राघवे ॥
Even long after Rāghava proceeded past, no one was able to take their eyes and thoughts off of that best of men!
2.17.14
यश्च रामं न पश्येत्तु
यं च रामो न पश्यति ।
निन्दित स्सर्वल्लोकेषु
स्वात्माऽप्येनं विगर्हते ॥
Anyone who had not seen Rāma and whom Rāma had not seen was not taken to kindly by anyone in any world, including by oneself.
2.17.15
सर्वेषु हि स धर्मात्मा
वर्णानां कुरुते दयाम् ।
चतुर्णां हि वयः स्थानाम्
तेन ते तमनुव्रताः ॥
He, the Dharmātma, was kind to people of all the four Varṇas and to people of all ages. Hence all the people clung to him.
2.17.16
चतुष्पथान्देवपथान्
चैत्यान्यायतनानि च ।
प्रदक्षिणं परिहरन्
जगाम नृपते स्सुतः ॥
The King's son proceeded on his way, passing the squares, temples, platforms, sacred trees and assembly halls to his right.
Tradition holds that we should always pass any object or person of reverence only to our right.
This is a common practice in temples everywhere. We always circumambulate the temple or the deity in the clockwise direction, making sure that our right hand is towards the temple or deity.
2.17.17
स राजकुलमासाद्य
मेघसङ्घोपमै श्शुभैः ।
प्रासादशृङ्गैर्विविधैः
कैलास शिखरोपमैः ।
आवारयद्भिर्गगनम्
विमानैरिव पाण्डुरैः ।
वर्धमानगृहैश्चापि
रत्नजालपरिष्कृतैः ।
तत्पृथिव्यां गृहवरम्
महेन्द्रसदनोपमम् ।
राजपुत्रः पितुर्वेश्म
प्रविवेश श्रिया ज्वलन् ॥
He then reached the King's palace that resembled a bank of clouds. Its beautiful white turrets of many designs, comparable to the peaks of Kailāsa mountain, blocked the view of the sky, like Vimānas. With its suites bearing the mark of Vardhamāna and lattices encrusted with gems, it was like the mansion of the great Indra on earth. The King's son entered that glorious and splendid house of his father.
Vimāna is airship.
2.17.20
स कक्ष्या धन्विभिर्गुप्ताः
तिस्रोऽतिक्रम्य वाजिभिः ।
पदातिरपरे कक्ष्ये
द्वे जगाम नरोत्तमः ॥
That best among men, went on his chariot through the first three courtyards that were protected by bowmen. Then he went through the remaining two courtyards on foot.
2.17.21
स सर्वा स्समतिक्रम्य
कक्ष्या दशरथात्मजः ।
सन्निवर्त्य जनं सर्वम्
शुद्धान्तः पुरमभ्यगात् ॥
After passing through all the courtyards, the son of Daṡaratha asked everyone to stay back and entered the private apartment of the king.
2.17.22
तस्मिन् प्रविष्टे पितुरन्तिकं तदा
जन स्स सर्वो मुदितो नृपात्मजे ।
प्रतीक्षते तस्य पुनः स्म निर्गमं
यथोदयं चन्द्रमसस्सरित्पतिः ॥
Everyone felt happy seeing the son of the king gain the audience of the king in privacy. Then, they waited for him to come out like the oceans would wait for the moon to emerge.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे सप्तदशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventeenth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
