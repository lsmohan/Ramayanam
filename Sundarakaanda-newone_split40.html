Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 40
In this Sarga, Seetā once again asks Hanumān to tell Rāma about her grief, and how she would not be able to bear any longer the sufferings and the cruel words of Rākshasis, and how difficult it is for her to put up with the stares of Rāvaṇa. Hanumān reassures her again saying that Rāma and Lakshmaṇa will come, destroy Laṅkā and rescue her. He then asks her whether there is any other token that she can give, to be given to Rāma. Seetā tells him that she had given the best of the best tokens. Hanumān gets ready to take off. Seetā passes on her best wishes to the brothers, to Sugreeva and to his ministers and to everyone else.
5.40.1
श्रुत्वा तु वचनं तस्य
वायुसूनोर्महात्मनः ।
उवाचात्महितं वाक्यम्
सीता सुरसुतोपमा ॥
Hearing the words of that Mahātma, the son of Vāyu, Seetā, who was comparable to a daughter of the Dēvas, said these words which betrayed the urgency she felt:
5.40.2
त्वां दृष्ट्वा प्रियवक्तारम्
सम्प्रहृष्यामि वानरः ।
अर्धसञ्जातसस्येव
वृष्टिं प्राप्य वसुन्धरा ॥
Seeing you speak words that are so pleasant, I feel very happy, O Vānara, like the earth with its half sprouted crop does upon being drenched with downpour!
5.40.3
यथा तं पुरुषव्याघ्रम्
गात्रैश्शोकाभिकर्शितैः ।
संस्पृशेयं सकामाहम्
तथा कुरु दयां मयि ॥
Please be so kind as to do what it takes for me to touch that tiger among men with this body that is so worn out with grief, with all my swelling desire.
Can any words, other than these of Vālmeeki Maharshi, express a woman’s feeling towards her beloved from whom she was separated, better?
5.40.4
अभिज्ञानं च रामस्य
दद्या हरिगणोत्तम ।
क्षिप्तामिषीकां काकस्य
कोपादेकाक्षिशातनीम् ॥
O best among the hordes of Vānaras! Tell Rāma about the reed of grass that he hurled at the crow in his rage which made it one-eyed, to jog his memory.
5.40.5
मनश्शिलायास्तिलको
गण्डपार्श्वे निवेशितः ।
त्वया प्रणष्टे तिलके
तं किलस्मर्तुमर्हसि ॥
You should remind him how once he put the Tilaka of Manaṡṡila on my cheeks, while it was the Tilaka on my forehead that was erased!
5.40.6
स वीर्यवान्कथं सीताम्
हृतां समनुमन्यसे ।
वसन्तीं रक्षसां मध्ये
महेन्द्रवरुणोपमः ॥
How could one so valiant as you, comparable to Mahēndra and Varuṇa, tolerate for a moment the carrying off of Seetā and her living among the Rākshasas?
5.40.7
एष चूडामणिर्दिव्यो
मया सुपरिरक्षितः ।
एतं दृष्ट्वा प्रहृष्यामि
व्यसने त्वामिवानघ ॥
I saved this divine Cūḍāmaṇi carefully. Seeing this is like seeing you, O sinless one! It cheers me up when I feel distressed.
5.40.8
एष निर्यातितश्श्रीमान्
मया ते वारिसम्भवः ।
अतः परं न शक्ष्यामि
जीवितुं शोकलालसा ॥
This splendid one, which emerged from the depths of ocean, to you, after which I can hardly survive, wallowing in grief.
5.40.9
असह्यानि च दुःखानि
वाचश्च हृदयच्छिदः ।
राक्षसीनां सुघोराणाम्
त्वत्कृते मर्षयाम्यहम् ॥
I am able to put up with these unbearable sufferings and the words of these extremely cruel Rākshasis that lacerate my heart, only because (I am waiting) for you.
5.40.10
धारयिष्यामि मासं तु
जीवितं शत्रुसूदन ।
ऊर्ध्वं मासान्न जीविष्ये
त्वया हीना नृपात्मज ॥
O slayer of enemies, I will be able to hold on to this life only for a month more! O prince! I will not be able to live even a little beyond a month without you!
5.40.11
घोरो राक्षसराजोऽयम्
दृष्टिश्च न सुखा मयि ।
त्वां च श्रुत्वा विषज्जन्तम्
न जीवेयमहं क्षणम् ॥
This king of Rākshasas is terrible. His stare at me is no matter of comfort. I cannot live any more, even for a moment, if I hear of any delay on your part!
This Ṡlōka nicely summarizes all that Seetā has to say.
5.40.12
वैदेह्या वचनं श्रुत्वा
करुणं साश्रु भाषितम् ।
अथाब्रवीन्महातेजा
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ॥
Hearing those pitiable words of Vaidēhee, that flew along with the tears, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, of immense power said:
5.40.13
त्वच्छोकविमुखो रामो
देवि सत्येन ते शपे ।
रामे दुःखाभिभूते तु
लक्ष्मणः परितप्यते ॥
Grieving for you, O Dēvi,  Rāma neglects everything else.  I swear this on truth.  And when Rāma is taken over by grief,  Lakshmaṇa becomes sad too. 
5.40.14
कथञ्चिद्भवती दृष्टा
न कालः परिशोचितुम् ।
इमं मुहूर्तं दुःखानाम्
अन्तं द्रक्ष्यसि भामिनि ॥
Somehow, you have been found.  This is no occasion to grieve.  This hour, as you shall see, my fair lady, is the beginning of the end of all sorrows!
5.40.15
तावुभौ पुरुषव्याघ्रौ
राजपुत्रावरिन्दमौ ।
त्वद्दर्शनकृतोत्साहौ
लङ्कां भस्मीकरिष्यतः ॥
Those two princes, tigers among men, who pulverize their enemies,  are eager to see you  and they will reduce Laṅkā to ashes. 
5.40.16
हत्त्वा च समरे क्रूरम्
रावणं सहबान्धवम् ।
राघवौ त्वां विशालाक्षि
स्वां पुरीं प्रापयिष्यतः ॥
Slaying the cruel Rāvaṇa along with his kith and kin  in the battle field, O you of wide eyes,  the Rāghavas will take you back to their city. 
5.40.17
यत्तु रामो विजानीयात्
अभिज्ञानमनिन्दिते ।
प्रीतिसञ्जननं तस्य
भूयस्त्वं दातुमर्हसि ॥
But you shall give me, O blameless one, such further tokens that Rāma would recognize immediately and that would stir joy in him!
5.40.18
साब्रवीद्दत्तमेवेति
मयाभिज्ञानमुत्तमम् ।
एतदेव हि रामस्य
दृष्ट्वा मत्केशभूषणम् ।
श्रद्धेयं हनुमन्वाक्यम्
तव वीर भविष्यति ॥
Then she said, ‘What I have given you is the best of the best tokens, O Hanumān! Seeing this, which adorns my hair, O Veera, Rāma would trust your word.’
5.40.19
स तं मणिवरं गृह्य
श्रीमान्प्लवगसत्तमः ।
प्रणम्य शिरसा देवीम्
गमनायोपचक्रमे ॥
Then taking that exquisite jewel, the blessed and eminent Vānara saluted the lady bowing his head and became ready to leave.
5.40.20
तमुत्पातकृतोत्साहम्
अवेक्ष्य हरिपुङ्गवम् ।
वर्धमानं महावेगम्
उवाच जनकात्मजा ।
अश्रुपूर्णमुखी दीना
बाष्पगद्गदया गिरा ॥
Then seeing that bull among Vānaras capable of attaining great speed, who was ready to fly and was increasing his body size, the despondent daughter of Janaka and her face filled with tears, said, with a voice choked with tears:
5.40.22
हनुमन्सिंहसङ्काशौ
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
सुग्रीवं च सहामात्यम्
सर्वान् ब्रूयाह्यनामयम् ॥
O Hanumān! Wish the two lion-like brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, and Sugreeva, his ministers and everyone else the best of health!
5.40.23
यथा च स महाबाहुः
मां तारयति राघवः ।
अस्माद्दुःखाम्बुसम्रोधात्
त्वं समाधातुमर्हसि ॥
You should so plead my cause with Rāghava that he of the mighty arm takes me to the other end of this ocean of grief.
5.40.24
इमं च तीव्रं मम शोकवेगं
रक्षोभिरेभिः परिभर्त्सनं च ।
ब्रूयास्तु रामस्य गतस्समीपम्
शिवश्च तेऽध्वाऽस्तु हरिप्रवीर ॥
O best of the Veeras among Vānaras! After you are with Rāma, please tell him about my torrent of grief and the continuous threats of the Rākshasis; may everything fare well for you on your way!
5.40.25
स राजपुत्र्य्राप्रतिवेदितार्थः
कपिः कृतार्थः परिहृष्टचेताः ।
अल्पावशेषं प्रसमीक्ष्य कार्यं
दिशं ह्युदीचीं मनसा जगाम ॥
The Vānara’s mind, his purpose served, the task ahead of him clearly laid out by the princess realizing that there was only a little bit left for him to do, already began moving in the northerly direction.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे चत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fortieth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
