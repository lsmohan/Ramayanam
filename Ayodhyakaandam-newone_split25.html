Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 25
In this Sarga, the reluctant Kousalyā gives her blessings to the determined Rāma, who is set to leave for the Vana.
She invokes gods and all powers to protect him during his stay in the Vana. She propitiates all the dangerous elements of nature, so that they would not harm Rāma. She hopes that his virtues and his steadfast adherence to Dharma will be a natural protection for him always.
She gets a Brāhmaṇa to perform a Hōma  for the welfare of Rāma. And finally, she gives kisses and hugs before bidding farewell to him. Rāma, then, leaves for the residence of Seetā.
2.25.1
सावनीय तमायासम्
उपस्पृश्य जलं शुचिः ।
चकार माता रामस्य
मङ्गलानि मनस्विनी ॥
Setting aside her grief determinedly and purifying herself by touching water, Rāma's mother whole heartedly invoked blessings on him:
2.25.2
न शक्यसे वारयितुम्
गच्छेदानीं रघूत्तम ।
शीघ्रं च विनिवर्तस्व
वर्तस्व च सतां क्रमे ॥
O best among the descendants of Raghu! I do not seem to be able to dissuade you. Hence may leave now, but come back very soon. Follow the footsteps of noble men.
2.25.3
यं पालयसि धर्मं त्वम्
धृत्या च नियमेन च ।
स वै राघवशार्दूल
धर्मस्त्वामभिरक्षतु ॥
O tiger among the descendants of Raghu! May the Dharma, which you are protecting with pluck and discipline, protect you!
2.25.4
येभ्यः प्रणमसे पुत्र
चैत्येष्वायतनेषु च ।
ते च त्वामभिरक्षन्तु
वने सह महर्षिभिः ॥
My son! May the gods to whom you pray in the shrines and temples, along with the Maharshis, protect you in the Vana!
2.25.5
यानि दत्तानि तेऽस्त्राणि
विश्वामित्रेण धीमता ।
तानि त्वामभिरक्षन्तु
गुणैस्समुदितं सदा ॥
May the Astras given to you by the magnificent Viṡwāmitra guard you always, O mountain of virtues!
2.25.6
पितृशुश्रूषया पुत्र
मातृशुश्रूषया तथा ।
सत्येन च महाबाहो
चिरं जीवाभिरक्षितः ॥
O my son, of mighty arm! May you have a long life, protected by your adherence to truth and your unwavering service to mother and father!
2.25.7
समित्कुश पवित्राणि
वेद्यश्चायतनानि च ।
स्थण्डिलानि विचित्राणि
शैला वृक्षाः क्षुपा ह्रदाः ।
पतङ्गाः पन्नगास्सिंहाः
त्वां रक्षन्तु नरोत्तम ॥
O best among men! May the Samidhas, the Darbha grass, and the rings made thereof, the fire altars and the temples, and the various sanctified grounds, the trees, plants, lakes, birds, and serpents protect you!
2.25.8
स्वस्तिसाध्याश्च विश्वे च
मरुतश्च महर्षयः ।
स्वस्ति धाता विधाता च
स्वस्ति पूषा भगोऽर्यमा ।
लोकपालाश्च ते सर्वे
वासवप्रमुखास्तथा ॥
May everything good be done to you by everyone, the Sādhyas, the Viṡwadēvas, the Maruts, Maharshis, Dhātā, Vidhātā, the suns Poosha, Bhaga, Āryama, Indra and the lords of the worlds!
2.25.10
ऋतवश्चैव पक्षाश्च
मासा स्संवत्सराः क्षपाः ।
दिनानि च मुहूर्ताश्च
स्वस्ति कुर्वन्तु ते सदा ॥
May the seasons, the fortnights, the months and years, the nights, the days, and each and every part of the days, secure your wellbeing always!
2.25.11
स्मृतिर्धृतिश्च धर्मश्च
पातु त्वां पुत्र सर्वतः ।
स्कन्दश्च भगवान्देव
स्सोमश्च स बृहस्पतिः ।
सप्तर्षयो नारदश्च
ते त्वां रक्षन्तु सर्वतः ॥
May your alertness, your pluck and your adherence to Dharma, my son, protect you everywhere! May the Bhagawan and Dēva Skanda and may the moon, Bṛhaspati, the seven Ṛshis and Nārada protect you everywhere!
In the days of mobile phones and internet, it is easy to be in touch with your near and dear who are away from home.
But, until the 21st century, life was very different. Anyone who was away from home for few years or months or weeks or even days, was not only out of sight but also out of reach.
Thos who were concerned about their wellbeing had no option but to keep their faith in gods and good fortune.
It is very unnerving to let the near and dear go away, not to be seen and heard for an extended period of time, but that was the hard reality.
However, there is a certain beauty to that life style, in which people submitted themselves to destiny and transferred the responsibility of their loved ones to it.
2.25.12
याश्चापि सर्वतस्सिध्दा
दिशश्च सदिगीश्वराः ।
स्तुता मया वने तस्मिन्
पान्तु त्वां पुत्र नित्यशः ॥
May the exalted cardinals and their lords to whom I pray, protect you, my son, always and everywhere, in the Vana!
2.25.13
शैलास्सर्वे समुद्राश्च
राजा वरुण एव च ।
द्यौरन्तरिक्षं पृथिवी
नद्यस्सर्वास्तथैव च ।
नक्षत्राणि च सर्वाणि
ग्रहाश्च सहदेवताः ।
अहोरात्रे तथा सन्ध्ये
पान्तु त्वां वनमाश्रितम् ॥
May all the mountains and the seas, the King Varuṇa, the Heaven, the sky and all the stars, the planets along with their presiding deities, the earth and all its rivers, the day and night and the dawn and dusk protect you as you dwell in the Vana.
2.25.15
ऋतवश्चैव षट्पुण्या
मासास्संवत्सरास्तथा ।
कलाश्च काष्ठाश्च तथा
तव शर्म दिशन्तु ते ॥
The six auspicious seasons, the months and the years, in their every Kalās and Kāshtas, be comfortable to you!
Kalās and Kāshtas are minute fractions of time, like milliseconds and nanoseconds.
2.25.16
महावने विचरतो
मुनिवेषस्य धीमतः ।
तवादित्याश्च दैत्याश्च
भवन्तु सुखदास्सदा ॥
May the sons of Aditi and Diti, O wise one, bless you with comfort, as you tread in the great Vanas in the attire of a Muni!
2.25.17
राक्षसानां पिशाचानाम्
रौद्राणां क्रूरकर्मणाम् ।
क्रव्यादानां च सर्वेषाम्
मा भूत्पुत्रक ते भयम् ॥
May you be free, my child, from the fear of fierce demons and ghouls, and all the carrion-eaters.
2.25.18
प्लवगा वृश्चिका दंशाम्
अशकाश्चैव कानने ।
सरीसृपाश्च कीटाश्च
मा भूवन् गहने तव ॥
May no harm come to you in the dense forest from monkeys, scorpions, gnats, mosquitoes, reptiles and insects!
2.25.19
महाद्विपाश्च सिंहाश्च
व्याघ्रा ऋक्षाश्च दंष्ट्रिणः ।
महिषा श्शृङ्गिणो रौद्रा
न ते द्रुह्यन्तु पुत्रक ॥
May you not be troubled by the gigantic elephants, the fanged lions, tigers, bears and the ferocious horned buffalos!
2.25.20
नृमांसभोजना रौद्रा
ये चान्ये सत्वजातयः ।
मा च त्वां हिंसिषुः पुत्र
मया संपूजितास्त्विह ॥
May the fearsome breeds of animals which subsist on human flesh not trouble you, my son, for they are propitiated by me here!
2.25.21
आगमास्ते शिवास्सन्तु
सिध्यन्तु च पराक्रमाः ।
सर्वसम्पत्तये राम
स्वस्तिमान्गच्छ पुत्रक ॥
O Rāma, may your path in the Vana be safe for you! May your enterprises bear fruit! May all be well and may you accomplish all, my son, as you go!
2.25.22
स्वस्ति तेऽस्त्वन्तरिक्षेभ्यः
पार्थिवेभ्यः पुनः पुनः ।
सर्वेभ्यश्चैव देवेभ्यो
ये च वै परिपन्थिनः ॥
May you be safe from all the powers that lurk in the air and on the earth, that are adverse to you!
2.25.23
गुरुस्सोमश्च सूर्यश्च
धनदोऽथ यमस्तथा ।
पान्तु त्वामर्चिता राम
दण्डकारण्यवासिनम् ॥
May Bṛhaspati, Candra, Surya, Kubēra and Yama be worshiped and protect you, O Rāma, as you dwell in the Daṇḍaka forest.
2.25.24
अग्निर्वायुस्तथा धूमो
मन्त्राश्चर्षिमुखाच्च्युताः ।
उपस्पर्शनकाले तु
पान्तु त्वां रघुनन्दन ॥
May Agni and Vāyu and smoke, and the Mantras uttered by Ṛshis protect you when you take bath, O delight of the Raghu dynasty!
2.25.25
सर्वलोकप्रभुर्ब्रह्मा
भूतभर्ता तथर्षयः ।
ये च शेषास्सुरास्ते त्वाम्
रक्षन्तु वनवासिनम् ॥
May the lord of the worlds (Ṡiva), the prop of all beings (Vishṇu) and Brahma, and the Ṛshis and every other Dēva guard over you while you live in the Vana!
2.25.26
इति माल्यैस्सुरगणान्
गन्धैश्चापि यशस्विनी ।
स्तुतिभिश्चानुरूपाभिः
आनर्चाऽयतलोचना ॥
The woman of wide eyes and flawless renown, thus invoked and exalted the Dēvas, doing Pūja to them with garlands and fragrances.
2.25.27
ज्वलनं समुपादाय
ब्राह्मणेन महात्मना ।
हावयामास विधिना
राममङ्गलकारणात् ॥
After arranging a sacred fire, she had a Hōma performed in the customary manner by a Mahātma Brāhmaṇa for the welfare of Rāma.
2.25.28
घृतं श्वेतानि माल्यानि
समिधश्श्वेतसर्षपान् ।
उपसम्पादयामास
कौसल्या परमाङ्गना ॥
Kousalyā, the best among women, arranged Ghee, garlands of white flowers, Samidhas, white mustard seed for the Hōma.
2.25.29
उपाध्याय स्सविधिना
हुत्वा शान्तिमनामयम् ।
हुतहव्यावशेषेण
बाह्यं बलिमकल्पयत् ॥
The Brāhmaṇa performed Ṡānti Hōma strictly as per the procedures laid in the Ṡastras with no omissions or commissions. He used the remnants of Havya as oblation outside of the Hōma Kuṇḍa.
2.25.30
मधु दध्यक्षतघृतैः
स्वस्तिवाच्यद्विजांस्ततः ।
वाचयामास रामस्य
वनेस्वस्त्ययनक्रियाः ॥
She had the Brāhmaṇas pronounce benedictions, with honey, curds, rice and Ghee. Then she had them recite Mantras for the well being of Rāma in the Vana.
2.25.31
ततस्तस्मै द्विजेन्द्राय
राममाता यशस्विनी ।
दक्षिणां प्रददौ काम्याम्
राघवं चेदमब्रवीत् ॥
Then the mother of Rāma, of spotless renown, honored the Brāhmaṇas with as much remuneration as they asked for. Then she said to Rāghava:
2.25.32
यन्मङ्गलं सहस्राक्षे
सर्वदेवनमस्कृते ।
वृत्रनाशे समभवत्
तत्ते भवतु मङ्गलम् ॥
May the same good fortune be bestowed on you, that was bestowed on the Thousand-eyed, the one who is honored by all the gods, at the time of destroying Vṛtra.
2.25.33
यन्मङ्गलं सुपर्णस्य
विनताऽकल्पयत्पुरा ।
अमृतं प्रार्थयानस्य
तत्ते भवतु मङ्गलम् ॥
May the same good fortune be bestowed on you, that was bestowed by Vinata on Garuḍa when he was seeking Amṛta.
2.25.34
अमृतोत्पादने दैत्यान्
घ्नतो वज्रधरस्य यत् ।
अदितिर्मङ्गलं प्रादात्
तत्ते भवतु मङ्गलम् ॥
May the same good fortune be bestowed on you, that was bestowed by Aditi on Indra, as he was decimating the Daityas at the time of extracting Amṛta.
2.25.35
त्रीन्विक्रमान्प्रक्रमतो
विष्णोरमिततेजसः ।
यदासीन्मङ्गलं राम
तत्ते भवतु मङ्गलम् ॥
May the same good fortune be bestowed on you, that was bestowed on the supremely radiant Vishṇu at the time of measuring the world in three steps.
2.25.36
ऋतवस्सागरा द्वीपा
वेदा लोका दिशश्च ते ।
मङ्गलानि महाबाहो
दिशन्तु शुभमङ्गलाः ॥
May the prosperous and auspicious seasons, oceans, islands, Vēdas and worlds and cardinals, O you of mighty arm, bestow good fortune on you!
2.25.37
इति पुत्रस्य शेषांश्च
कृत्वा शिरसि भामिनी ।
गन्धैश्चापि समालभ्य
राममायतलोचना ।
ओषधीं चापि सिद्धार्थाम्
विशल्यकरणीं शुभाम् ।
चकार रक्षां कौसल्या
मन्त्रैरभिजजाप च ॥
Then that wide eyed lady kept Akshatas on her son's head and applied sandal paste on his body. Next, Kousalyā tied a talisman of proven powers, which was also capable of removing impurities from the body, as a protection and muttered incantations over it.
2.25.39
उवाचातिप्रहृष्टेव
सा दुःखवशवर्तिनी ।
वाङ्ग्मात्रेण न भावेन
वाचाऽसंसज्जमानया ॥
Deeply engulfed by sadness, but pretending to be cheerful, she said these words in a faltering voice, that sure came only out of her lips, but not out of her heart.
2.25.40
आनम्य मूर्ध्नि चाघ्राय
परिष्वज्य यशस्विनी ।
अवदत्पुत्र सिद्धार्थो
गच्छ राम यथासुखम् ॥
After smelling his head and giving him a hug, that woman of spotless renown said: "May you leave now in all comfort to fulfill your undertaking!"
2.25.41
अरोगं सर्वसिद्धार्थम्
अयोध्यां पुनरागतम् ।
पश्यामि त्वां सुखं वत्स
सुस्थितं राजवर्त्मनि ॥
My dear! I shall see you back in Ayōdhyā in good health with your purpose accomplished and comfortably established in the kingdom.
2.25.42
प्रणष्टदुःखसङ्कल्पा
हर्षविद्योतितानना ।
द्रक्ष्यामि त्वां वनात्प्राप्तम्
पूर्णचन्द्रमिवोदितम् ॥
Freed from all my grief, and with my face sparkling in joy, I shall behold you returning from the Vana like the rising full moon.
2.25.43
भद्रासनगतं राम
वनवासादिहागतम् ।
द्रक्ष्यामि च पुनस्त्वां तु
तीर्णवन्तं पितुर्वचः ॥
O Rāma! I shall see you again, occupying the auspicious chair of state, after returning from your stay in the Vana and thus redeeming the word of your father!
2.25.44
मङ्गलैरुपसंपन्नो
वनवासादिहागतः ।
वध्वा मम च नित्यं त्वम्
कामान्संवर्ध याहि भोः ॥
O Rāma, may you leave now, but return from your stay in the Vana with everything boding well for you, to bestow on me and the daughter-in-law everything that we would wish for!
2.25.45
मयाऽर्चिता देवगणाश्शिवादयो
महर्षयो भूतमहासुरोरगाः ।
अभिप्रयातस्य वनं चिराय ते
हितानि काङ्क्षन्तु दिशश्च राघव ! ॥
O Rāghava! May the hosts of the gods, Ṡiva and others, the Maharshis, the mighty Asuras and Uragas and the four cardinals, who have received my worship, wish you well for long, as you set out for your stay in the Vana!
2.25.46
इतीव साऽश्रुप्रतिपूर्णलोचना
समाप्य च स्वस्त्ययनं यथाविधि ।
प्रदक्षिणं चैव चकार राघवं
पुनः पुनश्चापि निपीड्य सस्वजे ॥
Her eyes brimming with tears, she finished the pronouncing of blessings in a customary manner. Then she circumambulated Rāghava and gave him her tight embrace repeatedly.
2.25.47
तथा तु देव्या स कृतप्रदक्षिणो
निपीड्य मातुश्चरणौ पुनः पुनः ।
जगाम सीतानिलयं महायशा
स्स राघवः प्रज्वलित स्स्वया श्रिया ॥
After the queen circumambulated him, that man of great repute touched her feet repeatedly and then left for the residence of Seetā, glowing with the splendor that he was naturally blessed with!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे पञ्चविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty fifth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
