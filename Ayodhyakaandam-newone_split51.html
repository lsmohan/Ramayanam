Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 51
In this Sarga, Guha offers a comfortable bed to Lakshmaṇa and requests him to take rest. Guha promises that he will keep vigil for the night and that he is very much capable of holding up any intruder. Lakshmaṇa appreciates the gesture, but says that he cannot think of sleep while Rāma lay on floor along with Seetā.
Then he cries out to Guha, thinking of all possible ill-consequences including the death of Daṡaratha, Kousalyā and his mother. He is extremely doubtful that they would at all return from the Vana to see Daṡaratha safe and sound.
2.51.1
तं जाग्रतमदम्भेन
भ्रातुरर्थाय लक्ष्मणम् ।
गुहः सन्तापसन्तप्तो
राघवं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Guha, feeling greatly distressed, spoke in all sincerity to Lakshmaṇa, the prince of Raghu dynasty, who was keeping vigil on his brother:
2.51.2
इयं तात सुखा शय्या
त्वदर्थमुपकल्पिता ।
प्रत्याश्वसिहि साध्वस्याम्
राजपुत्र यथासुखम् ॥
O dear! This comfortable bed has been arranged for you. O prince! Please take rest comfortably on this bed.
2.51.3
उचितोऽयं जनस्सर्वः
क्लेशानां त्वं सुखोचितः ।
गुप्त्यर्थं जागरिष्यामः
काकुत्स्थस्य वयं निशाम् ।
न हि रामात्प्रियतमो
ममास्ति भुवि कश्चन ।
ब्रवीम्येतदहं सत्यम्
सत्येनैव च ते शपे ॥
We are accustomed to the rough and tough life, whereas you are accustomed to comfort. We will stay awake during night to keep vigil for Rāma. No one is dearer to me in this world than Rāma. I swear on truth itself that I mean what I say.
2.51.5
अस्य प्रसादादाशंसे
लोकेस्मिन् सुमहद्यशः ।
धर्मावाप्तिं च विपुलाम्
अर्थावाप्तिं च केवलम् ॥
It is by his grace alone that I will be able to conduct myself in accordance with Dharma, gain abundant prosperity and keep a good repute in this world.
2.51.6
सोऽहं प्रियसखं रामम्
शयानं सह सीतया ।
रक्षिष्यामि धनुष्पाणिः
सर्वतो ज्ञातिभिस्सह ॥
Hence, with this bow in my hand and along with all my kinsmen, I will guard my dear friend Rāma who is resting along with Seetā from every danger.
2.51.7
न हि मेऽविदितं किञ्चित्
वनेऽस्मिंश्चरतस्सदा ।
चतुरङ्गं ह्यपि बलम्
सुमहत्प्रसहेमहि ॥
There is nothing that I am not familiar with here, since I roam about this Vana all the time. We can hold up even a huge army equipped with all the four divisions.
2.51.8
लक्ष्मणस्तं तदोवाच
रक्ष्यमाणास्त्वयानघ ।
नात्र भीता वयं सर्वे
धर्ममेवानुपश्यता ॥
Then Lakshmaṇa said to him: "O sinless one! Protected by you in accordance with Dharma, we shall find nothing to fear here!"
2.51.9
कथं दशरथौ भूमौ
शयाने सह सीतया ।
शक्या निद्रा मया लब्धुम्
जीवितं वा सुखानि वा ॥
But how can I get sleep, or for that matter, think of living or of any comfort while the son of Daṡaratha is sleeping on the bare floor along with Seetā?
2.51.10
यो न देवासुरैः सर्वैः
शक्यः प्रसहितुं युधि ।
तं पश्य सुखसंविष्टम्
तृणेषु सह सीतया ॥
See how he, who cannot be faced in a fight even by all the Dēvas and Asuras combined, is finding comfort sleeping on a bed of grass along with Seetā!
2.51.11
यो मन्त्रतपसा लब्धो
विविधैश्च पराश्रमैः ।
एको दशरथस्येष्टः
पुत्रः सदृशलक्षणः ॥
It is upon going through much efforts and Tapa that Daṡaratha begot this dearest son who took after him in every way.
2.51.12
अस्मिन् प्रव्राजिते राजा
न चिरं वर्तयिष्यति ।
विधवा मेदिनी नूनम्
क्षिप्रमेव भविष्यति ॥
With him in exile like this, the king may not survive for long. The earth is soon destined to be a widow.
2.51.13
विनद्य सुमहानादम्
श्रमेणोपरताः स्त्रियः ।
निर्घोषोपरतं चातो
मन्ये राजनिवेशनम् ॥
By now, I think the women of the king's palace would have become exhausted by crying aloud bitterly and the place would have become all quiet.
2.51.14
कौसल्या चैव राजा च
तथैव जननी मम ।
नाशंसे यदि जीवन्ति
सर्वे ते शर्वरीमिमाम् ॥
I doubt very much whether Kousalyā and the King and my mother likewise will survive through this night.
2.51.15
जीवेदपि हि मे माता
शत्रुघ्नस्यान्ववेक्षया ।
तद्दुःखं यत्तु कौसल्या
वीरसूर्विनशिष्यति ॥
May be, my mother will survive, looking forward to being with Ṡatrughna; but should Kousalyā, mother of a heroic son, die, that will be of great grief to me.
2.51.16
अनुरक्तजनाकीर्णा
सुखालोकप्रियावहा ।
राजव्यसनसंसृष्टा
सा पुरी विनशिष्यति ॥
That city, full of loving people, pleasant to live in, and a source of happiness to everyone, will perish, grieving for her lost King.
2.51.17
कथं पुत्रं महात्मानम्
ज्येष्ठं प्रियमपश्यतः ।
शरीरं धारयिष्यन्ति
प्राणा राज्ञो महात्मनः ॥
For how can life remain in the body of that Mahātma king, when he can no longer see his beloved eldest son, a Mahātma?
2.51.18
विनष्टे नृपतौ पश्चात्
कौसल्या विनशिष्यति ।
अनन्तरं च माताऽपि
मम नाशमुपैष्यति ॥
As the king loses his life, Kousalyā will die; and then my mother too will perish.
2.51.19
अतिक्रान्तमतिक्रान्तम्
अनवाप्य मनोरथम् ।
राज्ये राममनिक्षिप्य
पिता मे विनशिष्यति ॥
Unable to realize his long cherished dream of installing Rāma in the kingdom, my father will die.
2.51.20
सिद्धार्थाः पितरं वृत्तम्
तस्मिन्कालेऽप्युपस्थिते ।
प्रेतकार्येषु सर्वेषु
संस्करिष्यन्ति भूमिपम् ॥
Those who perform the final rites for my father, the dead King, when that time comes, will indeed be fortunate.
2.51.21
रम्यचत्वरसंस्थानाम्
सुविभक्तमहापथाम् ।
हर्म्यप्रासादसम्पन्नाम्
गणिकावरशोभिताम् ।
रथाश्वगजसम्बाधाम्
तूर्यनादविनादिताम् ।
सर्वकल्याणसम्पूर्णाम्
हृष्टपुष्टजनाकुलाम् ।
आरामोद्यानसम्पन्नाम्
समाजोत्सवशालिनीम् ।
सुखिता विचरिष्यन्ति
राजधानीं पितुर्मम ॥
Then they will comfortably stroll about in the capital city of my father, of lovely squares, divided highways, mansions and palaces, chariots, elephants and horses, echoing the sounds of trumpets, splendid with the fairest of courtesans, full of auspicious qualities, filled with contented and joyful people, dotted with gardens and open spaces, and many a social celebration.
2.51.24
अपि जीवेद्दशरथो
वनवासात्पुनर्वयम् ।
प्रत्यागम्य महात्मानम्
अपि पश्येम सुव्रतम् ॥
Would Daṡaratha be alive perchance? Would we, after returning from our stay in Vana, be able to behold that Mahātma, who is steadfast in his vows?
2.51.25
अपि सत्यप्रतिज्ञेन
सार्धं कुशलिना वयम् ।
निवृत्ते वनवासेऽस्मिन्
अयोध्यां प्रविशेमहि ॥
After the completion of our stay in Vana, will we be able to enter Ayōdhyā and see him safe, sound and having made good his word?
2.51.26
परिदेवयमानस्य
दुखार्तस्य महात्मनः ।
तिष्ठतो राजपुत्रस्य
शर्वरी साऽत्यवर्तत ॥
The night wore out, as that son of the king, the Mahātma, racked with grief, stood by and wailed thus.
2.51.27
तथा हि सत्यं ब्रुवति प्रजाहिते
नरेन्द्रपुत्रे गुरुसौहृदाद्गुहः ।
मुमोच बाष्पं व्यसनाभिपीडितो
ज्वरातुरो नाग इव व्यथातुरः ॥
When the King's son, who sought the welfare of everyone, thus spoke in all honesty out of love for his elders, Guha shed tears, feeling distressed and sad like an elephant that had caught a bad fever.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्यकाण्डे एकपञ्चाश स्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty first Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
