Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 35
In this Sarga, Seetā asks Hanumān to tell about the distinguishing marks of Rāma and how he came to meet Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, so that she can trust him to be who he says he is.
Hanumān then describes to her in detail Rāma’s physique and other distinguishing marks and characteristics and how Rāma came to meet Sugreeva, how a friendship developed between the two, how Rāma killed Vali and established Sugreeva as the king, how Sugreeva sent the Vānaras in search of her, how the Vānaras led by Aṅgada lost their way on the Vindhya mountain, how Saṃpāti told them that she is Rāvaṇa’s house, how the Vānaras came to the northern shore of the ocean and how Hanumān jumped over the ocean to reach Laṅkā and finally got to see her in person.
Hanumān assures her that Rāma would come soon and take her back after killing Rāvaṇa and all his kith and kin.
5.35.1
तां तु रामकथां श्रुत्वा
वैदेही वानरर्षभात् ।
उवाच वचनं सान्त्वम्
इदं मधुरया गिरा ॥
On hearing the story of Rāma from that bull among Vānaras, Vaidēhee said these conciliatory words in her sweet voice:
5.35.2
क्व ते रामेण संसर्गः
कथं जानासि लक्ष्मणम् ।
वानराणां नराणां च
कथमासीत्समागमः ॥
How did you come to meet Rāma? How did you come to know Lakshmaṇa? How did humans and Vānaras happen to come together?
5.35.3
यानि रामस्य लिङ्गानि
लक्ष्मणस्य च वानर ।
तानि भूयस्समाचक्ष्व
न मां शोकस्समाविशेत् ॥
Describe to me again, O Vānara, the distinctive marks by which Rāma and Lakshmaṇa may be known, hearing which sorrow may not find its way again into my heart.
5.35.4
कीदृशं तस्य संस्थानम्
रूपं रामस्य कीदृशम् ।
कथमूरू कथं बाहू
लक्ष्मणस्य च शंस मे ॥
What kind of physique does Rāma have? How does he look? What kind of legs and arms does he have? Tell me also the same about Lakshmaṇa!
5.35.5
एवमुक्तस्तु वैदेह्या
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ।
ततो रामं यथातत्त्वम्
आख्यातुमुपचक्रमे ॥
Thus asked by Vaidēhee, Hanumān, son of Vāyu, began to tell about Rāma, in all detail:
5.35.6
जानन्ती बत दिष्ट्या माम्
वैदेहि परिपृच्छसि ।
भर्तुः कमलपत्त्राक्षि
संस्थानं लक्ष्मणस्य च ॥
O Vaidēhee of lotus-petal eyes! What can I be happier about? It is only my good fortune that you ask me to describe the physique of your husband and the looks of Lakshmaṇa, even though you know them all too well.
5.35.7
यानि रामस्य चिह्नानि
लक्ष्मणस्य च यानि वै ।
लक्षितानि विशालाक्षि
वदतश्शृणु तानि मे ॥
O you of wide eyes! I shall tell you about all the marks of identification of Rāma and the distinguishing marks of Lakshmaṇa, please listen!
5.35.8
रामः कमलपत्त्राक्षः
सर्वसत्त्वमनोहरः ।
रूपदाक्षिण्यसम्पन्नः
प्रसूतो जनकात्मजे ॥
O daughter of Janaka! Rāma’s eyes are like lotus petals. He ravishes the hearts of every creature. He is, by birth, stunningly charming and amazingly skillful.
5.35.9
तेजसाऽदित्य सङ्काशः
क्षमया पृथिवीसमः ।
बृहस्पतिसमो बुद्ध्या
यशसा वासवोपमः ॥
He is comparable to the sun in radiance, to the earth in forbearance, to Bṛhaspati in intellect and to Vāsava in renown.
5.35.10
रक्षिता जीवलोकस्य
स्वजनस्याभिरक्षिता ।
रक्षिता स्वस्य वृत्तस्य
धर्मस्य च परन्तपः ॥
He, the scorcher of foe, is the protector of all the creatures of the world, and especially his people. He zealously guards his integrity and also Dharma.
5.35.11
रामो भामिनि लोकस्य
चातुर्वर्ण्यस्य रक्षिता ।
मर्यादानां च लोकस्य
कर्ता कारयिता च सः ॥
O charming lady! He is the protector of the people of all four Varṇas in this world. He sets the order in the world and ensures that it is followed.
5.35.12
अर्चिष्मानर्चितोऽत्यर्थम्
ब्रह्मचर्यव्रते स्थितः ।
साधूनामुपकारज्ञः
प्रचारज्ञश्च कर्मणाम् ॥
Blazing with splendor, he is the object of everyone’s utmost admiration. He always keeps the big picture in mind. He is always at the service of good people and understands the nature and value of their deeds.
5.35.13
राजविद्याविनीतश्च
ब्राह्मणानामुपासिता ।
श्रुतवान्शीलसम्पन्नो
विनीतश्च परन्तपः ॥
He is well-trained in statecraft. He treats Brāhmaṇas with regard. He is learned in Ṡāstras. He is endowed with a great character. He is humble. He torments his foe.
5.35.14
यजुर्वेदविनीतश्च
वेदविद्भिस्सुपूजितः ।
धनुर्वेदे च वेदेषु
वेदाङ्गेषु च निष्ठितः ॥
He is trained in Yajurvēda. He is well respected by those who know the Vēdas. The science of archery and all the Vēdas and auxiliary sciences are of his constant study.
5.35.15
विपुलांसो महाबाहुः
कम्बुग्रीवश्शुभाननः ।
गूढजत्रुस्सुताम्राक्षो
रामो देवि जनैश्श्रुतः ॥
O Dēvi! He has broad shoulders and mighty arms. His neck is like conch and his face is lovely. The dense muscle of his chest makes his rib cage undetectable. His eyes are copper (honey) red. There is no one who has not heard of Rāma.
5.35.16
दुन्दुभिस्वननिर्घोष
स्निग्धवर्णः प्रतापवान् ।
सम स्समविभक्ताङ्गो
वर्णं श्यामं समाश्रितः ॥
His voice booms like kettle drum. He has a glowing blackish complexion. He is valiant and his limbs are symmetrical.
5.35.17
त्रिस्थिरस्त्रिप्रलम्बश्च
त्रिसमस्त्रिषु चोन्नतः ।
त्रिताम्रस्त्रिषु च स्निग्धो
गम्भीरस्त्रिषु नित्यशः ॥
Three are sturdy, three are long, three are symmetrical, three are raised, three are reddish, three are silky and three are imposing in him.
Ṡlōkas 17 through 20 refer to the features defined in Sāmudrika Sastra (the science of bodily features).
Ṡlōka 17
The sturdy three refer to chest, wrist and fist, symbols of royalty - uraṡca maṇibandhaṡca mushṭiṡca nṛpatē sthirāḥ
The long three refer to eyebrows, arms, testicles, symbols of opulence - pralaṃmabāyasya sa dhanī trayō bhrūmushkabāhavaḥ
The symmetrical three refer to hair, testicles and shanks, also symbols of royalty - kēṡāgraṃ vṛshaṇaṃ jānu samāyasya sa bhūpatiḥ
The raised three refer to chest, navel and lower stomach, also symbols of royalty - nābhyantaḥkukshivakshōbhirunnataḥ kshitipō bhavēt
The reddish three refer to the corners of the eye, nails and the pairs of palms and feet, symbolic of one who is born to enjoy life - nētrāntanakhapāṇyaṅghritalaistāmraiḥ sukhī bhavēt
The silky three refer to lines on the feet, hair, scrotum, symbolic of one who is born to be opulent - snigdhā bhavanti vai yēshāṃ pādarēkhāḥ ṡirōruhāḥ tathā liṅgamaṇistēshāṃ mahābhāgyaṃ vinirdiṡēt
The imposing three refer to voice, gait and navel, symbolic of one who commands immediate respect - svarē gatau ca nābhau ca gambhīrastrishuṡasyatē
Ṡlōka 18
The three places in which his figure was flat are breasts, nipples and lines (where wrinkles develop in old age, such as on stomach, neck and forehead) - pīnōpacitairnimnaiḥkshitipatayaḥ
cūcukaiḥstanaiḥ sukhinaḥ
snigdhānimnārēkhādhanināṃ
tadvyatyayēna nissvānām
The four parts in which his body was not oversized are neck, penis, back and shanks -
grīvā prajananaṃ pṛshṭhaṃ hrasvaṃ jaṅaghā ca pūjyatē
Three swirls in the hair on the head indicate that the person can manage and lead the entire earth - āvarta trayaruciraṃ yasya ṡiraḥ sa kshitibhṛtāṃ nētā
The number of lines (folds) at the base of the thumb indicate the number of Vēdas in which the person becomes an expert - mūlē'ṅgushṭhasya vēdānāṃ catasrastisra ēva vā ēkā dvē vā yathāyōgaṃ rēkhā jñēyā dvijanmanām
The number of lines (folds) on the forehead indicate the longevity of a person; one with a single line lives for twenty years, one with two lines lives for sixty years, one with three lines lives for hundred years and one with four lives for two hundred years -
lalāṭē yasya dṛṡyantē catustridvēkarēkhikāḥ ṡatadvayaṃ ṡataṃ shashṭhistasyāyurviṃṡatistathā
One who has lines underneath the feet or in the palm in the shapes of Vajra, flag post, conch or a goad would become a lord of people - yasya pādatalē vajradhvajaṡaṅkhāṅkuṡōpamāḥ rēkhāḥ samyak prakāṡantē manujēndraṃ tamādiṡēt pāṇau catastō rēkhāṡca yasya tishṭhantyabhaṅgurāḥ
Kishku is a measure of twenty four inches. Rāma was four Kishkus tall, or ninety six inches tall. One who is four Kishkus tall is considered an equal of Dēvas - shaṇṇavatyaṅgulōtsēdhō yaḥ pumān sa divaukasaḥ
The symmetrical four are the arms, thighs, knees and shanks - bāhujānūrujaṅghāṡca catvāryatha samāni ca
Ṡlōka 19
A person whose fourteen body parts that come in pairs - eyebrows, nostrils, eyes, ears, lips, nipples, elbows, wrists, shanks, testicles, pelvic bones, hands, feet and hips, are symmetrical is said to have the potential to command the earth - bhṛvau nāsāpuṭē nētrē karṇāvōshṭhau ca cūcukē kūrparau maṇibandhau ca jānunī vṛshaṇau kaṭī karau pādau sphijau yasya samau jñēyaḥ sa bhūpatiḥ
Shiny strong Daṃshṭras (canine, first premolar, second premolar, first molar) are considered to be auspicious.
snigdhā ghanāṡca daṡanāḥ sutīkshṇadaṃshṭrāḥ samāṡca ṡubhāḥ
His gait is comparable to that of four majestic beasts - lion, tiger, elephant and bull.
A person whose five body parts - eyes, teeth, skin, feet and hair, are shiny are said to lord over people. It is said that one with shiny eyes enjoys luxuries, one with shiny teeth begets good food, one with shiny skin begets a comfortable bed, one with shiny feet begets comfortable vehicles and one with shiny hair leads a comfortable life - cakshuḥ snēhēna saubhāgyaṃ dantasnēhēna bhōjanam tvacaḥ snēhēna ṡayanaṃ pāda snēhēna vāhanam snigdhanīlamṛdukuñcitāstathā mūrdhajāḥ sukhakarāḥ
The eight body parts are - two arms, two thighs, two shanks and the set of ten toes and the set of ten fingers - bāhū ca nalakau cūōrū jaṅghēcētyashṭhavaṃṡakāḥ
Ṡlōka 20
The ten delicate parts refer to face, eyes, mouth, tongue, lips, cheeks, breasts, nails, palms and feet -
mukhanētrāsyajihvōshṭhatālu stana nakhāḥ karau pādau ca daṡa padmāni padmākārāṇi yasya ca
The ten big parts refer to head, forehead, ears, neck, chest, heart, mouth, hands, legs and back - ṡirōlalāṭē ṡravaṇē grīvā vakshaṡca hṛttathā udaraṃ pāṇipādau ca pṛshṭhaṃ daṡa bṛhanti ca
The three with which the world is filled refer to his radiance, renown and wealth - tribhirvāptaṡca yasya syāttējasā yaṡasā ṡriyā
The two that are blemishless refer to the lineages on both sides, i.e., on the father’s side and mother’s side. - dvau ṡuklau tu ṡubhau ṡuddhau vaṃṡau mātuḥ pitastathā
The six that are prominent refer to armpits, stomach, chest, nose, shoulder blades and forehead - kakshaḥ kukshiṡca vakshaṡca ghrāṇaskandhau lalāṭikā sarvabhūtēshu nirdishṭā unnatāṅgāḥ ṡubhapradāḥ
A person whose nine parts - finger segments, hair on the head, hair on the body, nails, skin, penis, mustache, eyesight and intellect, are sharp are said to enjoy a long life - sūkshmāṇyaṅguliparvāṇi kēṡarōmanakhatvacaḥ ṡēphaṡca yēshāṃ sūkshmāṇi tē narā dīrghajīvinaḥ asphuṭitāgraṃ sūkshmaṃ ṡmaṡru ṡubhaṃ mṛdu ca saṃtataṃ snigdham sūkshmadṛk skshmadṛk sūkshmabuddiṡca yō naraḥ sa sukhī bhavēt
The goals refer to Dharma, Artha, Kāma and the three times refer to morning, noon and afternoon.
5.35.18
त्रिवलीवांस्त्र्यवनतः
चतुर्व्यङ्गस्त्रिशीर्षवान् ।
चतुष्कलश्चतुर्लेखः
चतुष्किष्कुश्चतुस्समः ॥
He has three lines on his stomach. His figure is flat in three places. He is not oversized in four parts. There are three swirls in his hair on the head, four cross lines (on the base of the thumb) four lines across (on forehead). He is four Kishkus tall and symmetrical in the four.
5.35.19
चतुर्दशसमद्वन्द्वः
चतुर्दंष्ट्रश्चतुर्गतिः ।
महोष्ठहनुनासश्च
पञ्चस्निग्धोऽष्टवंशवान् ॥
His fourteen pairs are symmetrical. The edges of his four teeth are sharp. His gait is comparable to that of four. His lips, chin and nose are prominent. Five of his body parts glow with sheen, and eight are shapely.
5.35.20
दशपद्मो दशबृहत्
त्रिभिर्व्याप्तो द्विशुक्लवान् ।
षडुन्नतो नवतनुः
त्रिभिर्व्याप्नोति राघवः ॥
The ten body parts of Rāghava are like lotuses and ten are big. His is spread throughout the world with three and both of his (lineages) are blemishless. The six of his body parts are prominent and the nine are sharp. He realizes (his goals) at all the three (times).
5.35.21
सत्यधर्मपरश्श्रीमान्
सङ्ग्रहानुग्रहे रतः ।
देशकालविभागज्ञः
सर्वलोकप्रियंवदः ॥
He, the blessed one, steadfastly adheres to Truth and Dharma. He is ever interested in gathering (people) around him and bestows grace on them. He knows the importance of utilizing the time and space at his disposal properly. He speaks courteously to all people.
5.35.22
भ्राता तस्य च वैमात्रः
सॏमित्रिरपराजितः ।
अनुरागेण रूपेण
गुणैश्चैव तथाविधः ॥
Sowmitri, his step brother, who knows no defeat from anyone, is exactly like him in his looks, virtues and in showering affection.
5.35.23
तावुभौ नरशार्दूलौ
त्वद्दर्शनसमुत्सुकौ ।
विचिन्वन्तौ महीं कृत्स्नाम्
अस्माभिरभिसङ्गतौ ॥
Those tigers among men, eager to find you, went searching all over the earth, and that is when they happened to meet us.
5.35.24
त्वामेव मार्गमाणौ तौ
विचरन्तौ वसुन्धराम् ।
ददर्शतुर्मृगपतिम्
पूर्वजेनावरोपितम् ।
ऋश्यमूकस्य पृष्ठे तु
बहुपादपसङ्कुले ।
भ्रातुर्भयार्तमासीनम्
सुग्रीवं प्रियदर्शनम् ॥
Seeking you and searching the entire earth, both of them happened to see Sugreeva of pleasing disposition, the lord of beasts, who was dislodged (from the kingdom) by his elder brother and was distraught with fear from him, sitting on the top of the Ṛshyamūka (mountain) which was thickly covered with trees.
5.35.26
वयं तु हरिराजं तम्
सुग्रीवं सत्यसङ्गरम् ।
परिचर्यामहे राज्यात्
पूर्वजेनावरोपितम् ॥
And we took upon ourselves to serve Sugreeva, the king of Vānaras, who holds himself to the promises he makes, after he was dislodged from the kingdom by his elder brother.
5.35.27
ततस्तौ चीरवसनौ
धनुः प्रवरपाणिनौ ।
ऋश्यमूकस्य शैलस्य
रम्यं देशमुपागतौ ॥
Then, those two, clad in tree bark, and holding powerful bows in their hands, came to the beautiful area of Ṛshyamūka mountain.
5.35.28
स तौ दृष्ट्वा नरव्याघ्रौ
धन्विनौ वानरर्षभः ।
अवप्लुतो गिरेस्तस्य
शिखरं भयमोहितः ॥
On seeing those two tigers among men armed with bows, the bull among Vānaras, confounded by terror, jumped up to the peak of the mountain.
5.35.29
ततस्स शिखरे तस्मिन्
वानरेन्द्रो व्यवस्थितः ।
तयोस्समीपं मामेव
प्रेषयामास सत्वरम् ॥
Then that Lord of Vānaras, stationing himself on that crest of the mountain, sent me specifically to them in all haste.
5.35.30
तावहं पुरुषव्याघ्रौ
सुग्रीववचनात्प्रभू ।
रूपलक्षणसम्पन्नौ
कृताञ्जलिरुपस्थितः ॥
Then I, taking instructions from Sugreeva, went to the presence of those two tigers among men, who were handsome and endowed with rich looks, with my palms joined in reverence.
5.35.31
तौ परिज्ञाततत्त्वार्थौ
मया प्रीतिसमन्वितौ ।
पृष्ठमारोप्य तं देशम्
प्रापितौ पुरुषर्षभौ ॥
I carried them on my back to that place (where Sugreeva was), after I earned their pleasure by telling them everything as it was and in full detail.
5.35.32
निवेदितौ च तत्त्वेन
सुग्रीवाय महात्मने ।
तयोरन्योन्यसल्लापात्
भृशं प्रीतिरजायत ॥
Then they told everything about them in detail to Mahātma Sugreeva. As they kept talking, a deep friendship developed among themselves.
5.35.33
ततस्तौ प्रीतिसम्पन्नौ
हरीश्वरनरेश्वरौ ।
परस्परकृताश्वासौ
कथया पूर्ववृत्तया ॥
The lord of men and the lord of Vānaras, filled with affection for each other, consoled each other and shared their respective stories that had happened thus far.
5.35.34
ततस्स सान्त्वयामास
सुग्रीवं लक्ष्मणाग्रजः ।
स्त्रीहेतोर्वालिना भ्रात्रा
निरस्तमुरुतेजसा ॥
The elder brother of Lakshmaṇa consoled Sugreeva, who had been cast away at a far off place by his brother Vāli of immense power for the sake of a woman.
5.35.35
ततस्त्वन्नाशजं शोकम्
रामस्याक्लिष्टकर्मणः ।
लक्ष्मणो वानरेन्द्राय
सुग्रीवाय न्यवेदयत् ॥
Then Lakshmaṇa told the Lord of Vānaras about the grief that Rāma, who does everything with ease, has been afflicted with after he lost you.
5.35.36
स श्रुत्वा वानरेन्द्रस्तु
लक्ष्मणेनेरितं वचः ।
तदासीन्निष्प्रभोऽत्यर्थम्
ग्रहग्रस्त इवांशुमान् ॥
Hearing the words that Lakshmaṇa told, the Lord of Vānaras felt so depressed that he looked like the sun eclipsed by the planet.
5.35.37
ततस्त्वद्गात्रशोभीनि
रक्षसा ह्रियमाणया ।
यान्याभरणजालानि
पातितानि महीतले ।
तानि सर्वाणि रामाय
आनीय हरियूथपाः ।
संहृष्टा दर्शयामासुः
गतिं तु न विदुस्तव ॥
The chief of Vānaras was then happy to bring and present to Rāma the jewels that had adorned your body that you had dropped down when you were being carried off by the Rākshasa to a place about which there was no clue.
5.35.39
तानि रामाय दत्तानि
मयैवोपहृतानि च ।
स्वनवन्त्यवकीर्णानि
तस्मिन्विगतचेतसि ॥
Then it was I who happened to bring and give them to Rāma. On seeing them, he fainted and they (slipped from his hand) and got scattered around, making a clinking noise.
5.35.40
तान्यङ्के दर्शनीयानि
कृत्वा बहुविधं तव ।
तेन देवप्रकाशेन
देवेन परिदेवितम् ॥
Then keeping those beautiful ornaments on his lap, that lord, whose splendor resembled that of Dēvas, lamented in many ways over your loss.
5.35.41
पश्यतस्तानि रुदतः
ताम्यतश्च पुनः पुनः ।
प्रादीपयन्दाशरथेः
तानि शोकहुताशनम् ॥
As he cried and wept repeatedly looking at them, those ornaments only fanned the fire of his grief.
5.35.42
शयितं च चिरं तेन
दुःखार्तेन महात्मना ।
मयाऽपि विविधैर्वाक्यैः
कृच्छ्रादुत्थापितः पुनः ॥
Then that Mahātma slumped on the ground distraught with sorrow and lay there for long when I, with great difficulty, helped him get up, offering many a word (of consolation).
5.35.43
तानि दृष्ट्वा महाबाहुः
दर्शयित्वा मुहुर्मुहुः ।
राघवस्सह सौमित्रिः
सुग्रीवे स न्यवेदयत् ॥
Then Rāghava of mighty arm, along with Sowmitri, showed them (to everyone around) again and again and gave them back to Sugreeva (for safe keeping).
5.35.44
स तवादर्शनादार्ये
राघवः परितप्यते ।
महता ज्वलता नित्यम्
अग्निनेवाग्निपर्वतः ॥
O noble one! Not seeing you, Rāghava burns greatly and perpetually with grief, like fire in a volcano.
5.35.45
त्वत्कृते तमनिद्रा च
शोकश्चिन्ता च राघवम् ।
तापयन्ति महात्मानम्
अग्न्यगारमिवाग्नयः ॥
Unable to sleep, grieving and thinking about you Rāghava, the Mahātma, burns like a fire pit with flames arising from it.
5.35.46
तवादर्शनशोकेन
राघवः प्रविचाल्यते ।
महता भूमिकम्पेन
महानिव शिलोच्चयः ॥
Rāghava is shaken greatly by the grief of not seeing you, like a rocky mountain by an earthquake.
5.35.47
काननानि सुरम्याणि
नदीः प्रस्रवणानि च ।
चरन्न रतिमाप्नोति
त्वामपश्यन्नृपात्मजे ॥
O princess! Not seeing you, he finds no delight even in the lovely woods, streams and rivers.
5.35.48
स त्वां मनुजशार्दूलः
क्षिप्रं प्राप्स्यति राघवः ।
समित्रबान्धवं हत्वा
रावणं जनकात्मजे ॥
Nevertheless, O daughter of Janaka, Rāghava, the tiger among men, is going to get you back soon after killing Rāvaṇa along with his kith and kin.
5.35.49
सहितौ रामसुग्रीवौ
उभावकुरुतां तदा ।
समयं वालिनं हन्तुम्
तव चान्वेषणं तथा ॥
Then Rāma and Sugreeva came to an agreement with each other to kill Vāli and to search for you.
5.35.50
ततस्ताभ्यां कुमाराभ्याम्
वीराभ्यां स हरीश्वरः ।
किष्किन्धां समुपागम्य
वाली युद्धे निपातितः ॥
Then those two young Veeras went to Kishkindhā and struck down Vāli, the Lord of Vānaras, in a fight.
5.35.51
ततो निहत्य तरसा
रामो वालिनमाहवे ।
सर्वर्क्षहरिसङ्घानाम्
सुग्रीवमकरोत्पतिम् ॥
Then Rāma killed Vāli in the fight by his might and made Sugreeva the lord of all the Vānaras and Ṛkshas.
5.35.52
रामसुग्रीवयोरैक्यम्
देव्येवं समजायत ।
हनुमन्तं च मां विद्धि
तयोर्दूतमिहागतम् ॥
O Dēvi, a feeling of oneness developed between Rāma and Sugreeva. Know me to be Hanumān who has come here as their envoy.
5.35.53
स्वराज्यं प्राप्य सुग्रीवः
समानीय हरीश्वरान् ।
त्वदर्थं प्रेषयामास
दिशो दश महाबलान् ॥
Sugreeva, after getting the kingdom for himself, summoned all the leaders of Vānaras of immense strength and sent them in all the ten directions to look for you.
5.35.54
आदिष्टा वानरेन्द्रेण
सुग्रीवेण महौजसा ।
अद्रिराज प्रतीकाशा:
सर्वतः प्रस्थिता महीम् ॥
Ordered thus by the mighty strong Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras, they, each one humongous like the king of mountains, went all over the earth.
5.35.55
ततस्ते मार्गमाणा वै
सुग्रीववचनातुराः ।
चरन्ति वसुधां कृत्स्नाम्
वयमन्ये च वानराः ॥
Since then, we and other Vānaras have been going all around the earth following the command of Sugreeva warily.
5.35.56
अङ्गदो नाम लक्ष्मीवान्
वालिसूनुर्महाबलः ।
प्रस्थितः कपिशार्दूलः
त्रिभागबलसंवृतः ॥
The mighty strong blessed son of Vāli, Aṅgada by name, a tiger among Vānaras, set out with a third of the Vānara force.
5.35.57
तेषां नो विप्रणष्टानाम्
विन्ध्ये पर्वतसत्तमे ।
भृशं शोकपरीतानाम्
अहोरात्रगणा गताः ॥
We, who started thus, lost our way among the mighty mountain range of the Vindhyas, and spent many scores of nights and days overwhelmed by great grief.
5.35.58
ते वयं कार्यनैराश्यात्
कालस्यातिक्रमेण च ।
भयाच्च कपिराजस्य
प्राणांस्त्यक्तुं व्यवस्थिताः ॥
Then we were all but set to give up our lives as we lost hope of accomplishing our mission, and as the time limit that was set had been exceeded and as we were afraid of the king of Vānaras.
5.35.59
विचित्य वनदुर्गाणि
गिरिप्रस्रवणानि च ।
अनासाद्य पदं देव्याः
प्राणांस्त्यक्तुं समुद्यताः ॥
Having searched the impenetrable Vanas, mountains, and streams, and having not found your whereabouts, we were all set to give up our lives.
5.35.60
दृष्ट्वा प्रायोपविष्टांश्च
सर्वान्वानरपुङ्गवान् ।
भृशं शोकार्णवे मग्नः
पर्यदेवयदङ्गदः ।
तव नाशं च वैदेहि
वालिनश्च वधं तथा ।
प्रायोपवेशमस्माकम्
मरणं च जटायुषः ॥
Seeing all of us, the bulls among Vānaras, ready to give up our lives by fasting, Aṅgada plunged in an ocean of grief, and lamented, dwelling on your loss, the slaying of Vāli, our plan to fast unto death and the death of Jaṭāyu, O Vaidēhee!
5.35.62
तेषां नः स्वामिसन्देशात्
निराशानां मुमूर्षताम् ।
कार्यहेतोरिवायातः
शकुनिर्वीर्यवान्महान् ॥
We gave up the hope of carrying out the mission laid out by our master, and were ready to take our lives. Right then, as if it were just to help us with the mission, a huge powerful bird came over there.
5.35.63
गृध्रराजस्य सोदर्यः
सम्पातिर्नाम गृध्रराट् ।
श्रुत्वा भ्रातृवधं कोपात्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
That king of eagles, Saṃpāti by name, the brother of the king of eagles, became enraged hearing the news of the slaying of his brother (by Rāvaṇa) and said these words:
5.35.64
यवीयान्केन मे भ्राता
हतः क्व च निपातितः ।
एतदाख्यातुमिच्छामि
भवद्भिर्वानरोत्तमाः ॥
O best of Vānaras! Who killed my younger brother? Where was he struck down? I would like you to tell me about it all!
5.35.65
अङ्गदोऽकथयत्तस्य
जनस्थाने महद्वधम् ।
रक्षसा भीमरूपेण
त्वामुद्दिश्य यथातथम् ॥
Then Aṅgada told him about the slaying of that great bird by the terrible looking Rākshasa in Janasthāna, for the sake of you, just as it happened.
5.35.66
जटायुषो वधं श्रुत्वा
दुःखितस्सोऽरुणात्मजः ।
त्वां शशंस वरारोहे
वसन्तीं रावणालये ॥
Hearing about the killing of Jaṭāyu, the son of Aruṇa felt deeply saddened. later, he told that you are in Rāvaṇa’s house.
5.35.67
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
सम्पातेः प्रीतिवर्धनम् ।
अङ्गदप्रमुखास्तूर्णम्
ततस्सम्प्रस्थिता वयम् ॥
Hearing those words of Saṃpāti which brought us cheer, all of us, led by Aṅgada, set ourselves out immediately.
5.35.68
विन्ध्यादुत्थाय सम्प्राप्ता
स्सागरस्यान्तमुत्तरम् ।
त्वद्धर्शनकृतोत्साहा
हृष्टास्तुष्टाः प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
The Vānaras, cheerful and pleased, started from the Vindhyas and reached the northern shore of the ocean, eager to have a sight of you.
5.35.69
अङ्गदप्रमुखास्सर्वे
वेलोपान्तमुपस्थिताः ।
चिन्तां जग्मुः पुनर्भीताः
त्वद्दर्शनसमुत्सुकाः ॥
All the chiefs, including Aṅgada, eager to get sight of you, reached the shore of the ocean. But they were scared again and began to worry.
5.35.70
अथाहं हरिसैन्यस्य
सागरं प्रेक्ष्य सीदतः ।
व्यवधूय भयं तीव्रम्
योजनानां शतं प्लुतः ॥
Allaying the fears that gripped that army of Vānaras, whose hearts began to sink at the sight of the (vastness of the) ocean, I flew over the hundred Yōjanas.
5.35.71
लङ्का चापि मया रात्रौ
प्रविष्टा राक्षसाकुला ।
रावणश्च मया दृष्टः
त्वं च शोकपरिप्लुता ॥
Then, in the night, I entered Laṅkā, teeming with Rākshasas. I also saw Rāvaṇa and finally you, who are overwhelmed with grief.
5.35.72
एतत्ते सर्वमाख्यातम्
यथावृत्तमनन्दिते ।
अभिभाषस्व मां देवि
दूतो दाशरथेरहम् ॥
O blemishless one! I have now given you an account of all that happened as it is. Be pleased to speak to me, O Dēvi, I am the envoy of the son of Daṡaratha!
5.35.73
तं मां रामकृतोद्योगम्
त्वन्निमित्तमिहागतम् ।
सुग्रीवसचिवं देवि
बुद्ध्यस्व पवनात्मजम् ॥
Please know that I am the son of Vāyu and the minister of Sugreeva, who has come here in an effort to help Rāma, looking for you!
5.35.74
कुशली तव काकुत्स्थः
सर्वशस्त्रभृतां वरः ।
गुरोराराधने युक्तो
लक्ष्मणश्च सुलक्षणः ॥
Your Kākutstha, the best among the ones who wield arms, is safe and well, and also Lakshmaṇa of auspicious marks who is devoted to the service of his elder brother.
5.35.75
तस्य वीर्यवतो देवि
भर्तुस्तव हिते रतः ।
अहमेकस्तु सम्प्राप्तः
सुग्रीववचनादिह ॥
O Dēvi! Fully dedicated to the welfare of your valiant husband, I came here all alone at the behest of Sugreeva.
5.35.76
मयेयमसहायेन
चरता कामरूपिणा ।
दक्षिणा दिगनुक्रान्ता
त्वन्मार्गविचयैषिणा ॥
I, capable of taking any form at will, came here in this southern direction all by myself with no help from anyone else, trying to find your whereabouts.
5.35.77
दिष्ट्याहं हरिसैन्यानाम्
त्वन्नाशमनुशोचताम् ।
अपनेष्यामि सन्तापम्
तवाभिगमशंसनात् ॥
By the good grace of Providence, I shall be able to dispel the grief of the Vānara forces who mourn your loss, by telling them of my reaching you.
5.35.78
दिष्ट्या हि मम न व्यर्थम्
देवि सागरलङ्घनम् ।
प्राप्स्याम्यहमिदं दिष्ट्या
त्वद्दर्शनकृतं यशः ॥
O Dēvi! By the grace of Providence, my crossing the ocean did not go in vain and I shall enjoy the credit for finding you.
5.35.79
राघवश्च महावीर्यः
क्षिप्रं त्वामभिपत्स्यते ।
समित्रबान्धवं हत्वा
रावणं राक्षसाधिपम् ॥
Rāghava of immense valor will get you back very soon killing the lord of Rākshasas along with his kith and kin.
5.35.80
माल्यवान्नाम वैदेहि
गिरीणामुत्तमो गिरिः ।
ततो गच्छति गोकर्णम्
पर्वतं केसरी हरिः ॥
O Vaidēhee! There is a mountain by name Mālyavān, best among the mountains. A Vānara by name Kēsari went from there to another mountain by name Gōkarṇa.
5.35.81
स च देवर्षिभिर्दिष्टः
पिता मम महाकपिः ।
तीर्थे नदीपतेः पुण्ये
शम्बसादनमुद्धरत् ॥
That great Vānara is my father. He killed Ṡambasādana on the sacred shore of the lord of the rivers at the behest of the Dēvarshis.
5.35.82
तस्याहं हरिणः क्षेत्रे
जातो वातेन मैथिलि ।
हनुमानिति विख्यातो
लोके स्वेनैव कर्मणा ॥
I was born to the wife of that Vānara from (the power of) Vāyu. I am known widely as Hanumān by virtue of one of my own exploits.
5.35.83
विश्वासार्थं तु वैदेहि
भर्तुरुक्ता मया गुणाः ।
अचिराद्राघवो देवि
त्वामितो नयिताऽनघे ॥
I sang the virtues of your husband, O Vaidēhee, in order to inspire confidence in you. Before it is too long, O sinless lady, Rāghava will take you away from here.
5.35.84
एवं विश्वासिता सीता
हेतुभिश्शोककर्शिता ।
उपपन्नैरभिज्ञानैः
दूतं तमवगच्छति ॥
Seetā, who was enervated by grief, then developed trust based on sound reasoning and recognized him as a messenger because of the sufficient evidence.
5.35.85
अतुलं च गता हर्षम्
प्रहर्षेण च जानकी ।
नेत्राभ्यां वक्रपक्ष्माभ्याम्
मुमोचाऽऽनन्दजं जलम् ॥
Jānaki was overjoyed. And in her exhilaration, she let the tears of joy flow from her eyes that had (lovely) curved eye lashes.
5.35.86
चारु तद्वदनं तस्याः
ताम्रशुक्लायतेक्षणम् ।
अशोभत विशालाक्ष्या
राहुमुक्त इवोडुराट् ॥
Her beautiful face with wide, white copper-tinged eyes glowed like the king of the night relieved from the grip of Rāhu.
King of the night refers to the moon.
5.35.87
हनुमन्तं कपिं व्यक्तम्
मन्यते नान्यथेति सा ।
अथोवाच हनूमांस्ताम्
उत्तरं प्रियदर्शनाम् ॥
She then felt certain that he was truly Hanumān and no one else. Then Hanumān further said to her of pleasing looks:
5.35.88
एतत्ते सर्वमाख्यातम्
समाश्वसिहि मैथिलि ।
किं करोमि कथं वा ते
रोचते प्रतियाम्यहम् ॥
O Maithili! I have told you everything. I only hope you feel better. What shall I do for you? What would you like? I shall now go back!
5.35.89
हतेऽसुरे संयति शम्बसादने
कपिप्रवीरेण महर्षिचोदनात् ।
ततोऽस्मि वायुप्रभवो हि मैथिलि
प्रभावतस्तत्प्रतिमश्च वानरः ॥
I am a Vānara, O Maithili, equal in power to Vāyu, having born of his power at the time when (Kēsari), the great Vānara, slayed Ṡambasādana in fight at the instance of Maharshis.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे पञ्चत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty fifth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
