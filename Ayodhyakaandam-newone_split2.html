Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 2
In this Sarga, Daṡaratha announces his intention to consecrate Rāma as the crown prince and seeks the approval of Brāhmaṇas, kings, and prominent people from cities and rural areas. He receives a resounding approval in unequivocal terms, to do so.
2.2.1
ततः परिषदं सर्वाम्
आमन्त्र्य वसुधाधिपः ।
हितमुद्धर्षणं चैवम्
उवाच प्रथितं वचः ॥
Then, the ruler of the earth, addressing the entire assembly, proclaimed the thrilling, celebrated and beneficial words:
2.2.2
दुन्दुभिस्वनकल्पेन
गम्भीरेणानुनादिना ।
स्वरेण महता राजा
जीमूत इव नादयन् ।
राजलक्षणयुक्तेन
कान्तेनानुपमेन च ।
उवाच रसयुक्तेन
स्वरेण नृपतिर्नृपान् ॥
The King spoke to the kings in a unique voice that rumbled like a rain-cloud, with notes reminiscent of kettle drum, so sublime and resonant, and yet so regal, with something in it that was at once singular and sweet.
2.2.4
विदितं भवतामेतत्
यथा मे राज्यमुत्तमम् ।
पूर्वकैर्मम राजेन्द्रैः
सुतवत्परिपालितम् ॥
It is well-known to all of you that this great kingdom was well governed by great kings, my ancestors, as if it was their dear child.
2.2.5
सोऽहमिक्ष्वाकुभिस्सर्वैः
नरेन्द्रैः परिपालितम् ।
श्रेयसा योक्तुकामोऽस्मि
सुखार्हमखिलं जगत् ॥
My interest is also in the welfare of this entire, deserving, world, just as it had been for all the kings of the Ikshwāku dynasty.
2.2.6
मयाप्याचरितं पूर्वैः
पन्थानमनुगच्छता ।
प्रजा नित्यमनिद्रेण
यथाशक्त्यभिरक्षिताः ॥
Following in the footsteps of my forefathers, I have been protecting the people to the best of my abilities by being ever vigilant.
2.2.7
इदं शरीरं कृत्स्नस्य
लोकस्य चरता हितम् ।
पाण्डुरस्याऽतपत्रस्यः
छायायां जरितं मया ॥
This body of mine has aged while ensuring the well being of this entire world under the shade of the white royal umbrella.
2.2.8
प्राप्य वर्षसहस्राणि
बहून्यायूंषि जीवतः ।
जीर्णस्यास्य शरीरस्य
विश्रान्तिमभिरोचये ॥
Having lived for many thousands of years, this worn out body is looking forward for retiring.
2.2.9
राजप्रभावजुष्टां हि
दुर्वहामजितेन्द्रियैः ।
परिश्रान्तोऽस्मि लोकस्य
गुर्वीं धर्मधुरं वहन् ॥
I am now tired, having served the mightiest of the responsibilities in the world, of keeping the Dharma, that calls for a variety of leadership qualities and full control of the senses.
2.2.10
सोऽहं विश्रममिच्छामि
पुत्रं कृत्वा प्रजाहिते ।
सन्निकृष्टानिमान्सर्वान्
अनुमान्य द्विजर्षभान् ॥
Hence, I would like to retire myself, after convincing all these assembled Brāhmaṇas, and after entrusting the responsibility of the welfare of people to my son.
2.2.11
अनुजातो हि मां सर्वैः
गुणैर्ज्येष्ठो ममात्मजः ।
पुरन्दरसमो वीर्ये
रामः परपुरञ्जयः ॥
My eldest son Rāma, conqueror of enemy cities equal in prowess to Indra and endowed with the best of the qualities, has taken after me.
2.2.12
तं चन्द्रमिव पुष्येण
युक्तं धर्मभृतां वरम् ।
यौवराज्ये नियोक्ताऽस्मि
प्रीतः पुरुषपुङ्गवम् ॥
It is my pleasure to crown him, the best among men, best among the upholders of Dharma and glowing like moon in conjunction with star Pushyami, as the heir-apparent.
2.2.13
अनुरूपस्स वै नाथो
लक्ष्मीवान् लक्ष्मणाग्रजः ।
त्रैलोक्यमपि नाथेन
येनस्यान्नाथवत्तरम् ॥
The lustrous elder brother of Lakshmaṇa who can make the best ruler for all the three worlds would certainly be a befitting ruler.
2.2.14
अनेन श्रेयसा सद्यः
संयोज्यैवमिमां महीम् ।
गतक्लेशो भविष्यामि
सुते तस्मिन्निवेश्य वै ॥
I shall be relieved of my worries by immediately entrusting the rule of this land to my son, for its continued welfare.
2.2.15
यदिदं मेऽनुरूपार्थम्
मया साधु सुमन्त्रितम् ।
भवन्तो मेऽनुमन्यन्ताम्
कथं वा करवाण्यहम् ॥
Let me know whether this, which I have planned out after careful consideration, is agreeable to you all, or what shall be done!
2.2.16
यद्यप्येषा मम प्रीतिः
हितमन्यद्विचिन्त्यताम् ।
अन्यामध्यस्थचिन्ता हि
विमर्दाभ्यधिकोदया ॥
Even though this is what I would like, may you please think of all possible good alternatives! Impartial considerations that are deliberated well, would only yield the best results!
2.2.17
इति ब्रुवन्तं मुदिताः
प्रत्यनन्दन्नृपा नृपम् ।
वृष्टिमन्तं महामेघम्
नर्दन्त इव बर्हिणः ॥
When the king spoke thus, the delighted kings applauded him like peacocks welcoming the monsoon clouds with ecstasy.
2.2.18
स्निग्धोऽनुनादी संजज्ञे
तत्र हर्षसमीरितः ।
जनौघोद्घुष्टसन्नादो
विमानं कम्पयन्निव ॥
And therewith rose a mighty huzza of approval from the friendly and delighted crowd, that reverberated and seemed to be shaking that lofty council chamber.
2.2.19
तस्य धर्मार्थविदुषो
भावमाज्ञाय सर्वशः ।
ब्राह्मणा जनमुख्याश्च
पौरजानपदैस्सह ।
समेत्य मन्त्रयित्वा तु
समतागतबुद्धयः ।
ऊचुश्च मनसा ज्ञात्वा
वृद्धं दशरथं नृपम् ॥
Having understood the view point of that savant of Dharma and Artha, in full, Brāhmaṇas, kings and other prominent men, along with the representatives from cities and villages joined together and deliberated.
Having arrived at an unanimous conclusion, they addressed the aged king Daṡaratha, in full awareness:
2.2.21
अनेकवर्षसाहस्रो
वृद्धस्त्वमसि पार्थिव ।
स रामं युवराजानम्
अभिषिञ्चिस्व पार्थिवम् ॥
O Ruler of the earth! Indeed, you are thousands of years old. Hence please consecrate Rāma as the crown prince to rule the kingdom!
2.2.22
इच्छामो हि महाबाहुम्
रघुवीरं महाबलम् ।
गजेन महताऽयान्तम्
रामं छत्रावृताननम् ॥
We want to see the mighty strong, mighty-armed Rāma, the hero of Raghu dynasty, riding on a mighty elephant, with his face shaded by the white umbrella of state.
2.2.23
इति तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राजा तेषां मनःप्रियं ।
अजानन्निव जिज्ञासुः
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
On hearing those words that reflected their hearts' desire, the King asked inquisitively, as if he were not aware of the answer:
Recall the similar instance in Sarga 16 of Bāla Kāṇḍa. Lord Vishṇu asks (1.16.2) the Dēvas, whether they know of any Upāya to kill the evil-minded Rāvaṇa, even though he is fully aware of the answer.
This approach is part of what makes a great leader. The leader should be fully aware of the answer that people would seek. Yet he should give them an opportunity to express it in their own words.
2.2.24
श्रुत्वैव वचनं यन्मे
राघवं पतिमिच्छथ ।
राजानः संशयोऽयं मे
किमिदं ब्रूत तत्त्वतः ॥
As soon as you heard my words, you are telling me that you like Rāma to be the lord. O kings! That makes me wonder why it is so! You must tell me exactly what you are feeling!
2.2.25
कथं नु मयि धर्मेण
पृथिवीमनुशासति ।
भवन्तो द्रष्टुमिच्छन्ति
युवराजं ममात्मजम् ॥
Why do you want to see my son as the crown prince while I am ruling this land just fine, with Dharma?
2.2.26
ते तमूचुर्महात्मानम्
पौरजानपदैस्सह ॥
Then, they, along with the people of cities and rural areas spoke to that Mahātma:
2.2.26
बहवो नृप कल्याणाः
गुणाः पुत्रस्य सन्ति ते ।
गुणान् गुणवतो देव
देवकल्पस्य धीमतः ।
प्रियानानन्दनान्कृत्स्नान्
प्रवक्ष्यामोऽद्य तान् शृणु ॥
There are many good and auspicious qualities in your son! O king! Please listen! We shall now speak about all the endearing and delightful virtues of wise Rāma, who is comparable to Dēvas.
2.2.28
दिव्यैर्गुणैश्शक्रसमो
रामस्सत्यपराक्रमः ।
इक्ष्वाकुभ्योऽपि सर्वेभ्यो
ह्यतिरिक्तो विशांपते ॥
O Daṡaratha, the lord of the people! Rāma is of proven valor. He is equal to Indra in his divine virtues. He surpasses everyone in the Ikshwāku dynasty easily!
2.2.29
रामस्सत्पुरुषो लोके
सत्यधर्मपरायणः ।
साक्षाद्रामाद्विनिर्वृत्तो
धर्मश्चापि श्रिया सह ॥
Rāma is most venerable of the men in this world. He is fully devoted to Truth and Dharma. From him alone, can emerge Dharma together with prosperity.
Becoming rich is the objective. Being righteous is an obligation. The two always do not go together. Becoming rich the right way is far more challenging than the wrong way.
This challenge is not specific to the current times. It existed even in the days of Rāmāyaṇa. Rāma earned the trust of the people that he can bring riches and prosperity, without compromising one for the other.
2.2.30
प्रजासुखत्त्वे चन्द्रस्य
वसुधायाः क्षमागुणैः ।
बुद्ध्या बृहस्पतेस्तुल्यो
वीर्ये साक्षाच्छचीपतेः ॥
He is like moon in making people happy, like earth in forbearance, like Bṛihaspati in wisdom and like none other than Indra in prowess.
2.2.31
धर्मज्ञस्सत्यसन्धश्च
शीलवाननसूयकः ।
क्षान्तस्सान्त्वयिता श्लक्ष्णः
कृतज्ञो विजितेन्द्रियः ॥
He is a savant of Dharma, adherent of Truth. He has a remarkable character and free from envy. He is patient and comforting. He is soft spoken, grateful and has full mastery over the senses.
2.2.32
मृदुश्च स्थिरचित्तश्च
सदा भव्योऽनसूयकः ।
प्रियवादी च भूतानाम्
सत्यवादी च राघवः ।
बहुश्रुतानां वृद्धानाम्
ब्राह्मणानामुपासिता ।
तेनास्येहातुला कीर्तिः
यशस्तेजश्च वर्धते ॥
He is soft. His mind is stable. He is tranquil and untroubled by envy. This prince of Raghu dynasty advocates only what is right and endearing to people. He waits assiduously on Brāhmaṇas who are learned and matured. His unparalleled name, fame and the splendor of power are growing day by day.
Notice the phrase 'right as well as endearing'. Whatever we do, should be right as well as endearing.
But, many a time, we see people leaning towards doing the 'endearing' thing, even though it is wrong. On the other hand, we also see people adamantly doing the right thing, even though it is not liked by anyone.
Finding out something that is both right and endearing, is an art. This combination is stressed upon in many books in many contexts in Indic literature, including Mahābhārata.
2.2.34
देवासुरमनुष्याणाम्
सर्वास्त्रेषु विशारदः ।
सम्यग्विद्याव्रतस्नातो
यथावत्साङ्गवेदवित् ॥
He is eloquent with all the Astras known to humans, Dēvas as well as Asuras. He is well graduated in studies and learned all the Vedas and Vēdāṅgas.
Vēdas are four - Ṛg, Yajuh, Sāma and Adharva. Vēdāṅgas are six - Ṡiksha, Vyākaraṇa, Chandas, Nirukta, Jyōtishya and Kalpa.
2.2.35
गान्धर्वे च भुवि श्रेष्ठो
बभूव भरताग्रजः ।
कल्याणाभिजनस्साधुः
अदीनात्मा महामतिः ॥
This elder brother of Bharata is the greatest exponent in music on this earth. He is surrounded by good and well meaning people. He is brilliant, good natured and never dispirited.
2.2.36
द्विजैरभिविनीतश्च
श्रेष्ठैर्धर्मार्थनैपुणैः ।
यदा व्रजति सङ्ग्रामम्
ग्रामार्थे नगरस्य वा ।
गत्वा सौमित्रिसहितो
नाविजित्य निवर्तते ॥
He has been well-instructed by Brāhmaṇas who are experts in Dharma and Artha. Never he, along with Lakshmaṇa, returned without winning a battle either over a city or a village.
2.2.37
सङ्ग्रामात्पुनरागम्य
कुञ्जरेण रथेन वा ।
पौरान् स्वजनवन्नित्यम्
कुशलं परिपृच्छति ।
पुत्रेष्वग्निषु दारेषु
प्रेष्यशिष्यगणेषु च ।
निखिलेनानुपूर्व्याच्च
पितापुत्रानिवौरसान् ॥
Returning from the battles, on elephant or chariot, he asks for the wellbeing of everyone in the city, as if they are his own.
Like a father, he inquires about the wellbeing of everyone all the time, including about their children, wives, fire rituals, staff, Ṡishyas and all others, one by one.
2.2.39
शुश्रूषन्ते च वश्शिष्याः
कच्चित्कर्मसु दंशिताः ।
इति नः पुरुषव्याघ्र
स्सदा रामोऽभिभाषते ॥
The tiger among men, Rāma always inquires of us "Are your Ṡishyas doing everything promptly?"
2.2.40
व्यसनेषु मनुष्याणाम्
भृशं भवति दुःखितः ।
उत्सवेषु च सर्वेषु
पितेव परितुष्यति ॥
He feels touched and sad when he sees people in trouble. He feels happy like a father on festive occasions.
2.2.41
सत्यवादी महेष्वासो
वृद्धसेवी जितेन्द्रियः ।
स्मितपूर्वाभिभाषी च
धर्मं सर्वात्मना श्रितः ॥
He is committed to truth. He is a great archer. He respects elders. He has mastered senses. He always leads conversations with a smile. He cleaves to Dharma with the entire force of his being.
2.2.42
सम्यग्योक्ता श्रेयसां च
न विगृह्य कथारुचिः ।
उत्तरोत्तरयुक्तौ च
वक्ता वाचस्पतिर्यथा ॥
His focus is on doing good. He is not interested in arguments. But, in discussions, he knows how to offer a counter point, like Vācaspati.
Vācaspati is another name for Bṛhaspati.
2.2.43
सुभ्रूरायतताम्राक्षः
साक्षाद्विष्णुरिव स्वयम् ।
रामो लोकाभिरामोऽयम्
शौर्यवीर्यपराक्रमैः ॥
With beautiful eyebrows and wide eyes tinged with red, Rāma, the darling of the world, is like Vishṇu Himself, in heroism, valor and bravery.
2.2.44
प्रजापालनतत्त्वज्ञो
न रागोपहतेन्द्रियः ।
शक्तस्त्रैलोक्यमप्येको
भोक्तुं किन्नु महीमिमाम् ॥
He knew the intricacies of governing people. His senses are not numbed by indulgence. He is capable of enjoying all the three worlds, all by himself. What to say of just this earth!
2.2.45
नास्य क्रोधः प्रसादश्च
निरर्थोऽस्ति कदाचन ।
हन्त्येव नियमाद्वध्यान्
अवध्ये न च कुप्यति ।
युनक्त्यर्थैः प्रहृष्टश्च
तमसौ यत्र तुष्यति ॥
Neither his anger, nor his favor is ever in vain. He strikes the guilty as duty demands. He never even raises an eyebrow on the innocent. He shows his largesse happily, with whomever he is pleased.
2.2.47
शान्तैस्सर्वप्रजाकान्तैः
प्रीतिसञ्जननैर्नृणाम् ।
गुणैर्विरुरुचे रामो
दीप्तस्सूर्य इवांशुभिः ॥
With his poise, charisma and qualities that are endearing to all people Rāma is shining like the sun who is shining with the spread of his rays!
2.2.48
तमेवंगुणसम्पन्नम्
रामं सत्यपराक्रमम् ।
लोकपालोपमं नाथम्
अकामयत मेदिनी ॥
The earth longs for such Rāma as lord, who is endowed with all those great qualities and who is truly valiant and no less than the Lōka Pālakas!
2.2.49
वत्सश्श्रेयसि जातस्ते
दिष्ट्याऽसौ तव राघव ।
दिष्ट्या पुत्रगुणैर्युक्तो
मारीच इव काश्यपः ॥
O descendant of Raghu! By your good fortune he was born as your son to bring all goodness to you!
Just as Mareeci was with Kaṡyapa, you are blessed with him, who bears every quality that is desired in a son!
2.2.50
बलमारोग्यमायुश्च
रामस्य विदितात्मनः ।
देवासुरमनुष्येषु
सगन्धर्वोरगेषु च ।
आशंसते जनस्सर्वो
राष्ट्रे पुरवरे तथा ।
आभ्यन्तरश्च बाह्यश्च
पौरजानपदो जनः ॥
People from the cities and the countryside, and people from very near and very far are wishing strength, good health and long life to Rāma, who is renowned for his character not only among Dēvas, Humans and Rākshasas but also among Gandharvas and Uragas.
2.2.52
स्त्रियो वृद्धास्तरुण्यश्च
सायं प्रातस्समाहिताः ।
सर्वान् देवान् नमस्यन्ति
रामस्यार्थे यशस्विनः ॥
Young and old, as well as women are praying to all the Dēvas in the mornings and the evenings for the welfare of the Rāma, the widely admired!
2.2.53
तेषामायाचितं देव
त्वत्प्रसादात्समृद्ध्यताम् ।
राममिन्दीवरश्यामम्
सर्वशत्रुनिबर्हणम् ।
पश्यामो यौवराज्यस्थम्
तव राजोत्तमात्मजम् ॥
O lord! O best among the kings ! May their wish be fulfilled by your grace! May we see Rāma, your son, the annihilator of the all the foes, who is dark like the pleasing moon lily as the crown prince.
2.2.54
तं देव देवोपममात्मजं ते
सर्वस्य लोकस्य हिते निविष्टम् ।
हिताय नः क्षिप्रमुदारजुष्टं
मुदाभिषेक्तुं वरद त्वमर्हसि ॥
O grantor of wishes! For the sake of all of us, may you be pleased to consecrate your noble son at once, who is comparable to the lord of Dēvas, endowed with all the great qualities, and interested in the welfare of the entire world!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे द्वितीयस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the second Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
