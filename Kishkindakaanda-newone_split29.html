Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 29
In this Sarga, after the monsoon season passes, seeing Sugreeva indulging himself in the inner quarters with the womenfolk without even attending the affairs of the state, Hanumān approaches him and reminds him of the favor he owes Rāma.
Hanumān reminds him of the great friendship he forged with such a powerful person as Rāma and tells him that he should do everything possible to keep that friendship and return the favor of Rāma. Hanumān tells him that he should immediately make arrangements for the search of Seetā. He tells Sugreeva that Rāma would be anxiously waiting to hear from him and that he should command the Vānaras to do whatever was needed before Rāma came asking for it.
4.29.1
समीक्ष्य विमलं व्योम
गतविद्युद्वलाहकम् ।
सारसाकुलसङ्घुष्टम्
रम्यज्योत्स्नानुलेपनम् ।
समृद्धार्थं च सुग्रीवम्
मन्दधर्मार्थसङ्ग्रहम् ।
अत्यर्थमसतां मार्गम्
एकान्तगतमानसम् ।
निर्वृत्तकार्यं सिद्धार्थम्
प्रमदाभिरतं सदा ।
प्राप्तवन्तमभिप्रेतान्
सर्वानेव मनोरथान् ।
स्वां च पत्नीमभिप्रेताम्
तारां चापि समीप्सिताम् ।
विहरन्तमहोरात्रम्
कृतार्थं विगतज्वरम् ।
क्रीडन्तमिव देवेशम्
नन्दनेऽप्सरसां गणैः ।
मन्त्रिषु न्यस्तकार्यं च
मन्त्रिणामनवेक्षकम् ।
उत्सन्नराज्यसन्देशम्
कामवृत्तमवस्थितम् ।
निश्चितार्थोऽर्थतत्त्वज्ञः
कालधर्मविशेषवित् ।
प्रसाद्य वाक्यैर्विविधैः
हेतुमद्भिर्मनोरमैः ।
वाक्यविद्वाक्य तत्त्वज्ञम्
हरीशं मारुतात्मजः ।
हितं तथ्यं च पथ्यं च
सामधर्मार्थनीतिमत् ।
प्रणयप्रीतिसंयुक्तम्
विश्वासकृतनिश्चयम् ।
हरीश्वरमुपागम्य
हनूमान्वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
(In due course), the sky became clear and clouds and lightning became a thing of the past. The sky resounded with the cries of the Sārasa cranes and was treated with a coat of delightful moonlight.
But Sugreeva, having gained opulence, became relaxed about the demands of Artha and Dharma.
Having realized all that he wished for, indulging himself with women, including his own wife whom he longed for and also Tara that he wished for, he stopped attending to the affairs (of the state) and took to the ways of the ignoble, drawn to spending time alone.
With his objects accomplished and with all the hardships behind him, he spent days and nights in dalliance, like the Lord of Dēvas sporting with the Apsarasas in Nandana.
With all the concerns about the kingdom allayed, he left all the affairs of the state to the ministers and did not even care to supervise them adopting a care-free lifestyle.
Then, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, who had a clear eye on what needed to be done, was adept in the science of polity and especially sensitive to the demands of Dharma and time, approached that Lord of Vānaras, and being adept with his words, he gained the pleasure of the king with many a charming and reasonable word.
Then he said to the king who was given to sensible advice, these beneficial, truthful and palatable words, considering Dharma, Artha, conciliation and polity, showing all his affection, concern and the confidence that was reposed in him:
4.29.9
राज्यं प्राप्तं यशश्चैव
कौली श्रीरभिवर्धिता ।
मित्राणां सङ्ग्रहश्शेषः
तं भवान्कर्तुमर्हति ॥
You have obtained the kingdom and won reputation. The welfare of the dynasty is established. What remains to be done is the firm securing of friends, to which you should attend now.
4.29.10
यो हि मित्रेषु कालज्ञः
सततं साधु वर्तते ।
तस्य राज्यं च कीर्तिश्च
प्रतापश्चाऽभिवर्धते ॥
He who cares for continued, timely and meaningful relationship with allies, would see his kingdom, renown and valor prosper.
4.29.11
यस्य कोशश्च दण्डश्च
मित्राण्यात्मा च भूमिप ।
समवेतानि सर्वाणि
स राज्यं महदश्नुते ॥
O lord of the earth! He who has all his treasury, army and allies managed equally well, enjoys a great realm.
4.29.12
तद्भवान्वृत्तसम्पन्नः
स्थितः पथि निरत्यये ।
मित्रार्थमभिनीतार्थम्
यथावत्कर्तुमर्हति ॥
Therefore you, who had a cherished record of good character, should stick to the safe and secure course and see to it that the promise you made to your friend is fulfilled.
4.29.13
सन्त्यज्य सर्वकर्माणि
मित्रार्थे योऽनुवर्तते ।
सम्भ्रमाद्धि कृतोत्साहः
सोऽनर्थैर्नावरुध्यते ॥
He who does not set aside everything else to expeditiously attend to the objects of an ally will (soon) find himself facing adversities.
4.29.14
यस्तु कालव्यतीतेषु
मित्रकार्येषु वर्तते ।
स कृत्वा महतोऽप्यर्थान्
न मित्रार्थेन युज्यते ॥
Regardless of however many other objects one may accomplish, one would not be credited for helping allies, if he delays in attending to their objects.
4.29.15
तदिदं मित्रकार्यं ते
कालातीतमरिन्दम ।
क्रियतां राघवस्यैतत्
वैदेह्याः परिमार्गणम् ॥
The favor that you owe your friend is overdue. The search for Seetā, for the sake of Rāma, should be commenced right away.
4.29.16
न च कालमतीतं ते
निवेदयति कालवित् ।
त्वरमाणोऽपि सन्प्राज्ञः
तव राजन्वशानुगः ॥
Even though it is overdue on your part, and even though he knows it and is anxious about it, he, being the patient and composed one that he is and being dependent on you, has not brought it up (yet).
4.29.17
कुलस्य हेतुः स्फीतस्य
दीर्घबन्धुश्च राघवः ।
अप्रमेयप्रभावश्च
स्वयं चाप्रतिमो गुणैः ॥
Rāghava is the basis for your (regaining) the dynasty. He is a well-wisher, now and forever. His prowess is measureless and he has no second in his virtues.
4.29.18
तस्य त्वं कुरु वै कार्यम्
पूर्वं तेन कृतं तव ।
हरीश्वर कपिश्रेष्ठान्
आज्ञापयितुमर्हसि ॥
You should do what he wants to be done, as he did what you wanted in the past. Set the best among the Vānaras on this business, O King of Vānaras!
4.29.19
न हि तावद्भवेत्कालो
व्यतीतश्चोदनादृते ।
चोदितस्य हि कार्यस्य
भवेत्कालव्यतिक्रमः ॥
The question of delay does not arise when acted upon before being prompted; but acting only after being asked will certainly be counted as an occasion of delay.
4.29.20
अकर्तुरपि कार्यस्य
भवान्कर्ता हरीश्वर ।
किं पुनः प्रतिकर्तुस्ते
राज्येन च धनेन च ॥
You, O Lord of Vānaras, help even those who have done no favor to you; how much more, then, should you return the favor to one who has helped you to gain the kingdom and riches?
4.29.21
शक्तिमानपिविक्रान्तो
वानरर्क्षगणेश्वर ।
कर्तुं दाशरथेः प्रीतिम्
आज्ञायां किन्न सज्जसे ॥
You are very powerful and daring. You are the lord of hordes of Vānaras and bears. How is it that you are relaxed on giving orders to do what pleases the son of Daṡaratha?
4.29.22
कामं खलु शरैश्शक्तः
सुरासुरमहोरगान् ।
वशे दाशरथिः कर्तुम्
त्वत्प्रतिज्ञामवेक्षते ॥
The son of Daṡaratha is very much capable of bringing all the Suras, Asuras and the great Uragas under his control with his arrows. He is just waiting for you to fulfill your promise.
4.29.23
प्राणत्यागाविशङ्केन
कृतं तेन तवप्रियम् ।
तस्य मार्गाम वैदेहीम्
पृथिव्यामपि चाम्बरे ॥
He has done a favor for you without even caring for his own life. We shall search for Vaidēhee, regardless of whether she is on the earth or in the sky.
4.29.24
देवदानव गन्धर्वाः
असुरास्समरुद्गणाः ।
न च यक्षा भयं तस्य
कुर्युः किमिव राक्षसाः ॥
Neither Dēvas, Dānavas, Gandharvas, nor Asuras joined by hosts of Maruts can cause fear in him; what to say of Rākshasas!
4.29.25
तदेवं शक्तियुक्तस्य
पूर्वं प्रियकृतस्तव ।
रामस्यार्हसि पिङ्गेश
कर्तुं सर्वात्मना प्रियम् ॥
Rāma is such an extremely powerful person and has done a favor to you in the past. O Lord of the red-eyed Vānaras, you should strive by all means to do what pleases him.
4.29.26
नाधस्तादवनौ नाप्सु
गतिर्नोपरि चाम्बरे ।
कस्यचित्सज्जतेऽस्माकम्
कपीश्वर तवाज्ञया ॥
O Lord of Vānaras! There is no one among us who will, if you command us so, hesitate for a moment to go anywhere in the nether world, on the earth, in the waters or in the sky above.
4.29.27
तदाज्ञापय कः किं ते
कुतो वापि व्यवस्यतु ।
हरयोऽह्यप्रधृष्यास्ते
सन्ति कोट्यग्रतोऽनघ ॥
O sinless one! You have more than a crore of invincible Vānaras under your command. Command us as to what should be done where and by whom!
4.29.28
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
काले साधु निवेदनम् ।
सुग्रीवस्सत्त्वसम्पन्नः
चकार मतिमुत्तमाम् ॥
On hearing those words, spoken well and at the right time, Sugreeva, who was endowed with great strength set his mind on that great task.
4.29.29
स सन्दिदेशाभिमतम्
नीलं नित्यकृतोद्यमम् ।
दिक्षु सर्वासु सर्वेषाम्
सैन्यानामुपसङ्ग्रहे ॥
Then he instructed his intention to Neela, who was ever ready for an expedition, to mobilize the forces from every direction and bring them to one place.
4.29.30
यथा सेना समग्रा मे
यूथपालाश्च सर्वशः ।
समागच्छन्त्यसङ्गेन
सेनाग्राणि तथा कुरु ॥
See to it that my entire army headed by its commanders and captains is assembled without delay.
4.29.31
ये त्वन्तपालाः प्लवगाः
शीघ्रगा व्यवसायिनः ।
समानयन्तु ते शीघ्रम्
त्वरिताश्शासनान्मम ।
स्वयं चानन्तरं सैन्यम्
भवानेवानुपश्यतु ॥
May all the swift-moving, energetic Vānaras who guard the frontiers be summoned here quickly under my orders; and you should personally supervise the army hereafter.
4.29.33
त्रिपञ्चरात्रादूर्ध्वं यः
प्राप्नुयान्नेह वानरः ।
तस्य प्राणान्तिको दण्डो
नात्र कार्या विचारणा ॥
Let it be clear, beyond any negotiation, that any Vānara who does not present himself here within fifteen days will be punished with the end of his life.
4.29.34
हरींश्च वृद्धानुपयातु साङ्गदो
भवान्ममाज्ञामधिकृत्य निश्चिताम् ।
इति व्यवस्थां हरिपुङ्गवेश्वरो
विधाय वेश्म प्रविवेश वीर्यवान् ॥
‘You, along with Aṅgada, shall inform the elder Vānaras of my decision, following my command.’ Having thus ordered the arrangements to be made, the powerful Lord of the Vānara chieftains retired to his abode.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे एकोनत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty ninth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
