Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 101
In this Sarga, Rāma is overwhelmed with grief, fearing that his brother Lakshmaṇa might die. Sushēṇa consoles Rāma, assuring him that Lakshmaṇa's heartbeat is regular, he is breathing normally and that his eyes are pleasant and serene.
Sushēṇa then tells Hanumān to bring the medicinal herbs Viṡalyakaraṇi, Sāvarṇyakaraṇi, Saṃjīvakaraṇi and Sandhānakaraṇi from the mountain on which they grow. Hanumān goes to the mountain, and unable to recognize the herbal plants, brings the whole mountain peak, along with all the trees and vegetation on it. Sushēṇa then picks the herbs he needs and cures Lakshmaṇa.
Lakshmaṇa then urges Rāma to continue with his mission and slay Rāvaṇa, and not to get distracted by the grief for his condition.
6.101.1
शक्त्या विनिहतं दृष्ट्वा
रावणेन बलीयसा ।
लक्ष्मणं समरे शूरम्
रुधिरौघपरिप्लुतम् ।
स दत्त्वा तुमुलं युद्धम्
रावणस्य दुरात्मनः ।
विसृजन्नेव बाणौघान्
सुषेणं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing the heroic Lakshmaṇa drenched with blood, struck in that fight by Rāvaṇa with Ṡakti with all his force, he gave a tough fight to Rāvaṇa, the evil-minded one, by shooting a volley of arrows. Then he said these words to Sushēṇa:
6.101.3
एष रावणवीर्येण
लक्ष्मणः पतितो भुवि ।
सर्पवद्वेष्टते वीरो
मम शोकमुदीरयन् ॥
Succumbing to Rāvaṇa's valor, Lakshmaṇa, the Veera, is writhing like a snake, fallen on the ground, aggravating my grief.
6.101.4
शोणितार्द्रमिमं वीरम्
प्राणैः प्रियतमं मम ।
पश्यतो मम का शक्तिः
योद्धुं पर्याकुलात्मनः ॥
What kind of fight can I put forth, with my heart in a turmoil seeing this Veera, who is more than life to me, bathed in blood!
6.101.5
अयं स समरश्लाघी
भ्राता मे शुभलक्षणः ।
यदि पञ्चत्वमापन्नः
प्राणैर्मे किं सुखेन च ॥
Should this brother of mine, who loves to fight and who shines with all the auspicious marks, die, what good is my life, and how can I be happy?
6.101.6
लज्जतीव हि मे वीर्यम्
भ्रश्यतीव कराद्धनुः ।
सायका व्यवसीदन्ति
दृष्टिर्बाष्पवशं गता ॥
It is a shame on my valor. My bow seems to slip from my hand and my arrows seem to falter. My eyes are blurred with tears.
6.101.7
अवसीदन्ति गात्राणि
स्वप्नयाने नृणामिव ।
चिन्ता मे वर्धते तीव्रा
मुमूर्षापि च जायते ।
भ्रातरं निहतं दृष्ट्वा
रावणेन दुरात्मना ।
विष्टनन्तं तु दुःखार्तम्
मर्मण्यभिहतं भृशम् ॥
My limbs sag as if I am walking half asleep. I feel extremely sad and feel like giving up my life seeing my brother groaning in agony struck by the evil-minded Rāvaṇa at his vitals.
6.101.9
राघवो भ्रातरं दृष्ट्वा
प्रियं प्राणं बहिश्चरम् ।
दुःखेन महताऽऽविष्टो
ध्यानशोकपरायणः ॥
Seeing his brother, who to him was like his own dear life that happened to be outside of him, in that state, Rāghava was overwhelmed with misery and was consumed by grief and helplessness.
6.101.10
परं विषादमापन्नो
विललापाकुलेन्द्रियः ।
भ्रातरं निहतं दृष्ट्वा
लक्ष्मणं रणपांसुषु ॥
Seeing his brother Lakshmaṇa struck down and smeared with the dust of the battlefield, he felt extremely miserable and wailed, his senses in turmoil.
6.101.11
विजयोऽपि मे शूर
न प्रियायोपकल्पते ।
अचक्षुर्विषयश्चन्द्रः
कां प्रीतिं जनयिष्यति ॥
Even success is not going to help me feel happy, my hero! What kind of happiness can the moon engender, unless he is in one's clear view!
6.101.12
किं मे राज्येन किं प्राणैः
युद्धकार्यं न विद्यते ।
यत्रायं निहतः शेते
रणमूर्धनि लक्ष्मणः ॥
What good is the kingdom or life for me? With Lakshmaṇa lying down in the battlefield like this, what is the use of continuing the fight?
6.101.13
देशे देशे कलत्राणि
देशे देशे च बान्धवाः ।
तं तु देशं न पश्यामि
यत्र भ्राता सहोदरः ॥
One can pick up wives in any country and one can acquire relatives in any place; but no country do I see where one can find a brother born from the same womb.
6.101.14
किं नु राज्येन दुर्धर्ष ‌-
लक्ष्मणेन विना मम ।
कथं वक्ष्याम्यहं त्वम्बाम्
सुमित्रां पुत्रवत्सलाम् ॥
What good is this kingdom to me, without the invincible Lakshmaṇa! What will I say to mother Sumitrā who adores her son!
6.101.15
उप्लम्भं न शक्ष्यामि
सोढुं दत्तं सुमित्रया ।
किं नु वक्ष्यामि कौसल्याम्
मातरं किं नु कैकयीम् ॥
How can I take the blame that Sumitrā will surely cast on me? What can I say to mother Kousalyā and to Kaikēyee?
6.101.16
भरतं किं नु वक्ष्यामि
शत्रुघ्नं च महाबलम् ।
सह तेन वनं यातो
विना तेनागतः कथम् ॥
What will I tell Bharata and Ṡatrughna of immense strength, having taken him along with me and returning without him?
6.101.17
इहैव मरणं श्रेयो
न तु बन्धु निगर्हणम् ।
किं मया दुष्कृतं कर्म
कृतमन्यत्र जन्मनि ।
येन मे धार्मिको भ्राता
निहतश्चाग्रतः स्थितः ॥
Death is a better option for me than facing the rebuke of my family members. I must have done some something terrible in another life to have a brother, a Dhārmika, lie struck down right in front of me.
6.101.18
हा भ्रातर्मनुज श्रेष्ठ
शूराणां प्रवर प्रभो ।
एकाकी किं नु मां त्यक्त्वा
परलोकाय गच्छसि ॥
O my brother! O best of men! O my lord! O best of the warriors! Why do you go to the higher worlds leaving me all alone?
6.101.19
विलपन्तं च मां भ्रातः
किमर्थं नावभाषसे ।
उत्तिष्ठ पश्य किं शेषे
दीनं मां पश्य चक्षुसा ॥
My brother, why do you not respond to me as I wail like this? Why do you lie down like this? Please get up, open your eyes, and see how miserable I am!
6.101.20
शोकार्तस्य प्रमत्तस्य
पर्वतेषु वनेषु च ।
विषण्णस्य महाबाहो
समाश्वासयिता मम ॥
O you of mighty arm! Were you not used to consoling me when I was miserable and lost in grief roaming in those forests and mountains?
6.101.21
राममेवं ब्रुवाणं तु
शोकव्याकुलितेन्द्रियम् ।
आश्वासयन्नुवाचेदम्
सुषेणः परमं वचः ॥
Then Sushēṇa said these words with all concern to console Rāma who was thus lamenting in grief, with his senses in turmoil.
6.101.22
नैव पञ्चत्वमापन्नो
लक्ष्मणो लक्ष्मिवर्धनः ।
न चास्य विकृतं वक्त्रम्
न च श्यामत्वमागतम् ॥
Lakshmaṇa, enhancer of wealth, is not dead at all; neither is his face deformed, nor has his body turned blue.
6.101.23
सुप्रभं च प्रसन्नं च
मुखमस्याभिलक्ष्यते ।
पद्मरक्ततलौ हस्तौ
सुप्रसन्ने च लोचने ॥
His face seems quite pleasant and radiant. His palms are lively, looking like red lotuses and his eyes are calm and serene.
6.101.24
नेदृशं दृश्यते रूपम्
गतासूनां विशाम्पते ।
विषादं मा कृथा वीर
सप्राणोऽयमरिन्दमः ॥
One who is not alive, O King, would not look like this! Please, O tormentor of the foe, do not yield to sadness!
6.101.25
आख्याति तु प्रसुप्तस्य
स्रस्तगात्रस्य भूतले ।
सोच्छ्वासं हृदयं वीर
कम्पमानं मुहुर्मुहुः ॥
O Veera! He is unconscious and his limbs sag as if they are lifeless, but he is breathing and his heart is beating keeping to a rhythm, which tells that he is alive.
6.101.26
एवमुक्त्वा महाप्राज्ञः
सुषेणो राघवं वचः ।
समीपस्थमुवाचेदम्
हनुमन्तमं महाकपिम् ॥
Saying these words to Rāghava, the highly sagacious Sushēṇa said this to Hanumān, the great Vānara, who was standing near him:
6.101.27
सौम्य शीघ्रमितो गत्वा
शैलमोषधिपर्वतम् ।
पूर्वं ते कथितो योऽसौ
वीर जाम्बवता तव ॥
O Veera! My dear! Please start quickly from here and go to the mountain on which grow medicinal herbs, of which you were told by Jāmbavān in the past!
6.101.28
दक्षिणे शिखरे जाताम्
महौषधिमिहानय ।
विशल्यकरणीं नाम्ना
सावर्ण्यकरणीं तथा ।
संजीवकरणीं वीर
संधानीं च महौषधीम् ।
संजीवनार्थं वीरस्य
लक्ष्मणस्य महात्मनः ॥
O Veera! Please bring here the great herbs Viṡalyakaraṇi, Sāvarṇyakaraṇi, Saṃjīvakaraṇi and also the great herb Sandhānakaraṇi that grow on its southern peak, to revive the life of the valiant Mahātma Lakshmaṇa.
6.101.30
इत्येवमुक्तो हनुमान्
गत्वा चौषधिपर्वतम् ।
चिन्तामभ्यगमच्छ्रीमान्
अजानंस्तां महौषधिम् ॥
Thus told, the blessed Hanumān went to the mountain of herbs, and, unable to recognize the herbs, thought to himself, feeling distressed:
6.101.31
अस्मिंस्तु शिखरे जाताम्
ओषधीं तां सुखावहाम् ।
प्रतर्केणावगच्छामि
सुषेणोऽप्येवमब्रवीत् ॥
I think, based on what Sushēṇa said, that those herbs which restore one to good health must be growing on this peak.
6.101.32
अगृह्य यदि गच्छामि
विशल्यकरणीमहम् ।
कालात्ययेन दोषः स्यात्
वैक्लव्यं च महद्भवेत् ॥
Time is of the essence now and it would be a disaster if I go without taking the Viṡalyakaraṇi with me.
6.101.33
इति संचिन्त्य हनुमान्
गत्वा क्षिप्रं महाबलः ।
आसाद्य पर्वतश्रेष्ठम्
त्रिः प्रकम्प्य गिरेः शिरः ॥
Thinking thus and without wasting any more time, Hanumān of immense strength approached the great mountain, and shook its peak three times.
6.101.34
फुल्लनानातरुगणम्
समुत्पाट्य महाबलः ।
गृहीत्वा हरिशार्दूलो
हस्ताभ्यां समतोलयत् ॥
That tiger among Vānaras, of immense strength, then uprooted it with its many trees in bloom, and lifted it up, grabbing it with his two hands.
6.101.35
स नीलमिव जीमूतम्
तोयपूर्णं नभस्थलात् ।
उत्पपात गृहीत्वा तु
हनुमान् शिखरं गिरेः ॥
Taking that peak of the mountain, which looked like a black cloud that is dense with moisture, Hanumān sprang up into the sky.
6.101.36
समागम्य महावेगः
संन्यस्य शिखरं गिरेः ।
विश्रम्य किञ्चिद्धनुमान्
सुषेणमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Hanumān of immense speed then reached (the battlefield) and put down the mountain peak. After taking rest for a brief while, he said this to Sushēṇa:
6.101.37
ओषधिं नावगच्छामि
तामहं हरिपुङ्गव ।
तदिदं शिखरं कृत्स्नम्
गिरेस्तस्याहृतं मया ॥
I could not identify the medicinal herbs, O bull among Vānaras! And hence, I brought this whole mountain peak.
6.101.38
एवं कथयमानं तम्
प्रशस्य पवनात्मजम् ।
सुषेणो वानरश्रेष्ठो
जग्राहोत्पाट्य चौषधीम् ॥
Sushēṇa, a prominent Vānara, praised the son of Vāyu who spoke thus, and plucked the herbs (from the mountain peak).
6.101.39
विस्मितास्तु बभूवुस्ते
सर्वे वानरपुंगवाः ।
दृष्ट्वा हनुमतः कर्म
सुरैरपि सुदुष्करम् ॥
All the bulls among Vānaras were amazed seeing that feat of Hanumān which is impossible even for the Dēvas.
6.101.40
ततः सङ्क्षोदयित्वा ताम्
ओषधीं वानरोत्तमः ।
लक्ष्मणस्य ददौ नस्तः
सुषेणः सुमहाद्युतेः ॥
Sushēṇa, the prominent Vānara, then crushed the herb and gave it to Lakshmaṇa of extraordinary splendor, to sniff.
6.101.41
सशल्यः स समाघ्राय
लक्ष्मणः परवीरहा ।
विशल्यो विरुजः शीघ्रम्
उदतिष्ठन्महीतलात् ॥
As soon as Lakshmaṇa, destroyer of enemy warriors, inhaled it, he was healed of his wounds and was freed from his pain, and got up from the ground instantly.
6.101.42
तमुत्थितं ते हरयो
भूतलात् प्रेक्ष्य लक्ष्मणम् ।
साधु साध्विति सुप्रीताः
सुषेणं प्रत्यपूजयन् ॥
On seeing Lakshmaṇa thus get up from the ground, the Vānaras were extremely delighted and applauded Sushēṇa, saying, ‘Well done! Well done!’
6.101.43
एह्येहीत्यब्रवीद्रामो
लक्ष्मणं परवीरहा ।
सस्वजे स्नेहगाढं च
बाष्पपार्याकुलेक्षणः ।
अब्रवीच्च परिष्वज्य
सौमित्रिं राघवस्तदा ॥
'Come to me! Come to me!', said Rāma, destroyer of enemy warriors, to Lakshmaṇa,and took him into his embrace with all affection, his eyes filled with tears. After taking him into his embrace, Rāghava said to Sowmitri:
6.101.45
दिष्ट्या त्वां वीर पश्यामि
मरणात् पुनरागतम् ।
न हि मे जीवितेनार्थः
सीतया चापि लक्ष्मण ।
को हि मे विजयेनार्थः
त्वयि पञ्चत्वमागते ॥
By the good grace of Providence, O Veera, I see you come back from the dead. My life and even Seetā would not have meant anything to me, much less winning the battle, had you lost your life.
6.101.46
इत्येवं वदतस्तस्य
राघवस्य महात्मनः ।
खिन्नः शिथिलया वाचा
लक्ष्मणो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
When Rāghava, the Mahātma, said this, Lakshmaṇa, who was still weak, responded in a faltering voice:
6.101.47
तां प्रतिज्ञां प्रतिज्ञाय
पुरा सत्यपराक्रम ।
लघुः कश्चिदिवासत्त्वो
नैवं वक्तुमिहार्हसि ॥
Having taken that vow, O you of true prowess, you shall not speak like this as if you were a weakling with no mettle.
6.101.48
न हि प्रतिज्ञां कुर्वन्ति
वितथां सत्यवादिनः ।
लक्षणं हि महत्त्वस्य
प्रतिज्ञापरिपालनम् ॥
Those who adhere to Truth would not let their words go in vain; is not honoring one's pledge the very essence of greatness?
6.101.49
नैराश्यमुपगन्तुं च
नालं ते मत्कृतेऽनघ ।
वधेन रावणस्याद्य
प्रतिज्ञामनुपालय ॥
You should not cave in to despair like this, because of me, O sinless one! Fulfill your pledge forthwith by slaying Rāvaṇa.
6.101.50
न जीवन् यास्यते शत्रुः
तव बाणपथं गतः ।
नर्दतस्तीक्ष्णदंष्ट्रस्य
सिंहस्येव महागजः ॥
No enemy can escape with his life having fallen within the range of your arrows, any more than even a mighty elephant can, from a roaring lion with sharp fangs.
6.101.51
अहं तु वधमिच्छामि
शीघ्रमस्य दुरात्मनः ।
यावदस्तं न यात्येष
कृतकर्मा दिवाकरः ॥
I wish to see this evil-minded one slain quickly, before the sun sets, his day’s work done.
6.101.52
यदि वधमिच्छसि रावणस्य सङ्ख्ये
यदि च कृतां त्वमिहेच्छसि प्रतिज्ञाम् ।
यदि तव राजसुताभिलाष आर्य
कुरु च वचो मम शीघ्रमद्य वीर ॥
O noble and valiant one! If you are keen on slaying Rāvaṇa in the battle, and on making good your word and wish to recover the princess, please follow my advice without any delay, this very day.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे एकोत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and first Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
