Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 29
In the previous Sarga, Rāma told Seetā about the many perils of life in the Vana and denied her request to take her with him.
In this Sarga, Seetā insists in many ways that he must take her with him. But Rāma does not yield and thinks that Vana is not an appropriate place for her.
2.29.1
एतत्तु वचनं श्रुत्वा
सीता रामस्य दुःखिता ।
प्रसक्ताश्रुमुखी मन्दम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those words of Rāma, Seetā became very sad and spoke these words slowly, with tears flowing down her face:
2.29.2
ये त्वया कीर्तिता दोषा
वने वस्तव्यतां प्रति ।
गुणानित्येव तान्विद्धि
तव स्नेहपुरस्कृतान् ॥
Whatever problems you mentioned of the life in Vana, can be considered to be blessings when I have your company and affection.
2.29.3
मृगा स्सिंहा गजाश्चैव
शार्दूला श्शरभास्तथा ।
पक्षिणस्सृमराश्चैव
ये चान्ये वनचारिणः ।
अदृष्टपूर्वरूपत्वात्
सर्वे ते तव राघव ।
रूपं दृष्ट्वाऽपसर्पेयुः
भये सर्वे हि बिभ्यति ॥
Deer, lions, elephants, tigers, Ṡarabhas, birds, Sṛmaras and all other beasts of Vana, O Rāghava, will be scared and flee on seeing you, the likes of whom they have never seen before.
What confidence she has in him!
2.29.5
त्वया च सह गन्तव्यम्
मया गुरुजनाज्ञया ।
त्वद्वियोगेन मे राम
त्यक्तव्यमिह जीवितम् ॥
The directive from my elders is to go with you always. If I cannot be with you, O Rāma, I would rather give up this life!
2.29.6
न च मां त्वत्समीपस्था -
मपि शक्नोतिराघव ।
सुराणामीश्वरश्शक्रः
प्रधर्षयितुमोजसा ॥
When I am by you, O Rāghava, not even Sakra, the lord of Dēvas, with all his might, can bother me!
2.29.7
पतिहीना तु या नारी
न सा शक्ष्यति जीवितुम् ।
काममेवं विधं राम
त्वया मम निदर्शितम् ॥
You have yourself strongly imprinted it on me that it is difficult for a woman to live without her husband!
2.29.8
अथ चापि महाप्राज्ञ
ब्राह्मणानां मया श्रुतम् ।
पुरा पितृगृहे सत्यम्
वस्तव्यं किल मे वने ॥
Besides, O learned one, a long time ago when I was living in my father's house, I heard from Brāhmaṇas that I am destined to live in Vana!
2.29.9
लक्षणिभ्यो द्विजातिभ्यः
शृत्वाऽहं वचनं पुरा ।
वनवासकृतोत्साहा
नित्यमेव महाबल ॥
I have ever been looking forward eagerly to living in Vana, O mighty one, having heard those words in the past from the Brāhmaṇas, who have the ability to foretell events based on attributes of one's physique.
2.29.10
आदेशो वनवासस्य
प्राप्तव्य स्स मया किल ।
सा त्वया सह तत्राहम्
यास्यामि प्रिय नान्यथा ॥
It must have been decreed on me to live in Vana. It can happen no other way, my dear, than my going there along with you!
2.29.11
कृतादेशा भविष्यामि
गमिष्यामि सह त्वया ।
कालश्चायं समुत्पन्नः
सत्यवाग्भवतु द्विजः ॥
Let me fulfill that decree. Let me go with you. I think that time has come. Let those words of the Brāhmaṇa come true!
2.29.12
वनवासेऽभिजानामि
दुःखानि बहुथा किल ।
प्राप्यन्ते नियतं वीर
पुरुषैरकृतात्मभिः ॥
I know there will be many challenges living in the Vana! But, O Veera, they truly are of concern only for those who lack determination.
2.29.13
कन्यया च पितुर्गेहे
वनवासश्शृतो मया ।
भिक्षिण्यास्साधुवृत्ताया
मम मातुरिहाग्रतः ॥
I also heard about my having to live in Vana from a good natured mendicant woman, in my father's house in my mother's presence, when I was young.
2.29.14
प्रसादितश्च वै पूर्वम्
त्वं मे बहुतिथं प्रभो ।
गमनं वनवासस्य
काङ्क्षितं हि सह त्वया ॥
My lord, I have earned your grace over a long period of time! I very much want to go with you to live in the Vana!
2.29.15
कृतक्षणाऽहं भद्रं ते
गमनं प्रति राघव ।
वनवासस्य शूरस्य
चर्या हि मम रोचते ॥
I have long been set for this moment of going to live in the Vana! O Rāghava, I sure love to take the challenge! May all bode well for you!
2.29.16
शुद्धात्मन्प्रेमभावाध्दि
भविष्यामि विकल्मषा ।
भर्तारमनुगच्छन्ती
भर्ता हि मम दैवतम् ॥
O pure soul! It is only purely out of love, and for no other foible of mine, that I like to follow my husband! For, husband alone is my lord!
2.29.17
प्रेत्यभावे हि कल्याणः
सङ्गमो मे सह त्वया ।
श्रुतिर्हि श्रूयते पुण्या
ब्राह्मणानां यशस्विनाम् ॥
We hear the word of Veda from well-regarded Brāhmaṇas, that I will be in auspicious union with you even after death.
2.29.18
इहलोके च पितृभिः
या स्त्री यस्य महामते ।
अद्भिर्दत्ता स्वधर्मेण
प्रेत्यभावेऽपि तस्य सा ॥
Whomever a woman is given to, along with ceremonial waters, by the parents according to their Dharma, to him alone she belongs even after death.
2.29.19
एवमस्मात्स्वकां नारीम्
सुवृत्तां हि पतिव्रताम् ।
नाभिरोचयसे नेतुम्
त्वं मां केनेह हेतुना ॥
For what reason are you reluctant to take me, a woman who is your own, of good character and is fully devoted to husband, from here!
2.29.20
भक्तां पतिव्रतां दीनाम्
मां समां सुखदुःखयोः ।
नेतुमर्हसि काकुत्स्थ
समान सुखदुःखिनीम् ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! You must take me with you, your loving and faithful wife, so miserable now, so constant in my devotion to you in joy and sorrow, making your joys and sorrows my own!
2.29.21
यदि मां दुःखितामेवम्
वनं नेतुं न चेच्छसि ।
विषमग्निं जलं वाऽहम्
आस्थास्ये मृत्युकारणात् ॥
If you are not willing to take me to Vana leaving me in such misery, I have to take recourse to poison or fire or water to bring death upon myself.
2.29.22
एवं बहुविधं तं सा
याचते गमनं प्रति ।
नानुमेने महाबाहुः
तां नेतुं विजनं वनम् ॥
Even though she pleaded in many ways like this, he of mighty arm was not willing to take her to the desolate Vana.
2.29.23
एवमुक्ता तु सा चिन्ताम्
मैथिली समुपागता ।
स्नापयन्तीव गामुष्णैः
अश्रुभिर्नयनच्युतैः ॥
After she was told so, such great grief took over the princess of Mithilā that the tears rolling from her eyes seemed to drench the entire earth.
2.29.24
चिन्तयन्तीं तथा तां तु
निवर्तयितुमात्मवान् ।
ताम्रोष्ठीं स तदा सीताम्
काकुत्स्थो बह्वसान्त्वयत् ॥
That considerate prince of Kākutstha lineage, then tried to console and dissuade that grief stricken, copper lipped Seetā, in many ways.
Copper here indicates the shiny tinge of red.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकोनत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty ninth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
