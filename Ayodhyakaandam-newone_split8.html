Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 8
In this Sarga, Manthara reproves Kaikēyee for being so innocently joyful about Rāma's consecration. She paints a scary and gloomy picture of Bharata's future, should Rāma become the king.
2.8.1
मन्थरा त्वभ्यसूयैनाम्
उत्सृज्याभरणं च तत् ।
उवाचेदं ततो वाक्यम्
कोपदुःखसमन्विता ॥
But, Manthara reproved her sharply. She threw away the ornament and spoke in anger and grief:
2.8.2
हर्षं किमिदमस्थाने
कृतवत्यसि बालिशे ।
शोकसागरमध्यस्थम्
आत्मानं नावबुध्यसे ॥
Why this unseasonable joy, silly woman? Little are you realizing that you are in the midst of ocean of sorrow!
2.8.3
मनसा प्रहसामि त्वाम्
देवि दुःखार्दिता सती ।
यच्छोचितव्ये हृष्टाऽसि
प्राप्येदं व्यसनं महत् ॥
Harrowed by grief, I can only laugh in wonder at you, my lady, seeing you rejoice in this great misfortune, when you should be weeping!
2.8.4
शोचामि दुर्मतित्वं ते
का हि प्राज्ञा प्रहर्षयेत् ।
अरेस्सपत्नीपुत्रस्य
वृद्धिं मृत्योरिवागताम् ॥
I can only lament your perversity. Which sensible woman would rejoice over the advance of a stepson, an enemy, that is approaching like death!
2.8.5
भरतादेव रामस्य
राज्यसाधारणाद्भयम् ।
तद्विचिन्त्य विषण्णाऽस्मि
भयं भीताऽद्धि जायते ॥
Rāma has reason to fear Bharata since he has an equal claim to the throne. A man that is gripped by fear, is to be feared. It is this thought that distresses me.
2.8.6
लक्ष्मणो हि महेष्वासो
रामं सर्वात्मना गतः ।
शत्रुघ्नश्चापि भरतम्
काकुत्स्थं लक्ष्मणो यथा ॥
Lakshmaṇa, who wields the great bow, is devoted to Rāma with all his soul. Ṡatrughna is devoted to Bharata as Lakshmaṇa is to the prince of Kākutstha lineage!
2.8.7
प्रत्यासन्नक्रमेणापि
भरतस्यैव भामिनि ।
राज्यक्रमो विप्रकृष्टः
तयोस्तावत्कनीयसोः ॥
Even according to the order of birth, my lady, Bharata's claim to the succession is only next in order. The claims of the younger two are even remote.
2.8.8
विदुषः क्षत्रचारित्रे
प्राज्ञस्य प्राप्तकारिणः ।
भयात्प्रवेपे रामस्य
चिन्तयन्ती तवात्मजम् ॥
I tremble to think of the danger to your son from Rāma, who is knowledgeable, shrewd and knows when and how to play the game of politics!
Succession is never an easy one, is it? Succession puts two people who grew up together or collaborated together at odds with each other.
2.8.9
सुभगा खलु कौसल्या
यस्याः पुत्रोऽभिषेक्ष्यते ।
यौवराज्येन महता
श्वः पुष्येण द्विजोत्तमैः ॥
Fortunate, indeed, is Kousalyā, whose son will be consecrated as the mighty crown prince by the eminent Brāhmaṇas tomorrow when the moon is in the constellation of Pushya!
2.8.10
प्राप्तां सुमहतीं प्रीतिम्
प्रतीतां तां हतद्विषम् ।
उपस्थास्यसि कौसल्याम्
दासीवत्त्वं कृताञ्जलिः ॥
You will wait with cupped hands like a servant, on her, who would be extremely joyous and full of glory, having her rivals struck completely!
2.8.11
एवं चेत्त्वं सहास्माभिः
तस्याः प्रेष्या भविष्यसि ।
पुत्रश्च तव रामस्य
प्रेष्यभावं गमिष्यति ॥
If that happens, you will be at her beck and call along with us, and your son will be reduced to Rāma's errand-boy.
2.8.12
हृष्टाः खलु भविष्यन्ति
रामस्य परमास्स्त्रियः ।
अप्रहृष्टा भविष्यन्ति
स्नुषास्ते भरतक्षये ॥
Rāma's womenfolk will rejoice, while your daughters-in-law will become strangers to happiness, with the downfall of Bharata.
2.8.13
तां दृष्ट्वा परमप्रीताम्
ब्रुवन्तीं मन्थरां ततः ।
रामस्यैव गुणान्देवी
कैकेयी प्रशशंस ह ॥
To that Manthara who spoke very unhappily thus, the lady Kaikēyee insisted on Rāma's merits, praising him.
2.8.14
धर्मज्ञो गुरुभिर्दान्तः
कृतज्ञस्सत्यवाक्छुचिः ।
रामो राज्ञ स्सुतो ज्येष्ठो
यौवराज्यमतोऽर्हति ॥
Rāma is the eldest son of the king. He is well disciplined by elders. He knows Dharma. He has a great sense of gratitude, bound by truth and honesty. He certainly deserves to be the crown prince.
2.8.15
भ्रातऽन्भृत्यांश्च दीर्घायुः
पितृवत्पालयिष्यति ।
सन्तप्स्यसे कथं कुब्जे
श्रुत्वा रामाभिषेचनम् ॥
Rāma, blessed with long life, will protect all his brothers and dependents, like a father. O humpback, why then, are you sad at the news of Rāma's consecration?
2.8.16
भरतश्चापि रामस्य
ध्रुवं वर्षशतात्परम् ।
पितृपैतामहं राज्यम्
अवाप्ता पुरुषर्षभः ॥
Hundred years after Rāma's rule, Bharata, the best of men, also will certainty get the kingdom that has come down from the ancestors.
2.8.17
सा त्वमभ्युदये प्राप्ते
वर्तमाने च मन्थरे ।
भविष्यति च कल्याणे
किमर्थं परितप्यसे ॥
We have been blessed with prosperity all along and it is, thus, going to continue into the future. Why are you so worried?
2.8.18
यथा मे भरतो मान्यः
तथा भूयोऽपि राघवः ।
कौसल्यातोऽतिरिक्तं च
सोऽनुशुश्रूषते हि माम् ॥
I hold Rāghava in high esteem, even more so than I do Bharata. Similarly, Rāma takes care of me, even more so than he does Kousalyā.
2.8.19
राज्यं यदि हि रामस्य
भरतस्यापि तत्तदा ।
मन्यते हि यथात्मानम्
तथा भ्रातऽश्च राघवः ॥
The kingdom belonging to Rāma can be, as much, considered to be the kingdom of Bharata, for the prince of Raghu dynasty regards his brothers just as he regards himself.
2.8.20
कैकेयीवचनं श्रुत्वा
मन्थरा भृशदुःखिता ।
दीर्घमुष्णं च निःश्वस्य
कैकेयीमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those words of Kaikēyee, Manthara became even more sad. Heaving a deep and burning sigh, she spoke to Kaikēyee:
2.8.21
अनर्थदर्शिनी मौर्ख्यान्
नात्मानमवबुध्यसे ।
शोकव्यसनविस्तीर्णे
मज्जन्ती दुःखसागरे ॥
You are not able to look after your own interests because of your stupidity. You are not realizing that you are sinking into a ocean of misery fraught with grief and peril.
2.8.22
भविता राघवो राजा
राघवस्यानु यस्सुतः ।
राजवंशात्तु कैकेयि
भरतःपरिहास्यते ॥
Once Rāghava becomes the king, his son will take it over from him. Bharata will, then, be removed from the line of dynasty forever, O Kaikēyee!
2.8.23
न हि राज्ञस्सुता स्सर्वे
राज्ये तिष्ठन्ति भामिनि ।
स्थाप्यमानेषु सर्वेषु
सुमहाननयो भवेत् ॥
Not all the sons of the king sit on the throne, lovely woman! For, if all of them are on the throne, it will simply be a disaster.
2.8.24
तस्माज्ज्येष्ठे हि कैकेयि
राज्यतन्त्राणि पार्थिवाः ।
स्थापयन्त्यनवद्याङ्गि
गुणवत्स्वितरेष्वपि ॥
Hence, O lovely figure, kings install either the eldest or one of the other more qualified sons, in the kingdom.
2.8.25
असावत्यन्तनिर्भग्नः
तव पुत्रो भविष्यति ।
अनाथवत्सुखेभ्यश्च
राजवंशाच्च वत्सले ॥
And this son of yours, like an orphan, O loving mother, will be cut off from the royal comforts and the dynasty forever!
2.8.26
साऽहं त्वदर्थे सम्प्राप्ता
त्वं तु मां नावबुद्ध्यसे ।
सपत्नि वृद्धौ या मे त्वम्
प्रदेयं दातुमिच्छसि ॥
You are not understanding that I am here to further your interests. And, moreover, you want to give me a gift on the advancement of your co-wife's interests!
2.8.27
ध्रुवं तु भरतं रामः
प्राप्य राज्यमकण्टकम् ।
देशान्तरं वा नयिता
लोकान्तरमथाऽपि वा ॥
Once Rāma gains the full control of the kingdom, with no further impediments left, it is for certain that he would send Bharata out of the kingdom or even out of the world!
2.8.28
बाल एव हि मातुल्यम्
भरतो नायितस्त्वया ।
सन्निकर्षाच्च सौहार्दम्
जायते स्थावरेष्वपि ॥
You have sent way Bharata to his maternal uncle's home since when he was a child! Affection towards even lifeless objects develop only when they are in the proximity!
2.8.29
भरतस्याप्यनुवशः
शत्रुघ्नोऽपि समं गतः ।
लक्ष्मणो हि यथा रामम्
तथाऽसौ भरतं गतः ॥
And Ṡatrughna, cleaving to Bharata, had gone along with him. Just as Lakshmaṇa is attached to Rāma, he is attached to Bharata.
2.8.30
श्रूयते हि द्रुमः कश्चित्
छेत्तव्यो वनजीविभिः ।
सन्निकर्षादिषीकाभिः
मोचितः परमाद्भयात् ॥
It is said that a gigantic tree, which would otherwise be targeted for cutting by forest dwellers, is relieved of its ultimate fear by the presence of spiky bushes around it.
2.8.31
गोप्ता हि रामं सौमित्रिः
लक्ष्मणं चापि राघवः ।
अश्विनोरिव सौभ्रात्रम्
तयोर्लोकेषु विश्रुतम् ॥
The son of Sumitrā surely protects Rāma and Rāma also protects Lakshmaṇa. Their brotherhood which is well known among the people is like that of Ashwini twins.
2.8.32
तस्मान्न लक्ष्मणे रामः
पापं किञ्चित्करिष्यति ।
रामस्तु भरते पापम्
कुर्यादिति न संशयः ॥
Hence, Rāma will not do even a bit of harm to Lakshmaṇa. But Rāma will harm Bharata, without a doubt.
2.8.33
तस्माद्राजगृहादेव
वनं गच्छतु ते सुतः ।
एतद्धि रोचते मह्यम्
भृशं चापि हितं तव ॥
Hence it is better your son goes to Vanas right from that king's palace! That feels better to me and should also be the best for you!
2.8.34
एवं ते ज्ञातिपक्षस्य
श्रेयश्चैव भविष्यति ।
यदि चेद्भरतो धर्मात्
पित्र्यं राज्यमवाप्स्यसि ॥
The only way to secure your people's welfare is for Bharata to gain the kingdom of his father in a righteous and proper manner.
2.8.35
स ते सुखोचितो बालो
रामस्य सहजो रिपुः ।
समृद्धार्थस्य नष्टार्थो
जीविष्यति कथं वशे ॥
This young boy, who deserves all comforts, is an intrinsic foe of Rāma. How can someone whose dreams are shattered can live under someone whose dreams are furthered!
2.8.36
अभिद्रुतमिवारण्ये
सिंहेन गजयूथपम् ।
प्रच्छाद्यमानं रामेण
भरतं त्रातुमर्हसि ॥
Bharata needs to be saved from Rāma from being pounced upon like a lead elephant that is being chased by a lion!
2.8.37
दर्पान्निराकृता पूर्वम्
त्वया सौभाग्यवत्तया ।
राममाता सपत्नी ते
कथं वैरं न शातयेत् ॥
You have slighted your co-wife earlier, with the pride of receiving favors from your husband. Why would the mother of Rāma refrain from taking revenge?
2.8.38
यदा हि रामः पृथिवीमवाप्स्यति
प्रभूतरत्नाकरशैलपत्तनाम् ।
तदा गमिष्यस्यशुभं पराभवं
सहैव दीना भरतेन भामिनि! ॥
O! pitiable woman! The moment Rāma becomes the lord of this earth with its oceans, mountains and towns, everything inauspicious and insulting will find its way to you and Bharata.
2.8.39
यदा हि रामः पृथिवीमवाप्स्यति
ध्रुवं प्रणष्टो भरतो भविष्यति ।
अतो हि सञ्चिन्तय राज्यमात्मजे
परस्य चैवाद्य विवासकारणम् ॥
Bharata's downfall is certain once Rāma gains the earth. Hence think of a reason for gaining the kingdom for your son and banishing, right away, the other's son.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे अष्टमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
