Baala Kaanda - Sarga 33
The previous Sarga left us in suspense and in a gloomy mood (like before the intermission in a movie). In the previous Sarga, the beautiful and playful daughters of Kuṡanābha, struck by the God of Wind became incapacitated hunchbacks.
In this Sarga, all of them regain their original beauty and glory by the touch of Brahmadatta, after getting married to him.
1.33.1
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
कुशनाभस्य धीमतः ।
शिरोभिश्चरणौ स्पृष्ट्वा
कन्याशतमभाषत ॥
Those hundred girls then bowed their heads at the feet of that considerate and conscientious Kuṡanābha and responded to his question:
1.33.2
वायुः सर्वात्मको राजन्
प्रधर्षयितुमिच्छति ।
अशुभं मार्गमास्थाय
न धर्मं प्रत्यवेक्षते ॥
O King! The God of Wind, who pervades everything, turned a blind eye to Dharma, and trekked an inauspicious path with defiance.
1.33.3
पितृमत्यः स्म भद्रं ते
स्वच्छन्दे न वयं स्थिताः ।
पितरं नो वृणीष्व त्वम्
यदि नो दास्यते तव ॥
We told him, "We are not independent. We are under the wings of our father. May all bode well for you! Please check with our father whether he would give us in matrimony to you!"
1.33.4
तेन पापानुबन्धेन
वचनं न प्रतीच्छता ।
एवं ब्रुवन्त्यः सर्वाः स्म
वायुना निहता भृशम् ॥
That wretched being would not care for our words! Rather he dealt a blow to us like this!
1.33.5
तासां तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राजा परमधार्मिकः ।
प्रत्युवाच महातेजाः
कन्याशतमनुत्तमम् ॥
Hearing those words of the hundred girls, the king, who was a great Dhārmika, responded:
1.33.6
क्षान्तं क्षमावतां पुत्र्यः
कर्तव्यं सुमहत्कृतम् ।
ऐकमत्यमुपागम्य
कुलं चावेक्षितं मम ॥
My dear daughters! You responded in the right and noble manner by forgiving and refraining from retaliation. All of you, having done the right thing in unison, saved the reputation of our lineage!
1.33.7
अलङ्कारो हि नारीणाम्
क्षमा तु पुरुषस्य वा ।
दुष्करं तद्धि वः क्षान्तम्
त्रिदशेषु विशेषतः ।
यादृशी वः क्षमा पुत्र्यः
सर्वासामविशेषतः ॥
Forbearance is a great virtue whether for women or men. But it is hard to be seen among the progeny of Dēvas. It is really commendable that you all, without exception, displayed that great quality of forbearance.
Ghṛtāci, the celestial damsel is the mother of the girls, and hence the girls are progeny of Dēvas.
1.33.8
क्षमा दानं क्षमा सत्यम्
क्षमा यज्ञश्च पुत्रिकाः ।
क्षमा यशः क्षमा धर्मः
क्षमया विष्ठितं जगत् ॥
My dear daughters! Being forbearing is being generous. Forbearance is the building block of all endeavors. Forbearance leads to truth. Forbearance leads to glory. Forbearance is at the root of righteousness. The world is stable because of forbearance.
Understanding, reciting and practicing this one Ṡlōka alone could bring peace and prosperity to the world.
The Sanskrit word Kshama is translated in this Ṡlōka as forbearance. It also has the connotations of consideration, compassion, kindness, patience and tolerance.
1.33.9
विसृज्य कन्याः काकुत्स्थ
राजा त्रिदशविक्रमः ।
मन्त्रज्ञो मन्त्रयामास
प्रदानं सह मन्त्रिभिः ।
देशकालौ प्रदानस्य
सदृशे प्रतिपादनम् ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! Then that king, equal in valor to the Dēvas, left the girls and went to his ministers to make plans for getting them married to a right person and at a right time and right place.
1.33.11
एतस्मिन्नेव काले तु
चूली नाम महामुनिः ।
ऊर्ध्वरेताः शुभाचारो
ब्राह्मं तप उपागमत् ॥
At about the same time, there was a great Tapasvi by name Cūli, who was celibate, principled and performed Tapa as prescribed in Vēdas.
1.33.12
तप्यन्तं तमृषिं तत्र
गन्धर्वी पर्युपासते ।
सोमदा नाम भद्रं ते
ऊर्मिलातनया तदा ॥
A Gandharva woman by name Sōmadā, daughter of Ūrmilā, used to serve that Ṛshi, as he was performing Tapa.
1.33.13
सा च तं प्रणता भूत्वा
शुश्रूषणपरायणा ।
उवास काले धर्मिष्ठा
तस्यास्तुष्टोऽभवद्गुरुः ॥
She served him for long time in a humble and obedient manner with due care and concern. That guru was, then, very pleased with her.
1.33.14
स च तां कालयोगेन
प्रोवाच रघुनन्दन ।
परितुष्टोऽस्मि भद्रं ते
किं करोमि तव प्रियम् ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! At the right time, he said to her: "I am very pleased with you. May all bode well for you! What can I do to please you?"
1.33.15
परितुष्टं मुनिं ज्ञात्वा
गन्धर्वी मधुरस्वरा ।
उवाच परमप्रीता
वाक्यज्ञा वाक्यकोविदम् ॥
Knowing that he was pleased, and feeling very happy about it, that conversant Gandharva woman, said in a pleasing voice to that eloquent Muni:
1.33.16
लक्ष्म्या समुदितो ब्राह्म्या
ब्रह्मभूतो महातपाः ।
ब्राह्मेण तपसा युक्तम्
पुत्रमिच्छामि धार्मिक ॥
With all the long and great Tapa you did, you are shining with the radiance of Brahma and there is no real difference between you and Brahma. O Dhārmika, please grant me a son who will be interested in Tapa of Brahma.
1.33.17
अपतिश्चास्मि भद्रं ते
भार्या चास्मि न कस्यचित् ।
ब्राह्मेणोपगतायाश्च
दातुमर्हसि मे सुतम् ॥
I have no husband. I am no one's wife. I have been only in your service. Please grant me a son, with the powers of your Tapa! May all bode well for you!
1.33.18
तस्याः प्रसन्नो ब्रह्मर्षिः
ददौ पुत्रमनुत्तमम् ।
ब्रह्मदत्त इति ख्यातम्
मानसं चूलिनः सुतम् ॥
Pleased with her request, that Brahmarshi gave her a son by the will of his mind who came to be known as Brahmadatta, the son of Cūli.
1.33.19
स राजा सौमदेयस्तु
पुरीमध्यावसत्तदा ।
काम्पिल्यां परया लक्ष्म्या
देवराजो यथा दिवम् ॥
That son of Sōmada lived with riches in a city called Kāmpilya, like Indra in Swarga.
1.33.20
स बुद्धिं कृतवान् राजा
कुशनाभः सुधार्मिकः ।
ब्रह्मदत्ताय काकुत्स्थ
दातुं कन्याशतं तदा ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! The Dhārmika king Kuṡanābha thought of giving his one hundred daughters in matrimony to that Brahmadatta.
1.33.21
तमाहूय महातेजा
ब्रह्मदत्तं महीपतिः ।
ददौ कन्याशतं राजा
सुप्रीतेनान्तरात्मना ॥
That radiant king, the ruler of the world (Kuṡanābha) invited Brahmadatta and gave him his one hundred daughters in matrimony completely happy and pleased about it in his heart.
1.33.22
यथाक्रमं ततः पाणीन्
जग्राह रघुनन्दन ।
ब्रह्मदत्तो महीपालः
तासां देवपतिर्यथा ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! The ruler of the world, the Indra-like Brahmadatta took the hand of each one of the hundred girls, one by one.
1.33.23
स्पृष्टमात्रे ततः पाणौ
विकुब्जा विगतज्वराः ।
युक्ताः परमया लक्ष्म्या
बभुः कन्याशतं तदा ॥
As soon as he touched their hands with his, those one hundred girls shined splendidly, freed from all their sufferings and from their invalid status.
1.33.24
स दृष्ट्वा वायुना मुक्ताः
कुशनाभो महीपतिः ।
बभूव परमप्रीतो
हर्षं लेभे पुनःपुनः ॥
Seeing the girls freed from the problems caused by the God of Wind, Kuṡanābha, the ruler of the world, felt extremely happy, recollecting it again and again.
1.33.25
कृतोद्वाहं तु राजानम्
ब्रह्मदत्तं महीपतिः ।
सदारं प्रेषयामास
सोपाध्यायगणं तदा ॥
Kuṡanābha sent off the newly married Brahmadatta, along with his new wives and all the Purōhitas.
1.33.26
सोमदापि सुसंहृष्टा
पुत्रस्य सदृशीं क्रियाम् ।
यथान्यायं च गन्धर्वी
स्नुषास्ताः प्रत्यनन्दत ।
स्पृष्ट्वास्पृष्ट्वा च ताः कन्याः
कुशनाभं प्रशस्य च ॥
Sōmada, the Gandharva woman, felt very happy about the marriage of her son Brahmadatta. She thanked and praised Kuṡanābha. She touched all of her daughters-in-law, one by one and congratulated them.
Vālmeeki brushes through, when recounting stories and events that were already well known.
But he always pauses to describe the human emotions that are exchanged among the people of the story, giving it life and flavor.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे त्रयस्त्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty third Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
