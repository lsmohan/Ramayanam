Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 25
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa sends his spies Ṡuka and Sāraṇa to find out the size, strength and other details of the Vānara army. They disguise themselves as Vānaras and enter the army, but get caught by Vibheeshaṇa and taken to Rāma.
Rāma, contrary to their fears, asks them to feel free to check the entire army out and report the details to Rāvaṇa. He passes a warning to Rāvaṇa through them that he will be attacking and destroying Laṅkā the next morning.
Ṡuka and Sāraṇa go back to Laṅkā and advice Rāvaṇa that it is better for him to give Seetā back to Rāma and make peace with him.
6.25.1
सबले सागरं तीर्णे
रामे दशरथात्मजे ।
अमात्यौ रावणः श्रीमान्
अब्रवीच्छुकसारणौ ॥
With Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, successfully crossing the ocean along with his forces, the rich Rāvaṇa said to his ministers, Ṡuka and Sāraṇa:
6.25.2
समग्रं सागरं तीर्णम्
दुस्तरं वानरं बलम् ।
अभूतपूर्वं रामेण
सागरे सेतुबन्धनम् ।
सागरे सेतुबन्धं तु
न श्रद्दध्यां कथञ्चन ॥
The Vānara force, in its entirety, has crossed the impassable ocean. Nothing like this – of Rāma building a bridge over the ocean – has ever happened. I find it hard to believe that a bridge has been built over the ocean.
6.25.3
अवश्यं चापि सङ्ख्येयम्
तन्मया वानरं बलम् ।
भवन्तौ वानरं सैन्यम्
प्रविश्यानुपलक्षितौ ।
परिमाणं च वीर्यं च
ये च मुख्याः प्लवङ्गमाः ।
मन्त्रिणो ये च रामस्य
सुग्रीवस्य च सम्मताः ।
ये पूर्वमभिवर्तन्ते
ये च शूराः प्लवङ्गमाः ।
स च सेतुर्यथा बद्धः
सागरे सलिलार्णवे ।
निवेशं च यथा तेषाम्
वानराणां महात्मनाम् ।
रामस्य व्यवसायं च
वीर्यं प्रहरणानि च ।
लक्ष्मणस्य च वीर्यं च
तत्त्वतो ज्ञातुमर्हथः ॥
It is imperative that I should now have an estimate of that Vānara force. Both of you should enter the Vānara force in disguise and find everything in detail, its size and prowess, who the chiefs among the Vānaras are, who the advisors that accompanied Rāma and Sugreeva are, who the leaders of the vanguard are, who the most valiant Vānaras are, how they built the bridge on the waters of the ocean, how the mighty Vānaras have been stationed, what Rāma’s plans are, how valiant he is and what kind of arsenal he has, and also about the valor of Lakshmaṇa.
6.25.8
कश्च सेनापतिस्तेषाम्
वानराणां महौजसाम् ।
एतज्ज्ञात्वा यथातत्त्वम्
शीघ्रमागन्तुमर्हथः ॥
Find out who the chief of army of those Vānaras of immense strength, for sure, is, and return quickly.
6.25.9
इति प्रतिसमादिष्टौ
राक्षसौ शुकसारणौ ।
हरिरूपधरौ वीरौ
प्रविष्टौ वानरं बलम् ॥
Thus ordered, the Rākshasas Ṡuka and Sāraṇa disguising themselves as Vānaras entered the Vānara army.
6.25.10
ततस्तद्वानरं सैन्यम्
अचिन्त्यं रोमहर्षणम् ।
सङ्ख्यातुं नाध्यगच्छेताम्
तदा तौ शुकसारणौ ॥
However, Ṡuka and Sāraṇa could not estimate the size of hair-raising Vānara force that was beyond imagination.
6.25.11
संस्थितं पर्वताग्रेषु
निर्दरेषु गुहासु च ।
समुद्रस्य च तीरेषु
वनेषूपवनेषु च ।
तरमाणं च तीर्णं च
तर्तुकामं च सर्वशः ।
निविष्टं निविशच्चैव
भीमनादं महाबलम् ।
तद्बलार्णवमक्षोभ्यम्
ददृशाते निशाचरौ ॥
The Rākshasas looked at that inviolable sea of fighters, some that had camped on the tops of mountains, some by the streams and the caves, some on the shoreline of the ocean and some in the woods and in groves on the outskirts.
Some of the army had crossed (the bridge), some was still crossing, while the rest was waiting to cross.
Some of that strong force which raised a mighty din had already camped, while some was still settling down.
6.25.13
तौ ददर्श महातेजाः
प्रच्छन्नौ च विभीषणः ।
आचचक्षेऽथ रामाय
गृहीत्वा शुकसारणौ ॥
Vibhishana, of immense power noticed the two of them in disguise. He caught both Ṡuka and Sāraṇa and presented them to Rāma:
6.25.14
तस्येमौ राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
मन्त्रिणौ शुकसारणौ ।
लङ्कायाः समनुप्राप्तौ
चारौ परपुरञ्जय ॥
These two, Ṡuka and Sāraṇa, are the officials of the King of the Rākshasas, who have come from Laṅkā to spy, O Vanquisher of the enemy citadel!
6.25.15
तौ दृष्ट्वा व्यथितौ रामम्
निराशौ जीविते तदा ।
कृताञ्जलिपुटौ भीतौ
वचनं चेदमूचतुः ॥
Extremely worried on seeing Rāma and giving up their hopes of getting away alive, they said these words, scared and their palms joined in reverence:
6.25.16
आवामिहागतौ सौम्य
रावणप्रहितावुभौ ।
परिज्ञातुं बलं कृत्स्नम्
तवेदं रघुनन्दन ॥
We both came here, O good sir, O scion of Raghus, sent by Rāvaṇa, to know all the details of this army.
6.25.17
तयोस्तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
रामो दशरथात्मजः ।
अब्रवीत् प्रहसन् वाक्यम्
सर्वभूतहिते रतः ॥
On hearing those words of theirs, Rāma the son of Daṡaratha, vested in the welfare of every being, said these words, laughing:
6.25.18
यदि दृष्टं बलं कृत्स्रम्
वयं वा सुसमीक्षिताः ।
यथोक्तं वा कृतं कार्यम्
छन्दतः प्रतिगम्यताम् ॥
If you are done with seeing the entire army and taking a good look at us and and completed all that you were bid to do, you can go back as you please!
6.25.19
अथ किञ्चिददृष्टं वा
भूयस्तद्द्रष्टुमर्हथः ।
विभीषणो वा कार्त्स्न्येन
भूयः सन्दर्शयिष्यति ॥
Or else, if there is anything that you have not seen, you shall again take a look at that too! Vibheeshaṇa will show all of it, again!
6.25.20
न चेदं ग्रहणं प्राप्य
भेतव्यं जीवितं प्रति ।
न्यस्तशस्त्रौ गृहीतौ वा
न दूतौ वधमर्हथः ॥
That you have been caught should not be a cause of fear regarding your life. Messengers who have surrendered their arms, or who have been caught, should not be killed.
6.25.21
पृच्छमानौ विमुञ्चैतौ
चारौ रात्रिञ्चरावुभौ ।
शत्रुपक्षस्य सततम्
विभीषण विकर्षणौ ॥
Release these two Night-rovers, O Vibhishana! Spies as they are, they try their best to thwart the enemy!
6.25.22
प्रविश्य नगरीं लङ्काम्
भवद्भ्यां धनदानुजः ।
वक्तव्यो रक्षसां राजा
यथोक्तं वचनं मम ॥
Both of you, go to the city of Laṅkā and tell these words to the king of Rākshasas, the younger brother of Kubēra, exactly as I tell you:
6.25.23
यद्बलं च समाश्रित्य
सीतां मे हृतवानसि ।
तद्दर्शय यथाकामम्
ससैन्यः सहबान्धवः ॥
Show now, as you please, all that strength, along with the army and relations, on which you relied when you carried off my Seetā.
What a Ṡlōka, packing the challenge to and contempt for the enemy, strength and confidence in oneself and the possessiveness of the beloved, in so few words!
6.25.24
श्वः काल्ये नगरीं लङ्काम्
सप्राकारां सतोरणाम् ।
राक्षसं च बलं पश्य
शरैर्विध्वंसितं मया ॥
At dawn tomorrow you shall see the city of Laṅkā with its ramparts and gateways, its Rākshasas and forces destroyed by my arrows.
6.25.25
क्रोधं भीममहंमोक्ष्ये
ससैन्ये त्वयि रावण ।
श्वः काल्ये वज्रवान् वज्रम्
दानवेष्विव वासवः ॥
I shall let loose tomorrow at dawn, O Rāvaṇa, my terrible wrath along with my army on you, like Vāsava, the wielder of Vajra let loose the Vajra on Dānavas!
6.25.26
इति प्रतिसमादिष्टौ
राक्षसौ शुकसारणौ ।
जयेति प्रतिनन्द्यैतौ
राघवं धर्मवत्सलम् ।
आगम्य नगरीं लङ्काम्
अब्रूतां राक्षसाधिपम् ॥
Discharged with this message, the Rākshasas Ṡuka and Sāraṇa thanked him, and hailing him with the cry, ‘Victory to you!’, returned to the city of Laṅkā, and informed the Lord of Rākshasas:
6.25.28
विभीषणगृहीतौ तु
वधार्हौ राक्षसेश्वर ।
दृष्ट्वा धर्मात्मना मुक्तौ
रामेणामिततेजसा ॥
O Lord of Rākshasas! We were caught by Vibheeshaṇa and could have very well been slain. We were released by Rāma, the Dharmātma of immense strength, when he saw us.
6.25.29
एकस्थानगता यत्र
चत्वारः पुरुषर्षभाः ।
लोकपालोपमाः शूराः
कृतास्त्रा दृढविक्रमाः ।
रामो दाशरथिः श्रीमान्
लक्ष्मणश्च विभीषणः ।
सुग्रीवश्च महातेजा
महेन्द्रसमाविक्रमः ।
एते शक्ताः पुरीं लङ्काम्
सप्राकारां सतोरणाम् ।
अत्पाट्य सङ्क्रामयितुम्
सर्वे तिष्ठन्तु वानराः ॥
The four heroic and valiant bulls among men, Rāma the son of Daṡaratha, Lakshmaṇa the blessed one, Vibheeshaṇa, Sugreeva of immense strength who equals Mahēndra in prowess, all of whom are equals of the Rulers of the Worlds, savants of Astras with unshakable prowess, coming together at one place, can uproot the city of Laṅkā with all its ramparts and gateways and transport it elsewhere; leave alone all the other Vānaras.
6.25.32
यादृशं तस्य रामस्य
रूपं प्रहरणानि च ।
वधिष्यति पुरीं लङ्काम्
एकस्तिष्ठन्तु ते त्रयः ॥
Rāma’s personality and weapons are such that he alone, all by himself, can destroy Laṅkā, leave alone the other three.
6.25.33
रामलक्ष्मणगुप्ता सा
सुग्रीवेण च वाहिनी ।
बभूव दुर्धर्षतरा
सेन्द्रैरपि सुरासुरैः ॥
Protected by Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva, that army is unassailable even for the Suras and Asuras, joined by Indra.
6.25.34
प्रहृष्टरूपा ध्वजिनी वनौकसां
महात्मनां सम्प्रति योद्धुमिच्छताम् ।
अलं विरेधेन शमो विधीयतां
प्रदीयतां दाशरथाय मैथिली ॥
The army of the mighty dwellers of the forest is in high spirits and ready to attack at any moment; it is time to bury the hatchet and make peace. May Maithili be given back to the son of Daṡaratha.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे पञ्चविंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty fifth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
