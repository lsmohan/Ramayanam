Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 9
In this Sarga, the worried Seetā tells Rāma that she does not like Rāma killing the Rākshasas for the sake of Ṛshis, as there is no enmity between them. She argues that wielding a weapon makes one ruthless. She urges him to live a life of a true Tapasvi, in conformance with life in the Vana, giving up the bow and arrows. But in the end, she confesses that it is out of agitation in her mind that she spoke what she did and asks Rāma to do what he thinks is right.
3.9.1
सुतीक्ष्णेनाभ्यनुज्ञातं
प्रस्थितं रघुनन्दनम् ।
हृद्यया स्निग्धया वाचा
भर्तारमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
When the scion of the Raghus, having taken leave of Suteekshṇa, was on his way, Seetā said these words to her husband, with all affection and sweetness:
3.9.2
अयं धर्मस्सुसूक्ष्मेण
विधिना प्राप्यते महान् ।
निवृत्तेन तु शक्योऽयं
व्यसनात्कामजादिह ॥
Dharma is magnificent. It can be achieved (only) by highly nuanced practices. It is possible only by refraining from indulgences born out of infatuation.
3.9.3
त्रीण्येव व्यसनान्यत्र
कामजानि भवन्त्युत ।
मिथ्यावाक्यं परमकं
तस्माद्गुरुतरावुभौ ।
परदाराभिगमनं
विना वैरं च रौद्रता ॥
There are at least three indulgences that are born out of infatuation. Perjury is the foremost of them. But there are two more, which are even worse; seeking another’s wife and wrath without a conflict.
3.9.4
मिथ्यावाक्यं न ते भूतं
न भविष्यति राघव ।
कुतोऽभिलाषणं स्त्रीणां
परेषां धर्मनाशनम् ॥
O Rāghava, you have never told a lie, nor will you ever. And there is no way you would ever covet others’ women, which amounts to nothing but ruining of Dharma.
3.9.5
तव नास्ति मनुष्येन्द्र
न चाभूत्ते कदाचन ।
मनस्यपि तथा राम
न चैतद्विद्यते क्वचित् ।
स्वदारनिरतस्त्वं च
नित्यमेव नृपात्मज ॥
Rāma, O Lord of men! It is simply not in you! Neither did you ever have it, nor would that thought ever cross your mind. O Prince, you are devoted to your wife at all times.
3.9.7
धर्मिष्ठस्सत्यसन्धश्च
पितुर्निर्देशकारकः ।
त्वयि धर्मश्च सत्यं च
त्वयि सर्वं प्रतिष्ठितम् ॥
You keep to Dharma, bind yourself to Truth and abide by the instructions of your father. In you, all Dharma and Truth are established.
3.9.8
तच्च सर्वं महाबाहो
शक्यं धर्तुं जितेन्द्रियैः ।
तव वश्येन्द्रियत्वं च
जानामि शुभदर्शन ॥
All this is possible only for those who have mastered their senses, O you of the mighty arm! And I know your absolute mastery of the senses; O you of pleasing looks!
3.9.9
तृतीयं यदिदं रौद्रं
परप्राणाभिहिंसनम् ।
निर्वैरं क्रियते मोहात्
तच्च ते समुपस्थितम् ॥
But the third, this misplaced wrath, without a known conflict, to take the lives of others, has come upon you now.
3.9.10
प्रतिज्ञातस्त्वया वीर
दण्डकारण्यवासिनाम् ।
ऋषीणां रक्षणार्थाय
वधस्संयति रक्षसाम् ॥
You have taken an oath, O Veera, to kill Rākshasas in fight for the sake of protecting the Ṛshis who live in Daṇḍaka Araṇya.
3.9.11
एतन्निमित्तं च वनं
दण्डका इति विश्रुतम् ।
प्रस्थितस्त्वं सह भ्रात्रा
धृतबाणशरासनः ॥
It is for this purpose that you head to the forest famed as Daṇḍaka along with your brother, wielding bow and arrows.
3.9.12
ततस्त्वां प्रस्थितं दृष्ट्वा
मम चिन्ताकुलं मनः ।
त्वद्वृत्तं चिन्तयन्त्या वै
भवेन्निश्श्रेयसं हितम् ॥
Seeing you set on it, my mind is filled with agitation. I observe (closely) what you do, to ensure that it works out well.
3.9.13
न हि मे रोचते वीर
गमनं दण्डकान्प्रति ।
कारणं तत्र वक्ष्यामि
वदन्त्याश्श्रूयतां मम ॥
I do not like, O Veera, the idea of heading towards Daṇḍaka. I will tell the reason, may you listen to my words:
3.9.14
त्वं हि बाणधनुष्पाणिः
भ्रात्रा सह वनं गतः ।
दृष्ट्वा वनचरान्सर्वान्
कच्चित्कुर्याश्शरव्ययम् ॥
You do expend your arrows on the beasts that roam in the Vana, having come to the Vana along with your bother, wielding bow and arrow.
3.9.15
क्षत्रियाणां मपि धनुः
हुताशस्येन्धनानि च ।
समीपतस्स्थितं तेजो
बलमुच्छ्रयते भृशम् ॥
A bow next to a Kshatriya and fuel near by a fire will increase quite a bit of their shine and strength.
3.9.16
पुरा किल महाबाहो
तपस्स्वी सत्यवाक्छुचिः ।
कस्मिंश्चिदभवत्पुण्ये
वने रतमृगद्विजे ॥
Once upon a time, O you of mighty arm, there lived a Tapasvi, truth-speaking and pure, in an auspicious Vana where birds and beasts lived happily.
3.9.17
तस्यैव तपसो विघ्नं
कर्तुमिन्द्रश्शचीपतिः ।
खङ्गपाणिरथागच्छत्
आश्रमं भटरूपधृत् ॥
Indra, the husband of Ṡacee, went to his Āṡrama to derail his Tapa, disguised as a soldier with a sword.
3.9.18
तस्मिंस्तदाश्रमपदे
निशितः खङ्ग उत्तमः ।
स न्यासविधिना दत्तः
पुण्ये तपसि तिष्ठतः ॥
He left the superb and sharp sword for safekeeping with that Tapasvi who lived in that holy Āṡrama.
3.9.19
स तच्छस्त्रमनुप्राप्य
न्यासरक्षणतत्परः ।
वने तु विचरत्येव
रक्षन्प्रत्ययमात्मनः ॥
Having got the weapon and being zealous in safeguarding what he was trusted with, he carried it with him wherever he went in the Vana, to be sincere to the confidence reposed in him.
3.9.20
यत्र गच्छत्युपादातुं
मूलानि च फलानि च ।
न विना याति तं खङ्गं
न्यासरक्षणतत्परः ॥
Even when he went about to collect fruits and roots, he would not go without that sword, zealous about guarding what was left in trust with him.
3.9.21
नित्यं शस्त्रं परिवहन्
क्रमेण स तपोधनः ।
चकार रौद्रीं स्वां बुद्धिं
त्यक्त्वा तपसि निश्चयम् ॥
Carrying the weapon all the time, that man who was rich in Tapa, gradually lost his resolve in doing Tapa and allowed his mind to be taken over by anger.
3.9.22
ततस्सरौद्रेऽभिरतः
प्रमत्तोऽधर्मकर्शितः ।
तस्य शस्त्रस्य संवासात्
जगाम नरकं मुनिः ॥
Then, with the constant proximity of the weapon, the Muni indulged himself in impatience and anger. Overtaken by numbness and enervated by the lack of Dharma he (finally) went to hell.
3.9.23
एवमेतत्पुरा वृत्तं
शस्त्रसंयोगकारणम् ।
अग्निसंयोगवद्धेतुः
शास्त्रसंयोग उच्यते ।
स्नेहाच्च बहुमानाच्च
स्मारये त्वां न शिक्षये ॥
This is what happened once upon a time, as a result of association with a weapon. It is said that association with a weapon is equivalent to association with fire.
I am reminding you of this, not to teach you, but out of love and respect.
3.9.24
न कथञ्चन सा कार्या
गृहीतधनुषा त्वया ।
बुद्धिर्वैरं विना हन्तुं
राक्षसान्दण्डकाश्रितान् ।
अपराधं विना हन्तुं
लोकान्वीर न कामये ॥
It is not right for you to wield the bow and kill the Rākshasas that live in the Daṇḍaka forest, without provocation. O Veera! I do not like people being killed without a fault of theirs.
3.9.26
क्षत्रियाणां तु वीराणां
वनेषु निरतात्मनाम् ।
धनुषा कार्यमेतावत्
आर्तानां त्वभिरक्षणम् ॥
Kshatriyas may be interested in the Vana, but the use of their bows should be limited to protecting those who are in danger.
3.9.27
क्वच शस्त्रं क्वच वनं
क्वच क्षात्रं तपः क्वच ।
व्याविद्धमिदमस्माभिः
देशधर्मस्तु पूज्यताम् ॥
What does a weapon have to do in the Vana? What does Kshatra Dharma have to do with Tapa? They are clearly incompatible. May the Dharma of the land be honored!
3.9.28
तदार्य कलुषा बुद्धिः
जायते शस्त्रसेवनात् ।
पुनर्गत्वा त्वयोध्यायां
क्षत्रधर्मं चरिष्यसि ॥
Wielding a weapon will corrupt the mind, O noble one! You may renew your Kshatra Dharma after going back to Ayōdhyā!
3.9.29
अक्षया तु भवेत्प्रीतिः
श्वश्रूश्वशुरयोर्मम ।
यदि राज्यं परित्यज्य
भवेस्त्वं निरतो मुनिः ॥
If you were to give up the kingdom and become interested in being a Muni, it would only be of immense joy to my mother-in-law and father-in-law.
3.9.30
धर्मादर्थः प्रभवति
धर्मात्प्रभवते सुखम् ।
धर्मेण लभते सर्वं
धर्मसारमिदं जगत् ॥
From Dharma alone Artha emerges. From Dharma alone happiness arises. With Dharma alone everything can be attained. Dharma is the essence of this world.
3.9.31
आत्मानं नियमैस्तैस्तै
कर्शयित्वा प्रयत्नतः ।
प्राप्यते निपुणैर्धर्मो
न सुखाल्लभ्यते सुखम् ॥
Smart people deliberately exhaust themselves by subjecting themselves to various disciplines. Happiness cannot be achieved by taking things lightly.
3.9.32
नित्यं शुचिमतिस्सौम्य
चर धर्मं तपोवने ।
सर्वं हि विदितं तुभ्यं
त्रैलोक्यमपि तत्त्वतः ॥
O gentle one, keeping your mind pure all the time, please practice Dharma in this Vana of Tapa. You know everything in depth that is there to be known in the three worlds!
3.9.33
स्त्रीचापलादेतदुदाहृतं मे
धर्मं च वक्तुं तव कस्समर्थः ।
विचार्य बुद्ध्या तु सहानुजेन
यद्रोचते तत्कुरु मा चिरेण ॥
I am a woman and I speak as my mind sways hither and thither. Who is capable of talking to you about Dharma? Think it over along with your brother and do what you feel is right. Let there be no delay.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे नवमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
