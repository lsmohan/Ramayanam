Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 122
In this Sarga, Rāma suggests that Vibheeshaṇa honor the Vānaras with jewels and moneys, as they were instrumental in his getting the lordship over the city of Laṅkā. Vibheeshaṇa does the same gladly and immediately.
Then Rāma, along with Seetā and Lakshmaṇa gets into the Pushpaka and bids farewell to the Vānaras and Rākshasas. In response, they tell him that they also would like to join him and see him consecrated in Ayōdhyā. Rāma is only too happy at their request and asks all of them get into the Pushpaka as well, which they do immediately. The Pushpaka then takes off to the skies, as commanded by Rāma.
6.122.1
उपस्थितं तु तं कृत्वा
पुष्पकं पुष्पभूषितम् ।
अविदूरे स्थितो रामम्
इरत्युवाच विभीषणः ॥
Having arranged the Pushpaka that was decorated with flowers, Vibheeshaṇa, standing not too far from Rāma, said this to him:
6.122.2
स तु बद्धाञ्जलिपुटो
विनीतो राक्षसेश्वरः ।
अब्रवीत्त्वरयोपेतः
किं करोमीति राघवम् ॥
The king of Rākshasas, his palms in joined reverence, obediently said to Rāma, 'What else can I do?', in his eagerness to serve.
6.122.3
तमब्रवीन्महातेजा
लक्ष्मणस्योपशृण्वतः ।
विमृश्य राघवो वाक्यम्
इदं स्नेहपुरस्कृतम् ॥
Then the extraordinarily powerful Rāghava, considering everything, said this with all affection, with Lakshmaṇa also listening:
6.122.4
कृतप्रयत्नकर्माणः
सर्व एव वनेचराः ।
रत्नैरर्थैश्च विविधैः
संपूज्यन्तां विभीषण ॥
All the Vana-dwellers who strove and accomplished mighty deeds should be honored, O Vibheeshaṇa, with jewels and monies!
6.122.5
सहामीभिस्त्वया लङ्का
निर्जिता राक्षसेश्वर ।
हृष्टैः प्राणभयं त्यक्त्वा
सङ्ग्रामेष्वनिवर्तिभिः ॥
It was with the aid of these, who never turned their back on the battlefield, who fought with the highest spirits, little caring for their own lives, that you have won Laṅkā.
These Ṡlōkas show how strategic Rāma is in his thinking. The Vānaras helped Rāma because he helped Sugreeva. But in the process Vibheeshaṇa also got the benefit of lordship over Laṅkā. Rāma bestowed it to him and hence he owes him something. Instead of asking for a payback for himself, Rāma asks the reward to be given to the Vānaras.
Thus he sows the seeds for good relationship between Vānaras and Rākshasas, and in essence, creates a three way alliance between him, Vānaras and Rākshasas, which will greatly help him in the long run, to prosper as the king of Ayōdhyā.
6.122.6
त इमे कृतकर्माणः
सर्व एव वनौकसः ।
धनरत्नप्रदानेन
कर्मैषां सफलं कुरु ॥
All these Vana-dwellers have done their part. Make their toil bear fruit by rewarding them with jewels and monies.
6.122.7
एव सम्मानिताश्चैते
नन्द्यमाना यथा त्वया ।
भविष्यन्ति कृतज्ञेन
निर्वृता हरियूथपाः ॥
These Vānara chiefs will be very happy, honored and felicitated by you thus out of gratitude.
6.122.8
त्यागिनं सङ्ग्रहीतारम्
सानुक्रोशं जितेन्द्रियम् ।
सर्वे त्वामभिगच्छन्ति
ततः सम्बोधयामि ते ॥
Then they will all come to you, who have mastered your senses, knowing how charitable, inviting and kind you are; which is why I made this suggestion.
6.122.9
हीनं रतिगुणैः सर्वैः
अभिहन्तारमाहवे ।
सेना त्यजति संविग्ना
नृपतिं त्वं नरेश्वर ॥
The forces will get frustrated and desert a king if he lacks any kind of feeling for them and just lets them get killed in the battles, O lord of peoples!
6.122.10
एवमुक्तस्तु रामेण
वानरांस्तान् विभीषणः ।
रत्नार्थसंविभागेन
सर्वानेवाभ्यपूजयत् ॥
Thus told by Rāma, Vibheeshaṇa honored all those Vānaras, distributing jewels and monies to them.
6.122.11
ततस्तान् पूजितान् दृष्ट्वा
रत्नैरर्थैश्च यूथपान् ।
आरुरोह ततो रामः
तद्विमानमनुत्तमम् ।
अङ्केनादाय वैदेहीम्
लज्जमानां यशस्विनीम् ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
विक्रान्तेन धनुष्मता ॥
After witnessing the chiefs thus honored with jewels and monies, Rāma of great prowess, bow in hand, got on to that unexcelled Vimāna, along with his brother Lakshmaṇa and taking the bashful and renowned Vaidēhee on his lap.
6.122.13
अब्रवीच्च विमानस्थः
पूजयन् सर्ववानरान् ।
सुग्रीवं च महावीर्यम्
काकुत्स्थः सविभीषणम् ॥
Seated in the Vimāna, Kākutstha said, after paying respects to all the Vānaras, to Sugreeva of great valor and to Vibheeshaṇa:
6.122.14
मित्रकार्यं कृतमिदम्
भवद्भिर्वानरोत्तमाः ।
अनुज्ञाता मया सर्वे
यथेष्टं प्रतिगच्छत ॥
This mission of your friend is completed by you, the best of the Vānaras; all of you now have my consent to return as you please!
6.122.15
यत्तु कार्यं वयस्येन
स्निग्धेन च हितेन च ।
कृतं सुग्रीव तत्सर्वम्
भवता धर्म भीरुणा ।
किष्किन्धां प्रतियाह्याशु
स्वसैन्येनाभिसंवृतः ॥
You, who are ever weary of transgressing Dharma, have done, all that a friend who is affectionate and interested in a friend's welfare would do. You may go back to Kishkindhā with all expediency, with all your forces about you.
6.122.16
स्वराज्ये वस लङ्कायाम्
मया दत्ते विभीषण ।
न त्वां धर्षयितुं शक्ताः
सेन्द्रा अपि दिवौकसः ॥
And you, Vibheeshaṇa, may live in the kingdom which is now your own, passed on to you by me. Even the dwellers of Heaven led by Indra will be powerless to assail you.
6.122.17
अयोध्यां प्रतियास्यामि
राजधानीं पितुर्मम ।
अभ्यनुज्ञातुमिच्छामि
सर्वांश्चामन्त्रयामि वः ॥
I shall now go back to Ayōdhyā, the capital city of my father; I would like to seek leave from you and so would I like to bid farewell to you all.
6.122.18
एवमुक्तास्तु रामेण
वानरास्ते महाबलाः ।
ऊचुः प्राञ्जलयो रामम्
राक्षसश्च विभीषणः ॥
Thus told by Rāma, those Vānaras of extraordinary strength and Vibheeshaṇa, the Rākshasa said to Rāma, their palms joined in reverence:
6.122.19
अयोध्यां गन्तुमिच्छामः
सर्वान्नयतु नो भवान् ।
मुद्युक्ता विचरिष्यामो
वनान्युपवनानि च ॥
We would like to come to Ayōdhyā; please take us all with you. We shall enjoy going around the gardens and pleasances over there.
Isn't it the most touching gesture that Rāma, or anyone in that situation, could ask for?
6.122.20
दृष्ट्वा त्वामभिषेकार्द्रम्
कौसल्यामभिवाद्य च ।
अचिरादागमिष्यामः
स्वगृहान्नृपसत्तम ॥
Seeing you wet from the water of consecration and offering our obeisances to Kousalyā, we will, not too long after, go to our own places.
6.122.21
एवमुक्तः स धर्मात्मा
वानरैः सविभीषणैः ।
अब्रवीद्वानरान्रामः
ससुग्रीवविभीषणान् ॥
Thus implored by Vānaras and Vibheeshaṇa, Rāma, the Dharmātma said to the Vānaras, Sugreeva and Vibheeshaṇa:
6.122.22
प्रियात् प्रियतरं लब्धम्
यदहं ससुहृज्जनः ।
सर्वैर्भवद्भिः सहितः
प्रीतिं लप्स्ये पुरीं गतः ॥
If I can indeed have the pleasure of going to that city with you all, my friends, it is like getting something even better than what I have already got.
6.122.23
क्षिप्रमारोह सुग्रीव
विमानं वानरैः सह ।
त्वमप्यारोह सामात्यो
राक्षसेन्द्र विभीषण ॥
O Sugreeva, get on to the Vimāna, along with the Vānaras, and Vibheeshaṇa, O lord of Rākshasas, you too get on it along with your ministers.
6.122.24
ततः स पुष्पकं दिव्यम्
सुग्रीवः सह वानरैः ।
आरुरोह मुदा युक्तः
सामात्यश्च विभीषणः ॥
Then Sugreeva, filled with joy, got on to that splendid Pushpaka along with the Vānaras and so did Vibheeshaṇa along with his ministers.
6.122.25
तेष्वारूढेषु सर्वेषु
कौबेरं परमासनम् ।
राघवेणाभ्यनुज्ञातम्
उत्पपात विहायसम् ॥
When they were all in, that great vehicle of Kubēra rose into the sky, with Rāghava’s permission.
6.122.26
खगतेन विमानेन
हंसयुक्तेन भास्वता ।
प्रहृष्टश्च प्रतीतश्च
बभौ रामः कुबेरवत् ॥
Riding in that shining Vimāna that was yoked with swans, the joyous Rāma shone like Kubēra, receiving praise from all.
6.122.27
ते सर्वे वानरर्क्षाश्च
राक्षसाश्च महाबलाः ।
यथासुखमसम्बाधम्
दिव्ये तस्मिन्नुपाविशन् ॥
All of those Vānaras, bears and the extraordinarily strong Rākshasas sat in that splendid Vimāna in comfort and without feeling any congestion.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्वाविंशत्युत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and twenty second Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
