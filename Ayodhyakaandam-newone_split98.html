Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 98
In this Sarga, Bharata sends Ṡatrughna along with many men and hunters, and Guha along with thousands of his kinsmen to search for Rāma. He himself then goes on foot searching for Rāma along with ministers, citizens, elders and Brāhmaṇas, leaving the army behind.
Finally he sees the column of smoke rising from Rāma's Āṡrama, and rejoices as one who has reached the farther shores of the waters.
2.98.1
निवेश्य सेनां तु विभुः
पद्भ्यां पादवतां वरः ।
अभिगन्तुं स काकुत्थ्सम्
इयेष गुरुवर्तकम् ॥
That leader, a man of strong legs, having ordered the army to camp, decided to proceed on foot to see Kākutstha who was abiding by the word of his father.
2.98.2
निविष्टमात्रे सैन्ये तु
यथोद्देशं विनीतवत् ।
भरतो भ्रातरं वाक्यं
शत्रुघ्नमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Once the army had settled down in an orderly way, according to plan, the humble Bharata told his brother Ṡatrughna:
2.98.3
क्षिप्रं वनमिदं सौम्य
नरसङ्घैस्समन्ततः ।
लुब्धैश्च सहितैरेभिः
त्वमन्वेषितुमर्हसि ॥
O gentle one, take many men and hunters with you and search everywhere in this Vana, without delay.
2.98.4
गुहो ज्ञातिसहस्रेण
शरचापासिधारिणा ।
समन्वेषतु काकुत्स्थम्
अस्मिन् परिवृतस्स्वयम् ॥
Let Guha also go personally searching for the scions of the Kākutstha dynasty, accompanied by thousands of his kinsmen armed with bows, arrows and knives.
2.98.5
अमात्यैस्सह पौरैश्च
गुरुभिश्च द्विजातिभिः ।
वनं सर्वं चरिष्यामि
पद्भ्यां परिवृतस्स्वयम् ॥
And I will personally go around the whole Vana on foot, accompanied by the ministers, citizens, elders and Brāhmaṇas.
2.98.6
यावन्न रामं द्रक्ष्यामि
लक्ष्मणं वा महाबलम् ।
वैदेहीं च महाभागं
न मे शान्तिर्भविष्यति ॥
I shall have no peace until I see Rāma or the mighty strong Lakshmaṇa or the blessed princess of Vidēha.
2.98.7
यावन्न चन्द्रसङ्काशं
द्रक्ष्यामि शुभमाननम् ।
भ्रातुः पद्मपलाशाक्षं
न मे शान्तिर्भविष्यति ॥
I shall have no peace so long as I do not see the face, beautiful as the moon, of my brother whose eyes are like lotus petals.
2.98.8
यावन्न चरणौ भ्रातुः
पार्थिवव्यञ्जनान्वितौ ।
शिरसा धारयिष्यामि
न मे शान्तिर्भविष्यति ॥
I shall have no peace till I place on my head the feet of my brother which bear all the marks of royalty.
2.98.9
यावन्न राज्ये राज्यार्हः
पितृपैतामहे स्थितः ।
अभिषेकजलक्लिन्नो
न मे शान्तिर्भविष्यति ॥
I shall have no peace till he who is worthy of the kingdom is sprinkled with the water of coronation and established in the kingdom of the forefathers.
2.98.10
सिद्धार्थः खलु सौमित्रिः
यश्चन्द्रविमलोपमम् ।
मुखं पश्यति रामस्य
राजीवाक्षं महाद्युति ॥
The son of Sumitrā is indeed blessed, since he sees the supremely radiant face of Rāma, that is comparable to spotless moon, and with eyes like the lotus.
2.98.11
कृतकृत्या महाभागा
वैदेही जनकात्मजा ।
भर्तारं सागरान्तायाः
पृथिव्या यानुगच्छति ॥
The blessed princess of Vidēha, daughter of Janaka, who followed him, the lord of all the earth up to the shores of the ocean, has indeed has her life fulfilled.
2.98.12
सुभगश्चित्रकूटोऽसौ
गिरिराजोपमो गिरिः ।
यस्मिन्वसति काकुत्स्थः
कुबेर इव नन्दने ॥
Fortunate is this Citrakūṭa mountain, which is comparable to the king of mountains (Himavān) in which Kākutstha lives, like Kubēra in (the garden of) Nandana.
2.98.13
कृतकार्यमिदं दुर्गं
वनं व्यालनिषेवितम् ।
यदध्यास्ते महातेजा
रामश्शस्त्रभृतां वरः ॥
The purpose of this impregnable Vana that is full of wild beasts, is served, with the supremely radiant Rāma, the foremost among those who wield weapons, living in it.
2.98.14
एवमुक्त्वा महातेजा
भरतः पुरुषर्षभ: ।
पद्भ्यामेव महाबाहुः
प्रविवेश महाद्वनम् ॥
Saying thus, the supremely radiant, Bharata of mighty arm, a bull among men, entered that great Vana on foot.
2.98.15
स तानि द्रुमजालानि
जातानि गिरिसानुषु ।
पुष्पिताग्राणि मध्येन
जगाम वदतां वरः ॥
He, foremost of men skilled in speech, made his way through the groves of trees that grew on mountain slopes, their tops in full bloom.
2.98.16
स गिरेश्चित्रकूटस्य
सालमासाद्य पुष्पितम् ।
रामाश्रमगतस्याग्नेः
ददर्श ध्वजमुच्छ्रितम् ॥
Upon reaching the Sāla tree that was in bloom on Citrakūṭa, he saw the rising column of smoke from the fire from Rāma's Āṡrama.
2.98.17
तं दृष्ट्वा भरतश्श्रीमान्
मुमोद सह बान्धवः ।
अत्र राम इति ज्ञात्वा
गतः पारमिवाम्भसः ॥
Seeing that, and knowing that Rāma was there, Bharata, of rich bearing, rejoiced along with his kin, like one who had reached the farther shore of the waters.
2.98.18
स चित्रकूटे तु गिरौ निशम्य
रामाश्रमं पुण्यजनोपपन्नम् ।
गुहेन सार्धं त्वरितो जगाम
पुनर्निवेश्यैव चमूं महात्मा ॥
Seeing the Āṡrama of Rāma that was frequented by men of Puṇya, on that Citrakūṭa mountain, that Mahātma sped fast towards it along with Guha, leaving the army at a distance.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे अष्टनवतितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninety eighth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
