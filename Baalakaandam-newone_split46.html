Baala Kaanda - Sarga 46
In the previous Sarga, the sons of Aditi killed the sons of Diti, in the fight that erupted as Amṛta came out of the ocean as a result of churning.
In this Sarga, Diti sets herself upon doing a thousand year old Tapa to get a son who can kill Indra, the leader among the sons of Aditi.
Rāmāyaṇa is called an epic for a reason. The stories herein are of epic scale and defy human considerations and imagination.
In this story, Diti setting herself upon a thousand year Tapa for getting a son that can kill Indra is beyond the scale of humans. So is the dutiful and patient service by Indra to his step-mother Diti, though with an intention.
So is Indra's patient wait to cut off the fetus, before it is born. So is the magical and brutal killing. If this leaves you in awe, wait for more exciting fare to come, as the story continues into the next Sarga.
1.46.1
हतेषु तेषु पुत्रेषु
दितिः परमदुःखिता ।
मारीचं काश्यपं राम
भर्त्तारमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
O Rāma! Diti was overwhelmed with sorrow as her sons were killed. She said to her husband Kāṡyapa, son of Mareeci:
1.46.2
हतपुत्राऽस्मि भगवन्
तव पुत्रैर्महाबलैः ।
शक्रहन्तारमिच्छामि
पुत्रं दीर्घतपोर्जितम् ॥
O Bhagawan! My sons were killed by your mighty strong sons. With long Tapa I would like to have a son who can kill Ṡakra (Indra).
1.46.3
साहं तपश्चरिष्यामि
गर्भं मे दातुमर्हसि ।
ईश्वरं शक्रहन्तारम्
त्वमनुज्ञातुमर्हसि ॥
I will perform (the required) Tapa. May you bestow me a son that can kill Ṡakra and control the entire world.
1.46.4
तस्यास्तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
मारीचः काश्यपस्तदा ।
प्रत्युवाच महातेजा
दितिं परमदुःखिताम् ॥
Hearing her words, the supremely radiant Kāṡyapa, the son of Mareeci, said to Diti who was deeply sad:
1.46.5
एवं भवतु भद्रं ते
शुचिर्भव तपोधने ।
जनयिष्यसि पुत्रं त्वम्
शक्रहन्तारमाहवे ॥
May it be so! May all bode well for you! You will bear a son that can kill Ṡakra in the fight. You are rich with Tapa! May you maintain Ṡuchi!
Maintaining Ṡuchi means keeping body and mind clean and pure in accordance with the regimen laid out in the Ṡāstras. 
1.46.6
पूर्णे वर्षसहस्रे तु
शुचिर्यदि भविष्यसि ।
पुत्रं त्रैलोक्यभर्तारम्
मत्तस्त्वं जनयिष्यसि ॥
If you manage to maintain Ṡuchi, you would have a son by me at the end of one thousand years who can rule all the three worlds.
Having a clean body and clean mind obviously would result in healthy and strong offspring.
The stories in the Rāmāyaṇa indirectly encourage and inspire people to consider being clean both inside and outside all the time in general, and during pregnancy in particular.
1.46.7
एवमुक्त्वा महातेजाः
पाणिना स ममार्ज ताम् ।
समालभ्य ततस्स्वस्ती -
त्युक्त्वा स तपसे ययौ ॥
After saying thus, the supremely radiant (Kāṡyapa) stroked down her body with his hand and bid farewell to her and left for doing Tapa.
The concept of Tapa is elaborate, deep and even enigmatic. It keeps recurring throughout the Rāmāyaṇa.
Kings, Dēvas and Dānavas perform Tapa mostly for gaining certain powers or to gain boons from the Gods.
Brāhmaṇas do Tapa mostly as a matter of spiritual practice to gain spiritual energy.
Gods themselves do Tapa (including Brahma - see 1.44.10), as that is their default state of being.
1.46.8
गते तस्मिन्नरश्रेष्ठ
दितिः परमहर्षिता ।
कुशप्लवनमासाद्य
तपस्तेपे सुदारुणम् ॥
O best among men! After he left (thus granting her wish), Diti felt better and happy. She went to the land of Kuṡaplavana and performed very demanding Tapa.
1.46.9
तपस्तस्यां हि कुर्वन्त्याम्
परिचर्यां चकार ह ।
सहस्राक्षो नरश्रेष्ठ
परया गुणसम्पदा ॥
O best among men! As she was performing Tapa, the thousand-eyed (Indra) took care of all her personal needs with utmost respect and regard.
1.46.10
अग्निं कुशान् काष्ठमपः
फलं मूलं तथैव च ।
न्यवेदयत् सहस्राक्षो
यच्चान्यदपि काङ्क्षितम् ॥
The thousand-eyed (Indra) took care of whatever she needed and wanted, like Kuṡa grass, twigs, water, fire, fruits and roots.
1.46.11
गात्रसंवहनैश्चैव
श्रमापनयनैस्तथा ।
शक्रस्सर्वेषु कालेषु
दितिं परिचचार ह ॥
Ṡakra took care of Diti all the time, as needed, including giving her massage to relieve her bodily stress.
1.46.12
अथ वर्षसहस्रे तु
दशोने रघुनन्दन ।
दितिः परमसम्प्रीता
सहस्राक्षमथाब्रवीत् ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Just as there were ten more years to go by before the completion of a thousand years, Diti, who was very pleased with Indra said:
1.46.13
याचितेन सुरश्रेष्ठ
पित्रा तव महात्मना ।
वरो वर्षसहस्रान्ते
मम दत्तस्सुतं प्रति ॥
O best among the Suras! Your father granted me a boon that I would have a son after a thousand years.
1.46.14
तपश्चरन्त्या वर्षाणि
दश वीर्यवतां वर ।
अवशिष्टानि भद्रं ते
भ्रातरं द्रक्ष्यसे ततः ॥
O best among Veeras! Just ten more years are left before my Tapa will come to its end. Then you will be able to see your new born brother. May all bode well for you!
1.46.15
तमहं त्वत्कृते पुत्र
समाधास्ये जयोत्सुकम् ।
त्रैलोक्यविजयं पुत्र
सह भोक्ष्यसि विज्वरः ॥
Dear son! For your sake I will convince him to win over all the three worlds. You, along with him, may enjoy it all, leaving all worries behind you.
1.46.16
एवमुक्त्वा दितिः शक्रम्
प्राप्ते मध्यं दिवाकरे ।
निद्रयापहृता देवी
पादौ कृत्वाऽथ शीर्षतः ॥
As mid-day was approaching, Diti fell asleep with her legs stretched in the direction where her head would be.
It is like someone crashing, after a tiring day of work, in the wrong direction on the bed, with feet in the direction of the headboard and head in the direction of footboard.
1.46.17
दृष्ट्वा तामशुचिं शक्रः
पादतः कृतमूर्द्धजाम् ।
शिरस्स्थाने कृतौ पादौ
जहास च मुमोद च ॥
Ṡakra, seeing her with her hair lying in the direction of feet, and feet lying where the head should be, thus violating the principles of Ṡuchi, had a laugh and felt very happy.
1.46.18
तस्याश्शरीरविवरम्
विवेश च पुरन्दरः ।
गर्भं च सप्तधा राम
बिभेद परमात्मवान् ॥
O Rāma! Then, the self-assured Purandara (Indra) entered her body through the opening and cut the fetus into seven pieces.
1.46.19
भिद्यमानस्ततो गर्भो
वज्रेण शतपर्वणा ।
रुरोद सुस्वरं राम
ततो दितिरबुध्यत ॥
O Rāma! Cut thus by the Vajra that has a hundred teeth, the fetus cried pitifully and Diti woke up by that sound.
Vajra is Indra's weapon that looks like a chain saw.
1.46.20
मा रुदो मा रुदश्चेति
गर्भं शक्रोऽभ्यभाषत ।
बिभेद च महातेजा
रुदन्तमपि वासवः ॥
The supremely radiant Vāsava (Indra) cut the fetus that was crying, saying: "Shhh... stay calm, stay quiet, do not cry!"
1.46.21
न हन्तव्यो न हन्तव्य
इत्येवं दितिरब्रवीत् ।
निष्पपात ततश्शक्रो
मातुर्वचनगौरवात् ॥
"Don't kill! Don't kill!" cried Diti. Heeding the words of his (step) mother Indra came out immediately.
1.46.22
प्राञ्जलिर्वज्रसहितो
दितिं शक्रोऽभ्यभाषत ।
अशुचिर्देवि सुप्ताऽसि
पादयोः कृतमूर्द्धजा ॥
With folded hands, which were still holding the Vajra, Ṡakra (Indra) said to Diti: You were sleeping violating the rules of Ṡuchi, with your feet where your head should have been.
1.46.23
तदन्तरमहं लब्ध्वा
शक्रहन्तारमाहवे ।
अभिदं सप्तधा देवि
तन्मे त्वं क्षन्तुमर्हसि ॥
Hence, I got the chance to cut the fetus into seven pieces, who could otherwise kill me in a fight. Please forgive me with good heart.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे षट्चत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty sixth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
