Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 10
In this Sarga, Sugreeva continues to tell Rāma the story of his enmity with Vāli. He tells how he tried to placate his brother and pleaded with him, gave back his kingdom, how Vāli disregarded his pleas, summoned all the ministers and citizens, and how in their midst accused Sugreeva of locking him up in the cave, how Vāli scorned and expelled him, how he took his wife away and how he settled on Ṛshyamūka after fleeing all around the earth in fear.
Rāma empathizes with Sugreeva saying that he could understand Sugreeva's plight as he was also in a similar one. He assures Sugreeva that Vāli would live only until he sets his eyes on him.
4.10.1
ततः क्रोधसमाविष्टम्
सम्रब्धं तमुपागतम् ।
अहं प्रसादयाञ्चक्रे
भ्रातरं प्रियकाम्यया ॥
I sought to placate my brother who came with a such tempestuous rage, for the good of everyone:
4.10.2
दिष्ट्यासि कुशली प्राप्तो
निहतश्च त्वया रिपुः ।
अनाथस्य हि मे नाथः
त्वमेकोऽनाथनन्दनः ॥
By good fortune, you have returned safe slaying your enemy. You, because of whom the hearts of the defenseless rejoice, are my sole refuge and I have no one else.
4.10.3
इदं बहुशलाकं ते
पूर्णचन्द्रमिवोदितम् ।
छत्रं सवालव्यजनम्
प्रतीच्छस्व मया धृतम् ॥
Please accept this many-ribbed umbrella that is (splendid) like the moon risen (above the horizon), held by me along with the fly-whisks made from the tail of the animal Camari.
4.10.4
आर्तश्चाथ बिलद्वारि
स्थितस्संवत्सरं नृप ।
दृष्ट्वाऽहं शोणितं द्वारि
बिलाच्चापि समुत्थितम् ॥
O King! I waited for a whole year helplessly at the entrance of the cave. Then I saw blood issuing from the mouth of the cave.
4.10.5
शोकसंविग्नहृदयो
भृशं व्याकुलितेन्द्रियः ।
अपिधाय बिलद्वारम्
शैलशृङ्गेण तत्तथा ।
तस्माद्देशादपाक्रम्य
किष्किन्धां प्राविशं पुनः ॥
My heart rent with grief and my senses thrown in a turmoil, I shut the entrance to the cave with a mountain crest and left, and returned to Kishkindhā.
4.10.6
विषादात्विह मां दृष्ट्वा
पौरैर्मन्त्रिभिरेव च ।
अभिषिक्तो न कामेन
तन्मेत्वं क्षन्तुमर्हसि ॥
The citizens and ministers, seeing me return grief-stricken, crowned me, though I had no desire for it. May you please forgive me!
4.10.7
त्वमेव राजा मानार्हः
सदा चाहं यथा पुरम् ।
राजभावे नियुक्तोऽहम्
मम त्वद्विरहात्कृतः ।
सामात्यपौरनगरम्
स्थितं निहतकण्टकम् ॥
You, the honorable, are the king always, as you were before. I played the role of king because you were not there. The kingdom, with all the ministers, citizens and cities is intact, free of troubles.
4.10.9
न्यासभूतमिदं राज्यम्
तव निर्यातयाम्यहम् ।
मा च रोषं कृथास्सौम्य
मयि शत्रु निषूदन ॥
O destroyer of enemies! I give this kingdom back to you which I have been holding in trust. O gentle one! Please do not be angry with me!
4.10.10
याचे त्वां शिरसा राजन्
मया बद्धोऽयमञ्जलिः ।
बलादस्मिन् समागम्य
मन्त्रिभिः पुरवासिभिः ।
राजभावे नियुक्तोऽहम्
शून्यदेशजिगीषया ॥
O King! I beg you bowing my head and with these palms of mine joined. It was the ministers and the people of the city that came together and compelled me to accept the kingship, from the fear of leaving the country headless.
4.10.11
स्निग्धमेवं ब्रुवाणं माम्
स तु निर्भर्त्स्य वानरः ।
धिक्त्वामिति च मामुक्त्वा
बहु तत्तदुवाच ह ॥
I thus spoke with love, but the Vānara browbeat me, yelling, ‘Dhik, you!’, repeatedly.
4.10.12
प्रकृतीश्च समानीय
मुन्त्रिणश्चैव सम्मतान् ।
मामाह सुहृदां मध्ये
वाक्यं परमगर्हितम् ॥
Then summoning all the honorable ministers and all the people (of the kingdom), he spoke extremely scornfully to me, in the midst of all my friends.
4.10.13
विदितं वो यथा रात्रौ
मायावी स महासुरः ।
मां समाह्वयत क्रूरो
युद्धाकाङ्क्षी सुदुर्मतिः ॥
All of you know that Māyāvi, the great Asura, the cruel and evil one, had called me in the night for a fight.
4.10.14
तस्य तद्भाषितम् श्रुत्वा
निस्सृतोऽहं नृपालयात् ।
अनुयातश्च मां तूर्णम्
अयं भ्राता सुदारुणः ॥
Hearing that call from him, I came out of the royal palace. This utterly wicked brother of mine followed me immediately.
4.10.15
स तु दृष्ट्वैव मां रात्रौ
सद्वितीयं महाबलः ।
प्राद्रवद्भयसन्त्रस्तो
वीक्ष्यावां समुपागतौ ।
अनुद्रुतश्च वेगेन
प्रविवेश महाबिलम् ॥
Seeing me accompanied by one more, he, though of immense strength, fled in fear that night. Seeing us coming at him together, he sped fast and entered a huge cave.
4.10.17
तं प्रविष्टं विदित्वा तु
सुघोरं सुमहद्बिलम् ।
अयमुक्तोऽथ मे भ्राता
मया तु क्रूरदर्शनः ॥
When I knew that he had entered a huge and dreadful cave, I told this brother of mine, who (apparently) harbored wicked plans:
4.10.18
अहत्वा नास्ति मे शक्तिः
प्रतिगन्तुमितः पुरीम् ।
बिलद्वारि प्रतीक्ष त्वम्
यावदेनं निहन्म्यहम् ॥
I cannot go back to the city from here without killing (him). Wait at this entrance of the cave while I go and I kill (him).
4.10.19
स्थितोऽयमिति मत्वा तु
प्रविष्टोऽहं दुरासदम् ।
तं च मे मार्गमाणस्य
गतस्संवत्सरस्तदा ॥
Assuming that he would wait there I entered that formidable cave. Then a year passed by while I searched for him.
4.10.20
स तु दृष्टो मया शत्रुः
अनिर्वेदाद्भयावहः ।
निहतश्च मया सद्यः
स सर्वैः सह बन्धुभिः ॥
Finally, I cast my merciless eye on that enemy who was then gripped by fear. In no time, he and all his relations were killed by me.
4.10.21
तस्यास्यात्तु प्रवृत्तेन
रुधिरौघेण तद्बिलम् ।
पूर्णमासीद्दुराक्रामम्
स्वनतः तस्य भूतले ॥
While he fell on the ground crying, the blood that welled from his mouth filled the cave, leaving no room to walk around.
4.10.22
सूदयित्वा तु तं शत्रुम्
विक्रान्तं दुन्दुभेस्सुतम् ।
निष्क्रामन्नेव पश्यामि
बिलस्य पिहितं मुखम् ॥
Having slain my enemy, the valiant son of Dundhubhi, I could not find the way out as the mouth of the cave was blocked.
4.10.23
विक्रोशमानस्य तु मे
सुग्रीवेति पुनः पुनः ।
यदा प्रतिवचो नास्ति
ततोऽहं भृशदुःखितः ॥
I shouted aloud calling ‘Sugreeva!’ repeatedly, but there was no reply and I became extremely miserable.
4.10.24
पादप्रहारैस्तु मया
बहुभिस्तद्विदारितम् ।
ततोऽहं तेन निष्क्रम्य
यथा पुरमुपागतः ॥
With many a kick I shattered that barrier. Then I got out and returned to the city.
4.10.25
तत्रानेनास्मि संरुद्धो
राज्यं मृगयताऽऽत्मनः ।
सुग्रीवेण नृशंसेन
विस्मृत्य भ्रातृसौहृदम् ॥
Thus I was locked up there by this cruel Sugreeva, who wanted this kingdom for himself, disregarding affection that brothers owe to each other.
4.10.26
एवमुक्त्वा तु मां तत्र
वस्त्रेणैकेन वानरः ।
निर्वासयामास तदा
वाली विगतसाध्वसः ॥
Saying this, Vāli, the Vānara, with no compunction whatsoever, expelled me right then, with just the single piece of clothing (that was on me).
4.10.27
तेनाहमपविद्धश्च
हृतदारश्च राघव ।
तद्भयाच्छ महीकृत्स्ना
क्रान्तेयं सवनार्णवा ॥
Expelled by him, my wife taken away, and afraid of him, I fled from place to place around the entire earth, including all its forests and oceans.
4.10.28
ऋष्यमूकं गिरिवरम्
भार्याहरणदुःखितः ।
प्रविष्टोऽस्मि दुराधर्षम्
वालिनः कारणान्तरे ॥
Saddened by the abduction of my wife, I have taken refuge in this great mountain Ṛshyamūka, which is forbidden ground to Vāli for certain reasons.
4.10.29
एतत्ते सर्वमाख्यातम्
वैरानुकथनं महत् ।
अनागसा मया प्राप्तम्
व्यसनं पश्य राघव ॥
Now I have told you the entire story about my great enmity. See the suffering that befell on me, for no fault of mine!
Sugreeva and Bharata are the two characters in Rāmāyaṇa that suffer for no fault of theirs, Sugreeva losing a kingdom and Bharata gaining a kingdom. Sugreeva earned everyone’s sympathy, but Bharata earned everyone’s suspicion, of having a hand in the entire episode.
Bharata lived under the shadow of suspicion for fourteen years, and Sugreeva suffered for an untold number of years.
Sugreeva had an honorable loss, but Bharata had a gain that put his honor and virtue in question, which is much harder to live with!
4.10.30
वालिनस्तु भयार्तस्य
सर्वलोक भयापह ।
कर्तुमर्हसि मे वीर
प्रसादं तस्य निग्रहात् ॥
O Veera! O you who remove the fear of all the worlds! May you bestow your grace on me, who is seized of fear of Vāli, and restrain him!
4.10.31
एवमुक्तस्स तेजस्वी
धर्मज्ञो धर्मसंहितम् ।
वचनं वक्तुमारेभे
सुग्रीवं प्रहसन्निव ॥
Thus besought, (Rāma) the powerful one who knew Dharma, answered Sugreeva with a smile, in these words buttressed by Dharma:
4.10.32
अमोघास्सूर्यसङ्काशा
ममैते निशिता श्शराः ।
तस्मिन्वालिनि दुर्वृत्ते
पतिष्यन्ति रुषान्विताः ॥
These unfailing sharp and sun-bright arrows of mine, shall fall on that evil Vāli with all the rage.
4.10.33
यावत्तं नाभिपश्यामि
तव भार्यापहारिणम् ।
तावत्स जीवेत्पापात्मा
वाली चारित्रदूषकः ॥
The evil Vāli, the abductor of your wife, who is guilty of setting a bad precedent, shall live only until I set my eyes upon him.
4.10.34
आत्मानुमानात्पश्यामि
मग्नं त्वां शोकसागरे ।
त्वामहं तारयिष्यामि
कामं प्राप्स्यसि पुष्कलम् ॥
I can understand the ocean of sorrow that you are in, going by my own experience. I shall ferry you out of it and you shall have all that your heart desires.
4.10.35
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
हर्षपौरुषवर्धनम् ।
सुग्रीवः परमप्रीतः
सुमहद्वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those words that augured joy and self-confidence, the extremely pleased Sugreeva said these pregnant words:
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे दशमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the tenth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
