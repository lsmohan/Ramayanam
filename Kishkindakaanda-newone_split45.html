Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 45
In this Sarga, the captains of the Vānaras, set out in the directions assigned to them, boast that each one of them can kill Rāvaṇa singlehandedly and bring Seetā back.
Rāma lives, along with Lakshmaṇa, on the Prasravaṇa mountain for the duration of the month that was set for finding Seetā.
4.45.1
सर्वांश्चाहूय सुग्रीवः
प्लवगान् प्लवगर्षभः ।
समस्तानब्रवीद्राजा
रामकार्यार्थसिद्धये ।
एवमेतद्विचेतव्यम्
भवद्भिर्वानरोत्तमैः ॥
Thus Sugreeva, the bull among Vānaras, summoned all the Vānaras and told all those excellent Vānaras that the search should proceed as instructed for accomplishing the mission of Rāma.
4.45.2
तदुग्रशासनं भर्तुः
विज्ञाय हरिपुङ्गवाः ।
शलभा इव सञ्छाद्य
मेदिनीं सम्प्रतस्थिरे ॥
Understanding the import of that stern command of their lord, the bulls among Vānaras started on their quest, covering the earth like moths.
4.45.3
रामः प्रस्रवणे तस्मिन्
न्यवसत्सहलक्ष्मणः ।
प्रतीक्षमाणस्तं मासम्
यस्सीताधिगमे कृतः ॥
Rāma lived, along with Lakshmaṇa, on the Prasravaṇa mountain for the duration of the month that was set for finding Seetā.
4.45.4
उत्तरां तु दिशं रम्याम्
गिरिराजसमावृताम् ।
प्रतस्थे सहसा वीरो
हरिश्शतबलिस्तदा ।
पूर्वां दिशं प्रतिययौ
विनतो हरियूथपः ॥
The valiant ape Ṡatabali set out immediately in the pleasant northerly direction crowned by the king of mountains. Vinata, a commander of Vānaras, started for the East.
4.45.6
ताराङ्गदादिसहितः
प्लवङ्गो पवनात्मजः ।
अगत्याचरितामाशाम्
दक्षिणां हरियूथपः ॥
The son of Vāyu, along with many captains of Vānara forces, like Tāra and Aṅgada went to the South, where Agastya lived.
4.45.7
पश्चिमां तु भृशं घोराम्
सुषेणः प्लवगोत्तमः ।
प्रतस्थे हरिशार्दूलो
दिशं वरुणपालिताम् ॥
Sushēṇa, the great tiger among the Vānaras, went towards the dreaded West guarded by Varuṇa.
4.45.8
ततस्सर्वा दिशो राजा
चोदयित्वा यथातथम् ।
कपिसेनापतीन्वीरो
मुमोद सुखितस्सुखम् ॥
Having thus dispatched all the captains of the Vānara forces in every direction as planned, the king, the Veera, felt relieved, and enjoyed in relaxation.
4.45.9
एवं संचोदितास्सर्वे
राज्ञा वानरयूथपाः ।
स्वां स्वां दिशमभिप्रेत्य
त्वरिता सम्प्रतस्थिरे ॥
Thus sent by the king, the Vānara chiefs set out in the directions assigned to them, in all haste.
4.45.10
नदन्तश्चोन्नदन्तश्च
गर्जन्तश्च प्लवङ्गमाः ।
क्ष्वेलन्तो धावमानाश्च
विनदन्तो महाबलाः ॥
With some of them shouting, some yelling, some roaring, some howling and some sounding like thunder, the Vānaras of great strength went running.
4.45.11
एवं संचोदितास्सर्वे
राज्ञा वानरयूथपाः ॥
The Vānara chiefs, thus sent by the king, (together and individually), declared:
4.45.11
आनयिष्यामहे सीताम्
हनिष्यामश्च रावणम् ॥
We will kill Rāvaṇa! We will bring Seetā!
4.45.12
अहमेको हनिष्यामि
प्राप्तं रावणमाहवे ।
ततश्चोन्मथ्य सहसा
हरिष्ये जनकात्मजाम् ॥
I shall singlehandedly slay Rāvaṇa, when I meet him on the battlefield, hammer all others whom I encounter and carry back Janaka’s daughter!
4.45.13
वेपमानां श्रमेणाद्य
भवद्भिः स्थीयतामिति ।
एक एवाहरिष्यामि
पातालादपि जानकीम् ॥
I shall fetch back the trembling and exhausted Jānaki, all by myself, even if she is in the Pātāla; you can all just stay here!
4.45.14
विधमिष्याम्यहं वृक्षान्
दारयिष्याम्यहं गिरीन् ।
धरणीं दारयिष्यामि
क्षोभयिष्यामि सागरान् ॥
I shall fling away the trees, tear down the mountains, tear apart the earth and set the oceans in a turmoil!
4.45.15
अहं योजनसङ्ख्यायाः
प्लविता नात्र संशयः ॥
Without a doubt, I can jump one hundred Yōjanas!
4.45.15
शतयोजनसङ्ख्यायाः
शतं समधिकं ह्यहम् ॥
I can jump more than one hundred Yōjanas!
4.45.16
भूतले सागरे वाऽपि
शैलेषु च वनेषु च ।
पातालस्यापि वा मध्ये
न ममाऽऽच्छिद्यते गतिः ॥
Nothing can stop my movement anywhere, be it on the earth or in the sea, in the mountains or the forests, or even in Pātāla!
4.45.17
इत्येकैकस्तदा तत्र
वानरा बलदर्पिताः ।
ऊचुश्च वचनं तत्र
हरिराजस्य सन्निधौ ॥
With their vanity of strength on full display, thus boasted each one of those Vānaras in the presence of the Lord of Vānaras.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे पञ्चचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty fifth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
