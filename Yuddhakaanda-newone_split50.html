Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 50
In this Sarga, Sugreeva finds that the Vānaras are panicking, mistaking Vibheeshaṇa to be Indrajit, and asks Jāmbavān to tell them that it is not the case and restore order among them. Vibheeshaṇa is himself in agony, seeing Rāma and Lakshmaṇa lying on the ground, locked in a mesh of arrows. He laments that his life itself is at risk now, never mind his aspirations to the throne. Sugreeva consoles him saying that Rāma and Lakshmaṇa are only unconscious, and not in death-pangs.
He tells Sushēṇa, his father-in-law, that he should go back to Kishkindhā taking Rāma and Lakshmaṇa with him after they regain consciousness and that he would kill Rāvaṇa and recover Seetā. Sushēṇa then tells him of the divine herbs to be found on the mountains in the milky ocean that were used by Bṛihaspati to heal the Dēvas wounded in the fight between them and the Dānavas. He tells Sugreeva to send Hanumān, Saṃpāti, Panasa and others to bring those herbs.
Meanwhile Garuḍa flies down from the sky, on seeing whom, the serpents, sons of Kadru, that were transformed into arrows by Indrajit, that were clutching Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, slithered away, fleeing that place. With the touch of Garuḍa, the wounds of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa get healed and they regain their strength, valor and all their other qualities, in double the measure. Garuḍa leaves after exchanging pleasantries, and advising the brothers and the Vānaras to guard themselves against the devious fighting techniques of the Rākshasas.
Seeing the brothers become their usual selves, the Vānaras jump in joy and head to the gateways of Laṅkā, letting off war whoops.
6.50.1
अथोवाच महातेजा
हरिराजो महाबलः ॥
The king of Vānaras, one of great power and immense strength asked:
6.50.1
किमियं व्यथिता सेना
मूढवातेव नौर्जले ॥
Why is the army agitated like a ship in the water caught in crosswinds?’
6.50.2
सुग्रीवस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
वालिपुत्रोऽङ्गदोऽब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those words of Sugreeva, Aṅgada, the son of Vāli, said:
The flow of the narration would be smoother without this interjection by Aṅgada, to which Sugreeva does not respond.
6.50.2
न त्वं पश्यसि रामं च
लक्ष्मणं च महाबलम् ।
शरजालाचितौ वीरौ
उभौ दशरथात्मजौ ।
शरतल्पे महात्मानौ
शयानौ रुधिरोक्षितौ ॥
Do you not see the mighty sons of Daṡaratha, the valiant Rāma and Lakshmaṇa of the immense strength, lying on the bed of arrows, their blood-bathed bodies sown thick with shafts?
6.50.4
अथाब्रवीद्वानरेन्द्रः
सुग्रीवः पुत्रमङ्गदम् ॥
Then, Sugreeva, the king of Vānaras said to Aṅgada, who was like a son to him:
6.50.4
नानिमित्तमिदं मन्ये
भवितव्यं भयेन तु ।
विषण्णवदना ह्येते
त्यक्तप्रहरणा दिशः ।
पलायन्तेऽत्र हरयः
त्रासादुत्फुल्ललोचनाः ॥
I don’t think this stampede is without a cause. With frightened faces the Vānaras flee in all directions, abandoning their weapons, their eyes bulging with terror.
6.50.6
अन्योन्यस्य न लज्जन्ते
न निरीक्षन्ति पृष्ठतः ।
विप्रकर्षन्ति चान्योन्यम्
पतितं लङ्घयन्ति च ॥
No one seems to be gentle to others. They do not so much as look behind them. They drag each other and jump over those who fall.
6.50.7
एतस्मिन्नन्तरे वीरो
गदापाणिर्विभीषणः ।
सुग्रीवं वर्धयामास
राघवं च निरैक्षत ॥
At that moment the valiant Vibheeshaṇa, armed with the mace, greeted prosperity to Sugreeva, and took a close look at Rāghava.
6.50.8
विभीषणं तं सुग्रीवो
दृष्ट्वा वानरभीषणम् ।
ऋक्षराजं समीपस्थम्
जाम्बवन्तमुवाच ह ॥
Finding that it was Vibheeshaṇa who was the cause of the Vānaras’ fright, Sugreeva told Jāmbavān, the king of Ṛkshas, who was by his side:
6.50.9
विभीषणोऽयं सम्प्राप्तो
यं दृष्ट्वा वानरर्षभाः ।
विद्रवन्ति परित्रस्ता
रावणात्मजशङ्कया ॥
It is Vibheeshaṇa who has come, on seeing whom the bulls among Vānaras flee in terror, imagining that it is Rāvaṇa’s son.
6.50.10
शीघ्रमेतान् सुसन्त्रस्तान्
बहुधा विप्रधावितान् ।
प्रर्यवस्थापयाख्याहि
विभीषणमुपस्थितम् ॥
Quickly restore order among these who are running in every direction  in panic by telling them that it is Vibheeshaṇa who has come.
6.50.11
सुग्रीवेणैवमुक्तस्तु
जाम्बवानृक्षपार्थिवः ।
वानरान् सान्त्वयामास
सन्निरुध्य प्रधावतः ॥
Thus told by Sugreeva, Jāmbavān, the king of Ṛkshas, consoled the Vānaras and stopped them from fleeing.
6.50.12
ते निवृत्ताः पुनः सर्वे
वानरास्त्यक्तसम्भ्रमाः ।
ऋक्षराजवचः श्रुत्वा
तं च दृष्ट्वा विभीषणम् ॥
On hearing the words of the king of Ṛkshas and seeing that it was Vibheeshaṇa, all the Vānaras returned giving up their fear.
6.50.13
विभीषणस्तु रामस्य
दृष्ट्वा गात्रं शरैश्चितम् ।
लक्ष्मणस्य च धर्मात्मा
बभूव व्यथितेन्द्रियः ॥
But Vibheeshaṇa, the Dharmātma, was in agony, seeing arrows pierced all over the bodies of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
6.50.14
जलक्लिन्नेन हस्तेन
तयोर्नेत्रे प्रमृज्य च ।
शोकसम्पीडितमना
रुरोद विललाप च ॥
Taking water into his hands and wiping their eyes with his wet hand, his heart distressed by grief, he cried and lamented:
6.50.15
इमौ तौ सत्त्वसम्पन्नौ
विक्रान्तौ प्रियसंयुगौ ।
इमामवस्थां गमितौ
राक्षसैः कूटयोधिभिः ॥
These two are extremely strong and are full of prowess, who love to be in a fight; it is the Rākshasas’ unfair fighting that has brought them to this pass.
6.50.16
भ्रातुः पुत्रेण मे तेन
दुष्पुत्रेण दुरात्मना ।
राक्षस्या जिह्मया बुद्ध्या
चालितावृजुविक्रमौ ॥
They, who only strike in a fair and square manner, were duped by my brother’s son an evil son and an evil soul, with crafty meanness that comes naturally to Rākshasas.
6.50.17
शरैरिमावलं विद्धौ
रुधिरेण समुक्षितौ ।
वसुधायामिमौ सुप्तौ
दृश्येते शल्यकाविव ॥
Lying unconscious on the ground, their bodies bathed in blood and pierced enough by arrows they look like porcupines.
6.50.18
ययोर्वीर्यमुपाश्रित्य
प्रतिष्ठा काङ्क्षिता मया ।
तावुभौ देहनाशाय
प्रसुप्तौ पुरुषर्षभौ ॥
These two bulls among men, on whom I pinned my hopes of securing sovereignty lie here deeply unconscious, with their bodies on the verge of deterioration.
6.50.19
जीवन्नद्य विपन्नोऽस्मि
नष्टराज्यमनोरथः ।
प्राप्तप्रतिज्ञश्च रिपुः
सकामो रावणः कृतः ॥
My dream of gaining the kingdom is dashed, and my life itself is at risk, while my enemy Rāvaṇa has made good his boast and realized his wish.
6.50.20
एवं विलपमानं तम्
परिष्वज्य विभीषणम् ।
सुग्रीवः सत्त्वसम्पन्नो
हरिराजोऽब्रवीदिदम् ॥
As Vibheeshaṇa thus lamented, the powerful Sugreeva, King of Vānaras, embraced him and said:
6.50.21
राज्यं प्राप्स्यसि धर्मज्ञ
लङ्कायां नात्र संशयः ।
रावणः सह पुत्रेण
सकामं नेह लप्स्यते ॥
O Dharmajña! You will attain the kingdom of Laṅkā without a doubt. Neither Rāvaṇa, nor his sons shall have their wishes fulfilled.
6.50.22
न रुजा पीडितावेतौ
उभौ राघवलक्ष्मणौ ।
त्यक्त्वा मोहं वधिष्येते
सगणं रावणं रणे ॥
Rāghava and Lakshmaṇa are not in the grip of a death-pang, they are in a faint; on recovering, they will destroy Rāvaṇa and all his retinue in the battle.
6.50.23
तमेनं सान्त्वयित्वा तु
समाश्वास्य च राक्षसम् ।
सुषेणं श्वशुरं पार्श्वे
सुग्रीवस्तमुवाच ह ॥
Having thus consoled and pacified the Rākshasa, Sugreeva said to Sushēṇa, his father-in-law, who was by his side:
6.50.24
सह शूरैर्हरिगणैः
लब्धसञ्ज्ञावरिन्दमौ ।
गच्छ त्वं भ्रातरौ गृह्य
किष्किन्धां रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
Go back to Kishkindhā along with contingents of valiant Vānaras, taking these brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, subduers of the foe, with you, as soon as they regain consciousness.
6.50.25
अहं तु रावणं हत्वा
सपुत्रं सहबान्धवम् ।
मैथिलीमानयिष्यामि
शक्रो नष्टामिव श्रियम् ॥
I shall slay Rāvaṇa, his sons and all his kin, and bring Maithili, just as Ṡakra recovered his lost wealth.
6.50.26
श्रुत्वैतद्वानरेन्द्रस्य
सुषेणो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
On hearing the words of the king of Vānaras, Sushēṇa said these words:
6.50.26
दैवासुरं महद्युद्धम्
अनुभूतं सुदारुणम् ।
तदा स्म दानवा देवान्
शरसंस्पर्शकोविदाः ।
निजघ्नुः शस्त्रविदुषः
छादयन्तो मुहुर्मुहुः ॥
I have witnessed an immense and extremely fierce battle between the Dēvas and the Asuras. In that battle the Dānavas, skilled in archery, struck down the Dēvas who were experts in Ṡastras by hiding themselves from view again and again.
6.50.28
तानार्तान्नष्टसञ्ज्ञांश्च
गतासूंश्च बृहस्पतिः ।
विद्याभिर्मन्त्रयुक्ताभिः
ओषधीभिश्चिकित्सति ॥
When they were distraught and lost consciousness and even their Prāṇa, Bṛihaspati helped them recover with his knowledge of healing chants and herbs.
6.50.29
तान्यौषधान्यानयितुम्
क्षीरोदं यान्तु सागरम् ।
जवेन वानराः शीघ्रम्
सम्पातिपनसादयः ॥
Let the Vānaras Saṃpāti, Panasa and others start immediately and go quickly to the milky ocean to bring those herbs.
6.50.30
हरयस्तु विजानन्ति
पार्वतीस्ता महौषधीः ।
सञ्जीवकरणीं दिव्याम्
विशल्यां देवनिर्मिताम् ॥
The Vānaras know those great herbs that grow on the mountain there. Let them fetch the divine herbs created by the Dēvas, which revive the dead and make men whole.
6.50.31
चन्द्रश्च नाम द्रोणश्च
क्षीरोदे सागरोत्तमे ।
अमृतं यत्र मथितम्
तत्र ते परमौषधी ॥
Those prime herbs are to be found on the mountains Candra and Drōṇa situated in the milky ocean, the best of oceans, where Amṛt was churned out.
6.50.32
तौ तत्र विहितौ देवैः
पर्वतौ तौ महोदधौ ।
अयं वायुसुतो राजन्
हनुमांस्तत्र गच्छतु ॥
Those two mountains were established in that great ocean by the Dēvas themselves. Let Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, go there, O King!
6.50.33
एतस्मिन्नन्तरे वायुः
मेघांश्चापि सविद्युतः ।
पर्यस्यन् सागरे तोयम्
कम्पयन्निव मेदिनीम् ॥
At that moment strong winds blew scattering clouds that issued lightning, sending up the water sky-high on the sea, and shaking the earth, as it were.
6.50.34
महता पक्षवातेन
सर्वद्वीपमहाद्रुमाः ।
निपेतुर्भिन्नविटपाः
समूला लवणाम्भसि ॥
That strong wind from the wings (of Garuḍa) snapped the boughs of all the huge trees on the islands and uprooted them, throwing them into the salty ocean.
6.50.35
अभवन् पन्नगास्त्रस्ता
भोगिनस्तत्रवासिनः ।
शीघ्रं सर्वाणि यादांसि
जग्मुश्च लवणार्णवम् ॥
The huge hooded serpents that lived there were terrified. All aquatic monsters quickly disappeared in the salt sea.
6.50.36
ततो मुहूर्ताद्गरुडम्
वैनतेयं महाबलम् ।
वानरा ददृशुः सर्वे
ज्वलन्तमिव पावकम् ॥
In a short while the Vānaras beheld Garuḍa, the son of Vinata, of immense strength, dazzling like a tongue of flame.
6.50.37
तमागतमभिप्रेक्ष्य
नागास्ते विप्रदुद्रुवुः ।
यैस्तौ सत्पुरुषौ बद्धौ
शरभूतैर्महाबलौ ॥
On seeing him come, the serpents which, turning into extremely powerful arrows, had bound those good men, slithered away.
6.50.38
ततः सुपर्णः काकुत्स्थौ
दृष्ट्वा प्रत्यभिनन्दितः ।
विममर्श च पाणिभ्याम्
मुखे चन्द्रसमप्रभे ॥
Garuḍa looked at the scions of the Kākutsthas, congratulated them and gently touched them with his hands on their moon-bright faces.
6.50.39
वैनतेयेन संस्पृष्टाः
तयोः संरुरुहुर्व्रणाः ।
सुवर्णे च तनू स्निग्धे
तयोराशु बभूवतुः ॥
Their wounds were healed at the touch of Vinata’s son, and their bodies forthwith became glossy and golden.
6.50.40
तेजो वीर्यं बलं चौज
उत्साहश्च महागुणः ।
प्रदर्शनं च बुद्धिश्च
स्मृतिश्च द्विगुणं तयोः ॥
Their prowess, valor, strength, and splendor as well as the energy and enterprise that are priceless, and their acuteness of perception, intelligence and power of memory were all doubled.
6.50.41
तावुत्थाप्य महावीर्यौ
गरुडो वासवोपमौ ।
उभौ तौ सस्वजे हृष्टौ
रामश्चैनमुवाच ह ॥
Garuḍa raised those men of extraordinary valor, who were comparable to Vāsava and embraced them, who rejoiced to see him. And Rāma told him:
6.50.42
भवत्प्रसादाद्व्यसनम्
रावणिप्रभवं महत् ।
आवामिह व्यतिक्रान्तौ
पूर्ववद्बलिनौ कृतौ ॥
By your grace we have been rescued from the terrible suffering caused by the son of Rāvaṇa, and have regained our former strength.
6.50.43
यथा तातं दशरथम्
यथाऽजं च पितामहम् ।
तथा भवन्तमासाद्य
हृदयं मे प्रसीदति ॥
My heart is filled with joy with you here, like it would be in the presence of my father Daṡaratha or my grandfather Aja.
6.50.44
को भवान् रूपसम्पन्नो
दिव्यस्रगनुलेपनः ।
वसानो विरजे वस्त्रे
दिव्याभरणभूषितः ॥
Who may you be, honored Sir, with charming features and adorned with divine garlands and unguents, clad in spotless clothes and wearing splendid ornaments?
6.50.45
तमुवाच महातेजा
वैनतेयो महाबलः ।
पतत्रिराजः प्रीतात्मा
हर्षपर्याकुलेक्षणः ॥
Vainatēya, the king of birds, who was endowed with immense strength and power, was pleased to hear what he said, and he answered him, his eyes beaming with joy:
6.50.46
अहं सखा ते काकुत्स्थ
प्रियः प्राणो बहिश्चरः ।
गरुत्मानिह सम्प्राप्तो
युवाभ्यां साह्यकारणात् ॥
Consider me as your friend, O Kākutstha, and as your Prāṇa that happened to be outside of you. I am known as Garuḍa and I have come here to help you.
6.50.47
असुरा वा महावीर्या
दानवा वा महाबलाः ।
सुराश्चापि सगन्धर्वाः
पुरस्कृत्य शतक्रतुम् ।
नेमं मोक्षयितुं शक्ताः
शरबन्धं सुदारुणम् ।
मायाबलादिन्द्रजिता
निर्मितं क्रूरकर्मणा ॥
Neither Asuras of extreme valor nor Dānavas of immense strength, nor again the Dēvas along with Gandharvas led by Indra, can free one from these terrible arrow-bonds fastened on you by Indrajit of cruel deeds, using magical powers.
6.50.49
एते नागाः काद्रवेयाः
तीक्ष्णदंष्ट्रा विषोल्बणाः ।
रक्षोमायाप्रभावेण
शरा भूत्वा त्वदाश्रिताः ॥
These serpents with sharp fangs and deadly poison are the sons of Kadru. Because of the magic powers of the Rākshasa they turned into arrows and wrapped themselves around you.
6.50.50
सभाग्यश्चासि धर्मज्ञ
राम सत्यपराक्रम ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
समरे रिपुघातिना ॥
O Rāma! O Dharmajña! You of true prowess, and your brother Lakshmaṇa, who strikes down the foe in battle are very fortunate.
6.50.51
इमं श्रुत्वा तु वृत्तान्तम्
त्वरमाणोऽहमागतः ।
सहसा युवयोः स्नेहात्
सखित्वमनुपालयन् ॥
On hearing what happened, I rushed here immediately out of affection for both of you, to do what is only expected of a true friend.
6.50.52
मोक्षितौ च महाघोरात्
अस्मात् सायकबन्धनात् ।
अप्रमादश्च कर्तव्यो
युवाभ्यां नित्यमेव हि ॥
You are now freed from these terrible bonds of the arrows. Both of you should be ever vigilant.
6.50.53
प्रकृत्या राक्षसाः सर्वे
सङ्ग्रामे कूटयोधिनः ।
शूराणां शुद्धभावानाम्
भवतामार्जवं बलम् ॥
You are brave, strong, pure hearted and straightforward but the Rākshasas are devious fighters, by nature.
6.50.54
तन्न विश्वसितव्यं वो
राक्षसानां रणाजिरे ।
एतेनैवोपमानेन
नित्यं जिह्मा हि राक्षसाः ॥
Therefore, never trust that Rākshasas would be fair on the battle field. This incident is just an indication of how devious the Rākshasas always are.
6.50.55
एवमुक्त्वा ततो रामम्
सुपर्णः सुमहाबलः ।
परिष्वज्य सुहृत् स्निग्धम्
आप्रष्टुमुपचक्रमे ॥
Having said this, Suparṇa of immense strength embraced Rāma with all affection of a well-wisher, and sought his permission (to leave).
6.50.56
सखे राघव धर्मज्ञ
रिपूणामपि वत्सल ।
अभ्यनुज्ञातुमिच्छामि
गमिष्यामि यथागतम् ॥
O Rāghava! O Dharmajña! My dear friend! O you, who are considerate to even your enemies! Please allow me to leave. I shall go just as I came!
6.50.57
न च कौतूहलं कार्यम्
सखित्वं प्रति राघव ।
कृतकर्मा रणे वीरः
सखित्वमनुवेत्स्यसि ॥
Spare yourself from being curious about our friendship, O Rāghava! You will know about our friendship, after you, O heroic one, have achieved your end in the war.
6.50.58
बालवृद्धावशेषां तु
लङ्कां कृत्वा शरोर्मिभिः ।
रावणं च रिपुं हत्वा
सीतां समुपलप्स्यसे ॥
With the surge of your arrows leaving alive no one but the too young and too old in Laṅkā, and killing your enemy Rāvaṇa, you shall recover Seetā.
6.50.59
इत्येवमुक्त्वा वचनम्
सुपर्णः शीघ्रविक्रमः ।
रामं च विरुजं कृत्वा
मध्ये तेषां वनौकसाम् ।
प्रदक्षिणं ततः कृत्वा
परिष्वज्य च वीर्यवान् ।
जगामाकाशमाविश्य
सुपर्णः पवनो यथा ॥
Having said this to Rāma and healed his wounds, Suparṇa, of immense swiftness and valor, circumambulated him in reverence amidst the Vānaras. After embracing him, he sprang into the sky and went away faster than the wind.
6.50.61
विरुजौ राघवौ दृष्ट्वा
ततो वानरयूथपाः ।
सिंहनादांस्तदा नेदुः
लाङ्गूलान् दुधुवुस्तदा ॥
On seeing the wounds of the scions of the Raghus thus disappear, the great Vānara chiefs roared like lions, flinging their tails.
6.50.62
ततो भेरीः समाजघ्नुः
मृदङ्गांश्चाप्यनादयन् ।
दध्मुः शङ्खान् सम्प्रहृष्टाः
क्ष्वेलन्त्यपि यथापुरम् ॥
They beat the drums, played the Mṛdaṅgas, blew on their conches in joy, and made noises as usual.
6.50.63
अपरेस्फोट्य विक्रान्ता
वानरा नगयोधिनः ।
द्रुमानुत्पाट्य विविधान्
तस्थुः शतसहस्रशः ॥
Other Vānaras of great prowess, who fought armed with trees, slapped their shoulders again and again, and pulled out all kinds of trees and stood ready in their hundreds and thousands.
6.50.64
विसृजन्तो महानादान्
त्रासयन्तो निशाचरान् ।
लङ्काद्वारण्युपाजग्मुः
योद्धुकामाः प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
Letting off loud war whoops striking terror in the Night-rovers, the Vānaras rushed to the gateways of Laṅkā eager to put up a good fight.
6.50.65
ततस्तु भीमस्तुमुलो निनादो
बभूव शाखामृगयूथपानाम् ।
क्षये निदाघस्य यथा घनानां
नादः सुभीमो नदतां निशीथे ॥
The din the Vānara chiefs raised with their shouts was like the thunder of clouds in the night when the dry summer comes to an end.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे पञ्चाशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fiftieth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
The first part of this Sarga seems to be an unauthentic hodgepodge for many reasons.
First of all, Rāma had already regained consciousness (in the previous Sarga), but this Sarga starts as if he and Lakshmaṇa are still unconscious.
Second, Sarga 46 shows Sugreeva in shock and Vibheeshaṇa consoling him, which is more likely, for Sugreeva regained his strength because of Rāma, where as Vibheeshaṇa is strong enough on his own to call out the excesses of his mighty brother Rāvaṇa. This Sarga shows Vibheeshaṇa in a low light, as one who laments the loss of the hope of gaining a kingdom, which contradicts the lofty portrayal of his character everywhere.
Third, Sarga 46 shows Vibheeshaṇa noticing that the Vānaras have lost their heart seeing Rāma and Lakshmaṇa struck down, and trying to help the Vānaras regain their composure, which is more likely, than what is described in this Sarga, of the Vānaras being afraid of Vibheeshaṇa mistaking him for Indrajit, which is unlikely as they are already acquainted with Vibheeshaṇa for a long while.
