Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 10
In this Sarga, Kaikēyee, completely brainwashed by Manthara, enters into the anger chamber, throws away all her ornaments, wears a dirty cloth and lies on the bare floor. Manthara is extremely pleased.
Daṡaratha enters his inner quarters and gets perturbed by not finding his dear wife waiting for him as usual. The attendant informs that she is in the anger chamber. He enters the anger chamber and tries to console her, promising that he would do and give whatever pleases her.
2.10.1
विदर्शिता यदा देवी
कुब्जया पापया भृशम् ।
तदा शेते स्म सा भूमौ
दिग्धविद्धेव किन्नरी ॥
Seriously misguided thus by that evil humpback that queen laid on the ground like a Kinnara woman struck by a poisoned dart.
2.10.2
निश्चित्य मनसा कृत्यम्
सा सम्यगिति भामिनी ।
मन्थरायै शनैस्सर्वम्
आचचक्षे विचक्षणा ॥
That pretty and skillful woman, her mind clear about what was to be done, revealed it to Manthara step by step.
2.10.3
सा दीना निश्चयं कृत्वा
मन्थरावाक्यमोहिता ।
नागकन्येव निश्वस्य
दीर्घमुष्णं च भामिनी ।
मुहूर्तं चिन्तयामास
मार्गमात्मसुखावहम् ॥
Captivated by the words of Manthara, that dejected pretty woman, having made up her mind, heaved a long and burning sigh like a Nāga damsel, thought for a moment about the next steps that would engender her happiness.
2.10.4
सा सुहृच्चार्थकामा च
तं निशम्य सुनिश्चयम् ।
बभूव परमप्रीता
सिध्दिं प्राप्येव मन्थरा ॥
Manthara, Kaikēyee's friend and well-wisher, was extremely pleased to hear her resolve, as if her objective was already accomplished.
2.10.5
अथ सा रुषिता देवी
सम्यक्कृत्वा विनिश्चयम् ।
संविवेशाबला भूमौ
निवेश्य भृकुटीं मुखे ॥
Firmly resolved on her course, that angry queen, of weaker gender, lay on the ground with a frown on her face.
2.10.6
ततश्चित्राणि माल्यानि
दिव्यान्याभरणानि च ।
अपविद्धानि कैकेय्या
तानि भूमिं प्रपेदिरे ॥
The various flower garlands and splendid ornaments that Kaikēyee had thrown lay scattered on the floor.
2.10.7
तया तान्यपविद्धानि
माल्यान्याभरणानि च ।
अशोभयन्त वसुधाम्
नक्षत्राणि यथा नभः ॥
All those garlands and ornaments strewn by her adorned the earth like stars illumining the sky.
2.10.8
क्रोधागारे निपतिता
सा बभौ मलिनाम्बरा ।
एकवेणीं दृढं बद्वा
गतसत्त्वेव किन्नरी ॥
Fallen on the floor of the anger chamber, clothes all soiled, hair bound firmly in a single knot, she looked like a Kinnara woman whose vitals were completely depleted.
2.10.9
आज्ञाप्य च महाराजो
राघवस्याभिषेचनम् ।
उपस्थानमनुज्ञाप्य
प्रविवेश निवेशनम् ।
अद्य रामाभिषेको वै
प्रसिद्ध इति जज्ञिवान् ।
प्रियार्हां प्रियमाख्यातुम्
विवेशान्तःपुरं वशी ॥
The King who had everything under control, having ordered the consecration of Rāma and having taken due leave from those assembled, entered the inner quarters to convey to his dear wife the good news of Rāma's consecration, now that it was certain and well accepted by everyone.
As has been mentioned a few times earlier, Vālmeeki is very visual with his narration. In the last few Sargas, the scene has been changing from one place to the other, from Daṡaratha's court to Kousalyā's palace to Rāma's palace to the streets of Ayōdhyā to Kaikēyee's home, all happening in parallel.
While the scene at Rāma's palace and the streets of Ayōdhyā has advanced to the day of consecration, the scene at Kaikēyee's palace and Daṡaratha's court is still on the day before consecration.
At the end of Sarga 5, Vasishṭha, having gone to Rāma's palace and initiating him to fast, returns to Daṡaratha's court. At that point, Daṡaratha dismisses the court and enters his inner quarters. This Ṡlōka picks up from that point.
2.10.11
स कैकेय्या गृहं श्रेष्ठम्
प्रविवेश महायशाः ।
पाण्डुराभ्रमिवाकाशम्
राहुयुक्तं निशाकरः ॥
That man of great renown entered Kaikēyee's splendid apartments like the moon entering the sky that is covered with white clouds, and like Rāhu lurking to eclipse.
Ancient astronomers of India tracked eclipses using the notion of Rāhu and Kētu and treating them as shadow planets.
2.10.12
शुकबर्हिणसङ्घुष्टम्
क्रौञ्चहंसरुतायुतम् ।
वादित्ररवसङ्घुष्टम्
कुब्जा वामनिकायुतम् ।
लतागृहैश्चित्रगृहैः
चम्पकाशोकशोभितैः ।
दान्तराजतसौवर्ण
वेदिकाभि स्समायुतम् ।
नित्यपुष्पफलैर्वृक्षैः
वापीभिश्चोपशोभितम् ।
दान्तरजतसौवर्णैः
संवृतं परमासनैः ।
विविधैरन्नपानैश्च
भक्ष्यैश्च विविधैरपि ।
उपपन्नं महार्हैश्च
भूषितैस्त्रिदिवोपमम् ।
तत्प्रविश्य महाराजः
स्वमन्तः पुरमृद्धिमत् ।
न ददर्श प्रियां राजा
कैकेयीं शयनोत्तमे ॥
The King entered his inner quarters that were filled with parrots and peacocks, pleasing with the sounds of swans and curlews, permeated with music from many instruments, filled with humpbacks and dwarfs, enchanting with varieties of pavilions with vines and Campaka and Aṡōka trees, boasting of Vēdikas covered with gold and silver, splendid with perennial flowering and fruit bearing trees and wells in‌‌‌-laid with steps, surrounded with chairs covered in gold and silver, suffused with varieties of drinks, foods and dishes, resembling the Heaven, full of women adorned with precious ornaments. But he could not find his dear Kaikēyee on her splendid couch.
2.10.17
स कामबलसंयुक्तो
रत्यर्थं मनुजाधिपः ।
अपश्यन्दयितां भार्याम्
पप्रच्छ विषसाद च ॥
That ruler of the peoples, filled with passion and seeking to make love, but not finding his dear wife there, felt very disappointed and asked for her.
2.10.18
न हि तस्य पुरा देवी
तां वेलामत्यवर्तत ।
न च राजा गृहं शून्यम्
प्रविवेश कदाचन ॥
Never before did the queen miss his arrival. Never before did the king enter a deserted house.
2.10.19
ततो गृहगतो राजा
कैकेयीं पर्यपृच्छत ।
यथा पूर्वमविज्ञाय
स्वार्थलिप्सुमपण्डिताम् ॥
The King who thus entered the house, asked for her as usual, unaware of the foolish Kaikēyee's intent of selfish ends.
2.10.20
प्रतीहारी त्वथोवाच
सन्त्रस्ता सुकृताञ्जलिः ।
देव देवी भृशं कृद्धा
क्रोधागारमभिदृता ॥
A trembling attendant, bowing with joined palms said: "My Lord, her ladyship is extremely angry and betook herself to the anger chamber!"
2.10.21
प्रतीहार्या वचश्शृत्वा
राजा परमदुर्मनाः ।
विषसाद पुनर्भूयो
लुलितव्याकुलेन्द्रियः ॥
Hearing those words of the attendant, the King who was already feeling bad felt even worse, with all his senses disquieted.
2.10.22
तत्र तां पतितां भूमौ
शयानामतथोचिताम् ।
प्रतप्त इव दुःखेन
सोऽपश्यज्जगतीपतिः ॥
There, that lord of the land was burnt down by sadness seeing her lying on the bare ground which was no bed for her.
2.10.23
स वृद्धस्तरुणीं भार्याम्
प्राणेभ्योपि गरीयसीम् ।
अपापः पापसङ्कल्पाम्
ददर्श धरणीतले ।
लतामिव विनिष्कृत्ताम्
पतितां देवतामिव ।
किन्नरीमिव निर्धूताम्
च्युतामप्सरसं यथा ।
मायामिव परिभ्रष्टाम्
हरिणीमिव संयताम् ॥
The old and straightforward man saw the young and ill-intentioned woman, his wife, who was dearer to him than his own life, who was looking like a severed creeper, like a fallen goddess, like a Kinnara woman hurled down, like an Apsara slipped from Heaven, like an illusion fallen down and like a female deer caught.
Vālmeeki contrasts the two people involved in the scene here as old vs. young and guileless vs. ill-intentioned.
Vālmeeki uses this contrasting technique in a few other occasions also, such as between Rāma and Ṡūrpaṇakhā, later in Araṇya Kāṇḍa.
2.10.26
करेणुमिव दिग्धेन
विद्धां मृगयुना वने ।
महागज इवारण्ये
स्नेहात्परिममर्श ताम् ॥
He caressed her with affection, like a majestic elephant would caress a female elephant struck down in a jungle by a hunter with a poisoned dart.
2.10.27
परिमृश्य च पाणिभ्याम्
अभिसंत्रस्तचेतनः ।
कामी कमलपत्राक्षीम्
उवाच वनितामिदम् ॥
That infatuated man touched that wide eyed woman with his hands, heSeetātingly, and said to her:
2.10.28
न तेऽहमभिजानामि
क्रोधमात्मनि संश्रितम् ।
देवि केनाभिशप्तासि
केन वासि विमानिता ।
यदिदं ममम् दुःखाय
शेशे कल्याणि पांसुषु ॥
O Queen, O Kalyāṇi! Little did I know that you are angry with me! Did anyone say bad words to you? Did anyone insult you? It pains me to see you lying down in the dust like this!
2.10.29
भूमौ शेषे किमर्थं त्वम्
मयि कल्याणचेतसि ।
भूतोपहतचित्तेव
मम चित्तप्रमाथिनी ॥
While I am here to expend all good to you, why do you hurt me by lying down on the floor, as if possessed by evil spirits!
2.10.30
सन्ति मे कुशला वैद्याः
त्वभितुष्टाश्च सर्वशः ।
सुखितां त्वां करिष्यन्ति
व्याधिमाचक्ष्व भामिनि ॥
There are many skilled and well-acclaimed royal physicians! They can make you feel better, tell me O lovely one, if you are sick!
Daṡaratha, in these Ṡlōkas, not knowing what he is going to be asked, shows his eagerness to oblige.
We have seen a similar situation in Sarga 18, when Daṡaratha was eager to oblige Viṡwāmitra, not knowing what he was going to hear.
2.10.31
कस्य वा ते प्रियं कार्यम्
केन वा विप्रियं कृतम् ।
कः प्रियं लभतामद्य
को वा सुमहदप्रियम् ॥
Tell me if you want to do favors to any one, or if anyone had done anything to displease you! Tell me whom I should extend favors upon and who should receive my greatest displeasure!
2.10.32
मा रोदीर्मा च कार्षिस्त्वम्
देवि संपरिशोषणम् ।
अवध्यो वध्यतां को वा
को वा वध्यो विमुच्यताम् ।
दरिद्रः को भवेदाढ्यो
द्रव्यवान्वाप्यकिञ्चनः ॥
My Queen! Please do not cry! Do not enervate yourself! If it would please you, may someone who deserves not, be killed, and someone who deserves to be killed, be forgiven, or may a destitute be made a rich man, and may a wealthy man be turned into nobody!
2.10.34
अहं चैव मदीयाश्च
सर्वे तव वशानुगाः ।
न ते किंचिदभिप्रायम्
व्याहन्तुमहमुत्सहे ॥
I and all of my people are at your service! I dare not do even a little bit against your intention!
2.10.35
आत्मनो जीवितेनापि
ब्रुहि यन्मनसेच्छसि ।
बलमात्मनि जानन्ती
न मां शङ्कितुमर्हसि ।
करिष्यामि तव प्रीतिम्
सुकृतेनापि ते शपे ॥
Tell me whatever you would like from me, even if it means my life! You shall not doubt me, as you very well know the power you have over me! I swear on all the good deeds I have done, that I will do whatever pleases you!
2.10.36
यावदावर्त ते चक्रम्
तावती मे वसुन्धरा ।
प्राचीनाः सिन्धुसौवीराः
सौराष्ट्रा दक्षिणापथाः ।
वङ्गाङ्गमगधा मत्स्याः
समृद्धाः काशिकोसलाः ॥
My lands extend everywhere where a wheel can roll, to the countries of Sindhu, Souveera and Sourāshṭra, to the countries of Vanga, Anga, Magadha and Matsya, to the rich countries of Kāṡi and Kōsala and to the countries towards East and South!
Does this not remind us of the British Empire of the past century, in which the 'sun never sets'?
Similarly, Daṡaratha's empire extends to all lands wherever 'a chariot can be driven'!
2.10.38
तत्र जातं बहुद्रव्यम्
धनधान्य मजाविकम् ।
ततो वृणीष्व कैकेयि
यद्यत्त्वं मनसेच्छसि ॥
Many goods, including grain, sheep, goats and wealth are produced there. O Kaikēyee! Ask for anything from there, that you laid your fancy on!
2.10.39
किमायासेन ते भीरु
उत्तिष्टोत्तिष्ट शोभने ।
तत्वं मे ब्रूहि कैकेयि
यतस्ते भयमागतम् ।
तत्ते व्यपनयिष्यामि
नीहरमिव रश्मिवान् ॥
You don't need to fret over anything, you timorous one! Get up, get up, you beautiful! Tell me, Kaikēyee, what is it that you fear! I shall destroy it, as the sun would dispel mist with his rays!
2.10.40
तथोक्ता सा समाश्वस्ता
वक्तुकामा तदप्रियम् ।
परिपीडयितुं भूयो
भर्तारमुपचक्रमे ॥
Having been reassured by those words, and having been intent on saying the disquieting words, she started to harrow her husband, again.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे दशमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the tenth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
