Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 56
In this Sarga, seeing the Vānara forces suffer under the assault of Akampana, Hanumān rushes there, and kills Akampana. The Rākshasa forces flee to Laṅkā and the Vānaras gather around Hanumān, praising him.
6.56.1
तद्दृष्ट्वा सुमहत् कर्म
कृतं वानरसत्तमैः ।
क्रोधमाहारयामास
युधि तीव्रमकम्पनः ॥
Seeing that tremendous onslaught by the great Vānara chiefs, Akampana worked himself up into a great fury.
6.56.2
क्रोधमूर्च्छितरूपस्तु
धून्वन् परमकार्मुकम् ।
दृष्ट्वा तु कर्म शत्रूणाम्
सारथिं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing the havoc caused by the enemy, mad with rage from head to toe and shaking his great bow, he said these words to the charioteer:
6.56.3
तत्रैव तावत्त्वरितम्
रथं प्रापय सारथे ।
यत्रैते बलिनो घ्नन्ति
सुबहून् राक्षसान् रणे ॥
Drive quickly, O charioteer, straight into the thick of the fight, where the strong Vānaras are slaughtering the Rākshasas in huge numbers.
6.56.4
एतेऽत्र बलवन्तो हि
भीमकायाश्च वानराः ।
द्रुमशैलप्रहरणाः
तिष्ठन्ति प्रमुखे मम ।
एतान्निहन्तुमिच्छामि
समरश्लाघिनो ह्यहम् ।
एतैः प्रमथितं सर्वम्
दृश्यते राक्षसं बलम् ॥
I want to kill these strong Vānaras who are standing right across me with formidable bodies and armed with trees and rocks, ready for a fight. It seems these are the ones who are mauling the entire Rākshasa forces.
6.56.6
ततः प्रजवनाश्वेन
रथेन रथिनां वरः ।
हरीनभ्यहनत् क्रोधात्
शरजालैरकम्पनः ॥
Then, Akampana, a great charioter, dashed furiously towards the Vānaras killing them with his showers of arrows.
6.56.7
न स्थातुं वानराः शेकुः
किं पुनर्योद्धुमाहवे ।
अकम्पनशरैर्भग्नाः
सर्व एव विदुद्रुवुः ॥
The Vānaras could not stand against him, much less fight back in that battle. Mangled by Akampana’s arrows, they fled.
6.56.8
तान् मृत्युवशमापन्नान
अकम्पनवशं गतान् ।
समीक्ष्य हनुमान् ज्ञातीन्
उपतस्थे महाबलः ॥
Seeing his kin under the seize of Akampana and thus doomed to death, Hanumān of immense strength rushed there.
6.56.9
तं महाप्लवगं दृष्ट्वा
सर्वे प्लवगयूथपाः ।
समेत्य समरे वीराः
संहृष्टाः पर्यवारयन् ॥
On seeing that mighty Vānara, all the Vānara chiefs, valiant in fight, were delighted and crowded around him.
6.56.10
अवस्थितं हनूमन्तम्
ते दृष्ट्वा हरियूथपाः ।
बभूवुर्बलवन्तो हि
बलवन्तं समाश्रिताः ॥
On seeing the strong Hanumān standing by them, and rallying under his leadership, the Vānara chiefs felt their strength multiplied.
6.56.11
अकम्पनस्तु शैलाभम्
हनूमन्तमवस्थितम् ।
महेन्द्र इव धाराभिः
शरैरभिववर्ष ह ॥
Akampana showered a downpour of arrows on Hanumān who stood immovable like a rock, like Mahēndra sends a downpour of rain.
6.56.12
अचिन्तयित्वा बाणौघान्
शरीरे पतितान् शितान् ।
अकम्पनवधार्थाय
मनो दध्रे महाबलः ॥
Caring two hoots about the sharp arrows that fell on his body, the Vānara of immense strength focused his mind solely on slaying Akampana.
6.56.13
स प्रसह्य महातेजा
हनूमान् मारुतात्मजः ।
अभिदुद्राव तद्रक्षः
कम्पयन्निव मेदिनीम् ॥
Laughing, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, of extraordinary power, dashed towards the Rākshasa, shaking the earth (underneath his feet).
6.56.14
तस्याभिनर्दमानस्य
दीप्यमानस्य तेजसा ।
बभूव रूपं दुर्धर्षम्
दीप्तस्येव विभावसोः ॥
It was difficult to behold him like it would to behold a raging fire, as he roared and blazed with his splendor.
6.56.15
आत्मानमप्रहरणम्
ज्ञात्वा क्रोधसमन्वितः ।
शैलमुत्पाटयामास
वेगेन हरिपुङ्गवः ॥
Realizing that he had not armed himself, that bull among Vānaras, in his wrath, lifted up a boulder immediately.
6.56.16
तं गृहीत्वा महाशैलम्
पाणिनैकेन मारुतिः ।
स विनद्य महानादम्
भ्रामयामास वीर्यवान् ॥
The valiant son of Vāyu, picking up that huge boulder with one hand, whirled it round and round as he uttered a strident shout.
6.56.17
ततस्तमभिदुद्राव
राक्षसेन्द्रमकम्पनम् ।
पुरा हि नमुचिं सङ्ख्ये
वज्रेणेव पुरन्दरः ॥
Then, he rushed on to Akampana, a lord of the Rākshasas, just as Purandara did on to Namuci once upon a time in a battle with Vajra in his hand.
6.56.18
अकम्पनस्तु तद्दृष्ट्वा
गिरिशृङ्गं समुद्यतम् ।
दूरादेव महाबाणैः
अर्धचन्द्रैर्व्यदारयत् ॥
Seeing from afar that mountain crest that was held up ready to be hurled, he (Akampana) broke it to bits with powerful arrows with crescent-shaped heads.
6.56.19
तत्पर्वताग्रमाकाशे
रक्षोबाणविदारितम् ।
विशीर्णं पतितं दृष्ट्वा
हनुमान् क्रोधमूर्च्छितः ॥
Hanumān was beside himself with fury, seeing the mountain crest broken up in mid air by the Rākshasa’s arrows and fallen on the ground, shattered.
6.56.20
सोऽश्वकर्णं समासाद्य
रोषदर्पान्वितो हरिः ।
तूर्णमुत्पाटयामास
महागिरिमिवोच्छ्रितम् ॥
The Vānara, his pride ruffled, quickly spotted an Aṡwakarṇa tree that was huge like a mountain, and plucked it up by the roots.
6.56.21
तं गृहीत्वा महास्कन्धम्
सोऽश्वकर्णं महाद्युतिः ।
प्रहस्य परया प्रीत्या
भ्रामयामास संयुगे ॥
Grabbing that Aṡwakarṇa tree of huge trunk, he of great splendor whirled it, laughing and finding great amusement in it.
6.56.22
प्रधावन्नूरुवेगेन
भभञ्ज तरसा द्रुमान् ।
हनुमान् परमक्रुद्धः
चरणैर्दारयत् क्षितिम् ॥
Hanumān dashed forth in rage, smashing the trees on his way with his force and with the speed of his thighs, and rending the earth with his feet.
6.56.23
गजांश्च सगजारोहान्
सरथान् रथिनस्तथा ।
जघान हनुमान् धीमान्
राक्षसांश्च पदातिगान् ॥
The sagacious Hanumān slew elephants and their riders, charioters along with their chariots and the Rākshasas who fought on foot.
6.56.24
तमन्तकमिव क्रुद्धम्
समरे प्राणहारिणम् ।
हनुमन्तमभिप्रेक्ष्य
राक्षसा विप्रदुद्रुवुः ॥
Seeing Hanumān, who in his rage seemed like Yama, putting an end to many lives in that battle, the Rākshasas took to their heels.
6.56.25
तमापतन्तं सङ्क्रुद्धम्
राक्षसानां भयावहम् ।
ददर्शाकम्पनो वीरः
चुक्षोभ च ननाद च ॥
Seeing him come dashing, enraged and striking terror into the hearts of the Rākshasas, Akampana, valiant as he is, became agitated and bellowed.
6.56.26
स चतुर्दशभिर्बाणैः
शितैर्देहविदारणैः ।
निर्बिभेद हनूमन्तम्
महावीर्यमकम्पनः ॥
Akampana shot Hanumān of the mighty valor with fourteen sharp arrows that would easily rend one’s body.
6.56.27
स तदा प्रतिविद्धस्तु
बह्वीभिः शरवृष्टिभिः ।
हनुमान् ददृशे वीरः
प्ररूढ इव सानुमान् ॥
With numerous showers of arrows lodged on him, the valiant Hanumān looked like a mountain that was full of trees.
6.56.28
विरराज महाकायो
महावीर्यो महामनाः ।
पुष्पिताशोकसङ्काशो
विधूम इव पावकः ॥
He, of huge body, great intellect and immense valor, shone like an Aṡōka tree in flower and like fire undimmed by smoke.
6.56.29
ततोऽन्यं वृक्षमुत्पाट्य
कत्वा वेगमनुत्तमम् ।
शिरस्यभिजघानाशु
राक्षसेन्द्रमकम्पनम् ॥
He then plucked another tree, and with a huge force, struck on the head of Akampana, the lord of Rākshasas, with it.
6.56.30
स वृक्षेण हतस्तेन
सक्रोधेन महात्मना ।
राक्षसो वानरेन्द्रेण
पपात च ममार च ॥
Hit thus by that enraged mighty Lord of Vānaras with the tree, the Rākshasa fell down and died.
6.56.31
तं दृष्ट्वा निहतं भूमौ
राक्षसेन्द्रमकम्पनम् ।
व्यथिता राक्षसाः सर्वे
क्षितिकम्प इव द्रुमाः ॥
Seeing Akampana, the lord of Rākshasas, fall dead on the ground, all the Rākshasas were rattled like trees when an earthquake occurs.
6.56.32
त्यक्तप्रहरणाः सर्वे
राक्षसास्ते पराजिताः ।
लङ्कामभिययुस्त्रस्ता
वानरैस्तैरभिद्रुताः ॥
Throwing away their weapons, the vanquished Rākshasas ran towards Laṅkā in fright, chased by the Vānaras.
6.56.33
ते मुक्तकेशाः सम्भ्रान्ता
भग्नमानाः पराजिताः ।
स्रवच्छ्रमजलैरङ्गैः
श्वसन्तो विप्रदुद्रुवुः ॥
Distraught, hair disheveled and their minds out of order having suffered defeat, they fled, gasping for breath and every limb drenched in sweat.
6.56.34
अन्योन्यं प्रममन्थुस्ते
विविशुर्नगरं भयात् ।
पृष्ठतस्ते सुसम्मूढाः
प्रेक्षमाणा मुहुर्मुहुः ॥
Jostling against each other and looking timorously behind them again and again, they entered the city, becoming senseless with terror.
6.56.35
तेषु लङ्कां प्रविष्टेषु
राक्षसेषु महाबलाः ।
समेत्य हरयः सर्वे
हनुमन्तमपूजयन् ॥
While the Rākshasas thus returned to Laṅkā, all the Vānaras of immense strength gathered around Hanumān and praised him.
6.56.36
सोऽपि प्रहृष्टस्तान् सर्वान्
हरीन् प्रत्यभ्यपूजयत् ।
हनुमान् सत्त्वसम्पन्नो
यथार्हमनुकूलतः ॥
Hanumān, one endowed with great power, also greatly pleased, appreciated all the Vānaras, as they deserved and expected.
6.56.37
विनेदुश्च यथाप्राणम्
हरयो जितकाशिनः ।
चकृषुश्च पुनस्तत्र
सप्राणानपि राक्षसान् ॥
The victorious Vānaras, basking in the glory of victory, shouted with all the force of their lung-power and dragged around (the bodies of) Rākshasas, even the ones that had some life left in them.
6.56.38
स वीरशोभामभजन्महाकपिः
समेत्य रक्षांसि निहत्य मारुतिः ।
महासुरं भीमममित्रनाशनः
विष्णुर्यथैवोरुबलं चमूमुखे ॥
Māruti, the great Vānara, dazzled with the hero’s glow, having met and destroyed the Rākshasas in a pitched battle, just as Vishnu, the destroyer of foe, did in the forefront of the battle lines, after destroying the great, powerful and dreaded Asura.
6.56.39
अपूजयन् देवगणास्तदा कपिम्
स्वयं च रामोऽतिबलश्च लक्ष्मणः ।
तथैव सुग्रीवमुखाः प्लवङ्गमा
विभीषणश्चैव महाबलस्तथा ॥
The hosts of Dēvas praised the Vānara, and so did Rāma himself and the exceedingly strong Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva and his Vānaras and the strong Vibheeshaṇa.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे षट्पञ्चाशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty sixth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
