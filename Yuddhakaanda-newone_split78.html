Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 78
In this Sarga, hearing that Kumbha and Nikumbha were slain, Rāvaṇa becomes sad and angry and sends Makarāksha, son of Khara, to fight with Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and the Vānaras. Makarāksha, who thinks very highly of his own prowess, excited at the prospect of a fight, proceeds, ignoring the bad omens that arise as he sets out.
6.78.1
निकुम्भं च हतं श्रुत्वा
कुम्भं च विनिपातितम् ।
रावणः परमामर्षी
प्रजज्वालानलो यथा ॥
Hearing that Nikumbha was slain and Kumbha was struck down, Rāvaṇa blazed like fire in extreme rage.
6.78.2
नैर्ऋतः क्रोधशोकाभ्याम्
द्वाभ्यां तु परिमूर्च्छितः ।
खरपुत्रं विशालाक्षम्
मकराक्षमचोदयत् ॥
With anger and grief overwhelming him, the Rākshasa instructed Khara’s son Makarāksha of huge eyes, saying:
6.78.3
गच्छ पुत्र मयाऽऽज्ञप्तो
बलेनाभिसमन्वितः ।
राघवं लक्ष्मणं चैव
जहि तांश्च वनौकसः ॥
Go, my son, taking with you an army under my order, destroy Rāghava, Lakshmaṇa and those Vānaras.
6.78.4
रावणस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
शूरमानी खरात्मजः ।
बाढमित्यब्रवीद्धृष्टो
मकाराक्षो निशाचरः ॥
Hearing those words of Rāvaṇa, the Night-rover Makarāksha, son of Khara, who thought highly of his own prowess, was very excited and said, 'for sure!'
6.78.5
सोऽबिवाद्य दशग्रीवम्
कृत्वा चापि प्रदक्षिणम् ।
निर्जगाम गृहाच्छुभ्रात्
रावणस्याज्ञया बली ॥
Saluting the Ten-necked and also circumambulating him in reverence, the strong one started from that splendid house, as ordered by Rāvaṇa.
6.78.6
समीपस्थं बलाध्यक्षम्
खरपुत्रोऽब्रवीदिदम् ।
रथश्चानीयतां शीघ्रम्
सैन्यं चाहूयतां त्वरात् ॥
Then the son of Khara said these words to the commander of the forces who stood near by, 'Please bring a chariot immediately, and call for the army!'
6.78.7
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
बलाध्यक्षो निशाचरः ।
स्यन्दनं च बलं चैव
समीपं प्रत्यपादयत् ॥
Hearing those words of his, the Night-rover who was the commander of the forces, brought near him a chariot and the army.
6.78.8
प्रदक्षिणं रथं कृत्वा
आरुरोह निशाचरः ।
सूतं सञ्चोदयामास
शीघ्रं मे रथमावह ॥
The Night-rover circumambulted the chariot, got into it, and instructed the charioteer to drive it immediately.
6.78.9
अथ तान् राक्षसान् सर्वान्
मकराक्षोऽब्रवीदिदम् ॥
Then Makarāksha said this to all those Rākshasas:
6.78.9
यूयं सर्वे प्रयुद्ध्यध्वम्
पुरस्तान्मम राक्षसाः ।
अहं राक्षसराजेन
रावणेन महात्मना ।
आज्ञप्तः समरे हन्तुम्
तावुभौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
All of you, O Rākshasas, shall now fight under my command. I have been ordered by Mahātma Rāvaṇa, the king of Rākshasas, to kill those two, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa in the battle.
6.78.11
अद्य रामं वधिष्यामि
लक्ष्मणं च निशाचराः ।
शाखामृगं च सुग्रीवम्
वानरांश्च शरोत्तमैः ॥
I shall kill today, O Night-rovers, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, and also Sugreeva, a beast of the branches, and all the Vānaras, with the best of arrows.
6.78.12
अद्य शूलनिपातैश्च
वानराणां महाचमूम् ।
प्रदहिष्यामि सम्प्राप्ताम्
शुष्केन्धनमिवानलः ॥
I shall burn the great army of Vānaras that has arrived here, like fire burns dry fuel, and strike it down with spears.
6.78.13
मकराक्षस्य तच्छ्रुत्वा
वचनं ते निशाचराः ।
सर्वे नानायुधोपेता
बलवन्तः समागताः ॥
Hearing those words of Makarāksha, all the strong Night-rovers gathered around him, holding every kind of weapon.
6.78.14
ते कामरूपिणः सर्वे
दंष्ट्रिणः पिङ्गलेक्षणाः ।
मातङ्गा इव नर्दन्तो
ध्वस्तकेशा भयानकाः ॥
All of them, who looked terrifying with their sharp fangs, yellow-tinged eyes and disheveled hair, shouted like the trumpeting of elephants.
6.78.15
परिवार्य महाकाया
महाकायं खरात्मजम् ।
अभिजग्मुस्ततो हृष्टाः
चालयन्तो वसुन्धराम् ॥
They of huge bodies gathered around the son of Khara of gigantic body and marched forth in excitement, shaking the earth with their tread.
6.78.16
शङ्खभेरीसहस्राणाम्
आहतानां समन्ततः ।
क्ष्वेलितास्फोटितानां च
ततः शब्दो महानभूत् ॥
There arose a mighty din from the blare of conches and blasts of thousands of kettledrums, the vaunting battle-cries and the slapping of shoulders.
6.78.17
प्रभ्रष्टोऽथ करात्तस्य
प्रतोदः सारथेस्तदा ।
पपात सहसा चैव
ध्वजस्तस्य च रक्षसः ॥
Then all of a sudden, the whip slipped from the hand of the Rākshasa’s charioteer and the flagstaff (of his chariot) tumbled down.
6.78.18
तस्य ते रथयुक्ताश्च
हया विक्रमवर्जिताः ।
चरणैराकुलैर्गत्वा
दीनाः सास्रमुखा ययुः ॥
The tread of the horses yoked to the chariot faltered. Their faces were woebegone, eyes teary, and they were devoid of their sprightly stride.
6.78.19
प्रवाति पवनस्तस्मिन्
सपांसुः खरदारुणः ।
निर्याणे तस्य रौद्रस्य
मकराक्षस्य दुर्मतेः ॥
A terribly hideous wind, full of dust, blew as the wicked and enraged Makarāksha set out.
6.78.20
तानि दृष्ट्वा निमित्तानि
राक्षसा वीर्यवत्तमाः ।
अचिन्त्य निर्गताः सर्वे
यत्र तौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
The Rākshasas of great valor ignored those bad omens that they saw, and proceeded to the place where Rāma and Lakshmaṇa were.
6.78.21
घनगजमहिषाङ्गतुल्यवर्णाः
समरमुखेष्वसकृद्गदासिभिन्नाः ।
अहमहमिति युद्धकौशलास्ते
रजनिचराः परितः समुन्नदन्तः ॥
Those Night-rovers, dark as clouds, elephants and buffaloes, veterans of war with plenty of scars from the strikes of maces and swords in many a high-pitched battle in the past, advanced, shouting, ‘I first! No, I!’
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे अष्टसप्ततितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventy eighth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
