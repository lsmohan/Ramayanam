Baala Kaanda - Sarga 15
In this Sarga, the Dēvas along with Gandharvas, Siddhas, Yakshas and Ṛshis pray to Lord Vishṇu and ask him to be born in a human form to kill Rāvaṇa, who is tormenting all the worlds.
This Sarga is special, because it happens completely in Dēva Lōka and also because it is in this Sarga that Lord Vishṇu appears for the first time in the narrative.
1.15.1
मेधावी तु ततो ध्यात्वा
स किञ्चिदिदमुत्तरम् ।
लब्धसञ्ज्ञस्ततस्तं तु
वेदज्ञो नृपमबव्रीत् ॥
Having remained plunged in thought for a while, that eminent scholar of Vēdas (Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga), came to a decision as to what should be done next, and said to the king:
1.15.2
इष्टिं तेऽहं करिष्यामि
पुत्रीयां पुत्रकारणात् ।
अथर्वशिरसि प्रोक्तैः
मन्त्रैः सिद्धां विधानतः ॥
There is a Yajña for getting offspring mentioned along with the Mantras in Atharva Ṡirasi. I will perform that Yajña for you as per the Ṡāstras so that you would beget offspring.
Atharva Ṡirasi is a part of Veda.
1.15.3
ततः प्रारब्धवानिष्टिम्
पुत्रीयां पुत्रकारणात् ।
जुहाव चाग्नौ तेजस्वी
मन्त्रदृष्टेन कर्मणा ॥
And that man of splendor commenced the Yajña for obtaining offspring, and made oblations into the fire according to rites prescribed in the Mantras.
1.15.4
ततो देवाः सगन्धर्वाः
सिद्धाश्च परमर्षयः ।
भागप्रतिग्रहार्थं वै
समवेता यथाविधि ॥
Then, all the Dēvas, Gandharvas, Siddhas and great Ṛshis gathered to receive the offerings from the Yajña that was meant for each of them.
1.15.5
ताः समेत्य यथान्यायम्
तस्मिन् सदसि देवताः ।
अब्रुवन् लोककर्त्तारम्
ब्रह्माणं वचनं महत् ॥
After thus making due appearance in that assembly, the Dēvas said the following words of great significance to Lord Brahma, creator of the worlds:
1.15.6
भगवन् त्वत्प्रसादेन
रावणो नाम राक्षसः ।
सर्वान्नो बाधते वीर्यात्
शासितुं तं न शक्नुमः ॥
O Bhagawan, the Rākshasa named Rāvaṇa harries us with the enormous strength he gained by your grace and we are not able to contain him.
1.15.7
त्वया तस्मै वरो दत्तः
प्रीतेन भगवन् पुरा ।
मानयन्तश्च तं नित्यम्
सर्वं तस्य क्षमामहे ॥
We are condoning all his excesses all the time being respectful of the boons given by you to him earlier.
1.15.8
उद्वेजयति लोकांस्त्रीन्
उच्छ्रितान् द्वेष्टि दुर्मतिः ।
शक्रं त्रिदशराजानम्
प्रधर्षयितुमिच्छति ॥
That wicked Rāvaṇa is a menace to all the three worlds. He resents every other person who is powerful. His eye is now on assailing Ṡakra, the King of Dēvas.
Ṡakra is another name for Indra.
1.15.9
ऋषीन् यक्षान् सगन्धर्वान्
असुरान् ब्राह्मणांस्तथा ।
अतिक्रामति दुर्धषो
वरदानेन मोहितः ॥
He became invincible because of the boons. He is abusing Ṛshis, Yakshas, Gandharvas, Brāhmaṇas and Asuras.
1.15.10
नैनं सूर्यः प्रतपति
पार्श्वे वाति न मारुतः ।
चलोर्मिमाली तं दृष्ट्वा
समुद्रोऽपि न कम्पते ॥
Neither is the sun able to scorch him nor is wind able to breeze by him. Even the restless, billow-wreathed sea freezes into stillness when it sees him.
1.15.11
तन्महन्नो भयं तस्मात्
राक्षसाद्घोरदर्शनात् ।
वधार्थं तस्य भगवन्
उपायं कर्त्तुमर्हसि ॥
We are scared by the very sight of him. O Bhagawan, please find a way to put an end to him.
1.15.12
एवमुक्तः सुरैः सर्वैः
चिन्तयित्वा ततोऽब्रवीत् ॥
When all the Dēvas pleaded thus, Lord Brahma pondered for a moment and said:
1.15.12
हन्ताऽयं विदितस्तस्य
वधोपायो दुरात्मनः ।
तेन गन्धर्वयक्षाणाम्
देवदानवरक्षसाम् ।
अवध्योऽस्मीति वागुक्ता
तथेत्युक्तं च तन्मया ॥
Yes, I can think of an Upāya to annihilate this evil-minded one! He asked for immunity from being killed by Dēvas, Dānavas, Rākshasas, Gandharvas and Yakshas. And I said Yes.
1.15.14
नाकीर्तयदवज्ञानात्
तद्रक्षो मानुषांस्तदा ।
तस्मात्स मानुषाद्वध्यो
मृत्युर्नान्योऽस्य विद्यते ॥
He did not include humans in that list as he was very dismissive of them. Hence he can be killed by a human. And there is no death for him from anyone else.
1.15.15
एतच्छ्रुत्वा प्रियं वाक्यम्
ब्रह्मणा समुदाहृतम् ।
सर्वे महर्षयो देवाः
प्रहृष्टास्तेऽभवंस्तदा ॥
Hearing those words of relief from Lord Brahma, all the Dēvas and Ṛshis felt very happy.
1.15.16
एतस्मिन्नन्तरे विष्णुः
उपयातो महाद्युतिः ।
शङ्खचक्रगदापाणिः
पीतवासा जगत्पतिः ॥
Meanwhile, the Lord of the Universe, the infinitely radiant Vishṇu appeared there in his yellow robes with Ṡankha, Cakra and Gada in his hands.
Ṡankha is conch. Cakra is discus. Gada is mace.
Consider today, i.e., the day you are reading this Ṡlōka, as the day that Lord Vishṇu came into your life to help you. He will take care of the Rākshasas, i.e., the adversities, in your life. He will do everything for you even if it means having to take birth as a human or having his kingdom taken away or having his wife abducted, or whatever.
Memorize this Ṡlōka and if you have young children, help them memorize it.
1.15.17
ब्रह्मणा च समागम्य
तत्र तस्थौ समाहितः ।
तमब्रुवन् सुराः सर्वे
समभिष्टूय सन्नताः ॥
He went up to Brahma and took a seat there in a solemn manner. All the Dēvas bowed to him and praised him. They said to him:
1.15.18
त्वां नियोक्ष्यामहे विष्णो
लोकानां हितकाम्यया ।
राज्ञो दशरथस्य त्वं
अयोध्याधिपतेर्विभोः ।
धर्मज्ञस्य वदान्यस्य
महर्षिसमतेजसः ।
तस्य भार्यासु तिसृषु
ह्रीश्रीकीर्त्युपमासु च ।
विष्णो पुत्रत्वमागच्छ
कृत्वात्मानं चतुर्विधम् ॥
O Vishṇu! For the sake of helping the worlds, we would like you to take the form of four sons to the three wives of King Daṡaratha. They are comparable to Hree, Sree and Keerti. Daṡaratha, the king of Ayōdhyā is generous, and savant of Dharma and shines with the brilliance of great Ṛshis.
Hree, Sree, Keerti are the names of the three daughters of Daksha. Hree means modesty and humility. Sree means affluence and endowment. Keerti means fame and reputation.
1.15.20
तत्र त्वं मानुषो भूत्वा
प्रवृद्धं लोककण्टकम् ।
अवध्यं दैवतैर्विष्णो
समरे जहि रावणम् ॥
Then, in that human form, take up a battle with Rāvaṇa and kill him, for he has immunity from being killed by us, the Dēvas. He is troubling all the worlds and his power is growing day by day.
1.15.21
स हि देवान् सगन्धर्वान्
सिद्धांश्च ऋषिसत्तमान् ।
राक्षसो रावणो मूर्खो
वीर्योत्सेकेन बाधते ॥
That insolent Rākshasa, Rāvaṇa, of unbridled strength is terrorizing Dēvas, Rākshasas, Gandharvas, Siddhas and exalted Munis.
1.15.22
ऋषयस्तु ततस्तेन
गन्धर्वाप्सरसस्तथा ।
क्रीडन्तो नन्दनवने
क्रूरेण किल हिंसिताः ॥
That wretched soul is tormenting Ṛshis, Gandharvas and even Apsaras that play in the gardens of heaven.
Apsaras are magical celestial damsels.
1.15.23
वधार्थं वयमायाताः
तस्य वै मुनिभिः सह ।
सिद्धगन्धर्वयक्षाश्च
ततस्त्वां शरणं गताः ॥
Hence all of us, including Munis, Siddhas, Gandharvas and Yakshas urge you to kill him. We repose our confidence in you.
1.15.24
त्वं गतिः परमा देव
सर्वेषां नः परन्तप ।
वधाय देवशत्रूणाम्
नृणां लोके मनः कुरु ॥
O Dēva, Tormentor of the insidious! You are the ultimate resort for all of us. Please take birth as a human and eliminate the enemies of the Dēvas.
1.15.25
एवमुक्तस्तु देवेशो
विष्णुस्त्रिदशपुङ्गवः ।
पितामहपुरोगांस्तान्
सर्वलोकनमस्कृतः ।
अब्रवीत् त्रिदशान् सर्वान्
समेतान् धर्मसंहितान् ॥
Having heard them, Vishṇu, the lord and foremost of the Dēvas, who was venerated by the beings of all the worlds, addressed Brahma and everyone else who had assembled there with a legitimate reason:
1.15.27
भयं त्यजत भद्रं वो
हितार्थं युधि रावणम् ।
सपुत्रपौत्रं सामात्यम्
समित्रज्ञातिबान्धवम् ।
हत्वा क्रूरं दुरात्मानम्
देवर्षीणां भयावहम् ।
दश वर्षसहस्राणि
दशवर्षशतानि च ।
वत्स्यामि मानुषे लोके
पालयन् पृथिवीमिमाम् ॥
Let go off your fears! Everything will bode well for you! For your sake, I will kill Rāvaṇa in a battle and his sons, grandsons, ministers, kith and kin who are causing fear among the Dēvas and Ṛshis with their cruel behavior and evil-minds. I will reign in the world of humans for eleven thousand years ruling the entire earth.
1.15.29
एवं दत्त्वा वरं देवो
देवानां विष्णुरात्मवान् ।
मानुषे चिन्तयामास
जन्मभूमिमथात्मनः ॥
Having promised them thus, the composed Vishṇu, the Dēva of Dēvas, thought about his place of birth in the world of humans.
1.15.30
ततः पद्मपलाशाक्षः
कृत्वात्मानं चतुर्विधम् ।
पितरं रोचयामास
तदा दशरथं नृपम् ॥
Then Vishṇu, the one with eyes wide and pleasant like lotus petals made himself into four parts and made king Daṡaratha to be his mortal parent.
1.15.31
ततो देवर्षिगन्धर्वाः
सरुद्राः साप्सरोगणाः ।
स्तुतिभिर्दिव्यरूपाभिः
तुष्टुवुर्मधुसूदनम् ॥
Then the Dēvas of various divine forms, along with Gandharvas, Rudras, and Apsaras sung the glory of Madhusūdana:
Vishṇu is known as Madhusūdana, the slayer of Madhu, a Rākshasa.
1.15.32
तमुद्धतं रावणमुग्रतेजसं
प्रवृद्धदर्पं त्रिदशेश्वरद्विषम् ।
विरावणं साधुतपस्विकण्टकं
तपस्विनामुद्धर तं भयावहम् ॥
Uproot that mighty Rāvaṇa, of frightful prowess, inflated pride and full of hatred towards Indra. He has become a hindrance and a source of fear for Tapasvis and the cause of grief for all people.
1.15.33
तमेव हत्वा सबलं सबान्धवं
विरावणं रावणमुग्रपौरुषम् ।
स्वर्लोकमागच्छ गतज्वरश्चिरं
सुरेन्द्रगुप्तं गतदोषकल्मषम् ॥
Eliminate that Rāvaṇa of frightful prowess, and his army, his kith and kin who are giving grief to all the people and then come back to Heaven that is protected by Indra, freed from all worries, anguish and concerns.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे पञ्चदशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifteenth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
