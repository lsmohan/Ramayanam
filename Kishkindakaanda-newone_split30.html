Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 30
In this Sarga, Rāma is worried about the condition of Seetā and feels dejected about his own fate and his dependency on the pleasure of Sugreeva.
Meanwhile Lakshmaṇa who had gone in search of fruit returns and feels terrible seeing his brother in such a state, sitting alone lamenting his fate. He then tries to cheer his brother up reminding him of his great prowess and the task ahead.
Rāma, upon hearing the motivating words of Lakshmaṇa, recovers from his doleful mood. He sees that the monsoon season has passed and the season of Ṡarat has arrived. He is outraged as he sees no sign of Sugreeva, even though it is already the season for the first waves of war expeditions.
Rāma then tells Lakshmaṇa to go to Kishkindhā and deliver his warning that Sugreeva better keep his promise or be ready to be sent to the abode of Yama and meet his brother Vāli, struck by his arrows.
4.30.1
गुहं प्रविष्टे सुग्रीवे
विमुक्ते गगने घनैः ।
वर्षरात्रेस्थितो रामः
कामशोकाभिपीडितः ।
पाण्डुरं गगनं दृष्ट्वा
विमलं चन्द्रमण्डलम् ।
शारदीं रजनीं चैव
दृष्ट्वा ज्योत्स्नानुलेपनाम् ।
कामवृत्तं च सुग्रीवम्
नष्टां च जनकात्मजाम् ।
दृष्ट्वा कालमतीतं च
मुमोह परमातुरः ॥
In a while, after Sugreeva entered the cave-city, the sky became clear of rain clouds. Rāma, who had lived through many nights of rain scorched by grief as well as the pangs of love (finally) saw the clear sky of the season of Ṡarat whose nights were coated by the moonlight. He fell into a swoon seeing Sugreeva indulged in dalliance while Seetā was lost and time was slipping by.
4.30.4
स तु संज्ञामुपागम्य
मुहूर्तान्मतिमान्पुनः ।
मनस्स्थामपि वैदेहीम्
चिन्तयामास राघवः ॥
Then the sagacious Rāghava, the king, recovered himself in a Muhūrta and started worrying about Vaidēhee, whose thoughts occupied his mind constantly.
4.30.5
दृष्ट्वा च विमलं व्योम
गतविद्युद्वलाहकम् ।
सारसारावसङ्घुष्ठम्
विललापाऽऽर्तया गिरा ॥
Seeing the sky become clear from clouds and lightning and filled with the cries of Sārasa birds, he started lamenting in a pitiful voice.
4.30.6
आसीनः पर्वतस्याग्रे
हेमधातुविभूषिते ।
शारदं गगनं दृष्ट्वा
जगाम मनसा प्रियाम् ॥
Rāma’s mind, as he sat on the top of the mountain that was adorned with rifts of gold ore seeing the sky of Ṡarat, became filled with the thoughts of his beloved.
4.30.7
सारसारावसन्नादैः
सारसारवनादिनी ।
याऽऽश्रमे रमते बाला
साऽद्य मे रमते कथम् ॥
I wonder what she, the young one, with a voice like that of Sārasas, who used to enjoy the cries of Sārasas along with me in the Āṡrama, is doing now!
4.30.8
पुष्पितांश्चासनान् दृष्ट्वा
काञ्चनानिव निर्मलान् ।
कथं सा रमते बाला
पश्यन्ती मामपश्यती ॥
How is she, my young love, able to hold herself watching the sight of gold-bright flowers of the Āsana trees while she waits for me without seeing a sign of me!
4.30.9
या पुरा कलहंसानाम्
स्वरेण कलभाषिणी ।
बुध्यते चारुसर्वाङ्गी
साऽद्य मे रमते कथम् ॥
How is she, my beloved, of beautiful figure who always spoke sweetly and used to be woken up by the dulcet notes of the sweet-throated swans able to manage herself?
4.30.10
निस्वनं चक्रवाकानाम्
निशम्य सहचारिणाम् ।
पुण्डरीकविशालाक्षी
कथमेषा भविष्यति ॥
How would she, of eyes wide like lotus petals, be able to manage herself hearing the cries of Cakravākas that move in pairs?
4.30.11
सरांसि सरितो वापीः
काननानि वनानि च ।
तां विना मृगशाबाक्षीम्
चरन्नाद्य सुखं लभे ॥
I find no joy at all wandering among the lakes and rivers and pools, and among the forests and the woods, without her of the fawn-eyes (by my side).
4.30.12
अपि तां मद्वियोगाच्च
सौकुमार्याच्च भामिनीम् ।
सुदूरं पीडयेत्कामः
शरद्गुणनिरन्तरः ॥
The God of Passion would surely torment her, the beautiful and delicate lady, with the abundant pleasantries of the Ṡarat, especially as she suffers from being away from me?
4.30.13
एवमादि नरश्रेष्ठो
विललाप नृपात्मजः ।
विहङ्ग इव सारङ्गः
सलिलं त्रिदशेश्वरात् ॥
That best of men, the prince, lamented in these strains like the Cātaka does while waiting for water from the Lord of Dēvas.
Cātaka birds drink only rain water as it falls from the sky.
4.30.14
ततश्चञ्चूर्य रम्येषु
फलार्थी गिरिसानुषु ।
ददर्श पर्युपावृत्तो
लक्ष्मीवान्लक्ष्मणोऽग्रजम् ॥
Lakshmaṇa of auspicious marks, as he returned after searching for fruits on the beautiful mountain sides, saw his elder brother in this state.
4.30.15
तं चिन्तया दुस्सहया परीतं
विसंज्ञमेकं विजने मनस्वी ।
भ्रातुर्विषादात्त्वरितोऽतिदीनः
समीक्ष्य सौमित्रिरुवाच रामम् ॥
Sowmitri saw Rāma sitting alone with no one around overwhelmed by unbearable sorrow. Feeling terrible and taking pity upon seeing his brother’s grief, he spoke anxiously:
4.30.16
किमार्य कामस्य वशंगतेन
किमात्मपौरुष्यपराभवेन ।
अयं ह्रिया संह्रियते समाधिः
किमत्र योगेन निवर्तितेन ॥
O noble one, of what use is it to cave in to the pangs of love? Of what use is it to undermine your prowess? Shaming oneself would only take away one’s focus. And, of what use is it to turn away from your object?
4.30.17
क्रियाभियोगं मनसः प्रसादं
समाधियोगानुगतं च कालम् ।
सहायसामर्थ्यमदीनसत्त्व:
स्वकर्म हेतुं च कुरुष्व हेतुम् ॥
Cheer up your mind. Focus on the task. Let every moment serve your purpose. Give up the despondency and count on the capabilities of allies and the skills that are uniquely your own.
4.30.18
न जानकी मानव वंशनाथ
त्वया सनाथा सुलभा परेण ।
न चाग्निचूडां ज्वलितामुपेत्य
न दह्यते वीर वरार्ह कश्चित् ॥
O Veera! O lord of the race of Manu! O you who deserves the best of everything! Janaki, with you for protector, cannot be appropriated by anyone else. Can one touch a blazing tongue of flame without burning oneself?
4.30.19
सलक्षणं लक्ष्मणमप्रधृष्यं
स्वभावजं वाक्यमुवाच रामः ।
हितं च पथ्यं च नयप्रसक्तं
ससाम धर्मार्थसमाहितं च ॥
Rāma then spoke to Lakshmaṇa of auspicious marks, words that mirrored his true nature and words that could not be argued with, words that bode well for the future, words that made sense under the given situation, and words that were in accordance with Dharma, Artha, polity and reason:
4.30.20
निस्संशयं कार्यमवेक्षितव्यं
क्रियाविशेषोऽह्यनुवर्तितव्यः ।
ननु प्रवृद्धस्य दुरासदस्य
कुमार कार्यस्य फलं च चिन्त्यम् ॥
Without doubt the task needs focus. Each and every step needs to be (carefully) coordinated. My dear, the favorable as well as adversarial factors and the outcomes as well as fallouts should be given deep consideration.
4.30.21
अथ पद्मपलाशाक्षीम्
मैथिलीमनुचिन्तयन् ।
उवाच लक्ष्मणं रामो
मुखेन परिशुष्यता ॥
Rāma went on, with these words, his face dried up and thinking of Maithili of lotus-petal eyes:
4.30.22
तर्पयित्वा सहस्राक्षः
सलिलेन वसुन्धराम् ।
निर्वर्तयित्वा सस्यानि
कृतकर्मा व्यवस्थितः ॥
The Thousand-eyed, having satiated the earth with water and making new vegetation take sprout, rests well content, his work done.
4.30.23
दीर्घगम्भीरनिर्घोषाः
शैलद्रुमपुरोगमाः ।
विसृज्य सलिलं मेघाः
परिश्रान्ता नृपात्मज ॥
The clouds that majestically rumbled for long, passing by the mountains and trees, have retired now, O prince, having emptied their loads of water.
4.30.24
नीलोत्पलदलश्यामाः
श्यामीकृत्वा दिशो दश ।
विमदा इव मातङ्गः
शान्तवेगाः पयोधराः ॥
The clouds that were dark as the petals of the black lotuses spreading darkness in all ten directions, have slowed down now, like elephants that are no more in rut.
4.30.25
जलगर्भा महामेघा
कुटजार्जुनगन्धिनः ।
चरित्वा विरतास्सौम्य
वृष्टिवातास्समुद्यताः ॥
The great clouds with loads of water and the rainy winds carrying the fragrance of Kuṭaja and Arjuna flowers, have retired, my dear, having done their rounds.
4.30.26
घनानां वारणानां च
मयूराणां च लक्ष्मण ।
नादः प्रस्रवणानां च
प्रशान्तस्सहसाऽनघ ॥
O Lakshmaṇa, O flawless one! The sounds of clouds, elephants, peacocks and the streams have calmed down pretty quickly.
4.30.27
अभिवृष्टा महामेघैः
निर्मलाश्चित्रसानवः ।
अनुलिप्ता इवाऽभान्ति
गिरयश्चन्द्ररश्मिभिः ॥
The mountains, with their beautiful crests, washed clean by the great clouds, look as if they are coated with the rays of the moon.
4.30.28
शाखासु सप्तच्छदपादपानाम्
प्रभासु तारार्कनिशाकराणाम् ।
लीलासु चैवोत्तम वारणानां
श्रियं विभज्याद्य शरत्प्रवृत्ता ॥
Ṡarat has commenced by bestowing her largesse among the boughs of the Saptaparṇa trees, among the splendor of stars, sun and moon, and among the gambols of great elephants.
The long description of Ṡarat from Ṡlōkas 28 through 57 in long meter, though beautiful, is considered unauthentic.
4.30.29
सम्प्रत्यनेकाश्रय चित्रशोभा
लक्ष्मीश्शरत्कालगुणोपपन्ना ।
सूर्याग्रहस्तप्रतिबोधितेषु
पद्माकरेष्वभ्यधिकं विभाति ॥
The loveliness of nature in Ṡarat, which is manifested diversely in a multitude of objects, shows itself in overflowing abundance in the lotus beds awakened by the touch of the fingers of the morning sun.
4.30.30
सप्तच्छदानां कुसुमोपगन्धी
षट्पादबृन्दैरनुगीयमानः ।
मत्तद्विपानां पवनानुसारी
दर्पं विनेष्यन्नधिकं विभाति ॥
The fragrance of the Saptaparṇa flowers that flew everywhere following the wind, itself followed by the swarms of singing bees, handsomely dwarfs the (smell of) elephants in rut.
4.30.31
अभ्यागतैश्चारुविशालपक्षैः
सरःप्रियैः पद्मरजोवकीर्णैः ।
महानदीनां पुलिनोपयातैः
क्रीडन्ति हंसास्सह चक्रवाकैः ॥
The swans play with the Cakravākas of large and beautiful plumage covered with the pollen of the lotuses, that normally love lakes, but were now making a visit to the sandy beaches of the great rivers.
4.30.32
मदप्रगल्भेषु च वारणेषु
गवां समूहेषु च दर्पितेषु ।
प्रसन्नतोयासु च निम्नगासु
विभाति लक्ष्मीर्बहुधा विभक्ता ॥
The richness of Ṡarat reveals itself variedly in the elephants that are in rut, in the herds of bulls that are prideful and in the waters of the rivers that are pleasant.
4.30.33
नभस्समीक्ष्याम्बुधरैर्विमुक्तं
विमुक्तबर्हाभरणा वनेषु ।
प्रियास्वसक्ता विनिवृत्तशोभा
गतोत्सवा ध्यानपरा मयूराः ॥
Seeing the sky become free from clouds, the peacocks shed their ornaments of plumage and lose interest in their playmates. With their splendor gone, their festive moods behind, they are now lost in meditation.
4.30.34
मनोज्ञगन्धैः प्रियकैरनल्पैः
पुष्पातिभारावनताग्रशाखैः ।
सुवर्णगौरैर्नयनाभिरामैः
उद्योतितानीव वनान्तराणि ॥
The interiors of the Vanas are lit up by the abundance of Priyaka trees of pleasant fragrance feasting the eyes with their golden yellow hue and their branches bowing down with the weight of flowers.
4.30.35
प्रियान्वितानां नलिनीप्रियाणां
वनप्रियाणां कुसुमोद्धतानाम् ।
मदोत्कटानां मदलालसानां
गजोत्तमानां गतयोऽद्य मन्दाः ॥
The great elephants that love water and lotuses, intoxicated with the scent of flowers, and reveling in the ichor (flowing from their temples), wander, along with their mates, at a leisurely pace.
4.30.36
व्यभ्रं नभश्शस्त्रविधौतवर्णं
कृशप्रवाहानि नदीजलानि ।
कह्लारशीताः पवनाः प्रवान्ति
तमोविमुक्ताश्च दिशः प्रकाशाः ॥
The sky, cleared of clouds, gleams like a sharpened sword. The flow of waters in the rivers have thinned down. The breezes are cooled down by lilies. The four quarters, freed from darkness, shine brightly.
4.30.37
सूर्यातपक्रामणनष्टपङ्का
भूमिश्चिरोदधाटितसान्द्ररेणुः ।
अन्योन्यवैरेणसमायुतानाम्
उद्योगकालोऽद्य नराधिपानाम् ॥
After the passing of a long time, dust rises into the air with the mire disappearing under the hot sun. The season has arrived for the rulers of people to come together to settle their scores.
4.30.38
शरद्गुणाप्यायितरूपशोभाः
प्रहर्षिताः पांशुसमुत्क्षिताङ्गाः ।
मदोत्कटास्सम्प्रति युद्धलुब्धा
वृषा गवां मध्यगता नदन्ति ॥
With the season of Ṡarat endowing them with added glow to their bodies and with dust covering every limb of theirs, the impassioned bulls in their high spirits call each other for a fight.
4.30.39
समन्मथा तीव्रतरानुरागा
कुलान्विता मन्दगतिः करेणुः ।
मदान्वितं सम्परिवार्य यान्तम्
वनेषु भर्तारमनुप्रयाति ॥
The she elephant, in the good company of its kind, filled with lust, spurred by intense passion, closely follows her lord, as he, filled with passion, wanders slowly in the Vana.
4.30.40
त्यक्त्वा वराण्यात्मविभूषणानि
बर्हाणि तीरोपगता नदीनाम् ।
निर्भर्त्स्यमाना इव सारसौघैः
प्रयान्ति दीना विमना मयूराः ॥
The peacocks, shedding off their ornaments, the plumage, reach the river banks. They walk away dispirited and unhappy, as if they were intimidated by the multitudes of Sārasa cranes.
4.30.41
वित्रास्य कारण्डवचक्रवाकान्
महारवैर्भिन्नकटा गजेन्द्राः ।
सरस्सु बद्धाम्बुजभूषणेषु
विक्षोभ्य विक्षोभ्य जलं पिबन्ति ॥
Frightening away the Kāraṇḍavas and Cakravākas by their loud trumpeting, the great tuskers, with ichor streaming down their temples, drink from the lakes adorned with lotuses in bloom, agitating the water again and again.
4.30.42
व्यपेतपङ्कासु सवालुकासु
प्रसन्नतोयासु सगोकुलासु ।
ससारसारावनिनादितासु
नदीषु हंसा निपतन्ति हृष्टाः ॥
The swans plunge joyously in the pleasant waters of the streams that are sandy and free from slush, resounding with the cries of Sārasas and attended by herds of cows.
4.30.43
नदीघनप्रस्रवणोदकानाम्
अतिप्रवृद्धानिलबर्हिणानाम् ।
प्लवङ्गमानां च गतोत्सवानाम्
ध्रुवं रवास्सम्प्रति सम्प्रणष्टाः ॥
The din of rivers, clouds and waterfalls, the raging winds and the peacocks and that of frogs whose days of rejoicing are over, has certainly ceased now.
4.30.44
अनेकवर्णास्सुविनष्टकायाः
नवोदितेष्वम्बुधरेषु नष्टाः ।
क्षुधार्दिता घोरविषा बिलेभ्यः
चिरोषिता विप्रसरन्ति सर्पाः ॥
Virulently poisonous snakes of different colors that disappeared when the clouds appeared, are coming out of their holes where they had lived for long, their bodies emaciated by excruciating hunger.
4.30.45
चञ्चच्चन्द्रकरस्पर्श
हर्षोन्मीलिततारका ।
अहो रागवती सन्ध्या
जहाति स्वयमम्बरम् ॥
The stars are joyously twinkling touched by the rays of the moon, as the scarlet twilight bids good bye to the sky, seemingly on its own!
4.30.46
रात्रिश्शशाङ्कोदितसौम्यवक्त्रा
तारागणोन्मीलितचारुनेत्रा ।
ज्योत्स्नांशुकप्रावरणा विभाति
नारीव शुक्लांशुकसंवृताङ्गी ॥
With the risen moon for her pleasant face, the twinkling stars for her beautiful eyes the moon-light for her garment, the sky is gorgeous like a woman clad in a white garment.
4.30.47
विपक्वशालिप्रसवानि भुक्त्वा
प्रहर्षिता सारसचारुपङ्क्ति: ।
नभस्समाक्रामति शीघ्रवेगाः
वातावधूता ग्रथितेव माला ॥
The Sārasa birds, content feeding on mature seeds of paddy, fly past in beautiful rows filling the sky as if they are flowers blown by the wind in the shape of garlands.
4.30.48
सुप्तैकहंसं कुमुदैरुपेतं
महाह्रदस्थं सलिलं विभाति ।
घनैर्विमुक्तं निशि पूर्णचन्द्रं
तारागणाकीर्णमिवान्तरिक्षम् ॥
The water in the great lake, with a single swan sleeping on it in the midst of an abundance of flowers, looks like the night sky clear of clouds, with the full moon shining and the stars strewn all over it.
4.30.49
प्रकीर्णहंसाकुलमेखलानां
प्रबुद्धपद्मोत्पलमालिनीनाम् ।
वाप्युत्तमानामधिकाऽद्य लक्ष्मीः
वराङ्गनानामिव भूषितानाम् ॥
The beauty of the wonderful step wells is enhanced by the scattered swans that look like waistbands and by the lotuses and lilies in bloom that look like garlands, just as the beauty of a lovely woman is enhanced by ornaments worn from head to toe.
4.30.50
वेणुस्वनव्यञ्जिततूर्यमिश्रः
प्रत्यूषकालेऽनिलसंप्रवृत्तः ।
सम्मूर्छितो गह्वरगोवृषाणाम्
अन्योन्यमापूरयतीव शब्दः ॥
The medley of wind-pipe like sounds from the bamboo reeds enhanced and spread everywhere by the morning breeze complements the bellowing of the bulls in the caves.
4.30.51
नवैर्नदीनां कुसुमप्रहासैः
व्याधूयमानैर्मृदुमारुतेन ।
धौतामलक्षौमपटप्रकाशैः
कूलानि काशैरुपशोभितानि ॥
The banks of the river look beautiful and seem to smile with the sheen of the white flowers of the reeds as they sway in the mild breeze, like bleached white silk cloth.
4.30.52
वनप्रचण्डा मधुपानशौण्डा:
प्रियान्विताष्षट्चरणाः प्रहृष्टाः ।
वनेषु मत्ताः पवनानुयात्रां
कुर्वन्ति पद्मासनरेणुगौराः ॥
The bumble bees that dominate the Vanas, skilled and happy in swilling the honey, covered with the pollen of lotuses and Āsana flowers, are getting, along with their mates, a joy ride on the blowing wind.
4.30.53
जलं प्रसन्नं कुसुम प्रहासम्
क्रौञ्चस्वनं शालिवनं विपक्वम् ।
मृदुश्च वायुर्विमलश्च चन्द्रः
शंसन्ति वर्षव्यपनीतकालम् ॥
The smiling flowers, pleasant waters, sounds of the Krouncas, matured paddy, gentle breeze and clear moon tell that the monsoon season is over.
4.30.54
मीनोपसन्दर्शितमेखलानां
नदीवधूनां गतयोऽद्य मन्दाः ।
कान्तोपभुक्तालसगामिनीनां
प्रभातकालेष्विव कामिनीनाम् ॥
The streams, that flaunt their fish like waist bands, move slowly, like women in the morning after they have been enjoyed by their lovers.
4.30.55
सचक्रवाकानि सशैवलानि
काशैर्दुकूलैरिव संवृतानि ।
सपत्ररेखानि सरोचनानि
वधूमुखानीव नदीमुखानि ॥
The rivers, with Cakravākas and moss on them and covered by the clothing of Kāṡa reeds, resemble the faces of young women painted with decorative marks and smeared with vermillion.
4.30.56
प्रफुल्लबाणासनचित्रितेषु
प्रहृष्टषट्पादनिकूजितेषु ।
गृहीतचापोद्यतदण्डचण्डः
प्रचण्डचारोऽद्य वनेषु कामः ॥
In the Vana, which is imprinted with the Bāṇa and Āsana trees in flower, and which resounds with the hum of happy bees, the God of Passion rampages with his bow and deadly shafts, ready to strike.
4.30.57
लोकं सुवृष्ट्या परितोषयित्वा
नदीस्तटाकानि च पूरयित्वा ।
निष्पन्नसस्यां वसुधां च कृत्वा
त्यक्त्वा नभस्तोयधराः प्रणष्टाः ॥
The clouds, satiating the world with heavy rains, filling the rivers and lakes to the brim and making the earth abundant with new crops and vegetation, have left the sky and vanished.
4.30.58
दर्शयन्ति शरन्नद्यः
पुलिनानि शनैः शनैः ।
नवसङ्गमसव्रीडा
जघनानीव योषितः ॥
The streams of the Ṡarat slowly reveal their sand-flats as bashful women do their hips at the time of their first ever mating.
4.30.59
प्रसन्नसलिलास्सौम्य
कुररीभिर्विनादिताः ।
चक्रवाकगणाकीर्णा
विभान्ति सलिलाशयाः ॥
The reservoirs, my dear, shine with their crystal waters, resounding with the cries of female Krouncas and dotted all over with flocks of Cakravākas.
4.30.60
असनास्सप्तपर्णाश्च
कोविदाराश्च पुष्पिताः ।
दृश्यन्ते बन्धुजीवाश्च
श्यामाश्च गिरिसानुषु ॥
On the flanks of mountains are to be seen the Asana, Saptaparṇa, Kōvidāra and the dark Bandhujeevas in flower.
4.30.61
हंससारसचक्राह्वैः
कुररैश्च समन्ततः ।
पुलिनान्यवकीर्णानि
नदीनां पश्य लक्ष्मण ॥
Look at the beaches of the rivers, Lakshmaṇa, which are dotted with swans, Sārasas, Cakravākas and Krouncas everywhere.
4.30.62
अन्योन्यबद्धवैराणाम्
जिगीषूणां नृपात्मज ।
उद्योगसमयस्सौम्य
पार्थिवानामुपस्थितः ॥
My dear prince! The season has arrived for the kings, who are at feud and want to win over each other, to go on war expeditions.
4.30.63
इयं सा प्रथमा यात्रा
पार्थिवानां नृपात्मज ।
न च पश्यामि सुग्रीवम्
उद्योगं वा तथाविधम् ॥
This is the time for the first wave of expeditions to start, but, O prince, I see no sign of Sugreeva being on any such expedition.
4.30.64
चत्वारो वार्षिका मासा
गता वर्षशतोपमाः ।
मम शोकाभिभूतस्य
तथा सीतामपश्यतः ॥
The four months of rains have gone by; to me, overwhelmed by grief at not seeing Seetā, they seem like a hundred years.
4.30.65
चक्रवाकीव भर्तारम्
पृष्ठतोऽनुगता वनम् ।
विषमं दण्डकारण्यम्
उद्यानमिव चाञ्गना ॥
She had followed her husband to the Vana like a female Cakravāka and on to the impenetrable Daṇḍaka Araṇya as if it were a pleasure-garden.
4.30.66
प्रियाविहीने दुःखार्ते
हृतराज्ये विवासिते ।
कृपां न कुरुते राजा
सुग्रीवो मयि लक्ष्मण ॥
Sugreeva the King, does not take pity, O Lakshmaṇa, on me, who has lost his beloved and is distraught with grief, deprived of kingdom and exiled.
4.30.67
अनाथो हृतराज्योऽयम्
रावणेन च धर्षितः ।
दीनो दूरगृहः कामी
मां चैव शरणं गतः ।
इत्येतैः कारणैस्सौम्य
सुग्रीवस्य दुरात्मनः ।
अहं वानरराजस्य
परिभूतः परन्तप ॥
My dear! O scorcher of the foe! I am being slighted by the evil-minded Sugreeva, the king of Vānaras, who thinks that: ‘This man has no other recourse, his kingdom is taken away, he is despondent, caught in the pangs of love, slighted by Rāvaṇa, away from home and has sought refuge in me’!
4.30.69
स कालं परिसङ्ख्याय
सीतायाः परिमार्गणे ।
कृतार्थस्समयं कृत्वा
दुर्मतिर्नावबुध्यते ॥
He, the evil-minded one, does not realize that it is time to start the search for Seetā, having agreed upon that time, now that he has won his object.
4.30.70
त्वं च किष्किन्धां प्रविश्य
ब्रूहि वानरपुङ्गवम् ।
मूर्खं ग्राम्यसुखे सक्तम्
सुग्रीवं वचनान्मम ॥
You go to Kishkindhā and convey this message of mine to that stupid Sugreeva, that chief of Vānaras, who is lost in the pleasure of women:
4.30.71
अर्थिनामुपपन्नानाम्
पूर्वं चाप्युपकारिणाम् ।
आशां संश्रुत्य यो हन्ति
स लोके पुरुषाधमः ॥
He who kills the hopes of people who are dependent on him, who have taken refuge in him and who have done him a favor earlier, (and that too,) after making a promise, is the most detestable of all men.
4.30.72
शुभं वा यदि वा पापम्
यो हि वाक्यमुदीरितम् ।
सत्येन प्रतिगृह्णाति
स वीरः पुरुषोत्तमः ॥
A Veera who keeps the word he utters, regardless of whether it results in gains or losses, is the foremost of spirited beings.
4.30.73
कृतार्था ह्यकृतार्थानाम्
मित्राणां न भवन्ति ये ।
तान्मृतानपि क्रव्यादाः
कृतघ्नान्नोपभुञ्जते ॥
Even the animals that feed on carcasses would not touch the dead body of such a person who fails to respond to the needy after having his own needs met.
4.30.74
नूनं काञ्चनपृष्ठस्य
विकृष्टस्य मया रणे ।
द्रष्टुमिच्छति चापस्य
रूपं विद्युद्गणोपमम् ॥
Clearly, it seems, you want to see me pull the string of the bow, that is inlaid with gold and flashes like lightning in battle.
4.30.75
घोरं ज्यातलनिर्घोषम्
क्रुद्धस्य मम संयुगे ।
निर्घोषमिव वज्रस्य
पुनस्संश्रोतुमिच्छसि ॥
You seem to want to hear again the dreadful twang of the bow-string that resembles the thud of Vajra in the battle, from me, in my wrath.
4.30.76
काममेवंगतेऽप्यस्य
परिज्ञाते पराक्रमे ।
त्वत्सहायस्य मे वीर
न चिन्ता स्यान्नृपात्मज ॥
Even if it turns out that the prowess of this man whom we know to be of no use to us, I shall not worry, having you, O Veera, O prince, helping me.
4.30.77
यदर्थमयमारम्भः
कृतः परपुरञ्जयः ।
समयं नाभिजानाति
कृतार्थः प्लवगेश्वरः ॥
O conqueror of the citadels of the foe! Having his objects met, the Lord of Vānaras does not realize the context and purpose because of which all of this started, and that it is time now to act!
4.30.78
वर्षासमयकालं तु
प्रतिज्ञाय हरीश्वरः ।
व्यतीतांश्चतुरो मासान्
विहरन्नावबुध्यते ॥
The Lord of Vānaras, having promised to act at the end of the season of rains, does not recognize the passing of four months, lost in dalliance.
4.30.79
सामात्यपरिषत्क्रीडन्
पानमेवोपसेवते ।
शोकदीनेषु नास्मासु
सुग्रीवः कुरुते दयाम् ॥
Diverting himself along with his officials and counsellors, Sugreeva is drunk all the time, showing no concern at all to our grief and despondency.
4.30.80
उच्यतां गच्छ सुग्रीवः
त्वया वीर महाबल ।
मम रोषस्य यद्रूपम्
ब्रूयाश्चैनमिदं वचः ॥
O Veera of immense strength! Go and tell Sugreeva how it would be to see me in wrath. Also, tell him this:
4.30.81
न च सङ्कुचितः पन्था
येन वाली हतो गतः ।
समये तिष्ठ सुग्रीव
मा वालिपथमन्वगाः ॥
The road that the slain Vāli took, is not yet closed; stick to your compact Sugreeva, do not follow the path of Vāli!
4.30.82
एक एव रणे वाली
शरेण निहतो मया ।
त्वां तु सत्यादतिक्रान्तम्
हनिष्यामि सबान्धवम् ॥
Vāli alone was killed in the fight by my arrow; but you, having broken your word, will be destroyed along with all your kin.
4.30.83
तदेवं विहिते कार्ये
यद्धितं पुरुषर्षभ ।
तत्तद्भ्रूहि नरश्रेष्ठ
त्वर कालव्यतिक्रमः ॥
O best of men! O bull among men! Hasten up! Let no time be lost! Things being what they are, tell (him) whatever serves the purpose:
4.30.84
कुरुष्व सत्यं मयि वानरेश्वर
प्रतिश्रुतं धर्ममवेक्ष्य शाश्वतम् ।
मा वालिनं प्रेत्य गतो यमक्षयं
त्वमद्य पश्येर्मम चोदितैश्शरैः ॥
O Lord of Vānaras! Consider the eternal Dharma. Fulfill the promise you made to me. Do not go to the abode of Yama to see Vāli, killed by the arrows shot by me!
4.30.85
स पूर्वजं तीव्रविवृद्धकोपं
लालप्यमानं प्रसमीक्ष्य दीनम् ।
चकार तीव्रां मतिमुग्रतेजा
हरीश्वरे मानववंश वर्धनः ॥
Seeing his elder brother in a towering rage, dolefully lamenting his fate, (Lakshmaṇa) of fierce power, augmenter of the race of Manu, made up his mind to take a tough stance against the ruler of the Vānaras.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे त्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirtieth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
