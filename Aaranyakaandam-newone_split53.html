Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 53
In this Sarga, Seetā, as she is being carried off helplessly by Rāvaṇa, taunts him for being the coward he is, in carrying her off when she was all alone. She warns him again that he cannot escape death at the hands of Rāma, who killed fourteen thousand Rākshasas single handedly without even taking the help of his brother. She makes it clear that she would not live for long without seeing her husband and being a captive of the enemy.
3.53.1
खमुत्पतन्तं तं दृष्ट्वा
मैथिली जनकात्मजा ।
दुःखिता परमोद्विग्ना
भये महति वर्तिनी ॥
When she saw him rise into the sky, Maithili, the daughter of Janaka, felt miserable and extremely agitated and was gripped by great fear.
3.53.2
रोषरोदनताम्राक्षी
भीमाक्षं राक्षसाधिपम् ।
रुदन्ती करुणं सीता
ह्रियमाणेदमब्रवीत् ॥
Seetā, crying pitifully, her eyes red as she cried and felt enraged, said these words to that lord of Rākshasas of terrifying eyes, who was thus carrying her off:
3.53.3
न व्यपत्रपसे नीच
कर्मणाऽनेन रावण ।
ज्ञात्वा विरहितां यन्माम्
चोरयित्वा पलायसे ॥
O Rāvaṇa! You are fleeing like a thief, after having stolen me when I was alone! O base creature! Are you not ashamed of what you are doing?
3.53.4
त्वयैव नूनं दुष्टात्मन्
भीरुणा हर्तुमिच्छता ।
ममापवाहितो भर्ता
मृगरूपेण मायया ॥
O evil one, it indeed looks like your plan to lure my husband away by tricking (us) in the form of a magical deer, in order to abduct me like a coward.
3.53.5
यो हि मामुद्यतस्त्रातुम्
सोऽप्ययं विनिपातितः ।
गृध्रराजः पुराणोऽसौ
श्वशुरस्य सखा मम ॥
Even the king among eagles, a good old friend of my father-in-law who exerted himself to rescue me was struck down now.
3.53.6
परमं खलु ते वीर्यम्
दृश्यते राक्षसाधम ।
विश्राव्य नामधेयं हि
युद्धेनास्मि जिता त्वया ॥
You base Rākshasa! The height of your prowess is clearly evident now that you could not announce your name and win me in a battle!
3.53.7
ईदृशं गर्हितं कर्म
कथं कृत्वा न लज्जसे ।
स्त्रियाश्च हरणं नीच
रहिते च परस्य च ॥
How is it that you are not ashamed, O base creature, to do an abominable act like this, of abducting another’s woman when she is alone?
3.53.8
कथयिष्यन्ति लोकेषु
पुरुषाः कर्म कुत्सितम् ।
सुनृशंसमधर्मिष्ठम्
तव शौण्डीर्यमानिनः ॥
Men of the world, for ages to come, will recount this act as damnable, extremely cruel and unrighteous, while you may preen yourself for your valor.
3.53.9
धिक्ते शौर्यं च सत्त्वं च
यत्त्वं कथितवांस्तदा ।
कुलाक्रोशकरं लेके
धिक्ते चारित्रमीदृशम् ॥
Dhik, your valor and strength that you have boasted of! Dhik, the kind of conduct that will make your entire lineage feel bad (forever) in this world!
It was not too long ago that families of common descent used to feel like a single unit, taking pride not only in their culture and cuisine, but also in their upright behavior. People used to hang their head in shame even if one of the members of their lineage behaved in a questionable manner.
3.53.10
किं कर्तुं शक्यमेवं हि
यज्जवेनैव धावसि ।
मुहूर्तमपि तिष्ठस्व
न जीवन्प्रतियास्यसि ॥
What can one do when you run away in headlong flight like this? Stop even for a brief while, and you will not escape with your life.
3.53.11
न हि चक्षुष्पथं प्राप्य
तयोः पार्थिवपुत्रयोः ।
ससैन्योऽपि समर्थस्त्वम्
मुहूर्तमपि जीवितुम् ॥
If you are only seen by the duo of princes, you will not, even if backed by your army, survive even for a Muhūrta.
3.53.12
न त्वं तयोश्शरस्पर्शम्
सोढुं शक्तः कथञ्चन ।
वने प्रज्वलितस्येव
स्पर्शमग्नेर्विहंगमः ॥
There is no way you can withstand the strike of their arrows, just as a bird cannot, the touch of a blazing forest fire.
3.53.13
साधु कुर्वाऽऽत्मनः पथ्यम्
साधु मां मुञ्च रावण ।
मत्प्रधर्षणरुष्टो हि
भ्रात्रा सह पतिर्मम ।
विधास्यति विनाशाय
त्वं मां यदि न मुञ्चसि ॥
O Rāvaṇa! Consider your own interest carefully and release me, in the right manner. If you do not let me go, my husband, along with his brother, enraged for violating me, will make an all out effort to destroy you.
3.53.14
येन त्वं व्यवसायेन
बलान्मां हर्तुमिच्छसि ।
व्यवसायस्स ते नीच
भविष्यति निरर्थकः ॥
Whatever endeavor you have undertaken, O base creature, in abducting me by force, will end up being of no avail!
3.53.15
न ह्यहं तमपश्यन्ती
भर्तारं विबुधोपमम् ।
उत्सहे शत्रुवशगा
प्राणान्धारयितुं चिरम् ॥
Without seeing my husband, who is like a Dēva, and being a captive of an enemy I would have no interest in living for long!
3.53.16
न नूनं चात्मनः पथ्यम्
श्रेयो वा समवेक्षसे ।
मृत्युकाले यथा मर्त्यो
विपरीतानि सेवते ॥
Man succumbs to weird behavior as the time of death nears. You similarly, and certainly, do not seem to know what is good for you and would bring prosperity to you.
3.53.17
मुमूर्षूणां हि सर्वेषाम्
यत्पथ्यं तन्न रोचते ।
पश्याम्यद्य हि कण्ठेत्वाम्
कालपाशावपाशितम् ॥
Everyone, when death knocks on the door, feels (weirdly) averse to what is good for them. I can clearly see now, the noose of death tied around your neck.
3.53.18
यथा चास्मिन्भयस्थाने
न बिभेषि दशानन ।
व्यक्तं हिरण्मयान् हि त्वम्
सम्पश्यसि महीरुहान् ॥
You are not even worried, O Ten-faced, when you should be really scared. It is clear that you are seeing golden trees.
There is a saying from olden times, that people see golden trees in their vision when they are about to die. This expression is used earlier in Ṡlōka 3.47.38 also.
3.53.19
नदीं वैतरणीं घोराम्
रुधिरौघनिवाहिनीम् ।
असिपत्रवनं चैव
भीमं पश्यसि रावण ॥
You will see the dreadful river of Vaitaraṇi with its flood of gore, and the terrible forest where trees have swords for leaves, O Rāvaṇa!
River Vaitaraṇi, forest with trees having swords for leaves and Ṡālmali trees with spikes are seen in hell.
3.53.20
तप्तकाञ्चनपुष्पां च
वैडूर्यप्रवरच्छदाम् ।
द्रक्ष्यसे शाल्मलीं तीक्ष्णाम्
आयसैः कण्टकैश्चिताम् ॥
You will see the Ṡālmali tree covered with iron spikes and bearing flowers of molten gold and leaves of lazulite.
3.53.21
न हि त्वमीदृशं कृत्वा
तस्यालीकं महात्मनः ।
धरितुं शक्ष्यसि चिरम्
विषं पीत्वेव निर्घृणः ॥
Committing this kind of offense against that Mahātma, O pitiless one, you can no more hope to live for long, like you would not after drinking poison.
3.53.22
बद्धस्त्वं कालपाशेन
दुर्निवारेण रावण ।
क्वगतो लप्स्यसे शर्म
भर्तुर्मम महात्मनः ॥
O Rāvaṇa! You are now tied by the noose of death, from which there is no escape. Where can you flee to find respite from my husband, the Mahātma?
3.53.23
निमेषान्तरमात्रेण
विना भ्रात्रा महावने ।
राक्षसा निहता येन
सहस्राणि चतुर्दश ।
स कथं राघवो वीरः
सर्वास्त्रकुशलो बली ।
न त्वां हन्याच्छरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
इष्टभार्यापहारिणम् ॥
Why would the mighty strong Rāghava, the Veera, who is skilled in all Astras and who killed fourteen thousand Rākshasas in a blink without even taking the help of his brother, refrain from killing you with his sharp arrows, for abducting his wife?
3.53.25
एतच्चान्यच्च परुषम्
वैदेही रावणाङ्कगा ।
भयशोकसमाविष्टा
करुणं विललाप ह ॥
Saying many a strong word in this strain Vaidēhee, trapped on the lap of Rāvaṇa, cried pitifully taken over by fear and grief.
3.53.26
तथा भृशार्तां बहुचैव भाषिणीं
विलापपूर्वं करुणं च भामिनीम् ।
जहार पापः करुणं विवेष्टतीं
नृपात्मजामागतगात्रवेपथुम् ॥
The sinful one thus carried off the young and beautiful princess, who was writhing pitiably and trembling all over, distraught with grief, and saying many a thing in a pitiful and woeful manner.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे त्रिपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty third Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
