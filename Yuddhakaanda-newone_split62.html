Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 62
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa explains the grave situation that he is in, with his treasury depleted, Rākshasas and their chiefs killed, Laṅkā including its woods turned into a sea of Vānaras, and himself defeated and gripped by the fear of Rāma. He begs Kumbhakarṇa to save him by destroying the enemy forces.
6.62.1
स तु राक्षसशार्दूलो
निद्रामदसमाकुलः ।
राजमार्गं श्रिया जुष्टम्
ययौ विपुलविक्रमः ॥
Meanwhile that tiger among Rākshasas, of extraordinary prowess, went along the opulent Royal pathway, his mind confounded by the drink and disturbed sleep.
6.62.2
राक्षसानां सहस्रैश्च
वृतः परमदुर्जयः ।
गृहेभ्यः पुष्पवर्षेण
कीर्यमाणस्तदा ययौ ॥
Surrounded by thousands of Rākshasas, the invincible one went along, showered with flowers from the houses on the way.
6.62.3
स हेमजालिविततम्
भानुभास्वरदर्शनम् ।
ददर्श विपुलं रम्यम्
राक्षसेन्द्रनिवेशनम् ॥
He beheld the spacious and beautiful palace of the King of the Rākshasas, which, with its golden casements all over, looked as bright as the sun.
6.62.4
स तत्तदा सूर्य इवाभ्रजालम्
प्रविश्य रक्षोधिपतेर्निवेशम् ।
ददर्श दूरेऽग्रजमासनस्थम्
स्वयम्भुवं शक्र इवासनस्थम् ॥
Entering the mansion of the ruler of the Rākshasas, like the sun entering a bank of clouds, he beheld from afar his elder brother seated on his throne, as Ṡakra might behold the Self-born.
6.62.5
भ्रातुः स भवनं गच्छन्
रक्षोगणसमन्वितः ।
कुम्भकर्णः पदन्यासैः
अकम्पयत मेदिनीम् ॥
Kumbhakarṇa shook the earth with his tread as he, surrounded by large numbers of Rākshasas, entered his brother’s home.
6.62.6
सोऽभिगम्य गृहं भ्रातुः
कक्ष्यामभिविगाह्य च ।
ददर्शोद्विग्नमासीनम्
विमाने पुष्पके गुरुम् ॥
Reaching his brother's house and passing the courtyard, he saw his agitated elder brother, seated in the Pushpaka Vimāna.
6.62.7
अथ दृष्ट्वा दशग्रीवः
कुम्भकर्णमुपस्थितम् ।
तूर्णमुत्थाय संहृष्टः
सन्निकर्षमुपानयत् ॥
Seeing Kumbhakarṇa arrive in his presence, the Ten-necked got up at once with delight, and had him brought close to him.
6.62.8
अथासीनस्य पर्यङ्के
कुम्भकर्णो महाबलः ।
भ्रातुर्ववन्दे चरणौ
किं कृत्यमिति चाब्रवीत् ॥
Kumbhakarṇa, of immense strength, prostrated at the feet of his brother who was seated on his couch, and asked, ‘What can I do (for you)?’
6.62.9
उत्पत्य चैनं मुदितो
रावणः परिषस्वजे ।
स भ्रात्रा सम्परिष्वक्तो
यथावच्छाभिनन्दितः ।
कुम्भकर्णः शुभं दिव्यम्
प्रतिपेदे वरासनम् ॥
Getting up, Rāvaṇa embraced him in joy. After having been thus embraced and received with honors Kumbhakarṇa took a seat that was auspicious and splendid.
6.62.10
स तदासनमाश्रित्य
कुम्भकर्णो महाबलः ।
संरक्तनयनः क्रोधात्
रावणं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Taking his seat, Kumbhakarṇa of immense strength, his eyes blood-red with anger, said these words to Rāvaṇa:
6.62.11
किमर्थमहमादृत्य
त्वया राजन् विबोधितः ।
शंस कस्माद्भयं तेऽस्ति
कोऽद्य प्रेतो भविष्यति ॥
What is the reason for my being woken up by you, O King, with all kindness? Tell me who you have a fear from and who should be turned into a ghost this very day?
6.62.12
भ्रातरं रावणः कुद्धम्
कुम्भकर्णमवस्थितम् ।
रोषेण परिवृत्ताभ्याम्
नेत्राभ्यां वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Rāvaṇa said these words to his angry brother Kumbhakarṇa who sat by him rolling his eyes in rage.
6.62.13
अयं ते सुमहान् कालः
शयानस्य महाबल ।
सुषुप्तस्त्वं न जानीषे
मम रामकृतं भयम् ॥
There passed a long time since you fell asleep, O you of immense strength! Being in sleep, you do not know about the fear caused by Rāma to me.
6.62.14
एष दाशरथी रामः
सुग्रीवसहितो बली ।
समुद्रं सबलस्तीर्त्वा
मूलं नः परिकृन्तति ॥
This strong one, Rāma, son of Daṡaratha, along with Sugreeva and his army crossed the ocean and is axing our roots.
6.62.15
हन्त पश्यस्व लङ्कायाम्
वनान्युपवनानि च ।
सेतुना सुखमागम्य
वानरैकार्णवीकृतम् ॥
Alas! Look how, with their coming with ease over the bridge, Laṅkā, along with its woods and gardens, has been turned into a sea of Vānaras!
6.62.16
ये रक्षसां मुख्यतमा
हतास्ते वानरैर्युधि ।
वानराणां क्षयं युद्धे
न पश्यामि कदाचन ।
न चापि वानरा युद्धे
जितपूर्वाः कदाचन ॥
The Rākshasas who were prominent were slain by the Vānaras in the battle; but I do not see any losses among the Vānaras in any manner in this battle. (For that matter), the Vānaras seem to know no defeat in any battle in the past.
6.62.18
तदेतद्भयमुत्पन्नम्
त्रायस्वेह महाबल ।
नाशय त्वमिमानद्य
तदर्थं बोधितो भवान् ॥
Such is the fear that has arisen, O you of immense strength! Please destroy them and save us now! That is the reason why you have been woken up!
6.62.19
सर्वक्षपितकोशं च
स त्वमभ्यवपद्य माम् ।
त्रायस्वेमां पुरीं लङ्काम्
बालवृद्धावशेषिताम् ॥
Please rescue me, whose treasury is left with nothing, and save this city of Laṅkā, which is left with nothing but the children and the aged.
6.62.20
भ्रातुरर्थे महाबाहो
कुरु कर्म सुदुष्करम् ।
मयैवं नोक्तपूर्वो हि
कच्चिद् भ्रातः परन्तप ॥
For the sake of your brother, O you of mighty arm, you must do this, however impossible it is. I have never before begged anybody like this, my brother, O scorcher of the foe!
6.62.21
त्वय्यस्ति तु मम स्नेहः
परा सम्भावना च मे ।
दैवासुरेषु युद्धेषु
बहुशो राक्षसर्षभ ।
त्वया देवाः प्रतिव्यूह्य
निर्जिताश्चासुरा युधि ॥
I have great love and utmost respect for you. O bull among Rākshasas, you have stood up to Dēvas and Asuras and vanquished them many a time when fights broke out between them.
6.62.22
तदेतत् सर्वमातिष्ठ
वीर्यं भीमपराक्रम ।
न हि ते सर्वभूतेषु
दृश्यते सदृशो बली ॥
O you of fierce prowess, you shall now show all your valor. There is no one equal to you in strength among all creatures.
6.62.23
कुरुष्व मे प्रियहितमेतदुत्तमम्
यथाप्रियं प्रियरणबान्धवप्रिय ।
स्वतेजसा विधम सपत्नवाहिनीम्
शरद्घनं पवन इवोद्यतो महान् ॥
Please do this, over which there is nothing that would please me more or do me more good! O you, who love a fight and your people, do this as you would please! Destroy the enemy forces by your power as a great wind scatters the post monsoon clouds.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्विषष्टितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty second Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
