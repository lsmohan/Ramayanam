Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 108
In this Sarga, Mātali reminds Rāma of the Brahma's Astra that was given to him by Agastya. Rāma then takes out that Astra from his quiver and shoots, aiming at the chest of Rāvaṇa. That Astra, in which was packed the power of all creatures, rends the heart of Rāvaṇa and he tumbles down to the ground from the chariot, dead.
The Vānaras shout in joy and Rākshasas flee to Laṅkā, chased by the Vānaras. The sky becomes clear, the sun shines bright, the cardinals become pleasant and Dēvas feel relaxed. Sugreeva, Vibheeshaṇa, Aṅgada, Lakshmaṇa and others celebrate and pay honors to Rāma.
6.108.1
अथ संस्मारयामास
राघवं मातलिस्तदा ॥
Then, Mātali reminded Rāghava:
6.108.1
अजानन्निव किं वीर
त्वमेनमनुवर्तसे ।
विसृजास्मै वधाय त्वम्
अस्त्रं पैतामहं प्रभो ।
विनाशकालः कथितो
यः सुरैः सोऽद्य वर्तत ॥
Why do you, O valiant one, continue with this course, as if you do not know anything better? Discharge Grandsire's Astra, my lord; the day of his doom that the Dēvas have decreed has arrived!
6.108.3
ततः संस्मारितो रामः
तेन वाक्येन मातलेः ।
जग्राह सशरं दीप्तम्
निश्वसन्तमिवोरगम् ।
यमस्मै प्रथमं प्रादात्
आगस्त्यो भगवानृषिः ।
ब्रह्मदत्तं महाबाणम्
अमोघं युधि वीर्यवान् ॥
Thus reminded by Mātali, the valiant Rāma took out a blazing arrow that hissed like a serpent, that never failed in a fight, that was given to him by the Ṛshi, Bhagawān Agastya which, in turn, was given to him by Brahma.
6.108.5
ब्रह्मणा निर्मितं पूर्वम्
इन्द्रार्थममितौजसा ।
दत्तं सुरपतेः पूर्वम्
त्रिलोकजयकाङ्क्षिणः ॥
It was fashioned by Brahma of limitless power for the sake of Indra, who wished to conquer all the three worlds, and was given to that lord of Suras, once upon a time.
6.108.6
यस्य वाजेषु पवनः
फले पावकभास्करौ ।
शरीरमाकाशमयम्
गौरवे मेरुमन्दरौ ॥
In its wings was the wind, in its tip were the sun and the fire, in its shaft was the vastness of the sky, and in its weight, it resembled the Mēru and the Mandara mountains.
6.108.7
जाज्वल्यमानं वपुषा
सुपुङ्खं हेमभूषितम् ।
तेजसा सर्वभूतानाम्
कृतं भास्करवर्चसम् ॥
Decked in gold, with excellent rings, and its body blazing, it shone with the luster of the sun and was packed with the power of all the creatures.
6.108.8
विधूममिव कालाग्निम्
दीप्तमाशीविषं यथा ।
परनागाश्ववृन्दानाम्
भेदनं क्षिप्रकारिणम् ॥
It resembled a venomous serpent and dazzled like the smokeless fire of Dissolution. Acting quickly, it could smash the hosts of elephants and horses of the foe.
6.108.9
द्वाराणां परिघाणां च
गिरीणामपि भेदनम् ।
नानारुधिरसिक्ताङ्गम्
मेदोदिग्धं सुदारुणम् ॥
It could smash the gateways, their Parighas and even mountains. That dreaded one was bedaubed in blood and was smeared with the fat (of the foe).
6.108.10
वज्रसारं महानादम्
नानासमितिदारणम् ।
सर्ववित्रासनं भीमम्
श्वसन्तमिव पन्नगम् ॥
Hard as the Vajra, it cut and tore through many an army with a terrible din. Frightful in its appearance and hissing like a snake, it was a terror for everyone.
6.108.11
कङ्कगृध्रबकानां च
गोमायुगणरक्षसाम् ।
नित्यं भक्ष्यप्रदं युद्धे
यमरूपं भयावहम् ॥
It was terrifying, and was nothing but Death itself that unfailingly provided food on battlefields for vultures, eagles, cranes, and packs of jackals and for Rākshasas.
6.108.12
नन्दनं वानरेन्द्राणाम्
रक्षसामवसादनम् ।
वाजितं विविधैर्वाजैः
चारुचित्रैर्गरुत्मतः ॥
Sped by the beautiful and multicolored eagle feathers, it was to bring joy to the Vānaras and destruction to the Rākshasas.
6.108.13
तमुत्तमेषुं लोकानाम्
इक्ष्वाकुभयनाशनम् ।
द्विषतां कीर्तिहरणम्
प्रहर्षकरमात्मनः ।
अभिमन्त्र्य ततो रामः
तं महेषुं महाबलः ।
वेदप्रोक्तेन विधिना
सन्दधे कार्मुके बली ॥
The powerful Rāma, of immense strength, chanted the appropriate Mantras, and fitted to his bow in a manner prescribed in the Vēdas, that great arrow, the best in all the worlds, that would bring joy to himself, and destroy the fears of Ikshwākus and the fame of the enemies.
6.108.15
तस्मिन् सन्धीयमाने तु
राघवेण शरोत्तमे ।
सर्वभूतानि वित्रेसुः
चचाल च वसुन्धरा ॥
All creatures were terrified and the earth shook as that arrow was fitted on to the bow by Rāghava.
6.108.16
स रावणाय सङ्क्रुद्धो
भृशमायम्य कार्मुकम् ।
चिक्षेप परमायत्तः
शरं मर्मविदारणम् ॥
Drawing the bow to its full and employing all his force in wrath, he shot that arrow that would tear the vitals apart, aimed at Rāvaṇa.
6.108.17
स वज्र इव दुर्धर्षो
वज्रिबाहुविसर्जितः ।
कृतान्त इव चावार्यो
न्यपतद्रावणोरसि ॥
It fell on Rāvaṇa’s chest, unstoppable like Death itself, and inviolable like the Vajra discharged by the hand of (Indra), its Wielder.
6.108.18
स विसृष्टो महावेगः
शरीरान्तकरः शरः ।
बिभेद हृदयं तस्य
रावणस्य दुरात्मनः ॥
Shot thus, that extremely fast, death-dealing arrow, rent the heart of the evil-minded Rāvaṇa.
6.108.19
रुधिराक्तः स वेगेन
जीवितान्तकरः शरः ।
रावणस्य हरन् प्राणान्
विवेश धरणीतलम् ॥
Daubed with blood, that arrow which could take lives, did take the life of Rāvaṇa with its impact, before burying itself in the earth.
6.108.20
स शरो रावणं हत्वा
रुधिरार्द्रीकृतच्छविः ।
कृतकर्मा निभृतवत्
स्वतूणीं पुनरागमत् ॥
Having slain Rāvaṇa, the arrow, drenched with blood and shining, quietly returned to its quiver, its work done.
6.108.21
तस्य हस्ताद्धतस्याशु
कार्मुकं तत् ससायकम् ।
निपपात सह प्राणैः
भ्रश्यमानस्य जीवितात् ॥
The bow and the arrow fell from his hand quickly, along with his life force, as he was thus struck down, and his life became a thing of the past.
6.108.22
गतासुर्भीमवेगस्तु
नैर्ऋतेन्द्रो महाद्युतिः ।
पपात स्यन्दनाद्भूमौ
वृत्रो वज्रहतो यथा ॥
Having lost his life, the ruler of the Rākshasas, of formidable force and extraordinary splendor, tumbled down to the ground from the chariot, like Vṛtra struck down by the Vajra.
6.108.23
तं दृष्ट्वा पतितं भूमौ
हतशेषा निशाचराः ।
हतनाथा भयत्रस्ताः
सर्वतः सम्प्रदुद्रुवुः ॥
Seeing him fallen on the ground, the Night-rovers that were left alive fled in all directions in terror, having lost their master.
6.108.24
नर्दन्तश्चाभिपेतुस्तान्
वानरा द्रुमयोधिनः ।
दशग्रीववधं दृष्ट्वा
विजयं राघवस्य च ॥
Having seen the Ten-necked slain and Rāghava victorious, the Vānaras who fought with trees for their weapons pursued them with strident shouts.
6.108.25
अर्दिता वानरैर्हृष्टैः
लङ्कामभ्यपतन् भयात् ।
गताश्रयत्वात् करुणैः
बाष्पप्रस्रवणैर्मुखैः ॥
Feeling miserable at having lost their protector, their faces streaming with tears, they fled to Laṅkā in terror, chased by the rejoicing Vānaras.
6.108.26
ततो विनेदुः संहृष्टा
वानरा जितकाशिनः ।
वदन्तो राघवजयम्
रावणस्य च तद्वधम् ॥
Thereupon the Vānaras, basking in the glory of victory, let off shouts in extreme delight, proclaiming Rāghava’s victory and Rāvaṇa’s death.
6.108.27
अथान्तरिक्षे व्यनदत्
सौम्यस्त्रिदशदुन्दुभिः ।
दिव्यगन्धवहस्तत्र
मारुतः ससुखो ववौ ॥
There was heard in the heavens celebratory beatings of the kettledrums of the Dēvas. There blew a pleasant breeze, wafting divinely pleasant scents.
6.108.28
निपपातान्तरिक्षाच्च
पुष्पवृष्टिस्तदा भुवि ।
किरन्ती राघवरथम्
दुरवापा मनोरमा ॥
From the sky fell a shower of flowers on the chariot of Rāghava, a rare spectacle that captivated one's heart.
6.108.29
राघवस्तवसंयुक्ता
गगने च विशुश्रुवे ।
साधु साध्विति वागग्र्या
दैवतानां महात्मनाम् ॥
From the sky were heard coveted chantings by Dēvas, the Mahātmas, praising Rāghava and exclaiming, ‘Well done! Well done!’
6.108.30
आविवेश महाहर्षो
देवानां चारणैः सह ।
रावणे निहते रौद्रे
सर्वलोकभयङ्करे ॥
With the fierce Rāvaṇa, a terror of all the worlds, slain, Dēvas, along with the Cāraṇas, went into a fit of extraordinary joy.
6.108.31
ततः सकामं सुग्रीवम्
अङ्गदं च महाबलम् ।
चकार राघवः प्रीतो
हत्वा राक्षसपुङ्गवम् ॥
Having thus slain the bull among Rākshasas, Rāghava was pleased to have the objective of Sugreeva and Aṅgada of immense strength fulfilled.
6.108.32
ततः प्रजग्मुः प्रशमं मरुद्गणा
दिशः प्रसेदुर्विमलं नभो ऽबवत् ।
मही चकम्पे न हि मारुतो ववौ
स्थिरप्रभश्चाप्यभवद्दिवाकरः ॥
The hosts of Maruts felt relaxed, the four quarters became pleasant, the sky became clear, the earth quaked no more, the wind blew pleasantly, and the sun shone with a steady luster.
6.108.33
ततस्तु सुग्रीवविभीषणाङ्गदाः
सुहृद्विशेषाः सहलक्ष्मणास्तदा ।
समेत्य हृष्टा विजयेन राघवं
रणेऽभिरामं विधिना ह्यपूजयन् ॥
Sugreeva, Vibheeshaṇa and Aṅgada, along with important friends and with Lakshmaṇa, came together on that battlefield rejoicing over the victory of Rāghava, and paid honors to the charming Rāma in a customary manner.
6.108.34
स तु निहतरिपुः स्थिरप्रतिज्ञः
स्वजनबलाभिवृतो रणे रराज ।
रघुकुलनृपनन्दनो महौजाः
त्रिदशगणैरभिसंवृतो यथेन्द्रः ॥
Having slain the foe and made good his word, the delight of the kings of the Raghu lineage shone on the battlefield, with all his people and the forces about him, like Indra with the companies of the Dēvas about him.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे अष्टोत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and eighth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
