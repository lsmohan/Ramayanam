Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 12
In this Sarga, Daṡaratha, the grief stricken King, responds to Kaikēyee's unreasonable, fear driven, misguided demand of the two boons of crowning Bharata and sending Rāma to Vana.
He laments. He pleads. He curses. He explains the repercussions for Rāma, Bharata, himself, herself, Kousalyā, Seetā, the Ikshwāku dynasty and all others. He speaks of his inability to comply. He pleads for mercy.
Ramayana is called a Kāvya not without a reason. It is moving scenes like these, of a perfectly sane person driven completely insane for no fault of his own, finding no source of comfort, that dot throughout its course that justify the glory this poem receives in the literary circles and in popular mind.
Hit by the bolt of lightning, of the boons asked by Kaikēyee, Daṡaratha goes through bouts of grief swinging from consciousness to unconsciousness and back, speaking half logically and half emotionally.
He responds to Kaikēyee as a husband who has been wronged, as a king whose dignity has been put at stake, as a father whose beloved son will be deprived of minimum comforts that even a common man in a rural place enjoys, and as a destitute who finds no recourse whatsoever out of the predicament except seeing himself die.
It is for no simple reason that this Sarga has moved the hearts of people for millennia!
2.12.1
ततश्शृत्वा महाराजः
कैकेय्या दारुणं वचः ।
चिन्तामभिसमापेदे
मुहूर्तं प्रतताप च ॥
On hearing those terrible words of Kaikēyee, the great King became engulfed in grief and felt stupefied for a moment.
2.12.2
किन्नु मे यदि वा स्वप्नः
चित्तमोहोऽपि वा मम ।
अनुभूतोपसर्गो वा
मनसो वाप्युपद्रवः ॥
Am I dreaming or under a delusion? Is my faculty of experience failing me? Is my mind severely impaired?
2.12.3
इति सञ्चिन्त्य तद्राजा
नाध्यगच्छत्तदासुखम् ॥
Thinking thus, the king could find no comfort.
2.12.3
प्रतिलभ्य चिरात्संज्ञाम्
कैकेयीवाक्यताडितः ।
व्यथितो विक्लबश्चैव
व्याघ्रीं दृष्ट्वा यथा मृगः ।
असंवृतायामासीनो
जगत्यां दीर्घमुच्छ्वसन् ।
मण्डले पन्नगो रुद्धो
मन्त्रैरिव महाविषः ।
अहो धिगिति सामर्षो
वाचमुक्त्वा नराधिपः ।
मोहमापेदिवान्भूय
श्शोकोपहतचेतनः ॥
It took a while for him to regain consciousness, after thus being lashed by the words of Kaikēyee. He was troubled and fazed like a deer that had caught sight of a tigress.
Collapsed on the bare floor, he heaved a long sigh like a virulent viper that was charmed into submission by Mantras! With his senses impaired by tides of grief, saying "Wretchedness!", he fell into a bout of delusion again!
2.12.6
चिरेण तु नृप स्संज्ञाम्
प्रतिलभ्य सुदुःखितः ।
कैकेयीमब्रवीत्क्रुद्धः
प्रदहन्निव चक्षुषा ॥
Regaining his senses after a while, he grieved immensely and spoke to Kaikēyee with such anger that he would burn her with his eyes:
2.12.7
नृशंसे दुष्टचारित्रे
कुलस्यास्य विनाशिनि ।
किं कृतं तव रामेण
पापं पापे मयापि वा ॥
Cruel and wicked woman! You have come to destroy this family! O evil woman! What harm did Rāma or I do to you!
2.12.8
यदा ते जननीतुल्याम्
वृत्तिं वहति राघवः ।
तस्यैव त्वमनर्थाय
किंनिमित्तमिहोद्यता ॥
What reason did you find to plan for Rāghava's destruction, who holds you in regard equal only to his mother!
2.12.9
त्वं मयाऽऽत्मविनाशार्थम्
भवनं स्वं प्रवेशिता ।
अविज्ञानान्नृपसुता
व्याली तीक्ष्णविषा यथा ॥
I brought you into this home thinking that you were a daughter of a king. Little did I know that I was bringing a highly venomous female viper for my own destruction!
2.12.10
जीवलोको यदा सर्वो
रामस्याह गुणस्तवम् ।
अपराधं कमुद्दिश्य
त्यक्ष्यामीष्टमहं सुतम् ॥
When all living beings of this world are singing praises of Rāma, what fault would I find in my beloved son to abandon him?
2.12.11
कौसल्यां वा सुमित्रां वा
त्यजेयमपि वा श्रियम् ।
जीवितं वाऽत्मनो रामम्
न त्वेव पितृवत्सलम् ॥
I might be able forgo Kousalyā or Sumitrā or all of this wealth or even my life! But I cannot let Rāma, who is devoted to me, his father, go!
2.12.12
परा भवति मे प्रीतिः
दृष्ट्वा तनयमग्रजम् ।
अपश्यतस्तु मे रामम्
नष्टा भवति चेतना ॥
I gain immense joy seeing the oldest of my sons. I feel lifeless if I do not see Rāma!
2.12.13
तिष्ठेल्लोको विना सूर्यम्
सस्यं वा सलिलं विना ।
न तु रामं विना देहे
तिष्ठेत्तु मम जीवितम् ॥
The world might do without the sun and the crops might, without water, but my life cannot stay in this body without Rāma!
2.12.14
तदलं त्यज्यतामेष
निश्चयः पापनिश्चये ।
अपि ते चरणै मूर्ध्ना
स्पृशाम्येष प्रसीद मे ॥
Hence, O evil-intentioned woman, enough of this, give up your intention! I shall touch your feet with my head. Have mercy on me!
2.12.15
किमिदिं चिन्तितं पापे
त्वया परमदारुणम् ।
अथ जिज्ञाससे मां त्वम्
भरतस्य प्रियाप्रिये ।
अस्तु यत्तत्त्वया पूर्वम्
व्याहृतं राघवं प्रति ।
स मे ज्येष्ठस्सुत श्रीमान्
धर्मज्येष्ठ इतीव मे ।
तत्त्वया प्रियवादिन्या
सेवार्थं कथितं भवेत् ॥
How could you think of such terrible things, o evil one! I hope you just mean to test my feelings towards Bharata!
Your utterance regarding Rāghava in the past that "This blessed one is like my dutiful eldest son!" must only have been made to keep me in good cheer!
2.12.18
तच्छ्रुत्वा शोकसन्तप्ता
सन्तापयसि मां भृशम् ।
आविष्टाऽसि गृहं शून्यम्
सा त्वं परवशं गता ॥
That is why you are burnt by grief hearing about it (Rāma's coronation) and are putting me in acute distress. You even entered this desolate place (anger chamber), influenced by others.
2.12.19
इक्ष्वाकूणां कुले देवि
सम्प्राप्तस्सुमहानयम् ।
अनयो नयसम्पन्ने
यत्र ते विकृता मतिः ॥
O Queen! You have been nothing but noble, thus far! But with this perversion in your mind, a great ignobility has befallen on the race of Ikshwākus!
2.12.20
नहि किञ्चिदयुक्तं वा
विप्रियं वा पुरा मम ।
अकरोस्त्वं विशालाक्षि
तेन न श्रद्दधाम्यहम् ॥
O wide-eyed one! You have never done anything that is inappropriate or that is against me! For that reason, I am unable to believe (what I am hearing)!
2.12.21
ननु ते राघवस्तुल्यो
भरतेन महात्मना ।
बहुशो हि सुबाले त्वम्
कथाः कथयसे मम ॥
O dear! Did you not say, in many ways, that Rāma is equal to Mahātma Bharata!
2.12.22
तस्य धर्मात्मनो देवि
वनवासं यशस्विनः ।
कथं रोचयसे भीरु
नव वर्षाणि पञ्च च ॥
O timorous one! How can you relish the thought of that renowned Dharmātma living in the Vana for nine years and five?
2.12.23
अत्यन्तसुकुमारस्य
तस्य धर्मे धृतात्मनः ।
कथं रोचयसे वासम्
अरण्ये भृशदारुणे ॥
How can you relish the thought of that tender Rāma, the staunch adherent of Dharma, living in the dreaded forest!
2.12.24
रोचयस्यभिरामस्य
रामस्य शुभलोचने ।
तव शुश्रूषमाणस्य
किमर्थं विप्रवासनम् ॥
O auspicious-eyed one! Why do you like to see Rāma, the darling of everyone, who is at your service always, in exile?
2.12.25
रामो हि भरताद्भूयः
तव शुश्रूषते सदा ।
विशेषं त्वयि तस्मात्तु
भरतस्य न लक्षये ॥
Also, Rāma serves you even more than Bharata does. Therefore, I fail to understand why you are so specific about Bharata!
2.12.26
शुश्रूषां गौरवं चैव
प्रमाणं वचनक्रियाम् ।
कस्ते भूयस्तरं कुर्यात्
अन्यत्र मनुजर्षभात् ।
बहूनां स्त्रीसहस्राणाम्
बहूनां चोपजीविनाम् ॥
Who, other than that bull among men, among the countless servants and the thousands of women, serves your wishes more sincerely and with utmost regard and respect?
2.12.27
परिवादोऽपवादो वा
राघवे नोपपद्यते ।
सान्त्वयन्सर्वभूतानि
रामश्शुद्धेन चेतसा ।
गृह्णाति मनुजव्याघ्र
प्रियैर्विषयवासिनः ॥
Rāghava can never be found fault with for omissions or commissions. That tiger among men makes friends of all beings by virtue of his pure heart, and gains the trust of all the people of the country by virtue of his endearing deeds.
2.12.29
सत्येन लोकान् जयति
दीनान् दानेन राघवः ।
गुरूञ्छुश्रूषया वीरो
धनुषा युधि शात्रवान् ॥
Rāghava wins over the world by his adherence to truth, the destitute by largesse, the Gurus by service, and that Veera wins over his foes in a battle with his bow.
2.12.30
सत्यं दानं तपस्त्यागो
मित्रता शौचमार्जवम् ।
विद्या च गुरुशुश्रूषा
ध्रुवाण्येतानि राघवे ॥
Truth, largesse, discipline, self-denial, friendliness, purity, uprightness, scholarship and service to the Gurus are firmly established in Rāghava.
2.12.31
तस्मिन्नार्जवसम्पन्ने
देवि देवोपमे कथम् ।
पापमाशंससे रामे
महर्षिसमतेजसि ॥
O queen! How can you think of evil for such Rāma, who is so upright, comparable to Dēvas and is radiant like Maharshis?
2.12.32
न स्मराम्यप्रियं वाक्यम्
लोकस्य प्रियवादिनः ।
स कथं त्वत्कृते रामम्
वक्ष्यामि प्रियमप्रियम् ॥
I cannot remember having ever heard a single unkind word from him who always speaks kindly to all beings. How then can I, for your sake, say unkind words to Rāma, who is dear to me?
2.12.33
क्षमा यस्मिन्दमस्त्याग
सत्यं धर्मः कृतज्ञता ।
अप्यहिंसा च भूतानाम्
तमृते का गतिर्मम ॥
What refuge have I but him, in whom are ever to be found forbearance, self-control, self-denial, truth, Dharma, gratitude, and above all, compassion towards all living beings?
2.12.34
मम वृद्धस्य कैकेयि
गतान्तस्य तपस्विनः ।
दीनं लालप्यमानस्य
कारुण्यं कर्तुमर्हसि ॥
I am old, Kaikeyi, and nearing my end; I am pitifully wailing and wretched; it behooves you to show mercy on me.
2.12.35
पृथिव्यां सागरान्तायाम्
यत्किञ्चिदधिगम्यते ।
तत्सर्वं तव दास्यामि
मा च त्वां मन्युराविशेत् ॥
I will give you everything that can be found anywhere on this earth that spreads to the oceans; only do not be angry with me.
2.12.36
अञ्जलिं कुर्मि कैकेयि
पादौ चापि स्पृशामि ते ।
शरणं भव रामस्य
माऽधर्मो मामिह स्पृशेत् ॥
I beseech you, O Kaikēyee, with my palms joined in supplication and I shall even touch your feet! May you be Rāma's refuge and may I be saved from the imputation of Adharma!
2.12.37
इति दुःखाभिसन्तप्तम्
विलपन्तमचेतनम् ।
घूर्णमानं महाराजम्
शोकेन समभिप्लुतम् ।
पारं शोकार्णवस्याशु
प्रार्थयन्तं पुनः पुनः ।
प्रत्युवाचाथ कैकेयी
रौद्रा रौद्रतरं वचः ॥
Then the indignant Kaikēyee spoke angrily to the great King who is lamenting in an agony of grief, driven out of his senses and writhing on the ground, swamped by a sea of sorrow and repeatedly crying for rescue!
2.12.39
यदि दत्त्वा वरौ राजन्
पुनः प्रत्यनुतप्यसे ।
धार्मिकत्वं कथं वीर
पृथिव्यां कथयिष्यसि ॥
O King! If you regret the boons you gave, how can you stand up to the world, O Veera, and defend your devotion to Dharma!
2.12.40
यदा समेता बहवः
त्वया राजर्षय स्सह ।
कथयिष्यन्ति धर्मज्ञ
तत्र किं प्रतिवक्ष्यसि ॥
O savant of Dharma! What will you tell when this comes up in the meeting of many Rājarshis?
2.12.41
यस्याः प्रसादे जीवामि
या च मामभ्यपालयत् ।
तस्याः कृतम् मया मिथ्या
कैकेय्या इति वक्ष्यसि ॥
Will you tell, "My words to Kaikēyee, who saved me and because of whom I am alive, are glib talk!"?
2.12.42
किल्बिषं त्वं नरेन्द्राणाम्
करिष्यसि नराधिप ।
यो दत्वा वरमद्यैव
पुनरन्यानि भाषसे ॥
O ruler of the lands, you bring disgrace to the dignity of all rulers of the peoples, by reneging on the boons that you gave!
2.12.43
शैब्यश्श्येनकपोतीये
स्वमांसं पक्षिणे ददौ ।
अलर्कश्चक्षुषी दत्वा
जगाम गतिमुत्तमाम् ॥
King Ṡaibya gave his own flesh to the hawk to save the pigeon from it. King Alarka gave his eyes and attained higher worlds.
Ṡaibya's story:
Once upon a time, Indra and Agni wanted to test the generous repute of King Ṡaibya.
Indra took the form of a hawk and Agni took the form of a pigeon. The hawk chased the pigeon, its prey. The pigeon flew to King Ṡaibya and sought refuge. King Ṡaibya gave his own flesh, equal to the weight of the pigeon, to save it from being a meal to the hawk!
Note: Ṡaibya is also known as Ṡibi.
Alarka's story:
A blind Brāhmaṇa approached King Alarka and asked for his eyes, in order to regain his eye sight. King Alarka, without a second thought, donated his eyes immediately to that Brāhmaṇa.
2.12.44
सागरस्समयं कृत्वा
न वेलामतिवर्तते ।
समयं माऽनृतं कार्षीः
पूर्ववृत्तमनुस्मरन् ॥
Never did the ocean violate its word and cross the shore. I hope your words do not go in vain, keeping those examples of the past in mind.
2.12.45
स त्वं धर्मं परित्यज्य
रामं राज्येऽभिषिच्य च ।
सह कौसल्यया नित्यम्
रन्तुमिच्छसि दुर्मते ॥
You want to forgo your Dharma by consecrating Rāma and copulate with Kousalyā forever, O evil mind!
2.12.46
भवत्वधर्मो धर्मो वा
सत्यं वा यदि वाऽनृतम् ।
यत्त्वया संश्रुतं मह्यम्
तस्य नास्ति व्यतिक्रमः ॥
May it be right or wrong, true or false, you shall not deviate from your promise to me!
2.12.47
अहं हि विषमद्यैव
पीत्वा बहु तवाग्रतः ।
पश्यतस्ते मरिष्यामि
रामो यद्यभिषिच्यते ॥
I will drink poison right now in front of you, and will die while you are watching, if Rāma is to be consecrated!
2.12.48
एकाहमपि पश्येयम्
यद्यहं राममातरम् ।
अञ्जलिं प्रतिगृह्णन्तीम्
श्रेयो ननु मृतिर्मम ॥
It is better for me to die rather than witness even for a day, Rāma's mother receiving my homage with my palms joined in supplication!
2.12.49
भरतेनात्मना चाहम्
शपे ते मनुजाधिप ।
यथा नान्येन तुष्येयम्
ऋते रामविवासनात् ॥
I swear on my Bharata and my own life, O lord of the people, that nothing will satisfy me, short of Rāma's banishment!
2.12.50
एतावदुक्त्वा वचनम्
कैकेयी विरराम ह ।
विलपन्तं च राजानम्
न प्रतिव्याजहार सा ॥
Kaikēyee finished what she had to say with those words, without responding to the King's lamentations in any manner.
2.12.51
श्रुत्वा तु राजा कैकेय्या
वृतं वरमशोभनम् ।
रामस्य च वने वासम्
चश्वर्यं भरतस्य च ।
नाभ्यभाषत कैकेयीम्
मुहूर्तं व्याकुलेन्द्रियः ॥
Hearing those utterly disgusting demands of Kaikēyee, of Rāma's exile to the Vana and endowments to Bharata, the King could not speak for a moment, with his senses in utter turmoil.
2.12.52
प्रैक्षतानिमिषो देवीम्
प्रियामप्रियवादिनीम् ।
तां हि वज्रसमां वाचम्
आकर्ण्य हृदयाप्रियाम् ।
दुःखशोकमयीं घोराम्
राजा न सुखितोऽभवत् ॥
He could do nothing but stare at his dear wife who was speaking such unkind words. The king could hardly find anything to comfort himself having heard those terrible, unkind and gut-wrenching words, causing anguish and distress that struck him like a bolt of thunder!
2.12.54
स देव्या व्यवसायं च
घोरं च शपथं कृतम् ।
ध्यात्वा रामेति निश्श्वस्य
छिन्नस्तरुरिवापतत् ॥
Thinking of her determination and the terrible oath she has taken, he fell down like a severed tree heaving deep sighs and crying 'Rāma!'
2.12.55
नष्टचित्तो यथोन्मत्तो
विपरीतो यथाऽतुरः ।
हृततेजा यथा सर्पो
बभूव जगतीपतिः ॥
That ruler of the world lost his mind like a madman, behaved crazily like an extremely sick person, and appeared like an enervated serpent!
2.12.56
दीनया तु गिरा राजा
इति होवाच कैकयीम् ॥
Then the King pleaded again with Kaikēyee pitifully:
2.12.56
अनर्थमिममर्थाभम्
केन त्वमुपदर्शिता ।
भूतोपहतचित्तेव
ब्रुवन्ती मां न लज्जसे ॥
Who convinced you that this disastrous idea is beneficial for you? Are you not ashamed of speaking like this to me as if possessed by evil spirits!
2.12.57
शीलव्यसनमेतत्ते
नाभिजानाम्यहं पुरा ।
बालायास्तत्त्विदानीं ते
लक्षये विपरीतवत् ॥
I did not notice your faulty character when you were young; but now I see how preposterous it is!
2.12.58
कुतो वा ते भयं जातम्
या त्वमेवंविधं वरम् ।
राष्ट्रे भरतमासीनम्
वृणीषे राघवं वने ॥
Who are you afraid of that you are asking for such boons, of installing Bharata in the kingdom and consigning Rāma to the Vana!
2.12.59
विरमैतेन भावेन
त्वमेतेनानृतेन वा ।
यदि भर्तुः प्रियं कार्यम्
लोकस्य भरतस्य च ॥
If you are interested in the wellbeing of your husband, Bharata and the world, you must give up these ideas and meaningless thoughts!
2.12.60
नृशंसे पापसङ्कल्पे
क्षुद्रे दुष्कृतकारिणि ।
किन्नु दुखमलीकं वा
मयि रामे च पश्यसि ॥
O cruel woman! O wickedly resolved! O mean soul! O evil monger! What offense or source of grief do you see in me or Rāma?
2.12.61
न कथञ्चिदृते रामात्
भरतो राज्यमावसेत् ।
रामादपि हि तं मन्ये
धर्मतो बलवत्तरम् ॥
Bharata will in no way occupy the kingdom, depriving Rāma of it! I think that Bharata, even more than Rāma, is a stronger adherent of Dharma!
2.12.62
कथं द्रक्ष्यामि रामस्य
वनं गच्छेति भाषिते ।
मुखवर्णं विवर्णं तम्
यथैवेन्दुमुपप्लुतम् ॥
How can I see the discolored face of Rāma, that would look like the eclipsed moon, when he is told "Go to the Vana!"
2.12.63
तां हि मे सुकृतां बुद्धिम्
सुहृद्भिस्सह निश्चिताम् ।
कथं द्रक्ष्याम्यपावृत्ताम्
परैरिव हतां चमूम् ॥
How can I see the well considered decision made in consultation with all the well wishers be whacked this way, like an army hacked by the enemy?
2.12.64
किं मां वक्ष्यन्ति राजानो
नानादिग्भ्य स्समागताः ।
बालो बताऽयमैक्ष्वाकः
चिरं राज्यमकारयत् ॥
What would the kings that arrived here from the many directions say about me? "How did this stupid descendant of Ikshwāku manage to reign for so long?"
2.12.65
यदा तु बहवो वृद्धा
गुणवन्तो बहुश्रुताः ।
परिप्रक्ष्यन्ति काकुत्स्थम्
वक्ष्यामि किमहं तदा ॥
What can I say when the many learned and virtuous elders question me about Kākutstha?
2.12.66
कैकेय्या क्लिश्यमानेन
रामः प्रव्राजितो मया ।
यदि सत्यं ब्रवीम्येतत्
तदसत्यं भविष्यति ॥
It will be taken as a lie even if I tell the truth that I have sent Rāma away because of the torture from Kaikēyee!
2.12.67
किं मां वक्ष्यति कौसल्या
राघवे वनमास्थिते ।
किं चैनां प्रतिवक्ष्यामि
कृत्वा विप्रियमीदृशम् ॥
What will Kousalyā say to me, if Rāghava is sent to the Vana? And what could I reply to her, having caused such havoc?
2.12.68
यदा यदा हि कौसल्या
दासीवच्च सखीव च ।
भार्यावद्भगिनीवच्च
मातृवच्चोपतिष्ठति ।
सततं प्रियकामा मे
प्रियपुत्रा प्रियंवदा ।
न मया सत्कृता देवी
सत्कारार्हा कृते तव ॥
Kousalyā serves me like an attendant, a friend, a wife, a sister and a mother, as and when needed.
That sweet-spoken mother of an endearing son, always wishes the best for me! But, I could never treat her the way she deserved, for fear of displeasing you!
2.12.70
इदानीं तत्तपति माम्
यन्मया सुकृतं त्वयि ।
अपथ्यव्यञ्जनोपेतम्
भुक्तमन्नमिवातुरम् ॥
All the good deeds I have done so far for you are hurting me like food with forbidden sauces would hurt a sick man.
2.12.71
विप्रकारं च रामस्य
सम्प्रयाणं वनस्य च ।
सुमित्रा प्रेक्ष्य वै भीता
कथं मे विश्वसिष्यति ॥
Seeing the ill-treatment meted out to Rāma and his journey to the Vana, Sumitrā will be scared. And how would she be able to trust me any more?
2.12.72
कृपणं बत वैदेही
श्रोष्यति द्वयमप्रियम् ।
मां च पञ्चत्वमापन्नम्
रामं च वनमाश्रितम् ॥
Alas! The princess of Vidēha would have to pitifully hear the two shocking news of Rāma being banished to the Vana and me finding my way to the afterworld!
2.12.73
वैदेही बत मे प्राणान्
शोचन्ती क्षपयिष्यति ।
हीना हिमवतः पार्श्वे
किन्नरेणेव किन्नरी ॥
How can I hold on to life, hearing the pitiful cries of the princess of Vidēha, like those of a Kinnara woman who lost her husband on the slopes of Himalayas!
2.12.74
न हि राममहं दृष्ट्वा
प्रवसन्तं महावने ।
चिरं जीवितुमाशंसे
रुदन्तीं चापि मैथिलीम् ॥
I will certainly not live for long seeing Rāma banished to the great forest and seeing the princess of Mithilā crying!
2.12.75
सा नूनं विधवा राज्यम्
सपुत्रा कारयिष्यसि ।
न हि प्रव्राजिते रामे
देवि जीवितुमुत्सहे ॥
I would certainly not like to live once Rāma leaves! O queen! You would certainly become a widow and rule the kingdom along with your son!
2.12.76
सतीं त्वामहमत्यन्तम्
व्यवस्याम्यसतीं सतीम् ।
रूपिणीं विषसंयुक्ताम्
पीत्वेव मदिरां नरः ॥
Like a man who drinks wine mixed with poison, I have mistaken you, the beautiful one, to be a good wife, while you, a wicked woman, are far from it.
2.12.77
अनृतैर्बहु मां सान्त्वै
स्सान्त्वयन्ती स्म भाषसे ।
गीतशब्देन संरुध्य
लुब्धो मृगमिवावधीः ॥
You have been keeping me comforted with many sweet lies, all along. And now you strike me like a hunter striking a deer that is engrossed in the melody of music.
2.12.78
अनार्य इति मामार्याः
पुत्रविक्रायकं ध्रुवम् ।
धिक्करिष्यन्ति रथ्यासु
सुरापं ब्राह्मणं यथा ॥
The nobles will certainly rebuke me in the chariot pathways as an ignoble, for selling my son, as they would censure a Brāhmaṇa for consuming liquor.
2.12.79
अहो दुःखमहो कृच्छ्रम्
यत्र वाचः क्षमे तव ।
दुःखमेवंविधं प्राप्तम्
पुराकृतमिवाशुभम् ॥
What misery and what a pity that I have to put up with these words of yours! Alas! Misery of this magnitude has befallen like retribution for the misdeeds of the past!
2.12.80
चिरं खलु मया पापे
त्वं पापेनाभिरक्षिता ।
अज्ञानादुपसम्पन्ना
रज्जुरुद्बन्धिनी यथा ॥
I, in my folly and out of my stupidity, have been taking care of you this long, but you have now become a noose around my neck!
2.12.81
रममाणस्त्वया सार्थम्
मृत्युं त्वां नाभिलक्षये ।
बालो रहसि हस्तेन
कृष्णसर्पमिवास्पृशम् ॥
Indulged in having fun with you, I have not realized that you are death incarnate! I have been like a child touching a venomous black serpent while no one happens to watch over!
2.12.82
मया ह्यपितृकः पुत्र
स्समहात्मा दुरात्मना ।
तं तु मां जीवलोकोऽयम्
नूनमाक्रोष्टुमर्हति ॥
I, a wretched one, would make no father for such Mahātma for a son! This world may justly denounce me as such!
2.12.83
बालिशो बत कामात्मा
राजा दशरथो भृशम् ।
यः स्त्रीकृते प्रियं पुत्रम्
वनं प्रस्थापयिष्यति ॥
Alas! How foolish and lustful can King Daṡaratha be to send away his beloved son to the Vana, for the sake of a woman!
2.12.84
व्रतैश्च ब्रह्मचर्यैश्च
गुरुभिश्चोपकर्शितः ।
भोगकाले महत्कृच्छ्रम्
पुनरेव प्रपत्स्यते ॥
Rāma has been deprived thus far, observing discipline, celibacy and serving Gurus. When the time has finally come for him to enjoy the pleasures of life, he has to now go through dire hardships again!
2.12.85
नालं द्वितीयं वचनम्
पुत्रो मां प्रतिभाषितुम् ।
स वनं प्रव्रजेत्युक्तो
बाढमित्येव वक्ष्यति ॥
My son would not say another word to me, except 'yes!', if I tell him to go to the Vana!
2.12.86
यदि मे राघवः कुर्यात्
वनं गच्छेति चोदितः ।
प्रतिकूलं प्रियं मे स्यात्
न तु वत्सः करिष्यति ॥
I would rather be happy if Rāghava does not obey my word, when I tell him to go to the Vana! But my dear son would never do that!
2.12.87
शुद्धभावो हि भावं मे
न तु ज्ञास्यति राघवः ।
स वनं प्रब्रजे त्युक्तो
बाढ मित्येव वक्ष्यति ॥
But being the pure hearted person that he is, Rāghava would not know my feelings and would only say 'yes!', when told to go to the Vana!
2.12.88
राघवे हि वनं प्राप्ते
सर्वलोकस्य धिक्कृतम् ।
मृत्युरक्षमणीयं माम्
नयिष्यति यमक्षयम् ॥
Once Rāma goes to the Vana, death will take me, the one despised by everybody and unfit for any reprieve, away to the world of Yama!
2.12.89
मृते मयि गते रामे
वनं मनुजपुङ्गवे ।
इष्टे मम जने शेषे
किं पापं प्रतिपत्स्यसे ॥
There is no telling what evil you would do to my remaining loved ones, once Rāma, the best among men is in the Vana and I am dead!
2.12.90
कौसल्या मां च रामं च
पुत्रौ च यदि हास्यति ।
दुःखान्यसहती देवी
मामेवानुमरिष्यति ॥
Should Kousalyā, the queen, lose me, Rāma and the other two sons, she would not be able to bear the sorrow and will die along with me!
2.12.91
कौसल्यां च सुमित्रां च
मां च पुत्रैस्त्रिभिस्सह ।
प्रक्षिप्य नरके सा त्वम्
कैकेयि सुखिता भव ॥
O! Kaikēyee, may you be happy, thus consigning Kousalyā, Sumitrā, me and the three sons to a hellish fate!
2.12.92
मया रामेण च त्यक्तम्
शाश्वतं सत्कृतं गुणैः ।
इक्ष्वाकुकुलमक्षोभ्यम्
आकुलं पालयिष्यसि ॥
You would certainly rule a shaken up Ikshwāku dynasty, which has been built to be unshakable and everlasting by virtue of many qualities, after I and Rāma give it up.
2.12.93
प्रियं चेद्भरतस्यैतत्
रामप्रव्राजनं भवेत् ।
मा स्म मे भरतः कार्षीत्
प्रेतकृत्यं गतायुषः ॥
Were Bharata to like the departure of Rāma to the Vana, may he not perform last rites for me when I die!
2.12.94
हन्तानार्ये ममामित्रे
सकामा भव कैकयि ।
मृते मयि गते रामे
वनं पुरुषपुङ्गवे ।
सेदानीं विधवा राज्यम्
सपुत्रा कारयिष्यसि ॥
O Kaikēyee! You are my enemy! You are ignoble! Alas! May your desire be fulfilled! With Rāma gone to the Vana and me leaving this world, rule the kingdom as a widow, along with your son!
2.12.96
त्वं राजपुत्रीवादेन
न्यवसो मम वेश्मनि ।
अकीर्तिश्चातुला लोके
ध्रुवः परिभवश्च मे ।
सर्वभूतेषु चावज्ञा
यथा पापकृतस्तथा ॥
You are living in my house, being the daughter of a King! I am certainly destined to ignominy and shame in this world! I would certainly be met with contempt, like anyone who commits wrongful acts !
2.12.97
कथं रथैर्विभुर्यात्वा
गजाश्वैश्च मुहुर्मुहुः ।
पद्भ्यां रामो महारण्ये
वत्सो मे विचरिष्यति ॥
How can Rāma, my beloved son, who always rides on chariots, elephants and horses, move around in the great forest on bare foot?
2.12.98
यस्य त्वाहारसमये
सूदाः कुण्डलधारिणः ।
अहंपूर्वाः पचन्ति स्म
प्रशस्तं पानभोजनम् ।
स कथन्नु कषायाणि
तिक्तानि कटुकानि च ।
भक्षयन्वन्यमाहारम्
सुतो मे वर्तयिष्यति ॥
My son eats food of exquisite cuisine, prepared by chefs well decorated with pendants on their ears, each vying to cook food for him ahead of others!
How can he manage by himself eating the astringent, bitter and pungent food of the Vana?
2.12.100
महार्हवस्त्रसंवीतो
भूत्वा चिरसुखोषितः ।
काषायपरिधानस्तु
कथं भूमौनिवत्स्यति ॥
How can he, who is used to wearing expensive clothing and to a comfortable life for a long time, live wearing saffron robes and lying on the bare floor?
2.12.101
कस्यैतद्धारुणं वाक्यम्
एवंविध मचिन्तितम् ।
रामस्यारण्यगमनम्
भरतस्याभिषेचनम् ॥
Whose is this kind of unthinkable and dreadful idea, of Rāma's departure to the Vana and Bharata's coronation?
2.12.102
धिगस्तु योषितो नाम
शठा स्स्वार्थपरास्सदा ।
न ब्रवीमि स्त्रिय स्सर्वा
भरतस्यैव मातरम् ॥
Shame! The very word 'woman' means nothing but perfidy and selfishness, ever! Nay, I speak not of all women, but only of Bharata's mother!
2.12.103
अनर्थभावेऽर्थपरे! नृशंसे
ममानुतापाय निविष्टभावे! ।
किमप्रियं पश्यसि मन्निमित्तं
हितानुकारिण्यथवाऽपि रामे ॥
O cruel one! O perverse one! O selfish one! You are bent hard on causing me misery! What is of concern to you, in me, or in Rāma, who does nothing but good!
2.12.104
परित्यजेयुः पितरो हि पुत्रान्
भार्याः पतींश्चापि कृतानुरागाः ।
कृत्स्नं हि सर्वं कुपितं जगत्स्या
द्दृष्ट्वैव रामं व्यसने निमग्नम् ॥
On seeing Rāma plunged in adversity, this entire world will be outraged. Wives, loving as they might be, will abandon their husbands and even fathers will abandon their sons.
Wives abandoning husbands and fathers abandoning children indicate the loss of faith in the fairness of life.
2.12.105
अहं पुनर्देवकुमाररूप
मलङ्कृतं तं सुतमाव्रजन्तम् ।
नन्दामि पश्यन्नपि दर्शनेन
भवामि दृष्ट्वैव च पुनर्युवेव ॥
The very sight of such a son coming towards me, well adorned and handsome like the son of Dēvas, makes me feel delighted, young and energetic all over again!
2.12.106
विनाऽपि सूर्येण भवेत्प्रवृत्ति
रवर्षता वज्रधरेण वाऽपि ।
रामं तु गच्छन्तमित स्समीक्ष्य
जीवेन्न कश्चित्त्विति चेतना मे ॥
The world might be able to live without the sun and without the wielder of Vajra showering rains, but I cannot contemplate anyone living seeing Rāma go away from here!
Indra, the God of Rain, is the wielder of Vajra!
2.12.107
विनाशकामामहिताममित्रा
मावासयं मृत्युमिवात्मनस्त्वाम् ।
चिरं बताङ्केन धृतासि सर्पी
महाविषा तेन हतोऽस्मि मोहात् ॥
You are no friend of mine. You are no well wisher of mine. You want to destroy me. Yet, alas, I held you, my death incarnate, in close quarters! You are like a venomous viper that I held on the lap for too long a time! I am now being ruined by that very delusion!
2.12.108
मया च रामेण च लक्ष्मणेन
प्रशास्तु हीनो भरतस्त्वया सह ।
पुरं च राष्ट्रं च निहत्य बान्धवान्
ममाहितानां च भवाभिहर्षिणी ॥
Without me, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, may Bharata, with you by his side rule the city and the kingdom killing all the near and dear! You would certainly bring delight to all my adversaries!
2.12.109
नृशंसवृत्ते व्यसनप्रहारिणि
प्रसह्य वाक्यं यदिहाद्य भाषसे ।
न नाम ते केन मुखात्पतन्त्यधो
विशीर्यमाणा दशना स्सहस्रधा ॥
O embodiment of cruelty! O tormentor of the distressed! Why are your teeth not being shattered into thousands of pieces and falling onto the ground from your mouth, for speaking such harsh words!
2.12.110
न किञ्चिदाहाहितमप्रियं वचो
न वेत्ति रामः परुषाणि भाषितुम् ।
कथन्नु रामे ह्यभिरामवादिनि
ब्रवीषि दोषान्गुणनित्यसम्मते ॥
Neither did Rāma utter a word that was hostile or unkind, ever. Nor did he know how to say a harsh word. How can you find any fault in Rāma, who is well regarded for his merits and pleasant disposition?
2.12.111
प्रताम्य वा प्रज्वल वा प्रणश्य वा
सहस्रशो वा स्फुटिता महीं व्रज ।
न ते करिष्यामि वच स्सुदारुणं
ममाहितं केकयराजपांसनि ॥
O shame of the house of Kēkaya kings! You may cry! You may burn! You may die! You may fall apart into thousand pieces on the ground! I will not fulfill your horrible wish that speaks doom to me!
2.12.112
क्षुरोपमां नित्यमसत्प्रियंवदां
प्रदुष्टभावां स्वकुलोपघातिनीम् ।
न जीवितुं त्वां विषहेऽमनोरमां
दिधक्षमाणां हृदयं सबन्धनम् ॥
You are like a knife! You were talking nothing but sweet lies all the time! You harbor nothing but evil thoughts! You bring destruction upon your own family! You torture my heart and you are despicable to look at! I cannot tolerate you living any further!
2.12.113
न जीवितं मेऽस्ति पुनःकुतस्सुखम्
विनाऽऽत्मजेनाऽत्मवतः कुतो रतिः ।
ममाहितं देवि न कर्तुमर्हसि
स्पृशामि पादावपि ते प्रसीद मे ॥
Without my son I would not even live, what to speak of having any comfort! Where is the pleasure of living anymore? You shall not bring such hardship on me! I shall touch your feet, please show mercy on me!
2.12.114
स भूमिपालो विलपन्ननाथवत्
स्त्रिया गृहीतो हृदयेऽतिमात्रया ।
पपात देव्याश्चरणौ प्रसारिता
वुभावसम्स्पृश्य यथाऽतुरस्तथा ॥
Wailing like a destitute, that ruler of the lands, who was firmly in the clutches of a woman that had crossed all bounds of decency, fell down like a seriously ill person, before he could reach the lady's outstretched feet.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे द्वादशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twelfth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
