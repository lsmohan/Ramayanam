Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 46
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa learns that the seven sons of ministers were killed by Hanumān. Without letting his true feelings known, he dismisses Hanumān as a creature specially created by Indra with the power of Tapa to take revenge upon him.
He instructs five of his army chiefs to go with an army of four divisions and contain Hanumān, albeit taking care to protect their own person. The army chiefs attack Hanumān, and he kills all of them and their army.
5.46.1
हतान्मन्त्रिसुतान् बुद्ध्वा
वानरेण महात्मना ।
रावणस्संवृताकारः
चकार मतिमुत्तमाम् ॥
Learning that the sons of the ministers were killed by the mighty Vānara, Rāvaṇa, keeping his feelings to himself, resolved on the best course open to him.
5.46.2
स विरूपाक्षयूपाक्षौ
दुर्धरं चैव राक्षसम् ।
प्रघसं भासकर्णं च
पञ्चसेनाग्रनायकान् ।
सन्दिदेश दशग्रीवो
वीरान्नयविशारदान् ।
हनुमद्ग्रहणव्यग्रान्
वायुवेगसमान्युधि ॥
He instructed five of his foremost valiant army Chiefs, Virūpāksha, Yūpāksha, Durdhara, Praghasa and Bhāsakarṇa, who were skilled in polity, swift as wind in battle, and zealous, for the capture of Hanumān:
5.46.4
यात सेनाग्रगास्सर्वे
महाबलपरिग्रहाः ।
सवाजिरथमातङ्गाः
स कपिश्शास्यतामिति ॥
March forth, all of you army Chiefs, attended by a great force, including horse, chariot and elephant divisions and may that Vānara be meted with punishment (that he deserves).
5.46.5
यत्नैश्च खलु भाव्यं स्यात्
तमासाद्य वनालयम् ।
कर्म चापि समाधेयम्
देशकालाविरोधिनम् ॥
Be extremely vigilant in dealing with that creature of the wilds. Adjust your plans and make sure they are not out of line with the situation as it develops.
5.46.6
न ह्यहं तं कपिं मन्ये
कर्मणा प्रतितर्कयन् ।
सर्वथा तन्महद्भूतम्
महाबलपरिग्रहम् ।
भवेदिन्द्रेण वा सृष्टम्
अस्मदर्थं तपोबलात् ॥
Based on what he has done so far, I do not think he is an ordinary Vānara. He must be, no matter how we analyze, some creature endowed with extraordinary strength created, perhaps, by Indra using the powers of Tapa for the sake of (taking revenge on) us.
5.46.7
सनागयक्षगन्धर्वा
देवासुरमहर्षयः ।
युष्माभिस्सहितैस्सर्वैः
मया सह विनिर्जिताः ।
तैरवश्यं विधातव्यम्
व्यलीकं किञ्चिदेव नः ।
तदेव नात्र सन्देहः
प्रसह्य परिगृह्यताम् ।
नावमान्यो भवद्भिश्च
हरिर्धीरपराक्रमः ॥
With your assistance, I vanquished the Dēvas, Asuras and Maharshis along with the Nāgas, Yakshas and Gandharvas. They can only be expected to cause us trouble, and there should be no doubt about it.
Put forth your efforts to capture him. This brave Vānara of great prowess should not be taken for granted by you.
Even after Hanumān declaring clearly that he is the envoy of Rāma, Rāvaṇa would not acknowledge it, for it would mean acknowledging his cowardly and shameful act of abducting Seetā in the absence of Rāma, in violation of Dharma. Hence, he tries to frame the development as a revenge against his past victories (over Dēvas and others).
5.46.10
दृष्टा हि हरयः पूर्वम्
मया विपुलविक्रमाः ।
वाली च सहसुग्रीवो
जाम्बवांश्च महाबलः ।
नीलस्सेनापतिश्चैव
ये चान्ये द्विविदादयः ।
नैवं तेषां गतिर्भीमा
न तेजो न पराक्रमः ।
न मतिर्न बलोत्साहौ
न रूपपरिकल्पनम् ॥
I have seen in the past, Vānaras of great prowess, such as Vāli, Sugreeva, Jāmbavān of immense strength, Neela the army chief, and others like Dwivida; they had nothing like this formidable swiftness, power, prowess, intelligence, strength, energy and ability to put forth a show like this.
5.46.12
महत्सत्त्वमिदं ज्ञेयम्
कपिरूपं व्यवस्थितम् ।
प्रयत्नं महदास्थाय
क्रियतामस्य निग्रहः ॥
Be sure to know that this is a creature extraordinaire that has taken the form of a Vānara. You need to put forth an extraordinary effort to contain him.
5.46.13
कामं लोकास्त्रयस्सेन्द्राः
ससुरासुरमानवाः ।
भवतामग्रतः स्थातुम्
न पर्याप्ता रणाजिरे ॥
Surely, not even all the three worlds, along with Indra, the Suras, Asuras, and humans, can make a stand against you on the battlefield.
5.46.14
तथापि तु नयज्ञेन
जयमाकाङ्क्षता रणे ।
आत्मा रक्ष्यः प्रयत्नेन
युद्धसिद्धिर्हि चञ्चला ॥
Even then, one who seeks to win the battle and is shrewd in strategy must protect his own person by every means; for victory in battle is ever uncertain.
5.46.15
ते स्वामिवचनं सर्वे
प्रतिगृह्य महौजसः ।
समुत्पेतुर्महावेगा
हुताशसमतेजसः ।
रथैर्मत्तैश्च मातङ्गैः
वाजिभिश्च महाजवैः ।
शस्त्रैश्च विविधैस्तीक्ष्णैः
सर्वैश्चोपचिता बलैः ॥
Taking those instructions from their master, they of great strength and fiery splendor marched forth with all swiftness, reinforced by a great army, including chariots, elephants in rut and fleet horses, armed with every kind of sharp weapon.
5.46.17
ततस्तं ददृशुर्वीरा
दीप्यमानं महाकपिम् ।
रश्मिमन्तमिवोद्यन्तम्
स्वतेजोरश्मिमालिनम् ।
तोरणस्थं महोत्साहम्
महासत्त्वं महाबलम् ॥
Then they saw the great Vānara of great energy, great power and great strength seated on the archway dazzling with the rays of his own splendor like the rising sun of massed rays.
5.46.19
महामतिं महावेगम्
महाकायं महाबलम् ।
तं समीक्ष्यैव ते सर्वे
दिक्षु सर्वास्ववस्थिताः ।
तै स्तैः प्रहरणैर्भीमैः
अभिपेतुस्ततस्ततः ॥
As soon as they found him of great intellect, great speed, giant body, great strength, they took stand in every direction, attacking him with their dreadful weapons from everywhere.
5.46.20
तस्य पञ्चायसास्तीक्ष्णाः
शिताः पीतमुखाश्शराः ।
शिरस्युत्पलपत्राभा
दुर्धरेण निपातिताः ॥
Durdhara struck him on the head with five sharp iron arrows with yellow tips that shone like black lilies.
5.46.21
स तैः पञ्चभिराविद्धः
शरैश्शिरसि वानरः ।
उत्पपात नदन् व्योम्नि
दिशो दश विनादयन् ॥
Hit by those five arrows on the head, the Vānara sprang into the air with a roar that reverberated in all the ten directions.
5.46.22
ततस्तु दुर्धरो वीरः
सरथस्सज्यकार्मुकः ।
किरन् शरशतैस्तीक्ष्णैः
अभिपेदे महाबलः ॥
The valiant Durdhara of immense strength, riding his chariot, with his bow ready and strung, attacked, spraying a hundred sharp arrows.
5.46.23
स कपिर्वारयामास
तं व्योम्नि शरवर्षिणम् ।
वृष्टिमन्तं पयोदान्ते
पयोदमिव मारुतः ॥
The Vānara, who was in the air, turned him who was showering arrows as ineffectual as wind does the rain-clouds at the end of the monsoon season.
5.46.24
अर्ध्यमानस्ततस्तेन
दुर्धरेणानिलात्मजः ।
चकार कदनं भूयो
व्यवर्धत च वेगवान् ॥
The swift son of Vāyu fought with Durdhara who was trying to harass him, increasing his body size even more.
5.46.25
स दूरं सहसोत्पत्य
दुर्धरस्य रथे हरिः ।
निपपात महावेगो
विद्युद्राशिर्गिराविव ॥
The Vānara jumped into the sky instantly and fell down with great speed on the chariot of Durdhara Like a mass of lightning on a mountain.
5.46.26
ततस्स मथिताष्टाश्वम्
रथं भग्नाक्षकूबरम् ।
विहाय न्यपतद्भूमौ
दुर्धरस्त्यक्तजीवितः ॥
(With that impact) Durdhara fell down dead from the chariot, whose eight horses turned into pulp and whose axle and axle-pole were completely smashed.
5.46.27
तं विरूपाक्षयूपाक्षौ
दृष्ट्वा निपतितं भुवि ।
सञ्जातरोषौ दुर्धर्षौ
उत्पेततुररिन्दमौ ॥
Seeing him (Durdhara) fall on the ground, the formidable Virūpāksha and Yūpāksha, who could crush their enemies, became angry and jumped (on Hanumān).
5.46.28
स ताभ्यां सहसोत्पत्य
विष्ठितो विमलेऽम्बरे ।
मुद्गराभ्यां महाबाहुः
वक्षस्यभिहतः कपिः ॥
And they struck the Vānara of mighty arm, who was hovering in the air, on the chest with Mudgaras.
5.46.29
तयोर्वेगवतोर्वेगम्
विनिहत्य महाबलः ।
निपपात पुनर्भूमौ
सुपर्णसमविक्रमः ॥
Frustrating their furious onset, he of immense strength and prowess equaling that of Suparṇa jumped down on the ground again.
5.46.30
स सालवृक्षमासाद्य
तमुत्पाट्य च वानरः ।
तावुभौ राक्षसौ वीरौ
जघान पवनात्मजः ॥
Finding a Sāla tree and uprooting it, the Vānara, son of Vāyu, killed those two valiant Rākshasas (with it).
5.46.31
ततस्तांस्त्रीन्हतान्ज्ञात्वा
वानरेण तरस्विना ।
अभिपेदे महावेगः
प्रसह्य प्रघसो हरिम् ।
भासकर्णश्च सङ्कृद्धः
शूलमादाय वीर्यवान् ॥
Then, finding those three killed by the mighty Vānara, Praghasa attacked the Vānara with great speed and force, and so did the enraged, valiant Bhāsakarṇa, armed with a spear.
5.46.33
एकतः कपिशार्दूलम्
यशस्विनमवस्थितम् ।
पट्टिसेन शिताग्रेण
प्रघसः प्रत्ययोधयत् ।
भासकर्णश्च शूलेन
राक्षसः कपिसत्तमम् ॥
They fought, positioning themselves on the same side of that renowned and eminent tiger among Vānaras, Praghasa with his Paṭṭiṡa of sharp tip, and Bhāsakarṇa, the Rākshasa, with his spear.
5.46.34
स ताभ्यां विक्षतैर्गात्रैः
असृग्दिग्धतनूरुहः ।
अभवद्वानरः क्रुद्धो
बालसूर्यसमप्रभः ॥
His body hit by that duo, the hair on his body became bedaubed with blood, and the Vānara shone like the rising sun.
5.46.35
समुत्पाट्य गिरेश्शृङ्गम्
समृगव्यालपादपम् ।
जघान हनुमान् वीरो
राक्षसौ कपिकुञ्जरः ॥
Plucking a mountain crest along with its deer, beasts and trees, Hanumān, the Veera, an elephant among Vānaras killed those two Rākshasas (by hurling it at them).
5.46.36
ततस्तेष्ववसन्नेषु
सेनापतिषु पञ्चसु ।
बलं तदवशेषं च
नाशयामास वानरः ॥
After the five army chiefs were thus killed, the Vānara destroyed what remained of their army.
5.46.37
अश्वैरश्वान् गजैर्नागान्
योधैर्योधान् रथैरथान् ।
स कपिर्नाशयामास
सहस्राक्ष इवासुरान् ॥
The Vānara destroyed horses (striking them) with (other) horses, elephants with elephants, chariots with chariots and warriors with warriors, like the Thousand-eyed did with the Asuras.
5.46.38
हतैर्नागैश्च तुरगैः
भग्नाक्षैश्च महारथैः ।
हतैश्च राक्षसैर्भूमी
रुद्धमार्गा समन्ततः ॥
Cluttered with dead elephants and horses, huge chariots with broken axles, and the slain Rākshasas, the paths everywhere became impassable.
5.46.39
ततः कपिस्तान्ध्वजिनीपतीन् रणे
निहत्य वीरान्सबलान्सवाहनान् ।
समीक्ष्य वीरः परिगृह्य तोरणं
कृतक्षणः काल इव प्रजाक्षये ॥
Having thus killed those army chiefs along with their armies and vehicles, the Vānara went back to the archway and sat on it waiting as before, like Time does, for annihilating the creatures.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे षट्चत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty sixth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
