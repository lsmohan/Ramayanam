Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 17
In this Sarga, a Rākshasi by name Ṡūrpaṇakhā, happens to come to Rāma’s Āṡrama by chance. Upon seeing Rāma, shining like a Dēva, she gets infatuated with him.
She asks him who he is. Rāma tells about Daṡaratha the king, himself, his brother Lakshmaṇa and his wife Seetā. Rāma then asks who she is. She tells her name and about her brothers Rāvaṇa, Kumbhakarṇa, Vibheeshaṇa, Khara and Nandana.
She tells that she is infatuated with Rāma and would like him to be her husband. She tells that Seetā is no good for him as a wife and she would eat her away and also Lakshmaṇa. Rāma laughs at her.
3.17.1
कृताभिषेको रामस्तु
सीता सौमित्रिरेव च ।
तस्माद्गोदावरीतीरात्
ततो जग्मुस्वमाश्रमम् ॥
After finishing their bath, Rāma, Seetā and Lakshmaṇa returned from the bank of the Gōdāvaree to their own Āṡrama.
3.17.2
आश्रमं तमुपागम्य
राघवस्सह लक्ष्मणः ।
कृत्वा पौर्वाह्णिकं कर्म
पर्णशालामुपागमत् ॥
After reaching the Āṡrama, Rāma, along with Lakshmaṇa, completed the morning rituals and entered the cottage.
3.17.3
उवास सुखितस्तत्र
पूज्यमानो महर्षिभिः ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
चकार विविधाः कथाः ॥
Rāma, the one honored by Maharshis, sat there relaxed, talking over various topics with his brother Lakshmaṇa.
3.17.4
स रामः पर्णशालायां
आसीनस्सह सीतया ।
विरराज महाबाहुः
चित्रया चन्द्रमा इव ॥
Seated with Seetā in the cottage, Rāma of the mighty arm shone like the moon in the company of star Citra.
3.17.5
तथाऽसीनस्य रामस्य
कथासंसक्तचेतसः ।
तं देशं राक्षसी काचित्
अजगाम यदृच्छया ॥
As Rāma sat there, engrossed in the discussions, a Rākshasi happened to come there by chance.
3.17.6
सा तु शूर्पणखा नाम
दशग्रीवस्य राक्षसः ।
भगिनी राममासाद्य
ददर्श त्रिदशोपमम् ॥
She, by the name of Ṡūrpaṇakhā, sister of the ten-headed Rākshasa (Rāvaṇa) approached Rāma and saw him shining like a Dēva.
3.17.7
सिंहोरस्कं महाबाहुं
पद्मपत्रनिभेक्षणम् ।
आजानुबाहुं दीप्तास्यम्
अतीव प्रियदर्शनम् ।
गजविक्रान्तगमनं
जटामण्डलधारिणम् ।
सुकुमारं महासत्त्वं
पार्थिवव्यञ्जनान्वितम् ।
राममिन्दीवरश्यामं
कन्दर्पसदृशप्रभम् ।
बभूवेन्द्रोपमं दृष्ट्वा
राक्षसी काममोहिता ॥
Seeing Rāma, broad-chested like a lion, with mighty arms that reached down to the knee, eyes like lotus petals and a face that was brilliant and exceptionally pleasant, adorning matted hair, gait majestic like that of an elephant, with delicate features and massive strength with unmistakable stamp of royalty on him, shining like Kandarpa with his lily-dark complexion, and looking like a peer of Indra, the Rākshasi was thoroughly infatuated with him.
3.17.10
सुमुखं दुर्मुखी रामं
वृत्तमध्यं महोदरी ।
विशालाक्षं विरूपाक्षी
सुकेशं ताम्रमूर्धजा ।
प्रीतिरूपं विरूपा सा
सुस्वरं भैरवस्वरा ।
तरुणं दारुणा वृद्धा
दक्षिणं वामभाषिणी ।
न्यायवृत्तं सुदुर्वृत्ता
प्रियमप्रियदर्शना ।
शरीरजसमाविष्टा
राक्षसी वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
The Rākshasi of crooked face to Rāma of charming face, she with a pot belly to him of flat stomach, she of crooked eyes to him of wide eyes, she of coppery thatch to him of lovely hair, she of revolting looks to him of pleasant appearance, she with a voice like dog’s to him of a musical voice, she who was terribly aged to him the youthful one, she of crafty speech to him of eloquence, she of wicked nature to him of fair conduct, she of repulsive presence to him of pleasant presence said these words, with her body overpowered by passion:
Vālmeeki never misses to notice contrasts among people and among things. Here he finds the most striking contrast between Rāma and Ṡūrpaṇakhā and describes it beautifully.
3.17.13
जटी तापसरूपेण
सभार्यश्शरचापधृत् ।
आगतस्त्वमिमं देशं
कथं राक्षससेवितम् ।
किमागमनकृत्यं ते
तत्त्वमाख्यातुमर्हसि ॥
How is it that you, sporting the hair like a Tapasvi and accompanied by your wife and carrying a bow and arrow came to this place frequented by Rākshasas? What has brought you here? Tell me truly!
3.17.14
एवमुक्तस्तु राक्षस्या
शूर्पणख्या परन्तपः ।
ऋजुबुद्धितया सर्वं
आख्यातुमुपचक्रमे ।
अनृतं न हि रामस्य
कदाचिदपि सम्मतम् ।
विशेषेणाऽश्रमस्थस्य
समीपे स्त्रीजनस्य च ॥
Thus questioned by Ṡūrpaṇakhā, the Rākshasi, Rāma, scorcher of the foe, started to tell her all about himself in a frank manner, as he would never like to lie, especially in the presence of women and in an Āṡrama:
3.17.16
आसीद्धशरथो नाम
राजा त्रिदशविक्रमः ।
तस्याहमग्रजः पुत्रो
रामो नाम जनैश्श्रुतः ॥
There was a king by name Daṡaratha, equal to Dēvas in valor. I am the eldest of his sons, known to all as Rāma.
3.17.17
भ्राताऽयं लक्ष्मणो नाम
यवीयान्मामनुव्रतः ।
इयं भार्या च वैदेही
मम सीतेति विश्रुता ॥
He, by name Lakshmaṇa, who follows me always, is my younger brother. She, well known as Seetā, princess of Vidēha kingdom, is my wife.
3.17.18
नियोगात्तु नरेन्द्रस्य
पितुर्मातुश्च यन्त्रितः ।
धर्मार्थं धर्मकाङ्क्षी च
वनं वस्तुमिगहातः ॥
At the behest of my father the king, and at the command of my mother, I came to this Vana, interested in upholding Dharma.
3.17.19
त्वां तु वेदितुमिच्छामि
कथ्यतां क्वासि कस्य वा ।
न हि तावन्मनोज्ञाङ्गी
राक्षसी प्रतिभासि मे ।
इह वा किं निमित्तं त्वं
आगता ब्रूहि तत्त्वतः  ।
साब्रवीद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राक्षसी मदनार्दिता ॥
I would like to know who you are. Where are you from? Whom are you related to? You look beautiful and not at all like a Rākshasi! Now tell me why you came here, factually. Hearing those words, the Rākshasi, pitifully under the spell of infatuation, said:
We see the lighter side of Rāma here again, poking fun at Ṡūrpaṇakhā, in saying that she looks beautiful and not at all like a Rākshasi.
We saw Rāma’s lighter side previously at the time of giving away cows to a Brāhmaṇa (2.32.35) and when he got a free ride from Virādha (3.3.21).
3.17.21
अहं शूर्पणखा नाम
राक्षसी कामरूपिणी ।
अरण्यं विचरामीदम्
एका सर्वभयङ्करा ॥
My name is Ṡūrpaṇakhā. I am a Rākshasi capable of taking any form at will. I move about this frightening Araṇya, all by myself.
3.17.22
रावणो नाम मे भ्राता
बलीयान्राक्षसेश्वरः ।
वीरो विश्रवसः पुत्रो
यदि ते श्रोत्रमागतः ॥
The mighty strong lord of Rākshasas, by name Rāvaṇa, is my brother. You must have heard of that Veera, who is the son of Viṡravas.
3.17.23
प्रवृद्धनिद्रश्च सदा
कुम्भकर्णो महाबलः ।
विभीषणस्तु धर्मात्मा
न तु राक्षसचेष्टितः ।
प्रख्यातवीर्यौ च रणे
भ्रातरौ खरदूषणौ ॥
And there is mighty strong Kumbhakarṇa who goes into prolonged slumber. And there is Vibheeshaṇa, the Dharmātma, who is very unlike a Rākshasa. And there are two other brothers, Khara and Dūshaṇa, who are far-famed for their valor in battle.
3.17.25
तानहं समतिक्रान्ता
राम त्वा पूर्वदर्शनात् ।
समुपेताऽस्मि भावेन
भर्तारं पुरुषोत्तमम् ॥
But I do not care about any of them. O Rāma! O foremost of spirited beings! As soon as I saw you, I felt, in my mind, that you are my husband.
3.17.26
अहं प्रभावसम्पन्ना
स्वच्छन्दबलगामिनी ।
चिराय भव मे भर्ता
सीतया किं करिष्यसि ॥
I am rich in my powers. I live as I please with my strength. You shall be my husband forever. What use can this Seetā be to you?
3.17.27
विकृता च विरूपा च
न चेयं सदृशी तव ।
अहमेवानुरूपा ते
भार्या रूपेण पश्य माम् ॥
She is ugly and out of shape. She is no match for you at all. I am the one who is a good match for you. See me as your wife.
3.17.28
इमां विरूपामसतीं
करालां निर्णतोदरीम् ।
अनेन ते सह भ्रात्रा
भक्षयिष्यामि मानुषीम् ॥
I shall gobble up this bad woman, the ugly and terrible looking one with flat stomach; and also this brother of yours.
3.17.29
ततः पर्वतशृङ्गाणि
वनानि विविधानि च ।
पश्यन्सह मया कान्त
दण्डकान्विचरिष्यसि ॥
O charming one! Then you shall roam the Daṇḍaka along with me, seeing the mountain crests and the variety of forests.
3.17.30
इत्येवमुक्तः काकुत्स्थः
प्रहस्य मदिरेक्षणाम् ।
इदं वचनमारेभे
वक्तुं वाक्यविशारदः ॥
Told thus, Kākutstha, who was skilled in speech, laughed at that woman whose eyes were whirling like those of a drunkard, and started to respond.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे सप्तदशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventeenth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
