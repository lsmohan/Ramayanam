Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 63
In this Sarga, Saṃpāti tells the Vānaras how he was persuaded by Niṡākara to hold on to his life and that he has been waiting to see the Vānaras.
While Saṃpāti speaks thus, he regains his wings, vigor and strength, and becomes happy like he never was before. He cites his gaining of the wings as a portent for the success of the Vānaras in their mission.
The Vānaras, feeling very happy, set themselves to search for Seetā.
4.63.1
एतैरन्यैश्च बहुभिः
वाक्यैर्वाक्यविशारदः ।
मां प्रशस्याभ्यनुज्ञाप्य
प्रविष्टस्स स्वमालयम् ॥
Saying these words and speaking further at length, that articulate speaker cheered me up and convinced me, before retiring into his dwelling.
4.63.2
कन्दरात्तु विसर्पित्वा
पर्वतस्य शनैश्शनैः ।
अहं विन्ध्यं समारुह्य
भवतः प्रतिपालये ॥
Leaving the cave and crawling ever so slowly to the top of the Vindhya, I have been waiting for you ever since.
4.63.3
अद्यत्वेतस्य कालस्य
साग्रं वर्षशतत्रयम् ।
देशकालप्रतीक्षोऽस्मि
हृदि कृत्वा मुनेर्वचः ॥
It has been more than three hundred years. I have been waiting for the time to come and the events to unfold, reminding myself of the words of the Muni.
4.63.4
महाप्रस्थानमासाद्य
स्वर्गते तु निशाकरे ।
मां निर्दहति सन्तापो
वितर्कैर्बहुभिर्वृतम् ।
उदितां मरणे बुद्धिम्
मुनिवाक्यैर्निवर्तये ॥
After Niṡākara launched upon his final journey and reached Heaven, I was consumed by anxiety, imagining all sorts of things. Whenever the thought of giving up this life arose, I shut it off by recalling the words of the Muni.
4.63.5
बुद्धिर्या तेन मे दत्ता
प्राणानां रक्षणे मम  ।
सा मेऽपनयते दुःखम्
दीप्तेवाग्निशिखा तमः ॥
The advice he gave to hold on to my life dispels my misery like a blazing tongue of flame dispels darkness.
Note: Read Sarga 59 here for a logical flow of narration. (As noted at the conclusion of Sarga 59, it is placed out of order).
4.63.6
तस्य त्वेवं ब्रुवाणस्य
संहतैर्वानरैस्सह ।
उत्पेततुस्तदा पक्षौ
समक्षं वनचारिणाम् ॥
As he was saying this in the midst of the Vānaras, his two wings sprouted right in front of the eyes of those Vana dwellers.
4.63.7
स दृष्ट्वा स्वां तनुं पक्षैः
उद्गतैररुणच्छदैः ।
प्रहर्षमतुलं लेभे
वानरांश्चेदमब्रवीत् ॥
He felt happy like never before, seeing his body with the fresh grown wings of reddish feathers. Then he said these words to the Vānaras:
4.63.8
निशाकरस्य राजर्षेः
प्रभावादमितौजसः ।
आदित्यरश्मिनिर्दग्धौ
पक्षौ पुनरुपस्थितौ ॥
By the power of Niṡākara, the Rājarshi of limitless radiance, my wings that had been burnt by the sun’s rays have been restored.
4.63.9
यौवने वर्तमानस्य
ममाऽऽसीद्य: पराक्रमः ।
तमेवाद्यावगच्छामि
बलं पौरुषमेव च ॥
I now feel the same degree of prowess that I used to enjoy in my youth; and also the same strength and vigor.
4.63.10
सर्वथा क्रियतां यत्नः
सीतामधिगमिष्यथ ।
पक्षलाभो ममायं वः
सिद्धिप्रत्ययकारकः ॥
Make an all-out effort and you will find Seetā. My regaining these wings is a sure indication that you too will meet with success.
4.63.11
इत्युक्त्वा तान्हरीन्सर्वान्
सम्पातिः पतगोत्तमः ।
उत्पपात गिरेश्शृङ्गात्
जिज्ञासुः खगमो गतिम् ॥
Saying this to all those Vānaras, Saṃpāti, the best among birds, flew up from the mountain crest, to test his ability to sail along the sky.
4.63.12
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
प्रतिसंहृष्टमानसाः ।
बभूवुर्हरिशार्दूला
विक्रमाभ्युदयोन्मुखाः ॥
Hearing those words spoken by him and their hearts filled with joy, those tigers among Vānaras became ready to deploy their prowess.
4.63.13
अथ पवनसमानविक्रमाः
प्लवगवराः प्रतिलब्धपौरुषाः ।
अभिजिदभिमुखा दिशं ययुः
जनकसुतापरिमार्गणोन्मुखाः ॥
with their vigor restored, those eminent Vānaras of prowess equaling that of the wind set out at Abhijit in the (southern) direction to search for the daughter of Janaka.
Abhijit Muhūrta, in general, refers to the time period of 24 minutes before the noon and 24 minutes after the noon combined.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे त्रिषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty third Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
