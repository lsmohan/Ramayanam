Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 95
In this Sarga, Rāma continues to describe the beauty of the Vana to Seetā, specifically of river Mandākini. He expresses his sentiment that his mind does not even think of Ayōdhyā and the kingdom, in the company of Seetā and Lakshmaṇa and the captivating beauty of that lovely place.
2.95.1
अथ शैलाद्विनिष्क्रम्य
मैथिलीं कोसलेश्वरः ।
अदर्शयच्छुभजलां
रम्यां मन्दाकिनीं नदीम् ॥
Then, getting down from the mountain, the lord of Kōsala pointed out the lovely river Mandākini, of auspicious waters, to the princess of Mithilā.
2.95.2
अब्रवीच्च वरारोहां
चारुचन्द्रनिभाननाम् ।
विदेहराजस्य सुतां
रामो राजीवलोचनः ॥
Rāma with eyes rivaling lotus petals said to the beautiful daughter of the King of Vidēha, of lovely moon-like face and shapely hips:
2.95.3
विचित्रपुलिनां रम्यां
हंससारससेविताम् ।
कमलैरुपसम्पन्नां
पश्य मन्दाकिनीं नदीम् ॥
Look at the beautiful river Mandākini with its picturesque sand-flats, the swans and Sārasa cranes crowding on it and with its abundant lotus flowers!
2.95.4
नानाविधैस्तीररुहैः
वृतां पुष्पफलद्रुमैः ।
राजन्तीं राजराजस्य
नलिनीमिव सर्वतः ॥
Surrounded by numerous kinds of trees growing on its banks, with their loads of fruit and flower, it dazzles the eye like lake Nalini of Rājarāja!
Rājarāja is Kubēra.
2.95.5
मृगयूथनिपीतानि
कलुषाम्भांसि साम्प्रतम् ।
तीर्थानि रमणीयानि
रतिं सञ्जनयन्ति मे ॥
Its lovely shallow shores, where the water is turbid now owing to herds of deer having just drunk there, make me joyfully excited.
2.95.6
जटाजिनधराः काले
वल्कलोत्तरवाससः ।
ऋषयस्त्ववगाहन्ते
नदीं मन्दाकिनीं प्रिये ॥
My darling! the Ṛshis with matted locks, clad in deer-skin and wearing tree bark for their upper garment, bathe in this river Mandākini regularly.
2.95.7
आदित्यमुपतिष्ठन्ते
नियमादूर्ध्वबाहवः ।
एते परे विशालाक्षि
मुनयस्संशितव्रताः ॥
And other Munis, who have taken exacting vows, pray regularly here to the Sun-god with their arms upraised, O wide-eyed one!
2.95.8
मारुतोद्धूतशिखरैः
प्रनृत्त इव पर्वतः ।
पादपैः पत्रपुष्पाणि
सृजद्भिरभितो नदीम् ॥
With the trees shedding flowers and leaves all about the river as their tops sway with the wind, the mountain looks as if it is dancing!
2.95.9
क्वचिन्मणिनिकाशोदां
क्वचित्पुलिनशालिनीम् ।
क्वचित्सिद्धजनाकीर्णां
पश्य मन्दाकिनीं नदीम् ॥
Look at this Mandākini which in places shines with its crystal clear water, in others with sand-flats and at others crowded with Siddhas!
2.95.10
निर्धूतान्वायुना पश्य
विततान्पुष्पसञ्चयान् ।
पोप्लूयमानानपरान्
पश्य त्वं जलमध्यगान् ॥
Look at the scattered heaps of flowers blown by the wind, and also the flowers floating in the middle of the river!
2.95.11
तांश्चातिवल्गुवचसो
रथाङ्गाह्वयना द्विजाः ।
अधिरोहन्ति कल्याणि
निष्कूजन्त श्शुभा गिरः ॥
The softly crooning birds known as Cakravākas climb on them, uttering their lovely calls, O auspicious one!
2.95.12
दर्शनं चित्रकूटस्य
मन्दाकिन्याश्च शोभने ।
अधिकं पुरवासाच्च
मन्ये तव च दर्शनात् ॥
O beautiful one, seeing Citrakūṭa and Mandākini, and especially you, gives me more pleasure than life in the city!
2.95.13
विधूतकलुषैस्सिद्धैः
तपोदमशमान्वितैः ।
नित्यविक्षोभितजलां
विगाहस्व मया सह ॥
Bathe along with me in this river whose waters are regularly stirred by the Siddhas who have freed themselves from all afflictions by the virtue of their Tapa, self-restraint and serenity.
2.95.14
सखीवच्छ विगाहस्व
सीते मन्दाकिनीं नदीम् ।
कमलान्यवमज्जन्ती
पुष्कराणि च भामिनि ॥
Enter into the Mandākini, O lovely one, as if you are her friend, with those red and white lotuses pressed into the waters.
2.95.15
त्वं पौरजनवद्व्यालान्
अयोध्यामिव पर्वतम् ।
मन्यस्व वनिते नित्यं
सरयूवदिमां नदीम् ॥
Consider always, my lady, this mountain as Ayōdhyā, this wildlife as the citizens, and this river as the Sarayu.
2.95.16
लक्ष्मणश्चापि धर्मात्मा
मन्निदेशे व्यवस्थितः ।
त्वं चानुकूला वैदेहि
प्रीतिं जनयथो मम ॥
Lakshmaṇa the Dharmātma ever ready to do my bidding, and you being always amiable to me, make me very happy.
2.95.17
उपस्पृशंस्त्रिषवणं
मधुमूलफलाशनः ।
नायोध्यायैन राज्याय
स्पृहयेऽद्य त्वया सह ॥
Now, enjoying three baths a day and eating honey, roots and fruits along with you, my mind does not even think of Ayōdhyā or the kingdom.
2.95.18
इमांहि रम्यां मृगयूथशालिनीं
निपीततोयां गजसिंहवानरैः ।
सुपुष्पितां पुष्पभरैरलङ्कृतां
नसोऽस्ति यस्स्यान्न गतक्लमस्सुखी ॥
There cannot be any one who is not happy and does not get over his fatigue by taking bath in this lovely river, which is frequented by deer herds, whose waters are adorned by wads of flowers and are drunk by elephants, lions and monkeys.
2.95.19
इतीव रामो बहुसङ्गतं वचः
प्रियासहायस्सरितं प्रति ब्रुवन् ।
चचार रम्यं नयनाञ्जनप्रभं
स चित्रकूटं रघुवंशवर्धनः ॥
Rāma, the enhancer of the lineage of Raghu, saying many such words of admiration about the river, sauntered along with Seetā, on that Citrakūṭa mountain that had the shine of mascara.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे पञ्चनवतितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninety fifth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
