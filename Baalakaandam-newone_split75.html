Baala Kaanda - Sarga 75
In this Sarga, Rāma, the son of Jamadagni, speaking directly to Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, narrates the history of the bows of Vishṇu and Ṡiva, and how he got them, and about the murder of his father which enraged him and made him kill all the Kshatriyas. Then he challenges him to mount an arrow on the bow of Vishṇu. He promises Rāma the pleasure of a duel with him as a reward.
1.75.1
राम दाशरथे राम
वीर्यं ते श्रूयतेऽद्भुतम् ।
धनुषो भेदनं चैव
निखिलेन मया श्रुतम् ॥
O Rāma! Valiant son of Daṡaratha! People speak of your marvelous strength. I have heard all about your breaking of the bow!
1.75.2
तदद्भुतमचिन्त्यं च
भेदनं धनुषस्त्वया ।
तच्छ्रुत्वाऽहमनुप्राप्तो
धनुर्गृह्यापरं शुभम् ॥
That you have broken that bow is truly unbelievable and astonishing. Hearing that, I have come here with another superb and auspicious bow.
1.75.3
तदिदं घोरसङ्काशम्
जामदग्न्यं महद्धनुः ।
पूरयस्व शरेणैव
स्वबलं दर्शयस्व च ॥
This mighty and dreadful bow comes from Jamadagni. May you show your prowess by drawing an arrow on this!
1.75.4
तदहं ते बलं दृष्ट्वा
धनुषोऽस्य प्रपूरणे ।
द्वन्द्वयुद्धं प्रदास्यामि
वीर्यश्लाघ्यस्य राघव ॥
O Rāma! If you can show me your strength by mounting an arrow on this bow, I shall grant you the pleasure of having a duel with me which men of strength hold in high esteem!
1.75.5
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राजा दशरथस्तदा ।
विषण्णवदनो दीनः
प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
King Daṡaratha, thereupon prayed humbly with folded hands and a grief stricken face:
1.75.6
क्षत्ररोषात्प्रशान्तस्त्वम्
ब्राह्मणश्च महायशाः ।
बालानां मम पुत्राणां
अभयं दातुमर्हसि ॥
O celebrated one! O Brāhmaṇa! You had verily become tranquil and pacified, after putting behind you, your indignation upon the Kshatriyas. May you assure my sons, the young lads, protection!
1.75.7
भार्गवाणां कुले जातः
स्वाध्यायव्रतशालिनाम् ।
सहास्राक्षे प्रतिज्ञाय
शस्त्रं निक्षिप्तवानसि ॥
You are born into the lineage of Bhṛgu That has been consistently devoted to their studies! Have you not taken a vow witnessed by the thousand-eyed Indra to renounce the use of arms!
1.75.8
स त्वं धर्मपरो भूत्वा
काश्यपाय वसुन्धराम् ।
दत्त्वा वनमुपागम्य
महेन्द्रकृतकेतनः ॥
And giving yourself up wholly for Dharma and giving away the entire land to Kāṡyapa, have you not left for the Vanas and made Mahēndra mountain your home?!
1.75.9
मम सर्वविनाशाय
सम्प्राप्तस्त्वं महामुने ।
न चैकस्मिन् हते रामे
सर्वे जीवामहे वयम् ॥
And here you come, O Mahā Muni, for my utter ruin! None of us would be able to stay alive if Rāma is killed!
1.75.10
ब्रुवत्येवं दशरथे
जामदग्न्यः प्रतापवान् ।
अनादृत्यैव तद्वाक्यम्
राममेवाभ्यभाषत ॥
The valiant son of Jamadagni simply ignored everything that Daṡaratha said and continued addressing Rāma directly:
1.75.11
इमे द्वे धनुषी श्रेष्ठे
दिव्ये लोकाभिविश्रुते ।
दृढे बलवती मुख्ये
सुकृते विश्वकर्मणा ॥
Both of these excellent, tough, strong and renowned bows are of divine origin. They were specially made by Viṡwakarma.
Viṡwakarma is the chief architect, engineer and builder of the Dēvas.
1.75.12
अतिसृष्टं सुरैरेकम्
त्र्यम्बकाय युयुत्सवे ।
त्रिपुरघ्नं नरश्रेष्ठ
भग्नं काकुत्स्थ यत्त्वया ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! O best among men! The bow broken by you is one of those two. It was given to the Three-eyed (Lord Ṡiva) by the Dēvas when he was ready to destroy Tripuras!
Tripuras refers to the three mobile cities built by Maya using gold, silver and bronze respectively, for three Rākshasas by names Tārakāsura, Kamalāksha and Vidyunmāli.
1.75.13
इदं द्वितीयं दुर्धर्षम्
विष्णोर्दत्तं सुरोत्तमैः ।
तदिदं वैष्णवं राम
धनुः परमभास्वरम् ।
समानसारं काकुत्स्थ
रौद्रेण धनुषा त्विदम् ॥
This, the second, the invincible, was given by the eminent Dēvas to Vishṇu. O Rāma! O prince of Kākutstha lineage! This glowing, radiant bow of Vishṇu is of the same caliber as that bow of Rudra!
Rudra is another name for Ṡiva.
1.75.14
तदा तु देवतास्सर्वाः
पृच्छन्ति स्म पितामहम् ।
शितिकण्ठस्य विष्णोश्च
बलाबलनिरीक्षया ॥
Then the Dēvas, out of curiosity, asked the Progenitor (Brahma) about the relative strength of Ṡitikaṇṭha (Ṡiva) and Vishṇu!
1.75.15
अभिप्रायं तु विज्ञाय
देवतानां पितामहः ।
विरोधं जनयामास
तयो स्सत्यवतां वरः ॥
The progenitor, assessing exactly what the Dēvas were asking and interested in nothing but the truth, created animosity between the two.
Brahma did not want to give his opinion. Rather he wanted the facts to speak for themselves.
1.75.16
विरोधे च महद्युद्धं
अभवद्रोमहर्षणम् ।
शितिकण्ठस्य विष्णोश्च
परस्परजिगीषुणोः ॥
Animosity then took over both Ṡitikaṇṭha and Vishṇu. Bent on winning over each other, they fought a great battle, that made one's hair stand on end.
1.75.17
तदा तु जृम्भितं शैवम्
धनुर्भीमपराक्रमम् ।
हुङ्कारेण महादेव
स्तम्भितोऽथ त्रिलोचनः ॥
Then the extremely strong and invincible bow of Ṡiva, the three eyed Mahā Dēva, was rendered immobile by a snort from Vishṇu!
1.75.18
देवैस्तदा समागम्य
सर्षिसघै स्सचारणैः ।
याचितौ प्रशमं तत्र
जग्मतुस्तौ सुरोत्तमौ ॥
Then, entreated by the assembled Dēvas, along with hosts of Ṛshis and Cāraṇas both of those supreme Dēvas were pacified.
1.75.19
जृम्भितं तद्धनुर्द्रृष्ट्वा
शैवं विष्णुपराक्रमैः ।
अधिकं मेनिरे विष्णुम्
देवास्सर्षिगणास्तदा ॥
Seeing that bow of Ṡiva made powerless by the prowess of Vishṇu, the hosts of Dēvas and Ṛshis considered Vishṇu to be superior.
1.75.20
धनू रुद्रस्तु सङ्कृद्धो
विदेहेषु महायशाः ।
देवरातस्य राजर्षेः
ददौ हस्ते ससायकम् ॥
The renowned Rudra who was greatly angered, then resigned that bow and the arrows to the hands of Rājarshi Dēvarāta of Vidēha.
1.75.21
इदं च वैष्णवं राम
धनुः परपुरञ्जयम् ।
ऋचीके भार्गवे प्रादात्
विष्णुः स न्यासमुत्तमम् ॥
O! Rāma, Vishṇu gave this bow which is capable of conquering hostile cities, to the trust of Ṛceeka of the lineage of Bhṛgu.
1.75.22
ऋचीकस्तु महातेजाः
पुत्रस्याप्रतिकर्मणः ।
पितुर्मम ददौ दिव्यम्
जमदग्नेर्महात्मनः ॥
The supremely radiant Ṛceeka gave this bow to his son, my father, the highly accomplished Mahātma, Jamadagni.
1.75.23
न्यस्तशस्त्रे पितरि मे
तपोबलसमन्विते ।
अर्जुनो विदधे मृत्युम्
प्राकृतां बुद्धिमास्थितः ॥
When my father, endowed with the energy of Tapa, had given up the use of weapons, Arjuna, prompted by mean motives, inflicted death upon him.
Arjuna here refers to Kārtaveeryārjuna.
1.75.24
वधमप्रतिरूपं तु
पितु श्शृत्वा सुदारुणम् ।
क्षत्रमुत्सादयन्रोषात्
जातं जातमनेकशः ।
पृथिवीं चाखिलां प्राप्य
काश्यपाय महात्मने ।
यज्ञस्यान्ते तदा राम
दक्षिणां पुण्यकर्मणे ।
दत्त्वा महेन्द्रनिलयः
तपोबलसमन्वितः ॥
Upon hearing about this extremely ruthless slaying of my father, I, in my wrath, extirpated the Kshatriya clan again and again, every time it took new roots. In the end I conferred the entire land which I gained thus, on Mahātma Kāṡyapa who has done many Puṇya Karmas. Thereafter, endowed with the energy of Tapa, I have been living on Mahēndra mountain.
1.75.27
अद्यतूत्तमवीर्येण
त्वया राम महाबल ।
श्रुतवान् धनुषो भेदम्
ततोऽहं द्रुतमागतः ॥
O! Mighty strong Rāma! I rushed here upon hearing about the breaking of the bow by your excellent prowess.
1.75.28
तदिदं वैष्णवं राम
पितृपैतामहं महत् ।
क्षत्रधर्मं पुरस्कृत्य
गृह्णीष्व धनुरुत्तमम् ॥
O! Rāma, in adherence to Kshatriya Dharma, take this excellent bow of Vishṇu inherited by me from my father and forefathers!
1.75.29
योजयस्व धनुश्श्रेष्ठे
शरं परपुरञ्जयम् ।
यदि शक्नोषि काकुत्स्थ
द्वन्द्वं दास्यामि ते ततः ॥
Try mounting the arrow on this best of bows that can conquer entire cities! O descendant of Kākutstha, Rāma! If you are able to do that, I will grant you the pleasure of a duel with me!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे पञ्चसप्ततितमस्सर्ग:॥
Thus concludes the seventy fifth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
