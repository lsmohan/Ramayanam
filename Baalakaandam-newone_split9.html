Baala Kaanda - Sarga 9
This Sarga starts with the story of Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga, the first of many sub-stories that fill Bāla Kāṇḍa.
What do children like? Among many other things, they like to hear stories. So it is only appropriate to have Bāla Kāṇḍa filled with stories, isn't it?
Throughout Bāla Kāṇḍa, we will read many stories which may or may not be directly related to the main story of Rāmāyaṇa. But they are the stories Rāma heard growing up.
1.9.1
एतच्छ्रुत्वा रहः सूतो
राजानमिदमब्रवीत् ।
ऋत्विग्भिरुपदिष्टोऽयम्
पुरावृत्तो मया श्रुतः ॥
Hearing what had been said, the charioteer (Sumantra) told the king in private: “There is an ancient legend, which I have also heard from the Ṛtviks”
1.9.2
सनत्कुमारो भगवान्
पूर्वं कथितवान् कथाम् ।
ऋषीणां सन्निधौ राजन्
तव पुत्रागमं प्रति ॥
Bhagawān Sanat Kumara, had predicted to the Ṛshis about the offspring that you would get in the future.
1.9.3
काश्यपस्य च पुत्रोऽस्ति
विभण्डक इति श्रुतः ।
ऋश्यशृङ्ग इति ख्यातः
तस्य पुत्रो भविष्यति ॥
Kāṡyapa has a son by name Vibhanḍaka, who is well known. He is going to have a son by name Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga, who will be well-known in his own right.
1.9.4
स वने नित्यसंवृद्धो
मुनिर्वनचरः सदा ।
नान्यं जानाति विप्रेन्द्रो
नित्यं पित्रनुवर्तनात् ॥
That Muni, one of the best Brāhmaṇas would live in the Vana along with his father and would not happen to come in contact with anyone else.
1.9.5
द्वैविध्यं ब्रह्मचर्यस्य
भविष्यति महात्मनः ।
लोकेषु प्रथितं राजन्
विप्रैश्च कथितं सदा ॥
He would be losing his innocence and serene celibacy that is praised by many Brāhmaṇas and noted by everyone in the world.
Phrases like 'known by everyone in the world', obviously should not be taken literally. It is the poetic 'everyone', meaning 'everyone indicated by the context'.
1.9.6
तस्यैवं वर्तमानस्य
कालः समभिवर्तत ।
अग्निं शुश्रूषमाणस्य
पितरं च यशस्विनम् ॥
He would be celibate for a long time spending time serving his renowned father and performing fire rituals.
1.9.7
एतस्मिन्नेव काले तु
रोमपादः प्रतापवान् ।
अङ्गेषु प्रथितो राजा
भविष्यति महाबलः ॥
There would be, at the same time, a valiant and strong king by name Rōmapāda, ruling the land of Aṅga.
1.9.8
तस्य व्यतिक्रमाद्राज्ञो
भविष्यति सुदारुणा ।
अनावृष्टिस्सुघोरा वै
सर्वभूतभयावहा ॥
It would happen that he transgresses the boundaries of Dharma and his kingdom would experience dreadful and devastating famine that grips every being in fear and despair.
1.9.9
अनावृष्ट्यां तु वृत्तायाम्
राजा दुःखसमन्वितः ।
ब्राह्मणान् श्रुतवृद्धांश्च
समानीय प्रवक्ष्यति ॥
The famine would make the king worried and he would request the presence of Brāhmaṇas, who are experts in Vēdas, and would ask:
1.9.10
भवन्तः श्रुतधर्माणो
लोकचारित्रवेदिनः ।
समादिशन्तु नियमम्
प्रायश्चित्तं यथा भवेत् ॥
"You are aware of many Dharmas and about the ways of the world. Please tell me if there is any remedial measure for this (famine)."
1.9.11
वक्ष्यन्ति ते महीपालम्
ब्राह्मणा वेदपारगाः ।
विभण्डकसुतं राजन्
सर्वोपायैरिहानय ॥
The Brāhmaṇas, experts in Vēdas, would advise the King, "Please bring the son of Vibhanḍaka here by some means."
1.9.12
आनाय्य च महीपाल
ऋश्यशृङ्गं सुसत्कृतम् ।
प्रयच्छ कन्यां शान्तां वै
विधिना सुसमाहितः ॥
O king! Bring Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga with all honors, and give your daughter Ṡānta in a customary manner and in all sincerity!"
1.9.13
तेषां तु वचनं श्रुत्वा
राजा चिन्तां प्रपत्स्यते ।
केनोपायेन वै शक्यम्
इहानेतुं स वीर्यवान् ॥
Upon hearing those words, the king would think about the means to bring that great person to his kingdom.
1.9.14
ततो राजा विनिश्चित्य
सह मन्त्रिभिरात्मवान् ।
पुरोहितममात्यांश्च
ततः प्रेष्यति सत्कृतान् ॥
After consulting his ministers, the king would ask the Purōhitas and the ministers to do accordingly.
1.9.15
ते तु राज्ञो वचः श्रुत्वा
व्यथिता विनताननाः ।
न गच्छेम ऋषेर्भीता
अनुनेष्यन्ति तं नृपम् ॥
They, being apprehensive and fearful of approaching Vibhanḍaka would beg their inability to carry on the task.
1.9.16
वक्ष्यन्ति चिन्तयित्वा ते
तस्योपायांश्च तत्क्षमान् ।
आनेष्यामो वयं विप्रम्
न च दोषो भविष्यति ॥
Then, they would think of an Upāya and tell the king that they would bring the Brāhmaṇa without any problem.
1.9.17
एवमङ्गाधिपेनैव
गणिकाभिर्ऋषेः सुतः ।
आनीतोऽवर्षयद्देवः
शान्ता चास्मै प्रदीयते ॥
With the help of courtesans, the king of the land of Aṅga will succeed in bringing the son of the Ṛshi. Ṡānta would be given to him in marriage, and the God of Rain would send showers.
1.9.18
ऋश्यशृङ्गस्तु जामाता
पुत्रान् तव विधास्यति ।
सनत्कुमारकथितम्
एतावद्व्याहृतं मया ॥
Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga, that son-in-law, will help you with having sons. So I heard as told by Sanat Kumara.
1.9.19
अथ हृष्टो दशरथः
सुमन्त्रं प्रत्यभाषत ।
यथर्श्यशृङ्गस्त्वानीतो
विस्तरेण त्वयोच्यताम् ॥
King Daṡaratha, felt very happy on hearing what Sumantra said, and asked him to expand on the details of how Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga was brought (to the kingdom of Aṅga).
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे नवमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
