Baala Kaanda - Sarga 44
This Sarga completes the episode of the descent of Gaṅgā. Lord Brahma showers profound praise on Bhageeratha for having accomplished the task of bringing down Gaṅgā from Heaven, in which his great ancestors like Sagara, Aṃṡumān and Dileepa failed.
We have made a note in the commentary of initial Sargas that Rāmāyaṇa is meant for listening, not reading. This Sarga is a very good example. If you read through it, you will find a great deal of repetition.
But if you hear it with the right emotion added to it by a story teller, you would feel it to be very gripping. This very same style and technique is used even in modern times such as in today's TV serials.
1.44.1
स गत्वा सागरं राजा
गङ्गयाऽनुगतस्तदा ।
प्रविवेश तलं भूमेः
यत्र ते भस्मसात्कृताः ॥
With Gaṅgā following him, the king (Bhageeratha) went to the ocean and then on to the netherworld, where his ancestors were lying as ashes.
1.44.2
भस्मन्यथाऽऽप्लुते राम
गङ्गायास्सलिलेन वै ।
सर्वलोकप्रभुर्ब्रह्मा
राजानमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
O Rāma, as the waters of Gaṅgā touched the ashes, The lords of all the worlds, Brahma, spoke to that king:
1.44.3
तारिता नरशार्दूल
दिवं याताश्च देववत् ।
षष्टिः पुत्रसहस्राणि
सगरस्य महात्मनः ॥
O tiger among men! The sixty thousand sons of Mahātma Sagara are now relieved and Have gone to Heaven, like Dēvas.
1.44.4
सागरस्य जलं लोके
यावत् स्थास्यति पार्थिव ।
सगरस्यात्मजास्तावत्
स्वर्गे स्थास्यन्ति देववत् ॥
O king! As long as there are waters in the oceans, so long will the sons of Sagara be in Heaven.
1.44.5
इयं हि दुहिता ज्येष्ठा
तव गङ्गा भविष्यति ।
त्वत्कृतेन च नाम्नाऽथ
लोके स्थास्यति विश्रुता ॥
Gaṅgā, hence onwards, will also be popularly known as your daughter ("Bhāgeerathi").
1.44.6
गङ्गा त्रिपथगा राजन्
दिव्या भागीरथीति च ।
त्रीन् पथो भावयन्तीति
ततस्त्रिपथगा स्मृता ॥
Gaṅgā will be known as Tripathagā and Bhāgeerathi, hence onwards. She will be called Tripathagā, since she flows through all the three paths.
1.44.7
पितामहानां सर्वेषाम्
त्वमत्र मनुजाधिप ।
कुरुष्व सलिलं राजन्
प्रतिज्ञामपवर्जय ॥
O lord of peoples! Offer these waters to all of your ancestors, to completely fulfill your mission.
1.44.8
पूर्वकेण हि ते राजन्
तेनातियशसा तदा ।
धर्मिणां प्रवरेणापि
नैष प्राप्तो मनोरथः ॥
O King! even your ancestor Sagara, who was well known and who was one of the best Dhārmikas could not accomplish what you have accomplished.
1.44.9
तथैवांशुमता तात
लोकेऽप्रतिमतेजसा ।
गङ्गां प्रार्थयता नेतुम्
प्रतिज्ञा नापवर्जिता ॥
O dear, even Aṃṡumān, the supremely radiant could not accomplish the dream of bringing Gaṅgā.
1.44.10
राजर्षिणा गुणवता
महर्षिसमतेजसा ।
मत्तुल्यतपसा चैव
क्षत्रधर्मस्थितेन च ।
दिलीपेन महाभाग
तव पित्रातितेजसा ।
पुनर्न शङ्किता नेतुम्
गङ्गां प्रार्थयताऽनघ ॥
O unaffiliated one! even your father Dileepa, who had done Tapa comparable to that of mine, who was of all desirable qualities, whose radiance equaled that of a Maharshi and who was firmly established in discharging the responsibilities expected of a King could not fulfill his dream of bringing Gaṅgā.
1.44.12
सा त्वया समतिक्रान्ता
प्रतिज्ञा पुरुषर्षभ ।
प्राप्तोऽसि परमं लोके
यशः परमसम्मतम् ॥
O bull among men! Only you could accomplish that mission and thereby you became well known in this world, which you deserve to be.
1.44.13
यच्च गङ्गावतरणम्
त्वया कृतमरिन्दम ।
अनेन च भवान् प्राप्तो
धर्मस्यायतनं महत् ॥
O tormentor of detractors! By fulfilling the mission of bringing Gaṅgā, you attained the distinction of attaining a great Dharma.
Dharma also means discharging a well-meaning responsibility.
1.44.14
प्लावयस्व त्वमात्मानम्
नरोत्तम सदोचिते ।
सलिले पुरुषव्याघ्र
शुचिः पुण्यफलो भव ॥
O best among humans! O tiger among men! Always take a dip in these waters and thereby stay clean, and may everything auspicious be bestowed on you!
1.44.15
पितामहानां सर्वेषाम्
कुरुष्व सलिलक्रियाम् ।
स्वस्ति तेऽस्तु गमिष्यामि
स्वं लोकं गम्यतां नृप ॥
O King, Offer these waters to all of your ancestors. May all bode well for you! I shall take leave now. May you return to your world.
1.44.16
इत्येवमुक्त्वा देवेशः
सर्वलोकपितामहः ।
यथागतं तथागच्छत्
देवलोकं महायशाः ॥
Saying thus, the Progenitor of all worlds, the lord of all the Dēvas, and the one known through all the worlds, returned to the Dēva Lōka.
1.44.17
भगीरथोऽपि राजर्षिः
कृत्वा सलिलमुत्तमम् ।
यथाक्रमं यथान्यायम्
सागराणां महायशाः ॥
The highly renowned Rājarshi Bhageeratha offered waters following the right process and procedures to that well known sons of Sagara.
1.44.18
कृतोदकश्शुची राजा
स्वपुरं प्रविवेश ह ।
समृद्धार्थो नरश्रेष्ठ
स्वराज्यं प्रशशास ह ॥
Having offered the waters and having fulfilled his mission, the king cleansed himself and entered his city and ruled his kingdom, as usual.
1.44.19
प्रमुमोद च लोकस्तम्
नृपमासाद्य राघव ।
नष्टशोकस्समृद्धार्थो
बभूव विगतज्वरः ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty, The king overcame his agony and sadness after accomplishing the mission and the entire world felt happy having him as the king.
1.44.20
एष ते राम गङ्गाया
विस्तरोऽभिहितो मया ।
स्वस्ति प्राप्नुहि भद्रं ते
सन्ध्याकालोऽतिवर्तते ॥
O Rāma! I have now narrated the story of Gaṅgā in detail. May all bode well and safe for you! I now see that it is a little past dusk.
1.44.21
धन्यं यशस्यमायुष्यम्
पुत्र्यं स्वर्ग्यमतीव च ।
यश्श्रावयति विप्रेषु
क्षत्रियेष्वितरेषु च ।
प्रीयन्ते पितरस्तस्य
प्रीयन्ते दैवतानि च ॥
Listening to this story brings riches, long life, great progeny and ascent to Heaven at the end. The ancestors and Dēvas would be happy with any one who narrates this story to Brāhmaṇas, Kshatriyas and all others.
1.44.22
इदमाख्यानमव्यग्रो
गङ्गावतरणं शुभम् ।
यश्शृणोति च काकुत्स्थ
सर्वान् कामानवाप्नुयात् ।
सर्वे पापाः प्रणश्यन्ति
आयुः कीर्तिश्च वर्द्धते ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! Whoever hears this story of the descent of Gaṅgā with full attention will realize all their wishes, will be rid of all Pāpas and will enjoy long life and good reputation!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे चतुश्चत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty fourth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
