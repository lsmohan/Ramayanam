Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 26
In this Sarga, Hanumān seeks Rāma’s permission for Sugreeva’s consecration to the throne in Kishkindhā, and he invites Rāma to come to Kishkindhā. Rāma says that he would not enter any city or village for fourteen years in accordance with the command of his father.
Rāma tells Sugreeva to install Aṅgada as crown prince. He also tells Sugreeva that he should take all effort to kill Rāvaṇa once the monsoon season, which had just started, comes to an end.
Sugreeva goes back to Kishkindhā and his friends consecrate him on the throne in a regal and customary manner. Everyone hails him upon assuming office. Sugreeva then consecrates Aṅgada as the crown prince and all sympathizers of Aṅgada feel happy.
Everyone praises Rāma and Lakshmaṇa repeatedly. Sugreeva informs Rāma of the completion of consecration and having regained Rumā, enjoys the kingdom.
4.26.1
ततश्शोकाभिसन्तप्तम्
सुग्रीवं क्लिन्नवाससम् ।
शाखामृगमहामात्राः
परिवार्योपतस्थिरे ॥
Then the great ministers of the beasts of the branches stood near and around Sugreeva who was burnt by grief and clad in wet clothes.
Vānaras, the monkeys are also referred to as the beasts of the branches, because they live on tree branches.
4.26.2
अभिगम्य महाबाहुम्
राममक्लिष्टकारिणम् ।
स्थिताः प्राञ्जलयस्सर्वे
पितामहमिवर्षयः ॥
Approaching Rāma of mighty arm who accomplished everything with ease, they stood with palms joined in reverence like Ṛshis around (Brahma), the grandsire.
4.26.3
ततः काञ्चनशैलाभः
तरुणार्कनिभाननः ।
अब्रवीत्प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यम्
हनूमान्मारुतात्मजः ॥
Then Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, who shone like a golden mountain, his face looking like the morning sun, spoke these words with palms joined in reverence:
4.26.4
भवत्प्रसादात्सुग्रीवः
पितृपैतामहं महत् ।
वानराणाम् सुदंष्ट्राणाम्
संपन्न बलशालिनाम् ।
महात्मानाम् सुदुष्प्रापम्
प्राप्तो राज्यमिदं प्रभो ॥
By your grace, Sugreeva obtained this rich kingdom of great and mighty strong Vānaras of imposing fangs, that came down from father to son, which would have otherwise been near impossible to obtain.
4.26.5
भवता समनुज्ञातः
प्रविश्य नगरं शुभम् ।
संविधास्यति कार्याणि
सर्वाणि ससुहृज्जनः ।
स्नातोऽयं विविधैर्गन्धैः
ओषधैश्च यथाविधि ॥
With your kind and full permission he shall enter the blessed city and after taking the (cleansing) bath with many herbs and fragrances in a customary manner, he shall attend to all the (royal) affairs.
4.26.7
अर्चयिष्यति रत्नैश्च
माल्यैश्च त्वां विशेषतः ।
इमां गिरिगुहां रम्याम्
अभिगन्तुं त्वमर्हसि ।
कुरुष्व स्वामिसम्बन्धम्
वानरान्सम्प्रहर्षय ॥
He shall honor you especially, with garlands and gems. You should come to this beautiful (city of) mountain caverns and make all the Vānaras happy by giving them a ruler to govern them.
4.26.8
एवमुक्तो हनुमता
राघवः परवीरहा ।
प्रत्युवाच हनूमन्तम्
बुद्धिमान्वाक्यकोविदः ॥
Thus requested by Hanumān, the sagacious Rāghava, destroyer of enemy warriors, who was good with his choice of words, responded to Hanumān, saying:
4.26.9
चतुर्दश समास्सौम्य
ग्रामं वा यदि वा पुरम् ।
न प्रवेक्ष्यामि हनुमन्
पितुर्निर्देशपालकः ॥
O Hanumān! O gentle one! Abiding by the word of my father, I will not enter a city or village for fourteen years.
4.26.10
सुसमृद्धां गुहां रम्याम्
सुग्रीवो वानरर्षभः ।
प्रविष्टो विधिवद्वीरः
क्षिप्रं राज्येऽभिषिच्यताम् ॥
May Sugreeva, the Veera, the bull among Vānaras, enter the splendid and affluent cave city without any delay and be crowned in the kingdom in a customary manner!
4.26.11
एवमुक्त्वा हनूमन्तम्
रामस्सुग्रीवमब्रवीत् ।
वृत्तज्ञो वृत्तसम्पन्नम्
उदारबलविक्रमम् ॥
Responding thus to Hanumān, Rāma, who knew the ways of the world, turned to Sugreeva of laudable character and immense strength and prowess and said:
4.26.12
इममप्यङ्गदं वीर
यौवराज्येऽभिषेचय ।
ज्येष्ठस्य स सुतो ज्येष्ठः
सदृशो विक्रमेण ते ।
अङ्गदोऽयमदीनात्मा
यौवराज्यस्य भाजनम् ॥
O Veera! Also, install this Aṅgada as the crown prince. He is the eldest son of your elder brother and is an equal to him in prowess. He is no weakling and is perfectly fit to be the crown prince.
4.26.14
पूर्वोऽयं वार्षिको मासः
श्रावणस्सलिलागमः ।
प्रवृत्तास्सौम्य चत्वारो
मासा वार्षिकसज्ञिकाः ॥
O gentle one! This is the month of Ṡrāvaṇa, and with this, the wet four month long monsoon season starts.
4.26.15
नायमुद्योगसमयः
प्रविश त्वं पुरीं शुभाम् ।
अस्मिन्वत्स्याम्यहं सौम्य
पर्वते सह लक्ष्मणः ॥
This is not a time for expeditions. O gentle one! You go to the blessed city and I shall live on this mountain along with Lakshmaṇa!
4.26.16
इयं गिरिगुहा रम्या
विशाला युक्तमारुता ।
प्रभूतसलिला सौम्य
प्रभूतकमलोत्पला ॥
This well ventilated mountain cave, my dear, is beautiful and spacious. There is an abundance of water here and also an abundance of lotuses and lilies.
4.26.17
कार्तिके समनुप्राप्ते
त्वं रावणवधे यत ।
एष नस्समयस्सौम्य
प्रविश त्वं स्वमालयम् ।
अभिषिंचस्व राज्ये च
सुहृदस्सम्प्रहर्षय ॥
You shall take all efforts to kill Rāvaṇa as soon as the month of Kārteeka sets in! My dear, let this be our agreement. (For now,) go to your place, get crowned in the kingdom and make all your friends rejoice!
4.26.18
इति रामाभ्यनुज्ञातः
सुग्रीवो वानराधिपः ।
प्रविवेश पुरीं रम्याम्
किष्किन्धां वालिपालिताम् ॥
Thus given leave by Rāma, Sugreeva, the bull among Vānaras, entered the beautiful city of Kishkindhā that Vali had ruled once.
4.26.19
तं वानरसहस्राणि
प्रविष्टं वानरेश्वरम् ।
अभिवाद्य प्रविष्टानि
सर्वतः पर्यवारयन् ॥
Saluting that Lord of Vānaras as he entered (the city) thousands and thousands of Vānaras also entered, gathering around him in every direction.
4.26.20
ततः प्रकृतयस्सर्वा
दृष्ट्वा हरिगणेश्वरम् ।
प्रणम्य मूर्ध्ना पतिता
वसुधायां समाहिताः ॥
All the estates of the realm, on seeing that Lord of the Vānaras, fell to the ground and bowed their heads sincerely.
4.26.21
सुग्रीवः प्रकृतीस्सर्वाः
सम्भाष्योत्थाप्य वीर्यवान् ।
भ्रातुरन्तःपुरं सौम्यम्
प्रविवेश महाबलः ॥
The valiant and strong Sugreeva raised them up and greeted them. He then entered the pleasant private apartments of his brother.
4.26.22
प्रविश्य त्वभिनिष्क्रान्तम्
सुग्रीवं वानरर्षभम् ।
अभ्यषिञ्चन्त सुहृदः
सहस्राक्षमिवामराः ॥
When Sugreeva, the bull among Vānaras came out, his friends crowned him as the Dēvas had crowned the Thousand-eyed (Indra).
4.26.23
तस्य पाण्डुरमाजह्रुः
छत्रं हेमपरिष्कृतम् ।
शुक्ले च वालव्यजने
हेमदण्डे यशस्करे ॥
They brought him a white umbrella decked with gold, and two white fly-whisks with golden handles, which would enhance his fame.
4.26.24
तथा सर्वाणि रत्नानि
सर्वबीजौषधीरपि ।
सक्षीराणां च वृक्षाणाम्
प्ररोहान्कुसुमानि च ।
शुक्लानि चैव वस्त्राणि
श्वेतं चैवानुलेपनम् ।
सुगन्धीनि च माल्यानि
स्थलजान्यम्बुजानि च ।
चन्दनानि च दिव्यानि
गन्धांश्च विविधान्बहून् ।
अक्षतं जातरूपं च
प्रियङ्गुमधुसर्पिषी ।
दधि चर्म च वैयाघ्रम्
वाराही चाप्युपानहौ ।
समालम्भनमादाय
गोरोचनं मनश्शिलाम् ।
आजग्मुस्तत्र मुदिता
वराः कन्यास्तु षोडश ॥
Then came sixteen lovely maids rejoicing, bringing with them gems, seeds and herbs of every kind, tender shoots of trees with milky sap, flowers, white garments, white unguents, fragrant garlands, land-lotuses, heavenly sandal, various kinds of perfumes, rice, gold, the Priyaṅgu seed, honey, ghee, curds, a tiger-skin, a pair of sandals made of boar-skin, cleansing powder, Gōrōcanā and orpiment.
Gōrōcanā is bright yellow prepared from the bile of cattle.
4.26.29
ततस्ते वानरश्रेष्ठम्
अभिषेक्तुं यथाविधि ।
रत्नैर्वस्त्रैश्च भक्ष्यैश्च
तोषयित्वा द्विजर्षभान् ॥
Then, after honoring the foremost of Brāhmaṇas with gems, garments and food, they crowned the great Vānara in a customary manner.
4.26.30
ततः कुशपरिस्तीर्णम्
समिद्धं जातवेदसम् ।
मन्त्रपूतेन हविषा
हुत्वा मन्त्रविदो जनाः ॥
Then the experts in Mantras poured oblations purified by Mantras into the blazing fire around which Darbha grass was placed.
4.26.31
ततो हेमप्रतिष्ठाने
वरास्तरणसंवृते ।
प्रासादशिखरे रम्ये
चित्रमाल्योपशोभिते ।
प्राङ्मुखं विविधैर्मन्त्रैः
स्थापयित्वा वरासने ।
नदीनदेभ्यस्संहृत्य
तीर्थेभ्यश्च समन्ततः ।
आहृत्य च समुद्रेभ्यः
सर्वेभ्यो वानरर्षभाः ।
अपः कनककुम्भेषु
निधाय विमलाश्शुभाः ।
शुभैर्वृषभशृङ्गैश्च
कलशैश्चापि काञ्चनैः ।
शास्त्रदृष्टेन विधिना
महर्षिविहितेन च ।
गजो गवाक्षो गवयः
शरभो गन्धमादनः ।
मैन्दश्च द्विविदश्चैव
हनुमान्जाम्बवान्नलः ।
अभ्यषिञ्चन्त सुग्रीवम्
प्रसन्नेन सुगन्धिना ।
सलिलेन सहस्राक्षम्
वसवो वासवं यथा ॥
Then they seated him facing east on an excellent seat with golden legs, on the terrace of a multi-storied mansion that was beautiful, covered in magnificent spreads, and splendid, decked with an assortment of garlands.
Then all the bulls among Vānaras such as Gaja, Gavāksha, Gavaya, Ṡarabha, Gandhamādana, Mainda, Dwivida, Hanumān, Jāmbavān and Nala consecrated Sugreeva, like Vasus did Vāsava, with clear, pleasant and fragrant water obtained from water sources from everywhere including from rivers flowing east and rivers flowing west and from the ocean, filled in golden pots and poured through the auspicious horns of great bulls as prescribed in the Sastras and as approved by the Maharshis.
4.26.37
अभिषिक्ते तु सुग्रीवे
सर्वे वानरपुङ्गवाः ।
प्रचुक्रुशुर्महात्मानो
हृष्टास्तत्र सहस्रशः ॥
When Sugreeva was crowned, the great bulls among Vānaras, hundreds of thousands of them, shouted with joy.
4.26.38
रामस्य तु वचः कुर्वन्
सुग्रीवो हरिपुङ्गवः ।
अङ्गदं सम्परिष्वज्य
यौवराज्येऽभ्यषेचयत् ॥
Sugreeva, a bull among Vānaras, then embraced Aṅgada and consecrated him as crown prince in accordance with the word of Rāma.
4.26.39
अङ्गदे चाभिषिक्ते तु
सानुक्रोशाः प्लवङ्गमाः ।
साधु साध्विति सुग्रीवम्
महात्मानोऽभ्यपूजयन् ॥
When Aṅgada was thus crowned, the mighty Vānaras, his sympathizers, praised Sugreeva, crying ‘Well done, well done!’
4.26.40
रामं चैव महात्मानम्
लक्ष्मणं च पुनः पुनः ।
प्रीताश्च तुष्टुवुस्सर्वे
तादृशे तत्र वर्तिनि ॥
As the activities proceeded thus, all of them extolled Rāma, the Mahātma, and Lakshmaṇa repeatedly.
4.26.41
हृष्टपुष्टजनाकीर्णा
पताकाध्वजशोभिता ।
बभूव नगरी रम्या
किष्किन्धा गिरिगह्वरे ॥
The beautiful city of Kishkindhā, in the hollow of the hills, adorned with banners and flag posts, was crowded with people who were filled with joy.
4.26.42
निवेद्य रामाय तदा महात्मने
महाभिषेकं कपिवाहिनीपतिः ।
रुमां च भार्यं प्रतिलभ्य वीर्यवान्
अवाप राज्यं त्रिदशाधिपो यथा ॥
Informing Rāma the Mahātma, of his coronation, and recovering his wife Rumā, that valiant leader of the Vānara army (enjoyed) the kingdom he regained, like Indra.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे षड्विंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty sixth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
