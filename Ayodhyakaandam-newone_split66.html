Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 66
In this Sarga, Kousalyā takes her dead husband's head on her lap and cries. She and other women of the inner quarters bewail their fate of having to put up with Kaikēyee, being widowed and of Rāma having gone to the Vana.
The ministers decide to keep the body of the king in an oil tub, and not hold the funeral immediately, as none of his sons is present.
2.66.1
तमग्निमिव संशान्तम्
अम्बुहीनमिवार्णवम् ।
हतप्रभमिवाऽऽदित्यम्
स्वर्गस्थं प्रेक्ष्य पार्थिवम् ।
कौसल्या बाष्पपूर्णाक्षी
विविधां शोककर्शिता ।
उपगृह्य शिरो राज्ञः
कैकेयीं प्रत्यभाषत ॥
Looking at the King who ascended to Heaven, who looked like a fire that has died down, like an ocean that is emptied of its water and like sun that has lost its fiery splendor, Kousalyā, drained by grief and with eyes full of tears took the King's head on her lap and spoke these words to Kaikēyee:
2.66.3
सकामा भव कैकेयि
भुङ्क्ष्व राज्यमकण्टकम् ।
त्यक्त्वा राजानमेकाग्रा
नृशंसे दुष्टचारिणि ॥
O cruel and wicked Kaikēyee! Your wish is fulfilled. You can now put your concerns regarding the King behind, and enjoy the kingdom, with nothing in your way!
2.66.4
विहाय मां गतो रामः
भर्ता च स्वर्गतो मम ।
विपथे सार्थहीनेव
नाहं जीवितुमुत्सहे ॥
Rāma left me and went away. My husband also left for Heaven. I am left in the middle of nowhere, with no purpose whatsoever. I have no penchant for living any more.
2.66.5
भर्तारं तं परित्यज्य
का स्त्री दैवतमात्मनः ।
इच्छेज्जीवितुमन्यत्र
कैकेय्यास्त्यक्तधर्मणः ॥
Which woman, except Kaikēyee who has given up Dharma, wishes to live abandoning her husband who is like a god to her?
2.66.6
न लुब्धो बुध्यते दोषान्
किम्पाकमिव भक्षयन् ।
कुब्जानिमित्तं कैकेय्या
राघवाणां कुलं हतम् ॥
Like a miser who, feeding on unwholesome food, does not realize the harm it will do him, Kaikēyee has ruined the family of the Ikshwākus because of a hunchback.
2.66.7
अनियोगे नियुक्तेन
राज्ञा रामं विवासितम् ।
सभार्यं जनकश्श्रुत्वा
परितप्स्यत्यहं यथा ॥
Janaka also would lament just as I do, hearing that Rāma along with his wife was sent in exile by the king, which he should not have been.
2.66.8
स मामनाथां विधवाम्
नाद्य जानाति धार्मिकः ।
रामः कमलपत्राक्षः
जीवन्नाशमितो गतः ॥
He (Rāma), the Dhārmika, the one with eyes like lotus petals, who has gone from here would not know that my life is ruined and that I have become a widow now!
2.66.9
विदेहराजस्य सुता
तथा सीता तपस्विनी ।
दुःखस्यानुचिता दुःखम्
वने पर्युद्विजिष्यति ॥
Seetā, the diligent one, the daughter of the King of Vidēha, who was not made for grief, will cry in the Vana, terrified.
2.66.10
नदतां भीमघोषाणाम्
निशासु मृगपक्षिणाम् ।
निशम्य नूनं सन्त्रस्ता
राघवं संश्रयिष्यति ॥
Hearing the fierce cries of birds and beasts at night, she will, in terror, take refuge in Rāghava.
2.66.11
वृद्धश्चैवाल्पपुत्रश्च
वैदेहीमनुचिन्तयन् ।
सोऽपि शोकसमाविष्टो
ननु त्यक्ष्यति जीवितम् ॥
He (Janaka) also, being old and sonless, would certainly give up his life grieving and thinking of the princess of Vidēha!
2.66.12
साऽहमद्यैव दिष्टान्तम्
गमिष्यामि पतिव्रता ।
इदं शरीर मालिङ्ग्य
प्रवेक्ष्यामि हुताशनम् ॥
Being who I am, the one devoted to my husband, I shall right away court death, embracing this body and enter into the fire.
2.66.13
तां ततस्सम्परिष्वज्य
विलपन्तीं तपस्विनीम् ।
व्यपनीय सुदुःखार्ताम्
कौसल्यां व्यावहारिकाः ॥
The attendants gently led Kousalyā away, who was suffering and lamenting in grief, embracing the body there.
2.66.14
तैलद्रोण्यामथामात्या
सम्वेश्य जगतीपतिम् ।
राज्ञस्सर्वाण्यथादिष्टाः
चक्रुः कर्माण्यनन्तरम् ॥
The ministers then placed the lord of the lands in a tub filled with oil and did everything as needed thereafter as per instructions.
2.66.15
न तु सङ्कलनं राज्ञो
विना पुत्रेण मन्त्रिणः ।
सर्वज्ञाः कर्तुमीषुस्ते
ततो रक्षन्ति भूमिपम् ॥
The Ministers, who were aware of everything, did not want to hold the funeral in the absence of his sons. So they preserved the body of the King.
2.66.16
तैलद्रोण्यां तु सचिवैः
शायितं तं नराधिपम् ।
हा मृतोऽयमिति ज्ञात्वा
स्त्रियस्ताः पर्यदेवयन् ॥
Seeing the King placed in the oil tub by the ministers, and the death of the King thus confirmed, the women wailed, 'Alas, He is dead!'
2.66.17
बाहूनुद्यम्य कृपणाः
नेत्रप्रस्रवणैर्मुखैः ।
रुदन्त्यश्शोकसन्तप्ताः
कृपणं पर्यदेवयन् ॥
The wretched women, with arms raised, and tears streaming down their faces as they wept, made piteous lament, tormented by grief, saying:
2.66.18
हा महाराज रामेण
सततं प्रियवादिना ।
विहीनास्सत्यसन्धेन
किमर्थं विजहासि नः ॥
O supreme King! Why have you abandoned us! We are already bereft of Rāma, who is always sweet-spoken and vowed to truth!
2.66.19
कैकेय्या दुष्टभावायाः
राघवेण वियोजिताः ।
कथं पतिघ्न्या वत्स्यामः
समीपे विधवा वयम् ॥
How can we, widowed and distanced from Rāma, live with the wicked Kaikēyee who killed her husband?
2.66.20
स हि नाथस्सदास्माकम्
तव च प्रभुरात्मवान् ।
वनं रामो गतश्श्रीमान्
विहाय नृपतिश्रियम् ॥
The sensible Rāma, who is the lord and master of us as well as you, has gone to the Vana giving up the opulence of sovereignty.
2.66.21
त्वया तेन च वीरेण
विना व्यसनमोहिताः ।
कथं वयं निवत्स्यामः
कैकेय्या च विदूषिताः ॥
How can we, taken over by sorrow, live without you and him, the Veera, enduring the abuses of Kaikēyee?
2.66.22
यया तु राजा रामश्च
लक्ष्मणश्च महाबलः ।
सीतया सह सन्त्यक्ताः
साकमन्यं न हास्यति ॥
Whom will she spare, she, the one who abandoned the King, Rāma and the mighty Lakshmaṇa, along with Seetā?'
Kaikēyee's actions caught everyone unawares. Hence all the other women of the king were genuinely apprehensive of what would come next.
2.66.23
ता बाष्पेण च संवीताः
शोकेन विपुलेन च ।
व्यवेष्टन्त निरानन्दा
राघवस्य वरस्त्रियः ॥
Thus lamenting, the noble women of Rāghava, overwhelmed by endless grief, convulsed on the floor cheerlessly.
2.66.24
निशा चन्द्रविहीनेव
स्त्रीव भर्तृविवर्जिता ।
पुरी नाराजतायोध्या
हीना राज्ञा महात्मना ॥
The city of Ayōdhyā, without the Mahātma King, was gloomy like a night without the moon and like a woman abandoned by her husband.
Vālmeeki treats Ayōdhyā as an equally important character of the story by itself. (It is unusual to see so much emphasis on the state of a City, in any other work, of ancient or modern times).
Rāma's (and also that of all the kings of Ikshwāku dynasty) commitment to Ayōdhyā is absolute and is as strong as that of any conscience head of a household to his household.
Ayōdhyā, as narrated by Vālmeeki, convulsed as much as or even more than anyone else, including the members of the Royal family, under the calamity that had befallen.
It is not for any simple reason that this Kāṇḍa  is named as Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa.
2.66.25
बाष्पपर्याकुलजना
हाहाभूतकुलाङ्गना ।
शून्यचत्वरवेश्मान्ता
न बभ्राज यथापुरम् ॥
With its people distressed and in tears, its housewives loudly wailing, its squares and house-fronts empty, the city did not have any semblance of its usual luster.
2.66.26
गते तु शोकात्त्रिदिवं नराधिपे
महीतलस्थासु नृपाङ्गनासु च ।
निवृत्तचारस्सहसा गतो रविः
प्रवृत्तचारा रजनी ह्युपस्थिता ॥
With the King gone to Heaven out of grief and the King's women lying on the ground, the sun withdrew from his sojourn and left, and the night set in, letting the nocturnal beings out.
2.66.27
ऋते तु पुत्राद्दहनं महीपते-
र्नरोचयन्ते सुहृदस्समागताः ।
इतीव तस्मिन् शयने न्यवेशयन्
वनिचिन्त्य राजानमचिन्त्य दर्शनम् ॥
Considering the fact that friends and everyone gathered would not like the funeral of the lord of the lands to be held without a son being present, they left the King on the bed, 'albeit in an unimaginable state.'
2.66.28
गतप्रभा द्यौरिव भास्करं विना
व्यपेतनक्षत्रगणेव शर्वरी ।
पुरी बभासे रहिता महात्मना
न चास्रकण्ठाऽकुलमार्गचत्वरा ॥
With its luster gone, like that of the sky without the sun and like that of the night without the moon, the city lost its glow without the Mahātma, with its pathways and square choked with tears.
2.66.29
नराश्च नार्यश्च समेत्य सङ्घशः
विगर्हमाणा भरतस्य मातरम् ।
तदा नगर्यां नरदेवसङ्क्षये
बभूवुरार्ता न च शर्म लेभिरे ॥
At the passing of the King, men and women of the city, distressed and unhappy, gathered in crowds condemning the mother of Bharata.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे षट्षष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty sixth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
