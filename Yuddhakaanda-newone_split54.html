Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 54
In this Sarga, the fierce battle between the Vānaras under the command of Aṅgada, and Rākshasas under the command of Vajradaṃshṭra continues. Then they both fight with each other, Vajradaṃshṭra hitting Aṅgada with thousands of arrows and Aṅgada hitting Vajradaṃshṭra with rocks and trees. Aṅgada kills Vajradaṃshṭra and the Rākshasas flee to Laṅkā, terrified and in shame.
6.54.1
स्वबलस्य च घातेन
अङ्गदस्य जयेन च ।
राक्षसः क्रोधमाविष्टो
वज्रदंष्ट्रो महाबलः ॥
With the destruction of his forces and the victory of Aṅgada, Vajradaṃshṭra, that Rākshasa of immense strength, was enraged.
6.54.2
स विस्फार्य धनुर्घोरम्
शक्राशनिसमप्रभम् ।
वानराणामनीकानि
प्राकिरच्छरवृष्टिभिः ॥
He tied the string of his formidable bow, which shone like the thunderbolt of Indra, and rained arrows on the Vānara forces.
6.54.3
राक्षसाश्चापि मुख्यास्ते
रथेषु समवस्थिताः ।
नानाप्रहरणाः शूराः
प्रायुद्ध्यन्त तदा रणे ॥
And the other notable Rākshasas also, seated in their own chariots, fought heroically, wielding a variety of weaponry.
6.54.4
वानराणां तु शूरा ये
सर्वे ते प्लवगर्षभाः ।
आयुद्ध्यन्त शिलाहस्ताः
समवेताः समन्ततः ॥
The heroic bulls among Vānaras, armed with rocks, put up a consolidated fight everywhere.
6.54.5
तत्रायुधसहस्राणि
तस्मिन्नायोधने भृशम् ।
राक्षसाः कपिमुख्येषु
पातयाञ्चक्रिरे तदा ॥
Then the Rākshasas hurled thousands of weapons on the notable Vānaras in that battle.
6.54.6
वानराश्चापि रक्षस्सु
गिरीन् वृक्षान् महाशिलाः ।
प्रवीराः पातयामासुः
मत्तवारणसन्निभाः ॥
And the extremely valiant Vānaras, who were imposing like elephants in rut, hurled rocks, trees and huge boulders on the Rākshasas.
6.54.7
शूराणां युद्ध्यमानानाम्
समरेष्वनिवर्तिनाम् ।
तद्राक्षसगणानां च
सुयुद्धं समवर्तत ॥
With the host of brave Rākshasas who never turned their back in a battle also fighting relentlessly, it turned out to be one of the greatest clashes.
6.54.8
प्रभिन्नशिरसः केचित्
भिन्नैः पादैश्च बाहुभिः ।
शस्त्रैररदितदेहास्तु
रुधिरेण समुक्षिताः ।
हरयो राक्षसाश्चैव
शेरते गां समाश्रिताः ।
कङ्कगृध्रबलाढ्याश्च
गोमायुकुलसङ्कुलाः ॥
Some of the Vānaras and Rākshasas, their heads broken, hands and feet mangled and bodies drenched in blood pounded by Ṡastras, lay hugging the ground, which was encircled by vultures and eagles and crowded by packs of wolves.
6.54.10
कबन्धानि समुत्पेतुः
भीरुणां भीषणानि वै ।
भुजपाणिशिरश्छिन्नाः
छिन्नकायाश्च भूतले ।
वानरा राक्षसाश्चापि
निपेतुस्तत्र वै रणे ॥
Headless trunks flew around frightening the weak hearted. Mangled bodies of Vānaras and Rākshasas with severed arms, hands and heads fell there in that battle on the ground.
6.54.11
ततो वानरसैन्येन
हन्यमानं निशाचरम् ।
प्राभज्यत बलं सर्वम्
वज्रदंष्ट्रस्य पश्यतः ॥
With the Night-rovers decimated by the Vānara army, the entire Rākshasa force broke down while Vajradaṃshṭra was watching.
6.54.12
राक्षसान् भयवित्रस्तान्
हन्यमानान् प्लवङ्गमैः ।
दृष्ट्वा स रोषताम्राक्षो
वज्रदंष्ट्रः प्रतापवान् ।
प्रविवेश धनुष्पाणिः
त्रासयन् हरिवाहिनीम् ॥
On seeing the Rākshasas decimated and terrorized by the Vānaras the fierce Vajradaṃshṭra, armed with the bow, charged into the Vānara army, putting fear into it.
6.54.14
शरैर्विदारयामास
कङ्कपत्रैरजिह्मगैः ।
बिभेद वानरांस्तत्र
सप्ताष्टौ नव पञ्च च ।
विव्याध परमक्रुद्धो
वज्रदंष्ट्रः प्रतापवान् ॥
He rent the Vānaras with arrows that flew straight, propelled by feathers of Kaṅka bird. The powerful Vajradaṃshṭra, extremely outraged, struck down the Vānaras, seven, eight, nine and five at a time.
6.54.15
त्रस्ताः सर्वे हरिगणाः
शरैः सङ्कृत्तदेहिनः ।
अङ्गदं सम्प्रधावन्ति
प्रजापतिमिव प्रजाः ॥
Their bodies rent by the arrows, the terrified Vānaras fled seeking Aṅgada’s protection, as men seeking refuge in the Creator.
6.54.16
ततो हरिगणान् भग्नान्
दृष्ट्वा वालिसुतस्तदा ।
क्रोधेन वज्रदंष्ट्रं तम्
उदीक्षन्तमुदैक्षत ॥
Seeing the Vānara warriors shattered, the son of Vāli cast a glance in his wrath, at Vajradaṃshṭra who was doing the same.
6.54.17
वज्रदंष्ट्रोऽङ्गदश्चोभौ
योयुध्येते परस्परम् ।
चेरतुः परमक्रुद्धौ
हरिमत्तगजाविव ॥
Aṅgada and Vajradaṃshṭra fought with each other in rage like a lion and an elephant in rut.
6.54.18
ततः शरसहस्रेण
वालिपुत्रं महाबलः ।
जघान मर्मदेशेषु
मातङ्गमिव तोमरैः ॥
The son of Vāli was struck in the vitals with thousands of arrows by Vajradaṃshṭra of immense strength as an elephant by Tōmaras.
6.54.19
रुधिरोक्षितसर्वाङ्गो
वालिसूनुर्महाबलः ।
चिक्षेप वज्रदंष्ट्राय
वृक्षं भीमपराक्रमः ॥
His whole body covered with blood, the son of Vāli of immense strength hurled a tree at Vajradaṃshṭra, showing his formidable prowess.
6.54.20
दृष्ट्वाऽऽपतन्तं तं वृक्षम्
असम्भ्रान्तश्च राक्षसः ।
चिच्छेद बहुधा सोऽपि
निकृत्तः पतितो भुवि ॥
Seeing the tree about to hit him, the Rākshasa, unruffled, cut it to many pieces, and it fell down on the ground.
6.54.21
तं दृष्ट्वा वज्रदंष्ट्रस्य
विक्रमं प्लवगर्षभः ।
प्रगृह्य विपुलं शैलम्
चिक्षेप च ननाद च ॥
On seeing the power of Vajradaṃshṭra that bull among Vānaras snatched a huge rock and flung it at him with a roar.
6.54.22
समापतन्तं तं दृष्ट्वा
रथादाप्लुत्य वीर्यवान् ।
गदापाणिरसम्भ्रान्तः
पृथिव्यां समतिष्ठत ॥
Seeing it fly towards him, the doughty warrior jumped off his chariot and stood unperturbed on the ground with a mace in his hand.
6.54.23
अङ्गदेन शिलाक्षिप्ता
गत्वा तु रणमूर्धनि ।
सचक्रकूबरं साश्वम्
प्रममाथ रथं तदा ॥
The rock hurled by Aṅgada fell on the chariot in the battlefield smashing it, wheels, yoke, horses and all.
6.54.24
ततोऽन्यं गिरिमाक्षिप्य
विपुलं द्रुमभूषितम् ।
वज्रदंष्ट्रस्य शिरसि
पातयामास सोऽङ्गदः ॥
Then, Aṅgada lodged another huge hillock along with the trees on it on the head of Vajradaṃshṭra.
6.54.25
अभवच्छोणितोद्गारी
वज्रदंष्ट्रः स मूर्च्छितः ।
मुहूर्तमभवन्मूढो
गदामालिङ्ग्य निःश्वसन् ॥
Vajradaṃshṭra, vomiting blood, fell into a swoon; he remained unconscious for some time, hugging his mace and breathing heavily.
6.54.26
स लब्धसञ्ज्ञो गदया
वालिपुत्रमवस्थितम् ।
जघान परमक्रुद्धो
वक्षोदेशे निशाचरः ॥
Recovering his senses, the enraged Night-rover hit the son of Vāli on the chest, with his mace.
6.54.27
गदां त्यक्त्वा ततस्तत्र
मुष्टियुद्धमवर्तत ।
अन्योन्यं जघ्नतुस्तत्र
तावुभौ हरिराक्षसौ ॥
Leaving aside the mace, they began a bout of fisticuffs. The Vānara and the Rākshasa went on punching each other.
6.54.28
रुधिरोद्गारिणो तौ तु
प्रहरैर्जनितश्रमौ ।
बभूवतुः सुविक्रान्तौ
अङ्गारकबुधाविव ॥
The two, who were as doughty as Aṅgāraka and Budha in their attack, began vomiting blood as they became weak from the blows they received.
6.54.29
ततः परमतेजस्वी
अङ्गदः प्लवगर्षभः ।
उत्पाट्य वृक्षं स्थितवान्
बहुपुष्पफलाञ्चितम् ॥
Then the extremely powerful Aṅgada, a bull among Vānaras, stood up, uprooting a tree laden with an abundance of fruit and flower.
6.54.30
जग्राह चार्षभं चर्म
खड्गं च विपुलं शुभम् ।
किङ्किणीजालसञ्छन्नम्
चर्मणा च परिष्कृतम् ॥
Vajradaṃshṭra armed himself with a shield made of bull’s hide decked with a meshwork of jingling bells, and quite a large sword, which had been cased in a splendid scabbard.
6.54.31
विचित्रांश्चेरतुर्मार्गान्
रुषितौ कपिराक्षसौ ।
जघ्नतुश्च तदाऽन्योन्यम्
निर्दयं जयकाङ्क्षिणौ ॥
The enraged Vānara and Rākshasa struck at each other mercilessly, adopting curious techniques, and intent on winning the fight.
6.54.32
व्रणैः समुत्थैः शोभेताम्
पुष्पिताविव किंशुकौ ।
युद्ध्यमानौ परिश्रान्तौ
जानुभ्यामवनीं गतौ ॥
Scarred and wounded, both of them looked like Kiṃṡuka trees in flower. Exhausted by the fight, both collapsed to the ground on their knees.
6.54.33
निमेषान्तरमात्रेण
अङ्गदः कपिकुञ्जरः ।
उदतिष्ठत दीप्ताक्षो
दण्डाहत इवोरगः ॥
In a split second, Aṅgada, an elephant among Vānaras, sprang up on his feet, his eyes blazing, like a serpent hit by a stick.
6.54.34
निर्मलेन सुधौतेन
खड्गेनास्य महच्छिरः ।
जघान वज्रदंष्ट्रस्य
वालिसूनुर्महाबलः ॥
And with his sharpened, glittering sword Vāli’s son of tremendous strength cut off Vajradaṃshṭra’s head.
This Sloka seems unauthentic. Vānaras use only trees and rocks as weapons, and not swords. Rāma, under oath, is in exile, and hence cannot use the assistance of any civilized species. Use of sword by any of his aides amounts to his taking the help of civilized species.
Also, the entire Sargas 53 and 54, about the fight between Aṅgada and Vajradaṃshṭra could be unauthentic; they are not present in the Eastern edition.
6.54.35
रुधिरोक्षितगात्रस्य
बभूव पतितं द्विधा ।
सरोषपरिवृत्ताक्षम्
शुभं खड्गहतं शिरः ॥
The body, drenched in blood, fell in two pieces. That head, which was fine once, (became eerie now) struck by the sword, its eyes still whirling in anger.
6.54.36
वज्रदंष्ट्रं हतं दृष्ट्वा
राक्षसा भयमोहिताः ।
त्रस्ताह्यभ्यद्रवन् लङ्काम्
वध्यमानाः प्लवङ्गमैः ।
विषण्णवदना दीना
ह्रिया किञ्चिदवाङ्मुखाः ॥
On seeing Vajradaṃshṭra slain, the Rākshasas were beside themselves with fright. They ran to Laṅkā in terror chased by the Vānaras. Woebegone, despondency writ on their faces, they had their heads hanging down in shame.
6.54.38
निहत्य तं वज्रधरप्रभावः
स वालिसूनुः कपिसैन्यमध्ये ।
जगाम हर्षं महितो महाबलः
सहस्रनेत्रस्त्रिदशैरिवावृतः ॥
Having slain him, Vāli’s son of immense strength, puissant as the Wielder of the thunderbolt, rejoiced in the midst of the Vānara forces like the Thousand-Eyed surrounded by Dēvas.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे चतुष्पञ्चाशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty fourth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
