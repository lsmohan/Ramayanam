Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 15
In this Sarga, Indrajit addresses his uncle Vibheeshaṇa disparagingly and accuses him of being a coward who is trying to scare everyone. He cites his own successes in the battle over Indra and how he terrorized the Dēvas a long time ago. He brags that even an ordinary Rākshasa is very well capable of slaying the princes, who are mere humans.
Vibheeshaṇa, in turn, chides Indrajit for being a child, for being stupid, for being rash, and for not understanding the prospect of the imminent destruction of Rāvaṇa at the hands of Rāma. He concludes, urging the king to give Seetā back to Rāma.
6.15.1
बृहस्पतेस्तुल्यमतेर्वचस्तत्
निशम्य यत्नेन विभीषणस्य ।
ततो महात्मा वचनं बभाषे
तत्रेन्द्रजिन्नैर्ऋतयोधमुख्यः ॥
Having impatiently heard those words of Vibheeshaṇa, the equal of Bṛhaspati in intellect, the mighty Indrajit, foremost of the Rākshasa warriors, spoke these words:
6.15.2
किं नाम ते तात कनिष्ठ वाक्यम्
अनर्थकं चैव सुभीतवच्च ।
अस्मिन् कुले यो ऽपि भवेन्न जातः
सोऽपीदृशं नैव वदेन्न कुर्यात् ॥
Oh my dear little uncle! What is happening (here)! You say such a cowardly and useless words! Even one who is not born into our family would not say or do anything like this!
6.15.3
सत्त्वेन वीर्येण पराक्रमेण
शौर्येण धैर्येण च तेजसा च ।
एकः कुलेऽस्मिन् पुरुषो विमुक्तो
विभीषणस्तात कनिष्ठ एषः ॥
It looks like every trace of strength, valor, prowess, might, courage and energy is lost in this one man in our family, Vibheeshaṇa, my little uncle!
6.15.4
किं नाम तौ मानुषराजपुत्रौ
अस्माकमेकेन हि राक्षसेन ।
सुप्राकृतेनापि निहन्तुंमेतौ
शक्यौ कुतो भीषयसे स्म भीरो ॥
Why should we care about those princes, mere humans! Any Rākshasa among us, even a very ordinary one, can slay them. Why do you try to scare (us), you coward?
6.15.5
त्रिलोकनाथो ननु देवराजः
शक्रो मया भूमितले निविष्टः ।
भयार्दिताश्चापि दिशः प्रपन्नाः
सर्वे तदा देवगणाः समग्राः ॥
Did I not throw down Ṡakra, the king of Dēvas, on to the earth? And then, did not the entire hordes of Dēvas flee in all directions, consigned to terror!
6.15.6
ऐरावतो विस्वरमुन्नदन् स
निपातितो भूमितले मया तु ।
विकृष्य दन्तौ तु मया प्रसह्य
वित्रासिता देवगणाः समग्राः ॥
I created terror in the hordes of Dēvas, with Airāvata wailing terribly when I pulled out its tusks forcibly and threw it down on to the ground.
6.15.7
सोऽहं सुराणामपि दर्पहन्ता
दैत्योत्तमानामपि शोककर्ता ।
कथं नरेन्द्रात्मजयोर्नशक्तः
मनुष्ययोः प्राकृतयोः सुवीर्यः ॥
Why would I, who had prowess enough to humble the pride of even the Suras and give grief even to the foremost of the Daityas, fail with these nondescript princes!
6.15.8
अथेन्द्रकल्पस्य दुरासदस्य
महौजसस्तद्वचनं निशम्य ।
ततो महार्थं वचनं भभाषे
विभीषणः शस्त्रभृतां वरिष्ठः ॥
Hearing the words of that invincible one of immense strength, who was an equal of Indra, Vibheeshaṇa, the foremost among weapon-wielders, responded with these serious words:
6.15.9
न तात मन्त्रे तव निश्चयोऽस्ति
बालस्त्वमद्याप्यविपक्वबुद्धिः ।
तस्मात्त्वया ह्यात्मविनाशनाय
वचोऽर्थहीनं बहुविप्रलप्तम् ॥
My dear! You are not steady in your thinking. You are a child. Your mind is not mature yet. That is why you talk nonsense endlessly, which can only lead to your own destruction.
6.15.10
पुत्रप्रवादेन तु रावणस्य
त्वमिन्द्रजिन्मित्रमुखोऽसि शत्रुः ।
यस्येदृशं राघवतो विनाशं
निशम्य मोहादनुमन्यसे त्वम् ॥
You heard all about the (imminent) destruction of Rāvaṇa at the hands of Rāghava and yet you go on with your stupidity. You might be his son, O Indrajit, but you are actually a foe in the form a friend.
6.15.11
त्वमेव वध्यश्च सुदुर्मतिश्च
स चापि वध्यो य इहानयत्त्वाम् ।
बालं दृढं साहसिकं च योऽद्य
प्रावेशयन्मन्त्रकृतां समीपम् ॥
You deserve to be punished with death. And whoever brought you, the childish, obstinate and rash one, here where important matters are being discussed also deserves to be punished with death.
6.15.12
मूढः प्रगल्भोऽविनयोपपन्नः
तीक्ष्णस्वभावोऽल्पमतिर्दुरात्मा ।
मूर्खस्त्वमत्यन्तसुदुर्मतिश्च
त्वमिन्द्रजिद् बालतया ब्रवीषि ॥
You are a conceited fool with no modesty at all. You are rash by nature, petty-minded and wicked. You are stupid and extremely evil-minded. You, O Indrajit, are talking like a child.
6.15.13
को ब्रह्मदण्डप्रतिमप्रकाशान्
अर्चिष्मतः कालनिकाशरूपान् ।
सहेत बाणान् यमदण्डकल्पान्
समक्षमुक्तान्युधि राघवेण ॥
Who can withstand the arrows, equals of Yama’s Daṇḍa that resemble (the fast approaching) end of Time, and blaze like the Brahma’s Daṇḍa, shot in the battle by Rāghava?
6.15.14
धनानि रत्नानि विभूषणानि
वासांसि दिव्यानि मणींश्च चित्रान् ।
सीतां च रामाय निवेद्य देवीं
वसेम राजन्निह वीतशोकाः ॥
Let us, O King, give back Seetā to Rāma along with money, precious gems, ornaments, splendid raiment and lovely jewels, and live here, giving up all worries!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे पञ्चदशः सर्ग॥
Thus concludes the fifteenth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
