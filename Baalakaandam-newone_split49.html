Baala Kaanda - Sarga 49
In this Sarga, the disturbing happenings of the previous Sarga are brought to a meaningful close.
Ṡakra's reason for the transgression is revealed. Ṡakra gets over the predicament of losing his testes. Ahalya completes Tapa to atone for her transgression. Rāma's arrival makes her visible again to the world. She is reunited with Goutama.
1.49.1
अफलस्तु ततः शक्रो
देवानग्निपुरोधसः ।
अब्रवीत् त्रस्तवदनः
सर्षिसङ्घान् सचारणान् ॥
Ṡakra, visibly perturbed after losing his testes, appealed to all the Dēvas led by Agni and to the Ṛshis and Cāraṇas:
1.49.2
कुर्वता तपसो विघ्नम्
गौतमस्य महात्मनः ।
क्रोधमुत्पाद्य हि मया
सुरकार्यमिदं कृतम् ॥
I succeeded in disrupting the Tapa of Mahātma Goutama, by making him lose his temper, for the sake of accomplishing the task of Dēvas.
The concept of balance and equilibrium is fundamental to Indic understanding of life and universe, as we have noted in previous Sargas.
Too much of power or riches or energy concentrated at one place or in one person is not good for the world, as we all know. If Goutama continues with his fervor for doing Tapa, he would accumulate too much of spiritual energy. That can disrupt the equilibrium and balance of the universe. The task of Dēvas is to maintain that equilibrium.
In that cause, Indra found no other choice but to incur the wrath of Goutama and bear with the consequences. (Taking personal losses in serving a bigger cause is a theme that is reinforced in the Rāmāyaṇa on many occasions. The message was clearly articulated to Rāma by Viṡwāmitra in Ṡlōka 1.25.17.)
This is the second time in the Rāmāyaṇa alone, that there is mention of Indra doing something inappropriate and taking a personal loss for the sake of serving a larger cause. The earlier one is when Indra killed Vṛtra (see 1.24.18).
1.49.3
अफलोऽस्मि कृतस्तेन
क्रोधात् सा च निराकृता ।
शापमोक्षेण महता
तपोऽस्यापहृतं मया ॥
But because of his wrath, I lost my testes. And Ahalya was also disavowed by him. Thus I was able to steal away his great Tapa.
1.49.4
तस्मात्सुरवराः सर्वे
सर्षिसङ्घाः सचारणाः ।
सुरसाह्यकरं सर्वे
सफलं कर्तुमर्हथ ॥
All of you, the great Suras, along with Ṛshis and Cāraṇas should do something to restore my testes, as I lost them in serving the cause of Dēvas.
1.49.5
शतक्रतोर्वचः श्रुत्वा
देवाः साग्निपुरोगमाः ।
पितृदेवानुपेत्याहुः
सर्वे सह मरुद्गणैः ॥
Hearing those words of Ṡakra, all the Dēvas, led by Agni and along with Maruts, approached Pitṛ Dēvatas and said:
Pitṛ Dēvatas are the ancestors who have ascended to Heaven.
1.49.6
अयं मेषः सवृषणः
शक्रो ह्यवृषणः कृतः ।
मेषस्य वृषणौ गृह्य
शक्रायाशु प्रयच्छथ ॥
Ṡakra lost his testes. Hence we request you to give him the testes of the goat that is offered to you.
1.49.7
अफलस्तु कृतो मेषः
परां तुष्टिं प्रदास्यति ।
भवतां हर्षणार्थे च
ये च दास्यन्ति मानवाः ॥
Humans offer goats with testes to you. You should be just as well happy even if the testes are not there on them.
1.49.8
अग्नेस्तु वचनं श्रुत्वा
पितृदेवाः समागताः ।
उत्पाट्य मेषवृषणौ
सहस्राक्षे न्यवेशयन् ॥
Pitṛ Dēvatas considered the request put forward by Agni. They took out the testes of the goat and gave them to the thousand-eyed Ṡakra.
1.49.9
तदाप्रभृति काकुत्स्थ
पितृदेवाः समागताः ।
अफलान् भुञ्जते मेषान्
फलैस्तेषामयोजयन् ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! Hence onwards the Pitṛ Dēvatas were contented with goats without testes. And those testes go to Indra.
1.49.10
इन्द्रस्तु मेषवृषणः
तदाप्रभृति राघव ।
गौतमस्य प्रभावेन
तपसश्च महात्मनः ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Because of the power of the great Tapa of Goutama, hence onwards, Indra came to be known as 'Mēsha Vṛshaṇa' (one with the testes of goat).
1.49.11
तदागच्छ महातेज
आश्रमं पुण्यकर्मणः ।
तारयैनां महाभागां
अहल्यां देवरूपिणीम् ॥
O supremely radiant Rāma! May you enter this Āṡrama of Goutama of pious deeds, to redeem the divine and fortunate Ahalya!
1.49.12
विश्वामित्रवचः श्रुत्वा
राघवः सहलक्ष्मणः ।
विश्वामित्रं पुरस्कृत्य
तमाश्रममथाविशत् ॥
The prince of Raghu dynasty, Rāma, along with Lakshmaṇa then followed Viṡwāmitra and entered that Āṡrama.
1.49.13
ददर्श च महाभागाम्
तपसा द्योतितप्रभाम् ।
लोकैरपि समागम्य
दुर्निरीक्ष्यां सुरासुरैः ।
प्रयत्नान्निर्मितां धात्रा
दिव्यां मायामयीमिव ।
सतुषारावृतां साभ्राम्
पूर्णचन्द्रप्रभामिव ।
मध्येऽम्भसो दुराधर्षाम्
दीप्तां सूर्यप्रभामिव ॥
Then they saw Ahalya, the blessed soul who was supremely radiant as a result of the long Tapa, who was created by lord Brahma with discreet effort, who was pleasing and fascinating, like the full moon behind white clouds on a snowy day and into whose eyes neither humans nor Dēvas could look straight because of her blinding radiance, that resembled the sun reflected in water.
1.49.15
सा हि गौतमवाक्येन
दुर्निरीक्ष्या बभूव ह ।
त्रयाणामपि लोकानाम्
यावद्रामस्य दर्शनम् ॥
She remained invisible thus far to all the three worlds because of Goutama's curse, until the appearance of Rāma at that place.
1.49.16
शापस्यान्तमुपागम्य
तेषां दर्शनमागता ।
राघवौ त्वतिथी तस्याः
पादौ जगृहतुस्तदा ॥
As the curse was lifted off her on their arrival, they could see her. The princes of Raghu dynasty touched her feet in respect.
1.49.17
स्मरन्ती गौतमवचः
प्रतिजग्राह सा च तौ ।
पाद्यमर्घ्यं तथाऽऽतिथ्यम्
चकार सुसमाहिता ।
प्रतिजग्राह काकुत्स्थो
विधिदृष्टेन कर्मणा ॥
Remembering the words of Goutama, she received them enthusiastically and gave them water to wash their feet and water to drink and food to eat in a customary manner. The prince of Kākutstha dynasty respectfully received her hospitalities.
1.49.19
पुष्पवृष्टिर्महत्यासीत्
देवदुन्दुभिः निःस्वनैः ।
गन्धर्वाप्सरसां चापि
महानासीत्समागमः ॥
There were showers of flowers accompanied by the sound of the snare-drums of the Dēvas. Gandharvas and Apsaras congregated (singing and dancing in celebration of that great moment.)
1.49.20
साधु साध्विति देवास्तां
अहल्यां समपूजयन् ।
तपोबलविशुद्धाङ्गीम्
गौतमस्य वशानुगाम् ॥
Ahalya, cleansed by the long Tapa, reunited with Goutama in full dignity, praised and hailed by all the Dēvas.
1.49.21
गौतमो हि महातेजा
अहल्यासहितः सुखी ।
रामं सम्पूज्य विधिवत्
तपस्तेपे महातपाः ॥
The supremely radiant, great Tapasvi Goutama, paid respects to Rāma along with Ahalya and went on to do Tapa.
1.49.22
रामोऽपि परमां पूजाम्
गौतमस्य महामुनेः ।
सकाशाद्विधिवत् प्राप्य
जगाम मिथिलां ततः ॥
Rāma, having received high respects from the great Muni Goutama in the customary manner, then left for Mithilā.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे एकोनपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty ninth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
