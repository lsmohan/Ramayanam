Baala Kaanda - Sarga 63
The journey of Viṡwāmitra towards Brahmatva becomes more serious in this Sarga. Brahma and all the Dēvas confer upon him the distinction of being a 'Ṛshi'.
Yet he falls to the lure of the senses and yields to the beauty of Mēnaka and spends a full ten years with her, only to realize that he had again slipped from his goal.
He continues the Tapa and earns the distinction of being a Maharshi.
The Tapa gets stretched for so long that even readers like us get tired of reading it. But it is not like Viṡwāmitra to give up. That is why he stands out as a star in the array of mythological personalities along with Bhageeratha.
By the way, what is the difference between Bhageeratha's effort and Viṡwāmitra's effort? Bhageeratha’s effort was for the good of others. Viṡwāmitra's effort was for his own betterment. Yet, both were arduous and both were challenging.
Rāma, the young lad, through these stories, is being taught that neither self-development nor social-development is easy, but achievable with persistence.
1.63.1
पूर्णे वर्षसहस्रे तु
व्रतस्नातं महामुनिम् ।
अभ्यागच्छन् सुराः सर्वे
तपःफलचिकीर्षवः ॥
As the thousand year Tapa had drawn to a close, and as the Mahā Muni had taken the end-of-the-ceremony bath all the Dēvas descended to bestow him with the reward for the Tapa.
1.63.2
अब्रवीत् सुमहातेजा
ब्रह्मा सुरुचिरं वचः ।
ऋषिस्त्वमसि भद्रं ते
स्वार्जितैः कर्मभिः शुभैः ॥
The magnificently lustrous Brahma said these sweetest of words: "You have now become a Ṛshi because of all the good deeds you have performed. May all bode well for you!"
1.63.3
तमेवमुक्त्वा देवेशः
त्रिदिवं पुनरभ्यगात् ।
विश्वामित्रो महातेजा
भूयस्तेपे महत्तपः ॥
After speaking thus to them, the lord of Dēvas returned to Heaven Viṡwāmitra of immense power returned to continue with his extraordinary Tapa.
1.63.4
ततः कालेन महता
मेनका परमाप्सराः ।
पुष्करेषु नरश्रेष्ठ
स्नातुं समुपचक्रमे ॥
O best among men (Rāma)! After a while, Mēnaka, the first and foremost of the Apsaras came to the Pushkara to bathe.
1.63.5
तां ददर्श महातेजा
मेनकां कुशिकात्मजः ।
रूपेणाप्रतिमां तत्र
विद्युतं जलदे यथा ॥
The supremely radiant son of Kuṡika saw her, the unparalleled beauty, glittering like lightning in the sky.
1.63.6
दृष्ट्वा कन्दर्पवशगो
मुनिस्तामिदमब्रवीत् ।
अप्सरः स्वागतं तेऽस्तु
वस चेह ममाश्रमे ।
अनुगृह्णीष्व भद्रं ते
मदनेन सुमोहितम् ॥
Becoming a slave of Kandarpa after seeing her, the Muni said: O Apsara! welcome to you. Please stay in my Āṡrama. I am now overpowered by Madana. Be kind to me! May all bode well for you.
Kandarpa and Madana are names for the God of Passion and romance.
The direct and no-nonsense approach is typical of stories in the Rāmāyaṇa and Mahābhārata.
We see both men (Viṡwāmitra, Rāvaṇa, et al) and women (Ṡūrpaṇakhā, Hidimbi, Rukmiṇi, et al) of these stories expressing their interest directly to the women or men they are attracted to.
However, the degree of interest varies, and can be considered as being of three levels:
Level 1 (Sex): Physical only.
Level 2 (Love): Physical and psychological.
Level 3 (Marriage): Physical, psychological and social.
Society, in those days seems to have been more open and clear. People seem to have a common understanding of these three levels. People seem to express their specific level of interest clearly.
Both the acceptance of, and clarity around these multiple levels is murky nowadays. People get into relationships (in the name of dating or wedding), with one of them 'hoping' for Level 3 relationship and the other 'fearing' the Level 3 relationship.
No wonder that the number of broken marriages and stress levels in marriages are very high.
1.63.7
इत्युक्ता सा वरारोहा
तत्र वासमथाकरोत् ।
तस्यां वसन्त्यां वर्षाणि
पञ्च पञ्च च राघव ।
विश्वामित्राश्रमे राम
सुखेन व्यतिचक्रमुः ॥
O son of Raghu dynasty! Then that woman stayed back. Ten years passed quickly and happily as she stayed there in Viṡwāmitra's Āṡrama.
1.63.9
अथ काले गते तस्मिन्
विश्वामित्रो महामुनिः ।
सव्रील इव संवृत्तः
चिन्ताशोकपरायणः ॥
After time had gone by in that manner, the Mahā Muni Viṡwāmitra felt ashamed and deeply saddened.
1.63.10
बुद्धिर्मुनेः समुत्पन्ना
सामर्षा रघुनन्दन ।
सर्वं सुराणां कर्मैतः
तपोऽपहरणं महत् ॥
O son of Raghu dynasty! Then the Muni became angry. He felt that it must be the act of Dēvas, Who were trying to deplete him of his immense spiritual energies.
1.63.11
अहोरात्रापदेशेन
गताः संवत्सरा दश ।
काममोहाभिभूतस्य
विघ्नोऽयं प्रत्युपस्थितः ॥
Days and nights, for ten full years, are lost due to lust and infatuation, disrupting my endeavor.
1.63.12
विनिश्वसन् मुनिवरः
पश्चात्तापेन दुःखितः ।
भीतामप्सरसं दृष्ट्वा
वेपन्तीं प्राञ्जलिं स्थिताम् ।
मेनकां मधुरैर्वाक्यैः
विसृज्य कुशिकात्मजः ।
उत्तरं पर्वतं राम
विश्वामित्रो जगाम ह ॥
O Rāma! Then that great Muni sighed with great regret and remorse. He saw the Apsara who was shaken and scared, standing in front of him with folded hands. The son of Kuṡika sent Mēnaka off with gracious words. Viṡwāmitra then went to the mountains in the north.
1.63.14
स कृत्वा नैष्ठिकीं बुद्धिम्
जेतुकामो महायशाः ।
कौशिकीतीरमासाद्य
तपस्तेपे सुदारुणम् ॥
That renowned Muni, then taking a vow to follow strict discipline, reached the banks of river Kouṡiki and did severe Tapa.
1.63.15
तस्य वर्षसहस्रं तु
घोरं तप उपासतः ।
उत्तरे पर्वते राम
देवतानामभूद्भयम् ॥
O Rāma, as he did Tapa for one thousand years in those northern mountains, the Dēvas became concerned.
Colossal amounts of spiritual energy resting in a person who has not overcome his senses, is like an atom bomb in the hands of a mad man. Hence the concern.
1.63.16
आमन्त्रयन् समागम्य
सर्वे सर्षिगणाः सुराः ।
महर्षिशब्दं लभताम्
साध्वयं कुशिकात्मजः ॥
Then the Dēvas, in consultation with all the Ṛshis, felt that this son of Kuṡika, would now deserve to be called a Maharshi.
1.63.17
देवतानां वचः श्रुत्वा
सर्वलोकपितामहः ।
अब्रवीन्मधुरं वाक्यम्
विश्वामित्रं तपोधनम् ॥
In accordance with that recommendation by the Dēvas, the Progenitor of all the worlds (Brahma) spoke these sweet words to Viṡwāmitra, who was enriched with Tapa.
1.63.18
महर्षे स्वागतं वत्स
तपसोग्रेण तोषितः ।
महत्त्वमृषिमुख्यत्वम्
ददामि तव कौशिक ॥
O dear Maharshi! Welcome to you. I am pleased with your immense Tapa. I confer upon you the distinction of being the foremost among the Ṛshis.
1.63.19
ब्रह्मणस्स वचः श्रुत्वा
सर्वलोकेश्वरस्य ह ।
न विषण्णो न सन्तुष्टो
विश्वामित्रस्तपोधनः ॥
Hearing those words of Brahma, the lord of all worlds, Viṡwāmitra, who was rich with Tapa, felt neither happy nor unhappy.
This is a major turning point in Viṡwāmitra's journey towards Brahmatva.
He has now come to the point of being indifferent to success and recognition. This is one of the main characteristics of being a Brāhmaṇa.
1.63.20
प्राञ्जलिः प्रणतो भूत्वा
सर्वलोकपितामहम् ।
प्रत्युवाच ततो वाचम्
विश्वामित्रो महामुनिः ॥
The Mahā Muni Viṡwāmitra, with folded hands paid respects to the Progenitor of all worlds and said:
1.63.21
महर्षिशब्दमतुलम्
स्वार्जितैः कर्मभिः शुभैः ।
यदि मे भगवानाह
ततोऽहं विजितेन्द्रियः ॥
If you, the Bhagawān, tell me that I have earned the unique distinction of being a Maharshi through my good deeds, I shall consider myself to have gained complete mastery over my senses.
This Ṡlōka signifies another great turning point in the life of Viṡwāmitra. For the first time he explicitly considers his untamed mind (one of the senses) being his real enemy.
1.63.22
तमुवाच ततो ब्रह्मा
न तावत्त्वं जितेन्द्रियः ।
यतस्व मुनिशार्दूल
इत्युक्त्वा त्रिदिवं गतः ॥
Then Brahma said to him, "O tiger among Munis, You have not yet mastered your senses, keep trying", and left to Heaven.
1.63.23
विप्रस्थितेषु देवेषु
विश्वामित्रो महामुनिः ।
ऊर्द्ध्वबाहुर्निरालम्बो
वायुभक्षस्तपश्चरन् ॥
As the Dēvas left thus, the Mahā Muni Viṡwāmitra continued with Tapa with hands upwards, leaning on no support and living only on air.
1.63.24
घर्मे पञ्चतपा भूत्वा
वर्षास्वाकाशसंश्रयः ।
शिशिरे सलिलस्थायी
रात्र्यहानि तपोधनः ।
एवं वर्षसहस्रं हि
तपो घोरमुपागमत् ॥
That Tapasvi continued with his intense Tapa in the middle of Five Fires in the summer, standing in the open in the monsoon season, and standing in waters in the fall and winter, day and night, for one thousand years.
Five Fires: The hot sun from above plus four fires lit up in four directions.
1.63.25
तस्मिन् सन्तप्यमाने तु
विश्वामित्रे महामुनौ ।
सम्भ्रमः सुमहानासीत्
सुराणां वासवस्य च ॥
Dēvas and Indra were amazed and perturbed by the intense Tapa of the Mahā Muni Viṡwāmitra.
1.63.26
रम्भामप्सरसं शक्रः
सह सर्वैर्मरुद्गणैः ।
उवाचात्महितं वाक्यं
अहितं कौशिकस्य च ॥
Indra, along with all the Maruts, called upon the Apsara Rambha and said these words that would advance their cause and deter the son of Kuṡika from his efforts.
Continuation in the next Sarga.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे त्रिषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty third Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
