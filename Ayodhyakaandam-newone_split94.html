Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 94
In this Sarga, Rāma describes the captivating beauty of Citrakūṭa to Seetā. He hopes that she too is enjoying the beauty of the Vana, living along with him.
He feels happy that he is making the word given by his father come true and also making Bharata happy. He declares that, living in that beautiful Vana along with her and Lakshmaṇa, he sees no reason for him to feel sad in spite of losing the kingdom and being away from his friends.
2.94.1
दीर्घकालोषितस्तस्मिन्
गिरौ गिरिवनप्रियः ।
वैदेह्याः प्रियमाकाङ्क्षन्
स्वं च चित्तं विलोभयन् ।
अथ दाशरथिश्चित्रं
चित्रकूटमदर्शयत् ।
भार्याममरसङ्काशः
शचीमिव पुरन्दरः ॥
Having lived there on the mountain long enough, and having a natural fascination for mountains and Vanas, the son of Daṡaratha, who was like a Dēva, showed to Seetā, the princess of Vidēha, like Indra to Ṡachi, the bewitching beauty of Citrakūṭa, to please her as well as to regale his own heart.
2.94.3
न राज्याद्भ्रंशनं भद्रे
न सुहृद्भिर्विनाभवः ।
मनो मे बाधते दृष्ट्वा
रमणीयमिमं गिरिम् ॥
Neither the loss of the kingdom nor deprivation of the company of friends bothers my mind, O auspicious one, seeing this beautiful and enchanting mountain.
2.94.4
पश्येममचलं भद्रे
नानाद्विजगणायुतम् ।
शिखरैः खमिवोद्विद्धैः
धातुमद्भिर्विभूषितम् ॥
O my darling! Look at this mountain, with its many flocks of birds and its peaks, rich with minerals, that seem to shoot into the sky.
2.94.5
केचिद्रजतसङ्काशाः
केचित्क्षतजसन्नीभाः ।
पीतमाञ्जिष्टवर्णाश्च
केचिन्मणिवरप्रभाः ।
पुष्यार्ककेतकाभाश्च
केचिज्ज्योतीरसप्रभाः ।
विराजन्तेऽचलेन्द्रस्य
देशा धातुविभूषिताः ॥
Some of the peaks of this mountain shine like silver, some red like blood, some yellow and madder-colored, some with the brilliance of emerald, some with the tints of topaz, crystal and the flower of the Kētaka, some with the luster of stars and some with the sheen of quicksilver!
2.94.7
नना मृग गण द्वीपि
तरक्ष्वृक्ष गणैर्वृतः ।
अदुष्टैर्भात्ययं शैलो
बहुपक्षिसमायुतः ॥
This mountain is splendid with its numerous and harmless herds of deer, tiger, leopard and bear, and its innumerable birds.
2.94.8
आम्रजम्ब्वसनैर्लोध्रैः
प्रियालैः पनसैर्धवैः ।
अङ्कोलैर्भव्यतिनिशैः
बिल्वतिन्दुक वेणुभिः ।
काश्मर्यरिष्टवरुणैः
मधूकैस्तिलकैस्तथा ।
बदर्यामलकैर्नीपैः
वेत्रधन्वनबीजकैः ।
पुष्पवद्भिः फलोपेतैः
छायावद्भिर्मनोरमैः ।
एवमादिभिराकीर्णः
श्रियं पुष्यत्ययं गिरिः ॥
The mountain is rich with enchanting flowering, fruit-bearing and shady trees like mango, rose-apple, Asana, Lōdhra, Priyāla, jack fruit, Dhava, Aṅkōla, lovely Tiniṡa, Bilva, Tiṇḍuka, bamboo, Kāṡmari, neem, Varuṇa, Madhūka, Tilaka, Badari, Āmla, Neepa, cane, Dhanvana and pomegranate.
2.94.11
शैलप्रस्थेषु रम्येषु
पश्येमान् रोमहर्षणान् ।
किन्नरान् द्वन्द्वशो भद्रे
रममाणान्मनस्विनः ॥
Look at those Kinnara people in pairs, O auspicious one, enjoying themselves on the picturesque flanks of the mountain, a sight that gives goose bumps!
2.94.12
शाखावसक्तान् खड्गांश्च
प्रवराण्यम्बराणि च ।
पश्य विद्याधरस्त्रीणां
क्रीडोद्धेशान्मनोरमान् ॥
Look at the sports grounds of the Vidyādharas and their women. Look at their beautiful garments and swords, hanging on the branches of the trees!
2.94.13
जलप्रपातैरुद्भेदैः
निष्यन्दैश्च क्वचित्क्वचित् ।
स्रवद्भिर्भात्ययं शैलः
स्रवन्मद इव द्विपः ॥
With water gushing from its waterfalls, and from its springs that seem to tear it apart, the mountain looks like an elephant exuding ichor.
2.94.14
गुहासमीरणो गन्धान्
नानापुष्पभवान्वहन् ।
घ्राणतर्पणमभ्येत्य
कं नरं न प्रहर्षयेत् ॥
Who will not be delighted by the breeze from the caverns that carries the perfume of a multitude of flowers, tickling their sense of smell?
2.94.15
यदीह शरदोऽनेकाः
त्वया सार्धमनिन्दिते ।
लक्ष्मणेन च वत्स्यामि
न मां शोकः प्रधक्ष्यति ॥
I could live here for many many years, with you, O faultless one, and with Lakshmaṇa, and no grief would ever consume me.
2.94.16
बहुपुष्पफले रम्ये
नानाद्विजगणायुते ।
विचित्रशिखरेह्यस्मिन्
रतवानस्मि भामिनि ॥
This mountain with its splendid peaks, its lovely flowers of many kinds and its flocks of birds, captivates me, O lovely woman!
2.94.17
अनेन वनवासेन
मया प्राप्तं फलद्वयम् ।
पितुश्चानृणता धर्मे
भरतस्य प्रियं तथा ॥
With this stay in the Vana, I have achieved two objectives: of not making my father's adherence to Dharma untrue, and that of making Bharata happy.
2.94.18
वैदेहि रमसे कच्चित्
चित्रकूटे मया सह ।
पश्यन्ती विविधान्भावान्
मनोवाक्कायसम्मतान् ॥
O princess of Vidēha! Are you enjoying yourself with me here in this Citrakūṭa, looking at these many sights that please the mind, speech and body!
This is the toughest question a husband whose fortunes are on the decline and whose good times are behind him, could ask his wife! All the guilt of having to bring the princess of Vidēha must be bearing down on him. Does not it wrench the heart of everyone even to witness this situation, let alone experience it?
2.94.19
इदमेवामृतं प्राहू
राज्ञि राजर्षयः परे ।
वनवासं भवार्थाय
प्रेत्य मे प्रपितामहाः ॥
My great grandfathers and ancient Rājarshis said that forest life like this should be thought of as no less than Amṛta, for a king who wants a great afterlife!
2.94.20
शिला श्शैलस्य शोभन्ते
विशाला श्शतशोऽभितः ।
बहुला बहुलैर्वर्णैः
नीलपीतसितारुणैः ॥
Hundreds of expansive rock-faces of the mountain sparkle all around, with a multitude of brilliant tints, blue, yellow, white and red.
2.94.21
निशिभान्त्यचलेन्द्रस्य
हुताशनशिखा इव ।
ओषध्यः स्वप्रभालक्ष्म्या
भ्राजमानास्सहस्रशः ॥
Herbs in thousands, on this lord of mountains, shine with the splendor of their own luster, like tongues of fire at the night.
2.94.22
केचित् क्षयनिभा देशाः
केचिदुद्यानसन्निभाः ।
केचिदेकशिला भान्ति
पर्वतस्यास्य भामिनि ॥
O lovely one! Some areas of the mountain look like houses, some like gardens and some seem like they are made of a single rock.
2.94.23
भित्त्वेव वसुधां भाति
चित्रकूटस्समुत्थितः ।
चित्रकूटस्य कूटोऽसौ
दृश्यते सर्वत श्शुभः ॥
Citrakūṭa looks as if it had risen rending the earth, and this peak of the Citrakūṭa looks equally beautiful from every direction.
2.94.24
कुष्ठस्थगरपुन्नाग
भूर्जपत्रोत्तरच्छदान् ।
कामिनां स्वास्तरान्पश्य
कुशेशयदलायुतान् ॥
Look at these great beds of lovers that are covered with Kushṭha, Sthagara, Punnāga and Bhūrja leaves and spread over by lotus petals.
2.94.25
मृदिताश्चापविद्धाश्च
दृश्यन्ते कमलस्रजः ।
कामिभिर्वनिते पश्य
फलानि विविधानि च ॥
Look at the lotus wreaths, my lady, crushed and cast aside by lovers; and look at these different fruits.
2.94.26
वस्वौकसारां नलिनीम्
अत्येतीवोत्तरान्कुरून् ।
पर्वतश्चित्रकूटोऽसौ
बहुमूलफलोदकः ॥
This Citrakūṭa mountain with its abundance of roots, fruits and water, seems to excel the city of Vasvoukasāra, the lake of Nalini, and the country of Uttara-Kuru.
2.94.27
इमं तु कालं वनिते विजह्रिवान्
त्वया च सीते सह लक्ष्मणेन च ।
रतिं प्रपत्स्ये कुलधर्मवर्धनीं
सतां पथि स्वैर्नियमैः परैः स्थितः ॥
I will be very happy, O Seetā, my lady, spending time here along with you and Lakshmaṇa, observing the highest discipline, following the example of great people, and enhancing the Dharma adhered to by the family.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे चतुर्नवतितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninety fourth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
