Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 93
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa, who is grieving for his son Indrajit, orders the army chiefs to go to the battle and slay Rāma by isolating him and inundating him with floods of arrows. Even if they don't succeed, he says, it would be easy for him to slay Rāma, once he is already weakened by them.
The Rākshasas go to the fight. The Vānaras give them a stiff resistance. But at the end, Vānaras, unable to bear the assault of the Rākshasas, go to Rāma seeking protection.
Then Rāma himself gets into the fray. He penetrates the Rākshasa army and decimates them with his sharp arrows. His speed is such that the Rākshasas are unable to tell where Rāma is at any given moment. They only see the terrible havoc wreaked by him. With many of them dead, the remaining flee to Laṅkā. The Dēvas, the Gandharvas, the Siddhas, and the supreme Ṛshis applaud Rāma for his exploits.
6.93.1
स प्रविश्य सभां राजा
दीनः परमदुःखितः ।
निषसादासने मुख्ये
सिंहः क्रुद्ध इव श्वसन् ॥
Entering the council hall, the King, who was dejected and extremely sad, took his seat on the great throne, sighing like an angry lion.
6.93.2
अब्रवीच्च स तान् सर्वान्
बलमुख्यान् महाबलः ।
रावणः प्राञ्जलीन् वाक्यम्
पुत्रव्यसनकर्शितः ॥
Exhausted by grief for his lost son, the extraordinarily strong Rāvaṇa said these words to all the army chiefs who stood with palms joined in reverence:
6.93.3
सर्वे भवन्तः सर्वेण
हस्त्यश्वेन समावृताः ।
निर्यान्तु रथसङ्घैश्च
पादातैश्चोपशोभिताः ॥
All of you shall set out taking with you all the horses and elephants, the chariots and the companies of infantry.
6.93.4
एकं रामं परिक्षिप्य
समरे हन्तुमर्हथ ।
वर्षन्तः शरवर्षेण
प्रावृट्काल इवाम्बुदाः ॥
You should surround and isolate Rāma and slay him, showering a downpour of arrows like rainclouds send a downpour in the monsoon season.
6.93.5
अथवाऽहं शरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
भिन्नगात्रं महाहवे ।
भवद्भिः श्वो निहन्तास्मि
रामं लोकस्य पश्यतः ॥
If not, I will slay Rāma, whose limbs have been broken by you in the great battle, with my sharp arrows tomorrow, while the whole world watches.
6.93.6
इत्येतद्वाक्यमादाय
राक्षसेन्द्रस्य राक्षसाः ।
निर्ययुस्ते रथैः शीघ्रैः
नानानीकैश्च संयुताः ॥
Taking these orders from the lord of Rākshasas, the Rākshasas marched forth in their swift chariots, along with many battalions.
6.93.7
परिघान् पट्टिशांश्चैव
शरखड्गपरश्वधान् ।
शरीरान्तकरान् सर्वे
चिक्षिपुर्वानरान् प्रति ।
वानराश्च द्रुमान् शैलान्
राक्षसान् प्रति चिक्षिपुः ॥
All of them hurled at Vānaras, Parighas, Paṭṭiṡas, arrows, swords and battle-axes, each one by itself powerful enough to destroy the whole body (of any warrior). And the Vānaras also responded hurling trees and rocks at the Rākshasas.
6.93.8
स सङ्ग्रामो महान् भीमः
सूर्यस्योदयनं प्रति ।
रक्षसां वानराणां च
तुमुलः समपद्यत ॥
The great and fierce fight that began with the sunrise between Rākshasas and Vānaras turned out to be tumultuous.
6.93.9
ते गदाभिर्विचित्राभिः
प्राशैः खड्गैः परश्वधैः ।
अन्योन्यं समरे जघ्नुः
तदा वानरराक्षसाः ॥
In that battle, Vānaras and Rākshasas struck each other with a variety of maces, Prāsas, swords and battle-axes.
6.93.10
एवं प्रवृत्ते सङ्ग्रामे
ह्यद्भुतं सुमहद्रजः ।
रक्षसां वानराणां च
शान्तं शोणितविस्रवैः ॥
In that battle which thus ensued, the dust that had risen to amazing heights subsided, dampened by the blood that flowed from the bodies of Rākshasas and Vānaras.
6.93.11
मातङ्गरथकूलाश्च
वाजिमत्स्या ध्वजद्रुमाः ।
शरीरसङ्घाटवहाः
प्रसस्रुः शोणितापगाः ॥
Rivers of blood flowed, with elephants and chariots for banks, horses for fish, flagstaffs for trees (on the banks), and the bodies of the dead for the floats therein.
6.93.12
ततस्ते वानराः सर्वे
शोणितौघपरिप्लुताः ।
ध्वजवर्मरथानश्वान्
नानाप्रहरणानि च ।
आप्लुत्याप्लुत्य समरे
राक्षसानां बभञ्जिरे ॥
Then all the Vānaras, drenched in blood, broke and smashed the flagstaffs, armors, chariots, horses and every kind of weapon jumping up and snatching them again and again.
6.93.14
केशान् कर्णललाटांश्च
नासिकाश्च प्लवङ्गमाः ।
रक्षसां दशनैस्तीक्ष्णैः
नखैश्चापि न्यकर्तयन् ॥
With their sharp teeth and claws the Vānaras tore apart the hair, ears, foreheads and noses of the Rākshasas.
6.93.15
एकैकं राक्षसं सङ्ख्ये
शतं वानरपुङ्गवाः ।
अभ्यधावन्त फलिनम्
वृक्षं शकुनयो यथा ॥
In that fight, a hundred bulls among Vānaras swooped down on each one of the Rākshasas like birds do, on trees loaded with fruit.
6.93.16
तथा गदाभिर्गुर्वीभिः
प्रासैः खड्गैः परश्वधैः ।
निजघ्नुर्वानरान् घोरान्
राक्षसाः पर्वतोपमाः ॥
And the Rākshasas, each looking like a mountain, struck the formidable Vānaras with huge maces, Prāsas, swords and hatchets.
6.93.17
राक्षसैर्वद्ध्यामानानाम्
वानराणां महाचमूः ।
शरण्यं शरणं याता
रामं दशरथात्मजम् ॥
The great army of Vānaras, thus battered by the Rākshasas, sought the protection of Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, the refuge.
6.93.18
ततो रामो महातेजा
धनुरादाय वीर्यवान् ।
प्रविश्य राक्षसं सैन्यम्
शरवर्षं ववर्ष ह ॥
Then Rāma of extraordinary power and valor took a bow and penetrated the Rākshasa army, assailing it with showers of arrows.
After describing the battle scenes for over ninety Sargas, one would think that Vālmeeki might be exhausted and would have nothing new to say. But with Rāma jumping into the fray, Vālmeeki's poetic spirits soar high. The way he describes the scene here is nothing like what was described before. Rākshasas are not simply destroyed by Rāma, but are totally discombobulated. The havoc is wreaked even before they can spot where Rāma is.
This Sarga (and the next one), which demonstrates the power of the pen of Vālmeeki, feels refreshing, after many of the previous Sargas which contain many Ṡlōkas that seem to have become corrupted over the course of time.
6.93.19
प्रविष्टं तु तदा रामम्
मेघाः सूर्यमिवाम्बरे ।
नाभिजग्मुर्महाघोरम्
निर्दहन्तं शराग्निना ॥
The Rākshasas could not so much as approach Rāma, who looked extremely fearsome penetrating into their ranks and burning them with the fire of his arrows, any more than clouds in the sky can approach the sun.
6.93.20
कृतान्येव सुघोराणि
रामेण रजनीचराः ।
ददृशुस्ते न वै रामम्
कर्माण्यसुकराणि ते ॥
The Rākshasas could not even spot where Rāma was; they could only see the terrible havoc he was wreaking, doing feats that were impossible for anyone else.
6.93.21
चालयन्तं महानीकम्
विधमन्तं महारथान् ।
ददृशुस्ते न वै रामम्
वातं वनगतं यथा ॥
They could not see where Rāma, who was rocking that great army and destroying the great chariots was any more than one can see the wind in the woods (that uproots the trees).
6.93.22
छिन्नं भिन्नं शरैर्दग्धम्
प्रभग्नं शस्त्रपीडितम् ।
बलं रामेण ददृशुः
न रामं शीघ्रकारिणम् ॥
They could only see the army being torn, broken, battered, burnt by arrows and oppressed by Ṡastras, but not Rāma, who was doing it all so swiftly.
6.93.23
प्रहरन्तं शरीरेषु
न ते पश्यन्ति राघवम् ।
इन्द्रियार्थेषु तिष्ठन्तम्
भूतात्मानमिव प्रजाः ॥
They could see bodies being struck, but not Rāghava (who was doing it), just as people cannot see the life force, but only the actions of the senses driven by it.
6.93.24
एष हन्ति गजानीकम्
एष हन्ति महारथान् ।
एष हन्ति शरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
पदातीन् वाजिभिः सह ।
इति ते राक्षसाः सर्वे
रामस्य सदृशान् रणे ।
अन्योन्यं कुपिता जघ्नुः
सादृश्याद्राघवस्य ते ॥
"Here he is, assailing the elephantry, here he is, smashing the great chariots, here he is again, striking down infantry and cavalry with his sharp arrows!" cried all the Rākshasas, at times mistaking their own folk for Rāghava and hitting each other in rage, thinking that the other was Rāma.
6.93.26
न ते ददृशिरे रामम्
दहन्तमपि वाहिनीम् ।
मोहिताः परमास्त्रेण
गान्धर्वेण महात्मना ॥
They could not see Rāma, who was burning the forces, as they were discombobulated by that Mahātma with the supreme Astra of Gandharvas.
6.93.27
ते तु रामसहस्राणि
रणे पश्यन्ति राक्षसाः ।
पुनः पश्यन्ति काकुत्स्थम्
एकमेव महाहवे ॥
The Rākshasas saw thousands of Rāmas in that great battle one moment, and the next, only one Kākutstha.
6.93.28
भ्रमन्तीं काञ्चनीं कोटिम्
कार्मुकस्य महात्मनः ।
अलातचक्रप्रतिमाम्
ददृशुस्ते न राघवम् ॥
They could see the gold-tipped end of the bow of Mahātma Rāghava whirling like a firebrand, but not him.
6.93.29
शरीरनाभिसत्त्वार्चिः
शरीरं नेमिकार्मुकम् ।
ज्याघोषतलनिर्घोषम्
तेजोबुद्धिगुणप्रभम् ।
दिव्यास्त्रगुणपर्यन्तम्
निघ्नन्तं युधि राक्षसान् ।
ददृशू रामचक्रं तत्
कालचक्रमिव प्रजाः ॥
To all the creatures, Rāma, crushing the Rākshasas to death in that fight, looked like the Wheel of Time with his body seeming to be its hub, his power its flames, the arrows its spokes, the bow its rim, the sound of the bowstring and the thud on the palms its rumbling, his power and brilliance its glow, and the power of the divine Astras its hallow.
6.93.31
अनीकं दशसाहस्रम्
रथानां वातरंहसाम् ।
अष्टादश सहस्राणि
कुञ्जराणां तरस्विनाम् ।
चतुर्दश सहस्राणि
सारोहाणां च वाजिनाम् ।
पूर्णे शतसहस्रे द्वे
राक्षसानां पदातिनाम् ।
दिवसस्याष्टमे भागे
शरैरग्निशिखोपमैः ।
हतान्येकेन रामेण
रक्षसां कामरूपिणाम् ॥
Rāma, in a matter of one-eighth of a day, with arrows that were like blazing flames, singlehandedly struck down a force of ten thousand chariots that were fast as wind, eighteen thousand powerful elephants, fourteen thousand horses along with their riders, a full two hundred thousand foot-soldiers of the Rākshasas, who could take any form at will.
6.93.34
ते हताश्वा हतरथाः
शान्ता विमथितध्वजाः ।
अभिपेतुः पुरीं लङ्काम्
हतशेषा निशाचराः ॥
With the horses killed, chariots destroyed, flagstaffs broken, the quietened Night-rovers who were left alive fled to the city of Laṅkā.
6.93.35
हतैर्गजपदात्यश्वैः
तद्बभूव रणाजिरम् ।
आक्रीडभूमिः क्रुद्धस्य
रुद्रस्येव महात्मनः ॥
With the dead elephants, foot-soldiers and horses, the battlefield looked like the playground of the angry and mighty Rudra.
6.93.36
ततो देवाः सगन्धर्वाः
सिद्धाश्च परमर्षयः ।
साधु साध्विति रामस्य
तत् कर्म समपूजयन् ॥
The Dēvas, the Gandharvas, the Siddhas, and the supreme Ṛshis applauded Rāma for those exploits, saying, 'Well done, well done!"
6.93.37
अब्रवीच्च तदा रामः
सुग्रीवं प्रत्यनन्तरम् ।
विभीषणं च धर्मात्मा
हनूमन्तं च वानरम् ।
जाम्बवन्तं हरिश्रेष्ठम्
मैन्दं द्विविदमेव च ।
एतदस्त्रबलं दिव्यम्
मम वा त्र्यम्बकस्य वा ॥
Rāma, the Dharmātma, told Sugreeva who was by his side, Vibheeshaṇa and the Vānara Hanumān, Jāmbavān, the foremost of the Ṛkshas, and also Mainda and Dwivida, "Only I and Tryambaka have the ability to deploy this (Gandharva) Astra!"
6.93.39
निहत्य तां राक्षसवाहिनीं तु
रामस्तदा शक्रसमो महात्मा ।
अस्त्रेषु शस्त्रेषु जितक्लमश्च
संस्तूयते देवगणैः प्रहृष्टैः ॥
Having destroyed that Rākshasa army, Mahātma Rāma, a peer of Ṡakra, who knew no fatigue in deploying Astras and Ṡastras, received applause from the rejoicing hosts of Dēvas.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे त्रिनवतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninety third Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
