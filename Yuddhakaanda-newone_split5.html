Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 5
In this Sarga, after the army settles down in the camps on the ocean shore, Rāma turns to Lakshmaṇa and expresses his grief over the separation from Seetā and his eagerness to see her and be reunited with her.
6.5.1
सा तु नीलेन विधिवत्
स्वारक्षा सुसमाहिता ।
सागरस्योत्तरे तीरे
साधु सेना निवेशिता ॥
The army settled well, in an orderly manner, on the northern shore of the ocean, with Neela arranging for its safety following all the conventional wisdom.
6.5.2
मैन्दश्च द्विविदश्चोभौ
तत्र वानरपुङ्गवौ ।
विचेरतुश्च तां सेनाम्
रक्षार्थं सर्वतो दिशम् ॥
Mainda and Dwivida, bulls among Vānaras, patrolled all around, ensuring its safety.
6.5.3
निविष्टायां तु सेनायाम्
तीरे नदनदीपतेः ।
पार्श्वस्थं लक्ष्मणं दृष्ट्वा
रामो वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
After the army settled down on the shore of the lord of rivers that flow towards the east and towards the west, Rāma looked at Lakshmaṇa who stood beside him and said these words:
6.5.4
शोकश्च किल कालेन
गच्छता ह्यपगच्छति ।
मम चापश्यतः कान्ताम्
अहन्यहनि वर्धते ॥
It is said grief fades away with the passage of time; but mine, not seeing my beloved, is worsening day by day.
6.5.5
न मे दुःखं प्रिया दूरे
न मे दुःखं हृतेति वा ।
एतदेवानुशोचामि
वयोऽस्या ह्यतिवर्तते ॥
My sadness is not over her being away and it is not over her being carried off, but it is over her youth that is draining away.
6.5.6
वाहि वात यतः कान्ता
तां स्पृष्ट्वा मामपि स्पृश ।
त्वयि मे गात्रसंस्पर्शः
चन्द्रे दृष्टिसमागमः ॥
O wind, please blow in the direction in which my beloved is, and then, after touching her, please touch me. You are the only medium through which I can touch her and our eyes can meet only when we both look at the moon.
6.5.7
तन्मे दहति गात्राणि
विषं पीतमिवाशये ।
हा नाथेति प्रिया सा माम्
ह्रियमाणा यदब्रवीत् ॥
The words she uttered, ‘Ah! Alas, my lord!’, as she was being carried off, lodged firmly in my heart burn every limb of my body like poison consumed.
6.5.8
तद्वियोगेन्धनवता
तच्चिन्ताविपुलार्चिषा ।
रात्रिन्दिवं शरीरं मे
दह्यते मदनाग्निना ॥
The fire of love, with the separation from her for firewood and the thoughts about her for clear flame, burns my body day and night.
6.5.9
अवगाह्यार्णवं स्वप्स्ये
सौमित्रे भवता विना ।
एवञ्च प्रज्वलन् कामः
न मा सुप्तं जले दहेत् ॥
I shall go down into the sea and sleep, even without you (guarding me); perhaps then, as I sleep in the water, the flame of love will not burn me.
6.5.10
बह्वेतत्कामयानस्य
शक्यमेतेन जीवितुम् ।
यदहं सा च वामोरूः
एकां धरणिमाश्रितौ ॥
That I and she of lovely thighs live on the same one earth seems to be more than enough to keep me, who is pining for love, alive.
6.5.11
केदारस्येव केदारः
सोदकस्य निरूदकः ।
उपस्नेहेन जीवामि
जीवन्तीं यच्छृणोमि ताम् ॥
Just as paddy in a dry field manages to survive next to a paddy field that is full of water, I am able to survive hearing the news that she is alive.
6.5.12
कदा नु खलु सुश्रोणीम्
शतपत्रायतेक्षणाम् ।
विजित्य शत्रून् द्रक्ष्यामि
सीतां स्फीतामिव श्रियम् ॥
Ah! when will I, having vanquished the enemies, see Seetā of lovely Kaṭee whose eyes are wide like lotuses who is like abundance of opulence personified!
6.5.13
कदा नु चारु बिम्बोष्ठम्
तस्याः पद्ममिवाननम् ।
ईषदुन्नाम्य पास्यामि
रसायनमिवातुरः ॥
When will I lift her lovely lotus face, with its lips like Bimba fruit, and swill (its sweetness), like one does a life-giving elixir?
6.5.14
तस्यास्तु संहतौ पीनौ
स्तनौ तालफलोपमौ ।
कदा नु खलु सोत्कम्पौ
श्लिष्यन्त्या मां भजिष्यतः ॥
When will her swaying, palm-fruit-like, full and rounded breasts that are pressed against each other, bestow their touch on me in an embrace?
6.5.15
सा नूनमसितापाङ्गी
रक्षोमध्यगता सती ।
मन्नाथा नाथहीनेव
त्रातारं नाधिगच्छति ॥
She of dark eyes, caught among the Rākshasis, finds no one coming to her rescue, as if there is no one who cares for her, in spite of having me for her husband.
6.5.16
कथं जनकराजस्य
दुहिता सा मम प्रिया ।
राक्षसीमध्यगा शेते
स्नुषा दशरथस्य च ॥
How can my beloved, the daughter of King Janaka and daughter-in-law of Daṡaratha, lie down to sleep, caught in the midst of the Rākshasis?
6.5.17
कदाऽविक्षोभ्यरक्षांसि
सा विधूयोत्पतिष्यति ।
विधूय जलदान्नीलान्
शशिरेखा शरत्स्विव ॥
Ah! When will she rise up, shaking out and pushing away the Rākshasas, like the digit of the moon that emerges in the post-monsoon season, pushing away the black rain clouds?
6.5.18
स्वभावतनुका नूनम्
शोकेनानशनेन च ।
भूयस्तनुतरा सीता
देशकालविपर्ययात् ॥
Seetā, slender by nature, would have become even thinner due to fasting and grief, with the situation taking this unimaginable turn.
6.5.19
कदा नु राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
निधायोरसि सायकान् ।
सीतां प्रत्याहरिष्यामि
शोकमुत्सृज्य मानसम् ॥
When will I regain Seetā, removing this grief from my heart and lodging arrows in the heart of the Lord of Rākshasas?
6.5.20
कदा नु खलु मां साध्वी
सीता सुरसुतोपमा ।
सोत्कण्ठा कण्ठमालम्ब्य
मोक्ष्यत्यानन्दजं पयः ॥
When will Seetā, the gentle and good woman, equal to the daughter of a Dēva, rush to hang her hands around my neck, shedding tears born out of joy?
6.5.21
कदा शोकमिमं घोरम्
मैथिलीविप्रयोगजम् ।
सहसा विप्रमोक्ष्यामि
वासः शुक्लेतरं यथा ॥
How soon can I discard this terrible grief that is engendered by the separation from Seetā, like a garment that is not clean and white?
6.5.22
एवं विलपतस्तस्य
तत्र रामस्य धीमतः ।
दिनक्षयान्मन्दरुचिः
भास्करोऽस्तमुपागमतः ॥
While the sagacious Rāma thus lamented, the sun set as the day receded, his brightness dimmed.
6.5.23
आश्वासितो लक्ष्मणेन
रामः सन्ध्यामुपासत ।
स्मरन् कमलपत्राक्षीम्
सीतां शोकाकुलीकृतः ॥
Consoled by Lakshmaṇa, Rāma, whose mind went into a turmoil with grief as he thought of Seetā of lotus-petal eyes, offered his prayers to Sandhya.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे पञ्चमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
