Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 84
In this Sarga, Vibheeshaṇa dismisses the possibility of someone killing Seetā, as she is zealously protected by Rāvaṇa. He tells Rāma and others that it is a trick played by Indrajit to the stop Vānaras from attacking him.
He tells Rāma that the immediate concern should be to kill Indrajit as he becomes invincible even to the Dēvas once he completes the Hōma at Nikumbhila. He asks Rāma to be at ease and to send Lakshmaṇa to interrupt the Hōma and eventually kill Indrajit.
6.84.1
राममाश्वासयाने तु
लक्ष्मणे भ्रातृवत्सले ।
निक्षिप्य गुल्मान् स्वस्थाने
तत्रागच्छद्विभीषणः ।
नानाप्रहरणैर्वीरैः
चतुर्भिः सचिवैर्वृतः ।
नीलाञ्जनचयाकारैः
मातङ्गैरिव यूथपः ॥
While Lakshmaṇa, who loved his brother, was thus consoling Rāma, Vibheeshaṇa stationed the forces in their respective camps, and came there accompanied by his four counselors, the valiant ones who looked like mounds of collyrium and were armed with every kind of weapon, like a lead elephant followed by its herd.
6.84.3
सोऽभिगम्य महात्मानम्
राघवं शोकलालसम् ।
वानरांश्चैव ददृशे
बाष्पपर्याकुलेक्षणान् ॥
Getting there, he saw Mahātma Rāghava plunged in grief, and Vānaras with tearful eyes.
6.84.4
राघवं च महात्मानम्
इक्ष्वाकुकुलनन्दनम् ।
ददर्श मोहमापन्नम्
लक्ष्मणस्याङ्कमाश्रितम् ॥
He saw Mahātma Rāghava, the delight of Ikshwāku race, lying on the lap of Lakshmaṇa, dazed and bewildered.
6.84.5
व्रीडितं शोकसन्तप्तम्
दृष्ट्वा रामं विभीषणः ।
अन्तर्दुःखेन दीनात्मा
किमेतदिति सोऽब्रवीत् ॥
Vibheeshaṇa, seeing that Rāma was burnt by grief and was feeling ashamed of himself, asked, 'What is the matter?', keeping the sorrow and sadness over it to himself.
6.84.6
विभीषणमुखं दृष्ट्वा
सुग्रीवं तांश्च वानरान् ।
लक्ष्मणोवाच मन्दार्थम्
इदं बाष्पपरिप्लुतः ॥
Looking at the face of Vibheeshaṇa, and then glancing at Sugreeva and the Vānaras, Lakshmaṇa, his face covered in tears, said these words that were depressing:
6.84.7
हतामिन्द्रजिता सीताम्
इह श्रुत्वैव राघवः ।
हनुमद्वचनात् सौम्य
ततो मोहमुपागतः ॥
Hearing the news brought by Hanumān that Seetā has been slain by Indrajit, Rāghava instantly fell in a swoon, my friend!
6.84.8
कथयन्तं तु सौमित्रिम्
सन्निवार्य विभीषणः ।
पुष्कलार्थमिदं वाक्यं
विसंज्ँं राममब्रवीत् ॥
Interrupting Sowmitri before he could complete his sentence, Vibheeshaṇa said these words of import to Rāma who had not yet regained his sense in full:
6.84.9
मनुजेन्द्रार्तरूपेण
यदुक्तं च हनूमता ।
तदयुक्तमहं मन्ये
सागरस्येव शोषणम् ॥
I am of the conviction that whatever was told by Hanumān in a state of concern and helplessness is as unlikely as oceans being dried up.
6.84.10
अभिप्रायं तु जानामि
रावणस्य दुरात्मनः ।
सीतां प्रति महाबाहो
न च घातं करिष्यति ॥
I know, O you of mighty arm, what exactly the evil-minded Rāvaṇa thinks about Seetā; he cannot kill her.
6.84.11
याच्यमानस्तु बहुशो
मया हितचिकीर्षुणा ।
वैदेहीमुत्सृजस्वेति
न च तत्कृतवान्वचः ॥
In spite of my begging him several times 'Please release Vaidēhee!', with his own interest in mind, he did not do that.
6.84.12
नैव साम्ना न दानेन
न भेदेन कुतो युधा ।
सा द्रुष्टुमपि शक्येत
नैव चान्येन केनचित् ॥
And it is impossible for anyone even to get a glance of her by either Sāma, or Dāna or Bhēda or even Daṇḍa.
6.84.13
वानरान् मोहयित्वा तु
प्रतियातः स राक्षसः ।
मायामयीं महाबाहो
तां विद्धि जनकात्मजाम् ॥
The Rākshasa has just tricked the Vānaras and gone way. Know, O you of mighty arm, that it is a phantom daughter of Janaka!
6.84.14
चैत्यं निकुम्भिलां नाम
प्राप्य होमं करिष्यति ।
हुतवानुपयातो हि
देवैरपि सवासवैः ।
दुराधर्षो भवत्येव
सङ्ग्रामे रावणात्मजः ॥
He has gone to a shrine by name Nikumbhila to perform a Hōma. Once the son of Rāvaṇa comes here after completing the offerings, he will become invincible in the battle even for the Dēvas led by Vāsava.
6.84.15
तेन मोहयता नूनम्
एषा माया प्रयोजिता ।
विघ्नमन्विच्छता तत्र
वानराणां पराक्रमे ॥
He has confounded the Vānaras with his tricks surely to divert them from going on the offense.
6.84.16
ससैन्यास्तत्र गच्छामो
यावत्तन्न समाप्यते ।
त्यजेमं नरशार्दूल
मिथ्यासन्तापमागतम् ।
सीदते हि बलं सर्वम्
दृष्ट्वा त्वां शोककर्शितम् ॥
Let us go there with the army before he completes the rite. Shake off this grief, O tiger among men, that befell on you with no basis in reality. The entire army has become downcast, seeing you wilt with grief.
6.84.18
इह त्वं स्वस्थहृदयः
तिष्ठ सत्त्वसमुच्छ्रितः ।
लक्ष्मणं प्रेषयास्माभिः
सह सैन्यानुकर्षिभिः ॥
Please remain here, setting your mind at ease and summoning courage to your aid. Please send Lakshmaṇa with us, and we will go taking the forces with us.
6.84.19
एष तं नरशार्दूलो
रावणिं निशितैः शरैः ।
त्याजयिष्यति तत् कर्म
ततो वध्यो भविष्यति ॥
With his sharp arrows, this tiger among men will make him give up the rite, and then he can be killed.
6.84.20
तस्यैते निशितास्तीक्ष्णाः
पत्रिपत्राङ्गवाजिनः ।
पतत्रिण इवासौम्याः
शराः पास्यन्ति शोणितम् ॥
His sharp arrows sped by the feathers of birds, shall drink his blood like birds of prey.
6.84.21
तं सन्दिश महाबाहो
लक्ष्मणं शुभलक्षणम् ।
राक्षसस्य विनाशाय
वज्रं वज्रधरो यथा ॥
Send Lakshmaṇa of auspicious marks, O you of mighty arm, to destroy the Rākshasa like the wielder of Vajra sends the Vajra.
6.84.22
मनुजवर न कालविप्रकर्षो
रिपुनिधनं प्रति यत् क्षमोऽद्य कर्तुम् ।
त्वमतिसृज रिपोर्वधाय वाणीम्
दिविजरिपोर्मथने यथा महेन्द्रः ॥
O best of men! There should be no delay now in destroying this foe. Please give the word of command to slay the enemy, like Mahēndra did for the destruction of the foes of Dēvas.
6.84.23
समाप्तकर्मा हि स राक्षसर्षभो
भवत्यदृश्यः समरे सुरासुरैः ।
युयुत्सता तेन समाप्तकर्मणा
भवेत्सुराणामपि संशयो महान् ॥
Once the rite is completed, that bull among Rākshasas becomes invisible in the fight even to Suras and Asuras. Once he enters the fray after completing the rite, it is doubtful whether even the Dēvas can destroy him.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे चतुरशीतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighty fourth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
