Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 81
In this short Sarga, Bharata is woken up in a royal manner with praises and the blowing of conches and pipe instruments. Bharata orders them to cease, declaring that he is not the king.
Meanwhile, Vasishṭha enters the assembly hall of Ikshwākus and orders the messengers to bring Bharata, Ṡatrughna, Yudhājit, Sumantra and others. When Bharata arrives every one receives him with much acclamation.
The assembly hall is seen to regain its former splendor that was seen there when Daṡaratha was present.
2.81.1
ततो नान्दीमुखीं रात्रिम्
भरतं सूतमागधाः ।
तुष्टुवुर्वाग्विशेषज्ञाः
स्तवैर्मङ्गलसंहितैः ॥
When the night that saw the performance of auspicious preliminary rites was almost spent, the bards and minstrels, who were skilled in the panegyrics of kings, extolled Bharata with praises along with auspicious notes.
2.81.2
सुवर्णकोणाभिहतः
प्राणदद्यामदुन्दुभिः ।
दध्मुश्शङ्खांश्च शतशो
नादांश्चोच्चावचस्वरान् ॥
The drums that indicate the passing of hours were struck by golden drumsticks. Conches and instruments that make a variety of sounds were blown in hundreds.
2.81.3
स तूर्यघोषस्सुमहान्
दिवमापूरयन्निव ।
भरतं शोकसन्तप्तम्
भूयश्शोकैररन्ध्रयत् ॥
Those soaring notes of the instruments that seemed to fill the heavens harrowed Bharata's heart, which was already burned with grief.
2.81.4
ततः प्रबुद्धो भरतः
तं घोषं सन्निवर्त्य च ।
नाहं राजेति चाप्युक्त्वा
शत्रुघ्नमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Waking up, Bharata had those sounds stopped, saying, 'I am not the King' and said these words to Ṡatrughna:
2.81.5
पश्य शत्रुघ्न कैकेय्या
लोकस्यापकृतं महत् ।
विसृज्य मयि दुःखानि
राजा दशरथो गतः ॥
O Ṡatrughna! See what a great harm has been done to the people by Kaikēyee! And King Daṡaratha has gone leaving me in this distress.
2.81.6
तस्यैषा धर्मराजस्य
धर्ममूला महात्मनः ।
परिभ्रमति राज्य श्रीः
नौरिवाकर्णिका जले ॥
This vast and rich kingdom of that righteous Mahātma king, which is rooted in Dharma, tosses about like a rudderless vessel in water.
2.81.7
यो हि नस्सुमहान्नाथः
सोऽपि प्रव्राजितो वनम् ।
अनया धर्ममुत्सृज्य
मात्रा मे राघवस्स्वयम् ॥
Rāghava, our outstanding leader, is in exile in the Vana sent by my mother herself in complete abandonment of Dharma.
2.81.8
इत्येवं भरतं प्रेक्ष्य
विलपन्तं विचेतनम् ।
कृपणं रुरुदुस्सर्वाः
सस्वरं योषित स्तदा ॥
Seeing Bharata thus disoriented and lamenting, all the women wailed aloud piteously in unison.
2.81.9
तथा तस्मिन्विलपति
वसिष्ठो राजधर्मवित् ।
सभामिक्ष्वाकुनाथस्य
प्रविवेश महायशाः ॥
While he lamented thus, Vasishṭha of great renown, a savant of royal affairs, entered the assembly hall of the Lord of Ikshwākus.
2.81.10
शातकुम्भमयीं रम्याम्
मणिरत्नसमाकुलाम् ।
सुधर्मामिव धर्मात्मा
सगणः प्रत्यपद्यत ॥
That Dharmātma, along with his attendants, entered the lovely assembly hall which was covered with gold and lavishly studded with precious stones and gems resembling Sudharma (the assembly hall of Indra).
2.81.11
स काञ्चनमयं पीठम्
सुखास्तरणसंवृतम् ।
अध्यास्त सर्ववेदज्ञो
दूताननुशशास च ॥
That savant of all Vedas sat on a golden seat, Which was covered with a comfortable spread, and issued orders to the messengers.
2.81.12
ब्राह्मणान् क्षत्रियान्वैश्यन्
अमात्यान्गणवल्लभान् ।
क्षिप्रमानयताऽव्यग्राः
कृत्यमात्ययिकं हि नः ॥
Bring Brāhmaṇas, Kshatriyas, Vaiṡyas, ministers and leaders of various groups at once. There is nothing to be worried about; we are faced with a business that brooks no delay.
Why are Ṡūdras not summoned? Because Ṡūdras are those people, who by nature are simple and like to stay away from the comlex nature of state or business or spiritual affairs.
2.81.13
सराजभृत्यं शत्रुघ्नम्
भरतं च यशस्विनम् ।
युधाजितं सुमन्त्रं च
ये च तत्र हिता जनाः ॥
(Bring) Ṡatrughna, the renowned Bharata, the royal staff, Yudhājit, Sumantra and the well-wishers that are there.
2.81.14
ततो हलहलाशब्दः
सुमहान्समपद्यत ।
रथैरश्वैर्गजैश्चापि
जनानामुपगच्छताम् ॥
Then there arose a great din of people coming by chariots, horses, and elephants.
2.81.15
ततो भरतमायान्तम्
शतक्रतुमिवामराः ।
प्रत्यनन्दन्प्रकृतयो
यथा दशरथं तथा ॥
Bharata who then arrived was received with great acclamation by the people just as Daṡaratha had been, and as Ṡatakratu (Indra) would be by Dēvatas.
2.81.16
ह्रद इव तिमिनागसंवृतः
स्तिमितजलो मणिशङ्खशर्करः ।
दशरथसुतशोभिता सभा
सदशरथेव बभौ यथा पुरा ॥
The assembly hall regained its splendor with the presence of the son of Daṡaratha that it had when Daṡaratha was present, and shined like a lake whose waters are settled with its gems, shells, fine sand, fish and snakes (in clear view).
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकाशीतितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighty first Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
