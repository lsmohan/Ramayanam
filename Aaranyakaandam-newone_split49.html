Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 49
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa, unperturbed by the warnings of Seetā, again boasts about his valor and prowess. He then gives up his mendicant form and shows his original terrifying form with all his ten heads.
He taunts Seetā for her devotion to Rāma and tells her that he alone is a worthy husband for her. Then he grabs her with his two hands and places her on his lap and on the chariot, which then rises into the air.
Seetā wails and pleads with every creature in the Vana, with the river Gōdāvaree, with the Prasravaṇa mountain, and with all the Dēvatas of the Vana, to convey to Rāma that she has been carried off by Rāvaṇa.
Then she sees Jaṭāyu perched on the tree and tells him also to convey everything as it happened to Rāma and Lakshmaṇa. She also cautions Jaṭāyu that Rāvaṇa is too strong to be restrained by him.
3.49.1
सीताया वचनं श्रुत्वा
दशग्रीवः प्रतापवान् ।
हस्ते हस्तं समाहत्य
चकार सुमहद्वपुः ॥
Hearing those words of Seetā, he, of ten necks, of great prowess, struck one of his palms against the other and made his body grow to a giant size.
3.49.2
स मैथिलीं पुनर्वाक्यम्
बभाषे च ततो भृशम् ।
नोन्मत्तया श्रुतौ मन्ये
मम वीर्यपराक्रमौ ॥
He who had no dearth of words to dole out, said these words again to Maithili: ‘I think, indulged in yourself, you have not paid attention to (the description of) my valor and prowess.’
3.49.3
उद्वहेयं भुजाभ्यां तु
मेदिनीमम्बरे स्थितः ।
आपिबेयं समुद्रं च
हन्यां मृत्युं रणे स्थितः ॥
I can stand in the sky and carry the earth on my shoulders. I can drink the ocean. In the battle field, I can kill death itself.
3.49.4
अर्कं रुन्ध्यां शरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
विभिन्ध्यां हि महीतलम् ।
कामरूपिणमुन्मत्ते
पश्य मां कामदं पतिम् ॥
I can block the sun and smash the earth with my sharp arrows. O crazy one, look at me, a husband who can grant every wish and who can take any form at will.
3.49.5
एवमुक्तवतस्तस्य
सूर्यकल्पे शिखिप्रभे ।
क्रुद्धस्य हरिपर्यन्ते
रक्ते नेत्रे बभूवतुः ॥
His anger rising as he said this, his eyes, resembling the sun, blazing like fire, became red and their far corners became black.
3.49.6
सद्यस्सौम्यं परित्यज्य
भिक्षुरूपं स रावणः ।
स्वं रूपं कालरूपाभम्
भेजे वैश्रवणानुजः ॥
In no time, the younger brother of Vaiṡravaṇa gave up the agreeable and gentle form of the mendicant and took his original form that resembled that of Yama.
3.49.7
संरक्तनयनश्श्रीमान्
तप्तकाञ्चनभूषणः ।
क्रोधेन महताविष्टो
नीलजीमूतसन्निभः ।
दशास्यः कार्मुकी बाणी
बभूव क्षणदाचरः ॥
He was overwhelmed with great rage and his eyes became blood-red. Looking like a black cloud, wielding bow and arrows, ornaments made of gold purified in fire, that Rākshasa of ten faces attained a (weird) splendor!
3.49.8
स परिव्राजकच्छद्म
महाकायो विहाय तत् ।
प्रतिपद्य स्वकं रूपम्
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ।
संरक्तनयनः क्रोधात्
जीमूतनिचयप्रभः ।
रक्ताम्बरधरस्तस्थौ
स्त्रीरत्नं प्रेक्ष्य मैथिलीम् ॥
Giving up the form of Parivrājaka, Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, stood in his original gigantic form, eyes blood-red with anger, shining like a black cloud and wearing a blood-red garment, looking at Maithili, a gem among women.
3.49.10
स तामसितकेशान्ताम्
भास्करस्य प्रभामिव ।
वसनाभरणोपेताम्
मैथिलीं रावणोऽब्रवीत् ॥
And Rāvaṇa said to Maithili, who was radiant like the sun, covered in garments and ornaments, with her hair black till the ends:
3.49.11
त्रिषु लोकेषु विख्यातम्
यदि भर्तारमिच्छसि ।
मामाश्रय वरारोहे
तवाऽहं सदृशः पतिः ॥
If you like to have someone who is famous in the three worlds as your husband, come to me, O you of excellent Kaṭee! I am the husband that is just right for you!
3.49.12
मां भजस्व चिराय त्वम्
अहं श्लाघ्यः पतिस्तव ।
नैव चाहं क्वचिद्भद्रे
करिष्ये तव विप्रियम् ।
त्यज्यतां मानुषे भावो
मयि भावः प्रणीयताम् ॥
My dear! Be with me forever. I will be your well regarded husband. I will never do anything that displeases you. Remove that human from your thoughts and let your mind focus on me.
3.49.14
राज्याच्च्युतमसिद्धार्थम्
रामं परिमितायुषम् ।
कैर्गुणैरनुरक्तासि
मूढे पण्डितमानिनि ।
यः स्त्रिया वचनाद्राज्यम्
विहाय ससुहृज्जनम् ।
अस्मिन्व्यालानुचरिते
वने वसति दुर्मतिः ॥
O fool, you think you know everything! What virtues of Rāma, who lost his kingdom and whose days are numbered, make you love him? That fool abjured the kingdom and his kith and kin based on the word of a woman, and is roaming in a Vana where wild beasts roam.
3.49.16
इत्युक्त्वा मैथिलीं वाक्यम्
प्रियार्हां प्रियवादिनीम् ।
अभिगम्य सुदुष्टात्मा
राक्षसः काममोहितः ।
जग्राह रावणस्सीताम्
बुधः खे रोहिणीमिव ॥
Saying these words to Maithili who always spoke pleasingly and who deserved to be spoken to in kind words, Rāvaṇa, the terribly wicked Rākshasa, caught in the pangs of lust, seized Seetā, like planet Budha besetting star Rōhiṇee in the sky.
3.49.17
वामेन सीतां पद्माक्षीम्
मूर्धजेषु करेण सः ।
ऊर्वोस्तु दक्षिणेनैव
परिजग्राह पाणिना ॥
He grabbed Seetā of lotus eyes by her hair with his left hand and by her thighs with his right hand.
3.49.18
तं दृष्ट्वा मृत्युसङ्काशम्
तीक्ष्णदंष्ट्रं महाभुजम् ।
प्राद्रवन् गिरिसङ्काशम्
भयार्ता वनदेवताः ॥
On seeing him of sharp teeth and great arms who looked like Death, the Dēvatas of the Vana fled in panic.
3.49.19
स च मायामयो दिव्यः
खरयुक्तः खरस्वनः ।
प्रत्यदृश्यत हेमाङ्गो
रावणस्य महारथः ॥
Then the great, splendid and magical chariot of Rāvaṇa, with all its parts laid in gold, appeared, yoked with donkeys and rumbling with the sound of braying of donkeys.
3.49.20
ततस्तां परुषैर्वाक्यैः
भर्त्सयन्स महास्वनः ।
अङ्केनादाय वैदेहीम्
रथमारोपयत्तदा ॥
Threatening her with his strident voice and harsh words, he grabbed Vaidēhee onto his lap and got her on to the chariot.
As has been warned by Rāma (Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa, Sargas 28), the Vana proved to be a dreadful place.
Seetā is facing this terrible fate for the second time, of being grabbed by a Rākshasa on to his lap. The first time she faced this was when Virādha grabbed her (3.2.10a) as soon as they entered the Daṇḍaka Araṇya.
3.49.21
सा गृहीता विचुक्रोश
रावणेन यशस्स्विनी ।
रामेति सीता दुःखार्ता
रामं दूरगतंवने ॥
Seized by Rāvaṇa, Seetā of great repute screamed in misery and desperation, ‘ O Rāma!’, calling upon Rāma, who had gone far away into the Vana.
3.49.22
तामकामां स कामार्तः
पन्नगेन्द्रवधूमिव ।
विवेष्टमानामादाय
उत्पपाताथ रावणः ॥
Rāvaṇa, griped with passion, took her, who had no love for him, as she writhed like a mighty female serpent, and rose up into the air.
3.49.23
ततस्सा राक्षसेन्द्रेण
ह्रियमाणा विहायसा ।
भृशं चुक्रोश मत्तेव
भ्रान्तचित्ता यथाऽऽतुरा ॥
As she was carried off along the sky by the lord of Rākshasas, she screamed terribly as if she was delirious and as if her senses were unhinged by pain:
3.49.24
हा लक्ष्मण महाबाहो
गुरुचित्तप्रसादक ।
ह्रियमाणां न जानीषे
रक्षसा माममर्षिणा ॥
Ah! Lakshmaṇa! O you of the mighty arm, you who are ever intent on pleasing your elder brother! You do not know that I am being carried off by an enraged Rākshasa!
3.49.25
जीवितं सुखमर्थांश्च
धर्महेतोः परित्यजन् ।
ह्रियमाणामधर्मेण
मां राघव न पश्यसि ॥
O Rāghava! You have given up your life, pleasures and goals for the sake of Dharma! Yet, you do not pay attention when I am being carried off in complete abandon of Dharma!
3.49.26
ननु नामाविनीतानाम्
विनेताऽसि परन्तप ।
कथमेवंविधं पापम्
न त्वं शासि हि रावणम् ॥
O scorcher of the foe! You indeed bring the undisciplined in discipline! Yet, how is it that you do not exercise your power over such a sinful one like this Rāvaṇa!
3.49.27
ननु सद्योऽविनीतस्य
दृश्यते कर्मणःफलम् ।
कालोऽप्यङ्गीभवत्यत्र
सस्यानामिव पक्तये ॥
But of course, the results of a brash act are not seen immediately; time has to play its part, just as it does in the ripening of a crop.
3.49.28
त्वं कर्म कृतवानेतत्
कालोपहतचेतनः ।
जीवितान्तकरं घोरम्
रामाद्व्यसनमाप्नुहि ॥
(O Rāvaṇa!) Your senses must have been undone by the (vagaries of) time for you to do something like this. You are destined to confront a terrible fate that sees the end of your life, from Rāma.  
3.49.29
हन्तेदानीं सकामास्तु
कैकेयी सह बान्धवैः ।
ह्रिये यद्धर्मकामस्य
धर्मपत्नी यशस्विनः ॥
Perhaps, Kaikēyee and her kindred would have their wish fulfilled, with me, who is wedded in Dharma to that renowned one who always wishes for Dharma, abducted.
At a moment of hardship, it is but natural to think of people who would sympathize with the hardship and people who would celebrate it. Rāma expresses the same feelings, when he spends his first night outside of the palace along with Seetā and Lakshmaṇa (See Ṡlōkas 2.53.6), after he was exiled.
3.49.30
आमन्त्रये जनस्थाने
कर्णिकारान्सुपुष्पितान् ।
क्षिप्रं रामाय शंसध्वम्
सीतां हरति रावणः ॥
I beseech you, O Karṇikāra bushes in bloom, please tell Rāma quick that Rāvaṇa is carrying off Seetā!
3.49.31
माल्यवन्तं शिखरिणम्
वन्दे प्रस्रवणं गिरिम् ।
क्षिप्रं रामाय शंस त्वम्
सीतां हरति रावणः ॥
I make my obeisance to you, O crested Prasravaṇa abounding in flowers, please tell Rāma quick that Rāvaṇa is carrying off Seetā!
3.49.32
हंसकारण्डवाकीर्णाम्
वन्दे गोदावरीं नदीम् ।
क्षिप्रं रामाय शंस त्वम्
सीतां हरति रावणः ॥
I bow to you, Gōdāvaree, crowded with swans and Kāraṇḍavas; please tell Rāma quick that Rāvaṇa is carrying off Seetā!
3.49.33
दैवतानि च यान्यस्मिन्
वने विविधपादपे ।
नमस्करोम्यहं तेभ्यो
भर्तुश्शंस्तु मां हृताम् ॥
O Dēvatas, whoever you might be, that reside in the different trees in the forest, I pay my obeisance to you, please tell my husband that I am being carried off.
3.49.34
यानि कानि चिदप्यत्र
सत्त्वानि निवसन्त्युत ।
सर्वाणि शरणं यामि
मृगपक्षिगणानपि ।
ह्रियमाणां प्रियां भर्तुः
प्राणेभ्योऽपि गरीयसीम् ।
विवशाऽपहृता सीता
रावणेनेति शंसत ॥
I beg each and every creature that lives here, however small it may be, and each and every bird and animal, please tell my husband, who loves me and holds me dearer than life, that I am helplessly being carried off by Rāvaṇa.
3.49.36
विदित्वा मां महाबाहुः
अमुत्रापि महाबलः ।
आनेष्यति पराक्रम्य
वैवस्वतहृतामपि ॥
He of mighty arm and mighty strength will find me and bring me back with his prowess, even if I am carried off by Yama and even if am kept in a different world.
3.49.37
सा तदा करुणा वाचो
विलपन्ती सुदुःखिता ।
वनस्पतिगतं गृध्रम्
ददर्शाऽयतलोचना ॥
The wide-eyed one wailing thus in terrible grief and saying those pitiable words, saw the eagle perched on a tree.
3.49.38
सा तमुद्वीक्ष्य सुश्रोणी
रावणस्य वशं गता ।
समाक्रन्दद्भयपरा
दुःखोपहतया गिरा ॥
On seeing him, she of excellent Kaṭee, caught in the clutches of Rāvaṇa and gripped by fear, shouted aloud, in her grief-stricken tone:
3.49.39
जटायो पश्य मामद्य
ह्रियमाणामनाथवत् ।
अनेन राक्षसेन्द्रेण
करुणं पापकर्मणा ॥
O Jaṭāyu! look at me, in this pitiful plight now, being carried off like an orphan, by this evil lord of Rākshasas!
3.49.40
नैष वारयितुं शक्यः
तव क्रूरो निशाचरः ।
सत्ववान् जितकाशी च
सायुधश्चैव दुर्मतिः ॥
This Night-rover, who is armed, cruel and wicked, possessing great strength and basking in (the glory of) his victories, cannot be restrained by you.
3.49.41
रामाय तु यथातत्त्वम्
जटायो हरणं मम ।
लक्ष्मणाय च तत्सर्वम्
आख्यातव्यमशेषतः ॥
O Jaṭāyu! Please tell Rāma and Lakshmaṇa all about my abduction, as it happened, without missing any detail.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे एकोनपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty ninth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
