Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 43
In this Sarga, after destroying the Vana and killing the Kiṅkaras, Hanumān thinks of destroying the palatial temple in the Vana. Climbing on to the top of the temple, Hanumān proclaims again the certain victory of Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva. Then one hundred of the guards of the temple attack him with a variety of weapons.
Hanumān quickly plucks a pillar out of that palace and whirls it, from which rises a fire that consumes the palatial temple. Then he kills the one hundred guards. Then he warns everyone that crores of Vānaras under the command of Sugreeva will come and kill all of them, and that they cannot hope to survive, having made Rāma their arch enemy.
5.43.1
ततस्स किङ्करान्हत्वा
हनुमान्ध्यानमास्थितः ॥
Hanumān, having killed the Kiṅkaras, pondered:
5.43.1
वनं भग्नं मया चैत्य
प्रासादो न विनाशितः ।
तस्मात्प्रासादमप्येवम्
इमं विध्वंसयाम्यहं ॥
I have destroyed the Vana, but not the palatial shrine. Hence I shall destroy this palace in the same way.
5.43.2
इति सञ्चिन्त्य मनसा
हनुमान्दर्शयन्बलम् ।
चैत्यप्रासादमाप्लुत्य
मेरुशृङ्गमिवोन्नतम् ।
आरुरोह कपिश्रेष्ठो
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ॥
Thinking thus in his mind, Hanumān, the best of Vānaras and the son of Vāyu, flew to the top of the palace that was as lofty as the crest of Mēru, displaying his strength.
5.43.4
आरुह्य गिरिसङ्काशम्
प्रासादं हरियूथपः ।
बभौ स सुमहातेजाः
प्रतिसूर्य इवोदितः ॥
Standing on that mountain-like palace, the chief among the Vānara warriors, of immense power, looked like another rising sun.
5.43.5
संप्रधृष्य च दुर्धर्षम्
चैत्यप्रासादमुत्तमम् ।
हनुमान्प्रज्वलन्लक्ष्म्या
पारियात्रोपमोऽभवत् ॥
Blazing with splendor and standing on that formidable and splendid palatial shrine, he looked like the mountain Pāriyātra.
5.43.6
स भूत्वा सुमहाकायः
प्रभावान्मारुतात्मजः ।
धृष्टमास्फोटयामास
लङ्कां शब्देन पूरयन् ॥
Assuming a huge body with his special power, the son of Vāyu clapped his shoulders, filling Laṅkā with that sound.
5.43.7
तस्यास्फोटितशब्देन
महता श्रोत्रघातिना ।
पेतुर्विहङ्गमास्तत्र
चैत्यपालाश्च मोहिताः ॥
The ear-piercing din caused by that clapping, brought down the birds from the sky and threw the guards of the shrine into a swoon.
5.43.8
अस्त्रविज्जयतां रामो
लक्ष्मणश्च महाबलः ।
राजा जयति सुग्रीवो
राघवेणाभिपालितः ॥
Victorious is Rāma, a savant of Astras, and Lakshmaṇa, the immensely strong, and victorious is the king Sugreeva, who has the support of Rāghava.
5.43.9
दासोऽहं कोसलेन्द्रस्य
रामस्याक्लिष्टकर्मणः ।
हनुमान्शत्रुसैन्यानाम्
निहन्ता मारुतात्मजः ॥
I am the servant of Rāma, the king of Kōsala who does everything with ease. I am Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, who decimates the armies of the enemies.
5.43.10
न रावणसहस्रं मे
युद्धे प्रतिबलं भवेत् ।
शिलाभिस्तु प्रहरतः
पादपैश्च सहस्रशः ॥
Not even an army of a thousand Rāvaṇas can stand against me in fight, as I assault with thousands of boulders and trees.
5.43.11
अर्दयित्वा पुरीं लङ्काम्
अभिवाद्य च मैथिलीम् ।
समृद्धार्थो गमिष्यामि
मिषतां सर्वरक्षसाम् ॥
Harrowing the city of Laṅkā, and making prostrations to Maithili, I shall go back, my mission fulfilled, as the Rākshasas look on helplessly.
5.43.12
एवमुक्त्वा विमानस्थः
चैत्यस्थान्हरियूथपः ।
ननाद भीमनिर्ह्रादो
रक्षसां जनयन्भयम् ॥
Saying these words, sitting on top of the tower of that shrine, the chief among Vānara warriors let off a frightful shout, terrifying the Rākshasas.
5.43.13
तेन शब्देन महता
चैत्यपालाश्शतं ययुः ।
गृहीत्वा विविधानस्त्रान्
प्रासान्खङ्गान्परश्वथान् ।
विसृजन्तो महाकाया
मारुतिं पर्यवारयन् ॥
Provoked by that mighty shout, one hundred guards of the shrine went forth armed with various Astras like Prāsas, swords and Paraṡus and surrounded the son of Vāyu, hurling them at his huge body.
5.43.14
ते गदाभिर्विचित्राभिः
परिघैः काञ्चनाङ्गदैः ।
आजघ्नुर्वानरश्रेष्ठम्
बाणैश्चादित्यसन्निभैः ॥
They attacked that eminent Vānara with a variety of maces, with Parighas that sported gold bands, and with arrows that shone like the sun.
5.43.15
आवर्त इव गङ्गायाः
तोयस्य विपुलो महान् ।
परिक्षिप्य हरिश्रेष्ठम्
स बभौ रक्षसां गणः ।
ततो वातात्मजः क्रुद्धो
भीम रूपं समास्थितः ॥
That horde of Rākshasas, as they circled the foremost of Vānaras, looked like a great whirlpool in the waters of river Gaṅgā, while the enraged son of Vāyu stood firmly in his terrifying form.
5.43.17
प्रासादस्य महन्तस्य
स्तम्बं हेमपरिष्कृतम् ।
उत्पाटयित्वा वेगेन
हनुमान्पवनात्मजः ॥
Then Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, plucked, in no time, one of the pillars that was decked with gold, of that huge mansion.
5.43.18
ततस्तं भ्रामयामास
शतधारं महाबलः ।
तत्र चाग्निस्समभवत्
प्रासादश्चाप्यदह्यत ॥
Then he of immense strength whirled it, which had hundred sides. Then fire arose from it, burning the mansion.
5.43.19
दह्यमानं ततो दृष्ट्वा
प्रासादं हरियूथपः ।
स राक्षसशतं हत्त्वा
वज्रेणेन्द्र इवासुरान् ।
अन्तरिक्षे स्थित श्रीमान्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
After watching that mansion burn down, the chief among the Vānara warriors killed the one hundred Rākshasas like Indra did the Asuras with Vajra. Then hovering in the air, the blessed one said these words:
5.43.20
मादृशानां सहस्राणि
विसृष्टानि महात्मनाम् ।
बलिनां वानरेन्द्राणाम्
सुग्रीववशवर्तिनाम् ।
अटन्ति वसुधां कृत्स्नाम्
वयमन्ये च वानराः ॥
Thousands under the command of Sugreeva, of immense strength, comparable to me have been dispatched; we and other Vānaras are now scourging the entire earth.
5.43.22
दशनागबलाः केचित्
केचिद्दशगुणोत्तराः ।
केचिन्नागसहस्रस्य
बभूवुस्तुल्यविक्रमाः ॥
Some of them are as strong as ten elephants, some ten times as much, and some equal a thousand elephants in their prowess.
5.43.23
सन्ति चौघबलाः केचित्
केचिद्वायुबलोपमाः ।
अप्रमेयबलाश्चान्ये
तत्रासन्हरियूथपाः ॥
Some of the Vānara warriors are as powerful as giant rapids, some are comparable to wind in strength and the strength of the others is beyond measure.
5.43.24
ईदृग्विधैस्तु हरिभिः
वृतो दन्तनखायुधैः ।
शतैश्शतसहस्रैश्च
कोटीभिरयुतैरपि ।
आगमिष्यति सुग्रीवः
सर्वेषां वो निषूदनः ॥
Sugreeva, with hundreds, tens of thousands, hundreds of thousands and crores of Vānaras like these, with teeth and nails for weapons following him, will come and kill all of you.
5.43.25
नेयमस्ति पुरी लङ्का
न यूयं न च रावणः ।
यस्मादिक्ष्वाकुनाथेन
बद्धं वैरं महात्मना ॥
No more will there be this city of Laṅkā, or you or Rāvaṇa, having yourself made Mahātma Rāma, the lord of Ikshwāku dynasty your arch enemy.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे त्रिचत्वारिशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty third Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
