Baala Kaanda - Sarga 35
The stories by Viṡwāmitra continue in this Sarga. Viṡwāmitra tells about the two daughters of Himavān (Himalaya Mountains), Gaṅgā and Uma, and how Gaṅgā happened to ascend to Heaven.
1.35.1
उपास्य रात्रिशेषं तु
शोणाकूले महर्षिभिः ।
निशायां सुप्रभातायाम्
विश्वामित्रोऽभ्यभाषत ॥
Viṡwāmitra, along with all the other Ṛshis spent the night on the banks of river Ṡōṇā. As the night was broken by dawn, he woke up and said (to Rāma):
Older people sleep less and get up sooner than younger people. Then they wake up the younger ones. That builds a bond between the young and old.
Who wouldn't reminisce nostalgically about the days when grandparents woke them up from bed in their childhood?
In the atomic families of today, it is the turn of the parents to wake up children. But it would not be same experience. It does not give the same emotional caressing that children get when woken up by grandparents.
1.35.2
सुप्रभाता निशा राम
पूर्वा सन्ध्या प्रवर्तते ।
उत्तिष्ठोत्तिष्ठ भद्रं ते
गमनायाभिरोचय ॥
O Rāma! The night in the eastern skies has receded and dawn is upon us, boding a fine morning. Please get up and get ready for the onward journey.
This Ṡlōka is a variation of Ṡlōka 1.23.2, which is also adopted as the first Ṡlōka of Sri Venkatēswara Suprabhātam.
1.35.3
तच्छ्रुत्वा वचनं तस्य
कृत्वा पौर्वाह्णिकीं क्रियाम् ।
गमनं रोचयामास
वाक्यं चेदमुवाच ह ॥
Rāma woke up with those words of Viṡwāmitra. He completed all the morning ablutions and asked the following while getting ready for the journey:
1.35.4
अयं शोणः शुभजलो
गाधः पुलिनमण्डितः ।
कतरेण पथा ब्रह्मन्
सन्तरिष्यामहे वयम् ॥
O Brahman! The waters of this river Ṡōṇā are enchanting and shallow with splendid beaches. Where shall we cross this river?
1.35.5
एवमुक्तस्तु रामेण
विश्वामित्रोऽब्रवीदिदम् ।
एष पन्था मयोद्दिष्टो
येन यान्ति महर्षयः ॥
To that question of Rāma, Viṡwāmitra said: “The Maharshis shall take the path that I had planned.”
1.35.6
एवमुक्ता महर्षयो
विश्वामित्रेण धीमता ।
पश्यन्तस्ते प्रयाता वै
वनानि विविधानि च ॥
Taking cue from those words of the learned Viṡwāmitra, they proceeded on their journey enjoying the many Vanas on the path.
1.35.7
ते गत्वा दूरमध्वानम्
गतेऽर्द्धदिवसे तदा ।
जाह्नवीं सरितां श्रेष्ठाम्
ददृशुर्मुनिसेविताम् ॥
After travelling quite a while, almost till noon, they reached the banks of river Gaṅgā, the prominent among all the rivers, that is worshipped by Munis.
1.35.8
तां दृष्ट्वा पुण्यसलिलाम्
हंससारससेविताम् ।
बभूवुर्मुनयः सर्वे
मुदिताः सहराघवाः ॥
All of them, including the prince of Raghu dynasty felt excited to see the enchanting waters of Gaṅgā full of Hamsa and Sārasa birds.
1.35.9
तस्यास्तीरे ततश्चक्रुः
ते आवासपरिग्रहम् ।
ततः स्नात्वा यथान्यायम्
सन्तर्प्य पितृदेवताः ।
हुत्वा चैवाग्निहोत्राणि
प्राश्य चामृतवद्धविः ।
विविशुर्जाह्नवीतीरे
शुचौ मुदितमानसाः ।
विश्वामित्रं महात्मानम्
परिवार्य समन्ततः ॥
All of them took a break on the banks of that river. They bathed and completed their daily ablutions and made offering to Pitṛ Dēvatas. They performed fire rituals and ate the Havis that was like Amṛt.
They then sat around the Mahātma Viṡwāmitra in every direction, on that sacred and clean banks of river Gaṅgā with pleasant minds and peaceful hearts.
1.35.11
अथ तत्र तदा रामो
विश्वामित्रमथाब्रवीत् ।
भगवन् श्रोतुमिच्छामि
गङ्गां त्रिपथगां नदीम् ।
त्रैलोक्यं कथमाक्रम्य
गता नदनदीपतिम् ॥
Then Rāma asked Viṡwāmitra: Bhagawan! I am curious to know about river Gaṅgā that flows in three paths in the three worlds and how it finally reaches its destination, the ocean, where all the east and west flowing rivers reach!
1.35.13
चोदितो रामवाक्येन
विश्वामित्रो महामुनिः ।
वृद्धिं जन्म च गङ्गाया
वक्तुमेवोपचक्रमे ॥
In response to that question of Rāma, the great Muni Viṡwāmitra began the story of the origin and course of the Gaṅgā.
1.35.14
शैलेन्द्रो हिमवान्नाम
धातूनामाकरो महान् ।
तस्य कन्याद्वयं राम
रूपेणाप्रतिमं भुवि ॥
The great Himavān, the king among mountains, the abode of all minerals and materials, had two daughters who were unequaled in the world.
1.35.15
या मेरुदुहिता राम
तयोर्माता सुमध्यमा ।
नाम्ना मनोरमा नाम
पत्नी हिमवतः प्रिया ॥
Their mother, by name Manōrama, the daughter of Mēru mountain, was his darling and beautiful wife.
1.35.16
तस्यां गङ्गेयमभवत्
ज्येष्ठा हिमवतः सुता ।
उमा नाम द्वितीयाऽभूत्
नाम्ना तस्यैव राघव ॥
To that Himavān, through that Manōrama, were born the older daughter known as Gaṅgā and the younger daughter known as Uma.
1.35.17
अथ ज्येष्ठां सुराः सर्वे
देवतार्थचिकीर्षया ।
शैलेन्द्रं वरयामासुः
गङ्गां त्रिपथगां नदीम् ॥
All the Dēvas, implored the king among mountains to give them the older daughter, the Gaṅgā, for fulfilling a divine purpose.
1.35.18
ददौ धर्मेण हिमवान्
तनयां लोकपावनीम् ।
स्वच्छन्दपथगां गङ्गाम्
त्रैलोक्यहितकाम्यया ॥
Considering it to be appropriate and responsible, Himavān gave his daughter, one who could make all the worlds auspicious, who had the free will to choose her own path, for the benefit of all the three worlds.
1.35.19
प्रतिगृह्य त्रिलोकार्थम्
त्रिलोकहितकारिणः ।
गङ्गामादाय तेऽगच्छन्
कृतार्थेनान्तरात्मना ॥
The Dēvas, interested in the welfare of all the three worlds, then took Gaṅgā with them and left, having their purpose fulfilled.
1.35.20
या चान्या शैलदुहिता
कन्याऽऽसीद्रघुनन्दन ।
उग्रं सा व्रतमास्थाय
तपस्तेपे तपोधना ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty, The other daughter of the king among mountains, had taken upon a serious Tapa with strict regimen.
1.35.21
उग्रेण तपसा युक्ताम्
ददौ शैलवरः सुताम् ।
रुद्रायाप्रतिरूपाय
उमां लोकनमस्कृताम् ॥
That prominent mountain gave his daughter who performed great Tapa, has no equal and who was revered by all the worlds, to Rudra (in marriage).
1.35.22
एते ते शैलराजस्य
सुते लोकनमस्कृते ।
गङ्गा च सरितां श्रेष्ठा
उमा देवी च राघव ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Gaṅgā, the best among rivers and Uma, the one greatly revered by all peoples were the daughters of that king among mountains.
1.35.23
एतत्ते सर्वमाख्यातम्
यथा त्रिपथगा नदी ।
खं गता प्रथमं तात
गतिं गतिमतां वर ॥
My dear Rāma of illustrious character! This is the detail of the story of how Gaṅgā that flows in three paths, happened to go to the Heaven first.
1.35.24
सैषा सुरनदी रम्या
शैलेन्द्रस्य सुता तदा ।
सुरलोकं समारूढा
विपापा जलवाहिनी ॥
This is that enchanting daughter of the king among mountains who flows in the form of waters, capable of cleaning all Pāpas, that ascended to the Dēva Lōka and became a divine river.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे पञ्चत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty fifth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
