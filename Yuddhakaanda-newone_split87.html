Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 87
In this Sarga, Vibheeshaṇa takes Lakshmaṇa to a huge banyan tree, by which Indrajit would perform the rite that makes him invisible and hence invincible. Vibheeshaṇa advises Lakshmaṇa that he should slay Indrajit before he reaches the tree. Meanwhile, Indrajit appears in his chariot and Lakshmaṇa challenges him to a fight.
However, Indrajit turns to Vibheeshaṇa and lashes out at him for deserting his own brother and his kindred, and serving the enemy. Vibheeshaṇa lashes out back at him, for the disrespect he showed to him, with no regard to his character, which is to follow Dharma and stay away from cruel deeds. He recounts all the evils of Rāvaṇa, and reminds that it is Rāvaṇa who abjured him.
6.87.1
एवमुक्त्वा तु सौमित्रिम्
जातहर्षो विभीषणः ।
धनुष्पाणिनमादाय
त्वरमाणो जगाम ह ॥
Saying this to Sowmitri in all excitement, and taking him, who was armed with a bow, along with him Vibheeshaṇa went forth quickly.
6.87.2
अविदूरं ततो गत्वा
प्रविश्य च महद्वनम् ।
दर्शयामास तत्कर्म
लक्ष्मणाय विभीषणः ॥
Vibheeshaṇa entered a huge grove, which did not take long to reach, and showed the place of the rite to Lakshmaṇa.
6.87.3
नीलजीमूतसङ्काशम्
न्यग्रोधं भीमदर्शनम् ।
तेजस्वी रावणभ्राता
लक्ष्मणाय न्यवेदयत् ॥
The powerful brother of Rāvaṇa pointed out to Lakshmaṇa a huge banyan tree that looked ominous like a black raincloud.
6.87.4
इहोपहारं भूतानाम्
बलवान् रावणात्मजः ।
उपहृत्य ततः पश्चात्
सङ्ग्राममभिवर्तते ॥
The strong son of Rāvaṇa goes to a fight after offering propitiating oblations to the spirits here.
6.87.5
अदृश्यः सर्वभूतानाम्
ततो भवति राक्षसः ।
निहन्ति समरे शत्रून्
बध्नाति च शरोत्तमैः ॥
Then he, the Rākshasa, becomes invisible to all creatures and then destroys his enemies in a fight binding them with the best of arrows.
6.87.6
तमप्रविष्टन्यग्रोधम्
बलिनं रावणात्मजम् ।
विध्वंसय शरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
सरथं साश्वसारथिम् ॥
Slay him, and his chariot, his horses and his charioteer with your sharp arrows before he reaches the banyan tree.
6.87.7
तथेत्युक्त्वा महातेजाः
सौमित्रिर्मित्रनन्दनः ।
बभूवावस्थितस्तत्र
चित्रं विस्फारयन् धनुः ॥
Saying 'yes', Sowmitri of immense power, the delight of his friends, took his stand right there, twanging his stylish bow.
6.87.8
स रथेनाग्निवर्णेन
बलवान् रावणात्मजः ।
इन्द्रजित् कवची धन्वी
सध्वजः प्रत्यदृश्यत ॥
Then Indrajit, the strong son of Rāvaṇa, with his coat of mail and bow appeared there in his chariot that shone like fire, with its flag post.
6.87.9
तमुवाच महातेजाः
पौलस्त्यमपराजितम् ।
समाह्वये त्वां समरे
सम्यग्युद्धं प्रयच्छ मे ॥
Then that immensely powerful one told the descendant of Paulastya, who never knew defeat, 'let me invite you to a battle; give me a fight that is worthy!'
6.87.10
एवमुक्तो महातेजा
मनस्वी रावणात्मजः ।
अब्रवीत् परुषं वाक्यम्
तत्र दृष्ट्वा विभीषणम् ॥
Thus told, the son of Rāvaṇa, of immense power and great self esteem spoke these harsh words on seeing Vibheeshaṇa there:
6.87.11
इह त्वं जातसंवृद्धः
साक्षाद्भ्राता पितुर्मम ।
कथं द्रुह्यसि पुत्रस्य
पितृव्यो मम राक्षस ॥
You were born and brought up here. You are my father’s own brother. How could you, my uncle, bring yourself to harm me, O Rākshasa?
6.87.12
न ज्ञातित्वं न सौहार्दम्
न जातिस्तव दुर्मते ।
प्रमाणं न च सौदर्यम्
न धर्मो धर्मदूषण ॥
O you, who flout Dharma! You do not care about Dharma or the ties of kinship! You show no elderly affection or care for the kindred or for the brother, O evil-minded one!
6.87.13
शोच्यस्त्वमसि दुर्बुद्धे
निन्दनीयश्च साधुभिः ।
यस्त्वं स्वजनमुत्सृज्य
परभृत्यत्वमागतः ॥
You, O evil-minded one, are pathetic! All good men must condemn you for deserting your own folk and going into the service of the foe.
6.87.14
नैतच्छिथिलया बुद्ध्या
त्वं वेत्सि महदन्तरम् ।
क्वच स्वजनसंवासः
क्वच नीचपराश्रयः ॥
Your mind has degenerated. You are not able to see the huge difference between living with your own folk and taking shelter with a wretched foe.
6.87.15
गुणवान् वा परजनः
स्वजनो निर्गुणोऽपि वा ।
निर्गुणः स्वजनः श्रेयान्
यः परः पर एव सः ॥
Even if the foe is full of virtues and your own folk have none of them, your own folk who lack virtues are better, for the foe is a foe.
6.87.16
यः स्वपक्षं परित्यज्य
परपक्षं निषेवते ।
स स्वपक्षे क्षयं प्राप्ते
पश्चात्तैरेव हन्यते ॥
One who abjures one's own side and serves the other side will be eventually killed by them, once his own side is exterminated.
6.87.17
निरनुक्रोशता चेयम्
यादृशी ते निशाचर ।
स्वजनेन त्वया शक्यम्
पौरुषं रावणानुज ॥
This kind of manliness that is devoid of all empathy towards one's own folk, is only possible for you, O Night-rover, O brother of Rāvaṇa!
6.87.18
इत्युक्तो भ्रातृपुत्रेण
प्रत्युवाच विभीषणः ॥
Spoken to thus, by the son of his brother, Vibheeshaṇa responded saying:
6.87.18
अजानन्निव मच्छीलम्
किं राक्षस विकत्थसे ।
राक्षसेन्द्रसुतासाधो
पारुष्यं त्यज गौरवात् ॥
Why do you prate, O Rākshasa, as if you are not aware of my character! Stop being uncivil at least out of respect for my age and kinship, o evil son of the lord of Rākshasas!
6.87.19
कुले यद्यप्यहं जातो
रक्षसां क्रूरकर्मणाम् ।
गुणो यः प्रथमो नृणाम्
तन्मे शीलमराक्षसम् ॥
Even though I am born into the race of Rākshasas given to cruel deeds, my nature, unlike that of Rākshasas, is to follow the highest virtues of humans.
6.87.20
न रमे दारुणेनाहम्
न चाधर्मेण वै रमे ।
भ्रात्रा विषमशीलेन
कथं भ्राता निरस्यते ॥
I delight neither in cruel deeds, nor do I indulge in Adharma! How could a brother abjure his own brother just because his nature is different from his own?
6.87.21
धर्मात्प्रच्युतशीलं हि
पुरुषं पापनिश्चयम् ।
त्यक्त्वा सुखमवाप्नोति
हस्तादाशीविषं यथा ॥
One who abandons a man with a character that slipped from the path of Dharma and is bent on sinful deeds, will certainly be happy, like one who shakes a poisonous viper off his hand.
6.87.22
परस्वहरणे युक्तम्
परदाराभिमर्शकम् ।
त्याज्यमाहुर्दुराचारम्
वेश्म प्रज्वलितं यथा ॥
It has been said that one who indulges in robbing other's possessions, in violating other's women and in following evil courses of action must be abjured like a house on fire.
6.87.23
परस्वानां च हरणम्
परदाराभिमर्शनम् ।
सुहृदामतिशङ्का च
त्रयो दोषाः क्षयाबहाः ॥
Snatching other's money, violating other's women, and being suspicious of one's own well-wishers are the three vices that bring destruction upon anyone.
6.87.24
महर्षिणां वधो घोरः
सर्वदेवैश्च विग्रहः ।
अभिमानश्च कोपश्च
वैरित्वं प्रतिकूलता ।
एते दोषा मम भ्रातुः
जीवितैश्वर्यनाशनाः ।
गुणान् प्रच्छादयामासुः
पर्वतानिव तोयदाः ॥
The horrid murder of Maharshis, the perpetual feud with the Dēvas, the monumental pride, the anger and the inveterate enmity and antagonism towards others — these evil qualities of my brother which can destroy anybody's life and power, have overshadowed his virtues like clouds do mountains.
6.87.26
दोषैरेतैः परित्यक्तो
मया भ्राता पिता तव ।
नेयमस्ति पुरी लङ्का
न च त्वं न च ते पिता ।
अतिमानी च बालश्च
दुर्विनीतश्च राक्षस ।
बद्धस्त्वं कालपाशेन
ब्रूहि मां यद्यदिच्छसि ॥
It is because of these evils that I abjured your father who is my brother. Neither this city nor yourself nor your father is going to survive, O arrogant pup! The noose of death is tightening around your neck and that is why you, O Rākshasa, who lack even an iota of humility speak to me thus, as you like!
6.87.28
अद्य ते व्यसनं प्राप्तम्
यन्मां परुषमुक्तवान् ।
प्रवेष्टुं न त्वया शक्यम्
न्यग्रोधो राक्षसाधम ॥
Realize that You, who talk so harshly to me, are now in big trouble; you cannot reach this banyan tree, O base Rākshasa!
6.87.29
धर्षयित्वा च काकुत्सम्
न शक्यं जीवितुं त्वया ।
युद्ध्यस्व नरदेवेन
लक्ष्मणेन रणे सह ।
हतस्त्वं देवताकार्यम्
करिष्यसि यमक्षये ॥
You can in no way escape with your life having challenged Kākutstha! Fight with Lakshmaṇa, a lord of men, in this battle! Slain, you will do the bidding of Dēvas in the abode of Yama!
6.87.31
निदर्शयस्वात्मबलं समुद्यतं
कुरुष्व सर्वायुधसायकव्ययम् ।
न लक्ष्मणस्यैत्य हि बाणगोचरं
त्वमद्य जीवन् सबलो गमिष्यसि ॥
Display all the strength that you can summon and expend all the weapons you have. Having come within range of Lakshmaṇa’s arrows, you are not going to escape today with your life, neither will your army.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे सप्ताशतीतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighty seventh Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
