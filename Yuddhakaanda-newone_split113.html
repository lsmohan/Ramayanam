Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 113
In this Sarga, Hanumān goes to Laṅkā and conveys to Seetā the wellbeing of Rāma, Lakshmaṇa, Sugreeva and the Vānaras and the victory of Rāma, the death of Rāvaṇa, and the consecration of Vibheeshaṇa as the king of Laṅkā. Seetā, overwhelmed with joy, becomes speechless. Then she thanks him profusely for bringing the best news in the world that she can ever receive.
Hanumān then tells Seetā that he would like to kill all the Rākshasis who harassed and troubled her thus far, by punching and pummeling them with his fists, knees, felling them down to the ground with their jaws, necks, ribs broken. Seetā, reflecting carefully, attributes all the trials and tribunals she went through to her ill-fortune and the will of Providence, and tells him that the Rākshasis did so only because they were told to, and not on their own, and hence should not be harmed.
Fully appreciating her sense of Dharma and kindness, Hanumān then asks her to give a message that he can carry back to Rāma. Seetā gives her simple message, 'I want to see my husband!' and Hanumān leaves, taking that message with him.
6.113.1
इति प्रतिसमादिष्टो
हनुमान् मारुतात्मजः ।
प्रविवेश पुरीं लङ्काम्
पूज्यमानो निशाचरैः ॥
Thus instructed, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, entered the city of Laṅkā, receiving all honor from the Night-rovers.
6.113.2
प्रविश्य च पुरीं लङ्काम्
अनुज्ञाप्य विभीषणम् ।
ततस्तेनाभ्यनुज्ञातो
हनुमान् वृक्षवाटिकाम् ।
संप्रविश्य यथान्यायम्
सीताया विदितो हरिः ।
ददर्श मृजया हीनाम्
सातङ्काम् रोहिणीमिव ।
वृक्षमूले निरानन्दाम्
राक्षसीभिः परिवृताम् ।
निभृतः प्रणतः प्रह्वः
सोऽभिगम्याभिवाद्य च ॥
Requesting the permission of Vibheeshaṇa and duly permitted by him, Hanumān, the Vānara who was already familiar to Seetā, entered the city of Laṅkā and then the (Aṡōka) pleasance, and saw the unkempt and gloomy Seetā, who was like Rōhiṇee beset with evil planets, seated at the base of the tree and surrounded by the Rākshasis. He approached her, saluted her and stood bent in obeisance.
6.113.5
दृष्ट्वा तमागतं देवी
हनुमन्तं महाबलम् ।
तूष्णीमास्त तदा दृष्ट्वा
स्मृत्वा प्रमुदिताऽभवत् ॥
The lady noticed Hanumān of immense strength coming, but remained silent. Then she looked at him again, and was very happy, recognizing him.
6.113.6
सौम्यं दृष्ट्वा मुखं तस्या
हनुमान् प्लवगोत्तमः ।
रामस्य वचनं सर्वम्
आख्यातुमुपचक्रमे ॥
Seeing her calm face, Hanumān, the great Vānara, began to tell all that Rāma had said.
6.113.7
वैदेहि कुशली रामः
सहसुग्रीवलक्ष्मणः ।
विभीषणसहायश्च
हरीणां सहितो बलैः ॥
O Vaidēhee! Rāma, along with Sugreeva and Lakshmaṇa and his friend Vibheeshaṇa and the Vānara forces, is doing well.
6.113.8
कुशलं चाहु सिद्धार्थो
हतशत्रुररिन्दमः ।
विभीषणसहायेन
रामेण हरिभिः सह ।
निहतो रावणो देवि
लक्ष्मणेन च वीर्यवान् ॥
Having slain the enemy, he the subduer of enemies, conveys his wellbeing to you. With the help of Vibheeshaṇa, and along with Vānaras and the valiant Lakshmaṇa, Rāma slew Rāvaṇa, my lady!
6.113.9
पृष्ट्वा तु कुशलं रामो
वीरस्त्वां रघुनन्दनः ।
अब्रवीत् परमप्रीतः
कृतार्थेनान्तरात्मना ॥
The valiant Rāma, delight of the Raghus, enquires about your welfare, and says, extremely happy at heart, having accomplished his object:
6.113.10
प्रियमाख्यामि ते देवि
त्वां तु भूयः सभाजये ।
दिष्ट्या जीवसि धर्मज्ञे
जयेन मम संयुगे ।
लब्धो नो विजयः सीते
स्वस्था भव गतव्यथा ॥
My lady! My love to you! And I have extremely happy news for you! By the good grace of Providence, O Dharmajña, you are alive till I came out victorious in the battle. Victory is ours, O Seetā; you can give up all your worries and be at ease!
6.113.12
रावणश्च हतः शत्रुः
लङ्का चेयं वशीकृता ।
मया ह्यलब्धनिद्रेण
दृतेन तव निर्जये ।
प्रतिज्ञैषा विनिस्तीर्णा
बद्ध्वा सेतुं महोदधौ ॥
Rāvaṇa has been slain, and Laṅkā is in my control. Resolved upon winning you back, and spending sleepless nights, I laid a bridge across the mighty ocean and thus redeemed my pledge.
6.113.13
सम्भ्रमश्च न गन्तव्यो
वर्तन्त्या रावणालये ।
विभीषणविधेयं हि
लङ्कैश्वर्यमिदं कृतम् ॥
You must not worry that you are still in Rāvaṇa’s home; all that is in Laṅkā is now under the control of Vibheeshaṇa.
6.113.14
तदाश्वसिहि विश्वस्ता
स्वगृहे परिवर्तसे ।
अयं चाभ्येति संहृष्टः
त्वद्दर्शनसमुत्सुकः ॥
Set your mind at ease and trust that you are in your own house. He (Vibheeshaṇa) who is happy and eager to see you, will be there.
6.113.15
एवमुक्ता समुत्पत्य
सीता शशिनिभानना ।
प्रहर्षेणावरुद्धा सा
व्याहर्तुं न शशाक ह ॥
Seetā, whose face was bright like the moon, sprang up hearing those words, but was unable to say a word, overwhelmed with extreme joy.
6.113.16
अब्रवीच्च हरिश्रेष्ठः
सीतामप्रतिजल्पतीम् ।
किं नु चिन्तयसे देवि
किं नु मां नाभिभाषसे ॥
Seeing Seetā not respond, that best of Vānaras said, 'What are you thinking, my lady, why do you not speak?'
6.113.17
एवमुक्ता हनुमता
सीता धर्मे व्यवस्थिता ।
अब्रवीत् परमप्रीता
हर्षगद्गदया गिरा ॥
Thus asked by Hanumān, Seetā, who was firmly on the path of Dharma, said in extreme joy, her voice choked with happiness:
6.113.18
प्रियमेतदुपश्रुत्य
भर्तुर्विजयसंश्रितम् ।
प्रहर्षवशमापन्ना
निर्वाक्याऽस्मि क्षणान्तरम् ॥
I was so carried away by joy on hearing the happy news of my husband’s victory that I was left speechless for a moment.
6.113.19
न हि पश्यामि सदृशम्
चिन्तयन्ती प्लवङ्गम ।
मत्प्रियाख्यानाकस्येह
तव प्रत्यभिनन्दनम् ॥
And try as hard as I might, O Vānara, I cannot find words to thank you enough, for the happy news you have brought.
6.113.20
न हि पश्यामि तत् सौम्य
पृथिव्यामपि वानर ।
सदृशं मत्प्रियाख्याने
तव दातुं भवेत् समम् ॥
And I cannot think of anything on earth, my dear Vānara, that can requite you, for making me happy by this news.
6.113.21
हिरण्यं वा सुवर्णं वा
रत्नानि विविधानि च ।
राज्यं वा त्रिषु लोकेषु
नैतदर्हति भाषितुम् ॥
Neither gold nor silver nor any kind of gem, nor even the sovereignty of the three worlds, can match this great news.
6.113.22
एवमुक्तस्तु वैदेह्या
प्रत्युवाच प्लवङ्गमः ।
गृहीतप्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यम्
सीतायाः प्रमुखे स्थितः ॥
Thus told by Vaidēhee, the Vānara responded with these words, standing before Seetā with his palms joined in reverence:
6.113.23
भर्तुः प्रियहिते युक्ते
भर्तुर्विजयकाङ्क्षिणि ।
स्निग्धमेवंविधं वाक्यम्
त्वमेवार्हसि भाषितुम् ॥
Only you, who constantly think of the welfare of your husband, and wish for his victory, could say such nice words.
6.113.24
तवैतद्वचनं सौम्ये
सारवत् स्निग्धमेव च ।
रत्नौघाद्विविधाच्चापि
देवराज्याद्विशिष्यते ॥
These words of yours, my dear, that are so poignant and so pleasing are more precious to me than heaps of gems of every kind or even the sovereignty over Heaven.
6.113.25
अर्थतश्च मया प्राप्ता
देवराज्यादयो गुणाः ।
हतशत्रुं विजयिनम्
रामं पश्यामि सुस्थितम् ॥
Seeing Rāma well settled and victorious with all the enemies slain, is like gaining sovereignty over Heaven, and much more, for me.
6.113.26
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
मैथिली जनकात्मजा ।
ततः शुभतरं वाक्यम्
उवाच पवनात्मजम् ॥
Hearing those words, Maithili, the daughter of Janaka, said these words to the son of Vāyu, which were even more pleasant and nice:
6.113.27
अतिलक्षणसम्पन्नम्
माधुर्यगुणभूषितम् ।
बुद्ध्या ह्यष्टाङ्गया युक्तम्
त्वमेवार्हसि भाषितुम् ॥
You alone could say such extraordinarily well chosen words, ornate with endearing qualities, and with all the eight aspects evident.
The eight aspects of making a good conversation are:
ग्रहणं धारणं चैव
स्मरणं प्रतिपादनम्
ऊहापोहोऽर्थविज्ञानम्
तत्वज्ञानं च धीगुणाः
1) Absorbing what is said, 2) internalizing it, 3) keeping memory of it, 4) reading between the words, 5) purging out whatever is incidental and spurious, 6) understanding the literal meaning, 7) understating the spirit and intent and 8) making a coherent statement.
6.113.28
श्लाघनीयोऽनिलस्य त्वम्
पुत्रः परमधार्मिकः ।
बलं शौर्यं श्रुतं सत्त्वम्
विक्रमो दाक्ष्यमुत्तमम् ।
तेजः क्षमाधृतिर्धैर्यम्
विनीतत्वं न संशयः ।
एते चान्ये च बहवो
गुणास्त्वय्येव शोभनाः ॥
You are the praiseworthy son of Vāyu, and an ardent adherent of Dharma. You are endowed, without a doubt, with many excellent qualities like strength, heroism, scholarship, mettle, prowess, capability, power, forbearance, fortitude, courage, humility and many other such.
6.113.30
अथोवाच पुनः सीताम्
असम्भ्रान्तो विनीतवत् ।
प्रगृहीताञ्जलिर्हर्षात्
सीतायाः प्रमुखे स्थितः ॥
Then, in high spirits, he said to Seetā, albeit in a humble and composed manner, standing in front of her with his palms joined in reverence:
6.113.31
इमास्तु खलु राक्षस्यो
यदि त्वमनुमन्यसे ।
हन्तुमिच्छाम्यहं सर्वा
याभिस्त्वं तर्जिता पुरा ।
क्लिश्यन्तीं पतिदेवां त्वाम्
अशोकवनिकां गताम् ।
घोररूपसमाचाराः
क्रूराः क्रूरतरेक्षणाः ॥
If you permit me, I would like to kill all these cruel Rākshasis whose glances are even more cruel and who are of terrible form and behavior, who have thus far harassed you, the one devoted to her husband and crying, stuck in this Aṡōka Vana.
6.113.33
इह श्रुता मया देवि
राक्षस्यो विकृताननाः ।
असकृत्परुषैर्वाक्यैः
वदन्त्यो रावणाज्ञया ॥
I have, my lady, heard these Rākshasis of crooked faces repeatedly saying terrible words as ordered by Rāvaṇa.
6.113.34
विकृता विकृताकाराः
क्रूराः क्रूरकचेक्षणाः ।
इच्छामि विविधैर्घातैः
हन्तुमेताः सुदारुणाः ।
राक्षस्यो दारुणकथा
वरमेतत् प्रयच्छ मे ॥
I like to kill these extremely terrible Rākshasas, who are crooked in nature as well as in form, who are cruel, with cruel glances and scary hair, striking them in every possible manner; please grant me just this one wish.
6.113.36
मुष्टिभिः पाणिघातैश्च
विशालैश्चौव बाहुभिः ।
जङ्घाजानुप्रहारैश्च
दन्तानां चैव पीडनैः ।
भक्षणैः कर्णनासानाम्
केशानां लुञ्चनैस्तथा ।
भृशं शुष्कमुखैश्चैव
दारणैर्लङ्घनैर्हतैः ।
विभिन्नशझ्कुग्रीवांस
पार्श्वकैश्च कलेवरैः ।
निपात्य हन्तुमिच्छामि
तव विप्रियकारिणीः ॥
I would like to kill these women who hurt you, with the blows from my fists, hands, huge arms, and blows from my calves and knees, knocking down their teeth chewing up their ears and nose, plucking out their hair, flattening their faces, tearing them apart, stamping them, and felling them down to the ground, their jaws, necks, shoulders, sides, and the skeletons broken.
6.113.39
एवंप्रहारैर्बहुभिः
संप्रहार्य यशस्विनि ।
घातये तीव्ररूपाभिः
याभिस्त्वं तर्जिता पुरा ॥
Striking with every kind of blow like these, I will kill these violent women, who had harassed you till now, O renowned one!
6.113.40
इत्युक्ता सा हनुमता
कृपणा दीनवत्सला ।
हनुमन्तमुवाचेदम्
चिन्तयित्वा विमृश्य च ॥
Thus told by Hanumān, reflecting carefully, that kind woman whose heart empathized with the distressed, said these words to him:
6.113.41
राजसंश्रयवश्यानाम्
कुर्वन्तीनां पराज्ञया ।
विधेयानां च दासीनाम्
कः कुप्येद्वानरोत्तम ॥
How could one be angry, O best of Vānaras, with these women who are under the protection and control of the king, and are simply carrying out the orders of others, being the obedient servants they are?
6.113.42
भाग्यवैषम्यदोषेण
पुरस्ताद्दुष्कृतेन च ।
मयैतत्प्राप्यते सर्वम्
स्वकृतं ह्युपभुज्यते ॥
I have had to undergo all this trial because of fortune turning against me and as a retribution to the sins I committed. After all, one has to pay for what one has done.
6.113.43
प्राप्तव्यं तु दशायोगात्
मयैतदिति निश्चितम् ।
दासीनां रावणस्याहम्
मर्षयामीह दुर्बला ॥
The planetary conjunctions have dictated that I must go through this; I had to powerlessly put up with these servants of Rāvaṇa.
6.113.44
आज्ञप्ता रावणेनेह
राक्षस्यस्तर्जयन्ति माम् ।
हते तस्मिन्न कुर्वन्ति
तर्जनं मारुतात्मज ॥
The Rākshasis that harassed me here did so under the orders of Rāvaṇa. Now that he is dead, they will not harass me any more, O son of Vāyu!
6.113.45
अयं व्याघ्रसमीपे तु
पुराणो धर्मसंहितः ।
ऋक्षेण गीतः श्लोकोऽस्ति
तं निबोध प्लवङ्गम ॥
A bear, so runs an old tale, uttered the following verse that reflects Dharma, responding to a tiger, O Vānara:
The old tale goes like this: Once upon a time, a man, being chased by a tiger, climbs on a tree, only to find a bear there. But the bear, treating him as a guest seeking protection, grants it. The tiger says to the bear, 'He is a human, a menace to both of us, throw him down!'. But the bear would not agree and goes to sleep. Then the tiger says to the man, 'Throw the bear down, I will eat it and go!' The man, seeing an opportunity to get rid of both the beasts, throws down the bear. But the bear, waking up in time and hanging on to a branch, recites this Ṡlōka.
6.113.46
न परः पापमादत्ते
परेषां पापकर्मणाम् ।
समयो रक्षितव्यस्तु
सन्तश्चारित्रभूषणाः ॥
An evolved soul would not focus on the evil in others or in their evil deeds, but on the maxims one should observe. All good men find their glory in their illustrious conduct.
6.113.47
पापानां वा शुभानां वा
वधार्हाणामथापि वा ।
कार्यं करुणमार्येण
न कश्चिन्नापराध्यति ॥
A noble man should act with compassion towards everyone, no matter whether they are wicked or good, or even deserving death, for there is no one who has never done something wrong.
6.113.48
लोकहिंसाविहाराणाम्
रक्षसां कामरूपिणाम् ।
कुर्वतामपि पापानि
नैव कार्यमशोभनम् ॥
True, the Rākshasas, who can take any form at will, go around inflicting pain on everyone and committing sinful deeds; even then, what is not appropriate should not be done to them.
6.113.49
एवमुक्तस्तु हनुमान्
सीतया वाक्यकोविदः ।
प्रत्युवाच ततः सीताम्
रामपत्नीमनिन्दिताम् ॥
Thus exhorted, Hanumān, a master of words, replied to Seetā, the blameless consort of Rāma:
6.113.50
युक्ता रामस्य भवती
धर्मपत्नी गुणान्विता ।
प्रतिसन्दिश मां देवि
गमिष्ये यत्र राघवः ॥
You, endowed with such noble qualities, are worthy of being the consort of Rāma, a helpmate in his adherence to Dharma! Please give me your message, I shall go where Rāghava is (and pass it on to him)!
6.113.51
एवमुक्ता हनुमता
वैदेही जनकात्मजा ।
अब्रवीद्द्रष्टुमिच्छामि
भर्तारं वानरोत्तम ॥
Thus asked by Hanumān, Vaidēhee, the daughter of Janaka, said: 'I wish to see my husband! O eminent Vānara!'
6.113.52
तस्यास्तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
हनुमान् मारुतात्मजः ।
हर्षयन् मैथिलीं वाक्यम्
उवाचेदं महामतिः ॥
Hearing those words of hers, the extraordinarily sagacious Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, said these words to Maithili, making her happy:
6.113.53
पूर्णचन्द्राननं रामम्
द्रक्ष्यस्यार्ये सलक्ष्मणम् ।
स्थिरमित्रं हतामित्रम्
शचीव त्रिदशेश्वरम् ॥
You shall, O noble lady, see this very day Rāma along with Lakshmaṇa, whose face glows like the full moon and who had solidified his allies and destroyed his enemies, as Ṡaci might the King of the Dēvas.
6.113.54
तामेवमुक्त्वा राजन्तीम्
सीतां साक्षादिव श्रियम् ।
आजगाम महावेगो
हनुमान् यत्र राघवः ॥
Saying this to Seetā, who shone as if she were Lakshmee herself, Hanumān of immense speed went to the place where Rāghava was.
6.113.55
सपदि हरिवरस्ततो हनुमान्
प्रतिवचनं जनके्श्वरात्मजायाः ।
कथितमकथयद्यथाक्रमेण
त्रिदशवरप्रतिमाय राघवाय ॥
Hanumān, the prominent Vānara, conveyed the message that the daughter of Lord Janaka gave him to Rāghava who was comparable to best of the Dēvas, exactly as it was told to him.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे त्रयोदशोत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and thirteenth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
