Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 13
In this Sarga, Hanumān contemplates on what could have happened to Seetā. He wonders whether she could have fallen into the ocean while being carried off by Rāvaṇa. He wonders whether she could have been killed when she resisted Rāvaṇa or from the sheer freight from seeing the terrifying wives of Rāvaṇa.
He then imagines the consequences of his returning and telling Aṅgada on the other side of the ocean and then telling Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva in Kishkindhā about not finding Seetā. He imagines that Rāma, upon hearing that there is no trace of Seetā, would give up his life, and then Lakshmaṇa, Bharata and Ṡatrughna would follow suit. Consequently, he imagines, Sugreeva and all the other Vānaras would do the same.
Then he concludes it is better for him to stay right there in Laṅkā and keep searching till he finds Seetā; and if she was not to be found, to spend the rest of his life under a tree, subsisting on whatever falls into his hands by providence.
He then sees a Vana with Aṡōka trees. He thinks he would go there, as he had not searched it so far. He prays for the blessings of all the Dēvas and other forces, and hopes to find Seetā.
5.13.1
विमानात्तु सुसम्क्रम्य
प्राकारं हरियूथपः ।
हनुमान्वेगवानासीत्
यथा विद्युद्घनान्तरे ॥
Hanumān, chief of Vānara warriors, jumped fast from the Vimāna to the ramparts like a flash of lightning that shoots from one cloud to another.
5.13.2
सम्परिक्रम्य हनुमान्
रावणस्य निवेशनात् ।
अदृष्ट्वा जानकीं सीताम्
अब्रवीद्वचनं कपिः ॥
Not finding Seetā, the daughter of Janaka, the Vānara thought, after he was completely out of Rāvaṇa’s residence:
5.13.3
भूयिष्ठं लोलिता लङ्का
रामस्य चरता प्रियम् ।
न हि पश्यामि वैदेहीम्
सीतां सर्वाङ्गशोभनाम् ॥
I have combed Laṅkā pretty well, earnestly interested in doing the best for Rāma. But I could not find Seetā, the princess of Vidēha, who is lovely from head to toe.
5.13.4
पल्वलानि तटाकानि
सरांसि सरितस्तथा ।
नद्योऽनूपवनान्ताश्च
दुर्गाश्च धरणीधराः ।
लोलिता वसुधा सर्वा
न तु पश्यामि जानकीम् ॥
Even after combing the entire earth, with its pools, lakes, streams, rivers, woods, water-meadows, thickets and mountains, I could not find Jānaki.
5.13.5
इह सम्पातिना सीता
रावणस्य निवेशने ।
आख्याता गृध्रराजेन
न च पश्यामि तामहम् ॥
Saṃpāti, the king of eagles, has asserted that Seetā is in the palace of Rāvaṇa; but I am unable to find her.
5.13.6
किं नु सीताऽथ वैदेही
मैथिली जनकात्मजा ।
उपतिष्ठेत विवशा
रावणं दुष्टचारिणम् ॥
Is it possible that Seetā, the daughter of Janaka, the princess of Vidēha and the cynosure of Mithilā has lost her will and yielded herself to the wicked Rāvaṇa!
5.13.7
क्षिप्रमुत्पततो मन्ये
सीतामादाय रक्षसः ।
बिभ्यतो रामबाणानाम्
अन्तरा पतिता भवेत् ॥
Or, is it possible that she fell down midway as the Rākshasa carried her off flying in a hurry, scared of the arrows of Rāma?
5.13.8
अथवा ह्रियमाणायाः
पथि सिद्धनिषेविते ।
मन्ये पतितमार्याया
हृदयं प्रेक्ष्य सागरम् ॥
Or, is it possible that the noble one lost her heart at the sight of the (vast) ocean (below), while she was being carried off along the path that the Siddhas frequent?
5.13.9
रावणस्योरुवेगेन
भुजाभ्यां पीडितेन च ।
तया मन्ये विशालाक्ष्या
त्यक्तं जीवितमार्यया ॥
Or, is it possible that the noble one of wide eyes gave up her life, under the pressure of Rāvaṇa’s arms and the speed of his thighs?
5.13.10
उपर्युपरि वा नूनम्
सागरं क्रमतस्तदा ।
विवेष्टमाना पतिता
सागरे जनकात्मजा ॥
The daughter of Janaka must have wriggled free and fallen into the ocean during the long flight across and high above the ocean.
5.13.11
अहो क्षुद्रेण वाऽनेन
रक्षन्ती शीलमात्मनः ।
अबन्धुर्भक्षिता सीता
रावणेन तपस्विनी ॥
Or, alas, could she, the poor suffering woman, while seeking to defend her honor, with no one by her side for support, have been devoured by the despicable wretch Rāvaṇa?
5.13.12
अथवा राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
पत्नीभिरसितेक्षणा ।
अदुष्टा दुष्टभावाभिः
भक्षिता सा भविष्यति ॥
Or, was she, of beautiful black eyes, eaten up by the wicked wives of the lord of Rākshasas, who entertained evil designs against her?
5.13.13
सम्पूर्णचन्द्रप्रतिमम्
पद्मपत्रनिभेक्षणम् ।
रामस्य ध्यायती वक्त्रम्
पञ्चत्वं कृपणा गता ॥
She may have died, full of misery, as her mind dwelt with yearning on Rāma’s face that resembles the full moon, endowed with eyes like lotus petals.
5.13.14
हा राम लक्ष्मणेत्येवम्
हाऽयोध्ये चेति मैथिली ।
विलप्य बहु वैदेही
न्यस्तदेहा भविष्यति ॥
She, from Mithilā of Vidēha kingdom, must have cried a lot, saying, ‘Ha Rāma! Ha Lakshmaṇa! Ha Ayōdhyā’, before she gave up her life.
5.13.15
अथवा निहिता मन्ये
रावणस्य निवेशने ।
नूनं लालप्यते सीता
पञ्जरस्थेव शारिका ॥
Or else, I shall consider that Seetā must be wailing, confined in the house of Rāvaṇa, like a parrot in a cage.
5.13.16
जनकस्य सुता सीता
रामपत्नी सुमध्यमा ।
कथमुत्पलपत्राक्षी
रावणस्य वशं व्रजेत् ॥
How could Seetā, the daughter of Janaka and wife of Rāma, of slender waist with eyes like black lilies, yield herself to Rāvaṇa?
5.13.17
विनष्टा वा प्रणष्टा वा
मृता वा जनकात्मजा ।
रामस्य प्रियभार्यस्य
न निवेदयितुं क्षमम् ॥
No matter whether she is lost or undiscovered or dead, it is not proper to report it to Rāma, who loves his wife dearly.
5.13.18
निवेद्यमाने दोषस्स्यात्
दोषस्स्यादनिवेदने ।
कथं नु खलु कर्तव्यम्
विषमं प्रतिभाति मे ॥
It would be wrong to report and it would also be wrong not to report. What is to be done? What a quandary!
5.13.19
अस्मिन्नेवंगते कार्ये
प्राप्तकालं क्षमं च किम् ।
भवेदिति मतं भूयो
हनुमान्प्रविचारयत् ॥
Hanumān agonized again over what was the right thing to do under the given circumstances where whatever had thus far happened, had already happened.
5.13.20
यदि सीतामदृष्ट्वाऽहम्
वानरेन्द्रपुरीमितः ।
गमिष्यामि ततः को मे
पुरुषार्थो भविष्यति ॥
What object would I have achieved if I go back to the city of the lord of Vānaras from here, without seeing Seetā?
5.13.21
ममेदं लङ्घनं व्यर्थम्
सागरस्य भविष्यति ।
प्रवेशश्चैव लङ्कायाः
राक्षसानां च दर्शनम् ॥
My crossing the ocean would come to naught; and so would my penetration into Laṅkā and seeing the Rākshasas!
5.13.22
किं मां वक्ष्यति सुग्रीवो
हरयो वा समागताः ।
किष्किन्धां समनुप्राप्तम्
तौ वा दशरथात्मजौ ॥
What will I say to Sugreeva and the assembled Vānaras, and to the two sons of Daṡaratha after returning to Kishkindhā?
5.13.23
गत्वा तु यदि काकुत्स्थम्
वक्ष्यामि परमप्रियम् ।
न दृष्टेति मया सीता
ततस्त्यक्ष्यति जीवितम् ॥
If I return and tell Kākutstha the extremely bad news that I could not find Seetā, he would give up his life right away.
5.13.24
परुषं दारुणं क्रूरम्
तीक्ष्णमिन्द्रियतापनम् ।
सीतानिमित्तं दुर्वाक्यम्
श्रुत्वा स न भविष्यति ॥
He would not survive hearing the bad, harsh, terrible, cruel and stinging words regarding Seetā that would burn all the senses.
5.13.25
तं तु कृच्छ्रगतं दृष्ट्वा
पञ्चत्वगतमानसम् ।
भृशानुरक्तो मेधावी
न भविष्यति लक्ष्मणः ॥
Seeing him thrown into such hardship and his mind all but dead, the sagacious Lakshmaṇa, who is extremely loving of him, would not survive either.
5.13.26
विनष्टौ भ्रातरौ श्रुत्वा
भरतोऽपि मरिष्यति ।
भरतं च मृतं दृष्ट्वा
शत्रुघ्नो न भविष्यति ॥
Hearing that the two brothers are dead, Bharata will also die. Seeing Bharata die, Ṡatrughna will not survive.
5.13.27
पुत्रान्मृतान्समीक्ष्याथ
न भविष्यन्ति मातरः ।
कौसल्या च सुमित्रा च
कैकेयी च न संशयः ॥
Seeing the sons dead, the mothers, Kousalyā, Sumitrā and Kaikēyee, for sure, will not survive.
5.13.28
कृतज्ञस्सत्यसन्धश्च
सुग्रीवः प्लवगाधिपः ।
रामं तथा गतं दृष्ट्वा
ततस्त्यक्ष्यति जीवितम् ॥
On seeing Rāma gone thus, Sugreeva, the lord of Vānaras, who is true to his word and who remembers the favors he received, would also give up his life.
5.13.29
दुर्मना व्यथिता दीना
निरानन्दा तपस्विनी ।
पीडिता भर्तृशोकेन
रुमा त्यक्ष्यति जीवितम् ॥
The tormented Ruma, losing every sense of happiness, will give up her life, extremely stressed and suffering from the grief for her husband, with a troubled mind and in a pitiable condition.
5.13.30
वालिजेन तु दुःखेन
पीडिता शोककर्शिता ।
पञ्चत्वं च गते राज्ञि
ताराऽपि न भविष्यति ॥
Tārā who is already tormented by sadness and battered by the grief for Vāli, will not survive when the king dies.
5.13.31
मातापित्रोर्विनाशेन
सुग्रीवव्यसनेन च ।
कुमारोऽप्यङ्गदः कस्मात्
धारयिष्यति जीवितम् ॥
How will the young Aṅgada be able to hang on to life, with both the mother and father lost and the (death of) Sugreeva adding to his grief?
5.13.32
भर्तृजेन तु दुःखेन
ह्यभिभूता वनौकसः ।
शिरांस्यभिहनिष्यन्ति
तलैर्मुष्टिभिरेव च ॥
Stricken by the grief over the loss of their lord, the Vana dwellers will batter their heads with their palms and fists.
5.13.33
सान्त्वेनानुप्रदानेन
मानेन च यशस्विना ।
लालिताः कपिराजेन
प्राणांस्त्यक्ष्यन्ति वानराः ॥
The Vānaras who are used to words of appreciation and showering of gifts and honors by the renowned king of Vānaras will (certainly) give up their lives.
5.13.34
न वनेषु न शैलेषु
न निरोधेषु वा पुनः ।
क्रीडामनुभविष्यन्ति
समेत्य कपिकुञ्जराः ॥
Neither in the Vanas, nor on the mountains, nor in the houses anymore, would the elephants among Vānaras be able to assemble for play or for fun.
5.13.35
सपुत्रदारास्सामात्या
भर्तृव्यसनपीडिताः ।
शैलाग्रेभ्यः पतिष्यन्ति
समेषु विषमेषु च ॥
Tormented by the grief (over the loss) of their lord, they will hurl themselves off the mountains on to the grounds, plain and uneven, along with their children, wives and friends.
5.13.36
विषमुद्बन्धनं वापि
प्रवेशं ज्वलनस्य वा ।
उपवासमथो शस्त्रम्
प्रचरिष्यन्ति वानराः ॥
The Vānaras will resort to poison or noose or to throwing themselves into fire or to fasting till death or to weapons.
5.13.37
घोरमारोदनं मन्ये
गते मयि भविष्यति ।
इक्ष्वाकुकुलनाशश्च
नाशश्चैव वनौकसाम् ॥
My return would only lead to terrible wailing, due to the destruction of the race of Ikshwākus and that of the dwellers of Vana.
5.13.38
सोऽहं नैव गमिष्यामि
किष्किन्धां नगरीमितः ।
न च शक्ष्याम्यहं द्रष्टुम्
सुग्रीवं मैथिलीं विना ॥
Hence I will not go to the city of Kishkindhā from here. Nor am I capable of seeing Sugreeva, without Maithili.
5.13.39
मय्यगच्छति चेहस्थे
धर्मात्मानौ महारथौ ।
आशया तौ धरिष्येते
वानराश्च मनस्विनः ॥
If I do not go but stay right here instead, the two Dharmātmas and the super-charioters will be able to hold on (to life) with a glimmer of hope, and so will be the Vānaras.
5.13.40
हस्तादानो मुखादानो
नियतो वृक्षमूलिकः ।
वानप्रस्थो भविष्यामि
ह्यदृष्ट्वा जनकात्मजाम् ॥
If I do not see the daughter of Janaka, I shall live an austere life at the base of trees in the woods, eating whatever falls into my hands and mouth providentially.
5.13.41
सागरानूपजे देशे
बहुमूलफलोदके ।
चितां कृत्वा प्रवेक्ष्यामि
समिद्धमरणीसुतम् ॥
Or, I shall make a fire on the shores of the ocean, an area plentiful in roots, fruits and water, and throw myself into the raging flames.
5.13.42
उपविष्टस्य वा सम्यक्
लिङ्गिनीं साधयिष्यतः ।
शरीरं भक्षयिष्यन्ति
वायसाः श्वापदानि च ॥
Or else, I shall sit steady resolved on fasting unto death letting the crows and beasts eat my body away.
5.13.43
इदं महर्षिभिर्दृष्टम्
निर्याणमिति मे मतिः ।
सम्यगापः प्रवेक्ष्यामि
न चेत्पश्यामि जानकीम् ॥
If I do not find Jānaki, I shall drown in the water. I think this is how even the Maharshis end their life.
5.13.44
सुजातमूला सुभगा
कीर्तिमाला यशस्विनी ।
प्रभग्ना चिररात्रीयम्
मम सीतामपश्यतः ॥
With my not finding Seetā, the beautiful creeper of my renown and fame that has genuine roots will find its end in this long night.
5.13.45
तापसो वा भविष्यामि
नियतो वृक्षमूलिकः ।
नेतः प्रतिगमिष्यामि
तामदृष्ट्वाऽस्तेक्षणाम् ॥
Or else, I will become a Tapasvi observing austerities at the base of a tree. I will not return from here without seeing her, of beautiful black eyes.
5.13.46
यदीतः प्रतिगच्छामि
सीतामनधिगम्य ताम् ।
अङ्गदस्सह तैस्सर्वैः
वानरैर्न भविष्यति ॥
If I return from here without knowing about her, Seetā, the Vānaras, including Aṅgada, will survive no more.
5.13.47
विनाशे बहवो दोषा
जीवन् भद्राणि पश्यति ।
तस्मात्प्राणान् धरिष्यामि
ध्रुवो जीवितसङ्गमः ॥
(Letting things) fall apart is wrong in many ways. There is a chance of seeing good times, by living. Hence I will hold on to my life. Those who live on, will certainly meet each other (some day).
5.13.48
एवं बहुविधं दुःखम्
मनसा  धारयन्मुहु: ।
नाध्यगच्छत्तदा पारम्
शोकस्य कपिकुञ्चरः ॥
With his mind harboring many gloomy thoughts like these repeatedly, the elephant among Vānaras could not reach the other shore of the (ocean of) sadness.
5.13.49
रावणं वा वधिष्यामि
दशग्रीवं महाबलम् ।
काममस्तु हृता सीता
प्रत्याचीर्णं भविष्यति ॥
Or, shall I kill Rāvaṇa, the mighty strong one with ten necks? At least revenge would thus be taken; never mind Seetā who had been carried off.
5.13.50
अथवैनं समुत्क्षिप्य
उपर्युपरि सागरम् ।
रामायोपहरिष्यामि
पशुं पशुपतेरिव ॥
Or, shall I lift and carry him off over and across the ocean and offer him to Rāma, like a sacrificial animal to the fire?
5.13.51
इति चिन्तां समापन्नः
सीतामनधिगम्य ताम् ।
ध्यानशोकपरीतात्मा
चिन्तयामास वानरः ॥
Not knowing anything about Seetā, the Vānara was overwhelmed with worry. Drowned in brooding and grief, he continued to ponder.
5.13.52
यावत्सीतां हि पश्यामि
रामपत्नीं यशस्विनीम् ।
तावदेतां पुरीं लङ्काम्
विचिनोमि पुनः पुनः ॥
I will search this city of Laṅkā again and again till I see the wife of Rāma, of great repute.
5.13.53
सम्पातिवचनाच्चापि
रामं यद्यानयाम्यहम् ।
अपश्यन् राघवो भार्याम्
निर्देहेत्सर्ववानरान् ॥
Even if I bring Rāma here, trusting the words of Saṃpāti, Rāghava, not seeing his wife, would burn up all the Vānaras.
5.13.54
इहैव नियताहारो
वत्स्यामि नियतेन्द्रियः ।
न मत्कृते विनश्येयुः
सर्वे ते नरवानराः ॥
Hence, I shall stay put right here, with limited food and refrained senses. All those humans and Vānaras should not have to perish because of me.
5.13.55
अशोकवनिका चेयम्
दृश्यते या महाद्रुमा ।
इमामधिगमिष्यामि
न हीयं विचिता मया ॥
I see a Vana of large Aṡōka trees. I shall go there. It has not been searched by me.
There seems to be a slight mix up in the order of Ṡlōkas here, perhaps due to the misplacement of palm leaves at some point.

5.13.56
वसून्रुद्रांस्तथाऽदित्यान्
अश्विनौ मरुतोऽपि च ।
नमस्कृत्वा गमिष्यामि
रक्षसां शोकवर्धनः ॥
I shall go making my obeisance to the Vasus, the Rudras, the Ādityas, the Aṡwins and the Maruts, to add to the sorrow of the Rākshasas.
5.13.57
जित्वा तु राक्षसान् सर्वान्
इक्ष्वाकुकुलनन्दिनीम् ।
सम्प्रदास्यामि रामाय
यथा सिद्धिं तपस्विने ॥
I shall defeat all the Rākshasas and present (Seetā), the delight of Ikshwāku Race, to Rāma, like Siddhi to Tapasvis.
5.13.58
सः मुहूर्तमिव ध्यात्वा
चिन्तावग्रथितेन्द्रियः ।
उदतिष्ठन्महातेजा
हनुमान् मारुतात्मजः ॥
Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, of immense power, whose senses were battered by grief, gathered himself to focus for a moment, and rose.
5.13.59
नमोऽस्तु रामाय सलक्ष्मणाय
देव्यै च तस्यै जनकात्मजायै ।
नमोऽस्तु रुद्रेंद्रयमानिलेभ्यो
नमोऽस्तु चन्द्रार्कमरुद्गणेभ्यः ॥
My salutations to Rāma and Lakshmaṇa and to the queen, the daughter of Janaka! My salutations to Rudra, Indra, Yama and Vāyu, and to the moon, the sun and the hordes of Maruts!
5.13.60
स तेभ्यस्तु नमस्कृत्य
सुग्रीवाय च मारुतिः ।
दिशस्सर्वास्समालोक्य
ह्यशोकवनिकां प्रति ॥
Paying obeisance to all of them and to Sugreeva, the son of Vāyu scanned all the directions and went toward the Aṡōka Vana.
5.13.61
स गत्वा मनसा पूर्वम्
अशोकवनिकां शुभाम् ।
उत्तरं चिन्तयामास
वानरो मारुतात्मजः ॥
The Vānara, son of Vāyu, contemplated in his mind about the beautiful Aṡōka Vana even before he went there and thought about (what to do).
5.13.62
ध्रुवं तु रक्षोबहुला
भविष्यति वनाकुला ।
अशोकवनिका पुण्या
सर्वसंस्कारसंस्कृता ॥
The auspicious Aṡōka Vana with many grooves, maintained carefully in every manner, for sure, must be crowded with many Rākshasas.
5.13.63
रक्षिणश्चात्र विहिता
नूनं रक्षन्ति पादपान् ।
भगवानपि सर्वात्मा
नातिक्षोभं प्रवाति वै ॥
The trees here, for sure, must be protected by guards. The venerable and omnipresent lord is also blowing soft, without shaking them violently.
5.13.64
संक्षिप्तोऽयं मयाऽत्मा च
रामार्थे रावणस्य च ।
सिद्धिं मे संविधास्यन्ति
देवाः सर्षिगणास्त्विह ॥
I contract my body to confound Rāvaṇa and to serve the cause of Rāma! May the Dēvas along with the companies of Ṛshis bless me with success!
5.13.65
ब्रह्मा स्वयंभूर्भगवान्
देवाश्चैव दिशन्तु मे ।
सिद्धिमग्निश्च वायुश्च
पुरुहूतश्च वज्रभृत् ।
वरुणः पाशहस्तश्च
सोमादित्यौ तथैव च ।
अश्विनौ च महात्मानौ
मरुतः शर्व एव च ॥
May the Self-born Lord, Brahma, the Dēvas, Agni, Vāyu, Indra, the Wielder of the Vajra, Varuṇa armed with the noose, and likewise the sun and the moon, the mighty Aṡwins and the Maruts, and Ṡarva too, grant me success!
5.13.67
सिद्धिं सर्वाणि भूतानि
भूतानां चैव यः प्रभुः ।
दास्यन्ति मम ये चान्ये
ह्यदृष्टाः पथि गोचराः ॥
All creatures, and the one who lords them all, as well as all the unseen powers that I may encounter on my way, will assuredly vouchsafe me success!
5.13.68
तदुन्नसं पाण्डुरदन्तमव्रणं
शुचिस्मितं पद्मपलाशलोचनम् ।
द्रक्ष्ये तदार्यावदनं कदान्वहं
प्रसन्नताराधिपतुल्यदर्शनम् ॥
Ah, when shall I see the spotless face of the noble one and its lotus-petal-like eyes, sporting a lofty nose and white teeth and bearing a lovely smile, that recalls the pleasant sight of the lord of the stars?
5.13.69
क्षुद्रेण पापेन नृशंसकर्मणा
सुदारुणालङ्कृतवेषधारिणा ।
बलाभिभूता ह्यबला तपस्विनी
कथं नु मे दृष्टिपथेऽद्य सा भवेत् ॥
How shall the frail and suffering one, who was carried off by force by him of mean, evil and cruel deeds whose costumes and decorations themselves are no less terrifying, fall into the range of my scanning eye today?
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे त्रयोदशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirteenth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
