Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 39
By now everyone is resigned to the fact that neither would Kaikēyee yield from her misplaced demands, nor would Rāma take any other course other than making his father's promise to Kaikēyee come true.
In this Sarga, Daṡaratha orders a fine chariot to be made ready for the departure of Rāma to Vana, and for the best clothes and jewelry for Seetā, that would last through her stay in the Vana.
Kousalyā tells Seetā to regard her husband just as she did before, even though he is penniless now. Seetā assures her mother-in-law that there would be no difference whatsoever in her regard for her husband.
Rāma takes leave of his mother Kousalyā and all the other three hundred and fifty mothers. The entire palace is filled with the din of cries of the women.
2.39.1
रामस्य तु वचः शृत्वा
मुनिवेषधरं च तम् ।
समीक्ष्य सह भार्याभी
राजा विगतचेतनः ।
नैनं दुःखेन सन्तप्तः
प्रत्यवैक्षत राघवम् ।
न चैनमभिसम्प्रेक्ष्य
प्रत्यभाषत दुर्मनाः ॥
The king, in the company of his wives, heard the words of Rāma. The sight of Rāma in the garbs of a Muni completely disoriented him. Fried by grief, he could not even manage to behold Rāghava. With his mind completely dysfunctional, he could neither see him nor respond to him.
2.39.3
स मुहूर्तमिवासंज्ञो
दुःखितश्च महीपतिः ।
विललाप महाबाहू
राममेवानुचिन्तयन् ॥
That lord of the lands cried as if he had lost his senses completely for a moment. He continued to lament, thinking about Rāma.
2.39.4
मन्ये खलु मया पूर्वम्
विवत्सा बहवःकृताः ।
प्राणिनो हिंसिता वापि
तस्मादिदमुपस्थितम् ॥
I must have separated many people in the past from their children and must have tormented many a being. I think that this calamity has befallen because of that.
2.39.5
न त्वेवानागते काले
देहाच्च्यवति जीवितम् ।
कैकेय्या क्लिश्यमानस्य
मृत्युर्मम न विद्यते ।
योऽहं पावकसङ्काशम्
पश्यामि पुरतः स्थितम् ।
विहाय वसने सूक्ष्मे
तापसाच्छादमात्मजम् ॥
I guess life won't leave the body until it is time. I don't see death coming to me even after being tormented thus by Kaikēyee and even after seeing my fiery son, my own blood, standing in front of me, covered in the robes of a Muni, leaving his fine garments behind!
2.39.7
एकस्याः खलु कैकेय्याः
कृतेऽयं क्लिश्यते जनः ।
स्वार्थे प्रयतमानायाः
संश्रित्य निकृतिं त्विमाम् ॥
Everyone is suffering because of one Kaikēyee, who is working towards her own ends by harboring such devious means.
2.39.8
एवमुक्त्वा तु वचनम्
बाष्पेण पिहितेन्द्रियः ।
रामेति सकृदेवोक्त्वा
व्याहर्तुं न शशाक ह ॥
After saying those words with tears in his eyes, with his senses completely burdened, he could speak no more than utter just one word: 'Rāma!'
2.39.9
संज्ञां तु प्रतिलभ्यैव
मुहूर्तात्स महीपतिः ।
नेत्राभ्यामश्रुपूर्णाभ्याम्
सुमन्त्रमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Regaining his senses after a few moments, the lord of the lands, his eyes suffused with tears, said to Sumantra:
2.39.10
औपवाह्यं रथं युक्त्वा
त्वमायाहि हयोत्तमैः ।
प्रापयैनं महाभागम्
इतो जनपदात्परम् ॥
Please bring a chariot appropriate for a long ride, with the finest of the horses yoked to it. And take this blessed soul from here, to the areas beyond the boundaries of human dwellings.
2.39.11
एवं मन्ये गुणवताम्
गुणानां फलमुच्यते ।
पित्रा मात्रा च यत्साधुः
वीरो निर्वास्यते वनम् ॥
I guess I must surmise that the best virtues of the best people would only yield results like this. How else is it possible that such a noble Veera is being sent away in exile, by his own father and mother?
2.39.12
राज्ञो वचनमाज्ञाय
सुमन्त्रः शीघ्रविक्रमः ।
योजयित्वाऽययौ तत्र
रथमश्वैरलङ्कृतम् ॥
Sumantra, in accordance with those words of the king, went swiftly and returned with a well decorated chariot, with splendid horses yoked to it.
2.39.13
तं रथं राजपुत्राय
सूतः कनकभूषितम् ।
आचचक्षेऽञ्जलिं कृत्वा
युक्तं परमवाजिभिः ॥
The charioteer then, with palms joined in reverence, informed the prince that the chariot, embellished with gold trappings and drawn by superb horses, was ready.
2.39.14
राजा सत्वरमाहूय
व्यापृतं वित्तसंञ्चये ।
उवाच देशकालज्ञम्
निश्चितं सर्वतश्शुचिम् ॥
The king then summoned an honest and strict officer from the treasury, who had a great sense of time and place, and said:
2.39.15
वासांसि च महार्हाणि
भूषणानि वराणि च ।
वर्षाण्येतानि सङ्ख्याय
वैदेह्याः क्षिप्रमानय ॥
Please bring immediately the most expensive garments and the finest jewelry, that would be enough for Seetā, for the count of years (that she will be away).
2.39.16
नरेन्द्रेणैवमुक्तस्तु
गत्वा कोशगृहं ततः ।
प्रायच्छत्सर्वमाहृत्य
सीतायै सममेव तत् ॥
Told thus by the lord of the peoples, he went to the locker room and brought everything and gave all of them to Seetā.
2.39.17
सा सुजाता सुजातानि
वैदेही प्रस्थिता वनम् ।
भूषयामास गात्राणि
तैर्विचित्रैर्विभूषणैः ॥
That high-born princess of Vidēha who was ready to leave for the Vana decorated her beautiful figure with those assorted and elegant ornaments.
2.39.18
व्यराजयत वैदेही
वेश्म तत्सुविभूषिता ।
उद्यतोंऽशुमतः काले
खं प्रभेव विवस्वतः ॥
The princess of Vidēha, thus decked completely, illuminated that house like the rays of rising sun illuminating the sky.
2.39.19
तां भुजाभ्यां परिष्वज्य
श्वश्रूर्वचनमब्रवीत् ।
अनाचरन्ती कृपणम्
मूर्ध्न्युपाघ्राय मैथिलीम् ॥
The mother-in-law then smelled her head and took that princess of Mithilā, who displayed absolutely no sense of despondency, into her arms in embrace, and said:
2.39.20
असत्यस्सर्वलोकेऽस्मिन्
सततं सत्कृताः प्रियैः ।
भर्तारं नानुमन्यन्ते
विनिपातगतं स्त्रियः ॥
It is seen everywhere in this world, that women of dubious devotion do not care about their husbands when their fortunes take a wrong turn, even though they had been well cared for by them all the while.
2.39.21
एष स्वभावो नारीणाम्
अनुभूय पुरा सुखम् ।
अल्पामप्यापदं प्राप्य
दुष्यन्ति प्रजहत्यपि ॥
Such is the nature of women that even after enjoying all the comforts, they turn for the worse and even desert, at the slightest of discomfort!
2.39.22
असत्यशीला विकृता
दुर्ग्राह्यहृदयास्सदा ।
युवत्यः पापसंङ्कल्पाः
क्षणमात्राद्विरागिणः ॥
Women of dubious character are inconstant and harbor evil thoughts. Their hearts can hardly be touched. And their love dries up in a matter of seconds.
2.39.23
न कुलं न कृतं विद्या
न दत्तं नापि सङ्ग्रहः ।
स्त्रीणां गृह्णाति हृदयम्
अनित्यहृदया हि ताः ॥
Women waver in their mind a lot. Neither the renown of the lineage, nor the accomplishments of learning, nor the favors done to them, nor the gifts given to them, nor all the respect shown to them are enough to win over their hearts.
2.39.24
साध्वीनां हि स्थितानां तु
शीले सत्ये श्रुते शमे ।
स्त्रीणां पवित्रं परमम्
पतिरेको विशिष्यते ॥
Women who are good natured, truthful, possessing good character and serenity of mind, and aware of the dictates of the custom, hold husband alone to be the ultimate and sacred.
2.39.25
स त्वया नावमन्तव्यः
पुत्रः प्रव्राजितो मम ।
तव दैवतमस्त्वेष
निर्धनः सधनोऽपि वा ॥
May my son, who is destined to live in exile, be never slighted by you. I hope you will treat him as your god, regardless of whether he has money or not.
2.39.26
विज्ञाय वचनं सीता
तस्या धर्मार्थसंहितम् ।
कृताञ्जलिरुवाचेदम्
श्वश्रूमभिमुखे स्थिताम् ॥
Appreciating in full those righteous and meaningful words and standing in front of the mother-in-law with palms joined in reverence, Seetā, said:
2.39.27
करिष्ये सर्वमेवाहम्
आर्या यदनुशास्ति माम् ।
अभिज्ञास्मि यथा भर्तुः
वर्तितव्यं श्रुतं च मे ॥
I shall do everything just as you, the noble woman, have instructed me. I have learned the same in the past and hence am aware of how to conduct myself with a husband.
2.39.28
न मामसज्जनेनार्या
समानयितुमर्हति ।
धर्माद्विचलितुं नाहम्
अलं चन्द्रादिव प्रभा ॥
May you, O noble lady, not equate me with those bad women! I am not capable of deviating from Dharma just as moonlight is not capable of leaving the moon!
2.39.29
नातन्त्री वाद्यते वीणा
नाचक्रो वर्तते रथः ।
नापतिस्सुखमेधेत
या स्यादपि शतात्मजा ॥
Neither can a Veeṇa produce music without strings, nor can a chariot go anywhere without wheels. Similarly, a woman can never know happiness without a husband, even if she has a hundred sons.
2.39.30
मितं ददाति हि पिता
मितं माता मितं सुतः ।
अमितस्य हि दातारम्
भर्तारं का न पूजयेत् ॥
What a father can give is limited. Limited is what a mother or sons can give. There is a no limit to what a husband can provide. Which woman would not respect her husband?
2.39.31
साहमेवं गता श्रेष्ठा
श्रुतधर्मपरावरा ।
आर्ये किमवमन्येऽहम्
स्त्रीणां भर्ता हि दैवतम् ॥
Having learned the best of the Dharma, and having conducted myself thus far, O noble lady, how could I ever disrespect him! Husband, indeed, is the god for a woman!
2.39.32
सीताया वचनं श्रुत्वा
कौसल्या हृदयङ्गमम् ।
शुद्धसत्त्वा मुमोचाश्रु
सहसा दुःखहर्षजम् ॥
On hearing those heart-warming words of Seetā, the immaculate Kousalyā shed tears of joy and grief, at once.
2.39.33
तां प्राञ्जलिरभिक्रम्य
मातृमध्येऽतिसत्कृताम् ।
रामः परमधर्मात्मा
मातरं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Then Rāma, the ultimate among Dharmātmas, with palms joined in reverence, circumambulated his mother, who was the most revered among all the mothers.
2.39.34
अम्ब मा दुःखिता भूस्त्वम्
पश्य त्वं पितरं मम ।
क्षयो हि वनवासस्य
क्षिप्रमेव भविष्यति ॥
O mother! May you not yield to grief! Please take good care of my father. My stay in Vana will come to an end, very soon.
2.39.35
सुप्तायास्ते गमिष्यन्ति
नव वर्षाणि पञ्च च ।
सा समग्रमिह प्राप्तम्
मां द्रक्ष्यसि सुहृद्वृतम् ॥
Nine and five years will pass by like a night's sleep. And then you shall see me, returning here with my friends, having fulfilled the promise!
2.39.36
एतावदभिनीतार्थम्
उक्त्वा स जननीं वचः ।
त्रयश्शतशतार्धाश्च
ददर्शा वेक्ष्य मातरः ॥
Having thus succinctly conveyed his firm resolve to his mother, he paused for a moment and looked at his three hundred and fifty mothers.
2.39.37
ताश्चापि स तथैवार्ता
मातऽर्दशरथात्मजः ।
धर्मयुक्तमिदं वाक्यम्
निजगाद कृताञ्जलिः ॥
With palms joined in reverence, the son of Daṡaratha spoke to those women who were equally distressed, these words that echo Dharma:
2.39.38
संवासात्परुषं किञ्चित्
अज्ञानाद्वापि यत्कृतम् ।
तन्मे समनुजानीत
सर्वाश्चामन्त्रयामि वः ॥
Please forgive me if I have done anything harsh, because of ignorance or intimacy. Please allow me to take leave of you.
2.39.39
वचनं राघवस्यैतत्
धर्मयुक्तं समाहितम् ।
शुश्रुवुस्ताः स्त्रियंस्सर्वाः
शोकोपहतचेतसः ॥
Hearing those well balanced words of Rāghava that were so informed by Dharma, the hearts of those women were wrung with grief.
2.39.40
जज्ञेऽथ तासां सन्नादः
क्रौञ्चीनामिव निस्वनः ।
मानवेन्द्रस्य भार्याणाम्
एवं वदति राघवे ॥
Then, as Rāghava said those words, the wives of that ruler of the people wailed loudly that was reminiscent of the cries of Krounca birds.
2.39.41
मुरजपणवमेघघोषवत्
दशरथवेश्म बभूव यत्पुरा ।
विलपितपरिदेवनाकुलं
व्यसनगतं तदभूत्सुदुःखितम् ॥
The palace of Daṡaratha, which used to reverberate with the sounds of musical instruments like Muraja and Paṇava resembling the rumbling of heavy clouds, was now filled with wailing and lamentation, and plunged in grief by the great tragedy that fell upon it!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकोनचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty ninth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
