Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 67
In this Sarga, Hanumān, asked by Rāma, relates all that Seetā said, including the story of the crow at Citrakūṭa and her pitiful pleas to be saved from the Rākshasas.
5.67.1
एवमुक्तस्तु हनुमान्
राघवेण महात्मना ।
सीताया भाषितं सर्वम्
न्यवेदयत राघवे ॥
Thus asked by Mahātma Rāghava, Hanumān, related all that Seetā said to Rāghava.
5.67.2
इदमुक्तवती देवी
जानकी पुरुषर्षभ ।
पूर्व वृत्तमभिज्ञानम्
चित्रकूटे यथातथम् ॥
O bull among men! Janaki, the lady, told this in detail as it happened in the past at Citrakūṭa:
5.67.3
सुखसुप्ता त्वया सार्धम्
जानकी पूर्वमुत्थिता ।
वायसस्सहसोत्पत्य
विददार स्तनान्तरे ॥
Jānaki, who was sleeping by you woke up before you did, when, all of a sudden, a crow jumped upon her and tore the skin between her breasts apart.
5.67.4
पर्यायेण च सुप्तस्त्वम्
देव्यङ्के भरताग्रज ।
पुनश्च किल पक्षी स
देव्या जनयति व्यथाम् ॥
It seems, O elder brother of Bharata, the bird came again and hurt her, after you took your turn to sleep on her lap.
5.67.5
पुनः पुनरुपागम्य
विददार भृशं किल ।
ततस्त्वं बोधितस्तस्याः
शोणितेन समुक्षितः ॥
It seems that you woke up, becoming wet by the blood, with that bird coming again and again and tearing her skin apart.
5.67.6
वायसेन च तेनैव
सततं बाध्यमानया ।
बोधितः किल देव्या त्वम्
सुखसुप्तः परन्तप ॥
You, it seems, O tormentor of foe, who were sleeping comfortably, was woken up by her, who was being pestered by the crow constantly.
This Ṡlōka seems to be an alternate version of the previous Ṡlōka.
5.67.7
तां तु दृष्ट्वा महाबाहो
दारितां च स्तनान्तरे ।
आशीविष इव क्रुद्धो
निश्वसन्नभ्यभाषथाः ॥
Seeing that obnoxious bruise it did between her breasts, O you of mighty arm, you became outraged and seem to have said, hissing like a venomous serpent:
5.67.8
नखाग्रैः केन ते भीरु
दारितं तु स्तनान्तरम् ।
कः क्रीडति सरोषेण
पञ्चवक्त्रेण भोगिना ॥
O timid one, who is it that dares to do such an obnoxious deed between your breasts? Who is it that dares to mess with an enraged five-hooded serpent?
5.67.9
निरीक्षमाणस्सहसा
वायसं समवैक्षथाः ।
नखैस्सरुधिरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
तामेवाभिमुखं स्थितम् ॥
As you looked around, it seems, you immediately and clearly saw a crow with blood on its sharp talons ready to jump on her.
5.67.10
सुतः किल स शक्रस्य
वायसः पततां वरः ।
धरान्तरचरश्शीघ्रम्
पवनस्य गतौ समः ॥
It seems that that eminent bird, an equal to the wind in its swiftness which lives in the crevices of the earth, was the son of Ṡakra.
5.67.11
ततस्तस्मिन्महाबाहो
कोपसंवर्तितेक्षणः ।
वायसे त्वं कृथाः क्रूराम्
मतिं मतिमतां वर ॥
Then, O sagacious one, O you of mighty arm, it seems that you, rolling your eyes in wrath, made up your mind to deal a dreadful blow to it.
5.67.12
स दर्भं संस्तराद्गृह्य
ब्रह्मास्त्रेण ह्ययोजयः ।
स दीप्त इव कालाग्निः
जज्वालाभिमुखः खगम् ॥
He drew a reed of Darbha grass from his grass mat and infused it with the power of the Astra of Brahma. And it blazed before the eyes of the bird like the fire of Dissolution.
5.67.13
क्षिप्तवांस्त्वं प्रदीप्तं हि
दर्भं तं वायसं प्रति ।
ततस्तु वायसं दीप्तः
स दर्भोऽनुजगाम ह ॥
You hurled that blazing Darbha towards the crow. That blazing Darbha then chased the crow.
5.67.14
स पित्रा च परित्यक्तः
सुरैश्च समहर्षिभिः ।
त्रीन् लोकान् सम्परिक्रम्य
त्रातारं नाधिगच्छति ।
पुनरेवागतस्त्रस्तः
त्वत्सकाशमरिंदम ॥
Disowned by his father, and by all the Dēvas and Maharshis alike, it went around all the three worlds, but could find no one that would save it. Then, O subduer of the foe, it came back to your presence again.
5.67.15
स तं निपतितं भूमौ
शरण्यश्शरणागतम् ।
वधार्हमपि काकुत्स्थ
कृपया पर्यपालयः ॥
Even though it fell on the ground seeking refuge and deserved to be killed, you, who are the ultimate recourse for all, spared him out of compassion, O Kākutsthā!
5.67.16
मोघमस्त्रं न शक्यं तु
कर्तुमित्येव राघव ।
भवांस्तस्याक्षि काकुस्य
हिनस्ति स्म स दक्षिणम् ॥
Then, as that Astra could not be allowed to go waste, O Rāghava, you took his right eye with it.
5.67.17
राम त्वां स नमस्कृत्य
राज्ञे दशरथाय च ।
विसृष्टस्तु तदा काक
प्रतिपेदे स्वमालयम् ॥
Let go off by you, that crow seems to have saluted you, O Rāma, and the king Daṡaratha, and went back to its own place.
5.67.18
एवमस्त्रविदां श्रेष्ठः
सत्त्ववान् शीलवानपि ।
किमर्थमस्त्रं रक्षस्सु
न योजयति राघवः ॥
Why does Rāghava, the best among the masters of Astras, not direct the Astras towards the Rākshasas, being a man of true strength and character?
5.67.19
न नागा नापि गन्धर्वा
नासुरा न मरुद्गणाः ।
न च सर्वे रणे शक्ता
रामं प्रतिसमासितुम् ॥
No one, be it the Nāgas, the Gandharvas, the hosts of Maruts, or even all put together, are capable of standing against Rāma in battle.
5.67.20
तस्य वीर्यवतः कश्चित्
यद्यस्ति मयि सम्भ्रमः ।
क्षिप्रं सुनिशितैर्बाणैः
हन्यतां युधि रावणः ॥
Even if that valiant one had the least of concern for me, may Rāvaṇa be killed in a fight immediately by his sharp arrows.
5.67.21
भ्रातुरादेशमाज्ञाय
लक्ष्मणो वा परन्तपः ।
स किमर्थं नरवरो
न मां रक्षति राघवः ॥
Why does not Lakshmaṇa, the tormentor of foe, the best among men, the scion of Raghus, save me, obtaining permission from his brother?
5.67.22
शक्तौ तौ पुरुषव्याघ्रौ
वाय्वग्निसमतेजसौ ।
सुराणामपि दुर्धर्षौ
किमर्थं मामुपेक्षतः ॥
Why do those capable tigers among men, who equal the wind and fire in power, whom even the Dēvas dare not assail, ignore me?
5.67.23
ममैव दुष्कृतं किञ्चित्
महदस्ति न संशयः ।
समर्थौ सहितौ यन्माम्
नावेक्षेते परन्तपौ ॥
There must have been, without a doubt, a big blunder that I had committed; otherwise why would those scorchers of foe, being as capable as they are, turn a cold shoulder to me?
5.67.24
वैदेह्या वचनं श्रुत्वा
करुणं साश्रु भाषितम् ।
पुनरप्यहमार्यां ताम्
इदं वचनमब्रुवम् ॥
Hearing those pitiful words of Vaidēhee, spoken with tears (swelling in her eyes), I told these words again to that noble lady:
5.67.25
त्वच्छोकविमुखो रामो
देवि सत्येन ते शपे ।
रामे दुःखाभिभूते तु
लक्ष्मणः परितप्यते ॥
Grieving for you, O Dēvi, Rāma neglects everything else. I swear this on truth. And when Rāma is deluged by grief, Lakshmaṇa becomes sad too.
5.67.26
कथञ्चिद्भवती दृष्टा
न कालः परिशोचितुम् ।
अस्मिन्मुहूर्ते दुःखानाम्
अन्तं द्रक्ष्यसि भामिनि ॥
Somehow, you have been found.  This is no occasion to grieve.  This hour, as you shall see, O lovely one, is the beginning of the end of all sorrows!
5.67.27
तावुभौ नरशार्दूलौ
राजपुत्रावनिन्दितौ ।
त्वद्दर्शनकृतोत्साहौ
लङ्कां भस्मीकरिष्यतः ॥
Those two blameless princes, tigers among men, are eager to see you and they will reduce Laṅkā to ashes.
5.67.28
हत्वा च समरे रौद्रम्
रावणं सह बान्धवम् ।
राघवस्त्वां वरारोहे
स्वां पुरीं नयते ध्रुवम् ॥
Rāghava, O you of lovely Kaṭee, will certainly kill the fearsome Rāvaṇa and his kin in the battle, and will take you to his city.
5.67.29
यत्तु रामो विजानीयात्
अभिज्ञानमनिन्दिते ।
प्रीतिसञ्जननं तस्य
प्रदातुं त्वमिहार्हसि ॥
O blameless one, please give me a token of identification that Rāma can recognize and will make him happy.
5.67.30
साभिवीक्ष्य दिशस्सर्वा
वेण्युद्ग्रथितमुत्तमम् ।
मुक्त्वा वस्त्राद्ददौ मह्यम्
मणिमेतं महाबल ॥
Looking all about her circumspectly, she untied the corner of her garment, took out this splendid jewel which is worn on the head, and gave it to me, O you of immense strength!
5.67.31
प्रतिगृह्य मणिं दिव्यम्
तव हेतो रघूद्वह ।
शिरसा तां प्रणम्यार्याम्
अहमागमने त्वरे ॥
Receiving this splendid jewel that is meant for you, O leader of Raghus, I made my bow to the noble lady and hurried to return.
5.67.32
गमने च कृतोत्साहम्
अवेक्ष्य वरवर्णिनी ।
विवर्धमानं च हि माम्
उवाच जनकात्मजा ।
अश्रुपूर्णमुखी दीना
बाष्पसन्दिग्धभाषिणी ।
ममोत्पतनसम्भ्रान्ता
शोकवेगसमाहता ॥
Seeing me grow in size and all set to start, that daughter of Janaka, her face filled with and her words blurred by tears, agitated seeing me ready to fly off and hit by a surge of grief, pitifully said these words:
5.67.34
हनुमन् सिंहसंकाशौ
उभौ तौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
सुग्रीवञ्च सहामात्यम्
सर्वान् ब्रूयाह्यनामयम् ॥
Please convey my wellbeing, O Hanumān, to both Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, who are like lions, and to Sugreeva and his ministers.
5.67.35
यथा च स महाबाहुः
मां तारयति राघवः ।
अस्माद्धुःखाम्बुसंरोधात्
त्वं समाधातुमर्हसि ॥
You should so plead my cause with Rāghava that he of the mighty arm takes me to the other end of this ocean of grief.
5.67.36
इमं च तीव्रं मम शोकवेगम्
रक्षोभिरेभिः परिभर्त्सनं च ।
ब्रूयास्तु रामस्य गतस्समीपम्
शिवश्च तेऽध्वास्तु हरिप्रवीर ॥
O best of the Veeras among Vānaras! After you are with Rāma, please tell him about my torrent of grief and the continuous threats of the Rākshasis; may everything fare well for you on your way!
5.67.37
एतत्तवार्या नृपराजसिंह
सीता वचः प्राह विषादपूर्वम् ।
एतच्च बुद्ध्वा गदितं मया त्वं
श्रद्धत्स्व सीतां कुशलां समग्राम् ॥
This is what, O lion among the rulers of people, that the noble Seetā told me in her grief. Please, from what I have said, be assured that Seetā is safe and well!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे सप्तषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty seventh Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
