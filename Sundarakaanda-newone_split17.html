Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 17
In this Sarga, Hanumān sees Rākshasis who are guarding Seetā, each one ugly and frightful in their own way, some with one eye, some with ugly feet, some with elephantine ears and so on. He sees Seetā doleful but keeping her spirits up, confident of her husband’s ability. Finding her, he is overwhelmed with joy.
5.17.1
ततः कुमुदषण्डाभ:
निर्मलं निर्मलोदयः ।
प्रजगाम नभश्चन्द्रो
हंसो नीलमिवोदकम् ॥
Then the clear moon, shining like a bed of lilies, rose up in the clear sky, resembling a (white) swan floating in dark water.
5.17.2
साचिव्यमिव कुर्वन् स
प्रभया निर्मलप्रभः ।
चन्द्रमा रश्मिभिश्शीतैः
सिषेवे पवनात्मजम् ॥
The moon of clear shine showered cool rays on the son of Vāyu, as if to give him company and counsel him with his brightness.
5.17.3
स ददर्श ततः सीताम्
पूर्णचन्द्रनिभाननाम् ।
शोकभारैरिव न्यस्ताम्
भारैर्नावमिवाम्भसि ॥
Then he beheld Seetā whose face resembled the full moon sinking under the weight of grief like a ship with its heavy load, in water.
5.17.4
दिदृक्षमाणो वैदेहीम्
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ।
स ददर्शाविदूरस्था
राक्षसीर्घोरदर्शनाः ॥
Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, while taking a good look at Vaidēhee also saw, not too far from there, Rākshasis of terrible appearance.
5.17.5
एकाक्षीमेककर्णां च
कर्णप्रावरणां तथा ।
अकर्णां शङ्कुकर्णां च
मस्तकोच्छ्वासनासिकाम् ॥
One had only one eye and another only one ear. One had huge ears that seemed to cover her, while another had no ears at all, while yet another had pointed ears that resembled conches. Another had a nose that stretched upwards as if it was touching her head.
5.17.6
अतिकायोत्तमाङ्गीं च
तनुदीर्घशिरोधराम् ।
ध्वस्तकेशीं तथाऽकेशीम्
केशकम्बलधारिणीम् ॥
One had a huge body and huge head and another had a long neck (like that of a crane). One had disheveled hair, while another had no hair at all, while yet another had hair that covered her like a thatch.
5.17.7
लम्बकर्णललाटां च
लम्बोदरपयोधराम् ।
लम्बोष्ठीं चुबुकोष्ठीं च
लम्बास्यां लम्बजानुकाम् ॥
One had long ears and a huge forehead while another had pendulous belly and breasts. One had drooping lips while another had lips for her chin. One had a very long face, while another had protruding knees.
5.17.8
ह्रस्वां दीर्घां तथा कुब्जाम्
विकटां वामनां तथा ।
करालां भुग्नवक्त्रां च
पिङ्गाक्षीं विकृताननाम् ॥
One was too short, another was too tall, while yet another was a hunchback and one more was crookedly small. One had buck teeth while another had a twisted mouth. One had yellow eyes while another had a crooked face.
5.17.9
विकृताः पिङ्गलाः कालीः
क्रोधनाः कलहप्रियाः ।
कालायसमहाशूल
कूटमुद्गरधारिणीः ॥
Some were crooked, some were black and some had yellow eyes. Some had a bad temper while others were given to quarreling. Some wielded huge iron spears and some Kūṭas and Mudgaras.
5.17.10
वराहमृगशार्दूल
महिषाजशिवामुखीः ।
गजोष्ट्रहयपादीश्च
निखातशिरसोऽपराः ॥
Some had faces resembling those of boars, deer, tigers, water buffalos, sheep and jackals. Some had hooves for feet recalling those of elephants, camels and horses. Some had their heads shrunk into their torsos.
5.17.11
एकहस्तैकपादाश्च
खरकर्ण्यश्वकर्णिकाः ।
गोकर्णीर्हस्तिकर्णीश्च
हरिकर्णीस्तथापराः ॥
Some had only one hand and others only one leg. Some had their ears like those of donkeys and others like those of horses, while yet others had theirs like those of cows, elephants and monkeys.
5.17.12
अनासा अतिनासाश्च
तिर्यङ्नासा विनासिकाः ।
गजसन्निभनासाश्च
ललाटोच्छ्वासनासिकाः ॥
Some had no nose, others had too big a one, while others had noses turned sideways while yet others had crooked noses. Some had a nose that resembled an elephant’s trunk while others had noses extending to their foreheads.
5.17.13
हस्तिपादा महापादा
गोपादाः पादचूलिकाः ।
अतिमात्रशिरोग्रीवा
अतिमात्रकुचोदरीः ॥
Some had huge feet, some had feet that looked like those of elephants and cows, while yet others had theirs covered with hair. Some had abnormally large heads and necks while others had abnormally large breasts and bellies.
5.17.14
अतिमात्रास्यनेत्राश्च
दीर्घजिह्वानखास्तथा ।
अजामुखीर्हस्तिमुखीः
गोमुखीस्सूकरीमुखीः ॥
Some had abnormally huge mouths and others, abnormally large eyes. Some had long tongues and others, long nails. Some had faces resembling those of sheep, some those of elephants, cows and boars.
5.17.15
हयोष्ट्रखरवक्त्राश्च
राक्षसीर्घोरदर्शनाः ।
शूलमुद्गरहस्ताश्च
क्रोधनाः कलहप्रियाः ॥
Some Rākshasis looked terrifying, with faces resembling those of horses, camels and donkeys. Wielding spears and Mudgaras in their hands, they were irascible and given to quarreling.
5.17.16
कराला धूम्रकेशीश्च
राक्षसीर्विकृताननाः ।
पिबन्तीः सततं पानम्
सदा मांससुराप्रियाः ।
मांसशोणितदिग्धाङ्गीः
मांसशोणितभोजनाः ॥
With buck teeth and hair rising like smoke, those Rākshasis with crooked faces indulged in nonstop swilling addicted to liquor and meat.
They lived on flesh and blood, their bodies smeared with them all over.
5.17.17
ता ददर्श कपिश्रेष्ठो
रोमहर्षणदर्शनाः ।
स्कन्धवन्तमुपासीनाः
परिवार्य वनस्पतिम् ॥
It was these kind of women sitting around the massive tree of huge trunk, the sight of whom made one’s hair stand on its end, that that great Vānara saw.
5.17.18
तस्याधस्ताच्च तां देवीम्
राजपुत्रीमनिन्दिताम् ।
लक्षयामास लक्ष्मीवान्
हनुमान् जनकात्मजाम् ॥
The blessed Hanumān looked once again at that lady, a blemish-less princess, daughter of Janaka, who was under the tree.
5.17.19
निष्प्रभां शोकसन्तप्ताम्
मलसङ्कुलमूर्धजाम् ।
क्षीणपुण्यां च्युतां भूमौ
तारां निपतितामिव ॥
Devoid of all luster, scorched by grief, her hair covered with dirt, she looked like a star fallen on the ground with its Puṇya exhausted.
5.17.20
चारित्रव्यपदेशाढ्याम्
भर्तृदर्शनदुर्गताम् ।
भूषणैरुत्तमैर्हीनाम्
भर्तृवात्सल्यभूषणाम् ॥
Endowed though with rich character, she was still poor for not having sight of her husband. Though lacking every ornament, she was still adorned with the love of her husband.
5.17.21
राक्षसाधिपसंरुद्धाम्
बन्धुभिश्च विना कृताम् ।
वियूथां सिंहसंरुद्धाम्
बद्धां गजवधूमिव ॥
With none of her people around her and entrapped by the lord of Rākshasas, she was like a cow-elephant chained and separated from the herd with a lion pouncing on her.
5.17.22
चन्द्ररेखां पयोदान्ते
शारदाभ्रैरिवावृताम् ।
क्लिष्टरूपामसंस्पर्शात्
अयुक्तामिव वल्लकीम् ॥
She was like the digit of the moon covered by post-monsoon clouds at the end of the rainy season. Haggard and unkempt, she was like a Veeṇa that is not tuned.
5.17.23
सीतां भर्तृवशे युक्ताम्
अयुक्तां राक्षसीवशे ।
अशोकवनिकामध्ये
शोकसागरमाप्लुताम् ॥
Seetā, who used to be under the protective arms of her husband, had now fallen into the hands of Rākshasis, submerged in an ocean of grief in the middle of that (enchanting) Aṡōka Vana.
5.17.24
ताभिः परिवृतां तत्र
सग्रहामिव रोहिणीम् ।
ददर्श हनुमान् देवीम्
लतामकुसुमामिव ॥
Hanumān saw her thus surrounded by them like the star Rōhiṇee by planets, and looking like a creeper with no flowers.
5.17.25
सा मलेन च दिग्धाङ्गी
वपुषा चाप्यलङ्कृता ।
मृणाली पङ्कदिग्धेव
विभाति न विभाति च ॥
With her limbs smeared with dust and her body lacking toilet, she was like a lotus stalk besmirched with mire shining on the one hand and not on the other.
5.17.26
मलिनेन तु वस्त्रेण
परिक्लिष्टेन भामिनीम् ।
संवृतां मृगशाबाक्षीम्
ददर्श हनुमान् कपिः ।
तां देवीं दीनवदनाम्
अदीनां भर्तृतेजसा ।
रक्षितां स्वेन शीलेन
सीतामसितलोचनाम् ॥
Hanumān, the Vānara, saw the lovely dame Seetā of dark fawn eyes, clad in her soiled and much worn garment, her face looking doleful, but her spirts not at all dented, confident of the abilities of her husband and having her own strength of character for a shield.
5.17.28
तां दृष्ट्वा हनुमान् सीताम्
मृगशाबनिभेक्षणाम् ।
मृगकन्यामिव त्रस्ताम्
वीक्षमाणां समन्ततः ।
दहन्तीमिव निःश्वासैः
वृक्षान् पल्लवधारिणः ।
संघातमिव शोकानाम्
दुःखस्योर्मिमिवोत्थिताम् ।
तां क्षमां सुविभक्ताङ्गी
विनाभरणशोभिनीम् ।
प्रहर्षमतुलं लेभे
मारुतिः प्रेक्ष्य मैथिलीम् ॥
Hanumān, having found Seetā, the princess of Mithilā of fawn eyes and perfectly symmetrical limbs, looking around in every direction like a scared fawn, seeming to burn the trees with young shoots with her hot sighs, looking as if all the grief gathered at one place and like a rising billow of sorrow, but shining bright even without ornaments, and looking like the mother earth herself, felt extremely happy.
5.17.31
हर्षजानि च सोऽश्रूणि
तां दृष्ट्वा मदिरेक्षणाम् ।
मुमुचे हनुमांस्तत्र
नमश्चक्रे च राघवम् ॥
Hanumān shed tears of joy seeing that lady of intoxicating eyes and bowed to Rāma (in his mind).
5.17.32
नमस्कृत्वा च रामाय
लक्ष्मणाय च वीर्यवान् ।
सीतादर्शनसंहृष्टो
हनुमान् संवृतोऽभवत् ॥
Overwhelmed with the joy of seeing Seetā, the valiant Hanumān bowed to Rāma and Lakshmaṇa (in his mind) and hid under the foliage.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे सप्तदशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventeenth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
