Baala Kaanda - Sarga 47
The previous Sarga left us in suspense regarding what would happen after the fetus of Diti was cut into pieces by Indra.
In this Sarga, Diti reconciles with Indra and asks him to make the seven pieces of the fetus to be the overseers of the seven Maruts under his command. Indra obliges her.
The Sarga ends with Sumati, the king of Viṡāla, receiving Viṡwāmitra.
1.47.1
सप्तधा तु कृते गर्भे
दितिः परमदुःखिता ।
सहस्राक्षं दुराधर्षम्
वाक्यं सानुनयाब्रवीत् ॥
Diti was overwhelmed by sadness upon the fetus being cut into seven pieces. However, she adapted a reconciliatory tone and said to the invincible thousand-eyed Indra:
1.47.2
ममापराधाद् गर्भोऽयम्
सप्तधा विफलीकृतः ।
नापराधोऽस्ति देवेश
तवात्र बलसूदन ॥
O lord of Dēvas, annihilator of Balāsura! It is all my fault that the fetus is cut into the seven pieces, and you cannot be held responsible for this!
1.47.3
प्रियं तु कर्तुमिच्छामि
मम गर्भविपर्यये ।
मरुतां सप्त सप्तानाम्
स्थानपाला भवन्त्विमे ॥
Since I have lost my fetus unexpectedly, I would request a favor from you. Make those seven pieces as overseers of the seven Maruts.
Maruts are seven states or layers of the 'Vāyu' or wind element.
Higgs-Boson, which was predicted in 1964 and detected in 2012 is at the border of energy and mass. In a similar way Ṛshis saw a gradual progression from energy into matter.
From the Ākāsa (space) emerges Vāyu (air).The energy in the Space is in wave (Ṡabda) form and when it becomes Vāyu, it acquires the first substantial (material) form.
Within the Vāyu state, Ṛshis envisioned seven sub-states, which are called the Maruts. Another way to understand Maruts is by likening them to the layers of atmosphere such as Troposphere, Stratosphere, Mesosphere, etc.
The concerns of particle physics and astronomy and the concerns of ancient Ṛshis in regards to the 'Brahman' (Brahman is described as the smallest of the small and the biggest of the big) are same. They both attempt at modeling the complexities of the Universe.
Hence the often drawn comparison between ancient Vedanta and modern physics. It is a germane and genuine comparison, and not artificial or meant to steal the thunder from modern achievements, as some would often mistake.
One may question whether the Ṛshis understood the complexity of Universe 'correctly' or not. But they certainly envisaged the complexities that could be expected in the process of 'something coming out of nothing'.
They did not dismiss the complexity of the emergence of universe, with a simplistic notion like "god said, 'let there be light' and there it is". They attempted to peer into it. That is why many modern day physicists who ridicule Abrahamic descriptions of the Universe pause and admire the Indic descriptions.
1.47.4
वातस्कन्धा इमे सप्त
चरन्तु दिवि पुत्रक ।
मारुता इति विख्याता
दिव्यरूपा ममात्मजाः ॥
Dear son! May my seven sons be known forever as Maruts living in divine forms, as overseers of the seven layers of air.
1.47.5
ब्रह्मलोकं चरत्वेक
इन्द्रलोकं तथापरः ।
दिवि वायुरिति ख्यातः
तृतीयोऽपि महायशाः ॥
One of them would go to the Brahma Lōka, another would be in Indra Lōka, and the third one to be known as Vāyu!
What we consider as Vāyu (air) or wind god is one of the seven Maruts.
1.47.6
चत्वारस्तु सुरश्रेष्ठ
दिशो वै तव शासनात् ।
सञ्चरिष्यन्तु भद्रं ते
देवभूता ममात्मजाः ।
त्वत्कृतेनैव नाम्ना च
मारुता इति विश्रुताः ॥
O best among Suras! May all bode well for you! The rest of the four of my sons should become Dēvas and overseers of four directions under your command. They would be known as Maruts, because of you!
Māruta comes from the phrase, “Mā Ruda!” – “don't cry!” which was uttered by Indra.
1.47.7
तस्यास्तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
सहस्राक्षः पुरन्दरः ।
उवाच प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यम्
दितिं बलनिषूदनः ॥
The annihilator of Bālāsura, the thousand-eyed Purandara (Indra), with folded hands said to Diti:
1.47.8
सर्वमेतद्यथोक्तं ते
भविष्यति न संशयः ।
विचरिष्यन्ति भद्रं ते
देवरूपास्तवात्मजाः ॥
Everything will happen as you said, for certain. Your sons will become Dēvas and shall be overseers as you wished!
1.47.9
एवं तौ निश्चयं कृत्वा
मातापुत्रौ तपोवने ।
जग्मतुस्त्रिदिवं राम
कृतार्थाविति नः श्रुतम् ॥
O Rāma! Having reached a compromise thus in that Tapōvana, both the mother and son went to Heaven with their respective missions accomplished. And thus we hear about them.
1.47.10
एष देशस्स काकुत्स्थ
महेन्द्राध्युषितः पुरा ।
दितिं यत्र तपस्सिद्धाम्
एवं परिचचार सः ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! This (Viṡāla) is the land where Diti performed Tapa and Indra served her, having made it his abode for a while.
1.47.11
इक्ष्वाकोस्तु नरव्याघ्र
पुत्रः परमधार्मिकः ।
अलम्बुसायामुत्पन्नो
विशाल इति विश्रुतः ।
तेन चासीदिह स्थाने
विशालेति पुरी कृता ॥
O tiger among men! Ikshwāku had a renowned son in Alambusa by name Viṡāla, who was a virtuous Dhārmika. This city named Viṡāla was built by him.
A single person creating a whole city is not very common. We see it happening a few times in history here and there. But in epic times, as can be expected, it occurs more often than normal.
1.47.13
विशालस्य सुतो राम
हेमचन्द्रो महाबलः ।
सुचन्द्र इति विख्यातो
हेमचन्द्रादनन्तरः ॥
O Rāma! Viṡāla's son was the mighty strong Hēmacandra. And after Hēmacandra came the renowned Sucandra.
1.47.14
सुचन्द्रतनयो राम
धूम्राश्व इति विश्रुतः ।
धूम्राश्वतनयश्चापि
सृञ्जयः समपद्यत ॥
O Rāma! Sucandra's son was the well-known Dhoomraṡwa. Dhoomraṡwa's son was Sṛñjaya.
1.47.15
सृञ्जयस्य सुतः श्रीमान्
सहदेवः प्रतापवान् ।
कुशाश्वः सहदेवस्य
पुत्रः परमधार्मिकः ॥
Sṛñjaya's son was the rich and valiant Sahadēva. And Sahadēva's son was Kuṡāṡwa, the supreme Dhārmika.
1.47.16
कुशाश्वस्य महातेजा
सोमदत्तः प्रतापवान् ।
सोमदत्तस्य पुत्रस्तु
काकुत्स्थ इति विश्रुतः ॥
Kuṡāṡwa's son was the supremely radiant and valiant Sōmadatta. Sōmadatta's son was the well-known Kākutstha.
1.47.17
तस्य पुत्रो महातेजाः
सम्प्रत्येष पुरीमिमाम् ।
आवसत्यमरप्रख्यः
सुमतिर्नाम दुर्जयः ॥
His indomitable, supremely radiant son who is comparable to Amaras (Dēvas), by name Sumati, is living in this city now.
1.47.18
इक्ष्वाकोस्तु प्रसादेन
सर्वे वैशालिका नृपाः ।
दीर्घायुषो महात्मानो
वीर्यवन्तः सुधार्मिकाः ॥
By the blessings of Ikshwāku, all the kings of Viṡāla lived long, and had been Mahātmas, Dhārmikas and spirited.
1.47.19
इहाद्य रजनीं राम
सुखं वत्स्यामहे वयम् ।
श्वः प्रभाते नरश्रेष्ठ
जनकं द्रष्टुमर्हसि ॥
O Rāma! We will take rest for the night here. We will proceed tomorrow to visit Janaka, the prominent among kings.
1.47.20
सुमतिस्तु महातेजा
विश्वामित्रमुपागतम् ।
श्रुत्वा नरवरश्रेष्ठः
प्रत्युद्गच्छन्महायशाः ॥
The best among men, supremely radiant and renowned Sumati learned about the arrival of Viṡwāmitra and went to receive him.
1.47.21
पूजां च परमां कृत्वा
सोपाध्यायः सबान्धवः ।
प्राञ्जलिः कुशलं पृष्ट्वा
विश्वामित्रमथाब्रवीत् ॥
He, along with his Gurus and relatives offered the best of his regards and respects to Viṡwāmitra and with folded hands enquired about his well-being and said:
1.47.22
धन्योऽस्म्यनुगृहीतोऽस्मि
यस्य मे विषयं मुनिः ।
सम्प्राप्तो दर्शनं चैव
नास्ति धन्यतरो मया ॥
O Muni! I am indeed fortunate. No one could be more fortunate than me. With your visit to my country, and with the fortune of being in your presence, I feel blessed.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे सप्तचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty seventh Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
