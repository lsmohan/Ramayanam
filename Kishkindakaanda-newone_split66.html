Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 66
In this Sarga, Jāmbavān asks Hanumān why he was sitting alone quietly. He reminds Hanumān that he is as strong as Garuḍa. He recounts the story of his birth to him. He tells that he is the son of Vāyu and is equal in strength to him. He reminds him of the episode of how he flew into the sky to snatch the rising sun, whom he mistook to be a fruit and how Brahma gave him the boon of not being slayed by any weapon in a battle and how Indra gave him the boon of commanding his death at a time he wills.
He tells Hanumān that he is like another Sugreeva for the Vānara army who were sunk in despair. He urges him to get up and launch forth with his full prowess to cross the ocean.
Hanumān then assumes a gigantic form of himself.
4.66.1
अनेकशतसाहस्रीम्
विषण्णां हरिवाहिनीम् ।
जाम्बवान्समुदीक्ष्यैवम्
हनूमन्तमथाब्रवीत् ॥
Jāmbavān, noting the sadness in which the army of hundreds of thousands of Vānaras had sunk, turned to Hanumān and said:
4.66.2
वीर वानरलोकस्य
सर्वशास्त्रविदां वर ।
तूष्णीमेकान्तमाश्रित्य
हनूमन्किं न जल्पसि ॥
O Veera among the world of Vānaras! O the foremost of the savants of all Ṡastras! O Hanumān! Why are you sitting by yourself, so silent and without saying a word?
4.66.3
हनूमन्हरिराजस्य
सुग्रीवस्य समो ह्यसि ।
रामलक्ष्मणयोश्चापि
तेजसा च बलेन च ॥
O Hanumān! You are an equal of Sugreeva, the king of Vānaras, and for that matter, of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa themselves in power and strength.
4.66.4
अरिष्टनेमिनः पुत्रो
वैनतेयो महाबलः ।
गरुत्मानिति विख्यातः
उत्तमस्सर्वपक्षिणाम् ॥
Garuḍa, the best among all birds, the son of Arishṭanēmi and Vinata, is well known for his immense strength.
4.66.5
बहुशो हि मया दृष्टः
सागरे स महाबलः ।
भुजङ्गानुद्धरन्पक्षी
महावेगो महायशाः ॥
I have seen, many a time, that bird of immense strength, of great speed and great renown, snatch the snakes from the ocean.
4.66.6
पक्षयोर्यद्बलं तस्य
तावद्भुजबलं तव ।
विक्रमश्चापि वेगश्च
न ते तेनापहीयते ॥
You have the same strength in your arms as he does in his wings. You are no inferior to him in your valor and speed.
4.66.7
बलं बुद्धिश्च तेजश्च
सत्त्वं च हरिपुङ्गव ।
विशिष्टं सर्वभूतेषु
किमात्मानं न सज्जसे ॥
You excel all creatures in strength, wisdom, power and vitality. O bull among Vānaras! How is it that you are not readying yourself?
4.66.8
अप्सराप्सरसां श्रेष्ठा
विख्याता पुञ्जिकस्थला ।
अञ्जनेति परिख्याता
पत्नी केसरिणो हरेः ॥
Puñjikasthalā, an Apsara, is the best of all Apsaras. She is famously known as Añjanā, the wife of the Kēsari, a Vānara.
4.66.9
विख्याता त्रिषु लोकेषु
रूपेणाप्रतिमा भुवि ।
अभिशापादभूत्तात
वानरी कामरूपिणी ।
दुहिता वानरेन्द्रस्य
कुञ्जरस्य महात्मनः ॥
My dear! She is famous in all the three worlds. There is none equal to her in beauty on earth.
Because of a curse, she became a Vānara woman who can take any form at will and the daughter Kuñjara, a Mahātma and the king of Vānaras.
4.66.10
मानुषं विग्रहं कृत्वा
रूपयौवनशालिनी ।
विचित्रमाल्याभरणा
कदाचित् क्षौम धारिणी ।
अचरत्पर्वतस्याग्रे
प्रावृडम्बुदसन्निभे ॥
Taking the form of a young and beautiful woman, clad in silks and wearing lovely flowers, she was once strolling on the top of a mountain that resembled a cloud in the monsoon season.
4.66.12
तस्या वस्त्रं विशालाक्ष्याः
पीतं रक्तदशं शुभम् ।
स्थितायाः पर्वतस्याग्रे
मारुतोऽपाहरच्छनैः ॥
When she of wide eyes was on the top of the mountain, Vāyu gently lifted up her lovely yellow garment of red border.
4.66.13
स ददर्श ततस्तस्या
वृत्तावूरू सुसंहतौ ।
स्तनौ च पीनौ सहितौ
सुजातं चारु चाननम् ॥
He then saw her rounded thighs that pressed against each other, the close and full breasts and the lovely well-formed face.
4.66.14
तां विशालायतश्रोणीम्
तनुमध्यां यशस्विनीम् ।
दृष्ट्वैव शुभसर्वाङ्गीं
पवनः काममोहितः ॥
The very sight of that renowned woman with her broad and wide Kaṭee, slender waist, and exquisite figure, made the wind—god succumb to love.
4.66.15
स तां भुजाभ्यां दीर्घाभ्याम्
पर्यष्वजत मारुतः ।
मन्मथाविष्टसर्वाङ्गो
गतात्मा तामनिन्दिताम् ॥
Losing his mind over that blemishless one and lust taking over his every limb, Vāyu embraced her with his long arms.
4.66.16
सा तु तत्रैव सम्भ्रान्ता
सुव्रता वाक्यमब्रवीत् ।
एकपत्नीव्रतमिदम्
को नाशयितुमिच्छति ॥
Then, quite bewildered at that (sudden development), she of high conventions said these words: ‘Who is it that is trying to mar my single-minded devotion to my husband?’
4.66.17
अञ्जनाया वचश्श्रुत्वा
मारुतः प्रत्यभाषत ॥
Hearing those words of Anjana, the wind—god responded:
4.66.17
न त्वां हिंसामि सुश्रोणि
मा भूत्ते मनसो भयम् ।
मनसाऽस्मि गतो यत्त्वाम्
परिष्वज्य यशस्विनि ॥
O you of lovely Kaṭee! I am not going to violate you! May your mind not cave in to fears! O you of great renown! I touched you only with my mind when I took you into my embrace!
4.66.18
वीर्यवान्बुद्धिसम्पन्नः
पुत्रस्तव भविष्यति ।
महासत्त्वो महातेजा
महाबलपराक्रमः ।
लङ्घने प्लवने चैव
भविष्यति मया समः  ॥
You will have a son of great valor and wisdom. Endowed with exceptional vitality, energy, strength and prowess, he will be my equal in taking a leap and jumping across.
4.66.20
एवमुक्ता ततस्तुष्टा
जननी ते महाकपे ।
गुहायां त्वां महाबाहो
प्रजज्ञे प्लवगर्षभम् ॥
O great Vānara of mighty arms! Thus told and pleased about it, your mother gave birth to you, the bull among Vānaras, in a cave.
4.66.21
अभ्युत्थितं ततस्सूर्यम्
बालो दृष्ट्वा महावने ।
फलं चेति जिघृक्षुस्त्वम्
उत्प्लुत्याभ्युद्गतो दिवम् ॥
Then, seeing the sun rise over the great Vana, you, being young, taking it for a fruit and wishing to seize it, sprang up and flew into the sky.
4.66.22
शतानि त्रीणि गत्वाऽथ
योजनानां महाकपे ।
तेजसा तस्य निर्धूतो
न विषादं ततोगतः ॥
O great Vānara! You did not lose heart even after going for three hundred Yōjanas and being scorched by the sun’s splendor.
4.66.23
त्वामप्युपगतं तूर्णम्
अन्तरिक्षं महाकपे ।
क्षिप्तमिन्द्रेण ते वज्रम्
कोपाविष्टेन धीमता ॥
O great Vānara! (Seeing) you leap into the sky suddenly, the sagacious Indra was outraged and hurled the Vajra at you.
4.66.24
तदा शैलाग्रशिखरे
वामो हनुरभज्यत ।
ततो हि नामधेयं ते
हनुमानिति कीर्त्यतम् ॥
Then you fell on top of the mountain peak when your left jaw was broken and that is how you came to be known as Hanumān.
Hanuma means jaw.
4.66.25
ततस्त्वां निहतं दृष्ट्वा
वायुर्गन्धवहस्स्वयम् ।
त्रैलोक्ये भृशसङ्कृद्धो
न ववौ वै प्रभञ्जनः ॥
Seeing you struck down thus, the wind god, the vehicle of smells, became very angry and froze himself in all the three worlds.
4.66.26
सम्भ्रान्ताश्च सूरास्सर्वे
त्रैलोक्ये क्षुभिते सति ।
प्रसादयन्ति संक्रुद्धम्
मारुतं भुवनेश्वराः ॥
With all the three worlds under shock, the bewildered Dēvas, the lords of the worlds, pacified the outraged Vāyu.
4.66.27
प्रसादिते च पवने
ब्रह्मा तुभ्यं वरं ददौ ।
अशस्त्रवध्यतां तात
समरे सत्यविक्रम ॥
My child! O you of true valor! After the wind—god was pacified, Brahma gave you a boon, that no weapon would be able to slay you on the battlefield.
4.66.28
वज्रस्य च निपातेन
विरुजं त्वां समीक्ष्य च ।
सहस्रनेत्रः प्रीतात्मा
ददौ ते वरमुत्तमम् ।
स्वच्छन्दतश्च मरणम्
तवस्यादिति वै प्रभो ॥
Amazed at your being completely unhurt from the strike of the Vajra, the thousand-eyed was very pleased and gave you the best of boons, that death shall wait upon your convenience, O lordly one!
4.66.29
स त्वं केसरिणः पुत्रः
क्षेत्रजो भीमविक्रमः ।
मारुतस्यौरसः पुत्रः
तेजसा चापि तत्समः ।
त्वं हि वायुसुतो वत्स
प्लवने चापि तत्समः ॥
Thus you, of formidable prowess, are the son of Kēsari, being born to his wife, and also the son of Vāyu, born of his power. You are also an equal to him in power. Being the son of Vāyu, my child, you are also an equal to him in your leap.
4.66.31
वयमद्य गतप्राणा
भवानस्मासु साम्प्रतम् ।
दाक्ष्यविक्रमसम्पन्नः
कपिराज इवापरः ॥
We are as good as dead now. You, endowed richly with ability and prowess, are like another king of Vānaras (Sugreeva) for us at this time.
4.66.32
त्रिविक्रमे मया तात
सशैलवनकानना ।
त्रिस्सप्तकृत्वः पृथिवी
परिक्रान्ता प्रदक्षिणम् ॥
My dear! I circumambulated the earth and all of its mountains, Vanas and forests, thrice seven times when Lord Vishṇu measured the worlds with his three strides.
Thrice seven times is a poetic expression for ‘twentyone’ times.
4.66.33
तथा चौषधयोऽस्माभिः
सञ्चिता देवशासनात् ।
निर्मथ्यममृतं याभिः
तदानीं नो महद्बलम् ॥
Similarly, following the directive of the Dēvas, I collected all the herbs that was used to churn the Amṛt (out of the ocean). Such is the great strength I used to possess then.
4.66.34
स इदानीमहं वृद्धः
परिहीनपराक्रमः ।
साम्प्रतं कालमस्माकम्
भवान्सर्वगुणान्वितः ॥
But now I am old and my prowess is diminished. At the present time you are the one among us who is endowed with all the virtues and abilities.
4.66.35
तद्विजृम्भस्व विक्रान्तः
प्लवतामुत्तमो ह्यसि ।
त्वद्वीर्यं द्रष्टुकामा हि
सर्वा वानरवाहिनी ॥
O you of great prowess! You are the best among all those who can leap! The entire army of Vānaras like to see your power. Unleash yourself!
4.66.36
उत्तिष्ठ हरिशार्दूल
लङ्घयस्व महार्णवम् ।
परा हि सर्वभूतानाम्
हनुमन्या गतिस्तव ॥
O tiger among Vānaras! Get up! Cross the great ocean! O Hanumān! Your speed is far above that of all the other creatures.
4.66.37
विषण्णा हरयस्सर्वे
हनुमन्किमुपेक्षसे ।
विक्रमस्व महावेग
विष्णुस्त्रीन्विक्रमानिव ॥
All the apes are in despair; how can you, O Hanumān, be indifferent? Launch forth with your terrific speed, as Vishnu did when he took the three strides!
4.66.38
ततः कपीनामृषभेण चोदितः
प्रतीतवेगः पवनात्मजः कपिः ।
प्रहर्षयंस्तां हरिवीरवाहिनीं
चकार रूपं महदात्मनस्तदा ॥
Then, urged by the bull among Vānaras, the son of Vāyu, the Vānara of renowned speed, to the delight of the army of Vānara Veeras assumed a gigantic form of himself.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे षट्षष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty sixth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
