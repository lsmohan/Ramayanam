Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 86
In this short Sarga, Guha narrates to Bharata the conversation he had with Lakshmaṇa during the night when Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Seetā stayed on the banks of the river Gaṅgā on their way to Vana.
2.86.1
आचचक्षेऽथ सद्भावम्
लक्ष्मणस्य महात्मनः ।
भरतायाप्रमेयाय
गुहो गहनगोचरः ॥
Then Guha, the dweller of the forest, narrated the good nature of Mahātma Lakshmaṇa to Bharata of immeasurable virtues.
2.86.2
तं जाग्रतं गुणैर्युक्तम्
शरचापासिधारिणम् ।
भ्रातृगुप्त्यर्थमत्यन्तम्
अहं लक्ष्मणमब्रुवम् ॥
I said to Lakshmaṇa of virtuous qualities, who was awake armed with bow, arrow and sword, keeping unremitting vigil for the protection of his brother:
2.86.3
इयं तात सुखा शय्या
त्वदर्थमुपकल्पिता ।
प्रत्याश्वसिहि शेष्वास्याम्
सुखं राघवनन्दन ॥
My dear son! Here is a comfortable bed ready for you. O delight of the Raghus, sleep comfortably keeping aside all worries.
2.86.4
उचितोऽयं जनस्सर्वो
दुःखानां त्वं सुखोचितः ।
धर्मात्मंस्तस्य गुप्त्यर्थम्
जागरिष्यामहे वयम् ॥
O Dharmātma! You are used to a life of comfort, whereas all these people are used to a life of hardship. We will stay awake and watch over him.
2.86.5
नहि रामात्प्रियतरो
ममास्ति भुवि कश्चन ।
मोत्सुकोऽभूर्ब्रवीम्येतत्
अथ सत्यं तवाग्रतः ॥
There is no one on this earth who is dearer to me than Rāma. Hence you should not have any apprehensions. Trust me, I am being honest.
2.86.6
अस्य प्रसादादाशंसे
लोकेऽस्मिन् सुमहद्यशः ।
धर्मावाप्तिं च विपुलाम्
अर्थकामौ च केवलम् ॥
It is only by his grace that I hope to earn a good name in this world, stick to Dharma, gain prosperity and enjoy the pleasures of life.
2.86.7
सोऽहं प्रियसखं रामम्
शयानं सह सीतया ।
रक्षिष्यामि धनुष्पाणि
सर्वैस्स्वैर्ज्ञातिभिस्सह ॥
I will guard Rāma, such a good friend of mine, who is asleep along with Seetā, with bow in my hand and with the aid of all my kinsmen.
2.86.8
न हि मेऽविदितं किञ्चित्
वनेऽस्मिंश्चरतस्सदा ।
चतुरङ्गं ह्यपि बलम्
प्रसहेम वयं युधि ॥
I move about in this forest all the time, and there is nothing that I am not familiar with here. We can hold even an army equipped with all the four divisions, in a battle.
2.86.9
एवमस्माभिरुक्तेन
लक्ष्मणेन महात्मना ।
अनुनीता वयं सर्वे
धर्ममेवानुपश्यता ॥
Hearing us say this, Mahātma Lakshmaṇa, guided solely by Dharma, implored us saying:
2.86.10
कथं दाशरथौ भूमौ
शयाने सहा सीताया ।
शक्या निद्रा मया लब्धुम्
जीवितं वा सुखानि वा ॥
How can I think of sleep when this son of Daṡaratha lies on the ground with Seetā, much less think of enjoying life or seeking comfort?
2.86.11
यो न देवासुरैस्सर्वैः
शक्यः प्रसहितुं युधि ।
तं पश्य गुह संविष्टम्
तृणेषु सह सीतया ॥
See, Guha, how he, for whom all the Dēvatas and Asuras combined are no match in a battle, lies on this bed of grass along with Seetā!
2.86.12
महता तपसा लब्धो
विविधैश्च परिश्रमैः ।
एको दशरथस्यैष
पुत्रस्सदृशलक्षणः ॥
Daṡaratha got this son of great virtues, by virtue of great Tapa and efforts of every kind.
2.86.13
अस्मिन्प्रव्राजिते राजा
न चिरं वर्तयिष्यति ।
विधवा मेदिनी नूनम्
क्षिप्रमेव भविष्यति ॥
The King will not survive long with him going into exile; the earth will certainly become a widow in no time.
2.86.14
विनद्य सुमहानादम्
श्रमेणोपरताः स्त्रियः ।
निर्घोषो विरतो नूनम्
अद्य राजनिवेशने ॥
The din of the royal palace must surely have subsided by now, with the women's loud clamor suppressed by their own exhaustion.
2.86.15
कौसल्या चैव राजा च
तथैव जननी मम ।
नाशंसे यदि जीवेयुः
सर्वे ते शर्वरीमिमाम् ॥
I doubt very much whether Kousalyā and the King, and my mother likewise, will live through this night.
2.86.16
जीवेदपि च मे माता
शत्रुघ्नस्यान्ववेक्षया ।
दुःखिता या तु कौसल्या
वीरसूर्विनशिष्यति ॥
My mother might survive looking up to Ṡatrughna, but Kousalyā, mother of the heroic son, will perish out of grief!
2.86.17
अतिक्रान्तमतिक्रान्तम्
अनवाप्य मनोरथम् ।
राज्ये राममनिक्षिप्य
पिता मे विनशिष्यति ॥
With his wishes undone one by one, unable to place Rāma on the throne, my father will perish.
2.86.18
सिद्धार्थाः पितरं वृत्तम्
तस्मिन्काले ह्युपस्थिते ।
प्रेतकार्येषु सर्वेषु
संस्करिष्यन्ति भूमिपम् ॥
Blessed are those who will be there when my father passes away, for they would be able to perform the last rites of the king.
2.86.19
रम्यचत्वरसंस्थानाम्
सुविभक्तमहापथाम् ।
हर्म्यप्रासाद संपन्नाम्
सर्वरत्नविभूषिताम् ।
गजाश्वरथसंबाधाम्
तूर्यनादविनादिताम् ।
सर्वकल्याणसंपूर्णाम्
हृष्टपुष्टजनाकुलाम् ।
आरामोद्यानसंपूर्णाम्
समाजोत्सवशालिनीम् ।
सुखिता विचरिष्यन्ति
राजधानीं पितुर्मम ॥
(Blessed are those) who move about happily in the capital city of my father, with its fine houses with well-placed courtyards, its well divided major highways, its mansions and palaces encrusted with every kind of precious stone, which teems with elephants, horses and chariots, resounds with the sounds of trumpets, and is filled with auspiciousness everywhere, thronged by delighted and prosperous people, full of pleasure gardens and parks and festive with community celebrations.
2.86.22
अपिसत्यप्रतिज्ञेन
सार्धं कुशलिना वयं ।
निवृत्ते समये ह्यस्मिन्
सुखिताः प्रविशेमहि ॥
Would we reenter Ayōdhyā happily at the end along with him, after he makes good of his word and remains safe?
2.86.23
परिदेवयमानस्य
तस्यैवं सुमहात्मनः ।
तिष्ठतो राजपुत्रस्य
शर्वरी साऽत्यवर्तत ॥
The night passed, as that prince, the illustrious Mahātma, stood there lamenting thus!
2.86.24
प्रभाते विमले सूर्ये
कारयित्वा जटा उभौ ।
अस्मिन् भागीरथीतीरे
सुखं सन्तारितौ मया ॥
When the sun rose in the clear sky in the morning, they had their locks matted on the very banks of this river Bhāgeerathi. And then they were ferried across by me in all comfort.
2.86.25
जटाधरौ तौ द्रुमचीरवाससौ
महाबलौ कुञ्जरयूथपोपमौ ।
वरेषुचापासिधरौ परन्तपौ
व्यपेक्षमाणौ सह सीतया गतौ ॥
That mighty strong duo, the harriers of the foe, who are like the lead elephants of a herd, proceeded with Seetā looking around vigilantly, wearing matted locks and robes of bark and armed with excellent bows, arrows and swords.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे षडशीतितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighty sixth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
