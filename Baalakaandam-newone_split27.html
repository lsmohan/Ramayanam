Baala Kaanda - Sarga 27
Viṡwāmitra passes on the extremely powerful, solemn and potent Astras to Rāma in this Sarga. Rāma, a young hero, is inheriting and getting connected with the treasured, fabled and magical Astras of antiquity, with lineage tracing back all the way to the creator, Lord Brahma.
Rāmāyaṇa is written to be performed and sung to people, rather than read. This Sarga makes a gripping scene especially in the styles of Yakshagāna and Kūcipūdi, and perhaps in other such powerful forms of storytelling that I may not be aware.
1.27.1
अथ तां रजनीमुष्य
विश्वामित्रो महायशाः ।
प्रहस्य राघवं वाक्यम्
उवाच मधुराक्षरम् ॥
After thus spending the night there, the renowned Viṡwāmitra said these pleasing words to the prince of Raghu dynasty with a smile:
1.27.2
परितुष्टोऽस्मि भद्रं ते
राजपुत्र महायशः ।
प्रीत्या परमया युक्तो
ददाम्यस्त्राणि सर्वशः ॥
I am exceedingly pleased with you, O prince of great renown! May all bode well for you! With infinite pleasure I shall impart the knowledge of all celestial Astras to you!
1.27.3
देवासुरगणान् वापि
सगन्धर्वोरगानपि ।
यैरमित्रान् प्रसह्याजौ
वशीकृत्य जयिष्यसि ।
तानि दिव्यानि भद्रं ते
ददाम्यस्त्राणि सर्वशः ॥
I will give you all the special Astras that would subdue enemies in a fight, even if they have the help of all the Dēvas, Asuras, Gandharvas and Uragas combined!
1.27.4
दण्डचक्रं महद्दिव्यम्
तव दास्यामि राघव ।
धर्मचक्रं ततो वीर
कालचक्रं तथैव च ।
विष्णुचक्रं तथात्युग्रम्
चन्द्रमस्त्रं तथैव च ।
वज्रमस्त्रं नरश्रेष्ठ
शैवं शूलवरं तथा ।
अस्त्रं ब्रह्मशिरश्चैव
ऐषीकमपि राघव ।
ददामि ते महाबाहो
ब्राह्ममस्त्रमनुत्तमम् ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Here I give you the great exalted Daṇḍa Cakra. O Veera! Here I give the Dharma Cakra and Kāla Cakra. O best among men! Here I give you the Astras Vishṇu Cakra, the enraging Indrāstra, the Vajrāstra, and the best Javelin of Ṡiva, the Brahmaṡira Astra and the Isheekāstra! O you of mighty arm! Here I give you the unparalleled Brahmāstra!
Boys, especially between ages three and ten, would love this Ṡlōka. The mighty and powerful always catch their imagination. It is the same imagination that the modern toy industry and video game industry depend upon.
It is also interesting that these very names are borrowed by the fireworks industry of Ṡivakāsi, Tamil Nadu for naming various types of fireworks.
1.27.7
गदे द्वे चैव काकुत्स्थ
मोदकी शिखरी उभे ।
प्रदीप्ते नरशार्दूल
प्रयच्छामि नृपात्मज ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage, O tiger among men! Here I give you two blazing clubs named Mōdaki and Ṡikhari.
1.27.8
धर्मपाशमहं राम
कालपाशं तथैव च ।
पाशं वारुणमस्त्रं च
ददाम्यहमनुत्तमम् ॥
O Rāma, here I give you Dhārmapāsa, Kālapāsa, and the exceptional Vārunastra Pāsam!
1.27.9
अशनी द्वे प्रयच्छामि
शुष्कार्द्रे रघुनन्दन ।
ददामि चास्त्रं पैनाकम्
अस्त्रं नारायणं तथा ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Here I give two thunderbolts, the dry one and the wet one! And I give you Painākastra and Nārāyanāstra.
1.27.10
आग्नेयमस्त्रं दयितम्
शिखरं नाम नामतः ।
वायव्यं प्रथनं नाम
ददामि च तवानघ ॥
O unaffiliated one! Here I give an Astra of Agni called Ṡikhara and an Astra of Vāyu called Prathana.
Agni is the God of Fire. Vāyu is the God of Wind.
1.27.11
अस्त्रं हयशिरो नाम
क्रौञ्चमस्त्रं तथैव च ।
शक्तिद्वयं च काकुत्स्थ
ददामि तव राघव ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty and Kākutstha lineage! Here I give you two Astras called Hayasira and Krounca and also two Ṡaktis.
1.27.12
कङ्कालं मुसलं घोरम्
कापालमथ कङ्कणम् ।
धारयन्त्यसुरा यानि
ददाम्येतानि सर्वशः ॥
Here I give you all the fearsome pounding stones like Kaṅkāla, Kāpāla and Kaṅkaṇa that are used by Asuras.
1.27.13
वैद्याधरं महास्त्रं च
नन्दनं नाम नामतः ।
असिरत्नं महाबाहो
ददामि नृवरात्मज ॥
O prince of mighty arm! Here I give a mighty Astra called Vaidyādhara and a special sword called Nandana.
1.27.14
गान्धर्वमस्त्रं दयितम्
मानवं नाम नामतः ।
प्रस्वापनप्रशमने
दद्मि सौरं च राघव ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Here I give an Astra called Gāndharva and a much wanted Astra called Mānava, one capable of inducing sleep and the other capable of waking up from sleep and also an Astra called Soura.
1.27.15
दर्पणं शोषणं चैव
सन्तापनविलापने ।
मदनं चैव दुर्द्धर्षम्
कन्दर्पदयितं तथा ।
पैशाचमस्त्रं दयितम्
मोहनं नाम नामतः ।
प्रतीच्छ नरशार्दूल
राजपुत्र महायशः ॥
O renowned prince! O tiger among men! Here I give the Astras called Darpaṇa, Sōshaṇa, Santāpana, Vilāpana and the invincible Madana, that is dear to Kandarpa and the Mōhana, that is dear to the Piṡācas!
1.27.17
तामसं नरशार्दूल
सौमनं च महाबल ।
संवर्धं चैव दुर्धर्षम्
मौसलं च नृपात्मज ।
सत्यमस्त्रं महाबाहो
तथा मायाधरं परम् ।
घोरं तेजःप्रभं नाम
त्वष्टुरस्त्रं सुदामनम् ।
दारुणं च भगस्यापि
शीतेषुमथ मानवम् ॥
O tiger among men! O prince of mighty arm and immense strength! Here I give you Tāmasa, Soumana, invincible Samvardha, Mousala, Satyāstra, the ultimate Māyādhara, the fearsome Tējaprabha, Sudāmana, the Astra of Tvashṭu Ṡitēshu, the terrible Astra of Bhaga and Mānavāstra.
1.27.20
एतान् राम महाबाहो
कामरूपान् महाबलान् ।
गृहाण परमोदारान्
क्षिप्रमेव नृपात्मज ॥
O Rāma, O prince of mighty arm! Receive right away these mighty strong, exalted Astras that can take multiple forms at will.
1.27.21
स्थितस्तु प्राङ्मुखो भूत्वा
शुचिर्मुनिवरस्तदा ।
ददौ रामाय सुप्रीतो
मन्त्रग्राममनुत्तमम् ॥
The eminent Muni purified himself and turned east and gave the entire collection of unequaled Astras to Rāma.
1.27.22
सर्वसङ्ग्रहणं येषाम्
दैवतैरपि दुर्लभम् ।
तान्यस्त्राणि तदा विप्रो
राघवाय न्यवेदयत् ॥
Thus the Brāhmaṇa gave those Astras which in their entirety, are difficult to be obtained even by Dēvas, to the prince of Raghu dynasty.
1.27.23
जपतस्तु मुनेस्तस्य
विश्वामित्रस्य धीमतः ।
उपतस्थुर्महार्हाणि
सर्वाण्यस्त्राणि राघवम् ॥
As Viṡwāmitra, that sagacious Muni, called upon them, all the priceless Astras joined the prince of Raghu dynasty.
1.27.24
ऊचुश्च मुदिताः सर्वे
रामं प्राञ्जलयस्तदा ।
इमे स्म परमोदाराः
किङ्करास्तव राघव ॥
All the revered and solemn Astras said with folded hands to Rāma: We are happy to be at your service hereafter!
1.27.25
प्रतिगृह्य च काकुत्स्थः
समालभ्य च पाणिना ।
मानसा मे भविष्यध्वम्
इति तानभ्यचोदयत् ॥
The prince of Kākutstha lineage received them by symbolically touching them, and ordered them to reside in his mind.
1.27.26
ततः प्रीतमना रामो
विश्वामित्रं महामुनिम् ।
अभिवाद्य महातेजा
गमनायोपचक्रमे ॥
The radiant and pleased Rāma, then paid thankful respects to the eminent Muni Viṡwāmitra and became ready to proceed with the journey.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे सप्तविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty seventh Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
