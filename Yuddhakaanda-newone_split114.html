Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 114
n this Sarga, Hanumān comes back to Rāma and tells him that Seetā wants to see him. Rāma falls in a pensive mood, heaving deep sighs on hearing it, and asks Vibheeshaṇa to bring her to him. Vibheeshaṇa informs Seetā through his womenfolk to become ready. Then he brings her to Rāma in a palanquin.
Rāma is beset simultaneously by joy, misery and anger on hearing that she has arrived. He asks Vibheeshaṇa to bring her to his presence. Ushers try to clear the crowds of the Vānara, Ṛksha and Rākshasa soldiers. Rāma gets annoyed and tells Vibheeshaṇa to stop it. He says that it is not inappropriate for women to be seen in public at times of calamities or crises or wars and in events like Swayamvaras, Yajñas and weddings, and that she should come walking, leaving aside the palanquin.
Seetā proceeds towards her husband, with all her limbs contracting under the weight of modesty. But once she gets a glance of her husband's face, that shone like the orb of rising moon, which she has not seen in a long while, she sheds off all her misery.
6.114.1
तमुवाच महाप्राज्ञः
सोऽभिवाद्य प्लवङ्गमः ।
रामं कमलपत्राक्षम्
वरं सर्वधनुष्मताम् ॥
The well-informed Vānaras paid obeisance to Rāma of lotus petal like eyes, the best of all the bowmen, and said to him:
6.114.2
यन्निमित्तोऽयमारम्भः
कर्मणां च फलोदयः ।
तां देवीं शोकसन्तप्ताम्
मैथिलीं द्रष्टुमर्हसि ॥
You should see Maithili, the lady who has been scorched by grief, for whose sake this enterprise was launched and in seeing whom all that was done bears fruit.
6.114.3
सा हि शोकसमाविष्टा
बाष्पपर्याकुलेक्षणा ।
मैथिली विजयं श्रुत्वा
तव हर्षमुपागमत् ॥
She, Maithili, who has been submerged in grief, and whose eyes were turbid with tears, was filled with joy on hearing of your victory.
6.114.4
पूर्वकात्प्रत्ययाच्चाहम्
उक्तो विश्वस्तया तया ।
भर्तारं द्रष्टुमिच्छामि
कृतार्थं सहलक्ष्मणम् ॥
Because of the trust she developed in me in the past, she, in full confidence, conveys her message through me - ‘I wish to see my husband, who has won his object, as well as Lakshmaṇa!'
6.114.5
एवमुक्तो हनुमता
रामो धर्मभृतां वरः ।
अगच्छत् सहसा ध्यानम्
ईषद्बाष्पपरिप्लुतः ॥
Thus told by Hanumān, Rāma, the foremost of the patrons of Dharma, fell immediately into a pensive mood, his eyes becoming slightly teary.
6.114.6
दीर्घमुष्णं विनिश्वस्य
मेदिनीमवलोकयन् ।
उवाच मेघसङ्काशम्
विभीषणमुपस्थितम् ॥
Heaving a long and hot sigh, and fixing his gaze on the ground, he said to Vibheeshaṇa, who was standing nearby, huge like a cloud:
6.114.7
दिव्याङ्गरागां वैदेहीम्
दिव्याभरणभूषिताम् ।
इह सीतां शिरस्स्नाताम्
उपस्थापय मा चिरम् ॥
Please bring Seetā here without delay, after she gets a head-bath, and is decorated with splendid ornaments and smeared with divine creams and lotions.
6.114.8
एवमुक्तस्तु रामेण
त्वरमाणो विभीषणः ।
प्रविश्यान्तःपुरं सीताम्
स्वाभिः स्त्रीभिरचोदयत् ॥
Thus told by Rāma, Vibheeshaṇa hastened to the inner quarters and informed Seetā, through his own womenfolk.
6.114.9
ततः सीतां महाभागां
दृष्ट्वोवाच विभीषणः ।
मुर्ध्नि बद्धाञ्जलिः श्रीमान्
विनीतो राक्षसेश्वरः ॥
Then, the blessed Vibheeshaṇa, lord of Rākshasas, looked at the greatly blessed Seetā raising his joined palms to his forehead, and said, with all deference:
6.114.10
दिव्याङ्गरागा वैदेहि
दिव्याभरणभूषिता ।
यानमारोह भद्रं ते
भर्ता त्वां द्रष्टुमिच्छति ॥
O Vaidēhee, your husband wishes to see you, please put on splendid ornaments and apply splendid lotions and creams on yourself and get into the vehicle; may all bode well for you!
6.114.11
एवमुक्ता तु वैदेही
प्रत्युवाच विभीषणम् ।
अस्नाता द्रष्टुमिच्छामि
भर्तारं राक्षसेश्वर ॥
Thus told, Vaidēhee replied to Vibheeshaṇa, 'I would like to see my husband without having a bath, O lord of Rākshasas!'
6.114.12
तस्यास्तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
प्रत्युवाच विभीषणः ।
यदाह राजा भर्ता ते
तत्तथा कर्तुमर्हसि ॥
Hearing those words of her's, Vibheeshaṇa responded, 'It behooves you to do what the king, your husband, bids you!'
6.114.13
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
मैथिली पतिदेवता ।
भर्तृभक्तिव्रता साध्वी
तथेति प्रत्यभाषत ॥
Hearing those words of his, Maithili, the good lady replied, 'so shall it be!' for her husband was her god and devotion to him was her creed.
6.114.14
ततः सीतां शिरस्स्नाताम्
संयुक्तां प्रतिकर्मणा ।
महार्हाभरणोपेताम्
महार्हाम्बरधारिणीम् ।
आरोप्य शिबिकां दीप्ताम्
परार्घ्याम्बरसंवृताम् ।
राक्षसैर्बहुभिर्गुप्ताम्
आजहार विभीषणः ॥
Then Vibheeshaṇa brought Seetā, who had taken head-bath, and was decorated well all over with priceless ornaments and wearing priceless garments, on a blazing palanquin that was draped with rich hangings and was guarded by many Rākshasas.
6.114.16
सोऽभिगम्य महात्मानम्
ज्ञात्वाऽपि ध्यानमास्थितम् ।
प्रणतश्च प्रहृष्टश्च
प्राप्तं सीतां न्यवेदयत् ॥
He then went to the Mahātma who remained quiet in a pensive mood even after noticing (Seetā coming), and bowing, reported with delight that Seetā had arrived.
6.114.17
तामागतामुपश्रुत्य
रक्षोगृहचिरोषिताम् ।
हर्षो दैन्यं च रोषश्च
त्रयं राघवमाविशत् ॥
When he heard that she, who had stayed at the house of the Rākshasa for a long time, had come, Rāghava was beset simultaneously with joy, misery and anger.
6.114.18
ततः पार्श्वगतं दृष्ट्वा
सविमर्शं विचारयन् ।
विभीषणमिदं वाक्यम्
अहृष्टः राघवोऽब्रवीत् ॥
Having carefully brooded on what was troubling him, Rāghava, without any happiness evident in his voice, told Vibheeshaṇa, who stood by his side:
6.114.19
राक्षसाधिपते सौम्य
नित्यं मद्विजये रत ।
वैदेही सन्निकर्षं मे
शीघ्रं समुपगच्छतु ॥
My dear lord of Rākshasas, who have ever rejoiced in my victories! May Vaidēhee come to my presence without delay!
6.114.20
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राघवस्य विभीषणः ।
तूर्णमुत्सारणं तत्र
कारयामास धर्मवित् ॥
On hearing those words of Rāghava, Vibheeshaṇa who knew Dharma, tried to clear the crowds.
6.114.21
कञ्चुकोष्णीषिणस्तत्र
वेत्रजर्जरपाणयः ।
उत्सारयन्तस्तान् योधान्
समन्तात् परिचक्रमुः ॥
Ushers dressed in long tunics and wearing turbans, their hands calloused wielding the cane, went around pushing away the soldiers.
6.114.22
ऋक्षाणां वानराणां च
राक्षसानां च सर्वशः ।
वृन्दान्युत्सार्यमाणानि
दूरमुत्तस्थुरन्ततः ॥
The crowds of bears, Vānaras and Rākshasas that were around everywhere moved afar, pushed away by them.
6.114.23
तेषामुत्सार्यमाणानाम्
निःस्वनः महानभूत् ।
वायुनोद्धूयमानस्य
सागरस्येव निःस्वनः ॥
There arose a huge din, loud as the roar of an ocean buffeted by the wind, as they were thus being pushed away.
6.114.24
उत्सार्यमाणांस्तान् दृष्ट्वा
समन्ताज्जातसम्भ्रमान् ।
दाक्षिण्यात्तदमर्षाच्च
वारयामास राघवः ॥
On seeing them thus being pushed away which upset them everywhere, and annoyed, Rāghava stopped it, empathizing with them.
6.114.25
संरब्धश्चाब्रवीद्रामः
चक्षुषा प्रदहन्निव ।
विभीषणं महाप्राज्ञम्
सोपालम्भमिदं वचः ॥
Looking at the exceedingly wise Vibheeshaṇa as if he would burn him with his eyes, Rāma angrily reproached him, saying these words:
6.114.26
किमर्थं मामनादृत्य
क्लिश्यतेऽयं त्वया जनः ।
निवर्तयैनमुद्योगम्
जनोऽयं स्वजनो मम ॥
Why do you, ignoring me, harass these people? Stop it at once, these are like my own people!
6.114.27
न गृहाणि न वस्त्राणि
न प्राकारास्तिरस्क्रियाः ।
नेदृशा राजसत्कारा
वृत्तमावरणं स्त्रियाः ॥
Neither houses, nor clothes, nor high walls, nor veils, nor the patronage of kings, but her own conduct is the one that guards a woman.
6.114.28
व्यसनेषु न कृच्छ्रेषु
न युद्धेषु स्वयंवरे ।
न क्रतौ न विवाहे च
दर्शनं दूष्यते स्त्रियाः ॥
It is not considered inappropriate for women to be seen in public at (times of) calamities or crises or wars and in (events like) Swayamvaras, Yajñas and weddings.
6.114.29
सैषा विपद्गता चैव
कृच्छ्रे च महति स्थिता ।
दर्शने नास्ति दोषोऽस्या
मत्समीपे विशेषतः ॥
She has gone through many troubles and has been in great distress; it is not inappropriate for one to see her, especially in my presence.
6.114.30
तदानय समीपं मे
शीघ्रमेनां विभीषण ।
सीता पश्यतु मामेषा
सुहृद्गणवृतं स्थितम् ॥
Therefore, leaving the palanquin, let her come here on foot, and may the Vana-dwellers see Vaidēhee in my presence.
6.114.31
एवमुक्तस्तु रामेण
सविमर्शो विभीषणः ।
रामस्योपानयत् सीताम्
सन्निकर्षं विनीतवत् ॥
Having been thus told by Rāma, Vibheeshaṇa thoughtfully and obediently brought Seetā to the presence of Rāma.
6.114.32
ततो लक्ष्मणसुग्रीवौ
हनुमांश्च प्लवङ्गमः ।
निशम्य वाक्यं रामस्य
बभूवुर्व्यथिता भृशम् ॥
Lakshmaṇa, Sugreeva and the Vānara Hanumān were greatly pained, hearing those words of Rāma.
6.114.33
कलत्रनिरपेक्षैश्च
इङ्गितैरस्य दारुणैः ।
अप्रीतमिव सीतायाम्
तर्कयन्ति स्म राघवम् ॥
Based on his cold attitude towards his wife and seeing the sternness in his gestures, they concluded that Rāghava was unhappy with Seetā.
6.114.34
लज्जया त्ववलीयन्ती
स्वेषु गात्रेषु मैथिली ।
विभीषणेनानुगता
भर्तारं साऽभ्यवर्तत ॥
With all her limbs contracting under the weight of modesty, she proceeded towards her husband, led by Vibheeshaṇa.
6.114.35
विस्मयाच्च प्रहर्षाच्च
स्नेहाच्च पतिदेवता ।
उदैक्षत मुखं भर्तुः
सौम्यं सोम्यतरानना ॥
In amazement and with delight and love, she, to whom her husband was god, glanced the pleasant face of her husband, her own face being even more pleasant.
6.114.36
अथ समपनुदन्मनःक्लमं सा
सुचिरमदृष्टमुदीक्ष्य वै प्रियस्य ।
वदनमुदितपूर्णचन्द्रकान्तं
विमलशशाङ्कनिभानना तदानीम् ॥
At that moment, she, whose face glowed like the full moon on a clear night, shed all her weariness in her heart, seeing the face of her beloved that shone like the orb of the rising moon, which she had not seen in a long while.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे चतुर्दशोत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and fourteenth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
