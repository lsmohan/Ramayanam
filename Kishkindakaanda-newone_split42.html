Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 42
In this Sarga, Sugreeva sends Arcishmān and his brothers known as Māreecis and Arcishmālyas along with two hundred thousand Vānaras under the leadership of Sushēṇa in the westerly direction to search for Seetā and Rāvaṇa.
Sugreeva gives them a detailed description of all the places that need to be searched in that direction, starting from Sourāshṭra, Bāhlika, Candracitra regions, all the way to the Mēru mountain and beyond, where the sun sets.
Sugreeva commands them to return and report to him after searching for Seetā. He tells them to abide by the command of Sushēṇa and also think of ways that better serve the purposes based on the circumstances as they emerge.
4.42.1
अथ प्रस्थाप्य सुग्रीवः
तान्हरीन्दक्षिणां दिशम् ।
अब्रवीन्मेघसङ्काशम्
सुषेणं नाम वानरम्  ॥
Having dispatched those Vānaras to the South, Sugreeva spoke to Sushēṇa of fierce valor who was like a huge cloud.
4.42.2
तारायाः पितरं राजा
श्वशुरं भीमविक्रमम् ।
अब्रवीत्प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यम्
अभिगम्य प्रणम्य च ॥
The king then approached and saluted Tārā's father, his father-in-law and spoke with his palms joined in reverence.
4.42.3
महर्षिपुत्रं मारीचम्
अर्चिष्मन्तं महाकपिम् ।
वृतं कपिवरैश्शूरैः
महेन्द्रसदृशद्युतिम् ।
बुद्धिविक्रमसम्पन्नम्
वैनतेयसमद्युतिम् ।
मरीचिपुत्रान्मारीचान्
अर्चिर्माल्यान्महाबलान् ।
ऋषिपुत्रांश्चतांत्सर्वान्
प्रतीचीमादिशद्दिशम् ॥
He sent to the West Arcishmān, the son of Maharshi Mareeci, whose splendor was like that of Mahēndra, whose speed equaled that of Garuḍa and who was gifted richly with prowess and wisdom along with the best of Vānaras, great Ṡūras, who surrounded him.
He also sent to the West all the other mighty strong sons of Maharshi Mareeci known as Māreecis and Arcishmālyas.
4.42.5
द्वाभ्यां शतसहस्राभ्याम्
कपीनां कपिसत्तमाः ।
सुषेणप्रमुखा यूयम्
वैदेहीं परिमार्गथ  ॥
O best of Vānaras! Accompanied by two hundred thousand Vānaras, you should search for Vaidēhee under the leadership of Sushēṇa.
4.42.6
सुराष्ट्रान्सहबाह्लीकान्
चन्द्रचित्रांस्तथैव च ।
स्फीताञ्जनपदान्रम्यान्
विपुलानि पुराणि च ।
पुन्नागगहनं कुक्षिम्
वकुलोद्दालकाकुलम् ।
तथा केतकषण्डांश्च
मार्गध्वं हरिपुंगवाः  ॥
O bulls among Vānaras! Search the regions of Sourāshṭra, Bāhlika, Candracitra, including the pleasant and prosperous countryside and the huge cities, the groves of Punnāga trees, and the central region abounding in Vakula and Uddālaka trees, and Kētaka groves.
4.42.8
प्रत्यक्स्रोतोवहाश्चैव
नद्यश्शीतजलाश्शिवाः ।
तापसानामरण्यानि
कान्तारगिरयश्च ये ।
तत्रस्थलींमरुप्राया
अत्युच्चशिरसश्शिला: ।
गिरिजालावृतां दुर्गाम्
मार्गित्वा पश्चिमां दिशम् ।
ततः पश्चिममागम्य
समुद्रं द्रष्टुमर्हथ ।
तिमिनक्राकुलजलम्
गत्वा द्रक्ष्यथ वानराः ॥
Search along the auspicious streams of cool waters flowing west, the woods where Tapa is done, forests, mountains and the barren lands, the extremely tall rocks and the undulating mountains that are difficult to access, before you go further west to see the Ocean, that is full of whales and crocodiles.
4.42.11
ततः केतकषण्डेषु
तमालगहनेषु च ।
कपयो विहरिष्यन्ति
नारिकेलवनेषु च ॥
There the Vānaras will be pleased to ramble in Kētaka groves, Tamāla thickets and coconut Vanas.
4.42.12
तत्र सीतां च मार्गध्वम्
निलयं रावणस्य च ।
वेलातलनिविष्टेषु
पर्वतेषु वनेषु च ।
मुरचीपत्तनं चैव
रम्यं चैव जटापुरम् ।
अवन्तीमङ्गलोपां च
तथा चालक्षितं वनम् ।
राष्ट्राणि च विशालानि
पत्तनानि ततस्ततः ॥
You should look there for Seetā and also for the abode of Rāvaṇa, in the mountains and forests that are on the shores of the ocean, in the city of Muraci, in the beautiful city of Jaṭa, in the cities of Avanti and Aṅgalōpa, in the Vana of Alakshita, and also in the countryside and in the big cities around there.
4.42.15
सिन्धुसागरयोश्चैव
सङ्गमे तत्र पर्वतः ।
महान् सोमगिरिर्नाम
शतशृङ्गो महाद्रुमः ॥
At the confluence of the river Sindhu and the ocean is the great mountain known as Sōmagiri with a hundred peaks and great trees (growing on it).
4.42.16
तत्र प्रस्थेषु रम्येषु
सिंहाः पक्षगमाः स्थिताः ।
तिमिमत्स्यगजांश्चैव
नीडान्यारोपयन्ति ते ॥
On its pleasant flanks are to be found lions with wings, which carry off to their nests whales, fish and elephants.
4.42.17
तानि नीडानि सिंहानाम्
गिरिशृङ्गगताश्च ये ।
दृप्तास्तृप्ताश्च मातङ्गाः
तोयदस्वननिस्वनाः ।
विचरन्ति विशालेऽस्मिन्
तोयपूर्णे समन्ततः ॥
And by those wide nests of the lions, which are surrounded by waters, roam the proud and contented tuskers trumpeting loudly like the rumble of clouds.
4.42.18
तत्र शृङ्गं दिवस्पर्शम्
काञ्चनं चित्रपादपम् ।
सर्वमाशु विचेतव्यम्
कपिभिः कामरूपिभिः ॥
The Vānaras who can take any form at will should search everywhere on the tall, golden peak that seems to touch the sky, on which grow amazing trees.
4.42.19
कोटिं तत्र समुद्रे तु
काञ्चनीं शतयोजनाम् ।
दुर्दर्षां पारियात्रस्य
गत्वा द्रक्ष्यथ वानराः ॥
O Vānaras! Reaching there, you will see the hundred Yōjana tall golden crest of the Pāriyātra mountain in the ocean whose dazzle makes it difficult to behold.
4.42.20
कोट्यस्तत्र चतुर्विंशत्
गन्धर्वाणां तरस्विनाम् ।
वसन्त्यग्निनिकाशानाम्
घोराणां कामरूपिणाम् ॥
There live on it twenty four crores of terrifying, mighty strong Gandharvas, who blaze like fire and are capable of taking any form at will.
4.42.21
पावकार्चिः प्रतीकाशाः
समवेतास्समन्ततः ।
नात्यासादयितव्यास्ते
वानरैर्भीमविक्रमैः ॥
O Vānaras! As fierce of valor as you are, you should not attempt to approach them, who, with their flame-like luster, congregate everywhere in that place.
4.42.22
नादेयं च फलं तस्मात्
देशात्किञ्चित्प्लवङ्गमैः ।
दुरासदा हि ते वीराः
सत्त्ववन्तो महाबलाः ।
फलमूलानि ते तत्र
रक्षन्ते भीमविक्रमाः ॥
The Vānaras should not take any fruit whatsoever from that region. The roots and fruits there are guarded by those gritty, mighty strong, unassailable Veeras of fierce prowess.
4.42.24
तत्र यत्नश्च कर्तव्यो
मार्गितव्या च जानकी ।
न हि तेभ्यो भयं किञ्चित्
कपित्वमनुवर्तताम् ॥
You should make every effort to search for Janaki there. You have nothing to fear from them if you stick to your monkey behavior.
4.42.25
तत्र वैढूर्यवर्णाभो
वज्रसंस्थानसंस्थितः ।
नानाद्रुमलताकीर्णो
वज्रो नाम महागिरिः ।
श्रीमान्समुदितस्तत्र
योजनानां शतं समम् ।
गुहास्तत्र विचेतव्याः
प्रयत्नेन प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
Beyond lies the great mountain known as Vajra, of the glitter of lazulite, and of an adamantine hardness, overgrown with every kind of tree and creeper.
Vānaras should make every effort to search that rich mountain which is exactly one hundred Yōjanas high, including its caves.
4.42.27
चतुर्भागे समुद्रस्य
चक्रवान्नाम पर्वतः ।
तत्र चक्रं सहस्रारम्
निर्मितं विश्वकर्मणा ।
तत्र पञ्चजनं हत्वा
हयग्रीवं च दानवम् ।
आजहार ततश्चक्रम्
शङ्खं च पुरुषोत्तमः ॥
A full one fourth of the ocean is occupied by a mountain called Cakravān, where Viṡwakarma built the thousand-toothed Cakra (Discuss).
This is where the foremost of the spirited beings (Vishṇu) killed the Dānavas Pañcajana and Hayagreeva, and took away the Cakra and Ṡankha from them.
4.42.29
तस्य सानुषु रम्येषु
विशालासु गुहासु च ।
रावणस्सह वैदेह्या
मार्गितव्यस्ततस्ततः ॥
Vaidēhee and Rāvaṇa should be searched for on its beautiful flanks and in its huge caves and areas around them.
4.42.30
योजनानि चतुष्षष्टिः
वराहो नाम पर्वतः ।
सुवर्णशृङ्गस्सुमहान्
अगाधे वरुणालये ॥
Then there is a huge mountain with golden peaks known as Varāha that stretches sixty four Yōjanas into the deep ocean.
4.42.31
तत्र प्राग्ज्योतिषं नाम
जातरूपमयं पुरम् ।
यस्मिन्वसति दुष्टात्मा
नरको नाम दानवः ॥
And then there is the city, all of gold, known as Prāgjyōtisha in which lives the evil-minded Asura known as Naraka.
4.42.32
तस्य सानुषु रम्येषु
विशालासु गुहासु च ।
रावणस्सह वैदेह्या
मार्गितव्यस्ततस्ततः ॥
Vaidēhee and Rāvaṇa should be searched for on its beautiful flanks and in its huge caves and areas around them.
4.42.33
तमतिक्रम्य शैलेन्द्रम्
काञ्चनान्तरनिर्दरम् ।
पर्वतस्सर्वसौवर्णो
धाराप्रस्रवणायुतः ॥
Beyond that king of mountains whose caves are all of gold, lies another mountain which is entirely of gold, from which flow many cascades and streams.
4.42.34
तं गजाश्च वराहाश्च
सिंह व्याघ्राश्च सर्वतः ।
अभिगर्जन्ति सततम्
तेन शब्देन दर्पिताः ॥
Elephants, boars, lions and tigers roar around there all the time, taking pride in the sounds they make.
4.42.35
यस्मिन्हरिहयः श्रीमान्
महेन्द्रः पाकशासनः ।
अभिषिक्तः सुरै राजा
मेघवान्नाम पर्वतः ॥
That mountain, known as Mēghavān, is where the blessed Mahēndra who slew Pāka and rode on green horses was consecrated by the Suras as the king.
4.42.36
तमतिक्रम्य शैलेन्द्रम्
महेन्द्रपरिपालितम् ।
षष्टि र्गिरिसहस्राणि
काञ्चनानि गमिष्यथ ।
तरुणादित्यवर्णानि
भ्राजमानानि सर्वतः ।
जातरूपमयैर्वृक्षै
शोभितानि सुपुष्पितैः ॥
Passing beyond that mountain protected by Mahēndra, you will come to a sixty thousand golden hills that shine like the rising sun from every angle, adorned by trees in full flower, glistening like gold.
4.42.38
तेषां मध्ये स्थितो राजा
मेरुरुत्तमपर्वतः ।
आदित्येन प्रसन्नेन
शैलो दत्तवरः पुरा ॥
Right in their midst stands the king of all those mountains, the great mountain Mēru, which was blessed with a boon by the sun god.
4.42.39
तेनैवमुक्तश्शैलेन्द्रः
सर्व एव त्वदाश्रयाः ।
मत्प्रसादाद्भविष्यन्ति
दिवा रात्रौ च काञ्चनाः ।
त्वयि ये चापि वत्स्यन्ति
देवगन्धर्वदानवाः ।
ते भविष्यन्ति रक्ताश्च
प्रभया काञ्चनप्रभाः ॥
That lord of the mountains was told by him: “I grant that everything that lives on you will shine like gold, day and night. The Dēvas, Gandharvas and Dānavas that live on you will shine with a golden glow and with a red shine”.
4.42.41
विश्वेदेवाश्च वसवः
मरुतश्च दिवौकसः ।
आगम्य पश्चिमां सन्ध्याम्
मेरुमुत्तमपर्वतम् ।
आदित्यमुपतिष्ठन्ति
तैश्च सूर्योऽभिपूजितः ।
अदृश्यस्सर्वभूतानाम्
अस्तं गच्छतिपर्वतम् ॥
The Viṡvēdēvas, the Vasus, the Maruts, and other dwellers of the heavens come to Mēru, the best of mountains, at the time of western (evening) twilight and worship the sun. The sun, duly worshiped by them, proceeds to the setting mountain, going out of sight of all creatures.
4.42.43
योजनानां सहस्राणि
दश तानि दिवाकरः ।
मुहूर्तार्धेन तं शीघ्रम्
अभियाति शिलोच्चयम् ॥
The sun swiftly traverses the distance of ten thousand Yōjanas in half a Muhūrta and reaches the mountain.
4.42.44
शृङ्गे तस्य महद्दिव्यम्
भवनं सूर्यसन्निभम् ।
प्रासादगणसम्बाधम्
विहितं विश्वकर्मणा ॥
There is a huge divine abode that is brilliant like the sun on the top of that mountain built by Viṡwakarma, consisting of numerous multistoried mansions.
4.42.45
शोभितं तरुभिश्चित्रैः
नानापक्षिसमाकुलैः ।
निकेतं पाशहस्तस्य
वरुणस्य महात्मनः ॥
Covered with amazing trees and frequented by many kinds of birds, it is the home of the mighty Varuṇa, the one armed with a noose.
4.42.46
अन्तरा मेरुमस्तं च
तालो दशशिरा महान् ।
जातरूपमयश्श्रीमान्
भ्राजते चित्रवेदिकः ॥
Between Mēru and this mountain of the setting sun is the dazzling huge ten-branched palm tree, all of gold, with a splendid platform around it.
4.42.47
तेषु सर्वेषु दुर्गेषु
सरस्सु च सरित्सु च ।
रावणस्सह वैदेह्या
मार्गितव्यस्ततस्ततः ॥
You should search for Rāvaṇa and Vaidēhee in every one of those fortresses, and on the lakes and rivers around there.
4.42.48
यत्र तिष्ठति धर्मज्ञः
तपसा स्वेन भावितः ।
मेरुसावर्णिरित्येव
ख्यातो वै ब्रह्मणा समः ॥
On that (mountain) sits the Dharmajña known widely as Mērusāvarṇa, an equal of Brahma, who is purified by his own Tapa.
4.42.49
प्रष्टव्यो मेरुसावर्णिः
महर्षिः सूर्यसन्निभः ।
प्रणम्य शिरसा भूमौ
प्रवृत्तिं मैथिलीं प्रति ॥
You should bow to him with your head touching the ground and ask that Maharshi Mērusāvarṇa, who is like the sun, for information about Maithili.
4.42.50
एतावज्जीवलोकस्य
भास्करो रजनीक्षये ।
कृत्वा वितिमिरं सर्वम्
अस्तं गच्छति पर्वतम् ॥
The sun, after the night recedes, drives away the darkness everywhere for the creatures, before proceeding to the setting mountain.
4.42.51
एतावद्वानरैश्शक्यम्
गन्तुं वानरपुङ्गवाः ।
अभास्करममर्यादम्
न जानीमस्ततः परम् ॥
O bulls among Vānaras! That is how far Vānaras can go. We do not know what lies beyond, where there is no sun and no land marks.
4.42.52
अवगम्य तु वैदेहीम्
निलयं रावणस्य च ।
अस्तं पर्वतमासाद्य
पूर्णे मासे निवर्तत ॥
You should return before the end of the month, reaching up to the mountain of the setting sun and finding out where Vaidēhee is, and where Rāvaṇa’s abode is.
4.42.53
ऊर्ध्वं मासान्न वस्तव्यम्
वसन्वध्यो भवेन्मम ।
सहैव शूरो युष्माभिः
श्वशुरो मे गमिष्यति ॥
You should not stay any more than a month lest your life deserves to be taken by me. My father-in-law, a Ṡūra, will go along with you.
4.42.54
श्रोतव्यं सर्वमेतस्य
भवद्भिर्दिष्टकारिभिः ।
गुरुरेष महाबाहुः
श्वशुरो मे महाबलः ॥
You, who are naturally good at following orders, should heed everything that he says. My father-in-law, of mighty arm and immense strength, is my revered senior.
4.42.55
भवन्तश्चापि विक्रान्ताः
प्रमाणं सर्व एव हि ।
प्रमाणमेनं संस्थाप्य
पश्यध्वं पश्चिमां दिशम् ॥
All of you, of great prowess, are quite illustrious in your own way; yet, take him to be your ultimate guide and explore the western quarter.
4.42.56
दृष्टायां तु नरेन्द्रस्य
पत्न्याममिततेजसः ।
कृतकृत्या भविष्यामः
कृतस्य प्रतिकर्मणा ॥
It is only when we have found the spouse of the lord of men of limitless power, that we would have done our duty, by requiting the favor.
4.42.57
अतोऽन्यदपि यत्कार्यम्
कार्यस्यास्य हितं भवेत् ।
सम्प्रधार्य भवद्भिश्च
देशकालार्थसंहितम् ॥
You should find out and do whatever else could serve the purpose better depending on the time, place and circumstance.
4.42.58
ततः सुषेणप्रमुखाः प्लवङ्गमाः
सुग्रीव वाक्यं निपुणं निशम्य ।
आमन्त्य्र सर्वे प्लवगाधिपं ते
जग्मुर्दिशं तां वरुणाभिगुप्ताम् ॥
Then Sushēṇa and the other Vānaras, having listened to Sugreeva’s words sincerely, took leave of the Lord of Vānaras, and left for the quarter protected by Varuṇa.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे द्विचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty second Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
