Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 24
In this Sarga, Rāma also sees the same terrible omens that Khara sees. Rāma tells Lakshmaṇa that it is going to be a big fight with possible danger to their lives.
He asks Lakshmaṇa to take his bow and arrows and take Seetā with him to a an impenetrable cave. He tells Lakshmaṇa that he would like to kill all the Rākshasas himself.
Meanwhile Dēvas, Gandharvas, Siddhas, Cāraṇas, Maharshis, Rājarshis, Brāhmaṇas, et al assemble to witness the fight between fourteen thousand Rākshasas on one side and just one, Rāma, on the other.
Then the Rākshasas approach Rāma, making loud noises. Rāma takes a good look at them and gets ready with his bow and arrows. Rāma, in his wrath, was fearsome for anyone to look at, like Shiva at the time of destroying the Yajña of Daksha. The army of Rākshasas looked like a mass of black clouds at sunrise.
3.24.1
आश्रमं प्रति याते तु
खरे खरपराक्रमे ।
तानेवौत्पातिकान्रामः
सह भ्रात्रा ददर्श ह ॥
When Khara of fierce valor marched towards the Āṡrama, Rāma also, along with his brother, saw the same bad omens as Khara did.
3.24.2
तानुत्पातान्महोघोरान्
उत्थितान्रोमहर्षणान् ।
प्रजानामहितान्दृष्ट्वा
रामो लक्ष्मणमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing those hair-raising omens that portended extreme disaster and danger to all beings, Rāma said to Lakshmaṇa:
3.24.3
इमान्पश्य महाबाहो
सर्वभूतापहारिणः ।
समुत्थितान्महोत्पातान्
संहर्तुं सर्वराक्षसान् ॥
O you of mighty arm! Look at these ominous portents that arose, boding danger to all beings and death to all Rākshasas!
3.24.4
अमी रुधिरधारास्तु
विसृजन्तः खरस्वनाः ।
व्योम्नि मेघा विवर्तन्ते
परुषा गर्दभारुणाः ॥
(Look at these) ruthless clouds, donkey-tan in color, that billow in the sky, making harsh sounds and pouring streams of blood!
3.24.5
सधूमाश्च शरास्सर्वे
मम युद्धाभिनन्दिनः ।
रुक्मपृष्ठानि चापानि
विवेष्टन्ते च लक्ष्मण ॥
O Lakshmaṇa, rejoicing in the prospect of a fight, all my arrows smolder, and bows decked with gold on their backs feel restless!
3.24.6
यादृशा इह कूजन्ति
पक्षिणौ वनचारिणः ।
अग्रतो नो भयं प्राप्तं
संशयो जीवितस्य च ॥
The cries of wild birds indicate that there is danger ahead of us, and even our lives could be at risk.
3.24.7
सम्प्रहारस्तु सुमहान्
भविष्यति न संशयः ।
अयमाख्याति मे बाहुः
स्फुरमाणो मुहुर्मुहुः ।
सन्निकर्षे तु न श्शूर
जयं शत्रोः पराजयम् ।
सप्रभं च प्रसन्नं च
तव वक्त्रं हि लक्ष्यते ॥
Lakshmaṇa, O valiant one, it is certain that there is going to be a massive fight. This right arm of mine, quivering repeatedly, foretells imminent success for us and defeat for the enemy; and your face too, is bright and cheerful.
3.24.9
उद्यतानां हि युद्धार्थं
येषां भवति लक्ष्मण ।
निष्प्रभं वदनं तेषां
भवत्यायुः परिक्षयः ॥
Those whose faces become wan, O Lakshmaṇa, as they prepare for battle, can count on their end coming near.
3.24.10
रक्षसां नर्दतां घोरः
श्रूयते च महाध्वनिः ।
आहतानां च भेरीणां
राक्षसैः क्रूरकर्मभिः ॥
We can hear the huge din of the terrible war-whoops let off by the Rākshasas and of the beating of drums by those Rākshasas of cruel deeds.
3.24.11
अनागतविधानं तु
कर्तव्यं शुभमिच्छता ।
आपदं शङ्कमानेन
पुरुषेण विपश्चिता ॥
Anyone who is thoughtful, who suspects trouble and wishes to remain safe, should prepare oneself accordingly.
3.24.12
तस्माद्गृहीत्वा वैदेहीं
शरपाणिर्धनुर्धरः ।
गुहामाश्रय शैलस्य
दुर्गां पादपसङ्कुलाम् ॥
Hence, arming yourself with bow and arrow and taking Vaidēhee with you, go into a cave in the mountain that is well covered with trees and is impenetrable.
3.24.13
प्रतिकूलितुमिच्छामि
न हि वाक्यमिदं त्वया ।
शापितो मम पादाभ्यां
गम्यतां वत्स मा चिरम् ॥
I do not want to hear any objection from you to this plan. Go without delay, my child, I swear by my feet!
3.24.14
त्वं हि शूरश्च बलवान्
हन्याह्येतान्न संशयः ।
स्वयं तु हन्तुमिच्छामि
सर्वानेव निशाचरान् ॥
I know you are valiant and strong, and you can, without a doubt, slay these (Rākshasas). But I wish to kill all these Night-rovers myself.
3.24.15
एवमुक्तस्तु रामेण
लक्ष्मणस्सह सीताया ।
शरानादाय चापं च
गुहां दुर्गां समाश्रयत् ॥
Thus told by Rāma, Lakshmaṇa took his bow and arrows and went into an impenetrable cave along with Seetā.
3.24.16
तस्मिन्प्रविष्टे तु गुहां
लक्ष्मणे सह सीतया ।
हन्त निर्युक्तमित्युक्त्वा
रामः कवचमाविशत् ॥
When Lakshmaṇa entered the cave along with Seetā, Rāma said (to himself) ‘Great, this is the right thing!’ and donned his armor.
3.24.17
स तेनाग्नि निकाशेन
कवचेन विभूषितः ।
बभूव राम स्तिमिरे
विधूमोऽग्निरिवोत्थितः ॥
Adorned with that fiery-bright armor, Rāma looked like a smokeless flame that arose in the dark.
3.24.18
स चापमुद्यम्य महत्
शरानादाय वीर्यवान् ।
बभूवावस्थितस्तत्र
ज्यास्वनैः पूरयन्दिशः ॥
Raising his great bow and taking the arrows the valiant one took his stand and filled the quarters with the twang of the bow string.
3.24.19
ततो देवास्सगन्धर्वाः
सिद्धाश्च सह चारणैः ।
समेयुश्च महात्मानो
युद्धदर्शनकाङ्क्षिणः ॥
Then all the Mahātmas, the Dēvas and the Gandharvas, the Siddhas and the Cāraṇas assembled there, eager to see the fight.
3.24.20
ऋषयश्च महात्मानो
लोके ब्रह्मर्षिसत्तमाः ।
समेत्य चोचुस्सहिता
अन्योन्यं पुण्यकर्मणः ॥
The Ṛshis who were Mahātmas, the eminent Brahmarshis, and men of Puṇya deeds of the world assembled there telling each other:
3.24.21
स्वस्ति गोब्राह्मणेभ्योऽस्तु
लोकानां येऽभिसङ्गताः ।
जयतां राघवो युद्धे
पौलस्त्यान् रजनीचरान् ।
चक्रहस्तो यथा युद्धे
सर्वानसुर पुङ्गवान् ॥
May all bode well to the cows and Brāhmaṇas who labor for the good of all beings! May victory be to Rāghava in the fight over the Night-rovers, the descendants of Pulastya, just as it was for the wielder of Cakra over all the prominent Asuras!
3.24.22
एवमुक्त्वा पुनः प्रोचुः
आलोक्य च परस्परम् ॥
Having said this, they looked at each other and continued:
3.24.23
चतुर्दश सहस्राणि
रक्षसां भीमकर्मणाम् ।
एकश्च रामो धर्मात्मा
कथं युद्धं भविष्यति ॥
Here are fourteen thousand Rākshasas of fierce deeds on one side and only one Rāma the Dharmātma on the other; it is to be seen how this fight will turn out!
3.24.24
इति राजर्षयस्सिद्धाः
सगणाश्च द्विजर्षभाः ।
जातकौतूहलास्तस्थुः
विमानस्थाश्च देवताः ॥
Saying thus, the Rājarshis and Siddhas, the Brāhmaṇas with their Ṡishyas, and the Dēvas from their Vimānas awaited with great curiosity.
3.24.25
आविष्टं तेजसा रामं
सङ्ग्रामशिरसि स्थितम् ।
दृष्ट्वा सर्वाणि भूतानि
भयाद्विव्यथिरे तदा ॥
Seeing Rāma charged with power as he stood ahead ready for the fray, all beings shuddered with fear.
3.24.26
रूपमप्रतिमं तस्य
रामस्याक्लिष्टकर्मणः ।
बभूव रूपं क्रुद्धस्य
रुद्रस्येव पिनाकिनः ॥
That matchless posture of Rāma, who did everything effortlessly, was like that of Rudra the holder of Pināka, in ire.
3.24.27
ततो गम्भीरनिर्ह्रादं
घोरवर्मायुधध्वजम् ।
अनीकं यातुधानानां
समन्तात्प्रत्यदृश्यत ॥
Then the army of the Rākshasas was seen flowing in from all sides, with the terrifying armors, weapons and pennants, raising a tremendous din.
3.24.28
सिंहनादं विसृजतां
अन्योन्यमभिगर्जताम् ।
चापानि विस्फारयतां
जृम्भतामप्यभीक्ष्णशः ।
विप्रघुष्टस्वनानां च
दुन्धुभीश्चापि निघ्नताम् ।
तेषां सुतुमुलश्शब्दः
पूरयामास तद्वनम् ॥
Then the Vana was filled with that din as they let off their war-whoops, shouted facing each other, beat their bows, yawned excessively, bawled their loudest, and beat their kettledrums.
3.24.30
तेन शब्देन वित्रस्ताः
श्वापदा वनचारिणः ।
दुद्रुवुर्यत्र निश्शब्दं
पृष्ठतो न व्यलोकयन् ॥
Terrified by that din, the beasts of the Vana fled searching for quiet places without looking back.
3.24.31
तत्त्वनीकं महाघोरं
रामं समुपसर्पत ।
धृतनानाप्रहरणं
गम्भीरं सागरोपमम् ॥
That extremely terrifying army, carrying many weapons and intense like the ocean, advanced towards Rāma.
3.24.32
रामोऽपि चारयंश्चक्षुः
सर्वतो रणपण्डितः ।
ददर्श खरसैन्यं तत्
युद्धाभिमुखमुत्थितम् ॥
And Rāma, expert in warfare, turning his gaze in every direction, saw that army of Khara advancing, ready for battle.
3.24.33
वितत्य च धनुर्भीमं
तूण्याश्चोद्धृत्य सायकान् ।
क्रोधमाहारयत्तीव्रं
वधार्थं सर्वरक्षसाम् ॥
Stretching his terrifying bow and taking arrows from his quiver, Rāma summoned his extreme wrath to slay all those Rākshasas.
Those who have mastered their senses will never be taken over by anger. Rather, they summon it as and when needed at their own discretion. In other words they are never a slave of anger, but a master of it.
3.24.34
दुष्प्रेक्ष्यस्सोऽभवत्कृद्धो
युगान्ताग्निरिव ज्वलन् ।
तं दृष्ट्वा तेजसाविष्टं
प्राद्रवन्वनदेवताः ॥
Seeing him charged with intense power and blazing in his wrath like the conflagration at the dissolution of the worlds, the Dēvatas of the Vana fled away.
3.24.35
तस्य क्रुद्धस्य रूपं तु
रामस्य ददृशे तदा ।
दक्षस्येव क्रतुं हन्तुं
उद्यतस्य पिनाकिनः ॥
At that moment, Rāma in his wrath looked like the wielder of the Pināka who was ready to destroy the Yajña of Daksha.
3.24.36
तत्कार्मुकैराभरणैर्ध्वजैश्च
तैर्वर्मभिश्चाग्निसमानवर्णैः ।
बभूव सैन्यं पिशिताशनानां
सूर्योदये नीलमिवाभ्रबृन्दम् ॥
That army of flesh-eaters, with its bows, pennants, decorations and the flame-bright armors looked like a black mass of clouds at sunrise.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे चतुर्विंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty fourth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
