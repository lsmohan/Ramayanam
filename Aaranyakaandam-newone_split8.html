Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 8
In this short Sarga, Rāma takes leave of Suteekshṇa and heads to the Daṇḍaka forest along with Lakshmaṇa and Seetā.
3.8.1
रामस्तु सहसौमित्रिः
सुतीक्ष्णेनाभिपूजितः ।
परिणाम्य निशां तत्र
प्रभाते प्रत्यबुध्यत ॥
Rāma along with Lakshmaṇa, spent the night receiving the hospitality of Suteekshṇa and woke up in the morning.
3.8.2
उत्थाय तु यथाकालं
राघवस्सहसीतया ।
उपास्पृश्य सुशीतेन
तोयेनोत्पलगन्धिना ॥
Rāghava, along with Seetā, getting up at the usual hour, touched cold water that had the fragrance of lilies.
3.8.3
अथ तेऽग्निं सुरांश्चैव
वैदेही रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
काल्यं विधिवदभ्यर्च्य
तपस्विशरणे वने ।
उदयन्तं दिनकरं
दृष्ट्वा विगतकल्मषाः ।
सुतीक्ष्णमभिगम्येदं
श्लक्ष्णं वचनमब्रुवन् ॥
Then they, Vaidēhee, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, worshipped the fire and the Dēvas in a manner apt in the Vana, where Tapasvis live.
And having freed themselves from all afflictions by gazing at the rising sun, they went to Suteekshṇa and spoke these words in a gentle manner:
3.8.5
सुखोषितास्स्म भगवन्
त्वया पूज्येन पूजिताः ।
अपृच्छामः प्रायास्यामो
मुनयस्त्वरयन्ति नः ॥
We spent the night in comfort receiving the hospitality from you, who deserves all deference.
We would like to take leave of you and proceed. The Munis are hurrying us.
3.8.6
त्वरामहे वयं द्रष्टुं
कृत्स्नमाश्रममण्डलम् ।
ऋषीणां पुण्यशीलानां
दण्डकारण्यवासिनाम् ॥
And we ourselves are eager to see the entire settlement of Āṡramas of the holy Ṛshis who have made the Daṇḍaka forest their home.
3.8.7
अभ्यनुज्ञातुमिच्छामः
सहैभिर्मुनिपुङ्गवैः ।
धर्मनित्यैस्तपोदान्तैः
विशिखैरिव पावकैः ॥
We beg for your permission to leave along with these eminent Munis who are like fire sans the flames, ever adhering to Dharma, Tapa and discipline.
3.8.8
अविषह्यातपो यावत्
सूर्यो नातिविराजते ।
अमार्गेणागतां लक्ष्मीम्
प्राप्येवान्वयवर्जितः ।
तावदिच्छामहे गन्तुम्
इत्युक्त्वा चरणौ मुनेः ।
ववन्दे सहसौमित्रिः
सीतया सह राघवः ॥
We would like to leave before the sun becomes hot and unbearable, like a man of modest origins upon winning a fortune by improper means.
Saying thus Rāma, along with Seetā and Lakshmaṇa offered Vandana to the feet of that Muni.
3.8.10
तौ संस्पृशन्तौ चरणौ
उत्थाप्य मुनिपुङ्गवः ।
गाढमालिङ्ग्य सस्नेहम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
That eminent Muni raised them who had touched his feet, embraced them heartily and said these words with all affection:
3.8.11
अरिष्टं गच्छ पन्थानं
राम सौमित्रिणा सह ।
सीतया चानया सार्धम्
छाययेवानुवृत्तया ॥
O Rāma, may you go your way in all safety, along with Sowmitri and Seetā who follows you like your shadow.
3.8.12
पश्याऽश्रमपदं रम्यं
दण्डकारण्यवासिनाम् ।
एषां तपस्विनां वीर
तपसा भावितात्मनाम् ॥
O Veera! Visit the beautiful Āṡramas of these Tapasvis who live in the Daṇḍaka forest and have realized the Self by their Tapa.
3.8.13
सप्राज्यफलमूलानि
पुष्पितानि वनानि च ।
प्रशस्तमृगयूथानि
शान्तपक्षिगणानि च ।
फुल्लपङ्कजषण्डानि
प्रसन्नसलिलानि च ।
कारण्डवविकीर्णानि
तटाकानि सरांसि च ।
द्रक्ष्यसे दृष्टिरम्याणि
गिरिप्रस्रवणानि च ।
रमणीयान्यरण्यानि
मयूराभिरुतानि च ॥
There you will see woods with plentiful flowers, fruits and roots. You will see droves of enchanting animals and flocks of peaceful birds. And you will see ponds with clear water with lotuses in bloom and Kāraṇḍa birds and mountain streams that are like candy to the eye. And you will see beautiful woods echoing with peacock caws.
3.8.16
गम्यतां वत्स सौमित्रे
भवानपि च गच्छतु ।
आगन्तव्यं च ते दृष्ट्वा
पुनरेवाश्रमं प्रति  ॥
Go, my child, and you too, O Sowmitri, my dear, go. Having seen them all, you should come to this Āṡrama again.
3.8.17
एवमुक्तस्तथेत्युक्त्वा
काकुत्स्थस्सहलक्ष्मणः ।
प्रदक्षिणं मुनिं कृत्वा
प्रस्थातुमुपचक्रमे ॥
Saying ‘Yes’, to those (affectionate) words, Kākutstha, along with Lakshmaṇa circumambulated the Muni and got ready to leave.
3.8.18
ततश्शुभतरे तूणी
धनुषी चायतेक्षणा ।
ददौ सीता तयोर्भ्रात्रोः
खङ्गौ च विमलौ ततः ॥
And then the wide-eyed Seetā handed the brothers their splendid quivers, bows and shiny swords.
3.8.19
आबध्य च शुभे तूणी
चापे चादाय सुस्वनौ ।
निष्क्रान्तावाश्रमाद्गन्तुम्
उभौ तौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
Tying those excellent quivers (on their back) and taking the bows as they made a jingle, both Rāma and Lakshmaṇa left the Āṡrama, to go on their path together.
3.8.20
श्रीमन्तौ रूपसम्पन्नौ
दीप्यमानौ स्वतेजसा ।
प्रस्थितौ धृतचापौ तौ
सीतया सह राघवौ ॥
The richly endowed scions of the Raghus, handsome and dazzling with their natural radiance, started on their way, wielding bows, with Seetā accompanying them.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे अष्टमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
