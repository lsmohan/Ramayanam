Baala Kaanda - Sarga 72
After the lineages of both sides were shared with each other in the previous two Sargas, Viṡwāmitra, in this Sarga, requests Janaka, on behalf of Daṡaratha and with the permission of Vasishṭha, for the hands of his daughters and the daughters of his brother Kuṡadhwaja, for the four sons of Daṡaratha, and Janaka agrees.
1.72.1
तमुक्तवन्तं वैदेहम्
विश्वामित्रो महामुनिः ।
उवाच वचनं वीरम्
वसिष्ठसहितो नृपम् ॥
Mahā Muni Viṡwāmitra together with Vasishṭha spoke these words to the valiant king of Vidēha, who had just described his lineage:
1.72.2
अचिन्त्यान्यप्रमेयानि
कुलानि नरपुङ्गव ।
इक्ष्वाकूणां विदेहानाम्
नैषां तुल्योऽस्ति कश्चन ॥
O best among men! Both the lineages are eminent beyond measure and beyond imagination. No one is comparable to the Ikshwākus and Vidēhās!
1.72.3
सदृशो धर्मसम्बन्धः
सदृशो रूपसम्पदा ।
रामलक्ष्मणयो राजन्
सीता चोर्मिलया सह ॥
O King! The match of Seetā and Ūrmilā, for Rāma and Lakshmaṇa is perfect both in their virtues, traditions and in their looks.
1.72.4
वक्तव्यं च नरश्रेष्ठ
श्रूयतां वचनं मम ।
भ्राता यवीयान् धर्मज्ञ
एष राजा कुशध्वजः ॥
O best among men! Truth must be told; listen to my words! This king Kuṡadhwaja, your younger brother, is a Dharmajña!
1.72.5
अस्य धर्मात्मनो राजन्
रूपेणाप्रतिमं भुवि ।
सुताद्वयं नरश्रेष्ठ
पत्न्यर्थं वरयामहे ॥
O King! O best among men! We also seek this Dharmātma's two daughters, unrivaled in their beauty on this earth, as brides.
1.72.6
भरतस्य कुमारस्य
शत्रुघ्नस्य च धीमतः ।
वरयामस्सुते राजन्
तयोरर्थे महात्मनोः ॥
O King! We seek the two daughters for the Mahatmas, the youthful Bharata and ingenious Ṡatrughna!
1.72.7
पुत्रा दशरथस्येमे
रूपयौवनशालिनः ।
लोकपालोपमास्सर्वे
देवतुल्यपराक्रमाः ॥
These sons of Daṡaratha are resplendent with youth and grace, comparable to the rulers of the worlds, matching Dēvas in their valor.
1.72.8
उभयोरपि राजेन्द्र
सम्बन्धेनानुबध्यताम् ।
इक्ष्वाकोः कुलमव्यग्रम्
भवतःपुण्यकर्मणः ॥
O lord of kings! May both your lineages, that of Ikshwāku and yours, of unblemished achievements be strongly bonded together with this alliance.
1.72.9
विश्वामित्रवच श्शृत्वा
वसिष्ठस्य मते तदा ।
जनकः प्रांजलिर्वाक्यम्
उवाच मुनिपुङ्गवौ ॥
Hearing those words of Viṡwāmitra, who spoke the mind of Vasishṭha, Janaka replied with folded hands to both those Munis:
1.72.10
कुलं धन्यमिदं मन्ये
येषां नो मुनिपुङ्गवौ ।
सदृशं कुलसम्बन्धम्
यदाज्ञापयथः स्वयम् ॥
O the duo of Mahā Munis! I consider that my lineage is blessed by this relationship between the two lineages that is prescribed by none other than you both, the two most eminent Munis.
1.72.11
एवं भवतु भद्रं वः
कुशध्वजसुते इमे ।
पत्न्यौ भजेतां सहितौ
शत्रुघ्नभरतावुभौ ॥
May all bode well for you! May these two daughters of Kuṡadhwaja become the wives of Ṡatrughna and Bharata and serve them.
1.72.12
एकाह्ना राजपुत्रीणाम्
चतसृणां महामुने ।
पाणीन् गृह्णन्तु चत्वारो
राजपुत्रा महाबलाः ॥
O Mahā Muni! May the mighty strong princes, four of them, together take the hands of the princesses on the same day!
1.72.13
उत्तरे दिवसे ब्रह्मन्
फल्गुनीभ्यां मनीषिणः ।
वैवाहिकं प्रशंसन्ति
भगो यत्र प्रजापतिः ॥
O Brahman! The learned say that between the duo of the stars of Phalguṇis, the day when Uttara Phalguṇi is with the moon is better for performing wedding ceremonies, because that star is ruled by Prajāpati (progenitor).
1.72.14
एवमुक्त्वा वचस्सौम्यम्
प्रत्युत्थाय कृताञ्जलिः ।
उभौ मुनिवरौ राजा
जनको वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
King Janaka, having spoken those gentle and agreeable words, stood up with folded hands and addressed again both those eminent Munis, saying:
1.72.15
परो धर्मः कृतो मह्यम्
शिष्योऽस्मिभवतोः सदा ।
इमान्यासनमुख्यानि
आसातां मुनिपुङ्गवौ ॥
You have fulfilled one of my ultimate responsibilities! I shall serve you as a Ṡishya forever. May you please take these esteemed seats!
1.72.16
यथा दशरथस्येयम्
तथाऽयोध्या पुरी मम ।
प्रभुत्वे नास्ति सन्देहो
यथाऽर्हं कर्तुमर्हथ ॥
Without a doubt, I consider Daṡaratha to be the lord of this city as much as I consider Ayōdhyā to be my responsibility. May both of you help in that direction!
1.72.17
तथा ब्रुवति वैदेहे
जनके रघुनन्दनः ।
राजा दशरथो हृष्टः
प्रत्युवाच महीपतिम् ॥
As the king of Vidēha spoke thus, the descendant of Raghu dynasty, king Daṡaratha felt happy and replied to that lord of the land:
1.72.18
युवामसङ्ख्येयगुणौ
भ्रातरौ मिथिलेश्वरौ ।
ऋषयो राजसङ्घाश्च
भवद्भ्यामभिपूजिताः ॥
Both of you, brothers and lords of Mithilā, are abodes of countless virtues. You indeed have honored many Ṛshis and kings!
1.72.19
स्वस्ति प्राप्नुहि भद्रं ते
गमिष्यामि स्वमालयम् ।
श्राद्धकर्माणि सर्वाणि
विधास्यामीति चाब्रवीत् ॥
May all bode well for you! May you find everything safe! I shall now return to my place and perform all the necessary rites and ceremonies.
1.72.20
तमापृष्ट्वा नरपतिम्
राजा दशरथस्तदा ।
मुनीन्द्रौ तौ पुरस्कृत्य
जगामाशु महायशाः ॥
That renowned king Daṡaratha, having taken leave of king Janaka left immediately, following the two Munis.
1.72.21
स गत्वा निलयं राजा
श्राद्धं कृत्वा विधानतः ।
प्रभाते काल्यमुत्थाय
चक्रे गोदानमुत्तमम् ॥
After reaching his guest quarters, he got the rites and ceremonies performed in a customary manner. He got up the next morning and gifted away cows.
1.72.22
गवां शतसहस्राणि
ब्राह्मणेभ्यो नराधिपः ।
एकैकशो ददौ राजा
पुत्रानुद्दिश्य धर्मतः ॥
That ruler of the peoples, in accordance with Dharma, then donated a hundred thousand cows to Brāhmaṇas in the name of each of his sons.
1.72.23
सुवर्णश्रुङ्गा स्सम्पन्ना
स्सवत्साः कांस्यदोहनाः ।
गवां शतसहस्राणि
चत्वारि पुरुषर्षभः ।
वित्तमन्यच्च सुबहु
द्विजेभ्यो रघुनन्दनः ।
ददौ गोदानमुद्दिश्य
पुत्राणां पुत्रवत्सलः ॥
That bull among men, descendant of Raghu, overflowing with affection towards his sons, gave away four hundred thousand milking cows, along with bronze vessels to hold the milk, along with their calves and with their horns capped in gold. In addition he also gave away abundant amounts of money.
1.72.25
स सुतैः कृतगोदानैः
वृतस्तु नृपतिस्तदा ।
लोकपालैरिवाभाति
वृतः स्सौम्यः प्रजापतिः ॥
The king who was surrounded by his sons, after performing the donation of cows, looked resplendent like the gratified Prajāpati surrounded by Lōka Pālakas.
Brahma creates Prajāpatis. Prajāpatis create different Lōkas (worlds). Each Lōka has a Pālaka (head, ruler). This structure is similar to the structure of a multi-national, multi-product conglomerate.
We fractally inherit the ability to create, comprehend and manage such complex conglomerates from the universe itself.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे द्विसप्ततितमस्सर्ग:॥
Thus concludes the seventy second Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
