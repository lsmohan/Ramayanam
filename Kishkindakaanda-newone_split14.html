Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 14
In this Sarga, Sugreeva implores Rāma to fulfill his promise without fail. Rāma tells Sugreeva that there is no need for concern now, as he can be clearly identified because of the creeper around his neck. Rāma assures him that he never made a promise that he could not keep. He reminds him of the power of his arrow that tore apart seven Sāla trees. Rāma assures Sugreeva that Vāli is all but dead. He asks him to call Vāli for a fight.
4.14.1
अथ बालार्कसदृशो
दृप्तसिंहगतिस्तदा ।
दृष्ट्वा रामं क्रियादक्षम्
सुग्रीवो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Then, Sugreeva, resembling the rising sun, with a gait like that of the proud lion, looked at Rāma, an expert at his task, and said these words:
4.14.2
हरिवागुरया व्याप्ताम्
तप्तकाञ्चनतोरणाम् ।
प्राप्ताः स्म ध्वजयन्त्राढ्याम्
किष्किन्धां वालिनः पुरीम् ॥
We have reached Kishkindhā, the city of Vāli, with its arches treated with gold purified in fire. It is fortified with machinery and flags are hoisted (everywhere). It is crowded with Vānaras that serve as traps for the enemy.
4.14.3
प्रतिज्ञा या त्वया वीर
कृता वालिवधे पुरा ।
सफलां कुरु तां क्षिप्रम्
लतां काल इवागतः ॥
O Veera! Make good your promise to kill Vāli soon, just as the right season brings the vine to fruition.
4.14.4
एवमुक्तस्तु धर्मात्मा
सुग्रीवेण स राघवः ।
तमेवोवाच वचनम्
सुग्रीवं शत्रुसूदनः ॥
Rāghava, the Dharmātma, the slayer of enemies, told thus by Sugreeva, replied directly, saying:
4.14.5
कृताभिज्ञानचिह्नस्त्वम्
आनया गजसाह्वया ।
लक्ष्मणेन समुत्पाट्य
यैषा कण्ठे कृता तव ॥
You can be recognized (clearly now), with the Gajapushpa creeper that Lakshmaṇa plucked and placed around your neck.
4.14.6
शोभसेऽह्यधिकं वीर
लतया कण्ठसक्तया ।
विपरीत इवाकाशे
सूर्यो नक्षत्रमालया ॥
With the creeper around your neck, O Veera, you shine all the more, as the sun in the sky would, if adorned with a garland of stars.
4.14.7
अद्य वालिसमुत्थं ते
भयं वैरं च वानर ।
एकेनाहं प्रमोक्ष्यामि
बाणमोक्षेण संयुगे ॥
I shall relieve you today, O Vānara, from the fear as well as the enmity that arose because of Vāli, with a single arrow, shot during the fight.
4.14.8
मम दर्शय सुग्रीव
वैरिणं भ्रातृरूपिणम् ।
वाली विनिहतो यावत्
वने पांसुषु वेष्टते ॥
O Sugreeva! Show me Vāli, your enemy in the guise of a brother! In no time, he will be rolling in the dust of the Vana, shot fatally.
4.14.9
यदि दृष्टिपथं प्राप्तो
जीवन्स विनिवर्तते ।
ततो दोषेण मा गच्छेत्
सद्यो गर्हेच्च मां भवान् ॥
If he goes back alive after I set eyes on him, you will be free to hold me guilty and blame me squarely.
4.14.10
प्रत्यक्षं सप्त ते साला
मया बाणेन दारिताः ।
ततोवेत्सि बलेनाद्य
वालिनं निहतं मया ॥
You saw with your own eyes how I tore apart the seven Sāla trees with a single arrow; know that, with the same strength, Vāli will be killed today.
4.14.11
अनृतं नोक्तपूर्वं मे
वीर कृच्छ्रेऽपि तिष्टता ।
धर्मलोभपरीतेन
न च वक्ष्ये कथञ्चन ॥
O Veera! Seized by the interest in Dharma, I have never said anything that I did not mean, even in the most trying situations, nor shall I ever do so.
4.14.12
सफलां च करिष्यामि
प्रतिज्ञां जहि सम्भ्रमम् ।
प्रसूतं कलमक्षेत्रं
वर्षेणेव शतक्रतुः ॥
I will make my promise bear fruit, as surely as a rice-field is made to sprout by the rains showered by Ṡatakratu; you may kill all your concerns.
4.14.13
तदाह्वाननिमित्तं त्वम्
वालिनो हेममालिनः ।
सुग्रीव कुरु तं शब्दम्
निष्पतेद्येन वानरः ॥
Therefore, O Sugreeva, make whatever sound is required to bring Vāli, the Vānara with the golden chain, out to extend the invitation (for a fight).
4.14.14
जितकाशी बलश्लाघी
त्वया चाधर्षितः पुरा ।
निष्पतिष्यत्यसङ्गेन
वाली स प्रियसंयुगः ॥
There is nothing to stop Vāli from rushing out given that he glows with victories, likes to sing praises of his own strength, has never been vanquished by you, and loves a fight.
4.14.15
रिपूणां धर्षितं श्रुत्वा
मर्षयन्ति न संयुगे ।
जानन्तस्तु स्वकं वीर्यम्
स्त्रीसमक्षं विशेषतः ॥
Those who are confident of their own strength, cannot bear hearing a challenge from an enemy to fight, especially in the presence of women.
4.14.16
स तु रामवचश्श्रुत्वा
सुग्रीवो हेमपिङ्गलः ।
ननर्द क्रूरनादेन
विनिर्भिन्दन्निवाम्बरम् ॥
Hearing those words of Rāma, Sugreeva of red gold complexion, let off a violent roar that seemed to split the sky.
4.14.17
तस्य शब्देन वित्रस्ता
गावो यान्ति हतप्रभाः ।
राजदोषपरामृष्टाः
कुलस्त्रिय इवाकुलाः ॥
Terrified by that noise, cows fled with their luster lost, like women of good families agitated by the touch of other men, in times of anarchy.
4.14.18
द्रवन्ति च मृगाश्शीघ्रम्
भग्ना इव रणे हयाः ।
पतन्ति च खगा भूमौ
क्षीणपुण्या इव ग्रहाः ॥
Deer ran away quickly like horses battered in a battle. Birds fell down on the ground from the sky like planets whose Puṇya is depleted.
4.14.19
ततस्सजीमूतगणप्रणादो
नादं ह्यमुञ्चत्त्वरया प्रतीतः ।
सूर्यात्मजश्शौर्यविवृद्धतेजाः
सरित्पतिर्वाऽनिलचञ्चलोर्मिः ॥
The son of the sun, known for making sounds that resemble the thunder of cloud masses, his prowess augured by valor, let off a roar in all haste, like the lord of rivers does with waves fanned by wind.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे चतुर्दशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fourteenth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
