Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 55
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa takes Seetā forcibly and shows around his opulent palace and tries to impress her, speaking about his exploits, power and grandeur.
He advises her to forget Rāma and pooh-poohs him as someone who lost his kingdom, a mere human with no strength and someone who cannot even dream of ever coming to the impenetrable Laṅkā. He tells her that her bad Karma is over with her stay in the Vana and it is time for her to enjoy along with him, taking rides in his air-car by name Pushpaka.
He promises her power over everyone in his realm including him and every other woman in his harem. He touches her feet with his heads and pleads for her grace.
Seetā, helpless and dejected, sheds tears covering her face with her garment.
3.55.1
सन्दिश्य राक्षसान्घोरान्
रावणोऽष्टौ महाबलान् ।
आत्मानं बुद्धिवैक्लब्यात्
कृतकृत्यममन्यत ॥
Having given orders to those eight horrid Rākshasas, Rāvaṇa in his perverse folly thought that he had done all that needed to be done.
3.55.2
स चिन्तयानो वैदेहीम्
कामबाणसमर्पितः ।
प्रविवेश गृहं रम्यम्
सीतां द्रष्टुमभित्वरन् ॥
Hit by the darts of love and his thoughts filled with Vaidēhee, he entered that beautiful house, eager to see Seetā.
3.55.3
स प्रविश्य तु तद्वेश्म
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ।
अपश्यद्राक्षसीमध्ये
सीतां शोकपरायणाम् ॥
Having entered that house, Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas saw Seetā who was taken over by grief, in the midst of the Rākshasis.
3.55.4
अश्रुपूर्णमुखीं दीनाम्
शोकभाराभिपीडिताम् ।
वायुवेगैरिवाक्रान्ताम्
मज्जन्तीं नावमर्णवे ।
मृगयूथपरिभ्रष्टाम्
मृगीं श्वभिरिवावृताम् ।
अधोमुखमुखीं सीताम्
अभ्येत्य च निशाचरः ॥
The night rover approached her who sat with her face hanging down, full of tears, feeling terrible, oppressed by the weight of her grief.
She was like a boat buffeted by strong winds on the high seas and sinking. She was like a deer that had strayed from its herd and was hemmed in by hounds.
3.55.6
तां तु शोकपरां दीनाम्
अवशां राक्षसाधिपः ।
स बलाद्दर्शयामास
गृहं देवगृहोपमम् ॥
The lord of Rākshasas then forcibly showed that house, which rivaled the house of Dēvas, to her who was not even in her own control, dejected and taken over by grief.
3.55.7
हर्म्यप्रासादसम्बाधम्
स्त्रीसहस्रनिषेवितम् ।
नानापक्षिगणैर्जुष्टम्
नानारत्नसमन्वितम् ।
दान्तकैस्तापनीयैश्च
स्फाटिकै राजतैरपि ।
वज्रवैडूर्यचित्रैश्च
स्तम्भैर्दृष्टिमनोहरैः ।
दिव्यदुन्दुभिनिर्ह्रादम्
तप्तकाञ्चनतोरणम् ॥
That house consisted of many mansions and palaces with thousands of women enjoying there.
It was studded with varieties of gems and its arches were inlaid with gold purified in fire.
With assorted works of ivory, crystals, gold, silver, diamond, lazulite, its pillars were simply captivating.
It was full of many kinds of flocks of birds. It reverberated with the sound of kettledrums.
3.55.9
सोपानं काञ्चनं चित्रम्
आरुरोह तया सह ।
दान्तका राजताश्चैव
गवाक्षाः प्रियदर्शनाः ।
हेमजालावताश्चासन्
तत्र प्रासादपङ्क्तयः ॥
He, taking her along with him, went up a stunning stair case made of gold, that led to lovely windows made of ivory and silver and rows of terraces covered with gold filigree.
3.55.11
सुधामणिविचित्राणि
भूमिभागानि सर्वशः ।
दशग्रीवस्स्वभवने
प्रादर्शयत मैथिलीम् ॥
The Ten-necked showed Maithili the flooring in his palace that was finished with stucco and inset with various kinds of gems.
3.55.12
दीर्घिकाः पुष्करिण्यश्च
नानावृक्षसमन्विताः ।
रावणो दर्शयामास
सीतां शोकपरायणाम् ॥
To that Seetā, who was taken over by grief, Rāvaṇa showed stepped wells and lotus pools surrounded by many kinds of trees.
3.55.13
दर्शयित्वा तु वैदेह्याः
कृत्स्नं तद्भवनोत्तमम् ।
उवाच वाक्यं पापात्मा
सीतां लोभितुमिच्छया ॥
Having thus shown every corner of that excellent palace to Vaidēhee he, of sinful mind, said these words in an attempt to tempt her:
3.55.14
दश राक्षसकोट्यश्च
द्वाविंशतिरथापराः ।
तेषां प्रभुरहं सीते
सर्वेषां भीमकर्मणाम् ।
वर्जयित्वा जरावृद्धान्
बालांश्च रजनीचरान् ॥
O Seetā! I am the lord of ten crore Rākshasas and another thirty two crores of terrifying deeds, not counting the children and the aged Night-rovers.
3.55.15
सहस्रमेकमेकस्य
मम कार्यपुरस्सरम् ।
यदिदं राजतन्त्रं मे
त्वयि सर्वं प्रतिष्ठितम् ।
जीवितं च विशालाक्षि
त्वं मे प्राणैर्गरीयसी ॥
For every single task I have a thousand to wait upon me. All of this elaborate administration and my life is at your beck and call now. O wide-eyed one! You are more than life itself to me.
3.55.17
बह्वीनामुत्तमस्त्रीणाम् 
मम योऽसौ परिग्रहः ।
तासां त्वमीश्वरा सीते
मम भार्या भव प्रिये ॥
Seetā, my dear, be my wife and be the boss of the many excellent women that I have secured.
3.55.18
साधु किं तेऽन्यया बुद्ध्या
रोचयस्व वचो मम ।
कामाभितप्तस्य मम
प्रसादं कर्तुमर्हसि ॥
Accept my wholesome advice; what will you gain by thinking differently? I am burning with desire (for you) please bestow your grace on me.
3.55.19
परिक्षिप्ता समुद्रेण
लङ्केयं शतयोजना ।
नेयं धर्षयितुं शक्या
सेन्द्रैरपि सुरासुरैः ॥
This Laṅkā is a hundred Yōjanas wide and is surrounded by the sea on all sides. Not even the Suras and Asuras, even if they are joined by Indra, can stand against it.
3.55.20
न देवेषु न यक्षेषु
न गन्धर्वेषु पक्षिषु ।
अहं पश्यामि लोकेषु
यो मे वीर्यसमो भवेत् ॥
Neither among Dēvas, nor among Yakshas, nor among Gandharvas, nor among birds, can I find my equal in strength, in all the worlds.
3.55.21
राज्यभ्रष्टेन दीनेन
तापसेन गतायुषा ।
किं करिष्यसि रामेण
मानुषेणाल्पतेजसा ॥
What will you do with Rāma, who has lost his kingdom, a mere human with little strength living like a Tapasvi in a pitiable state, and who is as good as dead ?
3.55.22
भजस्व सीते मामेव
भर्ताहं सदृशस्तव ।
यौवनं ह्यध्रुवं भीरु
रमस्वेह मया सह ॥
Be with me, O Seetā! I am a husband worthy of you. O timid one! Youth is not permanent; enjoy with me.
3.55.23
दर्शने माकृथा बुद्धिम्
राघवस्य वरानने ।
कास्य शक्तिरिहागन्तुम्
अपि सीते मनोरथैः ॥
O Seetā of beautiful face! Do not entertain hopes of seeing Rāghava; where does he have the ability, even to dream of coming here?
3.55.24
न शक्यो वायुराकाशे
पाशैर्बद्धुं महाजवः ।
दीप्यमानस्य वाप्यग्नेः
गृहीतुं विमलां शिखाम् ॥
Swift winds that course through the sky cannot be held down by ropes. Smokeless tongues of flame from a blazing fire cannot be caught (by hand).
3.55.25
त्रयाणामपि लोकानाम्
न तं पश्यामि शोभने ।
विक्रमेण नयेद्यस्त्वाम्
मद्बाहुपरिपालिताम् ॥
I can see no one in the three worlds, who has the prowess to wrest you, O lovely one, from the protection of my exceedingly strong arms!
3.55.26
लङ्कायां सुमहद्राज्यम्
इदं त्वमनुपालय ।
त्वत्प्रेष्या मद्विधाश्चैव
देवाश्चापि चराचराः ॥
Rule over this mighty kingdom from Laṅkā. Those like me, as well as the Dēvas, and (all) the moving and non-moving creatures shall be at your beck and call.
3.55.27
अभिषेकोदकक्लिन्ना
तुष्टा च रमयस्व माम् ।
दुष्कृतं यत्पुरा कर्म
वनवासेन तद्गतम् ।
यश्च ते सुकृतो धर्मः
तस्येह फलमाप्नुहि ॥
Bathed in the water of consecration, be happy and also make me happy, Your bad Karma was paid off by your stay in the Vana. Now enjoy the fruits of your good and Dharmic deeds.
3.55.28
इह माल्यानि सर्वाणि
दिव्यगन्धानि मैथिलि ।
भूषणानि च मुख्यानि
सेवस्व च मया सह ॥
Enjoy every kind of garland with heavenly scents and the most splendid of ornaments, here along with me, O Maithili!
3.55.29
पुष्पकं नाम सुश्रोणि
भ्रातुर्वैश्रवणस्य मे ।
विमानं सूर्यसङ्काशम्
तरसा निर्जितं मया ॥
O you of exquisite Kaṭee! There is the sun-bright aerial car known as Pushpaka that once belonged to my brother Vaiṡravaṇa, which I won over by my sheer strength!
3.55.30
विशालं रमणीयं च
तद्विमानमनुत्तमम् ।
तत्र सीते मया सार्धम्
विहरस्व यथासुखम् ॥
That superb aerial car is spacious and beautiful. You can ride in it along with me in all comfort.
3.55.31
वदनं पद्मसङ्काशम्
अमलं चारुदर्शनम् ।
शोकार्तं तु वरारोहे
न भ्राजति वरानने ॥
O you of exquisite Kaṭee and exceptional face! Your spotless and beautiful face that is like a lotus does not shine as it should, overcast by grief!
3.55.32
एवं वदति तस्मिन् सा
वस्त्रान्तेन वराङ्गना ।
पिधायेन्दुनिभं सीता
मुखमश्रूण्यवर्तयत् ॥
When he spoke thus, Seetā, the best of women, covered her face that resembled the moon with the end of her garment, and let her tears fall.
3.55.33
ध्यायन्तीं तामिवास्वस्थाम्
दीनां चिन्ताहतप्रभाम् ।
उवाच वचनं पापो
रावणो राक्षसेश्वरः ॥
The sinful Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, then said to her who was pitiable and unwell, all her cheer robbed by her worry, looking like one lost in herself:
3.55.34
अलं व्रीडेन वैदेहि
धर्मलोपकृतेन च ।
हर्षोऽयं देवि विस्पष्टो
यस्त्वामुपमिष्यति ॥
Enough of being worried, O Vaidēhee, about violating the norms of Dharma! What has come to you, my lady, is very clearly an opportunity for enjoyment!
3.55.35
एतौ पादौ मया स्निग्धौ
शिरोभिः परिपीडितौ ।
प्रसादं कुरु मे क्षिप्रम्
वश्यो दासोऽहमस्मिते ॥
I bow to touch the shiny feet (of yours) with my heads. You have all the power over me and I submit myself to you. Bestow your grace on me with no further delay.
3.55.36
नेमाः शून्याः कृथा वाचः
शुष्यमाणेन भाषिताः ।
न चापि रावणः काञ्चित्
मूर्ध्ना स्त्रीं प्रणमेत ह ॥
Do not make these pleas, from one who is emaciated in desperation, come to naught. For, this Rāvaṇa, has never bowed his head to plead with any woman.
3.55.37
एवमुक्त्वा दशग्रीवो
मैथिलीं जनकात्मजाम् ।
कृतान्तवशमापन्नो
ममेयमिति मन्यते ॥
Having said these words to Maithili, the daughter of Janaka, the Ten-necked, with his end nearing, thought, ‘she is mine!’
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे पञ्चपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty fifth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
