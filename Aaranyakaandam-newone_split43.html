Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 43
In this Sarga, as expected, Māreeca in his deer form succeeds in catching the eye of Seetā. Amazed at that enchanting deer, Seetā calls out to Rāma and Lakshmaṇa. As soon as he sees it, Lakshmaṇa suspects that this out-of-the-world deer must be the trick of Māreeca.
But Seetā interrupts Lakshmaṇa and says to Rāma how beautiful the deer is and how much it captivates her. She pleads with Rāma to catch it alive, so that she can keep it through the rest of their stay in the Vana and show it later to Bharata and her mothers-in-law, once they are back in the kingdom. She pleads with Rāma to get at least its hide, if it cannot be caught alive. She fancies sitting on the hide spread over a cushion of tender grass.
Rāma also becomes attracted to this exceptional deer. He is also convinced that a deer of this kind cannot be found anywhere else. He points to Lakshmaṇa the excitement on the face of Seetā. He says one should collect, with effort, valuable products from the Vana. He agrees that Seetā would be happy, sitting along with him, on its hide.
Rāma also acknowledges Lakshmaṇa’s caution. He says that if Lakshmaṇa’s suspicion is true, and if the deer is truly Māreeca, it deserves to be killed, just as Vātāpi was killed by Agastya. Rāma tells Lakshmaṇa that he will go and get the deer either alive or come back with its hide, and asks him to stay in full alert guarding Seetā, taking the help of the great bird Jaṭāyu.
3.43.1
सा तं सम्प्रेक्ष्य सुश्रोणी
कुसुमान्यपचिन्वती ।
हेमराजतवर्णाभ्याम्
पार्श्वाभ्यामुपशोभितम् ।
प्रहृष्टा चानवद्याङ्गी
मृष्टहाटकवर्णिनी ।
भर्तारमपि चक्रन्द
लक्ष्मणं चापि सायुधम् ॥
Then that beautiful woman, of perfect figure, great Kaṭee and a complexion of pure gold, while plucking flowers, having taken a good look at the deer, whose sides shone like gold and silver. felt very happy and called out to her husband and also to the armed Lakshmaṇa.
3.43.3
तयाऽऽहूतौ नरव्याघ्रौ
वैदेह्या रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
वीक्षमाणौ तु तौ देशम्
तदा ददृशतुर्मृगम् ॥
Called out to by Vaidēhee, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, the tigers among men looked in that direction and saw the deer.
3.43.4
शङ्कमानस्तु तं दृष्ट्वा
लक्ष्मणो राममब्रवीत् ।
तमेवैनमहं मन्ये
मारीचं राक्षसं मृगम् ॥
Lakshmaṇa felt suspicious seeing it and said to Rāma: ‘I think this deer can be none other than Māreeca, the Rākshasa’.
3.43.5
चरन्तोमृगयां हृष्टाः
पापेनोपाधिना वने ।
अनेन निहता राजन्
राजानः कामरूपिणा ॥
O lord! Many a King who came hunting to relax, was slain in the Vana by this sinful one, who can take various forms at will.
3.43.6
अस्य मायाविदो माया
मृगरूपमिदं कृतम् ।
भानुमत्पुरुषव्याघ्र
गन्धर्वपुरसन्निभम् ॥
O tiger among men! This brilliant deer form, with workmanship reminiscent of the city of Gandharvas, is his making,
3.43.7
मृगो ह्येवंविधो रत्न
विचित्रो नास्ति राघव ।
जगत्यां जगतीनाथ
मायैषा हि न संशयः ॥
O lord of the wordl! There is no deer in this world like this marvel, made of gems! O Rāghava! It certainly is witchcraft, without a doubt.
3.43.8
एवं ब्रुवाणं काकुत्स्थम्
प्रतिवार्य शुचिस्मिता ।
उवाच सीता संहृष्टा
चर्मणा हृतचेतना ॥
Seetā of winning smile, with her heart stolen by the beautiful skin (of the deer), interrupted Kākutstha (Lakshmaṇa) who was speaking thus and said (to Rāma):
3.43.9
आर्यपुत्राभिरामोऽसौ
मृगो हरति मे मनः ।
आनयैनं महाबाहो
क्रीडार्थं नो भविष्यति ॥
O son of nobility! This lovely deer has stolen my heart. O you of mighty arm, please get it, it will be our plaything.
3.43.10
इहाश्रमपदेऽस्माकम्
बहवः पुण्यदर्शनाः ।
मृगाश्चरन्ति सहिताः
सृमराश्चमरास्तथा ॥
Many great looking animals wander in groups in our Āṡrama grounds here, including Sṛmaras and Camaras.
3.43.11
ऋक्षाः पृषतसङ्घाश्च
वानराः किन्नरास्तथा ।
विचरन्ति महाबाहो
रुपश्रेष्ठा मनोहराः ॥
O you of mighty arm! Bears, spotted deer, monkeys and Kinnaras move around here stealing the heart with their exquisite forms.
3.43.12
न चास्य सदृशो राजन्
दृष्टपूर्वो मृगो मया ।
तेजसा क्षमया दीप्त्या
यथाऽयं मृगसत्तमः ॥
O King! I have never seen any other deer like this one in the past, which is so fit, radiant and tamable.
3.43.13
नानावर्णविचित्राङ्गो
रत्नबिन्दुसमाचितः ।
द्योतयन्वनमव्यग्रम्
शोभते शशिसन्निभः ॥
With its ease and loveliness, and with its multi-colored limbs and body speckled with gem-like spots, it makes the Vana shine like the moon does.
3.43.14
अहो रूपमहो लक्ष्मीः
स्वरसम्पच्च शोभना ।
मृगोऽद्भुतो विचित्राङ्गो
हृदयं हरतीव मे ॥
What a shape! What a luster! Even its sound is enchanting! This deer is really amazing. With its picturesque body it has taken my heart captive.
3.43.15
यदि ग्रहणमभ्येति
जीवन्नेव मृगस्तव ।
आश्चर्यभूतं भवति
विस्मयं जनयिष्यति ॥
If you can catch this deer alive, it would be wonderful and it will be a source of marvel!
3.43.16
समाप्तवनवासानाम्
राज्यस्थानां च नः पुनः ।
अन्तःपुरविभूषार्थो
मृग एष भविष्यति ॥
After the exile in the Vana ends and when we are back in the kingdom, this deer will prove to be a jewel adorning the inner quarters.
3.43.17
भरतस्यार्यपुत्रस्य
श्वश्रूणां मम च प्रभो ।
मृगरूपमिदं व्यक्तम्
विस्मयं जनयिष्यति ॥
O lord! It will certainly be a source of great marvel for Bharata, the son of nobility, for the mothers-in-law and also for me.
3.43.18
जीवन्न यदि तेऽभ्येति
ग्रहणं मृगसत्तमः ।
अजिनं नरशार्दूल
रुचिरं मे भविष्यति ॥
O tiger among men! If this cannot be caught alive by you, I would at least have its beautiful skin (for a prize).
3.43.19
निहतस्यास्य सत्त्वस्य
जाम्बूनदमयत्वचि ।
शष्पबृस्यां विनीतायाम्
इच्छाम्यहमुपासितुम् ॥
I would like to squat on the golden skin of this animal, if it is killed, spread on a cushion of tender grass.
3.43.20
कामवृत्तमिदं रौद्रम्
स्त्रीणामसदृशं मतम् ।
वपुषा त्वस्य सत्त्वस्य
विस्मयो जनितो मम ॥
Feeling obsessed with a wicked desire like this is regarded as unbecoming of a woman. But the body of this animal engenders marvel in me.
3.43.21
तेन काञ्चनवर्णेन
मणिप्रवरशृङ्गिणा ।
तरुणादित्यवर्णेन
नक्षत्रपथवर्चसा ।
बभूव राघवस्यापि
मनो विस्मयमागतम् ॥
Even Rāghava’s mind was caught in a daze of wonder at the sight of that golden-hued deer with the most exquisite gems for its horns, shining with hues of the young morning sun, and with the splendor of the star constellations.
3.43.22
एवं सीतावचः श्रुत्वा
तं दृष्ट्वा मृगमद्भुतम् ।
लोभितस्तेन रूपेण
सीतया च प्रचोदितः ।
उवाच राघवो हृष्टो
भ्रातरं लक्ष्मणं वचः ॥
Hearing the words of Seetā and seeing that amazing deer, he was also enticed by its wonderful shape. Urged earnestly by Seetā, a happy Rāghava said to his brother Lakshmaṇa:
3.43.24
पश्य लक्ष्मण वैदेह्याः
स्पृहां मृगगतामिमाम् ।
रूपश्रेष्ठतया ह्येष
मृगोऽद्य न भविष्यति ।
न वने नन्दनोद्धेशे
न चैत्ररथसंश्रये ।
कुतःपृथिव्यां सौमित्रे
योऽस्य कश्चित्समो मृगः ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! See how excited Vaidēhee is about this deer! A deer of exquisite shape like this would never again exist; neither in this Vana, nor in the Nandana Vana, nor in the gardens of Caitraratha. O Sowmitri! would there be a deer that is equivalent to this, anywhere on this earth?
3.43.26
प्रतिलोमानुलोमाश्च
रुचिरा रोमराजयः ।
शोभन्ते मृगमाश्रित्य
चित्राः कनकबिन्दवः ॥
The hair on its body curled up and down, is lovely, dotted as it is with golden spots.
3.43.27
पश्यास्य जृम्भमाणस्य
दीप्तामग्निशिखोपमाम् ।
जिह्वां मुखान्निस्सरन्तीम्
मेघादिव शतह्रदाम् ॥
Look at its blazing flame-like tongue that hangs out from its mouth as it yawns, like lightning from a cloud.
3.43.28
मसारगल्लर्कमुखः
शङ्खमुक्तानिभोदरः ।
कस्य नामाभिरूप्योऽसौ
न मनो लोभयेन्मृगः ॥
Whose mind will not be tempted by this indescribable creature, with its face like a goblet of sapphire and belly reminding of pearl and conch?
3.43.29
कस्य रूपमिदं दृष्ट्वा
जाम्बूनदमयं प्रभो ।
नानारत्नमयं दिव्यम्
न मनो विस्मयं व्रजेत् ॥
O lord! Whose mind will not be taken by amazement at the sight of this splendid golden deer, that seems to be studded with every kind of gem?
3.43.30
मांसहेतोरपि मृगान्
विनोदार्थं च धन्विन: ।
घ्नन्ति लक्ष्मण राजानो
मृगयायां महावने ॥
For the sake of meat as well as for practicing archery, kings go hunting in great Vanas killing the animals.
3.43.31
धनानि व्यवसायेन
विचीयन्ते महावने ।
धातवो विविधाश्चापि
मणिरत्नसुवर्णिनः ॥
It is only with effort that various kinds of minerals, gems and gold, the wealth of the great Vana, are collected.
3.43.32
तत्सारमखिलं नॄणाम्
धनं निचयवर्धनम् ।
मनसा चिन्तितं सर्वम्
यथा शुक्रस्य लक्ष्मण ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! All that invaluable wealth enhances the coffers (of the kings), just as all the (unfulfilled) wishes of men enhance Ṡukra’s treasure.
What happens to the funds granted to an institution or department or a program, if it is dissolved by the time the funds are received?
There is a saying that if people wish for something and die before it is granted, it will go to Ṡukra, the teacher of Rākshasas.
3.43.33
अर्थी येनार्थकृत्येन
संव्रजत्यविचारयन् ।
तमर्थमर्थशास्त्रज्ञाः
प्राहुरर्थ्याश्च लक्ष्मण ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! Scholars of the science of wealth and the experts in garnering it agree that true wealth is that which is pursued by those who truly yearn for it, forging ahead with no hesitation whatsoever.
3.43.34
एतस्य मृगरत्नस्य
परार्ध्ये काञ्चनत्वचि ।
उपवेक्ष्यति वैदेही
मया सह सुमध्यमा ॥
Vaidēhee of slender waist shall sit, along with me, on the wonderful golden skin of this gem of a deer!
3.43.35
न कादली न प्रियकी
न प्रवेणी न चाविकी ।
भवेदेतस्य सदृशी
स्पर्शनेनेति मे मतिः ॥
I think neither the touch of the skin of Kādalee deer, nor that of Priyakee type, nor that of Pravēṇee type, nor that of a sheep, can rival that of this deer.
3.43.36
एष चैव मृगश्श्रीमान्
यश्च दिव्यो नभश्चरः ।
उभावेतौ मृगौ दिव्यौ
तारामृगमहीमृगौ ॥
There are only two splendid deer, this exquisite one and the one in the moon. This one on the earth and that one in the sky are the only true deer (that are worthy of mention).
3.43.37
यदि वाऽयं तथा यन्माम्
भवेद्वदसि लक्ष्मण ।
मायैषा राक्षसस्येति
कर्तव्योस्य वधो मया ॥
O Lakshmaṇa, even if this is a trick of Rākshasa, as you say to me, it deserves to be killed.
3.43.38
एतेन हि नृशंसेन
मारीचेनाकृतात्मना ।
वने विचरता पूर्वम्
हिंसिता मुनिपुङ्गवाः ॥
It is this cruel and evil Māreeca that harrowed the eminent Munis when he used to roam in the Vana.
3.43.39
उत्थाय बहवो येन
मृगयायां जनाधिपाः ।
निहताः परमेष्वासाः
तस्माद्वध्यस्त्वयं मृगः ॥
Many kings who were great archers were also killed by him in the past, when he accosted them while they were hunting. Hence also, this animal deserves to be killed.
3.43.40
पुरस्तादिह वातापिः
परिभूय तपस्विनः ।
उदरस्थो द्विजान्हन्ति
स्वगर्भोऽश्वतरीमिव ॥
Vātāpi, in the past, used to approach Tapasvis with little regard and kill them, emerging from their stomachs, just as certain baby mules do.
3.43.41
स कदाचिच्चिराल्लोभात्
आससाद महामुनिम् ।
अगस्त्यं तेजसा युक्तम्
भक्षस्तस्य बभूव ह ॥
Then finally, one day he approached, in his greed, Agastya, a great Muni of great power, and became his meal.
3.43.42
समुत्थाने व तद्रूपम्
कर्तुकामं समीक्ष्य तम् ।
उत्स्मयित्वा तु भगवान्
वातापिमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing him trying to get up and regain his form, the Bhagawān said to him laughing:
3.43.43
त्वयाऽविगण्य वातापे
परिभूतास्स्वतेजसा ।
जीवलोके द्विजश्रेष्ठाः
तस्मादसि जरां गतः ॥
Hey Vātāpi! Many eminent Brāhmaṇas have met their end in this world because of your power and utter disregard. So, here you go, completely digested!
3.43.44
तदेवं न भवेद्रक्षः
वातापिरिव लक्ष्मण ।
मद्विधं योऽतिमन्येत
धर्मनित्यं जितेन्द्रियम् ।
भवेद्धतोऽयं वातापिः
अगस्त्येनेव मां गतः ॥
Just like Vātāpi, this Rākshasa is also not going to last. If he transgresses against the person like me, who always adheres to Dharma and who has mastered his senses, he will meet with the same fate as Vātāpi had met from Agastya.
3.43.45
इह त्वं भव संनद्दो
यन्त्रितो रक्ष मैथिलीम् ।
अस्यामायत्तमस्माकम्
यत्कृत्यं रघुनन्दन ॥
O delight of Raghu dynasty, stay here in full alert and guard Maithili with all your attention, for whatever we have to do depends on (keeping) her (safe).
3.43.46
अहमेनं वधिष्यामि
ग्रहीष्याम्यपि वा मृगम् ।
यावद्गच्छामि सौमित्रे
मृगमानयितुं द्रुतम् ॥
I will kill this deer or catch it. O Sowmitri! Let me go quick to get the animal.
3.43.47
पश्य लक्ष्मण वैदेहीम्
मृगत्वचि गतस्पृहाम् ।
त्वचा प्रधानया ह्येष
मृगोऽद्य न भविष्यति ॥
O Lakshmaṇa, keep your eye on Vaidēhee, who has lost her mind over the hide of this deer. Now, since the hide is of primary interest, this deer is not going to survive.
3.43.48
अप्रमत्तेन ते भाव्यम्
आश्रमस्थेन सीतया ।
यावत्पृषतमेकेन
सायकेन निहन्म्यहम् ।
हत्वैतच्चर्म चादाय
शीघ्रमेष्यामि लक्ष्मण ॥
Your job is to stay in full alert in the Āṡrama along with Seetā. I shall kill this spotted deer with a single arrow. After killing it, I will take its hide and return quickly, O Lakshmaṇa!
3.43.50
प्रदक्षिणेनातिबलेन पक्षिणा
जटायुषा बुद्धिमता च लक्ष्मण ।
भवाप्रमत्तः परिगृह्य मैथिलीं
प्रतिक्षणं सर्वत एव शङ्कितः ॥
Along with the exceedingly strong and intelligent bird Jaṭāyu, keep constant vigil, O Lakshmaṇa, prepared to meet danger at any moment from any quarter, with Maithili in your charge.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे त्रिचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty third Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
