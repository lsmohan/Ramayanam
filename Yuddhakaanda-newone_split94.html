Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 94
In this Sarga, the Rākshasis, some widowed, some with their children dead and others with their near and dear slain, wail. They recall with resentment, everything that had happened from Ṡūrpaṇakhā making advances to Rāma. They wonder with dismay why Rāvaṇa did not realize the power of Rāma, even after witnessing the destruction of powerful Veeras like Virādha, Khara, Dūshaṇa, Kabandha, Vāli, Kumbhakarṇa and even Indrajit. They recall that Rāvaṇa obtained immunity from death at the hands of Dēvas, Dānavas and Rākshasas but not at the hands of humans. They hug each other and cry bitterly.
6.94.1
तानि नागसहस्राणि
सारोहाणि च वाजिनाम् ।
रथानांत्वग्निवर्णानाम्
सध्वजानां सहस्रशः ।
राक्षसानां सहस्राणि
गदापरिघयोधिनाम् ।
काञ्चनध्वजचित्राणाम्
शूराणां कामरूपिणाम् ।
निहतानि शरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
तप्तकाञ्चनभूषणैः ।
रावणेन प्रयुक्तानि
रामेणाक्लिष्टकर्मणा ।
दृष्ट्वा श्रुत्वा च सम्भ्रान्ता
हतशेषा निशाचराः ।
राक्षसीश्च समागम्य
दीनाश्चिन्तापरिप्लुताः ॥
Having themselves witnessed or heard of thousands of elephants and horses along with their riders, chariots in their thousands blazing like fire along with their flagstaffs, Rākshasa warriors in thousands armed with maces and Parighas, and others carrying a variety of flagstaffs, and the warriors who could take any form at will sent by Rāvaṇa all struck down by Rāma who did everything with ease, with his sharp arrows that were adorned with gold purified in fire, the Night-rovers who were left alive joined the Rākshasis, deeply disturbed, downcast and overwhelmed with grief.
6.94.5
विधवा हतपुत्राश्च
क्रोशन्त्यो हतबान्धवाः ।
राक्षस्यः सह सङ्गम्य
दुःखार्ताः पर्यदेवयन् ॥
The Rākshasis, some widowed, some with their children dead and others with their near and dear slain, joined each other, crying and lamenting, oppressed by grief.
6.94.6
कथं शूर्पणखा वृद्धा
कराला निर्णतोदरी ।
आससाद वने रामम्
कन्दर्पमिव रूपिणम् ।
सुकुमारं महासत्त्वम्
सर्वभूतहिते रतम् ।
तं दृष्ट्वा लोकनिन्द्या सा
हीनरूपा प्रकामिता ॥
How could Ṡūrpaṇakhā, the potbellied old hag seek out in the forest Rāma, who is as handsome as Kandarpa with limbs delicately formed, but who is also mighty in his strength, and seeks the welfare of all creatures? How could she, who has no figure and who is condemned by everyone, bring herself to love him, merely by seeing him?
6.94.8
कथं सर्वगुणैर्हीना
गुणवन्तं महौजसम् ।
सुमुखं दुर्मुखी रामम्
कामयामास राक्षसी ॥
How could she, who lacks every single virtue seek him of all virtues and extraordinary power? How could this Rākshasi of ugly face make advances to Rāma of charming face?
6.94.9
जनस्यास्याल्पभाग्यत्वात्
वलिनी श्वेतमूर्धजा ।
अकार्यमपहास्यं च
सर्वलोकविगर्हितम् ।
राक्षसानां विनाशाय
दूषणस्य खरस्य च ।
चकाराप्रतिरूपा सा
राघवस्य प्रधर्षणम् ॥
It is because of the misfortune of these people and only for the destruction of the Rākshasas including Dūshaṇa and Khara, that this woman of unworthy figure with white hair and wrinkles dared to do this ridiculous thing of thrusting herself upon Rāghava that is worthy of condemnation from everyone in this world, which should have never been done.
6.94.11
तन्निमित्तमिदं वैरम्
रावणेन कृतं महत् ।
वधाय सीता साऽऽनीता
दशग्रीवेण रक्षसा ॥
It is because of her that Rāvaṇa incurred this deadly enmity. The Ten-necked Rākshasa abducted Seetā for his own destruction.
6.94.12
न च सीतां दशग्रीवः
प्राप्नोति जनकात्मजाम् ।
बद्धं बलवता वैरम्
अक्षयं राघवेण च ॥
The Ten-necked does not get Seetā, the daughter of Janaka, but only this endless enmity with Rāghava of immense strength.
6.94.13
वैदेहीं प्रार्थयानं तम्
विराधं प्रेक्ष्य राक्षसम् ।
हतमेकेन रामेण
पर्याप्तं तन्निदर्शनम् ॥
Is seeing the Rākshasa Virādha, who sought Vaidēhee, struck down by Rāma all by himself not enough, for an indication (of who he is)?
6.94.14
चतुर्दश सहस्राणि
रक्षसां भीमकर्मणाम् ।
निहतानि जनस्थाने
शरैरग्निशिखोपमैः ॥
Fourteen thousand Rākshasas of formidable exploits were destroyed in Janasthāna by arrows that burnt like tongues of flame.
6.94.15
खरश्च निहतः सङ्ख्ये
दूषणस्त्रिशिरास्तथा ।
शरैरादित्यसङ्काशैः
पर्याप्तं तन्निदर्शनम् ॥
Khara was killed in that battle, and so were Dūshaṇa and Triṡira with arrows that blazed like the sun. Is that not enough of an indication (of Rāma's power)?
6.94.16
हतो योजनबाहुश्च
कबन्धो रुधिराशनः ।
क्रोधान्नादं नदन् सोऽथ
पर्याप्तं तन्निदर्शनम् ॥
Is not the dead Kabandha of Yōjana long arms who fed on blood and let off deafening shouts in wrath enough of an indication (of Rāma's power)?
6.94.17
जघान बलिनं रामः
सहस्रनयनात्मजम् ।
वालिनं मेरुसङ्काशम्
पर्याप्तं तन्निदर्शनम् ॥
Rāma slew Vāli, the son of the Thousand-Eyed, who was strong like the Mēru mountain. Was that not an indication (of Rāma's power)?
6.94.18
ऋष्यमूके वसन् शैले
दीनो भग्नमनोरथः ।
सुग्रीवः स्थापितो राज्ये
पर्याप्तं तन्निदर्शनम् ॥
He installed Sugreeva, who was whiling his time on the Ṛshyamūka mountain, dejected and dreams dashed, in the kingdom. Was that not an Indication (of Rāma's power)?
6.94.19
धर्मार्थसहितं वाक्यम्
सर्वेषां रक्षसां हितम् ।
युक्तं विभीषणेनोक्तम्
मोहात्तस्य न रोचते ॥
Lost in a world of his own, he could not bring himself to heed the right advice given by Vibheeshaṇa that promoted Dharma and Artha and the welfare of all Rākshasas.
6.94.20
विभीषणवचः कुर्यात्
यदि स्म धनदानुजः ।
श्मशानभूता दुःखार्ता
नेयं लङ्का पुरी भवेत् ॥
If only Kubēra’s younger brother had followed the advice of Vibheeshaṇa, the city of Laṅkā would not have been reduced to a cremation ground (like this), oppressed by grief and misery.
6.94.21
कुम्भकर्णं हतं श्रुत्वा
राघवेण महाबलम् ।
अतिकायं च दुर्धर्षम्
लक्ष्मणेन हतं पुनः ।
प्रियं चेन्द्रजितं पुत्रम्
रावणो नावबुध्यते ॥
Even after hearing of the death of the extraordinarily strong Kumbhakarṇa at the hands of Rāghava, and of the slaying of the invincible Atikāya and of his darling son Indrajit by Lakshmaṇa, Rāvaṇa does not realize (what he is up against).
6.94.22
मम पुत्रो मम भ्राता
मम भर्ता रणे हतः ।
इत्येवं श्रूयते शब्दो
राक्षसानां कुले कुले ॥
‘I have lost my son’, ‘I have lost my brother ’, ‘I have lost my husband’ - such were the doleful cries heard in every Rākshasa household.
6.94.23
रथाश्चाश्वाश्च नागाश्च
हताः शतसहस्रशः ।
रणे रामेण शूरेण
राक्षसाश्च पदातयः ॥
Chariots, horses, elephants and foot-soldiers of the Rākshasa forces have been slain in their hundreds and thousands by the valiant Rāma in the battle.
6.94.24
रुद्रो वा यदि वा विष्णुः
महेन्द्रो वा शतक्रतुः ।
हन्ति नो रामरूपेण
यदि वा स्वयमन्तकः ॥
It must be either Rudra or Vishnu, or Mahēndra, the performer of the hundred sacrifices, or Death itself that kills us in the guise of Rāma.
6.94.25
हतप्रवीरा रामेण
निराशा जीविते वयम् ।
अपश्यन्तो भयस्यान्तम्
अनाथा विलपामहे ॥
With all our great warriors slain by Rāma, we have lost all hopes of life, and seeing no end to fear, we wail helplessly.
6.94.26
रामहस्ताद्दशग्रीवः
शूरो दत्तमहावरः ।
इदं भयं महाघोरम्
उत्पन्नं नावबुध्यते ॥
The Ten-necked, being a great hero, and the recipient of great boons, does not realize this extremely dreadful and very real threat from Rāma.
6.94.27
न देवा न च गन्धर्वा
न पिशाचा न राक्षसाः ।
उपसृष्टं परित्रातुम्
शक्ता रामेण संयुगे ॥
Neither the Dēvas nor the Gandharvas, neither the Piṡācas nor the Rākshasas, can save anyone that falls within the reach of Rāma on the battlefield.
6.94.28
उत्पाताश्चापि दृश्यन्ते
रावणस्य रणे रणे ।
कथयिष्यन्ति रामेण
रावणस्य निबर्हणम् ॥
Dreadful omens have been seen in battle after battle foreboding the death of Rāvaṇa at the hands of Rāma.
6.94.29
पितामहेन प्रीतेन
देवदानवराक्षसैः ।
रावणस्याभयं दत्तम्
मानुषेभ्यो न याचितम् ॥
Rāvaṇa was freed from the fear of Dēvas, Dānavas and Rākshasas by the pleased Grandsire; he did not even ask for the same from humans.
6.94.30
तदिदं मानुषं मन्ये
प्राप्तं निस्संशयं भयम् ।
जीवितान्तकरं घोरम्
रक्षसां रावणस्य च ॥
And surely, the terrible threat from humans, which seems set to put an end to the lives of Rākshasas and Rāvaṇa, has now become a reality.
6.94.31
पीड्यमानास्तु बलिना
वरदानेन रक्षसा ।
दीप्तैस्तपोभिर्विबुधाः
पितामहमपूजयन् ॥
The Dēvas, who were harrowed by the Rākshasa who became strong with the boons from the Grandsire, supplicated the Grandsire, performing fierce Tapa.
6.94.32
देवतानां हितार्थाय
महात्मा वै पितामहः ।
उवाच देवताः सर्वा
इदं तुष्टो महद्वचः ॥
The pleased Grandsire, the Mahātma, said these significant words to all the Dēvas to bring them relief:
6.94.33
अद्यप्रभृति लोकांस्त्रीन्
सर्वे दानवराक्षसाः ।
भयेन प्रावृता नित्यम्
विचरिष्यन्ति शाश्वतम् ॥
From this day onwards all Dānavas and Rākshasas will be gripped with fear forever, as they move about in the three worlds.
6.94.34
दैवतैस्तु समागम्य
सर्वैश्चेन्द्रपुरोगमैः ।
वृषध्वजस्त्रिपुरहा
महादेवः प्रसादितः ॥
All the Dēvas, getting together led by Indra, earned the grace of the Great God (Ṡiva), the Destroyer of the Three Cities who has a bull as the emblem on his flag.
6.94.35
प्रसन्नस्तु महादेवो
देवानेतद्वचोऽब्रवीत् ।
उत्पत्स्यति हितार्थं वो
नारी रक्षःक्षयावहा ॥
The pleased Great God said these words to the Dēvas: 'there will be born a woman who will bring destruction upon the Rākshasas, and relief to you.'
6.94.36
एषा देवैः प्रयुक्ता तु
क्षुद्यथा दानवान् पुरा ।
भक्षयिष्यति नः सीता
राक्षसघ्नी सरावणान् ॥
Like hunger consumed the Dānavas once upon a time, she, the nemesis of Rākshasas, deployed by the Dēvas, will consume all of us including Rāvaṇa.
6.94.37
रावणस्यापनीतेन
दुर्विनीतस्य दुर्मतेः ।
अयं निष्ठानको घोरः
शोकेन समभिप्लुतः ॥
It is because of the missteps of Rāvaṇa, the evil-minded one, who has no humility, that this terrible calamity has beset us, plunging us in grief.
6.94.38
तं न पश्यामहे लोके
यो नः शरणदो भवेत् ।
राघवेणोपसृष्टानाम्
कालेनेव युगक्षये ॥
There is no one that we can find in the whole wide world that can save us now that we are under attack by Rāghava who is like Death, marauding at the end of the Yuga.
6.94.39
नास्ति नः शरणं कश्चित्
भये महति तिष्ठताम् ।
दवाग्निवेष्टितानां हि
करेणूनां यथा वने ॥
Like a she-elephant beset by a wild fire in the forest, we are now gripped with a great fear with no refuge whatsoever.
6.94.40
प्राप्तकालं कृतं तेन
पौलस्त्येन महात्मना ।
यत एव भयं दृष्टम्
तमेव शरणं गतः ॥
The great scion of the Pulastyas (Vibheeshaṇa) did what is right at this time, by seeking refuge with the very same one from whom he saw the threat.
6.94.41
इतीव सर्वा रजनीचरस्त्रियः
परस्परं सम्परिरभ्य बाहुभिः ।
विषेदुरार्ता भयभारपीडिता
विनेदुरुच्चैश्च तदा सुदारुणम् ॥
Thus the women folk of the Night-rovers, with their arms thrown around each other, lamented helplessly, oppressed by the heaviness of fear, and wailed bitterly in loud voices.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे चतुर्नवतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninety fourth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
