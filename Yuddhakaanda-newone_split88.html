Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 88
In this Sarga, Lakshmaṇa and Indrajit engage in a fierce and tumultuous fight, each taunting, deriding and challenging the other. They blot the sky with their arrows. They look like trees in red flower, bleeding profusely. Each being the foremost of bowmen, each having mastered Astras, each being valiant, powerful, strong, unrelenting and each bent on winning the fight, the fight goes on for a long time, with neither of them showing any signs of fatigue.
6.88.1
विभीषणवचः श्रुत्वा
रावणिः क्रोधमूर्च्छितः ।
अब्रवीत् परुषं वाक्यम्
वेगेनाभ्युत्पपात ह ।
उद्यतायुधनिस्त्रिंशो
रथे सुसमलङ्कृते ।
कालाश्वयुक्ते महति
स्थितः कालान्तकोपमः ।
महाप्रमाणमुद्यम्य
विपुलं वेगवद्दृढम् ।
धनुर्भीमं परामृश्य
शरांश्चामित्रशातनान् ॥
Hearing the words of Vibheeshaṇa, Rāvaṇa's son, beside himself with rage, responded in harsh words and launched a swift attack raising his weapons and sword, holding the sturdy, hefty, big, powerful and frightful bow, his hands on arrows that oppress the foe, seated in the well-decorated chariot yoked with black horses, looking like one who puts an end to Time.
6.88.4
तं ददर्श महेष्वासो
रथे सुसमलङ्कृतः ।
अलङ्कृतममित्रघ्नम्
राघवस्यानुजं बली ।
हनुमत्पृष्ठमासीनम्
उदयस्थरविप्रभम् ॥
With a big bow in hand, welladorned with many jewels, and seated in his chariot, he saw the strong younger brother of Rāghava, a destroyer of the foe, who was equally adorned, and was carried by Hanumān on his back looking like the bright sun rising on the eastern mountain.
6.88.5
उवाचैनं समारब्धः
सौमित्रिं सविभीषणम्
तांश्च वानरशार्दूलान्॥
Geared up for the fight, he said to Sowmitri and Vibheeshaṇa and to all those tigers among Vānaras:
6.88.6
पश्यध्वं मे पराक्रमम्
अद्य मत्कार्मुकोत्सृष्टम् ।
शरवर्षं दुरासदम्
मुक्तं वर्षमिवाकाशे ।
वारयिष्यथ संयुगे
You shall see my prowess today. Try, if you can, to ward off the torrential downpour of arrows that I shall shoot from my bow against which none can stand.
6.88.7
अद्य मत्कार्मुकोत्सृष्टम्
शरवर्षं दुरासदम् ।
मुक्तं वर्षमिवाकाशे
धारयिष्यध संयुगे ॥
Today, in this battle, you are going to experience a shower of arrows shot from my bow, that will feel like a downpour from the sky.
6.88.8
अद्य वो मामका बाणा
महाकार्मुकनिस्सृताः ।
विधमिष्यन्ति गात्राणि
तूलराशिमिवानलः ॥
Today, my arrows, released from the great bow, are going to destroy your bodies, like fire does a pile of cotton.
6.88.9
तीक्ष्णसायकनिर्भिन्नान्
शूलशक्त्यष्टितोमरैः ।
अद्य वो गमयिष्यामि
सर्वानेव यमक्षयम् ॥
I shall send you all today, cut to pieces with my sharp arrows, spears, Ṡaktis, Yashṭis and Tōmaras, to the abode of Yama.
6.88.10
सृजतः शरवर्षाणि
क्षिप्रहस्तस्य संयुगे ।
जीमूतस्येव नदतः
कः स्थास्यति ममाग्रतः ॥
Who can stand against me, when I roar like a cloud sending a downpour of arrows with my deft hands in this fight?
6.88.11
रात्रियुद्धे तदा पूर्वम्
वज्राशनिसमैः शरैः ।
शायितौ स्थो मया भूमौ
विसञ्ज्ञौ सपुरस्सरौ ॥
It is only a little while ago, in that fight in the night, that both you and your attendants fell on the ground senseless, knocked down by my arrows that fell like Vajra and thunderbolt!
6.88.12
स्मृतिर्न तेऽस्ति वा मन्ये
व्यक्तं यातो यमक्षयम् ।
आशीविषमिव क्रुद्धम्
यन्मां योद्धुं व्यवस्थितः ॥
I think you do not remember it, and clearly want to go to the abode of Yama, which is why you stand here to fight with me who is in rage like a venomous serpent.
6.88.13
तच्छुत्वा राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
गर्जितं राघवस्तदा ।
अभीतवदनः क्रुद्धो
रावणिं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing that roar of the lord of Rākshasas, Rāghava, with the least of trepidation evident on his face, said these words to the son of Rāvaṇa, in rage:
6.88.14
उक्तश्च दुर्गमः पारः
कार्याणां राक्षस त्वया ।
कार्याणां कर्मणा पारम्
यो गच्छति स बुद्धिमान् ॥
You are hallucinating about things that will never come to fruition, O Rākshasa! Only one who can pursue all the way to the end, of what needs to be done, can be called a genius.
6.88.15
स त्वमर्थस्य हीनार्थो
दुरवापस्य केनचित् ।
वाचा व्याहृत्य जानीषे
कृतार्थोऽस्मीति दुर्मते ॥
Being at a loss about what you need to do, which is anyway impossible for anyone, you merely say it aloud in words and think that you have accomplished it, you wretched one!
6.88.16
अन्तर्धानगतेनाजौ
यस्त्वयाऽऽचरितस्तदा ।
तस्कराचरितो मार्गो
नैष वीरनिषेवितः ॥
What you did on the battlefield the other day, hiding yourself from our view, befits a slinking thief, not a heroic warrior.
6.88.17
यथा बाणपथं प्राप्य
स्थितोऽहं तव राक्षस ।
दर्शयस्वाद्य तत्तेजो
वाचा त्वं किं विकत्थसे ॥
Now that I am here within the range of your arrows, show me the power that you boast of, O Rākshasa! Why do you go on bragging?
6.88.18
एवमुक्तो धनुर्भीमम्
परामृश्य महाबलः ।
ससर्ज निशितान् बाणान्
इन्द्रजित् समितिञ्जयः ॥
Spoken to thus, Indrajit of immense strength, who won many a battle, laid his hand on the bow, and shot sharp arrows.
6.88.19
ते निसृष्टा महावेगाः
शराः सर्पविषोपमाः ।
सम्प्राप्य लक्ष्मणं पेतुः
श्वसन्त इव पन्नगाः ॥
Those extremely fast arrows that were shot thus, which were deadly as snake venom, reached Lakshmaṇa and fell on him, like hissing serpents.
6.88.20
शरैरतिमहावेगैः
वेगवान् रावणात्मजः ।
सौमित्रिमिन्द्रजिद्युद्धे
विव्याध शुभलक्षणम् ॥
The swift Indrajit, the son of Rāvaṇa, thus struck Sowmitri of auspicious marks in that fight with his extremely fast arrows.
6.88.21
स शरैरतिविद्धाङ्गो
रुधिरेण समुक्षितः ।
शुशुभे लक्ष्मणः श्रीमान्
विधूम इव पावकः ॥
Wounded all over the body by those arrows and covered with blood, the glorious Lakshmaṇa shone like smokeless flame.
6.88.22
इन्द्रजित्त्वात्मनः कर्म
प्रसमीक्ष्याधिगम्य च ।
विनद्य सुमहानादम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Pleased with his own accomplishment, Indrajit went forward, and letting off a loud war-whoop, said these words:
6.88.23
पत्रिणः शितधारास्ते
शरा मत्कार्मुकच्युताः ।
आदास्यन्तेऽद्य सौमित्रे
जीवितं जीवितान्तकाः ॥
Sharp and winged arrows, which bring death, shot from my bow shall take your life today, O Sowmitri!
6.88.24
अद्य गोमायुसङ्घाश्च
श्येनसङ्घाश्च लक्ष्मण ।
गृध्राश्च निपतन्तु त्वाम्
गतासुं निहतं मया ॥
Jackals in packs, eagles in flocks and vultures shall fall upon your body, O Lakshmaṇa, when you fall dead, slain by me.
6.88.25
क्षत्रबन्धुं सदानार्यम्
रामः परमदुर्मतिः ।
भक्तं भ्रातरमद्यैव
त्वां द्रक्ष्यति हतं मया ॥
Rāma, the extremely evil minded one, shall see you, an ignoble base Kshatriya, his highly devoted brother, slain by me this very day.
6.88.26
विस्रस्तकवचं भूमौ
व्यपविद्धशरासनम् ।
हृतोत्तमाङ्गं सौमित्रे
त्वामद्य निहतं मया ॥
(And he shall see) you today fallen on the ground slain by me, with your armor shed down, your bow dropped down, and your head cut off.
6.88.27
इति ब्रुवाणं संरब्धम्
परुषं रावणात्मजम् ।
हेतुमद्वाक्यमत्यर्थम्
लक्ष्मणः प्रत्युवाच ह ॥
To the son of Rāvaṇa, who spoke harsh words thus in wild excitement, Lakshmaṇa responded with reasonable words that made sense:
6.88.28
वाग्बलं त्यज दुर्बुद्धे
क्रूरकर्माऽसि राक्षस ।
अथ कस्माद्वदस्येतत्
सम्पादय सुकर्मणा ॥
O evil-minded one! O Rākshasa! Stop your boasting, you, who are so addicted to cruel deeds! Why do you say all this? Do something that is worth it.
6.88.29
अकृत्वा कत्थसे कर्म
किमर्थमिह राक्षस ।
कुरु तत्कर्म येनाहम्
श्रद्दध्यां तव कत्थनम् ॥
Why do you brag endlessly without actually doing anything of substance, O Rākshasa? Do something for real that makes me believe that your boast is not empty!
6.88.30
अनुक्त्वा परुषं वाक्यम्
किञ्चिदप्यनवाक्षिपन् ।
अविकत्थन् वधिष्यामि
त्वां पश्य पुरुषाधम ॥
Without saying one harsh word, without disparaging you even in the least, and without bragging about myself, you shall see, O vile one, how I will get to slay you!
6.88.31
इत्युक्त्वा पञ्च नाराचान्
आकर्णापूरितान् शितान् ।
निजघान महावेगान्
लक्ष्मणो राक्षसोरसि ॥
Saying this, Lakshmaṇa pulled the bow string up to his ears and shot five sharp Nārāca arrows with great speed, striking the Rākshasa on the chest.
6.88.32
सुपत्रवाजिता बाणा
ज्वलिता इव पन्नगाः ।
नैर्ऋतोरस्यभासन्त
सवितू रश्मयो यथा ॥
Those arrows, gleaming like serpents, sped by the excellent feathers and lodged on the Rākshasa’s chest, shone like rays emanating from the sun.
6.88.33
स शरैराहतस्तेन
सरोषो रावणात्मजः ।
सुप्रयुक्तैस्त्रिभिर्बाणैः
प्रतिविव्याध लक्ष्मणम् ॥
Struck by those arrows, the son of Rāvaṇa, was quite enraged and struck back at Lakshmaṇa with three well-shot arrows.
6.88.34
स बभूव तदा भीमो
नरराक्षससिंहयोः ।
विमर्दस्तुमुलो युद्धे
परस्परजयैषिणोः ॥
Then ensued a fierce and tumultuous fight between the lion among men and lion among Rākshasas, both of whom were determined to win over the other.
6.88.35
उभौ हि बलसम्पन्नौ
उभौ विक्रमशालिनौ ।
उभौ परमदुर्जेयौ
उभावपि सुविक्रान्तौ ।
सर्वशस्त्रास्त्रकोविदौ
अतुल्यबलतेजसौ ॥
Both of them were endowed with strength. Both of them were dashing and daring. Both were unassailable by any means. Both were extremely dashing and daring. Both were masters of all Ṡastras and Astras and both were strong and powerful with no one else equal to them.
6.88.37
युयुधाते महात्मानौ
तदा केसरिणाविव ।
बहूनवसृजन्तौ हि
मार्गणौघानवस्थितौ ।
नरराक्षससिंहौ तौ
प्रहृष्टावभ्ययुध्यताम् ॥
Those two Mahātmas fought like lions, shooting volleys of arrows. That lion among men and the lion among Rākshasas indulged in that fight with all excitement.
6.88.38
ततश्शरान् दाशरथिः
संधायामित्रकर्षणः ।
ससर्ज राक्षसेन्द्राय
कृद्धः सर्प इव श्वसन् ॥
The son of Daṡaratha, who could pull his enemies down with ease, mounted arrows on the bow and shot at the Lord of Rākshasas, hissing like an angry serpent.
6.88.39
तस्य ज्यातलनिरघोषम्
स श्रुत्वा राक्षसाधिपः ।
विवर्णवदनो भूत्वा
लक्ष्मणं समुदैक्षत ॥
But on hearing the thud of bowstring on palm, Rāvaṇa’s son, his face turning pale, (stood) simply staring at Lakshmaṇa.
6.88.40
विषण्णवदनं दृष्ट्वा
राक्षसं रावणात्मजम् ।
सौमित्रिं युद्धसंयुक्तम्
प्रत्युवाच विभीषणः ॥
Seeing that Rākshasa, the son of Rāvaṇa, who was downcast, Vibheeshaṇa encouraged Lakshmaṇa who was engaged in the fight:
6.88.41
निमित्तान्युपपश्यामि
यान्यस्मिन् रावणात्मजे ।
त्वर तेन महाबाहो
भग्न एष न संशयः ॥
I see such signs of weakness in the son of Rāvaṇa that you can easily strike him down now; hurry, O you of mighty arm!
6.88.42
ततस्संधाय सौमित्रिः
शरानाशीविषोपमान् ।
मुमोच निशितांस्तस्मिन्
सर्पानिव विषोल्बणान् ॥
Then Sowmitri took arrows that resembled venomous serpents and shot those sharp ones that were deadly like snakes with venom.
6.88.43
शक्राशनिसमस्पर्शैः
लक्ष्मणेनाहतः शरैः ।
मुहूर्तमभवन्मूढः
सर्वसंक्षुभितेन्द्रियः ॥
Struck by the arrows of Lakshmaṇa that fell like the thunderbolt of Indra, he was stupefied with shock, with all his senses in turmoil.
6.88.44
उपलभ्य मुहूर्तेन
संज्ञांप्रत्यागतेन्द्रियः ।
ददर्शावस्थितं वीरम्
आजौ दशरथात्मजम् ॥
Regaining consciousness after a while, and with all his senses becoming normal he beheld the son of Daṡaratha, a Veera, who stood firmly in that battle.
6.88.45
सोऽभिचक्राम सौमित्रिम्
रोषात्संरक्तलोचनः ।
अब्रवीच्चैनमासाद्य
पुनः स परुषं वचः ॥
His eyes blood-red with anger, he went up to Sowmitri and reaching him, again said these harsh words:
6.88.46
किं न स्मरसि तद्युद्धे
प्रथमे मत्पराक्रमम् ।
निबद्धस्त्वं सह भ्रात्रा
यदा भुवि विवेष्टसे ॥
Have you forgotten my prowess that you witnessed in that first encounter, when you along with your brother, bound by me, writhed on the ground?
6.88.47
युवां खलु महायुद्धे
शक्राशनिसमैः शरैः ।
शायितौ प्रथमं भूमौ
विसञ्ज्ञौ सपुरस्सरौ ॥
Did you not, along with your followers, fall on the ground unconscious in that great battle right away, knocked down by my arrows that fell like the thunderbolt of Indra?
6.88.48
स्मृतिर्वा नास्ति ते मन्ये
व्यक्तं वा यमसादनम् ।
गन्तुमिच्छसि यस्मात्त्वम्
मां धर्षयितुमिच्छसि ॥
I think you have clearly lost your memory, and are wishing to go to the abode of Yama, which is why you dare to challenge me!
6.88.49
यदि ते प्रथमे युद्धे
न दृष्टो मत्पराक्रमः ।
अद्य ते दर्शयिष्यामि
तिष्ठेदानीं व्यवस्थितः ॥
If you have not seen enough of my prowess in that first encounter, I shall show it to you today; stay now, right there!
6.88.50
इत्युक्त्वा सप्तभिबाणैः
अभिविव्याध लक्ष्मणम् ।
दशभिस्तु हनूमन्तम्
तीक्ष्णधारैः शरोत्तमैः ॥
Saying this he struck Lakshmaṇa with seven arrows, and Hanumān with ten best of arrows with razor-sharp edges.
6.88.51
ततः शरशतेनैव
सुप्रयुक्तेन वीर्यवान् ।
क्रोधाद्द्विगुणसंरब्धो
निर्बिभेद विभीषणम् ॥
Then that valiant one, with his rage and impatience doubled, tore apart Vibheeshaṇa, with one hundred perfectly aimed arrows.
6.88.52
तद्दृष्ट्वेन्द्रजिता कर्म
कृतं रामानुजस्तदा ।
अचिन्तयित्वा प्रहसन्
नैतत् किञ्चिदिति ब्रुवन् ।
मुमोच च शरान् घोरान्
सङ्गृह्य नरपुङ्गवः ।
अभीतवदनः क्रुद्धो
रावणिं लक्ष्मणौ युधि ॥
Seeing that feat of Indrajit, the son of Rāvaṇa, and caring two hoots about it, Lakshmaṇa, the younger brother of Rāma, a bull among men, said, laughing and without even winking, ‘Pooh! this is nothing!’ and grabbed formidable arrows and shot them at him.
6.88.54
नैवं रणगताः शूराः
प्रहरन्ते निशाचर ।
लघवश्चाल्पवीर्याश्च
सुखा हीमे शरास्तव ।
नैवं शूरास्तु युध्यन्ते
समरे जयकाङ्क्षिणः ॥
This is not how true warriors on the battlefield strike, O Night-rover! These arrows of yours, light and with little impact, actually feel very pleasant! This is not how warriors who seek victory fight in a battle!
6.88.56
इत्येवं तं ब्रुवन् धन्वी
शरैरभिववर्ष ह ॥
Saying this, the bowman sent a downpour of arrows.
6.88.56
तस्य बाणैः सुविध्वस्तम्
कवचं काञ्चनं महत् ।
व्यशीर्यत रथोपस्थे
ताराजालमिवाम्बरात् ॥
Shivered to pieces by those arrows, the extraordinary gold armor (of Indrajit) fell down on the floor of the chariot, like a cluster of stars from the sky.
6.88.57
विधूतवर्मा नाराचैः
बभूव स कृतव्रणः ।
इन्द्रजित् समरे वीरः
प्रत्यूषे भानुमानिव ॥
Relieved of his armor and his body wounded by the Nārācās in that fight, Indrajit, the Veera, looked like the morning sun.
6.88.58
ततः शरसहस्रेण
सङ्क्रुद्धो रावणात्मजः ।
बिभेद समरे वीरम्
लक्ष्मणं भीमविक्रमः ॥
Thereupon Rāvaṇa’s son of formidable valor, tore apart the valiant Lakshmaṇa in that fight with a thousand arrows, in wrath.
6.88.59
व्यशीर्यत महद्दिव्यम्
कवचं लक्ष्मणस्य च ।
कृतप्रतिकृतान्योन्यम्
बभूवतुरभिद्रुतौ ॥
The splendid armor of Lakshmaṇa also fell down. They kept attacking each other, each one outdoing the other.
6.88.60
अभीक्ष्णं निश्वसन्तौ तौ
युद्ध्येतां तुमुलं युधि ।
शरसङ्कृत्तसर्वाङ्गौ
सर्वतो रुधिरोक्षितौ ॥
Sighing heavily, they fought a tough battle, with all their limbs torn by arrows and their bodies smeared with blood all over.
6.88.61
सुदीर्घकालं तौ वीरौ
अन्योन्यं निशितैः शरैः ।
ततक्षतुर्महात्मानौ
रणकर्मविशारदौ ।
बभूवतुश्चात्मजये
यत्तौ भीमपराक्रमौ ॥
For quite a long while, the two valiant Mahātmas, extremely skilled in martial arts, hacked each other with sharp arrows.
Each one, of fierce prowess, put forward the best of his efforts to ensure his own victory over the other.
6.88.63
तौ शरौघैस्तदा कीर्णौ
निकृत्तकवचध्वजौ ।
स्रवन्तौ रुधिरं चोष्णम्
जलं प्रस्रवणाविव ॥
With their armors and flag staffs knocked down, and their bodies sprayed with volleys of arrows, and hot blood flowing from their bodies they looked like mountains with streams flowing from them.
6.88.64
शरवर्षं ततो घोरम्
मुञ्चतोर्भीमनिस्वनम् ।
सासारयोरिवाकाशे
नीलयोः कालमेघयोः ।
तयोरथ महान् कालो
व्यतीयाद्युध्यमानयोः ।
न च तौ युद्धवैमुख्यम्
श्रमं वाप्युपजग्मतुः ॥
Making a terrible din as they discharged their fierce floods of arrows, like two rainclouds letting down torrents of rain, they went on fighting for a long time, with neither of them tired of fighting or showing a sign of fatigue.
6.88.66
अस्त्राण्यस्त्रविदां श्रेष्ठौ
दर्शयन्तौ पुनःपुनः ।
शरानुच्चावचाकारान्
अन्तरिक्षे बबन्धतुः ॥
Both of them, being the foremost of the masters of Astras brandished their Astras repeatedly, and created a mesh in the sky out of their arrows of various kinds.
6.88.67
व्यपेतदोषमस्यन्तौ
लघु चित्रं च सुष्ठु च ।
उभौ तौ तुमुलं घोरम्
चक्रतुर्नरराक्षसौ ॥
Both the human and the Rākshasa shot (arrows) in a flawless manner at each other in that fierce and tumultuous battle, with ease, style and perfection.
6.88.68
तयोः पृथक्पृथग्भीमः
शुश्रुवे तलनिस्वनः ।
स कम्पं जनयामास
निर्घात इव दारुणः ॥
The terrifying sound of bowstring striking against their palms, each one's distinct, was heard (in endless succession), which, like thunder, made everything shake violently.
6.88.69
तयोः स भ्राजते शब्दः
तदा समरयत्तयोः ।
सुघोरयोर्निःस्वनतोः
गगने मेघयोरिव ॥
The din made by them who were wholly absorbed in the fight, was like the thunder of the clouds that rumbled ferociously in the sky.
6.88.70
सुवर्णपुङ्खैर्नाराचैः
बलवन्तौ कृतव्रणौ ।
प्रसुस्रुवाते रुधिरम्
कीर्तिमन्तौ जये धृतौ ॥
Both of them, of strength and renown, who fought with a firm resolve to win, bled profusely from the wounds made by Nārācās with gold rings on their shafts.
6.88.71
ते गात्रयोर्निपतिता
रुक्मपुङ्खाः शरा युधि ।
असृग्दिग्धा विनिष्पेतुः
विविशुर्धरणीतलम् ।
अन्ये सुनिशितैः शस्त्रैः
आकाशे सञ्जघट्टिरे ।
बभञ्जुश्चिच्छिदुश्चैव
तयोर्बाणाः सहस्रशः ॥
The arrows with gold rings on their shafts, in that fight, struck their bodies, came out streaked with blood, and penetrated into the ground. Others clashed against sharp weapons in mid air, while thousands of those arrows smashed and shattered each other.
6.88.73
स बभूव रणो घोरः
तयोर्बाणमयश्चयः ।
अग्निभ्यामिव दीप्ताभ्याम्
सत्रे कुशमयश्चयः ॥
That battle turned fierce with volleys of their arrows flying all over, blazing like burning bundles of reeds of Kuṡa grass in a Hōma.
6.88.74
तयोः कृतव्रणौ देहौ
शुशुभाते महात्मनोः ।
सपुष्पाविव निष्पत्रौ
वने शाल्मलिकिंशुकौ ॥
The wounded bodies of those Mahātmas shone like Ṡālmali and Kiṃṡuka trees in flower in the forest, sans leaves.
6.88.75
चक्रुतुस्तुमुलं घोरम्
सन्निपातं मुहुर्मुहुः ।
इन्द्रजिल्लक्ष्मणश्चैव
परस्परवधैषिणौ ॥
Indrajit and Lakshmaṇa, both bent on winning, locked themselves in a deadly combat again and again, in that tumultuous fight.
6.88.76
लक्ष्मणो रावणिं युद्धे
रावणिश्चापि लक्ष्मणम् ।
अन्योन्यं तावभिघ्नन्तौ
न श्रमं प्रत्यपद्यताम् ॥
Lakshmaṇa assaulted Rāvaṇa's son, and Rāvaṇa's son, Lakshmaṇa; showing no signs of fatigue, they kept on striking at each other.
6.88.77
बाणजालैः शरीरस्थैः
अवगाढैस्तरस्विनौ ।
शुशुभाते महावीर्यौ
प्ररूढाविव पर्वतौ ॥
With masses of arrows stuck deep into their bodies, those two men of extraordinary valor and power looked like mountains covered with trees.
6.88.78
तयो रुधिरसिक्तानि
संवृतानि शरैर्भृशम् ।
बभ्राजुः सर्वगात्राणि
ज्वलन्त इव पावकाः ॥
All their limbs, drenched in blood and covered all over with arrows, shone like blazing fire.
6.88.79
तयोरथ महान् कालो
व्यतीयाद्युध्यमानयोः ।
न च तौ युद्धवैमुख्यम्
श्रमं वाप्युपजग्मतुः ॥
A long time passed, while they fought showing no sign of war-weariness or fatigue.
6.88.80
अथ समरपरिश्रमं निहन्तुं
समरमुखेष्वजितस्य लक्ष्मणस्य ।
प्रियहितमुपपादयन्महात्माः
समरमुपेत्य विभीषणोऽवतस्थे ॥
To provide relief from the fatigue of fighting to Lakshmaṇa, who never knew defeat on the battlefront, and to serve his interest and make him feel better, Vibheeshaṇa joined that fight and took a stand.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे अष्टाशीतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighty eighth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
