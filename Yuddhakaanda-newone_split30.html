Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 30
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa is slightly shaken hearing the account given by Ṡārdūla about the Vānara army. He then asks Ṡārdūla to give a detailed account of the Vānara chiefs. Ṡārdūla describes each of the Vānara chiefs by name and makes it clear to Rāvaṇa that he has to either give back Seetā or prepare for a fight.
6.30.1
ततस्तमक्षोभ्यबलम्
लङ्काधिपतये चराः ।
सुवेले राघवं शैले
निविष्टं प्रत्यवेदयन् ॥
The spies submitted to the Lord of Laṅkā that Rāghava had established himself on Suvēla mountain with an army that could not be shaken in the least.
6.30.2
चाराणां रावणः श्रुत्वा
प्राप्तं रामं महाबलम् ।
जातोद्वेगोऽभवत् किञ्चित्
शार्दूलं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing from the spies about the arrival of Rāma of immense strength, Rāvaṇa was shaken a little and said these words to Ṡārdūla:
6.30.3
अयथावच्च ते वर्णो
दीनश्चासि निशाचर ।
नासि कच्चिदमित्राणाम्
क्रुद्धानां वशमागतः ॥
The color on your face is not what it should be, and you look woebegone, O Night-rover! Did you by any chance fall into the hands of the angry foe?
6.30.4
इति तेनानुशिष्टस्तु
वाचं मन्दमुदीरयत् ।
तदा राक्षसशार्दूलम्
शार्दूलो भयविह्वलः ॥
Thus inquired, Ṡārdūla, befuddled with fright, responded slowly in a dull voice to that tiger among Rākshasas:
6.30.5
न ते चारयितुं शक्या
राजन् वानरपुङ्गवाः ।
विक्रान्ता बलवन्तश्च
राघवेण च रक्षिताः ॥
It is impossible, O King, to spy upon those bulls among Vānaras. They are of great prowess and immense strength and are under the protection of Rāghava.
6.30.6
नापि सम्भाषितुं शक्याः
सम्प्रश्नोऽत्र न लभ्यते ।
सर्वतो रक्ष्यते पन्था
वानरैः पर्वतोपमैः ॥
It was not possible even to talk to them, let alone inquire about them; the approaches from all sides are guarded by Vānaras who are giant, like mountains.
6.30.7
प्रविष्टमात्रे ज्ञातोऽहम्
बले तस्मिन्नचारिते ।
बलादू गृहीतो रक्षोभिः
बहुधाऽस्मि विचारितः ॥
I was recognized the moment I entered that army with no one having to ask me (who I am). I was grabbed by the Rākshasas by force and was interrogated intensely.
6.30.8
जानुभिर्मुष्टिभिर्दन्तैः
तलैश्चाभिहतो भृशम् ।
परिणीतोऽस्मि हरिभिः
बलमध्ये अमर्षणैः ॥
Assailed by knees, fists, teeth and open palms, I was paraded in the midst of that army by the angry Vānaras.
6.30.9
परिणीय च सर्वत्र
नीतोऽहं रामसंसदम् ।
रुधिरादिग्धसर्वाङ्गो
विह्वलश्चलितेन्द्रियः ॥
After I was paraded everywhere, I was presented where Rāma was holding council, with all my limbs bleeding and with my senses in a disarray.
6.30.10
हरिभिर्वध्यमानश्च
याचमानः कृताञ्जलिः ।
राघवेण परित्रातो
जीवामीति यदृच्छया ॥
Being thus beaten almost to death by the Vānaras, and pleading for mercy with my palms joined, I was saved by Rāghava; it is because of sheer luck that I am still alive.
6.30.11
एष शैलैः शिलाभिश्च
पूरयित्वा महार्णवम् ।
द्वारमाश्रित्य लङ्काया
रामस्तिष्ठति सायुधः ॥
Having filled up the huge ocean with hills and rocks, Rāma is at the gateway of Laṅkā, armed.
6.30.12
गरुडव्यूहमास्थाय
सर्वतो हरिभिर्वृतः ।
मां विसृज्य महातेजा
लङ्कामेवाभिवर्तते ॥
He of immense power who let me go is advancing towards Laṅkā surrounded by Vānaras on all sides with the army deployed in Garuḍa formation.
6.30.13
पुरा प्राकारमायाति
क्षिप्रमेकतरं कुरु ।
सीतां वाऽस्मै प्रयच्छाशु
सुयुद्धं वा प्रदीयताम् ॥
Before he reaches the ramparts you must quickly decide on one of two courses – to give back Seetā at once or to give him a good fight.
6.30.14
मनसा तत्तदा प्रेक्ष्य
तच्छुत्वा राक्षसाधिपः ।
शार्दूलं सुमहद्वाक्यम्
अथोवाच स रावणः ॥
Having heard him and seeing the same (two possibilities) in his mind, Rāvaṇa, the Lord of Rākshasas, expressed his firm resolve to Ṡārdūla:
6.30.15
यदि मां प्रतियुद्ध्येरन्
देवगन्धर्वदानवाः ।
नैव सीतां प्रदास्यामि
सर्वलोकभयादपि ॥
I will not give up Seetā, even if all the Dēvas, Dānavas and Gandharvas join in an attack against me, nor even if all the worlds come together to threaten me.
6.30.16
एवमुक्त्वा महातेजा
रावणः पुनरब्रवीत् ॥
Having said this, the mighty powerful Rāvaṇa said again:
6.30.16
चारिता भवता सेना
कोऽत्र शूराः प्लवङ्गमाः ।
कीदृशाः किम्प्रभाः सोम्य
वानरा ये दुरासदाः ।
कस्य पुत्राश्च पौत्राश्च
तत्त्वमाख्याहि राक्षस ॥
My dear! You have reconnoitered that army, tell me who are the most valiant among those Vānaras. What is their presence like? Who among those Vānaras are the most formidable? How great is their prowess? Whose sons and grandsons are they? Tell me all about it, O Rākshasa!
6.30.18
तथाऽत्र प्रतिपत्स्यामि
ज्ञात्वा तेषां बलाबलम् ।
अवश्यं खलुसङ्ख्यानम्
कर्तव्यं युद्धमिच्छताम् ॥
Knowing their strengths and weaknesses, I shall be able to decide on what to do. Those who have set their mind on giving a fight, must carefully reckon with the strength of the enemy!
6.30.19
तथैवमुक्तः शार्दूलो
रावणेनोत्तमश्चरः ।
इदं वचनमारेभे
वक्तुं रावणसन्निधौ ॥
Thus asked by Rāvaṇa, Ṡārdūla, the best of the spies, began to narrate in the presence of Rāvaṇa with these words:
6.30.20
अथर्क्षरजसः पुत्रो
युधि राजा सुदुर्जयः ।
गद्गदस्याथ पुत्रोऽत्र
जाम्बवानिति विश्रुतः ॥
First there is (Sugreeva), their King, the son of Ṛksharajas, whom it is impossible to vanquish in a fight. Then there is the famed Jāmbavān, son of Gadgada.
6.30.21
गद्गदस्यैव पुत्रोऽन्यो
गुरुपुत्रः शतक्रतोः ।
कदनं यस्य पुत्रेण
कृतमेकेन रक्षसाम् ॥
Then there is (Dhūmra) another son of Gadgada too. Then there is (Kēsari) the son of the preceptor of Ṡatakratu (Bṛihaspati). It was his son (Hanumān) who worked havoc among the Rākshasas, singlehandedly.
6.30.22
सुषेणश्चापि धर्मात्मा
पुत्रो धर्मस्य वीर्यवान् ।
सौम्यः सोमात्मजश्चात्र
राजन् दधिमुखः कपिः ॥
Then there is the Dharmātma Sushēṇa, the valiant son of Yama, O King! And then there is the good Vānara Dadhimukha, son of Sōma.
6.30.23
सुमुखो दुर्मुखश्चात्र
वेगदर्शी च वानरः ।
मृत्युर्वानररूपेण
नूनं सृष्टः स्वयम्भुवा ॥
Sumukha, Durmukha and the Vānara Vēgadarsi are for sure death created by the Self-born in the semblance of Vānaras.
6.30.24
पुत्रो हुतवहस्याथ
नीलः सेनापतिः स्वयम् ।
अनिलस्य च पुत्रोऽत्र
हनुमानिति विश्रुतः ॥
Then there is Neela, the Commander-in-Chief, the son of the Courier of Havya. And then there is the son of Vāyu, well known in the world as Hanumān.
6.30.25
नप्ता शक्रस्य दुर्धर्षो
बलवानङ्गदो युवा ।
मैन्दश्च द्विविदश्चोभौ
बलिनावश्विसम्भवौ ॥
Then there is the strong, young and invincible Aṅgada, the grandson of Ṡakra. Then there is the strong duo of Mainda and Dwivida, born of the Aṡwins.
6.30.26
पुत्रा वैवस्वतस्यात्र
पञ्च कालान्तकोपमाः ।
गजो गवाक्षो गवयः
शरभो गन्धमादनः ॥
And then there are the five sons of Vaivaswata, Gaja, Gavāksha, Gavaya, Ṡarabha and Gandhamādana, who are comparable to Yama, the annihilator of time itself.
6.30.27
दश वानरकोट्यश्च
शूराणां युद्धकाङ्क्षिणाम् ।
श्रीमतां देवपुत्राणाम्
शेषं नाख्यातुमुत्सहे ॥
There are ten crores of these blessed sons of the Dēvas, who are valiant and eager for battle; what more can I say?
6.30.28
पुत्रो दशरथस्यैष
सिंहसंहननो युवा ।
दूषणो निहतो येन
खरश्च त्रिशिरास्तथा ॥
And then there is (Rāma) the youthful son of Daṡaratha, majestic like a lion, by whom were slain Dūshaṇa, Khara and Triṡira.
6.30.29
नास्ति रामस्य सदृशो
विक्रमे भुवि कश्चन ।
विराधो निहतो येन
कबन्धश्चान्तकोपमः ॥
There is no one on the face of the earth who is comparable to Rāma in prowess. He, by whom Virādha and Kabandha were slain, is like Yama.
6.30.30
वक्तुं न शक्तो रामस्य
नरः कश्चिद् गुणान् क्षितौ ।
जनस्थानगता येन
यावन्ता राक्षसा हताः ॥
There is none on earth who can recount the virtues and abilities of Rāma, by whom were slain all the Rākshasas in Janasthāna.
6.30.31
लक्ष्मणश्चात्र धर्मात्मा
मातङ्गानामिवर्षभः ।
यस्य बाणपथं प्राप्य
न जीवेदपि वासवः ॥
And then there is the Dharmātma Lakshmaṇa, who is like a bull among elephants; Vāsava himself would not be able to escape with his life if he were the target of his arrow.
6.30.32
श्वेतो ज्योतिर्मुखश्चात्र
भास्करस्यात्मसम्भवौ ।
वरुणस्य च पुत्रोऽन्यो
हेमकूटः प्लवङ्गमः ॥
And then there are Ṡwēta and Jyōtirmukha, born to the Sun-God himself; and the Vānara Hēmakūṭa, another son of Varuṇa.
6.30.33
विश्वकर्मसुतो वीरो
नलः प्लवगसत्तमः ।
विक्रान्तो बलवानत्र
वसुपुत्रः स दुर्धरः ॥
And then there is the valiant Nala, the eminent Vānara, son of Viṡwakarma, and Durdhara, son of Vasu, of great prowess and strength.
6.30.34
राक्षसानां वरिष्ठश्च
तव भ्राता विभीषणः ।
परिगृह्य पुरीं लङ्काम्
राघवस्य हिते रतः ॥
And then there is that foremost among Rākshasas, your brother Vibheeshaṇa, who having obtained the city of Laṅkā, is keen on promoting the interest of Rāghava.
6.30.35
इति सर्वं समाख्यातम्
तवेदं वानरं बलम् ।
सुवेलेऽधिष्ठितं शैले
शेषकार्ये भवान् गतिः ॥
And now I have told you all about this Vānara army that occupies the mountain Suvēla. It is for you to decide what is to be done next.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे त्रिंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirtieth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
