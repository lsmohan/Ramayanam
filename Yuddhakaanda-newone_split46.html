Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 46
In this Sarga, Vibheeshaṇa and all the Vānaras feel extremely sad and weep, seeing Rāma and Lakshmaṇa lie motionless in the battle field and streams of blood flowing from their bodies.
They look in every direction in the sky but fail to trace Indrajit. Only Vibheeshaṇa, with his own magical powers, is able to spot him. Indrajit announces to the Rākshasas that Rāma and Lakshmaṇa are dead. He also strikes the Vānara captains with his arrows before leaving for Lanka.
Sugreeva is gripped with fear. Vibheeshaṇa consoles him saying that those who are invested in truth and Dharma shall not fear death. He urges him to instill confidence in the Vānara army. He asks him to attend to the first order of business, which is to take care of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa till they regain consciousness.
Meanwhile, Indrajit goes to Rāvaṇa and tells him that Rāma and Lakshmaṇa are slain. Rāvaṇa springs up in joy. With all his worries put to rest, he heaps praise on his son.
6.46.1
ततो द्यां पृथिवीं चैव
वीक्षमाणा वनौकसः ।
ददृशुः सन्ततौ बाणैः
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
On seeing the brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa completely covered with arrows, the Vana dwellers kept looking at the sky and the earth.
Looking up and down is typical for monkeys.
6.46.2
वृष्ट्वेवोपरते देवे
कृतकर्मणि राक्षसे ।
आजगामाथ तं देशम्
ससुग्रीवो विभीषणः ॥
Like the Dēva was done sending down showers of rain, Indrajit was done with the task he set himself on, and Vibheeshaṇa came to that place along with Sugreeva.
6.46.3
नीलद्विविदमैन्दाश्च
सुषेणकुमुदाङ्गदाः ।
तूर्णं हनुमता सार्धम्
अन्वशोचन्त राघवौ ॥
Neela, Dwivida, Mainda, Sushēṇa, Kumuda and Aṅgada, along with Hanumān fell in grief instantly, concerned about the scions of the Raghus.
6.46.4
अचेष्टौ मन्दनिश्वासौ
शोणितेन परिप्लुतौ ।
शरजालाचितौ स्तब्धौ
शयानौ शरतल्पगौ ।
निश्वसन्तौ यथा सर्पौ
निश्चेष्टौ मन्दविक्रमौ ।
रुधिरस्रावदिग्धाङ्गौ
तापनीयाविव ध्वजौ ।
तौ वीरशयने वीरौ
शयानौ मन्दचेष्टितौ ।
यूथपैस्तैः परिवृतौ
बाष्पव्याकुललोचनैः ।
राघवौ पतितौ दृष्ट्वा
शरजालसमावृतौ ।
बभूवुर्व्यथिताः सर्वे
वानराः सविभीषणाः ॥
All the Vānaras and Vibheeshaṇa were harrowed by the sight of the heroic scions of the Raghus fallen on the battlefield, motionless, breathing feebly, their bodies overflowing with blood, as their bodies were caught in a tangle of arrows and they lay motionless on their bed of arrows.
Sighing like serpents, their movements feeble if any, their bodies wet with streams of blood, those two Veeras lay on the battle field hardly able to move their limbs and surrounded by the captains of their army whose eyes were turbid with tears.
6.46.8
अन्तरिक्षं निरीक्षन्तो
दिशः सर्वाश्च वानराः ।
न चैनं माययाच्छन्नम्
ददृशू रावणिं रणे ॥
They looked in the sky in every direction but could not track the whereabouts of the son of Rāvaṇa who made himself invisible with his magical powers.
6.46.9
तं तु मायाप्रतिच्छन्नम्
माययैव विभीषणः ।
वीक्षमाणो ददर्शाथ
भ्रातुः पुत्रमवस्थितम् ॥
But Vibheeshaṇa, with his own powers of magic, could spot where his brother’s son hid himself with his magical powers.
6.46.10
तमप्रतिमकर्माणम्
अप्रतिद्वन्द्वमाहवे ।
ददर्शान्तर्हितं वीरम्
वरदानाद्विभीषणः ।
तेजसा यशसा चैव
विक्रमेण च संयुतम् ॥
Vibheeshaṇa saw the valiant one of incomparable exploits who had no match in a fight and was famous for his power and prowess hiding himself from view by the power of the boon he had received.
6.46.11
इन्द्रजित्त्वात्मनः कर्म
तौ शयानौ समीक्ष्य च ।
उवाच परमप्रीतो
हर्षयन् सर्वनैर्ऋतान् ॥
And Indrajit, seeing the two lying down (on the ground) and extremely pleased with his own accomplishment, said these words, cheering up all the Rākshasas:
6.46.12
दूषणस्य च हन्तारौ
खरस्य च महाबलौ ।
सादितौ मामकैर्बाणैः
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्णौ ॥
I have brought the brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa of the immense strength, the slayers of Dūshaṇa and Khara, to naught with my arrows!
6.46.13
नेमौ मोक्षयितुं शक्यौ
एतस्मादिषुबन्धनात् ।
सर्वैरपि समागम्य
सर्षिसङ्घैः सुरासुरैः ॥
Even all the Dēvas and Asuras coming together, joined by hosts of Ṛshis, would not be able to release them from these bonds of arrows.
6.46.14
यत्कृते चिन्तयानस्य
शोकार्तस्य पितुर्मम ।
अस्पृष्ट्वा शयनं गात्रैः
त्रियामा याति शर्वरी ।
कृत्स्नेयं यत्कृते लङ्का
नदी वर्षास्विवाकुला ।
सोऽयं मूलहरोऽनर्थः
सर्वेषां शमितो मया ॥
I have diffused the scare that was striking at the root of everything, that threw the entire Laṅkā in turmoil like monsoon does the rivers, and that made my father worried, distressed, and pass the nights without resting his body on the bed even for a third of the night.
6.46.16
रामस्य लक्ष्मणस्यापि
सर्वेषां च वनौकसाम् ।
विक्रमा निष्फलाः सर्वे
यथा शरदि तोयदाः ॥
The prowess of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa and all the Vānaras is as futile as clouds in the post-monsoon season.
6.46.17
एवमुक्त्वा तु तान् सर्वान्
राक्षसान् परिपश्यतः ।
यूथपानपि तान् सर्वान्
ताडयामास रावणिः ॥
Having spoken thus to the Rākshasas who were looking at him from every direction, the son of Rāvaṇa, also struck at all those (Vānara) captains.
6.46.18
नीलं नवभिराहत्य
मैन्दं च द्विविदं तथा ।
त्रिभिस्त्रिभिरमित्रघ्न
तताप प्रवरेषुभिः ॥
He, the slayer of his adversaries, struck Neela with nine arrows and tormented Mainda and Dwivida each with three best of arrows.
6.46.19
जाम्बवन्तं महेष्वासो
विद्ध्वा बाणेन वक्षसि ।
हनूमतो वेगवतो
विससर्ज शरान् दश ॥
That great bowman struck Jāmbavān on the chest with one arrow, and let fly ten arrows at the swift Hanumān.
6.46.20
गवाक्षं शरभं चैव
द्वावप्यमिततेजसौ ।
द्वाभ्यां द्वाभ्यां महावेगो
विव्याध युधि रावणिः ॥
The son of Rāvaṇa hit Gavāksha and Ṡarabha, both of whom possessed limitless strength, with two arrows each, with amazing swiftness.
6.46.21
गोलाङ्गूलेश्वरं चैव
वालिपुत्रमथाङ्गदम् ।
विव्याध बहुभिर्बाणैः
त्वरमाणोऽथ रावणिः ॥
Without losing any time, the son of Rāvaṇa struck the chief of the Gōlāṅgūlas and Aṅgada, the son of Vāli, with many arrows.
6.46.22
तान् वानरवरान् भित्त्वा
शरैरग्निशिखोपमैः ।
ननाद बलवांस्तत्र
महासत्त्वः स रावणिः ॥
Having thus struck the great Vānaras with arrows that were like tongues of flame, the son of Rāvaṇa, of immense power and strength, let off a triumphant shout.
6.46.23
तानर्दयित्वा बाणौघैः
त्रासयित्वा च वानरान् ।
प्रजहास महाबाहुः
वचनं चेदमब्रवीत् ॥
Thus harrowing the Vānaras with floods of arrows and terrifying them, he of the mighty arm laughed and said these words:
6.46.24
शरबन्धेन घोरेण
मया बद्धौ चमूमुखे ।
सहितौ भ्रातरावेतौ
निशामयत राक्षसाः ॥
Look, O Rākshasas, at these brothers, both lying on the forefront of the battlefield, whom I entrapped in the dreaded bonds of arrows.
6.46.25
एवमुक्तास्तु ते सर्वे
राक्षसाः कूटयोधिनः ।
परं विस्मयमाजग्मुः
कर्मणा तेन हर्षिताः ॥
Thus told, the Rākshasas, devious fighters, were awestruck, delighted at his exploit.
6.46.26
विनेदुश्च महानादान्
सर्वतो जलदोपमाः ।
हतो राम इति ज्ञात्वा
रावणिं समपूजयन् ॥
And they, who were giant like clouds, let off mighty shouts exalting Rāvaṇa’s son, thinking that Rāma had been slain.
6.46.27
निष्पन्दौ तु तदा दृष्ट्वा
तावुभौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
वसुधायां निरुच्छ्वासौ
हतावित्यन्वमन्यत ॥
Seeing the two, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, lying on the ground motionless and scarcely breathing, Indrajit presumed them to be dead.
6.46.28
हर्षेण तु समाविष्ट
इन्द्रजित् समितिञ्जयः ।
प्रविवेश पुरीं लङ्काम्
हर्षयन् सर्वराक्षसान् ॥
And he, the winner of battles, filled with joy, entered the city of Laṅkā, bringing joy to the Rākshasas.
6.46.29
रामलक्ष्मणयोर्द्दष्ट्वा
शरीरे सायकैश्चिते ।
सर्वाणि चाङ्गोपाङ्गानि
सुग्रीवं भयमाविशत् ॥
Seeing the bodies of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa covered thick with arrows biting into every limb, Sugreeva was gripped with fear.
6.46.30
तमुवाच परित्रस्तम्
वानरेन्द्रं विभीषणः ।
सबाष्पवदनं दीनम्
शोकव्याकुललोचनम् ॥
To that king of Vānaras whose mind was in a turmoil with grief, whose face was covered with tears and who was distressed and gripped by extreme fear, Vibheeshaṇa said:
6.46.31
अलं त्रासेन सुग्रीव
बाष्पवेगो निगृह्यताम् ।
एवं प्रायाणि युद्धानि
विजयो नास्ति नैष्ठिकः ॥
Sugreeva, do not cave in to worry, hold off the streams of tears. This is what happens in a fight; there is no guarantee of victory.
6.46.32
सशेषभाग्यताऽस्माकम्
यदि वीर भविष्यति ।
मोहमेतौ प्रहास्येते
महात्मानौ महाबलौ ॥
If we have some little luck still left, these mighty ones of immense strength will recover from their swoon.
6.46.33
पर्यवस्थापयात्मानम्
अनाथं मां च वानर ।
सत्यधर्माभिरक्तानाम्
नास्ति मृत्युकृतं भयम् ॥
Please be steady and instill the same in me, who has no one else to turn to. One who is invested in truth and Dharma would not have any fear from death.
6.46.34
एवमुक्त्वा ततस्तस्य
जलक्लिन्नेन पाणिना ।
सुग्रीवस्य शुभे नेत्रे
प्रममार्ज विभिषणः ॥
Saying this, Vibheeshaṇa wiped Sugreeva’s eyes with his hands dipped in water.
6.46.35
ततः सलिलमादाय
विद्यया परिजप्य च ।
सुग्रीवनेत्रे धर्मात्मा
स ममार्ज विभीषणः ॥
Taking water (in his hands) and invoking spiritual powers in it, Vibheeshaṇa, the Dharmātma, wiped the eyes of Sugreeva.
6.46.36
प्रमृज्य वदनं तस्य
कपिराजस्य धीमतः ।
अब्रवीत् कालसम्प्राप्तम्
असम्भ्रममिदं वचः ॥
Having wiped the face of the sagacious king of Vānaras, he spoke these words that were apt at that time, holding himself steady:
6.46.37
न कालः कपिराजेन्द्र
वैक्लव्यमनुवर्तितुम् ।
अतिस्नेहोऽप्यकालेऽस्मिन्
मरणायोपकल्पते ॥
This is not the time to harbor disquiet, O paramount ruler of the Vānaras! To allow our love (for Rāma and Lakshmaṇa) to overwhelm us at the wrong moment is to invite death.
6.46.38
तस्मादुत्सृज्य वैक्लव्यम्
सर्वकार्यविनाशनम् ।
हितं रामपुरोगाणाम्
सैन्यानामनुचिन्त्यताम् ॥
Therefore cast off the disquiet which only ruins every enterprise, and think of what is the best course (now) for the armies led by Rāma.
6.46.39
अथवा रक्ष्यतां रामो
यावत् सञ्ज्ञाविपर्ययः ।
लब्धसञ्ज्ञौ हि काकुत्स्थौ
भयं नो व्यपनेष्यतः ॥
First, let us take care of Rāma till he regains consciousness. Once the scions of the Kākutsthas revive, they will dispel our fears.
6.46.40
नैतत् किञ्चन रामस्य
न च रामो मुमूर्षति ।
न ह्येनं हास्यते लक्ष्मीः
दुर्लभा या गतायुषाम् ॥
This is nothing for Rāma, he is not going to die. The lively charm, which is impossible to be found in someone who is dead, has not left him yet.
6.46.41
तस्मादाश्वासयात्मानम्
बलं चाश्वासय स्वकम् ।
यावत् कार्याणि सर्वाणि
पुनः संस्थापयाम्यहम् ॥
Therefore comfort yourself and your forces; meanwhile I shall restore all the operations to normalcy.
6.46.42
एते हि फुल्लनयनाः
त्रासादागतसाध्वसाः ।
कर्णे कर्णे प्रकथिता
हरयो हरिसत्तम ॥
O best of Vānaras! These Vānaras stare with wide-eyes, convulse with fear and whisper in each other’s ears.
6.46.43
मां तु दृष्ट्वा प्रधावन्तम्
अनीकं सम्प्रहर्षितुम् ।
त्यजन्तु हरयस्त्रासम्
भुक्तपूर्वामिव स्रजम् ॥
I shall try to cheer up the fleeing army and on seeing me (stand steady) the Vānaras shall discard fear like one discards a used flower garland.
6.46.44
समाश्वास्य तु सुग्रीवम्
राक्षसेन्द्रो विभीषणः ।
विद्रुतं वानरानीकम्
तत्समाश्वासयत् पुनः ॥
After cheering up Sugreeva, Vibheeshaṇa, the Lord of Rākshasas, cheered up the Vānara army that was all but set to flee.
6.46.45
इन्द्रजित्तु महामायः
सर्वसैन्यसमावृतः ।
विवेश नगरीं लङ्काम्
पितरं चाभ्युपागमत् ॥
And Indrajit, a master of magical powers, entered the city of Laṅkā with all his army and went to his father.
6.46.46
तत्र रावणमासीनम्
अभिवाद्य कृताञ्जलिः ।
आचचक्षे प्रियं पित्रे
निहतौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
With palms joined in reverence, he saluted the seated Rāvaṇa. He told his father the pleasing news that Rāma and Lakshmaṇa had been slain.
6.46.47
उत्पपात ततो हृष्टः
पुत्रं च परिषस्वजे ।
रावणो रक्षसां मध्ये
श्रुत्वा शत्रू निपातितौ ॥
On hearing that the enemy was destroyed, Rāvaṇa sprang up in joy and embraced his son amidst the assembled Rākshasas.
6.46.48
उपाघ्राय स मूर्ध्न्येनम्
पप्रच्छ प्रीतमानसः ।
पृच्छते च यथावृत्तम्
पित्रे सर्वं न्यवेदयत् ।
यथा तौ शरबन्धेन
निश्चेष्टौ निष्प्रभौ कृतौ ॥
His heart filled with joy, he smelled the head (of his son) and asked (for the detail). In response, he told everything that happened and how the two, bound by the power of his arrows, were made motionless and lusterless.
6.46.50
स हर्षवेगानुगतान्तरात्मा
श्रुत्वा वचस्तस्य महारथस्य ।
जहौ ज्वरं दाशरथेः समुत्थितं
प्रहृष्य वाचाऽभिननन्द पुत्रम् ॥
On hearing those words of the great charioter, his mind was flooded with joy. Putting all the worries caused by the son of Daṡaratha to rest, he heaped praise on his son in the exuberance of his joy.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे षट्चत्वारिंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty sixth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
