Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 99
In this Sarga, Bharata, after instructing Vasishṭha to bring his mothers, proceeds ahead to find Rāma, all the while feeling distressed and terrible about the fate Rāma met with, because of him.
He finally reaches the cottage of Rāma, seeing markers and signs of the presence of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa on the way. He then enters the cottage wherein he finds bows, arrows and quivers and also a Vēdi for Yajña.
Finally, he sees Rāma. His voice choked, he curses himself for being the unwitting cause of all that has happened. He falls, wailing, on the ground before he could reach the feet of Rāma.
Ṡatrughna, Sumantra and Guha also meet Rāma and Lakshmaṇa. Seeing those mighty rulers meet in a forest, even the forest dwellers shed tears.
2.99.1
निविष्टायां तु सेनायाम्
उत्सुको भरतस्तदा ।
जगाम भ्रातरं द्रष्टुं
शत्रुघ्नमनुदर्शयन् ॥
With the army camped, Bharata then proceeded eagerly to see his brother, leading the way, Ṡatrughna following him.
2.99.2
ऋषिं वसिष्ठं सन्दिश्य
मातॄर्मे शीघ्रमानय ।
इति त्वरितमग्रे स
जगाम गुरुवत्सलः ॥
He who was affectionate to elders, instructed Ṛshi Vasishṭha 'Please bring my mothers immediately', and proceeded ahead quickly.
2.99.3
सुमन्त्रस्त्वपि शत्रुघ्नम्
अदूरादन्वपद्यत ।
रामदर्शनजस्तर्षो
भरतस्येव तस्य च ॥
Sumantra too followed not far behind Ṡatrughna, with his thirst for the sight of Rāma not any less than that of Bharata.
2.99.4
गच्छन्नेवाथ भरतः
तापसालय संस्थिताम् ।
भ्रातुः पर्णकुटीं श्रीमान्
उटजं च ददर्श ह ॥
Bharata of rich bearing went on and saw his brother's hut covered with leaves that was among the residences of Munis.
2.99.5
शालायास्त्वग्रतस्तस्या
ददर्श भरतस्तदा ।
काष्ठानि चावभग्नानि
पुष्पाण्युपचितानि च ॥
In front of that hut, Bharata then saw split logs of wood and also plucked flowers.
2.99.6
स लक्ष्मणस्य रामस्य
ददर्शाऽश्रममीयुषः ।
कृतं वृक्षेष्वभिज्ञानं
कुशचीरैः क्वचित्क्वचित् ॥
As he got nearer to that Āṡrama, he saw here and there, tied to the trees, indicative markers of Kuṡa grass and robes of bark of Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
2.99.7
स ददर्श वने तस्मिन्
महतस्सञ्चयान्कृतान् ।
मृगाणां महिषाणां च
करीषैश्शीतकारणात् ॥
And in that Vana, he saw large piles of the droppings of deer and buffaloes, collected for burning to ward off the cold.
2.99.8
गच्छन्नेव महाबाहुः
द्युतिमान्भरतस्तदा ।
शत्रुघ्नं चाब्रवीद्धृष्टः
तानमात्यांश्च सर्वशः ॥
Then, while continuing to walk, the lustrous Bharata of mighty arm joyfully said to Ṡatrughna and all the ministers:
2.99.9
मन्ये प्राप्ताः स्म तं देशं
भरद्वाजो यमब्रवीत् ।
नातिदूरे हि मन्येऽहं
नदीं मन्दाकिनीमितः ॥
I think that we have reached the place Bharadwāja described and that the river Mandākini should not be far from here.
2.99.10
उच्चैर्बद्धानि चीराणि
लक्ष्मणेन भवेदयम् ।
अभिज्ञानकृतः पन्था
अकाले गन्तुमिच्छता ॥
These pieces of cloth must have been tied high by Lakshmaṇa, to serve as markers of the track at odd hours.
2.99.11
इदं चोदात्तदन्तानां
कुञ्जराणां तरस्विनाम् ।
शैलपार्श्वे परिक्रान्तम्
अन्योन्यमभिगर्जताम् ॥
This area on the mountain-side is where mighty strong elephants of huge tusks trumpet at each other challengingly.
2.99.12
यमेवाधातुमिच्छन्ति
तापसास्सततं वने ।
तस्यासौ दृश्यते धूमः
सङ्कुलः कृष्णवर्त्मनः ॥
There you see the thick black smoke of the sacred fire the Munis constantly tend to in the Vana.
2.99.13
अत्राहं पुरुषव्याघ्रं
गुरुसत्कारकारिणम् ।
आर्यं द्रक्ष्यामि संहृष्टो
महर्षिमिव राघवम् ॥
Here I shall rejoice finding the Maharshi-like noble Rāghava, that tiger among men, who honors and reveres elders.
2.99.14
अथ गत्वा मुहूर्तन्तु
चित्रकूटं स राघवः ।
मन्दाकिनीमनुप्राप्तः
तं जनं चेदमब्रवीत् ॥
Proceeding a little further on Citrakūṭa mountain, and reaching Mandākini, the scion of the Raghus said to those who accompanied him:
2.99.15
जगत्यां पुरषव्याघ्र
आस्ते वीरासने रतः ।
जनेन्द्रो निर्जनं प्राप्य
धिङ्मे जन्म सजीवितम् ॥
The tiger among the men who loves Veerāsana now sits on the bare ground. The lord of people now lives where there are no people. And life is breathing in me still, Dhik!
Veerāsana means heroic sitting posture. In this posture, one puts his right foot under the left thigh and left foot on the right knee.
2.99.16
मत्कृते व्यसनं प्राप्तो
लोकनाथो महाद्युतिः ।
सर्वान्कामान्परित्यज्य
वने वसति राघवः ॥
Hardship descended on the lord of the worlds, of supreme radiance, because of me. Rāghava lives in a Vana giving up everything that he would have wished for.
2.99.17
इति लोकसमाक्रुष्टः
पादेष्वद्य प्रसादयन् ।
रामस्य निपतिष्यामि
सीताया लक्ष्मणस्य च ॥
Having been blamed for this by all the world, I shall fall at the feet of Rāma seeking his kindness, and also at the feet of Seetā and Lakshmaṇa.
2.99.18
एवं संविलपं स्तस्मिन्
वने दशरथात्मजः ।
ददर्श महतीं पुण्यां
पर्णशालां मनोरमाम् ।
सालतालाश्वकर्णानां
पर्णैर्बहुभिरावृताम् ।
विशालां मृदुभिस्तीर्णां
कुशैर्वेदिमिवाध्वरे ।
शक्रायुधनिकाशैश्च
कार्मुकैर्भारसाधनैः ।
रुक्मपृष्ठैर्महासारैः
शोभितां शत्रुबाधकैः ।
अर्क रश्मि प्रतीकाशैः
घोरैस्तूणीगतैश्शरैः ।
शोभितां दीप्तवदनैः
सर्पैर्भोगवतीमिव ।
महारजतवासोभ्याम्
असिभ्यां च विराजिताम् ।
रुक्मबिन्दुविचित्राभ्यां
चर्मभ्यां चापि शोभिताम् ।
गोधाङ्गुळित्रैरासक्तैः
चित्रैः काञ्चनभूषितैः ।
अरिसंघैरनाधृष्यां
मृगैस्सिंहगुहा मिव ॥
The son of Daṡaratha, who was thus crying in the Vana, saw a magnificent and lovely cottage,
covered thick with soft leaves of Sāla, palm and Aṡvakarṇā trees and spacious like a Kuṡa-grass-covered Vēdi of Yajña,
adorned with bows equal to the weapons of Ṡakra, built for the task and powerful, great harriers of the foe with their backs covered with gold,
glowing with dreadful arrows with blazing tips shining like the rays of sun, stowed in quivers that protected them like serpents do Bhōgavati,
resplendent with swords and their gold scabbards, and leather shields laid with golden buttons,
wrist and finger guards decorated with curious designs in gold hanging on the walls,
and impregnable for the enemy hordes like a lion's lair for deer.
Bhōgavati is a city in the nether world.
2.99.24
प्रागुदक्प्रवणां वेदिं
विशालां दीप्तपावकाम् ।
ददर्श भरतस्तत्र
पुण्यां रामनिवेशने ॥
Bharata saw the spacious sacred Vēdi with bright fire with a depression in the north west corner in Rāma's home.
2.99.25
निरीक्ष्य स मुहूर्तं तु
ददर्श भरतो गुरुम् ।
उटजे राममासीनं
जटामण्डलधारिणम् ॥
Upon looking for a moment, he spotted his revered elder brother Rāma, seated in the cottage, wearing matted locks.
2.99.26
तं तु कृष्णाजिनधरं
चीरवल्कलवाससम् ।
ददर्श राममासीनम्
अभितः पावकोपमम् ।
सिंहस्कन्धं महाबाहुं
पुण्डरीकनिभेक्षणम् ।
पृथिव्यास्सागरान्तायाः
भर्तारं धर्मचारिणम् ।
उपविष्टं महाबाहुं
ब्रह्माणमिव शाश्वतम् ।
स्थण्डिले दर्भसंस्तीर्णे
सीतया लक्ष्मणेन च ॥
He saw the seated Rāma, blazing like fire and clad in tree bark and deer skin. He saw that lord of the ocean-girt earth, with his lion-shoulders, mighty arms and lotus eyes, devoted to the pursuit of Dharma, sitting on the ground covered with Darbha grass, like lord Brahma, along with Seetā and Lakshmaṇa.
2.99.29
तं दृष्ट्वा भरतश्श्रीमान्
दुःखशोकपरिप्लुतः ।
अभ्यधावत धर्मात्मा
भरतः कैकेयी सुतः ॥
As soon as he saw him, Bharata, the Dharmātma, the blessed son of Kaikēyee, sprang towards him overwhelmed by grief and misery.
2.99.30
दृष्ट्वैव विललापाऽर्तो
बाष्पसन्दिग्धया गिरा ।
अशक्नुवन् धारयितुं
धैर्याद्वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
As soon as he saw him, unable to summon his grit to contain his grief and agony he spoke indistinctly, with tear choked voice:
2.99.31
यस्संसदि प्रकृतिभिः
भवेद्युक्त उपासितुम् ।
वन्यैर्मृगैरुपासीनः
सोऽयमास्ते ममाग्रजः ॥
My elder brother, who should be attended upon by his subjects in an assembly, is now being attended by wild beasts.
2.99.32
वासोभिर्बहुसाहस्रैः
यो महात्मा पुरोचितः ।
मृगाजिने सोऽयमिह
प्रवस्ते धर्ममाचरन् ॥
The Mahātma who, in the past, was used to a wardrobe of thousands of garments is now covered in deer skin, in his path of adherence to Dharma!
2.99.33
अधारयद्यो विविधाः
चित्रास्सुमनसस्तदा ।
सोऽयं जटाभारमिमं
वहते राघवः कथम् ॥
How could Rāghava, who used to wear many lovely flowers, manage to wear matted locks!
2.99.34
यस्य यज्ञैर्यथादिष्टैः
युक्तो धर्मस्य सञ्चयः ।
शरीरक्लेशसम्भूतं
स धर्मं परिमार्गते ॥
One who is supposed to reap the fruits of Dharma by performing Yajñas as ordained, is now trying to find them in bearing physical hardship.
2.99.35
चन्दनेन महार्हेण
यस्याङ्गमुपसेवितम् ।
मलेन तस्याङ्गमिदं
कथमार्यस्य सेव्यते ॥
How could the noble one, whose body used to be perfumed with sandal paste of rare richness, allow himself to be covered with dirt?
2.99.36
मन्निमित्तमिदं दुःखं
प्राप्तो रामः सुखोचितः ।
धिग्जीवितं नृशंसस्य
मम लोकविगर्हितम् ॥
It is because of me that Rāma, who is used to comfort, suffers these hardships! Dhik, I am cruel and my life is abhorred by the world!
2.99.37
इत्येवं विलपन्दीनः
प्रस्विन्नमुखपङ्कजः ।
पादावप्राप्य रामस्य
पपात भरतो रुदन् ॥
Thus lamenting piteously, his lotus face covered with perspiration, Bharata fell down weeping, before he could reach Rāma's feet.
2.99.38
दुःखाभितप्तो भरतो
राजपुत्रो महाबलः ।
उक्त्वाऽऽर्येति सकृद्दीनं
पुनर्नोवाच किञ्चन ॥
The mighty strong prince Bharata, scorched by grief, uttered in a weak voice but one word 'Ārya!' and could say no more.
2.99.39
बाष्पाभिहतकण्ठश्च
प्रेक्ष्य रामं यशस्विनम् ।
आर्येत्येवाथ सङ्क्रुश्य
व्याहर्तुं नाशकत्तदा ॥
On seeing Rāma of great renown, his throat blocked by tears, he wailed crying 'Ārya!' and was not able to say anything else.
2.99.40
शत्रुघ्नश्चापि रामस्य
ववन्दे चरणौ रुदन् ।
तावुभौ स समालिङ्ग्य
रामश्चाश्रूण्यवर्तयत् ॥
Ṡatrughna too fell at the feet of Rāma weeping. Embracing both of them, Rāma too let the tears flow.
2.99.41
ततस्सुमन्त्रेण गुहेन चैव
समीयतू राजसुतावरण्ये ।
दिवाकरश्चैव निशाकरश्च
यथाऽम्बरे शुक्रबृहस्पतिभ्याम् ॥
Then the two princes embraced Sumantra and Guha in that forest, as the sun and the moon would Venus and Jupiter in the sky.
2.99.42
तान्पार्थिवान्वारणयूथपाभान्
समागतां स्तत्र महत्यरण्ये ।
वनौकसस्तेऽपि समीक्ष्य सर्वेऽ-
प्यश्रूण्यमुञ्चन्प्रविहाय हर्षम् ॥
All the usually happy forest dwellers also shed tears seeing those rulers of the earth, who were magnificent like lead elephants, meeting thus in a forest.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकोनशततमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninety ninth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
