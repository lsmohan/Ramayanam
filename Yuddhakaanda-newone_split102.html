Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 102
In this Sarga, Rāma resumes the fight with Rāvaṇa. The Dēvas, Gandharvas and Dānavas watching that fight from the sky feel that it is unfair that Rāma has to fight standing on the ground while Rāvaṇa does from the advantage of the chariot. Reacting to that valid observation, Mahēndra sends his own chariot along with the charioteer Mātali, and also his great bow and a Ṡakti. Rāma gets into the chariot and continues the fight, responding to all the Astras of Rāvaṇa with equally powerful ones.
Rāvaṇa manages, in that battle, to strike down the flagstaff of Rāma's chariot and mangle its horses with his arrows, and that worries the Vānaras, Vibheeshaṇa, Dēvas and Gandharvas, Dānavas, Cāraṇas, Siddhas and the Ṛshis. But that only makes Rāma even more furious. Suras, watching the fight from the sky, hail for the victory of Rāma and Asuras do the same for Rāvaṇa.
Meanwhile, Rāvaṇa hurls an extremely powerful spear at Rāma. Rāma tries to answer it with a volley of arrows. But the spear burns the arrows away like fire does moths. Seeing his arrows fail, he hurls the Ṡakti sent by Indra and destroys the spear of Rāvaṇa. Rāma then strikes down Rāvaṇa's horses, and tears his chest apart with arrows, and also plants three of them on his forehead. Rāvaṇa becomes worried and angry.
6.102.1
लक्ष्मणेन तु तद्वाक्यम्
उक्तं श्रुत्वा स राघवः ।
सन्दधे परवीरघ्नो
धनुरादाय वीर्यवान् ।
रावणाय शरान् घोरान्
विससर्ज चमूमुखे ॥
On hearing those words said by Lakshmaṇa, Rāghava, slayer of the foe, seized his bow, fitted formidable arrows to it and shot them at Rāvaṇa on that battle front.
6.102.2
अथान्यं रथमारुह्य
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ।
अभ्यद्रवत काकुत्स्थम्
स्वर्भानुरिव भास्करम् ॥
And the king of Rākshasas, getting into another chariot, attacked Kākutstha, as Rāhu does the sun.
6.102.3
दशग्रीवो रथस्थस्तु
रामं वज्रोपमैः शरैः ।
आजघान महाशैलम्
धाराभिरिव तोयदः ॥
Seated in his chariot, the Ten-necked battered Rāma with arrows that fell like the Vajra, as a cloud does a mountain with its torrents.
6.102.4
दीप्तपावक सङ्काशैः
शरैः काञ्चनभूषणैः ।
अभ्यवर्षद्रणे रामो
दशग्रीवं समाहितः ॥
And Rāma, with single-minded focus, sent showers of arrows decked in gold that burnt like tongues of flame, on the Ten-necked.
6.102.5
भूमौ स्थितस्य रामस्य
रथस्थस्य च रक्षसः ।
न समं युद्धमित्याहुः
देवगन्धर्वदानवाः ॥
The Dēvas, Gandharvas and Dānavas remarked that the fight between Rāma, standing on the ground and the Rākshasa, seated in his chariot was not a fair one.
6.102.6
ततो देववरः श्रीमान्
श्रुत्वा तेषां वचोऽमृतम् ।
आहूय मातलिं शक्रो
वचनं चेदमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing their words that were so well said, Ṡakra, the foremost of the Dēvas, summoned Mātali and said these words:
6.102.7
रथेन मम भूमिष्ठम्
शीघ्रं याहि रघूत्तमम् ।
आहूय भूतलं यातः
कुरु देव हितं महत् ॥
Take my chariot and go to the prominent descendant of Raghu who is on the earth. By getting on to the earth and being at his service, you do a huge service to the Dēvas themselves.
6.102.8
इत्युक्तो देवराजेन
मातलिर्देवसारथिः ।
प्रणम्य शिरसा देवम्
ततो वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Thus told by the king of the Dēvas Mātali, the charioteer of the Dēvas, said these words, saluting him, his head bowed:
6.102.9
शीघ्रं यास्यामि देवेन्द्र
सारध्यं च करोम्यहम् ।
ततो हयैश्च संयोज्य
हरितैः स्यन्दनोत्तमम् ॥
O Lord of Dēvas! I will go right away, yoking the green horses to that excellent chariot and shall drive it myself (for Rāghava).
6.102.10
ततः काञ्चनचित्राङ्गः
किङ्किणीशतभूषितः ।
तरुणादित्यसङ्काशो
वैडूर्यमयकूबरः ।
सदश्वैः काञ्चनापीडैः
युक्तः श्वेतप्रकीर्णकैः ।
हरिभिः सूर्यसङ्काशैः
हेमजालविभूषितैः ।
रुक्मवेणुध्वजः श्रीमान्
देवराजरथो वरः ।
देवराजेन सन्दिष्टो
रथमारुह्य मातलिः ।
अभ्यवर्तत काकुत्स्थम्
अवतीर्य त्रिविष्टपात् ॥
Thereupon Mātali, as instructed by the King of the Dēvas, got into his magnificent chariot, that had an array of gold fittings and hundreds of jingle bells, that shone like the morning sun, with its shaft decorated with lazulite, that was yoked with splendid steeds of green complexion, which shone like the sun with their gold accouterments and ornaments, that was equipped with white fly-whisks and was adorned with a beautiful flagstaff of gold, and got down from the the heaven to the earth and went to Kākutstha.
6.102.14
अब्रवीच्च तदा रामम्
सप्रतोदो रथे स्थितः ।
प्राञ्जलिर्मातलिर्वाक्यम्
सहस्राक्षस्य सारथिः ॥
Then, from that chariot, the charioteer of the Thousand-Eyed, whip in hand, said these words to Rāma, joining his palms in reverence:
6.102.15
सहस्राक्षेण काकुत्स्थ
रथोऽयं विजयाय ते ।
दत्तस्तव महासत्त्व
श्रीमान् शत्रुनिबर्हण ॥
O blessed one! O destroyer of the foe! O Kākutstha! O you of extraordinary strength! This chariot has been offered to you by the Thousand-Eyed, to help you win the battle!
6.102.16
इदमैन्द्रं महच्चापम्
कवचं चाग्निसन्निभम् ।
शराश्चादित्यसङ्काशाः
शक्तिश्च विमला शिता ॥
Here is the great bow of Indra, the armor that blazes like fire, arrows that shine like the sun, and a Ṡakti, clean and sharp.
6.102.17
आरुह्येमं रथं वीर
राक्षसं जहि रावणम् ।
मया सारथिना राजन्
महेन्द्र इव दानवान् ॥
O Veera! Please get into the chariot and kill Rāvaṇa, the Rākshasa, like Mahēndra did the Dānavas, while I drive this for you, O King!
6.102.18
इत्युक्तः सम्परिक्रम्य
रथं समभिवाद्य च ।
आरुरोह तदा रामो
लोकान् लक्ष्म्या विराजयन् ॥
Thus invited, Rāma went around the chariot in reverence and prostrated before it and then got into it, while brightening all the worlds with his splendor.
6.102.19
बभूव च महायुद्धम्
तुमुलं रोमहर्षणम् ।
रामस्य च महाबाहो
रावणस्य च रक्षसः ॥
Then there ensued a great, tumultuous and hair-raising fight, between Rāma of mighty arm and Rāvaṇa, the Rākshasa.
6.102.20
स गान्धर्वेण गान्धर्वम्
दैवं दैवेन राघवः ।
अस्त्रं राक्षसराजस्य
जघान परमास्त्रवित् ॥
Rāghava, master of Astras, struck down the Astras of the king of Rākshasas the Gandharva's Astra with Gandharva's Astra, and the Dēva's Astra with Dēva's Astra.
6.102.21
अस्त्रं तु परमं घोरम्
राक्षसं राक्षसाधिपः ।
ससर्ज परमक्रुद्धः
पुनरेव निशाचरः ॥
The King of Rākshasas, a Night-rover, in his violent rage, deployed the extremely dreadful Rākshasa Astra again.
6.102.22
ते रावणधनुर्मुक्ताः
शराः काञ्चनभूषणाः ।
अभ्यवर्तन्त काकुत्स्थम्
सर्पा भूत्वा महाविषाः ॥
The arrows decked with gold that were discharged from Rāvaṇa’s bow, turned into venomous serpents as they came upon Kākutstha.
6.102.23
ते दीप्तवदना दीप्तम्
वमन्तो ज्वलनं मुखैः ।
राममेवाभ्यवर्तन्त
व्यादितास्या भयानकाः ॥
They flew straight at Rāma, spewing fire from their blazing mouths that were opened wide and were terrifying.
6.102.24
तैर्वासुकिसमस्पर्शैः
दीप्तभोगैर्महाविषैः ।
दिशश्च सन्तताः सर्वाः
प्रदिशश्च समावृताः ॥
All the cardinals and the corners were completely covered by those serpents of virulent venom and blazing hoods, whose bite rivaled that of Vāsuki.
6.102.25
तान् दृष्ट्वा पन्नगान् रामः
समापतत आहवे ।
अस्त्रं गारुत्मतं घोरम्
प्रादुश्चक्रे भयावहम् ॥
Seeing those serpents thus swoop down in that battle, Rāma deployed the ghastly, frightful Garuḍa's Astra.
6.102.26
ते राघवशरा मुक्ता
रुक्मपुङ्खाः शिखिप्रभाः ।
सुपर्णाः काञ्चना भूत्वा
विचेरुः सर्पशत्रवः ॥
Those arrows decked with gold and shining like fire, shot by Rāma, turned into golden eagles, the natural foe of snakes, and pounced on them.
6.102.27
ते तान् सर्वान् शरान् जघ्नुः
सर्परूपान् महाजवान् ।
सुपर्णरूपा रामस्य
विशिखाः कामरूपिणः ॥
And those arrows of Rāma which were in the form of eagles and which could, for that matter, take any form at will, struck down all those extremely swift arrows (of Rāvaṇa) that were in the form of serpents.
6.102.28
अस्त्रे प्रतिहते क्रुद्धो
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ।
अभ्यवर्षत्तदा रामम्
घोराभिः शरवृष्टिभिः ॥
With his Astra thus foiled, the enraged Rāvaṇa, the King of Rākshasas, drenched Rāma in a fusillade of terrible arrows.
6.102.29
ततः शरसहस्रेण
राममक्लिष्टकारिणम् ।
अर्दयित्वा शरौघेण
मातलिं प्रत्यविध्यत ।
चिच्छेद केतुमुद्धिश्य
शरेणैकेन रावणः ॥
Having harrowed, with a thousand arrows, Rāma who does things with ease, Rāvaṇa struck Mātali with a flood of darts and cut down his flagstaff with a single arrow that he shot at it.
6.102.30
पातयित्वा रथोपस्थे
रथात् केतुं च काञ्चनम् ।
ऐन्द्रानपि जघानाश्वान्
शरजालेन रावणः ॥
Felling the golden flagstaff down from the chariot to its floor, Rāvaṇa also struck the horses of Indra with a volley of arrows.
6.102.31
तं दृष्ट्वा सुमहत्कर्म
रावणस्य दुरात्मनः ।
विषेदुर्देवगन्धर्वा
दानवाश्चारणैः सह ॥
Seeing that extraordinary achievement of the evil-minded Rāvaṇa, the Dēvas and Gandharvas along with the Dānavas and Cāraṇas were worried.
6.102.32
राममार्तं तदा दृष्ट्वा
सिद्धाश्च परमर्षयः ।
व्यथिता वानरेन्द्राश्च
बभूवुः सविभीषणाः ।
रामचन्द्रमसं दृष्ट्वा
ग्रस्तं रावणराहुणा ॥
Seeing Rāma thus oppressed, the Siddhas and the supreme Ṛshis were distressed. And so were all the Vānara chiefs and Vibheeshaṇa, seeing the moon called Rāma caught by the Rāhu called Rāvaṇa.
6.102.34
प्राजापत्यं च नक्षत्रम्
रोहिणीं शशिनः प्रियाम् ।
समाक्रम्य बुधस्तस्थौ
प्रजानामशुभावहः ॥
The planet Budha occulted the star Rōhiṇee, the beloved of the moon, which was presided over by Prajāpati, an ominous portent for all the creatures.
6.102.35
सधूमपरिवृत्तोर्मिः
प्रज्वलन्निव सागरः ।
उत्पपात तदा क्रुद्धः
स्पृशन्निव दिवाकरम् ॥
With its billows covered in haze, the sea blazed in anger as it shot up, seeming to touch the sun.
6.102.36
शस्त्रवर्णः सुपरुषो
मन्दरश्मिर्दिवाकरः ।
अदृश्यत कबन्धाङ्कः
संसक्तो धूमकेतुना ॥
And the sun itself was quite dreadful, steel-blue in color, rays dimmed, accompanied by a comet and with the silhouette of a headless trunk seen on its face.
6.102.37
कोसलानां च नक्षत्रम्
व्यक्तमिन्द्राग्निदैवतम् ।
आक्रम्याङ्गारकस्तस्थौ
विशाखामपि चाम्बरे ॥
The planet Aṅgāraka was seen occulting the star Viṡākha, the constellation of the Ikshwākus, which has Indra and Agni as its presiding deities.
6.102.38
दशास्यो विंशतिभुजः
प्रगृहीतशरासनः ।
अदृश्यत दशग्रीवो
मैनाक इव पर्वतः ॥
With his ten faces and twenty hands and bow grasped in his hand, the Ten-necked looked like Maināka mountain.
6.102.39
निरस्यमानो रामस्तु
दशग्रीवेण रक्षसा ।
नाशक्नोदभिसन्धातुम्
सायकान् रणमूर्धनि ॥
Thus being assailed by the Ten-necked Rākshasa on that battlefield, Rāma found himself unable to even mount arrows in the bow.
6.102.40
स कृत्वा भ्रुकुटिं क्रुद्धः
किञ्चित् संरक्तलोचनः ।
जगाम सुमहाक्रोधम्
निर्दहन्निव चक्षुषा ॥
He became extremely outraged, his eyebrows knotted in a frown, eyes turning slightly blood-red and seeming to burn everything with just a glance.
6.102.41
तस्य क्रुद्धस्य वदनम्
दृष्ट्वा रामस्य धीमतः ।
सर्वभूतानि वित्रेसुः
प्राकम्पत च मेदिनी ॥
Seeing the frown on the face of the sagacious Rāma, all the creatures were terrified, and the earth shook.
6.102.42
सिंहशार्दूलवान् शैलः
सञ्चचाल चलद्रुमः ।
बभूव चातिक्षुभितः
समुद्रः सरितां पतिः ॥
The mountain with its lions, tigers and the swaying trees, shook. The ocean, lord of streams, went into a great turmoil.
6.102.43
खगाश्च खरनिर्घोषा
गगने परुषा घनाः ।
औत्पातिकानि नर्दन्तः
समन्तात् परिचक्रमुः ॥
Birds with raucous cries and clouds thundering harshly billowing in every direction presaged that something ominous was in store.
6.102.44
रामं दृष्ट्वा सुसङ्क्रुद्धम्
उत्पातांश्च सुदारुणान् ।
वित्रेसुः सर्वभूतानि
रावणस्याभवद्भयेम् ॥
Seeing Rāma in rage and also the terrible evil portents, all creatures became terrified; and Rāvaṇa also caved in to fear.
6.102.45
विमानस्थास्तदा देवा
गन्धर्वाश्च महोरगाः ।
ऋषिदानवदैत्याश्च
गरुत्मन्तश्च खेचराः ।
ददृशुस्ते महायुद्धम्
लोकसंवर्तसंस्थितम् ।
नानाप्रहरणैर्भीमैः
शूरयोः सम्प्रयुद्ध्यतोः ॥
The Dēvas in their aerial cars, the Gandharvas, the great Uragas, the Ṛshis, the Dānavas and the Daityas, the eagles and the sojourners in the sky, witnessed that fierce and great battle, which seemed to put an end to the worlds, between those two great warriors who fought with a variety of weapons.
6.102.47
ऊचुः सुरासुराः सर्वे
तदा विग्रहमागताः ।
प्रेक्षमाणा महद्युद्धम्
वाक्यं भक्त्या प्रहृष्टवत् ॥
All the Suras and Asuras, who were watching the great battle, found themselves pitted against each other as they shouted in excitement in support of the side they took.
6.102.48
दशग्रीवं जयेत्याहुः
असुराः समवस्थिताः ।
देवा राममथोचुस्ते
त्वं जयेति पुनःपुनः ॥
The Asuras from their places shouted, ‘Victory to the Ten-necked!’, while the Dēvas repeatedly exclaimed, ‘May you be victorious, O Rāma!’
6.102.49
एतस्मिन्नन्तरे क्रोधात्
राघवस्य स रावणः ।
प्रहर्तुकामो दुष्टात्मा
स्पृशन् प्रहरणं महत् ।
वज्रसारं महानादम्
सर्वशत्रुनिबर्हणम् ।
शैलशृङ्गनिभैः कूटैः
चित्त दृष्टि भयावहम् ।
सधूममिव तीक्ष्णाग्रम्
युगान्ताग्निचयोपमम् ।
अतिरौद्रमनासाद्यम्
कालेनापि दुरासदम् ।
त्रासनं सर्वभूतानाम्
दारणं भेदनं तदा ।
प्रदीप्तमिव रोषेण
शूलं जग्राह रावणः ॥
Meanwhile the evil-minded Rāvaṇa, who could make everyone wail, grabbed in rage a great weapon, a blazing spear that was hard as Vajra and that could rend and break (the foe), to strike Rāghava.
Capable of destroying all the enemies, it made a huge clatter as it passed. It was imposing like a mountain peak and its tips were terrifying even to behold, making one's heart tremble.
Extremely terrifying, impossible to approach, spewing smoke from its tips, it resembled the raging fire at the end of the Yuga. Even Yama could not stand against it, which terrified all the creatures.
6.102.53
तच्छूलं परमक्रुद्धो
मध्ये जग्राह वीर्यवान् ।
अनीकैः समरे शूरै
राक्षसैः परिवारितः ॥
The valiant one, with armies of Rākshasa warriors about him on that battlefield, held it in the middle.
6.102.54
समुद्यम्य महाकायो
ननाद युधि भैरवम् ।
संरक्तनयनो रोषात्
स्वसैन्यमभिहर्षयन् ॥
And lifting it, he, of huge body, his eyes blood-red with rage, let off a menacing yell, cheering up the army.
6.102.55
पृथिवीं चान्तरिक्षं च
दिशश्च प्रदिशस्तथा ।
प्राकम्पयत्तदा शब्दो
राक्षसेन्द्रस्य दारुणः ॥
The earth, the sky, the cardinals and the corners were shaken by that terrible yell of the king of Rākshasas.
6.102.56
अतिकायस्य नादेन
तेन तस्य दुरात्मनः ।
सर्वभूतानि वित्रेसुः
सागरश्च प्रचुक्षुभे ॥
All creatures were terrified by the yell of that evil-minded one of humongous body, and the ocean was in a turmoil.
6.102.57
स गृहीत्वा महावीर्यः
शूलं तद्रावणो महत् ।
विनद्य सुमहानादम्
रामं परुषमब्रवीत् ॥
Rāvaṇa of extraordinary valor, grabbing that mighty spear and letting off a mighty yell, addressed Rāma in a harsh voice:
6.102.58
शूलोऽयं वज्रसारस्ते
राम रोषान्मयोद्यतः ।
तव भ्रातृसहायस्य
सम्यक् प्राणान् हरिष्यति ॥
O Rāma! This spear, hard as Vajra, which I raised now in extreme rage, shall instantly take your life, no matter how much help you can get from your brother.
6.102.59
रक्षसामद्य शूराणाम्
निहतानां चमूमुखे ।
त्वां निहत्य रणश्लाघी
करोमि तरसा समम् ।
तिष्ठेदानीं निहन्मि त्वाम्
एष शूलेन राघव ॥
I, famed for my skills in fight, shall shove you to meet the same fate that the many Rākshasa warriors who were slain on the battle front did. Stay right there, O Rāghava! I shall slay you with this spear.
6.102.60
एवमुक्त्वा स चिक्षेप
तच्छूलं राक्षसाधिपः ।
तद्रावणकरान्मुक्तम्
विद्युन्मालासमावृतम् ।
अष्टघण्टं महानादम्
वियद्गतमशोभत ॥
Saying this, the lord of Rākshasas hurled that spear. Hurled by Rāvaṇa’s hand, it shone in the sky, emitting flashes of lightning and making a tremendous noise with its eight bells.
6.102.62
तच्छूलं राघवो दृष्ट्वा
ज्वलन्तं घोरदर्शनम् ।
ससर्ज विशिखान् रामः
चापमायम्य वीर्यवान् ॥
Seeing the spear that was blazing balefully, the valiant Rāma bent his bow to the full and let arrows fly from it.
6.102.63
आपतन्तं शरौघेण
वारयामास राघवः ।
उत्पतन्तं युगान्ताग्निम्
जलौघैरिव वासवः ॥
Rāghava tried to stop it as it came on, with a volley of arrows, as Vāsava might seek to put down, with torrential downpour, the conflagration that rages at the end of the Yuga.
6.102.64
निर्ददाह स तान् बाणान्
रामकार्मुकनिस्सृतान् ।
रावणस्य महाशूलः
पतङ्गानिव पावकः ॥
The mighty spear of Rāvaṇa burnt away those arrows that surged from the bow of Rāma, like fire burning up moths.
6.102.65
तान् दृष्ट्वा भस्मसाद्भूतान्
शूल संस्पर्शचूर्णितान् ।
सायकानन्तरिक्षस्थान्
राघवः क्रोधमाहरत् ॥
Seeing those arrows pulverized and reduced to ashes as they came in contact with that spear, Rāghava worked himself up into a fury.
6.102.66
स तां मातलिनाऽऽनीताम्
शक्तिं वासवनिर्मिताम् ।
जग्राह परमक्रुद्धो
राघवो रघुनन्दनः ॥
The enraged scion and delight of the Raghu dynasty, then took the Ṡakti that was built by Vāsava, which Mātali had brought.
6.102.67
सा तोलिता बलवता
शक्तिर्घण्टाकृतस्वना ।
नभः प्रज्वालयामास
युगान्तोल्केव सप्रभा ॥
Hurled by that mighty one, that Ṡakti, creating a din with the sound of its bells, lit up the sky, shining like a meteor that emerges when the Yuga ends.
6.102.68
सा क्षिप्ता राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
तस्मिन् शूले पपात ह ।
भिन्नः शक्त्या महान् शूलो
निपपात हतद्युतिः ॥
Thus hurled, it fell on the spear of the lord of Rākshasas. The mighty spear, shattered by the Ṡakti, fell to the ground, its luster extinguished.
6.102.69
निर्बिभेद ततो बाणैः
हयानस्य महाजवान् ।
रामस्तीक्ष्णैर्महावेगैः
बाणवद्भिरजिह्मगैः ॥
Then Rāma struck down the fleet horses with sharp arrows that flew fast in a straight line with a cracking noise.
6.102.70
निर्बिभेदोरसि ततो
रावणं निशितैः शरैः ।
राघवः परमायत्तो
ललाटे पत्रिभिस्त्रिभिः ॥
He then tore apart Rāvaṇa's chest with sharp arrows. Exerting himself to the full, Rāghava struck him on the forehead with three winged darts.
6.102.71
स शरैर्भिन्नसर्वाङ्गो
गात्रप्रस्रुतशोणितः ।
राक्षसेन्द्रः समूहस्थः
फुल्लाशोक इवाबभौ ॥
With every limb mangled by arrows, and blood pouring out of his body, the King of Rākshasas shone like an Aṡōka tree in flower standing out in a grove.
6.102.72
स रामबाणैरभिविद्धगात्रो
निशाचरेन्द्रः क्षतजार्द्रगात्रः ।
जगाम खेदं च समाजमध्ये
क्रोधं च चक्रे सुभृशं तदानीम् ॥
His body beaten by the arrows of Rāma, blood pouring all over from it, the lord of Night-rovers, caved in to sorrow in the midst of everyone, and also worked up himself up into a fit of extreme fury.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्व्यधिकशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and second Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
