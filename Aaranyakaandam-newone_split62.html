Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 62
In this Sarga, Rāma, after it becomes clear that Seetā is nowhere to be seen, becomes delirious and talks to her as if she is present. He finds it difficult to fathom how it would be to go back to Ayōdhyā without Seetā, having left along with her. He tells Lakshmaṇa to leave him in the Vana and go back to Ayōdhyā and take care of the mothers and convey to Bharata to continue his rule.
3.62.1
सीतामपश्यन्धर्मात्मा
शोकोपहतचेतनः ।
विललाप महाबाहू
रामः कमललोचनः ॥
Not finding Seetā anywhere, Rāma, the Dharmātma of lotus eyes and mighty arm, lamented, his heart stricken with grief.
3.62.2
पश्यन्निव च तां सीताम्
अपश्यन्मदनार्दितः ।
उवाच राघवो वाक्यम्
विलापाश्रयदुर्वचम् ॥
With his love pouring out, Rāghava said these words, which came out with difficulty because of grief, as if he was looking at her, even though she was not seen:
3.62.3
त्वमशोकस्य शाखाभिः
पुष्पप्रियतया प्रिये ।
आवृणोषि शरीरं ते
मम शोकविवर्धिनी ॥
You, being fond of flowers, cover your body with the branches of the Aṡoka tree; but, O my beloved, you are only adding to my grief.
3.62.4
कदलीस्कन्धसदृशौ
कदल्या संवृतावुभौ ।
ऊरू पश्यामि ते देवि
नासि शक्ता निगूहितुम् ॥
I see you hiding your thighs that are like plantain-stems, behind the plantains; but, my queen, you cannot hide them from me!
3.62.5
कर्णिकारवनं भद्रे
हसन्ती देवि सेवसे ।
अलं ते परिहासेन
मम बाधावहेन वै ॥
You slip into the grove of Karṇikāra trees, laughing as you do so; my lady, enough of this jesting, it only causes me pain.
3.62.6
परिहासेन किं सीते
परिश्रान्तस्य मे प्रिये ।
अयं स परिहासोऽपि
साधु देवि न रोचते ॥
How could you jest with me, O Seetā, my beloved, when I am dead tired? Though I love fun, I cannot take it now, my lady!
3.62.7
विशेषेणाश्रमस्थाने
हासोऽयं न प्रशस्यते ।
अवगच्छामि ते शीलम्
परिहासप्रियं प्रिये ।
आगच्छ त्वं विशालाक्षि
शून्योऽयमुटजस्तव ॥
I know, my beloved, that you love to poke fun. But fun, especially in an Āṡrama, is not appreciated. O you of wide eyes, come, your cottage is empty!
3.62.8
सुव्यक्तं राक्षसैस्सीता
भक्षिता वा हृतापि वा ।
न हि सा विलपन्तं माम्
उपसम्प्रैति लक्ष्मण ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! It is clear that Seetā was either devoured or abducted by the Rākshasas! That is why she is not rushing to me when I cry like this!
3.62.9
एतानि मृगयूथानि
साश्रुनेत्राणि लक्ष्मण ।
शंसन्तीव हि वैदेहीम्
भक्षितां रजनीचरैः ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! These droves of deer with their teary eyes are indeed telling that Vaidēhee was devoured by the Night-rovers!
3.62.10
हा ममार्ये क्व यातासि
हा साध्वि वरवर्णिनि ।
हा सकामा त्वया देवी
कैकेयी सा भविष्यति ॥
O my noble one, where did you go? O gentle lady of lovely complexion, because of you, she, the lady Kaikēyee, will now have her wish fulfilled!
3.62.11
सीतया सह निर्यातो
विना सीतामुपागतः ।
कथं नाम प्रवेक्ष्यामि
शून्यमन्तःपुरं पुनः ॥
Having left along with Seetā, how can I go back without Seetā and enter those empty inner quarters?
3.62.12
निर्वीर्य इति लोको माम्
निर्दयश्चेति वक्ष्यति ।
कातरत्वं प्रकाशं हि
सीतापनयनेन मे ॥
The world is going to call me incompetent, and also unconcerned! With Seetā being taken away, it is clear how useless I am!
3.62.13
निवृत्तवनवासश्च
जनकं मिथिलाधिपम् ।
कुशलं परिपृच्छन्तम्
कथं शक्ष्ये निरीक्षितुम् ॥
How will I look into his eyes, when Janaka, the king of Mithilā, asks about my wellbeing upon my return?
3.62.14
विदेहराजो नूनं माम्
दृष्ट्वा विरहितं तया ।
सुतास्नेहेन सन्तप्तो
मोहस्य वशमेष्यति ॥
When he sees that I have come back without her, the King of Vidēha will certainly swoon, burned out by his love for his daughter.
3.62.15
अथवा न गमिष्यामि
पुरीं भरतपालिताम् ।
स्वर्गोऽपि सीतया हीनः
शून्य एव मतो मम ॥
May be I should not go to the city governed by Bharata. I think, even Heaven would be a desolate place for me, without Seetā.
3.62.16
मामिहोत्सृज्य हि वने
गच्छायोध्यां पुरीं शुभाम् ।
न त्वहं तां विना सीताम्
जीवेयं हि कथञ्चन ॥
May be you should leave me here and go to the auspicious city of Ayōdhyā. There is no way I can continue to live without her, Seetā.
3.62.17
गाढमाश्लिष्य भरतो
वाच्यो मद्वचनात्त्वया ।
अनुज्ञातोऽसि रामेण
पालयेति वसुन्धराम् ॥
Taking him into your warm embrace, you should say this to him, on my behalf: ‘You have Rāma’s consent to (continue to) rule the earth!’
Bharata agrees to rule Ayōdhyā only for fourteen years (Ṡlōkas 2.112.25b-26a).
3.62.18
अम्बा च मम कैकेयी
सुमित्रा च त्वया विभो ।
कौसल्या च यथान्यायम्
अभिवाद्या ममाऽज्ञया ।
रक्षणीया प्रयत्नेन
भवता सोक्तकारिणा ॥
O you of great obedience and abilities! Consider it my instruction and pay obeisance to my mother Kousalyā, Sumitrā and Kaikēyee, as would be the expectation, and take every effort to care for them!
3.62.20
सीतायाश्च विनाशोऽयम्
मम चामित्रकर्शन ।
विस्तरेण जनन्या मे
विनिवेद्यस्त्वया भवेत् ॥
O decimator of foes! You should brief my mother, in detail, about how Seetā and I have been destroyed.
3.62.21
इति विलपति राघवे सुदीने
वनमुपगम्य तया विना सुकेश्या ।
भयविकलमुखस्तु लक्ष्मणोऽपि
व्यथितमना भृशमातुरो बभूव ॥
Upon Rāghava lamenting thus in an extremely pitiful manner for losing Seetā of lovely tresses after coming to the Vana, Lakshmaṇa too was greatly distressed, his face disfigured with fear and his mind troubled.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे द्विषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty second Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
