Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 43
In this Sarga, Vānaras and Rākshasas engage in duels, with Aṅgada fighting Indrajit, Saṃpāti fighting Prajaṅgha, Hanumān fighting Jambumāli, Vibheeshaṇa fighting Mitraghna, Gaja fighting Tapana, Neela fighting Nikumbha, Sugreeva fighting Praghasa, Lakshmaṇa fighting Virūpāksha, Rāma fighting Agnikētu, Raṡmikētu, Suptaghna and Yajñakōpa, Mainda fighting Vajramushṭhi, Dwivida fighting Aṡaniprabha, Nala fighting Pratapana, and Sushēṇa fighting Vidyunmāli.
On this first day of the battle, the prominent Rākshasas Saptaparṇa, Agnikētu, Raṡmikētu, Suptaghna, Yajñakētu, Vajramushṭhi, Aṡaniprabha and Vidyunmāli are slain.
The Rākshasas, torn quite a bit apart, wait for the sun to set, to resume their counterattack, since they grow stronger in the night.
6.43.1
युद्ध्यातां तु ततस्तेषाम्
वानराणां महात्मनाम् ।
रक्षसां सम्बभूवाथ
बलकोपः सुदारुणः ॥
Terrible strength and anger were at display as the fight ensued between Vānaras, the Mahātmas, and Rākshasas.
6.43.2
ते हयैः काञ्चनापीडैः
ध्वजैश्चाग्निशिखोपमैः ।
रथैश्चादित्यसङ्काशैः
कवचैश्च मनोरमैः ।
निर्ययू राक्षसव्याघ्रा
नादयन्तो दिशो दश ।
राक्षसा भीमकर्माणः
रावणस्य जयैषिणः ॥
With horses adorned with golden decorations, flag posts that dazzled like tongues of flame, chariots that shone like the sun, and coats-of-mail that stole one’s heart, the formidable tigers among Rākshasas, wishing victory for Rāvaṇa, came out of the city, creating a din that spread in the ten directions.
6.43.4
वानराणामपि चमूः
बृहती जयमिच्छताम् ।
अभ्यधावत तां सेनाम्
रक्षसां कामरूपिणाम् ॥
And the huge Vānara army also, equally avid for victory, dashed towards the army of Rākshasas who could assume any form at will.
6.43.5
एतस्मिन्नन्तरे तेषाम्
अन्योन्यमभिधावताम् ।
रक्षसां वानराणां च
द्वन्द्वयुद्धमवर्तत ॥
With that, as they dashed into each other, there ensued several duels between the Rākshasas and Vānaras.
6.43.6
अङ्गदेनेन्द्रजित् सार्धम्
वालिपुत्रेण राक्षसः ।
अयुध्यत महातेजाः
त्र्यम्बकेण यथाऽन्तकः ॥
The extremely powerful Indrajit, the Rākshasa fought with Aṅgada, the son of Vāli, like Yama (the God of Death) fighting with (Ṡiva), the Lord of three eyes.
6.43.7
प्रजङ्घेन च सम्पातिः
नित्यं दुर्मर्षणो रणे ।
जम्बुमालिनमारब्धो
हनुमानपि वानरः ॥
Saṃpāti, who could never be stood against in battle, fought with Prajaṅgha, while Hanumān, the Vānara, began to fight with Jambumāli.
6.43.8
सङ्गतः सुमहाक्रोधो
राक्षसो रावणानुजः ।
समरे तीक्ष्णवेगेन
मित्रघ्नेन विभीषणः ॥
The Rākshasa Vibheeshaṇa, Rāvaṇa’s younger brother, was in extreme rage as he closed in combat with the dashing Mitraghna.
6.43.9
तपनेन गजः सार्धम्
राक्षसेन महाबलः ।
निकुम्भेन महातेजा
नीलोऽपि समयुद्ध्यत ॥
Gaja, of immense strength, fought with the Rākshasa Tapana, and the extremely powerful Neela fought with Nikumbha.
6.43.10
वानरेन्द्रस्तु सुग्रीवः
प्रघसेन समागतः ।
सङ्गतः समरे श्रीमान्
विरूपाक्षेण लक्ष्मणः ॥
Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras, confronted Praghasa, and the blessed Lakshmaṇa was locked in combat with Virūpāksha.
6.43.11
आग्निकेतुश्च दुर्धर्षो
रश्मिकेतुश्च राक्षसः ।
सुप्तघ्नो यज्ञकोपश्च
रामेण सह सङ्गताः ॥
The invincible Agnikētu, the Rākshasa Raṡmikētu, Suptaghna and Yajñakōpa were locked in a fight with Rāma.
6.43.12
वज्रमुष्टिस्तु मैन्देन
द्विविदेनाशनिप्रभः ।
राक्षसाभ्यां सुघोराभ्याम्
कपिमुख्यौ समागतौ ॥
The fierce Rākshasas Vajramushṭhi and Aṡaniprabha were locked in combat with the great Vānara chiefs Mainda and Dwivida respectively.
6.43.13
वीरः प्रतपनो घोरो
राक्षसो रणदुर्धरः ।
समरे तीक्ष्णवेगेन
नलेन समयुद्ध्यत ॥
The valiant and terrible Rākshasa Pratapana, who could not be stand against in battle, fought with Nala, who was impetuous in attack.
6.43.14
धर्मस्य पुत्रो बलवान्
सुषेण इति विश्रुतः ।
स विद्युन्मालिना सार्धम्
अयुध्यत महाकपिः ॥
Dharma’s son, the famed Sushēṇa, the strong and mighty Vānara, fought with Vidyunmāli.
6.43.15
वानराश्चापरे भीमा
राक्षसैरपरैः सह ।
द्वन्द्वं समीयुः सहसा
युद्ध्वा च बहुभिः सह ॥
Many other formidable Vānaras, who had fought with many already, were engaged in duels with other Rākshasas.
6.43.16
तत्रासीत् सुमहद्युद्धम्
तुमुलं रोमहर्षणम् ।
रक्षसां वानराणां च
वीराणां जयमिच्छताम् ॥
Then there ensued a fierce, tumultuous and hair-raising battle, between Rākshasa and Vānara warriors who were both keen on victory.
6.43.17
हरिराक्षसदेहेभ्यः
प्रसृताः केशशाद्वलाः ।
शरीरसङ्घाटवहाः
प्रसस्रुः शोणितापगाः ॥
Rivers of blood flowed from the bodies of the Vānaras and the Rākshasas, on which floated hair, like moss, and corpses, like logs.
6.43.18
आजघानेन्द्रजित् क्रुद्धो
वज्रेणेव शतक्रतुः ।
अङ्गदं गदया वीरम्
शत्रुसैन्यविदारणम् ॥
The enraged Indrajit struck, with his mace, the valiant Aṅgada who tears apart the enemy forces, like Ṡatakratu (Indra) striking with the Vajra.
6.43.19
तस्य काञ्चनचित्राङ्गम्
रथं साश्वं ससारथिम् ।
जघान समरे श्रीमान्
अङ्गदो वेगवान् कपिः ॥
The blessed Aṅgada, the swift Vānara, smashed the chariot (of Indrajit) that was beautifully decked with gold, along with its horses and the charioteer.
6.43.20
सम्पातिस्तु त्रिभिर्बाणैः
प्रजङ्घेन समाहतः ।
निजघानाश्वकर्णेन
प्रजङ्घं रणमूर्धनि ॥
Saṃpāti, struck by Prajaṅgha with three arrows, hit Prajaṅgha point-blank with an Aṡwakarṇa tree.
6.43.21
जम्बुमाली रथस्थस्तु
रथशक्त्या महाबलः ।
बिभेद समरे क्रुद्धो
हनूमन्तं स्तनान्तरे ॥
The immensely strong Jambumāli, seated in his chariot, angrily struck Hanumān between the nipples with the javelin, with which the chariot was equipped.
6.43.22
तस्य तं रथमास्थाय
हनूमान् मारुतात्मजः ।
प्रममाथ तलेनाशु
सह तेनैव रक्षसा ॥
Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, instantly sprang upon his chariot, and with a blow from his open palm smashed the chariot along with the Rākshasa.
6.43.23
नदन् प्रतपनो घोरो
नलं सोऽप्यन्वधावत ।
नलः प्रतपनस्याशु
पातयामास चक्षुषी ॥
The terrible Pratapana attacked Nala, yelling. Nala immediately dealt Pratapana a blow that sent his eyes flying from their sockets.
6.43.24
भिन्नगात्रः शरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
क्षिप्रहस्तेन रक्षसा ।
ग्रसन्तमिव सैन्यानि
प्रघसं वानराधिपः ।
सुग्रीवः सप्तपर्णेन
निर्बिभेद जघान च ॥
Wounded by the sharp arrows of the swift-handed Rākshasa Praghasa who seemed to be set for gobbling up his army, Sugreeva, the ruler of Vānaras, wounded and slew him with a Saptaparṇa tree.
6.43.25
अग्निकेतुश्च दुर्धर्षो
रश्मिकेतुश्च राक्षसः ।
सुप्तघ्नो यज्ञकोपश्च
रामं निर्बिभिदुः शरैः ॥
The unassailable Agnikētu, the Rākshasa Raṡmikētu, Suptaghna and Yajñakētu shot Rāma with arrows.
6.43.26
तेषां चतुर्णां रामस्तु
शिरांसि निशितैः शरै ।
क्रुद्धश्चतुर्भिश्चिच्छेद
घोरैरग्निशिखोपमैः ॥
The angered Rāma cut off the heads of those four with four sharp arrows that were fierce like tongues of flame.
6.43.27
वज्रमुष्टिस्तु मैन्देन
मुष्टिना निहतो रणे ।
पपात सरथः साश्वः
पुराट्ट इव भूतले ॥
Vajramushṭhi, slain by Mainda in the fight with a blow of the fist, fell down on the ground along with the chariot and horses like the attic of a citadel.
6.43.28
निकुम्भस्तु रणे नीलम्
नीलाञ्जनचयप्रभम् ।
निर्बिभेद शरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
करैर्मेघमिवांशुमान् ॥
Nikumbha, with sharp arrows, gored Neela, who was shining like a mound of collyrium, as the sun cleaves the clouds with its rays.
6.43.29
पुनः शरशतेनाथ
क्षिप्रहस्तो निशाचरः ।
बिभेद समरे नीलम्
निकुम्भः प्रजहास च ॥
Again the swift-handed Night-rover Nikumbha shot Neela in that fight with a hundred arrows, laughing loudly.
6.43.30
तस्यैव रथचक्रेण
नीलो विष्णुरिवाहवे ।
शिरश्चिच्छेद समरे
निकुम्भस्य च सारथेः ॥
Like Vishṇu uses his discus in a battle, Neela used the wheel of his own chariot in the fight to cut off the head of Nikumbha’s charioteer.
6.43.31
वज्राशनिसमस्पर्शो
द्विविदोऽप्यशनिप्रभम् ।
जघान गिरिशृङ्गेण
मिषतां सर्वरक्षसाम् ॥
Dwivida whirled a crest of the mountain that struck Aṡaniprabha like Vajra and thunderbolt, while all the Rākshasas watched.
6.43.32
द्विविदं वानरेन्द्रं तु
नगयोधिनमाहवे ।
शरैरशनि सङ्काशैः
स विव्याधाशनिप्रभः ॥
And Aṡaniprabha, with arrows that blazed like thunderbolts, struck Dwivida, the Lord of Vānaras, who fought hurling trees.
6.43.33
स शरैरतिविद्धाङ्गो
द्विविदः क्रोधमूर्च्छितः ।
सालेन सरथं साश्वम्
निजघानाशनिप्रभम् ॥
Dwivida, with his body struck all over by arrows, was beside himself with fury and smashed, with a Sāla tree, Aṡaniprabha and his chariot and horses.
6.43.34
विद्युन्माली रथस्थस्तु
शरैः काञ्चनभूषणैः ।
सुषेणं ताडयामास
ननाद च मुहुर्मुहुः ॥
Vidyunmāli, from his chariot, struck Sushēṇa with arrows decked with gold, repeatedly uttering war-cries.
6.43.35
तं रथस्थमथो दृष्ट्वा
सुषेणो वानरोत्तमः ।
गिरिशृङ्गेण महता
रथमाशु न्यपातयत् ॥
Seeing him seated in the chariot, Sushēṇa, the eminent Vānara, in no time, catapulted the chariot with a mountain crest.
6.43.36
लाघवेन तु संयुक्तो
विद्युन्माली निशाचरः ।
अपक्रम्य रथात्तूर्णम्
गदापाणिः क्षितौ स्थितः ॥
The Night-rover Vidyunmāli jumped skillfully out of the chariot in the nick of time, and stood on the ground, armed with a mace.
6.43.37
ततः क्रोधसमाविष्टः
सुषेणो हरिपुङ्गवः ।
शिलां सुमहतीं गृह्य
निशाचरमभिद्रवत् ॥
The enraged Sushēṇa, the bull among Vānaras, thereupon snatched a huge rock and dashed onto the Night-rover.
6.43.38
तमापतन्तं गदया
विद्युन्माली निशाचरः ।
वक्षस्यभिजघानाशु
सुषेणं हरिसत्तमम् ॥
As he came on, the Night-rover Vidyunmāli struck Sushēṇa, the eminent Vānara, swiftly on the chest with his mace.
6.43.39
गदाप्रहारं तं घोरम्
अचिन्त्य प्लवगोत्तमः ।
तां शिलां पातयामास
तस्योरसि महामृधे ॥
Caring two hoots about that terrible blow from the mace, that eminent Vānara hurled a huge rock on his chest, in that fierce fight.
6.43.40
शिलाप्रहाराभिहतो
विद्युन्माली निशाचरः ।
निष्पिष्टहृदयो भूमौ
गतासुर्निपपात ह ॥
His heart shattered by the blow from the rock, the Night-rover Vidyunmāli fell dead on the ground.
6.43.41
एवं तैर्वानरैः शूरैः
शूरास्ते रजनीचराः ।
द्वन्द्वे विमृदितास्तत्र
दैत्या इव दिवौकसैः ॥
Thus the valiant Night-rovers were pulverized by the heroic Vānaras in duels like the Daityas were by (Dēvas), the dwellers of Heaven.
6.43.42
भल्लैश्चान्यैर्गदाभिश्च
शक्तितोमरपट्टिशैः ।
अपविद्धैश्चापि रथैः
तथा सांङ्ग्रामिकैर्हयैः ।
निहतैः कुञ्जरैर्मत्तैः
तथा वानरराक्षसैः ।
चक्राक्षयुगदण्डैश्च
भग्नैर्धरणिसंश्रितैः ।
बभूवायोधनं घोरम्
गोमायुगणसङ्कुलम् ॥
The battlefield presented a ghastly sight with its broken swords, maces, javelins, Tōmaras and Paṭṭiṡas and others, with the slain elephants in rut, battle horses, and Vānaras and Rākshasas, with the smashed wheels, axles and carriage-shafts fallen on the ground, and with hordes of jackals that crowded there.
6.43.45
कबन्धानि समुत्पेतुः
दिक्षु वानररक्षसाम् ।
विमर्दे तुमुले तस्मिन्
देवासुररणोपमे ॥
In that stormy encounter, which resembled the battle between the Dēvas and the Asuras, beheaded bodies were seen flying in all directions.
6.43.46
विदार्यमाणा हरिपुङ्गवैस्तदा
निशाचराः शोणितदिग्धगात्राः ।
पुनः सुयुद्धं तरसा समाश्रिता
दिवाकरस्यास्तमयाभिकाङ्क्षिणः ॥
The Night-rovers, torn apart by the bulls among Vānaras, their bodies bedaubed with blood, re-formed in battle-array, summoning their strength and waiting impatiently for the sun to set.
Rākshasas grow stronger in the night, and hence they wait for the sun to set.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे त्रिचत्वारिंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty third Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
