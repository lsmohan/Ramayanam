Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 46
In this Sarga, after the Vānara chiefs left searching for Seetā, Rāma asks Sugreeva, ‘How did you come to know about the entire earth?’
Sugreeva then explains how he was chased by Vāli and how he ran all over the earth trying to escape from him, before settling down in a cave on the Ṛshyamūka mountain, where Vāli would never dare to enter because of the curse placed on him by Mataṅga.
4.46.1
गतेषु वानरेन्द्रेषु
रामस्सुग्रीवमब्रवीत् ।
कथं भवान्विजानीते
सर्वं वै मण्डलं भुवः ॥
After the chiefs of the Vānaras had left, Rāma asked Sugreeva, ‘How did you come to know about the entire earth?’
4.46.2
सुग्रीवस्तु ततो रामम्
उवाच प्रणतात्मवान् ।
श्रूयतां सर्वमाख्यास्ये
विस्तरेण वचो मम  ॥
Then Sugreeva, bowing to him in reverence, said: ‘May my words be heard, as I tell everything in detail’!
4.46.3
यदा तु दुन्दुभिं नाम
दानवं महिषाकृतिम् ।
परिकालयते वाली
मलयं प्रति पर्वतम् ।
तदा विवेश महिषो
मलयस्य गुहां प्रति ।
विवेश वाली तत्रापि
मलयं तज्जिघांसया ॥
When Vali chased Dundhubhi, the Dānava with a buffalo’s body, towards the Malaya mountain, the buffalo entered a cave in it. And Vali too entered the cave to kill him.
4.46.5
ततोऽहं तत्र निक्षिप्तो
गुहाद्वारि विनीतवत् ।
न च निष्क्रामते वाली
तदा संवत्सरे गते ॥
Then, I, the obedient one, was ordered to wait at the entrance. A whole year passed, but Vāli did not come out.
4.46.6
ततः क्षतजवेगेन
आपुपूरे तदा बिलम् ।
तदहं विस्मितो दृष्ट्वा
भ्रातृशोकविषार्दितः ॥
Then that cave was filled with a stream of blood. I was shocked to see the blood and was harrowed by grief for my brother, like one would be by a poisonous sting.
4.46.7
अथाहं गतबुद्धिस्तु
सुव्यक्तं निहतो गुरुः ।
शिला पर्वतसङ्काशा
बिलद्वारि मया कृता ।
अशक्नुवन्निष्क्रमितुम्
महिषो विनशिष्यति ॥
Then thinking to myself that my elder brother was clearly killed, I closed the entrance of the cave with a mountainous boulder, making sure that the buffalo would die, unable to come out.
4.46.8
ततोऽहमागां किष्किन्धाम्
निराशस्तस्य जीविते ।
राज्यं च सुमहत्प्राप्य
तारांश्च रुमया सह ।
मित्रैश्च सहितस्तत्र
वसामि विगतज्वरः ॥
Having lost all hope that he might still be alive, I returned to Kishkindhā and acquired the kingdom, and also Tārā and Ruma. I lived there along with my friends with no further worry.
4.46.10
आजगाम ततो वाली
हत्वा तं दानवर्षभम् ।
ततोऽहमददां राज्यम्
गौरवाद्भययन्त्रितः ॥
Then came Vāli, having slain that bull among Dānavas. Gripped with fear, and with due respect I handed over the kingdom to back to him.
4.46.11
स मां जिघांसुर्दुष्टात्मा
वाली प्रव्यथितेन्द्रियः ।
परिकालयते क्रोधात्
धावन्तं सचिवैस्सह ॥
With each of his senses caught in a rage, Vāli turned wretched and wanted to kill me. He chased me as I ran away along with my ministers.
4.46.12
ततोऽहं वालिना तेन
साऽनुबद्धः प्रधावितः ।
नदीश्च विविधाः पश्यन्
वनानि नगराणि च ॥
While I ran thus, being chased by Vāli, I saw many rivers, Vanas and cities.
4.46.13
आदर्शतलसङ्काशा
ततो वै पृथिवी मया ।
अलातचक्रप्रतिमा
दृष्टा गोष्पदवत्कृता ॥
It seemed as if I reduced the earth to the surface of a mirror, to the size of a circle made by a whirling firebrand and to the size of the hoof-print of a cow.
4.46.14
पूर्वां दिशं ततो गत्वा
पश्यामि विविधान् द्रुमान् ।
पर्वतान् सदरीन् रम्यान्
सरांसि विविधानि च ॥
I went in the easterly direction seeing many a tree and mountain and many a lovely stream and lake.
4.46.15
उदयं तत्र पश्यामि
पर्वतं धातुमण्डितम् ।
क्षीरोदं सागरं चैव
नित्यमप्सरसालयम् ॥
There I saw the mountain of the rising sun glistening with minerals and also the Milky Ocean, a constant resort of the Apsaras.
4.46.16
परिकाल्यमानस्तदा
वालिनाऽभिद्रुतोह्वम् ।
पुनरावृत्य सहसा
प्रस्थितोऽहं तदा विभो ।
दिशस्तस्यास्ततो भूयः
प्रस्थितो दक्षिणां दिशम् ।
विन्ध्यपादपसङ्कीर्णाम्
चन्दनद्रुमशोभिताम् ॥
My lord! Chased by Vāli, I kept running, retracing my steps and then heading in the southerly direction that is adorned with the thickets of trees of the Vindhya mountains and by many sandal trees.
4.46.18
द्रुमशैलान्तरे पश्यन्
भूयो दक्षिणतोऽपराम् ।
अपरां च दिशं प्राप्ता
वालिना समभिद्रुतः ॥
While trying to find a way out from the middle of those trees and mountains in the south, I ended up in the westerly direction, chased by Vāli.
4.46.19
सम्पश्यन्विविधान्देशान्
अस्तं च गिरिसत्तमम् ।
प्राप्य चास्तं गिरिश्रेष्ठम्
उत्तरं सम्प्रधावितः ॥
Going West, seeing various regions, running towards the setting mountain and finally reaching the setting mountain, I then ran towards the north.
4.46.20
हिमवन्तं च मेरुं च
समुद्रं च तथोत्तरम् ।
यदा न विन्दे शरणम्
वालिना समभिद्रुतः ।
तदा मां बुद्धिसम्पन्नो
हनूमान्वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
I ran from Himālayas to Mēru to the Northern Ocean. When I found no refuge as I was chased by Vāli, the sagacious Hanumān said these words:
4.46.21
इदानीं मे स्मृतं राजन्
यथा वाली हरीश्वरः ।
मतङ्गेन तदा शप्तो
ह्यस्मिन्नाश्रममण्डले ।
प्रविशेद्यदि वै वाली
मूर्धाऽस्य शतधा भवेत् ।
तत्र वासस्सुखोऽस्माकम्
निरुद्विग्नो भविष्यति ॥
I now remember, O King, how Vāli was once cursed by Mataṅga, saying, “If Vāli was to ever enter these Āṡrama grounds, his head shall break into a hundred pieces.” Therefore, let us go and live there, and we will have nothing to fear anymore.
4.46.23
ततः पर्वतमासाद्य
ऋष्यमूकं नृपात्मज ।
न विवेश तदा वाली
मतङ्गस्य भयात्तदा ॥
Thereupon, O prince, I went to the Ṛshyamūka mountain, and Vāli did not come there, for fear of Mataṅga.
4.46.24
एवं मया तदा राजन्
प्रत्यक्षमुपलक्षितम् ।
पृथिवीमण्डलं कृत्स्नम्
गुहामस्यागतस्ततः ॥
That was when I came to see the entire earth with my own eyes. Then onwards, I was bound to the cave.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे षट्चत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty sixth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
