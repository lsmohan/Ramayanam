Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 85
In this Sarga, Bharata asks Guha for directions to reach the Āṡrama of Bharadwāja. Guha says he and his kinsmen would help him cross the river and guide him to the Āṡrama, but that he is suspicious of his intentions, because of the huge army accompanying him, that he might kill Rāma.
Bharata feels hurt and swears that he is going only to bring Rāma back from the Vana. Guha lauds Bharata for his exceptional sacrifice of giving up a kingdom that has come to him without effort.
Bharata goes to bed, but is unable escape the weight of his grief. He goes to Guha for solace.
2.85.1
एवमुक्तस्तु भरतो
निषादाधिपतिं गुहम् ।
प्रत्युवाच महाप्राज्ञो
वाक्यं हेत्वर्थसंहितम् ॥
Thus addressed by the tribal chief, the sagacious Bharata responded saying these reasonable and meaningful words:
2.85.2
ऊर्जितः खलु ते कामः
कृतो मम गुरोस्सखे ।
यो मे त्वमीदृशीं सेनाम्
एकोऽभ्यर्चितुमिच्छसि ॥
O dear friend of my revered brother, your thought of serving this entire army all by yourself is laudable.
2.85.3
इत्युक्त्वा तु महातेजा
गुहं वचनमुत्तमम् ।
अब्रवीद्भरतश्श्रीमान्
निषादाधिपतिं पुनः ॥
The supremely radiant and magnificent Bharata, after saying those graceful words, went on to ask:
2.85.4
कतरेण गमिष्यामि
भरद्वाजाश्रमं गुह ।
गहनोऽयं भृशं देशो
गङ्गाऽनूपो दुरत्ययः ॥
O Guha! How would I reach Bharadwāja's Āṡrama! This region, covered by the waters of Gaṅgā is difficult to enter and pass through!
2.85.5
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राजपुत्रस्य धीमतः ।
अब्रवीत्प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यम्
गुहो गहनगोचरः ॥
Hearing those words of the sagacious prince, Guha who treads in the wilderness, answered with folded hands, saying:
2.85.6
दाशास्त्वाऽनुऽगमिष्यन्ति
धन्विनस्सुसमाहिताः ।
अहं त्वानुगमिष्यामि
राजपुत्र महायशः ॥
Alert fishermen armed with bows will accompany you. I shall also follow you, O prince of great renown!
2.85.7
कच्छिन्नदुष्टो व्रजसि
रामस्याक्लिष्टकर्मणः ।
इयं ते महती सेना
शङ्कां जनयतीव मे ॥
I hope you are going without any evil intentions towards Rāma for whom nothing is difficult; for, this large army of yours raises my suspicion.
2.85.8
तमेवमभिभाषन्तम्
आकाश इव निर्मलः ।
भरतश्श्लक्ष्णया वाचा
गुहं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
To Guha who was speaking thus, Bharata of the bearing of the pristine sky, said these words ever so gently:
2.85.9
माभूत्स कालो यत्कष्टम्
न मां शङ्कितुमर्हसि ।
राघवस्सहि मे भ्राता
ज्येष्ठः पितुसमो मतः ॥
May that day never dawn that will see such a terrible thing happen! You shall not suspect me, for I look upon Rāghava, my elder brother, as my father.
2.85.10
तं निवर्तयितुं यामि
काकुत्स्थं वनवासिनम् ।
बुद्धिरन्या न ते कार्या
गुह सत्यं ब्रवीमि ते ॥
I am going only to bring back Kākutstha who is dwelling in the Vana now. You should not mistake this in any other way, for I am telling you the truth.
The character of Bharata deserves sympathy like none other. Everyone lived fourteen years of terrible life. But living it under the long shadow of suspicion is, perhaps, the most terrible.
2.85.11
स तु संहृष्टवदनः
श्रुत्वा भरतभाषितम् ।
पुनरेवाब्रवीद्वाक्यम्
भरतं प्रति हर्षितः ॥
He was overjoyed to hear what Bharata said, and again told him these words with a beaming face:
2.85.12
धन्यस्त्वं न त्वया तुल्यम्
पश्यामि जगतीतले ।
अयत्नादागतं राज्यम्
यस्त्वं त्यक्तुमिहेच्छसि ॥
You are supremely blessed. I cannot think of your equal on earth; you wish to give up the kingdom that came to you without any effort.
2.85.13
शाश्वती खलु ते कीर्तिः
लोकाननुचरिष्यति ।
यस्त्वं कृच्छ्रगतं रामम्
प्रत्यानयितुमिच्छसि ॥
Your fame shall last forever in the worlds, since you wish to bring back Rāma who has fallen into difficult times.
2.85.14
एवं सम्भाषमाणस्य
गुहस्य भरतं तदा ।
बभौ नष्टप्रभस्सूर्यो
रजनी चाभ्यवर्तत ॥
As Guha was thus conversing with Bharata, the sun shined with diminished light, and the night descended.
2.85.15
संनिवेश्य स तां सेनाम्
गुहेन परितोषितः ।
शत्रुघ्नेन सह श्रीमान्
शयनं समुपागमत् ॥
Having disposed the army for the night, and heartened by Guha's gestures, the blessed one went to bed, and so did Ṡatrughna.
2.85.16
रामचिन्तामयश्शोको
भरतस्य महात्मनः ।
उपस्थितो ह्यनर्हस्य
धर्मप्रेक्षस्य तादृशः ॥
Grief engulfed the undeserving Mahātma Bharata, who always abides by Dharma, as the thoughts of Rāma seized him.
2.85.17
अन्तर्दाहेन दहनः
सन्तापयति राघवम् ।
वनदाहाभिसन्तप्तम्
गूढोऽग्निरिव पादपम् ॥
The fire of grief burnt the heart of that scion of the Raghus from inside, just as a tree in a scorched forest is consumed by the fire in its hollow.
2.85.18
प्रसृतस्सर्वगात्रेभ्यः
स्वेदं शोकाग्निसम्भवम् ।
यथा सूर्यांशुसन्तप्तो
हिमवान् प्रसृतोहिमम् ॥
Sweat flowed from each one of his limbs, triggered by the fire of grief, like melting ice flows from (every peak and slope of) Himavān, due to the scorching rays of the sun.
2.85.19
ध्याननिर्धरशैलेन
विनिश्श्वसितधातुना ।
दैन्यपादपसंघेन
शोकायासाधिशृङ्गिणा ।
प्रमोहानन्तसत्त्वेन
सन्तापौषधिवेणुना ।
आक्रान्तो दुःखशैलेन
महता कैकयीसुतः ॥
The son of Kaikēyee was crushed under the weight of a mountain of suffering, with its unbreakable boulders of unhappy broodings, its minerals of sighs, its thickets of despondency, its peaks of grief, fatigue and distress, its infinite beasts of stupor and its herbage and bamboo knots of pain.
2.85.21
विनिश्श्वसन्वै भृशदुर्मनास्ततः
प्रमूढसंज्ञः परमापदं गतः ।
शमं न लेभे हृदयज्वरार्दितो
नरर्षभो यूथहतो यथर्षभः ॥
Heaving great sighs, feeling extremely unhappy, with his faculties numbed and heart aching with pain, that bull among men could not find any solace, thus stuck in a hard situation, like a bull hounded by its herd.
2.85.22
गुहेन सार्थं भरतस्समागतो
महानुभावस्सजनस्समाहितः ।
सुदुर्मनास्तं भरतं तदा पुन-
र्गुह स्समाश्वासयदग्रजं प्रति ॥
Then Bharata, of majestic personality, unable to bear the distress. went to Guha along with his people. Guha consoled him with regard to his elder brother.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे पञ्चाशीतितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighty fifth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
