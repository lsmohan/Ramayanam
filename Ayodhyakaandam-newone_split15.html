Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 15
In this Sarga, Sumantra leaves Daṡaratha's palace to go to Rāma's palace. On the way, he sees all the arrangements made for Rāma's coronation, such as pots with water from all the rivers, white umbrella, eight virgins and so on.
He also sees crowds and dignitaries waiting for the ceremony. He hears them wonder why Daṡaratha is not seen. Sumantra goes back to Daṡaratha's palace to tell him that everyone is waiting for him. Daṡaratha tells him to first go and bring Rāma.
Sumantra, cutting through the crowds that assembled everywhere, reaches Rāma's palace that is majestic, imposing and impenetrable.
2.15.1
ते तु तां रजनीमुष्य
ब्राह्मणा वेदपारगाः ।
उपतस्थुरुपस्थानम्
सह राजपुरोहिताः ॥
The Brāhmaṇas who were savants of Vēdas, spent the night there and joined the King's Purōhita (Vasishṭha) nearby.
2.15.2
अमात्या बलमुख्याश्च
मुख्या ये निगमस्य च ।
राघवस्याभिषेकार्थे
प्रियमाणास्तु संगताः ॥
Ministers, chiefs of army and business bigwigs were pleased to assemble there on the occasion of Rāma's coronation.
2.15.3
उदिते विमले सूर्ये
पुष्ये चाभ्यागतेऽहनि ।
लग्ने कर्कटके प्राप्ते
जन्म रामस्य च स्थिते ।
अभिषेकाय रामस्य
द्विजेन्द्रैरुपकल्पितम् ॥
The head Brāhmaṇas had decided to perform the consecration ceremony after the bright Sun would appear in the sky with the ascendant in the Karkātaka constellation along with star Pushya just as it was at the time of Rāma's birth.
2.15.4
काञ्चना जलकुम्भाश्च
भद्रपीठं स्वलंकृतम् ।
रथः च सम्यगास्तीर्णो
भास्वता व्याघ्रचर्मणा ॥
Golden jars filled with water, a well-decorated throne and a chariot well spread with splendid tiger skin were ready.
2.15.5
गङ्गायमुनयोःपुण्यात्
सङ्गमादाहृतं जलम् ।
याश्चान्या स्सरितः पुण्या
ह्रदाः कूपास्सरांसि च ।
प्राग्वाहाश्चोर्ध्ववाहाश्च
तिर्यग्वाहा स्समाहिताः ।
ताभ्यश्चैवाहृतं तोयम्
समुद्रेभ्यश्च सर्वशः ॥
Water from the sacred confluence of Gaṅgā and Yamuna, and from other holy streams, wells, ponds and lakes, and from rivers flowing eastwards, westwards and upwards, and from rivers flowing sideways mixing with each other, and from seas from all directions, was kept ready.
2.15.7
सलाजाः क्षीरिभिश्छन्ना
घटाः काञ्चनराजताः ।
पद्मोत्पलयुता भान्ति
पूर्णाः परमवारिणा ॥
Gold and silver vessels filled with holy water and puffed corn, adorned with lotuses, water lilies and leaves from trees rich in sap, were kept ready.
2.15.8
क्षौद्रं दधि घृतं लाजा
दर्भा स्सुमनसः पयः ।
वेश्याश्चैव शुभाचारा
स्सर्वाभरणभूषिताः ॥
Honey, curds, Ghee, puffed grain, Darbha grass, flowers were made ready. Courtesans of dignified behavior and adorned with various ornaments were standing by.
2.15.9
चन्द्रांशुविकचप्रख्यम्
काञ्चनं रत्नभूषितम् ।
सज्जं तिष्ठति रामस्य
वालव्यजनमुत्तमम् ॥
A splendid fan made of Yak's tail, resembling the diffused rays of the moon, with a golden handle adorned with precious gems was held in readiness for Rāma.
2.15.10
चन्द्रमण्डलसङ्काशम्
आतपत्रं च पाण्डुरम् ।
सज्जं द्युतिकरं श्रीमत्
अभिषेकपुरस्कृतम् ॥
A beautiful, majestic white umbrella, luminous as the disc of the full moon that was most essential for the consecration, was also ready.
2.15.11
पाण्डुरश्च वृषस्सज्जः
पाण्डुरोऽश्वश्च सुस्थितः ।
प्रसृतश्च गजःश्रीमान्
औपवाह्यः प्रतीक्षते ॥
A white bull, a white horse and a majestic elephant in rut befitting for a ride by kings, are standing ready.
2.15.12
अष्टौ च कन्या माङ्गल्या
स्सर्वाभरणभूषिताः ।
वादित्राणि च सर्वाणि
वन्दिनश्च तथाऽपरे ॥
Eight auspicious virgins adorned with every kind of ornament were ready. Every kind of musical instrument was made ready. Panegyrists and many others were waiting, as well.
2.15.13
इक्ष्वाकूणां यथा राज्ये
संभ्रियेताभिषेचनम् ।
तथाजातीयमादाय
राजपुत्राभिषेचनम् ।
ते राजवचनात्तत्र
समवेतामहीपतिम् ।
अपश्यन्तोऽब्रुवन् को नु
राज्ञो नः प्रतिवेदयेत् ॥
Many kings arrived there bringing every kind of article necessary for the consecration of the prince, that was customary in the consecration ceremonies of the kings of Ikshwāku dynasty.
As they did not see the king there, they wondered as to who would inform the king of their arrival:
2.15.15
न पश्यामश्च राजानम्
उदितश्च दिवाकरः ।
यौवराज्याभिषेकश्च
सज्जो रामस्य धीमतः ॥
The sun has already risen. Everything is ready for the consecration of astute Rāma as the crown prince. But we do not see the king yet!
2.15.16
इति तेषु ब्रुवाणेषु
सार्वभौमान् महीपतीन् ।
अब्रवीत्तानिदं सर्वान्
सुमन्त्रो राजसत्कृतः ॥
Hearing those words of the sovereign kings who were well regarded by the King, Sumantra said to all of them:
2.15.17
रामं राज्ञो नियोगेन
त्वरया प्रस्थितोऽस्म्यहम् ।
पूज्या राज्ञो भवन्तस्तु
रामस्य च विशेषतः ।
अयं पृच्छामि वचनात्
सुखमायुष्मतामहम् ।
राज्ञः संप्रति बुद्धस्य
यच्चागमनकारणम् ॥
I am going in all haste to bring Rāma, by the order of the King. However, you are guests worthy of all honor to the king, and even more so to Rāma. Hence, per your words, O kings blessed with long life, I shall find from the king who is awake now about his well being and the reason for not being here.
2.15.19
इत्युक्त्वाऽन्तः पुरद्वारम्
आजगाम पुराणवित् ।
सदाऽसक्तं च तद्वेश्म
सुमन्त्रः प्रविवेश ह ॥
Saying thus, he, who was well versed with the chronicles of the past, went to the inner quarters, and entered the residence that always remained closed.
2.15.20
तुष्टावास्य तदा वंशम्
प्रविश्य स विशांपतेः ।
शयनीयं नरेन्द्रस्य
तदाऽऽसाद्य व्यतिष्ठत ॥
Then, he extolled the lineage of the King and reached the sleep chamber of the king.
2.15.21
सोऽत्यासाद्य तु तद्वेश्म
तिरस्करणिमन्तरा ।
आशीर्भिर्गुणयुक्ताभि
रभितुष्टाव राघवम् ॥
Staying behind the curtains at the entrance of the sleep chamber, he praised the king of Raghu dynasty invoking blessings and reciting virtues.
2.15.22
सोमसूर्यौ च काकुत्स्थ
शिववैश्रवणावपि ।
वरुणश्चाग्निरिन्द्रश्च
विजयं प्रदिशन्तु ते ॥
O king of Kākutstha lineage! May the moon and the sun, Ṡiva and Kubēra, Varuṇa, Agni and Indra grant you victory!
2.15.23
गता भगवती रात्रिः
अहः शिवमुपस्थितम् ।
बुद्ध्यस्व नृपशार्दूल
कुरु कार्यमनन्तरम् ॥
The goddess of night has departed. The auspicious day has arrived. O tiger among men, may you be awakened for your duties that are in order!
What a times! They considered even night as a goddess!
2.15.24
ब्राह्मणा बलमुख्याश्च
नैगमाश्चागता नृप ।
दर्शनं तेऽभिकांक्षन्ते
प्रतिबुध्यस्व राघव ॥
O king of Raghu dynasty! Brāhmaṇas, army commanders, heads of businesses and kings are awaiting your audience. May you be awakened!
2.15.25
स्तुवन्तं तं तदा सूतम्
सुमन्त्रं मन्त्रकोविदम् ।
प्रतिबुध्य ततो राजा
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Then, the king woke up and said to Sumantra, the charioteer and the eminent strategist, who was extolling him:
2.15.26
राममानय सूतेति
यदस्यभिहितोऽनया ।
किमिदं कारणं येन
ममाज्ञा प्रतिहन्यते ॥
O Charioteer! You have been told by this lady to bring Rāma here! What is the reason for not following that order?
2.15.27
न चैव संप्रसुप्तोऽहम्
आनयेहाशु राघवम् ।
इति राजा दशरथ
स्सूतं तत्रान्वशात्पुनः ॥
"I am not sleeping. Bring Rāma here at once!", King Daṡaratha ordered the charioteer again.
2.15.28
स राजवचनं श्रुत्वा
शिरसा प्रतिपूज्य तम् ।
निर्जगाम नृपावासान्
मन्यमानः प्रियं महत् ॥
Hearing those words of the king and saluting again with his head bowed, he left the king's apartment, thinking of nothing but the best.
2.15.29
प्रसन्नो राजमार्गं च
पताकाध्वजशोभितम् ।
हृष्टः प्रमुदित स्सूतो
जगामाशु विलोकयन् ॥
The charioteer, en route, saw the royal highway decorated with pennants and banners, and felt happy and delighted.
As has been noted previously, Vālmeeki is very visual. He describes the scene as if he is seeing it through the lens of a camera, while recording all the sounds around as well, live.
Imagine seeing the grand and awe-inspiring arrangements of India's Independence day celebrations through the eyes of the chauffeur who will be brining the Prime Minister of India from the Prime Minister's office to the Red Fort, for hoisting the flag!
Imagine seeing all the crowds and dignitaries! Imagine soaking yourself in the environment filled with emotion and excitement!
That will give you a sense of what Sumantra is seeing en route! Add your imagination to visualize the splendor, gaiety, excitement and anticipation of the scene.
2.15.30
स सूतस्तत्र शुश्राव
रामाधिकरणाः कथाः ।
अभिषेचनसंयुक्ताः
सर्वलोकस्य हृष्टवत् ॥
The charioteer, en route, heard everyone talking about the coronation of Rāma!
2.15.31
ततो ददर्श रुचिरम्
कैलासशिखरप्रभम् ।
रामवेश्म सुमन्त्रस्तु
शक्रवेश्मसमप्रभम् ॥
Thereafter, Sumantra saw the beautiful palace of Rāma, that was resplendent like Kailāsa mountain and resembling the palace of Indra!
2.15.32
महाकवाटविहितम्
वितर्दिशतशोभितम् ।
काञ्चनप्रतिमैकाग्रम्
मणिविद्रुमतोरणम् ।
शारदाभ्रघनप्रख्यम्
दीप्तं मेरुगुहोपमम् ।
मणिभिर्वरमाल्यानाम्
समुहद्भिरलंकृतम् ।
मुक्तामणिभिराकीर्णम्
चन्दनागरूधूपितम् ।
गन्धान्मनोज्ञान् विसृजत्
दार्दुरं शिखरं यथा ।
सारसैश्च मयूरैश्च
विनदद्भिर्विराजितम् ।
सुकृतेहामृगाकीर्णम्
सुकीर्णं भक्तिभिस्तथा ।
मनश्चक्षुश्च भूतानाम्
आददत्तिग्मतेजसा ।
चन्द्रभास्करसङ्काशम्
कुबेरभवनोपमम् ।
महेन्द्रधामप्रतिमम्
नानापक्षिसमाकुलम् ।
मेरुशृङ्गसमं सूतो
रामवेश्म ददर्श ह ।
उपस्थितैःसमाकीर्णम्
जनैरञ्जलिकारिभिः ।
उपादाय समाक्रान्तैः
तथा जानपदैर्जनैः ।
रामाभिषेकसुमुखैः
उन्मुखैस्समलंकृतम् ।
महामेघसमप्रख्यम्
उदग्रं सुविभूषितम् ।
नानारत्नसमाकीर्णम्
कुब्जकैरातकावृतम् ॥
Sumantra beheld Rāma's mansion with its entrance of mammoth doors that were kept shut, beautiful with hundreds of terraces, adorned with a golden statue on top, archways set with gems and corals; shining like the cloud of post-monsoon sky, resembling the caves of Mēru mountain, adorned with golden festoons of exquisite gems, discharging delightful fragrances of sandalwood and incense resembling the peaks of Dardura mountain, gay with the calls of peacocks and the Sārasa birds, with well-placed sculpted figures of deer in relief and panels of delicate carvings, captivating the eyes and the minds of beholders by its dazzling brilliance like that of the sun and the moon; vying in splendor with the mansion of Kubēra, resembling the abode of the great Indra, alive with a host of birds and rivaling a peak of Mēru mountain, filled with people who had brought gifts, waiting with palms joined in excitement and anticipation, festive with folks who came from the country side, high and imposing like great clouds, decorated with many kinds of gems, and crowded with humpbacks and Kirātas.
2.15.41
स वाजियुक्तेन रथेन सारथिः
नराकुलं राजकुलं विराजयन् ।
वरूथिना रामगृहाभिपातिना
पुरस्य सर्वस्य मनांसि हर्षयन् ॥
The charioteer rode the chariot that was covered with a fender and drawn by horses towards the palace of Rāma, electrifying the people and the kings alike on the way and filling everyone's minds with joy!
2.15.42
ततस्समासाद्य महाधनं महत्
प्रहृष्टरोमा स बभूब सारथिः ।
मृगैर्मयूरैश्च समाकुलोल्बणं
गृहं वरार्हस्य शचीपतेरिव ॥
Reaching that opulent residence befitting the great Rāma, that resembled the abode of Indra, splendid with peacocks and other animals, the charioteer had goose bumps.
2.15.43
स तत्र कैलासनिभास्स्वलंकृताः
प्रविश्य कक्ष्यास्त्रिदशालयोपमाः ।
प्रियान्वरान् राममते स्थितान् बहून्
व्यपोह्य शुद्धान्तमुपस्थितो रथी ॥
The charioteer passed through the outer courts that were beautifully decorated and resembled Kailāsa mountain. Then he passed by many prominent friends and staunch supporters of Rāma, and entered the inner apartment.
2.15.44
स तत्र शुश्राव च हर्षयुक्ताः
रामाभिषेकार्थकृता जनानाम् ।
नरेन्द्रसूनोरभिमङ्गलार्थाः
सर्वस्य लोकस्य गिरः प्रहृष्टः ॥
There, he heard chants of happy and joyful people wishing everything auspicious for the consecration of the son of the king!
2.15.45
महेन्द्रसद्मप्रतिमं तु वेश्म
रामस्य रम्यं मृगपक्षि जुष्टम् ।
ददर्श मेरोरिव श्रुङ्गमुच्चं
विभ्राजमानं प्रभया सुमन्त्रः ॥
Sumantra saw Rāma's palace, that was like the mansion of Indra, delightful with deer and birds, high and imposing like the Mēru mountain, and shining with dazzling splendor.
2.15.46
उपस्थितैरञ्जलिकारकैश्च
सोपायनैर्जानपदैर्जनैश्च ।
कोट्या परार्धैश्च विमुक्तयानैः
समाकुलं द्वारपथं ददर्श ॥
He saw the entrance crowded with people from all over the countryside, numbering in Crores and Parārdhas, bringing presents along with them, with vehicles parked elsewhere and standing with palms joined in reverence.
Parārdha is a big number with 17 zeros after 1.
2.15.47
ततो महामेघमहीधराभं
प्रभिन्न मत्यङ्कुशमत्यसह्यम् ।
रामौपवाह्यं रुचिरं ददर्श
शत्रुञ्जयं नागमुदग्रकायम् ॥
Then he saw Ṡatruñjaya, a huge elephant in rut that feared no goad, worthy for Rāma to mount, comparable to a great cloud and a vast mountain.
2.15.48
स्वलङ्कृतान् साश्वरथान् सकुञ्जरा-
नमात्य मुख्यांश्च ददर्श वल्लभान् ।
व्यपोह्य सूतस्सहितान् समन्ततः
समृद्धमन्तःपुरमाविवेश ह ॥
He saw well dressed principal ministers on their horses, elephants and chariots, who were held in high esteem by the King. Then, finding his way through the midst of the people assembled everywhere, he entered the luxurious inner apartments.
2.15.49
तदन्द्रिकूटाचलमेघसन्निभं
महाविमानोपमवेश्मसंयुतम् ।
अवार्यमाणः प्रविवेश सारथिः
प्रभूतरत्नं मकरो यथार्णवम् ॥
Unhindered, the charioteer entered the palace resembling the peak of a mountain and a great cloud with suites that were like mansions in their own right, as a crocodile would enter an ocean full of gems.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे पञ्चदशःसर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifteenth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
