Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 12
In this Sarga, at the behest of Rāvaṇa, Prahasta deploys forces for the protection of the city all around. Rāvaṇa then rambles incoherently to the audience in the council hall, praising their capabilities, describing his raging passion for Seetā, and his worry about the enemies who are already camped on the other side of the ocean.
Kumbhakarṇa, outraged about the impediment that Rāvaṇa is stuck in, first chastises him for abducting Seetā without a fully laid out strategy, and then assures him that he can kill all the enemies of Rāvaṇa, including Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and all the Vānaras.
6.12.1
स तां परिषदं कृत्स्नाम्
समीक्ष्य समितिञ्जयः ।
प्रचोदयामास तदा
प्रहस्तं वाहिनीपतिम् ॥
Then, scanning the entire assembly with his glance, he, who was ever victorious in battle, exhorted Prahasta, the commander of the forces:
6.12.2
सेनापते यथा ते स्युः
कृतविद्याश्चतुर्विधाः ।
योधानधिकरक्षायाम्
तथा व्यादेष्टुमर्हसि ॥
You should, O Commander-in-Chief, arrange for the protection of the city, deploying accomplished warriors of the four divisions.
6.12.3
स प्रहस्तः प्रतीतात्मा
चिकीर्षन् राजशासनम् ।
विनिक्षिपद्बलं सर्वम्
बहिरन्तश्च मन्दिरे ॥
And Prahasta, who was in full attention and eager to carry out the King’s orders, posted the entire force inside and outside the palace.
6.12.4
ततो विनिक्षिप्य बलम्
सर्वं नगरगुप्तये ।
प्रहस्तः प्रमुखे राज्ञो
निषसाद जगाद च ॥
After posting the forces thus to keep guard over the city, Prahasta came back and, seated in front of the king, said:
6.12.5
निहितं बहिरन्तश्च
बलं बलवतस्तव ।
कुरुष्वाविमनाः क्षिप्रम्
यदभिप्रेतमस्ति ते ॥
Your forces, O strong one, have been posted inside and outside. You may now do whatever you like and however quick, without a worry.
6.12.6
प्रहस्तस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
राजा राज्यहितैषिणः ।
सुखेप्सुः सुहृदां मध्ये
व्याजहार स रावणः ॥
On hearing those words of Prahasta who was devoted to the wellbeing of the kingdom, Rāvaṇa spoke in the midst of the well-wishers, unburdening himself (of the weight on his mind):
6.12.7
प्रियाप्रिये सुखं दुःखम्
लाभालाभौ हिताहिते ।
धर्मकामार्थकृच्छ्रेषु
यूयमर्हथ वेदितुम् ॥
You are very much capable of finding out what would be pleasant and what would be unpleasant, what would be easy and what would be difficult, what would be beneficial and what would be not, what would lead to our wellbeing and what would not in dealing with the challenges of the pursuit of Dharma, Artha and Kāma.
6.12.8
सर्वकृत्यानि युष्माभिः
समारब्धानि सर्वदा ।
मन्त्रकर्मनियुक्तानि
न जातु विफलानि मे ॥
No endeavor of mine that you had ever taken up in earnest and pursued according to a strategy has ever been unsuccessful.
6.12.9
स सोमग्रहनक्षत्रैः
मरुद्भिरिव वासवः ।
भवद्भिरहमत्यर्थम्
वृतः श्रियमवाप्नुयाम् ॥
I shall, with your support, achieve all prosperity as Vāsava did with the support of the moon, the planets and the Maruts.
6.12.10
अहं तु खलु सर्वान् वः
समर्थयितुमुद्यतः ।
कुम्भकर्णस्य तु स्वप्नात्
नेममर्थमचोदयम् ॥
I could discuss this with all of you, but not with Kumbhakarṇa, as he had been (long) asleep.
6.12.11
अयं हि सुप्तः षण्मासान्
कुम्भकर्णो महाबलः ।
सर्वशस्त्रभृतां मुख्यः
स इदानीं समुत्थितः ॥
The mighty strong Kumbhakarṇa, foremost among those who bear arms, has been asleep for six months and woke up just now.
6.12.12
इयं च दण्डकारण्यात्
रामस्य महीषी प्रिया ।
रक्षोभिश्चरिताद्देशात्
आनीता जनकात्मजा ॥
This beloved wife of Rāma, daughter of Janaka, was brought from the Daṇḍaka Araṇya, the region frequented by Rākshasas.
6.12.13
सा मे न शय्यामारोढुम्
इच्छत्यलसगामिनी ।
त्रिषु लोकेषु चान्या मे
न सीतासदृशी मता ॥
She of gentle strides, for whom I do not find an equal in any of the three worlds, is not willing to go to bed with me.
6.12.14
तनुमध्या पृथुश्रोणी
शारदेन्दुनिभानना ।
हेमबिम्बनिभा सौम्या
मायेव मयनिर्मिता ॥
So gentle and shining like a golden doll, she looks like a magical being created by Maya, with her slender waist, wide Kaṭee and face resembling the moon in post-monsoon sky.
6.12.15
सुलोहिततलौ श्लक्ष्णौ
चरणौ सुप्रतिष्ठितौ ।
दृष्ट्वा ताम्रनखौ तस्या
दीप्यते मे शरीरजः ॥
My longing for her love rages when I see her soft feet with red soles and copper-shiny nails that tread firmly and evenly.
6.12.16
हुताग्रेरर्चिसङ्काशाम्
एनां सौरीमिव प्रभाम् ।
उन्नसं विमलं वल्गु
वदनं चारुलोचनम् ।
पश्यंस्तदवशस्तस्याः
कामस्य वशमेयिवान् ॥
I lose all control over myself and become the slave of passion when I see her, who blazes like a flame that is well fed with oblations, who shares the radiance of the sun, and whose spotless face is lovely with its lofty nose and beautiful eyes.
6.12.17
क्रोधहर्षसमानेन
दुर्वर्णकरणेन च ।
शोकसन्तापनित्येन
कामेन कलुषीकृतः ॥
My mind is contaminated with passion for her, rendering all my emotions bland, making my anger and happiness indistinguishable from each other, and drowning me in sadness and grief forever.
6.12.18
सा तु संवत्सरं कालं
मामयाचत भामिनी ।
प्रतीक्षमाणा भर्तारम्
राममायतलोचना ॥
The lovely one of wide eyes begged of me a year’s respite, hoping that her husband Rāma would come to her rescue.
6.12.19
तन्मया चारुनेत्रायाः
प्रतिज्ञातं वचः शुभम् ।
श्रान्तोऽहं सततं कामात्
यातो हय इवाध्वनि ॥
I agreed to that reasonable request that she of lovely eyes made, but I am worn out with perpetual longing, like a horse that has run a long distance.
6.12.20
कथं सागरमक्षोभ्यम्
उत्तरन्ति वनौकसः ।
बहुसत्त्वझषाकीर्णम्
तौ वा दशरथात्मजौ ॥
There is no way that the Vana dwellers or the sons of Daṡaratha can cross the formidable ocean that teems with many sea creatures!
6.12.21
अथवा कपिनैकेन
कृतं नः कदनं महत् ।
दुर्ज्ञेयाः कार्यगतयो
ब्रूत यस्य यथा मतिः ॥
But, just a single Vānara was able to put up such a formidable fight and hence it is difficult to know how things will turn out. Please tell me what you each think!
6.12.22
मानुषान्मे भयं नास्ति
तथापि तु विमृश्यताम् ।
तदा देवासुरे युद्धे
युष्माभिः सहितोऽजयम् ।
ते मे भवन्तश्च तथा
सुग्रीवप्रमुखान् हरीन् ।
परे पारे समुद्रस्य
पुरस्कृत्य नृपात्मजौ ।
सीतायाः पदवीं प्राप्तौ
सम्प्राप्तौ वरुणालयम् ॥
I do not have fear of humans, but we do have to take things seriously. I, with your help, and you yourselves too, were victorious once upon a time even in the war between Dēvas and Asuras.
The princes, led by the Vānaras like Sugreeva, have reached the other shore of the ocean, having come to know of Seetā’s whereabouts.
6.12.25
अदेया च यथा सीता
वध्यौ दशरथात्मजौ ।
भवद्भिर्मन्त्र्यतां मन्त्रः
सुनीतं चाभिधीयताम् ॥
Think of a strategy of how Seetā need not be surrendered and how the sons of Daṡaratha can be slayed. Present (to me) a well laid out plan.
6.12.26
न हि शक्तिं प्रपश्यामि
जगत्यन्यस्य कस्यचित् ।
सागरं वानरैस्तीर्त्वा
निश्चयेन जयो मम ॥
I cannot see anyone in this world capable of crossing the ocean with the help of Vānaras. There is no doubt that victory will be mine.
6.12.27
तस्य कामपरीतस्य
निशम्य परिदेवितम् ।
कुम्भकर्णः प्रचुक्रोध
वचनं चेदमब्रवीत् ॥
Kumbhakarṇa was quite outraged on hearing those doleful plaints of that passion-stricken one, and said these words:
6.12.28
यदा तु रामस्य सलक्ष्मणस्य
प्रसह्य सीता खलु सा इहाहृता ।
सकृत् समीक्ष्यैव सुनिश्चितं तदा
भजेत चित्तं यमुनेव यामुनम् ॥
If only you had pondered well at least once before forcefully bringing Seetā here away from Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, your mind would have had the opportunity to collect its thoughts, like water (from the mountains) channeling into the Yamuna.
These seven Ṡlōkas, from 28 to 34, seems to be misplaced, may be because of shuffled palm leaves. These words would have been spoken by Vibheeshaṇa, but not by Kumbhakarṇa. These Ṡlōkas fit well to be part of the counsel of Vibheeshaṇa, in Sarga 9.
6.12.29
सर्वमेतन्महाराज
कृतमप्रतिमं तव ।
विधीयेत सहास्माभिः
आदावेवास्य कर्मणः ॥
There would have been nothing like it, if only you had all these consultations with us when you originally thought of doing what you did.
6.12.30
न्यायेन राजकार्याणि
यः करोति दशानन ।
न स सन्तप्यते पश्चात्
निश्चितार्थमतिर्नृपः ॥
O Ten-faced, a king who conducts the affairs of the state with clear objectives in mind and in an ethical manner would have nothing to resent later.
6.12.31
अनुपायेन कर्माणि
विपरीतानि यानि च ।
क्रियमाणानि दुष्यन्ति
हवींष्यप्रयतेष्विव ॥
Failure will befall extreme undertakings that lack proper strategy, like Yajña offerings made without the rigor of spiritual discipline.
6.12.32
यः पश्चात् पूर्वकार्याणि
कर्माण्यभिचिकीर्षति ।
पूर्वं चापरकार्याणि
न स वेद नयानयौ ॥
He who does first what should be done later and later what should have been done first, has no sense of strategy.
6.12.33
चपलस्य तु कृत्येषु
प्रसमीक्ष्याधिकं बलम् ।
छिद्रमन्ये प्रपद्यन्ते
क्रौञ्चस्य खमिव द्विजाः ॥
Others discover and exploit the weaknesses in the undertakings of one who does things heedlessly relying on one’s own excessive strength, like birds availing themselves of the opening in the Krounca mountain.
6.12.34
त्वयेदं महदारब्धम्
कार्यमप्रतिचिन्तितम् ।
दिष्ट्या त्वां नावधीद्रामो
विषमिश्रमिवामिषम् ॥
You have embarked on this huge undertaking without giving it careful thought; it is only by the grace of Providence that Rāma did not yet kill you, leaving you out as one would spoiled meat.
6.12.35
तस्मात्त्वया समारब्धम्
कर्म ह्यप्रतिमं परैः ।
अहं समीकरिष्यामि
हत्वा शत्रूंस्तवानघ ॥
O sinless one! I will kill the enemies and finish off the incomparable attack you launched against the enemies.
6.12.36
अहमुत्सादयिष्यामि
शत्रूंस्तव विशाम्पते ।
यदि शक्रविवस्वन्तौ
यदि पावकमारुतौ ।
तावहं योधयिष्यामि
कुबेरवरुणावपि ॥
O King, I shall rout out your enemies, whoever they may be, be it Ṡakra and Sūrya, or Agni and Vāyu, or Kubēra and Varuṇa.
6.12.37
गिरिमात्रशरीरस्य
महापरिघयोधिनः ।
नर्दतस्तीक्ष्णदंष्ट्रस्य
बिभियाद्वै पुरन्दरः ॥
Even Indra should be terrified of my mountainous size and sharp fangs, and as I fight with a huge Parigha and let off a thunderous shout.
6.12.38
पुनर्मां स द्वितीयेन
शरेण निहनिष्यति ।
ततोऽहं तस्य पास्यामि
रुधिरं काममाश्वस ॥
Before he shoots me with another arrow, I shall have drunk quite a bit of his blood; please be at ease in your mind.
6.12.39
वधेन वै दाशरथेः सुखावहं
जयं तवाहर्तुमहं यतिष्ये ।
हत्वा च रामं सह लक्ष्मणेन
खादामि सर्वान् हरियूथमुख्यान् ॥
I will try to secure you the pleasure of defeating and killing the son of Daṡaratha. Having killed Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, I will eat up all the Vānara chiefs.
6.12.40
रमस्व कामं पिब चाग्र्यवारुणीं
कुरुष्व कार्याणि हितानि विज्वरः ।
मया तु रामे गमिते यमक्षयं
चिराय सीता वशगा भविष्यति ॥
Sport yourself as you please! Enjoy the best of the liquors! Do whatever is good for you, without a worry! With Rāma sent to the abode of Yama by me, Seetā will be yours forever.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्वादशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twelfth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
