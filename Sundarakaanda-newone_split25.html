Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 25
In this Sarga, the defiant Seetā, persecuted by the Rākshasis, cries over her helpless situation, wherein she cannot even take her own life!
5.25.1
अथ तासां वदन्तीनाम्
परुषं दारुणं बहु ।
राक्षसीनामसौम्यानाम्
रुरोद जनकात्मजा ॥
Those vicious Rākshasis went on saying many harsh and cruel words and the daughter of Janaka cried bitterly.
5.25.2
एवमुक्ता तु वैदेही
राक्षसीभिर्मनस्विनी ।
उवाच परमत्रस्ता
भाष्पगद्गदया गिरा ॥
When the Rākshasis badgered her thus, the terribly scared and sensitive Vaidēhee said, her throat choked with tears:
5.25.3
न मानुषी राक्षसस्य
भार्या भवितुमर्हति ।
कामं खादत मां सर्वा
न करिष्यामि वो वचः ॥
A human cannot be the wife of a Rākshasa. All of you, eat me, if you choose, but I will not do what you want!
5.25.4
सा राक्षसीमध्यगता
सीता सुरसुतोपमा ।
न शर्म लेभे दुःखार्ता
रावणेन च तर्जिता ॥
Threatened by Rāvaṇa, surrounded by Rākshasis, the distressed Seetā, who was like a daughter of the Dēvas, found no source of comfort.
5.25.5
वेपते स्माधिकं सीता
विशन्ती वाङ्गमात्मनः ।
वने यूथपरिभ्रष्टा
मृगी कोकैरिवार्दिता ॥
Like a doe that had strayed from the herd in the forest and was harried by wolves, Seetā trembled violently, and all her limbs seemed to sink into herself.
5.25.6
सा त्वशोकस्य विपुलाम्
शाखामालम्ब्य पुष्पिताम् ।
चिन्तयामास शोकेन
भर्तारं भग्नमानसा ॥
Holding on to a big bough of Aṡōka tree that was in full bloom, she, with her heart broken, wept, thinking of her husband.
5.25.7
सा स्नापयन्ती विपुलौ
स्तनौ नेत्रजलस्रवैः ।
चिन्तयन्ती न शोकस्य
तदान्तमधिगच्छति ॥
With her flowing tears bathing her ample bosom, she kept on brooding, finding no end to her grief.
5.25.8
सा वेपमाना पतिता
प्रवाते कदली यथा ।
राक्षसीनां भयत्रस्ता
विवर्णवदनाऽभवत् ॥
Frightened by the Rākshasis, she shook like a banana plant that fell on the ground in high winds, and her face became pale and lifeless.
5.25.9
तस्याः सा दीर्घविपुला
वेपन्त्या परमस्त्रियाः ।
ददृशे कम्पिनी वेणी
व्यालीव परिसर्पती ॥
As that noble woman shivered, her thick and long plait swung reminding one of a sidewinding female snake.
5.25.10
सा निःश्वसन्ती दुःखार्ता
शोकोपहतचेतना ।
आर्ता व्यसृजदश्रूणि
मैथिली विललाप च ॥
Maithili, feeling helpless and distressed, cried bitterly, shedding tears and heaving sighs, her heart broken by grief.
5.25.11
हा रामेति च दुःखार्ता
हा पुनर्लक्ष्मणेति च ।
हा श्वश्रु मम कौसल्ये
हा सुमित्रेति भामिनी ॥
That lovely woman, overcome with grief, wailed, saying ‘O Rāma!’ and then saying, ‘O Lakshmaṇa!’, ‘O my mother-in-law Kousalyā!’ and ‘O Sumitrā!’
5.25.12
लोकप्रवादः सत्योऽयम्
पण्डितैः समुदाहृतः ।
अकाले दुर्लभो मृत्युः
स्त्रिया वा पुरुषस्य वा ।
यदहमेवं क्रूराभी
राक्षसीभिरिहार्दिता ।
जीवामि हीना रामेण
मुहूर्तमपि दुःखिता ॥
The common saying in the world, which is oft quoted by the matured that there is no such thing as death for men or women before its time comes, must be true;
or else, how is it possible that I live even for an hour without Rāma, crying and enduring torture like this inflicted by these cruel Rākshasis?
5.25.14
एषाऽल्पपुण्या कृपणा
विनशिष्याम्यनाथवत् ।
समुद्रमध्ये नौः पूर्णा
वायुवेगैरिवाहता ॥
I, with little Puṇya left, shall perish in my misery, with no one to care for me, like a fully loaded ship in mid-sea, hit by the speed of the winds.
5.25.15
भर्तारं तमपश्यन्ती
राक्षसीवशमागता ।
सीदामि खलु शोकेन
कूलं तोयहतं यथा ॥
Fallen into the clutches of the Rākshasis and seeing no sign of my husband, I shall collapse in grief, like a bank hit by rushing waters.
5.25.16
तं पद्मदलपत्राक्षम्
सिंहविक्रान्तगामिनम् ।
धन्याः पश्यन्ति मे नाथम्
कृतज्ञं प्रियवादिनम् ॥
Blessed indeed are those who get to see my lord of lotus petal-like eyes, whose gait is like that of a lion, whose speech is always pleasant and who never forgets a favor done to him.
5.25.17
सर्वथा तेन हीनाया
रामेण विदितात्मना ।
तीक्ष्णं विषमिवास्वाद्य
दुर्लभं मम जीवितम् ॥
Without Rāma, who has absolute clarity about himself, I, by no means, can hang on to this life, like one who has consumed dreadful poison.
5.25.18
कीदृशं तु महापापम्
मया जन्मान्तरे कृतम् ।
येनेदं प्राप्यते दुःखम्
मया घोरं सुदारुणम् ॥
What kind of sin must I have committed over the course of my many births, to be subject to this dreadful and extremely terrible misery!
5.25.19
जीवितं त्यक्तुमिच्छामि
शोकेन महता वृता ।
मया नावाप्यते कामो
राक्षसीभिः सुरक्षिता ॥
Engulfed in this terrible misery, I only wish to give up this life; but, guarded well by these Rākshasis, my wish will not come true.
5.25.20
धिगस्तु खलु मानुष्यम्
धिगस्तु परवश्यताम् ।
न शक्यं यत्परित्यक्तुम्
आत्मच्छन्देन जीवितम् ॥
Dhik, being a human! Dhik, getting in the hands of others, and being unable to take my own life as I wish!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे पञ्चविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty fifth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
