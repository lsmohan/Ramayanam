Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 37
In this Sarga, Seetā reflects on Rāma’s grief and his abilities, assuring herself of his virtues and prowess. Hanumān assures Seetā that Rāma will lose no time in retrieving her after hearing about her from him.
Hanumān also offers to carry Seetā on his back across the ocean and lodge her in the presence of her husband. Seetā, pleased, retorts in a lighter tone saying how could it be possible for such a small Vānara to carry her on his back. Hanumān, taking the slight in his stride, reveals to her his true form, the one imposing like a mountain and blazing like fire.
Seetā concedes that she knew his power, for no ordinary creature can cross the wide ocean. However she rejects his offer saying that it does not serve the purpose for many reasons. First of all, she could fall off from his back when he travels at a high speed high above the ocean, Hanumān would have to fight the chasing Rākshasas. Second, it is possible that Rākshasas might retrieve her back and lodge her in a location that could be hard for anyone to find. And also, she says, that it is not appropriate for her, a devoted wife, to touch the body of another man.
The final and most important reason, she says, is that it would be befitting of Rāma to kill the Rākshasas himself, and retrieve her from Rāvaṇa. She urges Hanumān to see to it that Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and the Vānara chiefs are in Laṅkā as soon as possible.
5.37.1
सीता तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
पूर्णचन्द्रनिभानना ।
हनूमन्तमुवाचेदम्
धर्मार्थसहितं वचः ॥
Having heard his words, Seetā, her face resembling the full moon, said these words imbued with Dharma and Artha:
We see it in Rāmāyaṇa quite a few times - the mention of ‘words imbued with Dharma and Artha’ – What is the purport?
Well, Indian philosophy sees that every action we perform serves one or more of the three purposes: Dharma (fulfills an obligation or responsibility that is appropriate and righteous), Artha (furthers one’s objectives and goals) and Kama (gives pleasure or entertainment).
Even a simple conversation by a well-meaning person serves one or more of these three objectives. A conversation that does not serve any of these three, will obviously be wasteful and even insipid.
Here, Vālmeeki is indicating which of the three purposes Seetā intends to serve in speaking to Hanumān.
5.37.2
अमृतं विषसंसृष्टम्
त्वया वानर भाषितम् ।
यच्च नान्यमना रामो
यच्च शोकपरायणः ॥
Your words are like Amṛt, but mixed with poison; for you said Rāma is not thinking of anything else (except me), but he is also wholly given to grief.
5.37.3
ऐश्वर्ये वा सुविस्तीर्णे
व्यसने वा सुदारुणे ।
रज्ज्वेव पुरुषं बद्ध्वा
कृतान्तः परिकर्षति ॥
Providence, which has the final say about everything, can drag a man with a halter around the neck, into endless opulence as well as into terrible sufferings.
5.37.4
विधिर्नूनमसंहार्यः
प्राणिनां प्लवगोत्तम ।
सौमित्रिं मां च रामं च
व्यसनैः पश्य मोहितान् ॥
No creature can overcome its destiny; look, O best of Vānaras, how Sowmitri, me and Rāma, are driven to dizziness by the hardships.
5.37.5
शोकस्यास्य कदा पारम्
राघवोऽधिगमिष्यति ।
प्लवमानः परिश्रान्तो
हतनौस्सागरे यथा ॥
When will Rāghava reach the shore of this ocean of grief, exhausted like one swimming for life, his ship wrecked in the middle of the high seas?
5.37.6
राक्षसानां वधं कृत्वा
सूदयित्वा च रावणम् ।
लङ्कामुन्मूलितां कृत्वा
कदा द्रक्ष्यति मां पतिः ॥
When will my husband get to see me, killing the Rākshasas, destroying Rāvaṇa and extricating Laṅkā by its roots?
5.37.7
स वाच्यस्संत्वरस्वेति
यावदेव न पूर्यते ।
अयं संवत्सरः कालः
तावद्धि मम जीवितम् ॥
He should be told to hurry before the time limit of one year expires, for I can hope to be alive only that long.
5.37.8
वर्तते दशमो मासो
द्वौ तु शेषौ प्लवङ्गम ।
रावणेन नृशंसेन
समयो यः कृतो मम ॥
O Vānara! This is already the tenth month and only two more months remain in the time limit set for me by the cruel Rāvaṇa.
5.37.9
विभीषणेन च भ्रात्रा
मम निर्यातनं प्रति ।
अनुनीतः प्रयत्नेन
न च तत्कुरुते मतिम् ॥
His brother Vibheeshaṇa tried his best to persuade him to send me back, but he would not listen.
5.37.10
मम प्रतिप्रदानं हि
रावणस्य न रोचते ।
रावणं मार्गते संख्ये
मृत्युः कालवशं गतम् ॥
Rāvaṇa would not even entertain the idea of giving me back; for death is on the hunt for Rāvaṇa in the battlefield, his time being up.
5.37.11
ज्येष्ठा कन्या नला नाम
विभीषणसुता कपे ।
तया ममेदमाख्यातम्
मात्रा प्रहितया स्वयम् ॥
O Vānara! The eldest daughter of Vibheeshaṇa, Nalā by name, told about this herself, sent to me by her mother.
5.37.12
असंशयं हरिश्रेष्ठ
क्षिप्रं मां प्राप्स्यते पतिः ।
अन्तरात्मा च मे शुद्धः
तस्मिंश्च बहवो गुणाः ॥
Without doubt, O best of Vānaras, my lord will recover me soon, for I am pure from inside out and he is full of virtues.
5.37.13
उत्साहः पौरुषं सत्त्वम्
आनृशंस्यं कृतज्ञता ।
विक्रमश्च प्रभावश्च
सन्ति वानर राघवे ॥
Rāghava is enthusiastic, entrepreneurial, strong, compassionate, heroic, effective and has a keen sense of gratitude, O Vānara!
5.37.14
चतुर्दशसहस्राणि
राक्षसानां जघान यः ।
जनस्थाने विना भ्रात्रा
शत्रुः कस्तस्य नोद्विजेत् ॥
Which foe of his would not feel uneasy, after seeing him kill fourteen thousand Rākshasas in Janasthāna without even taking the help of his brother?
5.37.15
न स शक्यस्तुलयितुम्
व्यसनैः पुरुषर्षभः ।
अहं तस्य प्रभावज्ञा
शक्रस्येव पुलोमजा ॥
No suffering can shake that bull among men; I know his abilities as well as the daughter of Pulōma knows those of Ṡakra.
5.37.16
शरजालांशुमान्शूरः
कपे रामदिवाकरः ।
शत्रुरक्षोमयं तोयम्
उपशोषं नयिष्यति ॥
O Vānara! Rāma is like the sun with the host of arrows for the rays. He can dry up his enemies, the Rākshasas, like sun does water.
5.37.17
इति सञ्जल्पमानां ताम्
रामार्थे शोककर्शिताम् ।
आश्रुसम्पूर्णनयनाम्
उवाच वचनं कपिः ॥
When she spoke thus (assuring herself), exhausted with the grief for Rāma and her eyes filled with tears, the Vānara responded with these words:
5.37.18
मम श्रुत्वैव तु वचः
क्षिप्रमेष्यति राघवः ।
चमूं प्रकर्षन्महतीम्
हर्यृक्षगणसङ्कुलाम् ॥
The moment he hears the word from me, Rāghava will come, with no delay, pulling together a mighty army brimming with Vānaras and Ṛkshas.
5.37.19
अथवा मोचयिष्यामि
त्वामद्यैव वरानने ।
अस्माद्धुःखादुपारोह
मम पृष्ठमनिन्दिते ॥
Or else, I can relieve you from this suffering right away, O you of beautiful face; get on my back, O blameless one!
5.37.20
त्वां हि पृष्ठगतां कृत्वा
सन्तरिष्यामि सागरम् ।
शक्तिरस्ति हि मे वोढुम्
लङ्कामपि सरावणाम् ॥
I can cross the ocean having you on my back; I have the strength to carry even the entire Laṅkā along with Rāvaṇa !
5.37.21
अहं प्रस्रवणस्थाय
राघवायाद्य मैथिलि ।
प्रापयिष्यामि शक्राय
हव्यं हुतमिवानलः ॥
I will deliver you, O Maithili, to Rāghava on the Prasravaṇa right way, like Agni does Havya to Ṡakra.
5.37.22
द्रक्ष्यस्यद्वैव वैदेहि
राघवं सहलक्ष्मणम् ।
व्यवसायसमायुक्तम्
विष्णुं दैत्यवधे यथा ॥
O princess of Vidēha! You shall, right away, see Rāghava along with Lakshmaṇa all set and ready, like Vishṇu, to kill the Rākshasas!
5.37.23
त्वद्दर्शनकृतोत्साहम्
आश्रमस्थं महाबलम् ।
पुरन्दरमिवासीनम्
नागराजस्य मूर्धनि ॥
He, the mighty strong one, is sitting in the Āṡrama, eagerly waiting to see you, like Indra sitting on top of the king of elephants.
5.37.24
पृष्ठमारोह मे देवि
मा विचारय शोभने ।
योगमन्विच्छ रामेण
शशाङ्केनेव रोहिणी ॥
Hesitate no more, O beautiful one! Please get on my back! Think of getting united with Rāma like Rōhiṇee does with the moon!
5.37.25
कथयन्तीव चन्द्रेण
सूर्येण च महार्चिषा ।
मत्पृष्ठमधिरुह्य त्वम्
तराकाशमहार्णवौ ॥
You can cross the ocean sitting on my back, going along the sky as if you were there to chit-chat with the moon and the blazing sun.
5.37.26
न हि मे सम्प्रयातस्य
त्वामितो नयतोऽङ्गने ।
अनुगन्तुं गतिं शक्ताः
सर्वे लङ्कानिवासिनः ॥
None of the people who live in Laṅkā is capable of chasing me down as I carry you from here, O lady!
5.37.27
यथैवाहमिह प्राप्तः
तथैवाहमसंशयः ।
यास्यामि पश्य वैदेहि
त्वामुद्यम्य विहायसम् ॥
Without a doubt, I shall go from here as easily as I came here, as you shall see, O Vaidēhee, travelling in the sky carrying you.
5.37.28
मैथिली तु हरिश्रेष्ठात्
शृत्वा वचनमद्भुतम् ।
हर्षविस्मितसर्वाङ्गी
हनुमन्तमथाब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those wonderful words from that best of Vānaras, Maithili, happy and every limb of hers thrilled with joy, said these words to Hanumān:
5.37.29
हनुमन्दूरमध्वानम्
कथं मां वोढुमिच्छसि ।
तदेव खलु ते मन्ये
कपित्वं हरियूथप ॥
O Hanumān! How can you carry me over such a long distance? I think you truly are betraying your flippant monkey nature, O chief of Vānara army!
5.37.30
कथं वाल्पशरीरस्त्वम्
मामितो नेतुमिच्छसि ।
सकाशं मानवेन्द्रस्य
भर्तुर्मे प्लवगर्षभ ॥
How do you hope to take me from here to the presence of my husband, that lord of peoples, O bull among Vānaras, with this tiny body of yours!
5.37.31
सीताया वचनं श्रुत्वा
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ।
चिन्तयामास लक्ष्मीवान्
नवं परिभवं कृतम् ॥
Hearing those words of Seetā, the resplendent Hanumān, son of Vāyu, thought, ‘Ha, I have been slighted, for the first time!’
5.37.32
न मे जानाति सत्त्वं वा
प्रभावं वाऽसितेक्षणा ।
तस्मात्पश्यतु वैदेही
यद्रूपं मम कामतः ॥
Vaidēhee of dark eyes, does not know either my strength or my abilities. Hence, I shall show her the form I could take if I wish.
5.37.33
इति सञ्चिन्त्य हनुमान्
तदा प्लवगसत्तमः ।
दर्शयामास वैदेह्याः
स्वरूपमरिमर्दमः ॥
Then, thinking thus, Hanumān, the best among Vānaras and the tormentor of his foe, showed his original form to Vaidēhee.
5.37.34
स तस्मात्पादपाद्धीमान्
आप्लुत्य प्लवगर्षभः ।
ततो वर्धितुमारेभे
सीताप्रत्ययकारणात् ॥
Then, that astute bull among Vānaras jumped down from the tree and started to increase the size of his body to convince Seetā.
5.37.35
मेरुमन्दरसङ्काशो
बभौ दीप्तानलप्रभः ।
अग्रतो व्यवतस्थे च
सीताया वानरोत्तमः ॥
Imposing like the Mēru and Mandara, glowing with the resplendence of a blazing fire, that best of Vānaras then stood in front of Seetā.
5.37.36
हरिः पर्वतसङ्काशः
ताम्रवक्त्रो महाबलः ।
वज्रदंष्ट्रनखो भीमो
वैदेहीमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
The mighty strong Vānara of red face, his teeth and nails sharp like Vajra, looking fiery and humongous like a mountain, said these words to Vaidēhee:
5.37.37
सपर्वतवनोद्देशाम्
साट्टप्राकारतोरणाम् ।
लङ्कामिमां सनाथां वा
नयितुं शक्तिरस्ति मे ॥
I have the strength to carry off this entire Laṅkā with its mountainous and forest areas, with its forts, ramparts and gateways, along with its lord.
5.37.38
तदवस्थाप्यतां बुद्धिः
अलं देवि विकाङ्क्षया ।
विशोकं कुरु वैदेहि
राघवं सहलक्ष्मणम् ॥
Hence, O Dēvi, make up your mind and lay all your apprehensions to rest. O Vaidēhee, free Rāghava and Lakshmaṇa from their grief.
5.37.39
तं दृष्ट्वा भीमसङ्काशम्
उवाच जनकात्मजा ।
पद्मपत्रविशालाक्षी
मारुतस्यौरसं सुतम् ॥
Seeing that son of Vāyu in that dreadful form, the daughter of Janaka, her eyes wide like lotus petals, said:
5.37.40
तव सत्त्वं बलं चैव
विजानामि महाकपे ।
वायोरिव गतिं चैव
तेजश्चाग्नेरिवाद्भुतम् ॥
O great Vānara! I do know your strength and power, and speed that rivals that of Vāyu, and radiance that is amazing, like that of Agni.
5.37.41
प्राकृतोऽन्यः कथं चेमाम्
भूमिमागन्तुमर्हति ।
उदधेरप्रमेयस्य
पारं वानरपुङ्गव ॥
How could any ordinary creature have come to this place at all, on the farther shore of the measureless ocean, O bull among Vānaras!
5.37.42
जानामि गमने शक्तिम्
नयने चापि ते मम ।
अवश्यं सम्प्रधार्याशु
कार्यसिद्धिर्महात्मनः ॥
I know the power of your speed and your ability to carry me. But we should carefully consider how best the purpose of that Mahātma might be served immediately.
5.37.43
अयुक्तं तु कपिश्रेष्ठ
मम गन्तुं त्वयाऽनघ ।
वायुवेगसवेगस्य
वेगो मां मोहयेत्तव ॥
I do not think I can go with you, O best of Vānaras, O sinless one! I will feel dizzy with your speed that is equal to that of Vāyu!
5.37.44
अहमाकाशमापन्ना
ह्युपर्युपरि सागरम् ।
प्रपतेयं हि ते पृष्ठात्
भयाद्वेगेन गच्छतः ॥
Once I am in the sky at a high altitude above the ocean, and as you travel at a high speed, I will fall off from your back out of sheer fright.
5.37.45
पतिता सागरे चाहम्
तिमिनक्रझषाकुले ।
भवेयमाशु विवशा
यादसामन्नमुत्तमम् ॥
Falling into the ocean that is infested with whales, crocodiles and big fish, I would no more be in my control and will become a good meal for those aquatic creatures.
5.37.46
न च शक्ष्ये त्वया सार्धम्
गन्तुं शत्रुविनाशन ।
कलत्रवति सन्देहः
त्वय्यपि स्यादसंशयः ॥
I cannot go with you, O destroyer of the foe, for without a doubt, you will put yourself at risk burdened with the responsibility of my safety.
5.37.47
ह्रियमाणां तु मां दृष्ट्वा
राक्षसा भीमविक्रमाः ।
अनुगच्छेयुरादिष्टा
रावणेन दुरात्मना ॥
Seeing me being carried off, Rākshasas of terrible prowess will be sent by the wicked Rāvaṇa in pursuit.
5.37.48
तैस्त्वं परिवृतश्शूरैः
शूलमुद्गरपाणिभिः ।
भवेस्त्वं संशयं प्राप्तो
मया वीर कलत्रवान् ॥
Surrounded by those Sūras with spears and clubs in their hands, you yourself, O Veera, who have to protect me, will fall in danger.
5.37.49
सायुधा बहवो व्योम्नि
राक्षसास्त्वं निरायुधः ।
कथं शक्ष्यसि संयातुम्
मां चैव परिरक्षितुम् ॥
With many armed Rākshasas pitted against you in the sky, how would you be able to move forward and at the same time protect me?
5.37.50
युध्यमानस्य रक्षोभिः
तव तैः क्रूरकर्मभिः ।
प्रपतेयं हि ते पृष्ठात्
भयार्ता कपिसत्तम ॥
While you fight with those Rākshasas of cruel deeds, O best of Vānaras, I would fall off from your back out of sheer terror!
5.37.51
अथ रक्षांसि भीमानि
महान्ति बलवन्ति च ।
कथञ्चित्सांपराये त्वाम्
जयेयुः कपिसत्तम ॥
Then those terrible and mighty strong Rākshasas will somehow manage to defeat you, O best of Vānaras!
5.37.52
अथवा युध्यमानस्य
पतेयं विमुखस्य ते ।
पतितां च गृहीत्वा माम्
नयेयुः पापराक्षसाः ॥
Or I might fall off, when you, fighting them, fail to notice it, and these wicked Rākshasas mighty carry me off.
5.37.53
मां वा हरेयुस्त्वद्धस्तात्
विशसेयुरथापि वा ।
अव्यवस्थौ हि दृश्येते
युद्धे जयपराजयौ ॥
Or they might wrest me from your hands or kill me; for victory and defeat in a battle are never certain.
5.37.54
अहं वापि विपद्येयम्
रक्षोभिरभितर्जिता ।
त्वत्प्रयत्नो हरिश्रेष्ठ
भवेन्निष्फल एव तु ॥
Or I might fall in danger under the threats of the Rākshasas, and then all your efforts would be in vain, O best of Vānaras!
5.37.55
कामं त्वमसि पर्याप्तो
निहन्तुं सर्वराक्षसान् ।
राघवस्य यशो हीयेत्
त्वया शस्तैस्तु राक्षसैः ॥
You may very well be capable of killing all the Rākshasas; but that would put a dent on the glory of Rāghava, with Rākshasas killed by you.
5.37.56
अथवाऽदाय रक्षांसि
न्यसेयुस्सम्वृते हि माम् ।
यत्र ते नाभिजानीयुः
हरयो नापि राघवौ ॥
Or the Rākshasas might take and hide me in a secret place, which neither the Vānaras nor the scions of Raghu would be able to spot.
5.37.57
आरम्भस्तु मदर्थोऽयम्
ततस्तव निरर्थकः ।
त्वया हि सह रामस्य
महानागमने गुणः ॥
All the efforts you made for my sake would only go in vain; it makes better sense for Rāma to come along with you.
5.37.58
मयि जीवितमायत्तम्
राघवस्य महात्मनः ।
भ्रात्रूणां च महाबाहो
तव राजकुलस्य च ॥
O you of mighty arm! The lives of Rāghava, the Mahātma, and his brothers, and the members of your royal family are latched onto mine.
5.37.59
तौ निराशौ मदर्थं तु
शोकसन्तापकर्शितौ ।
सह सर्वर्क्षहरिभिः
त्यक्ष्यतः प्राणसङ्ग्रहम् ॥
Both of them, giving up their hopes of recovering me, will get burnt down with grief and sorrow, and might give up their lives along with all the Ṛkshas and Vānaras.
5.37.60
भर्तुर्भक्तिं पुरस्कृत्य
रामादन्यस्य वानर ।
न स्पृशामि शरीरं तु
पुंसो वानरपुङ्गव ॥
In cherishing my devotion to my husband, I shall not touch the body of any man other than Rāma, O best of Vānaras!
5.37.61
यदहं गात्रसंस्पर्शम्
रावणस्य बलाद्गता ।
अनीशा किं करिष्यामि
विनाथा विवशा सती ॥
If I had perforce touched Rāvaṇa’s body, what could I have done (to prevent it), having no capability of my own, having protection from none and having completely lost control over myself?
5.37.62
यदि रामो दशग्रीवम्
इह हत्त्वा सबान्धवम् ।
मामितो गृह्य गच्छेत
तत्तस्य सदृशं भवेत् ॥
It would be befitting of him, if Rāma kills the Ten-necked along with his kith and kin, and takes me away from here.
Even at the risk of her being killed in two months, she wants the episode to close in a manner that saves the stature, dignity and reputation of Rāma!
That indeed is truly becoming of a royal woman!
5.37.63
श्रुता हि दृष्टाश्च मया पराक्रमाः
महात्मनस्तस्य रणावमर्दिनः ।
न देवगन्धर्वभुजङ्गराक्षसाः
भवन्ति रामेण समा हि संयुगे ॥
I have heard, as well as seen directly, the prowess of that Mahātma who stamps out the foe in battle; neither the Dēvas, nor Gandharvas, nor serpents, nor Rākshasas can stand up to Rāma in a fight.
5.37.64
समीक्ष्य तं संयति चित्रकार्मुकम्
महाबलं वासवतुल्यविक्रमम् ।
सलक्ष्मणं को विषहेत राघवं
हुताशनं दीप्तमिवानिलेरितम् ॥
Who can stand up to the mighty strong Rāghava holding his amazing bow, his prowess equaling that of Indra, that too aided by Lakshmaṇa, blazing like fire abetted by strong winds?
5.37.65
सलक्ष्मणं राघवमाजिमर्दनं
दिशागजं मत्तमिव व्यवस्थितम् ।
सहेत को वानरमुख्य संयुगे
युगान्तसूर्यप्रतिमं शरार्चिषम् ॥
O chief of Vānaras! Who can stand up to Rāghava who stands majestic, like the elephant of the cardinal points in rut, blazing like the sun at the time of Dissolution, with his arrows for the rays, crushing (the enemy) in the battlefield!
5.37.66
स मे हरिश्रेष्ठ सलक्ष्मणं पतिं
सयूथपं क्षिप्रमिहोपपादय ।
चिराय रामं प्रति शोककर्शितां
कुरुष्व मां वानरमुख्य हर्षिताम् ॥
Hence, O best of Vānaras! See to it that my husband, Lakshmaṇa and the great army chiefs are here as soon as possible! O chief of Vānaras! Make me, who is worn out with grief for long, yearning for Rāma, happy!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे सप्तत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty seventh Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
