Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 66
In this Sarga, Rāma, pressing the jewel against his bosom, cries bitterly. He recalls the moments of the wedding when she wore the jewel on her head, and with that, also his father and father-in-law. He asks Hanumān to repeat everything Seetā said in detail, again and again.
5.66.1
एवमुक्तो हनुमता
रामो दशरथात्मजः ।
तं मणिं हृदये कृत्वा
प्ररुरोद सलक्ष्मणः ॥
Thus told by Hanumān, Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, pressed that jewel to his bosom and cried bitterly, and so did Lakshmaṇa.
5.66.2
तं तु दृष्ट्वा मणिश्रेष्ठम्
राघवश्शोककर्शितः ।
नेत्राभ्यामश्रुपूर्णाभ्याम्
सुग्रीवमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing that gem of a jewel and worn out by grief, his eyes filled with tears, he said this to Sugreeva:
5.66.3
यथैव धेनुस्स्रवति
स्नेहाद्वत्सस्य वत्सला ।
तथा ममापि हृदयम्
मणिरत्नस्य दर्शनात् ॥
My heart melts away seeing this gem of a jewel, like an affectionate cow, doting its calf, does.
5.66.4
मणिरत्नमिदं दत्तम्
वैदेह्याश्श्वशुरेण मे ।
वधूकाले यथाबद्धम्
अधिकं मूर्ध्नि शोभते ॥
It was given to Vaidēhee by my father-in-law on the occasion of her wedding, when, being fixed on her head it shone all the more.
5.66.5
अयं हि जलसम्भूतो
मणिस्सज्जनपूजितः ।
यज्ञे परमतुष्टेन
दत्तश्शक्रेण धीमता ॥
This jewel, which emerged from the depths of the ocean, esteemed by all good men, was a gift from the sagacious Ṡakra who was pleased by a Yajña.
5.66.6
इमं दृष्ट्वा मणिश्रेष्ठम्
यथा तातस्य दर्शनम् ।
अद्यास्म्यवगतस्सौम्य
वैदेहस्य तथा विभोः ॥
My dear! My lord! Seeing this excellent jewel, I feel, as if I am seeing my father and Vaidēhee.
5.66.7
अयं हि शोभते तस्याः
प्रियाया मूर्ध्नि मे मणिः ।
अस्याद्य दर्शनेनाहम्
प्राप्तां तामिव चिन्तये ॥
This jewel used to shine, adorned on the head of my beloved. Seeing this, I feel as if she herself is here.
5.66.8
किमाह सीता वैदेही
ब्रूहि सौम्य पुनः पुनः ।
शोकाग्निना दह्यमानं
सिञ्चमां वाक्यवारिणा ॥
My dear, repeat to me, again and again, what Seetā, the princess of Vidēha, said! Please drench me, who is burnt by grief, with the showers of (her) words!
5.66.9
इतस्तु किं दुःखतरम्
यदिमं वारिसम्भवम् ।
मणिं पश्यामि सौमित्रे
वैदेहीमागतां विना ॥
What greater grief could there be, O Sowmitri, than seeing this jewel found in the sea coming to me without Vaidēhee?
5.66.10
चिरं जीवति वैदेही
यदि मासं धरिष्यति ।
क्षणं सौम्य न जीवेयम्
विना तामसितेक्षणाम् ॥
If Vaidēhee can live for a month, it is like living forever; as for me, I cannot live even for a moment, my dear, without her, the black-eyed one!
5.66.11
नय मामपि तं देशम्
यत्र दृष्टा मम प्रिया ।
न तिष्ठेयं क्षणमपि
प्रवृत्तिमुपलभ्य च ॥
Take me also to that place where you have seen my beloved! I cannot stay here even for a moment, having known her whereabouts!
5.66.12
कथं सा मम सुश्रोणी
भीरुभीरुस्सती सदा ।
भयावहानां घोराणाम्
मध्ये तिष्ठति रक्षसाम् ॥
How is my wife, of lovely Kaṭee, who is timid always and about everything, able to survive, staying in the midst of horrid and fearsome Rākshasas!
5.66.13
शारदस्तिमिरोन्मुक्तो
नूनं चन्द्र इवांबुदैः ।
आवृतो वदनं तस्या
न विराजति साम्प्रतम् ॥
Her face must certainly be lackluster now like the cloud-covered bright moon of the post-monsoon season.
5.66.14
किमाह सीता हनुमन्
तत्त्वतः कथयाद्य मे ।
एतेन खलु जीविष्ये
भेषजेनातुरो यथा ॥
Tell me now, O Hanumān, what Seetā said, in complete detail! I will be able to hang on to my life hearing them, like a patient does with the help of medicine.
5.66.15
मधुरा मधुरालापा
किमाह मम भामिनी ।
मद्विहीना वरारोहा
हनुमन् कथयस्व मे ॥
Tell me, O Hanumān, what that lovely woman of lovely Kaṭee, gentle in nature and sweet in speech, said, managing to live without me!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे षट्षष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty sixth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
