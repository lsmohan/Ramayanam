Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 106
In this Sarga, Rāma and Rāvaṇa face each other from their respective chariots, driven by able and loyal charioteers. There ensues a fierce fight between them, each bent on slaying the other. Then there arise many omens portending the imminent destruction of Rāvaṇa and victory of Rāma. Rāma continues the fight with more zest, observing those omens.
6.106.1
सारथिः स रथं हृष्टः
परसैन्यप्रधर्षणम् ।
गन्धर्वनगराकारम्
समुच्छ्रितपताकिनम् ।
युक्तं परमसम्पन्नैः
वाजिभिर्हेममालिभिः ।
युद्धोपकरणैः पूर्णम्
पताकाध्वजमालिनम् ।
ग्रसन्तमिव चाकाशम्
नादयन्तं वसुन्धराम् ।
प्रणाशं परसैन्यानाम्
स्वसैन्यानां प्रहर्षणम् ।
रावणस्य रथं क्षिप्रम्
चोदयामास सारथिः ॥
Then the spirited charioteer drove the chariot of Rāvaṇa that spelled menace for the enemy forces and caused cheer among his own forces, yoked with prized horses that were decked with golden chains and loaded fully with implements of war. Huge like the city of Gandharvas and its flags raised high, it seemed to swallow the sky, rattle the earth and pulverize the enemy forces.
6.106.4
तमापतन्तं सहसा
स्वनवन्तं महाध्वजम् ।
रथं राक्षसराजस्य
नरराजो ददर्श ह ।
कृष्णवाजिसमायुक्तम्
युक्तं रौद्रेण वर्चसा ।
तडित्पताकागहनम्
दर्शितेन्द्रायुधप्रभम् ॥
The king of men saw that chariot of the king of Rākshasas come dashing and rumbling with its baleful splendor, huge flagstaff, its flags flashing like lightning, and yoked with black horses.
6.106.6
शरधारा विमुञ्चन्तम्
धारासारमिवाम्बुदम् ।
तं दृष्ट्वा मेघसङ्काशम्
आपतन्तं रथं रिपोः ।
गिरैर्वज्राभिमृष्टस्य
दीर्यतः सदृशस्वनम् ।
विस्फारयन् वै वेगेन
बालचन्द्रनतं धनुः ।
उवाच मातलिं रामः
सहस्राक्षस्य सारथिम् ॥
Seeing that chariot of the enemy, which shone like a cloud, come dashing, pouring forth arrows like a cloud sending a downpour, making a din that resembled that made by the splitting of mountains as they were struck by Vajra, Rāma, twanging the bow that was bent into the shape of a crescent moon, said these words to Mātali, the charioteer of the Thousand-Eyed:
6.106.9
मातले पश्य संरब्धम्
आपतन्तं रथं रिपोः ।
यथाऽऽपसव्यं पतता
वेगेन महता पुनः ।
समरे हन्तुमात्मानम्
तथा तेन कृता मतिः ॥
Look at the enemy’s chariot advancing impetuously. From the extreme speed with which he is advancing and that too in the counterclockwise direction, he seems to be determined on killing himself in this battle.
6.106.10
तदप्रमादमातिष्ठन्
प्रत्युद्गच्छ रथं रिपोः ।
विध्वंसयितुमिच्छामि
वायुर्मेघमिवोत्थितम् ॥
Hence, drive right against the chariot of the enemy, but steadily, without giving any scope for a mishap. I want to blow him away, like the wind does a cloud that rises high above.
6.106.11
अविक्लबमसम्भ्रान्तम्
अव्यग्रहृदयेक्षणम् ।
रश्मिसञ्चारनियतम्
प्रचोदय रथं द्रुतम् ॥
Drive the chariot quick, but without giving in to fear or worry and with a steady heart and steady eyes, maneuvering it well with the reins.
6.106.12
कामं न त्वं समाधेयः
पुरन्दररथोचितः ।
युयुत्सुरहमेकाग्रः
स्मारये त्वां न शिक्षये ॥
You, who are used to the Purandara's chariot, need not be told what to do; I am not trying to direct you, but mean to just remind you, because I want to put all my focus on the fight.
6.106.13
परितुष्टः स रामस्य
तेन वाक्येन मातलिः ।
प्रचोदयामास रथम्
सुरसारथिसत्तमः ।
अपसव्यं ततः कुर्वन्
रावणस्य महारथम् ।
चक्र संभूत रजसा
रावणं व्यवधूनयन् ॥
Pleased with those words of Rāma, Mātali, the foremost of the charioteers of the Dēvas, drove the chariot in the anticlockwise direction around the chariot of Rāvaṇa, covering Rāvaṇa with the dust raised by its wheels.
6.106.15
ततः क्रुद्धो दशग्रीवः
ताम्रविस्फारितेक्षणः ।
रथप्रतिमुखं रामम्
सायकैरवधूनयत् ॥
Then the Ten-necked, his eyes red and opened wide in rage, shook Rāma, who was right across from his chariot, assailing him with arrows.
6.106.16
धर्षणामर्षितो रामो
धैर्यं रोषेण लम्भयन् ।
जग्राह सुमहावेगम्
ऐन्द्रं युधि शरासनम् ।
शरांश्च सुमहातेजाः
सूर्यरश्मिसमप्रभान् ॥
Annoyed by that belligerence, the extraordinarily powerful Rāma, his anger giving wings to his courage, seized the bow of Indra and also arrows that glittered like the rays of the sun.
6.106.18
तदुपोढं महद्युद्धम्
अन्योन्यवधकाङ्क्षिणोः ।
परस्पराभिमुखयोः
दृप्तयोरिव सिंहयोः ॥
Then ensued a great fight between the two who were each bent upon slaying the other, as they confronted each other like two proud lions.
6.106.19
ततो देवाः सगन्धर्वाः
सिद्धाश्च परमर्षयः ।
समेयुर्द्वैरथं दृष्टुम्
रावणक्षयकाङ्क्षिणः ॥
Then the Dēvas, along with Gandharvas, Siddhas and the supreme Ṛshis, wishing for the destruction of Rāvaṇa, assembled there to witness the chariot-duel.
6.106.20
समुत्पेतुरथोत्पाता
दारुणा रोमहर्षणाः ।
रावणस्य विनाशाय
राघवस्य जयाय च ॥
Then there arose terrible and hair-raising omens portending the destruction of Rāvaṇa and victory of Rāma.
6.106.21
ववर्ष रुधिरं देवो
रावणस्य रथोपरि ।
वाता मण्डलिनस्तीव्रा
ह्यपसव्यं प्रचक्रमुः ॥
The Dēva (Parjanya) showered blood on Rāvaṇa’s chariot. Fierce whirlwinds circled it in an anticlockwise direction.
6.106.22
महद् गृध्रकुलं चास्य
भ्रममाणं नभस्स्थले ।
येन येन रथो याति
तेन तेन प्रधावति ॥
A flock of vultures hovering in the sky followed Rāvaṇa’s chariot wherever it went.
6.106.23
सन्ध्यया चावृता लङ्का
जपापुष्पनिकाशया ।
दृश्यते सम्प्रदीप्तेव
दिवसेऽपि वसुन्धरा ॥
Dusk seemed to have descended upon Laṅkā (all of a sudden), with the glow of Japa flower. The grounds appeared red, even during that daytime, as if they were burning.
6.106.24
सनिर्घाता महोल्काश्च
सम्प्रपेतुर्महास्वनाः ।
विषादयंस्ते रक्षांसि
रावणस्य तदाऽहिताः ॥
Huge meteors, along with thunder fell, making a huge din, boding ill to Rāvaṇa and throwing the Rākshasas into distress.
6.106.25
रावणश्च यतस्तत्र
सञ्चचाल वसुन्धरा ।
रक्षसां च प्रहरताम्
गृहीता इव बाहवः ॥
The earth, wherever Rāvaṇa went, shook. The arms of the Rākshasas, raised to strike, seemed to be held back (by someone invisible).
6.106.26
ताम्राः पीताः सिताश्वेताः
पतिताः सूर्यरश्मयः ।
दृश्यन्ते रावणस्याङ्गे
पर्वतस्येव धातवः ॥
There fell on Rāvaṇa’s limbs sunbeams that were red and yellow, white and dark, looking like the veins of ore on a mountain.
6.106.27
गृध्रैरनुगताश्चास्य
वमन्त्यो ज्वलनं मुखैः ।
प्रणेदुर्मुखमीक्षन्त्यः
संरब्धमशिवं शिवाः ॥
Jackals, with vultures hovering right behind them, stared at him eerily, howling inauspiciously with a lot of commotion and spewing fire from their mouths.
6.106.28
प्रतिकूलं ववौ वायू
रणे पांसून् समाकिरन् ।
तस्य राक्षसराजस्य
कुर्वन् दृष्टिविलोपनम् ॥
The wind blew right against the king of Rākshasas, blowing the dust all over that battlefield and obfuscating his view.
6.106.29
निपेतुरिन्द्राशनयः
सैन्ये चास्य समन्ततः ।
दुर्विषह्यस्वना घोरा
विना जलधरोदयम् ॥
Lightning struck his army on all sides with a terrible deafening clatter, without any clouds emerging in the sky.
6.106.30
दिशश्च प्रदिशः सर्वा
बभूवुस्तिमिरावृताः ।
पांसुवर्षेण महता
दुर्दर्शं च नभोऽभवत् ॥
The cardinals and the diagonals were enveloped in darkness, and the sky could hardly be seen because of a huge shower of dust.
6.106.31
कुर्वन्त्यः कलहं घोरम्
शारिकास्तद्रथं प्रति ।
निपेतुः शतशस्तत्र
दारुण दारुणारुताः ॥
With a great clamor, hundreds of horrid Ṡārika birds fell on his chariot, shrieking ominously.
6.106.32
जघनेभ्यः स्फुलिङ्गांश्च
नेत्रेभ्योऽश्रूणि सन्ततम् ।
मुमुचुस्तस्य तुरगाः
तुल्यमग्निं च वारि च ॥
Sparks flew from the loins and tears dropped from the eyes of the horses incessantly, fire and water thus issuing from them at the same time.
6.106.33
एवम्प्रकारा बहवः
समुत्पाता भयावहाः ।
रावणस्य विनाशाय
दारुणाः सम्प्रजज्ञिरे ॥
Many frightful and terrible omens like these emerged, foreboding the destruction of Rāvaṇa.
6.106.34
रामस्यापि निमित्तानि
सौम्यानि च शुभानि च ।
बभूवुर्जयशंसीनि
प्रादुर्भूतानि सर्वशः ॥
As far as Rāma was concerned, though, many a sign, pleasant and auspicious, emerged all around, foretelling his victory.
6.106.35
निमित्तानि च सौम्यानि
राघवः स्वजयाय च ।
दृष्ट्वा परमसंहृष्टो
हतं मेने च रावणम् ॥
Seeing these pleasant signs that indicated his victory Rāghava was extremely happy and felt that Rāvaṇa was all but dead.
6.106.36
ततो निरीक्ष्यात्मगतानि राघवो
रणे निमित्तानि निमित्तकोविदः ।
जगाम हर्षं च परां च निर्वृत्तिं
चकार युद्धे ह्यधिकं च विक्रमम् ॥
Rāghava, a savant of the science of omens, seeing indications on his own body, (that forebode victory for him), felt extremely happy and relieved, and threw himself more zestfully into the fray.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे षडुत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and sixth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
