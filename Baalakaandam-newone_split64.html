Baala Kaanda - Sarga 64
This Sarga is an extension of the previous Sarga and could have been included in it.
The Tapa of Viṡwāmitra gets intense in this Sarga. The Dēvas get concerned and send the Apsara Rambha to distract him. But he realizes just in time that he is yielding to carnal desires and manages not to fall for her.
However, he is unable stop himself from getting angry, and curses her. Thus he succeeds in reining in his Kāma (desires) but not in managing Krōdha (anger), yet.
He vows to himself to continue the intense Tapa till he attains Brahmatva.
1.64.1
सुरकार्यमिदं रम्भे
कर्तव्यं सुमहत्त्वया ।
लोभनं कौशिकस्येह
काममोहसमन्वितम् ॥
For the sake of the great cause of Dēvas, you should seduce the son of Kuṡika with your charms.
1.64.2
तथोक्ता साप्सरा राम
सहस्राक्षेण धीमता ।
व्रीडिता प्राञ्जलिर्भूत्वा
प्रत्युवाच सुरेश्वरम् ॥
O Rāma! The Apsara blushed at that command of the thousand-eyed, astute Indra and said with folded hands, to that lord of Dēvas:
1.64.3
अयं सुरपते घोरो
विश्वामित्रो महामुनिः ।
क्रोधमुत्सृजते घोरम्
मयि देव न संशयः ।
ततो हि मे भयं देव
प्रसादं कर्तुमर्हसि ॥
O lord of Dēvas! May I beg to be excuse! The Mahā Muni Viṡwāmitra is dreadful. I am scared that he will empty the vials of his wrath on me, without doubt.
Wow! How many people would be able to say 'no' like this to their bosses, even in these 'modern' times?
1.64.4
एवमुक्तस्तया राम
रम्भया भीतया तदा ।
तामुवाच सहस्राक्षो
वेपमानां कृताञ्जलिम् ॥
O Rāma! To those words of Rambha, who was scared and shaking and standing with folded hands, the thousand-eyed Indra said:
1.64.5
मा भैषि रम्भे भद्रं ते
कुरुष्व मम शासनम् ।
कोकिलो हृदयग्राही
माधवे रुचिरद्रुमे ।
अहं कन्दर्पसहितः
स्थास्यामि तव पार्श्वतः ॥
O Rambha! May all bode well for you! Do not be scared. Just do as I say. I will take the form of a Kōkila bird, along with Kandarpa and will perch on the branches of beautiful trees of spring, chirping sweetly enough to arouse passion in anybody's heart.
1.64.7
त्वं हि रूपं बहुगुणम्
कृत्वा परमभास्वरम् ।
तमृषिं कौशिकं रम्भे
भेदयस्व तपस्विनम् ॥
O Rambha! Enhance your appearance multi-fold. Make yourself most attractive. Distract that Ṛshi, the son of Kuṡika.
1.64.8
सा श्रुत्वा वचनं तस्य
कृत्वा रूपमनुत्तमम् ।
लोभयामास ललिता
विश्वामित्रं शुचिस्मिता ॥
Upon those words, Rambha made herself exceptionally lovely and attractive and enticed Viṡwāmitra with her beauty and flashing smile.
1.64.9
कोकिलस्य तु शुश्राव
वल्गु व्याहरतः स्वनम् ।
सम्प्रहृष्टेन मनसा
तत एनामुदैक्षत ॥
He heard the sweet chirping of the Kōkila bird. He then saw her with a ravished heart.
1.64.10
अथ तस्य च शब्देन
गीतेनाप्रतिमेन च ।
दर्शनेन च रम्भाया
मुनिः सन्देहमागतः ॥
That sound of Kōkila, that exceptional voice, and the appearance of Rambha made the Muni suspicious.
1.64.11
सहस्राक्षस्य तत्कर्म
विज्ञाय मुनिपुङ्गवः ।
रम्भां क्रोधसमाविष्टः
शशाप कुशिकात्मजः ॥
He understood it to be the act of the thousand-eyed. The son of Kuṡika then became outraged and cursed Rambha:
1.64.12
यन्मां लोभयसे रम्भे
कामक्रोधजयैषिणम् ।
दशवर्षसहस्राणि
शैली स्थास्यसि दुर्भगे ॥
O wretched Rambha! You are trying to seduce me, while I am working hard to overcome desire and anger. May you lie as a stone for ten thousand years!
Years of life are wasted by many, blaming others!
1.64.13
ब्राह्मणः सुमहातेजाः
तपोबलसमन्वितः ।
उद्धरिष्यति रम्भे त्वाम्
मत्क्रोधकलुषीकृताम् ॥
O Rambha! A supremely radiant Brāhmaṇa who has gained immense spiritual energy through Tapa will eventually provide you the relief from this anger of mine.
1.64.14
एवमुक्त्वा महातेजा
विश्वामित्रो महामुनिः ।
अशक्नुवन् धारयितुम्
क्रोधं सन्तापमागतः ॥
That supremely radiant Mahā Muni Viṡwāmitra, thus cursed her, unable to contain his anger.
1.64.15
तस्य शापेन महता
रम्भा शैली तदाऽभवत् ।
वचः श्रुत्वा च कन्दर्पो
महर्षेः स च निर्गतः ॥
With that curse, Rambha became a stone. Hearing those words of the Maharshi, Indra and Kandarpa receded quietly.
1.64.16
कोपेन सुमहातेजाः
तपोऽपहरणे कृते ।
इन्द्रियैरजितै राम
न लेभे शान्तिमात्मनः ॥
That supremely radiant Muni thus lost his spiritual energy overpowered by anger. He could not find peace, having been unable to overcome the pull of the senses.
1.64.17
बभूवास्य मनश्चिन्ता
तपोपहरणे कृते ॥
After losing his spiritual energy, he thought:
1.64.17
नैव क्रोधं गमिष्यामि
न च वक्ष्ये कथञ्चन ।
अथवा नोच्छ्वसिष्यामि
संवत्सरशतान्यपि ।
अहं विशोषयिष्यामि
ह्यात्मानं विजितेन्द्रियः ॥
I will not get angry any more. I will not speak a word, no matter what. I would rather go breathless for a hundred years and enervate my body and win over my senses.
1.64.19
तावद्यावद्धि मे प्राप्तम्
ब्राह्मण्यं तपसाऽऽर्जितम् ।
अनुच्छ्वसन्नभुञ्जानः
तिष्ठेयं शाश्वतीः समाः ।
न हि मे तप्यमानस्य
क्षयं यास्यन्ति मूर्तयः ॥
Till I attain Brahmatva with my Tapa, I will not breathe or eat, no matter how long it takes. May my Tapa prevent my body from deteriorating!
1.64.20
एवं वर्षसहस्रस्य
दीक्षां स मुनिपुङ्गवः ।
चकाराप्रतिमां लोके
प्रतिज्ञां रघुनन्दन ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! That foremost among Munis took that exceptional vow for one thousand years.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे चतुःषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty fourth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
