Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 6
In this Sarga, Sugreeva tells Rāma that he learned the details of Rāma’s situation from his good minister, Hanumān. He promises to find and bring Seetā no matter where she was. He thinks that the woman who dropped her upper garment and ornaments as she was being carried off by a Rākshasa must be Seetā. He brings the ornaments and the garment from the cave where he had kept them and shows them to Rāma. Rāma cries, his tears flowing unchecked, upon seeing them. He asks Sugreeva to tell more about the Rākshasa and in which direction he went.
4.6.1
पुनरेवाब्रवीत्प्रीतो
राघवं रघुनन्दनम् ।
अयमाख्याति मे राम
सचिवो मन्त्रिसत्तमः ।
हनुमान्यन्निमित्तं त्वम्
निर्जनं वनमागतः ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
वसतश्च वने तव ।
रक्षसाऽपहृता भार्या
मैथिली जनकात्मजा ।
त्वया वियुक्ता रुदती
लक्ष्मणेन च धीमता ।
अन्तरप्रेप्सुना तेन
हत्वा गृध्रं जटायुषम् ।
भार्यावियोगजं दुःखम्
प्रापितस्तेन रक्षसा ॥
Pleased, he (Sugreeva) said again to Rāghava, the delight of the lineage of Raghu: ‘O Rāma! Hanumān, my counsellor and an excellent minister explained to me why you have come to the desolate Vana, how you were living in the Vana along with your brother Lakshmaṇa, and how your wife Maithili, the daughter of Janaka was carried off by a Rākshasa when he found a chance while you and the sagacious Lakshmaṇa were away and how he killed Jaṭāyu, the eagle, throwing you into the grief of separation from your wife.’
4.6.5
भार्यावियोगजं दुःखम्
न चिरात्त्वं विमोक्ष्यसे ।
अहं तामानयिष्यामि
नष्टां वेदश्रुतीमिव ॥
You shall be relieved of the grief of separation from your wife very shortly. I will fetch her back (with the urgency of) fetching the lost Vēdas.
4.6.6
रसातले वा वर्तन्तीं
वर्तन्ती वा नभस्थले ।
अहमानीय दास्यामि
तव भार्यामरिन्दम ॥
O subduer of enemies! I shall fetch and give your wife to you no matter whether she is in the nether regions or in the sky.
4.6.7
इदं तथ्यं मम वचः
त्वमवेहि च राघव ।
न शक्या सा जरयितुम्
सेन्द्रैरपि स्सुरासुरैः ।
तव भार्या महाबाहो
भक्ष्यं विषकृतं यथा ॥
O Rāghava! Count on my words to come true! O you of the mighty arm! She cannot be held for long, by any one, be it Suras along with Indra, or Asuras, any more than poisoned food can be in the stomach.
4.6.8
त्यज शोकं महाबाहो
तां कान्तामानयामि ते ।
अनुमानात्तु जानामि
मैथिली सा न संशयः ।
ह्रियमाणा मया दृष्टा
रक्षसा क्रूरकर्मणा ।
क्रोशन्ती राम रामेति
लक्ष्मणेति च विस्वरम् ।
स्फुरन्ती राक्षसस्याङ्के
पन्नगेन्द्रवधूर्यथा ॥
O you of might arm! Abdicate your grief. I will bring your woman back to you. I suspect, without doubt, that I know her, Maithili. I saw her being carried off by the cruel Rākshasa. I think that she was the one who cried, ‘Rāma! Rāma! Lakshmaṇa!’, in a bitter voice writhing on the lap of Rākshasa like the mate of a mighty serpent.
4.6.11
आत्मना पञ्चमं मां हि
दृष्ट्वा शैलतले स्थितम् ।
उत्तरीयं तया त्यक्तम्
शुभान्याभरणानि च ॥
Seeing the five of us, including me, on the top of the mountain, she dropped her upper garment and her beautiful ornaments.
4.6.12
तान्यस्माभिर्गृहीतानि
निहितानि च राघव ।
आनयिष्याम्यहं तानि
प्रत्यभिज्ञातुमर्हसि ॥
We have taken and kept them safely. I shall bring them; you should be able to recognize them.
4.6.13
तमब्रवीत्ततो रामः
सुग्रीवं प्रियवादिनम् ।
आनयस्व सखे शीघ्रम्
किमर्थं प्रविलम्बसे ॥
And Rāma urged Sugreeva, who had given him good news, ‘Fetch them quick, my friend, why delay?’
4.6.14
एवमुक्तस्तु सुग्रीव
शैलस्य गहनां गुहाम् ।
प्रविवेश ततश्शीघ्रं
राघवप्रियकाम्यया ॥
Sugreeva, thus asked, went immediately into a mountain cave that was difficult to access, eager to please Rāghava.
4.6.15
उत्तरीयं गृहीत्वा तु
शुभान्याभरणानि च ।
इदं पश्येति रामाय
दर्शयामास वानरः ॥
The Vānara fetched the upper garment and the exquisite ornaments and showed them to Rāma saying, ‘See, here!’
4.6.16
ततो गृहीत्वा वासस्तु
श्शुभान्याभरणानि च ।
अभवद्बाष्पसंरुद्धो
नीहारेणेव चन्द्रमाः ॥
Taking that garment and the lovely ornaments, he was overtaken by tears, like the moon by mist:
4.6.17
सीतास्नेहप्रवृत्तेन
स तु बाष्पेण दूषितः ।
हा प्रियेति रुदन्धैर्यम्
उत्सृज्य न्यपतत्क्षितौ ॥
Impeded by the tears that welled because of his love for Seetā, he cried, ‘Ha, my beloved!’ and lost his composure and fell on the ground.
4.6.18
हृदि कृत्वा स बहुशः
तमलङ्कारमुत्तमम् ।
निशश्वास भृशं सर्पो
बिलस्थ इव रोषितः ॥
Pressing those splendid jewels repeatedly to his breast, he heaved great sighs like an angry serpent in its hole.
4.6.19
अविच्छिन्नाश्रुवेगस्तु
सौमित्रिं वीक्ष्य पार्श्वतः ।
परिदेवयितुं दीनम्
रामस्समुपचक्रमे ॥
With tears flowing fast and unchecked, Rāma turned to Sowmitri who was by his side and started lamenting pitifully:
4.6.20
पश्य लक्ष्मण वैदेह्या
सन्त्यक्तं ह्रियमाणया ।
उत्तरीयमिदं भूमौ
शरीराद्भूषणानि च ॥
Lakshmaṇa, look at this upper garment and the ornaments (that were) on her body that Vaidēhee dropped on to the ground, as she was being carried off.
4.6.21
शाद्वलिन्यां ध्रुवं भूमौ
सीतया ह्रियमाणया ।
उत्सृष्टं भूषणमिदम्
तथारूपं हि दृश्यते ॥
These ornaments must have been dropped on the grass lands by Seetā, as she was being carried off, for they look just as they were (undamaged).
4.6.22
एवमुक्तस्तु रामेण
लक्ष्मणो वाक्यमब्रवीत् | ।
नाहम् जानामि केयूरे
नाहम् जानामि कुण्डले | ।
नूपुरेत्वभिजानामि
नित्यम् पाद अभिवंदनात् ॥
When Rāma said this, Lakshmaṇa responded saying: ‘I do not know the bracelets nor the ear-pendants; but I know the anklets, as I used to prostrate myself at her feet every day’.
This Ṡlōka, though very touching, is widely considered unauthentic.
4.6.23
ततस्तु राघवो वाक्यम्
सुग्रीवमिदमब्रवीत् ।
ब्रूहि सुग्रीव कं देशम्
ह्रियन्ती लक्षिता त्वया ।
रक्षसा रौद्ररूपेण
मम प्राणैः प्रिया प्रिया ॥
Then Rāghava turned to Sugreeva and said: ‘O Sugreeva! Tell me to which region you have seen my life, my dear, my beloved being carried off by that fierce looking Rākshasa?’
4.6.25
क्व वा वसति तद्रक्षो
महद्व्यसनदं मम ।
यन्निमित्तमहं सर्वान्
नाशयिष्यामि राक्षसान् ॥
Where does that Rākshasa, who caused such a grief to me and whom I am going to destroy along with all the other Rākshasas, live?
4.6.26
हरता मैथिलीं येन
मां च रोषयता भृशम् ।
आत्मनो जीवितान्ताय
मृत्युद्वारमपावृतम् ॥
Who was he that opened wide the door of death to himself, bringing an end to his life, by carrying off Maithili and earning my terrible wrath?
4.6.27
मम दयिततमा हृता वनात्
रजनिचरेण विमथ्य येन सा ।
कथय मम रिपुं तमद्य वै
प्लवगपते यमसादनं नयामि ॥
O Lord of Vānaras! Tell me about the Rākshasa, my foe, by whom, she, my dearest, was carried off forcibly! I will send him to the abode of Yama right away.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे षष्ठस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
