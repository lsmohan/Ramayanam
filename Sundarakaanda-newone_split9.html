Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 9
In this Sarga, Hanumān sees the Vimāna called Pushpaka. From there, the flavors of the foods lead him to Rāvaṇa’s inner quarters and to his bed room. There he see the wives of Rāvaṇa sleeping, exhausted, having played love sports and drinking. Looking around, he then sees the splendid bedstead of Rāvaṇa.
5.9.1
तस्यालयवरिष्ठस्य
मध्ये विपुलमायतं ।
ददर्श भवनश्रेष्ठम्
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ॥
Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, saw a huge palace in the midst of the complex of exquisite mansions.
5.9.2
अर्धयोजनविस्तीर्णम्
आयतं योजनं हि तत् ।
भवनं राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
बहुप्रासादसङ्कुलम् ॥
That house of the lord of Rākshasas, consisting of many individual mansions was half a Yōjana wide and one Yōjana long.
5.9.3
मार्गमाणस्तु वैदेहीम्
सीतामायतलोचनाम् ।
सर्वतः परिचक्राम
हनूमानरिसूदनः ॥
Searching for the wide-eyed Seetā, the princess of the Vidēha kingdom, Hanumān, the subduer of foe, went over every part of it.
5.9.4
उत्तमं राक्षसावासम्
हनुमानवलोकयन् ।
आससादाथ लक्ष्मीवान्
राक्षसेन्द्रनिवेशनम् ।
चतुर्विषाणैर्द्विरदैः
त्रिविषाणैस्तथैव च ।
परिक्षिप्तमसम्बाधम्
रक्ष्यमाणमुदायुधैः ॥
Hanumān then approached and inspected that spacious residence of the lord of Rākshasas, which was inhabited by many Rākshasas and protected by two-tusked, three-tusked and four-tusked elephants and guards wielding weapons.
5.9.6
राक्षसीभिश्च पत्नीभी
रावणस्य निवेशनम् ।
आहृताभिश्च विक्रम्य
राजकन्याभिरावृतम् ॥
It was the abode of Rākshasis and the wives of Rāvaṇa. It was filled with the princesses who were abducted by force.
5.9.7
तन्नक्रमकराकीर्णम्
तिमिङ्गिलझषाकुलम् ।
वायुवेगसमाधूतम्
पन्नगैरिव सागरम् ॥
It resembled an ocean tossed by high winds, crowded with crocodiles, sea-monsters and the tumult of whales and great fishes, and serpents.
5.9.8
या हि वैश्रवणे लक्ष्मीः
या चेन्द्रे हरिवाहने ।
सा रावणगृहे सर्वा
नित्यमेवानपायिनी ॥
The opulence that is hallmark of the palace of Vaiṡravaṇa and the palace of Indra who had green horses for his mount, was seen in that house of Rāvaṇa, never depleted and never dried up.
5.9.9
या च राज्ञः कुबेरस्य
यमस्य वरुणस्य च ।
तादृशी तद्विशिष्टा वा
ऋद्धी रक्षोगृहेष्विह ॥
The opulence in the house of the Rākshasa matched, and even excelled, that in the houses of King Kubēra, Yama and Varuṇa.
5.9.10
तस्य हर्म्यस्य मध्यस्थम्
वेश्म चान्यत्सुनिर्मितं ।
बहुनिर्यूहसङ्कीर्णम्
ददर्श पवनात्मजः ॥
In the midst of that great palace, the son of Vāyu saw another structure beautifully fashioned with multitudes of sculpted figures on it, of elephants in rut.
5.9.11
ब्रह्मणोऽर्थे कृतं दिव्यम्
दिवि यद्विश्वकर्मणा ।
विमानं पुष्पकं नाम
सर्वरत्नविभूषितम् ।
परेण तपसा लेभे
यत्कुबेरः पितामहात् ।
कुबेरमोजसा जित्वा
लेभे तद्राक्षसेश्वरः ॥
Adorned with every kind of gem, it was a Vimāna known as Pushpaka built originally for Lord Brahma by Viṡwakarma. Kubēra, by performing extreme Tapa, received it from the Grandsire. The lord of Rākshasas won it from Kubēra by his sheer valor.
5.9.13
ईहामृगसमायुक्तैः
कार्तस्वरहिरण्मयैः ।
सुकृतैराचितं स्तम्भैः
प्रदीप्तमिव च श्रिया ।
मेरुमन्दरसङ्काशैः
उल्लिखद्भिरिवाम्बरं ।
कूटागारैश्शुभाकारैः
सर्वतस्समलङ्कृतम् ॥
Decorated well everywhere and dazzling in its opulence, it was supported by well-crafted pillars made of gold and silver covered with figures of wolves in relief.
With its well-designed rooms and halls it was tall like Mēru and Mandara, as if it was rising high to touch the sky.
5.9.15
ज्वलनार्कप्रतीकाशम्
सुकृतं विश्वकर्मणा ।
हेमसोपानसंयुक्तम्
चारुप्रवरवेदिकम् ॥
Well-constructed by Viṡwakarma, it dazzled like fire and the sun. It had golden steps and beautiful and excellent platforms.
5.9.16
जालवातायनैर्युक्तम्
काञ्चनैः स्स्फाटिकैरपि ।
इन्द्रनीलमहानील
मणिप्रवरवेदिकम् ॥
It sported lattice-windows and vent-holes made of gold and crystal. Its platforms were encrusted with the best Indraneela and Mahāneela gems.
5.9.17
विद्रुमेण विचित्रेण
मणिभिश्च महाघनैः ।
निस्तुलाभिश्च मुक्ताभिः
तलेनाभिविराजितम् ॥
Its mosaic floors were glittering, studded with extremely expensive gems, corals and pearls for which there was no match.
5.9.18
चन्दनेन च रक्तेन
तपनीयनिभेन च ।
सुपुण्यगन्धिना युक्तम्
आदित्यतरुणोपमम् ॥
The shine of the gold and red colored fragrant sandal with which it was coated, made it shine like the morning sun.
5.9.19
कूटागारैर्वराकारैः
विविधैस्समलङ्कृतम् ।
विमानं पुष्पकं दिव्यम्
आरुरोह महाकपिः ॥
The great Vānara then climbed on to that splendid Vimāna, Pushpaka, that was fashioned well with excellent rooms of many shapes and forms.
5.9.20
तत्र स्थस्स तदा गन्धम्
पानभक्ष्यान्नसम्भवम् ।
दिव्यं सम्मूर्छितं जिघ्रत्
रूपवन्तमिवानिलम् ॥
Standing there, he smelled the divine flavors that emanated from the drinks and foods, which spread around as if to give a shape and texture to the air in there.
5.9.21
स गन्धस्तं महासत्त्वम्
बन्धुर्बन्धुमिवोत्तमम् ।
इत एहीत्युवाचेन
तत्र यत्र स रावणः ॥
The aroma of those flavors seemed to beckon him, of great strength, saying ‘come this way, come this way!’, like one does to an esteemed relation, leading to the place where Rāvaṇa was.
5.9.22
ततस्तां प्रस्थितश्शालाम्
ददर्श महतीं शुभाम् ।
रावणस्य मनः कान्ताम्
कान्तामिव वरस्त्रियम् ॥
Heading in that direction he saw a huge beautiful hall which had won the heart of Rāvaṇa like a lovely and beautiful woman.
5.9.23
मणिसोपानविकृताम्
हेमजालविभूषिताम् ।
स्फाटिकैरावृततलाम्
दन्तान्तरितरूपिकाम् ॥
Its steps were spread with gems. Its windows were shining with gold. Its floor was studded with crystals set with ivory in between.
5.9.24
मुक्ताभिश्च प्रवालैश्च
रूप्यचामीकरैरपि ।
विभूषितां मणिस्तम्भैः
सुबहुस्तम्भभूषिताम् ॥
Its jeweled pillars were shining with pearls, corals, silver and gold. It was splendid with multitudes of such well-made pillars.
5.9.25
नम्रैरृजुभिरत्युच्चैः
समन्तात्सुविभूषितैः ।
स्तम्भैः पक्षैरिवात्युच्चैः
दिवं संप्रस्थितामिव ॥
With its well decorated, tall pillars, some slightly curved and some straight, it seemed to have its wings stretched ready to fly into the sky.
5.9.26
महत्या कुथयास्तीर्णाम्
पृथिवीलक्षणाङ्कया ।
पृथिवीमिव विस्तीर्णाम्
सराष्ट्रगृहमालिनीम् ॥
It seemed to be as wide as the earth itself, spread with a carpet whose design showed the earth and its features such as the various countries and clusters of residences.
5.9.27
नादितां मत्तविहगैः
दिव्यगन्धाधिवासिताम् ।
परार्ध्यास्तरणोपेताम्
रक्षोधिपनिषेविताम् ॥
Used by the Lord of the Rākshasas, it resounded with the chatter of amorous birds, fragrant with divine scents and furnished with invaluable bed-spreads.
5.9.28
धूम्रामगरुधूपेन
विमलां हंसपाण्डुराम् ।
चित्रां पुष्पोपहारेण
कल्माषीमिव सुप्रभाम् ॥
Spotlessly white as a swan, smoky with the incense of Agaru and lovely with the decoration of flowers, it shone like a brindled cow.
5.9.29
मनस्संह्लादजननीम्
वर्णस्यापि प्रसादिनीम् ।
तां शोकनाशिनीं दिव्याम्
श्रियः सञ्जननीमिव ॥
It was divine, seeming to be capable of removing one’s grief, giving peace to the mind, enhancing the complexion of the body and even granting opulence to one!
5.9.30
इन्द्रियाणीन्द्रियार्थैस्तु
पञ्च पञ्चभिरुत्तमैः ।
तर्पयामास मातेव
तदा रावणपालिता ॥
Well managed by Rāvaṇa, it offered, like a (doting) mother does, five kinds of pleasures that satisfy the five senses respectively.
5.9.31
स्वर्गोऽयं देवलोकोऽयम्
इन्द्रस्येयं पुरी भवेत् ।
सिद्धिर्वेयं परा हि स्यात्
इत्यमन्यत मारुतिः ॥
The son of Vāyu thought, ‘This is like Swarga; this is like the world of Dēvas! This must be the city of Indra! This must be a reward for ultimate Tapa!’
5.9.32
प्रध्यायत इवापश्यत्
प्रदीपांस्तत्र काञ्चनान् ।
धूर्तानिव महाधूतैः
देवनेन पराजितान् ॥
The bright golden lamps there seemed to be lost in brooding like gamblers defeated by master-gamblers in a game of dice.
5.9.33
दीपानां च प्रकाशेन
तेजसा रावणस्य च ।
अर्चिर्भिर्भूषणानां च
प्रदीप्तेत्यभ्यमन्यत ॥
He felt that its dazzle owed itself not only to the light from the lamps, but also to the reflections of the gems and equally to the radiance of Rāvaṇa.
5.9.34
ततोऽपश्यत्कुथाऽऽसीनं
नानावर्णाम्बरस्रजम् ।
सहस्रं वरनारीणाम्
नानावेषविभूषितम् ॥
Then he saw the thousands of lovely women clad in colorful costumes wearing beautiful garlands lying on the carpet.
5.9.35
परिवृत्तेऽर्धरात्रे तु
पाननिद्रावशं गतम् ।
क्रीडित्वोपरतं रात्रौ
सुष्वाप बलवत्तदा ॥
With the clock turning past mid-night, they were in deep slumber, drunken to numbness and having retired from love sports that have lasted well into the late night.
5.9.36
तत्प्रसुप्तं विरुरुचे
निश्शब्दान्तरभूषणम् ।
निश्शब्दहंसभ्रमरम्
यथा पद्मवनं महत् ॥
With them being fast asleep and the sounds of their ornaments absolutely stilled, that place shone like a large bed of lotuses where the swans and bees had gone silent.
5.9.37
तासां संवृतदन्तानि
मीलिताक्षीणि मारुतिः ।
अपश्यत्पद्मगन्धीनि
वदनानि सुयोषिताम् ॥
The son of Vāyu saw the faces of those beautiful women, fragrant as lotuses, eyes covered by the eyelids and teeth (by the lips).
5.9.38
प्रबुद्धानीव पद्मानि
तासां भूत्वा क्षपाक्षये ।
पुनस्संवृतपत्राणि
रात्राविव बभुस्तदा ॥
With the best part of the night already behind, their faces, which were like lotuses in full bloom, shone like lotuses again, only with their petals closed due to night-fall.
5.9.39
इमानि मुखपद्मानि
नियतं मत्तषट्पदाः ।
अम्बुजानीव फुल्लानि
प्रार्थयन्ति पुनः पुनः ।
इतिवामन्यत श्रीमान्
उपपत्त्या महाकपिः ।
मेने हि गुणतस्तानि
समानि सलिलोद्भवैः ॥
‘Surely the amorous bees would seek these lotus faces again and again mistaking them for real lotuses that bloom on the water’ thought that blessed great Vānara, with good reason, seeing them lack no quality that the water borne ones have.
5.9.41
सा तस्य शुशुभे शाला
ताभिस्त्रीभिर्विराजिता ।
शारदीव प्रसन्ना द्यौः
ताराभिरभिशोभिता ॥
That apartment of his (Rāvaṇa’s), lit up by the presence of those women, shone like the pleasant post-monsoon sky embellished by the stars.
5.9.42
स च ताभिः परिवृतः
शुशुभे राक्षसाधिपः ।
यथा ह्युडुपतिश्शीमान्
ताराभिरभिसंवृतः ॥
Surrounded by them, the lord of Rākshasas shone like the splendid moon surrounded by stars.
5.9.43
याश्च्यवन्तेऽम्बरात्ताराः
पुण्यशेषसमावृताः ।
इमास्तास्सङ्गताः कृत्स्ना
इति मेने हरिस्तदा ॥
"All the stars, that have fallen from the heavens before their Puṇya was completely exhausted, must have gathered here" thought the Vānara.
5.9.44
ताराणामिव सुव्यक्तम्
महतीनां शुभार्चिषाम् ।
प्रभावर्णप्रसादाश्च
विरेजुस्तत्र योषिताम् ॥
The radiance, color and grace of those women shone like those of the most bright and splendid stars.
5.9.45
व्यावृत्तगुरुपीनस्रक्
प्रकीर्णवरभूषणाः ।
पानव्यायामकालेषु
निद्रापहृतचेतसः ॥
With their big and plump garlands and exquisite ornaments strewn hither and thither while they played love sports and became drunk, the women were now robbed of their senses by sleep.
5.9.46
व्यावृत्ततिलकाः काश्चित्
काश्चिदुद्भ्रान्तनूपुरा: ।
पार्श्वे गलितहाराश्च
काश्चित्परमयोषितः ॥
Some of those lovely women had their Tilaka smudged. Some had their anklets displaced. Some had their necklaces hanging to one side.
5.9.47
मुक्ताहाराऽवृताश्चान्याः
काश्चिद्विस्रस्तवाससः ।
व्याविद्धरशनादामाः
किशोर्य इव वाहिताः ॥
Some are wrapped in pearl necklaces. Some had their clothes slipping off. Some had their waist strings tangled. They lay like draught fillies let loose to relax on the ground.
5.9.48
सुकुण्डलधराश्चान्या
विच्छिन्नमृदितस्रजः ।
गजेन्द्रमृदिताः फुल्ला
लता इव महावने ॥
Others wearing splendid ear-pendants, with their garlands torn and crushed, looked like forest creepers whose blooms were trampled upon by the lord of tuskers.
5.9.49
चन्द्रांशुकिरणाभाश्च
हाराः कासांचिदुत्कटाः ।
हंसा इव बभुस्सुप्ताः
स्तनमध्येषु योषिताम् ॥
The necklaces worn by some lay between their breasts, glowing with the sheen of moonlight, shining like sleeping swans.
5.9.50
अपरासां च वैडूर्याः
कादम्बा इव पक्षिणः ।
हेमसूत्राणि चान्यासाम्
चक्रवाका इवाभवन् ॥
The lazulite necklaces of some looked like Kādamba birds and the gold girdles of others looked like Cakravāka birds.
5.9.51
हंसकारण्डवाकीर्णाः
चक्रवाकोपशोभिताः ।
आपगा इव ता रेजुः
जघनैः पुलिनैरिव ॥
With their wide hips for sand flats, (and their ornaments for) the swarms of Haṃsa, Kāraṇḍa and Cakravāka birds, they shone like rivers.
5.9.52
किङ्किणीजालसङ्कोशाः
ता हैमविपुलाम्बुजाः ।
भावग्राहा यशस्तीराः
सुप्ता नद्य इवाऽऽबभुः ॥
Their anklet-bells for unopened buds and their gold jewelry for the blown lotuses, their fame for the banks and their passion for the crocodiles, they shone like sleeping rivers.
5.9.53
मृदुष्वङ्गेषु कासाञ्चित्
कुचाग्रेषु च संस्थिताः ।
बभुर्वर्भूषणानीव
शुभा भूषणराजयः ॥
The lovely imprints left on the soft limbs and nipples of some, by the ornaments they had worn, looked like ornaments themselves.
5.9.54
अंशुकान्ताश्च कासाञ्चित्
मुखमारुतकम्पिताः ।
उपर्युपरि वक्त्राणाम्
व्याधूयन्ते पुनः पुनः ॥
The frills of the garments of some, gently blown by their breathing, fluttered again and again over their faces.
5.9.55
ताः पताका इवोद्धूताः
पत्नीनां रुचिरप्रभाः ।
नानावर्णसुवर्णानाम्
वक्त्रमूलेषु रेजिरे ॥
Those frills of the garments of the wives (of Rāvaṇa), shining in many lovely colors, fluttered beautifully from the the base of their faces, like flags.
5.9.56
ववल्गुश्चात्र कासांचित्
कुण्डलानि शुभार्चिषाम् ।
मुखमारुतसंसर्गात्
मन्दं मन्दं सुयोषिताम् ॥
Even the shining and lovely ear pendants of some of those lovely women fluttered gently under their breath.
5.9.57
शर्कराऽसवगन्धैश्च
प्रकृत्या सुरभिः सुखः ।
तासां वदननिश्वासः
सिषेवे रावणं तदा ॥
The breath from their faces, which was innately pleasant and fragrant, carried the smell of sweet wine with it to Rāvaṇa.
5.9.58
रावणाननशङ्काश्च
काश्चिद्रावणयोषितः ।
मुखानि स्म सपत्नीनाम्
उपाजिघ्रन्पुनः पुनः ॥
(Being drunk and sleepy), some of Rāvaṇa’s women kissed the faces of their co-wives again and again, taking them to be the face of Rāvaṇa.
5.9.59
अत्यर्थं सक्तमनसो
रावणे ता वरस्त्रियः ।
अस्वतन्त्राः सपत्नीनाम्
प्रियमेवाऽऽचरंस्तदा ॥
The beautiful women who had completely lost control over themselves caught in the pangs of love for Rāvaṇa, only pleased their cowives (in doing so).
5.9.60
बाहूनुपनिधायान्याः
पारिहार्यविभूषितान् ।
अंशुकानि च रम्याणि
प्रमदास्तत्र शिश्यिरे ॥
The lovely women lay rested using their arms loaded with bangles and beautiful garments as pillows.
5.9.61
अन्या वक्षसि चान्यस्याः
तस्याः काचित्पुनर्भुजम् ।
अपरा त्वङ्कमन्यस्याः
तस्याश्चाप्यपरा भुजौ ॥
One woman rested on the breasts of another one. That woman rested on the shoulder of another. And that woman rested on the thigh of another one, while the other one rested on the breasts of another.
5.9.62
ऊरुपार्श्वकटीपृष्ठम्
अन्योन्यस्य समाश्रिताः ।
परस्परनिविष्टाङ्ग्यो
मदस्नेहवशानुगाः ॥
With love and lust consuming them, they threw their limbs on each other, and lay with their limbs, thighs, sides, hips and bottoms jostling.
5.9.63
अन्योन्यभुजसूत्रेण
स्त्रीमाला ग्रथिता हि सा ।
मालेव ग्रथिता सूत्रे
शुशुभे मत्तषट्पदा ॥
Arms linked together, they weaved a garland of women, that shone like a row of amorous bees (hovering) on a garland of flowers.
5.9.64
लतानां माधवे मासि
फुल्लानां वायुसेवनात् ।
अन्योन्य मालाग्रथितम्
संसक्तकुसुमोच्चयम् ।
व्यतिवेष्टितसुस्कन्धम्
अन्योन्यभ्रमराकुलम् ।
आसीद्वनमिवोद्धूतम्
स्त्रीवनं रावणस्य तत् ॥
That Vana of Rāvaṇa’s women, as they lay with their beautiful arms interlocked and their hair twined with each other’s, looked like a Vana of creepers that bloomed in full by the touch of the month of spring, with their masses of flowers pressed against each other and enmeshed into each other into a garland.
5.9.66
उचितेष्वपि सुव्यक्तम्
न तासां योषितां तदा ।
विवेकः शक्य आधातुम्
भूषणाङ्गाम्बरस्रजाम् ॥
Then, with those women in that state, even those who knew them well could not tell apart one’s ornament, garment, garland or limb from the other’s.
5.9.67
रावणे सुखसंविष्टे
तास्त्रियो विविधप्रभाः ।
ज्वलन्तः काञ्चना दीपाः
प्रैक्षन्तानिमिषा इव ॥
With Rāvaṇa sound asleep, the blazing golden lamps seemed to (have taken the opportunity to fearlessly) stare at those women, each of whom was splendid in her own way.
5.9.68
राजर्षिपितृदैत्यानाम्
गन्धर्वाणां च योषितः ।
राक्षसानां च याः कन्याः
तस्य कामवशं गताः ॥
All those women, who came from the families of royal sages, Daityas, Gandharvas and Rākshasas, had given themselves to him out of infatuation for him.
These Ṡlōkas, from 68 to 71, which are confusing and contradicting, do not seem to be authentic. Some translations do not carry them.
5.9.69
युद्धकामेन ताः सर्वा
रावणेन हृताः स्त्रियः ।
समदा मदनेनैव
मोहिताः काश्चिदागताः ॥
Rāvaṇa, ever interested in a fight, carried them all off by force. A few among those, the lustful, however, came to him, consumed by infatuation.
5.9.70
न तत्र काचित्प्रमदा प्रसह्य
वीर्योपपन्नेन गुणेन लब्धा ।
न चान्यकामापि न चान्यपूर्वा
विना वरार्हां जनकात्मजां ताम् ॥
There was no woman who was taken by force by the valiant one of many virtues. None, except the lovely daughter of Janaka, was in love with anyone else or had ever been.
5.9.71
न चाकुलीना न च हीनरूपा
नादक्षिणा नानुपचारयुक्ता ।
भार्याभवत्तस्य न हीनसत्त्वा
न चापि कान्तस्य न कामनीया ॥
And no one had become his wife, who was not high-born or lacked beauty, who was not capable or was not well treated, who was not energetic or was undesirable to a lover.
5.9.72
बभूव बुद्धिस्तु हरीश्वरस्य
यदीदृशी राघवधर्मपत्नी ।
इमा यथा राक्षसराजभार्याः
सुजातमस्येति हि साधुबुद्धेः ॥
Then that chief of Vānaras, who would think no mean thought, told himself, ‘How nice it would have been for the Rākshasa King, if Rāghava’s lawfully wedded wife also was like these wives!’
5.9.73
पुनश्च सोऽचिन्तयादार्तरूपो
ध्रुवं विशिष्टा गुणतो हि सीता ।
अथायमस्यां कृतवान्महात्मा
लङ्केश्वरः कष्टमनार्यकर्म ॥
Then he felt very sad, thinking, ‘Seetā is certainly a woman of extraordinary character. What the mighty lord of Laṅkā has done to her is ignoble! What a pity!’
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे नवमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
