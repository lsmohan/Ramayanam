Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 8
In this Sarga, Sugreeva expresses his joy in gaining the friendship of Rāma, and how much it means to him. Sugreeva reflects on how each one would stand for the other in a friendship. Rāma echoes the same sentiment about friendship.
Sugreeva then tells Rāma, though hesitatingly, how he was humiliated by his brother, his wife was taken away from him, and how he was forced out of his own kingdom, and how he was managing to live with the help of a few trusted aides.
Sugreeva also tells Rāma that his brother Vāli had sent Vānaras many a time to destroy him, but he was able to kill them all. He also tells that he suspected Rāma also to be one sent by Vāli and that was the reason for his not approaching Rāma readily.
Rāma tells Sugreeva that the humiliation faced by Sugreeva moved him deeply, and assures that he will kill Vāli with his sharp arrows. Rāma asks Sugreeva about the background of their enmity in detail and Sugreeva begins to tell what had happened.
4.8.1
परितुष्टस्तु सुग्रीवः
तेन वाक्येन वानरः ।
लक्ष्मणस्याग्रतो रामम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Feeling very happy at his words, Sugreeva, the Vānara said these words to Rāma in the presence of Lakshmaṇa.
4.8.2
सर्वथाऽहमनुग्राह्यो
देवतानां न संशयः ।
उपपन्नो गुणोपेतः
सखा यस्य भवान्मम ॥
Without a doubt, Dēvas have endowed me with their grace in every possible way, with you of great qualities becoming my friend.
4.8.3
शक्यं खलु भवेद्राम
सहायेन त्वयाऽनघ ।
सुरराज्यमपि प्राप्तुम्
स्वराज्यं किं पुनः प्रभो ॥
O Rāma, O sinless one! It is possible, with your help, to obtain even the kingdom of Dēvas, what to say of recovering one’s own?
4.8.4
सोऽहं सभाज्यो बन्धूनाम्
सुहृदां चैव राघव ।
यस्याग्निसाक्षिकं मित्रम्
लब्धं राघववंशजम् ॥
I would gain the respect of my relations and friends, having obtained someone born in the lineage of Raghu for a friend, witnessed by lord Agni.
4.8.5
अहमप्यनुरूपस्ते
वयस्यो ज्ञास्यसे शनैः ।
न तु वक्तुं समर्थोऽहम्
स्वयमात्मगतान्गुणान् ॥
You would also know, over time, I am a friend worthy of you; I shall not sing my own praises.
4.8.6
महात्मनां तु भूयिष्ठम्
त्वद्विधानां कृतात्मनाम् ।
निश्चला भवति प्रीतिः
धैर्यमात्मवतामिव ॥
Friendship of Mahātmas like you, who have won self-control, is as steady as the courage of the man of steadfast will.
4.8.7
रजतं वा सुवर्णं वा
वस्त्राण्याभरणानि च ।
अविभक्तानि साधूनाम्
अवगच्छन्ति साधवः ॥
Good people intrinsically understand that there is no such distinction among them as ‘yours and mine’, be it in relation to silver or gold or ornaments or clothing.
4.8.8
आढ्यो वापि दरिद्रो वा
दुःखितस्सुखितोऽपि वा ।
निर्दोषो वा सदोषो वा
वयस्यः परमा गतिः ॥
A friend is one’s best resort (at any time), be he wallowing in riches or be he penniless, be he enjoying good times or bad times, be he perfect or imperfect.
4.8.9
धनत्यागस्सुखत्यागो
देशत्यागोऽपि वानघ ।
वयस्यार्थे प्रवर्तन्ते
स्नेहं दृष्ट्वा तथाविधम् ॥
O sinless one! Those who realize the nature of true friendship do not hesitate to give up wealth, happiness, or country for the sake of a friend.
4.8.10
तत्तथेत्यब्रवीद्रामः
सुग्रीवं प्रियवादिनम् ।
लक्ष्मणस्याग्रतो लक्ष्म्या
वासवस्येव धीमतः ॥
‘That is certainly so!’, agreed Rāma, answering Sugreeva who spoke so pleasingly, in the presence of the sagacious Lakshmaṇa who was as resplendent as Indra.
4.8.11
ततो रामं स्थितं दृष्ट्वा
लक्ष्मणं च महाबलम् ।
सुग्रीवस्सर्वतश्चक्षुः
वने लोलमपातयत् ॥
Seeing that Rāma and the mighty strong Lakshmaṇa were both standing, Sugreeva looked around the Vana in all directions with his roving eye.
4.8.12
स ददर्श ततस्सालम्
अविदूरे हरीश्वरः ।
सुपुष्पमीषत्पत्राढ्यम्
भ्रमरैरुपशोभितम् ॥
Then the Lord of Vānaras saw a Sāla tree, not far from there, that is full of flowers and a few leaves and attractive with bees that haunted it.
4.8.13
तस्यैकां पर्णबहुलाम्
भङ्क्त्वा शाखां सुपुष्पिताम् ।
सालस्यास्तीर्य सुग्रीवो
निषसाद सराघवः ॥
Breaking off one of its branches laden with flowers and as many leaves, Sugreeva spread it on the ground and sat on it along with Rāghava.
4.8.14
तावासीनौ ततो दृष्ट्वा
हनूमानपि लक्ष्मणम् ।
सालशाखां समुत्पाट्य
विनीतमुपवेशयत् ॥
Seeing them seated, Hanumān broke off another branch of the Sāla tree and offered it for a seat to Lakshmaṇa, the modest one.
4.8.15
सुखोपविष्टं रामं तु
प्रसन्नमुदधिं यथा ।
फलपुष्पसमाकीर्णे
तस्मिन् गिरिवरोत्तमे ।
ततः प्रहृष्टस्सुग्रीवः
श्लक्ष्णं मधुरया गिरा ।
उवाच प्रणयाद्रामम्
हर्षव्याकुलिताक्षरम् ॥
When Rāma, who was serene like the calm sea, had settled down comfortably in his seat on that best of mountains that abounded in fruit and flower, Sugreeva, who was very happy, spoke to him out of affection these words in a sweet and gentle tone, punctuated only by bouts of joy.
4.8.17
अहं विनिकृतो भ्रात्रा
चराम्येष भयार्दितः ।
ऋष्यमूकं गिरिवरम्
हृतभार्यस्सुदुःखितः ॥
Humiliated by my brother, wife abducted, bitterly grieving, I wander, haunted by fear, on this Ṛshyamūka, a great mountain.
4.8.18
सोऽहं त्रस्तो भये मग्नो
वसाम्युद्भ्रान्तचेतनः ।
वालिना निकृतो भ्रात्रा
कृतवैरश्च राघव ॥
Scared, deeply in fear, humiliated by my brother Vāli, my implacable enemy, I live with my mind completely disoriented.
4.8.19
वालिनो मे भयार्तस्य
सर्वलोकाभयङ्कर ।
ममापि त्वमनाथस्य
प्रसादं कर्तुमर्हसि ॥
You, who can redeem all the worlds from fear, should bestow your grace upon me, wallowing in the fear of Vāli as I am with no other recourse whatsoever.
4.8.20
एवमुक्तस्तु तेजस्वी
धर्मज्ञो धर्मवत्सलः ।
प्रत्युवाच स काकुत्स्थः
सुग्रीवं प्रहसन्निव ॥
Thus besought, the powerful Kākutstha, who also knew Dharma and loved it, replied to Sugreeva with a smile:
4.8.21
उपकारफलं मित्रम्
अपकारोऽरिलक्षणम् ।
अद्यैव तं वधिष्यामि
तव भार्यापहारिणम् ॥
It is in the nature of a friend to be of help and in the nature of an enemy to cause damage. This very day I shall kill the one who has robbed your wife.
4.8.22
इमे हि मे महावेगाः
पत्रिणस्तिग्मतेजसः ।
कार्तिकेयवनोद्भूताः
शरा हेमविभूषिताः ।
कङ्कपत्रपरिच्छन्ना
महेन्द्राशनिसन्निभाः ।
सुपर्वाणस्सुतीक्ष्णाग्राः
सरोषा भुजगा इव ॥
These extremely sharp pointed and feathered shafts of mine, decked with gold, Parvas smoothened at the joints, made of reeds from the Vana where Kārtikēya was born, exceedingly swift and fierce, sped by the feathers of Kaṅka bird, comparable to the thunderbolt of the great Indra his like serpents.
4.8.24
वालिसंज्ञममित्रं ते
भ्रातरं कृतकिल्बिषम् ।
शरैर्विनिहतं पश्य
विकीर्णमिव पर्वतम् ॥
You shall see your enemy and brother by the name of Vāli who has committed a heinous offense, fall like a mountain, slain by these arrows.
4.8.25
राघवस्य वचश्श्रुत्वा
सुग्रीवो वाहिनीपतिः ।
प्रहर्षमतुलं लेभे
साधु साध्विति चाब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those words of Rāghava, Sugreeva, the leader of the (Vānara) army, was extremely delighted, and said ‘Great! Excellent!’
4.8.26
राम शोकाभिभूतोऽहम्
शोकार्तानां भवान्गतिः ।
वयस्य इति कृत्वा हि
त्वय्यहं परिदेवये ॥
I am overwhelmed with grief, O Rāma, and you are the refuge of the grief-stricken. It is because I look upon you as my friend that I have come to you with my tale of woe.
4.8.27
त्वं हि पाणिप्रदानेन
वयस्यो मेऽग्निसाक्षिकम् ।
कृतः प्राणैर्बहुमतः
सत्येनापि शपामि ते ॥
You have, by giving me your hand in friendship with the fire for witness, become my bosom friend whom I value more than I do my own life; I say this swearing on truth.
4.8.28
वयस्य इति कृत्वा च
विस्रब्धः प्रवदाम्यहम् ।
दुःखमन्तर्गतं यन्मे
मनो हरति नित्यशः ॥
And because you are my friend, I confide in you and tell about the grief that is eating out my heart from deep inside.
4.8.29
एतावदुक्त्वा वचनम्
बाष्पदूषितलोचनः ।
बाष्पोपहतया वाचा
नोच्चैश्शक्नोति भाषितुम् ॥
After saying all that he could, his eyes blurred with tears, he could not utter any more words aloud, because his voice was choked with tears.
4.8.30
बाष्पवेगं तु सहसा
नदीवेगमिवागतम् ।
धारयामास धैर्येण
सुग्रीवो रामसन्निधौ ॥
But summoning courage to his aid, Sugreeva managed to stem the torrent of tears that deluged him like a river in spate, in the presence of Rāma.
4.8.31
सन्निगृह्य तु तं बाष्पम्
प्रमृज्य नयने शुभे ।
विनिश्श्वस्य च तेजस्वी
राघवं वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Restraining his tears and wiping his fine eyes, he of great strength, heaved a deep sigh and said these words to Rāghava:
4.8.32
पुराऽहं वालिना राम
राज्यात्स्वादवरोपितः ।
परुषाणि च संश्राव्य
निर्धूतोऽस्मि बलीयसा ॥
I have been dethroned by Vāli from my own kingdom and was humiliated in terrible words by him, the stronger (of the two of us).
4.8.33
हृता भार्या च मे तेन
प्राणेभ्योऽपि गरीयसी ।
सुहृदश्च मदीया ये
संयता बन्धनेषु ते ॥
My wife, who is more than life to me, was robbed, and whoever else was friendly to me were put in shackles by him.
4.8.34
यत्नवांश्च सुदुष्टात्मा
मद्विनाशाय राघव ।
बहुशस्तत्प्रयुक्ताश्च
वानरा निहता मया ॥
O Rāghava! That exceedingly wicked one tried his best to destroy me; many Vānaras sent by him (to destroy me) were killed successfully by me.
4.8.35
शङ्कया त्वेतया चेह
दृष्ट्वा त्वामपि राघव ।
नोपसर्पाम्यहं भीतो
भये सर्वे हि बिभ्यति ॥
O Rāghava! It was because of this suspicion that I was scared of, and did not approach even you; everyone would be afraid when there is cause for fear, would they not?
4.8.36
केवलं हि सहाया मे
हनुमत्प्रमुखास्त्विमे ।
अतोऽहं धारयाम्यद्य
प्राणान् कृच्छ्रगतोऽपि सन् ॥
Only Hanumān and these others are my aids; it is only because of them I manage to survive, though in dire straits.
4.8.37
एते हि कपयस्स्निग्धा
मां रक्षन्ति समन्ततः ।
सह गच्छन्ति गन्तव्ये
नित्यं तिष्ठन्ति च स्थिते ॥
These Vānaras, my loving friends, protect me on all sides; they go with me where I must go, and stay with me where I stay, always.
4.8.38
सङ्क्षेपस्त्वेष मे राम
किमुक्त्वा विस्तरं हि ते ।
स मे ज्येष्ठो रिपुर्भ्राता
वाली विश्रुतपौरुषः ॥
O Rāma! This, in brief, is my story, and there is no point in elaborating further. He, Vāli, my elder brother, known widely for his prowess, is now my sworn enemy.
4.8.39
तद्विनाशेऽपि मे दुःखम्
प्रणष्टं स्यादनन्तरम् ।
सुखं मे जीवितं चैव
तद्विनाशनिबन्धनम् ॥
My hardships will vanish only after he is destroyed. My life and my happiness are tied to his destruction.
4.8.40
एष मे राम शोकान्तः
शोकार्तेन निवेदितः ।
दुःखितस्सुखितो वापि
सख्युर्नित्यं सखा गतिः ॥
I have put forth plainly how my grief could end, in my despondency caused by that very grief. A friend is the only recourse for a friend, in adverse as well as in favorable times.
4.8.41
श्रुत्वैतद्वचनं रामः
सुग्रीवमिदमब्रवीत् ।
किं निमित्तमभूद्वैरम्
श्रोतुमिच्छामि तत्त्वतः ॥
Hearing those words, Rāma said these words to Sugreeva: ‘How did this enmity arise? I would like to know in detail’.
4.8.42
सुखं हि कारणं श्रुत्वा
वैरस्य तव वानर ।
आनन्तर्याद्विधास्यामि
सम्प्रधार्य बलाबलम् ॥
O Vānara! After listening to the reason for your enmity, and gauging the strengths and weaknesses, I can easily and immediately do what is to be done.
4.8.43
बलवान् हि ममामर्षः
श्रुत्वा त्वामवमानितम् ।
वर्तते हृदयोत्कम्पी
प्रावृड्वेग इवाम्भसः ॥
Hearing the story of your humiliation, my very being shakes with extreme rage that rushes like water in rainy season.
4.8.44
हृष्टः कथय विस्रब्धो
यावदारोप्यते धनुः ।
सृष्टश्च हि मया बाणो
निरस्तश्च रिपुस्तव ॥
Lighten up and tell me, taking me into full confidence. In no time, the arrow shot from my stringed bow will see the extinction of your foe.
4.8.45
एवमुक्तस्तु सुग्रीवः
काकुत्स्थेन महात्मना ।
प्रहर्षमतुलं लेभे
चतुर्भिस्सह वानरैः ॥
Thus assured by Mahātma Kākutstha, Sugreeva, along with the other four Vānaras, was overjoyed.
4.8.46
ततः प्रहृष्टवदनः
सुग्रीवो लक्ष्मणाग्रजे ।
वैरस्य कारणं तत्त्वम्
आख्यातुमुपचक्रमे ॥
And Sugreeva, with happiness evident on his face, began narrating the cause of the enmity in detail to that elder brother of Lakshmaṇa.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे अष्टमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
