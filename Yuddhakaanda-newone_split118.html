Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 118
In this Sarga, Agni, taking the form of a person, rises from the pyre with Seetā seated on his lap, her person, clothing and ornaments intact. Agni, the witness of all the worlds then vouches for her purity and her abduction against her will and for her being kept in confinement in Rāvaṇa's inner apartments, heavily guarded. He then commands Rāma to take her back.
Then Rāma says to Agni that he knew all along that Seetā is completely devoted to him and only him without a doubt, but she needed to undergo this purification to lead a normal life again with her head held high in this world. He also adds that if he takes her back without her purity verified, the world would take him to be an imbecile consumed by lust for her.
He tells all the Dēvas that he would follow the advice given by them and gets reunited happily with Seetā.
6.118.1
एतच्छ्रुत्वा शुभं वाक्यम्
पितामहसमीरितम् ।
अङ्केनादाय वैदेहीम्
उत्पपात विभावसुः ॥
Having heard those auspicious words spoken by the Grandsire, Agni, the God of Fire rose up, with Vaidēhee seated on his lap.
6.118.2
स विधूय चितां तां तु
वैदेहीं हव्यवाहनः ।
उत्तस्थौ मूर्तिमानाशु
गृहीत्वा जनकात्मजाम् ॥
Agni, the courier of oblations, taking the form of a person, and carrying the daughter of Janaka with him, rose up, pushing the pyre aside.
6.118.3
तरुणादित्यसङ्काशाम्
तप्तकाञ्चनभूषणाम् ।
रक्ताम्बरधरां बालाम्
नीलकुञ्चितमूर्धजाम् ।
अक्लिष्टमाल्याभरणाम्
तथारूपामनिन्दिताम् ।
ददौ रामाय वैदेहीम्
अङ्के कृत्वा विभावसुः ॥
Agni then passed on the blameless Vaidēhee who was youthful with curly black tresses and glowing like the morning sun clad in red garments and decked with ornaments made of gold purified in fire, who looked just as she was before, with even her garlands and ornaments intact, from his lap to Rāma.
6.118.5
अब्रवीच्च तदा रामम्
साक्षी लोकस्य पावकः ॥
Then Agni, a witness to the entire world, said to Rāma:
6.118.5
एषा ते राम वैदेही
पापमस्यां न विद्यते ।
नैव वाचा न मनसा
नैव बुद्ध्या न चक्षुषा ।
सुवृत्ता वृत्तशौण्डीर
न त्वामतिचचार ह ॥
Here, O Rāma who is fastidious about character, is Vaidēhee of immaculate character, who knows no sin. Neither in her word, nor in heart, nor in mind, nor through her eyes was she guilty of transgression against you.
6.118.7
रावणेनापनीतैषा
वीर्योत्सिक्तेन रक्षसा ।
त्वया विरहिता दीना
विवशा निर्जनाद्वनात् ॥
Rāvaṇa, the Rākshasa who was swollen with the pride of his valor, carried her off, the distressed one who had no protection without you around, from the forest where no other single soul was present.
Why is Agni saying this? Why is he repeating the well-known fact that Seetā was abducted by Rāvaṇa?
Well, what exactly happened on the day when she was abducted is known only to Seetā, and to Rāvaṇa and Jaṭāyu, both of whom are no more.
So Agni, a witness to the entire world, is clearing any kind of suspicion in anyone's mind.
He is vouching for the fact that she was truly abducted, putting to rest any possible suspicions that she might have herself solicited Rāvaṇa, or that she might have fallen for him when he made advances, or offered enticements, like Ahalya did with Indra.
6.118.8
रुद्धा चान्तःपुरे गुप्ता
त्वच्चित्ता त्वत्परायणा ।
रक्षिता राक्षसीभिश्च
घोराभिर्घोरबुद्धिभिः ॥
She, whose heart was lodged in you and who saw only you as her destination, was kept confined in the inner quarters guarded by frightful Rākshasis, who were extremely cruel by nature.
6.118.9
प्रलोभ्यमाना विविधम्
भर्त्स्यमाना च मैथिली ।
नाचिन्तयत तद्रक्षः
त्वद्गतेनान्तरात्मना ॥
Maithili was threatened and was wooed with every kind of enticement; but she, her heart cleaved to you, did not cave in to that Rākshasa's will.
6.118.10
विशुद्धभावां निष्पापाम्
पतिगृह्णीष्व राघव ।
न किञ्चिदभिधातव्या
अहमाज्ञापयामि ते ॥
Take back this pure-hearted sinless one who is beyond any further reproach; I command you to do so.
6.118.11
ततः प्रीतमना रामः
श्रुत्वैतद्वदतां वरः ।
दध्यौ मुहूर्तं धर्मात्मा
बाष्पव्याकुललोचनः ॥
On hearing those words, Rāma's heart was filled with joy. The Dharmātma, the foremost of the articulate souls, thought for a while, his eyes becoming turbid with tears.
This Ṡlōka, which is near identical to the first Ṡlōka of the previous Sarga seems to be an unauthentic insertion that happened in the course of time.
Rāma thinking 'for a while' at this juncture is not appropriate. The flow of narration is much smoother without this Ṡlōka here.
6.118.12
एवमुक्तो महातेजा
द्युतिमान् दृढविक्रमः ।
अब्रवीत् त्रिदशश्रेष्ठम्
रामो धर्मभृतां वरः ॥
Thus told, Rāma of extraordinary power, great splendor and indefatigable prowess, the foremost of the patrons of Dharma replied to that eminent Dēva:
6.118.13
अवश्यं चापि लोकेषु
सीता पावनमर्हति ।
दीर्घकालोषिता हीयम्
रावणान्तःपुरे शुभा ॥
Seetā, the good lady, needed this purification (to lead a normal life again with her head held high) in this world, having lived in the inner-quarters of Rāvaṇa for long.
6.118.14
बालिशः बत कामात्मा
रामो दशरथात्मजः ।
इति वक्ष्यन्ति मां सन्तो
जानकीमविशोध्य हि ॥
The good men of the world would jeer at me saying, 'Alas, what an imbecile Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, is, to be consumed by his lust (for her)!' if Jānaki is not subjected to thorough scrutiny (before I take her back).
6.118.15
अनन्यहृदयां भक्ताम्
मच्चित्तपरिवर्तिनीम् ।
अहमप्यवगच्छामि
मैथिलीं जनकात्मजाम् ॥
I do know fully well that Maithili, the daughter of Janaka, who dwells in my heart, is devoted to me and has no place in her heart for anyone else.
6.118.16
इमामपि विशालाक्षीम्
रक्षितां स्वेन तेजसा ।
रावणो नातिवर्तेत
वेलामिव महोदधिः ॥
Rāvaṇa could have no more transgressed against her, the wide eyed one who had her own radiance for a guard, than an ocean can, its shore.
6.118.17
प्रत्ययार्थं तु लोकानाम्
त्रयाणां सत्यसंश्रयः ।
उपेक्षे चापि वैदेहीम्
प्रविशन्तीं हुताशनम् ॥
For the sake of helping the three worlds gain the same trust (in her), I, fastidious about truth, pretended indifference when Vaidēhee entered the fire.
6.118.18
न हि शक्तः स दुष्टात्मा
मनसाऽपि हि मैथिलीम् ।
प्रधर्षयितुमप्राप्याम्
दीप्तामग्निशिखामिव ॥
That evil-minded one could have, in no way, transgressed, even in his thought, against Maithili, who is as unapproachable as a blazing tongue of flame.
6.118.19
नेयमर्हति चैश्वर्यम्
रावणान्तःपुरे शुभा ।
अनन्या हि मया सीता
भास्करेण प्रभा यथा ॥
She, the good lady, could not care less about the opulence of Rāvaṇa's inner quarters. Seetā is mine, and no one else's, just as the sunlight is the sun's.
6.118.20
विशुद्धा त्रिषु लोकेषु
मैथिली जनकात्मजा ।
न विहातुं मया शक्या
कीर्तिरात्मवता यथा ॥
Maithili, the daughter of Janaka, is the purest of all, in all the three worlds. I can no more give her up than a man of self-esteem can give up his reputation.
6.118.21
अवश्यं तु मया कार्यम्
सर्वेषां वो वचः शुभम् ।
स्निग्धानां लोकमान्यानाम्
एवं च वदतां हितम् ॥
I shall, by all means, follow the good advice that all of you, who enjoy the homage of all the worlds, have given me with love, keeping my welfare in mind.
6.118.22
इत्येवमुक्त्वा विजयी महाबलः
प्रशस्यमानः स्वकृतेन कर्मणा ।
समेत्य रामः प्रियया महायशाः
सुखं सुखार्होऽनुबभूव राघवः ॥
Having said these words, Rāma, the scion of Raghus, of great renown, the victor of extraordinary strength, who received praises for his exploits, and who deserved all the happiness in the world, was happy, reunited with his beloved.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे अष्टादशोत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and eighteenth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
