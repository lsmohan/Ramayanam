Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 45
In this Sarga, Seetā, upon hearing the cry of Māreeca in Rāma’s voice, becomes agitated and feels restless and asks Lakshmaṇa to rush to find Rāma. But Lakshmaṇa does not heed her words and tells her that it is not Rāma’s voice, but a trick of the Rākshasa. He tries to assure her that not even all the strong men of the world combined can prevail over the strength of Rāma.
Seetā, in her concern and worry over Rāma’s safety, gets angry with Lakshmaṇa for not rushing immediately to Rāma’s rescue. She speaks harshly to him, saying that he is an enemy in disguise. She even accuses him of coveting her, either on his own behalf or on behalf of Bharata. She does not stop crying in spite his trying to console her. She continues her sharp attacks on him. She swears that she would give up her life if there is no Rāma for her and that she would not touch another man even with her foot.
At the end, finding no other option, Lakshmaṇa pays obeisance to her and leaves to look for Rāma, in spite of seeing bad omens.
3.45.1
आर्तस्वरं तु तं भर्तुः
विज्ञाय सदृशं वने ।
उवाच लक्ष्मणं सीता
गच्छ जानीहि राघवम् ॥
Recognizing that cry of distress to resemble the voice of her husband, Seetā told Lakshmaṇa: "Please go and find (out what happened to) Rāghava!"
3.45.2
न हि मे हृदयं स्थाने
जीवितं वाऽवतिष्ठति ।
क्रोशतः परमार्तस्य
श्रुतश्शब्दो मया भृशम् ।
आक्रन्दमानं तु वने
भ्रातरं त्रातुमर्हसि ॥
I heard Rāma’s voice, crying in extreme distress. My heart and my life are restless. You ought to go and save your brother, who is crying.
3.45.3
तं क्षिप्रमभिधाव त्वम्
भ्रातरं शरणैषिणम् ।
रक्षसां वशमापन्नम्
सिंहानामिव गोवृषम् ॥
Go, rush immediately to your brother who is crying for help caught by the Rākshasas, like a cow by lions.
3.45.4
न जगाम तथोक्तस्तु
भ्रातुराज्ञाय शासनम् ।
तमुवाच ततस्तत्र
कुपिता जनकात्मजा ॥
But he did not go, keeping his brother’s orders in mind. Then Janaka’s daughter became angry and said to him:
3.45.5
सौमित्रे मित्ररूपेण
भ्रातुस्त्वमसि शत्रुवत् ।
यस्त्वमस्यामवस्थायाम्
भ्रातरं नाभिपत्स्यसे ॥
O Sowmitri! You do not respond to your brother in a plight like this! You are your brother’s enemy, disguised as a friend.
3.45.6
इच्छसि त्वं विनश्यन्तम्
रामं लक्ष्मण मत्कृते ।
लोभात्त्वं मत्कृते नूनम्
नानुगच्छसि राघवम् ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! You want to see Rāma perish, because you want me. You indeed covet me and hence are not following Rāghava!
3.45.7
व्यसनं ते प्रियं मन्ये
स्नेहो भ्रातरि नास्तिते ।
तेन तिष्ठसि विस्रब्दः
तमपश्यन्महाद्युतिम् ॥
It seems his trouble is your pleasure. You have no love for your brother. That is why you remain here unmoved even when the supremely radiant one is not seen.
3.45.8
किं हि संशयमापन्ने
तस्मिन्निह मया भवेत् ।
कर्तव्यमिह तिष्ठन्त्या
यत्प्रधानस्त्वमागतः ॥
When his life, for whose sake you came here mainly, is in question, what purpose does it serve if you sit with me here?
3.45.9
इति ब्रुवाणां वैदेहीम्
बाष्पशोकपरिप्लुताम् ।
अब्रवील्लक्ष्मणस्त्रस्ताम्
सीतां मृगवधूमिव ॥
To that Seetā, princess of Vidēha kingdom, who spoke like that, drenched in tears and frightened like a doe, Lakshmaṇa said:
3.45.10
पन्नगासुरगन्धर्व
देवमानुषराक्षसैः ।
अशक्यस्तव वैदेहि
भर्ता जेतुं न संशयः ॥
Neither Pannagas, nor Asuras, nor Gandharvas, nor Dēvas, nor humans, nor Rākshasas are capable of defeating your husband. There is no doubt about his, Vaidēhee!
3.45.11
देवि देवमनुष्येषु
गन्धर्वेषु पतत्त्रिषु ।
राक्षसेषु पिशाचेषु
किन्नरेषु मृगेषु च ।
दानवेषु च घोरेषु
स न विद्येत शोभने ।
यो रामं प्रतियुध्येत
समरे वासवोपमम् ॥
O lively lady! There is none among Dēvas, humans, Gandharvas, Rākshasas, Piṡācas, Kinnaras or the dreadful Dānavas, or among the birds or beasts, who can stand up to Rāma, the one comparable to Vāsava, in a fight.
3.45.13
अवध्यस्समरे रामो
नैवं त्वं वक्तुमर्हसि ।
न त्वामस्मिन्वने हातुम्
उत्सहे राघवं विना ॥
Rāma cannot be killed in a fight, period. You shall not speak like this. I cannot leave you (alone) in this Vana, without Rāma (here).
3.45.14
अनिवार्यं बलं तस्य
बलैर्बलवतामपि ।
त्रिभिर्लोकैस्समुद्युक्तैः
सेश्वरैरपि सामरैः ॥
All the men of strength and their forces combined, cannot prevail against his strength; nor all the three worlds coming together along with their lords, and the Dēvas.
3.45.15
हृदयं निर्वृतं तेऽस्तु
सन्तापस्त्यज्यतामयम् ।
आगमिष्यति ते भर्ता
शीघ्रं हत्वा मृगोत्तमम् ॥
May your heart be at ease! You shall leave your worry alone. Your husband will come soon, killing that best of the deer.
3.45.16
न च तस्य स्वरो व्यक्तम्
मायया केनचित्कृतः ।
गन्धर्वनगरप्रख्या
माया सा तस्य रक्षसः ॥
That is not the voice of Rāma. It is, clearly, faked by someone. It is the make of that Rākshasa, like a city created by a magician.
3.45.17
 न्यासभूताऽसि वैदेहि
न्यस्ता मयि महात्मना ।
रामेण त्वं वरारोहे
न त्वां त्यक्तुमिहोत्सहे ॥
O Vaidēhee of excellent Kaṭee! You are left under my protection by Rāma, the Mahātma. I cannot bring myself to abandon you here.
3.45.18
कृतवैराश्च वैदेहि
वयमेतैर्निशाचरैः ।
खरस्य निधनादेव
जनस्थानवधं प्रति ॥
We have made the Night-rovers hostile to us by killing Khara and other Rākshasas of Janasthāna.
3.45.19
राक्षसा विविधा वाचो
विसृजन्ति महावने ।
हिंसाविहारा वैदेहि
न चिन्तयितुमर्हसि ॥
Rākshasas, who roam around causing menace, let off all kinds of sounds in this wide Vana. O Vaidēhee! You shall not be worried by them.
3.45.20
लक्ष्मणेनैवमुक्ता सा
क्रुद्धा संरक्तलोचना ।
अब्रवीत्परुषं वाक्यम्
लक्ष्मणं सत्यवादिनम् ॥
Hearing those words of Lakshmaṇa, she became angry and her eyes turned red. She responded harshly to Lakshmaṇa, who was speaking nothing but the truth.
Under extreme circumstances, everyone is liable to misunderstand even those whom they trust most.
3.45.21
अनार्याकरणारम्भ
नृशंस कुलपांसन ।
अहं तव प्रियं मन्ये
रामस्य व्यसनं महत् ॥
O cruel one, you are a disgrace to your race! You are bent upon doing what should not be done! I can only think that you rejoice in Rāma’s tribulation.
3.45.22
रामस्य व्यसनं दृष्ट्वा
तेनैतानि प्रभाषसे ।
नैतच्चित्रं सपत्नेषु
पापं लक्ष्मण यद्भवेत् ।
त्वद्विधेषु नृशंसेषु
नित्यं प्रच्छन्नचारिषु ॥
You see Rāma in trouble; and speak like this? There is no surprise that a cruel enemy like you who moves about in disguise, harbors such evil (thought)!
3.45.24
सुदुष्टस्त्वं वने रामम्
एकमेकोऽनुगच्छसि ।
मम हेतोः प्रतिच्छन्नः
प्रयुक्तोभरतेन वा ॥
You are so evil that you followed Rāma one on one to the Vana, while secretly coveting me, or at the instigation of Bharata.
Seetā, in her concern and worry, goes overboard with her words, to drive Lakshmaṇa to the rescue of Rāma.
3.45.25
तन्न सिध्यति सौमित्रे
तव वा भरतस्य वा ।
कथमिन्दीवरश्यामम्
पद्मपत्रनिभेक्षणम् ।
उपसंश्रित्य भर्तारम्
कामयेयं पृथग्जनम् ॥
That is not going to happen, O Sowmitri, whether it is coming from you or Bharata! How could I, having had the love of the dark-lily-complexioned and lotus-petal-eyed husband wish for other men?
3.45.26
समक्षं तव सौमित्रे
प्राणांस्त्यक्षे न संशयः ।
रामं विना क्षणमपि
न हि जीवामि भूतले ॥
O Sowmitri, I am going to give up my life right in front of you. I would not live on the surface of the earth even for a moment, without Rāma!
3.45.27
इत्युक्तः परुषं वाक्यम्
सीतया रोमहर्षणम् ।
अब्रवील्लक्ष्मणस्सीताम्
प्राञ्जलिर्विजितेन्द्रियः ॥
Hearing those harsh and hair-raising words of Seetā Lakshmaṇa, master of his senses, said to her, palms joined in reverence:
3.45.28
उत्तरं नोत्सहे वक्तुम्
दैवतं भवती मम ।
वाक्यमप्रतिरूपं तु
न चित्रं स्त्रीषु मैथिलि ।
स्वभावस्त्वेष नारीणाम्
एवं लोकेषु दृश्यते ॥
I do not feel like retorting to you, for you are like a goddess to me. O Maithili! Words unparalleled like these are not a surprise from a woman, for such is the nature of women as is seen throughout the world.
3.45.30
विमुक्तधर्माश्चपलाः
तीक्ष्णा भेदकराः स्त्रियः ।
न सहे हीदृशं वाक्यम्
वैदेहि जनकात्मजे ।
श्रोत्रयोरुभयोर्मेद्य
तप्तनाराचसन्निभम् ॥
Women who forgo Dharma are whimsical, sharp-tongued and are the cause of rupture (in relationships). O Vaidēhee, O daughter of Janaka, I cannot bear words like these, which hit my two ears now, like flaming hot Nārāca arrows.
3.45.31
उपशृण्वन्तु मे सर्वे
साक्षिभूता वनेचराः ।
न्यायवादी यथान्यायम्
उक्तोऽहं परुषं त्वया ॥
May all creatures of the Vana bear witness that I, who said what was proper and just, was repaid with such unjust and harsh words.
3.45.32
धिक्त्वामद्य प्रणश्य त्वम्
यन्मामेवं विशङ्कसे ।
स्त्रीत्वाद्दुष्ट स्वभावेन
गुरुवाक्ये व्यवस्थितम् ॥
Dhik, may you perish this very day, for making an allegation like this on me, who is simply following the instructions of the elder brother, being vile, like any other woman!
3.45.33
गमिष्ये यत्र काकुत्स्थ
स्वस्ति तेऽस्तु वरानने ।
रक्षन्तु त्वां विशालाक्षि
समग्रा वनदेवताः ॥
I will go to where Kākutstha is. May all bode well for you, O you of best countenance! May all the Dēvatas of the Vana protect you, O you of wide eyes!
3.45.34
निमित्तानि च घोराणि
यानि प्रादुर्भवन्ति मे ।
अपि त्वां सह रामेण
पश्येयं पुनरागतः ।
न वेत्येतन्न जानामि
वैदेहि जनकात्मजे ॥
O Vaidēhee, O daughter of Janaka! I feel such baleful omens stemming within me, that I do not know whether I will see you together with Rāma upon my return.
3.45.36
लक्ष्मणेनैवमुक्ता सा
रुदन्ती जनकात्मजा ।
प्रत्युवाच ततो वाक्यम्
तीव्रं बाष्पपरिप्लुता ॥
Spoken to thus by Lakshmaṇa, she, the daughter of Janaka, responded sharply, crying and drenched in tears:
3.45.37
गोदावरीं प्रवेक्ष्यामि
विना रामेण लक्ष्मण ।
आबन्धिष्येऽथवा त्यक्ष्ये
विषमे देहमात्मनः ॥
O Lakshmaṇa, bereft of Rāma I shall throw myself into Gōdāvaree, or hang myself or jump from heights and give up my life.
3.45.38
पिबाम्यहं विषं तीक्ष्णम्
प्रवेक्ष्यामि हुताशनम् ।
न त्वहं राघवादन्यम्
पदापि पुरुषं स्पृशे ॥
I will drink virulent poison, or consign myself to fire, but would not touch any other man than Rāghava, even with my foot.
3.45.39
इति लक्ष्मणमाक्रुश्य
सीता दुःखसमन्विता ।
पाणिभ्यां रुदती दुःखात्
उदरं प्रजघान ह ॥
Abusing Lakshmaṇa thus, Seetā, taken over by anguish, beat her belly with her hands and cried out in anguish.
3.45.40
तामार्तरूपां विमना रुदन्तीं
सौमित्रिरालोक्य विशालनेत्राम् ।
आश्वासयामास न चैव भर्तु
स्तं भ्रातरंकिञ्चिदुवाच सीता ॥
Seeing the wide-eyed one crying, losing her mind and looking like an embodiment of distress, Sowmitri tried to console her. But Seetā would not utter even a single word to her husband’s brother.
3.45.41
ततस्तु सीतामभिवाद्य लक्ष्मणः
कृताञ्जलिः किञ्चिदभिप्रणम्य च ।
अन्वीक्षमाणो बहुशश्च मैथिलीम्
जगाम रामस्य समीपमात्मवान् ॥
Then Lakshmaṇa, a man of conscience, left to reach where Rāma was, after paying obeisance, taking a slight bow with palms joined in reverence and looking back at Maithili many times.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे पञ्जचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty fifth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
