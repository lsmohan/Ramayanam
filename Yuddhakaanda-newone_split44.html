Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 44
Streams of blood flow, with weapons, bodies and hair floating in it. The air is filled with the groans of dying Rākshasas and Vānaras. The battle field becomes a ghastly sight. Meanwhile, Rākshasas make Rāma and Lakshmaṇa their main target and the two of them destroy them all. They strike the six chiefs Yamaṡatru, Mahāpārṡwa, Mahōdara, Vajradaṃshṭra, Ṡuka and Sāraṇa with arrows, and manage to escape with their bare lives by running away from the battle.
Aṅgada, meanwhile, strikes down the horses and charioteer of Indrajit, earning applause from everyone including Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
6.44.1
युद्ध्यतामेव तेषां तु
तदा वानररक्षसाम् ।
रविरस्तं गतो रात्रिः
प्रवृत्ता प्राणहारिणी ॥
As the Vānaras and the Rākshasas thus fought, the sun had set, and the night that was to devour lives, came on.
6.44.2
अन्योन्यं बद्धवैराणाम्
घोराणां जयमिच्छताम् ।
सम्प्रवृत्तं निशायुद्धम्
तदा वानररक्षसाम् ॥
A night of fighting ensued between the Vānaras and the Rākshasas, both fierce, resolved on victory and locked in irreconcilable animosity.
6.44.3
राक्षसोऽसीति हरयो
हरिश्चासीति राक्षसाः ।
अन्योन्यं समरे जघ्नुः
तस्मिंस्तमसि दारुणे ॥
They killed each other in that dreadful darkness, the Vānaras shouting, ‘You, the Rākshasa!’ and the Rākshasas crying, ‘You, the Vānara!’
6.44.4
जहि दारय चैहीति
कथं विद्रवसीति च ।
एवं सुतुमुलः शब्दः
तस्मिंस्तमसि शुश्रुवे ॥
A cacophony of cries, “Kill! Tear Apart! Come, why do you run away!” was heard in that darkness.
6.44.5
कालाः काञ्चनसन्नाहाः
तस्मिंस्तमसि राक्षसाः ।
सम्प्रादृश्यन्त शैलेन्द्रा
दीप्तौषधिवना इव ॥
The Rākshasas, black and clad in golden armor, looked like mountains with healing herbs glowing on them.
6.44.6
तस्मिंस्तमसि दुष्पारे
राक्षसाः क्रोधमूर्च्छिताः ।
परिपेतुर्महावेगा
भक्षयन्तः प्लवङ्गमान् ॥
In that darkness that seemed never ending, the Rākshasas, besides themselves with anger, rampaged all over the place, devouring the Vānaras.
6.44.7
ते हयान् काञ्चनापीडान्
ध्वजांश्चाग्निशिखोपमान् ।
आप्लुत्य दशनैस्तीक्ष्णैः
भीमकोपा व्यदारयन् ।
वानरा बलिनो युद्धेऽ
क्षोभयन् राक्षसीं चमूम् ॥
And the Vānaras, in a terrible rage, sprang up on the horses crested with gold and banners that blazed like tongues of flame, and tore them with their sharp teeth.
6.44.8
कुञ्जरान् कुञ्जरारोहान्
पताकाध्वजिनो रथान् ।
चकर्षुश्च ददंशुश्च
दशनैः क्रोधमूर्च्छिताः ॥
They dragged down elephants and their riders and chariots hung with flags and banners and tore them apart with their fangs in their fury.
6.44.9
लक्ष्मणश्चापि रामश्च
शरैराशीविषोपमैः ।
दृश्यादृश्यानि रक्षांसि
प्रवराणि निजघ्नतुः ॥
Lakshmaṇa and also Rāma struck down noted Rākshasas, who could only be seen indistinctly (in that night).
6.44.10
तुरङ्गखुरविध्वस्तम्
रथनेमिसमुत्थितम् ।
रुरोध कर्णनेत्राणि
युद्ध्यतां धरणीरजः ॥
Dust created from the pounding of the earth by hooves of the horses rose up into the air behind the wheels of the chariots, filling the eyes and ears of the fighters.
6.44.11
वर्तमाने महाघोरे
सङ्ग्रामे रोमहर्षणे ।
रुधिरौघा महाघोरा
नद्यस्तत्र प्रसुस्रुवुः ॥
As that terrible and hair-raising fight went on, there flowed revolting rivers of blood.
6.44.12
ततो भेरीमृदङ्गानाम्
पणवानां च निस्वनः ।
शङ्खवेणुस्वनोन्मिश्रः
सम्बभूवाद्भुतोपमः ॥
There rose then a mighty din of drum, Mṛdaṅga and Paṇava mingled with the blare of conches and flutes.
6.44.13
हतानां स्तनमानानाम्
राक्षसानां च निस्वनः ।
शस्तानां वानराणां च
सम्बभूवातिदारुणः ॥
And also heard, were the dreadful groans of the Rākshasas who received mortal blows and of the Vānaras who were all but dead.
6.44.14
हतैर्वानरवीरैश्च
शक्तिशूलपरश्वधैः ।
निहतैः पर्वताग्रैश्च
राक्षसैः कामरूपिभिः ।
शस्त्रपुष्पोपहारा च
तत्रासीद्युद्धमेदिनी ।
दुर्ज्ञेया दुर्निवेशा च
शोणितास्रावकर्दमा ॥
With the Vānara warriors killed by javelin, spear and hatchet and the Rākshasas who could take any form at will struck down by mountain crests, and with the gore of blood flowing and the weapons floating in it like flowers, the battle field became an impassable, ghastly sight.
6.44.16
सा बभूव निशा घोरा
हरिराक्षसहारिणी ।
कालरात्रीव भूतानाम्
सर्वेषां दुरतिक्रमा ॥
That horrid night, which swallowed up the Vānaras and the Rākshasas, looked as if it would spare no creature any more than the night that puts an end to the world itself.
6.44.17
ततस्ते राक्षसास्तत्र
तस्मिंस्तमसि दारुण ।
राममेवाभ्यवर्तन्त
संसृष्टाः शरवृष्टिभिः ॥
Then, in that frightful darkness, the Rākshasas made Rāma their target, deluging him with a downpour of arrows.
6.44.18
तेषामापततां शब्दः
क्रुद्धानामपि गर्जताम् ।
उद्वर्त इव सप्तानाम्
समुद्राणां प्रशुश्रुवे ॥
The din of their angry roar as they advanced was like the roar of the seven seas when the world comes to an end.
6.44.19
तेषां रामः शरैष्षङ्भिः
षड् जघान निशाचरान् ।
निमेषान्तरमात्रेण
शितैरग्निशिखोपमैः ॥
Rāma struck six of them in a split second with six sharp arrows, that were like tongues of flame.
6.44.20
यमशत्रुश्च दुर्धर्षो
महापार्श्वमहोदरौ ।
वज्रदंष्ट्रो महाकायः
तौचोभौ शुकसारणौ ।
ते तु रामेण बाणौघैः
सर्वे मर्मसु ताडिताः ।
युद्धादपसृतास्तत्र
सावशेषायुषोऽभवन् ॥
They, the unassailable Yamaṡatru, Mahāpārṡwa and Mahōdara, Vajradaṃshṭra of the huge body, and the duo of Ṡuka and Sāraṇa, struck by Rāma in their vitals with a flood of arrows, managed to escape with their bare lives, by running away from the battle.
6.44.22
तत्र काञ्चनचित्राङ्गैः
शरैरग्निशिखोपमैः ।
दिशश्चकार विमलाः
प्रदिशश्च महाबलः ॥
He of immense strength illuminated the cardinals and the spaces in between them with his arrows that glowed like tongues of flame with their golden shafts.
6.44.23
ये त्वन्ये राक्षसा भीमा
रामस्याभिमुखे स्थिताः ।
तेऽपि नष्टाः समासाद्य
पतङ्ग इव पावकम् ॥
All other Rākshasas, however formidable, who dared to make a stand against Rāma were destroyed like moths by the fire.
6.44.24
सुवर्णपुङ्खैर्विशिखैः
सम्पतद्भिः सहस्रशः ।
बभूव रजनी चित्रा
खद्योतैरिव शारदी ॥
With thousands of arrows powered with golden feathers speeding in all directions, the night was enchanting like post-monsoon nights with fireflies.
6.44.25
राक्षसानां च निनदैः
हरीणां चापि निस्वनैः ।
सा बभूव निशा घोर
भूयो घोरतरा तदा ॥
The horror of the night seemed even more horrific with the cries of the Rākshasas and the Vānaras.
6.44.26
तेन शब्देन महता
प्रवृद्धेन समन्ततः ।
त्रिकूटः कन्दराकीर्णः
प्रव्याहरदिवाचलः ॥
With that mighty din which arose everywhere reverberating in its many caverns, the Trikūṭa mountain seemed as if it was trying to say something.
6.44.27
गोलाङ्गूला महाकायाः
तमसा तुल्यवर्चसः ।
सम्परिष्वज्य बाहुभ्याम्
भक्षयन् रजनीचरान् ॥
The Gōlāṅgūlas of humongous bodies, dark as the darkness of the night, crushed the Rākshasas in their bear’s hug and devoured them.
6.44.28
अङ्गदस्तु रणे शत्रुम्
निहन्तुं समुपस्थितः ।
रावणिं निजघानाशु
सारथिं च हयानपि ॥
Aṅgada, resolved on destroying the foe, struck swiftly at the son of Rāvaṇa, his charioteer and horses.
6.44.29
वर्तमाने तदा घोरे
सङ्ग्रामे भृशदारुणे ।
इन्द्रजित्तु रथं त्यक्त्वा
हताश्वो हतसारथिः ।
अङ्गदेन महामायः
तत्रैवान्तरधीयत ॥
As the terrible and extremely brutal fight went on, Indrajit, a great master of trickery, vanished instantly, his horses and charioteer destroyed by Aṅgada.
6.44.31
तत्कर्म वालिपुत्रस्य
सर्वे देवाः सहर्षिभिः ।
तुष्टुवुः पूजनार्हस्य
तौ चोभौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
All the Dēvas, along with the Ṛshis praised that exploit of Vāli’s son, who deserved all the honor, and so did the brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa.
6.44.32
प्रभावं सर्वभूतानि
विदुरिन्द्रजितो युधि ।
तेन ते तं महात्मानम्
तुष्टा दृष्ट्वा प्रधर्षितम् ॥
All the creatures who were very much aware of Indrajit’s prowess in battle felt happy seeing that mighty warrior defeated by him (Aṅgada).
6.44.33
ततः प्रहृष्टाः कपयः
ससुग्रीवविभीषणाः ।
साधु साध्विति नेदुश्च
दृष्ट्वा शत्रुं प्रधर्षितम् ॥
And the delighted Vānaras, along with Sugreeva and Vibheeshaṇa, exclaimed ‘Splendid! Well done!’, on seeing the enemy put to rout.
6.44.34
इन्द्रजित्तु तदा तेन
निर्जितो भीमकर्मणा ।
संयुगे वालिपुत्रेण
क्रोधं चक्रे सुदारुणम् ॥
But Indrajit, who had been worsted in battle by Vāli’s son of formidable exploits, became terribly outraged.
6.44.35
सोऽन्तर्धानगतः पापो
रावणी रणकर्कशः ।
अदृश्यो निशितान् बाणान्
मुमोचाशनिवर्चसः ॥
The wicked son of Rāvaṇa, ruthless in battle, stayed invisible while he let off his arrows that were sharp and powerful as the thunderbolt.
6.44.36
स रामं लक्ष्मणं चैव
घोरैर्नागमयैः शरैः ।
बिभेद समरे क्रुद्धः
सर्वगात्रेषु राक्षसः ॥
In his fury in that battle, the Rākshasa struck Rāma and Lakshmaṇa all over their bodies with arrows that were in the shape of serpents.
6.44.37
मायया संवृतस्तत्र
मोहयन् राघवौ युधि ।
अदृश्यः सर्वभूतानाम्
कूटयोधी निशाचरः ।
बबन्ध शरबन्धेन
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ॥
Hiding himself from the view of all creatures with his magical powers, and confounding the scions of Raghu as well, that Night-rover, fighting unfairly, tied down the brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa with his arrows.
6.44.38
तौ तेन पुरुषव्याघ्रौ
क्रुद्धेनाशीविषैः शरैः ।
सहसा निहतौ वीरौ
तदा प्रैक्षन्त वानराः ॥
The Vānaras saw the valiant brothers, tigers among men, struck down suddenly by the irate foe with arrows that were like venomous snakes.
6.44.39
प्रकाशरूपस्तु यदा न शक्तः
तौ बाधितुं राक्षसराजपुत्रः ।
मायां प्रयोक्तुं समुपाजगाम
बबन्ध तौ राजसुतौ महात्मा ॥
Finding it impossible to fight in the open, the powerful son of the Rākshasa King resorted to his magical powers to tie the two princes down.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे चतुश्चत्वारिंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty fourth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
