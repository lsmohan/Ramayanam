Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 83
This Sarga describes the first leg of Bharata's journey to the Vana, from the city of Ayōdhyā to the river Gaṅgā, with his entourage consisting of family members, ministers, Purōhitas, people of various trades and leaders of all communities and groups. After reaching Gaṅgā, he instructs the army to camp for the night wherever they like on its vast banks, which are beautiful with rows of Cakravāka birds.
2.83.1
ततस्समुत्थितः काल्यम्
आस्थाय स्यन्दनोत्तमम् ।
प्रययौ भरतश्शीघ्रम्
रामदर्शनकाङ्क्षया ॥
Getting up at daybreak and getting on to a superb chariot, Bharata went forth swiftly, longing to see Rāma.
2.83.2
अग्रतः प्रययुस्तस्य
सर्वे मन्त्रिपुरोधसः ।
अधिरुह्य हयैर्युक्तान्
रथान्सूर्यरथोपमान् ॥
Ahead of him went all the ministers and Purōhitas, riding in horse-drawn chariots, which resembled the chariot of the sun.
2.83.3
नवनागसहस्राणि
कल्पितानि यथाविधि ।
अन्वयुर्भरतं यान्तम्
इक्ष्वाकुकुलनन्दनम् ॥
Nine thousand elephants, marshaled in due order, followed the journeying Bharata, the delight of the Ikshwākus.
2.83.4
षष्टी रथसहस्राणि
धन्विनो विविधायुधाः ।
अन्वयुर्भरतं यान्तम्
राजपुत्रं यशस्विनम् ॥
Sixty-thousand chariots and archers holding weapons of various kinds followed the renowned prince Bharata on the journey.
2.83.5
शतं सहस्राण्यश्वानाम्
समारूढानि राघवम् ।
अन्वयुर्भरतं यान्तम्
सत्यसन्धं जितेन्द्रियम् ॥
A hundred thousand mounted horses accompanied Bharata, the scion of the Raghus, who was wedded to truth and had mastered his senses, on his journey.
2.83.6
कैकेयी च सुमित्रा च
कौसल्या च यशस्विनी ।
रामानयनसंहृष्टा
ययुर्यानेन भास्वता ॥
Kaikēyee, Sumitrā and the renowned Kousalyā, delighted to hear that Rāma was to be fetched back, went in splendid vehicles.
2.83.7
प्रयाताश्चार्यसङ्घाता
रामं द्रष्टुं सलक्ष्मणम् ।
तस्यैव च कथाश्चित्राः
कुर्वाणा हृष्टमानसाः ॥
Large numbers of nobility, with joyful hearts, went forth to see Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, recollecting his deeds.
Interestingly, Vālmeeki does not narrate the childhood of Rāma and his brothers at all, in Bāla Kāṇḍa. However, in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa he gives us a glimpse of Rāma's character, capabilities and the reputation that he had earned, through the words of the people of Ayōdhyā, many a time, as he does in this Ṡlōka.
From a literary perspective, I surmise that it would have been better if Vālmeeki had chosen to narrate a few incidents of Rāma's childhood, as Vyāsa did, of Krishna's childhood in Bhāgavatam.
2.83.8
मेघश्यामं महाबाहुम्
स्थिरसत्त्वं दृढव्रतम् ।
कदा द्रक्ष्यामहे रामम्
जगतश्शोकनाशनम् ॥
When shall we see that mighty-armed Rāma of cloud-like dark complexion and unshakable courage, and who is steadfast in his vows and is a destroyer of sorrows of this world?
2.83.9
दृष्ट एव हि नश्शोकम्
अपनेष्यति राघवः ।
तमस्सर्वस्य लोकस्य
समुद्यन्निव भास्करः ॥
The very sight of Rāghava removes all our sufferings, like the rising sun would, the darkness of the entire world.
2.83.10
इत्येवं कथयन्तस्ते
सम्प्रहृष्टाः कथाश्शुभाः ।
परिष्वजानाश्चान्योन्यम्
ययुर्नागरिका जनाः ॥
Hugging each other and sharing such pleasant thoughts the citizens went along in a festive mood.
2.83.11
ये च तत्रापरे सर्वे
सम्मता ये च नैगमाः ।
रामं प्रति ययुर्हृष्टा
स्सर्वाः प्रकृतयस्तथा ॥
All others who enjoyed the esteem (of the royal family) and merchants and members of guilds and all the subjects, then journeyed forth with gladdened hearts, to see Rāma.
2.83.12
मणिकाराश्च ये केचित्
कुम्भकाराश्च शोभनाः ।
सूत्रकर्मकृतश्चैव
ये च शस्त्रोपजीविनः ।
मयूरकाः क्राकचिका
रोचका वेधकास्तथा ।
दन्तकारा स्सुधाकारा
स्तथा गन्धोपजीविनः ।
सुवर्णकाराः प्रख्याताः
तथा कम्बलधावकाः ।
स्नापकोष्णोदका वैद्या
धूपकाश्शौण्डिकास्तथा ।
रजकास्तुन्नवायाश्च
ग्रामघोषमहत्तराः ।
शैलूषाश्च सह स्त्रीभिः
ययुः कैवर्तकास्तथा ॥
Also joining them were gem-cutters, skilled potters, tailors, armament-makers, artificers in peacock plumes, sawyers, polishers, perforators, workers in ivory and stucco, perfumers, goldsmiths of renown, blanket cleaners, bath-attendants and masseurs, physicians, fishermen, incense-burners, brewers, launderers, weavers, chieftains of villages and herdsmen colonies, and actors with their women troupes.
Vālmeeki takes the opportunity to give us a list of common trades and vocations of the day.
2.83.16
समाहिता वेदविदो
ब्राह्मणा वृत्तसम्मताः ।
गोरथैर्भरतं यान्तम्
अनुजग्मु स्सहस्रशः ॥
Thousands of imperturbable Brāhmaṇas who were well versed in Vēdas and held in high esteem for their conduct followed Bharata on the journey in ox-drawn chariots.
2.83.17
सुवेषाश्शुद्धवसनाः
ताम्रमृष्टानुलेपनाः ।
सर्वे ते विविधैर्यानैः
शनैर्भरतमन्वयुः ॥
All of them, well-adorned, clad in clean garments, their bodies anointed with rich red unguents, followed Bharata in various vehicles at a leisurely pace.
2.83.18
प्रहृष्टमुदिता सेना
साऽन्वयात्कैकयीसुतम् ।
भ्रातुरानयने यान्तम्
भरतं भ्रातृवत्सलम् ॥
The army, happy and joyous, followed the son of Kaikēyee who loved his brother, as he was going forth to fetch him.
2.83.19
ते गत्वा दूरमध्वानम्
रथयानाश्वकुञ्जरैः ।
समासेदुस्ततो गङ्गाम्
शृङ्गिबेरपुरं प्रति ।
यत्र रामसखो वीरो
गुहो ज्ञातिगणैर्वृतः ।
निवसत्यप्रमादेन
देशं तं परिपालयन् ॥
After travelling a considerable distance on chariots, carriages, horses and elephants, they reached river Gaṅgā near Ṡṛṅgibērapura, in the region where the valiant Guha, Rāma's friend, with his kinsmen, ruled, guarding it with all vigilance.
2.83.21
उपेत्य तीरं गङ्गायाः
चक्रवाकैरलङ्कृतम् ।
व्यवातिष्ठत सा सेना
भरतस्यानुयायिनी ॥
The army accompanying Bharata reached the bank of river Gaṅgā that was adorned with Cakravāka birds, and halted there.
2.83.22
निरीक्ष्यानुगतां सेनाम्
तां च गङ्गां शिवोदकाम् ।
भरतस्सचिवान्सर्वान्
अब्रवीद्वाक्यकोविदः ॥
Seeing the army that followed him and Gaṅgā with her sanctifying waters, Bharata, who speaks well, said to all of his officials:
2.83.23
निवेशयत मे सैन्यम्
अभिप्रायेण सर्वतः ।
विश्रान्ताः प्रतरिष्यामः
श्व इदानीमिमां नदीम् ॥
Let the army camp here, wherever they like. We shall take rest and cross the river tomorrow.
2.83.24
दातुं च तावदिच्छामि
स्वर्गतस्य महीपतेः ।
और्ध्वदेहनिमित्तार्थम्
अवतीर्योदकं नदीम् ॥
In the meanwhile, I wish to get into the river and offer libations of water for the benefit, in the after-life, of the King, who has gone to Heaven.
2.83.25
तस्यैवं ब्रुवतोऽमात्याः
तथेत्युक्त्वा समाहिताः ।
न्यवेशयंस्तां छन्देन
स्वेन स्वेन पृथक्पृथक् ॥
When he said this, his ministers said 'so shall it be', and with all attention, got the army units to pitch their camps at places of their choice.
2.83.26
निवेश्य गङ्गामनु तां महानदीं
चमूं विधानैः परिबर्हशोभिनीम् ।
उवास रामस्य तदा महात्मनो
विचिन्तयानो भरतो निवर्तनम् ॥
Bharata, having got that army that was looking splendid with the various equipments encamped, and having made necessary arrangements for them on the bank of that mighty river Gaṅgā, stayed there thinking about bringing Mahātma Rāma back.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे त्र्यशीतितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighty third Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
