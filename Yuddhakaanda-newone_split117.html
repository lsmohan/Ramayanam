Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 117
In this Sarga, the foremost of the Dēvas, Vaiṡravaṇa, Yama, Indra, Varuṇa, Ṡiva and Brahma come together to Rāma, and ask him how he could behave like an ordinary man, without knowing himself to be the all pervasive Vishṇu. Rāma tells that he thinks of himself as Rāma, son of Daṡaratha, a human. He asks them to reveal who he truly is. Then they tell him he is none other than Nārāyaṇa, the all-pervasive prop of all creatures beyond whom nothing exists, and Seetā is none other than Lakshmee, his consort. And they thank him for serving their cause by slaying Rāvaṇa.
6.117.1
ततो हि दुर्मना रामः
श्रुत्वैवं वदतां गिरः ।
दध्यौ मुहूर्तं धर्मात्मा
बाष्पव्याकुललोचनः ॥
Rāma, the Dharmātma, feeling terrible on hearing those words that were uttered, was lost in thought for a while, his eyes becoming turbid with tears.
This Ṡlōka, a near identical repetition of Ṡlōka 11 in the next Sarga, except for two syllables, seems to be a misplaced insertion, and does not make any sense in this context.
It is not explicit whose words it is referring to. If they are Seetā's as the context suggests, it is inappropriate that Rāma reacts to them so late, after Seetā had already entered the fire and this is not a time for him to be lost in thought 'for a while'.
6.117.2
ततो वैश्रवणो राजा
यमश्च पितृभिः सह ।
सहस्राक्षश्च देवेशो
वरुणश्च जलेश्वरः ।
षडर्धनयनः श्रीमान्
महादेवो वृषध्वजः ।
कर्ता सर्वस्य लोकस्य
ब्रह्मा ब्रह्मविदां वरः ।
एते सर्वे समागम्य
विमानैः सूर्यसन्निभैः ।
आगम्य नगरीं लङ्काम्
अभिजग्मुश्च राघवम् ॥
And at that time, Vaiṡravaṇa, the King, Yama along with the Pitṛ Dēvatas, the Thousand-Eyed lord of the Dēvas, Varuṇa the lord of waters, the gracious Three-eyed Supreme Dēva who had a bull for the emblem on his flag, Brahma, the creator of all the worlds and the foremost of the exponent of Vēdas, — all together descended on the city of Laṅkā, in their sun-bright Vimānas, and came to Rāghava.
6.117.5
ततः सहस्ताभरणान्
प्रगृह्य विपुलान् भुजान् ।
अब्रुवंस्त्रिदशश्रैष्ठाः
प्राञ्जलिं राघवं स्थितम् ॥
Then those eminent Dēvas said to Rāghava who stood with his long arms adorned with ornaments and joined palms raised in salutation:
6.117.6
कर्ता सर्वस्य लोकस्य
श्रेष्ठो ज्ञानवतां वरः ।
उपेक्षसे कथं सीताम्
पतन्तीं हव्यवाहने ॥
How could you, the foremost of all those who know all that is to be known, and who fashion all the worlds, remain silent while Seetā threw herself into fire?
6.117.7
कथं देवगणश्रेष्ठम्
आत्मानं नावबुध्यसे ।
ऋतधामा वसुः पूर्वम्
वसूनां त्वं प्रजापतिः ॥
How is it that you do not seem to realize that you are foremost among the Dēvas? In the far past, you were Ṛtadhāma, the first of the Vasus, and were the great Progenitor.
6.117.8
त्रयाणां त्वं हि लोकानाम्
आदिकर्ता स्वयम्प्रभुः ।
रुद्राणामष्टमो रुद्रः
साध्यानामसि पञ्चमः ।
अश्विनौ चापि ते कर्णौ
चन्द्रसूर्यौ च चक्षुषी ॥
You are the lord of yourself and were the primordial force that fashioned the three worlds. You are the eighth of the Rudras and fifth of the Sādhyas. The Aṡwins are your ears, and the sun and the moon, your eyes.
6.117.9
अन्ते चादौ च लोकानाम्
दृश्यसे त्वं परन्तप ।
उपेक्षसे च वैदेहीम्
मानुषः पाकृतो यथा ॥
You are the only one to be seen before the worlds come into being and after they cease to exist, O scorcher of the foe! Why do you ignore Vaidēhee, like an ordinary man!
6.117.10
इत्युक्तो लोकपालैस्तैः
स्वामी लोकस्य राघवः ।
अब्रवीत्रिदशश्रेष्ठान्
रामो धर्मभृतां वरः ॥
Thus addressed by the Guardians of the world, Rāghava, the lord of the world and foremost of the patrons of Dharma said to those foremost of the Dēvas:
6.117.11
आत्मानं मानुषं मन्ये
रामं दशरथात्मजम् ।
योऽहं यस्य यतश्चाहम्
भगवांस्तद् ब्रवीतु मे ॥
I think of himself as Rāma, son of Daṡaratha, a human. Be pleased, O Bhagawan, to reveal to me who I truly am, who I belong to and where I came from!
6.117.12
इति ब्रुवन्तं काकुत्स्थम्
ब्रह्मा ब्रह्मविदां वरः ।
अब्रवीच्छृणु मे राम
सत्यं सत्यपराक्रम ॥
With Kākutstha imploring thus, Brahma, the foremost of the knowers of Brahman, said, 'Listen O Rāma of true prowess, here is the Truth!'
6.117.13
भवान्नारायणो देवः
श्रीमांश्चक्रायुधो विभुः ।
एकशृङ्गो वराहस्त्वम्
भूतभव्यसपत्नजित् ॥
You are the blessed lord Nārāyaṇa, the Dēva who wields a discuss as a weapon. You are the primordial Boar with single tusk. You are the vanquisher of foes, past and future.
6.117.14
अक्षरं ब्रह्म सत्यं च
मध्ये चान्ते च राघव ।
लोकानां त्वं परो धर्मो
विष्वक्सेनश्चतुर्भुजः ॥
You, O Rāghava, are the true Brahman that does not perish in the Middle or at the End. You are the ultimate Dharma of all the worlds. You, of four arms, are the chief of defense forces everywhere.
6.117.15
शार्ङ्गधन्वा हृषीकेशः
पुरुषः पुरुषोत्तमः ।
अजितः खड्गधृद्विष्णुः
कृष्णश्चैव बृहद्बलः ॥
You are the wielder of the Sārṅga bow. You are the Controller of the senses. You are the spirit embedded in all. You are the foremost of all spirited beings. You are the invincible one who wields the sword (called Nandaka). You are Vishṇu, the all-pervasive. You are Krishna, who comforts the earth. You are the one of supreme strength.
6.117.16
सेनानीर्ग्रामणीश्च त्वम्
बुद्धिः सत्त्वं क्षमा दमः ।
प्रभवश्चाप्ययश्च त्वम्
उपेन्द्रो मधुसूदनः ॥
You are the chief of defense forces. You are the chief of the community. You are the intellect and you are the power. You are the embodiment of forbearance and you are the master over senses. You are Upēndra, and the slayer of Madhu.
6.117.17
इन्द्रकर्मा महेन्द्रस्त्वम्
पद्मनाभो रणान्तकृत् ।
शरण्यं शरणं च त्वाम्
आहुर्दिव्या महर्षयः ॥
You are the overseer of all and you are Mahēndra. From your navel emerges the lotus. You are the one who puts an end to all fights. The celestial Maharshis exalt you as the refuge for all those who seek refuge.
6.117.18
सहस्रशृङ्गो वेदात्मा
शतशीर्षो महर्षभः ।
त्वं त्रयाणां हि लोकानाम्
आदिकर्ता स्वयम्प्रभुः ।
सिद्धानामपि साध्यानाम्
आश्रयश्चासि पूर्वजः ॥
You are the soul of the Vēdas that have thousands of peaks (branches) and hundreds of heads (injunctions) that expound the important rites.
You are the lord of yourself and were the primordial force that fashioned the three worlds. Existing even before Siddhas and Sādhyas, you are the repose for all of them.
6.117.20
त्वं यज्ञस्त्वं वषट्कारः
त्वमोङ्कारः परात्परः ।
प्रभवं निधनं वा ते
नो विदुः को भवानिति ॥
You are the Yajña. You are the essence addressed by the Mantra Vashaṭ. You are the primordial Aum. You are the ultimate of the ultimate. No one knows either your beginning or your end, or who in reality you are.
6.117.21
दृश्यसे सर्वभूतेषु
ब्राह्मणेषु च गोषु च ।
दिक्षु सर्वासु गगने
पर्वतेषु वनेषु च ॥
You are seen (everywhere), in all creatures, in Brāhmaṇas, in cows, and in every direction, in the sky, in the mountains and in the forests.
6.117.22
सहस्रचरणः श्रीमान्
शतशीर्षः सहस्रदृक् ।
त्वं धारयसि भूतानि
पृथिवीं च सपर्वताम् ॥
You are the gracious Lord with thousands of feet, hundreds of heads and thousand of eyes. You are the prop for all creatures, and the earth with its mountains.
6.117.23
अन्ते पृथिव्याः सलिले
दृश्यसे त्वं महोरगः ।
त्रीन् लोकान् धारयन्
राम देवगन्धर्वदानवान् ॥
You, O Rāma, are the great serpent that supports the three worlds and all the Dēvas, the Gandharvas and the Asuras, that can be seen in the waters when this world ends.
6.117.24
अहं ते हृदयं राम
जिह्वा देवी सरस्वती ।
देवा गात्रेषु रोमाणि
ब्रह्मणा निर्मिताः प्रभो ॥
I am your heart, O Rāma, and goddess Saraswati is your tongue. The Dēvas, fashioned by Brahma, are the hair on your limbs, O Lord!
6.117.25
निमेषस्ते स्मृता रात्रिः
उन्मेषस्ते भवेद्दिवा ।
संस्कारास्तेऽभवन् वेदा
न तदस्ति त्वया विना ॥
It is said that it becomes night when you close your eyes and day, when you open them. The Dēvas are your commandments. There exists nothing apart from you.
6.117.26
जगत् सर्वं शरीरं ते
स्थैर्यं ते वसुधातलम् ।
अग्निः कोपः प्रसादस्ते
सोमः श्रीवत्सलक्षणः ॥
The entire universe is your body and the expanse of the earth is your sturdiness. Your wrath is the fire and your grace is the moon. You are Vishṇu who bears the Sreevatsa mark.
6.117.27
त्वया लोकास्त्रयः क्रान्ताः
पुरा स्वैर्विक्रमैस्त्रिभिः ।
महेन्द्रश्च कृतो राजा
बलिं बद्ध्वा सुदारुणम् ॥
You occupied all the three worlds with three measures of your feet. You reestablished Mahēndra as the king, reining the extremely dreadful Bali.
6.117.28
सीता लक्ष्मीर्भवान् विष्णुः
देवः कृष्णः प्रजापतिः ।
वधार्थं रावणस्येह
प्रविष्टो मानुषीं तनुम् ॥
Seetā is Lakshmee and you are Vishṇu, the Dēva of dark form, and are the Progenitor. You entered into a human body to slay Rāvaṇa here.
6.117.29
तदिदं नः कृतं कार्यम्
त्वया धर्मभृतां वर ।
निहतो रावणो राम
प्रहृष्टो दिवमाक्रम ॥
O Rāma, O foremost of the patrons of Dharma! You have served our cause, slaying Rāvaṇa! The Heaven is open for you, the jubilant one!
6.117.30
अमोघं बलवीर्यं ते
अमोघस्ते पराक्रमः ।
अमोघं दर्शनं राम
अमोघस्तव संस्तवः ॥
Your strength and valor never fails, and so is your prowess. Neither paying a visit to you, nor singing your praises ever go in vain.
6.117.31
अमोघास्ते भविष्यन्ति
भक्तिमन्तो नरा भुवि ।
ये त्वां देवम् ध्रुवं भक्ताः
पुराणं पुरुषोत्तमम् ।
प्राप्नुवन्ति सदा कामान्
इह लोके परत्र च ॥
Those men of the world who are devoted to you will never fail. Those whose devotion firmly lies in you, the ancient, the foremost of all spirited beings, beget all they wish for, in this world as well as in the next.
6.117.32
इममार्षं स्तवं नित्यम्
इतिहासं पुरातनम् ।
ये नराः कीर्तयिष्यन्ति
नास्ति तेषां पराभवः ॥
The men who recite everyday this hymn of praise spoken by the Ṛshis in the context of events chronicled in the far past, will never suffer disgrace.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे सप्तदशोत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and seventeenth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
