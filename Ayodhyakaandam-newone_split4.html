Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 4
In this Sarga Daṡaratha speaks to Rāma in private about the consecration the next day. The concern about the Bharata factor comes into the open. Rāma meets Kousalyā, Sumitrā, Lakshmaṇa and Seetā in Kousalyā's house.
2.4.1
गतेष्वथ नृपो भूयः
पौरेषु सह मन्त्रिभिः ।
मन्त्रयित्वा ततश्चक्रे
निश्चयज्ञस्सनिश्चयम् ॥
After the citizens had left, their lord, the king, who knew to weigh options, discussed again with his ministers, and pronounced his conclusion:
Copper red colored eyes - I wonder, is it like 'honey eyes?' ?
2.4.2
श्व एव पुष्यो भविता
श्वोऽभिषेच्यस्तु मे सुतः ।
रामो राजीवताम्राक्षो
यौवराज्य इति प्रभुः ॥
May Rāma, my son, the one with copper red colored eyes that are wide like lotus petals, be consecrated as crown prince tomorrow, when the moon is in the constellation Pushya!
2.4.3
अथाऽन्तर्गृहमाविश्य
राजा दशरथस्तदा ।
सूतमामन्त्रयामास
रामं पुनरिहानय ॥
Then, the king Daṡaratha went to the inner quarters and directed the charioteer to fetch Rāma again.
Sumantra seems to be special among the ministers, serving also as a personal secretary and personal driver. Sometimes his name is mentioned explicitly. At other times it is implied.
2.4.4
प्रतिगृह्य स तद्वाक्यम्
सूतः पुनरुपाययौ ।
रामस्य भवनं शीघ्रम्
राममानयितुं पुनः ॥
Thus commanded, the charioteer went with all speed to Rāma's residence to bring him again.
2.4.5
द्वार्स्थैरावेदितं तस्य
रामायाऽऽगमनं पुनः ।
श्रुत्वैव चापि रामस्तम्
प्राप्तं शङ्कान्वितोऽभवत् ॥
The doorkeepers conveyed about his arrival to Rāma. Hearing about his arrival again, Rāma wondered why.
2.4.6
प्रवेश्य चैनं त्वरितम्
रामो वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Rāma told them to admit him immediately, and said:
2.4.6
यदागमनकृत्यं ते
भूयस्तद्ब्रूह्यशेषतः ॥
Tell me the reason for your being here again, in detail!
2.4.7
तमुवाच तत स्सूतो
राजा त्वां द्रष्टुमिच्छति ।
श्रुत्वा प्रमाणमत्र त्वम्
गमनायेतराय वा ॥
Then, the charioteer said: "All I can say is that the king wishes to see you. It is up to your discretion whether to go or not!"
2.4.8
इति सूतवचश्श्रुत्वा
रामोऽथ त्वरयाऽन्वितः ।
प्रययौ राजभवनम्
पुनर्द्रष्टुं नरेश्वरम् ॥
Hearing those words of the charioteer, Rāma, with a sense of urgency, left to the king's palace again to see the lord of the people.
2.4.9
तं श्रुत्वा समनुप्राप्तम्
रामं दशरथो नृपः ।
प्रवेशयामास गृहम्
विवक्षुः प्रियमुत्तमम् ॥
Having heard that Rāma had arrived, King Daṡaratha had him admitted to tell him the best of news.
2.4.10
प्रविशन्नेव च श्रीमान्
राघवो भवनं पितुः ।
ददर्श पितरं दूरात्
प्रणिपत्य कृताञ्जलिः ॥
As soon as he entered the palace of the king, the richly endowed prince of Raghu dynasty saw his father from a distance and approached him with palms joined in reverence, and prostrated.
2.4.11
प्रणमन्तं समुत्थाप्य
तं परिष्वज्य भूमिपः ।
प्रदिश्य चास्मै रुचिरम्
आसनं पुनरब्रवीत् ॥
Raising him, who was prostrating, the king embraced him, offered a splendid seat, and then said:
2.4.12
राम वृद्धोऽस्मि दीर्घायुः
भुक्ता भोगा मयेप्सिताः ।
अन्नवद्भिः क्रतुशतै
स्तथेष्टं भूरिदक्षिणैः ॥
O Rāma! I have lived a long life and I am old now. I enjoyed all the pleasures that I had a mind to. I have duly performed hundreds of Yajñas gifting away ample food and money!
These Ṡlōkas are essentially a list of things that one would aspire to. Ṡlōka 14 is especially important. It puts the goals of life very succinctly as being two fold:
1) to enjoy oneself
2) to pay off the debts to the Dēvas, Ṛshis, ancestors and Brāhmaṇas.
The second part categorizes the obligation of every person in a unique manner.
Debt to Dēvas: The Indic concept of Dēvas is very straight forward. They simply represent the physical and conceptual powers that make up the static and dynamic nature of the universe.
Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva, are examples of conceptual powers of commissioning/creating, managing/protecting and decommissioning/dissolving.
Panca Bhūtas and the related forces of the nature are the physical powers. That is why we consider Wind, Fire, Earth, Mountains, Rivers and such, as Dēvatas.
Paying the debt of the Dēvas is done by being thankful to all the natural resources and preserving them, from the secular point of view, and doing Yajñas and giving offerings from the spiritual point of view.
Debt to Ṛshis: Ṛshis are visionaries who establish systems or invent Mantras that give spiritual energy. Paying the debt of the Ṛshis is done by preserving the systems.
Debt to Ancestors: We are here because of our ancestors. Paying the debt of the ancestors is done by providing and caring for our progeny, as our ancestors did for us.
Debt to Brāhmaṇas: All sciences (Ṡāstras), both spiritual and temporal, which are a vital part of our living owe their existence to Brāhmaṇas. Paying the debt of Brāhmaṇas is done by giving provisions and funds to them.
2.4.13
जातमिष्टमपत्यं मे
त्वमद्यानुपमं भुवि ।
दत्तमिष्टमधीतं च
मया पुरुषसत्तम ॥
O distinguished among men! In you, I have a son who unparalleled in the world today because of the charitable deeds, Yajñas and study of Vedas that I have done.
2.4.14
अनुभूतानि चेष्टानि
मया वीर सुखान्यपि ।
देवर्षिपितृविप्राणाम्
अनृणोऽस्मि तथाऽत्मनः ॥
O Veera! I have experienced every pleasure that I had mind to. I am also free of my debts to the Dēvas, Ṛshis and ancestors and Brāhmaṇas!
2.4.15
न किञ्चिन्मम कर्तव्यम्
तवान्यत्राभिषेचनात् ।
अतो यत्त्वामहं ब्रूयाम्
तन्मे त्वं कर्तुमर्हसि ॥
There is nothing left to be done for me except your consecration. Hence, may you do in accordance with what I am going to tell you now!
2.4.16
अद्य प्रकृतयस्सर्वाः
त्वमिच्छन्ति नराधिपम् ।
अतस्त्वां युवराजानम्
अभिषेक्ष्यामि पुत्रक ॥
All the subjects want to see you as their ruler. My dear son! Hence I would like to consecrate you as the crown prince!
2.4.17
अपि चाद्याऽशुभान्राम
स्वप्ने पश्यामि दारुणान् ।
सनिर्घाता दिवोल्का च
पततीह महास्वना ॥
Besides, O Rāma, I am having frightening and ominous dreams! Meteors are seen even during day time along with loud thunder!
2.4.18
अवष्टब्धं च मे राम
नक्षत्रं दारुणैर्ग्रहैः ।
आवेदयन्ति दैवज्ञाः
सूर्याङ्गारकराहुभिः ॥
My natal star, say the astrologers, is beset by the malefic planets, the Sun, Mars and Rāhu.
2.4.19
प्रायेण हि निमित्तानाम्
ईदृशानां समुद्भवे ।
राजा हि मृत्युमाप्नोति
घोरां वाऽऽपदमृच्छति ॥
When such portents occur, it is considered, in general, that the King dies or faces grave calamity.
2.4.20
तद्यावदेव मे चेतो
न विमुह्यति राघव ।
तावदेवाभिषिञ्चस्व
चला हि प्राणिनां मतिः ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Hence, I like to see you consecrated while my mind is still thinking clearly; for the minds of all beings are fickle.
2.4.21
अद्य चन्द्रोऽभ्युपगतः
पुष्यात्पूर्वं पुनर्वसू ।
श्वः पुष्ययोगं नियतम्
वक्ष्यन्ते दैवचिन्तकाः ॥
Today the moon is in the constellation of Punarvasu which precedes the constellation of Pushya. Tomorrow, it will be in the constellation of Pushya, a conjunction that astrologers regard as auspicious.
2.4.22
ततः पुष्येऽभिषिञ्चस्व
मनस्त्वरयतीव माम् ।
श्वस्त्वाऽहमभिषेक्ष्यामि
यौवराज्ये परन्तप ॥
My mind urges me to consecrate you in that Pushya. O tormentor of foes! I shall consecrate you tomorrow as the crown prince,!
2.4.23
तस्मात्त्वयाऽद्य प्रभृति
निशेयं नियतात्मना ।
सह वध्वोपवस्तव्या
दर्भप्रस्तरशायिना ॥
Therefore may you fast starting tonight With full discipline and may you, along with my daughter-in-law sleep on the ground covered with Darbha grass.
2.4.24
सुहृदश्चाप्रमत्तास्त्वाम्
रक्षन्त्वद्य समन्ततः ।
भवन्ति बहु विघ्नानि
कार्याण्येवंविध्नानि हि ॥
May your friends keep guard over you vigilantly on all sides. Indeed, events like these are liable to many impediments!
2.4.25
विप्रोषितश्च भरतो
यावदेव पुरादितः ।
तावदेवाभिषेकस्ते
प्राप्तकालो मतो मम ॥
I feel that now, when Bharata is away from the city, is the right time for your consecration.
2.4.26
कामं खलु सतां वृत्ते
भ्राता ते भरतस्स्थितः ।
ज्येष्ठानुवर्ती धर्मात्मा
सानुक्रोशो जितेन्द्रियः ॥
Your brother Bharata is truly established in the path of virtuous people. He is a Dharmātma. He is compassionate. He has mastered his senses. And he conforms and abides by his elder brother.
2.4.27
किन्तु चित्तं मनुष्याणाम्
अनित्यमिति मे मतिः ।
सतां च धर्मनित्यानाम्
कृतशोभि च राघव ॥
Yet, O prince of Raghu dynasty, I feel that human nature won't be the same at all times, even in the case of virtuous, well honed and Dharma-adhering people.
2.4.28
इत्युक्तस्सोऽभ्यनुज्ञातः
श्वोभाविन्यभिषेचने ।
व्रजेति रामः पितरम्
अभिवाद्याभ्ययाद्गृहम् ॥
Learning thus about the consecration next day, and getting permission to leave, Rāma paid obeisance to his father and left for his residence.
2.4.29
प्रविश्य चात्मनो वेश्म
राज्ञोद्दिष्टेऽभिषेचने ।
तत्क्षणेन विनिर्गम्य
मातुरन्तःपुरं ययौ ॥
Immediately after reaching home after the consecration was confirmed by the king, he left for the inner quarters of his mother.
2.4.30
तत्र तां प्रवणामेव
मातरं क्षौमवासिनीम् ।
वाग्यतां देवतागारे
ददर्शाऽऽयाचतीं श्रियम् ॥
He saw his mother there, in the Pūja room clad in silk garments, praying in silence for his well-being.
2.4.31
प्रागेव चागता तत्र
सुमित्रा लक्ष्मण स्तथा ।
सीता चानायिता श्रुत्वा
प्रियं रामाभिषेचनम् ॥
Sumitrā and Lakshmaṇa were already there, hearing the news of Rāma's consecration. Seetā had also been requested to be there, by then.
2.4.32
तस्मिन् काले हि कौसल्या
तस्थावामीलितेक्षणा ।
सुमित्रयाऽन्वास्यमाना
सीतया लक्ष्मणेन च ॥
At that time, attended by Sumitrā, Seetā and Lakshmaṇa, Kousalyā was seen there sitting with eyes half-closed.
2.4.33
श्रुत्वा पुष्येण पुत्रस्य
यौवराज्याऽभिषेचनम् ।
प्राणायामेन पुरुषम्
ध्यायमाना जनार्दनम् ॥
Having heard about the consecration of her son as the crown prince when moon would be in the Pushya constellation, she was doing Prānāyāma, meditating upon Janārdana.
Janārdana is another name for Vishṇu.
2.4.34
तथा सनियमामेव
सोऽभिगम्याभिवाद्य च ।
उवाच वचनं रामो
हर्षयंस्तामिदं तदा ॥
Then Rāma approached her, who was still in those observances, and after paying obeisance, said these pleasing words:
2.4.35
अम्ब पित्रा नियुक्तोऽस्मि
प्रजापालनकर्मणि ।
भविता श्वोऽभिषेको मे
यथा मे शासनं पितुः ॥
O mother! I have been commissioned by father with the responsibilities of governing the people. By his order, the consecration will be taking place tomorrow.
2.4.36
सीतयाप्युपवस्तव्या
रजनीयं मया सह ।
एवमृत्विगुपाध्यायै
स्सह मामुक्तवान्पिता ॥
Father, along with Ṛtviks and Upādhyāyas told me to fast tonight along with Seetā.
2.4.37
यानि यान्यत्र योग्यानि
श्वोभाविन्यभिषेचने ।
तानि मे मङ्गलान्यद्य
वैदेह्याश्चैव कारय ॥
May you help with the performance of all auspicious rites that are appropriate for me and the princess of Vidēha ahead of the consecration tomorrow!
2.4.38
एतच्छ्रुत्वा तु कौसल्या
चिरकालाभिकाङ्क्षितम् ।
हर्षबाष्पकलं वाक्यम्
इदं राममभाषत ॥
Hearing those words for which she was waiting for a long time, Kousalyā said these words to Rāma while her eyes were blurred with tears of joy:
2.4.39
वत्स राम चिरं जीव
हतास्ते परिपन्थिनः ।
ज्ञातीन्मे त्वं श्रिया युक्त
स्सुमित्रायाश्च नन्दय ॥
My dear Rāma! May you be blessed with long life! May those who wish you ill be destroyed. May you prosper and bring delight to my side of the family as well as to that of Sumitrā.
2.4.40
कल्याणे बत नक्षत्रे
मयि जातोऽसि पुत्रक ।
येन त्वया दशरथो
गुणैराराधितः पिता ॥
My dear! You are born to me under such an auspicious star, that you are able to please your father Daṡaratha, with all your great virtues.
2.4.41
अमोघं बत मे क्षान्तम्
पुरुषे पुष्करेक्षणे ।
येयमिक्ष्वाकुराज्यश्रीः
पुत्र त्वां संश्रयिष्यति ॥
My unwavering faith and devotion in the lotus eyed Vishnu had proven to be of merit, because of which the fortune of the kingdom of Ikshwāku has chosen you, my son!
2.4.42
इत्येवमुक्तो मात्रेदम्
रामो भ्रातरमब्रवीत् ।
प्राञ्जलिं प्रह्वमासीनम्
अभिवीक्ष्य स्मयन्निव ॥
Thus spoken to by his mother, he then spoke, with a smile, to his brother whom he saw standing there with palms joined in a humble manner.
2.4.43
लक्ष्मणेमां मया सार्धम्
प्रशाधि त्वं वसुन्धराम् ।
द्वितीयं मेऽन्तरात्मानम्
त्वामियं श्रीरुपस्थिता ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! May you also govern this earth along with me! This great fortune covets you as much, as you are none other than my second self!
2.4.44
सौमित्रे भुङ्क्ष्व भोगांत्स्वम्
इष्टान्राज्यफलानि च ।
जीवितं च हि राज्यं च
त्वदर्थमभिकामये ॥
O dear son of Sumitrā! May you enjoy all that comes to your mind and all the fruits of this kingdom! This life and kingdom, I desire, only for your sake!
2.4.45
इत्युक्त्वा लक्ष्मणं रामो
मातरावभिवाद्य च ।
अभ्यनुज्ञाप्य सीतां च
जगाम स्वं निवेशनम् ॥
Having said this to Lakshmaṇa, having paid obeisance to the mothers and having obtained the permission to leave, Rāma left for his residence along with Seetā.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे चतुर्थस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fourth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
