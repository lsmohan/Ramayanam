Baala Kaanda - Sarga 7
Success may have only one face. But it has many brains, eyes, hands and legs behind it. The CEO gets projected, but there are several smart and hardworking executives and experts behind the scenes. The leader gets projected, but there would be several lieutenants behind him, that contribute to the success.
This Sarga sings the glory of the ministers that were behind the successful and ideal rule of king Daṡaratha. Vālmeeki makes it a point to give credit where it is due.
1.7.1
तस्यामात्या गुणैरासन्
इक्ष्वाकोस्तु महात्मनः ।
मन्त्रज्ञाश्चेङ्गितज्ञाश्च
नित्यं प्रियहिते रताः ॥
That king from Ikshwāku dynasty was assisted by highly qualified ministers capable of assessing his intent and forging a strategy that furthered his best interests.
1.7.2
अष्टौ बभूवुर्वीरस्य
तस्यामात्या यशस्विनः ।
शुचयश्चानुरक्ताश्च
राजकृत्येषु नित्यशः ॥
The well reputed Veera (Daṡaratha) had eight ministers who were completely vested in providing clean administration consistently.
(Why is there no good word in English equivalent to 'Veera'?)
1.7.3
धृष्टिर्जयन्तो विजयः
सिद्धार्थो ह्यर्थसाधकः ।
अशोको मन्त्रपालश्च
सुमन्त्रश्चाष्टमोऽभवत् ॥
Their names were: Dṛshṭi, Jayanta, Vijaya, Siddhārtha, Artha Sādhaka, Aṡoka, Mantrapāla and Sumantra.
Interestingly, of the eight names, four are evergreen and sound as modern as any other name.
And it is interesting to know that both the words Aṡoka and Siddhārtha which were popularly associated with Buddha are also names of Daṡaratha's ministers.
1.7.4
ऋत्विजौ द्वावभिमतौ
तस्यास्तामृषिसत्तमौ ।
वसिष्ठो वामदेवश्च
मन्त्रिणश्च तथाऽपरे ॥
Daṡaratha had two great Ṛshis, Vasishṭha and Vāmadēva. as his Ṛtviks whom he especially liked. He also had many other ministers.
1.7.5
विद्याविनीता ह्रीमन्तः
कुशला नियतेन्द्रियाः ।
श्रीमन्तश्च महात्मानः
शास्त्रज्ञा दृढविक्रमाः ।
कीर्तिमन्तः प्रणिहिता
यथावचनकारिणः ।
तेजःक्षमायशःप्राप्ताः
स्मितपूर्वाभिभाषिणः ॥
His ministers were trained in many skills and knowledgeable in many Ṡāstras. They were humble and had full mastery of their senses. They were tactful and had a pleasant disposition. They were stead-fast in their prowess. They were discerning and judicious. They were known for their brilliance and forbearance. They were Mahātmas and they always stood by their word. They were all well to do and well-regarded.
1.7.7
क्रोधात्कामार्थहेतोर्वा
न ब्रूयुरनृतं वचः ।
तेषामविदितं किञ्चित्
स्वेषु नास्ति परेषु वा ।
क्रियमाणं कृतं वापि
चारेणापि चिकीर्षितम् ॥
They did not lie, even in a moment of anger or desperation. They did not involve in dubious activities to fulfill their desires or for gaining riches. Their spies and informants were everywhere and there was nothing they did not know about their people and other people, that was happening, had happened or would be happening.
1.7.8
कुशला व्यवहारेषु
सौहृदेषु परीक्षिताः ।
प्राप्तकालं तु ते दण्डम्
धारयेयुः सुतेष्वपि ॥
Their loyalty was time-tested. They were tactful in dealing with all manners of issues. They brought everyone to justice, as needed, even if it were their own children.
1.7.9
कोशसङ्ग्रहणे युक्ता
बलस्य च परिग्रहे ।
अहितं वापि पुरुषम्
न विहिंस्युरदूषकम् ॥
They were experts in keeping the treasury full and the army filled with the strongest of people. They never gave trouble without reason even to adversaries.
1.7.10
वीराश्च नियतोत्साहा
राजशास्त्रमनुष्ठिताः ।
शुचीनां रक्षितारश्च
नित्यं विषयवासिनाम् ॥
They were Veeras in their own right. They were always lively and motivated. They adhered to sound principles of administration. They avidly protected the good people who lived in the kingdom.
1.7.11
ब्रह्मक्षत्रमहिंसन्तः
ते कोशं समपूरयन् ।
सुतीक्ष्णदण्डाः सम्प्रेक्ष्य
पुरुषस्य बलाबलम् ॥
They managed the treasury without laying undue burden on Brāhmaṇas and Kshatriyas. The punishment they meted out to various offenders was within the limits of the offender's ability to bear.
1.7.12
शुचीनामेकबुद्धीनाम्
सर्वेषां सम्प्रजानताम् ।
नासीत्पुरे वा राष्ट्रे वा
मृषावादी नरः क्वचित् ॥
They were clean and united in matters of the state. Under their watch, no one in the cities or in the entire kingdom dared to lie.
1.7.13
कश्चिन्न दुष्टस्तत्रासीत्
परदाररतो नरः ।
प्रशान्तं सर्वमेवासीत्
राष्ट्रं पुरवरं च तत् ॥
There were no evil or mean people, and no one would seduce others' women. The entire kingdom and its capital were peaceful.
1.7.14
सुवाससः सुवेषाश्च
ते च सर्वे सुशीलिनः ।
हितार्थं च नरेन्द्रस्य
जाग्रतो नयचक्षुषा ॥
The ministers were well dressed and well mannered. They ensured the best for the king by being politically alert at all times.
1.7.15
गुरौ गुणगृहीताश्च
प्रख्याताश्च पराक्रमे ।
विदेशेष्वपि विज्ञाताः
सर्वतो बुद्धिनिश्चयात् ॥
They were eager to learn from their elders. They were renowned for their prowess. They were known for their smartness not only at home but also abroad.
1.7.16
सन्धिविग्रहतत्त्वज्ञाः
प्रकृत्या सम्पदान्विताः ।
मन्त्रसंवरणे शक्ताः
श्लक्ष्णास्सूक्ष्मासु बुद्धिषु ।
नीतिशास्त्रविशेषज्ञाः
सततं प्रियवादिनः ॥
They knew when and how to make peace and when and how to press on. They were naturally opulent. They were of fine intellect. They were good at guarding their strategies from others. They were extremely good at the principles of statecraft. They always spoke in a pleasant manner.
1.7.18
ईदृशैस्तैरमात्यैश्च
राजा दशरथोऽनघः ।
उपपन्नो गुणोपेतैः
अन्वशासद्वसुन्धराम् ॥
Accompanied with such of those virtuous ministers the exalted king Daṡaratha ruled the earth.
1.7.19
अवेक्षमाणश्चारेण
प्रजा धर्मेण रञ्जयन् ।
प्रजानां पालनं कुर्वन्
अधर्मं परिवर्जयन् ।
विश्रुतस्त्रिषु लोकेषु
वदान्यः सत्यसङ्गरः ।
स तत्र पुरुषव्याघ्रः
शशास पृथिवीमिमाम् ॥
King Daṡaratha, tiger among men, with full grasp of the kingdom through informants and spies governed the people according to Dharma, keeping them off unethical and wrong behavior, earning a name in all the three worlds for his generosity and his adherence to truth.
1.7.21
नाध्यगच्छद्विशिष्टं वा
तुल्यं वा शत्रुमात्मनः ।
मित्रवान्नतसामन्तः
प्रतापहतकण्टकः ।
स शशास जगद्राजा
दिवं देवपतिर्यथा ॥
With no foe equal or superior, With good allies, subdued provincial kings, With power that destroyed all troublemakers, King Daṡaratha ruled the earth like Indra ruled the heaven.
1.7.22
तैर्मन्त्रिभिर्मन्त्रहिते नियुक्तैः
वृतोऽनुरक्तैः कुशलैः समर्थैः ।
स पार्थिवो दीप्तिमवाप युक्तः
तेजोमयैर्गोभिरिवोदितोऽर्कः ॥
With the help of those able, talented well-wishing and strategic ministers the king shone gloriously like the sun rising in the east spreading bright rays.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे सप्तमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventh Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
