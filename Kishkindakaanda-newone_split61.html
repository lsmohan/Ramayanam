Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 61
In this Sarga, Saṃpāti tells Niṡākara how he and his brother Jaṭāyu foolishly tried to fly up to the sun and how his wings got burnt, while he protected Jaṭāyu with his wings. He tells Niṡākara that having lost his kingdom, brother and wings, he no more wishes to live.
4.61.1
ततस्तद्दारुणं कर्म
दुष्करं सहसात्कृतम् ।
आचचक्षे मुनेस्सर्वम्
सूर्यानुगमनं तथा ॥
Thereupon I told the Muni all about that sorry episode of following (unto) the sun, an impossible undertaking, driven nothing but by impulse.
4.61.2
भगवन्व्रणयुक्तत्वात्
लज्जया चाकुलेन्द्रियः ।
परिश्रान्तो न शक्नोमि
वचनं परिभाषितुम् ॥
O Bhagawan! I am unable to speak coherently, being wounded, tired and overwhelmed by shame.
4.61.3
अहं चैव जटायुश्च
सङ्घर्षाद्धर्पमोहितौ ।
आकाशं पतितौ दूरात्
जिज्ञासन्तौ पराक्रमम् ॥
Jaṭāyu and I, impelled by our foolish pride, competed with each other to test our valor and flew far into the sky.
4.61.4
कैलासशिखरे बद्ध्वा
मुनीनामग्रतः पणम् ।
रविस्स्यादनुयातव्यो
यावदस्तं महागिरिम् ॥
We made a bet in the presence of the assembled Munis on the peak of Kailāsa, that we should follow the sun to the mountain behind which he sets.
4.61.5
अथाऽऽवां युगपत्प्राप्तौ
अपश्याव महीतले ।
रथचक्रप्रमाणानि
नगराणि पृथक्पृथक् ॥
Flying into the sky simultaneously, we saw on the earth cities, each distinct from the other and looking no bigger than a cart-wheel.
4.61.6
क्व चिद्वादित्रघोषश्च
क्व चिद्भूषणविस्वनः ।
गायन्तीश्चाङ्गना बह्वीः
पश्यावो रक्तवाससः ॥
Sounds from music instruments arose from some, and the clang of ornaments from others. We saw many women, clad in red garments, singing.
4.61.7
तूर्णमुत्पत्य चाकाशम्
आदित्यपथमास्थितौ ।
आवामालोकयावस्तत्
वनं शाद्वलसंस्थितम्  ॥
Very quickly, getting high into the sky, we followed the tracks of the sun. When we looked down, the Vana there looked like a green sward.
4.61.8
उपलैरिव सञ्छन्ना
दृश्यते भूश्शिलोच्चयैः ।
आपगाभिश्च संवीता
सूत्रैरिव वसुन्धरा ॥
The mountains seemed like pebbles covering the earth, and the rivers like threads worn by the earth.
4.61.9
हिमवांश्चैव विन्ध्यश्च
मेरुश्च सुमहागिरिः ।
भूतले सम्प्रकाशन्ते
नागा इव जलाशये ॥
The Himālayas, the Vindhya and Mēru the huge mountain shone on earth like elephants in a lake.
4.61.10
तीव्रस्स्वेदश्च खेदश्च
भयं चासीत्तदाऽऽवयोः ।
समाविशत मोहश्च
ततो मूर्छा च दारुणा ॥
But then, we felt scared, worried and started sweating profusely. We also felt disoriented and terrible, losing our senses.
4.61.11
न च दिग्ज्ञायते याम्या
नाग्नेयी न च वारुणी ।
युगान्ते नियतो लोको
हतो दग्ध इवाग्निना ॥
It was not clear which was the direction of Yama, which was of Agni and which was of Varuṇa. The world was still, like it would be at the end of the time, burnt down by fire.
The quarter ruled by Yama is south. The quarter ruled by Agni is south east. The quarter ruled by Varuṇa is west.
4.61.12
मनश्च मे हतं भूयः
चक्षुः प्राप्य तु संश्रयम् ।
यत्नेन महता ह्यस्मिन्
मनस्सन्धाय चक्षुषी ॥
My mind became numb. But, relying on my eyes, I locked my mind with great effort, on what the eyes were seeing.
4.61.13
यत्नेन महता भूयो
भास्करः प्रतिलोकितः ।
तुल्यः पृथ्वीप्रमाणेन
भास्करः प्रतिभाति नौ ॥
And again with great effort we were able to take a look at the sun when he looked as big as the earth to us.
4.61.14
जटायुर्मामनापृच्छ्य
निपपात महीं ततः ।
तं दृष्ट्वा तूर्णमाकाशात्
आत्मानं मुक्तवानहम् ॥
Then Jaṭāyu, without even telling me, descended precipitately down to the earth. Seeing him do that, I swiftly let myself down.
4.61.15
पक्षाभ्यां च मया गुप्तो
जटायुर्न प्रदह्यते ।
प्रमादात्तत्र निर्दग्धः
पतन्वायुपथादहम् ॥
With my wings covering him, Jaṭāyu escaped from being burnt. As I fell from the track of the wind, I was burnt because of my carelessness.
4.61.16
आशङ्के तं निपतितम्
जनस्थाने जटायुषम् ।
अहं तु पतितो विन्ध्ये
दग्धपक्षो जडीकृतः ॥
I guess Jaṭāyu had fallen down at Janasthāna. I fell on the Vindhya in a daze, my wings burnt.
4.61.17
राज्याच्च हीनो भ्रात्रा च
पक्षाभ्यां विक्रमेण च ।
सर्वथा मर्तुमेवेच्छन्
पतिष्ये शिखराद्गिरेः ॥
Having lost my kingdom, my brother, my wings and my valor, I long for nothing more than death, and I shall hurl myself down from the top of the mountain.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे एकषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty first Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
