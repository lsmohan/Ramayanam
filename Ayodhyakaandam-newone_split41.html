Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 41
This Sarga describes the gloom that descended up on the king's palace and the city of Ayōdhyā, after Rāma's departure.
2.41.1
तस्मिन्स्तु पुरुषव्याघ्रे
विनिर्याति कृताञ्जलौ ।
आर्तशब्दोऽहि सञ्जज्ञे
स्त्रीणामन्तःपुरे महान् ॥
As that tiger among men, with palms joined in reverence, departed, a great cry of distress broke out from the women in the inner quarters.
2.41.2
अनाथस्य जनस्यास्य
दुर्बलस्य तपस्विनः ।
यो गतिश्शरणं चासीत्
स नाथः क्व नु गच्छति ॥
Ah! where does he, the master, go, he, the refuge and protector of all these people who are drained, weak and recourseless now!
2.41.3
न क्रुध्यत्यभिशप्तोऽपि
क्रोधनीयानि वर्जयन् ।
क्रुद्धान्प्रसादयन्सर्वान्
समदुःखः क्व गज्छति ॥
Ah! where does he, the man of equipoise at times of comfort as well as hardship go, he, who never gets angry even when accused falsely, condones even acts that should be met with indignation, consoles even those who are bitter!
2.41.4
कौसल्यायां महातेजा
यथा मातरि वर्तते ।
तथा यो वर्ततेऽस्मासु
महात्मा क्व नु गच्छति ॥
Ah! Where does he, the supremely radiant Mahātma, go, he, who treats all of us in the same manner that he treats his own mother Kousalyā!
2.41.5
कैकेय्या क्लिश्यमानेन
राज्ञा सञ्चोदितो वनम् ।
परित्राता जनस्यास्य
जगतः क्व नु गच्छति ॥
Ah! Where does he, the protector all the world and its inhabitants, go, he, who is troubled by Kaikēyee and ordered by the king to live in Vana!
2.41.6
अहो निश्चेतनो राजा
जीवलोकस्य सम्प्रियम् ।
धर्म्यं सत्यव्रतं रामम्
वनवासे प्रवत्स्यति ॥
Alas! This senseless king is sending Rāma, the darling of all living beings, the devotee of Dharma and the adherent of truth, away to the Vana !
2.41.7
इति सर्वा महिष्यस्ता
विवत्सा इव धेनवः ।
रुरुदुश्चैव दुःखार्ताः
सस्वरं च विचुक्रुशुः ॥
Thus all the queens of the king, like cows that had lost their young, wept and wailed aloud in unison, overcome by grief.
2.41.8
स तमन्तः पुरे घोरम्
आर्तशब्दं महीपतिः ।
पुत्रशोकाभिसन्तप्तः
श्रुत्वा चासीत्सुदुःखितः ॥
The ruler of the world, who was already fried by the grief on account of his son, plunged in to deeper distress hearing those terrible and desolate cries from the inner quarters.
2.41.9
नाग्निहोत्राण्यहूयन्त
नापचन् गृहमेधिनः ।
अकुर्वन्न प्रजाः कार्यम्
सूर्यश्चान्तरधीयत ॥
No fire rituals and oblations were made. No householder cooked food. None of the public services were attended to. And the sun also disappeared.
I cannot stop sharing my joy at this beautiful transition of the scene into the night by Vālmeeki with the last sentence of the Ṡlōkas  here.
2.41.10
व्यसृजन् कबलान्नागा
गावो वत्सान्न पाययन् ।
पुत्रं प्रथमजं लब्ध्वा
जननी नाभ्यनन्दत ॥
Elephants did not touch their portions. Cows did not suckle their young. Even women who gave birth to their first son could not feel the joy.
2.41.11
त्रिशङ्कुर्लोहिताङ्गश्च
बृहस्पतिबुधावपि ।
दारुणा स्सोममभ्येत्य
ग्रहास्सर्वे व्यवस्थिताः ॥
The celestial being Triṡanku and the red planet Kuja, and also Bṛhaspati and Budha and all the other dreadful planets balefully beset upon the moon.
Bṛhaspati is Jupiter and Budha is Mercury.
We use the word 'planet' for 'Graha', for lack of a better alternative, but it should be noted that they are not identical.
Graha is a generic term for any object that moves in the skies against the backdrop of stationary star constellations, which includes even the Sun and the Moon.
A planet, on other hand, at least in the modern sense, is used in the specific sense of a large body that orbits a star in general and Sun in particular.
Neither is right or wrong, but just different.
2.41.12
नक्षत्राणि गतार्चींषि
ग्रहाश्च गततेजसः ।
विशाखास्तु सधूमाश्च
नभसि प्रचकाशिरे ॥
The stars lost their glitter and planets their radiance. The shine of the star constellation of Viṡākha was dimmed by smog in the sky.
Special mention of Viṡākha was made because it was the star of Kōsala country.
2.41.13
कालिकानिलवेगेन
महोदधिरिवोत्थितः ।
रामे वनं प्रव्रजिते
नगरं प्रचचाल तत् ॥
Just as a mighty ocean would swell in turbulence fanned by the tearing winds of apocalypse, the entire city was shaken by the departure of Rāma to the Vana.
2.41.14
दिशः पर्याकुलास्सर्वाः
तिमिरेणेव संवृताः ।
न ग्रहो नापि नक्षत्रम्
प्रचकाशे नकिञ्चन ॥
The cardinals became disoriented, clouded by darkness. Nothing, not even the stars or planets, could be seen shining.
2.41.15
अकस्मान्नागरस्सर्वो
जनो दैन्यमुपागमत् ।
आहारे वा विहारे वा
न कश्चिदकरोन्मनः ॥
In no time, everyone in the city fell into a depressing gloom. And no one had the mind to eat or play.
2.41.16
शोकपर्यायसन्तप्तः
सततं दीर्घमुच्छ्वसन् ।
अयोध्यायां जनस्सर्वः
शुशोच जगतीपतिम् ॥
Everyone in Ayōdhyā, tormented by grief, heaving long sighs, felt sorry for the lord of the land.
2.41.17
बाष्पपर्याकुलमुखो
राजमार्गगतो जनः ।
न हृष्टो लक्ष्यते कश्चित्
सर्व श्शोकपरायणः ॥
The faces of the people on the royal pathway swelled with tears. No happiness could be seen anywhere. Everyone was overwhelmed by grief.
2.41.18
न वाति पवनश्शीतो
न शशी सौम्यदर्शनः ।
न सूर्यस्तपते लोकम्
सर्वं पर्याकुलं जगत् ॥
Neither was the breeze cool, nor the moon pleasant and nor was the sun was able to warm up the world. The entire world was agitated.
2.41.19
अनर्थिनस्सुताः स्त्रीणाम्
भर्तारो भ्रातरस्तथा ।
सर्वे सर्वं परित्यज्य
राममेवान्वचिन्तयन् ॥
Neither the sons, nor the husbands were thinking of the women. Nor were the brothers thinking of each other. Everyone gave up everything, thinking of nothing but Rāma.
2.41.20
ये तु रामस्य सुहृदः
सर्वे ते मूढचेतसः ।
शोकभारेण चाक्रान्ताः
शयनं न जहुस्तदा ॥
As for all the friends of Rāma, with minds stupefied and overwhelmed by grief, they could not even move from their beds.
2.41.21
ततस्त्वयोध्या रहिता महात्मना
पुरन्दरेणेव मही सपर्वता ।
चचाल घोरं भयशोकपीडिता
सनागयोधाश्वगणा ननाद च ॥
Ayōdhyā, bereft of that Mahātma, rocked frightfully under the shock of fear and grief, along with the elephantry, cavalry and infantry as the earth with its mountains might, bereft of the protection by Indra.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty first Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
