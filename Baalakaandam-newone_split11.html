Baala Kaanda - Sarga 11
In this Sarga, King Daṡaratha goes to the kingdom of Aṅga and requests Rōmapāda to send Ṡānta and Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga to Ayōdhyā. Rōmapāda sends Ṡānta and Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga accordingly. And everyone in Ayōdhyā receives them merrily.
See the contrast between this Sarga with the previous Sarga. In the previous Sarga, Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga is enticed to the kingdom of Aṅga in a friendly manner and without the permission of his father. In this Sarga, Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga is invited to the kingdom of Kōsala with full process and protocol and with the permission of his father-in-law. After all, success has many roads. It all depends on the context.
1.11.1
भूय एव च राजेन्द्र
शृणु मे वचनं हितम् ।
यथा स देवप्रवरः
कथायामेवमब्रवीत् ॥
O king, let me continue with the good words I heard from the distinguished Dēva (Sanat Kumara).
1.11.2
इक्ष्वाकूणां कुले जातो
भविष्यति सुधार्मिकः ।
राजा दशरथो नाम
श्रीमान् सत्यप्रतिश्रवः ॥
A well-endowed, Dharma-following king with firm allegiance to truth, by name Daṡaratha would be born in the lineage of Ikshwāku.
1.11.3
अङ्गराजेन सख्यं च
तस्य राज्ञो भविष्यति ।
कन्या चास्य महाभागा
शान्ता नाम भविष्यति ॥
He would become a friend of the king of Aṅga, who would have a daughter by name Ṡānta.
1.11.4
पुत्रस्त्वङ्गस्य राज्ञस्तु
रोमपाद इति श्रुतः ।
अङ्गराजं दशरथो
गमिष्यति महायशाः ॥
The famous King Daṡaratha would go to the king of Aṅga, known as Rōmapāda and would ask:
1.11.5
अनपत्योऽस्मि धर्मात्मन्
शान्ताभर्ता मम क्रतुम् ।
आहरेत त्वयाऽऽज्ञप्त:
सन्तानार्थं कुलस्य च ॥
"O Dharmātma, I am devoid of offspring. Please send the husband of Ṡānta to help me perform a Yāga that will bless our lineage with offspring."
1.11.6
श्रुत्वा राज्ञोऽथ तद्वाक्यम्
मनसा स विचिन्त्य च ।
प्रदास्यते पुत्रवन्तम्
शान्ताभर्तारमात्मवान् ॥
The considerate Rōmapāda, would think about the request and would send the husband of Ṡānta.
1.11.7
प्रतिगृह्य च तं विप्रम्
स राजा विगतज्वरः ।
आहरिष्यति तं यज्ञम्
प्रहृष्टेनान्तरात्मना ॥
Feeling relieved, the king would take that Brāhmaṇa with him and would perform the Yajña with a cheerful heart.
1.11.8
तं च राजा दशरथो
यष्टुकामः कृताञ्जलिः ।
ऋश्यशृङ्गं द्विजश्रेष्ठम्
वरयिष्यति धर्मवित् ।
यज्ञार्थं प्रसवार्थं च
स्वर्गार्थं च जनेश्वरः ।
लभते च स तं कामम्
विप्रमुख्याद्विशाम्पतिः ॥
The Dharma-following ruler of people, king Daṡaratha, desirous of performing a Yajña would request Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga, the eminent Brāhmaṇa, with folded hands to perform the Yajña that would bless him with offspring now and ascent to Heaven in the end. And his wishes would be fulfilled by the distinguished Brāhmaṇa.
1.11.10
पुत्राश्चास्य भविष्यन्ति
चत्वारोऽमितविक्रमाः ।
वंशप्रतिष्ठानकराः
सर्वलोकेषु विश्रुताः ॥
Four sons of immense prowess will be born to him who would be well regarded in all the worlds, and would bring glory and fame to their lineage.
1.11.11
एवं स देवप्रवरः
पूर्वं कथितवान् कथाम् ।
सनत्कुमारो भगवान्
पुरा देवयुगे प्रभुः ॥
Thus foretold Bhagawān Sanat Kumara, the distinguished among Dēvas, during the times of Dēva Yuga.
Rāma lived in Trētā Yuga, the second of the four Yugas (ages). The first Yuga is known as Kṛta Yuga or Dēva Yuga.
1.11.12
स त्वं पुरुषशार्दूल
तमानय सुसत्कृतम् ।
स्वयमेव च राजेन्द्र
गत्वा सबलवाहनः ॥
O tiger among men! You should go in person along with fitting vehicles and retinue and invite him (Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga) with full honors.
The lives of kings, administrators, managers, diplomats and such are regularly laden with protocols and processes.
Ṡlōkas like these, which are about protocols and processes, will resonate easily with people with similar bent of mind. But for others, it feels artificial and heavy.
1.11.13
अनुमान्य वसिष्ठं च
सूतवाक्यं निशम्य च ।
सान्तःपुरः सहामात्यः
प्रययौ यत्र स द्विजः ॥
On that advice from Sumantra and after receiving due consent from Vasishṭha, the king departed to the Brāhmaṇa's place along with ministers and confidants.
1.11.14
वनानि सरितश्चैव
व्यतिक्रम्य शनैः शनैः ।
अभिचक्राम तं देशम्
यत्र वै मुनिपुङ्गवः ॥
After crossing many rivers and forests, he reached the kingdom in which the Brāhmaṇa lived.
1.11.15
आसाद्य तं द्विजश्रेष्ठम्
रोमपादसमीपगम् ।
ऋषिपुत्रं ददर्शादौ
दीप्यमानमिवानलम् ॥
Immediately, he visited the distinguished Brāhmaṇa, and the son of the Ṛshi, who was glowing with fiery brilliance, seated next to the king Rōmapāda.
1.11.16
ततो राजा यथान्यायम्
पूजां चक्रे विशेषतः ।
सखित्वात्तस्य वै राज्ञः
प्रहृष्टेनान्तरात्मना ॥
That king rejoicing on seeing him, received him with all ceremonial honors and paid obeisance and respects in excess of what custom demanded, to his good friend and the visiting dignitary.
1.11.17
रोमपादेन चाख्यातम्
ऋषिपुत्राय धीमते ।
सख्यं सम्बन्धकं चैव
तदा तं प्रत्यपूजयत् ॥
Rōmapāda told about their friendship and relationship to the sagacious son of the Ṛshi who greeted him respectfully.
1.11.18
एवं सुसत्कृतस्तेन
सहोषित्वा नरर्षभः ।
सप्ताष्टदिवसान् राजा
राजानमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Daṡaratha felt very happy with the hospitality of Rōmapāda. After staying there for seven to eight days, he said to Rōmapāda:
1.11.19
शान्ता तव सुता राजन्
सह भर्त्रा विशाम्पते ।
मदीयं नगरं यातु
कार्यं हि महदुद्यतम् ॥
O king, the ruler of people! I have set out on a great endeavor. Can I have your daughter Ṡānta along with her husband in my city?
1.11.20
तथेति राजा संश्रुत्य
गमनं तस्य धीमतः ।
उवाच वचनं विप्रम्
गच्छ त्वं सह भार्यया ॥
Rōmapāda agreed to send the sagacious Brāhmaṇa and asked him to go along with his wife.
It is worth recalling my commentary about the feminists towards the end of the previous Sarga. Just as Ṡānta's consent was not sought before marrying her off to Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga, Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga's consent was not sought about his willingness (or otherwise) to go with Daṡaratha.
Who should seek whose consent? In a democratic model, everyone should seek everyone's consent. But in a hierarchical model, such as in a business division, in a military unit or in a large joint family, the person in-charge decides. And it is in his or her own best interest to make meaningful, respectful and judicious decisions.
1.11.21
ऋषिपुत्रः प्रतिश्रुत्य
तथेत्याह नृपं तदा ।
स नृपेणाभ्यनुज्ञातः
प्रययौ सह भार्यया ॥
The son of the Ṛshi agreed and with the permission of the king, departed along with his wife.
1.11.22
तावन्योन्याञ्जलिं कृत्वा
स्नेहात्संश्लिष्य चोरसा ।
ननन्दतुर्दशरथो
रोमपादश्च वीर्यवान् ॥
Daṡaratha and the valiant Rōmapāda held each other in a tight embrace with each one's heart touching the other's in celebration of their long friendship.
"A friend in need is a friend indeed." goes the saying. Both giving help and receiving help renews and rejuvenates long lasting friendships. It is indeed a moment for celebration and exhilaration.
Wish you had some moments like these in your life and I wish you more of them!
1.11.23
ततः सुहृदमापृच्छ्य
प्रस्थितो रघुनन्दनः ।
पौरेभ्यः प्रेषयामास
दूतान् वै शीघ्रगामिनः ।
क्रियतां नगरं सर्वम्
क्षिप्रमेव स्वलङ्कृतम् ।
धूपितं सिक्तसम्मृष्टम्
पताकाभिरलङ्कृतम् ॥
The descendant of Raghu (Daṡaratha) bid farewell to his friend and started on his return journey. He sent swift messengers ahead of him, to tell the people of his city to wash and clean the city and fill it with scented vapors and decorate it with flags.
The instinct to celebrate successes with decoration is universal and defies the constraints of time and place.
The description in these Ṡlōkas feels like that of a home coming ceremony in honor and celebration of winning sports teams. Or perhaps, more.
1.11.25
ततः प्रहृष्टाः पौरास्ते
श्रुत्वा राजानमागतम् ।
तथा प्रचक्रुस्तत्सर्वम्
राज्ञा यत्प्रेषितं तदा ॥
The people of the city were excited about the return of the king and did all that the king told them to do.
Vālmeeki's style is interesting. He moves the story in only sketchy detail, but seems to pause to describe the moods (ups and downs) of the people in great detail and in a beautiful manner. This style is in common with that of many art forms like Kūcipūdi and the musicals of Broadway.
And there is a reason. Events are incidental and experience is real. That is what is called 'Rasa', in the Indic tradition.
Kāvyam Rasa Pradhānam - the essence of a Kāvya is Rasa - the experience of human emotions. The question in and about life is not how you got here, but whether or not you are able to experience it in its fullness.
1.11.26
ततः स्वलङ्कृतं राजा
नगरं प्रविवेश ह ।
शङ्खदुन्दुभिर्निर्घोषैः
पुरस्कृत्य द्विजर्षभम् ॥
The king, then, entered the well-decorated city, with that eminent Brāhmaṇa in front of him, amidst blares of conches and blasts of drums.
1.11.27
ततः प्रमुदिताः सर्वे
दृष्ट्वा तं नागरा द्विजम् ।
प्रवेश्यमानं सत्कृत्य
नरेन्द्रेणेन्द्रकर्मणा ॥
All the people of the city merrily greeted the Brāhmaṇa who was duly honored and introduced by the king in a manner befitting the king of Dēvas.
1.11.28
अन्तःपुरं प्रवेश्यैनम्
पूजां कृत्वा विधानतः ।
कृतकृत्यं तदाऽऽत्मानम्
मेने तस्योपवाहनात् ॥
He took him to the inner quarters of the palace and did Pūja according to customs and tradition. He felt accomplished with the arrival (of Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga).
1.11.29
अन्तःपुरस्त्रियः सर्वाः
शान्तां दृष्ट्वा तथाऽऽगताम्  ।
सह भर्त्रा विशालाक्षीम्
प्रीत्याऽऽनन्दमुपागमन् ॥
The women of the palace felt very happy to see the wide-eyed and endearing Ṡānta who came along with her husband.
1.11.30
पूज्यमाना च ताभिः सा
राज्ञा चैव विशेषतः ।
उवास तत्र सुखिता
कञ्चित्कालं सहर्त्विजा ॥
Ṡānta, along with the Ṛtvik (Ṛṡyaṡṛṅga) lived there for a while, enjoying the hospitality given by them, and especially by the king.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे एकादशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eleventh Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
