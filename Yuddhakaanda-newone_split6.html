Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 6
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa, humbled by the havoc wrought by Hanumān in Laṅkā, asks all the Rākshasas to think about the best strategy that they should adopt to counter Rāma. He warns them that it is certain that Rāma will somehow cross the ocean and come along with the Vānara army to lay siege on Laṅkā.
6.6.1
लङ्कायां तु कृतं कर्म
घोरं दृष्ट्वा भयावहम् ।
राक्षसेन्द्रो हनुमता
शक्रेणेव महात्मना ।
अब्रवीद्राक्षसान् सर्वान्
ह्रिया किञ्चिदवाङ्मुखः ॥
Having seen the terrible havoc wrought by Hanumān in Laṅkā, like it would have been by the mighty Ṡakra, the Lord of Rākshasas, humbled and face turned slightly down, said to all the Rākshasas:
6.6.2
धर्षिता च प्रविष्टा च
लङ्का दुष्प्रसहा पुरी ।
तेन वानरमात्रेण
दृष्टा सीता च जानकी ॥
The invincible city of Laṅkā was challenged and trespassed, and Seetā, the daughter of Janaka was also located, by a mere Vānara!
6.6.3
प्रासादो धर्षितश्चैत्यः
प्रवरा राक्षसा हताः ।
आविला च पुरी लङ्का
सर्वा हनुमता कृता ॥
The palatial shrine was attacked, prominent Rākshasas were killed, and the entire city of Laṅkā was thrown into turmoil by Hanumān.
6.6.4
किं करिष्यामि भद्रं वः
किं वा युक्तमनन्तरम् ।
उच्यतां नः समर्थं यत्
कृतं च सुकृतं भवेत् ॥
What shall I do? What is the right thing to do next? Please suggest what would be most effective, and will turn out right, when done. May all bode well for you!
6.6.5
मन्त्रमूलं हि विजयम्
प्राहुरार्या मनस्विनः ।
तस्माद्वै रोचये मन्त्रम्
रामं प्रति महाबलाः ॥
The noble and thoughtful say, ‘at the root of victory is the right strategy’; Hence, O you of immense strength, I would like to think of a strategy to counter Rāma.
6.6.6
त्रिविधाः पुरुषा लोके
उत्तमाधममध्यमाः ।
तेषां तु समवेतानाम्
गुणदोषौ वदाम्यहम् ॥
There are three kinds of men in this world, the best, the worst and the ordinary. Let me tell how they differ in their virtues and vices.
6.6.7
मन्त्रिभिर्हितसंयुक्तैः
समर्थैर्मन्त्रनिर्णये ।
मित्रैर्वापि समानार्थैः
बान्धवैरपि वाधिकैः ।
सहितो मन्त्रयित्वा यः
कर्मारम्भान् प्रवर्तयेत् ।
दैवे च कुरुते यत्नम्
तमाहुः पुरुषोत्तमम् ॥
He who begins an enterprise after deliberating with well-wishing advisors capable of deciding a strategy, or with friends who share the same object, or with a good number of relatives, and further tries to invoke the blessing of Providence, is said to be the best among spirited beings.
6.6.9
एकोऽर्थं विमृशेदेको
धर्मे प्रकुरुते मनः ।
एकः कार्याणि कुरुते
तमाहुर्मध्यमं नरम् ॥
He who deliberates over an object all alone, decides what is Dharma all by himself and also does things all by himself is said to be an ordinary man.
6.6.10
गुणदोषावनिश्चित्य
त्यक्त्वा धर्मव्यपाश्रयम् ।
करिष्यामीति यः कार्यम्
उपेक्षेत् स नराधमः ॥
He who plans on doing things without due deliberation of the pros and cons and giving up all sense of Dharma only to let things slip at the end is the worst of men.
6.6.11
यथेमे पुरुषा नित्यम्
उत्तमाधममध्यमाः ।
एवं मन्त्रा हि विज्ञेया
उत्तमाधममध्यमाः ॥
Just as there are best, ordinary and worst among men, it should be noted that there are best, ordinary and worst among strategies.
6.6.12
ऐकमत्यमुपागम्य
शास्त्रदृष्टेन चक्षुषा ।
मन्त्रिणो यत्र निरताः
तमाहुर्मन्त्रमुत्तमम् ॥
That strategy, which the advisors, coming together, enthusiastically endorse after examining it under the lens of the science (of polity), is said to be the best.
6.6.13
बह्वीरपि मतीर्गत्वा
मन्त्रिणामर्थनिर्णयः ।
पुनर्यत्रैकतां प्राप्तः
स मन्त्रो मध्यमः स्मृतः ॥
That strategy, which meets with diverse reactions from the advisors who are responsible for making the decision, but finally gets everyone’s endorsement, is said to be ordinary.
6.6.14
अन्योन्यं मतिमास्थाय
यत्र सम्प्रतिभाष्यते ।
न चैकमत्ये श्रेयोऽस्ति
मन्त्रः सोऽधम उच्यते ॥
That strategy, which attracts comments that diverge from each other, and which does not enjoy the strength of unity of minds, is said to be the worst strategy.
6.6.15
तस्मात् सुमन्त्रितं साधु
भवन्तो मतिमत्तमाः ।
कार्यं सम्प्रतिपद्यन्ताम्
एतत् कृत्यं मतं मम ॥
Therefore, you, the best among the strategists, should decide upon the best course of action; and I would like to just do accordingly.
6.6.16
वानराणां हि धीराणाम्
सहस्रैः परिवारितः ।
रामोऽभ्येति पुरीं लङ्काम्
अस्माकमुपरोधकः ॥
Followed by thousands of brave Vānaras, Rāma is coming to lay siege on our city of Laṅkā.
6.6.17
तरिष्यति च सुव्यक्तम्
राघवः सागरं सुखम् ।
तरसा युक्तरूपेण
सानुजः सबलानुगः ।
समुद्रमुच्छोषयति
वीर्येणान्यत् करोति वा ॥
It is very clear that Rāghava can comfortably cross the ocean with his strength and by appropriate means, with his brother and the army following him. He is powerful enough to dry up the ocean or find some other means (to cross it).
6.6.19
अस्मिन्नेवङ्गते कार्ये
विरुद्धे वानरैः सह ।
हितं पुरे च सैन्ये च
सर्वं सम्मन्त्र्यतां मम ॥
Now, that being so, and our having also earned the enmity of Vānaras, please think over about everything as to what is good for my city and my army.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे षष्ठः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
