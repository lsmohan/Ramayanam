Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 58
In this Sarga, Rāma expresses his fear to Lakshmaṇa that Seetā may not be safe, and he would give up life if Seetā does not greet him with a smile when he reaches the Āṡrama.
3.58.1
स दृष्ट्वा लक्ष्मणं दीनम्
शून्ये दशरथात्मजः ।
पर्यपृच्छत धर्मात्मा
वैदेहीमागतं विना ॥
On seeing Lakshmaṇa who was downcast and had come leaving Vaidēhee alone, the son of Daṡaratha, the Dharmātma, asked him reflectively:
3.58.2
प्रस्थितं दण्डकारण्यम्
या मामनुजगाम ह ।
क्व सा लक्ष्मण वैदेही
यां हित्वा त्वमिहाऽऽगतः ॥
O Lakshmaṇa! Where is the Vaidēhee who followed me as I set out to Daṇḍaka Araṇya and whom you left alone and came here!
3.58.3
राज्यभ्रष्टस्य दीनस्य
दण्डकान्परिधावतः ।
क्व सा दुःखसहाया मे
वैदेही तनुमध्यमा ॥
Where is the Vaidēhee of slender waist who stood by the miserable me during the difficult times when I lost the kingdom and ran to Daṇḍaka!
Rāma is at the worst of his times now. He is certain now that he has lost Seetā. He feels terrible about what happened.
He has more than one thing to feel bad about. He feels bad that he could not protect Seetā. He feels bad about her plight. He feels bad about not having her with him. In short, he thinks of himself as an idiot.
Consequently, he sees even his own past in a bad light. Instead of seeing himself as one who came to the Daṇḍaka to make his father a man of his word, or one who killed fourteen thousand Rākshasas singlehandedly, he now sees himself as an incapable and unworthy person, who was just allowed to go from the kingdom, unceremoniously.
3.58.4
यां विना नोत्सहे वीर
मुहूर्तमपि जीवितुम् ।
क्व सा प्राणसहाया मे
सीता सुरसुतोपमा ॥
Where is the Seetā, prop of my life, who is like the daughter of Suras without whom I would not like to live even for a short while?
3.58.5
पतित्वममराणां वा
पृथिव्याश्चापि लक्ष्मण ।
तां विना तपनीयाभाम्
नेच्छेयं जनकात्मजाम् ॥
Where is the daughter of Janaka, of golden complexion, without whom, O Veera, I would not care to enjoy even the lordship over the earth or over the Dēvas!
3.58.6
कच्चिज्जीवति वैदेही
प्राणैः प्रियतरा मम ।
कच्चित्प्रव्राजनं सौम्य
न मे मिथ्या भविष्यति ॥
My dear, I hope that Vaidēhee, who is dearer than life to me, is alive! I hope that my exile to Vana is not a failure!
3.58.7
सीतानिमित्तं सौमित्रे
मृते मयि गते त्वयि ।
कच्चित्सकामा सुखिता
कैकेयी सा भविष्यति ॥
I hope, O Sowmitri, I would not be dead because of Seetā and you would not have to go back (alone) making Kaikēyee happy and her wish fulfilled!
3.58.8
सपुत्रराज्यां सिद्धार्थाम्
मृतपुत्रा तपस्विनी ।
उपस्थास्यतिकौसल्या
कच्चित्सौम्य न केकयीम् ॥
I hope, my dear, with her son dead, the grieving Kousalyā would not have to serve Kaikēyee who would have her wish fulfilled in having her son and kingdom to herself!
3.58.9
यदि जीवति वैदेही
गमिष्याम्याश्रमं पुनः ।
सुवृत्ता यदि वा वृत्ता
प्राणांस्त्यक्ष्यामि लक्ष्मण ॥
I can go back to the Āṡrama only if Vaidēhee is alive. If she of great character has died, I would (also) give up my life, O Lakshmaṇa!
3.58.10
यदि मामाश्रमगतम्
वैदेही नाभिभाषते ।
पुनः प्रहसिता सीता
विनशिष्यामि लक्ष्मण ॥
I will all but be destroyed, O Lakshmaṇa, if Seetā, the princess of Vidēha, is not (there) to greet me with a smile when I reach the Āṡrama!
3.58.11
ब्रूहि लक्ष्मण वैदेही
यदि जीवति वा न वा ।
त्वयि प्रमत्ते रक्षोभिः
भक्षिता वा तपस्विनी ॥
Tell me, Lakshmaṇa, is Vaidēhee alive or not? I hope she, the despondent one, was not devoured by the Rākshasas, because you were not on full alert!
3.58.12
सुकुमारी च बाला च
नित्यं चादुःखदर्शिनी ।
मद्वियोगेन वैदेही
व्यक्तं शोचति दुर्मनाः ॥
The young and delicate Vaidēhee, a stranger to sadness, must be feeling terrible and crying, being separated from me!
3.58.13
सर्वथा रक्षसा तेन
जिह्मेन सुदुरात्मना ।
वदता लक्ष्मणेत्युच्चैः
तवापि जनितं भयम् ॥
Understandably, fear must have been engendered in you when the crooked and wicked Rākshasa let off the loud cry calling out, ‘O Lakshmaṇa!’

It is possible that some manuscripts had this short version while others had the long version and both are preserved by the scholars. This short version can be considered redundant, in light of the availability of the long version.
3.58.14
श्रुतस्तु शङ्के वैदेह्या
स स्वरस्सदृशो मम ।
त्रस्तया प्रेषितस्त्वं च
द्रष्टुं मां शीघ्रमागतः ॥
I fear that Vaidēhee heard that voice, so like mine, and you came here in all haste to see me, sent by her in her worry.
3.58.15
सर्वथा तु कृतं कष्टम्
सीतामुत्सृजता वने ।
प्रतिकर्तुं नृशंसानाम्
रक्षसां दत्तमन्तरम् ॥
By leaving her alone in the Vana you have caused great trouble in many ways. It gives a chance for those cruel Rākshasas to take revenge.
3.58.16
दुःखिताः खरघातेन
राक्षसाः पिशिताशनाः ।
तैस्सीता निहता घोरैः
भविष्यति न संशयः ॥
The flesh-eating Rākshasas are very sad because Khara was killed; Seetā will be killed by those horrid creatures, without a doubt.
3.58.17
अहोऽस्मिन् व्यसने मग्नः
सर्वथा शत्रुसूदन ।
किं न्विदानीं करिष्यामि
शङ्के प्राप्तव्यमीदृशम् ॥
Such a predicament has befallen and I am completely sunk in this misery; O destroyer of foes, what shall I do!
3.58.18
इति सीतां वरारोहाम्
चिन्तयन्नेव राघवः ।
आजगाम जनस्थानम्
त्वरया सह लक्ष्मणः ॥
Worried thus about Seetā of excellent Kaṭee, Rāghava reached Janasthāna quickly, along with Lakshmaṇa.
3.58.19
विगर्हमाणोऽनुजमार्तरूपं
क्षुधा श्रमाच्चैव पिपासया च ।
विनिश्श्वसन् शुष्कमुखो विवर्णः
प्रतिश्रयं प्राप्य समीक्ष्य शून्यम् ।
स्वमाश्रमं सम्प्रविगाह्य वीरो
विहारदेशाननुसृत्य कांश्चित् ।
एतत्तदित्येव निवासभूमौ
प्रहृष्टरोमा व्यथितो बभूव ॥
Reproaching his younger brother who was like an embodiment of distress, that Veera reached his Āṡrama area, with his mouth parched and his face pale, and breathing heavily from hunger, fatigue and thirst.
Finding the place empty, and ensuring it indeed was his Āṡrama, by visiting the areas where they used to spend time enjoying and the grounds where they used to live, he sank in agony, his hair standing on its end.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे अष्टपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty eighth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
