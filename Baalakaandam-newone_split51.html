Baala Kaanda - Sarga 51
In this Sarga, Ṡatānanda, the Purōhita of Janaka, inquires about the reunion of his mother Ahalya with his father Goutama. He begins telling the story of the great Muni Viṡwāmitra to Rāma.
1.51.1
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
विश्वामित्रस्य धीमतः ।
हृष्टरोमा महातेजाः
शतानन्दो महातपाः ।
गौतमस्य सुतो ज्येष्ठः
तपसा द्योतितप्रभः ।
रामसन्दर्शनादेव
परं विस्मयमागतः ॥
Hearing those words of sagacious Viṡwāmitra, the supremely radiant great Tapasvi Ṡatānanda, the eldest son of Goutama, glistening with Tapa, had goose bumps. And he was overwhelmed by seeing Rāma.
Once someone pays for his or her crime or mistake, he or she should be treated as if the crime or mistake had never been committed.
In the case of Ahalya, an unthinkable transgression occurred. But she paid for it through Tapa.
How should she be treated after the Tapa? In Sarga 49, we have seen exactly how Goutama received her back.
In the first part of this Sarga, we witness how Ṡatānanda, the son of Ahalya and Goutama, speaks of her.
1.51.3
स तौ निषण्णौ सम्प्रेक्ष्य
सुखासीनौ नृपात्मजौ ।
शतानन्दो मुनिश्रेष्ठम्
विश्वामित्रमथाब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing those duo of princes, sitting next to him, Ṡatānanda said to Viṡwāmitra, the best among Munis:
1.51.4
अपि ते मुनिशार्दूल
मम माता यशस्विनी ।
दर्शिता राजपुत्राय
तपोदीर्घमुपागता ॥
O tiger among Munis! Have you shown my renowned mother, who has performed long Tapa, to these princes?
1.51.5
अपि रामे महातेजा
मम माता यशस्विनी ।
वन्यैरुपाहरत्पूजाम्
पूजार्हे सर्वदेहिनाम् ॥
Did my radiant and reputed mother serve Rāma, who is worthy of being honored by all the peoples, with all the best products of the forest?
1.51.6
अपि रामाय कथितम्
यथावृत्तं पुरातनम् ।
मम मातुर्महातेजो
दैवेन दुरनुष्ठितम् ॥
O supremely radiant one! Have you told Rāma about all that happened to my supremely radiant mother who had committed wrongful acts, driven by destiny?
1.51.7
अपि कौशिक भद्रं ते
गुरुणा मम सङ्गता ।
माता मम मुनिश्रेष्ठ
रामसन्दर्शनादितः ॥
O Kouṡika, best among Munis! May all bode well for you! Is my mother with my father since the time she has seen Rāma?
1.51.8
अपि मे गुरुणा रामः
पूजितः कुशिकात्मज ।
इहागतो महातेजाः
पूजां प्राप्तो महात्मनः ॥
O son of Kuṡika! Did my father pay respects to Rāma? Was my supremely radiant father who came there, received well by Mahātma Rāma?
1.51.9
अपि शान्तेन मनसा
गुरुर्मे कुशिकात्मज ।
इहागतेन रामेण
प्रयतेनाभिवादितः ॥
O son of Kuṡika! Was my father received and respected by the pious Rāma with a calm mind and consideration?
1.51.10
तच्छ्रुत्वा वचनं तस्य
विश्वामित्रो महामुनिः ।
प्रत्युवाच शतानन्दम्
वाक्यज्ञो वाक्यकोविदम् ॥
In response to those questions, the great, eloquent Muni Viṡwāmitra said to the astute Ṡatānanda:
1.51.11
नातिक्रान्तं मुनिश्रेष्ठ
यत्कर्तव्यं कृतं मया ।
सङ्गता मुनिना पत्नी
भार्गवेणेव रेणुका ॥
O best among Munis! Nothing was overlooked. Everything that needs to be done was done by me. The wife was reunited with the Muni, like Rēṇukā was with Jamadagni.
1.51.12
तच्छ्रुत्वा वचनं तस्य
विश्वामित्रस्य भाषितम् ।
शतानन्दो महातेजा
रामं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
After hearing those comforting words of Viṡwāmitra the supremely radiant Ṡatānanda said to Rāma:
1.51.13
स्वागतं ते नरश्रेष्ठ
दिष्ट्या प्राप्तोऽसि राघव ।
विश्वामित्रं पुरस्कृत्य
महर्षिमपराजितम् ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Best among men! Welcome to you! By the grace of Providence you are led by the invincible Maharshi Viṡwāmitra!
The story of Viṡwāmitra starts here.
1.51.14
अचिन्त्यकर्मा तपसा
ब्रह्मर्षिरतुलप्रभः ।
विश्वामित्रो महातेजा
वेत्स्येनं परमां गतिम् ॥
The supremely radiant Viṡwāmitra is a Brahmarshi. His achievements are unfathomable. His luminance by virtue of Tapa is unparalleled. It is great that you are seeing the ultimate guide in him!
1.51.15
नास्ति धन्यतरो राम
त्वत्तोऽन्यो भुवि कश्चन ।
गोप्ता कुशिकपुत्रस्ते
येन तप्तं महत्तपः ॥
O Rāma! No one is as fortunate as you on this earth. You are being protected by the son of Kuṡika, who is wrought by Tapa.
1.51.16
श्रूयतामभिधास्यामि
कौशिकस्य महात्मनः ।
यथा बलं यथा वृत्तम्
तन्मे निगदतः शृणु ॥
Let me tell you about the life story and fortitude of this Mahātma, the son of Kuṡika.
1.51.17
राजाभूदेष धर्मात्मा
दीर्घकालमरिन्दमः ।
धर्मज्ञः कृतविद्यश्च
प्रजानां च हिते रतः ॥
He used to be a king for a long time, fully engrossed in the welfare of his people, well-versed in all subjects and living by Dharma.
1.51.18
प्रजापतिसुतस्त्वासीत्
कुशो नाम महीपतिः ।
कुशस्य पुत्रो बलवान्
कुशनाभः सुधार्मिकः ॥
There used to be a king by name Kuṡa, the son of Prajāpati. Kuṡanābha, who was strong and a Dhārmika, was the son of Kuṡa.
1.51.19
कुशनाभसुतस्त्वासीत्
गाधिरित्येव विश्रुतः ।
गाधेः पुत्रो महातेजा
विश्वामित्रो महामुनिः ॥
Kuṡanābha's son was the renowned Gādhi. Gādhi's son is this supremely radiant great Muni Viṡwāmitra.
1.51.20
विश्वामित्रो महातेजाः
पालयामास मेदिनीम् ।
बहुवर्षसहस्राणि
राजा राज्यमकारयत् ॥
The supremely radiant Viṡwāmitra ruled the earth for many thousands of years.
1.51.21
कदाचित्तु महातेजा
योजयित्वा वरूथिनीम् ।
अक्षौहिणीपरिवृतः
परिचक्राम मेदिनीम् ॥
Once upon a time, that supremely radiant Viṡwāmitra went around the earth with an army of Akshouhiṇi.
An Akshouhiṇi consists of 109,350 soldiers, 65,610 horses, 21,870 elephants and 21,870 chariots.
1.51.22
नगराणि च राष्ट्राणि
सरितश्च तथा गिरीन् ।
आश्रमान् क्रमशो राम
विचरन्नाजगाम ह ।
वसिष्ठस्याश्रमपदम्
नानावृक्षसुमाकुलम् ।
नानामृगगणाकीर्णम्
सिद्धचारणसेवितम् ।
देवदानवगन्धर्वैः
किन्नरैरुपशोभितम् ।
प्रशान्तहरिणाकीर्णम्
द्विजसङ्घनिषेवितम् ।
ब्रह्मर्षिगणसङ्कीर्णम्
देवर्षिगणसेवितम् ।
तपश्चरणसंसिद्धैः
अग्निकल्पैर्महात्मभिः ।
अब्भक्षैर्वायुभक्षैश्च
शीर्णपर्णाशनैस्तथा ।
फलमूलाशनैर्दान्तैः
जितरोषैर्जितेन्द्रियैः ।
ऋषिभिर्वालखिल्यैश्च
जपहोमपरायणैः ।
अन्यैर्वैखानसैश्चैव
समन्तादुपशोभितम् ॥
O Rāma! he went around seeing many states, cities, mountains, rivers and Āṡramas and finally reached the Āṡrama of Vasishṭha.
It was full of varieties of trees, diverse species of animals, many types of birds and deer roaming freely without fear.
It was adorned by Dēvas, Dānavas, Gandharvas and Kinnaras. It was teeming with Siddhas, Cāraṇas, Brahmarshis and Dēvarshis.
There were many Mahātmas there, comparable to fire, who gained Siddhi after doing long Tapa. Some of them were living only on fruits and roots, some on dried leaves shed by the trees, some on liquids, and some breathing only air. They completely overcame anger and gained complete control over their minds and senses.
The Āṡrama was shining with many Ṛshis, Vālakhilyas, Vaikhanasas and many others who were dedicated to Japa and Hōma.
Vālmeeki is taking us here, into the splendid Āṡrama of Vasishṭha.
1.51.28
वसिष्ठस्याश्रमपदम्
ब्रह्मलोकमिवापरम् ।
ददर्श जयतां श्रेष्ठो
विश्वामित्रो महाबलः ॥
The mighty Viṡwāmitra, the foremost among the victorious, saw that Āṡrama of Vasishṭha that looked like another Brahma Lōka.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे एकपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty first Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
