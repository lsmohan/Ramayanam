Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 21
In this Sarga, Rāma lies down on the shore of the ocean, resolute on giving up his life, if the ocean does not appear in front of him, and reveal to him an Upāya to cross it. Even after lying down thus, observing rigorous discipline for three days, the ocean does not appear to him in person. Seeing his gentle pleadings go in vain, Rāma becomes annoyed with the ocean and shoots fierce arrows into it, setting it in turmoil. The worried Lakshmaṇa asks him to refrain and so do the Ṛshis from above in the sky.
6.21.1
ततः सागरवेलायाम्
दर्भानास्तीर्य राघवः ।
अञ्जलिं प्राङ्मुखः कृत्वा
प्रतिशिश्ये महोदधेः ।
बाहुं भुजगभोगाभम्
उपधायारिसूदनः ।
जातरूपमयैश्चैव
भूषणैर्भूषितं पुरा ॥
Spreading Darbha on the shore of the ocean, paying respects to the magnificent ocean with his palms joined in reverence, Rāghava, the destroyer of foe turned to the east and lay down making his arm, which was like a serpent’s body and which, once upon a time, was adorned with gold ornaments, as his pillow.
6.21.3
मणिकाञ्चनकेयूर
मुक्ताप्रवरभूषणैः ।
भुजैः परमनारीणाम्
अभिमृष्टमनेकधा ॥
That arm felt every kind of touch of women’s hands, which themselves were decked with splendid shoulder-rings of gold ornaments set with precious pearl and gem.
This Ṡlōka could be about the arm of Rāvaṇa, more apt to be part of tenth Sarga of Sundara Kāṇḍa, but could be appearing here due to a shuffle of the palm leaves.
6.21.4
चन्दनागरुभिश्चैव
पुरस्तादभिसेवितम् ।
बालसूर्यप्रतीकाशैः
चन्दनैरुपशोभितम् ॥
That arm, once upon a time, was smeared with sandal and Agaru. It had glowed like the rising sun with the rich hues of sandal paste.
6.21.5
शयने चोत्तमाङ्गेन
सीतायाः शोभितं पुरा ।
तक्षकस्येव सम्भोगम्
गङ्गाजलनिषेवितम् ॥
That arm, which was splendid like the torso of Takshaka in the waters of Gaṅgā, once upon a time, supported Seetā’s head in bed.
6.21.6
संयुगे युगसङ्काशम्
शत्रूणां शोकवर्द्धनम् ।
सुहृदानन्दनं दीर्घम्
सागरान्तव्यपाश्रयम् ॥
Strong like a yoke, it added to the misery of enemies on the battlefield. Long enough to extend protection to the entire sea-girded earth, it gladdened the hearts of friends.
6.21.7
अस्यता च पुनः सव्यम्
ज्याघातविगतत्वचम् ।
दक्षिणो दक्षिणं बाहुम्
महापरिघसन्निभम् ॥
And the left arm had its skin peeled off bruised by the arrows he shot repeatedly, while the right arm of that capable one was strong like a Parigha.
6.21.8
गोसहस्रप्रदातारम्
उपधाय महद्भुजम् ।
अद्य मे मरणं वाऽथ
तरणं सागरस्य वा ।
इति रामो धृतिं कृत्वा
महाबाहुर्महोदधिम् ।
अधिशिश्ये स विधिवत्
प्रयतोऽत्र स्थितो मुनिः ॥
With that hand, which had gifted away thousands of cows, for his pillow, Rāma of mighty arm, lay down by the great ocean, observing rigorous discipline in a customary manner like a Muni, resolved firmly in his mind, ‘I have to, today, either find a way to cross the ocean, or give up my life.’
6.21.10
तस्य रामस्य सुप्तस्य
कुशास्तीर्णे महीतले ।
नियमादप्रमत्तस्य
निशास्तिस्रोऽतिचक्रमुः ॥
Three nights passed, as he slept on the ground covered with Kuṡa grass without a lapse in his regimen of observances.
6.21.11
स त्रिरात्रोषितस्तत्र
नयज्ञो धर्मवत्सलः ।
उपासत तदा रामः
सागरं सरितां पतिम् ॥
He who was well-versed in statecraft, and who loved Dharma, prayed to the ocean, the lord of streams, spending three nights thus.
6.21.12
न च दर्शयते रूपम्
मन्दो रामस्य सागरः ।
प्रयतेनापि रामेण
यथार्हमभिपूजितः ॥
But the lord of the ocean, was dull-witted enough not to appear to Rāma, though Rāma prayed to him with all deference that the lord of the ocean deserves.
6.21.13
समुद्रस्य ततः क्रुद्धो
रामो रक्तान्तलोचनः ।
समीपस्थमुवाचेदम्
लक्ष्मणं शुभलक्षणम् ॥
Then the annoyed Rāma, the ends of whose eyes turned red, said this to Lakshmaṇa of auspicious marks, who stood beside him, regarding the ocean:
6.21.14
अवलेपः समुद्रस्य
न दर्शयति यत्स्वयम् ।
प्रशमश्च क्षमा चैव
आर्जवं प्रियवादिता ।
असामर्थ्यं फलन्त्येते
निर्गुणेषु सतां गुणाः ॥
How arrogant is this ocean, not to appear in his original form? The virtues like serenity, forbearance, honesty and gentle speech, of good men are in vain with the virtue-less.
6.21.15
आत्मप्रशंसिनं दुष्टम्
धृष्टं विपरिधावकम् ।
सर्वत्रोत्सृष्टदण्डं च
लोकः सत्कुरुते नरम् ॥
This world seems to revere a resolute but evil man who constantly brags about himself and jumps from one thing to another, and who deals out punishment left and right.
6.21.16
न साम्ना शक्यते कीर्तिः
न साम्ना शक्यते यशः ।
प्राप्तुं लक्ष्मण लोकेऽस्मिन्
जयो वा रणमूर्द्धनि ॥
O Lakshmaṇa, neither lasting fame nor good repute can be earned in this world, it seems, by being gentle, just as success cannot be, in the front lines of the battlefield.
6.21.17
अद्य मद्बाणनिर्भिन्नैः
मकरैर्मकरालयम् ।
निरुद्धतोयं सौमित्रे
प्लवद्भिः पश्य सर्वतः ॥
You shall see this day, O Sowmitri, the waters of this abode of sea-monsters covered with carcasses of crocodiles that my arrows have cleft.
6.21.18
महाभोगानि मत्स्यानाम्
करिणां च करानिव ।
भोगिनां पश्य भोगानि
मया छिन्नानि लक्ष्मण ॥
You shall see, O Lakshmaṇa, bodies of fish, huge carcasses of great serpents that resemble trunks of elephants, torn into bits by my arrows.
6.21.19
सशङ्खशुक्तिकाजालम्
समीनमकरं शरैः ।
अद्य युद्धेन महता
समुद्रं परिशोषये ॥
I shall wage a mighty war and dry up the waters of this ocean with its huge mass of conch shells and oyster shells and its fish and crocodiles.
6.21.20
क्षमया हि समायुक्तम्
मामयं मकरालयः ।
असमर्थं विजानाति
धिक् क्षमामीदृशे जने ॥
This abode of crocodiles mistakes me, who is being patient, for being incompetent. Patience is, Dhik, futile with people!
6.21.21
न दर्शयति साम्ना मे
सागरो रूपमात्मनः ।
चापमानय सौमित्रे
शरांश्चाशीविषोपमान् ।
सागरं शोषयिष्यामि
पद्भ्यां यान्तु प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
I plead to him earnestly, yet the ocean does not appear in his own form! O Sowmitri, bring my bow. I will dry up this ocean with arrows that are like venomous serpents. Then the Vānaras can simply cross on foot.
6.21.23
अद्याक्षोभ्यमपि क्रुद्धः
क्षोभयिष्यामि सागरम् ।
वेलासु कृतमर्यादम्
सहसोर्मिसमाकुलम् ।
निर्मर्यादं करिष्यामि
सायकैर्वरुणालयम् ।
महार्णवं क्षोभयिष्ये
महादानवसङ्कुलम् ॥
I, in my rage, (might as well) throw this imperturbable ocean into turmoil. With my arrows, I will make this abode of Varuṇa of thousands of turbulent billows break its vow of never crossing the shoreline. I will put this great body of water, which is filled with Dānavas, into turmoil.
6.21.25
एवमुक्त्वा धनुष्पाणिः
क्रोधविस्फारितेक्षणः ।
बभूव रामो दुर्द्धर्षो
युगान्ताग्निरिव ज्वलन् ॥
Saying this, Rāma, with bow in his arm, and his eyes enlarged in wrath, blazed like the conflagration at the end of Time, whom none could stand against.
6.21.26
सम्पीड्य च धनुर्घोरम्
कम्पयित्वा शरैर्जगत् ।
मुमोच विशिखानुग्रान्
वज्रानिव शतक्रतुः ॥
Then, firmly holding the dreaded bow, he let fierce shafts fly, like Indra does with the Vajra, making the whole world tremble.
6.21.27
ते ज्वलन्तो महावेगाः
तेजसा सायकोत्तमाः ।
प्रविशन्ति समुद्रस्य
सलिलं त्रस्तपन्नगम् ॥
Those extremely fast and best of the arrows, blazing and powerful, entered the waters of the ocean, frightening the serpents in it.
6.21.28
तोयवेगः समुद्रस्य
समीनमकरो महान् ।
स भभूव महाघोरः
समारुतरवस्तदा ॥
Then the waters of that ocean became extremely turbulent.
With the deafening din of the gale that erupted and the loud squeals of its fish and crocodiles it became extremely dreadful.
6.21.29
महोर्मिमालाविततः
शङ्खशुक्तिसमावृतः ।
सधूमपरिवृत्तोर्मिः
सहसाऽऽसीन्महोदधिः ॥
In no time, the mighty ocean was spread with a huge succession of misty and whirling billows and with the conch shells that emerged on the surface.
6.21.30
व्यथिताः पन्नगाश्चासन्
दीप्तास्या दीप्तलोचनाः ।
दानवाश्च महावीर्याः
पातालतलवासिनः ॥
The serpents with blazing eyes and burning mouths were in agony, and so were the Dānavas of immense strength, who inhabited the nether world.
6.21.31
ऊर्मयः सिन्धुराजस्य
सनक्रमकरास्तदा ।
बिन्ध्यमन्दरसङ्काशाः
समुत्पेतुः सहस्रशः ॥
Thousands of ­billows, huge as the mountains of Vindhya and Mandara, carrying alligators and crocodiles, rose from the surface of the king of streams.
6.21.32
आघूर्णिततरङ्गौघः
सम्भ्रान्तोरगराक्षसः ।
उद्वर्तितमहाग्राहः
संवृत्तः सलिलाशयः ॥
That body of water went into a whirl, with its throngs of waves going topsy-turvy, its huge crocodiles turning upside down and its Uragas and Rākshasas becoming dazed.
6.21.33
ततस्तु तं राघवमुग्रवेगम्
प्रकर्षमाणं धनुरप्रमेयम् ।
सौमित्रिरुत्पत्य समुच्छ्वसन्तं
मामेति चोक्त्वा धनुराललम्बे ॥
As Rāghava hissed and drew his matchless bow with terrific speed, (the worried) Sowmitri jumped up, and catching hold of that bow, said, 'Please, no!'
6.21.34
एतद्विनासिह्युदधेस्तवार्य
संपत्सते वीरतमस्य कार्यम् ।
भवद्विधाः क्रोधवशं न यान्ति
दीर्घं भवान्पश्यतु साधुवृत्तम् ॥
You do not have to destroy this ocean to accomplish the mission that you, the best of the Veeras, is on! One like you should not cave in to anger! Please think of the example set by great people, that have been upheld for ages!
6.21.35
अन्तर्हितैश्चापि तथान्तरिक्षे
ब्रह्मर्षिभिश्चैव सुरर्षिभिश्च ।
शब्दः कृतः कष्टमिति ब्रुवद्भिः
मामेतिचोक्त्वामहता स्वरेण ॥
Then the Brahmarshis and Surarshis stationed in the sky, invisible, said in a loud agitated voice, 'Please, no, this will be terrible!'
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे एकविंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty first Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
