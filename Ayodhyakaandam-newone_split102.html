Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 102
In this very short Sarga, Bharata pressing further on Rāma's return, informs him of the passing of their father.
2.102.1
रामस्य वचनं श्रुत्वा
भरतः प्रत्युवाच ह ।
किं मे धर्माद्विहीनस्य
राजधर्मः करिष्यति ॥
Having heard Rāma's words, Bharata replied: What use is this science of polity to me who has fallen away from Dharma?
2.102.2
शाश्वतोऽयं सदा धर्मः
स्थितोऽस्मासु नरर्षभ ।
ज्येष्ठपुत्रे स्थिते राजन्
न कनीयान्नृपोभवेत् ॥
It is an accepted Dharma among us, O King, that the younger one shall not become the king when there is someone elder to him.
2.102.3
स समृद्धां मया सार्धम्
अयोध्यां गच्छ राघव ।
अभिषेचयचात्मानं
कुलस्यास्य भवाय नः ॥
Return with me, O Rāghava, to the plentiful Ayōdhyā and crown yourself for the good of our lineage.
2.102.4
राजानं मानुषं प्राहुः
देवत्वे सम्मतो मम ।
यस्य धर्मार्थसहितं
वृत्तमाहुरमानुषम् ॥
A king is only a human, people say. But I think he is a Dēva, for he achieves Artha while preserving Dharma with his super human capabilities.
2.102.5
केकयस्थे च मयि तु
त्वयि चारण्य माश्रिते ।
दिवमार्यो गतो राजा
यायजूकस्सतां मतः ॥
While I was in the Kēkaya country and you were in the forest making it your new residence, the noble King who was liked by all good people and who had performed many Yajñas, ascended to Heaven.
2.102.6
निष्क्रान्तमात्रे भवति
ससीते सहलक्ष्मणे ।
दुःखशोकाभिभूतस्तु
राजा त्रिदिवमभ्यगात् ॥
The moment you left along with Seetā and Lakshmaṇa, the King, overcome by grief and sorrow, departed to the abode of the Dēvas.
2.102.7
उत्तिष्ठ पुरुषव्याघ्र
क्रियतामुदकं पितुः ।
अहं चायं च शत्रुघ्नः
पूर्वमेव कृतोदकौ ॥
Get up, O tiger among men, and offer water libations to our father. Ṡatrughna and I have already offered water libations.
2.102.8
प्रियेण किल दत्तं हि
पितृलोकेषु राघव ।
अक्षय्यं भवतीत्याहुः
भवांश्चैव पितुः प्रियः ॥
Is it not said that the offerings by a dear one will give an eternal life in the world of Pitṛ Dēvatas? O Rāghava, you are the dearest of all to father.
2.102.9
त्वामेव शोचंस्तव दर्शनेप्सुः
त्वय्येव सक्तामनिवर्त्य बुद्धिम् ।
त्वया विहीनस्तव शोकमग्न-
स्त्वां संस्मरन्नस्तमितः पिता ते ॥
Having lost you, stricken by grief, thinking only of you and yearning to see you, unable to divert his mind from you to whom it clung, your father called it a day.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे द्व्युत्तरशततमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and second Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
