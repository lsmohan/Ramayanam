Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 75
In this Sarga, concluding that Rāvaṇa can no more go on the offense with Kumbhakarṇa having been slain, Sugreeva orders the Vānaras to set fire to Laṅkā, and they do so. Houses, mansions, towers tumble to the ground burned down. Weapons, clothes, precious gems and everything get consumed by the fire. Horses and elephants flee in confusion. Women wailed and flee with their children.
Meanwhile, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, their wounds completely healed, shower a rain of arrows on Laṅkā. The plans of Rākshasas to fight back are in shambles. Meanwhile, Sugreeva on the one hand, and Rāvaṇa on the other, instruct their respective armies to continue the fight without holding back. A great fight then ensues between Vānaras and Rākshasas.
6.75.1
ततोऽब्रवीन्महातेजाः
सुग्रीवो वानराधिपः ।
अर्थ्यं विज्ञापयंश्चापि
हनुमन्तमिदं वचः ॥
Thereupon Sugreeva, the extraordinarily powerful ruler of Vānaras, told Hanumān these words, directing him on what was to be done next.
6.75.2
यतो हतः कुम्भकर्णः
कुमाराश्च निषूदिताः ।
नेदानीमुपनिर्हारम्
रावणो दातुमर्हति ॥
With his sons and Kumbhakarṇa slain, Rāvaṇa can no more give us the pleasure of a fight.
6.75.3
ये ये महाबलाः सन्ति
लघवश्च प्लवङ्गमाः ।
लङ्कामभिपतन्त्वाशु
गृह्योल्काः प्लवगर्षभाः ।
हरयो हरिसंकाशाः
प्रदग्धुं रावणालयम् ॥
Let all the bulls among Vānaras who are swift and of immense strength and all the Vānaras that rival lions go to Laṅkā quickly with torches to burn down the abode of Rāvaṇa.
6.75.4
ततोऽस्तं गत आदित्ये
रौद्रे तस्मिन्निशामुखे ।
लङ्कामभिमुखाः सोल्का
जग्मुस्ते प्लवगर्षभाः ॥
Then, after the sun had set and at the onset of terrible night, the bulls among Vānaras advanced upon Laṅkā carrying torches.
6.75.5
उल्काहस्तैर्हरिगणैः
सर्वतः समभिद्रुताः ।
आरक्षस्था विरूपाक्षाः
सहसा विप्रदुद्रुवुः ॥
As soon as the hordes of Vānaras advanced from every direction with torches in their hands, the crooked-eyed guards (of the city) took to their heels.
6.75.6
गोपुराट्टप्रतोलीषु
चर्यासु विविधासु च ।
प्रासादेषु च संहृष्टाः
ससृजुस्ते हुताशनम् ॥
They went on a spree, setting fire to the gateways, guard-houses, to the mansions and to every kind of dwelling on the side streets.
6.75.7
तेषां गृहसहस्राणि
ददाह हुतभुक् तदा ।
प्रासादाः पर्वताकाराः
पतन्ति धरणीतले ॥
The fire consumed thousands of houses. Palaces lofty as mountains tumbled down to the ground.
6.75.8
अगरुर्दह्यते तत्र
परं चैव सुचन्दनम् ।
मौक्तिका मणयः स्निग्धा
वज्रं चापि प्रवालकम् ॥
Agaru, exquisite sandal, shiny pearls, diamonds,and coral were consumed by the fire.
6.75.9
क्षौमं च दह्यते तत्र
कौशेयं चापि शोभनम् ।
आविकं विविधं चौर्णम्
काञ्चनं भाण्डमायुधम् ॥
Lovely garments made of white silk and wool, gold utensils and weapons were consumed by the fire.
6.75.10
नानाविकृतसंस्थानम्
वाजिभाण्डपरिच्छदम् ।
गजग्रैवेयकक्ष्याश्च
रथभाण्डाश्च संस्कृताः ।
तनुत्राणि च योधानाम्
हस्त्यश्वानां च वर्म च ।
खड्गा धनूंषि ज्याबाणाः
तोमराङ्कुशशक्तयः ।
रोमजं वालजं चर्म
व्याघ्रजं चाण्डजं बहु ।
मुक्तामणिविचित्रांश्च
प्रासादांश्च समन्ततः ।
विविधानस्त्रसंघातान्
अग्निर्दहति तत्र वै ॥
Saddles and other accoutrement of horses in myriad shapes, the halters of elephants and the bands on their backs, the well-wrought fittings of chariots, the armors of warriors, the coats of mail of horses and elephants, swords, bows, bowstrings, arrows, Tōmaras, Ṡaktis, articles made of wool like blankets, articles made of tails like fly-whisks, bucklers, tiger-skins, birds, palaces everywhere studded with a variety of pearls and diamonds, and a variety of piles of weapons were all consumed by that fire.
6.75.14
नानाविधान् गृहांश्चित्रान्
ददाह हुतभूक् तदा ।
आवासान् राक्षसानां च
सर्वेषां गृहगृध्नुनाम् ॥
The fire consumed dwellings of all designs, of all the Rākshasas, who dearly loved their houses.
6.75.15
हेमचित्रतनुत्राणाम्
स्रग्दामाम्बरधारिणाम् ।
शीधुपानचलाक्षाणाम्
मदविह्वलगामिनाम् ।
कान्तालम्बितवस्त्राणाम्
शत्रुसञ्जातमन्युनाम् ।
गदाशूलासिहस्तानाम्
खादतां पिबतामपि ।
शयनेषु महार्हेषु
प्रसुप्तानां प्रियैः सह ।
त्रस्तानां गच्छतां तूर्णम्
पुत्रानादाय सर्वतः ।
तेषां शतसहस्राणि
तदा लङ्कानिवासिनाम् ।
अदहत् पावकस्तत्र
जज्वाल च पुनःपुनः ॥
The fire raged again and again, consuming hundreds of thousands of the dwellers of Laṅkā, some reeling under intoxication their eyes whirling and gait unsteady and their clothes wrapped partly around the bodies of their wives, some becoming angry with the foe and holding maces and spears in their hands, some simply eating and drinking, some happily sleeping on exquisite beds along with their lovers, and some running in fear taking their children along with them.
6.75.19
सारवन्ति महार्हाणि
गम्भीरगुणवन्ति च ।
हेमचन्द्रार्धचन्द्राणि
चन्द्रशालोन्नतानि च ।
तत्र चित्रगवाक्षाणि
साधिष्ठानानि सर्वशः ।
मणिविद्रुमचित्राणि
स्पृशन्तीव दिवाकरम् ।
क्रौञ्चबर्हिणवीणानाम्
भूषणानां च निस्वनैः ।
नादितान्यचला भानि
वेश्मान्यग्निर्ददाह सः ॥
The stately and expensive homes, majestic like mountains, dazzling like the sun, lofty as if they would touch the sun, with solemn appearance and designs, furnished with couches and beds all over, the rooms on their terraces shaped like the full moon and the half moon, their casements wrought in stylish designs, studded with corals and diamonds, and resounding with the sounds of Krounca birds and peacocks, music of Veeṇas and tinkling of ornaments were consumed by the fire.
6.75.22
ज्वलनेन परीतानि
तोरणानि चकाशिरे ।
विद्युद्भिरिव नद्धानि
मेघजालानि धर्मगे ॥
The arched gateways enveloped by the fire shone like banks of clouds accompanied by lightning at the onset of the monsoon season.
6.75.23
ज्वलनेन परीतानि
गृहाणि प्रचकाशिरे ।
दावाग्निदीप्तानि यथा
शिखराणि महागिरेः ॥
Engulfed in fire, the houses looked like the peaks of a great mountain caught by wildfires.
6.75.24
विमानेषु प्रसुप्ताश्च
दह्यमाना वराङ्गनाः ।
त्यक्ताभरणसर्वाङ्गा
हा हेत्युच्चैर्विचुक्रुशुः ॥
The best of the women who were sound asleep on the top floors of mansions, their bodies bare of any ornament, were caught in the fire and wailed in high pitched voices, ‘Alas, alas!’
6.75.25
तत्र चाग्निपरीतानि
निपेतुर्भवनान्यथ ।
वज्रिवज्रहतानीव
शिखराणि महागिरेः ॥
Engulfed in the fire and burnt, the houses tumbled down like like the peaks of great mountains struck down by the wielder of Vajra.
6.75.26
तानि निर्दह्यमानानि
दूरतः प्रचकाशिरे ।
हिमवच्छिखराणीव
दीप्तौषधिवनानि च ॥
The burning mansions looked from afar like the glowing groves of divine herbs on the peaks of Himavān.
6.75.27
हर्म्याग्रैर्दह्यमानैश्च
ज्वालाप्रज्वलितैरपि ।
रात्रौ सा दृश्यते लङ्का
पुष्पितैरिव किंशुकैः ॥
With the tops of the mansions burning bright and engulfed in flames, Laṅkā, in that night, looked like Kiṃṡuka trees in flower.
6.75.28
हस्त्यध्यक्षैर्गजैर्मुक्तैः
मुक्तैश्च तुरगैरपि ।
बभूव लङ्का लोकान्ते
भ्रान्तग्राह इवार्णवः ॥
With the elephants and horses (running everywhere) having been released by their keepers, Laṅkā looked like the ocean at the time of apocalypse, with all its crocodiles agitated and stirred.
6.75.29
अश्वं मुक्तं गजो दृष्ट्वा
क्वचिद्भीतोऽपसर्पति ।
भीतो भीतं गजं दृष्ट्वा
क्वचिदश्वो निवर्तते ॥
In one place, an elephant shied away from a stray horse that had been released. In another place, a horse turned back scared of an elephant that was also in fright.
6.75.30
लङ्कायां दह्यमानायाम्
शुशुभे स महार्णवः ।
छायासंसक्तसलिलो
लोहितोद इवार्णवः ॥
The flames of Laṅkā, reflected in the waters of the great ocean, gave a bewitching illusion that the waters of the ocean were crimson red.
Vālmeeki, as we have noticed many a time in Bāla Kāṇḍa, is very visual and offers multiple 'camera angles'. Describing the war scene from within the middle of the battlefield or from the midst of the burning streets of Laṅkā is one thing, but zooming out the camera all the way out into the ocean, where there is no one that belongs to the story line, is quite another thing; a master stroke of visualization.
6.75.31
सा बभूव मुहूर्तेन
हरिभिर्दीपिता पुरी ।
लोकस्यास्य क्षये घोरे
प्रदीप्तेव वसुन्धरा ॥
The city, set ablaze by the Vānaras, looked like the earth burning at the time of the worlds coming to an end.
6.75.32
नारीजनस्य धूमेन
व्याप्तस्योच्चैर्विनेदुषः ।
स्वनो ज्वलनतप्तस्य
शुश्रुवे दशयोजनम् ॥
The loud wailing of the women folk, suffocated by the smoke and scorched by the fire, was heard as far away as ten Yōjanas.
6.75.33
प्रदग्धकायानपरान्
राक्षसान्निर्गतान् बहिः ।
सहसाऽभ्युत्पतन्ति स्म
हरयोऽथ युयुत्सवः ॥
The Vānaras, lusting for a fight, swooped on the other Rākshasas who were rushing out with their bodies caught in the fire.
6.75.34
उद्घुष्टं वानराणां च
राक्षसानां च निस्वनः ।
दिशो दश समुद्रं च
पृथिवीं चान्वनादयत् ॥
The clamor of the Vānaras and the cries of the Rākshasas reverberated in all the ten directions and all over the earth and the oceans.
6.75.35
विशल्यौ तु महात्मानौ
तावुभौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
असम्भ्रान्तौ जगृहतुः
तदोभे धनुषी वरे ॥
Both Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, the Mahātmas, perfectly healed and well composed, grabbed their respective splendid bows.
6.75.36
ततो विष्फारयानस्य
रामस्य धनुरुत्तमम् ।
बभूव तुमुलः शब्दो
राक्षसानां भयावहः ॥
The twang of the great bow of Rāma created a great din, terrifying the Rākshasas.
6.75.37
अशोभत तदा रामो
धनुर्विष्फारयन् महत् ।
भगवानिव सङ्क्रुद्धो
भवो वेदमयं धनुः ॥
As he twanged the great bow, Rāma looked like the enraged Bhagawān Bhava (Ṡiva) twanging His bow, which was nothing other than Vēdas taken form.
6.75.38
उद्घुष्टं वानराणां च
राक्षसानां च निस्वनम् ।
ज्याशब्दस्तावुभौ शब्दौ
अति रामस्य शुश्रुवे ॥
The sound from the twang of the bow of Rāma was heard above both the clamor of Vānaras and the cries of Rākshasas.
6.75.39
वानरोद्घुष्टघोषश्च
राक्षसानां च निस्वनः ।
ज्याशब्दश्चापि रामस्य
त्रयं व्याप दिशो दश ॥
The three, the sound from the twang of Rāma's bow, the clamor of Vānaras, and the cries of Rākshasas, filled all the ten directions.
6.75.40
तस्य कार्मुकमुक्तैश्च
शरैस्तत्पुरगोपुरम् ।
कैलासशृङ्गप्रतिमम्
विकीर्णमपतद्भुवि ॥
The central tower of that city, which was huge like the crest of Kailāsa, fell on the ground shattered struck by the arrows shot from that bow of his.
6.75.41
ततो रामशरान् दृष्ट्वा
विमानेषु गृहेषु च ।
सन्नाहो राक्षसेन्द्राणाम्
तुमुलः समपद्यत ॥
Once the Rākshasas saw the arrows of Rāma falling on the towers and the houses, all their preparations for the fight became chaotic.
6.75.42
तेषां सन्नह्यमानानाम्
सिंहनादं च कुर्वताम् ।
शर्वरी राक्षसेन्द्राणाम्
रौद्रीव समपद्यत ॥
With their preparations for the fight and utterances of battle-cries, the great Rākshasas turned that night into one that brings the worlds to an end.
6.75.43
आदिष्टा वानरेन्द्रास्तु
सुग्रीवेण महात्मना ॥
(Meanwhile), the mighty Vānaras were instructed by Sugreeva, the Mahātma:
6.75.43
आसन्नं द्वारमासाद्य
युध्यध्वं प्लवगर्षभाः ।
यश्च वो वितथं कुर्यात्
तत्र तत्राप्युपस्थितः ।
स हन्तव्योऽभिसम्प्लुत्य
राजशासनदूषकः ॥
Oh ye bulls among Vānaras! Go to the nearest entrances and put up a stiff fight. Should you fail to do so wherever you are stationed, you incur the offense of transgressing the king's order; and shall be pounced upon and killed.
6.75.45
तेषु वानरमुख्येषु
दीप्तोल्कोज्ज्वलपाणिषु ।
स्थितेषु द्वारमासाद्य
रावणं मन्युराविशत् ॥
When the Vānara chiefs arrived at the gate, their hands lit up by the flames of torches they held, Rāvaṇa flew into a rage.
6.75.46
तस्य जृम्भितविक्षेपात्
व्यामिश्रा वै दिशो दश ।
रूपवानिव रुद्रस्य
मन्युर्गात्रेष्वदृश्यत ॥
When he stretched his arms and body (in that rage), he seemed to push all the directions away. It seemed as if Rudra's wrath found a form in every limb of his body.
6.75.47
स निकुम्भं च कुम्भं च
कुम्भकर्णात्मजावुभौ ।
प्रेषयामास सङ्क्रुद्धो
राक्षसैर्बहुभिः सह ॥
Then he, in rage, sent Kumbha and Nikumbha, the sons of Kumbhakarṇa, supported by many Rākshasas (to fight the enemy).
6.75.48
यूपाक्षः शोणिताक्षश्च
प्रजङ्घः कम्पनस्तथा ।
निर्ययुः कौम्भकर्णिभ्याम्
सह रावणशासनात् ॥
Yūpāksha, Ṡōṇitāksha, Prajaṅgha, and Kampana, went along with the sons of Kumbhakarṇa, as instructed by Rāvaṇa.
6.75.49
शशास चैव तान् सर्वान्
राक्षसान् स महाबलान् ।
राक्षसा गच्छताद्यैव
सिंहनादं च नादयन् ॥
He gave clear instructions to all of those Rākshasas of extraordinary strength, 'Go right away, roaring like lions, O Rākshasas!'
6.75.50
ततस्तु चोदितास्तेन
राक्षसा ज्वलितायुधाः ।
लङ्काया निर्ययुर्वीराः
प्रणदन्तः पुनःपुनः ॥
And those Rākshasa warriors, thus exhorted, marched forth from Laṅkā with shining weapons, shouting battle-cries.
6.75.51
रक्षसां भूषणस्थाभिः
भाभिः स्वाभिश्च सर्वशः ।
चक्रुस्ते सप्रभं व्योम
हरयश्चाग्निभिः सह ॥
Rākshasas with the glitter of their ornaments and the sheen of their bodies, and the Vānaras with the fire (from torches in their hands) lit up the sky bright.
6.75.52
तत्र ताराधिपस्याभा
ताराणां च तथैव च ।
तयोराभरणभा च
ज्वलिताद्यामभासयत् ॥
The brilliance of the Lord of stars (moon), of the stars and of their ornaments, lit up the sky bright.
6.75.53
चन्द्राभा भूषणाभा च
गृहाणां ज्वलतां च भा ।
हरिराक्षससैन्यानि
भ्राजयामास सर्वतः ॥
The brilliance of the moon light, that of the ornaments and that of the burning houses, lit up the Vānara and Rākshasa forces everywhere.
6.75.54
तत्र चोर्ध्वं प्रदीप्तानाम्
गृहाणां सागरः पुनः ।
भाभिः संसक्तसलिलः
चलोर्मिः शुशुबेऽधिकम् ॥
And then, the ocean too, with its dancing billows, made an extremely fascinating sight with its waters reflecting the light from the high-rising fires from the burning houses.
6.75.55
पताकाध्वजसंयुक्तम्
उत्तमासिपरश्वधम् ।
भीमाश्वरथमातङ्गम्
नानापत्तिसमाकुलम् ।
दीप्तशूलगदाखड्ग
प्रासतोमरकार्मुकम् ।
तद्राक्षसबलं घोरम्
भीमविक्रमपौरुषम् ।
ददृशे ज्वलितप्रासम्
किङ्किणीशतनादितम् ।
हेमजालाचितभुजम्
व्यावेष्ठित परश्वधम् ।
व्याघूर्णितमहाशस्त्रम्
बाणसंयुक्तकार्मुकम् ।
गन्धमाल्यमधूत्सेक
सम्मोदितमहानिलम् ॥
With its flags and banners, its superb swords and battle-axes, its formidable horses, chariots and elephants, its multitude of tumultuous cavalry, its bright spears, maces, swords, Prāsas, Tōmaras, and bows and with its Prāsas glistening, its thousands of bells jingling, the arms of its warriors decked in gold, its battle-axes flashed and waved, its formidable weapons flung and its bows mounted with arrows, that Rākshasa army of terrific prowess and pride looked formidable, while the air became filled with the fragrance of the flower garlands and the scent of heady liquor.
6.75.59
घोरं शूरजनाकीर्णम्
महाम्बुधरनिस्वनम् ।
तद्दृष्ट्वा बलमायान्तम्
राक्षसानां दुरासदम् ।
सञ्चचाल प्लवङ्गानाम्
बलमुच्चैर्ननाद च ॥
Seeing that terrible force of Rākshasas that was difficult to stand against, packed with great warriors advancing, the Vānara army was shaken and let off loud shouts.
6.75.60
जवेनाप्लुत्य च पुनः
तद्बलं रक्षसां महत् ।
अभ्ययात् प्रत्यरिबलम्
पतङ्गा इव पावकम् ॥
And that great Rākshasa army leaped forward with all the speed towards the enemy forces, like moths to the fire.
6.75.61
तेषां भुजपरामर्श
व्यामृष्टपरिघाशनि ।
राक्षसानां बलं श्रेष्ठम्
भूयस्तरमशोभत ॥
The glitter of their iron clubs that could strike like thunderbolts, polished by constant handling, doubled the magnificence of the Rākshasa forces.
6.75.62
तत्रोन्मत्ता इवोत्पेतुः
हरयोऽथ युयुत्सवः ।
तरुशैलैरभिघ्नन्तो
मुष्टिभिश्च निशाचरान् ॥
In their eagerness to fight, the Vānaras jumped up as if they were mad and assailed the Rākshasas with trees, boulders and their doubled fists.
6.75.63
तथैवापततां तेषाम्
कपीनामसिभिः शितैः ।
शिरांसि सहसा जह्नू
राक्षसा भीमदर्शनाः ॥
And as quickly as the Vānaras came on, the Rākshasas, whose very appearance was so frightful, cut off their heads with sharp swords.
6.75.64
दशनैर्हृतकर्णाश्च
मुष्टिनिष्कीर्णमस्तकाः ।
शिलाप्रहारभग्नाङ्गा
विचेरुस्तत्र राक्षसाः ॥
The Rākshasas trudged around, some with their ears bitten off, some with their heads smashed by blows of fists and others with their limbs broken, struck by rocks.
6.75.65
तथैवाप्यपरे तेषाम्
कपीनामसिभिः शितैः ।
प्रवरानभितो जघ्नुः
घोररूपा निशाचराः ॥
Some other frightfullooking Night-rovers managed to strike down, with sharp swords, prominent Vānaras everywhere.
6.75.66
घ्नन्तमन्यं जघानान्यः
पातयन्तमपातयत् ।
गर्हमाणं जगर्हेऽन्यो
दशन्तमपरोऽदशत् ॥
The one who attacked the others was himself attacked by another. The one who struck down the others was himself struck down by another. The one who insulted the others was himself insulted by another. The one who bit others was himself bitten by another.
6.75.67
देहीत्यन्यो ददात्यन्यो
ददामीत्यपरः पुनः ।
किं क्लेशयसि तिष्ठेति
तत्रान्योन्यं बभाषिरे ॥
‘Come, strike’, one dared the other. Before another responded, ‘Yes, I shall', yet another one struck him down. Each one yelled at the other, ‘Don’t you worry, just wait right there’.
6.75.68
विप्रलम्बितवसशत्रं च
विमुक्तकवचायुधम् ।
समुद्यतमहाप्रासम्
यष्टिशूलासिसङ्कुलम् ।
प्रावर्तत महारौद्रम्
युद्धं वानररक्षसाम् ॥
And that extremely fierce fight continued between Vānaras and Rākshasas with a tumultuous wielding of huge Prāsas, Yashṭis, spears and swords. But then, the Ṡastras were brought to naught and fighters were stripped of their weapons and armors.
6.75.70
वानरान् दश सप्तेति
राक्षसा जघ्नुराहवे ।
राक्षसान् दश सप्तेति
वानराश्चाभ्यपातयन् ॥
The Rākshasas struck down the Vānaras by sevens and tens. And the Vānaras downed the Rākshasas likewise.
6.75.71
विस्रस्तकेशवसनम्
विध्वस्तकवचध्वजम् ।
बलं राक्षसमालम्ब्य
वानराः पर्यवारयन् ॥
Vānaras surrounded and caught the Rākshasa forces whose clothes and hair were in disorder and whose armor and banners were smashed.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे पञ्चसप्ततितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the Seventy Fifth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
