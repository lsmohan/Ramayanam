Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 5
In this short Sarga, Daṡaratha asks Vasishṭha to help Rāma with fasting. Vasishṭha goes to Rāma's palace. He initiates Rāma to undertake the fast. He then returns, watching the merry crowds that filled the highways, on his way back. Daṡaratha, after receiving Vasishṭha and ensuring that the fasting was initiated, dismisses the assembly in his court and retires to the inner quarters. 
2.5.1
सन्दिश्य रामं नृपति
श्श्वोभाविन्यभिषेचने ।
पुरोहितं समाहूय
वसिष्ठमिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Having seen Rāma off thus, the king invited Vasishṭha, the Purōhita and spoke regarding the consecration next day:
2.5.2
गच्छोपवासं काकुत्स्थम्
कारयाद्य तपोधन ।
श्रीयशोराज्यलाभाय
वध्वा सह यतव्रतम् ॥
O the one enriched with Tapa! May you go and help the prince of Kākutstha lineage and my daughter-in-law who are diligent with observances, with the fasting for gaining prosperity, reputation and the kingdom!
2.5.3
तथेति च स राजानम्
उक्त्वा वेदविदां वरः ।
स्वयं वसिष्ठो भगवान्
ययौ रामनिवेशनम् ।
उपवासयितुं रामम्
मन्त्रवन्मन्त्रकोविदः ।
ब्राह्मं रथवरं युक्तम्
आस्थाय सुदृढव्रतः ॥
Saying 'yes!' to the king, Bhagawān Vasishṭha, the highly disciplined eminent scholar of Vedas and savant of Mantras, went to the residence of Rāma, in person, to help him undertake the fasting with Mantras, on a noble chariot that is appropriate for Brāhmaṇas.
Note the mention of the chariot that is appropriate for Brāhmaṇas. While a king's chariot is decorated in a regal manner, perhaps with a host of armaments hanging around it, a Brāhmaṇa's chariot would be simple and just enough to serve the purpose of transportation. 
2.5.5
स रामभवनं प्राप्य
पाण्डुराभ्रघनप्रभम् ।
तिस्रः कक्ष्या रथेनैव
विवेशमुनिसत्तमः ॥
That distinguished Muni, reached the mansion of Rāma, which was splendid like a huge white cloud,  and went right through all the three gateways on the chariot. 
The privilege and regard for Vasishṭha is indicated by his ability to go through all the three gateways on the chariot without being checked or stopped by the gatekeepers.
2.5.6
तमागतमृषिं रामः
त्वरन्निव ससम्भ्रमः ।
मानयिष्यन्समानार्हम्
निश्चक्राम निवेशनात् ॥
To receive that honorable Ṛshi with due honor Rāma came out of his residence in all haste and excitement.
2.5.7
अभ्येत्य त्वरमाणश्च
रथाभ्याशं मनीषिणः ।
ततोऽवतारयामास
परिगृह्य रथात्स्वयम् ॥
Having reached the chariot instantaneously, he, in person, helped that great sage alight from the chariot.
Rāma's gesture is not only being respectful, but also touching. Isn't it?
Vālmeeki never misses an opportunity to bring our attention to the human-human and human-nature interactions and relationships, which are celebrated by Indic Civilization, from the days of Rāmāyaṇa to now and by the celebrated poets as well as by the common man.
2.5.8
स चैनं प्रश्रितं दृष्ट्वा
सम्भाष्याभिप्रसाद्य च ।
प्रियार्हं हर्षयन्रामम्
इत्युवाच पुरोहितः ॥
Seeing that obedient Rāma, who deserves affection, the Purōhita greeted him, making him happy and well-disposed and said:
2.5.9
प्रसन्नस्ते पिता राम
यौवराज्यमवाप्स्यसि ।
उपवासं भवानद्य
करोतु सह सीतया ॥
O Rāma! Your father is pleased with you. You will be gaining the office of crown prince. May you undertake fasting today along with Seetā!
2.5.10
प्रातस्त्वामभिषेक्ता हि
यौवराज्ये नराधिपः ।
पिता दशरथः प्रीत्या
ययातिं नहुषो यथा ॥
Your father, King Daṡaratha is pleased to consecrate you as the crown prince tomorrow morning, like Nahusha consecrated Yayāti.
2.5.11
इत्युक्त्वा स तदा रामम्
उपवासं यतव्रतम् ।
मन्त्रवत्कारयामास
वैदेह्या सहितं मुनिः ॥
Saying thus, the Muni helped that highly disciplined Rāma along with the princess of Vidēha to undertake the fast with appropriate Mantras.
2.5.12
ततो यथावद्रामेण
स राज्ञो गुरुरर्चितः ।
अभ्यनुज्ञाप्य काकुत्स्थम्
ययौ रामनिवेशनात् ॥
After being honored by Rāma in an appropriate manner and after taking permission from that prince of Kākutstha lineage that Guru of the king departed from the residence of Rāma.
2.5.13
सुहृद्भिस्तत्र रामोऽपि
सहासीनः प्रियंवदैः ।
सभाजितो विवेशाऽथ
ताननुज्ञाप्य सर्वशः ॥
Rāma spent some time there with his friends who were making pleasant conversation, being respectful to him in every manner. Then, after taking proper leave from them he left for his inner quarters.
2.5.14
हृष्टनारीनरयुतम्
रामवेश्म तदा बभौ ।
यथा मत्तद्विजगणम्
प्रफुल्लनलिनं सरः ॥
With every woman and man being happy, Rāma's residence was shining like a lake full of lotuses in full bloom and flocks of intoxicated birds.
2.5.15
स राजभवनप्रख्यात्
तस्माद्रामनिवेशानात् ।
निर्गत्य ददृशे मार्गम्
वसिष्ठो जनसंवृतम् ॥
After leaving Rāma's house that vied with the royal palace, Vasishṭha saw the roads full of crowds.
Vālmeeki is using Vasishṭha's commute back to the royal palace through the royal pathways as an opportunity to give a picture of the jubilation among the people of the city.
2.5.16
बृन्दबृन्दैरयोध्यायाम्
राजमार्गास्समन्ततः ।
बभूवुरभिसम्बाधाः
कुतूहलजनैर्वृताः ॥
All the highways of Ayōdhyā became narrow filled with crowds of people gathered there in a festive mood.
2.5.17
जनबृन्दोर्मिसंघर्ष
हर्षस्वनवतस्तदा ।
बभूव राजमार्गस्य
सागरस्येव निस्वनः ॥
The noise of those highways with those stampeding and merry making crowds resembled the roar of the ocean.
One of my first memories in USA, circa 1990, was the noise of the highways, which did remind me of the roar of the ocean.
Perhaps in the near-zero-noise-pollution days of Rāmāyaṇa, even the noises made by crowds must have reminded the roar of the ocean.
2.5.18
सिक्तसम्मृष्टरथ्या हि
तदहर्वनमालिनी ।
आसीदयोध्यानगरी
समुच्छ्रितगृहध्वजा ॥
On that day, the streets of Ayōdhyā were swept and washed. It was festooned with garlands everywhere. Flags were flying high on the flag posts of every house.
2.5.19
तदा ह्ययोध्यानिलय
स्सस्त्रीबालाबलो जनः ।
रामाभिषेकमाकाङ्क्षन्
नाकाङ्क्षदुदयं रवेः ॥
On that day, all the people of Ayōdhyā, women, children and the infirm alike looked forward to the next morning, looking forward to Rāma's consecration.
2.5.20
प्रजालङ्कारभूतं च
जनस्यानन्दवर्धनम् ।
उत्सुकोऽभूज्जनो द्रष्टुम्
तमयोध्यामहोत्सवम् ॥
People were eager to witness that great celebration in Ayōdhyā, which would be a source of enhanced felicity and an occasion to put on their best clothes.
2.5.21
एवं तज्जनसम्बाधम्
राजमार्गं पुरोहितः ।
व्यूहन्निव जनौघं तम्
शनै राजकुलं ययौ ॥
The Purōhita had to, slowly, cut through such throngs of people on the royal pathways on his way to reaching the royal mansion.
2.5.22
सिताभ्रशिखरप्रख्यम्
प्रासादमधिरुह्य सः ।
समीयाय नरेन्द्रेण
शक्रेणेव बृहस्पतिः ॥
Climbing to the top of that mansion, which resembled a mountain peak covered with a white cloud, he approached the King, as Bṛhaspati joining Ṡakra.
2.5.23
तमागतमभिप्रेक्ष्य
हित्वा राजासनं नृपः ।
पप्रच्छ स च तस्मै तत्
कृतमित्यभ्यवेदयत् ॥
Having seen him coming, the king got up from the royal throne and asked whether the task was complete, and got the answer in affirmative.
2.5.24
तेन चैव तदा तुल्यम्
सहासीनास्सभासदः ।
आसनेभ्यस्समुत्तस्थुः
पूजयन्तः पुरोहितम् ॥
Just as he did, people of the assembly who were seated along with him, also rose from their seats and paid respects to the Purōhita.
This Ṡlōka reminds me a scene from Richard Attenborough's film Gandhi. In that film, Gandhi arrives in the court to be prosecuted.
And the judge, in deference to him, gets up from his seat. All others also rise from their seats to show respect, following the lead of the judge.
2.5.25
गुरुणा त्वभ्यनुज्ञातो
मनुजौघं विसृज्य तम् ।
विवेशान्तः पुरं राजा
सिंहो गिरिगुहामिव ॥
After obtaining permission from the Guru and after dismissing the assembly, the king retreated into his inner quarters like a lion retreating into its cave.
2.5.26
तदग्य्रवेषप्रमदाजनाकुलं
महेन्द्रवेश्मप्रतिमं निवेशनम् ।
विदीपयंश्चारु विवेश पार्थिवः
शशीव तारागणसङ्कुलं नभः ॥
That ruler of the earth entered the beautiful inner quarter which was like the abode of Mahēndra and was filled with women of exquisite attire, like the moon brightening the sky filled with stars!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे पञ्चमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
