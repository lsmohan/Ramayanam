Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 47
In this Sarga, the Vānara captains go in every direction in search of Seetā, searching all the places as instructed by Sugreeva.
At the end of a month’s search, the search parties that went in the easterly, northerly and westerly directions return, unable to find Seetā.
They report to Sugreeva, in the presence of Rāma, that Seetā could not be found in any of those directions. They express their confidence that Hanumān would find Seetā in the southerly direction in which she had been carried off.
4.47.1
दर्शनार्थं तु वैदेह्याः
सर्वतः कपिकुंजराः  ।
व्यादिष्टाः कपिराजेन
यथोक्तं जग्मुरञ्जसा ॥
The elephants among Vānaras went in all haste in search of Vaidēhee in every direction, following the instructions of the king of Vānaras faithfully.
4.47.2
ते सरांसि सरित्कक्षान्
आकाशं नगराणि च ।
नदीदुर्गांस्तथा शैलान्
विचिन्वन्ति समन्ततः ॥
They searched everywhere by the lakes, by the rivers, in the thickets, in the open spaces, and in the cities, and in places that were difficult to access because of rivers and hills.
4.47.3
सुग्रीवेण समाख्याताः
सर्वे वानरयूथपाः ।
तत्र देशान्विचिन्वन्ति 
सशैलवनकाननान् ॥
The captains of the Vānaras searched all those areas including the mountains, Vanas and forests as instructed by Sugreeva.
4.47.4
विचित्य दिवसं सर्वे
सीताधिगमने धृताः ।
समायान्ति स्म मेदिन्याम्
निशाकालेषु वानराः ॥
All those Vānaras used to go out searching for Seetā with resolve during the day and assemble together during the night on plain ground.
4.47.5
सर्वर्तुकांश्च देशेषु
वानारास्सफलद्रुमान् ।
आसाद्य रजनीं शय्याम्
चक्रुस्सर्वेष्वहस्सु ते ॥
During the day, all those Vānaras sought trees that gave fruit perennially in the respective regions and places to rest during the night.
4.47.6
तदहः प्रथमं कृत्वा
मासे प्रस्रवणं गताः ।
कपिराजेन सङ्गम्य
निराशाः कपिकुंजराः  ॥
Having lost all hopes (of finding Seetā) those elephants among Vānaras returned to the Prasravaṇa mountain and met the King of Vānaras within a month from the date of their departure.
4.47.7
विचित्य तु दिशं पूर्वाम्
यथोक्तां सचिवैस्सह ।
अदृष्ट्वा विनतस्सीताम्
आजगाम महाबलः ॥
Having searched the easterly direction as he had been instructed, along with his counsellors, and unable to find Seetā, the mighty strong Vinata returned.
4.47.8
दिशमप्युत्तरां सर्वाम्
विचित्य स महाकपिः ।
आगतस्सह सैन्येन
वीरश्शतबलस्तदा ॥
Then, having searched the northerly direction, Ṡatabali, the great Vānara returned along with his army.
4.47.9
सुषेणः पश्चिमामाशाम्
विचित्य सह वानरैः ।
समेत्य मासे पूर्णे तु
सुग्रीवमुपचक्रमे ॥
Sushēṇa, having searched the westerly direction along with other Vānaras, returned and joined Sugreeva, just as the month drew to a close.
4.47.10
तं प्रस्रवणपृष्ठस्थम्
समासाद्याभिवाद्य च ।
आसीनं सह रामेण
सुग्रीवमिदमब्रुवन्  ॥
Then they approached, saluted and said to Sugreeva, who sat on the Prasravaṇa mountain along with Rāma:
4.47.11
विचिताः पर्वतास्सर्वे
वनानि गहनानि च ।
निम्नगास्सागरान्ताश्च
सर्वे जनपदाश्च ये ॥
We have searched all the mountains, the dense Vanas, the rivers and all the regions up to the sea-coast.
4.47.12
गुहाश्च विचितास्सर्वा
याश्च ते परिकीर्तिताः  ।
विचिताश्च महागुल्मा
लताविततसन्तताः ॥
We have also searched all the caves you mentioned, and the great bushes that are overrun by creepers.
4.47.13
गहनेषु च देशेषु
दुर्गेषु विषमेषु च ।
सत्त्वान्यतिप्रमाणानि
विचितानि हतानि च ।
ये चैव गहना देशा
विचितास्ते पुनः पुनः ॥
All the places that were dense, impenetrable and uneven were searched; the dense ones more than once. All the creatures that were abnormally huge were also searched and even killed.
4.47.14
उदारसत्त्वाभिजनो महात्मा
स मैथिलीं ज्ञास्यति वानरेन्द्र ।
दिशं तु यामेव गता तु सीता
तामास्थितोवायुसुतो हनूमान् ॥
O Lord of Vānaras! Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, the Mahātma of noble descent, will certainly find Seetā, in the direction that she had been carried off.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे सप्तचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty seventh Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
