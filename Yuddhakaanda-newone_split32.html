Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 32
In this Sarga, Seetā laments and wails, believing the words of Rāvaṇa that Rāma is killed and the head of Rāma and his bow shown to her are real.
Meanwhile, Prahasta and all the ministers come to see him, and Rāvaṇa leaves the Aṡōka pleasance to give them audience. The fake head and bow disappear as soon as he leaves. Rāvaṇa orders the army chiefs to assemble the forces, which they do immediately.
6.32.1
सा सीता तच्छिरो दृष्ट्वा
तच्च कार्मुकमुत्तमम् ।
सुग्रीवप्रतिसंसर्गम्
आख्यातं च हनूमता ।
नयने मुखवर्णं च
भर्तुस्तत्सदृशं मुखम् ।
केशान् केशान्तदेशं च
तं च चूडामणिं शुभम् ।
एतैः सर्वैरभिज्ञानैः
अभिज्ञाय सुदुःखिता ।
विजगर्हेऽत्र कैकेयीम्
कोशन्ती कुररी यथा ॥
Seeing the head and the great bow, remembering the compact with Sugreeva that Hanumān had mentioned, and noticing the resemblance of the eyes and the complexion of the face to her husband’s, and similarly the hair, the forehead and the beautiful crest jewel, and convinced by all those proofs, Seetā plunged in grief, wailing like a female osprey, cursing Kaikēyee.
6.32.4
सकामा भव कैकेयि
हतोऽयं कुलनन्दनः ।
कुलमुत्सादितं सर्वम्
त्वया कलहशीलया ॥
You have your wish now, Kaikēyee; the delight of his race is slain and the entire family has been torn up by the roots by you with your propensity for sowing discord.
6.32.5
आर्येण किं ते कैकेयि
कृतं रामेण विप्रियम् ।
यन्मया चीरवसनः
त्वया प्रस्थापितो वनम् ॥
What did the noble Rāma do to offend you, O Kaikēyee, that made you exile him to the forest clad in tree bark along with me?
6.32.6
एवमुक्त्वा तु वैदेहीम्
वेपमाना तपस्विनी ।
जगाम जगतीं बाला
छिन्ना तु कदली यथा ॥
Saying this, the poor young and suffering Vaidēhee fell down on the ground shaking like a plantain tree that was axed.
6.32.7
सा मुहूर्तात् समाश्वस्य
प्रतिलभ्य च चेतनाम् ।
तच्छिरः समुपास्थाय
विललापायतेक्षणा ॥
Recovering after a while and coming to her senses and drawing that head close to her, she of wide eyes lamented:
6.32.8
हा हतास्मि महाबाहो
वीरव्रतमनुव्रत ।
इमां ते पश्चिमावस्थाम्
गतास्मि विधवा कृता ॥
Alas, I am undone, O you of mighty arm who have been faithful to the hero’s vow! With this final fate of yours I am now reduced to a widow.
6.32.9
प्रथमं मरणं नार्या
भर्तुर्वैगुण्यमुच्यते ।
सुवृत्त साधुवृत्तायाः
संवृत्तस्त्वं ममाग्रतः ॥
It is said that the death of a husband before that of his wife speaks of her evil conduct. But O you of exemplary conduct! You have died ahead of me in spite of my good conduct!
6.32.10
दुःखाद्दुःखं प्रपन्नाया
मग्नायाः शोकसागरे ।
यो हि मामुद्यतस्त्रातुम्
सोऽपि त्वं विनिपातितः ॥
As you made a great effort to save me from drowning in an ocean of grief hit by sorrow after sorrow, you too were struck down.
6.32.11
सा श्वश्रूर्मम कौसल्या
त्वया पुत्रेण राघव ।
वत्सेनेव यथा धेनुः
विवत्सा वत्सला कृता ॥
The doting Kousalyā, my mother-in-law, is now bereft of her offspring, O Rāghava, like a cow of its calf.
6.32.12
आदिष्टं दीर्घमायुस्ते
यैरचिन्त्यपराक्रम ।
अनृतं वचनं तेषाम्
अल्पायुरसि राघव ॥
Those who prophesied long life for you have been proved wrong, O you of unimaginable prowess! Your life has been all too short, Rāghava!
6.32.13
अथवा नश्यति प्रज्ञा
प्राज्ञस्यापि सतस्तव ।
पचत्येनं यथा कालो
भूतानां प्रभवो ह्ययम् ॥
You are wise and brilliant; but even the wise man’s brilliance deserts him, when Time that controls all creatures confounds him.
6.32.14
अदृष्टं मृत्युमापन्नः
कस्मात्त्वं नयशास्त्रवित् ।
व्यसनानामुपायज्ञः
कुशलो ह्यसि वर्जने ॥
How could you have been struck down by death, unseen, when you were so accomplished in statecraft and skilled in finding expedients to thwart adversities?
6.32.15
तथा त्वं सम्परिष्वज्य
रौद्रयाऽतिनृशंसया ।
कालरात्र्या ममाच्छिद्य
हृतः कमललोचन ॥
You are torn and dragged away from me, O you of lotus eyes, by that cruel night of destruction, which took you into her strong embrace!
6.32.16
उपशेषे महाबाहो
मां विहाय तपस्विनीम् ।
प्रियामिव समाश्लिष्य
पृथिवीं पुरुषर्षभ ॥
O bull among men, O you of the mighty arm! You lie hugging the earth as if she were your beloved, abandoning me to my suffering.
6.32.17
अर्चितं सततं यत्तत्
गन्धमाल्यैर्मया तव ।
इदं ते मत्प्रियं वीर
धनु काञ्चनभूषणम् ॥
This is that bow of yours adorned with gold, which I loved so much, O valiant one! You always made it a point to decorate it with scents and flowers, with my help.
6.32.18
पित्रा दशरथेन त्वम्
श्वशुरेण ममानघ ।
सर्वैश्च पितृभिः सार्धम्
नूनं स्वर्गे समागतः ॥
You certainly join, O sinless one, Daṡaratha, your father and my father-in-law, and all your ancestors in Heaven.
6.32.19
दिवि नक्षत्रभूतस्त्वम्
महत्कर्मकृतां प्रियम् ।
पुण्यं राजर्षिवंशं त्वम्
आत्मनः समवेक्षसे ॥
Having become a star in the sky, the love of those who have done great deeds, you will be able to see from your place your own line of royal sages of exceeding merit.
In many cultures, there is this common notion that people who have done great deeds become stars adorning the sky. Hence, sky, in this Ṡlōka is referred to as the love of people who have done great deeds.
6.32.20
किं मां न प्रेक्षसे राजन्
किं मां न प्रतिभाषसे ।
बालां बालेन सम्प्राप्ताम्
भार्यां मां सहचारिणीम् ॥
Why do you not look at me, O king, why do you not speak to me, your wife who walked with you, the young one who joined you when you too were young?
6.32.21
संश्रुतं गृह्णता पाणिम्
चरिष्यामीति यत्त्वया ।
स्मर तन्मम काकुत्स्थ
नय मामपि दुःखिताम् ॥
Remember, O scion of the Kākutsthas, you promised, taking my hand (in marriage), that you would be my constant companion! Take me, the miserable one, too with you.
6.32.22
कस्मान्मामपहाय त्वम्
गतो गतिमतां वर ।
अस्माल्लोकादमुं लोकम्
त्यक्त्वा मामपि दुःखिताम् ॥
True, you are the foremost of those who aim high; but why do you have to leave me, the grieving one, behind as you go from this world to the other world?
6.32.23
कल्याणैरुचितं गात्रम्
परिष्वक्तं मयैव तु ।
क्रव्यादैस्तच्छरीरं ते
नूनं विपरिकृष्यते ॥
Your body that deserved the best unguents and embraced by me alone will now be dragged by the wildest of beasts.
6.32.24
अग्निष्टोमादिभिर्यज्ञैः
इष्टवानाप्तदक्षिणैः ।
अग्निहोत्रेण संस्कारम्
केन त्वं तु न लप्स्यसे ॥
You performed many Yajñas like Agnishṭōma giving away ample gifts; how could you not have your last rites performed in a customary manner, with fire-rituals?
6.32.25
प्रव्रज्यामुपपन्नानाम्
त्रयाणामेकमागतम् ।
परिप्रक्ष्यति कौसल्या
लक्ष्मणं शोकलालसा ॥
Kousalyā, seeing Lakshmaṇa, only one of the three who set out on exile, returning will be consumed by grief.
6.32.26
स तस्याः परिपृच्छन्त्या
वधं मित्रबलस्य ते ।
तव चाख्यास्यते नूनम्
निशायां राक्षसैर्वधम् ॥
Quizzed by her, he would, for sure, have to convey about the slaying of your allied forces and yourself in the night by the Rākshasas.
6.32.27
सा त्वां सुप्तं हतं श्रुत्वा
मां च रक्षोगृहं गताम् ।
हृदयेनावदीर्णेन
न भविष्यति राघव ॥
Hearing that you were killed while asleep and that I have been held captive in the house of a Rākshasa, her heart will be in shatters and she will not survive, O Rāghava!
6.32.28
मम हेतोरनार्याया
अनघः पार्थिवात्मजः ।
रामः सागरमुत्तीर्य
सत्त्ववान् गोष्पदे हतः ॥
For the sake of me, the worthless one, the powerful Rāma, a sinless prince, crossed the ocean only to be killed in a cow’s-hoof puddle.
6.32.29
अहं दाशरथेनोढा
मोहात् स्वकुलपांसनी ।
आर्यपुत्रस्य रामस्य
भार्या मृत्युरजायत ॥
The son of Daṡaratha married me, who has undone the name of her family, only out of impulse. The wife of Rāma of noble lineage, turned out to be his death.
6.32.30
नूनमन्यां मया जातिम्
वारितं दानमुत्तमम् ।
याऽहमद्येह शोचामि
भार्या सर्वातिथेरपि ॥
I must have, in one of my past lives, obstructed the highest of the giveaways (of giving one’s daughter away in marriage); for, here I am grieving, being the wife of one who was charitable enough to treat everyone in the world as his esteemed guest.
6.32.31
साधु घातय मां क्षिप्रम्
रामस्योपरि रावण ।
समानय पतिं पत्न्या
कुरु कल्याणमुत्तमम् ॥
Please kill me too, quick, O Rāvaṇa, and throw me on Rāma; bring the husband and the wife together and thus perform the most auspicious of deeds!
6.32.32
शिरसा मे शिरश्चास्य
कायं कायेन योजय ।
रावणानुगमिष्यामि
गतिं भर्तुर्महात्मनः ॥
Unite my head with his head and my body with his body. I shall follow the footsteps of my husband, a Mahātma, O Rāvaṇa!
6.32.33
इति सा दुःखसन्तप्ता
विललापायतेक्षणा ।
भर्तुः शिरो धनुस्तत्र
समीक्ष्य च पुनःपुनः ॥
Thus she of the wide eyes who was burning with grief, wept, looking repeatedly at her husband’s head and at the bow.
6.32.34
एवं लालप्यमानायाम्
सीतायां तत्र राक्षसः ।
अभिचक्राम भर्त्तारम्
अनीकस्थः कृताञ्जलिः ॥
As Seetā was thus moaning and wailing, a Rākshasa sentry approached the lord with his palms joined in reverence.
6.32.35
विजयस्वार्यपुत्रेति
सोऽभिवाद्य प्रसाद्य च ।
न्यवेदयदनुप्राप्तम्
प्रहस्तं वाहिनीपतिम् ॥
After hailing him with the cry of ‘Victory to Thee, O you of noble lineage!’ he informed that Prahasta, the chief of the army, was at the door.
6.32.36
अमात्यैः सहितैः सर्वैः
प्रहस्तः समुपस्थितः ।
तेन दर्शनकामेन
अहं प्रस्थापिताः प्रभो ॥
Prahasta is here, along with all the ministers, wanting to see you. I have been sent here (to convey his arrival), my lord!
6.32.37
नूनमस्ति महाराज
राजभावात् क्षमान्वितम् ।
किञ्चिदात्ययिकं कार्यम्
तेषां त्वं दर्शनं कुरु ॥
There certainly is some emergency that requires the attention and patience of the king; be pleased therefore to see them, O great king!
6.32.38
एतच्छुत्वा दशग्रीवो
राक्षसप्रतिवेदितम् ।
अशोकवनिकां त्यक्त्वा
मन्त्रिणां दर्शनं ययौ ॥
On hearing what the Rākshasa conveyed, Rāvaṇa left the Aṡōka pleasance and went to see the officials.
6.32.39
स तु सर्वं समर्थ्यैव
मन्त्रिभिः कृत्यमात्मनः ।
सभां प्रविश्य विदधे
विदित्वा रामविक्रमम् ॥
Being well aware of the prowess of Rāma and having decided everything that he had to discus with the ministers, he entered the assembly and did the same.
6.32.40
अन्तर्धानं तु तच्छीर्षम्
तच्च कार्मुकमुत्तमम् ।
जगाम रावणस्यैव
निर्याणसमनन्तरम् ॥
The moment Rāvaṇa left, the head and that splendid bow alike disappeared.
6.32.41
राक्षसेन्द्रस्तु तैः सार्धम्
मन्त्रिभिर्भीमविक्रमैः ।
समर्थयामास तदा
रामकार्यविनिश्चयम् ॥
The Lord of Rākshasas then decided, in consultation with the Ministers of formidable prowess, what should be done regarding Rāma.
6.32.42
अविदूरस्थितान् सर्वान्
बलाध्यक्षान् हितैषिणः ।
अब्रवीत् कालसदृशो
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ॥
Rāvaṇa, the Lord of Rākshasas, who was like Yama, then told all the chiefs of the army that were standing not too far from him and were zealous in furthering his interest:
6.32.43
शीघ्रं भेरीनिनादेन
स्फुटकोणाहतेन मे ।
समानयध्वं सैन्यानि
वक्तव्यं च न कारणम् ॥
Quickly summon my forces by the sound of the drum, beating it with the drumstick with all vigor; no reasons should be given.
6.32.44
ततस्तथेति प्रतिगृह्य तद्वचो
बलाधिपास्ते सहसा महद्बलम् ।
समानयंश्चैव समागतं च ते
न्यवेदयन् भर्तरि युद्धकाङ्क्षिणि ॥
Receiving his orders and saying, ‘it will be done’, the army chiefs gathered the tremendous forces and informed their belligerent master that the forces were assembled.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे द्वात्रिंशः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty second Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
