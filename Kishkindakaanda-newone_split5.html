Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 5
In this Sarga, Hanumān reaches the Malaya mountain and introduces Rāma and Lakshmaṇa to Sugreeva. He tells their story and about his wife Seetā being abducted by a Rākshasa. He also tells Sugreeva that they are seeking his friendship.
Sugreeva feels very happy and tells Rāma that Rāma seeking his friendship is not only an honor but also of benefit to him. Then Hanumān prepares fire and Rāma and Sugreeva seal their friendship witnessed by fire. Both pledge allegiance to each other.
Sugreeva tells about his brother Vāli who has abducted his wife, and fearing whom he stays in the impenetrable Vana. Sugreeva requests Rāma to do something that would relieve him from his fears. Rāma assures Sugreeva that he would kill Vāli that very day, with his sharp arrows. Sugreeva feels happy.
As the two seal their friendship, the left eyes of Seetā, Vāli and Rāvaṇa twitch.
4.5.1
ऋष्यमूकात्तु हनुमान्
गत्वा तु मलयं गिरिम् ।
आचचक्षे तदा वीरौ
कपिराजाय राघवौ ॥
Hanumān went to the Malaya mountain from Ṛshyamūka mountain, and told about the Rāghavas, the Veeras, to the king of the Vānaras.
4.5.2
अयं रामो महाप्राज्ञ
सम्प्राप्तो दृढविक्रम ।
लक्ष्मणेन सह भ्रात्रा
रामोऽयं सत्यविक्रमः ॥
O sagacious one! O you of unfaltering prowess! This is Rāma, a man of true valor. He is here now along with his brother Lakshmaṇa.
4.5.3
इक्ष्वाकूणां कुले जातो
रामो दशरथात्मजः ।
धर्मे निगदितश्चैव
पितुर्निर्देशपारगः ॥
Rāma, son of Daṡaratha, born into the lineage of Ikshwāku, is widely known for his adherence to Dharma and fidelity in carrying out the word of his father.
4.5.4
राजसूयाश्वमेधैश्च
वह्निर्येनाभितर्पितः ।
दक्षिणाश्च तथोत्सृष्टा
गावश्शतसहस्रशः ।
तपसा सत्यवाक्येन
वसुधा येन पालिता ।
स्त्री हेतोस्तस्य पुत्रोऽयम्
रामस्त्वां शरणं गतः ॥
He, (Daṡaratha), propitiated Agni with the Rājasūya and Ashwamēdha Yajñas, giving away ample honorariums and gifting away cows in hundreds of thousands. He ruled the earth diligently upholding truth. This one, his son, had to live in the Vana because of a woman (Kaikēyee).
4.5.6
तस्यास्य वसतोऽरण्ये
नियतस्य महात्मनः ।
रावणेन हृता भार्या
स त्वां शरणमागतः ॥
While this Mahātma was leading a life of discipline in the Vana, his wife was carried off by Rāvaṇa; and he has come seeking our help.
4.5.7
भवता सख्यकामौ तौ
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
प्रतिगृह्यार्चयस्वैतौ
पूजनीयतमावुभौ ॥
These brothers, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, who are worthy of all respect and regard, have come seeking your friendship. You shall receive them with due honors.
4.5.8
श्रुत्वा हनुमतो वाक्यम्
सुग्रीवो हृष्टमानसः ।
दर्शनीयतमो भूत्वा
प्रीत्या प्रोवाच राघवम् ॥
Hearing those words of Hanumān, Sugreeva’s heart was filled with joy. With his entire being lit up, he said to Rāghava affectionately:
4.5.9
भवान्धर्मविनीतश्च
विक्रान्तस्सर्ववत्सलः ।
आख्याता वायुपुत्रेण
तत्त्वतो मे भवद्गुणाः ॥
You are an adherent of Dharma, your prowess is supreme and you are kind to all beings. I am informed of all of your virtues in detail by the son of Vāyu.
4.5.10
तन्ममैवैष सत्कारो
लाभश्चैवोत्तमः प्रभो ।
यत्त्वमिच्छसि सौहार्दम्
वानरेण मया सह ॥
O lord, that you should seek my friendship, a Vānara, is not only an honor, but also beneficial to me.
4.5.11
रोचते यदि वा सख्यम्
बाहुरेष प्रसारितः ।
गृह्यतां पाणिना पाणिः
मर्यादा बध्यतां ध्रुवा ॥
If you like my friendship, may you hold my stretched hand with your hand and let a gentlemen’s agreement bind us verily.
4.5.12
एतत्तु वचनं श्रुत्वा
सुग्रीवेण सुभाषितम् ।
सम्प्रहृष्टमना हस्तम्
पीडयामास पाणिना ।
हृद्यं सौहृदमालम्ब्य
पर्यष्वजत पीडितम् ॥
On hearing Sugreeva’s well-spoken words, he (Rāma) was delighted and warmly pressed his hand with his. With affection overwhelming his heart, he gave him a tight embrace.
4.5.13
ततो हनूमान्सन्त्यज्य
भिक्षुरूपमरिन्दमः ।
काष्ठयोस्स्वेन रूपेण
जनयामास पावकम् ।
दीप्यमानं ततो वह्निं
पुष्पैरभ्यर्च्य सत्कृतम् ।
तयोर्मध्येऽथ सुप्रीतो
निदधे सुसमाहितः ॥
Then Hanumān the subduer of enemies, discarded his mendicant guise and and assumed his original form. He created fire using fire-sticks. He worshipped the well-lit fire with flowers. Then, pleased and with sincere attention, he placed the fire between them.
The text of Rāmāyaṇa has a few minor inconsistencies starting from the fifty seventh Sarga of Araṇya Kāṇḍa, wherein Rāma turns back to his Āṡrama, after killing Māreeca.
These inconsistencies must be the result of the struggle of modern compilers of Rāmāyaṇa in reconciling differences among the various palm-leaf manuscripts from various regions of India.
We see one such minor inconsistency here. Hanumān gave up his mendicant form already (Ṡlōka 4.4.34). But the same thing is seen here again.
4.5.15
ततोऽग्निं दीप्यमानं तौ
चक्रतुश्च प्रदक्षिणम् ।
सुग्रीवो राघवश्चैव
वयस्यत्वमुपागतौ ॥
Then the two, Sugreeva and Rāghava, went around the bright fire, entering into a friendly alliance.
4.5.16
ततस्सुप्रीतमनसौ
तावुभौ हरिराघवौ ।
अन्योन्यमभिवीक्षन्तौ
न तृप्तिमुपजग्मतुः ॥
And the Vānara and Rāghava, feeling very pleased in their hearts, looked at each other (for long), as if they could not have enough of each other.
4.5.17
त्वं वयस्योऽसि मे हृद्यो
ह्येकं दुःखं सुखं च नौ ।
सुग्रीवो राघवं वाक्यम्
इत्युवाच प्रहृष्टवत् ॥
Sugreeva and Rāghava said these words of joy (to each other): ‘You are now my bosom friend. We will be one through thick and thin’.
4.5.18
ततस्सुपर्णबहुलाम्
छित्वा शाखां सुपुष्पिताम् ।
सालस्यास्तीर्य सुग्रीवो
निषसाद सराघवः ॥
And then, breaking a branch from a Sāla tree that was covered thick with leaves and flowers and placing it on the ground for a seat, Sugreeva sat on it along with Rāghava.
4.5.19
लक्ष्मणायाथ संहृष्टो
हनूमान् प्लवगर्षभः ।
शाखां चन्दनवृक्षस्य
ददौ परमपुष्पिताम् ॥
Hanumān, a bull among Vānaras, feeling very happy, offered Lakshmaṇa a branch of sandal tree covered with flowers.
4.5.20
ततः प्रहृष्टस्सुग्रीवः
श्लक्ष्णं मधुरया गिरा ।
प्रत्युवाच तदा रामम्
हर्षव्याकुललोचनः ॥
Then, Sugreeva, rejoicing, his eyes turbid with joy, said to Rāma, in a soft and gentle voice:
4.5.21
अहं विनिकृतो राम
चरामीह भयार्दितः ।
हृतभार्यो वने त्रस्तो
दुर्गमेतदुपाश्रितः ॥
O Rāma! Humiliated, robbed of wife, afraid, I took shelter in this impenetrable Vana. I wander around here feeling scared and helpless.
4.5.22
सोऽहं त्रस्तो वने भीतो
वसाम्युद्भ्रान्तचेतनः ।
वालिना निकृतो भ्रात्रा
कृतवैरश्च राघव ॥
O Rāghava! Humiliated by my brother Vāli, my sworn enemy, I live in this Vana trembling with fear, my mind in a perpetual flutter of anxiety.
4.5.23
वालिनो मे महाभाग
भयार्तस्याभयं कुरु ।
कर्तुमर्हसि काकुत्स्थ
भयं मे न भवेद्यथा ॥
Save me from the fear of Vāli, O blessed one, of whom I go in constant dread. You should, O Kākutstha, do what would abolish my fears.
4.5.24
एवमुक्तस्तु तेजस्वी
धर्मज्ञो धर्मवत्सलः ।
प्रत्यभाषत काकुत्स्थः
सुग्रीवं प्रहसन्निव ॥
Thus implored, Kākutstha, the powerful one, who knows and protects Dharma, responded to Sugreeva with a smile:
4.5.25
उपकारफलं मित्रम्
विदितं मे महाकपे ।
वालिनं तं वधिष्यामि
तव भार्यापहारिणम् ॥
O great Vānara! I know that a friend in need is a friend indeed. I will slay that Vānara who abducted your wife.
4.5.26
अमोघास्सूर्यसङ्काशा
ममैते निशिताश्शराः ।
तस्मिन्वालिनि दुर्वृत्ते
निपतिष्यन्ति वेगिताः ।
कङ्कपत्रप्रतिच्छन्ना
महेन्द्राशनिसन्निभाः ।
तीक्ष्णाग्रा ऋजुपर्वाणः
सरोषा भुजगा इव ॥
These excellent and sharp arrows of mine, with sun-like flash and burn, with pointed heads, winged with the feathers of Kaṅka bird, rivaling the thunderbolt of the great Indra, with their Parvas in perfect alignment, shot with force, will fall like enraged serpents, on Vāli of evil character.
4.5.28
तमद्य वालिनं पश्य
तीक्ष्णैराशीविषोपमैः ।
शरैर्विनिहतं भूमौ
विकीर्णमिव पर्वतम् ॥
You shall see Vāli fallen on the ground right this day, like a mountain thrown away, hit by sharp arrows that rival venomous serpents.
4.5.29
स तु तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राघवस्यात्मनो हितम् ।
सुग्रीवः परमप्रीतः
सुमहद्वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Sugreeva, hearing those words of Rāghava that augured well for him, was overjoyed, and he replied in words that were deeply felt:
4.5.30
तव प्रसादेन नृसिंह राघव
प्रियां च राज्यं च समाप्नुयामहम् ।
तथा कुरु त्वं नरदेव वैरिणं
यथा न हिंस्यात्स पुनर्ममाग्रजम् ॥
O Rāghava! O lion among men! By your grace, I shall regain both my kingdom and my beloved. O lord of people! Today, do what is necessary to keep my elder brother, my foe, from tormenting me.
4.5.31
सीता कपीन्द्रक्षणदाचराणां
राजीवहेमज्वलनोपमानि ।
सुग्रीवरामप्रणयप्रसङ्गे
वामानि नेत्राणि समं स्फुरन्ति ॥
When Sugreeva and Rāma thus sealed their friendship, the left eyes of Seetā, of the King of the Vānaras (Vāli), and of the Rākshasa (Rāvaṇa), which were of the hue of the lotus, of gold and of fire respectively, twitched simultaneously.
A twitching left eye is considered a bad omen for men and good omen for women.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे पञ्चमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
