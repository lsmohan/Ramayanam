Baala Kaanda - Sarga 5
The previous four Sargas gave the background of how Rāmāyaṇa got to be written and how it went to the people.
The actual story of Rāmāyaṇa starts with this Sarga. It gives a splendid description of the city of Ayōdhyā, including its physical layout, its people and their activities. It is surprising how closely this description matches with any modern city that is doing exceptionally well.
1.5.1
सर्वापूर्वमियं येषाम्
आसीत्कृत्स्ना वसुन्धरा ।
प्रजापतिमुपादाय
नृपाणां जयशालिनाम् ॥
This earth, in all its entirety and fullness has been under the care of many successful kings starting from Prajāpati.
The origin of the earth has been imagined and conceived in many ways by many peoples on the face of the earth. Even with all the advances in sciences today there are still gaps in our understanding of the origin of earth and there are many imaginations to fill those gaps in the folklore of science.
Poems like Rāmāyaṇa (and their counterparts in other parts of the world) record the legend of the origin of earth as known at that time.
But the most important aspect that needs to be appreciated from these starting Ṡlōkas, is that the longing of humans to trace back to their origins is eternal and persistent.
1.5.2
येषां स सगरो नाम
सागरो येन खानितः ।
षष्टिः पुत्रसहस्राणि
यं यान्तं पर्यवारयन् ॥
One among them is known as Sagara Who had sixty thousand sons. He dug the earth to create oceans and hence oceans are known as Sāgaras.
1.5.3
इक्ष्वाकूणामिदं तेषाम्
राज्ञां वंशे महात्मनाम् ।
महदुत्पन्नमाख्यानम्
रामायणमिति श्रुतम् ॥
This glorious story known as Rāmāyaṇa emerged from the descendants of the dynasty of great king Ikshwāku.
1.5.4
तदिदं वर्तयिष्यामि
सर्वं निखिलमादितः ।
धर्मकामार्थसहितम्
श्रोतव्यमनसूयया ॥
I will spread this in the entire world. This story, which delves into the aspects of Dharma, Artha and Kāma is worth listening to from end to end without apprehensions and prejudices.
This Ṡlōka, perhaps, is the most important Ṡlōka about Rāmāyaṇa. It tells why and how one should care about Rāmāyaṇa.
1.5.5
कोसलो नाम मुदितः
स्फीतो जनपदो महान् ।
निविष्टः सरयूतीरे
प्रभूतधनधान्यवान् ॥
The great and vast country known as Kōsala, on the banks of the river Sarayu, is replete with food and riches and is full of happiness.
Of late, we are recognizing that a country should not be measured by its GNP (Gross National Product), but by its happiness index.
It is interesting that,that is exactly the measure mentioned in this Ṡlōka, regarding the country Kōsala, in addition to the GNP (Dhana, Dhānya).
1.5.6
अयोध्या नाम नगरी
तत्रासील्लोकविश्रुता ।
मनुना मानवेन्द्रेण
या पुरी निर्मिता स्वयम् ॥
The well known city called Ayōdhyā is in that kingdom. It is built by Manu, the lord of peoples, himself.
1.5.7
आयता दश च द्वे च
योजनानि महापुरी ।
श्रीमती त्रीणि विस्तीर्णा
सुविभक्तमहापथा ॥
It is a rich and big city that spans an area of twelve Yōjanas in length and three Yōjanas in width, divided and connected by well laid out grand avenues.
Yōjana is a measure of distances in those days. It is interpreted to be equivalent to 5 miles by some scholars and 8 miles by others.
1.5.8
राजमार्गेण महता
सुविभक्तेन शोभिता ।
मुक्तपुष्पावकीर्णेन
जलसिक्तेन नित्यशः ॥
It is resplendent with well divided royal pathways that are washed daily and decorated with flowers strewn around.
1.5.9
तां तु राजा दशरथो
महाराष्ट्रविवर्द्धनः ।
पुरीमावासयामास
दिवं देवपतिर्यथा ॥
King Daṡaratha, the developer of mighty kingdoms, made this city as his abode just as the chief of Dēvas made Heaven as his abode.
1.5.10
कवाटतोरणवतीम्
सुविभक्तान्तरापणाम् ।
सर्वयन्त्रायुधवतीम्
उपेतां सर्वशिल्पिभिः ॥
It is a gated city with arched entrances. It has well laid out market streets. It is full of machineries and armories. People with skills in building every type of artifact lived there.
1.5.11
सूतमागधसम्बाधाम्
श्रीमतीमतुलप्रभाम् ।
उच्चाट्टालध्वजवतीम्
शतघ्नीशतसङ्कुलाम् ॥
It is teeming with eulogists and genealogists. It is shining with abundance, surrounded by stately edifices decorated with flags, and hundreds of cannons mounted on them.
When things are in abundance, skills and arts of every sort, even those of obscure value, flourish. People can make a living even by singing eulogies and praising genealogies. A city bristling with them speaks for its prosperity.
1.5.12
वधूनाटकसङ्घैश्च
संयुक्तां सर्वतः पुरीम् ।
उद्यानाम्रवणोपेताम्
महतीं सालमेखलाम् ॥
It is wide and big. It is full of mango orchards. It is skirted by the majestic Sāla trees. It is surrounded by many great suburbs. It has many female performing artists.
The grandeur of the city of Ayōdhyā matches our imagination of any great modern city.
However, today's riches are built on top of painful damage to the environment.
This description of Ayōdhyā gives the hope that it is indeed possible to build great cities without damaging the environment.
1.5.13
दुर्गगम्भीरपरिघाम्
दुर्गामन्यैर्दुरासदाम् ।
वाजिवारणसम्पूर्णाम्
गोभिरुष्ट्रैः खरैस्तथा ॥
It has fortifications and impassable moats to protect it from invaders. It boasts of numerous horses, elephants, cows, camels and donkeys.
1.5.14
सामन्तराजसङ्घैश्च
बलिकर्मभिरावृताम् ।
नानादेशनिवासैश्च
वणिग्भिरुपशोभिताम् ॥
It is filled with provincial kings who arrive there to pay their dues and tributes. Its splendor is accentuated by the presence of businessmen who arrive from a wide variety of lands.
1.5.15
प्रासादै रत्नविकृतैः
पर्वतैरिवशोभिताम् ।
कूटागारैश्च सम्पूर्णाम्
इन्द्रस्येवामरावतीम् ॥
It is like Amarāvati, the city of Indra filled with palaces and mansions adorned with precious stones and rising like beautiful mountains.
There is a natural beauty to the mountains. Bringing the same beauty out of artificial buildings is not easy.
But the aesthetic finish and facades of the palaces and mansions of Ayōdhyā make them comparable to mountains in beauty and splendor.
1.5.16
चित्रामष्टापदाकाराम्
वरनारीगणैर्युताम् ।
सर्वरत्नसमाकीर्णाम्
विमानगृहशोभिताम् ॥
The amazing city with its octagonal layout shines with high rise buildings, varieties of precious stones and the best of women folk.
1.5.17
गृहगाढामविच्छिद्राम्
समभूमौ निवेशिताम् ।
शालितण्डुलसम्पूर्णाम्
इक्षुकाण्डरसोदकाम् ॥
It is filled with residences built on leveled ground abutting each other densely. Food grains like rice are aplenty and the water is sweet like sugar cane juice.
1.5.18
दुन्दुभीभिर्मृदङ्गैश्च
वीणाभिः पणवैस्तथा ।
नादितां भृशमत्यर्थम्
पृथिव्यां तामनुत्तमाम् ॥
That exceptional city echoed with the sounds of musical instruments like Veeṇa, Mṛdaṅga, Dundhubhi and Paṇava.
1.5.19
विमानमिव सिद्धानाम्
तपसाधिगतं दिवि ।
सुनिवेशितवेश्मान्ताम्
नरोत्तमसमावृताम् ॥
Like an abode afloat in paradise with souls that arrive there through Tapa it is filled with exquisitely built dwellings full of the best of people in the world.
1.5.20
ये च बाणैर्न विध्यन्ति
विविक्तमपरापरम् ।
शब्दवेध्यं च विततम्
लघुहस्ता विशारदाः ।
सिंहव्याघ्रवराहाणाम्
मत्तानां नर्दतां वने ।
हन्तारो निशितैः शस्त्रैः
बलाद्बाहुबलैरपि ।
तादृशानां सहस्रैस्तां
अभिपूर्णां महारथैः ।
पुरीमावासयामास
राजा दशरथस्तदा ॥
That city, where Daṡaratha lived, has thousands of highly skilled and great warriors, capable of killing the roaring and virulent lions, tigers, boars in a jungle with or without weapons, yet principled enough not to aim their arrows at people who are found lonely or fleeing away in fear, or not having reasonable support in their defense, or not being seen, but only heard.
1.5.23
तामग्निमद्भिर्गुणवद्भिरावृतां
द्विजोत्तमैर्वेदषडङ्गपारगैः ।
सहस्रदैः सत्यरतैर्महात्मभिः
महर्षिकल्पैर्ऋषिभिश्च केवलैः ॥
The Brāhmaṇas of the city are Mahātmas, virtuous, established in upholding truth, and well versed in Vēdas and the six Vēdāngas performing the Agni rituals as per the custom. Many of them are comparable to Maharshis and others can be called Ṛshis in their own right.
Six Vēdāngas: Ṡiksha, Vyākaraṇa, Chandas, Nirukta, Jyōtisha and Kalpa.
It is interesting that Brāhmaṇas of the city are mentioned at the end, after everything and everyone else.
Brāhmaṇas in cities are like scientists employed by the industry, with focus on 'applied research'.
Brāhmaṇas in the Vanas are like scientists in the laboratories, with focus on 'fundamental research'. Nature is their laboratory.
This Ṡlōka describes the Brāhmaṇas who live in the cities. Note that this Ṡlōkas compares them with Ṛshis, but doesn't call them quite so.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे पञ्चमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
