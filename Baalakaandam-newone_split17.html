Baala Kaanda - Sarga 17
The British were baffled and had to take notice, when the quiet and docile people of India suddenly marched to the seas to make salt in every corner of the vast country in protest of the tax on salt.
The quiet, dignified, friendly and orderly nation suddenly raised its defiant voice when things culminated to that point and when it found a leader in Mahātma Gandhi.
Now, think about an oppression and disrespect that is multiple times larger, stretched over a period that spanned generations and against people that were dignified, orderly and pursuing higher goals in life. Think about the moment when they had to rebel and put an end to the oppressing force.
Think about the time when the powers of Dēvas, Siddhas, Uragas, Ṛshis, Vidyādharas and others, who found hope and leadership in the incarnation of Vishṇu, took the form of Vānaras and inundated the earth, like thick clouds and vast mountains.
This Sarga brings that scene to life.
1.17.1
पुत्रत्वं तु गते विष्णौ
राज्ञस्तस्य महात्मनः ।
उवाच देवताः सर्वाः
स्वयम्भूर्भगवानिदम् ॥
As Vishṇu was to be born as the son of that king, Brahma said to all the Dēvas:
1.17.2
सत्यसन्धस्य वीरस्य
सर्वेषां नो हितैषिणः ।
विष्णोः सहायान् बलिनः
सृजध्वं कामरूपिणः ।
मायाविदश्च शूरांश्च
वायुवेगसमान् जवे ।
नयज्ञान् बुद्धिसम्पन्नान्
विष्णुतुल्यपराक्रमान् ।
असंहार्यानुपायज्ञान्
सिंहसंहननान्वितान् ।
सर्वास्त्रगुणसम्पन्नान्
अमृतप्राशनानिव ।
अप्सरस्सु च मुख्यासु
गन्धर्वीणां तनूषु च ।
सृजध्वं हरिरूपेण
पुत्रांस्तुल्यपराक्रमान् ॥
Create great many helpers to the valiant Vishṇu, who is the well-wisher of all of us and is known for keeping his word at any cost. Give birth to many sons in the form of Vānaras through the Apsara and Gandharva women with divine bodies and with knowledge of all weapons, who are strong and courageous, who can change form at will, who can move with the speed of wind, who are highly intelligent and strategic in their thinking, who cannot be killed, who know Upāyas, who can be counted as peers in their valor to Vishṇu and to all of you that have taken Amṛta.
Amṛta is the nectar that gave the Dēvas immortality and perpetual youth.
1.17.6
पूर्वमेव मया सृष्टो
जाम्बवानृक्षपुङ्गवः ।
जृम्भमाणस्य सहसा
मम वक्त्रादजायत ॥
I have already created Jāmbavān in the form of a bear. He was born from my face when I yawned.
Rāvaṇa must be so strong that so many mightily strong Vānaras need to be created to kill him.
1.17.7
ते तथोक्ता भगवता
तत् प्रतिश्रुत्य शासनम् ।
जनयामासुरेवं ते
पुत्रान् वानररूपिणः ॥
They agreed to what they were told and created many sons in the form of Vānaras.
1.17.8
ऋषयश्च महात्मानः
सिद्धविद्याधरोरगाः ।
चारणाश्च सुतान्वीरान्
ससृजुर्वनचारिणः ॥
The great Ṛshis, Siddhas, Vidyādharas, Uragas and Cāraṇas also created many valiant sons in the form of Vānaras.
1.17.9
वानरेन्द्रं महेन्द्राभम्
इन्द्रो वालिनमूर्जितम् ।
सुग्रीवं जनयामास
तपनस्तपतां वरः ॥
Indra created Vāli, with mighty body comparable to Mahēndra mountain. Surya, scorcher of scorchers created Sugreeva.
1.17.10
बृहस्पतिस्त्वजनयत्
तारं नाम महाहरिम् ।
सर्ववानरमुख्यानाम्
बुद्धिमन्तमनुत्तमम् ॥
Bṛhaspati created a great Vānara called Tāra, who stood out among all the Vānaras and was unexcelled in his intellect.
1.17.11
धनदस्य सुतः श्रीमान्
वानरो गन्धमादनः ।
विश्वकर्मा त्वजनयन्
नलं नाम महाहरिम् ॥
Kubēra created a Vānara by name Gandhamādana with rich bearing. Viṡwakarma created a great Vānara by name Nala.
Kubēra holds the Finance Portfolio in Indra's cabinet. Viṡwakarma is the chief architect and engineer of Dēvas.
1.17.12
पावकस्य सुतः श्रीमान्
नीलोऽग्निसदृशप्रभः ।
तेजसा यशसा वीर्यात्
अत्यरिच्यत वानरान् ॥
Agni created Neela, with radiance equal to fire and with rich bearing who stood out among all the Vānaras in valor, reputation and radiance.
1.17.13
रूपद्रविणसम्पन्नौ
अश्विनौ रूपसम्मतौ ।
मैन्दं च द्विविदं चैव
जनयामासतुः स्वयम् ॥
The Ashwini Dēvas known for being the most handsome, created the handsome Mainda and Dwivinda.
1.17.14
वरुणो जनयामास
सुषेणं नाम वानरम् ।
शरभं जनयामास
पर्जन्यस्तु महाबलम् ॥
Varuṇa created Sushēṇa, a great Vānara. Parjanya created the mighty Ṡarabha.
1.17.15
मारुतस्यात्मजः श्रीमान्
हनुमान्नाम वानरः ।
वज्रसंहननोपेतो
वैनतेयसमो जवे ॥
Māruta, the God of Wind created the valiant Hanumān with lustrous bearing and a body that was as strong as diamond and speed that was equivalent to that of Vainatēya.
Vainatēya is Garuḍa, the eagle, son of Vinata and vehicle of Vishṇu.
1.17.16
ते सृष्टा बहुसाहस्रा
दशग्रीववधे रताः ।
अप्रमेयबला वीरा
विक्रान्ताः कामरूपिणः ॥
They created many thousands of warriors of supreme strength, that could change their form at will, determined on killing the ten headed Rāvaṇa.
1.17.17
मेरुमन्दरसङ्काशा
वपुष्मन्तो महाबलाः ।
ऋक्षवानरगोपुच्छाः
क्षिप्रमेवाभिजज्ञिरे ॥
Soon, many mighty Vānaras, bears and Gōpuccas sprang up with bodies that were like Mēru and Mandara mountains.
Gōpuccas is another species among apes.
1.17.18
यस्य देवस्य यद्रूपम्
वेषो यश्च पराक्रमः ।
अजायत समस्तेन
तस्य तस्य सुतः पृथक् ॥
Each of them thus created, inherited the strengths and features of the Dēvas who created them.
1.17.19
गोलाङ्गूलीषु चोत्पन्नाः
केचित्सम्मतविक्रमाः ।
ऋक्षीषु च तथा जाता
वानराः किन्नरीषु च ॥
Vānaras with noted prowess were born to Kinnara, Ṛksha, and Gōlāṅgūla women.
Gōlāṅgūla is another species among apes.
1.17.20
देवा महर्षिगन्धर्वाः
तार्क्ष्या यक्षा यशस्विनः ।
नागाः किम्पुरुषाश्चैव
सिद्धविद्याधरोरगाः ।
बहवो जनयामासुः
हृष्टास्तत्र सहस्रशः ।
वानरान् सुमहाकायान्
सर्वान् वै वनचारिणः ।
अप्सरस्सु च मुख्यासु
तथा विद्याधरीषु च ।
नागकन्यासु च तथा
गन्धर्वीणां तनूषु च ॥
In all, the many prominent Dēvas, Gandharvas, Tārkshyas, Yakshas, Nāgas, Kimpurushas, Siddhas, Vidyādhara and Uragas created thousands of Vānaras with mighty bodies through the Apsara, Vidyādhara, Nāgakanya and Gandharva women.
1.17.23
कामरूपबलोपेता
यथाकामं विचारिणः ।
सिंहशार्दूलसदृशा
दर्पेण च बलेन च ॥
The Vānaras were strong, capable of changing form at will and capable of going to places as they wished and comparable to lions and tigers in their proud demeanor and strength.
1.17.24
शिलाप्रहरणाः सर्वे
सर्वे पादपयोधिनः ।
नखदंष्ट्रायुधाः सर्वे
सर्वे सर्वास्त्रकोविदाः ॥
They were experts in the use of all forms of weapons. They could turn everything into a weapon including their nails and teeth, and the trees and boulders around.
For some people, the superlatives in these Ṡlōkas may seem ridiculous. I want to remind them that Rāmāyaṇa was written for singing. These superlatives would sound fantastic in the right art form of singing. Especially, people in south India, familiar with Burra Katha and Yaksha Gāna would know what I am saying. If you are not familiar with any of these art forms, just listen to a good romantic song in any of the movies.
Then you would know how to enjoy superlatives and the poetry without getting caught up in 'logic'.
1.17.25
विचालयेयुः शैलेन्द्रान्
भेदयेयुः स्थिरान् द्रुमान् ।
क्षोभयेयुश्च वेगेन
समुद्रं सरितां पतिम् ॥
They could shake mountains, uproot strong and well rooted trees, agitate even the great oceans, into which all the great rivers merge, with their great speed.
1.17.26
दारयेयुः क्षितिं पद्भ्याम्
आप्लवेयुर्महार्णवम् ।
नभःस्थलं विशेयुश्च
गृह्णीयुरपि तोयदान् ॥
They could shatter the earth by stamping with their bare feet. They could jump high enough into the sky to catch the clouds. They could jump long enough to cross mighty oceans.
1.17.27
गृह्णीयुरपि मातङ्गान्
मत्तान् प्रव्रजतो वने ।
नर्दमानाश्च नादेन
पातयेयुर्विहङ्गमान् ॥
They could effortlessly subdue the wildest elephants in rut roaming in the wild. They could make birds drop down from the sky just with their roaring voices.
1.17.28
ईदृशानां प्रसूतानि
हरीणां कामरूपिणाम् ।
शतं शतसहस्राणि
यूथपानां महात्मनाम् ॥
Hundreds of a hundred thousand warriors like these were created who could change their form at will and who were capable of leading great armies.
You can easily relate the scene described in these Ṡlōkas, if you have been to mountains and forests full of monkeys, such as Hampi.
Hundreds of thousands of them suddenly spring up all around you, making your heart beat twice or thrice as fast in a matter of seconds.
1.17.29
ते प्रधानेषु यूथेषु
हरीणां हरियूथपाः ।
बभूवुर्यूथपश्रेष्ठा
वीरांश्चाजनयन् हरीन् ॥
Those warriors became the chiefs of armies and raised many armies of brave soldiers.
1.17.30
अन्ये ऋक्षवतः प्रस्थान्
उपतस्थुः सहस्रशः ।
अन्ये नानाविधान् शैलान्
काननानि च भेजिरे ॥
Some of them, in their thousands, lived on the sierras of Ṛkshavān. Some lived in other mountains, hillocks and forests.
1.17.31
सूर्यपुत्रं च सुग्रीवम्
शक्रपुत्रं च वालिनम् ।
भ्रातरावुपतस्थुस्ते
सर्व एव हरीश्वराः ।
नलं नीलं हनूमन्तं
अन्यांश्च हरियूथपान् ॥
All those Vānaras were led by Vāli, the son of Indra and Sugreeva, the son of Surya, Hanumān, Nala, Neela and other chiefs.
1.17.32
ते तार्क्ष्यबलसम्पन्नाः
सर्वे युद्धविशारदाः ।
विचरन्तोऽर्दयन् दर्पात्
सिंहव्याघ्रमहोरगान् ॥
Those Vānaras, well versed in the art of war having strength equivalent to Garuḍa, moved around arrogantly, becoming a nightmare even for the tigers, lions and mighty snakes.
1.17.33
तांश्च सर्वान् महाबाहुः
वाली विपुलविक्रमः ।
जुगोप भुजवीर्येण
ऋक्षगोपुच्छवानरान् ॥
Vāli of mighty arm and great prowess gave protection to all those Vānaras, Gōpuccas and Ṛkshas under the strength of his arms.
1.17.34
तैरियं पृथिवी शूरैः
सपर्वतवनार्णवा ।
कीर्णा विविधसंस्थानैः
नानाव्यञ्जनलक्षणैः ॥
The mountains, forests and oceans were flooded with a variety of species of Vānaras, each with its own distinctive features.
1.17.35
तैर्मेघवृन्दाचलकूटकल्पैः
महाबलैर्वानरयूथपालैः ।
बभूव भूर्भीमशरीररूपैः
समावृता रामसहायहेतोः ॥
Those swarms of Vānaras of mighty strength and fearsome bodies and shapes, along with their chiefs descended on earth to assist Rāma, covering it like ranges of mountains and swaths of clouds.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे सप्तदशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventeenth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
