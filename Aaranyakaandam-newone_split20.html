Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 20
In this Sarga, the Rākshasas, fourteen in all, go to Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, led by Ṡūrpaṇakhā. Upon seeing them, Rāma tells Lakshmaṇa to stand guard for Seetā.
Rāma tells the Rākshasas who he is and asks them why they harass him. He warns them that he will kill them for their sin of harming the Ṛshis, unless they flee away. Quite unaware of the strength of Rāma, they mock at him saying that it would be him who would die, if at all he could stand against them in the fight. They hurl their spears at Rāma and Rāma shatters them with his arrows. Then with fourteen more arrows he kills everyone of them.
Ṡūrpaṇakhā runs again back to Khara and cries pitifully, narrating all that that has happened.
3.20.1
ततश्शूर्पणखा घोरा
राघवाश्रममागता ।
राक्षसानाचचक्षे तौ
भ्रातरौ सह सीतया ॥
Then the dreadful Ṡūrpaṇakhā, came to Rāghava’s Āṡrama and showed the Rākshasas the two brothers and Seetā.
3.20.2
ते रामं पर्णशालायां
उपविष्टं महाबलम् ।
ददृशुस्सीतया सार्धं
वैदेह्या लक्ष्मणेन च ॥
They saw Rāma of immense strength seated in the thatched cottage along with Vaidēhee and Lakshmaṇa.
3.20.3
तान्दृष्ट्वा राघवश्श्रीमान्
आगतांस्तां च राक्षसीम् ।
अब्रवीद्भ्रातरं रामो
लक्ष्मणं दीप्ततेजसम् ॥
On seeing them and the Rākshasi, the blessed Rāghava said to his brother Lakshmaṇa of fiery radiance:
3.20.4
मुहूर्तं भव सौमित्रे
सीतायाः प्रत्यनन्तरः ।
इमानस्या वधिष्यामि
पदवीमागतानिह ॥
O Lakshmaṇa, stay here with Seetā for a moment. I will kill these (Rākshasas) who have treaded here following this (Rākshasi).
3.20.5
वाक्यमेतत्ततश्श्रुत्वा
रामस्य विदितात्मनः ।
तथेति लक्ष्मणो वाक्यं
रामस्य प्रत्यपूजयत् ॥
Hearing those words of Rāma, who was fully aware of who he was, Lakshmaṇa said the words, ‘Certainly, I will!’, with all due regard.
3.20.6
राघवोऽपि महच्चापं
चामीकरविभूषितम् ।
चकार सज्यं धर्मात्मा
तानि रक्षांसि चाब्रवीत् ॥
And Rāghava, the Dharmātma, strung his massive bow adorned with gold, and said to those Rākshasas:
3.20.7
पुत्रौ दशरथस्यावां
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
प्रविष्टौ सीतया सार्धं
दुश्चरं दण्डकावनम् ।
फलमूलाशनौ दान्तौ
तापसौ धर्मचारिणौ ।
वसन्तौ दण्डकारण्ये
किमर्थमुपहिंसथ ॥
We are Rāma and Lakshmaṇa, the sons of Daṡaratha. We have come with Seetā to this Daṇḍaka Vana which is difficult to traverse. We are leading a disciplined life, like a Tapasvi, in this Daṇḍaka Araṇya subsisting on fruits and roots. Why do you harass us?
3.20.9
युष्मान्पापात्मकान्हन्तुं
विप्रकारान्महाहवे ।
ऋषीणां तु नियोगेन
प्राप्तोऽहं सशरायुधः ।
तिष्ठतैवात्र सन्दुष्टा
नोपावर्तितुमर्हथ ।
यदि प्राणैरिहार्थो वा
निवर्तध्वं निशाचराः ॥
I am here, armed with bow and arrow, to put up a fierce fight with you. I will kill you evil creatures, as requested by the Ṛshis whom you molest! You wicked Night-rovers, stay there, without turning back! Or run away, if you value your lives!
3.20.11
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राक्षसास्ते चतुर्दश ।
ऊचुर्त्वाचं सुसंक्रुद्धा
ब्रह्मघ्नाश्शूलपाणयः ।
संरक्तनयना घोरा
रामं रक्तान्तलोचनम्   ।
परुषा मधुराभाषं
हृष्टादृष्टपराक्रमम् ॥
The repulsive Rākshasas, murderers of Brāhmaṇas, armed with spears, fourteen in all, were enraged. They with bloodshot eyes and harsh voices, said these words to the one who had red streaks in his eyes and sweet voice, blithely, not having seen his valor yet:
3.20.13
क्रोधमुत्पाद्य नो भर्तुः
खरस्य सुमहात्मनः ।
त्वमेव हास्यसे प्राणान्
अद्यास्माभिर्हतो युधि ॥
Having provoked the wrath of our lord Khara, the supreme Mahātma, it is you who will lose your life today, slain by us in the fight.
In recent times the word Mahātma, known as a title to Mr. Gandhi, has acquired a very specific and slightly different meaning than it had during the times of Rāmāyaṇa, when it meant a person who is exceptional and distinct from common folk, in some way, at least from the eyes of the beholder, as is the case here.
3.20.14
का हि ते शक्तिरेकस्य
बहूनां रणमूर्धनी ।
अस्माकमग्रतः स्थातुं
किं पुनर्योद्दुमाहवे ॥
How can you, a lone man, even face us who are many, in the battle line, let alone fight with us?
3.20.15
एहि बाहुप्रयुक्तैर्नः
परिघैश्शूलपट्टिसैः ।
प्राणांस्त्यक्षसि वीर्यं च
धनुश्च करपीडितम् ॥
Come on, you shall lose your life, power and the bow that you are holding on to, assailed by the iron rods, spears and tridents hurled from our hands!
3.20.16
इत्येवमुक्त्वा सङ्कृद्धा
राक्षसास्ते चतुर्दश ।
चिक्षिपुस्तानि शूलानि
राघवं प्रति दुर्जयम् ॥
Saying this, the fourteen incensed Rākshasas hurled those spears at Rāghava, the invincible.
3.20.17
तानि शूलानि काकुत्स्थः
समस्तानि चतुर्दश ।
तावद्भिरेव चिच्छेद
शरैः काञ्चनभूषणैः ॥
Kākutstha destroyed all those fourteen spears with as many arrows bedecked in gold.
3.20.18
ततः पश्चान्महातेजा
नाराचान्सूर्यसन्निभान् ।
जग्राह परमक्रुद्धः
चतुर्दश शिलाशितान् ॥
Then he of supreme radiance took out in a towering rage fourteen arrows with iron heads that had been sharpened on the whetstone, and dazzling like the sun.
3.20.19
गृहीत्वा धनुरायम्य
लक्ष्यानुद्धिश्य राक्षसान् ।
मुमोच राघवो बाणान्
वज्रानिव शतक्रतुः ॥
Taking the bow and drawing it at full length, Rāghava shot the arrows at his target, the Rākshasas, as Indra hurls the thunderbolt.
3.20.20
ते भित्त्वा रक्षसां वेगात्
वक्षांसि रुधिराप्लुताः ।
विनिष्पेतुस्तदा भूमौ
न्यमज्जन्ताशनिस्वनाः ॥
With their speed, they shattered the Rākshasas, emerged wet with the blood from their breasts and rammed into the ground with the thunder of a lightning.
3.20.21
ते भिन्नहृदया भूमौ
छिन्नमूला इव द्रुमाः ।
निपेतुश्शोणितार्द्राङ्गा
विकृता विगतासवः ॥
The Rākshasas fell down dead, their hearts shattered, their bodies red with blood and disfigured, like trees that had been axed at the root.
3.20.22
तान् दृष्ट्वा पतितान्भूमौ
राक्षसी क्रोधमूर्छिता ।
परित्रस्ता पुनस्तत्र
व्यसृजद्भैरवस्वनान् ॥
On seeing them fallen on the ground, the Rākshasi swooned in anger and let off fearful yells in her fright.
3.20.23
उपगम्य खरं सा तु
किञ्चित्संशुष्कशोणिता ।
पपात पुनरेवार्ता
सनिर्यासेव सल्लकी ॥
She ran to Khara and fell again in front of him in great distress, with the blood from her wounds having more or less dried up, making her look like a vine with its resin dried.
3.20.24
भ्रातुस्समीपे शोकार्ता
ससर्ज निनदं मुहुः ।
सस्वरं मुमुचे बाष्पं
विषण्णवदना तदा ॥
She cried loudly near her brother again and again pitifully in misery, with her face changing color and shedding tears and wailing aloud.
3.20.25
निपातितान् दृश्य रणे तु राक्षसान्
प्रधाविता शूर्पणखा पुनस्ततः ।
वधं च तेषां निखिलेन रक्षसां
शशंस सर्वं भगिनी खरस्य सा ॥
Seeing the Rākshasas thus struck in fight, Ṡūrpaṇakhā ran (thus) to Khara and told everything about the slaying of all the Rākshasas.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे विंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twentieth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
