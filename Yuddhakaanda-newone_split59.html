Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 59
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa gains a better sense of the power of his enemy forces after he hears that Prahasta, the Commander-in-Chief, is slain. He then decides that he himself should go to the battlefront. Rāvaṇa orders his army to take position at the vital points of the city, so that the city, unprotected, is not ransacked by the Vānara army.
Rāma asks Vibheeshaṇa who the chief warriors are, among that army following Rāvaṇa. Vibheeshaṇa shows him Akampana, Indrajit, Atikāya, Mahōdara, Piṡāca, Triṡira, Kumbha, Nikumbha, Narāntaka, and finally Rāvaṇa himself. Rāma is mesmerized by the brilliance of Rāvaṇa.
Sugreeva takes the first shot at Rāvaṇa, by hurling a mountain peak, which Rāvaṇa shivers into pieces. Struck by another arrow Sugreeva falls unconscious. Thereupon Gavāksha, Gavaya, Sushēṇa, Ṛshabha, Jyōtirmukha and Nala, pursue Rāvaṇa, but tumble down on to the ground hit by the arrows of Rāvaṇa.
Seeing Vānaras fall down, Rama, Lakshmaṇa and Hanumān come to the forefront to tackle Rāvaṇa. Hanumān and Rāvaṇa exchange blows, each sending the other into a swoon. Seeing Hanumān fallen dazed, Neela attacks Rāvaṇa. A fierce fight breaks out between Neela and Rāvaṇa. Neela shrinks himself and alights on the chariot of Rāvaṇa, and jumps from the flagstaff to the bow to the crown of Rāvaṇa, amusing everyone. Rāvaṇa then hurls Agni's Astra at Neela and Neela falls down to the ground.
Then Rāvaṇa goes after Lakshmaṇa and a fight breaks out between them. Rāvaṇa's bow gets broken and he gets shaken by the arrows of Lakshmaṇa, and in response he hurls Ṡakti at Lakshmaṇa. Struck by Ṡakti, Lakshmaṇa falls down. Rāvaṇa tries to crush him, but in vain. Seeing this, Hanumān delivers a deadly blow to Rāvaṇa, which sends him reeling. Hanumān then lifts and takes Lakshmaṇa to Rāma.
Then Rāma himself, with Hanumān as his mount, rushes to the forefront to fight with Rāvaṇa. Rāvaṇa then strikes Hanumān, inflicting a wound on him, which infuriates Rāma. Rāma smashes the chariot of Rāvaṇa along with its horses, charioteer, and the armory on it. He then strikes Rāvaṇa with an arrow that shakes him to the core. Rāma tells him to go back to Laṅkā, take rest and come prepared to resume the fight later. Rāvaṇa goes back to Laṅkā with his pride and spirits flogged out of him.
6.59.1
तस्मिन् हते राक्षससैन्यपाले
प्लवङ्गमानामृषभेण युद्धे ।
भीमायुधं सागरतुल्यवेगं
विदुद्रुवे राक्षसराजसैन्यम् ॥
With the Commander-in-Chief of the Rākshasa army slain by that bull among Vānaras in battle, the forces of the Rākshasa King, possessing the swiftness of the surging ocean, and armed with a formidable array of weapons could do nothing but flee.
6.59.2
गत्वाऽथ रक्षोधिपतेः शशंसुः
सेनापतिं पावकसूनुशस्तम् ।
तच्चापि तेषां वचनं निशम्य
रक्षोधिपः क्रोधवशं जगाम ॥
They went and told the King of the Commander-in-Chief’s death at the hands of the son of Agni. Hearing those words from them, The lord of Rākshasas was taken over by rage.
6.59.3
सङ्ख्ये प्रहस्तं निहतं निशम्य
शोकार्दितः क्रोधपरीतचेताः ।
उवाच तान्नैर्ऋतयोथमुख्यान्
इन्द्रो यथा निर्जरयूथमुख्यान् ॥
Hearing that Prahasta was killed in the battle, distressed by grief and his mind filled with rage, he addressed the great Rākshasa Captains as Indra might, the chiefs among the immortals.
6.59.4
नावज्ञा रिपवे कार्या
यैरिन्द्रबलसूदनः ।
सूदितः सैन्यपालो मे
सानुयात्रः सकुञ्जरः ॥
What the enemy has done, by slaying my Commander-in-Chief who had killed even the forces of Indra, along with all his elephants and warriors, cannot be dismissed as a trifle.
6.59.5
सोऽहं रिपुविनाशाय
विजयायाविचारयन् ।
स्वयमेव गमिष्यामि
रणशीर्षं तदद्भुतम् ॥
Hence, I myself will head to that amazing battlefront with no hesitation whatsoever, to destroy the enemy.
6.59.6
अद्य तद्वानरानीकम्
रामं च सहलक्ष्मणम् ।
निर्दहिष्यामि वाणौघैः
वनं दीप्तैरिवाग्निभिः ॥
I shall burn up this very day that Vānara army and Rāma and Lakshmaṇa with a spate of arrows like a wildfire does a forest.
6.59.7
स एवमुक्त्वा ज्वलनप्रकाशं
रथं तुरङ्गोत्तमराजयुक्तम् ।
प्रकाशमानं वपुषा ज्वलन्तं
समारुरोहामरराजशत्रुः ॥
Having said this, the foe of the King of the Dēvas whose body was glowing with the dazzle of fire got into his chariot to which the best of royal steeds were yoked.
6.59.8
स शङ्खभैरीपणवप्रणादैः
आस्फोटितक्ष्वेलितसिंहनादैः ।
पुण्यैः स्तवैश्चाप्यभिपूज्यमानः
तदा ययौ राक्षसराजमुख्यः ॥
And that eminent king of Rākshasas proceeded, honored by the blare of conch, beating of the kettle drum and Paṇava, the sounds of slapping shoulders, lion-like war-whoops and the singing of holy hymns.
6.59.9
स नीलजीमूतनिकाशरूपैः
मांसाशनैः पावकदीप्तनेत्रेः ।
बभौ वृतो राक्षसराजमुख्यो
भूतैर्वृतो रुद्र इवामरेशः ॥
The eminent king of Rākshasas followed by (the Rākshasas) who looked like black clouds, who gorged on flesh and whose eyes blazed red like fire, looked like Rudra, the overlord of the Dēvas surrounded by elementals.
6.59.10
ततो नगर्याः सहसा महौजा
निष्क्रम्य तद्वानरसैन्यमुग्रम् ।
महार्णवाभ्रस्तनितं ददर्शं
समुद्यतं पादपशैलहस्तम् ॥
Quickly emerging from the city, he of immense strength beheld the fierce Vānara army ready armed with trees and rocks, making a mighty din like that of the roar of the ocean and the thundering of clouds.
6.59.11
तद्राक्षसानीकमतिप्रचण्डम्
आलोक्य रामो भुजगेन्द्रबाहुः ।
विभीषणं शस्त्रभृतां वरिष्ठम्
उवाच सेनानुगतः पृथुश्रीः ॥
Rāma, whose arms resembled the king of the serpents and who glowed with his innate splendor and who was followed by his own army, saw the infuriated Rākshasa army come out, and asked Vibheeshaṇa, the foremost among those who wield weapons:
6.59.12
नानापताकाध्वजशस्त्रजुष्टं
प्रासासिशूलायुधशस्त्रजुष्टम् ।
सैन्यं महेन्द्रोपमनागजुष्टं
कस्येदमक्षोभ्यमभीरुजुष्टम् ॥
Who leads this army which, with its multitude of banners and buntings, Prāsas, swords, spears and other weapons of every kind and elephants as huge as the Mahēndra mountain, seems imperturbable and has no room for cowards?
6.59.13
ततस्तु रामस्य निशम्य वाक्यं
विभीषणः शक्रसमानवीर्यः ।
शशंस रामस्य बलप्रवेकं
महात्मनां राक्षसपुङ्गवानाम् ॥
Hearing those words of Rāma, Vibheeshaṇa, an equal to Ṡakra in valor, told him about that eminent army consisting of great bulls among Rākshasas.
6.59.14
योऽसौ गजस्कन्धगतो महात्मा
नवोदितार्कोपमताम्रवक्त्रः ।
प्रकम्पयन्नागशिरोऽभ्युपैति
ह्यकम्पनं त्वेनमवेहि राजन् ॥
The majestic one with face ruddy as the rising sun, riding on the back of the elephant making its head wobble under his weight, is Akampana, O King.
6.59.15
योऽसौ रथस्थो मृगराजकेतुः
धुन्वन्धनुः शक्रधनुःप्रकाशम् ।
करीव भात्युग्रविवृत्तदंष्ट्रः
स इन्द्रजिन्नाम वरप्रधानः ॥
The one who rides on that chariot with the lion as mascot on the flag, shaking the bow that gleams like a rainbow, his curved and fierce fangs resembling the tusks of an elephant is Indrajit, who is endowed with the power of many boons.
6.59.16
यश्चैष विन्ध्यास्तमहेन्द्रकल्पो
धन्वी रथस्थोऽतिरथोऽतिवीरः ।
विस्फारयंश्चापमतुल्यमानं
नाम्नाऽतिकायोऽतिविवृद्धकायः ॥
The super-charioter of surpassing valor, riding on that chariot, twanging his bow of giant dimensions, whose over-sized body reminds one of Vindhya and Mahēndra mountains and the mountain of the setting sun, is known to fame as Atikāya.
6.59.17
योऽसौ नवार्कोदितताम्रचक्षुः
आरुह्य घण्टानिनदप्रणादम् ।
गजं खरं गर्जति वै महात्मा
महोदरो नाम स एष वीरः ॥
The one who shouts raucously, riding on an elephant whose bells also makes a mighty din, and whose eyes are red like the rising sun is the warrior known as Mahōdara.
6.59.18
योऽसौ हयं काञ्चनचित्रभाण्डम्
आरुह्य सन्ध्याभ्रगिरिप्रकाशः ।
प्रासं समुद्यम्य मरीचिनद्धं
पिशाच एषोऽशनितुल्यवेगः ॥
The one riding on the horse which is caparisoned all over with gold, imposing like a mountain covered by clouds at sunset, holding up a glittering Prāsa is Piṡāca, who is as fast as lightning.
6.59.19
यश्चैष शूलं निशितं प्रगृह्य
विद्युत्प्रभं किङ्करवज्रवेगम् ।
वृषेन्द्रमास्थाय शशिप्रकाशम्
आयाति सोऽसौ त्रिशिरा यशस्वी ॥
The one riding on the great bull that shines white like the moon, and holds in his hand a trident that is as swift in striking and as obedient to his will as Indra’s thunderbolt, is the famous Triṡira.
The Triṡira described here must be different from the Triṡira who was killed in Janasthāna.
6.59.20
असौ च जीमूतनिकाशरूपः
कुम्भः पृथुव्यूढसुजातवक्षाः ।
समाहितः पन्नगराजकेतुः
विस्फारयन्याति धनुर्विधून्वन् ॥
The one who looks like a black cloud, whose broad and well-padded chest is comely, who has the king of serpents for a mascot and who comes twanging and shaking his bow, is Kumbha.
6.59.21
यश्चैष जाम्बूनदवज्रजुष्टम्
दीप्तं सधूमं परिघं प्रगृह्य ।
आयाति रक्षोबलकेतुभूतः
सोऽसौ निकुम्भोऽद्भुतघोरकर्मा ॥
He who comes armed with a Parigha that is decked with gold and set with brilliant gems, whose dazzle seems to rise like smoke, who has many a marvelous and terrible deed to his credit, is Nikumbha, a poster boy of the Rākshasa forces.
6.59.22
यश्चैष चापासिशरौघजुष्टं
पताकिनं पावकदीप्तरूपम् ।
रथं समास्थाय विभात्युदग्रो
नरान्तकोऽसौ नगशृङ्गयोधी ॥
The intense one who stands out, riding on the chariot that is packed with bows, swords and arrows, and adorned with flags is Narāntaka who duels with mountain peaks (for lack of an opponent to fight with who is worthy of his steel).
6.59.23
यश्चैष नानाविधघोररूपैः
व्याघ्रोष्ट्रनागेन्द्रमृगाश्ववक्त्रैः ।
भूतैर्वृतो भाति विवृत्तनेत्रैः
सोऽसौ सुराणामपि दर्पहन्ता ।
यत्रैतदिन्दुप्रतिमं विभातिः
छत्रं सितं सूक्ष्मशलाकमग्र्यम् ।
अत्रैष रक्षोधिपतिर्महात्मा
भूतैर्वृतो रुद्र इवावभाति ॥
And the one who is surrounded by many goblins of horrible shapes, with their rolling eyes and faces that resemble those of tigers, camels, tuskers, deer and horses, is the king of the Rākshasas, who vanquished the pride of even the Dēvas.
He, a Mahātma, who resembles Rudra surrounded by the hosts of elementals, dazzles the eye under that slender-ribbed white umbrella that shines like the moon.
6.59.25
असौ किरीटी चलुकुण्डलास्यो
नगेन्द्रविन्ध्योपमभीमकायः ।
महेन्द्रवैवस्वतदर्पहन्ता
रक्षोधिपः सूर्य इवावभाति ॥
With his crown and dangling ear-pendants, his body as formidable as Vindhya, the king of mountains, the ruler of the Rākshasas, who has humbled the pride of Mahēndra and Vaivaswata, blazes like the sun.
6.59.26
प्रत्युवाच ततो रामो
विभीषणमरिन्दमम् ॥
Then Rāma responded to Vibheeshaṇa, subduer of the foe:
6.59.26
अहो दीप्तो महातेजा
रावणो राक्षसेश्वरः ।
आदित्य इव दुष्प्रेक्षो
रश्मिभिर्भाति रावणः ।
न व्यक्तं लक्षये ह्यस्य
रूपं तेजस्समावृतम् ॥
What brilliance and what immense power does Rāvaṇa, the ruler of the Rākshasas, possess!
Rāvaṇa is difficult to behold like the rays of blazing sun! His true form cannot be seen clearly, covered in his dazzling radiance!
6.59.28
देवदानववीराणाम्
वपुर्नैवंविधं भवेत् ।
यादृशं राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
वपुरेतद्विराजते ॥
The body of no Dēva or Dānava Veera radiates with such a brilliance as that of the Rākshasa king.
6.59.29
सर्वे पर्वतसङ्काशाः
सर्वे पर्वतयोधिनः ।
सर्वे दीप्तायुधधरा
योधाश्चास्य महौजसः ॥
And all the fighters whom he of extraordinary power commands are huge like mountains. Armed with shining weapons, they can subdue even mountains in fight.
6.59.30
विभाति रक्षोराजोऽसौ
प्रदीप्तैर्भीमदर्शनैः ।
भूतैः परिवृतस्तीक्ष्णैः
देहवद्भिरिवान्तकः ॥
The ruler of the Rākshasas, surrounded by warriors who are dazzling with intensity and who look fierce with huge bodies, looks like Yama himself.
6.59.31
दिष्ट्याऽयमद्य पापात्मा
मम दृष्टिपथं गतः ।
अद्य क्रोधं विमोक्ष्यामि
सीताहरणसम्भवम् ॥
By the grace of Providence, this sinner has fallen in the line of my sight. And today I shall vent my wrath that was engendered by the abduction of Seetā.
6.59.32
एवमुक्त्वा ततो रामो
धनुरादाय वीर्यवान् ।
लक्ष्मणानुचरस्तस्थौ
समुद्धृत्य शरोत्तमम् ॥
Saying this, the valiant Rāma, with Lakshmaṇa by his side, stood ready with bow in hand and holding a mighty arrow up.
6.59.33
ततः स रक्षोधिपतिर्महात्मा
रक्षांसि तान्याह महाबलानि ॥
Meanwhile, the mighty ruler of the Rākshasas told the great Rākshasa army:
6.59.33
द्वारेषु चर्यागृहगोपुरेषु
सुनिर्वृतास्तिष्ठत निर्विशङ्काः ।
इहागतं मां सहितं भवद्भिः
वनौकसश्छिद्रमिदं विदित्वा ।
शून्यां पुरीं दुष्प्रसहां प्रमथ्य
प्रधर्षयेयुः सहसा समेताः ॥
Take positions comfortably on the gateways and on the towers of the houses lining the major pathways, with nothing else for you to worry about.
If the Vānaras come to know that you have accompanied me here, they might take advantage of the opportunity and ransack the city in your absence, whose defenses are otherwise formidable.
6.59.35
विसर्जयित्वा सहितांस्ततस्तान्
गतेषु रक्षस्सु यथानियोगम् ।
व्यदारयद्वानसागरौघम्
महाझषः पूर्णमिवार्णवौघम् ॥
Having thus dismissed the large contingents, and after those Rākshasas took positions as ordered, he tore his path through that vast ocean of Vānaras, like a whale tearing its way through the surging ocean.
6.59.36
तमापतन्तं सहसा समीक्ष्य
दीप्तेषुचापं युधि राक्षसेन्द्रम् ।
महत् समुत्पाट्य महीधराग्रं
दुद्राव रक्षोधिपतिं हरीशः ॥
On seeing the King of Rākshasas attack suddenly, thus armed with dazzling bow and arrows, the King of the Vānaras grabbed a huge mountain-peak and rushed upon him.
6.59.37
तच्छैलशृङ्गं बहुवृक्षसानुं
प्रगृह्य चिक्षेप निशाचराय ।
तमापतन्तं सहसा समीक्ष्य
बिभेद बाणैस्तपनीयपुङ्खैः ॥
And taking that crest with the many trees on its flanks, he hurled it at the Night-rover; who seeing it coming cut it down instantly with his arrows with golden rings on their shafts.
6.59.38
तस्मिन् प्रवृद्धोत्तमसानुवृक्षे
शृङ्गे विकीर्णे पतिते पृथिव्याम् ।
महाहिकल्पं शरमन्तकाभं
समाददे राक्षसलोकनाथः ॥
And just as that peak with the full-grown trees on it was shivered to bits and fell down on the ground, the overlord of the Rākshasa world mounted another arrow (on his bow) that was like a great serpent and was like Yama himself.
6.59.39
स तं गृहीत्वाऽनिलतुल्यवेगम्
सविस्फुलिङ्गज्वलनप्रकाशम् ।
बाणं महेन्द्राशनितुल्यवेगम्
चिक्षेप सुग्रीववधाय रुष्टः ॥
And he, the furious one, shot that arrow, which flew at the speed of the wind and that of Mahēndra’s thunderbolt, and which glowed like fire emitting sparks, to slay Sugreeva.
6.59.40
स सायको रावणबाहुमुक्तः
शक्राशनिप्रख्यवपुः शिताग्रः ।
सुग्रीवमासाद्य बिभेद वेगात्
गुहेरिता क्रौञ्चमिवोग्रशक्तिः ॥
Leaving the hand of Rāvaṇa, that sharp-edged arrow with a body that recalled Indra’s thunderbolt fell on Sugreeva and clove him, like the fierce Ṡakti hurled by Guha did the Krounca mountain.
6.59.41
स सायकार्तो विपरीतचेताः
कूजन् पृथिव्यां निपपात वीरः ।
तं प्रेक्ष्य भूमौ पतितं विसञ्ज्ञं
नेदुः प्रहृष्टा युधि यातुधानाः ॥
The valiant warrior uttered a distressed cry afflicted by that arrow, and fell down senseless on the ground.
Seeing him fallen unconscious on the ground in the fight the Rākshasas shouted in delight.
6.59.42
ततो गवाक्षो गवयः सुषेणः
त्वथर्षभो ज्योतिमुखो नलश्च ।
शैलान् समुद्यम्य विवृद्धकायाः
प्रदुद्रुवुस्तं प्रति राक्षसेन्द्रम् ॥
Thereupon Gavāksha, Gavaya, Sushēṇa, Ṛshabha, Jyōtirmukha and Nala, all of them of gigantic bodies, pursued the King of Rākshasas holding boulders high in their hands.
6.59.43
तेषां प्रहारान् स चकार मोघान्
रक्षोधिपो बाणगणैः शिताग्रैः ।
तान् वानरेन्द्रानपि बाणजालैः
विभेद जाम्बूनदचित्रपुङ्खैः ॥
The Rākshasa King brought the impact of their assault to naught with torrents of sharp-edged arrows, and he tore apart the great Vānaras themselves with arrows having golden rings on their shafts.
6.59.44
ते वानरेन्द्रास्त्रिदशारिबाणैः
भिन्ना विपेतुर्भुवि भीमकायाः ।
ततस्तु तद्वानरसैन्यमुग्रं
प्रच्छादयामास स बाणजालैः ॥
Those great Vānaras with frightful bodies, tumbled down on the ground pierced by the arrows of the foe of the Dēvas. And then he blotted that fierce army of Vānaras out from view with a torrent of arrows.
6.59.45
ते वध्यमानाः पतिताश्च वीरा
नानद्यमाना भयशल्यविद्धाः ।
शाखामृगा रावणसायकार्ता
जग्मुः शरण्यं शरणं स्म रामम् ॥
Thus beaten and struck down, those Vānara Veeras screamed, gripped by terror that bit like an arrow.
The beasts of the branches, sore and distraught by those arrows of Rāvaṇa, went seeking refuge with Rāma, the savior in need.
6.59.46
ततो महात्मा स धनुर्धनुष्मान्
आदाय रामः सहसा जगाम ।
तं लक्ष्मणः प्राञ्जलिरभ्युपेत्य
उवाच वाक्यं परमार्थयुक्तम् ॥
Then, Rāma, a Mahātma and a great bowman rushed immediately, taking a bow. Lakshmaṇa approached him with palms joined in reverence, and said these words that served the ultimate purpose:
6.59.47
काममार्यः सुपर्याप्तो
वधायास्य दुरात्मनः ।
विधमिष्याम्यहं चैतम्
अनुजानीहि मां विभो ॥
O noble one! I should suffice to slay this evil one. My lord! Please allow me, I shall make a pulp out of him!
6.59.48
तमब्रवीन्महतेजा
रामः सत्यपराक्रमः ॥
Rāma, of extraordinary power and true valor, said to him:
6.59.48
गच्छ यत्नपरश्चापि
भव लक्ष्मण संयुगे ।
रावणो हि महावीर्यो
रणेऽद्भुतपराक्रमः ।
त्रैलोक्येनापि सङ्क्रुद्धो
दुष्प्रसह्यो न संशयः ॥
Go, but make sure, O Lakshmaṇa, you watch out carefully. Rāvaṇa is a great Veera, and his prowess in battle is nothing short of amazing. Let there be no misgiving; when he is roused to wrath, not even all the three worlds coming together can stand against him.
6.59.50
तस्य च्छिद्राणि मार्गस्व
स्वच्छिद्राणि च लक्षय ।
चक्षुषा धनुषात्मानम्
गोपायस्व समाहितः ॥
Try to find out his vulnerabilities, while being cognizant of your own. Guard yourself in rapt attention with your vigilant eye and your bow.
6.59.51
राघवस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
परिष्वज्याभिपूज्य च ।
अभिवाद्य च रामाय
ययौ सौमित्रिराहवम् ॥
Hearing those words of Rāghava, Sowmitri proceeded to the battle field, after embracing him, saluting him, and paying him respects.
6.59.52
स रावणं वारणहस्तबाहुम्
ददर्श भीमोद्यतदीप्तचापम् ।
प्रच्छादयन्तं शरवृष्टिजालैः
तान्वानरान् भिन्नविकीर्णदेहान् ॥
He then saw Rāvaṇa whose arms resembled the trunks of elephants and who held up a frightful and dazzling bow in his hand and who blotted out from view the Vānaras of torn and mangled bodies with his shoals of arrows.
6.59.53
तमालोक्य महातेजा
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ।
निवार्य शरजालानि
विदुर्द्राव स रावणम् ॥
(Meanwhile), the immensely powerful Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, saw Rāvaṇa and rushed on to him, stemming the tide of arrows.
6.59.54
रथं तस्य समासाद्य
भुजमुद्यम्य दक्षिणम् ।
त्रासयन् रावणं धीमान्
हनुमान् वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Reaching his chariot, Hanumān raised his right hand, and said these words, threatening Rāvaṇa, confidently:
6.59.55
देवदानवगन्धर्वैः
यक्षैश्च सह राक्षसैः ।
अवध्यत्वं त्वया प्राप्तम्
वानरेभ्यस्तु ते भयम् ॥
You obtained immunity from death at the hands of the Dēvas, the Dānavas, Gandharvas, Yakshas and the Rākshasas, but you do have cause to fear the Vānaras.
6.59.56
एष मे दक्षिणो बाहुः
पञ्चशाखः समुद्यतः ।
विधमिष्यति ते देहे
भूतात्मानं चिरोषितम् ॥
This right hand of mine, raised and with its five fingers stretched, will smash the life force that has been inhabiting your body for a long while.
6.59.57
श्रुत्वा हनुमतो वाक्यम्
रावणो भीमविक्रमः ।
संरक्तनयनः क्रोधात्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
On hearing those words of Hanumān, Rāvaṇa of formidable prowess, said these words, his eyes red with anger:
6.59.58
क्षिप्रं प्रहर निश्शङ्कम्
स्थिरां कीर्तिमवाप्नुहि ।
ततस्त्वां ज्ञातविक्रान्तम्
नाशयिष्यामि वानर ॥
Go for it without any hesitation and strike quickly so you might gain a lasting reputation. I will then know how great your prowess is before I destroy you, O Vānara!
6.59.59
रावणस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
वायुसूनुर्वचोऽब्रवीत् ।
प्रहृतं हि मया पूर्वम्
अक्षं स्मर सुतं तव ॥
Hearing those words of Rāvaṇa, the son of Vāyu said these words: '(It might serve you better to) recall how I struck down your son Aksha in the past!'
6.59.60
एवमुक्तो महातेजा
रावणो राक्षसेश्वरः ।
आजघानानिलसुतम्
तलेनोरसि वीर्यवान् ॥
Having thus been rebutted, Rāvaṇa, the Rākshasa King of immense power and valor, struck the son of Vāyu on the chest, with his open palm.
6.59.61
स तलाभिहतस्तेन
चचाल च मुहुर्मुहुः ।
स्थित्वा मुहूर्तं तेजस्वी
स्थैर्यं कृत्वा महामतिः ।
आजघानाभिसंक्रुद्धः
तलेनैवामरद्विषम् ॥
He reeled under that blow, shaking repeatedly. Steadying and gathering himself up after a while, the powerful, extremely self-assured and enraged one responded in kind, delivering a mighty blow with his open palm on the chest of the enemy of the Dēvas.
6.59.62
ततस्तलेनाभिहतो
वानरेण महात्मना ।
दशग्रीवः समाधूतो
यथा भूमिचलेऽचलः ॥
Struck thus by the open palm of the mighty Vānara, the Ten-necked tottered like a mountain in an earthquake.
6.59.63
सङ्ग्रामे तं तथा दृष्ट्वा
रावणं तलताडितम् ।
ऋषयो वानराः सिद्धा
नेदुर्देवाः सहासुरैः ॥
On seeing Rāvaṇa struck down thus in battle, the Ṛshis, the Vānaras, the Siddhas, the Dēvas and the Asuras shouted in excitement.
6.59.64
अथाश्वास्य महातेजा
रावणो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ।
साधु वानर वीर्येण
श्लाघनीयोऽसि मे रिपुः ॥
Gathering himself together, the powerful Rāvaṇa said these words: 'Well done, O Vānara! You deserve my esteem as a foe, for the valor you showed!'
6.59.65
रावणेनैवमुक्तस्तु
मारुतिर्वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
When Rāvaṇa spoke thus, Māruti responded with these words:
6.59.66
धिगस्तु मम वीर्येण
यस्त्वं जीवसि रावण ।
सकृत्तु प्रहरेदानीम्
दुर्बुद्धे किं विकत्थसे ।
ततस्त्वां मामिका मुष्टिः
नयिष्यति यमक्षयम् ॥
Damn my prowess, O Rāvaṇa, for you are still alive! Stop blustering, you evil one! You strike one more time now and you will be sent to the abode of Yama by this fist of mine!
6.59.67
ततो मारुतिवाक्येन
क्रोदस्तस्य तदाऽज्वलत् ।
संरक्तनयनो यत्नात्
मुष्टिमुद्यम्य दक्षिणम् ।
पातयामास वेगेन
वानरोरसि वीर्यवान् ॥
Māruti’s words provoked a blazing wrath in him; with bloodshot eyes, the valiant one raised the fist of his right hand and landed a swift blow on the chest of the Vānara, putting all his force behind it.
6.59.69
हनुमान् वक्षसि व्यूढे
सञ्चचाल पुनःपुनः ।
विह्वलं तं तदा दृष्ट्वा
हनुमन्तं महाबलम् ।
रथेनातिरथः शीघ्रम्
नीलं प्रति समभ्यगात् ॥
Hanumān reeled and rocked under that blow on his chest. Seeing that Hanumān of immense strength was dazed, (and leaving him alone), the mighty charioter swiftly advanced towards Neela, in his chariot.
6.59.70
राक्षसानामधिपतिः
दशग्रीवः प्रतापवान् ।
पन्नगप्रतिमैर्भीमैः
परमर्मातिभेदनौः ।
शरैरादीपयामास
नीलं हरिचमूपतिम् ॥
The valiant Ten-necked overlord of the Rākshasas burnt Neela, the Commander of the Vānara army, with fierce arrows that bit like serpents into the vitals of the foe.
6.59.72
स शरौघसमायस्तः
नीलो कपिचमूपतिः ।
करेणैकेन शेलाग्रम्
रक्षोधिपतयेऽसृजत् ॥
Oppressed by that hail of arrows, Neela, the chief of Vānara army, lifted a mountain peak with just one hand and hurled it at the Ruler of the Rākshasas.
6.59.73
हनुमानपि तेजस्वी
समाश्वस्तो महामनाः ।
विप्रेक्षमाणो युद्धेप्सुः
सरोषमिदमब्रवीत् ।
नीलेन सह संयुक्तम्
रावणं राक्षसेश्वरम् ।
अन्येन युद्ध्यमानस्य
न युक्तमभिधावनम् ॥
Meanwhile, the powerful Hanumān had fully recovered and was ready to fight again; but noticing that Rāvaṇa was engaged in combat with Neela, he, one of high principles, vented his annoyance saying, 'it would not be proper to attack one who is engaged in fighting another'.
6.59.75
रावणोऽथ महातेजाः
तं शृङ्गं सप्तभिः शरैः ।
आजघान सुतीक्ष्णाग्रैः
तद्विकीर्णं पपात ह ॥
Rāvaṇa of immense power, hit the mountain peak (hurled by Neela) with seven extremely sharp arrows, and it fell down, shivered to bits.
6.59.76
तद्विकीर्णं गिरेः शृङ्गम्
दृष्ट्वा हरिचमूपतिः ।
कालाग्निरिव जज्वाल
क्रोधोन परवीरहा ॥
On seeing that mountain peak thus smashed, the leader of the Vānara army, destroyer of enemy warriors, became fiery in his wrath, like the Conflagration that puts an end to the world does.
6.59.77
सोऽश्वकर्णान् धवान् सालान्
चूतांश्चापि सुपुष्पितान् ।
अन्यांश्च विविधान् वृक्षान्
नीलश्चिक्षेप संयुगे ॥
And Neela hurled at the foe many trees like Aṡwakarṇa, Dhava, Sāla, and Cūta and others that were in full flower.
6.59.78
स तान् वृक्षान् समासाद्य
प्रतिचिच्छेद रावणः ।
अभ्यवर्षत् सुघोरेण
शरवर्षेण पावकिम् ॥
As those trees came upon him, Rāvaṇa cut them to pieces and showered a terrible flood of arrows on the son of Agni.
6.59.79
अभिवृष्टः शरौघेण
मेघेनेव महाचलः ।
ह्रस्वं कृत्वा तदा रूपम्
ध्वजाग्रे निपपात ह ॥
Thus drenched by that flood of arrows like a great mountain by a raining cloud, he then shrunk his body and alighted on the top of the flagstaff (of Rāvaṇa’s chariot).
6.59.80
पावकात्मजमालोक्य
ध्वजाग्रे समुपस्थितम् ।
जज्वाल रावणः क्रोधात्
ततो नीलो ननाद च ॥
Seeing the son of Agni settle on top of the flagstaff Rāvaṇa became fiery with anger, and Neela crowed with delight.
6.59.81
ध्वजाग्रे धनुषश्चाग्रे
किरीटाग्रे च तं हरिम् ।
लक्ष्मणोऽथ हनूमांश्च
दृष्ट्वा रामश्च विस्मिताः ॥
Seeing that Vānara hop from the flagstaff on to the top of Rāvaṇa’s bow, and from that to the top of his crown, Lakshmaṇa, Hanumān and Rāma were amused.
6.59.82
रावणोऽथ महातेजाः
कपिलाघवविस्मितः ।
अस्त्रमाहारयामास
दीप्तमाग्नेयमद्भुतम् ॥
And Rāvaṇa, amazed at the agility of the Vānara, invoked the power of the blazing and marvelous Astra presided over by Agni.
6.59.83
ततस्ते चुक्रुशुर्हृष्टा
लब्धलक्षाः प्लवङ्गमाः ।
नीललाघवसम्भ्रान्तम्
दृष्ट्वा रावणमाहवे ॥
Seeing Rāvaṇa bewildered in that fight by Neela’s extraordinary agility, the Vānaras found a reason to cheer up and chortled with delight.
6.59.84
वानराणां च नादेन
संरब्धो रावणस्तदा ।
सम्भ्रमाविष्टहृदयो
न किञ्चित् प्रत्यपद्यत ॥
Annoyed by the noises made by the Vānaras, Rāvaṇa, his mind in a whirl, could not think straight.
6.59.85
आग्नेयेनाथ संयुक्तम्
गृहीत्वा रावणः शरम् ।
ध्वजशीर्षस्थितं नीलम्
उदैक्षत निशाचरः ॥
Grasping in his hand the arrow infused with the energy of Agni, Rāvaṇa, the Night-rover, looked at Neela, who was perched on top of the flagstaff.
6.59.86
ततोऽब्रवीन्महातेजा
रावणो राक्षसेश्वरः ॥
Then, Rāvaṇa, the extremely powerful ruler of Rākshasas said:
6.59.86
कपे लाघवयुक्तोऽसि
मायया परयाऽनया ।
जीवितं खलु रक्षस्व
यदि शक्तोऽसि वानर ।
तानि तान्यात्मरूपाणि
सृजसि त्वमनेकशः ॥
O Vānara! You sure are quite skillful and are good at these exceptional magic tricks! But try saving your life if you can, O Vānara, rather than taking up a multitude of forms!
6.59.88
तथापि त्वां मया युक्तः
सायकोऽस्त्रप्रयोजितः ।
जीवतं परिरक्षन्तम्
जीविताद् भ्रंशयिष्यति ॥
This arrow, powered by the Astra, shot by me shall take your life, however much you may try to save it!
6.59.89
एवमुक्त्वा महाबाहू
रावणो राक्षसेश्वरः ।
सन्धाय बाणमस्त्रेण
चमूपतिमताडयत् ॥
Saying this, Rāvaṇa, ruler of the Rākshasas, of huge arms, struck the Commander of the Vānaras with the arrow powered by the Astra.
6.59.90
सोऽस्त्रयुक्तेन बाणेन
नीलो वक्षसि ताडितः ।
निर्दह्यमानः सहसा
निपपात महीतले ॥
Struck on the chest by that arrow powered by the Astra, Neela caught fire and tumbled down to the ground instantly.
6.59.91
पितृमाहात्म्यसंयोगात्
आत्मनश्चापि तेजसा ।
जानुभ्यामपतद्भूमौ
न च प्राणैर्व्ययुज्यत ॥
Thanks to the miraculous powers of his father, and his own exceptional strength, he did not lose his life, but only fell on his knees.
The arrow was powered by Agni's Astra. However, Agni being the father of Neela, saw to it that its impact on him was minimal.
6.59.92
विसञ्ज्ञं वानरं दृष्ट्वा
दशग्रीवो रणोत्सुकः ।
रथेनाम्बुदनादेन
सौमित्रिमभिदुद्रुवे ॥
Seeing that the Vānara had swooned, the Ten-necked, ravening for a fight, bore down upon Sowmitri, seated in his chariot which rumbled like a cloud.
6.59.93
आसाद्य रणमध्ये तं
वारयित्वा स्थितो ज्वलन् ।
धनुर्विस्फारयामास
कम्पयन्निव मेदिनीम् ॥
Reaching him in the thick of the fray, and stopping him (from his onslaught), he stood blazing, twanging his bow, which seemed to shake the earth.
6.59.94
तमाह सौमित्रिरदीनसत्त्वो
विस्फारयन्तं धनुरप्रमेयम् ।
अभ्येहि मामेव निशाचरेन्द्र
न वानरांस्त्वं प्रतियोद्धुमर्हः ॥
As he twanged his peerless bow, the dauntless Sowmitri said to him, ‘Come along and engage me, O lord of Rākshasas, you should not be fighting with Vānaras!'
6.59.95
स तस्य वाक्यं प्रतिपूर्णघोषं
ज्याशब्दमुग्रं च निशम्य राजा ।
आसाद्य सौमित्रिमवस्थितं तं
कोपान्वितो वाक्यमुवाच रक्षः ॥
Hearing that full-throated challenge and the fierce twanging of his bow (that accompanied it), the Rākshasa King went near Sowmitri who stood (like a rock) and said these words angrily:
6.59.96
दिष्ट्याऽसि मे राघव दृष्टिमार्गं
प्राप्तोऽन्तगामी विपरीतबुद्धिः ।
अस्मिन् क्षणे यास्यसि मृत्युलोकम्
संसाद्यमानो मम बाणजालैः ॥
As Providence would have it, O scion of the Raghus, you, in your perverse eagerness to meet your end, have fallen in the line of my eyesight. You shall go to the World of the Dead this very minute, tormented by the volleys of arrows I shall fire at you.
6.59.97
तमाह सौमित्रिरविस्मयानो
गर्जन्तमुद्वृत्तसिताग्रदंष्ट्रम् ॥
Not amused in the least, Sowmitri said to him who was roaring and baring the white tips of his teeth:
6.59.97
राजन्न गर्जन्ति महाप्रभावा
विकत्थसे पापकृतां वरिष्ठ ।
जानामि वीर्यं तव राक्षसेन्द्र
बलं प्रतापं च पराक्रमं च ।
अवस्थितोऽहं शरचापपाणिः
आगच्छ किं मोघविकत्थनेन ॥
Men of real prowess do not vaunt themselves thus, O King! You bluster thus, being the foremost of the sinners. O king of Rākshasas, I know all about your valor, strength, mettle and prowess. Come along, here I stand with bow and arrows in my hand, why rant in vain!
6.59.99
स एवमुक्तः कुपितः ससर्ज
रक्षोधिपः सप्त शरान् सुपुङ्खान् ।
तान् लक्ष्मणः काञ्चनचित्रपुङ्खैः
चिच्छेद बाणैर्निशिताग्रधारैः ॥
Thus taunted, the Rākshasa King in his rage let off seven arrows with splendid rings on the shafts. Lakshmaṇa cut them down with sharp-edged arrows whose rings on the shafts were made of gold.
6.59.100
तान् प्रेक्षमाणः सहसा निकृत्तान्
निकृत्तभोगानिव पन्नगेन्द्रान् ।
लङ्केश्वरः क्रोधवशं जगाम
ससर्ज चान्यान्निशितान् पृषत्कान् ॥
Seeing them instantly cut down and falling like the severed hoods of the lords of serpents, the ruler of Laṅkā became furious and shot a few more sharp arrows.
6.59.101
स बाणवर्षं तु ववर्ष तीव्रं
रामानुजः कार्मुकसम्प्रयुक्तम् ।
क्षुरार्धचन्द्रोत्तमकर्णिभल्लैः
शरांश्च चिच्छेद न चुक्षुभे च ॥
The younger brother of Rāma, remaining wholly unperturbed, discharged a torrential shower of arrows from his bow.
And he cut down the arrows (of Rāvaṇa) with a variety of arrows, like the razor-sharp ones, the crescent-headed ones, the excellent Karṇis and the Bhallas.
6.59.102
स बाणजालान्यथ तानि तानि
मोघानि पश्यं स्त्रिदशारिराजः ।
विसिस्मये लक्ष्मणलाघवेन
पुनश्च बाणान्निशितान् मुमोच ॥
Astounded at the dexterity of Lakshmaṇa that rendered his torrent of arrows futile, the King of the foes of the Dēvas shot again more sharp arrows.
6.59.103
स लक्ष्मणश्चाशु शरान् शिताग्रान्
महेन्द्रवज्राशनितुल्यवेगान् ।
सन्धाय चापे ज्वलनप्रकाशान्
ससर्ज रक्षोधिपतेर्वधाय ॥
Lakshmaṇa immediately mounted sharp-edged arrows that flew as fast as Mahēndra’s Vajra and thunderbolt and that glowed like fire on his bow and released them to take the life of the King of Rākshasas.
6.59.104
स तान् प्रचिच्छेद हि राक्षसेन्द्रः
शितान् शरान् लक्ष्मणमाजघान ।
शरेण कालाग्निसमप्रभेण
स्वयम्भुदत्तेन ललाटदेशे ॥
The lord of the Rākshasas cut those sharp arrows to pieces, and then hit Lakshmaṇa on the forehead with an arrow given to him by the Self-born, which was as potent as the Fire of Dissolution that puts an end to the worlds.
6.59.105
स लक्ष्मणो रावणसायकार्तः
चचाल चापं शिथिलं प्रगृह्य ।
पुनश्च सञ्ज्ञां प्रतिलभ्य कृच्छ्रात्
चिच्छेद चापं त्रिदशेन्द्रशत्रोः ॥
Lakshmaṇa, oppressed by that arrow shot by Rāvaṇa, trembled and lost the grip of his bow. Then, regaining control with quite an effort, he broke the bow of the enemy of the king of the Dēvas.
6.59.106
निकृत्तचापं त्रिभिराजघान
बाणैस्तदा दाशरथिः शिताग्रः ।
स सायकार्तो विचचाल राजा
कृच्छ्राच्च सञ्ज्ञां पुनराससाद ॥
The son of Daṡaratha struck him, whose bow was severed, with three sharp edged arrows.
And the king (of Rākshasas), oppressed by those arrows, was shaken. With great difficulty, he then steadied himself.
6.59.107
स कृत्तचापः शरताडितश्च
मेदार्द्रगात्रो रुधिरावसिक्तः ।
जग्राह शक्तिं समुदग्रशक्तिः
स्वयम्भुदत्तां युधि देवशत्रुः ॥
Struck by the arrows, bow broken, body wet with his own fat and drenched in blood, but possessing great energy innately, he grabbed a Ṡakti given to him by the Self-born.
6.59.108
स तां विधूमानलसन्निकाशां
वित्रासिनीं वानरवाहिनीनाम् ।
चिक्षेप शक्तिं तरसाज्वलन्तीम्
सौमित्रये राक्षसराष्ट्रनाथः ॥
And the ruler of the Rākshasa kingdom hurled at Sowmitri, with all his force, that blazing Ṡakti which glowed like smokeless fire, striking terror in the hearts of the Vānara hosts.
6.59.109
तामापतन्तीं भरतानुजोग्रैः
जघान बाणैश्च हुताग्निकल्पैः ।
तथापि सा तस्य विवेश शक्तिः
भुजान्तरं दाशरथेर्विशालम् ॥
As it came along, the younger brother of Bharata assailed it with fierce arrows that blazed like the tongues of fire coming out of a Yajña pit. And yet it struck the son of Daṡaratha right in the center of his spacious chest.
6.59.110
स शक्तिमान् शक्तिसमाहतः सन्
जज्वाल भूमौ स रघुप्रवीरः ।
तं विह्वलन्तं सहसाऽभ्युपेत्य
जग्राह राजा तरसा भुजाभ्याम् ॥
Even after falling down on the ground having been struck by the Ṡakti, that great warrior of the Raghus continued to glow with splendor.
But as he reeled (under the impact), the King quickly caught him with his arms using all the force at his command.
6.59.111
हिमवान् मन्दरो मेरुः
त्रैलोक्यं वा सहामरैः ।
शक्यं भुजाभ्यामुद्धर्तुम्
न शक्यो भरतानुजः ॥
He could have, with his arms, lifted the Himālaya, the Mandara and the Mēru mountains and all the three worlds along with the Dēvas, but not the younger brother of Bharata.
6.59.112
शक्त्या ब्राह्म्याऽपि सौमित्रिः
ताडितस्तु स्तनान्तरे ।
विष्णोरचिन्त्यं स्वं भागम्
आत्मानं प्रत्यनुस्मरत् ॥
Though struck between the nipples by Ṡakti that was infused with the power of Brahma Himself, Sowmitri reminded himself that he was an inalienable part of Vishṇu.
This Ṡlōka, which refers to the divine nature of Lakshmaṇa is considered unauthentic.
6.59.113
ततो दानवदर्पघ्नम्
सौमित्रिं देवकण्टकः ।
तं पीडयित्वा बाहुभ्याम्
न प्रभुर्लङ्घनेऽभवत् ।
अथैवं वैष्णवं भागम्
मानुषं देहमास्थितम् ॥
He, who was a thorn in the side of the Dēvas, used all the power of his arms to crush, but could not even overpower Sowmitri, queller of the pride of Dānavas, in whom an Amṡa of Vishnu was housed.
6.59.114
ततः वायुसुतः क्रुद्धो
रावणं समभिद्रवत् ।
आजघानोरसि क्रुद्धो
वज्रकल्पेन मुष्टिना ॥
At that moment the enraged son of Vāyu attacked Rāvaṇa and gave him a blow on the chest with his fist, whose impact was like that of Vajra.
6.59.115
तेन मुष्टिप्रहारेण
रावणो राक्षसेश्वरः ।
जानुभ्यामपतद्भूमौ
चचाल च पपात च ॥
By that blow of the fist, Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, fell down on the ground on his knees. He tumbled down, shaking violently.
6.59.116
आस्यैश्च नेत्रैः श्रवणैः
पपात रुधिरं बहु ।
विघूर्णमानो निश्चेष्टो
रथोपस्थ उपाविशत् ।
विसञ्ज्ञो मूर्च्छितश्चासीत्
न च स्थानं समालभत् ॥
Streams of blood gushed out of his face, eyes and ears. His head in a whirl, he lay motionless, collapsed on the seat of the chariot. He became unconscious and fallen into a swoon, and could not stand firmly on his feet.
6.59.118
विसञ्ज्ञं रावणं दृष्ट्वा
समरे भीमविक्रमम् ।
ऋषयो वानराः सर्वे
नेदुर्देवाः सवासवाः ॥
Seeing that Rāvaṇa, of fierce prowess in fight, fallen unconscious, all the Ṛshis, the Vānaras and Dēvas including Indra, shouted in excitement.
6.59.119
हनुमानथ तेजस्वी
लक्ष्मणं रावणार्दितम् ।
अनयद्राघवाभ्याशम्
बाहुभ्यां परिगृह्य तम् ॥
Then, Hanumān of immense power lifted Lakshmaṇa, who was oppressed by Rāvaṇa, with his hands, and took him to Rāma.
6.59.120
वायुसूनोः सुहृत्त्वेन
भक्त्या परमया च सः ।
शत्रूणामप्रकम्प्योऽपि
लघुत्वमगमत् कपेः ॥
He, whom enemies found difficult to even move, became very light for the son of Vāyu, because of the affection and exceptional devotion that Vānara showed towards him.
6.59.121
तं समुत्सृज्य सा शक्तिः
सौमित्रिं युधि दुर्जयम् ।
रावणस्य रथे तस्मिन्
स्थानं पुनरुपागता ॥
After thus subduing Sowmitri in that battle, the Ṡakti left him and returned to its place in Rāvaṇa’s chariot.
6.59.122
आश्वस्तश्च विशल्यश्च
लक्ष्मणः शत्रुसूदनः ।
विष्णोर्भागममीमांस्यम्
आत्मानं प्रत्यनुस्मरन् ॥
As Lakshmaṇa, slayer of the foe, recalled that he was an inalienable part of Vishṇu, his wounds became healed and he became normal.
This Ṡlōka, which refers to the divine nature of Lakshmaṇa is considered unauthentic.
6.59.123
रावणोऽपि महातेजाः
प्राप्य सञ्ज्ञां महाहवे ।
आददे निशितान् बाणान्
जग्राह च महद्धनुः ॥
Rāvaṇa, of immense power, too, having regained consciousness in that great battle, took up a huge bow and sharp arrows.
6.59.124
निपातितमहावीराम्
वानराणां महाचमूम् ।
राघवस्तु रणे दृष्ट्वा
रावणं समभिद्रवत् ॥
Seeing that the great Veeras of the great Vānara army were struck down in that battle, Rāghava dashed toward Rāvaṇa.
6.59.125
अथैनमुपसङ्गम्य
हनुमान् वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Then Hanumān came up to him and said these words:
6.59.125
मम पृष्टं समारुह्य
राक्षसं शास्तुमर्हसि ।
विष्णुर्यथा गरुत्मन्तम्
आरुह्यामरवैरिणम् ॥
Get upon my back and strike the Rākshasa, as Vishṇu did the foe of the Dēvas riding on Garuḍa.
6.59.126
तच्छुत्वा राघवो वाक्यम्
वायुपुत्रेण भाषितम् ।
अथारुरोह सहसा
हनूमन्तं महाकपिम् ।
रथस्थं रावणं सङ्ख्ये
ददर्श मनुजाधिपः ॥
Hearing those words spoken by the son of Vāyu, Rāghava, the lord of people, got on the shoulders of Hanumān, the great Vānara, and beheld Rāvaṇa who was seated in his chariot in that battlefield.
6.59.128
तमालोक्य माहातेजाः
प्रदुद्राव स रावणम् ।
वैरोचनमिव क्रुद्धो
विष्णुरभ्युद्यतायुधः ॥
He of immense power saw Rāvaṇa and rushed upon him in rage holding up his weapons, like Vishṇu did upon Virōcana.
6.59.129
ज्याशब्दमकरोत्तीवम्
वज्रनिष्पेषनिस्वनम् ।
गिरा गम्भीरया रामो
राक्षसेन्द्रमुवाच ह ॥
Twanging his bowstring sharply, whose noise fell like thunder on the ear, Rāma addressed the King of Rākshasas in a deep booming voice, telling him:
6.59.130
तिष्ठ तिष्ठ मम त्वं हि
कृत्वा विप्रियमीद्दशम् ।
क्व नु राक्षसशार्दूल
गतो मोक्षमवाप्स्यसि ॥
Stop right there! Having done such harm to me, where can you go to find relief, O tiger among Rākshasas?
6.59.131
यदीन्द्रवैवस्वतभास्करान् वा
स्वयम्भुवैश्वानरशङ्करान् वा ।
गमिष्यसि त्वं दश वा दिशोऽथवा
तथापि मे नाद्य गतो विमोक्ष्यसे ॥
Now that you have come within my hold, you cannot find relief regardless of where you go - to Indra, Vaivaswata or Bhāskara, or to the Self-born, Vaiṡwānara or Ṡaṅkara, or flee in any of the ten directions.
6.59.132
यश्चैष शक्त्या निहतस्त्वयाऽद्य
गच्छन्विषादं सहसाऽभ्युपेत्य ।
स एष रक्षोगणराज मृत्युः
सपुत्रपौत्रस्य तवाद्ययुद्धे ॥
He who is suffering struck by your Ṡakti, will rise in no time and would be the death for you, your children and grandchildren on the battlefield this very day, O Ruler of the Rākshasa race!
6.59.133
एतेन चात्यद्भुतदर्शनानि
शरैर्जनस्थानकृतालयानि ।
चतुर्दशान्यात्तवरायुधानि
रक्षस्सहस्राणि निषूदितानि ॥
It is by these very arrows that the fourteen thousand Rākshasas who had Janasthāna for their abode, who presented a marvelous appearance and who were armed with superb weapons, were killed.
6.59.134
राघवस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
राक्षसेन्द्रो महाबलः ।
वायुपुत्रं महावेगम्
वहन्तं राघवं रणे ।
रोषेण महराविष्टः
पूर्ववौरमनुस्मरन् ।
आजघान शरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
कालानलशिखोपमैः ॥
Hearing what Rāghava said, the ruler of the Rākshasas of immense strength became extremely furious and struck the son of Vāyu, who served as his mount in that battle, remembering the past brush with him, with arrows sharp as the tongues of the flames of the fire of the Dissolution.
6.59.136
राक्षसेनाहवे तस्य
ताडितस्यापि सायकैः ।
स्वभावतेजोयुक्तस्य
भूयस्तेजोऽभ्यवर्धत ॥
The arrows of the Rākshasa that struck him in the battle, helped only enhance the natural power he was endowed with.
6.59.137
ततो रामो महातेजा
रावणेन कृतव्रणम् ।
दृष्ट्वा प्लवगशार्दूलम्
कोपस्य वशमेयिवान् ॥
Seeing that a wound was inflicted on the tiger among Vānaras by Rāvaṇa, Rāma of immense power was infuriated.
6.59.138
तस्याभिचङ्क्रम्य रथं सचक्रं
साश्वध्वजच्छत्रमहापताकम् ।
ससारथिं साशनिशूलखड्गं
रामः प्रचिच्छेद शरैः सुपुङ्खैः ॥
Rāma then rushed upon him and, with his arrows bearing splendid rings on the shafts, tore apart his chariot along with its wheels, horses, flagstaff, umbrella, great flag, charioteer, Vajras, spears and swords.
6.59.139
अथेन्द्रशत्रुं तरसा जघान
बाणेन वज्राशनिसन्निभेन ।
भुजान्तरे व्यूढसुजातरूपे
वज्रेण मेरुं भगवानिवेन्द्रः ॥
Then, with an arrow that fell like Indra’s Vajra and thunderbolt, he struck Indra’s foe on his broad and imposing breast, as Indra did the Mēru mountain with his Vajra.
6.59.140
यो वज्रपाताशनिसन्निपातात्
न चुक्षुभे नापि चचाल राजा ।
स रामबाणाभिहतो भृशार्तः
चचाल चापं च मुमोच वीरः ॥
The valiant King, who had never been shaken or perturbed by the strike of even Vajra and thunderbolt, felt quite distressed, struck by the arrow of Rāma; and the bow fell from his hand as he tottered.
6.59.141
तं विह्वलन्तं प्रसमीक्ष्य रामः
समाददे दीप्तमथार्धचन्द्रम् ।
तेनार्कवर्णं सहसा किरीटं
चिच्छेद रक्षोधिपतेर्महात्मा ॥
Seeing him quite shaken, Rāma, a Mahātma, took out a blazing arrow with a crescent head, and, with it, shivered the crown of the king of Rākshasas that was brilliant like the sun.
6.59.142
तं निर्विषाशीविषसन्निकाशं
शान्तार्चिषं सूर्यमिवाप्रकाशम् ।
गतश्रियं कृत्तकिरीटकूटम्
उवाच रामो युधि राक्षसेन्द्रम् ॥
Then Rāma, in that battle, said to the Ruler of the Rākshasas who lost his glow with the crown being smashed, rendered powerless like a serpent that had lost its venom, and dull like the sun who had lost his brilliance, with his rays dimmed:
6.59.143
कृतं त्वया कर्म महत् सुभीमं
हतप्रवीरश्च कृतस्त्वयाऽहम् ।
तस्मात्परिश्रान्त इव व्यवस्य
न त्वां शरैर्मृत्युवशं नयामि ॥
You have indeed accomplished quite a terrific feat in having many of my warriors killed; so, I take it that you are quite exhausted, and I spare you from being sent to the throws of death.
6.59.144
प्रयाहि जानामि रणार्दितस्त्वं
प्रविश्य रात्रिंचरराज लङ्काम् ।
आश्वास्य निर्याहि रथी च धन्वी
तदा बलं द्रक्ष्यसि मे रथस्थः ॥
Go, O king of Night-rovers, I know you are exhausted by the fight! Go to Laṅkā, take rest, recover, and then come back with chariot and bow; then, from your chariot, you can witness my strength!
6.59.145
स एवमुक्तो हतदर्पहर्षो
निकृत्तचापः स हताश्वसूतः ।
शरार्दितः कृत्तमहाकिरीटो
विवेश लङ्कां सहसा स राजा ॥
Thus told, the King hurried back to Laṅkā, his pride and high spirits flogged out of him, his bow broken, his horses and charioteer slain, himself sore and oppressed by the battering arrows, and his great crown smashed.
6.59.146
तस्मिन् प्रविष्टे रजनीचरेन्द्रे
महाबले दानवदेवशत्रौ ।
हरीन् विशल्यान् सह लक्ष्मणेन
चकार रामः परमाहवाग्रे ॥
At the height of that fierce battle, when the King of the Rākshasas of immense strength, the foe of the Dēvas and the Dānavas, had gone back, Rāma saw to it that Lakshmaṇa and the Vānaras were healed of their wounds.
6.59.147
तस्मिन् प्रभिन्ने त्रिदशेन्द्रशत्रौ
सुरासुरा भूतगणा दिशश्च ।
ससागराः सर्षिमहोरागाश्च
तथैव भूम्यम्बुचराश्च हृष्टाः ॥
With the foe of the Lord of the Dēvas thus vanquished, the Suras, the Asuras, the hosts of elementals, the cardinals, the seas, the Ṛshis, the great Uragas and the denizens of earth and water rejoiced.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे एकोनषष्टितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty ninth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
