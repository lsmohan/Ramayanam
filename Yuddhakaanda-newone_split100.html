Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 100
In this Sarga, the battle between Rāma and Rāvaṇa continues, with Vibheeshaṇa and Lakshmaṇa also joining the fray.
Rāvaṇa deploys an Astra built by Maya. Rāma strikes it with the Astra of Gandharvas. Rāvaṇa then dispatches the Astra of the sun, which showers down blazing discs. Rāma strikes them down with sharp arrows.
Meanwhile Lakshmaṇa smashes the Rāvaṇa’s flagstaff, cuts the head of his charioteer and breaks his bow, and Vibheeshaṇa springs up and kills his horses. Rāvaṇa, jumping off the chariot, hurls a Ṡakti at Vibheeshaṇa. Lakshmaṇa strikes it down while it is still in flight and inundates Rāvaṇa with arrows, while he is getting ready to hurl another powerful Ṡakti at Vibheeshaṇa. Rāvaṇa becomes angry with Lakshmaṇa and hurls the Ṡakti at him instead. Lakshmaṇa falls on the ground, with that Ṡakti plunging deep and cleaving his heart.
Rāma's heart sinks, seeing Lakshmaṇa in that condition. But it is no time for grief. He pulls out the Ṡakti from Lakshmaṇa's chest and continues the fight with Rāvaṇa. Unable to bear the torrents of arrows showered by Rāma, Rāvaṇa flees the battlefield.
6.100.1
तस्मिन् प्रतिहतेऽस्त्रे तु
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ।
क्रोधं च द्विगुणं चक्रे
क्रोधाच्चास्त्रमनन्तरम् ॥
With that Astra rendered futile, Rāvaṇa, the ruler of the Rākshasas, his rage doubled, dispatched another Astra.
6.100.2
मयेन विहितं रौद्रम्
अन्यदस्त्रं महाद्युतिः ।
उत्स्रष्टुं रावणो भीमम्
राघवाय प्रचक्रमे ॥
Rāvaṇa of extraordinary splendor discharged that fierce and formidable Astra, made by Maya, at Rāghava.
6.100.3
ततः शूलानि निश्चेरुः
गदाश्च मुसलानि च ।
कार्मुकाद्दीप्यमानानि
वज्रसाराणि सर्वशः ॥
Then, there emerged dazzling spears, maces, pestles that were as strong as diamonds from every side of the bow.
6.100.4
मुद्गराः कूटपाशाश्च
दीप्ताश्चाशनयस्तथा ।
निष्पेतुर्विविधास्तीक्ष्णा
वाता इव युगक्षये ॥
Many sharp weapons like Mudgaras, Kūṭas, Pāṡas, and also blazing Vajra-like weapons emerged, like gales at the end of Yuga.
6.100.5
तदस्त्रं राघवः श्रीमान्
उत्तमास्त्रविदां वरः ।
जघान परमास्त्रेण
गान्धर्वेण महाद्युतिः ॥
The blessed Rāghava of extraordinary splendor, the foremost of the masters of the best Astras, struck it down with the supreme Astra of the Gandharvas.
6.100.6
तस्मिन् प्रतिहतेऽस्त्रे तु
राघवेण महात्मना ।
रावणः क्रोधताम्राक्षः
सौरमस्त्रमुदैरयत् ॥
With his Astra thus struck down by Mahātma Rāghava, Rāvaṇa, his eyes red with anger, dispatched the Astra of the sun.
6.100.7
ततश्चक्राणि निष्पेतुः
भास्वराणि महान्ति च ।
कार्मुकाद्भीमवेगस्य
दशग्रीवस्य धीमतः ॥
Then, huge dazzling discs emerged from the bow of that sagacious Ten-necked, of terrific speed.
6.100.8
तैरासीद्गगनं दीप्तम्
सम्पतद्भिः समन्ततः ।
पतद्भिश्च दिशो दीप्ताः
चन्द्रसूर्यग्रहैरिव ॥
As they flew and fell everywhere, the sky and the cardinals lit up, as if with the moon, the sun and the planets.
6.100.9
तानि चिच्छेद बाणौघैः
चक्राणि स तु राघवः ।
आयुधानि च चित्राणि
रावणस्य चमूमुखे ॥
Rāghava smashed those discs and the weapons of strange shapes, with his volleys of arrows, on that battlefront.
6.100.10
तदस्त्रं तु हतं दृष्ट्वा
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ।
विव्याध दशभिर्बाणै
रामं सर्वेषु मर्मसु ॥
Seeing that Astra thus struck down, Rāvaṇa, the king of Rākshasas, struck at every vital part of Rāma's body, with ten arrows.
6.100.11
स विद्धो दशभिर्बाणैः
महाकार्मुकनिस्सृतैः ।
रावणेन महातेजा
न प्राकम्पत राघवः ॥
Rāghava was perturbed in the least, hit by those ten arrows that emerged from the great bow of Rāvaṇa of extraordinary power.
6.100.12
ततो विव्याध गात्रेषु
सर्वेषु समितिञ्जयः ।
राघवस्तु सुसङ्क्रुद्धो
रावणं बहुभिः शरैः ॥
Rāghava, who was ever victorious in battle, struck Rāvaṇa with a multitude of arrows on every part of his body, in extreme rage.
6.100.13
एतस्मिन्नन्तरे क्रुद्धो
राघवस्यानुजो बली ।
लक्ष्मणः सायकान् सप्त
जग्राह परवीरहा ॥
Meanwhile, the strong Lakshmaṇa, younger brother of Rāghava, and destroyer of enemy warriors, enraged, grabbed seven arrows.
6.100.14
तैः सायकैर्महावेगै
रावणस्य महाद्युतिः ।
ध्वजं मनुष्यशीर्षं तु
तस्य चिच्छेद नैकधा ॥
With those arrows of immense force, he, of extraordinary splendor cut to pieces Rāvaṇa’s flagstaff that had a man’s head as the emblem.
6.100.15
सारथेश्चापि बाणेन
शिरो ज्वलितकुण्डलम् ।
जहार लक्ष्मणः श्रीमान्
नैर्ऋतस्य महाबलः ॥
The blessed Lakshmaṇa also severed, with his arrows, the head, along with its dazzling ear-pendants, of the charioteer of that Rākshasa of immense strength.
6.100.16
तस्य बाणैश्च चिच्छेद
धनुर्गजकरोपमम् ।
लक्ष्मणो राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
पञ्चभिर्निशितैस्तदा ॥
And then, with five sharp arrows, Lakshmaṇa broke the Rākshasa king’s bow that was like the trunk of an elephant.
6.100.17
नीलमेघनिभांश्चास्य
सदश्वान् पर्वतोपमान् ।
जघानाप्लुत्य गदया
रावणस्य विभीषणः ॥
Vibheeshaṇa sprang up and killed, with his mace, the splendid horses of Rāvaṇa, that were huge like mountains and shone like black clouds.
6.100.18
हताश्वात्तु तदा वेगात्
अवप्लुत्य महारथात् ।
क्रोधमाहारयत्तीव्रम्
भ्रातरं प्रति रावणः ॥
With the horses slain, Rāvaṇa quickly jumped off the great chariot, and frowned with extreme wrath at his brother.
6.100.19
ततः शक्तिं महाशक्तिः
प्रदीप्तामशनीमिव ।
विभीषणाय चिक्षेप
राक्षसेन्द्रः प्रतापवान् ॥
Then the lord of Rākshasas of scorching valor and extraordinary power, hurled at Vibheeshaṇa, a Ṡakti that blazed like thunderbolt.
6.100.20
अप्राप्तामेव तां बाणैः
त्रिभिश्चिच्छेद लक्ष्मणः ।
अथोदतिष्ठत् सन्नादो
वानराणां तदा रणे ॥
But even before it made its way (to Vibheeshaṇa), Lakshmaṇa cut it off with three arrows in that battle; and then there were heard loud shouts (of joy) from the Vānaras.
6.100.21
संपपात त्रिधा छिन्ना
शक्तिः काञ्चनमालिनी ।
सविस्फुलिङ्गा ज्वलिता
महोल्केव दिवश्च्युता ॥
That Ṡakti, decked with golden chains, cut into three pieces, fell blazing and emitting sparks, like a huge meteor from the sky.
6.100.22
ततः सम्भाविततराम्
कालेनापि दुरासदाम् ।
जग्राह विपुलां शक्तिम्
दीप्यमानां स्वतेजसा ॥
Then he took another huge Ṡakti that dazzled with its own splendor, and was celebrated for its invincibility even by Yama.
6.100.23
सा वेगिता बलवता
रावणेन दुरात्मना ।
जज्वाल सुमहातेजा
शक्राशनिसमप्रभा ॥
That extraordinarily powerful one (Ṡakti), sped by the strong evil-minded Rāvaṇa, dazzled with the glow of Ṡakra's thunderbolt.
6.100.24
एतस्मिन्नन्तरे वीरो
लक्ष्मणस्तं विभीषणम् ।
प्राणसंशयमापन्नम्
तूर्णमभ्यवपद्यत ॥
Meanwhile the valiant Lakshmaṇa rushed with all speed to the rescue of Vibheeshaṇa, whose life was in danger.
6.100.25
तं विमोक्षयितुं वीरः
चापमायम्य लक्ष्मणः ।
रावणं शक्तिहस्तं वै
शरवर्षैरवाकिरत् ॥
To save him (from the danger), the valiant Lakshmaṇa bent his bow to the full and sprayed a shower of arrows on Rāvaṇa who was holding that Ṡakti in his hand.
6.100.26
कीर्यमाणः शरौघेण
विसृष्टेन महात्मना ।
न प्रहर्तुं मनश्चक्रे
विमुखीकृतविक्रमः ॥
As he was thus sprayed with volleys of arrows by that Mahātma, he decided not to strike, his mind turning away from the urge to assault.
6.100.27
मोक्षितं भ्रातरं दृष्ट्वा
लक्ष्मणेन स रावणः ।
लक्ष्मणाभिमुखस्तिष्ठन्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Seeing his brother rescued by Lakshmaṇa, Rāvaṇa turned to Lakshmaṇa and said these words:
6.100.28
मोक्षितस्ते बलश्लाघिन्
यस्मादेवं विभीषणः ।
विमुच्य राक्षसं शक्तिः
त्वयीयं विनिपात्यते ॥
O you, of empty boasts of strength! It is good that you have rescued Vibheeshaṇa; leaving that Rākshasa alone, this Ṡakti will be lodged on you instead.
6.100.29
एषा ते हृदयं भित्त्वा
शक्तिर्लोहितलक्षणा ।
मद्बाहुपरिघोत्सृष्टा
प्राणानादाय यास्यति ॥
This Ṡakti, marked with the blood (of enemies), hurled by my Parigha-like arm, will cleave your heart and walk away with your life.
6.100.30
इत्येवमुक्त्वा तां शक्तिम्
अष्टघण्टां महास्वनाम् ।
मयेन मायाविहिताम्
अमोघां शत्रुघातिनीम् ।
लक्ष्मणाय समुद्दिश्य
ज्वलन्तीमिव तेजसा ।
रावणः परमक्रुद्धः
चिक्षेप च ननाद च ॥
Saying this in extreme wrath, Rāvaṇa hurled, aimed at Lakshmaṇa, the Ṡakti that dazzled with its own splendor, struck down enemies without ever failing, had eight bells that made piercing sound adorning it, built by Maya with his skills of magic, and let off a mighty roar.
6.100.32
सा क्षिप्ता भीमवेगेन
शक्राशनिसमस्वना ।
शक्तिरभ्यपतद्वेगात्
लक्ष्मणं रणमूर्धनि ॥
Hurled with tremendous force, the Ṡakti, with the sound of Ṡakra's thunderbolt, swiftly reached Lakshmaṇa on that battlefield.
6.100.33
तामनुव्याहरच्छक्तिम्
आपतन्तीं स राघवः ।
स्वस्त्यस्तु लक्ष्मणायेति
मोघा भव हतोद्यमा ॥
As it was about to fall on him, Rāghava prayed to the Ṡakti, 'May all be well with Lakshmaṇa! And may you go in vain in achieving your purpose!
6.100.34
रावणेन रणे शक्तिः
क्रुद्धेनाशीविषोपमा ।
मुक्ताऽऽशूरस्यभीतस्य
लक्ष्मणस्य ममज्ज सा ॥
Hurled by Rāvaṇa in wrath in that battle, the Ṡakti, which was like a venomous serpent, plunged in no time into the chest of Lakshmaṇa who knew no fear.
6.100.35
न्यपतत् सा महावेगा
लक्ष्मणस्य महोरसि ।
जिह्वेवोरगराजस्य
दीप्यमाना महाद्युतिः ॥
Dazzling like the tongue of the king of serpents with its extraordinary splendor, it plunged into the mighty chest of Lakshmaṇa with its extraordinary force.
6.100.36
ततो रावणवेगेन
सुदूरमवगाढया ।
शक्त्या निर्भिन्नहृदयः
पपात भुवि लक्ष्मणः ॥
Lakshmaṇa fell on the ground, with that Ṡakti plunging deep and cleaving his heart, thrown with such force by Rāvaṇa.
6.100.37
तदवस्थं समीपस्थो
लक्ष्मणं प्रेक्ष्य राघवः ।
भ्रातृस्नेहान्महातेजा
विषण्णहृदयोऽभवत् ॥
Rāghava of extraordinary power, who was by his side, saw the plight of Lakshmaṇa; and his heart, full of love for his brother, sank.
6.100.38
स मुहूर्तमिव ध्यात्वा
बाष्पव्याकुललोचनः ।
बभूव संरब्धतरो
युगान्त इव पावकः ॥
Eyes turbid with tears, he thought for a while, and went into an extreme rage, like fire at the end of the Yuga.
6.100.39
न विषादस्य कालोऽयम्
इति सञ्चिन्त्य राघवः ।
चक्रे सुतुमुलं युद्धम्
रावणस्य वधे धृतः ।
सर्वयत्नेन महता
लक्ष्मणं परिवीक्ष्य च ॥
Looking at Lakshmaṇa and realizing that it was not the time to grieve, he put up a tumultuous fight, putting forth all his best efforts, determined to slay Rāvaṇa.
6.100.40
स ददर्श ततो रामः
शक्त्या भिन्नं महाहवे ।
लक्ष्मणं रुधिरादिग्धम्
सपन्नगमिवाचलम् ॥
Then, Rāma beheld Lakshmaṇa smeared with blood struck by Ṡakti in that great battle, looking like a mountain with a serpent on its flanks.
6.100.41
तामपि प्रहितां शक्तिम्
रावणेन बलीयसा ।
यत्नतस्ते हरिश्रेष्ठा
न शेकुरवमर्दितुम् ।
अर्दिताश्चैव बाणौघैः
ते प्रवेकेण रक्षसाम् ॥
The great Vānaras tried hard, but could not pull out that Ṡakti, which Rāvaṇa had thrown with all his force, with themselves being assailed with volleys of arrows by him, who stood tall among Rākshasas.
Conflicting palm leaf versions must have caused the discrepancy here; Rāvaṇa's bow is broken (see Ṡlōka 6.100.16 above), which is why he resorts to the use of Ṡakti, but again, starting from this Ṡlōka, you see him shooting arrows.
6.100.43
सौमित्रिं सा विनिर्भिद्य
प्रविष्टा धरणीतलम् ।
तां कराभ्यां परामृश्य
रामः शक्तिं भयावहाम् ।
बभञ्ज समरे क्रुद्धो
बलवान् विचकर्ष च ॥
Piercing through Sowmitri’s body, it pinned him to the ground. Seizing that frightening Ṡakti with both hands, Rāma, of immense strength, pulled it out and broke it into two.
6.100.44
तस्य निष्कर्षतः शक्तिम्
रावणेन बलीयसा ।
शराः सर्वेषु गात्रेषु
पातिता मर्मभेदिनः ॥
While he was thus engaged in pulling the Ṡakti, the strong Rāvaṇa struck every limb of his with arrows that tore his vitals.
6.100.45
अचिन्तयित्वा तान् बाणान्
समाश्लिष्य च लक्ष्मणम् ।
अब्रवीच्छ हनूमन्तम्
सुग्रीवं चैव राघवः ॥
Unmindful of those arrows, Rāghava embraced Lakshmaṇa and said to Hanumān and Sugreeva:
6.100.46
लक्ष्मणं परिवार्येह
तिष्ठध्वं वानरोत्तमाः ।
पराक्रमस्य कालोऽयम्
सम्प्राप्तो मे चिरेप्सितः ।
पापात्माऽयं दशग्रीवो
वध्यतां पापनिश्चयः ।
काङ्क्षितं चातकस्येव
घर्मान्ते मेघदर्शनम् ॥
O eminent Vānaras! Stay guard around Lakshmaṇa! The time for which I have been waiting for, like the Cātaka bird till the end of summer for a glimpse of the raincloud, has come, to show my prowess and slay this sinful Ten-necked who is bent on sinful deeds.
6.100.48
अस्मिन् मुहूर्ते न चिरात्
सत्यं प्रतिशृणोमि वः ।
अरावणमरामं वा
जगद्द्रक्ष्यथ वानराः ॥
I can vouch to you, O Vānaras, that you will see the world, in a matter of an hour, become devoid either of Rāvaṇa or of Rāma.
6.100.49
राज्यनाशं वने वासम्
दण्डके परिधावनम् ।
वैदेह्याश्च परामर्शम्
रक्षोभिश्च समागमम् ।
प्राप्तं दुःखं महद्घोरम्
क्लेशं च निरयोपमम् ।
अद्य सर्वमहं त्यक्ष्ये
निहत्वा रावणं रणे ॥
I have gone through many an extremely terrible tribulation that plunged me into a hellish grief, from losing the kingdom to being exiled to the Vana to running here and there in Daṇḍaka, to seeing Vaidēhee touched by Rākshasas. I shall be relieved of all of it, striking down Rāvaṇa in the battle today.
6.100.51
यदर्थं वानरं सैन्यम्
समानीतमिदं मया ।
सुग्रीवश्च कृतो राज्ये
निहत्वा वालिनं रणे ।
यदर्थं सागरः क्रान्तः
सेतुर्बद्धश्च सागरे ।
सोऽयमद्य रणे पापः
चक्षुर्विषयमागतः ॥
This sinful one, who made me bring the Vānara forces here, after striking down Vāli and establishing Sugreeva in the kingdom, and by building a bridge over the ocean and crossing it, has fallen, in this battle, in the line of my sight.
6.100.53
चक्षुर्विषयमागम्य
नायं जीवितुमर्हति ।
दृष्टिं दृष्टिविषस्येव
सर्पस्य मम रावणः ।
यथा वा वैनतेयस्य
दृष्टिं प्राप्तो भुजंगमः ॥
Having fallen in the line of my sight, Rāvaṇa cannot afford to live any longer, like one fallen in the line of sight of a snake whose very sight is venomous, and like a snake on whom Vainatēya has set his eyes.
6.100.55
स्वस्थाः पश्यत दुर्धर्षा
युद्धं वानरपुङ्गवाः ।
आसीनाः पर्वताग्रेषु
ममेदं रावणस्य च ॥
O bulls among Vānaras whom none dare attack! Sitting comfortably on the mountain peaks you shall watch this fight between me and Rāvaṇa!
6.100.56
अद्य रामस्य रामत्वम्
पश्यन्तु मम संयुगे ।
त्रयो लोकाः सगन्धर्वाः
सदेवाः सर्षिचारणाः ॥
Today, the people of all the three worlds, including the Gandharvas, the Dēvas, the Ṛshis and the Cāraṇas shall see, in my fight, what makes Rāma, Rāma!
6.100.57
अद्य कर्म करिष्यामि
यल्लोकाः सचराचराः ।
सदेवाः कथयिष्यन्ति
यावद्भूमिर्धरिष्यति ।
समागम्य सदा लोके
यथा युद्धं प्रवर्तितम् ॥
Today I shall do what all the worlds, with their mobile and immobile beings and the Dēvas, will talk about till this earth lasts, whenever they meet.
6.100.58
एवमुक्त्वा शितैर्बाणैः
तप्तकाञ्चनभूषणैः ।
आजघान दशग्रीवम्
रणे रामः समाहितः ॥
Saying this, Rāma, with full focus, struck the Ten-necked, in that battle, with sharp arrows decked with gold purified in fire.
6.100.59
अथ प्रदीप्तैर्नाराचैः
मुसलैश्चापि रावणः ।
अभ्यवर्षत्तदा रामम्
धाराभिरिव तोयदः ॥
Then, Rāvaṇa sent a shower of blazing Nārācās and pestles on Rāma, like a cloud pouring down torrents of rain.
6.100.60
रामरावणमुक्तानाम्
अन्योन्यमभिनिघ्नताम् ।
शराणां च शराणां च
बभूव तुमुलः स्वनः ॥
A tumultuous din arose, as the arrows shot by Rāma and Rāvaṇa hit each other.
6.100.61
ते भिन्नाश्च विकीर्णाश्च
रामरावणयोः शराः ।
अन्तरिक्षात् प्रदीप्ताग्रा
निपेतुर्धरणीतले ॥
The arrows of Rāma and Rāvaṇa, broken and scattered, fell down on the ground from the sky, their tips burning.
6.100.62
तयोर्ज्यातलनिर्घोषो
रामरावणयोर्महान् ।
त्रासनः सर्वभूतानाम्
बभूवाद्भुतदर्शनः ॥
The great din from the thud of the bowstring striking the palms of Rāma and Rāvaṇa created an amazing spectacle, frightening all the creatures.
6.100.63
विकीर्यमाणः शरजालवृष्टिभिः
महात्मना दीप्तधनुष्मतार्दितः ।
भयात्प्रदुद्राव समेत्य रावणो
यथाऽनिलेनाभिहतो बलाहकः ॥
Inundated and oppressed by the torrents of the arrows shot by the Mahātma (Rāma) with a blazing bow, Rāvaṇa, terrified, fled along with his army, like a cloud driven by the wind.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे शततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundredth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
