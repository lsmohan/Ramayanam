Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 20
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa pleads with Seetā to love him. He says that he cannot take his eyes off from whichever of her limbs it falls upon, even though she is emaciated and her body lacks toilet.
He implores her to dress well and wear every ornament and enjoy all the luxuries of the kingdom along with him. He asks her to think beyond the current predicament. He says that Rāma is no equal to him in any manner, has no chance of getting a glimpse of her again, and might even be dead by now.
He asks her to become the queen and lord over all the women of his inner apartments, who he says are the best of the best in all the three worlds, endowed with every excellence. He promises that all of them will be at her service.
5.20.1
स तां परिवृतां दीनाम्
निरानन्दां तपस्विनीम् ।
साकारैर्मधुरैर्वाक्यैः
न्यदर्शयत रावणः ॥
Rāvaṇa talked, in sweet words and with pleasing gestures, to Seetā who was in a pitiful state, suffering and knowing no joy, surrounded (by the Rākshasis):
5.20.2
मां दृष्ट्वा नागनासोरु
गूहमाना स्तनोदरम् ।
अदर्शनमिवात्मानम्
भयान्नेतुं त्वमिच्छसि ॥
Oh you of elephant-trunk-like thighs! You hide your breasts and belly on seeing me, as if you, in your dread of me, could make yourself invisible!
5.20.3
कामये त्वां विशालाक्षि
बहुमन्यस्व मां प्रिये ।
सर्वाङ्गगुणसम्पन्ने
सर्वलोकमनोहरे ॥
I want you, O wide-eyed one! Please consider me, my dear! You ravish the hearts of all people with your every limb exquisitely chiseled!
5.20.4
नेह केचिन्मनुष्या वा
राक्षसाः कामरूपिणः ।
व्यपसर्पतु ते सीते
भयं मत्तः समुत्थितम् ॥
There are no others here, neither humans nor Rākshasas who can take any form at will. And may you overcome the fear, O Seetā, that could have engendered from my presence!
5.20.5
स्वधर्मो रक्षसां भीरु
सर्वथैव न संशयः ।
गमनं वा परस्त्रीणाम्
हरणं सम्प्रमथ्य वा ॥
It is in the very nature of Rākshasas, O timid one, by all means and without a doubt, to seek other’s women and carry them off forcibly.
5.20.6
एवं चैतदकामां तु
न त्वां स्प्रक्ष्यामि मैथिलि ।
कामं कामः शरीरे मे
यथाकामं प्रवर्तताम् ॥
Even that being so, I will not touch you, O Maithili, until you develop love for me, even if it means putting up with this lust in my body that rages uncontrollably.
5.20.7
देवि नेह भयं कार्यम्
मयि विश्वसि हि प्रिये ।
प्रणयस्व च तत्त्वेन
मैवं भूः शोकलालसा ॥
O Dēvi! Harbor no fear from me on this count. You can trust me, my dear! I want you to love me genuinely. Do not constantly wallow in sadness.
5.20.8
एकवेणी धराशय्या
ध्यानं मलिनमम्बरम् ।
अस्थानेऽप्युपवासश्च
नैतान्यौपयिकानि ते ॥
Keeping hair in a single plait, sleeping on bare grounds, brooding over things, wearing dirty garment, going on fast without a reason; all these are not right for you.
5.20.9
विचित्राणि च माल्यानि
चन्दनान्यगरूणि च ।
विविधानि च वासांसि
दिव्यान्याभरणानि च ।
महार्हाणि च पानानि
शयनान्यासनानि च ।
गीतं नृत्तं च वाद्यं च
लभ मां प्राप्य मैथिलि ॥
Come to me Maithili and enjoy everything – beautiful flower garlands, perfume of sandal and Agaru, every kind of garment, splendid jewelry, drinks, beds and couches that only the most opulent deserve, and song and dance and music!
5.20.11
स्त्रीरत्नमसि मैवं भूः
कुरु गात्रेषु भूषणम् ।
मां प्राप्य हि कथं नु स्यात्
त्वमनर्हा सुविग्रहे ॥
You are a jewel among women; do not be like this; What could possibly prevent you from decorating your body with ornaments, after you have me (interested in you)!
5.20.12
इदं ते चारु सञ्जातम्
यौवनं व्यतिवर्तते ।
यदतीतं पुनर्नैति
स्रोतः शीघ्रमपामिव ॥
This captivatingly evolved youth of yours is slipping away with time. And it will not come back, like water rushing in the rapids.
5.20.13
त्वां कृत्वोपरतो मन्ये
रूपकर्ता स विश्वसृक् ।
न हि रूपोपमा त्वन्या
तवास्ति शुभदर्शने ॥
I guess the Creator of the World who gives a shape to everything, called it a day after creating you, (or else, how is it possible that) there is no one else comparable to you in beauty, O you of pleasing looks!
5.20.14
त्वां समासाद्य वैदेहि
रूपयौवनशालिनीम् ।
कः पुमानतिवर्तेत
साक्षादपि पितामहः ॥
Having got you, full of beauty and youth, O Vaidēhee, which man can go past, ignoring you, even if it were the Grandsire!
5.20.15
यद्यत्पश्यामि ते गात्रम्
शीतांशुसदृशानने ।
तस्मिंस्तस्मिन् पृथुश्रोणि
चक्षुर्मम निबध्यते ॥
O you of moon-bright face and wide Kaṭee! No matter which limb of yours I see, it arrests my eyes!
5.20.16
भव मैथिलि भार्या मे
मोहमेनं विसर्जय ।
बह्वीनामुत्तमस्त्रीणाम्
आहृतानामितस्ततः ।
सर्वासामेव भद्रं ते
ममाग्रमहिषी भव ॥
O Maithili! Give up your foolishness and be my wife! I have brought many excellent women from every corner (of the world)! Be the chief among all my queens! May all bode well for you!
5.20.17
लोकेभ्यो यानि रत्नानि
सम्प्रमथ्याहृतानि वै ।
तानि मे भीरु सर्वाणि
राज्यं चैतदहं च ते ॥
O timid one! All the precious stones that I have secured forcibly from all over the world shall be yours, and this kingdom and myself as well!
5.20.18
विजित्य पृथिवीं सर्वाम्
नानानगरमालिनीम् ।
जनकाय प्रदास्यामि
तव हेतोर्विलासिनि ॥
O lovely dame! I can conquer the entire earth along with the many cities that dot it, and bestow it to Janaka, for your sake!
5.20.19
नेह पश्यामि लोकेऽन्यम्
यो मे प्रतिबलो भवेत् ।
पश्य मे सुमहद्वीर्यम्
अप्रतिद्वन्द्वमाहवे ॥
I cannot think of anyone else in this entire world who can rival me in strength. You should realize how mighty I am; there is no one who can challenge me in the battle field.
5.20.20
असकृत्संयुगे भग्ना
मया विमृदितध्वजाः ।
अशक्ताः प्रत्यनीकेषु
स्थातुं मम सुरासुराः ॥
Crushed in many battles by me and their flag posts shattered, no Sura or Asura dare stand in an army that opposes me.
5.20.21
इच्छ मां क्रियतामद्य
प्रतिकर्म तवोत्तमम् ।
सप्रभाण्यवसज्यन्ताम्
तवाङ्गे भूषणानि च ।
साधु पश्यामि ते रूपम्
संयुक्तं प्रतिकर्मणा ॥
Love me. All you should do now is to decorate yourself to the best. Let every part of your body be bedecked with dazzling ornaments. I want to see your beauty enhanced by all those embellishments.
5.20.23
प्रतिकर्माभिसंयुक्ता
दाक्षिण्येन वरानने ।
भुंक्ष्व भोगान्यथाकामम्
पिब भीरु रमस्व च ॥
O timid one of lovely face! Decorate yourself in every possible way and yield to your delicate tastes! You should enjoy every luxury as you please and drink and make merry.
5.20.24
यथेष्टं च प्रयच्छ त्वम्
पृथिवीं वा धनानि च ।
ललस्व मयि विस्रब्धा
धृष्टमाज्ञापयस्व च ॥
Give away as much of my land and wealth as you like. Keep trust in me and enjoy. Command me without any apprehension.
5.20.25
मत्प्रसादाल्ललन्त्याश्च
ललन्तां बान्धवास्तव ।
ऋद्धिं ममानुपश्य त्वम्
श्रियं भद्रे यशश्च मे ॥
As you enjoy, with my grace, so will all your relatives. Just take a good look at my affluence, wealth and renown, my dear!
5.20.26
किं करिष्यसि रामेण
सुभगे चीरवाससा ।
निक्षिप्तविजयो रामो
गतश्रीर्वनगोचरः ।
व्रती स्थण्डिलशायी च
शङ्के जीवति वा न वा ॥
O lovely one! What will you do with Rāma who is clad in tree bark? I even wonder whether Rāma is alive or not, relieved of his wealth, roaming in the forests, observing austerities, sleeping on bare ground, and having lost the means to win in any battle.
5.20.27
न हि वैदेहि रामस्त्वाम्
द्रष्टुं वा प्युपलप्स्यते ।
पुरोबलाकैरसितैः
मेघैर्ज्योत्स्नामिवावृताम् ॥
Rāma will not be able to steal even a bare glimpse of you, O Vaidēhee, like one cannot get that of moonlight, on which a curtain of dark clouds (is drawn by) a line of cranes flying in front.
5.20.28
न चापि मम हस्तात्त्वाम्
प्राप्तुमर्हति राघवः ।
हिरण्यकशिपुः कीर्तिम्
इन्द्रहस्तगतामिव ॥
Nor can Rāghava hope to retrieve you from my hands as Hiraṇyakaṡipu could his wife Keerti, from the hands of Indra.
5.20.29
चारुस्मिते चारुदति
चारुनेत्रे विलासिनि ।
मनो हरसि मे भीरु
सुपर्णः पन्नगं यथा ॥
O timid one! O charming dame! You snatched my heart with your lovely eyes beautiful smile and beautiful teeth, as Suparṇa does a serpent.
5.20.30
क्लिष्टकौशेयवसनाम्
तन्वीमप्यनलङ्कृताम् ।
त्वां दृष्ट्वा स्वेषु दारेषु
रतिं नोपलभाम्यहम् ॥
I am not able to enjoy love with my wives after seeing you, even though you are emaciated, wear crumpled silk cloth, and lack any toilet or ornament.
5.20.31
अन्तःपुरनिवासिन्यः
स्त्रियः सर्वगुणान्विताः ।
यावन्त्यो मम सर्वासाम्
ऐश्वर्यम् कुरु जानकि ॥
O Jānaki! Become the queen over all the women, whosoever are in my inner quarters, embodiments of every excellence.
5.20.32
मम ह्यसितकेशान्ते
त्रैलोक्यप्रवराः स्त्रियः ।
तास्त्वां परिचरिष्यन्ति
श्रियमप्सरसो यथा ॥
O you of black tresses! My women who are the best of the best in all the three worlds, shall wait upon you like Apsaras do upon goddess Lakshmee.
5.20.33
यानि वैश्रवणे सुभ्रु
रत्नानि च धनानि च ।
तानि लोकांश्च सुश्रोणि
मां च भुङ्क्ष्व यथासुखम् ॥
O you of lovely eyebrows and beautiful Kaṭee! Enjoy all the wealth and gems of Vaiṡravaṇa, and also mine and all that is in the three worlds!
5.20.34
न रामस्तपसा देवि
न बलेन न विक्रमैः ।
न धनेन मया तुल्यः
तेजसा यशसापि वा ॥
Rama is no equal to me, my lady, in either Tapa, strength, prowess, wealth, power or fame!
5.20.35
पिब विहर रमस्व भुङ्क्ष्व भोगान्
धननिचयं प्रदिशामि मेदिनीं च ।
मयि लल ललने यथासुखं त्वं
त्वयि च समेत्य ललन्तु बान्धवास्ते ॥
Drink, play, be merry, and enjoy all the luxuries! I will give you all my treasures and lands. O playful one, play with me, enjoying as you please! And your relations also shall come together and enjoy with you.
5.20.36
कुसुमिततरुजालसन्ततानि
भ्रमरयुतानि समुद्रतीरजानि ।
कनकविमलहारभूषिताङ्गी
विहर मया सह भीरु काननानि ॥
O timid one, enjoy walking along with me, with all your body decked in pure gold ornaments, in the forest by the shore of the ocean which is full of trees covered by flowers and humming bees.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे विंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twentieth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
