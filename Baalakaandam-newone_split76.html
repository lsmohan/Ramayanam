Baala Kaanda - Sarga 76
In this Sarga, Rāma mounts an arrow on the bow of Vishṇu. Rāma, the son of Jamadagni realizes that Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha is none other than Vishṇu himself. After Rāma’s arrow takes away his privilege of higher Lōkas that he attained through Tapa, he returns to Mahēndra mountain.
1.76.1
श्रुत्वा तज्जामदग्न्यस्य
वाक्यं दाशरथिस्तदा ।
गौरवाद्यन्त्रितकथः
पितू राममथाब्रवीत् ॥
On hearing the words of the son of Jamadagni, the son of Daṡaratha, out of respect to his father spoke in a restrained voice:
1.76.2
श्रुतवानस्मि यत्कर्म
कृतवानसि भार्गव ।
अनुरुंध्यामहे ब्रह्मन्
पितुरानृण्यमास्थितम् ॥
O descendant of Bhṛgu! I am well aware of what you have done! O Brahman! Congratulations on redeeming (the murder of) your father!
1.76.3
वीर्यहीनमिवाशक्तम्
क्षत्रधर्मेण भार्गव ।
अवजानासि मे तेजः
पश्य मेऽद्य पराक्रमम् ॥
But you slight me as if I am a weakling and unfitting of my Kshatra Dharma! O descendant of Bhṛgu! You shall now see my power and prowess!
1.76.4
इत्युक्त्वा राघवः क्रुद्धो
भार्गवस्य शरासनम् ।
शरं च प्रतिजग्राह
हस्ताल्लघुपराक्रमः ॥
The angry prince of Raghu dynasty, saying thus, effortlessly snatched the bow from the hands of the descendant of Bhṛgu along with the arrow.
1.76.5
आरोप्य स धनू रामः
शरं सज्यं चकार ह ।
जामदग्न्यं ततो रामम्
रामः क्रुद्धोऽब्रवीद्वचः ॥
After bending the bow and stringing it, Rāma mounted the arrow on it and said to Rāma, son of Jamadagni, in wrath:
1.76.6
ब्राह्मणोऽसीति पूज्यो मे
विश्वामित्रकृतेन च ।
तस्माच्छक्तो न ते राम
मोक्तुं प्राणहरं शरम् ॥
Because you are a Brāhmaṇa and related to Viṡwāmitra, you deserve to be respected. Hence I cannot release this arrow upon you, which can take away your life.
1.76.7
इमां पादगतिं राम
तपोबलसमार्जितान् ।
लोकानप्रतिमान्वा ते
हनिष्यामि यदिच्छसि ॥
O Rāma! Hence I would destroy either the speed of your movement or your privilege to the unparalleled Lōkas that you acquired through your power of Tapa! Let me know your preference!
Dialogs similar to this can be heard even in modern day movies. Vālmeeki’s expressions are truly timeless!
1.76.8
न ह्ययं वैष्णवो दिव्यः
शरः परपुरञ्जयः ।
मोघः पतति वीर्येण
बलदर्पविनाशनः ॥
This divine arrow of Vishṇu, that can destroy all the cities of the enemies, and hubris and arrogance as well, cannot go for naught.
1.76.9
वरायुधधरं रामम्
द्रष्टुं सर्षिगणास्सुराः ।
पितामहं पुरस्कृत्य
समेतास्तत्र सङ्घशः ।
गन्धर्वाप्सरसश्चैव
सिद्धचारणकिन्नराः ।
यक्षराक्षसनागाश्च
तद्द्रष्टुं महदद्भुतम् ॥
All the Dēvas led by the Progenitor along with Ṛshis, Gandharvas, Apsaras, Siddhas, Rākshasas and Nāgas assembled to see that marvelous spectacle of Rāma armed with that superb weapon.
1.76.11
जडीकृते तदा लोके
रामे वरधनुर्धरे ।
निर्वीर्यो जामदग्न्योऽसौ
रामो राममुदैक्षत ॥
Rāma, the son of Jamadagni, watched Rāma (the son of Daṡaratha) holding that superb bow, in awe, as the worlds were rendered motionless, and he himself was rendered impotent.
1.76.12
तेजोभिहतवीर्यत्वात्
जमदग्न्यो जडीकृतः ।
रामं कमलपत्राक्षम्
मन्दं मन्दमुवाच ह ॥
After that mighty prowess destroyed his strength and rendered him motionless the son of Jamadagni spoke slowly and gently to Rāma, the lotus eyed:
1.76.13
काश्यपाय मया दत्ता
यदा पूर्वं वसुन्धरा ।
विषये मे न वस्तव्यम्
इति मां काश्यपोऽब्रवीत् ॥
A long time ago, when the entire lands on the earth were given to Kāṡyapa by me he made it clear that I should not live in any part within that area.
This Ṡlōka illustrates the meaning of true giving without any strings, whatsoever, attached.
1.76.14
सोऽहं गुरुवचः कुर्वन्
पृथिव्यां न वसे निशाम् ।
कृता प्रतिज्ञा काकुत्स्थ
कृता भूः काश्यपस्य हि ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! I vowed to him so, and in deference to those words of my Guru, I would not like to spend the night on this earth!
1.76.15
तदिमां त्वं गतिं वीर
हन्तुं नार्हसि राघव ।
मनोजवं गमिष्यामि
महेन्द्रं पर्वतोत्तमम् ॥
O Veera! Hence please do not hamper my speed. O prince of Raghu dynasty! I will now depart instantaneously, with the speed of mind, back to that great Mahēndra mountain!
1.76.16
लोकास्त्वप्रतिमा राम
निर्जितास्तपसा मया ।
जहि तान् शरमुख्येन
मा भूत्कालस्य पर्ययः ॥
O Rāma! May you hence target, with that exceptional arrow my privilege to the exceptional Lōkas, which has been won by me with Tapa, without any further delay!
1.76.17
अक्षय्यं मधुहन्तारम्
जानामि त्वां सुरेश्वरम् ।
धनुषोऽस्य परामर्शात्
स्वस्ति तेऽस्तु परंतप ॥
From your ability to handle this bow, I realize that you are none other than the imperishable lord of Dēvas, the slayer of Madhu! O tormentor of foes! May all bode well for you!
Madhu is one of the Rākshasas killed by lord Vishṇu.
1.76.18
एते सुरगणास्सर्वे
निरीक्षन्ते समागताः ।
त्वामप्रतिमकर्माणं
अप्रतिद्वन्द्वमाहवे ॥
All the assembled Dēvas are watching you, who are of stupendous deeds and without a rival in fight.
1.76.19
न चेयं मम काकुत्स्थ
व्रीला भवितुमर्हति ।
त्वया त्रैलोक्यनाथेन
यदहं विमुखीकृतः ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! It is indeed no matter of shame for me, to be defeated by the lord of the three worlds!
1.76.20
शरमप्रतिमं राम
मोक्तुमर्हसि सुव्रत ।
शरमोक्षे गमिष्यामि
महेन्द्रं पर्वतोत्तमम् ॥
O Rāma! O highly disciplined! You may now release that exceptional arrow! After you release that arrow, I will go back to Mahēndra mountain, the best among mountains!
1.76.21
तथा ब्रुवति रामे तु
जामदग्न्ये प्रतापवान् ।
रामो दाशरथिश्श्रीमान्
चिक्षेप शरमुत्तमम् ॥
When Rāma, the son of Jamadagni spoke thus, the valiant Rāma, son of Daṡaratha, deployed that exceptional arrow.
1.76.22
स हतान् दृश्य रामेण
स्वांल्लोकान् तपसाऽऽर्जितान् ।
जामदग्न्यो जगामाशु
महेन्द्रं पर्वतोत्तमम् ॥
Seeing the privilege to all the Lōkas gained by the merit of Tapa having thus been destroyed, the son of Jamadagni left for the exceptional Mahēndra mountain.
1.76.23
ततो वितिमिरास्सर्वा
दिशश्चोपदिशस्तथा ।
सुरास्सर्षिगणा रामम्
प्रशशंसुरुदायुधम् ॥
Then the darkness in every direction and in-between was cleared. All the Dēvas along with the host of Ṛshis lauded Rāma, who was wielding that exceptional weapon.
1.76.24
रामं दाशरथिं रामो
जामदग्न्यः प्रशस्य च ।
ततः प्रदक्षिणी कृत्य
जगामात्मगतिं प्रभुः ॥
Rāma, the son of Jamadagni also lauded Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha. After performing Pradakshiṇa to him, he left for his destination.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे षट्सप्ततितमस्सर्ग:॥
Thus concludes the seventy sixth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
