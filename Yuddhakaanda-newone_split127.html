Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 127
In this Sarga, Bharata instructs Ṡatrughna to prepare Ayōdhyā for receiving Rāma. Ṡatrughna arranges for the streets of Ayōdhyā to be cleaned and decorated, and the road between Nandigrāma and Ayōdhyā repaired of its dips and bumps.
Everyone in Ayōdhyā, the women of Daṡaratha, the ministers, the armed forces, prominent businessmen, artisans and the citizens go to Nandigrāma to receive Rāma. Bharata, in the company of all of them, placing the sandals on his head, goes to receive Rāma.
Rāma, along with Seetā, Lakshmaṇa, Vānaras and Rākshasas, arrives in the Pushpaka. Everyone shouts, 'Here is Rāma!' on seeing him. All of them, getting down from their vehicles and standing on the ground, behold him who is seated in the Vimāna.
Then the Vimāna lands, and Bharata, helped to get on to it, prostrates to Rāma, and then to Lakshmaṇa and Seetā. He then embraces each one of the Vānara chiefs. He, with a sense of deep appreciation tells Sugreeva that he is like the fifth brother to the four of them. Then Ṡatrughna prostrates to Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Seetā.
Then Rāma bows and touches the feet of his mother Kousalyā, and then pays obeisance to Sumitrā, Kaikēyee and to all his other step-mothers. All the citizens say to him, "Welcome to you, O you of mighty arm, the delight of Kousalyā!"
Bharata then brings the sandals himself and puts them on the feet of Rāma, saying, "Here is your kingdom, which I looked after since the time you left, with the treasury, granaries and army increased ten-fold!"
Rāma then discharges the Pushpaka, and that Vimāna goes back to Kubēra.
6.127.1
श्रुत्वा तु परमानन्दं
भरतः सत्यविक्रमः ।
हृष्टमाज्ञापयामास
शत्रुघ्नं परवीरहा ॥
Hearing the news that brought him supreme joy, Bharata of true prowess instructed Ṡatrughna, destroyer of enemy warriors:
6.127.2
दैवतानि च सर्वाणि
चैत्यानि नगरस्य च ।
सुगन्धमाल्यैर्वादित्रैः
अर्चन्तु शुचयो नराः ॥
May the men, purifying themselves, offer Pūja at all the shrines in the city with incense, garlands and musical instruments.
6.127.3
सूताः स्तुतिपुराणज्ञाः
सर्वे वैतालिकास्तथा ।
सर्वे वादित्रकुशला
गणिकाश्चैव संघशः ।
राजदारास्तथामात्याः
सैन्याः सेनाङ्गनागणाः ।
ब्राह्मणाश्च सराजन्याः
श्रेणिमुख्यास्तथा गणाः ।
अभिनिर्यान्तु रामस्य
द्रष्टुं शशिनिभं मुखम् ॥
May all chroniclers who are well-versed in hymns of exaltation and ancient lore, all the bards that sing praises, all those skilled with musical instruments, the bands of courtesans, the women of the king, the ministers, the armies, the bands of mercenaries, Brāhmaṇas along with Kshatriyas, the leaders of the traders' guilds, and the bands of artisans come out to see the moon-like face of Rāma!
In these two Ṡlōkas, we get a feel for all the constituents of the society during the times of Rāmāyaṇa.
6.127.5
भरतस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
शत्रुघ्नः परवीरहा ।
विष्टीरनेकसाहस्रीः
चोदयामास भागशः ॥
Hearing those words of Bharata, Ṡatrughna, destroyer of enemy warriors, deployed thousands of workers on various tasks, instructing:
6.127.6
समीकुरुत निम्नानि
विषमाणि समानि च ।
स्थानानि च निरस्यन्तां
नन्दिग्रामादितः परम् ॥
May all the depressions be leveled and all the uneven places be made even from this Nandigrāma onwards.
6.127.7
सिञ्चन्तु पृथिवीं कृत्स्नां
हिमशीतेन वारिणा ।
ततोऽभ्यवकिरंस्त्वन्ये
लाजैः पुष्पैश्च सर्वतः ॥
May all the grounds be sprinkled with ice-cold water. Then, may they be strewn, everywhere, with flowers and puffed grain.
6.127.8
समुच्छ्रितपताकास्तु
रथ्याः पुरवरोत्तमे ।
शोभयन्तु च वेश्मानि
सूर्यस्योदयनं प्रति ॥
May flags be hoisted on the streets of this best of cities. May all the houses be decorated by the time the sun rises.
6.127.9
स्रग्दाममुक्तपुष्पैश्च
सुगन्धैः पञ्चवर्णकैः ।
राजमार्गमसम्बाधं
किरन्तु शतशो नराः ॥
May hundreds of men decorate the royal pathways, which know no congestion, with garlands and loose flowers of all five colors and fragrance.
6.127.10
ततस्तच्छासनं श्रुत्वा
शत्रुघ्नस्य मुदान्विताः ।
धृष्टिर्जयन्तो विजयः
सिद्धार्थश्चार्थसाधकः ।
अशोको मन्त्रपालश्च
सुमन्त्रश्चापि निर्ययुः ॥
On hearing that command of Ṡatrughna, Dṛshṭi, Jayanta, Vijaya, Siddhārtha, Arthasādhaka, Aṡōka, Mantrapāla and Sumantra set out with joy.
6.127.12
मत्तैर्नागसहस्रैश्च
सध्वजेः सुविभूषितैः ।
अपरे हेमकक्ष्याभिः
सगजाभिः करेणुभिः ।
निर्ययुस्त्वरया युक्ता
रथैश्च सुमहारथाः ॥
They went, with all the urgency, riding on thousands of elephants in rut that were well-decorated and with flags hoisted on them, and some on elephants and she-elephants decked with gold girdles, and the great charioters on chariots.
6.127.13
शक्त्यष्टिपाशहस्तानां
सध्वजानां पताकिनाम् ।
तुरगाणां सहस्त्रैश्च
मुख्यैर्मुख्यतरान्वितैः ।
पदातीनां सहस्त्रैश्च
वीराः परिवृता ययुः ॥
Those Warriors were accompanied by selected horses in thousands decorated with flag posts and flags, with even more selected horsemen riding on them with Ṡaktis, Yashṭis and nooses in their hands, and also by foot soldiers in thousands.
6.127.15
ततो यानान्युपारूढाः
सर्वा दशरथस्त्रियः ।
कौसल्यां प्रमुखे कृत्वा
सुमित्रां चापि निर्ययुः ।
कैकेय्या सहिताः सर्वा
नन्दिग्राममुपागमन् ॥
Then all the women of Daṡaratha, led by Kousalyā and Sumitrā, along with Kaikēyee, got into the carriages and arrived at Nandigrāma.
6.127.16
द्विजातिमुख्यैर्धर्मात्मा
श्रेणीमुख्यैः सनैगमैः ।
माल्यमोदक हस्तैश्च
मन्त्रिभिर्भरतो वृतः ।
शङ्खभेरीनिनादैश्च
बन्दिभिश्चाभिवन्दितः ।
आर्यपादौ गृहीत्वा तु
शिरसा धर्मकोविदः ।
पाण्डुरं छत्रमादाय
शुक्लमाल्योपशोभितम् ।
शुक्ले च वालव्यजने
राजार्हे हेमभूषिते ।
उपवासकृशो दीनः
चीरकृष्णाजिनाम्बरः ।
भ्रातुरागमनं श्रुत्वा
तत्पूर्वं हर्षमागतः ।
प्रत्युद्ययौ तदा रामं
महात्मा सचिवैः सह ॥
Placing the sandals of the noble one on his head, taking priceless, gold-decked, white fly-whisks that were worthy of a king and a white umbrella decked with white garlands,
Bharata, the Dharmātma, a savant of Dharma, who was downcast and had withered with fasting and had the first flicker of happiness in a long time only a while ago upon hearing the news of the arrival of his brother,
went to receive Mahātma Rāma, clad in tree bark and deer skin, accompanied by the blare of conches and the blast of kettledrums, and the praises of the panegyrists,
along with the ministers, the foremost of the Brāhmaṇas, the chiefs of business guilds, the heads of business houses and counselors carrying sweets and bouquets.
6.127.21
अश्वानां खरशब्दैश्च
रथनेमिस्वनेन च ।
शङ्खदुन्दुभिनादेन
संचचालेव मेदिनी ॥
The earth seemed, as it were, to shake with the sound of horse hoofs, the rattling of rims of chariot wheels and by the blare of conches and blast of kettledrums.
6.127.22
कृत्स्नं तु नगरं तत्तु
नन्दिग्राममुपागमत् ।
समीक्ष्य भरतो वाक्यम्
उवाच पवनात्मजम् ॥
The entire City of Ayōdhyā literally reached Nandigrāma. Glancing around, Bharata said these words to the son of Vāyu:
6.127.23
कच्चिन्न खलु कापेयी
सेव्यते चलचित्तता ।
न हि पश्यामि काकुत्स्थं
राममार्यं परन्तपम् ॥
I do not see Rāma, the scorcher of foe, the noble descendant of Kākutstha. I hope you, like a true monkey, have not been flippant (in giving me the news of the arrival of Rāma)!
6.127.24
अथैवमुक्ते वचने
हनूमानिदमब्रवीत् ।
अर्थ्यं विज्ञापयन्नेव
भरतं सत्यविक्रमम् ॥
When he was asked thus, Hanumān respectfully offered the following explanation, to Bharata of true prowess:
6.127.25
सदा फलान्कुसुमितान्
वृक्षान्प्राप्य मधुस्रवान् ।
भरद्वाजप्रसादेन
मत्तभ्रमरनादितान् ।
तस्य चैष वरो दत्तो
वासवेन परन्तप ।
ससैन्यस्य तदातिथ्यं
कृतं सर्वगुणान्वितम् ।
निस्वनः श्रूयते भीमः
प्रहृष्टानां वनौकसाम् ।
मन्ये वानरसेना सा
नदीं तरति गोमतीम् ॥
With trees in flower and fruit and oozing honey perennially and the bees humming around them by the grace of Bharadwāja, and also with the same granted as a boon by Vāsava, O scorcher of foe, they seem to be getting ready to host you and your army with everything desirable; from the mighty din of the rejoicing Vana dwellers that is being heard, I think the Vānara army is crossing Gōmati.
6.127.28
रजोवर्षं समुद्भूतं
पश्य वालुकिनीं प्रति ।
मन्ये सालवनं रम्यं
लोपयन्ति प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
See the the billows of dust rising, in the direction of Vālukini. I think the Vānaras are shaking the trees in the lovely Sāla groves.
6.127.29
तदेतद्दृश्यते दूरात्
विमलं चन्द्रसंनिभम् ।
विमानं पुष्पकं दिव्यं
मनसा ब्रह्मनिर्मितम् ॥
That one, which can be seen at a distance, that shines like the moon, is the divine Vimāna called Pushpaka that has been fashioned by Brahma, from his own imagination.
6.127.30
रावणं बान्धवैः सार्धं
हत्वा लब्धं महात्मना ।
तरुणादित्यसंकाशं
विमानं रामवाहनम् ।
धनदस्य प्रसादेन
दिव्यमेतन्मनोजवम् ॥
The Mahātma won it by killing Rāvaṇa and all his kith and kin. Shining like the rising sun, it serves as Rāma's vehicle. This splendid one, which flies at the speed of thought was begotten by Kubēra from the pleased Brahma.
6.127.31
एतस्मिन्भ्रातरौ वीरौ
वैदेह्या सह राघवौ ।
सुग्रीवश्च महातेजा
राक्षसेन्द्रो विभीषणः ॥
The valiant brothers, the scions of Raghu dynasty, along with Vaidēhee are in it, and so are Sugreeva, the extremely powerful one and Vibheeshaṇa, the king of Rākshasas.
6.127.32
ततो हर्षसमुद्भूतो
निस्वनो दिवमस्पृशत् ।
स्त्रीबालयुववृद्धानां
रामोऽयमिति कीर्तितः ॥
Then there arose a din of joyous shouts, whose reverberations seemed to touch the sky, from the women, children, youth and the old, that pronounced, 'Here is Rāma!'
6.127.33
रथकुञ्जरवाजिभ्यः
तेऽवतीर्य महीं गताः ।
ददृशुस्तं विमानस्थं
नराः सोममिवाम्बरे ॥
All the men, getting down on to the ground from their chariots, elephants and horses, beheld him, who was in the Vimāna, like one does the moon in the sky.
6.127.34
प्राञ्जलिर्भरतो भूत्वा
प्रहृष्टो राघवोन्मुखः ।
स्वागतेन यथार्हेण
ततो राममपूजयत् ॥
The overjoyed Bharata, his palms joined in reverence and face turned towards Rāma, welcomed him in a manner that was worthy of him, and paid due respects.
6.127.35
मनसा ब्रह्मणा सृष्टे
विमाने लक्ष्मणाग्रजः ।
रराज पृथुदीर्घाक्षो
वज्रपाणिरिवापरः ॥
The older brother of Lakshmaṇa, of large and wide eyes, seated in that Vimāna, which was created by Brahma from his imagination, shone as if he were another Wielder of Vajra.
6.127.36
ततो विमानाग्रगतं
भरतो भ्रातरं तदा ।
ववन्दे प्रणतो रामं
मेरुस्थमिव भास्करम् ॥
Then Bharata bowed and saluted his brother Rāma, who, seated at the top of that Vimāna, shone like the sun on the top of Mēru.
6.127.37
ततो रामाभ्यनुज्ञातं
तद्विमानमनुत्तमम् ।
हंसयुक्तं महावेगम्
निपपात महीतले ॥
Then with the permission of Rāma, the unexcelled Vimāna of extraordinary speed that was yoked with swans, landed on the earth.
6.127.38
आरोपितो विमानं
तद्भरतः सत्यविक्रमः ।
राममासाद्य मुदितः
पुनरेवाभ्यवादयत् ॥
Helped to get onto that Vimāna, Bharata of true prowess approached Rāma joyously and prostrated once again.
6.127.39
तं समुत्थाप्य काकुत्स्थः
चिरस्याक्षिपथं गतम् ।
अङ्के भरतमारोप्य
मुदितः परिषष्वजे ॥
Raising Bharata up, whom his eyes could see only after such a long time, Kākutstha made him sit on his lap and rejoiced, taking him into his embrace.
6.127.40
ततो लक्ष्मणमासाद्य
वैदेहीं च परन्तपः ।
अथाभ्यवादयत्प्रीतो
भरतो नाम चाब्रवीत् ॥
Then Bharata, scorcher of the foe, went to Lakshmaṇa and to Vaidēhee and was so pleased to prostrate and announce his name to them.
6.127.41
सुग्रीवं कैकयी पुत्रो
जाम्बवन्तं तथाङ्गदम् ।
मैन्दं च द्विविदं नीलम्
ऋषभं परिषष्वजे ॥
Then the son of Kaikēyee gave a hug to Jāmbavān, and as well to Aṅgada, Mainda, Dwivida, Neela, and Ṛshabha.
6.127.42
सुषेणं च नलं चैव
गवाक्षम् गन्धमादनम् ।
शरभं पनसं चैव
परितः परिष्वजे ॥
He also gave a hug to Sushēṇa, Nala, Gavāksha, Gandhamādana, Ṡarabha, Panasa and others who were around.
6.127.43
ते कृत्वा मानुषं रूपं
वानराः कामरूपिणः ।
कुशलं पर्यपृच्छंस्ते
प्रहृष्टा भरतं तदा ॥
Then, those Vānaras, who could change their form at will, assumed the form of humans and joyously inquired about the welfare of Bharata.
6.127.44
अथाब्रवीद्राजपुत्रः
सुग्रीवं वानरर्षभम् ।
परिष्वज्य महातेजा
भरतो धर्मिणां वरः ॥
Thereupon, Bharata, a prince of extraordinary power and foremost of the adherents of Dharma, embraced Sugreeva, a bull among Vānaras, and said:
6.127.45
त्वमस्माकं चतुर्णां
वैभ्राता सुग्रीव पञ्चमः ।
सौहार्दाज्जायते मित्रम्
अपकारोऽरिलक्षणम् ॥
You are a fifth brother for the four of us, O Sugreeva! A friend is born of affection; harming is the mark of an enemy.
6.127.46
विभीषणं च भरतः
सान्त्ववाक्यमथाब्रवीत् ।
दिष्ट्या त्वया सहायेन
कृतं कर्म सुदुष्करम् ॥
Then Bharata said these appreciative words to Vibheeshaṇa, "By the good grace of Providence, the extremely difficult mission was accomplished with your help!"
6.127.47
शत्रुघ्नश्च तदा रामम्
अभिवाद्य सलक्ष्मणम् ।
सीतायाश्चरणौ पश्चात्
ववन्दे विनयान्वितः ॥
Then Ṡatrughna prostrated to Rāma and Lakshmaṇa. And then, he bowed at the feet of Seetā, with all deference.
6.127.48
रामो मातरमासाद्य
विवर्णां शोककर्शिताम् ।
जग्राह प्रणतः पादौ
मनो मातुः प्रहर्षयन् ॥
Going to his mother, who was pale and whithered with grief, Rāma bowed and touched her feet filling the heart of that mother with joy.
6.127.49
अभिवाद्य सुमित्रां च
कैकेयीं च यशस्विनीम् ।
स मातॄश्च तदा सर्वाः
पुरोहितमुपागमत् ॥
And he made his obeisance to Sumitrā and the famous Kaikēyee and to all his other step-mothers, and to the Purōhita who had come.
6.127.50
स्वागतं ते महाबाहो
कौसल्यानन्दवर्धन ।
इति प्राञ्जलयः सर्वे
नागरा राममब्रुवन् ॥
All the citizens said to Rāma, their palms joined and raised in reverence, "Welcome to you, O you of mighty arm, the delight of Kousalyā!"
6.127.51
तन्यञ्जलिसहस्राणि
प्रगृहीतानि नागरैः ।
व्याकोशानीव पद्मानि
ददर्श भरताग्रजः ॥
Bharata’s older brother beheld those thousands of joined palms that looked like lotuses that had just opened.
6.127.52
पादुके ते तु रामस्य
गृहीत्वा भरतः स्वयम् ।
चरणाभ्यां नरेन्द्रस्य
योजयामास धर्मवित् ॥
Then Bharata, who knew Dharma, brought the sandals himself and put them on the feet of the lord of peoples.
6.127.53
अब्रवीच्च तदा रामं
भरतः स कृताञ्जलिः ॥
His palms joined in reverence, Bharata said to Rāma:
6.127.53
एतत्ते रक्षितं राजन्
राज्यं निर्यातितं मया ।
अद्य जन्म कृतार्थं मे
संवृत्तश्च मनोरथः ।
यस्त्वां पश्यामि राजानम्
अयोध्यां पुनरागतम् ॥
Here is your kingdom, O King, looked after by me, since you left. The purpose of my life is served and my heart's desire is fulfilled, seeing you return to be the king of Ayōdhyā.
6.127.55
अवेक्षतां भवान्कोशं
कोष्ठागारं पुरं बलम् ।
भवतस्तेजसा सर्वं
कृतं दशगुणं मया ॥
Be pleased to inspect the treasury and the granaries, the houses and the army; thanks to your power, everything is multiplied tenfold by me.
6.127.56
तथा ब्रुवाणं भरतं
दृष्ट्वा तं भ्रातृवत्सलम् ।
मुमुचुर्वानरा बाष्पं
राक्षसश्च विभीषणः ॥
Seeing Bharata, who so deeply loved his brother, speak thus, the Vānaras and the Rākshasa Vibheeshaṇa shed tears.
6.127.57
ततः प्रहर्षाद्भरतम्
अङ्कमारोप्य राघवः ।
ययौ तेन विमानेन
ससैन्यो भरताश्रमम् ॥
Placing Bharata on his lap with delight, Rāghava thereupon flew, along with the army, to Bharata's Āṡrama in that Vimāna.
6.127.58
भरताश्रममासाद्य
ससैन्यो राघवस्तदा ।
अवतीर्य विमानाग्रात्
अवतस्थे महीतले ॥
Reaching Bharata's Āṡrama, Rāghava got down from the front of the Vimāna, along with the army and stood on the ground.
6.127.59
अब्रवीच्च तदा रामः
तद्विमानमनुत्तमम् ।
वह वैश्रवणं देवम्
अनुजानामि गम्यताम् ॥
Rāma then said to that unexcelled Vimāna, "I shall let you go and serve as a transport to the Dēva Vaiṡravaṇa!"
6.127.60
ततो रामाभ्यनुज्ञातं
तद्विमानमनुत्तमम् ।
उत्तरां दिशमुद्दिश्य
जगाम धनदालयम् ॥
Thus given leave by Rāma, that unexcelled Vimāna, went in the northerly direction to the abode of Kubēra.
6.127.61
विमानं पुष्पकं दिव्यं
सम्गृहीतं तु रक्षसा ।
अगमद्धनदं वेगात्
रामवाक्यप्रचोदितम् ॥
Pushpaka, the celestial aerial vehicle, which was captured by the Rākshasa, went to Kubēra in all speed, following those words of Rāma.
6.127.62
पुरोहितस्यात्मसमस्य राघवो
बृहस्पतेः शक्र इवामराधिपः ।
निपीड्य पादौ पृथगासने शुभे
सहैव तेनोपविवेश वीर्यवान् ॥
Rāghava then clasped the feet of his Purōhita, who was worthy of him, like Ṡakra, the ruler of the Amaras, did those of Bṛhaspati. The valiant one then sat on a splendid chair, seating him also on another splendid one.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे सप्तविंशत्युत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and twenty seventh Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
