Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 46
Vālmeeki smoothly transitioned us with the last Ṡlōka of the previous Sarga, into the quietness of the Vana, away from the hustle and bustle of the city.
In this Sarga, Rāma puts the thoughts of Ayōdhyā behind, consoles himself that his father and mother would be all right under the kind care of Bharata.
He tells Lakshmaṇa not to worry about having to go in exile and assures him that everything would be all right ahead of them.
Lakshmaṇa stays awake all night, reminiscing the virtues of Rāma with Sumantra.
Rāma gets up early in the morning and feels sad about the citizens who are sleeping under the trees, leaving their homes behind, in order to follow him. In order to not cause further inconvenience to them, Rāma suggests that they should leave for the Vana before the citizens get up. Lakshmaṇa agrees. Sumantra makes the chariot ready. They all drive off, crossing the river Tamasā.
2.46.1
ततस्तु तमसातीरम्
रम्यमाश्रित्य राघवः ।
सीतामुद्वीक्ष्य सौमित्रिम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Upon reaching the delightful bank of river Tamasā, Rāghava looked at Seetā, and said to the son of Sumitrā:
2.46.2
इयमद्य निशा पूर्वा
सौमित्रे प्रहिता वनम् ।
वनवासस्य भद्रं ते
स नोत्कण्ठितुमर्हसि ॥
O son of Sumitrā! This would be the first night of life in Vana after we have been exiled to the Vana. May you not have any kind of worry. Everything will bode well for you.
Lakshmaṇa was very angry about Rāma being exiled. Hence, Rāma is making sure that Lakshmaṇa is well disposed to the stay in the Vana.
2.46.3
पश्य शून्यान्यरण्यानि
रुदन्तीव समन्ततः ।
यथानिलयमायद्भिः
निलीनानि मृगद्विजैः ॥
Look at these woods that seem empty with the birds and beasts finding their hiding places. It feels as if it is crying, in every direction.
2.46.4
अद्यायोध्या तु नगरी
राजधानी पितुर्मम ।
सस्त्रीपुंसा गतानस्मान्
शोचिष्यति न संशयः ॥
Today, the City of Ayōdhyā, my father's capital, with all its men and women, will no doubt be brooding over us who have come away!
2.46.5
अनुरक्ता हि मनुजा
राजानं बहुभिर्गुणैः ।
त्वां च मां च नरव्याघ्र
शत्रुघ्न भरतौ तथा ॥
O tiger among men! People love all of us, the king, you, me, Ṡatrughna and Bharata for various good reasons.
2.46.6
पितरं चानुशोचामि
मातरं च यशस्विनीम् ।
अपि वान्धौ भवेतां तु
रुदन्तौ तावभीक्ष्णशः ॥
Father and the well regarded mother, I worry, may become blind, crying endlessly.
2.46.7
भरतः खलु धर्मात्मा
पितरं मातरं च मे ।
धर्मार्थकामसहितैः
वाक्यैर्वाश्वासयिष्यति ॥
Bharata, the Dharmātma, will indeed console father and my mother with righteous, meaningful and endearing words.
2.46.8
भरतस्यानृशंसत्वम्
विचिन्त्याहं पुनः पुनः ।
नानुशोचामि पितरम्
मातरं चापि लक्ष्मण ॥
O Lakshmaṇa, I would hold myself from worrying about father and mother, reminding myself again and again, of the gentle attitude of Bharata.
2.46.9
त्वया कार्यं नरव्याघ्र
मामनुव्रजता कृतम् ।
अन्वेष्टव्या हि वैदेह्या
रक्षणार्थे सहायता ॥
O tiger among men, it is good that you have come following me. I would any way have looked for some help, to protect the princess of Vidēha.
2.46.10
अद्भिरेव तु सौमित्रे
वत्स्याम्यद्य निशामिमाम् ।
एतध्दि रोचते मह्यम्
वन्येऽपि विविधे सति ॥
Tonight, O son of Sumitrā, I somehow feel like taking only water, though various kinds of forest produce are available.
2.46.11
एवमुक्त्वा तु सौमित्रिम्
सुमन्त्रमपि राघवः ।
अप्रमत्तस्त्वमश्वेषु
भव सौम्येत्युवाच ह ॥
Having said this to the son of Sumitrā, Rāghava told Sumantra, "O gentle one, please look to the horses carefully!"
2.46.12
सोऽश्वान्सुमन्त्रः संयम्य
सूर्येऽस्तं समुपागते ।
प्रभूतयवसान् कृत्वा
बभूव प्रत्यनन्तरः ॥
When the sun set, Sumantra tethered the horses, gave them plenty of fodder and returned.
2.46.13
उपास्य तु शिवां सन्ध्याम्
दृष्ट्वा रात्रिमुपस्थिताम् ।
रामस्य शयनं चक्रे
सूतः सौमित्रिणा सह ॥
Sumantra then paid salutations to the auspicious dusk. As the night approached, he made the bed for Rāghava with the help of Lakshmaṇa.
2.46.14
तां शय्यां तमसातीरे
वीक्ष्य वृक्षदलैः कृताम् ।
रामः सौमित्रिणा सार्धम्
सभार्यस्संविवेश ह ॥
Seeing that bed of leaves on the bank of river Tamasā ready, Rāma lay down on it along with his wife. Lakshmaṇa also lay down.
2.46.15
सभार्यं सम्प्रसुप्तं तम्
भ्रातरं वीक्ष्य लक्ष्मणः ।
कथयामास सूताय
रामस्य विविधान् गुणान् ॥
Lakshmaṇa, in admiration, looked at his brother who was asleep with his wife. Speaking to the charioteer, he reflected on the exceptional virtues of Rāma.
2.46.16
जाग्रतो ह्येव तां रात्रिम्
सौमित्रेरुदितो रविः ।
सूतस्य तमसातीरे
रामस्य ब्रुवतो गुणान् ॥
Thus, speaking to the charioteer about the virtues of Rāma on those banks of river Tamasā, that son of Sumitrā ended up staying awake all night and the sun rose meanwhile.
2.46.17
गोकुलाकुलतीरायाः
तमसाया विदूरतः ।
अवसत्तत्र तां रात्रिम्
रामः प्रकृतिभिस्सह ॥
Thus Rāma spent the night along with all the people, not far from river Tamasā, whose banks were crowded with herds of cows.
2.46.18
उत्थाय स महातेजाः
प्रकृतीस्ता निशाम्य च ।
अब्रवीद्भ्रातरं रामो
लक्ष्मणं पुण्यलक्षणम् ॥
Getting up and looking at those people, that man of great radiance said to his brother Lakshmaṇa of pleasing appearance:
2.46.19
अस्मद्व्यपेक्षान् सौमित्रे
निरपेक्षान् गृहेष्वपि ।
वृक्षमूलेषु संसुप्तान्
पश्य लक्ष्मण साम्प्रतम् ॥
O son of Sumitrā, look at these people! With love towards us that surpasses their love for their own homes, they are sleeping under the trees now!
2.46.20
यथैते नियमं पौराः
कुर्वन्त्यस्मन्निवर्तने ।
अपि प्राणान्न्यसिष्यन्ति
न तु त्यक्ष्यन्ति निश्चयम् ॥
These citizens are determined in coaxing us to return. They seem to be ready to give up even their lives than giving up their intent.
2.46.21
यावदेव तु संसुप्ताः
तावदेव वयं लघु ।
रथमारुह्य गच्छामः
पन्थानमकुतोभयम् ॥
So, while they are sound asleep, let us quickly get into the chariot and go on our way along the road, with no further worries.
2.46.22
अतो भूयोऽपि नेदानीम्
इक्ष्वाकुपुरवासिनः ।
स्वपेयुरनुरक्ता माम्
वृक्षमूलानि संश्रिताः ॥
Then the inhabitants of the city of Ikshwākus will not have to sleep again under the trees like this because of their love for me.
2.46.23
पौरा ह्यात्मकृताद्दुःखात्
विप्रमोच्या नृपात्मजैः ।
न तु खल्वात्मना योज्या
दुःखेन पुरवासिनः ॥
As the sons of the king, we should save citizens from hardships that they might bring upon themselves, and not be the ones to bring hardships upon them!
2.46.24
अब्रवील्लक्ष्मणो रामम्
साक्षाद्धर्ममिवस्थितम् ।
रोचते मे तथा प्राज्ञ
क्षिप्र मारुह्यतामिति ॥
Lakshmaṇa answered Rāma, who was like Dharma incarnate, "I like that idea, O wise one, let us get in quick!"
2.46.25
अथ रामोऽब्रवीच्छ्रीमान्
सुमन्त्रं युज्यतां रथः ।
गमिष्यामि ततोऽरण्यम्
गच्छ शीघ्रमितः प्रभो ॥
Then the majestic and gracious Rāma said to Sumantra: O lord, make the chariot ready! I shall go to the Vana. Start from here immediately!
2.46.26
सूतस्ततस्सत्त्वरितः
स्यन्दनं तैर्हयोत्तमैः ।
योजयित्व्राऽथ रामाय
प्राञ्जलिः प्रत्यवेदयत् ॥
The charioteer then hastened and yoked those exquisite horses. He said to Rāma with palms joined in reverence:
2.46.27
अयं युक्तो महाबाहो
रथस्ते रथिनां वर ।
त्वमारोहस्व भद्रं ते
ससीत स्सहलक्ष्मणः ॥
O you of mighty arm! O foremost among the charioters! The chariot is ready. May you get in quick along with Seetā and Lakshmaṇa! May all bode well for you!
Ratha (रथ) means chariot.
Rathi (रथि) means one who owns a chariot and/or rides on a chariot and especially adept in fighting a battle mounted on a chariot, as opposed to on a horse or elephant.
Sarathi (सारथि) means one who drives a chariot.
The image of Arjuna riding on the chariot and Krishna driving it in the war of Mahabharata is familiar to many of us. In that picture, we see a Ratha, a Rathi and a Sarathi.
The word Rathi (रथि) is used very often in traditional Indian texts. But unfortunately, there is no equivalent of it in English. So I made up the word charioter (note the singe 'e') as equivalent of Rathi (रथि).
2.46.28
तं स्यन्दनमधिष्ठाय
राघवस्सपरिच्छदः ।
शीघ्रगामाकुलावर्ताम्
तमसामतरन्नदीम् ॥
Taking his seat in the chariot, having first had all his goods stowed away in it, Rāghava crossed the river Tamasā with its swift and swirling currents.
2.46.29
स सन्तीर्य महाबाहुः
श्रीमान् शिवमकण्टकम् ।
प्रापद्यत महामार्गम्
अभयं भयदर्शिनाम् ॥
Crossing it, the blessed one of mighty arm took the fine highway which was free from dangers, where even the timorous felt safe.
2.46.30
मोहनार्थं तु पौराणाम्
सूतं रामोऽब्रवीद्वचः ।
उदङ्मुखः प्रयाहि त्वम्
रथमास्थाय सारथे ।
मुहूर्तं त्वरितं गत्वा
निवर्तय रथं पुनः ।
यथा न विद्युः पौरा माम्
तथा कुरु समाहितः ॥
Rāma, in order to hoodwink the citizens (to not follow him) gave these instructions to the charioteer: O Charioteer! Take your seat and drive to the north. Drive quick and far enough and then stop, so that the citizens would not know where I am. Do it with care and diligence.
Ṡlōkas 30 to 34 seem to be an alternate (and not so well written) version of Ṡlōkas 21 to 29.
Alternates like these in a text that is as old as Rāmāyaṇa are to be expected to creep in.
However, it is rare to see two versions preserved next to each other like this, in a well compiled edition.
If it were left to me, I would have, without any heSeetātion, purged Ṡlōkas 30 to 34 from the compilation.
2.46.32
रामस्य वचनं श्रुत्वा
तथा चक्रे स सारथिः ।
प्रत्यागम्य च रामस्य
स्यन्दनं प्रत्यवेदयत् ॥
The charioteer heard Rāma and after acting accordingly, came back to inform Rāma that the chariot is ready.
2.46.33
तौ सम्प्रयुक्तं तु रथं समास्थितौ
तदा ससीतौ रघुवंशवर्धनौ ।
प्रचोदयामास ततस्तुरङ्गमान्
स सारथिर्येन पथा तपोवनम् ॥
When the two illustrious scions of the race of Raghu had settled themselves in the chariot, along with Seetā, the charioteer drove the horses forward along the road that led to the Vana where people perform Tapa.
2.46.34
तत स्समास्थाय रथं महारथः
ससारथिर्दाशरथिर्वनं ययौ ।
उदङ्मुखं तं तु रथं चकार स
प्रयाणमाङ्गल्य निमित्तदर्शनात् ॥
As it augurs well to travel in the northerly direction, Sumantra turned the chariot in that direction. The son of Daṡaratha, charioter paramount, thus mounted the chariot driven by the charioteer and headed to the Vana.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे षट्चत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty-sixth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
