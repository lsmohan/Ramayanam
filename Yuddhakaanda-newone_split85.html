Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 85
In this Sarga, Vibheeshaṇa reiterates to Rāma that the killing of Seetā is nothing more than a trick by Indrajit. He asks Rāma to send Lakshmaṇa to slay Indrajit, before he completes the Yajña at Nikumbhila. Rāma instructs Lakshmaṇa to take all the forces with him and fight with Indrajit, assisted by Hanumān and Jāmbavān. Lakshmaṇa accordingly goes to Nikumbhila along with Hanumān, Jāmbavān, Neela and Vibheeshaṇa and the forces.
6.85.1
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राघवः शोककर्शितः ।
नोपधारयते व्यक्तम्
यदुक्तं तेन रक्षसा ॥
Rāghava heard what the Rākshasa said, but could not clearly make out what it was, as he was too dazed and overwrought with grief.
6.85.2
ततो धैर्यमवष्टभ्य
रामः परपुरञ्जयः ।
विभीषणमुपासीनम्
उवाच कपिसन्निधौ ॥
But gathering himself as much as he could, Rāma, conqueror of enemy cities, said this to Vibheeshaṇa who was by his side, in the presence of the Vānaras:
6.85.3
नैर्ऋताधिपते वाक्यम्
यदुक्तं ते विभीषण ।
भूयस्तच्छ्रोतुमिच्छामि
ब्रूहि यत्ते विवक्षितम् ॥
O Vibheeshaṇa, O Lord of Rākshasas! I would like to hear again what you have said. Please tell me what you meant to say.
6.85.4
राघवस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
सुदीनवदुदाहृतम् ।
यत्नात्पुनरिदं वाक्यम्
बभाषे स विभीषणः ॥
Hearing those words of Rāghava that were spoken in a state of despondency, Vibheeshaṇa, who was skilled in speech, repeated once again emphatically what he had already said.
6.85.5
यथाज्ञप्तं महाबाहो
त्वया गुल्मनिवेशनम् ।
तत्तथाऽनुष्ठितं वीर
त्वद्वाक्यसमनन्तरम् ॥
The army divisions were deployed at all the appropriate points just as you ordered, O you of mighty arm! Everything was done according to your instructions without any delay, O Veera!
6.85.6
तान्यनीकानि सर्वाणि
विभक्तानि समन्ततः ।
विन्यस्ता यूथपाश्चैव
यथान्यायं विभागशः ॥
All the fighting forces were divided appropriately into various divisions and were deployed everywhere, each under its own commanding officer.
6.85.7
भूयस्तु मम विज्ञाप्यम्
तच्छृणुष्व महाप्रभो ॥
I have more to submit to you, please listen, O mighty lord!
6.85.7
त्वय्यकारणसन्तप्ते
सन्तप्तहृदया वयम् ।
त्यज राजन्निमं शोकम्
मिथ्या सन्तापमागतम् ।
तदियं त्यज्यतां चिन्ता
शत्रुहर्षविवर्धनी ॥
Our hearts burn when you grieve for no good reason. O King! Give up this grief that resulted from a false calamity. This misery that only makes the enemy happy should be abjured by you.
6.85.9
उद्यमः क्रियतां वीर
हर्षः समुपसेव्यताम् ।
प्राप्तव्या यदि ते सीता
हन्तव्याश्च निशाचराः ॥
You must become cheerful and put forth a good effort, O Veera, if Seetā is to be recovered and the Rākshasas are to be killed.
6.85.10
रघुनन्दन वक्ष्यामि
श्रूयतां मे हितं वचः ।
साध्वयं यातु सौमित्रिः
बलेन महता वृतः ।
निकुम्भिलायां सम्प्राप्य
हन्तुं रावणिमाहवे ।
धनुर्मण्डलनिर्मुक्तैः
आशीविषविषोपमैः ॥
O delight of Raghus! Please listen, I shall tell you what will serve your interest better. Sowmitri shall proceed, taking a great force with him, to reach Nikumbhila and slay the son of Rāvaṇa in fight, with arrows that resemble venomous serpents, let off from his curved bow.
6.85.12
तेन वीर्येण तपसा
वरदानात् स्वयम्भुवः ।
अस्त्रं ब्रह्मशिरः प्राप्तम्
कामगाश्च तुरङ्गमाः ॥
He, the valiant one, gained the Astra called Brahmaṡira and horses that can go anywhere as commanded, by virtue of his Tapa and the boons he obtained from the Self-born.
6.85.13
स एष सह सैन्येन
प्राप्तः किल निकुम्भिलाम् ।
यद्युत्तिष्ठेत् कृतं कर्म
हतान् सर्वांश्च विद्धि नः ॥
It seems that he is already at Nikumbhila along with the army. Should he emerge out of it completing the rite, take it from me that we are all dead.
6.85.14
निकुम्भिलामसम्प्राप्तम्
अहुताग्निं च यो रिपुः ।
त्वामाततायिनं हन्यात्
इन्द्रशत्रोः स ते वधः ।
वरो दत्तो महाबाहो
सर्वलोकेश्वरेण वै ।
इत्येवं विहितो राजन्
वधस्तस्यैष धीमतः ॥
O you of mighty arm! The lord of all worlds has given him the boon saying, "O foe of Indra! Your death is at the hands of the foe who intercepts you while you are well armed, before you reach Nikumbhila and complete the oblations in the fire, and strikes you!" This is the kind of death that is ordained for that sagacious one, O King!
6.85.16
वधायेन्द्रजितो राम
सन्दिशस्व महाबल ।
हते तस्मिन् हतं विद्धि
रावणं ससुहृज्जनम् ॥
O Rāma! O you of immense strength! Give the instructions for slaying Indrajit. Once he is slain, know that all well-wishers of Rāvaṇa are slain.
6.85.17
विभीषणवचः श्रुत्वा
राघवो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Hearing those words of Vibheeshaṇa, Rāghava said these words:
6.85.17
जानामि तस्य रौद्रस्य
मायां सत्यपराक्रम ।
स हि ब्रह्मास्त्रवित् प्राज्ञो
महामायो महाबलः ।
करोत्यसञ्ज्ञान् सङ्ग्रामे
देवान् सवरुणानपि ॥
O you of true prowess! Yes, I am aware of his magic tricks and the power of his wrath. He knows the Astra of Brahma. He is well aware of everything. He is a great trickster and possesses immense strength. He can make the Dēvas including Varuṇa fall into a faint in a battle.
6.85.19
तस्यान्तरिक्षे चरतो
सरथस्य महायशः ।
न गतिर्ज्ञायते तस्य
सूर्यस्येवाभ्रसम्प्लवे ॥
Once he is sky-bound in his chariot, his location cannot be detected, O you of great renown, any more than that of the sun when hidden behind masses of clouds.
6.85.20
राघवस्तु रिपोर्ज्ञात्व
मायावीर्यं दुरात्मनः ।
लक्ष्मणं कीर्तिसम्पन्नम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Rāghava, completely aware of the valor of the evil-minded foe, which was derived from black magic, said these words to Lakshmaṇa of great renown.
6.85.21
यद्वानरेन्द्रस्य बलम्
तेन सर्वेण संवृतः ।
हनुमत्प्रमुखैश्चैव
यूथपैः सह लक्ष्मण ।
जाम्बवेनर्क्षपतिना
सहसैन्येन संवृतः ।
जहि तं राक्षससुतम्
मायाबलविशारदम् ॥
Supported by all the forces of the Lord of the Vānaras, assisted by Vānara captains like Hanumān, and by Jāmbavān, the lord of Ṛkshas, and his forces, kill that son of Rākshasa, who derives his strength from black magic.
6.85.23
अयं त्वां सचिवैः सार्द्धम्
महात्मा रजनीचरः ।
अभिज्ञस्तस्य मायानाम्
पृष्ठतोऽनुगमिष्यति ॥
And this Night-rover, who is aware of his tricks, will march behind you, along with his counselors.
6.85.24
राघवस्य वचः श्रुत्वा
लक्ष्मणः सविभीषणः ।
जाग्राह कार्मुकश्रेष्ठम्
अत्यद्भुतपराक्रमः ॥
Lakshmaṇa of amazing prowess armed himself with a great bow on hearing those words of Rāghava along with Vibheeshaṇa.
6.85.25
सन्नद्धः कवची खड्गी
सशरो हेमचापधृत् ।
रामपादावुपस्पृश्य
हृष्टः सौमित्रिरब्रवीत् ॥
Donning a coat of mail and arming himself with sword, bow embellished with gold and arrows, Sowmitri, in high spirits, touched Rāma’s feet, and said:
6.85.26
अद्य मत्कार्मुकोन्मुक्ताः
शरा निर्भिद्य रावणिम् ।
लङ्कामभिपतिष्यन्ति
हंसाः पुष्करिणीमिव ॥
Today, the arrows shot from my bow shall pierce through Rāvaṇa's son, and fall in Laṅkā, like swans landing on a lotus-pool.
6.85.27
अद्यैव तस्य रौद्रस्य
शरीरं मामकाः शराः ।
विधमिष्यन्ति भित्त्वा तम्
महाचापगुणच्युताः ॥
This very day, my arrows, leaving the string of the great bow, shall cut to pieces the body of that violent one.
6.85.28
स एवमुक्त्वा द्युतिमान्
वचनं भ्रातुरग्रतः ।
स रावणिवधाकाङ्क्षी
लक्ष्मणस्त्वरितो ययौ ॥
Having said this in his brother’s presence, Lakshmaṇa, glowing with splendor, departed swiftly, eager to slay Rāvaṇa’s son.
6.85.29
सोऽभिवाद्य गुरोः पादौ
कृत्वा चापि प्रदक्षिणम् ।
निकुम्भिलामभिययौ
चैत्यं रावणिपालितम् ॥
Making his prostrations at his elder brother’s feet and reverently going round him, he headed out to Nikumbhila, the shrine under the protection of Rāvaṇa’s son.
6.85.30
विभीषणेन सहितो
राजपुत्रः प्रतापवान् ।
कृतस्वस्त्ययनो भ्रात्रा
लक्ष्मणस्त्वरितो ययौ ॥
Lakshmaṇa, the prince of tormenting valor received the blessings of his brother and proceeded swiftly along with Vibheeshaṇa.
6.85.31
वानराणां सहस्रैस्तु
हनुमान् बहुभिर्वृतः ।
विभीषणश्च सामात्यः
तदा लक्ष्मणमन्वगात् ॥
Then, Hanumān with thousands of Vānaras around him, and Vibheeshaṇa along with his counselors followed Lakshmaṇa.
6.85.32
महता हरिसैन्येन
सवेगमभिसंवृतः ।
ऋक्षराजबलं चैव
ददर्श पथि विष्ठितम् ॥
As he proceeded swiftly with the vast Vānara army following him, he saw the army of the king of Ṛkshas already on the way.
6.85.33
स गत्वा दूरमध्वानम्
सौमित्रिर्मित्रनन्दनः ।
राक्षसेन्द्रबलं दूरात्
अपश्यद्व्यूहमास्थितम् ॥
After marching for a while, Sowmitri, in whom his friends rejoiced, saw from afar the forces of the Rākshasa King in battle formation.
6.85.34
स तं प्राप्य धनुष्पाणिः
मायायोगमरिन्दमः ।
तस्थौ ब्रह्मविधानेन
विजेतुं रघुनन्दनः ।
विभीषणेन सहितो
राजपुत्रः प्रतापवान् ।
अङ्गदेन च वीरेण
तथाऽनिलसुतेन च ॥
That prince of tormenting valor, delight of Raghus and subduer of foe, along with Vibheeshaṇa, Aṅgada the Veera and along with the son of Vāyu, reached him, the one with magic powers, and took a stand with bow in his hand, to win over him as ordained by Brahma.
6.85.36
विविधममलशस्त्रभास्वरं तत्
ध्वजगहनं विपुलं महारथैश्च ।
प्रतिभयतममप्रमेयवेगम्
तिमिरमिव द्विषतां बलं विवेश ॥
He entered the enemy forces that were of immeasurable speed, that shone with the splendor of a variety of gleaming weapons, that were difficult to penetrate dense with great chariots and their flag posts, and that stirred extreme fear, like darkness does.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे पञ्चाशीतितमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the eighty fifth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
