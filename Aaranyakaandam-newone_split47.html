Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 47
There are three parts in this Sarga.
First, Seetā, in response to the inquiry of Rāvaṇa, from the previous Sarga, tells about herself, her husband and how he was exiled from the kingdom by his father in order to keep the word he had given to his wife Kaikēyee.
Then she asks about the details of the guest, Rāvaṇa disguised as a Brāhmaṇa. Then Rāvaṇa reveals who he really is and how infatuated he is with her. He asks her to become the chief among his queens and enjoy life along with him in Laṅkā, a great city built on top of a mountain in an island.
Then Seetā lambasts Rāvaṇa for daring to covet her. She shows her contempt for him and tells him that there is that kind of a difference between him and Rāma that exists between a jackal and a lion. Coveting a lioness like her, the wife of Rāma, she warns him, is like trying to capture fire in a piece cloth.
3.47.1
रावणेन तु वैदेही
तदा पृष्टा जिहीर्षुणा  ।
परिव्राजकरूपेण
शशंसात्मानमात्मना ॥
Thus asked by Rāvaṇa who was there to abduct her, albeit in the form of a Parivrājaka, she told all about herself.
3.47.2
ब्राह्मणश्चातिथिश्चायम्
अनुक्तो हि शपेत माम् ।
इति ध्यात्वा मुहूर्तं तु
सीता वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Keeping in mind that the guest was a Brāhmaṇa who might curse her if she spoke anything inappropriate, Seetā said the following words:
3.47.3
दुहिता जनकस्याहम्
मैथिलस्य महात्मनः ।
सीता नाम्नास्मि भद्रं ते
रामभार्या द्विजोत्तम ॥
O eminent Brāhmaṇa! May all bode well for you! I am the daughter of Mahātma Janaka of Mithilā. My name is Seetā. I am the wife of Rāma.
3.47.4
उषित्वा द्वादश समा
इक्ष्वाकुणां निवेशने ।
भुञ्जना मानुषान्भोगान्
सर्वकामसमृद्धिनी ॥
I stayed for twelve years in the house of Ikshwākus, enjoying all luxuries a human can afford, in abundance.
3.47.5
ततस्त्रयोदशे वर्षे
राजाऽमन्त्रयत प्रभुः ।
अभिषेचयितुं रामम्
समेतो राजमन्त्रिभिः ॥
Then, in the thirteenth year, the King, the lord, along with all the royal ministers thought of consecrating Rāma to the throne.
3.47.6
तस्मिन्सम्भ्रियमाणे तु
राघवस्याभिषेचने ।
कैकेयी नाम भर्तारम्
आर्या सा याचते वरम् ॥
When the preparations were afoot for the coronation of Rāghava, the noble lady by name Kaikēyee asked her husband for a boon.
3.47.7
प्रतिगृह्य तु कैकेयी
श्वशुरं सुकृतेन मे ।
मम प्रव्राजनं भर्तुः
भरतस्याभिषेचनम् ।
द्वावयाचत भर्तारम्
सत्यसन्धं नृपोत्तमम् ॥
Kaikēyee, as a reward for the favor she had done earlier, asked her husband, the best of the kings, the one who always kept his word, two boons: to send my husband in exile and to consecrate Bharata to the throne.
3.47.8
नाद्य भोक्ष्ये न च स्वप्स्ये
न पास्येच कथञ्चन ।
एष मे जीवितस्यान्तो
रामो यद्यभिषिच्यते ॥
‘I am, in no way, going to eat, drink or sleep and will end my life, if Rāma is consecrated!’
3.47.9
इति ब्रुवाणां कैकेयीम्
श्वशुरो मे स मानदः ।
अयाचतार्थैरन्वर्थैः
न च याञ्चां चकार सा ॥
When Kaikēyee asked thus, my father-in-law who treated everyone with respect, begged hard of her, promising many a desirable thing, but she would not accede to his request.
3.47.10
मम भर्तामहातेजा
वयसा पञ्चविंशकः ।
अष्टादश हि वर्षाणि
मम जन्मनि गण्यते ॥
My supremely powerful husband at that time, was twenty five years old and eighteen years had passed since my birth.
3.47.11
रामेति प्रथितो लोके
गुणवान्सत्यवान्शुचिः ।
विशालाक्षो महाबाहुः
सर्वभूतहिते रतः ॥
Rāma of mighty arm and wide eyes is well known in the world as a man of many merits and as a man of his word, clean in every sense and interested in the well being of everyone.
3.47.12
कामार्तस्तु महातेजाः
पिता दशरथस्स्वयम् ।
कैकेय्याः प्रियकामार्थम्
तं रामं नाभ्यषेचयत् ॥
His father Daṡaratha of mighty power, taken over by his love for Kaikēyee, did not consecrate Rāma, to fulfill her dear wish.
3.47.13
अभिषेकाय तु पितुः
समीपं राममागतम् ।
कैकेयी मम भर्तारम्
इत्युवाच धृतं वचः ॥
To my husband, who went to take the audience of his father for being consecrated, Kaikēyee spoke these words in no uncertain terms:
3.47.14
तव पित्रा समाज्ञप्तम्
ममेदं शृणु राघव ।
भरताय प्रदातव्यम्
इदं राज्यमकण्टकम् ॥
O Rāghava! Listen! By the orders of Your father, this kingdom must be handed over to Bharata, without (causing) any trouble!
3.47.15
त्वया हि खलु वस्तव्यम्
नव वर्षाणि पञ्च च ।
वने प्रव्रज काकुत्स्थ
पितरं मोचयानृतान् ॥
O Kākutstha! You have to go in exile and stay in the Vana for nine and five years to save your father from the imputation of untruth!
3.47.16
तथेत्युक्त्वा च तां रामः
कैकेयीमकुतोभयः ।
चकार तद्वचस्तस्या
मम भर्ता दृढव्रतः ॥
Rāma, my husband who is not afraid of anything and is faithful to his creed, said ‘Yes’ and did in accordance with her word.
3.47.17
दद्यान्न प्रतिगृह्णीयात्
सत्यं ब्रूयान्न चानृतम् ।
एतद्ब्राह्मण रामस्य
ध्रृवं व्रतमनुत्तमम् ॥
O Brāhmaṇa, Rāma, at all costs, follows his lofty creed of being at the giving, but not the receiving end, and speaking the truth and not a lie.
3.47.18
तस्य भ्राता तु द्वैमात्रो
लक्ष्मणो नाम वीर्यवान् ।
रामस्य पुरुषव्याघ्रः
सहायस्समरेऽरिहा ॥
His step brother by name Lakshmaṇa, a valiant tiger among men, is his companion in a fight.
3.47.19
स भ्राता लक्ष्मणो नाम
धर्मचारी दृढव्रतः ।
अन्वगच्छद्दनुष्पाणिः
प्रव्रजन्तं मया सह ॥
That brother whose name is Lakshmaṇa and who adheres to Dharma and is resolute in his purpose, followed him, bow in hand, into exile, along with me.
3.47.20
जटी तापसरूपेण
मया सह सहानुजः ।
प्रविष्टो दण्डकारण्यम्
धर्मनित्यो जितेन्द्रियः ॥
He who always keeps to Dharma and has mastered his senses, came to Daṇḍaka Araṇya wearing matted locks and adopting the form of a Tapasvi, along with me and his brother.
3.47.21
ते वयं प्रच्युता राज्यात्
कैकेय्यास्तु कृते त्रयः ।
विचरामो द्विजश्रेष्ठ
वनं गम्भीरमोजसा ॥
O great Brāhmaṇa! Thus, because of Kaikēyee, castoff from the kingdom, we three are biding our time in this deep Vana, relying on our own abilities.
3.47.22
समाश्वस मुहूर्तं तु
शक्यं वस्तुमिह त्वया ।
आगमिष्यति मे भर्ता
वन्यमादाय पुष्कलम् ।
रुरून्गोधा न्वराहांश्च
हत्वाऽदायाऽमिषान्बहून् ॥
Take rest for a moment; you should stay here. My husband will come, bringing with him a variety of wild meats from his kill such as that of black striped deer, mongoose and boar.
3.47.24
स त्वं नाम च गोत्रञ्च
कुलं चाचक्ष्व तत्त्वतः ।
एकश्च दण्डकारण्ये
किमर्थं चरसि द्विज ॥
O Brāhmaṇa! What is your name? And what is your Kula and Gōtra? Why are you in Daṇḍaka Araṇya, all by yourself?
Now a days we use the word Kula synonymous with caste. It actually means lineage.
3.47.25
एवं बृवन्त्यां सीतायाम्
रामपत्न्यां महाबलः ।
प्रत्युवाचोत्तरं तीव्रम्
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ॥
To Seetā, wife of Rāma, who asked thus, Rāvaṇa, the mighty strong lord of Rākshasas replied in a blunt tone:
3.47.26
येन वित्रासिता लोकाः
सदेवासुरपन्नगाः ।
अहं तु रावणो नाम
सीते रक्षोगणेश्वरः ॥
O Seetā! I am the lord of the bands of Rākshasas! My name is Rāvaṇa, of whom all the worlds including Dēvas, Asuras and Pannagas, are terrified!
3.47.27
त्वां तु काञ्चनवर्णाभाम्
दृष्ट्वा कौशेयवासिनीम् ।
रतिं स्वकेषु दारेषु
नाधिगच्छाम्यनिन्दिते ॥
O blameless one! Having seen you, of golden complexion dressed in yellow silks, I am no more able to find pleasure in my wives!
3.47.28
बह्वीनामुत्तमस्त्रीणाम्
आहृतानामितस्ततः ।
सर्वासामेव भद्रं ते
ममाग्रमहिषी भव ॥
Be the chief queen among my many wives, the best of the women I have brought from everywhere! May all bode well for you!
3.47.29
लङ्कानाम समुद्रस्य
मम मध्ये महापुरी ।
सागरेण परिक्षिस्ता
निविष्टा नगमूर्धनि ॥
The great city by name Laṅkā that is in the middle of the ocean, is mine. It is surrounded by the ocean and is situated on top of a mountain.
3.47.30
तत्र सीते मया सार्धम्
वनेषु विहरिष्यसि ।
न चास्यारण्य वासस्य
स्पृहयिष्यसि भामिनि ॥
O Seetā! O lovely lady! You can enjoy the gardens there along with me. You will completely forget all about your stay in the Araṇya.
3.47.31
पञ्च दास्यस्सहस्राणि
सर्वाभरणभूषिताः ।
सीते परिचरिष्यन्ति
भार्या भवसि मे यदि ॥
O Seetā! Once you become my wife, you will be waited upon by five thousand maids who are decked with every kind of jewel.
3.47.32
रावणेनैवमुक्ता तु
कुपिता जनकात्मजा ।
प्रत्युवाचानवद्याङ्गी
तमनादृत्य राक्षसम् ॥
When Rāvaṇa said this, the daughter of Janaka, of perfect figure, became angry and replied to that Rākshasa in all contempt:
3.47.33
महागिरिमिवाकम्प्यम्
महेन्द्रसदृशं पतिम् ।
महोदधिमिवाक्षोभ्यम्
अहं राममनुव्रता ॥
I am devoted to my husband Rāma, who is comparable to the great Indra, who can be moved no more than a great mountain can be and who can be shaken no more than a great ocean can be!
Till now, we have seen the soft side of Seetā, in imploring Rāma to take her along with him to the Vana (Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa, Sargas 27 and 29), in imploring him not to get on the wrong side of the Rākshasas (Araṇya Kāṇḍa, Sarga 9) and in asking him to get the golden deer (Araṇya Kāṇḍa, Sarga 43).
Starting now, we will see the lioness in her. All women of character have both these sides in them and they show up when the occasion calls for them.
Her reaction upon hearing ‘Ha Seetā and Ha Lakshmaṇa!’ was panic and worry. But, not after much time has passed, when accosted by the lord of all the Rākshasas, Rāvaṇa himself, she shows no fear. She, indeed, roars like a lioness here.
From now on, we see in her only the sense of regret for the disaster she brought upon herself, Rāma and others, and her grief over the separation from Rāma.
3.47.34
सर्वलक्षणसम्पन्नम्
न्यग्रोधपरिमण्डलम् ।
सत्यसन्धं महाभागम्
अहं राममनुव्रता ॥
I am devoted to my greatly blessed husband Rāma, who always cleaves to truth, who bears all the marks of human excellence, and whose expanse rivals that of a banyan tree.
3.47.35
महाबाहुं महोरस्कम्
सिंहविक्रान्तगामिनम् ।
नृसिंहं सिंहसङ्काशम्
अहं राममनुव्रता ॥
I am devoted to my husband Rāma of mighty arm, mighty chest, who is a lion among men, who rivals a lion and has the imposing gait of a lion.
3.47.36
पूर्णचन्द्राननं रामम्
राजवत्सं जितेन्द्रियम् ।
पृथुकीर्तिं महात्मानम्
अहं राममनुव्रता ॥
I am devoted to the enchanting Rāma of full moon-like face, a royal son, a Mahātma of splendid renown, who has mastered his senses.
3.47.37
त्वं पुनर्जम्बुकस्सिंहीम्
मामिच्छसि सुदुर्लभाम् ।
नाहं शक्या त्वया स्प्रष्टुम्
आदित्यस्य प्रभा यथा ॥
And you, a jackal that you are, dare covet me, a lioness, who is completely beyond your reach. You can no more hope to touch me than one can the glowing orb of the sun.
3.47.38
पादपान्काञ्चनान्नूनम्
बहून्पश्यसि मन्दभाक् ।
राघवस्य प्रियां भार्याम्
यस्त्वमिच्छसि रावण ॥
O Rāvaṇa! You certainly are seeing many golden trees, with your end nearing, by coveting the dear wife of Rāghava!
There is a saying from olden times, that people see golden trees in their vision when they are about to die.
3.47.39
क्षुधितस्य हि सिंहस्य
मृगशत्रोस्तरस्विनः ।
आशीविषस्य वदनात्
दंष्ट्रामादातुमिच्छसि ॥
You are wishing to pick the fang of a hungry and swift lion that prowls on deer from its mouth (which is open) like that of a venomous serpent.
3.47.40
मन्दरं पर्वतश्रेष्ठम्
पाणिना हर्तुमिच्छसि ।
कालकूटं विषं पीत्वा
स्वस्तिमान्गन्तुमिच्छसि ॥
You are trying to pluck, by your hand, Mandara, the giant of mountains. You want to remain safe even after drinking deadly poison.
3.47.41
अक्षि सूच्या प्रमृजसि
जिह्वाया लेक्षि च क्षुरम् ।
राघवस्य प्रियां भार्याम्
योऽधिगन्तुं त्वमिच्छसि ॥
By trying to cross with the dear wife of Rāghava, you are gouging out your eye with a needle and licking the razor’s sharp edge with your tongue.
3.47.42
अवसज्य शिलां कण्ठे
समुद्रं तर्तुमिच्छसि ।
सूर्याचन्द्रमसौ चोभौ
पाणिभ्यां हर्तुमिच्छसि ।
यो रामस्य प्रियां भार्याम्
प्रधर्षयितुमिच्छसि ॥
By trying to slight the dear wife of Rāghava, you are trying to cross a sea with a boulder tied to your neck and trying to capture the sun and the moon with your two bare hands.
3.47.43
अग्निं प्रज्वलितं दृष्ट्वा
वस्त्रेणाहर्तुमिच्छसि ।
काल्याणवृत्तां रामस्य
यो भार्यांहर्तुमिच्छसि ॥
By trying to take the virtuous wife of Rāma, you are trying to hold, with a cloth, a blazing fire that you happen to see.
3.47.44
अयोमुखानां शूलानाम्
अग्रे चरितुमिच्छसि ।
रामस्य सदृशीं भार्याम्
योऽधिगन्तुं त्वमिच्छसि ॥
By trying to cross with someone like the wife of Rāma, you are trying to walk on the iron spikes of a spear.
3.47.45
यदन्तरं सिंहाशृगालयोर्वने
यदन्तरं स्यन्दिनिका समुद्रयोः ।
सुराग्र्य सौवीरकयोर्यदन्तरं
तदन्तरं वै तव राघवस्य च ॥
There is that kind of difference between you and Rāghava, that there is between a jackal and a lion, between a rivulet and an ocean and between sour gruel and exquisite wine.
3.47.46
यदन्तरं काञ्चनसीसलोहयो-
र्यदन्तरं चन्दनवारिपङ्कयोः ।
यदन्तरं हस्तिबिडालयोर्वने
तदन्तरं दाशरथेस्तवैव च ॥
There is that kind of difference between you and the son of Daṡaratha, that there is between lead and gold, between mire and sandal paste and between a house cat and a wild tusker.
3.47.47
यदन्तरं वायसवैनतेययो-
र्यदन्तरं मद्गुमयूरयोरपि ।
यदन्तरं सारसगृध्रयोर्वने
तदन्तरं दाशरथेस्तवैव च ॥
There is that kind of difference between you and the son of Daṡaratha, that there is between a crow and Garuḍa, between a cormorant and a peacock, and between a vulture and a Sārasa bird in the Vana.
3.47.48
तस्मिन्सहस्राक्षसमप्रभावे
रामे स्थिते कार्मुकबाणपाणौ ।
हृतापि तेऽहं न जरां गमिष्ये
वज्रं यथा मक्षिकयाऽवगीर्णम् ॥
With Rāma, whose valor is comparable to that of the thousand-eyed, standing (firm and tall) holding the bow and arrows in his hands, you cannot digest me, like a gem swallowed by a fly, even if you succeed in abducting me.
3.47.49
इतीव तद्वाक्यमदुष्टभावा
सुदुष्टमुक्त्वा रजनीचरं तम् ।
गात्रप्रकम्पाद्व्यथिता बभूव
वातोद्धता सा कदलीव तन्वी ॥
Having thus spoken to that evil Night-rover, she, who was far removed from any evil, being the delicate one that she was, her body shaking like a banana plant in a violent wind, was taken over by distress.
3.47.50
तां वेपमानामुपलक्ष्य सीतां
स रावणो मृत्युसमप्रभावः ।
कुलं बलं नाम च कर्म चात्मनः
समाचचक्षे भयकारणार्थम् ॥
Seeing Seetā thus shaken, Rāvaṇa, who was as dreadful as death, recounted his name and lineage, his power and his (terrible) deeds in detail, to intimidate her:
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे सप्तचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty seventh Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
