Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 32
In this Sarga, Sugreeva wonders why Lakshmaṇa is angry while he had not done anything wrong. He wonders whether his enemies had relayed made-up stories about him to Lakshmaṇa. He says that he can never hope to requite Rāma for the favor he has done. He asks his ministers for counsel.
Hanumān then counsels Sugreeva saying that Rāghava cannot be found at fault for being angry, as he sees that Sugreeva is not ready for the expedition, even though the season of Ṡarat has arrived.
Hanumān suggests firmly that Sugreeva should go to Lakshmaṇa and prostrate to him and regain his pleasure.
4.32.1
अङ्गदस्य वचश्श्रुत्वा
सुग्रीवस्सचिवैस्सह ।
लक्ष्मणं कुपितं श्रुत्वा
मुमोचासनमात्मवान् ॥
Sugreeva, a man of integrity, got up from his seat when he, along with his ministers, heard from Aṅgada that Lakshmaṇa was angry.
4.32.2
स च तानब्रवीद्वाक्यम्
निश्चित्य गुरुलाघवम् ।
मन्त्रज्ञान्मन्त्रकुशलो
मन्त्रेषु परिनिष्ठितः  ॥
He, who was skilled in strategy and steady in his deliberations then assessed the good and bad and spoke to them who were adept at strategies:
4.32.3
न मे दुर्व्याहृतं किञ्चित्
नापि मे दुरनुष्ठितम् ।
लक्ष्मणो राघवभ्राता
क्रुद्धः किमिति चिन्तये ॥
Neither did I say nor did I do anything wrong. I wonder why Lakshmaṇa, the brother of Rāghava, is angry.
4.32.4
असुहृद्भिर्ममामित्रैः
नित्यमन्तरदर्शिभिः ।
मम दोषानसम्भूतान्
श्रावितो राघवानुजः ॥
My enemies, always in search of an opportunity, lacking even basic decency, would have relayed made-up stories about me to the younger brother of Rāghava.
4.32.5
अत्र तावद्यथाबुद्धि
सर्वैरेव यथाविधि ।
भावस्य निश्चयस्तावत्
विज्ञेयो निपुणं शनैः ॥
You should decide carefully, to the best of your abilities, the most appropriate thing to be done in this matter.
4.32.6
न खल्वस्ति मम त्रासो
लक्ष्मणान्नापि राघवात् ।
मित्रं त्वस्थानकुपितम्
जनयत्येव सम्भ्रमम् ॥
It is not that I fear Lakshmaṇa, or even Rāghava. But the wrath of a (good) friend, with no ground whatsoever for it, is bound to raise concern, does it not?
4.32.7
सर्वथा सुकरं मित्रम्
दुष्करं परिपालनम् ।
अनित्यत्वात्तु चित्तानाम्
प्रीतिरल्पेऽपि भिद्यते ॥
There are many easy ways to make friends. But it is not so easy to keep them. Human nature being fickle, friendships collapse even for trivial reasons.
4.32.8
अतो निमित्तं त्रस्तोऽहम्
रामेण तु महात्मना ।
यन्मयोपकृतं शक्यम्
प्रतिकर्तुं न तन्मया ॥
This is what I am scared about. I can never hope to requite Mahātma Rāma for the favor he has done for me.
4.32.9
सुग्रीवेणैवमुक्ते तु
हनुमान्हरिपुङ्गवः ।
उवाच स्वेन तर्केण
मध्ये वानरमन्त्रिणाम् ॥
When Sugreeva said this, Hanumān, a bull among Vānaras, put forward his thoughts in the presence of all the ministers:
4.32.10
सर्वथा नैतदाश्चर्यम्
यस्त्वं हरिगणेश्वर ।
न विस्मरसि सुस्निग्धम्
उपकारं कृतं शुभम् ॥
O Lord of Vānara hosts! There is no surprise at all that you have not forgotten the signal service done to you out of friendship.
4.32.11
राघवेण तु वीरेण
भयमुत्सृज्य दूरतः ।
त्वत्प्रियार्थं हतो वाली
शक्रतुल्यपराक्रमः ॥
Rāghava, the Veera, with no fear whatsoever, killed Vāli of prowess equivalent to that of Ṡakra, just to please you.
4.32.12
सर्वथा प्रणयात्कृद्धो
राघवो नात्र संशयः ।
भ्रातरं सम्प्रहितवान्
लक्ष्मणं लक्ष्मिवर्धनम् ॥
I do not think it is because of lack of love that Rāghava is angry or that he has sent his brother Lakshmaṇa, the augmenter of prosperity.
4.32.13
त्वं प्रमत्तो न जानीषे
कालं कालविदां वर ।
फुल्लसप्तच्छदश्यामा
प्रवृत्ता तु शरच्छुभा ॥
Though you are the foremost among those who have a great sense of time, you did not notice the passing of time and the arrival of Ṡarat, with the dark-tinted sheen of the Saptaparṇa trees in bloom, having sunk in a lull.
4.32.14
निर्मलग्रहनक्षत्रा
द्यौः प्रनष्टवलाहका ।
प्रसन्नाश्च दिशस्सर्वाः
सरितश्च सरांसि च ॥
With the clouds receding, the sky is clear (glistening) with stars and planets. The streams, the lakes and all the cardinals are (calm, clear and) pleasant.
4.32.15
प्राप्तमुद्योगकालं तु
नावैषि हरिपुङ्गव ।
त्वं प्रमत्त इति व्यक्तम्
लक्ष्मणोऽयमिहागतः ॥
O bull among Vānaras! You have not realized that the season for expeditions has arrived. Your negligence was clear and that is why Lakshmaṇa has come here.
4.32.16
आर्तस्य हृतदारस्य
पुरुषं पुरुषान्तरात् ।
वचनं मर्षणीयं ते
राघवस्य महात्मनः ॥
You should give a patient ear to the word of Rāghava, distraught at the loss of his wife, that he has sent through a third person, which might well be harsh.
4.32.17
कृतापराधस्य हि ते
नान्यत्पश्याम्यहं क्षमम् ।
अन्तरेणाञ्जलिं बद्ध्वा
लक्ष्मणस्य प्रसादनात् ॥
I cannot see what else you can do, being guilty of committing a lapse than plead for the grace of Lakshmaṇa, with your palms joined in reverence.
4.32.18
नियुक्तैर्मन्त्रिभिर्वाच्यो
ह्यवश्यं पार्थिवो हितम् ।
अत एव भयं त्यक्त्वा
ब्रवीम्यवधृतं वचः ॥
A king would expect only the best counsel from the appointed ministers. Hence I have expressed my considered opinion fearlessly.
4.32.19
अभिक्रुद्धस्समर्थो हि
चापमुद्यम्य राघवः ।
सदेवासुरगन्धर्वम्
वशे स्थापयितुं जगत् ॥
Rāghava in his wrath, holding the bow up in his hand, is capable of bringing the entire world including the Dēvas, Asuras and Gandharvas, under his control.
4.32.20
न स क्षमः कोपयितुम्
यः प्रसाद्यः पुनर्भवेत् ।
पूर्वोपकारं स्मरता
कृतज्ञेन विशेषतः ॥
His wrath should not be stirred, rather his pleasure should be regained, especially keeping the favor he has done earlier in mind and being grateful for it.
4.32.21
तस्य मूर्ध्ना प्रणम्य त्वम्
सपुत्रस्ससुहृज्जनः ।
राजंस्तिष्ठस्व समये
भर्तुर्भार्येव तद्वशे  ॥
O King! Bow your head and prostrate to him along with your kith and kin. Keep true to your vows and be obedient (to him) like a wife to her husband.
4.32.22
न रामरामानुजशासनं त्वया
कपीन्द्र युक्तं मनसाऽप्यपोहितुम् ।
मनो हि ते ज्ञास्यति मानुषं बलं
स राघवस्यास्य सुरेन्द्रवर्चसः ॥
You should not, O Lord of Vānaras, let the idea of flouting the orders of Rāma and his younger brother, even enter your mind.
In your heart of hearts, you are well aware of the power of these (two) humans, Rāghava’s and his (Lakshmaṇa), whose splendor rivals that of the Lord of Dēvas.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे द्वात्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty second Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
