Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 38
In this Sarga, Hanumān praises Seetā for her virtue of not touching another man. He explains what propelled him to make the offer of carrying her on his back across the ocean. He asks her to give a token of identity to prove that he indeed saw her, if she cannot go with him.
Then Seetā tells him of an incident that happened when Rāma and she were alone at the foot of the mountain of Citrakūṭa. She tells how a crow came when Rāma was sleeping on her lap and started pecking at her between her breasts and how Rāma woke up when drops of blood fell on him and how he, in his anger, hurled the Astra of Brahma at the crow and how it fled, and how the arrow chased the crow, and how the crow finding no one who could save it from that Astra had to finally come back, seeking refuge in Rāma alone.
Then she conveys her message for Rāma and Lakshmaṇa. She implores Hanumān, that it is upon him to urge Rāma and Lakshmaṇa into action, to rescue her. Hanumān assures her that it is the beginning of the end of her sorrows and that the two brothers will come to rescue her, reducing Laṅkā to ashes.
Seetā then gives a gemstone ornament that she wore in her tresses to Hanumān and tells him to give it to Rāma. Holding that ornament, Hanumān trembles like a tree in high wind, because of the power it had, having been worn by Seetā, daughter of Janaka.
5.38.1
ततस्स कपिशार्दूलः
तेन वाक्येन तोषितः ।
सीतामुवाच तच्छृत्वा
वाक्यं वाक्यविशारदः ॥
Then that tiger among Vānaras, who knew what counted as well-made conversation, was pleased with her words. Hearing all that Seetā said, he said these words to her:
5.38.2
युक्तरूपं त्वया देवि
भाषितं शुभदर्शने ।
सदृशं स्त्रीस्वभावस्य
साध्वीनां विनयस्य च ॥
O Dēvi of auspicious looks! What you said is appropriate and to be expected of a woman, and betrays the modesty of a virtuous woman.
5.38.3
स्त्रीत्वं न तु समर्थं हि
सागरं व्यतिवर्तितुम् ।
मामधिष्ठाय विस्तीर्णम्
शतयोजनमायतम् ॥
Agreed, it is difficult for a woman to cross the wide ocean that stretches for a hundred-Yōjanas, even if it is by sitting on my back.
5.38.4
द्वितीयं कारणं यच्च
ब्रवीषि विनयान्विते ।
रामादन्यस्य नार्हामि
संस्पर्शमिति जानकि ।
एतत्ते सदृशं देवि
पत्न्यास्तस्य महात्मनः ।
का ह्यन्या त्वामृते देवि
ब्रूयाद्वचनमीदृशम् ॥
O Seetā, O daughter of Janaka, O you full of modesty! The second reason you gave, ‘it is not appropriate for me to touch anyone other than Rāma’, can only be expected of the wife of a Mahātma like him! Who else but you would speak thus, O Dēvi?
5.38.6
श्रोष्यते चैव काकुत्स्थः
सर्वं निरवशेषतः ।
चेष्टितं यत्त्वया देवि
भाषितं मम चाग्रतः ॥
Kākutstha shall hear everything, with nothing left unmentioned, that you did and said in my presence.
5.38.7
कारणैर्बहुभिर्देवि
रामप्रियचिकीर्षया ।
स्नेहप्रस्कन्नमनसा
मयैतत्समुदीरितम् ॥
There are many reasons, O Dēvi, for saying what I said, (not the least of which is) my eagerness to please Rāma and my heart melting away with affection.
5.38.8
लङ्काया दुष्प्रवेशत्वात्
दुस्तरत्वान्महोदधेः ।
सामर्थ्यादात्मनश्चैव
मयैतत्समुदीरितम् ॥
I said what I said considering the difficulty in crossing the ocean, the difficulty in penetrating Laṅkā, and my own capability (to carry you back).
5.38.9
इच्छामि त्वां समानेतुम्
अद्यैव रघुबन्धुना ।
गुरुस्नेहेन भक्त्या च
नान्यथैतदुदाहृतम् ॥
I wish to unite you this very day with the scion of the Raghus, propelled by my love and devotion to my master; there is no other reason for saying what I said.
5.38.10
यदि नोत्सहसे यातुम्
मया सार्थमनिन्दिते ।
अभिज्ञानं प्रयच्छ त्वम्
जानीयाद्राघवो हि यत् ॥
If you do not like to go with me, O blameless one, give me a token of identity that Rāghava would recognize.
5.38.11
एवमुक्ता हनुमता
सीता सुरसुतोपमा ।
उवाच वचनं मन्दम्
बाष्पप्रग्रथिताक्षरम् ॥
Thus told by Hanumān, Seetā who was like a princess of Dēvas, spoke in a paced manner, with each of her words pierced with tears:
5.38.12
इदं श्रेष्ठमभिज्ञानम्
ब्रूयास्त्वं तु मम प्रियम् ॥
Please tell my beloved this best of the memories that he would recognize:
5.38.12
शैलस्य चित्रकूटस्य
पादे पूर्वोत्तरे पुरा ।
तापसाश्रमवासिन्याः
प्राज्यमूलफलोदके ।
तस्मिन्सिद्धाश्रमे देशे
मन्दाकिन्या विदूरतः ।
तस्योपवनषण्डेषु
नानापुष्पसुगन्धिषु ।
विहृत्य सलिले क्लिन्ना
ममाङ्के समुपाविशः ॥
Once upon a time, you sat by my lap, wet after playing in the streams of clear water, by the woods filled with the fragrance of many kinds of flowers that were not too far from Mandākini at the foot of the Citrakūṭa mountain on its north eastern side where fruits and roots abounded, a place that Siddhas made their home.
5.38.15
ततो मांससमायुक्तो
वायसः पर्यतुण्डयत् ।
तमहं लोष्टमुद्यम्य
वारयामिस्म वायसम् ॥
A crow, greedy for flesh, started pecking at me with its beak. I tried to ward the crow off by throwing a clod of mud.
5.38.16
दारयन्स च मां काकः
तत्त्रैव परिलीयते ।
न चाप्युपारमन्मांसात्
भक्षार्थि बलिभोजनः ॥
It kept hovering around, tearing my flesh. Hungry for a meal, the crow would not move away from where the flesh was.
5.38.17
उत्कर्षन्त्यां च रशनाम्
क्रुद्धायां मयि पक्षिणि ।
स्रस्यमाने च वसने
ततो दृष्टा त्वया ह्यहम् ॥
You saw me, when I was thus annoyed by that bird and was pulling up my waist-belt to hold my garment that was slipping off.
5.38.18
त्वयाऽपहसिता चाहम्
क्रुद्धा संलज्जिता तदा ।
भक्षगृध्नेन काकेन
दारिता त्वामुपागता ॥
You then made fun of me and I became annoyed and felt ashamed. Torn by that crow that was greedy for food, I came to you.
5.38.19
आसीनस्य च ते श्रान्ता
पुनरुत्सङ्गमाविशम् ।
क्रुध्यन्ती च प्रहृष्टेन
त्वयाऽहं परिसान्त्विता ॥
Tired, I then came and sat on your lap again. You enjoyed the whole thing, but seeing me angry, you consoled me.
5.38.20
बाष्पपूर्णमुखी मन्दम्
चक्षुषी परिमार्जती ।
लक्षिताऽहं त्वया नाथ
वायसेन प्रकोपिता ॥
O my lord! You saw me angered by the crow, face filled with tears and wiping my eyes gently.
5.38.21
परिश्रमात्प्रसुप्ता च
राघवाङ्केऽप्यहं चिरम् ।
पर्यायेण प्रसुप्तश्च
ममाङ्के भरताग्रजः ॥
Tired, I fell asleep on Rāghava’s lap for a long time. Then the elder brother of Bharata took h turn and slept on my lap.
5.38.22
स तत्र पुनरेवाथ
वायसस्समुपागमत् ।
ततस्सुप्तप्रबुद्धां माम्
रामस्याङ्कात्समुत्थिताम् ॥
Then just as I woke up from sleep and got up from Rāma’s lap, the crow came again.
5.38.23
वायसस्सहसागम्य
विददार स्तनान्तरे ।
पुनः पुनरथोत्पत्य
विददार स मां भृशम् ॥
The crow dashed onto me and tore away at me between my breasts. It kept flying up and down tearing away hard at me.
5.38.24
ततस्समुक्षितो रामो
मुक्तैश्शोणितबिन्दुभिः ।
वायसेन ततस्तेन
बलवत्क्लिश्यमानया ।
स मया बोधितश्श्रीमान्
सुखसुप्तः परन्तपः ॥
Then the drops of blood fell on Rāma. That blessed one, who torments his foe, who was enjoying his sleep, was woken up by me, who was badgered hard by the crow.
5.38.25
स मां दृष्ट्वा महाबाहुः
वितुन्नां स्तनयोस्तदा ।
आशीविष इव क्रुद्धः
श्वसन्वाक्यमभाषत ॥
Then, he of mighty arm, seeing me with my breasts torn, became outraged and said these words, hissing like a venomous serpent:
5.38.26
केन ते नागनासोरु
विक्षतं वै स्तनान्तरम् ।
कः क्रीडति सरोषेण
पञ्चवक्त्रेण भोगिना ॥
O you with thighs like elephant’s trunk! Who is it that wounded your breasts? Who is it that wants to play with a five-hooded serpent?
5.38.27
वीक्षमाणस्ततस्तं वै
वायसं समुदैक्षत ।
नखैस्सरुधिरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
मामेवाभिमुखं स्थितम् ॥
Looking around, he saw the crow with its sharp talons smeared in blood and its face turned towards me!
5.38.28
पुत्रः किल स शक्रस्य
वायसः पततां वरः ।
धरान्तरगतश्शीघ्रम्
पवनस्य गतौ समः ॥
It seemed that that eminent bird, an equal to the wind in its swiftness, which lives in the crevices of the earth, was the son of Ṡakra.
5.38.29
ततस्तस्मिन्महाबाहुः
कोपसंवर्तितेक्षणः ।
वायसे कृतवान्क्रूराम्
मतिं मतिमतां वरः ॥
He of mighty arm, the best among thoughtful men, his eyes rolling with rage, resolved in his mind to show no mercy to that crow.
5.38.30
स दर्भं संस्तराद्गृह्य
ब्राह्मेणास्त्रेण योजयत् ।
स दीप्त इव कालाग्निः
जज्वालाभिमुखो द्विजम् ॥
He drew a reed of Darbha grass from his grass mat and infused it with the power of the Astra of Brahma. And it blazed before the eyes of the bird like the fire of Dissolution.
5.38.31
स तं प्रदीप्तं चिक्षेप
दर्भं तं वायसं प्रति ।
ततस्तं वायसं दर्भः
सोम्बरेऽनुजगाम ह ॥
And he hurled that blazing Darbha at the crow. The Darbha chased the crow as it fled into the sky.
5.38.32
अनुसृष्टस्तदा काको
जगाम विविधां गतिम् ।
लोककाम इमं लोकम्
सर्वं वै विचचार ह ॥
Thus chased, the crow flew in every direction. Looking for someone to save it, it went all around the earth.
5.38.33
स पित्रा च परित्यक्तः
सुरैश्च समहर्षिभिः ।
त्रीन्लोकान्सम्परिक्रम्य
तमेव शरणं गतः ॥
Disowned by his father, and by all the Dēvas and Maharshis alike, he went around all the three worlds in vain, and finally sought refuge in him (Rāma) alone.
5.38.34
स तं निपतितं भूमौ
शरण्यश्शरणागतम् ।
वधार्हमपि काकुत्स्थः
कृपया पर्यपालयत् ॥
Seeing him thus fall on the ground seeking refuge, Kākutstha who is the ultimate recourse for all, spared him out of compassion, though he deserved to be killed.
5.38.35
परिद्यूनं विषण्णं च
स तमायान्तमब्रवीत् ।
मोघं कर्तुं न शक्यं तु
ब्राह्ममस्त्रं तदुच्यताम् ॥
Then he spoke to him who came back tired and repenting, “Once invoked, the Astra of Brahma cannot go in vain; tell me what to do!”
5.38.36
हिनस्तु दक्षिणाक्षि त्वत्
शर इत्यथ सोऽब्रवीत् ।
ततस्तस्याक्षि काकस्य
हिनस्ति स्म स दक्षिणम् ।
दत्त्वा स दक्षिणं नेत्रम्
प्राणेभ्यः परिरक्षितः ॥
He then said, ‘let your arrow take my right eye!’ Then that arrow took the right eye of the crow. The crow could save its life by giving up its right eye.
5.38.38
स रामाय नमस्कृत्य
राज्ञे दशरथाय च ।
विसृष्टस्तेन वीरेण
प्रतिपेदे स्वमालयम् ॥
Saluting Rāma and the king Daṡaratha he took leave from that Veera, and went to his own dwelling.
5.38.39
मत्कृते काकमात्रे तु
ब्रह्मास्त्रं समुदीरितम् ।
कस्माद्यो मां हरेत्त्वत्तः
क्षमसे तं महीपते ॥
You have hurled the Astra of Brahma even on a mere a crow for my sake; what then is the reason for tolerating someone who has abducted me!
5.38.40
स कुरुष्व महोत्साहः
कृपां मयि नरर्षभ ।
त्वया नाथवती नाथ
ह्यनाथा इव दृश्यते ॥
O bull among men! Take pity on me and please put forth your best effort! My lord! One who has you as her lord should not feel like one without a protector.
5.38.41
आनृशंस्यं परो धर्मः
त्वत्त ऐव मया श्रुतः ।
जानामि त्वां महावीर्यम्
महोत्साहं महाबलम् ।
अपारपारमक्षोभ्यम्
गाम्भीर्यात्सागरोपमम् ।
भर्तारं ससमुद्राया
धरण्या वासवोपमम् ॥
You have yourself said that not being inconsiderate is the greatest of virtues. I know your immense power, zeal and strength. Like an ocean, you are deep, imperturbable and immeasurably vast, from one end to the other. You are comparable to Vāsava. You are the lord of the entire earth skirted by the oceans.
5.38.43
एवमस्त्रविदां श्रेष्ठः
सत्यवान्बलवानपि ।
किमर्थमस्त्रं रक्षस्सु
न योजयसि राघव ॥
O Rāghava! Why do you not direct your Astras towards the Rākshasas, being a man of true strength and being the best among those who command Astras?
5.38.44
न नागा नाऽपि गन्धर्वा
नासुरा न मरुद्गणाः ।
रामस्य समरे वेगम्
शक्ताः प्रतिसमाधितुं ॥
Neither Nāgas, nor Gandharvas, nor Dēvas, nor the hosts of Maruts can stand against the speed of Rāma in the battle field.
5.38.45
तस्य वीर्यवतः कश्चित्
यद्यस्ति मयि सम्भ्रमः ।
किमर्थं न शरैस्तीक्ष्णैः
क्षयं नयति राक्षसान् ॥
If someone like him, a heroic one, has the least of concern for me, why does he not bring destruction upon the Rākshasas with his sharp arrows?
5.38.46
भ्रातुरादेशमादाय
लक्ष्मणो वा परन्तपः ।
कस्य हेतोर्न मां वीरः
परित्राति महाबलः ॥
Why does not even Lakshmaṇa, a Veera of immense strength and a scorcher of his foe, rescue me, taking permission from Rāghava?
5.38.47
यदि तौ पुरुषव्याघ्रौ
वाय्वग्निसमतेजसौ ।
सुराणामपि दुर्धर्षौ
किमर्थं मामुपेक्षतः ॥
Why are those tigers among men, who equal the wind and fire in power, whom even the Dēvas dare not assail, indifferent to me?
5.38.48
ममैव दुष्कृतं किञ्चित्
महदस्ति न संशयः ।
समर्थावपि तौ यन्माम्
नावेक्षेते परन्तपौ ॥
There must have been, without a doubt, a big blunder that I had committed; otherwise why would those scorchers of foe, being as capable as they are, turn a cold shoulder to me?
5.38.49
वैदेह्या वचनं श्रुत्वा
करुणं साश्रुभाषितम् ।
अथाब्रवीन्महातेजा
हनुमान्मारुतात्मजः ॥
Hearing those pitiable words of Vaidēhee, spoken with her throat choked with tears, Hanumān, the immensely strong son of Vāyu, said:
5.38.50
त्वच्छोकविमुखो रामो
देवि सत्येन ते शपे ।
रामे दुःखाभिपन्ने च
लक्ष्मणः परितप्यते ॥
Grieving for you, O Dēvi, Rāma neglects everything else. I swear this on truth. And when Rāma is deluged by grief, Lakshmaṇa becomes sad too.
5.38.51
कथञ्चिद्भवती दृष्टा
न कालः परिदेवितुम् ।
इमं मुहूर्तं दुःखानाम्
द्रक्ष्यस्यन्तमनिन्दिते ॥
Somehow, you have been found.  This is no occasion to grieve.  This hour, as you shall see, O blameless one, is the beginning of the end of all sorrows!
5.38.52
तावुभौ पुरुषव्याघ्रौ
राजपुत्रौ महाबलौ ।
त्वद्दर्शनकृतोत्साहौ
लङ्कां भस्मीकरिष्यतः ॥
Those two princes of immense strength, tigers among men, are eager to see you and they will reduce Laṅkā to ashes.
5.38.53
हत्त्वा च समरे क्रूरम्
रावणं सहबान्धवम् ।
राघवस्त्वां विशालाक्षि
नेष्यति स्वां पुरीं प्रति ॥
Slaying the cruel Rāvaṇa along with his kith and kin in the battle field, O you of wide eyes, Rāghava will take you back to his own city.
5.38.54
ब्रूहि यद्राघवो वाच्यो
लक्ष्मणश्च महाबलः ।
सुग्रीवो वापि तेजस्वी
हरयोऽपि समागताः ॥
Please tell me what Rāghava, Lakshmaṇa of immense strength, the powerful Sugreeva and the assemblage of Vānaras should be told.
5.38.55
इत्युक्तवति तस्मिंस्तु
सीता सुरसुतोपमा ।
उवाच शोकसन्तप्ता
हनुमन्तं प्लवङ्गमम् ॥
When she was spoken to thus, Seetā, who resembled a daughter of Dēvas, said to Hanumān, the Vānara, burning with grief:
5.38.56
कौसल्या लोकभर्तारम्
सुषुवे यं मनस्विनी ।
तं ममार्थे सुखं पृच्छ
शिरसा चाभिवादय ॥
Please inquire about the wellbeing of the lord of the worlds, whom Kausalya, a large hearted lady, has given birth to, and make prostrations, bowing the head on my behalf.
5.38.57
स्रजश्च सर्वरत्नानि
प्रिया याश्च वराङ्गनाः ।
ऐश्वर्यं च विशालायाम्
पृथिव्यामपि दुर्लभम् ।
पितरं मातरं चैव
सम्मान्याभिप्रसाद्य च ।
अनुप्रव्रजितो रामम्
सुमित्रा येन सुप्रजाः ॥
He, in whom Sumitrā has a wonderful son, gave up the garlands, all the precious gems, beautiful women whom he loved and the vast riches that are difficult to acquire on this earth, and followed Rāma, after paying respect to and with the blessings of his father and mother.
Seetā goes on an endless praise for Lakshmaṇa here (in seven Ṡlōkas, as opposed to one Ṡlōka in which she inquiries about Rāma’s wellbeing), recalling his virtues and the affection and regard they have for each other.
Underlying this praise is her repentance over the extremely harsh words she spoke to him when he refused to leave her alone, to go to the presumed rescue of Rāma who went chasing the deer.
This is the first time since then that she got an opportunity to convey to Lakshmaṇa (through Hanumān) all the affection and regard she has for him and how much she appreciates his affection and regard. She falls only a wee bit short of conveying her explicit apologies for her harshness, which in the first place, caused all this unspeakable trouble and immeasurable grief.
5.38.59
आनुकूल्येन धर्मात्मा
त्यक्त्वा सुखमनुत्तमम् ।
अनुगच्छति काकुत्स्थम्
भ्रातरं पालयन्वने ॥
That Dharmātma left behind the best of the best comforts and followed his brother Kākutstha to be of help to him and to protect him.
5.38.60
सिंहस्कन्धो महाबाहुः
मनस्वी प्रियदर्शिनः ।
पितृवद्वर्तते रामे
मातृवन्मां समाचरन् ॥
He of mighty arm and lion’s shoulders, with magnanimous heart and pleasing looks, treated Rāma like his father and me like his mother.
5.38.61
ह्रियमाणां तदा वीरो
न तु मां वेद लक्ष्मणः ।
वृद्धोपसेवी लक्ष्मीवान्
शक्तो न बहुभाषिता ।
राजपुत्रः प्रियः श्रेष्ठः
सदृशः श्वशुरस्य मे ॥
Lakshmaṇa, the Veera did not know that I was being carried off. He, the blessed one, who serves his elders, is a man of many abilities and few words. He, a great prince, is the beloved and the likeness of my father-in-law.
5.38.62
मत्तः प्रियतरो नित्यम्
भ्राता रामस्य लक्ष्मणः ।
नियुक्तो धुरि यस्यां तु
तामुद्वहति वीर्यवान् ॥
Lakshmaṇa, his brother, is dearer to Rāma than even I am. He, the man of prowess, carries out well any responsibility that is laid upon him.
5.38.63
यं दृष्ट्वा राघवो नैव
वृत्तमार्यमनुस्मरेत् ।
स ममार्थाय कुशलम्
वक्तव्यो वचनान्मम ॥
Ask about his wellbeing on my behalf, having whom by his side, Rāghava does not miss even his deceased father.
5.38.64
मृदुर्नित्यं शुचिर्दक्षः
प्रियो रामस्य लक्ष्मणः ।
यथा हि वानरश्रेष्ठ
दुःखक्षयकरो भवेत् ।
त्वमस्मिन्कार्यनिर्योगे
प्रमाणं हरिसत्तमः ।
राघवस्त्वत्समारम्भान्
मयि यत्नपरो भवेत् ॥
Lakshmaṇa, the capable one who is ever pleasant, simple and dear to Rāma, should be the one who destroys this sorrow, and that, O eminent and best of Vānaras, depends on the key role you play! It is only with your concerted effort that Rāghava would focus all his energies on me (my rescue).
5.38.66
इदं ब्रूयाश्च मे नाथम्
शूरं रामं पुनः पुनः ॥
Please tell this time and again to Rāma, the Ṡūra, my lord:
5.38.67
जीवितं धारयिष्यामि
मासं दशरथात्मज ।
ऊर्ध्वं मासान्न जीवेयम्
सत्येनाहं ब्रवीमि ते ॥
I can hold on to this life for only one more month, O son of Daṡaratha! I will not live any longer than a month; What I say is nothing but the truth.
5.38.68
रावणेनोपरुद्धां माम्
निकृत्य पापकर्मणा ।
त्रातुमर्हसि वीर त्वम्
पातालादिव कौशिकीम् ॥
O Veera! You shall rescue me from this Rāvaṇa of evil deeds, who humiliated me by taking me captive, as Indra’s sovereignty was retrieved from Pātāla!
5.38.69
सा निरीक्ष्य ततः सर्वम्
वेण्यां ग्रथितमुत्तमम् ।
विमुच्य प्रददौ तस्मै
मणिरत्नं हनूमते ।
देयोऽयं राघवायेति
सीता सुरसुतोपमा ॥
Then looking around everywhere, Seetā, who was like a daughter of the Dēvas, found an excellent jewel that was locked in her tresses and took it off and gave it to Hanumān saying, ‘give this to Rāghava’.
5.38.70
मणिरत्नं कपिवरः
प्रतिगृह्याभिवाद्य च ।
सीतां प्रदक्षिणं कृत्वा
प्रणतः पार्व्शतः स्थितः ॥
The eminent Vānara took that gem of a jewel, saluted and circumambulated her and stood by her with deference.
5.38.71
हर्षेण महता युक्तः
सीतादर्शनजेन सः ।
हृदयेन गतो रामम्
शरीरेण तु विष्ठितः ॥
Feeling extremely happy about having seen Seetā, his mind went to Rāma, while his body stood there.
5.38.72
मणिवरमुपगृह्य तं महार्हं
जनकनृपात्मजया धृतं पुरस्तात् ।
द्रुम इव पवनाधूतमूर्तिः
क्षुभिततनुर्हनुमान् कृतस्तदानीम् ॥
Holding that gem of a jewel that only great souls deserve, which was worn till then by the daughter of king Janaka, Hanumān felt his body shaking like a tree hit by high winds.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे अष्टत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty eighth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
