Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 45
In this Sarga, the seven sons of ministers sent by Rāvaṇa, along with their armies, attack Hanumān. They rained a barrage of arrows on Hanumān, which did not harm Hanumān any more than a downpour on a mountain. Pounding with his fist, chest and thighs and rending with his nails, Hanumān kills all of them, turning the streets into streams of blood. The entire Laṅkā reverberates with horrible shrieks.
5.45.1
ततस्ते राक्षसेन्द्रेण
चोदिता मन्त्रिणस्सुताः ।
निर्ययुर्भवनात्तस्मात्
सप्तसप्तार्चिवर्चसः ।
महाबलपरीवारा
धनुष्मन्तो महाबलाः ।
कृतास्त्रास्त्रविदां श्रेष्ठाः
परस्परजयैषिणः ।
हेमजालपरिक्षिप्तैः
ध्वजवद्भिः पताकिभिः ।
तोयदस्वननिर्घोषैः
वाजियुक्तैर्महारथैः ।
तप्तकाञ्चनचित्राणि
चापान्यमितविक्रमाः ।
विस्फारयन्तस्संहृष्टाः
तटित्वन्त इवाम्बुदाः ॥
Directed by the King of Rākshasas, the seven mighty strong sons of ministers, best among the savants of Astras,
possessing limitless prowess, armed with bows, trained well in Astras, glowing like fire, eager to outdo the rest,
marched forth in high spirits from that palace on imposing chariots drawn by horses, rumbling and flashing like rainclouds with flag posts and flags decorated with lacework of gold,
supported by great contingents of army, twanging the strings of the bows that had a variety of decorations made of gold purified in fire on them.
5.45.5
जनन्यस्तु ततस्तेषाम्
विदित्वा किङ्करान्हतान् ।
बभूवुश्शोकसम्भ्रान्ताः
सबान्धवसुहृज्जनाः ॥
But their mothers, as well as their relations and friends felt very sad and worried, having already learnt about the slaughter of Kiṅkaras.
5.45.6
ते परस्परसङ्घर्षात्
तप्तकाञ्चनभूषणाः ।
अभिपेतुर्हनूमन्तम्
तोरणस्थमवस्थितम् ॥
Each vying to be ahead of the other, wearing ornaments made of gold purified in fire, they rushed to Hanumān who was ready (for the fight), at the archway.
5.45.7
सृजन्तो बाणवृष्टिं ते
रथगर्जितनिस्स्वनाः ।
वृष्टिमन्त इवांभोदा
विचेरुर्नैऋताम्बुदाः ॥
Those cloud-like Rākshasas, moved about like rain clouds creating a torrent of arrows, with the rumble of chariots for thunder.
5.45.8
अवकीर्णस्ततस्ताभिः
हनुमान्शरवृष्टिभिः ।
अभवत्संवृताकारः
शैलराडिव वृष्टिभिः ॥
With those volleys of arrows showering on him, Hanumān looked like the king of mountains with its outline blurred by a downpour.
5.45.9
स शरान्मोघयामास
तेषामाशुचरः कपिः ।
रथवेगं च वीराणाम्
विचरन्विमलेऽम्बरे ॥
The swift moving Vānara rendered those arrows and the speed of the chariots of those Veeras futile, darting around in the clear sky.
5.45.10
स तैः क्रीडन्धनुष्मद्भिः
व्योम्नि वीरः प्रकाशते ।
धनुष्मद्भिर्यथा मेघैः
मारुतः प्रभुरम्बरे ॥
Like the lordly God of Wind playing with clouds and their rainbows in the sky, that Veera shone in the air playing with those bow-wielders.
5.45.11
स कृत्वा निनदं घोरम्
त्रासयंस्तां महाचमूम् ।
चकार हनुमान्वेगम्
तेषु रक्षस्सु वीर्यवान् ॥
Letting off a terrible yell and terrifying that mighty army, the valiant Hanumān increased the speed (of attack) towards those Rākshasas.
5.45.12
तलेनाभ्यहनत्कांश्चित्
पादैः कांश्चित्परन्तपः ।
मुष्टिनाभ्यहनत्कांश्चित्
नखैः कांश्चिद्व्यदारयत् ॥
He, scorcher of the foe, struck some with the open palm of his hand, some with his feet, some he pummeled with his fist, and some he rent with his nails.
5.45.13
प्रममाथोरसा कांश्चित्
ऊरुभ्यामपरान्कपिः ।
केचित्तस्य निनादेन
तत्रैव पतिता भुवि ॥
The Vānara crushed some with his chest and others with his thighs; some fell to the ground just hearing his roar.
5.45.14
ततस्तेष्ववसन्नेषु
भूमौ निपतितेषु च ।
तत्सैन्यमगमत्सर्वम्
दिशोदश भयार्दितम् ॥
When they fell down dead on the ground, the entire army, panic-stricken, scattered in all ten directions.
5.45.15
विनेदुर्विस्वरं नागा
निपेतुर्भुवि वाजिनः ।
भग्ननीडध्वजच्छत्रैः
भूश्च कीर्णाऽभवद्रथैः ॥
Elephants wailed loudly and horses fell down to the ground. The earth was scattered with chariots, their roofs, flagstaffs and umbrellas broken.
5.45.16
स्रवता रुधिरेणाथ
स्रवन्त्यो दर्शिताः पथि ।
विविधैश्च स्वरैर्लङ्का
ननाद विकृतं तदा ॥
The streets looked like rivers with the flowing blood. Laṅkā reverberated with every kind of horribly scream.
5.45.17
स तान्प्रवृद्धान्विनिहत्य राक्षसान्
महाबलश्चण्डपराक्रमः कपिः ।
युयुत्सुरन्यैः पुनरेव राक्षसैः
तमेव वीरोऽभिजगाम तोरणम् ॥
Having destroyed those over-vaunting Rākshasas, the Vānara, a Veera of fiery prowess and immense strength, returned to the archway, eager for a fight again with more Rākshasas.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे पञ्चचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty fifth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
