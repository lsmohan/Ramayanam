Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 3
In the previous Sarga, King Daṡaratha receives an uproar of approval for the coronation Rāma as the crown prince. In this Sarga, upon the request of the king, Vasishṭha and Vāmadēva give directions to the concerned, for the consecration of Rāma. And upon the request of the king, Sumantra fetches Rāma and Daṡaratha tells him to become the crown prince and affectionately implores him to be a good person and earn the goodwill of everyone.
2.3.1
तेषामञ्जलिपद्मानि
प्रगृहीतानि सर्वशः ।
प्रतिगृह्याब्रवीद्राजा
तेभ्यः प्रियहितं वचः ॥
Hearing the words of those citizens, who said it in many ways, with their palms cupped like lotuses King Daṡaratha spoke these substantive and pleasing words:
2.3.2
अहोऽस्मि परमप्रीतः
प्रभावश्चातुलो मम ।
यन्मे ज्येष्ठं प्रियं पुत्रम्
यौवराज्यस्थमिच्छथ ॥
Ah! how it delights me, and how greatly it makes me feel proud, that you wish to see my eldest and dearly loved son as the crown prince!
2.3.3
इति प्रत्यर्च्य तान्राजा
ब्राह्मणानिदमब्रवीत् ।
वसिष्ठं वामदेवं च
तेषामेवोपशृण्वताम् ॥
After reciprocating their gesture, while all of them were listening, that king addressed Vasishṭha, Vāmadēva and other Brāhmaṇas:
2.3.4
चैत्रश्श्रीमानयं मासः
पुण्यः पुष्पितकाननः ।
यौवराज्याय रामस्य
सर्वमेवोपकल्प्यताम् ॥
This month of Caitra is auspicious and holy with the trees in full bloom. May all arrangements be made for the coronation of Rāma as the crown prince.
2.3.5
राज्ञस्तूपरते वाक्ये
जनघोषो महानभूत् ॥
Barely before the king finished those words was there a tumultuous uproar of applause from the people.
2.3.5
शनैस्तस्मिन्प्रशान्ते च
जनघोषे जनाधिपः ।
वसिष्ठं मुनिशार्दूलम्
राजा वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
After that tumultuous applause quieted down slowly, the king, the lord of peoples, spoke to Vasishṭha, the tiger among Munis:
2.3.6
अभिषेकाय रामस्य
यत्कर्म सपरिच्छदम् ।
तदद्य भगवन् सर्वम्
आज्ञापयितुमर्हसि ॥
O Bhagawan! Now, please give us directions regarding everything that needs to be done including all the required arrangements, for the consecration of Rāma !
2.3.7
तच्छ्रुत्वा भूमिपालस्य
वसिष्ठो द्विजसत्तमः ।
आदिदेशाग्रतो राज्ञः
स्थितान्युक्तान् कृताञ्जलीन् ॥
That eminent Brāhmaṇa, upon hearing those words of the ruler of the earth, gave directions to the officials who were waiting in front of the king with palms joined in obedience:
2.3.8
सुवर्णादीनि रत्नानि
बलीन् सर्वौषधीरपि ।
शुक्लमाल्यांश्च लाजांश्च
पृथक्च मधुसर्पिषी ।
अहतानि च वासांसि
रथं सर्वायुधान्यपि ।
चतुरङ्गबलं चैव
गजं च शुभलक्षणम् ।
चामरव्यजने श्वेते
ध्वजं छत्रं च पाण्डुरम् ।
शतं च शातकुम्भानाम्
कुम्भानामग्निवर्चसाम् ।
हिरण्यशृङ्गमृषभम्
समग्रं व्याघ्रचर्म च ।
उपस्थापयत प्रातः
अग्न्यगारं महीपतेः ॥
Please make ready gold, gems and votive offerings, herbs, garland of white flowers and puffed grain, new clothes, honey and ghee in separate vessels, an army contingent with all the four divisions, an elephant with all auspicious marks, a pair of fly-whisks made of Yak's hair, a flagstaff, a white umbrella and one hundred fire-bright golden pots, a bull with its horns cupped in gold and a whole tiger-skin by the morning at the king's fire ritual chamber.
2.3.12
यच्चान्यत्किञ्चिदेष्टव्यम्
तत्सर्वमुपकल्प्यताम् ।
अन्तःपुरस्य द्वाराणि
सर्वस्य नगरस्य च ।
चन्दनस्रग्भिरर्च्यन्ताम्
धूपैश्च घ्राणहारिभिः ॥
Whatever else that might be needed, regardless of how insignificant it is, might be arranged. Let the doorways of the palace and the city be decorated with sandal paste and garlands, and fragrant incense that draws every one by the nose.
2.3.14
प्रशस्तमन्नं गुणवत्
धधिक्षीरोपसेचनम् ।
द्विजानां शतसाहस्रे
यत्प्रकाममलं भवेत् ॥
May delicious food be prepared with curds and milk, enough to completely satisfy one hundred thousand Brāhmaṇas.
2.3.15
सत्कृत्य द्विजमुख्यानाम्
श्वःप्रभाते प्रदीयताम् ।
घृतं दधि च लाजाश्च
दक्षिणाश्चापि पुष्कलाः ॥
May all the prominent Brāhmaṇas be honored tomorrow morning, and may they be supplied aplenty with ghee, curds, puffed grain and honorarium.
2.3.16
सूर्येऽभ्युदितमात्रे श्वो
भविता स्वस्तिवाचनम् ।
ब्राह्मणाश्च निमन्त्र्यन्ताम्
कल्प्यन्तामासनानि च ॥
May auspicious invocations be done as soon as the sun rises tomorrow. May the Brāhmaṇas be properly invited and may proper seats be arranged for them.
2.3.17
आबध्यन्तां पताकाश्च
राजमार्गश्च सिंच्यताम् ।
सर्वे च तालावचरा
गणिकाश्च स्वलङ्कृताः ।
कक्ष्यां द्वितीयामासाद्य
तिष्ठन्तु नृपवेश्मनः ॥
May the royal pathways be washed and decorated with flags and banners. May cymbalists and bejeweled dancers be stationed in the second court of the King's palace.
2.3.18
देवायतनचैत्येषु
सान्नभक्षाः सदक्षिणाः ।
उपस्थापयितव्यास्स्युः
माल्ययोग्याः पृथक्पृथक् ॥
May people suitable to hold garlands, stocked with gifts, food and other eatables be convened at all temples and shrines.
The meaning of 'suitable to hold the garlands' part is not clear. I translated it as is from the original Ṡlōka.
2.3.19
दीर्घासिबद्धा योधाश्च
सन्नद्धा मृष्टवाससः ।
महाराजाङ्गणं सर्वे
प्रविशन्तु महोदयम् ॥
May fully alert warriors with long swords, wearing fine uniforms, be attending the glorious courtyard of the king.
2.3.20
एवं व्यादिश्य विप्रौ तौ
क्रियास्तत्र सुनिष्ठितौ ।
चक्रतुश्चैव यच्छेषम्
पार्थिवाय निवेद्य च ॥
Those two thoughtful Brāhmaṇas, having thus assigned the tasks, and completing whatever else was left to be done, reported to king.
2.3.21
कृतमित्येव चाब्रूताम्
अभिगम्य जगत्पतिम् ।
यथोक्तवचनं प्रीतौ
हर्षयुक्तौ द्विजर्षभौ ॥
Those two bulls among Brāhmaṇas, satisfied and happy, went to the ruler of the world and reported that everything was done as had been asked for.
2.3.22
ततस्सुमन्त्रं द्युतिमान्
राजा वचनमब्रवीत् ।
रामः कृतात्मा भवता
शीघ्रमानीयतामिति ॥
Then, the effulgent king, addressing Sumantra, said: "May Rāma, the noble minded, be brought here by you immediately!"
2.3.23
स तथेति प्रतिज्ञाय
सुमन्त्रो राजशासनात् ।
रामं तत्रानयाञ्चक्रे
रथेन रथिनां वरम् ॥
And Sumantra, promising 'Yes, sire!', went on a chariot to fetch Rāma, the foremost of charioteers, in accordance with that king's command.
2.3.24
अथ तत्र समासीनाः
तदा दशरथं नृपम् ।
प्राच्योदीच्याः प्रतीच्याश्च
दाक्षिणात्याश्च भूमिपाः ।
म्लेच्छाश्चार्याश्च ये चान्ये
वनशैलान्तवासिनः ।
उपासाञ्चक्रिरे सर्वे
तं देवा इव वासवम् ॥
Then the kings from the eastern, western and southern countries, from Mlēccha and Ārya countries, from forest areas and mountain areas, seated around Daṡaratha, paid homage to him, like Dēvas do to Indra.
2.3.26
तेषां मध्ये स राजर्षिः
मरुतामिव वासवः ।
प्रासादस्थो रथगतम्
ददर्शायान्तमात्मजम् ॥
That Rājarshi, sitting at the center of them like Indra among Dēvas in that palace, had seen his son coming on the chariot:
Like we noted many times before, Vālmeeki would have been a good screenplay writer. He thinks very visually.
Daṡaratha is sitting in the palace, the symbol of power. Rāma is coming on a chariot, a symbol of speed and abilities.
He is nearing the center of power by virtue of his abilities.
2.3.27
गन्धर्वराजप्रतिमम्
लोके विख्यातपौरुषम् ।
दीर्घबाहुं महासत्त्वम्
मत्तमातङ्गगामिनम् ।
चन्द्रकान्ताननं रामम्
अतीव प्रियदर्शनम् ।
रूपौदार्यगुणैः पुंसाम्
दृष्टिचित्तापहारिणम् ।
घर्माभितप्ताः पर्जन्यम्
ह्लादयन्तमिव प्रजाः ।
न ततर्प समायान्तम्
पश्यमानो नराधिपः ॥
Daṡaratha could not have enough of seeing Rāma thus coming, comparable to the king of Gandharvas, valor known to the entire world, hands long and imposing revealing his mighty prowess, gait resembling that of an elephant in rut, countenance lovely like the moon, looks exceptionally enchanting, presence drawing the eyes and magnificence and merits stealing the hearts, of even men, pleasing everyone like a cloud would a parched land!
There are many Ṡlōkas in Rāmāyaṇa that describe the exquisite and majestic character of Rāma. But this short description is unique.
It captures the mood of the arrival of a hero in his full splendor, to be received by all the kings and peoples that were already his, but going to be his in a formal manner.
Vālmeeki, in these Ṡlōkas, gives very visual description of Rāma's personality and movements, from alighting the chariot to ascending the steps of the palace. Enjoy it by visualizing it frame by frame!
2.3.30
अवतार्य सुमन्त्रस्तम्
राघवं स्यन्दनोत्तमात् ।
पितुस्समीपं गच्छन्तम्
प्राञ्जलिः पृष्ठतोऽन्वगात् ॥
Sumantra assisted that prince of Raghu dynasty to alight that splendid chariot and followed him from behind with palms joined in respect as he was proceeding towards his father.
2.3.31
स तं कैलासशृङ्गाभम्
प्रासादं नरपुङ्गवः ।
आरुरोह नृपं द्रष्टुम्
सह सूतेन राघवः ॥
The prince of Raghu dynasty, the best among men, along with the charioteer, ascended the steps of that palace, which was imposing like the peak of Kailāsa mountain, to see the king.
2.3.32
स प्राञ्जलिरभिप्रेत्य
प्रणतः पितुरन्तिके ।
नाम स्वं श्रावयन्रामो
ववन्दे चरणौ पितुः ॥
Rāma approached his father with palms joined in reverence, bowing in front of him telling his name, and touching his feet to pay respects.
2.3.33
तं दृष्ट्वा प्रणतं पार्श्वे
कृताञ्जलिपुटं नृपः ।
गृह्याञ्जलौ समाकृष्य
सस्वजे प्रियमात्मजम् ॥
Seeing him bowing by his side, taking his cupped palms into his own, and drawing him close to himself, the king embraced his beloved son.
2.3.34
तस्मै चाभ्युदितं दिव्यम्
मणिकाञ्चनभूषितम् ।
दिदेश राजा रुचिरम्
रामाय परमासनम् ॥
The king pointed Rāma to an elevated, magnificent and best of the seats that was splendidly encrusted with gems and gold.
2.3.35
तदासनवरं प्राप्य
व्यदीपयत राघवः ।
स्वयैव प्रभया मेरुम्
उदये विमलो रविः ॥
Having taken that best of the seats, the prince of Raghu dynasty enhanced the luster of that seat with his own, like the pristine morning sun illuminating Mēru mountain.
2.3.36
तेन विभ्राजता तत्र
सा सभाऽभिव्यरोचत ।
विमलग्रहनक्षत्रा
शारदी द्यौरिवेन्दुना ॥
That august assembly was, then, Illumined by that lustrous Rāma, like the clear post-monsoon sky with planets and stars is illuminated by the moon.
2.3.37
तं पश्यमानो नृपतिः
तुतोष प्रियमात्मजम् ।
अलङ्कृतमिवात्मानम्
आदर्शतलसंस्थितम् ॥
Seeing his beloved son, the king was happy and felt as if he was seeing himself adorned reflected in a mirror.
2.3.38
स तं सस्मितमाभाष्य
पुत्रं पुत्रवतां वरः ।
उवाचेदं वचो राजा
देवेन्द्रमिव काश्यपः ॥
Then, that best among those blessed with great sons, spoke to his son, with a smile, like Kāṡyapa addressing Dēvēndra:
2.3.39
ज्येष्ठायामसि मे पत्न्याम्
सदृश्यां सदृशस्सुतः ।
उत्पन्नस्त्वं गुणश्रेष्ठो
मम रामात्मजः प्रियः ॥
O Rāma!, you are a worthy son born to my worthy eldest wife. You, endowed with excellent virtues, are very dear to me!
2.3.40
यतस्त्वया प्रजाश्चेमाः
स्वगुणैरनुरञ्जिताः ।
तस्मात्त्वं पुष्ययोगेन
यौवराज्यमवाप्नुहि ॥
As you have won the affection of all these people by your qualities, may you become the crown prince when the moon enters the constellation of Pushya!
2.3.41
कामतस्त्वं प्रकृत्यैव
विनीतो गुणवानसि ।
गुणवत्यपि तु स्नेहात्
पुत्र वक्ष्यामि ते हितम् ॥
O son! You are, by nature, virtuous and humble! Yet, good as you are, I would like to say a few words for your good, out of affection:
2.3.42
भूयो विनयमास्थाय
भव नित्यं जितेन्द्रियः ।
कामक्रोधसमुत्थानि
त्यजेथा व्यसनानि च ॥
Cultivating humility even more assiduously, may you constantly maintain mastery over your senses! May you shed all addictions born out of fancy or anger!
2.3.43
परोक्षया वर्तमानो
वृत्त्या प्रत्यक्षया तथा ।
अमात्यप्रभृतीस्सर्वाः
प्रकृतीश्चानुरञ्जय ॥
May you ensure that you endear yourself to the ministers and all the peoples through your direct and indirect actions!
2.3.44
कोष्ठागारायुधागारैः
कृत्वा सन्निचयान्बहून् ।
तुष्टानुरक्तप्रकृतिः
यः पालयति मेदिनीम् ।
तस्यनन्दन्ति मित्राणि
लब्ध्वाऽमृतमिवामराः ।
तस्मात्त्वमपि चात्मानम्
नियम्यैवं समाचर ॥
Whosoever rules the earth by accumulating many coffers and arsenals that are full and uses them to make the subjects happy, will have allies that are as happy as Dēvas having Amṛta. Hence, may you ensure that you do the same!
2.3.46
तच्छ्रुत्वा सुहृदस्तस्य
रामस्य प्रियकारिणः ।
त्वरिताश्शीघ्रमभ्येत्य
कौसल्यायै न्यवेदयन् ॥
Hearing those words, the friends and well wishers of Rāma immediately went to Kousalyā and relayed the news to her.
2.3.47
सा हिरण्यं च गाश्चैव
रत्नानि विविधानि च ।
व्यादिदेश प्रियाख्येभ्यः
कौसल्या प्रमदोत्तमा ॥
That wonderful woman, Kousalyā, gave gold, cows and various kinds of gems to the messengers of these news.
2.3.48
अथाऽभिवाद्य राजानम्
रथमारुह्य राघवः ।
ययौ स्वं द्युतिमद्वेश्म
जनौघैः प्रतिपूजितः ॥
Then, the prince of Raghu dynasty, after paying obeisance to the king, took the chariot and left for his splendid mansion cheered by the crowds along the way.
2.3.49
ते चापि पौरा नृपतेर्वचस्त -
च्छृत्वा तथा लाभमिवेष्टमाशु ।
नरेन्द्रमामन्त्र्य गृहाणि गत्वा
देवान्समानर्चुरतिप्रहृष्टाः ॥
Those people also, hearing that pronouncement of the king, which was endearing and portending great benefit, took leave of that lord of kings, and left for their respective homes, and prayed to the Dēvas happily.
Vālmeeki says 'portending great benefit', as opposed to saying 'beneficial', to make the distinction between possibility and certainty.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे तृतीयस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the third Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
