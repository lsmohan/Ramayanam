Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 48
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa comes to terms with the fact that the young Aksha was killed. He instructs Indrajit, after extolling his virtues and capabilities and posing full confidence in him, to attack the Vānara. He implores him to plan carefully as the Vānara is very powerful and swift.
With his spirits riding high, Indrajit goes in his splendid chariot to attack Hanumān. Both of them being of immense speed and strength, none could find a slip in the other that would allow him to gain an upper hand.
Failing to hit Hanumān with his arrows, and realizing that it is impossible to kill the Vānara, Indrajit casts the Astra of Brahma on him. With the power of that Astra, Hanumān becomes motionless. The Rākshasas quickly gather around him and tie him with ropes of hemps and twine of tree bark. But Hanumān gets freed from the Astra, as that Astra becomes ineffective when another bond is imposed on it.
The Rākshasas drag him to the court of Rāvaṇa, beating him with fists and sticks on the way. Rāvaṇa and Hanumān see each other. Rāvaṇa orders his chief ministers to question him.
5.48.1
ततस्तु रक्षोधिपतिर्महात्मा
हनूमताक्षे निहते कुमारे ।
मनस्समाधाय सदेवकल्पं
समादिदेशेन्द्रजितं सरोषः ॥
Coming to terms with the fact that the young Aksha was killed by Hanumān, the mighty lord of Rākshasas, resentful and angry, instructed Indrajit who was comparable to Dēvas (in his prowess):
5.48.2
त्वमस्त्रविच्छस्त्रविदां वरिष्ठः
सुरासुराणामपि शोकदाता ।
सुरेषु सेन्द्रेषु च दृष्टकर्मा
पितामहाराधनसञ्चितास्त्रः ॥
You are well-versed with the use of Astras. You are the foremost of those who wield weapons. You have given grief even to Suras and Asuras. Even Suras and Indra had witnessed your exploits. You accumulated many Astras, worshipping the Grandsire.
5.48.3
तवास्त्रबलमासाद्य
ससुराः समरुद्गणाः ।
न शेकुस्समरे स्थातुम्
सुरेश्वरसमाश्रिताः ॥
No one in the court of the lord of Suras, even along with all the Suras and the hosts of the Maruts, could stand against you in battle, on tasting the power of your Astras.
5.48.4
न कश्चित्त्रिषु लोकेषु
संयुगे न गतश्रमः ।
भुजवीर्याभिगुप्तश्च
तपसा चाभिरक्षितः ।
देशकालविभागज्ञः
त्वमेव मतिसत्तमः ॥
There is no one in the three worlds who was not worn out (by you) in battle. You are a mastermind who can gauge any situation, having your own doughty arm for armor and your own Tapa for a shield.
5.48.5
न तेऽस्त्यशक्यं समरेषु कर्मणा
न तेऽस्त्यकार्यं मतिपूर्वमन्त्रणे ।
न सोऽस्ति कश्चित्त्रिषु सङ्ग्रहेषु वै
न वेद यस्तेऽस्त्रबलं बलं च ते ॥
There is nothing in the matters of warfare that can be a challenge for your abilities. For that matter, there is nothing that you cannot accomplish when you think through and lay out a strategy. There is no one in the three worlds who do not know your strength or the power of your Astras.
5.48.6
ममानुरूपं तपसो बलं च ते
पराक्रमश्चास्त्रबलं च संयुगे ।
न त्वां समासाद्य रणावमर्दे
मनश्श्रमं गच्छति निश्चितार्थम् ॥
In strength and Tapa, you are an equal to me, and so are you in your prowess and in the power of Astras in battle. My mind is freed from stress, assured that the mission will be accomplished, when you are in charge in the fray.
5.48.7
निहताः किङ्करास्सर्वे
जम्बुमाली च राक्षसः ।
अमात्यपुत्रा वीराश्च
पञ्च सेनाग्रयायिनः ।
बलानि सुसमृद्धानि
साश्वनागरथानि च ॥
All the Kiṅkaras have been killed, so too the Rākshasa Jambumāli, the valiant sons of ministers and the five army chiefs, and the splendidly equipped armies with the full complement of cavalry, elephantry and chariotry.
5.48.8
सहोदरस्ते दयितः
कुमारोऽक्षश्च सूदितः ।
न हि तेष्वेव मे सारो
यस्त्वय्यरिनिषूदन ॥
Your beloved brother, the young Aksha, also has been killed. O destroyer of the foe, I did not have as much confidence in any one of them, as I have in you!
5.48.9
इदं हि दृष्ट्वा मतिमन्महद्बलं
कपेः प्रभावं च पराक्रमं च ।
त्वमात्मनश्चापि समीक्ष्य सारं
कुरुष्व वेगं स्वबलानुरूपम् ॥
Keeping in mind the power and prowess of the Vānara and also your own grit, O astute one, attack with your full strength!
5.48.10
बलावमर्दस्वयि संनिकृष्टे
यथा गते शाम्यति शान्तशत्रौ ।
तथा समीक्ष्यात्मबलं परं च
समारभस्वास्त्रविदां वरिष्ठ ॥
Your involvement (in the fight) should reduce the clamor of battlefield to quietude with the enemy subdued and gone; and so shall you, O best among the savants of Astras, plan your attack, with the full awareness of your own strength and that of the enemy as well!
5.48.11
न वीरसेना गणशोच्यवन्ति
न वज्रमादाय विशालसारम् ।
न मारुतस्यास्य गतेः प्रमाणं
न चाग्निकल्पः करणेन हन्तुम् ॥
O Veera! He, who had caused so much of grief to the hordes (of Rākshasas), cannot be won by deploying an army and not even by wielding Vajra of immense power. He is like fire, and wind is no match for him; he cannot be killed by the use of hands or fists.
5.48.12
तमेवमर्थं प्रसमीक्ष्य सम्यक्
स्वकर्मसाम्याद्धि समाहितात्मा ।
स्मरंश्च दिव्यं धनुषोऽस्त्रवीर्यं
व्रजाक्षतं कर्म समारभस्व ॥
Carefully consider things as they are. Keeping your mind steady and considering this as your own task and banking on the power of the divine Astras that emerge from your bow, please go forth on your endeavor, with nothing coming in your way.
5.48.13
न खल्वियं मतिश्श्रेष्ठा
यत्त्वां सम्प्रेषयाम्यहम् ।
इयं च राजधर्माणाम्
क्षत्रियस्य मतिर्मता ॥
Sending you like this may not be the best option, but this is what a Kshatriya is supposed to do in discharging the responsibilities of a kingdom.
5.48.14
नानाशस्त्रेषु सङ्ग्रामे
वैशारद्यमरिन्दम ।
अवश्यमेव बोद्धव्यम्
काम्यश्च विजयो रणे ॥
O subduer of foe, one (A Kshatriya) must acquire expertise in the use of every kind of weapon in fight wishing for nothing but victory in battle.
5.48.15
ततः पितुस्तद्वचनं निशम्य
प्रदक्षिणं दक्षसुतप्रभावः ।
चकार भर्तारमदीनसत्त्वो
रणाय वीरः प्रतिपन्नबुद्धिः ॥
Hearing those words of his father, the Veera, powerful like the son of Daksha, circumambulated that lord in reverence and resolved in his mind to go for the fight, with his spirit riding high.
5.48.16
ततस्तैस्स्वगणैरिष्टैः
इन्द्रजित् प्रतिपूजितः ।
युद्धोद्धतः कृतोत्साहः
सङ्ग्रामं प्रत्यपद्यत ॥
His own people who were dear to him extolling him, Indrajit went forth to the battle, irrepressibly eager for the fray.
5.48.17
श्रीमान्पद्मपलाशाक्षो
राक्षसाधिपतेस्सुतः ।
निर्जगाम महातेजाः
समुद्र इव पर्वसु ॥
The extremely powerful and richly endowed son of the lord of Rākshasas, whose eyes were like lotus petals, went forth, swelling like the ocean on a full-moon day.
5.48.18
स पक्षिराजोपमतुल्यवेगैः
व्यालैश्चतुर्भिः सिततीक्षणदंष्ट्रैः ।
रथं समायुक्तमसहयवेगम्
समारुरोहेन्द्रजिदिन्द्रकल्पः ॥
Indrajit, who was like Indra, got into a chariot whose speed nothing could check and to which were yoked four fierce beasts with sharp white fangs, all of which were as fast as the king of birds.
5.48.19
स रथी धन्विनां श्रेष्ठः
शस्त्रज्ञोस्त्रविदां वरः ।
रथेनाभिययौ क्षिप्रम्
हनुमान्यत्र सोऽभवत् ॥
That charioter, who was foremost among bowmen, well-versed in the use of weapons and best among the experts in Astras, left immediately on his chariot to the place where Hanumān was.
5.48.20
स तस्य रथनिर्घोषम्
ज्यास्वनं कार्मुकस्य च ।
निशम्य हरिवीरोऽसौ
संप्रहृष्टतरोऽभवत् ॥
The heroic Vānara was only delighted to hear the rumbling of his chariot and the twanging of his bowstring.
5.48.21
सुमहच्चापमादाय
शितशल्यांश्च सायकान् ।
हनुमन्तमभिप्रेत्य
जगाम रणपण्डितः ॥
Taking a great bow and sharp-tipped arrows, the veteran warrior went towards Hanumān.
5.48.22
तस्मिंस्ततः संयति जातहर्षे
रणाय निर्गच्छति चापपाणौ ।
दिशश्च सर्वाः कलुषा बभूवुः
मृगाश्च रौद्रा बहुधा विनेदुः ॥
When he thus went forth for a fight, with all enthusiasm and a bow in his hand, all the quarters were overcast and fierce beasts emitted harsh cries.
5.48.23
समागतास्तत्र तु नागयक्षा
महर्षयश्चक्रचराश्च सिद्धाः ।
नभस्समावृत्य च पक्षिसङ्घाः
विनेदुरुच्चैः परमप्रहृष्टाः ॥
There gathered in the sky Nāgas, Yakshas, Maharshis and Siddhas who sojourn among the celestial constellations, and hosts of birds, uttering loud and delighted cries.
5.48.24
आयान्तं सरथं दृष्ट्वा
तूर्णमिन्द्रजितं कपिः ।
विननाद महानादम्
व्यवर्धत च वेगवान् ॥
Seeing Indrajit coming along swiftly in his chariot, the swift Vānara let out a great shout and started increasing in size.
5.48.25
इन्द्रजित्तु रथं दिव्यम्
आस्थितश्चित्रकार्मुकः ।
धनुर्विष्फारयामास
तटिदूर्जितन्निस्स्वनम् ॥
In response, Indrajit, seated in his splendid chariot and holding a stylish bow, twanged the bowstring, raising a din like thunder.
5.48.26
ततस्समेतावतितीक्ष्णवेगौ
महाबलौ तौ रणनिर्विशङ्कौ ।
कपिश्च रक्षोधिपतेस्तनूजः
सुरासुरेन्द्राविव बद्धवैरौ ॥
The Vānara and the son of the Rākshasa King met, both possessed of terrible speed and immense strength, each with the least doubt about winning the battle, locked in enmity like Suras and Asuras.
5.48.27
स तस्य वीरस्य महारथस्य
धनुष्मतः संयति सम्मतस्य ।
शरप्रवेगं व्यहनत्प्रवृद्धः
चचार मार्गे पितुरप्रमेयः ॥
Swelling to a huge size, the Vānara of unimaginable (strength) moved with ease in the track of his father making waste of the speed of the arrows of that bow-wielding Veera, a super-charioter, who was well-regarded in battle.
5.48.28
ततश्शरानायततीक्ष्णशल्यान्
सुपत्रिणः काञ्चनचित्रपुङ्खान् ।
मुमोच वीरः परवीरहन्ताः
सुसन्नतान् वज्रनिपातवेगान् ॥
Then that Veera, a slayer of the foe, shot long, shapely, and sharp-tipped arrows that were decorated with beautiful feathers and splendid golden rings, which fell swiftly with the impact of the thunderbolt.
5.48.29
ततस्तु तत्स्यन्दननिस्स्वनं च
मृदङ्गभेरीपटहस्वनं च ।
विकृष्यमाणस्य च कार्मुकस्य
निशम्य घोषं पुनरुत्पपात ॥
Hearing the sound of the rumble of the chariot, the din of Mṛdaṅga, kettle drum and Paṭaha and the twang of bowstring as it was drawn, the Vānara sprang up again.
This Ṡlōka seems to be out of place, possibly due to a shuffle of the palm leaves over the course of time. This Ṡlōka would flow well if it is placed between the 20th and 21st Ṡlōkas.
5.48.30
शराणामन्तरेष्वाशु
व्यवर्तत महाकपिः ।
हरिस्तस्याभिलक्ष्यस्य
मोघयन्लक्ष्यसंग्रहम् ॥
Quickly gliding in between the arrows, the great Vānara made those shots of his, who was very good at his aim, fail in hitting their target.
5.48.31
शराणामग्रतस्तस्य
पुनः समभिवर्तत ।
प्रसार्य हस्तौ हनुमान्
उत्पपातानिलात्मजः ॥
Then again, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, placed himself straight in the track of the arrows, only to spring again the next moment, stretching his arms.
5.48.32
तावुभौ वेगसम्पन्नौ
रणकर्मविशारदौ ।
सर्वभूतमनोग्राहि
चक्रतुर्युद्धमुत्तमम् ॥
The two, possessed of terrific swiftness, and expert in various feats of war, fought a splendid fight stealing the hearts of all creatures.
5.48.33
हनुमतो वेद न राक्षसोऽन्तरं
न मारुतिस्तस्य महात्मनोऽन्तरम् ।
परस्परं निर्विषहौ बभूवतुः
समेत्य तौ देवसमानविक्रमौ ॥
The Rākshasa could not discover a way to strike Hanumān, neither could the son of Vāyu, to strike that Mahātma. Each one of them, equal to Dēvas in prowess, was unable to take on the other, as they clashed.
5.48.34
ततस्तु लक्ष्ये स विहन्यमाने
शरेष्वमोघेषु च संपतत्सु ।
जगाम चिन्तां महतीं महात्मा
समाधिसंयोगसमाहितात्मा ॥
When he saw that his unfailing arrows were falling off, failing to hit the target, that Mahātma, who had a steady mind and was good at aiming and striking the target, did some serious thinking.
5.48.35
ततो मतिं राक्षसराजसूनुः
चकार तस्मिन् हरिवीरमुख्ये ।
अवध्यतां तस्य कपेस्समीक्ष्य
कथं निगच्छेदिति निग्रहार्थम् ॥
Realizing that it was not possible to kill the Vānara, the son of the Rākshasa King thought of what best he could do to subdue and take that chief of the Vānara Veeras captive.
5.48.36
ततः पैतामहं वीरः
सोऽस्त्रमस्त्रविदां वरः ।
संदधे सुमहातेजाः
तं हरिप्रवरं प्रति ॥
Then that Veera of immense power, who was the best among the savants of Astras, launched the Grandsire's Astra, towards the best of the Vānara Veeras.
5.48.37
अवध्योऽयमिति ज्ञात्वा
तमस्त्रेणास्त्रतत्त्ववित् ।
निजग्राह महाबाहुः
मारुतात्मजमिन्द्रजित् ॥
Realizing that he cannot be killed, Indrajit of mighty arm, a savant of Astras, restrained the son of Vāyu with an Astra.
This Ṡlōka which conveys, in brief, the same meaning as the previous two Ṡlōkas, 35 and 36, could be a case of poetic repetition or could be due to the process of compilation that called for retaining slight variations from two different sources of palm leaves.
5.48.38
तेन बद्धस्ततोऽस्त्रेण
राक्षसेन स वानरः ।
अभवन्निर्विचेष्टश्च
पपात स महीतले ॥
Thus bound by the Astra of that Rākshasa, the Vānara became motionless and fell down on the ground.
5.48.39
ततोऽथ बुद्ध्वा स तदस्त्रबन्धम्
प्रभोः प्रभावाद्विगतात्मवेगः ।
पितामहानुग्रहमात्मनश्च
विचिन्तयामास हरिप्रवीरः ॥
Realizing that he was bound by that Astra and that his speed was lost because of the power of that Lord (the Grandsire), that best among the Vānara Veeras thought of the special favor the Grandsire had bestowed on him.
5.48.40
ततस्स्वायम्बुवैर्मन्त्रैः
ब्रह्मास्त्रमभिमन्त्रितम् ।
हनुमांश्चिन्तयामास
वरदानं पितामहात् ॥
Realizing that the power of Self-born had been invoked with the appropriate Mantras when that Astra of Brahma was cast upon him, Hanumān remembered the boon granted to him by the Grandsire.
5.48.41
न मेऽस्त्रबन्धस्य च शक्तिरस्ति
विमोक्षणे लोकगुरोः प्रभावात् ।
इत्येव मत्वा विहितोऽस्त्रबन्धो
मयात्मयोनेरनुवर्तितव्यः ॥
I do not have the ability to free myself from the bond of this Astra because of the power of the Eldest of the Lōkas (Brahma). This I have to acknowledge and I have no option but to be bound by this Astra of Self-born, cast by the enemy.
5.48.42
स वीर्यमस्त्रस्य कपिर्विचार्य
पितामहानुग्रहमात्मनश्च ।
विमोक्षशक्तिं परिचिन्तयित्वा
पितामहाज्ञामनुवर्तते स्म ॥
Realizing the power of the Astra, and keeping in mind how he could (eventually) free himself from it using the special favor that the Grandsire had bestowed on him, the Vānara allowed himself to be bound by the commandment of the Grandsire (for the time being).
5.48.43
अस्त्रेणापि हि बद्धस्य
भयं मम न जायते ।
पितामहमहेन्द्राभ्याम्
रक्षितस्यानिलेन च ॥
I am bound by the Astra, but it is no cause of concern for me, as I am being protected by the Grandsire, Mahēndra and the God of Wind.
5.48.44
ग्रहणे चापि रक्षोभिः
महन्मे गुणदर्शनम् ।
राक्षसेन्द्रेण संवादः
तस्माद्गृह्णन्तु मां परे ॥
(In fact), I can see a great advantage in being captured by the Rākshasas. I might talk to the King of Rākshasas. So let the enemy capture me.
5.48.45
स निश्चितार्थः परवीरहन्ता
समीक्ष्यकारी विनिवृत्तचेष्टः ।
परैः प्रसह्याभिगतैर्निगृह्य
ननाद तैस्तै: परिभर्त्स्यमानः ॥
Having thus resolved, he, the slayer of enemy Veeras, who gauged the situation well, lay motionless.
He let off a loud cry, when the enemy approached and forcefully restrained him, even as everyone kept threatening him.
5.48.46
ततस्तं राक्षसा दृष्ट्वा
निर्विचेष्टमरिंदमम् ।
बबन्धुश्शणवल्कैश्च
द्रुमचीरैश्च संहतैः ॥
When the Rākshasas found that subduer of foe lying down there with no reaction whatsoever, they tied him with hempen rope and twisted tree-bark.
5.48.47
स रोचयामास परैश्च बन्धनम्
प्रसह्य वीरैरभिनिग्रहं च ।
कौतूहलान्मां यदि राक्षसेन्द्रो
द्रष्टुं व्यवस्येदिति निश्चितार्थः ॥
He actually liked getting captured by the enemy and being restrained by the Veeras forcibly because he felt certain that the Lord of Rākshasas would give audience to him out of curiosity.
5.48.48
स बद्धस्तेन वल्केन
विमुक्तोऽस्त्रेण वीर्यवान् ।
अस्त्रबन्धः स चान्यं हि
न बन्धमनुवर्तते ॥
The valiant one, being tied by the hempen cord, became free of the Astra, for the bond of Astra becomes ineffective when another bond is imposed upon it.
5.48.49
अथेन्द्रजित्तु द्रुमचीरबद्धम्
विचार्य वीरः कपिसत्तमं तम् ।
विमुक्तमस्त्रेण जगाम चिन्ताम्
नान्येन बद्धो ह्यनुवर्ततेऽस्त्रम् ॥
Then Indrajit, the Veera, knowing that the Astra would become ineffective should another bond be imposed upon it, realized that the eminent Vānara, who was bound by the ropes of tree bark, became free from the hold of the Astra.
5.48.50
अहो महत्कर्म कृतं निरर्थकम्कम्
न राक्षसैर्मन्त्रगतिर्विमृष्टा ।
पुनश्च मन्त्रे विहतेऽस्त्रमन्यत्
प्रवर्तते संशयिता स्स्मसर्वे ॥
Alas! A great accomplishment has been rendered futile; the Rākshasas did not understand how the Mantra works. Once rendered futile, a Mantra will not work again; we are all in a bind now, what to do?
5.48.51
अस्त्रेण हनुमान्मुक्तो
नात्मानमवबुध्यत ।
कृष्यमाणस्तु रक्षोभिः
तैश्च बन्धैर्निपीडितः ॥
However, Hanumān, dragged by the Rākshasas and hurt by the tight bonds, did not seem to know that he had became free from the bond of the Astra.
5.48.52
हन्यमानस्ततः क्रूरै
राक्षसैः काष्ठमुष्टिभिः ।
समीपं राक्षसेन्द्रस्य
प्राकृष्यत स वानरः ॥
The Vānara was beaten with sticks and fists and dragged by those ruthless Rākshasas to the presence of their lord.
5.48.53
अथेन्द्रजित्तं प्रसमीक्ष्य मुक्तम्
अस्त्रेण बद्धं द्रुमचीरसूत्रैः ।
न्यदर्शयत्तत्र महाबलं तं
हरिप्रवीरं सगणाय राज्ञे ॥
Then Indrajit showed that eminent Vānara, a Veera of immense strength, to the King and his Court knowing well that he was free from the bond of the Astra and was held only by the strands of tree bark.
5.48.54
तं मत्तमिव मातङ्गम्
बद्धं कपिवरोत्तमम् ।
राक्षसा राक्षसेन्द्राय
रावणाय न्यवेदयन् ॥
The Rākshasas showed that best of the best of Vānaras who looked like an elephant in rut kept under harness to Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas.
5.48.55
कोऽयं कस्य कुतोवात्र
किं कार्यं को व्यपाश्रयः ।
इति राक्षसवीराणाम्
तत्र सञ्जज्ञिरे कथाः ॥
The Rākshasa Veeras present there wondered asking each other: Who is he? Whom does he belong to? Where did he come from and for what purpose? Who is behind him?
5.48.56
हन्यतां दह्यतां वापि
भक्ष्यतामिति चापरे ।
राक्षसास्तत्र सङ्क्रुद्धाः
परस्परमथाब्रुवन् ॥
The enraged Rākshasas there said to each other: He should be killed, burnt and eaten up!
5.48.57
अतीत्य मार्गं सहसा महात्मा
स तत्र रक्षोधिपपादमूले ।
ददर्श राज्ञः परिचारवृद्धान्
गृहं महारत्नविभूषितं च ॥
Quickly covering the distance, the Mahātma saw the King’s house that was studded with gems and elderly attendants seated at the feet of the lord of Rākshasas.
5.48.58
स ददर्श महातेजा
रावणः कपिसत्तमम् ।
रक्षोभिर्विकृताकारैः
कृष्यमाणमितस्ततः ॥
The extremely powerful Rāvaṇa then saw that eminent Vānara being dragged haphazardly by the Rākshasas of ugly shape and form.
5.48.59
राक्षसाधिपतिं चापि
ददर्श कपिसत्तमः ।
तेजोबलसमायुक्तम्
तपन्तमिव भास्करम् ॥
The eminent Vānara also saw the lord of Rākshasas endowed with power and strength and blazing like the sun.
5.48.60
सरोषसम्वर्तितताम्रदृष्टिः
दशाननस्तं कपिमन्ववेक्ष्य ।
अथोपविष्टान् कुलशीलवृद्धान्
समादिशत्तं प्रति मन्त्रिमुख्यान् ॥
With his copper-red eyes rolling in resentment and anger, the Ten-faced looked at the Vānara. He ordered his chief ministers, the elders of his clan of matured character that were seated about him, to question him.
5.48.61
यथाक्रमं तैस्स कपिर्विपृष्टः
कार्यार्थमर्थस्य च मूलमादौ ।
निवेदयामास हरीश्वरस्य
दूतः सकाशादहमागतोऽस्मि ॥
Questioned by them in successive order about the purpose of his coming and the main reason behind it, the Vānara said at first, ‘I have come here sent by the Lord of Vānaras, as his envoy’.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे अष्टचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty eighth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
