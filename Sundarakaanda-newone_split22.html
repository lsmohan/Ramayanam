Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 22
In this Sarga, Rāvaṇa tells Seetā that it is because of his love for her that he is not able to harm her and that he could have easily killed her. He reminds her that she has only two more months before she yields to him and goes to bed with him, failing which she would be chopped in the kitchen and served as breakfast for him.
While Seetā is thus being threatened, the Dēva and Gandharva women who are with Rāvaṇa empathize with her through their facial expressions. Finding comfort in their support, Seetā chides Rāvaṇa again and wonders why there is no one who could give proper advice to him. She warns him that he had invited his own death, by abducting her. She insults him by pointing to his cowardly act of stealing of her while Rāma and Lakshmaṇa were away. She tells him that he could hope to stay alive only as long as he doesn’t fall in the line of sight of Rāma.
Enraged, Rāvaṇa instructs the Rākshasis guarding Seetā to subdue her with temptations as well as threats. Meanwhile one of his lovers, Dhānyamālini, comes up to him and implores him to enjoy sporting with her and disregard the pale and miserable Seetā, who is a mere human. She reflects that Seetā, after all, seems not destined to enjoy his riches.
His ego boosted by those words, Rāvaṇa leaves.
5.22.1
सीताया वचनं श्रुत्वा
परुषं राक्षसाधिपः ।
प्रत्युवाच ततः सीताम्
विप्रियं प्रियदर्शनाम् ॥
Hearing those harsh words of Seetā, the lord of Rākshasas then said these very unpleasant words, to the one who looked so pleasant:
5.22.2
यथा यथा सान्त्वयिता
वश्यः स्त्रीणां तथा तथा ।
यथा यथा प्रियं वक्ता
परिभूतस्तथा तथा ॥
The more a man tries to pacify women, the more they take him for granted. The more a man tries to please them, the more they scorn him.
5.22.3
सन्नियच्छति मे क्रोधम्
त्वयि कामः समुत्थितः ।
द्रवतोऽमार्गमासाद्य
हयानिव सुसारथिः ॥
Like a good charioteer controlling runaway horses, my desire for you is restraining my anger.
5.22.4
वामः कामो मनुष्याणाम्
यस्मिन् किल निबध्यते ।
जने तस्मिंस्त्वनुक्रोशः
स्नेहश्च किल जायते ॥
Desire, for men, is a weakness; for they develop affection and a soft corner towards the very same person who ties them down.
5.22.5
एतस्मात्कारणान्न त्वाम्
घातयामि वरानने ।
वधार्हामवमानार्हाम्
मिथ्याप्रव्रजिते रताम् ॥
That is why I cannot bring myself to hurt you, O you of lovely face, even though you deserve to be humiliated and killed for still being interested in the one who pretends to lead a holy life in exile!
5.22.6
परुषाणीह वाक्यानि
यानि यानि ब्रवीषि माम् ।
तेषु तेषु वधो युक्तः
तव मैथिलि दारुणः ॥
O Maithili! You deserve to be killed mercilessly and repeatedly for each and every one of the harsh words you said to me.
5.22.7
एवमुक्त्वा तु वैदेहीम्
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ।
क्रोधसंरम्भसंयुक्तः
सीतामुत्तरमब्रवीत् ॥
After saying these words to Vaidēhee, Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, taken over by raging anger, said a few more words:
5.22.8
द्वौ मासौ रक्षितव्यौ मे
योऽवधिस्ते मया कृतः ।
ततः शयनमारोह
मम त्वं वरवर्णिनि ॥
I shall respect the respite I have granted you and wait for two months more. You will, then, go to bed with me, O you of lovely complexion!
5.22.9
ऊर्ध्वं द्वाभ्यां तु मासाभ्याम्
भर्तारं मामनिच्छतीम् ।
मम त्वां प्रातराशार्थम्
आलभन्ते महानसे ॥
If you do not like to accept me as your husband even after the passing of two months, you will be chopped in the kitchen for my breakfast.
5.22.10
तां तर्ज्यमानां सम्प्रेक्ष्य
राक्षसेन्द्रेण जानकीम् ।
देवगन्धर्वकन्यास्ताः
विषेदुर्विकृतेक्षणाः ॥
Seeing Jānaki thus threatened by the lord of Rākshasas, the Dēva and Gandharva women (who accompanied Rāvaṇa) felt sad and rolled their eyes.
5.22.11
ओष्ठप्रकारैरपरा
वक्त्रनेत्रैस्तथापराः ।
सीतामाश्वासयामासुः
तर्जितां तेन रक्षसा ॥
Some with the (silent) movement of their lips and others with expressions on their faces and in their eyes, tried to comfort Seetā, as she was thus being threatened by the Rākshasa.
5.22.12
ताभिराश्वासिता सीता
रावणं राक्षसाधिपम् ।
उवाचात्महितं वाक्यम्
वृत्तशौण्डीर्यगर्वितम् ॥
Comforted by them, Seetā said these poignant and spirited words that betrayed the strength of her character, to the lord of Rākshasas:
5.22.13
नूनं न ते जनः कश्चित्
अस्ति निःश्रेयसे रतः ।
निवारयति यो न त्वाम्
कर्मणोऽस्माद्विगर्हितात् ॥
There certainly seems to be no one interested enough in your well-being here to dissuade you from this reproachable course.
5.22.14
मां हि धर्मात्मनः पत्नीम्
शचीमिव शचीपतेः ।
त्वदन्यस्त्रिषु लोकेषु
प्रार्थयेन्मनसापि कः ॥
Even the thought of desiring me, the wife of a Dharmātma, would not cross anyone’s mind other than yours, like that of desiring Ṡaci, the wife of Indra.
5.22.15
राक्षसाधम रामस्य
भार्याममिततेजसः ।
उक्तवानसि यत्पापम्
क्व गतस्तस्य मोक्ष्यसे ॥
O base Rākshasa! Where can you escape to, having said these sinful words to the wife of Rāma, of immense power?
5.22.16
यथा दृप्तश्च मातङ्गः
शशश्च सद्दृशो युधि ।
तथा मातङ्गवद्रामः
त्वं नीचः शशवत् स्मृतः ॥
It would be like a proud elephant engaged in a fight with a hare! Think of Rāma as the elephant and you, base creature, as the hare!
5.22.17
स त्वमिक्ष्वाकुनाथं वै
क्षिपन्निह न लज्जसे ।
चक्षुषोर्विषयं तस्य
न तावदुपगच्छसि ॥
Are you not ashamed of ridiculing the Lord of Ikshwākus, by remaining here and avoiding falling in his line of sight!
5.22.18
इमे ते नयने क्रूरे
विरूपे कृष्णपिङ्गले ।
क्षितौ न पतिते कस्मात्
मामनार्य निरीक्षितः ॥
O ignoble one! I cannot understand why those ugly black and yellow eyes of yours do not fall off even as you stare at me.
5.22.19
तस्य धर्मात्मनः पत्नीम्
स्नुषां दशरथस्य च ।
कथं व्याहरतो मां ते
न जिह्वा व्यवशीर्यते ॥
And why does your tongue not fall off, when you address me, the wife of that Dharmātma and daughter-in-law of Daṡaratha, like this?
5.22.20
असन्देशात्तु रामस्य
तपसश्चानुपालनात् ।
न त्वां कुर्मि दशग्रीव
भस्म भर्मार्ह तेजसा ॥
You deserve to be reduced to ashes through my power of Tapa, but I refrain from doing so, O Ten-necked, because I abide by the precepts of Tapa and because I do not have the permission of Rāma.
5.22.21
नापहर्तुमहं शक्या
त्वया रामस्य धीमतः ।
विधिस्तव वधार्थाय
विहितो नात्र संशयः ॥
You could possibly not have been able to abduct me, who belongs to the sagacious Rāma; Providence must have, without a doubt, arranged so to bring death upon you.
5.22.22
शूरेण धनदभ्रात्रा
बलैः समुदितेन च ।
अपोह्य रामं कस्माद्धि
दारचौर्यं त्वया कृतम् ॥
Otherwise, why would you, a man of valor, brother of Kubēra, with large forces (under your command), resort to getting Rāma out of the way and stealing his wife?
5.22.23
सीताया वचनं श्रुत्वा
रावणो राक्षसाधिपः ।
विवृत्य नयने क्रूरे
जानकीमन्ववैक्षत ॥
Hearing those words of Seetā, the daughter of Janaka, Rāvaṇa, the lord of the Rākshasas, stared at her, rolling his cruel eyes.
5.22.24
नीलजीमूतसङ्काशो
महाभुजशिरोधरः ।
सिंहसत्त्वगतिः श्रीमान्
दीप्त जिह्वाग्रलोचनः ॥
Then, Rāvaṇa, the lord of Rākshasas, looking like a huge black cloud, with mighty shoulders, burly neck, lion’s gait, evident opulence, flaming eyes and tip of the tongue,
The Ṡlōkas 24 to 30 form a single sentence. Translation demands some words to be rearranged to conform to sentence structure of English language. For that reason, the word Rāvaṇa taken from Ṡlōka 30, and the phrase, ‘lord of Rākshasas’ taken from Ṡlōka 27 are used here.
5.22.25
चलाग्रमकुटप्रांशुः
चित्रमाल्यानुलेपनः ।
रक्तमाल्याम्बरधरः
तप्ताङ्गदविभूषणः  ॥
with the top of his crown shaking, with exotic garlands and creams on his body, clad in a red garment and adorned with ornaments made of purified gold,
5.22.26
श्रोणिसूत्रेण महता
मेचकेन सुसंवृतः ।
अमृतोत्पादनद्धेन
भुजगेनेव मन्दरः ॥
the black band tied around his waist looking like the serpent roped around Mandara mountain to extract Amṛt.
5.22.27
ताभ्यां स परिपूर्णाभ्याम्
भुजाभ्यां राक्षसेश्वरः ।
शुशुभेऽचलसङ्काशः
शृङ्गाभ्यामिव मन्दरः ॥
imposing like Mandara mountain with his two strong and mighty arms looking like two peaks,
5.22.28
तरुणादित्यवर्णाभ्याम्
कुण्डलाभ्यां विभूषितः ।
रक्तपल्लवपुष्पाभ्याम्
अशोकाभ्यामिवाचलः ॥
adorned with ear-pendants that shone like the morning sun, looking like a mountain with Aṡōka trees bearing red tender shoots and flowers,
5.22.29
स कल्पवृक्षप्रतिमो
वसन्त इव मूर्तिमान् ।
श्मशानचैत्यप्रतिमो
भूषितोऽपि भयङ्करः ॥
resembling the great wishing tree, looking like an embodiment of spring, adorned well but terrifying like a shrine in the cremation grounds,
5.22.30
अवेक्षमाणो वैदेहीम्
कोपसंरक्तलोचनः ।
उवाच रावणः सीताम्
भुजङ्ग इव निःश्वसन् ॥
stared at Vaidēhee with his blood-red eyes, and said these words to Seetā, hissing like a serpent:
The sentence that started in Ṡlōka 24 ends here with this Ṡlōka.
5.22.31
अनयेनाभिसम्पन्नम्
अर्थहीनमनुव्रते ।
नाशयाम्यहमद्य त्वाम्
सूर्यः सन्ध्यामिवौजसा ॥
You are cleaving to someone who seems to have everything sans wealth. I will destroy you now as easily as the sun dispels (darkness of the) dawn by his brightness.
5.22.32
इत्युक्त्वा मैथिलीं राजा
रावणः शत्रुरावणः ।
सन्दिदेश ततः सर्वा
राक्षसीर्घोरदर्शनाः ।
एकाक्षीमेककर्णां च
कर्णप्रावरणां तथा ।
गोकर्णीं हस्तिकर्णीं च
लम्बकर्णीमकर्णिकाम् ।
हस्तिपाद्यश्वपाद्यौ च
गोपादीं पादचूलिकाम् ।
एकाक्षीमेकपादीं च
पृथुपादीमपादिकाम् ।
अतिमात्रशिरोग्रीवाम्
अतिमात्रकुचोदरीम् ।
अतिमात्रास्यनेत्रां च
दीर्घजिह्वामजिह्विकाम् ।
अनासिकां सिंहमुखीम्
गोमुखीं सूकरीमुखीम् ॥
Saying this to Maithili, the king Rāvaṇa, who made his enemies wail, instructed all the Rākshasis there who looked horrid, one with a single eye, one with a single ear, one with ears large enough to cover her body, one with ears that looked like those of a cow and another with ears that looked like those of an elephant, one with sagging ears and another with no ears at all, one with feet that looked like those of an elephant and another with feet that looked like those of a horse, yet another with feet that looked like those of a cow, one with hair on her feet and one with only one leg, one with huge feet and one with no feet at all, one with humongous head and humongous neck, one with humongous breasts and humongous belly, one with humongous face and humongous eyes, one with a hanging tongue and another with no tongue, one with a face that looked like that of a lion, another with a face like that of a cow, yet another with a face like that of a pig, and another with no nose at all.
5.22.36
यथा मद्वशगा सीता
क्षिप्रं भवति जानकी ।
तथा कुरुत राक्षस्यः
सर्वाः क्षिप्रं समेत्य च ॥
O Rākshasis! All of you get together and do whatever it takes to make Seetā, the daughter of Janaka yield to me with no more delay.
5.22.37
प्रतिलोमानुलोमैश्च
सामदानादिभेदनैः ।
आवर्जयत वैदेहीम्
दण्डस्योद्यमनेन च ॥
Tame Vaidēhee, be friendly (with her) at times and tough at others. Employ all the (four) techniques, Sāma, Dāna, Bhēda as well as Daṇḍa.
5.22.38
इति प्रतिसमादिश्य
राक्षसेन्द्रः पुनः पुनः ।
काममन्युपरीतात्मा
जानकीं पर्यतर्जयत् ॥
Instructing thus repeatedly, the lord of Rākshasas threatened Jānaki, consumed by passion and anger.
5.22.39
उपगम्य ततः शीघ्रम्
राक्षसी धान्यमालिनी ।
परिष्वज्य दशग्रीवम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Then a Rākshasi, Dhānyamālini, came up to the Ten-necked without losing time and said these words, hugging him:
5.22.40
मया क्रीड महाराज
सीतया किं तवानया ।
विवर्णया कृपणया
मानुष्या राक्षसेश्वर ॥
O great king! Sport with me, why bother about this pale and miserable Seetā, a mere human!
5.22.41
नूनमस्या महाराज
न दिव्यान् भोगसत्तमान् ।
विदधात्यमरश्रेष्ठः
तव बाहुबलार्जितान् ॥
O great king! It is clear that (Lord Brahma), the supreme among the Dēvas, did not destine her for the enjoyment of the splendid luxuries that you have won with the strength of your very own arms.
5.22.42
अकामां कामयानस्य
शरीरमुपतप्यते ।
इच्छन्तीं कामयानस्य
प्रीतिर्भवति शोभना ॥
To be in love with a woman who is not loving, hurts even the body. It is beautiful and enjoyable to love only the woman who is interested.
5.22.43
एवमुक्तस्तु राक्षस्या
समुत्क्षिप्तस्ततो बली ।
प्रहसन्मेघसङ्काशो
राक्षसः स न्यवर्तत ॥
With his ego thus boosted by those words of the Rākshasi, the mighty strong Rākshasa, who was like a huge cloud, smiled and left.
5.22.44
प्रस्थितः स दशग्रीवः
कम्पयन्निव मेदिनीम् ।
ज्वलद्भास्करवर्णाभम्
प्रविवेश निवेशनम् ॥
His powerful strides seeming to cause tremors on the earth, the Ten-necked then left and entered his residence that shone bright like the dazzling sun.
5.22.45
देवगन्धर्वकन्याश्च
नागकन्याश्च सर्वतः ।
परिवार्य दशग्रीवम्
विविशुस्तं गृहोत्तमम् ॥
The Dēva, Gandharva and Nāga women who were surrounding the Ten-necked, also entered that splendid residence.
5.22.46
स मैथिलीं धर्मपरामवस्थितां
प्रपेपमानां परिभर्त्स्य रावणः ।
विहाय सीतां मदनेन मोहितः
स्वमेव वेश्म प्रविवेश भास्वरम् ॥
Having thus threatened the trembling Maithili who remained bound to Dharma, Rāvaṇa, who was overpowered by passion, left Seetā and entered his dazzling residence.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे द्वाविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty second Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
