Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 70
In this Sarga, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa cut off the arms of Kabandha, who is about to swallow them. Then the subdued Kabandha asks who they are. Lakshmaṇa tells about Rāma, about his exile and about the abduction of Seetā. Kabandha gets delighted and says that it is his good fortune that he sees them and had his hands cut off by them.
3.70.1
तौ तु तत्र स्थितौ दृष्ट्वा
भ्रातरौ रामलक्ष्मणौ ।
बाहुपाशपरिक्षिप्तौ
कबन्धो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
Looking at the brothers Rāma and Lakshmaṇa who were standing there thus caught in the noose of his arms, Kabandha said these words:
3.70.2
तिष्ठतः किं नु मां दृष्ट्वा
क्षुधार्तं क्षत्रियर्षभौ ।
आहारार्थं तु सन्दिष्टौ
दैवेन गतचेतसौ ॥
Why do you, standing there, your minds lost, stare at me who is hungry? You, bulls of Kshatriyas, are sent by the gods as food.
3.70.3
तच्छ्रुत्वा लक्ष्मणो वाक्यम्
प्राप्तकालं हितं तदा ।
उवाचाऽर्तिं समापन्नो
विक्रमे कृतलक्षणः ॥
Hearing those words, feeling very concerned and summoning his courage, Lakshmaṇa spoke the best that can be done at that moment:
3.70.4
त्वां च मां च पुरा तूर्णम्
आदत्ते राक्षसाधमः ।
तस्मादसिभ्यामस्याशु
बाहू छिन्दावहै गुरू ॥
This base Rākshasa can grab us in no time. Therefore, O revered one, let us hack off his arms right away with our swords!
3.70.5
भीषणोऽयं महाकायो
राक्षसो भुजविक्रमः ।
लोकं ह्यतिजितं कृत्वा
ह्यावां हन्तुमिहेच्छति ॥
This frightful and monstrous Rākshasa who has overcome every one who came his way with the strength of his arms, wants to kill us now!
3.70.6
निश्चेष्टानां वधो राजन्
कुत्सितो जगतीपतेः ।
क्रतुमध्योपनीतानाम्
पशूनामिव राघव ॥
O King! O Rāghava! Killing of creatures that cannot move is condemned for a king, as it is of an animal brought to the Yajña grounds!
3.70.7
एतत्सञ्जल्पितं श्रुत्वा
तयोः क्रुद्धस्तु राक्षसः ।
विदार्याऽस्यं तदा रौद्रः
तौ भक्षयितुमारभत् ॥
Enraged at what he heard them say to each other, the Rākshasa opened his cruel mouth wide to swallow them.
3.70.8
ततस्तौ देशकालज्ञौ
खड्गाभ्यामेव राघवौ ।
अच्छिन्दतां सुसंहृष्टौ
बाहू तस्यांसदेशतः ॥
The two, who knew the right timing and right place, cut off his arms at the shoulders with their swords, taking delight in doing so.
3.70.9
दक्षिणो दक्षिणं बाहुम्
असक्तमसिना ततः ।
चिच्छेद रामो वेगेन
सव्यं वीरस्तु लक्ष्मणः ॥
The dexterous Rāma, unhindered, hacked off the right arm swiftly, and valiant Lakshmaṇa, for his part, hewed down the left arm as quickly.
3.70.10
स पपात महाबाहुः
छिन्नबाहुर्महास्वनः ।
खं च गां च दिशश्चैव
नादयञ्जलदो यथा ॥
With his arms severed, he of giant arms fell down, with the earth, the sky and the four quarters reverberating with his loud cry like the rumble of a thunderous cloud.
3.70.11
स निकृत्तौ भुजौ दृष्ट्वा
शोणितौघपरिप्लुतः ।
दीनः पप्रच्छ तौ वीरौ
कौ युवामिति दानवः ॥
Then that Dānava, seeing that his arms were cut off and were drenched in a deluge of blood, asked the two Veeras in a pitiful voice: ‘Who are you two?’
3.70.12
इति तस्य ब्रुवाणस्य
लक्ष्मणश्शुभलक्षणः ।
शशंस राघवं तस्य
कबन्धस्य महात्मनः ॥
To that mighty Kabandha who was asking thus, Lakshmaṇa of auspicious marks told about Rāghava:
3.70.13
अयमिक्ष्वाकुदायादो
रामो नाम जनैश्श्रुतः ।
अस्यैवावरजं विद्दि
भ्रातरं मां च लक्ष्मणम् ॥
He is born into the dynasty of Ikshwākus. He is known among people as Rāma. I am his younger brother, know me as Lakshmaṇa!
3.70.14
मात्रा प्रतिहतो राज्ये
रामः प्रवाजितो वनम् ।
मया सह चरत्येष
भार्यया च महत् वनम् ॥
With his kingdom robbed by his (step) mother, he came to Vana in exile, living along with me and his wife in the great Vana.
3.70.15
अस्य देवप्रभावस्य
वसतो विजने वने ।
रक्षसाऽपहृता पत्नी
यामिच्छन्ताविहागतौ ॥
While he who, is equal to Dēvas in prowess, is living in this desolate forest, his wife was abducted by a Rākshasa, seeking whom we treaded this way.
3.70.16
त्वं तु को वा किमर्थं वा
कबन्धसदृशो वने ।
आस्येनोरसि दीप्तेन
भग्नजङ्घो विचेष्टसे ॥
But who are you? And why do you, all but a trunk with broken legs with your fiery face on your belly, lie here in the forest, rolling?
3.70.17
एवमुक्तः कबन्धस्तु
लक्ष्मणेनोत्तरं वचः ।
उवाच परमप्रीतः
तदिन्द्रवचनं स्मरन् ॥
Thus asked by Lakshmaṇa, the extremely joyful Kabandha replied, recollecting the words of Indra:
3.70.18
स्वागतं वां नरव्याघ्रौ
दिष्ट्या पश्यामि चाप्यहम् ।
दिष्ट्या चेमौ निकृत्तौ मे
युवाभ्यां बाहुबन्धनौ ॥
O tigers among men! Welcome! It is by my good fortune that I see you! It is because of luck that these arms that were fetters were severed by you!
3.70.19
विरूपं यच्च मे रूपम्
प्राप्तं ह्यविनयाद्यथा ।
तन्मे शृणु नरव्याघ्र
तत्त्वतश्शंसतस्तव ॥
Listen, O tiger among men, I shall tell you in all detail how this form, a crooked one that is, has come to me because of my own conceit!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे सप्ततितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventieth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
