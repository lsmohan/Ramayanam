Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 23
In this Sarga Lakshmaṇa, totally upset with the turn of events, expresses his sadness and outrage over the decision of his father and Kaikēyee and the acceptance of it by Rāma.
He does not appreciate Rāma attributing the turn of events to the will of destiny. He feels that a strong willed man can override destiny. He wants Rāma's coronation to proceed as planned and says that he will kill everyone and anyone that might come in the way.
But at the end, Rāma makes it clear that the right course is to uphold the promise made by their father, and that he is firmly established in that opinion.
Bāla Kāṇḍa is full of stories of the past. It hardly establishes the main characters of Rāmāyaṇa, except that of Daṡaratha in the episode of Viṡwāmitra's request and Rāma's character in the episode of the slaying of Tāṭaka and encounter with Rāma, the son of Jamadagni.
Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa, on the other hand, establishes each character, through its response to the unexpected turn of events. In the previous Sargas it established the characters of Kaikēyee, Daṡaratha, Kousalyā and Rāma, along with mood and preferences of the peoples of the kingdom. In this Sarga, it establishes the character of Lakshmaṇa for the first time.
Through him, Vālmeeki also illustrates a universal pattern of human behavior. Whenever there is a setback or insult to a leader, the support base and family members find it harder to digest, and react strongly, than the leader himself. The strength of the leader lies in his ability to stand firm and carry all of them along with him.
2.23.1
इति ब्रुवति रामे तु
लक्ष्मणोऽधश्शिरा मुहुः ।
शृत्वा मध्यं जगामेव
मनसा दुःखहर्षयोः ॥
As Rāma spoke thus, Lakshmaṇa kept his head lowered. His mind oscillated between sadness and happiness.
Sadness, for the obvious reason. Happiness, perhaps, is at the clarity and resolve of his brother.
2.23.2
तदा तु बध्द्वा भ्रुकुटीम्
भ्रुवोर्मध्ये नरर्षभः ।
निशश्वास महासर्पो
बिलस्थ इव रोषितः ॥
Contracting his eye brows and frowning, that bull among men hissed like an angry viper in a hole.
Like we noted earlier, Lakshmaṇa is symbolic of the support base.
It always takes more time for the support base (and family members) to accept the setbacks than for the leader.
It is when the support base does not have full trust in the leader, that they dump the leader and choose a new leader in place.
2.23.3
तस्य दुष्प्रतिवीक्षं तत्
भृकुटीसहितं तदा ।
बभौ क्रुद्धस्य सिंहस्य
मुखस्य सदृशं मुखम् ॥
His face, with contracted eyebrows, was frightening to look at like the face of a provoked lion.
2.23.4
अग्रहस्तं विधुन्वंस्तु
हस्ती हस्तमिवात्मनः ।
तिर्यगूर्ध्वं शरीरे च
पातयित्वा शिरोधराम् ।
अग्राक्ष्णा वीक्षमाणस्तु
तिर्यग्भ्रातरमब्रवीत् ॥
Waving his palm like the trunk of an elephant, shaking his head sideways and up and down, seeing his brother obliquely through the corner of his eye, he said:
2.23.5
अस्थाने सम्भ्रमो यस्य
जातो वै सुमहानयम् ।
धर्मदोष प्रसङ्गेन
लोकस्यानतिशङ्कया ।
कथंह्येतदसम्भ्रान्तः
त्वद्विधो वक्तुमर्हति ।
यथा दैवमशौडीरम्
शौण्डीर क्षत्रियर्षभ ॥
What is this? Where did this completely misplaced bewilderment of yours come from? Is it from your excessive concern about subverting Dharma? Or are you completely oblivious to public opinion? How can you, the best among the courageous Kshatriyas, in the right state of mind, speak so highly of the frivolous will of god?
2.23.7
किन्नाम कृपणं दैवम्
अशक्तमभिशंससि ।
पापयोस्ते कथं नाम
तयोश्शङ्का न विद्यते ॥
Why do you exalt the weak and pathetic destiny? Why are you blind to the evil plan of those two?
The two being Daṡaratha and Kaikēyee.
2.23.8
सन्ति धर्मोपधा श्लक्ष्णाः
धर्मात्मन्किं न बुध्यसे ।
तयोस्सुचरितं स्वार्थम्
शाठ्यात्परिजिहीर्षतोः ॥
They are taking advantage of your good character by appealing softly to your sense of Dharma! O Dharmātma! Why are you not able to see that their conspiracy is completely driven by selfishness?
2.23.9
यदि नैवं व्यवसितम्
स्याद्धि प्रागेव राघव ।
तयोः प्रागेव दत्तश्च
स्याद्वरः प्रकृतश्च सः ॥
O Rāghava! They must have intentionally planned this for a while! Otherwise, the boons would have been redeemed a long while ago.
2.23.10
लोकविद्विष्टमारब्धम्
त्वदन्यस्याभिषेचनम् ।
नोत्सहे सहितुं वीर
तत्र मे क्षन्तुमर्हसि ॥
Any effort to consecrate anyone other than you is not acceptable to anyone. I cannot accept this by any means. Please forgive me for that!
2.23.11
येनेय मागता द्वैधम्
तव बुद्धिर्महामते ।
स हि धर्मो मम द्वेष्यः
प्रसङ्गाद्यस्य मुह्यसि ॥
I hate that Dharma, whose very mention allures you, because of which, O man o great discernment, you have given in to this thinking!
2.23.12
कथं त्वं कर्मणा शक्तः
कैकेयीवशवर्तिनः ।
करिष्यसि पितुर्वाक्यम्
अधर्मिष्ठं विगर्हितम् ॥
How can a capable person like you, bring himself to oblige the unjust and reprehensible words of his father who is in the clutches of Kaikēyee!
2.23.13
यद्ययं किल्बिषाद्भेदः
कृतोऽप्येवं न गृह्यते ।
जायते तत्र मे दुःखम्
धर्मसङ्गश्च गर्हितः ॥
This conspiracy that is born out of evil intentions, must be taken to be such. If not, it makes me feel sad. Following a Dharma (that supports evil intentions) is reprehensible.
2.23.14
मनसाऽपि कथं कामम्
कुर्यास्त्वं कामवृत्तयोः ।
तयोस्त्वहितयोर्नित्यम्
शत्र्वोः पित्रभिधानयोः ॥
How could you contemplate, even for a moment, fulfilling the wish of these two? They are your enemies, and parents only in name. They are intent on having their way! They never had your welfare in mind!
2.23.15
यद्यपि प्रतिपत्तिस्ते
दैवी चापि तयोर्मतम् ।
तथाप्युपेक्षणीयं ते
न मे तदपि रोचते ॥
Even if you feel that their thoughts are driven by Providence, I do not like your not doing anything about it!
2.23.16
विक्लबो वीर्यहीनो यः
स दैवमनुवर्तते ।
वीरास्सम्भावितात्मानो
न दैवं पर्युपासते ॥
Only the confused and weak cave in to destiny. A Veera with self respect would not leave things to destiny!
2.23.17
दैवं पुरुषकारेण
यः समर्थः प्रबाधितुम् ।
न दैवेन विपन्नार्थः
पुरुषस्सोऽवसीदति ॥
Any man who can override destiny with his own abilities, would not have to suffer the adversities caused by destiny.
2.23.18
द्रक्ष्यन्ति त्वद्य दैवस्य
पौरुषं पुरुषस्य च ।
दैवमानुषयोरद्य
व्यक्ता व्यक्तिर्भविष्यति ॥
May everyone witness today the power of destiny versus the power of man! The difference between man and god will become clear!
2.23.19
अद्य मत्पौरुषहतम्
दैवं द्रक्ष्यन्ति वै जनाः ।
यद्दैवादाहतं तेऽद्य
दृष्टं राज्याभिषेचनम् ॥
Let people see today the Destiny, which has foiled your crowning, itself being foiled by my prowess.
2.23.20
अत्यङ्कुशमिवोद्दामम्
गजं मदबलोद्धतम् ।
प्रधावितमहं दैवम्
पौरुषेण निवर्तये ॥
Destiny may be running wild, like a mighty elephant in rut that broke away its shackles and does not care about the goad! But I will bring it back under control, with my strength!
2.23.21
लोकपालास्समस्ता स्ते
नाद्य रामाभिषेचनम् ।
न च कृत्स्नास्त्रयो लोका
विहन्युः किं पुनः पिता ॥
Not even the lords of all the worlds together, can stop the consecration of Rāma today! Neither would all the three worlds in their entirety! What to speak of then, about our father!
2.23.22
यैर्निवासस्तवारण्ये
मिथो राजन्समर्थितः ।
अरण्ये ते निवत्स्यन्ति
चतुर्दश समास्तथा ॥
O King! Whosoever plotted your stay in forest shall find themselves living in the forest for fourteen years!
2.23.23
अहं तदाशां छेत्स्यामि
पितुस्तस्याश्च या तव ।
अभिषेकविघातेन
पुत्रराज्याय वर्तते ॥
I shall extinguish the hope of father and the hope of that woman who wants to secure the kingdom for her son by preventing your crowning!
2.23.24
मद्बलेन विरुद्धाय
न स्याद्दैवबलं तथा ।
प्रभविष्यति दुःखाय
यथोग्रं पौरुषं मम ॥
One would incur more suffering by standing against my strength, than by standing against the strength of destiny! Such is my dreadful valor!
2.23.25
ऊर्ध्वं वर्षसहस्रान्ते
प्रजापाल्यमनन्तरम् ।
आर्यपुत्राः करिष्यन्ति
वनवासं गते त्वयि ॥
May you rule the kingdom for over thousand years! And may your sons, O noble man, take over when you go to Vanas at the end of your rule!
2.23.26
पूर्वं राजर्षिवृत्त्या हि
वनवासो विधीयते ।
प्रजा निक्षिप्य पुत्रेषु
पुत्रवत्परिपालने ॥
Custom holds, since ancient times, that Rājarshis retire in the Vanas, after entrusting their sons with ruling the people as if they are their own children!
2.23.27
स चेद्राजन्यनेकाग्रे
राज्यविभ्रमशङ्कया ।
नैवमिच्छसि धर्मात्मन्
राज्यं राम त्वमात्मनि ।
प्रतिजाने च ते वीर
माऽभूवं वीरलोकभाक् ।
राज्यं च तव रक्षेयम्
अहं वेलेव सागरम् ॥
O Rāma, O Dharmātma! I hope you are not apprehensive of disturbances arising in the kingdom because of the lack of the king's wholehearted support! I swear to you, O Veera, that I will protect your kingdom like the shore protecting the ocean. Or else, may I never attain the afterworld of Veeras!
2.23.29
मङ्गलैरभिषिञ्चस्व
तत्र त्वं व्यापृतो भव ।
अहमेको महीपालान्
अलं वारयितुं बलात् ॥
May you focus on the proceedings of the consecration, with all the auspicious and sacred arrangements! I can singlehandedly ward off any opposing kings, by force!
2.23.30
न शोभार्थाविमौ बाहू
न धनुर्भूषणाय मे ।
नाऽसिराबन्थनार्थाय
न शरास्तम्भहेतवः ।
अमित्रदमनार्थं मे
सर्वमेतच्चतुष्टयम् ॥
These hands are not made for show, nor is this bow for ornament; this sword is not for tying around the waist, nor these arrows meant to remain inactive. All these four are meant for containing the foes!
2.23.32
न चाहं कामयेऽत्यर्थम्
यस्स्याच्छत्रुर्मतो मम ।
असिना तीक्ष्णधारेण
विद्युच्चलितवर्चसा ।
प्रगृहीतेन वै शत्रुम्
वज्रिणं वा न कल्पये ॥
I do not like to have anyone alive whom I consider to be a foe. Armed with my sharp-edged sword that leaps like the flash of lightning, I hardly care about any foe, even if he is the wielder of the thunderbolt!
2.23.33
खड्गनिष्पेषनिष्पिष्टैः
गहना दुश्चरा च मे ।
हस्त्यश्वनरहस्तोरु
शिरोभिर्भविता मही ॥
The earth shall be impassable, filled with the trunks, hands, thighs and heads of elephants, horses and men hacked and hewed by my sword.
2.23.34
खड्गधाराहता मेऽद्य
दीप्यमाना इवाद्रयः ।
पतिष्यन्ति द्विपा भूमौ
मेघा इव सविद्युतः ॥
Hit by the strikes of my sword, elephants shall fall down on the ground like mountains in flames and clouds with lightning.
The huge black bodies of elephants covered in blood are compared to mountains with red wild fires.
The black bodies of elephants with huge white tusks are compared to black clouds accompanied by lightning.
2.23.35
बद्धगोधाङ्गुलित्राणे
प्रगृहीतशरासने ।
कथं पुरुषमानी स्यात्
पुरुषाणां मयि स्थिते ॥
While I stand firmly holding the bow with my fingers donned by the skin of monitor lizard, how can any man dare boast of his manliness?
2.23.36
बहुभिश्चैकमत्यस्यन्
एकेन च बहून्जनान् ।
विनियोक्ष्याम्यहं बाणान्
नृवाजिगजमर्मसु ॥
I will cast each one far away with multiple arrows. I will cast many away with a single arrow. And I will cast my arrows at the vitals of men, horses and elephants.
2.23.37
अद्य मेऽस्त्रप्रभावस्य
प्रभावः प्रभविष्यति ।
राज्ञश्चाप्रभुतां कर्तुम्
प्रभुत्वं तव च प्रभोः ॥
My lord! Today, the power of my arrows can deprive the king of his authority and establish you in power.
2.23.38
अद्य चन्दनसारस्य
केयूरामोक्षणस्य च ।
वसूनां च विमोक्षस्य
सुहृदां पालनस्य च ।
अनुरूपाविमौ बाहू
राम कर्म करिष्यतः ।
अभिषेचनविघ्नस्य
कर्तऽणां ते निवारणे ॥
Today these arms of mine shall show themselves fit not merely for being decorated with sandal-paste and wearing armlets, or for dispensing largesse or for the defense of friends; but for engaging themselves, O Rāma, in repulsing those who interpose obstacles to your crowning.
2.23.40
ब्रवीहि कोऽद्यैव मया वियुज्यताम्
तवा सुहृत्प्राणयशस्सुहृज्जनैः ।
यथा तवेयं वसुधा वशे भवे
त्तथैव मां शाधि तवास्मि किङ्करः ॥
Tell me which of your enemies should be relieved of his life, fame and friends this very day! Command me with whatever to be done to bring this earth under your control! I am at your service!
2.23.41
विमृज्य बाष्पं परिसान्त्व्यचासकृत्
स लक्ष्मणं राघववंशवर्धनः ।
उवाच पित्र्ये वचने व्यवस्थितं
निबोध मामेष हि सौम्य! सत्पथः ॥
Wiping Lakshmaṇa's tears and calming him by repeated appeals, the enhancer of the lineage of Raghu said: "Understand this clearly, my dear, I stand firmly by the words of father; for that is the right course!"
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे त्रयोविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty third Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
