Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 44
In the previous Sarga Kousalyā, overcome by grief, feels that life is unbearable for her.
In this Sarga, Sumitrā makes her feel better by reminding her of the innate strength and unwavering support that Rāma enjoys and by speaking reassuringly of his return and regaining the kingdom.
2.44.1
विलपन्ती तथा तां तु
कौसल्यां प्रमदोत्तमाम् ।
इदं धर्मे स्थिता धर्म्यम्
सुमित्रा वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥
As Kousalyā, the best among women, thus lamented, Sumitrā, who was firmly grounded in Dharma, spoke to her these words informed by Dharma:
The word Dharma has many shades of meaning. It could mean righteous, appropriate, customary, responsible and so on. We should interpret it based on the context.
2.44.2
तवार्ये सद्गुणैर्युक्तः
पुत्र स्स पुरुषोत्तमः ।
किं ते विलपितेनैवम्
कृपणं रुदितेन वा ॥
Why are you lamenting and weeping like a helpless one? This son of yours, O noble lady, is the best among all men and endowed with all virtues!
When adversity takes us down, we forget the big picture and all that is still intact, as it happens with Kousalyā here.
The context that Kousalyā is in repeats itself in everyone's life, many a time.
A boy has his prized cricket bat. Just before the day of that 'big' match he is waiting for, something falls on it and it chips off. The boy feels that nothing is left in his life any more to live for.
A student prepares well for her exams ahead of time but falls sick a day before the exams and the scores get affected. She now trails behind ten of her peers and feels that nothing is left in her life any more to live for.
You experience many such situations in the course of life. From situation to situation, your turn could be to grieve like Kousalyā or console like Sumitrā.
When your turn is to console like Sumitrā, you will do an excellent job, by mastering the perfect script given in this Sarga, readymade for you:
Ṡlōkas 2-4: Remind that everything that matters is still intact.
Ṡlōkas 5-7: Remind that all the support structures are still in place.
Ṡlōkas 8-10: Remind that the world will still be kind and considerate.
Ṡlōkas 11-13: Remind that the innate qualities are still intact.
Ṡlōkas 14-15: Speak positively of normalcy being restored in a short time.
Ṡlōkas 18-20: Reassure that nothing is impossible, since all the support is still in place.
Ṡlōkas 21-31: Paint a vivid and generous picture of a positive turn of events.
2.44.3
यस्तवार्ये गतः पुत्रः
त्यक्त्वा राज्यं महाबलः ।
साधु कुर्वन् महात्मानम्
पितरं सत्यवादिनम् ।
शिष्टैराचरिते सम्यक्
शश्वत्प्रेत्यफलोदये ।
रामो धर्मे स्थित श्रेष्ठो
न स शोच्यः कदाचन ॥
O noble lady, your son Rāma is mighty strong. He abjured the kingdom to make his great father be known as the keeper of his word. He followed the path of all good men, a path that leads even to a better afterlife. There is nothing to be sad about that exemplary Rāma, who is firmly established in Dharma.
2.44.5
वर्तते चोत्तमां वृत्तिम्
लक्ष्मणोऽस्मिन् सदानघः ।
दयावान् सर्वभूतेषु
लाभस्तस्य महात्मनः ॥
The sinless Lakshmaṇa, who is compassionate towards all creatures, is always there to do the best for him, which is an additional strength to that Mahātma.
2.44.6
अरण्यवासे यद्दुःखम्
जानती वै सुखोचिता ।
अनुगच्छति वैदेही
धर्मात्मानं तवात्मजम् ॥
The princess of Vidēha, who deserves all comforts, accompanied your son, the Dharmātma, knowing fully well the hardships of forest life.
2.44.7
कीर्तिभूतां पताकां यो
लोके भ्रमयति प्रभुः ।
धर्मसत्यव्रतधनः
किं न प्राप्तस्तवात्मजः ॥
Your son hoisted the flag of fame all around the world, and he amassed a wealth of adherence to truth and Dharma. What is there for him not to attain?
2.44.8
व्यक्तं रामस्य विज्ञाय
शौचं माहात्म्यमुत्तमम् ।
न गात्रमंशुभिस्सूर्यः
सन्तापयितुमर्हति ॥
Knowing fully well Rāma's immaculate purity and supreme greatness, the sun would not, for sure, burn his skin with his rays.
2.44.9
शिवस्सर्वेषु कालेषु
काननेभ्यो विनिस्सृतः ।
राघवं युक्तशीतोष्णः
सेविष्यति सुखोऽनिलः ॥
Wholesome, comfortable and temperate breezes from the forest would graciously fan Rāghava in all seasons.
2.44.10
शयानमनघं रात्रौ
पितेवाभिपरिष्वजन् ।
रश्मिभिस्संस्पृशन् शीतैः
चन्द्रमाह्लादयिष्यति ॥
When the sinless one lies down at night, the moon, enveloping him in his embrace like a father, will caress him with the touch of his cool rays.
2.44.11
ददौ चास्त्राणि दिव्यानि
यस्मै ब्रह्मा महौजसे ।
दानवेन्द्रं हतं दृष्ट्वा
तिमिध्वजसुतं रणे ।
स शूरः पुरुषव्याघ्रः
स्वबाहुबलमाश्रितः ।
असन्त्रस्तोऽप्यरणस्थो
वेश्मनीव निवत्स्यति ॥
When the supremely radiant Rāma killed the son of the chief of Dānavas, whose flag bore the symbol of a whale, Brahma gave him many celestial weapons. Such a brave and mighty tiger among men, can live even in the Vana as fearlessly as he would at home, relying on the strength of his stout arms alone.
2.44.13
यस्येषुपथमासाद्य
विनाशं यान्ति शत्रवः ।
कथं न पृथिवी तस्य
शासने स्थातुमर्हति ॥
All those that oppose him meet their end in the trajectory of his flying arrows. Hence, how can the earth but be amenable to his will?
2.44.14
या श्रीश्शौर्यं च रामस्य
या च कल्याणसत्वता ।
निवृत्तारण्यवास स्स
क्षिप्रं राज्यमवाप्स्यति ॥
So fascinating is Rāma's person, so irresistible his prowess and so abundant his strength, that he is bound to gain the kingdom without delay upon his return from the forest.
2.44.15
सूर्यस्यापि भवेत्सूर्यो
ह्यग्नेरग्नि प्रभोः प्रभुः ।
श्रियः श्रीश्च भवेदग्र्या
कीर्तिः कीर्त्याः क्षमाक्षमा ।
दैवतं दैवतानां च
भूतानां भूतसत्तमः ।
तस्य के ह्यगुणा देवि
वने वाप्यथवा पुरे ॥
He is sun to the sun, fire to the fire and lord to the lords. His riches define richness. His renown sets the standard for fame. His forbearance alone can be counted as forbearance. He is the god for gods and the best among all beings. Can anything go wrong, O lady, for such a one, either in the Vana or in the city?
2.44.17
पृथिव्या सह वैदेह्या
श्रिया च पुरुषर्षभः ।
क्षिप्रं तिसृभिरेताभि
स्सह रामोऽभिषेक्ष्यते ॥
Very soon will Rāma, that bull among men, be crowned along with all these three— the earth, the wealth and the princess of Vidēha.
2.44.18
दुःखजं विसृजन्त्यस्रम्
निष्क्रामन्तमुदीक्ष्य यम् ।
अयोध्यायां जनास्सर्वे
शोकवेगसमाहताः ।
कुशचीरधरं देवम्
गच्छन्तमपराजितम् ।
सीतेवानुगता लक्ष्मी
स्तस्य किं नाम दुर्लभम् ॥
The people of Ayōdhyā could not help being overwhelmed by the impetuosity of their grief and shed tears in sorrow, when he left. Seetā, the embodiment of wealth, could not help but follow him, even when he left wearing nothing but robes made of Kuṡa grass. Would there be anything that is out of reach for such a one who has never known defeat?
2.44.20
धनुर्ग्रहवरो यस्य
बाणखड्गास्त्रभृत्स्वयम् ।
लक्ष्मणो व्रजति ह्यग्रे
तस्य किं नाम दुर्लभम् ॥
With Lakshmaṇa himself holding the bow, arrows, sword and armaments walking ahead of him, can there be anything that is out of reach for him?
2.44.21
निवृत्तवनवासं तम्
द्रष्टासि पुनरागतम् ।
जहिशोकं च मोहं च
देवि सत्यं ब्रवीमि ते ॥
Trust me, O Queen, you shall see him return from his stay in the Vana. Throw off your sadness and gloom.
2.44.22
शिरसा चरणावेतौ
वन्दमानमनिन्दिते ।
पुनर्द्रक्ष्यसि कल्याणि
पुत्रं चन्द्रमिवोदितम् ॥
You shall see again, like the risen moon, O blameless and auspicious one, your son bowing his head to touch these feet.
2.44.23
पुनः प्रविष्टं दृष्ट्वा तम्
अभिषिक्तं महाश्रियम् ।
समुत्स्रक्ष्यसि नेत्राभ्याम्
क्षिप्रमानन्दजं पयः ॥
Seeing him back in the city, crowned and endowed with all opulence, you will, before long, be shedding tears of joy.
2.44.24
मा शोको देवि दुःखं वा
न रामे दृश्यतेऽशिवम् ।
क्षिप्रं द्रक्ष्यसि पुत्रं त्वम्
ससीतं सहलक्ष्मणम् ॥
You must not lament or grieve, my lady; nothing adverse will happen to Rāma. You will soon see your son along with Seetā and Lakshmaṇa.
2.44.25
त्वया शेषो जनश्चैव
समाश्वास्यो यदाऽनघे ।
किमिदानीमिदं देवि
करोषि हृदि विक्लबम् ॥
When you should console all these people, O sinless one, how could you be tormented thus in your heart?
2.44.26
नार्हा त्वं शोचितुं देवि
यस्यास्ते राघवस्सुतः ।
न हि रामात्परो लोके
विद्यते सत्पथे स्थितः ॥
You must not grieve, my lady, you who have Rāghava for a son. No one is above Rāma in treading the path of virtue.
2.44.27
अभिवादयमानं तम्
दृष्ट्वा ससुहृदं सुतम् ।
मुदाऽश्रृ मोक्ष्यसे क्षिप्रम्
मेघलेखेव वार्षिकी ॥
Seeing your son prostrate himself in front of you along with his friends, you will be soon be shedding tears of joy, like a row of clouds in the monsoon season.
2.44.28
पुत्रस्ते वरदः क्षिप्रम्
अयोध्यां पुनरागतः ।
पाणिभ्यां मृदुपीनाभ्याम्
चरणौ पीडयिष्यति ॥
Back in Ayōdhyā soon, your son, lord of bounty, will again press your feet with his hands that are so soft and plump.
2.44.29
अभिवाद्य नमस्यन्तम्
शूरं ससुहृदं सुतम् ।
मुदाऽस्रैः प्रोक्ष्यसि पुनः
मेघराजिरिवाचलम् ॥
You will be drenching your valiant son in tears of joy, as the rain-cloud does the hills, when he prostrates at your feet along with his friends.
2.44.30
आश्वासयन्ती विविधैश्च वाक्यैः
वाक्योपचारे कुशलाऽनवद्या ।
रामस्य तां मातरमेवमुक्त्वा
देवी सुमित्रा विरराम रामा ॥
The charming and flawless lady Sumitrā, who was skilled in words of consolation, concluded with those words to Rāma's mother.
2.44.31
निशम्य तल्लक्ष्मणमातृवाक्यं
रामस्य मातुर्नरदेवपत्न्याः ।
सद्यश्शरीरे विननाश शोकः
शरद्गतो मेघ इवाल्पतोयः ॥
On hearing those words of Lakshmaṇa's mother, the grief that beset the wife of the lord of men, Rāma's mother, exited from her being, like a wooly cloud with the arrival of post-monsoon season.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे चतुश्चत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty-fourth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
