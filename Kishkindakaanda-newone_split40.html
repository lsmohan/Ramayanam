Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 40
In this Sarga, Sugreeva describes the strength, abilities and loyalty of the Vānaras that have arrived, to Rāma. He tells Rāma that all of them are now in his service and at his command, and that he should issue orders to them.
Rāma, in simple words, tells that all they need to do is to find where Seetā is and where Rāvaṇa lives, and then a plan of action can be decided. He asks Sugreeva to direct the army to that effect.
Then Sugreeva directs one of the Vānara captains by name Vinata to go in the easterly direction and search for Seetā and Rāvaṇa on every mountain, in every Vana and by every river. Sugreeva gives Vinata a detailed description of all the places that need to be searched in the easterly direction.
Sugreeva commands Vinata and all the other Vānaras under him to come back within a month and report to him after searching every place that he mentioned, and also places that he had missed to mention.
4.40.1
अथ राजा समृद्धार्थः
सुग्रीवः प्लवगेश्वरः ।
उवाच नरशार्दूलम्
रामं परबलार्दनम् ॥
Then the king Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras, with his (immediate) object fulfilled said to Rāma, the tiger among men and the harrier of the enemy:
4.40.2
आगता विनिविष्टाश्च
बलिनः कामरूपिणः ।
वानरेन्द्रा महेन्द्राभा 
ये मद्विषयवासिनः ॥
All the Vānaras who live in my realm, who are endowed with strength and can take any form at will, each one comparable to Mahēndra, have come and have been suitably housed.
4.40.3
त इमे बहुविक्रान्तैः
हरिभिर्भीमविक्रमैः ।
आगता वानरा घोरा
दैत्यदानवसन्निभाः ॥
The frightful Vānaras of exceedingly terrific prowess, the equals of Daityas and Dānavas, have arrived here.
4.40.4
ख्यातकर्मापदानाश्च
बलवन्तो जितक्लमाः ।
पराक्रमेषु विख्याता
व्यवसायेषु चोत्तमाः ।
पृथिव्यम्बुचरा राम
नानानगनिवासिनः ।
कोट्योघाश्च इमे प्राप्ता
वानरास्तव किङ्कराः ॥
O Rāma! Crores of them, celebrated for their feats and past exploits in the field, who know no fatigue, who move on both earth and water and live on various mountains have come here to be at your service.
4.40.6
निदेशवर्तिनस्सर्वे
सर्वे गुरुहिते स्थिताः  ।
अभिप्रेतमनुष्ठातुम्
तव शक्ष्यन्त्यरिन्दम ॥
All of them follow orders to the letter and firmly stand up for the welfare of their master. O subduer of foe! They are capable of executing whatever task you assign to them.
4.40.7
त इमे बहुसाहस्रैः
अनेकै भीमविक्रमैः ।
आगता वानरा घोरा
दैत्यदानवसन्निभाः ॥
These multitudes of Vānaras, who have come in their many thousands, are fierce and are equals of Daityas and Dānavas.
4.40.8
यन्मन्यसे नरव्याघ्र
प्राप्तकालं तदुच्यताम् ।
तत्सैन्यं त्वद्वशे युक्तम्
आज्ञापयितुमर्हसि ॥
O tiger among men! This army is now at your disposal. Tell them what you think is the right time (for the expedition) and command them as you wish.
4.40.9
काममेषामिदं कार्यम्
विदितं मम तत्त्वतः ।
तथाऽपि तु यथायुक्तम्
आज्ञापयितुमर्हसि ॥
I know well enough the work that is ahead; even so, it is for you to issue orders.
4.40.10
इति ब्रुवाणं सुग्रीवम्
रामो दशरथात्मजः ।
बाहुभ्यां सम्परिष्वज्य
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
When Sugreeva said this, Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha, embraced him and said these words:
4.40.11
ज्ञायतां सौम्य वैदेही
यदि जीवति वा न वा ।
स च देशो महाप्राज्ञ
यस्मिन्वसति रावणः ॥
O gracious and sagacious one! May it be found whether Vaidēhee is alive or not and where Rāvaṇa lives.
4.40.12
अधिगम्य तु वैदेहीम्
निलयं रावणस्य च ।
प्राप्तकालं विधास्यामि
तस्मिन्काले सह त्वया ॥
After finding Vaidēhee and Rāvaṇa’s abode, I shall, in consultation with you, decide what should be done next.
4.40.13
नाहमस्मिन् प्रभुः कार्ये
वानरेश न लक्ष्मणः ।
त्वमस्य हेतुः कार्यस्य
प्रभुश्च प्लवगेश्वर ॥
In this matter, O Lord of Vānaras, I am not the master; nor is Lakshmaṇa. You are the driver as well as the commander in this matter.
4.40.14
त्वमेवाऽऽज्ञापय विभो
मम कार्यविनिश्चयम् ।
त्वं हि जानासि यत्कार्यम्
मम वीर न संशयः ॥
O lord! You issue the order regarding what is to be done. O Veera! You know very well, without a doubt, everything about my object.
4.40.15
सुहृद्विनीतो विक्रान्तः
प्राज्ञः कालविशेषवित् ।
भवानस्मध्दिते युक्तः
सुहृदाप्तोऽर्थवित्तमः ॥
You are my best and intimate friend. You are powerful and yet bear humility. You are sagacious with a great sense of timing. You are devoted to my wellbeing. Your show utmost sincerity to the object.
4.40.16
एवमुक्तस्तु सुग्रीवो
विनतं नाम यूथपम् ।
अब्रवीद्रामसान्निध्ये
लक्ष्मणस्य च धीमतः ।
शैलाभं मेघनिर्घोषम्
ऊर्जितं प्लवगेश्वरः ॥
Thereupon Sugreeva, in the presence of Rāma and the sagacious Lakshmaṇa, addressed a captain and a leader of Vānaras by name Vinata, who was mountainous in size with a voice like thunder and resolute in purpose:
4.40.17
सोमसूर्यनिभैस्सार्धम्
वानरैर्वानरोत्तम ।
देशकालनयैर्युक्तः
विज्ञः कार्यविनिश्चये ।
वृतश्शतसहश्रेण
वानराणां तरस्विनाम् ।
अधिगच्छ दिशं पूर्वाम्
सशैलवनकाननाम् ॥
O best of Vānaras! You have a great sense of time and place, as well as tact. You are astute in judging what needs to be done and when.
Please go in the easterly direction and to the mountains and Vanas and forests there, taking with you hundreds of thousands of powerful Vānaras who are comparable to the sun and moon.
4.40.19
तत्र सीतां च वैदेहीम्
निलयं रावणस्य च ।
मार्गध्वं गिरिदुर्गेषु 
वनेषु च नदीषु च ॥
And there, look for Seetā, the princess of Vidēha, and for the abode of Rāvaṇa in the caverns of mountains, in the Vanas and by the rivers.
4.40.20
नदीं भागीरथीं रम्याम्
सरयूं कौशिकीं तथा ।
कालिन्दीं यमुनां रम्याम्
यामुनं च महागिरिम् ।
सरस्वतीं च सिन्धुं च
शोणं मणिनिभोदकम् ।
महीं कालमहीं चैव
शैलकाननशोभिताम् ॥
(Search by) the river Ganga, and the enchanting Sarayu and Kouṡiki, and the beautiful Yamuna that originates from Kalinda, and the Saraswati and Sindhu and the Ṡōṇa whose waters are as clear as gems, and the Mahee, and the Kālamahee that is splendid with its fine hills and forests.
4.40.22
ब्रह्ममालान्विदेहांश्च
मालवान्काशिकोसलान् ।
मागधांश्च महाग्रामान्
पुण्ड्रांस्त्वङ्गांस्तथैव च ।
पत्तनं कोशकाराणाम्
भूमिं च रजताकराम् ॥
(Search in) the countries of Brahmamāla, Vidēha, Mālava and Kāsi, Kōsala and also in Magadha and its great villages, the Puṇḍra, the Aṅga, the places where the silkworm is bred and where silver is mined.
4.40.24
सर्वं च तद्विचेतव्यम्
मृगयद्भिस्ततस्ततः ।
रामस्य दयितां भार्याम्
सीतां दशरथस्नुषाम् ॥
As you look for Seetā, Rāma’s beloved wife and the daughter-in-law of Daṡaratha, all those places should be searched thoroughly.
4.40.25
समुद्रमवगाढांश्च
पर्वतान्पत्तनानि च ।
मन्दरस्य च ये कोटिम्
संश्रिताः केचिदायताम् ।
कर्णप्रावरणाश्चैव
तथा चाप्योष्ठकर्णकाः ।
घोरलोहमुखाश्चैव
जवनाश्चैकपादकाः ।
अक्षया बलवन्तश्च
तथैव पुरुषादकाः ।
किरातास्तीक्ष्णचूडाश्च
हेमाभाः प्रियदर्शनाः ।
आममीनाशनाश्चापि
किराता द्वीपवासिनः ।
अन्तर्जलचरा घोरा
नरव्याघ्रा इति स्मृताः  ।
एतेषामाश्रयास्सर्वे
विचेयाः काननौकसः ।
गिरिभिर्ये च गम्यन्ते
प्लवनेन प्लवेन च ॥
O forest dwellers! You should search every place that can be reached by hopping over mountains or by boat, and also the mountains and towns that stretch into oceans, and the habitations along the long ridges of Mandara.
(Search) among those populations who cover themselves with their ears, who have lips for their ears, who have frightful faces that seem to be made of iron, who have just one leg, and among those endless hordes of hefty man-eaters and among the hunters with sharp heads.
(Search) among those with a golden complexion and pleasing appearance, the island-fishers who live on raw fish, and fierce amphibious creatures who are known as human tigers.
4.40.30
यत्नवन्तो यवद्वीपम्
सप्तराज्योपशोभितम् ।
सुवर्णरूप्यकद्वीपम्
सुवर्णाकरमण्डितम् ।
यवद्वीपमतिक्रम्य
शिशिरो नाम पर्वतः ।
दिवं स्पृशति शृङ्गेण
देवदानवसेवितः ।
एतेषां गिरिदुर्गेषु
प्रपातेषु वनेषु च ।
मार्गध्वं सहितास्सर्वे
रामपत्नीं यशस्विनीम् ॥
Putting forth every effort, search for the reputed wife of Rāma, on the island of Yava that houses the seven kingdoms, the islands of Suvarṇa and Rajata that have an abundance of gold mines, and on the mountain by name Ṡiṡira that is beyond the island of Yava which touches the sky with its peaks and which is frequented by Deva and Dānavas, and in all the nooks and crannies of the mountains, Vanas and cascades.
4.40.33
ततो रक्तजलं प्राप्य 
शोणाख्यं शीघ्र वाहिनीम्  ।
गत्वा पारं समुद्रस्य
सिद्धचारणसेवितम् ।
तस्य तीर्थेषु रम्येषु
विचित्रेषु वनेषु च ।
रावणस्सह वैदेह्या
मार्गितव्यस्ततस्ततः ॥
Then you should go to the swift flowing river Ṡōṇa, and then to the far side of the ocean frequented by Siddhas and Cāraṇas.
You should search in every place there, on those beautiful beaches and the enchanting Vanas, for the princess of Vidēha and Rāvaṇa.
4.40.35
पर्वत प्रभवा नद्यः
सुभीम बहु निष्कुटाः ।
मार्गितव्या दरीमन्तः
पर्वताः च वनानि च ॥
Search the mountains and their caverns, the Vanas, the imposing rivers that originate from those mountains with thickets on their banks.
4.40.36
ततस्समुद्रद्वीपांश्च
सुभीमान्द्रष्टुमर्हथ ।
ऊर्मिमन्तं महारौद्रम् 
क्रोशन्तमनिलोद्धतम् ॥
You should then look into the dreadful isles of the ocean and the ocean itself that is frightful, roaring with its tides stirred by high winds.
4.40.37
तत्रासुरा महाकायाः
छायां गृह्णन्ति नित्यशः ।
ब्रह्मणा समनुज्ञाता
दीर्घकालं बुभुक्षिताः ॥
There, Asuras with huge bodies, suffering from hunger for a long time, catch hold of living creatures by their shadows, as they are so ordained (to live) by lord Brahma.
Each creature, for its sustenance, needs food. And there are an astonishing variety of methods that creatures employ to catch their food. Some of them are too weird for our sensibilities; nevertheless they are natural.
The expression, ‘as ordained by lord Brahma’, in this Ṡlōka is used in the same sense that we, in modern times, use the phrase, ‘according to their innate nature’.
4.40.38
तं कालमेघप्रतिमम्
महोरगनिषेवितम् ।
अभिगम्य महानादम्
तीर्थेनैव महोदधिम् ।
ततो रक्तजलं भीमम्
रोहितं नाम सागरम् ।
गत्वा प्रेक्ष्यथ तां चैव
बृहतीं कूटशाल्मलीम् ॥
When you negotiate through that great roaring ocean, which reminds one of a heavy dark cloud in which huge serpents abound, you will see another ocean called Rōhita with blood red waters, and then a huge tree called Kūṭaṡālmali.
4.40.40
गृहं च वैनतेयस्य
नानारत्नविभूषितम् ।
तत्र कैलाससङ्काशम्
विहितं विश्वकर्मणा ॥
There (you will see) the house of Vainatēya, adorned with many a gem and huge as the Kailāsa, which was built by Viṡwakarma.
4.40.41
तत्र शैलनिभा भीमा
मन्देहा नाम राक्षसाः ।
शैलशृङ्गेषु लम्बन्ते
नानारूपा महाबलाः ॥
There, on the peaks of the mountain, (you will see) fierce Rākshasas called Mandēhas, huge as rocks and in different shapes and forms, hanging upside down from the cliffs of the mountain.
4.40.42
ते पतन्ति जले नित्यम्
सूर्यस्योदयनं प्रति ।
अभितप्ताश्च सूर्येण
लम्बन्ते स्म पुनः पुनः ।
निहता ब्रह्मतेजोभिः
अहन्यहनि राक्षसाः ॥
Every day at the time of sunrise, those Rākshasas fall into the water, burnt by the rays of the sun and by the power of water oblations given by the Brāhmaṇas; and again they (go back) and hang upside down (from the cliffs).
4.40.43
ततः पाण्डुरमेघाभम्
क्षीरोदं नाम सागरम् ।
गत्वा द्रक्ष्यथ दुर्धर्षा
मुक्ताहारमिवोर्मिभिः ॥
O you invincible ones! Going there, you will see the Milky Ocean that is splendid like a white cloud, whose tides look like pearl necklaces.
Indeed, a beautiful aerial view.
4.40.44
तस्य मध्ये महान् श्वेतो
ऋषभो नाम पर्वतः ।
दिव्यगन्धैः कुसुमितैः
आचितैश्च नगैर्वृतः ।
सरश्च राजतैः पद्मैः
ज्वलितैर्हेमकेसरैः ।
नाम्ना सुदर्शनं नाम
राजहंसैस्समाकुलम् ॥
At the center of it lies a great white mountain known as Ṛshabha, nestled between hills (covered with) trees in flower of divine fragrance.
You will also see the lake called Sudarṡana with silvery lotuses and their dazzling golden filaments, crowded everywhere by royal swans.
4.40.46
विबुधाश्चारणा यक्षाः
किन्नराश्चाप्सरोगणाः ।
हृष्टास्समधिगच्छन्ति
नलिनीं तां रिरंसवः ॥
Dēvas, Cāraṇas, Yakshas, Kinnaras and bevies of Apsaras go excitedly to that lotus pool to play sport.
4.40.47
क्षीरोदं समतिक्रम्य
तदा द्रक्ष्यथ वानराः ।
जलोदं सागरं शीघ्रम्
सर्वभूतभयावहम् ॥
Right after passing beyond the Milky Ocean, O Vānaras, you will see the greatest of the Oceans, the ocean of pure water, which instills terror in all creatures.
4.40.48
तत्र तत्कोपजं तेजः
कृतं हयमुखं महत् ।
अस्याहुस्तन्महावेगम्
ओदनं सचराचरम् ।
तत्र विक्रोशतां नादो
भूतानां सागरौकसाम् ।
श्रूयते च समर्थानाम्
दृष्ट्वा तद्वडवामुखम् ॥
This is where, it is said, the fire in the shape of a horse’s face that was born out of that anger, persists.
It consumes, for food, the gush of (the ocean) and all its mobile and immobile creatures.
The helpless squeal of the creatures that live in the ocean, as they pass by the face of the Vaḍava, can be clearly heard.
The anger referred to in this Ṡlōka is that of a Muni called Aurasa. The story of Aurasa is narrated in the Ādi Parva of Mahabharata in detail.
Aurasa, irked when some kings come to kill him, releases his Yōgic fire that starts to burn the world. Then his ancestors descend from Heaven and pacify him. Then he contains the fire in the bottom of the ocean, which is ever ready to emerge from a cavity whose opening is in the shape of a horse’s mouth. Oceanic creatures, as they pass by this fire, squeal as they are helplessly consumed by it. At the time of the dissolution of the worlds, it is said, that this fire would engulf even the oceans.
This legendary fire is referred to as Vaḍavāgni or Baḍabānala alternately. Vaḍava or Baḍaba means horse.
This episode might actually be referring to the presence of under-ocean volcanoes.
4.40.50
स्वादूदस्योत्तरे देशे
योजनानि त्रयोदश ।
जातरूपशिलो नाम
सुमहान्कनकप्रभः ॥
On the farther shore of that ocean of pure water, at a distance of thirteen Yōjanas, lies the great mountain called Jātarūpaṡila that dazzles like gold.
4.40.51
तत्र चन्द्रप्रतीकाशम्
पन्नगं धरणीधरम् ।
पद्मपत्रविशालाक्षम्
ततो द्रक्ष्यथ वानराः ।
आसीनं पर्वतस्याग्रे
सर्वभूतनमस्कृतम् ।
सहस्रशिरसं देवम्
अनन्तं नीलवाससम् ॥
You will then see, seated on the top of the mountain, the serpent god Ananta of a thousand heads, white as the moonlight, wearing a black garment, with eyes wide like lotus petals, who bears the weight of the earth and who is saluted by all the Dēvas.
4.40.53
त्रिशिराः काञ्चनः केतुः
तालस्तस्य महात्मनः ।
स्थापितः पर्वतस्याग्रे
विराजति सवेदिकः ॥
On the top of that great mountain shines a triple headed palm tree that is established as a flagstaff with a golden Vēdika around it.
4.40.54
पूर्वस्यां दिशि निर्माणम्
कृतं तत्त्रिदशेश्वरैः ।
ततः परं हेममयः
श्रीमानुदयपर्वतः ।
तस्य कोटिर्दिवं स्पृष्ट्वा
शतयोजनमायता ।
जातरूपमयी दिव्या
विराजति सवेदिका ॥
Supported by a solid Vēdika, it touches the sky with its hundred Yōjanas-long crowning ridge, all of it pure gold.
It was planted there by the supreme Dēvas to mark the outer limit of the eastern quarter. Beyond that lies the glorious golden mountain of the rising sun.
4.40.56
सालैस्तालैस्तमालैश्च
कर्णिकारैश्च पुष्पितैः ।
जातरूपमयैर्दिव्यैः
शोभते सूर्यससंनिभैः ॥
Its beauty is enhanced by the golden Sāla, Tāla and Karṇikāra trees in bloom, shining bright as the sun.
4.40.57
तत्र योजनविस्तारम्
उच्छ्रितं दशयोजनम् ।
शृङ्गं सौमनसं नाम
जातरूपमयं ध्रुवम् ॥
There (you will see) a solid, all-gold peak known as Soumanasa, which is one Yōjana wide and ten Yōjanas high.
4.40.58
तत्रपूर्वं पदं कृत्वा
पुरा विष्णुस्त्रिविक्रमे ।
द्वितीयं शिखरे मेरोः
चकार पुरुषोत्तमः ॥
This is where Vishṇu, the foremost of spirited beings, once upon a time, laid his foot first, before laying the second one on Mēru mountain, when he covered the world in three strides.
This Ṡlōka refers to the Vāmana Avatāra.
4.40.59
उत्तरेण परिक्रम्य
जम्बूद्वीपं दिवाकरः ।
दृश्यो भवति भूयिष्ठम्
शिखरं तन्महोच्छ्रयम् ॥
The sun, after crossing over the Jambūdweepa on its northern side reappears in clear sight on this great peak.
4.40.60
तत्र वैखानसा नाम
वालखिल्या महर्षयः ।
प्रकाशमाना दृश्यन्ते
सूर्यवर्णास्तपस्विनः ॥
There can be seen the shining Vālakhilya Maharshis known as Vaikhanasas who are radiant like the sun, practicing austerities.
4.40.61
अयं सुदर्शनो द्वीपः
पुरो यस्य प्रकाशते ।
यस्मिंस्तेजश्च चक्षुश्च
सर्वप्राणभृतामपि ॥
That isle, known as Sudarṡana, is where the radiance (of the sun) that gives life to all creatures and sight to the eyes casts itself first.
4.40.62
शैलस्य तस्य शृङ्गेषु
कन्दरेषु वनेषु च ।
रावणस्सह वैदेह्या
मार्गितव्यस्ततस्ततः ॥
You should look for Rāvaṇa and Vaidēhee everywhere on that mountain and on its peaks, caverns and Vanas.
4.40.63
काञ्चनस्य च शैलस्य
सूर्यस्य च महात्मनः ।
आविष्टा तेजसा सन्ध्या
पूर्वा रक्ता प्रकाशते ॥
The radiance of the great sun reflected on that golden mountain shines vividly red at dawn.
4.40.64
पूर्वमेतत्कृतं द्वारम्
पृथिव्या भुवनस्य च ।
सूर्यस्योदयनं चैव
पूर्वा ह्येषा दिगुच्यते ॥
This (easterly) quarter is known as Pūrva because this was made to be the first entry point into the world of earth, where the sun would rise.
Pūrva - the first one, the one before everything else.
4.40.65
तस्य शैलस्य पृष्ठेषु
निर्झरेषु गुहासु च ।
रावणस्सह वैदेह्या
मार्गितव्यस्ततस्ततः ॥
Rāvaṇa and Vaidēhee should be looked for on the flanks, cascades and caverns of that mountain.
4.40.66
ततः परमगम्यास्यात्
दिक्पूर्वा त्रिदशावृता ।
रहिता चन्द्रसूर्याभ्याम्
अदृश्या तिमिरावृता ॥
No one can see or go beyond that point in the eastern quarter where there is no sun or moon. That place is populated by Dēvas and enveloped in darkness.
4.40.67
शैलेषु तेषु सर्वेषु
कन्दरेषु वनेषु च ।
ये च नोक्ता मयोद्देशा 
विचेया तेषु जानकी ॥
You should search for Jānaki everywhere among those mountains, caves and forests, and also those places that I might have missed to mention.
4.40.68
एतावद्वानरैश्शक्यम्
गन्तुं वानरपुङ्गवाः ।
अभास्करममर्यादम्
न जानीमस्ततः परम् ॥
O bulls among Vānaras! This is how far Vānaras can go. We do not know what lies beyond, where there is no sun and no markers.
4.40.69
अभिगम्य तु वैदेहीम्
निलयं रावणस्य च ।
मासे पूर्णे निवर्तध्वम्
उदयं प्राप्य पर्वतम् ॥
Going up to that mountain where the sun rises, and knowing the whereabouts of Vaidēhee and the abode of Rāvaṇa you should return within a month.
4.40.70
ऊर्ध्वं मासान्न वस्तव्यम्
वसन्वध्यो भवेन्मम ।
सिद्धार्थास्सन्निवर्तध्वम्
अधिगम्य च मैथिलीम् ॥
You should spend no more than a month, and whoever does so, will see death at my hands. Come back, your mission fulfilled with the discovery of Maithili’s whereabouts!
4.40.71
महेन्द्रकान्तां वनषण्डमण्डितां
दिशं चरित्वा निपुणेन वानराः ।
अवाप्य सीतां रघुवंशजप्रियां
ततो निवृत्तास्सुखिनो भविष्यथ ॥
O Vānaras! You shall live happily ever after, having skillfully combed the quarter that is the favorite of Indra and that is splendid with the groves of Vanas and having obtained Maithili, the beloved of the one born into the lineage of Raghu.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे चत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fortieth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
