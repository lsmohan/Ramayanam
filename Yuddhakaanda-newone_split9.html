Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 9
In this Sarga, Vibheeshaṇa tells Rāvaṇa that it is not prudent to provoke the enmity of Rāma, a man of extraordinary power and discipline,  who adheres to Dharma and who is supported by a massive Vānara army.
He reminds Rāvaṇa that Rāma did not commit any offense for his wife to be carried off, and he killed Khara only in self-defense, and the abduction of another man’s wife is sinful, only causing one’s destruction.
He further says that it is prudent to give Seetā back to Rāma, before Laṅkā is invaded and destroyed. He urges Rāvaṇa to take pity on all the Rākshasas and spare their lives.
6.9.1
ततो निकुम्भो रभसः
सूर्यशत्रुर्महाबलः ।
सुप्तघ्नो यज्ञहा रक्षो
महापार्श्वो महोदरः ।
अग्निकेतुश्च दुर्धर्षो
रश्मिकेतुश्च राक्षसः ।
इन्द्रजिच्च महातेजा
बलवान् रावणात्मजः ।
प्रहस्तोऽथ विरूपाक्षो
वज्रदंष्ट्रो महाबलः ।
धूम्राक्षश्चातिकायश्च
दुर्मुखश्चैव राक्षसः ।
परिघान् पट्टिशान् प्रासान्
शक्तिशूलपरश्वधान् ।
चापानि च सबाणानि
खड्गांश्च विपुलान् शितान् ।
प्रगृह्य परमक्रुद्धाः
समुत्पत्य च राक्षसाः ।
अब्रुवन् रावणं सर्वे
प्रदीप्ता इव तेजसा ॥
Thereupon Nikumbha, Rabhasa, Sūryaṡatru of immense strength, Suptaghna, Yajñakōpa, Mahāpārṡwa, Mahōdara, the formidable Agnikētu, the Rākshasa Raṡmikētu, Prahasta, Virūpāksha, Indrajit, the powerful son of Rāvaṇa, the immensely strong Vajradaṃshṭra, Dhūmrāksha, Nikumbha and the Rākshasa Durmukha, all sprang up in their places in a great frenzy, wielding their Parighas, Paṭṭiṡas, spears, Prāsas, Ṡaktis, Paraṡus, bows and sharp arrows and great and sharp swords, and said to Rāvaṇa who lit up all the directions with his radiance:
6.9.6
अद्य रामं वधिष्यामः
सुग्रीवं च सलक्ष्मणम् ।
कृपणं च हनूमन्तम्
लङ्का येन प्रधर्षिता ॥
Today, we will slay Rāma, Sugreeva, Lakshmaṇa and the pitiable Hanumān who dared to assault Laṅkā.
6.9.7
तान् गृहीतायुधान् सर्वान्
वारयित्वा विभीषणः ।
अब्रवीत् प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यम्
पुनः प्रत्युपवेश्य तान् ॥
Stopping all of them who were up with their arms, and making them take their seats again, Vibheeshaṇa addressed Rāvaṇa with palms joined in reverence:
6.9.8
अप्युपायैस्त्रिभिस्तात
योऽर्थः प्राप्तुं न शक्यते ।
तस्य विक्रमकालांस्तान्
युक्तानाहुर्मनीषिणः ॥
My dear! Great people say that the right time for deploying force to accomplish an object is only after the other three Upāyas fail.
6.9.9
प्रमत्तेष्वभियुक्तेषु
दैवेन प्रहृतेषु च ।
विक्रमास्तात सिद्ध्यन्ति
परीक्ष्य विधिना कृताः ॥
Force that is deployed after due diligence and according to conventional wisdom does succeed, but only when deployed against an enemy who is either not alert, or engaged with other foes or reeling under a doomed fate.
6.9.10
अप्रमत्तं कथं तं तु
विजिगीषुं बले स्थितम् ।
जितरोषं दुराधर्षम्
प्रधर्षयितुमिच्छथ ॥
How could you think of attacking someone who is unassailable, highly alert, bent on scoring a victory, well-supported by an army and and in full control of his temper!
6.9.11
समुद्रं लङ्घयित्वा तु
घोरं नदनदीपतिम् ।
कृतं हनुमता कर्म
दुष्करं तर्कयेत वा ॥
Who could have even imagined the possibility of doing what Hanumān did in crossing the dreaded sea, the lord of the east and west flowing rivers?
6.9.12
बलान्यपरिमेयानि
वीर्याणि च निशाचराः ।
परेषां सहसाऽवज्ञा
न कर्तव्या कथञ्चन ॥
We should not, by any means, quickly underestimate the valor and size of the army force, which could be limitless!
6.9.13
किं च राक्षसराजस्य
रामेणापकृतं पुरा ।
आजहार जनस्थानात्
यस्य भार्यां यशस्विनः ॥
What offense did Rāma of renown commit against the king of Rākshasas, for his wife to be carried off from Janasthāna?
6.9.14
खरो यद्यतिवृत्तस्तु
रामेण निहतो रणे ।
अवश्यं प्राणिनां प्राणा
रक्षितव्या यथाबलम् ॥
That Khara, who exceeded his limits, was killed by Rāma in the fight, is justifiable, for every creature owes it to itself to save its own life with all its abilities!
6.9.15
अयशस्यमनायुष्यम्
परदाराभिमर्शनम् ।
अर्थक्षयकरं घोरम्
पापस्य च पुनर्भवम् ॥
The abduction of another man’s wife is terrible, sinful, brings infamy, destroys one’s objects and puts one’s life in peril.
6.9.16
एतन्निमित्तं वैदेह्या
भयं नः सुमहद्भवेत् ।
आहृता सा परित्याज्या
कलहार्थे कृतेन किम् ॥
Because of this, we have great fear from (having abducted) Vaidēhee. She, who was carried off, should be released. What good is it to provoke a fight?
6.9.17
न नः क्षमं वीर्यवता
तेन धर्मानुवर्तिना ।
वैरं निरर्थकं कर्तुम्
दीयतामस्य मैथिली ॥
It is not proper for us to unnecessarily provoke the enmity of a valiant man who sticks to the path of righteousness. Let his Maithili be given back.
6.9.18
यावन्न सगजां साश्वाम्
बहुरत्नसमाकुलाम् ।
पुरीं दारयते बाणैः
दीयतामस्य मैथिली ॥
Before he rends with his arrows the city that is full of precious gems of various kinds, along with its elephants and horses, let his Maithili be given back.
6.9.19
यावत् सुघोरा महती
दुर्द्धर्षा हरिवाहिनी ।
नावस्कन्दति नो लङ्काम्
तावत्सीता प्रदीयताम् ॥
Before the terribly formidable, massive and invincible army of Vānaras invades our Laṅkā, let his Maithili be given back to him.
6.9.20
विनश्येद्धि पुरी लङ्का
शूराः सर्वे च राक्षसाः ।
रामस्य दयिता पत्नी
स्वयं न यदि दीयते ॥
If we do not, of our own accord, give the beloved wife of Rāma back, the city of Laṅkā will be destroyed and so will be the heroic Rākshasas.
6.9.21
प्रसादये त्वां बन्धुत्वात्
कुरुष्व वचनं मम ।
हितं पथ्यमहं ब्रूमि
दीयतामस्य मैथिली ॥
Because of our relationship, I am pleading with you. Please do what I say. I am only telling what is good and beneficial to you. Let his Maithili be given back.
6.9.22
पुरा शरत्सूर्यमरीचिसन्निभान्
नवाग्रपुङ्खान् सुदृढान्नृपात्मजः ।
सृजत्यमोघान् विशिखान् वधाय ते
प्रदीयतां दाशरथाय मैथिली ॥
Let Maithili be given back to the son of Daṡaratha, before the prince releases his sturdy arrows with freshly made arrowheads, that emerge (in every direction, unobstructed) like the rays of the sun in the post monsoon, to kill you.
6.9.23
त्यज स्वकोपं सुखधर्मनाशनं
भजस्व धर्मं रतिकीर्तिवर्द्धनम् ।
प्रसीद जीवेम सपुत्रबान्धवाः
प्रदीयतां दाशरथाय मैथिली ॥
Please give up your anger that would only destroy your Dharma and happiness! Adhere to Dharma that would only increase your happiness and fame!
Take pity upon us, so that we will be spared to live along with our kith and kin. Let Maithili be given back to the son of Daṡaratha!
6.9.24
विभीषणवचः श्रुत्वा
रावणो राक्षसेश्वरः ।
विसर्जयित्वा तान् सर्वान्
प्रविवेश स्वकं गृहम् ॥
Hearing those words of Vibheeshaṇa, Rāvaṇa, the Lord of Rākshasas, dismissed all of them and retired to his own residence.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे नवमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the ninth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
