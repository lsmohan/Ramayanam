Baala Kaanda - Sarga 40
In this Sarga, the Dēvas are assured by Lord Brahma that all the sons of Sagara, who were digging the earth indiscriminately killing many living beings in that process, will themselves be killed by Kapila, a form of Vishṇu.
The princes continue to dig the earth in all directions. They see the mighty elephants that are holding the earth in position. Finally they find the horse where Kapila lives. They go on to kill him. But the powerful Kapila turns them into ashes.
1.40.1
देवतानां वचः श्रुत्वा
भगवान् वै पितामहः ।
प्रत्युवाच सुसन्त्रस्तान्
कृतान्तबलमोहितान् ॥
Hearing those words of the Dēvas, who were disoriented and frightened by the acts of Providence, the Progenitor of all peoples, lord Brahma, said:
1.40.2
यस्येयं वसुधा कृत्स्ना
वासुदेवस्य धीमतः ।
कापिलं रूपमास्थाय
धारयत्यनिशं धराम् ।
तस्य कोपाग्निना दग्धा
भविष्यन्ति नृपात्मजाः ॥
All this earth belongs to Vāsudēva, the brilliant. As Kapila, he is holding it in place. All the princes (sons of Sagara) will be burnt to ashes by the wrath of Kapila.
1.40.3
पृथिव्याश्चापि निर्भेदो
दृष्ट एव सनातनः ।
सगरस्य च पुत्राणाम्
विनाशोऽदीर्घजीविनाम् ॥
May it be understood that the end of the princes is nearing, and hence were they breaking the ground indiscriminately.
1.40.4
पितामहवचः श्रुत्वा
त्रयस्त्रिंशदरिन्दम ।
देवाः परमसंहृष्टाः
पुनर्जग्मुर्यथागतम् ॥
O tormentor of foes! Hearing those words of the Progenitor, the thirty three Dēvas left to their original places, feeling relieved and happy at heart.
1.40.5
सगरस्य च पुत्राणाम्
प्रादुरासीन्महात्मनाम् ।
पृथिव्यां भिद्यमानायाम्
निर्घातसमनिःस्वनः ॥
The mighty sons of king Sagara heard a loud sound like that of a thunderbolt while they were breaking the earth apart.
1.40.6
ततो भित्त्वा महीं सर्वे
कृत्वा चापि प्रदक्षिणम् ।
सहिताः सागराः सर्वे
पितरं वाक्यमब्रुवन् ॥
After going around the entire earth and breaking it apart, they returned together to their father Sagara and said:
1.40.7
परिक्रान्ता मही सर्वा
सत्त्ववन्तश्च सूदिताः ।
देवदानवरक्षांसि
पिशाचोरगकिन्नराः ।
न च पश्यामहेऽश्वं तं
अश्वहर्तारमेव च ।
किं करिष्याम भद्रं ते
बुद्धिरत्र विचार्यताम् ॥
We went around every part of the earth. Many mighty Dēvas, Dānavas, Rākshasas, Piṡācas, Uragas and Kinnaras died.
Even then, we could find neither the horse nor the abductor of the horse! What shall we do! Please direct us. May all bode well for you!
1.40.9
तेषां तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
पुत्राणां राजसत्तमः ।
समन्युरब्रवीद्वाक्यम्
सगरो रघुनन्दन ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Hearing those words of his sons, the notable king became angry and said:
1.40.10
भूयः खनत भद्रं वो
निर्भिद्य वसुधातलम् ।
अश्वहर्तारमासाद्य
कृतार्थाश्च निवर्तथ ॥
Go again and break the surface of the entire earth and dig everywhere till you find the abductor of the horse! May all bode well for you!
1.40.11
पितुर्वचनमासाद्य
सगरस्य महात्मनः ।
षष्टिः पुत्रसहस्राणि
रसातलमभिद्रवन् ॥
Having heard those reinforcing words from the father, the sixty thousand sons went to the nether layers of the world.
1.40.12
खन्यमाने ततस्तस्मिन्
ददृशुः पर्वतोपमम् ।
दिशागजं विरूपाक्षम्
धारयन्तं महीतलम् ॥
As they were digging, they saw a mighty elephant by name Viroopāksha that was as big as a mountain, holding the earth.
1.40.13
सपर्वतवनां कृत्स्नाम्
पृथिवीं रघुनन्दन ।
शिरसा धारयामास
विरूपाक्षो महागजः ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! That mighty elephant by name Viroopāksha was bearing the entire earth on its head, along with all the mountains and forests.
1.40.14
यदा पर्वणि काकुत्स्थ
विश्रमार्थं महागजः ।
खेदाच्चालयते शीर्षम्
भूमिकम्पस्तदा भवेत् ॥
O prince of Kākutstha dynasty! We experience earthquakes whenever that mighty elephant shakes its head when tired.
1.40.15
तं ते प्रदक्षिणं कृत्वा
दिशापालं महागजम् ।
मानयन्तो हि ते राम
जग्मुर्भित्त्वा रसातलम् ॥
O Rāma! They paid respects to that mighty elephant by bowing and circumambulating, and proceeded to dig the earth.
1.40.16
ततः पूर्वां दिशं भित्त्वा
दक्षिणां बिभिदुः पुनः ।
दक्षिणस्यामपि दिशि
ददृशुस्ते महागजम् ।
महापद्मं महात्मानम्
सुमहत्पर्वतोपमम् ।
शिरसा धारयन्तं ते
विस्मयं जग्मुरुत्तमम् ॥
Thus, after digging in the east, they proceeded to dig to the south. And even there, they found a mighty elephant by name Mahāpadma, that looked like a mountain holding the earth on its head, and were amazed by that sight.
1.40.18
ततः प्रदक्षिणं कृत्वा
सगरस्य महात्मनः ।
षष्टिः पुत्रसहस्राणि
पश्चिमां बिभिदुर्दिशम् ।
पश्चिमायामपि दिशि
महान्तमचलोपमम् ।
दिशागजं सौमनसम्
ददृशुस्ते महाबलाः ॥
Then those sixty thousand sons of Mahātma Sagara paid respects to that mighty elephant by circumambulating. They went, then, digging to the west. And even there, those mighty sons saw a mighty elephant named Soumanasa, that looked like a mountain.
1.40.20
तं ते प्रदक्षिणं कृत्वा
पृष्ट्वा चापि निरामयम् ।
खनन्तः समुपक्रान्ता
दिशं हैमवतीं ततः ॥
They paid respects by circumambulating and enquired about its wellbeing. Then they went on to dig in the direction of the Himalayas (i.e., to the North).
1.40.21
उत्तरस्यां रघुश्रेष्ठ
ददृशुर्हिमपाण्डरम् ।
भद्रं भद्रेण वपुषा
धारयन्तं महीमिमाम् ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! In the northerly direction also, they saw an elephant that was white like snow, which was holding the weight of the earth with its amazing body.
1.40.22
समालभ्य ततः सर्वे
कृत्वा चैनं प्रदक्षिणम् ।
षष्टिः पुत्रसहस्राणि
बिभिदुर्वसुधातलम् ॥
They then paid respects by touching its body and circumambulating. Then, those sixty thousand sons continued to dig the earth.
1.40.23
ततः प्रागुत्तरां गत्वा
सागराः प्रथितां दिशम् ।
रोषादभ्यखनन् सर्वे
पृथिवीं सगरात्मजाः ॥
Then they went on in the north-easterly direction. All those renowned sons of Sagara, and dug all of the earth, with rage.
1.40.24
ते तु सर्वे महात्मानो
भीमवेगा महाबलाः ।
ददृशुः कपिलं तत्र
वासुदेवं सनातनम् ॥
Those mighty strong and blazingly fast sons with great zeal in their minds saw Kapila, who was a form of Vāsudeva (Vishṇu).
1.40.25
हयं च तस्य देवस्य
चरन्तमविदूरतः ।
प्रहर्षमतुलं प्राप्ताः
सर्वे ते रघुनन्दन ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! They also saw the horse moving around nearby. They all felt extremely happy.
1.40.26
ते तं हयहरं ज्ञात्वा
क्रोधपर्याकुलेक्षणाः ।
खनित्रलाङ्गलधरा
नानावृक्षशिलाधराः ।
अभ्यधावन्त सङ्क्रुद्धाः
तिष्ठ तिष्ठेति चाब्रुवन् ॥
They recognized that it was their great horse. They were enraged with Kapila and sprang on him wielding the earth-digging instruments, stones and trees in their hands, shouting at him: 'Stop!'
1.40.28
अस्माकं त्वं हि तुरगम्
यज्ञीयं हृतवानसि ।
दुर्मेधस्त्वं हि सम्प्राप्तान्
विद्धि नः सगरात्मजान् ॥
You have abducted the horse of our Yajña! O evil mind! Know that we are sons of Sagara!
1.40.29
श्रुत्वा तु वचनं तेषाम्
कपिलो रघुनन्दन ।
रोषेण महताऽऽविष्टो
हुङ्कारमकरोत्तदा ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Hearing those words, Kapila became very angry and shouted at them:
1.40.30
ततस्तेनाप्रमेयेन
कपिलेन महात्मना ।
भस्मराशीकृताः सर्वे
काकुत्स्थ सगरात्मजाः ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! The unimaginable power of that Mahātma Kapila burned the sons of Sagara to ashes.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे चत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fortieth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
