Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 29
[This Sarga, like the previous one, which is set in long meter, though beautiful in its own right, is very likely unauthentic. Its content is a repeat of the last part of the Sarga 27.]
In this Sarga, Seetā sees many good omens, which assuage her sufferings and renew her strength.
5.29.1
तथागतां तां व्यथितामनिन्दितां
व्यपेतहर्षां परिदीनमानसाम् ।
शुभां निमित्तानि शुभानि भेजिरे
नरं श्रिया जुष्टमिवोपजीविनः ॥
Just as those who enjoy living as dependents gather around a person who becomes wealthy, good omens came together to that auspicious and blemish-less lady who was in such a dire situation with all her joys wiped out and her mind extremely distressed.
5.29.2
तस्याः शुभं वाममरालपक्ष्म
राजीवृतं कृष्णविशालशुक्लम् ।
प्रास्पन्दतैकं नयनं सुकेश्या
मीनाहतं पद्ममिवाभिताम्रम् ॥
One of the eyes of that lady with lovely tresses, the left one, black, white, wide and beautiful with curved eye lashes fluttered a little, like a red lotus rubbed against by a fish.
5.29.3
भुजश्च चार्वञ्चितपीनवृत्तः
परार्थ्यकालागरुचन्दनार्हः ।
अनुत्तमेनाध्युषितः प्रियेण 
चिरेण वामः समवेपताऽशु ॥
Right then, her left shoulder, which was beautiful, round and plump, that once rested on her peerless beloved, which deserved the best black Agaru and sandal, also fluttered for quite a while.
5.29.4
गजेन्द्रहस्तप्रतिमश्च पीन
स्तयोर्द्वयोः सम्हतयोः सुजातः ।
प्रस्पन्दमानः पुनरूरुरस्या
रामं पुरस्तात् स्थितमाचचक्षे ॥
One of her two well-formed thighs that were pressed against each other, plump like the trunk of an elephant, also fluttered, telling (her) that Rāma was right in front of her.
5.29.5
शुभं पुनर्हेमसमानवर्णम् 
ईषद्रजोध्वस्तमिवामलाक्ष्याः ।
वासस्स्थितायाश्शिखराग्रदन्त्याः 
किञ्चित्परिस्रंसत चारुगात्य्राः ॥
And as she, with her beautiful figure, bright eyes and teeth with sharp edges, stood there, her lovely golden garment slipped a little bit.
5.29.6
एतैर्निमित्तैरपरैश्च सुभ्रूः 
सम्बोधिता प्रागपि साधु सिद्धैः ।
वातातपक्लान्तमिव प्रणष्टं 
वर्षेण बीजं प्रतिसंजहर्ष ॥
Prodded by these and other good omens, which were known to forebode good times, she of beautiful eyebrows felt elated, like a seed that is all but lost, dried up by heat and dry winds does, upon the fall of rain.
5.29.7
तस्याः पुनर्बिम्बफलाधरोष्ठं
स्वक्षिभ्रुकेशान्तमरालपक्ष्म ।
वक्त्रं बभासे सितचारुदन्तम्
राहोर्मुखाच्चन्द्र इव प्रमुक्तः ॥
Her face, with its Bimba fruit like lips, beautiful eyes, eyebrows, curved eyelashes and lovely white teeth, shone like the moon emerging out of the eclipse by Rahu.
5.29.8
सा वीतशोका व्यपनीततन्द्री
शान्तज्वरा हर्षविबुद्धसत्त्वा ।
अशोभतार्या वदनेन शुक्ले 
शीतांशुना रात्रिरिवोदितेन ॥
With her grief and fatigue gone, her sufferings assuaged and her strength renewed with joy, that noble lady shone like the night of the waxing fortnight, her face like the rising moon of cool rays.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे एकोनत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty ninth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
