Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 62
In this Sarga, the Vānaras continue to drink and make merry, encouraged by Hanuman and Aṅgada. Dadhimukha who was in charge of the Vana, with the help of his guards, tries to stop him. Drunk and ecstatic about finding Seetā, the Vānaras do not heed the orders of Dadhimukha. Even Aṅgada beats up Dadhimukha, uncle of his own father. Feeling helpless, he goes, along with his guards, to report to Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva.
5.62.1
तानुवाच हरिश्रेष्ठो
हनुमान्वानरर्षभः ।
अव्यग्रमनसो यूयम्
मधु सेवत वानराः ।
अहमावारयिष्यामि
युष्माकं परिपन्थिनः ॥
Then Hanumān, the best of Vānaras, and a bull among them, told them, ‘Go ahead and drink the liquor as you please, without any hesitation, I will stop anyone who tries to stop you!’
5.62.2
श्रुत्वा हनुमतो वाक्यम्
हरीणां प्रवरोऽङ्गदः ।
प्रत्युवाच प्रसन्नात्मा
पिबन्तु हरयो मधु ॥
On hearing those words of Hanumān, Aṅgada, the best of the best of Vānaras joined him, saying in a merry tone, ‘May the Vānaras enjoy drinking!’
5.62.3
अवश्यं कृतकार्यस्य
वाक्यं हनुमतो मया ।
अकार्यमपि कर्तव्यम्
किमङ्ग पुनरीदृशम् ॥
I must oblige whatever Hanumān asks for, even if it is something that should not be done, for he has accomplished his (great) mission; then, what to say of something (as simple as) this?
5.62.4
अङ्गदस्य मुखाच्छ्रुत्वा
वचनं वानरर्षभाः ।
साधुसाध्विति संहृष्टा
वानराः प्रत्यपूजयन् ॥
Hearing those words uttered by Aṅgada, those bulls among Vānaras, shouted back to him in joy, ‘Excellent, excellent!’
5.62.5
पूजयित्वाङ्गदं सर्वे
वानरा वानरर्षभम् ।
जग्मुर्मधुवनं यत्र
नदीवेग इव द्रुमम् ॥
Paying due respects to Aṅgada, the bull among Vānaras, all those Vānaras rushed to the Madhuvana swiftly, like a river in spate.
5.62.6
ते प्रविष्टा मधुवनम्
पालानाक्रम्य वीर्यतः ।
अतिसर्गाच्च पटवो
दृष्ट्वा श्रुत्वा च मैथिलीम् ।
पपुस्सर्वे मधु तदा
रसवत्फलमाददुः ॥
Entering that Madhuvana, overpowering the guards with their valor, bolstered by the due permission (of Aṅgada) and (ecstatic about) finding and hearing about Maithili, they drank all the liquor and enjoyed the juicy fruits.
5.62.8
उत्पत्य च ततस्सर्वे
वनपालान् समागतान् ।
ताडयन्ति स्म शतशः
सक्तान्मधुवने तदा ॥
They jumped on all the guards of the Vana who came together in their hundreds and beat them up in that Madhuvana.
5.62.9
मधूनि द्रोणमात्राणि
बाहुभिः परिगृह्य ते ।
पिबन्ति सहितास्सर्वे
निघ्नन्ति स्म तथापरे ॥
All of them got together, snatched the liquor and drank it pitcher by pitcher; and some even broke them off!
5.62.10
केचित्पीत्वा प्रविध्यन्ति
मधूनि मधुपिङ्गलाः ।
मधूच्छिष्टेन केचिच्च
जग्मुरन्योन्यमुत्कटाः ॥
Some, of honey complexion, drank as much as they threw away. Some, with empty vessels, jumped on to each other, totally drunk.
5.62.11
अपरे वृक्षमूले तु
शाखां गृह्य व्यवस्थिताः ।
अत्यर्थं च मदग्लानाः
पर्णान्यास्तीर्य शेरते ॥
Some grabbed tree branches and slid down to the base of the trees. Some, drunken to a fault, slept, making a bed out of leaves.
5.62.12
उन्मत्तभूताः प्लवगाः
मधुमत्ताश्च हृष्टवत् ।
क्षिपन्ति च तथान्योन्यम्
स्खलन्ति च तथाऽपरे ॥
Intoxicated by the liquor, the Vānaras seemed as if they were hysterical with euphoria. They pushed away each other and others lost their balance.
5.62.13
केचित् क्ष्वेलां प्रकुर्वन्ति
केचित् कूजन्ति हृष्टवत् ।
हरयोमधुना मत्ताः
केचित्सुप्ता महीतले ॥
Some let off war whoops. Some cooed with ecstasy. Some of the Vānaras, intoxicated by the liquor, slept off on the bare ground.
5.62.14
कृत्वा केचिद्धसन्त्यन्ये
केचित्कुर्वन्ति चेतरत् ।
कृत्वा केचिद्वदन्त्यन्ये
केचिद्बुध्यन्ति चेतरत् ॥
Some laughed, doing something silly. Others did something else that was equally silly. Some bragged, doing something else. And some (enjoyed) finding a second meaning in it.
5.62.15
येऽप्यत्र मधुपाला स्स्युः
प्रेष्या दधिमुखस्य तु ।
तेऽपि तैर्वानरैर्भीमैः
प्रतिषिद्धा दिशो गताः ॥
And the guards of the liquor, who worked there for Dadhimukha, accosted by the fierce Vānaras, ran away in all directions.
5.62.16
जानुभिस्तु प्रकृष्टाश्च
देवमार्गं प्रदर्शिताः ।
अब्रुवन् परमोद्विग्ना
गत्वा दधिमुखं वचः ॥
Dragged by their knees, and shown the private parts, the extremely disturbed guards went to Dadhimukha and reported:
5.62.17
हनूमता दत्तवरैः
हतं मधुवनं बलात् ।
वयं च जानुभिः कृष्टा
देवमार्गं च दर्शिताः ॥
With the free reign given to them by Hanumān, they are using all their power to destroy the Madhuvana. Dragged by our knees, we were shown their private parts.
5.62.18
ततो दधिमुखः क्रुद्धो
वनपस्तत्र वानरः ।
हतं मधुवनं श्रुत्वा
सान्त्वयामास तान् हरीन् ॥
Hearing that the Madhuvana was destroyed, Dadhimukha who was in charge, was furious. He then calmed down those Vānaras, (saying):
5.62.19
इहागच्छत गच्छामो
वानरान् बलदर्पितान् ।
बलेन वारयिष्यामो
मधु भक्षयतो वयम् ॥
Come here, we will go and stop those Vānaras, Who, haughty with their strength, are swilling the liquor forcibly.
5.62.20
श्रुत्वा दधिमुखस्येदम्
वचनं वानरर्षभाः ।
पुनर्वीरा मधुवनम्
तेनैव सहसा ययुः ॥
Hearing those words of Dadhimukha, those bulls among Vānaras, Veeras, went back again to the Madhuvana at once, along with him.
5.62.21
मध्ये चैषां दधिमुखः
प्रगृह्य तरसा तरुम् ।
समभ्यधावद्वेगेन
ते च सर्वे प्लवङ्गमाः ॥
Dadhimukha, who was in their midst, plucked a tree with all his strength and dashed, with all the other Vānaras doing the same.
5.62.22
ते शिलाः पादपांश्चापि
पर्वतांश्चापि वानराः ।
गृहीत्वाऽऽभ्यगमन् क्रुद्धा
यत्र ते कपिकुञ्जराः ॥
Grabbing rocks, trees and boulders, the Vānaras went, in their fury, to where those elephants among Vānaras were.
5.62.23
ते स्वामिवचनं वीरा
हृदयेष्ववसज्य तत् ।
त्वरया ह्यभ्यधावन्त
सालतालशिलायुधाः ॥
Taking the command of their master to heart, they marched fast, armed with Sāla and Tāla trees and boulders.
5.62.24
वृक्षस्थांश्च तलस्थांश्च
वानरान् बलदर्पितान् ।
अभ्यक्रामंस्ततो वीराः
पालास्तत्र सहस्रशः ॥
Thousands of those guards, Veeras, who were on the ground and on the trees attacked the Vānaras who were haughty with their strength.
5.62.25
अथ दृष्ट्वा दधिमुखम्
क्रुद्धं वानरपुङ्गवाः ।
अभ्यधावन्त वेगेन
हनुमत्प्रमुखास्तदा ॥
On seeing that furious Dadhimukha, the bulls among Vānaras, such as Hanumān, went running (towards him) swiftly.
5.62.26
तं सवृक्षं महाबाहुम्
आपतन्तं महाबलम् ।
आर्यकं प्राहरत्तत्र
बाहुभ्यां कुपितोऽङ्गदः ॥
Then the angry Aṅgada, with his hands, hit the uncle of his father, of immense strength and mighty arm, who was armed with a tree.
5.62.27
मदान्धश्च न वेदैनम्
आर्यकोऽयं ममेति सः ।
अथैनं निष्पिपेषाशु
वेगवद्वसुधातले ॥
Blinded by intoxication, he did not even realize that he was the uncle of his own father, (who deserved to be respected). He threw him down on the ground and pummeled him.
5.62.28
स भग्नबाहूरुभुजो
विह्वलश्शोणितोक्षितः ।
मुमोह सहसा वीरो
मुहूर्तं कपिकुञ्जरः ॥
His arms, thighs and shoulders smashed, his head in a whirl, and with blood all over him, that Veera, an elephant among Vānaras, fell  immediately in a swoon, for a while.
5.62.29
स समाश्वस्य सहसा
सङ्कृद्धो राजमातुलः ।
वानरान्वारयामास
दण्डेन मधुमोहितान् ॥
Regaining his senses and becoming furious, that Vānara, uncle of the king, tried to stop those inebriated Vānaras.
5.62.30
स कथञ्चिद्विमुक्तस्स्तैः
वानरैर्वानरर्षभः ।
उवाचैकान्तमाश्रित्य
भृत्यान् स्वान् समुपागतान् ॥
Escaping somehow from those Vānaras, that bull among Vānaras went aside and spoke to his staff who gathered there:
5.62.31
एते तिष्ठन्तु गच्छामो
भर्ता नो यत्र वानरः ।
सुग्रीवो विपुलग्रीवः
सह रामेण तिष्ठति ॥
Let them be there. Let us go to where our master, Sugreeva of the broad neck, is, with Rāma.
5.62.32
सर्वं चैवाङ्गदे दोषम्
श्रावयिष्यामि पार्थिवे ।
अमर्षी वचनं श्रुत्वा
घातयिष्यति वानरान् ॥
We will tell the king that it is all the fault of Aṅgada. Enraged on hearing all about this, he will punish the Vānaras.
5.62.33
इष्टं मधुवनं ह्येतत्
पार्थिवस्य महात्मनः ।
पितृपैतामहं दिव्यम्
देवैरपि दुरासदम् ॥
This Madhuvana, that was passed on to him by this fathers and forefathers, which even the Dēvas dare not touch, is dear to that king, a Mahātma.
5.62.34
स वानरानिमान् सर्वान्
मधुलुब्धान् गतायुषः ।
घातयिष्यति दण्डेन
सुग्रीवस्ससुहृज्जनान् ॥
All these Vānaras, who are after the liquor, are as good as dead; Sugreeva will punish them with death and everyone who sides with them.
5.62.35
वध्या ह्येते दुरात्मानो
नृपाज्ञापरिभाविनः ।
अमर्षप्रभवो रोषः
सफलो नो भविष्यति ॥
These rascals, who disrespected the King’s orders, indeed deserve to be killed. Our resentment, born from being disdained, will thus be redeemed.
5.62.36
एवमुक्त्वा दधिमुखो
वनपालान्महाबलः ।
जगाम सहसोत्पत्य
वनपालैस्समन्वितः ॥
Having said this to those guards of the Vana, the mighty strong Dadhimukha flew off immediately, along with the guards.
5.62.37
निमेषान्तरमात्रेण
स हि प्राप्तो वनालयः ।
सहस्रांशुसुतो धीमान्
सुग्रीवो यत्र वानरः ॥
In no time, that Vānara reached the place where the sagacious Sugreeva, the son of the Thousand-rayed, was.
Thousand-rayed refers to the sun.
5.62.38
रामं च लक्ष्मणं चैव
दृष्ट्वा सुग्रीवमेव च ।
समप्रतिष्ठां जगतीम्
आकाशान्निपपात ह ॥
He landed from the sky on the flat ground, where he saw Rāma, Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva.
5.62.39
सन्निपत्य महावीर्यः
सर्वैस्तैः परिवारितः ।
हरिर्दधिमुखः पालैः
पालानां परमेश्वरः ।
स दीनवदनो भूत्वा
कृत्वा शिरसि चाञ्जलिम् ।
सुग्रीवस्य शुभौ मूर्ध्ना
चरणौ पत्यपीडयत् ॥
Descending along with the guards, the Vānara Dadhimukha of great valor and chief of all those guards, joined his palms and placed them on his head in reverence, with a woebegone face. He then touched the auspicious feet of Sugreeva with his head.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे द्विषष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty second Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
