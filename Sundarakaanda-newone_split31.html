Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 31
In this Sarga, Hanumān, atop the Ṡiṃṡupa tree, sings the stories of Daṡaratha, Rāma, Rāma’s exile, Seetā’s abduction, Rāma’s friendship with Sugreeva, Rāma’s slaying of Vāli, Vānaras going in search of Seetā, his crossing the ocean and finally finding Seetā.
Hearing the words of Hanumān, Seetā becomes extremely surprised and feels extremely happy. She looks around and above and sees Hanumān on the tree.
5.31.1
एवं बहुविधां चिन्ताम्
चिन्तयित्वा महाकपिः ।
संश्रवे मधुरं वाक्यम्
वैदेह्या व्याजहार ह ॥
The great Vānara, thinking the many aspects thus, spoke these sweet words so that Vaidēhee could hear them:
5.31.2
राजा दशरथो नाम
रथकुञ्जरवाजिमान् ।
पुण्यशीलो महाकीर्तिः
इक्ष्वाकूणां महायशाः ॥
There was a king by name Daṡaratha, who commanded many chariots, elephants and horses. He is famously known as a man of Puṇya deeds. He is of great renown among Ikshwākus.
5.31.3
राजर्षीणां गुणश्रेष्ठः
तपसा चर्षिभिस्समः ।
चक्रवर्तिकुले जातः
पुरन्दरसमो बले ॥
He excelled among Rājarshis in virtues. He surpassed even Rishis in his Tapa. He was born into a lineage of emperors. He equaled Indra in his strength.
5.31.4
अहिंसारतिरक्षुद्रो
घृणी सत्यपराक्रमः ।
मुख्यश्चेक्ष्वाकुवंशस्य
लक्ष्मीवान् लक्ष्मिवर्धनः ॥
He was committed to non-violence. He was the opposite of anything that is base. He was compassionate and a man of true valor. He was a prominent person in the lineage of Ikshwākus. He was rich and enhanced the prosperity (of his kingdom).
5.31.5
पार्थिवव्यञ्जनैर्युक्तः
पृथुश्रीः पार्थिवर्षभः ।
पृथिव्यां चतुरन्तायाम्
विश्रुतस्सुखदस्सुखी ॥
He had all the marks of royalty. He was wealthy with a vast expanse of lands. He was the bull among kings. His fame spread to the four corners of the world. He enjoyed comforts and ensured that everyone had them.
5.31.6
तस्य पुत्रः प्रियो ज्येष्ठः
ताराधिपनिभाननः ।
रामो नाम विशेषज्ञः
श्रेष्ठस्सर्वधनुष्मताम् ॥
His beloved elder son by name Rāma, whose face shone like the lord of stars, is the best among all archers and is cognizant of everything that matters.
5.31.7
रक्षिता स्वस्य वृत्तस्य
स्वजनस्य च रक्षिता ।
रक्षिता जीवलोकस्य
धर्मस्य च परन्तपः ॥
Rāma safeguards his character, Dharma, his people, nay, all beings in the world, and ruthlessly torments his foes.
5.31.8
तस्य सत्याभिसन्धस्य
वृद्धस्य वचनात्पितुः ।
सभार्यस्सह च भ्रात्रा
वीरः प्रव्राजितो वनम् ॥
Because of the word given by his father who was a man of his word, that Veera went in exile along with his wife and brother.
5.31.9
तेन तत्र महारण्ये
मृगयां परिधावता ।
राक्षसा निहताश्शूरा
बहवः कामरूपिणः ॥
He used to go out hunting in that great forest, and kill many a strong Rākshasa who could take any form at will.
5.31.10
जनस्थानवधं श्रुत्वा
हतौ च खरदूषणौ ।
ततस्त्वमर्षापहृता
जानकी रावणेन तु ।
वञ्चयित्वा वने रामम्
मृगरूपेण मायया ॥
Hearing about the killing of Khara and Dūshaṇa in Janasthāna Rāvaṇa became outraged and abducted Jānaki, after distracting Rāma, tricking him in the guise of a deer.
5.31.11
स मार्गमाणस्तां देवीम्
रामस्सीतामनिन्दिताम् ।
आससाद वने मित्रम्
सुग्रीवं नाम वानरम् ॥
While searching for the blemish-less lady in that Vana, Rāma met a friend, a Vānara known as Sugreeva.
5.31.12
ततस्स वालिनं हत्वा
रामः परपुरञ्जयः ।
प्रायच्छत्कपिराज्यं तत्
सुग्रीवाय महाबलः ॥
Then, Rāma who conquered the cities of his enemies, killed Vāli and gave the kingdom of Vānaras to Sugreeva.
5.31.13
सुग्रीवेणापि सन्दिष्टा
हरयः कामरूपिणः ।
दिक्षु सर्वासु तां देवीम्
विचिन्वन्ति सहस्रशः ॥
Ordered by Sugreeva, thousands of Vānaras who can take any form at will, went in all directions in search of that lady.
5.31.14
अहं सम्पातिवचना
शतयोजनमायतम् ।
अस्या हेतोर्विशालाक्ष्याः
सागरं वेगवान्प्लुतः ॥
Following the guidance given by Saṃpāti, I, who am extremely fast, jumped over the ocean that is one hundred Yōjanas wide, for the sake of that wide-eyed one.
5.31.15
यथारूपां यथावर्णां
यथालक्ष्मीवतीं च ताम् ।
अश्रौषं राघवस्याहम्
सेयमासादिता मया ॥
Now I have found her, who is exactly as I heard Rāghava describing, in her form, complexion and the marks on her body.
5.31.16
विररामैवमुक्त्वासौ
वाचं वानरपुङ्गवः ।
जानकी चापि तच्छ्रुत्वा
परं विस्मयमागता ॥
With those words, the bull among Vānaras completed what he wanted to say. Jānaki was very surprised to hear those words.
5.31.17
ततस्सा वक्रकेशान्ता
सुकेशी केशसंवृतम् ।
उन्नम्य वदनं भीरुः
शिंशुपावृक्षमैक्षत ॥
Then she, whose tresses had curly ends, timidly lifted her face that was skirted by that beautiful hair, and looked at the Ṡiṃṡupa tree.
Valmiki, vivid always in his description of the visuals, is describing here an aerial shot/view of Seetā’s face.
5.31.18
निशम्य सीता वचनं कपेश्च
दिशश्च सर्वाः प्रदिशश्च वीक्ष्य ।
स्वयं प्रहर्षं परमं जगाम
सर्वात्मना राममनुस्मरन्ती ॥
Seetā, whose own mind was preoccupied with the thoughts of Rāma, heard those words of the Vānara and felt extremely happy and looked around in all directions.
5.31.19
सा तिर्यगूर्ध्वं च तथाप्यधस्तात्
निरीक्षमाणा तमचिन्त्यबुद्धिम् ।
ददर्श पिङ्गाधिपतेरमात्यं
वातात्मजं सूर्यमिवोदयस्थम् ॥
Then looking up, down and around, she saw the unimaginably astute Hanumān, the son of Vāyu and minister of the Vānara Lord who looked like the sun rising above the eastern mountain.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे एकत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty first Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
