Aranya Kaanda - Sarga 28
In this Sarga, with everyone including Dūshaṇa and Triṡira dead, the fight between Rāma and Khara becomes personal, each gauging the other and each determined to kill the other.
Each of them rains showers of arrows covering the sky without a gap. Khara showing his skill and displaying his dexterity, gains upper hand to begin with, by breaking the bow of Rāma.
Rāma then becomes furious and takes the bow of Vishṇu given to him by Agastya. With thirteen arrows, like child’s play, he strikes Khara’s chariot, kills the horses, the charioteer and then hits Khara on the chest.
Khara jumps out of the broken chariot with a club in his hand. Dēvas and Ṛshis applaud Rāma’s unimaginable feat, with their palms joined in reverence.
3.28.1
निहतं दूषणं दृष्ट्वा
रणे त्रिशिरसा सह ।
खरस्याप्यभवत्त्रासो
दृष्ट्वा रामस्य विक्रमम् ॥
Seeing Dūshaṇa and also Triṡira slain in the fight and seeing the valor of Rāma, Khara became fearful.
3.28.2
स दृष्ट्वा राक्षसं सैन्यम्
अविषह्यं महाबलः ।
हतमेकेन रामेण
त्रिशिरोदूषणावपि ।
तद्बलं हतभूयिष्ठं
विमनाः प्रेक्ष्य राक्षसः ।
आससाद खरो रामं
नमुचिर्वासवं यथा ॥
Seeing that immense army, including Triṡira and Dūshaṇa, which none could withstand, destroyed by Rāma all alone, and his forces practically wiped out, the Rākshasa Khara lost his mind and attacked Rāma like Namuci attacking Vāsava.
3.28.4
विकृष्य बलवच्चापं
नाराचान्रक्तभोजनान् ।
खरश्चिक्षेप रामाय
क्रुद्धानाशीविषानिव ॥
Drawing (the string of his) bow with all his strength, Khara shot at Rāma blood-thirsty Nārācās that resembled angry vipers.
Nārācā is a type of arrow.
3.28.5
ज्यां विधून्वन्सुबहुशः
शिक्षयास्त्राणि दर्शयन् ।
चचार समरे मार्गान्
शरै रथगतः खरः ॥
Khara, on his chariot, wrought many a path in that fight. He pulled the string (of his bow) in different styles displaying his skill with Astras.
3.28.6
स सर्वाश्च दिशो बाणैः
प्रदिशश्च महारथः ।
पूरयामास तं दृष्ट्वा
रामोऽपि सुमहद्धनुः ॥
That great charioter filled all the quarters and in between with his arrows. Rāma also, seeing him (do that), became ready with his bow.
3.28.7
स सायकैर्दुर्विषहैः
सुस्फुलिङ्गैरिवाग्निभिः ।
नभश्चकाराविवरं
पर्जन्य इव वृष्टिभिः ॥
He filled the sky without a gap like a cloud would with a downpour, with arrows that were unbearable like fire that crackled and spewed sparks.
3.28.8
तद्बभूव शितैर्बाणैः
खररामविसर्जितैः ।
पर्याकाशमनाकाशं
सर्वतश्शरसङ्कुलम् ॥
In every direction, the sky was filled without a gap with the sharp arrows that were let off by Khara and Rāma.
3.28.9
शरजालावृतस्सूर्यो
न तदा स्म प्रकाशते ।
अन्योन्यवधसंरम्भात्
उभयोस्संप्रयुध्यतोः ॥
With both of them fighting thus, determined to slay each other, the sun did not shine, clouded by the torrents of arrows.
3.28.10
ततो नालीकनाराचैः
तीक्ष्णाग्रैश्च विकर्णिभिः ।
आजघान खरो रामं
तोत्रैरिव महाद्विपम् ॥
Khara then struck Rāma with Nārācās, Vikarṇis, pipe-shaft arrows and sharp-nosed arrows like a tusker with a goad.
3.28.11
तं रथस्थं धनुष्पाणिं
राक्षसं पर्यवस्थितम् ।
ददृशुस्सर्वभूतानि
पाशहस्तमिवान्तकम् ॥
All creatures felt that that Rākshasa firmly standing on the chariot and holding a bow was like death with a noose in his hand.
3.28.12
हन्तारं सर्वसैन्यस्य
पौरुषे पर्यवस्थितम् ।
परिश्रान्तं महासत्वं
मेने रामं खरस्तदा ॥
Khara imagined that the mighty strong Rāma was exhausted in killing the entire army, though he was standing his ground firmly and boldly.
3.28.13
तं सिंहमिव विक्रान्तं
सिंहविक्रान्तगामिनम् ।
दृष्ट्वा नोद्विजते रामः
सिंहः क्षुद्रमृगं यथा ॥
Seeing him, who was mighty like a lion with the gait of a mighty lion, Rāma was no more agitated than a lion would at the sight of a menial beast.
3.28.14
ततः सूर्यनिकाशेन
रथेन महता खरः ।
आससाद रणे रामं
पतङ्ग इव पावकम् ॥
Khara, on his great chariot that was (shining) like the sun, advanced towards Rāma in that fight like a moth rushing to a flame.
3.28.15
ततोऽस्य सशरं चापं
मुष्टिदेशे महात्मनः ।
खरश्चिच्छेद रामस्य
दर्शयन्पाणिलाघवम् ॥
Khara, showing his dexterity, then cut Mahātma Rāma’s bow, on which an arrow was also mounted, at the point where it was held by the fist.
3.28.16
स पुनस्त्वपरान्सप्त
शरानादाय वर्मणि ।
निजघान खरः क्रुद्धः
शक्राशनिसमप्रभान् ॥
Then, in his wrath, Khara took seven more arrows that resembled the thunderbolt of Ṡakra, and hit the armor (of Rāma).
3.28.17
ततस्तत्प्रहतं बाणैः
खरमुक्तैस्सुपर्वभिः ।
पपात कवचं भूमौ
रामस्यादित्यवर्चसः ॥
Hit by those arrows of smooth nodes released by Khara, the armor of Rāma, which was shining like the sun, fell on the ground.
3.28.18
ततश्शरसहस्रेण
राममप्रतिमौजसम् ।
अर्दयित्वा महानादं
ननाद समरे खरः ॥
Then hurting Rāma of incomparable valor with a thousand arrows, Khara let off a mighty war-whoop.
3.28.19
स शरैरर्दितः क्रुद्धः
सर्वगात्रेषु राघवः ।
रराज समरे रामो
विधूमोऽग्निरिव ज्वलन् ॥
With all the limbs hurt by those arrows, Rāma, the scion of Raghus, became angry, blazing like a smokeless flame in that battlefield.
3.28.20
ततो गम्भीरनिर्ह्रादं
रामश्शत्रुनिबर्हणः ।
चकारान्ताय स रिपोः
सज्यमन्यन्महद्धनुः ॥
Then Rāma, destroyer of foe, took another great bow and strung it making a deep rumbling noise, to put an end to that foe.
3.28.21
सुमहद्वैष्णवं यत्तत्
अतिसृष्टं महर्षिणा ।
वरं तद्धनुरुद्यम्य
खरं समभिधावत ॥
Raising aloft that best of bows of Vishṇu, which was given by Maharshi (Agastya), he dashed forth to face Khara.
3.28.22
ततः कनकपुङ्खैस्तु
शरैस्सन्नतपर्वभिः ।
बिभेद रामस्सङ्क्रुद्धः
खरस्य समरे ध्वजम् ॥
Rāma, in his rage, then broke the flag of Khara with arrows that had gold girdles and furrowed nodes.
3.28.23
स दर्शनीयो बहुधा
विकीर्णः काञ्चनध्वजः ।
जगाम धरणीं सूर्यो
देवतानामिवाज्ञया ॥
That eyeful flag with golden post, torn into many pieces, fell straight onto the ground, as if it were the sun falling, ordered by Dēvas (to appear on the earth).
3.28.24
तं चतुर्भिः खरः क्रुद्धो
रामं गात्रेषु मार्गणैः ।
विव्याध युधि मर्मज्ञो
मातङ्गमिव तोमरैः ॥
Then, in that fight, the enraged Khara, who knew the vulnerable points (of the human body), struck Rāma’s limbs with four arrows like one would an elephant with spears.
3.28.25
स रामो बहुभिर्बाणैः
खरकार्मुकनिस्सृतैः ।
विद्धो रुधिरसिक्ताङ्गो
बभूव रुषितो भृशम् ॥
Hit by the many arrows that sprung form the bow of Khara, and with his limbs wet with blood, Rāma became furious.
3.28.26
स धनुर्धन्विनां श्रेष्ठः
प्रगृह्य परमाहवे ।
मुमोच परमेष्वासः
षट्छरानभिलक्षितान् ॥
He, the best of archers and the possessor of the ultimate bow, took the bow and with perfect aim, released six arrows.
3.28.27
शिरस्येकेन बाणेन
द्वाभ्यां बह्वोरथार्दयत् ।
त्रिभिश्चन्द्रार्धवक्त्रैश्च
वक्षस्यभिजघान ह ॥
He hit the head with one arrow, harrowed the arms with two and struck the chest with three of crescent head.
3.28.28
ततः पश्चान्महातेजा
नाराचान्भास्करोपमान् ।
जिघांसू राक्षसङ्कृद्धः
त्रयोदश समाददे ॥
Then, in his fury, resolved to kill the Rākshasa, he, of immense power, took thirteen sun-bright arrows.
3.28.29
ततोऽस्य युगमेकेन
चतुर्भिश्चतुरो हयान् ।
षष्ठेन तु शिरस्सङ्ख्ये
खरस्य रथसारथेः ।
त्रिभिस्त्रिवेणुं बलवान्
द्वाभ्यामक्षं महाबलः ।
द्वादशेन तु बाणेन
खरस्य सशरं धनुः ।
छित्वा वज्रनिकाशेन
राघवः प्रहसन्निव ।
त्रयोदशेनेन्द्रसमो
बिभेद समरे खरम् ॥
Rāghava, an equal in his immense strength to Indra, in that fight, struck the yoke of Khara’s chariot with one arrow, its four horses with four, its driver’s head with the sixth, its axle with another three and its axle-pole with two more.
Breaking the bow of Khara along with its arrow with the twelfth, he hit Khara himself with the thunderbolt-like thirteenth arrow, laughing playfully.
3.28.32
प्रभग्नधन्वा विरथो
हताश्वो हतसारथिः ।
गदापाणिरवप्लुत्य
तस्थौ भूमौ खरस्तदा ॥
With the bow broken, with no chariot, the horses killed, and the charioteer dead Khara jumped down to the ground taking a club in his hand.
3.28.33
तत्कर्म रामस्य महारथस्य
समेत्य देवाश्च महर्षयश्च ।
अपूजयन्प्राञ्जलयः प्रहृष्टाः
तदा विमानाग्रगतास्समेताः ॥
Then the Dēvas on the Vimānas rejoiced along with the Maharshis (on the ground). Joining their palms in reverence, they lauded that great feat of Rāma, the great charioter.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अरण्यकाण्डे अष्टाविंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the twenty eighth Sarga in Araṇya Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
