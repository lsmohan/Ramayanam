Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 55
In this Sarga, it suddenly occurs to Hanumān that Seetā might have also been burnt by the fire that he set off. He curses himself for succumbing to anger and doing it thoughtlessly. He knew that if Seetā was harmed, the entire purpose of his mission would be lost and no one but he would be responsible for that. With Seetā lost, he thinks, Rāma and Lakshmaṇa would not survive, and with them lost, Sugreeva and Vānaras would not survive, and so too Bharata and Ṡatrughna; and the entire world would be submerged in grief, with the lineage of Ikshwākus thus destroyed.
When he ponders thus, he sees some good omens and regains his confidence. He feels certain that Seetā cannot be touched by fire, as she had her own immense virtues for her armor. At the same time he hears amazed Cāraṇas talking about the unimaginable task that Hanumān had accomplished by burning down Laṅkā  leaving Seetā alone unscathed.
Hanumān feels happy knowing that Seetā is unhurt and decides to see her again before returning.
5.55.1
सन्दीप्यमानां विध्वस्ताम्
त्रस्तरक्षोगणां पुरीम् ।
आवेक्ष्य हनुमान् लङ्काम्
चिन्तयामास वानरः ।
तस्याभूत्सुमहांस्त्रासः
कुत्सा चात्मन्यजायत ॥
After seeing the city of Laṅkā completely burnt, destroyed and its hordes of Rākshasas gripped in fear, a great fear gripped Hanumān, the Vānara, and he thought, cursing himself:
5.55.2
लङ्कां प्रदहता कर्म
किंस्वित्कृतमिदं मया ।
धन्यास्ते पुरुषश्रेष्ठा
ये बुद्ध्या कोपमुत्थितम् ।
निरुन्धन्ति महात्मानो
दीप्तमग्निमिवाम्भसा ॥
What have I done, burning down this Laṅkā? Fortunate indeed are those truly great souls whose anger is kept in check by their discernment, like fire is, by the water.
5.55.4
क्रुद्धः पापं न कुर्यात्कः
क्रुद्धो हन्याद्गुरूनपि ।
क्रुद्धः परुषया वाचा
नरस्साधूनधिक्षिपेत् ॥
Taken over by anger, which man would not say harsh words, throw insults at saintly men, commit sinful deeds and even kill revered elders?
5.55.5
वाच्यावाच्यं प्रकुपितो
न विजानाति कर्हिचित् ।
नाकार्यमस्ति क्रुद्धस्य
नावाच्यं विद्यते क्वचित् ॥
One who is taken over by anger, would not be able to distinguish between what is appropriate to say and what is not. There is nothing that a man possessed by rage would not say or do.
5.55.6
यस्समुत्पतितं क्रोधम्
क्षमयैव निरस्यति ।
यथोरगस्त्वचं जीर्णाम्
स वै पुरुष उच्यते ॥
Only he who can cast aside his anger as quickly as it rises, like a snake sloughing off its worn-out skin, only can be said to be a capable man.
5.55.7
धिगस्तु मां सुदुर्बुद्धिम्
निर्लज्जं पापकृत्तमम् ।
अचिन्तयित्वा तां सीताम्
अग्निदं स्वामिघातुकम् ॥
Dhik me, the wretched one, for shamelessly committing the sin of hurting my master! Without thinking about Seetā, I set (everything on) fire!
5.55.8
यदि दग्धा त्वियं लङ्का
नूनमार्याऽपि जानकी ।
दग्धा तेन मया भर्तुः
हतं कार्यमजानता ॥
With Laṅkā burnt down, so would be Jānaki, the noble one! I ruined the purpose of my lord, even before I knew it!
5.55.9
यदर्थमयमारम्भः
तत्कार्यमवसादितम् ।
मया हि दहता लङ्काम्
न सीता परिरक्षिता ॥
The very purpose for which all this effort was made, has been undone; Seetā is, of course, not saved, by (the folly of) my burning down Laṅkā!
5.55.10
ईषत्कार्यमिदं कार्यम्
कृतमासीन्न संशयः ।
तस्य क्रोधाभिभूतेन
मया मूलक्षयः कृतः ॥
Without a doubt, just when everything is all but accomplished, I was undone by anger and ruined the very purpose of it all.
5.55.11
विनष्टा जानकी नूनम्
न ह्यदग्धः प्रदृश्यते ।
लङ्कायां कश्चिदुद्धेशः
सर्वा भस्मीकृता पुरी ॥
Jānaki must have perished, without a doubt, for there is no part of the city, that has not been burnt down as far as I can see; the entire city is reduced to ashes.
5.55.12
यदि तद्विहतं कार्यम्
मम प्रज्ञाविपर्ययात् ।
इहैव प्राणसंन्यासो
ममापि ह्यद्य रोचते ॥
I shall lay down my life right here, the mission I am on thus undone by my lack of judgement.
5.55.13
किमग्नौ निपताम्यद्य
आहोस्विद्बडबामुखे ।
शरीरमाहो सत्त्वानाम्
दद्मि सागरवासिनाम् ॥
Now, shall I throw myself into the fire or fall into the jaws of the great fire in the depths of the ocean or consign my body (as food) to the creatures of the sea?
5.55.14
कथं हि जीवता शक्यो
मया द्रष्टुं हरीश्वरः ।
तौ वा पुरुषशार्दूलौ
कार्यसर्वस्वघातिना ॥
Having ruined the entire mission, how can I continue to be alive and see the Lord of Vānaras and the duo of the tigers among men?
5.55.15
मया खलु तदेवेदम्
रोषदोषात्प्रदर्शितम् ।
प्रथितं त्रिषु लोकेषु
कपित्वमनवस्थितम् ॥
I have indeed demonstrated the proverbial fickleness of a monkey that is well-known in the three worlds, having succumbed to anger.
5.55.16
धिगस्तु राजसं भावम्
अनीशमनवस्थितम् ।
ईश्वरेणापि यद्रागान्
मया सीता न रक्षिता ॥
Dhik the arrogance, which knows no control! I was capable of it in every manner, but Seetā could not be saved by me, because of my arrogance!
5.55.17
विनष्टायां तु सीतायाम्
तावुभौ विनशिष्यतः ।
तयोर्विनाशे सुग्रीवः
सबन्धुर्विनशिष्यति ॥
If Seetā perishes, those two will perish; and with them, Sugreeva will too, along with his kin.
5.55.18
एतदेव वचश्श्रुत्वा
भरतो भ्रातृवत्सलः ।
धर्मात्मा सहशत्रुघ्नः
कथं शक्ष्यति जीवितुम् ॥
How would Bharata, the Dharmātma, the affectionate brother, and Ṡatrughna hold on to their lives hearing the news?
5.55.19
इक्ष्वाकुवंशे धर्मिष्ठे
गते नाशमसंशयम् ।
भविष्यन्ति प्रजास्सर्वाः
शोकसन्तापपीडिताः ॥
With the lineage of the Ikshwākus thus perishing, their subjects will all be harrowed by the agony of grief, for certain.
5.55.20
तदहं भाग्यरहितो
लुप्तधर्मार्थसङ्ग्रहः ।
रोषदोषपरीतात्मा
व्यक्तं लोकविनाशनः ॥
With good fortune deserting me and my mind succumbing to anger, I stand here forfeiting Dharma and Artha and causing the destruction of the world.
5.55.21
इति चिन्तयतस्तस्य
निमित्तान्युपपेदिरे ।
पूर्वमप्युपलब्धानि
साक्षात्पुनरचिन्तयत् ॥
When he was thus pondering sadly, there appeared auspicious omens, such as the ones he had seen before. Then he thought again:
5.55.22
अथवा चारुसर्वाङ्गी
रक्षिता स्वेन तेजसा ।
न नशिष्यति कल्याणी
नाग्निरग्नौ प्रवर्तते ॥
May be that good dame, whose every limb is lovely, did not perish, her own power (of Tapa) protecting her, for fire cannot burn fire!
5.55.23
न हि धर्मात्मनस्तस्य
भार्याममिततेजसः ।
स्वचारित्राभिगुप्तां ताम्
स्प्रष्टुमर्हति पावकः ॥
Fire cannot touch the wife of that Dharmātma of limitless puissance, who has her own virtue for an armor.
5.55.24
नूनं रामप्रभावेण
वैदेह्यास्सुकृतेन च ।
यन्मां दहनकर्मायम्
नादहद्धव्यवाहनः ॥
Did fire, whose nature is to burn, not leave me, indeed, without a burn, because of Rāma’s greatness and Vaidēhee’s good deeds?
5.55.25
त्रयाणां भरतादीनाम्
भ्रात्रूणां देवता च या ।
रामस्य च मनः कान्ता
सा कथं विनशिष्यति ॥
How could she, who is like a Dēvata for the three brothers including Bharata, and who is a darling of Rāma’s heart, perish?
5.55.26
यद्वा दहनकर्मायम्
सर्वत्र प्रभुरव्ययः ।
न मे दहति लाङ्गूलम्
कथमार्यां प्रधक्ष्यति ॥
If he, the imperishable Lord who burns down everything, would not burn my tail, why would he burn the noble one?
5.55.27
पुनश्चाचिन्तयत्तत्र
हनुमान्विस्मितस्तदा ।
हिरण्यनाभस्य गिरेः
जलमध्ये प्रदर्शनम् ॥
And Hanumān recollected, with renewed wonder, the mountain of the golden navel (Maināka) showing itself in the midst of the ocean.
5.55.28
तपसा सत्यवाक्येन
अनन्यत्वाच्च भर्तरि ।
अपि सा निर्दहेदग्निम्
न तामग्निः प्रधक्ष्यति ॥
By virtue of her austerities, truthfulness and wholehearted devotion to her lord, she can burn the fire itself; fire, by no means, can burn her.
5.55.29
स तथा चिन्तयंस्तत्र
देव्या धर्मपरिग्रहम् ।
शुश्राव हनुमान्वाक्यम्
चारणानां महात्मनाम् ॥
As Hanumān thus dwelt in his mind on the immensity of the Dēvi’s virtues, he heard Cāraṇas, the Mahātmas, saying:
5.55.30
अहो खलु कृतं कर्म
दुष्करं हि हनूमता ।
अग्निं विसृजताऽभीक्ष्णम्
भीमं राक्षसवेश्मनि ॥
What an unimaginable task has Hanumān accomplished, setting all the houses of the Rākshasas on a terrible and extensive fire!
5.55.31
प्रपलायितरक्षः स्त्री
बालवृद्धसमाकुला ।
जनकोलाहलाध्माता
क्रन्दन्तीवाद्रिकन्दरैः ॥
With the Rākshasa women, children and old people fleeing in panic, and filled with the uproar of people everywhere, it (Laṅkā) seems like the  wailing of mountain caves.
5.55.32
दग्धेयं नगरी सर्वा
साट्टप्राकारतोरणा ।
जानकी न च दग्धेति
विस्मयोऽद्भुत एव नः ॥
It is so surprising to us that the whole city with all its ramparts, towers and gateways is burnt, but not Jānaki.
5.55.33
स निमित्तैश्च दृष्टार्थैः
कारणैश्च महागुणैः ।
ऋषिवाक्यैश्च हनुमान्
अभवत्प्रीतमानसः ॥
Hanumān was very pleased with those omens that had always been proved right, the many and weighty reasons why it could not have fallen out otherwise and the words of the sages.
5.55.34
ततः कपिः प्राप्तमनोरथार्थः
तामक्षतां राजसुतां विदित्वा ।
प्रत्यक्षतस्तां पुनरेव दृष्टवा
प्रतिप्रयाणाय मतिं चकार ॥
Reaching the goal that he had set in his mind and hearing that the princess was left unscathed, the Vānara decided to see her once again before setting himself off on the return journey.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे पञ्चपञ्चाशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the fifty fifth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
