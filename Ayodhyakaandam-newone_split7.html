Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 7
In this Sarga, Manthara, a senior maid of Kaikēyee, comes to know about Rāma's consecration and tries to instigate Kaikēyee against it. But, Kaikēyee says that she makes no distinction between Rāma and Bharata and expresses her happiness about the consecration of Rāma.
2.7.1
ज्ञातिदासी यतो जाता
कैकेय्यास्तु सहोषिता ।
प्रासादं चन्द्रसङ्काशम्
आरुरोह यदृच्छया ॥
The servant maid of Kaikēyee from her side of the family, who was with her since her birth happened to go up to the top of the palace that was white like moon.
Note that in the previous Sarga, the scene had advanced in Rāma's palace and in the streets of Ayōdhyā, from the day before consecration to the day of consecration.
In parallel, something else was going on at the palace of Kaikēyee. The parallel story starts in this Sarga from the day before the consecration.
2.7.2
सिक्तराजपथां कृत्स्नाम्
प्रकीर्णकुसुमोत्कराम् ।
अयोध्यां मन्थरा तस्मात्
प्रसादादन्ववैक्षत ॥
Manthara, from the heights of that palace saw all the royal highways of the city of Ayōdhyā well washed and strewn with flowers.
2.7.3
पताकाभिर्वरार्हाभिः
ध्वजैश्च समलङ्कृताम् ।
वृतां छन्दपथैश्चापि
शिरस्स्नातजनैर्वृताम् ।
माल्यमोदकहस्तैश्च
द्विजेन्द्रैरभिनादिताम् ।
शुक्लदेवगृहद्वाराम्
सर्ववादित्रनिस्वनाम् ।
सम्प्रहृष्टजनाकीर्णाम्
ब्रह्मघोषाभिनादिताम् ।
प्रहृष्टवरहस्त्यश्वाम्
सम्प्रणर्दितगोवृषाम् ।
प्रहृष्टमुदितैः पौरैः
उच्छ्रितध्वजमालिनीम् ।
अयोध्यां मन्थरा दृष्ट्वा
परं विस्मयमागता ॥
From that palace, Manthara saw the city of Ayōdhyā adorned with flag posts, banners and buntings, boasting of many roundabouts, filled with people with washed hair, resounding with the chants of great Brāhmaṇas holding garlands and a variety of sweets in their hands, having temples with white washed doorways, filled with sounds of musical instruments and Vēdic chants overflowing with happy and merry people, and with fine horses, elephants and mooing bulls.
2.7.7
प्रहर्षोत्फुल्लनयनाम्
पाण्डुरक्षौमवासिनीम् ।
अविदूरे स्थितां दृष्ट्वा
धात्रीं पप्रच्छ मन्थरा ॥
She asked a nanny standing nearby, clad in white silk garments, eyes wide open in delight:
2.7.8
उत्तमेनाभिसंयुक्ता
हर्षेणार्थपरा सती ।
राममाता धनं किन्नु
जनेभ्यस्सम्प्रयच्छति ॥
For what purpose does the mother of Rāma, giving away money to the people with such a great joy?
Manthara's opening dialogues clearly shows what is foremost on her mind, jealousy towards Kousalyā.
2.7.9
अतिमात्रप्रहर्षोऽयम्
किं जनस्य च शंस मे ।
कारयिष्यति किं वापि
सम्प्रहृष्टो महीपतिः ॥
Tell me, why is there such exhilaration among people? Does the ruler of the land has any special occasion of joy?
2.7.10
विदीर्यमाणा हर्षेण
धात्री तु परया मुदा ।
आचचक्षेऽथ कुब्जायै
भूयसीं राघव श्रियम् ॥
The nanny who was bursting at seems with joy, told that humpback with great happiness about the great fortune that would befall on the prince of Raghu dynasty!
2.7.11
श्वः पुष्येण जितक्रोधम्
यौवराज्येन राघवम् ।
राजा दशरथो रामम्
अभिषेचयिताऽनघम् ॥
Tomorrow, when the moon is in the constellation of Pushya, King Daṡaratha will consecrate the flawless Rāghava who has mastered his temper, as the crown prince.
Rāma is often referred to as Rāghava, though the term can mean any descendant of Raghu. Based on the context, it could be referring to Rāma, Daṡaratha, Bharata or others.
2.7.12
धात्र्यास्तु वचनं शृत्वा
कुब्जा क्षिप्रममर्षिता ।
कैलासशिखराकारात्
प्रासादादवरोहत ॥
No sooner had the humpback heard the words of the nanny, than she became unhappy and climbed down from the top of the palace that resembled the Kailāsa mountain.
2.7.13
सा दह्यमाना कोपेन
मन्थरा पापदर्शिनी ।
शयानामेत्य कैकेयीम्
इदं वचनमब्रवीत् ॥
Burning with fury, that woman of negative attitude, reached Kaikēyee, who was sleeping, and said:
2.7.14
उत्तिष्ठ मूढे किं शेषे
भयं त्वामभिवर्तते ।
उपप्लुतमघौघेन
किमात्मानं न बुध्यसे ॥
Get up, foolish woman! How can you lie down when a great peril is fast threatening you? Can you not see the flood-tide of grief that is going to engulf you ?
It is amazing that a maid can address her mistress as 'foolish woman!'. It is because of two things:
1. The maid knows her mistress since her birth.
2. The maid is very much part of the family or the establishment.
A senior maid of those times was like a senior officer of modern times.
However, when we hear the word 'maid', we conjure up the image of a lowly and submissive slave. When we hear the word 'senior officer', we conjure up the image of a man of power.
But notice that the 'maid' of Rāmāyaṇa times could walk up to her mistress and thrash her out! Can a senior officer do that, in modern times?
2.7.15
अनिष्टे सुभगाकारे
सौभाग्येन विकत्थसे ।
चलं हि तव सौभाग्यम्
नद्यास्स्रोत इवोष्णगे ॥
It only appears that you are the darling, but you are not even the favorite! Enough of your boasting of being one! Your fortune as a happy married woman is as uncertain as the flow of a stream in summer!
2.7.16
एवमुक्ता तु कैकेयी
रुष्टया परुषं वचः ।
कुब्जया पापदर्शिन्या
विषादमगमत्परम् ॥
Those harsh words of the irate and evil minded humpback Manthara made Kaikēyee very sad.
2.7.17
कैकेयी त्वब्रवीत्कुब्जाम्
कच्चित्क्षेमं नु मन्थरे ।
विषण्णवदनां हि त्वाम्
लक्षये भृशदुःखिताम् ॥
Then, Kaikēyee responded to that humpback saying: "O! Manthara, Has anything gone wrong? I am seeing you downcast and extremely sad!
2.7.18
मन्थरा तु वच श्श्रुत्वा
कैकेय्या मधुराक्षरम् ।
उवाच क्रोधसंयुक्ता
वाक्यं वाक्यविशारदा ॥
Hearing those words of Kaikēyee, spoken in a gentle tone, Manthara, skillful with words, said angrily:
2.7.19
सा विषण्णतरा भूत्वा
कुब्जा तस्या हितैषिणी ।
विषादयन्ती प्रोवाच
भेदयन्ती च राघवम् ॥
Interested in her wellbeing, the humpback put on an even more woebegone look and set herself to rouse her fears and instigate her against Rāghava.
2.7.20
अक्षय्यं सुमहद्देवि
प्रवृत्तं त्वद्विनाशनम् ।
रामं दशरथो राजा
यौवराज्येऽभिषेक्ष्यति ॥
My lady! Endless and intense disaster is about to besiege you! King Daṡaratha is going to consecrate Rāma as the crown prince!
2.7.21
साऽस्म्यगाधे भये मग्ना
दुःखशोकसमन्विता ।
दह्यमानाऽनलेनेव
त्वद्धितार्थमिहागता ॥
I am plunged in unfathomable depths of fear and consumed by the fire of grief and sadness. I have come here to do the best I can, for you.
2.7.22
तव दुःखेन कैकेयि
मम दुःखं महद्भवेत् ।
त्वद्वृद्धौ मम वृद्धिश्च
भवेदत्र न संशयः ॥
Your grief, O Kaikēyee, gives me even greater grief! Your prosperity alone brings me prosperity, without doubt!
2.7.23
नराधिपकुले जाता
महिषी त्वं महीपतेः ।
उग्रत्वं राजधर्माणाम्
कथं देवि न बुध्यसे ॥
Born into a royal family and being the queen of a ruler of the earth, how could you be so ignorant, my lady, of the ruthlessness of politics?
2.7.24
धर्मावादी शठो भर्ता
श्लक्ष्णवादी च दारुणः ।
शुद्धभावे न जानीषे
तेनैव मतिसन्धिता ॥
Your husband swears by Dharma, but is perfidious. He speaks ingratiatingly, but is cruel at heart. Being straightforward by nature, you are unable to realize that you have been beguiled by him!
2.7.25
उपस्थितः प्रयुञ्जानः
त्वयि सान्त्वमनर्थकम् ।
अर्थेनैवाद्य ते भर्ता
कौसल्यां योजयिष्यति ॥
Your husband pleases you with sweet nothings, but he confers favors of substance, only on Kousalyā.
2.7.26
उपवाह्य स दुष्टात्मा
भरतं तव बन्धुषु ।
काल्ये स्थापयिता रामम्
राज्ये निहतकण्टके ॥
That evil man has sent away Bharata to be with your people and having that obstacle out of the way, he is going to install Rāma in charge of the kingdom tomorrow morning.
2.7.27
शत्रुः पतिप्रवादेन
मात्रेव हितकाम्यया ।
आशीविष इवाङ्केन
बाले परिधृतस्त्वया ॥
you kid! You are hanging on to your enemy who is in the form of a husband, like taking a venomous serpent onto the lap, wishing it well like a mother!
2.7.28
यथा हि कुर्यात्सर्पो वा
शत्रुर्वा प्रत्युपेक्षितः ।
राज्ञा दशरथेनाद्य
सपुत्रा त्वं तथा कृता ॥
What an ignored serpent or enemy would do, is what has been done by King Daṡaratha to you and your son.
2.7.29
पापेनानृतसान्त्वेन
बाले नित्यसुखोचिते ।
रामं स्थापयता राज्ये
सानुबन्धा हता ह्यसि ॥
My child! You are long accustomed to royal comforts! But you are now being ruined by that liar and glib-talker who gave you offspring, as he is set to install Rāma in the kingdom.
2.7.30
सा प्राप्तकालं कैकेयि
क्षिप्रं कुरु हितं तव ।
त्रायस्व पुत्रमात्मानम्
मां च विस्मयदर्शने ॥
O Kaikēyee! O enchanting beauty! Act swiftly, when the time is opportune, for securing your own well being. Save your son, yourself and also me!
2.7.31
मन्थाराया वचश्श्रुत्वा
शयाना सा शुभानना ।
उत्तस्थौ हर्षसम्पूर्णा
चन्द्रलेखेव शारदी ॥
Hearing those words of Manthara, that Kaikēyee of charming face, rose from her bed, full of joy like the crescent moon in the post monsoon sky.
The post-monsoon nights are clear and dark. When Kaikēyee rose up and spoke, she brought the pleasantness of the moon to the dark and gloomy mood cast by Manthara.
2.7.32
अतीव सा तु संहृष्टा
कैकेयी विस्मयान्विता ।
एकमाभरणं तस्यै
कुब्जायै प्रददौ शुभम् ॥
Kaikēyee was surprised and felt extremely happy, and gave one of her beautiful ornaments to that humpback.
2.7.33
दत्वा त्वाभरणं तस्यै
कुब्जायै प्रमदोत्तमा ।
कैकेयी मन्थरां दृष्ट्वा
पुनरेवाब्रवीदिदम् ॥
After giving the ornament to that humpback, Kaikēyee, the best of the women, spoke again to Manthara:
2.7.34
इदं तु मन्थरे मह्यम्
आख्यासि परमं प्रियम् ।
एतन्मे प्रियमाख्यातुः
किं वा भूयः करोमि ते ॥
It is the most welcome news that you have brought me, Manthara! What more shall I do for you who have brought me such glad tidings!
2.7.35
रामे वा भरते वाऽहम्
विशेषं नोपलक्षये ।
तस्मात्तुष्टाऽस्मि यद्राजा
रामं राज्येऽभिषेक्ष्यति ॥
I make no distinction between Rāma and Bharata. Therefore, I am happy to know that the king will consecrate Rāma to rule the kingdom!
2.7.36
न मे परं किञ्चिदितस्त्वया पुनः
प्रियं प्रियार्हे! सुवचं वचःपरम् ।
तथा ह्यवोचस्त्वमतः प्रियोत्तरं
वरं परं ते प्रददामि तं वृणु ॥
You could never bring me more happy news! You deserve all the affection I can shower on you. Ask for something better that is a match for the joy you have brought! And I shall give it to you !
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे सप्तमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the seventh Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
