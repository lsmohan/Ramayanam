Baala Kaanda - Sarga 74
In this Sarga, King Janaka gives copious amounts of dowry to his daughters who are leaving forever, to their new home. King Daṡaratha starts his return journey to Ayōdhyā. But on the way, something unexpected happens. Rāma, the son of Jamadagni, who had killed many Kshatriyas in the past as he was enraged at his father's murder, appears all of a sudden. Everyone becomes dazed and concerned at his fearsome and unexpected appearance.
1.74.1
अथ रात्र्यां व्यतीतायाम्
विश्वामित्रो महामुनिः ।
आपृष्ट्वा तौ च राजानौ
जगामोत्तरपर्वतम् ।
आशीर्भिः पूरयित्वा च
कुमारांश्च सराघवान् ॥
After the night passed, the Mahā Muni Viṡwāmitra showered blessings on those young princes of Raghu Dynasty, took leave of them and left for the northern mountains.
1.74.2
विश्वामित्रे गते राजा
वैदेहं मिथिलाधिपम् ।
आपृष्ट्वाऽथ जगामाशु
राजा दशरथः पुरीम् ॥
After Viṡwāmitra left, the king Daṡaratha took leave of the king of Vidēha and promptly left for his own city.
1.74.3
गच्छन्तं तं तु राजानं
अन्वगच्छन्नराधिपः ।
अथ राजा विदेहानाम्
ददौ कन्याधनं बहु ॥
The lord of the peoples, the king of Vidēha followed the departing king and gave immense dowry to the girls.
In recent times, we refer to the term dowry only in wrong or negative contexts, where money is demanded or extracted by the bridegroom or his side of the family, from the bride’s family.
But in its original connotation, this term used to refer to the gifts property, money, etc., that were given to the bride by her parents, at the time of her departure from the maternal household to her future home, the husband's household. Dowry refers to the gifts given out of love and affection to their daughter.
1.74.4
गवां शतसहस्राणि
बहूनि मिथिलेश्वरः ।
कम्बलानां च मुख्यानाम्
क्षौमकोट्यंबराणि च ।
हस्त्यश्वरथपादातम्
दिव्यरूपं स्वलङ्कृतम् ।
ददौ कन्यापिता तासाम्
दासीदासमनुत्तमम् ॥
That lord of Mithilā and the father of the brides gave them hundreds of thousands of cows, excellent woolen blankets, Crores of silk garments, well-decorated elephants, horses, chariots and foot-soldiers and excellent male and female servants.
1.74.6
हिरण्यस्य सुवर्णस्य
मुक्तानां विद्रुमस्य च ।
ददौ परमसंहृष्टः
कन्याधनमनुत्तमम् ॥
Immensely pleased, he gave gold, silver, pearls and corals as dowry.
1.74.7
दत्त्वा बहु धनं राजा
समनुज्ञाप्य पार्थिवम् ।
प्रविवेश स्वनिलयम्
मिथिलां मिथिलेश्वरः ॥
The lord of Mithilā, after thus giving copious amounts of wealth went back to his residence in Mithilā taking proper leave from the king (Daṡaratha).
1.74.8
राजाऽप्ययोध्याधिपतिः
सह पुत्रैर्महात्मभिः ।
ऋषीन् सर्वान् पुरस्कृत्य
जगाम सबलानुगः ॥
The King, the lord of Ayōdhyā went ahead, with his Mahātma sons and all the Ṛshis leading him and the army following him.
1.74.9
गच्छन्तं तं नरव्याघ्रम्
सर्षिसङ्घं सराघवम् ।
घोराः स्म पक्षिणो वाचो
व्याहरन्ति ततस्ततः ॥
As that tiger among men travelled along with hosts of Ṛshis and the princes of Raghu dynasty, birds screamed overhead with grating noises, off and on.
1.74.10
भौमाश्चैव मृगा स्सर्वे
गच्छन्ति स्म प्रदक्षिणम् ।
तान् दृष्ट्वा राजशार्दूलो
वसिष्ठं पर्यपृच्छत ॥
All the animals that roam the land started moving around them in circles. Seeing that, that tiger among kings asked Vasishṭha:
1.74.11
असौम्याः पक्षिणो घोरा
मृगाश्चापि प्रदक्षिणाः ।
किमिदं हृदयोत्कम्पि
मनो मम विषीदति ॥
The birds are making harsh and terrible noises. The animals are going around us in a circle.  What is this that makes my heart tremble? I am worried!
1.74.12
राज्ञो दशरथस्यैतः
श्रुत्वा वाक्यं महानृषिः ।
उवाच मधुरां वाणीम्
श्रूयतामस्य यत्फलम् ॥
That Maharshi, hearing those words of the king Daṡaratha assuaged him with these gentle and pleasing words: "Listen, I shall tell you what this portends!"
1.74.13
उपस्थितं भयं घोरम्
दिव्यं पक्षिमुखाच्च्युतम् ।
मृगाः प्रशमयन्त्येते
सन्तापस्त्यज्यतामयम् ॥
These animals provide relief from a   dreadful event of divine making portended by the cries of the birds! Therefore, you may give up your worries! 
1.74.14
तेषां संवदतां तत्र
वायुः प्रादुर्बभूव ह ।
कम्पयन् पृथिवीं सर्वाम्
पातयंश्च द्रुमांच्छुभान् ॥
As they were discussing thus, strong winds blew, shaking the earth and tearing down the trees.
1.74.15
तमसा संवृतस्सूर्य
स्सर्वा न प्रबभुर्दिश ।
भस्मना चावृतं सर्वम्
संमूढमिव तद्बलम् ॥
The sun was engulfed by darkness. There was no light in any direction. Everything was enveloped in dust. The army was totally disoriented.
1.74.16
वसिष्ठश्चर्षयश्चान्ये
राजा च ससुतस्तदा ।
संसज्ञा इव तत्रासन्
सर्वमन्यद्विचेतनम् ॥
Only Vasishṭha, the Ṛshis, the king and his sons, remained in their full senses. Everyone else felt dazed and numbed.
1.74.17
तस्मिंस्तमसि घोरे तु
भस्मच्छन्नेव सा चमूः ।
ददर्श भीमसङ्काशम्
जटामण्डलधारिणम् ।
भार्गवं जामदग्न्यं तम्
राजराजविमर्दिनम् ।
कैलासमिव दुर्धर्षम्
कालाग्निमिव दुस्सहम् ।
ज्वलंतमिव तेजोभिः
दुर्निरीक्ष्यं पृथग्जनैः ।
स्कन्धे चासज्य परशुम्
धनुर्विद्युद्गणोपमम् ।
प्रगृह्य शरमुख्यं च
त्रिपुरघ्नं यथा शिवम् ॥
In that dreadful darkness, the army, looking pale as if covered by ashes, saw the fearsome descendant of Bhṛgu, the son of Jamadagni, who was wearing a crown of matted hair, who had killed all the kings of kings, who was invincible like the mountain of Kailāsa who was unbearable like the fire of time, blazing with blinding light and impossible for ordinary beings to get a glimpse, with Paraṡu on his shoulder, and holding an significant bow in his hand that shone like swarms of lightning flashes, reminding of lord Ṡiva that destroyed all the three worlds.
Paraṡu is a two pronged axe. Mount Kailāsa is the abode of Ṡiva.
1.74.21
तं दृष्ट्वा भीमसङ्काशम्
ज्वलन्तमिव पावकम् ।
वसिष्ठप्रमुखा विप्रा
जपहोमपरायणाः ।
सङ्गता मुनयस्सर्वे
सञ्जजल्पुरथो मिथः ॥
Seeing him, so terrible in appearance, like a blazing fire, all the Brāhmaṇas including the eminent Vasishṭha who consistently perform Japa and Hōma, got together and discussed among themselves:
1.74.22
कच्चित्पितृवधामर्षी
क्षत्रं नोत्सादयिष्यति ।
पूर्वं क्षत्रवधं कृत्वा
गतमन्युर्गतज्वरः ।
क्षत्रस्योत्सादनं भूयो
न खल्वस्य चिकीर्षितम् ॥
We hope he is not going to exterminate all the Kshatriyas, still holding the rage from his father's murder! He killed Kshatriyas in the past, till he felt relieved of his rage and pain. We hope he is not planning to destroy Kshatriyas again!
1.74.24
एवमुक्त्वाऽर्घ्यमादाय
भार्गवं भीमदर्शनम् ।
ऋषयो राम रामेति
वचो मधुरमब्रुवन् ॥
Thinking thus, the Ṛshis received that fearsome looking descendant of Bhṛgu, addressing him 'O Rāma, Rāma!' in a pleasing manner, offering him Arghya.
Arghya is water offered for drinking, often cleansed with Mantras. 
1.74.25
प्रतिगृह्य तु तां पूजां
ऋषिदत्तां प्रतापवान् ।
रामं दाशरथिं रामो
जामदग्न्योऽभ्यभाषत ॥
The valiant Rāma, the son of Jamadagni, accepted the homage offered by the Ṛshis, and spoke to Rāma, the son of Daṡaratha.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे चतुस्सप्ततितमस्सर्ग:॥
Thus concludes the seventy fourth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
