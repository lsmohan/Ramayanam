Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 37
In this Sarga, Sugreeva asks Hanumān to send Vānaras everywhere, to all mountains, rivers, oceans, to summon all the Vānaras that live there to hasten up and come to Kishkindhā for advancing the cause of Rāma. He asks him to warn them that they will be punished with their life, if they do not show up within ten days. Hanumān, obeying his command, sends Vānaras of great valor in every direction.
Thousands of thousands of crores of Vānaras from everywhere leave, following that command. Those who go to the Himālayas, see the place where a Yajña was performed in dedication of Mahēṡwara and bring the fruits, roots and herbs growing on those Yajña grounds, as gifts to Sugreeva. Sugreeva is happy with the progress.
4.37.1
एवमुक्तस्तु सुग्रीवो
लक्ष्मणेन महात्मना ।
हनूमन्तं स्थितं पार्श्वे
वचनं चेदमब्रवीत् ॥
Thus told by Mahātma Lakshmaṇa, Sugreeva said these words to Hanumān who was by his side:
4.37.2
महेन्द्रहिमवद्विन्ध्य
कैलासशिखरेषु च ।
मन्दरे पाण्डुशिखरे
पञ्चशैलेषु ये स्थिताः ।
तरुणादित्यवर्णेषु
भ्राजमानेषु नित्यशः  ।
पर्वतेषु समुद्रान्ते
पश्चिमायां तु ये दिशि ।
आदित्यभवने चैव
गिरौ सन्ध्याभ्रसन्निभे ।
पद्मातालवनं भीमाः 
संश्रिता हरिपुङ्गवाः ।
अञ्जनाम्बुदसङ्काशाः
कुञ्जरप्रतिमौजसः ।
अञ्जने पर्वते चैव
ये वसन्ति प्लवङ्गमाः ।
महाशैल  गुहावासा
वानराः कनकप्रभाः ।
मेरुपार्श्वगताश्चैव
ये च धूम्रगिरिं श्रिताः ।
तरुणादित्यवर्णाश्च
पर्वते ये महारुणे ।
पिबन्तो मधुमैरेयम्
भीमवेगाः प्लवङ्गमाः ।
वनेषु च सुरम्येषु
सुगन्धिषु महत्सु च ।
तापसाश्रमरम्येषु
वनान्तेषु समन्ततः ।
तांस्तांस्त्वमानय क्षिप्रम्
पृथिव्यां सर्ववानरान् ।
सामदानादिभिः कल्पैः
वानरैर्वेगवत्तरैः ॥
Send Vānaras of tremendous speed to every place and bring the Vānaras who are there, motivating them with inducements, appealing to their good senses and using other such techniques;
those who live on the peaks of the five mountains Mahēndra, Himālaya, Vindhya, Kailāsa, and the white-crested Mandara;
those who live on the mountains in the West beyond the ocean that are always splendid, shining like the rising sun, and on the mountain Āditya Bhavana that resembles an evening cloud;
and the fierce and best of the Vānaras who live in the Vana called Padmācala;
and the Vānaras who are as strong as elephants resembling collyrium-black clouds who live on the Anjana mountain,
and those Vānaras that shine like gold who live in the caves of Mahāṡaila mountain, on the flanks of the Mēru and on the Smoky Mountain;
and those Vānaras of terrific speed who are of the color of the morning sun, that live on the Mahāruṇa mountain who drink liquor made from dates;
and those who live in the large, enchanting and fragrant Vanas and in the beautiful Āṡramas of Tapasvis in the interiors of the Vanas.
4.37.10
प्रेषिताः प्रथमं ये च
मयाज्ञाता महाजवाः ।
त्वरणार्थं तु भूयस्त्वम्
सम्प्रेषय हरीश्वरान् ॥
Please send again more Vānara leaders to hasten up the huge numbers that were sent earlier by my orders.
4.37.11
ये प्रसक्ताश्च कामेषु
दीर्घसूत्राश्च वानराः ।
इहानयस्व तान् शीघ्रम्
सर्वानेव कपीश्वरान् ॥
Bring here quick all the leaders of Vānaras who might be still indulged in love sports or might simply be procrastinating.
4.37.12
अहोभिर्दशभिर्ये च
नागच्छन्ति ममाज्ञया ।
हन्तव्यास्ते दुरात्मानो
राजशासनदूषकाः ॥
The evil ones who do not reach here within ten days according to my orders shall be condemned to death for contempt of the king’s orders.
4.37.13
शतान्यथ सहस्राणि
कोट्यश्च मम शासनात् ।
प्रयान्तु कपिसिंहानाम्
निदिशो मम ये स्थिताः ॥
May all the hundreds, thousands and crores of lions among Vānaras who are under my command start (right away)!
4.37.14
मेघपर्वतसङ्काशाः
छादयन्त इवाम्बरम् ।
घोररूपाः कपिश्रेष्ठा
यान्तु मच्छासनादितः ॥
May the best of the Vānaras of fierce forms that resemble clouds and mountains, come here clouding the sky, per my orders.
4.37.15
ते गतिज्ञा गतिं गत्वा
पृथिव्यां सर्ववानराः ।
आनयन्तु हरीन्सर्वान्
त्वरिताश्शासनान्मम ॥
May all those Vānaras who know the routes go expediently and bring every Vānara in the world quickly; this is my command!
4.37.16
तस्य वानरराजस्य
श्रुत्वा वायुसुतो वचः ।
दिक्षु सर्वासु विक्रान्तान्
प्रेषयामास वानरान् ॥
Upon hearing those words of the king of Vānaras, the son of Vāyu sent Vānaras of great valor in all directions.
4.37.17
ते पदं विष्णुविक्रान्तम्
पतत्रिज्योतिरध्वगाः ।
प्रयाताः प्रहिता राज्ञा
हरयस्तु क्षणेन वै ॥
In no time, the Vānaras thus sent by the king, took to the skies that were once occupied by Vishṇu, like birds and stars.
‘Occupied by Vishṇu refers to the instance of Vishṇu covering the sky with second foot in the Vāmana Avatāra.
4.37.18
ते समुद्रेषु गिरिषु
वनेषु च सरस्सु च ।
वानरा वानरान्सर्वान्
रामहेतोरचोदयन् ॥
Those Vānaras urged the Vānaras of Vanas, mountains, lakes and oceans, to make all haste for Rāma’s cause.
4.37.19
मृत्युकालोपमस्याज्ञाम्
राजराजस्य वानराः ।
सुग्रीवस्याययुश्श्रुत्वा
सुग्रीव भय शन्किताः  ॥
Hearing that command of Sugreeva, the king of kings of Vānaras, which was no less than a decree from the God of Death, the Vānaras came forthwith, in their extreme fear of Sugreeva.
4.37.20
ततस्तेऽञ्जनसङ्काशा
गिरेस्तस्मान्महाजवाः ।
तिस्रः कोट्यः प्लवङ्गानाम्
निर्ययुर्यत्र राघवः ॥
Three crores of them, of extreme speed, who were as black as collyrium, then started from that mountain, to where Rāghava was.
4.37.21
अस्तं गच्छति यत्रार्कः
तस्मिन्गिरिवरे रताः  ।
सन्तप्त हेमवर्णाभाः
तस्मात्कोट्यो दश च्युताः ॥
Ten crores of those who glowed with the gleam of gold purified in fire started from the best of the mountains where the sun sets, which was their dwelling place.
4.37.22
कैलासशिखरेभ्यश्च
सिंहकेसरवर्चसाम् ।
ततः कोटिसहस्राणि
वानराणां समागमन्  ॥
A thousand crore Vānaras of the sheen of lion’s mane, came together from the peaks of Kailāsa.
4.37.23
फलमूलेन जीवन्तो
हिमवन्तमुपाश्रिताः ।
तेषां कोटिसहस्राणाम्
सहस्रं समवर्तत ॥
A thousand of thousand cores who dwelt in the Himālayas living on roots and fruits came along together.
4.37.24
अङ्गारकसमानानाम्
भीमानां भीमकर्मणाम् ।
विन्ध्याद्वानरकोटीनाम्
सहस्राण्यपतन्द्रुतम् ॥
Those who were fierce like Mars and were capable of magnificent deeds came immediately, in their thousands of crores, from the Vindhya mountain.
4.37.25
क्षीरोदवेलानिलयाः
तमालवनवासिनः ।
नारिकेलाशनाश्चैव
तेषां सङ्ख्या न विद्यते ॥
Countless were the numbers that came from the Tamāla Vanas and the beaches of the Milky Ocean, who fed on coconuts.
4.37.26
वनेभ्यो गह्वरेभ्यश्च
सरिद्भ्यश्च महाबला ।
आगच्छद्वानरी सेना
पिबन्तीव दिवाकरम् ॥
An army of Vānaras of immense strength, that seemed to engulf the sun came along from Vanas, valleys and rivers.
4.37.27
ये तु त्वरयितुं याता
वानरास्सर्ववानरान् ।
ते वीरा हिमवच्छैले
ददृशुस्तं महाद्रुमम् ॥
The Vānara Veeras who went to the Himālayas to hasten up all the Vānaras there, saw a magnificent tree there.
4.37.28
तस्मिन्गिरिवरे पुण्ये 
यज्ञो माहेश्वरः पुरा ।
सर्वदेवमनस्तोषो
बभूव सुमनोरमः ॥
A Yajña dedicated to Mahēṡwara that stole everyone’s heart and that pleased all the Dēvas was once performed on that great holy mountain.
4.37.29
अन्ननिष्यन्दजातानि
मूलानि च फलानि च ।
अमृतास्वादकल्पानि
ददृशुस्तत्र वानराः ॥
There the Vānaras saw roots and fruits delicious like nectar, that grew from the food that was splattered (as part of performing the Yajña).
4.37.30
तदन्नसम्भवं दिव्यम्
फलमूलं मनोहरम् ।
यः कश्चित्सकृदश्नाति
मासं भवति तर्पितः ॥
He who has a single meal of the fruits and roots that grew from that food, is free from hunger for an entire month.
4.37.31
तानि मूलानि दिव्यानि
फलानि च फलाशनाः ।
औषधानि च दिव्यानि
जगृहुर्हरियूथपाः ॥
It was on such divine roots and fruits and other herbs, that the fruit-eating, eminent Vānaras fed on.
4.37.32
तस्माच्च यज्ञायतनात्
पुष्पाणि सुरभीणि च ।
आनिन्युर्वानरा गत्वा
सुग्रीवप्रियकारणात् ॥
The Vānaras who went there brought fragrant flowers from the Yajña grounds for the delectation of Sugreeva.
4.37.33
ते तु सर्वे हरिवराः
पृथिव्यां सर्ववानरान् ।
सञ्चोदयित्वा त्वरितम् 
यूथानां जग्मुरग्रतः ॥
And all those great Vānaras, after urging the Vānaras all over the earth, returned quickly, ahead of them.
4.37.34
ते तु तेन मुहूर्तेन
कपयः शीघ्र चारिणः ।
किष्किन्धां त्वरया प्राप्ताः
सुग्रीवो यत्र वानरः ॥
Those Vānaras of great speed, quickly returned to Kishkindhā where Sugreeva the Vānara was, at that Muhūrta.
4.37.35
ते गृहीत्वौषधीस्सर्वाः
फलमूलं च वानराः ।
तं प्रतिग्राहयामासुः
वचनं चेदमब्रुवन् ॥
Bringing along with them the herbs, roots and fruits, they said these words to Sugreeva after giving them to him:
4.37.36
सर्वे परिगताश्शैलाः
समुद्राश्च वनानि च ।
पृथिव्यां वानरास्सर्वे
शासनादुपयान्ति ते ॥
We went to all mountains, rivers and Vanas on this earth and all the Vānaras will be arriving, following your command.
4.37.37
एवं श्रुत्वा ततो हृष्टः
सुग्रीवः प्लवगाधिपः ।
प्रतिजग्राह च प्रीतः
तेषां सर्वमुपायनम् ॥
Sugreeva, the Lord of Vānaras was very happy hearing those words. Pleased, he accepted the gifts brought by them.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे सप्तत्रिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the thirty seventh Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
