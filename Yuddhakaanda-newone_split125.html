Yuddha Kaanda - Sarga 125
In this Sarga, Rāma instructs Hanumān to go to Ṡṛṅgibērapura and convey the news to his friend Guha and then to go to Ayōdhyā and convey the same to Bharata. He asks Hanumān to carefully observe the reaction of Bharata and assess what Bharata truly thinks about his return. He also tells Hanumān to convey to Bharata that Bharata can have the kingdom for himself, should he so desire.
Hanumān, as instructed, goes to Ṡṛṅgibērapura, crossing the confluence of the Gaṅgā and Yamuna on his way, and conveys the news to Guha. Then crossing the rivers Vālukini, Varūthee and Gōmati, he reaches Nandigrāma where he sees Bharata leading an ascetic life, with even his ministers and army chiefs in ascetic garbs, from where he was ruling the earth, keeping the sandals of Rāma in front of him.
Hanumān conveys the news of the arrival of Rāma along with Seetā, Lakshmaṇa and friends. Bharata falls in a swoon overwhelmed with joy. Recovering, he embraces Hanumān and drenches him with tears of joy.
6.125.1
अयोध्यां तु समालोक्य
चिन्तयामास राघवः ।
प्रियकामः प्रियं रामः
ततस्त्वरितविक्रमम् ॥
The fast paced Rāma, scion of Raghus, who always liked to do what pleases (others), beheld Ayōdhyā and wondered what he could do that would please everyone.
6.125.2
चिन्तयित्वा ततो दृष्टिम्
वानरेषु न्यपातयत् ।
उवाच धीमांस्तेजस्वी
हनूमन्तं प्लवङ्गमम् ॥
Pondering over it, he turned his eye to the Vānaras. He, the sagacious and powerful one then told Hanumān, the best of Vānaras:
6.125.3
अयोध्यां त्वरितो गत्वा
शीघ्रं प्लवगसत्तम ।
जानीहि कच्चित्कुशली
जनो नृपतिमन्दिरे ॥
Go fast to Ayōdhyā, O eminent Vānara, and find out quickly whether everyone in the king's palace is doing well.
6.125.4
शृङ्गिबेरपुरं प्राप्य
गुहं गहनगोचरम् ।
निषादाधिपतिं ब्रूहि
कुशलं वचनान्मम ॥
Go to Ṡṛṅgibērapura and inquire, on my behalf, the wellbeing of Guha, the Lord of Nishādas, who dwells in the dense forests.
6.125.5
श्रुत्वा तु मां कुशलिनम्
अरोगं विगतज्वरम् ।
भविष्यति गुहः प्रीतः
स ममात्मसमः सखा ॥
He, a friend whom I regard as my other self, will be pleased to hear that I am doing well, in good health and free from worries.
6.125.6
अयोध्यायाश्च ते मार्गम्
प्रवृत्तिं भरतस्य च ।
निवेदयिष्यति प्रीतो
निषादाधिपतिर्गुहः ॥
And Guha, the Lord of Nishādas, will gladly show you the path to Ayōdhyā and will give news about Bharata.
6.125.7
भरतस्तु त्वया वाच्यः
कुशलं वचनान्मम ।
सिद्धार्थं शंस मां तस्मै
सभार्यं सहलक्ष्मणम् ॥
You should tell Bharata, on my behalf, that I am doing well. Convey to him that I have fulfilled my vow, along with my wife and Lakshmaṇa.
6.125.8
हरणं चापि वैदेह्या
रावणेन बलीयसा ।
सुग्रीवेण च संसर्गम्
वालिनश्च वधं रणे ॥
And also about the abduction of Vaidēhee by the powerful Rāvaṇa, about getting to meet Sugreeva and about the slaying of Vāli, in that fight.
6.125.9
मैथिल्यन्वेषणं चैव
यथा चाधिगता त्वया ।
लङ्घयित्वा महातोयम्
आपगापतिमव्ययम् ॥
And also about the search for Seetā, and how you accomplished it, crossing the great, imperishable, expanse of waters of the lord of streams.
6.125.10
उपयानं समुद्रस्य
सागरस्य च दर्शनम् ।
यथा च कारितः सेतू
रावणश्च यथा हतः ॥
And also about our march to the ocean, my getting an audience with the lord of the ocean, about building a bridge over it, and how Rāvaṇa was killed.
6.125.11
वरदानं महेन्द्रेण
ब्रह्मणा वरुणेन च ।
महादेवप्रसादाच्च
पित्रा मम समागमम् ॥
And also about the boons given by Mahēndra, Brahma and Varuṇa, and about my seeing my father, thanks to the grace of Mahādēva.
6.125.12
उपयातं च मां सौम्य
भरतस्य निवेदय ।
सह राक्षसराजेन
हरीणामीश्वरेण च ॥
And, my dear, convey to Bharata that I am here along with the king of Rākshasas and the Lord of Vānaras.
6.125.13
जित्वा शत्रुगणान् रामः
प्राप्य चानुत्तमं यशः ।
उपयाति समृद्धार्थः
सह मित्रैर्महाबलैः ॥
And that Rāma has arrived, vanquishing all the hordes of enemies, gaining a renown that is excelled by none, fulfilling all his vows and accompanied by his friends of extraordinary strength.
6.125.14
एतच्छुत्वा यमाकारम्
भजते भरतस्ततः ।
स च ते वेदितव्यः स्यात्
सर्वं यच्चापि मां प्रति ॥
You should let me know the reaction of Bharata and all that he feels and says about me, upon hearing this.
6.125.15
ज्ञेयाश्च सर्वे वृत्तान्ता
भरतस्येङ्गितानि च ।
तत्त्वेन मुखवर्णेन
दृष्ट्या व्याभाषणेन च ॥
You should find out everything in detail, the deep and true feelings of Bharata, assessing them from the change of color on his face and from what his eyes and words convey.
6.125.16
सर्वकामसमृद्धं हि
हस्त्यश्वरथसङ्कुलम् ।
पितृपैतामहं राज्यम्
कस्य नावर्तयेन्मनः ॥
Whose mind will not succumb to the temptation of the kingdom that has come down from one's forefathers, teeming with elephants, horses and chariots, and offering every comfort and luxury?
6.125.17
सङ्गत्या भरतः श्रीमान्
राज्येनार्थी स्वयं भवेत् ।
प्रशास्तु वसुधां सर्वाम्
अखिलां रघुनन्दनः ॥
If the blessed Bharata, because of his long association, wants the kingdom for himself, may he, the delight of Raghus, rule the entire, unbroken, expanse of the earth!
6.125.18
तस्य बुद्धिं च विज्ञाय
व्यवसायं च वानर ।
यावन्न दूरं याताः स्म
क्षिप्रमागन्तुमर्हसि ॥
Having found out what he is thinking, and what his plans are, quickly return before we go too far.
6.125.19
इति प्रतिसमादिष्टो
हनुमान् मारुतात्मजः ।
मानुषं धारयन् रूपम्
अयोध्यां त्वरितो ययौ ॥
Thus instructed, Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, taking the form of a human, set out to Ayōdhyā in all haste.
6.125.20
अथोत्पपात वेगेन
हनुमान् मारुतात्मजः ।
गरुत्मानिव वेगेन
जिघृक्षन् पन्नगोत्तमम् ॥
Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, sprang into the sky with the speed of Garuḍa pouncing on a mighty serpent.
6.125.21
लङ्घयित्वा पितृपथम्
विहगेन्द्रालयं शुभम् ।
गङ्गायमुनयोर्भीमम्
समतीत्य समागमम् ।
शृङ्गिबेरपुरं प्राप्य
गुहमासाद्य वीर्यवान् ।
स वाचा शुभया हृष्टो
हनुमानिदमब्रवीत् ॥
Flying along the path of his father, the lovely habitat of the great birds, crossing the awe-inspiring confluence of Gaṅgā and Yamuna, the valiant Hanumān reached Ṡṛṅgibērapura, approached Guha and was glad to convey this good news to him:
6.125.23
सखा तु तव काकुत्स्थो
रामः सत्यपराक्रमः ।
सहसीतः ससौमित्रिः
स त्वां कुशलमब्रवीत् ॥
Your friend Rāma of true prowess, a descendant of Kākutstha, along with Seetā and Sowmitri inquires about your wellbeing.
6.125.24
पञ्चमीमद्य रजनीम्
उषित्वा वचनान्मुनेः ।
भरद्वाजाभ्यनुज्ञातम्
द्रक्ष्यस्यद्यैव राघवम् ॥
This very day, you shall see Rāghava after he takes leave of Bharadwāja, having spent the night of the fifth day of the bright fortnight with him upon the request of that Muni.
6.125.25
एवमुक्त्वा महातेजाः
सम्प्रहृष्टतनूरुहः ।
उत्पपात महावेगात्
वेगवानविचारयन् ॥
Having conveyed that much, the hair on his body standing on end, he, of extraordinary power and speed, leaped into the sky with extreme speed and without a care.
6.125.26
सोऽपश्यद्रामतीर्थं च
नदीं वालुकिनीं तथा ।
वरूथीं गोमतीं चैव
भीमं शालवनं तथा ।
प्रजाश्च बहुसाहस्रीः
स्फीतान् जनपदानपि ॥
On his way, he saw Rāmateertham and the rivers Vālukini, Varūthee, Gōmati and the awe-inspiring forest of Ṡāla trees, multitudes of people in their thousands, and the vast countryside.
6.125.27
स गत्वा दूरमध्वानम्
त्वरितः कपिकुञ्जरः ।
आससाद द्रुमान् फुल्लान्
नन्दिग्रामसमीपगान् ।
सुराधिपस्योपवने
यथा चैत्ररथे द्रुमान् ।
स्त्रीभिः सपुत्रैर्वृद्धैश्च
रममाणैः स्वरलङ्कृतैः ॥
Traveling for a long distance with all speed, that elephant among Vānaras reached the outskirts of Nandigrāma, where the trees were in full bloom, rivaling the trees in Caitraratha, the pleasance of the Lord of Suras, with women, children, and old people, all dressed up and enjoying around them.
Able men of prime age work, and with that, the women, children, and old people get to enjoy life with little care. Women, children, and old people may work, like Daṡaratha himself did, but should not have to; and that has been the ethos of Indic civilization, and likely that of other civilizations too.
6.125.29
क्रोशमात्रे त्वयोध्यायाः
चीरकृष्णाजिनाम्बरम् ।
ददर्श भरतं दीनम्
कृशमाश्रमवासिनम् ॥
With Ayōdhyā still a distance of a Krōṡa away, he saw Bharata, downcast, emaciated, clad in tree bark and deer skin, living in an Āṡrama.
The five Ṡlōkas starting with this one constitute a single long sentence, with multiple clauses. For the sake of convenience, I translated each clause into a (short) sentence of its own.
6.125.30
जटिलं मलदिग्धाङ्गम्
भ्रातृव्यसनकर्शितम् ।
फलमूलाशिनं दान्तम्
तापसं धर्मचारिणम् ॥
He, in agony on account of his brother, was focused on Dharma alone, doing Tapa, his body unkempt and smeared with dust, his hair matted in locks, rejecting his sensual urges and limiting his food to fruits and roots.
Everything men do traces back to one or more of the three urges: Dharma (doing what is needed or expected of one), Artha (doing something out of interest and passion) and Kāma (doing something for pleasure and indulgence).
Bharata, or anyone in a similar situation, would not be in the mood of pursuing the urges of Artha or Kāma, and would do the minimum expected of them, i.e., only Dharma.
The phrase 'Dharma Cāri' means one pursuing the urge of Dharma. But it also, based on the context, means, one pursuing only one Dharma, to the exclusion of the other two. We saw this phrase used earlier for Ṡabari.
6.125.31
समुन्नतजटाभारम्
वल्कलाजिनवाससम् ।
नियतं भावितात्मानम्
ब्रह्मर्षिसमतेजसम् ॥
With those heavy locks of matted hair rising high, clad with clothes made of tree bark, keeping to a strict discipline, and keeping his soul extremely pure, he was shining with the splendor that equals that of a Brahmarshi.
Men and women are different, even in their attitude towards spirituality.
Men, when they become deeply spiritual, withdraw themselves to the extent that they do not even care about the external world, including keeping their bodies clean and tidy.
Women, on the other hand, clean themselves as nicely as they can, to offer themselves like a flower to the almighty.
Men have an internal connection with the almighty, and women, a personal connection. Men dive into the deep reality of Nature, and women, merge into it, like a river in an ocean.
6.125.32
पादुके ते पुरस्कृत्य
प्रशासन्तं वसुन्धराम् ।
चातुर्वर्ण्यस्य लोकस्य
त्रातारं सर्वतो भयात् ॥
With the sandals placed right in front of him, he was ruling the earth, freeing the people of all the four Varṇas from fear from any quarter.
What is expected of a king is described in a single Ṡlōka here.
6.125.33
उपस्थितममात्यैश्च
शुचिभिश्च पुरोहितैः ।
बलमुख्यैश्च युक्तैश्च
काषायाम्बरधारिभिः ॥
He was sitting with ministers, army chiefs, Purōhitas that were pure (in mind and body), all clad in saffron clothes, around him.
6.125.34
न हि ते राजपुत्रं तम्
चीरकृष्णाजिनाम्बरम् ।
परिभोक्तुं व्यवस्यन्ति
पौरा वै धर्मवत्सलम् ॥
With their prince, who loved Dharma, clad in tree bark and deer skin, which subject of his would have the heart to indulge in comforts or luxuries?
6.125.35
तं धर्ममिव धर्मज्ञम्
देहवन्तमिवापरम् ।
उवाच प्राञ्जलिर्वाक्यम्
हनुमान् मरुतात्मजः ॥
Hanumān, the son of Vāyu, his palms joined in reverence, said to that Dharmajña, who looked like Dharma itself in a human form:
6.125.36
वसन्तं दण्डकारण्ये
यं त्वं चीरजटाधरम् ।
अनुशोचसि काकुत्स्थम्
स त्वां कुशलमब्रवीत् ॥
The Kākutstha, who lives in Daṇḍaka Araṇya clad in tree bark and with matted locks, for whose sake you are grieving inquires your wellbeing.
6.125.37
प्रियमाख्यामि ते देव
शोकं त्यज सुदारुणम् ।
अस्मिन् मुहूर्ते भ्रात्रा त्वम्
रामेण सह सङ्गतः ॥
Let me tell you, O lord, something that would please you. Give up your terrible grief, you shall meet your brother Rāma, this very hour.
6.125.38
निहत्य रावणं रामः
प्रतिलभ्य च मैथिलीम् ।
उपयाति समृद्धार्थः
सह मित्रैर्महाबलैः ॥
Having slain Rāvaṇa and recovering Maithili, Rāma, along with his friends of extraordinary strength, comes, all his objects fulfilled.
6.125.39
लक्ष्मणश्च महातेजा
वैदेही च यशस्विनी ।
सीता समग्रा रामेण
महेन्द्रेण यथा शची ॥
Lakshmaṇa of extraordinary splendor and Seetā, the princess of Vidēha, of great renown along with Rāma, like Ṡacee with Mahēndra, are coming.
6.125.40
एवमुक्तो हनुमता
भरतः कैकयीसुतः ।
पपात सहसा हृष्टो
हर्षान्मोहमुपागमत् ॥
Thus told by Hanumān, Bharata, who loved his brother, was so happy that he fell in a swoon immediately, overwhelmed with joy.
6.125.41
ततो मुहूर्तादुत्थाय
प्रत्याश्वस्य च राघवः ।
हनुमन्तमुवाचेदम्
भरतः प्रियवादिनम् ॥
Getting up in a while and regaining his composure, Bharata, a descendant of Raghu, said this to Hanumān, who brought him the pleasing news:
6.125.42
अशोकजैः प्रीतिमयैः
कपिमालिङ्ग्य सम्भ्रमात् ।
सिषेच भरतः श्रीमान्
विपुलैरस्त्रबिन्दुभिः ॥
Happy, pleased and excited, the blessed Bharata embraced the Vānara and drenched him with his copious tears of joy.
6.125.43
देवो वा मानुषो वा त्वम्
अनुक्रोशादिहागतः ।
प्रियाख्यानस्य ते सौम्य
ददामि ब्रुवतः प्रियम् ।
गवां शतसहस्रं च
ग्रामाणां च शतं परम् ।
सुकुण्डलाः शुभाचारा
भार्याः कन्याश्च षोडश ।
हेमवर्णाः सुनासोरूः
शशिसौम्याननाः स्त्रियः ।
सर्वाभरणसम्पन्नाः
सम्पन्नाः कुलजातिभिः ॥
I do not know whether you are a human or a Dēva, who has come here with all the kindness to give me this pleasing news.
For giving the pleasing news, my dear, I give you a hundred thousand cows, and a hundred villages, and sixteen virgins for your wives who are of good character, golden complexion, shapely noses, lovely thighs, faces as pleasing as the moon and decked with every kind of ornament including ear-pendants, all of noble families.
6.125.46
निशम्य रामागमनं नृपात्मजः
कपिप्रवीरस्य तदद्भुतोपमम् ।
प्रहर्षितो रामदिदृक्षयाऽभवत्
पुनश्च हर्षादिदमब्रवीद्वचः ॥
On hearing the news of the arrival of Rāma conveyed by that Vānara of great valor, which was nothing short of a miracle for him, the prince was overwhelmed with joy. Eager to see Rāma, he said these words, with all joy:
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
युद्धकाण्डे पञ्चविंशत्युत्तरशततमः सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the one hundred and twenty fifth Sarga in Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
