Baala Kaanda - Sarga 45
In this Sarga, Rāma asks Viṡwāmitra about the city of Viṡāla. Viṡwāmitra starts telling the background story, which includes the churning of the milky ocean (Ksheera Sāgara Mathanam), one of the most celebrated legends of Indian mythology.
(Ksheera Sāgara Mathanam, as described here in the Bāla Kāṇḍa is a shortened version. A more elaborate version is available in Ṡrimad Bhāgavatam).
1.45.1
विश्वामित्रवचश्श्रुत्वा
राघवस्सहलक्ष्मणः ।
विस्मयं परमं गत्वा
विश्वामित्रमथाब्रवीत् ॥
Rāma, along with Lakshmaṇa was wonderstruck by the story told by Viṡwāmitra and said to him:
1.45.2
अत्यद्भुतमिदं ब्रह्मन्
कथितं परमं त्वया ।
गङ्गावतरणं पुण्यम्
सागरस्यापि पूरणम् ॥
O Brahman! The story of the descent of Gaṅgā and the filling of auspicious ocean by those waters is amazing and wonderful.
1.45.3
तस्य सा शर्वरी सर्वा
सह सौमित्रिणा तदा ।
जगाम चिन्तयानस्य
विश्वामित्रकथां शुभाम् ॥
The night passed as he (Rāma) along with Lakshmaṇa discussed and reminisced the auspicious stories narrated by Viṡwāmitra.
1.45.4
ततः प्रभाते विमले
विश्वामित्रं महामुनिम् ।
उवाच राघवो वाक्यम्
कृताह्निकमरिन्दमः ॥
After daybreak as Viṡwāmitra completed his morning ablutions, the prince of Raghu dynasty, the tormentor of foes, said to him:
1.45.5
गता भगवती रात्रिः
श्रोतव्यं परमं श्रुतम् ।
क्षणभूतेव नौ रात्रि
स्सम्वृत्तेयं महातपः ।
इमां चिन्तयतस्सर्वाम्
निखिलेन कथां तव ॥
O accomplished one with great Tapa (Viṡwāmitra)! We heard the best of the stories that should be heard. The goddesses of night have left us. Reminiscing the stories that you told, the night passed for us like a second.
Referring to all natural powers that are beyond the control of humans as gods and goddesses is a unique way of relating to them. Here, 'night' is referred to as a 'goddess'.
As many of the readers would know, even 'sleep' and 'death' are referred to as goddesses in Indian culture.
1.45.6
तराम सरितां श्रेष्ठाम्
पुण्यां त्रिपथगां नदीम् ।
नौरेषा हि सुखास्तीर्णा
ऋषीणां पुण्यकर्मणाम् ।
भगवन्तमिह प्राप्तम्
ज्ञात्वा त्वरितमागता ॥
Now we shall cross this great and auspicious river Tripathagā. This well covered boat of Ṛshis has been made ready for you, knowing of your arrival here.
1.45.8
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
राघवस्य महात्मनः ।
सन्तारं कारयामास
सर्षिसङ्घस्सराघवः ॥
Upon hearing those words of the Mahātma, the prince of Raghu dynasty, Viṡwāmitra prepared to cross the river along with all the Ṛshis and the princes of Raghu dynasty.
1.45.9
उत्तरं तीरमासाद्य
सम्पूज्यर्षिगणं तदा ।
गङ्गाकूले निविष्टास्ते
विशालां ददृशुः पुरीम् ॥
They reached the northern bank of Gaṅgā and rested there after paying homage to the Ṛshis who helped them cross the river. Then they saw a city called Viṡāla.
1.45.10
ततो मुनिवरस्तूर्णम्
जगाम सहराघवः ।
विशालां नगरीं रम्याम्
दिव्यां स्वर्गोपमां तदा ॥
The best among the Munis, then, along with the princes of Raghu dynasty went to that splendid, beautiful and heaven-like city of Viṡāla.
1.45.11
अथ रामो महाप्राज्ञो
विश्वामित्रं महामुनिम् ।
पप्रच्छ प्राञ्जलिर्भूत्वा
विशालामुत्तमां पुरीम् ॥
Then the astute Rāma, with folded hands, asked the great Muni Viṡwāmitra about that city of Viṡāla.
1.45.12
कतरो राजवंशोऽयम्
विशालायां महामुने ।
श्रोतुमिच्छामि भद्रं ते
परं कौतूहलं हि मे ॥
O great Muni! What is the dynasty that rules this city of Viṡāla? I am very curious and would like to know. May all bode well for you!
1.45.13
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
रामस्य मुनिपुङ्गवः ।
आख्यातुं तत्समारेभे
विशालस्य पुरातनम् ॥
Hearing those words of Rāma, the eminent Muni started telling the story of that legendary city Viṡāla.
1.45.14
श्रूयतां राम शक्रस्य
कथां कथयतश्शुभाम् ।
अस्मिन् देशे तु यद्वृत्तम्
तदपि शृणु राघव ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty, let me start with the auspicious story of Indra and then what happened in this city.
1.45.15
पूर्वं कृतयुगे राम
दितेः पुत्रा महाबलाः ।
अदितेश्च महाभाग
वीर्यवन्तस्सुधार्मिकाः ॥
O Rāma! In the ages gone by, in the Kṛta Yuga there were sons of Diti, who were strong and the sons of Aditi, who were Dhārmikas and spirited.
1.45.16
ततस्तेषां नरश्रेष्ठ
बुद्धिरासीन्महात्मनाम् ।
अमरा अजराश्चैव
कथं स्याम निरामयाः ॥
Those great souls wanted to become immortal, disease-free and young forever.
1.45.17
तेषां चिन्तयतां राम
बुद्धिरासीन्महात्मनाम् ।
क्षीरोदमथनं कृत्वा
रसं प्राप्स्याम तत्र वै ॥
O Rāma! Then it occurred to those great souls that they could churn the milky ocean and obtain its nectar and consume it.
1.45.18
ततो निश्चित्य मथनम्
योक्त्रं कृत्वा च वासुकिम् ।
मन्थानं मन्दरं कृत्वा
ममन्थुरमितौजसः ॥
Having decided on churning the ocean, those supremely radiant souls made the Mandara mountain as the churning rod and the great serpent Vāsuki as the churning rope and started churning the ocean.
1.45.19
अथ वर्षसहस्रेण
योक्त्रसर्पशिरांसि च ।
वमन्त्यति विषं तत्र
ददंशुर्दशनैश्शिलाः ॥
After a thousand years passed thus churning, the heads of the snake that was used as the churning rope started spewing poison and started biting the rocks with their teeth.
1.45.20
उत्पपाताग्निसङ्काशम्
हालाहलमहाविषम् ।
तेन दग्धं जगत्सर्वम्
सदेवासुरमानुषम् ॥
Then arose a fiery poison called 'Hālāhala' which started burning the entire world along with all the Dēvas, Asuras and Humans.
From our recent memory, we can recollect the Bhopal gas tragedy. There is a lot buried under the earth and in the milky ocean. The Milky ocean symbolically represents the mystery of the universe.
Nature knows how to keep the harmful substances away from us, and us away from them. But when we churn they all come out. There is no guarantee all that is hidden from us is valuable.
The gas that came out in the Bhopal factory was a result of several combinations of chemicals that would not have occurred naturally, but did due to the churning of nature by humans.
1.45.21
अथ देवा महादेवम्
शङ्करं शरणार्थिनः ।
जग्मुः पशुपतिं रुद्रम्
त्राहि त्राहीति तुष्टुवुः ॥
Then the Dēvas went to the great, fierce Dēva, Ṡaṅkara, the lord of all beings, seeking respite, and begged him to save them.
1.45.22
एवमुक्तस्ततो देवैः
देवदेवेश्वरः प्रभुः ।
प्रादुरासीत्ततोऽत्रैव
शङ्खचक्रधरो हरिः ॥
As the Dēvas were imploring the Dēva of the Dēvas (Ṡiva), Hari (Vishṇu) appeared there adorned with Ṡankha and Cakra.
Ṡankha is conch and Cakra is discuss.
1.45.23
उवाचैनं स्मितं कृत्वा
रुद्रं शूलभृतं हरिः ।
दैवतैर्मथ्यमाने तु
यत्पूर्वं समुपस्थितम् ।
त्वदीयंहि सुरश्रेष्ठ
सुराणामग्रजोऽसि यत् ।
अग्रपूजामिमां मत्वा
गृहाणेदं विषं प्रभो ॥
With a smile on his face, Hari told Rudra, the bearer of Ṡūla: "what came first from this churning done by Dēvas goes to you, the first among all the Dēvas. Consider that you are offered the first among the honors, and take this poison."
Indic civilization knows how to make humor even out of gods. What Vishṇu says here is quite funny. But for those, whose palate does not agree with fun among gods, there is also a deep meaning.
Vishṇu says that Ṡiva is first among the gods. The form of Ṡiva represents the universe before it has taken a form, for which Vishṇu became responsible.
1.45.25
इत्युक्त्वा च सुरश्रेष्ठः
तत्रैवान्तरधीयत ।
देवतानां भयं दृष्ट्वा
श्रुत्वा वाक्यं तु शार्ङ्गिणः ।
हालाहलविषं घोरम्
स जग्राहामृतोपमम् ॥
The chief of the Dēvas, Vishṇu disappeared after speaking thus. To assuage the fears of Dēvas and to respect the words of Vishṇu, He (Ṡiva) consumed the Hālāhala as if it were Amṛta.
1.45.26
देवान्विसृज्य देवेशो
जगाम भगवान् हरः ।
ततो देवासुरास्सर्वे
ममन्थू रघुनन्दन ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! After the lord of Dēvas, Bhagawān Hara (Ṡiva) left, all the Dēvas and Asuras continued the churning.
1.45.27
प्रविवेशाथ पातालम्
मन्थानः पर्वतोऽनघ ।
ततो देवास्सगन्धर्वाः
तुष्टुवुर्मधुसूदनम् ॥
Then the mountain that was used as the churning rod sunk into the depths of the nether world. All the Dēvas then, along with Gandharvas prayed to the annihilator of the Rākshasa Madhu (Vishṇu):
1.45.28
त्वं गतिः सर्वभूतानाम्
विशेषेण दिवौकसाम् ।
पालयास्मान्महाबाहो
गिरिमुद्धर्तुमर्हसि ॥
You are the ultimate resort and the savior of all beings and especially of the Dēvas. O you of mighty arm, please take care of us. Only you would be able to lift this mountain.
1.45.29
इति श्रुत्वा हृषीकेशः
कामठं रूपमास्थितः ।
पर्वतं पृष्ठतः कृत्वा
शिश्ये तत्रोदधौ हरिः ॥
Hearing those pleas, Hṛshikesha (Vishṇu) took the form of a turtle and lifted it on his back and reclined in that ocean supporting it.
Hṛshikesha is one who is in charge of all Indriyas (senses).
1.45.30
पर्वताग्रे तु लोकात्मा
हस्तेनाक्रम्य केशवः ।
देवानां मध्यतः स्थित्वा
ममन्थ पुरुषोत्तमः ॥
Kēsava, the soul of all the worlds and the foremost of all active beings, also stood among the Dēvas and held the top of the mountain with his hand (so that it would stay stable).
1.45.31
अथ वर्षसहस्रेण
सदण्डस्सकमण्डलुः ।
पूर्वं धन्वन्तरिर्नाम
अप्सराश्च सुवर्चसः ॥
After another thousand years of churning, Dhanvantari with Daṇḍa and Kamaṇḍala and beautiful Apsaras emerged from it.
Daṇḍa is a staff used by Munis, as a support to their hand. Kamaṇḍala is a water pitcher. Dhanvantari would turn out to be the physician of Dēvas. Apsaras were women that would become the nymphs of Dēvas.
1.45.32
अप्सु निर्मथनादेव
रसास्तस्माद्वरस्त्रियः ।
उत्पेतुर्मनुजश्रेष्ठ
तस्मादप्सरसोऽभवन् ॥
O best among men! Because those women came out of churning liquid (Apsu) they came to be known as Apsaras.
1.45.33
षष्टिः कोट्योऽभवंस्तासां
अप्सराणां सुवर्चसाम् ।
असङ्ख्येयास्तु काकुत्स्थ
यास्तासां परिचारिकाः ॥
O prince of Kākutstha lineage! Sixty crores of those gorgeous Apsaras were thus born. And the attendants for them were beyond count.
1.45.34
न तास्स्म प्रतिगृह्णन्ति
सर्वे ते देवदानवाः ।
अप्रतिग्रहणादेव
तेन साधारणास्स्मृताः ॥
However, none of the Dēvas and Dānavas claimed them as theirs and they were all left to be common to all.
1.45.35
वरुणस्य ततः कन्या
वारुणी रघुनन्दन ।
उत्पपात महाभागा
मार्गमाणा परिग्रहम् ॥
O prince of Raghu dynasty! Then came the enticing and blessed damsel Vāruṇi and wondered who would take her.
I have never been able to find a satisfactory explanation of what Vāruṇi (Sura) is. It is generally described as a type of intoxicating liquid. Its significance is not clear. It is also not clear why Dēvas consumed it and Dānavas did not.
In this Ṡlōka, Vāruṇi is referred to as a woman symbolically, just as Gaṅgā was referred to as a woman in the earlier Sargas.
1.45.36
दितेः पुत्रा न तां राम
जगृहुर्वरुणात्मजाम् ।
अदितेस्तु सुता वीर
जगृहुस्तामनिन्दिताम् ॥
O Rāma! The sons of Diti would not court her, the daughter of Varuṇa. O Veera! But the sons of Aditi took her, the untainted.
1.45.37
असुरास्तेन दैतेयाः
सुरास्तेनादितेस्सुताः ।
हृष्टाः प्रमुदिताश्चासन्
वारुणीग्रहणात्सुराः ॥
For not taking the Sura the sons of Diti came to be known as Asuras and the sons of Aditi are known as Suras. Suras felt happy for taking Vāruṇi.
1.45.38
उच्चैश्श्रवा हयश्रेष्ठो
मणिरत्नं च कौस्तुभम् ।
उदतिष्ठन्नरश्रेष्ठ
तथैवामृतमुत्तमम् ॥
O best among men! Then came out Ucchaiṡrava, the best among horses and Koustubha, the best among precious stones and then Amṛta, the potion of immortality.
1.45.39
अथ तस्य कृते राम
महानासीत्कुलक्षयः ।
अदितेस्तु ततः पुत्रा
दितेः पुत्रानसूदयन् ॥
In the fight for that Amṛta, clans (in their entirety) became decimated. The sons of Aditi killed the sons of Diti.
1.45.40
एकतोऽभ्यागमन् सर्वे
ह्यसुरा राक्षसैस्सह ।
युद्धमासीन्महाघोरम्
वीर त्रैलोक्यमोहनम् ॥
With the sons of Diti along with Rākshasas on one side, and the sons of Aditi on the other side a terrible battle took place that held all the three worlds by awe.
1.45.41
यदा क्षयं गतं सर्वम्
तदा विष्णुर्महाबलः ।
अमृतं सोऽहरत्तूर्णम्
मायामास्थाय मोहिनीम् ॥
As everything was perishing, the mighty strong Vishṇu took away the Amṛta quickly, by deluding everyone in the form of Mōhini.
1.45.42
ये गताभिमुखं विष्णुं
अक्षयं पुरुषोत्तमम् ।
सम्पिष्टास्ते तदा युद्धे
विष्णुना प्रभविष्णुना ॥
Everyone that tried to stop Vishṇu, the foremost of spirited beings, was crushed to the ground in a fight by that capable Vishṇu.
1.45.43
अदितेरात्मजा वीरा
दितेः पुत्रान्निजघ्निरे ।
तस्मिन् युद्धे महाघोरे
दैतेयादित्ययोर्भृशम् ॥
In that terrible fight between the sons of Diti and Aditi, sons of Aditi killed the sons of Diti.
Ṡlōkas 43 and 44 do not seem to go with the flow, by structure and by content. I suspect that these Ṡlōkas must have got here because of probable shuffling of palm leaves somewhere.
1.45.44
निहत्य दितिपुत्रांश्च
राज्यं प्राप्य पुरन्दरः ।
शशास मुदितो लोकान्
सर्षिसङ्घान् सचारणान् ॥
Having killed all the sons of Diti, Purandara (Indra) gained the kingdom including all the Ṛshis and Cāraṇas and ruled over it happily.
The word Cāraṇa is interpreted variedly as Dēvas and as Singers.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
बालकाण्डे पञ्चचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty fifth Sarga in Bāla Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
