Ayodhya Kaanda - Sarga 69
In the previous Sarga, the messengers, after many days of travel from Ayōdhyā, reach the city of Girivraja by night fall.
The same night Bharata sees a torrent of strange dreams in which he finds Daṡaratha in an inconceivable form, such as falling from the top of a mountain into a filthy hole of cow dung and riding in a chariot drawn by donkeys. He sees many other inconceivable scenes such as the moon falling on the earth and oceans drying up.
He feels worried that the dreams might be an omen for the death of Rāma, Lakshmaṇa, the King, or his own self. He feels frightened and at a loss to fathom the source of such a gripping fear.
He shares the details of the dreams and the fear that overtook him, with his friends, who tried to cheer him up, upon seeing him depressed.
2.69.1
यामेव रात्रिं ते दूताः
प्रविशन्ति स्म तां पुरीम् ।
भरतेनापि तां रात्रिम्
स्वप्नो दृष्टोऽयमप्रियः ॥
Bharata had an unpleasant dream on the same night in which the messengers entered the city.
2.69.2
व्युष्टामेव तु तां रात्रिम्
दृष्ट्वा तं स्वप्नमप्रियम् ।
पुत्रो राजाधिराजस्य
सुभृशं पर्यतप्यत ॥
The next morning, the son of the king of kings became extremely worried about those unpleasant dreams.
2.69.3
तप्यमानं समाज्ञाय
वयस्याः प्रियवादिनः ।
आयासं हि विनेष्यन्तः
सभायां चक्रिरे कथाः ॥
His friends, who always spoke endearingly, noticed that he was worried and tried to engage him in a conversation in the assembly to cheer him up.
2.69.4
वादयन्ति तथा शान्तिम्
लासयन्त्यपि चापरे ।
नाटकान्यपरे प्राहुः
हास्यानि विविधानि च ॥
To help him gain normalcy, some played on instruments, some enacted dramatic scenes, and some told various kinds of jokes.
2.69.5
स तैर्महात्मा भरतः
सखिभिः प्रियवादिभिः ।
गोष्ठीहास्यानि कुर्वद्भिः
न प्राहृष्यत राघवः ॥
Those pleasing words and conversations of his friends could not make that Mahatma Rāghava any cheerful.
2.69.6
तमब्रवीत्प्रियसखो
भरतं सखिभिर्वृतम् ।
सुहृद्भिः पर्युपासीनः
किं सखे नानुमोदसे ॥
One of the dearest friends asked Bharata who was in the midst of all of his friends: "What is the reason for you not being happy, even though you are surrounded by all your friends?"
2.69.7
एवं ब्रुवाणं सुहृदम्
भरतः प्रत्युवाच ह ।
श्रुणु त्वं यन्निमित्तं मे
दैन्यमेतदुपागतम् ॥
To that friend who asked thus, Bharata replied, "Listen, I will tell you why I am feeling so depressed!"
2.69.8
स्वप्ने पितरमद्राक्षम्
मलिनं मुक्त मूर्धजम् ।
पतन्तमद्रिशिखरात्
कलुषे गोमयह्रदे ॥
In my dream, I saw my father, dirty, and with his hair disheveled, falling from the top of a mountain into a turbid pool full of cow dung !
2.69.9
प्लवमानश्च मे दृष्टः
स तस्मिन्गोमयह्रदे ।
पिबन्नञ्जलिना तैलम्
हसन्नपि मुहुर्मुहुः ॥
I saw him floating in that pool of cow dung and drinking oil from his cupped hands and laughing fitfully.
2.69.10
ततस्तिलोदनम् भुक्त्वा
पुनः पुनरधश्शिराः ।
तैलेनाभ्यक्तसर्वाङ्गः
तैलमेवान्वगाहत ॥
Then, eating rice mixed with sesame seeds, he, with his head hanging down and his body all smeared with oil, dived again and again into a bath of oil.
2.69.11
स्वप्नेऽपि सागरं शुष्कम्
चन्द्रं च पतितं भुवि ।
उपरुद्धां च जगतीम्
तमसेव समावृताम् ।
औपवाह्यस्य नागस्य
विषाणं शकलीकृतम् ।
सहसाचापि संशान्तम्
ज्वलितं जातवेदसम् ।
अवतीर्णां च पृथिवीम्
शुष्कांश्च विविधान् द्रुमान् ।
अहं पश्यामि विध्वस्तान्
सधूमांश्चापि पर्वतान् ॥
Also, in the dream, I saw the ocean dried up, the moon fallen on earth and the world obscured as if covered by darkness. I saw the tusk of the elephant that the king rides on, broken into pieces. I saw a blazing fire go out suddenly and the earth shattered, and different kinds of trees dried up, and mountains crumbled, with smoke rising from them.
2.69.14
पीठे कार्ष्णायसे चैनम्
निषण्णं कृष्णवाससम् ।
प्रहसन्ति स्म राजानम्
प्रमदाः कृष्णपिङ्गलाः ॥
Women, black and red in complexion, were laughing at the king who sat on an iron seat covered with a black cloth.
2.69.15
त्वरमाणश्च धर्मात्मा
रक्तमाल्यानुलेपनः ।
रथेन खरयुक्तेन
प्रयातो दक्षिणामुखः ॥
That Dharmātma, wearing red garlands and bedaubed with red paste, went southbound in a chariot yoked with donkeys.
2.69.16
प्रहसन्तीव राजानम्
प्रमदा रक्तवासिनी ।
प्रकर्षन्ती मया दृष्टा
राक्षसी विकृतानना ॥
I saw a Rākshasa woman with an ugly face clad in red seem to be laughing at the king and dragging him along!
2.69.17
एवमेतन्मया दृष्टम्
इमां रात्रिं भयावहम् ।
अहं रामोऽथवा राजा
लक्ष्मणो वा मरिष्यति ॥
This is what I saw on this dreadful night. Either I or Rāma, or the king or Lakshmaṇa, is going to die.
2.69.18
नरो यानेन यस्स्वप्ने
खर युक्तेन याति हि ।
अचिरात्तस्य धूमाग्रम्
चितायां सम्प्रदृश्यते ॥
If anyone is seen in a dream riding a chariot drawn by donkeys, smoke rising from his funeral pyre also will be seen, not before too long.
2.69.19
एतन्निमित्तं दीनोऽहम्
तन्नवः प्रतिपूजये ।
शुष्यतीव च मे कण्ठः
न स्वस्थमिव मे मनः ॥
This is why I feel dispirited and am unable to respond to you in kind. My throat feels dry and my mind feels unsettled.
2.69.20
न पश्यामि भयस्थानम्
भयं चैवोपधारये ।
भ्रष्टश्च स्वरयोगो मे
छाया चोपहता मम ।
जुगुप्सन्निव चाऽत्मानम्
न पश्यमि च कारणम् ॥
I do not see a reason for fear; yet, I feel terrified. My voice is subdued and my glow has taken a beating. I feel disgusted about myself, but I cannot see a reason why.
2.69.21
इमां च दुस्स्वप्नगतिं निशाम्यताम्
अनेकरूपामवितर्कितां पुरा ।
भयं महत्तद्धृदयान्नयाति मे
विचिन्त्य राजानमचिन्तदर्शनम् ॥
After seeing the torrent of these strange dreams which could never have even been imagined before, the great fear that has taken possession of my heart will not go away, as I keep brooding over the sight of the king in such an inconceivable form!
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
अयोध्याकाण्डे एकोनसप्ततितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixty ninth Sarga in Ayōdhyā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
