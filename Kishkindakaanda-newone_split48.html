Kishkindha Kaanda - Sarga 48
In this Sarga Aṅgada, Tāra, Hanumān and others go in the southerly direction searching for Seetā. They search the Vindhya Mountains and its caves. They go further and come across an area where there are no people, birds or beasts, no water in the rivers, no trees and no flower or fruit on the trees, because of the curse laid by a great Muni by name Kaṇḍu. Then they come across an Asura who Aṅgada kills by hitting with his open palm. Then they continue the search and finally become tired and dejected.
4.48.1
सह ताराङ्गदाभ्यां तु
सहसा  हनुमान्कपिः ।
सुग्रीवेण यथोद्दिष्टम्
गन्तुं देशं प्रचक्रमे ॥
Hanumān immediately started to the region he was assigned along with Tāra and Aṅgada.
4.48.2
स तु दूरमुपागम्य
सर्वैस्तैः कपिसत्तमैः ।
ततो विचित्य विन्ध्यस्य
गुहाश्च गहनानि च ।
पर्वताग्रान्नदीदुर्गान्
सरांसि विपुलान्द्रुमान् ।
वृक्षषण्डांश्च विविधान्
पर्वतान्घनपादपान् ॥
Going a long distance along with those great Vānaras, he searched the caves and thickets of the Vindhyas, the mountains, the mountain crests, the rivers the huge trees, groves of trees, wild trees and places that were difficult to access.
4.48.4
अन्वेषमाणास्ते सर्वे
वानरास्सर्वतो दिशम् ।
न सीतां ददृशुर्वीरा
मैथिलीं जनकात्मजाम् ॥
The Vānaras, those Veeras, search as they did in every direction, failed to find the princess of Mithilā, the daughter of Janaka.
4.48.5
ते भक्षयन्तो मूलानि
फलानि विविधान्यपि ।
अन्वेषमाणा दुर्धर्षा
न्यवसं स्तत्र तत्र ह ॥
Feeding themselves on roots and various kinds of fruits, those invincible (Vānaras) went on searching taking rest here and there.
4.48.6
स तु देशो दुरन्वेषो
गुहागहनवान्महान् ।
निर्जलं निर्जनं शून्यम्
गहनं घोरदर्शनम् ॥
That region was very difficult to search with huge caves and thickets that were difficult to access. It was a desolate, impenetrable and frightful place, with no people and no water.
4.48.7
तादृशान्यप्यरण्यानि
विचित्य भृशपीडिताः ।
स देशश्च दुरन्वेष्यो
गुहागहनवान्महान् ॥
Searching those kinds of forests and places that were difficult to search, full of huge caves and inaccessible places, they felt quite beaten.
4.48.8
त्यक्त्वा तु तं तदा देशम्
सर्वे वै हरियूथपाः ।
देशमन्यं दुराधर्षम्
विविशुश्चाकुतो भयाः ॥
Then, leaving that region behind, the Vānara captains, who knew no fear, moved on to another impenetrable area.
4.48.9
यत्र वन्ध्यफला वृक्षा
विपुष्पाः पर्णवर्जिताः ।
निस्तोयास्सरितो यत्र
मूलं यत्र सुदुर्लभम् ॥
The trees there bore no fruit. They did not have any flower or leaves. There was no water in the rivers and even roots were hard to be found.
4.48.10
न सन्ति महिषा यत्र
न मृगा न च हस्तिनः ।
शार्दूलाः पक्षिणो वापि
ये चान्ये वनगोचराः ॥
No buffalos, no deer, no elephants, no tigers, no birds and no other wild creatures were to be found there.
4.48.11
न यत्रवृक्षा नौषध्यो
न वल्ल्यो नापि वीरुधः ।
स्निग्धपत्रास्स्थले यत्र
पद्मिन्यः फुल्लपङ्कजाः ।
प्रेक्षणीयास्सुगन्धाश्च
भ्रमरैश्चापिवर्जिताः ॥
No trees, no shrubs, no vines and no creepers were there in that place. There were no lotus vines with tender leaves and lotuses in bloom to attract the eye or to give fragrance, nor were there any bees.
4.48.12
कण्डुर्नाम महाभागः
सत्यवादी तपोधनः ।
महर्षिः परमामर्षी
नियमैर्दुष्प्रधर्षणः ॥
There was a truly blessed Maharshi by name Kaṇḍu, who was rich with Tapa, who stood by Truth, who was invincible by virtue of his vows and who was easily irascible.
4.48.13
तस्य तस्मिन्वने पुत्रो
बालको दशवार्षिकः ।
प्रणष्टो जीवितान्ताय
क्रुद्धस्तेन महामुनिः ॥
That great Muni became angry when his son met with his end upon entering that Vana.
4.48.14
तेन धर्मात्मना शप्तम्
कृत्स्नं तत्रमहद्वनम् ।
अशरण्यं दुराधर्षम्
मृगपक्षिविवर्जितम् ॥
Then, with a curse laid by that Dharmātma, the vast Vana in its entirety became inaccessible, and it could afford no shelter (to any creature), with no bird or beast living in it.
4.48.15
तस्य ते काननान्तांस्तु
गिरीणां कन्दराणि च ।
प्रभवानि नदीनां च
विचिन्वन्ति समाहिताः ॥
Then they searched, with more attention, the forests, hills, canyons and the the places where the rivers were born.
4.48.16
तत्र चापि महात्मानो
नापश्यञ्जनकात्मजाम् ।
हर्तारं रावणं वापि
सुग्रीवप्रियकारिणः ॥
Those Mahātmas, as eager as they were to please Sugreeva, could not find the daughter of Janaka, nor Rāvaṇa, her abductor.
4.48.17
ते प्रविश्या तु तं भीमम्
लतागुल्मसमावृतम् ।
दद्दृशुर्भीमकर्माणम्
असुरं सुरनिर्भयम् ॥
Entering another frightful region covered by creepers and thickets, they saw an Asura of terrifying deeds, who had no fear of Suras.
4.48.18
तं दृष्ट्वा वानरा घोरम्
स्थितं शैलमिवासुरम् ।
गाढं परिहितास्सर्वे
दृष्ट्वा तं पर्वतोपमम् ॥
Seeing that horrid Asura standing there like a mountain, all of those Vānaras (became ready to confront him), drawing their clothes around their waists.
4.48.19
सोऽपि तान्वानरान्सर्वान्
नष्टास्स्थेत्यब्रवीद्बली ।
अभ्यधावत सङ्कृद्धो
मुष्टिमुद्यम्य सङ्गतम् ॥
He, the strong one also, in his rage, ran towards them lifting his clenched fist and saying to all of those Vānaras, ‘Dead you are!’
4.48.20
तमापतन्तं सहसा
वालिपुत्रोऽङ्गदस्तदा ।
रावणोऽयमिति ज्ञात्वा
तलेनाभिजघान ह ॥
As he swiftly bore down upon them, Aṅgada, the son of Vāli, took him for Rāvaṇa and hit him with his open palm.
4.48.21
स वालिपुत्राभिहतो
वक्त्राच्छोणितमुद्वमन् ।
असुरो न्यपतद्भूमौ
पर्यस्त इव पर्वतः ॥
Struck by Vāli’s son, the Asura fell upside down on the ground like a mountain, vomiting blood.
4.48.22
ते तु तस्मिन्निरुच्छ्वासे
वानरा जितकाशिनः ।
विचिन्वन्प्रायशस्तत्र
सर्वं तद्गिरिगह्वरम् ॥
With the Asura breathing his last, the Vānaras, elated with the victory, searched almost all of the mountain cave.
4.48.23
विचितं तु ततः कृत्वा
सर्वे ते काननौकसः ।
अन्यदेवापरं घोरम्
विविशुर्गिरिगह्वरम् ॥
Then those forest dwellers, making sure that that cave had been searched, went on to another frightful mountain cave.
4.48.24
ते विचित्य पुनः खिन्ना
विनिष्पत्य समागताः ।
एकान्ते वृक्षमूले तु
निषेदुर्दीनमानसाः ॥
They searched it again and came out tired and got together at the base of a tree, feeling dejected.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
किष्किन्धाकाण्डे अष्टचत्वारिंशस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the forty eighth Sarga in Kishkindhā Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
