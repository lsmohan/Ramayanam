Sundara Kaanda - Sarga 60
In this Sarga, Aṅgada, agreeing with Hanumān, suggests that they should take Jānaki with them before going back to Rāma. But Jāmbavān disagrees saying that it is not in alignment with what Rāma intended.
5.60.1
तस्य तद्वचनं श्रुत्वा
वालिसूनुरभाषत ॥
Hearing his words, the son of Vāli said:
There seems to be some Ṡlōkas missing in this Sarga, which should have been present between the Ṡlōkas 6 and 7. Jāmbavān’s response to Sugreeva, for example, is more detailed in the translation of N. Raghunathan, which is as follows:
When he thus declared his mind, the worthy Jāmbavān, who was experienced in worldly matters, objected for a relevant reason, while affectionately applauding his motive, ‘ What you say, O great ape, would not be the right course to follow, immensely sagacious as you are. We were ordered to search the great Southern quarter; but neither the King of the apes nor the wise Rāma told us to bring Seetā with us. Rāghava, that tiger among men, keeping in mind the dignity of his own race, would hardly like the idea of Seetā being brought back by us somehow. Having vowed in the presence of the ape chiefs that he would himself win back Seetā, how could that King go back on his word? All that we have done would be so much labor wasted. It would give him no satisfaction. All the prowess so far displayed would prove of no account, great apes. So we shall go to the place where Rāma and Lakshmaṇa and Sugreeva of the immense power are to be found and acquaint them with what we have done. It is not, O Prince, as if the course you have suggested is improper; but we should seek to achieve our object in conformity with Rāma’s own ideas and wishes.’
5.60.2
अयुक्तं तु विना देवीम्
दृष्टवद्भिश्च वानराः ।
समीपं गन्तुमस्माभी
राघवस्य महात्मनः ॥
O Vānaras! Having found her, it is not right for us to go to the presence of Rāghava, the Mahātma, without taking her with us.
5.60.3
दृष्टा देवी न चानीता
इति तत्र निवेदनम् ।
अयुक्तमिव पश्यामि
भवद्भिः ख्यातविक्रमैः ॥
I see it as inappropriate for you, of famed prowess, to report, ‘We have seen the Dēvi, but did not bring her!’
5.60.4
न हि नः प्लवने कश्चित्
न्ऽपि कश्चित्पराक्रमे ।
तुल्यस्सामरदैत्येषु
लोकेषु हरिसत्तमाः ॥
There is no one in all the worlds, including among Dēvas and Daityas, that are equal to us, O best of Vānaras, in flight or in attack.
5.60.5
तेष्वेवं हतवीरेषु
राक्षसेषु हनूमता ।
किमन्यदत्र कर्तव्यम्
गृहीत्वा याम जानकीम् ॥
With the Rākshasa Veeras killed by Hanumān, what else is there for us to do? Let us take Jānaki and go!
5.60.6
तमेवं कृतसङ्कल्पम्
जाम्बवान् हरिसत्तमः ।
उवाच परमप्रीतो
वाक्यमर्थवदर्थवित् ॥
When he had all but made up his mind thus, Jāmbavān, the best of the Vānaras and a savant of worldly affairs, said these words, with a hint, albeit in a pleasing manner:
5.60.7
न तावदेषा मतिरक्षमा नो
यथा भवान् पश्यति राजपुत्र ।
यथा तु रामस्य मतिर्निविष्टा
तथा भवान् पश्यतु कार्यसिद्धिम् ॥
O Prince! It is not that inappropriate for us to do the way you see things, but you shall see what would accomplish the object in alignment with what Rāma has in mind.
इत्यार्षे वाल्मीकीये
श्रीमद्रामायणे आदिकाव्ये
सुन्दरकाण्डे षष्टितमस्सर्गः॥
Thus concludes the sixtieth Sarga in Sundara Kāṇḍa of the glorious Rāmāyaṇa, the first ever poem of humankind, composed by Maharshi Vālmeeki.
